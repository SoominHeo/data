<p><i><b>Dig Dug</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ãã£ã°ãã°</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Digu Dagu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade game</a> developed and published by <a href="/wiki/Namco" title="Namco">Namco</a> in Japan in 1982. It runs on <a href="/wiki/Namco_Galaga" title="Namco Galaga">Namco Galaga</a> hardware, and was published outside Japan by <a href="/wiki/Atari,_Inc." title="Atari, Inc.">Atari, Inc.</a>.</p>
<p>Atari also obtained the license for home versions of <i>Dig Dug</i>, and released it for the <a href="/wiki/Atari_2600" title="Atari 2600">Atari 2600</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atari_5200" title="Atari 5200">Atari 5200</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atari_7800" title="Atari 7800">Atari 7800</a>, <a href="/wiki/Intellivision" title="Intellivision">Intellivision</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apple_II" title="Apple II">Apple II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atari_8-bit_family" title="Atari 8-bit family">Atari 8-bit family</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commodore_VIC-20" title="Commodore VIC-20">VIC-20</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commodore_64" title="Commodore 64">Commodore 64</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IBM_PC" title="IBM PC">IBM PC</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Texas_Instruments_TI-99/4A" title="Texas Instruments TI-99/4A">TI-99/4A</a>. Namco ported <i>Dig Dug</i> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nintendo_Family_Computer" title="Nintendo Family Computer">Nintendo Family Computer</a> in 1985.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Gakken" title="Gakken">Gakken</a> made a table top handheld game of <i>Dig Dug</i> in 1982. It was one of a series of 3 flip-top games with <a href="/wiki/Vacuum_fluorescent_display" title="Vacuum fluorescent display">VFD</a> screen and magnifying <a href="/wiki/Fresnel_lens" title="Fresnel lens">Fresnel lens</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dig_Dug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Digdug.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="288" data-file-width="224" height="257" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a0/Digdug.png/200px-Digdug.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/a/a0/Digdug.png 1.5x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Digdug.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Screenshot of Round 1</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The objective of <i>Dig Dug</i> is to eliminate underground-dwelling monsters by inflating them with an air pump until they explode, or by dropping rocks on them. There are two kinds of enemies in the game: "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pooka" title="Pooka">Pookas</a>" (a race of cute round red monsters, said to be modeled after tomatoes, that wear yellow goggles) and "Fygars" (a race of green <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragons</a> that can breathe fire while their wings flash).</p>
<p>The player's character is the eponymous Dig Dug, dressed in white and blue and able to dig tunnels through dirt. Dig Dug will be killed if he is caught by either a Pooka or a Fygar, burned by a Fygar's fire, or crushed by a rock.</p>
<p>It takes four (three in some versions) 'pumps' with the player's action button to inflate a monster until it pops. A partially inflated monster will deflate and recover after a few seconds, but half-inflating is a useful way to stun an enemy for a few moments, especially to make sure it remains in the path of a falling rock. The player can also pass through the enemy while it is deflating.</p>
<p>The monsters normally crawl through the tunnels in the dirt but can turn into <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">ghostly eyes</a> and travel slowly through the dirt. The last enemy in a round will try to escape off the top left of the screen.</p>
<p>More points will be awarded for exploding an enemy further down in the dirt (the levels are color-coded). Additionally, Fygars are worth double points if exploded horizontally, since they can only breathe fire horizontally in the direction they are facing. Extra points are also awarded for dropping rocks on enemies in order to eliminate them rather than inflating them. If one enemy is killed by the rock, it is worth 1000 points. The next two add 1500 points each, and any after that, add 2000. The act of mining is itself worth points - giving 10 points for each block mined, so some players will do as much of it as possible while the threat from the remaining monsters is minimal.</p>
<p>After the player drops two rocks, a bonus item appears at the center of the screen, awarding points if the player can collect it before it disappears. These items consists of various fruits and vegetables, as well as the flagship from the Namco game <a href="/wiki/Galaxian" title="Galaxian">Galaxian</a>, and appear even if either of the dropped rocks fails to crush any enemies. In the original arcade version, the most points attainable from a single bonus item is 8000 from the <a href="/wiki/Pineapple" title="Pineapple">pineapple</a>, which appears in round 18 and every round thereafter.</p>
<p>If the player should drop a rock on a foe at the same time he pumps it to death, a <a href="/wiki/Glitch" title="Glitch">glitch</a> will occur whereupon all enemies will promptly disappear, but the game will not progress and the player will be free to dig through all dirt. Attaining the next level of play will then remain impossible, but the glitch can be resolved by forcing a rock to drop.</p>
<p>The round numbers are represented by flowers in the top right of the screen, and each new round is noted at the beginning of each round. After every fourth round, the color of the dirt will alternate (as seen in this article's screenshot graphic). In successive rounds more monsters appear on each screen, and they move quicker. A round is completed successfully when the last monster is dispatched or succeeds in fleeing.</p>
<p>In the coin-operated version the game will end on round 256 (round 0), since the board is essentially an unplayable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kill_screen" title="Kill screen">kill screen</a>. When the round starts, a Pooka will be placed directly on top of where the player starts, and will kill Dig Dug instantly. There is no way to kill it, but The Atari version corrects this problem. No tunnels are drawn, save for the one that goes from the top-center to center. Strangely, a couple Pookas and Fygars also appear, but they don't have a tunnel to be placed in, so they are just overlaid onto the dirt.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Protagonist">Protagonist</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dig_Dug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Protagonist">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although Namco has officially given the character of the original <i>Dig Dug</i> the name Dig Dug, in other games where he makes an appearance, the protagonist goes by the name Taizo Hori (in Japanese order, HORI Taizo), and is the father of Susumu Hori, the main character in the <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Driller" title="Mr. Driller">Mr. Driller</a></i> series. He is also the ex-husband of Toby "Kissy" Masuyo, the heroine of <i><a href="/wiki/Baraduke" title="Baraduke">Baraduke</a></i>. His name is a pun on the Japanese phrase "Horitai zo" (æãããã) or "I want to dig!" (æã = dig, ãã = want, ã =Â !) â a similar pun might be rendered in English as "Will Dig" or "Wanda (Want To) Dig". Many American gamers learned of his real name via the Nintendo DS game <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Driller_Drill_Spirits" title="Mr. Driller Drill Spirits">Mr. Driller Drill Spirits</a></i>, where he is also a playable character. He is additionally featured in an unlockable gallery of Mr. Driller items in <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mr._Driller_2&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mr. Driller 2 (page does not exist)">Mr. Driller 2</a></i>. In the <i>Mr. Driller</i> series, Hori is known as the "Hero of the Dig Dug Incident". In Japan, he is also the Hero of the South Island incident and is the honorary chairman of the Driller Council to whom most of the characters answer. This contrasts greatly with the PC remake <i>Dig Dug Deeper</i>, where the hero is simply named <i>Dig Dug</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dig_Dug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Dig Dug</i> was rated the sixth most popular coin-operated video game of all time by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Killer_List_of_Video_Games" title="Killer List of Video Games">Killer List of Video Games</a> website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1984 <i>Softline</i> readers named computer versions of <i>Dig Dug</i> the tenth-worst Apple and fourth-worst Atari program of 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stgame19840304_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-stgame19840304-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequels_and_re-releases">Sequels and re-releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dig_Dug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sequels and re-releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some bootleg arcade versions of <i>Dig Dug</i> were made, under the name <i>Zig Zag</i>. One version looked exactly like the original,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the other changed both the sounds and colors, as well as adding a pickaxe power-up that made the player move faster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>A <a href="/wiki/Game_Boy" title="Game Boy">Game Boy</a> version was released in 1992 with the choices of the original and new versions was added.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Another version was released on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Namco_(plug-n-play_game)" title="Namco (plug-n-play game)">Plug 'N Play System</a>, along with <i><a href="/wiki/Galaxian" title="Galaxian">Galaxian</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pac-Man" title="Pac-Man">Pac-Man</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rally-X" title="Rally-X">Rally-X</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Bosconian" title="Bosconian">Bosconian</a></i>. In October 2006, a version of <i>Dig Dug</i> was released on <a href="/wiki/Xbox_Live_Arcade" title="Xbox Live Arcade">Xbox Live Arcade</a>, and became <a href="/wiki/List_of_Xbox_360_games_compatible_with_Xbox_One" title="List of Xbox 360 games compatible with Xbox One">backwards compatible</a> on <a href="/wiki/Xbox_One" title="Xbox One">Xbox One</a> in May 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Namco_Networks" title="Namco Networks">Namco Networks</a> ported <i>Dig Dug</i> to <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Windows</a> (bought online) in 2009 which also includes an "Enhanced" mode which replaces all of the original sprites with the sprites from <i><a href="/wiki/Dig_Dug:_Digging_Strike" title="Dig Dug: Digging Strike">Dig Dug: Digging Strike</a></i>, Namco Networks also made a bundle (also bought online) which includes their Windows version of <i>Dig Dug</i> as well as their port of the original <i><a href="/wiki/Pac-Man" title="Pac-Man">Pac-Man</a></i> called <i>Namco All-Stars: Pac-Man and Dig Dug</i>. The arcade version has also been released on the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a> <a href="/wiki/Virtual_Console" title="Virtual Console">Virtual Console</a> in Japan on October 20, 2009, along with its sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Dig_Dug_II" title="Dig Dug II">Dig Dug II</a></i> and the original <i>Dig Dug</i> was released as part of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pac-Man_Anniversary_Arcade_Machines" title="Pac-Man Anniversary Arcade Machines">Pac-Man's Arcade Party</a></i> 30th Anniversary arcade machine in 2010. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NES" title="NES">NES</a> version for the Virtual Console was released in 2008 for the Wii, 2013 for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_3DS" title="Nintendo 3DS">Nintendo 3DS</a> and 2015 for the <a href="/wiki/Wii_U" title="Wii U">Wii U</a>, but as an import for Western regions when ported to the former. The arcade version was released on Xbox One and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">PlayStation 4</a> on April 20, 2016 as part of Bandai Namco's <i>Arcade Game Series</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>A 1985 sequel to this game, the overhead-view oriented <i><a href="/wiki/Dig_Dug_II" title="Dig Dug II">Dig Dug II</a></i>, met with less success in the arcades. <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Driller" title="Mr. Driller">Mr. Driller</a></i> (1999) was originally conceived as a sequel, with the working title <i>Dig Dug 3</i>, but it developed into a distinct but related series. Another sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Dig_Dug:_Digging_Strike" title="Dig Dug: Digging Strike">Dig Dug: Digging Strike</a></i>, was released in 2005 for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_DS" title="Nintendo DS">Nintendo DS</a>. This combined the side-view play of the original with the overhead play of the sequel and added a narrative link to the <i>Mr. Driller</i> series. A 3D remake of the original, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Dig_Dug_Deeper" title="Dig Dug Deeper">Dig Dug Deeper</a></i>, was released for PC in 2001 by Infogrames. The original <i>Dig Dug</i> was released for the <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a> console via <a href="/wiki/Xbox_Live_Arcade" title="Xbox Live Arcade">Xbox Live Arcade</a> on October 11, 2006. The original <i>Dig Dug</i> is also available for play via the <a href="/wiki/GameTap" title="GameTap">GameTap</a> subscription gaming service, and was shown in one of the television commercials for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gametap" title="Gametap">Gametap</a> website in 2005. <a class="external autonumber" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oSFVoqsa7yM" rel="nofollow">[1]</a> It was re-released for the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Virtual_Console" title="Virtual Console">Virtual Console</a> in North America on June 9, 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and in Europe on August 29, 2008, at a cost of 600 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wii_Points" title="Wii Points">Wii Points</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dig_Dug_Arrangement"><i>Dig Dug Arrangement</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dig_Dug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dig Dug Arrangement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1996, Namco packaged both this game and an updated variant and re-released it in arcades with the title <i><a href="/wiki/Namco_Classic_Collection_Vol._2" title="Namco Classic Collection Vol. 2">Namco Classic Collection Vol. 2</a></i>. The updated variant was named <i>Dig Dug Arrangement</i>, and allowed two players to play simultaneously, unlike the original. Out of the six created <i>Arrangement</i> games, this version has the least amount of changes. The graphics are updated and the rounds are different. There are also new features such as giant rocks which can fall down to the bottom of the screen, and special power-up items.</p>
<p><i>Dig Dug Arrangement</i> was re-released alongside the original <i>Dig Dug</i> and ten other Namco games on the <a href="/wiki/Namco_Museum#Namco_Museum_.28PS2.2C_Xbox.2C_GameCube.29" title="Namco Museum">PS2, Xbox and GameCube versions of Namco Museum</a>.</p>
<p>In this version of Dig Dug, there are balls (inspired by <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmo_Gang_the_Puzzle" title="Cosmo Gang the Puzzle">Cosmo Gang the Puzzle</a></i>) that can kill a lot of enemies in a row.</p>
<p>In 2005, Namco released another game with the title <i>Dig Dug Arrangement</i>, as part of <i><a href="/wiki/Namco_Museum_Battle_Collection" title="Namco Museum Battle Collection">Namco Museum Battle Collection</a></i>. It is an entirely different game from <i>Namco Classic Collection Vol. 2'</i>s <i>Dig Dug Arrangement</i> but still has the concept of being an updated variant of <i>Dig Dug</i> by having new graphics, obstacles, enemies, boss battles, power-ups, and so on. The <i>Battle Collection</i> edition of <i>Dig Dug Arrangement</i> was also released as part of <i><a href="/wiki/Namco_Museum#Namco_Museum_Virtual_Arcade" title="Namco Museum">Namco Museum Virtual Arcade</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a>, but with the multiplayer features removed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dig_Dug_Remix"><i>Dig Dug Remix</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dig_Dug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Dig Dug Remix">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Dig Dug Arrangement</i> from <i>Namco Museum Battle Collection</i> was also ported to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IOS_Apple" title="IOS Apple">iOS</a> renamed <i>Dig Dug Remix</i> with the original <i>Dig Dug</i> included, but all multiplayer features were removed from that port.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mobile_game">Mobile game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dig_Dug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mobile game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, Namco Networks released a version of <i>Dig Dug</i> for cell phones and <a href="/wiki/Palm_OS" title="Palm OS">Palm OS</a>/<a href="/wiki/Windows_Mobile" title="Windows Mobile">Windows Mobile</a> devices that is authentic to the arcade original in terms of graphics and controls, even though the levels are as they are in the NES version of <i>Dig Dug</i>. Unlike the arcade version, there is no kill screen at level 256, but rather the levels go on past 500.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cameos">Cameos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dig_Dug&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cameos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The character Pooka has many cameos in Namco games, most often as an enemy in Namco games such as the <i><a href="/wiki/Pac-Man_World" title="Pac-Man World">Pac-Man World</a></i> series. Pooka was playable for the first time in the game <i><a href="/wiki/Ms._Pac-Man_Maze_Madness" title="Ms. Pac-Man Maze Madness">Ms. Pac-Man Maze Madness</a></i> as an unlockable character for the multiplayer modes. He is also available to play as in <i><a href="/wiki/Pac-Man_World_Rally" title="Pac-Man World Rally">Pac-Man World Rally</a></i>, as well as Fygar. Pooka also appeared in the Atari licensed version of the Namco arcade game <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pole_Position" title="Pole Position">Pole Position</a></i> on one of the roadside billboards. In <i><a href="/wiki/Pac-Man_World" title="Pac-Man World">Pac-Man World</a></i>, he appears as one of the friends of Pac-Man who was kidnapped by Toc-Man.</p>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/R4:_Ridge_Racer_Type_4" title="R4: Ridge Racer Type 4">R4: Ridge Racer Type 4</a></i>, there is an American racing team with <i>Dig Dug</i> artwork on its hauler and is named the "Dig Racing Team", run by manager Robert Chrisman. It is the "expert" team of the game. Also in R4, the track "Phantomile" has a giant statue of Pooka alongside Pac-Man on the left hand side of the finishing straight. The "Pooka Line" track, which is the first in the game, has a giant screen with a Pooka and Fygar chasing <i>Dig Dug'</i>s protagonist in arcade-style graphics, which changes to him inflating a Pooka when the player takes the lead. In <i><a href="/wiki/Ridge_Racer_64" title="Ridge Racer 64">Ridge Racer 64</a></i>, however, "Dig Racing Team" has to be unlocked by winning all the tracks in Stage 3 in first place â and waiting for the credits to roll, then defeating the car on the "Renegade Expert" track in Car Attack mode. This same game also features Pooka as a selectable car, which is unlocked by breaking the time record in any of the Ridge Racer Extreme tracks on Time Attack mode.</p>
<p>The <i>Dig Dug</i> universe and some of its characters appear in the <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Driller" title="Mr. Driller">Mr. Driller</a></i> games, starring Taizo Hori's son, Susumu. Additionally, he is also a playable character in the Japan only RPG, <i><a href="/wiki/Namco_%C3%97_Capcom" title="Namco Ã Capcom">Namco Ã Capcom</a></i>.</p>
<p><i>Dig Dug</i> was parodied in the <i><a href="/wiki/Robot_Chicken" title="Robot Chicken">Robot Chicken</a></i> episode "President Evil" and the <i><a href="/wiki/Drawn_Together" title="Drawn Together">Drawn Together</a></i> episode "The One Wherin There Is a Big Twist, Part II".</p>
<p>In the Disney movie <i><a href="/wiki/Wreck-It_Ralph" title="Wreck-It Ralph">Wreck-It Ralph</a></i>, Dig Dug, a Pooka, and a Fygar are three of the characters in Game Central Station.</p>
<p>Taizo Hori appeared in an episode of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_Battle" title="Death Battle">Death Battle</a></i>, where he fought <a href="/wiki/Bomberman" title="Bomberman">Bomberman</a>.</p>
