<p><b>Diniyar Rinatovich Bilyaletdinov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Динияр Ринатович Билялетдинов</span>, <a href="/wiki/Tatar_language" title="Tatar language">Tatar</a>: <small><a href="/wiki/Tatar_alphabet" title="Tatar alphabet">Cyrillic</a></small> <span lang="tt">Динияр Ринат улы Билалетдинев</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Tatar_alphabet" title="Tatar alphabet">Latin</a></small> <i><span lang="tt">Diniyar Rinat uğlı Bilaletdinev</span></i>; born 27 February 1985 in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Volga_Tatars" title="Volga Tatars">Tatar</a> born-Russian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midfielder_(association_football)" title="Midfielder (association football)">midfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/FC_Rubin_Kazan" title="FC Rubin Kazan">FC Rubin Kazan</a>.</p>
<p>Bilyaletdinov has played for <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a> since 2005, earning 46 caps and scoring 6 goals. He was part of their team which reached the semi-finals at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_2008" title="Euro 2008">Euro 2008</a>.</p>

<p>A native of Moscow, Bilyaletdinov began his career in the Lokomotiv Moscow youth teams. After making his debut in 2004 at age of 19, he became a first team regular. In his first season the club won the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">Russian Premier League</a> and was named the league's Young Player of the Year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-efcprofile_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-efcprofile-2">[2]</a></sup> He was the Lokomotiv <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a> for the 2007 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Everton">Everton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diniyar_Bilyaletdinov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Everton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bilyaletdinov signed a four-year contract with English club <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> in August 2009 for an undisclosed fee – believed to be £9 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> – upon recommendation from national team manager <a href="/wiki/Guus_Hiddink" title="Guus Hiddink">Guus Hiddink</a>, who was at <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> at that time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The move made him Everton's third most expensive signing in their history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>He made his debut for Everton coming on as an 89th-minute substitute in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> later in the month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 17 October 2009, Bilyaletdinov scored his first goal for the club in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> and scored again on the following week in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>, but received his first red card in the Premier League later on in the match.</p>
<p>He contributed two assists in his first-ever start for Everton in the inaugural <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> in a 4–0 win over <a href="/wiki/AEK_Athens_F.C." title="AEK Athens F.C.">AEK Athens</a>, and scored against them again in the last group stage game. Bilyaletdinov scored seven goals in his first season in England, including one against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> that was voted the club's goal of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On the final day of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Everton_F.C._season" title="2009–10 Everton F.C. season">2009–10</a> which is 9 May 2010, he scored another brilliant goal in Everton's 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> which was arguably better than the goal of the season he scored.</p>
<p>The following season, Bilyaletdinov began to find himself on the bench, but in the second half of the season, he made more appearances following the departure of <a href="/wiki/Steven_Pienaar" title="Steven Pienaar">Steven Pienaar</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Bilyaletdinov scored his first goal of the 2010-11 Premier League season coming on as a substitute against <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> and scored his second against Wolverhampton Wanderers. In the final game of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Everton_F.C._season" title="2010–11 Everton F.C. season">2010–11</a> season, on 14 May, Bilyaletdinov received another red card in a 1–0 loss against <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Brom</a>. After the game, the club decided not to appeal his sending off, so he served a three-match ban ahead of the new season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, despite scoring 9 goals in 77 appearances, he failed to make a major impact during his time at Everton and had been on the fringes of the first team squad during the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Everton_F.C._season" title="2011–12 Everton F.C. season">2011–12 season</a> after finding himself playing in the reserves. In November 2011, Bilyaletdinov said that he sought to leave the club if he did not get a first team place, as he feared losing his place in the national team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Bilyaletdinov made his last appearance for Everton in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> on 14 January 2012. After leaving Everton, Bilyaletdinov spoke out by criticising their tactics and lack of creativity with a problem of scoring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spartak_Moscow">Spartak Moscow</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diniyar_Bilyaletdinov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Spartak Moscow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2012, Bilyaletdinov was sold to <a href="/wiki/FC_Spartak_Moscow" title="FC Spartak Moscow">Spartak Moscow</a> for an undisclosed fee,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> as Everton wished to raise funds to buy other players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> After his move to Spartak Moscow, Bilyaletdinov was given the number 25 shirt.</p>
<p>Bilyaletdinov made his debut for Spartak Moscow in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/FC_Rubin_Kazan" title="FC Rubin Kazan">Rubin Kazan</a> on 5 March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On 6 May 2012, Bilyaletdinov scored his first goal in over three years since leaving the country and first for the club in a 3–2 win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit Saint Petersburg</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anzhi_Makhachkala">Anzhi Makhachkala</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diniyar_Bilyaletdinov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Anzhi Makhachkala">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2014, Bilyaletdinov was loaned out by <a href="/wiki/FC_Spartak_Moscow" title="FC Spartak Moscow">Spartak Moscow</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Football_Premier_League" title="Russian Football Premier League">RFPL</a> rivals <a href="/wiki/FC_Anzhi_Makhachkala" title="FC Anzhi Makhachkala">Anzhi Makhachkala</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diniyar_Bilyaletdinov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bilya.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="304" data-file-width="161" height="283" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Bilya.JPG/150px-Bilya.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/34/Bilya.JPG 1.5x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bilya.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Diniyar Bilyaletdinov before home match against FYR Macedonia</div>

<p>After Russia failed to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>, he and several younger players were recalled to the U-21 squad for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship_qualification#Qualification_Playoffs" title="2006 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification">2006 UEFA U21 championship playoffs</a> against Denmark<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> but lost out to the Danes.</p>
<p>Bilyaletdinov played in ten out of twelve <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a> qualifiers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-efcprofile_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-efcprofile-2">[2]</a></sup> He scored the equalising goal against <a href="/wiki/Israel_national_football_team" title="Israel national football team">Israel</a>, but the team lost in final minutes, 1–2. In the following match against <a href="/wiki/Andorra_national_football_team" title="Andorra national football team">Andorra</a>, he assisted <a href="/wiki/Dmitri_Sychev" title="Dmitri Sychev">Dmitri Sychev</a> in scoring the winning goal, resulting in the team qualifying for the tournament proper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He was called up to Russia's squad for Euro 2008 and played in all of Russia's run to the tournament's semi-final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-efcprofile_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-efcprofile-2">[2]</a></sup> For the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification">FIFA 2010 World Cup qualification</a> he was called up for six of the ten matches and scored twice in the play-off first leg against <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia</a>, which Russia won 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> His two goals were not enough as Russia lost the second leg 1–0 and Slovenia progressed to the World Cup on the <a href="/wiki/Away_goals_rule" title="Away goals rule">away goals rule</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diniyar_Bilyaletdinov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bilyaletdinov is an ethnic <a href="/wiki/Tatars" title="Tatars">Tatar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> His father <a href="/wiki/Rinat_Bilyaletdinov" title="Rinat Bilyaletdinov">Rinat Bilyaletdinov</a> was also a professional footballer and is now a coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-efcprofile_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-efcprofile-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 June 2011, Bilyaletdinov married Maria, a former cheerleader for the basketball club <a href="/wiki/WBC_CSKA_Moscow" title="WBC CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Their first son, Timur, was born on 21 March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
