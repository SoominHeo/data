<p><b>Didier</b> is a <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> name. It comes from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Roman" title="Ancient Roman">Ancient Roman</a> name <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Didius" title="Didius">Didius</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Extremely common among Haitian men. It has a dualistic meaning. On one hand the name can be translated as extremely desirable but it can also be translated as longing.</p>
<p>During the 5th century AD, with the Christianisation of ancient pagan names, it has become associated with the name <i><a href="/wiki/Desiderius_(given_name)" title="Desiderius (given name)">Desiderius</a></i>, related to <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>desiderium</i> - which can be translated as "ardent desire" or "the longed-for".</p>
