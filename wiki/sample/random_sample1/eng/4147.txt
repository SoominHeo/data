<p><b>D-Link Corporation</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">友訊科技</span>) is a Taiwanese multinational <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Networking_equipment" title="Networking equipment">networking equipment</a> manufacturing corporation headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a>, Taiwan. It was founded in March 1986 in Taipei as <i>Datex Systems Inc.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It began as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Network_adapter" title="Network adapter">network adapter</a> vendor and has gone on to become a designer, developer, and manufacturer of networking solutions for both the consumer and business markets.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The company is known for the bad security of the products they sell, to the point that the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a> has sued D-Link for failing to take reasonable steps to secure their routers and IP cameras.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D-Link&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>D-Link Corporation changed its name from Datex Systems Inc. in 1994, when it went public and when it became the first networking company on the Taiwan Stock Exchange. It is now publicly traded on the TSEC and NSE stock exchanges. It was founded by seven individuals including Ken Kao, the late Chairman of D-Link.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2007, it was the leading networking company in the small to medium business (SMB) segment worldwide with 21.9% market share.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In March 2008, it became the market leader in Wi-Fi product shipments worldwide, with 33% of the total market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2007, the company was featured in the "Info Tech 100", a listing of the world's best IT companies. It was also ranked as the 9th best IT company in the world for shareholder returns by <i><a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_Businessweek" title="Bloomberg Businessweek">BusinessWeek</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Product_range">Product range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D-Link&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Product range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>D-Link's products are geared towards the networking and communications market. Its business products include switches, surveillance network cameras, firewalls, iSCSI SANs and business wireless, while consumer products cover consumer wireless devices, broadband devices, and the Digital Home devices (which include media players, storage, and surveillance camera/NVR).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vulnerabilities">Vulnerabilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D-Link&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Vulnerabilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 2010, it was reported that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HNAP" title="HNAP">HNAP</a> vulnerabilities had been found on some D-Link routers. D-Link was also criticized for their response which was deemed confusing as to which models were affected and downplayed the seriousness of the risk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2013, version v1.13 for the DIR-100 revA was reported to include a backdoor in the firmware. By passing a specific <a href="/wiki/User_agent" title="User agent">user agent</a> in an HTTP request to the router, normal authentication is bypassed. It was reported that this backdoor had been present for some time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Computerworld reported in January 2015 that ZynOS, a firmware used by some D-Link routers (as well as <a href="/wiki/ZTE" title="ZTE">ZTE</a>, <a href="/wiki/TP-Link" title="TP-Link">TP-Link</a>, and others), are vulnerable to <a href="/wiki/DNS_hijacking" title="DNS hijacking">DNS hijacking</a> by an unauthenticated remote attacker, specifically when remote management is enabled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later in 2015, it was reported that D-Link leaked the private keys used to sign firmware updates for the DCS-5020L security camera and a variety of other D-Link products. The key expired in September 2015, but had been published online for seven months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also in 2015, D-Link was criticized for more HNAP vulnerabilities,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and worse, introducing new vulnerabilities in their "fixed" firmware updates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 5, 2017, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Trade_Commission" title="Federal Trade Commission">Federal Trade Commission</a> sued D-Link for failing to take reasonable steps to secure their routers and IP cameras. As D-Link marketing was misleading customers into believing their products were secure. The complaint also says security gaps could allow hackers to watch and record people on their D-Link cameras without their knowledge, target them for theft, or record private conversations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Server_misuse">Server misuse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D-Link&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Server misuse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2006, D-Link was accused of <a href="/wiki/NTP_server_misuse_and_abuse#D-Link_and_Poul-Henning_Kamp" title="NTP server misuse and abuse">NTP vandalism</a>, when it was found that its routers were sending time requests to a small <a href="/wiki/Network_Time_Protocol" title="Network Time Protocol">NTP server</a> in Denmark, incurring thousands of dollars of costs to its operator. D-Link initially refused to accept responsibility.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Later, D-link products were found also to be abusing other time servers, including some operated by the US military and <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="GPL_violation">GPL violation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=D-Link&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: GPL violation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 6, 2006, the gpl-violations.org project prevailed in court litigation against D-Link Germany GmbH regarding D-Link's alleged inappropriate and copyright infringing use of parts of the <a href="/wiki/Linux_kernel" title="Linux kernel">Linux kernel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
