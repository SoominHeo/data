<p><b>Dimitrovgrad</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_Cyrillic_language" title="Serbian Cyrillic language">Serbian Cyrillic</a>: <span lang="sr"><span class="Unicode">Димитровград</span></span>) is a town and municipality located in the <a href="/wiki/Pirot_District" title="Pirot District">Pirot District</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_and_Eastern_Serbia" title="Southern and Eastern Serbia">eastern Serbia</a>. According to 2011 census, the municipality of Dimitrovgrad has a population of 10,118 people and the town 6,278.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitrovgrad,_Serbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dimitrovgrad_Town_Hall_-_Serbia.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1800" data-file-width="2684" height="101" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Dimitrovgrad_Town_Hall_-_Serbia.jpg/150px-Dimitrovgrad_Town_Hall_-_Serbia.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Dimitrovgrad_Town_Hall_-_Serbia.jpg/225px-Dimitrovgrad_Town_Hall_-_Serbia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Dimitrovgrad_Town_Hall_-_Serbia.jpg/300px-Dimitrovgrad_Town_Hall_-_Serbia.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dimitrovgrad_Town_Hall_-_Serbia.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Dimitrovgrad Town Hall.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The official <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a> name is <i>Dimitrovgrad</i> (Димитровград), but the name <i>Caribrod</i> (Цариброд) is also used in Serbian. In <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>, the name <i>Tsaribrod</i> (Цариброд) is preferred because there is another town called Dimitrovgrad on the Maritsa river in Bulgaria and Tsaribrod was used before the town was named after <a href="/wiki/Georgi_Dimitrov" title="Georgi Dimitrov">Georgi Dimitrov</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">Communist</a> leader who advocated a union between the <a href="/wiki/Bulgarians" title="Bulgarians">Bulgarians</a> and remaining <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslav</a> nations to form the <a href="/wiki/Balkan_Federation" title="Balkan Federation">Balkan Federation</a>. The idea was abandoned when Yugoslav leader <a href="/wiki/Josip_Broz_Tito" title="Josip Broz Tito">Josip Broz Tito</a> failed to reach agreements with <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tito-Stalin_split" title="Tito-Stalin split">Tito-Stalin split</a>), however Dimitrov himself did not become unpopular in Yugoslavia from the breakdown and subsequently, the name of the town continued to be in honour of him despite many Bulgarians themselves having preferred Caribrod.</p>
<p>There have been attempts to return the old name, <i>Caribrod</i>, but the last referendum, held on Sunday, June 13, 2004, was invalid due to a low turnout. Only 47.8% of the 9,811 voters on the electoral roll turned out to vote, while 50% was the required minimum. Of those who voted on the issue, 2,586 were in favour of keeping the name <i>Dimitrovgrad</i>, while 1,786 wanted the name <i>Caribrod</i> to be returned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>According to the 2002 census data, the population of the Dimitrovgrad municipality was 11,748 people, and it was composed of:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popis_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-popis-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bulgarians" title="Bulgarians">Bulgarians</a> = 5,836 (50%)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbs</a> = 3,000 (25.58%)</li>
<li>Others = 2,907 (24.42%)</li>
</ul>
<p>According to the 2011 census data,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011_census_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-2011_census-5">[5]</a></sup> the population of the Dimitrovgrad municipality was 10,118 people, and it was composed of:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bulgarians" title="Bulgarians">Bulgarians</a> = 5,413 (53.5%)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbs</a> = 2,819 (27.86%)</li>
<li>Others (including persons who refused to declare an ethnicity)</li>
</ul>
<p>Regardless of ethnic self-identification, most of the town's population speaks a South Slavic dialect usually called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torlak" title="Torlak">Torlak</a></i> which includes linguistic features of both <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a> (especially phonology) and <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a> (postposed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Definite_article" title="Definite article">definite articles</a> and lack of the <a href="/wiki/Infinitive" title="Infinitive">infinitive</a> verb form), as well as some entirely unique <a href="/wiki/Vocabulary" title="Vocabulary">vocabulary</a>.</p>

<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_road" title="Roman road">Roman road</a> <a href="/wiki/Via_Militaris" title="Via Militaris">Via Militaris</a> was built in the 1st century AD and went through the town. In May 2010, well-preserved remains of the road were excavated during the work on <a href="/wiki/Pan-European_Corridor_X" title="Pan-European Corridor X">Corridor 10</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>An Ottoman military base operated in the west of Dimitrovgrad prior to the Balkan nations full independence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Serbian and Bulgarian Prime ministers met at the then Tsaribrod in 1912 to discuss the disputed territories in <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(region)" title="Macedonia (region)">Macedonia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Serbs,_Croats_and_Slovenes" title="Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes">Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes</a> gained parts of the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Bulgaria" title="Principality of Bulgaria">Principality of Bulgaria</a> (known in Bulgaria as <a href="/wiki/Western_Outlands" title="Western Outlands">Western Outlands</a>) which included Tsaribrod following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Neuilly" title="Treaty of Neuilly">Treaty of Neuilly</a>, signed on November 27, 1919.</p>
<p>From 1929 to 1941, Caribrod was part of the <a href="/wiki/Morava_Banovina" title="Morava Banovina">Morava Banovina</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Yugoslavia" title="Kingdom of Yugoslavia">Kingdom of Yugoslavia</a>. It was occupied by <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bulgaria" title="Kingdom of Bulgaria">Kingdom of Bulgaria</a> between 1941 and 1944 during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.</p>
