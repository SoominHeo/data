<p><b>Dimitris Avramopoulos</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Δημήτρης Αβραμόπουλος</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a> of the conservative <a href="/wiki/New_Democracy_(Greece)" title="New Democracy (Greece)">New Democracy</a> party, and former career <a href="/wiki/Diplomat" title="Diplomat">diplomat</a>. He has served in various high-level cabinet posts, including <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Foreign_Affairs_(Greece)" title="Minister for Foreign Affairs (Greece)">Minister for Foreign Affairs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_National_Defence_(Greece)" title="Minister for National Defence (Greece)">Minister for National Defence</a>, and was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Athens" title="Mayor of Athens">Mayor of Athens</a> in 1995–2002. Since 1 November 2014 he is serving as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EU_Commissioner" title="EU Commissioner">EU Commissioner</a> for <a href="/wiki/List_of_European_Commission_portfolios#Home_Affairs" title="List of European Commission portfolios">Migration, Home Affairs</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Commissioner_for_Justice,_Fundamental_Rights_and_Citizenship" title="European Commissioner for Justice, Fundamental Rights and Citizenship">Citizenship</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Juncker_Commission" title="Juncker Commission">Juncker Commission</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Avramopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Avramopoulos was born at Athens in 1953, into a family which had originally come from <a href="/wiki/Elis_(regional_unit)" title="Elis (regional unit)">Ilia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arcadia" title="Arcadia">Arcadia</a>. He served his 26 months <a href="/wiki/Military_service" title="Military service">military service</a> from 1978 to 1980 in the <a href="/wiki/Hellenic_Air_Force" title="Hellenic Air Force">Hellenic Air Force</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is married to Vivian, by whom he has two sons, Filippos and Iasonas. Apart from his native <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>, he speaks <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> fluently.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diplomatic_career">Diplomatic career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Avramopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Diplomatic career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1980, Avramopoulos joined the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_(Greece)" title="Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Greece)">Ministry of Foreign Affairs</a> in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, where he worked until 1983. From 1988 to 1992, he served as Greek <a href="/wiki/Consul_(representative)" title="Consul (representative)">Consul</a> to Belgium in <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge" title="Liège">Liège</a>. At the same time he was a Special Adviser to <a href="/wiki/Konstantinos_Mitsotakis" title="Konstantinos Mitsotakis">Konstantinos Mitsotakis</a>, President and Leader of the <a href="/wiki/New_Democracy_(Greece)" title="New Democracy (Greece)">New Democracy</a>. During this time he also represented Greece in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conference_for_Security_and_Co-operation_in_Europe" title="Conference for Security and Co-operation in Europe">Conference for Security and Co-operation in Europe</a>. In 1992 he became Official Spokesman of the Greek Ministry of Foreign Affairs and was appointed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consul_General" title="Consul General">Consul General</a> of Greece in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>. In 1993 he was promoted as Director of the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Greece" title="Prime Minister of Greece">Prime Minister of Greece</a>'s Diplomatic Office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Avramopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mayor_of_Athens">Mayor of Athens</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Avramopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mayor of Athens">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1993, Avramopoulos resigned from Greek diplomatic service to enter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliamentary_politics" title="Parliamentary politics">parliamentary politics</a> as a member of <a href="/wiki/New_Democracy_(Greece)" title="New Democracy (Greece)">New Democracy</a> and was elected a member of its Central Committee. From 1993 to 1994 he served as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_the_Hellenic_Parliament" title="Member of the Hellenic Parliament">Member of the Hellenic Parliament</a>. In 1994 he was elected <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Athens" title="Mayor of Athens">Mayor of Athens</a>, and was re-elected in October 1998 in a historical first-round landslide victory. From 1995 to 1999 he served as Chairman of the <i>Central Union of Local Authorities</i> of Greece.</p>
<p>In 1995 he founded the “Permanent Conference of the Mayors of the Capitals of South-East Europe” and served as its first President. From 1996 to 2000 he served as Vice-President of the Executive Committee of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Union_of_Local_Authorities" title="International Union of Local Authorities">International Union of Local Authorities</a> (IULA) and from 1997 to 2002, as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_the_Council_of_Europe" title="Congress of the Council of Europe">Committee of the Regions of the European Union</a>. Elected as President of the <a href="/wiki/World_Cities_Summit" title="World Cities Summit">Summit Conference of the Mayors of the World</a> in 2000, he served until 2002, during which time he founded the "<i>World Union of Olympic Cities</i>", "<i>Athens’ International Prize for Democracy</i>", and “<i>World Institute of Global and Cities’ Diplomacy</i>”, <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ministerial_posts">Ministerial posts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Avramopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ministerial posts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2001, he split from New Democracy with his <a href="/wiki/Movement_of_Free_Citizens" title="Movement of Free Citizens">Movement of Free Citizens</a> (KEP), but in a surprising move merged back in June 2002. When in 2004 New Democracy won the <a href="/wiki/Greek_legislative_election,_2004" title="Greek legislative election, 2004">parliamentary elections</a>, Avramopoulos was appointed <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Tourism_(Greece)" title="Ministry of Tourism (Greece)">Minister of Tourism</a>, serving until 2006, and afterwards, from 2006 until 2009, as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Health_and_Social_Solidarity" title="Ministry of Health and Social Solidarity">Minister of Health and Social Solidarity</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Greek_legislative_election,_2009" title="Greek legislative election, 2009">October 2009 election</a>, Avramopoulos was re-elected to the <a href="/wiki/Hellenic_Parliament" title="Hellenic Parliament">Hellenic Parliament</a> representing the constituency of <a href="/wiki/Athens_A" title="Athens A">Athens A</a>. After the election of <a href="/wiki/Antonis_Samaras" title="Antonis Samaras">Antonis Samaras</a> to the “New Democracy” Party Presidency he was appointed President of the Organizing Committee of the 8th Party Congress held at Athens in June 2010. In July 2010, he became Vice-President of “New Democracy”, serving until 1 November 2014.</p>
<p>On November 11, 2011 he was appointed <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_National_Defence_(Greece)" title="Minister for National Defence (Greece)">Minister for National Defence</a> in the coalition government of <a href="/wiki/Lucas_Papademos" title="Lucas Papademos">Lucas Papademos</a>, resigning from his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliamentary_seat" title="Parliamentary seat">parliamentary seat</a>, thus adhering to the New Democracy policy that no serving Opposition Party <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_the_Greek_Parliament" title="Member of the Greek Parliament">Deputies</a> may hold <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministerial_office" title="Ministerial office">ministerial office</a>.</p>
<p>At the May 6, 2012 election, Avramopoulos was returned as Deputy for Athens A, being re-elected in the June 17, 2012 general election. On June 21, 2012 he was appointed <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Foreign_Affairs_(Greece)" title="Minister for Foreign Affairs (Greece)">Minister for Foreign Affairs</a>. After the Cabinet reshuffle of June 25, 2013, he became Greek Minister for Defence again, serving until his nomination as Greece's <a href="/wiki/European_Commissioner" title="European Commissioner">European Commissioner</a> in November 2014.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_Commissioner_for_Migration_and_Home_Affairs">European Commissioner for Migration and Home Affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Avramopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: European Commissioner for Migration and Home Affairs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 27 July 2014, Prime Minister Antonis Samaras nominated Avramopoulos as Greece's next member of the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Before the nomination, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dora_Bakoyiannis" title="Dora Bakoyiannis">Dora Bakoyiannis</a> was widely seen to be a strong contender for the Commission post.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Samaras also defied calls from the centre-left Pasok party for <a href="/wiki/Maria_Damanaki" title="Maria Damanaki">Maria Damanaki</a> to continue as European Commissioner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jean-Claude_Juncker" title="Jean-Claude Juncker">Jean-Claude Juncker</a> then nominated Avramopoulos as European Commissioner for Migration and Home Affairs. In this capacity, Avramopoulos shares competency over cyber-security matters with <a href="/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Oettinger" title="Günther Oettinger">Günther Oettinger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the aftermath of the victory of the left-wing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SYRIZA" title="SYRIZA">SYRIZA</a> party in the <a href="/wiki/Greek_legislative_election,_January_2015" title="Greek legislative election, January 2015">25 January election</a> in 2015, Avramopoulos' name was widely circulated in Greek media as the most likely choice for SYRIZA's candidate in the second round of the <a href="/wiki/Greek_presidential_election,_2014%E2%80%9315" title="Greek presidential election, 2014–15">election</a> for the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Greece" title="President of Greece">Presidency of Greece</a>. According to the Greek media reports, the nomination would be seen both as a gesture of conciliation with the right, but would crucially also enable SYRIZA to nominate its own European Commissioner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 30 January Samaras phoned Avramopoulos and assured him that <i>New Democracy</i> would support his eventual nomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_positions">Other positions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Avramopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Other positions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From the outset of his political career, Avramopoulos served for twenty years as Honorary President of the <i>Athens’ International Prize for Democracy</i> for <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> (until 2013). He has also been Chairman of the Steering Committee on Cities´ Diplomacy, established by the Global Forum (Rome) and the World Bank Institute (Washington D.C.). He was elected President of the “World Institute of Global and Cities’ Diplomacy”, an independent NGO based in Rome as well as Executive President of the “World Union of Olympic Cities”, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IOC" title="IOC">Olympic Games</a> NGO.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Greek-Turkish_rapprochement">Greek-Turkish rapprochement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Avramopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Greek-Turkish rapprochement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Avramopoulos has a friendly relation with the Turkish President <a href="/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan" title="Recep Tayyip Erdoğan">Recep Tayyip Erdoğan</a> since they were Mayors of Athens and Istanbul respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He is deemed one of the main proponents of <a href="/wiki/Greek%E2%80%93Turkish_relations" title="Greek–Turkish relations">Greek-Turkish rapprochement</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_degrees">Academic degrees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Avramopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Academic degrees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Avramopoulos read Public Law and Political Science at <a href="/wiki/National_and_Kapodistrian_University_of_Athens" title="National and Kapodistrian University of Athens">Athens University Law School</a>, graduating with the degree of <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> (BA). He then undertook postgraduate studies receiving a Specialization Diploma in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Organisation" title="International Organisation">International Organisation</a> at <a href="/wiki/Boston_University_Brussels" title="Boston University Brussels">Boston University</a>, and later a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">Master's degree</a> in European Studies at the Institute of European Affairs, <a href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_libre_de_Bruxelles" title="Université libre de Bruxelles">Université libre de Bruxelles</a>. He has been conferred honorary doctorates by <a href="/wiki/Adelphi_University" title="Adelphi University">Adelphi University</a> (Long Island, New York), <a href="/wiki/Deree_College" title="Deree College">Deree College</a> (Athens), <a href="/wiki/Drexel_University" title="Drexel University">Drexel University</a> (Philadelphia), <a href="/wiki/Kingston_University" title="Kingston University">Kingston University</a> (London), European College of Parma (Italy)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and has been elected Honorary Professor of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Russian Academy of Sciences">Russian Academy of Sciences</a> and of <a href="/wiki/Peking_University" title="Peking University">Peking University</a> (Beijing).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Distinctions_and_awards">Distinctions and awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Avramopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Distinctions and awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Avramopoulos received, in 2006, the “Vincitore Assoluto” Award of the Premio Internazionale “Giuseppe Sciacca”, and has been presented with distinctions by many foreign Cities and Municipalities: Famagusta, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beirut" title="Beirut">Beirut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, Brooklyn N.Y., Bucharest, Caracas, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kiev" title="Kiev">Kiev</a>, Krotone, Ljubljana, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a> -<a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, New York, Nicosia, Paris, <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, Providence, Rhode Island, <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, Sofia, State of Illinois, <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tbilisi" title="Tbilisi">Tbilisi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tirana" title="Tirana">Tirana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valletta" title="Valletta">Valletta</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_D.C." title="Washington D.C.">Washington D.C.</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xian" title="Xian">Xian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a>. He has been honoured with the Honorary Freedom of 40 Greek and foreign cities.</p>
