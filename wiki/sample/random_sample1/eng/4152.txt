<p><b>Dimitrios "Dimitris" Papadopoulos</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el">Δημήτρης Παπαδόπουλος</span>; born 20 October 1981, in <a href="/wiki/Gagarin,_Uzbekistan" title="Gagarin, Uzbekistan">Gagarin, Uzbekistan</a>) is a Greek professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Atromitos_F.C." title="Atromitos F.C.">Atromitos</a>. He is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Greece_national_football_team" title="Greece national football team">Greek national team</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Akratitos_FC">Akratitos FC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Akratitos FC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He started his career from the youth academies of Greek minnows <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Akratitos_FC" title="Akratitos FC">Akratitos</a>, and made his debut in 1999, contributing to the team's promotion to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alpha_Ethniki" title="Alpha Ethniki">Alfa Ethniki</a> in 2001 for the first time in its history.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Burnley">Burnley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Burnley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shortly after, Papadopoulos was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> in England, for a transfer fee reported to be €500,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yorkshire_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-yorkshire-1">[1]</a></sup> He stayed at the club for two seasons. In July 2003, Burnley sold Papadopoulos to <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_F.C." title="Panathinaikos F.C.">Panathinaikos</a> for a reported €200,000 including a sell-on clause in which Burnley could profit from future transfers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yorkshire_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-yorkshire-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Panathinaikos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Papadopoulos starred in his very first season with <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_F.C." title="Panathinaikos F.C.">Panathinaikos</a> becoming its top scorer with 17 goals in 26 games and winning the double with the club. It was the first championship in almost 10 years of <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C." title="Olympiacos F.C.">Olympiacos</a> domination in the Greek championship.</p>
<p>He was subsequently voted best footballer of that year in the league and was awarded a place in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_2004" title="Euro 2004">Euro 2004</a> Greek squad that went on to win the tournament. He played in the game against <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a> and had a contribution in the Greece goal that determined its qualification to the next round. He also took part in the Greek Olympic Football team during the Olympic Games of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athens_2004" title="Athens 2004">Athens 2004</a> in the very same summer.</p>
<p>An integral part of the Panathinaikos offence, he signed a three-year extension with the team in the summer of 2006 and his stellar performances were one of the reasons that the team's board let fellow starter <a href="/wiki/Michalis_Konstantinou" title="Michalis Konstantinou">Michalis Konstantinou</a> leave the team.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lecce">Lecce</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lecce">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 13 November 2008 <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/U.S._Lecce" title="U.S. Lecce">Lecce</a> announced to have signed Papadopoulos in a free transfer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He made his Italian Serie A debut for Lecce on 18 January 2009 in a home match at the <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Via_del_Mare" title="Stadio Via del Mare">Stadio Via del Mare</a> against <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa C.F.C.</a> coming on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Munari" title="Gianni Munari">Gianni Munari</a> late in the match. He scored his first goal in the Serie A on 19 April against <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">A.S. Roma</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Olimpico" title="Stadio Olimpico">Stadio Olimpico</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dinamo_Zagreb">Dinamo Zagreb</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Dinamo Zagreb">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 26 June 2009, he signed a three-year contract worth €650.000 annually with the Croatian champions <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dinamo_Zagreb" title="Dinamo Zagreb">Dinamo Zagreb</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He contributed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dinamo_Zagreb" title="Dinamo Zagreb">Dinamo Zagreb</a>, scoring goals to go forward in the Croatian cup and to win the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prva_HNL" title="Prva HNL">Croatian League</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Celta_Vigo">Celta Vigo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Celta Vigo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 January 2010, Papadopoulos signed a contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celta_Vigo" title="Celta Vigo">Celta Vigo</a> until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Things looked good for him but in late April, he got injured and was unable to play quite regularly. During the next summer, Celta Vigo signed striker David Rodríguez and winger De Lucas in order to strengthen the squad. This relegated Papadopoulos to the position of third striker of the team and therefore he was not included in many of the team's lineups during that season. At the beginning of the 2010/2011 season, Celta Vigo's manager Paco Herrera claimed that he would not count with Papadopoulos any more,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> putting pressure on him to leave the club due to his high salary and his poor performances. Although he played more than 20 matches, he failed to score more than a single goal. His contract was due to expire in 2013.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Levadiakos">Levadiakos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Levadiakos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2012, Papadopoulos moved on loan to <a href="/wiki/Levadiakos_F.C." title="Levadiakos F.C.">Levadiakos</a> of the Greek Super League,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> but he failed to score a goal although the team finished in 7th place.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Panthrakikos">Panthrakikos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Panthrakikos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the summer of 2012 he moved to <a href="/wiki/Panthrakikos_F.C." title="Panthrakikos F.C.">Panthrakikos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He made his debut against his former team <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_F.C." title="Panathinaikos F.C.">Panathinaikos</a>, scoring the winning goal. On 8 October he scored a penalty in the 1-0 away victory against <a href="/wiki/Levadiakos_F.C." title="Levadiakos F.C.">Levadiakos</a>, another ex-team. On 11 November 2012, Papadopoulos scored his fourth goal of the season against <a href="/wiki/Asteras_Tripoli_F.C." title="Asteras Tripoli F.C.">Asteras Tripolis</a> giving the victory to Panthrakikos and he was elected Man of the Match. On 26 November 2012 he scored two goals against Aris Thessaloniki allowing Panthrakikos to win 4-0. Papadopoulos had not scored two goals in one match of the Greek league since 26 November 2006. On 15 December 2012 he scored the second goal of the 2-0 away win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kerkyra_F.C." title="Kerkyra F.C.">Kerkyra</a>. On 16 January 2013 he made his first appearance in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_Cup" title="Greek Cup">Greek Cup</a> after several years against <a href="/wiki/Apollon_Smyrni_F.C." title="Apollon Smyrni F.C.">Apollon Smyrni</a> replacing <a href="/wiki/Leonidas_Kyvelidis" title="Leonidas Kyvelidis">Leonidas Kyvelidis</a> in the 78th minute. On 19 January 2013, he scored another goal against Panathinaikos at the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Stadium_(Athens)" title="Olympic Stadium (Athens)">Athens Olympic Stadium</a>, drawing the goal of <a href="/wiki/Toch%C3%A9_(footballer)" title="Toché (footballer)">Toché</a>. On 13 March 2013 he reached the semifinals of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_Cup" title="Greek Cup">Greek Cup</a> with Panthrakikos. On 7 April 2013 he scored a penalty leading to a 3-1 win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OFI_Crete" title="OFI Crete">OFI Crete</a>; with this goal Papadopoulos reached 10 goals in the Greek Super League.</p>
<p>Papadopoulos was named Best Player of the Super League. The player of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panthrakikos" title="Panthrakikos">Panthrakikos</a> stood out with his performances in the Greek Super League. He was the top goal-scorer of his team, scoring 11 times. In critical games, Papadopoulos excelled, helping his team to finish mid-table and avoid relegation comfortably. Panthrakikos also reached the semi-finals of the Greek Cup. PSAP spokesman Stamatis Sirigos added: <i>"With his character, leadership skills and experience, Dimitros Papadopoulos helped his team-mates and boosted their confidence. All this combined with his ethos was recognised with his first position in the vote of professional footballers in Greece".</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the other hand, Papadopoulos put several frustrating campaigns behind him : <i>"I always believed I could get my career back on track,"</i> he said at Monday's award ceremony in Athens. <i>"I was given that chance at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panthrakikos" title="Panthrakikos">Panthrakikos</a>, where we fought a tough war in order to secure our place in the league."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atromitos">Atromitos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Atromitos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a great season, in the summer of 2013 he signed a two-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Atromitos_F.C." title="Atromitos F.C.">Atromitos</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> a team that got 4th place in the previous year of <a href="/wiki/Superleague_Greece" title="Superleague Greece">Greek Superleague</a>. He scored his first goal with the club on 17 August 2013, in a 2-2 home draw against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ergotelis" title="Ergotelis">Ergotelis</a>.</p>
<p>On 30 August 2013 in a dramatic game against <a href="/wiki/AZ_Alkmaar" title="AZ Alkmaar">AZ Alkmaar</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Europa_League" title="Europa League">Europa League</a>, the latter beat Atromitos after fire caused a delay in playing the remaining 33 minutes. Spectators were evacuated after the match was halted in the 57th minute, with Atromitos leading 1-0, but trailing 3-2 on aggregate, through a goal from Dimitris Papadopoulos before play was stopped. When the action resumed next day, <a href="/wiki/Thanasis_Karagounis" title="Thanasis Karagounis">Thanasis Karagounis</a> made it 2-0 in the second leg but the Greek team could not find a winning goal as the tie ended 3-3 on aggregate, with the Dutch side advancing into the group phase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On a 0-0 cup game against the Greek giant <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C." title="Olympiacos F.C.">Olympiakos</a> after an excellent appearance he said in front of TV : <i>"We have a good team confronting any opponent. Our team has experienced players who know what to do in such games. In the first half Olympiacos pushing us so we could not keep the ball, and be aggressive. In the second half we got better and by entering Karamanos the whole match changed. Against Olympiakos we tried to have a good run defense, as our mail target was not to nil in the first match and see what happens in the second".</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of the 2013/14 regular season he scored 14 league goals being the top scorer of his club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On 27 May 2014 <a href="/wiki/Atromitos_F.C." title="Atromitos F.C.">Atromitos</a> announced the end of cooperation with the striker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PAOK">PAOK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: PAOK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 August 2014, Papadopoulos, although, he was linked with <a href="/wiki/Veria_F.C." title="Veria F.C.">Veria</a> as they had an oral agreement, signed a contract with <a href="/wiki/Superleague_Greece" title="Superleague Greece">Superleague Greece</a> team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAOK_F.C." title="PAOK F.C.">PAOK</a> for one year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On 18 September, he made his debut with the shirt of PAOK in the Europa League by scoring the fifth goal in the 6-1 victory of PAOK against <a href="/wiki/FC_Dinamo_Minsk" title="FC Dinamo Minsk">Dinamo Minsk</a>. The 22 September debut with the jersey of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAOK_F.C." title="PAOK F.C.">PAOK</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Superleague_Greece" title="Superleague Greece">Greek Superleague</a>, against Panaitolikos. November 30 marked his first goal with his New Jersey scoring the 2-2 against <a href="/wiki/Panthrakikos_F.C." title="Panthrakikos F.C.">Panthrakikos</a>, his former team contributing to the victory of Paok 3-2. hyenas Greek voted Footballer of the Year in 2014 and 4 February 2015, scored his second goal against OFI Crete coached <a href="/wiki/Nikos_Anastopoulos" title="Nikos Anastopoulos">Nikos Anastopoulos</a>, just replaced <a href="/wiki/Gennaro_Gattuso" title="Gennaro Gattuso">Gennaro Gattuso</a>. On 18 of March scored the winning goal against Ergotelis 1-0. While March 21 marks his fourth goal against Panathinaikos away. April 5 marked his two goals for his team's victory 2-1 against Xanthi. On 3 May 2015, he scored another goal contributing to the home victory of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAOK_F.C." title="PAOK F.C.">PAOK</a> for 1-0 against <a href="/wiki/Levadiakos_F.C." title="Levadiakos F.C.">Levadiakos</a>, his former team. Papadopoulos, after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAOK" title="PAOK">PAOK</a> was close to make a sensational move to <a href="/wiki/Kar%C5%9F%C4%B1yaka_S.K." title="Karşıyaka S.K.">Karşıyaka</a> after agreed to sign a one-year deal,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> but the deal was not fulfilled due to financial reasons. On 28 August 2015, Papadopoulos turned down an offer of a year contract from <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> club <a href="/wiki/AEL_Kalloni_F.C." title="AEL Kalloni F.C.">Kalloni</a>, waiting for a better offer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asteras_Tripoli">Asteras Tripoli</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Asteras Tripoli">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 8 September 2015, Papadopoulos signed a year contract with <a href="/wiki/Superleague_Greece" title="Superleague Greece">Superleague Greece</a> team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asteras_Tripoli" title="Asteras Tripoli">Asteras Tripoli</a> for an undisclosed fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He made his debut in <a href="/wiki/Superleague_Greece" title="Superleague Greece">Superleague Greece</a> with his new team against <a href="/wiki/AEL_Kalloni_F.C." title="AEL Kalloni F.C.">Kalloni</a>. On 18 October 2015, in his first match in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2015-16_Superleague_Greece" title="2015-16 Superleague Greece">2015-16 season</a> as a member of the starting XI, he missed a penalty in a 1-1 home draw against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skoda_Xanthi" title="Skoda Xanthi">Skoda Xanthi</a> as the ball stopped at the intersection of the beams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On 8 November he scored his first goal with the new team giving the victory away against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AEK_Athens" title="AEK Athens">AEK Athens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 20 January 2016, he solved in a mutual consent his contract with the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_spell_to_Atromitos">Second spell to Atromitos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Second spell to Atromitos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 January 2016, Papadopoulos signed a contract with his former team, <a href="/wiki/Atromitos_F.C." title="Atromitos F.C.">Atromitos</a> until the summer of 2017, for an undisclosed fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> He made his debut in <a href="/wiki/Superleague_Greece" title="Superleague Greece">Superleague Greece</a> the 24 January against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAS_Giannina" title="PAS Giannina">PAS Giannina</a>. On 10 February 2016 he reached the semifinals of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_Cup" title="Greek Cup">Greek Cup</a>, against his ex-club <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_F.C." title="Panathinaikos F.C.">Panathinaikos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On 28 November 2016, he scored his first goal with the club, in a triumphal 4-3 away win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAOK" title="PAOK">PAOK</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team">National team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dimitris_Papadopoulos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: National team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dimitrios Papadopoulos made his debut in the Greek National Team on 20 November 2002 against Republic of Ireland and he was included in the squad of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_2004" title="Euro 2004">Euro 2004</a> that went on to win the tournament. His pass for the goal of <a href="/wiki/Zisis_Vryzas" title="Zisis Vryzas">Zisis Vryzas</a> allowed Greece to qualify from the group stage in expense of Russia and Spain and then become Champions of Euro 2004. He also participated in all three games of <a href="/wiki/Greece_national_under-23_football_team" title="Greece national under-23 football team">Greece Olympic</a> football team in the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a> scoring 1 goal. As Euro 2004 winner, Dimitrios Papadopoulos was included in the Greek squad for the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2005 FIFA Confederations Cup">2005 FIFA Confederations Cup</a> in Germany. <a href="/wiki/Otto_Rehhagel" title="Otto Rehhagel">Otto Rehhagel</a> also included him in the squad during <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying">qualification</a> but he did not make it to the final selection for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a> in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>.</p>
<p>On 29 August 2013, the coach of the Greek National team, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Santos_(Portuguese_footballer)" title="Fernando Santos (Portuguese footballer)">Fernando Santos</a> announced the return of Dimitrios Papadopoulos after six years <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> for the match against <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein_national_football_team" title="Liechtenstein national football team">Liechtenstein</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vaduz" title="Vaduz">Vaduz</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Rheinpark_Stadion" title="Rheinpark Stadion">Rheinpark Stadion</a> stadium for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification">2014 FIFA World Cup qualification</a>. He came in in the 84th minute replacing <a href="/wiki/Lazaros_Christodoulopoulos" title="Lazaros Christodoulopoulos">Lazaros Christodoulopoulos</a>.</p>
