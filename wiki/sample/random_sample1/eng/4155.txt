<p><i><b>WWE Diva Search</b></i> (formerly <i>WWE Raw Diva Search</i>) is a talent competition held by <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a>. The first winner was <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Koeppe" title="Jaime Koeppe">Jaime Koeppe</a>, but she did not receive a contract like subsequent winners of the contest. Other winners included <a href="/wiki/Christy_Hemme" title="Christy Hemme">Christy Hemme</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Massaro" title="Ashley Massaro">Ashley Massaro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Layla_El" title="Layla El">Layla El</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eve_Torres" title="Eve Torres">Eve Torres</a>. In addition to the winners of the yearly contest, WWE has hired several contestants as <a href="/wiki/WWE_Diva" title="WWE Diva">Divas</a>. In 2011, WWE announced plans to bring back the Diva Search.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> As of 2016, <a href="/wiki/Rosa_Mendes" title="Rosa Mendes">Milena Roucka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryse_Ouellet" title="Maryse Ouellet">Maryse Ouellet</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nikki_Bella" title="Nikki Bella">Nikki Bella</a>, from the 2006 Raw Diva Search, are the last remaining Divas to come from the Diva Search. It was rumored that the show would return in 2015, but that plan has been ultimately scrapped.</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Candice_Michelle_No_Mercy_2007.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1986" data-file-width="1125" height="265" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Candice_Michelle_No_Mercy_2007.jpg/150px-Candice_Michelle_No_Mercy_2007.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Candice_Michelle_No_Mercy_2007.jpg/225px-Candice_Michelle_No_Mercy_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Candice_Michelle_No_Mercy_2007.jpg/300px-Candice_Michelle_No_Mercy_2007.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>

<a href="/wiki/Michelle_McCool" title="Michelle McCool">Michelle McCool</a> was the first Diva Search contestant to become the inaugural <a href="/wiki/WWE_Divas_Championship" title="WWE Divas Championship">Divas Champion</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 2004, WWE decided to make an extension of the Diva Search, which included a one-year contract worth $250,000 and was held on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wire_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-wire-8">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corporate_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-corporate-11">[8]</a></sup> Over 7,000 females applied to be a part of the contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-angel_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-angel-5">[2]</a></sup> The final ten contestants were chosen on a casting special aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spike_(TV_channel)" title="Spike (TV channel)">Spike TV</a> on July 15, with guest judges <a href="/wiki/Triple_H" title="Triple H">Triple H</a>, <a href="/wiki/Randy_Orton" title="Randy Orton">Randy Orton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Jericho" title="Chris Jericho">Chris Jericho</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adam_Copeland" title="Adam Copeland">Edge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corporate_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-corporate-11">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>They began airing the weekly Raw Diva Search segments on July 19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wire_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-wire-8">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corporate_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-corporate-11">[8]</a></sup> Despite reported claims by the company that the contest would be handled in a classy manner, the contest involved prospective divas performing suggestive acts on live <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a>, such as "seducing" a male wrestler. Fans were allowed to vote for the winners,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wire_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-wire-8">[5]</a></sup> but the outcome of the voting process was questioned by media outlets. The eventual winner of the competition was Christy Hemme, who went on to pose in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Playboy_(magazine)" title="Playboy (magazine)">Playboy</a></i> and competed at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_21" title="WrestleMania 21">WrestleMania 21</a> for the WWE Women's Championship where she was accompanied to the ring by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amy_Dumas" title="Amy Dumas">Lita</a>, but ended up with <a href="/wiki/Trish_Stratus" title="Trish Stratus">Trish Stratus</a> retaining the title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nude_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-nude-6">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Finalists (winner Hemme, runner-up Carmella DeCesare, Amy Weber, and Joy Giovanni)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sun_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-sun-9">[6]</a></sup> took part in a segment titled "Diss the Diva" on August 30, 2004,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[9]</a></sup> which was censored at several points due to foul language and obscenities from the contestants when they were asked to taunt one another verbally. Backstage, the four contestants bickered with and disliked each other, as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sun_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-sun-9">[6]</a></sup> Also, during an earlier segment in the competition, Maria Kanellis gave DeCesare the <a href="/wiki/Middle_finger" title="Middle finger">middle finger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-philly_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-philly-10">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite only one winner being crowned in the competition each year, WWE hired a large number of the contestants from past contests and immediately placed them in on-camera in non-wrestling roles. From the 2004 Diva Search, Michelle McCool, Amy Weber,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sun_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-sun-9">[6]</a></sup> Candice Michelle,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corporate_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-corporate-11">[8]</a></sup> Maria Kanellis,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-philly_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-philly-10">[7]</a></sup> and Joy Giovanni all received contracts after losing the contest. Several of the girls were sent to <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Valley_Wrestling" title="Ohio Valley Wrestling">Ohio Valley Wrestling</a> to train.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sun_9-6"><a href="#cite_note-sun-9">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes_2">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Diva_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the 2005 WWE Raw Diva Search, the eight finalists began appearing on <i>Raw</i> on June 27. One contestant was eliminated each week until there was a final three.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corporate2005_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-corporate2005-15">[10]</a></sup> Contestant Leyla Milani accidentally exposed herself by falling out of her top while attempting to navigate an obstacle course on the July 4 edition of <i>Raw</i>, leading to a "disqualification" in the contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ashley Massaro was declared the winner on August 15 and received $250,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corporate2005_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-corporate2005-15">[10]</a></sup> Massaro wanted to train to wrestle, however, due to her family's involvement in <a href="/wiki/Amateur_wrestling" title="Amateur wrestling">amateur wrestling</a>. As in 2004, other contestants were hired by WWE, including Kristal Marshall, Trenesha Biggers (eliminated in the Top 25) and Elisabeth Rouffaer.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes_3">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Diva_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The eight finalists made their first appearance on WWE programming on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i> on July 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corporate2006_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-corporate2006-20">[13]</a></sup> The Diva Search had an awkward beginning on July 10 with an introductory segment on <i>Raw</i> featuring new host <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Mizanin" title="Mike Mizanin">Mike Mizanin</a>. During the segment, Mizanin appeared to have forgotten his lines and stammered on for several minutes when trying to explain the voting procedures for the contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miz_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-miz-21">[14]</a></sup> When introducing the contestants, Mizanin mispronounced names and looked uncomfortable in his new role as Diva Search emcee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miz_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-miz-21">[14]</a></sup> Each week, the contestants appeared on both <i>Raw</i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_Friday_Night_SmackDown" title="WWE Friday Night SmackDown">SmackDown!</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corporate2006_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-corporate2006-20">[13]</a></sup> The final three appeared on a special that aired on August 16, 2006 on the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a>, where the winner was announced.</p>
<p>Layla El won the 3rd annual Raw Diva Search<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fhm_19-8"><a href="#cite_note-fhm-19">[12]</a></sup> and was placed on <i>SmackDown!</i> in late 2006. WWE also hired several of the eliminated Diva contestants including Maryse Ouellet, <a href="/wiki/The_Bella_Twins" title="The Bella Twins">The Garcia Twins</a>, Rebecca DiPietro, <a href="/wiki/Brooke_Tessmacher" title="Brooke Tessmacher">Brooke Adams</a>, Amy Zidian, and Milena Roucka.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes_4">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Diva_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first competition was on WWE.com on September 10, which was also when the voting started. The winner was announced on the October 29 edition of <i>Raw</i>. Unlike past Diva Searches, this Search was the first to take place exclusively on WWE.com with most of the pre-taped segments TV. Tarryn Terrell, <a href="/wiki/Angela_Fong" title="Angela Fong">Angela Fong</a> and Lena Yada were called up to the roster despite being eliminated.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2008">2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Diva_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2008 edition was supposed to take place that year, but it was cancelled due to the failure of the 2007 edition.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Diva_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wrestling_Observer_Newsletter" title="Wrestling Observer Newsletter">Wrestling Observer Newsletter</a> reported in February 2013 that WWE held an un-aired diva search in the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21_February_2013_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-21_February_2013-30">[22]</a></sup> Among the prospects that participated were future WWE performers <a href="/wiki/Eva_Marie" title="Eva Marie">Eva Marie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lana_(wrestling)" title="Lana (wrestling)">Lana</a>, <a href="/wiki/JoJo_Offerman" title="JoJo Offerman">JoJo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Devin_Taylor_(wrestler)" title="Devin Taylor (wrestler)">Devin Taylor</a>, and Veronica Lane, with the former winning the contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FoxNewsMag07142015_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-FoxNewsMag07142015-32">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.2F16">2015/16</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Diva_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: 2015/16">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_31" title="WrestleMania 31">WrestleMania 31</a>, WWE announced that new shows will air on the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Network" title="WWE Network">WWE Network</a>, including a returning <i>WWE Diva Search</i>, which was expected to premiere in the fall of 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[25]</a></sup> There was no additional information given until July 2016, when WWE surveyed fans about potential new WWE Network shows, including a new season of Diva Search under a new name (<i>WWE Superstar Search</i>) due to the fact that the term "Diva" was ultimately phased out that year. In addition, the show would've been hosted by <a href="/wiki/Lita_(wrestler)" title="Lita (wrestler)">Lita</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trish_Stratus" title="Trish Stratus">Trish Stratus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[26]</a></sup></p>
