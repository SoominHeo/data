<p><b>Dibër County</b> is a county in North Albania. The Dibër County borders on the <a href="/wiki/Lezh%C3%AB_County" title="Lezhë County">Lezhë County</a> in the northwest, <a href="/wiki/Kuk%C3%ABs_County" title="Kukës County">Kukës County</a> in the northeast, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Republic of Macedonia</a> in the east, <a href="/wiki/Elbasan_County" title="Elbasan County">Elbasan County</a> in the southeast, <a href="/wiki/Tirana_County" title="Tirana County">Tirana County</a> in southwest and <a href="/wiki/Durr%C3%ABs_County" title="Durrës County">Durrës County</a> in the west.</p>
<p>The capital of Dibër County is <a href="/wiki/Peshkopi" title="Peshkopi">Peshkopi</a>. It is the only county capital in Albania that does not share its name with its county. Peshkopi lies east of the <a href="/wiki/Black_Drin" title="Black Drin">Black Drin</a> river. The Drin valley is the lowest part of the district. Mineral ores such as chromium, sulphur, and marble have been discovered in the district. It is also an important industrial center in Albania, particularly in relation to the food industry.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mat_(river)" title="Mat (river)">Mat</a> is one of the biggest rivers in northern Albania. Its source is near <a href="/wiki/Martanesh" title="Martanesh">Martanesh</a>, in Dibër County. The territory is largely composed of mountains and hills, which has only been opened up by National Road 6, which links the coastal town of <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Milot" title="Milot">Milot</a> with <a href="/wiki/Peshkopi" title="Peshkopi">Peshkopi</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dib%C3%ABr_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Albania" title="Geography of Albania">Geography of Albania</a></div>
<p>The region includes a part of the historical region of <b>Dibra</b>, the other part of which is now in <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" title="Republic of Macedonia">Macedonia</a>. The whole area is mountainous and several mountain ranges run in a north-south direction. The most known mountains include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mount_Korab" title="Mount Korab">Mount Korab</a>, the highest mountain in the country, <i>Mali i Dejës</i> and <i>Lura Mountains</i>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lur%C3%AB_National_Park" title="Lurë National Park">Lurë National Park</a> encompasses 1,280 hectares on the eastern side of the <a href="/wiki/Kunora_e_Lur%C3%ABs" title="Kunora e Lurës">Kunora e Lurës</a>, the highest summit in the Lurë Mountains, which reaches a height of 2,119 m (6,952 ft) above sea level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The park is renowned for its <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lur%C3%AB_Lakes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lurë Lakes (page does not exist)">12 lakes</a> and wildlife that attract visitors year-round. The southern part of the park has a meadow of multi-color flowers and coniferous trees called the Field of Mares which offers scenic views. The park offers variety of possibilities for eco-tourism, winter sports, equitation, horse riding, and other activities.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Dib%C3%ABr_valley" title="Dibër valley">Dibër valley</a> is a section of the Drin valley in the border region between Macedonia and Albania. The Dibër valley is fertile. Wolves and bears are still found in the large forests of the valley. A large portion of the inhabitants work in agriculture.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mat" title="Mat">Mat</a> river is the most important waterway in the region along with <a href="/wiki/Drin_(river)" title="Drin (river)">Drin</a>. There are also the reservoirs of Shkopet and Ulza in northwestern end of the area. The region, and especially the central part, is rich in chromium, which plays an important role in the economy of the country.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dib%C3%ABr_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Until 2000, Dibër County was subdivided into three districts: <a href="/wiki/Bulqiz%C3%AB_District" title="Bulqizë District">Bulqizë</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dib%C3%ABr_District" title="Dibër District">Dibër</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mat_District" title="Mat District">Mat</a>. Since the 2015 local government reform, the county consists of the following 4 municipalities: <a href="/wiki/Bulqiz%C3%AB" title="Bulqizë">Bulqizë</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dib%C3%ABr,_Albania" title="Dibër, Albania">Dibër</a>, <a href="/wiki/Klos,_Dib%C3%ABr" title="Klos, Dibër">Klos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mat,_Albania" title="Mat, Albania">Mat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Before 2015, it consisted of the following 35 municipalities:</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dib%C3%ABr_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:227px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kulle_ne_fshatin_e_Kalase_se_Dodes.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3168" data-file-width="4752" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Kulle_ne_fshatin_e_Kalase_se_Dodes.JPG/225px-Kulle_ne_fshatin_e_Kalase_se_Dodes.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Kulle_ne_fshatin_e_Kalase_se_Dodes.JPG/338px-Kulle_ne_fshatin_e_Kalase_se_Dodes.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Kulle_ne_fshatin_e_Kalase_se_Dodes.JPG/450px-Kulle_ne_fshatin_e_Kalase_se_Dodes.JPG 2x" width="225"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kulle_ne_fshatin_e_Kalase_se_Dodes.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Typical architecture in northeast Albania</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>According to the last national census from 2011 this county has 137,047 inhabitants. The population is <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> and has rich traditions. They speak <a href="/wiki/Gheg_Albanian" title="Gheg Albanian">Gheg</a> dialect.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dib%C3%ABr_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The region is an important producer of <a href="/wiki/Chrome_plating" title="Chrome plating">chrome</a>. Other industries include power generation and light industry. The region produces a large variety of agricultural products especially dairy products, nuts and fruits.</p>
