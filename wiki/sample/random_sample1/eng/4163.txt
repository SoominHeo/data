<p>A <b>display device</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Output_device" title="Output device">output device</a> for presentation of <a href="/wiki/Information" title="Information">information</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Visual" title="Visual">visual</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-University_of_West_Florida_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-University_of_West_Florida-1">[1]</a></sup> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tactile" title="Tactile">tactile</a> form (the latter used for example in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Refreshable_Braille_display" title="Refreshable Braille display">tactile electronic displays</a> for blind people).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Office_of_the_Chief_information_Officer_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Office_of_the_Chief_information_Officer-2">[2]</a></sup> When the input information is supplied has an electrical signal, the display is called an <i>electronic display</i>.</p>
<p>Common applications for <i><a href="/wiki/Electronic_visual_display" title="Electronic visual display">electronic visual displays</a></i> are <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">televisions</a> or <a href="/wiki/Computer_monitor" title="Computer monitor">computer monitors</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Segment_displays">Segment displays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Display_device&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Segment displays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Digital_clock_changing_numbers.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="900" data-file-width="2560" height="105" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Digital_clock_changing_numbers.jpg/300px-Digital_clock_changing_numbers.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Digital_clock_changing_numbers.jpg/450px-Digital_clock_changing_numbers.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Digital_clock_changing_numbers.jpg/600px-Digital_clock_changing_numbers.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Digital_clock_changing_numbers.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Digital clocks display changing numbers.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Some displays can show only <a href="/wiki/Numerical_digit" title="Numerical digit">digits</a> or <a href="/wiki/Alphanumeric" title="Alphanumeric">alphanumeric</a> characters. They are called <b>segment displays</b>, because they are composed of several segments that switch on and off to give appearance of desired <a href="/wiki/Glyph" title="Glyph">glyph</a>. The segments are usually single <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LED" title="LED">LEDs</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LCD" title="LCD">liquid crystals</a>. They are mostly used in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_watch" title="Digital watch">digital watches</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pocket_calculator" title="Pocket calculator">pocket calculators</a>. There are several types:</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Common_segment_displays.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="202" data-file-width="659" height="67" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Common_segment_displays.svg/220px-Common_segment_displays.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Common_segment_displays.svg/330px-Common_segment_displays.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Common_segment_displays.svg/440px-Common_segment_displays.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Common_segment_displays.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The common segment displays shown side by side: <a href="/wiki/Seven-segment_display" title="Seven-segment display">7-segment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nine-segment_display" title="Nine-segment display">9-segment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fourteen-segment_display" title="Fourteen-segment display">14-segment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sixteen-segment_display" title="Sixteen-segment display">16-segment</a> displays.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Seven-segment_display" title="Seven-segment display">Seven-segment display</a> (most common, digits only)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fourteen-segment_display" title="Fourteen-segment display">Fourteen-segment display</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sixteen-segment_display" title="Sixteen-segment display">Sixteen-segment display</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hitachi_HD44780_LCD_controller" title="Hitachi HD44780 LCD controller">HD44780 LCD controller</a> a widely accepted protocol for LCDs.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Full-area_2-dimensional_displays">Full-area 2-dimensional displays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Display_device&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Full-area 2-dimensional displays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2-dimensional" title="2-dimensional">2-dimensional</a> displays that cover a full area (usually a <a href="/wiki/Rectangle" title="Rectangle">rectangle</a>) are also called <b>video displays</b>, since it is the main modality of presenting <a href="/wiki/Video" title="Video">video</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Applications">Applications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Display_device&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Applications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Full-area 2-dimensional displays are used in, for example:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Television_set" title="Television set">Television sets</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Computer_monitor" title="Computer monitor">Computer monitors</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Head-mounted_display" title="Head-mounted display">Head-mounted display</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Broadcast_reference_monitor" title="Broadcast reference monitor">Broadcast reference monitor</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical_monitor" title="Medical monitor">Medical monitors</a></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Underlying_technologies_2">Underlying technologies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Display_device&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Underlying technologies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Underlying technologies for full-area 2-dimensional displays include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cathode_ray_tube" title="Cathode ray tube">Cathode ray tube</a> display (CRT)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light-emitting_diode_display" title="Light-emitting diode display">Light-emitting diode display</a> (LED)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electroluminescent_display" title="Electroluminescent display">Electroluminescent display</a> (ELD)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Electronic_paper" title="Electronic paper">Electronic paper</a>, <a href="/wiki/E_Ink" title="E Ink">E Ink</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Plasma_display" title="Plasma display">Plasma display</a> panel (PDP)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liquid_crystal_display" title="Liquid crystal display">Liquid crystal display</a> (LCD)
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/High-Performance_Addressing" title="High-Performance Addressing">High-Performance Addressing</a> display (HPA)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thin-film_transistor" title="Thin-film transistor">Thin-film transistor</a> display (TFT)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organic_light-emitting_diode" title="Organic light-emitting diode">Organic light-emitting diode</a> display (OLED)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Surface-conduction_electron-emitter_display" title="Surface-conduction electron-emitter display">Surface-conduction electron-emitter display</a> (SED) (experimental)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Field_emission_display" title="Field emission display">Field emission display</a> (FED) (experimental)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laser_TV" title="Laser TV">Laser TV</a> (forthcoming)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Carbon_nanotube" title="Carbon nanotube">Carbon nanotubes</a> (experimental)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Quantum_dot_display" title="Quantum dot display">Quantum dot display</a> (experimental)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Interferometric_modulator_display" title="Interferometric modulator display">Interferometric modulator display</a> (IMOD)</li>
<li>Digital microshutter display (DMS)</li>
</ul>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Multiplexed_display" title="Multiplexed display">multiplexed display</a> technique is used to drive most display devices.</p>
