<p><b>Diafra Sakho</b> (born 24 December 1989) is a Senegalese professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Striker_(association_football)" title="Striker (association football)">striker</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a>.</p>
<p>He began his career in the lower divisions of French football, with <a href="/wiki/FC_Metz" title="FC Metz">Metz</a> and on loan at <a href="/wiki/US_Boulogne" title="US Boulogne">Boulogne</a>, before joining West Ham in August 2014. Since May 2014, he has been a full international for the <a href="/wiki/Senegal_national_football_team" title="Senegal national football team">Senegal national team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diafra_Sakho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Metz">Metz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diafra_Sakho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Metz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sakho was born in <a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%A9diawaye" title="Guédiawaye">Guédiawaye</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugman_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hugman-1">[1]</a></sup> He started is career in <a href="/wiki/Dakar" title="Dakar">Dakar</a> with Génération Foot, a football academy and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feeder_club" title="Feeder club">feeder club</a> for <a href="/wiki/FC_Metz" title="FC Metz">FC Metz</a>, at age 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shoot_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-shoot-3">[3]</a></sup> After six months with the club he moved to France in 2007 to start training with Metz. After 17 goals in 22 games for FC Metz's reserve team in the 2009–10 season he was awarded a first-team place. He made his first-team debut in a goalless draw at home to <a href="/wiki/Stade_Brestois_29" title="Stade Brestois 29">Brest</a>, on 19 January 2010. His debut goal came on 10 September, in a 1–1 home draw with <a href="/wiki/FC_Nantes" title="FC Nantes">Nantes</a>. He was mainly used as a substitute in his first three seasons and was loaned to <a href="/wiki/US_Boulogne" title="US Boulogne">Boulogne</a> in January 2012. In May 2012, with Metz in <a href="/wiki/Championnat_National" title="Championnat National">Championnat National</a>, the third tier of French football, new manager <a href="/wiki/Albert_Cartier" title="Albert Cartier">Albert Cartier</a> gave Sakho a starting place in the team. The following season Sakho scored 19 goals in 33 league appearances as Metz finished in second place and won promotion back to <a href="/wiki/Ligue_2" title="Ligue 2">Ligue 2</a> at their first attempt. In season 2013–14 he scored 20 goals in 36 appearances as Metz won the Ligue 2 title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WHU_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-WHU-4">[4]</a></sup> For his performances, Sakho was voted Ligue 2 Player of the Year for the 2013–14 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WHU_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-WHU-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="West_Ham_United">West Ham United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diafra_Sakho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: West Ham United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 14 August 2014, Sakho signed a four-year contract for an undisclosed fee with <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a>, becoming their seventh signing before the start of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_West_Ham_United_F.C._season" title="2014–15 West Ham United F.C. season">2014–15 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WHU_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-WHU-4">[4]</a></sup> Upon his joining, he said: "This is a proud day to have signed for West Ham, I really love English football and I've dreamed of coming to play for one of the big English clubs. Today, West Ham have given me that opportunity and I didn't hesitate to grab it".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WHU_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-WHU-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>Sakho made his West Ham debut on 23 August 2014, in a 3–1 away win against <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a>, coming on as a 63rd-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Cole" title="Carlton Cole">Carlton Cole</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 26 August, he scored his first competitive goal for the club against <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a> in the second round of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Sakho's first League goal came on 15 September in a 2–2 away draw against <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>. Initially credited as an own goal by Hull defender <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Davies" title="Curtis Davies">Curtis Davies</a>, the goal was later awarded to Sakho.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He made his home debut against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> on 20 September, and scored his second goal with a chip over goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Simon_Mignolet" title="Simon Mignolet">Simon Mignolet</a> as the Hammers won 3–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 29 September Sakho scored in a 2–1 away defeat by <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>. This was his fourth goal in his first four starts for the club, breaking a 26-year-old record previously held by <a href="/wiki/Leroy_Rosenior" title="Leroy Rosenior">Leroy Rosenior</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 25 October 2014 Sakho scored West Ham's second goal in a 2–1 home win against reigning champions, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>. In doing so he became the first West Ham player to score in six straight Premier League games and matched <a href="/wiki/Micky_Quinn" title="Micky Quinn">Micky Quinn</a>'s Premier League record, set in 1992, of scoring in his first six starts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> For his performances, Sakho was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League_Player_of_the_Month" title="Premier League Player of the Month">Premier League Player of the Month</a> for <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Premier_League#Monthly_awards" title="2014–15 Premier League">October 2014</a>, with his manager <a href="/wiki/Sam_Allardyce" title="Sam Allardyce">Sam Allardyce</a> named <a href="/wiki/Premier_League_Manager_of_the_Month" title="Premier League Manager of the Month">Manager of the Month</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-potm1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-potm1-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sakho was injured during a game on 1 January 2015 against <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> This resulted in him being withdrawn from the Senegal squad for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2015 Africa Cup of Nations">2015 Africa Cup of Nations</a>. Senegal officials announced that they would try to prevent him playing for West Ham during the duration of the competition in Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> With Senegal still involved in the competition, he returned to fitness with officials from the country asking him to fly to Equatorial Guinea so that his fitness could be assessed. Unable to fly to Africa he was taken from London to <a href="/wiki/Ashton_Gate_Stadium" title="Ashton Gate Stadium">Ashton Gate</a> by <a href="/wiki/Limousine" title="Limousine">limousine</a> and on 25 January 2015 he scored the only goal in West Ham's FA Cup win against <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On 31 January 2015, with Senegal reported to have reported the case to <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>, Sakho was withdrawn from West Ham's squad for an away game against Liverpool.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> West Ham were fined 100,000 <a href="/wiki/Swiss_franc" title="Swiss franc">Swiss francs</a> by FIFA for playing Sakho in the FA Cup during the Africa Cup of Nations, avoiding a maximum penalty of being expelled from the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Sakho finished as West Ham's top scorer in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014-15_West_Ham_United_F.C._season" title="2014-15 West Ham United F.C. season">2014–15</a> season despite finishing the end of the season out injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He scored 12 goals, 10 in the league and one in both the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.9316_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diafra_Sakho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

Sakho playing for West Ham United in 2015</div>

<p>Sakho opened the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_West_Ham_United_F.C._season" title="2015–16 West Ham United F.C. season">2015–16 season</a> by scoring the first two goals in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> first qualifying round first-leg game on 2 July, as West Ham won 3–0 at home against Andorran team <a href="/wiki/FC_Lusitanos" title="FC Lusitanos">Lusitanos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lusitans1_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-lusitans1-22">[22]</a></sup> In the return leg a week later, he was sent-off for violent conduct in the 15th minute after "raising his hands" to Lusitanos player <a href="/wiki/Mois%C3%A9s_San_Nicol%C3%A1s" title="Moisés San Nicolás">Moisés San Nicolás</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> His opening goal in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Premier_League" title="2015–16 Premier League">2015–16 Premier League</a> season came on 29 August 2015 in a 0–3 away win against Liverpool. Sakho scored West Ham's third goal in a game which was their first win at <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a> for 52 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> On 19 September, he scored his second league goal of the season against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> at the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Manchester_Stadium" title="City of Manchester Stadium">City of Manchester Stadium</a> in a 2–1 win against the hosts who had not previously conceded a goal that season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 November 2015, Sakho suffered a thigh injury against West Bromwich Albion which would rule him out for three months, joining <a href="/wiki/Dimitri_Payet" title="Dimitri Payet">Dimitri Payet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Enner_Valencia" title="Enner Valencia">Enner Valencia</a> among West Ham's attacking absentees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2016, West Ham rejected a bid of £11 million from <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a>. In July Sakho handed-in a transfer request to West Ham.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In August 2016, a proposed transfer to <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> collapsed after Sakho failed a medical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.9317_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diafra_Sakho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Sakho did not make an appearance for West Ham until November 2016 when he appeared against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Soccerway_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Soccerway-29">[29]</a></sup> In his second game back he scored in a 1-1 away draw with <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>, becoming the first West Ham striker to score in the 2016-17 season. However, he suffered a hamstring injury which was expected to keep him out for four weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-201617sakho_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-201617sakho-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diafra_Sakho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sakho made his international debut for <a href="/wiki/Senegal_national_football_team" title="Senegal national football team">Senegal</a> in a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso_national_football_team" title="Burkina Faso national football team">Burkina Faso</a>, on 21 May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WHU_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-WHU-4">[4]</a></sup> His debut goal came four days later, in a 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Kosovo_national_football_team" title="Kosovo national football team">Kosovo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WHU_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-WHU-4">[4]</a></sup> In November 2014 he was recalled to the Senegal squad by coach, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Giresse" title="Alain Giresse">Alain Giresse</a>, for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Africa_Cup_of_Nations_qualification" title="2015 Africa Cup of Nations qualification">2015 Africa Cup of Nations qualification</a> games against <a href="/wiki/Egypt_national_football_team" title="Egypt national football team">Egypt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Botswana_national_football_team" title="Botswana national football team">Botswana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Sakho missed the final tournament due to a back injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diafra_Sakho&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sakho was arrested on 6 August 2015 on suspicion of common assault, criminal damage and <a href="/wiki/Malicious_Communications_Act_1988" title="Malicious Communications Act 1988">malicious communication</a> towards his girlfriend. He was bailed until late September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[35]</a></sup> On 23 August he was again arrested, on suspicion of threatening to kill and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Witness_intimidation" title="Witness intimidation">witness intimidation</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[36]</a></sup> and was bailed without charge two days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bailed_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-bailed-38">[37]</a></sup> He denied all allegations against him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bailed_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-bailed-38">[37]</a></sup> In November 2015, police investigation cleared Sakho of any wrongdoing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[38]</a></sup></p>
