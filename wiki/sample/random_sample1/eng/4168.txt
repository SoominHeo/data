<p><b>Diego Antonio Caccia Torres</b> (born March 9, 1971 in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>), also known as <b>Diego Torres</b>, is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentine" title="Argentine">Argentine</a> two <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a>-nominated pop <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Musician" title="Musician">musician</a> and <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> (of films, theater and television). He is the son of the legendary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentine" title="Argentine">Argentine</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Lolita_Torres" title="Lolita Torres">Lolita Torres</a> (1930-2002).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_Torres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Diego Torres was born in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. He's the son of popular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentine" title="Argentine">argentine</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a> and actress <a href="/wiki/Lolita_Torres" title="Lolita Torres">Lolita Torres</a>. In 1989, Torres' music career began with a band called <i>"La Marca."</i> Later, he began his first steps on TV starring in the successful TV series <i>"<a href="/wiki/La_Banda_del_Golden_Rocket" title="La Banda del Golden Rocket">La Banda del Golden Rocket</a>"</i> which stayed on air for three years. He loves soccer. Torres has won 3 awards along with 17 nominations for the Latin Grammys.</p>
<p>In 1992 he launched his first album, <i>"Diego Torres,"</i> produced by Cachorro López. This album was rewarded 3 times platinum. Two years later, and a little distant from TV, he launched his second album called <i>"Tratar de estar mejor"</i> which received 5 platinum albums in Argentina and gold and platinum albums in other <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin American</a> countries. This album launched Torres to the international scene.</p>
<p>In 1996 the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> producer Celso Valli produced Torres' third album, <i>"Luna Nueva"</i>, and months later Diego started touring with his band all across Latin America, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. The album went on sale, soon reaching gold and quickly being rewarded with the platinum in Argentina.</p>
<p>In 1999 he recorded his fourth album called <i>"<a href="/wiki/Tal_Cual_Es" title="Tal Cual Es">Tal Cual Es</a>"</i>, produced by Cachorro López. This album showed Diego's talent and creativity to compose and mix different Latin sounds, such as flamenco guitars as well as various other tropical sounds.</p>
<p>His next album was named <i>"<a href="/wiki/Un_Mundo_Diferente" title="Un Mundo Diferente">Un Mundo Diferente</a>"</i> which was recorded in the second half of 2001 in Buenos Aires and <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>. This album presents a variety of styles and merges with several Latin rhythm styles in most songs, such as in his most successful song so far, "<a href="/wiki/Color_Esperanza" title="Color Esperanza">Color Esperanza</a>", which stayed at the top of the Argentine Singles/Airplay Chart for twelve consecutive weeks. The album received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nomination in 2003.</p>
<p>2004 brought a new challenge to Diego: the possibility of recording an <a href="/wiki/MTV_Unplugged" title="MTV Unplugged">MTV Unplugged</a>, which aired in the <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> networks of Brazil, Latin America, Spain and United States. The reconversion of the acoustics in some of his hits along with the presentation of his new themes were part of this show, which was also produced on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CD" title="CD">CD</a> and <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a>.</p>
<p>In 2006 he released his sixth studio album <i>"<a href="/wiki/Andando" title="Andando">Andando</a>"</i>, proceeded by the lead single "Abriendo Caminos" featuring Dominican singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Juan_Luis_Guerra" title="Juan Luis Guerra">Juan Luis Guerra</a>. In 2005 he received the Platinum <a href="/wiki/Konex_Award" title="Konex Award">Konex Award</a> as best Argentine male pop/ballad singer, shared with <a href="/wiki/Sandro_de_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Sandro de América">Sandro de América</a>.</p>
<p>In 2010 he released his seventh studio album <i>"<a href="/wiki/Distinto" title="Distinto">Distinto</a>"</i> featuring his first the number-one <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Latin_Songs" title="Hot Latin Songs">Hot Latin Song</a> single "<a href="/wiki/Guapa_(song)" title="Guapa (song)">Guapa</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
