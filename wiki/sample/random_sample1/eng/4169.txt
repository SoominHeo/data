<div class="hatnote" role="note">This name uses <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>: the first or paternal <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">family name</a> is <i>Tristán</i> and the second or maternal family name is <i>Herrera</i>.</div>
<p><b>Diego Tristán Herrera</b> (born 5 January 1976) is a Spanish retired professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>
<p>At his peak, he was considered amongst the best players in his position in Europe, displaying a vast array of skills: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dribble" title="Dribble">dribble</a>, shot accuracy, aerial ability and off-the-ball movements. He is best known for his <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_de_La_Coru%C3%B1a" title="Deportivo de La Coruña">Deportivo de La Coruña</a> spell, where he spent six years, often overshadowed by physical and personal problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PAIS_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-PAIS-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over the course of eight <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> seasons, Tristán amassed totals of 227 games and 95 goals. He appeared with the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spanish national team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 World Cup</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_Trist%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_Trist%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/La_Algaba" title="La Algaba">La Algaba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Seville" title="Province of Seville">Province of Seville</a>, Tristán came through the youth ranks of local <a href="/wiki/Real_Betis" title="Real Betis">Real Betis</a>, going on to finish his sporting formation with the <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca_B" title="RCD Mallorca B">B-side</a> of <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a>.</p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1998–99 Segunda División">one season</a> in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a> he made his <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> debut in <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_La_Liga" title="1999–2000 La Liga">1999–2000</a>, against <a href="/wiki/CD_Numancia" title="CD Numancia">CD Numancia</a> on 12 September 1999 (scoring in the process), and finished his first top flight campaign with 18 goals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Deportivo">Deportivo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_Trist%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Deportivo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After nearly moving to league powerhouse <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> during the 2000 summer, a deal barred by <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Sanz" title="Lorenzo Sanz">Lorenzo Sanz</a>'s exit from the club's presidency, Tristán joined <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_de_La_Coru%C3%B1a" title="Deportivo de La Coruña">Deportivo de La Coruña</a>, forming a dreaded attacking partnership (on occasions, as <i>Depor</i> mainly operated in a <a href="/wiki/Formation_(association_football)" title="Formation (association football)">4–5–1 formation</a>) with Dutchman <a href="/wiki/Roy_Makaay" title="Roy Makaay">Roy Makaay</a>.</p>
<p>As Makaay was first-choice during <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_La_Liga" title="2000–01 La Liga">2000–01</a>, Tristán threatened to quit Deportivo when he found himself in and out of the side, but eventually became an undisputed starter in <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_La_Liga" title="2001–02 La Liga">the following season</a> and responded, netting 21 league goals for the <a href="/wiki/Javier_Irureta" title="Javier Irureta">Javier Irureta</a>-led team, a competition best. He added six in <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2001–02 UEFA Champions League">the campaign</a>'s <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> and five in the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> this included a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> in a 5–0 home win against former club Mallorca, on 7 April 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Afterwards, Tristán injured his ankle on international duty, losing again the starting berth to Makaay who even won the <a href="/wiki/European_Golden_Shoe" title="European Golden Shoe">European Golden Boot</a> for his performances. The former could not adapt to his secondary role, but still scored 19 overall goals for the <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicians</a> during <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_La_Liga" title="2002–03 La Liga">that season</a>.</p>
<p>Even when Makaay left Deportivo in June 2003 for <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">FC Bayern Munich</a>, Tristán never regained his form and confidence: in the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_La_Liga" title="2003–04 La Liga">2003–04 campaign</a> he played just ten full matches and was taken off in 20. His 18 further appearances as <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> made him miss only eight out of 56 matches, but he only produced 13 goals (eight in the league, two in <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Copa_del_Rey" title="2003–04 Copa del Rey">the domestic cup</a> and three in <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2003–04 UEFA Champions League">the Champions League</a>, including one in a 3–8 away defeat to <a href="/wiki/AS_Monaco_FC" title="AS Monaco FC">AS Monaco FC</a> on 5 November 2003).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Journeyman">Journeyman</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_Trist%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Journeyman">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In total, Tristán scored 87 goals in the shirt of Deportivo in four seasons, leaving in July 2006 – he was released on 1 September along with teammate <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Scaloni" title="Lionel Scaloni">Lionel Scaloni</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> After being linked with several clubs in Spain and overseas, including <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a>, he agreed to rejoin Mallorca after a six-year hiatus,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> but was released on 31 January 2007 due to a lack of fitness, form and goals.</p>
<p>Tristán signed a one-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>'s <a href="/wiki/A.S._Livorno_Calcio" title="A.S. Livorno Calcio">A.S. Livorno Calcio</a> in July 2007, as the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italians</a> searched for a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>-bound <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_Lucarelli" title="Cristiano Lucarelli">Cristiano Lucarelli</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> who signed with <a href="/wiki/FC_Shakhtar_Donetsk" title="FC Shakhtar Donetsk">FC Shakhtar Donetsk</a>, but he ultimately failed to impress with the <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a> team, scoring only once during <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Serie_A" title="2007–08 Serie A">the campaign</a> and eventually dropping down to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>.</p>
<p>On 29 September 2008 it was confirmed Tristán was undergoing a trial at <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 14 October he signed a contract,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> making his debut on 8 December as a late substitute in the 0–2 home defeat against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club in a 2–1 home victory over <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> on 28 December, also coming from the bench.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="C.C3.A1diz">Cádiz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_Trist%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cádiz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 24 July 2009, Tristán joined second division club <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1diz_CF" title="Cádiz CF">Cádiz CF</a> after having been released by West Ham at the end of <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Premier_League" title="2008–09 Premier League">the season</a>, thus returning to his native <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a> after 14 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He regained some of his scoring form in <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2009–10 Segunda División">his first and only season</a>, but his team was relegated after finishing in 19th position; in the last league match, a 4–2 home win against Numancia on 19 June 2010 – with the striker grabbing a brace – proved insufficient.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diego_Trist%C3%A1n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 2 June 2001, courtesy of his stellar Deportivo performances, Tristán earned a debut for <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a>, scoring in a 4–1 home win against <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> in a <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2002 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">qualifier</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oviedo" title="Oviedo">Oviedo</a>. In the finals in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and Japan he appeared sparingly for the quarterfinalists after picking up an injury,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PAIS_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-PAIS-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and did not manage to find the net.</p>
<p>Tristán scored on his last <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">cap</a>, a 3–0 friendly win in <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> on 6 September 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
