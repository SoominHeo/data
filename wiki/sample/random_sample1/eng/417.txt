<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Pentax *ist D</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Pentax *ist D</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_SLR" title="Digital SLR">digital SLR</a> camera produced by <a href="/wiki/Pentax" title="Pentax">Pentax</a>, released in 2003. The *ist D produces a 6.1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megapixel" title="Megapixel">megapixel</a> resolution image, using same sensor as <a href="/wiki/Nikon_D100" title="Nikon D100">Nikon D100</a>. It was the smallest and lightest dSLR at the time, but still well equipped. For example, it had a large and bright <a href="/wiki/Pentaprism" title="Pentaprism">pentaprism</a> viewfinder, compared to <a href="/wiki/Pentamirror" title="Pentamirror">pentamirror</a> in other similarly priced competition.</p>
