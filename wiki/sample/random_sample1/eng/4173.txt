<p><b>Dionysios</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Διονύσιος</span>) or <b>Dimitrios</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Δημήτριος</span>) Kasdaglis (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Κάσδαγλης</span>) (10 October 1872 – 1931) was a <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a>-<a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptian</a> <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> player. He competed in the <a href="/wiki/1896_Summer_Olympics" title="1896 Summer Olympics">1896 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1906_Intercalated_Games" title="1906 Intercalated Games">1906 Intercalated Games</a>, also in Athens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sports-reference_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sports-reference-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the Greek wiki entry, his name was Dimitrios and he was mistakenly believed to be named Dionysios.</p>

<p>Kasdaglis hailed from <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> but according to the official bulletin he participated as a member of the Greek team. He made it to the finals in both the singles and doubles events. In the singles, he defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defert" title="Defert">Defert</a> of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in the first round, <a href="/wiki/Konstantinos_Akratopoulos" title="Konstantinos Akratopoulos">Konstantinos Akratopoulos</a> of <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> in the second, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Momcsill%C3%B3_Tapavicza" title="Momcsilló Tapavicza">Momcsilló Tapavicza</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> in the semifinals before facing <a href="/wiki/John_Pius_Boland" title="John Pius Boland">John Pius Boland</a> of <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">Great Britain and Ireland</a> in the final. Boland proved the better player, and Kasdaglis finished second. The medal is credited to Kasdaglis as a Greek by the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a>.</p>
<p>For the doubles tournament, Kasdaglis paired with <a href="/wiki/Demetrios_Petrokokkinos" title="Demetrios Petrokokkinos">Demetrios Petrokokkinos</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Mixed_team_at_the_1896_Summer_Olympics" title="Mixed team at the 1896 Summer Olympics">mixed team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mixed_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mixed-2">[2]</a></sup> They defeated another pair of Greeks, <a href="/wiki/Konstantinos_Paspatis" title="Konstantinos Paspatis">Konstantinos Paspatis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evangelos_Rallis" title="Evangelos Rallis">Evangelos Rallis</a>, in the first round and the British/<a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> pair of <a href="/wiki/George_S._Robertson" title="George S. Robertson">George S. Robertson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Flack" title="Edwin Flack">Edwin Flack</a> in the semifinals. In the final, Kasdaglis again faced Boland, this time paired with <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Traun" title="Friedrich Traun">Friedrich Traun</a> of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Kasdaglis and Petrokokkinos lost that match to give Kasdaglis his second silver medal.</p>
