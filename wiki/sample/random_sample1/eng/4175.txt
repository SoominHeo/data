<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Deoxyribonucleotide</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>A <b>deoxyribonucleotide</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Monomer" title="Monomer">monomer</a>, or single unit, of <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a>, or deoxyribonucleic acid. Each deoxyribonucleotide comprises three parts: a <a href="/wiki/Nitrogenous_base" title="Nitrogenous base">nitrogenous base</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Deoxyribose" title="Deoxyribose">deoxyribose</a> sugar, and one <a href="/wiki/Phosphate" title="Phosphate">phosphate</a> group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The nitrogenous base is always bonded to the 1' carbon of the deoxyribose, which is distinguished from ribose by the presence of a proton on the 2' carbon rather than an -OH group. The phosphate groups bind to the 5' carbon of the sugar.</p>
<p>When deoxyribonucleotides polymerize to form DNA, the phosphate group from one nucleotide will bond to the 3' carbon on another nucleotide, forming a <a href="/wiki/Phosphodiester_bond" title="Phosphodiester bond">phosphodiester bond</a> via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dehydration_synthesis" title="Dehydration synthesis">dehydration synthesis</a>. New nucleotides are always added to the 3' carbon of the last nucleotide, so synthesis always proceeds from 5' to 3'.</p>
