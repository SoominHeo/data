<p><b>Darren Arthur Reed</b> (born June 18, 1963), better known by his stage name <b>Dizzy Reed</b>, is an American musician and occasional actor. He is best known as the keyboardist for the rock band <a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a>, with whom he has played, toured, and recorded since 1990.</p>
<p>Aside from lead singer <a href="/wiki/Axl_Rose" title="Axl Rose">Axl Rose</a>, Reed was the longest-standing and only member of Guns N' Roses to remain from the band's <i><a href="/wiki/Use_Your_Illusion" title="Use Your Illusion">Use Your Illusion</a></i> era, until early 2016 when guitarist <a href="/wiki/Slash_(musician)" title="Slash (musician)">Slash</a> and bassist <a href="/wiki/Duff_McKagan" title="Duff McKagan">Duff McKagan</a> returned to the band. In 2012, he was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a> as a member of Guns N' Roses, although he did not attend the ceremony. He was also a member of the Australian-American supergroup <a href="/wiki/The_Dead_Daisies" title="The Dead Daisies">The Dead Daisies</a> with his Guns N' Roses bandmate <a href="/wiki/Richard_Fortus" title="Richard Fortus">Richard Fortus</a>, ex-<a href="/wiki/Whitesnake" title="Whitesnake">Whitesnake</a> member <a href="/wiki/Marco_Mendoza" title="Marco Mendoza">Marco Mendoza</a>, ex-<a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe" title="Mötley Crüe">Mötley Crüe</a> frontman <a href="/wiki/John_Corabi" title="John Corabi">John Corabi</a> and session drummer <a href="/wiki/Brian_Tichy" title="Brian Tichy">Brian Tichy</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dizzy_Reed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reed was born as Darren Arthur Reed on June 18, 1963 in <a href="/wiki/Hinsdale,_Illinois" title="Hinsdale, Illinois">Hinsdale</a>, Illinois, and was raised in Colorado.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-R_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-R-1">[1]</a></sup> Reed was described as reclusive and introverted, however he has since denied this. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His grandmother began teaching him to play the organ when he was a young child, and before he was out of elementary school, he formed small local bands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-R_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-R-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DRao_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-DRao-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dizzy_Reed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:197px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Guns_n%C2%B4Roses_Poliedro_de_Caracas_27_de_Marzo_2010_r_1_(cropped).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="375" data-file-width="195" height="375" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6f/Guns_n%C2%B4Roses_Poliedro_de_Caracas_27_de_Marzo_2010_r_1_%28cropped%29.jpg" width="195"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Guns_n%C2%B4Roses_Poliedro_de_Caracas_27_de_Marzo_2010_r_1_(cropped).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Reed after a Guns N' Roses concert in 2010.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>As an adult, Reed pursued a music career in Los Angeles. He was a founding member of the club band The Wild in the late 1980s, with whom he spent five years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RJ_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-RJ-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reed met the original lineup of <a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a> in 1985 while his band, The Wild, rehearsed in a neighboring studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-R_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-R-1">[1]</a></sup> He kept in touch, and in 1990 was invited by friend <a href="/wiki/Axl_Rose" title="Axl Rose">Axl Rose</a> to join the group for the recording of the two <i><a href="/wiki/Use_Your_Illusion" title="Use Your Illusion">Use Your Illusion</a></i> albums.</p>
<p>Reed soon became an accepted member of the group and his work was heard on the majority of tracks on both albums. As a member of Guns N' Roses, Reed has become well known for his keyboard, piano, and backing vocal work during live performances, music videos and on such songs as "Dust N' Bones", "<a href="/wiki/Live_and_Let_Die_(song)" title="Live and Let Die (song)">Live and Let Die</a>", "Bad Obsession" "<a href="/wiki/November_Rain" title="November Rain">November Rain</a>", "Garden of Eden", "Don't Damn Me", "Bad Apples", "<a href="/wiki/Civil_War_(song)" title="Civil War (song)">Civil War</a>", "14 Years", "<a href="/wiki/Yesterdays_(Guns_N%27_Roses_song)" title="Yesterdays (Guns N' Roses song)">Yesterdays</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knockin%27_On_Heaven%27s_Door" title="Knockin' On Heaven's Door">Knockin' On Heaven's Door</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Get_in_the_Ring" title="Get in the Ring">Get in the Ring</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Pretty_Tied_Up" title="Pretty Tied Up">Pretty Tied Up</a>" and "Locomotive", as well his contributions to some of the band's newer tracks, including "<a href="/wiki/Chinese_Democracy_(song)" title="Chinese Democracy (song)">Chinese Democracy</a>", "Shacklers Revenge", "<a href="/wiki/Better_(Guns_N%27_Roses_song)" title="Better (Guns N' Roses song)">Better</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Street_of_Dreams_(Guns_N%27_Roses_song)" title="Street of Dreams (Guns N' Roses song)">Street of Dreams</a>" "If the World", "There Was a Time", "Catcher in the Rye", "Scraped", "Riad N' the Bedouins", "I.R.S" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prostitute" title="Prostitute">Prostitute</a>." When not playing keyboards or piano, Reed frequently provides backup on percussion and vocals during live Guns N' Roses performances. He is also known for playing percussions during live performances of songs such as "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mr_Brownstone" title="Mr Brownstone">Mr Brownstone</a>","<a href="/wiki/Nightrain" title="Nightrain">Nightrain</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_the_Jungle" title="Welcome to the Jungle">Welcome to the Jungle</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Rocket_Queen" title="Rocket Queen">Rocket Queen</a>".</p>
<p>Reed continues to record and play live with the current Guns N' Roses line-up, and has now been a member of Guns N' Roses longer than any other member besides <a href="/wiki/Axl_Rose" title="Axl Rose">Axl Rose</a>. However, since he joined the band in 1990, five years after its formation in 1985, he cannot be described as an original member. Although Reed did not co write any songs during the Illusion sessions, for <i><a href="/wiki/Chinese_Democracy" title="Chinese Democracy">Chinese Democracy</a></i> he co wrote "Street of Dreams" with Axl Rose and <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Stinson" title="Tommy Stinson">Tommy Stinson</a> and "There Was a Time" and "I.R.S" with Rose and Paul Tobias, as well as the non-album single "<a href="/wiki/Oh_My_God_(Guns_N%27_Roses_song)" title="Oh My God (Guns N' Roses song)">Oh My God</a>" with Rose and Tobias. It has also been confirmed that the unfinished demo that did not make the cut on <i>Chinese Democracy</i> called "Silkworms" was written by Reed himself and the bands other keyboardist <a href="/wiki/Chris_Pitman" title="Chris Pitman">Chris Pitman</a>. As well as singing during Guns N' Roses live performances, Reed also serves as a backing vocalist. He sang backing vocals on a few songs on the <i>Use Your Illusion</i> albums, notable examples were "<a href="/wiki/November_Rain" title="November Rain">November Rain</a>", "Garden of Eden", "Bad Apples" and "<a href="/wiki/Civil_War_(song)" title="Civil War (song)">Civil War</a>" as well as "<a href="/wiki/You_Can%27t_Put_Your_Arms_Around_a_Memory" title="You Can't Put Your Arms Around a Memory">You Can't Put Your Arms Around a Memory</a>" from <i><a href="/wiki/%22The_Spaghetti_Incident%3F%22" title='"The Spaghetti Incident?"'>"The Spaghetti Incident?"</a>.</i></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_outside_Guns_N.27_Roses">Work outside Guns N' Roses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dizzy_Reed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Work outside Guns N' Roses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dead_Daisies_(cropped).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="367" data-file-width="379" height="213" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Dead_Daisies_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Dead_Daisies_%28cropped%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Dead_Daisies_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Dead_Daisies_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/07/Dead_Daisies_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dead_Daisies_(cropped).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Reed playing with <a href="/wiki/The_Dead_Daisies" title="The Dead Daisies">The Dead Daisies</a> in 2013.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Outside of Guns N' Roses, Reed played on albums for his former bandmates Slash, <a href="/wiki/Duff_McKagan" title="Duff McKagan">Duff McKagan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gilby_Clarke" title="Gilby Clarke">Gilby Clarke</a>. He also guested on former Guns N' Roses bassist Tommy Stinson's 2004 solo effort <i>Village Gorilla Head</i>. Reed is additionally a fan of <a href="/wiki/Larry_Norman" title="Larry Norman">Larry Norman</a>, a pioneer of <a href="/wiki/Christian_music" title="Christian music">Christian music</a>, and played on Norman's <i>Copper Wires</i> album. Most recently, he has composed music for the film scores <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Still_Life_(2006_film)" title="The Still Life (2006 film)">The Still Life</a></i>, released in 2006, and <i>Celebrity Art Show</i> (2008).</p>
<p>When he is not touring or recording with Guns N' Roses, Reed frequently tours with his hard rock cover band Hookers N' Blow, in which he plays keyboard and guitar and occasionally sings lead vocals. For his work with Hookers N' Blow, Reed was named Outstanding Keyboardist of the Year at the 2007 Rock City Awards ("Rockies"). Hookers N' Blow was also named Best Cover Band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rockies_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-rockies-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reed has also dabbled in acting, appearing as 'Mumbles' in the 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/Charlie%27s_Death_Wish" title="Charlie's Death Wish">Charlie's Death Wish</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sludge_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-sludge-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reed was a member of <a href="/wiki/The_Dead_Daisies" title="The Dead Daisies">The Dead Daisies</a> alongside Guns N' Roses guitarist Richard Fortus, both left the band in 2015 to focus on Guns N' Roses.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dizzy_Reed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reed was divorced after 20 years of marriage, to wife Lisa, an author and special education teacher for ten years. He filed for divorce in 2010. They have two daughters; Skye, born in 1992 and Shade born in 1996. He also has one son from a previous relationship, Justin Gunn-Reed, born in 1988.</p>
<p>In 2005, Reed took the unusual step of seeking admission to a college fraternity well after the traditional age of inductees, and on January 22, 2006 was admitted to the <a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell University</a> chapter of <a href="/wiki/Zeta_Psi" title="Zeta Psi">Zeta Psi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sludge_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-sludge-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zetapsi_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-zetapsi-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
