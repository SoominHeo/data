<p><b>Dita Von Teese</b> (born <b>Heather Renée Sweet</b>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vonTeeseParis_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-vonTeeseParis-1">[1]</a></sup> September 28, 1972) is an American <a href="/wiki/American_burlesque" title="American burlesque">burlesque</a> dancer, <a href="/wiki/Vedette_(cabaret)" title="Vedette (cabaret)">vedette</a>, model, costume designer, entrepreneur and occasional actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Werle2009_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Werle2009-2">[2]</a></sup> She is thought to have helped repopularize burlesque performance,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Willson2007_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Willson2007-3">[3]</a></sup> and was formerly married to <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Manson" title="Marilyn Manson">Marilyn Manson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dita_Von_Teese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Von Teese was born in <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_Michigan" title="Rochester, Michigan">Rochester, Michigan</a>, the middle of three daughters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimesNakAmb_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-TimesNakAmb-5">[5]</a></sup> Her father was a <a href="/wiki/Machinist" title="Machinist">machinist</a> and her mother a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manicurist" title="Manicurist">manicurist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimesNakAmb_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-TimesNakAmb-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artteese_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-artteese-6">[6]</a></sup> She is of <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armenian_people" title="Armenian people">Armenian</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_people" title="German people">German</a> heritage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Dita has stated that one of her grandmothers was half-Armenian and adopted into an Anglo-Saxon American family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Von Teese is known for her fascination with <a href="/wiki/1940s_in_film" title="1940s in film">1940s cinema</a> and classic vintage style. This began at a young age and was fostered by her mother, who would buy clothes for her daughter to dress up. Her mother was a fan of old, <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_States#Golden_Age_of_Hollywood" title="Cinema of the United States">Golden Age-era Hollywood films</a>, and from her Von Teese developed a fascination with the actresses of that day, especially <a href="/wiki/Betty_Grable" title="Betty Grable">Betty Grable</a>.</p>
<p>She was classically trained as a ballet dancer from an early age, and danced solo at age 13 for a local ballet company. Though she originally wanted to be a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ballerina" title="Ballerina">ballerina</a>, Von Teese states that "by 15, I was as good as I'd ever be."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artteese_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-artteese-6">[6]</a></sup> She was later to incorporate this element into her burlesque shows, where she frequently goes <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/En_pointe" title="En pointe">en pointe</a></i>. The family relocated from Michigan to <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_California" title="Orange County, California">Orange County, California</a>, when her father's job moved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimesNakAmb_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-TimesNakAmb-5">[5]</a></sup> Von Teese attended <a href="/wiki/University_High_School_(Irvine,_California)" title="University High School (Irvine, California)">University High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Irvine,_California" title="Irvine, California">Irvine, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocregister_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ocregister-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Von Teese was a teenager, her mother took her to buy her first <a href="/wiki/Bra" title="Bra">bra</a>, made from plain white cotton, and gave her a plastic egg containing a pair of wrinkly, flesh-colored <a href="/wiki/Pantyhose" title="Pantyhose">pantyhose</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocregister_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-ocregister-9">[9]</a></sup> Von Teese says she was disappointed, as she had been hoping to receive beautiful lacy garments and stockings, of the type she had glimpsed in her father's <i><a href="/wiki/Playboy" title="Playboy">Playboy</a></i> magazines. This fueled her passion for lingerie. She worked in a lingerie store as a salesgirl when she was 15, eventually as a <a href="/wiki/Buyer" title="Buyer">buyer</a>. Von Teese has been fond of wearing elaborate lingerie such as <a href="/wiki/Corset" title="Corset">corsets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basque_(clothing)" title="Basque (clothing)">basques</a> with <a href="/wiki/Fully_fashioned_stockings" title="Fully fashioned stockings">fully fashioned stockings</a> ever since. In college, Von Teese studied historic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Costuming" title="Costuming">costuming</a> and aspired to work as a <a href="/wiki/Wardrobe_stylist" title="Wardrobe stylist">stylist</a> for films. She is a trained <a href="/wiki/Costume_designer" title="Costume designer">costume designer</a>, often designing (and <a href="/wiki/Copyright" title="Copyright">copyrighting</a>) her photo shoots herself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-burlesqueisback_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-burlesqueisback-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dita_Von_Teese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Von Teese got her stage name by adopting the name Dita as a tribute to silent film actress <a href="/wiki/Dita_Parlo" title="Dita Parlo">Dita Parlo</a>. For her breakthrough December 2002 <i>Playboy</i> cover, she was required to have a surname, so she chose <i>Von Treese</i> from the phonebook. In the magazine, this was misspelled as <i>Von Teese</i>, which she decided to stick with.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fetish_and_glamour_modeling">Fetish and glamour modeling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dita_Von_Teese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fetish and glamour modeling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Von Teese achieved some level of recognition in the fetish world as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tightlacer" title="Tightlacer">tightlacer</a>. Through the wearing of a <a href="/wiki/Corset" title="Corset">corset</a> for many years, she had reduced her waistline to 22 inches (56 cm), and can be laced down as far as 16.5 inches (42 cm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Von Teese appeared on a number of <a href="/wiki/Fetish_magazine" title="Fetish magazine">fetish magazine</a> covers, including <i><a href="/wiki/Bizarre_(magazine)" title="Bizarre (magazine)">Bizarre</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bizarre_Magazine_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bizarre_Magazine-13">[13]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Marquis_(magazine)" title="Marquis (magazine)">Marquis</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> It was around this time when she appeared on the cover of <a href="/wiki/Midori_(author)" title="Midori (author)">Midori</a>'s book, <i>The Seductive Art of Japanese Bondage</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Von Teese was featured in <i>Playboy</i> in 1999, 2001, and 2002, with a cover-featured pictorial in 2002. The German <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Atrocity_(band)" title="Atrocity (band)">Atrocity</a> chose her as the cover model for their 2008 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Werk_80_II" title="Werk 80 II">Werk 80 II</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Burlesque">Burlesque</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dita_Von_Teese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Burlesque">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Von Teese is best known for her burlesque routines and is frequently dubbed the "Queen of Burlesque" in the press.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Von Teese began performing burlesque in 1992, and, as a proponent of <a href="/wiki/Neo-Burlesque" title="Neo-Burlesque">Neo-Burlesque</a>, has helped to popularize its revival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-suicidegirls_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-suicidegirls-19">[19]</a></sup> In her own words, she "puts the <a href="/wiki/Teasing" title="Teasing">tease</a> back into <a href="/wiki/Striptease" title="Striptease">striptease</a>" with long, elaborate dance shows with props and characters, often inspired by 1930s and 1940s musicals and films. Some of her more famous dances have involved a <a href="/wiki/Carousel" title="Carousel">carousel</a> horse, a giant powder compact, a filigree heart and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slipper_Bathtub" title="Slipper Bathtub">clawfoot bathtub</a> with a working shower head. Her signature show features a giant <a href="/wiki/Martini_(cocktail)" title="Martini (cocktail)">martini</a> glass. Her feather <a href="/wiki/Fan_dance" title="Fan dance">fan dance</a>, inspired by burlesque dancer <a href="/wiki/Sally_Rand" title="Sally Rand">Sally Rand</a>, featured the world's largest feather fans, now on display in Hollywood's <a href="/wiki/Sex_museum" title="Sex museum">Museum of Sex</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-burlesque_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-burlesque-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her burlesque career has included some memorable performances. She once appeared at a benefit for the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Academy_of_Art" title="New York Academy of Art">New York Academy of Art</a> wearing nothing but $5 million worth of diamonds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diamonds_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-diamonds-21">[21]</a></sup> Additionally, Von Teese became the first guest star at Paris's <a href="/wiki/Crazy_Horse_(cabaret)" title="Crazy Horse (cabaret)">Crazy Horse</a> cabaret club with her appearance in October 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Also in 2006, Von Teese appeared on an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Next_Top_Model" title="America's Next Top Model">America's Next Top Model</a></i> (cycle 7) doing a workshop to teach the contestants about sexiness by means of burlesque dancing and posing. In 2007, Von Teese performed at the adult entertainment event Erotica 07 in London alongside Italian <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Belladonna_(band)" title="Belladonna (band)">Belladonna</a>.</p>
<p>Von Teese's first book (in collaboration with Bronwyn Garrity), which consisted of her opinions on the history of burlesque and fetish, <i>Burlesque and the Art of the Teese / Fetish and the Art of the Teese</i>, was published in 2006 by <a href="/wiki/HarperCollins" title="HarperCollins">HarperCollins</a> (and in New York by Regan Books). <i><a href="/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine)" title="Vanity Fair (magazine)">Vanity Fair</a></i> called her a "Burlesque Superheroine".</p>
<p>Von Teese participated in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2009" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2009">Eurovision Song Contest 2009</a> in Moscow, Russia, as part of the stage performance for the <a href="/wiki/Germany_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2009" title="Germany in the Eurovision Song Contest 2009">German</a> entry "<a href="/wiki/Miss_Kiss_Kiss_Bang" title="Miss Kiss Kiss Bang">Miss Kiss Kiss Bang</a>". The act placed 20th out of 25 participants in the final round of the contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Later, she said her cleavage was censored during the show because it was too voluptuous.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dita_Von_Teese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Von Teese has performed in adult and mainstream films. In her early years, she appeared in fetish-related, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soft-core" title="Soft-core">soft-core</a> pornographic movies, such as <i>Romancing Sara</i>, <i>Matter of Trust</i>, in which she is billed as Heather Sweet, and also in two <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Blake_(director)" title="Andrew Blake (director)">Andrew Blake</a> <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_pornography" title="Hardcore pornography">hard-core</a> fetish films, <i>Pin Ups 2</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and <i>Decadence</i>.</p>
<p>In recent years, she has appeared in more mainstream features, such as the 2005 short film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Death_of_Salvador_Dali" title="The Death of Salvador Dali">The Death of Salvador Dali</a></i>, written by Delaney Bishop, which won best screenplay and best <a href="/wiki/Cinematography" title="Cinematography">cinematography</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SXSW" title="SXSW">SXSW</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raindance_Film_Festival" title="Raindance Film Festival">Raindance Film Festival</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mill_Valley_Film_Festival" title="Mill Valley Film Festival">Mill Valley Film Festival</a>, and won Best Actress at <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_Film_Festival" title="Beverly Hills Film Festival">Beverly Hills Film Festival</a>. She starred in the feature film <i>Saint Francis</i> in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition, she has appeared in a number of music videos, including the video for the <a href="/wiki/Green_Day" title="Green Day">Green Day</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Redundant_(song)" title="Redundant (song)">Redundant</a>,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> the video for "Zip Gun Bop" by <a href="/wiki/Swing_music" title="Swing music">swing</a> band <a href="/wiki/Royal_Crown_Revue" title="Royal Crown Revue">Royal Crown Revue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agent_Provocateur_(lingerie)" title="Agent Provocateur (lingerie)">Agent Provocateur</a>'s video for their cover of <a href="/wiki/Joy_Division" title="Joy Division">Joy Division</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/She%27s_Lost_Control" title="She's Lost Control">She's Lost Control</a>," and (performing her martini-glass burlesque routine) the video for "<a href="/wiki/Mobscene" title="Mobscene">Mobscene</a>" by Marilyn Manson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> She was featured in a striptease/burlesque act in <a href="/wiki/George_Michael" title="George Michael">George Michael</a>'s live tour 2008, for the song "Feelin' Good".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> She also appeared in the music video "<a href="/wiki/Up_in_the_Air_(song)" title="Up in the Air (song)">Up in the Air</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Thirty_Seconds_to_Mars" title="Thirty Seconds to Mars">Thirty Seconds to Mars</a> in 2013.</p>
<p>She stated in 2007, "I don't understand why women feel the need to go into acting as soon as they become famous ... But I suppose if the part were aesthetically correct, then maybe I could consider it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2011, Von Teese guest-starred in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> police procedural drama <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>, in which her friend <a href="/wiki/Eric_Szmanda" title="Eric Szmanda">Eric Szmanda</a> stars, in the episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Kiss_Before_Frying" title="A Kiss Before Frying">A Kiss Before Frying</a>". She played Rita von Squeeze, a <a href="/wiki/Femme_fatale" title="Femme fatale">femme fatale</a> version of herself, who seduces Szmanda's character <a href="/wiki/Greg_Sanders" title="Greg Sanders">Greg Sanders</a> in a plot inspired by <a href="/wiki/Film_noir" title="Film noir">film noir</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fashion_and_modeling">Fashion and modeling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dita_Von_Teese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fashion and modeling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Von Teese has appeared on a number of best-dressed lists<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> and frequents the front row of fashion shows, particularly <a href="/wiki/Christian_Dior" title="Christian Dior">Christian Dior</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vivienne_Westwood" title="Vivienne Westwood">Vivienne Westwood</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jean_Paul_Gaultier" title="Jean Paul Gaultier">Jean Paul Gaultier</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Jacobs" title="Marc Jacobs">Marc Jacobs</a>, labels she is often seen wearing. Speaking of her love of Gaultier, Von Teese has said, "Jean Paul Gaultier, John Galliano, and Vivienne Westwood were the first designers that really made an impact on me. Jean Paul has a lot of the same obsessions that I do, like corsetry, ballet-peach satin and silks, black silk velvet, bullet bras and girdles. When I was a teenager, I would look for 1930s peach satin lingerie pieces to try to get his look for less, because there was no way I could afford his things back then. It was a dream come true to meet him and become friends with him. I met him the first time when I was doing my first fashion pictorial, which was for Flaunt magazine, and it was a huge story of me wearing the most important pieces from his haute couture archives, and that was a dream!" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has also done <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catwalk" title="Catwalk">catwalk</a> work. During <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Fashion_Week" title="Los Angeles Fashion Week">Los Angeles Fashion Week</a> for spring 2004, she modeled for former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_kid" title="Club kid">club kid</a> <a href="/wiki/Richie_Rich_(designer)" title="Richie Rich (designer)">Richie Rich</a>'s fashion label, <a href="/wiki/Heatherette" title="Heatherette">Heatherette</a>. In 2005, she appeared in the Autumn/Winter Ready-to-Wear show for Giambattista Valli, a former designer for <a href="/wiki/Ungaro" title="Ungaro">Ungaro</a>, in Paris. In the 2006 <a href="/wiki/Milan_Fashion_Week" title="Milan Fashion Week">Milan Fashion Week</a>, Von Teese was on the runway, opening for the Moschino diffusion label, Moschino Cheap &amp; Chic, autumn/winter 2006/7 show. In 2007, she appeared twice in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jean_Paul_Gaultier" title="Jean Paul Gaultier">Jean Paul Gaultier</a> <a href="/wiki/Haute_couture" title="Haute couture">haute couture</a> show during the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Fashion_Week" title="Paris Fashion Week">Paris Fashion Week</a>. She has starred in several ad campaigns. She appeared in <a href="/wiki/Vivienne_Westwood" title="Vivienne Westwood">Vivienne Westwood</a>'s spring/summer 2005 collection adverts and became the face of Australian clothing range "Wheels and Dollbaby" for their 2006/7 Spring/Summer advertising campaign. She was an ambassador/spokesmodel for HIV/AIDS awareness when she was selected (along with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eve_(entertainer)" title="Eve (entertainer)">Eve</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deborah_Harry" title="Deborah Harry">Deborah Harry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Marie_Presley" title="Lisa Marie Presley">Lisa Marie Presley</a>) for <a href="/wiki/MAC_Cosmetics" title="MAC Cosmetics">MAC Cosmetics</a>' Viva Glam VI campaign (a collection of lipsticks and lipgloss where 100% of the proceeds are for worldwide AIDS charities and to raise awareness for HIV/AIDS). Von Teese has appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine)" title="Vanity Fair (magazine)">Vanity Fair</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Vogue_(magazine)" title="Vogue (magazine)">Vogue</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Elle_(magazine)" title="Elle (magazine)">Elle</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and international issues of nearly every fashion magazine. She has campaigned on behalf of <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals</a> (PETA) and has appeared in their ads. She participated at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PETA" title="PETA">PETA</a>'s campaign "Animal Birth Control (ABC)."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Von Teese states that she never uses a stylist. "The one time I hired a stylist, they picked up a pair of my 1940s shoes and said, 'These would look really cute with jeans.' I immediately said, 'You’re out of here.'" She does her own make-up, and dyes her naturally blonde hair black at home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artteese_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-artteese-6">[6]</a></sup> Von Teese's unique style is "inspired by eccentric women like <a href="/wiki/Luisa_Casati" title="Luisa Casati">Luisa Casati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Piaggi" title="Anna Piaggi">Anna Piaggi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Isabella_Blow" title="Isabella Blow">Isabella Blow</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has designed a new lingerie range with <a href="/wiki/Wonderbra" title="Wonderbra">Wonderbra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> One of Dita Von Teese's trademark items is the garter belt with six garters instead of the usual four, with two each at the front, side seams and back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In May 2012, Dita Von Teese launched her makeup collection "Classics" in partnership with the German cosmetic brand Art Deco. Compact powder, blushers, eye styler, eye shadows, mascara and lipstick were produced to recreate the burlesque performer's retro look.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Dita Von Teese launched her own clothing line<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Alexander2012_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-Alexander2012-40">[40]</a></sup> and underwear line called Von Follies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ella2012Nov_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ella2012Nov-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ella2012March_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ella2012March-42">[42]</a></sup> In 2013, Dita was both model and muse for the 'Dita' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3D_printed" title="3D printed">3D printed</a> gown designed by Michael Schmidt and Francis Bitonti and 3D printed by <a href="/wiki/Shapeways" title="Shapeways">Shapeways</a> exactly to fit her body.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Constructed of over 3,000 interlocked 3D printed components and over 12,000 <a href="/wiki/Swarovski" title="Swarovski">Swarovski</a> crystals it is one of the first fully articulated 3D printed garments to take the technology from haute couture to a sensual fabric-like form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Von Teese began working with <a href="/wiki/Myer" title="Myer">Myer</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> an Australian department store, on a lingerie line fronted by Australian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Model_(people)" title="Model (people)">model</a> <a href="/wiki/Stefania_Ferrario" title="Stefania Ferrario">Stefania Ferrario</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dita_Von_Teese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Von Teese frequently takes <a href="/wiki/Pilates" title="Pilates">Pilates</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dressage" title="Dressage">dressage</a> lessons. Her house is dedicated to early 20th-century décor, including a complete pink kitchen with vintage appliances. She also has a room dedicated to hats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimesNakAmb_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-TimesNakAmb-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Von Teese currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EducaDita_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-EducaDita-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> She is a collector of vintage china, particularly <a href="/wiki/Egg_cup" title="Egg cup">egg cups</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tea_set" title="Tea set">tea sets</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vintage_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-vintage-49">[49]</a></sup> and drives a <a href="/wiki/Packard_One-Twenty" title="Packard One-Twenty">1939 Packard One Twenty Eight</a>, a 1946 <a href="/wiki/1941_Ford" title="1941 Ford">Ford Super De Luxe</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/BMW_Z4" title="BMW Z4">BMW Z4</a> and a 1965 <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_S-Type_(1963)" title="Jaguar S-Type (1963)">Jaguar S-Type</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> She also owned a 1939 <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_New_Yorker" title="Chrysler New Yorker">Chrysler New Yorker</a> from 1997 to December 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_to_Marilyn_Manson">Marriage to Marilyn Manson</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dita_Von_Teese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Marriage to Marilyn Manson">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Manson" title="Marilyn Manson">Marilyn Manson</a> had been a longtime fan of hers and was a member of her website. They first met when he asked her to dance in one of his music videos and though she was unable to, the two remained in contact. On Manson's 32nd birthday, in 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> she arrived with a bottle of <a href="/wiki/Absinthe" title="Absinthe">absinthe</a> and they became a couple. Manson proposed on March 22, 2004, and gave her a 1930s, 7-carat (1.4 g), European round-cut diamond engagement ring. On November 28, 2005, they were married in a private, non-denominational ceremony at home. A larger ceremony was held on December 3 at Gurteen Castle in <a href="/wiki/Kilsheelan" title="Kilsheelan">Kilsheelan</a> (<a href="/wiki/County_Tipperary" title="County Tipperary">County Tipperary</a>), Ireland, the home of their friend, <a href="/wiki/Gottfried_Helnwein" title="Gottfried Helnwein">Gottfried Helnwein</a>. The wedding was officiated by surrealist film director and comic book writer <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_Jodorowsky" title="Alejandro Jodorowsky">Alejandro Jodorowsky</a>, was attended by actor <a href="/wiki/Eric_Szmanda" title="Eric Szmanda">Eric Szmanda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Marie_Presley" title="Lisa Marie Presley">Lisa Marie Presley</a>, burlesque star <a href="/wiki/Catherine_D%27lish" title="Catherine D'lish">Catherine D'Lish</a>, artist <a href="/wiki/Jessicka" title="Jessicka">Jessicka</a>, designer <a href="/wiki/Betony_Vernon" title="Betony Vernon">Betony Vernon</a>, producer <a href="/wiki/Christian_Hejnal" title="Christian Hejnal">Christian Hejnal</a>, and Manson's band mate <a href="/wiki/Madonna_Wayne_Gacy" title="Madonna Wayne Gacy">Madonna Wayne Gacy</a> and appeared as a feature in the pages of <i><a href="/wiki/Vogue_(magazine)" title="Vogue (magazine)">Vogue</a></i>.</p>
<p>On December 29, 2006, Von Teese filed for divorce from Manson citing "irreconcilable differences".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcdivorce_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcdivorce-54">[54]</a></sup> Von Teese left their house empty-handed on Christmas Eve, and was not able to get in touch with Manson to inform him of her intention to divorce him. In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i>, Von Teese stated, "I wasn't supportive of his partying or his relationship with another girl. As much as I loved him, I wasn't going to be part of that." Von Teese also stated that she gave Manson an ultimatum, and said that "it didn't work. Instead, it made me the enemy."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EducaDita_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-EducaDita-47">[47]</a></sup> Von Teese did not seek spousal support and expressed no interest in his assets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcdivorce_54-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbcdivorce-54">[54]</a></sup> The news broke for the public and for Manson on his birthday on January 5, 2007, when he <a href="/wiki/Service_of_process" title="Service of process">was served</a> the divorce papers.</p>
