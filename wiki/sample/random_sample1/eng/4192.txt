<p>A <b>diploma</b> (from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Greek</a> δίπλωµα <i>díplōma</i>, meaning "folded paper") is a <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/certificate" title="wikt:certificate">certificate</a> or <a href="/wiki/Deed" title="Deed">deed</a> issued by an educational institution, such as a <a href="/wiki/College" title="College">college</a> or <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a>, that testifies that the recipient has successfully completed a particular course of study,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OED_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-OED-1">[1]</a></sup> or (US) recording that an <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">academic degree</a> has been awarded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In some countries, e.g. the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, the word diploma also refers to an academic award (e.g. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diploma_of_higher_education" title="Diploma of higher education">diploma of higher education</a>, <a href="/wiki/Graduate_diploma" title="Graduate diploma">graduate diploma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate_diploma" title="Postgraduate diploma">postgraduate diploma</a>). Historically, it can also refer to a charter or official document,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OED_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-OED-1">[1]</a></sup> thus <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diplomatic" title="Diplomatic">diplomatic</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diplomatic_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-diplomatic-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Diplomat" title="Diplomat">diplomat</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Diplomacy" title="Diplomacy">diplomacy</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OED_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-OED-1">[1]</a></sup> via the <i>Codex Juris Gentium Diplomaticus</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diplomatic_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-diplomatic-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The diploma (as a document certifying a qualification) may also be called a <b>testamur</b>, <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "we testify" or "certify" (testari), so called from the word with which the certificate begins;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> this is commonly used in Australia to refer to the document certifying the award of a degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Alternatively, this document can simply be referred to as a degree certificate or graduation certificate, or as a <b>parchment</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The certificate that a <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel</a> laureate receives is also called a diploma.</p>
<p>The term diploma is also used in some historical contexts, to refer to documents signed by a King affirming a grant or tenure of specified land and its conditions (see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon_Charters" title="Anglo-Saxon Charters">Anglo-Saxon Charters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diplomatics" title="Diplomatics">Diplomatics</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, there are three varieties of Diploma currently recognized by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Qualifications_Framework" title="Australian Qualifications Framework">Australian Qualifications Framework</a> (AQF):</p>
<ul>
<li>a "Diploma", a qualification granted by <a href="/wiki/Australian_Qualifications_Framework#Vocational_Education_and_Training_and_Higher_Education_sectors" title="Australian Qualifications Framework">vocational education and training</a> (VET) sector or university. It is typically completed with 12 to 18 months of full-time study. When accepted for credit as part of a bachelor's degree, it is usually deemed to be equivalent to the first year of the degree.</li>
<li>an "Advanced Diploma", which is equivalent to an Australian "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Associate_Degree" title="Associate Degree">Associate Degree</a>".</li>
<li>a "Graduate Diploma", which is undertaken after completing a bachelor's degree (or being deemed to have equivalent knowledge). This can be in a field other than that covered by said degree (e.g., the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graduate_Diploma_of_Education" title="Graduate Diploma of Education">Graduate Diploma of Education</a> necessary to become a school teacher in most Australian states). It can also be a coursework-only qualification undertaken as additional study in a specialisation within one's degree area.</li>
</ul>
<p>The "Vocational Graduate Diploma" was a short lived AQF qualification equivalent to the "Graduate Diploma", intended to be delivered exclusively in the VET sector. On January 1, 2015, all such qualifications being offered lost the word "Vocational" from their title. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.asqa.gov.au/news/2629/retitling-of-vocational-graduate-diplomas-and-vocational-graduate-certificates.html" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, diplomas are awarded by colleges of applied arts and technology whereas <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bachelor_degree" title="Bachelor degree">bachelor degrees</a> are awarded by universities. In <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, depending on the provincial legislation, there may also be a difference between a college and a university.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany_and_the_German_academic_education_system_in_Europe">Germany and the German academic education system in Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Germany and the German academic education system in Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dd>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">For more detailed information about the German Diplom, see <a href="/wiki/Diplom" title="Diplom">Diplom</a>.</div>
</dd>
</dl>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Germany" title="Education in Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Ukraine" title="Education in Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Serbia" title="Education in Serbia">Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Hungary" title="Education in Hungary">Hungary</a> and other countries that adopted the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> academic education system, diploma (in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Diplom" title="Diplom">Diplom</a></i>) is the standard academic degree, needing at least 3.5 years to complete it, being comparable with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s" title="Bachelor's">Bachelor's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">Master's degree</a> in one.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Diplom#Greece" title="Diplom">Diplom § Greece</a></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, diplomas can be awarded by educational institutes as a proof of a certain educational level. The diploma in engineering is a degree provided by Greek technical universities and universities after the successful completion of a five-year integrated study program and it is equivalent to the Master of Engineering degree, which is awarded by the European universities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hong_Kong">Hong Kong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hong Kong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Advanced_diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Advanced diploma (page does not exist)">advanced diploma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Higher_diploma" title="Higher diploma">higher diploma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Associate_degree" title="Associate degree">associate degree</a> are below the standard of the honours <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a>. <a href="/wiki/Academic_certificate" title="Academic certificate">Certificate</a> (not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate_certificate" title="Postgraduate certificate">postgraduate certificate</a>) and diploma are below the standard of advanced diploma, higher diploma and associate degree. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postgraduate_Certificate" title="Postgraduate Certificate">Postgraduate Certificates</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postgraduate_Diploma" title="Postgraduate Diploma">Postgraduate Diplomas</a> are taken after the bachelor's degree, and are more vocational oriented than a master's degree.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, a diploma is a specific academic award usually earned in professional/vocational courses, e.g., <a href="/wiki/Diploma_in_Nursing" title="Diploma in Nursing">Diploma in Nursing</a> Engineering, Pharmacy, Design, etc. In such cases, a diploma is specific in rank than a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's degree</a> of that discipline but equivalent to general degree in that discipline; for example, <a href="/wiki/Diploma_in_Engineering" title="Diploma in Engineering">Diploma in Engineering</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronics_Engineering" title="Electronics Engineering">Electronics Engineering</a> is rated differently than <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Technology" title="Bachelor of Technology">Bachelor of Technology</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronics_Engineering" title="Electronics Engineering">Electronics Engineering</a> but is equivalent to <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a> in <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">Electronics</a>.</p>
<p>Also, a diploma is concentrated for the area of study. For example, a <a href="/wiki/Diploma_in_Engineering" title="Diploma in Engineering">Diploma in Engineering</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronics_Engineering" title="Electronics Engineering">Electronics Engineering</a> may be in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Advanced_Communication_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Advanced Communication Systems (page does not exist)">Advanced Communication Systems</a> (ACS) or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Integrated_Circuits" title="Integrated Circuits">Integrated Circuits</a> (IC) or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_Electronics" title="Industrial Electronics">Industrial Electronics</a> (IE). On successful completion of Parts I and II Technician Examination of Institution of Mechanical Engineers, it is equivalent to Diploma in Mechanical Engineering as per a government of India circular. Similarly <a href="/wiki/Diploma_in_Engineering" title="Diploma in Engineering">Diploma in Engineering</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_Engineering" title="Civil Engineering">Civil Engineering</a> is rated differently than <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Technology" title="Bachelor of Technology">Bachelor of Technology</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_Engineering" title="Civil Engineering">Civil Engineering</a> but is equivalent to <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a> in <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">Geology</a>. Another example of a diploma concentrated for the area of study is <a href="/wiki/Diploma_in_Engineering" title="Diploma in Engineering">Diploma in Engineering</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_Engineering" title="Civil Engineering">Civil Engineering</a>, which may be in <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">Architecture</a> or <a href="/wiki/Urban_planning" title="Urban planning">Urban planning</a> or <a href="/wiki/Landscape_architecture" title="Landscape architecture">Landscape architecture</a>. On successful completion of Parts I and II Technician Examination of Institution of Civil Engineers, it is equivalent to Diploma in Civil Engineering as per a government of India circular.</p>
<p>Successful completion of the <a href="/wiki/Diploma_in_Engineering" title="Diploma in Engineering">Diploma</a> Studies (subject to course selection) allows articulation (also known as Lateral Entry) into the second year of <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Engineering" title="Bachelor of Engineering">Bachelor of Engineering</a>/<a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Technology" title="Bachelor of Technology">Bachelor of Technology</a> programs.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>, a <a href="/wiki/National_Diploma_(Ireland)" title="National Diploma (Ireland)">National Diploma</a> was awarded before 2004. It was at the same level as the ordinary bachelor's degree and below the honours <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's degree</a>, whilst the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Higher_Diploma" title="Higher Diploma">Higher Diploma</a> is taken after the bachelor's degree. There is <a href="/wiki/BTEC_Extended_Diploma" title="BTEC Extended Diploma">BTEC Extended Diploma</a> also after which one gets progression to a Degree(University).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Latin_America">Latin America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Latin America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and other Latin American countries, a diploma may be awarded for short courses of vocational training. The university-issued diplomas finalizing higher education are most often called <i>título</i> (title) or <i>certificado</i> (certificate).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pakistan">Pakistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Pakistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, a Diploma is a specific academic award usually earned in professional/vocational courses e.g. Engineering, <a href="/wiki/Nursing" title="Nursing">Nursing</a>, Fashion, etc. In such cases, a dipelor's degree of that discipline. As diploma studies started on vocational route of study after school for three years, the <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Technology" title="Bachelor of Technology">Bachelor of Technology</a> is the logical next degree for diploma graduates. Also Diploma is concentrated for the area of study, e.g., <a href="/wiki/Diploma_in_Engineering" title="Diploma in Engineering">Diploma in Engineering</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronics_Engineering" title="Electronics Engineering">Electronics Engineering</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_Electronics" title="Industrial Electronics">Industrial Electronics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_Engineering" title="Civil Engineering">Civil Engineering</a>. Graduates of Diplomas work as associates to Engineers and are often called Associate Engineers. Few <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postgraduate_Diploma" title="Postgraduate Diploma">Postgraduate Diploma</a> or PGD are ranked higher than the <a href="/wiki/Bachelor" title="Bachelor">Bachelor</a> degree as they are completed after graduation. These are normally a year's worth of coursework after a university degree.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Spain" title="Kingdom of Spain">Kingdom of Spain</a>, university diplomas are issued in the name of the <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">King</a>. As such, they are Public Official documents, strictly regulated and protected by the law. They are internationally recognized by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hague_Convention_Abolishing_the_Requirement_of_Legalisation_for_Foreign_Public_Documents" title="Hague Convention Abolishing the Requirement of Legalisation for Foreign Public Documents">Hague Convention</a> of October 5, 1961.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">UK</a>, <i>diploma</i> can refer to several different types of qualification. A Diploma can be a qualification worth 37 or more <a href="/wiki/Credit_Accumulation_and_Transfer_Scheme" title="Credit Accumulation and Transfer Scheme">credits</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regulated_Qualifications_Framework" title="Regulated Qualifications Framework">Regulated Qualifications Framework</a>, e.g. Entry Level Diploma, Level 3 Diploma. The <a href="/wiki/Diploma_of_Higher_Education" title="Diploma of Higher Education">Diploma of Higher Education</a> is a higher education award below the standard of a bachelor's degree, at the same level as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foundation_Degree" title="Foundation Degree">Foundation Degree</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Higher_National_Diploma" title="Higher National Diploma">Higher National Diploma</a> is a vocational qualification at the same level. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graduate_Diploma" title="Graduate Diploma">Graduate Diploma</a> is a one year qualification at bachelor's degree level, normally taken by those who have already obtained a degree. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postgraduate_Diploma" title="Postgraduate Diploma">Postgraduate Diploma</a> is an a award at the level of a master's degree, but normally taking less time to complete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some universities may offer other qualifications, such as the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>'s Undergraduate Diploma (a 1-year, part-time cost at the same level as a Diploma of Higher Education)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and Advanced Diploma (equivalent to a Graduate Diploma).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The document certifying the award of a degree is normally called a degree certificate in the UK, and details are printed in English, not in Latin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the USA, the word <i>diploma</i> usually refers to the actual document received at the end of high school, undergraduate studies, or graduate/professional school. This can lead to some confusion when persons who obtained US degrees are presenting their academic credentials in other countries.</p>
<p>Diploma also can refer to a specific academic or training award. An example of the former is in the field of Nursing where the <a href="/wiki/Diploma_in_Nursing" title="Diploma in Nursing">Diploma in Nursing</a> was offered by hospital based schools.</p>
<p>In an even wider fashion, a <a href="/wiki/Diplomat" title="Diplomat">diplomat</a> can refer to a person holding a specific accreditation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diploma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/IB_Diploma_Programme" title="IB Diploma Programme">International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma</a> is a pre-university qualification normally taken by students in the final two years of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_school" title="High school">high school</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IBDiploma_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-IBDiploma-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
