<p><b>Richard "Dick" Lee Peng Boon</b> (born 24 August 1956) is a Singaporean pop singer, composer, songwriter, and playwright. He is best known as a <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Idol" title="Singapore Idol">Singapore Idol</a> judge, but often too as spokesperson for the New Asian generation.</p>
<p>He was born to a <a href="/wiki/Peranakan" title="Peranakan">Peranakan</a> father, Lee Kip Lee, (who wrote for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Straits_Times" title="The Straits Times">The Straits Times</a></i>), and a Chinese mother, Elizabeth Tan. He was the eldest child in the family of five, with three brothers and a deceased sister. He received his early education at St. Michael's School (now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Joseph%27s_Institution_Junior" title="St. Joseph's Institution Junior">SJI Junior</a>) and his secondary education at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Joseph%27s_Institution" title="St. Joseph's Institution">St. Joseph's Institution</a>. In 1992, he married <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Jacintha_Abisheganaden" title="Jacintha Abisheganaden">Jacintha Abisheganaden</a> and divorced a few years later. He is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dick_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dick_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dick Lee started his career in 1971 when, at the age of fifteen, Dick participated in various talent contests with the group, Harmony, and Dick and the Gang (teaming with his siblings). His first album, <i>Life Story</i>, featuring his compositions, was released in 1974.</p>
<p>Throughout the 70s and 80s, Dick championed the acceptance of Asian elements in pop music. His pioneering album, <i>Life in the Lion City</i> (1984), won acclaim for just that. But the album that shot him to regional prominence was his 1989 release, <i>The Mad Chinaman</i>. Dick has also won several awards in Singapore, Hong Kong and Japan. In 1983, he co-produced <a href="/wiki/Zircon_Lounge" title="Zircon Lounge">Zircon Lounge</a>'s debut album <i>Regal Vigour</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_90s">The 90s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dick_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The 90s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1990, Dick moved to Japan where he continued to develop the new Asian identity through his solo work, as well as collaborations with top Asian artistes like <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Lam" title="Sandy Lam">Sandy Lam</a> from Hong Kong and Japanese group <a href="/wiki/Zoo_(Japanese_band)" title="Zoo (Japanese band)">Zoo</a>. He has to date, written countless songs for the top singing talents in Asia.</p>
<p>Dick's album, <i>Transit Lounge</i>, released under the <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> Label, won both critical and popular praise during the same time when he was also the regional Vice-President of Artiste and Repertoire for Sony Music Asia, based in Hong Kong from 1998 to 2000. Dick's most recent album, <i>Everything</i>, released in November 2000, features a rare collection of his works written since the 1970s. In December 2001, Dick and his friend Leonard T contributed a song to a charity CD – <i>Love Is The Answer For Kids With Aids, KK Outreach for Kids Fund</i>. The song "It All Begins With Love" is heard frequently in Singapore's radio stations.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Plays_and_musicals">Plays and musicals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dick_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Plays and musicals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dick is also passionate about musicals. He has written many staged musicals including <i><a href="/wiki/Beauty_World_(musical)" title="Beauty World (musical)">Beauty World</a></i> (1988), <i>Fried Rice Paradise</i> (1991), <i>Kampong Amber</i> (1994), <i><a href="/wiki/Sing_to_the_Dawn" title="Sing to the Dawn">Sing to the Dawn</a></i> (1996), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotpants" title="Hotpants">Hotpants</a></i> (1997), <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Cheung" title="Jacky Cheung">Jacky Cheung</a>'s acclaimed <i><a href="/wiki/Snow.Wolf.Lake" title="Snow.Wolf.Lake">Snow.Wolf.Lake</a></i> (1997), <i>Nagraland</i> (1992) and <i><a href="/wiki/Puteri_Gunung_Ledang_(musical)" title="Puteri Gunung Ledang (musical)">Puteri Gunung Ledang</a></i> (2006). Dick continues his love for theatre by being Associate Artiste Director of the <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Repertory_Theatre" title="Singapore Repertory Theatre">Singapore Repertory Theatre</a>, an appointment he has held since 1998.</p>
<p>For 2000, Dick composed the songs to the musical of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phua_Chu_Kang" title="Phua Chu Kang">Phua Chu Kang</a></i>, a highly popular sitcom, for the Singapore President's Star Charity Drive. Produced and broadcast by <a href="/wiki/Television_Corporation_of_Singapore" title="Television Corporation of Singapore">Television Corporation of Singapore</a> (TCS), now known as Mediacorp Studios (MCS), the musical raised over a record-setting $2 million.</p>
<p>In 2002 he wrote and co-directed his first dance musical <i>re:MIX</i> for Singapore Repertory Theatre Young Company (SRT Young Company), as well as a specially commissioned work, <i><a href="/wiki/Forbidden_City:_Portrait_of_An_Empress" title="Forbidden City: Portrait of An Empress">Forbidden City: Portrait of An Empress</a></i>, one of the highlights of Singapore's prestigious new cultural centre, The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esplanade_-_Theatres_on_the_Bay" title="Esplanade - Theatres on the Bay">Esplanade - Theatres on the Bay</a> opening festival in October that year and a popular re-run in September 2003, and again in 2006. It was directed by <a href="/wiki/Steven_Dexter" title="Steven Dexter">Steven Dexter</a> and will go on a world tour in 2008, with the first stop being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London%27s_West_End" title="London's West End">London's West End</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_millennium">New millennium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dick_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: New millennium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A dream come true for Dick was to be appointed the Creative Director for Singapore's 2002 <a href="/wiki/Singapore_National_Day_Parade" title="Singapore National Day Parade">National Day Parade</a>. Coincidentally, his song "<a href="/wiki/Leave_(album)" title="Leave (album)">We Will Get There</a>" was selected to be 2002 theme song for the parade. Top Singapore-born Chinese pop superstar <a href="/wiki/Stefanie_Sun" title="Stefanie Sun">Stefanie Sun</a> performed the song and also included it in her own top-selling album. This was his second National Day theme song, having written "<a href="/wiki/Home_(Kit_Chan_song)" title="Home (Kit Chan song)">Home</a>", performed by <a href="/wiki/Kit_Chan" title="Kit Chan">Kit Chan</a>, in 1998.</p>
<p>In 2003, he penned the English lyrics of "Treasure The World", the image song of J-ASEAN campaign by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Japan_Foundation" title="The Japan Foundation">The Japan Foundation</a>. Artistes from Japan and 10 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ASEAN" title="ASEAN">ASEAN</a> countries recorded this song for the campaign in English and their respective native languages. In July 2003, the highest honour to Dick's works came in the form of the prestigious Fukuoka Arts and Culture Prize, an award by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Asian_Culture_Prize" title="Fukuoka Asian Culture Prize">Fukuoka Asian Culture Prize</a> committee to recognise an individual's substantial contributions to the arts scene in Asia.</p>
<p>Lee wrote an autobiography, <i>The Adventures of the Mad Chinaman</i>, in 2004.</p>
<p>In 2004, he joined <i><a href="/wiki/Singapore_Idol" title="Singapore Idol">Singapore Idol</a></i> as a judge alongside fellow Singaporeans Florence Lian and <a href="/wiki/Ken_Lim" title="Ken Lim">Ken Lim</a>. He also returned as a judge for Singapore Idol for the second and third seasons in 2006 and 2009 respectively.</p>
<p>On 17 and 18 December 2004, Lee held a 30th anniversary concert, titled <i>Life Stories</i> at the Kallang Theatre. Guest stars included Singapore Idol winner <a href="/wiki/Taufik_Batisah" title="Taufik Batisah">Taufik Batisah</a> and runner up <a href="/wiki/Sylvester_Sim" title="Sylvester Sim">Sylvester Sim</a>, Kumar, ex-wife Jacintha, Koh Chieng Mun, <a href="/wiki/Hossan_Leong" title="Hossan Leong">Hossan Leong</a>, and others.</p>
<p>In 2009, he penned the theme song for the <a href="/wiki/APEC_Singapore_2009" title="APEC Singapore 2009">APEC Singapore 2009</a> summit which was performed in front of world leaders such as <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> by Kit Chan during the Singapore Evening at the APEC Singapore 2009 summit on 14 November 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Esplanade_%E2%80%93_Theatres_on_the_Bay" title="Esplanade – Theatres on the Bay">Esplanade – Theatres on the Bay</a>. He was also the director of the 30-minute musical extravaganza involving 376 Singaporean artistes in a concert extravaganza enhanced by multimedia projections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, Lee was the Creative Director of Singapore's 44th National Day Parade.</p>
<p>In 2011, Lee returned with <i>The Adventures of the Mad China man</i>, a concert, and <i>Beauty Kings</i>, an original comedy play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Lee opened MAD (Modern Asian Diner) in Singapore. It is a 5-way partnership with 4 other homegrown local companies namely Tung Lok group, Bakerzin, Bar Stories and Top Wines. Lee also performed — and was a character — in <a href="/wiki/TheatreWorks_(Singapore)" title="TheatreWorks (Singapore)">TheatreWorks</a>’ <i>National Broadway Company</i> production for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esplanade_-_Theatres_on_the_Bay" title="Esplanade - Theatres on the Bay">Esplanade - Theatres on the Bay</a>'s 10th anniversary celebrations.</p>
<p>In 2013, Lee returned to musicals by composing the 90-minute <i>LightSeeker</i>, which premiered at <a href="/wiki/Resorts_World_Sentosa" title="Resorts World Sentosa">Resorts World Sentosa</a>. He was also named a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steinway" title="Steinway">Steinway</a> Artist and became Creative Director of the revamped <a href="/wiki/Rediffusion_Singapore" title="Rediffusion Singapore">Rediffusion Singapore</a> radio station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Lee held a concert, <i>Dick Lee: Celebrating 40 Years In Music</i>, at the Drama Centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In the same year, a revamped version of his 1997 musical <i>Hotpants</i> was restaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After his third stint as National Day Parade's Creative Director, Lee will return to helm 2015's golden jubilee show; and he has been tasked to write the next big National Day song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Dick Lee was the creative director of Singapore 50th National Day Parade, also he created 2015's NDP theme song, "Our Singapore" which will perform later on 9 Aug by JJ Lin.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fashion_Interest">Fashion Interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dick_Lee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fashion Interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Apart from music, Dick's other passion is for fashion, having studied fashion design at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harrow_School_of_Art" title="Harrow School of Art">Harrow School of Art</a> in London. His interest began at the early age of 16 when he designed for his mother's boutique Midteen.</p>
<p>His fashion career has taken him to Carrie Models, where he scouted and trained the top Singapore models, and designing his own labels for his boutique Ping Pong, as well as in Hemispheres, the first young designer store he set up with a partner.</p>
<p>Other forays in the fashion world include Display Director for Tangs departmental store in 1984, fashion editor of Female magazine in 1986 and one of the founders of the Society of Designing Arts, which spearheaded the introduction of Singapore designers to the local fashion market.</p>
<p>From 1982–1990, Dick also ran his own event company Runway Productions, which specialised in fashion and tourism events. His company also had a hand in the creation of the <i>Boom Boom Room</i>, a successful cabaret featuring the now renowned talents of drag comedian Kumar.</p>
<p>In January 2004, his composition Sandarken won the Best Malay Pop from the COMPASS Awards.</p>
<p>On the platform of his musical talent, Dick has always been actively involved in fund-raising projects for several beneficiaries including <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Repertory_Theatre" title="Singapore Repertory Theatre">Singapore Repertory Theatre</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kandang_Kerbau_Women%27s_and_Children%27s_Hospital" title="Kandang Kerbau Women's and Children's Hospital">KK Children's Hospital</a>, <a href="/wiki/Make-A-Wish_Foundation" title="Make-A-Wish Foundation">Make-A-Wish Foundation</a> and Church of St Ignatius.</p>
