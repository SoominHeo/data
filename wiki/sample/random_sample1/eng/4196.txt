<p><b>Richard Clifford</b> "<b>Dick</b>" <b>Taylor</b> (born 28 January 1943) is an English musician, best known as the guitarist and founding member of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Pretty_Things" title="The Pretty Things">The Pretty Things</a>.</p>
<p>Taylor was an early bassist for <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">the Rolling Stones</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rockhall_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-rockhall-1">[1]</a></sup> but left the band to become an art student at <a href="/wiki/Sidcup_Art_College" title="Sidcup Art College">Sidcup Art College</a>. Whilst there he formed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Pretty_Things" title="The Pretty Things">The Pretty Things</a> in September 1963. He now lives on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight" title="Isle of Wight">Isle of Wight</a>, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dick_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taylor was born in Livingstone Hospital, <a href="/wiki/Dartford" title="Dartford">Dartford</a>, Kent, England and he attended <a href="/wiki/Dartford_Grammar_School" title="Dartford Grammar School">Dartford Grammar School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-3">[3]</a></sup> In July 1962, while he was at <a href="/wiki/Sidcup_Art_College" title="Sidcup Art College">Sidcup Art College</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">the Rolling Stones</a> was formed when Taylor, <a href="/wiki/Mick_Jagger" title="Mick Jagger">Mick Jagger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keith_Richards" title="Keith Richards">Keith Richards</a>' three piece, Little Boy Blue and the Blue Boys joined <a href="/wiki/Brian_Jones" title="Brian Jones">Brian Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ian_Stewart_(musician)" title="Ian Stewart (musician)">Ian Stewart</a>'s Rollin' Stones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rockhall_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-rockhall-1">[1]</a></sup> Initially, Taylor played lead guitar in the band, but switched to bass to accommodate Jones, who quickly went on to become an accomplished <a href="/wiki/Slide_guitar" title="Slide guitar">slide guitarist</a>. In November 1962, Taylor left to return to art college, and the band-mates posted an advertisement seeking another bassist. <a href="/wiki/Bill_Wyman" title="Bill Wyman">Bill Wyman</a> responded, and succeeded him as The Rolling Stones' bassist in December 1962.</p>
<p>Later in 1963, Taylor formed The Pretty Things<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rockhall_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-rockhall-1">[1]</a></sup> with vocalist <a href="/wiki/Phil_May_(singer)" title="Phil May (singer)">Phil May</a> and once again played his preferred guitar position. He left the Pretty Things in 1969, after the release of their concept album <i><a href="/wiki/S.F._Sorrow" title="S.F. Sorrow">S.F. Sorrow</a></i>. In the following years, Taylor produced <a href="/wiki/Hawkwind" title="Hawkwind">Hawkwind</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hawkwind_(album)" title="Hawkwind (album)">debut album</a>, on which he also played guitar, as well as <a href="/wiki/Cochise_(band)" title="Cochise (band)">Cochise</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cochise_(album)" title="Cochise (album)">first album</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skin_Alley" title="Skin Alley">Skin Alley</a>'s first album. His contribution to <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Sound_recording_and_reproduction" title="Sound recording and reproduction">recording</a> by Auntie Pus, and during the second half of the 1980s Taylor played guitar with the English <a href="/wiki/Post-punk" title="Post-punk">post-punk</a> band <a href="/wiki/The_Mekons" title="The Mekons">the Mekons</a>. He recorded with <a href="/wiki/Andre_Williams" title="Andre Williams">Andre Williams</a> in Chicago, Illinois for <a href="/wiki/George_Paulus" title="George Paulus">George Paulus</a>' <a href="/wiki/St._George_Records" title="St. George Records">St. George Records</a>. The core members of The Pretty Things remain in the band to this day, and released the <i>Balboa Island</i> album in 2007 on the Cote Basque record label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
