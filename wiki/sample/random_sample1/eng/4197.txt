<p><b>Dylan Baker</b> (born October 7, 1959) is an American actor, known for playing supporting roles in both major studio and <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent films</a> along with regular work in television and on stage.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dylan_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baker was born in <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_New_York" title="Syracuse, New York">Syracuse, New York</a>, but was raised in <a href="/wiki/Lynchburg,_Virginia" title="Lynchburg, Virginia">Lynchburg, Virginia</a>. He began his acting career as a teenager in regional theater productions. He attended <a href="/wiki/Holy_Cross_Regional_Catholic_School_(Lynchburg,_Virginia)" title="Holy Cross Regional Catholic School (Lynchburg, Virginia)">Holy Cross Regional Catholic School</a> and then went on to attend Darlington School and finally graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_Preparatory_School" title="Georgetown Preparatory School">Georgetown Preparatory School</a> in 1976.</p>
<p>Baker attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/College_of_William_and_Mary" title="College of William and Mary">College of William and Mary</a> in Virginia and later graduated from <a href="/wiki/Southern_Methodist_University" title="Southern Methodist University">Southern Methodist University</a> in 1980. Baker then received a Masters in Fine Arts from the <a href="/wiki/Yale_School_of_Drama" title="Yale School of Drama">Yale School of Drama</a>, where he studied alongside <a href="/wiki/Chris_Noth" title="Chris Noth">Chris Noth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Clarkson" title="Patricia Clarkson">Patricia Clarkson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-www.tvguide.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-www.tvguide.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dylan_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baker's Broadway theatre credits include <i><a href="/wiki/Eastern_Standard" title="Eastern Standard">Eastern Standard</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/La_B%C3%AAte" title="La Bête">La Bête</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mauritius_(play)" title="Mauritius (play)">Mauritius</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/God_of_Carnage" title="God of Carnage">God of Carnage</a></i>. He won an <a href="/wiki/Obie_Award" title="Obie Award">Obie Award</a> in 1986 for his performance in the <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">off-Broadway</a> play <i><a href="/wiki/Not_About_Heroes" title="Not About Heroes">Not About Heroes</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-www.tvguide.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-www.tvguide.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The next year, he made his motion picture debut in the 1987 film <i><a href="/wiki/Planes,_Trains_and_Automobiles" title="Planes, Trains and Automobiles">Planes, Trains and Automobiles</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-www.tvguide.com_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-www.tvguide.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Baker's first recurring TV role was on <a href="/wiki/Steven_Bochco" title="Steven Bochco">Steven Bochco</a>'s highly acclaimed <i><a href="/wiki/Murder_One_(TV_series)" title="Murder One (TV series)">Murder One</a></i>. Since then, he has appeared in such TV series as <i><a href="/wiki/Northern_Exposure" title="Northern Exposure">Northern Exposure</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Criminal_Intent" title="Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent">Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Without_a_Trace" title="Without a Trace">Without a Trace</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_West_Wing" title="The West Wing">The West Wing</a></i>, and the short-lived sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pitts" title="The Pitts">The Pitts</a></i>.</p>
<p>He garnered major critical attention in 1998 with his performance as a tormented <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pedophile" title="Pedophile">pedophile</a> in <a href="/wiki/Todd_Solondz" title="Todd Solondz">Todd Solondz</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Happiness_(1998_film)" title="Happiness (1998 film)">Happiness</a></i>. He also gained notice for his portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Lizard_(comics)" title="Lizard (comics)">Dr. Curt Connors</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_2" title="Spider-Man 2">Spider-Man 2</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_3" title="Spider-Man 3">Spider-Man 3</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2000, he portrayed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_of_Defense" title="Secretary of Defense">Secretary of Defense</a> <a href="/wiki/Robert_McNamara" title="Robert McNamara">Robert McNamara</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Days_(film)" title="Thirteen Days (film)">Thirteen Days</a></i>, a historical drama about the <a href="/wiki/Cuban_Missile_Crisis" title="Cuban Missile Crisis">Cuban Missile Crisis</a>. He also held a small role in <i><a href="/wiki/Requiem_for_a_Dream" title="Requiem for a Dream">Requiem for a Dream</a></i>, where he played a doctor who sees <a href="/wiki/Jared_Leto" title="Jared Leto">Jared Leto</a>'s character with a severely infected, rotting arm. In 2002 he portrayed Alexander Rance, an accountant for the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Outfit" title="Chicago Outfit">Chicago Outfit</a>, in <i><a href="/wiki/Road_to_Perdition" title="Road to Perdition">Road to Perdition</a></i>.</p>
<p>During the short-lived 2007 series <i><a href="/wiki/Drive_(2007_TV_series)" title="Drive (2007 TV series)">Drive</a></i>, Baker played the role of John Trimble, a father suffering from a <a href="/wiki/Terminal_illness" title="Terminal illness">terminal illness</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, Baker played William Cross in <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Kings_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Kings (U.S. TV series)">Kings</a></i>, in which his wife <a href="/wiki/Becky_Ann_Baker" title="Becky Ann Baker">Becky Ann Baker</a> played Jessie Shepherd, the mother of <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">protagonist</a> David Shepherd. Baker also guest starred in an episode of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monk_(TV_Series)" title="Monk (TV Series)">Monk</a></i>, playing a theater critic in "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mr._Monk_and_the_Critic" title="Mr. Monk and the Critic">Mr. Monk and the Critic</a>".</p>
<p>Baker guest starred in the November 2010 <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/A_Pox_on_Our_House" title="A Pox on Our House">A Pox on Our House</a>".</p>
<p>Baker guest starred in the <a href="/wiki/Burn_Notice_(season_4)" title="Burn Notice (season 4)">season four</a> finale of <i><a href="/wiki/Burn_Notice" title="Burn Notice">Burn Notice</a></i> as Raines, an old spy friend of Michael's. He reprised the role in the season five premiere, and also permitted his likeness to be used in the <a href="/wiki/Burn_Notice" title="Burn Notice">Burn Notice</a> graphic novel "A New Day". In 2010, Baker played Hollis B. Chenery in <i><a href="/wiki/Secretariat_(film)" title="Secretariat (film)"><i>Secretariat</i></a>.</i></p>
<p>Baker played Pashto-speaking CIA agent Jerry Boorman in season 4 of <a href="/wiki/Damages_(TV_series)#Season_four" title="Damages (TV series)"><i>Damages</i></a>.</p>
<p>In 2012, Baker guest-starred in "<a href="/wiki/Upper_West_Side_Story" title="Upper West Side Story">Upper West Side Story</a>", an episode of the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/White_Collar_(TV_series)" title="White Collar (TV series)">White Collar</a></i>; he also has a recurring role as the father of <a href="/wiki/Katharine_McPhee" title="Katharine McPhee">Katharine McPhee</a>'s character Karen Cartwright on the NBC TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Smash_(TV_series)" title="Smash (TV series)">Smash</a></i>. He appeared in the USA Network miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Political_Animals_(miniseries)" title="Political Animals (miniseries)">Political Animals</a></i> which aired in summer 2012. For his performance as Colin Sweeney on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Wife" title="The Good Wife">The Good Wife</a></i>, he was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Guest_Actor_in_a_Drama_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actor in a Drama Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actor in a Drama Series</a> in 2010, 2012, and 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2014 it was announced that Baker will join <a href="/wiki/Helen_Mirren" title="Helen Mirren">Helen Mirren</a> on Broadway in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Audience_(2013_play)" title="The Audience (2013 play)">The Audience</a></i>. The play depicts meetings between Queen Elizabeth II and important historical figures. Baker will play former Prime Minister John Major. The play opened on March 8, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Audiobooks">Audiobooks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dylan_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Audiobooks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Baker is a prolific narrator of <a href="/wiki/Audiobook" title="Audiobook">audiobooks</a>, having narrated a wide range of books, from fiction (<i><a href="/wiki/The_Grapes_of_Wrath" title="The Grapes of Wrath">The Grapes of Wrath</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Corrections" title="The Corrections">The Corrections</a></i>) to biographies (<i><a href="/wiki/Steve_Jobs_(book)" title="Steve Jobs (book)">Steve Jobs</a></i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> to <i><a href="/wiki/Canadian_Caper" title="Canadian Caper">Argo</a></i>, the true story of how six Americans were rescued from Iran, by the CIA and Hollywood. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dylan garnered the 2002 Audie Award for Abridged Fiction for his reading of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Corrections" title="The Corrections">The Corrections</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Franzen" title="Jonathan Franzen">Jonathan Franzen</a>. Baker has also recorded <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Franzen" title="Jonathan Franzen">Jonathan Franzen</a>'s latest novel, (<i><a href="/wiki/Purity_(novel)" title="Purity (novel)">Purity</a></i>).</p>
<p><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dylan_Baker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1990, he married actress Becky Gelke, now known professionally as <a href="/wiki/Becky_Ann_Baker" title="Becky Ann Baker">Becky Ann Baker</a>. They have a daughter, Willa, and reside in New York City.</p>
<p>On September 1, 2015, Baker tried to save the life of his neighbor, Broadway and movie actress and dancer Vivien Eng, in the high-rise where he lives in New York City when her apartment caught fire but was driven back by smoke and fire. Firefighters eventually got the seriously injured woman out of the apartment and rushed to the hospital. Unfortunately her injuries were too severe and she succumbed to her injuries September 3, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
