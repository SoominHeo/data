<p>The <b>Women's sprint <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing" title="Cross-country skiing">cross-country skiing</a></b> competition in the classical technique at the <b><a href="/wiki/2010_Winter_Olympics" title="2010 Winter Olympics">2010 Winter Olympics</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> was held on 17 February at <a href="/wiki/Whistler_Olympic_Park" title="Whistler Olympic Park">Whistler Olympic Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Whistler,_British_Columbia" title="Whistler, British Columbia">Whistler, British Columbia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sked_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sked-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Chandra_Crawford" title="Chandra Crawford">Chandra Crawford</a> was the defending <a href="/wiki/2006_Winter_Olympics" title="2006 Winter Olympics">Olympic</a> champion in this event, though that event was in the freestyle technique.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Arianna_Follis" title="Arianna Follis">Arianna Follis</a> was the defending <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing_at_the_FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships_2009" title="Cross-country skiing at the FIS Nordic World Ski Championships 2009">world champion</a> in this event which was also held in freestyle technique.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alena_Proch%C3%A1zkov%C3%A1" title="Alena Procházková">Alena Procházková</a> of <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a> won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008-09_Cross-Country_Skiing_World_Cup" title="2008-09 Cross-Country Skiing World Cup">test event</a> that took place at Olympic venue on 16 January 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The last <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Cross-Country_Skiing_World_Cup" title="2009–10 Cross-Country Skiing World Cup">World Cup</a> event prior to the 2010 Games in this format took place on 6 February 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Canmore,_Alberta" title="Canmore, Alberta">Canmore, Alberta</a> and was won by <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Justyna_Kowalczyk" title="Justyna Kowalczyk">Justyna Kowalczyk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Follis did not qualify for the event at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_Olympics" title="Winter Olympics">Winter Olympics</a>. Crawford and Procházková were eliminated in the quarterfinals. Kowalczyk won silver. It was Bjørgen's second medal at these <a href="/wiki/2010_Winter_Olympics" title="2010 Winter Olympics">Olympics</a> and first gold for her. Majdič won her first Olympic medal and the first for <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a> in cross-country skiing. During the warm-up she crashed down a bank, into an unprotected 3 m (9.8 ft) deep gorge and landed on ice and rocks, sustaining five broken ribs and a <a href="/wiki/Pneumothorax" title="Pneumothorax">pneumothorax</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtvslo_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-rtvslo-6">[6]</a></sup> In spite of agonizing pain, she finished third in the sprint a few hours later, winning the first individual Winter Olympic medal for Slovenia in 16 years and its first ever in <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Cross-country skiing at the Winter Olympics">cross-country skiing at the Winter Olympics</a>. She earned the highest praise from other skiers, staff and the media for her amazing bravery<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> as she attended the medal giving ceremony with a tube in her chest to relieve pneumothorax.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rtvslo_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-rtvslo-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
