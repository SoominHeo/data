<p>The <b>Gnaphalieae</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Tribe_(biology)" title="Tribe (biology)">tribe</a> of flowering plants in the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Asteraceae" title="Asteraceae">Asteraceae</a>. It is most closely related to the tribes <a href="/wiki/Anthemideae" title="Anthemideae">Anthemideae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astereae" title="Astereae">Astereae</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Calenduleae" title="Calenduleae">Calenduleae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-panero_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-panero-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gnaphalieae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This group is most diverse in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. It is sometimes commonly called the <b>pussy's-toes tribe</b>. There are only a few genera in the northern hemisphere, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Antennaria" title="Antennaria">Antennaria</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Leontopodium" title="Leontopodium">Leontopodium</a></i> (Edelweiss), and <i><a href="/wiki/Anaphalis" title="Anaphalis">Anaphalis</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bayer_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bayer-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The classification of the tribe into subtribes is unclear, with a number of past classifications not being supported by late 20th-century evidence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bayer_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-bayer-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
