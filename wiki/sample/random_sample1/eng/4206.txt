<p><b>Tân Ấp Railway Station</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/North%E2%80%93South_Railway_(Vietnam)" title="North–South Railway (Vietnam)">North–South Railway</a> (Reunification Express) line in <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>. It serves the town of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=T%C3%A2n_%E1%BA%A4p&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tân Ấp (page does not exist)">Tân Ấp</a> in <a href="/wiki/Qu%E1%BA%A3ng_B%C3%ACnh_Province" title="Quảng Bình Province">Quảng Bình Province</a>. From 1933, the station also served as a terminus of the Tân Ấp–Xóm Cục railway, the only stretch of railway opened as part of the aborted Thakhek–Tân Ấp railway.</p>
<p>It is uncertain when this stretch of railway was closed, but part, if not all of the railway was reopened by <a href="/wiki/North_Vietnam" title="North Vietnam">North Vietnam</a> in the 1960s as part of their efforts to aid <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vietcong" title="Vietcong">Vietcong</a> guerillas travelling southwards along the <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_trail" title="Ho Chi Minh trail">Hồ Chí Minh trail</a>, which begins just south of Xóm Cục in the <a href="/wiki/M%E1%BB%A5_Gi%E1%BA%A1_Pass" title="Mụ Giạ Pass">Mụ Giạ Pass</a>.</p>
