<p>A <i><b>ttukbaegi</b></i> is <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Ceramic_glaze" title="Ceramic glaze">unglazed</a> <a href="/wiki/Earthenware" title="Earthenware">earthenware</a> <a href="/wiki/Pottery" title="Pottery">pot</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> used to cook or serve <i><a href="/wiki/Jjigae" title="Jjigae">jjigae</a></i> (stew), <i><a href="/wiki/Guk" title="Guk">guk</a></i> (soup), or other boiled dishes in <a href="/wiki/Korean_cuisine" title="Korean cuisine">Korean cuisine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Made from coarse sand-mud.</p>
