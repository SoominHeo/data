<p><b>Ttukseom Resort Station</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">뚝섬유원지역</span>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_transit_station" title="Rapid transit station">rapid transit station</a> on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Subway_Line_7" title="Seoul Subway Line 7">Seoul Subway Line 7</a>. It is located in <a href="/wiki/Noyu-dong" title="Noyu-dong">Noyu-dong</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwangjin-gu" title="Gwangjin-gu">Gwangjin-gu</a> administrative district of <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>. It is the only station in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> to be located on the underside of a bridge, beneath <a href="/wiki/Cheongdam_Bridge" title="Cheongdam Bridge">Cheongdam Bridge</a>. The station also runs underneath a highway with two exits being south of the highway and two north. The south exits serve <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ttukseom_Resort&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ttukseom Resort (page does not exist)">Ttukseom Resort</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> while the north exits serve Noyu-dong which is administered by and consists of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jayang_3-dong" title="Jayang 3-dong">Jayang 3-dong</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jayang_4-dong" title="Jayang 4-dong">Jayang 4-dong</a> which contain some <a href="/wiki/Mixed-use_development" title="Mixed-use development">residential towers with small shops</a> and other mixed commercial and residential areas. The station is serviced by a single bus route which is located at exit 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Before Line 7 opened the station was known as Jayang Station (자양역).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ttukseom_Resort_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ttukseom Resort Station was opened on August 1, 2000. It was part of the last section of Line 7 to be completed creating a continuous track from <a href="/wiki/Jangam_Station" title="Jangam Station">Jangam Station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Onsu_Station" title="Onsu Station">Onsu Station</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ttukseom_Resort_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is elevated with 2 side platforms. At either end of the station are two exits; however, the station is not continuous. It is interrupted by the highway that runs perpendicular to it. There are elevators at both ends of the station. The south part of the station exits directly into Ttukseom Resort. The resort is a river park which has been open since October 2009. The park features a <a href="/wiki/Musical_fountain" title="Musical fountain">lit fountain</a>, swimming pool, restaurants, boat rentals, walking paths, <a href="/wiki/Climbing_wall" title="Climbing wall">climbing wall</a> and bicycle rentals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the past, festivals have been held in the area<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and it has been the home of the Beautiful Flea Market since 2004 which is run by The Beautiful Store.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The north side of the platform allows access to the only bus route serving the station. As well it provides direct access to Noyu-dong. The area immediately around the station is mainly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High-rise" title="High-rise">high-rise</a> residential towers with commercial space on the lower floors. While Jayang 4-dong contains primarily <a href="/wiki/Low-rise" title="Low-rise">low-rise</a> residential and mixed-use development, Jayang 3-dong is mainly high-rise development.</p>
