<p><b>La Roux</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/uː/ long 'oo' in 'food'">uː</span></span>/</a></span></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i>lah-<b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">ROO</span></span></b></i></a></span>) is an English <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> act formed in 2006 by singer <b>Eleanor "Elly" Jackson</b> and record producer Ben Langmaid. Their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/La_Roux_(album)" title="La Roux (album)">La Roux</a></i> (2009) was a critical and commercial success, winning a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> and producing hit singles such as "<a href="/wiki/In_for_the_Kill_(song)" title="In for the Kill (song)">In for the Kill</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Bulletproof_(La_Roux_song)" title="Bulletproof (La Roux song)">Bulletproof</a>". Recording of a follow-up album was marred by unsuccessful collaborations, the cancellation of two planned release dates, and reported conflict between the duo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NME.com-1">[1]</a></sup> Langmaid ultimately left the group, and Jackson released a second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Trouble_in_Paradise_(La_Roux_album)" title="Trouble in Paradise (La Roux album)">Trouble in Paradise</a></i>, in 2014, maintaining the former duo's name.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Roux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9310:_Beginnings_and_self-titled_album">2006–10: Beginnings and self-titled album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Roux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2006–10: Beginnings and self-titled album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, Jackson and Langmaid were introduced by a mutual friend. Their first project was named "Automan" and they wrote largely <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_music" title="Acoustic music">acoustic music</a> due to Jackson's great admiration of acts like <a href="/wiki/Nick_Drake" title="Nick Drake">Nick Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joni_Mitchell" title="Joni Mitchell">Joni Mitchell</a>. Her interest later shifted to electronic music,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LaRouxbio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-LaRouxbio-2">[2]</a></sup> drawing inspiration from acts such as <a href="/wiki/Orchestral_Manoeuvres_in_the_Dark" title="Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark">Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The band's name refers to Jackson's red hair and tomboyish appearance, mingling the masculine ("le roux") and feminine ("la rousse") <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> terms; she has said: "To me, it means 'red-haired one'—and it does, vaguely. It's just a male version of 'red-haired one'".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>La Roux's debut single, "<a href="/wiki/Quicksand_(La_Roux_song)" title="Quicksand (La Roux song)">Quicksand</a>", was released by French independent record label <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitsun%C3%A9_Music" title="Kitsuné Music">Kitsuné Music</a> in December 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LaRouxbio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-LaRouxbio-2">[2]</a></sup> They then signed to <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">Polydor Records</a> in order to release their debut album. La Roux's second single, "<a href="/wiki/In_for_the_Kill_(song)" title="In for the Kill (song)">In for the Kill</a>", was released on 16 March 2009. Later, two prominent <a href="/wiki/Dubstep" title="Dubstep">dubstep</a> producers, <a href="/wiki/Skream" title="Skream">Skream</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skrillex" title="Skrillex">Skrillex</a>, created remixes of the track.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Another remix was made by Lifelike.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> "In for the Kill" debuted at number eleven on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> on 22 March 2009, peaking at number two four weeks later. "<a href="/wiki/Bulletproof_(La_Roux_song)" title="Bulletproof (La Roux song)">Bulletproof</a>", later remixed by dubstep producer and DJ Chrispy, was released on 21 June 2009 and debuted at number one on the UK Singles Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scala_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scala-8">[8]</a></sup> The song was released on 11 August 2009 in the United States and topped the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Songs" title="Hot Dance Club Songs">Hot Dance Club Songs</a> chart the week of 17 September. As of 11 June 2010 it had peaked at number 8 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and had sold over 2.0 million copies. The band's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/La_Roux_(album)" title="La Roux (album)">La Roux</a></i>, peaked at 70 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and at number four on the Dance/Electronic chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Not_Your_Toy" title="I'm Not Your Toy">I'm Not Your Toy</a>", released on 29 September 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> reached number twenty-seven on the UK chart. On 10 September 2009, Jackson confirmed in an interview with French musical website Hall Musique that "As If by Magic" would be the fifth single to be released from the album,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and on 15 July 2010, she uploaded a behind-the-scenes preview of the music video for "Tigerlily" on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> discussing its theme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> However, neither "As If By Magic" nor "Tigerlily" were ever issued as singles, and the full "Tigerlily" video also remains unreleased.</p>
<p>In order to promote <i>La Roux</i>, the band was the supporting act on <a href="/wiki/Lily_Allen" title="Lily Allen">Lily Allen</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lily_Allen_concert_tour_(2009)" title="Lily Allen concert tour (2009)">UK tour</a> of March 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> La Roux headlined the Samsung NME Radar Tour 2009 and played alongside <a href="/wiki/Magistrates_(band)" title="Magistrates (band)">Magistrates</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heartbreak_(band)" title="Heartbreak (band)">Heartbreak</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> They were scheduled to perform at the <a href="/wiki/Glastonbury_Festival" title="Glastonbury Festival">Glastonbury</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reading_Festival" title="Reading Festival">Reading</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oxegen" title="Oxegen">Oxegen</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeds_Festival" title="Leeds Festival">Leeds</a> festivals as well as the <a href="/wiki/Scala_(club)" title="Scala (club)">Scala</a>, Southend Chinnerys and Coventry Kasbah venues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scala_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Scala-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In July and August 2009, La Roux toured North America. The eight-city tour included performances at <a href="/wiki/Osheaga_Festival" title="Osheaga Festival">Osheaga Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/All_Points_West_Music_%26_Arts_Festival" title="All Points West Music &amp; Arts Festival">All Points West Music &amp; Arts Festival</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On 23 July, the group performed on the late-night talk show <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The group returned to North America for a seven-date tour in October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The group sold out their two Australian dates within minutes forcing an upgrade in venues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In November, the duo embarked on an eleven-date United Kingdom and Ireland tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> La Roux gained fifth position on the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Sound_of..." title="Sound of...">Sound of 2009</a> poll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> website featured La Roux on their New Band of the Day column.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <i>The Guardian</i> also featured La Roux as one of the "Best New Acts of 2009" in January of that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian20090118_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian20090118-24">[24]</a></sup> The album has been nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Prize" title="Mercury Prize">Mercury Prize</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mercury_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mercury-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sales of "In for the Kill" spiked 600 percent when Skream's "Let's Get Ravey" remix of the song appeared on the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i> in August 2010. Later that month, it was announced that an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> remix has been released in tandem with the November US tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In October, a second video for the song was released that was shot at New York's landmark <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Chelsea" title="Hotel Chelsea">Hotel Chelsea</a>. The song was released in the United States 7 October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.932015:_Departure_of_Langmaid_and_Trouble_in_Paradise">2011–2015: Departure of Langmaid and <i>Trouble in Paradise</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Roux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011–2015: Departure of Langmaid and Trouble in Paradise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2011, at the <a href="/wiki/53rd_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="53rd Annual Grammy Awards">53rd Annual Grammy Awards</a>, La Roux won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Dance/Electronica_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Dance/Electronica Album">Grammy Award for Best Electronic/Dance Album</a> for their self-titled debut album. Their single "In for the Kill" was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Dance_Recording" title="Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording">Best Dance Recording</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grammy53_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grammy53-28">[28]</a></sup> Jackson wrote on Twitter "This is the happiest I think I've ever been". This contrasts Elly Jackson's previous comments at the 2010 NME Awards, where she lightheartedly stated that the group "rarely wins awards". Ben Langmaid stated in a post-award interview, that the day before the Grammy Awards, the duo were rewarded with a double platinum set of discs from their record company, describing the weekend of the Grammy Awards as "My happiest weekend ever". In 2010, Jackson added vocals to <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>'s platinum-selling album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Beautiful_Dark_Twisted_Fantasy" title="My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy">My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy</a></i>, which was released on 22 November 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Jackson contributed vocals to West's debut album with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Watch_the_Throne" title="Watch the Throne">Watch the Throne</a></i>, on the track "That's My Bitch". The album was released on 9 August 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In addition to her appearance on Kanye West's album in 2010, Jackson performed on <a href="/wiki/Chromeo" title="Chromeo">Chromeo</a>'s third album <i><a href="/wiki/Business_Casual_(Chromeo_album)" title="Business Casual (Chromeo album)">Business Casual</a></i>, providing chorus vocals for the track "Hot Mess".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>La Roux were scheduled to start writing material for their second album during Christmas 2009 at their manager's home. Jackson said, "I think there will be a natural instrument or two on there though, which will make a change from the first record." She added that due to limited time, she did not think there would be "anything in terms of a second record for a while".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> As of January 2011, La Roux were writing their second album with a somewhat established <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> band whose members are friends of Jackson. Jackson told the <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i>, "I know I'm not going to stop writing until we've got songs that can compete on the same level as 'In For The Kill' and 'Bulletproof'".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> In January 2012, Jackson described the second album as "a lot cheekier. Not '<a href="/wiki/Cheeky_Song_(Touch_My_Bum)" title="Cheeky Song (Touch My Bum)">Touch My Bum</a>' cheeky or <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carry_On_(film_series)" title="Carry On (film series)">Carry On</a></i> cheeky—just slightly suggestive [...] A lot of music that's quite sexual is aggressive, but the music itself doesn't sound sexy—it's more people shouting, 'Look at my bum! Look!' I'm not turned on by it, I'm terrified." She also noted she felt a lot of pressure to replicate the quality of the duo's debut album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Unlike the first album, album two was said to draw more on acoustic sounds and instruments, including guitars, organs and percussion; however, analog and digital synthesizers would still feature as part of the overall sound. Jackson confirmed she would be playing instruments on the record, in addition to co-writing, co-producing and providing vocals.</p>
<p>La Roux started a low-key comeback tour on 28 March 2013 in Brighton with two more venues in Bristol and Coventry during the following week. It was confirmed via a copy of the set list that there were four new songs: "Uptight Downtown", "Kiss and Not Tell", "Sexotheque" and "Tropical Chancer". Early reactions to the new material were positive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Proof of a new album surfaced on 21 March 2014, when <a href="/wiki/Tim_Jonze" title="Tim Jonze">Tim Jonze</a>, music columnist for <i>The Guardian</i>, tweeted that he "heard the new La Roux album yesterday and it was bloody brilliant." The official La Roux page responded with "Thanks!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> On 8 April 2014, La Roux announced their second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Trouble_in_Paradise_(La_Roux_album)" title="Trouble in Paradise (La Roux album)">Trouble in Paradise</a></i>, would be released on 21 July 2014, preceded by buzz single "Let Me Down Gently", which premiered on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> on 12 May 2014. During the promotions for the album, Jackson revealed that she and Langmaid had parted ways, and she was now the sole member of La Roux.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NME.com-1">[1]</a></sup> Langmaid responded that he was "truly saddened" by the split, and added that he was still a fixture in the album's writing, including "Let Me Down Gently", which both he and Jackson enjoyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015 Jackson provided joint lead vocals for the tracks "Tutti Frutti" and "People on the High Line", along with backing vocals on "Plastic" for <a href="/wiki/New_Order_(band)" title="New Order (band)">New Order</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Complete" title="Music Complete">Music Complete</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Roux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Elly Jackson's first interest was in <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk music</a>. She was particularly interested in <a href="/wiki/Carole_King" title="Carole King">Carole King</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Drake" title="Nick Drake">Nick Drake</a> whose music she discovered in her parents' record collection. Jackson's early musical material consisted of folk ditties influenced by <a href="/wiki/Joni_Mitchell" title="Joni Mitchell">Joni Mitchell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Her taste in music changed during her mid-teens when she became involved in the <a href="/wiki/Rave" title="Rave">rave</a> scene. She lists <a href="/wiki/Gerry_Rafferty" title="Gerry Rafferty">Gerry Rafferty</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>, <a href="/wiki/Depeche_Mode" title="Depeche Mode">Depeche Mode</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Eurythmics" title="Eurythmics">Eurythmics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenny_Wilson_(singer)" title="Jenny Wilson (singer)">Jenny Wilson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Knife" title="The Knife">The Knife</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> as her influences. In July 2010, La Roux curated and mixed the latest volume of <i>Sidetracked</i>, a compilation series from <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_(club)" title="Renaissance (club)">Renaissance</a> that allows artists from the electronic field free rein to showcase their musical interests. The mix eclectically features the likes of <a href="/wiki/Japan_(band)" title="Japan (band)">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heaven_17" title="Heaven 17">Heaven 17</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joyce_Sims" title="Joyce Sims">Joyce Sims</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karin_Dreijer_Andersson" title="Karin Dreijer Andersson">Fever Ray</a> (lead singer of <a href="/wiki/The_Knife" title="The Knife">The Knife</a>), <a href="/wiki/Doris_Troy" title="Doris Troy">Doris Troy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tears_for_Fears" title="Tears for Fears">Tears for Fears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gerry_Rafferty" title="Gerry Rafferty">Gerry Rafferty</a> and many more of their influences. It also includes an exclusive recording of <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Under_My_Thumb" title="Under My Thumb">Under My Thumb</a>", a cover La Roux have been featuring in their then-recent live sets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jackson is unhappy with what she perceives as a "normality" fashion trend among a majority of today's musical acts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Her androgynous clothing and hair style has been compared to the 1980s group <a href="/wiki/A_Flock_of_Seagulls" title="A Flock of Seagulls">A Flock of Seagulls</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LesterPaul_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-LesterPaul-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Jackson strongly opposes stylists who try to make her appearance more classically feminine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Members">Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Roux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Eleanor Kate "Elly" Jackson</b> (born 12 March 1988)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> is an English singer, songwriter and the sole member of La Roux, a former <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> duo which is now a solo project. Jackson is well known for having reddish toned hair and <a href="/wiki/Androgyny" title="Androgyny">androgynous</a> style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Jackson has a <a href="/wiki/Soprano" title="Soprano">soprano</a> vocal range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> Jackson was born in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bill" title="The Bill">The Bill</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Emmerdale" title="Emmerdale">Emmerdale</a></i> actress <a href="/wiki/Trudie_Goodwin" title="Trudie Goodwin">Trudie Goodwin</a> and actor Kit Jackson, and has an older sister named Jessica. Jackson's great grandfather was <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Bernard" title="Anthony Bernard">Anthony Bernard</a>, founder of the London Chamber Orchestra and first musical director of the Shakespeare Memorial Theatre in Stratford upon Avon. Jackson attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pimlico_School" title="Pimlico School">Pimlico School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sydenham_High_School" title="Sydenham High School">Sydenham High School</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Russell_School" title="Royal Russell School">Royal Russell School</a>. Jackson has accumulated a lesbian cult following. Jackson was frequently bullied in school because of appearance and weight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Touring_members">Touring members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Roux&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Touring members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mickey O'Brien is the longest member of Elly's live band, having joined in 2008. O'Brien was born in London, England. She played in a punk/rock bands, and she was involved in the London scene at the time with The Libertines. Mickey is also the younger cousin of <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ed_O%27Brien" title="Ed O'Brien">Ed O'Brien</a>. Mickey also is a few generations related from the socialist James Gralton, who Ken Loach made the film of; Jimmy's Hall. Both their families still reside in County Leitrim, Ireland. She currently is working on her own solo material and also composes scores - ongoing work comprises her writing and producing the music for a documentary: Dispossession: The Great Social Housing Swindle; made by the makers of Invisible Britain Sleaford Mods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> She also wrote music for aspiring writer/director Vivian Tse's short film: Seclusion <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>The rest of the live band consisted of William, Ed and Matty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> They were fired in 2016 to make way for a new line-up (with Mickey).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>The latest line-up in 2016 comprises Seye from Gorillaz, Gbenga from Metronomy and Jay Sharrock from Miles Kane's band as well as Mickey<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>Former member Ben Langmaid did not participate in the music videos or live performances with Jackson. Langmaid, a London-based producer and composer, has been involved with various other music projects in the past. He was a DJ throughout the 1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Savage_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-Savage-60">[60]</a></sup> He has a close association with <a href="/wiki/Rollo_Armstrong" title="Rollo Armstrong">Rollo Armstrong</a> of <a href="/wiki/Faithless" title="Faithless">Faithless</a>; the two musicians went to the same school and later collaborated as Huff &amp; Puff in the mid-1990s,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-61">[61]</a></sup> releasing the <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a> single 'Help Me Make It' in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LaRouxbio_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-LaRouxbio-2">[2]</a></sup> Langmaid also recorded as Atomic and was one half of Huff &amp; Herb,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_61-1"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-61">[61]</a></sup> before contributing as a songwriter to <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Kubb_(band)" title="Kubb (band)">Kubb</a>'s debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Mother_(Kubb_album)" title="Mother (Kubb album)">Mother</a></i>, released in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LaRouxbio_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-LaRouxbio-2">[2]</a></sup> This project was again linked to Rollo Armstrong; Kubb's frontman Harry Collier was introduced to Langmaid after Collier sang '<a href="/wiki/Happy_Birthday_to_You" title="Happy Birthday to You">Happy Birthday to You</a>' to Armstrong whilst working as a waiter in a <a href="/wiki/North_London" title="North London">North London</a> organic café.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KubbBio_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-KubbBio-62">[62]</a></sup> Langmaid turned down the offer of a permanent position in Kubb.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KubbBio_62-1"><a href="#cite_note-KubbBio-62">[62]</a></sup> In 2011, Langmaid wrote material for the early 1990s <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>-styled girl group War of Words.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Savage_60-1"><a href="#cite_note-Savage-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
