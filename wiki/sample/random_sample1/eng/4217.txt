<p>The <b>Mercat de Sant Josep de la Boqueria</b>, often simply referred to as <b>La Boqueria</b> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Catalan" title="Help:IPA for Catalan">[ɫa bɔkəˈɾiə]</a></span> is a large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_market" title="Public market">public market</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ciutat_Vella" title="Ciutat Vella">Ciutat Vella</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and one of the city's foremost tourist landmarks, with an entrance from <a href="/wiki/La_Rambla,_Barcelona" title="La Rambla, Barcelona">La Rambla</a>, not far from the <a href="/wiki/Liceu" title="Liceu">Liceu</a>, Barcelona's <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> house. The market has a very diverse selection of goods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=La_Boqueria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first mention of the Boqueria market in Barcelona dates from 1217, when tables were installed near the old city gate to sell meat. From December 1470 onwards, a pig market was held at this site; at this time it was known as Mercat Bornet. Later, until 1794, it was known simply as Mercat de la Palla, or straw market. In the beginning, the market was not enclosed and had no official status, being regarded simply as an extension of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pla%C3%A7a_Nova&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Plaça Nova (page does not exist)">Plaça Nova</a> market, which extended to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pla%C3%A7a_del_Pi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Plaça del Pi (page does not exist)">Plaça del Pi</a>. The current name is believed to derive from the Catalan <i>boc</i>, meaning "goat", therefore a <i>boqueria</i> would be a place where <a href="/wiki/Goat_meat" title="Goat meat">goat meat</a> is sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later, the authorities decided to construct a separate market on <a href="/wiki/La_Rambla,_Barcelona" title="La Rambla, Barcelona">La Rambla</a>, housing mainly <a href="/wiki/Fishmonger" title="Fishmonger">fishmongers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Butcher" title="Butcher">butchers</a>. It was not until 1826 that the market was legally recognized, and a convention held in 1835 decided to build an official structure. Construction began on March 19, 1840 under the direction of the architect <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mas_Vil%C3%A0&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mas Vilà (page does not exist)">Mas Vilà</a>. The market officially opened in the same year, but the plans for the building were modified many times. The inauguration of the structure finally took place in 1853. A new fish market opened in 1911, and the metal roof that still exists today was constructed in 1914.</p>
