<p><b>Lago Verde</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_Chile" title="Communes of Chile">commune</a> located at the headwaters of the <a href="/wiki/Cisnes_River" title="Cisnes River">Cisnes River</a> in <a href="/wiki/Coyhaique_Province" title="Coyhaique Province">Coyhaique Province</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ais%C3%A9n_Region" title="Aisén Region">Aisén Region</a>. The commune is administered by the municipality of Lago Verde, the principal settlement.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lago_Verde,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the time of European arrival, the indigenous people in the area were the <a href="/wiki/Tehuelche_people" title="Tehuelche people">Tehuelche</a>, who were nomadic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hunter_gatherer" title="Hunter gatherer">hunter gatherers</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hist_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hist-8">[8]</a></sup> however, the area has been occupied since at least 2,800 <a href="/wiki/Before_Present" title="Before Present">BP</a>, and likely back as far as the end of the <a href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">last ice age</a> (11,400 BP).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Some Europeans settled in the area in the early twentieth century, beginning in 1914 there were settlers in the upper reaches of the <a href="/wiki/Cisnes_River" title="Cisnes River">Cisnes River</a>. The grant for the <a href="/wiki/Estancia" title="Estancia">estancia</a> "Sociedad Ganadera Cisnes" of over 1,000 square kilometres (386 sq mi) was made in the early 1920s. It encompassed almost all of what is now the La Tapera district. Nonetheless, the area was not formally organized until the establishment of a settlement, Villa La Tapera, on 26 December 1926.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hist_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hist-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1936 land for the establishment of the town of Lago Verde was set aside. In 1950, a young Frenchman, Edward Simon. purchased several hectares in the town and established a hotel, the "Cacique Blanco", with the ranching and farming activities to support it. In 1966 Villa La Tapera achieved town status. In 1979 the commune of Lago Verde was created. In 1981 Lago Verde became a municipality. And in 1982 Villa Amengual was founded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hist_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hist-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1992 the road to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=La_Junta,_Ays%C3%A9n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="La Junta, Aysén (page does not exist)">La Junta</a> was completed with a bridge over the Rosselot River. This increased communications within the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hist_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-Hist-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lago_Verde,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Statistics_Institute_(Chile)" title="National Statistics Institute (Chile)">National Statistics Institute</a>, Lago Verde has 869 inhabitants, with an area of 5,622.3 km<sup>2</sup> (2,171 sq mi) which makes the commune an entirely <a href="/wiki/Rural_area" title="Rural area">rural area</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INE_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-INE-5">[5]</a></sup> The population of the commune has been declining; it was 1,062 in the 2002 census, and 1,168 in the 1992 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Comuna-Plan_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Comuna-Plan-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lago_Verde,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a commune, Lago Verde is a third-level <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_division_of_Chile" title="Administrative division of Chile">administrative division of Chile</a> administered by a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council" title="Municipal council">municipal council</a>, headed by an <a href="/wiki/Alcalde" title="Alcalde">alcalde</a> who is directly elected every four years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Munitel_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Munitel-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Official_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Official-3">[3]</a></sup> The 2012-2016 alcalde is Nelson Opazo López.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The presious governor was Gaspar Aldea Cadagán.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Comuna-Plan_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Comuna-Plan-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_of_Chile" title="Electoral divisions of Chile">electoral divisions of Chile</a>, Lago Verde is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Chile" title="Chamber of Deputies of Chile">Chamber of Deputies</a> by René Alinco (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Party" title="Christian Democratic Party">PDC</a>) and David Sandoval (<a href="/wiki/Independent_Democratic_Union" title="Independent Democratic Union">UDI</a>) as part of the 59th electoral district, which includes the entire <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ais%C3%A9n_Region" title="Aisén Region">Aisén Region</a>. The commune is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Chile" title="Senate of Chile">Senate</a> by Antonio Horvath Kiss (RN) and Patricio Walker Prieto (PDC) as part of the 18th senatorial constituency (Aisén Region).</p>
