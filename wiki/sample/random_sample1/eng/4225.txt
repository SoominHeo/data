<p>The <a href="/wiki/Graphics_processing_unit" title="Graphics processing unit">graphics processing unit</a> (GPU) codenamed <b>Radeon R600</b> is the foundation of the <b><a href="/wiki/Radeon" title="Radeon">Radeon</a> HD 2000</b> series and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FireGL" title="FireGL">FireGL</a> 2007 series <a href="/wiki/Video_card" title="Video card">video cards</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/ATI_Technologies" title="ATI Technologies">ATI Technologies</a>. The HD 2000 cards competed with nVidia's <a href="/wiki/GeForce_8_series" title="GeForce 8 series">GeForce 8 series</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This article is about all products under the <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brand</a> "Radeon HD 2000 Series". They all contain a GPU which implements <a href="/wiki/TeraScale_(microarchitecture)#TeraScale_1" title="TeraScale (microarchitecture)">TeraScale 1</a>, ATI's first <a href="/wiki/Unified_shader_model" title="Unified shader model">Unified shader model</a> microarchitecture for PCs.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_acceleration">Video acceleration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Video acceleration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Unified_Video_Decoder" title="Unified Video Decoder">Unified Video Decoder</a> (UVD) <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor_intellectual_property_core" title="Semiconductor intellectual property core">SIP core</a> is on-<a href="/wiki/Die_(integrated_circuit)" title="Die (integrated circuit)">die</a> in the HD 2400 and the HD 2600. The HD 2900 GPU dice do not have a UVD core, as its <a href="/wiki/Stream_processing" title="Stream processing">stream processors</a> were powerful enough to handle most of the steps of video acceleration in its stead except for entropy decoding and bitstream processing which are left for the CPU to perform.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_features">Other features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>HDTV encoding support is implemented via the integrated AMD Xilleon encoder; the companion <i>Rage Theater</i> chip used on the Radeon X1000 series was replaced with the digital <i>Theater 200</i> chip, providing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Video_In_Video_Out" title="Video In Video Out">VIVO</a> capabilities.</p>
<p>For display outputs, all variants include two dual-link <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TMDS" title="TMDS">TMDS</a> transmitters, except for HD 2400 and HD 3400, which include one single and one dual-link <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TMDS" title="TMDS">TMDS</a> transmitters. Each DVI output includes dual-link <a href="/wiki/High-bandwidth_Digital_Content_Protection" title="High-bandwidth Digital Content Protection">HDCP</a> encoder with on-chip decipher key. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High-Definition_Multimedia_Interface" title="High-Definition Multimedia Interface">HDMI</a> was introduced, supporting display resolutions up to 1,920×1,080, with integrated HD audio controller with 5.1-channel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LPCM" title="LPCM">LPCM</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dolby_AC-3" title="Dolby AC-3">AC3</a> encoding support. Audio is transmitted via DVI port, with specially designed DVI-to-HDMI dongle for HDMI output that carries both audio and video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RV635_Ref_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-RV635_Ref-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>All variants support <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ATI_CrossFire#Current_generation_.28CrossFire_X.29" title="ATI CrossFire">CrossFireX</a> technology. CrossFire efficiency was improved and shows performance approaching the theoretical maximum of twice the performance of a single card.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TechReportR600_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-TechReportR600-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AnandR600reviewPrey_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-AnandR600reviewPrey-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Desktop_products">Desktop products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Desktop products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_AMD_graphics_processing_units#Radeon_R600_.28HD_2xxx.2C_HD_3xxx.29_Series" title="List of AMD graphics processing units">Radeon R600 (HD 2xxx, HD 3xxx) Series</a></div>
<p>The R600 family is called the <i><a href="/wiki/Radeon" title="Radeon">Radeon</a> HD 2000</i> series, with the enthusiast segment being the <i>Radeon HD 2900 series</i> which originally comprised the Radeon HD 2900 XT with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GDDR3" title="GDDR3">GDDR3</a> memory released on May 14, and the higher-clocked <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GDDR4" title="GDDR4">GDDR4</a> version in early July.</p>
<p>The mainstream and budget segment products were the Radeon HD 2600 and Radeon HD 2400 series respectively, both launched June 28, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Previously there were no HD 2000 series products being offered in the performance segment while ATI used models from the previous generation to address that target market; this situation did not change until the release of variants of the Radeon HD 2900 series, the Radeon HD 2900 Pro and GT, which filled the gap of the performance market for a short period of time.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radeon_HD_2400">Radeon HD 2400</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Radeon HD 2400">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sapphire_ATI_Radeon_HD_2400_XT.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3000" data-file-width="4000" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Sapphire_ATI_Radeon_HD_2400_XT.jpg/220px-Sapphire_ATI_Radeon_HD_2400_XT.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Sapphire_ATI_Radeon_HD_2400_XT.jpg/330px-Sapphire_ATI_Radeon_HD_2400_XT.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Sapphire_ATI_Radeon_HD_2400_XT.jpg/440px-Sapphire_ATI_Radeon_HD_2400_XT.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sapphire_ATI_Radeon_HD_2400_XT.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
ATI Radeon HD 2400 XT</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Radeon HD 2400 series was based on the codenamed RV610 GPU. It had 180 million transistors on a 65 nm fabrication process. The Radeon HD 2400 series used a 64-bit-wide memory bus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMDPR1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMDPR1-6">[6]</a></sup> The die size is 85 mm<sup>2</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beyond3D_2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beyond3D_2-7">[7]</a></sup> The official PCB design implements only a passive-cooling heatsink instead of a fan, and official claims of power consumption are as little as 35 W.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The core has 16 kiB unified <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vertex/texture_cache&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vertex/texture cache (page does not exist)">vertex/texture cache</a> away from dedicated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vertex_cache" title="Vertex cache">vertex cache</a> and L1/L2 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Texture_cache" title="Texture cache">texture cache</a> used in higher end model.</p>
<p>Reports has that the first batch of the RV610 core (silicon revision A12), only being released to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/System_builder" title="System builder">system builders</a>, has a bug that hindered the UVD from working properly, but other parts of the die operated normally. Those products were officially supported with the release of Catalyst 7.10 driver, which the cards were named as Radeon HD 2350 series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Several reports from owners of HD 2400 Pro suggest the card do not fully support hardware decoding for all H.264/VC-1 videos. The device driver, even with the latest stable version, seem to only honor hardware decoding for formats specified in the Blu-ray and HD-DVD specification. As a result of such restriction, the card is not deemed very useful for hardware video decoding since the majority of the H.264/VC-1 videos on the net are not encoded in those formats (even though the hardware itself is fully capable of doing such decoding work). This device driver restriction has led to the development of a third party driver patch, "ExDeus ATI HD Registry Tweak", to unlock the potential of HD 2400 Pro for full support of H.264/VC-1 hardware video decoding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radeon_HD_2600">Radeon HD 2600</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Radeon HD 2600">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Radeon HD 2600 series was based on the codenamed RV630 GPU and packed 390 million transistors on a 65 nm fabrication process. The Radeon HD 2600-series video cards included GDDR3 support, a 128-bit memory ring bus and 4-phase digital PWM,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMDPR1_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-AMDPR1-6">[6]</a></sup> spanning a die size of 153 mm<sup>2</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beyond3D_1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beyond3D_1-12">[12]</a></sup> Neither of the GDDR3 reference PCI-E designs required additional power connectors whereas the HD 2600 Pro and XT AGP variants required additional power through either 4-pin or 6-pin power connectors,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Official claims state that the Radeon HD 2600 series consumes as little as 45 W of power.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Radeon_HD_2600_X2">Radeon HD 2600 X2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Radeon HD 2600 X2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Radeon HD 2600 X2 is a dual-GPU product which includes 2 RV630 dies on a single <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Printed_Circuit_Board" title="Printed Circuit Board">PCB</a> with a <a href="/wiki/PCI_Express" title="PCI Express">PCI-E</a> bridge splitting the PCI-E ×16 bandwidth into two groups of PCI-E ×8 lanes (each 2.0 Gbit/s). The card provides 4 <a href="/wiki/Digital_Visual_Interface" title="Digital Visual Interface">DVI</a> outputs or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High-Definition_Multimedia_Interface" title="High-Definition Multimedia Interface">HDMI</a> outputs via dongle and supports CrossFire configurations. AMD calls this product the <i>Radeon HD 2600 X2</i> as seen by some vendors and as observed inside the <a href="/wiki/INF_file" title="INF file">INF file</a> of Catalyst 7.9 version 8.411. Sapphire and other vendors including PowerColor and GeCube have either announced or demonstrated their respective dual GPU (connected by crossfire) products.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Catalyst 7.9 added support for this hardware in September 2007. However, AMD did not provide much publicity to promote it. A vendor may offer cards containing 256 MiB, 512 MiB, or 1 GiB of video memory. Although the memory technology utilized is at a vendor's discretion, most vendors have opted for GDDR3 and <a href="/wiki/DDR2_SDRAM" title="DDR2 SDRAM">DDR2</a> due to lower manufacturing cost and positioning of this product for the mainstream rather than performance market segment and also a big success.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radeon_HD_2900">Radeon HD 2900</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Radeon HD 2900">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Radeon HD 2900 series was based on the codenamed R600 GPU, packed 700 million transistors on an 80 nm fabrication process and had a 420 mm<sup>2</sup> die size.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The Radeon HD 2900 XT was the first graphics card product to implement <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pulse_width_modulation#Digital" title="Pulse width modulation">digital PWM</a> on board, specifically 7-phase PWM, and was the first graphics card from ATI to support <a href="/wiki/DirectX" title="DirectX">DirectX</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DirectX_10" title="DirectX 10">10</a>. It was also the first product of the R600 architecture and was launched on May 14, 2007.</p>
<p>The Radeon HD 2900 Pro was clocked lower at 600 MHz core and 800 MHz memory (1,600 MHz effective), configured with 512 MiB or 1 GiB (GDDR3/GDDR4) of video memory and the same 512-bit memory controller as the Radeon HD 2900 XT instead of the previously rumored 256-bit memory controller.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Tech_Report_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Tech_Report-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Radeon HD 2900 GT was a 48-shader cluster variant clocked the same as the HD 2900 Pro with 256 MiB of video memory on a 256-bit interface.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mobile_products">Mobile products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mobile products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_AMD_graphics_processing_units#Radeon_R600_.28HD_2xxx.2C_HD_3xxx.29_Series" title="List of AMD graphics processing units">List of R600 GPUs</a></div>
<p>All Mobility Radeon HD 2000 series share the same feature set support as their desktop counterparts, as well as the addition of the battery-conserving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ATI_PowerPlay" title="ATI PowerPlay">PowerPlay</a> 7.0 features, which are augmented from the previous generation's PowerPlay 6.0.</p>
<p>The Mobility Radeon HD 2300 is a budget product which includes UVD in silica but lacks unified shader architecture and <a href="/wiki/DirectX" title="DirectX">DirectX</a> 10.0/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_Level_Shader_Language" title="High Level Shader Language">SM</a> 4.0 support, limiting support to <a href="/wiki/DirectX" title="DirectX">DirectX</a> 9.0c/SM 3.0 using the more traditional architecture of the previous generation. A high-end variant, the Mobility Radeon HD 2700, with higher core and memory frequencies than the Mobility Radeon HD 2600, was released in mid-December 2007.</p>
<p>The Mobility Radeon HD 2400 is offered in two model variants; the standard HD 2400 and the HD 2400 XT.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Mobility Radeon HD 2600 is also available in the same two flavors; the plain HD 2600 and, at the top of the mobility lineup, the HD 2600 XT.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The half-generation update treatment had also applied to mobile products. Announced prior to <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Electronics_Show" title="Consumer Electronics Show">CES</a> 2008 was the Mobility Radeon HD 3000 series. Released in the first quarter of 2008, the Mobility Radeon HD 3000 series consisted of two families, the Mobility Radeon HD 3400 series and the Mobility Radeon HD 3600 series. The Mobility Radeon HD 3600 series also featured the industry's first implementation of on-board 128-bit GDDR4 memory.</p>
<p>About the time of late March to early April, 2008, AMD renewed the device ID list on its website <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> with the inclusion of Mobility Radeon HD 3850 X2 and Mobility Radeon HD 3870 X2 and their respective device IDs. Later in Spring <a href="/wiki/Intel_Developer_Forum" title="Intel Developer Forum">IDF</a> 2008 held in Shanghai, a development board of the Mobility Radeon HD 3870 X2 was demonstrated alongside a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centrino_2" title="Centrino 2">Centrino 2</a> platform demonstration system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The Mobility Radeon HD 3870 X2 was based on two M88 GPUs with the addition of a PCI Express switch chip on a single PCB. The development board used for demonstration was a PCI Express 2.0 ×16 card, while the final product is expected to be on AXIOM/<a href="/wiki/Mobile_PCI_Express_Module" title="Mobile PCI Express Module">MXM</a> modules.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Graphics_device_drivers">Graphics device drivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Graphics device drivers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AMD.27s_proprietary_graphics_device_driver_.22Catalyst.22">AMD's proprietary graphics device driver "Catalyst"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: AMD's proprietary graphics device driver &quot;Catalyst&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AMD_Catalyst" title="AMD Catalyst">AMD Catalyst</a></div>
<p>AMD Catalyst is being developed for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a>. As of July 2014, other operating system are not officially supported. This may be different for the <a href="/wiki/AMD_FirePro" title="AMD FirePro">AMD FirePro</a> brand, which is based on identical hardware but features OpenGL-certified graphics device drivers.</p>
<p>AMD Catalyst supports of course all features advertised for the Radeon brand.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Microsoft_Windows">Microsoft Windows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Microsoft Windows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Purple Pill tool issue, which could allow unsigned drivers to be loaded into <a href="/wiki/Windows_Vista" title="Windows Vista">Windows Vista</a> and tamper with the operating system <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kernel_(computer_science)" title="Kernel (computer science)">kernel</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> was resolved in the Catalyst 7.8 release (version 8.401).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AVIVO#AVIVO_Video_Converter" title="AVIVO">AVIVO video converter</a> for <a href="/wiki/Windows_Vista" title="Windows Vista">Windows Vista</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Color_temperature" title="Color temperature">color temperature</a> control in Catalyst Control Center was added with the release of Catalyst 7.9, package version 8.411. Software CrossFire was enabled for HD 2600 and HD 2400 series video cards with the release of Catalyst 7.10 (package version 8.421)</p>
<p>The Catalyst 8.1, package version 8.451, supports for MultiView technology for accelerated OpenGL rendering on multiple video card setup (CrossFire). The driver also allows CrossFire configurations for Radeon HD 3850 and HD 3870 video cards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Catalyst 8.3 is described by AMD as a milestone release,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> supporting DirectX 10.1, <a href="/wiki/AMD_700_chipset_series#Multi-graphics" title="AMD 700 chipset series">ATI CrossFire X technology</a> and allowing the mixing of different Radeon HD 3800 series video cards to form a CrossFire X setup with 2 to 4 GPUs. Catalyst 8.3 introduced to new video controls to further enhance the video playback quality, these controls includes edge enhancement and noise reduction settings. There is also the support for extended desktop in CrossFire X mode. The anti-aliasing support for Unreal Engine 3.0 in DirectX 9.0 games, support for CFAA filters (wide tent and box tent) to be enabled when Super AA is enabled, and other features as developer support for hardware <a href="/wiki/Tessellation#Tessellations_and_computer_graphics" title="Tessellation">surface tessellation</a>, hardware accelerated wide aspect ratio LCD scaling, HydraVision support for <a href="/wiki/Windows_Vista" title="Windows Vista">Windows Vista</a> allowing to add maximum 9 virtual desktops and new <a href="/wiki/Folding@home" title="Folding@home">Folding@home</a> client are also officially supported in this release.</p>
<p>The Catalyst 8.5, package version 8.493<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> brought new features include <a href="/wiki/Component_video" title="Component video">component video</a> with <a href="/wiki/480i" title="480i">480i</a> and <a href="/wiki/480p" title="480p">480p</a> resolutions, <a href="/wiki/SECAM" title="SECAM">SECAM</a> TV output support, <a href="/wiki/1080p" title="1080p">1080p</a> <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">HDTV</a> custom mode via HDMI, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1080p24" title="1080p24">1080p24</a> (1080p resolution at 24 Hz) support, HDMI Audio for non-standard TV modes (CEA 861b), support for adaptive anti-aliasing (and later, in Catalyst 8.6, also support for custom filters <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>) under <a href="/wiki/OpenGL" title="OpenGL">OpenGL</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Windows_XP_SP3" title="Windows XP SP3">Windows XP SP3</a> support and un-install utility enhancements. The driver also includes performance improvements and fixes some instability issues and rendering issues on some games.</p>
<p>The Radeon HD 2000 series has been transitioned to legacy support, where drivers will be updated only to fix bugs instead of being optimized for new applications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Current Catalyst drivers do not support the AGP versions of Radeon HD 2000/3000 series cards with RIALTO bridge. Installing Catalyst drivers on those cards will yield the following error message: "setup did not find a driver compatible with your current hardware or operating system." or simply fail outright. The AGP cards in question are supported unofficially by ATI/AMD with a <a href="/wiki/Hotfix" title="Hotfix">hot-fixed</a> Catalyst driver-set each month since May 2008 with the Catalyst 8.5 hotfix.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Their PCI vendor IDs are listed below:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_and_open-source_graphics_device_driver_.22Radeon.22">Free and open-source graphics device driver "Radeon"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title='Edit section: Free and open-source graphics device driver "Radeon"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Free_and_open-source_graphics_device_driver#ATI.2FAMD" title="Free and open-source graphics device driver">free and open-source "radeon" graphics device driver</a></div>
<p>The free and open-source drivers are primarily developed on <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> and for Linux, but have been ported to other operating systems as well. Each driver is composed out of five parts:</p>
<ol>
<li>Linux kernel component <a href="/wiki/Direct_Rendering_Manager" title="Direct Rendering Manager">Direct Rendering Manager</a> (DRM)</li>
<li>Linux kernel component <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KMS_driver" title="KMS driver">KMS driver</a>: basically the device driver for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Display_controller" title="Display controller">display controller</a></li>
<li>user-space component libDRM</li>
<li>user-space component in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mesa_3D" title="Mesa 3D">Mesa 3D</a>;</li>
<li>a special and distinct 2D graphics device driver for <a href="/wiki/X.Org_Server" title="X.Org Server">X.Org Server</a>, which if finally about to be replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glamor_(software)" title="Glamor (software)">Glamor</a></li>
</ol>
<p>The free and open-source "Radeon" graphics driver supports most of the features implemented into the Radeon line of GPUs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Documentation_release">Documentation release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_2000_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Documentation release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The free and open-source "Radeon" graphics device drivers are <i>not</i> reverse engineered, but based on documentation released by AMD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Initial register documentation and parser code to execute the AtomBIOS ROM routines were released in September 2007. The R600 family Instruction Set Architecture guide was released on June 11, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Sample code and register headers for the R600 and R700 3D engines were released in December 2008. AMD released the specifications for both the r6xx and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radeon_R700" title="Radeon R700">r7xx</a> families on January 26, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
