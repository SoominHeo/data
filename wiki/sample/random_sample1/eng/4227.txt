<p><b>Radom Governorate</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Радомская Губерния</span>, <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <span lang="pl" xml:lang="pl"><i>Gubernia radomska</i></span>) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governorates_of_the_Russian_Empire" title="Governorates of the Russian Empire">governorate</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Congress_Poland" title="Congress Poland">Congress Poland</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radom_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was created in 1844 from the merger of the <a href="/wiki/Sandomierz_Governorate" title="Sandomierz Governorate">Sandomierz Governorate</a> with <a href="/wiki/Kielce_Governorate" title="Kielce Governorate">Kielce Governorate</a>. Its capital was in <a href="/wiki/Radom" title="Radom">Radom</a> (previously a capital of the Sandomierz Governorate).</p>
<p>It was divided into 8 <a href="/wiki/Powiat" title="Powiat">powiats</a>: <a href="/wiki/Kielce" title="Kielce">Kielce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miech%C3%B3w" title="Miechów">Miechów</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olkusz" title="Olkusz">Olkusz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Opat%C3%B3w" title="Opatów">Opatów</a>, <a href="/wiki/Opoczno" title="Opoczno">Opoczno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radom" title="Radom">Radom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sandomierz" title="Sandomierz">Sandomierz</a>.</p>
<p>In 1866 the <a href="/wiki/Kielce_Governorate" title="Kielce Governorate">Kielce Governorate</a> was once again made an independent entity, and thus split off from the Radom Governorate.</p>
