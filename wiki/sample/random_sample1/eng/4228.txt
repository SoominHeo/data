<p><b>Lee Doo-hyun</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 이두현; born 1980), better known by his stage name <b>Ra.D</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 라디) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Producer_(music)" title="Producer (music)">producer</a> who debuted in 2002 with the album <i>My Name is Ra.D</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After establishing his own record label, RealCollabo, he has been participating in the production of many artists' albums since. After a 6-year hiatus, he made a comeback in February 2013 with the single "It's Been a While".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ra.D has produced for artists such as <a href="/wiki/2PM" title="2PM">2PM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brown_Eyed_Girls" title="Brown Eyed Girls">Brown Eyed Girls</a>' <a href="/wiki/Gain_(singer)" title="Gain (singer)">Gain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dynamic_Duo_(South_Korean_duo)" title="Dynamic Duo (South Korean duo)">Dynamic Duo</a>, KeBee, UMC, <a href="/wiki/Ahn_Trio" title="Ahn Trio">Ahn Trio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cho_PD" title="Cho PD">Cho PD</a>.</p>
<p>On October 26, 2015, he announced RealCollabo would be closed after established for seven years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> "Draw You", a collaboration project of Ra.D, d.ear, Brother Su, and <a href="/wiki/Jooyoung" title="Jooyoung">Jooyoung</a>, is the last release from this label (co-release with 24thStreet).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>
