<p><b>Radio Nikkei</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ラジオNIKKEI</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Rajio Nikkei</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>'s nationwide free-to-air <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shortwave" title="Shortwave">shortwave</a> <a href="/wiki/Commercial_broadcasting" title="Commercial broadcasting">commercial</a> radio station. It started broadcast on August 27, 1954. Radio Nikkei 2 started broadcast on September 2, 1963.</p>
<p>Radio Nikkei's headquarters are located at Kotohira Tower, 2-8 <a href="/wiki/Toranomon,_Minato,_Tokyo" title="Toranomon, Minato, Tokyo">Toranomon</a> 1-chōme, <a href="/wiki/Minato,_Tokyo" title="Minato, Tokyo">Minato, Tokyo</a>. Its headquarters was previously at 9-15 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Akasaka,_Tokyo" title="Akasaka, Tokyo">Akasaka</a> 1-chōme, <a href="/wiki/Minato,_Tokyo" title="Minato, Tokyo">Minato, Tokyo</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radio_Nikkei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is operated by <b>Nikkei Radio Broadcasting Corporation</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">株式会社<b>日経ラジオ社</b></span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kabushiki-gaisha <b>Nikkei Rajio-sha</b></i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, whose main share is held by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nihon_Keizai_Shimbun" title="Nihon Keizai Shimbun">Nihon Keizai Shimbun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">Tokyo Stock Exchange</a>. The headquarters and the main studio are located at Kotohira Tower at Toranomon, Minato, Tokyo. It also has its branch office in Ōtemae, <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D-ku,_Osaka" title="Chūō-ku, Osaka">Chūō-ku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>.</p>
<p>Along with the headquarters' studio, the studios in Tokyo are inside the Tokyo Stock Exchange and two other public satellite studios. In Osaka, it also has studios in the branch office and a satellite studio.</p>
<p>Besides its shortwave coverage Radio Nikkei is also available on mobile phones and online through Radiko.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Programming">Programming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radio_Nikkei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Programming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The language is almost exclusively in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> except an English language learning programme.</p>
<p>The station features the following four genres as the core of its programming: finance, <a href="/wiki/Japan_Racing_Association" title="Japan Racing Association">JRA</a> horse racing (weekends), health-medical, and culture.</p>
<p>Radio Nikkei 1 is for general programs and eastern Japan horse-racing coverage. Radio Nikkei 2 is for live company stock price announcement and western Japan horse-racing coverage. Radio Nikkei 2's programming on weekdays is named RN2 which is aimed for businesspersons at their 30s and 40s with music programs.</p>
<p>Although Radio Nikkei is a member of Nikkei Media Group, its programming does not have a strong connection with the group's television networks, <a href="/wiki/TX_Network" title="TX Network">TXN</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nikkei_CNBC" title="Nikkei CNBC">Nikkei CNBC</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Frequencies.2C_beam.2C_modulation_and_hours">Frequencies, beam, modulation and hours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radio_Nikkei&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Frequencies, beam, modulation and hours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From its transmitting stations in <a href="/wiki/Nagara,_Chiba" title="Nagara, Chiba">Nagara, Chiba</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nemuro,_Hokkaid%C5%8D" title="Nemuro, Hokkaidō">Nemuro, Hokkaidō</a>, it transmits on the following <a href="/wiki/Frequency" title="Frequency">frequencies</a>, power and <a href="/wiki/Call_sign" title="Call sign">call signs</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li>Radio Nikkei 1
<ul>
<li>3.925 MHz / 50 kW (JOZ); 10 kW (JOZ4): JOZ, JOZ4</li>
<li>6.055 MHz / 50 kW: JOZ2</li>
<li>9.595 MHz / 50 kW: JOZ3</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Radio Nikkei 2
<ul>
<li>3.945 MHz / 10 kW: JOZ5</li>
<li>6.115 MHz / 50 kW: JOZ6</li>
<li>9.760 MHz / 50 kW: JOZ7</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>The horizontal <a href="/wiki/Dipole_antenna" title="Dipole antenna">dipole antennas</a> radiate towards the northeast and the southwest direction, which would cover the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_archipelago" title="Japanese archipelago">Japanese archipelago</a>.</p>
<p>As of June 2007, its shortwave transmissions are in an ordinary <a href="/wiki/Amplitude_modulation" title="Amplitude modulation">amplitude modulation</a>: double sideband full carrier (A3E).</p>
<p>Radio Nikkei 1 begins at 05:40 in <a href="/wiki/Japan_Standard_Time" title="Japan Standard Time">Japan Standard Time</a> (JST), 20:40 (the day before) in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Coordinated_Time" title="Universal Coordinated Time">UTC</a> and ends at 22:30JST=13:30UTC (Mondays to Thursdays), 23:30JST=14:30UTC (Fridays), 23:15JST=14:15UTC (Saturdays) and 21:00JST=12:00UTC (Sundays).</p>
<p>Radio Nikkei 2 begins at 08:00JST=23:00UTC and ends at 16:30JST=07:30UTC (Mondays to Fridays), 18:00=09:00UTC (Saturdays and Sundays).</p>
<p>The schedule changes subject to program availability.</p>
