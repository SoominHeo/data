<p><b>Lala Yusifova</b> (<a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_language" title="Azerbaijani language">Azerbaijani</a>: <span lang="az" xml:lang="az"><i>Lalə Yusifova</i></span>, born October 16, 1996 in <a href="/wiki/Baku" title="Baku">Baku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>) is a retired <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_people" title="Azerbaijani people">Azerbaijani</a> <a href="/wiki/Rhythmic_gymnastics" title="Rhythmic gymnastics">rhythmic gymnast</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lala_Yusifova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yusifova began competing in junior internationals in 2009. She won the silver medal in ball at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Rhythmic_Gymnastics_European_Championships" title="2010 Rhythmic Gymnastics European Championships">2010 European Junior Championships</a> and briefly competed as a member of the Azerbaijani Group gymnasts who won the bronze medal <a href="/wiki/2011_Rhythmic_Gymnastics_European_Championships" title="2011 Rhythmic Gymnastics European Championships">2011 European Junior Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Yusifova debuted as a full senior in 2012. In the absence of most veteran rhythmic gymnasts competing in Grand Prix after the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> she was able to compete at the <a href="/wiki/Rhythmic_Gymnastics_Grand_Prix_Series" title="Rhythmic Gymnastics Grand Prix Series">2012 Grand Prix Final</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brno" title="Brno">Brno</a> where she won silver medal in ribbon and clubs behind the reigning Olympic silver medalist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daria_Dmitrieva" title="Daria Dmitrieva">Daria Dmitrieva</a>. In 2013, at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_Rhythmic_Gymnastics_World_Cup_Series" title="2013 Rhythmic Gymnastics World Cup Series">2013 Miss Valentine Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tartu" title="Tartu">Tartu</a>, Estonia, Yusifova won the bronze medal in hoop and at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_Rhythmic_Gymnastics_World_Cup_Series" title="2013 Rhythmic Gymnastics World Cup Series">2013 Irina Deleanu Cup</a> she won another bronze medal in hoop. She competed at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Rhythmic_Gymnastics_European_Championships" title="2013 Rhythmic Gymnastics European Championships">2013 European Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, Austria where she and teammate <a href="/wiki/Marina_Durunda" title="Marina Durunda">Marina Durunda</a> represented Azerbaijan finishing fourth in the Team event. She qualified in clubs and finished 7th at the finals. She won the silver medal at the 2013 Azerbaijan National Championships behind champion <a href="/wiki/Marina_Durunda" title="Marina Durunda">Marina Durunda</a>. At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_Rhythmic_Gymnastics_World_Cup_Series" title="2013 Rhythmic Gymnastics World Cup Series">2013 World Cup Final</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>, Russia, she finished 8th in all-around and qualified in ball final where she finished 8th.</p>
<p>Yusifova tied for 13th place with <a href="/wiki/Jana_Berezko-Marggrander" title="Jana Berezko-Marggrander">Jana Berezko-Marggrander</a> in the All-around at the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Rhythmic_Gymnastics_Championships" title="2013 World Rhythmic Gymnastics Championships">2013 World Championships</a>. She competed at the <a href="/wiki/Rhythmic_Gymnastics_Grand_Prix_Series" title="Rhythmic Gymnastics Grand Prix Series">2013 Grand Prix Brno</a> and finished 9th in the all-around. She competed at the <a href="/wiki/Rhythmic_Gymnastics_Grand_Prix_Series" title="Rhythmic Gymnastics Grand Prix Series">2013 Grand Prix Final</a> in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and finished 6th in all-around. She qualified to four event finals and won bronze in clubs. At the 2013 Aeon Cup in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, Yusifova finished 5th in Team event and 6th in the All-around finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Yusifova missed competing in the early season due to shoulder injury. In April, Yusifova returned to competition at the 2014 Azerbaijan National Championships where she won the all-around silver medal behind reigning national champion Marina Durunda. Yusifova competed in her first event internationally at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_Rhythmic_Gymnastics_World_Cup_Series" title="2014 Rhythmic Gymnastics World Cup Series">2014 Corbeil-Essonnes World Cup</a> and finished 16th in all-around, she qualified to 1 event final and finished 6th in ball. Shortly, after her brief return, Yusifova announced her retirement on September 3.</p>
