<p><i><b>Lala Pipo: A Lot of People</b></i> (Japanese: ララピポ) is a 2005 Japanese novel by Hideo Okuda. It was made into a movie by the same name in 2009. <i>Lala Pipo</i> is the directorial debut of Masayuki Miyano. The screenplay was written by <a href="/wiki/Tetsuya_Nakashima" title="Tetsuya Nakashima">Tetsuya Nakashima</a>, writer-director of <i><a href="/wiki/Kamikaze_Girls" title="Kamikaze Girls">Kamikaze Girls</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Memories_of_Matsuko" title="Memories of Matsuko">Memories of Matsuko</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The novel and movie both chronicle the life of six people involved with the <a href="/wiki/Pornography_in_Japan" title="Pornography in Japan">Japanese porn industry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The book is divided into 6 chapters, with each chapter telling the story of one character.</p>
<p>The book is distributed by <a href="/wiki/Vertical_(company)" title="Vertical (company)">Vertical, Inc.</a>, and the movie is distributed by <a href="/wiki/Third_Window_Films" title="Third Window Films">Third Window Films</a> in the UK.</p>
