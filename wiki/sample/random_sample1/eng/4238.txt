<p><b>Rahman</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">رحمان</span>‎‎, <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Arabic transliteration"><i>Raḥmān</i></span><i>) is an Arabic male name, short for</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abdur_Rahman" title="Abdur Rahman">Abdu r-Raḥmān</a> <i>"Servant of the Most Merciful",</i> ar-Rahman <i>being one of the <a href="/wiki/Names_of_God_in_Islam" title="Names of God in Islam">Names of God in Islam</a>.</i> <i>Raḥmān</i> is the <a href="/wiki/Elative_(gradation)" title="Elative (gradation)">elative</a> of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ra%E1%B8%A5%C4%ABm" title="Raḥīm">raḥīm</a></i> "merciful",<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> based on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triconsonantal_root" title="Triconsonantal root">triconsonantal root</a> <a href="/wiki/R-%E1%B8%A4-M" title="R-Ḥ-M">R-Ḥ-M</a> With <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nisbat_(onomastics)" title="Nisbat (onomastics)">nisba</a>, the name becomes <i>Rahmani</i> and is used as a surname.</p>
