<p><b>Ramón Ismael Medina Bello</b>, nicknamed <i>El Mencho</i> (born April 29, 1966 in <a href="/wiki/Gualeguay,_Entre_R%C3%ADos" title="Gualeguay, Entre Ríos">Gualeguay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Entre_R%C3%ADos_Province" title="Entre Ríos Province">Entre Rios</a>) is a former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentine" title="Argentine">Argentine</a> football <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Striker_(association_football)" title="Striker (association football)">striker</a> who has played at club level in Argentina and Japan.</p>
<p>He began his professional football career in 1986 with <a href="/wiki/Racing_Club_de_Avellaneda" title="Racing Club de Avellaneda">Racing Club de Avellaneda</a>. In 1989 he was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a>, where he played until 1993. By that time he was part of the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina national team</a>, he won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copa_America" title="Copa America">Copa America</a> 1991 in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>. He was also part of the national team in the <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1994 FIFA World Cup">1994 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>Medina Bello went to play in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_F._Marinos" title="Yokohama F. Marinos">Yokohama Marinos</a> until 1996 when he returned to River Plate. He retired in 1999 in <a href="/wiki/Talleres_de_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Talleres de Córdoba">Talleres de Córdoba</a> and after 2 years of retirement surprinsingly appeared playing for <a href="/wiki/Sportivo_Dock_Sud" title="Sportivo Dock Sud">Sportivo Dock Sud</a> in the Argentine Soccer League Fourth Division. In 2005 he played for Juventud Unida where he retired for good.</p>
