<p><b>Kim Won-sik</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">김원식</span>, born February 15, 1993)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raviprofile_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-raviprofile-1">[1]</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Ravi</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">라비</span>), is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a>, signed under <a href="/wiki/Jellyfish_Entertainment" title="Jellyfish Entertainment">Jellyfish Entertainment</a>. He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boy_group" title="Boy group">boy group</a> <a href="/wiki/VIXX" title="VIXX">VIXX</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vixxdebut_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-vixxdebut-2">[2]</a></sup> and VIXX sub-unit <a href="/wiki/VIXX_LR" title="VIXX LR">VIXX LR</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He debuted as a solo artist on January 9, 2017, with the release of his debut mini album <i>R.EAL1ZE</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ravi_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.932013:_Debut_with_VIXX_and_Composing">2012–2013: Debut with VIXX and Composing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ravi_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2012–2013: Debut with VIXX and Composing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/VIXX" title="VIXX">VIXX</a></div>
<p>Ravi was one of ten trainees who were contestants in <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s survival reality show <i><a href="/wiki/MyDOL" title="MyDOL">MyDOL</a></i>. He was also one of the six contestants selected to be part of the final line-up of the new boy group <a href="/wiki/VIXX" title="VIXX">VIXX</a>. The group debuted with "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_Hero_(EP)" title="Super Hero (EP)">Super Hero</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> on May 24, 2012<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vixxdebut_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-vixxdebut-2">[2]</a></sup> on <a href="/wiki/M_Countdown" title="M Countdown">M! Countdown</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> During <i><a href="/wiki/MyDOL" title="MyDOL">MyDOL</a></i>; Ravi was featured in the music videos for <a href="/wiki/Brian_Joo" title="Brian Joo">Brian Joo</a>'s "Let This Die" and <a href="/wiki/Seo_In-guk" title="Seo In-guk">Seo In-guk</a>'s "Shake It Up".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brianjooletthisdiemv_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-brianjooletthisdiemv-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seoingukshakeitupmv_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-seoingukshakeitupmv-9">[9]</a></sup> Ravi participated in writing "<a href="/wiki/Super_Hero_(VIXX_song)" title="Super Hero (VIXX song)">Super Hero</a>", VIXX's debut single.</p>
<p>In 2013, he appeared in episode 4 of <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a>'s television drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Heirs" title="The Heirs">The Heirs</a></i> alongside his group members.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.932015:_Collaborations_and_debut_with_sub-unit:_VIXX_LR">2014–2015: Collaborations and debut with sub-unit: VIXX LR</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ravi_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014–2015: Collaborations and debut with sub-unit: VIXX LR">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/VIXX_LR" title="VIXX LR">VIXX LR</a></div>
<p>In 2014, Ravi collaborated with American artist Chad Future with "Rock the World" from Future's first mini album. Ravi also appeared in the music video for the single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, after much deliberation, Ravi became a contestant of <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s television rap competition series <i><a href="/wiki/Show_Me_the_Money_4" title="Show Me the Money 4">Show Me the Money 4</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> but was eliminated in the second round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Ravi was featured as a rapper in <a href="/wiki/Rain_(entertainer)" title="Rain (entertainer)">Rain</a>’s Chinese song "Diamond Love" from the soundtrack of the Chinese drama <i>Diamond Lover</i> (克拉戀人).</p>
<p>On August 7, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Jellyfish_Entertainment" title="Jellyfish Entertainment">Jellyfish Entertainment</a> released a video trailer on VIXX’s official website after a mysterious countdown with a silhouette of VIXX’s last special album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boys%27_Record_(single)" title="Boys' Record (single)">Boys' Record</a></i>. As time went by, members of VIXX disappeared until finally only <a href="/wiki/Leo_(singer)" title="Leo (singer)">Leo</a> and Ravi were left behind, which caused fans to speculate that it meant another comeback for all six members. A video trailer of <a href="/wiki/VIXX_LR" title="VIXX LR">VIXX LR</a> was then revealed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/VIXX_LR" title="VIXX LR">VIXX LR</a> was confirmed by <a href="/wiki/Jellyfish_Entertainment" title="Jellyfish Entertainment">Jellyfish Entertainment</a> to be VIXX’s first official sub-unit composed of rapper Ravi and vocalist <a href="/wiki/Leo_(singer)" title="Leo (singer)">Leo</a>. Their debut mini album, <i><a href="/wiki/Beautiful_Liar_(EP)" title="Beautiful Liar (EP)">Beautiful Liar</a></i>, was released on August 17, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On the same day VIXX LR held their first showcase for <i><a href="/wiki/Beautiful_Liar_(EP)" title="Beautiful Liar (EP)">Beautiful Liar</a></i> at Yes24 Muv Hall in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>′s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mapo-gu" title="Mapo-gu">Mapo-gu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2015, Ravi was featured in the ballad girl group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Melody_Day" title="Melody Day">Melody Day</a>'s single "When It Rains" (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">비가 내리면</span>), as part of their Winter Ballad Project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 31, 2015, Ravi dropped a teaser on his Twitter and Instagram accounts for his first mixtape <i>[R.EBIRTH]</i> with tracks composed, written and produced by himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.93present:_.5BR.EBIRTH.5D.2C_DamnRa_and_solo_debut_with_R.EAL1ZE">2016–present: <i>[R.EBIRTH]</i>, <i>DamnRa</i> and solo debut with <i>R.EAL1ZE</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ravi_(rapper)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2016–present: [R.EBIRTH], DamnRa and solo debut with R.EAL1ZE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 4, 2016, Ravi revealed the first pre-release to his mixtape <i>[R.EBIRTH]</i>, with the track "Where Should I Go" featuring Microdot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The second pre-release "OX" featuring <a href="/wiki/Basick" title="Basick">Basick</a>, the winner of <i><a href="/wiki/Show_Me_the_Money_4" title="Show Me the Money 4">Show Me the Money 4</a></i>, was released on January 20, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The third pre-release "Good Girls" (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">착한 여자</span>) featuring <a href="/wiki/Hanhae" title="Hanhae">Hanhae</a> and Soulman was released on February 4, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The fourth and final pre-release "Move" was released on February 22, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> All the mixtape pre-releases have been released on <a href="/wiki/VIXX" title="VIXX">VIXX</a>'s official <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YouTube_channel" title="YouTube channel">YouTube channel</a> and on Ravi's official <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>. On March 4, 2016 the track list was revealed on Ravi's Instagram.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The full version of <i>[R.EBIRTH]</i> was released on March 12, 2016 available for free as <a href="/wiki/Streaming_media" title="Streaming media">streaming media</a> also on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> To celebrate the release of <i>[R.EBIRTH]</i>, Ravi held a showcase, titled <i>Ravi’s 1st Live Party [R.EBIRTH]</i>, on March 19 and March 20, 2016 at the <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Card" title="Hyundai Card">Hyundai Card Understage</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Rappers <a href="/wiki/Basick" title="Basick">Basick</a>, Esbee, and DJ/producer SAM&amp;SP3CK attended the show as featured performers and VIXX members <a href="/wiki/Leo_(singer)" title="Leo (singer)">Leo</a>, <a href="/wiki/N_(singer)" title="N (singer)">N</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ken_(singer)" title="Ken (singer)">Ken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hong-bin" title="Lee Hong-bin">Hongbin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hyuk_(singer)" title="Hyuk (singer)">Hyuk</a> also attended as guests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 14, 2016, Ravi participated in Jellyfish Entertainment’s new music channel project <i><a href="/wiki/Jelly_Box" title="Jelly Box">Jelly Box</a></i> and released the single "DamnRa" featuring SAM&amp;SP3CK; a DJ/producer duo. "DamnRa" was released along with a performance music video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On September 26, Ravi released a mixtape track titled "Who are U" and featuring Superbee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of October 2016 with the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Kratos_(EP)" title="Kratos (EP)">Kratos</a></i>, Ravi has contributed to the writing and composing of over 46 <a href="/wiki/List_of_songs_recorded_by_VIXX" title="List of songs recorded by VIXX">songs recorded by VIXX</a>.</p>
<p>On December 26, 2016, it was announced that Ravi would debut as a solo artist with a mini album titled <i>R.EAL1ZE</i> on January 9, 2017 and will hold a solo concert from January 6 to January 8 at the Yes24 Muv Hall in Seoul’s <a href="/wiki/Mapo_District" title="Mapo District">Mapo District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> On January 4, 2017, Ravi pre-released a single, "Home Alone" (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">나홀로 집에</span>) featuring <a href="/wiki/Jung_Yong-hwa" title="Jung Yong-hwa">Jung Yong-hwa</a> from his upcoming first mini album <i>R.EAL1ZE</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> On January 9, Ravi released his debut mini album <i>R.EAL1ZE</i>, which contains three tracks from his previous mixtape plus four new tracks including the title track “BOMB” featuring <a href="/wiki/San_E" title="San E">San E</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> He was a producer, and personally in charge of the composition, arrangement and writing the lyrics of every song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
