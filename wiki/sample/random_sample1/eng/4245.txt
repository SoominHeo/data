<p><b>Plaza de Toros de Las Ventas</b> (often shortened to <b>Las Ventas</b>) is a famous <a href="/wiki/Bullring" title="Bullring">bullring</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.</p>
<p>Situated in the Guindalera quarter of the district of <a href="/wiki/Salamanca_(Madrid)" title="Salamanca (Madrid)">Salamanca</a>, it was inaugurated on June 17, 1931. It has a <a href="/wiki/Seating_capacity" title="Seating capacity">seating capacity</a> of 25,000.</p>
<p>This bullring was designed by the architect José Espeliú in the <a href="/wiki/Neo-Mud%C3%A9jar" title="Neo-Mudéjar">Neo-Mudéjar</a> (Moorish) style with ceramic incrustations. The seats are situated in ten "tendidos". The price of the seats depends upon how close they are to the arena and whether they are in the sun or the shade (the latter being more expensive). The bullfighting season starts in March and ends in October; bullfights are held every day during the San Isidro Fiesta, and every Sunday or holiday during the season. Bullfights start at 6 or 7pm and last for two to three hours.</p>
<p>"Las Ventas" is located in the east of Madrid.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Ventas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Redondel-las_ventas.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2000" data-file-width="3008" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Redondel-las_ventas.jpg/250px-Redondel-las_ventas.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Redondel-las_ventas.jpg/375px-Redondel-las_ventas.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Redondel-las_ventas.jpg/500px-Redondel-las_ventas.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Redondel-las_ventas.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
View of the empty arena of Las Ventas. It is about 65 meters in diameter, one of the world's widest rings.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>From 1913 to 1920, the national passion (bullfighting) gained such an important status that Madrid's former main bullring in Carretera de Aragón was not big enough. It was <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_G%C3%B3mez_Ortega" title="José Gómez Ortega">José Gómez Ortega</a> "Joselito" who declared that a new "monumental" bullring had to be built, to open this part of Spain's heritage and culture to the whole city of Madrid. His friend the architect José Espeliú began to work on the project.</p>
<p>A family called Jardón donated the land to the Madrid Provincial Council, provided that they could run the arena for fifty years. The deputation accepted the proposal on November 12, 1920. On March 19, 1922, in the exact center of the prospective arena, the first stone was placed. The construction of the bullring would cost 12 million <a href="/wiki/Spanish_peseta" title="Spanish peseta">pesetas</a> (4.5 million over budget), and it would replace the old bullring, dating from 1874.</p>
<p>"Las Ventas" was finished in 1929 and two years later, June 17, 1931, a charity bullfight was held with a full-capacity crowd to inaugurate it. Bullfighting stopped during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a> and did not resume until May 1939.</p>
<p>There is a <a href="/wiki/Pasodoble" title="Pasodoble">Pasodoble</a> called 'Plaza de las Ventas' and the composer <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maestro_Manuel_Lillo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maestro Manuel Lillo (page does not exist)">Maestro Manuel Lillo</a> dedicated to this arena.</p>

<p>"Las Ventas" is divided into a ring or arena, and a group of zones called "patios". Its architecture is <a href="/wiki/Neo-Mud%C3%A9jar" title="Neo-Mudéjar">Neo-Mudéjar</a>, with ceramic representations of the heraldic crests of the different Spanish provinces. The arena has a diameter of 60 meters. The seating capacity is divided into 10 "tendidos" (group of 27 rows around the arena), some of them in the shade and the rest in the sun.</p>
<p>The president of the 'corrida' sits in the 10th Tendido. The Royal Box is of outstanding design, with its Mudéjar architecture, a complete bathroom and a lift. Opposite to the Royal Box, in the covered grandstand roof, is the clock. The bullring has five gates, plus three more called "toriles", from where the bulls enter the arena. The gate of the "cuadrillas", between "tendidos" 3 and 4, has access to the horse yard. Inside this door, the "paseillo" starts and the "picadores" (those who pierce the bull with the lance) come out from here to the arena ("suerte de varas"). The dragging gate, that leads to the skinning room, is between "tendidos" 1 and 2. The famous "Puerta Grande" (Big Gate), also called the Gate of Madrid, is between "tendidos" 7 and 8. Going out through this door, especially during the Fiesta of San Isidro, is every bullfighter's ambition.</p>
<p>There are also a chapel and a small infirmary with two operating rooms.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_uses">Other uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Ventas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Concert_venue">Concert venue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Ventas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Concert venue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a> performed here on July 2, 1965. In 1991, <a href="/wiki/Diana_Ross" title="Diana Ross">Diana Ross</a> performed at Las Ventas for her <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Here_and_Now_World_Tour_1991-92&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Here and Now World Tour 1991-92 (page does not exist)">Here and Now World Tour 1991-92</a>, and in 1996, Australian rock band <a href="/wiki/AC/DC" title="AC/DC">AC/DC</a> performed at the bullring for their <a href="/wiki/Ballbreaker_World_Tour" title="Ballbreaker World Tour">Ballbreaker World Tour</a>, recording the performance for their concert video, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Bull" title="No Bull">No Bull</a></i>. In the summer of 2003, <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a> played a concert at Las Ventas, their only stop in Spain that year. In 2009, <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a> performed there as part of her <a href="/wiki/KylieX2008" title="KylieX2008">KylieX2008</a> tour, and on October 28, 2011, <a href="/wiki/Coldplay" title="Coldplay">Coldplay</a> performed at Las Ventas during the American Express <i>Unstaged</i> music series.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Red_Bull_X_Fighters">Red Bull X Fighters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Ventas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Red Bull X Fighters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The venue has also hosted the Red Bull X Fighters FMX since 2002.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tennis_court">Tennis court</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Ventas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tennis court">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2008, the arena was converted into a tennis <a href="/wiki/Clay_court" title="Clay court">clay court</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Davis_Cup_Team" title="Spanish Davis Cup Team">Spanish Davis Cup Team</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Nadal" title="Rafael Nadal">Rafael Nadal</a>, played their semifinal against the United States, and won it in front of their home crowd.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theatre">Theatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Ventas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Theatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 2014, the venue can be set up as a theatre, under the name Gran Teatro Ruedo Las Ventas, with a capacity of 858 seats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
