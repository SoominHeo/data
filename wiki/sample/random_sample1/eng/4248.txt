<p><i><b>The Last Exorcism</b></i> is a 2010 American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Found_footage_(pseudo-documentary)" title="Found footage (pseudo-documentary)">found footage</a> <a href="/wiki/Supernatural" title="Supernatural">supernatural</a> <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> directed and edited by <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Stamm" title="Daniel Stamm">Daniel Stamm</a>. It stars <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Fabian" title="Patrick Fabian">Patrick Fabian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Bell" title="Ashley Bell">Ashley Bell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iris_Bahr" title="Iris Bahr">Iris Bahr</a>, and Louis Herthum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film follows a disillusioned <a href="/wiki/Evangelism" title="Evangelism">evangelical</a> minister, who after years of performing <a href="/wiki/Exorcism" title="Exorcism">exorcisms</a> decides to participate in a documentary chronicling his last exorcism while exposing the fraud of his ministry. After receiving a letter from a farmer asking for help in driving out the <a href="/wiki/Devil" title="Devil">devil</a>, he meets the farmer's afflicted daughter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> This film received positive reviews from critics and was a box-office success.</p>
<p>A sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Exorcism_Part_II" title="The Last Exorcism Part II">The Last Exorcism Part II</a></i>, was released on March 1, 2013.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last_Exorcism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reverend Cotton Marcus (<a href="/wiki/Patrick_Fabian" title="Patrick Fabian">Patrick Fabian</a>) lives in <a href="/wiki/Baton_Rouge,_Louisiana" title="Baton Rouge, Louisiana">Baton Rouge, Louisiana</a> with his wife and son. Marcus, who lost his faith after the birth of his ill son, is accustomed to performing fake exorcisms on "possessed" individuals. He chooses an exorcism request sent by farmer Louis Sweetzer (<a href="/wiki/Louis_Herthum" title="Louis Herthum">Louis Herthum</a>), who claims his livestock are being slaughtered by his daughter Nell (<a href="/wiki/Ashley_Bell" title="Ashley Bell">Ashley Bell</a>) who is possessed.</p>
<p>After listening to the details of the case, Marcus claims Nell is possessed by a powerful demon named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abalam" title="Abalam">Abalam</a>. Prior to the exorcism, Marcus bamboozles the family into believing he is driving out a demon. After the ritual, Marcus and his film crew leave, believing they have cured her of a mental state that was misdiagnosed as a <a href="/wiki/Demonic_possession" title="Demonic possession">possession</a>. That night, Nell appears in Marcus' motel room, apparently unwell. Marcus takes Nell to the hospital for tests, which conclude that Nell is in perfect physical condition. Marcus goes to see Louis' former pastor, Joseph Manley. Manley informs Marcus that he has not seen Nell for two years. In the morning, Louis takes Nell home, but chains her to her bed after she slices her brother Caleb's (<a href="/wiki/Caleb_Landry_Jones" title="Caleb Landry Jones">Caleb Landry Jones</a>) face with a knife. That night, the crew find a drawing of a dead cat.</p>
<p>Nell steals their camera and goes into her father's barn, where she brutally smashes a cat to death while filming it with a camera. She returns to the house and approaches Marcus with the camera, and just as she's about to make contact, the crew stop her. The crew then discover two more of her paintings. The first depicts Marcus standing before a large flame, holding up a crucifix. Also depicted is Iris cut into pieces, and Daniel decapitated.</p>
<p>When everyone learns Nell is pregnant, Marcus accuses Louis of <a href="/wiki/Incest" title="Incest">incest</a>, which he denies, insisting that Nell is a virgin and has been defiled by the demon. The crew has a confrontation with Nell, who slashes Marcus's hand with a pair of scissors and then flees. The crew decide to leave, and when they see Nell sitting on the porch, Marcus approaches her and she tackles him. Louis is about to kill Nell with his shotgun, while Nell is begging him to. To keep Louis from killing her, Marcus offers to attempt a second exorcism.</p>
<p>Marcus confronts the entity, Abalam, who agrees to release Nell only if Marcus can remain silent for ten seconds. Abalam begins breaking Nell's fingers as it counts aloud. After breaking three of her fingers, Marcus yells for Abalam to stop. Abalam then asks Marcus if he wants a "blowing job." Marcus realizes that a demon would know the actual name of the sex act and concludes that Nell is not a demon, but a very disturbed girl. Nell anguishes over losing her virginity to a boy named Logan, resulting in her mental breakdown rather than possession. Marcus arranges for Pastor Manley to come to the house, and leaves. On their way home, Marcus and the crew meet Logan, who explains that the only contact he had with Nell was a brief conversation 6 months ago at a party held at Pastor Manley's home. Logan admits that he is gay and therefore did not have sex with her. Marcus realizes that Pastor Manley was lying about having not seen Nell for two years. He returns to the Sweetzer farmhouse, where they find numerous <a href="/wiki/Occult" title="Occult">occult</a> and <a href="/wiki/Counterculture" title="Counterculture">countercultural</a> symbols on the walls, and Nell and Louis missing.</p>
<p>Marcus and the crew wander into the woods, where they see a large fire and a congregation of hooded cultists, led by Pastor Manley. Louis is tied up, gagged, and blindfolded while hooded figures pray around an altar, atop which Nell is bound. She gives birth to an inhuman child. Manley throws the child into the fire, which causes the fire to grow rapidly as demonic roars emanate from within. At that moment, Marcus' faith is restored, and he grabs his cross and rushes towards the fire to combat the evil. Iris and Daniel are discovered; Iris is tackled by a member of the congregation and killed with an axe. Daniel continues to run, until he stops to take a deep breath. He then looks around to see if he was followed, before turning back to find Caleb, who is wearing clean clothes and a bandage covering his wound. Caleb then decapitates Daniel and the camera falls to the ground, before it cuts out.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last_Exorcism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film was slated to be a part of the <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> Film Festival 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> However, on February 12, 2010, <a href="/wiki/Lionsgate" title="Lionsgate">Lionsgate</a> purchased the rights for the US Distribution<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and pulled the film from the SXSW fest and set the release of the film for August 27, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film had its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_premiere" title="Film premiere">world premiere</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LA_Film_Festival" title="LA Film Festival">LA Film Festival</a> on June 24, 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and was here introduced by <a href="/wiki/Eli_Roth" title="Eli Roth">Eli Roth</a> and Daniel Stamm. Members of the cast were also introduced on stage, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Fabian" title="Patrick Fabian">Patrick Fabian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Bell" title="Ashley Bell">Ashley Bell</a>, Louis Herthum, <a href="/wiki/Caleb_Landry_Jones" title="Caleb Landry Jones">Caleb Landry Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iris_Bahr" title="Iris Bahr">Iris Bahr</a>, and Tony Bentley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <i>The Last Exorcism</i> was the last screened film on August 30, 2010, on the <a href="/wiki/Fright_Fest" title="Fright Fest">Film4 FrightFest</a> 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Disgusting" title="Bloody Disgusting">Bloody Disgusting</a> hosted the screening of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">2010 San Diego Comic-Con International</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and the second screening on 24 July 2010 is narrated by <a href="/wiki/Eli_Roth" title="Eli Roth">Eli Roth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Poster_controversy">Poster controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last_Exorcism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Poster controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">UK</a>, a poster image for the film showed a young girl, wearing a blood-spattered dress, bent over backwards below a crucifix. This poster received 77 complaints, the complainants describing it as "offensive", "distressing", and "unsuitable for public viewing". Two people also claimed the girl in the poster seemed to have suffered a <a href="/wiki/Sexual_assault" title="Sexual assault">sexual assault</a>, a complaint which was not upheld. The <a href="/wiki/Advertising_Standards_Authority_(United_Kingdom)" title="Advertising Standards Authority (United Kingdom)">Advertising Standards Agency</a> decided that the image could not be used on a publicly visible poster since that was an untargeted medium but was acceptable on the back cover of <i><a href="/wiki/Cineworld" title="Cineworld">Cineworld</a></i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Viral_campaign">Viral campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last_Exorcism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Viral campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Last Exorcism</i> used <a href="/wiki/Chatroulette" title="Chatroulette">Chatroulette</a> as the medium of its viral campaign involving a girl who pretends to unbutton her top seductively, then stops and turns into a monster. At the end, the URL of the film's official website is flashed on screen.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last_Exorcism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Last Exorcism</i> was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a> on January 4, 2011. The Blu-ray includes the DVD of the film and a <a href="/wiki/Digital_copy" title="Digital copy">digital copy</a> as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>

<p><i>The Last Exorcism</i> has received generally positive reviews from critics, garnering a 72% "Fresh" rating on <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a> <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> based on 151 reviews, with the site's consensus being "It doesn't fully deliver on the chilly promise of its <a href="/wiki/Blair_Witch" title="Blair Witch">Blair Witch</a>-style premise, but <i>The Last Exorcism</i> offers a surprising number of clever thrills."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The film received a 63 out of 100 on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, indicating "generally favorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Movies" title="Yahoo! Movies">Yahoo! Movies</a> the film holds a B- based on twelve reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last_Exorcism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Last Exorcism</i> opened at #2 at the U.S. box office the weekend of August 27, 2010, behind <i><a href="/wiki/Takers" title="Takers">Takers</a></i>. It grossed $20,366,613 from 2,874 theaters in its first three days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> <i>The Last Exorcism</i> had a budget of $1.8 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BoxOfficeMojo_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-BoxOfficeMojo-3">[3]</a></sup> The film remained in the top five, falling to number four in its second weekend. The film went on to gross $41 million domestically and $26.7 million foreign to total $67.7 million worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BoxOfficeMojo_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-BoxOfficeMojo-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last_Exorcism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Last_Exorcism_Part_II" title="The Last Exorcism Part II">The Last Exorcism Part II</a></div>
<p>On August 23, 2011, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> announced that a <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">sequel</a> was in the works.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comingsoon_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-comingsoon-34">[34]</a></sup> On April 20, 2012, an audition sheet revealed the film's full title as <i>Beginning of the End: The Last Exorcism II</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The film was directed by <a href="/wiki/Ed_Gass-Donnelly" title="Ed Gass-Donnelly">Ed Gass-Donnelly</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comingsoon_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-comingsoon-34">[34]</a></sup> with Damien Chazelle (<i><a href="/wiki/Guy_and_Madeline_on_a_Park_Bench" title="Guy and Madeline on a Park Bench">Guy and Madeline on a Park Bench</a></i>) providing the screenplay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Ashley Bell reprises her role as Nell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comingsoon_34-2"><a href="#cite_note-comingsoon-34">[34]</a></sup> On January 2, 2013, the poster for the film was released, revealing the final title to be <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Exorcism_Part_II" title="The Last Exorcism Part II">The Last Exorcism Part II</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The film was also produced by <a href="/wiki/Eli_Roth" title="Eli Roth">Eli Roth</a>.</p>
