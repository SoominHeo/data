<p><b>Old Havana</b> (Spanish: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i><b>La Habana Vieja</b></i></span>) is the city-center (downtown) and one of the 15 municipalities (or boroughs) forming <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. It has the second highest population density in the city and contains the core of the original city of Havana. The positions of the original Havana city walls are the modern boundaries of Old Havana.</p>
<p>Old Havana is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNESCO_World_Heritage_Site" title="UNESCO World Heritage Site">UNESCO World Heritage Site</a>.</p>

<p>Havana Vieja was founded by the Spanish in 1519 in the natural harbor of the Bay of Havana. It became a stopping point for the treasure laden Spanish <a href="/wiki/Galleon" title="Galleon">Galleons</a> on the crossing between the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a>. In the 17th century it was one of the main shipbuilding centers. The city was built in <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">baroque</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neoclassicism" title="Neoclassicism">neoclassic</a> style. Many buildings have fallen in ruin in the later half of the 20th century, but a number are being restored. The narrow streets of Old Havana contain many buildings, accounting for perhaps as many as one-third of the approximately 3,000 buildings found in Old Havana. It is the ancient city formed from the port, the official center and the Plaza de Armas. In 1555 Old Havana was destroyed and burned by the French corsair <a href="/wiki/Jacques_de_Sores" title="Jacques de Sores">Jacques de Sores</a>. The pirate had taken Havana easily, plundering the city and burning much of it to the ground. After limiting the scarce defenders, De Sores left without obtaining the enormous wealth that he was hoping to find in Havana. The city remained devastated and set on fire. Since the incident, the Spanish brought soldiers and started building fortresses and walls to protect the city. <a href="/wiki/Castillo_de_la_Real_Fuerza" title="Castillo de la Real Fuerza">Castillo de la Real Fuerza</a> was the first fortress built; initiated in 1558, the construction was overseen by the engineer Bartolomé Sanchez.</p>

<p>Old Havana resembles Cadiz and Tenerife. <a href="/wiki/Alejo_Carpentier" title="Alejo Carpentier">Alejo Carpentier</a> called it "de las columnas"(of the columns), but it could also be named for the gateways, the revoco, the deterioration and the rescue, the intimacy, the shade, the cool, the courtyards... In her there are all the big ancient monuments, the forts, the convents and churches, the palaces, the alleys, the arcade, the human density. The Cuban State has undertaken enormous efforts to preserve and to restore Old Havana through the efforts of the Office of the Historian of the City, directed by <a href="/wiki/Eusebio_Leal" title="Eusebio Leal">Eusebio Leal</a>.</p>

<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Malec%C3%B3n,_Havana" title="Malecón, Havana">Malecón</a> is the avenue that runs along the seawall at the northern shore of Havana, from Old Havana to the <a href="/wiki/Almendares_River" title="Almendares River">Almendares River</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Paseo_del_Prado,_Havana" title="Paseo del Prado, Havana">Paseo del Prado, Havana</a> is the street that forms the western edge of Old Havana, being its boundary with <a href="/wiki/Centro_Habana" title="Centro Habana">Centro Habana</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Morro_Castle_(fortress)" title="Morro Castle (fortress)">Castillo del Morro</a>, picturesque fortress guarding the entrance to Havana bay. The construction of the castle Los Tres Reyes del Morro owed to the step along in Havana of the English pirate Sir Francis Drake. The king of Spain arranged its construction on a big stone which was known by the name of El Morro. He sent the field master Juan de Texeda, accompanied of the military engineer Battista Antonelli, who came to Havana in 1587 and began the task at once.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/La_Caba%C3%B1a" title="La Cabaña">La Cabaña</a> fortress, located on the east side of the Havana Bay. The most impressive fortress of the Spanish colony was La Cabaña. It impresses with its 18th-century walls, constructed at the same time as El Morro. Every night at 9 p.m., some soldiers dressed in suits of the epoch shoot from her the “el cañonazo de las nueve”, (gunshot of the nine). It went off every day to warn of the closing of the doors of the wall that surrounded the city.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Salvador_de_la_Punta_Fortress" title="San Salvador de la Punta Fortress">San Salvador de la Punta Fortress</a>, In the shore opposite to the Castle of El Morro, at the beginning of the curve of El Malecon, there rises the fortress of San Salvador de la Punta, of minor architectural dimensions. It was constructed in 1590, and in 1629 the Chapter of Havana decided, to defend better the port, to join her in the night with the El Morro by using a thick chain that prevented the entry of enemy ships.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Castillo_de_la_Real_Fuerza" title="Castillo de la Real Fuerza">Castillo de la Real Fuerza</a>, The fortress or (lit.) Castle of the Royal Army is another big monument that closes the Plaza de las Armas. It was the first big fortification of the city, initiated in 1558 on the ruins of an ancient fortress. In the same year, the Crown sent to Cuba the engineer Bartolomé Sanchez, supervised by 14 official and main stonemasons in order to reconstruct the castle, which had been set fire and destroyed by the French corsair Jacques de Sores.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Havana_cathedral" title="Havana cathedral">Catedral de San Cristóbal</a>, the most prominent building on the <i><a href="/wiki/Plaza_de_la_Catedral" title="Plaza de la Catedral">Plaza de la Catedral</a></i>. The Cathedral was raised on the chapel after 1748 by order of the bishop from <a href="/wiki/Salamanca" title="Salamanca">Salamanca</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jose_Felipe_de_Trespalacios&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jose Felipe de Trespalacios (page does not exist)">Jose Felipe de Trespalacios</a>. It is one of the most beautiful and sober churches of the American baroque.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Capitol_Building_(Havana)" title="National Capitol Building (Havana)">National Capitol</a>, styled after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panth%C3%A9on_(Paris)" title="Panthéon (Paris)">Panthéon (Paris)</a>, looking similar to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Capitol" title="U.S. Capitol">U.S. Capitol</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Galician Center, Central Park, The Galician Center, of neobarroque style was established as a social club of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galician_people" title="Galician people">Galician</a> emigrants between 1907 and 1914. Built on the Theater Tacon (nowadays Great Theater of Havana), it was open during the Carnival of 1838 with five masked dances.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Plaza_de_Armas" title="Plaza de Armas">Plaza de Armas</a> – the main touristic square. The origin of its name is military, since from the end of the 16th century the ceremonies and the military events took place here.</li>
<li>Gran Teatro de la Havana, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Theater_of_Havana" title="Great Theater of Havana">Great Theater of Havana</a> is famous, particularly for the acclaimed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Ballet_of_Cuba" title="National Ballet of Cuba">National Ballet of Cuba</a> and its founder <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Alonso" title="Alicia Alonso">Alicia Alonso</a>. It sometimes performs the National Opera. The theater is also known as concert hall, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garcia_Lorca" title="Garcia Lorca">Garcia Lorca</a>, the biggest in Cuba.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_the_Revolution_(Cuba)" title="Museum of the Revolution (Cuba)">Museum of the Revolution</a>, located in the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presidential_Palace" title="Presidential Palace">Presidential Palace</a>, with the boat <a href="/wiki/Granma_(yacht)" title="Granma (yacht)">Granma</a> on display in front of the museum.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco_de_la_Habana_Basilica" title="San Francisco de la Habana Basilica">San Francisco de la Habana Basilica</a>, Habana Vieja, The set of church and convent of San Francisco de Asis, byline of the year 1608, and it was reconstructed in 1737.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Threats">Threats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Havana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Threats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Old_Havana_back_street.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1944" data-file-width="2592" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Old_Havana_back_street.JPG/220px-Old_Havana_back_street.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Old_Havana_back_street.JPG/330px-Old_Havana_back_street.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Old_Havana_back_street.JPG/440px-Old_Havana_back_street.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Old_Havana_back_street.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
An unrestored street in Old Havana</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ike" title="Hurricane Ike">Hurricane Ike</a> destroyed many structures in Old Havana, overturning years of conservation work directed at the iconic antiquated buildings of the area. Not only did it damage historic buildings, but it forced many of Old Havana's residents to flee for safety.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The threats that hurricanes pose adds to an already tenuous state for Old Havana's many historic buildings. Age, decay, and neglect combine with natural factors in a complex set of threats to the long-term preservation of this historic old town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="UNESCO_Heritage_site">UNESCO Heritage site</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Havana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: UNESCO Heritage site">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1982, La Habana Vieja was inscribed in the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage List</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> A safeguarding campaign was launched a year later to restore the authentic character of the buildings.</p>
