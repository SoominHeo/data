<p><b>Lae</b>(<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Morobe_Province" title="Morobe Province">Morobe Province</a> and is the second-largest city in <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>. It is located near the delta of the <a href="/wiki/Markham_River" title="Markham River">Markham River</a> and at the start of the <a href="/wiki/Highlands_Highway" title="Highlands Highway">Highlands Highway</a>, which is the main land transport corridor between the Highlands region and the coast. Lae is the largest cargo port of the country and is the industrial hub of Papua New Guinea. The city is known as the <i>Garden City</i> and home of the <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea_University_of_Technology" title="Papua New Guinea University of Technology">University of Technology</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morobe_Province_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Morobe_Province-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Lucas (1972)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> describes the history of Lae into four periods; the mission phase (1886–1920), the gold phase (1926 until World War II), the timber and agricultural phase (until 1965) and the industrial boom (from 1965 with the opening of the Highlands Highway<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Romaine1992_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Romaine1992-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Between 1884 and 1918 the <a href="/wiki/German_New_Guinea_Company" title="German New Guinea Company">German New Guinea Company</a> established trading posts in <a href="/wiki/Kaiser-Wilhelmsland" title="Kaiser-Wilhelmsland">Kaiser Wilhelmsland</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_New_Guinea" title="German New Guinea">German New Guinea</a> and on 12 July 1886, a German missionary, <a href="/wiki/Johann_Flierl" title="Johann Flierl">Johann Flierl</a>, a pioneer missionary for the Southern Australian Lutheran Synod and the <a href="/wiki/Johann_Flierl#Connection_to_Neuendettelsau_Mission_Society" title="Johann Flierl">Neuendettelsau Mission Society</a>, sailed to Simbang in <a href="/wiki/Finschhafen" title="Finschhafen">Finschhafen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kaiser-Wilhelmsland" title="Kaiser-Wilhelmsland">Kaiser-Wilhelmsland</a> and arrived at Lae shortly after.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The mission society provided clergy and religious education for Lutheran settlements in Missouri, Iowa and Ohio, Australia, and anywhere else "free thinking" Lutherans had settled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, Eastern New Guinea came under British control (Australia) and many of the Germanic names were replaced by English or Indigenous ones. <i>Adolf Haven</i> was then referred to as Morobe Harbor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Australian Officials or <a href="/wiki/Kiap" title="Kiap">Kiap</a>'s were stationed at various locations within the area <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and in 1921 the military administration transitioned to a civilian administration, a gold prospector named Cecil John Levien was appointed District Officer (Kiap) of Morobe.</p>

<p>On 1 January 1923 Levien acquired a mining right for the area and shortly after formed a syndicate called <i>Guinea Gold (No Liability)</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Guinea Gold syndicate formed Guinea Airways Limited in November 1927.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1927 Levien arranged for the construction of the airstrip at Lae to assist the gold mine productions around <a href="/wiki/Wau,_Papua_New_Guinea" title="Wau, Papua New Guinea">Wau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lae was declared a town under the <i>New Guinea Boundaries Ordinance</i> on 31 March 1931 at the height of the gold rush era <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and Lae became the prototype for New Guinean towns built up around <a href="/wiki/Lae_Airfield" title="Lae Airfield">airstrips</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Romaine1992_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Romaine1992-4">[4]</a></sup> The Europeans lived to the East of <a href="/wiki/Lae_Airfield" title="Lae Airfield">Lae Airfield</a> while the New Guineans lived to the West.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Romaine1992_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Romaine1992-4">[4]</a></sup> Cargo arrived in Lae and then was transported by air to the goldfields in <a href="/wiki/Wau,_Papua_New_Guinea" title="Wau, Papua New Guinea">Wau</a>.</p>
<p>In July 1937, Lae made world news when American aviator <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Earhart" title="Amelia Earhart">Amelia Earhart</a> was last seen flying out of the airport on her way back to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. She was never seen again.</p>
<p>When the volcanic eruptions occurred in <a href="/wiki/Rabaul" title="Rabaul">Rabaul</a> in 1937, a decision was made to transfer the capital of the Territory of New Guinea to Lae. <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> impeded the transfer and the town was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Lae-Salamaua" title="Invasion of Lae-Salamaua">occupied</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a> on 8 March 1942. Lae, Rabaul and <a href="/wiki/Salamaua" title="Salamaua">Salamaua</a> became the major Japanese bases in New Guinea.</p>
<p>The naval <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Bismarck_Sea" title="Battle of the Bismarck Sea">Battle of the Bismarck Sea</a> in March 1943<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> was fought over the Japanese attempt to reinforce Lae with troops sent by sea from Rabaul, an attempt foiled by sustained Allied attack on the Japanese troop transports. In mid-1943, after defeats in the <a href="/wiki/Kokoda_Track_campaign" title="Kokoda Track campaign">Kokoda Track campaign</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Buna%E2%80%93Gona" title="Battle of Buna–Gona">Battle of Buna–Gona</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Wau" title="Battle of Wau">Battle of Wau</a>, the Japanese retreated to Lae and Salamaua. However, the <a href="/wiki/Salamaua%E2%80%93Lae_campaign" title="Salamaua–Lae campaign">Salamaua–Lae campaign</a> involved many weeks of fierce fighting, before the town fell to the Allies on 16 September.</p>

<p>In 1971 the Australian Colonial Administration established the first properly constituted Local Government of Lae town and in 1972 Lae was proclaimed a city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morobe_Province_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Morobe_Province-2">[2]</a></sup> Lae's development after the war is directly linked to the development of the highlands. <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">Coffee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">tea</a> were being grown and a port was needed. Later priority was given on road access, and the Highlands Highway came into existence. The mineral boom occurred in the 1980s and 1990s.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_earthquakes_in_Papua_New_Guinea" title="List of earthquakes in Papua New Guinea">List of earthquakes in Papua New Guinea</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Maoke_Plate_map-fr.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="370" data-file-width="476" height="171" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Maoke_Plate_map-fr.png/220px-Maoke_Plate_map-fr.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Maoke_Plate_map-fr.png/330px-Maoke_Plate_map-fr.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Maoke_Plate_map-fr.png/440px-Maoke_Plate_map-fr.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Maoke_Plate_map-fr.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a> and tectonic plates: <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Plate" title="Pacific Plate">Pacific Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australian_Plate" title="Australian Plate">Australian Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Plate" title="Caroline Plate">Caroline Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Banda_Sea_Plate" title="Banda Sea Plate">Banda Sea Plate</a> (as "Mer de Banda"), <a href="/wiki/Woodlark_Plate" title="Woodlark Plate">Woodlark Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bird%27s_Head_Plate" title="Bird's Head Plate">Bird's Head Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maoke_Plate" title="Maoke Plate">Maoke Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Sea_Plate" title="Solomon Sea Plate">Solomon Sea Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Bismarck_Plate" title="North Bismarck Plate">North Bismarck Plate</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Bismarck_Plate" title="South Bismarck Plate">South Bismarck Plate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manus_Plate" title="Manus Plate">Manus Plate</a> (in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>).</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Lae is located on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Ring_of_Fire" title="Pacific Ring of Fire">Pacific Ring of Fire</a> and geologic instability has produced numerous <a href="/wiki/Fault_(geology)" title="Fault (geology)">faults</a>, resulting in earthquakes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lae sits between the larger <a href="/wiki/Indo-Australian_Plate" title="Indo-Australian Plate">Indo-Australian Plate</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Plate" title="Pacific Plate">Pacific Plate</a> on the <a href="/wiki/South_Bismarck_Plate" title="South Bismarck Plate">South Bismarck Plate</a> in the Ramu-Markham Fault Zone where the New Guinea Highlands Deforming Zone and South Bismarck <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tectonic_plates" title="Tectonic plates">tectonic plates</a> are converging at up to 50 mm/yr. The city is caught in a giant geological vice and the seismic hazard is significant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Association_of_Surveyors_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Association_of_Surveyors-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>More than 15 years of measurements have been analysed with results indicating how rapidly Lae city and its survey network is deforming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Association_of_Surveyors_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Association_of_Surveyors-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Ramu-Markham Fault Zone, which follows the northern edge of the Markham Valley, is the active plate boundary between the South Bismarck Plate and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terranes" title="Terranes">tectonostratigraphic terranes</a> within the New Guinea Highlands Deforming Zone. The Ramu-Markham Fault Zone has generated large thrust earthquakes (e.g. 6 April 1999 MW 6.4, 16 km North of Lae, near Hobu, and 22 November 2007 MW 6.8, 110 km North of Lae). Geological evidence suggests that major earthquakes in pre-historic times have occurred in the Lae area,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crook_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Crook-17">[17]</a></sup> and that there is the potential for another large earthquake to occur anytime within the next 100 years <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crook_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Crook-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> (in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Association_of_Surveyors_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-Association_of_Surveyors-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/1998_Papua_New_Guinea_earthquake" title="1998 Papua New Guinea earthquake">1998 earthquake</a> occurred near <a href="/wiki/Vanimo" title="Vanimo">Vanimo</a> resulting in 2200 casualties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC19980720_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC19980720-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mount_Lunaman">Mount Lunaman</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mount Lunaman">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mount Lunaman is 96 metres (315 ft) high and has a radio tower at the highest point marked by red fixed obstruction lights to assist navigation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> At the base of Mount Lunaman at the southern and south-eastern face are the suburbs of Voco Point and Chinatown. The terraces are located to the West of Mount Lunaman.</p>
<p>Mount Lunaman is known to the locals as <i>Lo' Wamung</i>, which means "first hill",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <i>Hospital Hill</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Laffin1956_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Laffin1956-22">[22]</a></sup> and Fortress Hill by the German settlers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mount Lunaman and the Lae urban area have been the subject of several tectonic studies relating to plate shift.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crook_Pages_105.E2.80.93118_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Crook_Pages_105.E2.80.93118-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mount Lunaman was an important landmark for both Japanese and the Allies:</p>
<dl>
<dd>"The men of the South Australian battalion hammered and sawed vigorously at the top of the terrace. They were reconstructing, with captured Japanese tools, the skeleton of the cottage formerly used as the Japanese commander's sanctum. A hole beneath the door led by an underground tunnel to a labyrinth of passages and apertures which honeycombed Mount Lunaman".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p>After the war it was believed that Mount Lunaman contained the remains of many Japanese soldiers who defended Lae using tunnels:</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lae City Council is also known as Lae Urban Local-Level Government. It is an Urban Municipal Authority, responsible for the policy decisions, management and administration of the City, by way of providing the municipal services to the residents of the City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morobe_Province_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Morobe_Province-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd><i>Lae City Aims to become one of the Garden Cities of Asia Pacific Region by 2012, by creating a modern, safe, vibrant City, with a strong economy, whilst enhancing its cultural heritage, and by developing a Healthy, well Educated, Harmonious Community, which will enjoys a good living environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morobe_Province_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Morobe_Province-2">[2]</a></sup></i></dd>
</dl>
<p>The Lae Urban Local-Level Government is a third-tier Government. The political structure consists of the Lord Mayor as the head, who is elected by the people, with five elected, and three nominated Councilors. The six elected Councilors representing the six Wards in the City. The nominated Councilors represented the Chamber of Commerce, the Workers Federation Union, and the Women, Youth and Churches. The Council makes decisions on the policy issues relating to the City, and the Council Management implement the decisions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morobe_Province_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Morobe_Province-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads">Roads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Roads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Lae City has 137 kilometers of roads, which the National Government is responsible for the maintenance of the Independence Drive, the Markham Road, and the Milford Haven Road, while the Lae City Council maintains the rest of the roads in the City. Due to lack of funding, almost all the roads have deteriorated over the years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morobe_Province_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-Morobe_Province-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Markets">Markets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Markets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lae City boasts of having the best food market in Papua New Guinea. This is due to the fact that the Morobe Province produces the best taros, bananas, <a href="/wiki/Sweet_potato" title="Sweet potato">sweet potatoes</a>, yams, fruits and vegetables etc., which have been sought after by many Papua New Guineans as well as expatriates. The Lae Main Market also receives and sells foodstuff and vegetables from the Highlands Provinces. Apart from Lae Main Market, wards and mini-markets are also available to cater for the needs of the growing population of the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morobe_Province_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-Morobe_Province-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="University_of_Technology">University of Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: University of Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea_University_of_Technology" title="Papua New Guinea University of Technology">Papua New Guinea University of Technology</a></div>
<p>The Papua New Guinea University of Technology is based 8 kilometres (5.0 mi) outside of Lae and is the second largest <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> in PNG after its 'sister' university the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Papua_New_Guinea" title="University of Papua New Guinea">University of Papua New Guinea</a> in <a href="/wiki/Port_Moresby" title="Port Moresby">Port Moresby</a>. While University of Papua New Guinea concentrates on the arts, pure sciences, law and medicine, the University of Technology focuses on research in technological or applied sciences. It is the only technological university in the <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">South Pacific</a>, outside of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lae_War_Cemetery">Lae War Cemetery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Lae War Cemetery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lae_War_Cemetery" title="Lae War Cemetery">Lae War Cemetery</a></div>
<p>Lae War Cemetery was established in 1944, and is located adjacent to the Botanical Gardens in the center of the city. The cemetery holds the remains of over 2,800 soldiers, many of whom died in the <a href="/wiki/Salamaua%E2%80%93Lae_campaign" title="Salamaua–Lae campaign">Salamaua–Lae campaign</a>, but also those who died in Japanese detention on the Island. It is also the resting place of two <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Cross" title="Victoria Cross">Victoria Cross</a> recipients.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lae_Botanic_Gardens">Lae Botanic Gardens</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Lae Botanic Gardens">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lae_Botanic_Gardens" title="Lae Botanic Gardens">Lae Botanic Gardens</a></div>
<p>Lae Botanic gardens are beautifully maintained but not always open to the public. There is an old Australian Aeroplane (circa World War II) in the center of the gardens.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bumbu_river">Bumbu river</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Bumbu river">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Bumbu river starts at the Adzera Mountain range, through Taraka to Kamkumung, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Butibam" title="Butibam">Butibam</a>, Bumbu and to the <a href="/wiki/Huon_Gulf" title="Huon Gulf">Huon Gulf</a>. Following a flood in 1992, the population was relocated to a temporary settlement called Tent Siti (City).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morobe_Province_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-Morobe_Province-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Angau_General_Hospital">Angau General Hospital</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Angau General Hospital">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/ANGAU_General_Hospital" title="ANGAU General Hospital">ANGAU General Hospital</a></div>
<p>Angau General Hospital is located in the central Lae area next to the old <a href="/wiki/Lae_Airfield" title="Lae Airfield">Lae Airfield</a>. It is a main referral hospital for the general Morobe Province area, as well as the other provinces connected by road link . It contains the only Radiotherapy facility in the country and thus serves as the main referral center for cancer patients.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lae_.28Nadzab.29_Airport">Lae (Nadzab) Airport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Lae (Nadzab) Airport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lae_Nadzab_Airport" title="Lae Nadzab Airport">Lae Nadzab Airport</a></div>
<p>Nadzab Airport is located 56 kilometres (35 mi) outside of Lae City, along the <a href="/wiki/Highlands_Highway" title="Highlands Highway">Highlands Highway</a>, next to the Markham River. There are local buses that will take you to Lae, and also escorted secured transfers can transfer you from Nadzab Airport to your accommodation in Lae City.</p>
<p>From Port Moresby, Lae is accessible only by domestic flights. Air Niugini, Airlines of Papua New Guinea (also known as "Airlines PNG") and Travel Air "Mangi lo Ples" services the Port Moresby-Lae route.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_care">Health care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Health care">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Visitors must ensure that they have proper health insurance. Lae International Hospital is of good developing world standard. It is a 19-bed facility with 1 emergency bed. It has 6 doctors, one General Practitioner, an Anesthesiologist, a Surgeon, a Radiologist and two Obstetricians. All doctors are from the Philippines and all nurses are local nationals.</p>
<p>The other private hospital is Tusa Private Hospital (corner of Huon Rd and 13th St). There is also a public hospital called Angau Memorial Hospital located on Markham Road.</p>
<p>For travelers to Lae, they should seek expert medical advice regarding malaria prophylaxis as well as Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, Japanese Encephalitis and Typhoid vaccinations. Of note there was a significant outbreak of Cholera based in the Morobe District in 2009 and consideration of vaccination would be prudent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
