<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laos_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Laos established their national football association in 1951. The South East Asian nation is still waiting to make its entrance into a major international competition. Laos have never qualified for the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_Cup" title="Asian Cup">Asian Cup</a> or <a href="/wiki/Asian_Games" title="Asian Games">Asian Games</a> and as an international side, their appearances have been restricted to regional tournaments such as the <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asian_Games" title="Southeast Asian Games">Southeast Asian Games</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ASEAN_Football_Championship" title="ASEAN Football Championship">Tiger Cup</a>. After years of internal strife, <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a> is well on the road to economic and political recovery. With the country achieving political stability, football has made an impact on Laotians. Since making their appearance at the <a href="/wiki/1995_Southeast_Asian_Games" title="1995 Southeast Asian Games">1995 Southeast Asian Games</a>, Laos has competed in the ASEAN Football Championship (Tiger Cup). Although new to the regional tournaments, Laos has displayed passion and talent. In 1995, they beat Brunei and the Philippines and two years later in the Jakarta SEA Games, they also beat Malaysia. Domestic competitions are also active with over 60 clubs competing at various levels. Domestic football is amateur although most of the top teams are drawn from government ministries and public services. In the qualifying preliminary rounds for the <a href="/wiki/2004_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2004 AFC Asian Cup">2004 AFC Asian Cup</a>, they beat <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_national_football_team" title="Bangladesh national football team">Bangladesh</a> 2–1. In the 2006 World Cup Asian zone qualifiers, they qualified for the second round as a lucky loser after <a href="/wiki/Guam_national_football_team" title="Guam national football team">Guam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nepal_national_football_team" title="Nepal national football team">Nepal</a> both withdrew from competition, but proceeded to lose all its games (with Qatar, Iran and Jordan). They also advanced to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_-_AFC_Second_Round" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification - AFC Second Round">second round</a> of <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(AFC)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC)">Asian qualifying</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, after defeating <a href="/wiki/Cambodia_national_football_team" title="Cambodia national football team">Cambodia</a> 8–6 on aggregate. In the second round, they lost to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China_PR_national_football_team" title="China PR national football team">China PR</a> 13–3 on aggregate. Laos has defeated their much more established counterparts such as <a href="/wiki/Brunei_national_football_team" title="Brunei national football team">Brunei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cambodia_national_football_team" title="Cambodia national football team">Cambodia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines_national_football_team" title="Philippines national football team">Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore_national_football_team" title="Singapore national football team">Singapore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_national_football_team" title="Malaysia national football team">Malaysia</a>. Laos' first appearance in a continental tournament was in 2014, when they played at the <a href="/wiki/2014_AFC_Challenge_Cup" title="2014 AFC Challenge Cup">2014 AFC Challenge Cup</a>.</p>
