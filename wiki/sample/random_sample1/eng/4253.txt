<p><b>Rayo OKC</b>, also known as <b>Rayo Oklahoma City</b>, was an American professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">soccer</a> team based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City,_Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma City, Oklahoma">Oklahoma City, Oklahoma</a>. They joined the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Soccer_League" title="North American Soccer League">North American Soccer League</a> (NASL) – the second tier of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_soccer_pyramid" title="American soccer pyramid">American soccer pyramid</a> – beginning with the <a href="/wiki/2016_North_American_Soccer_League_season" title="2016 North American Soccer League season">2016 season</a>. They played home games at <a href="/wiki/Yukon_High_School" title="Yukon High School">Yukon High School</a>'s Miller Stadium in <a href="/wiki/Yukon,_Oklahoma" title="Yukon, Oklahoma">Yukon, Oklahoma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The club suspended operations following the 2016 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rayo_OKC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On November 10, 2015, the NASL announced that a new club called Rayo OKC would begin to play in the 2016 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The new team is majority-owned by Raúl Martín Presa, the owner of <a href="/wiki/Rayo_Vallecano" title="Rayo Vallecano">Rayo Vallecano</a> of the Spanish <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a>, with a minority stake owned by Oklahoma native Sean Jones former owner of the Oklahoma City FC franchise, Rayo OKC was also co-founded and managed by "Sold Out Strategies," a sports management company headed by former <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_Blazers_(1992%E2%80%932009)" title="Oklahoma City Blazers (1992–2009)">Oklahoma City Blazers</a> general manager Brad Lund. Former <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio_Scorpions" title="San Antonio Scorpions">San Antonio Scorpions</a> head coach <a href="/wiki/Alen_Marcina" title="Alen Marcina">Alen Marcina</a> was named Rayo head coach on January 7, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcina_Appointed_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Marcina_Appointed-6">[6]</a></sup> Marcina resigned on August 1, being replaced by Gerard Nus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nus_Replaces_Marcina_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nus_Replaces_Marcina-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2016, it was announced that co-founding partner and management Sold Out Strategies had pulled out of Rayo OKC'S day-to-day operations. In September, it was reported that minority owner Sean Jones had part of the temporary artificial playing field removed during the night and held at an undisclosed location to protect his investment pending a resolution. After a week and a half of negotiations it was reported that Rayo OKC management and Sean Jones had reached a mutual agreement and the missing portions of the artificial playing turf were returned.</p>
<p>On November 7, 2016, following the conclusion of the 2016 NASL Playoffs, head coach Gerard Nus stepped down and returned to Spain to continue his role as assistant technical director for Rayo OKC'S parent club, Rayo Vallecano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nus_Leaves_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nus_Leaves-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 1, 2016, it reported that Rayo OKC had released all of their players and did not have its majority owners or anyone from the Rayo OKC management representing them at the NASL's board of directors meeting in Atlanta.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In January 2017, it was confirmed by the NASL that Rayo OKC would not be participating in the 2017 season, eventually resulting in the dissolving the franchise.</p>
