<p>The <b>Lawra District</b> is one of the nine (9) <a href="/wiki/District" title="District">districts</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Upper_West_Region" title="Upper West Region">Upper West Region</a> of north <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Lawra" title="Lawra">Lawra</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawra_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lawra District is one of the nine Districts that make up the Upper West Region and derives its legal existence from Legislative Instrument (L.I) 1434 of 1988.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawra_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawra_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lawra District lies in the North Western corner of the Upper West Region between Longitudes 2°25"W and 2°45"W and Latitudes 10°20"N and 11°00"N.</p>
<p>It is bounded to the East and South by the <a href="/wiki/Jirapa/Lambussie_District" title="Jirapa/Lambussie District">Jirapa/Lambussie District</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lambussie_Karni_District" title="Lambussie Karni District">Lambussie-Karni District</a>. The total area of the District as at 2014 (GSS, 2012) is estimated to be 483.6 square kilometres. This constitutes about 2.6% of the Region’s total area, which is estimated at 18,476 square kilometres. This follows the splitting of Nandom in 2012 as its own district.</p>
