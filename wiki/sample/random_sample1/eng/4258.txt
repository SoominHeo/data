<p><b>Lautém</b> (<a href="/wiki/Tetum_language" title="Tetum language">Tetum</a>: <span lang="tet" xml:lang="tet"><i>Lautein</i></span>) is one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Districts_of_East_Timor" title="Districts of East Timor">districts</a> of <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">East Timor</a>, on the eastern end of the island of <a href="/wiki/Timor" title="Timor">Timor</a>. It has a population of 64,135 (census 2010) and an area of 1,813 km². Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Lospalos" title="Lospalos">Lospalos</a>, which lies 248 km east of the national capital, <a href="/wiki/Dili" title="Dili">Dili</a>. The subdistricts are <a href="/wiki/Iliomar_Subdistrict" title="Iliomar Subdistrict">Iliomar</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Laut%C3%A9m_Subdistrict&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lautém Subdistrict (page does not exist)">Lautém</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lospalos_Subdistrict&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lospalos Subdistrict (page does not exist)">Lospalos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luro_Subdistrict" title="Luro Subdistrict">Luro</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tutuala_Subdistrict&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tutuala Subdistrict (page does not exist)">Tutuala</a>.</p>
<p>To the west the district borders the districts of <a href="/wiki/Baucau_District" title="Baucau District">Baucau</a> and <a href="/wiki/Viqueque_District" title="Viqueque District">Viqueque</a>. To the north lies the <a href="/wiki/Banda_Sea" title="Banda Sea">Banda Sea</a>, and to the south the <a href="/wiki/Timor_Sea" title="Timor Sea">Timor Sea</a>. The district also includes the easternmost point of the island, <i>Kap Cutcha</i> in the subdistrict of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tutuala_Subdistrict&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tutuala Subdistrict (page does not exist)">Tutuala</a>, and the small island <a href="/wiki/Jaco_Island" title="Jaco Island">Jaco</a>.</p>
<p>The district of Lautém is identical to what was the council of the same name in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Timor" title="Portuguese Timor">Portuguese Timor</a>. At that time, many of the localities had Portuguese names, such as <i>Vila Nova de Malaca</i> (today <a href="/wiki/Laut%C3%A9m_(city)" title="Lautém (city)">Lautém</a>), <i>Nova Nazaré</i> (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Com_(East_Timor)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Com (East Timor) (page does not exist)">Com</a>), <i>Nova Sagres</i> (<a href="/wiki/Tutuala" title="Tutuala">Tutuala</a>) and <i>Nova Âncora</i> (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Laivai&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Laivai (page does not exist)">Laivai</a>).</p>
<p>Lautém has beautiful sand beaches and a wild, mountainous, and raw rugged unspoilt landscape. Many of the endemic birds of East Timor live here. Near the city of Lautém there are cave drawings. Numerous stone sarcophagi and animistic shrines are found throughout the district.</p>
<p>In addition to the official languages of <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tetum" title="Tetum">Tetum</a>, in the district there are 30,000 speakers of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papuan_language" title="Papuan language">Papuan language</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fataluku" title="Fataluku">Fataluku</a>, mainly in the east of the district, many of whom do not speak a second language.</p>
<p>Lautém District is known for its birding (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Birds_of_East_Timor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Birds of East Timor (page does not exist)">birds of East Timor</a>).</p>
