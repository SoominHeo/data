<p><b>Hüseyin Rauf Orbay</b> (27 July 1881 – 16 July 1964) was an <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> naval officer, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkish</a> statesman and diplomat.</p>
<p>Hüseyin Rauf was born in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> in 1881 to an <a href="/wiki/Abkhazians" title="Abkhazians">Abkhazian</a> family Ashkharua.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-google_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-google-1">[1]</a></sup> As an officer in the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Navy" title="Ottoman Navy">Ottoman Navy</a>, he achieved fame for his actions as the captain of the <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruiser</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Ottoman_cruiser_Hamidiye" title="Ottoman cruiser Hamidiye">Hamidiye</a></i> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Balkan_War" title="First Balkan War">First Balkan War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was Chief of Naval Staff during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and by October 1918 was Minister of Marine and led the delegation that signed the <a href="/wiki/Armistice_of_Mudros" title="Armistice of Mudros">Armistice of Mudros</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Rauf Orbay also played a role in assisting <a href="/wiki/Mustafa_Kemal_Atat%C3%BCrk" title="Mustafa Kemal Atatürk">Mustafa Kemal Atatürk</a> in a near court-martial during a feud with <a href="/wiki/Djemal_Pasha" title="Djemal Pasha">Djemal Pasha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Enver_Pasha" title="Enver Pasha">Enver Pasha</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 31 October 1918, he signed <a href="/wiki/Armistice_of_Mudros" title="Armistice of Mudros">Armistice of Mudros</a> as the Minister of Navy, which ended the Ottoman Empire's participation in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. When the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_War_of_Independence" title="Turkish War of Independence">Turkish War of Independence</a> began, he resigned from his position and went to <a href="/wiki/Ankara" title="Ankara">Ankara</a> to collaborate with <a href="/wiki/Mustafa_Kemal_Atat%C3%BCrk" title="Mustafa Kemal Atatürk">Mustafa Kemal Atatürk</a>. He was elected as a member of the representative committee in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congress_of_Erzurum" title="Congress of Erzurum">Congress of Erzurum</a> on 23 July 1919. He joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congress_of_Sivas" title="Congress of Sivas">Congress of Sivas</a> as a delegate for <a href="/wiki/Sivas" title="Sivas">Sivas</a> on 4 September 1919 and was elected deputy chairman.</p>
<p>When the War of Independence ended he became the first <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Turkey" title="List of Prime Ministers of Turkey">Prime Minister</a> of the new provisional <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Grand_National_Assembly" title="Government of the Grand National Assembly">Government of the Grand National Assembly</a> on 11 August 1922. In 1924, he was one of the founders of the <i>Terakkiperver Cumhuriyet Fırkasi</i> (<a href="/wiki/Progressive_Republican_Party_(Turkey)" title="Progressive Republican Party (Turkey)">Progressive Republican Party</a>) at the request of Atatürk as part of Atatürk's attempt to begin the tradition of multiparty democracy in the young Republic, in opposition to Atatürk's <a href="/wiki/Republican_People%27s_Party_(Turkey)" title="Republican People's Party (Turkey)">Republican People's Party</a>. When this party was closed down in 1925 after Atatürk found that <a href="/wiki/Islamism" title="Islamism">Islamist</a> reactionaries had infiltrated its ranks, Rauf went to exile in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> for 10 years. Later, he was cleared of all accusations and became a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish_Grand_National_Assembly" title="Turkish Grand National Assembly">Turkish parliament</a>.</p>

<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Rauf Orbay was the Turkish ambassador in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> helping keep Turkey out of the war. He always firmly believed in the Republic of Turkey and always stressed that <a href="/wiki/Mustafa_Kemal_Atat%C3%BCrk" title="Mustafa Kemal Atatürk">Mustafa Kemal Atatürk</a> was the only person who could have organised and lead the transformation of the crumbling Ottoman Empire into modern Turkey.</p>
