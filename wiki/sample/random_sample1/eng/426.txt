<p>The <b>2010 G20 Seoul Summit</b> was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_G20_summits" title="List of G20 summits">fifth meeting</a> of the <a href="/wiki/G20" title="G20">G20</a> heads of government/heads of state, to discuss the <a href="/wiki/Global_financial_system" title="Global financial system">global financial system</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_economy" title="World economy">world economy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koreatimes1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-koreatimes1-1">[1]</a></sup> which took place in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, South Korea, on November 11–12, 2010. Korea was the first non-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G8" title="G8">G8</a> nation to host a G20 leaders' summit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The G20 is the premier forum for discussing, planning, and monitoring international economic cooperation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The theme of the summit was "Shared Growth Beyond Crisis".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Agenda">Agenda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_G20_Seoul_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Agenda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The summit leaders addressed several mid- and long-term policy issues,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> including</p>
<ul>
<li>Ensuring global economic recovery<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-agenda1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-agenda1-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Framework for strong, sustainable, and balanced global growth<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-agenda1_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-agenda1-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Strengthening the international <a href="/wiki/Financial_regulation" title="Financial regulation">financial regulatory system</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-agenda1_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-agenda1-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Modernizing the <a href="/wiki/International_financial_institutions" title="International financial institutions">international financial institutions</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-agenda1_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-agenda1-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Global financial safety nets<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-agenda2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-agenda2-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>Development issues<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-agenda2_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-agenda2-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>The risk of a <a href="/wiki/Currency_war" title="Currency war">currency war</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Representatives met in advance of the leaders' summit. These <a href="/wiki/Sherpa_(emissary)" title="Sherpa (emissary)">sherpas</a> were tasked to draft a closing statement for the summit. The debate over currency <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exchange_rates" title="Exchange rates">exchange rates</a> and imbalances was reported to have been "heated".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_G20_Seoul_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2010_G20_Seoul_summit_preparations" title="2010 G20 Seoul summit preparations">2010 G20 Seoul summit preparations</a></div>
<p>The summit logo incorporated two images: the sun rising over the sea and a traditional Korean lantern (<i><a href="/wiki/Cheongsachorong" title="Cheongsachorong">cheongsachorong</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koreatimes1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-koreatimes1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Originally, three new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artificial_islands" title="Artificial islands">artificial islands</a> built on the <a href="/wiki/Han_River_(Korea)" title="Han River (Korea)">Han River</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Banpo_Bridge" title="Banpo Bridge">Banpo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dongjak_Bridge" title="Dongjak Bridge">Dongjak bridges</a> were going to be used as the main venue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-koreatimes2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-koreatimes2-9">[9]</a></sup> However, delayed construction of the islands led for the main summit venue to relocate to <a href="/wiki/COEX_Convention_%26_Exhibition_Center" title="COEX Convention &amp; Exhibition Center">COEX Convention &amp; Exhibition Center</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Korea_Armed_Forces" title="Republic of Korea Armed Forces">Republic of Korea Armed Forces</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Metropolitan_Police_Agency" title="Seoul Metropolitan Police Agency">Seoul Metropolitan Police Agency</a> provided security for the venues.</p>
<p>A group of <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> artists, consisting of <a href="/wiki/Park_Gyu-ri" title="Park Gyu-ri">Gyu-ri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seohyun" title="Seohyun">Seohyun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jun._K" title="Jun. K">Jun. K</a>, <a href="/wiki/2AM_(band)" title="2AM (band)">Changmin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rainbow_(South_Korean_band)" title="Rainbow (South Korean band)">Jaekyung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-hyun_(singer)" title="Kim Jong-hyun (singer)">Jonghyun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Sung-min_(singer)" title="Lee Sung-min (singer)">Sungmin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kahi_(entertainer)" title="Kahi (entertainer)">Kahi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luna_(singer)" title="Luna (singer)">Luna</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Song_Jieun" title="Song Jieun">Ji Eun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yong_Jun-hyung" title="Yong Jun-hyung">Junhyung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heo_Ga-yoon" title="Heo Ga-yoon">Gayoon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Min_(artist)" title="Min (artist)">Min</a>, <a href="/wiki/G.O_(singer)" title="G.O (singer)">G.O</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bumkey" title="Bumkey">Bumkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/G.NA" title="G.NA">G.NA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Son_Dam-bi" title="Son Dam-bi">Son Dam-bi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seo_In-guk" title="Seo In-guk">Seo In-guk</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IU_(singer)" title="IU (singer)">IU</a>, and Anna, credited as <i>Group of 20</i> recorded a song titled "Let's Go" for the summit.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_G20_Seoul_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most world leaders and international media arrived via <a href="/wiki/Incheon_International_Airport" title="Incheon International Airport">Incheon International Airport</a> and traveled to the summit venue via motorcades along the highway from the airport.</p>
<p>Transportation around the summit venue was upgraded with electric buses to help media and others around the city.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attendance">Attendance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_G20_Seoul_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Attendance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Obama_and_Erdo%C4%9Fan_speaking.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2731" data-file-width="4096" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Obama_and_Erdo%C4%9Fan_speaking.jpg/220px-Obama_and_Erdo%C4%9Fan_speaking.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Obama_and_Erdo%C4%9Fan_speaking.jpg/330px-Obama_and_Erdo%C4%9Fan_speaking.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Obama_and_Erdo%C4%9Fan_speaking.jpg/440px-Obama_and_Erdo%C4%9Fan_speaking.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

American President Obama and Turkish Prime Minister Erdoğan in conversation.</div>

</div>
<p>The participants of the Seoul summit included the leaders and representatives of core members of the G20, which comprises 19 countries and the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, which is represented by its two governing bodies, the <a href="/wiki/European_Council" title="European Council">European Council</a> and the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Representatives from other countries and regional organizations were invited to take part in the summit.</p>
<p>The South Korean government declined to invite the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, which had been invited to attend all four previous G20 summits. A Korean spokesman said that "a certain region had been over-represented" in the past; and for this Asian summit, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> was invited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cho_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-cho-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>This was the first summit at which there were four women among the leaders. In addition to President <a href="/wiki/Cristina_Fern%C3%A1ndez_de_Kirchner" title="Cristina Fernández de Kirchner">Cristina Fernández de Kirchner</a> of <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Julia_Gillard" title="Julia Gillard">Julia Gillard</a> of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, Chancellor <a href="/wiki/Angela_Merkel" title="Angela Merkel">Angela Merkel</a> of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, and the president-elect of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dilma_Rousseff" title="Dilma Rousseff">Dilma Rousseff</a>, accompanied her nation's delegation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>This was the first G20 summit for Australia's Prime Minister Gillard, who had only been elected shortly before the <a href="/wiki/2010_G20_Toronto_summit" title="2010 G20 Toronto summit">Toronto summit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> This was also the first opportunity for Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hsien_Loong" title="Lee Hsien Loong">Lee Hsien Loong</a> of <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> to listen and to make his voice heard at the G20 leaders' meetings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cho_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-cho-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Security">Security</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_G20_Seoul_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Security">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Security for the G20 summit presented a unique array of problems. In addition to the security of the main venue, <a href="/wiki/COEX_Convention_%26_Exhibition_Center" title="COEX Convention &amp; Exhibition Center">COEX</a>, South Korea was more broadly responsible for providing a safe venue for the delegations who come to the summit. The <a href="/wiki/National_Police_Agency_(South_Korea)" title="National Police Agency (South Korea)">National Police Agency</a> led the security detail for the summit, both at the convention venue and the airport as well. Other police and security agencies involved were:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Presidential_Security_Service" title="Presidential Security Service">Presidential Security Service</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_Intelligence_Service_(South_Korea)" title="National Intelligence Service (South Korea)">National Intelligence Service</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Defense_(Republic_of_Korea)" title="Ministry of National Defense (Republic of Korea)">Ministry of National Defense</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Public_Administration_and_Security_(South_Korea)" title="Ministry of Public Administration and Security (South Korea)">National Emergency Management Agency</a></li>
</ul>

<p>In preparation, anti-terrorism drills were held by members of the South Korean police, military, special forces and private sector as part of the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Ulchi-Freedom_Guardian" title="Ulchi-Freedom Guardian">Ulchi-Freedom Guardian</a> exercises against simulated hostage situations and chemical, biological and radiological attacks as a preparation for the summit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Plans for accommodating peaceful protesters were paired with plans for mitigating disruptive demonstrations.</p>
<p>The G20 raised security concerns unrelated to demonstrators protesting the presence of the leaders of 20 economies in Seoul. For example, some analysts projected that anything perceived as a success for South Korea would be simultaneously construed in <a href="/wiki/Pyongyang" title="Pyongyang">Pyongyang</a> as a threat to North Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite public endorsements by attending leaders, most commentators looking back on the summit have argued that only limited progress was made, especially on the headline issue of currency war and addressing trade imbalances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shuns_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-shuns-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Leaders were generally unable to agree on key issues, with commentators such as economist <a href="/wiki/Eswar_Prasad" title="Eswar Prasad">Eswar Prasad</a> noting the absence of the sense of unity that had been present at summits during the worse of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007%E2%80%932010" title="Financial crisis of 2007–2010">global financial crisis of 2007–2009</a>. IMF managing director <a href="/wiki/Dominique_Strauss-Kahn" title="Dominique Strauss-Kahn">Dominique Strauss-Kahn</a> said this particular summit was "more of a G20 debate than a G20 conclusion".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shuns_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-shuns-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>Relating to the need to <a href="/wiki/Balance_of_payments#Imbalances" title="Balance of payments">rebalance</a> the world economy, agreement had been reached to work on indicative guidelines which will set suggested maximum limits for <a href="/wiki/Current_account" title="Current account">current account</a> surpluses and deficits, though these are not due to be fleshed out until 2011. G20 leaders also agreed to endorse the <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Development_Consensus" title="Seoul Development Consensus">Seoul Development Consensus</a>, a set of guidelines and principles for working together with less development nations to improve economic growth and reduce poverty. In contrast to the older <a href="/wiki/Washington_Consensus" title="Washington Consensus">Washington Consensus</a> which it supersedes, the Seoul Consensus is less free market–orientated, allowing a larger role for state intervention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
