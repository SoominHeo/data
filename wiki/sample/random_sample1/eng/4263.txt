<p><b>Raúl Fernández Valverde</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ɾaˈul feɾˈnandeð βalˈβeɾðe]</a></span>; born 6 October 1985 in <a href="/wiki/Lima" title="Lima">Lima</a>, Peru) is a professional Peruvian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> goalkeeper. He started to play for the youth team of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universitario_de_Deportes" title="Universitario de Deportes">Universitario de Deportes</a> and made his way to the first-team.</p>
<p>He is also goalkeeper of the <a href="/wiki/Peru_national_football_team" title="Peru national football team">Peru national football team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Fern%C3%A1ndez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Universitario_de_Deportes">Universitario de Deportes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Fern%C3%A1ndez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Universitario de Deportes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fernández got his start in the youth system of Universitario de Deportes of the Peruvian first division. He made his professional debut with the club in a 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Uni%C3%B3n_Huaral" title="Unión Huaral">Unión Huaral</a> on July 31, 2005.</p>
<p>After Juan Flores signed with <a href="/wiki/Cienciano" title="Cienciano">Cienciano</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_Peruana_2007" title="Primera División Peruana 2007">2007 season</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Carvallo" title="José Carvallo">José Carvallo</a> took over as Universitario's starting goalkeeper. Carvallo gained the team's trust because of his goalkeeping skills. However, during the Clausura 2007, the coach Julio Gomez decided to give Fernández a chance to start. He made his debut on 22 September 2007 playing against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Total_Clean" title="Total Clean">Total Clean</a> and gained Gomez's trust because of his style of goalkeeping. Fernández became the starting goalkeeper of Universitario after that game.</p>
<p>In 2008, his second season in Universitario, Fernàndez was immediately inserted into the starting eleven and appeared in 26 official matches, helping his team to win the <a href="/wiki/2008_Torneo_Descentralizado" title="2008 Torneo Descentralizado">Torneo Apertura 2008</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Gareca" title="Ricardo Gareca">Ricardo Gareca</a> as a coach.</p>
<p>Fernandez was one of the key players to Universitario's success in the Peruvian domestic league of 2009. His performances throughout the year, especially in the first play-off game against <a href="/wiki/Alianza_Lima" title="Alianza Lima">Alianza Lima</a> got the attention of international scouts who would later contact him from various clubs in Europe. Fernández would go on to play in 143 matches for Universitario between his debut and 2010.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="OGC_Nice">OGC Nice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Fern%C3%A1ndez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: OGC Nice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fernández signed with <a href="/wiki/OGC_Nice" title="OGC Nice">OGC Nice</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> on December 2010. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universitario_de_Deportes" title="Universitario de Deportes">Universitario de Deportes</a> opted to take him on loan for six months. He made his debut for OGC Nice in the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_France" title="Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a>, in a 1–2 victory against <a href="/wiki/Toulouse_FC" title="Toulouse FC">Toulouse</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Stadium_Municipal" title="Stadium Municipal">Stadium Municipal</a>. He had a good performance in the OCG Nice's match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dijon_FC" title="Dijon FC">Dijon</a>, when he stopped a penalty and led his team to the semifinals of the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_France" title="Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FC_Dallas">FC Dallas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Fern%C3%A1ndez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: FC Dallas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fernandez signed with FC Dallas on 10 January 2013. He was the starting goalkeeper for the MLS All-Stars in 2013. After two seasons with Dallas, Fernández was released at the end of the 2014 season following their acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Dan_Kennedy_(soccer)" title="Dan Kennedy (soccer)">Dan Kennedy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Fern%C3%A1ndez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His international career began in 2008. <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_del_Solar" title="José del Solar">José del Solar</a> called up Fernàndez to play his first international game for his country against <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica_national_football_team" title="Costa Rica national football team">Costa Rica</a>, in a friendly match. In June 2011 he was called up by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sergio_Markarian" title="Sergio Markarian">Sergio Markarian</a> for the national team for <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Copa América">Copa America 2011</a> in Argentina.</p>
