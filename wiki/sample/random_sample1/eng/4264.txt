<p><b>Raúl Alonso Jiménez Rodríguez</b> (born 5 May 1991) is a Mexican professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Forward" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> for Portuguese club <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico national team</a>.</p>
<p>He began his career in <a href="/wiki/Club_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Club América">Club América</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Am%C3%A9rica_Reserves" title="Club América Reserves">youth system</a> before debuting in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_de_M%C3%A9xico" title="Primera División de México">first division</a> in October 2011. He won his first championship with América in 2013, winning that years' <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Liga_MX_season#Clausura" title="2012–13 Liga MX season">Clausura</a> tournament, where he was the team's second-highest goalscorer. In August 2014, Jiménez was sold to Spanish club <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>, who were the reigning league champions. After one season, he was sold to Benfica.</p>
<p>Jiménez was a part of the <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_under-23_football_team" title="Mexico national under-23 football team">Mexican squad</a> that won the gold medal at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_Olympics" title="Summer Olympics">Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics">2012</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A full international since 2013, Jiménez has represented his country at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2013_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="2013 CONCACAF Gold Cup">2013 CONCACAF Gold Cup</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2013 FIFA Confederations Cup">2013 FIFA Confederations Cup</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2015 Copa América">2015 Copa América</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Centenario" title="Copa América Centenario">Copa América Centenario</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Am.C3.A9rica">América</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: América">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312_season">2011–12 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011–12 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Jiménez began his career playing for <a href="/wiki/Club_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Club América">Club América</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Am%C3%A9rica_Reserves" title="Club América Reserves">youth system</a>, being considered a promising young striker. On 9 October 2011, under interim-coach <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Tena" title="Alfredo Tena">Alfredo Tena</a>, Jiménez made his professional debut during the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Club_Am%C3%A9rica_season#Apertura" title="2011–12 Club América season">Apertura</a> tournament against <a href="/wiki/Monarcas_Morelia" title="Monarcas Morelia">Monarcas Morelia</a>, in a match which ended in a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He scored his first goal on 30 October in a 2–3 loss to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puebla_FC" title="Puebla FC">Puebla</a>, scoring in the 2nd minute of the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He appeared six times and scored one goal in his debut tournament.</p>
<p>Jiménez did not appear in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Club_Am%C3%A9rica_season#Clausura" title="2011–12 Club América season">Clausura 2012</a> until 11 February—week seven of the tournament—against <a href="/wiki/Club_Atlas" title="Club Atlas">Atlas</a>, which ended in a 1–1 draw, coming on as a second-half substitute for <a href="/wiki/Christian_Berm%C3%BAdez" title="Christian Bermúdez">Christian Bermúdez</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He scored his first goal of the tournament on 9 March in the 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Club_Tijuana" title="Club Tijuana">Tijuana</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Caliente" title="Estadio Caliente">Estadio Caliente</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Jiménez appeared twelve times and scored once during the Clausura, with América being eliminated in the semi-finals by <a href="/wiki/C.F._Monterrey" title="C.F. Monterrey">Monterrey</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313_season">2012–13 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2012–13 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After participating in the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics">Summer Olympics</a> with <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_under-23_football_team" title="Mexico national under-23 football team">Mexico</a> and the sale of <a href="/wiki/Vicente_Mat%C3%ADas_Vuoso" title="Vicente Matías Vuoso">Vicente Matías Vuoso</a>, Jiménez won a spot in América's starting eleven for the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Club_Am%C3%A9rica_season#Apertura" title="2012–13 Club América season">Apertura 2012</a> tournament, playing as a second-striker alongside <a href="/wiki/Christian_Ben%C3%ADtez" title="Christian Benítez">Christian Benítez</a> in manager <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Herrera" title="Miguel Herrera">Miguel Herrera</a>'s 5-3-2 tactical scheme. He was also given the vacant number 9 shirt, switching from the number 47 shirt he debuted with. Jiménez scored on 15 September in the 2–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Santos_Laguna" title="Santos Laguna">Santos Laguna</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Azteca" title="Estadio Azteca">Estadio Azteca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He would go on to score two weeks later in the 1–1 draw against Monarcas Morelia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Jiménez received his first red card during América's 2–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/San_Luis_F.C." title="San Luis F.C.">San Luis</a>, meaning he would miss "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Cl%C3%A1sico_(Mexico)" title="El Clásico (Mexico)">El Súper Clásico</a>" against <a href="/wiki/C.D._Guadalajara" title="C.D. Guadalajara">Guadalajara</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 3 November, he scored the second goal in the 4–0 win over <a href="/wiki/C.F._Pachuca" title="C.F. Pachuca">Pachuca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 17 November, Jiménez scored in the 1–2 defeat to Morelia in the second-leg of the quarter-final. Despite the loss, América won the series by a 3–2 aggregate score and advanced to the semi-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> América would ultimately be eliminated in the semi-finals by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Toluca" title="Club Toluca">Toluca</a>. Jimenéz ended the tournament with sixteen appearances and scoring four goals.</p>
<p>Jiménez began the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Club_Am%C3%A9rica_season#Clausura" title="2012–13 Club América season">Clausura 2013</a> tournament as a starter in the first two league matches, and scored his first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brace_(sports)" title="Brace (sports)">brace</a> in the 4–0 victory over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C.F._Atlante" title="C.F. Atlante">Atlante</a> on 19 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He bagged another brace on 2 February in América's 3–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Quer%C3%A9taro_F.C." title="Querétaro F.C.">Querétaro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 31 March, Jiménez scored two headers in the 2–0 victory over Guadalajara at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estadio_Omnilife" title="Estadio Omnilife">Estadio Omnilife</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Jimenéz finished the regular phase of the tournament with eight goals, and scored in América's 1–0 away win over <a href="/wiki/Club_Universidad_Nacional" title="Club Universidad Nacional">Pumas UNAM</a> in the first-leg of the quarter-final series of the playoffs on 8 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In the second-leg of the semi-final series against Monterrey, Jimenéz scored from a penalty and assisted Christian Benitez in the second goal to win the match 2–1 (4–3 on aggregate), sending América to the <a href="/wiki/2013_Liga_MX_Clausura_Liguilla#Final" title="2013 Liga MX Clausura Liguilla">Final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Cruz_Azul" title="Cruz Azul">Cruz Azul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Jiménez played in both legs and scored in the subsequent penalty shoot-out which América ultimately won, thus being crowned league champions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314_season">2013–14 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013–14 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 3 August 2013 Jiménez scored his first goal of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Liga_MX_season" title="2013–14 Liga MX season">Apertura</a> tournament in the 3–0 win over Atlas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He would go on to score in the following three-consecutive matches in wins over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlante_FC" title="Atlante FC">Atlante</a>, Pachuca, and Morelia, respectively, taking his goal tally to four goals in five matches played. He would score again on 13 September in América's 1–2 defeat to Santos Laguna.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On 5 October, Jiménez scored a header and assisted in <a href="/wiki/Luis_Gabriel_Rey" title="Luis Gabriel Rey">Luis Gabriel Rey</a>'s goal in the 2–0 victory over Guadalajara.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Jiménez ended the regular-phase of the Apertura with seven goals in twelve games played. He scored in the second-leg of the quarter-finals against <a href="/wiki/Tigres_UANL" title="Tigres UANL">Tigres UANL</a> which ended in a 1–1 draw at the Estadio Azteca on 1 December, with América advancing, despite a 2–2 aggregate score, due to the away-goals rule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> América would win the semi-final matches against Toluca and advance once again to the league final, though failing to achieve the repeat after losing both legs to <a href="/wiki/Club_Le%C3%B3n" title="Club León">León</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Liga_MX_season#Clausura" title="2013–14 Liga MX season">Clausura 2014</a> tournament saw Jiménez make 17 appearances and score 8 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In the quarter-final match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Santos_Laguna" title="Club Santos Laguna">Santos Laguna</a> on 30 April, Jiménez scored a hat-trick in the 5–3 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> However, América would lose the second-leg of the series 1–3 and being eliminated from the playoffs, despite a 6–6 aggregate score due to the away-goals rule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Jiménez began the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Liga_MX_season" title="2014–15 Liga MX season">Apertura 2014</a> tournament scoring four goals in the first three games. On 26 July, he scored the first goal in América's 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Club_Tijuana" title="Club Tijuana">Tijuana</a>. On 4 August, he scored a hat-trick and assisted <a href="/wiki/Oribe_Peralta" title="Oribe Peralta">Oribe Peralta</a> in the 4–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Puebla_F.C." title="Puebla F.C.">Puebla</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Jiménez played his final game for América on 9 August 2014 against Tigres UANL, providing the assist for the second goal scored by <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Lay%C3%BAn" title="Miguel Layún">Miguel Layún</a> in the 2–0 win.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atl.C3.A9tico_Madrid">Atlético Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Atlético Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 13 August 2014, <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> reached an agreement with Club América for Jiménez,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> for a fee reported to be in the region of €11 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The following day, he signed a six-year deal after passing the medical examination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Jiménez made his debut on 16 August, in the final of the <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_de_Carranza_Trophy" title="Ramón de Carranza Trophy">Ramón de Carranza Trophy</a> pre-season tournament against <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a>, starting the match and being substituted out in the 79th minute. Atlético won 2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season_2">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Jiménez made his competitive debut in the first leg of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Supercopa_de_Espa%C3%B1a" title="2014 Supercopa de España">Supercopa de España</a> on 19 August, a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>, playing the final 12 minutes in place of fellow debutant <a href="/wiki/Mario_Mand%C5%BEuki%C4%87" title="Mario Mandžukić">Mario Mandžukić</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Jiménez played in the second leg as well coming in as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Antoine_Griezmann" title="Antoine Griezmann">Antoine Griezmann</a> in the 73rd minute of the match, the game ended in a 1–0 win and an aggregated score of 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jiménez made his La Liga debut on 25 August 2014, in a goalless draw against <a href="/wiki/Rayo_Vallecano" title="Rayo Vallecano">Rayo Vallecano</a>. He scored his first league goal for Atlético on 27 September, in a 4–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla</a>, coming off the bench. It was his only for Atlético that season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Benfica">Benfica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Benfica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 August 2015, Jiménez who had been expected to complete a loan deal to <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham</a>, failed to show up to his medical claiming he had missed his flight to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> after he had overslept.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-westham_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-westham-34">[34]</a></sup> However, multiple sources had reported his agent <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Mendes" title="Jorge Mendes">Jorge Mendes</a> had suggested he spurn the move, in favour of a move to <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a> side <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-westham_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-westham-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 August 2015, Jiménez joined Portuguese champions Benfica, signing a five-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Benfica spent €9.836 million to bring Jiménez to the club, although this included unspecified amounts on agent's fees and signing-on fees in addition to the transfer fee itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The €9.836 million may have included a €1 million transfer fee, and €3 million to acquire 50% of the player's economic rights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.9316_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Jimenéz scored his first goal for Benfica on 29 August against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moreirense" title="Moreirense">Moreirense</a>, scoring a header in the 75th minute to level the score 1–1, with Benfica eventually winning the match 3–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 November, Jimenéz scored his first two goals in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>, striking at both ends in each half as Benfica salvaged a 2–2 away draw against <a href="/wiki/FC_Astana" title="FC Astana">Astana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On 9 March 2016, he helped Benfica to reach the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League_knockout_phase" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League knockout phase">quarter-finals</a> of the Champions League in a 2–1 away victory against <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit Saint Petersburg</a>. In the 85th minute of the match, Jimenéz struck a long-range shot which was saved by <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Lodygin" title="Yuri Lodygin">Yuri Lodygin</a> onto the bar, with the ball rebounding to <a href="/wiki/Nicol%C3%A1s_Gait%C3%A1n" title="Nicolás Gaitán">Nicolás Gaitán</a> who tied the match 1–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> On 9 April, he came off the bench and scored the winning goal in the 2–1 victory against <a href="/wiki/Associa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Acad%C3%A9mica_de_Coimbra_%E2%80%93_O.A.F." title="Associação Académica de Coimbra – O.A.F.">Académica</a>, securing Benfica's lead in the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Primeira_Liga" title="2015–16 Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Four days later, due to the suspension of <a href="/wiki/Jonas_Gon%C3%A7alves_Oliveira" title="Jonas Gonçalves Oliveira">Jonas</a> in the first leg, Jiménez started the second leg of the Champions League quarter-final match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>, scoring the first goal in the 2–2 draw at the <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_da_Luz" title="Estádio da Luz">Estádio da Luz</a>; a 3–2 defeat on aggregate meant Benfica were eliminated from the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 April, he scored another winner in the league, this time scoring the lone goal against <a href="/wiki/Rio_Ave_F.C." title="Rio Ave F.C.">Rio Ave</a>, extending Benfica's lead in the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> On 15 May, Jiménez came on as a second-half substitute as Benfica were crowned league champions after defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clube_Desportivo_Nacional" title="Clube Desportivo Nacional">Nacional</a> 4–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Five days later, he capped off the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_S.L._Benfica_season" title="2015–16 S.L. Benfica season">season</a> scoring from the penalty spot in the 6–2 win over <a href="/wiki/C.S._Mar%C3%ADtimo" title="C.S. Marítimo">Marítimo</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2016_Ta%C3%A7a_da_Liga_Final" title="2016 Taça da Liga Final">Taça da Liga final</a>, where he also finished as the competition's top goalscorer with four goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.9317_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 21 July 2016, it was announced that Benfica had purchased the remaining 50% of Jiménez's economic rights for €12 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> It was widely reported that this had brought the total value of his transfer to around €22 million, making Jiménez the most expensive player in Portuguese football history,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[note 1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[49]</a></sup> and the most expensive Mexican player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico_U23">Mexico U23</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Mexico U23">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_Toulon_Tournament">2012 Toulon Tournament</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2012 Toulon Tournament">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2012, Jiménez was selected by coach <a href="/wiki/Luis_Fernando_Tena" title="Luis Fernando Tena">Luis Fernando Tena</a> to participate in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Toulon_Tournament" title="2012 Toulon Tournament">2012 Toulon Tournament</a> with the under-23 national team. Jiménez managed to score Mexico's third goal in the 4–2 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-21 football team">Netherlands</a> in the semi-finals on 30 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[51]</a></sup> Mexico would subsequently win the final, defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkey_A2_national_football_team" title="Turkey A2 national football team">Turkey</a> 3–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gomez_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gomez-53">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_Summer_Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2012 Summer Olympics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Jiménez made the final cut in the squad participating in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London,_England" title="London, England">London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[53]</a></sup> He made his Olympic debut in the group stage match against <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_under-23_football_team" title="South Korea national under-23 football team">South Korea</a> on 26 July, which ended in a 0–0 draw, with Jiménez coming in as a substitute and coming close to scoring a late goal, with the ball ricocheting off the goalkeeper's left-hand post.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[54]</a></sup> He subsequently made appearances against <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_under-23_football_team" title="Switzerland national under-23 football team">Switzerland</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[55]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Senegal_national_football_team" title="Senegal national football team">Senegal</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[56]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_under-23_football_team" title="Japan national under-23 football team">Japan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[57]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_under-23_football_team" title="Brazil national under-23 football team">Brazil</a> in the final, coming on as a substitute in those matches. Mexico would go on to defeat Brazil 2–1 and win the gold medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[58]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Borden_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-Borden-60">[59]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico_national_team">Mexico national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Mexico national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jiménez made his debut for the senior national team in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_football_team" title="Denmark national football team">Denmark</a> on 30 January 2013, coming on as a second-half substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[60]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_FIFA_Confederations_Cup">2013 FIFA Confederations Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: 2013 FIFA Confederations Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 20 May 2013, Jiménez was named in the 23-man squad participating in the <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2013 FIFA Confederations Cup">2013 FIFA Confederations Cup</a> held in Brazil,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[61]</a></sup> and featured in all three group games for a combined 96 minutes. On 22 June, he was named in the starting eleven for the third match against <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a>, being substituted off in the 90th minute for <a href="/wiki/Javier_Aquino" title="Javier Aquino">Javier Aquino</a> in Mexico's 2–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[62]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup">2013 CONCACAF Gold Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: 2013 CONCACAF Gold Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 26 June 2013, it was announced that Jiménez was included in Mexico's 23-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2013_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="2013 CONCACAF Gold Cup">2013 CONCACAF Gold Cup</a>, replacing midfielder <a href="/wiki/David_Cabrera_Pujol" title="David Cabrera Pujol">David Cabrera</a> due to injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[63]</a></sup> He was a starter and played in every group stage match, managing to score against <a href="/wiki/Canada_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="Canada men's national soccer team">Canada</a> in Mexico's 2–0 win on 11 July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[64]</a></sup> On 20 July, Jiménez scored the only goal in the quarterfinal victory over <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team" title="Trinidad and Tobago national football team">Trinidad and Tobago</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[65]</a></sup> Mexico would be eliminated by <a href="/wiki/Panama_national_football_team" title="Panama national football team">Panama</a> in the semifinal.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_FIFA_World_Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: 2014 FIFA World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 5 June 2014, Jiménez was confirmed in coach Miguel Herrera's final 23-man team participating in the 2014 FIFA World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[66]</a></sup> He did not appear in Mexico's opener against <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">Cameroon</a>. On 17 June, Jiménez made his World Cup debut against host nation <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>, entering as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Giovani_dos_Santos" title="Giovani dos Santos">Giovani dos Santos</a> in the 84th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[67]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_Copa_Am.C3.A9rica">2015 Copa América</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ra%C3%BAl_Jim%C3%A9nez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: 2015 Copa América">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Mexico's second game of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2015 Copa América">2015 Copa América</a> tournament against hosts <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a>, Jiménez headed from a corner to give Mexico the 2–1 lead in an eventual 3–3 draw at the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Nacional_Julio_Mart%C3%ADnez_Pr%C3%A1danos" title="Estadio Nacional Julio Martínez Prádanos">Estadio Nacional</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[68]</a></sup> In their next match against <a href="/wiki/Ecuador_national_football_team" title="Ecuador national football team">Ecuador</a> on 19 June, he scored from the penalty spot after <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Achilier" title="Gabriel Achilier">Gabriel Achilier</a> brought down <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Ayala" title="Hugo Ayala">Hugo Ayala</a>, but the 1–2 defeat saw Mexico eliminated in last place in <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Group_A" title="2015 Copa América Group A">their group</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[69]</a></sup></p>
