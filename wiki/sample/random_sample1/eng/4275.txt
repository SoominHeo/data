<p><b>Ryan Matthew Goins</b> (born February 13, 1988) is an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Second_baseman" title="Second baseman">second baseman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shortstop" title="Shortstop">shortstop</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amateur_career">Amateur career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Goins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Amateur career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Goins attended <a href="/wiki/Stony_Point_High_School" title="Stony Point High School">Stony Point High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Round_Rock,_Texas" title="Round Rock, Texas">Round Rock, Texas</a>, where he played on his school's baseball team as a shortstop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He then enrolled at <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Baptist_University" title="Dallas Baptist University">Dallas Baptist University</a>, where he played <a href="/wiki/College_baseball" title="College baseball">college baseball</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Baptist_Patriots" title="Dallas Baptist Patriots">Dallas Baptist Patriots</a> from 2007 to 2009. In three seasons, Goins hit 32 <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home runs</a> and recorded 117 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Runs_batted_in" title="Runs batted in">runs batted in</a> (RBIs). In 2009, he set the Patriots record for home runs in a single season with 22.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2008, Goins played for the <a href="/wiki/Waterloo_Bucks" title="Waterloo Bucks">Waterloo Bucks</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Northwoods_League" title="Northwoods League">Northwoods League</a> during the offseason.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Goins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor_league_career">Minor league career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Goins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Minor league career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ryan_Goins_on_April_14,_2010.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1950" data-file-width="1300" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Ryan_Goins_on_April_14%2C_2010.jpg/200px-Ryan_Goins_on_April_14%2C_2010.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Ryan_Goins_on_April_14%2C_2010.jpg/300px-Ryan_Goins_on_April_14%2C_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Ryan_Goins_on_April_14%2C_2010.jpg/400px-Ryan_Goins_on_April_14%2C_2010.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

<p>The Blue Jays drafted Goins in the fourth round of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_MLB_draft" title="2009 MLB draft">2009 MLB draft</a>. He was assigned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rookie_League" title="Rookie League">Rookie</a> <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast_League_Blue_Jays" title="Gulf Coast League Blue Jays">Gulf Coast League Blue Jays</a>, and also played for the <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball#Class_A-Short_Season" title="Minor League Baseball">Short Season</a> <a href="/wiki/Auburn_Doubledays" title="Auburn Doubledays">Auburn Doubledays</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball#Class_A_.28Low-A.29" title="Minor League Baseball">Class-A</a> <a href="/wiki/Lansing_Lugnuts" title="Lansing Lugnuts">Lansing Lugnuts</a> in 2009, collectively batting .246 with 17 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BRM_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BRM-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2010, Goins played 77 games with the Lugnuts and batted .308 before he was promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball#Class-A_Advanced_.28High_A.29" title="Minor League Baseball">Advanced-A</a> <a href="/wiki/Dunedin_Blue_Jays" title="Dunedin Blue Jays">Dunedin Blue Jays</a>. In 124 total games in 2010, Goins batted .271 with 3 home runs and 53 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BRM_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-BRM-4">[4]</a></sup> Apart from 1 rehab game in the Gulf Coast, Goins played the entire 2011 season in Dunedin, and batted .284 with 3 home runs and 52 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BRM_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-BRM-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Goins played for the <a href="/wiki/Double-A_(baseball)" title="Double-A (baseball)">Double-A</a> <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire_Fisher_Cats" title="New Hampshire Fisher Cats">New Hampshire Fisher Cats</a> in 2012, batting .289 with 7 home runs and 61 RBIs in a career-high 136 games played.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BRM_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-BRM-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ryan_Goins_Promoted_to_Toronto_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ryan_Goins_Promoted_to_Toronto-5">[5]</a></sup> After the season ended Goins played 13 games with the <a href="/wiki/Salt_River_Rafters" title="Salt River Rafters">Salt River Rafters</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Fall_League" title="Arizona Fall League">Arizona Fall League</a>, and hit .133 with 5 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BRM_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-BRM-4">[4]</a></sup> The Blue Jays added him to their 40-man roster after the 2012 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> For most of the 2013 season, Goins played for the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">Triple-A</a> <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Bisons" title="Buffalo Bisons">Buffalo Bisons</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ryan_Goins_Promoted_to_Toronto_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ryan_Goins_Promoted_to_Toronto-5">[5]</a></sup> He was batting .257 with 6 home runs and 46 RBIs for the Bisons before his promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BRM_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-BRM-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toronto_Blue_Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Goins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Toronto Blue Jays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.932014">2013–2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Goins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013–2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Goins was called up by the Blue Jays on August 22, 2013, when <a href="/wiki/Maicer_Izturis" title="Maicer Izturis">Maicer Izturis</a> was placed on the 15-day disabled list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He made his MLB debut on August 23, batting ninth and playing second base against the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>. Goins would later move to shortstop and finished the game 2–4 with a <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">double</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He recorded his first career RBI on August 25 when he grounded out in the ninth inning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On August 31, 2013, Goins tied a Blue Jays franchise record by hitting in his 8th consecutive game to begin his major league career. <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Barfield" title="Jesse Barfield">Jesse Barfield</a> also opened his career with an 8-game hitting streak in 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Goins' hit streak ended at 8 games on September 1 against the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a>, when he went 0–4 with 2 strikeouts. He hit his first big league home run on September 18, off <a href="/wiki/David_Huff_(baseball)" title="David Huff (baseball)">David Huff</a> of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>, and would finish the season with a .252 average, 2 home runs, and 8 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BR_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-BR-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<p>Goins attended 2014 spring training, seeing regular playing time at second base. He was announced as the starting second baseman for the Blue Jays on March 24, after posting a batting average of just .176.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He was optioned to Triple-A Buffalo on April 28.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On July 22, Goins was recalled from Buffalo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He set career-highs in both hits and RBI, with 4 each, in a 14–1 win over the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> on July 28.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He was sent down once again, before being called up on September 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In total for 2014, Goins batted .188 with 1 home run and 15 RBI in 67 games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BR_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-BR-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.93present">2015–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Goins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2015–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Goins did not make the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Opening_Day" title="Opening Day">Opening Day</a> roster out of spring training, with Toronto opting to open the season with an eight-man bullpen. On April 16, he was recalled from Buffalo after <a href="/wiki/Todd_Redmond" title="Todd Redmond">Todd Redmond</a> was designated for assignment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Goins became the starting shortstop on April 28, as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Reyes_(shortstop)" title="José Reyes (shortstop)">José Reyes</a> was placed on the disabled list with a cracked rib.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> After Reyes returned from the disabled list on May 25, Goins remained with the Blue Jays as a utility infielder. On July 26, he became the starting second baseman due to an injury to <a href="/wiki/Devon_Travis" title="Devon Travis">Devon Travis</a>. It was at this time that Blue Jays' hitting coach, <a href="/wiki/Brook_Jacoby" title="Brook Jacoby">Brook Jacoby</a>, made a change to Goins' batting stance. Rather than hold the bat above his shoulder while awaiting a pitch, Jacoby had Goins lower the bat and rest it on his shoulder before swinging.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The change paid immediate dividends, as Goins experienced the most productive month of his career, batting .314 with two home runs, 16 walks, and an .885 <a href="/wiki/On-base_plus_slugging" title="On-base plus slugging">on-base plus slugging</a> (OPS) in August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On September 1, Goins hit a two-run <a href="/wiki/Walk-off_home_run" title="Walk-off home run">walk-off home run</a> in the 10th inning to defeat the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a> 5–3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Goins would record the first five-hit game for a shortstop in franchise history on September 30, aiding the Blue Jays to a division-clinching 15–2 victory over the Baltimore Orioles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Goins played in a career-high 128 games in the 2015 regular season, and batted .250 with five home runs and 45 RBI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BR_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-BR-13">[13]</a></sup> He played in all five games of the <a href="/wiki/2015_American_League_Division_Series" title="2015 American League Division Series">2015 American League Division Series</a>, and went hitless in 17 at-bats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BR_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-BR-13">[13]</a></sup> In game 3 of the <a href="/wiki/2015_American_League_Championship_Series" title="2015 American League Championship Series">2015 American League Championship Series</a> against the Kansas City Royals, Goins hit his first postseason home run, drove in three runs and scored twice to help Toronto to an 11–8 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Goins began the 2016 season as the team's primary second baseman, with Devon Travis still recovering from offseason shoulder surgery. On July 1, 2016, Goins made his professional pitching debut, being pressed into service in the 18th inning of a 19 inning marathon game against the Cleveland Indians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Goins was placed on the 15-day disabled list the following day with forearm tightness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> He was activated on August 1, and optioned to Buffalo on August 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> On August 19, Goins was recalled from Triple-A. He finished the 2016 regular season with a .186 batting average, three home runs, and 12 RBI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BR_13-4"><a href="#cite_note-BR-13">[13]</a></sup> Goins was on the <a href="/wiki/2016_American_League_Wild_Card_Game" title="2016 American League Wild Card Game">Wild Card</a> roster but did not play in the game. He was not on the <a href="/wiki/2016_American_League_Division_Series" title="2016 American League Division Series">Division Series</a> roster. During the Division Series, Devon Travis suffered a knee injury that kept him out of the lineup for two games. With team management unsure of Travis' health, Goins was added to the <a href="/wiki/2016_American_League_Championship_Series" title="2016 American League Championship Series">Championship Series</a> roster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
