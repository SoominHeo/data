<p><b>Ryan James Shawcross</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/ɔː/ 'au' in 'fraud'">ɔː</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ɒ/ short 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span> (born 4 October 1987) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre-back</a> and is <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a> of <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>.</p>
<p>Shawcross began his career with <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> and made his debut for the club against <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra_F.C." title="Crewe Alexandra F.C.">Crewe Alexandra</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>; he then spent time on loan at Belgian feeder club <a href="/wiki/Royal_Antwerp_F.C." title="Royal Antwerp F.C.">Royal Antwerp</a>. He joined <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> on loan in August 2007 and made an instant impact scoring on his debut against <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a>. His good form prompted Stoke to pay Manchester United £1 million in January 2008 to keep him at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britannia_Stadium" title="Britannia Stadium">Britannia Stadium</a>. He formed a good defensive partnership with <a href="/wiki/Leon_Cort" title="Leon Cort">Leon Cort</a> as Stoke gained promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> for the first time, ending 23 years away from the top flight.</p>
<p>Despite making a bad start to Premier League football, Shawcross established himself as first-choice centre-back along with <a href="/wiki/Abdoulaye_Faye" title="Abdoulaye Faye">Abdoulaye Faye</a>. Stoke comfortably stayed up, and after another mid-table finish in <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2009–10 Stoke City F.C. season">2009–10</a>, Shawcross was handed the <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">Captain's armband</a> by manager <a href="/wiki/Tony_Pulis" title="Tony Pulis">Tony Pulis</a> ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2010–11 Stoke City F.C. season">2010–11</a> season. With Faye drifting out of the side, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Huth" title="Robert Huth">Robert Huth</a> became Shawcross's defensive partner and the team reached the <a href="/wiki/2011_FA_Cup_Final" title="2011 FA Cup Final">2011 FA Cup Final</a>, finishing runners-up to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>. The run to the final also meant that Stoke entered the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> in <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2011–12 Stoke City F.C. season">2011–12</a>, and they reached the round of 32 losing to Spanish side <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia</a>. Shawcross continued to be a regular under Pulis in <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2012–13 Stoke City F.C. season">2012–13</a> and then under <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hughes" title="Mark Hughes">Mark Hughes</a> in <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2013–14 Stoke City F.C. season">2013–14</a> which saw him win the <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C._Player_of_the_Year" title="Stoke City F.C. Player of the Year">player of the year award</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Shawcross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Chester" title="Chester">Chester</a>, Shawcross grew-up in the Welsh town of <a href="/wiki/Buckley" title="Buckley">Buckley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flintshire" title="Flintshire">Flintshire</a> where he attended Mountain Lane Primary School and <a href="/wiki/Elfed_High_School" title="Elfed High School">Elfed High School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> While living in Buckley, Shawcross played in the youth team at <a href="/wiki/Cymru_Alliance" title="Cymru Alliance">Cymru Alliance</a> side <a href="/wiki/Buckley_Town_F.C." title="Buckley Town F.C.">Buckley Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary school</a> days, Shawcross played representative level football for <a href="/wiki/Flintshire" title="Flintshire">Flintshire</a> Boys; this team counts <a href="/wiki/Gary_Speed" title="Gary Speed">Gary Speed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Owen" title="Michael Owen">Michael Owen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ian_Rush" title="Ian Rush">Ian Rush</a> amongst its former players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> When Shawcross was 15 years old, he and a number of Flintshire Boys players had a trial at Football League club <a href="/wiki/Wrexham_A.F.C." title="Wrexham A.F.C.">Wrexham</a> who offered contracts to some players but not to Shawcross. However, just three weeks later, he had a trial at <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> and was offered a contract at their academy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Shawcross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_United">Manchester United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Shawcross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Manchester United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shawcross progressed through the youth teams at <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> and Shawcross made his senior debut on 25 October 2006 as a substitute for fellow debutant <a href="/wiki/Michael_Barnes_(footballer)" title="Michael Barnes (footballer)">Michael Barnes</a> in a 2–1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> third round win away at <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra_F.C." title="Crewe Alexandra F.C.">Crewe Alexandra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He also came on in the final minute of a 1–0 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Southend_United_F.C." title="Southend United F.C.">Southend United</a> in the next round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In January 2007, he was sent on loan to Belgian side <a href="/wiki/Royal_Antwerp_F.C." title="Royal Antwerp F.C.">Royal Antwerp</a>, where he appeared regularly, and helped Antwerp to make it into the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Second_Division" title="Belgian Second Division">Belgian Second Division</a> play-offs, scoring three goals, before returning to Manchester in the summer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Royal_Antwerp_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Royal_Antwerp-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stoke_City">Stoke City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Shawcross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Stoke City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 9 August 2007, Shawcross was sent on loan again, this time to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> club <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>, for six months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He scored his first two goals for Stoke during his first two games, one on his debut during a 1–0 win on the opening day of the season, against <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> with the other during a League Cup game against <a href="/wiki/Rochdale_A.F.C." title="Rochdale A.F.C.">Rochdale</a> in which Stoke lost on penalties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> After Stoke's 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C." title="Charlton Athletic F.C.">Charlton Athletic</a> Shawcross expressed his delight at Stoke's early season from.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In October 2007 he was involved in a tackle with <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> player <a href="/wiki/Francis_Jeffers" title="Francis Jeffers">Francis Jeffers</a> who sustained ligament damage and a three-month lay-off from the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Despite this Shawcross was named as the Championship's player of the month for October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POTM_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-POTM-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Stoke made advances to make Shawcross' loan permanent during the January 2008 transfer window,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and the move was finally completed on 18 January 2008, with Stoke paying Manchester United an initial £1m, which eventually rose to a club record £2m due to Shawcross' appearances for Stoke and the team achieving promotion the following May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Additionally, Alex Ferguson negotiated a clause that gives Manchester United first refusal on any offer for the player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He lost his place in the side for the final few games of the promotion winning season to loan signing <a href="/wiki/Chris_Riggott" title="Chris Riggott">Chris Riggott</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Although he did play in the final match against Leicester City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Championship_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Championship-21">[21]</a></sup> At the end of the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2007–08 Stoke City F.C. season">2007–08</a> season, Shawcross was named in the Championship <a href="/wiki/PFA_Team_of_the_Year_(2000s)" title="PFA Team of the Year (2000s)">PFA Team of the Year</a> along with team mates <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Fuller" title="Ricardo Fuller">Ricardo Fuller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liam_Lawrence" title="Liam Lawrence">Liam Lawrence</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PFA_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-PFA-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>After starting in Stoke's first game in the Premier League in a 3–1 defeat away at <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> Shawcross lost his place in the side to new signing <a href="/wiki/Ibrahima_Sonko" title="Ibrahima Sonko">Ibrahima Sonko</a> and he stated that he might have to leave Stoke if the manager signed another defender.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Shawcross re-claimed his place in side in late 2008 due to Sonko's poor performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In a match against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> in November 2008 a Shawcross off-the-pitch tackle led to a three-week injury to <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_Adebayor" title="Emmanuel Adebayor">Emmanuel Adebayor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Following his return to the side Shawcross scored in games against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The goal against Boro proved to be vital win for Stoke in the fight from relegation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> After a fine debut <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> season, Shawcross was linked to several clubs <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> rivals however both Shawcross and <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Tony_Pulis" title="Tony Pulis">Tony Pulis</a> dismissed this speculation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>He scored from a header in Stoke's first match of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2009–10 Stoke City F.C. season">2009–10 season</a>, helping them to a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On 12 December 2009, he scored his second goal in the 2009–10 season for Stoke against <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> in a 2–2 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Shawcross was again involved in an incident with Emmanuel Adebayor in a <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> fifth round replay against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>, Adebayor struck Shawcross in the face and was sent-off. Shawcross went on to score Stoke's second goal in a 3–1 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> On 27 February 2010, in a game against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>, Shawcross was sent off after his tackle left Arsenal midfielder <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Ramsey" title="Aaron Ramsey">Aaron Ramsey</a> with a broken leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Shawcross himself wept when he saw the extent of the injury to Ramsey, who had to be stretchered off and taken to hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Shawcross, who apologized via text message,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> was defended by his team mates <a href="/wiki/Danny_Pugh" title="Danny Pugh">Danny Pugh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rory_Delap" title="Rory Delap">Rory Delap</a> after the match,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> On the same day, Shawcross received a call-up to the England national side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Egypt_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-Egypt-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shawcross was given the Stoke City captaincy for the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2010–11 Stoke City F.C. season">2010–11 season</a> taking over from <a href="/wiki/Abdoulaye_Faye" title="Abdoulaye Faye">Abdoulaye Faye</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> After a number of impressive performances for Stoke Shawcross has again been linked with a move away from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britannia_Stadium" title="Britannia Stadium">Britannia Stadium</a>, however City chairman <a href="/wiki/Peter_Coates" title="Peter Coates">Peter Coates</a> issued a 'Hand off warning'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for almost a year in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Stoke beat <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> in the quarter final and Shawcross has said he will be proud to lead out Stoke City at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley</a> in the semi-final against <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Stoke beat Bolton 5–0 earning a place in the clubs first <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup_Final" title="FA Cup Final">FA Cup Final</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Shawcross therefore became the first Stoke City captain to play in a <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup_Final" title="FA Cup Final">FA Cup Final</a>, losing to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Despite the defeat Stoke were able to claim a place in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>Prior to the start of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2011–12 Stoke City F.C. season">2011–12 season</a> manager <a href="/wiki/Tony_Pulis" title="Tony Pulis">Tony Pulis</a> confirmed that Shawcross would remain as club captain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> He had a busy season playing in 49 of the clubs 56 fixtures helping the club reach the last 32 of the Europa League and the quarter final of the FA Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> He was named as captain for a third season running, with Pulis warning Shawcross against complacency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> In a Premier League match against <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> on 15 December 2012 Shawcross was deliberately headbutted by <a href="/wiki/Marouane_Fellaini" title="Marouane Fellaini">Marouane Fellaini</a> which was missed by referee <a href="/wiki/Mark_Halsey" title="Mark Halsey">Mark Halsey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> After the match Fellaini issued a public apology to Shawcross and was given a retrospective three match ban.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> On 3 January 2013, Shawcross signed a <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">5<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span>-year contract extension with the "Potters", keeping him at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britannia_Stadium" title="Britannia Stadium">Britannia Stadium</a> until the summer of 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> After agreeing to his new contract <a href="/wiki/Tony_Pulis" title="Tony Pulis">Tony Pulis</a> stated that Shawcross can go on to become a legend at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> He played in 40 matches in <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2012–13 Stoke City F.C. season">2012–13</a> season, scoring once against Wigan Athletic as Stoke finished in 13th position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012.2F2013_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-2012.2F2013-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of the 2012–13 season Tony Pulis was sacked and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hughes" title="Mark Hughes">Mark Hughes</a>, who decided that Shawcross would retain the captaincy for a fifth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> On 24 August 2013 Shawcross scored the winning goal against <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a> which earned City their first win of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2013–14 Stoke City F.C. season">2013–14</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> On 12 April 2014 Shawcross made his 200th appearance in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> becoming the first Stoke player to reach that milestone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> Shawcross missed just one match in 2013–14 as Stoke finished in 9th position in the Premier League and he also won the <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C._Player_of_the_Year" title="Stoke City F.C. Player of the Year">player of the year award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-POTY_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-POTY-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shawcross scored Stoke's first goal of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2014–15 Stoke City F.C. season">2014–15</a> season in a 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> on 24 August 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> Against <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea City</a> on 29 October 2014, Shawcross was penalized by referee <a href="/wiki/Michael_Oliver_(referee)" title="Michael Oliver (referee)">Michael Oliver</a> for holding <a href="/wiki/Wilfried_Bony" title="Wilfried Bony">Wilfried Bony</a> at a corner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> This prompted Mark Hughes to take Shawcross off man-marking duties at set-plays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> He made his 300th appearance for Stoke on 29 November against Liverpool.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> On 1 January 2015 he scored against his former club Manchester United in a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> He missed February and most of March with a back injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> He played 37 times for Stoke in 2014–15 as the Potters finished in 9th position for a second season running and they ended the campaign with a 6–1 victory against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2014.2F2015_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-2014.2F2015-71">[71]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shawcross was ruled out for the first two months of the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2015–16 Stoke City F.C. season">2015–16</a> season after undergoing back surgery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> He returned from injury on 27 October 2015 playing in a League Cup victory against Chelsea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> However his season was continually disrupted by his back injury restricting him to 23 appearances as Stoke again finished in ninth position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2015.2F2016_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-2015.2F2016-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Shawcross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2008, Shawcross won his first call up to the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England under-21</a> squad for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2009 UEFA under-21 Championship</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship_qualification" title="2009 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification">Qualifying</a> match against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_under-21_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland under-21 national football team">Republic of Ireland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> Shawcross previously played for the <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales</a> under-15 team, against Belgium and the Republic of Ireland. Following Shawcross' fine form in his debut <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> season, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Pulis" title="Tony Pulis">Tony Pulis</a>, his manager at <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke</a> tipped Shawcross to break into the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England senior side</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2009, it was announced that changes have been proposed to the eligibility criteria for British players, which would make Shawcross eligible to play for Wales. Wales under-21 coach <a href="/wiki/Brian_Flynn" title="Brian Flynn">Brian Flynn</a> commented that "People who grew up in Wales often can't represent the country they've lived in most of their life because their nearest maternity hospital is in England".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> In October 2009, FIFA ratified the changes to allow players with five years of compulsory education in a country before the age of 16 to represent that country. Despite this, Shawcross was named in an <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> squad for the first time for their match against <a href="/wiki/Egypt_national_football_team" title="Egypt national football team">Egypt</a> in March 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Egypt_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-Egypt-41">[41]</a></sup> He was named in the matchday squad but was an unused substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> Shawcross has been backed by his defensive partner, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Huth" title="Robert Huth">Robert Huth</a> to earn an England call up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> In July 2011 Shawcross confirmed that he will not consider playing for Wales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 July 2012 however, Wales national team manager <a href="/wiki/Chris_Coleman_(footballer)" title="Chris Coleman (footballer)">Chris Coleman</a> said he would consult <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> and Wales team ex-captain <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Ramsey" title="Aaron Ramsey">Aaron Ramsey</a> regarding the possibility of Shawcross to play for the Welsh team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> However Shawcross again stated that his only international ambition is to play for England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shawcross was called up to the England team by <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hodgson" title="Roy Hodgson">Roy Hodgson</a> on 4 October 2012 for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2014 FIFA World Cup qualifiers</a> against <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_national_football_team" title="San Marino national football team">San Marino</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> He made his England debut in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a> on 14 November 2012 at the <a href="/wiki/Friends_Arena" title="Friends Arena">Friends Arena</a>, coming on as a 74th minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Steven_Caulker" title="Steven Caulker">Steven Caulker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> During Shawcross' 16 minutes on the pitch Swedish striker <a href="/wiki/Zlatan_Ibrahimovi%C4%87" title="Zlatan Ibrahimović">Zlatan Ibrahimović</a> scored a hat-trick which included a 30-yard bicycle kick, Shawcross was not called up again by <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hodgson" title="Roy Hodgson">Roy Hodgson</a> and speaking in March 2016 Shawcross said that Ibrahimovic ended his England career before it had started.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup></p>

The couple have three children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup> He has been involved in charity work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup></p>
