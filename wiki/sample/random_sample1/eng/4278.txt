<p><b>Matthew Ryan Phillippe</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɪ/ or /ə/ 'e' in 'roses'">ᵻ</span><span title="'p' in 'pie'">p</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born September 10, 1974)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmref_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmref-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American actor, director, and writer. After appearing on the soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/One_Life_to_Live" title="One Life to Live">One Life to Live</a></i>, he came to fame in the late 1990s with starring roles in a string of films, including <i><a href="/wiki/I_Know_What_You_Did_Last_Summer" title="I Know What You Did Last Summer">I Know What You Did Last Summer</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cruel_Intentions" title="Cruel Intentions">Cruel Intentions</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/54_(film)" title="54 (film)">54</a></i>. In the 2000s, he appeared in several films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Gosford_Park" title="Gosford Park">Gosford Park</a></i> (2001), <i><a href="/wiki/Crash_(2004_film)" title="Crash (2004 film)">Crash</a></i> (2004), and war drama <i><a href="/wiki/Flags_of_Our_Fathers_(film)" title="Flags of Our Fathers (film)">Flags of Our Fathers</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Breach_(film)" title="Breach (film)">Breach</a></i> (2007), and <i><a href="/wiki/Stop-Loss_(film)" title="Stop-Loss (film)">Stop-Loss</a></i> (2008). In 2010, Phillippe starred as Pulitzer Prize-winning photographer <a href="/wiki/Greg_Marinovich" title="Greg Marinovich">Greg Marinovich</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Bang-Bang_Club_(film)" title="The Bang-Bang Club (film)">The Bang-Bang Club</a></i>. Currently, he stars in the lead role of Bob Lee Swagger in the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a> <a href="/wiki/Thriller_(genre)" title="Thriller (genre)">thriller</a> <a href="/wiki/Drama_(film_and_television)" title="Drama (film and television)">drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Shooter_(TV_series)" title="Shooter (TV series)">Shooter</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-screenrant.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-screenrant.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was married to actress <a href="/wiki/Reese_Witherspoon" title="Reese Witherspoon">Reese Witherspoon</a> from 1999 to 2007; together, they have a daughter and a son. He also has a daughter from a relationship with actress <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Knapp" title="Alexis Knapp">Alexis Knapp</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Phillippe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Phillippe was born in <a href="/wiki/New_Castle,_Delaware" title="New Castle, Delaware">New Castle, Delaware</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His mother, Susan, ran a day care center in the family's house, and his father, Richard Phillippe, was a chemist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmref_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-filmref-1">[1]</a></sup> Phillippe is of part French descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He has three sisters. He attended <a href="/wiki/New_Castle_Christian_Academy" title="New Castle Christian Academy">New Castle Baptist Academy</a>, where he played basketball and soccer, as well as earned a black belt in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tae_Kwon_Do" title="Tae Kwon Do">Tae Kwon Do</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Philadelphia_Style_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Philadelphia_Style-5">[5]</a></sup> he was also the yearbook editor in his senior year. Phillippe also attended and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Barbizon_Modeling_and_Acting_School" title="Barbizon Modeling and Acting School">Barbizon Modeling and Talent Agency</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_Delaware" title="Wilmington, Delaware">Wilmington, Delaware</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Phillippe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career_.281992.E2.80.931999.29">Early career (1992–1999)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Phillippe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career (1992–1999)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Phillippe's acting career began after being signed to Cathy Parker Management in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voorhees,_New_Jersey" title="Voorhees, New Jersey">Voorhees, New Jersey</a>. Shortly after he made an appearance in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> daytime drama <i><a href="/wiki/One_Life_to_Live" title="One Life to Live">One Life to Live</a></i>. His character <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billy_Douglas_(One_Life_to_Live)" title="Billy Douglas (One Life to Live)">Billy Douglas</a>, whom he played from 1992 to 1993, was the first gay teenager on a daytime soap opera.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW_Harris_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-EW_Harris-8">[8]</a></sup> After leaving the show, Phillippe moved to Los Angeles, where he appeared in a number of small parts in various television series including <i><a href="/wiki/Matlock_(TV_series)" title="Matlock (TV series)">Matlock</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Due_South" title="Due South">Due South</a></i>, the TV miniseries <i>The Secrets of Lake Success</i>, and movies, including the 1995 film <i><a href="/wiki/Crimson_Tide_(film)" title="Crimson Tide (film)">Crimson Tide</a></i> and the 1996 film <i><a href="/wiki/White_Squall_(film)" title="White Squall (film)">White Squall</a></i>.</p>
<p>He was cast in the 1997 horror film, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Know_What_You_Did_Last_Summer" title="I Know What You Did Last Summer">I Know What You Did Last Summer</a></i>, which co-starred <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Michelle_Gellar" title="Sarah Michelle Gellar">Sarah Michelle Gellar</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freddie_Prinze,_Jr." title="Freddie Prinze, Jr.">Freddie Prinze, Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Love_Hewitt" title="Jennifer Love Hewitt">Jennifer Love Hewitt</a>. The film was a success, and led to Phillippe gaining wider renown and being cast in a few more high-profile films, including <i><a href="/wiki/54_(film)" title="54 (film)">54</a></i> in 1998 opposite <a href="/wiki/Neve_Campbell" title="Neve Campbell">Neve Campbell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salma_Hayek" title="Salma Hayek">Salma Hayek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Myers" title="Mike Myers">Mike Myers</a>. In 1999 he starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Cruel_Intentions" title="Cruel Intentions">Cruel Intentions</a></i>, a modern retelling of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choderlos_de_Laclos" title="Choderlos de Laclos">Choderlos de Laclos</a> novel <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Les_Liaisons_Dangereuses" title="Les Liaisons Dangereuses">Les Liaisons Dangereuses</a></i>, which also starred Phillippe's future wife <a href="/wiki/Reese_Witherspoon" title="Reese Witherspoon">Reese Witherspoon</a>, as well as his <i><a href="/wiki/I_Know_What_You_Did_Last_Summer" title="I Know What You Did Last Summer">I Know What You Did Last Summer</a></i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Michelle_Gellar" title="Sarah Michelle Gellar">Sarah Michelle Gellar</a>. It was a success among its intended teenage audience, cementing Phillippe's ability to play characters that require sex appeal. Phillippe can also be seen in the <a href="/wiki/Marcy_Playground" title="Marcy Playground">Marcy Playground</a> music video "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comin%27_Up_From_Behind" title="Comin' Up From Behind">Comin' Up From Behind</a>", which appears on the film's soundtrack.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_success_and_later_work_.282000.E2.80.932011.29">Critical success and later work (2000–2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Phillippe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critical success and later work (2000–2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the years following, he appeared in the crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Way_of_the_Gun" title="The Way of the Gun">The Way of the Gun</a></i>, starred as a famed software engineer in the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Antitrust_(film)" title="Antitrust (film)">Antitrust</a></i>, and co-starred in <a href="/wiki/Robert_Altman" title="Robert Altman">Robert Altman</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Gosford_Park" title="Gosford Park">Gosford Park</a></i>, which was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Oscar</a> for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Picture" title="Academy Award for Best Picture">Best Picture</a>. Subsequently, Phillippe had supporting parts in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Igby_Goes_Down" title="Igby Goes Down">Igby Goes Down</a></i> (2002) and <i><a href="/wiki/Crash_(2004_film)" title="Crash (2004 film)">Crash</a></i> (2005), which won the Oscar for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Picture" title="Academy Award for Best Picture">Best Picture</a>. His 2003 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_I_Inside" title="The I Inside">The I Inside</a></i> premiered on cable.</p>
<p>In 2006, Phillippe played real-life Navy corpsman <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Bradley_(Iwo_Jima)" title="John Bradley (Iwo Jima)">John Bradley</a> in the war film <i><a href="/wiki/Flags_of_Our_Fathers_(film)" title="Flags of Our Fathers (film)">Flags of Our Fathers</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a> and following the journey of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Marines" title="United States Marines">United States Marines</a> who lifted the flag at the Battle of <a href="/wiki/Iwo_Jima" title="Iwo Jima">Iwo Jima</a>. Phillippe has said that the film was the "best experience" of his career because of its "personal meaning" to him,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-voa.com_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-voa.com-9">[9]</a></sup> and that he would have "given [his] life" to fight in World War II, noting that both of his grandfathers fought in the war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cleveland.com_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-cleveland.com-10">[10]</a></sup> His performance was positively received by film critic <a href="/wiki/Richard_Roeper" title="Richard Roeper">Richard Roeper</a>, who thought it was Phillippe's best performance to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ebandro_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ebandro-11">[11]</a></sup> Phillippe's next role was in the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Breach_(film)" title="Breach (film)">Breach</a></i>, in which he played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FBI" title="FBI">FBI</a> investigator <a href="/wiki/Eric_O%27Neill" title="Eric O'Neill">Eric O'Neill</a> opposite <a href="/wiki/Chris_Cooper" title="Chris Cooper">Chris Cooper</a>. He has since commented that he believes Cooper to be "the best actor America has to offer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He then starred in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chaos_(2006_film)" title="Chaos (2006 film)">Chaos</a></i>, in which he plays a police officer, <i><a href="/wiki/Five_Fingers_(2006_film)" title="Five Fingers (2006 film)">Five Fingers</a></i>, a drama set in <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimberly_Peirce" title="Kimberly Peirce">Kimberly Peirce</a>'s Iraq war film <i><a href="/wiki/Stop-Loss_(film)" title="Stop-Loss (film)">Stop-Loss</a></i>, and the futuristic <i><a href="/wiki/Franklyn" title="Franklyn">Franklyn</a></i>.</p>

<p>Next up for Phillippe was a rare comedic role as Lt. Dixon Piper in <i><a href="/wiki/MacGruber_(film)" title="MacGruber (film)">MacGruber</a></i>, a film based on the <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i> (SNL) skit of the same name. It was released in the US and Canada on May 21, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newsinfilm.com_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-newsinfilm.com-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cinema_Blend_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cinema_Blend-14">[14]</a></sup> As part of the film's promotion, Phillippe made his SNL hosting debut on April 17, 2010, along with first-time musical performer <a href="/wiki/Kesha" title="Kesha">Kesha</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Two days later, on April 19, 2010, Phillippe <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_WWE_Raw_guest_hosts" title="List of WWE Raw guest hosts">co-hosted</a> <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">WWE Raw</a> with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Swift_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jonathan Swift (actor) (page does not exist)">Jonathan Swift</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charo" title="Charo">Charo</a>, also in support of <i>MacGruber</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>That same year, Phillippe starred in the Canadian/South African production <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bang_Bang_Club_(film)" title="The Bang Bang Club (film)">The Bang-Bang Club</a></i>, which tells the real-life story of the <a href="/wiki/Bang-Bang_Club" title="Bang-Bang Club">Bang-Bang Club</a>, four South African photographers whose images documented the bloody end of <a href="/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">apartheid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anevib.com_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-anevib.com-17">[17]</a></sup> Phillippe stars as <a href="/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize" title="Pulitzer Prize">Pulitzer Prize</a>-winning photographer <a href="/wiki/Greg_Marinovich" title="Greg Marinovich">Greg Marinovich</a>. Filming for the movie took place in South Africa, beginning in March 2009. Phillippe described the filming process as "really run-and-gun. There were no comforts, and I actually appreciate that." He noted that the experience affected him, explaining that "we were shooting during the day in <a href="/wiki/Soweto" title="Soweto">Soweto</a>, and it was an endless procession of funerals—death is so common. Kids there have no quality of life. It has made me want to get more involved in Africa-based charities."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Philadelphia_Style_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Philadelphia_Style-5">[5]</a></sup> An early cut of the film was shown at <a href="/wiki/Cannes_Film_Festival" title="Cannes Film Festival">Cannes Film Festival</a> in May 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cannes_09_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cannes_09-18">[18]</a></sup> with the final version shown at Cannes in May 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cannes_10_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cannes_10-19">[19]</a></sup> The film officially premiered in September 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2010 Toronto International Film Festival">2010 Toronto International Film Festival</a>. The US rights to the movie were picked up by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film" title="Tribeca Film">Tribeca Film</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> which premiered the movie at the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film_Festival" title="Tribeca Film Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a> and opened the movie in <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">limited release</a> on April 22, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the summer of 2010, Phillippe began filming the adaptation of popular crime novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lincoln_Lawyer_(film)" title="The Lincoln Lawyer (film)">The Lincoln Lawyer</a></i>, taking on the role of Louis Roulet, a wealthy Los Angeles playboy accused of a crime for which his culpability is unclear. The movie, which also stars <a href="/wiki/Matthew_McConaughey" title="Matthew McConaughey">Matthew McConaughey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marisa_Tomei" title="Marisa Tomei">Marisa Tomei</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_H._Macy" title="William H. Macy">William H. Macy</a>, opened on March 18, 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-22">[22]</a></sup> and was generally well received by critics, scoring 82% on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> as of late March 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lawyerrt_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-lawyerrt-23">[23]</a></sup> Phillippe filmed his next project, heist action movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Setup_(film)" title="Setup (film)">Setup</a></i>, in December 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Grand_Rapids,_Michigan" title="Grand Rapids, Michigan">Grand Rapids</a>, Michigan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Set_Up_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Set_Up-24">[24]</a></sup> Also starring <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">Bruce Willis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenna_Dewan" title="Jenna Dewan">Jenna Dewan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a>, the movie focuses on a group of young men from Detroit whose attempt at a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diamond_heist" title="Diamond heist">diamond heist</a> goes awry and brings them into conflict with a mob boss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Set_Up_2_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Set_Up_2-25">[25]</a></sup> The movie had a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Straight-to-DVD" title="Straight-to-DVD">straight-to-DVD</a> release on September 20, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Set_Up_3_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Set_Up_3-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Phillippe next filmed the dramatic dark comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Revenge_for_Jolly!" title="Revenge for Jolly!">Revenge for Jolly!</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Elijah_Wood" title="Elijah Wood">Elijah Wood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kristen_Wiig" title="Kristen Wiig">Kristen Wiig</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adam_Brody" title="Adam Brody">Adam Brody</a>, in the summer of 2011. The movie, which tells the story of a man set on finding his dog's murderer, premiered at the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film_Festival" title="Tribeca Film Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Revenge_For_Jolly_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Revenge_For_Jolly-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Revenge_For_Jolly2_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Revenge_For_Jolly2-28">[28]</a></sup> Phillippe subsequently began work on another movie, <i><a href="/wiki/Straight_A%27s" title="Straight A's">Straight A's</a></i>, in August 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Straight_A.27s_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Straight_A.27s-29">[29]</a></sup> Filmed in <a href="/wiki/Shreveport,_Louisiana" title="Shreveport, Louisiana">Shreveport, Louisiana</a>, the movie stars Phillippe as a man who has been in and out of rehab for years and is now haunted by his mother's ghost, and also features <a href="/wiki/Anna_Paquin" title="Anna Paquin">Anna Paquin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luke_Wilson" title="Luke Wilson">Luke Wilson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Straight_A.27s_2_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Straight_A.27s_2-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_television_and_focus_on_directing_and_producing_.282012.E2.80.93present.29">Return to television and focus on directing and producing (2012–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Phillippe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Return to television and focus on directing and producing (2012–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Starting in November 2011, Phillippe spent several months filming a 10-episode arc on the fifth and final season of the critically acclaimed TV show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Damages_(TV_show)" title="Damages (TV show)">Damages</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Damages_1_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Damages_1-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Damages_4_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Damages_4-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Damages_6_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Damages_6-33">[33]</a></sup> Playing Channing McClaren, a <a href="/wiki/Julian_Assange" title="Julian Assange">Julian Assange</a>-like character,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Damages_3_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Damages_3-34">[34]</a></sup> it was his first regular TV role since his breakout part on <i>One Life to Live</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Damages_2_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Damages_2-35">[35]</a></sup> The season aired from July to September 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvwise_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvwise-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vs_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-vs-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-damagesfinale_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-damagesfinale-38">[38]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2012, Phillippe focused on his directorial debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Catch_Hell" title="Catch Hell">Catch Hell</a></i>, an indie thriller in which Phillippe stars as a fading film actor who must devise a creative escape after he is kidnapped and tortured while making a movie in <a href="/wiki/Shreveport,_Louisiana" title="Shreveport, Louisiana">Shreveport, Louisiana</a>. In addition to directing, co-producing, and starring in the film, Phillippe also co-wrote the script with Joe Gossett.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-varietyshreveport_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-varietyshreveport-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shreveport2_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-shreveport2-40">[40]</a></sup> Phillippe has said that the film is based partly on the life experiences of himself and his friends (including the filming of <i>Straight A's</i> in Shreveport in 2011), but is partly meant to be "a fun scary movie in the vein of <i><a href="/wiki/Misery_(film)" title="Misery (film)">Misery</a></i>". Filming took place on location in Shreveport in the fall of 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shreveport3_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-shreveport3-41">[41]</a></sup> The movie was released on October 10, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2013, Phillippe began filming the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Reclaim_(film)" title="Reclaim (film)">Reclaim</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. The movie features Phillippe as an American man who travels to Puerto Rico with his wife (played by <a href="/wiki/Rachelle_Lefevre" title="Rachelle Lefevre">Rachelle Lefevre</a>) to adopt an orphan from <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>. After a confrontation with a local (played by <a href="/wiki/John_Cusack" title="John Cusack">John Cusack</a>), the child vanishes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> <i>Reclaim</i> was released on September 19, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2014, <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> picked up the pilot for <i><a href="/wiki/Secrets_and_Lies_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Secrets and Lies (U.S. TV series)">Secrets and Lies</a></i>, which was shot in <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte, North Carolina</a> in early 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> The 10-episode mystery series debuted on March 1, 2015, as a midseason entry in the 2014–15 season. It is based on the <a href="/wiki/Secrets_%26_Lies_(Australian_TV_series)" title="Secrets &amp; Lies (Australian TV series)">Australian series of the same name</a> and stars Phillippe as a family man who becomes a prime murder suspect after he discovers the body of a child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>Phillippe has also been attached to a number of possible future film roles, including <i>Chronicle</i>, a film to be directed by <a href="/wiki/Jay_Alaimo" title="Jay Alaimo">Jay Alaimo</a> that will see Phillippe, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Long" title="Justin Long">Justin Long</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Hawkes_(actor)" title="John Hawkes (actor)">John Hawkes</a> in a story "about two childhood friends who reunite to launch the biggest marijuana dealership in New York City".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chronicle_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chronicle-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>Phillippe, <a href="/wiki/Breckin_Meyer" title="Breckin Meyer">Breckin Meyer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seth_Green" title="Seth Green">Seth Green</a> and David E. Siegal run a production company called Lucid Films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cleveland.com_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-cleveland.com-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2010, Phillippe and Meyer began "getting a show going for <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a>", serving as executive producers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Philadelphia_Style_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Philadelphia_Style-5">[5]</a></sup> The comedy, <i>Heavy and Rolling</i>, tells the story of a <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> towncar driver who assumes different identities as he moves towards madness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heavy_and_Rolling_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-Heavy_and_Rolling-50">[50]</a></sup> Phillippe also served as executive producer and narrator on <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Isolated_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Isolated (film) (page does not exist)">Isolated</a></i>, a documentary that follows five <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surfers" title="Surfers">surfers</a> as they travel to remote <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a> in search of untouched waves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Isolated_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-Isolated-52">[52]</a></sup> The documentary premiered in January 2013 at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara_International_Film_Festival" title="Santa Barbara International Film Festival">Santa Barbara International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Phillippe was cast in the lead role of Bob Lee Swagger on the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a> thriller drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Shooter_(TV_series)" title="Shooter (TV series)">Shooter</a></i>, which premiered in the fall of 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-screenrant.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-screenrant.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Phillippe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1997, Phillippe attended a party given for actress <a href="/wiki/Reese_Witherspoon" title="Reese Witherspoon">Reese Witherspoon</a>'s 21st birthday. Upon meeting Witherspoon, Phillippe was reported to have spent all night talking to her, with Witherspoon telling Phillippe, "I think you're my birthday present."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-janema_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-janema-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daily_Caller_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Caller-56">[56]</a></sup> They started dating and became engaged in December 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daily_Caller_56-1"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Caller-56">[56]</a></sup> The following year, the pair starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Cruel_Intentions" title="Cruel Intentions">Cruel Intentions</a></i> together. Phillippe and Witherspoon married on June 5, 1999, in a small ceremony at Old Wide Awake Plantation near <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston</a>, South Carolina, when Witherspoon was six months pregnant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daily_Caller_56-2"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Caller-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mock_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mock-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 9, 1999,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> their daughter Ava Elizabeth Phillippe, named after actress <a href="/wiki/Ava_Gardner" title="Ava Gardner">Ava Gardner</a>, was born.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mock_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mock-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moms_.26_Babies_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-Moms_.26_Babies-59">[59]</a></sup> Their son, Deacon Reese Phillippe, was born in October 23, 2003, and named after Phillippe's ancestor, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Deacon_Phillippe" title="Deacon Phillippe">Deacon Phillippe</a>, a baseball player at the turn of the 20th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fuller_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fuller-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 30, 2006, Phillippe and Witherspoon released a statement announcing that they had decided to formally separate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-split_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-split-61">[61]</a></sup> Witherspoon filed for divorce on November 8, 2006, citing <a href="/wiki/Irreconcilable_differences" title="Irreconcilable differences">irreconcilable differences</a> as the cause.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-file_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-file-62">[62]</a></sup> In light of the couple's lack of a <a href="/wiki/Prenuptial_agreement" title="Prenuptial agreement">prenuptial agreement</a>, she requested that the court refuse to grant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spousal_support" title="Spousal support">spousal support</a> to Phillippe, and asked for joint legal custody and sole physical custody of the pair's two children. Phillippe filed for joint physical custody of the children on May 15, 2007, and did not seek any spousal support.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ryan_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-ryan-63">[63]</a></sup> The couple's marriage officially ended on October 5, 2007, with final divorce arrangements settled on June 13, 2008, according to court documents. Phillippe and Witherspoon share joint custody of their children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>Phillippe began dating Australian actress <a href="/wiki/Abbie_Cornish" title="Abbie Cornish">Abbie Cornish</a> in 2007 after meeting on the set of <i><a href="/wiki/Stop-Loss_(film)" title="Stop-Loss (film)">Stop-Loss</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cornish_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-cornish-65">[65]</a></sup> They split up in February 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cornish2_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-cornish2-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>Phillippe subsequently dated model/actress <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Knapp" title="Alexis Knapp">Alexis Knapp</a> in the summer of 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-knapp1_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-knapp1-67">[67]</a></sup> before splitting in September 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-knapp2_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-knapp2-68">[68]</a></sup> After their breakup, Knapp discovered that she was pregnant, and gave birth to their daughter, Kailani Merizalde Phillippe-Knapp, on July 1, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-knapp4_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-knapp4-69">[69]</a></sup> Phillippe was present during the birth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-knapp3_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-knapp3-70">[70]</a></sup></p>
<p>Phillippe began dating law student Paulina Slagter in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<p>Phillippe was a close friend of Brooklyn rapper <a href="/wiki/Sean_Price" title="Sean Price">Sean Price</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> After Price's death in August 2015, it was reported that Phillippe got a tattoo to honor Price's memory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
