<p><b>Hangul</b> (also known as <b>Hangul Word Processor</b> or <b>HWP</b>) is a proprietary word processing application published by the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> company <a href="/wiki/Hancom" title="Hancom">Hancom Inc.</a>. Hangul's specialized support for the <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean written language</a> has gained it widespread use in South Korea, especially by the government.</p>
<p>The software's name is derived from the Korean word <a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko">한글</span>, <i>hangeul</i>) for the alphabet used to write Korean.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Document_formats">Document formats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hangul_(word_processor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Document formats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hangul saves documents in HWP format, with the filename extension *.<b>hwp</b>. It is one of the standard document formats of the South Korean government. It is widely used for a number of reasons: Koreans require <a href="/wiki/Desktop_publishing" title="Desktop publishing">DTP</a> level layout features to word processor, and its unique program can process <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_Korean" title="Middle Korean">Middle Korean</a>.</p>
<p>HWP files, up to the versions created with Hangul '97, can be opened with <a href="/wiki/OpenOffice.org" title="OpenOffice.org">OpenOffice.org</a> (version 4.1.1 or earlier<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) or <a href="/wiki/LibreOffice" title="LibreOffice">LibreOffice</a>. However, files created with later editions of Hangul, including Hangul Wordian, Hangul 2002, Hangul 2005 and Hangul 2007 cannot be opened with OpenOffice or LibreOffice, due to the major changes in the document structure. These later versions of Hangul do not provide support for opening and saving of files in <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Word" title="Microsoft Word">Microsoft Word</a> format, but users are not necessarily aware of this. Consequently, Korean Hangul users may often send files to non-Koreans in .hwp format, not realizing the recipient will be unable to open such files.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hangul was intended in 2009 to gain support for reading and writing of <a href="/wiki/Office_Open_XML" title="Office Open XML">Office Open XML</a> and <a href="/wiki/OpenDocument" title="OpenDocument">OpenDocument</a> files.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The HWP binary format specification has been published online free by <a href="/wiki/Hancom" title="Hancom">Hancom</a> on June 29, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hangul_Viewer">Hangul Viewer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hangul_(word_processor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hangul Viewer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Haansoft offers a freely available Hangul document viewer program called "Hancom Office Viewer 2010 SE" (한컴오피스 뷰어 2010 SE).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Haansoft_near_bankruptcy">Haansoft near bankruptcy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hangul_(word_processor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Haansoft near bankruptcy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Haansoft was on the verge of bankruptcy after the release of its 2002 version, due to the widespread use of illegal copies. A campaign to support the development of Korean software and promote the purchase of legal copies of Hangul allowed Haansoft to recover.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
