<p><i><b>Rise of Nations: Thrones and Patriots</b></i> is the official <a href="/wiki/Expansion_pack" title="Expansion pack">expansion pack</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Real-time_strategy" title="Real-time strategy">real-time strategy</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rise_of_Nations_(video_game)" title="Rise of Nations (video game)">Rise of Nations</a></i>. The game is the second in a series of <i>Rise of Nations</i> games by <a href="/wiki/Big_Huge_Games" title="Big Huge Games">Big Huge Games</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ronbhghome_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ronbhghome-7">[7]</a></sup> <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> had its premiere release for Microsoft Windows on April 27, 2004 in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dates_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-dates-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canadadate_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-canadadate-4">[4]</a></sup> It was <a href="/wiki/Porting" title="Porting">ported</a> to and released for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mac_OS_X" title="Mac OS X">Mac OS X</a> in November 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignmacport_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-ignmacport-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>Rise of Nations: Extended Edition</i> was released on <a href="/wiki/Steam_(software)" title="Steam (software)">Steam</a> on June 12, 2014 and includes both the original game and <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> with updated graphics and Steamworks integration for multiplayer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-steam_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-steam-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-microsoftgamestudiospr_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-microsoftgamestudiospr-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Thrones and Patriots</i> expanded on a variety of features in the original game, such as new <a href="/wiki/Monument" title="Monument">monuments</a> (called "<a href="/wiki/Wonders_of_the_World" title="Wonders of the World">Wonders</a>"), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nations" title="Nations">nations</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Form_of_government" title="Form of government">governments</a>, and campaigns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bhgrontpcow_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-bhgrontpcow-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignreview_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ignreview-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msftrongov_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-msftrongov-12">[12]</a></sup> Gameplay still remains similar to <i>Rise of Nations</i>, with its wide span of ages and players still manage and expand their nations at the macro and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Micromanagement_(computer_gaming)" title="Micromanagement (computer gaming)">micro</a> levels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bhgrongold_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-bhgrongold-13">[13]</a></sup> However, <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> has campaigns which offer more <a href="/wiki/Turn-based_strategy" title="Turn-based strategy">turn-based strategy</a> features than the original and the ability to make decisions outside of combat. Up to eight players can play in multiplayer matches, with <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a> being able to fill slots at the server host's choice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tim Train, executive producer of <i>Thrones and Patriots</i>, stated that various features were added to the game in an attempt to balance gameplay, including the addition of armed <a href="/wiki/Caravan_(travellers)" title="Caravan (travellers)">caravans</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merchants" title="Merchants">merchants</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timtraininterviewgspot_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-timtraininterviewgspot-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> claimed that <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> is "a great game [which] gets better," in reference to its predecessor <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rise_of_Nations_(video_game)" title="Rise of Nations (video game)">Rise of Nations</a></i> and other critical reviews also praised many features of the game, though some noted deficiencies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignreview_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ignreview-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1up_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-1up-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gi_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-gi-16">[16]</a></sup> <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> has received a compilation score of 87.86% and 88% on <a href="/wiki/GameRankings" title="GameRankings">GameRankings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamerankings_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-gamerankings-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-metacritic-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rise_of_Nations:_Thrones_and_Patriots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Koreariseofnationtp.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="263" data-file-width="350" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/1a/Koreariseofnationtp.PNG/200px-Koreariseofnationtp.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/1a/Koreariseofnationtp.PNG/300px-Koreariseofnationtp.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/1/1a/Koreariseofnationtp.PNG 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Koreariseofnationtp.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> campaign</div>

<p>Within <i>Rise of Nations: Thrones and Patriots</i> players control various units during battles, including land, sea, and aerial units. Units used during gameplay include <a href="/wiki/Sling_(weapon)" title="Sling (weapon)">slingers</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Musketeers" title="Musketeers">musketeers</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tanks" title="Tanks">tanks</a>, large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persia" title="Persia">Persian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_elephants" title="War elephants">war elephants</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aircraft_carriers" title="Aircraft carriers">aircraft carriers</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_missiles" title="Nuclear missiles">nuclear missiles</a>. Players progress in different ways in <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> by using various means including <a href="/wiki/Espionage" title="Espionage">espionage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Politics" title="Politics">political</a> strategy, <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a> races and <a href="/wiki/Population_growth" title="Population growth">population growth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msftgsgameplay_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-msftgsgameplay-19">[19]</a></sup> The gameplay takes place from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_history" title="Ancient history">ancient history</a> to <a href="/wiki/Modern_history" title="Modern history">modern times</a>. Players are able to lead their nations at the macro and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Micromanagement_(computer_gaming)" title="Micromanagement (computer gaming)">micro</a> levels, and build their nation up and expand it throughout the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bhgrongold_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-bhgrongold-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Thrones and Patriots</i> added six new nations to the original <i>Rise of Nations</i> game. The 'New World' situation playable in the game was enhanced by the introduction of the European nation—the Dutch. Three North American nations were added, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">Americans</a>, referring to the ex-colonial civilization, and two <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">Native American</a> nations were added, which were the <a href="/wiki/Lakota_people" title="Lakota people">Lakota</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Iroquois" title="Iroquois">Iroquois</a>. Two Asian nations, the <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indians</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Persian_people" title="Persian people">Persians</a>, were introduced. The other nation to be added was the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a>. New Wonders were made available for building, such as the <a href="/wiki/Hanging_Gardens_of_Babylon" title="Hanging Gardens of Babylon">Hanging Gardens of Babylon</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_City" title="Forbidden City">Forbidden City</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Red_Fort" title="Red Fort">Red Fort</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msftronfeatures_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-msftronfeatures-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>New features of the game were added to the new nations, for instance the Lakota, which is unable to build farms to generate food, instead receive a constant food supply for each of their citizens, scouts, and cavalry units. They can make buildings in any region that is not possessed by their enemy, can raze buildings quickly, receiving a full refund. Additionally, they receive resources when enemy resources are destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msftlakotainfo_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-msftlakotainfo-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaigns">Campaigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rise_of_Nations:_Thrones_and_Patriots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Campaigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Rise of Nations: Thrones and Patriots</i> added four new "<a href="/wiki/Hegemony" title="Hegemony">Conquer the World</a>" campaigns: <a href="/wiki/Alexander_the_Great" title="Alexander the Great">Alexander the Great</a>, <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>, the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bhgrontpcow_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-bhgrontpcow-9">[9]</a></sup> The Alexander the Great campaign allows the player to choose whether or not to raze or incorporate conquered territories. A player's decision to raze may cause the rest of the enemies to either fight harder, or question their <i><a href="/wiki/Casus_belli" title="Casus belli">casus belli</a></i> and ally with you.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignreview_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-ignreview-10">[10]</a></sup> The Napoleon campaign challenges the player to conquer Europe in a limited amount of time, with diplomacy, alliances, conquest, and bribing with land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup> Players must have intuitive leadership in this campaign because faulty decisions by the player can force themselves, that is to say, Napoleon, into exile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bhgrontpcow_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-bhgrontpcow-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a> campaign allows the player to choose either the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">Americans</a>, a European power, or a <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">Native American</a> tribe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup> If the player chooses to lead the Americans, they must pay taxes to <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, until they declare independence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bhgrontpcow_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-bhgrontpcow-9">[9]</a></sup> The map of a battle incorporates the region's environment, for example large forests in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup> The Cold War introduces the ability to fire nuclear missiles in the main map and initiate espionage missions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bhgrontpcow_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-bhgrontpcow-9">[9]</a></sup> Armies can also move anywhere in their nation's territory without using up a turn. <i>Rise of Nations: Thrones and Patriots'</i> campaigns now offer more <a href="/wiki/Turn-based_strategy" title="Turn-based strategy">turn-based strategy</a> into the game rather than <a href="/wiki/Real-time_strategy" title="Real-time strategy">real-time strategy</a> being the only gameplay aspect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timtraininterviewgspot_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-timtraininterviewgspot-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Governments">Governments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rise_of_Nations:_Thrones_and_Patriots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Governments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Once the player builds the Senate building, he or she may choose a government "upgrade" of either a <a href="/wiki/Despotism" title="Despotism">Despotism</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">Republic</a>, each giving the player a different permanent gameplay bonus. Each government has a different "Patriot", effectively a stronger version of the "General" unit with different bonuses, depending on which government is chosen. They are used during combat to assist players in battle. A player can only have one Patriot unit at a time. When a Patriot unit is killed, a new one is automatically created in the Senate afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msftrongov_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-msftrongov-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timtraininterviewgspot_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-timtraininterviewgspot-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>After reaching the Gunpowder Age, the player may choose to upgrade to either a <a href="/wiki/Monarchy" title="Monarchy">Monarchy</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">Democracy</a>, each with its own different permanent gameplay bonus, and then after reaching the Industrial Age, the player may upgrade again, to either <a href="/wiki/Capitalism" title="Capitalism">Capitalism</a> or <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">Socialism</a>. The previous gameplay bonuses remain, allowing players to select the most appropriate combination of benefits as the game evolves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msftrongov_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-msftrongov-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Multiplayer">Multiplayer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rise_of_Nations:_Thrones_and_Patriots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Multiplayer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Rise of Nations</i> features a highly customizable <a href="/wiki/Multiplayer_video_game" title="Multiplayer video game">multiplayer</a> mode. Up to eight players can play in a game with a variety of modes to choose from, with <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">AI</a> filling spots at the server host's choice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-6"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/GameSpy_Technology" title="GameSpy Technology">GameSpy Technologies</a> had hosted matchmaking and other servers for multiplayer games played online through their dedicated server until December 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gspressrelease_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-gspressrelease-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespymultiplayershut_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-gamespymultiplayershut-23">[23]</a></sup> A new feature that was introduced into the game was the ability to use homemade scripts in online play. The scripts are able to be made in the built in script editor, and came with a pre-made script that disallowed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Missile_silos" title="Missile silos">missile silos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bhgfilesscript_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-bhgfilesscript-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rise_of_Nations:_Thrones_and_Patriots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Brian_Reynolds_E3_2003.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="839" data-file-width="626" height="295" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Brian_Reynolds_E3_2003.jpg/220px-Brian_Reynolds_E3_2003.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Brian_Reynolds_E3_2003.jpg/330px-Brian_Reynolds_E3_2003.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Brian_Reynolds_E3_2003.jpg/440px-Brian_Reynolds_E3_2003.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Brian_Reynolds_E3_2003.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microsoft_Game_Studios" title="Microsoft Game Studios">Microsoft Game Studios</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_Huge_Games" title="Big Huge Games">Big Huge Games</a> announced the development of the expansion pack for <i>Rise of Nations</i>, <i>Rise of Nations: Thrones and Patriots</i>, on November 21, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignannouncerontp_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-ignannouncerontp-25">[25]</a></sup> By December 16 of that year, the game's development was about 70% complete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignpreview_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-ignpreview-26">[26]</a></sup> A website was launched for the game on February 13, 2004, which contained information, screenshots, and other details about the expansion pack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignrontpwebsiteannouce_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-ignrontpwebsiteannouce-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Big_Huge_Games" title="Big Huge Games">Big Huge Games</a> added multiple new features to the game during its development, in an attempt to fix issues with the original version. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/GameSpot" title="GameSpot">GameSpot</a>, executive producer Tim Train explained the development of the expansion pack. He stated how some nations were previously either too weak or too strong, and so features were added to balance gameplay. One way they did this is armed <a href="/wiki/Caravan_(travellers)" title="Caravan (travellers)">caravans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Merchant" title="Merchant">merchants</a> were added to the Dutch nation, in order to weaken raids initiated by other players who rush their opponent. He said <a href="/wiki/Big_Huge_Games" title="Big Huge Games">Big Huge Games</a> brought in professional players in order to get suggestions on how to improve the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timtraininterviewgspot_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-timtraininterviewgspot-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The expansion pack <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> was released for Microsoft Windows in North America on April 27, 2004, and subsequently in Europe on May 21, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dates_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-dates-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ukdate_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ukdate-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-germandate_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-germandate-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NPD_Group" title="NPD Group">NPD</a> sales figures for May, the first full month of <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> release in the United States, showed it as fourth in PC sales for the month, with its predecessor needed for the <a href="/wiki/Expansion_pack" title="Expansion pack">expansion pack</a> going from twelfth in sales in April to fifth in sales in May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ronbhghome_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-ronbhghome-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-salesmay2004_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-salesmay2004-28">[28]</a></sup> By October 27, 2004, combined sales of <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> and the base game <i>Rise of Nations</i> had passed one million copies worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-onemillionsales_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-onemillionsales-29">[29]</a></sup> On October 28, 2004, <i>Rise of Nations: Gold Edition</i> was released for Microsoft Windows. The new product included both the original <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rise_of_Nations_(video_game)" title="Rise of Nations (video game)">Rise of Nations</a></i> and <i>Thrones and Patriots</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignrongoldedition_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-ignrongoldedition-30">[30]</a></sup> In November 2004, a <a href="/wiki/Porting" title="Porting">port</a> of <i>Rise of Nations: Gold Edition</i> was released for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mac_OS_X" title="Mac OS X">Mac OS X</a>. It was published by <a href="/wiki/MacSoft_Games" title="MacSoft Games">MacSoft Games</a> and developed by both <a href="/wiki/Big_Huge_Games" title="Big Huge Games">Big Huge Games</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westlake_Interactive" title="Westlake Interactive">Westlake Interactive</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignmacport_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-ignmacport-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-macsoftron_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-macsoftron-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2012, official servers for <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> were shut down by <a href="/wiki/GameSpy_Technology" title="GameSpy Technology">GameSpy Technologies</a>, which made it difficult for multiplayer matches to be played officially online. Programs like <a href="/wiki/GameRanger" title="GameRanger">GameRanger</a> could be used to continue playing multiplayer games online without the usage of official game servers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespymultiplayershut_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-gamespymultiplayershut-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The rights to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Game_asset" title="Game asset">assets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_property" title="Intellectual property">intellectual property</a> of <i>Rise of Nations,</i> <i>Thrones and Patriots,</i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rise_of_Legends" title="Rise of Legends">Rise of Legends</a></i> and the Big Huge Games trademark were sold in the bankruptcy auction of <a href="/wiki/38_Studios" title="38 Studios">38 Studios</a> to <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Studios" title="Microsoft Studios">Microsoft Studios</a> on December 11, 2013. <a href="/wiki/Gross_(economics)" title="Gross (economics)">Gross</a> proceeds of the auction totaled $320,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-microsoftgamestudiospr_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-microsoftgamestudiospr-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Rise of Nations: Extended Edition</i> is a re-release of the main game and the expansion on <a href="/wiki/Steam_(software)" title="Steam (software)">Steam</a> that was released on June 12, 2014. Graphical changes to the game include updated textures, lighting and water. Other changes to the game include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steamworks" title="Steamworks">Steamworks</a> integration which will add <a href="/wiki/Cloud_computing" title="Cloud computing">cloud saves</a>, Steam Trading Cards, <a href="/wiki/Achievement_(video_gaming)" title="Achievement (video gaming)">achievements</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twitch_(website)" title="Twitch (website)">Twitch</a> integration and multiplayer with <a href="/wiki/Elo_rating_system" title="Elo rating system">Elo</a> ranked matches to the game. <i>Extended Edition</i> is developed by SkyBox Labs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-steam_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-steam-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modding">Modding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rise_of_Nations:_Thrones_and_Patriots&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Modding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Rise of Nations: Thrones and Patriots</i> retains the support of a loyal fanbase as of June 2012, which continues to produce a variety of <a href="/wiki/Mod_(video_gaming)" title="Mod (video gaming)">mods</a> for the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RonH_mod_topics_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-RonH_mod_topics-35">[35]</a></sup> The game can be modified in many aspects by enthusiasts, including the addition of new units, nations, buildings, scenarios, conquer the world campaigns and scripts. The modding community has been supported by the release of various packs by Big Huge Games, including the 3dsmax5 exporter plugin, and a detailed guide on the scenario editor and creating scripts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ms_scen_editor_.26_BHG_exporter_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-ms_scen_editor_.26_BHG_exporter-36">[36]</a></sup> Modifications released so far by the community have encompassed themes such as the Second World War, Vietnam War, a hypothetical Third World War, Medieval combat and Roman era warfare. While a variety of modding communities came into existence after the game's release, most sources assert that the most popular remains <i>Rise of Nations Heaven</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ronh_most_popular_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-ronh_most_popular-37">[37]</a></sup></p>

<p><i>Rise of Nations: Thrones and Patriots</i> has received a compilation score of 88% on both <a href="/wiki/GameRankings" title="GameRankings">GameRankings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamerankings_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-gamerankings-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metacritic_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-metacritic-18">[18]</a></sup> In its review, <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> claimed that <i>Thrones and Patriots</i> is "a great game [which] gets better," in reference to its predecessor <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rise_of_Nations_(video_game)" title="Rise of Nations (video game)">Rise of Nations</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignreview_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-ignreview-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/1UP.com" title="1UP.com">1UP.com</a> stated that the game is what expansion packs should try to mimic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1up_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-1up-15">[15]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Informer" title="Game Informer">Game Informer</a></i> declared that <i>Rise of Nations: Thrones and Patriots</i> is "one of the essential pieces of any strategy gamer’s collection."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gi_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-gi-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/GameSpot" title="GameSpot">GameSpot</a> commented that the game is a terrific experience and it not only provides exceptional content, but that it also improves an already good game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-8"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the expansion pack, many insignificant changes were introduced to complement the existing game structure, rather than radically alter it, and critics considered the continuing of the original game format to be good.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignreview_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-ignreview-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-9"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/GameSpy" title="GameSpy">GameSpy</a> claimed that, even with all of <i>Thrones and Patriots'</i> additions, the framework of the game still feels excellent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gspy_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-gspy-38">[38]</a></sup> However, IGN declared that the developers only added many features into the game "in the hopes that it would seem coherent," rather than actually giving the game a "central theme."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignreview_10-6"><a href="#cite_note-ignreview-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Both IGN and GameSpot claimed the game's government mechanics were similar to those of the game series <i><a href="/wiki/Civilization_(series)" title="Civilization (series)">Civilization</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignreview_10-7"><a href="#cite_note-ignreview-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-10"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup> However, GameSpy stated the game's <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a> for "managing the individual movements of units and citizens...[was] strategically brain dead." Their review explained that the wide range of upgrades, units, and technology research in different buildings requires <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Micromanagement_(computer_gaming)" title="Micromanagement (computer gaming)">micromanagement</a> and the use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hotkeys" title="Hotkeys">hotkeys</a>, especially during online play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gspy_38-2"><a href="#cite_note-gspy-38">[38]</a></sup> The review stated that the game had biased gameplay times in single-player with different nations, such as <a href="/wiki/Lakota_people" title="Lakota people">Lakota</a> having very limited use, and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">Americans</a> having a massive amount of playtime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignreview_10-8"><a href="#cite_note-ignreview-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>GameSpot stated that the multiplayer functionality of <i>Rise of Nations: Thrones and Patriots</i> had some lag, even when on a <a href="/wiki/Local_area_network" title="Local area network">local network</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gamespotreview_11-11"><a href="#cite_note-gamespotreview-11">[11]</a></sup> 1UP.com reported problems with <a href="/wiki/Crash_(computing)" title="Crash (computing)">crashes</a> in both the single and multiplayer modes of <i>Thrones and Patriots</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1up_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-1up-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In awarding <i>Rise of Nations: Thrones and Patriots</i> "Best Expansion Pack" of 2004, IGN acclaimed the additions of more playable nations and new government mechanics that <i>Rise of Nations</i> didn't have, and the new "Conquer the World" campaigns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ignpcawards_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-ignpcawards-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
