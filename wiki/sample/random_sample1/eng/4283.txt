<p>The <b>lightweight</b> division in <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial arts</a> contains different weight classes:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Fighting_Championship" title="Ultimate Fighting Championship">UFC</a> lightweight division, with an upper limit at 155 <a href="/wiki/Pound_(mass)" title="Pound (mass)">lb</a> (70.3 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kg" title="Kg">kg</a>)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Shooto" title="Shooto">Shooto</a> lightweight division, which limits competitors to 145 lb (65.8 kg)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/ONE_Championship" title="ONE Championship">ONE Championship</a>'s lightweight limits between 156 and 170 lb (70.4 to 77.1 kg)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ROAD_Fighting_Championship" title="ROAD Fighting Championship">ROAD FC</a>'s lightweight division, with an upper limit at 154 <a href="/wiki/Pound_(mass)" title="Pound (mass)">lb</a> (70 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kg" title="Kg">kg</a>)</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ambiguity_and_clarification">Ambiguity and clarification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lightweight_(MMA)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ambiguity and clarification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For the sake of uniformity, most <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> mixed martial arts media outlets consider Lightweight competitors to be between 146 and 155 lb (66 and 70 kg). This encompasses The Shooto Welterweight division (154 lb / 70 kg).</p>
<p>The UFC's lightweight division was reinstated at <i><a href="/wiki/UFC_58" title="UFC 58">UFC 58</a></i> after falling into disuse following <i><a href="/wiki/UFC_49" title="UFC 49">UFC 49</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Sean_Sherk" title="Sean Sherk">Sean Sherk</a>, a former <a href="/wiki/Welterweight_(MMA)" title="Welterweight (MMA)">welterweight</a> contender, defeated <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Florian" title="Kenny Florian">Kenny Florian</a> at <i><a href="/wiki/UFC_64" title="UFC 64">UFC 64</a></i>, becoming the first UFC lightweight champion since 2002.</p>
<p>The lightweight limit, as defined by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nevada_State_Athletic_Commission" title="Nevada State Athletic Commission">Nevada State Athletic Commission</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Boxing_Commissions" title="Association of Boxing Commissions">Association of Boxing Commissions</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is 155 lb (70 kg).</p>
