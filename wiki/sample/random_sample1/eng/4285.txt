<p><b>Lifehouse</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles</a> comprising <a href="/wiki/Jason_Wade" title="Jason Wade">Jason Wade</a> (lead vocals, guitar), <a href="/wiki/Bryce_Soderberg" title="Bryce Soderberg">Bryce Soderberg</a> (bass, vocals) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rick_Woolstenhulme,_Jr." title="Rick Woolstenhulme, Jr.">Rick Woolstenhulme, Jr.</a> (drums, percussion). The band came to mainstream prominence in 2001 with the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Hanging_by_a_Moment" title="Hanging by a Moment">Hanging by a Moment</a>" from their debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Name_Face" title="No Name Face">No Name Face</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About.com_Article_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-About.com_Article-2">[2]</a></sup> The song was number one for three weeks on the <i>Billboard</i> Modern Rock Tracks chart and peaked at number 2 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 charts, and the single won a <i>Billboard</i> Music Award for <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Year-End_Hot_100_singles_of_2001" title="Billboard Year-End Hot 100 singles of 2001">Hot 100 Single of the Year</a>, having spent twenty weeks in the top 10 and more than a year on the charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002, Lifehouse released their follow-up album <i><a href="/wiki/Stanley_Climbfall" title="Stanley Climbfall">Stanley Climbfall</a></i>. Since then, they have released five more albums: the eponymous <i><a href="/wiki/Lifehouse_(album)" title="Lifehouse (album)">Lifehouse</a></i> in 2005, <i><a href="/wiki/Who_We_Are_(Lifehouse_album)" title="Who We Are (Lifehouse album)">Who We Are</a></i> in 2007, <i><a href="/wiki/Smoke_%26_Mirrors_(Lifehouse_album)" title="Smoke &amp; Mirrors (Lifehouse album)">Smoke and Mirrors</a></i> in 2010, <i><a href="/wiki/Almer%C3%ADa_(album)" title="Almería (album)">Almería</a></i> in 2012 and <i><a href="/wiki/Out_of_the_Wasteland" title="Out of the Wasteland">Out of the Wasteland</a></i> in 2015. These albums have spawned the singles "<a href="/wiki/You_and_Me_(Lifehouse_song)" title="You and Me (Lifehouse song)">You and Me</a>", "<a href="/wiki/First_Time_(Lifehouse_song)" title="First Time (Lifehouse song)">First Time</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Whatever_It_Takes_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Whatever It Takes (Lifehouse song)">Whatever It Takes</a>", which have all been top 40 hits in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>To date, Lifehouse has sold over 15 million records worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite popular belief, Jason Wade has stated that Lifehouse is not a 'Christian band', though some songs have spiritual influence, the lyrics are open to interpretation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lifehouse_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1996.E2.80.9399:_Blyss_and_formation_of_Lifehouse">1996–99: Blyss and formation of Lifehouse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lifehouse_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1996–99: Blyss and formation of Lifehouse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jason_Wade" title="Jason Wade">Jason Wade</a> had been writing songs since his parents' divorce as a coping mechanism. After moving to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in 1995, he met <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bassist</a> Sergio Andrade, his next-door neighbor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wolfe_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wolfe-8">[8]</a></sup> The following year, Wade, Andrade, and Jon "Diff" Palmer formed "<b>Blyss</b>", the forerunner of Lifehouse, and began to play live shows at high schools and colleges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wolfe_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wolfe-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDB_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-IMDB-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JesusFreakHideout_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-JesusFreakHideout-10">[10]</a></sup> Later, Collin Hayden and Aaron Lord were added to the band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IdolMe_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-IdolMe-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Soon thereafter, word-of-mouth reached producer <a href="/wiki/Ron_Aniello" title="Ron Aniello">Ron Aniello</a>, who introduced Jason to <a href="/wiki/Jude_Cole" title="Jude Cole">Jude Cole</a>, who in turn introduced him to <a href="/wiki/DreamWorks_Records" title="DreamWorks Records">DreamWorks Records</a> principal Michael Ostin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDB_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-IMDB-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1998, Anniello produced Blyss's first demos, with the financial backing of DreamWorks Records. Some of these recordings would form the 1999 EP <i><a href="/wiki/Diff%27s_Lucky_Day" title="Diff's Lucky Day">Diff's Lucky Day</a></i>, which would be sold at live concerts or distributed among friends and music industry contacts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IdolMe_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-IdolMe-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000.E2.80.9301:_No_Name_Face">2000–01: <i>No Name Face</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lifehouse_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000–01: No Name Face">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/No_Name_Face" title="No Name Face">No Name Face</a></div>
<p>In 2000, Blyss re-recorded, remixed, and released twelve of the fifteen <i>Diff's Lucky Day</i> session demos as <i><a href="/wiki/No_Name_Face" title="No Name Face">No Name Face</a></i>, their debut major label release under the name of <i>Lifehouse</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JesusFreakHideout_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-JesusFreakHideout-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IdolMe_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-IdolMe-11">[11]</a></sup> On their choosing the band name, frontman Jason Wade said, "It's about what we do as a band and for me personally. Most of this record is about my life and about life's circumstances. Not only my life, but other peoples' lives. We thought Lifehouse was a good name for it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>No Name Face</i> met significant commercial success and established the band, and eventually would sell in excess of 4 million copies worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDB_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-IMDB-9">[9]</a></sup> This was in part due to the success of "<a href="/wiki/Hanging_by_a_Moment" title="Hanging by a Moment">Hanging by a Moment</a>", Lifehouse's first commercial single. Due to the charisma and talent of frontman Wade, DreamWork Record's focus was primarily on him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> "Hanging by a Moment" was the third song in chart history to be named the "No. 1 Song of the Year" on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> despite not having reached No. 1 on any weekly <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 survey (after <a href="/wiki/Sam_the_Sham" title="Sam the Sham">Sam the Sham &amp; The Pharaohs</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Wooly_Bully" title="Wooly Bully">Wooly Bully</a>" in 1965 and <a href="/wiki/Faith_Hill" title="Faith Hill">Faith Hill</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Breathe_(Faith_Hill_song)" title="Breathe (Faith Hill song)">Breathe</a>" in 2000). The second single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Sick_Cycle_Carousel" title="Sick Cycle Carousel">Sick Cycle Carousel</a>", was not as commercially successful as "Hanging by a Moment", peaking at No. 35 on the <i>Billboard</i> Modern Rock chart; the third and final single was "<a href="/wiki/Breathing_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Breathing (Lifehouse song)">Breathing</a>", a reworking of a song featured on <i>Diff's Lucky Day</i>.</p>
<p>The song "Everything" was the first of many Lifehouse songs to be featured in the future hit <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">WB</a> drama <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smallville_(TV_series)" title="Smallville (TV series)">Smallville</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IdolMe_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-IdolMe-11">[11]</a></sup> Seven of the band's songs would be featured in episodes of the series' first four seasons, and the band itself would perform live at the Smallville <a href="/wiki/Prom" title="Prom">prom</a> at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Smallville_(season_4)" title="Smallville (season 4)">fourth-season episode "Spirit"</a>. The song, "You and Me", would be released on the series' second soundtrack, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smallville:_The_Metropolis_Mix" title="Smallville: The Metropolis Mix">Smallville: The Metropolis Mix</a></i>, in an extended form, subtitled the "Wedding Version" ("<a href="/wiki/You_and_Me_(Lifehouse_song)" title="You and Me (Lifehouse song)">You and Me (Wedding Version)</a>"). The song "Everything" was also used in the series' 200th episode, "Homecoming", in the show's 10th and final season, as an homage to the series pilot and first-season finale episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The song "Somewhere in Between", also from <i>No Name Face,</i> was featured in the first-season episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Falcon_Beach" title="Falcon Beach">Falcon Beach</a></i>, "The Blame Game".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>By the time Lifehouse's first album was released, Palmer had left the band, and Wade and Andrade were the only remaining founding members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDB_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-IMDB-9">[9]</a></sup> Soon after recording <i>No Name Face</i>, Wade and Andrade met <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rick_Woolstenhulme" title="Rick Woolstenhulme">Rick Woolstenhulme</a>, who auditioned for the needed drummer part and joined the band as full-time drummer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDB_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-IMDB-9">[9]</a></sup> Before the audition, Woolstenhulme had been rehearsing in an adjacent room to Lifehouse and frequently he and Wade would pass each other without meeting the other.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JesusFreakHideout_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-JesusFreakHideout-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002.E2.80.9304:_Stanley_Climbfall">2002–04: <i>Stanley Climbfall</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lifehouse_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2002–04: Stanley Climbfall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Climbfall" title="Stanley Climbfall">Stanley Climbfall</a></div>
<p>Shortly after a long tour for <i>No Name Face</i>, Lifehouse reentered the studio to cut their second album, <i>Stanley Climbfall</i>. The album's initial success was short-lived and its singles, 2002's "<a href="/wiki/Spin_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Spin (Lifehouse song)">Spin</a>" and 2003's "<a href="/wiki/Take_Me_Away_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Take Me Away (Lifehouse song)">Take Me Away</a>", were vastly overshadowed by the success of the first album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Shortly after the album debuted, Rick's brother Sean Woolstenhulme (of <a href="/wiki/The_Calling" title="The Calling">The Calling</a>) officially became the fourth member of the band in June 2002. He had previously toured with Lifehouse. The band's final show of their tour promoting the album was in September 2003.</p>
<p>In April 2004, Sergio Andrade confirmed that he had decided to leave the band to pursue individual projects. Shortly after his departure, Sean also left the band to pursue other musical projects such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abandoned_Pools" title="Abandoned Pools">Abandoned Pools</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Jimmy_Chamberlin_Complex" title="The Jimmy Chamberlin Complex">The Jimmy Chamberlin Complex</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004.E2.80.9305:_Lifehouse">2004–05: <i>Lifehouse</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lifehouse_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2004–05: Lifehouse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lifehouse_(album)" title="Lifehouse (album)">Lifehouse (album)</a></div>
<p>Jason Wade and Rick Woolstenhulme remained the active members of Lifehouse. In September 2004, <a href="/wiki/Bryce_Soderberg" title="Bryce Soderberg">Bryce Soderberg</a> (previously of AM Radio) signed on as Lifehouse's new bassist. On July 6, 2004, they went to <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a> to begin work on their eponymous third album, spending less than two months in the studio to record thirteen songs produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Alagia" title="John Alagia">John Alagia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Lifehouse_(album)" title="Lifehouse (album)">Lifehouse</a></i> was released on March 22, 2005.</p>
<p>The album's first single, "<a href="/wiki/You_and_Me_(Lifehouse_song)" title="You and Me (Lifehouse song)">You and Me</a>", was released for airplay on January 18, 2005. It was written several years prior and was originally performed by Jason Wade for the 2000 independent film <i>All Over Again</i>. The song was a success and stayed on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 for 62 weeks (the fifth longest-charted in history), peaking at No. 5. The song also appears on the soundtrack to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smallville_(TV_series)" title="Smallville (TV series)">Smallville</a></i>. One episode, "<a class="extiw" href="http://www.wikia.com/wiki/c:Smallville:Spirit" title="wikicities:c:Smallville:Spirit">Spirit</a>", featured the band actually performing it and three other songs from the album ("Come Back Down", "Blind", and "Undone") on the show. The song has also appeared on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_Case_(TV_series)" title="Cold Case (TV series)">Cold Case</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Legal" title="Boston Legal">Boston Legal</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gavin_%26_Stacey" title="Gavin &amp; Stacey">Gavin &amp; Stacey</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Everwood" title="Everwood">Everwood</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_4400" title="The 4400">The 4400</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Medium_(TV_series)" title="Medium (TV series)">Medium</a> as well as the commercial for the final episode of Zoey 101</i>. The song "All In All" was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Scrubs_(TV_series)" title="Scrubs (TV series)">Scrubs</a></i>. The music video for the album's second single, "<a href="/wiki/Blind_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Blind (Lifehouse song)">Blind</a>", was released October 19, 2005. It starred <a href="/wiki/Tina_Majorino" title="Tina Majorino">Tina Majorino</a> and featured several other up-and-coming young actors such as Sarch McClain, Stephen Cheung, Christopher Thien Duc Van and Andy Walters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JesusFreakHideout_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-JesusFreakHideout-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, the newly-written song "Good Enough" was featured in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wild" title="The Wild">The Wild</a></i> and was repeated over the end credits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9308:_Who_We_Are">2006–08: <i>Who We Are</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lifehouse_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2006–08: Who We Are">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Who_We_Are_(Lifehouse_album)" title="Who We Are (Lifehouse album)">Who We Are (Lifehouse album)</a></div>
<p>Lifehouse began recording their fourth record in mid-2006 at <a href="/wiki/Kiefer_Sutherland" title="Kiefer Sutherland">Kiefer Sutherland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jude_Cole" title="Jude Cole">Jude Cole</a>'s recording studio, Ironworks Studios, in Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Their fourth album, entitled <i>Who We Are</i>, was released on June 19, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The album features Wade, Woolstenhulme and Soderberg as its lineup, and "rocks a little harder" than their previous album, according to the band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The album's release was followed by a tour with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Goo_Goo_Dolls" title="The Goo Goo Dolls">The Goo Goo Dolls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colbie_Caillat" title="Colbie Caillat">Colbie Caillat</a>, which began June 15 at the Dodge Theatre in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phoenix,_AZ" title="Phoenix, AZ">Phoenix, AZ</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whoweare_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-whoweare-24">[24]</a></sup> after which the band embarked on a "worldwide headlining tour".</p>
<p>The album's first single was a bright, pop rock love song "<a href="/wiki/First_Time_(Lifehouse_song)" title="First Time (Lifehouse song)">First Time</a>", released on April 24, 2007. The song debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> at number 48 in May,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and peaked at No. 3 on the Hot Adult Top 40 Charts.</p>
<p>The album's second single, "<a href="/wiki/Whatever_It_Takes_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Whatever It Takes (Lifehouse song)">Whatever It Takes</a>", was released to radio stations on October 29, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The song's music video premiered on Yahoo Music on November 16, 2007.</p>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Broken_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Broken (Lifehouse song)">Broken</a>", the album's third single,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> has appeared on several TV series, including <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i> ("Lay Your Hands On Me"), <i><a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minds" title="Criminal Minds">Criminal Minds</a></i> ("In Birth and Death", season 3, episode 2), <i><a href="/wiki/One_Tree_Hill_(TV_series)" title="One Tree Hill (TV series)">One Tree Hill</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/One_Life_to_Live" title="One Life to Live">One Life to Live</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hills_(TV_series)" title="The Hills (TV series)">The Hills</a></i>. Most recently, it was featured in the motion picture soundtrack <i><a href="/wiki/The_Time_Traveler%27s_Wife_(film)#Music" title="The Time Traveler's Wife (film)">The Time Traveler's Wife</a></i>. It peaked at No. 83 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. The music video for "Broken" officially debuted on VH1's Top 20 Countdown on September 20, 2008.</p>
<p>The song "Who We Are" was used in an advertisement on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%26E_Network" title="A&amp;E Network">A&amp;E</a> to promote their original programming.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Lifehouse held a contest to make a video for the song "Make Me Over".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The band gave 60 USC School of Cinematic Arts graduate students a chance to shoot the video for their new single. The winning video premiered at a gala event on December 5, 2008 at the Paley Center for Media in Beverly Hills, CA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of October 2008, the album had been certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">gold</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.9311:_Smoke_.26_Mirrors">2008–11: <i>Smoke &amp; Mirrors</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lifehouse_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2008–11: Smoke &amp; Mirrors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Smoke_%26_Mirrors_(Lifehouse_album)" title="Smoke &amp; Mirrors (Lifehouse album)">Smoke &amp; Mirrors (Lifehouse album)</a></div>
<p>Lifehouse announced in August 2008 that they would be starting on their fifth album. The band entered the studio on November 12, 2008 to begin working on the project. They stated in an interview at ESPN Magazine's NEXT Party during <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLIII" title="Super Bowl XLIII">Super Bowl XLIII</a> that the album should be released in late 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 11, 2009, the band announced that Ben Carey was an official member of the band. They said that they had been "a four-piece for some time" and that Ben is "family to us". Carey had been touring with the band since 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lifehouse revealed during their 2009 tour that the album would also include the band's previously released single "From Where You Are." It was announced on October 15 that the new album would be called <i><a href="/wiki/Smoke_%26_Mirrors_(Lifehouse_album)" title="Smoke &amp; Mirrors (Lifehouse album)">Smoke &amp; Mirrors</a></i> and would be released on December 8, 2009. However, on November 13, 2009, the band stated on its web page that it would be delayed until February 2, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The first single, "<a href="/wiki/Halfway_Gone" title="Halfway Gone">Halfway Gone</a>", was released on October 27, 2009. The music video premiered on the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> Top 20 on VH1.com at the end of November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The album was released in a standard, deluxe, and two-disc deluxe edition. On December 22, it was reported that the album had been pushed back once again, this time to February 23, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The album was eventually released on March 2, 2010.</p>
<p>The album debuted at No. 6 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> with 54,203 sold. This marked the highest chart debut of their ten-year career, beating out first week chart position for their 2002 sophomore set <i><a href="/wiki/Stanley_Climbfall" title="Stanley Climbfall">Stanley Climbfall</a></i>, which debuted at No. 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312:_Almer.C3.ADa">2011–12: <i>Almería</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lifehouse_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2011–12: Almería">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Almer%C3%ADa_(album)" title="Almería (album)">Almería (album)</a></div>
<p>In August 2011, Lifehouse announced on their official Facebook page that they were working on their sixth studio album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 11, 2012, Lifehouse released a new single featuring <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Bedingfield" title="Natasha Bedingfield">Natasha Bedingfield</a> entitled "<a href="/wiki/Between_the_Raindrops" title="Between the Raindrops">Between the Raindrops</a>". A month later, on October 18, the band announced that the title of their sixth studio album would be <i>Almería</i>, named after a city in Spain famous as the locale of many classic <a href="/wiki/Spaghetti_Western" title="Spaghetti Western">Spaghetti Western</a> films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> <i>Almería</i> was released on December 11 in the US and December 12 worldwide. The album was met with mixed reviews, and failed to spawn a second single. A tour was planned for spring 2013 but never happened.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present:_Hiatus_and_Out_of_the_Wasteland">2013–present: Hiatus and <i>Out of the Wasteland</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lifehouse_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2013–present: Hiatus and Out of the Wasteland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Out_of_the_Wasteland" title="Out of the Wasteland">Out of the Wasteland</a></div>
<p>On July 24, 2013, Wade posted a letter to the band's Facebook page saying that Lifehouse had parted with Geffen and was on a temporary hiatus as each band member turned attention to other projects (with the assurance that the band would continue to make records in the future).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Drummer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rick_Woolstenhulme,_Jr." title="Rick Woolstenhulme, Jr.">Rick Woolstenhulme, Jr.</a> began touring with the <a href="/wiki/Goo_Goo_Dolls" title="Goo Goo Dolls">Goo Goo Dolls</a>; bassist <a href="/wiki/Bryce_Soderberg" title="Bryce Soderberg">Bryce Soderberg</a> initiated a new band titled KOMOX; lead guitarist Ben Carey continued work in country rock band Elvis Monroe; and singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Jason_Wade" title="Jason Wade">Jason Wade</a> worked on his as-yet-unreleased <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a> solo record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_Lifehouse_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-About_Lifehouse-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The band's final show before their break was on September 25, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> As of March 2014, they began playing shows again, though not as part of an official tour.</p>
<p>On December 8, 2014, Lifehouse stated on their official website that Ben Carey would no longer be a part of Lifehouse and instead would be focusing on his own projects; for live shows, the band later revealed that Steve Stout would fill in on lead guitar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Hillström previously worked with Soderberg on his KOMOX project.</p>
<p>Lifehouse reentered the studio in May 2014. On November 15, 2014, the band announced a new song from an upcoming album called "<a href="/wiki/Flight_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Flight (Lifehouse song)">Flight</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> The song was released independently on November 18, along with an announcement that the band's hiatus was officially over and a new album would be released in April 2015 titled <i>Seven</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> The first official single "<a href="/wiki/Hurricane_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Hurricane (Lifehouse song)">Hurricane</a>" was released on January 27, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> On January 27, <i>Billboard</i>.com revealed a new album title <i>Out of the Wasteland</i> and published the official standard-edition track list of the album, giving an official release date of May 19, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_Lifehouse_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-About_Lifehouse-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album was released online and in stores on May 26, 2015 and debuted at number-one on the Independent Albums chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> A special <a href="/wiki/Target_Corporation" title="Target Corporation">Target</a> edition was made available with four bonus tracks. The band was to begin touring in support of the album on June 19, as an opening during the second North American leg of the <a href="/wiki/No_Fixed_Address_Tour" title="No Fixed Address Tour">No Fixed Address Tour</a>, before the tour was cancelled due to the health of headlining band <a href="/wiki/Nickelback" title="Nickelback">Nickelback</a>'s lead singer, <a href="/wiki/Chad_Kroeger" title="Chad Kroeger">Chad Kroeger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> A European headlining tour commenced on September 15, 2015 in Amsterdam, with extra dates added due to high demand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_information">Other information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lifehouse_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shortly after the death of <a href="/wiki/George_Harrison" title="George Harrison">George Harrison</a>, the band performed an acoustic rendition of "<a href="/wiki/Something_(Beatles_song)" title="Something (Beatles song)">Something</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Call_with_Carson_Daly" title="Last Call with Carson Daly">Last Call with Carson Daly</a></i>.</p>
<p>The band has also covered the <a href="/wiki/Keane_(band)" title="Keane (band)">Keane</a> single "<a href="/wiki/Somewhere_Only_We_Know" title="Somewhere Only We Know">Somewhere Only We Know</a>" live on <i>Yahoo! Music</i>. The group also stated that Keane's album <i>Under the Iron Sea</i> was their favorite album of 2007.</p>
<p>In 2007, the band did a cover of The Rolling Stones' "Beast of Burden" for the Stripped Music Sessions. The band said that they wanted to "pay homage to the godfathers of rock".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wade wrote the song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/From_Where_You_Are_(Lifehouse_song)" title="From Where You Are (Lifehouse song)">From Where You Are</a>" for <a href="/wiki/Allstate" title="Allstate">Allstate's</a> Teen Driving Program. The song was featured on the "Tall Lights" commercial and aired on NBC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
