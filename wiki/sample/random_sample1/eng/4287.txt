<p>The <b>S1</b> service of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S-Bahn_Rhein-Main" title="S-Bahn Rhein-Main">S-Bahn Rhein-Main</a> system bearing the KBS (German <a href="/wiki/List_of_scheduled_railway_routes_in_Germany" title="List of scheduled railway routes in Germany">scheduled railway route</a>) number 645.1 is an important railway connection running east-west. It operates between the Hesse state capital <a href="/wiki/Wiesbaden" title="Wiesbaden">Wiesbaden</a> and the southern <a href="/wiki/Offenbach_(district)" title="Offenbach (district)">Offenbach rural district</a> serving the densely populated area along the <a href="/wiki/Main_(river)" title="Main (river)">Main</a> river.</p>
<p>With a route length of over 70 km (43 mi), 30 stations and a journey time of 87 minutes, it is the longest S-Bahn service in the Rhine-Main area. The average speed of travel is about 49.72 km/h (30.9 mph) and a station distance of about 2 km (1.2 mi).</p>
<p>Usually <a href="/wiki/DBAG_Class_423" title="DBAG Class 423">DBAG Class 423</a> railcars are used on this service. Its predecessor <a href="/wiki/DB_Class_420" title="DB Class 420">class 420</a> is only used for shortened shuttle services.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Routes">Routes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S1_(Rhine-Main_S-Bahn)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Routes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Taunus_railway">Taunus railway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S1_(Rhine-Main_S-Bahn)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taunus railway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Taunus_Railway" title="Taunus Railway">Taunus Railway</a></div>
<p>The western branch of the service starts at the central station of Wiesbaden and utilises the oldest railway route of the Rhine-Main area running between <a href="/wiki/Mainz-Kastel_station" title="Mainz-Kastel station">Mainz-Kastel</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt-H%C3%B6chst_station" title="Frankfurt-Höchst station">Frankfurt-Höchst</a> parallel to the Main and the <a href="/wiki/Taunus" title="Taunus">Taunus</a> mountains from which the railway line derived its name. The about 31 km (19 mi) long route crosses the southern part of Wiesbaden, the <a href="/wiki/Main-Taunus-Kreis" title="Main-Taunus-Kreis">Main-Taunus-Kreis</a> and the westernmost part of Frankfurt. The service uses the existing double track route together with national, regional, and freight transportation. It serves eight stations. The Taunus Railway was opened in stages between 26 September 1839 and 19 May 1840 and has been used by S-Bahn services since 1978.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main-Lahn_railway">Main-Lahn railway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S1_(Rhine-Main_S-Bahn)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main-Lahn railway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Main-Lahn_Railway" title="Main-Lahn Railway">Main-Lahn Railway</a></div>
<p>Between Farbwerke and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt-Griesheim_station" title="Frankfurt-Griesheim station">Frankfurt-Griesheim</a> this service shares the Main-Lahn line with freight and shunting operations. National and regional services use the parallel running Taunus railway in this section. The Main-Lahn railway was completed on 15 October 1877 and has been used by S-Bahn services since 1978.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="City_tunnel">City tunnel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S1_(Rhine-Main_S-Bahn)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: City tunnel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Citytunnel_Frankfurt" title="Citytunnel Frankfurt">Citytunnel Frankfurt</a></div>
<p>The city tunnel is an underground, pure S-Bahn route used by almost all services (except for the S7 service which terminates at the central station). The tunnel was opened in four stages in 1978, 1983, 1990 and 1992. In a short section between Mühlberg and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Offenbach-Kaiserlei_station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Offenbach-Kaiserlei station (page does not exist)">Offenbach-Kaiserlei</a> the <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt%E2%80%93Hanau_(south_bank)_railway" title="Frankfurt–Hanau (south bank) railway">South Main railway</a> is used. The section from Mühlberg to <a href="/wiki/Offenbach_Ost_station" title="Offenbach Ost station">Offenbach Ost</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Offenbach_City_Tunnel" title="Offenbach City Tunnel">Offenbach City Tunnel</a> was opened in 1995.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rodgau_railway">Rodgau railway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S1_(Rhine-Main_S-Bahn)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rodgau railway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rodgau_Railway" title="Rodgau Railway">Rodgau Railway</a></div>
<p>This line was opened in 1896 and has been used since 2003 only by S-Bahn services.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=S1_(Rhine-Main_S-Bahn)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The S1 was one of the first six services of the Rhine-Main S-Bahn system. In a prior test operation it ran between Wiesbaden Central Station and Frankfurt Central Station. The service was then called R1 where the letter "R" stands for regional. After the opening of the Frankfurt Citytunnel the service was renamed to S1 and extended to the new Hauptwache underground station. Further extensions of the tunnel followed in 1983 (Konstablerwache) and 1990 (Ostendstraße and Lokalbahnhof) so that the Südbahnhof (South station) became the service's eastern terminal. In 1992 the eastern branch of the Citytunnel to the Mühlberg station was opened and the S1 started operation in this section shutting down its service to Lokalbahnhof and Südbahnhof. With the opening of the Offenbach Citytunnel, the service was extended eastwards to the station Offenbach Ost (Offenbach East).</p>
<p>In 2003 the Rodgau railway was included. After two years of construction work this route was changed to S-Bahn operation. Since then the new eastern terminal station is Rödermark-Ober-Roden.</p>
