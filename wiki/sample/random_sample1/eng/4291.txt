<p><b>Rajai Lavae Davis</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="/ʒ/ 's' in 'pleasure'">ʒ</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'v' in 'vie'">v</span><span title="/ɪ/ or /ə/ 'e' in 'roses'">ᵻ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born October 19, 1980) is an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Outfielder" title="Outfielder">outfielder</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB). He previously played for the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a>, <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Tigers" title="Detroit Tigers">Detroit Tigers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a>.</p>
<p>Davis began his career as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Switch-hitter" title="Switch-hitter">switch-hitter</a>, but became a full-time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right-handed" title="Right-handed">right-handed</a> hitter prior to the 2004 season. He also throws right-handed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mlb-bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-bio-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Davis was born in <a href="/wiki/Norwich,_Connecticut" title="Norwich, Connecticut">Norwich, Connecticut</a>, on October 19, 1980. He played <a href="/wiki/Little_League_Baseball" title="Little League Baseball">Little League Baseball</a> in <a href="/wiki/Willimantic,_Connecticut" title="Willimantic, Connecticut">Willimantic, Connecticut</a>, for the Willimantic Fire Fighters. In 1991, Davis helped the Willimantic Little League All-Star Team win the District 11 championship. Rajai also played in the Willimantic Youth Football League.</p>
<p>In 1999, Davis graduated from New London High School where he earned letters playing <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, and <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">football</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-media_guide_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-media_guide-2">[2]</a></sup> During the summer he was a member of the New London <a href="/wiki/American_Legion_Baseball" title="American Legion Baseball">American Legion</a> team.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After high school, Davis attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Connecticut#Avery_Point_campus" title="University of Connecticut">University of Connecticut at Avery Point</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NJCAA" title="NJCAA">NJCAA</a> in <a href="/wiki/Groton,_Connecticut" title="Groton, Connecticut">Groton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mlb-bio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> While in college, Davis played for the <a href="/wiki/New_England_Collegiate_Baseball_League" title="New England Collegiate Baseball League">New England Collegiate Baseball League</a>'s Middletown Giants (now the <a href="/wiki/Futures_Collegiate_Baseball_League" title="Futures Collegiate Baseball League">Futures Collegiate Baseball League</a>'s <a href="/wiki/North_Shore_Navigators" title="North Shore Navigators">North Shore Navigators</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor_leagues">Minor leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Minor leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Davis was drafted by the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> in the 38th round (1,134th overall) of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2001_Major_League_Baseball_Draft" title="2001 Major League Baseball Draft">2001 Major League Baseball Draft</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Second_baseman" title="Second baseman">second baseman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8.2F20.2F07-fsn_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-8.2F20.2F07-fsn-3">[3]</a></sup> He played six full minor league seasons before making his major league debut. He had a .305 career minor league <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">batting average</a> to go along with 251 <a href="/wiki/Stolen_base" title="Stolen base">stolen bases</a> in 322 attempts. In addition, Davis made three consecutive All-Star appearance at three different levels of play from 2002 to 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cube_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-cube-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_leagues">Major leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Major leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pittsburgh_Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pittsburgh Pirates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Davis made his major league debut for the Pirates on August 14, 2006, going 0-for-1 in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pinch_hit" title="Pinch hit">pinch hit</a> appearance against the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a>. He stole his first base two days later, but did not get his first Major League <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hit</a> for another two weeks. It came on August 29, in the form of a pinch hit <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">double</a> off <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Zambrano" title="Carlos Zambrano">Carlos Zambrano</a>. He made his first and only defensive appearance of 2006 the next day, playing three <a href="/wiki/Inning" title="Inning">innings</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_field" title="Right field">right field</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mlb-bio_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-bio-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="San_Francisco_Giants">San Francisco Giants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: San Francisco Giants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Davis was traded to the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a> with pitcher Stephen MacFarland on July 31, 2007, for <a href="/wiki/Matt_Morris_(baseball)" title="Matt Morris (baseball)">Matt Morris</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trade_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-trade-5">[5]</a></sup> He made his Giants debut the next night against the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a>, stealing a base and throwing out a runner from <a href="/wiki/Center_fielder" title="Center fielder">center field</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8.2F1.2F07-boxscore_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-8.2F1.2F07-boxscore-6">[6]</a></sup> In his time with the Giants, Davis was a semi-regular in the Giants <a href="/wiki/Batting_order_(baseball)" title="Batting order (baseball)">lineup</a>, filling in for <a href="/wiki/Dave_Roberts_(outfielder)" title="Dave Roberts (outfielder)">Dave Roberts</a> or <a href="/wiki/Barry_Bonds" title="Barry Bonds">Barry Bonds</a> whenever necessary.</p>
<p>On August 13, the Giants played a <a href="/wiki/Doubleheader_(baseball)" title="Doubleheader (baseball)">doubleheader</a> in Pittsburgh, which was Davis' first time back since the trade. He started in center in both games, going a combined three-for-seven, with three doubles, two <a href="/wiki/Run_(baseball)" title="Run (baseball)">runs scored</a>, two stolen bases, and a <a href="/wiki/Run_batted_in" title="Run batted in">run batted in</a> (RBI).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8.2F13.2F07-boxscore1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-8.2F13.2F07-boxscore1-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8.2F13.2F07-boxscore2_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-8.2F13.2F07-boxscore2-8">[8]</a></sup> Additionally, in the second game, Davis made a spectacular diving catch on the <a href="/wiki/Warning_track" title="Warning track">warning track</a> in left-center field.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8.2F13.2F07-recap_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-8.2F13.2F07-recap-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Catch_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Catch-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Just three days later, he robbed <a href="/wiki/Andruw_Jones" title="Andruw Jones">Andruw Jones</a> of a possible <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home run</a> to end the game at <a href="/wiki/Turner_Field" title="Turner Field">Turner Field</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Catch_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Catch-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8.2F16.2F07-recap_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-8.2F16.2F07-recap-11">[11]</a></sup> Davis kept the ball as a souvenir.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8.2F20.2F07-fsn_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-8.2F20.2F07-fsn-3">[3]</a></sup> The next night, Davis hit his first career home run off <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_Marlins" title="Florida Marlins">Florida Marlins</a> <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">starting pitcher</a> <a href="/wiki/Scott_Olsen" title="Scott Olsen">Scott Olsen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8.2F17.2F07-recap_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-8.2F17.2F07-recap-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Davis was second among the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> (NL) rookie leaders in stolen bases, with 22. The leader, <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Chris_Young_(outfielder)" title="Chris Young (outfielder)">Chris Young</a>, had 27 – but also had nearly three times as many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/At-bat" title="At-bat">at-bats</a> as Davis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn-rookies_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-espn-rookies-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Oakland_Athletics">Oakland Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Oakland Athletics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On April 23, 2008, Davis was claimed off waivers by the <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a> and was added to the active roster. With Oakland in 2008, he stole 25 bases in 196 at-bats. In 2009, Davis became the primary center fielder for the A's hit .305 with 41 stolen bases in 125 games, primarily in center field. In 2010 he also started for most of the season hitting .284 with 52 RBIs and 50 stolen bases. When <a href="/wiki/Coco_Crisp" title="Coco Crisp">Coco Crisp</a> came back from injury, Davis switched from center to left and right field for the remainder of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Toronto_Blue_Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Toronto Blue Jays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Following the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/David_DeJesus" title="David DeJesus">David DeJesus</a>, the Athletics traded Davis to the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a> for <a href="/wiki/Trystan_Magnuson" title="Trystan Magnuson">Trystan Magnuson</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daniel_Farquhar" title="Daniel Farquhar">Daniel Farquhar</a> on November 17, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a game against the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels_of_Anaheim" title="Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim">Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim</a> on August 14, 2011, Davis tore his hamstring running to first base. He was placed on the 15-day disabled list after the game. Prior to the injury, Davis ranked third in the American League in stolen bases with 34.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<p>On May 18, 2012, in a home game against the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a>, Davis had the first multi home run game of his career, hitting two home runs and four RBIs in a 14–5 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On July 30 in a game against the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a>, Davis hit his first career lead-off home run. The home run, his sixth of the season, established a career high for Davis in a single season. On August 12, 2012, Davis recorded five RBIs against the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>, establishing a single game career high in RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Davis finished second in stolen bases, with 46, for the 2012 campaign (<a href="/wiki/Mike_Trout" title="Mike Trout">Mike Trout</a> of the Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim led the league with 49).</p>
<p>Davis was placed on the disabled list on May 11, 2013, with a strained left oblique.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> After a brief two game rehab assignment with the <a href="/wiki/Dunedin_Blue_Jays" title="Dunedin Blue Jays">Dunedin Blue Jays</a>, Davis was activated from the disabled list on June 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On June 8, in the bottom of the 18th inning against the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a>, Davis hit a single that scored <a href="/wiki/Emilio_Bonifacio" title="Emilio Bonifacio">Emilio Bonifacio</a> in the longest Blue Jays game in franchise history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In a game against the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> on July 28, Davis tied a Blue Jays franchise record by stealing four bases, joining <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Damaso_Garcia" title="Damaso Garcia">Damaso Garcia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Collins" title="Dave Collins">Dave Collins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Alomar" title="Roberto Alomar">Roberto Alomar</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Otis_Nixon" title="Otis Nixon">Otis Nixon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Detroit_Tigers">Detroit Tigers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Detroit Tigers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On December 11, 2013, the Tigers signed Davis to a two-year, $10 million contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On June 30, 2014, he hit a walk-off grand slam home run against <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a> closer <a href="/wiki/Sean_Doolittle" title="Sean Doolittle">Sean Doolittle</a> to win the game 5–4. It was the first walk-off homer of any kind for Davis in his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 30, 2014, Davis recorded his 32nd stolen base of the season, and his 300th stolen base of his career. Davis became just the second active player to record a 30-steal season for three different teams. Davis' 300 stolen bases ranks him 12th among active players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2014, Davis played in 134 games for Detroit, batting .282 with 36 stolen bases. He was used in an outfield platoon in 2015, playing in 112 games. Despite limited plate appearances this season (370), he hit a career-high 11 triples.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cleveland_Indians">Cleveland Indians</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cleveland Indians">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On December 17, 2015, Davis signed a one-year, $5.25 million contract with the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 2, 2016, Davis hit for the cycle in a game against the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a>. It was the eighth cycle in <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a> franchise history, the first since <a href="/wiki/Travis_Hafner" title="Travis Hafner">Travis Hafner</a> in 2003, and the sixth player in Major League history to hit for a reverse cycle. He led off the game with a home run, hit a triple in the third inning, a double in the sixth inning, and finished the cycle with a single in the ninth inning.</p>
<p>On November 2, 2016, during <a href="/wiki/2016_World_Series#Game_7" title="2016 World Series">Game 7 of the 2016 World Series</a>, Davis hit a game tying, two-run home run in the eighth inning off Cubs pitcher <a href="/wiki/Aroldis_Chapman" title="Aroldis Chapman">Aroldis Chapman</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Slint_with_Oakland_Athletics">Second Slint with Oakland Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rajai_Davis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Second Slint with Oakland Athletics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On January 1, 2017 it was announced that Davis had signed a one-year deal with the Athletics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
