<p><b>LaTavia Marie Roberson</b> (born November 1, 1981) is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vocalist" title="Vocalist">vocalist</a>, songwriter, and actress. Roberson rose to fame in the late 1990s as an original member of the R&amp;B group <a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child" title="Destiny's Child">Destiny's Child</a>, one of the world's <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_girl_groups" title="List of best-selling girl groups">best-selling girl groups</a> of all time. <a class="external text" href="http://www.iamlatavia.com" rel="nofollow">www.iamlatavia.com</a></p>
<p>Following her departure from the group, Roberson formed Anjel along with former Destiny's Child member <a href="/wiki/LeToya_Luckett" title="LeToya Luckett">LeToya Luckett</a>. After their album was shelved, Roberson went on hiatus and returned to Houston. Roberson also starred in various stage plays including <i>Those Jeans</i>, <i>How to Love</i>, and <i>Not My Family</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Roberson also became a main cast-member for <a href="/wiki/R%26B_Divas:_Atlanta" title="R&amp;B Divas: Atlanta">R&amp;B Divas: Atlanta</a> alongside singers such as <a href="/wiki/Angie_Stone" title="Angie Stone">Angie Stone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keke_Wyatt" title="Keke Wyatt">Keke Wyatt</a> and long-time friend <a href="/wiki/Kameelah_Williams" title="Kameelah Williams">Meelah</a> of <a href="/wiki/702_(group)" title="702 (group)">702</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Roberson has sold over 25 million records with <a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child" title="Destiny's Child">Destiny's Child</a>. Her work has earned her several awards and nominations, including two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a> and three <a href="/wiki/Soul_Train_Music_Awards" title="Soul Train Music Awards">Soul Train Music Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LaTavia_Roberson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LaTavia Roberson was born on November 1, 1981, she is of <a href="/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans">African American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Creole_people" title="Louisiana Creole people">Louisiana Creole</a> (African, Native American, and French) descent. In the 1980s, Prior to joining Destiny's Child, LaTavia was a child model, appearing in commercials such as Soft and Beautiful's Just for Me Hair Care relaxer for kids.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LaTavia_Roberson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990.E2.80.932000:_Girl.27s_Tyme_and_Destiny.27s_Child">1990–2000: Girl's Tyme and Destiny's Child</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LaTavia_Roberson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990–2000: Girl's Tyme and Destiny's Child">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When LaTavia was nine years old, she auditioned for a girl group to become a rapper and dancer, and was one of the many girls who got through. She met <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9_Knowles" title="Beyoncé Knowles">Beyoncé Knowles</a> on the same day of the audition for the group and became best friends, singing and dancing with other local girls in which LaTavia and Beyoncé made a video for the song "One Time". She then met <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Rowland" title="Kelly Rowland">Kelly Rowland</a> in elementary school, after hearing her sing she told her to try out for the group which she did. Initially LaTavia was a rapper and dancer at the time and did not sing; subsequently she had vocal lessons and, after singing at local events, LaTavia got her break when she entered the singing competition TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Search" title="Star Search">Star Search</a></i> with Beyoncé, Kelly Rowland, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tamar_Davis" title="Tamar Davis">Tamar Davis</a> also LaTavia's cousins, sisters Nikki and Nina Taylor. The group, then named "Girl's Tyme" were heartbroken after losing the competition. Beyonce's father, <a href="/wiki/Mathew_Knowles" title="Mathew Knowles">Mathew Knowles</a> began to manage the group whom changed the line-up as the group was then reduced to a quartet, with best-friend Beyoncé, Rowland, and Beyoncé's elementary school friend <a href="/wiki/LeToya_Luckett" title="LeToya Luckett">LeToya Luckett</a>. The group underwent several name changes: The Dolls, Something Fresh, and Cliché. They were shortly signed to <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra Records</a>. However the group were dropped by the label. After plenty of training done by the manager to become prepared and ready for the music industry they were then signed by <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> in 1997 by the name of <a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child" title="Destiny's Child">Destiny's Child</a> with Knowles as <a href="/wiki/Lead_vocalist" title="Lead vocalist">lead vocalist</a>, Rowland as second-lead vocalist plus alongside Roberson and Luckett as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Background_vocalists" title="Background vocalists">background vocalists</a> with Roberson as <a href="/wiki/Alto" title="Alto">alto</a> adding the low notes and Luckett as the <a href="/wiki/Soprano" title="Soprano">soprano</a> adding the high notes to the group's harmony,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> however also occasional leads. Roberson was also designated as the group's <a href="/wiki/Spokesperson" title="Spokesperson">spokesperson</a>. In 1998, Destiny's Child released their self-titled debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child_(album)" title="Destiny's Child (album)">Destiny's Child</a></i>. The singles were "<a href="/wiki/No,_No,_No_(Destiny%27s_Child_song)" title="No, No, No (Destiny's Child song)">No, No, No</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a> and "<a href="/wiki/With_Me_(Destiny%27s_Child_song)" title="With Me (Destiny's Child song)">With Me</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Roberson can be heard rapping in the song "Illusions". Later in 1998 their song "<a href="/wiki/Get_on_the_Bus_(song)" title="Get on the Bus (song)">Get on the Bus</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a> (which was released as a single in Europe) was featured on the <i><a href="/wiki/Why_Do_Fools_Fall_in_Love_(film)" title="Why Do Fools Fall in Love (film)">Why Do Fools Fall in Love</a></i> soundtrack. The song would later service as the lead single for their second album. Destiny's Child toured as an opening act on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TLC_(band)" title="TLC (band)">TLC</a>'s <a href="/wiki/FanMail_Tour" title="FanMail Tour">FanMail Tour</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a>'s debut tour.</p>
<p>In 1999, Destiny's Child released their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Writing%27s_on_the_Wall" title="The Writing's on the Wall">The Writing's on the Wall</a></i>. It became one of the biggest selling albums released by a female group and was certified eight-times platinum in the U.S. The album includes four hit singles, "<a href="/wiki/Bills,_Bills,_Bills" title="Bills, Bills, Bills">Bills, Bills, Bills</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Bug_a_Boo" title="Bug a Boo">Bug a Boo</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Say_My_Name" title="Say My Name">Say My Name</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Jumpin%27,_Jumpin%27" title="Jumpin', Jumpin'">Jumpin', Jumpin'</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> LaTavia also gained co-writing contribution more on this album than their first. Roberson performed lead on two songs: "Sweet Sixteen" and "Where'd You Go". Additionally LaTavia sang lead on the song "Can't Help Myself" but this song was only released on the albums <i>Houston Edition</i>. In late 1999 in the midst of the success of <i>The Writing's on the Wall</i>, Roberson and <a href="/wiki/LeToya_Luckett" title="LeToya Luckett">LeToya Luckett</a> attempted to bring in a third-party mediator to work alongside their manager <a href="/wiki/Mathew_Knowles" title="Mathew Knowles">Mathew Knowles</a>; however, the pair soon found themselves on the outs with Knowles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> When the "Say My Name" video debuted in February 2000, they were replaced by two new members, <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Williams_(singer)" title="Michelle Williams (singer)">Michelle Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Farrah_Franklin" title="Farrah Franklin">Farrah Franklin</a> which led to several lawsuits with the outcome of Roberson and Luckett being entitled to royalty checks from the previous albums they had made with the group.</p>
<p>Whilst being in Destiny's Child, with the other members, LaTavia had appeared in artists music videos such as <a href="/wiki/Jagged_Edge_(group)" title="Jagged Edge (group)">Jagged Edge</a>. Roberson had also performed with Destiny's Child in live shows and had won awards such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soul_Train_Awards" title="Soul Train Awards">Soul Train Awards</a>. She had also starred in TV roles such as <a href="/wiki/Smart_Guy" title="Smart Guy">Smart Guy</a> and the movie Beverly Hills along with the other members of the group.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001.E2.80.932010:_Post-Destiny.27s_Child">2001–2010: Post-Destiny's Child</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LaTavia_Roberson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2001–2010: Post-Destiny's Child">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, LaTavia and ex-destiny's child group member LeToya had achieved two Grammys for their contribution to <a href="/wiki/Say_My_Name" title="Say My Name">Say My Name</a>. They had also created a group called Anjel with two other girls, Naty Quinones and Tiffany Beaudon (whom then was replaced by Natasha Ramos). They recorded over 22 tracks but did not release an album. The tracks, however, were leaked on the internet. The band appeared in Jagged Edge's music video "Where the Party At" Remix. The record company was having difficulty so the group then parted ways for solo careers in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, LaTavia got invited to replace <a href="/wiki/Kandi_Burruss" title="Kandi Burruss">Kandi</a> in the group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xscape_(band)" title="Xscape (band)">Xscape</a>, but passed on the opportunity due to her priority to take care of her family at home in Houston.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, LaTavia along with other <a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child" title="Destiny's Child">Destiny's Child</a> past members <a href="/wiki/LeToya_Luckett" title="LeToya Luckett">LeToya Luckett</a> and <a href="/wiki/Farrah_Franklin" title="Farrah Franklin">Farrah Franklin</a> appeared in the TV series <a href="/wiki/Boulevard_of_Broken_Dreams_(TV_series)" title="Boulevard of Broken Dreams (TV series)"><i>Boulevard of Broken Dreams</i></a> telling their side of the story about what happened during their time with Destiny's Child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2008, LaTavia was in a stage play titled <i>Those Jeans</i>. It was described as "a truly well written love story, about a fashion designer and a high fashion photographer, looking for love in all the wrong places". It ran from April 18, 2008 to July 12, 2008. In 2009, LaTavia featured in the song "Swagga Check" by Young Sween on his album <i>The Goodie Room</i>, released by Fleet Street Records; the song was mistakenly named "Holdin On to You" on iTunes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, LaTavia appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Real_Housewives_of_Atlanta" title="The Real Housewives of Atlanta">The Real Housewives of Atlanta</a></i> speaking to her then-lawyer <a href="/wiki/The_Real_Housewives_of_Atlanta" title="The Real Housewives of Atlanta">Phaedra Parks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> talking about how she dealt with alcoholic problems and getting in trouble with the law for a DWI.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.932015:_R.26B_Divas.2C_stage_plays_and_motherhood">2013–2015: <i>R&amp;B Divas</i>, stage plays and motherhood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LaTavia_Roberson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013–2015: R&amp;B Divas, stage plays and motherhood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, LaTavia starred in J. F. Bailey's stage plays <i>How to Love</i> and <i>Not My Family</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On August 21, 2013, LaTavia welcomed her first child, a girl named Lyric Richard, with record producer <a href="/wiki/Don_Vito_(producer)" title="Don Vito (producer)">Don Vito</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2014, LaTavia was added to the main cast of <i><a href="/wiki/R%26B_Divas:_Atlanta" title="R&amp;B Divas: Atlanta">R&amp;B Divas: Atlanta</a></i> season 3, which her main storyline included being afraid to sing on the show, as she felt as if she wasn't ready to do so, despite the show being called R&amp;B Divas. Season 3 of R&amp;B Divas consisted of eleven episodes which LaTavia had starred in. (including the reunion shows).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> She has also appeared on <a href="/wiki/The_Wendy_Williams_Show" title="The Wendy Williams Show">the Wendy Williams Show</a>. In 2015, she had gotten into the hair business with Luxury Hair Direct which specialises in hair extensions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> LaTavia was also involved with the charity 'Saving Our Daughters'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.93present:_Web-series_and_upcoming_films">2016–present: Web-series and upcoming films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LaTavia_Roberson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016–present: Web-series and upcoming films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, Roberson has started an online YouTube-based series called 'The Online Diary of LaTavia Roberson.' <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2017, she will make her film debut as actress in the horror movie <i>"But Deliver Us From Evil"</i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Eric_Roberts" title="Eric Roberts">Eric Roberts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Later that year, she will appears in two movies <i>"Dirty South House Arrest"</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and <i>"The Hills"</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In spring of 2017, LaTavia is also set to release her <a href="/wiki/Autobiography" title="Autobiography">autobiography</a> which she describes as a <a href="/wiki/Memoir" title="Memoir">memoir</a> to her fans entitled 'I Am LaTavia'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> She engaged in a feud with the publication after alleging they misquoted and misrepresented her interview online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Survivor_Lawsuit">Survivor Lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LaTavia_Roberson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Survivor Lawsuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 2000, Roberson and Luckett filed a lawsuit against Mathew Knowles and the group. This began a media battle in which the two sides exchanged barbs in magazine and newspaper interviews. Roberson and Luckett agreed to a settlement that required them to drop the part of their lawsuit that targeted their former group members (though they retained the suit against Mathew Knowles) and required both sides to stop disparaging each other in public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Roberson and Luckett filed another suit against Destiny's Child. They claimed that the first single, "<a href="/wiki/Survivor_(Destiny%27s_Child_song)" title="Survivor (Destiny's Child song)">Survivor</a>", from Destiny's Child's 2001 album <a href="/wiki/Survivor_(Destiny%27s_Child_album)" title="Survivor (Destiny's Child album)">of the same name</a> violated their previous settlement due to its lyrics which they believed referenced the ugly split with lyrics such as 'thought I couldn't sell without you, sold nine million' . The lawsuit however, was dropped.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LaTavia_Roberson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Roberson has revealed that she had suffered with an alcohol problem after Destiny's Child to self soothe early childhood trauma. This led to being arrested with a DWI, however after Roberson went through counselling and is using her experience to help others going through the same thing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Roberson had given birth to daughter Lyric Richard in August 2013 with producer <a href="/wiki/Don_Vito_(producer)" title="Don Vito (producer)">Don Vito</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In June 2016, she suffered a late pregnancy loss.</p>
