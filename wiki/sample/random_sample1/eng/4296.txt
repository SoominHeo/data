<p><b>Latakia Governorate</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">مُحافظة اللاذقية</span>‎‎ / <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ALA-LC" title="ALA-LC">ALA-LC</a>: <i>Muḥāfaẓat al-Lādhiqīyah</i>) is one of the fourteen <a href="/wiki/Governorates_of_Syria" title="Governorates of Syria">governorates</a> (provinces) of <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>. It is situated in western Syria, bordering <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. Its reported area varies in different sources from 2,297 km²<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to 2,437 km².<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The governorate has a population of 991,000 (2010 estimate). Members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alawite" title="Alawite">Alawite</a> sect form a majority in the governorate, although <a href="/wiki/Armenians" title="Armenians">Armenians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Turkmen" title="Syrian Turkmen">Turkmen</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunni" title="Sunni">Sunni</a> <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a> form the majorities in the <a href="/wiki/Kessab" title="Kessab">Kessab</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jabal_Turkman" title="Jabal Turkman">Jabal Turkman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jabal_al-Akrad" title="Jabal al-Akrad">Jabal al-Akrad</a> regions respectively. The capital of Latakia had, by 2010 estimates, 400,000 inhabitants, 50% of whom were Alawites, 30% were Sunni, and 20% Christian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latakia_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The governorate was historically part of the <a href="/wiki/Alawite_State" title="Alawite State">Alawite State</a>, which existed from 1920–1936. The city of Latakia was the capital of the state.</p>
<p>The region has been relatively peaceful since the beginning of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syrian_civil_war" title="Syrian civil war">Syrian civil war</a> in 2011. However, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syrian_rebels" title="Syrian rebels">Syrian rebels</a> have had a consistent presence in the northeastern mountains.</p>

<p>The governorate is divided into four <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Syria" title="Districts of Syria">districts</a> (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manatiq" title="Manatiq">manatiq</a></i>):</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Al-Haffah_District" title="Al-Haffah District">Al-Haffah</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jableh_District" title="Jableh District">Jableh</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Latakia_District" title="Latakia District">Latakia</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Qardaha_District" title="Qardaha District">Qardaha</a></li>
</ul>
<p>These are further divided into 22 sub-districts (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nahiya" title="Nahiya">nawahi</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latakia_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>East of the coastal plains, the governorate is covered by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/An-Nusayriyah_Mountains" title="An-Nusayriyah Mountains">An-Nusayriyah Mountains</a>.</p>
<p>Latakia is traversed (twice) by the northeasterly line of equal latitude and longitude.</p>
