<p>The <b>Latvian Olympic Committee</b> (<a href="/wiki/Latvian_language" title="Latvian language">Latvian</a>: <span lang="lv" xml:lang="lv"><i>Latvijas Olimpiskā Komiteja</i></span> or LOK) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-profit_organization" title="Non-profit organization">non-profit organization</a> that is the <a href="/wiki/National_Olympic_Committee" title="National Olympic Committee">National Olympic Committee</a> for <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>. It was created in 1922 and recognised by the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> in 1923. Its headquarters are in <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latvian_Olympic_Committee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Committee was created on 23 April 1922. In 1923 it received a notice from <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> that Latvia could take part in the next <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Occupation_of_the_Baltic_states" title="Occupation of the Baltic states">occupation of Latvia</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> in 1940, its activity was suspended. During this time, Latvians competed as part of <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union_at_the_Olympics" title="Soviet Union at the Olympics">USSR delegation</a>.</p>
<p>The committee was reformed on 17 September 1988 and recognised by the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">IOC</a> in 1991.</p>
