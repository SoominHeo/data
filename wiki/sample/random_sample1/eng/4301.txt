<p><b>Latina</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[laˈtiːna]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:It-Latina.ogg" title="File:It-Latina.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/40/It-Latina.ogg" title="It-Latina.ogg">listen</a>)</small>) is the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Latina" title="Province of Latina">province of Latina</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lazio" title="Lazio">Lazio</a> region, in central Italy. As of 2011<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Latina,_Lazio&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the city has 115,895 inhabitants and is thus the second-largest city of the region, after the national capital <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>. It was founded in 1932 under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_fascism" title="Italian fascism">fascist administration</a>, as <i><b>Littoria</b></i>, when the area surrounding it, which had been a swamp since antiquity, was drained.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latina,_Lazio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Inaugurazione_Littoria_001.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="661" data-file-width="898" height="184" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Inaugurazione_Littoria_001.jpg/250px-Inaugurazione_Littoria_001.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Inaugurazione_Littoria_001.jpg/375px-Inaugurazione_Littoria_001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Inaugurazione_Littoria_001.jpg/500px-Inaugurazione_Littoria_001.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Inaugurazione_Littoria_001.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The inauguration of Littoria in 1932.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Latina was founded by <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Benito Mussolini</a> on 30 June 1932 as <i><b>Littoria</b></i>, named for the <a href="/wiki/Fasces#Antiquity" title="Fasces">fascio littorio</a>. The city was inaugurated on 18 December of the same year. Littoria was populated with settlers coming mainly from <a href="/wiki/Friuli" title="Friuli">Friuli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Veneto" title="Veneto">Veneto</a>, who formed the so-called Venetian-Pontine community (today surviving only in some peripheral boroughs). The edifices and the monuments, mainly in <a href="/wiki/Rationalism_(architecture)" title="Rationalism (architecture)">rationalist</a> style, were designed by famous architects and artists such as <a href="/wiki/Marcello_Piacentini" title="Marcello Piacentini">Marcello Piacentini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angiolo_Mazzoni" title="Angiolo Mazzoni">Angiolo Mazzoni</a> and Duilio Cambellotti.</p>
<p>In 1934 it became a provincial capital and, after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, renamed <i><b>Latina</b></i> in 1946. With the arrival of other people mostly from Lazio itself, the original Venetian-like dialect was increasingly substituted by a form of <a href="/wiki/Romanesco_dialect" title="Romanesco dialect">Romanesco dialect</a>.</p>
<p>The city coat of arms is a blue shield with a stylized drawing of the City Hall Clock Tower in the middle, standing on a field of green, and flanked by two stalks of <a href="/wiki/Wheat" title="Wheat">wheat</a> tied at the base with a red ribbon engraved with the motto "<i>LATINA OLIM PALUS</i>" ("Latina, once a swamp") in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>. The shield is surmounted by a <a href="/wiki/Mural_crown" title="Mural crown">mural crown</a>. The arms combine the three colors of the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Italy" title="Flag of Italy">Flag of Italy</a> (red, white, and green) with the "<i>Azzurro Savoia</i>" (the blue of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Savoy" title="House of Savoy">House of Savoy</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latina,_Lazio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city has some <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_industry" title="Pharmaceutical industry">pharmaceutical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_industry" title="Chemical industry">chemical industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheese" title="Cheese">cheese</a> production and has a strong service sector. Latina is also an important centre for agriculture (vegetables, flowers, sugar, fruit, cheese and derivates).</p>
<p>The former <a href="/wiki/Latina_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Latina Nuclear Power Plant">Latina nuclear power plant</a> is now shutdown and undergoing decommissioning.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Frazioni"><i>Frazioni</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latina,_Lazio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Frazioni">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Frazione" title="Frazione">frazioni</a> of Latina are: Latina Lido, Latina Scalo, Borgo Bainsizza, Borgo Carso, Borgo Faiti, Borgo Grappa, Borgo Isonzo, Borgo Montello, Borgo Piave, Borgo Podgora, Borgo Sabotino, Borgo Santa Maria, <a href="/wiki/Borgo_San_Michele" title="Borgo San Michele">Borgo San Michele</a>, Chiesuola, and Tor Tre Ponti.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latina,_Lazio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Latina is located in the Province of Latina, part of the southern section of the region of <a href="/wiki/Lazio" title="Lazio">Lazio</a>. The city is about 62 kilometres (39 miles) south of Rome The municipality borders on <a href="/wiki/Aprilia,_Lazio" title="Aprilia, Lazio">Aprilia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cisterna_di_Latina" title="Cisterna di Latina">Cisterna di Latina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nettuno" title="Nettuno">Nettuno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pontinia" title="Pontinia">Pontinia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sabaudia" title="Sabaudia">Sabaudia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sermoneta" title="Sermoneta">Sermoneta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sezze" title="Sezze">Sezze</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latina,_Lazio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Latina has a <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Csa</i>) like most of southern Italy.</p>
