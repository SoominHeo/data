<p>The <b>Almighty Latin King and Queen Nation</b> (ALKQN, ALKN, LKN) is a large <a href="/wiki/Hispanic" title="Hispanic">Hispanic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Street_gang" title="Street gang">street gang</a> in the United States of America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gangwar2004_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-gangwar2004-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-history1-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Info1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Info1-4">[4]</a></sup> Its roots date to 1943 in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago, Illinois</a>, where it developed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a> community, which had expanded during a period of increased migration to the mainland, in modern times, having great influence in Ecuador, Peru, Venezuela, and regions in Brazil.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Latin_Kings">Latin Kings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Latin Kings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Latin King street gang was formed in Chicago in 1943 and consisted of Chicano and Mexican males. The gang has some Cuban, Central American, and Dominican members. In Spain, the membership consisted of Latin American and Caribbean immigrants. In Brazil, members consist of Indigenous, Afro-Brazilian, and some White and Asian-Brazilian members along with some Caribbean immigrant membership as well.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subdivisions_and_size">Subdivisions and size</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Subdivisions and size">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Founded with the philosophy of "overcoming racial prejudice" and creating an organization of "Kings" and "Queens," the Latin Kings evolved into a criminal enterprise operating throughout the United States under two umbrella factions—Motherland, also known as KMC (King Manifesto and Constitution), and Bloodline (New York City). All members of the gang identify as Latin Kings. Individuals of any nationality may be accepted as members.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><b>Motherland faction:</b></p>
<p>Latin Kings associating with the Motherland faction also identify as "Almighty Latin King Nation (ALKN)," and make up more than 160 structured chapters operating in 158 cities in 31 states. The membership of those Latin Kings in Chicago is estimated to be 20,000 to 35,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pop_size_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-pop_size-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Bloodline faction:</b></p>
<p>The Bloodline Manifesto was founded by <a href="/wiki/Luis_Felipe_(murderer)" title="Luis Felipe (murderer)">Luis Felipe</a> AKA "King Blood" in 1986 in the New York State Collins correctional Facility. Latin Kings associating with New York State Bloodline Chapter also identify as the "Almighty Latin King and Queen Nation (ALKQN)." Membership is estimated to be as many as 7,500, divided among several dozen Tribes operating in 15 cities in 5 states. New York State Bloodline Latin Kings share a common culture and structure with KMC and respect them as the Motherland, but not all chapters report to the Chicago leadership hierarchy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Organizational_structure">Organizational structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Organizational structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Latin Kings have a hierarchical organizational structure: they have numerous "chapters" or "tribes" across the country,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> which adhere to a regional, state, and a national system. Officers (Inca, Cacique and Enforcer) are supported by a "Crown Council" of seven members. The Council sets rules and regulations and holds disciplinary hearings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gangwar2004_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-gangwar2004-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The hierarchy rises to regional officers and ultimately to 2 supreme regional incas based in Chicago. The head (or heads) of the entire criminal organization are known as 'coronas' (crowns in English). One retired detective said in 2004: "When you compare them to other street gangs like the Bloods and the <a href="/wiki/Crips" title="Crips">Crips</a>, none compare to the organization of the Latin Kings."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gangwar2004_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-gangwar2004-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Discipline">Discipline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Discipline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>When any member believes that another member has violated a regulation, they begin the disciplinary process by submitting a Procedures for Violation Form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This form includes a variety of information about the allegation including the violation, the version of the accused, and members present or other witnesses. If the member is found to be guilty of the violation at their Crown hearing, they may be subject to a range of penalties depending on the severity of the offense:</p>
<p><i>Non-Corporal Punishments</i></p>
<ul>
<li>Probation - for a period ranging between two weeks and two months, can be imposed in addition to other punishments</li>
<li>Fines (Multas) - may also be used as a way to recoup the expense of gang property when it is destroyed or lost by a member</li>
<li>Stripping - the member is stripped of their rank and titles, unlikely to ever rise to their previous rank again</li>
<li>Suspension - the member is stripped of all offices and duties, is not permitted to wear their colors, and is addressed as a novice</li>
<li>Community service - particular assignments made on a case-by-case basis</li>
</ul>
<p><i>Corporal Punishments</i></p>
<ul>
<li>B.O.S. (Beat on sight) - beating of undetermined length of time</li>
<li>Three minute physical - three-minute beating by at least three members</li>
<li>Five minute physical - five-minute beating by five members</li>
<li>T.O.S. (Terminate on sight) - death</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Markings">Markings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Markings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Latin King colors are Black , Red , Gold. Gang markings consist of a 5 or 3-point "sacred crown," writings of LK, ALK, ALKN, ALKQN abbreviations (or the whole words); and drawings of the Lion and/or the King Master.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Latin King symbolism is usually accompanied with the name and number of the Tribe, region or city of the gang. The Latin Kings are members of the <a href="/wiki/People_Nation" title="People Nation">People Nation</a> alliance of gangs, and are therefore opposed to the rival <a href="/wiki/Folk_Nation" title="Folk Nation">Folk Nation</a> gang alliance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Florida_Department_of_Corrections_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Florida_Department_of_Corrections-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most Kings wear black/and or gold. The teams Kings represent are usually Chicago teams; such as the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bears" title="Chicago Bears">Chicago Bears</a>. but also the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Steelers" title="Pittsburgh Steelers">Pittsburgh Steelers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Penguins" title="Pittsburgh Penguins">Pittsburgh Penguins</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans_Saints" title="New Orleans Saints">New Orleans Saints</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Kings" title="Los Angeles Kings">Los Angeles Kings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boston_Bruins" title="Boston Bruins">Boston Bruins</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".22Kingism.22_ideology">"Kingism" ideology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title='Edit section: "Kingism" ideology'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to L.A.Kaufman's in the February 2015 issue of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Magazine" title="New York Magazine">New York Magazine</a></i>,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"What also made the Kings different was their unique mixture of intense discipline, revolutionary politics and a homemade religion called "Kingism"—adding idealism and a bootcamp rigor to the usual gang camaraderie—a potent mixture for troubled ghetto kids whose lives lacked structure and hope".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_York_Magazine_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_York_Magazine-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Kingism as an ideology is a unique blend of tribal gang rhetoric and religious mysticisms. As one member put it, the Manifesto is "considered our Bible," and reading it is to go "from the darkness to the light."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kingism_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-kingism-10">[10]</a></sup> This can readily be seen in the following passage from the King's manifesto explaining the significance of the salute, which readily combines organizational fealty with Judeo-Christian theism and some variety of nature worship:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>A fist upon our heart. It means "I DIE FOR YOU" for you are flesh of my flesh, blood of my blood, son of my mother who is the universal nature and follower of Yahve, who is the Almighty King of Kings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kingism_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-kingism-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Latin Kings operate under strict codes and guidelines that are conveyed in a lengthy constitution, and they follow the teachings of the King Manifesto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-google1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-google1-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> According to the Manifesto there are three stages, or cycles of Nation life that constitute Kingism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-google2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-google2-13">[13]</a></sup> They are:</p>
<ol>
<li><b>The Primitive Stage</b>: "That stage in life where the King warrior acts on impulse, executing his action without giving them the serious thought that they demand. A stage of immaturity where the King warrior's time is spend gang banging, getting high, and being recognized as big and bad."</li>
<li><b>The Conservative Stage</b>: "At this level the King warrior becomes tired of the primitive stage. He no longer wishes to participate in the senseless routine of gang fighting, hanging on the corner or being recognized as big and bad. Most often at this level the King warrior gets married and retires. It is inappropriate to call this stage maturity stage due to the fact that the King warrior at this time does not really become mature in the sense of maturity. Instead he becomes mummified or reaches a level of accepting life as it has been taught to him by the existing system that exploits all people of color-dehumanizes them and maintains them under the economic and social yoke of slavery."</li>
<li><b>The New King Stage</b>: "The stage of awareness and decision. The new King recognizes that the time for revolution is at hand. Revolution of the mind! The revolution of knowledge! A revolution that will bring freedom to the enslaved, to all Third World people as we together sing and praise with joy what time it is-it is Nation time!... For him there are no horizons between races, sexes and senseless labels. for him everything has meaning, human life is placed above materialistic values... When a man become a new King the will of the Nation becomes his will, for to be at variance with the Nation is one thing that cannot endure. The Almighty Latin King Nation requires wholehearted and complete devotion."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
</ol>
<p>According to the Manifesto, "The New King is the end product of complete awareness, perceiving three-hundred and sixty degrees of enlightenment; his observations are free and independent; his thoughts are not clouded by any form of prejudice. The New King no longer views the rival warrior as the cause of his ills; instead, he fights against the Anti-King System (social injustices and inequality), a system which seeks to deny and oppress his people: the Oppressed <a href="/wiki/Third_World" title="Third World">Third World</a> Peoples.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-google2_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-google2-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="King_Motherland_Chicago_Latin_Kings">King Motherland Chicago Latin Kings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: King Motherland Chicago Latin Kings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ALKQN_5_SWEATER_PATCH.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="312" data-file-width="240" height="312" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8b/ALKQN_5_SWEATER_PATCH.jpg" width="240"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ALKQN_5_SWEATER_PATCH.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Latin Kings Sweater Patch</div>

</div>
<p>The Chicago faction of the Latin Kings is recognized as the largest Hispanic street gang, and one of the largest Chicago-based street gangs, in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Unlike <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mara_Salvatrucha" title="Mara Salvatrucha">MS-13</a> and <a href="/wiki/18th_Street_gang" title="18th Street gang">18th Street gang</a>—whose great portion of gang membership exists in Central and South America—the Latin Kings have a heavier presence within the United States.</p>
<p>The gang has over 25,000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> members in the city of Chicago alone and have organized chapters in over 41 states and several Latin American and European countries, including: Mexico, Spain, Dominican Republic, Cuba, Canada, Italy, Puerto Rico, Portugal, Brazil, Ecuador, Peru, United Kingdom and others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The Latin Kings are mostly of Latino descent, with some Black, White, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_American" title="Asian American">Asian</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arab_American" title="Arab American">Middle Eastern</a> members as well.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bloodline_Latin_Kings">Bloodline Latin Kings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bloodline Latin Kings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In New York City, the Latin Kings are organized into individual "Tribes" or "Castles" and In some cases use the names of ancient "Inca" Indians such as <a href="/wiki/Flushing,_Queens" title="Flushing, Queens">Flushing, Queens</a>'s "Maya Tribe" and Long Island's "Toltec tribe" or local names such as The "<a href="/wiki/Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="Flatbush, Brooklyn">Flatbush, Brooklyn</a> Sun Tribe The "<a href="/wiki/Sunset_Park,_Brooklyn" title="Sunset Park, Brooklyn">Sunset Park</a> Tribe" and The "<a href="/wiki/Borough_Park,_Brooklyn" title="Borough Park, Brooklyn">Borough Park</a> Homicide Squad". Other Boroughs such as Queens include names such as "Jamaica Wolf Pack Tribe", "Crown City Tribe", and "Lef-Rak City Tribe". Other names can consist of Divisions such as D-1, D-2, and so on, all located in Long Island's Toltec tribe, which extends across the whole island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership">Leadership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Leadership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1986, to avoid imprisonment for his criminal activities in Chicago, <a href="/wiki/Luis_Felipe_(murderer)" title="Luis Felipe (murderer)">Luis Felipe</a> (a.k.a. King Blood) fled to New York and started his own chapter of the Latin Kings known as the Bloodline. He designated himself as Inca and Supreme Crown of the state of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. Soon after arriving in New York Felipe was arrested and convicted of murdering his girlfriend. In 1995 Antonio Fernandez (a.k.a. <a href="/wiki/King_Tone" title="King Tone">King Tone</a>) was designated Inca and Supreme Crown of New York State and New Jersey, and the ALKQN once again began a transformation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Know_Gangs_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Know_Gangs-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Almighty_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Almighty-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Street_Gang:_1986.E2.80.931995">Street Gang: 1986–1995</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Street Gang: 1986–1995">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1986 to the internal power struggle that erupted in 1994, the ALKQN solidified its role as a gang through crimes such as murder, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racketeering" title="Racketeering">racketeering</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Racketeer_Influenced_and_Corrupt_Organizations_Act" title="Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act">RICO Act</a> charges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Almighty_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Almighty-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1991, Felipe was returned to prison after a short release for parole violations stemming from the receipt of stolen goods. However, Felipe continued to guide the ALKQN members, who now numbered about 2,000, both incarcerated and free. In 1994, with the rapid growth of the Latin Kings, an internal power struggle erupted and violence within the Kings ensued. Between June 1993 and February 1994, seven Latin Kings were murdered. Following the outbreaks of internal gang violence, Luis Felipe and 19 others were charged with murder and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racketeering" title="Racketeering">racketeering</a>; the indictments ended in 1995 with 39 Latin Kings and 1 Latin Queen indicted under the RICO Act.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Almighty_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-Almighty-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The details of the charges against Felipe were later revealed: Felipe was charged with ordering the killing of William (Lil Man) Cartagena. Cartegena was taken to an abandoned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bronx" title="Bronx">Bronx</a> apartment where he was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strangled" title="Strangled">strangled</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Decapitated" title="Decapitated">decapitated</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mutilated" title="Mutilated">mutilated</a> and his corpse set on fire. Although Felipe was in prison, the government later alleged he had ordered a TOS ("Terminate On Sight") to all Latin Kings for the murder of Cartagena. This letter and many others were how Felipe was initially linked to three murders on the streets of New York; testimony from former Kings was used as further evidence of the orders. The letters had been copied and stored by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Department_of_Corrections_and_Community_Supervision" title="New York State Department of Corrections and Community Supervision">NY Department of Corrections</a>, who were not aware of the significance of the letters until a federal task force was formed that linked homicide investigators from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NYPD" title="NYPD">NYPD</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FBI" title="FBI">FBI agents</a>, and DOC investigators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leader_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leader-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reformation:_1996.E2.80.931999">Reformation: 1996–1999</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Reformation: 1996–1999">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1999, following the trial of Luis Felipe,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes2_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes2-24">[24]</a></sup> Antonio Fernandez, who was recently blessed as the Inca and Supreme Crown of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_State" title="New York State">New York State</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> kneeled with other Latin Kings in front of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_district_court" title="United States district court">Federal District Court in Manhattan</a> and is quoted as stating: <i>It's time for a fresh start... Now they can't hold our past against us.</i> 1999 is believed to be the beginning of the ALKQN's transformation from a street gang to a "street organization."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Latin Kings and Queens began appearing en masse at political demonstrations in support of the Latino community. To further its transformation and efforts to legitimize, the organization began to hold its monthly meetings (universals) at St. Mary's Episcopal Church in <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">West Harlem</a>. At this time, the membership of the Latin Kings is believed to have swelled to 3,000 incarcerated and 4,000 free. The monthly universals drew attendance of 500-600 regularly. Internal changes to the organization began to take place as Fernandez amended the ALKQN manifesto to include parliamentary elections and new procedures for handling inter-organizational grievances and removing death as a possible punishment, replacing it with "vanishing", the act of being banished from the movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Almighty_22-3"><a href="#cite_note-Almighty-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the ALKQN, 1997 began with Felipe being sentenced to the harshest penalty passed down since World War II. Felipe was sentenced to 250 years in prison, the first 45 to be spent in <a href="/wiki/Solitary_confinement" title="Solitary confinement">solitary confinement</a>. He was a close friend of criminal (YACS) member Pavle Stanimirovic writes about it as he witnessed this brutality in Solitary confinement. The other 39 members were sentenced to an average of 20 years in prison for their roles in the crimes. The year would bring further legal troubles as Fernandez and 31 others are arrested in a raid in the <a href="/wiki/Lower_East_Side" title="Lower East Side">Lower East Side</a> and charged with disorderly conduct. The Special Commissioner of Investigation for Schools soon after charges the ALKQN with infiltrating the school system; a school security guard with five years of service was dismissed on charges of unprofessional conduct for his association with the Latin Kings. The year came to a close with Fernandez being arrested in December by the FBI for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Domestic_abuse" title="Domestic abuse">domestic abuse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Almighty_22-4"><a href="#cite_note-Almighty-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The pending charges against Fernandez were dropped in early 1998. Following the release of Fernandez, a joint operation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FBI" title="FBI">FBI</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department" title="New York City Police Department">New York City Police Department</a> (NYPD), <a href="/wiki/Immigration_and_Naturalization_Service" title="Immigration and Naturalization Service">Immigration and Naturalization Service</a> (INS), <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Police" title="New York State Police">New York State Police</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Drug_Enforcement_Administration" title="Drug Enforcement Administration">Drug Enforcement Administration</a> (DEA) comes to a close with the arrests of 92 suspected ALKQN members. The Latin King leadership insists over half of those arrested are not members. The operation, dubbed Operation Crown, cost the city over one million dollars and took 19 months to complete.</p>
<p>Fernandez was released after four days on $350,000 bail, which was paid for by contributions from community members must mandatory pay their dues collected every week that goes into the "Caja" Box that the treasurer would be in charge of collecting .Over half of the arrested were charged with <a href="/wiki/Misdemeanor" title="Misdemeanor">misdemeanors</a>, other were charged with weapons possession and drug trafficking. Fernandez was eventually permitted, though on house arrest, to attend monthly universal meetings. It was during his time on <a href="/wiki/House_arrest" title="House arrest">house arrest</a> that the Latin Queens underwent a shake up in leadership, dismissing many of the leaders in order to bring in more politically focused members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Almighty_22-5"><a href="#cite_note-Almighty-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Latin Kings during this period begin to gain legitimacy. First, <a href="/wiki/Lolita_Lebr%C3%B3n" title="Lolita Lebrón">Lolita Lebrón</a>, who was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_Nationalist_Party" title="Puerto Rican Nationalist Party">Puerto Rican Nationalist Party</a>, appointed the New York State ALKQN to protect her during a demonstration in front of the United Nations. Following the UN demonstration, Rafael Cancel-Miranda, a Puerto Rican nacionalista who spent 25 years in federal prison, attended a monthly universal. Before years' end, Adelfa Vera, Puerto Rican activist, attended a monthly universal and was given sacred ALKQN beads by the present leadership. Adelfa was praised during the meeting and stated "These kids are hope for our liberation struggle. I can die in peace, because we found the continuation."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Almighty_22-6"><a href="#cite_note-Almighty-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1998, Fernandez<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes2_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes2-24">[24]</a></sup> pleaded guilty to conspiracy to sell and distribute heroin. In 1999, he was sentenced to 13 years in prison, which he began serving at <a href="/wiki/United_States_Penitentiary,_Leavenworth" title="United States Penitentiary, Leavenworth">Leavenworth Federal Penitentiary</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a> and was placed in solitary confinement. He was eventually transferred again and placed in general population. He has since been released.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Massachusetts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Operation Dethrone was an investigation by the Western <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> Gang Task Force, consisting of the FBI, the Chicopee Police Department, the Hampden County Sheriff’s Department, the Holyoke Police Department, the Massachusetts State Police, the Springfield Police Department, and the West Springfield Police Department. The first phase of the investigation, went after the Lawrence chapter of the ALKQN, was converted to an Organized Crime Drug Enforcement Task Force/High Intensity Drug Trafficking Area case involving numerous federal, state, and local law enforcement agencies. After the significant disruption of the <a href="/wiki/Lawrence,_Massachusetts" title="Lawrence, Massachusetts">Lawrence</a> chapter in February 2004, information provided by cooperating defendants led to the significant disruption of the Springfield chapter in June 2005. 57 ALKQN members have been indicted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sc_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-sc-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Latin_Queens">Latin Queens</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Latin_Kings_(gang)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Latin Queens">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While originally the Latin Kings are thought to be a male organization, it eventually began to absorb women and give them an equal share.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The Latin Queens constituted the female Queen Anubis and Queen Maat of the ALKQN.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cultural1104_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cultural1104-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Latin Queen agenda is composed of self-respect, independence, family support, ethnic identity and self-empowerment. Seeking such goals has attracted a wide variety of females who had been drug addicted, victimized and/or neglected by families, spouses and partners. Sociologists studying the Latin Kings and Queens have observed the different methods in which both groups attempt to "reclaim and regulate" their environments. The Latin Queens are believed to focus more on their private space issues such as home life and protection and nurturing of their bodies, as opposed to the Latin Kings, who are more concerned with loss of public spaces in their own communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cultural1104_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cultural1104-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>The evolution of the ALKQN has been viewed by outside sources as being assisted by the addition and greater role in which Queen Loki and Queen Vailor have played, exposing the ALKQN to a greater range of cross-class supporters than would have been possible prior to their integration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cultural1104_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cultural1104-28">[28]</a></sup> In countries such as <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, Latin Queens are helping to legitimize the ALKQN through integration with government sponsored programs. In <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, the 200 persons including Queen Tragedy and King Zeus and the rest of the Latin Kings and Queens tribe was designated as the Cultural Association of Latin Kings and Queens of Catalonia. The "cultural program" designation was bestowed through government sponsored programs to assist gangs with integration into society and is led by Latin Queen Melody, Erika Jaramillo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Latin09_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Latin09-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
