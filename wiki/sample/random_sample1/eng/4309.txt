<p><b>Lapu-Lapu</b>, officially the <b>City of Lapu-Lapu</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cebuano_language" title="Cebuano language">Cebuano</a>: <span lang="ceb"><i>Dakbayan sa Lapu-Lapu</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">Filipino</a>: <span lang="fil"><i>Lungsod ng Lapu-Lapu</i></span>) and formerly called <b>Opon</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_the_Philippines#Income_classification" title="Cities of the Philippines">1st city income class highly urbanized city</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippine_region" title="Philippine region">region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Central_Visayas" title="Central Visayas">Central Visayas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">Cebu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. It is one of the cities that make up <a href="/wiki/Metro_Cebu" title="Metro Cebu">Metro Cebu</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. It is geographically located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippine_province" title="Philippine province">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">Cebu</a>, administratively independent from the province but grouped under Cebu by the <a class="external text" href="http://psa.gov.ph/" rel="nofollow">Philippine Statistics Authority</a>. According to the 2015 census, it has a population of 408,112.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSO15-07_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-NSO15-07-3">[3]</a></sup> In the 2016 electoral roll, it had 188,815 registered voters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comelec_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-comelec-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city occupies <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mactan_Island" title="Mactan Island">Mactan Island</a>, a few kilometers off the main <a href="/wiki/Cebu#Cebu_Island" title="Cebu">island of Cebu</a>. It also has some of the barangays under its jurisdiction on the <a href="/wiki/Olango_Island_Group" title="Olango Island Group">Olango Island Group</a>. The city is linked to <a href="/wiki/Mandaue" title="Mandaue">Mandaue</a> on mainland Cebu by the <a href="/wiki/Mactan-Mandaue_Bridge" title="Mactan-Mandaue Bridge">Mactan-Mandaue Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_Fernan_Bridge" title="Marcelo Fernan Bridge">Marcelo Fernan Bridge</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mactan-Cebu_International_Airport" title="Mactan-Cebu International Airport">Mactan-Cebu International Airport</a>, the second busiest airport in the Philippines, is located in Lapu-Lapu.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lapu-Lapu,_Philippines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation">

<td class="mbox-image">
<div style="width:52px"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg"><img alt="" data-file-height="399" data-file-width="512" height="39" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" width="50"/></a></div>

<p>In the 16th century Mactan Island was colonized by Spain. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Augustinian" title="Augustinian">Augustinian</a> friars founded the town of <b>Opon</b> in 1730 and it became a city in 1961. It was renamed after <a href="/wiki/Datu" title="Datu">Datu</a> <a href="/wiki/Lapu-Lapu" title="Lapu-Lapu">Lapu-Lapu</a>, the island's chieftain who defeated <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> explorer <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a> in 1521 in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mactan" title="Battle of Mactan">Battle of Mactan</a>, commemorated at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lapu-Lapu_shrine" title="Lapu-Lapu shrine">Lapu-Lapu shrine</a> in Punta Engaño.</p>
<p>On 27 April 1521, Lapu-Lapu, a native chieftain, successfully repulsed the foreign invaders in Mactan. He defeated the Spanish troops and killed their leader, Ferdinand Magellan. The municipality of Opon was founded by the Augustinian missionaries in 1730. It was ceded to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesuits" title="Jesuits">Jesuits</a> in 1737, and later restored to the Augustinians. When the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Revolution" title="Philippine Revolution">Philippine Revolution</a> spread to the Visayas in 1896, the people organized themselves into local revolutionary units.</p>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino-American_War" title="Filipino-American War">Filipino-American War</a>, a military government was established. The continued resistance of the people of Cebu prompted the American government to restore military control over the province on 17 July 1901. In 1905, Opon held its first municipal election and Pascual dela Serna was elected town president.</p>
<p>The presence of oil tanks in Opon made the town an object of Japanese raids a week after the outbreak of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> in December 1941. The enemy planes succeeded in blowing up two of about fourteen oil tanks in Opon. A unit of the <a href="/wiki/Kawaguchi_Detachment" title="Kawaguchi Detachment">Kawaguchi Detachment</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Imperial_Forces" title="Japanese Imperial Forces">Japanese Imperial Forces</a> landed on the east coast of Cebu on 10 April 1942. Later, the resistance movement was organized by <a href="/wiki/James_M._Cushing" title="James M. Cushing">Colonel James M. Cushing</a>, leader of the southern and central units, and Harry Fenton of the northern unit of the Cebu Resistance Movement.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Visayas" title="Battle of the Visayas">Victor II operations</a> of the American Division led by Major General <a href="/wiki/William_Howard_Arnold" title="William Howard Arnold">William Arnold</a> landed in Cebu on 26 March 1945, and subsequently liberated the province.</p>
<p>Congressman Manuel A. Zosa, the representative of the Sixth District of Cebu, sponsored the Bill converting the former municipality of Opon into the present day city of Lapu-Lapu. This was the Republic Act 3134, known as the City Charter of Lapu-Lapu which was signed on 17 June 1961 by Philippine President <a href="/wiki/Carlos_P._Garcia" title="Carlos P. Garcia">Carlos P. Garcia</a>. Lapu-Lapu was inaugurated on 31 December 1961, with Mariano Dimataga, the last municipal mayor, as the first city mayor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a fast growing commercial city, some of its commercial and industrial firms are the General Milling Company, one of the largest in the country; the Cebu Shipyard and Engineering Works, pioneered by Dad Cleland; and the oil companies which resumed their operations after the war. The air flight in the city is served by the Mactan International Airport.</p>
<p>Historically, the city includes the site of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mactan" title="Battle of Mactan">Battle of Mactan</a>. On 1 August 1973, by virtue of Presidential Decree No. 2060, President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinand_E._Marcos" title="Ferdinand E. Marcos">Ferdinand Marcos</a> declared the site of the battle a national shrine; the preservation, restoration and/or reconstruction of which shall be under the supervision and control of the National Historical Commission in collaboration with the Department of Tourism. Mactan is also the birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Leonila_Garcia" title="Leonila Garcia">Leonila Dimataga-Garcia</a>, wife of Carlos P. Garcia, the fourth President of the Republic.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lapu-Lapu,_Philippines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mactan-Cebu_International_Airport" title="Mactan-Cebu International Airport">Mactan-Cebu International Airport</a> is located in Lapu-Lapu which connected to mainland <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">Cebu</a> via <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_Fernan_Bridge" title="Marcelo Fernan Bridge">Marcelo Fernan Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mactan-Mandaue_Bridge" title="Mactan-Mandaue Bridge">Mactan-Mandaue Bridge</a> over the sea separating the <a href="/wiki/Mactan" title="Mactan">Mactan</a> island from the island of <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">Cebu</a>. The airport is the main gateway to <a href="/wiki/Central_Visayas" title="Central Visayas">Central Visayas</a> serving international flights to <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. MCIA is the second busiest airport in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> after <a href="/wiki/Ninoy_Aquino_International_Airport" title="Ninoy Aquino International Airport">Ninoy Aquino International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a>.</p>
