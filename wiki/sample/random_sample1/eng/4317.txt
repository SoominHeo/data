<p><b>Rahmon Nabiyevich Nabiyev</b> (<a href="/wiki/Tajik_language" title="Tajik language">Tajik</a>: <span lang="tg" xml:lang="tg">Раҳмон Набиев</span>, alternative spelling <b>Rakhmon Nabiev</b>; October 5, 1930 – April 11, 1993) served as the <a href="/wiki/First_Secretary_of_the_Communist_Party_of_Tajikistan" title="First Secretary of the Communist Party of Tajikistan">First Secretary of the Communist Party of Tajikistan</a> and twice as the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Tajikistan" title="President of Tajikistan">President of Tajikistan</a>. He partly instigated the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tajik_Civil_War" title="Tajik Civil War">Tajik Civil War</a>. Rising out of the regional <a href="/wiki/Nomenklatura" title="Nomenklatura">nomenklatura</a>, Nabiyev ascended to power in 1982 as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Secretary" title="General Secretary">First Secretary</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Tajikistan" title="Communist Party of Tajikistan">Communist Party of Tajikistan</a>. In 1985 he was ousted in a <a href="/wiki/Political_corruption" title="Political corruption">corruption</a> <a href="/wiki/Scandal" title="Scandal">scandal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unep_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-unep-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After Tajik leaders declared the independence of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Tajikistan" title="Republic of Tajikistan">Republic of Tajikistan</a> in September 1991, Nabiyev orchestrated his way back into power on August 31, only to step down on October 6 as pressure mounted for him to vacate the office during the campaign for the presidential elections. Nabiyev <a href="/wiki/Tajikistani_presidential_election,_1991" title="Tajikistani presidential election, 1991">won the elections</a> and on December 2, 1991 he became the first elected President of Tajikistan.</p>
<p>Disputes concerning the election led to opposition street demonstrations, which developed into a civil war in May 1992. As fighting escalated, Nabiyev's own pro-government <a href="/wiki/Militia" title="Militia">militias</a> staged a <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a> in September 1992. He resigned office from the safety of his home province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sughd_Province" title="Sughd Province">Leninabad</a>. By December 1992 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kulyab" title="Kulyab">Kulyab</a> province's former <a href="/wiki/Apparatchik" title="Apparatchik">apparatchik</a> turned <a href="/wiki/Paramilitary" title="Paramilitary">paramilitary</a>-leader, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emomalii_Rahmon" title="Emomalii Rahmon">Emomalii Rahmon</a>, was in power. The cause of Rahmon Nabiev's death is not clear. Officially, he died of a heart attack, but in other versions of the story, he shot himself, or was killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
