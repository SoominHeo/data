<p><b>Rock in Rio</b> is a recurring music festival originating in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. It later branched into other locations such as <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>.</p>
<p>Six incarnations of the festival were held in Rio de Janeiro, in 1985, 1991, 2001, 2011, 2013 and 2015, seven in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, in 2004, 2006, 2008, 2010, 2012, 2014 and 2016, three in Madrid in 2008, 2010 and 2012, and one in Las Vegas, in 2015. The seventh Brazilian edition will happen from September 15 to 24, 2017. Brazilian entrepreneur and advertiser Roberto Medina was responsible for the inception and organization of the festival, as well as moving the 2004 edition to <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, while controversially keeping the brand "Rock in Rio".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rir13_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-rir13-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2011, Rock in Rio returned to its original location, Rio de Janeiro, with a new line-up of singers and groups.</p>
<p>Rock in Rio is one of the largest music festivals in the world, with 1.5 million people attending the first event, 700,000 attending the second and fourth, about 1.2 million attending the third, and about 350,000 people attending each of the 3 Lisbon events.</p>

<p>The first edition of the festival was held from January 11–20, 1985. <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Benson" title="George Benson">George Benson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rod_Stewart" title="Rod Stewart">Rod Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/AC/DC" title="AC/DC">AC/DC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yes_(band)" title="Yes (band)">Yes</a> were the headliners, each occupying top spot for two nights (Benson, however, ceded it to <a href="/wiki/James_Taylor" title="James Taylor">James Taylor</a> for their second night in the same bill, due to the huge delay Taylor's extended performance had caused to his concert two days before). About 1.4 million people attended the 10-day-long festival.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Rock in Rio in numbers</dt>
</dl>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/City_of_Rock" title="City of Rock">City of Rock</a>, which was built for the festival, covered an area of 250,000 m² (around 2.7 million square feet)</li>
<li>1,600,000 liters of beverages were served, using 4 million plastic cups.</li>
<li>900,000 hamburgers.</li>
<li>500,000 pizza slices.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/McDonald%27s" title="McDonald's">McDonald's</a> sold 58,000 hamburgers in a single day, which was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Record" title="Guinness World Record">Guinness World Record</a> until the fourth edition in 2011, where 79,000 hamburgers were sold by <a href="/wiki/Bob%27s" title="Bob's">Bob's</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rir4_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rir4-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<li>Both <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a> shows were filmed (on January 11 and January 18) and broadcast throughout <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> by <a href="/wiki/Rede_Globo" title="Rede Globo">Globo</a>. Each show was watched by nearly 200 million people in over 60 countries and in front of 350,000–500,000 people for each night setting a world record for the biggest paying audience ever at the time. It was later broadcast in the United States on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> as "<i>Queen: Live in Rio</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a> was the only non-Brazilian act to play just one concert at the festival (on its opening night, headlined by <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>), due to other bookings, while every other international act performed twice. During the song "Revelations", lead singer <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Dickinson" title="Bruce Dickinson">Bruce Dickinson</a> hit himself accidentally with a guitar and cut his eyebrow. Dickinson continued to sing in spite of heavy bleeding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> They performed in front of a crowd of 350,000 people too.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Go-Go%27s" title="The Go-Go's">The Go-Go's</a> broke up after their two Rock in Rio performances, (headlined by <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rod_Stewart" title="Rod Stewart">Rod Stewart</a>) though they would later reunite in the 1990s. The two Rock in Rio shows were the debut and swansong for short-tenured Go-Go's member Paula Jean Brown, who had replaced <a href="/wiki/Jane_Wiedlin" title="Jane Wiedlin">Jane Wiedlin</a>. (Wiedlin would return to the reunited 1990s lineup.)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chris_Frantz" title="Chris Frantz">Chris Frantz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tina_Weymouth" title="Tina Weymouth">Tina Weymouth</a>, at the time <a href="/wiki/Talking_Heads" title="Talking Heads">Talking Heads</a> drummer and bass player, respectively, took part at <a href="/wiki/The_B-52%27s" title="The B-52's">the B-52's</a> concerts. They joined the band from the third song on and played along with them for the remainder of the performance.</li>
<li>The January 20 appearance of the B-52's would turn out to be their last with guitarist <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Wilson_(American_musician)" title="Ricky Wilson (American musician)">Ricky Wilson</a>, who died from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIDS" title="AIDS">AIDS</a> in October of that year as the band was finishing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bouncing_Off_the_Satellites" title="Bouncing Off the Satellites">their next album</a>.</li>
<li>Initially, <a href="/wiki/Def_Leppard" title="Def Leppard">Def Leppard</a> was scheduled to play at the festival. However, they dropped out about two months before the event, due to delays on the recording process of the album <i><a href="/wiki/Hysteria_(Def_Leppard_album)" title="Hysteria (Def Leppard album)">Hysteria</a></i>. They were replaced by <a href="/wiki/Whitesnake" title="Whitesnake">Whitesnake</a>. Eleven days before the date Def Leppard would have played at the festival, drummer <a href="/wiki/Rick_Allen_(drummer)" title="Rick Allen (drummer)">Rick Allen</a> suffered an accident that cost him his left arm.</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rock_in_Rio_3">Rock in Rio 3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_in_Rio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rock in Rio 3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The third <i>Rock in Rio</i> festival took place in 2001 and its seven nights were headlined, respectively, 200.000 audience. by <a href="/wiki/Sting_(musician)" title="Sting (musician)">Sting</a>, <a href="/wiki/R.E.M." title="R.E.M.">R.E.M.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%27N_Sync" title="'N Sync">'N Sync</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neil_Young" title="Neil Young">Neil Young</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a> recorded their set and released it as the live album <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_in_Rio_(album)" title="Rock in Rio (album)">Rock in Rio</a></i>. The profits from the sale of the album were donated to the <a href="/wiki/Clive_Burr" title="Clive Burr">Clive Burr</a> fund, which would help the former drummer pay mounting medical bills for treatment of his <a href="/wiki/Multiple_sclerosis" title="Multiple sclerosis">multiple sclerosis</a>.</p>
<p>Another notable appearance at <i>Rock in Rio 3</i> was that of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> hard rock band <a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a>, with a new line-up featuring guitarist <a href="/wiki/Buckethead" title="Buckethead">Buckethead</a>, original member and lead singer <a href="/wiki/Axl_Rose" title="Axl Rose">Axl Rose</a> (vocals), and longtime member <a href="/wiki/Dizzy_Reed" title="Dizzy Reed">Dizzy Reed</a> (keyboards). <a href="/wiki/Carlinhos_Brown" title="Carlinhos Brown">Carlinhos Brown</a>, the opening act on the day Guns N' Roses performed, was attacked by water bottles throughout his whole performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Bassist <a href="/wiki/Nick_Oliveri" title="Nick Oliveri">Nick Oliveri</a> of <a href="/wiki/Queens_of_the_Stone_Age" title="Queens of the Stone Age">Queens of the Stone Age</a> performed in the nude for part of their set and was arrested for indecent exposure after the concert, being released soon after.</p>

<p>The fourth edition of Rock in Rio, back to its origins, was held on September 23, 24, 25, 29 and 30, and October 1 and 2, 2011, at an area (<a class="extiw" href="https://pt.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parque_Ol%C3%ADmpico_Cidade_do_Rock" title="pt:Parque Olímpico Cidade do Rock">pt</a>) to be built next to the old <a href="/wiki/City_of_Rock" title="City of Rock">City of Rock</a> – which is currently the site of the future <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Village" title="Olympic Village">Olympic Village</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a>.</p>
<p>Three different stages were employed, with the headlining concerts at the Palco Mundo (World Stage), the secondary ones in Palco Sunset (Sunset Stage), and DJs playing at a specialized stage for electronic music. The closing acts, <a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a> and <a href="/wiki/System_of_a_Down" title="System of a Down">System of a Down</a>, entered following a poll on the festival's website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Maroon_5" title="Maroon 5">Maroon 5</a> was a last hour addition, following <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> leaving for personal reasons.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lisboa.2C_Portugal.2C_and_Madrid.2C_Spain">Lisboa, Portugal, and Madrid, Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_in_Rio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Lisboa, Portugal, and Madrid, Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rock_in_Rio_Lisboa">Rock in Rio Lisboa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_in_Rio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Rock in Rio Lisboa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the huge success of Rock in Rio 3 in Brazil, Roberta Medina decided to organize a festival of the same stature in Lisbon. The decision to maintain the name Rock in Rio was controversial, and detractors of the idea in Brazil started calling it <i>Rock in Rio Tejo</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> after the <a href="/wiki/Tagus" title="Tagus">Tagus</a> river (rio Tejo, in Portuguese) which runs through the Portuguese capital.</p>
<p>The first edition of Rock in Rio Lisboa, as the festival was officially called, took place in 2004. Although the festival had a slight change of name, it kept the same structure as the Brazilian editions. An entire <i>City of Rock</i>, with an area of over 260,000 audience was erected at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parque_Bela_Vista" title="Parque Bela Vista">Bela Vista park</a>, with a large centre stage and several tents where different artists would perform, simultaneously.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Las_Vegas">Las Vegas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_in_Rio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Las Vegas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rock_in_Rio_USA">Rock in Rio USA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_in_Rio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Rock in Rio USA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Rock_in_Rio_USA" title="Rock in Rio USA">Rock in Rio USA</a>" was the first North American edition of the festival, being held in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> on May 8, 9, 15 and 16, 2015. The festival took place on two weekends, oriented towards rock and pop music. Rock Weekend took place on May 8 and 9, and Pop Weekend took place on May 15 and 16. It took place in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_of_Rock_(Las_Vegas)" title="City of Rock (Las Vegas)">City of Rock (Las Vegas)</a>, located north of the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Strip" title="Las Vegas Strip">Las Vegas Strip</a>.</p>
