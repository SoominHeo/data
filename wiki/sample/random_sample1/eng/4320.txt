<p><b>Randall Brenes Moya</b> (born 13 August 1983) is a <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rican</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>, who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> for <a href="/wiki/C.S._Cartagin%C3%A9s" title="C.S. Cartaginés">Cartaginés</a>. He also plays for the <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica_national_football_team" title="Costa Rica national football team">Costa Rica national football team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Brenes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Brenes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nicknamed <i>El Chiqui</i> (meaning Short or Small), due his height when he was at youth levels, he turned professional with <a href="/wiki/C.S._Cartagin%C3%A9s" title="C.S. Cartaginés">Cartaginés</a> before moving to the north of Norway. Making an impressive rookie season scoring 21 goals in only 30 games.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bod.C3.B8.2FGlimt">Bodø/Glimt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Brenes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bodø/Glimt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He joined <a href="/wiki/FK_Bod%C3%B8/Glimt" title="FK Bodø/Glimt">Bodø/Glimt</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> with his compatriot <a href="/wiki/Roy_Miller_(footballer)" title="Roy Miller (footballer)">Roy Miller</a>, who was also a teammate in Cartaginés, in 2005. At this point Bodø/Glimt was part of the Norwegian top division (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Tippeligaen</a>), but got relegated the same year. The team therefore played in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adeccoligaen" title="Adeccoligaen">Adeccoligaen</a> in 2006 where Brenes was top scorer with 13 goals, 6 of them off headers. In 2007, the team gained promotion to Tippeligaen after winning at home and away against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Odd_Grenland" title="Odd Grenland">Odd Grenland</a> from Tippeligaen. Bodø/Glimt had a big positive surprise the following season in Tippeligaen, ending on a fourth place.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kongsvinger">Kongsvinger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Brenes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Kongsvinger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Brenes was loaned at the middle of the season to Adeccoligaen team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KIL_Toppfotball" title="KIL Toppfotball">Kongsvinger</a> to help them remain the at the division, his compatriot Roy Miller, in the meantime, was sold to <a href="/wiki/Rosenborg_BK" title="Rosenborg BK">Rosenborg</a> of <a href="/wiki/Trondheim" title="Trondheim">Trondheim</a>. In 2009, Brenes signed a full-time contract and helped Kongsvinger win promotion to Tippeligaen. But after the season ended, Brenes came to an agreement with the club to terminate his contract, because he wanted to move home to Costa Rica with his family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cartagin.C3.A9s">Cartaginés</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Brenes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cartaginés">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Randall Brenes back for the tournament in Summer 2010 and Winter tournament in the same year shared the scoring with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ever_Alfaro" title="Ever Alfaro">Éver Alfaro</a> and helping the team reach the semifinals. Brenes spent the 2010 and 2011 seasons with Cartaginés and winning two scoring titles, one in the tournament in 2010 winter and the other in the 2011 winter tournament and being the best player declared by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_de_Costa_Rica" title="Primera División de Costa Rica">Primera División de Costa Rica</a> in the winter 2011 and carrying to Cartaginés to fourth in the table, scoring in four minutes in the first half on the first semifinal but losing against the Herediano 3–1 on aggregate.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Khazar_Lankaran">Khazar Lankaran</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Brenes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Khazar Lankaran">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 February 2012 Brenes signed a 2-year long contract with Khazar Lankaran.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On 20 February 2012, made his debut in the season in a 2–1 defeat against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gabala_FC" title="Gabala FC">Gabala</a> and entering the 35th minute change by <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_Semedo" title="António Semedo">António Semedo</a> and receiving a yellow card. On 7 March 2012, plays its first game start against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PFC_Turan_Tovuz" title="PFC Turan Tovuz">Turan</a>, playing 90 minutes. 14 March 2012, plays its first match in the quarterfinals of the Azerbaijan Cup in the 3–2 defeat against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PSC_Inter_Baku" title="PSC Inter Baku">Baku Inter</a>, scoring his first goal for the club and the second goal of the team at minute 57 and playing 90 minutes. On 28 March 2012, played 90 minutes in the second leg of the Azerbaijan Cup against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Inter_Baku" title="FC Inter Baku">Inter Baku</a> scoring his second goal, a 1–1 draw at 76 minutes. On 11 May 2012, income also a starter in the 4–1 victory against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FK_Qaraba%C4%9F" title="FK Qarabağ">Qarabağ</a>, scoring the second goal at 22 minutes and out of change by <a href="/wiki/Stevan_Bates" title="Stevan Bates">Stevan Bates</a> at minute 70. In the 2012–13 season, Brenes played four games for the club, all the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012-13_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2012-13 UEFA Europa League">Europe League</a>. On 5 July 2012, playing 90 minutes against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JK_N%C3%B5mme_Kalju" title="JK Nõmme Kalju">Nõmme Kalju</a> and received a yellow card 53 minutes. On 10 July 2012, plays the second leg entering the 84th minute change by Sadio Tounkara. Then play in both games as a substitute, for the second round of Europa League against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lech_Poznan" title="Lech Poznan">Lech Poznan</a>. On 31 July 2012, Brenes signed his termination of contract due to breaches of the team with the player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cartagin.C3.A9s_2">Cartaginés</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Brenes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cartaginés">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 7 August 2012, Brenes sign a 1-year long contract with Cartaginés.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sandnes_Ulf">Sandnes Ulf</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Brenes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sandnes Ulf">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 4 August 2014, Brenes signed a loan deal with Sandnes Ulf.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brenes_ulf_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-brenes_ulf-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Brenes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He made his debut for <i>the <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica_national_football_team" title="Costa Rica national football team">Ticos</a></i> in 2005 as a substitute against Cuba at the <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="CONCACAF Gold Cup">CONCACAF Gold Cup</a> Finals, immediately making an impact by scoring 2 goals. In January 2011, he returned to play in the selection of <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_La_Volpe" title="Ricardo La Volpe">Ricardo La Volpe</a> in Panama <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Centroamericana" title="2011 Copa Centroamericana">2011 Copa Centroamericana</a>, playing 5 games. On 26 March 2011, Randall Brenes played on the first game of the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Nacional_de_Costa_Rica_(2011)" title="Estadio Nacional de Costa Rica (2011)">Estadio Nacional de Costa Rica</a> against China and be in the history scoring the second goal of Costa Rica. In June 2011, played in two <a href="/wiki/2011_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup">2011 CONCACAF Gold Cup</a> matches against <a href="/wiki/Cuba_national_football_team" title="Cuba national football team">Cuba</a> and <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador_national_football_team" title="El Salvador national football team">El Salvador</a> scoring in stoppage time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In July, Brenes played two match against <a href="/wiki/Colombia_national_football_team" title="Colombia national football team">Colombia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2011 Copa América">2011 Copa América</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_FIFA_World_Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Brenes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2014 FIFA World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of January 2014, he has earned a total of 36 caps, scoring 8 goals and has represented his country in 8 <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="FIFA World Cup qualification">FIFA World Cup qualification</a> matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 20 June 2014, Brenes make his debut at the second round of group stage matches in the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, entering replacing of <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Ruiz" title="Bryan Ruiz">Bryan Ruiz</a> at 81 minutes, playing 9 minutes plus 4 minutes replenishment at the <a href="/wiki/Itaipava_Arena_Pernambuco" title="Itaipava Arena Pernambuco">Itaipava Arena Pernambuco</a>, taking a chance on goal but his shot went wide. In the third round of group stage matches, against <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mineir%C3%A3o" title="Mineirão">Estádio Mineirão</a>, entry from the beginning and came out in the 59th minute by <a href="/wiki/Christian_Bola%C3%B1os" title="Christian Bolaños">Christian Bolaños</a>. On 29 June 2014, plays his third game in the cup against <a href="/wiki/Greece_national_football_team" title="Greece national football team">Greece</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Itaipava_Arena_Pernambuco" title="Itaipava Arena Pernambuco">Itaipava Arena Pernambuco</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_knockout_stage#Round_of_16" title="2014 FIFA World Cup knockout stage">round of 16</a>, entering as a substitute in the 83rd minute, replacing of <a href="/wiki/Christian_Bola%C3%B1os" title="Christian Bolaños">Christian Bolaños</a>, playing all the extra time and make history with the <i>Ticos</i>, winning the game 5-3 on penalties shoot-out after the 1-1 on the 120th minutes and advance to the Quarter-finals.</p>
