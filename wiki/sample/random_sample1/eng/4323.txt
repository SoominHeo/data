<p><b>Lanzarote</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/ secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="'z' in 'Zion'">z</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ɒ/ short 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[lanθaˈɾote, lansaˈɾote]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> island, the easternmost of the autonomous <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, approximately 125 kilometres (78 miles) off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and 1,000 kilometres (621 miles) from the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a>. Covering 845.94 square kilometres (326.62 square miles), it is the fourth-largest of the islands in the archipelago. With 141,938 inhabitants, Lanzarote is the third-most populous island of the Canary Islands, after <a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gran_Canaria" title="Gran Canaria">Gran Canaria</a>. In the centre-west of the island is the <a href="/wiki/Timanfaya_National_Park" title="Timanfaya National Park">Timanfaya National Park</a>, which is one of the main attractions of Lanzarote. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Arrecife" title="Arrecife">Arrecife</a>.</p>
<p>The first recorded name for the island, given by <a href="/wiki/Angelino_Dulcert" title="Angelino Dulcert">Angelino Dulcert</a>, was <b>Insula de Lanzarotus Marocelus</b>, after the Genovese navigator <a href="/wiki/Lancelotto_Malocello" title="Lancelotto Malocello">Lancelotto Malocello</a>, from which the modern name is derived. The island's name in <a href="/wiki/Guanche_language" title="Guanche language">the native language</a> was Tyterogaka or Tytheroygaka, which may mean "one that is all ochre" (referring to the island's predominant colour).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diccionario_.C3.8Dnsuloamaziq-Tyterogaka_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Diccionario_.C3.8Dnsuloamaziq-Tyterogaka-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lanzarote is located 11 kilometres (7 miles) north-east of <a href="/wiki/Fuerteventura" title="Fuerteventura">Fuerteventura</a> and just over 1 kilometre (0.62 miles) from <a href="/wiki/Graciosa,_Canary_Islands" title="Graciosa, Canary Islands">Graciosa</a>. The dimensions of the island are 60 kilometres (37 miles) from north to south and 25 kilometres (16 miles) from west to east. Lanzarote has 213 kilometres (132 miles) of coastline, of which 10 kilometres (6 miles) are sand, 16.5 kilometres (10 miles) are beach, and the remainder is rocky. Its landscape includes the mountain ranges of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Famara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Famara (page does not exist)">Famara</a> (671 metres (2,201 ft))<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in the north and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ajaches&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ajaches (page does not exist)">Ajaches</a> (608 metres or 1,995 feet) to the south. South of the Famara massif is the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=El_Jable&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="El Jable (page does not exist)">El Jable</a> desert which separates Famara and Montañas del Fuego. The highest peak is Peñas del Chache rising to 670 metres (2,200 feet) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_sea_level" title="Above sea level">above sea level</a>. The "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tunnel_of_Atlantis" title="Tunnel of Atlantis">Tunnel of Atlantis</a>," the largest underwater volcanic tunnel in the world, is part of the <a href="/wiki/Cueva_de_los_Verdes" title="Cueva de los Verdes">Cueva de los Verdes</a> <a href="/wiki/Lava_tube" title="Lava tube">lava tube</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Often called 'Island of Eternal Spring' Lanzarote<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> is one of the Canary Islands in which has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropical" title="Subtropical">subtropical</a>-<a href="/wiki/Desert_climate" title="Desert climate">desert climate</a> according to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climatic_classification" title="Köppen climatic classification">Köppen climatic classification</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The small amount of precipitation is mainly concentrated in the winter. Rainfall during summer is a rare phenomenon (there has never been any recorded rainfall in July) and several summers are completely dry without any precipitation. On average the Island receives approximately 16 days of precipitation between December and February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> Sometimes, the hot sirroco wind prevails causing dry and dusty conditions across the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> Average precipitation in June and August is less than 0.5 millimetres (0.020 inches). It closely borders on a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical climate</a>, with winter means of 18 °C (64 °F) and summer means of 25 °C (77 °F).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Lanzarote is the easternmost island of the Canary Islands and has a volcanic origin. It was born through fiery eruptions and has solidified lava streams as well as extravagant rock formations. The island emerged about 15 million years ago as product of the <a href="/wiki/Canary_hotspot" title="Canary hotspot">Canary hotspot</a>. The island, along with others, emerged after the break-up of the African and the American continental plates. The greatest recorded eruptions occurred between 1730 and 1736 in the area now designated <a href="/wiki/Timanfaya_National_Park" title="Timanfaya National Park">Timanfaya National Park</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>As of 2010<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, 139,000 people live on Lanzarote<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-datoslanzarote4078_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-datoslanzarote4078-6">[6]</a></sup> which is an increase of 9.4% from 2006 (127,000).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-datoslanzarote3291_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-datoslanzarote3291-7">[7]</a></sup> The seat of the island government (<i>Cabildo Insular</i>) is in the capital, <a href="/wiki/Arrecife" title="Arrecife">Arrecife</a>, which has a population of 59,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-datoslanzarote4078_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-datoslanzarote4078-6">[6]</a></sup> The majority of the inhabitants (73.9%) are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_people" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a>, with a sizeable number of residents from other European nations, mainly <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> (4.0%), <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">Germans</a> (2.6%) and <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> (2.5%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-datoslanzarotepdf_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-datoslanzarotepdf-8">[8]</a></sup> Other populous groups include immigrants from <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Africa" title="Western Africa">Western Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, which constitute a large proportion of the remaining 15.6% of the population.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Plants">Plants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Plants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are five hundred different kinds of plants on the island of which 17 species are <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a>. These plants have adapted to the relative scarcity of water in the same way as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Succulent" title="Succulent">succulents</a>. They include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canary_Island_date_palm" title="Canary Island date palm">Canary Island date palm</a> (<i>Phoenix canariensis</i>), which is found in damper areas of the north, the Canary Island pine (<i><a href="/wiki/Pinus_canariensis" title="Pinus canariensis">Pinus canariensis</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/Fern" title="Fern">ferns</a>, and wild olive trees (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olea_europaea" title="Olea europaea">Olea europaea</a></i>). <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laurisilva" title="Laurisilva">Laurisilva</a></i> trees which once covered the highest parts of Risco de Famara are rarely found today. After winter rainfall, the vegetation comes to a colourful bloom between February and March.</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Vineyard" title="Vineyard">vineyards</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Ger%C3%ADa" title="La Gería">La Gería</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lanzarote_(DO)" title="Lanzarote (DO)">Lanzarote</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Denominaci%C3%B3n_de_Origen" title="Denominación de Origen">DO</a></i> wine region, are a protected area. Single vines are planted in pits 4–5 metres (13–16 feet) wide and 2–3 metres (6 feet 7 inches–9 feet 10 inches) deep, with small stone walls around each pit. This agricultural technique is designed to harvest rainfall and overnight dew and to protect the plants from the winds.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fungi">Fungi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Fungi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are 180 different species of <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichen-forming fungi</a>. These survive in the suitable areas like rock surfaces, and promote weathering.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animals">Animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Animals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Apart from bats and other mammals which accompanied humans to the island (including the <a href="/wiki/Dromedary" title="Dromedary">dromedary</a> which was used for agriculture and is now a tourist attraction), there are few vertebrate species on Lanzarote. These include birds (such as <a href="/wiki/Falcon" title="Falcon">falcons</a>), and reptiles. Some interesting endemic animals are the <i><a href="/wiki/Gallotia" title="Gallotia">Gallotia</a></i> lizards, and the blind <i><a href="/wiki/Munidopsis_polymorpha" title="Munidopsis polymorpha">Munidopsis polymorpha</a></i> crabs found in the Jameos del Agua lagoon, which was formed by a volcanic eruption. The island is also home to one of two surviving populations of the threatened <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canarian_Egyptian_vulture" title="Canarian Egyptian vulture">Canarian Egyptian vulture</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lanzarote is estimated the first Canary Island to be settled. The <a href="/wiki/Phoenicia" title="Phoenicia">Phoenicians</a> may have settled there around 1100 BC, though no material evidence survives. The <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greek</a> writers and philosophers <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plato" title="Plato">Plato</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plutarch" title="Plutarch">Plutarch</a> described the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garden_of_the_Hesperides" title="Garden of the Hesperides">garden of the Hesperides</a>, a mythic orchard at the far West of the world, which some like to identify with the Canaries.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The first known record came from <a href="/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder" title="Pliny the Elder">Pliny the Elder</a> in the encyclopaedia <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naturalis_Historia" title="Naturalis Historia">Naturalis Historia</a></i> on an expedition to the Canary Islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The names of the islands (then called <a href="/wiki/Fortunate_Isles" title="Fortunate Isles"><i>Insulae Fortunatae</i>, the "Fortunate Isles"</a>) were recorded as <i>Junonia</i> (Fuerteventura), <i>Canaria</i> (<a href="/wiki/Gran_Canaria" title="Gran Canaria">Gran Canaria</a>), <i>Ninguaria</i> (<a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a>), <i>Junonia Major</i> (<a href="/wiki/La_Palma" title="La Palma">La Palma</a>), <i>Pluvialia</i>(<a href="/wiki/El_Hierro" title="El Hierro">El Hierro</a>) and <i>Capraria</i> (<a href="/wiki/La_Gomera" title="La Gomera">La Gomera</a>). Lanzarote and <a href="/wiki/Fuerteventura" title="Fuerteventura">Fuerteventura</a>, the two easternmost Canary Islands, were only mentioned as the archipelago of the "purple islands".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The source is a but unclear about this. maybe someone can fix this. (November 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> poet <a href="/wiki/Lucan" title="Lucan">Lucan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">Greek</a> <a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">astronomer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geographer" title="Geographer">geographer</a> <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy" title="Ptolemy">Ptolemy</a> gave their precise locations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-archaeology9705_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-archaeology9705-12">[12]</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Western_Roman_Empire" title="Fall of the Western Roman Empire">fall of the Western Roman Empire</a>, the Canary islands were ignored until 999 when the <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a> arrived at the island which they dubbed <i>al-Djezir al-Khalida</i> and other names.</p>
<p>In 1336, a ship arrived from <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> under the guidance of <i>Lanzarote da Framqua</i>, alias <a href="/wiki/Lancelotto_Malocello" title="Lancelotto Malocello">Lancelotto Malocello</a>. A fort was later built in the area of Montaña de Guanapay near today's Teguise. Castilian slaving expeditions in 1385 and 1393 seized hundreds of <a href="/wiki/Guanches" title="Guanches">Guanches</a> and sold them in Spain, initiating the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slave_trade" title="Slave trade">slave trade</a> in the islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jean_de_B%C3%A9thencourt" title="Jean de Béthencourt">Jean de Béthencourt</a> arrived in 1402, heading a private expedition under Castilian auspices. Bethencourt first visited the south of Lanzarote at Playas de Papagayo, and within a matter of months the French overran the island, which lacked mountains and gorges to serve as retreats for the small remaining population of Guanches, so many of whom were taken away as slaves, it was said that only 300 men remained.</p>
<p>At the southern end of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Yaiza" title="Yaiza">Yaiza</a>, in the area known as "<i>El Rubicón</i>", the first European settlement in the Canary Islands in 1402, where the conquest of the Archipelago began he was installed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juliosanchezrodriguez.com_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-juliosanchezrodriguez.com-14">[14]</a></sup> In this place the Cathedral of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martial" title="Saint Martial">Saint Martial of Limoges</a>. This cathedral was destroyed by English pirates in the sixteenth century and is currently in Femés, the Hermitage of Saint Martial of Limoges dedicated to this saint. This diocese was moved in 1483 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Palmas_de_Gran_Canaria" title="Las Palmas de Gran Canaria">Las Palmas de Gran Canaria</a> (<a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Canarias" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Canarias">Roman Catholic Diocese of Canarias</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juliosanchezrodriguez.com_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-juliosanchezrodriguez.com-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1404, the Castilians with the support of the King of Castile came and fought the local Guanches, who were further decimated. The islands of <a href="/wiki/Fuerteventura" title="Fuerteventura">Fuerteventura</a> and <a href="/wiki/El_Hierro" title="El Hierro">El Hierro</a> were later similarly conquered. In 1477 a decision by the royal council of Castile confirmed a grant of Lanzarote and Fuerteventura, with the smaller islands of Ferro and Gomera to the Castilian nobles Herrera, who held their fief until the end of the 18th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1585, the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> admiral <a href="/wiki/Murat_Reis_the_Elder" title="Murat Reis the Elder">Murat Reis</a> temporarily seized Lanzarote. In the 17th century, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pirates" title="Pirates">pirates</a> raided the island and took 1,000 inhabitants to <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cueva_de_los_Verdes" title="Cueva de los Verdes">Cueva de los Verdes</a>.</p>
<p>From 1730 to 1736 (for 2,053 days), the island was hit by a series of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volcanic_eruption" title="Volcanic eruption">volcanic eruptions</a>, producing 32 new volcanoes in a stretch of 18 kilometres (11 miles). The minister of Yaiza, Don Andrés Lorenzo Curbelo documented the eruption in detail until 1731. Lava covered a quarter of the island's surface, including the most fertile soil and eleven villages. One hundred smaller volcanoes were located in the area called Montañas del Fuego, the "Mountains of Fire".</p>
<p>In 1768, drought affected the deforested island, and winter rains did not fall. Much of the population was forced to emigrate to <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, including a group which formed a significant addition to the Spanish settlers in Texas at San Antonio de Bexar in 1731. Another volcanic eruption occurred within the range of Tiagua in 1824 which was less violent than the major eruption between 1730 and 1736.</p>
<p>In 1927, Lanzarote, along with Fuerteventura, became part of the province of <a href="/wiki/Las_Palmas" title="Las Palmas">Las Palmas</a>.</p>
<p>Several archaeological expeditions have uncovered the prehistoric settlement at the archaeologic site of El Bebedero in <a href="/wiki/Teguise_(village)" title="Teguise (village)">Teguise (village)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In one of those expeditions, by a team from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Las_Palmas_de_Gran_Canaria" title="University of Las Palmas de Gran Canaria">Universidad de Las Palmas de Gran Canaria</a> and a team from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Zaragoza" title="University of Zaragoza">Universidad de Zaragoza</a>, yielded about 100 Roman potsherds, nine pieces of metal, and one piece of glass. The artefacts were found in strata dated between the first and 4th centuries. The finds show that Romans did trade with the Canaries, though there is no evidence of settlements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-archaeology9705_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-archaeology9705-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Biosphere_Reserve_controversy">Biosphere Reserve controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Biosphere Reserve controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The island has a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biosphere_Reserve" title="Biosphere Reserve">Biosphere Reserve</a> protected site status. According to a report in the <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i>, this status was endangered by a local corruption scandal. Since May 2009, police have arrested the former president of Lanzarote, the former mayor of Arrecife and more than 20 politicians and businessmen in connection with illegal building permits along Lanzarote's coastline. UNESCO has threatened to revoke Lanzarote's Biosphere Reserve status, "(i)f the developments are not respecting local needs and are impacting on the environment".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft-eco_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ft-eco-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indepenent-ft_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-indepenent-ft-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The President of the <a href="/wiki/Cabildo_(council)" title="Cabildo (council)">Cabildo</a> of Lanzarote denied "any threat to Lanzarote's UNESCO status".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-greenslade-controversy_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-greenslade-controversy-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notables">Notables</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Notables">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Famous people who were born or have lived on the island are <a href="/wiki/Rosana_Arbelo" title="Rosana Arbelo">Rosana Arbelo</a>, a singer; <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Manrique" title="César Manrique">César Manrique</a>, an artist; <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Saramago" title="José Saramago">José Saramago</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_people" title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_for_Literature" title="Nobel Prize for Literature">Nobel Prize for Literature</a> winner who died there; <a href="/wiki/Princess_Alexia_of_Greece_and_Denmark" title="Princess Alexia of Greece and Denmark">Princess Alexia of Greece and Denmark</a> and husband, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Morales_Quintana" title="Carlos Morales Quintana">Carlos Morales Quintana</a>; Jean Arriete Prud'homme (Surname later <a href="/wiki/Hispanicization" title="Hispanicization">Hispanicized</a> to Perdomo), a <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a> nobleman who arrived with <a href="/wiki/Jean_de_B%C3%A9thencourt" title="Jean de Béthencourt">Jean de Béthencourt</a> and gave his name to the village of Arrieta.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The most established festival on the island is held each year on 15 September in the village of <a href="/wiki/Mancha_Blanca" title="Mancha Blanca">Mancha Blanca</a> in the municipality of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tinajo" title="Tinajo">Tinajo</a> in honour of <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_Sorrows" title="Our Lady of Sorrows">Our Lady of Sorrows</a> (<i>Virgen de los Dolores</i>), also called Virgin of the Volcanoes (the Patron Saint of Lanzarote). In this pilgrimage people from all over the island, mostly dressed in traditional costumes, participate.</p>
