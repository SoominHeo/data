<p><b>Lanseria International Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association_airport_code" title="International Air Transport Association airport code">IATA</a>: <b>HLA</b>, <a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization_airport_code" title="International Civil Aviation Organization airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>FALA</b>) is a privately owned <a href="/wiki/International_airport" title="International airport">international airport</a> that is situated north of <a href="/wiki/Randburg" title="Randburg">Randburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sandton" title="Sandton">Sandton</a> to the north west of <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>. The airport can handle <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> up to the size of the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_757" title="Boeing 757">Boeing 757-300</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanseria_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lanseria Airport started out as a grass strip airfield in 1972, the brainchild of two <a href="/wiki/Pretoria" title="Pretoria">Pretoria</a> pilots – Fanie Haacke and Abe Sher. The land was originally bought by <a href="/wiki/Krugersdorp" title="Krugersdorp">Krugersdorp</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roodepoort" title="Roodepoort">Roodepoort</a> Municipality together with the Transvaal Peri-Urban Board and contracted to Lanseria Management Company on a 99-year lease since 1972.</p>
<p>The airport was officially opened by the Minister of Transport at the time, Hannes Rall, on 16 August 1974. Soon after its opening, Lanseria Airport hosted the Air Africa '75 (in 1975).</p>
<p>When <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Mandela" title="Nelson Mandela">Nelson Mandela</a> was released from prison in 1990 he was flown to Johannesburg landing at Lanseria Airport.</p>
<p>On 15 November 2012 the airport was sold to a consortium consisting of Harith, an infrastructure development fund management company; the women's empowerment company Nozala; and the Government Employee Pension Fund, through the Public Investment Corporation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 November 2013 the airport opened its new 45-meter-wide 07/25 Runway and also closing the existing 30-meter-wide 06/24 runway. Kulula was the first carrier to land on the new runway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aerodrome_Information">Aerodrome Information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanseria_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aerodrome Information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Runway 07 is equipped with <a href="/wiki/Instrument_landing_system" title="Instrument landing system">ILS</a> CAT I, and despite the name, is directed at 047° East of North.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The single runway has a 1.5% gradient, sloping up towards the South-West end of the runway; despite this gradient, the preferred landing direction is from the South, landing on Runway 07. This is primarily because winds of the Highveld of South Africa are usually Northerly winds, blowing South.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_facilities">Other facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanseria_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Airways_Corporation_(South_Africa)" title="National Airways Corporation (South Africa)">National Airways</a> has its head office building on the airport property.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Various maintenance and avionics companies are situated on the airport including Interjet Maintenance, MPT Maintenance, ExecuJet, Lanseria Jet centre and NAC with various other smaller outfits. The maintenance facilities at Lanseria International Airport provide small to midsize aircraft maintenance mainly focused on corporate aircraft and small regional airliners, up to a Bombardier CRJ700 or similar.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanseria_International_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>9 October 1977 – During Airshow Africa '77 – a <a href="/wiki/Britten-Norman_Trislander" title="Britten-Norman Trislander">Britten-Norman Trislander</a> performed a wing-over but had insufficient altitude to recover and the aircraft impacted the runway, bounced into the air and came to rest some 500m further off to the side of the runway. The flying controls were disabled and main gear detached. One wing engine detached. The crew were not injured but the aircraft was written off.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>On 2 October 1993, an <a href="/wiki/Aermacchi_MB-326" title="Aermacchi MB-326">Impala Mark I</a> (no. 489) of the <a href="/wiki/South_African_Air_Force" title="South African Air Force">SAAF</a> <a href="/wiki/Silver_Falcons" title="Silver Falcons">Silver Falcons</a> aerobatic team crashed after suffering separation of the right wing during a performance at the Lanseria Airshow. The pilot ejected but was killed, as the ejection was initiated outside of the design envelope of the <a href="/wiki/Ejection_seat" title="Ejection seat">ejection seat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
