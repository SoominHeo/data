<p><b>Lancia</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[ˈlantʃa]</a></span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Automotive_industry" title="Automotive industry">automobile manufacturer</a> founded in 1906 by <a href="/wiki/Vincenzo_Lancia" title="Vincenzo Lancia">Vincenzo Lancia</a> as <b>Lancia &amp; C.</b>. It became part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiat" title="Fiat">Fiat Group</a> in 1969; the current company, <b>Lancia Automobiles S.p.A.</b>, was established in 2007.</p>
<p>The company has a strong <a href="/wiki/Rallying" title="Rallying">rally</a> heritage and is noted for using letters of the <a href="/wiki/Greek_alphabet" title="Greek alphabet">Greek alphabet</a> for its model names.</p>
<p>Lancia vehicles are no longer sold outside of Italy, and comprise only the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Ypsilon" title="Lancia Ypsilon">Ypsilon</a> supermini range, as Fiat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEO" title="CEO">CEO</a> <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Marchionne" title="Sergio Marchionne">Sergio Marchionne</a> foreshadowed in January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-repubblica_futuro_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-repubblica_futuro-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foundation_and_early_years">Foundation and early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Foundation and early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lancia &amp; C. Fabbrica Automobili was founded on 29 November 1906 in <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a> by <a href="/wiki/Fiat_S.p.A." title="Fiat S.p.A.">Fiat</a> racing drivers, <a href="/wiki/Vincenzo_Lancia" title="Vincenzo Lancia">Vincenzo Lancia</a> (1881-1937) and his friend, Claudio Fogolin (1872-1945). The first car manufactured by Lancia was the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancia_Alfa-12HP" title="Lancia Alfa-12HP">Tipo 51</a>" or "12 HP" (later called "Alfa"), which remained in production between from 1907 to 1908. It had a small four-cylinder engine with a power output of 28 hp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-classicargarage.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-classicargarage.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1910 Lancia components were exported to the United States where they were assembled and sold as <a href="/wiki/SGV_(automobile)" title="SGV (automobile)">SGV's</a> by the SGV Company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1915, Lancia also manufactured its first truck, the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Jota" title="Lancia Jota">Jota</a> that continued as a dedicated series. In 1937, Vincenzo died of a heart attack and both his wife, Adele Miglietti Lancia, and his son, <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Lancia" title="Gianni Lancia">Gianni Lancia</a>, took over control of the company. They persuaded <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Jano" title="Vittorio Jano">Vittorio Jano</a> to join as an engineer. Jano had already made a name for himself by designing various Alfa Romeo models, including some of its most successful race cars ever such as the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_6C" title="Alfa Romeo 6C">6C</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_P2" title="Alfa Romeo P2">P2</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_P3" title="Alfa Romeo P3">P3</a>.</p>
<p>Lancia is renowned in the automotive world for introducing cars with numerous innovations. These include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancia_Theta-35HP" title="Lancia Theta-35HP">Theta</a> of 1913, which was the first European production car to feature a complete electrical system as standard equipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lancia.com_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-lancia.com-5">[5]</a></sup> Lancia's first car adopting a <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a> chassis – the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Lambda" title="Lancia Lambda">Lambda</a> produced from 1922 to 1931 - featured '<a href="/wiki/Sliding_pillar_suspension" title="Sliding pillar suspension">Sliding Pillar</a>' independent front suspension that incorporated the spring and hydraulic damper into a single unit (a feature that would be employed in subsequent Lancia's, up to the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Appia" title="Lancia Appia">Appia</a> that was replaced in 1963). 1948 saw the first 5 speed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gearbox" title="Gearbox">gearbox</a> to be fitted to a production car (Series 3 Ardea). Lancia premiered the first full-production <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V6 engine</a>, in the 1950 <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Aurelia" title="Lancia Aurelia">Aurelia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ritzsite.nl_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ritzsite.nl-6">[6]</a></sup> after earlier industry-leading experiments with <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">V8</a> and <a href="/wiki/V12_engine" title="V12 engine">V12 engine</a> configurations. It was also the first manufacturer to produce a <a href="/wiki/V4_engine" title="V4 engine">V4 engine</a>. Other innovations involved the use of <a href="/wiki/Independent_suspension" title="Independent suspension">independent suspension</a> in production cars (in an era where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live_axle" title="Live axle">live axles</a> where common practice for both the front and rear axles of a car) and rear transaxles, which were first fitted to the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Aurelia" title="Lancia Aurelia">Aurelia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Flaminia" title="Lancia Flaminia">Flaminia</a> range. This drive for innovation, constant quest for excellence, fixation of quality, complex construction processes and antiqued production machinery meant that all cars essentially had to be hand-made. With little commonality between the various models, the cost of production continued to increase extensively, while demand did not eventually affecting Lancia's viability.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Gianni Lancia, a graduate engineer was president of Lancia from 1947 to 1955. In 1956 the Pesenti family took over control of Lancia with <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Pesenti" title="Carlo Pesenti">Carlo Pesenti</a> (1907–1984) in charge.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1969_to_present">1969 to present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1969 to present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fiat launched a take-over bid in October 1969 which was accepted by Lancia as the company was losing significant sums of money, with losses in 1969 being <span style="white-space: nowrap">GB£</span>20m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> This was not the end of the distinctive Lancia marque, and new models in the 1970s such as the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Stratos" title="Lancia Stratos">Stratos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Gamma" title="Lancia Gamma">Gamma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Beta" title="Lancia Beta">Beta</a> served to prove that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiat" title="Fiat">Fiat</a> wished to preserve the image of the brand it had acquired.</p>
<p>During the 1970s and 1980s, Lancia had great success in <a href="/wiki/Lancia_in_rallying" title="Lancia in rallying">rallying</a>, winning many <a href="/wiki/World_Rally_Championship" title="World Rally Championship">World Rally Championships</a>.</p>
<p>During the 1980s, the company cooperated with <a href="/wiki/Saab_Automobile" title="Saab Automobile">Saab Automobile</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Delta" title="Lancia Delta">Lancia Delta</a> being sold as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saab_600" title="Saab 600">Saab 600</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. The 1985 <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Thema" title="Lancia Thema">Lancia Thema</a> also shared a platform with the <a href="/wiki/Saab_9000" title="Saab 9000">Saab 9000</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiat_Croma" title="Fiat Croma">Fiat Croma</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_164" title="Alfa Romeo 164">Alfa Romeo 164</a>. During the 1990s, all models were closely related to other Fiat models.</p>
<p>Starting from 1 February 2007, Fiat's automotive operations were reorganised.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stampa_automobiles_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-stampa_automobiles-8">[8]</a></sup> Fiat Auto became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiat_Group_Automobiles_S.p.A." title="Fiat Group Automobiles S.p.A.">Fiat Group Automobiles S.p.A.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiat_S.p.A." title="Fiat S.p.A.">Fiat S.p.A.</a>'s branch handling mainstream automotive production. Simultaneously the current company, <b>Lancia Automobiles S.p.A.</b>, was created from the pre-existing brand, and controlled 100% by FGA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stampa_automobiles_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-stampa_automobiles-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2011, Lancia moved in a new direction and added new models manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler</a> and sold under the Lancia badge in many European markets. Conversely, Lancia built models began to be sold in right-hand drive markets under the Chrysler badge. In 2015 Lancia's parent company Fiat Group Automobiles S.p.A. became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FCA_Italy_S.p.A." title="FCA Italy S.p.A.">FCA Italy S.p.A.</a>, reflecting the earlier incorporation of Fiat S.p.A. into <a href="/wiki/Fiat_Chrysler_Automobiles" title="Fiat Chrysler Automobiles">Fiat Chrysler Automobiles</a>.</p>

<dl>
<dt>1907</dt>
</dl>
<p>From 1907 to 1910 Lancia cars didn't bear a true badge, but rather a brass plaque identifying the manufacturer (Lancia &amp; C.) and chassis code; although some models did have a brass Lancia script on the grille.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lanciapress_logo_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-lanciapress_logo-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>1911</dt>
</dl>
<p>The original Lancia logo was designed by Count <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Biscaretti_di_Ruffia" title="Carlo Biscaretti di Ruffia">Carlo Biscaretti di Ruffia</a>. In 1910 Vincenzo Lancia asked Biscaretti di Ruffia to design a badge for the company; the Count submitted six <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watercolour" title="Watercolour">watercolour</a> proposal sketches. Vincenzo Lancia chose a round one, composed by a blue <a href="/wiki/Lance" title="Lance">lance</a> and flag bearing a Lancia script ("Lancia" means "lance" in Italian) in gold, over a four-spoke steering wheel, with a hand <a href="/wiki/Throttle" title="Throttle">throttle</a> detail on the right spoke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lanciapress_logo_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-lanciapress_logo-9">[9]</a></sup> The first car to bear the Lancia logo was the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Gamma" title="Lancia Gamma">Gamma 20 HP</a> in 1911.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lanciapress_logo_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-lanciapress_logo-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>1929</dt>
</dl>
<p>In 1929 the logo acquired its final layout: the previous round badge was superimposed on a blue shield in the shape of a <a href="/wiki/Reuleaux_triangle" title="Reuleaux triangle">Reuleaux triangle</a> (as found in one of Biscaretti di Ruffia's six original proposals).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lanciapress_logo_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-lanciapress_logo-9">[9]</a></sup> Though first applied on the 1929 <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Dikappa" title="Lancia Dikappa">Dikappa</a>, this badge was only used consintently starting with the 1936 <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Aprilia" title="Lancia Aprilia">Aprilia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lanciapress_logo_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-lanciapress_logo-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dt>1957</dt>
</dl>
<p>Beginning with the 1957 <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Flaminia" title="Lancia Flaminia">Flaminia</a>, Lancia cars switched from the traditional vertical split grille to an horizontal, full-width one. The logo was therefore moved inside the grille opening, and changed to a more stylized chromed metal open-work design; shield and steering wheel became chrome frames, the only remaining enameled surface being the blue field of the flag. This new metal logo was used on most models with some exceptions, namely <a href="/wiki/Zagato" title="Zagato">Zagato</a>-bodied <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Fulvia" title="Lancia Fulvia">Lancia Fulvias</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Flavia" title="Lancia Flavia">Flavias</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_2000" title="Lancia 2000">Lancia 2000 Berlina</a> (which reprised the traditional upright grille and the round enameled badge) and the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Stratos" title="Lancia Stratos">Stratos HF</a> (whose ornaments lacked the triangular shield).</p>
<dl>
<dt>1974</dt>
</dl>
<p>In 1974 the badge was redesigned on <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Agnelli" title="Gianni Agnelli">Gianni Agnelli</a>'s request;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lanciapress_logo_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-lanciapress_logo-9">[9]</a></sup> it went back to a modernised silver, white and blue version of the 1929 design. Flag and lance were unified in a single shape and dispensed with the earlier minute detailing, the Lancia letters became all of the same size, and the steering wheel became also outlined in blue and lost the hand throttle detail. This logo debuted on the 1979 <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Delta#First_generation" title="Lancia Delta">Lancia Delta</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lanciapress_logo_9-6"><a href="#cite_note-lanciapress_logo-9">[9]</a></sup> and made its way on the other models as they adopted the split grille introduced by the Delta. Though lightly revised in 2000 with the addition of a chrome shield surround, the 1974 logo was used through four decades, up to 2006.</p>
<dl>
<dt>2007</dt>
</dl>
<p>The current logo, designed by Robilant Associati, was presented at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Geneva_Motor_Show" title="Geneva Motor Show">Geneva Motor Show</a>—a couple of months after the creation of Lancia Automobiles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lanciapress_logo_9-7"><a href="#cite_note-lanciapress_logo-9">[9]</a></sup> While the traditional chrome-framed blue shield has been retained and made three-dimensional, for the first time since 1911 lance and flag are absent; the steering wheel has been stylized into a chromed circle, from which two spikes converge towards the modern Lancia logotype in the centre.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lancia_family">Lancia family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lancia family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Cavalier Giuseppe Lancia</b> (1860 (<a href="/wiki/Cuneo" title="Cuneo">Cuneo</a>) - 1919 (<a href="/wiki/Bordighera" title="Bordighera">Bordighera</a>)) is an Italian businessman and father of <a href="/wiki/Vincenzo_Lancia" title="Vincenzo Lancia">Vincenzo Lancia</a>. When he was sixteen he started a business with food in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. Later for few years he made relationships with <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> and he created a food industry in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. His efforts and innovations made his company a great success. His company was one of the first food companies in the country and showed new methods in this sector. When he made a fortune he returned to <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. When he goes back his rang in <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a> go to town advisor. By his education Giuseppe is a translator. In 1875 he is married for Marianna Orazzi. In 1876, their first son Giovanni is born. He loves education, humanities and the <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek language</a>. In 1879 their daughter Margherita was born. Unfortunately, she died in 1894. In 1881 their third child <b>Vincenzo Lancia</b> was born. Their second daughter was born at 1884 - Anna Maria later Anna Maria-Giacobinni. The Lancia family at that time was important for <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>. The members of the family liked to go on opera and theatre. In their free time. the Lancias spend their time at a villa near <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Vincenzo_Lancia" title="Vincenzo Lancia">Vincenzo Lancia</a> was born on 24 of August 1881 in <a href="/wiki/Fobello" title="Fobello">Fobello</a> near <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>. His father wanted Vincenzo to be a lawyer, but didn't have much interest in the humanities. He met the Battista brothers as well as Giovanni Ceirano and became interested in science and technology, especially automobiles. He saw his first cars in <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>. One of his friends Carlo Bishareti di Ruffia had a Benz and that was the first important automobile in his life. When <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIAT" title="FIAT">FIAT</a> was founded in 1899 Vincenzo was very active in the company and later became one of the most famous test drivers of Italian automobile brands. In 1922, Vincenzo married his secretary - Adele Miglietti. Vincenzo and Adele had three children Gianni, Eleonora and Maria. He died on February 15, 1937.</p>
<p><b>Gianni Lancia</b> was born on 24 November 1924 in <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>. He finished his education with his sisters at the Technical University of Pisa. From the time he was a little boy Gianni loved sports, but his greatest passion was motor racing. This led him to become a driver for the Lancia team. Gianni became the boss of Lancia in 1950. Unfortunately, he invested a lot of money in expensive prototypes and other unprofitable ventures that led him to sell a big part of the company to Carlo Pesenti in 1957. After that he started a business in the food industry. For a few years he lived in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. He had two sons, Mariele and Vincenzo from his first marriage and had one son (Lorenzo Lancia) from his marriage to Jacqueline Sassard .</p>

<p>The Ypsilon is a premium 5-door <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supermini_car" title="Supermini car">supermini car</a> produced since 2011. It is based on an updated <a href="/wiki/Fiat_500_(2007)" title="Fiat 500 (2007)">Fiat 500</a> platform. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Past_car_models">Past car models</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Past car models">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lancia has a long tradition of passenger, fast touring, sports and racing cars. They have tended to emphasize quality, appearance and sophisticated design, somewhat at the expense of power and competitive pricing. Among the most beautiful, desirable and unusual models are various <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Zagato" title="Lancia Zagato">Lancia Zagato</a> models.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Aurelia" title="Lancia Aurelia">Lancia Aurelia</a> introduced the front engine rear transmission configuration later used by Ferrari, Alfa Romeo, Porsche, GM, and Maserati, as well as the V6 engine, which is now common. It also had <a href="/wiki/Inboard_brake" title="Inboard brake">inboard rear brakes</a>, an important way of reducing un-sprung weight.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Stratos" title="Lancia Stratos">Lancia Stratos</a> was a successful rally car during the 1970s and helped the company to improve its sporting credentials.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Thema" title="Lancia Thema">Lancia Thema</a> <a href="/wiki/Executive_car" title="Executive car">executive car</a> was a re-branded second generation <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_300" title="Chrysler 300">Chrysler 300</a> unveiled in 2011 to replace the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Thesis" title="Lancia Thesis">Thesis</a>. It re–used the name of the Italian made 1984–94 <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Thema" title="Lancia Thema">Thema</a> saloon. Previously available in various European markets, for the United Kingdom and Ireland it was only sold as the <b>Chrysler 300C</b>. It has since been discontinued in 2015.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Voyager#Lancia_Voyager" title="Chrysler Voyager">Lancia Voyager</a> was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Large_MPV" title="Large MPV">large MPV</a> unveiled in 2011, which was based on the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Town_%26_Country" title="Chrysler Town &amp; Country">Chrysler Town &amp; Country</a>. It was marketed in various European markets, for the United Kingdom and Ireland it was only sold as the <b>Chrysler Grand Voyager</b>. It has since been discontinued in 2015.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_cars">Special cars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Special cars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the end of 1957 Lancia made their first <a href="/wiki/Limousine" title="Limousine">limousine</a> for the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Italy" title="President of Italy">President of Italy</a>, the model Lancia Florida.<br/>
In 1989 Lancia made a <a href="/wiki/Limousine" title="Limousine">limousine</a> version of the model <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Thema" title="Lancia Thema">Thema</a>.<br/>
In 1999 Lancia made a <a href="/wiki/Limousine" title="Limousine">limousine</a> version of the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Kappa" title="Lancia Kappa">Kappa</a> and in 2004 <a href="/wiki/Geneva_Motor_Show" title="Geneva Motor Show">Geneva Motor Show</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stola_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stola (company) (page does not exist)">Stola</a> showed a <a href="/wiki/Limousine" title="Limousine">limousine</a> version of the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Thesis" title="Lancia Thesis">Lancia Thesis</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Export_markets">Export markets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Export markets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2014, in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/La_Repubblica" title="La Repubblica">La Repubblica</a></i>, Fiat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEO" title="CEO">CEO</a> <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Marchionne" title="Sergio Marchionne">Sergio Marchionne</a> foreshadowed that Lancia would become an Italy–only brand, and focus only on the Ypsilon supermini range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-repubblica_futuro_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-repubblica_futuro-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While some models had been imported on a small scale during the 1950s–1960s, Lancias were officially sold in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> from 1975. Sales were comparatively slow, and the range was withdrawn at the same time as Fiat in 1982.</p>
<p>In 2009, following Fiat's acquisition of a stake in United States-based <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler</a> and part of Chrysler's restructuring plans, it was stated that Fiat plans for the Chrysler brand and Lancia to co–develop products, with some vehicles being shared. Olivier Francois, Lancia's CEO, took over as CEO of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chrysler_(division)" title="Chrysler (division)">Chrysler</a> division in October 2009. Fiat has also announced that, depending on the market, some Chrysler cars would be sold as Lancias and vice versa.</p>
<p>Francois plans to re-establish the Chrysler brand as an upscale brand, was somewhat muddied by the discontinuance of the <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_(automobile)" title="Plymouth (automobile)">Plymouth</a> brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> At the 2010 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detroit_Auto_Show" title="Detroit Auto Show">Detroit Auto Show</a>, a Chrysler-badged <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Delta" title="Lancia Delta">Lancia Delta</a> was on display,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but this did not result in sales in the United States, with proposals to instead modify an Alfa Romeo for sale by 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lancia's reputation was significantly undermined in 1980, when defective Lancia Beta models, suffering from significant suspension sub-frame corrosion problems, were purchased back from owners by the company in a highly publicised campaign. These cars were subsequently crushed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GH1-1980_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-GH1-1980-16">[16]</a></sup> The brand never recovered from the damage inflicted during the Beta recall and, combined with a range of related factors (including poor residual values, which made their range uncompetitive), decided to withdraw from the <a href="/wiki/Right-_and_left-hand_traffic" title="Right- and left-hand traffic">right hand drive</a> market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT1-2011_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-DT1-2011-17">[17]</a></sup> The last model be sold in the United Kingdom was the Delta, boosted by its rallying reputation, withdrawn from sale in 1995.</p>
<p>Since 1995, there have been continuous rumours suggesting Lancia's return to the United Kingdom. In November 2005, <i><a href="/wiki/What_Car%3F" title="What Car?">What Car?</a></i> reported rumours over the alleged return, to rival "affordable" premium makes, such as <a href="/wiki/Saab_Automobile" title="Saab Automobile">Saab</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volvo" title="Volvo">Volvo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In September 2006, <i>What Car?</i> reported that Lancia were officially returning to the United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The relaunch date was set for August 2008. In April 2008, <i><a href="/wiki/Car_(magazine)" title="Car (magazine)">Car</a></i> reported that Lancia had postponed the relaunch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>These were credible since Lancia models, by that time, shared common parts with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiat" title="Fiat">Fiat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo" title="Alfa Romeo">Alfa Romeo</a> models that were imported, sold and maintained by an existing dealership network. The cost to reestablish the brand were therefore minimal. In December 2008, however, Fiat cancelled re–launch plans, due to financial concerns coinciding with the global financial crisis, and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Recession_in_Europe" title="Great Recession in Europe">recession</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Lancia Ypsilon and Delta models were eventually re–introduced to the United Kingdom, but were re–branded as Chrysler. In January 2014, the Delta model was dropped from this line–up. In March 2015, Fiat Group announced that the Chrysler brand would be discontinued in the U.K. in 2017, citing a desire to focus largely on the Jeep brand instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A small number of Lancia models were previously sold in Japan, such as <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Fulvia" title="Lancia Fulvia">Fulvia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Stratos" title="Lancia Stratos">Stratos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Delta" title="Lancia Delta">Delta</a>. More recently, some models have been sold under the Chrysler brand, such as the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Ypsilon" title="Lancia Ypsilon">Ypsilon</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lancia_in_motorsport">Lancia in motorsport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Lancia in motorsport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formula_One">Formula One</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Formula One">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Grand_Prix_results" title="Lancia Grand Prix results">Lancia Grand Prix results</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>After Vincenzo Lancia's son <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Lancia" title="Gianni Lancia">Gianni</a> became director of the firm, it started to take part more frequently in motorsport, eventually deciding to build a Grand Prix car. <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Jano" title="Vittorio Jano">Vittorio Jano</a> was the new designer for Lancia and his <a href="/wiki/Lancia_D50" title="Lancia D50">Lancia D50</a> was entered into the <a href="/wiki/1954_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1954 Spanish Grand Prix">1954 Spanish Grand Prix</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> took the pole position and drove the fastest lap. In the <a href="/wiki/1955_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1955 Monaco Grand Prix">1955 Monaco Grand Prix</a> Ascari crashed into the harbour after missing a chicane. One week later Ascari was killed in an accident driving a Ferrari sports car at Monza. With Ascari's death and Lancia's financial problems the company withdrew from Grand Prix racing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-atlasf1.autosport.com_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1.autosport.com-23">[23]</a></sup> Altogether Lancia took two victories and ten podiums in Formula One.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4mula1.ro_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-4mula1.ro-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Remnants of the Lancia team were transferred to <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ddavid.com_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-ddavid.com-25">[25]</a></sup> where <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> won the <a href="/wiki/1956_Formula_One_season" title="1956 Formula One season">1956 championship</a> with a Lancia-Ferrari car.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rallying">Rallying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Rallying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Lancia_in_rallying" title="Lancia in rallying">Lancia in rallying</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lancia_Delta_S4.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="1280" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Lancia_Delta_S4.jpg/220px-Lancia_Delta_S4.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Lancia_Delta_S4.jpg/330px-Lancia_Delta_S4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Lancia_Delta_S4.jpg/440px-Lancia_Delta_S4.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>Lancia has been very successful in motorsport over the years, and mostly in the arena of <a href="/wiki/Rallying" title="Rallying">rallying</a>. Prior to the forming of the <a href="/wiki/World_Rally_Championship" title="World Rally Championship">World Rally Championship</a>, Lancia took the final <a href="/wiki/International_Championship_for_Manufacturers" title="International Championship for Manufacturers">International Championship for Manufacturers</a> title with the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Fulvia" title="Lancia Fulvia">Fulvia</a> in 1972. In the WRC, they remain the most statistically successful marque (despite having withdrawn at the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1993_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="1993 World Rally Championship season">1993 season</a>), winning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_World_Rally_Championship_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of World Rally Championship Constructors' Champions">constructors' titles</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Stratos" title="Lancia Stratos">Stratos</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1974_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="1974 World Rally Championship season">1974</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1975_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="1975 World Rally Championship season">1975</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1976_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="1976 World Rally Championship season">1976</a>), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancia_037" title="Lancia 037">037</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1983_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="1983 World Rally Championship season">1983</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Delta" title="Lancia Delta">Delta</a> (six consecutive wins from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1987_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="1987 World Rally Championship season">1987</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1992_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="1992 World Rally Championship season">1992</a>). The Delta is also the most successful individual model designation ever to compete in rallying. All this gave Lancia a total of 11 Championships over the years.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Juha_Kankkunen" title="Juha Kankkunen">Juha Kankkunen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miki_Biasion" title="Miki Biasion">Miki Biasion</a> both won two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_World_Rally_Championship_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of World Rally Championship Drivers' Champions">drivers' titles</a> with the Delta. Among other drivers to take several World Rally Championship wins with Lancia were <a href="/wiki/Markku_Al%C3%A9n" title="Markku Alén">Markku Alén</a>, <a href="/wiki/Didier_Auriol" title="Didier Auriol">Didier Auriol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sandro_Munari" title="Sandro Munari">Sandro Munari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Darniche" title="Bernard Darniche">Bernard Darniche</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walter_R%C3%B6hrl" title="Walter Röhrl">Walter Röhrl</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Waldeg%C3%A5rd" title="Björn Waldegård">Björn Waldegård</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henri_Toivonen" title="Henri Toivonen">Henri Toivonen</a>. The history of the brand in rallying is also tainted with tragedy, with deaths of Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Attilio_Bettega" title="Attilio Bettega">Attilio Bettega</a> at the 1985 <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_Corse" title="Tour de Corse">Tour de Corse</a> in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancia_037" title="Lancia 037">Lancia 037</a> and then Finnish championship favourite Toivonen in a <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Delta_S4" title="Lancia Delta S4">Lancia Delta S4</a> at the same rally exactly a year later. These deaths would eventually lead to the end of <a href="/wiki/Group_B" title="Group B">Group B</a> rallying.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_car_racing">Sports car racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Sports car racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1953, Lancia introduced the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_D24" title="Lancia D24">D24</a> sports racer, which was an evolution of D23 model, but rebodied as a spider by Pininfarina. Its most significant victories were: the <a href="/wiki/1953_Carrera_Panamericana" title="1953 Carrera Panamericana">1953 Carrera Panamericana</a>, the <a href="/wiki/1954_Mille_Miglia" title="1954 Mille Miglia">1954 Mille Miglia</a> and the 1954 <a href="/wiki/Targa_Florio" title="Targa Florio">Targa Florio</a>.</p>

<p>During Lancia's dominance of rallying, the company also expanded into sports cars in the late 1970s until the mid-1980s. Originally running the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancia_Stratos_HF" title="Lancia Stratos HF">Stratos HF</a> in <a href="/wiki/Group_4_(racing)" title="Group 4 (racing)">Group 4</a>, as well as a brief interlude with a rare <a href="/wiki/Group_5_(racing)" title="Group 5 (racing)">Group 5</a> version, the car was replaced with the successful <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Montecarlo" title="Lancia Montecarlo">Beta Montecarlo</a> Turbo winning the FIA's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1980_World_Championship_for_Makes" title="1980 World Championship for Makes">1980 World Championship for Makes</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1981_World_Endurance_Championship_for_Makes" title="1981 World Endurance Championship for Makes">1981 World Endurance Championship for Makes</a> and the 1980 <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Rennsport_Meisterschaft" title="Deutsche Rennsport Meisterschaft">Deutsche Rennsport Meisterschaft</a>.</p>

<p>In 1982 the team moved up to Group 6 with the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_LC1" title="Lancia LC1">LC1</a> Spyder, followed by the <a href="/wiki/Group_C" title="Group C">Group C</a> <a href="/wiki/Lancia_LC2" title="Lancia LC2">LC2</a> coupé which featured a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari" title="Ferrari">Ferrari</a> powerplant in 1983. The LC2 was a match for the standard-setting <a href="/wiki/Porsche_956" title="Porsche 956">Porsche 956</a> in terms of raw speed, securing 13 pole positions over its lifetime, however its results were hampered by poor reliability and <a href="/wiki/Fuel_economy_in_automobiles" title="Fuel economy in automobiles">fuel economy</a> and it only managed to win three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Endurance_Championship" title="European Endurance Championship">European</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_Sportscar_Championship" title="World Sportscar Championship">World Endurance Championship</a> races. The team's inability to compete against the dominant <a href="/wiki/Porsche_956" title="Porsche 956">Porsche 956</a> and <a href="/wiki/Porsche_962" title="Porsche 962">962</a> sports cars led it to drop out of sportscar racing at the end of 1986 in order to concentrate on rallying, although private teams continued to enter LC2s with declining results until the early 1990s.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_vehicles">Commercial vehicles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Commercial vehicles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lancia produced a wide range of vans, trucks, buses and military vehicles from the beginning, forming Lancia Veicoli Industriali in 1912. Lancia slowly withdrew from the commercial sectors during the late 1960s and production of commercial vehicles ended in the early 1970s, shortly after Fiat's takeover of the company, with some models transferred to <a href="/wiki/Iveco" title="Iveco">Iveco</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_and_sponsorship">Media and sponsorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lancia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Media and sponsorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2009, the British motoring television show <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(2002_TV_series)" title="Top Gear (2002 TV series)">Top Gear</a></i> suggested that Lancia had more 'great' models than any other car company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The presenters went on to test the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Gamma" title="Lancia Gamma">Gamma Coupé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Fulvia" title="Lancia Fulvia">Fulvia Coupé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Aprilia" title="Lancia Aprilia">Aprilia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Montecarlo" title="Lancia Montecarlo">Montecarlo</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancia_Beta_Coup%C3%A9" title="Lancia Beta Coupé">Beta Coupé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Beta#HPE" title="Lancia Beta">HPE</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancia_Stratos_HF" title="Lancia Stratos HF">Stratos</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancia_037" title="Lancia 037">037</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Delta#Integrale_Evoluzione_II" title="Lancia Delta">Delta Integrale Evo II</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancia_Thema_8.32" title="Lancia Thema 8.32">Thema 8.32</a>. They also stated during their review that Lancia made the best looking cars, even though they are unreliable.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Lancia sponsored the <a href="/wiki/Venice_Film_Festival" title="Venice Film Festival">Venice Film Festival</a> for five years, ending in 2012, with the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_Thema" title="Lancia Thema">Lancia Thema</a> used to transport stars to the festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Lancia was sponsor of ninth and eleventh World Summit of <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a> Laureates.</p>
