<p><a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_colonel_(United_Kingdom)" title="Lieutenant colonel (United Kingdom)">Lieutenant Colonel</a> <b>Ralph Howard Broome</b> <span style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">DSO</a> <a href="/wiki/Military_Cross" title="Military Cross">MC</a></span> (5 July 1889 – 25 January 1985) was a <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> officer and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bobsled" title="Bobsled">bobsledder</a> who competed during the early 1920s. he was born in <a href="/wiki/Dalhousie,_India" title="Dalhousie, India">Dalhousie</a>, Himachal Pradesh, India, and died in <a href="/wiki/Poole" title="Poole">Poole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Olympic_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Olympic-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Broome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Broome won a silver medal in the four-man event at the <a href="/wiki/1924_Winter_Olympics" title="1924 Winter Olympics">1924 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chamonix" title="Chamonix">Chamonix</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Olympic_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Olympic-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Earlier, Broome had been awarded the <a href="/wiki/Military_Cross" title="Military Cross">Military Cross</a> (<a href="/wiki/1915_Birthday_Honours" title="1915 Birthday Honours">1915</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">Distinguished Service Order</a> (1918) while serving with the <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire_Regiment" title="Wiltshire Regiment">Wiltshire Regiment</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">the Second World War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He retired from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Tank_Regiment" title="Royal Tank Regiment">Royal Tank Corps</a> as a Lieutenant Colonel in 1935.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
