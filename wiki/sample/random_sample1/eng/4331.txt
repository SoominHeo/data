<p><b>Ralf Fährmann</b> (born 27 September 1988) is a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a> for <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralf_F%C3%A4hrmann&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ralf_F%C3%A4hrmann.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1533" data-file-width="1026" height="269" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Ralf_F%C3%A4hrmann.jpg/180px-Ralf_F%C3%A4hrmann.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Ralf_F%C3%A4hrmann.jpg/270px-Ralf_F%C3%A4hrmann.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Ralf_F%C3%A4hrmann.jpg/360px-Ralf_F%C3%A4hrmann.jpg 2x" width="180"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Ralf Fährmann playing for <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a></div>

<p>Fährmann made his professional debut for <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a> on 13 September 2008 in 3–3 draw away to <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fussballdaten_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-fussballdaten-1">[1]</a></sup> On 16 September 2008, he played his first international match, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APOEL_F.C." title="APOEL F.C.">APOEL</a> in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> tie.</p>
<p>At the end of the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_FC_Schalke_04_season" title="2008–09 FC Schalke 04 season">2008–09 season</a>, he left Schalke and moved to <a href="/wiki/Eintracht_Frankfurt" title="Eintracht Frankfurt">Eintracht Frankfurt</a> on free transfer, signing a contract valid to 30 June 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fährmann returned to Schalke in 2011 after the team sold <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Neuer" title="Manuel Neuer">Manuel Neuer</a> to <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/DFL-Supercup" title="DFL-Supercup">2011 DFL-Supercup</a>, Fährmann was named Man of the Match. After 90 minutes, the game finished 0–0 and it went on directly to a penalty shootout. Fährmann saved two penalty kicks, one from <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Gro%C3%9Fkreutz" title="Kevin Großkreutz">Kevin Großkreutz</a> and one from <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Peri%C5%A1i%C4%87" title="Ivan Perišić">Ivan Perišić</a>, thus allowing Schalke to raise the Supercup trophy for the first time in the club's history. In the start of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_FC_Schalke_04_season" title="2013–14 FC Schalke 04 season">2013–14 season</a>, he was named second choice goalkeeper behind <a href="/wiki/Timo_Hildebrand" title="Timo Hildebrand">Timo Hildebrand</a>. While Hildebrand was injured, he became the first choice goalkeeper and his impressive performances saw him keep the number one spot for the remainder of the season.</p>
<p>On 4 May 2014, Schalke announced that Fährmann extended his contract with Schalke until 30 June 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
