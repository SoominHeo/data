<p><i><b>Rambo</b></i> is a 2008 American <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action film</a> directed, co-written by and starring <a href="/wiki/Sylvester_Stallone" title="Sylvester Stallone">Sylvester Stallone</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rambo_2008_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rambo_2008-3">[3]</a></sup> reprising his iconic role as <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_veteran" title="Vietnam veteran">Vietnam veteran</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Rambo" title="John Rambo">John Rambo</a>. It is the fourth and final installment in the <i><a href="/wiki/Rambo_(film_series)" title="Rambo (film series)">Rambo</a></i> franchise, set twenty years after the events of the previous film, <i><a href="/wiki/Rambo_III" title="Rambo III">Rambo III</a></i>. This film is dedicated to the memory of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Crenna" title="Richard Crenna">Richard Crenna</a>, who played the character <a href="/wiki/Sam_Trautman" title="Sam Trautman">Colonel Sam Trautman</a> in the previous three films, and who died of heart failure in 2003.</p>
<p>The film is about John Rambo (Stallone), a hardened former <a href="/wiki/Special_Forces_(United_States_Army)" title="Special Forces (United States Army)">Green Beret</a>, who is hired by a church pastor to help rescue a group of <a href="/wiki/Christian_mission" title="Christian mission">Christian missionaries</a> who were kidnapped by men from a brutal <a href="/wiki/State_Peace_and_Development_Council" title="State Peace and Development Council">Burmese military regime</a>.</p>
<p>The film was released on January 25, 2008. Upon its release, <i>Rambo</i> received a mixed reception from film critics who generally praised its acting, action sequences, and soundtrack by <a href="/wiki/Brian_Tyler" title="Brian Tyler">Brian Tyler</a>, but were critical of its writing, plot, and depictions of extreme violence. The film grossed $113,244,290 during its run at the international box office and $41,500,683 in DVD sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-the-numbers1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-the-numbers1-4">[4]</a></sup> The film had its cable television premiere on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spike_(TV_channel)" title="Spike (TV channel)">Spike TV</a> on July 11, 2010. However, it was the extended cut that was broadcast, not the theatrical version. The extended cut was released on <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray" title="Blu-ray">Blu-ray</a> two weeks later.</p>
<p>Despite not being as successful as the previous films, Stallone had confirmed that a sequel titled <i>Rambo: Last Blood</i> was still in the works but was converted into a reboot for the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> This film was ultimately cancelled in March 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rambo_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amid the political protests of the <a href="/wiki/Saffron_Revolution" title="Saffron Revolution">Saffron Revolution</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Burma</a> (Myanmar), ruthless <a href="/wiki/State_Peace_and_Development_Council" title="State Peace and Development Council">SPDC</a> officer Major Pa Tee Tint leads an army to pillage small villages in a campaign of fear. His soldiers sadistically slaughter innocents, abduct teenage boys to be drafted into his army and hold women hostage to be raped as sex slaves. Meanwhile, twenty years <a href="/wiki/Rambo_III" title="Rambo III">after the events in Afghanistan</a>, John Rambo now lives in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, making a meager living as a snake catcher. A doctor and missionary named Michael Burnett hires Rambo to use his boat to ferry their group up the <a href="/wiki/Salween_River" title="Salween River">Salween River</a> into Burma on a humanitarian mission to provide medical aid to a village of <a href="/wiki/Karen_people" title="Karen people">Karen tribespeople</a>.</p>
<p>During the trip, the boat is stopped by pirates demanding Sarah Miller, the only female in the group, in exchange for passage. Rambo is forced to kill them to protect her. Michael is greatly disturbed by Rambo's actions and upon arriving at their destination sends him back, claiming they no longer want his help. The village the missionaries are giving care to is attacked by Tint's soldiers. The missionaries are abducted and the villagers are all savagely massacred.</p>
<p>The pastor of the missionaries church comes to Thailand and asks Rambo to guide a team of five mercenaries on a rescue mission. Rambo takes the mercenary team to the drop-off point and offers to help but the team leader Lewis, a former <a href="/wiki/Special_Air_Service" title="Special Air Service">SAS</a> soldier, refuses. Myint, a <a href="/wiki/Karen_National_Liberation_Army" title="Karen National Liberation Army">Karen rebel</a> familiar with the area, leads the mercenaries to the village of the massacre. As they survey the damage, a squad of Tint's soldiers arrive in a cargo truck with a small group of hostages, intent on torturing them. Outnumbered, the mercenaries take cover and watch helplessly as the soldiers prey on their hostages. Having secretly followed the mercenaries, Rambo emerges in time to singlehandedly kill all the soldiers with his bow and arrows, allowing the hostages to escape unharmed. Rambo joins the mercenary team and they make their way to Tint's soldier's camp. They stealthily rescue the American missionaries and Burmese hostages and flee under cover of night.</p>
<p>The next morning, Tint and his soldiers pursue them and manage to capture everyone except for Rambo, Sarah and School Boy, the mercenary's sniper. Rambo saves them from being executed by hijacking Tint's jeep mounted with an <a href="/wiki/M2_Browning" title="M2 Browning">M2 Browning machine gun</a>. Rambo guns down much of Tint's army and a firefight ensues between the mercenaries and Tint's soldiers. Several of the missionaries and mercenaries are killed. The Karen rebels, led by Myint, arrive and join the fight, helping to overwhelm Tint's soldiers and kill them all. Rambo then kills Tint by disemboweling him as he tries to escape.</p>
<p>Some time later, Rambo returns to the United States to finally visit his father at his home in Arizona.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rambo_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In between the making of the third and fourth films in the <i>Rambo</i> franchise, the films' original producer, <a href="/wiki/Carolco_Pictures" title="Carolco Pictures">Carolco Pictures</a>, went out of business. In 1997, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miramax_Films" title="Miramax Films">Miramax Films</a> purchased the <i>Rambo</i> franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SS_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-SS-7">[7]</a></sup> The following year, Miramax subsidiary <a href="/wiki/Dimension_Films" title="Dimension Films">Dimension Films</a> intended to make another film, and a writer was hired to write the script, but attempts to make it were deterred by Stallone, who had stated that he no longer wanted to make action movies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2005, the studio sold those rights to <a href="/wiki/Nu_Image" title="Nu Image">Nu Image</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millennium_Films" title="Millennium Films">Millennium Films</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SS_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-SS-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Filming started on January 22, 2007 and ended on May 4, 2007. It was shot in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiang_Mai,_Thailand" title="Chiang Mai, Thailand">Chiang Mai, Thailand</a> as well as in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a> and <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.</p>
<p>While filming near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Burma</a>, Stallone and the rest of the crew narrowly avoided being shot by the Burmese military. Stallone described Burma as a "hellhole". He said "we had shots fired above our heads" and that he "witnessed survivors with legs cut off and all kinds of land-mine injuries, maggot-infested wounds and ears cut off."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NZ_Herald_10467584_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-NZ_Herald_10467584-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_titles">Alternative titles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rambo_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Alternative titles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>John Rambo</i> was the original working title for the film but was changed in the US because Stallone thought that audiences might think that this is the final film in the <i>Rambo</i> series (due to the then recently released <i><a href="/wiki/Rocky_Balboa_(film)" title="Rocky Balboa (film)">Rocky Balboa</a></i>), which was not his original intent. In many other countries, the title <i>John Rambo</i> is used because the first <i>Rambo</i> film, <i>First Blood</i>, was released as <i>Rambo</i> in those countries. The film premiered on US television as <i>Rambo</i>, but the title sequence referred to it as <i>John Rambo</i>.</p>
<p>On October 12, 2007, Lionsgate announced that the film title was being changed to <i>Rambo: To Hell and Back</i>. After some negative feedback from the online community, Stallone spoke with AICN's <a href="/wiki/Harry_Knowles" title="Harry Knowles">Harry Knowles</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and said:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><i>"Lionsgate jumped the gun on this. I just was thinking that the title</i> John Rambo <i>was derivative of</i> Rocky Balboa <i>and might give people the idea that this is the last</i> Rambo <i>film, and I don't necessarily feel that it will be. He's definitely a superb athlete, there's no reason he can't continue onto another adventure. Like <a href="/wiki/John_Wayne" title="John Wayne">John Wayne</a> with</i> <a href="/wiki/The_Searchers_(film)" title="The Searchers (film)">The Searchers</a><i>."</i></p>
</blockquote>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rambo_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rambo_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Rambo</i> opened in 2,751 North American theaters on January 25, 2008 and grossed $6,490,000 on its opening day,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-18">[18]</a></sup> and $18,200,000 over its opening weekend. It was the second highest-grossing movie for the weekend in the U.S. and Canada behind <i><a href="/wiki/Meet_the_Spartans" title="Meet the Spartans">Meet the Spartans</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The film has a box office gross of $113,344,290, of which $42,754,105 was from Canada and the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an unprecedented move, Europe's biggest cinema chain (and the third biggest in the world), <a href="/wiki/Odeon_Cinemas" title="Odeon Cinemas">Odeon</a>, controversially refused to show the film on any of its screens in the United Kingdom, blaming "commercial differences".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The film was shown in Ireland and the United Kingdom by other theater chains such as <a href="/wiki/Empire_Cinemas" title="Empire Cinemas">Empire Cinemas</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vue_(cinema)" title="Vue (cinema)">Vue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cineworld" title="Cineworld">Cineworld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ward_Anderson" title="Ward Anderson">Ward Anderson</a>. The film was not shown in the French-speaking part of <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> due to legal and commercial problems with the distributor, even if it was available on screens of France and the Swiss German-speaking part.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rambo_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Rambo</i> received mixed reviews, with critics praising the film's action sequences and Stallone's performance, but criticizing the film's excessive violence. On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the film has a 37% "Rotten" rating, based on 142 reviews, with an average rating of 4.8/10. The site's critical consensus reads: "Sylvester Stallone knows how to stage action sequences, but the movie's uneven pacing and excessive violence (even for the franchise) is more nauseating than entertaining".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a score of 46 out of 100, based on 26 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his review for <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.O._Scott" title="A.O. Scott">A.O. Scott</a> wrote, "Mr. Stallone is smart enough—or maybe dumb enough, though I tend to think not—to present the mythic dimensions of the character without apology or irony. His face looks like a misshapen chunk of granite, and his acting is only slightly more expressive, but the man gets the job done. Welcome back."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scott_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-scott-24">[24]</a></sup> Michael H. Price of <i>Fort Worth Business Press</i> wrote, "Stallone invests the role with a realistic acceptance of the aging process, and with traces reminiscent of <a href="/wiki/Humphrey_Bogart" title="Humphrey Bogart">Humphrey Bogart</a> in 1951’s <i><a href="/wiki/The_African_Queen_(film)" title="The African Queen (film)">The African Queen</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a> in 1992’s <i><a href="/wiki/Unforgiven" title="Unforgiven">Unforgiven</a></i> — to say nothing of the influences that the original <i><a href="/wiki/First_Blood" title="First Blood">First Blood</a></i> had absorbed from <a href="/wiki/Marlon_Brando" title="Marlon Brando">Marlon Brando</a> in 1953’s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wild_One" title="The Wild One">The Wild One</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Laughlin" title="Tom Laughlin">Tom Laughlin</a> in 1971’s <i><a href="/wiki/Billy_Jack" title="Billy Jack">Billy Jack</a></i>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Jonathan Garret (a former writer for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta_Journal_Constitution" title="Atlanta Journal Constitution">Atlanta Journal Constitution</a>) said in an interview: "<i>Rambo</i> is the most violent movie I have ever seen. The last 11 minutes of the film are so violent, it makes <i><a href="/wiki/We_Were_Soldiers" title="We Were Soldiers">We Were Soldiers</a></i> look like <i><a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a></i>".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>When asked what his take on the film was, <i><a href="/wiki/First_Blood_(novel)" title="First Blood (novel)">First Blood</a></i> writer <a href="/wiki/David_Morrell" title="David Morrell">David Morrell</a> said:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>I'm happy to report that overall I’m pleased. The level of violence might not be for everyone, but it has a serious intent. This is the first time that the tone of my novel <i>First Blood</i> has been used in any of the movies. It's spot-on in terms of how I imagined the character — angry, burned-out, and filled with self-disgust because Rambo hates what he is and yet knows it's the only thing he does well. ... I think some elements could have been done better, [but] I think this film deserves a solid three stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_in_Burma">Reception in Burma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rambo_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception in Burma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The film is currently banned by the Burmese government. The military <a href="/wiki/State_Peace_and_Development_Council" title="State Peace and Development Council">ruling party</a> has ordered DVD vendors in Burma not to distribute the film due to the movie's content.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-28">[28]</a></sup> Despite having never been released there theatrically or on DVD, <i>Rambo</i> is, however, available there in bootleg versions. Despite the film being unpopular among some of the population due to the negative portrayal of the <a href="/wiki/Tatmadaw" title="Tatmadaw">Tatmadaw</a>, the opposition youth group <a href="/wiki/Generation_Wave" title="Generation Wave">Generation Wave</a> copied and distributed the film as anti-Tatmadaw propaganda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAPP_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-AAPP-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to Karen Freedom Fighters, the movie gave them a great boost of morale. Burmese freedom fighters have even adopted dialogue from the movie (most notably "Live for nothing, or die for something") as rallying points and battle cries. "That, to me," said Sylvester Stallone, "is one of the proudest moments I've ever had in film."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-28">[28]</a></sup> Also, overseas Burmese have praised the movie for its vivid portrayal of the military's oppression of the Karen people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rambo_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray Disc</a> editions were released in the U.S. on May 27, 2008. The DVD is in 1 and 2 disc editions. The Special edition has a 2.40 <a href="/wiki/Anamorphic_widescreen" title="Anamorphic widescreen">anamorphic widescreen</a> presentation and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dolby_Digital_EX" title="Dolby Digital EX">Dolby Digital 5.1 EX</a> track. The single editions have a standard 5.1 Dolby Digital track. The Blu-ray Disc has <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dolby_Digital_EX" title="Dolby Digital EX">Dolby Digital 5.1 EX</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_Theater_System#DTS-HD_Master_Audio" title="Digital Theater System">DTS HD 7.1</a> Tracks. The DVD and Blu-ray Disc on disc one have the film, deleted scenes, 6 featurettes, and commentary by Sylvester Stallone. The Blu-ray Disc also has 2 extra special features, that includes a trailer gallery.</p>
<p>The 2-disc DVD and Blu-ray Disc editions have a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_Copy" title="Digital Copy">digital copy</a> of the film. There is also a 6 disc DVD set of all four <i><a href="/wiki/Rambo_(film_series)" title="Rambo (film series)">Rambo</a></i> films, packaged in a limited edition tin case with over 20 bonus features. A Blu-ray Disc set with <i>Rambo 1-3</i> was also released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> was released in the UK on June 23, 2008.</p>
<p>The film was the 19th best selling DVD of 2008 with 1.7m units sold and an overall gross of $39,206,346.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-the-numbers1_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-the-numbers1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Extended_cut">Extended cut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rambo_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Extended cut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When asked about the moral of the film in a Daily Yomiuri Online interview, Sylvester Stallone mentioned that he will be doing an Extended Cut of the film, which will go by the original title of <i>John Rambo</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Nevertheless, the news became most well known after a May 2008 interview with Jay Leno when he announced the work of a director's cut, and that the proceeds would go to Burma. On top of this, an online petition appeared shortly after the announcement in order to "motivate" Stallone in completing this new cut. At the 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic-Con_International" title="Comic-Con International">Comic-Con</a>, it was vaguely announced that the director's cut (or as they labeled it, "extended cut") would be released in 2009, though no other specifics were given.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Additionally, the director's cut premiered at the 2008 Zurich Film Festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> DVD Active announced it would premiere only on Blu-ray Disc in Canada and the United States on July 27, 2010.</p>
<p>The extended cut's Blu-ray was released under the original title, "<i>Rambo</i>", however, the film replaces the original title with "<i>John Rambo</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The extended cut reinstates most of the deleted/extended scenes featured in the theatrical version's 2-disc special edition release, as well as some unreleased footage and some significant alternations. The extended cut premiered on cable television (VIA <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spike_(TV_channel)" title="Spike (TV channel)">Spike TV</a>) on July 11, 2010, two weeks before its Blu-ray release. It then had its encore presentation on August 8 and August 9, 2010, promoting Stallone's then latest film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Expendables_(2010_film)" title="The Expendables (2010 film)">The Expendables</a></i>. The extended cut runs 99 minutes long, whereas the theatrical version runs 91 minutes. Of those run times the end credits roll for 11 minutes.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rambo_(2008_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2012, Sean Hood offered an update on his Twitter account stating that <i>Rambo 5</i> is on hold as Stallone finishes <i><a href="/wiki/The_Expendables_2" title="The Expendables 2">The Expendables 2</a></i>. Hood said that he hasn't decided if <i>Rambo 5</i> will be an 'Unforgiven' or a 'passing of the torch.' Meanwhile, Stallone confirmed a fifth installment, saying this one would be the last one, currently under the title <i>Rambo: Last Stand</i> (some sources, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internet_Movie_Database" title="Internet Movie Database">IMDB</a>, state that the title will be <i>Rambo: Last Blood</i>). The film would bring Rambo to Arizona's Mexican border and to <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> as well.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Rumor also states that Rambo would be fighting <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">ISIS</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 5, 2016, Sylvester Stallone stated a fifth film is no longer in production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
