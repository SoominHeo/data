<p><b>Lampa</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_Chile" title="Communes of Chile">commune</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Chile" title="List of cities in Chile">city</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Chacabuco_Province" title="Chacabuco Province">Chacabuco province</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Metropolitan_Region" title="Santiago Metropolitan Region">Santiago Metropolitan Region</a>. Lampa is situated near the <a href="/wiki/Chicauma" title="Chicauma">Chicauma</a> mountain range, part of which was added to the <a href="/wiki/La_Campana_National_Park" title="La Campana National Park">La Campana National Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lampa,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lampa appears in the official census records as early at 1788.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> According to the 2002 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Statistics_Institute_(Chile)" title="National Statistics Institute (Chile)">National Statistics Institute</a>, Lampa spans an area of 451.9 km<sup>2</sup> (174 sq mi) and has 40,228 inhabitants (20,571 men and 19,657 women). Of these, 28,229 (70.2%) lived in <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban areas</a> and 11,999 (29.8%) in <a href="/wiki/Rural_area" title="Rural area">rural areas</a>. The population grew by 60.7% (15,195 persons) between the 1992 and 2002 censuses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INE_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-INE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lampa,_Chile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_Chile" title="Communes of Chile">commune</a>, Lampa is a third-level <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_division_of_Chile" title="Administrative division of Chile">administrative division of Chile</a> administered by a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council" title="Municipal council">municipal council</a>, headed by an <a href="/wiki/Alcalde" title="Alcalde">alcalde</a> who is directly elected every four years. The 2012-2016 alcalde is Graciela Ortúzar Novoa (<a href="/wiki/National_Renewal_(Chile)" title="National Renewal (Chile)">RN</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Official_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Official-1">[1]</a></sup> The communal council has the following members:</p>
<ul>
<li>Juan Amigo Astudillo (IND)</li>
<li>Rodrigo Plaza Muñoz (<a href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Party_(Chile)" title="Christian Democratic Party (Chile)">PDC</a>)</li>
<li>Carmen Gloria Ruminot Jorquera (<a href="/wiki/Independent_Democratic_Union" title="Independent Democratic Union">UDI</a>)</li>
<li>Felipe González Pino (IND)</li>
<li>Marcos Álvarez Tobar (<a href="/wiki/Party_for_Democracy_(Chile)" title="Party for Democracy (Chile)">PPD</a>)</li>
<li>Marcelo Jaque Peña (<a href="/wiki/Independent_Regionalist_Party" title="Independent Regionalist Party">PRI</a>)</li>
</ul>
<p>The regional <a href="/wiki/Intendant" title="Intendant">intendant</a>, appointed by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Chile" title="President of Chile">president</a>, is Fernando Echeverría.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Intendants_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Intendants-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_of_Chile" title="Electoral divisions of Chile">electoral divisions of Chile</a>, Lampa is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Chile" title="Chamber of Deputies of Chile">Chamber of Deputies</a> by Mr. Patricio Melero (UDI) and Mr. Gabriel Silber (PDC) as part of the 16th electoral district, (together with <a href="/wiki/Colina,_Chile" title="Colina, Chile">Colina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tiltil" title="Tiltil">Tiltil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quilicura" title="Quilicura">Quilicura</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pudahuel" title="Pudahuel">Pudahuel</a>). The commune is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Chile" title="Senate of Chile">Senate</a> by Guido Girardi Lavín (<a href="/wiki/Party_for_Democracy_(Chile)" title="Party for Democracy (Chile)">PPD</a>) and Jovino Novoa Vásquez (UDI) as part of the 7th senatorial constituency (Santiago-West).</p>
