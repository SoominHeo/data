<p><i><b>Rapti</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Nepali_language" title="Nepali language">Nepali</a>: <span lang="ne" xml:lang="ne"><a class="extiw" href="https://ne.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E0%A4%B0%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%AA%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%A4%E0%A5%80_%E0%A4%85%E0%A4%9E%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%9A%E0%A4%B2" title="ne:राप्ती अञ्चल">राप्ती अञ्चल</a></span> <span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Rapti.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/83/Rapti.ogg" title="Rapti.ogg">Listen</a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Rapti.ogg" title="File:Rapti.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span>) is one of the fourteen <a href="/wiki/List_of_zones_of_Nepal" title="List of zones of Nepal">zones</a>, located in the <a href="/wiki/Mid-Western_Development_Region,_Nepal" title="Mid-Western Development Region, Nepal">Mid-Western Development Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>. It is named after the <a href="/wiki/West_Rapti_River" title="West Rapti River">West Rapti River</a> which drains <i><a href="/wiki/Rolpa_District" title="Rolpa District">Rolpa</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pyuthan_District" title="Pyuthan District">Pyuthan</a></i> and part of <i><a href="/wiki/Dang_Deukhuri_District" title="Dang Deukhuri District">Dang</a></i> districts. The remainder of Dang and part of <i><a href="/wiki/Salyan_District,_Nepal" title="Salyan District, Nepal">Salyan</a></i> district are drained by the <a href="/wiki/Suheli_River" title="Suheli River"><i>Babai</i></a>. The remainder of Salyan and all of <i><a href="/wiki/Rukum_District" title="Rukum District">Rukum</a></i> districts are drained by the <a href="/wiki/Bheri_River" title="Bheri River"><i>Bheri</i></a>.</p>
<p>The headquarters of <b>Rapti</b> is <i><a href="/wiki/Tulsipur,_Rapti" title="Tulsipur, Rapti">Tulsipur</a></i> and the largest city is <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tribhuvannagar" title="Tribhuvannagar">Tribhuvannagar</a></i> (<i>Ghorahi</i>). Other main cities and towns of Rapti zone are <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pyuthan_Khalanga" title="Pyuthan Khalanga">Pyuthan Khalanga</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bijuwar" title="Bijuwar">Bijuwar</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Liwang,_Rolpa" title="Liwang, Rolpa"><i>Liwang</i></a>, <i>Lamahi</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Musikot" title="Musikot">Musikot</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rukumkot" title="Rukumkot">Rukumkot</a> (Shova)</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Chaurjahari" title="Chaurjahari">Chaurjahari</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rapti_Zone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dang District begins at the border with <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. Since the border follows the southern edge of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dudhwa_Range" title="Dudhwa Range">Dudhwa Range</a></i>—a subrange of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siwaliks" title="Siwaliks">Siwaliks</a></i>, here there is no Nepalese <i>Outer Terai</i> extending onto the main <i><a href="/wiki/Indo-Gangetic_Plain" title="Indo-Gangetic Plain">Indo-Gangetic Plain</a></i>. Dang has two <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inner_Terai_Valleys_of_Nepal#Dang_and_Deukhuri_Valleys" title="Inner Terai Valleys of Nepal">Inner Terai Valleys</a></i>. <i>Deukhuri</i> lies beyond the Dudhwas, then a second low subrange—the <i>Dang Range</i>—separates Deukhuri from <i>Dang Valley</i>. Beyond Dang Valley the district extends to the crest of the higher <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mahabharat_Lekh" title="Mahabharat Lekh">Mahabharat Range</a></i>.</p>
<p>The two valleys have abundant level and gently-sloping land with fair to good soil development, and abundant groundwater. <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">Malaria</a> made them nearly uninhabitable except to the <i><a href="/wiki/Tharu_people" title="Tharu people">Tharu</a></i> ethnic group that had evolved resistance. In the 1960s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DDT#Use_against_malaria" title="DDT">DDT</a> came into use to suppress the mosquito vectors and the way was open to settlers from the hills who used debt and lawsuits to displace and even enslave Tharus.</p>
<p>Dang is the most developed and most rapidly developing of the zone's five districts. <i><a href="/wiki/Mahendra_Highway" title="Mahendra Highway">Mahendra Highway</a>,</i> Nepal's main east-west route, follows Deukhuri Valley. Dang Valley has two important towns, Tribuvan nagar and Tulsipur, and an all-weather airport.</p>
<p>Pyuthan, Rolpa and Salyan Districts are in the <b>Middle Hills</b> extending north from the crest of the Mahabharat Range. Pyuthan has a <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>-growing <a href="/wiki/Alluvial_plain" title="Alluvial plain">alluvial plain</a> along <a href="/wiki/West_Rapti_River" title="West Rapti River"><i>Jhimruk Khola</i></a>, surrounded by villages of rice-growing <i><a href="/wiki/Bahun" title="Bahun">Bahun</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Chhetri" title="Chhetri">Chhetri</a></i> farmers served by bazaar towns of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newar" title="Newar">Newar</a></i> merchants. Rolpa district mainly lies along <i>Mardi Khola</i>, the other large Rapti tributary that is more eroded into an inner gorge and less suited to traditional irrigation projects.</p>
<p>Pyuthan and Rolpa extend north to a rugged 3-4,000 meter ridgeline marking the limits of the <a href="/wiki/West_Rapti_River" title="West Rapti River">Rapti</a> Basin. <i><a href="/wiki/Kham_Magar" title="Kham Magar">Kham Magar</a></i> live in small villages throughout these highlands up to about 2,500 meters. They herd sheep, goats and cattle in high summer pastures as far north as the western <a href="/wiki/Dhaulagiri" title="Dhaulagiri">Dhaulagiri</a> Himalaya in Rukum district, moving south to the Mahabharat Range in winter. Kham also cultivate subtropical and temperate fruit trees such as mulberry, citrus and Asian pear as cash crops. Until it was outlawed in the 1970s they cultivated hemp and made hashish bought by government agents to be sold in monopoly stores. Termination of these arrangements increased Kham outmigration in search of employment and contributed to discontent with the Shah regime.</p>
<p>Salyan resembles Pyuthan in having a mix of rice-growing lowlands inhabited by caste Hindus, and uplands inhabited by Kham peoples. It is drained by the Babai and Bheri rivers.</p>
<p>Rukum is Rapi Zone's northernmost, most mountainous district including the western part of <i><a href="/wiki/Dhaulagiri" title="Dhaulagiri">Dhaulagiri</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Himalaya" title="Himalaya">Himalaya</a></i> drained by the Bheri River. At lower elevations it is populated by <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Khas_people" title="Khas people">Khas people</a></i> said to be ancestral to most of Nepal's <a href="/wiki/Bahun" title="Bahun">Bahuns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chhetri" title="Chhetri">Chhetris</a> living further east. Kham Magars live higher up. A notable valley called Rukumkot lies near the district's geographical center.</p>
<p>Rapti Zone has a history of radical politics since the mid-20th century and in the 1990s became a center of the Maoist (<i>maobadi</i>) rebellion against the royal government and the fragile democracy that the late <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Birendra" title="King Birendra">King Birendra</a></i> eventually supported.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_Development">Economic Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rapti_Zone&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economic Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Historically, geographic isolation inhibited economic development in Rapti Zone. Trans-himalayan <a href="/wiki/Salt_road" title="Salt road">trade routes</a> lay to the east along the <i><a href="/wiki/Gandaki_River" title="Gandaki River">Kaligandaki River</a></i> and to the west along the <a href="/wiki/Bheri_River" title="Bheri River">Bheri</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ghaghara" title="Ghaghara"><i>Karnali</i></a>. Compared to these, a hypothetical transhimalayan trade route through Rapti would have been impeded by a wide Inner Terai malarial belt, by ridges up to 4,000 meters exiting the Rapti watershed, then by the Dhaulagiri Himalayas and the only comparatively lower <i>Kagmara</i> and Tibetan border ranges before reaching the Tibetan Plateau.</p>
<p>Elsewhere in Nepal the Outer Terai began developing in the mid 1930s because the <i><a href="/wiki/Rana_dynasty" title="Rana dynasty">Rana dynasty</a></i> sought limited industrial development and because development could easily spread from India across a border with little impediment. However, in Rapti Zone the border turned north to follow the base of the first range of foothills. Areas suited for development were confined to valleys beyond these low but rugged mountains. Furthermore, these valleys remained dangerously <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malarial" title="Malarial">malarial</a> until <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USAID" title="USAID">USAID</a> began spraying <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DDT#Use_against_malaria" title="DDT">DDT</a> in 1954.</p>
<p>In Rapti's Middle Hills the only economic activity of interest to the government in Kathmandu was cottage <a href="/wiki/Hashish" title="Hashish">hashish</a> production from scattered plantings of <i><a href="/wiki/Cannabis_indica" title="Cannabis indica">Cannabis indica</a></i>. Government agents carried <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">marijuana</a> and balls of <i><a href="/wiki/Charas" title="Charas">charas</a></i> to Kathmandu for retail and export to India. This trade ended in 1974 in response to U.S. pressure and inducements. USAID promoted temperate climate fruit and food crops as replacement cash crops, but this program failed due to lack of adequate transportation out of the hills. Otherwise there was limited local trade, no industry and hardly any agriculture beyond subsistence to tax, so the national government found little incentive for involvement in Rapti zone.</p>
<p>By the 1960s land-hungry hill people began to settle in the Dang and Deukhuri valleys and in the 1980s the east-west <i><a href="/wiki/Mahendra_Highway" title="Mahendra Highway">Mahendra Highway</a></i> was built across the country, followed by spur roads south to <i><a href="/wiki/Koilabas" title="Koilabas">Koilabas</a></i> and north to Pyuthan, Rolpa, Dang Valley, <i><a href="/wiki/Swargadwari" title="Swargadwari">Swargadwari</a></i> and Salyan. This enabled ordinary Nepalis to reach the rest of the country in a day or two by inexpensive buses instead of by limited, expensive <a href="/wiki/Nepal_Airlines" title="Nepal Airlines">air service</a> out of <a href="/wiki/Tribhuvannagar_Airport" title="Tribhuvannagar Airport">Dang airport</a> or roundabout routes through India using <a href="/wiki/Indian_Railways" title="Indian Railways">trains</a> and buses going east or west as well as several days on foot, so Rapti Zone's historic condition of isolation largely ended and then connection increased with the arrival of landline and cellular telephones, radio broadcasts and Internet.</p>
<p>The growing highway network replaced human porterage and greatly reduced transport costs. More goods were on sale locally, at lower prices. It also helped feed a <a href="/wiki/Diaspora#Asian_diaspora" title="Diaspora">diaspora</a> sending Nepalese youth and fathers off to India, the Middle East, Europe and even the U.S. for employment and education. Another effect was to foster invidious comparison between increasing amenities of <a href="/wiki/Bazaar" title="Bazaar">bazaar</a> towns along the spur roads versus unchanged austerity in the hinterlands above that were mainly populated by Kham Magar <a href="/wiki/Adivasi" title="Adivasi"><i>janajatis</i></a>. This added to historic grievances from government neglect and favoritism toward <a href="/wiki/Pahari_people" title="Pahari people">upper-caste hill Hindus</a>, so Kham living in the highlands became ripe for recruitment by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unified_Communist_Party_of_Nepal_(Maoist)" title="Unified Communist Party of Nepal (Maoist)"><i>Maobadi</i></a> movement and the national government was virtually pushed out of Rolpa and Rukum during the 1996-2006 <a href="/wiki/Nepalese_Civil_War" title="Nepalese Civil War">Nepalese Civil War</a>.</p>
