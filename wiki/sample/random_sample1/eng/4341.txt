<p><b>Llangefni</b> (<small>Welsh pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Welsh" title="Help:IPA for Welsh">[ɬanˈɡɛvni]</a></span>) is the <a href="/wiki/County_town" title="County town">county town</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Academy_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Academy-2">[2]</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Anglesey" title="Anglesey">Anglesey</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> and contains the principal offices of the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Anglesey_County_Council" title="Isle of Anglesey County Council">Isle of Anglesey County Council</a>. The <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2011" title="United Kingdom Census 2011">United Kingdom Census 2011</a> recorded Llangefni's population as 5,116 people, making it the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Anglesey_towns_by_population" title="List of Anglesey towns by population">second largest settlement on the island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ONS_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ONS-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Llangefni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is near the centre of Anglesey, and is on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Cefni" title="River Cefni">River Cefni</a>, after which it is named. Its attractions include the <a href="/wiki/Oriel_Ynys_M%C3%B4n" title="Oriel Ynys Môn">Oriel Ynys Môn</a> museum, which details the history of Anglesey and houses the legacy collection of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Tunnicliffe" title="Charles Tunnicliffe">Charles Tunnicliffe</a>. In the west of the town is a large secondary school, <a href="/wiki/Ysgol_Gyfun_Llangefni" title="Ysgol Gyfun Llangefni">Ysgol Gyfun Llangefni</a> (Llangefni Comprehensive School), and in the north a Victorian parish church, St Cyngar's, set in a wooded riverside location called <a href="/wiki/The_Dingle,_Anglesey" title="The Dingle, Anglesey">the Dingle</a>. The town was formerly named Llangyngar, Welsh for "St Cyngar's church".</p>

<p>Llangefni is a commercial and farming town in Anglesey and in the past Llangefni hosted the largest cattle market on the island.</p>
<p>There is a relatively large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_estate" title="Industrial estate">industrial estate</a> housing a large chicken processing plant - the largest single industrial operation in the town] and several other small businesses.</p>
<p>The town had a <a href="/wiki/Llangefni_railway_station" title="Llangefni railway station">station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Anglesey_Central_Railway" title="Anglesey Central Railway">Anglesey Central Railway</a> line which opened in 1864. It closed in 1964 although goods trains continued to pass through the town until 1993. Although no longer used, the tracks have not been removed. The nearest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Llanfairpwllgwyngyll_railway_station" title="Llanfairpwllgwyngyll railway station">station</a> is now at <a href="/wiki/Llanfairpwllgwyngyll" title="Llanfairpwllgwyngyll">Llanfairpwllgwyngyll</a>, five miles away <a href="/wiki/As_the_crow_flies" title="As the crow flies">as the crow flies</a>. There are frequent buses to the larger settlements of <a href="/wiki/Bangor,_Gwynedd" title="Bangor, Gwynedd">Bangor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Holyhead" title="Holyhead">Holyhead</a> as well as to the smaller towns of <a href="/wiki/Amlwch" title="Amlwch">Amlwch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beaumaris" title="Beaumaris">Beaumaris</a>. By road the town is just 2 kilometres from the major <a href="/wiki/A55_road" title="A55 road">A55</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A5_road" title="A5 road">A5</a> roads, via the short <a href="/wiki/A5114_road" title="A5114 road">A5114</a>. Water for the town comes from <a href="/wiki/Llyn_Cefni" title="Llyn Cefni">Llyn Cefni</a>, a reservoir 1.5 miles (2.4 km) to the northwest.</p>
<p>Llangefni hosted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Eisteddfod" title="National Eisteddfod">National Eisteddfod</a> in 1957 and 1983, and in 1999 gave its name to the Eisteddfod held at the nearby village of <a href="/wiki/Llanbedrgoch" title="Llanbedrgoch">Llanbedrgoch</a>. It also hosted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urdd" title="Urdd">Urdd</a> <a href="/wiki/Eisteddfod" title="Eisteddfod">Eisteddfod</a> (youth Eisteddfod) in 2004. The town also has a college, <a href="/wiki/Coleg_Menai" title="Coleg Menai">Coleg Menai</a> (Llangefni site).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Llangefni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The local <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> club, <a href="/wiki/Llangefni_Town_F.C." title="Llangefni Town F.C.">Llangefni Town</a>, was promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Premier_League" title="Welsh Premier League">Welsh Premier League</a> at the end of the 2006–07 season, but relegated one season later.</p>
<p>The local <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rugby_Union" title="Rugby Union">rugby</a> club is <a href="/wiki/Llangefni_RFC" title="Llangefni RFC">Llangefni RFC</a>, which plays in the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Rugby_Union" title="Welsh Rugby Union">WRU</a> leagues. The club recently gained promotion to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WRU_Division_Two_West" title="WRU Division Two West">Division 2 West</a>, but the WRU then decided to demote the club back to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WRU_Division_Four_North" title="WRU Division Four North">Division 4 North</a> Wales league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Welsh_Language_Skills">Welsh Language Skills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Llangefni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Welsh Language Skills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 2011 Census, Llangefni is the community with the highest percentage of <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a> speakers on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Anglesey_County_Council" title="Isle of Anglesey County Council">Isle of Anglesey</a>, and the 6th highest in Wales. 80.7% of residents aged three and over reported being able to speak Welsh in the 2011 Census, as compared to 83.8% reporting being able to do so in the 2001 Census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
