<p><b>Lang Lang</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">郎朗</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Láng Lǎng</span></i>; born 14 June 1982) is a popular Chinese concert <a href="/wiki/Pianist" title="Pianist">pianist</a> who has performed with leading orchestras in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and his native <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lang_Lang_(pianist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lang Lang was born in <a href="/wiki/Shenyang" title="Shenyang">Shenyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">China</a>. His father Lang Guoren is a descendant of the <a href="/wiki/Manchu_people" title="Manchu people">Manchu</a> <a href="/wiki/Niohuru" title="Niohuru">Niohuru</a> family, which brought forth a long line of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing" title="Qing">Qing</a> Empresses. The elder Lang is also a musician, who plays the traditional Chinese stringed instrument <a href="/wiki/Erhu" title="Erhu">erhu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> At the age of two, Lang watched the <i><a href="/wiki/Tom_and_Jerry" title="Tom and Jerry">Tom and Jerry</a></i> episode <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cat_Concerto" title="The Cat Concerto">The Cat Concerto</a></i> which features the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Rhapsody_No._2" title="Hungarian Rhapsody No. 2">Hungarian Rhapsody No. 2</a> by <a href="/wiki/Franz_Liszt" title="Franz Liszt">Franz Liszt</a>. According to Lang, this first contact with Western music is what motivated him to learn the <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He began piano lessons with Professor Zhu Ya-Fen at age three. At the age of five, he won first place at the Shenyang Piano Competition and performed his first public recital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amg_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-amg-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Lang Lang was nine years old, he intended to audition for Beijing's <a href="/wiki/Central_Conservatory_of_Music" title="Central Conservatory of Music">Central Conservatory of Music</a>, and, having difficulties with his lessons, was expelled from his piano tutor's studio for "lack of talent".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-looks_back_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-looks_back-5">[5]</a></sup> Another music teacher at his state school noticed Lang Lang's sadness, and decided to comfort him by playing a record of Mozart's <a href="/wiki/Piano_Sonata_No._10_(Mozart)" title="Piano Sonata No. 10 (Mozart)">Piano Sonata No. 10 in C major, K. 330</a>; she asked him to play along with the second movement. This reminded Lang Lang of his love of the instrument. "Playing the K. 330 brought me hope again," he recalled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-looks_back_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-looks_back-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lang Lang was later admitted into the conservatory where he studied under Professor Zhao Ping-Guo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1993, he won the Xing Hai Cup Piano Competition in Beijing and, in 1994, was awarded first prize for outstanding artistic performance at the fourth International Competition for Young Pianists in <a href="/wiki/Ettlingen" title="Ettlingen">Ettlingen</a>, Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amg_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-amg-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1995, at 13 years of age, he played the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89tudes_(Chopin)" title="Études (Chopin)">Op. 10 and Op. 25</a> <a href="/wiki/%C3%89tude" title="Étude">études</a> by <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Chopin" title="Frédéric Chopin">Chopin</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Concert_Hall" title="Beijing Concert Hall">Beijing Concert Hall</a> and, in the same year, won first place at the <a href="/wiki/International_Tchaikovsky_Competition_for_Young_Musicians" title="International Tchaikovsky Competition for Young Musicians">International Tchaikovsky Competition for Young Musicians</a> in Japan,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amg_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-amg-4">[4]</a></sup> playing Chopin's <a href="/wiki/Piano_Concerto_No._2_(Chopin)" title="Piano Concerto No. 2 (Chopin)">Piano Concerto No. 2</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Philharmonic_Orchestra" title="Moscow Philharmonic Orchestra">Moscow Philharmonic Orchestra</a> in a concert broadcast by <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK Television</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telarc_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-telarc-7">[7]</a></sup> When 14, he was a featured soloist at the <a href="/wiki/China_National_Symphony_Orchestra" title="China National Symphony Orchestra">China National Symphony</a>'s inaugural concert, which was broadcast by <a href="/wiki/China_Central_Television" title="China Central Television">China Central Television</a> and attended by President <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Zemin" title="Jiang Zemin">Jiang Zemin</a>.</p>
<p>In 1997, at 15 years of age, he and his father left for America and he began studies with <a href="/wiki/Gary_Graffman" title="Gary Graffman">Gary Graffman</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Institute_of_Music" title="Curtis Institute of Music">Curtis Institute of Music</a> in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Performing_and_recording_career">Performing and recording career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lang_Lang_(pianist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Performing and recording career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Katherine_McPhee.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1342" data-file-width="2016" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Katherine_McPhee.jpg/220px-Katherine_McPhee.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Katherine_McPhee.jpg/330px-Katherine_McPhee.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Katherine_McPhee.jpg/440px-Katherine_McPhee.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Katherine_McPhee.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lang Lang and singer <a href="/wiki/Katharine_McPhee" title="Katharine McPhee">Katharine McPhee</a> perform at the <a href="/wiki/National_Memorial_Day_Concert" title="National Memorial Day Concert">National Memorial Day Concert</a> in Washington, D.C., 24 May 2009</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Lang_Lang_discography" title="Lang Lang discography">Lang Lang discography</a></div>
<p>Lang Lang has given recitals and concerts in many major cities and was the first Chinese pianist to be engaged by the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Philharmonic" title="Berlin Philharmonic">Berlin Philharmonic</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Philharmonic" title="Vienna Philharmonic">Vienna Philharmonic</a> and some top American orchestras.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> A <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Tribune" title="Chicago Tribune">Chicago Tribune</a></i> music critic called him "the biggest, most exciting young keyboard talent I have encountered in many a year of attending piano recitals".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Lang Lang has been praised by musicians and critics around the world – the conductor <a href="/wiki/Jahja_Ling" title="Jahja Ling">Jahja Ling</a> remarked, "Lang Lang is special because of his total mastery of the piano... He has the flair and great communicative power."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Public_Radio" title="National Public Radio">National Public Radio</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Morning_Edition" title="Morning Edition">Morning Edition</a> remarked that "Lang Lang has conquered the classical world with dazzling technique and charisma."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It is often noted that Lang successfully straddles two worlds – classical prodigy and rock-like "superstar", a phenomenon summed up by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> journalist Emma Pomfret, who wrote, "I can think of no other classical artist who has achieved Lang Lang's broad appeal without dumbing down."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lang Lang's performance style was controversial when he stormed into the classical music scene in 1999. Since that time, Pianist <a href="/wiki/Earl_Wild" title="Earl Wild">Earl Wild</a> called him "the <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">J. Lo</a> of the piano." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Others have described him as immature, but admitted that his ability to "conquer crowds with youthful bravado" is phenomenal among classical musicians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WOW_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-WOW-16">[16]</a></sup> His maturity in subsequent years was reported by <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Yorker" title="The New Yorker">The New Yorker</a></i>: "The ebullient Lang Lang is maturing as an artist."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WOW_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-WOW-16">[16]</a></sup> In April 2009, when <a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)"><i>Time</i> Magazine</a> included Lang Lang in its list of the 100 most influential people, <a href="/wiki/Herbie_Hancock" title="Herbie Hancock">Herbie Hancock</a> described his playing as "so sensitive and so deeply human", commenting: "You hear him play, and he never ceases to touch your heart."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Time-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, after a sold-out <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a> debut with <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Temirkanov" title="Yuri Temirkanov">Yuri Temirkanov</a>, he traveled to Beijing with the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Orchestra" title="Philadelphia Orchestra">Philadelphia Orchestra</a> on a tour celebrating its 100th anniversary, during which he performed to an audience of 8,000 at the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hall_of_the_People" title="Great Hall of the People">Great Hall of the People</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DG_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-DG-18">[18]</a></sup> The same year, he made an acclaimed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_Proms" title="BBC Proms">BBC Proms</a> debut, prompting a music critic of the British newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> to write, "Lang Lang took a sold-out <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> by storm... This could well be history in the making".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DG_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-DG-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2003, he returned to the BBC Proms for the First Night concert with <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Slatkin" title="Leonard Slatkin">Leonard Slatkin</a>. After his recital debut with the Berlin Philharmonic, the <i><a href="/wiki/Berliner_Zeitung" title="Berliner Zeitung">Berliner Zeitung</a></i> wrote: "Lang Lang is a superb musical performer whose artistic touch is always in service of the music".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> However, recent reviews have been mixed. Lately, a plethora of music critics have protested against too much showmanship; not enough care; not enough sensitivity. But audiences continue to adore him. Lang Lang has become one of those artists whose career prospers outside the boundaries of critical approval. The pianist is bemused by the backlash: ""You get many good reviews from the beginning," he says, "and then the critics start criticising you. It's strange. The things they liked you for first - unique, fresh - they say is great. And then later they say you're too fresh, too unique. But they're the same thing!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lang Lang was the featured soloist on the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe</a> winning score of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Painted_Veil_(2006_film)" title="The Painted Veil (2006 film)">The Painted Veil</a></i> and can be heard on the soundtrack of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Banquet_(2006_film)" title="The Banquet (2006 film)">The Banquet</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFS_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-SFS-21">[21]</a></sup> He has recorded for the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Grammophon" title="Deutsche Grammophon">Deutsche Grammophon</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telarc" title="Telarc">Telarc</a> labels.. His album of the <a href="/wiki/Piano_Concerto_No._1_(Beethoven)" title="Piano Concerto No. 1 (Beethoven)">first</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piano_Concerto_No._4_(Beethoven)" title="Piano Concerto No. 4 (Beethoven)">fourth</a> Beethoven piano concertos with the <a href="/wiki/Orchestre_de_Paris" title="Orchestre de Paris">Orchestre de Paris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christoph_Eschenbach" title="Christoph Eschenbach">Christoph Eschenbach</a> debuted at No. 1 on the Traditional Classical Billboard Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2008, he was the pianist on <a href="/wiki/Mike_Oldfield" title="Mike Oldfield">Mike Oldfield</a>'s 2008 album <i><a href="/wiki/Music_of_the_Spheres_(Mike_Oldfield_album)" title="Music of the Spheres (Mike Oldfield album)">Music of the Spheres</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In 2010, he signed with <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> for a reported $3 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2008, Lang Lang partnered with <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> in the project <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Symphony_Orchestra" title="YouTube Symphony Orchestra">YouTube Symphony Orchestra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lang Lang has also recorded piano works for the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Gran_Turismo_5" title="Gran Turismo 5">Gran Turismo 5</a>'</i>s soundtrack, mostly under the "Classical" subgenre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> This included versions of <a href="/wiki/Danny_Boy" title="Danny Boy">Danny Boy</a>, Beethoven's <a href="/wiki/Piano_Sonata_No._8_(Beethoven)" title="Piano Sonata No. 8 (Beethoven)">8th Piano Sonata</a>, and one of the game's intro pieces, the third movement from <a href="/wiki/Piano_Sonata_No._7_(Prokofiev)" title="Piano Sonata No. 7 (Prokofiev)">Prokofiev's 7th Piano Sonata</a>.</p>
<p>Finally, he has performed for numerous international dignitaries including the former <a href="/wiki/Secretary-General_of_the_United_Nations" title="Secretary-General of the United Nations">Secretary-General of the United Nations</a> <a href="/wiki/Kofi_Annan" title="Kofi Annan">Kofi Annan</a>, President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">Queen Elizabeth II</a>, President <a href="/wiki/Hu_Jintao" title="Hu Jintao">Hu Jintao</a> of China, President <a href="/wiki/Horst_K%C3%B6hler" title="Horst Köhler">Horst Köhler</a> of Germany, <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Prince Charles</a>, as well as Russian Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>, French President <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Sarkozy" title="Nicolas Sarkozy">Nicolas Sarkozy</a> and Polish President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lech_Kaczynski" title="Lech Kaczynski">Lech Kaczynski</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="White_House_state_dinner">White House state dinner</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lang_Lang_(pianist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: White House state dinner">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the White House state dinner in honour of <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="President of the People's Republic of China">President of China</a> <a href="/wiki/Hu_Jintao" title="Hu Jintao">Hu Jintao</a> on 19 January 2011, one of the tunes Lang played was the song "<a href="/wiki/My_Motherland" title="My Motherland">My Motherland</a>" from the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Battle_on_Shangganling_Mountain" title="Battle on Shangganling Mountain">Battle on Shangganling Mountain</a></i>, an anti-imperialist film on the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>. The song's lyrics include the line "We deal with wolves with guns", which in the film referred indirectly to the United States Army.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WSJ_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-29">[29]</a></sup> Although the tune is popular and has lost much of its political and historical significance in China,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> the performance was said to be interpreted by some as insulting the USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In response to the controversy Lang Lang denied that he had intended to insult the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-32">[32]</a></sup> He later released a statement stating that he "selected this song because it has been a favorite of mine since I was a child. It was selected for no other reason but for the beauty of its melody."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-32">[32]</a></sup> White House spokesperson Tommy Vietor also responded by saying <i>My Motherland</i> is "widely known and popular in China for its melody. Lang Lang played the song without lyrics or reference to any political theme ....Any suggestion that this was an insult to the United States is just flat wrong."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abc_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-abc-33">[33]</a></sup></p>

<p>Lang Lang has performed at various open-air venues, including <a href="/wiki/Central_Park" title="Central Park">Central Park</a> New York, <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Bowl" title="Hollywood Bowl">Hollywood Bowl</a> Los Angeles, the <a href="/wiki/Ravinia_Festival" title="Ravinia Festival">Ravinia Festival</a> Chicago, Theaterplatz in <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a> and Derby Park <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-montalvo_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-montalvo-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2007, he played at a concert from the Teatro del Silenzio, <a href="/wiki/Lajatico" title="Lajatico">Lajatico</a>, Italy, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Bocelli" title="Andrea Bocelli">Andrea Bocelli</a>. He performed "Io ci sarò" with Bocelli, and Liszt's "Hungarian Rhapsody". The performance is available on a DVD entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Vivere_Live_in_Tuscany" title="Vivere Live in Tuscany">Vivere Live in Tuscany</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2007, Lang Lang performed at the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a> concert in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-montalvo_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-montalvo-35">[35]</a></sup> Collaborating with <a href="/wiki/Seiji_Ozawa" title="Seiji Ozawa">Seiji Ozawa</a>, he appeared at the New Year's Eve gala opening for the National Center for the Performing Arts in Beijing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFS_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-SFS-21">[21]</a></sup> He also participated in the opening concert at Munich's Olympic Stadium with <a href="/wiki/Mariss_Jansons" title="Mariss Jansons">Mariss Jansons</a>, marking the commencement of the 2006 <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> and, in a celebratory concert held the night before the last match of the 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_Cup" title="Euro Cup">Euro Cup</a> finals, Lang Lang played with the Vienna Philharmonic under <a href="/wiki/Zubin_Mehta" title="Zubin Mehta">Zubin Mehta</a> in front of <a href="/wiki/Sch%C3%B6nbrunn_Palace" title="Schönbrunn Palace">Schönbrunn Palace</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, an audience estimated at between one and four billion people saw Lang Lang's performance in Beijing's opening ceremony for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> where he was promoted as a symbol of the youth and future of China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> During these games, he was also featured on the German TV network <a href="/wiki/ZDF" title="ZDF">ZDF</a> and made several appearances on NBC's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Today_(NBC_program)" title="Today (NBC program)">The Today Show</a></i> Summer Olympics broadcasts. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics_Opening_Ceremony" title="2008 Summer Olympics Opening Ceremony">opening ceremony</a> he performed a melody from the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River_Cantata" title="Yellow River Cantata">Yellow River Cantata</a> with seven-year-old Li Muzi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Lang Lang also collaborated with a German band <a href="/wiki/Schiller_(band)" title="Schiller (band)">Schiller</a> to record "Time for Dreams", used to promote some coverage of the 2008 Olympics broadcast in Germany.</p>
<p>In February 2008, Lang Lang and jazz pianist <a href="/wiki/Herbie_Hancock" title="Herbie Hancock">Herbie Hancock</a> performed together at the <a href="/wiki/50th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="50th Annual Grammy Awards">50th Annual Grammy Awards</a>, playing <a href="/wiki/George_Gershwin" title="George Gershwin">George Gershwin</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Rhapsody_in_Blue" title="Rhapsody in Blue">Rhapsody in Blue</a></i>. The two were again brought in by <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines" title="United Airlines">United Airlines</a> for the reintroduction of their "It's Time to Fly" advertising campaign with a series of new animated commercials aired during the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In April 2008, he premiered <a href="/wiki/Tan_Dun" title="Tan Dun">Tan Dun</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Piano_Concerto_(Tan_Dun)" title="Piano Concerto (Tan Dun)">First Piano Concerto</a>, subtitled "The Fire".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Hancock and Lang Lang continued to collaborate with a world tour in summer 2009. Lang played at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="2009 Nobel Peace Prize">2009 Nobel Peace Prize</a> ceremony for US President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> and at the Nobel Peace Prize concert in Oslo the next day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lang Lang has made numerous TV appearances including <i>The Today Show</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">The Tonight Show with Jay Leno</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CBS_Early_Show" title="CBS Early Show">CBS Early Show</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/60_Minutes" title="60 Minutes">60 Minutes</a></i>. He has featured in publications including <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Yorker" title="The New Yorker">The New Yorker</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Esquire_(magazine)" title="Esquire (magazine)">Esquire</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Vogue_(magazine)" title="Vogue (magazine)">Vogue</a></i> (Germany), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/GQ" title="GQ">GQ</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Die_Welt" title="Die Welt">Die Welt</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Reader%27s_Digest" title="Reader's Digest">Reader's Digest</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)" title="People (magazine)">People</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Lang Lang holds the title of the first Ambassador of the <a href="/wiki/YouTube_Symphony_Orchestra" title="YouTube Symphony Orchestra">YouTube Symphony Orchestra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, he performed at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a> accompanied by <a href="/wiki/Marc_Yu" title="Marc Yu">Marc Yu</a>, an 11-year-old pianist and musical child prodigy from Pasadena, California, who made his Carnegie Hall debut at the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lang Lang was featured in the award-winning German-Austrian documentary <a href="/wiki/Pianomania" title="Pianomania">Pianomania</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> which was directed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lilian_Franck&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lilian Franck (page does not exist)">Lilian Franck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Cibis" title="Robert Cibis">Robert Cibis</a>. The film premiered theatrically in North America, Asia and throughout Europe, and is a part of the <a href="/wiki/Goethe-Institut" title="Goethe-Institut">Goethe-Institut</a> catalogue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, he was featured at the Carnegie Hall's China Festival and performed with the New York Philharmonic on New Year's Eve at Avery Fisher Hall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Lang Lang opened the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Last_Night_of_the_Proms" title="Last Night of the Proms">Last Night of the Proms</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> performing with the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Symphony_Orchestra" title="BBC Symphony Orchestra">BBC Symphony Orchestra</a>. He played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liszt" title="Liszt">Liszt</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Piano_Concerto_No._1_(Liszt)" title="Piano Concerto No. 1 (Liszt)">1st Piano Concerto</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chopin" title="Chopin">Chopin</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Andante_spianato_et_grande_polonaise_brillante" title="Andante spianato et grande polonaise brillante">Grande Polonaise Brillante</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2012, he played Liszt's <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Rhapsody_No._6" title="Hungarian Rhapsody No. 6">Hungarian Rhapsody No. 6</a> and Gershwin's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhapsody_In_Blue" title="Rhapsody In Blue">Rhapsody In Blue</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Jubilee_Concert" title="Diamond Jubilee Concert">Queen's Diamond Jubilee Concert</a> at <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_Palace" title="Buckingham Palace">Buckingham Palace</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> In 2012 Lang Lang gave a masterclass to a select few pianists at the Royal College of Music featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lara_%C3%96mero%C4%9Flu" title="Lara Ömeroğlu">Lara Ömeroğlu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_James_Bartlett" title="Martin James Bartlett">Martin James Bartlett</a>.</p>
<p>In January 2014, Lang Lang collaborated with heavy metal band <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> at the <a href="/wiki/56th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="56th Annual Grammy Awards">56th Annual Grammy Awards</a>, performing their iconic 1988 anti-war classic "<a href="/wiki/One_(Metallica_song)" title="One (Metallica song)">One</a>".</p>
<p>On 3 July 2014, Lang Lang played at <a href="/wiki/Byblos_International_Festival" title="Byblos International Festival">Byblos International Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Byblos" title="Byblos">Byblos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 September, he played with Korean artist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PSY" title="PSY">PSY</a> for opening ceremony of <a href="/wiki/2014_Asian_Games" title="2014 Asian Games">2014 Asian Games</a> in Incheon, South Korea.</p>
<p>On 18 October, Lang Lang performed at the Red Velvet Ball in St. Louis' Powell Symphony Hall.</p>
<p>On 8 February 2015, Lang Lang played with <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a> at the <a href="/wiki/57th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="57th Annual Grammy Awards">57th Annual Grammy Awards</a>, performing Pharrell's hit song "<a href="/wiki/Happy_(Pharrell_Williams_song)" title="Happy (Pharrell Williams song)">Happy</a>".</p>
<p>On 30 April 2015, Lang Lang performed at the Expo 2015 opening concert with Andrea Bocelli at Piazza del Duomo in Milan.</p>
<p>On 4 July 2015, Lang Lang performed "<a href="/wiki/Rhapsody_in_Blue" title="Rhapsody in Blue">Rhapsody in Blue</a>" during "<a href="/wiki/A_Capitol_Fourth" title="A Capitol Fourth">A Capitol Fourth</a>", a U.S. Independence Day celebration televised from <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>.</p>
<p>In a cameo appearance in the 30 December 2015 episode of "<a href="/wiki/Mozart_in_the_Jungle" title="Mozart in the Jungle">Mozart in the Jungle</a>", Lang Lang performed a version of <a href="/wiki/Franz_Liszt" title="Franz Liszt">Franz Liszt</a>’s "<a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Rhapsody_No._2" title="Hungarian Rhapsody No. 2">Hungarian Rhapsody No. 2</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Books">Books</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lang_Lang_(pianist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Books">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lang Lang's autobiography, <i>Journey of a Thousand Miles</i>, published by <a href="/wiki/Random_House" title="Random House">Random House</a> in eight languages, was released in the summer of 2008. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Delacorte_Press" title="Delacorte Press">Delacorte Press</a> also released a version of the autobiography specifically for younger readers, entitled <i>Playing with Flying Keys</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_outreach">Awards and outreach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lang_Lang_(pianist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Awards and outreach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lang Lang has received many awards and made many television appearances. His <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Grammophon" title="Deutsche Grammophon">DG</a> recording of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beethoven" title="Beethoven">Beethoven</a> Piano Concertos Nos. 1 and 4 with <a href="/wiki/Christoph_Eschenbach" title="Christoph Eschenbach">Christoph Eschenbach</a> was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> during the year of its release.</p>
<p>He appeared in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_magazine" title="Time magazine">Time magazine</a>'s 2009 list of the <a href="/wiki/Time_100" title="Time 100">100 Most Influential People in the World</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Time-17">[17]</a></sup> and in <a href="/wiki/Gramophone_(magazine)" title="Gramophone (magazine)">Gramophone</a> magazine's Hall of Fame in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> In 2008, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recording_Academy" title="Recording Academy">Recording Academy</a> named him their Cultural Ambassador to China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> More recently, Lang Lang has been chosen as an official worldwide ambassador to the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Expo_2010" title="Expo 2010">Shanghai Expo</a>. Lang Lang was appointed by the United Nations' Children's Fund (<a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a>) as an <a href="/wiki/List_of_UNICEF_Goodwill_Ambassadors" title="List of UNICEF Goodwill Ambassadors">International Goodwill Ambassador</a> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> The Chinese government selected him as a vice-president of the <a href="/wiki/All-China_Youth_Federation" title="All-China Youth Federation">All-China Youth Federation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a> reported that Lang Lang is "evangelical in his efforts to spread the popularity of classical music."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> In October 2008, he launched the Lang Lang International Music Foundation in New York with the support of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy" title="Grammy">Grammys</a> and <a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> In May 2009, Lang Lang and his three chosen scholars from the foundation – Charlie Liu, Anna Larsen, and Derek Wang, aged between eight and 10 years old – performed together on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oprah_Winfrey_Show" title="The Oprah Winfrey Show">The Oprah Winfrey Show</a></i>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2011, Lang Lang was engaged by <a href="/wiki/Telef%C3%B3nica" title="Telefónica">Telefónica</a> to make appearances concerning culture, technology, education and social commitment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 22 July 2012, Lang carried the London 2012 Olympic torch through <a href="/wiki/Hornchurch" title="Hornchurch">Hornchurch</a> on its Redbridge to Bexley leg. On 24 August 2012, he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Merit_of_the_Federal_Republic_of_Germany" title="Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany">Cross of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany</a> for his engagement in the <a href="/wiki/Schleswig-Holstein_Musik_Festival" title="Schleswig-Holstein Musik Festival">Schleswig-Holstein Musik Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 28, 2013, Lang Lang was chosen by Secretary-General <a href="/wiki/Ban_Ki-moon" title="Ban Ki-moon">Ban Ki-moon</a> to be a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Messenger_of_Peace" title="United Nations Messenger of Peace">United Nations Messenger of Peace</a> — a role he says is more important than his music because it can help improve the lives of children around the world through education.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>His Sony 'Chopin Album' received the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Echo_Klassik" title="Echo Klassik">Echo Klassik</a> Award.</p>
