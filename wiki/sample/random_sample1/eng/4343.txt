<p><b>Lambert Wilson</b> (born 3 August 1958) is a French actor. He is internationally known for his portrayal of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merovingian_(The_Matrix)" title="Merovingian (The Matrix)">The Merovingian</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Matrix_Reloaded" title="The Matrix Reloaded">The Matrix Reloaded</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Matrix_Revolutions" title="The Matrix Revolutions">The Matrix Revolutions</a></i>. Wilson is the son of <a href="/wiki/Georges_Wilson" title="Georges Wilson">Georges Wilson</a>, who was an actor, theatrical manager and director of the <a href="/wiki/Th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre_National_Populaire" title="Théâtre National Populaire">Théâtre National Populaire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lambert_Wilson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wilson screen tested for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Living_Daylights" title="The Living Daylights">The Living Daylights</a></i> (1987) for the role of <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a>, appearing in test footage opposite <a href="/wiki/Maryam_d%27Abo" title="Maryam d'Abo">Maryam d'Abo</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Bond_girl" title="Bond girl">Bond girl</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Living_Daylights" title="The Living Daylights">The Living Daylights</a></i>) as <a href="/wiki/Tatiana_Romanova" title="Tatiana Romanova">Tatiana Romanova</a>, re-enacting scenes from <i><a href="/wiki/From_Russia_with_Love_(film)" title="From Russia with Love (film)">From Russia with Love</a></i> (1963). Wilson was featured in a series of <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Klein" title="Calvin Klein">Calvin Klein</a> ads featuring <a href="/wiki/Christy_Turlington" title="Christy Turlington">Christy Turlington</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eternity_(perfume)" title="Eternity (perfume)">Eternity</a> in 1991, as well as a poster ad, years later in 1998.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Wilson released <i>Musicals</i> on the <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a> label in 1989 (re-issued in 2004), with <a href="/wiki/John_McGlinn" title="John McGlinn">John McGlinn</a> conducting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orchestre_Philharmonique_de_Monte-Carlo" title="Orchestre Philharmonique de Monte-Carlo">Orchestre Philharmonique de Monte-Carlo</a>. It features him singing songs of the American Musical Theatre catalogue, those well-known ("<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maria_(1956_song)" title="Maria (1956 song)">Maria</a>" from <i><a href="/wiki/West_Side_Story" title="West Side Story">West Side Story</a></i>, "There But For You Go I" from Lerner &amp; Loewe's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brigadoon_(musical)" title="Brigadoon (musical)">Brigadoon</a></i>, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_ABC_Caf%C3%A9" title="The ABC Café">The Cafe Song</a>" from <i><a href="/wiki/Les_Mis%C3%A9rables_(musical)" title="Les Misérables (musical)">Les Misérables</a></i>, "Johanna" from <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Sondheim" title="Stephen Sondheim">Stephen Sondheim</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sweeney_Todd:_The_Demon_Barber_of_Fleet_Street_(musical)" title="Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street (musical)">Sweeney Todd</a></i>), rare ("Love Song" from <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Weill" title="Kurt Weill">Kurt Weill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jay_Lerner" title="Alan Jay Lerner">Alan Jay Lerner</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Life_(musical)" title="Love Life (musical)">Love Life</a></i>, "It Must Be So" from <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Bernstein" title="Leonard Bernstein">Leonard Bernstein</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Candide_(musical)" title="Candide (musical)">Candide</a></i>, and "Silly People", which was cut from Sondheim's <i><a href="/wiki/A_Little_Night_Music" title="A Little Night Music">A Little Night Music</a></i>), and those in-between ("Finishing the Hat" from <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_in_the_Park_with_George" title="Sunday in the Park with George">Sunday in the Park with George</a></i>, "You Do Something to Me" from <a href="/wiki/Cole_Porter" title="Cole Porter">Cole Porter</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Fifty_Million_Frenchmen" title="Fifty Million Frenchmen">Fifty Million Frenchmen</a></i>, "Never Will I Marry" from <a href="/wiki/Frank_Loesser" title="Frank Loesser">Frank Loesser</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Greenwillow" title="Greenwillow">Greenwillow</a></i>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>He has directed stage presentations of <a href="/wiki/Alfred_de_Musset" title="Alfred de Musset">Alfred de Musset</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Les_Caprices_de_Marianne" title="Les Caprices de Marianne">Les Caprices de Marianne</a></i> starring Laure Marsac at Paris' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bouffes_du_Nord" title="Bouffes du Nord">Bouffes du Nord</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Jean_Racine" title="Jean Racine">Jean Racine</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B%C3%A9r%C3%A9nice" title="Bérénice">Bérénice</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Kristin_Scott_Thomas" title="Kristin Scott Thomas">Kristin Scott Thomas</a> and Didier Sandre at <a href="/wiki/Avignon" title="Avignon">Avignon</a> and then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chaillot" title="Chaillot">Chaillot</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In November 2012, he was selected as a member of the main competition jury at the 2012 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Film_Festival_of_Marrakech" title="International Film Festival of Marrakech">International Film Festival of Marrakech</a>. In April 2013, Wilson was invited by MINUSTAH (the United Nations Stabilization Mission in Haiti) to visit Haiti in the capacity of helping with various UN-backed environment and cultural programmes. He was the master of ceremonies for the opening and closing ceremonies of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2014 Cannes Film Festival">2014 Cannes Film Festival</a> and <a href="/wiki/2015_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2015 Cannes Film Festival">2015 Cannes Film Festival</a>.</p>
<p>In February 2016, he released a tribute album called <i>Wilson chante Montand</i> to the singer <a href="/wiki/Yves_Montand" title="Yves Montand">Yves Montand</a> to commemorate the 25th anniversary of the death of the artist. Among the 17 tracks on the album there is <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/What_Can_I_Do%3F_(%C3%89dith_Piaf_song)" title="What Can I Do? (Édith Piaf song)">Mais qu’est-ce que j’ai ?</a></i> which was composed by <a href="/wiki/Henri_Betti" title="Henri Betti">Henri Betti</a> with the lyrics by <a href="/wiki/%C3%89dith_Piaf" title="Édith Piaf">Édith Piaf</a> in 1947. The musical arrangements of the 17 songs were made by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bruno_Fontaine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bruno Fontaine (page does not exist)">Bruno Fontaine</a>.</p>
