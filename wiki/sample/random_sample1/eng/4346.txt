<p><b>Bertrada of Laon</b> (born between 710 and 727 – 12 July 783), also known as <b>Bertrada the Younger</b> or <b>Bertha Broadfoot</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cf." title="Cf.">cf.</a> Latin: <i>Regina pede aucae</i> i.e. the queen with the goose-foot), was a <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Frankish</a> queen. She was the wife of <a href="/wiki/Pepin_the_Short" title="Pepin the Short">Pepin the Short</a> and the mother of <a href="/wiki/Charlemagne" title="Charlemagne">Charlemagne</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carloman,_son_of_Pippin_III" title="Carloman, son of Pippin III">Carloman</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gisela_(daughter_of_Pepin_the_Short)" title="Gisela (daughter of Pepin the Short)">Gisela</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nickname">Nickname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bertrada_of_Laon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nickname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bertrada's nickname "Bertha Broadfoot" dates back to the 13th century, when it was used in <a href="/wiki/Adenes_Le_Roi" title="Adenes Le Roi">Adenes Le Roi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Trouv%C3%A8re" title="Trouvère">trouvère</a> <i>Li rouman de Berte aus grands piés</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchelerLe_Roi1874_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchelerLe_Roi1874-1">[1]</a></sup> The exact reason that Bertrada was given this nickname is unclear. It is possible that Bertrada was born with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clubfoot" title="Clubfoot">clubfoot</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPelletier201452_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPelletier201452-2">[2]</a></sup> although Adenes does not mention this in his poem.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchelerLe_Roi1874_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchelerLe_Roi1874-1">[1]</a></sup> The nickname might have been a reference to an ancient legend about a Germanic goddess named <a href="/wiki/Perchta" title="Perchta">Perchta</a>, to real and mythological queens named Bertha, or to several similarly-named Christian queens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGrimm18358_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGrimm18358-3">[3]</a></sup> Many myths and legends exist in Europe and Asia, in which clubfooted people are described as the link between the world of the living and the spirit world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGinzburgAymard1989206-251_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGinzburgAymard1989206-251-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bertrada_of_Laon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_ancestry">Early life and ancestry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bertrada_of_Laon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early life and ancestry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bertrada was born sometime between 710 and 727 in <a href="/wiki/Laon" title="Laon">Laon</a>, in today's <a href="/wiki/Aisne" title="Aisne">Aisne, France</a>, to Count <a href="/wiki/Charibert_of_Laon" title="Charibert of Laon">Charibert of Laon</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipani1989_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipani1989-5">[5]</a></sup> and Gisele of Aquitaine.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Charibert's father might have been related to Hugobertides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKeats-RohanSettipani200018_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKeats-RohanSettipani200018-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPinoteaude_Vaulchier200443_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPinoteaude_Vaulchier200443-7">[7]</a></sup> Charibert's mother was <a href="/wiki/Bertrada_of_Pr%C3%BCm" title="Bertrada of Prüm">Bertrada of Prüm</a>, who founded <a href="/wiki/Pr%C3%BCm_Abbey" title="Prüm Abbey">Prüm Abbey</a> along with Charibert. Bertrada of Prüm was possibly the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Theuderic_III" title="Theuderic III">Theuderic III</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipani1989_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipani1989-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_children">Marriage and children</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bertrada_of_Laon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Marriage and children">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bertrada married <a href="/wiki/Pepin_the_Short" title="Pepin the Short">Pepin the Short</a>, the son of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a>, the Frankish "<a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_the_Palace" title="Mayor of the Palace">Mayor of the Palace</a>", in 741. However, Pepin and Bertrada were too closely related for their marriage to be legal at that time; the union was not canonically sanctioned until 749, after the birth of Charlemagne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKurze18958_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKurze18958-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to French historian Léon Levillain, Bertrada was Pepin's first and only wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETessier1952_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETessier1952-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993180-187_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993180-187-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELevillain194455_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELevillain194455-11">[11]</a></sup> Other sources suggest that Pepin had previously married a "Leutberga" or "Leutbergie", with whom Pepin would have had five children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDucret2007_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDucret2007-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bertrada and Pepin are known to have had seven children: three sons and four daughters. Of these, <a href="/wiki/Charlemagne" title="Charlemagne">Charlemagne</a> (c. 742 – 814),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993188_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993188-13">[13]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carloman,_son_of_Pippin_III" title="Carloman, son of Pippin III">Carloman</a> (751 – 771)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185-14">[14]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gisela_(daughter_of_Pepin_the_Short)" title="Gisela (daughter of Pepin the Short)">Gisela</a> (757 – 811) survived to adulthood. Pepin, born in 756, died in his infancy in 762. Bertrada and Pepin also had <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Berthe_of_the_Franks&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Berthe of the Franks (page does not exist)">Berthe</a>, Adelaide, and Rothaide. Gisela became a nun at <a href="/wiki/Chelles_Abbey" title="Chelles Abbey">Chelles Abbey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185-187_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185-187-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pepin tried to divorce Bertrada a few years after their marriage, but the Pope opposed the divorce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBiblioth.C3.A8que_de_l.27.C3.89cole_des_chartes194359_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBiblioth.C3.A8que_de_l.27.C3.89cole_des_chartes194359-16">[16]</a></sup> The reason is still unknown, but according to historian <a href="/wiki/Christian_Settipani" title="Christian Settipani">Christian Settipani</a>, Pepin might have wanted to marry a woman named Angla, who was the daughter of Theodrade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Queen_of_the_Franks">Queen of the Franks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bertrada_of_Laon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Queen of the Franks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In 751, Pepin and Bertrada became King and Queen of the Franks, following Pepin's successful coup against the Frankish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merovingian" title="Merovingian">Merovingian</a> monarchs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEM.C3.A9moires_couronn.C3.A9s_et_autres_m.C3.A9moires_publi.C3.A9s_par_l.27Acad.C3.A9mie_royale_des_sciences.2C_des_lettres_et_des_beaux-arts_de_Belgique186197_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEM.C3.A9moires_couronn.C3.A9s_et_autres_m.C3.A9moires_publi.C3.A9s_par_l.27Acad.C3.A9mie_royale_des_sciences.2C_des_lettres_et_des_beaux-arts_de_Belgique186197-17">[17]</a></sup> Pepin was crowned in June 754, and Bertrada, Charlemagne, and Carloman were blessed by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Stephen_II" title="Pope Stephen II">Pope Stephen II</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993184_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993184-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBernard200491_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBernard200491-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>After Pepin's death in 768, Bertrada lost her title as Queen of the Franks. Charlemagne and Carloman inherited the two halves of Pepin's kingdom. Bertrada stayed at the court and often tried to stop arguments between the two brothers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185-14">[14]</a></sup> Some historians credit Bertrada's support for her elder son Charlemagne over her younger son Carloman, and her diplomatic skills, for Charlemagne's early success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELewis2008_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELewis2008-20">[20]</a></sup> Although her influence over Charlemagne may have diminished in time, she lived at his court, and, according to <a href="/wiki/Einhard" title="Einhard">Einhard</a>, their relationship was excellent. Bertrada recommended that Charlemagne set aside his legal wife, <a href="/wiki/Himiltrude" title="Himiltrude">Himiltrude</a>, and marry <a href="/wiki/Desiderata_of_the_Lombards" title="Desiderata of the Lombards">Desiderata</a>, a daughter of the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombard</a> king <a href="/wiki/Desiderius" title="Desiderius">Desiderius</a>, but Charlemagne soon divorced Desiderata. Einhard claims this was the only episode that ever strained relations between mother and son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_and_death">Later life and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bertrada_of_Laon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Later life and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bertrada retired from the court after Carloman's death in 771 to live in <a href="/wiki/Choisy-au-Bac" title="Choisy-au-Bac">Choisy-au-Bac</a>, where Charlemagne had set aside a royal house for her. Choisy-au-Bac was favorable because of its history of being the home and burial place of several Merovingian kings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185_14-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bertrada died on 12 July 783 in Choisy-au-Bac.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185_14-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipanivan_Kerrebrouck1993185-14">[14]</a></sup> Charlemagne buried her in the <a href="/wiki/Basilica_of_St_Denis" title="Basilica of St Denis">Basilica of St Denis</a> near Pepin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELes_gisants_de_la_basilique_de_Saint-Denis2014_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELes_gisants_de_la_basilique_de_Saint-Denis2014-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_literature">In literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bertrada_of_Laon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: In literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bertrada inspired Adenes Le Roi to write the trouvère <i>Li rouman de Berte aus grands piés</i> in 1270. Adenes referred to her as "Bertha Broadfoot", the earliest known usage of that nickname.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchelerLe_Roi1874_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchelerLe_Roi1874-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bertrada is also referred to as "Bertha Broadfoot" in <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Villon" title="François Villon">François Villon</a>'s 15th-century poem <i><a href="/wiki/Ballade_des_dames_du_temps_jadis" title="Ballade des dames du temps jadis">Ballade des dames du temps jadis</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVillonc._1460_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVillonc._1460-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
