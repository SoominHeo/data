<p><b>Lääne County</b> (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et"><i>Lääne maakond</i></span>), or <i><b>Läänemaa</b></i> (literally "Western land"; <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de"><i>Wiek</i></span>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <span lang="la"><i>Rotalia</i></span>), is one of 15 <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Estonia" title="Counties of Estonia">counties</a> of <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>. It is located in western Estonia and borders the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Harju_County" title="Harju County">Harju County</a> to the north-east, <a href="/wiki/Rapla_County" title="Rapla County">Rapla County</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A4rnu_County" title="Pärnu County">Pärnu County</a> to the south, and the island counties of <a href="/wiki/Saare_County" title="Saare County">Saare</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hiiu_County" title="Hiiu County">Hiiu</a> to the west. In January 2009 Lääne County had a population of 23,810 – constituting 2.0% of the total population in Estonia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-County_population_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-County_population-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="County_Government">County Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%A4%C3%A4ne_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: County Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=County_Governments_of_Estonia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="County Governments of Estonia (page does not exist)">County Government</a> (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et"><i>Maavalitsus</i></span>) is led by a <a href="/wiki/County_Governors_of_Estonia" title="County Governors of Estonia">Governor</a> (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et"><i>maavanem</i></span>), who is appointed by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Estonia" title="Government of Estonia">Government of Estonia</a> for a term of five years. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Municipalities">Municipalities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%A4%C3%A4ne_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Municipalities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The county is subdivided into <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Estonia" title="Municipalities of Estonia">municipalities</a>. There are one urban municipality, <a href="/wiki/Haapsalu" title="Haapsalu">Haapsalu</a> (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et"><i>linn</i></span> – town) and nine rural municipalities (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et"><i>vallad</i></span> – parishes) in Lääne County.</p>

<p>In the first centuries AD political and administrative subdivisions began to emerge in Estonia. Two larger subdivisions appeared: the parish (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et"><i>kihelkond</i></span>) and the county (<a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et"><i>maakond</i></span>). A parish consisted of several villages. Nearly all parishes had at least one fortress. The defense of the local area was directed by the highest official, the parish elder. A county was composed of several parishes, also headed by an elder. By the 13th century the following major districts had developed in Estonia: <a href="/wiki/Saare_County" title="Saare County">Saaremaa</a> (Osilia), Läänemaa (Rotalia or Maritima), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harjumaa" title="Harjumaa">Harjumaa</a> (Harria), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%C3%A4vala" title="Rävala">Rävala</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revalia" title="Revalia">Revalia</a>), <a href="/wiki/Virumaa" title="Virumaa">Virumaa</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vironia" title="Vironia">Vironia</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J%C3%A4rvamaa" title="Järvamaa">Järvamaa</a> (Jervia), <a href="/wiki/Sakala_County" title="Sakala County">Sakala</a> (Saccala), and Ugandi (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ugaunia" title="Ugaunia">Ugaunia</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Raun2001p11_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Raun2001p11-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Läänemaa</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <span lang="la"><i>Rotalia, Maritima</i></span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Raun2001p11_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Raun2001p11-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Low_German" title="Low German">Low German</a>: <i>Wiek</i>) was an <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independent</a> country on the east coast of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltic_sea" title="Baltic sea">Baltic sea</a>, bordered by <a href="/wiki/Revala" title="Revala">Revala</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harjumaa" title="Harjumaa">Harjumaa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alempois" title="Alempois">Alempois</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sackalia" title="Sackalia">Sakala</a>. Läänemaa had an area of approximately 1900 <a href="/wiki/Hide_(unit)" title="Hide (unit)">hides</a>. Early in 1220 troops from <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, initially led by King <a href="/wiki/John_I_of_Sweden" title="John I of Sweden">John I</a>, had invaded Läänemaa. The Swedish army took the <a href="/wiki/Lihula" title="Lihula">Lihula</a> <a href="/wiki/Fortification" title="Fortification">stronghold</a> and set up a small <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a>. Swedish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jarl_in_Sweden" title="Jarl in Sweden">Jarl</a> <a href="/wiki/Charles_the_Deaf" title="Charles the Deaf">Karl Döve</a> and Bishop <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karl_Magnusson" title="Karl Magnusson">Karl Magnusson</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Link%C3%B6ping" title="Bishop of Linköping">Linköping</a>, both from the powerful <a href="/wiki/House_of_Bjelbo" title="House of Bjelbo">House of Bjelbo</a>, also remained in the castle. On August 8, 1220 the united <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C5%92sel" title="Œsel">Œselian</a> and Rotalian armies encircled the castle at dawn. It was set ablaze in the course of the fierce battle that ensued. The Swedish troops tried to make their way out, but were killed on site apart from a few who succeeded in escaping to <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a>, held by <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>. The Jarl, the Bishop, and almost 500 other Swedes were killed, leaving no Swedish presence in Estonia at all. The short-lived Swedish attempt to gain foothold in Estonia was motivated by the quickly <a href="/wiki/Livonian_Crusade" title="Livonian Crusade">advancing Danish and Low German crusaders</a> who had been able to conquer most of the area in the early 13th century. Defeat in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lihula" title="Battle of Lihula">Battle of Lihula</a> discouraged the Swedish expansion to <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a> for more than 300 years, and the country was left for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teutonic_Knights" title="Teutonic Knights">Teutonic Knights</a>, Low German Bishops and Denmark to divide. In the meantime, Sweden focused on <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish-Novgorodian_Wars" title="Swedish-Novgorodian Wars">Swedish-Novgorodian Wars</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Livonian_Crusade" title="Livonian Crusade">Livonian Crusade</a> Läänemaa became the main territory of The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishopric_of_%C3%96sel-Wiek" title="Bishopric of Ösel-Wiek">Bishopric of Ösel-Wiek</a>, an independent Bishopric. Parts of the <a href="/wiki/Haapsalu_Castle" title="Haapsalu Castle">Bishop's castle</a> are still preserved in <a href="/wiki/Haapsalu" title="Haapsalu">Haapsalu</a>.</p>

<p>After the German conquest in 1227, Lääne County became the center of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishopric_of_%C3%96sel-Wiek" title="Bishopric of Ösel-Wiek">Bishopric of Ösel-Wiek</a>, an independent principality within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Livonian_Confederation" title="Livonian Confederation">Livonian Confederation</a>. Parts of <a href="/wiki/Haapsalu_Castle" title="Haapsalu Castle">Bishop's castle</a> are still preserved in <a href="/wiki/Haapsalu" title="Haapsalu">Haapsalu</a>.</p>
