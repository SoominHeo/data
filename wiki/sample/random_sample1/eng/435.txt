<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="October_26">October 26</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Korean_by-elections,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: October 26">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main focus of the October by-election was about filling the vacant seat of the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Seoul" title="Mayor of Seoul">mayor of Seoul</a>. The election was eventually won by an independent candidate <a href="/wiki/Park_Won-soon" title="Park Won-soon">Park Won-soon</a> against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_National_Party" title="Grand National Party">Grand National Party</a> candidate, <a href="/wiki/Na_Kyung-won" title="Na Kyung-won">Na Kyung-won</a>. On an anecdotal view, an unnamed representative of an electoral district in Seoul said that the general public mood against the Grand National Party was closely similar to political events in 2004, when former president <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roh_Mu-hyun" title="Roh Mu-hyun">Roh Mu-hyun</a> was on the verge of being impeached.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Korean_by-elections,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Blue_House">Blue House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Korean_by-elections,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Blue House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Blue_House" title="Blue House">Blue House</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Lee_Myung-bak_government" title="Lee Myung-bak government">Lee Myung-bak government</a> has been negatively impacted the most. President <a href="/wiki/Lee_Myung-bak" title="Lee Myung-bak">Lee Myung-bak</a> did not make any official commentary right after the election's result due to a real estate dispute and due to the growing younger voters' opposition against him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Grand_National_Party">Grand National Party</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Korean_by-elections,_2011&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Grand National Party">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/DDoS_attacks_during_the_October_2011_South_Korean_by-election" title="DDoS attacks during the October 2011 South Korean by-election">DDoS attacks during the October 2011 South Korean by-election</a></div>
<p>This October by-election is known to have a negative impact on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Right_wing" title="Right wing">right wing</a> political establishments in South Korea, notably <a href="/wiki/Lee_Myung-bak_government" title="Lee Myung-bak government">Lee Myung-bak government</a> and the Grand National Party. Grand National Party politician, <a href="/wiki/Jeon_Yeo-ok" title="Jeon Yeo-ok">Jeon Yeo-ok</a>, made a remark that "the Grand National Party is a party that betrays its supporters" after the loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The youth faction of the GNP made a suggestion to rename the party to another name to avoid criticisms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> There has been a growing pessimistic outlook for the Grand National Party. Former <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_South_Korea" title="List of Prime Ministers of South Korea">Prime Minister</a> and current head of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Presidential_Commission_for_Shared_Growth_for_Large_and_Small_Companies&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Presidential Commission for Shared Growth for Large and Small Companies (page does not exist)">Presidential Commission for Shared Growth for Large and Small Companies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chung_Un-chan" title="Chung Un-chan">Chung Un-chan</a>, mentioned in the GNP <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a> branch seminar on November 17 how "the GNP doesn't receive any affection" and "would most likely lose the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_presidential_election,_2012" title="South Korean presidential election, 2012">next presidential election</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Some high-profile GNP members deny that this by-election is a loss. For instance, chairperson <a href="/wiki/Hong_Jun-pyo" title="Hong Jun-pyo">Hong Jun-pyo</a> responded as "neither a win nor a loss".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Hong's comment later triggered backlashes among other GNP members, including <a href="/wiki/Won_Hee-ryong" title="Won Hee-ryong">Won Hee-ryong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
