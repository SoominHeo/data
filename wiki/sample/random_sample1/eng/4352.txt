<p><i><b>Random! Cartoons</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Nicktoons_(TV_channel)" title="Nicktoons (TV channel)">Nicktoons</a> <a href="/wiki/Animation" title="Animation">animation</a> showcase. Like <i><a href="/wiki/Oh_Yeah!_Cartoons" title="Oh Yeah! Cartoons">Oh Yeah! Cartoons</a></i>, it was created by <a href="/wiki/Fred_Seibert" title="Fred Seibert">Fred Seibert</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Frederator_Studios" title="Frederator Studios">Frederator Incorporated</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_Animation_Studio" title="Nickelodeon Animation Studio">Nickelodeon Animation Studio</a> for Nicktoons, and was the fourth of their cartoon "laboratories".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was premiered on December 6, 2008 and ended on December 20, 2009. The series aired in reruns on Nicktoons until September 1, 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Random!_Cartoons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Creator <a href="/wiki/Fred_Seibert" title="Fred Seibert">Fred Seibert</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> ordered thirteen full-length episodes (39 seven-minute shorts) for the show, originally announced in 2005 as <i><a href="/wiki/Oh_Yeah!_Cartoons" title="Oh Yeah! Cartoons">Oh Yeah! Cartoons</a></i> season four.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unlike <i>Oh Yeah!</i>, there is no host. On January 10, 2006, <a href="/wiki/ASIFA-Hollywood" title="ASIFA-Hollywood">ASIFA-Hollywood</a> hosted an advance screening of six shorts as well as a question and answer session with the filmmakers at the Nicktoons Studios in <a href="/wiki/Burbank,_California" title="Burbank, California">Burbank, California</a>. Two of the shorts were selected to become television series and one of the shorts was selected to become a web series. Those shorts were <i>Adventure Time</i>, <i>The Bravest Warriors</i>, and <i>Fanboy</i>. <i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time" title="Adventure Time">Adventure Time</a></i> began airing as a full-length series on April 5, 2010 on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>The Bravest Warriors</i>, under the new name <i><a href="/wiki/Bravest_Warriors" title="Bravest Warriors">Bravest Warriors</a></i>, began airing on November 8, 2012 on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Hangover" title="Cartoon Hangover">Cartoon Hangover</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <i>Fanboy</i>, under the new name <i><a href="/wiki/Fanboy_%26_Chum_Chum" title="Fanboy &amp; Chum Chum">Fanboy &amp; Chum Chum</a></i>, began airing as a full-length series on November 7, 2009 on <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>.</p>
<p>In late March 2013, the show returned to a daytime airing slot at 10:00–11:00 am after only being aired two times a month at 5:00 am for a long time on Nicktoons.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Random!_Cartoons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Frederator_Studios" title="Frederator Studios">Frederator Studios</a> has persisted in the tradition of surfacing independent animation voices. They've started several cartoon shorts "incubators" featuring new talent, characters, and series including <i><a href="/wiki/What_a_Cartoon!" title="What a Cartoon!">What a Cartoon!</a></i> (Cartoon Network, 1995), <i><a href="/wiki/Oh_Yeah!_Cartoons" title="Oh Yeah! Cartoons">Oh Yeah! Cartoons</a></i> (Nickelodeon, 1998), <i>The Meth Minute 39</i> (Channel Frederator, 2008),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Too_Cool!_Cartoons" title="Too Cool! Cartoons">Too Cool! Cartoons</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Hangover" title="Cartoon Hangover">Cartoon Hangover</a>, 2012), and <i>GO! Cartoons</i> (<a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Hangover" title="Cartoon Hangover">Cartoon Hangover</a>, 2016).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> These laboratories have spun off notable series like <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dexter%E2%80%99s_Laboratory" title="Dexter’s Laboratory">Dexter’s Laboratory</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Powerpuff_Girls" title="The Powerpuff Girls">The Powerpuff Girls</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Johnny_Bravo" title="Johnny Bravo">Johnny Bravo</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cow_%26_Chicken" title="Cow &amp; Chicken">Cow &amp; Chicken</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Courage_the_Cowardly_Dog" title="Courage the Cowardly Dog">Courage the Cowardly Dog</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fairly_OddParents" title="The Fairly OddParents">The Fairly OddParents</a></i>, <i>Nite Fite</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fanboy_%26_Chum_Chum" title="Fanboy &amp; Chum Chum">Fanboy &amp; Chum Chum</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time" title="Adventure Time">Adventure Time</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bravest_Warriors" title="Bravest Warriors">Bravest Warriors</a></i>, <i>Rocket Dog</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Bee_and_PuppyCat" title="Bee and PuppyCat">Bee and PuppyCat</a></i>.</p>
