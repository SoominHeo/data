<p><b>Randall Thomas Davidson, 1st Baron Davidson of Lambeth</b>, <span style="font-size:100%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knight_Grand_Cross_of_the_Royal_Victorian_Order" title="Knight Grand Cross of the Royal Victorian Order">GCVO</a>, <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">PC</a></span> (7 April 1848 â 25 May 1930) was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishop</a> of Scottish origin who served as <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishop of Canterbury">Archbishop of Canterbury</a> from 1903 to 1928.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_education">Background and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Davidson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Davidson was the son of Henry Davidson, a grain merchant, of <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> and Henrietta, daughter of John Campbell Swinton of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kimmerghame" title="Kimmerghame">Kimmerghame</a>; his parents were Scottish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presbyterian" title="Presbyterian">Presbyterians</a>. His education was mostly in small private schools that he later described as unsatisfactory and long lamented his lack of proficiency in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>. Later, he studied at <a href="/wiki/Harrow_School" title="Harrow School">Harrow</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Brooke_Westcott" title="Brooke Westcott">Brooke Westcott</a> was his final year housemaster, and at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Oxford" title="Trinity College, Oxford">Trinity College, Oxford</a>. In his final year of schooling, he was involved in a shooting accident that threatened the loss of his leg. According to his own account, it was only much later in his life, after the discovery and use of X-ray technology, that it was found that a considerable number of shotgun pellets still remained in his body. The accident left him with a hernia and he was a lifelong truss wearer, this caused him continuous difficulty as the hernia regularly dropped, especially when he was preaching.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thepeerage.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-thepeerage.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ministry">Ministry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Davidson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ministry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Davidson served as <a href="/wiki/Chaplain" title="Chaplain">chaplain</a> to <a href="/wiki/Archibald_Campbell_Tait" title="Archibald Campbell Tait">Archibald Campbell Tait</a> when Tait was <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishop of Canterbury">Archbishop of Canterbury</a>. He later married Tait's daughter. After Tait's death, Davidson remained at <a href="/wiki/Lambeth_Palace" title="Lambeth Palace">Lambeth Palace</a> as chaplain to the succeeding Archbishop of Canterbury, <a href="/wiki/Edward_White_Benson" title="Edward White Benson">Edward White Benson</a>. A favourite of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>, Davidson was appointed <a href="/wiki/Dean_of_Windsor" title="Dean of Windsor">Dean of Windsor</a> at a very young age. The Queen relied heavily on him for advice regarding church appointments and only reluctantly agreed to his preferment to episcopal office. He was nominated, <a href="/wiki/Canonical_election" title="Canonical election">elected</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Confirmation_of_bishops" title="Confirmation of bishops">confirmed</a> as <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Rochester" title="Bishop of Rochester">Bishop of Rochester</a> before his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Episcopal_ordination" title="Episcopal ordination">episcopal ordination</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edward_Benson_(bishop)" title="Edward Benson (bishop)">Edward Benson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishop of Canterbury">Archbishop of Canterbury</a>, on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Mark" title="St Mark">St Mark</a>'s day (25 April) 1891 at <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey" title="Westminster Abbey">Westminster Abbey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He later served as <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Winchester" title="Bishop of Winchester">Bishop of Winchester</a> between 1895<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and 1903 before becoming Archbishop of Canterbury in 1903. He played a major part in the funeral ceremonies for Queen Victoria in 1901, taking care, along with <a href="/wiki/Sir_James_Reid,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir James Reid, 1st Baronet">James Reid</a>, of the wake at <a href="/wiki/Osborne_House" title="Osborne House">Osborne House</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight" title="Isle of Wight">Isle of Wight</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Archbishop_of_Canterbury">Archbishop of Canterbury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Davidson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Archbishop of Canterbury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Randall_Thomas_Davidson.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="537" data-file-width="237" height="498" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Randall_Thomas_Davidson.png/220px-Randall_Thomas_Davidson.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2f/Randall_Thomas_Davidson.png 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Randall_Thomas_Davidson.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Davidson caricatured by <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Ward" title="Leslie Ward">Spy</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(British_magazine)" title="Vanity Fair (British magazine)">Vanity Fair</a>, 1901</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Davidson was appointed Archbishop of Canterbury in 1903 and remained in office until his resignation in 1928. He was the longest holder of the office since the <a href="/wiki/English_Reformation" title="English Reformation">English Reformation</a>. He was also the first Archbishop of Canterbury to retire, all his predecessors having died in office. Roger Lloyd, Church of England historian, thought that Davidson was one of the two or three greatest Archbishops of Canterbury. Others have remarked that Davidson's memory for people was prodigious.</p>
<p>Davidson reacted to the papal bull <i><a href="/wiki/Apostolicae_curae" title="Apostolicae curae">Apostolicae curae</a></i> by stressing "the strength and depth of the Protestantism of England" and regarded other differences with Rome as much more important than its views on Anglican orders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> This view seems to have been widely held at the time, judging from the reaction of Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Vaughan" title="Herbert Vaughan">Herbert Vaughan</a>, Roman Catholic <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Westminster" title="Archbishop of Westminster">Archbishop of Westminster</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 October 1919 Davidson reluctantly agreed to receive a delegation of the South African National Native Congress (later the African National Congress or ANC) led by <a href="/wiki/Sol_Plaatje" title="Sol Plaatje">Solomon Plaatje</a> who came to England to petition the imperial government against the notorious 1913 Natives' Land Act in South Africa. The meeting at Lambeth Palace was made possible by the intervention of The Rev John Harris, energetic organizing secretary of the Anti-Slavery Society (APS). Behind the scenes Harris was advised by Davidson that he "should wish it, however, to be perfectly clear that the interview has no official character, that it must be regarded as absolutely private, and that no account of it must appear in print.' This, Davidson clearly emphasized was to 'avoid any misunderstanding his position in such matters'. Davidson warned Harris in no uncertain words that he must not be expected to give an answer of any formal kind whatsoever. For the SANNC, the interview with Davidson was a great disappointment. Not only was Davidson pressed for time, but not in the least willing to speak out against the hardship brought about by 1913 Natives' Land Act.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Davidson presided over two <a href="/wiki/Lambeth_Conference" title="Lambeth Conference">Lambeth Conferences</a> and was present at five of the first six, Bell's biography suggests that at least part of the reason for his resignation was his natural reluctance to face the work and worry of the seventh conference, eventually held in 1930. Davidson resigned after <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Common_Prayer" title="Book of Common Prayer">Prayer Book</a> revision failed to pass the House of Commons in 1928. As there was no procedure for resignation a commission of three bishops was hastily appointed to receive his letter of resignation and convey it to the King.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Davidson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Davidson was appointed a Knight Commander of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Victorian_Order" title="Royal Victorian Order">Royal Victorian Order</a> (KCVO) in 1902,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Most_Honourable_Privy_Council" title="Her Majesty's Most Honourable Privy Council">Privy Counsellor</a> in 1903 and a Knight Grand Cross of the Royal Victorian Order (GCVO) in 1904.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Davidson was also awarded the Royal Victorian Chain for his service as Archbishop of Canterbury.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randall_Davidson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Davidson married Edith, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Archibald_Campbell_Tait" title="Archibald Campbell Tait">Archibald Campbell Tait</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishop of Canterbury">Archbishop of Canterbury</a>, in 1878. They had no children.</p>
<p>He died in May 1930, aged 82, when his barony as Lord Davidson of Lambeth became extinct. His wife died in June 1936.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thepeerage.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-thepeerage.com-1">[1]</a></sup> Both are buried in the cloister garden at <a href="/wiki/Canterbury_Cathedral" title="Canterbury Cathedral">Canterbury Cathedral</a>.</p>
