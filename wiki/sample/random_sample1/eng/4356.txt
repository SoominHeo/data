<p><b>Randall "Randy" Duane Couture</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ʊər/ 'our' in 'tour'">ʊər</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born June 22, 1963) is an American <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, retired <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial artist</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Collegiate_wrestling" title="Collegiate wrestling">collegiate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greco-Roman_wrestling" title="Greco-Roman wrestling">Greco-Roman wrestler</a>. During his tenures in the <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Fighting_Championship" title="Ultimate Fighting Championship">Ultimate Fighting Championship</a> (UFC), Couture became a three-time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Heavyweight_Championship" title="UFC Heavyweight Championship">UFC Heavyweight Champion</a>, two-time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Light_Heavyweight_Championship" title="UFC Light Heavyweight Championship">UFC Light Heavyweight Champion</a>, an interim UFC Light Heavyweight Champion and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_UFC_champions#tournament_winners" title="List of UFC champions">UFC 13 Heavyweight Tournament Winner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Couture is the first of only three fighters to hold two UFC championship titles in two different divisions (along with <a href="/wiki/B.J._Penn" title="B.J. Penn">B.J. Penn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conor_McGregor" title="Conor McGregor">Conor McGregor</a>).</p>
<p>Couture has competed in a record 15 title fights. He holds the most title reigns in the UFC with five. His last fight with <a href="/wiki/Lyoto_Machida" title="Lyoto Machida">Lyoto Machida</a> marked his 24th fight in the UFC, the tenth largest number of fights in the UFC (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Bisping" title="Michael Bisping">Michael Bisping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frank_Mir" title="Frank Mir">Frank Mir</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tito_Ortiz" title="Tito Ortiz">Tito Ortiz</a> are tied for first with 27). Couture is the fourth member of the <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Fighting_Championship" title="Ultimate Fighting Championship">UFC Hall of Fame</a>. He is the only person over the age of 40 to have won a UFC championship fight, having done so four times. Couture has also been one of the few MMA champions to have recovered a title he had previously lost, and is the only fighter to have achieved it three times (twice at Heavyweight, once at Light Heavyweight).</p>
<p>Couture was an <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic</a> wrestling alternate and has lived in <a href="/wiki/Corvallis,_Oregon" title="Corvallis, Oregon">Corvallis, Oregon</a>, throughout much of his career, where he served as an assistant wrestling coach and a strength and conditioning coach for <a href="/wiki/Oregon_State_University" title="Oregon State University">Oregon State University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He established <a href="/wiki/Team_Quest" title="Team Quest">Team Quest</a> with <a href="/wiki/Matt_Lindland" title="Matt Lindland">Matt Lindland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dan_Henderson" title="Dan Henderson">Dan Henderson</a>, a training camp for fighters, based out of <a href="/wiki/Gresham,_Oregon" title="Gresham, Oregon">Gresham, Oregon</a>, and headed by coach Robert Folis. In 2005, Couture moved to Las Vegas, where he opened his own extensive chain of gyms under the name <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xtreme_Couture" title="Xtreme Couture">Xtreme Couture</a>. Couture partnered with <a href="/wiki/Bas_Rutten" title="Bas Rutten">Bas Rutten</a> to open Legends Gym in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollywood,_California" title="Hollywood, California">Hollywood, California</a>.</p>
<p>Couture is generally recognized as a <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts#Strategies.23Clinch_fighting" title="Mixed martial arts">clinch</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ground-and-pound" title="Ground-and-pound">ground-and-pound</a> fighter who uses his wrestling ability to execute <a href="/wiki/Takedown_(grappling)" title="Takedown (grappling)">take downs</a>, establish <a href="/wiki/Grappling_position" title="Grappling position">top position</a> and successively strike the opponent on the bottom. Couture has displayed a variety of skills in <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catch_wrestling" title="Catch wrestling">catch wrestling</a> and has <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Submission_(combat_sport_term)" title="Submission (combat sport term)">submitted</a> four opponents using different <a href="/wiki/Chokehold" title="Chokehold">chokeholds</a>. Couture is the only athlete in UFC history to win a championship after becoming a <a href="/wiki/UFC_Hall_of_Fame" title="UFC Hall of Fame">Hall of Fame</a> member and is the oldest title holder ever in the UFC and MMA.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Couture was born in <a href="/wiki/Everett,_Washington" title="Everett, Washington">Everett, Washington</a>, the son of Sharan Amelia (née King) and Edward Lewis "Ed" Couture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He wrestled at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alderwood_Middle_School" title="Alderwood Middle School">Alderwood Middle School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lynnwood,_Washington" title="Lynnwood, Washington">Lynnwood, Washington</a>, then moved on to <a href="/wiki/Lynnwood_High_School" title="Lynnwood High School">Lynnwood High School</a>, where he won a State Championship during his senior year in wrestling. He served in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Army" title="U.S. Army">U.S. Army</a> from 1982 to 1988, attaining the rank of Sergeant in the <a href="/wiki/101st_Airborne_Division" title="101st Airborne Division">101st Airborne</a>, where he "wrestled and did a little boxing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> While he was in the Army he applied for tryouts with the U.S. Army Freestyle Wrestling team; however due to a clerical error his application was sent to the Greco-Roman tryouts and rather than wait until the next year he decided to pursue it.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Despite never having competed in Greco-Roman he made the team.</p>
<p>Upon discharge, Couture became a three-time Olympic team alternate (1988, 1992 and 1996), a semifinalist at the 2000 Olympic Trials, a three-time <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">NCAA</a> Division I <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All-American" title="All-American">All-American</a> and a two-time NCAA Division I runner-up (1991 and 1992) at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oklahoma_State_University" title="Oklahoma State University">Oklahoma State University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1992, he was the Division I runner-up at 190 pounds, coming in second after <a href="/wiki/Mark_Kerr_(fighter)" title="Mark Kerr (fighter)">Mark Kerr</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He was working as a wrestling coach at <a href="/wiki/Oregon_State_University" title="Oregon State University">Oregon State University</a> when he decided to compete in MMA.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mixed_martial_arts_career">Mixed martial arts career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mixed martial arts career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ultimate_Fighting_Championship_and_RINGS">Ultimate Fighting Championship and RINGS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ultimate Fighting Championship and RINGS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Being called to the tournament as an alternative on three weeks notice,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Couture made his professional mixed martial arts debut at <a href="/wiki/UFC_13" title="UFC 13">UFC 13</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> in May 1997, as part of a four-man <a href="/wiki/Heavyweight" title="Heavyweight">Heavyweight</a> tournament. His first opponent was <a href="/wiki/Tony_Halme" title="Tony Halme">Tony Halme</a>, who outweighed him by nearly 100 pounds (45 kg; 7.1 st). Couture immediately hit a double-leg takedown and, after some ground and pound, moved to <a href="/wiki/Back_mount" title="Back mount">back mount</a> and secured a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rear-naked_choke" title="Rear-naked choke">rear-naked choke</a> submission to win in under a minute. In the tournament final, he defeated Steven Graham, another larger opponent (290 lb), by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Technical_knockout" title="Technical knockout">TKO</a> at 3:13 into the first round.</p>
<p>On October 17, 1997, at <a href="/wiki/UFC_15" title="UFC 15">UFC 15</a>, Couture fought <a href="/wiki/Vitor_Belfort" title="Vitor Belfort">Vitor Belfort</a> to determine the number one contender for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Heavyweight_Championship" title="UFC Heavyweight Championship">UFC Heavyweight Championship</a>. Couture was an underdog, as 19-year-old Belfort was the <a href="/wiki/UFC_12" title="UFC 12">UFC 12 Heavyweight Tournament Champion</a>, winning all of his matches with devastating punches. After circling away from Belfort's left hand, Couture got the <a href="/wiki/Clinch_fighting" title="Clinch fighting">clinch</a>. The fighters broke up and, when Belfort attempted a flurry of punches, Couture hit a takedown. He immediately gained <a href="/wiki/Side_control" title="Side control">side control</a> and landed strikes. As Belfort scrambled to his feet, Couture landed knee strikes. He clinched again and wore Belfort down with dirty boxing. By the 7-minute mark, Belfort was exhausted. Couture again took him down, and finished him with punches from back mount, for one of the biggest upsets in MMA at the time.</p>
<p>At <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Japan" title="UFC Japan">UFC Japan</a> on December 21, Couture challenged the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Heavyweight_Championship" title="UFC Heavyweight Championship">UFC Heavyweight Champion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Smith_(kickboxer)" title="Maurice Smith (kickboxer)">Maurice Smith</a> to his second title defense since winning the belt from <a href="/wiki/Mark_Coleman" title="Mark Coleman">Mark Coleman</a> earlier that year. It was a slow-paced fight, and neither fighter significantly damaged the other, but Couture hit several takedowns and held positional control throughout the fight. After 21 minutes, he won a majority decision and became the new UFC Heavyweight Champion.</p>
<p>In 1998, UFC matchmakers wanted Couture to defend the belt against Bas Rutten, former <a href="/wiki/Pancrase" title="Pancrase">King of Pancrase</a>. Couture instead signed with <a href="/wiki/Vale_Tudo_Japan" title="Vale Tudo Japan">Vale Tudo Japan</a>, and was stripped of the title. He had his first and only match against <a href="/wiki/Shooto" title="Shooto">Shooto</a> veteran and grappling especialist <a href="/wiki/Enson_Inoue" title="Enson Inoue">Enson Inoue</a>. After taking the fight to the ground, Couture tapped out to an armbar, just over 90 seconds into the bout.</p>
<p>Still in Japan, Couture was signed up with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fighting_Network_RINGS" title="Fighting Network RINGS">Fighting Network RINGS</a>, debuting against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mikhail_Illoukhine" title="Mikhail Illoukhine">Mikhail Illoukhine</a> on March 20, 1999, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RINGS" title="RINGS">RINGS</a>. Couture submitted to a <a href="/wiki/Armlock" title="Armlock">kimura</a>, though the loss was controversial due to Illoukhine locking the hold while they were being re-positioned on the center of the ring. After that loss, he took a break from MMA to focus on his amateur wrestling career.</p>
<p>Couture returned to MMA in October 2000, for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RINGS" title="RINGS">RINGS King of Kings Tournament 2000</a>. He won a unanimous decision over UFC veteran <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Horn" title="Jeremy Horn">Jeremy Horn</a> in his first fight, and then another over <a href="/wiki/Pancrase" title="Pancrase">Pancrase</a> veteran <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryushi_Yanagisawa" title="Ryushi Yanagisawa">Ryushi Yanagisawa</a>. These two wins qualified him for the final event of the tournament, in March 2001. Before that, he was offered a shot at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Heavyweight_Championship" title="UFC Heavyweight Championship">UFC Heavyweight Championship</a> against <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Randleman" title="Kevin Randleman">Kevin Randleman</a> on Nov. 17, 2000. He was taken down in the first two rounds, but defended well from his back, negating most of Randleman's ground and pound attempts. In the third round, he tripped Randleman to the mat and landed several strikes from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Full_mount" title="Full mount">full mount</a> for a TKO victory and his second UFC Heavyweight Championship.</p>
<p>In March 2001, Couture continued in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RINGS" title="RINGS">RINGS King of Kings Tournament 2000 Final</a>. After winning an even decision over veteran <a href="/wiki/Tsuyoshi_Kohsaka" title="Tsuyoshi Kohsaka">Tsuyoshi Kohsaka</a> in the first fight, he got caught in a <a href="/wiki/Guillotine_choke" title="Guillotine choke">guillotine choke</a> by <a href="/wiki/Valentijn_Overeem" title="Valentijn Overeem">Valentijn Overeem</a> after committing himself in a takedown and had to submit. <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Rodrigo_Nogueira" title="Antônio Rodrigo Nogueira">Antônio Rodrigo Nogueira</a> won the tournament, and Couture returned to the UFC.</p>
<p>His first title defense was against Brazilian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kickboxer" title="Kickboxer">kickboxer</a> <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rizzo" title="Pedro Rizzo">Pedro Rizzo</a>, at <a href="/wiki/UFC_31" title="UFC 31">UFC 31</a>. This was the first UFC event under <a href="/wiki/Zuffa" title="Zuffa">Zuffa</a> management, with <a href="/wiki/Dana_White" title="Dana White">Dana White</a> as the new president. Both fighters inflicted substantial damage. After five 5-minute rounds, Couture won a close unanimous decision. Some fans felt Rizzo had won, so the UFC set up an immediate rematch for <a href="/wiki/UFC_34" title="UFC 34">UFC 34</a>, in November 2001. This time, Couture won decisively by TKO in the third round.</p>
<p>His third title defense was in March 2002, against up-and-comer <a href="/wiki/Josh_Barnett" title="Josh Barnett">Josh Barnett</a>. In the second round, Barnett mounted Couture and landed several strikes to win the title by TKO. After the fight, it was revealed Barnett had tested positive for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anabolic_steroids" title="Anabolic steroids">anabolic steroids</a>. He was subsequently stripped of the title and cut from the UFC.</p>
<p>Couture then faced <a href="/wiki/Ricco_Rodriguez" title="Ricco Rodriguez">Ricco Rodriguez</a> for the vacant UFC Heavyweight Championship at <a href="/wiki/UFC_39" title="UFC 39">UFC 39</a>, in late 2002. After dominating the first three rounds, 39-year-old Couture became noticeably fatigued. In the fifth round, Rodriguez took him down and landed an elbow strike to his orbital bone, breaking it and making him submit. This was the first time a UFC fight had finished in the fifth round.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Move_to_light_heavyweight_and_trilogy_with_Chuck_Liddell">Move to light heavyweight and trilogy with Chuck Liddell</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Move to light heavyweight and trilogy with Chuck Liddell">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Randy_Couture_vs._Chuck_Liddell" title="Randy Couture vs. Chuck Liddell">Randy Couture vs. Chuck Liddell</a></div>
<p>After two consecutive losses to larger opponents in the <a href="/wiki/Heavyweight_(MMA)" title="Heavyweight (MMA)">Heavyweight</a> division, Couture moved down to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_Heavyweight_(MMA)" title="Light Heavyweight (MMA)">Light Heavyweight</a> division. In his Light Heavyweight debut, he fought long-time number one contender <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Liddell" title="Chuck Liddell">Chuck Liddell</a> for the UFC Interim Light Heavyweight Championship. He was again the underdog but, after outstriking Liddell for three rounds, took the fight to the ground and won by TKO via strikes from full mount,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> becoming the first UFC fighter to win titles in two weight classes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naturaltv-bio_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-naturaltv-bio-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>His next match, against five-time defending champion <a href="/wiki/Tito_Ortiz" title="Tito Ortiz">Tito Ortiz</a>, was billed as a "Champion vs. Champion" fight. 40-year-old Couture won a unanimous decision to become the undisputed UFC Light Heavyweight Champion.</p>
<p>Couture's first title defense was against <a href="/wiki/Vitor_Belfort" title="Vitor Belfort">Vitor Belfort</a>, whom he had previously defeated in 1997 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_15" title="UFC 15">UFC 15</a>. In the first round, as Couture closed the distance to attempt a clinch, Belfort grazed his right eye with a left hook. His glove opened a cut, and Belfort was declared the winner when the cageside doctor advised the fight be stopped. A rubber match took place later that year. Couture dominated all three rounds before winning by doctor stoppage due to a cut, and became a two-time UFC Light Heavyweight Champion.</p>
<p>On April 16, 2005, in a rematch with Liddell, Couture lost his title and suffered the first knockout loss of his career. He came back in August to defeat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Van_Arsdale" title="Mike Van Arsdale">Mike Van Arsdale</a> and reestablish himself as a top contender. He faced Liddell for the third and final time in a championship match at <a href="/wiki/UFC_57" title="UFC 57">UFC 57</a>, on February 4, 2006. He was knocked out in the second round and, immediately afterwards, announced his retirement from MMA.</p>

<p>On June 24, 2006, on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Fighter_3_Finale" title="The Ultimate Fighter 3 Finale">The Ultimate Fighter 3 Finale</a>, Couture became the fourth inductee to the <a href="/wiki/UFC_Hall_of_Fame" title="UFC Hall of Fame">UFC Hall of Fame</a>, joining <a href="/wiki/Royce_Gracie" title="Royce Gracie">Royce Gracie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dan_Severn" title="Dan Severn">Dan Severn</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ken_Shamrock" title="Ken Shamrock">Ken Shamrock</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After retiring from MMA, Couture became a regular broadcast commentator for UFC events and co-host of <i>Before the Bell</i> and <i>After the Bell</i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Fight_Network" title="The Fight Network">The Fight Network</a>. He appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Rob_Schneider" title="Rob Schneider">Rob Schneider</a> movie <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Stan" title="Big Stan">Big Stan</a></i>, with fellow mixed martial artists <a href="/wiki/Don_Frye" title="Don Frye">Don Frye</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Sapp" title="Bob Sapp">Bob Sapp</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 17, 2006, Couture fought and drew with <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_jiu-jitsu" title="Brazilian jiu-jitsu">Brazilian jiu-jitsu</a> champion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ronaldo_de_Souza" title="Ronaldo de Souza">Ronaldo "Jacare" Souza</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Submission_wrestling" title="Submission wrestling">submission wrestling</a> contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> After the match Couture invited Souza to train at his gym. Souza accepted and started training at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xtreme_Couture" title="Xtreme Couture">Xtreme Couture</a> in Las Vegas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Couture was featured in the season two premiere episode of the Spike TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Pros_vs._Joes" title="Pros vs. Joes">Pros vs. Joes</a></i>, which aired January 25, 2007. His teammates on the episode were <a href="/wiki/Michael_Irvin" title="Michael Irvin">Michael Irvin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Willis" title="Kevin Willis">Kevin Willis</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Canseco" title="José Canseco">José Canseco</a>. He returned for the finale, where he took part in a <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">football</a>-based round. His teammates were Willis, <a href="/wiki/Randall_Cunningham" title="Randall Cunningham">Randall Cunningham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Smith_(defensive_end)" title="Bruce Smith (defensive end)">Bruce Smith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roy_Jones_Jr." title="Roy Jones Jr.">Roy Jones Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Hardaway" title="Tim Hardaway">Tim Hardaway</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Reclaiming_the_Heavyweight_Championship">Reclaiming the Heavyweight Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reclaiming the Heavyweight Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>On January 11, 2007, Couture announced his return from retirement in an interview on the Spike TV magazine show, "Inside the UFC".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In a conversation with <a href="/wiki/Joe_Rogan" title="Joe Rogan">Joe Rogan</a>, he confirmed he would face <a href="/wiki/Tim_Sylvia" title="Tim Sylvia">Tim Sylvia</a> for the UFC Heavyweight Championship at <a href="/wiki/UFC_68" title="UFC 68">UFC 68</a>, on March 3, 2007. He also announced he had signed a four-fight, two-year deal with the UFC.</p>
<p>At the age of 43, Couture defeated Sylvia by unanimous decision to become UFC Heavyweight Champion for a third time (a UFC record).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Couture's first punch, eight seconds into the fight, sent the <span class="nowrap">6 ft 8 in</span> (2.03 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meter" title="Meter">m</a>) Sylvia to the mat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He controlled the pace of the fight for five rounds, smothering Sylvia with strikes and numerous takedowns. All three judges scored the bout 50–45 for Couture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>At <a href="/wiki/UFC_74" title="UFC 74">UFC 74</a> on August 25, 2007, Couture successfully defended the title against Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Gonzaga" title="Gabriel Gonzaga">Gabriel Gonzaga</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> defeating him via TKO by strikes. Couture suffered a broken left arm when he blocked one of Gonzaga's kicks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-411Mania_Broken_Arm_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-411Mania_Broken_Arm-22">[22]</a></sup> The kick cleanly split his <a href="/wiki/Ulna" title="Ulna">ulna</a>, requiring him to wear a <a href="/wiki/Splint_(medicine)" title="Splint (medicine)">splint</a> for six weeks.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Resignation_and_dispute_with_the_UFC">Resignation and dispute with the UFC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Resignation and dispute with the UFC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On October 11, 2007, Couture announced he was severing all ties with the UFC, leaving two contracted fights, a position as an on-air analyst and the UFC Heavyweight Championship behind. He received $250,000 (plus $936,000 of PPV revenue) for his comeback fight with <a href="/wiki/Tim_Sylvia" title="Tim Sylvia">Tim Sylvia</a>. He received a $250,000 purse for defeating <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Gonzaga" title="Gabriel Gonzaga">Gabriel Gonzaga</a> (plus a $35,000 "Fight of the Night" bonus and $787,000 in PPV revenue).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He complained <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Liddell" title="Chuck Liddell">Chuck Liddell</a> was allegedly paid more than he was, despite losing his previous two fights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Couture cited the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC" title="UFC">UFC</a>'s failure to sign #1 ranked <a href="/wiki/Heavyweight_(MMA)" title="Heavyweight (MMA)">Heavyweight</a> fighter <a href="/wiki/Fedor_Emelianenko" title="Fedor Emelianenko">Fedor Emelianenko</a>, as well as disputes with UFC management, for his decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Transition from Fighter to Coach</b> Randy coached Oregon Fighter Musio Chavez for the Inter-collegiate Fight League.</p>
<p>On October 18, 2007, UFC president Dana White said Couture remained the UFC Heavyweight Champion despite his plans to quit, and reiterated he would not release Couture from the final two fights on his contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Couture held a press conference on October 25, 2007, where he denied his departure from UFC was a "retirement", set forth his grievances about his pay, and reiterated his belief that he would be free from any contractual obligations to the UFC after nine months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On October 30, 2007, White and <a href="/wiki/Zuffa" title="Zuffa">Zuffa</a> CEO Lorenzo Fertitta held another press conference, where White reiterated the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC" title="UFC">UFC</a>'s position of twelve days prior,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and released documents refuting Couture's claims about the pay he received.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>White and Couture had a "good" conversation at <a href="/wiki/UFC_78" title="UFC 78">UFC 78</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> They met again on Thanksgiving weekend to discuss a possible UFC return, but Couture said he had no desire to do so at the time.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Cornerman" title="Cornerman">Cornering</a> some of his fighters from <a href="/wiki/Xtreme_Couture_Mixed_Martial_Arts" title="Xtreme Couture Mixed Martial Arts">Xtreme Couture</a> at <a href="/wiki/HDNet_Fights" title="HDNet Fights">HDNet Fights</a> on December 15 in Dallas, Texas, Couture answered questions about <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fedor_Emilianenko" title="Fedor Emilianenko">Fedor Emilianenko</a>, stating that he would like to fight him in October, once his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC" title="UFC">UFC</a> contract expired, if the UFC could not come to a co-promotion agreement with <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/M-1_Global" title="M-1 Global">M-1 Global</a> beforehand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 15, 2008, Zuffa filed a lawsuit in <a href="/wiki/Nevada_District_Courts" title="Nevada District Courts">Clark County District Court</a> in Nevada, citing breach of contract and irreparable damage,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> seeking over $10,000 in damages. This suit only concerned Couture's employment contract and not his promotional contract. On February 28, 2008, Judge Jennifer Togliatti handed down the first ruling in the case, issuing a <a href="/wiki/Preliminary_injunction" title="Preliminary injunction">preliminary injunction</a> barring Couture from participating in an <a href="/wiki/International_Fight_League" title="International Fight League">IFL</a> event held the following day.</p>
<p>On August 2, 2008, a Texas appeals court granted Zuffa's request for a stay against a motion for a declaratory judgment in a suit filed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HDNet" title="HDNet">HDNet</a> regarding Couture's contractual status with the UFC. The stay effectively ended the dispute in the state of Texas, and Zuffa was allowed to move forward with the Nevada suit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_the_UFC">Return to the UFC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Return to the UFC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On September 2, 2008, the UFC announced it had signed Couture a new three-fight contract. On November 15, he returned at <a href="/wiki/UFC_91" title="UFC 91">UFC 91</a> in Las Vegas, Nevada, where he lost the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Heavyweight_Championship" title="UFC Heavyweight Championship">UFC Heavyweight Championship</a> to <a href="/wiki/Brock_Lesnar" title="Brock Lesnar">Brock Lesnar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> At first a closely contested match, Lesnar knocked Couture down in the second round and struck him with hammerfists for a TKO victory at 3:07.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In a post-fight interview with <a href="/wiki/Joe_Rogan" title="Joe Rogan">Joe Rogan</a>, Couture declared his desire to keep fighting and said he felt he was still becoming a better fighter, blaming the loss on his performance, not his age.</p>
<p>On August 29, 2009, Couture faced former UFC Interim Heavyweight Champion and former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Pride_Champions#World_Heavyweight_Championship" title="List of Pride Champions">PRIDE Heavyweight Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Rodrigo_Nogueira" title="Antônio Rodrigo Nogueira">Antônio Rodrigo Nogueira</a> at <a href="/wiki/UFC_102" title="UFC 102">UFC 102</a> in Portland, Oregon,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> and lost a unanimous decision. After the bout, Couture stated he felt he was in the best shape of his life, and would wait and see what the UFC had in store for him. The fight received the "Fight of the Night" award.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_light_heavyweight_and_retirement">Return to light heavyweight and retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Return to light heavyweight and retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After the Nogueira loss, Couture signed a new six-fight deal, which superseded his previous contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 14, 2009, at <a href="/wiki/UFC_105" title="UFC 105">UFC 105</a>, Couture faced <a href="/wiki/Brandon_Vera" title="Brandon Vera">Brandon Vera</a>. Vera landed effective strikes and scored a takedown, but Couture won a somewhat controversial unanimous decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The fight was his first at Light Heavyweight since losing to Chuck Liddell in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> With the win, the 46-year-old Couture became the oldest fighter to win a UFC bout.</p>

<p>Couture fought fellow UFC Hall of Famer <a href="/wiki/Mark_Coleman" title="Mark Coleman">Mark Coleman</a> at <a href="/wiki/UFC_109" title="UFC 109">UFC 109</a>. The bout marked the first time UFC Hall of Famers fought each other in the UFC. They were scheduled to meet at <a href="/wiki/UFC_17" title="UFC 17">UFC 17</a> in 1998, but an injury to Couture forced the cancellation of the bout. They wrestled each other in a freestyle match at the 1989 Olympic Festival at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oklahoma_State_University" title="Oklahoma State University">Oklahoma State University</a>; Coleman won the match by one point. Couture modified his training for this bout, focusing on catch wrestling and refining his boxing under coach Gil Martinez.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The combined age of these fighters (91) is the highest in any UFC match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Couture made Coleman pass out to a rear-naked choke submission in the second round, getting his first submission win in over four years.</p>
<p>Couture was scheduled to fight <a href="/wiki/Rich_Franklin" title="Rich Franklin">Rich Franklin</a> at <a href="/wiki/UFC_115" title="UFC 115">UFC 115</a>, but Franklin instead fought Chuck Liddell, replacing Liddell's original opponent, <a href="/wiki/Tito_Ortiz" title="Tito Ortiz">Tito Ortiz</a>. Couture instead faced three-time boxing world champion <a href="/wiki/James_Toney" title="James Toney">James Toney</a> at <a href="/wiki/UFC_118" title="UFC 118">UFC 118</a>. Couture dominated Toney, taking him down and mounting him within seconds, and quickly making him submit to an <a href="/wiki/Arm_triangle_choke" title="Arm triangle choke">arm triangle choke</a>. Many felt that this fight had been made as an attempt to repair damage done to the credibility of MMA after <a href="/wiki/Ray_Mercer" title="Ray Mercer">Ray Mercer</a> knocked out former UFC Heavyweight Champion <a href="/wiki/Tim_Sylvia" title="Tim Sylvia">Tim Sylvia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Couture had stated he was interested in fighting either <a href="/wiki/Lyoto_Machida" title="Lyoto Machida">Lyoto Machida</a> or <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Rua" title="Maurício Rua">Maurício Rua</a> (in a non-title bout). Since Rua had an upcoming title defense against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jon_Jones_(fighter)" title="Jon Jones (fighter)">Jon Jones</a>, UFC matchmakers gave him Machida. They fought on April 30, 2011, at <a href="/wiki/UFC_129" title="UFC 129">UFC 129</a>, before 55,000 fans in Toronto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Couture had stated before the bout it would be his final fight. Machida knocked him out in the second round with a jumping <a href="/wiki/Front_kick" title="Front kick">front kick</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jumpingfrontkick_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-jumpingfrontkick-47">[47]</a></sup> After the fight, Couture announced he was "finally done fighting", at the age of 47.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bellator_Fighting_Championships">Bellator Fighting Championships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bellator Fighting Championships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 29, 2013, Couture signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bellator_Fighting_Championships" title="Bellator Fighting Championships">Bellator</a> to be a coach in the first season of their reality show, <i><a href="/wiki/Fight_Master:_Bellator_MMA" title="Fight Master: Bellator MMA">Fight Master: Bellator MMA</a></i> which debuted in June 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Couture also did commentary for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bellator_96" title="Bellator 96">Bellator 96</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Couture made a cameo appearance on the season finale of the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Unit" title="The Unit">The Unit</a></i>, as a military guard. He played fight commentator Terry Flynn in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Redbelt" title="Redbelt">Redbelt</a></i>. He appeared on an episode of <a href="/wiki/History_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="History (U.S. TV channel)">The History Channel</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Human_Weapon" title="Human Weapon">Human Weapon</a></i> on September 27, 2007, and starred in the 2008 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Scorpion_King_2:_Rise_of_a_Warrior" title="The Scorpion King 2: Rise of a Warrior">The Scorpion King 2: Rise of a Warrior</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb.com_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-imdb.com-50">[50]</a></sup> He played Toll Road in the 2010 movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Expendables_(2010_film)" title="The Expendables (2010 film)">The Expendables</a></i> and reprised the role in the <a href="/wiki/The_Expendables_2" title="The Expendables 2">2012</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Expendables_3" title="The Expendables 3">2014 sequels</a>. He did several <a href="/wiki/Public_service_announcement" title="Public service announcement">public service announcements</a>, one against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crystal_meth" title="Crystal meth">crystal meth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> He starred alongside <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">Bruce Willis</a> in the 2011 film, <i><a href="/wiki/Setup_(2011_film)" title="Setup (2011 film)">Setup</a></i>, as an enforcer named Petey. In 2012, he played the leading role of hard-bitten cop Paul Ross in Brandon Nutt's action film <i>Hijacked</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Dominic_Purcell" title="Dominic Purcell">Dominic Purcell</a>.</p>
<p>On September 4, 2014, it was announced that Couture would be one of the celebrities competing on the <a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._season_19)" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 19)">19th season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. TV series)">Dancing with the Stars</a></i>. He paired with professional dancer <a href="/wiki/Karina_Smirnoff" title="Karina Smirnoff">Karina Smirnoff</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> The couple was eliminated on the third week of competition finishing in eleventh place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>Couture has appeared in 3 episodes of the TV series <a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Five-0_(2010_TV_series)" title="Hawaii Five-0 (2010 TV series)">Hawaii Five-0</a> as Jason Duclair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Randy_Couture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Couture was previously married to Sharon, Tricia, and Kim Couture (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N%C3%A9e" title="Née">née</a> Borrego).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> He and Kim filed for divorce in May 2009. He continued to coach her and support her MMA career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> He has three children: sons <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Couture" title="Ryan Couture">Ryan</a> and Caden, and daughter Aimee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> Asked if MMA could be improved, he suggested health insurance, retirement plans as well as more equal compensation regarding other athletes and sports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> He lives in Las Vegas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> He enjoys hunting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
