<p><b>Ransom Riggs</b> (born 1979) is an American writer and filmmaker best known for the book <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Peregrine%27s_Home_for_Peculiar_Children" title="Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children">Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ransom_Riggs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Riggs was born in <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a> in 1979 on a 200-year-old farm, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, where he attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pine_View_School_for_the_Gifted" title="Pine View School for the Gifted">Pine View School for the Gifted</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RasnomRiggsBio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-RasnomRiggsBio-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Medley_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Medley-2">[2]</a></sup> He studied <a href="/wiki/English_literature" title="English literature">English literature</a> at <a href="/wiki/Kenyon_College" title="Kenyon College">Kenyon College</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and studied film at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rife_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rife-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ransom_Riggs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His work on short films for the Internet and blogging for <a href="/wiki/Mental_Floss" title="Mental Floss">Mental Floss</a> got him a job writing <i>The Sherlock Holmes Handbook</i> which was released as a <a href="/wiki/Tie-in" title="Tie-in">tie-in</a> to the 2009 <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)" title="Sherlock Holmes (2009 film)">Sherlock Holmes</a></i> film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rife_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rife-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Riggs had collected curious <a href="/wiki/Vernacular_photography" title="Vernacular photography">vernacular photographs</a> and approached his publisher, <a href="/wiki/Quirk_Books" title="Quirk Books">Quirk Books</a>, about using some of them in a <a href="/wiki/Picture_book" title="Picture book">picture book</a>. On the suggestion of an editor, Riggs used the photographs as a guide from which to put together a narrative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rife_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rife-4">[4]</a></sup> The resulting book was <i>Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children</i>, which made <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times_Best_Seller_list" title="The New York Times Best Seller list">The New York Times Best Seller list</a>, and was adapted into the 2016 film <a href="/wiki/Miss_Peregrine%27s_Home_for_Peculiar_Children_(film)" title="Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children (film)">of the same name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another book inspired by old photographs, <i>Talking Pictures</i> was published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harper_Collins" title="Harper Collins">Harper Collins</a> in October 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Hollow_City_(novel)" title="Hollow City (novel)">Hollow City: The Second Novel of Miss Peregrine's Children</a></i> was released on January 14, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The third installment in the Miss Peregrine's series, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Library_of_Souls" title="Library of Souls">Library of Souls</a></i>, was announced in early 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deseretnews.com_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-deseretnews.com-8">[8]</a></sup> It was released on September 22, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deseretnews.com_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-deseretnews.com-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ransom_Riggs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Riggs currently lives in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica, California</a> and in 2013 married author <a href="/wiki/Tahereh_Mafi" title="Tahereh Mafi">Tahereh Mafi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
