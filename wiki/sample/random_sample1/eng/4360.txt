<p><b>Ralph Anthony Hill</b> (December 26, 1908 – October 17, 1994) was an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> runner. He set an American record over the mile in 1930 and won a silver medal in the 5000 m event at the 1932 Olympics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hill studied at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oregon" title="University of Oregon">University of Oregon</a> when competing in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1932_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1932 Summer Olympics">1932 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> on the 5000 m. In an exciting race on August 5, 1932, he came in second behind <a href="/wiki/Lauri_Lehtinen" title="Lauri Lehtinen">Lauri Lehtinen</a>, with each runner recording a time of 14:30.0. The judges deliberated for an hour before they could decide not to disqualify Lehtinen, as he had appeared to block Hill twice. Hill refused to file a protest, stating that he believed Lehtinen's obstruction of him was accidental. <a href="/wiki/Lauri_Virtanen" title="Lauri Virtanen">Lauri Virtanen</a> came in third 14 seconds behind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-sr-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After college Hill made a career of farming near Klamath Falls. The local <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henley_High_School_(Oregon)" title="Henley High School (Oregon)">Henley High School</a> renamed its football field in September 1992 after its alumnus Hill.</p>
