<p><b>Kim Nam-joon</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">김남준</span>; born September 12, 1994), better known as <b>Rap Monster</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> rapper, songwriter, and record producer. He is the leader and main rapper of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_pop_idol" title="Korean pop idol">South Korean boy group</a> <a href="/wiki/BTS_(band)" title="BTS (band)">BTS</a>, managed under <a href="/wiki/Big_Hit_Entertainment" title="Big Hit Entertainment">Big Hit Entertainment</a>. In 2015, he released his first solo <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>, <i>RM</i>. Rap Monster is notable for his large input in songwriting and production in BTS's discography. He has over 60 songs accredited to his name by the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Music_Copyright_Association" title="Korea Music Copyright Association">Korea Music Copyright Association</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rap_Monster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rap Monster was born on September 12, 1994, in <a href="/wiki/Ilsan" title="Ilsan">Ilsan</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and has one younger sister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Before his debut with <a href="/wiki/BTS_(band)" title="BTS (band)">BTS</a>, he was an underground rapper under the stage name "Runch Randa". He released a number of tracks and once collaborated with fellow underground rapper <a href="/wiki/Zico_(rapper)" title="Zico (rapper)">Zico</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-INTERVIEW_Bangtan_Boys_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-INTERVIEW_Bangtan_Boys-3">[3]</a></sup> He is part of the underground Korean hip hop crew called Daenamhyup, consisting of the members Marvel J, l11ven, Supreme Boi, Iron, Kyum2, Kidoh, Samsoon, Illipse and DJ Snatch. The group had been active from 2009 until 2013, although Rap Monster has not participated in recent years to concentrate on BTS. The activities seem to have stopped with some members participating in a new crew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Artist_Spotlight:_DaeNamHyup_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Artist_Spotlight:_DaeNamHyup-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Rap Monster has many notable academic achievements. He achieved an 850 on the <a href="/wiki/TOEIC" title="TOEIC">TOEIC</a> when he was in junior high.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He was part of the top 1.3% of the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">nation</a> in the preparatory university entrance exams for language, math, foreign language and social studies, and has an <a href="/wiki/Intelligence_quotient" title="Intelligence quotient">IQ</a> of 148.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Alongside his native language Korean, he is also fluent in English and Japanese as Rap Monster continued studying Japanese after debut since all Big Hit trainees received basic Japanese tuitions. He watched the popular American sitcom <a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a> multiple times to help him learn English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rap_Monster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BTS">BTS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rap_Monster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: BTS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/BTS_(band)" title="BTS (band)">BTS (band)</a></div>
<p>Rap Monster was recruited as BTS's first member back in 2010 during a Big Hit talent audition. Rap Monster trained for years with two other underground rappers, Jung Ho Seok (J-Hope) and Min Yoon Gi (Suga).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On June 13, 2013, Rap Monster made his debut as a member of <a href="/wiki/BTS_(band)" title="BTS (band)">BTS</a> on <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a></i> with the track "No More Dream" from their debut single album <i>2 Cool 4 Skool</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He has produced and written lyrics for a variety of tracks on all of BTS's albums.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_work">Solo work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rap_Monster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Solo work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rap Monster has collaborated with many respected Korean and American artists. On March 4, 2015, he released a single with <a href="/wiki/Warren_G" title="Warren G">Warren G</a> entitled, "P.D.D (Please Don't Die)" alongside its music video, which featured behind-the-scene footage of their collaboration in LA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In an interview for <i>Hiphopplaya</i> webzine, Rap Monster received praise from Warren G, who stated: <i>"If I comment from an artist and a producer point of view, Rap Monster is one of the best rappers I know. The way he delivers his rap is outstanding"</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.5BINTERVIEW.5D_Even_if_You_say_Bad_things.2CI_really_hope_you_would_listen_to_the_mixtape_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-.5BINTERVIEW.5D_Even_if_You_say_Bad_things.2CI_really_hope_you_would_listen_to_the_mixtape-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 31, 2014, <a href="/wiki/BTS_(band)" title="BTS (band)">BTS</a> performed an intro performance on <a href="/wiki/MBC_Gayo_Daejejeon" title="MBC Gayo Daejejeon">MBC Gayo Daejejeon</a>, which featured <a href="/wiki/Krizz_Kaliko" title="Krizz Kaliko">Krizz Kaliko</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Kickin%27_and_Screamin%27" title="Kickin' and Screamin'">Spaz</a>". Krizz Kaliko and Rap Monster later produced the song: "Rush", which was released on March 20 on BTS's <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>, along with Rap Monster's solo mixtape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Along with EE and Dino J, Rap Monster collaborated with hip hop project group <a href="/wiki/MFBTY" title="MFBTY">MFBTY</a> for the song, "Bucku Bucku (부끄부끄)". He was featured in "Bucku Bucku"'s music video, and also had a cameo in MFBTY's other music video for their song, "Bang Diggy Bang Bang (방뛰기방방)."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rap_Monster_Is_Proving_Assumptions_About_Korean_Idol_Rappers_Wrong_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rap_Monster_Is_Proving_Assumptions_About_Korean_Idol_Rappers_Wrong-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On the <i>4things</i> show, <a href="/wiki/Tiger_JK" title="Tiger JK">Tiger JK</a> from MFBTY stated that Rap Monster is "the industry junior who changed his preconceptions about idols."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 10, 2015, a picture of Rap Monster was released on his blog, along with the words: "RM by Rap Monster. You do You, I do I". This picture helped announce that<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Rap Monster would be releasing his first solo <a href="/wiki/Mixtape" title="Mixtape">mixtape</a>: <i>RM</i> on March 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> His first track, "Awakening (각성)" was released with a music video on March 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Two more tracks from his mixtape, "Do You" and "Joke (농담)" also had music videos released on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The mixtape was ranked 48th on <a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)"><i>Spin</i></a>'s "50 Best Hip Hop Albums of 2015".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 9, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Primary_(musician)" title="Primary (musician)">Primary</a> released his mini-album, <i>2-1,</i> on which Rap Monster, along with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kwon_Jin_Ah" title="Kwon Jin Ah">Kwon Jin Ah</a>, was featured in the song, "U".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rap_Monster_Is_Proving_Assumptions_About_Korean_Idol_Rappers_Wrong_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rap_Monster_Is_Proving_Assumptions_About_Korean_Idol_Rappers_Wrong-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>For his new album, <i>Andre</i>, Yankie released an animated music video for his track, "ProMeTheUs",on June 5. The song features Rap Monster as well as <a href="/wiki/Dok2" title="Dok2">Dok2</a>, Juvie Train, Double K, Topbob and Don Mills.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rap Monster collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Entertainment" title="Marvel Entertainment">Marvel</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four_(2015_film)" title="Fantastic Four (2015 film)">Fantastic Four</a></i> film's soundtrack. The digital single, "Fantastic" featuring <a href="/wiki/Mandy_Ventrice" title="Mandy Ventrice">Mandy Ventrice</a>, was released on August 4, through <a href="/wiki/Melon_(online_music_service)" title="Melon (online music service)">Melon</a>, Genie, <a href="/wiki/Naver" title="Naver">Naver Music</a> and other music sites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 20, Rap Monster participated in the 2015 All Force One (A.F.O) Hot &amp; Cool Concert at AX-Korea, along with other hip hop artists: Palo Alto, <a href="/wiki/Verbal_Jint" title="Verbal Jint">Verbal Jint</a>, Supreme Boi, <a href="/wiki/Show_Me_the_Money_(South_Korean_TV_series)" title="Show Me the Money (South Korean TV series)">Sik-k</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Show_Me_the_Money_(South_Korean_TV_series)" title="Show Me the Money (South Korean TV series)">Incredivle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 30, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Homme_(band)" title="Homme (band)">Homme's</a> new single "Dilemma" was released, which Rap Monster co-produced alongside Bang Shi Hyuk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> This was his second official production after his participation on BTS "Epilogue: Young Forever"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and the unofficial <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> release of "I Know" .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> This was also his second time to co-write and co-compose outside of BTS after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GLAM_(band)" title="GLAM (band)">GLAM's</a> "Party (XXO)" in 2012 and "I Like That" in 2013. <a class="external autonumber" href="https://www.komca.or.kr/foreign2/eng/S01.jsp" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rap_Monster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On March 23, <a href="/wiki/TVN_(South_Korea)" title="TVN (South Korea)">tvN</a> released the preview for an upcoming variety talk show, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Brain:_Problematic_Men" title="Hot Brain: Problematic Men">Hot Brain: Problematic Men</a></i>, which features Rap Monster as a panel member. During the show, Rap Monster and five other male celebrities discuss social topics with guests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rap Monster is a model for the coffee brand <i>K'hawah</i>, along with Iron and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shin_Jimin" title="Shin Jimin">Jimin</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AOA_(band)" title="AOA (band)">AOA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2016, 2 books called "HipHopHada" were released. They consist of the self-written life histories of 42 Korean hip hop artists, including Rap Monster<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> American hip-hop magazine XXL has put together a list of the ten Korean rappers that they think their readers should check out which also included Rap Monster. Writer Peter A. Berry compares Rap Monster to “Pitbull or Flo Rida for those pop vibes,” and promises, “Rap Monster rarely fails to live up to his name.” He describes the young star as “one of the region’s most dexterous rappers, capable of switching flows effortlessly as he glides across an array of diverse instrumentals.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
