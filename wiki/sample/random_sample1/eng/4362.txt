<p><b>Holly Lapsley Fletcher</b> (born 7 August 1996), known by the stage name <b>Låpsley</b>, is an English singer, songwriter, musician and producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skinny_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Skinny-1">[1]</a></sup> Her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Long_Way_Home_(L%C3%A5psley_album)" title="Long Way Home (Låpsley album)">Long Way Home</a></i> was released on 4 March 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%A5psley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Låpsley was born in <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York, North Yorkshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her middle name, Lapsley, is her mother's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maiden_name" title="Maiden name">maiden name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She changed "Lapsley" to "Låpsley" by adding the Scandinavian character <a href="/wiki/%C3%85" title="Å">Å</a>, partly due to her perceived <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_Scotland" title="Scandinavian Scotland">ties with Scandinavia through her Scottish heritage</a>, as well as how it looked when written.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She grew up in <a href="/wiki/Southport" title="Southport">Southport, Merseyside</a>, where she attended <a href="/wiki/Greenbank_High_School" title="Greenbank High School">Greenbank High School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2011, at the age of 14, she was nominated by her teachers as a "Gold Ambassador" to promote sports and the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">Olympics</a> in her school on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Youth_Sport_Trust" title="Youth Sport Trust">Youth Sport Trust</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-potw_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-potw-7">[7]</a></sup> Her sporting interests included <a href="/wiki/Yachting" title="Yachting">yachting</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-potw_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-potw-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%A5psley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present">2013–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%A5psley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2013–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Having been part of a number of bands in the Merseyside area, Låpsley won the "One to Watch" prize at Merseyside's GIT (Getintothis) Award in April 2014, following over half a million listens of her solo bedroom-recorded <i>Monday EP</i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soundcloud" title="Soundcloud">Soundcloud</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GIT_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GIT-8">[8]</a></sup> She played the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Introducing" title="BBC Introducing">BBC Introducing</a> stage at <a href="/wiki/Glastonbury_Festival" title="Glastonbury Festival">Glastonbury Festival</a> in June 2014 having been championed by <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> DJ's <a href="/wiki/Huw_Stephens" title="Huw Stephens">Huw Stephens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zane_Lowe" title="Zane Lowe">Zane Lowe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Her track <i>Painter (Valentine)</i> was playlisted by BBC Radio 1 in September 2014 and received daytime airplay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Låpsley signed with <a href="/wiki/XL_Recordings" title="XL Recordings">XL Recordings</a> in October 2014. On 30 November 2014, Låpsley was revealed as one of acts on the BBC <a href="/wiki/Sound_of..." title="Sound of...">Sound of...</a>2015 long list. Her <i>Understudy</i> EP was released on 5 January 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gigxl_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-gigxl-11">[11]</a></sup> Her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Long_Way_Home_(L%C3%A5psley_album)" title="Long Way Home (Låpsley album)">Long Way Home</a></i> was released on 4 March 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theedge1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-theedge1-12">[12]</a></sup> She performed at <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a> in July 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, the track "8896" by Låpsley was included in the sound track of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Honey_(film)" title="American Honey (film)">American Honey</a></i>, directed by Andrea Arnold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%A5psley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Låpsley enjoys yachting and owns a boat called Theodore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-id_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-id-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%A5psley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>A</b> <span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_note_a1"><b><a href="#ref_note_a1">^</a></b></span> "Falling Short" did not enter the <i><a href="/wiki/Ultratop" title="Ultratop">Ultratop</a> 50</i> Flandres singles chart, but peaked at number 3 on the <i>Ultratip</i> chart, which acts as an indicator chart for new releases in Belgium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>B</b> <span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_note_b1"><b><a href="#ref_note_b1">^</a></b></span> "Hurt Me" did not enter the <i>Ultratop 50</i> Wallonie singles chart, but peaked at number 20 on the <i>Ultratip</i> chart, which acts as an indicator chart for new releases in Belgium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BEL-WA_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-BEL-WA-29">[29]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>C</b> <span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_note_c1"><b><a href="#ref_note_c1">^</a></b></span> "Operator (He Doesn't Call Me)" did not enter the <i>Ultratop 50</i> Flandres singles chart, but peaked at number 32 on the <i>Ultratip</i> chart, which acts as an indicator chart for new releases in Belgium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BEL-FL_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-BEL-FL-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
