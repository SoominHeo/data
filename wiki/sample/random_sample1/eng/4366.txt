<p>The <b>Lyakhovsky Islands</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: Ляховские острова <i>Lyakhovskiye ostrova</i>) are the southernmost group of the <a href="/wiki/New_Siberian_Islands" title="New Siberian Islands">New Siberian Islands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">arctic</a> seas of eastern <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. The islands are named in honour of <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Lyakhov" title="Ivan Lyakhov">Ivan Lyakhov</a>, who explored them in 1773.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lyakhovsky_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>They are separated from the mainland by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laptev_Strait" title="Laptev Strait">Laptev Strait</a> (60 km wide), and from the <a href="/wiki/Anzhu_Islands" title="Anzhu Islands">Anzhu Islands</a> group by the <a href="/wiki/Sannikov_Strait" title="Sannikov Strait">Sannikov Strait</a> (50 km). Two islands dominate the group:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Lyakhovsky_Island" title="Great Lyakhovsky Island">Great Lyakhovsky Island</a> (Большой Ляховский: <i>Bolshoy Lyakhovsky</i>) 4,600 km² with a maximum altitude of 270 m on <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Emy_Tas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Emy Tas (page does not exist)">Emy Tas</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Little_Lyakhovsky_Island" title="Little Lyakhovsky Island">Little Lyakhovsky Island</a> (Малый Ляховский: <i>Maly Lyakhovsky</i>) 1,325 km²</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stolbovoy_Island" title="Stolbovoy Island">Stolbovoy</a> is a large island detached from the group.</li>
<li>Off Great Lyakhovsky Island's southwestern cape lies a small islet called <b>Ostrov Khopto-Terer</b>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Semyonovsky_Island" title="Semyonovsky Island">Semyonovsky Island</a> has now disappeared after heavy erosion.Before its disappearance, it was at 4 km<sup>2</sup>, one of the smallest islands in the <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grigorov1946_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grigorov1946-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gavrilovothers2003_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gavrilovothers2003-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lyakhovsky_Islands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Part of the action of two novels by <a href="/wiki/Jules_Verne" title="Jules Verne">Jules Verne</a>, <i>Waif of the Cynthia</i> (1885) and <i><a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Cascabel" title="César Cascabel">César Cascabel</a></i> (1890), takes place there. In the latter, the term "Liakhov Islands" refers to the New Siberian group as a whole, as the principal action is on <a href="/wiki/Kotelny_Island" title="Kotelny Island">Kotelny Island</a>.</p>
