<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ledu District</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Ledu District</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">乐都区</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Lèdū Qū</span></i>) is a district in <a href="/wiki/Haidong" title="Haidong">Haidong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qinghai" title="Qinghai">Qinghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. Nearby are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pinganyi" title="Pinganyi">Pinganyi</a> and the city of <a href="/wiki/Xining" title="Xining">Xining</a>. Ledu used to be named Nianbo county before 1929. The county dates back to 1724.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On 8 February 2013 Ledu was upgraded from a county into a district.</p>
<p>Ledu District is served by the <a href="/wiki/G6_Beijing%E2%80%93Lhasa_Expressway" title="G6 Beijing–Lhasa Expressway">G6 Beijing–Lhasa Expressway</a>.</p>
