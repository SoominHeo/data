<p>The <b>Professional Football League</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Первенство Профессиональной футбольной лиги</span>), formerly the <b>Russian Second Division</b> is the third level of Russian professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a>.</p>
<p>In 1998–2010, it was run by the <a class="extiw" href="https://ru.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D0%9F%D1%80%D0%BE%D1%84%D0%B5%D1%81%D1%81%D0%B8%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B0%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BD%D0%B0%D1%8F_%D1%84%D1%83%D1%82%D0%B1%D0%BE%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BD%D0%B0%D1%8F_%D0%BB%D0%B8%D0%B3%D0%B0_(%D0%A0%D0%BE%D1%81%D1%81%D0%B8%D1%8F)" title="ru:Профессиональная футбольная лига (Россия)">Professional Football League</a>. The 2011–12 season was run by the Department of Professional Football of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Football_Union" title="Russian Football Union">Russian Football Union</a> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Департамент профессионального футбола Российского футбольного союза (ДПФ РФС), Departament professional'nogo futbola Rossijskogo futbol'nogo soyuza (DPF RFS)</span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> From 2013–14 season the league is again run by the Professional Football League and the name Second Division is no longer used.</p>
<p>The PFL is geographically divided into 5 zones: <i>West</i> (Northwestern <a href="/wiki/European_Russia" title="European Russia">European Russia</a>), <i>Centre</i> (Northern and Eastern European Russia), <i>South</i> (Southern European Russia), <i>Ural-Povolzhye</i> (Southern <a href="/wiki/Ural_Mountains" title="Ural Mountains">Urals</a> and Western <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>), and <i>East</i> (Rest of Siberia). The number of clubs in each zone varies between years. In the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Russian_Professional_Football_League" title="2015–16 Russian Professional Football League">2015–16 season</a>, there are 62 clubs in the division.</p>
<p>The winners of each zone are automatically promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Football_National_League" title="Russian Football National League">Russian National Football League</a> (known before 2011 as the First Division). The bottom finishers of each zone lose professional status and are relegated to the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Amateur_Football_League" title="Russian Amateur Football League">Russian Amateur Football League</a>. In the lowest populated <i>East</i> zone, each club plays each opponent three times, while in all other zones traditional home-and-away scheme applies.</p>
