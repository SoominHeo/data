<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_criteria">Qualifying criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swimming_at_the_2011_World_Aquatics_Championships&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2010, the FINA Bureau approved the swimming qualification system for the 2011 World Aquatics Championships. This represented the first time that a qualification system (including time standards) was used for the swimming portion of the world championships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Times had to be swum at a FINA approved competition/meet between March 1, 2010 and June 30, 2011. The qualification meets included continental championships in 2010 and 2011 (e.g. European Championships, Asian Games, Pan American Games); and international competitions approved by FINA in advance. All meets for the qualification were swam in 50 meters course.</p>

<p>The qualifying system for individual events was similar to what is employed for the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Qualification" title="Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Qualification">Olympics</a>. Historically, there has been a limit of 2-swimmers per country per individual event, and one (1) relay team per country per relay event. This limitation remains; however, if a country wished to have more than one (1) male and one (1) female swimmer in up to 2 events each, then an athlete(s) had to meet one of two standards:</p>
<ul>
<li>a <i>B standard</i> which qualifies up to one swimmer per event, or</li>
<li>an <i>A standard</i> which qualifies up to two swimmers per event, where both swimmers must meet this faster standard.</li>
</ul>

<p>Note: prelims/semifinals/finals will be swum in events 200 m and shorter; prelims/finals in events 400 m or longer. For prelims/semifinals/finals events, prelims and semis will be held on the same day, with finals being the evening of the following day. For the 400 m events and the 800 m relays, prelims and finals are the same day. For the individual 800 m and 1500 m races, prelims are in the morning of one day, with finals in the evening of the next day. Preliminary sessions began at 9:00 a.m.; finals at 6:00 p.m.</p>
