<p><b>Runglish</b>, <b>Rusinglish</b>, <b>Ruglish</b> or <b>Russlish</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">русинглиш / рунглиш</span>, <i>rusingliš</i> / <i>rungliš</i>), is a Russian–English <a href="/wiki/Macaronic_language" title="Macaronic language">macaronic language</a>. The term "runglish" was popularized in 2000 as a name for one of the languages aboard the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmonaut" title="Cosmonaut">Cosmonaut</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sergei_Krikalyov" title="Sergei Krikalyov">Sergei Krikalyov</a> said: "We say jokingly that we communicate in 'Runglish,' a mixture of Russian and English languages, so that when we are short of words in one language we can use the other, because all the crew members speak both languages well." NASA has since begun listing Runglish as one of the on-board languages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NASA-1">[1]</a></sup> Although less widespread than other <a href="/wiki/Pidgin" title="Pidgin">pidgins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Creole_language" title="Creole language">creoles</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Tok_Pisin" title="Tok Pisin">Tok Pisin</a>, Runglish is spoken in a number of English-Russian communities, most notably the Russian-speaking community of <a href="/wiki/Brighton_Beach" title="Brighton Beach">Brighton Beach</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_literature">In literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Runglish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some notable novels have foreshadowed the development of Runglish. A small subplot in <a href="/wiki/Arthur_C._Clarke" title="Arthur C. Clarke">Arthur C. Clarke</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/2010:_Odyssey_Two" title="2010: Odyssey Two">2010: Odyssey Two</a></i> concerned the crew of a Russo-American spaceship, who attempted to break down boredom with a <i>Stamp Out Russlish!!</i> campaign. As the story went, both crews were fully fluent in each other's languages, to the point that they found themselves crossing over languages in mid-conversation, or even simply speaking the other language even when there was no-one who had it as their native tongue present. <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Burgess" title="Anthony Burgess">Anthony Burgess</a>' novel <i><a href="/wiki/A_Clockwork_Orange_(novel)" title="A Clockwork Orange (novel)">A Clockwork Orange</a></i> has a famous form of Runglish called <a href="/wiki/Nadsat" title="Nadsat">Nadsat</a>. (See: <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Concordance:A_Clockwork_Orange" title="wikt:Concordance:A Clockwork Orange">Concordance: A Clockwork Orange</a>)</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_resistance">Official resistance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Runglish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Official resistance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Russian government declared 2007 to be the "Year of the Russian Language". It has been claimed that this was in part to give support to what is seen as proper Russian against such influences as the spread of English and Runglish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-3">[3]</a></sup> However, Yuri Prokhorov, the head of the Russian State Institute of Foreign Languages, stated that "Young people always develop fashionable ways of communicating. (But) it is Russian words used incorrectly that damages the purity of the language, not the introduction of foreign words."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Runglish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Appointments: Аппойнтменты (<i>Appojntmenty</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Iced Coffee: Аисд кофе (<i>Aisd kofe</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Would you like that sliced or in one piece?: Вам наслайсовать или писом? (<i>Vam naslajsovatj ili pisom?</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Main1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Main1-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Driving upstate on the highways: Драйвуем в апстейт по хайвеям (<i>Drajvujem v apstejt po hajwejam</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Voices1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Voices1-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Sliced Cheese: Слайсающий чиз (<i>Slajsajuçij čiz</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Voices1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Voices1-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>To merge branches: Смержить бранчи (<i>Smeržitj branči</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>To manage: Сменеджить (<i>Smenedžitj</i>)</li>
<li>I sent you message with attached request: Я засендил тебе месседж с приаттаченым реквестом (<i>Ya zasendil tebe messedž s priattachenim rekvestom</i>)</li>
</ul>
