<p>There are eleven <b><a href="/wiki/Time_zone" title="Time zone">time zones</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a></b>, which currently observe times ranging from <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B02:00" title="UTC+02:00">UTC+02:00</a> to <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B12:00" title="UTC+12:00">UTC+12:00</a>. <a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time" title="Daylight saving time">Daylight saving time</a> is not used in Russia (since March 2011).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Daylight_saving_time">Daylight saving time</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Daylight saving time">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to 2011, Russia moved its clocks backward and forward on the same annual cycle as Europe. On 27 March 2011, clocks were advanced as usual, but they did not go back on 30 October 2011, effectively making Moscow Time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC%2B4" title="UTC+4">UTC+4</a> permanently.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-End_to_DST_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-End_to_DST-4">[4]</a></sup> On 26 October 2014, following another change in the law, the clocks in most of the country were moved back one hour, but summer Daylight Time was not reintroduced; Moscow Time returned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC%2B3" title="UTC+3">UTC+3</a> permanently.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_zone_boundaries">History of zone boundaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History of zone boundaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Introduction_of_a_time_zone_1880">Introduction of a time zone 1880</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Introduction of a time zone 1880">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>, most of the nation observed <a href="/wiki/Solar_time" title="Solar time">solar time</a>. During the late 19th century, Moscow Mean Time was introduced, originally at <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B02:30" title="UTC+02:30">GMT+02:30</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeandate_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-timeandate-6">[6]</a></sup> 2:30 corresponds to 37.5°, the longitude of Moscow. Other parts of Russia kept solar time for several years. At this time Russia had the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julian_Calendar" title="Julian Calendar">Julian Calendar</a> with 12 days less date compared to Western Europe, so it is possible to say the Moscow actually had GMT-285:30 (GMT-11d 21½h). Russia adopted the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>, which most of Europe already used, in 1918.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1919_time_change">1919 time change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1919 time change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the Soviet Union was created, Moscow Time became <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B02:00" title="UTC+02:00">UTC+02</a> and the various other time zones were introduced throughout Russia and the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, for example <a href="/wiki/Irkutsk_Time" title="Irkutsk Time">Irkutsk Time</a> GMT+7 (Irkutsk has since this always been MSK+5).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeandate_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-timeandate-6">[6]</a></sup> Between 1917-1922 the time was less ordered, with daylight savings time some of those years, some with two hours addition, and some of those years with one or two hours extra winter time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeandate_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-timeandate-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Time_change_in_1930">Time change in 1930</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Time change in 1930">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 June 1930, the Soviet Union advanced all clocks by one hour, effectively making the nation run on daylight saving time all year. <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Time" title="Moscow Time">Moscow Time</a> was now GMT+3 and <a href="/wiki/Irkutsk_Time" title="Irkutsk Time">Irkutsk Time</a> GMT+8 (MSK+5). One hour extra daylight saving time for the summers were started in 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeandate_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-timeandate-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1981_zone_boundary_changes">1981 zone boundary changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1981 zone boundary changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 April 1981, 00:00:00, <a href="/wiki/Oymyakonsky_District" title="Oymyakonsky District">Oymyakonsky District</a> changed its time zone from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MSK%2B6" title="MSK+6">MSK+6</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MSK%2B8" title="MSK+8">MSK+8</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1982_zone_boundary_changes">1982 zone boundary changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 1982 zone boundary changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 April 1982, 00:00:00, <a href="/wiki/Chukotka_Autonomous_Okrug" title="Chukotka Autonomous Okrug">Chukotka Autonomous Okrug</a> changed its time zone from MSK+10 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MSK%2B9" title="MSK+9">MSK+9</a>, thus eliminating Anadyr Time (MSK+10 or UTC+13 without DST).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The change occurred during DST effectively changing the offset from UTC+14 to UTC+13, the offset without DST was therefore changed from UTC+13 to UTC+12.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1988_zone_boundary_changes">1988 zone boundary changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 1988 zone boundary changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 27 March 1988, 02:00:00, <a href="/wiki/Saratov_Oblast" title="Saratov Oblast">Saratov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volgograd_Oblast" title="Volgograd Oblast">Volgograd</a> oblasts changed its time zone from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MSK%2B1" title="MSK+1">MSK+1</a> to <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Time" title="Moscow Time">MSK</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The change occurred during DST effectively changing the offset from UTC+5 to UTC+4, the offset without DST was therefore changed from UTC+4 to UTC+3.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1989_zone_boundary_changes">1989 zone boundary changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 1989 zone boundary changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 26 March 1989, 02:00:00, <a href="/wiki/Astrakhan_Oblast" title="Astrakhan Oblast">Astrakhan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirov_Oblast" title="Kirov Oblast">Kirov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ulyanovsk_Oblast" title="Ulyanovsk Oblast">Ulyanovsk</a> oblasts changed its time zone from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MSK%2B1" title="MSK+1">MSK+1</a> to <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Time" title="Moscow Time">MSK</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1991_and_1992_time_change">1991 and 1992 time change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 1991 and 1992 time change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Russia and most republics in the Soviet Union turned their clocks back one hour on 31 March 1991, but Russia reversed this the following year.</p>

<p>On 28 March 2010, the following changes were introduced, which, in particular, led to abolition of two of the eleven time zones.</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Udmurtia" title="Udmurtia">Udmurt Republic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samara_Oblast" title="Samara Oblast">Samara Oblast</a> started using <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Time" title="Moscow Time">Moscow Time</a>, thus eliminating <a href="/wiki/Samara_Time" title="Samara Time">Samara Time</a> (MSK+1 or UTC+4 without DST).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UdmurtR_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-UdmurtR-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SamaraO_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-SamaraO-20">[20]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kemerovo_Oblast" title="Kemerovo Oblast">Kemerovo Oblast</a> started using <a href="/wiki/Omsk_Time" title="Omsk Time">Omsk Time</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KemerovoO_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-KemerovoO-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chukotka_Autonomous_Okrug" title="Chukotka Autonomous Okrug">Chukotka Autonomous Okrug</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka_Krai" title="Kamchatka Krai">Kamchatka Krai</a> started using <a href="/wiki/Magadan_Time" title="Magadan Time">Magadan Time</a>, thus eliminating <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka_Time" title="Kamchatka Time">Kamchatka Time</a> (MSK+9 or UTC+12 without DST).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChukotkaAOKamchatkaK_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-ChukotkaAOKamchatkaK-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Although the Russian government wants to reduce the number of time zones even further, there have been protests in far-eastern Russia on the recent changes, including protests and a 20,000-strong petition in support of Kamchatka returning to UTC+12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<p>The decree No. 725 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> (31 August 2011) changed UTC offset for Moscow Time and the other time zones. Moscow Time Zone now used <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B04:00" title="UTC+04:00">UTC+04:00</a> all year around. The notions of <a href="/wiki/Decree_time" title="Decree time">decree time</a> and daylight saving time were abolished in the law, but in fact, this law mandated permanent daylight saving time (or even double daylight saving time in regions that had not abolished the decree time).</p>
<p>Some areas changed offset from Moscow. Some districts of the Sakha Republic switched from Vladivostok Time to Yakutsk Time (Zone 7):</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tomponsky_District" title="Tomponsky District">Tomponsky District</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ust-Maysky_District" title="Ust-Maysky District">Ust-Maysky District</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Some districts switched from Magadan Time to Vladivostok Time:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oymyakonsky_District" title="Oymyakonsky District">Oymyakonsky District</a> (Sakha Republic)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kurilsky_District" title="Kurilsky District">Kurilsky District</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuzhno-Kurilsky_District" title="Yuzhno-Kurilsky District">Yuzhno-Kurilsky District</a> (Sakhalin Oblast)</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_time_and_zone_boundary_changes">2014 time and zone boundary changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: 2014 time and zone boundary changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Map_of_Russia_-_Time_Zones_(October_2014).svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="630" data-file-width="1092" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Map_of_Russia_-_Time_Zones_%28October_2014%29.svg/300px-Map_of_Russia_-_Time_Zones_%28October_2014%29.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Map_of_Russia_-_Time_Zones_%28October_2014%29.svg/450px-Map_of_Russia_-_Time_Zones_%28October_2014%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Map_of_Russia_-_Time_Zones_%28October_2014%29.svg/600px-Map_of_Russia_-_Time_Zones_%28October_2014%29.svg.png 2x" width="300"/></a>

<p>As a result of the <a href="/wiki/Annexation_of_Crimea_by_the_Russian_Federation" title="Annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation">annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation</a>, local authorities in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Crimea" title="Republic of Crimea">Republic of Crimea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sevastopol" title="Sevastopol">Sevastopol</a> decreed that clocks in the newly proclaimed Russian <a href="/wiki/Federal_subjects_of_Russia" title="Federal subjects of Russia">federal subjects</a> should jump ahead two hours at 22:00 on 29 March 2014 to switch from <a href="/wiki/Eastern_European_Time" title="Eastern European Time">Eastern European Time</a> (UTC+2) to <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Time" title="Moscow Time">Moscow Time</a> (UTC+4).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2014, further changes were passed, which took effect on 26 October 2014. All of Russia moved back one hour, so <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Time" title="Moscow Time">Moscow Time</a> became UTC+3. Some areas changed offset from Moscow:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Udmurtia" title="Udmurtia">Udmurtia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samara_Oblast" title="Samara Oblast">Samara Oblast</a> remained on UTC+04:00, (thus reinstating <a href="/wiki/Samara_Time" title="Samara Time">Samara Time</a>, MSK+1)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kemerovo_Oblast" title="Kemerovo Oblast">Kemerovo Oblast</a> remained on UTC+07:00 (went from Omsk to Krasnoyarsk Time)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Zabaykalsky_Krai" title="Zabaykalsky Krai">Zabaykalsky Krai</a> moved back two hours to UTC+08:00 (went from Yakutsk to Irkutsk Time)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Magadan_Oblast" title="Magadan Oblast">Magadan Oblast</a> moved back two hours to UTC+10:00 (went from Magadan Time, MSK+8 to Vladivostok Time, MSK+7)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chukotka_Autonomous_Okrug" title="Chukotka Autonomous Okrug">Chukotka Autonomous Okrug</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka_Krai" title="Kamchatka Krai">Kamchatka Krai</a> remained on UTC+12:00 (thus reinstating <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka_Time" title="Kamchatka Time">Kamchatka Time</a>, MSK+9)</li>
<li>The parts of the <a href="/wiki/Magadan_Time" title="Magadan Time">Magadan Time</a> zone that remained on MSK+8, were given a new time zone name, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Srednekolymsk_Time" title="Srednekolymsk Time">Srednekolymsk Time</a>, UTC+11.</li>
</ul>
<p>Annual DST changes is not observed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_zone_boundary_changes">2016 zone boundary changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: 2016 zone boundary changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The following time zone changes occurred on 27 March 2016:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Astrakhan_Oblast" title="Astrakhan Oblast">Astrakhan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ulyanovsk_Oblast" title="Ulyanovsk Oblast">Ulyanovsk</a> oblasts moved forward one hour from UTC+03:00 to UTC+04:00 (from Moscow to Samara time)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Altai_Krai" title="Altai Krai">Altai Krai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Altai_Republic" title="Altai Republic">Altai Republic</a> moved forward one hour from UTC+06:00 to UTC+07:00 (from Omsk to Krasnoyarsk time)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Zabaykalsky_Krai" title="Zabaykalsky Krai">Zabaykalsky Krai</a> moved forward one hour from UTC+08:00 to UTC+09:00 (from Irkutsk to Yakutsk time)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sakhalin_Oblast" title="Sakhalin Oblast">Sakhalin Oblast</a> moved forward one hour from UTC+10:00 to UTC+11:00 (from Vladivostok to Srednekolymsk time), except <a href="/wiki/Severo-Kurilsky_District" title="Severo-Kurilsky District">Severo-Kurilsky District</a>, which was already in UTC+11:00 (Srednekolymsk Time)</li>
</ul>
<p>The following time zone change occurred on 24 April 2016:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Magadan_Oblast" title="Magadan Oblast">Magadan Oblast</a> moved forward one hour from UTC+10:00 to UTC+11:00 (from Vladivostok to Srednekolymsk time)</li>
</ul>
<p>After these changes, the UTC+11:00 time zone is also named Magadan Time or Sakhalin Time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following time zone change occurred on 29 May 2016:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tomsk_Oblast" title="Tomsk Oblast">Tomsk Oblast</a> moved forward one hour from UTC+06:00 to UTC+07:00 (from Omsk to Krasnoyarsk time)</li>
</ul>
<p>The following time zone change occurred on 24 July 2016:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Novosibirsk_Oblast" title="Novosibirsk Oblast">Novosibirsk Oblast</a> moved forward one hour from UTC+06:00 to UTC+07:00 (from Omsk to Krasnoyarsk time)</li>
</ul>
<p>The following time zone change occurred on 4 December 2016:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Saratov_Oblast" title="Saratov Oblast">Saratov Oblast</a> moved forward one hour from UTC+03:00 to UTC+04:00 (from Moscow to Samara time)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Railway_time">Railway time</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Railway time">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All timetables on <a href="/wiki/Russian_Railways" title="Russian Railways">Russian Railways</a> (except <a href="/wiki/Sakhalin_Railway" title="Sakhalin Railway">Sakhalin</a> railways) follow Moscow Time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Airports, however, follow local time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>

<p>For Russia, the <a href="/wiki/Tz_database" title="Tz database">tz database</a> contains several zones in the file <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zone.tab" title="Zone.tab">zone.tab</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_zones_2">List of zones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Time_in_Russia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: List of zones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The list below shows the 16 zones for Russia as defined in the file zone.tab of the database. The database aims to identify regions that had the same time offset rules since 1970.</p>
<p>Two zones, namely <a href="/wiki/Asia/Omsk" title="Asia/Omsk">Asia/Omsk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia/Novosibirsk" title="Asia/Novosibirsk">Asia/Novosibirsk</a>, each cover area that did not observe the same rule set since 1970, all now using <a href="/wiki/Omsk_Time" title="Omsk Time">Omsk Time</a>.</p>
<p>On the last Sunday in October 2011, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daylight-saving_time" title="Daylight-saving time">daylight-saving time</a> ended in tzdata, but all zones moved forward one hour. In other words, the clocks did not change, but the names of the time zones reverted permanently to their standard time variants and there will be no more daylight-saving time going forward.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>If available, the change column lists the offset changes that caused a creation of a new zone in the tz database.</p>
<p>"Initial zone" means that in 1970 there was already a difference in time offset from the offsets in any other zone.</p>
