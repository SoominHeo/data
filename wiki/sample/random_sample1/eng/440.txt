<p>The <b>2011 Iraqi protests</b> came in the wake of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tunisian_revolution" title="Tunisian revolution">Tunisian revolution</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Egyptian_revolution" title="2011 Egyptian revolution">2011 Egyptian revolution</a>. They resulted in at least 45 deaths, including at least 29 on 25 February 2011, the "Day of Rage".</p>
<p>Several protests in March were against the <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi</a>-led <a href="/wiki/Saudi-led_intervention_in_Bahrain" title="Saudi-led intervention in Bahrain">intervention in Bahrain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-reuters1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/2011_Kurdish_protests_in_Iraq" title="2011 Kurdish protests in Iraq">Protests also took place</a> in <a href="/wiki/Iraqi_Kurdistan" title="Iraqi Kurdistan">Iraqi Kurdistan</a>, an autonomous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurdish_people" title="Kurdish people">Kurdish</a> region in Iraq's north, and lasted for 62 days.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In an effort to prevent potential unrest, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraqi</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Iraq" title="Prime Minister of Iraq">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Nouri_al-Maliki" title="Nouri al-Maliki">Nouri al-Maliki</a> announced that he will not run for a third term in 2014, and called for a constitutional term limit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Nevertheless, hundreds of protesters gathered in several major Iraqi urban areas on 12 February (notably Baghdad and Karbala) demanding a more effective approach to the issue of national security and investigation into federal corruption cases, as well as government action towards making public services fair and accessible. In response, the government of Iraq subsidised electricity costs.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Protests">Protests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Protests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Israel's <i><a href="/wiki/Haaretz" title="Haaretz">Haaretz</a></i> reported that a 31-year-old man in <a href="/wiki/Mosul" title="Mosul">Mosul</a> died after he self-immolated in protest against unemployment. <i>Haaretz</i> also reported a planned "Revolution of Iraqi Rage" to be held on 25 February near the <a href="/wiki/Green_Zone" title="Green Zone">Green Zone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="12_February">12 February</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 12 February">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Hundreds of protesters gathered in several major Iraqi urban areas, most notably in <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">Baghdad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karbala" title="Karbala">Karbala</a>, demanding a more effective approach to the issue of national security and an investigation into federal corruption cases, as well as government action towards making public services fair and accessible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In response, the government of Iraq subsidised electricity costs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="16.E2.80.9329_February">16–29 February</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 16–29 February">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 29 February, up to 2,000 protesters took over a provincial council building in the city of Kut. The protesters demanded the provincial governor resign due to a lack of basic services such as electricity and water. Up to three people were reported killed and 30 injured.</p>
<p>On 17 February, two people were killed as protesters threw stones at the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Democratic_Party" title="Kurdistan Democratic Party">Kurdistan Democratic Party</a>, headed by <a href="/wiki/Masoud_Barzani" title="Masoud Barzani">Masoud Barzani</a>, president of Iraq's semi-autonomous northern Kurdish region in Sulaimaniya, Iraqi Kurdistan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 February, around a thousand demonstrators blockaded a bridge in Basra, demanding the resignation of the provincial governor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="23_February">23 February</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 23 February">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Lt. Gen. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Abdul-Aziz_Al-Kubaisi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Abdul-Aziz Al-Kubaisi (page does not exist)">Abdul-Aziz Al-Kubaisi</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">عبد العزيز الكبيسي</span>‎‎) resigned from his post as the Director General at the Iraqi Ministry of Defense, gave up his military rank, and removed it from his shoulders on television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shorouknews_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-shorouknews-12">[12]</a></sup> Following this step, he was arrested by security forces. Al Kubaisi described the Iraqi government as corrupt and called on all officers to declare their resignation and join the demonstrators, who are planning a demonstration on 25 February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shorouknews_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-shorouknews-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The three officers, including one colonel, responded to this call and announced their resignations. Uday Zaidi, who has previously organized protests, revealed that these officers have joined the demonstrations. Zaidi told <i>Al Jazeera</i> that the 37 personnel from the Ministry of Interior have also resigned and joined the crowds of demonstrators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-shorouknews_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-shorouknews-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="24_February">24 February</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 24 February">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 24 February, <a href="/wiki/Muntadhar_al-Zaidi" title="Muntadhar al-Zaidi">Muntadhar al-Zaidi</a>, an Iraqi journalist famous for the 2008 shoeing incident, was arrested for allegedly taking part in the protests. Al-Zaidi is popular in Iraq for what Iraqis see as his act of defiance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="25_February.E2.80.93.22Day_of_Rage.22">25 February–"Day of Rage"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title='Edit section: 25 February–"Day of Rage"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Major protests were held throughout Iraq on 25 February, centering on the nation's high unemployment, corruption, and poor public services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> During the protests, crowds stormed provincial buildings, in addition to <a href="/wiki/Prison_escape" title="Prison escape">jailbreaking</a> prisoners and forcing local officials to resign. At least twenty-nine people were killed across the country as a result of protests on this day, though the <a href="/wiki/2011_Kurdish_protests_in_Iraq" title="2011 Kurdish protests in Iraq">deadliest protests</a> took place in <a href="/wiki/Iraqi_Kurdistan" title="Iraqi Kurdistan">Iraqi Kurdistan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpdayofrage_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-wpdayofrage-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="26_February">26 February</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 26 February">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Protests were diminished from the 25 February "Day of Rage," due to the deaths that resulted during it. Hundreds were detained by Iraqi security forces, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Journalists" title="Journalists">journalists</a>, artists, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intellectuals" title="Intellectuals">intellectuals</a>. One of the artists that was arrested, Hussam al-Ssair, later stated that "It was like they were dealing with a bunch of <a href="/wiki/Al-Qaeda" title="Al-Qaeda">al-Qaeda</a> operatives, not a group of journalists."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpdayofrage_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-wpdayofrage-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="16_March">16 March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 16 March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>New protests arose at Baghdad and Basra against the <a href="/wiki/Peninsula_Shield_Force" title="Peninsula Shield Force">Saudi-led</a> intervention in Bahrain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-reuters1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="17_March">17 March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 17 March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>At <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kerbela" title="Kerbela">Kerbela</a> about 3,000 people demonstrated against Saudi-Arabia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Nouri al-Maliki criticized the Saudi intervention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="April.E2.80.93May">April–May</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: April–May">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Beginning on 9 April 2011, the 8th anniversary of the fall of Saddam Hussein, the protests escalated with thousands protesting in Baghdad's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tahrir_Square,_Baghdad" title="Tahrir Square, Baghdad">Tahrir Square</a> and all over the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The protests extended to anger at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_occupation_of_Iraq" title="US occupation of Iraq">US occupation</a> and culminated on 26 May 2011 with a demonstration organized by <a href="/wiki/Muqtada_al-Sadr" title="Muqtada al-Sadr">Muqtada al-Sadr</a>. Reports of participants vary from 20,000 (Iran's <i><a href="/wiki/Press_TV" title="Press TV">Press TV</a></i>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> to 100 thousand (Iraq's official <i><a href="/wiki/Al_Sabaah" title="Al Sabaah">Al Sabaah</a></i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> to half a million people (Baghdad's independent <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=New_Sabah&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="New Sabah (page does not exist)">New Sabah</a></i> <sup><a class="extiw" href="https://ar.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%B5%D8%A8%D8%A7%D8%AD_(%D8%AC%D8%B1%D9%8A%D8%AF%D8%A9_%D8%B9%D8%B1%D8%A7%D9%82%D9%8A%D8%A9)" title="ar:الصباح (جريدة عراقية)">[ar]</a></sup>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> (The highest figures are not unlikely, as similar protests by Muqtada al-Sadr have drawn up to a million people, as in 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>)</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="10_June">10 June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 10 June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>About 400 protesters converged on Tahrir Square in <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">Baghdad</a> after Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Nouri_al-Maliki" title="Nouri al-Maliki">Nouri al-Maliki</a>'s deadline for reform expired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="12_August">12 August</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 12 August">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Dozens protested in Tahrir Square, calling for Oil Minister <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Karim_Luaibi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Karim Luaibi (page does not exist)">Karim Luaibi</a> to be fired, a planned port named for ex-President of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> <a href="/wiki/Hosni_Mubarak" title="Hosni Mubarak">Hosni Mubarak</a> to be scrapped, and <a href="/wiki/Political_prisoner" title="Political prisoner">political prisoners</a> to be released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2_December">2 December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2 December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2011_Dohuk_riots" title="2011 Dohuk riots">2011 Dohuk riots</a> refers to riots by <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> <a href="/wiki/Kurds" title="Kurds">Kurds</a> on 2 December 2011 which were instigated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friday_prayers" title="Friday prayers">Friday prayers</a>' sermons calling for <a href="/wiki/Jihad" title="Jihad">Jihad</a> against liquor stores and massage parlours in <a href="/wiki/Zakho" title="Zakho">Zakho</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Dohuk_Governorate" title="Dohuk Governorate">Dohuk Governorate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>. The riots soon developed to looting and burning down of <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrian</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yazidi" title="Yazidi">Yazidi</a>-owned properties in other towns in Iraqi Kurdistan over the next couple of days.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Responses">Responses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Responses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic">Domestic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Iraqi_protests&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Domestic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In response to the initial round of protests, Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Nouri_al-Maliki" title="Nouri al-Maliki">Nouri al-Maliki</a> said that his ministers who do not improve their ministries face dismissal. An MP also called for provincial elections to be brought forward by two years.</p>
