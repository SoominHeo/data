<p><b>The London Mathematical Society</b> (<b>LMS</b>) is one of the United Kingdom's <a href="/wiki/Learned_society" title="Learned society">learned societies</a> for <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> (the others being the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Statistical_Society" title="Royal Statistical Society">Royal Statistical Society</a> (RSS) and the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Mathematics_and_its_Applications" title="Institute of Mathematics and its Applications">Institute of Mathematics and its Applications</a> (IMA)).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Mathematical_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:De_Morgan_House.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3544" data-file-width="2720" height="287" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/De_Morgan_House.jpg/220px-De_Morgan_House.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/De_Morgan_House.jpg/330px-De_Morgan_House.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/De_Morgan_House.jpg/440px-De_Morgan_House.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:De_Morgan_House.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
De Morgan House</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Society was established on 16 January 1865, the first president being <a href="/wiki/Augustus_De_Morgan" title="Augustus De Morgan">Augustus De Morgan</a>. The earliest meetings were held in <a href="/wiki/University_College_London" title="University College London">University College</a>, but the Society soon moved into <a href="/wiki/Burlington_House" title="Burlington House">Burlington House</a>, <a href="/wiki/Piccadilly" title="Piccadilly">Piccadilly</a>. The initial activities of the Society included talks and publication of a journal.</p>
<p>The LMS was used as a model for the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/American_Mathematical_Society" title="American Mathematical Society">American Mathematical Society</a> in 1888.</p>
<p>The Society was granted a <a href="/wiki/Royal_charter" title="Royal charter">royal charter</a> in 1965, a century after its foundation. In 1998 the Society moved from rooms in Burlington House into <b>De Morgan House</b> (named after the society's first president), at 57–58 <a href="/wiki/Russell_Square" title="Russell Square">Russell Square</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bloomsbury" title="Bloomsbury">Bloomsbury</a>, to accommodate an expansion of its staff. The Society is also a member of the UK <a class="external text" href="http://www.sciencecouncil.org" rel="nofollow">Science Council</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposal_for_unification_with_the_IMA">Proposal for unification with the IMA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Mathematical_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Proposal for unification with the IMA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 4 July 2008, the Joint Planning Group for the LMS and IMA proposed a merger of two societies to form a single, unified society. The proposal was the result of eight years of consultations and the councils of both societies commended the report to their members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Those in favour of the merger argued a single society would give mathematics in the UK a coherent voice when dealing with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_Research_Councils" title="UK Research Councils">Research Councils</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> While accepted by the IMA membership, the proposal was rejected by the LMS membership on 29 May 2009 by 591 to 458 (56% to 44%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Mathematical_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Society publishes books and periodicals; organizes mathematical conferences; provides funding to promote mathematics research and education; and awards a number of prizes and fellowships for excellence in mathematical research.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Mathematical_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Society's periodical publications include three printed <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">journals</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><b>Bulletin of the London Mathematical Society</b></i></li>
<li><i><b>Journal of the London Mathematical Society</b></i></li>
<li><i><b>Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society</b></i></li>
</ul>
<p>Other publications include an electronic journal, the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Journal_of_Computation_and_Mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Journal of Computation and Mathematics (page does not exist)">Journal of Computation and Mathematics</a></i>; and a regular members' newsletter. It also publishes the journal <i><a href="/wiki/Compositio_Mathematica" title="Compositio Mathematica">Compositio Mathematica</a></i> on behalf of its owning foundation, and copublishes <i><a href="/wiki/Nonlinearity_(journal)" title="Nonlinearity (journal)">Nonlinearity</a></i> with the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Physics" title="Institute of Physics">Institute of Physics</a>. The Society publishes four book series: a series of <i>Monographs</i>, a series of <i>Lecture Notes</i>, a series of <i>Student Texts</i>, and (jointly with the <a href="/wiki/American_Mathematical_Society" title="American Mathematical Society">American Mathematical Society</a>) the <i>History of Mathematics</i> series; it also co-publishes four series of translations: <i><a href="/wiki/Russian_Mathematical_Surveys" title="Russian Mathematical Surveys">Russian Mathematical Surveys</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Izvestiya:_Mathematics" title="Izvestiya: Mathematics">Izvestiya: Mathematics</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sbornik:_Mathematics" title="Sbornik: Mathematics">Sbornik: Mathematics</a></i> (jointly with the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Russian Academy of Sciences">Russian Academy of Sciences</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Turpion_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Turpion (company) (page does not exist)">Turpion</a>), and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Transactions_of_the_Moscow_Mathematical_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Transactions of the Moscow Mathematical Society (page does not exist)">Transactions of the Moscow Mathematical Society</a></i> (jointly with the American Mathematical Society).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prizes">Prizes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Mathematical_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Prizes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The named prizes are:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/De_Morgan_Medal" title="De Morgan Medal">De Morgan Medal</a> (triennial) — the most prestigious;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/P%C3%B3lya_Prize_(LMS)" title="Pólya Prize (LMS)">Pólya Prize</a> (two years out of three);</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Louis_Bachelier_Prize" title="Louis Bachelier Prize">Louis Bachelier Prize</a> (biennial);</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Senior_Berwick_Prize" title="Senior Berwick Prize">Senior Berwick Prize</a>;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Senior_Whitehead_Prize" title="Senior Whitehead Prize">Senior Whitehead Prize</a> (biennial);</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Naylor_Prize_and_Lectureship" title="Naylor Prize and Lectureship">Naylor Prize and Lectureship</a>;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Berwick_Prize" title="Berwick Prize">Berwick Prize</a>;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%B6hlich_Prize" title="Fröhlich Prize">Fröhlich Prize</a> (biennial);</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Whitehead_Prize" title="Whitehead Prize">Whitehead Prize</a> (annual).</li>
</ul>
<p>In addition, the Society jointly with the <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Mathematics_and_its_Applications" title="Institute of Mathematics and its Applications">Institute of Mathematics and its Applications</a> awards the <a href="/wiki/David_Crighton" title="David Crighton">David Crighton</a> Medal every three years.</p>
