<p>The <b>Renren Network</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">人人网</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Rénrénwǎng</span></i>; literally: "Everyone's Website"), formerly known as the <b>Xiaonei Network</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">校内网</span>; literally: "on-campus network") is a <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Social_networking_service" title="Social networking service">social networking service</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is popular among college students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In February 2011, Renren made a pre-IPO announcement that it had 160 million registered users.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Later, in April 2011, it had to update its statement accurately to "a total of 31 million active monthly users."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chao_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chao-6">[6]</a></sup> Renren Inc. has its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Chaoyang_District,_Beijing" title="Chaoyang District, Beijing">Chaoyang District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. Renren also has offices in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Renren, which started as Xiaonei.com, was founded by a group of students, including Wang Xing , Wang Huiwen and Lai Binqiang at Tsinghua University and Tingjing University in December 2005. Joseph Chen, who had himself tried to launch a student community website called ChinaRen as early as 1999 and sold it in 2000, acquired Xiaonei through his new company Oak Pacific in October 2006.</p>
<p>Born in 1970 in China, Chen moved with his family to the US where he studied at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and later earned an MBA from Stanford University.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He remains the chairman and CEO.[6]</p>
<p>In March 2008, Xiaonei launched its WAP version, which users can access through mobile phones.[7] Xiaonei features an instant messaging service (Rénrénzhuōmiàn, Chinese: 人人桌面) designed typically for its users using XMPP, which is more popular than Facebook chat.[8]</p>
<p>In August 2009, Xiaonei officially changed its name to Renren, as well as its domain to www.renren.com. And also, the logo has changed from the Chinese word "xiaonei" to the Chinese word "renren"("ren", meaning "people" or "person", and "everyone" when used twice in a row). This name change from 'inside the school' (Xiaonei 校内) to 'everybody' (renren 人人) reflected the expansion aspirations held by Oak Pacific - to be more than a network dedicated to students, but instead be the biggest Chinese social network website.[9]</p>
<p>In April 2011, the company filed with the SEC to raise $584 million in a US IPO,[5] offering Renren stock on the New York Stock Exchange. The company reported 2010 revenue of $76 million USD.[10]</p>
<p>At the beginning of creating "renren.com", users were only able to use specific IP address from specified colleges. This action was to encourage all the users to use their true names to post blogs and pictures.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Renren Inc. has its headquarters on the 23rd floor of the Jing An Center (S: 静安中心, T: 靜安中心, P: <i>Jìng'ān Zhōngxīn</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Chaoyang_District,_Beijing" title="Chaoyang District, Beijing">Chaoyang District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. Renren has its Shanghai office in the <a href="/wiki/SOHO_China" title="SOHO China">SOHO</a> Zhong Shan Square (S: SOHO中山广场, T: SOHO中山廣場, P: <i>SOHO Zhōngshān Guǎngchǎng</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Changning_District" title="Changning District">Changning District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>. Renren has its Guangzhou office in the China Shine Plaza (S: 耀中广场, T: 耀中廣場, P: <i>Yàozhōng Guǎngchǎng</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Tianhe_District" title="Tianhe District">Tianhe District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_Platform">Open Platform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Open Platform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2007, Xiaonei officially facilitated its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_Platform" title="Open Platform">Open Platform</a>, allowing third parties to integrate with platform by rendering modifications and adding functionalities. The APIs Xiaonei listed are quite similar with those Facebook provided, and some of Xiaonei's developers are developing based on the SDK of Facebook.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Privacy_Policy">Privacy Policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Privacy Policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Renren has a privacy policy to preserve personal information. Different privacy levels for profile, contact info and blogs can be modified in the privacy settings, thus others that fail to match the privacy requirements cannot browse the specific information or contents of a given user.</p>
<p>In April 2008, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SoftBank" title="SoftBank">SoftBank</a> bought a 14% share of Xiaonei, becoming its largest shareholder. As a result, a rumor came out that “the personal data of millions of Chinese college students on Xiaonei will be exposed to the Japanese since the Japanese company became the boss of Xiaonei". Soon, such claim was spread all over various popular forums, QQ groups, and social networking sites like Xiaonei. Later, Xiaonei declared the denouncement towards its rivals for slander and denied what had been described in the rumor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Privacy_Leakage_April_2011">Privacy Leakage April 2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Privacy Leakage April 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 29, 2011 a number of Renren users received a personal message saying that there's someone loving you, along with a piece of malicious code, providing the user's ID, name, school, birth date, cell-phone number, MSN, etc. to a third-party website. Such personal message exploited a bug of Renren and a built-in automatic-forwarding feature. So a great number of users were affected. On the next day, such news was reported by major IT news websites in China but those articles were removed. Only a series of screenshots,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> a short statement<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and a piece of attacking code survived. The community has criticized Renren's reaction towards this event. They did not clarify what happened to its users or to the media, but tried to delete the data and also made use of its public relationship team to delete the posts on every IT news websites.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Functions">Functions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Functions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Renren is a typical SNS with a variety of functions with its own characteristics. In general, it is updated once or twice a year when the system's functions require rectification and further development. In addition, it changes the web page's design on special days, like Spring Festival or National Day.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Profile">Profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As Renren mainly caters to college students, the key information to make one’s identity effective is <a href="/wiki/College" title="College">college</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_school" title="High school">high school</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middle_school" title="Middle school">middle school</a> and hometown. At present, 32,000 universities and colleges, 56,000 high schools and 85,000 companies in China and 1,500 universities in 29 other countries are available on Renren’s confirmation system. The social networks for each of these specified colleges, schools and companies have been established. In the additional section of personal profiles, users can also put on information about their contact information, hobbies, favorite music, movies, the clubs they joined, etc. To personalize the profile, Renren has also developed functions to edit profile music and background.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Friends">Friends</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Friends">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Registered users can add friends to the friend list. By specifying educational information in the profile, a user can easily find other users. Users can be also affiliated with groups (usually an existing class, school or organization).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Applications_and_censorship">Applications and censorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Applications and censorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are some common kinds of expressions on Renren, such as renewing the mood (lit. updating personal state), blogging, voting and sharing others’ blogs. In addition, users upload photos and share photos, articles and external links (usually videos). Leaving a message or commenting on photos, blogs, status, etc. is quite commonly seen; the interacting users leaving and replying to messages is considered a prominent instance of basic communication on Renren. Activities can be held online by calling on friends to participate. There are also several peripheral applications, such as online mini games, regular games, doggies, testing, parking lot, and selling and buying of friends.</p>
<p>However, Renren’s censorship is fairly strict, especially to sensitive keywords in the blogs, compared with other SNS websites in China. Blog entries containing keywords like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiananmen_Square_Massacre" title="Tiananmen Square Massacre">Tiananmen Square Massacre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Falun_Gong" title="Falun Gong">Falun Gong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zhao_Ziyang" title="Zhao Ziyang">Zhao Ziyang</a> cannot be released. Others that are suspected to be related to political topics, obscenity or thrillers are manually censored by administrators, delaying or blocking their release.</p>
<p>Renrenzhuomian is a kind of instant message service provided to renren.net users and it is established on year 2009. It allows users viewing news and messages online, generating new short blogs, replying to others, and forwarding news by simply opening this software and logging into the account.</p>
<p>Nowadays, people show more preference to install this simple and smart program to log in to Renren, browsing and sharing news with others, rather than login to the original webpage on browsers. This is mainly because the establishment of Renrenzhuomian mobile app (for both IOS and Android). People can log in to their renren accounts anywhere and this is much more convenient.</p>
<p>Additional functions include:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Log in with an invisible status</li>
</ul>
<p>One can click to choose their online status and if they choose the "invisible status", others cannot see. This function is useful when someone wants to peek at some news but wants to remain anonymus.</p>
<ul>
<li>Online chatting</li>
</ul>
<p>Multi-users chatting is available in Renrenzhuomian. One can talk to several people at the same time and all people will be added to a chatting group automatically.</p>
<ul>
<li>GIF emoticon</li>
</ul>
<p>GIF emoticon is available in Renrenzhuomian, which is not supported technically on the websites. GIF emoticon can present more vivid expression and young users like this function.</p>
<ul>
<li>Batch picture downloading</li>
</ul>
<p>Renrenzhuomian enables users to download all the pictures in an album in a single click. Batch downloading function is not available on renren websites however it is available on mobile apps and it quite effective when you need it.</p>
<ul>
<li>Widget notification</li>
</ul>
<p>User gets notification when someone replies or when fresh news is posted by friends. Renrenzhuomian provides faster and more effective notification.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_Studies">Academic Studies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Academic Studies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Unlike many other social networks, Renren has collaborated with academic researchers to understand the growth and structure of its user community. The results have produced several academic publications. One of the first studies <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> studied the Renren social graph structure and found it similar to prior studies of Facebook's social graph. The study also used Renren's unique feature, a per-user "recent visitors" log, to create an anonymous history of users and how they browse each other's profiles, which was the first of its kind. A second study <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> detailed the process of analyzing and improving detection of fake users (also called Sybil accounts) on Renren.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Points_and_levels">Points and levels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Points and levels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Renren users earn "points" for various activities, such as logging in regularly, posting updates, and receiving comments and replies from contacts. As users earn points, their "level" on the website increases, and at certain levels users gain extra privileges such as access to additional emoticons, <a href="/wiki/Skin_(computing)" title="Skin (computing)">skins</a> for their profile, and the ability to view other users' profiles without their knowledge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Alternatively, users can gain access to all privileges by paying a monthly fee to become a VIP user.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Renrendou">Renrendou</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Renrendou">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <b>Renrendou</b>, or <b>Renren beans</b> (人人豆), is the virtual currency on Renren. Due to some functions charging users fees, Renrendou can be used to purchase gifts, commence the Purple Bean service (紫豆服务), exchange for game coins and advertise. 1 RMB is equivalent to 1 Renrendou.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kaixin">Kaixin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Kaixin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Renren's parent company, Oak Pacific Interactive, launched a copy of <a href="/wiki/Kaixin001" title="Kaixin001">Kaixin001</a> called Kaixin. On September 29, 2010, Oak Pacific announced it would integrate Renren with Kaixin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Users can use the same username to log in to Renren and Kaixin. Kaixin used the same layout as Kaixin001.</p>
<p>On April 11, 2011, Renren was found guilty of infringing on Kaixin001's rights and ordered to pay 400,000 <a href="/wiki/Chinese_yuan" title="Chinese yuan">yuan</a> to its rival. Kaixin001.com announced on April 26, 2011 that it had appealed the April 11 ruling as it was unsatisfied with the amount of compensation as well as the lack of change regarding Renren's usage of Kaixin.com. It also demanded an official apology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
