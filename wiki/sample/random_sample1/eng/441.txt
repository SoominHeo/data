<p>The 64th annual <b><a href="/wiki/Cannes_Film_Festival" title="Cannes Film Festival">Cannes Film Festival</a></b> was held from 11 to 22 May 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-festival-cannes.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-festival-cannes.com-1">[1]</a></sup> American actor <a href="/wiki/Robert_De_Niro" title="Robert De Niro">Robert De Niro</a> served as the president of the jury for the main competition<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and French filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Michel_Gondry" title="Michel Gondry">Michel Gondry</a> headed the jury for the short film competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> South Korean film director <a href="/wiki/Bong_Joon-ho" title="Bong Joon-ho">Bong Joon-ho</a> was the head of the jury for the <a href="/wiki/Cam%C3%A9ra_d%27Or" title="Caméra d'Or">Caméra d'Or</a> prize, which is awarded to the best first-time filmmaker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Midnight_in_Paris" title="Midnight in Paris">Midnight in Paris</a></i>, written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Woody_Allen" title="Woody Allen">Woody Allen</a>, opened the festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-midnight_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-midnight-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9lanie_Laurent" title="Mélanie Laurent">Mélanie Laurent</a> hosted the opening and closing ceremonies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Italian film director <a href="/wiki/Bernardo_Bertolucci" title="Bernardo Bertolucci">Bernardo Bertolucci</a> was presented with the third Honorary <a href="/wiki/Palme_d%27Or" title="Palme d'Or">Palme d'Or</a> Award at the opening ceremony of the festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Though the award had been given out sporadically in the past the Honorary <a href="/wiki/Palme_d%27Or" title="Palme d'Or">Palme d'Or</a> was supposed to presented annually after 2011. However it was not given again until the <a href="/wiki/2015_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2015 Cannes Film Festival">2015 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gus_Van_Sant" title="Gus Van Sant">Gus Van Sant</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Restless_(2011_film)" title="Restless (2011 film)"><i>Restless</i></a> opened the <a href="/wiki/Un_Certain_Regard" title="Un Certain Regard">Un Certain Regard</a> section.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thr41311_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-thr41311-11">[11]</a></sup> Jailed Iranian film directors <a href="/wiki/Jafar_Panahi" title="Jafar Panahi">Jafar Panahi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Rasoulof" title="Mohammad Rasoulof">Mohammad Rasoulof</a> were honoured at the festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <i>Goodbye</i> by Rasoulof and Panahi's <i>This Is Not a Film</i> was screened at the festival, and Panahi was awarded the Carrosse d'Or. Four female directors featured in the main competition: Australian <a href="/wiki/Julia_Leigh" title="Julia Leigh">Julia Leigh</a>, Japan's <a href="/wiki/Naomi_Kawase" title="Naomi Kawase">Naomi Kawase</a>, Scottish director <a href="/wiki/Lynne_Ramsay" title="Lynne Ramsay">Lynne Ramsay</a> and France's own <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ma%C3%AFwenn_Le_Besco" title="Maïwenn Le Besco">Maïwenn Le Besco</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Danish film director <a href="/wiki/Lars_von_Trier" title="Lars von Trier">Lars von Trier</a> caused controversy with comments he made at the press conference of his film <i><a href="/wiki/Melancholia_(2011_film)" title="Melancholia (2011 film)">Melancholia</a></i>. When he was asked about the relation between the influences of <a href="/wiki/German_Romanticism" title="German Romanticism">German Romanticism</a> in the film and his own German heritage, the director made jokes about Jews and Nazis. He said he understood <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> and admired the work of architect <a href="/wiki/Albert_Speer" title="Albert Speer">Albert Speer</a>, and jokingly announced that he was a Nazi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The Cannes Film Festival first issued an official apology for the remarks the same day and clarified that Trier is not a Nazi or an antisemite,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> then declared the director "<a href="/wiki/Persona_non_grata" title="Persona non grata">persona non grata</a>" the following day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The film remained in competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Winner">Winner</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Cannes_Film_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Winner">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Palme_d%27Or" title="Palme d'Or">Palme d'Or</a> was won by the American film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tree_of_Life_(film)" title="The Tree of Life (film)">The Tree of Life</a></i> directed by <a href="/wiki/Terrence_Malick" title="Terrence Malick">Terrence Malick</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tree_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tree-20">[20]</a></sup> Two of the film's producers, Bill Pohlad and Sarah Green, accepted the prize on behalf of the reclusive Malick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tree_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tree-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-21">[21]</a></sup> <i>The Tree of Life</i> is the first American film to win the Palme d'Or since <i><a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit_9/11" title="Fahrenheit 9/11">Fahrenheit 9/11</a></i> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-21">[21]</a></sup> Head of the jury, Robert De Niro, said it was difficult to choose a winner, but <i>The Tree of Life</i> "ultimately fit the bill".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-21">[21]</a></sup> De Niro explained, "It had the size, the importance, the intention, whatever you want to call it, that seemed to fit the prize."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forbes_21-3"><a href="#cite_note-Forbes-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
