<p><b>Leonard Kirk</b> is an American-born <a href="/wiki/Comic_book" title="Comic book">comic book</a> artist living in Canada. He has worked on such properties as <i><a href="/wiki/Supergirl" title="Supergirl">Supergirl</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_Society_of_America" title="Justice Society of America">JSA</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_(comic_book)" title="Batman (comic book)">Batman</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Witchblade" title="Witchblade">Witchblade</a></i>. Currently Kirk is exclusively signed for <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBR5.26.06_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBR5.26.06-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leonard_Kirk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:4.20.08LeonardKirkByLuigiNovi.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1062" data-file-width="1446" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/4.20.08LeonardKirkByLuigiNovi.JPG/220px-4.20.08LeonardKirkByLuigiNovi.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/4.20.08LeonardKirkByLuigiNovi.JPG/330px-4.20.08LeonardKirkByLuigiNovi.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/4.20.08LeonardKirkByLuigiNovi.JPG/440px-4.20.08LeonardKirkByLuigiNovi.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:4.20.08LeonardKirkByLuigiNovi.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Kirk at the 2008 <a href="/wiki/New_York_Comic_Con" title="New York Comic Con">New York Comic Con</a></div>

<p>Kirk first broke into the comics field pencilling issue #5 of the <a href="/wiki/Malibu_Comics" title="Malibu Comics">Malibu Comics</a> title <i><a href="/wiki/Dinosaurs_for_Hire" title="Dinosaurs for Hire">Dinosaurs for Hire</a></i> and issue #1 of <i><a href="/wiki/Captain_Canuck" title="Captain Canuck">Captain Canuck</a></i> vol.2. He then went on to pencil <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Deep_Space_Nine" title="Star Trek: Deep Space Nine">Star Trek: Deep Space Nine</a></i> comics for Malibu.</p>
<p>In 1995, he began working with Marvel, penciling the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultragirl" title="Ultragirl">Ultragirl</a></i> miniseries.</p>
<p>In 1997, he began a long association with <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">DC Comics</a>, beginning with a nearly 60-issue run on the <a href="/wiki/Peter_David" title="Peter David">Peter David</a> written <i>Supergirl</i> series. Following that, he penciled the <a href="/wiki/Dan_Jolley" title="Dan Jolley">Dan Jolley</a> written <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bloodhound_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bloodhound (comics) (page does not exist)">Bloodhound</a></i>, which was canceled within a year.</p>
<p>He penciled the <a href="/wiki/Fred_Van_Lente" title="Fred Van Lente">Fred Van Lente</a> written <a href="/wiki/Scorpion_(comics)" title="Scorpion (comics)">Scorpion</a> story in the Marvel anthology title <i><a href="/wiki/Amazing_Fantasy" title="Amazing Fantasy">Amazing Fantasy</a></i>. Following that, he illustrated the miniseries, <i><a href="/wiki/Freshmen_(comics)" title="Freshmen (comics)">Freshmen</a></i>, written by <a href="/wiki/Seth_Green" title="Seth Green">Seth Green</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hugh_Sterbakov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hugh Sterbakov (page does not exist)">Hugh Sterbakov</a>, for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Cow" title="Top Cow">Top Cow</a>.</p>
<p>He returned for a year to work at DC, filling in on <i><a href="/wiki/Aquaman" title="Aquaman">Aquaman</a></i> and doing the layouts in <i><a href="/wiki/Detective_Comics" title="Detective Comics">Detective Comics</a>'</i> "<a href="/wiki/One_Year_Later" title="One Year Later">One Year Later</a>" storyline <i><a href="/wiki/Face_the_Face" title="Face the Face">Face the Face</a></i> that ran through both <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_(comic_book)" title="Batman (comic book)">Batman</a></i> and <i>Detective Comics</i>.</p>
<p>Later in 2006, Kirk signed an exclusive deal with Marvel Comics,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBR5.26.06_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBR5.26.06-1">[1]</a></sup> his first project being a six issue <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Parker_(comics)" title="Jeff Parker (comics)">Jeff Parker</a> written <i><a href="/wiki/Agents_of_Atlas" title="Agents of Atlas">Agents of Atlas</a></i> miniseries, which included Wakandan-born <a href="/wiki/S.H.I.E.L.D." title="S.H.I.E.L.D.">S.H.I.E.L.D.</a> agent, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Derek_Khanata" title="Derek Khanata">Derek Khanata</a> from the Scorpion story he penciled in <i>Amazing Fantasy</i>. Marvel has proceeded to assign him to pencil <i><a href="/wiki/Marvel_Adventures" title="Marvel Adventures">Marvel Adventures</a>: The Avengers</i> (from issue #13 onwards), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Spider-Man_Family" title="The Amazing Spider-Man Family">The Amazing Spider-Man Family</a></i>, and a fill-in for the <i><a href="/wiki/World_War_Hulk" title="World War Hulk">World War Hulk</a></i> storyline in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Hulk" title="The Incredible Hulk">The Incredible Hulk</a></i> #108.</p>
<p>In 2008 Kirk provided the art for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captain_Britain_and_MI:_13" title="Captain Britain and MI: 13">Captain Britain and MI: 13</a></i>, written by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Cornell" title="Paul Cornell">Paul Cornell</a>, which began in May <a href="/wiki/2008_in_comics" title="2008 in comics">of that year</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011 Kirk began a run on <i><a href="/wiki/X-Factor_(comics)" title="X-Factor (comics)">X-Factor</a></i>, reuniting him with his <i>Supergirl</i> collaborator, Peter David, penciling issues 225 - 228. Kirk would return to that title with issue 233, and remained the regular penciller on it until it ended in mid-2013 with issue 256. David has named Kirk one of the three artists whose art has mostly closely matched the visuals he conceived when writing comic book scripts (the others being <a href="/wiki/George_P%C3%A9rez" title="George Pérez">George Pérez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dale_Keown" title="Dale Keown">Dale Keown</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Q.26A1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Q.26A1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
