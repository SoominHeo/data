<p><b>Reinier "René" Lambertus van de Kerkhof</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈrɛiniːr rəˈneː lɑmˈbɛrtɵs fɑn də ˈkɛrkɦɔf]</a></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> born 16 September 1951) is a former <a href="/wiki/Football_in_the_Netherlands" title="Football in the Netherlands">Dutch football</a> player, who played for <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a>, <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national football team</a>.</p>
<p>Van de Kerkhof and his <a href="/wiki/Twin" title="Twin">twin</a> brother <a href="/wiki/Willy_van_de_Kerkhof" title="Willy van de Kerkhof">Willy</a> were squad members of the Dutch national team that made the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> final in <a href="/wiki/1974_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1974 FIFA World Cup">1974</a> and became key players in the team that made the <a href="/wiki/1978_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1978 FIFA World Cup">1978</a> final. Overall, Van de Kerkhof appeared 47 times for his country, scoring five goals.</p>
<p>He is well remembered due to the incident before the <a href="/wiki/1978_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1978 FIFA World Cup Final">1978 World Cup final</a>, when the opponent <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentinians</a> objected to the cast he wore on his injured forearm since it could have injured an Argentinian player. Despite the bandage having been passed by <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> and worn in previous matches, the referee, Italian <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Gonella" title="Sergio Gonella">Sergio Gonella</a> vacillated, while the Dutch players threatened to walk off the field. Finally an extra layer of padding was applied to the bandage as a solution, and the match could start.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>1978 was his most successful year, besides becoming runners-up at the World Cup, he also won the <a href="/wiki/1977%E2%80%9378_UEFA_Cup" title="1977–78 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>, the <a href="/wiki/1977%E2%80%9378_Eredivisie" title="1977–78 Eredivisie">Dutch championship</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KNVB_Beker" title="KNVB Beker">KNVB Beker</a> with PSV that year: "we had won everything possible – the title, the Dutch Cup and the UEFA Cup, and we had five or six players who had been runners-up at the FIFA World Cup in Argentina with the Netherlands. It was a beautiful period under coach <a href="/wiki/Kees_Rijvers" title="Kees Rijvers">Kees Rijvers</a>", Van de Kerkhof later would tell to Berend Scholten. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>René van de Kerkhof was named by <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9" title="Pelé">Pelé</a> as one of the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_100" title="FIFA 100">top 125 greatest living footballers</a> in March 2004.</p>
