<p><b>Leonard Albert</b> "<b>Lenny</b>" <b>Kravitz</b> (born May 26, 1964) is an American singer, songwriter, record producer, and actor. His "retro" style incorporates elements of <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">blues</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">hard rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Psychedelic_rock" title="Psychedelic rock">psychedelic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ballad_(music)" title="Ballad (music)">ballads</a>. In addition to singing lead and backing vocals, Kravitz often plays all of the instruments himself when recording.</p>
<p>He won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Male_Rock_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Male Rock Vocal Performance">Best Male Rock Vocal Performance</a> four years in a row from 1999 to 2002, breaking the record for most wins in that category as well as setting the record for most consecutive wins in one category by a male. He has been nominated for and won other awards, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards" title="American Music Awards">American Music Awards</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="MTV Video Music Awards">MTV Video Music Awards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radio_Music_Awards" title="Radio Music Awards">Radio Music Awards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brit_Awards" title="Brit Awards">Brit Awards</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blockbuster_Entertainment_Award" title="Blockbuster Entertainment Award">Blockbuster Entertainment Awards</a>. On December 1, 2011, Kravitz was made an Officer of the <a href="/wiki/Ordre_des_Arts_et_des_Lettres" title="Ordre des Arts et des Lettres">Ordre des Arts et des Lettres</a>. He played <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_Hunger_Games_characters#Cinna" title="List of The Hunger Games characters">Cinna</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film_series)" title="The Hunger Games (film series)">the <i>Hunger Games</i> film series</a></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lenny_Kravitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kravitz was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manhattan,_New_York" title="Manhattan, New York">Manhattan, New York</a>, the son of actress <a href="/wiki/Roxie_Roker" title="Roxie Roker">Roxie Roker</a> (1929–1995) and <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> television news producer Sy Kravitz. His father was from a <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jewish</a> family<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telgjuly_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-telgjuly-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> (with origins from <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>; see the <a href="/wiki/Kravits" title="Kravits">Kravits</a> surname). His mother was of <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_the_Bahamas" title="Demographics of the Bahamas">Bahamian</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African-American" title="African-American">African-American</a> descent, and was from a <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Kravitz is the cousin (once removed) of television weather presenter <a href="/wiki/Al_Roker" title="Al Roker">Al Roker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> During his early years, Kravitz did not grow up in a religious environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telgjuly_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-telgjuly-1">[1]</a></sup> After a spiritual experience when he was 13, he started attending church, becoming a <a href="/wiki/Nondenominational_Christianity" title="Nondenominational Christianity">non-denominational Christian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telgjuly_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-telgjuly-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kravitz was named after his uncle, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Private_First_Class" title="Private First Class">Private First Class</a> <a href="/wiki/Leonard_M._Kravitz" title="Leonard M. Kravitz">Leonard M. Kravitz</a>, who was killed in action in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> at the age of 20, while defending against a Chinese attack and saving most of his platoon; he was posthumously awarded the <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Cross_(United_States)" title="Distinguished Service Cross (United States)">Distinguished Service Cross</a> but was denied the Medal of Honor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2014, he posthumously received the Medal of Honor in a ceremony that awarded it to 23 other servicemen who were passed over because of their ethnicity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Kravitz grew up spending weekdays on the <a href="/wiki/Upper_East_Side" title="Upper East Side">Upper East Side</a> of Manhattan, with his parents, attending <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P.S._6" title="P.S. 6">P.S. 6</a> for elementary school, and weekends at his grandmother Bessie Roker's house in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bedford-Stuyvesant,_Brooklyn" title="Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn">Bedford-Stuyvesant</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>.</p>
<p>Kravitz began banging on pots and pans in the kitchen, playing them as drums at the age of three. At the age of five, he wanted to be a musician. He began playing the drums and soon added guitar. Kravitz grew up listening to the music his parents listened to: <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues" title="Rhythm and blues">R&amp;B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">classical</a>, opera, <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">gospel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">blues</a>. "My parents were very supportive of the fact that I loved music early on, and they took me to a lot of shows," Kravitz said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Henderson2002_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Henderson2002-10">[10]</a></sup> Around the age of seven, he saw <a href="/wiki/The_Jackson_5" title="The Jackson 5">The Jackson 5</a> perform at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a>, which became his favorite group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian2002_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian2002-11">[11]</a></sup> His father, who was also a jazz promoter, was friends with <a href="/wiki/Duke_Ellington" title="Duke Ellington">Duke Ellington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Vaughan" title="Sarah Vaughan">Sarah Vaughan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Count_Basie" title="Count Basie">Count Basie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ella_Fitzgerald" title="Ella Fitzgerald">Ella Fitzgerald</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Short" title="Bobby Short">Bobby Short</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miles_Davis" title="Miles Davis">Miles Davis</a> and other jazz greats. Ellington even played "<a href="/wiki/Happy_Birthday_to_You" title="Happy Birthday to You">Happy Birthday</a>" for him one year when he was about 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ebony1994_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ebony1994-12">[12]</a></sup> He was exposed to the <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul music</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motown_Records" title="Motown Records">Motown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stax_Records" title="Stax Records">Stax</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Brown" title="James Brown">James Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aretha_Franklin" title="Aretha Franklin">Aretha Franklin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al_Green" title="Al Green">Al Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Mayfield" title="Curtis Mayfield">Curtis Mayfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gladys_Knight" title="Gladys Knight">Gladys Knight</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Isley_Brothers" title="The Isley Brothers">The Isley Brothers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gamble_and_Huff" title="Gamble and Huff">Gamble and Huff</a> growing up who were key influences on his musical style. Kravitz often went to see New York theater, where his mother worked. His mother encouraged his dreams of pursuing music.</p>
<p>In 1974, the Kravitz family relocated to Los Angeles when Kravitz's mother landed her role on <i>The Jeffersons</i>. At his mother's urging, Kravitz joined the California Boys Choir for three years, where he performed a classical repertoire, and sang with the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Opera" title="Metropolitan Opera">Metropolitan Opera</a>. He took part in <a href="/wiki/Gustav_Mahler" title="Gustav Mahler">Mahler's</a> <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._3_(Mahler)" title="Symphony No. 3 (Mahler)">Third Symphony</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Bowl" title="Hollywood Bowl">Hollywood Bowl</a>. It was in Los Angeles that Kravitz was first introduced to rock music, listening to <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Grateful_Dead" title="The Grateful Dead">The Grateful Dead</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_Sabbath" title="Black Sabbath">Black Sabbath</a>, <a href="/wiki/Creedence_Clearwater_Revival" title="Creedence Clearwater Revival">Creedence Clearwater Revival</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KISS_(band)" title="KISS (band)">KISS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pink_Floyd" title="Pink Floyd">Pink Floyd</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Who" title="The Who">The Who</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian2002_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-guardian2002-11">[11]</a></sup> "I was attracted to the cool style, the girls, the rock 'n' roll lifestyle," Kravitz said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian2002_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-guardian2002-11">[11]</a></sup> Kravitz's other musical influences at the time included <a href="/wiki/Fela_Kuti" title="Fela Kuti">Fela Kuti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Withers" title="Bill Withers">Bill Withers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Gaye" title="Marvin Gaye">Marvin Gaye</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharoah_Sanders" title="Pharoah Sanders">Pharoah Sanders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miles_Davis" title="Miles Davis">Miles Davis</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian2002_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-guardian2002-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Marley" title="Bob Marley">Bob Marley</a> proved later to be influential as well. Kravitz attended <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_High_School" title="Beverly Hills High School">Beverly Hills High School</a>. <a href="/wiki/Maria_McKee" title="Maria McKee">Maria McKee</a>, actor <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Cage" title="Nicolas Cage">Nicolas Cage</a> and musician <a href="/wiki/Slash_(musician)" title="Slash (musician)">Slash</a> were his classmates. In 1978, Kravitz was accepted into the school's well-respected music program. He taught himself to play piano and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bass_(guitar)" title="Bass (guitar)">bass</a>, and made friends with <a href="/wiki/Zoro_(drummer)" title="Zoro (drummer)">Zoro</a> who would later become his long-time collaborator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Kravitz wanted to be a <a href="/wiki/Session_musician" title="Session musician">session musician</a>. He also appeared as an actor in television commercials during this time.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lenny_Kravitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1989.E2.80.9390:_Virgin_Records_and_career_debut">1989–90: Virgin Records and career debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lenny_Kravitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1989–90: Virgin Records and career debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With record labels still telling him his music was not "black enough" or "white enough", Kravitz decided to record an album on his own. Kravitz had met <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recording_engineer" title="Recording engineer">recording engineer</a>/keyboardist/bassist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_Hirsch" title="Henry Hirsch">Henry Hirsch</a> in 1985 when recording a demo at his <a href="/wiki/Hoboken,_New_Jersey" title="Hoboken, New Jersey">Hoboken, New Jersey</a> recording studio. The two shared an interest in using real instruments and vintage recording equipment, as well as a love of R&amp;B, jazz, and rock. Kravitz would go on to collaborate with Hirsch on most of his albums. Kravitz began working on his debut album with Hirsch over the next year and a half, with Kravitz's father paying for the studio time. Kravitz met <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saxophonist" title="Saxophonist">saxophonist</a> <a href="/wiki/Karl_Denson" title="Karl Denson">Karl Denson</a> and invited him to play on the song, "<a href="/wiki/Let_Love_Rule_(song)" title="Let Love Rule (song)">Let Love Rule</a>". Kravitz was so impressed with his playing that Denson played on much of the album. Denson toured with Kravitz for the next five years.</p>
<p>In October 1988, after completing most of the recording, Kravitz approached friend Stephen Elvis Smith who had served as the Music Supervisor on <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Bonet" title="Lisa Bonet">Lisa Bonet</a>'s spin-off of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cosby_Show" title="The Cosby Show">The Cosby Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Different_World" title="A Different World">A Different World</a></i>. Smith had also worked with Kravitz' mother <a href="/wiki/Roxie_Roker" title="Roxie Roker">Roxie Roker</a>, on the hit sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jeffersons" title="The Jeffersons">The Jeffersons</a></i>. Kravitz urged Smith to manage his career and assist him in finding a record deal. In less than a month of shopping the recordings, five labels (Warner Bros, Elektra, Geffen, Capitol and Virgin) were in a bidding war for Kravitz. Eventually a deal was made with Virgin Records in January 1989, and signed by Virgin A&amp;R executive Nancy Jeffries. The label was excited about the music he was making, music inspired by his relationship with wife Bonet and their new daughter, Zoe. On Smith's urging, Kravitz dropped the name Romeo Blue and reclaimed the Lenny Kravitz moniker. About his time as Romeo Blue, Kravitz said, "Ultimately, it got me back to myself. And when I finally did accept myself for myself, music started flowing out of me."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Henderson2002_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Henderson2002-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kravitz released his début album <i><a href="/wiki/Let_Love_Rule" title="Let Love Rule">Let Love Rule</a></i> on September 6, 1989, a combination of rock and funk with a 1960s vibe. Music critics were mixed: some felt Kravitz was a gifted new artist, others felt he was overpowered by his musical influences. The album was a moderate success in the United States, but became an instant hit outside of the US, especially in Europe. Lisa Bonet directed the debut music video for the title track, "Let Love Rule". Stephen Smith signed Kravitz with talent booking agency CAA, who soon were fielding offers for Kravitz, first on a club tour, and then in opening slots for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tom_Petty_%26_the_Heartbreakers" title="Tom Petty &amp; the Heartbreakers">Tom Petty &amp; the Heartbreakers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a> and David Bowie. Having played essentially all of the instruments on the album, Kravitz had to quickly assemble a touring band to support the Let Love Rule release. They included a childhood friend, <a href="/wiki/Zoro_(drummer)" title="Zoro (drummer)">Zoro</a> on drums (formerly of Bobby Brown's band), Adam Widoff (guitar), Lebron Scott (bass guitar, recruited from Curtis Mayfield's band after seeing them perform in a NYC club), Kenneth Crouch (keyboards) and <a href="/wiki/Karl_Denson" title="Karl Denson">Karl Denson</a> on saxophone. In May 2009, a 20th Anniversary Deluxe Edition of "Let Love Rule" was released worldwide by Virgin. The double disk includes a booklet with rare photos, and 18 additional demos, bonus tracks and live recordings. Kravitz launched a LLR(20) tour of Europe and the United States in support of the re-release.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1991.E2.80.932001:_Popularity_established">1991–2001: Popularity established</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lenny_Kravitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1991–2001: Popularity established">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1990, Kravitz produced the song "<a href="/wiki/Justify_My_Love" title="Justify My Love">Justify My Love</a>" for <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>, which he co-wrote with <a href="/wiki/Ingrid_Chavez" title="Ingrid Chavez">Ingrid Chavez</a>. The song, which appeared on her greatest hits album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Immaculate_Collection" title="The Immaculate Collection">The Immaculate Collection</a></i> and created controversy because of its explicit video, went to number 1 for two consecutive weeks. When MTV banned the video, the video was quickly made available for sale at record stores, and immediately sold over 500,000 copies. Kravitz separated from Lisa Bonet in 1991,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-behindthemusic_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-behindthemusic-14">[14]</a></sup> amid rumors of an affair between him and Madonna. Kravitz has denied any infidelity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-behindthemusic_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-behindthemusic-14">[14]</a></sup> Kravitz and Bonet divorced in 1993. In 1991, Kravitz produced the self-titled album <i><a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Paradis_(album)" title="Vanessa Paradis (album)">Vanessa Paradis</a></i> for French singer and actress <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Paradis" title="Vanessa Paradis">Vanessa Paradis</a>. He played most of the instruments and co-wrote most of the songs on the album. He also released his second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Mama_Said_(album)" title="Mama Said (album)">Mama Said</a></i>, which was his first album to reach the <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">Top 40</a>. The songs on the album were about Bonet and dedicated to her, documenting his depression over their breakup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ebony1994_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-ebony1994-12">[12]</a></sup> Kravitz's biggest single yet, "<a href="/wiki/It_Ain%27t_Over_%27til_It%27s_Over" title="It Ain't Over 'til It's Over">It Ain't Over 'til It's Over</a>", went to number 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. The single "<a href="/wiki/Always_on_the_Run" title="Always on the Run">Always on the Run</a>", a tribute to his mother,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ebony1994_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-ebony1994-12">[12]</a></sup> featured <a href="/wiki/Slash_(musician)" title="Slash (musician)">Slash</a> on guitar. "Stand by My Woman" and "What Goes Around Comes Around" followed. <a href="/wiki/Sean_Lennon" title="Sean Lennon">Sean Lennon</a> co-wrote and played piano on the song "All I Ever Wanted".</p>
<p>In 1993, Kravitz wrote "Line Up" for <a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Steven_Tyler" title="Steven Tyler">Steven Tyler</a>, and appeared on <a href="/wiki/Mick_Jagger" title="Mick Jagger">Mick Jagger</a>'s solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Wandering_Spirit_(album)" title="Wandering Spirit (album)">Wandering Spirit</a></i>, in a cover of the <a href="/wiki/Bill_Withers" title="Bill Withers">Bill Withers</a> soul classic "<a href="/wiki/Use_Me_(Bill_Withers_song)" title="Use Me (Bill Withers song)">Use Me</a>", and played guitar on the title track of <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Buddha_of_Suburbia_(soundtrack)" title="The Buddha of Suburbia (soundtrack)">The Buddha of Suburbia</a></i>. That year Kravitz also got to work with idols <a href="/wiki/Al_Green" title="Al Green">Al Green</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Mayfield" title="Curtis Mayfield">Curtis Mayfield</a>. In 1993, <i><a href="/wiki/Are_You_Gonna_Go_My_Way" title="Are You Gonna Go My Way">Are You Gonna Go My Way</a></i> was released, reaching number 12 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 and Kravitz earned a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BRIT_Awards" title="BRIT Awards">BRIT Award</a> for best international male artist in 1994. The <a href="/wiki/Are_You_Gonna_Go_My_Way_(song)" title="Are You Gonna Go My Way (song)">title track</a> won a <a href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Award_for_Best_Male_Video" title="MTV Video Music Award for Best Male Video">MTV Video Music Award for Best Male Video</a> for the video produced by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Romanek" title="Mark Romanek">Mark Romanek</a>, in which Kravitz slung his dreadlocks and wore high-heeled platform boots. During the presentation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="MTV Video Music Awards">MTV Video Music Awards</a>, he performed the song with <a href="/wiki/John_Paul_Jones_(musician)" title="John Paul Jones (musician)">John Paul Jones</a> of <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a> on bass. Several singles from the album would follow, including "Believe", "Is There Any Love In Your Heart", and "Heaven Help/Spinning Around Over You". This album was the first to feature guitarist <a href="/wiki/Craig_Ross" title="Craig Ross">Craig Ross</a>, who has also played on all his subsequent albums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> One song, "Eleutheria", was influenced by the island <a href="/wiki/Eleuthera" title="Eleuthera">Eleuthera</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a> where Kravitz built a house and recording studio at that time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ebony1994_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-ebony1994-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1993, he also released the EP <i>Spinning Around Over You</i>, which included four live tracks from his "Universal Love Tour". A feature documentary about his 1994 tour entitled <i>Alive from Planet Earth</i> was directed by <a href="/wiki/Doug_Nichol" title="Doug Nichol">Doug Nichol</a> and released.</p>
<p>In 1994, Kravitz recorded a funk-rock version of the song "<a href="/wiki/Deuce_(song)" title="Deuce (song)">Deuce</a>" for the <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">KISS</a> cover album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KISS_My_Ass:_Classic_KISS_Regrooved" title="KISS My Ass: Classic KISS Regrooved">KISS My Ass: Classic KISS Regrooved</a></i>. The track featured <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a> on harmonica and background vocals. This song was one of three radio singles from the album, and was also the album's lead-off track. Roxie Roker, Kravitz's mother, died in California on December 2, 1995, of breast cancer at the age of 66. In 1995, Lenny Kravitz released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Circus_(Lenny_Kravitz_album)" title="Circus (Lenny Kravitz album)">Circus</a></i>, which went to number 10 on the <i>Billboard</i> chart on the back of his past achievement. However, the album only had two hit singles: "<a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Is_Dead" title="Rock and Roll Is Dead">Rock and Roll Is Dead</a>" and "Can't Get You Off My Mind".</p>
<p>With <i><a href="/wiki/5_(Lenny_Kravitz_album)" title="5 (Lenny Kravitz album)">5</a></i> (1998), Kravitz embraced digital technology such as <a href="/wiki/Pro_Tools" title="Pro Tools">Pro Tools</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sampler_(musical_instrument)" title="Sampler (musical instrument)">samplers</a> for the first time. Recorded in both his own New York City carriage house, and <a href="/wiki/Compass_Point_Studios" title="Compass Point Studios">Compass Point Studios</a> in The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>, Kravitz employed engineer/producer <a href="/wiki/Terry_Manning" title="Terry Manning">Terry Manning</a> to handle the recording duties. <i>5</i> introduced his music to an even wider audience thanks to the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Fly_Away_(Lenny_Kravitz_song)" title="Fly Away (Lenny Kravitz song)">Fly Away</a>" being featured prominently in both car manufacturer and airline commercials. <i>5</i> would reach number 28 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, with "Fly Away" reaching number 12 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, topped both the Billboard Mainstream Rock Tracks and Modern Rock Tracks charts, and reached number 1 in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. He would win the first of his four consecutive <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Male_Rock_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Male Rock Vocal Performance">Grammy for Best Male Rock Vocal Performance</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards_of_1999" title="Grammy Awards of 1999">Grammy Awards of 1999</a>. Other hits from the album included "If You Can't Say No", that was also remixed by dance producer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brian_Transeau" title="Brian Transeau">Brian Transeau</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/I_Belong_to_You_(Lenny_Kravitz_song)" title="I Belong to You (Lenny Kravitz song)">I Belong to You</a>". For the "I Belong to You" video Lenny can be seen without his signature <a href="/wiki/Dreadlocks" title="Dreadlocks">dreadlocks</a>. In 1999 he produced and sang with <a href="/wiki/Cree_Summer" title="Cree Summer">Cree Summer</a> on her solo album <a href="/wiki/Street_Fa%C3%ABrie" title="Street Faërie">Street Faërie</a>.</p>
<p>His <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover version</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Guess_Who" title="The Guess Who">The Guess Who</a>'s hit "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Woman_(song)" title="American Woman (song)">American Woman</a>" won him another Grammy at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards_of_2000" title="Grammy Awards of 2000">Grammy Awards of 2000</a> and helped The Guess Who's song reach a new audience. Kravitz's version of the song originally came from the soundtrack of <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers:_The_Spy_Who_Shagged_Me" title="Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me">Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me</a></i> and was added to <i>5</i> as a bonus track in 1999. Kravitz worked on two songs for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Invincible_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Invincible (Michael Jackson album)">Invincible</a></i> album released in 2001; a snippet of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Another_Day_(Michael_Jackson_song)" title="Another Day (Michael Jackson song)">Another Day</a>" has leaked, and the full version was officially released on the album <i><a href="/wiki/Michael_(album)" title="Michael (album)">Michael</a></i> in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kravitz released a <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Lenny_Kravitz_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Lenny Kravitz album)">Greatest Hits</a></i> album in 2000. It proved to be his most successful album, reaching #2 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 and selling nearly 11 million copies worldwide and ultimately becoming one of the most commercially successful albums of the decade. The single "<a href="/wiki/Again_(Lenny_Kravitz_song)" title="Again (Lenny Kravitz song)">Again</a>" earned him his third consecutive Grammy for the Best Male Rock Vocal in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards_of_2001" title="Grammy Awards of 2001">Grammy Awards of 2001</a> and peaked at number 4 on the US <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. Kravitz also co-wrote the song "God Gave Me Everything" with <a href="/wiki/Mick_Jagger" title="Mick Jagger">Mick Jagger</a> in this period, appearing on Jagger's 2001 solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Goddess_in_the_Doorway" title="Goddess in the Doorway">Goddess in the Doorway</a></i> and in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Mick" title="Being Mick">Being Mick</a></i>. In 2001, Kravitz participated in a benefit auction for the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Organization" title="Red Hot Organization">Red Hot Organization</a>, in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a> to increase public AIDS awareness, which ran from February 28 until April 11, 2001. The event featured rare RHO memorabilia and the work of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> photographer <a href="/wiki/Mark_Seliger" title="Mark Seliger">Mark Seliger</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001.E2.80.9305:_Lenny_and_Baptism">2001–05: <i>Lenny</i> and <i>Baptism</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lenny_Kravitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2001–05: Lenny and Baptism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Kravitz released his sixth album <i><a href="/wiki/Lenny_(album)" title="Lenny (album)">Lenny</a></i> in October 2001. The album was recorded in Miami. Kravitz wrote the song "Bank Robber Man" after the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Police_Department" title="Miami Police Department">Miami Police Department</a> detained and cuffed him while walking to the gym with his trainer because police stated that he matched the description of a bank robber. Kravitz did not have any identification with him at the time and the police on the scene did not believe that he was indeed Lenny Kravitz. The bank teller who was robbed was then brought to the scene and said Kravitz was not the bank robber. Miami Police later sent officers to Kravitz's home to apologize for the detention. When asked if he thought the incident was a case of <a href="/wiki/Racial_profiling" title="Racial profiling">racial profiling</a>, Kravitz said he wasn’t sure although some of the lyrics in the song suggest otherwise. The first single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Dig_In" title="Dig In">Dig In</a>", went to number 1 in Argentina and the top 10 in Italy and Portugal. The video for "Dig In" was originally supposed to be shot at the top of the <a href="/wiki/Empire_State_Building" title="Empire State Building">Empire State Building</a> on September 12, 2001 but the terrorist attacks on the <a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Center_(1973%E2%80%932001)" title="World Trade Center (1973–2001)">World Trade Center</a> on September 11, 2001 caused the location to be moved. The video was later shot off the coast Miami. He won his fourth Grammy in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards_of_2002" title="Grammy Awards of 2002">2002</a>. "Stillness of Heart", "Believe in Me", and "If I Could Fall In Love" were subsequent singles from the album.</p>
<p>Kravitz was the subject of a photo book by former chief photographer for <i>Rolling Stone</i>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Seliger" title="Mark Seliger">Mark Seliger</a>. Published in November 2001, Seliger captures Kravitz on tour, with family, with friends and in posed portraits. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> invited Kravitz to appear on the track "Guns and Roses" on his 2002 <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Blueprint%C2%B2:_The_Gift_%26_the_Curse" title="The Blueprint²: The Gift &amp; the Curse">The Blueprint²: The Gift &amp; the Curse</a></i>. Kravitz would also join <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">P. Diddy</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharrell" title="Pharrell">Pharrell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Loon_(rapper)" title="Loon (rapper)">Loon</a> on the track "Show Me Your Soul" from the <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_Boys_II_(soundtrack)" title="Bad Boys II (soundtrack)">Bad Boys II Soundtrack</a></i>. In September 2002, Kravitz appeared alongside other rock stars in the episode "<a href="/wiki/How_I_Spent_My_Strummer_Vacation" title="How I Spent My Strummer Vacation">How I Spent My Strummer Vacation</a>" of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i>. In early 2003, Kravitz released the track "We Want Peace" as a download-only track as a protest against the <a href="/wiki/2003_invasion_of_Iraq" title="2003 invasion of Iraq">2003 invasion of Iraq</a>, he performed this alongside famous <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraqi</a> singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kazem_Al_Saher" title="Kazem Al Saher">Kazem Al Saher</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_The_Vote" title="Rock The Vote">Rock The Vote</a>. The track reached #1 on the world internet download charts and <a href="/wiki/MP3.com" title="MP3.com">MP3.com</a> download chart. Kravitz also appeared on <i>Unity</i>, the official album of the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Athens Olympics</a>, and performed a cover of "<a href="/wiki/Have_You_Ever_Been_(To_Electric_Ladyland)" title="Have You Ever Been (To Electric Ladyland)">Have You Ever Been (To Electric Ladyland)</a>" on the album <i><a href="/wiki/Power_of_Soul:_A_Tribute_to_Jimi_Hendrix" title="Power of Soul: A Tribute to Jimi Hendrix">Power of Soul: A Tribute to Jimi Hendrix</a></i>.</p>
<p>Kravitz's seventh album <i><a href="/wiki/Baptism_(Lenny_Kravitz_album)" title="Baptism (Lenny Kravitz album)">Baptism</a></i> was released in May 2004. The first single was "<a href="/wiki/Where_Are_We_Runnin%27%3F" title="Where Are We Runnin'?">Where Are We Runnin'?</a>". The single "California" failed to be commercially successful, but "Storm", featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, reached the charts. "Calling All Angels" was successful in various countries and a huge hit in Brazil, however it was "Lady" that became the album's surprise hit, making the US Top 30 and propelling <i>Baptism</i> to gold status. Also in 2004, he appeared on <a href="/wiki/N.E.R.D" title="N.E.R.D">N.E.R.D</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Fly_or_Die" title="Fly or Die">Fly or Die</a></i>. From March 2005, Kravitz toured all over the world with the tour <i>Electric Church</i>, which ended at the <a href="/wiki/Brixton_Academy" title="Brixton Academy">Brixton Academy</a>, London in July 2005. Kravitz served as the opening act for <a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a> who are long term friends of Kravitz on their fall 2005 tour. The tour began on October 30 at the <a href="/wiki/Mohegan_Sun_Arena" title="Mohegan Sun Arena">Mohegan Sun Arena</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uncasville" title="Uncasville">Uncasville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>. The night before that tour started, October 29, 2005, Lenny's father, TV producer Seymour "Sy" Kravitz, died. During that first show, Lenny broke the news to the stunned crowd and stated it was not a time to be sad, but rather a time to celebrate because he is now in Heaven. Lenny then dedicated <i><a href="/wiki/Let_Love_Rule" title="Let Love Rule">Let Love Rule</a></i> to his father. That tour was so successful that it was extended through February 25, 2006 and ended in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anaheim" title="Anaheim">Anaheim</a>, California.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9309:_Live_Earth.2C_charity_work.2C_and_It_Is_Time_for_a_Love_Revolution">2006–09: Live Earth, charity work, and <i>It Is Time for a Love Revolution</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lenny_Kravitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2006–09: Live Earth, charity work, and It Is Time for a Love Revolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2006, Kravitz contributed "Breathe" to absoluttracks, a project sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Absolut_Vodka" title="Absolut Vodka">Absolut Vodka</a>. This song was re-mixed by ten musical producers and distributed via the internet. Kravitz appeared in the audience of Madonna's <a href="/wiki/Confessions_Tour" title="Confessions Tour">Confessions Tour</a> (2006) during numerous shows. He later joined Madonna live on stage to play guitar on the song, "I Love New York", at the last of four Paris shows. Kravitz founded a design firm named <a href="/wiki/Kravitz_Design" title="Kravitz Design">Kravitz Design</a>, stating if he hadn't been a musician he would have been a designer. Kravitz Design, focused on interior and furniture design, has designed residential spaces, as well as a chandelier for the crystal company <a href="/wiki/Swarovski" title="Swarovski">Swarovski</a>, named "Casino Royale".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 7, 2007, Kravitz performed at the <a href="/wiki/Live_Earth_concert,_Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Live Earth concert, Rio de Janeiro">Brazilian leg</a> of <a href="/wiki/Live_Earth" title="Live Earth">Live Earth</a> in Rio de Janeiro,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> making him one of three major international rock stars to perform two huge free concerts at the world-famous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copacabana_(Rio_de_Janeiro)" title="Copacabana (Rio de Janeiro)">Copacabana Beach</a> along with Macy Gray and the Rolling Stones. Kravitz had already played there on March 21, 2005, drawing 300,000 people on a concert of his own. The <a href="/wiki/Live_Earth" title="Live Earth">Live Earth</a> concert, with eight other acts on the bill, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharrell" title="Pharrell">Pharrell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Macy_Gray" title="Macy Gray">Macy Gray</a>, took 400,000 to the beach. Also in 2007, Kravitz released a version of "<a href="/wiki/Cold_Turkey" title="Cold Turkey">Cold Turkey</a>" by <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> on the charity CD <i><a href="/wiki/Instant_Karma:_The_Amnesty_International_Campaign_to_Save_Darfur" title="Instant Karma: The Amnesty International Campaign to Save Darfur">Instant Karma: The Amnesty International Campaign to Save Darfur</a></i>. Kravitz also spent time recording his latest album, <i><a href="/wiki/It_Is_Time_for_a_Love_Revolution" title="It Is Time for a Love Revolution">It Is Time for a Love Revolution</a></i>, released February 5, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On September 25, 2007, the <a href="/wiki/Fats_Domino" title="Fats Domino">Fats Domino</a> tribute album "Goin' Home ; A Tribute To Fats Domino" was released. Kravitz was on the song "Whole Lotta Lovin'" along with <a href="/wiki/Rebirth_Brass_Band" title="Rebirth Brass Band">Rebirth Brass Band</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trombone_Shorty" title="Trombone Shorty">Troy "Trombone Shorty" Andrews</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Wesley" title="Fred Wesley">Fred Wesley</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pee_Wee_Ellis" title="Pee Wee Ellis">Pee Wee Ellis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maceo_Parker" title="Maceo Parker">Maceo Parker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kravitz performed at the <a href="/wiki/Grey_Cup" title="Grey Cup">Grey Cup</a> halftime show in Toronto at the <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Centre" title="Rogers Centre">Rogers Centre</a> on November 25, 2007, where the <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_Roughriders" title="Saskatchewan Roughriders">Saskatchewan Roughriders</a> beat the <a href="/wiki/Winnipeg_Blue_Bombers" title="Winnipeg Blue Bombers">Winnipeg Blue Bombers</a> 23-19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The original video for "<a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Be_Waiting_(Lenny_Kravitz_song)" title="I'll Be Waiting (Lenny Kravitz song)">I'll Be Waiting</a>" was shot in <a href="/wiki/Central_Park" title="Central Park">Central Park</a> in New York City with <a href="/wiki/Marc_Webb" title="Marc Webb">Marc Webb</a> directing but that video was later shelved and a new version, which Kravitz co-directed with <a href="/wiki/Philip_Andelman" title="Philip Andelman">Philip Andelman</a>, was filmed in Lenny's New York City recording studio. The video premiered on VH1's Top 20 Countdown at number 3. On January 17, 2008, Kravitz embarked on a 9 city mini-tour to promote his new album <i>It Is Time for a Love Revolution</i>. The tour started in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Monica" title="Santa Monica">Santa Monica</a>, California and ended in New York City on February 1. The tour was done in association with Myspace and called the "Get on the Bus" tour. At each stop on the tour, they would pick up one contest winner and their guest and they rode on the "Love Revolution" bus until the end of the tour. Steven Tyler of Aerosmith, a close friend of Lenny's would appear at The Orpheum Theater in Boston. The New York City winner, Tyrone Good was presented a train ticket from Penn Station to travel to Philadelphia and rode with the tour the rest of the way starting in Philadelphia (The Electric Factory), Boston (The Orpheum Theater), and finally New York City (The Hammerstein Ballroom). Ryan Neuerburg, the San Francisco winner, embarked on almost the entire tour with his father, Tom Neuerburg. The winners were featured in the YRB February Edition alongside Lenny Kravitz in a magazine pull-out. The photo shoot took place at the Electric Factory in Philadelphia on stage. The winner's pull out was featured in another issue of YRB which had Lil' Wayne on the cover.</p>
<p>On February 11, 2008, Kravitz was admitted to Miami Hospital suffering from severe <a href="/wiki/Bronchitis" title="Bronchitis">bronchitis</a>. He had been suffering from a series of severe respiratory tract infections since mid-January, and the illness developed into bronchitis. Kravitz's illness had forced him to postpone Canadian dates and his trip to Europe to promote his album <i>It Is Time for a Love Revolution</i>. On March 19, 2008 he canceled the South American part of his tour due to the same illness. The decision affected planned concerts in <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, Mexico, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. In Argentina Kravitz had a performance in the biggest rock festival there, Quilmes Rock Fest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On July 15, 2008, Lenny was honored in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, Italy with the key to the city in a special toast ceremony for his work with the United Nations Millennium Campaign to end <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">world poverty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<p>Kravitz made his feature film acting debut in <i><a href="/wiki/Precious_(film)" title="Precious (film)">Precious</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-varietyoct07_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-varietyoct07-24">[24]</a></sup> which premiered at the Sundance Film Festival in January 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-varietydec08_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-varietydec08-25">[25]</a></sup> During the Italian leg of his 53 date Let Love Rule (20)09 European tour, Kravitz had been instructed to cancel some shows due to a bad bout of <a href="/wiki/Bronchitis" title="Bronchitis">bronchitis</a>. Under strict orders from his doctor, the singer was told to rest so that he could make a quick recovery and return to the stage for the rest of his tour. Shows scheduled for Rome on June 5 and Brescia on June 6 were postponed to late July.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9312:_U2_tour_and_Black_and_White_America">2009–12: U2 tour and <i>Black and White America</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lenny_Kravitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2009–12: U2 tour and Black and White America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kravitz's next album, tentatively titled <i>Funk</i>, was tentatively re-titled <i>Negrophilia</i> and was due out sometime in 2010. This is believed to be a project that Kravitz has been working on since 1997. Some of the original tracks for <i>Funk</i> were recorded while he was in New Orleans at <a href="/wiki/Allen_Toussaint" title="Allen Toussaint">Allen Toussaint</a>'s studio while taking a break for several months from recording in New York City. A video on Kravitz's Twitter page shows him working on one of the songs for the album, called "Super Love", in his GTS studios in the Bahamas. Another video shows him working on another track titled "Life Ain't Never Been Better Than It Is Now" in his GTS Studios. On his Twitter page, Kravitz said that the album title was <i>Negrophilia</i> but then "felt like something else". Eventually the album was later named <i><a href="/wiki/Black_and_White_America" title="Black and White America">Black and White America</a></i>. The album was released on August 22, 2011 in Europe and August 30, 2011 in the U.S.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The text near this tag is dated.">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On February 20, 2011 the first single "Come on Get It" was released. On June 6, 2011 the second single "<a href="/wiki/Stand_(Lenny_Kravitz_song)" title="Stand (Lenny Kravitz song)">Stand</a>" was released. It was announced that Kravitz would be supporting <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a> on their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/360_Tour" title="360 Tour">360 Tour</a> on the second North American leg in 2010. Kravitz agreed to support them for four shows. Though the shows were postponed until 2011, Kravitz remained committed for four of the shows.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The text near this tag is dated.">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup> In June 2010 it was announced that Kravitz would guest star on an episode of the upcoming season of <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 1, 2011, Kravitz was honored with one of the highest cultural awards in France when he was made an Officer of the <a href="/wiki/Ordre_des_Arts_et_des_Lettres" title="Ordre des Arts et des Lettres">Ordre des Arts et des Lettres</a> by French cultural minister Frederic Mitterrand in Paris. Kravitz stated he was "particularly touched" to receive the award in France as his success in the country pre-dated his success in the United States and still enjoys great record sales in the country today.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Kravitz joins other American recipients such as Martin Scorsese, George Clooney, and Bob Dylan. On February 26, 2012, he performed at the Daytona International Speedway (several songs carried live on Fox) for the Daytona 500, the opening race of the 2012 NASCAR Sprint Cup season. A sample of "Are You Gonna Go My Way" was used in American singer <a href="/wiki/Chris_Willis" title="Chris Willis">Chris Willis</a>'s single "Too Much In Love", released on August 16, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>

<p>Lenny Kravitz played the part of Katniss' creative stylist, Cinna, in the first two <i>Hunger Games</i> films, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film)" title="The Hunger Games (film)">The Hunger Games</a></i> released on March 23, 2012<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Catching_Fire" title="The Hunger Games: Catching Fire">The Hunger Games: Catching Fire</a></i> released on November 22, 2013.</p>
<p>On September 23, 2014, Lenny Kravitz released his tenth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Strut_(album)" title="Strut (album)">Strut</a></i> on his own Roxie Records via <a href="/wiki/Kobalt_Label_Services" title="Kobalt Label Services">Kobalt Label Services</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Chamber_(Lenny_Kravitz_song)" title="The Chamber (Lenny Kravitz song)">The Chamber</a>" was released on June 24, 2014, followed by "Sex" on August 6 and "New York City" on October 21. along with the title track on February 17 and then the final single "The Pleasure And The Pain" on June 26.</p>
<p>On February 1, 2015, Kravitz performed alongside <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLIX_halftime_show" title="Super Bowl XLIX halftime show">Super Bowl XLIX halftime show</a>.</p>
<p>On August 4, 2015, Kravitz was performing a concert in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>. During a guitar solo, he squatted down, causing his leather pants to rip. Kravitz was not wearing underwear <a href="/wiki/Wardrobe_malfunction" title="Wardrobe malfunction">and his genitals were briefly exposed to the audience</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> He did not face any legal repercussions from the incident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a designer, Kravitz launched a furniture collection in partnership with CB2 in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1985, Kravitz's parents divorced, which had a profound impact on him. His relationship with his father became extremely strained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Kravitz focused on his music to help him get through this period. That year, Kravitz met actress <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Bonet" title="Lisa Bonet">Lisa Bonet</a> backstage at a <a href="/wiki/New_Edition" title="New Edition">New Edition</a> concert. Bonet worked on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cosby_Show" title="The Cosby Show">The Cosby Show</a></i>, the number one rated show on television. They were close friends for two years before falling in love. Like Kravitz, Bonet is half African-American and half Russian-Jewish. Kravitz moved back to New York City where <i>The Cosby Show</i> was produced in 1987, moving in with Bonet.</p>
<p>Kravitz and Bonet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elope" title="Elope">eloped</a> to be married on November 16, 1987, her 20th birthday, in a <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> ceremony. Kravitz, still known as Romeo Blue at the time, suddenly found himself in the headlines of <a href="/wiki/Tabloid_journalism" title="Tabloid journalism">tabloid</a> newspapers. They had a daughter, <a href="/wiki/Zo%C3%AB_Kravitz" title="Zoë Kravitz">Zoë Isabella Kravitz</a>, born on December 1, 1988,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> who became an actress, singer, and model. Kravitz and Bonet divorced amicably in 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> He then dated French singer and actress <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Paradis" title="Vanessa Paradis">Vanessa Paradis</a> until 1997. In 2001, he began dating model <a href="/wiki/Adriana_Lima" title="Adriana Lima">Adriana Lima</a>. In 2002, the couple was engaged, but less than a year later, the engagement was called off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kravitz identifies himself as a Christian in a religious sense, "through choice but I'm also a Jew, it's all the same to me".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> During another interview Kravitz stated, "I'm half Jewish, I'm half black, I look in-between."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> He also notes that spirituality "has been an important issue in my growth", given his upbringing by parents of different faiths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Such spirituality is prominently featured in many of his songs, such as the lyrics on his album <i>Baptism</i>, and having his back inscribed with a tattoo stating, "My Heart Belongs to Jesus Christ."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> As late as 2011, Kravitz stated that his 2005 religious commitment to remain celibate until remarriage was unchanged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
