<p><i><b>Red</b></i> is a 2010 American <a href="/wiki/Action_film#Sub-genres" title="Action film">action comedy film</a> inspired by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Limited_series" title="Limited series">limited comic book series</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_(WildStorm_comics)" title="Red (WildStorm comics)">of the same name</a> created by <a href="/wiki/Warren_Ellis" title="Warren Ellis">Warren Ellis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cully_Hamner" title="Cully Hamner">Cully Hamner</a> and published by the <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">DC Comics</a> imprint <a href="/wiki/Homage_Comics" title="Homage Comics">Homage</a>. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">Bruce Willis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Freeman" title="Morgan Freeman">Morgan Freeman</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Malkovich" title="John Malkovich">John Malkovich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mary-Louise_Parker" title="Mary-Louise Parker">Mary-Louise Parker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helen_Mirren" title="Helen Mirren">Helen Mirren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karl_Urban" title="Karl Urban">Karl Urban</a>, with German film director <a href="/wiki/Robert_Schwentke" title="Robert Schwentke">Robert Schwentke</a> directing a <a href="/wiki/Screenplay" title="Screenplay">screenplay</a> by Jon Hoeber and Erich Hoeber. In the film version, the title is derived from the designation of former <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">CIA</a> Agent Frank Moses (Bruce Willis), meaning "Retired, Extremely Dangerous".</p>
<p>The film was released on October 15, 2010. The film grossed $199 million worldwide. In 2011, the film received a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Motion_Picture_%E2%80%93_Musical_or_Comedy" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy">Best Musical or Comedy Film</a>. A sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Red_2_(film)" title="Red 2 (film)">Red 2</a></i>, was released on July 19, 2013. Another sequel, <i>Red 3</i>, is in development.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_(2010_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frank Moses, retired <a href="/wiki/Black_operation" title="Black operation">black-ops</a> <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">CIA</a> agent, lives alone in <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>. Lonely, Frank often chats on the phone with Sarah Ross, a worker at the <a href="/wiki/General_Services_Administration" title="General Services Administration">General Services Administration</a>'s Pension Office in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a>. He creates opportunities to talk to her by tearing up his pension checks and calling her to say they had never arrived.</p>
<p>One night, a "<a href="/wiki/Wetwork" title="Wetwork">wetwork</a>" (assassination) squad raids Frank's house and attempts to kill him, but he easily wipes them out. Knowing they have tapped his phone, he believes Sarah will be targeted. In Kansas City, as Sarah refuses to go with him, he forcibly ties her up and gags her with duct tape. Meanwhile, CIA agent William Cooper is assigned by his boss, Cynthia Wilkes, to hunt down and kill Frank.</p>
<p>To find out who is targeting him, Frank tracks down his old associates for help. He goes to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Orleans,_Louisiana" title="New Orleans, Louisiana">New Orleans, Louisiana</a> and visits his C.I.A. <a href="/wiki/Mentorship" title="Mentorship">mentor</a>, Joe Matheson, who now lives in a <a href="/wiki/Nursing_home_care" title="Nursing home care">nursing home</a>. Joe tells Frank that the same hit squad murdered a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reporter" title="Reporter">reporter</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>. Locked in a motel by Frank, Sarah escapes. Another agent, posing as a police officer, tries to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kidnap" title="Kidnap">kidnap</a> her, but Frank returns in time. Cooper attacks them, but Frank tricks the police into arresting Cooper and escapes with Sarah. The two head to New York City and find clues left behind by the deceased reporter, which leads them to a hit list.</p>
<p>They then find Marvin Boggs, another former black ops agent and a paranoid conspiracy theorist. Marvin tells them the people on the list, including Frank and Marvin, are connected to a secret 1981 mission in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>. Another person on the list, Gabriel Singer, is still alive. The trio tracks down Singer, who tells them that the mission involved extracting a person from a village. Singer is then assassinated by a helicopter-borne machine-gunner, and the team escapes as Cooper closes in.</p>
<p>Frank goes to ex-Russian secret agent Ivan Simanov, who helps him infiltrate <a href="/wiki/George_Bush_Center_for_Intelligence" title="George Bush Center for Intelligence">CIA headquarters</a>. In the CIA archive, the records keeper, who has much respect for Frank, simply hands him the Guatemala file. Frank confronts Cooper in his office and the two have a vicious fight. Though victorious, Frank is shot during his escape. Having escaped an attempt on his life, Joe arrives and helps extract the team. They hide out in the home of former <a href="/wiki/Wetwork" title="Wetwork">wetwork</a> agent Victoria (<a href="/wiki/Helen_Mirren" title="Helen Mirren">Helen Mirren</a>), who treats Frank's wound and joins the team.</p>
<p>The file gives them clue to the next lead, Alexander Dunning, an <a href="/wiki/Arms_trafficking" title="Arms trafficking">illegal arms dealer</a>. Frank, Marvin and Joe enter Dunning's mansion, with Joe posing as a buyer, while Victoria and Sarah keep watch outside. They interrogate Dunning, who reveals the target for extraction was the now–<a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_United_States" title="Vice President of the United States">Vice President</a> Robert Stanton (<a href="/wiki/Julian_McMahon" title="Julian McMahon">Julian McMahon</a>). Stanton ordered the hit on the people involved in the mission to hide the fact that he massacred village civilians.</p>
<p>Cooper and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FBI" title="FBI">FBI</a> surround Dunning's mansion. Cooper tries to negotiate Frank's surrender, and Frank tells him about the Vice President's treachery. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terminally_ill" title="Terminally ill">terminally ill</a> Joe pretends to be Frank, walks outside, and is killed by sniper from the Vice President's personal hit squad. The confusion, as well as Victoria's cover fire, buys the team enough time to leave the mansion, but Sarah is captured. They escape with the help of Ivan, who is Victoria's old flame. Frank calls Cooper from his family's phone and warns him against harming Sarah.</p>
<p>The team, along with Ivan, kidnaps Stanton. Frank calls Cooper, offering to trade Stanton for Sarah. At the meeting point, Dunning arrives. After a short dialogue, Dunning injures Stanton, revealing himself and Cynthia Wilkes to be masterminds behind the assassinations. Disgusted with Wilkes' corruption, Cooper pretends to arrest Frank, but shoots Wilkes. Marvin and Victoria kill Dunning's bodyguards, and Frank kills Dunning by crushing his windpipe. Cooper lets Frank's team go. As they leave the scene, Frank and Sarah are eager to start a new life together.</p>
<p>Ivan reminds Frank of his favor. A few months later, Frank and Marvin are in <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldova</a> with a stolen nuclear device. They flee from Moldovan Army troops with Marvin wearing a dress and in a wooden wheelbarrow being pushed by Frank.</p>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">Bruce Willis</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filming_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-filming-3">[3]</a></sup> as Francis "Frank" Moses</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_(2010_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><span id="Development"></span> Gregory Noveck, a representative of <a href="/wiki/DC_Comics" title="DC Comics">DC Comics</a> working in Hollywood to get their titles made into films, wanted the comic developed but Warner Brothers was not interested. The creators of the comic exercised their right to go elsewhere but this required approval from all divisions of Warner including television before it could be approved. After several years, in 2008 Noveck was allowed to take the project elsewhere, to Mark Vahradian at Di Bonaventura Productions. Unusually this made it the first film from DC not produced by Warner Bros., post-purchase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thr-escape_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-thr-escape-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2008, <a href="/wiki/Summit_Entertainment" title="Summit Entertainment">Summit Entertainment</a> announced plans to adapt <a href="/wiki/Warren_Ellis" title="Warren Ellis">Warren Ellis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cully_Hamner" title="Cully Hamner">Cully Hamner</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_(WildStorm_comics)" title="Red (WildStorm comics)">Red</a></i>. <i>Red</i> was adapted for the big screen by brothers Erich and Jon Hoeber, who also wrote the adaptations of <i><a href="/wiki/Whiteout_(2009_film)" title="Whiteout (2009 film)">Whiteout</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alice_(2009)" title="Alice (2009)">Alice</a></i>. The project was produced by <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_di_Bonaventura" title="Lorenzo di Bonaventura">Lorenzo di Bonaventura</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/G.I._Joe:_The_Rise_of_Cobra" title="G.I. Joe: The Rise of Cobra">GI Joe</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Transformers_(film)" title="Transformers (film)">Transformers</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-red_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-red-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p><span id="Casting"></span> By April 2009, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">Bruce Willis</a> was reportedly in discussions with Summit to take the starring role of Frank Moses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-willis_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-willis-13">[13]</a></sup> It was reported in July 2009 that <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Freeman" title="Morgan Freeman">Morgan Freeman</a> was in talks to co-star alongside <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">Bruce Willis</a> in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-freeman_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-freeman-14">[14]</a></sup> Also in July 2009, Robert Schwentke, the director of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Time_Traveler%27s_Wife_(film)" title="The Time Traveler's Wife (film)">The Time Traveler's Wife</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Flightplan" title="Flightplan">Flightplan</a></i>, was in negotiations to direct <i>Red</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-schwentke_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-schwentke-15">[15]</a></sup> In August 2009 Schwentke confirmed to <i><a href="/wiki/MTV_News" title="MTV News">MTV News</a></i> that he was on board. He stated that he loved the script but there are differences between the comic and the movie stating; "It's very funny, which the comic book isn't ... It's not as violent as the comic book," and that "The script that I've read is obviously different from the comic, because I don't think the comic gives you enough for a two-hour movie."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-schwentke2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-schwentke2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2009, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Helen_Mirren" title="Helen Mirren">Helen Mirren</a> would work alongside Freeman and Willis in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mirren_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-mirren-7">[7]</a></sup> It was also reported in November 2009 that <a href="/wiki/John_C._Reilly" title="John C. Reilly">John C. Reilly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mary-Louise_Parker" title="Mary-Louise Parker">Mary-Louise Parker</a> were in negotiations to join the cast. Reilly would play a retired CIA agent who is paranoid that everyone is out to kill him. Parker would play the romantic interest, a federal pension worker who becomes embroiled in the Willis character's struggle to stay alive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parker_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-parker-16">[16]</a></sup> In the same month <a href="/wiki/Julian_McMahon" title="Julian McMahon">Julian McMahon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Borgnine" title="Ernest Borgnine">Ernest Borgnine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Dreyfuss" title="Richard Dreyfuss">Richard Dreyfuss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Cox_(actor)" title="Brian Cox (actor)">Brian Cox</a> entered negotiations to join the cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcmahon_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-mcmahon-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p><span id="Writing"></span> In December 2009, creator Warren Ellis stated on his mailing list that "(I) Read the <i>RED</i> script. Not bad. Not the book, but not bad. Funny. Especially when you know the casting. Very tight piece of work. Talked to the producers last week. They're all kind of giddy over the casting coups. Who wouldn't want to see Helen Mirren with a sniper rifle?"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ellis_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ellis-17">[17]</a></sup> Also in December 2009 Summit Entertainment announced a release date of October 22, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-releasedate_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-releasedate-18">[18]</a></sup> The same month <a href="/wiki/James_Remar" title="James Remar">James Remar</a> was cast in an unspecified role,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-remar_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-remar-10">[10]</a></sup> in addition to <a href="/wiki/Karl_Urban" title="Karl Urban">Karl Urban</a> as "Cooper".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-urban_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-urban-8">[8]</a></sup> In January 2010 it was reported that <a href="/wiki/John_Malkovich" title="John Malkovich">John Malkovich</a> had signed to star opposite Bruce Willis, replacing John C. Reilly, who exited the role in late December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-malkovich_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-malkovich-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><span id="Locations"></span> <a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> began on January 18, 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto, Canada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filming_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-filming-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>Red</i> was shot in and around the Toronto metropolitan area for nine weeks before moving on to the road and ending in <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a> in late March for the final two weeks of principal photography.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filming_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-filming-3">[3]</a></sup> Filming in the <a href="/wiki/French_Quarter" title="French Quarter">French Quarter</a> of New Orleans commenced in March 2010.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Additional photography was shot for a <a href="/wiki/Post-credits_scene" title="Post-credits scene">post-credits scene</a> in Louisiana in August 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_(2010_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_(2010_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span id="Critics"></span> <i>Red</i> has a 72% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> based on 193 reviews and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weighted_mean" title="Weighted mean">average</a> rating of 6.4/10. The consensus reads: "It may not be the killer thrill ride you'd expect from an action movie with a cast of this caliber, but <i>Red</i> still thoroughly outshines most of its big-budget counterparts with its wit and style."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> gave the film a score of 61/100 based on a <a href="/wiki/Normalization_(statistics)" title="Normalization (statistics)">normalized</a> rating of 37 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-meta-25">[25]</a></sup> Justin Chang of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> stated <i>Red</i> is "An amusing, light-footed caper about a team of aging CIA veterans rudely forced out of retirement".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> John DeFore of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> stated "Although tailor-made for genre fans, it benefits from flavors of humor and romance that keep its appeal from being fanboy-only".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Conversely, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> gave the film two stars out of four, stating that it is "neither a good movie nor a bad one. It features actors that we like doing things we wish were more interesting."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/A._O._Scott" title="A. O. Scott">A. O. Scott</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> said, "It is possible to have a good time at RED, but it is not a very good movie. It doesn't really try to be, and given the present state of the Hollywood economy, this may be a wise choice".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Turan" title="Kenneth Turan">Kenneth Turan</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> said, "It's not that it doesn't have effective moments, it's that it doesn't have as many as it thinks it does. The film's inescapable air of glib self-satisfaction is not only largely unearned, it's downright irritating".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_(2010_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On its opening weekend <i>Red</i> earned an estimated $22.5 million on around 4,100 screens at 3,255 locations, coming in second behind <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jackass_3-D" title="Jackass 3-D">Jackass 3-D</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The film closed in theaters on February 3, 2011, grossing over $90 million in the United States and $108.6 million in foreign markets. The film received an overall gross of $199 million worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_(2010_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><span id="Sequel"></span><span id="Sequels"></span></p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Red_2_(film)" title="Red 2 (film)">Red 2 (film)</a></div>
<p>The film's financial success surpassed producer <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_di_Bonaventura" title="Lorenzo di Bonaventura">Lorenzo di Bonaventura</a>'s expectations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-THR-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-34">[34]</a></sup> In October 2011, Summit Entertainment officially announced that <i>Red 2</i> would be released on August 2, 2013, with Jon and Erich Hoeber rehired to write the screenplay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV2_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTV2-35">[35]</a></sup> In March 2013, the film's release date was moved from August 2, 2013 to July 19, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SHH_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-SHH-36">[36]</a></sup> The sequel fared less than its predecessor both critically and financially. The film received mixed reviews from critics and grossed $148.1 million worldwide.</p>
