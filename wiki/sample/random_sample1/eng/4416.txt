<p><i><b>Red</b></i> is a play by American writer <a href="/wiki/John_Logan_(writer)" title="John Logan (writer)">John Logan</a> about artist <a href="/wiki/Mark_Rothko" title="Mark Rothko">Mark Rothko</a> first produced by the <a href="/wiki/Donmar_Warehouse" title="Donmar Warehouse">Donmar Warehouse</a>, London in December 2009. The original production was directed by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Grandage" title="Michael Grandage">Michael Grandage</a> and performed by <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Molina" title="Alfred Molina">Alfred Molina</a> as Rothko and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Redmayne" title="Eddie Redmayne">Eddie Redmayne</a> as his fictional<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> assistant Ken.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-donmar_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-donmar-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The production, with its two leads, transferred to <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Golden_Theater" title="John Golden Theater">John Golden Theater</a> for a limited engagement which began on March 11, 2010 and closed on June 27.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It was the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> winner for <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award_for_Best_Play" title="Tony Award for Best Play">Best Play</a>. Additionally, Redmayne won a 2010 <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tony_Award_for_Best_Performance_by_a_Featured_Actor_in_a_Play" title="Tony Award for Best Performance by a Featured Actor in a Play">Best Performance by a Featured Actor in a Play</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tonys_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tonys-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_(play)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"There is only one thing I fear in life, my friend... One day the black will swallow the red."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-donmar_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-donmar-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mark_Rothko" title="Mark Rothko">Mark Rothko</a> is in his New York studio in 1958-9, having been commissioned to paint a group of murals for the expensive and exclusive Four Seasons restaurant. He gives orders to his assistant, Ken, as he mixes the paints, makes the frames, and paints the canvases. Ken, however, brashly questions Rothko's theories of art and his acceding to work on such a commercial project. For his part, Rothko dislikes the rise of <a href="/wiki/Pop_art" title="Pop art">Pop art</a>.</p>
<p>Ultimately, Rothko stops working on the project and decides to return the money. He explains to Ken that the Four Seasons is an inappropriate place for his murals to be seen.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_(play)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reviews for the London production were mixed for the play but positive for Molina's performance. Michael Billington in <i>The Guardian</i> wrote: "Alfred Molina, with his large frame and beetling eyebrows, has exactly the fierce intensity of an artist whose paintings were a dynamic battle between Apollo and Dionysus, ."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In reviewing the Broadway production, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Kuchwara" title="Michael Kuchwara">Michael Kuchwara</a> of the <i>Associated Press</i> wrote: "They are the tantalizing first words of <i>Red,</i> John Logan's engrossing, often enthralling new play about art, an artist and the act of creation." Those first words were "<i>What do you see?</i>" <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> wrote that "Alfred Molina is majestic".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_(play)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The play won the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Drama_League_Award" title="Drama League Award">Drama League Award</a> for Distinguished Production of a Play and Molina won the Distinguished Performance Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The play was nominated for a total of seven <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Awards</a>, winning six, including: Best Play, Best Featured Actor in a Play for Eddie Redmayne, Best Direction of a Play for Michael Grandage, Best Scenic Design of a Play for Christopher Oram, Best Lighting Design of a Play for Neil Austin, and Best Sound Design of a Play for Adam Cork.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tonys_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tonys-5">[5]</a></sup> In addition, Alfred Molina was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award_for_Best_Actor_in_a_Play" title="Tony Award for Best Actor in a Play">Tony Award for Best Actor in a Play</a> for his role as Mark Rothko. All in all, it received the most wins out of any other production that season. The play also won the <a href="/wiki/Drama_Desk_Award" title="Drama Desk Award">Drama Desk Award</a> for Outstanding Play while Grandage and Austin were honoured with Drama Desk Awards for their work. Molina, Cork and Oram were also similarly nominated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_productions">Other productions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_(play)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other productions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In October 2013 "Rojo" premiered in Chile at <a href="/wiki/Centro_Mori" title="Centro Mori">Centro Mori</a> Bellavista theatre, starring Luis Gnecco and Martin Bacigalupo, directed by Rodrigo Sepúlveda and produced by <a class="external text" href="http://www.thecowcompany.com/" rel="nofollow">The Cow Company</a>.</p>
<p>Geva Theatre Center in Rochester, NY mounted an acclaimed production of RED from October 20 - November 15, 2015 starring Stephen Caffrey as Mark Rothko and John Ford-Dunker as Ken. Directed by Skip Greer, the production featured scenic design by Robert Koharchik, costume design by Ann M. Emo, lighting design by Kendall Smith and sound design by Dan Roach.</p>
<p>Red also played in New National Theater - The PIT, Tokyo Japan started from August 21 - October 4, 2015, starring Tanaka <a href="/wiki/Tetsushi_Tanaka" title="Tetsushi Tanaka">Tetsushi Tanaka</a> as Rothko and Oguri <a href="/wiki/Shun_Oguri" title="Shun Oguri">Shun Oguri</a> as Ken, directed by Eriko Ogawa.</p>
<p>On October 29th, 2016 it will open at The Junction in Dubai before transferring to the JamJar. The production will star Osman Aboubakr as Rothko and Deepak Venugopal as Ken, with direction by <a href="/wiki/Alex_Broun" title="Alex Broun">Alex Broun</a>.</p>
