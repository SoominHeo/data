<p><b>Red rice</b> is a variety of <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a> that is colored red by its <a href="/wiki/Anthocyanin" title="Anthocyanin">anthocyanin</a> content. It is usually eaten unhulled or partially hulled, and has a red husk, rather than the more common brown. Red rice has a nutty flavor. Compared to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polished_rice" title="Polished rice">polished rice</a>, it has the higher nutritional value of rices eaten with the <a href="/wiki/Cereal_germ" title="Cereal germ">germ</a> intact.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Varieties">Varieties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_rice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Varieties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Varieties of red rice include:</p>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Oryza_longistaminata" title="Oryza longistaminata">Oryza longistaminata</a></i>, also known as red rice</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Oryza_punctata" title="Oryza punctata">Oryza punctata</a></i>, also known as red rice</li>
<li>Red rice, also known as <a href="/wiki/Weedy_rice" title="Weedy rice">weedy rice</a>, a low-yielding rice variety that persists as a weed in fields of better-quality rice</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rakthashali" title="Rakthashali">Rakthashali</a>, a rare rice variety</li>
<li>Thai <a href="/wiki/Red_Cargo_rice" title="Red Cargo rice">Red Cargo rice</a>, a non-glutinous long grain rice variety</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bhutanese_red_rice" title="Bhutanese red rice">Bhutanese red rice</a>, a medium-grain rice grown in the Kingdom of Bhutan in the eastern Himalayas</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Camargue_red_rice" title="Camargue red rice">Camargue red rice</a>, a relatively new variety of rice cultivated in the wetlands of the Camargue region of southern France</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Matta_rice" title="Matta rice">Matta rice</a> Kerala Matta rice, also known as Rosematta rice, Palakkadan Matta rice, Kerala Red rice, and Red parboiled rice, is an indigenous variety of rice grown in Palakkad District of Kerala. It is popular in Kerala and Sri Lanka, where it is used for idlies and appams, and eaten plain.</li>
</ul>
