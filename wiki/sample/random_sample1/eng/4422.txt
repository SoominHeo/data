<p><b>Red Hill</b> is a borough in <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Montgomery County, Pennsylvania">Montgomery County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, and is primarily a lake shore lowlands suburban community northwest of Philadelphia in the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_River" title="Delaware River">Delaware River Valley</a> <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">watershed</a>, as is the balance of its county. The population was 2,383 at the 2010 census.</p>
<p>It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Upper_Perkiomen_School_District" title="Upper Perkiomen School District">Upper Perkiomen School District</a>.</p>
<p>It is also part of the strip of small towns that run together along <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Route_29" title="Pennsylvania Route 29">Route 29</a>; Red Hill, <a href="/wiki/Pennsburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Pennsburg, Pennsylvania">Pennsburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/East_Greenville,_Pennsylvania" title="East Greenville, Pennsylvania">East Greenville</a>, colloquially known as 'Red Pennsville'.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_Hill,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Red_Hill_Historic_District" title="Red Hill Historic District">Red Hill Historic District</a> was added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_Hill,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Red Hill is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Red_Hill%2C_Pennsylvania&amp;params=40_22_35_N_75_29_4_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">40°22′35″N</span> <span class="longitude">75°29′4″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">40.37639°N 75.48444°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">40.37639; -75.48444</span></span></span></a></span> (40.376288, -75.484308).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the borough has a total area of 0.7 square miles (1.8 km<sup>2</sup>), all of it land.</p>

<p>As of the 2010 census, the borough was 96.4% White, 1.4% Black or African American, 0.7% Asian, and 1.3% were two or more races. 2.1% of the population were of Hispanic or Latino ancestry <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.usatoday.com/news/nation/census/profile/PA" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>.</p>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-5">[5]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 2,196 people, 899 households, and 576 families residing in the borough. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 3,046.0 people per square mile (1,177.6/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 944 housing units at an average density of 1,309.4 per square mile (506.2/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the borough was 97.81% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.32% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.05% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.09% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.05% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.32% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.37% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 1.55% of the population.</p>
<p>There were 899 households, out of which 27.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 52.5% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 8.1% had a female householder with no husband present, and 35.9% were non-families. 30.1% of all households were made up of individuals, and 19.2% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.44 and the average family size was 3.08.</p>
<p>In the borough the population was spread out, with 23.8% under the age of 18, 7.4% from 18 to 24, 28.4% from 25 to 44, 22.4% from 45 to 64, and 18.1% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 38 years. For every 100 females there were 91.1 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 87.2 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the borough was $45,313, and the median income for a family was $58,529. Males had a median income of $35,857 versus $26,295 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the borough was $20,633. About 3.1% of families and 4.1% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 2.3% of those under age 18 and 10.8% of those age 65 or over.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_government">Politics and government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red_Hill,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Politics and government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Red Hill has a <a href="/wiki/City_manager" title="City manager">city manager</a> form of government with a <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a> and borough council.</p>
<p>The borough is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives,_Pennsylvania_District_6" title="United States House of Representatives, Pennsylvania District 6">Sixth Congressional District</a> (represented by Rep. <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Costello" title="Ryan Costello">Ryan Costello</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pennsylvania%27s_147th_Representative_District" title="Pennsylvania's 147th Representative District">Pennsylvania's 147th Representative District</a> (represented by Rep. <a href="/wiki/Marcy_Toepel" title="Marcy Toepel">Marcy Toepel</a>) and the 24th State Senate District (represented by Sen. <a href="/wiki/Bob_Mensch" title="Bob Mensch">Bob Mensch</a>).</p>
