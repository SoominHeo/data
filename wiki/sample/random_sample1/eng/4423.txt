<p><b>Ledipasvir</b> is a drug for the treatment of <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_C" title="Hepatitis C">hepatitis C</a> that was developed by <a href="/wiki/Gilead_Sciences" title="Gilead Sciences">Gilead Sciences</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After completing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phase_III_clinical_trials" title="Phase III clinical trials">Phase III clinical trials</a>, on February 10, 2014 Gilead filed for U.S. approval of a ledipasvir/<a href="/wiki/Sofosbuvir" title="Sofosbuvir">sofosbuvir</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fixed-dose_combination" title="Fixed-dose combination">fixed-dose combination</a> tablet for genotype 1 hepatitis C.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The ledipasvir/sofosbuvir combination is a direct-acting antiviral agent that interferes with HCV replication and can be used to treat patients with genotypes 1a or 1b without <a href="/wiki/Interferon" title="Interferon">PEG-interferon</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ribavirin" title="Ribavirin">ribavirin</a>.</p>
<p>Ledipasvir is an <a href="/wiki/Discovery_and_development_of_NS5A_inhibitors" title="Discovery and development of NS5A inhibitors">inhibitor</a> of <a href="/wiki/NS5A" title="NS5A">NS5A</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_C_virus" title="Hepatitis C virus">hepatitis C virus</a> protein.</p>
<p>Data presented at the 20th Conference on Retroviruses and Opportunistic Infections in March 2013 showed that a triple regimen of the nucleotide <a href="/wiki/Structural_analog" title="Structural analog">analog</a> inhibitor sofosbuvir, ledipasvir, and <a href="/wiki/Ribavirin" title="Ribavirin">ribavirin</a> produced a 12-week post-treatment sustained virological response (SVR12) rate of 100% for both treatment-naive patients and prior non-responders with HCV genotype 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The sofosbuvir/ledipasvir coformulation is being tested with and without ribavirin. In February 2014 Gilead filed for United States <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration" title="Food and Drug Administration">Food and Drug Administration</a> (FDA) approval of ledipasvir/sofosbuvir oral treatment, without interferon and ribavirin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 10th, 2014 the FDA approved the combination product <a href="/wiki/Ledipasvir/sofosbuvir" title="Ledipasvir/sofosbuvir">ledipasvir/sofosbuvir</a> called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harvoni" title="Harvoni">Harvoni</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medical_uses">Medical uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ledipasvir&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Medical uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ledipasvir is most commonly used in combination with sofosbuvir for treatment in chronic hepatitis C genotype 1 patients. This drug has been tested and shown efficacy in treatment-naive and treatment experienced patients. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ICON_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ICON-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adverse_effects">Adverse effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ledipasvir&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Adverse effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to clinical trials, ledipasvir/sofosbuvir has been very well tolerated with the most common side effects being fatigue and headache.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Package_insert_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Package_insert-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Interactions">Interactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ledipasvir&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Interactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most drug-drug interactions with Harvoni involve <a href="/wiki/P-glycoprotein" title="P-glycoprotein">Pgp</a>-inducers such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._John%E2%80%99s_wort" title="St. John’s wort">St. John’s wort</a> or <a href="/wiki/Rifampicin" title="Rifampicin">rifampicin</a>. Concomitant use will decrease the blood concentration of Harvoni and thus, have reduced therapeutic effects. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Package_insert_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Package_insert-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mechanism_of_action">Mechanism of action</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ledipasvir&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mechanism of action">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ledipasvir inhibits an important viral phosphoprotein, <a href="/wiki/NS5A" title="NS5A">NS5A</a>, which is involved in viral replication, assembly, and secretion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UW_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-UW-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sofosbuvir, on the other hand, is metabolized to a <a href="/wiki/Uridine" title="Uridine">uridine</a> triphosphate mimic, which acts as a <a href="/wiki/RNA" title="RNA">RNA</a> chain terminator when incorporated into RNA by <a href="/wiki/NS5B" title="NS5B">NS5B</a> polymerase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UW_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-UW-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cost">Cost</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ledipasvir&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cost">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Similar to sofosbuvir, the cost of Harvoni has been a controversial topic. It costs $1,125 per pill in the US, translating to $63,000 for an 8-week treatment course, $94,500 for a 12-week treatment course, or $189,000 for a 24-week treatment course. Gilead justifies the cost by outweighing the benefit of curing hepatitis C over the cost of spending double on liver transplants or temporarily treating liver diseases. Gilead has provided a ledipasvir/sofosbuvir assistance program for eligible underserved or underinsured hepatitis C patients who cannot afford the costs of treatment. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UW_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-UW-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2015 Gilead modified the eligibility criteria to receive Support Path benefits for HCV patients in the United States.</p>
