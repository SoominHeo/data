<p><b>Lemony Snicket</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Pen_name" title="Pen name">pen name</a> of American novelist <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Handler" title="Daniel Handler">Daniel Handler</a> (born February 28, 1970). Snicket is the author of several children's books, also serving as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narrator" title="Narrator">narrator</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Series_of_Unfortunate_Events" title="A Series of Unfortunate Events">A Series of Unfortunate Events</a></i> (his best-known work) and a character within it and <i><a href="/wiki/All_the_Wrong_Questions" title="All the Wrong Questions">All the Wrong Questions</a></i>. Because of this, the name "Lemony Snicket" may refer to either the fictional character or the real person.</p>
<p>As a character, Snicket is a harried, troubled writer and photographer <a href="/wiki/False_accusation" title="False accusation">falsely</a> accused of felonies, and is continuously hunted by the police and his enemies, the fire-starting side of the secret organization Volunteer Fire Department (V.F.D.). As a child, he was kidnapped and inducted as a "neophyte" into V.F.D., where he was trained in <a href="/wiki/Rhetoric" title="Rhetoric">rhetoric</a> and sent on seemingly pointless missions, while all connections were severed from his former life, apart from his siblings Jacques and Kit (who were also kidnapped and inducted).</p>
<p>In the organization, he met and fell in love with a peer named <a href="/wiki/Beatrice_Baudelaire" title="Beatrice Baudelaire">Beatrice</a>, whom he eventually decided to marry. He was falsely accused of murder and arson after a series of unfortunate events (after which the real-world series is in some ways named). Eventually, the fallacies grew so much that <i>The Daily Punctilio</i> reported his death. Beatrice eventually moved on and married <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bertrand_Baudelaire" title="Bertrand Baudelaire">Bertrand Baudelaire</a>, becoming the mother of <a href="/wiki/Violet_Baudelaire" title="Violet Baudelaire">Violet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Klaus_Baudelaire" title="Klaus Baudelaire">Klaus</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sunny_Baudelaire" title="Sunny Baudelaire">Sunny Baudelaire</a>, the protagonists of <i>A Series of Unfortunate Events</i>. Fourteen years thereafter, Beatrice and Bertrand were murdered in a house fire, leaving the Baudelaires orphans. Snicket feels indebted to his former fiancée and embarks on a quest to chronicle the lives of the Baudelaire children until they become old enough to face the troubles of the world on their own.</p>
<p>Snicket is the subject of a fictional autobiography entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Lemony_Snicket:_The_Unauthorized_Autobiography" title="Lemony Snicket: The Unauthorized Autobiography">Lemony Snicket: The Unauthorized Autobiography</a></i> which contains an introduction from a fictionalized version of Daniel Handler. A pamphlet called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/13_Shocking_Secrets_You%27ll_Wish_You_Never_Knew_About_Lemony_Snicket" title="13 Shocking Secrets You'll Wish You Never Knew About Lemony Snicket">13 Shocking Secrets You'll Wish You Never Knew About Lemony Snicket</a></i> was released in promotion of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_End_(A_Series_of_Unfortunate_Events)" title="The End (A Series of Unfortunate Events)">The End</a></i>. Other works by Snicket include <i><a href="/wiki/The_Baby_in_the_Manger" title="The Baby in the Manger">The Baby in the Manger</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Composer_Is_Dead" title="The Composer Is Dead">The Composer Is Dead</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Horseradish:_Bitter_Truths_You_Can%27t_Avoid" title="Horseradish: Bitter Truths You Can't Avoid">Horseradish: Bitter Truths You Can't Avoid</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Latke_Who_Couldn%27t_Stop_Screaming" title="The Latke Who Couldn't Stop Screaming">The Latke Who Couldn't Stop Screaming</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lump_of_Coal" title="The Lump of Coal">The Lump of Coal</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/All_the_Wrong_Questions" title="All the Wrong Questions">All the Wrong Questions</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/13_Words" title="13 Words">13 Words</a></i>.</p>
<p>In the film, <i><a href="/wiki/Lemony_Snicket%27s_A_Series_of_Unfortunate_Events" title="Lemony Snicket's A Series of Unfortunate Events">Lemony Snicket's A Series of Unfortunate Events</a></i>, Lemony Snicket is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Jude_Law" title="Jude Law">Jude Law</a>, who documents the events of the film on a typewriter from inside a clock tower. In the <a href="/wiki/Lemony_Snicket%27s_A_Series_of_Unfortunate_Events_(video_game)" title="Lemony Snicket's A Series of Unfortunate Events (video game)">video game</a> based on the film his voice is provided by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Curry" title="Tim Curry">Tim Curry</a>. In the <i><a href="/wiki/A_Series_of_Unfortunate_Events_(TV_series)" title="A Series of Unfortunate Events (TV series)">Series of Unfortunate Events</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> series, Snicket is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Warburton" title="Patrick Warburton">Patrick Warburton</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name_origin">Name origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lemony_Snicket&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Lemony Snicket</i> originally came from research from Handler's first book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Basic_Eight" title="The Basic Eight">The Basic Eight</a></i>. Handler wanted to receive material from organizations that he found "offensive or funny" but did not want to use his real name, so he invented "Lemony Snicket" as a pseudonym.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beatrice_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-beatrice-1">[1]</a></sup> The name's similarity to <a href="/wiki/Jiminy_Cricket" title="Jiminy Cricket">Jiminy Cricket</a> was "likely a <a href="/wiki/Freudian_slip" title="Freudian slip">Freudian slip</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Handler described Jiminy Cricket as "exactly the kind of overly moralistic, cheerful narrator who I despise." When writing <i>A Series of Unfortunate Events</i>, he and his editor thought that the books should be published under the narrator's name, rather than his.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beatrice_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-beatrice-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>Lemony Snicket came from a family of three children. His brother Jacques (who was murdered in <i>The Vile Village</i>) and sister Kit were also V.F.D. members and friends of the Baudelaire parents. Both Jacques and Kit appear as supporting characters in the <i>Series of Unfortunate Events</i> books. He also knew <a href="/wiki/Count_Olaf" title="Count Olaf">Count Olaf</a> in his early life, as the two attended school together. In his youth, Lemony Snicket attended a V.F.D.-run boarding school with several other characters from the series. He received later tuition at a V.F.D. headquarters in the Mortmain Mountains, and was employed by a newspaper called <i>The Daily Punctilio</i> after graduating. He was an <a href="/wiki/Obituary" title="Obituary">obituary</a> spell-checker and theater critic.</p>
<p>Lemony Snicket conducted an ill-fated romance with the actress <a href="/wiki/Beatrice_Baudelaire" title="Beatrice Baudelaire">Beatrice Baudelaire</a>. Lemony and Beatrice were at one point engaged to be married, but Beatrice broke off the engagement for unclear reasons, and returned her ring to Lemony at some point later, along with a two-hundred page book explaining why the two could not be wed. It is revealed that Lemony Snicket was believed to be dead by the Baudelaire parents, as <i>The End</i> states that, following with the tradition of naming children after someone who has died, Violet was to be named Lemony if she was a boy. Beatrice most probably discovered the truth sometime after marrying <a href="/wiki/Baudelaire_family#Bertrand_Baudelaire" title="Baudelaire family">Bertrand Baudelaire</a>, as she would presumably have no reason to send the ring and book to Lemony if she consistently believed his death. As Beatrice was the Baudelaires' mother, she died in the fire that destroyed the Baudelaire mansion. She is not to be confused with another <a href="/wiki/Beatrice_Baudelaire#Kit_Snicket.27s_Daughter" title="Beatrice Baudelaire">Beatrice Baudelaire</a>, the daughter of Kit Snicket, and adopted sister of the Baudelaire orphans. Snicket frequently alludes to Beatrice in his narration and dedicates each <i>Series of Unfortunate Events</i> book to her. Snicket is also known to have been close "friends" with a woman with the first initial "R.", who was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/V.F.D._members#Duchess_of_Winnipeg" title="V.F.D. members">Duchess of Winnipeg</a>.</p>
<p>Lemony Snicket has charged himself with the task of researching and writing the sad story of the Baudelaire orphans for "many personal and legal reasons".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He traces their movements and collects evidence relating to their adventures, but it is possible that he never met <a href="/wiki/Violet_Baudelaire" title="Violet Baudelaire">Violet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Klaus_Baudelaire" title="Klaus Baudelaire">Klaus</a> or <a href="/wiki/Sunny_Baudelaire" title="Sunny Baudelaire">Sunny</a> in person. Many fans often identify him, though, as a <a href="/wiki/Taxicab" title="Taxicab">taxi</a> driver who appeared briefly in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Penultimate_Peril" title="The Penultimate Peril">The Penultimate Peril</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Reptile_Room" title="The Reptile Room">The Reptile Room</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wide_Window" title="The Wide Window">The Wide Window</a></i>, and who is implied to be in the possession of the sugar bowl, a key plot element of the series, after recovering it from the pond within which it had been hidden.</p>
<p>As the series progresses, it becomes increasingly clear that Snicket knew Baudelaire orphans' parents well through their connections to V.F.D. However, as mentioned in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hostile_Hospital" title="The Hostile Hospital">The Hostile Hospital</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_End_(novel)" title="The End (novel)">The End</a></i>, despite all of Lemony's research and hard work, he still does not know the current location, position or status of the Baudelaire children. Lemony was recruited by V.F.D. as a child, according to the <i>Little Snicket Lad</i> song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In <i>The Beatrice Letters</i>, his niece, the daughter of Kit Snicket, also named Beatrice Baudelaire, mentions that she believes he is a <a href="/wiki/Detective" title="Detective">detective</a> of some sort, a reference to his investigations into the case of the Baudelaire children.</p>
<p>Snicket is frequently disparaging of himself; he has described himself as a <a href="/wiki/Cowardice" title="Cowardice">coward</a>, and at various points in his novels comments that he would not have been as brave as the Baudelaire children had he been in their situation. He also confesses that he has done things that were not noble, most notably the original theft of the sugar bowl from <a href="/wiki/Esm%C3%A9_Squalor" title="Esmé Squalor">Esmé Squalor</a>. He implied he had a part in the murder of <a href="/wiki/Count_Olaf" title="Count Olaf">Count Olaf</a>'s parents, and that Beatrice was involved as well.</p>
<p>In the narration of the books, Snicket describes doing many unusual things in his free time, including hiding all traces of his actions, locating new hiding places, considering suspicious dishes, and researching the perilous lives of the Baudelaire children. He claims to often write himself citations for bravery in an attempt to cheer himself up, but these attempts are always in vain.</p>

<p>Handler has also written or contributed to other works under the Lemony Snicket persona that are not related to <i>A Series of Unfortunate Events</i>. He has stated "there's a chance some other matters may take up Mr. Snicket's attention, that he may research and publish, but I'm always wary of making such promises".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>As Snicket, Handler wrote an <a href="/wiki/Foreword" title="Foreword">introduction</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endnotes" title="Endnotes">endnotes</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bears%27_Famous_Invasion_of_Sicily" title="The Bears' Famous Invasion of Sicily">The Bears' Famous Invasion of Sicily</a></i>, his favorite children's book, that referenced <i>A Series of Unfortunate Events</i>. <i><a href="/wiki/Noisy_Outlaws" title="Noisy Outlaws">Noisy Outlaws, Unfriendly Blobs, and Some Other Things That Aren't as Scary, Maybe, Depending on How You Feel About Lost Lands, Stray Cellphones, Creatures from the Sky, Parents Who Disappear in Peru, a Man Named Lars Farf, and One Other Story We Couldn't Quite Finish, So Maybe You Could Help Us Out</a></i>, a 2005 <a href="/wiki/McSweeney%27s" title="McSweeney's">McSweeney's</a> short story compilation, has an introduction and unfinished short story attributed to Lemony Snicket.</p>
<p>Snicket also wrote <i><a href="/wiki/The_Composer_Is_Dead" title="The Composer Is Dead">The Composer Is Dead</a></i>, a murder mystery designed to introduce young readers to the instruments of the orchestra; it was previously produced as an orchestral work by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Symphony_Orchestra" title="San Francisco Symphony Orchestra">San Francisco Symphony Orchestra</a>, with Handler narrating as Snicket, and a recording of the performance is to be included with every copy of the expanded book.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Snicket wrote the introduction to the 1989–90 edition of Fantagraphics Books's <i>The Complete Peanuts</i> series. Handler's face is never shown in the <i>A Series of Unfortunate Events</i> books, although in <i>The End</i>, it appears in an illustration, with his eyes obscured by cucumber slices.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
