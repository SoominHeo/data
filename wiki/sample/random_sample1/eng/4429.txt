<p><b>Rémi Garde</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ʁemi ɡaʁd]</a></span>, born 3 April 1966) is a French professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> coach and former player.</p>
<p>He played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> and <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>, spending most of his career with his first club <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Lyon</a>, with whom he won the <a href="/wiki/1988%E2%80%9389_French_Division_2" title="1988–89 French Division 2">1988–89 French Division 2</a>. In 1993 he moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RC_Strasbourg" title="RC Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a>, reaching the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_France" title="Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a> final and winning the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Intertoto_Cup" title="UEFA Intertoto Cup">UEFA Intertoto Cup</a> in his second season. He joined <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> in 1996, where he won the <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_FA_Premier_League" title="1997–98 FA Premier League">1997–98 FA Premier League</a> before retiring through injury a year later. Garde was <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">capped</a> by the <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France national team</a>, featuring in their squad at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1992" title="UEFA Euro 1992">UEFA Euro 1992</a>.</p>
<p>After retiring, he worked as a coach and assistant manager at Lyon, before taking the managerial position there in 2011. He won the Coupe de France and <a href="/wiki/Troph%C3%A9e_des_Champions" title="Trophée des Champions">Trophée des Champions</a> the following year, and left in 2014 for personal reasons. He was hired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aston_Villa" title="Aston Villa">Aston Villa</a> in November 2015, but left in March 2016 after five months in charge of the team.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A9mi_Garde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Club">Club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A9mi_Garde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/L%27Arbresle" title="L'Arbresle">L'Arbresle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rh%C3%B4ne_(department)" title="Rhône (department)">Rhône</a>, Garde started his playing career at nearby <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Lyon</a> in 1982, helping the club achieve promotion to <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7things_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-7things-1">[1]</a></sup> Playing as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a> or <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Sweeper_.28libero.29" title="Defender (association football)">sweeper</a>, he became club <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a>. Garde left Lyon in 1993 to join <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RC_Strasbourg" title="RC Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a>, where he spent three seasons and reached a <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_France" title="Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a> <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Coupe_de_France#Final" title="1994–95 Coupe de France">final</a> in <a href="/wiki/1995_Coupe_de_France_Final" title="1995 Coupe de France Final">1995</a>. In the summer, the team won the <a href="/wiki/1995_UEFA_Intertoto_Cup" title="1995 UEFA Intertoto Cup">1995 UEFA Intertoto Cup</a>, defeating Austria's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Admira_Wacker" title="Admira Wacker">Admira Wacker</a> in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fra_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-fra-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He moved to England in August 1996, joining <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> after compatriot <a href="/wiki/Ars%C3%A8ne_Wenger" title="Arsène Wenger">Arsène Wenger</a> recommended him to the club; Wenger was not Arsenal's manager at this stage, as he was seeing out his contract at <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Grampus" title="Nagoya Grampus">Nagoya Grampus</a>. Garde joined on the same day as <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Vieira" title="Patrick Vieira">Patrick Vieira</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7things_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-7things-1">[1]</a></sup> though unlike Vieira, the 30-year-old Garde was intended to be more of an experienced cover player rather than a future first-team regular.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7things_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-7things-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garde became known for his tidy and reliable performances as backup for Vieira or <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_Petit" title="Emmanuel Petit">Emmanuel Petit</a>; he played a total of 45 matches over three seasons for Arsenal, and was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Double_(association_football)" title="Double (association football)">Double</a>-winning side of <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_in_English_football" title="1997–98 in English football">1997–98</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7things_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-7things-1">[1]</a></sup> making 10 league appearances that season meaning he only narrowly qualified for a <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> winners' medal. He also made one appearance during Arsenal's <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> run, which came in the quarter-final replay at <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7things_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-7things-1">[1]</a></sup> The game went to a penalty shootout and despite Garde missing his kick, Arsenal prevailed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was however not part of the squad as they won the <a href="/wiki/1998_FA_Cup_Final" title="1998 FA Cup Final">1998 FA Cup Final</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newcastle_United" title="Newcastle United">Newcastle United</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>.</p>
<p>With age and a persistent knee injury limiting his appearances, Garde retired from professional football in June 1999, just after he had narrowly missed out on a second successive double with the club, who had finished second in the league and lost the <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_FA_Cup" title="1998–99 FA Cup">FA Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_FA_Cup#Semi-finals" title="1998–99 FA Cup">semi-final</a> to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A9mi_Garde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Garde made his debut for <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> at the 1990 Kuwait Tournament at the <a href="/wiki/Al-Sadaqua_Walsalam_Stadium" title="Al-Sadaqua Walsalam Stadium">Al-Sadaqua Walsalam Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kuwait_City" title="Kuwait City">Kuwait City</a>. His first appearance came against <a href="/wiki/Kuwait_national_football_team" title="Kuwait national football team">the hosts</a> in a 1–0 win on 21 January. He won six caps for his country, and was a member of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1992_squads#France" title="UEFA Euro 1992 squads">French squad</a> at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1992" title="UEFA Euro 1992">Euro 92</a> but did not play at all in the competition, in which the French were eliminated at the group stage in Sweden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7things_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-7things-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A9mi_Garde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lyon">Lyon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A9mi_Garde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lyon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a period working as a pundit on <a href="/wiki/Television_in_France" title="Television in France">French television</a>, he rejoined his old club Lyon as a coach in 2003, and helped the club to win Ligue 1 titles in <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Ligue_1" title="2003–04 Ligue 1">2003–04</a> and <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Ligue_1" title="2004–05 Ligue 1">2004–05</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7things_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-7things-1">[1]</a></sup> After the departure of <a href="/wiki/Paul_Le_Guen" title="Paul Le Guen">Paul Le Guen</a> as manager in the summer of 2005, Garde became assistant to his replacement <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9rard_Houllier" title="Gérard Houllier">Gérard Houllier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7things_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-7things-1">[1]</a></sup> In May 2007, with his contract due to expire, Garde was linked to a return to Arsenal as <a href="/wiki/Director_of_football" title="Director of football">director of football</a>, a new role created to replace that of the recently departed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice-chairman" title="Vice-chairman">vice-chairman</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Dein" title="David Dein">David Dein</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, he worked as the director of the <a href="/wiki/Centre_Tola_Vologe" title="Centre Tola Vologe">Centre Tola Vologe</a>, Lyon's training complex. On 22 June 2011, he was appointed as Lyon's new manager to replace <a href="/wiki/Claude_Puel" title="Claude Puel">Claude Puel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>While at the helm of Les Gones, Garde won the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_France" title="Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a> title of <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Coupe_de_France" title="2011–12 Coupe de France">2011–12</a> as well as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trophee_des_Champions" title="Trophee des Champions">Trophee des Champions</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_Trophee_des_Champions" title="2012 Trophee des Champions">same year</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-R._Garde_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-R._Garde-6">[6]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He left at the end of the 2013–14 season for personal and family reasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aston_Villa">Aston Villa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R%C3%A9mi_Garde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aston Villa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 November 2015, Garde agreed a three-and-a-half year deal to become the manager of <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> who were bottom of the table.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Remi_Garde:_Aston_Villa_confirm_ex-Lyon_boss_as_manager_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Remi_Garde:_Aston_Villa_confirm_ex-Lyon_boss_as_manager-9">[9]</a></sup> Six days later, in Garde's first match in charge, his new team drew 0–0 with league leaders <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garde put a strong emphasis on discipline at Villa. He dropped midfielder <a href="/wiki/Jack_Grealish" title="Jack Grealish">Jack Grealish</a> from the first team after he went partying following a 4–0 loss at <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> and warned the teams not to party excessively over the <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 29 March 2016, with Villa still bottom of the league, Garde left the club by mutual consent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
