<p><b>Remedy Entertainment Oy</b>, internationally <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doing_business_as" title="Doing business as">doing business as</a> <b>Remedy Entertainment Ltd.</b> (commonly referred to as <b>Remedy</b>), is a <a href="/wiki/Finns" title="Finns">Finnish</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">video game developer</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Espoo" title="Espoo">Espoo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>. Founded in 1995, the company is best known for developing <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Max_Payne_(series)" title="Max Payne (series)">Max Payne</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Alan_Wake" title="Alan Wake">Alan Wake</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Quantum_Break" title="Quantum Break">Quantum Break</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remedy_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_and_Death_Rally">Early years and <i>Death Rally</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remedy_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years and Death Rally">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Old_Remedy_logo.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="110" data-file-width="330" height="73" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3a/Old_Remedy_logo.png/220px-Old_Remedy_logo.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/3/3a/Old_Remedy_logo.png 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Old_Remedy_logo.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The original Remedy Entertainment logo, which caused <a href="/wiki/LucasArts" title="LucasArts">LucasArts</a> to threaten with legal action, due to it resembling the upper portion of LucasArts' logo<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Remedy was founded on 31 August 1995 by <a href="/wiki/Demoscene" title="Demoscene">demoscene</a> members from groups such as <a href="/wiki/Future_Crew" title="Future Crew">Future Crew</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The year after Remedy released its first game <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Rally" title="Death Rally">Death Rally</a></i>, a top-down perspective racing shooter published by <a href="/wiki/3D_Realms" title="3D Realms">Apogee Software</a> and distributed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GT_Interactive" title="GT Interactive">GT Interactive</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In the game the player competes in deadly races with armed cars, trying to finish first while destroying opponents and fulfilling missions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Still attached to the demoscene, Remedy took part in the 1997 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assembly_(demo_party)" title="Assembly (demo party)">Assembly</a> with <i>Final Reality</i>, a combination of a <a href="/wiki/Demo_(computer_programming)" title="Demo (computer programming)">demo</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Benchmark_(computing)" title="Benchmark (computing)">3D benchmark program</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Later that year Remedy founded a new company named <a href="/wiki/Futuremark" title="Futuremark">Futuremark</a> for developing benchmark software that would eventually turn into <a href="/wiki/3DMark" title="3DMark">3DMark</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1998, <a href="/wiki/LucasArts" title="LucasArts">LucasArts</a> approached them, citing that their logo was copied from the top portion of the LucasArts logo, and threatened legal action.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Remedy was by that time already in the process of redesigning their logo, so they complied by taking their old logo offline from their website, and introducing their new logo a little later.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Max_Payne"><i>Max Payne</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remedy_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Max Payne">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, Remedy released its second game, <i><a href="/wiki/Max_Payne_(video_game)" title="Max Payne (video game)">Max Payne</a></i>, which tells the story of the eponymous fugitive undercover cop in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, vengeful for the murder of his family, framed for the murder of a DEA agent, and hunted by <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department" title="New York City Police Department">New York City Police Department</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Mafia" title="American Mafia">American Mafia</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The game is best known for its <a href="/wiki/Film_noir" title="Film noir">film noir</a> influences and popularizing <a href="/wiki/Bullet_time" title="Bullet time">bullet time</a> in video games.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In May 2002, Remedy sold all the rights to <i>Max Payne</i> to <a href="/wiki/Take-Two_Interactive" title="Take-Two Interactive">Take-Two Interactive</a> for <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$10 million</span> and 969,932 shares of stock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The following year, a sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Max_Payne_2:_The_Fall_of_Max_Payne" title="Max Payne 2: The Fall of Max Payne">Max Payne 2: The Fall of Max Payne</a></i>, was released by <a href="/wiki/Rockstar_Games" title="Rockstar Games">Rockstar Games</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The latest addition to the series, <i><a href="/wiki/Max_Payne_3" title="Max Payne 3">Max Payne 3</a></i>, was developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rockstar_Studios" title="Rockstar Studios">Rockstar Studios</a> and was released in May 2012.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alan_Wake_and_Death_Rally_remake"><i>Alan Wake</i> and <i>Death Rally</i> remake</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remedy_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Alan Wake and Death Rally remake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, Remedy revealed that it was developing a new <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_property" title="Intellectual property">intellectual property</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Alan_Wake" title="Alan Wake">Alan Wake</a></i>, a game about a writer of the same name whose wife disappears during the couple's vacation in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> While searching for his wife, Alan realizes the events are coming to life from a thriller he does not even remember writing.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><i>Alan Wake</i> combines a <a href="/wiki/Third-person_shooter" title="Third-person shooter">third-person shooter</a> video game with <a href="/wiki/Psychological_horror" title="Psychological horror">psychological horror</a>, drawing inspiration from <a href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks" title="Twin Peaks">Twin Peaks</a> and the works of <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The game was released by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microsoft_Game_Studios" title="Microsoft Game Studios">Microsoft Game Studios</a> in May 2010.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Alan Wake was extended with two <a href="/wiki/Downloadable_content" title="Downloadable content">downloadable content</a> packs, titled <i>The Signal</i> and <i>The Writer</i>, which act as new episodes in the episodic storyline of <i>Alan Wake</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In March 2011, Remedy published a remake of <i>Death Rally</i>, developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cornfox_%26_Brothers" title="Cornfox &amp; Brothers">Cornfox &amp; Brothers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mountain_Sheep" title="Mountain Sheep">Mountain Sheep</a>, for <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In February 2012, Remedy and Microsoft Studios released <i><a href="/wiki/Alan_Wake%27s_American_Nightmare" title="Alan Wake's American Nightmare">Alan Wake's American Nightmare</a></i>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stand-alone_expansion_pack" title="Stand-alone expansion pack">stand-alone expansion pack</a> that loosely continues the story of <i>Alan Wake</i>, set in the small town of Night Springs, <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In the game Alan tries to chase down his evil <a href="/wiki/Doppelg%C3%A4nger" title="Doppelgänger">doppelgänger</a>, Mr. Scratch, who threatens Wake to take away everything he loves, including his wife.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quantum_Break_and_Agents_of_Storm"><i>Quantum Break</i> and <i>Agents of Storm</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remedy_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Quantum Break and Agents of Storm">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Xbox_One" title="Xbox One">Xbox One</a> event, on 21 May 2013, Remedy and Microsoft Studios announced <i><a href="/wiki/Quantum_Break" title="Quantum Break">Quantum Break</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The game was released on 5 April 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> At the 2013 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VGX_(award_show)" title="VGX (award show)">VGX award show</a>, Remedy revealed <i>Agents of Storm</i>, which was released by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Flaregames&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Flaregames (page does not exist)">Flaregames</a> for iOS in October 2014.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2015, during the development of <i>Quantum Break</i>, Remedy's CEO, Matias Myllyrinne, and head of franchise development, Oskari Häkkinen, departed from the company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The game was released in April 2016.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_projects">Current projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remedy_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Current projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Remedy announced that it had formed a new team and is currently working on two different projects, neither of which will be related to Alan Wake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In the following month, Remedy announced their partnership with <a href="/wiki/Smilegate" title="Smilegate">Smilegate</a>, and that they would handle the single-player story mode of Smilegate's upcoming game <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CrossFire_2&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CrossFire 2 (page does not exist)">CrossFire 2</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
