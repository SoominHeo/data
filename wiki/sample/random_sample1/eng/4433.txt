<p><b>Lebanese cuisine</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">المطبخ اللبناني</span>‎‎) (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i><b>cuisine libanaise</b></i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_cuisine" title="Mediterranean cuisine">Mediterranean cuisine</a>. It includes an abundance of <a href="/wiki/Whole_grain" title="Whole grain">whole grains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruits</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vegetable" title="Vegetable">vegetables</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starches" title="Starches">starches</a>, fresh <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fish_(food)" title="Fish (food)">fish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seafood" title="Seafood">seafood</a>; animal fats are consumed sparingly. <a href="/wiki/Poultry" title="Poultry">Poultry</a> is eaten more often than <a href="/wiki/Red_meat" title="Red meat">red meat</a>. When red meat is eaten it is usually <a href="/wiki/Lamb_and_mutton" title="Lamb and mutton">lamb</a> on the coast, and <a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goat</a> meat in the mountain regions. It also includes copious amounts of <a href="/wiki/Garlic" title="Garlic">garlic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olive_oil" title="Olive oil">olive oil</a>, often seasoned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lemon_juice" title="Lemon juice">lemon juice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Olive oil, herbs, garlic and lemon are typical flavors found in the Lebanese diet.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arak_(distilled_beverage)" title="Arak (distilled beverage)">Arak (عرق)</a>, an anise-flavored liqueur, is the Lebanese national drink and usually served with a traditional convivial Lebanese meal. Another historic and traditional drink in Lebanon is <a href="/wiki/Lebanese_wine" title="Lebanese wine">wine (نبيذ)</a>.</p>
<p>In Lebanon some desserts are specifically prepared on special occasions: the <i><a href="/wiki/Meghli" title="Meghli">meghli</a></i>, for instance, is served to celebrate a newborn baby in the family.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lebanese_cuisine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lebanese cuisine is an ancient one and part of the <a href="/wiki/Levantine_cuisine" title="Levantine cuisine">Levantine</a> cuisine. Many dishes in the Lebanese cuisine can be traced back thousands of years to eras of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phoenicia" title="Phoenicia">Phoenician</a> rule. More recently, Lebanese cuisine was influenced by the different foreign civilizations that held power. From 1516 to 1918, the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Turks</a> controlled Lebanon and introduced a variety of foods that have become staples in the Lebanese diet, such as cooking with <a href="/wiki/Lamb_and_mutton" title="Lamb and mutton">lamb</a>.</p>
<p>After the Ottomans were defeated in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> (1914–1918), <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> took control of Lebanon until 1943, when the country achieved its independence. The <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> introduced foods such as <a href="/wiki/Flan" title="Flan">flan</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caramel_custard" title="Caramel custard">caramel custard</a> dessert dating back to the 16th century AD, and <a href="/wiki/Croissant" title="Croissant">croissants</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lebanese_cuisine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ftayerbelefet.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2304" data-file-width="3072" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Ftayerbelefet.JPG/230px-Ftayerbelefet.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Ftayerbelefet.JPG/345px-Ftayerbelefet.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Ftayerbelefet.JPG/460px-Ftayerbelefet.JPG 2x" width="230"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ftayerbelefet.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Ftayer be lefet (<a href="/wiki/Turnip" title="Turnip">turnip</a> <a href="/wiki/Turnover_(food)" title="Turnover (food)">turnovers</a>)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Most often foods are grilled, baked or sautéed in olive oil; butter or cream is rarely used other than in a few desserts. Vegetables are often eaten raw, pickled, or cooked. <a href="/wiki/Herb" title="Herb">Herbs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spice" title="Spice">spices</a> are used frequently and in large quantities. Like most Mediterranean countries, much of what the Lebanese eat is dictated by the seasons and what is available. Lebanese cuisine also varies by region.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>, very rarely are drinks served without being accompanied by food. Similar to the <a href="/wiki/Tapas" title="Tapas">tapas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <i>mezeluri</i> of <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> and aperitivo of <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mezze" title="Mezze">mezze</a></i> is an array of small dishes placed before the guests creating an array of colors, flavors, textures and aromas. This style of serving food is less a part of family life than it is of entertaining and cafés. Mezze may be as simple as raw or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pickled_vegetables" title="Pickled vegetables">pickled vegetables</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hummus" title="Hummus">hummus</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baba_ghanouj" title="Baba ghanouj">baba ghanouj</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bread" title="Bread">bread</a>, or it may become an entire meal consisting of grilled marinated <a href="/wiki/Seafood" title="Seafood">seafood</a>, skewered meats, a variety of cooked and raw salads and an arrangement of desserts.</p>
<p>Although simple fresh fruits are often served towards the end of a Lebanese meal, there is also <a href="/wiki/Dessert" title="Dessert">dessert</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Baklava" title="Baklava">baklava</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>. Although baklava is the most internationally known dessert, there is a great variety of Lebanese desserts.</p>
<p>A typical <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mezze" title="Mezze">mezze</a></i> will consist of an elaborate variety of thirty hot and cold dishes and may include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Salads such as <a href="/wiki/Tabbouleh" title="Tabbouleh">tabbouleh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fattoush" title="Fattoush">fattoush</a>, together with dip such as <a href="/wiki/Hummus" title="Hummus">hummus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baba_ghanoush" title="Baba ghanoush">baba ghanoush</a> or <i>moutabal</i>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kebbeh" title="Kebbeh">kebbeh</a>.</li>
<li>Some patties such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sambusac" title="Sambusac">Sambusac</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dolma" title="Dolma">Stuffed grape leaves</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Family cuisine offers also a range of dishes, such as stews (or yakhneh), which can be cooked in many forms depending on the ingredients used and are usually served with <a href="/wiki/Meat" title="Meat">meat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rice_vermicelli" title="Rice vermicelli">rice vermicelli</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pita" title="Pita">Lebanese flat bread</a>, called <a href="/wiki/Pita" title="Pita">pita</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Staple_food" title="Staple food">staple</a> to every Lebanese meal and can be used in place of a fork.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine_by_Village">Cuisine by Village</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lebanese_cuisine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cuisine by Village">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These recipes are attributed to these regions in Lebanon, although you find them now as main dishes at most Lebanese homes across the country with local variations.</p>
<ul>
<li>Ain-Zibdeh: Hareeseh (wheat and chicken)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Baalbek" title="Baalbek">Baalbek</a>: Safiha Baalbakieh (meat-stuffed puff pastry)</li>
<li>Baino: Kebbe and Lahme bil-khal (meat mixed with crushed wheat and meat soaked in vinegar)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Baskinta" title="Baskinta">Baskinta</a>: Makhlouta (meat, rice, and nuts)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Beirut" title="Beirut">Beirut</a>: Samkeh Harra and Akhtabout (spicy fish and octopus), Roastuts</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Beit_Chabab" title="Beit Chabab">Beit Chabab</a>: Riz bi-Djaj (chicken with rice)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Beit_Mery" title="Beit Mery">Beit Mery</a>: Kebbe Lakteen (pumpkin-flavoured meat)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beiteddine" title="Beiteddine">Beiteddine</a>: Kafta Bithine (spiced meat with sesame concentrate)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broummana" title="Broummana">Broummana</a>: Deleh Mehshi (stuffed rib cage of lamb)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bsharri" title="Bsharri">Bsharri</a>: Koussa bil-Laban (meat and rice-stuffed zucchini cooked in yoghurt)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dhour_El_Choueir" title="Dhour El Choueir">Dhour El Choueir</a>: <a href="/wiki/Shishbarak" title="Shishbarak">Shish Barak</a> (dough balls stuffed with ground beef and cooked in yoghurt)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Douma,_Lebanon" title="Douma, Lebanon">Douma</a>: Laban Immo (cooked yoghurt and lamb with rice)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ehden" title="Ehden">Ehden</a>: Kebbe Zghartweih (oven-cooked meat and crushed wheat blend)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El-Koura" title="El-Koura">El Koura</a>: Abu Shoushe (topinambur and lentils stew)</li>
<li>Ferzol: <a href="/wiki/Freekeh" title="Freekeh">Freikeh</a> (cooked wheat with meat)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hammana" title="Hammana">Hammana</a>: Fasoulya Hammanieh (kidney bean stew)</li>
<li>Ihmej: Ghameh (stuffed cow intestines)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jbeil" title="Jbeil">Jbeil</a>: Koussa and Wark Inab bil-Kastaletah (stuffed zucchini, grape vines and steak)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Al-Qalamoun" title="Al-Qalamoun">Qalamoun</a>: Fresh Carrot juice with ice cream inside</li>
<li>Kfar meshki: Kebbe bil-Kishk (meat mixed with wheat and yoghurt)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Marjayoun" title="Marjayoun">Marjayoun</a>: Vine leaves with squash and grilled chicken</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nabatieh" title="Nabatieh">Nabatieh</a>: Sfiha (aka: lahm baajin but much tastier)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ras_el-Matn" title="Ras el-Matn">Ras El Matn</a>: Fatet (yoghurt, fried bread and nuts)</li>
<li>Rashana: Mjadrat Fasoulya (lentils and kidney beans)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rashaya" title="Rashaya">Rashaya Al Wadi</a>: Kebbe Heeleh (meatballs)</li>
<li>Saghbeen: Zinkoul bil-Laban (meat filled pastry and yoghurt)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shouf" title="Shouf">Shouf</a>: Fatet Batinjan (yoghurt, fried bread and aubergine) and <a href="/wiki/Mate_(beverage)" title="Mate (beverage)">mate</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sidon" title="Sidon">Sidon</a>: Riz bil-Foul (Rice and fava beans)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tripoli,_Lebanon" title="Tripoli, Lebanon">Tripoli, Lebanon</a>: Mjadrah and Fattoush (crushed lentils and salad)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tyre,_Lebanon" title="Tyre, Lebanon">Tyre</a>: Saiyadit al-Samak (rice and fish)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Zahl%C3%A9" title="Zahlé">Zahlé</a>: Kebbe Zahleweieh (meat and crushed wheat blend)</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ackawi_cheese" title="Ackawi cheese">Ackawi</a> – white cheese salty or not depending on choice. Usually used in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manaeesh" title="Manaeesh">Manaeesh</a> (Lebanese-style pies)</li>
<li>Adas Bil Hamod - Soup made out of <a href="/wiki/Lentil" title="Lentil">lentils</a> and lemon juice.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baba_ghanouj" title="Baba ghanouj">Baba ghanouj</a> – a dip made of char-grilled <i>aubergine</i> (<a href="/wiki/Eggplant" title="Eggplant">eggplant</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tahina" title="Tahina">tahina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olive_oil" title="Olive oil">olive oil</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lemon_juice" title="Lemon juice">lemon juice</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Garlic" title="Garlic">garlic</a> puree.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Baklava" title="Baklava">Baklava</a> – a dessert of layered filo pastry filled with <a href="/wiki/Nut_(fruit)" title="Nut (fruit)">nuts</a> and steeped in attar syrup (<a href="/wiki/Orange_(fruit)" title="Orange (fruit)">orange</a> or <a href="/wiki/Rose_water" title="Rose water">rose water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a>) or honey, usually cut in a triangular or diamond shape.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Roasting" title="Roasting">Roasted</a> <a href="/wiki/Nut_(fruit)" title="Nut (fruit)">nuts</a> – a mix of more than 20 types, mostly dry roasted.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Balila_(dish)" title="Balila (dish)">Balila</a> – known as <a href="/wiki/Cumin" title="Cumin">cumin</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chickpeas" title="Chickpeas">chickpeas</a>.</li>
<li>Barout del batata – spicy lamb served with potatoes</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Batata_harra" title="Batata harra">Batata harra</a> – literally <i>"spicy potatoes"</i>.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Burghul_Banadoura&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Burghul Banadoura (page does not exist)">Burghul Banadoura</a> – <a href="/wiki/Bulgur" title="Bulgur">bulgur</a> and tomato</li>
<li>Daoud Bacha – meatballs with tomato sauce</li>
<li>Djaj Mechwi – grilled chicken with peas</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fattoush" title="Fattoush">Fattoush</a> – 'peasant' salad of tossed greens with pita bread, <a href="/wiki/Cucumber" title="Cucumber">cucumbers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tomato" title="Tomato">tomatoes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chickweed" title="Chickweed">chickweed</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mentha" title="Mentha">mint</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Falafel" title="Falafel">Falafel</a> – small deep-fried patties made of highly spiced ground <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chickpeas" title="Chickpeas">chickpeas</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fried_cauliflower" title="Fried cauliflower">Fried cauliflower</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fried_eggplant" title="Fried eggplant">Fried eggplant</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fatayer" title="Fatayer">Fatayer</a> – a <a href="/wiki/Turnover_(food)" title="Turnover (food)">turnover</a> pastry, often made with sbanegh (<a href="/wiki/Spinach" title="Spinach">spinach</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ful_medames" title="Ful medames">Fuul</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Vicia_faba" title="Vicia faba">vicia faba</a></i>) slow cooked mash of brown <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beans" title="Beans">beans</a> and red <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lentils" title="Lentils">lentils</a> dressed with <a href="/wiki/Lemon" title="Lemon">lemon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olive_oil" title="Olive oil">olive oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cumin" title="Cumin">cumin</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Halva" title="Halva">Halva</a> – a sweet sesame paste, usually formed into a slab and studded with <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nut_(fruit)" title="Nut (fruit)">nuts</a>. Slices of the slab are served.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hummus" title="Hummus">Hummus</a> – dip or spread made of blended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chickpeas" title="Chickpeas">chickpeas</a>, sesame <a href="/wiki/Tahini" title="Tahini">tahini</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lemon_juice" title="Lemon juice">lemon juice</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Garlic" title="Garlic">garlic</a>, and typically eaten with pita bread.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kanafeh" title="Kanafeh">Kunafi</a> – either shoelace pastry dessert stuffed with sweet <a href="/wiki/Nabulsi_cheese" title="Nabulsi cheese">white cheese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nut_(fruit)" title="Nut (fruit)">nuts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syrup" title="Syrup">syrup</a>, or more commonly the version with semolina pastry served on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sesame_seed" title="Sesame seed">sesame seed</a> bun with sweet sugar syrup (very popular for breakfast) made with <a href="/wiki/Rice_vermicelli" title="Rice vermicelli">rice vermicelli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Butter" title="Butter">butter</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pistachio" title="Pistachio">pistachios</a> or other nuts. Often found in sweet shops and bakeries.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kibbeh" title="Kibbeh">Kibbeh</a> – Finely minced meat mixed with <a href="/wiki/Bulgur" title="Bulgur">bulgur</a> (cracked wheat) that can be made in different forms including stuffed with ground meat/onions: layered, pan baked, fried balls/patties, and/or cooked in <a href="/wiki/Yogurt" title="Yogurt">yogurt</a>, or eaten raw.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kibbeh_nayyeh" title="Kibbeh nayyeh">Kibbeh nayyeh</a> – raw kibbeh: finely minced meat mixed with bulgur (cracked wheat) and eaten like <a href="/wiki/Steak_tartare" title="Steak tartare">steak tartare</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kafta" title="Kafta">Kafta</a> – Paties, fingers, stars or a flat cake of <a href="/wiki/Ground_meat" title="Ground meat">minced meat</a>, onions, parsly, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spices" title="Spices">spices</a> that can be baked or charcoal-grilled on skewers.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kousa_Mahshi" title="Kousa Mahshi">Kousa Mahshi</a> – <a href="/wiki/Zucchini" title="Zucchini">zucchini</a> (many varieties are used) stuffed with meat and rice.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kubideh" title="Kubideh">Kubideh</a> – ground lamb or chicken threaded on a stick and grilled, served with <i>pivaz</i> (a mix of minced <a href="/wiki/Parsley" title="Parsley">parsley</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Onions" title="Onions">onions</a>, ground <a href="/wiki/Cumin" title="Cumin">cumin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sumac" title="Sumac">sumac</a>).</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Labneh" title="Labneh">Labneh</a> – strained yogurt cheese, spreadable and garnished with good olive oil and <a href="/wiki/Sea_salt" title="Sea salt">sea salt</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sfiha" title="Sfiha">Lahm bil ajĩn</a> – a pastry covered with <a href="/wiki/Ground_meat" title="Ground meat">minced meat</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Onions" title="Onions">onions</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nut_(fruit)" title="Nut (fruit)">nuts</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ma%27amoul" title="Ma'amoul">Ma'amoul</a> – stuffed cookies made from semolina with ground <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Date_(fruit)" title="Date (fruit)">date</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pistachio" title="Pistachio">pistachio</a> or <a href="/wiki/Walnut" title="Walnut">walnut</a> filling. Shaped in a wooden mould called a <i>tabi, and</i> made specially for <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> holidays (such as <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ramadan" title="Ramadan">Ramadan</a>).</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mfaraket_Koussa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mfaraket Koussa (page does not exist)">Mfaraket Koussa</a> – spicy zucchini.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Makdous" title="Makdous">Makdous</a> – stuffed pickled <a href="/wiki/Eggplant" title="Eggplant">eggplant</a> (usually with nuts) preserved in olive oil.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manaeesh" title="Manaeesh">Manaeesh</a> – Mini pizzas (usually folded) that are made in bakeries, traditionally garnished with <a href="/wiki/Cheese" title="Cheese">cheese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Za%27atar" title="Za'atar">Za'atar</a>, spicy diced tomatoes, Lebanese <a href="/wiki/Kashk" title="Kashk">kashk</a>, or minced meat and onions. Some bakeries allow you to bring your own toppings and build your own or buy the ones they sell there. Served for breakfast, lunch and dinner.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mujaddara" title="Mujaddara">Mujaddara</a> (<i>imjaddarra</i>) – cooked <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lentils" title="Lentils">lentils</a> combined with <a href="/wiki/Wheat" title="Wheat">wheat</a> or <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>, garnished with onions that have been sautéed in vegetable oil.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mulukhiyah" title="Mulukhiyah">Mulukhiyah</a> – A stew with made with leaves of the Nalta/<a href="/wiki/Corchorus_olitorius" title="Corchorus olitorius">Tossa Jute</a> plant, <a href="/wiki/Chicken" title="Chicken">chicken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beef" title="Beef">beef</a>, and garnished with raw chopped onions soaked in <a href="/wiki/Vinegar" title="Vinegar">vinegar</a>, served over rice. Sometimes, toasted pita chips are placed under the rice.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mutabbel" title="Mutabbel">Mutabbel</a> – a mix of slow cooked eggplant and <a href="/wiki/Tahini" title="Tahini">tahini</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pastirma" title="Pastirma">Pastirma</a> – Tender cooked <a href="/wiki/Meat" title="Meat">meat</a>, usually served with vegetables.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Qatayef" title="Qatayef">Qatayef</a> – a sort of sweet <a href="/wiki/Dumpling" title="Dumpling">dumpling</a> filled with cream or nuts.</li>
<li>Qawarma – chopped lamb, salted and kept in the grease of the animal</li>
<li>Samkeh Harra – grilled <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> that has been marinated with <a href="/wiki/Chili_pepper" title="Chili pepper">chili</a>, <a href="/wiki/Citrus" title="Citrus">citrus</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cilantro" title="Cilantro">cilantro</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Shanklish" title="Shanklish">Shanklish</a> – aged cheese balls</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Shawarma" title="Shawarma">Shawarma</a> – a sandwich with marinated meat (either <a href="/wiki/Lamb_and_mutton" title="Lamb and mutton">lamb</a> or <a href="/wiki/Chicken" title="Chicken">chicken</a>) that is skewered on big rods and cooked slowly, then shaved and placed in a 10-inch pita with <a href="/wiki/Pickling" title="Pickling">pickles</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tomatoes" title="Tomatoes">tomatoes</a>, and other tangy condiments.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Shish_taouk" title="Shish taouk">Shish taouk</a> – grilled chicken skewers that utilize only white meat, marinated in olive oil, lemon, parsley, and sumac.</li>
<li>Siyyadiyeh – Fish cooked in <a href="/wiki/Saffron" title="Saffron">saffron</a> and served on rice with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Onions" title="Onions">onions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sumac" title="Sumac">sumac</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Tahini" title="Tahini">tahini</a> sauce (the most important part of the dish) and served on rice, which originated in the southern areas of Lebanon.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tabbouleh" title="Tabbouleh">Tabbouleh</a> – minced/chopped parsley salad with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burghul" title="Burghul">burghul</a> (cracked wheat), <a href="/wiki/Tomato" title="Tomato">tomatoes</a>, onions, and <a href="/wiki/Mentha" title="Mentha">mint</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tahini" title="Tahini">Tahini</a> – sesame paste</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Toum" title="Toum">Toum</a> – garlic sauce</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wara%27_Enab&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wara' Enab (page does not exist)">Wara' Enab</a> – <a href="/wiki/Grape_leaves" title="Grape leaves">grape leaves</a> stuffed normally with meat and rice or made lenten style with just rice with lemon sauce.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Za%27atar" title="Za'atar">Za'atar</a> – dried ground <a href="/wiki/Thyme" title="Thyme">thyme</a>, sesame seeds and sumac that can differ from region to region and from family to family. Most are made in house, but can be bought at Lebanese larders.</li>
<li>Znood Es-sett – filo pastry cigars with various fillings.</li>
<li>Lebanese "Seven Spice" Blend – a mixture of equal parts of <a href="/wiki/Allspice" title="Allspice">allspice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_pepper" title="Black pepper">black pepper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cinnamon" title="Cinnamon">cinnamon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cloves" title="Cloves">cloves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fenugreek" title="Fenugreek">fenugreek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nutmeg" title="Nutmeg">nutmeg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ginger" title="Ginger">ginger</a>. It is commonly used to flavor many Lebanese dishes.</li>
</ul>
