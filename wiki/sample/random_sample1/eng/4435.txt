<p>The <b>Lebanese National Anthem</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar">النشيد الوطني اللبناني</span>‎‎ <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="DIN 31635 Arabic"><i>an-našīd al-waṭaniyy al-lubnāniyy</i></span>) was written by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rashid_Nakhle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rashid Nakhle (page does not exist)">Rashid Nakhle</a> and composed by <a href="/wiki/Wadih_Sabra" title="Wadih Sabra">Wadih Sabra</a>. It was adopted on 12 July 1927, seven years after the proclamation of the state of Greater Lebanon during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_Mandate_of_Lebanon" title="French Mandate of Lebanon">French mandate</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lebanese_National_Anthem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lebanese national anthem was chosen following an open nationwide competition.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lebanese_National_Anthem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The music of the national anthem is influenced by Beirut's exposure to western culture by the end of the 19th century, it was composed by French trained artist <a href="/wiki/Wadih_Sabra" title="Wadih Sabra">Wadih Sabra</a> in 1925.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FZ_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FZ-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lebanese_National_Anthem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Jadeed" title="Al-Jadeed">Al-Jadeed</a> TV channel showed a mini documentary on the Lebanese National Anthem, claiming the music to have been plagiarized from a song dedicated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muhammad_Ibn_%27Abd_al-Karim_al-Khattabi" title="Muhammad Ibn 'Abd al-Karim al-Khattabi">Muhammad Ibn 'Abd al-Karim al-Khattabi</a>, the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Rif_Republic" title="Rif Republic">Rif Republic</a>, which was composed by Lebanese <a class="extiw" href="https://ar.wikipedia.org/wiki/%D9%85%D8%AD%D9%85%D8%AF_%D9%81%D9%84%D9%8A%D9%81%D9%84" title="ar:محمد فليفل">Mohammed Flayfel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It later showed another documentary containing documents which proved the claim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adoption">Adoption</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lebanese_National_Anthem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Adoption">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <b>Lebanese National Anthem</b> is adopted by Rashid Nakhle and Wadih Sabra.</p>
