<p>The <b>2012 FA Community Shield</b> was the 90th <a href="/wiki/FA_Community_Shield" title="FA Community Shield">FA Community Shield</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> match played on 12 August 2012 between the winners of the previous season's <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Premier_League" title="2011–12 Premier League">Premier League</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_FA_Cup" title="2011–12 FA Cup">FA Cup</a> competitions. The match was contested by the <a href="/wiki/2012_FA_Cup_Final" title="2012 FA Cup Final">2012 FA Cup</a> winners, <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>, and the champions of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Premier_League" title="2011–12 Premier League">2011–12 Premier League</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>.</p>
<p>The 2012 Community Shield was not played at its usual venue, <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>, as the stadium hosted the final of the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Olympic football tournament</a> on the same weekend as the proposed date for the Community Shield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It was played instead at <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa's</a> home ground, <a href="/wiki/Villa_Park" title="Villa Park">Villa Park</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>. It was the first time since the fixture was played at <a href="/wiki/Maine_Road" title="Maine Road">Maine Road</a> in <a href="/wiki/1973_FA_Charity_Shield" title="1973 FA Charity Shield">1973</a> that the Community Shield had been hosted at a venue other than Wembley (old and new) or the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Stadium" title="Millennium Stadium">Millennium Stadium</a>. Manchester City won the game 3–2 to claim their first Community Shield since <a href="/wiki/1972_FA_Charity_Shield" title="1972 FA Charity Shield">1972</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-match">Pre-match</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_FA_Community_Shield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-match">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2012 Community Shield was broadcast live in the United Kingdom on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITV1" title="ITV1">ITV1</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-itv_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-itv-7">[7]</a></sup> Traditionally, the Community Shield has started at 15:00 <a href="/wiki/British_Summer_Time" title="British Summer Time">BST</a>, but the match was due to be played on the final day of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Olympics</a>, and therefore started at 13:30 BST.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entry">Entry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_FA_Community_Shield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Entry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Premier_League" title="2011–12 Premier League">2011–12 Premier League</a> and <a href="/wiki/2012_FA_Cup_Final" title="2012 FA Cup Final">2012 FA Cup Final</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> qualified by winning the 2012 FA Cup Final, beating <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> 2–1 in the final at Wembley. Chelsea beat <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> 4–0, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers" title="Queens Park Rangers">Queens Park Rangers</a> 1–0, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birmingham_City" title="Birmingham City">Birmingham City</a> 2–0, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leicester_City" title="Leicester City">Leicester City</a> 5–2 and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur" title="Tottenham Hotspur">Tottenham Hotspur</a> 5–1 en route to the final. The 2012 victory was Chelsea's seventh FA Cup triumph and consequently gained a berth in the 2012 Community Shield. The 2012 Community Shield will be Chelsea's tenth appearance in the fixture.</p>
<p>Manchester City qualified for their second consecutive Community Shield by clinching the Premier League title in the final minutes of the season. City made a strong start to the league season, beating rivals Manchester United 6–1 at Old Trafford in October and maintained the lead in the league for the majority of the season. However, a drop in form in March and April meant they fell eight points behind leaders Manchester United who would later drop points to Wigan Athletic and Everton. City won the last six league matches of the season, including a crucial 1–0 victory over United at the Etihad Stadium on 30 April which put City top of the league with a superior <a href="/wiki/Goal_difference" title="Goal difference">goal difference</a>. City needed to now win the final two matches to win the league, irrespective of what United did. They beat Newcastle at St James Park, meaning victory against Queens Park Rangers would seal the title for City. Although taking an early lead, QPR scored twice in the 48th and 66th minute. It looked as if City had blown their chance to win the league with United winning 1–0 at Sunderland. However, two stoppage time goals – one from <a href="/wiki/Edin_D%C5%BEeko" title="Edin Džeko">Edin Džeko</a> and a last-minute goal from <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Ag%C3%BCero" title="Sergio Agüero">Sergio Agüero</a> – completed a remarkable comeback to give City a 3–2 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The league being decided on goal difference was the closest finish since Arsenal and Liverpool in 1989 and ensured Manchester City won their first top-flight league title since 1968. The 2012 Community Shield was Manchester City's ninth appearance in the fixture.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Venue">Venue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_FA_Community_Shield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Venue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:2012_Community_Shield.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3000" data-file-width="4000" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/2012_Community_Shield.JPG/220px-2012_Community_Shield.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/2012_Community_Shield.JPG/330px-2012_Community_Shield.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/2012_Community_Shield.JPG/440px-2012_Community_Shield.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<a href="/wiki/Villa_Park" title="Villa Park">Villa Park</a> was selected as the venue for the Community Shield as <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a> was unavailable during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In order to avoid clashing with the Olympics, <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">The Football Association</a> (FA) agreed that the Community Shield would not be played in London, igniting speculation that the Community Shield would follow suit with the <a href="/wiki/Supercoppa_Italiana" title="Supercoppa Italiana">Supercoppa Italiana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Troph%C3%A9e_des_Champions" title="Trophée des Champions">Trophée des Champions</a> and be played overseas. Despite the financial incentive, the FA announced that Community Shield would be held in England.</p>
<p>In previous years when Wembley has been unavailable, the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Stadium" title="Millennium Stadium">Millennium Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a> was used to host major events; however, its use was not seen as viable due to its hosting of other Olympic football matches in the days leading up to the Shield. Venues that were also considered included the <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a> stadium in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, which hosts its final match at the 2012 Olympics on 7 August, <a href="/wiki/St_James%27_Park" title="St James' Park">St James' Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Stadium_of_Light" title="Stadium of Light">Stadium of Light</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sunderland,_Tyne_and_Wear" title="Sunderland, Tyne and Wear">Sunderland</a>, the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Manchester_Stadium" title="City of Manchester Stadium">Etihad Stadium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a> in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> and <a href="/wiki/Villa_Park" title="Villa Park">Villa Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 August 2011, however, it was announced that Villa Park was the interim choice to host the Shield, as the other, larger venues would "still be in Olympic mode".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This decision was confirmed on 18 May 2012 after the football season had concluded and it became clear Villa Park would be an ideal neutral venue halfway between the two competing clubs from <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Officials">Officials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_FA_Community_Shield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Officials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The match officials for the game were confirmed on 3 July 2012. <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Friend" title="Kevin Friend">Kevin Friend</a> of <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire_and_Rutland_County_Football_Association" title="Leicestershire and Rutland County Football Association">Leicestershire</a> was named as <a href="/wiki/Referee_(association_football)" title="Referee (association football)">referee</a>, having previously been the <a href="/wiki/Assistant_referee_(association_football)#Fourth_official" title="Assistant referee (association football)">fourth official</a> at both the <a href="/wiki/2011_Football_League_Cup_Final" title="2011 Football League Cup Final">2011 League Cup Final</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2011_FA_Community_Shield" title="2011 FA Community Shield">2011 Community Shield</a>, and refereed the 2009 FA Vase Final. Friend was assisted by Michael McDonough of <a href="/wiki/Northumberland_Football_Association" title="Northumberland Football Association">Northumberland</a> and Richard West from <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_County_Football_Association" title="East Riding County Football Association">East Riding of Yorkshire</a>, while the fourth official was <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Taylor_(referee)" title="Anthony Taylor (referee)">Anthony Taylor</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cheshire_County_Football_Association" title="Cheshire County Football Association">Cheshire</a>, and Charles Breakspear (<a href="/wiki/Surrey_County_Football_Association" title="Surrey County Football Association">Surrey</a>) was the reserve assistant referee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-referee_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-referee-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Match">Match</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_FA_Community_Shield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Match">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
