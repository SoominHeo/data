<p><b>Leandra Medeiros Cerezo</b>, known professionally as <b>Lea T</b> (born 17 April 1981),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a>-born, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a>-raised <a href="/wiki/Transgender" title="Transgender">transgender</a> fashion model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She is the <a href="/wiki/Muse" title="Muse">muse</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (January 2017)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Tisci" title="Riccardo Tisci">Riccardo Tisci</a>, creative director of French luxury brand <a href="/wiki/Givenchy" title="Givenchy">Givenchy</a>; her professional last name of "T" stands for Tisci.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is the face of American hair-care brand <a href="/wiki/Redken" title="Redken">Redken</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vingan_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vingan-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Friedman_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Friedman-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is also a pop culture icon of <a href="/wiki/Transgender" title="Transgender">transgender</a> advocacy in the <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She insists <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> people should not be discriminated against.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lea_T&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lea T is the daughter of well-known former Brazilian football player <a href="/wiki/Toninho_Cerezo" title="Toninho Cerezo">Toninho Cerezo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She grew up in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Lea T was assigned male at birth. In a January 2011 interview, the model stated her intentions to undergo <a href="/wiki/Sex_reassignment_surgery" title="Sex reassignment surgery">sex reassignment surgery</a> in March of that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The surgery actually occurred in March 2012, in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canalf_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-canalf-13">[13]</a></sup> In an interview to <a href="/wiki/Fant%C3%A1stico" title="Fantástico">Fantástico</a>, in January 2013, she described the surgery in a negative way<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fantastico_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-fantastico-14">[14]</a></sup> but two weeks later she said that she "[felt] satisfied with the results".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caras_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-caras-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lea T has stated that she has an attraction to both sexes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lea_T&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She was first discovered by <a href="/wiki/Givenchy" title="Givenchy">Givenchy</a> senior designer <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Tisci" title="Riccardo Tisci">Riccardo Tisci</a> and became the face of Givenchy in late 2010. Her first runway show was for <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Herchcovitch" title="Alexandre Herchcovitch">Alexandre Herchcovitch</a> during <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Fashion_Week" title="São Paulo Fashion Week">São Paulo Fashion Week</a> in January 2011.</p>
<p>She has been featured in editorials in <i><a href="/wiki/Vogue_Paris" title="Vogue Paris">Vogue Paris</a></i>, <i>Hercules Magazine</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interview_Magazine" title="Interview Magazine">Interview Magazine</a></i>, <i>Cover Magazine</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/LOVE_(magazine)" title="LOVE (magazine)">Love Magazine</a></i>. In 2011, she was also the cover star of two editions of the Spring/Summer 2011 edition of <i>Love Magazine</i>, one as the solo model and another featuring her kissing <a href="/wiki/Kate_Moss" title="Kate Moss">Kate Moss</a>.</p>
<p>In 2013, she participated in the Italian version of <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars" title="Dancing with the Stars">Dancing with the Stars</a></i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rai_Uno" title="Rai Uno">Rai Uno</a> entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Ballando_con_le_Stelle_(series_9)" title="Ballando con le Stelle (series 9)">Ballando con le Stelle</a></i>.</p>
<p>In February 2015, Lea T was chosen by <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> magazine one of the 12 women who changed the Italian fashion, alongside names like <a href="/wiki/Miuccia_Prada" title="Miuccia Prada">Miuccia Prada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Dello_Russo" title="Anna Dello Russo">Anna Dello Russo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Franca_Sozzani" title="Franca Sozzani">Franca Sozzani</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2014 she became the face of American hair-care brand <a href="/wiki/Redken" title="Redken">Redken</a>, thus making her the first openly transgender model to front a global cosmetics brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vingan_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Vingan-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Friedman_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Friedman-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lea T became the first openly transgender person ever to participate in the opening ceremonies of an Olympics when she led the Brazilian team into the stadium on her bike during the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Rio Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
