<p><b>Leandro Greco</b> (born 19 July 1986) is an <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Italy" title="Football in Italy">Italian footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona_F.C." title="Hellas Verona F.C.">Verona</a> on loan from <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leandro_Greco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A midfielder, Greco was a regular in the Roma youth squad. He made his Serie A debut for AS Roma in 2004–05. In 2005–06, Greco managed to make only a handful of appearances, all as a substitute player.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Verona.2C_Pisa_.26_Piacenza">Verona, Pisa &amp; Piacenza</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leandro_Greco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Verona, Pisa &amp; Piacenza">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He was farmed to <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona_F.C." title="Hellas Verona F.C.">Hellas Verona</a> for the following two seasons, playing a total of 40 games (26 in his first season, 14 in his second). Verona acquired half of the registration rights in June 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> for €300,000, but bought back by Roma in June 2008 for just €150,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He played the second half of 2008–09 on loan to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pisa_Calcio" title="Pisa Calcio">Pisa</a> of <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pisa_july21_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-pisa_july21-3">[3]</a></sup> His loan stint was cut short on January 2010, and Greco was successively sent to another Serie B club, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piacenza_Calcio" title="Piacenza Calcio">Piacenza</a>, for the remainder of the season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Roma">Return to Roma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leandro_Greco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Return to Roma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Greco returned to Roma for the 2010–11 season and was immediately put on transfer list, but no club showed interest in signing him, so he was placed on the first team instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corrieresport_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-corrieresport-4">[4]</a></sup> An injury crisis head coach <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Ranieri" title="Claudio Ranieri">Claudio Ranieri</a> to bring him on the bench for a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> game against <a href="/wiki/FC_Basel" title="FC Basel">FC Basel</a>, during which Greco entered as a substitute for the final 15 minutes and scored a goal only one minute after entering; the game ended in a 3–2 win for Roma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Greco's good performances led Ranieri to call him up for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rome_derby" title="Rome derby">Rome derby</a>, during which he came on for injured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%A9my_Menez" title="Jérémy Menez">Jérémy Menez</a> during the first half and provided a solid performance in a 2–0 win for his side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-corrieresport_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-corrieresport-4">[4]</a></sup> He was promoted to the starting lineup for subsequent games against <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>.</p>
<p>Greco's contract was extended along with <a href="/wiki/Daniele_De_Rossi" title="Daniele De Rossi">Daniele De Rossi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simone_Perrotta" title="Simone Perrotta">Simone Perrotta</a> in February 2012. Greco signed a new 3-year contract with annual gross salary of €1.254 million plus bonuses, moreover, 2011–12 season gross salary also increased to €0.8 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the start of <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Serie_A" title="2012–13 Serie A">2012–13 Serie A</a>, Greco was included in the pre-season camp,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> however he trained separately due to minor injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympiacos">Olympiacos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leandro_Greco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Olympiacos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 24 July 2012 Greek Champions <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C." title="Olympiacos F.C.">Olympiacos F.C.</a> signed the 26 years old Leandro Greco for 3 years<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> on free transfer. Leandro scored his first goal for the Peireus outfilt in the Champions League game against <a href="/wiki/Montpellier_HSC" title="Montpellier HSC">Montpellier HSC</a> (3–1 home win).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Livorno">Livorno</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leandro_Greco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Livorno">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 July 2013 Greco returned to Italy for <a href="/wiki/A.S._Livorno_Calcio" title="A.S. Livorno Calcio">A.S. Livorno Calcio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 20 June 2014 his contract with Livorno was terminated in mutual consent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genoa">Genoa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leandro_Greco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Genoa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 20 June 2014, Leandro Greco has signed a 3 years contract with the serie A team <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa C.F.C.</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
