<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Leo August Pochhammer</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Leo August Pochhammer</b> (25 August 1841, <a href="/wiki/Stendal" title="Stendal">Stendal</a> – 24 March 1920, <a href="/wiki/Kiel" title="Kiel">Kiel</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussian</a> mathematician who was educated in Berlin, obtaining his Ph.D. in 1863 under <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Kummer" title="Ernst Kummer">Ernst Kummer</a>. He became a lecturer in 1874, then professor of mathematics at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kiel" title="University of Kiel">Christian-Albrechts-Universität of Kiel</a> (1877-1919), where he was appointed Rektor (commissioner of education) in 1893. He is known for his work on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_function" title="Special function">special functions</a> and introduced the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pochhammer_symbol" title="Pochhammer symbol">Pochhammer symbol</a>, now generally used for expressing <a href="/wiki/Hypergeometric_function" title="Hypergeometric function">hypergeometric functions</a> in a compact notation.</p>
