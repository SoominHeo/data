<p><b>Léon Mart</b> (18 September 1914 – 14 July 1984) was a <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourgian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">footballer</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%A9on_Mart&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mart played his entire career in Luxembourg, for <a href="/wiki/CS_Fola_Esch" title="CS Fola Esch">CS Fola Esch</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%A9on_Mart&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He played as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centre_forward" title="Centre forward">centre forward</a> for <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_national_football_team" title="Luxembourg national football team">Luxembourg's national team</a>, and scored a national record 16 goals in 24 international appearances (including non official games) between 1933 and 1946.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He played in one <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="FIFA World Cup qualification">FIFA World Cup qualification</a> match and in one Olympic Games match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
