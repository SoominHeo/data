<p><b>Leon Peter Barnett</b> (born 30 November 1985) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a> club <a href="/wiki/Bury_F.C." title="Bury F.C.">Bury</a>. He primarily plays a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre-back</a>, but has also played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">left-back</a>, right-back, <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">central midfielder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Barnett&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Barnett&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Barnett's first season, at the age of 13, he played as a pacey right sided midfielder for local team Bushmead Wanderers. This position he retained over the next couple of seasons and was a prolific goalscorer having scored over 40 goals using his incredible pace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Luton_Town">Luton Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Barnett&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Luton Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Barnett started his football career at <a href="/wiki/Luton_Town_F.C." title="Luton Town F.C.">Luton Town</a> and progressed through their <a href="/wiki/Youth_system" title="Youth system">youth system</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Luton_Town_F.C._season" title="2002–03 Luton Town F.C. season">2002–03 season</a>, he made his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First-team" title="First-team">first-team</a> debut for the club, coming on as a 86th-minute <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> for <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Johnson_(footballer,_born_1968)" title="Marvin Johnson (footballer, born 1968)">Marvin Johnson</a> in a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Kingfield_Stadium" title="Kingfield Stadium">away</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Woking_F.C." title="Woking F.C.">Woking</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a> on 22 October 2002, but failed to feature again during the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2002.2F2003_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2002.2F2003-3">[3]</a></sup> Barnett featured twice in the Football League Trophy and once in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Luton_Town_F.C._season" title="2003–04 Luton Town F.C. season">2003–04 season</a>, but was still yet to make a league appearance for the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2003.2F2004_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2003.2F2004-4">[4]</a></sup> He signed a professional contract on 27 September 2004,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> one day before making a starting appearance in a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Liberty_Stadium" title="Liberty Stadium">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea City</a> in the Football League Trophy, during which he was shown a <a href="/wiki/Fouls_and_misconduct_(association_football)#Red_card_.28dismissal.29" title="Fouls and misconduct (association football)">red card</a> after only 12 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 November 2004, Barnett joined <a href="/wiki/Southern_Football_League" title="Southern Football League">Southern League Premier Division</a> club <a href="/wiki/Aylesbury_United_F.C." title="Aylesbury United F.C.">Aylesbury United</a> on a one-month loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He made his Aylesbury debut in a 3–1 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Histon_F.C." title="Histon F.C.">Histon</a> two days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Barnett's final appearance was in a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Penydarren_Park" title="Penydarren Park">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Merthyr_Tydfil_F.C." title="Merthyr Tydfil F.C.">Merthyr Tydfil</a> on 18 December 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aylesbury_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Aylesbury-9">[9]</a></sup> He made a total of six appearances for the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aylesbury_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Aylesbury-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the expiration of his loan at Aylesbury, Barnett returned to Luton, but failed to make any further appearances during the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2004.2F2005_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2004.2F2005-10">[10]</a></sup> He was however named in the matchday squad for the 2–1 <a href="/wiki/Racecourse_Ground" title="Racecourse Ground">away</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Wrexham_A.F.C." title="Wrexham A.F.C.">Wrexham</a> on 23 April 2005, which was enough to guarantee Luton the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a> title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wrexham_1.E2.80.932_Luton_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wrexham_1.E2.80.932_Luton-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barnett began the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Luton_Town_F.C._season" title="2005–06 Luton Town F.C. season">2005–06 season</a> by making his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">Football League</a> debut as a 90th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Russell_Perrett" title="Russell Perrett">Russell Perrett</a> in a 1–0 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KC_Stadium" title="KC Stadium">away</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> on 17 September 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Three days later, he made a starting appearance in a 1–0 <a href="/wiki/Madejski_Stadium" title="Madejski Stadium">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> second round, but was substituted in the 55th minute and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Keith_Keane" title="Keith Keane">Keith Keane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Barnett was not included in Luton's matchday squads following the match until 29 October 2005, when he came on as a 62nd-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Chris_Coyne" title="Chris Coyne">Chris Coyne</a> in a 1–0 <a href="/wiki/Ricoh_Arena" title="Ricoh Arena">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He made starting appearances in a 4–0 <a href="/wiki/Bramall_Lane" title="Bramall Lane">away</a> defeat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> leaders <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a> on 1 November 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> followed by a 3–2 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> on 5 November 2005 to make three consecutive appearances for the club, all of which resulted in defeats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Barnett remained an unused substitute in a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Carrow_Road" title="Carrow Road">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a> two weeks later,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> but returned to the <a href="/wiki/Starting_lineup#Association_football" title="Starting lineup">starting lineup</a> three days later in a 4–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra_F.C." title="Crewe Alexandra F.C.">Crewe Alexandra</a>, ending a run of four straight league defeats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He featured as a substitute in his next two matches, a 3–0 away defeat to Championship leaders Reading on 3 December 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> prior to a 1–0 <a href="/wiki/Portman_Road" title="Portman Road">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a> four weeks later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Barnett was restored to the starting lineup two days later in a 2–1 home defeat to arch-rivals <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He featured as a 74th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Markus_Heikkinen" title="Markus Heikkinen">Markus Heikkinen</a> as Luton were defeated 5–3 at home to <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> in the FA Cup third round, a match they led 3–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Barnett continued to feature on the substitutes bench throughout the month of January, making two substitute appearances for <a href="/wiki/Enoch_Showunmi" title="Enoch Showunmi">Enoch Showunmi</a> in 2–0 wins over <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> This initially continued into the month of February, as he remained an unused substitute in a 3–2 home defeat to Hull City,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> before coming on as a 41st-minute substitute for Chris Coyne in a 5–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Preston_North_End_F.C." title="Preston North End F.C.">Preston North End</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> For the remainder of February, Barnett was a regular first-team player, making starting appearances in a 3–3 draw with <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> a 3–2 home win over Championship leaders Reading, their first defeat since the opening day of the season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> prior to a 2–1 <a href="/wiki/Elland_Road" title="Elland Road">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> However, Barnett soon returned to the substitutes bench during March, mostly an unused substitute, but made one starting appearance in a 2–1 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> on 11 March 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> During the month of April, Barnett featured in the final five matches, to finish the season with a total of 22 appearances in all competitions,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2005.2F2006_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2005.2F2006-31">[31]</a></sup> with Luton finishing in 10th-place in the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Football_League_Championship" title="2005–06 Football League Championship">2005–06 Championship</a> table.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barnett made a bright start to the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Luton_Town_F.C._season" title="2006–07 Luton Town F.C. season">2006–07 season</a>, scoring his first professional goal in a 2–0 home win over Leicester City on the opening day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Two weeks later, he scored a <a href="/wiki/Header_(association_football)" title="Header (association football)">header</a>, his second professional goal in a 2–2 draw with <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> on 19 August 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Later in the season, he scored another header in a 3–2 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> on 3 March 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Barnett featured in all but seven league matches, making 43 appearances in all competitions and scoring three times,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2006.2F2007_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2006.2F2007-36">[36]</a></sup> but could not prevent Luton from being relegated to League One after a 1–0 <a href="/wiki/Pride_Park_Stadium" title="Pride Park Stadium">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> on 20 April 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> He won the <a href="/wiki/Luton_Town_F.C._Player_of_the_season#Supporters.E2.80.99_Club_Player_of_the_Season" title="Luton Town F.C. Player of the season">Luton Town Player of the Season</a> award, voted for by the club's supporters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Barnett_and_Bell_are_Luton_award_winners_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Barnett_and_Bell_are_Luton_award_winners-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="West_Bromwich_Albion">West Bromwich Albion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Barnett&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: West Bromwich Albion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Barnett completed a move to Championship club <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> on 26 July 2007 for an initial fee of £2.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Barnett_completes_West_Brom_move_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Barnett_completes_West_Brom_move-39">[39]</a></sup> The fee would rise by a further £250,000 depending on appearances and the club would have to pay Luton a further £250,000 should they gain promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Barnett_completes_West_Brom_move_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-Barnett_completes_West_Brom_move-39">[39]</a></sup> He signed a four-year contract, keeping him at the club until 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Barnett made his debut for the club in a 2–1 <a href="/wiki/Turf_Moor" title="Turf Moor">away</a> defeat to Burnley on the opening day of the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C._season" title="2007–08 West Bromwich Albion F.C. season">2007–08 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club in a 3–2 <a href="/wiki/Glanford_Park" title="Glanford Park">away</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Scunthorpe_United_F.C." title="Scunthorpe United F.C.">Scunthorpe United</a> on 22 September 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Barnett scored his second goal of the season in a 1–1 draw with Stoke City on 3 October 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> His performance in a 2–1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walkers_Stadium" title="Walkers Stadium">away</a> win over Leicester City on 8 December 2007 earned him a place in the Championship Team of the Week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Barnett later scored a header, his third goal of the season in a 1–1 draw with Watford on 12 April 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> He played the full 90 minutes in a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Loftus_Road" title="Loftus Road">away</a> win over Queens Park Rangers on the final day of the season, which was enough to guarantee West Brom the Championship title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> As a result, this triggered a promotion clause which was agreed with his former club Luton, thus the latter received an add-on of £250,000 from his transfer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Barnett made 36 appearances during the season, scoring three times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2007.2F2008_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2007.2F2008-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following West Brom's promotion, Barnett made his Premier League debut on the opening day of the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C._season" title="2008–09 West Bromwich Albion F.C. season">2008–09 season</a> in a 1–0 <a href="/wiki/Emirates_Stadium" title="Emirates Stadium">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> One week later, he made another starting appearance in a 2–1 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Barnett played in the League Cup second round match against <a href="/wiki/Hartlepool_United_F.C." title="Hartlepool United F.C.">Hartlepool United</a>, who produced an upset by defeating West Brom 3–1 after <a href="/wiki/Overtime_(sports)#Association_football_2" title="Overtime (sports)">extra time</a>, with Barnett responsible for losing possession in the lead up to their first goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> He played the full 90 minutes in a 0–0 draw with <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> four days later, helping to keep a <a href="/wiki/Shutout#Association_football" title="Shutout">clean sheet</a> to earn West Brom their first point of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Barnett made another starting appearance two weeks later, helping West Brom to win their first match of the season in a 3–2 win over <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> by winning a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty</a> after he was tripped by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Green" title="Robert Green">Robert Green</a>, which was converted by <a href="/wiki/Roman_Bedn%C3%A1%C5%99" title="Roman Bednář">Roman Bednář</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> However, during West Brom's next match against <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(region)" title="West Midlands (region)">West Midlands</a> rivals <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>, he was at fault for conceding both goals and lost 2–1 as a result.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> His poor performance meant he was dropped to the substitutes bench and remained an unused substitute for the subsequent match, a 1–0 <a href="/wiki/Riverside_Stadium" title="Riverside Stadium">away</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> on 27 September 2008, West Brom's first away win of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Barnett was as an unused substitute for a further eight matches,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-SW-56">[56]</a></sup> during which time he sustained a knee injury,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> before coming on as a 27th-minute substitute for the injured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abdoulaye_Meite" title="Abdoulaye Meite">Abdoulaye Meite</a> in a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Bridge_(stadium)" title="Stamford Bridge (stadium)">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> on 26 December 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> Five starting appearances followed, but he sustained an injury and had to be substituted in the 39th minute during the latter match, a 3–0 home win over Middlesbrough on 17 January 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Barnett's injury meant he missed a 2–2 draw with Burnley one week later,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> before returning to the starting lineup for the next match, a 3–2 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> He made one further starting appearance in a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Craven_Cottage" title="Craven Cottage">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> two weeks later,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> before a recurrence of his knee injury ruled him out for the rest of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Coventry_City_.28loan.29">Coventry City (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Barnett&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Coventry City (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 4 November 2009, Barnett joined West Brom's Championship rivals Coventry City on loan until the end of January,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> having made just two substitute appearances in the league during the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C._season" title="2009–10 West Bromwich Albion F.C. season">2009–10 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2009.2F2010_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2009.2F2010-65">[65]</a></sup> Barnett made his debut for the club in a 2–1 away defeat to Derby County on 6 November 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2009.2F2010_65-1"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2009.2F2010-65">[65]</a></sup> After making 11 appearances for the club during his initial loan spell, Barnett's loan with Coventry was extended until the end of the season on 2 February 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> He conceded two penalties in consecutive league appearances, both of which saw him sent off in a 4–1 <a href="/wiki/St_James%27_Park" title="St James' Park">away</a> defeat to Newcastle United on 17 February 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> followed by a 2–1 home win over Scunthorpe United 10 days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> Barnett made 20 league appearances for Coventry during his time with the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2009.2F2010_65-2"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2009.2F2010-65">[65]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 26 August 2010, he joined Norwich City on loan until 4 January 2011, to cover for injuries sustained to <a href="/wiki/Michael_Nelson_(footballer)" title="Michael Nelson (footballer)">Michael Nelson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zak_Whitbread" title="Zak Whitbread">Zak Whitbread</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> after having played his first match of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C._season" title="2010–11 West Bromwich Albion F.C. season">2010–11 season</a> for West Brom two days previously in a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_Road" title="Brisbane Road">away</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a> in the League Cup second round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> He was handed squad number 20 at Norwich and made his debut two days later in a 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest_F.C." title="Nottingham Forest F.C.">Nottingham Forest</a> at the <a href="/wiki/City_Ground" title="City Ground">City Ground</a>, forming a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Ward" title="Elliott Ward">Elliott Ward</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> On 20 November 2010, Barnett scored his first goal for the club with a header from a <a href="/wiki/David_Fox_(footballer)" title="David Fox (footballer)">David Fox</a> corner in a 1–1 draw with Leeds United.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was confirmed on 10 December 2010 that Barnett would join Norwich on a permanent three-and-a-half deal for an undisclosed fee, starting from 1 January 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> On the following day, he conceded a penalty in a 2–0 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a>, for which he received his second yellow card of the match and was subsequently sent off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> In protest, he threw the ball at referee Simon Hooper, which earned him an extended two-match ban from <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">The Football Association</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> Barnett suffered a hamstring injury in a 2–1 home win over Reading on 12 February 2011, which ruled him out for the rest of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> In his absence, Norwich won promotion to the Premier League as runners-up after a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Fratton_Park" title="Fratton Park">away</a> win over Portsmouth on 2 May 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barnett played in his first Premier League match for Norwich as a 71st-minute substitute for the injured Zak Whitbread in a 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> on the opening day of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Norwich_City_F.C._season" title="2011–12 Norwich City F.C. season">2011–12 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> He started the next game, a 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>, but was adjudged by referee <a href="/wiki/Neil_Swarbrick" title="Neil Swarbrick">Neil Swarbrick</a> to have committed a <a href="/wiki/Professional_foul" title="Professional foul">professional foul</a> on <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Walters" title="Jonathan Walters">Jonathan Walters</a> in the second half, conceding a penalty and receiving a red card.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> On 26 September 2011, Barnett scored his first Premier League goal in a 2–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> After featuring a 2–1 home defeat to Leicester City in the FA Cup fifth round on 18 February 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> Barnett was only named in the matchday squad on two further occasions, but remained an unused substitute in both matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_56-1"><a href="#cite_note-SW-56">[56]</a></sup> He ended the season having made 18 appearances and scoring once.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2011.2F2012_82-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2011.2F2012-82">[82]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barnett's first appearance of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Norwich_City_F.C._season" title="2012–13 Norwich City F.C. season">2012–13 season</a> was in a 1–1 draw with Queens Park Rangers on 25 August 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> He made a further seven Premier League appearances, before featuring in three successive cup matches,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2012.2F2013_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2012.2F2013-84">[84]</a></sup> the last of which was a 1–0 home defeat to former club Luton Town in the FA Cup fourth round on 26 January 2013, the first time a non-league club had knocked a Premier League club out of the FA Cup since 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> On transfer deadline day, it appeared Barnett was no longer in the future plans of manager <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hughton" title="Chris Hughton">Chris Hughton</a>, after he was offered as part of a bid in an attempt to sign <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a> defender <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Davies" title="Curtis Davies">Curtis Davies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Cardiff_City_.28loan.29">Cardiff City (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Barnett&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cardiff City (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 14 March 2013, Barnett completed a one-month loan move to Championship club Cardiff City as a replacement for injured captain <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hudson_(footballer,_born_1982)" title="Mark Hudson (footballer, born 1982)">Mark Hudson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> Barnett made his debut for the club two days later in a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_Stadium" title="Hillsborough Stadium">away</a> win over Sheffield Wednesday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> He made eight appearances for the club,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2012.2F2013_84-1"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2012.2F2013-84">[84]</a></sup> forming a defensive partnership with <a href="/wiki/Ben_Turner_(footballer)" title="Ben Turner (footballer)">Ben Turner</a> as Cardiff went on to win the Championship title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> His loan was later extended until the end of the season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup> but he was recalled by Norwich on 24 April 2013 to cover for injuries to <a href="/wiki/Michael_Turner_(footballer)" title="Michael Turner (footballer)">Michael Turner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steven_Whittaker" title="Steven Whittaker">Steven Whittaker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wigan_Athletic">Wigan Athletic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Barnett&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Wigan Athletic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 August 2013, Barnett signed a three-year contract with newly relegated Championship club Wigan Athletic, transferring from Norwich City for an undisclosed fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup> He scored on his debut in a 4–0 <a href="/wiki/Oakwell" title="Oakwell">away</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a> on the opening day of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Wigan_Athletic_F.C._season" title="2013–14 Wigan Athletic F.C. season">2013–14 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup> Barnett featured in a 2–0 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Community_Shield" title="FA Community Shield">FA Community Shield</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a> one week later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> He scored his second goal for the club on 20 August 2013, rescuing a point in the final minute of a 2–2 draw with <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_Rovers_F.C." title="Doncaster Rovers F.C.">Doncaster Rovers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup> Barnett later made his <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a> debut in a 3–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/NK_Maribor" title="NK Maribor">NK Maribor</a> on 8 October 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup> His third goal for the club was the opening goal of a 2–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> on 2 November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> Barnett scored his first Europa League goal in a 2–1 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/S.V._Zulte_Waregem" title="S.V. Zulte Waregem">Zulte Waregem</a> on 28 November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup> Later in the season, Barnett scored a header from a <a href="/wiki/Jordi_G%C3%B3mez" title="Jordi Gómez">Jordi Gómez</a> free kick in a 3–1 away win over Ipswich Town on 15 March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> Wigan secured a place in the Championship play-offs after a 1–0 <a href="/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_(stadium)" title="St Andrew's (stadium)">away</a> win over Birmingham City on 29 April 2014, during which Barnett replaced <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Maloney" title="Shaun Maloney">Shaun Maloney</a> as a 90th-minute substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup> A 2–1 defeat to Queens Park Rangers in the play-off semi-final second leg ended Wigan's hopes of an instant return to the Premier League after a 96th-minute winning goal from <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Austin" title="Charlie Austin">Charlie Austin</a>, before which Barnett replaced <a href="/wiki/Gary_Caldwell" title="Gary Caldwell">Gary Caldwell</a> as a 74th-minute substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup> Barnett ended his first season with the club having made 53 appearances and scoring five goals in all competitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2013.2F2014_102-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2013.2F2014-102">[102]</a></sup></p>
<p>Barnett began the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Wigan_Athletic_F.C._season" title="2014–15 Wigan Athletic F.C. season">2014–15 season</a> by playing the full 90 minutes in a shock 2–1 <a href="/wiki/Pirelli_Stadium" title="Pirelli Stadium">away</a> defeat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Two" title="Football League Two">League Two</a> club <a href="/wiki/Burton_Albion_F.C." title="Burton Albion F.C.">Burton Albion</a> in the League Cup first round on 12 August 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-103"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> He remained an unused substitute for the majority of August and September,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_56-2"><a href="#cite_note-SW-56">[56]</a></sup> with the exception of substitute appearances in a 4–0 home win over Birmingham City on 30 August 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-104"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup> a 2–0 <a href="/wiki/Dean_Court" title="Dean Court">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a> on 27 September 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-105"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup> prior to a 0–0 draw with Nottingham Forest three days later, during which he replaced the injured <a href="/wiki/Emmerson_Boyce" title="Emmerson Boyce">Emmerson Boyce</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup> In October, he was named in the starting lineup and played the full 90 minutes in every match,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2014.2F2015_107-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2014.2F2015-107">[107]</a></sup> until receiving a red card for tripping over <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Rodallega" title="Hugo Rodallega">Hugo Rodallega</a> in penalty area, thus conceding a penalty in a 3–3 draw with Fulham on 1 November 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-108"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup> After serving his suspension, Barnett returned to the starting lineup, continuing to play the full 90 minutes of every match,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2014.2F2015_107-1"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2014.2F2015-107">[107]</a></sup> up to and including a 2–1 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Rotherham_United_F.C." title="Rotherham United F.C.">Rotherham United</a> on 20 December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-109"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup> However, his sequence of starting appearances was ended by a hamstring injury which resulted in his absence from a 2–0 away win over Leeds United and the subsequent two matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-110"><a href="#cite_note-110">[110]</a></sup> He returned to first-team football on 10 January 2015 in a 3–1 away defeat to Birmingham City, replacing the injured <a href="/wiki/Iv%C3%A1n_Ramis" title="Iván Ramis">Iván Ramis</a> in the 66th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-111"><a href="#cite_note-111">[111]</a></sup> Subsequently, Barnett made a further five starting appearances during January and February,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2014.2F2015_107-2"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2014.2F2015-107">[107]</a></sup> before another injury saw him return to the sidelines for a 1–0 away win over Reading on 17 February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-112"><a href="#cite_note-112">[112]</a></sup> Upon his return from injury, he was named on the substitutes bench for the remainder of the season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_56-3"><a href="#cite_note-SW-56">[56]</a></sup> thus ending the season having made 21 appearances in all competitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2014.2F2015_107-3"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2014.2F2015-107">[107]</a></sup></p>
<p>With Wigan now in League One, following their relegation from the Championship at the end of the 2014–15 season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-113"><a href="#cite_note-113">[113]</a></sup> Barnett's first appearance of the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Wigan_Athletic_F.C._season" title="2015–16 Wigan Athletic F.C. season">2015–16 season</a> was once more a League Cup first round fixture, this time a 2–1 home defeat to League One rivals <a href="/wiki/Bury_F.C." title="Bury F.C.">Bury</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-114"><a href="#cite_note-114">[114]</a></sup> He scored Wigan's first goal during a 3–2 <a href="/wiki/Proact_Stadium" title="Proact Stadium">away</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Chesterfield_F.C." title="Chesterfield F.C.">Chesterfield</a> on 5 September 2015 to begin a comeback from 2–0 down in the final 10 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-115"><a href="#cite_note-115">[115]</a></sup> Barnett was sent off for a professional foul in Wigan's next match, a 3–2 <a href="/wiki/Vale_Park" title="Vale Park">away</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/Port_Vale_F.C." title="Port Vale F.C.">Port Vale</a> one week later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-116"><a href="#cite_note-116">[116]</a></sup> Following his return from suspension, Barnett made eight consecutive starting appearances during September and October,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2015.2F2016_117-0"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2015.2F2016-117">[117]</a></sup> but was dropped to the bench and remained an unused substitute during a 1–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Swindon_Town_F.C." title="Swindon Town F.C.">Swindon Town</a> on 31 October 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-118"><a href="#cite_note-118">[118]</a></sup> For the remainder of the season his first-team opportunities remained limited, restricting him to 24 appearances in all competitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2015.2F2016_117-1"><a href="#cite_note-Games_played_by_Leon_Barnett_in_2015.2F2016-117">[117]</a></sup> Barnett was not included in the matchday squad for Wigan's 4–0 <a href="/wiki/Bloomfield_Road" title="Bloomfield Road">away</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a>, which secured their instant return to the Championship,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-119"><a href="#cite_note-119">[119]</a></sup> before remaining an unused substitute in a 4–1 home defeat to Barnsley on the final day of the season which secured Wigan the League One title despite the result.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-120"><a href="#cite_note-120">[120]</a></sup> On 3 June 2016, Wigan announced that Barnett would be released upon the expiry of his contract on 1 July 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-121"><a href="#cite_note-121">[121]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bury">Bury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Barnett&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Bury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Barnett signed a two-year contract with League One club Bury on 19 July 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-122"><a href="#cite_note-122">[122]</a></sup> He made his debut in a 1–0 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Oldham_Athletic_A.F.C." title="Oldham Athletic A.F.C.">Oldham Athletic</a> on 20 August 2016, during which he scored an <a href="/wiki/Own_goal" title="Own goal">own goal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-123"><a href="#cite_note-123">[123]</a></sup> One week later, Barnett scored first goal for Bury to begin a second half comeback from 3–0 down to earn a 3–3 draw <a href="/wiki/Bescot_Stadium" title="Bescot Stadium">away</a> at <a href="/wiki/Walsall_F.C." title="Walsall F.C.">Walsall</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-124"><a href="#cite_note-124">[124]</a></sup></p>
