<p><i><b>Ray</b></i> is a 2004 American <a href="/wiki/Musical_film" title="Musical film">musical</a> <a href="/wiki/Biographical_film" title="Biographical film">biographical film</a> focusing on 30 years in the life of <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues" title="Rhythm and blues">rhythm and blues</a> musician <a href="/wiki/Ray_Charles" title="Ray Charles">Ray Charles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independently produced film</a> was written, produced and directed by <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Hackford" title="Taylor Hackford">Taylor Hackford</a>, and stars <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Foxx" title="Jamie Foxx">Jamie Foxx</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Title_role" title="Title role">title role</a>. Foxx received an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor#2000s" title="Academy Award for Best Actor">Best Actor</a> for his performance as well as the <a href="/wiki/62nd_Golden_Globe_Awards" title="62nd Golden Globe Awards">Golden Globe</a>, <a href="/wiki/58th_British_Academy_Film_Awards" title="58th British Academy Film Awards">BAFTA</a>, <a href="/wiki/11th_Screen_Actors_Guild_Awards" title="11th Screen Actors Guild Awards">Screen Actors Guild</a> and <a href="/wiki/10th_Critics%27_Choice_Awards" title="10th Critics' Choice Awards">Critics' Choice</a> awards, becoming the second actor to win all five major lead actor awards for the same performance, and the only one to win the Golden Globe in the Musical or Comedy (rather than the Drama) category.</p>
<p>Charles was set to attend an opening of the completed film, but died of liver disease in June, several months before its premiere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MusicLegendRay_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MusicLegendRay-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Raised on a sharecropping plantation in Northern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_(U.S._state)" title="Florida (U.S. state)">Florida</a>, Ray Charles Robinson went blind at the age of seven, shortly after witnessing his younger brother drown. Inspired by a fiercely independent mother who insisted he make his own way in the world, Charles found his calling and his gift behind a piano keyboard. Touring across the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chitlin_circuit" title="Chitlin circuit">chitlin circuit</a>, the soulful singer gained a reputation before exploding onto the worldwide stage when he pioneered the incorporation of rhythm and blues, <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock and roll</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gospel" title="Gospel">gospel</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Country_Music" title="Country Music">country</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orchestral" title="Orchestral">orchestral</a> influences into his inimitable style.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film's production was entirely financed by <a href="/wiki/Philip_Anschutz" title="Philip Anschutz">Philip Anschutz</a>, through his <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Bay_Productions" title="Bristol Bay Productions">Bristol Bay Productions</a> company. Taylor Hackford said in a DVD bonus feature that it took 15 years to make the film; or more specifically, as he later clarified in the liner notes of the soundtrack album, this is how long it took him to secure the financing. It was made on a budget of $40 million.</p>
<p>Charles was given a <a href="/wiki/Braille" title="Braille">Braille</a> copy of the film's original script; he objected only to a scene showing him taking up piano grudgingly, and a scene implying that Charles had shown mistress and lead "Raelette" Margie Hendricks how to shoot <a href="/wiki/Heroin" title="Heroin">heroin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MusicLegendRay_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-MusicLegendRay-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Ray</i> debuted at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2004 Toronto International Film Festival">2004 Toronto International Film Festival</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Ray</i> was released in theaters on October 29, 2004. The film went on to become a box-office hit, earning $75 million in the U.S. with an additional $50 million internationally, bringing its worldwide gross to $125 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reaction">Critical reaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Critical reaction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film received mostly positive reviews, with most of the praise going to Jamie Foxx's performance for which he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Actor">Academy Award for Best Actor</a>. The film did receive some negative reviews, with critics complaining that the film suffered from a mediocre screenplay which was buoyed by powerful performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RT_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-RT-4">[4]</a></sup> As of March 2014, <i>Ray</i> has a certified fresh rating of 81% based on 197 reviews at <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, with Critics Consensus say it's "An engrossing and energetic portrait of a great musician's achievements and foibles, Ray is anchored by Jamie Foxx's stunning performance as Ray Charles."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RT_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-RT-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to music critic <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a>, "Foxx does the impossible—radiates something approaching the charisma of the artist he's portraying... that's the only time an actor has ever brought a pop icon fully to life on-screen."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_from_noted_events">Differences from noted events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Differences from noted events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film's credits state that <i>Ray</i> is based on true events, but includes some characters, names, locations, and events which have been changed and others which have been "fictionalized for dramatization purposes." Examples of the fictionalized scenes include:</p>
<ul>
<li>The film's portrayal of Charles' brother George's death in 1935 shows him drowning to death in a metal tub after Ray doesn't attempt to rescue him because he assumes he is just playing; Ray's mother then discovers George drowning when calling the boys in for dinner. Though George did drown to death in a metal tub, Ray did try to pull him out, but was unable to do so due to George's large body weight;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raybro_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-raybro-6">[6]</a></sup> Ray then ran inside to tell his mother what happened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raybro_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-raybro-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>In the studio scene where Charles is taught the "Mess Around," he is told it is in the "key of G", but the "Mess Around" is actually in the key of E flat.</li>
<li>In the film, when he arrives in Seattle at the club where he is going to audition, Ray meets a teenage Quincy Jones that very night. This event is only partly true, as Ray actually met Quincy a few days after arriving.</li>
<li>Throughout the film, it is suggested that Ray's depression and heroin addiction were fueled by nervous breakdowns he had over the deaths of both George and his mother, as well as his blindness. In reality, the death of his mother did give him a nervous breakdown and was thought to be a leading cause of his depression,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ipppav_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ipppav-7">[7]</a></sup> but the death of George and his blindness did not lead to nervous breakdowns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ipppav_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-ipppav-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>It is true that Charles kicked his heroin addiction after undergoing treatment in a psychiatric hospital during 1965, as stated towards the end of the film, but it is not mentioned that he would often use gin and marijuana as substitutes for heroin throughout much of the remaining years of his life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ipppav_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-ipppav-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raygugeor_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-raygugeor-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>In the scene where "<a href="/wiki/What%27d_I_Say" title="What'd I Say">What'd I Say</a>" is being played, Charles is depicted as playing a <a href="/wiki/Rhodes_piano" title="Rhodes piano">Fender Rhodes electric piano</a>, but in reality, he used a <a href="/wiki/Wurlitzer_electric_piano" title="Wurlitzer electric piano">Wurlitzer electric piano</a> on the original recording and begun using it on tour in 1956, because he didn't trust the tuning and quality of the pianos provided to him at every venue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-evans109_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-evans109-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>In the film, when his backing singer and mistress Margie Hendricks informs Ray she is pregnant with his child, Ray suggests she should have an abortion, out of loyalty to Della; Margie decides to keep the baby and soon leaves Ray to pursue a separate singing career after he refuses to abandon his family, move in with her and welcome the baby into his life. In reality, Hendricks did conceive a child with Charles and abandoned him after he refused to leave Della, but Charles never asked her to have an abortion, and welcomed any child he conceived, whether from Della or any mistress, into his personal life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raygugeor_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-raygugeor-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>The film shows Ray having a party in Los Angeles sometime in 1965 when he is informed that Margie has died of a drug overdose, and he is devastated by the tragedy. In reality, her death from an alleged drug overdose did reportedly upset Charles, but it did not occur until 1973.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>In the scene where Charles is about to enter a segregated music hall in Augusta, Georgia, in 1962, a group of civil rights activists protesting just outside the hall successfully persuade him not to perform; Charles then declares that he will no longer perform in segregated public facilities and in response, the Georgia state legislature passes a resolution banning Charles from ever performing again in the state. In reality, a group of civil rights activists did successfully persuade Charles to reject this invitation, but the advice came in the form of a telegram rather than a street protest;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raygugeor_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-raygugeor-8">[8]</a></sup> Charles also did make up for the gig later, and was never banned from performing in Georgia and still accepted invitations to perform at segregated public facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raygugeor_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-raygugeor-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>During the final scene in the film where Charles' version of "<a href="/wiki/Georgia_on_My_Mind" title="Georgia on My Mind">Georgia on My Mind</a>" becomes Georgia's state song, Charles is congratulated by his wife Della, and a resolution is also passed to lift the lifetime ban he had received in 1962 after he declared he would no longer perform at segregated public facilities. In reality, by the time "Georgia on My Mind" became Georgia's state song in 1979, Charles and Delia had already divorced, so she wasn't present when Charles performed at the Georgia State Legislature;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raygugeor_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-raygugeor-8">[8]</a></sup> and since he had never been banned from performing in Georgia in the first place, no such resolution was ever passed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raygugeor_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-raygugeor-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
