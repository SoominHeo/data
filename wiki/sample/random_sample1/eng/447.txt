<p>The <b>2012 Guatemala earthquake</b> occurred on November 7 at <abbr title="16:35:47 UTC">10:35:47 local time</abbr>. The shock had a <a href="/wiki/Moment_magnitude_scale" title="Moment magnitude scale">moment magnitude</a> of 7.4 and a maximum <a href="/wiki/Mercalli_intensity_scale" title="Mercalli intensity scale">Mercalli Intensity</a> of VII (<i>Very strong</i>). The <a href="/wiki/Epicenter" title="Epicenter">epicenter</a> was located in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, 35 kilometres (22 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Champerico" title="Champerico">Champerico</a> in the department of <a href="/wiki/Retalhuleu_Department" title="Retalhuleu Department">Retalhuleu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USGS_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-USGS-1">[1]</a></sup> The region is one of many earthquakes, where the <a href="/wiki/Cocos_Plate" title="Cocos Plate">Cocos Plate</a> is being subducted along the <a href="/wiki/Middle_America_Trench" title="Middle America Trench">Middle America Trench</a> beneath the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Plate" title="North American Plate">North American</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Plate" title="Caribbean Plate">Caribbean Plates</a>, near their <a href="/wiki/Triple_junction" title="Triple junction">triple junction</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The quake was reportedly felt in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> and in parts of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Damage to buildings was reported in several cities in Guatemala, including <a href="/wiki/San_Marcos,_Guatemala" title="San Marcos, Guatemala">San Marcos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quetzaltenango" title="Quetzaltenango">Quetzaltenango</a> and the capital <a href="/wiki/Guatemala_City" title="Guatemala City">Guatemala City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBCNews2012-11-08_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBCNews2012-11-08-5">[5]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Tsunami_Warning_Center" title="Pacific Tsunami Warning Center">Pacific Tsunami Warning Center</a> issued a warning about the possibility of a local <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> within 100 to 200 miles (160 to 320 km) of the epicenter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hufpost2012-11-07_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-hufpost2012-11-07-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>With 39 dead, this is the most intense and deadliest earthquake that has hit Guatemala since the <a href="/wiki/1976_Guatemala_earthquake" title="1976 Guatemala earthquake">earthquake of 1976</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_crece_numero_evacuados_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Prensa_Libre_crece_numero_evacuados-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes.com_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-latimes.com-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Damage_and_casualties_by_country">Damage and casualties by country</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Guatemala_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Damage and casualties by country">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guatemala">Guatemala</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Guatemala_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Guatemala">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/San_Marcos_Department" title="San Marcos Department">San Marcos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quetzaltenango_Department" title="Quetzaltenango Department">Quetzaltenango</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solol%C3%A1_Department" title="Sololá Department">Sololá</a>, <a href="/wiki/Totonicap%C3%A1n_Department" title="Totonicapán Department">Totonicapán</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quich%C3%A9_Department" title="Quiché Department">Quiché</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huehuetenango_Department" title="Huehuetenango Department">Huehuetenango</a> were the hardest hit departments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_da.C3.B1os_occidente_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Prensa_Libre_da.C3.B1os_occidente-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_estado_de_calamidad_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Prensa_Libre_estado_de_calamidad-10">[10]</a></sup> Preliminary reports mention a death toll of at least 42 in Guatemala, of which 29 in San Marcos, 11 in Quetzaltenango, and 2 in Sololá.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBCNews2012-11-08_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBCNews2012-11-08-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> According to the minister of energy of Guatemala, the earthquake left 73,000 households without power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The Guatemalan president initially declared a 30-day "state of calamity" in the departments of Retalhuleu, Sololá, Totonicapán, San Marcos, Quetzaltenango, Quiché and Huehuetenango.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_estado_de_calamidad_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Prensa_Libre_estado_de_calamidad-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The "state of calamity" was subsequently prolonged until being revoked on July 25, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In Guatemala, as of November 11, 9,414 houses were found damaged, and 18,755 people were evacuated, 7,218 of which stayed in 61 refuges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_crece_numero_evacuados_2-2"><a title="Pan-American Highway">Pan-American Highway</a> in Guatemala (CA-1) was damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_tres_departamentos_17-0"><a <p>There were collapsed buildings and reported deaths and injuries in San Marcos. Accounts of deaths and injuries in San Marcos were initially difficult to confirm due to communication interruption and roads blocked by landslides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Many people in San Marcos, fearing possible aftershocks, gathered on the cold and dark streets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_remueven_escombros_San_Marcos_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Prensa_Libre_remueven_escombros_San_Marcos-19">[19]</a></sup> At least 50 houses were damaged in <a href="/wiki/El_Quetzal" title="El Quetzal">El Quetzal</a>, 9 of which no longer inhabitable, and the dwellers had to take refuge in the community public school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> More than 300 people stayed in 10 refuges in the department.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_remueven_escombros_San_Marcos_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Prensa_Libre_remueven_escombros_San_Marcos-19">[19]</a></sup> Lack of water in the refuges in San Pedro Sacatepéquez and San Marcos due to the collapse of piping caused preoccupations about the sanitary conditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Eight people were buried in a landslide in <a href="/wiki/Concepci%C3%B3n_Chiquirichapa" title="Concepción Chiquirichapa">Concepción Chiquirichapa</a>. Ten deaths were reported in Quetzaltenango department.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> 156 houses were damaged in Quetzaltenango Department, and government buildings in Quetzaltenango City (Xela) were slightly damaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Retalhuleu, situated in the south, is close to the epicenter. The Departmental Palace of Retalhuleu, a historical building, was damaged, which caused the government offices therein to be relocated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sololá reported the most damaged houses with number of 4,756, followed by 3,870 in San Marcos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_tres_departamentos_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Prensa_Libre_tres_departamentos-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cracks were reported in <a href="/wiki/National_Palace_(Guatemala)" title="National Palace (Guatemala)">Palacio Nacional de la Cultura</a> in Guatemala City, a former government seat built between 1939 and 1943.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some houses of the coffee growers were damaged. No major damage was reported for agricultural products such as coffee, vegetables and potatoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Guatemala_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Minor damage was reported in the state of <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a>, which neighbors Guatemala. In Chiapas, public buildings were evacuated, and telephone and internet services were interrupted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Over a dozen of buildings in Chiapas had minor fissures; the city hall of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tapachula,_Chiapas" title="Tapachula, Chiapas">Tapachula</a>, the installations of the Desarrollo Integral de la Familia, along with a school in <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_Hidalgo,_Chiapas" title="Ciudad Hidalgo, Chiapas">Ciudad Hidalgo</a>, were among the places affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the state of <a href="/wiki/Oaxaca" title="Oaxaca">Oaxaca</a>, several aftershocks of the earthquake were felt throughout various communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The quake was also felt in the states of <a href="/wiki/Campeche" title="Campeche">Campeche</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guerrero" title="Guerrero">Guerrero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Michoacán">Michoacán</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morelos" title="Morelos">Morelos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puebla" title="Puebla">Puebla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">Tabasco</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, workers rushed to the streets from the office buildings, and no major damage was reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes.com_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-latimes.com-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="El_Salvador">El Salvador</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Guatemala_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: El Salvador">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a>, many urban dwellers evacuated their homes in the capital city, but there were no reports of any damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Guatemala_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nicaragua">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> emitted a tsunami alert after the quake and alerted several communities along the coastlines to mobilize to higher altitudes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relief_and_reconstruction">Relief and reconstruction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Guatemala_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Relief and reconstruction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The minister of finance of Guatemala expressed that 800 million <a href="/wiki/Guatemalan_quetzal" title="Guatemalan quetzal">quetzales</a> were ready for covering emergent needs due to the earthquake. The Ministry of Health of Guatemala assigned 1 million quetzales to the hospital of San Marcos and dispatched two trucks with medicines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_da.C3.B1os_occidente_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Prensa_Libre_da.C3.B1os_occidente-9">[9]</a></sup> In Guatemala City, various organizations collected donated provisions for people affected by the earthquake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The minister of interior announced a fund of 25 million quetzales for the reconstruction of damaged police stations, prisons, and government buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>The United States Ambassador to Guatemala announced to offer 50,000 USD to Coordinadora Nacional para la Reducción de Desastres (CONRED) for immediate humanitarian help. The United States and Taiwan would offer 110,000 USD in total as economical support. The <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Development_Bank" title="Inter-American Development Bank">Inter-American Development Bank</a> would offer 200,000 USD. Various countries offered to provide help in personnel or matter, including Venezuela and Chile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/World_Food_Programme" title="World Food Programme">World Food Programme</a> offered 72 tonnes of emergency food aid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 9, the authorities started the evaluation of the damaged houses in San Marcos and San Pedro Sacatepéquez to decide which to be repaired and which to be demolished and rebuilt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The reconstruction in San Marcos had started on November 27.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> It was estimated that the reconstruction of the houses could need at least a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prensa_Libre_tres_departamentos_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-Prensa_Libre_tres_departamentos-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
