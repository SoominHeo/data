<p><b>Rae Dawn Chong</b> (born February 28, 1961) is a Canadian-American actress. She is best known for her roles in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Quest_for_Fire_(film)" title="Quest for Fire (film)">Quest for Fire</a></i> (1981), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Color_Purple_(1985_film)" title="The Color Purple (1985 film)">The Color Purple</a></i> (1985), <i><a href="/wiki/Commando_(1985_film)" title="Commando (1985 film)">Commando</a></i> (1985), and <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Runner" title="Time Runner">Time Runner</a></i> (1993). She is the daughter of comedian and actor <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Chong" title="Tommy Chong">Tommy Chong</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rae_Dawn_Chong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chong was born in 1961 in <a href="/wiki/Edmonton" title="Edmonton">Edmonton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, Canada, the first daughter of Maxine Sneed and comedian/actor <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Chong" title="Tommy Chong">Tommy Chong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrank1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-jrank1-2">[2]</a></sup> Chong's father is of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_Canadian" title="Chinese Canadian">Chinese</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scotch-Irish_Canadian" title="Scotch-Irish Canadian">Scotch-Irish</a> descent, and her mother is of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Afro-Canadian" title="Afro-Canadian">Afro-Canadian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cherokee" title="Cherokee">Cherokee</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pratt_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-pratt-3">[3]</a></sup> Her sister <a href="/wiki/Robbi_Chong" title="Robbi Chong">Robbi Chong</a> is a model and actress. They have three younger half-brothers (one adopted) and a half-sister by their father's second wife. In addition to Rae Dawn, two of her sisters and their half-brother <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Chong" title="Marcus Chong">Marcus Chong</a> have pursued acting careers.</p>
<p>Chong has said that her paternal grandfather left a poor village in China in the 1930s to live with an aunt in Canada, and although he spoke Cantonese, he refused to teach his children or grandchildren; "I think my grandfather had great racial shame, which was hard on us growing up... We grew up desperate to know anything about our Chinese culture."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rae_Dawn_Chong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After acting in a few television roles, Chong's second feature film was <i><a href="/wiki/Quest_for_Fire_(film)" title="Quest for Fire (film)">Quest for Fire</a></i> (1981), for which she won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genie_Award" title="Genie Award">Genie Award</a> for Best Performance by an Actress in a Leading Role in 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Other notable roles have been in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Choose_Me" title="Choose Me">Choose Me</a></i> (1984), <i><a href="/wiki/Beat_Street" title="Beat Street">Beat Street</a></i> (1984), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Color_Purple_(1985_film)" title="The Color Purple (1985 film)">The Color Purple</a></i> (1985), and <i><a href="/wiki/Commando_(1985_film)" title="Commando (1985 film)">Commando</a></i> (1985). She appeared with her father in <i><a href="/wiki/Cheech_%26_Chong%27s_The_Corsican_Brothers" title="Cheech &amp; Chong's The Corsican Brothers">Cheech &amp; Chong's The Corsican Brothers</a></i> (1984), and <i><a href="/wiki/Far_Out_Man" title="Far Out Man">Far Out Man</a></i> (1990).</p>
<p>Her most active period in films was during the late 1980s and throughout the 1990s. She has continued working in television and film. She was considered for the role of <a href="/wiki/Anne_Lindsey" title="Anne Lindsey">Anne Lindsey</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Highlander:_The_Series" title="Highlander: The Series">Highlander: The Series</a></i>.</p>
<p>Chong played the love interest in <a href="/wiki/Mick_Jagger" title="Mick Jagger">Mick Jagger</a>'s video "<a href="/wiki/Just_Another_Night_(Mick_Jagger_song)" title="Just Another Night (Mick Jagger song)">Just Another Night</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_family">Marriage and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rae_Dawn_Chong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriage and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chong married Owen Bayliss, a stockbroker, and they had a son named Morgan. They divorced in 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrank_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-jrank-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1989, she married again, to actor <a href="/wiki/C._Thomas_Howell" title="C. Thomas Howell">C. Thomas Howell</a>, her co-star in the feature film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soul_Man_(1986)" title="Soul Man (1986)">Soul Man</a></i>. They divorced in 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrank_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-jrank-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Chong married Nathan Ulrich.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
