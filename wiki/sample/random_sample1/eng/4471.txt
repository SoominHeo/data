<p><b>George Raymond "Ray" Stevenson</b> (born 25 May 1964) is an Northern Irish-English actor. He is known for playing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Titus_Pullo_(Character_of_Rome)" title="Titus Pullo (Character of Rome)">Titus Pullo</a> in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>/<a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Rome_(TV_series)" title="Rome (TV series)">Rome</a></i> (2005–2007) and in film as <a href="/wiki/Dagonet" title="Dagonet">Dagonet</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/King_Arthur_(2004_film)" title="King Arthur (2004 film)">King Arthur</a></i> (2004). Stevenson has portrayed two <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a> characters: <a href="/wiki/Punisher" title="Punisher">Frank Castle/The Punisher</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Punisher:_War_Zone" title="Punisher: War Zone">Punisher: War Zone</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Super_Hero_Squad_Show" title="The Super Hero Squad Show">The Super Hero Squad Show</a></i>; and <a href="/wiki/Volstagg" title="Volstagg">Volstagg</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Thor_(film)" title="Thor (film)">Thor</a></i> and its sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Thor:_The_Dark_World" title="Thor: The Dark World">Thor: The Dark World</a></i>. In the film <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_the_Irishman" title="Kill the Irishman">Kill the Irishman</a></i>, Stevenson portrayed Cleveland mobster <a href="/wiki/Danny_Greene" title="Danny Greene">Danny Greene</a>. In 2012 he appeared in the seventh season of <i><a href="/wiki/Dexter_(TV_series)" title="Dexter (TV series)">Dexter</a></i> as Isaak Sirko.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_Stevenson_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stevenson was born in <a href="/wiki/Lisburn" title="Lisburn">Lisburn</a>, in <a href="/wiki/County_Antrim" title="County Antrim">County Antrim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, the second of three sons of a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> pilot father and an <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He moved with his family to <a href="/wiki/Lemington" title="Lemington">Lemington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle upon Tyne</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, in 1972, at the age of eight, and later to <a href="/wiki/Cramlington" title="Cramlington">Cramlington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northumberland" title="Northumberland">Northumberland</a>, where he was brought up. Stevenson attended <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Old_Vic_Theatre_School" title="Bristol Old Vic Theatre School">Bristol Old Vic Theatre School</a>, graduating at the age of 29.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_Stevenson_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_Stevenson_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Stevenson has starred in several feature films, primarily in supporting roles. He made his debut in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Theory_of_Flight" title="The Theory of Flight">The Theory of Flight</a></i> (1998) with <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Branagh" title="Kenneth Branagh">Kenneth Branagh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Helena_Bonham_Carter" title="Helena Bonham Carter">Helena Bonham Carter</a>, where he played the part of a <a href="/wiki/Gigolo" title="Gigolo">gigolo</a> hired to help Bonham Carter's character to lose her virginity. In 2002, Stevenson starred in a short film <i>No Man's Land,</i> which was the antipodal picture from actor/director Edward Hicks prior to his graduation from the <a href="/wiki/London_Film_School" title="London Film School">London Film School</a>. In the 12½ minute film, set during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, Stevenson portrayed an experienced private who is forced to help a novice officer (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_Tristan_Birkin" title="David Tristan Birkin">David Birkin</a>), back to safety after they both find themselves trapped and isolated in "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/No_Man%27s_Land" title="No Man's Land">No Man's Land</a>" during the <a href="/wiki/First_Battle_of_Ypres" title="First Battle of Ypres">Battle of Ypres</a>. In 2004, Stevenson starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Clive_Owen" title="Clive Owen">Clive Owen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keira_Knightley" title="Keira Knightley">Keira Knightley</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/King_Arthur_(2004_film)" title="King Arthur (2004 film)">King Arthur</a></i>. He portrayed <a href="/wiki/Dagonet" title="Dagonet">Dagonet</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Round_Table" title="Round Table">knight of the round table</a> who sacrifices his life for his comrades.</p>
<p>Stevenson's first leading role in a film was in the <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> horror picture <i><a href="/wiki/Outpost_(film)" title="Outpost (film)">Outpost</a></i> (2008), portraying a mercenary pitted against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nazi" title="Nazi">Nazi</a> <a href="/wiki/Zombie" title="Zombie">zombies</a> in a bunker somewhere in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a>. That same year, he also starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Punisher:_War_Zone" title="Punisher: War Zone">Punisher: War Zone</a></i>, as <a href="/wiki/Punisher" title="Punisher">Frank Castle</a>, "The Punisher," a former Marine turned vigilante after the murder of his family. In 2010, Stevenson played the primary <a href="/wiki/Antagonist" title="Antagonist">antagonist</a> in the comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Other_Guys" title="The Other Guys">The Other Guys</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Mark_Wahlberg" title="Mark Wahlberg">Mark Wahlberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Will_Ferrell" title="Will Ferrell">Will Ferrell</a>.</p>
<p>In 2011, Stevenson appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_the_Irishman" title="Kill the Irishman">Kill the Irishman</a></i>, based on the book <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/To_Kill_the_Irishman" title="To Kill the Irishman">To Kill the Irishman</a></i>, as <a href="/wiki/Danny_Greene" title="Danny Greene">Danny Greene</a>, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_American" title="Irish American">Irish American</a> mobster who took on the Italian mob in <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> in the 1970s. He worked with Kenneth Branagh again, this time as a director, in the adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Thor_(film)" title="Thor (film)">Thor</a></i>, starring in the role of <a href="/wiki/Volstagg" title="Volstagg">Volstagg</a>, one of <a href="/wiki/Thor_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Thor (Marvel Comics)">Thor's</a> trusted comrades. Also that year, he played <a href="/wiki/Porthos" title="Porthos">Porthos</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paul_W.S._Anderson" title="Paul W.S. Anderson">Paul W.S. Anderson</a>'s adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Three_Musketeers_(2011_film)" title="The Three Musketeers (2011 film)">The Three Musketeers</a></i>. On 8 July 2011, Stevenson was cast as <a href="/wiki/Firefly_(G.I._Joe)" title="Firefly (G.I. Joe)">Firefly</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/G.I._Joe:_Retaliation" title="G.I. Joe: Retaliation">G.I. Joe: Retaliation</a></i> (which was released in 2013). Stevenson played 2014 along <a href="/wiki/Samuel_L._Jackson" title="Samuel L. Jackson">Samuel L. Jackson</a> in the Finnish-American thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Game_(2014_film)" title="Big Game (2014 film)">Big Game</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, he starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Divergent_(film)" title="Divergent (film)">Divergent</a></i>, based on the first book of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Divergent_Series" title="The Divergent Series">The Divergent Series</a></i>, written by <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Roth" title="Veronica Roth">Veronica Roth</a>. He reprised his role in the sequels, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Divergent_Series:_Insurgent" title="The Divergent Series: Insurgent">The Divergent Series: Insurgent</a></i>, released in March 2015, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Divergent_Series:_Allegiant" title="The Divergent Series: Allegiant">The Divergent Series: Allegiant</a></i>, released in March 2016.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_Stevenson_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He is known for playing the character of legionary Titus Pullo in the BBC/HBO television series <i><a href="/wiki/Rome_(TV_series)" title="Rome (TV series)">Rome</a></i>. He shares the lead starring role in the series with <a href="/wiki/Kevin_McKidd" title="Kevin McKidd">Kevin McKidd</a>.</p>
<p>Other TV-work includes guest appearances in popular TV series including <i><a href="/wiki/Waking_the_Dead_(TV_series)" title="Waking the Dead (TV series)">Waking the Dead</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Murphy%27s_Law_(TV_series)" title="Murphy's Law (TV series)">Murphy's Law</a></i> as well as lead roles in <i>City Central</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/At_Home_with_the_Braithwaites" title="At Home with the Braithwaites">At Home with the Braithwaites</a></i>. He has also appeared in several TV films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Kind_of_Life" title="Some Kind of Life">Some Kind of Life</a></i> in 1995, in which he co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Jane_Horrocks" title="Jane Horrocks">Jane Horrocks</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Return_of_the_Native" title="The Return of the Native">The Return of the Native</a></i>, which also featured <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Zeta-Jones" title="Catherine Zeta-Jones">Catherine Zeta Jones</a>. Some of his earliest parts were in two <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Cookson" title="Catherine Cookson">Catherine Cookson</a> films: <i>The Dwelling Place</i> (1994) and <i>The Tide of Life</i> (1996). Ray reprises his role this time as the voice of the Punisher in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Super_Hero_Squad_Show" title="The Super Hero Squad Show">The Super Hero Squad Show</a></i>. Stevenson plays as Isaak Sirko in the seventh season of <i><a href="/wiki/Dexter_(TV_series)" title="Dexter (TV series)">Dexter</a></i> in 2012.</p>
<p>On 24 March 2015 it was announced by producers that Stevenson would be joining the cast of the STARZ series <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Sails_(TV_series)" title="Black Sails (TV series)">Black Sails</a></i> as the character Edward Teach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage">Stage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_Stevenson_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His stage work includes playing the part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesus_Christ" title="Jesus Christ">Jesus Christ</a> in the <a href="/wiki/York_Mystery_Plays" title="York Mystery Plays">York Mystery Plays</a> in 2000 at <a href="/wiki/York_Minster" title="York Minster">York Minster</a>. In 2001 he took the part of Roger in the play <i>Mouth to Mouth</i> by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Elyot" title="Kevin Elyot">Kevin Elyot</a>, at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albery_Theatre" title="Albery Theatre">Albery Theatre</a> in London with <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Duncan" title="Lindsay Duncan">Lindsay Duncan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Maloney" title="Michael Maloney">Michael Maloney</a>. His most well-known part is perhaps that of the Cardinal in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Duchess_of_Malfi" title="The Duchess of Malfi">The Duchess of Malfi</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/John_Webster" title="John Webster">John Webster</a> with <a href="/wiki/Janet_McTeer" title="Janet McTeer">Janet McTeer</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">Royal National Theatre</a> in 2003.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ray_Stevenson_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stevenson was married in <a href="/wiki/City_of_Westminster" title="City of Westminster">Westminster</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, in 1997, to actress <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Gemmell" title="Ruth Gemmell">Ruth Gemmell</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> whom he met when they worked together on the TV drama <i><a href="/wiki/Band_of_Gold_(TV_series)" title="Band of Gold (TV series)">Band of Gold</a></i> (1995). They later played a husband and wife on screen in <i><a href="/wiki/Peak_Practice" title="Peak Practice">Peak Practice</a></i> (1997). The couple divorced after eight years of marriage. His current girlfriend is Italian anthropologist Elisabetta Caraccia, whom he met while filming <i>Rome</i>. The couple have two sons: Sebastiano Derek Stevenson, born on 24 December 2007, and Leonardo George Stevenson, born on 11 April 2011.</p>
