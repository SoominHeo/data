<p><b>Louise Amanda Harman</b> (born 19 December 1985),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> better known by the <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Lady Sovereign</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a>. She is best known for the songs "<a href="/wiki/9_to_5_(Lady_Sovereign_song)" title="9 to 5 (Lady Sovereign song)">9 to 5</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_Me_or_Hate_Me" title="Love Me or Hate Me">Love Me or Hate Me</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_family">Early life and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Sovereign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lady Sovereign was born Louise Amanda Harman in 1985 to Colin Andrew Harman and Nicola Harman (nÃ©e Wood), in Wembley, northwest London. She grew up in the run-down Chalkhill Estate â a <a href="/wiki/Council_house" title="Council house">council estate</a> that has since been demolished â with her parents and two siblings, older sister Chloe Christie (born 1984) and younger brother Richie Colin (born 1987).</p>
<p>Lady Sovereign was a <a href="/wiki/Tomboy" title="Tomboy">tomboy</a> who played <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> and hated wearing dresses. She had difficulty fitting in at school and was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Expulsion_(academia)" title="Expulsion (academia)">expelled</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preston_Manor_High_School" title="Preston Manor High School">Preston Manor High School</a> at 15 because of her abysmal attendance record and bad behaviour. A number of menial jobs followed, until she decided to pursue rap after hearing Ms. Dynamite on the radio: "When I heard <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ms_Dynamite" title="Ms Dynamite">Ms. Dynamite's</a> track 'Boooo!' in 2001 it inspired me. I hadn't heard a female MC before that. She's real. Her presence, her image, the way she does everything... she opened so many doors for us girls, MCs are real, they write their own stuff, they rap about what they know, the stuff around them."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lady Sovereign began uploading some of her songs and a picture to various music web sites when she was 15. She told <i><a href="/wiki/Newsweek" title="Newsweek">Newsweek</a></i> in 2006 that comments initially ran along the lines of, "You're white. You're a girl. You're British. You're crap."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newsweek_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Newsweek-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lady Sovereign signed with Universal in 2004. She released a number of solo records and opened for Basement Jaxx, The Streets, Dizzee Rascal, D12, and Obie Trice.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Her mother Nicola was diagnosed with a <a href="/wiki/Terminal_illness" title="Terminal illness">terminal</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brain_tumour" title="Brain tumour">brain tumour</a> and died on 14 March (<a href="/wiki/Mothering_Sunday" title="Mothering Sunday">Mothering Sunday</a> in the UK) 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Sovereign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005.E2.80.9306:_Early_singles_and_EPs">2005â06: Early singles and EPs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Sovereign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2005â06: Early singles and EPs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"The Battle" began a string of singles that would push Lady Sovereign into the spotlight. While "A Little Bit of Shhh!", "9 to 5", and "<a href="/wiki/Bad_Ass_Strippa" title="Bad Ass Strippa">Sad Arse Stripah</a>" were selling well, free Internet-only freestyles like "Tango" and "Cheeky" were becoming just as popular. On 15 November 2005, Chocolate Industries released the <i>Vertically Challenged</i> EP, which collected most of her singles recorded to date. In April 2006 she released her second EP, <i>Blah Blah</i> in the UK. She released yet another EP, but was limited for US radios; <i>Size Don't Matter!</i></p>

<p>Aside from her own records, 2005 saw Lady Sovereign appear as a guest or contributor on several projects. She began 2005 by appearing on the grime compilation <i>Run the Road</i>, both as a solo artist and with <a href="/wiki/The_Streets" title="The Streets">The Streets</a>. When <a href="/wiki/The_Ordinary_Boys" title="The Ordinary Boys">The Ordinary Boys</a> released the single "Boys Will Be Boys", Lady Sovereign came back with a reply remix, featuring the music and chorus of the original, but with mostly her own vocals to the tune of "girls will be girls". In May 2006, she was featured on the Ordinary Boys single, "Nine2Five", a remixed version of her own "9 to 5", credited as "The Ordinary Boys vs Lady Sovereign." "Nine2Five" entered the UK top 40 singles at number 39 on downloads only, and jumped to number 6 upon availability as a CD and 7" vinyl single during the week commencing 22 May 2005. This is her highest chart position to date.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9309:_Public_Warning">2006â09: <i>Public Warning</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Sovereign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006â09: Public Warning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Public_Warning" title="Public Warning">Public Warning</a></div>
<p>Lady Sovereign capped off 2005 by meeting with the American hip hop artist and <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a> of the labels <a href="/wiki/Def_Jam_Recordings" title="Def Jam Recordings">Def Jam Recordings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>. This marked her foray into the American music scene. With <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Usher_(entertainer)" title="Usher (entertainer)">Usher</a> and <a href="/wiki/L.A._Reid" title="L.A. Reid">L.A. Reid</a> seated next to him, Jay-Z asked for one on-the-spot <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_rap" title="Freestyle rap">freestyle</a> from Sovereign before offering her a contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Def_Jam" title="Def Jam">Def Jam</a>. With the single "Hoodie", Lady Sovereign released her full-length debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Public_Warning" title="Public Warning">Public Warning</a></i>, on Def Jam in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewsComAU1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-NewsComAU1-7">[7]</a></sup> Lady Sovereign is the first non-American female to ever be signed to Def Jam Record Label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Voice1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Voice1-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inde1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inde1-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 31 October 2006, her debut album, <i>Public Warning</i>, was released, featuring "<a href="/wiki/Random_(Lady_Sovereign_song)" title="Random (Lady Sovereign song)">Random</a>", "<a href="/wiki/9_to_5_(Lady_Sovereign_song)" title="9 to 5 (Lady Sovereign song)">9 to 5</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Hoodie_(Lady_Sovereign_song)" title="Hoodie (Lady Sovereign song)">Hoodie</a>" and single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_Me_or_Hate_Me" title="Love Me or Hate Me">Love Me or Hate Me</a>", which was also released on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SovHomepage1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-SovHomepage1-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The music video for the single features Lady Sovereign mocking stereotypical ideals of femininity by satirizing popular dress and mannerisms. Some of the lyrics include <i>I'm fat, I need a diet/No, in fact I'm just here lyin'/I ain't got the biggest breast-ises,/but I write all the bestest hits/I've got hairy armpits/But I don't walk around like this/I wear a big baggy t-shirt that hides that nasty shit.</i> With the lyrics continuing in this satirical nature, Lady Sovereign continues to comment on and express her dissonance with female lifestyles in popular culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> She began an American tour on 23 October 2006 and soon after appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">Late Show with David Letterman</a></i>. In addition to her own tour, she appeared as the opening act for various artists, including The Streets and <a href="/wiki/Gwen_Stefani" title="Gwen Stefani">Gwen Stefani</a> on her 2007 <a href="/wiki/The_Sweet_Escape_Tour" title="The Sweet Escape Tour">The Sweet Escape Tour</a>.</p>
<p>On 17 October 2006 "Love Me or Hate Me" became the first video by a British artist to reach number 1 on the United States (and original) version of <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">Total Request Live</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewsComAU1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-NewsComAU1-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Voice1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Voice1-8">[8]</a></sup> "Love Me or Hate Me" was released in the UK on 29 January 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> with the album following on 5 February 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lady Sovereign has recorded a cover of the <a href="/wiki/Sex_Pistols" title="Sex Pistols">Sex Pistols</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Pretty_Vacant" title="Pretty Vacant">Pretty Vacant</a>" for <i>Music from <a href="/wiki/The_O.C." title="The O.C.">The O.C.</a> Mix 6 â Covering Our Tracks</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.93present:_Jigsaw">2009âpresent: <i>Jigsaw</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Sovereign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2009âpresent: Jigsaw">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Jigsaw_(Lady_Sovereign_album)" title="Jigsaw (Lady Sovereign album)">Jigsaw (Lady Sovereign album)</a></div>
<p>Lady Sovereign released a second album in spring 2009. She also went on tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> After a final dispute with Island Records, it was revealed on her official website that she would release the album through her independent record label, Midget Records, and that it was fully written and some songs were already recorded. She also said the album was going to be released in the United States and United Kingdom on 7 April 2009. On Monday, 8 December 2008, Lady Sovereign restated that <i><a href="/wiki/Jigsaw_(Lady_Sovereign_album)" title="Jigsaw (Lady Sovereign album)">Jigsaw</a></i> would be released on 6 April 2009. She also released a free single entitled "I Got You Dancing", making it available on her MySpace and official website.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_and_commercial_appearances">Television and commercial appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Sovereign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Television and commercial appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lady Sovereign has appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Soccer_AM" title="Soccer AM">Soccer AM</a></i> several times. She has appeared in a feature story on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> and in an educational movie called <i>X-ED</i>.</p>
<p>In late 2006, "Love Me Or Hate Me" was played in a Verizon Wireless advertisement. It also appears on the soundtrack of the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Need_for_Speed:_Carbon" title="Need for Speed: Carbon">Need for Speed: Carbon</a></i>, and is the theme music for the show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Bad_Girls_Club" title="The Bad Girls Club">The Bad Girls Club</a></i>. "9 to 5" also appears on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Bad_Girls_Club" title="The Bad Girls Club">The Bad Girls Club</a></i>, as well as on the soundtrack of the <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a>'s version of EA Sports' <i><a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_(video_game)" title="2006 FIFA World Cup (video game)">FIFA World Cup 2006</a></i> video game, on the <i><a href="/wiki/Ugly_Betty" title="Ugly Betty">Ugly Betty</a></i> soundtrack and features in the 2007 film <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Trinian%27s_(2007_film)" title="St Trinian's (2007 film)"><i>St. Trinian's</i></a>, "Random" appears on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midnight_Club_3:_DUB_Edition_Remix" title="Midnight Club 3: DUB Edition Remix">Midnight Club 3: DUB Edition Remix</a> soundtrack, and features on an episode of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malcolm_in_The_Middle" title="Malcolm in The Middle">Malcolm in The Middle</a>, "I Got You Dancing" was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Need_for_Speed:_Nitro" title="Need for Speed: Nitro">Need for Speed: Nitro</a></i>, "Hoodie" was used for a <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Party" title="Mario Party">Mario Party</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_DS" title="Nintendo DS">Nintendo DS</a> commercial.</p>
<p>Some of Lady Sovereign's such as "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/9_to_5" title="9 to 5">9 to 5</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/So_Human" title="So Human">So Human</a>" were also used on the BBC 1 show Waterloo Road in 2009/10. Bizarre Creation's <i><a href="/wiki/Project_Gotham_Racing_4" title="Project Gotham Racing 4">Project Gotham Racing 4</a></i> video game on the <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a> features the song "Blah Blah" by Lady Sovereign, EA's <i><a href="/wiki/Fight_Night_Round_4" title="Fight Night Round 4">Fight Night Round 4</a></i> video game features Lady Sovereign's "Bang Bang" track, "So Human" appears in season 2 episode 1 of the television series '<a href="/wiki/90210_(TV_series)" title="90210 (TV series)">90210</a>', "Jigsaw" was featured in the second episode of the fifth season of MTV-show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hills_(TV_series)" title="The Hills (TV series)">The Hills</a></i>, called 'Everything Happens For A Reason'.</p>
<p>On 23 October 2006, Sovereign made an appearance on American talk show the <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">Late Show with David Letterman</a></i>. During her performance of "Love Me Or Hate Me" the lyrics "If you hate me, then fuck you" were changed to "if you hate me, then I hate you". Lady Sovereign served as the host and spokesperson for <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> and Chocolate Industries' <i><a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Swim" title="Chocolate Swim">Chocolate Swim</a></i>. In January 2010 <a href="/wiki/Bally_Total_Fitness" title="Bally Total Fitness">Bally Total Fitness</a> selected Lady Sovereign and her song "I Got You Dancing" for the featured artist section on their website, the song is also being used in their television commercials..</p>
<p>On 22 January 2010, Sovereign was due to appear on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/This_Week_(BBC_One_TV_series)" title="This Week (BBC One TV series)">This Week</a></i>, a BBC political review show, to discuss the laws regarding self-defence for householders. Early in the programme, she was shown backstage waving to camera in anticipation of her appearance. However, when the segment began, presenter <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Neil" title="Andrew Neil">Andrew Neil</a> apologised to viewers and explained the rapper had "done a runner." Neil instead discussed the topic with regular contributors <a href="/wiki/Michael_Portillo" title="Michael Portillo">Michael Portillo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diane_Abbott" title="Diane Abbott">Diane Abbott</a> and concluded by joking "Who needs Lady Sovereign? Who <i>is</i> Lady Sovereign?" Abbott laughed and responded "I don't know."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a>'</i>s Rich Pelley on 30 January, Lady Sovereign said of the incident: "I had a panic attack! My hands went stiff and I started hyperventilating. I didn't want to do it hours before but I went anyway, then I just changed my mind at the last minute, I guess."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lady Sovereign appeared on British game show <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Wipeout" title="Total Wipeout">Total Wipeout</a>'</i>s eighth celebrity special. She made it to the second round by default but failed to advance any further. She was the third evicted housemate from the seventh series of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celebrity_Big_Brother_2010_(UK)" title="Celebrity Big Brother 2010 (UK)">Celebrity Big Brother</a></i>. She was nominated for both the first and second evictions. Having survived the first double eviction, she was evicted the second time around with 69.5% of the vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Sovereign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 2009, while in <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a>, Australia for the Parklife Festival, Lady Sovereign was arrested for <a href="/wiki/Assault" title="Assault">assault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Public_intoxication" title="Public intoxication">drunk and disorderly behaviour</a> after spitting on a nightclub bouncer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> She had only been in the country seven hours before the assault occurred. She was ordered to pay a fine of <a href="/wiki/Australian_dollar" title="Australian dollar">$</a>400, and $200 in compensation to the bouncer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lady Sovereign was involved in a feud with fellow British female rapper, <a href="/wiki/Jentina" title="Jentina">Jentina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Sovereign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a 2010 interview with in <i><a href="/wiki/Diva_(magazine)" title="Diva (magazine)">Diva</a></i> magazine, Lady Sovereign said that she <a href="/wiki/Coming_out" title="Coming out">came out as gay</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> in the house during <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celebrity_Big_Brother_2010_(UK)" title="Celebrity Big Brother 2010 (UK)">Celebrity Big Brother</a></i> but that it did not end up in the broadcast show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
