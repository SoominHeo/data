<p><b>Lady Antebellum</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> group formed in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a> in 2006. The group is composed of <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Scott_(singer)" title="Hillary Scott (singer)">Hillary Scott</a> (lead and background vocals), <a href="/wiki/Charles_Kelley" title="Charles Kelley">Charles Kelley</a> (lead and background vocals, guitar), and <a href="/wiki/Dave_Haywood" title="Dave Haywood">Dave Haywood</a> (background vocals, guitar, piano, mandolin). Scott is the daughter of country music singer <a href="/wiki/Linda_Davis" title="Linda Davis">Linda Davis</a>, and Kelley is the brother of pop singer <a href="/wiki/Josh_Kelley" title="Josh Kelley">Josh Kelley</a>.</p>
<p>The group made its debut in 2007 as guest vocalists on <a href="/wiki/Jim_Brickman" title="Jim Brickman">Jim Brickman</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/Never_Alone_(Jim_Brickman_song)" title="Never Alone (Jim Brickman song)">Never Alone</a>", before signing to <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records_Nashville" title="Capitol Records Nashville">Capitol Nashville</a>. Lady Antebellum has released five albums for Capitol: <i><a href="/wiki/Lady_Antebellum_(album)" title="Lady Antebellum (album)">Lady Antebellum</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Need_You_Now_(Lady_Antebellum_album)" title="Need You Now (Lady Antebellum album)">Need You Now</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Own_the_Night" title="Own the Night">Own the Night</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_(Lady_Antebellum_album)" title="Golden (Lady Antebellum album)">Golden</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/747_(album)" title="747 (album)">747</a></i>, plus one Christmas album (<i><a href="/wiki/On_This_Winter%27s_Night" title="On This Winter's Night">On This Winter's Night</a></i>). Their first three albums are <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified platinum</a> or higher by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA). The albums have produced sixteen singles on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> charts, of which nine have reached number one. Their longest-lasting number one single is "<a href="/wiki/Need_You_Now_(Lady_Antebellum_song)" title="Need You Now (Lady Antebellum song)">Need You Now</a>", which spent five weeks at that position in 2009; both that song and 2011's "<a href="/wiki/Just_a_Kiss_(song)" title="Just a Kiss (song)">Just a Kiss</a>" reached number one on the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Contemporary_(chart)" title="Adult Contemporary (chart)">Adult Contemporary</a> charts.</p>
<p>Lady Antebellum was awarded Top New Duo or Group by the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music" title="Academy of Country Music">Academy of Country Music</a> and New Artist of the Year by the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association" title="Country Music Association">Country Music Association</a> in 2008. They were nominated for two Grammy Awards at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/51st_Grammy_Awards" title="51st Grammy Awards">51st Grammy Awards</a> and two more at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/52nd_Grammy_Awards" title="52nd Grammy Awards">52nd Grammy Awards</a>. Of these nominations, they took home the award for Best Country Performance by Duo or Group with Vocals for "I Run to You". They were awarded Top Vocal Group, Song of the Year ("Need You Now"), and Single of the Year ("Need You Now") at the 44th <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ACM_Awards" title="ACM Awards">ACM Awards</a> on April 18, 2010. They won five awards at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/53rd_Grammy_Awards" title="53rd Grammy Awards">53rd Grammy Awards</a>, including Song of the Year and Record of the Year for "Need You Now".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Lady Antebellum also scored "<a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Album">Best Country Album</a>" at the 54th Grammy Awards. By August 2013, the group had sold more than 12.5 million digital singles and 10 million albums in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>Lady Antebellum was formed in 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">[4]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Kelley" title="Charles Kelley">Charles Kelley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Haywood" title="Dave Haywood">Dave Haywood</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Scott_(singer)" title="Hillary Scott (singer)">Hillary Scott</a>. Scott is the daughter of country music singer <a href="/wiki/Linda_Davis" title="Linda Davis">Linda Davis</a>, best known for her duet vocals on <a href="/wiki/Reba_McEntire" title="Reba McEntire">Reba McEntire</a>'s 1993 hit "<a href="/wiki/Does_He_Love_You" title="Does He Love You">Does He Love You</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cst1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-cst1-5">[5]</a></sup> and Charles Kelley is the brother of pop and country artist <a href="/wiki/Josh_Kelley" title="Josh Kelley">Josh Kelley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Hillary Scott attended Donelson Christian Academy in <a href="/wiki/Donelson,_Tennessee" title="Donelson, Tennessee">Donelson, Tennessee</a>. Kelley moved to Nashville in mid-2005 from <a href="/wiki/Winston-Salem,_North_Carolina" title="Winston-Salem, North Carolina">Winston-Salem</a>, North Carolina, where he had been working construction with his brother John. Trying to become a successful solo country artist, Kelley convinced his old middle-school classmate, Haywood, to move to Nashville from <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> in 2006 so they could write music together. Shortly thereafter, Scott recognized Kelley from Myspace, and they started to talk at a Nashville music club. Kelley invited Scott to join him and Haywood in the new group, which assumed the name Lady Antebellum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-observe_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-observe-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the <i><a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2" title="BBC Radio 2">BBC Radio 2</a> Drivetime Show</i> August 9, 2010, the band explained to the host, <a href="/wiki/Liza_Tarbuck" title="Liza Tarbuck">Liza Tarbuck</a>, that the name Antebellum comes from when the group were photographing "antebellum" homes. The <a href="/wiki/Antebellum_architecture" title="Antebellum architecture">antebellum architectural style</a> describes the large plantation homes in the American South. The Latin word <i>bellum</i> means "war"; "antebellum" therefore means "before the war". In America specifically, the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_United_States_(1789%E2%80%931849)" title="History of the United States (1789–1849)">Antebellum era</a> commonly refers to the period before the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">Civil War</a>. While photographing the houses one of the group said that there's a great band name in there, and they adopted the Lady Antebellum name shortly after. The trio then began performing at local venues in Nashville before being signed in July 2007 to a recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a> Nashville. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Sirius_Satellite_Radio" title="Sirius Satellite Radio">Sirius Satellite Radio</a>, Scott said she was rejected from auditions for <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> twice, not even making it past the first round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cst1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-cst1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>Shortly after the trio signed to the label, recording artist <a href="/wiki/Jim_Brickman" title="Jim Brickman">Jim Brickman</a> chose Lady Antebellum to sing on his 2007 single "<a href="/wiki/Never_Alone_(Jim_Brickman_song)" title="Never Alone (Jim Brickman song)">Never Alone</a>," which reached No. 14 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adult_contemporary" title="Adult contemporary">adult contemporary</a> charts. In mid-2007, Lady Antebellum also wrote a song for the MTV reality television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hills_(TV_series)" title="The Hills (TV series)">The Hills</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Their solo debut single "<a href="/wiki/Love_Don%27t_Live_Here_(Lady_Antebellum_song)" title="Love Don't Live Here (Lady Antebellum song)">Love Don't Live Here</a>" was released in September 2007, with a music video for the song following in December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tv_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-tv-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> This song was the lead-off single to the band's <a href="/wiki/Lady_Antebellum_(album)" title="Lady Antebellum (album)">self-titled debut album</a>. Released on April 15, 2008, <i>Lady Antebellum</i> was produced by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Worley" title="Paul Worley">Paul Worley</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Shaw_(singer)" title="Victoria Shaw (singer)">Victoria Shaw</a>, a Nashville songwriter and former solo artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">[4]</a></sup> "Love Don't Live Here" reached No. 3 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> charts. The album itself was the first album by a new duo or group to debut at Number One on the <i>Billboard</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Country_Albums" title="Top Country Albums">Top Country Albums</a> charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>A second single, "<a href="/wiki/Lookin%27_for_a_Good_Time" title="Lookin' for a Good Time">Lookin' for a Good Time</a>", was released in June 2008 and peaked at No. 11 in December. In addition, Lady Antebellum was signed as an opening act on <a href="/wiki/Martina_McBride" title="Martina McBride">Martina McBride</a>'s Waking Up Laughing Tour in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lady Antebellum also contributed the song "I Was Here" to the <i><a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Team_USA_Soundtrack" title="AT&amp;T Team USA Soundtrack">AT&amp;T Team USA Soundtrack</a></i>, a song which peaked at No. 24 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bubbling_Under_Hot_100" title="Bubbling Under Hot 100">Bubbling Under Hot 100</a> based on downloads. In December 2008, their rendition of "<a href="/wiki/Baby,_It%27s_Cold_Outside" title="Baby, It's Cold Outside">Baby, It's Cold Outside</a>" peaked at No. 3 on the same chart. Their third official single, "<a href="/wiki/I_Run_to_You" title="I Run to You">I Run to You</a>", was released in January 2009. It eventually became the trio's first Number One in July 2009. On October 7, 2009, their debut album was certified platinum by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> for shipments of one million copies in the United States.</p>
<p>Haywood and Kelley co-wrote labelmate <a href="/wiki/Luke_Bryan" title="Luke Bryan">Luke Bryan</a>'s 2009 single "<a href="/wiki/Do_I" title="Do I">Do I</a>", on which Scott also sings backing vocals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> This song is the first single from Bryan's second studio album "Doin' My Thing", which was released on October 6, 2009.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9311:_Need_You_Now">2009–11: <i>Need You Now</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Antebellum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009–11: Need You Now">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2009, the group released their fourth single, "<a href="/wiki/Need_You_Now_(Lady_Antebellum_song)" title="Need You Now (Lady Antebellum song)">Need You Now</a>", the lead-off single and title track to their <a href="/wiki/Need_You_Now_(Lady_Antebellum_album)" title="Need You Now (Lady Antebellum album)">second studio album</a>, which was also produced by Worley. It debuted at No. 50 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart and became their second Number One hit on the charts for the week of November 28, 2009. The song also reached No. 2 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a> and topped the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Adult_Contemporary" title="Hot Adult Contemporary">Hot Adult Contemporary</a> chart, making it a crossover hit. A second single, "<a href="/wiki/American_Honey" title="American Honey">American Honey</a>", was released to radio on January 11, 2010 and would become their third No. 1 hit. The third single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Our_Kind_of_Love" title="Our Kind of Love">Our Kind of Love</a>", was released on May 31, 2010 and went No. 1 in September 2010. The song was notable for being written in collaboration with a non-Nashville songwriter in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Busbee_(songwriter)" title="Busbee (songwriter)">busbee</a>, who at that point had previously written for <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Timbaland" title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Katharine_McPhee" title="Katharine McPhee">Katharine McPhee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitquarters_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters-14">[14]</a></sup> Their fourth single from this album, "<a href="/wiki/Hello_World_(song)" title="Hello World (song)">Hello World</a>", was officially released on October 4, 2010.</p>
<p>The album was released on January 26, 2010 and debuted at No. 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Country_Albums" title="Top Country Albums">Top Country Albums</a> charts for the week of February 2, 2010, selling 480,922 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Four weeks after the album's release, it was certified platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAA_albums_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA_albums-16">[16]</a></sup> On April 28, 2010, they performed on the results show of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_9)" title="American Idol (season 9)">American Idol</a></i>, performing "Need You Now". On September 20, 2010, they launched their first headlining tour, "Need You Now 2010" in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a>. On October 28, 2010, they performed the National Anthem before Game 2 at the <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Series" title="2010 World Series">2010 World Series</a>. Lady Antebellum appeared on the 44th Annual Country Music Awards on November 10, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312:_Own_the_Night_and_On_This_Winter.27s_Night">2011–12: <i>Own the Night</i> and <i>On This Winter's Night</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Antebellum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011–12: Own the Night and On This Winter's Night">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The week of January 9, 2011, the group ventured into the studio to begin recording for their third studio album. In an interview with <i>Entertainment Weekly</i>, Charles Kelley said, "We actually just went ahead and decided we’re gonna take two, two and a half months straight in the studio to create this thing and not have all this distraction. Hopefully that will be a good thing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 2, 2011, the group released the first single from their upcoming album, titled "<a href="/wiki/Just_a_Kiss_(song)" title="Just a Kiss (song)">Just a Kiss</a>". The group performed the single on stage on <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s result show on May 5, 2011. It was a commercial and critical success, debuting and peaking at number 7 on the Billboard Hot 100, making it their highest debut on the chart. It also topped the Billboard Hot Country Songs, making it their fifth number-one single on the chart. On June 7, 2011, they announced the title of third album; called <i><a href="/wiki/Own_the_Night" title="Own the Night">Own the Night</a></i>, it was released on September 13, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The album cover and track listing was released on July 18, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> All together four singles were released from Own the Night. Follow up singles were "We Owned the Night", "Dancin' Away with My Heart" and "Wanted You More" which charted moderally in the Hot Country Songs.</p>
<p>Lady A released a "Lady Hazed" version of <a href="/wiki/Jason_Aldean" title="Jason Aldean">Jason Aldean</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/Dirt_Road_Anthem" title="Dirt Road Anthem">Dirt Road Anthem</a>" entitled "Country Club Anthem" on their installment of Webisode Wednesdays on August 10, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On October 1, 2011, the group performed as the musical guests on <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lady Antebellum released their first Christmas album <i><a href="/wiki/On_This_Winter%27s_Night" title="On This Winter's Night">On This Winter's Night</a></i> on October 22, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9315:_Golden.2C_747.2C_and_hiatus">2013–15: <i>Golden</i>, <i>747</i>, and hiatus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Antebellum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013–15: Golden, 747, and hiatus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 22, 2013, "<a href="/wiki/Downtown_(Lady_Antebellum_song)" title="Downtown (Lady Antebellum song)">Downtown</a>," the first single from a new album, was released to country radio. It was made available on iTunes starting February 5, 2013, and reached number one on the <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart in April 2013. The new album, <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_(Lady_Antebellum_album)" title="Golden (Lady Antebellum album)">Golden</a></i>, was released on May 7, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The album's second single, "<a href="/wiki/Goodbye_Town" title="Goodbye Town">Goodbye Town</a>", was released to country radio on May 13, 2013, and peaked at number 11 on the Country Airplay chart. The third single from the <i>Golden</i> era, "<a href="/wiki/Compass_(Lady_Antebellum_song)" title="Compass (Lady Antebellum song)">Compass</a>", was released to the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> on October 1, 2013 and reached number one on the Country Airplay chart in March 2014. It is one of the new recordings featured on the deluxe edition <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Re-issue" title="Re-issue">re-issue</a> of <i>Golden</i>, which was released on November 12, 2013. Unlike all of their previous releases, it was produced by <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Chapman_(record_producer)" title="Nathan Chapman (record producer)">Nathan Chapman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> They also sang backing vocals on <a href="/wiki/Darius_Rucker" title="Darius Rucker">Darius Rucker</a>'s 2013 single "<a href="/wiki/Wagon_Wheel_(song)" title="Wagon Wheel (song)">Wagon Wheel</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Conaway_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Conaway-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Bartender_(Lady_Antebellum_song)" title="Bartender (Lady Antebellum song)">Bartender</a>" was released to country radio on May 12, 2014, as the lead single for the group's sixth studio album, and was made available to digital retailers on May 19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In July 2014, the album's title was confirmed as <i><a href="/wiki/747_(album)" title="747 (album)">747</a></i>, and the track listing was confirmed. Chapman produced this album as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> "Bartender" became the group's ninth number 1 hit on the <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> charts in 2014, with "<a href="/wiki/Freestyle_(song)" title="Freestyle (song)">Freestyle</a>" as the second single. The third single "<a href="/wiki/Long_Stretch_of_Love" title="Long Stretch of Love">Long Stretch of Love</a> was released on February 7, 2015, in the UK and on March 23, to country radio in the US. In October 2015, while appearing on <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i>, the group announced that they would be taking some down time once they finish with their Wheels Up Tour. During their break Kelley will be working on his solo career. Kelley stated that Lady Antebellum is his priority but wanted to try working alone out. On September 28, 2015, he released his debut solo single titled "The Driver" which features <a href="/wiki/Dierks_Bentley" title="Dierks Bentley">Dierks Bentley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Paslay" title="Eric Paslay">Eric Paslay</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Solo_Project_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Solo_Project-29">[29]</a></sup> On October 12, 2015, Charles Kelley announced his tour dates for his solo tour. He is excited to get out of his comfort zone and having fans not know every word to all of his songs. He will be playing some Lady A songs, but his tour will showcase only his soon-to-be-released solo material.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Also during the break, Hillary Scott announced that she and her family (her <a href="/wiki/Linda_Davis" title="Linda Davis">mother</a>, father and sister) would be working a gospel album titled <i>Love Remains</i> which was released on 29th July.</p>
<p>In 2016, Lady Antebellum were selected as one of 30 artists to perform on "<i>Forever Country</i>", a mash-up track of <a href="/wiki/Take_Me_Home,_Country_Roads" title="Take Me Home, Country Roads">Take Me Home, Country Roads</a>, <a href="/wiki/On_the_Road_Again_(Willie_Nelson_song)" title="On the Road Again (Willie Nelson song)">On the Road Again</a> and <a href="/wiki/I_Will_Always_Love_You" title="I Will Always Love You">I Will Always Love You</a> which celebrates 50 years of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CMA_Awards" title="CMA Awards">CMA Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2017.E2.80.93present:_Heart_Break">2017–present: <i>Heart Break</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Antebellum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2017–present: Heart Break">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 19, 2017, they released "<a href="/wiki/You_Look_Good" title="You Look Good">You Look Good</a>" as the lead single off their upcoming seventh studio album <i>Heart Break</i>. It is set to be released on June 9, 2017, and will be further promoted with the You Look Good World Tour, which is set to begin on May 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lady_Antebellum_Back_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lady_Antebellum_Back-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity">Charity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lady_Antebellum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Charity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since January 2012, Lady Antebellum has been partnered with the charity myLIFEspeaks, to raise awareness of and provide facilities for disabled orphans in <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>. At the start of the year 2013, the members of the group also created the organization called <a href="/wiki/LadyAID" title="LadyAID">LadyAID</a>, which was created in hopes of bringing awareness to children who suffer and cannot help themselves locally, nationally and around the world. The main focus of Lady Antebellum are the children in hospitals, mainly in the United States and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_received_by_Lady_Antebellum" title="List of awards and nominations received by Lady Antebellum">List of awards and nominations received by Lady Antebellum</a></div>
<p>Since Lady Antebellum won the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a> New Artist of the Year award in 2008, they have accumulated seven more awards, including their first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy</a> in 2010 for Best Country Performance by Duo or Group with Vocals. At the 2009 CMA's, the group ended <a href="/wiki/Rascal_Flatts" title="Rascal Flatts">Rascal Flatts</a>' six-year reign as Vocal Group of the Year. At the 2010 CMA's, the group became the first artist in CMA Award history to receive the Single of the Year honor for two consecutive years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the 53rd Annual Grammy Awards, the trio won the Song of the Year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> They won Top Vocal Group at the 2012 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ACM_Awards" title="ACM Awards">ACM Awards</a> April 1, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
