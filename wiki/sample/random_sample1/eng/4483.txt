<p><a href="/wiki/Major_general_(United_States)" title="Major general (United States)">Major General</a> <b>Raymond Oscar "Tubby" Barton</b> (August 22, 1889 – February 27, 1963) was a career <a href="/wiki/Officer_(armed_forces)" title="Officer (armed forces)">officer</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> and combat commander in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. As commander of the <a href="/wiki/4th_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="4th Infantry Division (United States)">4th Infantry Division</a> during World War II, Barton is one of only eleven U.S. Army <a href="/wiki/General_officer" title="General officer">general officers</a> who commanded their divisions for the duration of their combat service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Order374_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Order374-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_early_career">Background and early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raymond_O._Barton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He graduated from the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Military_Academy" title="United States Military Academy">United States Military Academy</a> class of 1912. As commander of the 1st Battalion, <a href="/wiki/8th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="8th Infantry Regiment (United States)">8th Infantry Regiment</a> he served in Germany from 1917 to 1923, being the last formation to leave.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raymond_O._Barton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FirstGermany.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="475" data-file-width="684" height="174" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/FirstGermany.jpg/250px-FirstGermany.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/FirstGermany.jpg/375px-FirstGermany.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/FirstGermany.jpg/500px-FirstGermany.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FirstGermany.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Major General Raymond O. Barton and Colonel Buck Lanham, Germany, September 14, 1944</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>He commanded the <a href="/wiki/4th_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="4th Infantry Division (United States)">4th Infantry Division</a> from 3 July 1942 to 26 December 1944 and led them into battle from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D-Day" title="D-Day">D-Day</a> at <a href="/wiki/Utah_Beach" title="Utah Beach">Utah Beach</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> to the <a href="/wiki/Liberation_of_Paris" title="Liberation of Paris">Liberation of Paris</a>, and into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Hurtgen_Forest" title="Battle of the Hurtgen Forest">Battle of the Hurtgen Forest</a> before leaving the command due to health problems on December 27, 1944.</p>
<p>During the war he became friends with <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Hemingway" title="Ernest Hemingway">Ernest Hemingway</a> who sought his favor as the war correspondent assigned to the division and the two corresponded after.</p>
<p>Hemingway wrote to Barton:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">You had one of the greatest divisions in American military history.</td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Hurtgen_Forest" title="Battle of Hurtgen Forest">Battle of Hurtgen Forest</a> on the Weisser Weh stream near Grosshau, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> General Barton gave up his belt for tourniquet material to medic <a href="/wiki/Russell_J._York" title="Russell J. York">Russell J. York</a> of his division at York's request. Lives were saved, and a <a href="/wiki/Silver_Star" title="Silver Star">Silver Star</a> was personally awarded to Technician (Medical) 4th Grade York by General Barton for his actions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raymond_O._Barton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barton died in 1963 and was buried at Westover Memorial Park in <a href="/wiki/Augusta,_Georgia" title="Augusta, Georgia">Augusta, Georgia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Findagrave_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Findagrave-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_culture">Popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raymond_O._Barton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Longest_Day_(film)" title="The Longest Day (film)">The Longest Day</a></i> he is played by <a href="/wiki/Edmond_O%27Brien" title="Edmond O'Brien">Edmond O'Brien</a>. He appears in a scene where he allows his assistant division commander, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theodore_Roosevelt,_Jr." title="Theodore Roosevelt, Jr.">Theodore Roosevelt, Jr.</a> (played by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Fonda" title="Henry Fonda">Henry Fonda</a>), to lead the division ashore at D-Day.</p>
