<p><i><b>Labor Day</b></i> is a 2013 American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> based on the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Labor_Day_(novel)" title="Labor Day (novel)">novel of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Joyce_Maynard" title="Joyce Maynard">Joyce Maynard</a>. Directed by <a href="/wiki/Jason_Reitman" title="Jason Reitman">Jason Reitman</a>, the film stars <a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Brolin" title="Josh Brolin">Josh Brolin</a>. The film was co-produced by <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a> and Indian Paintbrush, premiering at the <a href="/wiki/Telluride_Film_Festival" title="Telluride Film Festival">Telluride Film Festival</a> on August 29, 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-la_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-la-1">[1]</a></sup> and was a <a href="/wiki/2013_Toronto_International_Film_Festival#Special_Presentations" title="2013 Toronto International Film Festival">Special Presentation</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2013 Toronto International Film Festival">2013 Toronto International Film Festival</a>. The film was released in the United States on January 31, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-release-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1987, Adele Wheeler is a depressed single mom who lives in a rural home with her 13-year-old son, Henry. While they are clothes shopping, a bloody man approaches Henry and makes them take him home to look after him. The man is revealed to be Frank Chambers, a convict who is wanted by the local police after breaking out of jail. Through flashbacks, it is revealed that Frank is a <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_veteran" title="Vietnam veteran">Vietnam veteran</a> who returned home and married his pregnant girlfriend, Mandy. A year after the baby's birth, Frank and Mandy had a fight, in which he asks if he's even the baby's father. During the fight, he pushed her against a radiator, resulting in her death. Simultaneously, through imagery, it is implied that the baby drowned. Frank was sent to jail for murder.</p>
<p>Adele tells Frank, seen through flashbacks, that she had a number of miscarriages after Henry, culminating with the full-term stillbirth of a baby girl. This has left Adele with severe social anxiety and depression, which Henry's father later explains to Henry as the reasons their marriage failed. Henry has tried to be both a son and a husband (innocently) but he realizes he can't provide all of the things Adele needs. Adele is a passionate woman who loves to dance and teaches both Henry and Frank separately to dance. Frank teaches Henry car repairs and other handyman/fatherly things. He also teaches Adele and Henry how to play baseball and how to bake a peach pie.</p>
<p>Adele and Frank fall in love and plan to escape to Canada with Henry, packing the car and cleaning out the house. Meanwhile, Henry develops a friendship with a mature, but manipulative girl named Eleanor, and goes to see her one more time before they leave. She manipulates him into thinking Adele and Frank are going to abandon him and he accidentally reveals Frank's past. Adele assures Henry she would never leave him. The morning they are going to leave, Henry takes a note to his father's house and leaves it in his mailbox. While he is walking home, a policeman offers to drive him home, and Henry has no choice but to accept. The policeman is suspicious of the packed car and nearly-empty house, but eventually leaves. Adele goes to the bank to get all the money out of her account, and the bankers too are suspicious. While Adele is gone, the neighbor comes over to give Adele some cinnamon rolls, and speaks to Frank. She, too, is suspicious of who he may be. Henry's father finds the note that Henry left, and calls the house wondering what is going on. Before Adele, Frank, and Henry can escape, they hear police sirens approaching. Frank ties Henry and Adele up before he goes out to surrender, so that they won't be charged with harboring a fugitive. It is not revealed who called the police to report Frank's presence at the house. Adele wants to plea in Frank's case but is warned by her attorney that if she does, Henry might be taken away from her. She writes letters to Frank but to protect her he returns them all unopened.</p>
<p>Years later, adult Henry has become the successful owner of a pie shop and is contacted by Frank, who has seen Henry and his shop in a magazine. He tells Henry he will be released soon and asks him whether or not he should see his mother again. Henry lets Frank know that his mother is still single and lives in the same house. We see Frank appearing to her and they embrace. Adele and Frank in the Autumn years of their life walk in love and Henry takes solace in the fact that he won't have to worry about her being alone.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2009, it was announced that Reitman was working on a screenplay, based on Joyce Maynard's novel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Talking about the story, Reitman said that "I read it, and I saw the movie in my head. It challenged me in a way that I liked. It was different from everything else I’ve read."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He also admitted that it was completely different from his previous work and said that "[it] deals with a very complex drama. And I may not nail it on this film, it may just be my first step."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-t_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-t-6">[6]</a></sup> Reitman wanted to make the film right after his 2009 film <i><a href="/wiki/Up_in_the_Air_(2009_film)" title="Up in the Air (2009 film)">Up in the Air</a></i>, but due to Winslet's scheduling conflicts, he chose to direct <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Adult_(film)" title="Young Adult (film)">Young Adult</a></i> first.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-v-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Reitman had Kate Winslet and Josh Brolin in mind for the lead roles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In June 2011, it was revealed that Winslet and Brolin had joined the cast of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On casting the actors, he said, "I know what actors I want for it. I'll be able to go to them easily"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-t_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-t-6">[6]</a></sup> and that "[Winslet] makes those characters beautiful and sexual. I don’t know another actor who does that. I don’t know what I would have done if she’d said no."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-v-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2012, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/James_Van_Der_Beek" title="James Van Der Beek">James Van Der Beek</a> has joined the cast of the film as a Police Officer and <a href="/wiki/Gattlin_Griffith" title="Gattlin Griffith">Gattlin Griffith</a> as young Henry Wheeler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In June 2012, it was confirmed that <a href="/wiki/Alexie_Gilmore" title="Alexie Gilmore">Alexie Gilmore</a>, Brighid Fleming, Lucas Hedges and Micah Fowler had joined the cast of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Later <a href="/wiki/Tobey_Maguire" title="Tobey Maguire">Tobey Maguire</a> rounded out the cast and joined the film as adult Henry Wheeler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-production">Pre-production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Pre-production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Production began for the film on June 5, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Reitman and the film's art director Steve Saklad searched a number of houses in <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> as most of the film is set inside the Wheelers' home. According to Reitman, "We searched the entire state of Massachusetts for that house. My location manager has never looked at that many locations looking for one place. For weeks we would just drive down the street, knocking on people’s doors. The house we found was perfect but it was very modern. Steve brought it back to 1987."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-v-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Principal photography for the film began on June 13, 2012, in Massachusetts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The filming locations included <a href="/wiki/Belchertown,_Massachusetts" title="Belchertown, Massachusetts">Belchertown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shelburne_Falls,_Massachusetts" title="Shelburne Falls, Massachusetts">Shelburne Falls</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sutton,_Massachusetts" title="Sutton, Massachusetts">Sutton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mansfield,_Massachusetts" title="Mansfield, Massachusetts">Mansfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natick,_Massachusetts" title="Natick, Massachusetts">Natick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medfield,_Massachusetts" title="Medfield, Massachusetts">Medfield</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Medway,_Massachusetts" title="Medway, Massachusetts">Medway, Massachusetts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On 25 January, filming moved to <a href="/wiki/Acton,_Massachusetts" title="Acton, Massachusetts">Acton</a> and scenes shot around Piper Road and at a house located in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The movie was also filmed at <a href="/wiki/Canobie_Lake_Park" title="Canobie Lake Park">Canobie Lake Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Salem,_New_Hampshire" title="Salem, New Hampshire">Salem, New Hampshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The filming finished on 17 August 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first image of Winslet, Brolin, and Griffith was released on July 23, 2013, along with the announcement of film having its world premiere at <a href="/wiki/2013_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2013 Toronto International Film Festival">2013 Toronto International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> After its premiere at TIFF, the official poster for the film was revealed on 20 September 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The first official trailer of the film was released on October 31, 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> followed by a second trailer released in November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Paramount partnered with the <a href="/wiki/American_Pie_Council" title="American Pie Council">American Pie Council</a> (APC) in promoting the film,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-variety-30">[30]</a></sup> and the APC produced materials promoting both the film and National Pie Day (January 23, eight days before the film's general American release).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<p>The soundtrack was composed by <a href="/wiki/Rolfe_Kent" title="Rolfe Kent">Rolfe Kent</a> who previously composed music for Reitman's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thank_You_for_Smoking_(film)" title="Thank You for Smoking (film)">Thank You for Smoking</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Up_in_the_Air_(2009_film)" title="Up in the Air (2009 film)">Up in the Air</a></i> (2009) and <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Adult_(film)" title="Young Adult (film)">Young Adult</a></i> (2011). The album features <i>I'm Going Home</i> from <a href="/wiki/Arlo_Guthrie" title="Arlo Guthrie">Arlo Guthrie</a> and <i>Here Before</i> from <a href="/wiki/Vashti_Bunyan" title="Vashti Bunyan">Vashti Bunyan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> It also contains guitar pieces by <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Segovia" title="Andrés Segovia">Andrés Segovia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shin-Ichi_Fukuda" title="Shin-Ichi Fukuda">Shin-Ichi Fukuda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Talking about the music, Kent said that "You know it’s simple to compose happy or sad music, but to create something simple yet sophisticated, that calls the listener to be curious and yet uncertain and perhaps a little unnerved, well it called me to forget everything I knew about composition and discover a whole new musical language. It was at once incredibly stressful, and deeply rewarding."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Film Music Magazine's</i> Daniel Schweiger praised the soundtrack as "most impactful insights to the human condition, while completely surprising with its cinematic, and musical authorships."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Kaya Savas of <i>Film Music Media</i> gave the album four and a half star out of five and said that "There is beauty, sadness and uneasiness all tackled with a wonderfully calculated approach."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-s_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-s-39">[39]</a></sup></p>

<p>The film had a limited release on December 27, 2013, for a one-week awards-qualifying run and had a wide release on January 31, 2014, in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> + <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a> in US on April 29, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Bonus features include deleted scenes, a "End of Summer: making-of" Labor Day segment, and commentary featuring Reitman, cinematographer Steelberg, and <a href="/wiki/Assistant_director" title="Assistant director">first assistant director</a>/co-producer Jason Blumenfeld.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film was opened wide along with <i><a href="/wiki/That_Awkward_Moment" title="That Awkward Moment">That Awkward Moment</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl" title="Super Bowl">Super Bowl weekend</a>. It grossed an estimated $5.3 million in its first three days and ranked seventh on its opening weekend, in domestic box office rankings by <a href="/wiki/Rentrak" title="Rentrak">Rentrak</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> The film grossed $13.4 million in the U.S. and $5.9 million in the rest of the world, resulting in a worldwide gross of $19.3 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mojo_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Mojo-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Labor_Day_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to aggregate review site <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> the film holds a 34% approval rating based on 184 reviews, with a rating average of 5.2 out of 10. The consensus states: "Kate Winslet and Josh Brolin make for an undeniably compelling pair, but they can't quite rescue <i>Labor Day</i> from the pallid melodrama of its exceedingly ill-advised plot."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a weighted mean rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the film holds an average score of 52, based on 43 reviews, which indicates "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Todd_McCarthy" title="Todd McCarthy">Todd McCarthy</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i>, in his review said that "the film emits frequent pangs of emotion and tension, which enable it to prevail over threats from the cliches and inevitabilities of the story's format. There is more than one instance when events will cause many viewers' hearts to leap, as they say, into their throats, and the wrap-up is quietly satisfying."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Peter Debruge of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> stated that <i>Labor Day</i> brims with such carefully observed details, all of them a little too elegant to feel entirely genuine, and yet impossible to fault" and that Winslet "communicates Adele's fragility in a matter of a few short scenes."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lou_Lumenick" title="Lou Lumenick">Lou Lumenick</a> in his review for the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i>, compared the film with <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_Perfect_World" title="A Perfect World">A Perfect World</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
