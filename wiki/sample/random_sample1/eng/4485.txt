<p><b>Leeuwarden</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈleːʋ.ɑrdə(n)]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:453_Leeuwarden.ogg" title="File:453 Leeuwarden.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f8/453_Leeuwarden.ogg" title="453 Leeuwarden.ogg">listen</a>)</small>, <a href="/wiki/West_Frisian_language" title="West Frisian language">Frisian</a>: <i>Ljouwert</i> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_West_Frisian" title="Help:IPA for West Frisian">[ˈʎɔːw(ə)t]</a></span>, <a href="/wiki/Stadsfries_dialects" title="Stadsfries dialects">Stadsfries</a>: <i>Liwwadden</i>) is a city and municipality with a population of 108,249 in <a href="/wiki/Friesland" title="Friesland">Friesland</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. It is the provincial <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a> and seat of the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Friesland" title="States of Friesland">States of Friesland</a>.</p>
<p>The oldest remains of houses in the city date back to the 2nd century AD. Leeuwarden has been <span class="cleanup-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">permanently inhabited</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="Needs a hint on what happened to habitation between C2 and C10 (October 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> since the 10th century. It was granted <a href="/wiki/Town_privileges" title="Town privileges">city privileges</a> in 1435. The city was liberated from <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_in_World_War_II#German_occupation" title="Netherlands in World War II">German occupation in World War II</a> by <a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Canadian_Dragoons" title="The Royal Canadian Dragoons">The Royal Canadian Dragoons</a> in 1945. It is the economic hub of the province of Friesland, situated in a green and water-rich environment, with lakes, villages and recreational areas. Leeuwarden is a former royal residence and has a historic centre, many listed buildings, and a large shopping centre with squares and restaurants. Leeuwarden has been awarded the title European Capital of Culture 2018.</p>
<p>One important cultural and historical event is the <a href="/wiki/Elfstedentocht" title="Elfstedentocht">Elfstedentocht</a> (Eleven Cities Tour), an ice skating-tour of eleven cities in Friesland, starting and finishing in Leeuwarden.</p>
<p>Besides the city of Leeuwarden, population centres in the municipality with a population of 1,000 or more are <a href="/wiki/Grou" title="Grou">Grou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goutum" title="Goutum">Goutum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wergea" title="Wergea">Wergea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jirnsum" title="Jirnsum">Jirnsum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reduzum" title="Reduzum">Reduzum</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wirdum,_Friesland" title="Wirdum, Friesland">Wirdum</a>. The municipality is governed by the mayor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ferd_Crone&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ferd Crone (page does not exist)">Ferd Crone</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferd_Crone" title="nl:Ferd Crone">nl</a>)</span> and a coalition of the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Netherlands)" title="Labour Party (Netherlands)">Labour Party</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Appeal" title="Christian Democratic Appeal">Christian Democratic Appeal</a>, and PAL-<a href="/wiki/GroenLinks" title="GroenLinks">GroenLinks</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name "Leeuwarden" (or older spelling variants) first came into use for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nijehove&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nijehove (page does not exist)">Nijehove</a>, the most important of the three villages that later merged into one, namely <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oldehove,_Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Oldehove, Leeuwarden (page does not exist)">Oldehove</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hoek,_Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hoek, Leeuwarden (page does not exist)">Hoek</a> in the early 9th century (Villa Lintarwrde c. 825).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> There is much uncertainty about the origin of the city's name. Historian and archivist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wopke_Eekhoff&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wopke Eekhoff (page does not exist)">Wopke Eekhoff</a> summed up a total of over 200 different spelling variants, of which <i>Leeuwarden</i> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>), <i>Liwwadden</i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stadsfries" title="Stadsfries">Stadsfries</a>), and <i>Ljouwert</i> (<a href="/wiki/West_Frisian_language" title="West Frisian language">West Frisian</a>) are still in use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Groot_p._12_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Groot_p._12-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second syllable is easily explained: <i>Warden</i>, Frisian/Dutch for an artificial dwelling hill, is a designation of <a href="/wiki/Terp" title="Terp">terps</a>, reflecting the historical situation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Groot_p._12_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Groot_p._12-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first part of the name, <i>leeuw</i>, means <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">lion</a> in modern standard Dutch. This interpretation corresponds with the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> adopted by the city, which features a <a href="/wiki/Lion_(heraldry)" title="Lion (heraldry)">heraldic lion</a>. However, modern standard Dutch was not used in this region in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, when the city was called <i>Lintarwrde</i>. Some scholars argue that the name of the city is derived from <i>leeu-</i>, a corruption of <i>luw-</i> (Dutch for sheltered from the wind, cf. the <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">maritime</a> term <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeward" title="Leeward">leeward</a>) or from <i>lee-</i> (a Dutch word for waterway). The last one suits the watery province of <a href="/wiki/Friesland" title="Friesland">Friesland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Groot_p._12_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Groot_p._12-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The name is similar to that of the French commune <a href="/wiki/Lewarde" title="Lewarde">Lewarde</a>, located in the <a href="/wiki/Nord_(French_department)" title="Nord (French department)">Nord Department</a>, an originally Flemish-speaking area annexed to France in the 17th Century.</p>

<p>The oldest remains of houses date back to the 2nd century AD in the Roman era and were discovered during an excavation near the <a href="/wiki/Oldehove_(tower)" title="Oldehove (tower)">Oldehove</a>.</p>
<p>The area has been <span class="cleanup-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">permanently inhabited</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="As in the lead, seems an unexplained jump between C2 and C10 (October 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> since the 10th century, and was mentioned as a city in German sources in 1285; <a href="/wiki/Town_privileges" title="Town privileges">city privileges</a> were granted in 1435. Situated along the <a href="/wiki/Middelzee" title="Middelzee">Middelzee</a>, it was an active trade centre until the waterway silted up in the 15th century, making it harder to trade.</p>

<p>In 1901 the city had a population of 32,203. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, after extensive occupation by the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> forces, on 15 April 1945, <a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Canadian_Dragoons" title="The Royal Canadian Dragoons">The Royal Canadian Dragoons</a>, disobeying direct orders, charged into the heavily defended city and defeated the Germans, who were driven out by the next day. The Royal Canadian Dragoons still fly the flag of the city of Leeuwarden wherever they are stationed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On Saturday 19 October 2013, a fire broke out in a clothes shop on a busy pedestrian street. The fire started late in the afternoon and destroyed over 15 shops and flats. Everyone on the street was evacuated as the blaze damaged dozens of properties. A 24-year-old man who was living in one of the flats died because the fire was not under control until Sunday morning. Due to this the fire burnt all through the night. The man called the fire brigade, before collapsing because the services could not reach him. The birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Mata_Hari" title="Mata Hari">Mata Hari</a> was destroyed. The firemen took 4 and a half hours to put out the fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Leeuwarden has since renovated the street and shops in this area.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heraldry">Heraldry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Heraldry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> of Leeuwarden is the official symbol of the municipality of Leeuwarden. It consists of a <a href="/wiki/Azure_(heraldry)" title="Azure (heraldry)">blue</a> <a href="/wiki/Escutcheon_(heraldry)" title="Escutcheon (heraldry)">escutcheon</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Or_(heraldry)" title="Or (heraldry)">golden</a> <a href="/wiki/Lion_(heraldry)" title="Lion (heraldry)">lion</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Crown_(headgear)" title="Crown (headgear)">crown</a>. The fact Leeuwarden carries a lion in its seal seems logical, considering that "Leeuw" is Dutch for "Lion". However, it is very plausible the oldest name of the city conceals an indication of water rather than an animal. Some sources tell the lion had been called into life after the name became official. It is also possible the coat of arms was a gift to the city from the powerful <i>Minnema</i> family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>Leeuwarden is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Leeuwarden&amp;params=3_12_N_5_47_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">3°12′N</span> <span class="longitude">5°47′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">3.200°N 5.783°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">3.200; 5.783</span></span></span></a></span> in the centre of the province of <a href="/wiki/Friesland" title="Friesland">Friesland</a> in the north of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Leeuwarden_-_Voorstreek_met_Bonifatiustoren.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2032" data-file-width="1524" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Leeuwarden_-_Voorstreek_met_Bonifatiustoren.jpg/170px-Leeuwarden_-_Voorstreek_met_Bonifatiustoren.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Leeuwarden_-_Voorstreek_met_Bonifatiustoren.jpg/255px-Leeuwarden_-_Voorstreek_met_Bonifatiustoren.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Leeuwarden_-_Voorstreek_met_Bonifatiustoren.jpg/340px-Leeuwarden_-_Voorstreek_met_Bonifatiustoren.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Leeuwarden_-_Voorstreek_met_Bonifatiustoren.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Leeuwarden's historic centre with the tower of the church of St. Bonifatius</div>

<p>Well-known buildings in the city centre include the <i>Kanselarij</i> (the former chancellery), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stadhouderlijk_hof" title="Stadhouderlijk hof">Stadhouderlijk hof</a>, former residence of the stadtholders of Friesland, the <i>Waag</i> (old trade centre of the city), the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Boniface_church,_Leeuwarden" title="Saint Boniface church, Leeuwarden">Saint Boniface church</a> and the leaning tower <i><a href="/wiki/Oldehove_(tower)" title="Oldehove (tower)">Oldehove</a></i>. The tallest building in the city is the 115-metre Achmeatoren (<a href="/wiki/Achmea" title="Achmea">Achmea</a> insurance tower).</p>
<p>Leeuwarden is also the site of the country's largest cattle market, and on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ascension_Day" title="Ascension Day">Ascension Day</a>, the largest flower market in the Netherlands is held here.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Froskep%C3%B4lemolen,_Leeuwarden" title="Froskepôlemolen, Leeuwarden">Froskepôlemolen</a></i> is the last surviving windmill of over 130 years old known to have <a href="/wiki/Windmills_in_Leeuwarden" title="Windmills in Leeuwarden">stood in Leeuwarden</a>. The remains of the <i><a href="/wiki/Cammingha-Buurstermolen,_Leeuwarden" title="Cammingha-Buurstermolen, Leeuwarden">Cammingha-Buurstermolen</a></i> were demolished in 2000. The bases of two other windmills, <i>Wielinga-Stam</i> and <i>De Haan</i> also survived.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Friese_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Friese-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Slauerhoffbrug" title="Slauerhoffbrug">Slauerhoffbrug</a> is a fully automatic <a href="/wiki/Bascule_bridge" title="Bascule bridge">bascule bridge</a> named after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jan_Jacob_Slauerhoff" title="Jan Jacob Slauerhoff">Jan Jacob Slauerhoff</a>. It uses two arms to swing a section of road in and out of place within the road itself. This movable bridge is also known as the ‘Flying’ Drawbridge. One of the main designers is Emile Asari.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Monuments">Monuments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Monuments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Leeuwarden has 617 national monuments and is therefore in the top 15 of Dutch monument municipalities. There are several protected areas within the city centre, such as the canals, the new canal area and the 'Hollanderwijk', which is an old neighbourhood of Leeuwarden. Next to the 617 national monuments in Leeuwarden, there are also 375 iconic buildings.</p>
<p>Among these monuments are houses, churches, windmills, old warehouses, shops and offices but also parks, statues and the canals. The oldest monuments date back to the 13th century, including the 'Jacobijnerkerk', which is a monastery church. After the 13th century a lot of important buildings were built in Leeuwarden, hence the large number of national monuments. At the moment, there are still protected areas in and around Leeuwarden which are expected to have a high archaeological value.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Finish_11-stedentocht_1956.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="9323" data-file-width="6970" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Finish_11-stedentocht_1956.jpg/170px-Finish_11-stedentocht_1956.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Finish_11-stedentocht_1956.jpg/255px-Finish_11-stedentocht_1956.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Finish_11-stedentocht_1956.jpg/340px-Finish_11-stedentocht_1956.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Finish_11-stedentocht_1956.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>Leeuwarden is the starting and finishing point for the celebrated <a href="/wiki/Elfstedentocht" title="Elfstedentocht">Elfstedentocht</a>, a 200 km-long <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating" title="Speed skating">speed skating</a> race over the Frisian waterways that is held when winter conditions in the province allow. As of 2015, it last took place in January 1997, preceded by the races of 1986 and 1985. In 1986 the Dutch king Willem-Alexander participated in the Eleven cities tour, with the pseudonym <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/W.A._van_Buren" title="W.A. van Buren">W.A. van Buren</a>, which is the pseudonym of the royal family of The Netherlands. The city's local football team, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cambuur_Leeuwarden" title="Cambuur Leeuwarden">Cambuur Leeuwarden</a> plays in the <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Jupiler_League" title="Jupiler League">Jupiler League</a>. In the season 2005/06, the club narrowly escaped bankruptcy. Its <i>Cambuurstadion</i> opened in 1995. The football team has proposed plans for a new stadium in the east side of the city, which will cost €35 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The city's basketball team, <a href="/wiki/Aris_Leeuwarden" title="Aris Leeuwarden">Aris Leeuwarden</a> plays in the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Basketball_League" title="Dutch Basketball League">Dutch Basketball League</a> since 2004.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Frisian_Solar_Challenge" title="Frisian Solar Challenge">Frisian Solar Challenge</a> is a solar boat race that starts and finishes in Leeuwarden. The race starts in Leeuwarden, the capital of Friesland, and follows the classic route of the Eleven City Tour. Following the canals, rivers, and lakes, with the occasional portage, the race features 40 teams from eight countries, including Belgium, Germany, Great Britain, Luxembourg, the Netherlands, Poland, Sweden, and the one long-distance entry, a team from the Federal University of Rio in Brazil.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_capital">Cultural capital</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Cultural capital">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 6, 2013 Leeuwarden was voted <a href="/wiki/European_Capital_of_Culture" title="European Capital of Culture">European Capital of Culture</a> for the year 2018. This was done by a jury of international members, and they all voted for Leeuwarden unanimously. The title of Lwd2018 is 'Iepen Mienskip'. 'Iepen' is the Frisian word for ‘open’, whereas 'Mienskip' represents the sense of community within Leeuwarden. Lwd2018 connects different kinds of communities with each other. This is why the slogan of Lwd2018 is Criss-Crossing Communities.</p>
<p>Since the winning of the title Cultural Capital the city has been able to show its ambitions to a bigger audience. Leeuwarden has gained an energy boost from the nomination and is working together with a lot of companies in order to get ready for the big event. More information about Leeuwarden Cultural Capital 2018 and all the events, activities and news can be found on www.2018.nl.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In the Netherlands, a municipality is governed by the <a href="/wiki/College_van_burgemeester_en_wethouders" title="College van burgemeester en wethouders">college of mayor and aldermen</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council_(Netherlands)" title="Municipal council (Netherlands)">municipal council</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ferd_Crone&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ferd Crone (page does not exist)">Ferd Crone</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Netherlands)" title="Labour Party (Netherlands)">Labour Party</a> has been <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Leeuwarden" title="Mayor of Leeuwarden">mayor of Leeuwarden</a> since 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mayor_now_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mayor_now-1">[1]</a></sup> Since the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_municipal_elections,_2014" title="Dutch municipal elections, 2014">2014 municipal elections</a>, the Labour Party (3 aldermen), <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_Appeal" title="Christian Democratic Appeal">Christian Democratic Appeal</a> (2 aldermen), PAL <a href="/wiki/GroenLinks" title="GroenLinks">GroenLinks</a> (1 alderman) form a coalition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council_(Netherlands)" title="Municipal council (Netherlands)">municipal council</a> of Leeuwarden has 39 seats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>As provincial capital, Leeuwarden is also the seat of the <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Commissioner" title="King's Commissioner">King's Commissioner</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Jorritsma" title="John Jorritsma">John Jorritsma</a> and the <a href="/wiki/States_of_Friesland" title="States of Friesland">States of Friesland</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sister_cities">Sister cities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Sister cities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Oryol" title="Oryol">Oryol</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Oryol_Oblast" title="Oryol Oblast">Oryol Oblast</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> is a sister city of Leeuwarden. <b>Oryol</b> or <b>Orel</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Орёл</span>; <small>IPA: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Russian" title="Help:IPA for Russian">[ɐˈrʲɵl]</a></span>, lit. <i>eagle</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">city</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_centre" title="Administrative centre">administrative centre</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oryol_Oblast" title="Oryol Oblast">Oryol Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the <a href="/wiki/Oka_River" title="Oka River">Oka River</a>, approximately 360 kilometers (220 mi) south-southwest of <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Leeuwarden_railway_station" title="Leeuwarden railway station">Leeuwarden railway station</a> is the main railway station of Leeuwarden. The station was finished on 27 October 1863. The first railway went from Leeuwarden to Harlingen. Nowadays, the station is a lot bigger and has facilities such as a supermarket, hairdresser, drugstore, bookstore and a flower shop. There are local, regional, and national trains connecting the city to the surrounding area, the neighboring provinces of <a href="/wiki/Groningen_(province)" title="Groningen (province)">Groningen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drenthe" title="Drenthe">Drenthe</a>, and the rest of the Netherlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The other stations in the municipality are <a href="/wiki/Leeuwarden_Achter_de_Hoven_railway_station" title="Leeuwarden Achter de Hoven railway station">Leeuwarden Achter de Hoven</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeuwarden_Camminghaburen_railway_station" title="Leeuwarden Camminghaburen railway station">Leeuwarden Camminghaburen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Grou-Jirnsum_railway_station" title="Grou-Jirnsum railway station">Grou-Jirnsum</a>. A fifth station <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeuwarden_Werpsterhoek_railway_station" title="Leeuwarden Werpsterhoek railway station">Leeuwarden Werpsterhoek</a> is planned to be opened in 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Leeuwarden, there are 40 local, regional, and national bus services provided by <a href="/wiki/Arriva" title="Arriva">Arriva</a> with destinations in the city, to other towns in Friesland, and to <a href="/wiki/Alkmaar" title="Alkmaar">Alkmaar</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Holland" title="North Holland">North Holland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> There are also six regional bus services provided by <a href="/wiki/Qbuzz" title="Qbuzz">Qbuzz</a> eastward to <a href="/wiki/Oosterwolde,_Friesland" title="Oosterwolde, Friesland">Oosterwolde</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drachten" title="Drachten">Drachten</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeuwarden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stedelijk_Gymnasium_Leeuwarden.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Stedelijk_Gymnasium_Leeuwarden.jpg/220px-Stedelijk_Gymnasium_Leeuwarden.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Stedelijk_Gymnasium_Leeuwarden.jpg/330px-Stedelijk_Gymnasium_Leeuwarden.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Stedelijk_Gymnasium_Leeuwarden.jpg/440px-Stedelijk_Gymnasium_Leeuwarden.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Leeuwarden has a number of respected <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hogeschool" title="Hogeschool">universities of applied science</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hoger_beroepsonderwijs" title="Hoger beroepsonderwijs">HBO</a> in Dutch), such as the <a href="/wiki/Van_Hall_Instituut" title="Van Hall Instituut">Van Hall Instituut</a> (agricultural and life sciences), the <a href="/wiki/Stenden_University_of_Applied_Sciences" title="Stenden University of Applied Sciences">Stenden University of Applied Sciences</a> (hotel management, economical and media management)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=NHL_Hogeschool&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="NHL Hogeschool (page does not exist)">NHL Hogeschool</a> (economical, technical and arts).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Besides higher education, the city is also home to three regional vocational schools (<i>MBO</i>): the Friese Poort,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Friesland College,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and Nordwin College.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although the city has no scientific university, several dependencies are located here, including those of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wageningen_University" title="Wageningen University">Wageningen University</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universiteit_Twente" title="Universiteit Twente">Universiteit Twente</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rijksuniversiteit_Groningen" title="Rijksuniversiteit Groningen">Rijksuniversiteit Groningen</a>. About 16,000 students, among them an increasing number of foreign students, study at technical schools. Leeuwarden is also presenting itself more and more as a City of Science. The city invests in science, professional universities and in future development and research. Leeuwarden is proud of its collaborations with scientific institutes such as Wetsus (Water technology), Centre of Expertise Water technology (CEW), the Dairy Campus, the Wadden Academy and the University Campus Fryslan (UCF). On the science campus of Leeuwarden, three professional universities collaborate with students and businesses in projects where theoretical knowledge is linked to business practice.</p>
