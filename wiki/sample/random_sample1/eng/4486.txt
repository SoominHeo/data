<p><b>Riwoqê County</b>, or <b>Riwoche</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tibetan_script" title="Tibetan script">Tibetan</a>: རི་བོ་ཆེ་རྫོང་, <a href="/wiki/Wylie_transliteration" title="Wylie transliteration">Wylie</a> ri bo che rdzong; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_character" title="Chinese character">Chinese</a>: 类乌齐县; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">Pinyin</a>: Lèiwūqí Xiàn) is a county of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamdo_Prefecture" title="Chamdo Prefecture">Chamdo Prefecture</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region" title="Tibet Autonomous Region">Tibet Autonomous Region</a>.</p>
<p>Located in the former province of <a href="/wiki/Kham" title="Kham">Kham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riwoche_Monastery" title="Riwoche Monastery">Riwoche Monastery</a> located here became the seat of the Taklung Kagyu, one of the four chief schools of the <a href="/wiki/Kagyu" title="Kagyu">Kagyu</a> sect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
