<p>The <b>Leizhou Peninsula</b>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postal_romanization" title="Postal romanization">formerly romanized</a> as the Luichow Peninsula, is a <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> in the southernmost part of <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> Province in southern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leizhou_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Qing naval forces were stationed at the Leizhou Peninsula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dennys1874_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dennys1874-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leizhou_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Leizhou Peninsula is the third largest peninsula in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> with an area of c. 8,500 square kilometers (3,300 sq mi) located on the southwestern end of Guangdong, with the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Tonkin" title="Gulf of Tonkin">Gulf of Tonkin</a> to the west and the 30 km wide <a href="/wiki/Qiongzhou_Strait" title="Qiongzhou Strait">Qiongzhou Strait</a> to the south, separating the peninsula from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hainan_Island" title="Hainan Island">Hainan Island</a>. It is the most southerly point of continental China.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>Geologically, <a href="/wiki/Basalt" title="Basalt">basalt</a> <a href="/wiki/Terrace_(agriculture)" title="Terrace (agriculture)">terraces</a> account for 43% of the peninsula's area. The rest is divided up between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_terrace" title="Marine terrace">marine terraces</a> (27%) and <a href="/wiki/Alluvial_plain" title="Alluvial plain">alluvial plains</a> (17%). Leizhou Peninsula is dotted with a few dormant <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcanoes</a>, beaches, and low-lying <a href="/wiki/Diluvium" title="Diluvium">diluvial plains</a>.</p>
<p>Leizhou has two separate <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_field" title="Volcanic field">volcanic fields</a>: a <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a>–<a href="/wiki/Holocene" title="Holocene">Holocene</a> field at the northern end of the peninsula west of <a href="/wiki/Zhanjiang" title="Zhanjiang">Zhanjiang</a> <span class="nowrap">(<span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%9B%B7" title="wikt:雷">雷</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%8C%97" title="wikt:北">北</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%81%AB%E5%B1%B1" title="wikt:火山">火山</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%BE%A4" title="wikt:群">群</a></span>,</span> <i>Leibei Huoshanqun</i>) and the northern end of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Qionglei&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Qionglei (page does not exist)">Qionglei</a> or Leiqiong volcanic field, which extends across the strait into northern Hainan <span class="nowrap">(<span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%9B%B7" title="wikt:雷">雷</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%8D%97" title="wikt:南">南</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%81%AB%E5%B1%B1" title="wikt:火山">火山</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%BE%A4" title="wikt:群">群</a></span>,</span> <i>Leinan Huoshanqun</i>). The volcanoes derive from the east-to-west tectonic extension and thinning of the <a href="/wiki/Lithosphere" title="Lithosphere">lithosphere</a> connected with the creation of the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a>'s basin. Two Pleistocene-era <a href="/wiki/Basalt" title="Basalt">basaltic</a> <a href="/wiki/Stratovolcano" title="Stratovolcano">stratovolcanoes</a> are <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yingfengling&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yingfengling (page does not exist)">Yingfengling</a> <span class="nowrap">(<span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%B9%B0%E5%B3%B0%E5%B2%AD" title="wikt:鹰峰岭">鹰峰岭</a></span>)</span> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tianyang_(volcano)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tianyang (volcano) (page does not exist)">Tianyang</a> <span class="nowrap">(<span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%94%B0%E6%B4%8B" title="wikt:田洋">田洋</a></span>),</span> 15 km (9.3 mi) apart in the center of the peninsula.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> There is also a third volcanic field responsible for some of the islands offshore <span class="nowrap">(<span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%81%AB%E5%B1%B1" title="wikt:火山">火山</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%B2%A9" title="wikt:岩">岩</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%B2%9B%E5%B1%BF" title="wikt:岛屿">岛屿</a></span>,</span> <i>Huoshanyan Daoyu</i>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wildlife">Wildlife</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leizhou_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wildlife">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Critically endangered whales such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Pacific_Right_Whale" title="North Pacific Right Whale">North Pacific Right Whales</a> and western <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gray_Whale" title="Gray Whale">Gray Whales</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Humpback_Whale" title="Humpback Whale">Humpback Whales</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_Whale" title="Blue Whale">Blue Whales</a> were once known to occur around the peninsula<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in the winter and spring to calve. Waters such as Wailuo Harbor were ideal habitats for these giants. These whales were heavily hunted and were wiped out by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_whaling" title="Japanese whaling">Japanese whalers</a> in this regions. (Japanese whalers established whaling stations at various sites along the Chinese and Korean coasts including on the island of Hainan and at <a href="/wiki/Daya_Bay" title="Daya Bay">Daya Bay</a>). Possibly, some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bryde%27s_Whale" title="Bryde's Whale">Bryde's Whales</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minke_Whale" title="Minke Whale">Minke Whales</a> still occur in the adjacent waters including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hainan_Island" title="Hainan Island">Hainan Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Tonkin" title="Gulf of Tonkin">Gulf of Tonkin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Around the peninsula has declared to be parts of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_white_dolphin" title="Chinese white dolphin">Chinese white dolphin</a> sanctuary. <a href="/wiki/Dugong" title="Dugong">Dugongs</a> still occur in small number.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leizhou_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The peninsula lies in tropical South China. The region is under the influence of continental northeastern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monsoons" title="Monsoons">monsoons</a> and maritime southeastern and southwestern monsoons. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Typhoons" title="Typhoons">Typhoons</a> occasionally occur, both from the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> and the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a>. Annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> is 1400–1700mm.</p>
