<p><b>Rachel Anne Griffiths</b> (born 18 December 1968) is an Australian actress. She came to prominence with the 1994 film <i><a href="/wiki/Muriel%27s_Wedding" title="Muriel's Wedding">Muriel's Wedding</a></i> and her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> nominated performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Hilary_and_Jackie" title="Hilary and Jackie">Hilary and Jackie</a></i> (1998). She portrayed masseuse <a href="/wiki/Brenda_Chenowith" title="Brenda Chenowith">Brenda Chenowith</a> in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Six_Feet_Under_(TV_series)" title="Six Feet Under (TV series)">Six Feet Under</a></i> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarah_Whedon" title="Sarah Whedon">Sarah Walker Laurent</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Brothers_%26_Sisters_(2006_TV_series)" title="Brothers &amp; Sisters (2006 TV series)">Brothers &amp; Sisters</a></i>. Griffiths has received a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a>, two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Awards" title="Screen Actors Guild Awards">Screen Actors Guild Awards</a> and three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Film_Institute_Awards" title="Australian Film Institute Awards">Australian Film Institute Awards</a> for her work.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rachel_Griffiths&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Griffiths was born in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, but spent her early childhood on the <a href="/wiki/Gold_Coast,_Queensland" title="Gold Coast, Queensland">Gold Coast</a>. She is the daughter of Anna, an art teacher and arts/education consultant, and Edward Griffiths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She moved to Melbourne at the age of five, with her mother and two older brothers. When she was 11, her father left home with an 18-year-old woman. She attended <a href="/wiki/Star_of_the_Sea_College" title="Star of the Sea College">Star of the Sea College</a>, a high school in Gardenvale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After earning a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Education" title="Bachelor of Education">Bachelor of Education</a> degree in drama and dance at <a href="/wiki/Victoria_College,_Melbourne" title="Victoria College, Melbourne">Victoria College, Rusden</a>, she began her career as a member of Woolly Jumpers, a <a href="/wiki/Geelong" title="Geelong">Geelong</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Community_theatre" title="Community theatre">community theatre</a> group. She had her first success as the creator and performer of <i>Barbie Gets Hip</i>, which played at the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Fringe_Festival" title="Melbourne Fringe Festival">Melbourne Fringe Festival</a> in 1991.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rachel_Griffiths&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Griffiths and <a href="/wiki/Toni_Collette" title="Toni Collette">Toni Collette</a> were relative unknowns when they were cast as best friends and fellow outcasts in the 1994 film <i>Muriel's Wedding</i>. Her performance won her critical acclaim and both the <a href="/wiki/Film_Critics_Circle_of_Australia" title="Film Critics Circle of Australia">Australian Film Critics Award</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Film_Institute_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="Australian Film Institute Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role">Australian Film Institute Awards</a> for Best Supporting Actress. She followed in 1996 with the role of an earthy, ill-mannered pig farmer's daughter in <a href="/wiki/Michael_Winterbottom" title="Michael Winterbottom">Michael Winterbottom</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Jude_(film)" title="Jude (film)">Jude</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1997, Griffiths sparked a controversy after attending uninvited the opening of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crown_Casino" title="Crown Casino">Crown Casino</a> in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> while topless. She stated a wish to protest the views taken by the media and state government towards the new casino, inspired by the story of <a href="/wiki/Lady_Godiva" title="Lady Godiva">Lady Godiva</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Griffiths joined forces again with <i>Muriel's Wedding</i> director <a href="/wiki/P._J._Hogan" title="P. J. Hogan">P. J. Hogan</a> for her American film debut, <i><a href="/wiki/My_Best_Friend%27s_Wedding" title="My Best Friend's Wedding">My Best Friend's Wedding</a></i>, in 1997. That same year she starred in <i><a href="/wiki/My_Son_the_Fanatic_(film)" title="My Son the Fanatic (film)">My Son the Fanatic</a></i>, a British film in which she portrayed a tough <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prostitute" title="Prostitute">prostitute</a> who becomes involved with a considerably older <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistani</a> taxicab driver, played by <a href="/wiki/Om_Puri" title="Om Puri">Om Puri</a>. Griffiths received an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Best Supporting Actress</a> for her portrayal of real-life flautist <a href="/wiki/Hilary_du_Pr%C3%A9" title="Hilary du Pré">Hilary du Pré</a> opposite <a href="/wiki/Emily_Watson" title="Emily Watson">Emily Watson</a> as her sister, famed cellist <a href="/wiki/Jacqueline_du_Pr%C3%A9" title="Jacqueline du Pré">Jacqueline "Jackie" du Pre</a>, in <i><a href="/wiki/Hilary_and_Jackie" title="Hilary and Jackie">Hilary and Jackie</a></i> (1998).</p>
<p>She then appeared in 2001's <i><a href="/wiki/Blow_(film)" title="Blow (film)">Blow</a></i>, opposite <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Depp" title="Johnny Depp">Johnny Depp</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ray_Liotta" title="Ray Liotta">Ray Liotta</a>. In 2001, Griffiths was cast as one of the leads in <i><a href="/wiki/Six_Feet_Under_(TV_series)" title="Six Feet Under (TV series)">Six Feet Under</a></i>. Her performance as emotionally scarred <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Massage_therapist" title="Massage therapist">massage therapist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brenda_Chenowith" title="Brenda Chenowith">Brenda Chenowith</a>, earned her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild" title="Screen Actors Guild">Screen Actors Guild</a> Awards, as well as two <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> nominations. In the third season, she missed four episodes due to her first pregnancy.</p>
<p>Her second pregnancy was written into the show's final season and she appeared in almost every episode of the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She also played a supportive housewife in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rookie_(2002_film)" title="The Rookie (2002 film)">The Rookie</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Quaid" title="Dennis Quaid">Dennis Quaid</a> for which she garnered generally good reviews.</p>
<p>As of 2006, she became part of the ensemble cast, co-starring alongside <a href="/wiki/Sally_Field" title="Sally Field">Sally Field</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calista_Flockhart" title="Calista Flockhart">Calista Flockhart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Balthazar_Getty" title="Balthazar Getty">Balthazar Getty</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Rhys" title="Matthew Rhys">Matthew Rhys</a>, of the dramatic series <i><a href="/wiki/Brothers_%26_Sisters_(2006_TV_series)" title="Brothers &amp; Sisters (2006 TV series)">Brothers &amp; Sisters</a></i>, in which she portrays <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Walker_(Brothers_%26_Sisters)" title="Sarah Walker (Brothers &amp; Sisters)">Sarah Walker</a>, who inherits control of the family business after her father's death. Griffiths received a 2007 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emmy" title="Emmy">Emmy</a> nomination and a 2008 Emmy nomination for her work on the series. Griffiths received 2008 and 2009 Golden Globe nominations for her work on <i>Brothers &amp; Sisters</i>. Additionally, she appeared as "Inez Scull" in the 2008 mini-series adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Larry_McMurtry" title="Larry McMurtry">Larry McMurtry</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Comanche_Moon" title="Comanche Moon">Comanche Moon</a></i>.</p>
<p>Griffiths made her <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> debut in <i>Other Desert Cities</i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Joe_Mantello" title="Joe Mantello">Joe Mantello</a> and co-starring <a href="/wiki/Judith_Light" title="Judith Light">Judith Light</a>, which began previews on 10 October 2011, opening on 3 November 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2015, Griffiths made her debut as a television director when she directed three episodes of the second series of the Australian teen drama <i><a href="/wiki/Nowhere_Boys" title="Nowhere Boys">Nowhere Boys</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rachel_Griffiths&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Griffiths married Australian artist <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Taylor_(painter)" title="Andrew Taylor (painter)">Andrew Taylor</a> on 31 December 2002 in the chapel of her high school, <a href="/wiki/Star_of_the_Sea_College" title="Star of the Sea College">Star of the Sea College</a>, in Melbourne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> They have three children: a son born in 2003, and two daughters, one born in 2005 and the other in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Griffiths is a devout <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
