<p><b>Rachel Skarsten</b> (born April 23, 1985) is a Canadian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a> best known for her role as <a href="/wiki/Black_Canary" title="Black Canary">Dinah Lance</a> on the television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birds_of_Prey_(TV_Series)" title="Birds of Prey (TV Series)">Birds of Prey</a></i>, Tamsin in <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_Girl" title="Lost Girl">Lost Girl</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Elizabeth I of England</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Reign_(TV_series)" title="Reign (TV series)">Reign</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rachel_Skarsten&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rachel Alice Marie Skarsten was born in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, to a Canadian mother and a Norwegian father, Mary Aileen (née Self) and Dr. Stan Skarsten. She has one younger brother. Rachel trained with the Royal Academy of Dance for 12 years and attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claude_Watson_School_for_the_Arts" title="Claude Watson School for the Arts">Claude Watson School for the Arts</a> from grades 4 to 8. There she majored in visual arts and the cello. She continued in the Claude Watson Arts Program at <a href="/wiki/Earl_Haig_Secondary_School" title="Earl Haig Secondary School">Earl Haig Secondary School</a> from grades 9-12, majoring in visual arts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After being discovered by an agent at a memorial for her father, she went on to be cast as a regular in shows such as Little Men before landing her "big break" as a lead in the Warner Brothers television series <i>Birds of Prey</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After filming wrapped, Skarsten left Los Angeles and returned to Canada where she completed a double degree in English Literature and Classical Studies at the prestigious <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_University" title="Queen's University">Queen's University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kingston,_Ontario" title="Kingston, Ontario">Kingston, Ontario</a>. Following this, she returned to acting with arcs on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_LA_Complex" title="The LA Complex">The LA Complex</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Flashpoint_(TV_series)" title="Flashpoint (TV series)">Flashpoint</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Listener_(TV_series)" title="The Listener (TV series)">The Listener</a></i> as well as work in Canadian independents such as <i>Servitude</i> and <i>Two Hands to Mouth</i>. She played Andrea in the movie adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Fifty_Shades_of_Grey_(film)" title="Fifty Shades of Grey (film)">Fifty Shades of Grey</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
