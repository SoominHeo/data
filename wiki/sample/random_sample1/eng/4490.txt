<p>The <b>Reykjavík Summit</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Summit_(meeting)" title="Summit (meeting)">summit meeting</a> between <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">U.S. President</a> <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a> and <a href="/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Communist_Party" title="General Secretary of the Communist Party">General Secretary of the Communist Party</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev" title="Mikhail Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</a>, held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hofdi" title="Hofdi">Hofdi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Reykjav%C3%ADk" title="Reykjavík">Reykjavík</a>, on 11–12 October 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The talks collapsed at the last minute, but the progress that had been achieved eventually resulted in the 1987 <a href="/wiki/Intermediate-Range_Nuclear_Forces_Treaty" title="Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces Treaty">Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces Treaty</a> between the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Negotiations">Negotiations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reykjav%C3%ADk_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Negotiations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Reagan_Gorbachev_negotiate_outside_Reykjavik_Summit.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="518" data-file-width="750" height="207" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Reagan_Gorbachev_negotiate_outside_Reykjavik_Summit.jpg/300px-Reagan_Gorbachev_negotiate_outside_Reykjavik_Summit.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Reagan_Gorbachev_negotiate_outside_Reykjavik_Summit.jpg/450px-Reagan_Gorbachev_negotiate_outside_Reykjavik_Summit.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Reagan_Gorbachev_negotiate_outside_Reykjavik_Summit.jpg/600px-Reagan_Gorbachev_negotiate_outside_Reykjavik_Summit.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Reagan_Gorbachev_negotiate_outside_Reykjavik_Summit.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Reagan and Gorbachev in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hofdi" title="Hofdi">Hofdi</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1986, Gorbachev had proposed banning all <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ballistic_missiles" title="Ballistic missiles">ballistic missiles</a>, but Reagan wanted to continue research on the <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Defense_Initiative" title="Strategic Defense Initiative">Strategic Defense Initiative</a> (SDI), which involved the militarization of outer space. Yet Soviet suspicion of SDI continued, and U.S.-Soviet relations — already strained by the failure of the <a href="/wiki/Geneva_Summit_(1985)" title="Geneva Summit (1985)">Geneva Summit</a> the previous year<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> — were further strained by the <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Daniloff" title="Nicholas Daniloff">Daniloff</a>-<a href="/wiki/Gennadi_Zakharov" title="Gennadi Zakharov">Zakharov</a> espionage affair.</p>
<p>At Reykjavík, Reagan sought to include discussion of <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Russia_and_the_Soviet_Union#The_collapse_of_the_Soviet_Union_and_emigration_to_Israel" title="History of the Jews in Russia and the Soviet Union">emigration of Soviet Jews</a> and dissidents, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_invasion_of_Afghanistan" title="Soviet invasion of Afghanistan">Soviet invasion of Afghanistan</a>. Gorbachev sought to limit the talks solely to <a href="/wiki/Arms_control" title="Arms control">arms control</a>. The Soviets acceded to the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zero_option" title="Zero option">double-zero</a>" proposal for eliminating INF weapons from Europe, as initially proposed by President Reagan in November 1981 (INF denoting "Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces" as distinct from ICBMs, or <a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_ballistic_missile" title="Intercontinental ballistic missile">intercontinental ballistic missiles</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mann-2">[2]</a></sup> The Soviets also proposed to eliminate 50% of all strategic arms, including <a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_ballistic_missile" title="Intercontinental ballistic missile">ICBMs</a>, and agreed not to include British or French weapons in the count. All this was proposed in exchange for an American pledge not to implement strategic defences for the next ten years, in accordance with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SALT_I" title="SALT I">SALT I</a>.</p>
<p>The Americans countered with a proposal to eliminate all ballistic missiles within ten years, but required the right to deploy strategic defences against remaining threats afterwards. Gorbachev then suggested eliminating all nuclear weapons within a decade. Gorbachev, however, citing a desire to strengthen the <a href="/wiki/Anti-Ballistic_Missile_Treaty" title="Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty">Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty</a> (ABM Treaty), added the condition that any SDI research be confined to laboratories for the ten-year period in question. Reagan argued that his proposed SDI research was allowed by any reasonable interpretation of the ABM treaty, and that he could not forget the pledge he made to Americans to investigate whether SDI was viable. He also promised to share SDI technology, a promise which Gorbachev said he doubted would be fulfilled, as the Americans would not even share oil-drilling technology.</p>
<p>Some, including Reagan staffer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jack_F._Matlock,_Jr." title="Jack F. Matlock, Jr.">Jack F. Matlock, Jr.</a>, attribute Reagan’s refusal to compromise on SDI testing to a mistaken belief that the proposed restrictions would be detrimental to the program, whereas in reality, Matlock contends, they would have had little effect on research that was still in its very early stages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The talks finally stalled, Reagan asking if Gorbachev would "turn down a historic opportunity because of a single word," referring to his insistence on laboratory testing. Gorbachev asserted that it was a matter of principle, and the summit concluded.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Result">Result</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reykjav%C3%ADk_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Result">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite getting unexpectedly close to the potential elimination of all nuclear weapons, the meeting adjourned with no agreement; however, both sides discovered the extent of the concessions the other side was willing to make.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mann-2">[2]</a></sup> Human rights became a subject of productive discussion for the first time. An agreement by Gorbachev to on-site inspections, a continuing American demand which had not been achieved in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partial_Test_Ban_Treaty" title="Partial Test Ban Treaty">Partial Test Ban Treaty</a> of 1963 or the ABM and SALT I pacts of 1972, constituted a significant step forward.</p>
<p>Despite its apparent failure, participants and observers have referred to the summit as an enormous breakthrough which eventually facilitated the INF Treaty (<a href="/wiki/Intermediate-Range_Nuclear_Forces_Treaty" title="Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces Treaty">Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces Treaty</a>), signed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_Summit_(1987)" title="Washington Summit (1987)">Washington Summit</a> on 8 December 1987.</p>
