<p><b>Leyte Gulf</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Bay" title="Bay">gulf</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Visayas" title="Eastern Visayas">Eastern Visayan region</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. The bay is part of the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Sea" title="Philippine Sea">Philippine Sea</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, and is bounded by two islands; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samar_(island)" title="Samar (island)">Samar</a> in the north and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leyte_(island)" title="Leyte (island)">Leyte</a> in the west. On the south of the bay is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mindanao_Island" title="Mindanao Island">Mindanao Island</a>, separated from Leyte by the <a href="/wiki/Surigao_Strait" title="Surigao Strait">Surigao Strait</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geography_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-geography-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dinagat_Island" title="Dinagat Island">Dinagat Island</a> partly encloses the gulf to the southeast, and the small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homonhon_Island" title="Homonhon Island">Homonhon Island</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suluan_Island" title="Suluan Island">Suluan Island</a>, sit astride the eastern entrance to the Gulf. It is approximately 130 km (80 mi) north-south, and 60 km (40 mi) east-west.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geography_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-geography-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Several municipalities are situated on the coast of the gulf: <a href="/wiki/Balangiga,_Eastern_Samar" title="Balangiga, Eastern Samar">Balangiga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giporlos,_Eastern_Samar" title="Giporlos, Eastern Samar">Giporlos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guiuan,_Eastern_Samar" title="Guiuan, Eastern Samar">Guiuan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lawaan,_Eastern_Samar" title="Lawaan, Eastern Samar">Lawaan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes,_Eastern_Samar" title="Mercedes, Eastern Samar">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quinapondan,_Eastern_Samar" title="Quinapondan, Eastern Samar">Quinapondan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salcedo,_Eastern_Samar" title="Salcedo, Eastern Samar">Salcedo</a>. There are also eleven marine reserves in the gulf region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>Leyte Gulf was also the scene of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte_Gulf" title="Battle of Leyte Gulf">Battle of Leyte Gulf</a>, which extends to Surigao Strait during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Surigao_Strait" title="Battle of Surigao Strait">Battle of Surigao Strait</a>, the largest naval battle of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and started the end of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_occupation_of_the_Philippines" title="Japanese occupation of the Philippines">Japanese occupation</a> in the Philippines.</p>
<p>In 2013, <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Haiyan" title="Typhoon Haiyan">Typhoon Haiyan</a> stirred up a <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> in Leyte Gulf, resulting to massive loss of lives, agricultural land and property along <a href="/wiki/Leyte" title="Leyte">Leyte</a>'s shores.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<div style="clear:left;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fishing">Fishing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_Gulf&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fishing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Leyte Gulf is identified by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leyte_State_University" title="Leyte State University">Leyte State University</a> as one of the important fishing grounds of Leyte and Samar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Like other rich fishing grounds such as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maqueda_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maqueda Bay (page does not exist)">Maqueda Bay</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carigara_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carigara Bay (page does not exist)">Carigara Bay</a>, the gulf is known for abundant catches of anchioves, herring, shrimp and crabs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It was also once one of the richest sources of <a href="/wiki/Scylla_serrata" title="Scylla serrata">mud crabs</a> in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Fish harvest has been in decline in the gulf due to the use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dynamite_fishing" title="Dynamite fishing">dynamite fishing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Haiyan" title="Typhoon Haiyan">Typhoon Haiyan</a> has damaged the hard coral cover within the gulf's area, further reducing the fish harvest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
