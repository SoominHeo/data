<p><b>Leyte</b> (also <b>Northern Leyte</b>; <a href="/wiki/Waray_language" title="Waray language">Waray</a>: <span lang="war"><i>Norte san/Amihanan nga Leyte</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Cebuano_language" title="Cebuano language">Cebuano</a>: <span lang="ceb"><i>Amihanang Leyte</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">Filipino</a>: <span lang="fil"><i>Hilagang Leyte</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_the_Philippines" title="Provinces of the Philippines">province</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Visayas" title="Eastern Visayas">Eastern Visayas</a> <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_the_Philippines" title="Regions of the Philippines">region</a>, occupying the northern three-quarters of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leyte_Island" title="Leyte Island">Leyte Island</a>. Its capital is the city of <a href="/wiki/Tacloban" title="Tacloban">Tacloban</a>. Leyte is situated west of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samar_Island" title="Samar Island">Samar Island</a>, north of <a href="/wiki/Southern_Leyte" title="Southern Leyte">Southern Leyte</a> and south of <a href="/wiki/Biliran" title="Biliran">Biliran</a>. To the west of Leyte across the <a href="/wiki/Camotes_Sea" title="Camotes Sea">Camotes Sea</a> is the province of <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">Cebu</a>.</p>
<p>The historical name of the Philippines, "Las Islas Felipenas", named by Spanish explorer <a href="/wiki/Ruy_L%C3%B3pez_de_Villalobos" title="Ruy López de Villalobos">Ruy López de Villalobos</a> in honor of <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Prince Philip of Spain</a>, used to refer to the islands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leyte_Island" title="Leyte Island">Leyte</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samar_Island" title="Samar Island">Samar</a>, until it was adopted to refer to the entire archipelago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FastFactsPhilProv-Leyte_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FastFactsPhilProv-Leyte-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Leyte is also known as the site of <a href="/wiki/Largest_naval_battle_in_history" title="Largest naval battle in history">the largest naval battle in modern history</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte_Gulf" title="Battle of Leyte Gulf">Battle of Leyte Gulf</a>, which took place during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>.</p>
<p>On 8 November 2013, the province was largely destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Haiyan" title="Typhoon Haiyan">Super Typhoon Yolanda (Haiyan)</a>, killing a reported 10,000 people, having previously suffered similar destruction and loss of life in 1991 during <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Thelma" title="Tropical Storm Thelma">Tropical Storm Thelma</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-colonial_period">Pre-colonial period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-colonial period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The island of Leyte was once the location of <i>Mairete</i>, meaning <i>land of Ete</i>, a historic community which was ruled by Datu Ete. Before being colonized by Spain, the island was once home to indigenous animist Warays to the East and other indigenous animist Visayan groups to the west.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish_period">Spanish period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Spanish period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Spanish explorer <a href="/wiki/Ruy_L%C3%B3pez_de_Villalobos" title="Ruy López de Villalobos">Ruy López de Villalobos</a>, first came to the island in 1543 and named it <i>Las Islas Felipinas</i>. When the Spanish government established government in <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">Cebu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leyte" title="Leyte">Leyte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samar" title="Samar">Samar</a> became part of the province of <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">Cebu</a>. In 1595, the religious Jesuits established mission in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carigara" title="Carigara">Carigara</a> which preceded the mission established in <a href="/wiki/Palo,_Leyte" title="Palo, Leyte">Palo</a> in 1596 and Ormoc and Alangalang missions in 1597. In 1735, <a href="/wiki/Leyte" title="Leyte">Leyte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samar" title="Samar">Samar</a> were separated from <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">Cebu</a> to be established as a single provincial government with <a href="/wiki/Carigara,_Leyte" title="Carigara, Leyte">Carigara</a> as the first provincial capital. In 1768, <a href="/wiki/Leyte" title="Leyte">Leyte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samar" title="Samar">Samar</a> were split into two separate provinces.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_period">American period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: American period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Division_of_Leyte">Division of Leyte</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Division of Leyte">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On March 27, 1923, <i>Act No. 3117</i> was proposed to divide Leyte into <i>Occidental Leyte</i> and <i>Oriental Leyte</i> but was not proclaimed by the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_the_Philippines" title="Governor-General of the Philippines">Governor-General of the Philippines</a>. On May 22, 1959, <i>Republic Act No. 2227</i> was passed into law that separated the third Congressional District of Leyte into a separate province of <a href="/wiki/Southern_Leyte" title="Southern Leyte">Southern Leyte</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LawPhilNet-RA2227_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-LawPhilNet-RA2227-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_Leyte_Gulf">Battle of Leyte Gulf</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Battle of Leyte Gulf">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte_Gulf" title="Battle of Leyte Gulf">Battle of Leyte Gulf</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte_Gulf" title="Battle of Leyte Gulf">Battle of Leyte Gulf</a> took place in the seas surrounding this island from 23 October to 26 October 1944. It was <a href="/wiki/Largest_naval_battle_in_history" title="Largest naval battle in history">the largest naval battle in modern history</a>, when at least 212 Allied ships clashed with the remnants of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a>, some 60 ships, including the super battleships <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Yamato" title="Japanese battleship Yamato">Yamato</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Musashi" title="Japanese battleship Musashi">Musashi</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte" title="Battle of Leyte">First Battle of Leyte</a> occurred on 20 October 1944. A successful Allied invasion of the island was the crucial element to the eventual Filipino and American victory in the Philippines.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Leyte covers a total area of 6,313.33 square kilometres (2,437.59 sq mi)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nscb_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-nscb-6">[6]</a></sup> occupying the northern and central sections of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leyte_Island" title="Leyte Island">Leyte</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Visayas" title="Eastern Visayas">Eastern Visayas</a> region. The province is situated west of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samar_Island" title="Samar Island">Samar Island</a>, north of <a href="/wiki/Southern_Leyte" title="Southern Leyte">Southern Leyte</a>, south of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biliran_Province" title="Biliran Province">Biliran</a>, and east of the <a href="/wiki/Camotes_Sea" title="Camotes Sea">Camotes Sea</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ph_fil_leyte.png"><img alt="Ph fil leyte.png" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2780" data-file-width="1941" height="243" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Ph_fil_leyte.png/170px-Ph_fil_leyte.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Ph_fil_leyte.png/255px-Ph_fil_leyte.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Ph_fil_leyte.png/340px-Ph_fil_leyte.png 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ph_fil_leyte.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Leyte comprises 40 <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_the_Philippines" title="Municipalities of the Philippines">municipalities</a> and three <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_the_Philippines" title="Cities of the Philippines">cities</a>, all clustered into 5 <a href="/wiki/Legislative_districts_of_Leyte" title="Legislative districts of Leyte">congressional districts</a>.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ormoc_City" title="Ormoc City">Ormoc City</a> is an <i>independent component city</i>, while the capital <a href="/wiki/Tacloban" title="Tacloban">Tacloban</a> was declared a <i>highly urbanized city</i> in 2008. Both cities govern themselves independently of the province and their residents do not vote for elective provincial officials.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baybay,_Leyte" title="Baybay, Leyte">Baybay</a> attained cityhood in 2007 but reverted to its municipal status when the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_Philippines" title="Supreme Court of the Philippines">Supreme Court</a> declared its <a href="/wiki/Municipal_charter" title="Municipal charter">city charter</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unconstitutional" title="Unconstitutional">unconstitutional</a> in 2008. It regained its city status following the reversal of the Supreme Court decision dated December 22, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TribuneOnlineOrg_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-TribuneOnlineOrg-7">[7]</a></sup> In August 2010, however, a resolution had been passed reverting 16 cities, one of which was Baybay, to municipal status. It was on February 15, 2011 that the Supreme Court reversed its decision once again, allowing Baybay, along with the other 16 cities, to retain their cityhood status.</p>
<div class="plainlist">

<p>The population of Leyte in the 2015 census was 1,724,679 people,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSO15-08_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-NSO15-08-3">[3]</a></sup> with a density of 270 inhabitants per square kilometre or 700 inhabitants per square mile. When Tacloban City is included for geographical purposes, the population is 1,966,768 people, with a density of 302/km<sup>2</sup> (782/sq mi).</p>
<p>The people of Leyte are divided into two main groups, primarily by language. In the west and south are the <a href="/wiki/Cebuano_people" title="Cebuano people">Cebuanos</a>, while in the north and east are the <a href="/wiki/Waray_people" title="Waray people">Warays</a>. But since then, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waray-Waray_language" title="Waray-Waray language">Waray-Waray</a> (Leyte dialect) is considered as the <a href="/wiki/Lingua_franca" title="Lingua franca">lingua franca</a> of the whole province, especially in the metropolitan area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tacloban_City" title="Tacloban City">Tacloban City</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Cebuano_language" title="Cebuano language">Cebuano</a> speaking Leyteños (locally known as <i>Kana</i>) and the Waraynons. This is also used as a lingua franca between Leyteños and other Filipinos of other ethnolinguistic backgrounds. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baybay_City" title="Baybay City">city of Baybay</a>, a separate language known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baybayanon_language" title="Baybayanon language">Baybayanon</a> is spoken.</p>
<p>There are also some Spanish mestizos and some natives of the province who can understand and speak <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language_in_the_Philippines" title="Spanish language in the Philippines">Spanish</a> due to the province's colonial history. Most Leyteños also can also speak and understand <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">Filipino language</a>, the national language of the Philippines and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> although it is just their second and third languages respectively.</p>
<p>Leyte is predominantly Roman Catholic Christian province. According to the Year 2000 census, 97% of Leyte's population is Roman Catholic Christians, one of the highest percentage in the Visayas.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> While the remaining 3% are adherents of other different Christian sects, denominations and churches such as the indigenous <a href="/wiki/Iglesia_ni_Cristo" title="Iglesia ni Cristo">Iglesia ni Cristo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Jesus_Christ" title="Kingdom of Jesus Christ">Kingdom of Jesus Christ</a> and Members Church of God International or popularly known as <i>Ang Dating Daan</i> and the foreign religious groups like Mormons, Baptists, Evangelicals, Pentecostals, and Seventh-Day Adventists (<i>Sabadistas</i>) and other Protestant groups.</p>
<p>Islam is also present in the province and concentrated in the metropolitan Tacloban area. It comprised 0.3% of Tacloban City's population and all adherents are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maranao" title="Maranao">Maranao</a> and other Moro migrants from the <a href="/wiki/Mindanao" title="Mindanao">Mindanao</a> region who work mostly as traders.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_provincial_seal">Official provincial seal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Official provincial seal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="floatleft"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Leyte_Logo.png"><img alt="Leyte Logo.png" data-file-height="311" data-file-width="311" height="120" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Leyte_Logo.png/120px-Leyte_Logo.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Leyte_Logo.png/180px-Leyte_Logo.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Leyte_Logo.png/240px-Leyte_Logo.png 2x" width="120"/></a></div>
<p>The <i>upper portion</i> is a perspective of the national Freedom Park commemorating the landing of <a href="/wiki/General_of_the_Army_(United_States)" title="General of the Army (United States)">General</a> <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a> and the American Liberation Forces in Leyte during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The <i>white cross</i> represents the 2nd phase of Leyte's development when Magellan stopped here on his way to Cebu. The <i>alphabet on the cross argent</i> is the ancient Visayan paleographic syllabary of the letter L which stands for Leyte. The <i>stars around the inner circle</i> symbolize the forty-one (41) towns of Leyte and the everlasting flame symbolizes the soldiers who died during World War II.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Leyte is a mixed agriculture, fishing, industrial, energy and mining. <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">Rice</a> is farmed in the lowland plains areas specifically those around Tacloban, while <a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">coconut</a> farming, is the main cash crop in upland and mountainous areas. Sugarcane plantation is no. 1 produce in Ormoc City. Since Leyte is an island province, fishing is a major source of livelihood among coastal residents.</p>
<p>The province is the site of the largest geothermal plant in Asia, making it one of the resource-rich provinces of the Philippines. Excess energy of the numerous powerplants in the geothermal valley that generate electricity is supplied to the national grid that adds to the energy demand in Luzon and Mindanao. Mining industry has started to pick up in the province with the exploration in <a href="/wiki/MacArthur,_Leyte" title="MacArthur, Leyte">MacArthur</a>. The iron smelting in <a href="/wiki/Isabel,_Leyte" title="Isabel, Leyte">Isabel</a> has been operational for more than 20 years since its inception.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leyte_ICT_Park">Leyte ICT Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Leyte ICT Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Leyte Information Communications Technology (ICT) Park is one of the economic zones approved by the Philippine Economic Zone Authority (PEZA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PEZA-ITParks_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-PEZA-ITParks-9">[9]</a></sup> Located at Pawing, Palo, Leyte, the 6.8 facility hosts two (2) <a href="/wiki/Business_process_outsourcing" title="Business process outsourcing">business process outsourcing</a> (BPO) companies, namely, the Expert Global Solutions (EGS) Company (formerly APAC Customer Services, Inc.) and ACUDATA, Inc. (a financial and data services BPO).</p>
<p>Leyte is emerging<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (March 2013)">according to whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> to be an ICT-BPO Hub for <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Visayas" title="Eastern Visayas">Eastern Visayas</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leyte_(province)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Leyte is connected by air, with only one commercial airport located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tacloban_City" title="Tacloban City">Tacloban City</a>. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Z._Romualdez_Airport" title="Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport">Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport</a>, which is one of the busiest airports in the Philippines, has two major airlines; <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Airlines" title="Philippine Airlines">Philippine Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cebu_Pacific" title="Cebu Pacific">Cebu Pacific</a>. Tacloban's airport primarily serves connecting flights from major Philippine cities (Manila and Cebu).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Philtranco" title="Philtranco">Philtranco</a>, which has a bus stop in Tacloban and Ormoc, operates a route along Manila-Maharlika highway, passing through <a href="/wiki/Allen,_Northern_Samar" title="Allen, Northern Samar">Allen, Northern Samar</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samar_Island" title="Samar Island">Samar Island</a> (via ferry boat) from <a href="/wiki/Matnog,_Sorsogon" title="Matnog, Sorsogon">Matnog, Sorsogon</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bicol_region" title="Bicol region">Bicol region</a>.</p>
<p>Commercial seaport is of vital importance. Major ports of the province are located at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ormoc_City" title="Ormoc City">Ormoc City</a>,in the south and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tacloban_City" title="Tacloban City">Tacloban City</a>, in the north. Small ports are also located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palompon" title="Palompon">Palompon</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Isidro,_Leyte" title="San Isidro, Leyte">San Isidro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bato,_Leyte" title="Bato, Leyte">Bato</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hilongos" title="Hilongos">Hilongos</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baybay_City" title="Baybay City">Baybay City</a>.</p>
