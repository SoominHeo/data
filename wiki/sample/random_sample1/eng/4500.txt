<p><b>Rainn Dietrich Wilson</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/iː/ long 'e' in 'seed'">iː</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'w' in 'wind'">w</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/ən/ 'on' in 'button'">ən</span></span>/</a></span></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> born January 20, 1966) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> actor. He is best known for his role as <a href="/wiki/Egomania" title="Egomania">egomaniac</a> <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Schrute" title="Dwight Schrute">Dwight Schrute</a> on the American version of the television comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Office_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Office (U.S. TV series)">The Office</a></i>. Other television roles include Lieutenant Backstrom in the FOX drama <a href="/wiki/Backstrom_(TV_series)" title="Backstrom (TV series)"><i>Backstrom</i></a>. In film, Wilson has had numerous parts in such movies as <i><a href="/wiki/Super_(2010_American_film)" title="Super (2010 American film)">Super</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juno_(film)" title="Juno (film)">Juno</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Rocker_(film)" title="The Rocker (film)">The Rocker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sahara_(2005_film)" title="Sahara (2005 film)">Sahara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Almost_Famous" title="Almost Famous">Almost Famous</a></i> as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voice_actor" title="Voice actor">voicing</a> an alien villain in 2009's <i><a href="/wiki/Monsters_vs._Aliens" title="Monsters vs. Aliens">Monsters vs. Aliens</a></i>. He published an <a href="/wiki/Autobiography" title="Autobiography">autobiography</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bassoon_King" title="The Bassoon King">The Bassoon King</a></i>, in November 2015. He co-founded the digital media company <a href="/wiki/SoulPancake" title="SoulPancake">SoulPancake</a> in 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainn_Wilson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wilson was born in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>, the son of Shay Cooper, a <a href="/wiki/Yoga" title="Yoga">yoga</a> teacher and actress, and Robert G. Wilson, a novelist, artist, and business consultant who wrote the science fiction novel <i>Tentacles of Dawn</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Wilson has Norwegian ancestry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kellogg_Middle_School" title="Kellogg Middle School">Kellogg Middle School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shorecrest_High_School" title="Shorecrest High School">Shorecrest High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shoreline,_Washington" title="Shoreline, Washington">Shoreline, Washington</a>, where he played the clarinet and bassoon in the band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He transferred to and graduated from <a href="/wiki/New_Trier_High_School" title="New Trier High School">New Trier High School</a> after his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Wilmette,_Illinois" title="Wilmette, Illinois">Wilmette</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, to serve at the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith" title="Bahá'í Faith">Bahá'í</a> <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_House_of_Worship_(Wilmette,_Illinois)" title="Bahá'í House of Worship (Wilmette, Illinois)">National Center</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Wilson has a <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">theatre</a> background from <a href="/wiki/Tufts_University" title="Tufts University">Tufts University</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Washington" title="University of Washington">University of Washington</a>, and he has taught acting classes.</p>
<p>Wilson holds an <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Fine_Arts" title="Master of Fine Arts">MFA</a> from <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tisch_School_of_the_Arts#Graduate_Acting_Program" title="Tisch School of the Arts">Graduate Acting Program</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Tisch_School_of_the_Arts" title="Tisch School of the Arts">Tisch School of the Arts</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and was a member of <a href="/wiki/The_Acting_Company" title="The Acting Company">The Acting Company</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> While acting in theatrical productions in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, he drove a moving van to make ends meet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-msnbc_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-msnbc-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wilson worked extensively in the theater for his first ten years after graduating from <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">N.Y.U</a>. He was an actor at the <a href="/wiki/The_Public_Theater" title="The Public Theater">Public Theater</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ensemble_Studio_Theatre" title="Ensemble Studio Theatre">Ensemble Studio Theater</a>, <a href="/wiki/Playwrights_Horizons" title="Playwrights Horizons">Playwrights Horizons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roundabout_Theatre_Company" title="Roundabout Theatre Company">The Roundabout</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Guthrie_Theater" title="Guthrie Theater">The Guthrie Theater</a>, among others. He was nominated for three <a href="/wiki/Helen_Hayes_Award" title="Helen Hayes Award">Helen Hayes Awards</a> for Best Supporting Actor for his work at the <a href="/wiki/Arena_Stage" title="Arena Stage">Arena Stage</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainn_Wilson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Wilson played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Characters_of_Six_Feet_Under#Arthur_Martin" title="Characters of Six Feet Under">Arthur Martin</a>, the intern at Fisher &amp; Diaz Funeral Home in <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Six_Feet_Under_(TV_series)" title="Six Feet Under (TV series)">Six Feet Under</a></i> and Dwight Schrute in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Office_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Office (U.S. TV series)">The Office</a></i>, on which he directed three episodes: the Season 6 "The Cover-Up", the Season 7 "Classy Christmas", and the Season 8 "Get the Girl", and guest-starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Monk_(TV_series)" title="Monk (TV series)">Monk</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Numbers_(TV_series)" title="Numbers (TV series)">NUMB3RS</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tim_and_Eric_Awesome_Show,_Great_Job" title="Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job">Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job</a>,</i> <i><a href="/wiki/Reno_911!" title="Reno 911!">Reno 911</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Roadies_(TV_series)" title="Roadies (TV series)">Roadies</a>.</i></p>
<p>On February 24, 2007, Wilson hosted <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>, becoming the second cast member from <i>The Office</i> to host (after <a href="/wiki/Steve_Carell" title="Steve Carell">Steve Carell</a>). During the <a href="/wiki/2007_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2007 FIFA Women's World Cup">2007 FIFA Women's World Cup</a>, Wilson appeared in ads for the 2007 <a href="/wiki/United_States_women%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States women's national soccer team">United States women's national soccer team</a> as public relations manager "Jim Mike." In August 2010, Wilson appeared in the music video for <a href="/wiki/Ferraby_Lionheart" title="Ferraby Lionheart">Ferraby Lionheart</a>'s "Harry and Bess" and <a href="/wiki/Andy_Grammer" title="Andy Grammer">Andy Grammer</a>'s "Keep Your Head Up" as the "creepy elevator guy", and made the final cut for the role of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Arrested_Development_characters#Gob_Bluth" title="List of Arrested Development characters">Gob</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Arrested_Development_(TV_series)" title="Arrested Development (TV series)">Arrested Development</a></i>, but was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Will_Arnett" title="Will Arnett">Will Arnett</a>.</p>
<p>He was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy</a> for Best Supporting Actor three different times for his role as <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Schrute" title="Dwight Schrute">Dwight Schrute</a> in 2007, 2008, and 2009. He won two <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award" title="Screen Actors Guild Award">SAG awards</a> for best comedy ensemble for a television show for <a href="/wiki/The_Office_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Office (U.S. TV series)"><i>The Office</i></a> and one for best drama ensemble for <i><a href="/wiki/Six_Feet_Under_(TV_series)" title="Six Feet Under (TV series)">Six Feet Under</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>He also starred in films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Almost_Famous" title="Almost Famous">Almost Famous</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Sweethearts" title="America's Sweethearts">America's Sweethearts</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAADASSSSS!" title="BAADASSSSS!">BAADASSSSS!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Galaxy_Quest" title="Galaxy Quest">Galaxy Quest</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/House_of_1000_Corpses" title="House of 1000 Corpses">House of 1000 Corpses</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sahara_(2005_film)" title="Sahara (2005 film)">Sahara</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Mimzy" title="The Last Mimzy">The Last Mimzy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Juno_(film)" title="Juno (film)">Juno</a></i> (as the convenience store clerk Rollo), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rocker_(film)" title="The Rocker (film)">The Rocker</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/My_Super_Ex-Girlfriend" title="My Super Ex-Girlfriend">My Super Ex-Girlfriend</a></i>. Wilson starred in the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Atomic" title="Fox Atomic">Fox Atomic</a> comedy <i>The Rocker</i>, released on August 20, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2009 he lent his voice to <a href="/wiki/DreamWorks_Animation" title="DreamWorks Animation">DreamWorks Animation</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Monsters_vs._Aliens" title="Monsters vs. Aliens">Monsters vs. Aliens</a></i>, as the villainous alien overlord Gallaxhar,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and was featured in<i><a href="/wiki/Transformers:_Revenge_of_the_Fallen" title="Transformers: Revenge of the Fallen">Transformers: Revenge of the Fallen</a></i>, in which he played a university professor. In 2011, he played the lead role as the unhinged protagonist in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_(2011_film)" title="Super (2011 film)">Super</a></i> and the black sheep brother in <i><a href="/wiki/Peep_World" title="Peep World">Peep World</a>.</i></p>
<p>More recently he played in the independent horror comedy, <i><a href="/wiki/Cooties_(film)" title="Cooties (film)">Cooties</a></i> and the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boy_(2015_film)" title="The Boy (2015 film)">The Boy</a></i> for Elijah Wood’s production company,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/SpectreVision" title="SpectreVision">SpectreVision</a>. He recently finished shooting <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shimmer_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shimmer Lake (page does not exist)">Shimmer Lake</a></i>, a Netflix original movie, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Permanent_film&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Permanent film (page does not exist)">Permanent</a></i>, an independent comedy and <a href="/wiki/Meg_(film)" title="Meg (film)"><i>Meg</i></a> for <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Brothers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rainn is voicing the character of <a href="/wiki/Gargamel" title="Gargamel">Gargamel</a> in the 2017 animated reboot of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Smurfs:_The_Lost_Village" title="The Smurfs: The Lost Village">The Smurfs: The Lost Village</a> for <a href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures_Animation" title="Sony Pictures Animation">Sony Pictures Animation</a>.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> crime-drama <i><a href="/wiki/Backstrom_(TV_series)" title="Backstrom (TV series)">Backstrom</a></i>, Wilson played Evert Backstrom, an offensive, self-destructive detective who is part of a team of eccentric criminologists. The series is based on <a href="/wiki/Leif_G._W._Persson" title="Leif G. W. Persson">Leif G. W. Persson</a>'s Swedish book series of the same name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Wilson also served as the show's producer. It was cancelled by Fox after 13 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wilson co-wrote the <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times_Best_Seller_list" title="The New York Times Best Seller list">New York Times Bestseller</a> <a href="/wiki/SoulPancake" title="SoulPancake"><i>SoulPancake: Chew on Life’s Big Questions</i></a> and wrote a humorous memoir about his personal life, career and faith called <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bassoon_King" title="The Bassoon King">The Bassoon King</a></i> that was published in November 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yvonne_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yvonne-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="SoulPancake">SoulPancake</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainn_Wilson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: SoulPancake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wilson founded the website and YouTube channel <a href="/wiki/SoulPancake" title="SoulPancake">SoulPancake</a>. As of January 24, 2016, the channel has over 1.6 million subscribers, and over 225 million video views. <a href="/wiki/SoulPancake" title="SoulPancake">SoulPancake</a> has been featured on <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" title="Oprah Winfrey">Oprah Winfrey</a>'s Satellite Radio Show and Super Soul Sunday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The tagline of the brand is: "We make stuff that matters." They were named one of <a href="/wiki/Fast_Company_(magazine)" title="Fast Company (magazine)">Fast Company</a>'s 10 Most Innovative Companies in Video for 2015.<sup><a href="/wiki/SoulPancake#cite_note-1" title="SoulPancake">[1]</a></sup> More recently, <a href="/wiki/SoulPancake" title="SoulPancake">SoulPancake</a> was ranked #114 on the 2015 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inc._500" title="Inc. 500">Inc. 500</a> Fastest-Growing Private Companies in America List<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> It was recently purchased by <a href="/wiki/Participant_Media" title="Participant Media">Participant Media</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainn_Wilson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wilson is married to writer <a href="/wiki/Holiday_Reinhorn" title="Holiday Reinhorn">Holiday Reinhorn</a>. They met in an acting class in college and married on the <a href="/wiki/Kalama_River" title="Kalama River">Kalama River</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_(U.S._state)" title="Washington (U.S. state)">Washington</a>. The couple have a son, Walter Mckenzie, born in 2004, and reside in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agoura_Hills" title="Agoura Hills">Agoura Hills</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. They have a home outside of <a href="/wiki/Sisters,_Oregon" title="Sisters, Oregon">Sisters, Oregon</a>. They have two <a href="/wiki/Pit_bull" title="Pit bull">pit bulls</a>, Oona and Pilot; two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vietnamese_Potbelly_Pig" title="Vietnamese Potbelly Pig">vietnamese pot-bellied pigs</a>, Snorty and Amy; as well as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zonkey" title="Zonkey">zonkey</a> named Derek.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He and his family are members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baha%27i_Faith" title="Baha'i Faith">Baha'i Faith</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He hosts a podcast for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Baha%27i_Blog&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Baha'i Blog (page does not exist)">Baha'i Blog</a> called the Baha'i Blogcast, where he interviews notable people about the intersection of their faith and their work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On Bill Maher's <i><a href="/wiki/Real_Time_with_Bill_Maher" title="Real Time with Bill Maher">Real Time</a></i>, Wilson described himself as a diverse independent, voting for <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_Party_(United_States)" title="Green Party (United States)">Green Party</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> candidates.</p>
<p>Wilson's charitable works include fundraising for the <a href="/wiki/Mona_Foundation" title="Mona Foundation">Mona educational organization</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In 2013, along with his wife and Dr Kathryn Adams, he co-founded <a href="/wiki/Lid%C3%A8_Haiti" title="Lidè Haiti">Lidè Haiti</a>, an educational initiative that uses the arts and literacy to empower adolescent girls in rural Haiti. They currently work in 13 locations with over 500 girls, providing scholarships to many of them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
