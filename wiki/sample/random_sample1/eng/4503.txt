<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bridges_.28man-made_and_natural.29">Bridges (man-made and natural)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Bridges (man-made and natural)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_National_Monument" title="Rainbow Bridge National Monument">Rainbow Bridge National Monument</a>, a natural rock formation located in Utah, United States</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_(Kansas)" title="Rainbow Bridge (Kansas)">Rainbow Bridge (Kansas)</a>, in Kansas</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_(Niagara_Falls)" title="Rainbow Bridge (Niagara Falls)">Rainbow Bridge (Niagara Falls)</a>, on the United States–Canada border</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge,_Oxford" title="Rainbow Bridge, Oxford">Rainbow Bridge, Oxford</a>, in Oxford, England</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_(Texas)" title="Rainbow Bridge (Texas)">Rainbow Bridge (Texas)</a>, in southeast Texas, United States, the highest bridge in the state</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_(Tokyo)" title="Rainbow Bridge (Tokyo)">Rainbow Bridge (Tokyo)</a>, in Tokyo, Japan</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_(La_Conner,_Washington)" title="Rainbow Bridge (La Conner, Washington)">Rainbow Bridge (La Conner, Washington)</a>, connects Fidalgo Island and La Conner, crossing Swinomish Channel in Skagit County, Washington</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Medley_Footbridge" title="Medley Footbridge">Medley Footbridge</a>, sometimes called Rainbow Bridge</li>
<li>A 12th-century Chinese camelback bridge depicted in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qingming_Scroll" title="Qingming Scroll">Qingming Scroll</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_(film)" title="Rainbow Bridge (film)"><i>Rainbow Bridge</i> (film)</a>, a 1972 film featuring the music of Jimi Hendrix</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_(album)" title="Rainbow Bridge (album)"><i>Rainbow Bridge</i> (album)</a> (1971), a 1971 album by Jimi Hendrix</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_uses">Other uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rainbow_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Marsh Rainbow Arch, a bridge design by <a href="/wiki/James_Barney_Marsh" title="James Barney Marsh">James Barney Marsh</a> (1856–1936) of the <a href="/wiki/Marsh_Engineering_Company" title="Marsh Engineering Company">Marsh Engineering Company</a>. Seven of these "Rainbow Arch Bridge" crossings in various locations appear on the US National Register of Historic Places.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bifr%C3%B6st" title="Bifröst">Bifröst</a>, a burning, rainbow bridge that reaches from the realm of mankind to the realm of the gods in Norse mythology</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_(pets)" title="Rainbow Bridge (pets)">Rainbow Bridge (pets)</a>, a concept in the pet owner community, referring to a metaphorical or mythological place where pets go upon their deaths, to be later reunited with their owners</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_(M*A*S*H)" title="Rainbow Bridge (M*A*S*H)">Rainbow Bridge (M*A*S*H)</a>, an episode of the television series <i>M*A*S*H</i></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainbow_Bridge_(1963_film)" title="Rainbow Bridge (1963 film)"><i>Rainbow Bridge</i> (1963 film)</a>, a Japanese anime film</li>
</ul>
