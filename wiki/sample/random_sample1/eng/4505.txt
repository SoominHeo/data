<p><b>Leila Bronia Josefowicz</b> (born October 20, 1977) is an American-Canadian classical <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">violinist</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leila_Josefowicz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Josefowicz was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Missisauga,_Ontario" title="Missisauga, Ontario">Missisauga, Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. When she was a young child her family moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a> where she started studying the <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">violin</a> at the age of three and a half using the <a href="/wiki/Suzuki_method" title="Suzuki method">Suzuki method</a>. Her father, physicist Jack Yitzhak Josefowicz, and mother, biologist Wendy Josefowicz, learned with her. At age five, she started formal lessons with Idel Low. At eight she began studies with the distinguished violin teacher <a href="/wiki/Robert_Lipsett" title="Robert Lipsett">Robert Lipsett</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Colburn_School" title="The Colburn School">The Colburn School</a>. Leila's parents, valuing a well-rounded education, felt it important that she stay in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_school_(government_funded)" title="Public school (government funded)">public school</a> system, rejecting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_schooling" title="Home schooling">home schooling</a>, despite a very full schedule of music activities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Leila was 13 the Josefowiczes moved to <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> so she could attend the prestigious <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Institute_of_Music" title="Curtis Institute of Music">Curtis Institute of Music</a>, where she studied with <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Laredo" title="Jaime Laredo">Jaime Laredo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jascha_Brodsky" title="Jascha Brodsky">Jascha Brodsky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Felix_Galimir" title="Felix Galimir">Felix Galimir</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseph_Gingold" title="Joseph Gingold">Joseph Gingold</a>. Leila also attended the <a href="/wiki/Julia_R._Masterman_School" title="Julia R. Masterman School">Julia R. Masterman School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> while at Curtis, completing a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_music" title="Bachelor of music">bachelor of music</a> degree and her <a href="/wiki/High_school_diploma" title="High school diploma">high school diploma</a> in the same year.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leila_Josefowicz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While still in her teens, Josefowicz played with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Symphony_orchestras" title="Symphony orchestras">symphony orchestras</a> in Europe, Asia and North America, including Philadelphia, Cleveland, Los Angeles, Houston, Chicago, Boston, Montreal and Toronto.</p>
<p>Josefowicz made her <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a> debut in 1994 performing the <i><a href="/wiki/Violin_Concerto_(Tchaikovsky)" title="Violin Concerto (Tchaikovsky)">Tchaikovsky Concerto</a></i> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Neville_Marriner" title="Sir Neville Marriner">Sir Neville Marriner</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_of_St._Martin_in_the_Fields" title="Academy of St. Martin in the Fields">Academy of St. Martin in the Fields</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The same year she signed an exclusive recording contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philips_Classics" title="Philips Classics">Philips Classics</a>, recording the Tchaikovsky and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Sibelius" title="Jean Sibelius">Sibelius</a> concertos. Other recordings followed on Warner Classics,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nonesuch_Records" title="Nonesuch Records">Nonesuch Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Grammophon" title="Deutsche Grammophon">Deutsche Grammophon</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> labels that include masterworks for solo violin, recital repertoire and the concertos of <a href="/wiki/Romantic_music" title="Romantic music">Romantic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modernism_(music)" title="Modernism (music)">modern</a> composers.</p>
<p>Josefowicz has kept a busy international schedule as a soloist, performing regularly around the world including North and South America, Europe, Japan, China, New Zealand and Australia. She has a strong interest in <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Musical_improvisation" title="Musical improvisation">improvisation</a>, and new music.</p>
<p>Josefowicz is acclaimed for championing new compositions, including the works of <a href="/wiki/John_Adams_(composer)" title="John Adams (composer)">John Adams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Knussen" title="Oliver Knussen">Oliver Knussen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Ad%C3%A8s" title="Thomas AdÃ¨s">Thomas AdÃ¨s</a>. In 2008-09, she performed the world premieres of concertos written for her by <a href="/wiki/Esa-Pekka_Salonen" title="Esa-Pekka Salonen">Esa-Pekka Salonen</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Philharmonic" title="Los Angeles Philharmonic">Los Angeles Philharmonic</a> (April 2009); <a href="/wiki/Steven_Mackey" title="Steven Mackey">Steven Mackey</a> with the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Symphony" title="St. Louis Symphony">St. Louis Symphony</a> (October 2008); and <a href="/wiki/Colin_Matthews" title="Colin Matthews">Colin Matthews</a> with the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Birmingham_Symphony_Orchestra" title="City of Birmingham Symphony Orchestra">City of Birmingham Symphony Orchestra</a> (September 2009) and the violin concerto <i><a href="/wiki/Scheherazade.2" title="Scheherazade.2">Scheherazade.2</a></i> written for Josefowicz by <a href="/wiki/John_Adams_(composer)" title="John Adams (composer)">John Adams</a> performed with the New York Philharmonic (March 2015). Performing in the world's major concert halls, Josefowicz continues to play traditional masterworks and champions contemporary compositions, exposing audiences around the world to the aforementioned new violin concertos, with ensembles including the Chicago Symphony Orchestra (Salonen), New York City Ballet Orchestra (Salonen), St. Louis Symphony Orchestra (AdÃ¨s), Finnish Radio Symphony Orchestra (Matthews), New World Symphony (AdÃ¨s), Royal Scottish National Orchestra (Knussen), Los Angeles Philharmonic Orchestra (Mackey), Cleveland Orchestra (Adams), Royal Concertgebouw Orchestra (AdÃ¨s), Minnesota Orchestra (VÃ¤nskÃ¤), and Atlanta Symphony Orchestra (AdÃ¨s).</p>
<p>Josefowicz's concert tours take her to all corners of the world. Some of her recent and upcoming engagements in Europe also include appearances with the Leipzig Gewandhaus Orchestra, the London, Munich and Czech Philharmonics, and the London Symphony; performances of the new Salonen concerto in Paris, Stockholm, Lisbon and Ferrara, Italy with the composer on the podium; and a fifth appearance at the London Proms.</p>
<p>Josefowicz received an Avery Fisher Career Grant in 1994. In 2007, she was named a USA Cummings Fellow, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Artists" title="United States Artists">United States Artists</a>. For her advocacy of new contemporary works for the violin, Josefowicz was named a 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MacArthur_Fellow" title="MacArthur Fellow">MacArthur Fellow</a>.</p>
