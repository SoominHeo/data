<p>The <b>Legends Football League</b> (<b>LFL</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Woman" title="Woman">women</a>'s 7-on-7 tackle <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_league" title="Sports league">league</a>, with games played in the spring and summer at <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">NBA</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">NHL</a> and <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">MLS</a> arenas and stadiums. The league was founded in 2009 as the <b>Lingerie Football League</b> and was rebranded as the Legends Football League in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nt100311a_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nt100311a-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-namechange_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-namechange-2">[2]</a></sup> The league's founder and chairman is Mitchell S. Mortaza, and the league's administrative offices are located in Los Angeles.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:312px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lingerie_League_(7354243642).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="667" data-file-width="1000" height="207" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Lingerie_League_%287354243642%29.jpg/310px-Lingerie_League_%287354243642%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Lingerie_League_%287354243642%29.jpg/465px-Lingerie_League_%287354243642%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Lingerie_League_%287354243642%29.jpg/620px-Lingerie_League_%287354243642%29.jpg 2x" width="310"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Referees and players during 2012 All-Fantasy Game, Sydney</div>

<p>The concept of the LFL originated from an alternative <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl" title="Super Bowl">Super Bowl</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halftime" title="Halftime">halftime</a> television special called the <a href="/wiki/Legends_Cup_(LFL)" title="Legends Cup (LFL)">Lingerie Bowl</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> event broadcast opposite the Super Bowl halftime show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbs1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbs1-3">[3]</a></sup> The first three Lingerie Bowls were held annually from 2004 to 2006 and were billed as Lingerie Bowls I, II, and III. From 2007 to 2009, the next three planned Lingerie Bowls (billed as Lingerie Bowls IV, V, and VI) were all cancelled for various reasons. In 2009, LFL chairman Mitch Mortaza expanded the concept from a single annual exhibition game to a ten-team lingerie football league.</p>
<p>Most LFL teams in the United States use the same color scheme as a professional men's football team in the area. Generally, this would be the local <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> teams, although <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Football_League_(2009%E2%80%932012)" title="United Football League (2009–2012)">UFL</a> (<a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Locomotives" title="Las Vegas Locomotives">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Destroyers" title="Virginia Destroyers">Orlando</a>), and former NFL (Los Angeles) teams' color schemes have been used in areas with no current NFL team. In Canada, the teams' colors are based on either <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Football_League" title="Canadian Football League">CFL</a> teams (<a href="/wiki/Toronto_Argonauts" title="Toronto Argonauts">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_Roughriders" title="Saskatchewan Roughriders">Regina</a>) or <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> teams (<a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Canucks" title="Vancouver Canucks">BC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saskatoon_Blades" title="Saskatoon Blades">Saskatoon</a>). As of 2014, some teams in Australia use the local <a href="/wiki/Australian_state_colours" title="Australian state colours">state colors</a>. In addition, a small number of US based have adopted their own color schemes not reflective of other local teams.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Many of the teams are coached by former <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a> players and coaches who are already well known in their respective cities. Many of the players have a background in competitive athletics at the college and semi-pro level, in sports such as <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Softball" title="Softball">softball</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">soccer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Physical_fitness" title="Physical fitness">fitness</a>-style <a href="/wiki/Bodybuilding" title="Bodybuilding">bodybuilding</a>. A few also have experience in tackle football from playing in other semi-pro leagues.</p>
<p>On January 10, 2013, the Lingerie Football League announced it would change its name to the Legends Football League (while still retaining the LFL moniker). The league announced that the athletes would wear "performance apparel" instead of lingerie, but the uniforms look very much the same as before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Business_Insider_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Business_Insider-4">[4]</a></sup> In addition to the new uniforms, redesigned shoulder pads were introduced to provide more protection for players. Other league changes included eliminating images of sexy women from team logos and changing the league tagline from "True Fantasy Football" to "Women of the Gridiron".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-namechange_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-namechange-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rules">Rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Playing style is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Full-contact" title="Full-contact">full-contact</a> and similar to other <a href="/wiki/Indoor_American_football" title="Indoor American football">indoor football</a> leagues. Uniforms consist of shoulder pads, elbow pads, knee pads, performance wear, and <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a>-style <a href="/wiki/Football_helmet" title="Football helmet">helmets</a> with clear plastic visors in lieu of face masks. Prior to the 2013 season, players wore <a href="/wiki/Garter_(stockings)" title="Garter (stockings)">garters</a>, bras, and panties.</p>
<p>There are no <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Field_goal_(American_and_Canadian_football)" title="Field goal (American and Canadian football)">field goals</a> and no <a href="/wiki/Punt_(gridiron_football)" title="Punt (gridiron football)">punts</a>. There is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kickoff_(American_football)" title="Kickoff (American football)">kickoff</a> to start the game and second half.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Philly_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Philly-5">[5]</a></sup> Kicking off after every scoring drive was added before the 2013 Pacific Cup, but was removed shortly after the first game of the LFL Australia season. A team must attempt to get a first <a href="/wiki/Down_(gridiron_football)" title="Down (gridiron football)">down</a> on every fourth down. After a <a href="/wiki/Touchdown" title="Touchdown">touchdown</a>, a team can attempt a one-point conversion from the one-yard line, or a <a href="/wiki/Two-point_conversion" title="Two-point conversion">two-point conversion</a> from the three-yard line. As of 2015, teams are given the option to punt if within their own 10 yard line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lflus.com_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-lflus.com-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are seven women on each side of the 50-yard field, one fewer than the eight players usually found in <a href="/wiki/Arena_football" title="Arena football">arena football</a> or other indoor leagues. Teams consist of 20 players, only 14 of whom are active on game day. This means that there are usually three or four players who play both offense and defense.</p>
<p>The standard offensive formation features 1 <a href="/wiki/Quarterback" title="Quarterback">quarterback</a>, 1 <a href="/wiki/Running_back" title="Running back">running back</a>, 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Center_(American_football)" title="Center (American football)">center</a>, 1 guard, 1 tight end, and 2 <a href="/wiki/Wide_receiver" title="Wide receiver">wide receivers</a>. The standard defensive formation features 2 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defensive_lineman" title="Defensive lineman">defensive linewomen</a>, 1 <a href="/wiki/Linebacker" title="Linebacker">linebackers</a>, 2 <a href="/wiki/Cornerback" title="Cornerback">cornerbacks</a>, and 2 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Safety_(American_football_position)" title="Safety (American football position)">safeties</a> (strong safety and free safety).</p>
<p>The field is 50 yards between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/End_zones" title="End zones">end zones</a>, 30 <a href="/wiki/Yard" title="Yard">yards</a> wide, and the end zones are 8 yards deep, roughly the same as other indoor leagues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News14_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-News14-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>A game consists of four ten-minute quarters and a 12-minute halftime. In the event of a tie, an extra 8 minute quarter is played. If still tied, the game is called a draw, and each team receives a one in the tie column in the standings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lflus.com_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-lflus.com-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_leagues">Global leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Global leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The LFL began in the United States, but in 2012, the LFL launched a companion league in Canada. The LFL also kicked off an LFL league in Australia in December 2013.</p>
<p>The LFL plans to launch a fourth global league – LFL Europa – in 2015. LFL Europa would include teams in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a> (Ireland), <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> (England), <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> (Germany).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> (Spain) and <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a> (Germany) had previously been mentioned as potential franchise cities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lfl_world_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-lfl_world-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, the LFL is planning to have the champions of each of the four proposed global leagues (US, Canada, Australia, and Europe) compete in an inaugural <a href="/wiki/LFL_World_Bowl" title="LFL World Bowl">LFL World Bowl</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, Brazil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lfl_world_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-lfl_world-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The LFL also hopes to launch a Latin American league in the near future which would include six franchises throughout Mexico, Brazil, and Argentina, but this league has no expected start date as of yet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a successful publicity tour in China and Japan, LFL officials are now looking to debut LFL Asia in the near future, although a set date has not been set until LFL Europa and LFL Latin America have been established.</p>
<p>In addition to adding new leagues, the league is planning on combining LFL's US, Canada and Latin America into one big division known as LFL North America. That is planned for some time after LFL Canada resumes and LFL Latin America debuts.</p>
<p>From January 2015 on, there will three global divisions: LFL Americas, with clubs from Canada, Mexico, and the US; LFL Oceania, with clubs from Australia and New Zealand; and LFL Europa, with clubs from Europe. Every four years, there will be an LFL World Bowl, in which two teams from LFL Americas and one team each team from LFL Oceania and LFL Europa will battle in a single elimination tournament. The LFL is looking for franchise owners as the league continues to grow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Global_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Global-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 10, 2015, LFL announced in its State of the Franchise that there would be no international play through 2017. The sole focus will be on growing the league in the US, the exception being international exhibition games to introduce prospective countries to the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Seasons">Seasons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Seasons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.932010">2009–2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2009–2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_LFL_season" title="2009–10 LFL season">2009–10 LFL season</a></div>
<p>Ten teams played in the inaugural <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_LFL_season" title="2009 LFL season">2009–2010 LFL season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The league schedule ran from September 4, 2009, to January 29, 2010, with one game each Friday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nwi8_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-nwi8-14">[14]</a></sup> Teams played one game each against the other four teams in their conference. The top two teams in each conference advanced to the conference championship games held on February 4, 2010, in Miami, and the conference champions played in Lingerie Bowl VII on February 6, 2010. The Western Conference <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Temptation" title="Los Angeles Temptation">Los Angeles Temptation</a> defeated the Eastern Conference <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bliss" title="Chicago Bliss">Chicago Bliss</a> by the score of 27–14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbc4_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-nbc4-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The LFL held its first "All-Fantasy Game" on June 10, 2010, in <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>, Mexico. The Eastern Conference defeated the Western Conference, 36–14. The All-Fantasy Game's co-MVPs were Philadelphia Passion's Tyrah Lusby and Miami Caliente's Anonka Dixon.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.932011">2010–2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2010–2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_LFL_season" title="2010–11 LFL season">2010–11 LFL season</a></div>
<p>For the 2010–2011 season, the LFL added new franchises in <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Fantasy" title="Orlando Fantasy">Orlando</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Charm" title="Baltimore Charm">Baltimore</a>, while the New York Majesty and Denver Dream suspended operations. This kept the number of league teams at 10.</p>
<p>The 2011 Lingerie Bowl was held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas, Nevada</a>, on February 6, 2011. The Western Conference champion Los Angeles Temptation defeated Eastern Conference champion Philadelphia Passion, 26–25.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2011 All-Fantasy Game was held in <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Ontario" title="Hamilton, Ontario">Hamilton, Ontario</a> on July 30, 2011, at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copps_Coliseum" title="Copps Coliseum">Copps Coliseum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Trailing 18–6 in the second half, the Eastern Conference rallied to win, 24–18, over the Western Conference. Anonka Dixon, quarterback for the Orlando Fantasy, was awarded offensive MVP for her 3 touchdown passes and game-winning rushing touchdown, while Liz Gorman, a safety with the Tampa Breeze, was named defensive MVP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.932012">2011–2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2011–2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_LFL_season" title="2011–12 LFL season">2011–12 LFL season</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:125px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lingerie_Football_League_(logo).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="99" data-file-width="123" height="99" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/9/9f/Lingerie_Football_League_%28logo%29.jpg" width="123"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lingerie_Football_League_(logo).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The logo of the Lingerie Football League</div>

<p>The LFL expanded from 10 franchises to 12 for the 2011–2012 season. The LFL accepted five expansion franchises – the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cleveland_Crush" title="Cleveland Crush">Cleveland Crush</a>, <a href="/wiki/Green_Bay_Chill" title="Green Bay Chill">Green Bay Chill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Sin" title="Las Vegas Sin">Las Vegas Sin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Valkyrie" title="Minnesota Valkyrie">Minnesota Valkyrie</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Triumph" title="Toronto Triumph">Toronto Triumph</a>. Meanwhile, three franchises from the previous season – the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Desire" title="Dallas Desire">Dallas Desire</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Seduction" title="San Diego Seduction">San Diego Seduction</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Miami_Caliente" title="Miami Caliente">Miami Caliente</a> – suspended operations.</p>
<p>The season kicked off on August 26, 2011, and culminated with Lingerie Bowl IX on February 4, 2012. The LFL Eastern and Western Conference championship games were played back-to-back on January 28, 2012, at <a href="/wiki/Citizens_Business_Bank_Arena" title="Citizens Business Bank Arena">Citizens Business Bank Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ontario,_California" title="Ontario, California">Ontario, California</a>. For the second straight year, the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Temptation" title="Los Angeles Temptation">Los Angeles Temptation</a> won the Western Conference championship while the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Passion" title="Philadelphia Passion">Philadelphia Passion</a> won the Eastern Conference championship, setting up a rematch in the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Legends_Cup_(LFL)" title="Legends Cup (LFL)">Lingerie Bowl</a> of the previous year's championship game. The 2012 Lingerie Bowl was played at <a href="/wiki/Orleans_Arena" title="Orleans Arena">Orleans Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, in the afternoon prior to the start of <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVI" title="Super Bowl XLVI">Super Bowl XLVI</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The Los Angeles Temptation won its third consecutive Lingerie Bowl with a 28–6 victory over Philadelphia Passion. The Temptation's Ashley Salerno and Amber Reed were co-MVP's, with Salerno throwing three touchdown passes and Reed scoring two rushing touchdowns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, the LFL expanded its annual "All-Fantasy" game into a three-game international series, with one game in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and two games in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The Mexico All-Fantasy Game took place on May 5, 2012, at the <a href="/wiki/Palacio_de_los_Deportes" title="Palacio de los Deportes">Palacio de los Deportes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>; the Western Conference defeated the Eastern Conference, 37–7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The second match of the "2012 LFL All-Fantasy Tour" took place in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brisbane,_Australia" title="Brisbane, Australia">Brisbane, Australia</a>, on June 2, 2012, at the <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_Convention_%26_Exhibition_Centre" title="Brisbane Convention &amp; Exhibition Centre">Brisbane Convention &amp; Exhibition Centre</a>; the Western Conference again emerged victorious, this time by a 45–36 score.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The Western Conference took a 3–0 series sweep with a 31–24 victory in the third and final "All-Fantasy" match in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney,_Australia" title="Sydney, Australia">Sydney, Australia</a>, on June 9, 2012, at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allphones_Arena" title="Allphones Arena">Allphones Arena</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LA_Temptation_vs_Seattle_Mist.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="911" data-file-width="1372" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/LA_Temptation_vs_Seattle_Mist.jpg/260px-LA_Temptation_vs_Seattle_Mist.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/LA_Temptation_vs_Seattle_Mist.jpg/390px-LA_Temptation_vs_Seattle_Mist.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/LA_Temptation_vs_Seattle_Mist.jpg/520px-LA_Temptation_vs_Seattle_Mist.jpg 2x" width="260"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2013_LFL_US_season" title="2013 LFL US season">2013 LFL US season</a></div>
<p>The league delayed the next LFL US season from fall/winter 2012 to April 2013 in order to shift to a spring/summer schedule and to focus on the 2012 LFL Canada season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the 2013 season, the LFL accepted two expansion franchises – the <a href="/wiki/Omaha_Heart" title="Omaha Heart">Omaha Heart</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Steam" title="Atlanta Steam">Atlanta Steam</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Triumph" title="Toronto Triumph">Toronto Triumph</a> switched leagues, joining LFL Canada for its inaugural <a href="/wiki/2012_LFL_Canada_season" title="2012 LFL Canada season">2012 season</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Fantasy" title="Orlando Fantasy">Orlando Fantasy</a> officially suspended operations, again leaving the league at 12 teams. The Tampa Breeze relocated to <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">Jacksonville, Florida</a> and were renamed the <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville_Breeze" title="Jacksonville Breeze">Jacksonville Breeze</a>. The league also aligned their twelve teams into four three-team divisions for the 2013 season.</p>
<p>The 2013 LFL US season kicked off on March 30, 2013. Divisional championship games were held on August 17, 2013, and the conference championship games were held on August 24, 2013, in <a href="/wiki/Ontario,_California" title="Ontario, California">Ontario, California</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Passion" title="Philadelphia Passion">Philadelphia Passion</a> advanced to their third straight <a href="/wiki/Legends_Cup_(LFL)" title="Legends Cup (LFL)">Legends Cup</a> (formerly the Lingerie Bowl) by winning the Eastern Conference championship, while the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bliss" title="Chicago Bliss">Chicago Bliss</a> claimed the Western Conference title. The 2013 Legends Cup was held at <a href="/wiki/Orleans_Arena" title="Orleans Arena">Orleans Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, on September 1, 2013, with the Chicago Bliss triumphing, 38–14.</p>
<p>The Pacific Cup was once again played at ShoWare Center on December 6, 2013. This time the Seattle Mist would face the Los Angeles Temptation due to the cancellation of the 2013 Canada season and not enough preparation from the BC Angels. Several key players from the Angels joined the Mist while a few key players from Las Vegas Sin joined the Temptation. The Mist won by a score of 27–25.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2014_LFL_US_season" title="2014 LFL US season">2014 LFL US season</a></div>
<p>In the 2013 offseason, the LFL announced that it had temporarily suspended operations for the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Passion" title="Philadelphia Passion">Philadelphia Passion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Valkyrie" title="Minnesota Valkyrie">Minnesota Valkyrie</a> franchises, both of which are not competing in the 2014 LFL US season. The Philadelphia Passion are expected to return in 2015 under new leadership, while the Minnesota Valkyrie is not returning due to LFL USA's new stadium business model structure. The earliest expected return for Minnesota is 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cleveland_Crush" title="Cleveland Crush">Cleveland Crush</a> is moving to <a href="/wiki/Toledo,_Ohio" title="Toledo, Ohio">Toledo, Ohio</a> and play its home games at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huntington_Center_(Toledo)" title="Huntington Center (Toledo)">Huntington Center</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2015_LFL_US_season" title="2015 LFL US season">2015 LFL US season</a></div>
<p>The 2015 season took place from April to September, ending with the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Legends_Cup_(LFL)" title="Legends Cup (LFL)">Legends Cup</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mist" title="Seattle Mist">Seattle Mist</a> won the title 27-21, defeating the two-time champion <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bliss" title="Chicago Bliss">Chicago Bliss</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On September 16, it was announced that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austin,Texas" title="Austin,Texas">Austin,Texas</a> would receive an expansion team for 2016. It was later announced the team would be called the Austin Acoustic. It was announced at the same time that they would replace the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Sin" title="Las Vegas Sin">Las Vegas Sin</a>, with the Sin being place on suspension. They will play at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cedar_Park_Center" title="Cedar Park Center">Cedar Park Center</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On November 2, it was announced that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas, Texas</a> would rejoin the league in 2016 after being on hiatus since the end of the 2010–2011 season, retaining the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Desire" title="Dallas Desire">Dallas Desire</a> name. The Desire will play alongside the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mist" title="Seattle Mist">Seattle Mist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Temptation" title="Los Angeles Temptation">Los Angeles Temptation</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Austin_Acoustic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Austin Acoustic (page does not exist)">Austin Acoustic</a> in the Western Conference. They will play at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._Pepper_Arena" title="Dr. Pepper Arena">Dr. Pepper Arena</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On November 12, the league announced a final team for the 2016 season, playing in New England. it was announced they would be named the New England Liberty. They will join the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bliss" title="Chicago Bliss">Chicago Bliss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Steam" title="Atlanta Steam">Atlanta Steam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Omaha_Heart" title="Omaha Heart">Omaha Heart</a> in the Eastern Conference. They will play at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verizon_Wireless_Arena" title="Verizon Wireless Arena">Verizon Wireless Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On December 4, The LFL All-Fantasy Game returned after a 3-year hiatus and took place at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Estadio_Nacional_Mateo_Flores&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Estadio Nacional Mateo Flores (page does not exist)">Estadio Nacional Mateo Flores</a> in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>. The Eastern Conference defeated the Western Conference 39-19.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Seasons_2">Seasons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Seasons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2012_LFL_Canada_season" title="2012 LFL Canada season">2012 LFL Canada season</a></div>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_LFL_season" title="2011–12 LFL season">2011–12 LFL United States season</a>, the All-Fantasy Game was played in <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Ontario" title="Hamilton, Ontario">Hamilton, Ontario</a>. This was due in part to the league's announcement that in 2012 there would be a Canadian Lingerie Football League. LFL Canada was originally scheduled for a twelve-week season with teams in six markets,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lfl360.com_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-lfl360.com-31">[31]</a></sup> with the first regular season game on August 25, 2012, culminating with Lingerie Bowl I Canada on November 17, 2012, one week before the <a href="/wiki/100th_Grey_Cup" title="100th Grey Cup">100th Grey Cup</a>.</p>
<p>On September 28, 2011, it was announced that, in addition to the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Triumph" title="Toronto Triumph">Toronto Triumph</a> who began play in LFL United States, LFL Canada's other five markets would consist of <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calgary" title="Calgary">Calgary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edmonton" title="Edmonton">Edmonton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec_City" title="Quebec City">Quebec City</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>; ironically, none of those five markets would actually get an LFL franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lfl_canada_teams_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-lfl_canada_teams-32">[32]</a></sup> On February 9, 2012, the LFL announced that <a href="/wiki/Abbotsford,_British_Columbia" title="Abbotsford, British Columbia">Abbotsford, British Columbia</a>, would be the next Canadian city to host a team in the League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The franchise played its home games at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abbotsford_Entertainment_%26_Sports_Centre" title="Abbotsford Entertainment &amp; Sports Centre">Abbotsford Entertainment &amp; Sports Centre</a> and competed in the Western Division of LFL Canada. On February 20, 2012, the LFL announced that the franchise would be named the <a href="/wiki/BC_Angels" title="BC Angels">BC Angels</a> following the results of an online fan vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The decision for Abbotsford to host a team sparked some controversy, including expressed concern from at least one city councilor, as "Abbotsford is a deeply religious agricultural community."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lfl_team_in_Abbotsford_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-lfl_team_in_Abbotsford-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 22, 2012, the LFL announced that <a href="/wiki/Regina,_Saskatchewan" title="Regina, Saskatchewan">Regina, Saskatchewan</a> would be the next Canadian city to host a team in the League. Home games would be played at the <a href="/wiki/Brandt_Centre" title="Brandt Centre">Brandt Centre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-regina_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-regina-36">[36]</a></sup> On March 6, the LFL announced that the franchise would be named the <a href="/wiki/Regina_Rage" title="Regina Rage">Regina Rage</a> after an online fan vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reginarage_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-reginarage-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Six days later, on February 28, the LFL announced that <a href="/wiki/Saskatoon" title="Saskatoon">Saskatoon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a> would join Regina as LFL Canada's second team in the Wheat Province. Home games would be played at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Credit_Union_Centre" title="Credit Union Centre">Credit Union Centre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saskatoon_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-saskatoon-38">[38]</a></sup> Upon releasing the <a href="/wiki/Saskatoon_Sirens" title="Saskatoon Sirens">Saskatoon Sirens</a>' logo and colors, the league announced that LFL Canada was set at four teams for the 2012 season, with the league playing an eight-game schedule, scheduled to end with Lingerie Bowl I Canada the week before the Grey Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2012 LFL Canada season began on August 25, 2012 and culminated with Lingerie Bowl Canada I on November 17, 2012, between the <a href="/wiki/Saskatoon_Sirens" title="Saskatoon Sirens">Saskatoon Sirens</a> and the <a href="/wiki/BC_Angels" title="BC Angels">BC Angels</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abbotsford_Entertainment_%26_Sports_Centre" title="Abbotsford Entertainment &amp; Sports Centre">Abbotsford Entertainment &amp; Sports Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Abbotsford,_British_Columbia" title="Abbotsford, British Columbia">Abbotsford, British Columbia</a>. The BC Angels won the inaugural championship game 25–12 with BC Angels' quarterback Mary Anne Hanson and receiver Aleesa Garcia named as the game's MVPs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 15, 2012, the first annual Pacific Cup was played between the Seattle Mist of LFL US and the BC Angels of LFL Canada. Dubbed the 'Border War', the game was hosted by the Seattle Mist at the <a href="/wiki/ShoWare_Center" title="ShoWare Center">ShoWare Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kent,_Washington" title="Kent, Washington">Kent, Washington</a>, with home venues alternating each season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The Mist won 38-18.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_2">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2013_LFL_Canada_season" title="2013 LFL Canada season">2013 LFL Canada season</a></div>
<p>On June 27, 2013, the 2013 LFL Canada schedule was released. LFL Canada accepted one proposed expansion team, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calgary,_Alberta" title="Calgary, Alberta">Calgary Fillies</a>; their home games would be played at the <a href="/wiki/Stampede_Corral" title="Stampede Corral">Stampede Corral</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Meanwhile, the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Triumph" title="Toronto Triumph">Toronto Triumph</a> suspended operations for the 2013 season, bringing the number of LFL Canada teams back to four.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> On September 16, 2013, the LFL officially postponed the entire 2013 LFL Canada season until 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, the BC Angels were expected to participate in the second-annual "Pacific Cup", an exhibition game between the Angels and LFL US's <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mist" title="Seattle Mist">Seattle Mist</a>. The 2013 contest was scheduled for December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> On October 3, it was announced that the Angels would be replaced in the game by the LA Temptation, citing not enough preparation. However, a few key players from the Angels would make the trip to Seattle to join the Mist. A couple of them are staying to play with the Mist for their 2014 Season.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>Australia hosted two games of the 2012 LFL All-Fantasy Game Tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Two exhibition games between the Eastern and Western Conferences took place on Australia's east coast in <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>. <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a> native and wide receiver for the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Temptation" title="Los Angeles Temptation">Los Angeles Temptation</a> <a href="/wiki/Chloe_Butler" title="Chloe Butler">Chloe Butler</a> served as the ambassador of LFL Football coming to Australia and captained the Western Conference squad.</p>
<p>LFL Australia premiered in December 2013. The New South Wales Surge, Queensland Brigade, Victoria Maidens, and Western Australia Angels were the four teams playing in the inaugural 2013–14 LFL Australia season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315">2014–15</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: 2014–15">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On March 23, 2014, LFL Australia accepted one proposed expansion team, the Adelaide Arsenal their home games would be played at the <a href="/wiki/Hindmarsh_Stadium" title="Hindmarsh Stadium">Coopers Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> However, on September 29 the 2014–2015 season was cancelled due to the lack of a broadcast partner with competition planned to resume in 2015–2016 including an expansion into New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2015, it was revealed that the players and coaches had actually left after disputes and issues with LFL management, namely Commissioner Mortaza. Players and coaches left and joined the <a href="/wiki/Ladies_Gridiron_League" title="Ladies Gridiron League">Ladies Gridiron League</a>, originally a subsidiary of the LFL now operating as its own league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasts">Broadcasts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Broadcasts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 2009–2010, weekly games were shot in 1080i high-definition and broadcast on some <a href="/wiki/MyNetworkTV" title="MyNetworkTV">MyNetworkTV</a> affiliate stations, international stations, and online.</p>
<p>In 2010, <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a> licensed the broadcast rights to 20 regular season and two conference playoff games and aired highlights of those games on a program entitled <i>LFL Presents: LFL, Friday Night Football on MTV2</i>. For the 2011–12 season, MTV2 also broadcast the championship game, in addition to presenting the games in their entirety and broadcasting them live at 9:00 PM ET.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, the league began airing their games exclusively online on a week-delayed basis, releasing their games every Saturday on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. Games are shot in 1080i.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the start of the 2013–2014 Australia season, LFL signed an agreement with <a href="/wiki/7mate" title="7mate">7mate</a> in Australia to broadcast games every Saturday night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, <a href="/wiki/Fuse_(TV_channel)" title="Fuse (TV channel)">Fuse</a> became the exclusive broadcaster of LFL games in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Games are broadcast on a week-delay basis on Saturday nights, and are later uploaded to YouTube on Fridays. In the fall, <a href="/wiki/Oxygen_(TV_channel)" title="Oxygen (TV channel)">Oxygen Media</a> premiered the reality series, <i>Pretty. Strong.</i>, which focuses on the lives and careers of the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bliss" title="Chicago Bliss">Chicago Bliss</a>. The show was produced by Relativity Television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the 2009–2010 season, the Legends Football League has partnered with Five Stone music to compose music for the weekly game highlights, commercials, sound effects, radio shows, and music bed for the games on MTV2 and the international TV stations.</p>
<p>The LFL theme song was originally composed by Five Stone in a collaboration with the vocalist Piper from the band Flipsyde. The LFL theme song changed in 2013 to "The Chosen" by Iowa-based group Drenalin.</p>
<p>Starting with the 2013 Australia season, the LFL has dropped "The Chosen" and opted to go with cold openings with dialogue instead of music.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fantasy_football">Fantasy football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Fantasy football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, the Legends Football League partnered with <a href="/wiki/Fantazzle" title="Fantazzle">Fantazzle Fantasy Sports</a> to present a <a href="/wiki/Fantasy_football_(American)" title="Fantasy football (American)">fantasy football</a> game for the LFL.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>It lasted for a year, before being shutdown and repackaged in 2013 as LFL PartyDeck,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> a social networking site exclusively for LFL fans, players, and coaches.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_game">Video game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Video game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, the Legends Football League teamed with Japanese-based <a href="/wiki/Yuke%27s" title="Yuke's">Yuke's</a> Co. Ltd to design and develop an official LFL game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> The video game still does not have a firm release date.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The league has met with criticism. Critics say the league degrades female athletes through "pernicious objectification".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> The league was accused in its earlier years of fining players for wearing too many clothes, as well as not paying medical bills for injured players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> However, the league has responded, claiming that fines were given because the equipment worn was in direct conflict with the league's sponsored gear (e.g. Rawlings and Cutters, at the time).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Following the rebranding, the league now allows players to wear other clothing (bras, panties/thongs, extra padding, medical wraps) under the uniform.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Players are still required to pay for their own medical insurance and care.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The league also prohibited players from commenting on personnel matters, a rule that prompted the vast majority of the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Triumph" title="Toronto Triumph">Toronto Triumph</a>, including team captain Krista Ford, to quit in protest in October 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> The league claims that that policy has also changed since the rebranding, allowing players to express changes and ideas to keep the league functioning.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Originally a professional league with players receiving a cut of net revenue, Mortaza stopped paying his players beginning in the 2011 season and converted the league into an amateur organization; players must also pay for their own health insurance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> League founder Mortaza admitted at one point the league was marketed toward "mostly beer-drinking college students aged 21 and up."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> The league has since attempted to reach a broader audience, including couples, kids, and families.</p>
<p>Some players have responded ambivalently or positively to the uniforms, with one quoted as saying "I just appreciate playing football, I don’t care what they put me in", and another favoring the limited clothing, saying "It's more comfortable this way."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blaze329_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-blaze329-70">[70]</a></sup> LFL players with experience in <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a> competitions have noted that the typical LFL uniform is comparable to other uniforms, with Elizabeth Govrick of the Minnesota Valkyrie stating she "ran track and I was wearing, you know, stuff if not close to almost smaller than what I’m wearing out on the football field. You take beach volleyball, you take volleyball, you take other sports where it's pretty much the same thing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> Adrian Purnell of the Jacksonville Breeze said that the outfits are only for marketing purposes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other players are quoted as viewing the uniforms as a necessary evil, with one saying "Maybe one day, girls won't have to wear lingerie to get people interested [in women's football]."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> Liz Gorman, a player with the Jacksonville Breeze, stated she'd rather wear a conventional uniform: "I mean, I don't like it. You'd rather wear full clothing. I have a bunch of scrapes on me." She also indicated a belief that the league is likely to change to conventional uniforms in the future: "You look back at basketball, you used to have to wear skirts. Obviously it's changed, they have the WNBA now. So if you look back, women's sports has constantly evolved and I think that this sports league is going to end up changing the uniform."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>Even before uniform changes occurred during the rebranding of the league in 2013, players defended their ability to protect themselves from being objectified. Abbie Sullivan of the Cleveland Crush stated, "just because we play in the LFL does not mean we promote promiscuity. We are smart females. We are athletes who take care of our bodies. We are the total package." Similarly, Toledo Crush player Marija Condric stated, "Everyone is entitled to their own opinion. Sit down and watch. You’ll forget what we are wearing and you will be thinking about how hard that girl got hit on that last play."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-womensviewsonnews.org_75-0"><a href="#cite_note-womensviewsonnews.org-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uniforms">Uniforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Uniforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Along with the rebrand of the league in 2013, the league made the decision to change the uniforms. Before, the uniforms in the Lingerie Football League consisted of an athletic bra and underwear that were more revealing than protective. The uniforms had lace and ribbons and the athletes all had to wear garter belts. The league traded these uniforms in for "performance wear."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Business_Insider_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Business_Insider-4">[4]</a></sup> The league felt like the uniforms were too heavy on sex appeal. In accordance with the name change, they thought it was appropriate to also change the uniforms. The new uniforms resemble the uniforms worn by volleyball players.</p>
<p>While the US remained with the old uniforms for the 2013 season, minus the ribbons and garters, LFL Australia's debut season in the beginning of 2014 marked the debut of the new "performance wear" uniforms. Uniforms replaced ribbons with shoelaces on the front of the top and bottom.</p>
<p>The new uniforms made their debut in the US for the 2014 season. In addition to the shoelaces, the US uniforms feature brighter, more vibrant colors for the teams, with some teams getting new color schemes for the new season. For the most part, LFL has done away with white color away uniforms starting this season, with both away and home teams sporting what could be considered home color uniforms.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> White uniforms returned for the 2015 season, but only for certain teams.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In partnership with Sleefs, LFL announced new uniforms with brand new colors for the upcoming 2016 season. Numbers and logos are now printed on the uniform as opposed to previously being sewn on. In addition, the league also announced that teams will have a choice of camo or black alternates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LFL_Official_Facebook_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-LFL_Official_Facebook-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
<p>The uniforms have received extensive criticism for their revealing nature. In a recent ESPN article, Sarah Spain, the co-host of espnW, wrote, "After watching these women play, I can honestly say I respect the heck out of them as athletes, but I'll still never respect the Lingerie Football League, no matter what name they give it." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> Many of the players also have issues with the uniforms. Heather Furr, a quarterback for the Chicago Bliss said "I think with little girls watching the game ... if they can see past the uniform and see us as role models, then that's what I want."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Safety">Safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legends_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Legends_Football_League_Australia_-_(Victoria_Maidens_vs_NSW_Surge)_(11888877965).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3665" data-file-width="3257" height="270" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Legends_Football_League_Australia_-_%28Victoria_Maidens_vs_NSW_Surge%29_%2811888877965%29.jpg/240px-Legends_Football_League_Australia_-_%28Victoria_Maidens_vs_NSW_Surge%29_%2811888877965%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Legends_Football_League_Australia_-_%28Victoria_Maidens_vs_NSW_Surge%29_%2811888877965%29.jpg/360px-Legends_Football_League_Australia_-_%28Victoria_Maidens_vs_NSW_Surge%29_%2811888877965%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Legends_Football_League_Australia_-_%28Victoria_Maidens_vs_NSW_Surge%29_%2811888877965%29.jpg/480px-Legends_Football_League_Australia_-_%28Victoria_Maidens_vs_NSW_Surge%29_%2811888877965%29.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Legends_Football_League_Australia_-_(Victoria_Maidens_vs_NSW_Surge)_(11888877965).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>The league has received many complaints from players in regards to safety. Since the uniforms cover very little skin, the players are very susceptible to injury. Ex-lingerie football player Nikki Johnson was one of the many players that experienced injuries in her time in the league. Those injuries included injuries sustained from hard hits and a broken wrist that required surgery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> She, like many other players, believed the league gave very little protection to the women by providing them with weak shoulder pads and weak helmets. Due to lack of coverage around torso, arms, and legs, they can sustain scrapes and bruises very easily.</p>
<p>As part of the reformation and brand change, LFL and partner Rawlings changed the shoulder pads to be more protective, including a harder plastic shell to cover the shoulders instead of just a pad. In addition, the helmets were given more padding inside and the addition of a concussion meter, meant to go off and alert the trainer of a head injury, regardless of the players saying that they are fine. This putting into place a system where, if a player goes out of action for a head injury, they must pass a physical and mental test before being allowed back on the playing field.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>At the start of the 2014 US season, LFL unveiled new helmets by Bauer, designed with a harder outer shell and more padding than the CCM helmets used the past 4 years. They are still equipped with the concussion meters. Chinstraps, while still by Russell, are thicker and have more padding.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The shoulder pads were remodeled again for the 2014 season, with more padding given to the shoulders and a thicker plate given to the chest pad. They also introduced new sturdier elbow and knee pads, in addition to new gloves designed by Champion Gloves, replacing Cutters as the leagues official glove.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
