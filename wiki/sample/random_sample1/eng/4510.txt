<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Reginald Alec Martin</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Reginald Alec Martin</b> (1908–1971) was a <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> author of a variety of children's series and other novels. He usually wrote under a pseudonym, including <b>E. C. Eliott</b> (or <b>E. C. Eliot</b>) and <b>Rex Dixon</b>.</p>
<p>As Eliott he wrote the <a href="/wiki/Kemlo" title="Kemlo">Kemlo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tas_(series)" title="Tas (series)">Tas</a> series of books. As Dixon he wrote the <a href="/wiki/Pocomoto" title="Pocomoto">Pocomoto</a> series of books. His other books for children included the Joey series, the Ginger Pennylove series, and the Dance and Co. Detectives series (<i>The Mystery of...</i>), under the name of Robert Martin.</p>
<p>In 1954 he wrote <i>The Wheel in the Sky</i>, a science fiction novel about the building of Earth's first man-made satellite, published under the pseudonym Rafe Bernard.</p>
<p>He wrote westerns for adults under the names Hank McCoy, Brett Cameron, Scott Martin, and Tex Bancroft.</p>
<p>He also wrote series books based on the popular 1960s television shows <i><a href="/wiki/Daktari" title="Daktari">Daktari</a></i> (as Frank Denver), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Invaders" title="The Invaders">The Invaders</a></i> (as Rafe Bernard), and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Girl_from_U.N.C.L.E." title="The Girl from U.N.C.L.E.">The Girl from U.N.C.L.E.</a></i> (as Simon Latter).</p>
