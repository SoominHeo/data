<p><b>Lê Khả Phiêu</b> (born 27 December 1931 at Đông Khê Commune, Đông Sơn District, <a href="/wiki/Thanh_H%C3%B3a_Province" title="Thanh Hóa Province">Thanh Hóa Province</a>) was the General Secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Vietnam" title="Communist Party of Vietnam">Communist Party of Vietnam</a> from December 1997 to April 2001 and has a military background.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gb_143_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gb_143-1">[1]</a></sup> He served in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vietnam_People%27s_Army" title="Vietnam People's Army">Vietnam People's Army</a> during the <a href="/wiki/First_Indochina_War" title="First Indochina War">First</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Indochina_War" title="Second Indochina War">Second Indochina Wars</a>, join in Cambodian war, the head of the General political Department of the People's Army.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He has usually been seen as a conservative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> However, this categorization has been challenged by historian Martin Gainsborough, who notes that Phiêu made some remarkably outspoken comments about problems in the party before the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenth_National_Congress_of_the_Communist_Party_of_Vietnam" title="Tenth National Congress of the Communist Party of Vietnam">Tenth Party Congress</a>. He criticized what he called 'illness of partyization' (<i>bệnh đảng hoá</i>), meaning that the Party controls everything.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Phiêu was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prot%C3%A9g%C3%A9" title="Protégé">protégé</a> of his predecessor, <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BB%97_M%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Di" title="Đỗ Mười">Đỗ Mười</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He was elevated to the Politburo in the early 1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
