<p><b>Leffe</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span></span>/</a></span></span> is a premium beer <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brand</a> owned by <a href="/wiki/InBev" title="InBev">InBev Belgium</a>, the European operating arm of the global <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anheuser%E2%80%93Busch_InBev" title="Anheuser–Busch InBev">Anheuser–Busch InBev</a> brewery giant. There are several beers in the range, and they are marketed as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abbey_beer" title="Abbey beer">Abbey beers</a>. They are brewed in large quantities and are widely distributed.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leffe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The abbey of Leffe was founded in 1152 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meuse_River" title="Meuse River">Meuse River</a> in the province of <a href="/wiki/Namur_(province)" title="Namur (province)">Namur</a> in southern Belgium. Like many monasteries across Europe, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Premonstratensian" title="Premonstratensian">Premonstratensian</a> (Norbertine) canons of the abbey brewed ale, starting in 1240.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Using knowledge passed from generation to generation and ingredients found in the wild near the abbey, the canons developed a unique ale with a subtle taste and high alcohol content, brewed only at the abbey.</p>
<p>The abbey has been damaged by both natural and human circumstances over the years: it was destroyed by a flood in 1460, a fire swept through the settlement in 1466, billeted troops damaged the brewery in 1735, and the outbreak of the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a> in 1794 resulted in it being abandoned and the brewery destroyed. The <a href="/wiki/Canon_(priest)" title="Canon (priest)">canons</a> returned in 1902.</p>
<p>In 1952 the production of beer was continued after a partnership with the <a href="/wiki/Flemish_Region" title="Flemish Region">Flemish</a> based Lootvoet brewery in <a href="/wiki/Overijse" title="Overijse">Overijse</a>. This brewery was later bought by the international beer company <a href="/wiki/Interbrew" title="Interbrew">Interbrew</a> (now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AB_InBev" title="AB InBev">AB InBev</a>). Leffe was then brewed in <a href="/wiki/Mont-Saint-Guibert" title="Mont-Saint-Guibert">Mont-Saint-Guibert</a> until Interbrew closed that brewery. Now all Leffe brands are brewed at the <a href="/wiki/Stella_Artois" title="Stella Artois">Stella Artois</a> brewery in <a href="/wiki/Leuven" title="Leuven">Leuven</a>.</p>
<p>The 1952 agreement between the Leffe abbey and a commercial brewery is said to have been the first of its kind (royalties continue to be paid to the abbey). Today, Belgium's beer category called "abbey" is thriving - with several beers brewed under similar licences to Leffe as well as abbey beers named after abbey ruins or abbeys that no longer exist. The <a href="/wiki/Heineken_brands#Affligem_Brewery" title="Heineken brands">Affligem</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grimbergen_(beer)" title="Grimbergen (beer)">Grimbergen</a> beers are a part of <a href="/wiki/Heineken_International" title="Heineken International">Heineken's</a> international portfolio. Other notable abbey brands include <a href="/wiki/Corsendonk" title="Corsendonk">Corsendonk</a>.</p>
<p>The Leffe museum in the town of <a href="/wiki/Dinant" title="Dinant">Dinant</a> is open to visitors.</p>
