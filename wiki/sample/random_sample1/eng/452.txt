<p><b><a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">Grenada</a></b> competed at the <b><a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> from 27 July to 12 August 2012. This was the nation's eighth consecutive appearance at the Olympics. Few weeks before the Games, Grenadian athletes trained at <a href="/wiki/Broadbridge_Heath" title="Broadbridge Heath">Broadbridge Heath</a> Leisure Centre near <a href="/wiki/Horsham" title="Horsham">Horsham</a>, West Sussex.</p>
<p>Grenada Olympic Committee sent the nation's largest delegation to the Games, with a total of 10 athletes (6 men and 4 women), competing in 4 different sports. Taekwondo jin <a href="/wiki/Andrea_St._Bernard" title="Andrea St. Bernard">Andrea St. Bernard</a>, who competed for the nation's Olympic debut in her sport, was the oldest athlete of the team, at age 32. Track runner and world junior champion <a href="/wiki/Kirani_James" title="Kirani James">Kirani James</a>, the youngest athlete at age 19, was Grenada's flag bearer at the opening ceremony, and subsequently, became the nation's first Olympic medalist when he took the gold medal in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_400_metres" title="Athletics at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's 400 metres">Men's 400 metres</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics">Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grenada_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Athletics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 2012 Summer Olympics">Athletics at the 2012 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Athletes from Grenada have so far achieved qualifying standards in the following athletics events (up to a maximum of 3 athletes in each event at the 'A' Standard, and 1 at the 'B' Standard):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Men.27s_100_m">Men's 100 m</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grenada_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Men's 100 m">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Paul_Williams_(athlete)" title="Paul Williams (athlete)">Paul Williams</a> was the only Grenadan participant in the 2012 Olympic <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_100_metres" title="Athletics at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's 100 metres">Men's 100m sprint</a> event. He participated in the fifth heat of the 4 August qualification match against seven other competitors, including Jamaican finalist <a href="/wiki/Asafa_Powell" title="Asafa Powell">Asafa Powell</a>. Williams ran his event in 10.65 seconds, ranking seventh place. He completed the event 0.61 seconds slower than Jamaican runner <a href="/wiki/Asafa_Powell" title="Asafa Powell">Asafa Powell</a>, who ranked first in the heat. Overall, Williams ranked 54th out of the 65 athletes who ranked in the qualification round. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryan_Bailey_(athlete)" title="Ryan Bailey (athlete)">Ryan Bailey</a> of the United States of America, who ranked first in the qualification round, was 0.77 seconds faster than Williams, who did not advance to further rounds.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Men.27s_400_m">Men's 400 m</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grenada_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Men's 400 m">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Grenada sent two athletes, <a href="/wiki/Rondell_Bartholomew" title="Rondell Bartholomew">Rondell Bartholomew</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kirani_James" title="Kirani James">Kirani James</a>, as part of their delegation to represent it in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_400_metres" title="Athletics at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's 400 metres">Men's 400m sprint</a>. James won the country's first Olympic gold in a personal best of 43.94 seconds. He becomes the eighth fastest man in history over one lap and sets a new national record. Bartholomew did pull out of the race, so he didn't progress to do the heat.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Women.27s_200_m">Women's 200 m</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grenada_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Women's 200 m">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Grenada sent <a href="/wiki/Janelle_Redhead" title="Janelle Redhead">Janelle Redhead</a>, as part of their delegation to represent it in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_200_metres" title="Athletics at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's 200 metres">Women's 200m sprint</a>. Redhead participated in the second heat during the 6 August qualification round, ranking third of nine athletes. Redhead earned a time of 23.08  seconds. Overall, Redhead ranked 22nd<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="Should this be '22nd', '20th' or something else? (September 2013)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> of 57 athletes. She did progress to the quarterfinals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Women.27s_400_m">Women's 400 m</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grenada_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Women's 400 m">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kanika_Beckles" title="Kanika Beckles">Kanika Beckles</a> was the only Grenadan participant in the 2012 Olympic <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_400_metres" title="Athletics at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's 400 metres">women's 400m sprint</a> event. Beckles did not start the race, so she didn't progress to do the heat</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Swimming">Swimming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grenada_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Swimming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Swimming at the 2012 Summer Olympics">Swimming at the 2012 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Esau Simpson was the sole Grenadan participant in the 2012 Olympic Men's 100 metre freestyle. He participated in the second heat of the 31 July qualification round. Simpson finished his event in 53.26 seconds, ranking first among the other competitors in his heat. Overall, Simpson was ranked 43rd, of the 49 athletes who swam in the qualification round. As a result, Esau Simpson did not advance to any further rounds of competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
