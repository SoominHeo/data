<p><i><b>Rectify</b></i> is an American television drama series based on the life of a man after he is released from prison after a wrongful conviction and long sentence. It was created by <a href="/wiki/Ray_McKinnon_(actor)" title="Ray McKinnon (actor)">Ray McKinnon</a> and is the first original series from <a href="/wiki/SundanceTV" title="SundanceTV">SundanceTV</a>. It stars <a href="/wiki/Aden_Young" title="Aden Young">Aden Young</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abigail_Spencer" title="Abigail Spencer">Abigail Spencer</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._Smith-Cameron" title="J. Smith-Cameron">J. Smith-Cameron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Clemens" title="Adelaide Clemens">Adelaide Clemens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clayne_Crawford" title="Clayne Crawford">Clayne Crawford</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luke_Kirby" title="Luke Kirby">Luke Kirby</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-premiere_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The series premiered on April 22, 2013 with a first season run of six episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-premiere_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-2">[2]</a></sup> On May 1, 2013, SundanceTV ordered a second season of ten episodes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-renewal_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-renewal-3">[3]</a></sup> which premiered on June 19, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> A third season consisting of six episodes premiered on July 9, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S3Premiere_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-S3Premiere-5">[5]</a></sup> Prior to its third-season premiere, <i>Rectify</i> was renewed for a fourth season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> later confirmed to be the final season, which premiered on October 26, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rectify&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Daniel Holden was imprisoned as a teenager for the rape and murder of his 16-year-old girlfriend, Hanna. After nineteen years on <a href="/wiki/Death_row" title="Death row">death row</a>, analysis of <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a> evidence from his trial contradicts the prosecution's case, and an appeals court <a href="/wiki/Vacated_judgment" title="Vacated judgment">vacates</a> the judgment of his original trial. Daniel is allowed to return to his hometown of Paulie, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deadline_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-deadline-9">[9]</a></sup> The subsequent adjustments and events in the lives of Daniel, his extended family, and the townsfolk are explored as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">character</a> study in a slowly unfolding <a href="/wiki/Southern_Gothic" title="Southern Gothic">Southern Gothic</a> story.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast_and_characters">Cast and characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rectify&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast and characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_cast">Main cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rectify&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Aden_Young" title="Aden Young">Aden Young</a> as Daniel Holden, convicted for the rape and strangulation murder of his teenage girlfriend Hanna but later released from prison after the discovery of conflicting DNA evidence.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Abigail_Spencer" title="Abigail Spencer">Abigail Spencer</a> as Amantha Holden, Daniel's younger sister who has always believed in his innocence.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/J._Smith-Cameron" title="J. Smith-Cameron">J. Smith-Cameron</a> as Janet Talbot, Daniel's mother.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Clemens" title="Adelaide Clemens">Adelaide Clemens</a> as Tawney Talbot, Teddy's wife, and Daniel, Amantha, and Jared's deeply religious sister-in-law.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Clayne_Crawford" title="Clayne Crawford">Clayne Crawford</a> as Ted "Teddy" Talbot, Jr., Daniel's stepbrother and Tawney's husband. His father married Daniel's mother after Daniel's father died, while Daniel was in prison.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Luke_Kirby" title="Luke Kirby">Luke Kirby</a> as Jon Stern, Daniel's new lawyer. He did not defend Daniel in the rape and murder trial. He is in a relationship with Daniel's sister.</li>
<li>Bruce McKinnon as Ted Talbot, Sr. (recurring season 1, main seasons 2–4), Daniel's stepfather. He was once a worker at the tire store owned by Daniel's father but took over after his death.</li>
<li>Jake Austin Walker as Jared Talbot (recurring season 1, main seasons 2–4), Daniel's younger half-brother.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/J.D._Evermore" title="J.D. Evermore">J.D. Evermore</a> as Carl Daggett (recurring seasons 1–3, main season 4), a local sheriff.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_production">Development and production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rectify&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Development and production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series began development in 2008 when it was originally planned to air on <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> with <a href="/wiki/Walton_Goggins" title="Walton Goggins">Walton Goggins</a> set to play the lead role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Three years after originally being developed at AMC, sister channel <a href="/wiki/SundanceTV" title="SundanceTV">SundanceTV</a> announced it had ordered the series for six episodes, to become the channel's first original scripted series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Production for the second season began on February 3, 2014, in <a href="/wiki/Griffin,_Georgia" title="Griffin, Georgia">Griffin, Georgia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Production began on the fourth and final season in April 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rectify&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2012, <a href="/wiki/Abigail_Spencer" title="Abigail Spencer">Abigail Spencer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clayne_Crawford" title="Clayne Crawford">Clayne Crawford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Clemens" title="Adelaide Clemens">Adelaide Clemens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jonah_Lotan" title="Jonah Lotan">Jonah Lotan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/J._Smith-Cameron" title="J. Smith-Cameron">J. Smith-Cameron</a> were all cast in the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deadline_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-deadline-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Aden_Young" title="Aden Young">Aden Young</a> was cast as the series lead in May 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Luke_Kirby" title="Luke Kirby">Luke Kirby</a> was cast in June 2012, replacing Lotan as Daniel Holden's lawyer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rectify&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rectify&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The first season of <i>Rectify</i> received critical acclaim, scoring a <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> rating of 82 out of 100 based on 28 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> One of the positive reviews of <i>Rectify</i> was from the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i>, which called the series "mesmerizing."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> A less positive review in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> noted the slow pace of the series after the first episode and a quarter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Review aggregator <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gave the first season a rating of 90% based on 30 reviews, with an average rating of 8.4 out of 10, with the critical consensus: "<i>Rectify</i> is a stylish drama that rewards patient viewers with deep characters and plotlines."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second season continued to receive critical acclaim, with a Metacritic rating of 92 out of 100 based on 16 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Rotten Tomatoes gave the second season a 95% rating, based on 21 reviews with an average rating of 9 out of 10 and the critical consensus: "Its scenic landscapes and rewarding slow burn prove <i>Rectify</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s second season to be just as good, if not better, than the first."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Tim Goodman of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> wrote that "it remains as riveting and unique as ever."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The third season also received critical acclaim with a Metacritic rating of 89 out of 100 based on 11 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Rotten Tomatoes gave the third season a 100% rating, based on 19 reviews with an average rating of 8.1 out of 10 and the critical consensus: "<i>Rectify</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s subtlety draws viewers in deeper during season three – and they continue to be rewarded with quality acting, compelling dialogue, and thought-provoking drama."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Jeff Jensen of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> wrote, "It's rich with beautifully crafted scenes that capture the distance, anger, and confusion of a fragmenting family and souls in flux."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Brian Lowry of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> wrote, "<i>Rectify</i> remains a master class in nuance — in small looks and long pauses that say more than pages of dialogue."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>The fourth and final season also received universal acclaim with a Metacritic rating of 99 out of 100 based on 11 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> It is the second-highest rated show on the site; the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/Murphy_Brown" title="Murphy Brown">Murphy Brown</a></i> has a score of 100 out of 100 based on five reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Rotten Tomatoes gave the fourth season a 100% rating, based on 12 reviews with an average rating of 9.5 out of 10 and the critical consensus: "In its final season, <i>Rectify</i> endures as a vital and compelling drama, poignantly driven by a narrative that envelops its characters in complexity, humanity, and a bittersweet beauty."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Matt Roush of <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i> called <i>Rectify</i> "One of TV's truest, finest and deepest dives into pure character drama."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Malcolm Jones of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Beast" title="The Daily Beast">The Daily Beast</a></i> wrote that "<i>Rectify</i> is the best series I have ever seen on television. Not may be. Not might be. It just is."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Damien_Echols" title="Damien Echols">Damien Echols</a>, who as one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Memphis_3" title="West Memphis 3">West Memphis 3</a> was convicted and served nearly two decades in prison before being released in party based on DNA evidence, wrote about the series in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Huffington_Post" title="The Huffington Post">The Huffington Post</a>:</i> "<i>Rectify</i> is the story of a man who was sentenced to death for a crime he didn’t commit, and spent 19 years on death row before getting out. Much like in my own real life case, the local politicians refuse to admit he’s innocent even after DNA testing points towards someone else. In fact, there was so much about this show that mirrored my own life I began to wonder how much of my story had crept into the script." And, "I can tell you from first hand experience that <i>Rectify</i> is a very realistic show."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rectify&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Abigail_Spencer" title="Abigail Spencer">Abigail Spencer</a> was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Television_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Drama_Series" title="Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Drama Series">Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Drama Series</a> in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> For the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/67th_Writers_Guild_of_America_Awards" title="67th Writers Guild of America Awards">67th Writers Guild of America Awards</a>, Kate Powers and <a href="/wiki/Ray_McKinnon_(actor)" title="Ray McKinnon (actor)">Ray McKinnon</a> were nominated for Best Episodic Drama for "Donald the Normal".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> For the <a href="/wiki/5th_Critics%27_Choice_Television_Awards" title="5th Critics' Choice Television Awards">5th Critics' Choice Television Awards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aden_Young" title="Aden Young">Aden Young</a> was nominated for Best Actor in a Drama Series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>The series was awarded with a 2014 <a href="/wiki/Peabody_Award" title="Peabody Award">Peabody Award</a>, with the organization stating, "A powerful, subtle dramatic series about a death-row inmate released after nearly two decades thanks to new DNA evidence, it ponders whether what’s been lost can ever be repaid, not just to him but to everyone he and his alleged crimes touched."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/6th_Critics%27_Choice_Television_Awards" title="6th Critics' Choice Television Awards">6th Critics' Choice Television Awards</a>, the series received three nominations, for Best Drama Series, <a href="/wiki/Aden_Young" title="Aden Young">Aden Young</a> for Best Actor in a Drama Series, and <a href="/wiki/Clayne_Crawford" title="Clayne Crawford">Clayne Crawford</a> for Best Supporting Actor in a Drama Series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
