<p>"<b>Ren'ai Shashin</b>" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">恋愛写真</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Love Photograph</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the 13th single by Japanese singer <a href="/wiki/Ai_Otsuka" title="Ai Otsuka">Ai Otsuka</a>, released on the <a href="/wiki/Avex_Trax" title="Avex Trax">Avex Trax</a> label. It is also her third and final single released in 2006.</p>
<p>The ballad was used as the theme song of the movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tada,_Kimi_o_Aishiteru" title="Tada, Kimi o Aishiteru">Tada, Kimi o Aishiteru</a></i>. The title of the movie was originally <i>Heavenly Forest</i>, but was changed to a phrase from the song's lyrics after the director listened to the song.</p>
<p>The b-side, "Hanikami Jane", an upbeat song, was used in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_commercial" title="Television commercial">television commercial</a> for Look: a la mode chocolate. The third track is a live version of "Haneari Tamago", a song originally on Otsuka's 3rd album <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Cook" title="Love Cook">Love Cook</a></i>.</p>
<p>The single debuted at number 2 during its first week, selling 77,570 copies and making it Otsuka's highest debut sales of the year. "Ren'ai Shashin" was second only to <a href="/wiki/Kisarazu_Cat%27s_Eye" title="Kisarazu Cat's Eye">Kisarazu Cat's Eye</a>'s "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seaside_Byebye&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seaside Byebye (page does not exist)">Seaside Byebye</a>". It sold a total of 129,855 copies in 2006, making it the 75th best-selling single of the year.</p>
