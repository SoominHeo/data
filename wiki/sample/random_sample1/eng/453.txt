<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Modern_pentathlon" title="Modern pentathlon">modern pentathlon</a></b> at the <b><a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> was held from 11 to 12 August 2012. The <a href="/wiki/Modern_pentathlon_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s" title="Modern pentathlon at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's">men's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modern_pentathlon_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s" title="Modern pentathlon at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's">women's</a> events each involved 36 athletes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-London2012_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-London2012-1">[1]</a></sup> The venues for the events were the <a href="/wiki/Copper_Box" title="Copper Box">Copper Box</a> (fencing), the <a href="/wiki/London_Aquatics_Centre" title="London Aquatics Centre">Aquatics Centre</a> (swimming), and <a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Park" title="Greenwich Park">Greenwich Park</a> (horse riding and combined running and shooting).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the July 2011 modern pentathlon Olympic test event, <a href="/wiki/Union_Internationale_de_Pentathlon_Moderne" title="Union Internationale de Pentathlon Moderne">UIPM</a> president Klaus Schormann told Olympic news source Around the Rings that transportation was the key issue for the UIPM during the 2012 Olympics. "The transportation is important to get from the swimming and fencing to here in Greenwich Park," he said. "This should be properly organized, for the athletes but also for the spectators as well."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/David_Svoboda" title="David Svoboda">David Svoboda</a>, representing the Czech Republic, won the men's event, and <a href="/wiki/Laura_Asadauskait%C4%97" title="Laura Asadauskaitė">Laura Asadauskaitė</a> of Lithuania won the women's.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Format">Format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Modern_pentathlon_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Modern pentathlon contained five events; <a href="/wiki/Shooting_sport" title="Shooting sport">pistol shooting</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%89p%C3%A9e" title="Épée">épée</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fencing_(sport)" title="Fencing (sport)">fencing</a>, 200 m <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_swimming" title="Freestyle swimming">freestyle swimming</a>, <a href="/wiki/Show_jumping" title="Show jumping">show jumping</a>, and a 3 km <a href="/wiki/Cross_country_running" title="Cross country running">cross-country run</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-4">[4]</a></sup> It was the first Olympic Games in which the running and shooting events were combined; athletes faced three rounds of shooting each followed by a 1 km run. In each of the three rounds of firing, athletes had to shoot five targets, loading the gun after each shot; they were then allowed to resume their running. Misses were not penalised, but exceeding a maximum total time of 70 seconds would result in a penalty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pistols were replaced with <a href="/wiki/Laser_guns" title="Laser guns">laser guns</a> after a successful trial at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Summer_Youth_Olympics" title="2010 Summer Youth Olympics">2010 Summer Youth Olympics</a> in Singapore. The use of laser guns reduced the cost of the shooting section by approximately two-thirds and opened up new venues for the sport because of the increased levels of safety.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualification">Qualification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Modern_pentathlon_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Modern_pentathlon_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Qualification" title="Modern pentathlon at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Qualification">Modern pentathlon at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Qualification</a></div>
<p>Thirty-six athletes qualified for each of the two events; a maximum of two athletes per gender may qualify from any nation. Qualification methods were the same for both the men's and women's events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The host nation <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Great Britain at the 2012 Summer Olympics">Great Britain</a> was awarded one qualifying place automatically and two invitational positions would be allocated by the UIPM once the rest of the qualifiers were decided.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first allocation of places to athletes based on competition results occurred between January and August 2011. Five continental championships accounted for 19 places; one from Africa, four from Pan-America, five from Asia, eight from Europe and one from Oceania. The winner of the 2011 World Cup final, which was held in London's Greenwich Park in July as the first official test event ahead of the Games, qualified. The top three placed athletes at the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Modern_Pentathlon_Championships" title="2011 World Modern Pentathlon Championships">2011 World Championships</a> in September, which were due to be held in Cairo but were moved to Moscow due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Egyptian_revolution" title="2011 Egyptian revolution">Egyptian revolution</a>, also qualified.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2012, the top three ranked athletes, who had not yet qualified by other means, at the <a href="/wiki/2012_World_Modern_Pentathlon_Championships" title="2012 World Modern Pentathlon Championships">World Championships</a> in Rome, May 2012 were to be allocated places. The final seven places were to be awarded based on pentathlon's world rankings as of 1 June 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
