<p>The <span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Rentenmark.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0f/Rentenmark.ogg" title="Rentenmark.ogg"><b>Rentenmark</b></a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Rentenmark.ogg" title="File:Rentenmark.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span> (<i>RM</i>) was a currency issued on 15 October 1923 to stop the <a href="/wiki/Hyperinflation" title="Hyperinflation">hyperinflation</a> of 1922 and 1923 in <a href="/wiki/Hyperinflation_in_the_Weimar_Republic" title="Hyperinflation in the Weimar Republic">Weimar Germany</a>. It was subdivided into 100 <i>Rentenpfennig</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=German_Rentenmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Occupation_of_the_Ruhr" title="Occupation of the Ruhr">Occupation of the Ruhr</a> in early 1923 by French and Belgian troops, referred to as the <i>Ruhrkampf</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Cuno_cabinet" title="Cuno cabinet">German government</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Cuno" title="Wilhelm Cuno">Wilhelm Cuno</a> reacted by announcing a policy of passive resistance. This caused the regional economy of the Ruhr - the industrial heartland of Germany - to virtually grind to a halt. The occupation authorities reacted with arrests and deportations to strikes and sabotage. Those displaced and left without income by the <i>Ruhrkampf</i> and their families fell back on public income support. Tax revenues plunged as economic activity slowed dramatically. The government covered its need for funds mainly by printing money. As a result, inflation spiked and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Papiermark" title="Papiermark">Papiermark</a> went into freefall on the currency market. Foreign currency reserves at the <a href="/wiki/Reichsbank" title="Reichsbank">Reichsbank</a> dwindled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Akten_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Akten-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As <a href="/wiki/Hyperinflation_in_the_Weimar_Republic" title="Hyperinflation in the Weimar Republic">hyperinflation</a> took hold, the cabinet of Cuno resigned in August 1923 and was replaced by the cabinet of <a href="/wiki/Gustav_Stresemann" title="Gustav Stresemann">Gustav Stresemann</a>. After Stresemann reshuffled his cabinet in early October, <a href="/wiki/Hans_Luther" title="Hans Luther">Hans Luther</a> became Minister of Finance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Akten_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Akten-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Working with <a href="/wiki/Hjalmar_Schacht" title="Hjalmar Schacht">Hjalmar Schacht</a> at the Reichsbank, Luther quickly came up with a stabilization plan for the currency which combined elements of a monetary reform by economist <a href="/wiki/Karl_Helfferich" title="Karl Helfferich">Karl Helfferich</a> with ideas of Luther's predecessor in office <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Hilferding" title="Rudolf Hilferding">Rudolf Hilferding</a>. With the help of the emergency law (<i>Ermächtigungsgesetz</i>) of 13 October 1923 which gave the government the power to issue decrees on financial and economic matters, the plan was implemented that same day, 15 October 1923.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The newly created <i>Rentenmark</i> replaced the old <i>Papiermark</i>. Because of the economic crisis in Germany after World War I, there was no gold available to back the currency. Luther thus used Helfferich's idea of a currency backed by real goods. The new currency was backed by the land used for agriculture and business. This was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mortgage" title="Mortgage">mortgaged</a> (<i>Rente</i> is a technical term for mortgage in German) to the tune of 3.2 billion <a href="/wiki/German_gold_mark" title="German gold mark">Goldmarks</a>, based on the 1913 wealth charge called <i>Wehrbeitrag</i> which had helped fund the German war effort in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. Notes worth 3.2 billion Rentenmarks were issued. The Rentenmark was introduced at a rate of one Rentenmark to equal one million million old marks, with an exchange rate of one <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">United States dollar</a> to equal 4.2 Rentenmarks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Act creating the Rentenmark backed the currency by means of twice yearly payments on property, due in April and October, payable for five years. Although the Rentenmark was not initially <a href="/wiki/Legal_tender" title="Legal tender">legal tender</a>, it was accepted by the population and its value was relatively stable. The Act prohibited the recently privatised Reichsbank from continuing to discount bills and the inflation of the Papiermark immediately stopped.</p>
<p>Thus the monetary policy spearheaded by Schacht at the Reichsbank and the fiscal policy of Finance Minister Hans Luther brought the period of hyperinflation in Germany to an end. The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_reichsmark" title="German reichsmark">Reichsmark</a></i> became the new legal tender on 30 August 1924, equal in value to the Rentenmark. This marked a return to a gold-backed currency in connection with the implementation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dawes_plan" title="Dawes plan">Dawes plan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Rentenbank continued to exist after 1924 and the notes and coins continued to circulate. The last Rentenmark notes were valid until 1948.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coins">Coins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=German_Rentenmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Coins were issued dated 1923, 1924 and 1925 in denominations of 1, 2, 5, 10 and 50 Rentenpfennig. Only small numbers of Rentenpfennig coins were produced in 1925. A few 1 Rentenpfennig coins were struck dated 1929. The 1 and 2 Rentenpfennig were minted in <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">bronze</a>, with the 5, 10, and 50 Rentenpfennig coins in aluminium-bronze. These coins had the same design features and motifs as coins of the <a href="/wiki/Reichsmark" title="Reichsmark">Reichsmark</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Weimar_Republic" title="Weimar Republic">Weimar</a> and early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Reich" title="Third Reich">Third Reich</a> periods.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Banknotes">Banknotes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=German_Rentenmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Banknotes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first issue of banknotes was dated 1 November 1923 and was in denominations of 1, 2, 5, 10, 50, 100, 500 and 1000 Rentenmark. Later issues of notes were 10 and 50 Rentenmark (1925), 5 Rentenmark (1926), 50 Rentenmark (1934) and 1 and 2 Rentenmark and dated 1937.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Currencies_of_Germany">Currencies of Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=German_Rentenmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Currencies of Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="wikitable" style="margin: 0.5em auto; clear: both; font-size:95%;">
<tr style="text-align: center;">
<td colspan="1" rowspan="2" style="width: 30%; text-align: center;">Preceded by:<br/>
<b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_papiermark" title="German papiermark">German papiermark</a></b><br/>
<small><b>Ratio:</b> 1 Rentenmark = 1,000,000,000,000 Papiermark, and 4.2 Rentenmark = US$1</small></td>
<td colspan="1" rowspan="2" style="text-align: center;"><b>Currency of Germany</b><br/>
15 November 1923 – 29 August 1924</td>
<td colspan="1" rowspan="2" style="text-align: center;"><i>Circulates in Germany</i><br/>
30 August 1924 – 1948<br/>
<small><b>Note:</b> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_reichsmark" title="German reichsmark">Reichsmark</a> was the legal tender</small></td>
<td colspan="1" rowspan="1" style="width: 30%; text-align: center;">Succeeded by:<br/>
<b><a href="/wiki/East_German_mark" title="East German mark">East German mark</a></b><br/>
<small><b>Reason:</b> reaction to the change over in <a href="/wiki/Bizone" title="Bizone">Trizone</a> (later <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>)</small><br/>
<small><b>Ratio:</b> 1 Mark = 7 Rentenmark on the first 70 Rentenmark for private individuals, otherwise 1 Kuponmark = 10 Rentenmark</small></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1" rowspan="1" style="width: 30%; text-align: center;">Succeeded by:<br/>
<b><a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Mark" title="Deutsche Mark">Deutsche Mark</a></b><br/>
<small><b>Reason:</b> intended to protect West Germany from the second wave of <a href="/wiki/Hyperinflation" title="Hyperinflation">hyperinflation</a> and stop the rampant barter and <a href="/wiki/Black_market" title="Black market">black market</a> trade</small><br/>
<small><b>Ratio:</b> 1 Deutsche Mark = 1 Rentenmark for first 600 RM, 1 Deutsche Mark = 10 Rentenmark thereafter, plus each person received 40 Deutsche Mark</small></td>
</tr>
