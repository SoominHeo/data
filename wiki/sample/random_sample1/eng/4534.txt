<p><b>Lien Chan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">連戰</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Lián Zhàn</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Pe%CC%8Dh-%C5%8De-j%C4%AB" title="Pe̍h-ōe-jī">Pe̍h-ōe-jī</a>: <i><span lang="nan" xml:lang="nan">Liân Chiàn</span></i>; born August 27, 1936, in <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" title="Republic of China">China</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a> in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>. He was <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Premier of the Republic of China">Premier of the Republic of China</a> from 1993 to 1997, <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Vice President of the Republic of China">Vice President of the Republic of China</a> from 1996 to 2000, and was the Chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> (KMT) from 2000 to 2005, apart from various ministerial posts he had also held. Upon his retirement as KMT Chairman in August 2005, he was given the title Honorary Chairman of the Kuomintang. He is highly credited after holding a <a href="/wiki/2005_Pan%E2%80%93Blue_visits_to_mainland_China" title="2005 Pan–Blue visits to mainland China">groundbreaking visit to Mainland China</a> in his capacity as the Chairman of Kuomintang to meet with the Secretary-General of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> on 29 April 2005, the first meeting between the two party leaders after the end of <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a> in 1949, in which subsequently helped thaw the long-stalled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cross-strait_relations" title="Cross-strait relations">cross-strait relations</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Lien Chan was born in <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi" title="Shaanxi">Shaanxi</a> province, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> as the only child of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chen-tung&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lien Chen-tung (page does not exist)">Lien Chen-tung</a> (連震東) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chao_Lan-k%27un&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chao Lan-k'un (page does not exist)">Chao Lan-k'un</a> (趙蘭坤). His paternal grandfather, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lien_Heng" title="Lien Heng">Lien Heng</a>, was the writer of <i>The General History of Taiwan</i> (台灣通史), a book that is often cited for the quote, "Taiwan's sorrow is that it has no history." Lien earned a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's degree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Political_science" title="Political science">political science</a> from the <a href="/wiki/National_Taiwan_University" title="National Taiwan University">National Taiwan University</a> in 1957 and a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">Master of Arts</a> in International Law and Diplomacy in 1961. He received a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">PhD</a> in political science in 1965 from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago" title="University of Chicago">University of Chicago</a> and married former Miss Republic of China <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fang_Yu" title="Fang Yu">Fang Yu</a> the same year. Lien is also currently a Trustee Emeritus on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Board_of_Trustees" title="Board of Trustees">Board of Trustees</a> to the University of Chicago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lien held assistant professorships of political science at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wisconsin%E2%80%93Madison" title="University of Wisconsin–Madison">University of Wisconsin–Madison</a> from 1966 to 1967 and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Connecticut" title="University of Connecticut">University of Connecticut</a> from 1967 to 1968. He returned to Taiwan in 1968 to become visiting professor of political science at the National Taiwan University, serving as chairman of the Political Science Department and dean of the Graduate Institute of Political Science the following year.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His official positions included Ambassador to <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a> (1975–1976), Minister of Communications and Transportation (1981–1987), Vice Premier (1987–1988), Foreign Minister (1988–1990) before becoming Governor of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Province" title="Taiwan Province">Taiwan Province</a> (1990–1993). In 1993 he was appointed <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Premier of the Republic of China">Premier of the Republic of China</a>. In 1996, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Teng-hui" title="Lee Teng-hui">Lee Teng-hui</a> selected him as <a href="/wiki/Running_mate" title="Running mate">running mate</a> in the presidential election. After Lee and Lien won the election for the presidency and the vice-presidency respectively, Lien retained the premiership. This proved unpopular, and opposition parties claimed that this was possibly in conflict with the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Constitution of the Republic of China">constitution</a>, leading to a <a href="/wiki/Judicial_Yuan" title="Judicial Yuan">Council of Grand Justices</a> decision ruling that Lien's retention of these dual roles was "constitutional but inappropriate." In response, on 31 August 1997, he resigned as Premier but remained Vice President. Before becoming Chairman of the KMT, he was Vice Chairman (1993–2000) and a member of the Central Committee (1984–2000).</p>

<p>Lien took an active role as a representative of <a href="/wiki/Lee_Teng-hui" title="Lee Teng-hui">Lee Teng-hui</a> in quasi-official diplomacy in the mid-1990s. One of the greatest moments of his career is his 1995 meeting with <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A1clav_Havel" title="Václav Havel">Václav Havel</a>, in which Lien likened the democratic reforms of the Lee Teng-hui administration as being similar to the <a href="/wiki/Velvet_Revolution" title="Velvet Revolution">Velvet Revolution</a>.</p>
<p>In contrast to his political opponents, Lien Chan, while vice president, was generally considered arrogant, aloof and out of touch with the public.</p>
<p>He finished third in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ROC_presidential_election,_2000" title="ROC presidential election, 2000">2000 Presidential elections</a> despite having the backing of the KMT. One popular theory on Taiwan, which has some support from Lee's subsequent actions, has that Lien Chan was chosen to head the KMT's ticket by President Lee Teng-hui in a deliberate effort to destroy the KMT. Many have noted in support of this theory that Lien has never been elected on his own to any political office, and his ill-fated campaign for President was the first campaign that he ever undertook. During the 2000 Presidential campaign Lien campaigned on a platform of following in Lee's footsteps and continuing localization reforms. He touted a "3-S" slogan of "Safety, Security and Stability", claiming the selection of a DPP candidate would bring China's wrath and a return of fascism.</p>

<p>Part of Lien's defeat may have been due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/921_earthquake" title="921 earthquake">massive 7.4 earthquake</a> on September 21, 1999. As the Vice President and KMT candidate, Lien bore the brunt of public dissatisfaction with government relief efforts, in one instance being chased onto his helicopter by furious victims. In a poll following the earthquake, the undecided vote rose to 47%.</p>
<p>After the defeat of the KMT in 2000, Lien was able to achieve Lee's ouster and assume the leadership of the KMT. He had adopted a platform to erase the Lee effect from Taiwan and "Return to the Good Old Days", a call to return to the heyday of <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Ching-kuo" title="Chiang Ching-kuo">Chiang Ching-kuo</a>. The KMT launched a party wide purge to eradicate Lee supporters and then held a membership drive to attract KMT loyalists. According to Lien, the drive was successful, but many outside observers note a significant drop in membership, as many of the former members who had joined the party as a compulsory act of conscripted military service never renewed. Lien as the Chairman of the KMT also vowed to learn from his loss and remake the KMT party. He held forums to discuss erasing the KMT's image as a corrupt institution and Lien promised to give up property seized by the KMT after the Japanese exodus. The KMT has returned some properties to the government and it considers the matter to be closed, but others disagree. Lien has been criticized for trying to give the land away to local governments as a type of payoff for support.</p>
<p>Lien Chan ran for president with Soong as his running mate in a <a href="/wiki/Pan-Blue_Coalition" title="Pan-Blue Coalition">combined KMT-PFP ticket</a> against <a href="/wiki/Chen_Shui-bian" title="Chen Shui-bian">Chen Shui-bian</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ROC_Presidential_Election_2004" title="ROC Presidential Election 2004">2004 Presidential elections</a>, though it had been thought that Lien and Soong personally disliked each other. During the 2000 campaigns, Lien accused Soong of positioning his family graves to interfere with Lien's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feng_Shui" title="Feng Shui">Feng Shui</a> forcing Lien to reposition his graves.</p>
<p>Initially, it was believed that the Lien-Soong ticket would be a sure win, given that both men garnered a combined 59.9% of the vote in 2000. However, the lead gradually diminished to a dead heat, and both sides were forced to moderate their positions. In December 2003, Lien said that while the KMT was opposed to "immediate independence," it did not wish to be classed as "pro-reunificationist" either. The platform was changed from promoting the eventual reunification of China to preserving the status quo and leaving Taiwan's status to be settled by future generations. Chen also floated the possibility of eventual reunification.</p>
<p>With the platforms largely similar, the campaign was filled largely with personal attacks between Lien and Chen. On March 20, the day after President Chen and Vice President <a href="/wiki/Annette_Lu" title="Annette Lu">Annette Lu</a> were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwan_assassination_attempt" title="Taiwan assassination attempt">shot while campaigning</a>, Lien lost the election by 0.2% of the vote. He refused to concede and decided to challenge the results. Pan-Blue supporters staged several mass rallies in the ensuing weeks, with some gatherings turning into riots. Lien sought to nullify the election and institute a recount. The recount confirmed the result, but by a narrower margin. His appeals to cancel the result and hold new elections were rejected by the courts.</p>

<p>In 2005, after Ma was elected as KMT chairman to succeed Lien, the KMT Central Committee offered the unprecedented title of "Chairman Emeritus" (Honorary Chairman) to Lien. Lien did not explicitly say whether he was accepting the title, but his failure to decline appears to be an implicit acceptance.</p>
<p>In December 2010, Lien was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Confucius_Peace_Prize" title="Confucius Peace Prize">Confucius Peace Prize</a> in China, which was instituted as a reaction to the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a> awarded to <a href="/wiki/Liu_Xiaobo" title="Liu Xiaobo">Liu Xiaobo</a>. Lien's office said to the Taipei Times: “We’ve never heard of such an award and of course Mr Lien has no plans to accept it.” <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Corruption_allegations">Corruption allegations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Corruption allegations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some have speculated that his immense wealth was obtained through <a href="/wiki/Black_gold_(politics)" title="Black gold (politics)">black gold</a>. Among the allegations, his father Lien Chen-tung was the interior minister responsible of Taiwanese agricultural policy. Through his influence, the family purchased farmland in the 50's and 60's and illegally rezoned it. In an investigation by his political opponents, one DPP legislator Tang Bi-A was quoted as saying, "It is doubtful that Lien Chan and his father could accumulate billions in wealth for their family when both of them were government officials receiving limited salaries."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cross-strait_relations">Cross-strait relations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cross-strait relations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="April_2005_breakthrough_journey_to_mainland_China">April 2005 breakthrough journey to mainland China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: April 2005 breakthrough journey to mainland China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_Pan-Blue_visits_to_mainland_China" title="2005 Pan-Blue visits to mainland China">2005 Pan-Blue visits to mainland China</a></div>

<p>On April 26, 2005, Lien Chan traveled to <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">mainland China</a> to meet with the leaders of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> (CPC). His meeting with CPC leader <a href="/wiki/Hu_Jintao" title="Hu Jintao">Hu Jintao</a> was the highest level exchange since <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek" title="Chiang Kai-shek">Chiang Kai-shek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a> met in <a href="/wiki/Chongqing" title="Chongqing">Chongqing</a> on August 28, 1945, to celebrate the victory in the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War" title="Second Sino-Japanese War">Second Sino-Japanese War</a> and discuss a possible truce in the impending <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a>.</p>
<p>On April 27, Lien visited the <a href="/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen_Mausoleum" title="Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum">Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum</a> in Nanjing. On April 28, he arrived in Beijing and visited the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palace_Museum" title="Palace Museum">Palace Museum</a>.</p>
<p>On the afternoon of April 29, he met with <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="President of the People's Republic of China">President of the People's Republic of China</a> <a href="/wiki/Hu_Jintao" title="Hu Jintao">Hu Jintao</a> (in his capacity as <a href="/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="General Secretary of the Communist Party of China">General Secretary of the Communist Party of China</a>) and they reached a 5-point consensus, reaffirming that Taiwan is part of China and that the two parties would work together to prevent formal Taiwan independence.</p>
<p>Before meeting with Hu on April 29, Lien Chan delivered a speech at <a href="/wiki/Peking_University" title="Peking University">Peking University</a>, which his 96-year-old mother Zhao Lankun attended nearly 80 years ago. On April 30, he headed to his birthplace <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a>. He revisited <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Houzaimen_Primary_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Houzaimen Primary School (page does not exist)">Houzaimen Primary School</a>, which he attended 60 years ago. He also visited the Great Mausoleum of <a href="/wiki/Qin_Shi_Huang" title="Qin Shi Huang">Qin Shi Huang</a>, China's first emperor. Early on May 1, he paid homage to his grandmother's tomb near Qingliangsi.</p>
<p>Later that day, Lien arrived in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, where he attended a banquet hosted by Shanghai CPC Party Secretary General <a href="/wiki/Chen_Liangyu" title="Chen Liangyu">Chen Liangyu</a>. On May 2, he met with <a href="/wiki/Wang_Daohan" title="Wang Daohan">Wang Daohan</a>, the 90-year-old chairman of the mainland-based <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Relations_Across_the_Taiwan_Straits" title="Association for Relations Across the Taiwan Straits">Association for Relations Across the Taiwan Straits</a>, and the representatives of Taiwanese businesspeople. He returned to Taiwan at noon on May 3.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="October_2005_visit_to_Shenyang">October 2005 visit to Shenyang</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: October 2005 visit to Shenyang">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2005, Lien made his second visit to the mainland. He visited <a href="/wiki/Shenyang" title="Shenyang">Shenyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a> to pay homage to his maternal grandmother's tomb at Lansheng Village and the school where his mother studied. He also visited the 9.18 Historical Museum to commemorate the <a href="/wiki/Mukden_Incident" title="Mukden Incident">Mukden Incident</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912%E2%80%9349)" title="Republic of China (1912–49)">Republic of China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006_visit_to_Mainland_China">2006 visit to Mainland China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2006 visit to Mainland China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In April 2006, Lien departed to Mainland China to visit <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a>. He paid tribute to his ancestors in <a href="/wiki/Zhangzhou" title="Zhangzhou">Zhangzhou</a> and received an honorary doctor's degree from <a href="/wiki/Xiamen_University" title="Xiamen University">Xiamen University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Xiamen" title="Xiamen">Xiamen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 April 2006, Lien arrived in <a href="/wiki/Hangzhou" title="Hangzhou">Hangzhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zhejiang" title="Zhejiang">Zhejiang</a>. He was welcomed by <a href="/wiki/Xia_Baolong" title="Xia Baolong">Xia Baolong</a>, deputy secretary of Zhejiang Provincial Committee of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Hangzhou_Xiaoshan_International_Airport" title="Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport">Hangzhou Xiaoshan International Airport</a>. In Hangzhou, Lien met with provincial government leaders and visited the <a href="/wiki/West_Lake" title="West Lake">West Lake</a>.</p>
<p>Two days later, Lien visited Beijing to attend the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cross-Straits_Economic_Trade_and_Culture_Forum" title="Cross-Straits Economic Trade and Culture Forum">Cross-Straits Economic Trade and Culture Forum</a>. Lien met with CPC Secretary-General <a href="/wiki/Hu_Jintao" title="Hu Jintao">Hu Jintao</a> at the forum, where both underscored the peaceful development of relations between the two sides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_visit_to_Shanghai">2010 visit to Shanghai</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2010 visit to Shanghai">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2010, Lien visited <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> to attend the opening ceremony of the <a href="/wiki/Expo_2010" title="Expo 2010">Shanghai World Expo 2010</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_visit_to_Beijing">2013 visit to Beijing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2013 visit to Beijing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2013, Lien visited Beijing to meet with <a href="/wiki/Xi_Jinping" title="Xi Jinping">Xi Jinping</a>, the newly elected <a href="/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="General Secretary of the Communist Party of China">General Secretary</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a>. Lien pronounced a new "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=16-character_principle&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="16-character principle (page does not exist)">16-character principle</a>" for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cross-strait_relations" title="Cross-strait relations">cross-strait relations</a>, which are <a href="/wiki/One-China_policy" title="One-China policy">one China</a> (一個中國), cross-strait peace (兩岸和平), mutually beneficial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_reunification" title="Chinese reunification">integration</a> (互利融合) and rejuvenation of the <a href="/wiki/Zhonghua_minzu" title="Zhonghua minzu">Chinese nation</a> (振興中華).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> This was unpopular and 'controversial' in Taiwan and criticized for being 'treacherous' to the Taiwanese people, and President Ma has distanced from this statement by saying this is 'absolutely Mr. Lien’s personal view.'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lien also met with <a href="/wiki/Yu_Zhengsheng" title="Yu Zhengsheng">Yu Zhengsheng</a> (chairman-designate of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_People%27s_Political_Consultative_Conference" title="Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference">Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference</a>), <a href="/wiki/Wang_Huning" title="Wang Huning">Wang Huning</a> (member of the <a href="/wiki/Politburo_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="Politburo of the Communist Party of China">Politburo of the CPC</a>), <a href="/wiki/Li_Zhanshu" title="Li Zhanshu">Li Zhanshu</a> (chief of the <a href="/wiki/General_Office_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="General Office of the Communist Party of China">General Office of the CPC</a>), <a href="/wiki/Dai_Bingguo" title="Dai Bingguo">Dai Bingguo</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_Councilor" title="State Councilor">state councilor of the PRC</a>), <a href="/wiki/Wang_Yi_(politician)" title="Wang Yi (politician)">Wang Yi</a> (director of the <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Affairs_Office" title="Taiwan Affairs Office">Taiwan Affairs Office of the State Council</a>), <a href="/wiki/Chen_Yunlin" title="Chen Yunlin">Chen Yunlin</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zheng_Lizhong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zheng Lizhong (page does not exist)">Zheng Lizhong</a> (president and vice president of <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Relations_Across_the_Taiwan_Straits" title="Association for Relations Across the Taiwan Straits">ARATS</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lien and his wife <a href="/wiki/Lien_Fang_Yu" title="Lien Fang Yu">Lien Fang Yu</a> also visited <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Aerospace_Command_and_Control_Center" title="Beijing Aerospace Command and Control Center">Beijing Aerospace Command and Control Center</a> and met with <a href="/wiki/Liu_Wang" title="Liu Wang">Liu Wang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liu_Yang_(astronaut)" title="Liu Yang (astronaut)">Liu Yang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jing_Haipeng" title="Jing Haipeng">Jing Haipeng</a>, astronauts of the <a href="/wiki/Shenzhou_9" title="Shenzhou 9">Shenzhou 9</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_visit_to_Beijing">2014 visit to Beijing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2014 visit to Beijing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lien and delegates from 80 business leaders and civil group representatives, including former <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Solidarity_Union" title="Taiwan Solidarity Union">Taiwan Solidarity Union</a> Chairman <a href="/wiki/Shu_Chin-chiang" title="Shu Chin-chiang">Shu Chin-chiang</a>, visited Beijing on 17 February to meet with <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Zhijun" title="Zhang Zhijun">Zhang Zhijun</a>, the head of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Affairs_Office" title="Taiwan Affairs Office">Taiwan Affairs Office</a>, and on 18 February with <a href="/wiki/Xi_Jinping" title="Xi Jinping">Xi Jinping</a>, <a href="/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="General Secretary of the Communist Party of China">General Secretary of the Communist Party of China</a> <a href="/wiki/Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">Central Committee</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Diaoyutai_State_Guesthouse" title="Diaoyutai State Guesthouse">Diaoyutai State Guesthouse</a> for non-governmental exchanges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> This 3-day trip came after the invitation from the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his meeting with Xi, Lien said that the KMT and CPC should make cross-strait relations to progress under the <i>One-China Framework</i> (一中架構).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_visit_to_Beijing">2015 visit to Beijing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2015 visit to Beijing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lien was invited to a Chinese military parade marking the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Sino%E2%80%93Japanese_War" title="Second Sino–Japanese War">Second Sino–Japanese War</a> in September 2015. Despite drawing criticism from both the Kuomintang<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and Democratic Progressive Party,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Lien decided to go, saying that he intended to promote a stronger Cross-Strait relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> During the visit, Lien asked Beijing to strongly consider supporting ROC President <a href="/wiki/Ma_Ying-jeou" title="Ma Ying-jeou">Ma Ying-jeou</a>'s East China Sea Peace Initiative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> However, Lien's comments on the Communist role in the war against Japan were directly condemned by the Ma administration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The day after Lien's 3 September return from China,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Kuomintang Chairman <a href="/wiki/Eric_Chu" title="Eric Chu">Eric Chu</a> stated that the party would review Lien's visit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The DPP's <a href="/wiki/Annette_Lu" title="Annette Lu">Annette Lu</a> and Chou Ni-an of the <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Solidarity_Union" title="Taiwan Solidarity Union">Taiwan Solidarity Union</a> filed separate charges of treason against Lien with the Taiwan High Prosecutors’ Office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Two separate incidents also showed public displeasure with his visit. On September 5, a celebration of Lien's birthday and wedding anniversary were disrupted by protesters<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and in Tainan, a statue of Lien's grandfather Lien Ya-tang was vandalized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<p>Lien was selected by President <a href="/wiki/Ma_Ying-jeou" title="Ma Ying-jeou">Ma Ying-jeou</a> as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Chinese_Taipei_Representatives_to_APEC" title="List of Chinese Taipei Representatives to APEC">special envoy</a> to represent the Republic of China (participating as <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei" title="Chinese Taipei">Chinese Taipei</a>) at the <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific_Economic_Cooperation" title="Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation">Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation</a> (APEC) in 2008-2012.</p>
<p>While at APEC, Lien also met with PRC President <a href="/wiki/Hu_Jintao" title="Hu Jintao">Hu Jintao</a>, the highest level of official exchange between the Mainland and Taiwan on the international stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_family_name" title="Chinese family name">family name</a> is Lien; his <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">given name</a> is Chan."Chan" means "<a href="/wiki/Battle" title="Battle">battles</a>" and his full name literally means "successive battles." The name originated from Lien Heng who, sick with <a href="/wiki/Hepatocellular_carcinoma" title="Hepatocellular carcinoma">liver cancer</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, wrote to his pregnant daughter-in-law in <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a>:</p>
<dl>
<dd>"<a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> will battle inevitably. If the child born is a <a href="/wiki/Boy" title="Boy">boy</a>, name him Lien Chan, signifying that the strength coming from within oneself will never diminish and can overcome the enemies and be victorious. It also has the meaning of reviving the former <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a>, reorganizing the <a href="/wiki/Light" title="Light">light</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hope" title="Hope">hope</a> of our <a href="/wiki/Homeland" title="Homeland">homeland</a>." (『中、日必將一戰，如生男則名連戰，寓有自強不息，克敵制勝，有復興故國、重整家園光明希望。』)</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lien_Chan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lien is married to <a href="/wiki/Lien_Fang_Yu" title="Lien Fang Yu">Lien Fang Yu</a>. They have two sons, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Lien" title="Sean Lien">Sean Lien</a> and Lien Sheng-Wu (連勝武), and two daughters, Lien Hui-Hsin (連惠心) and Lien Yong-Hsin (連詠心).</p>
