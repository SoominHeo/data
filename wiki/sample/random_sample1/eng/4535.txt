<p><b>Lianzhou</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">连州</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">連州</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Liánzhōu</span></i>), formerly Lian County or Lianxian (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postal_romanization" title="Postal romanization">postal</a>: Linhsien), is a <a href="/wiki/County-level_city" title="County-level city">county-level city</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, under the administration of the <a href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_city" title="Prefecture-level city">prefecture-level city</a> of <a href="/wiki/Qingyuan" title="Qingyuan">Qingyuan</a>. It is known as the host city of the Lianzhou International Photography Festival (LIPF) and as a historic and cultural landmark of Guangdong Province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lianzhou:_Looking_at_Society_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lianzhou:_Looking_at_Society-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_History_of_Lianzhou_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_History_of_Lianzhou-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianzhou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lianzhou became part of the Changsha Commandery during the <a href="/wiki/Qin_dynasty" title="Qin dynasty">Qin dynasty</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han dynasty</a>, which began in 206 BC, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangxian" title="Yangxian">Yangxian</a> prefecture consisted of three cities: Lianzhou, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liannan" title="Liannan">Liannan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lianshan_Zhuang_and_Yao_Autonomous_County" title="Lianshan Zhuang and Yao Autonomous County">Lianshan</a>. Emperor Kaihuang later put Lianzhou in the Ben prefecture during the <a href="/wiki/Sui_dynasty" title="Sui dynasty">Sui dynasty</a> (581–618 AD).</p>
<p>On December 8, 1949, the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist</a> <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> took over Lianzhou, then called Lianxian, from the <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a>. On December 20, 1949, the People’s Government of Lianxian was established. On April 22, 1994, Lianxian was renamed Lianzhou City. In 1996, the government of Guangdong Province named Lianzhou as a National Famous Historic and Cultural City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_History_of_Lianzhou_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_History_of_Lianzhou-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianzhou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Located in the northwest portion of Guangdong, Lianzhou extends from 112° 47' to 112° 7' E <a href="/wiki/Longitude" title="Longitude">longitude</a> and from 24° 37'N to 25° 12' N <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a>. It is situated on the <a href="/wiki/Lian_River" title="Lian River">Lian River</a> and the Little Bei River, southeast of <a href="/wiki/Yangshan_County" title="Yangshan County">Yangshan County</a>, southwest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liannan" title="Liannan">Liannan County</a>, and north of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Linwu" title="Linwu">Linwu County</a>. The total area under the city's administration is 2,663.33 square kilometres (1,028.32 square miles).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Geographical_Location_of_Lianzhou_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Geographical_Location_of_Lianzhou-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianzhou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lianzhou has a <a href="/wiki/Subtropics" title="Subtropics">subtropical</a> climate and is affected by the <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a>. From October through March, it experiences the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northeast_monsoon" title="Northeast monsoon">northeast monsoon</a>, and from April through September, it experiences the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_monsoon" title="Summer monsoon">summer monsoon</a>, mainly with a southwest <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">wind</a>. The city's <a href="/wiki/Alpine_climate" title="Alpine climate">mountain climate</a> is good for crop growth and four-season <a href="/wiki/Tillage" title="Tillage">tillage</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Climate_Feature_of_Lianzhou_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Climate_Feature_of_Lianzhou-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianzhou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lianzhou consists of ten towns (Lianzhou, Bao'an, Xingzi, Longping, Xian, Dongbei, Fengyang, Xijiang, Jiubei, and Dalubian) and two <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nationality_townships&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nationality townships (page does not exist)">nationality townships</a> (Yao'an and Sanshui).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-An_overview_of_Lianzhou_city_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-An_overview_of_Lianzhou_city-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cityscape">Cityscape</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianzhou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cityscape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Natural_features">Natural features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianzhou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Natural features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The natural beauty of Lianzhou has been noted by poets such as <a href="/wiki/Liu_Yuxi" title="Liu Yuxi">Liu Yuxi</a> and intellectuals such as <a href="/wiki/Han_Yu" title="Han Yu">Han Yu</a>, who wrote about the area's landscape in <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yanxiting_Ji&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yanxiting Ji (page does not exist)">Yanxiting Ji</a></i> (吾州之山水名天下).</p>
<p>In the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ming_Dynasty" title="Ming Dynasty">Ming Dynasty</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Qu_Dajun&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Qu Dajun (page does not exist)">Qu Dajun</a> explored the gorges in the <a href="/wiki/Lingnan" title="Lingnan">Lingnan</a> area and wrote <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Guangdong_Xinyu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Guangdong Xinyu (page does not exist)">Guangdong Xinyu</a></i>, in which he praised the <a href="/wiki/Huangchuan_Three_Gorges" title="Huangchuan Three Gorges">Huangchuan Three Gorges</a>.</p>
<p>Tanling Rae'era is the highest lake in Guangdong Province, 640 meters above <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">sea level</a>. The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lianzhou_Underground_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lianzhou Underground River (page does not exist)">Lianzhou Underground River</a>, which runs through four mountains, is approximately 1,500 meters in length. Called "The First Underground River in Guangdong Province", it has many oddly-shaped <a href="/wiki/Stalactite" title="Stalactite">stalactites</a>, sea fogs, steep cliffs, old pines, <a href="/wiki/Hot_spring" title="Hot spring">thermal springs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Waterfall" title="Waterfall">waterfalls</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_sites">Cultural sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianzhou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cultural sites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lianzhou's cultural monuments include <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yanxi_Arbour&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yanxi Arbour (page does not exist)">Yanxi Arbour</a>, which was built during the <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Huiguang_Tower&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Huiguang Tower (page does not exist)">Huiguang Tower</a>, which was constructed during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_and_Southern_Dynasties" title="Northern and Southern Dynasties">Northern and Southern dynasties</a> more than 1,500 years ago. It is known as the Oriental Leaning Tower for its tilt.</p>
<p>The Fushan Old Temple, one of the <a href="/wiki/Grotto-heavens" title="Grotto-heavens">Grotto-heavens</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taoists" title="Taoists">Taoists</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_and_culture_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-History_and_culture-7">[7]</a></sup> was also built during the Northern and Southern dynasties.</p>
