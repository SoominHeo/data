<p>A <b>groin attack</b> is a deliberate attempt to cause pain to the <a href="/wiki/Groin" title="Groin">groin</a> area of one's opponent. Often used in self-defense, the technique can be quickly debilitating, due to the large number of sensitive <a href="/wiki/Nerve" title="Nerve">nerve</a> endings in the <a href="/wiki/Penis" title="Penis">penis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Testicle" title="Testicle">testicles</a> of males, as well as the highly innervated <a href="/wiki/Vulva" title="Vulva">vulva</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ovaries" title="Ovaries">ovaries</a> of females. A sufficiently powerful blow may fracture the pubic bone of the victim, resulting in physical disability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This technique has been popularized as a comedic device in various forms of media.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Low_blow">Low blow</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Groin_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Low blow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Below_the_belt" title="Below the belt">Below the belt</a></div>
<p>An attack to the groin is considered to be a "<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/low_blow" title="wikt:low blow">low blow</a>" not only in the literal sense, but is the origin of the metaphor. In a playful attack, or attack in the framework of a sport, a low blow is seen as unfair or improper and is often considered <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warrior_code#Warrior_code" title="Warrior code">dishonourable</a>. Strikes to the groin are almost universally forbidden in martial arts competitions, including <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial arts</a> competitions where full-contact strikes are normal and permitted, eventually resulting in <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/penalty" title="wikt:penalty">penalties</a> and <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Disqualification" title="wikt:Disqualification">disqualifications</a>. Testicle attacks were, however, allowed until the 1980s in international Thai boxing (<a href="/wiki/Muay_Thai" title="Muay Thai">Muay Thai</a>) and are still permitted in Thailand itself (though the boxers wear cups to lessen the impact). Many martial arts organizations require competitors to wear protective cups against accidental blows, as do many other sports. Direct strikes to the groin are generally considered illegal in <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> as well, and unofficial rings may consider it shameful, however, in certain "hardcore" matches the rules are relaxed and such attacks are allowed by mutual consent.</p>
<p>In street fighting, a low blow may be considered more appropriate as a self-defense technique. The attack can allow a much weaker combatant to temporarily disable an assailant, making it easy for them to escape. When ones opponent is at close range, a knee attack to the groin is easy to execute and difficult to defend against. It is often, though not always, effective.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects_on_sexes">Effects on sexes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Groin_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Effects on sexes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Male">Male</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Groin_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Male">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Groin attacks enacted upon male victims are the most widely known, and people—male and female—are often taught to use the groin attack to discourage potential rapists and other attackers.</p>
<p>Groin strikes are debilitating and incapacitating and pose potential harm to males due to the high sensitivity of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_gonad" title="Human gonad">human gonads</a> and vulnerability of the <a href="/wiki/Testicular_pain" title="Testicular pain">external testicles</a>. The pain associated with testicular trauma travels from the testicles into the abdominal cavity through the <a href="/wiki/Spermatic_plexus" title="Spermatic plexus">spermatic plexus</a>, the primary nerve of the testicles and as a result thereof leading to shock, nausea, shortage of breath, dizziness, vomiting and blackout, depending on the severity of the injury. In extreme cases, a hard strike to the testicles can cause one or both testicles to rupture, potentially sterilizing or even killing the victim. In such cases hospitalization is necessary and if the testicle cannot be repaired, it must be removed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Female">Female</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Groin_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Female">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Contrary to depictions in popular culture, in which females are no more vulnerable in their groin than anywhere else, a strike or squeeze to the <a href="/wiki/Vulva" title="Vulva">vulva</a> or <a href="/wiki/Clitoris" title="Clitoris">clitoris</a> and ovaries will cause considerable pain and can incapacitate females due to the high density of nerve endings in these structures. The pain associated with a trauma to the clitoris travels through the <a href="/wiki/Pudendal_nerve" title="Pudendal nerve">pudendal nerve</a> and travels through the <a href="/wiki/Ovarian_plexus" title="Ovarian plexus">ovarian plexus</a> for a trauma to the ovaries. In extreme cases, the female victim may vomit or black out.</p>
<p>Both males and females have external genitalia which are very susceptible to blunt trauma due to the soft and tender structure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> If delivered with enough force, blunt force applied to the female genitalia can drive the clitoris up into the pubic bone, compressing the clitoris between the material and the victim's pubic bone. Potential long-term health problems can arise for female targets of groin attacks including nerve damage to the clitoris, the pubic bone fracture because women's bones are thinner (and the bone of the pelvis of women is thinner and may break), <a href="/wiki/Hematoma" title="Hematoma">hematoma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vulvodynia" title="Vulvodynia">vulvodynia</a> and rupture of the ovaries (<a href="/wiki/Ovarian_apoplexy" title="Ovarian apoplexy">ovarian apoplexy</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> An attack to the female genitalia may lead to nerve damage which may affect sexual arousal.</p>

<p><i>When men fight with one another, and the wife of the one approaches to rescue her husband from the hand of him who is beating him, and puts out her hand and seizes his genitals, you shall sever her hand; you shall have no compassion.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
