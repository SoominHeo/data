<p><b>Logar</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pashto_language" title="Pashto language">Pashto</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ps" xml:lang="ps">لوګر</span>‎, <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">لوگَر</span>‎‎) is one of the 34 <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Afghanistan" title="Provinces of Afghanistan">provinces of Afghanistan</a>, located in the eastern section of the country. It is divided into seven <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Afghanistan" title="Districts of Afghanistan">districts</a> and contains hundreds of villages. <a href="/wiki/Puli_Alam" title="Puli Alam">Puli Alam</a> is the capital of the province.</p>
<p>As of 2013, Logar has a population of about 373,100.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cso_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-cso-1">[1]</a></sup> It is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multiethnic_society" title="Multiethnic society">multi-ethnic</a> tribal society, with about 60% belonging to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pashtun_people" title="Pashtun people">Pashtun</a> group and the rest being <a href="/wiki/Tajiks" title="Tajiks">Tajiks</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hazara_people" title="Hazara people">Hazaras</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-understandingwar_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-understandingwar-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The word of Logar is a combination of two Pashto words: <i>Loy</i> (لوى "Big") and <i>Ghar</i> (غر "mountain"). The <a href="/wiki/Logar_River" title="Logar River">Logar River</a> enters the province through the west and leaves to the north.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logar_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Afghanistan" title="History of Afghanistan">History of Afghanistan</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre_Islamic_era">Pre Islamic era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logar_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre Islamic era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mauryan_Empire" title="Mauryan Empire">Mauryan Empire</a></div>
<p>The Logar province territory fell to the <a href="/wiki/Maurya_Empire" title="Maurya Empire">Maurya Empire</a>, which was led by <a href="/wiki/Chandragupta_Maurya" title="Chandragupta Maurya">Chandragupta Maurya</a>. The Mauryas introduced <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a> to the region, and were planning to capture more territory of Central Asia until they faced local Greco-Bactrian forces. Seleucus is said to have reached a <a href="/wiki/Peace_treaty" title="Peace treaty">peace treaty</a> with Chandragupta by giving control of the territory south of the Hindu Kush to the Mauryas upon intermarriage and 500 elephants.</p>

<p>Having consolidated power in the northwest, Chandragupta pushed east towards the Nanda Empire. Afghanistan's significant ancient tangible and intangible <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pre_Islamic_Hindu_and_Buddhist_heritage_of_Afghanistan" title="Pre Islamic Hindu and Buddhist heritage of Afghanistan">Buddhist heritage</a> is recorded through wide-ranging archaeological finds, including religious and artistic remnants. Buddhist doctrines are reported to have reached as far as <a href="/wiki/Balkh" title="Balkh">Balkh</a> even during the life of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddha" title="Buddha">Buddha</a> (563 BCE to 483 BCE), as recorded by <a href="/wiki/Xuanzang" title="Xuanzang">Husang Tsang</a>.</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>In this context a legend recorded by Husang Tsang refers to the first two lay disciples of Buddha, Trapusa and <a href="/wiki/Bahlikas" title="Bahlikas">Bhallika</a> responsible for introducing <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a> in that country. Originally these two were merchants of the kingdom of Balhika, as the name Bhalluka or Bhallika probably suggests the association of one with that country. They had gone to India for trade and had happened to be at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bodhgaya" title="Bodhgaya">Bodhgaya</a> when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buddha" title="Buddha">Buddha</a> had just attained enlightenment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logar_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_war_in_Afghanistan" title="Soviet war in Afghanistan">Soviet war in Afghanistan</a>, Logar was known among some Afghans as the Bab al-Jihad (Gates of Jihad) because it became a fierce theatre of war between US-backed/trained <a href="/wiki/Mujahideen" title="Mujahideen">mujahideen</a> groups and the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Afghanistan" title="Democratic Republic of Afghanistan">Soviet-backed Afghan government troops</a>. It was one of the main supply routes of mujahideen rebels coming from Pakistan. Like other parts of the country, Logar has also seen heavy fighting during the 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kakar_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kakar-6">[6]</a></sup> Swedish journalist Borge Almqvist, who visited the province in 1982, wrote that: "Everywhere in the Logar province the most common sight except for ruins are graves".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Soviet operations included using bombing, the use of flammable liquids to burn alive people in hiding, poisoning of drinking water, and destruction of crops and farmland. One writer who witnessed the events argue that the Soviet actions in Logar amounted to genocide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kakar_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kakar-6">[6]</a></sup> Some of the notable rebel fighters were Fazlullah Mujaddidi, Sayed Rasool Hashimi, Malim Tor, Mohammad Wali Nasiri, Taher Shirzad, Asadullah Fallah.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>By 1995 the province had fallen to the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Emirate_of_Afghanistan" title="Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan">Taliban government</a>. After the removal of the Taliban and formation of the <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_Hamid_Karzai" title="Presidency of Hamid Karzai">Karzai administration</a> in late 2001, the <a href="/wiki/International_Security_Assistance_Force" title="International Security Assistance Force">International Security Assistance Force</a> (ISAF) and <a href="/wiki/Afghan_National_Security_Forces" title="Afghan National Security Forces">Afghan National Security Forces</a> (ANSF) gradually took over security of the area. The <a href="/wiki/Provincial_Reconstruction_Team_Logar" title="Provincial Reconstruction Team Logar">Provincial Reconstruction Team Logar</a> (PRT Logar) was established in March 2008. It provided a number of benefits to the locals, including security, development, and jobs.</p>
<p>In the meantime, <a href="/wiki/Taliban_insurgency" title="Taliban insurgency">Taliban insurgents</a> are often causing major disturbances in the area. This includes major attacks on key projects, suicide bombings in civilian area, and assassinations of Afghan government employees. On 19 August 2014, a major <a href="/wiki/Taliban" title="Taliban">Taliban</a> offensive took place with 700 militants aiming to take control of the province,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> while the <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>-led foreign force mistakenly killed three civilians in an air strike in December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logar_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Afghanistan" title="Geography of Afghanistan">Geography of Afghanistan</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Khoshi_valley.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="540" data-file-width="720" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Khoshi_valley.jpg/220px-Khoshi_valley.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Khoshi_valley.jpg/330px-Khoshi_valley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Khoshi_valley.jpg/440px-Khoshi_valley.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Khoshi_valley.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The main river valley in the <a href="/wiki/Khoshi_District" title="Khoshi District">Khoshi District</a> of Logar province. Extensive irrigation and canal works, known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karez" title="Karez">karez</a>, provide water for the majority of the agriculture in southeastern Afghanistan.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Logar can be generally described as a relatively flat river valley in the north and central regions, surrounded by rugged mountains to the east, south, and southwest. The district of Azra, in the east, consists almost entirely of mountains, while travel to the Paktia Province to the south is limited to the <a href="/wiki/Tera_Pass" title="Tera Pass">Tera Pass</a>, a 2896 m high road that was recently completed as part of the international reconstruction effort in Afghanistan. The Kabul-Gardez Highway runs north-south through Logar Province, from the Mohammed Agha District south to the Paktiya Province border.</p>
<p>Although the government of Afghanistan recognizes the Azra district as being in Logar, many widely accepted maps include it in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paktia" title="Paktia">Paktia</a> province to the south.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_governance">Politics and governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logar_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Politics and governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Afghanistan" title="Politics of Afghanistan">Politics of Afghanistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_governors_of_Logar" title="List of governors of Logar">List of governors of Logar</a></div>
<p>The last governor of the province was <a href="/wiki/Arsala_Jamal" title="Arsala Jamal">Arsala Jamal</a>. He was assassinated by anti-Afghan forces,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (October 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> often described as <a href="/wiki/Taliban" title="Taliban">Taliban</a> militants. All <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_in_Afghanistan" title="Law enforcement in Afghanistan">law enforcement</a> activities throughout the province are managed by the <a href="/wiki/Afghan_National_Police" title="Afghan National Police">Afghan National Police</a> (ANP). The border with neighboring Pakistan is monitored by the <a href="/wiki/Afghan_Border_Police" title="Afghan Border Police">Afghan Border Police</a> (ABP). A provincial police chief is assigned to lead both the ANP and the ABP. The Police Chief represents the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Interior_(Afghanistan)" title="Ministry of the Interior (Afghanistan)">Ministry of the Interior</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kabul" title="Kabul">Kabul</a>. The ANP and ABP are backed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_of_Afghanistan" title="Military of Afghanistan">military</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/International_Security_Assistance_Force" title="International Security Assistance Force">NATO</a>-led forces.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Capital">Capital</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logar_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Capital">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Puli_Alam.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="479" data-file-width="720" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Puli_Alam.jpg/220px-Puli_Alam.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Puli_Alam.jpg/330px-Puli_Alam.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Puli_Alam.jpg/440px-Puli_Alam.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Puli_Alam.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Puli_Alam" title="Puli Alam">Puli Alam</a>, the capital of Logar province.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Logar's capital is the city of <a href="/wiki/Puli_Alam" title="Puli Alam">Puli Alam</a>, located in the district of the same name. It sits on the main road running from Kabul south to <a href="/wiki/Gardez" title="Gardez">Gardez</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khowst" title="Khowst">Khowst</a> province.</p>
<p>Puli Alam has seen reconstruction since the fall of the Taliban. The main road to Kabul was completed in 2006, significantly reducing travel time to the national capital. Additional projects include numerous schools, radio stations, government facilities, and a major Afghan National Police base situated just south of the city.</p>
<p>Like most Afghan cities, there is little municipal planning or services. Electricity is provided by diesel generators, and wells are the primary source of drinking water.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Healthcare">Healthcare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logar_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Healthcare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Health_in_Afghanistan" title="Health in Afghanistan">Health in Afghanistan</a></div>
<p>The percentage of households without clean drinking water fell from 45% in 2005 to 14% in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cimicweb.org_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-cimicweb.org-10">[10]</a></sup> The percentage of births attended to by a skilled birth attendant increased from 9% in 2005 to 73% in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cimicweb.org_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-cimicweb.org-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logar_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Afghanistan" title="Education in Afghanistan">Education in Afghanistan</a></div>
<p>The overall literacy rate (6+ years of age) increased from 21% in 2005 to 30% in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cimicweb.org_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-cimicweb.org-10">[10]</a></sup> The overall net enrolment rate (6–13 years of age) increased from 22% in 2005 to 45% in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cimicweb.org_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-cimicweb.org-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The overall literacy rate in Logar province was 21% in 2005 however, while nearly one-third (31%) of men are literate this is true for just under one-tenth (9%) of women. There are around 168 primary and secondary schools in the province catering for 81,538 students. There are nearly 2,082 teachers working in schools in the Logar province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mrrd_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-mrrd-11">[11]</a></sup> There are several girls schools in the province, mostly located in Koshi and Pul-e-alam. Due to the large Taliban presence in Chark and Baraki Barak, the freedom of women in Logar does not always allow for an education.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCS_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-CCS-12">[12]</a></sup> As of 2007, the province had a literacy rate of 17%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCS_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-CCS-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>As of 2013, the total population of Logar province is about 373,100,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cso_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-cso-1">[1]</a></sup> which is multi-ethnic and a tribal society. About 60 percent of Logar's residents belong to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pashtun_people" title="Pashtun people">Pashtun</a> group; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tajik_people" title="Tajik people">Tajiks</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hazara_people" title="Hazara people">Hazaras</a> form the remainder of the population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-understandingwar_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-understandingwar-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCS_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-CCS-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logar_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Sport_in_Afghanistan" title="Sport in Afghanistan">Sport in Afghanistan</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">Cricket</a> is the most popular sport in the province. Logar has been a major supplier of players for the <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan_national_cricket_team" title="Afghanistan national cricket team">Afghanistan national cricket team</a>. Former players include Dawlat Ahmadzai, Ahmad Shah Pakteen and former national team captain Raees Ahmadzai. Among the active national players hailing from Logar are: <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Nabi" title="Mohammad Nabi">Mohammad Nabi</a> (captain of the national team), Shahpoor Zadran, Hashmatullah Shahidi, Nasir Jamal Ahmadzai and Gulbadin Naib Ahmadzai.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Football_in_Afghanistan" title="Football in Afghanistan">Football</a> is the second most popular sport in the province. De Abasin Sapih (meaning "Weaves of Abasin") plays at Afghanistan premier league. Abasin means "the father of the rivers" in Pashto language and refers to the <a href="/wiki/Indus_River" title="Indus River">Indus River</a>. The team represents the provinces of Paktia, Logar, Paktika and <a href="/wiki/Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">Khyber Pakhtunkhwa</a>.</p>
