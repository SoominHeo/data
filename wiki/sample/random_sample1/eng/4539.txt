<p><b>Ronald Runaldo Venetiaan</b> (born June 18, 1936) served as the 6th and 8th <a href="/wiki/President_of_Suriname" title="President of Suriname">President of Suriname</a>. His first term as president ran from 1991 to 1996 after which he lost in the elections to <a href="/wiki/Jules_Wijdenbosch" title="Jules Wijdenbosch">Jules Wijdenbosch</a>. In 2000 however, he regained his former position on the <a href="/wiki/New_Front_for_Democracy_and_Development" title="New Front for Democracy and Development">New Front</a> banner, receiving an absolute majority of 37 from 51 votes in the Parliament.</p>
<p>In 2005 he was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surinamese_legislative_election,_2005" title="Surinamese legislative election, 2005">re-elected</a> to serve a third term as president and sworn in on August 12, 2005.</p>

<p>Venetiaan is a mathematician<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> beside all political activity. His surname means <i><a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venetian</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>.</p>
