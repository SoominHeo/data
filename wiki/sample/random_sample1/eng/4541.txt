<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ronald Reagan Freedom Award</h1>

<p>The <b>Ronald Reagan Freedom Award</b> is the highest civilian honor bestowed by the private <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan_Presidential_Library" title="Ronald Reagan Presidential Library">Ronald Reagan Presidential Foundation</a>. The award is given to "those who have made monumental and lasting contributions to the cause of freedom worldwide."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Ronald_Reagan_Freedom_Award_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Ronald_Reagan_Freedom_Award-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Until her death, the award was given by Former <a href="/wiki/First_Lady_of_the_United_States" title="First Lady of the United States">First Lady</a> <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Reagan" title="Nancy Reagan">Nancy Reagan</a> on behalf of her husband, who died in June 2004. The award was first given in 1992, by President <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a> himself, as well as in 1993, but in 1994 Mrs. Reagan presented the award instead of her husband. Ronald Reagan had been diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Alzheimer%27s_disease" title="Alzheimer's disease">Alzheimer's disease</a> a few months before, and was not able to attend the ceremony.</p>
<p>In order to receive the award, the potential recipient must "have made monumental and lasting contributions to the cause of freedom worldwide," as well as "embody President Reagan's lifelong belief that one man or woman truly can make a difference."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Ronald_Reagan_Freedom_Award_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Ronald_Reagan_Freedom_Award-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recipients">Recipients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ronald_Reagan_Freedom_Award&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Recipients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Former President <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George H. W. Bush</a>, who was awarded the medal on February 6, 2007, the anniversary of Ronald Reagan's 96th birthday, remarked, "I wish I had a little Ronald Reagan in me when it came to communicating with the American people. Had I been blessed with my predecessor's remarkable skill, who knows? I might still be employed." On a more serious note, he said later in the speech: "Working with Ronald Reagan was one of the greatest joys of my life."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Bush served as Reagan's <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_United_States" title="Vice President of the United States">Vice President</a> for the eight years that Reagan was <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a>.</p>
<p>On September 17, 2008, the award was presented to former Soviet dissident <a href="/wiki/Natan_Sharansky" title="Natan Sharansky">Natan Sharansky</a> by former first lady Nancy Reagan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>1992 – <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev" title="Mikhail Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</a>, former General Secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a></li>
<li>1993 – <a href="/wiki/Colin_Powell" title="Colin Powell">General Colin Powell</a>, former <a href="/wiki/National_Security_Advisor_(United_States)" title="National Security Advisor (United States)">National Security Advisor</a> to President Reagan</li>
<li>1994 – <a href="/wiki/Yitzhak_Rabin" title="Yitzhak Rabin">Yitzhak Rabin</a>, former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Israel" title="Prime Minister of Israel">Prime Minister of Israel</a></li>
<li>1995 – <a href="/wiki/Hussein_of_Jordan" title="Hussein of Jordan">King Hussein I</a>, then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Jordan" title="King of Jordan">King of Jordan</a></li>
<li>1997 – <a href="/wiki/Bob_Hope" title="Bob Hope">Bob Hope</a>, former entertainer.</li>
<li>1998 – <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Thatcher" title="Margaret Thatcher">Margaret Thatcher</a>, former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister of the United Kingdom</a></li>
<li>2000 – <a href="/wiki/Billy_Graham" title="Billy Graham">The Reverend Billy Graham</a>, evangelical minister</li>
<li>2002 – <a href="/wiki/Rudy_Giuliani" title="Rudy Giuliani">Rudy Giuliani</a>, former mayor of New York</li>
<li>2007 – <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George H. W. Bush</a>, former <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, served under Reagan as <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_United_States" title="Vice President of the United States">Vice President of the United States</a></li>
<li>2008 – <a href="/wiki/Natan_Sharansky" title="Natan Sharansky">Natan Sharansky</a>, former <a href="/wiki/KGB" title="KGB">KGB</a> prisoner, human rights activist, Israeli politician.</li>
<li>2011 – <a href="/wiki/Lech_Wa%C5%82%C4%99sa" title="Lech Wałęsa">Lech Wałęsa</a>, former <a href="/wiki/Solidarity_(Polish_trade_union)" title="Solidarity (Polish trade union)">Solidarity</a> leader and former president of Poland</li>
</ul>
