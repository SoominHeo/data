<p><b>Jean Ronny Johnsen</b> (born 10 June 1969) is a Norwegian former <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played at both professional and international levels as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre back</a> or <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>.</p>
<p>Johnsen played club football in Norway, Turkey, and England for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sem_IF&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sem IF (page does not exist)">Sem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stokke_IL" title="Stokke IL">Stokke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eik-T%C3%B8nsberg" title="Eik-Tønsberg">Eik-Tønsberg</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FK_Lyn" title="FK Lyn">Lyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lillestr%C3%B8m_SK" title="Lillestrøm SK">Lillestrøm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Be%C5%9Fikta%C5%9F_J.K." title="Beşiktaş J.K.">Beşiktaş</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> and <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A5lerenga_Fotball" title="Vålerenga Fotball">Vålerenga</a>.</p>
<p>He was capped 62 times for <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a>, and competed at the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1998 FIFA World Cup">1998 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ronny_Johnsen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Sandefjord" title="Sandefjord">Sandefjord</a>, Johnsen grew up in <a href="/wiki/Stokke" title="Stokke">Stokke</a> where he played for the local sides <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sem_IF&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sem IF (page does not exist)">Sem IF</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stokke_IL" title="Stokke IL">Stokke IL</a> during his youth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-karriereh.C3.B8ydepunkter_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-karriereh.C3.B8ydepunkter-1">[1]</a></sup> He started his senior career as a striker in the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_First_Division" title="Norwegian First Division">second tier</a> side <a href="/wiki/Eik-T%C3%B8nsberg" title="Eik-Tønsberg">Eik-Tønsberg</a> in 1987,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> but was retrained as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre back</a> by head coach <a href="/wiki/Nils_Johan_Semb" title="Nils Johan Semb">Nils Johan Semb</a>. Johnsen wanted to play as a striker and after he transferred to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FK_Lyn" title="FK Lyn">Lyn</a> ahead of the 1992 season he played as a striker for the club in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Tippeligaen</a>. Half-way through the 1994 season, Johnsen transferred to <a href="/wiki/Lillestr%C3%B8m_SK" title="Lillestrøm SK">Lillestrøm</a>, where he was used more as a centre back then a striker,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoF_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-HoF-3">[3]</a></sup> before he moved to <a href="/wiki/Be%C5%9Fikta%C5%9F_J.K." title="Beşiktaş J.K.">Beşiktaş</a> in 1995. After a half season in Turkey, Johnsen was approached by <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> manager, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Ferguson" title="Alex Ferguson">Alex Ferguson</a> who wanted to sign him. Johnsen turned down the first offer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> but transferred to Manchester United during the summer of 1996, and signed a five-year contract with the club. With a fee of £1.2million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Johnsen became the then most expensive Norwegian defender.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-snl_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-snl-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>While at Manchester United, he won 4 league titles (1997, 1999, 2000, and 2001), and one <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> in 1999 with the club. He was also a part of the team that won the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> in 1999. He covered the central midfield position in Manchester United's away win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juventus_FC" title="Juventus FC">Juventus</a> in the semi-final in a game that is considered by many to be the best of his career.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (March 2013)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> He also played the whole of the final in Barcelona, this time in defence, alongside <a href="/wiki/Jaap_Stam" title="Jaap Stam">Jaap Stam</a>. He has the rare distinction of also winning four medals in four consecutive appearances with the club. He was on the pitch for the title clincher, FA Cup final, and Champions League final in 1999, and because of injury, the 2000 league title clincher against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>. Although he fell well short of the 10 appearances required for a title winner's medal in 2000, he received a title winner's medal through special dispensation – similar to the one credited to <a href="/wiki/Dion_Dublin" title="Dion Dublin">Dion Dublin</a> for the same reason seven years earlier. The following season, he just met the criteria for a title medal.</p>
<p>While at Manchester United, he scored eight goals in all competitions – the first coming against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FA_Charity_Shield" title="FA Charity Shield">FA Charity Shield</a> in August 1997. He remained with them until his contract expired at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_in_English_football" title="2001–02 in English football">2001–02</a> season, when he was given a free transfer.</p>
<p>In 2002, Johnsen and fellow Norwegian international <a href="/wiki/%C3%98yvind_Leonhardsen" title="Øyvind Leonhardsen">Øyvind Leonhardsen</a> had a trial at German side Schalke 04. After that move fell through, Johnsen signed with <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>. In two years at Aston Villa, he scored once in the league, against Leeds United in February 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After leaving Aston Villa, Johnsen was offered a trial at <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> and played against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chievo_Verona" title="Chievo Verona">Chievo Verona</a> in a friendly, ultimately he could not agree terms with the club. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Johnsen then signed for <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> but only played a handful of games before being released with concerns over his fitness levels.</p>
<p>In February 2005, Johnsen announced his retirement from professional football. Shortly afterwards he changed his mind, signing a one-year contract with the <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> club <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A5lerenga_Fotball" title="Vålerenga Fotball">Vålerenga</a>. Subsequently, he renewed this contract three times for the following 2006, 2007 and 2008 seasons.</p>
<p>Johnsen retired as an active football player on 3 November 2008, with Vålerenga losing their final game of the 2008 season 1–0 to <a href="/wiki/SK_Brann" title="SK Brann">SK Brann</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ronny_Johnsen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Johnsen did not represent Norway at youth level; but while Johnsen was playing for Eik at the second tier,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-karriereh.C3.B8ydepunkter_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-karriereh.C3.B8ydepunkter-1">[1]</a></sup> national team coach <a href="/wiki/Egil_Olsen" title="Egil Olsen">Egil Olsen</a>, because of a recommendation from Nils Johan Semb,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoF_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-HoF-3">[3]</a></sup> included Johnsen in his squad without having seen him play. Johnsen made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norwegian national team</a> in a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a> in 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-karriereh.C3.B8ydepunkter_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-karriereh.C3.B8ydepunkter-1">[1]</a></sup> He played as wing, striker and midfielder during his early career at the national team, before he was used primarily as a centre back. Johnsen played most of the matches in the <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="1994 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">qualification</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1994 FIFA World Cup">1994 FIFA World Cup</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoF_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-HoF-3">[3]</a></sup> and played as a striker when Norway won 3–0 away against <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a> in 1993 and qualified for the World Cup,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> but was left out of the squad that travelled to the United States. Four years later, Johnsen was an obvious choice as a centre back, and played all four matches in the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1998 FIFA World Cup">1998 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoF_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-HoF-3">[3]</a></sup> Johnsen played 61 matches for Norway, until May 2004 when he played against <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Three years later, he made a comeback for the national team, and played the whole match home against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>, which was Johnsen's last match at international level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoF_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-HoF-3">[3]</a></sup> He was 38 years and 73 days when he was last capped, making him the second oldest player for the Norwegian national team, only beaten by <a href="/wiki/Gunnar_Thoresen_(footballer)" title="Gunnar Thoresen (footballer)">Gunnar Thoresen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
