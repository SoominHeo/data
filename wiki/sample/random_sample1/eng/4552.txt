<p><b>Rodney Sturt "Rod" Taylor</b> (11 January 1930 – 7 January 2015) was an Australian actor of film and television. He appeared in over 50 films, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Catered_Affair" title="The Catered Affair">The Catered Affair</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Time_Machine_(1960_film)" title="The Time Machine (1960 film)">The Time Machine</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/One_Hundred_and_One_Dalmatians" title="One Hundred and One Dalmatians">One Hundred and One Dalmatians</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Seven_Seas_to_Calais" title="Seven Seas to Calais">Seven Seas to Calais</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Birds_(film)" title="The Birds (film)">The Birds</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_in_New_York" title="Sunday in New York">Sunday in New York</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Cassidy" title="Young Cassidy">Young Cassidy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_of_the_Sun" title="Dark of the Sun">Dark of the Sun</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Liquidator_(1965_film)" title="The Liquidator (1965 film)">The Liquidator</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darker_Than_Amber_(film)" title="Darker Than Amber (film)">Darker Than Amber</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Train_Robbers" title="The Train Robbers">The Train Robbers</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino's</a> 2009 film <i><a href="/wiki/Inglourious_Basterds" title="Inglourious Basterds">Inglourious Basterds</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a>, which was his final film appearance.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rod_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taylor was born on 11 January 1930<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Lidcombe,_New_South_Wales" title="Lidcombe, New South Wales">Lidcombe</a>, a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, the only child of William Sturt Taylor, a steel construction contractor and commercial artist, and Mona Taylor (née Thompson), a writer of more than a hundred short stories and children's books. His middle name comes from his great-great grand uncle, Captain <a href="/wiki/Charles_Sturt" title="Charles Sturt">Charles Sturt</a>, a British explorer of the Australian Outback in the 19th century.</p>
<p>Taylor attended <a href="/wiki/Parramatta_High_School" title="Parramatta High School">Parramatta High School</a> and later studied at the <a href="/wiki/UNSW_Art_%26_Design" title="UNSW Art &amp; Design">East Sydney Technical and Fine Arts College</a>. For a time he worked as a commercial artist, but decided to become an actor after seeing <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier" title="Laurence Olivier">Laurence Olivier</a> in an <a href="/wiki/The_Old_Vic" title="The Old Vic">Old Vic</a> touring production of <i><a href="/wiki/Richard_III_(play)" title="Richard III (play)">Richard III</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rod_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rod_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Taylor acquired extensive radio and stage experience in Australia, where his radio work included a period on <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Hills_(radio_serial)" title="Blue Hills (radio serial)">Blue Hills</a></i> and a role as <a href="/wiki/Tarzan" title="Tarzan">Tarzan</a>. Earlier in his career he had to support himself by working at Sydney's <a href="/wiki/Mark_Foy%27s" title="Mark Foy's">Mark Foy's</a> department store, designing and painting window and other displays during the day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1951, he took part in a re-enactment of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Sturt" title="Charles Sturt">Charles Sturt</a>'s voyage down the Murrumbidgee and Murray Rivers, playing Sturt's offsider, <a href="/wiki/George_Macleay" title="George Macleay">George Macleay</a>. A short documentary, <i><a href="/wiki/Inland_with_Sturt" title="Inland with Sturt">Inland with Sturt</a></i> (1951), was based on it. Taylor also appeared in a number of theatre productions for Australia's <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Theatre_(Australia)" title="Mercury Theatre (Australia)">Mercury Theatre</a>.</p>
<p>Taylor made his feature film debut in the Australian <a href="/wiki/Lee_Robinson_(director)" title="Lee Robinson (director)">Lee Robinson</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Coral_Sea" title="King of the Coral Sea">King of the Coral Sea</a></i> (1954), playing an American. He later played <a href="/wiki/Israel_Hands" title="Israel Hands">Israel Hands</a> in a Hollywood-financed film shot in Sydney, <i><a href="/wiki/Long_John_Silver_(film)" title="Long John Silver (film)">Long John Silver</a></i> (1954), an unofficial sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/Treasure_Island" title="Treasure Island">Treasure Island</a></i>. Following these two films, Taylor was awarded the 1954 Rola Show Australian Radio Actor of the Year Award,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> which included a ticket to London via Los Angeles, but Taylor did not continue on to London.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hollywood">Hollywood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rod_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hollywood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Taylor soon landed roles in television shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/Studio_57" title="Studio 57">Studio 57</a></i> and the films <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_on_Frisco_Bay" title="Hell on Frisco Bay">Hell on Frisco Bay</a></i> (1955) and <i><a href="/wiki/Giant_(1956_film)" title="Giant (1956 film)">Giant</a></i> (1956). In 1955, he guest-starred in the third episode ("The Argonauts") of the first hour-long <a href="/wiki/Western_(genre)" title="Western (genre)">Western</a> television series, <i><a href="/wiki/Cheyenne_(1955_TV_series)" title="Cheyenne (1955 TV series)">Cheyenne</a></i>, an <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> program starring <a href="/wiki/Clint_Walker" title="Clint Walker">Clint Walker</a>. Taylor and <a href="/wiki/Edward_Andrews" title="Edward Andrews">Edward Andrews</a> played gold seekers Clancy and Duncan, respectively, who are best friends until they strike it rich, only to see Native Americans release their gold dust to the wind. The episode was a remake of the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Treasure_of_the_Sierra_Madre" title="The Treasure of the Sierra Madre">The Treasure of the Sierra Madre</a></i> (1948). Taylor was considered for one of the leads in <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Television" title="Warner Bros. Television">Warner Bros. Television</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Maverick_(TV_series)" title="Maverick (TV series)">Maverick</a></i>.</p>
<p>Toward the end of 1955, Taylor unsuccessfully <a href="/wiki/Screen_test" title="Screen test">screen tested</a> to play boxer <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Graziano" title="Rocky Graziano">Rocky Graziano</a> in <a href="/wiki/Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer" title="Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer">Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Somebody_Up_There_Likes_Me_(1956_film)" title="Somebody Up There Likes Me (1956 film)">Somebody Up There Likes Me</a></i> after <a href="/wiki/James_Dean" title="James Dean">James Dean</a>'s death, but his use of a Brooklyn accent and physical prowess in the test impressed the studio enough to gain him a long-term contract. At MGM, he played a series of support roles in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Catered_Affair" title="The Catered Affair">The Catered Affair</a></i> (1956),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Raintree_County_(film)" title="Raintree County (film)">Raintree County</a></i> (1957), and <i><a href="/wiki/Ask_Any_Girl_(film)" title="Ask Any Girl (film)">Ask Any Girl</a></i> (1959). He had a significant role in <i><a href="/wiki/Separate_Tables_(film)" title="Separate Tables (film)">Separate Tables</a></i> (1958), which won Oscars for two of its stars, <a href="/wiki/David_Niven" title="David Niven">David Niven</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wendy_Hiller" title="Wendy Hiller">Wendy Hiller</a>. He also made a strong impression guest-starring in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Zone" title="The Twilight Zone">The Twilight Zone</a></i> titled "<a href="/wiki/And_When_the_Sky_Was_Opened" title="And When the Sky Was Opened">And When the Sky Was Opened</a>" (1959).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stardom">Stardom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rod_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stardom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Taylor's first leading role in a feature film was in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Time_Machine_(1960_film)" title="The Time Machine (1960 film)">The Time Machine</a></i> (1960), <a href="/wiki/George_Pal" title="George Pal">George Pal</a>'s adaptation of the science-fiction classic by <a href="/wiki/H._G._Wells" title="H. G. Wells">H. G. Wells</a> with Taylor as the time traveler who, thousands of years in the future, falls for a woman played by <a href="/wiki/Yvette_Mimieux" title="Yvette Mimieux">Yvette Mimieux</a>. Taylor played a character not unlike that of his <i>Twilight Zone</i> episode of a year earlier and the film <i><a href="/wiki/World_Without_End_(film)" title="World Without End (film)">World Without End</a></i> in 1956.</p>
<p>In the 1960–1961 television season, Taylor starred as foreign correspondent Glenn Evans in the ABC dramatic series <i><a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_(TV_series)" title="Hong Kong (TV series)">Hong Kong</a></i>. His principal co-star was <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Bochner" title="Lloyd Bochner">Lloyd Bochner</a>; <a href="/wiki/Jack_Kruschen" title="Jack Kruschen">Jack Kruschen</a> played the bartender, Tully. The program faced stiff competition on Wednesday evenings from <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Wagon_Train" title="Wagon Train">Wagon Train</a></i>, hence lasted for only one season. He voiced Pongo (a Dalmatian dog) in <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a>'s animated feature <i><a href="/wiki/One_Hundred_and_One_Dalmatians" title="One Hundred and One Dalmatians">One Hundred and One Dalmatians</a></i> (1961) and also guest-starred on <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Maxwell" title="Marilyn Maxwell">Marilyn Maxwell</a>'s short-lived ABC series <i><a href="/wiki/Bus_Stop_(TV_series)" title="Bus Stop (TV series)">Bus Stop</a></i> around the same time. In 1962, he starred in an episode of NBC's <i><a href="/wiki/The_DuPont_Show_of_the_Week" title="The DuPont Show of the Week">The DuPont Show of the Week</a></i> ("<a href="/wiki/The_Ordeal_of_Dr._Shannon" title="The Ordeal of Dr. Shannon">The Ordeal of Dr. Shannon</a>"), an adaptation of <a href="/wiki/A._J._Cronin" title="A. J. Cronin">A. J. Cronin</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/Shannon%27s_Way" title="Shannon's Way">Shannon's Way</a></i>.</p>
<p>Taylor starred in <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Hitchcock" title="Alfred Hitchcock">Alfred Hitchcock</a>'s horror/thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Birds_(film)" title="The Birds (film)">The Birds</a></i> (1963), along with <a href="/wiki/Tippi_Hedren" title="Tippi Hedren">Tippi Hedren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suzanne_Pleshette" title="Suzanne Pleshette">Suzanne Pleshette</a>, <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Cartwright" title="Veronica Cartwright">Veronica Cartwright</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Tandy" title="Jessica Tandy">Jessica Tandy</a>, playing a man whose town and home come under attack by menacing birds. Taylor then starred with <a href="/wiki/Jane_Fonda" title="Jane Fonda">Jane Fonda</a> in the romantic comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_in_New_York" title="Sunday in New York">Sunday in New York</a></i> (also 1963).</p>
<p>During the mid-1960s, Taylor worked mostly for MGM. His credits including <i><a href="/wiki/The_V.I.P.s_(film)" title="The V.I.P.s (film)">The V.I.P.s</a></i> (1963), his first feature film role as an Australian, with <a href="/wiki/Richard_Burton" title="Richard Burton">Richard Burton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Taylor" title="Elizabeth Taylor">Elizabeth Taylor</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Maggie_Smith" title="Maggie Smith">Maggie Smith</a>; <i><a href="/wiki/Fate_Is_the_Hunter_(film)" title="Fate Is the Hunter (film)">Fate Is the Hunter</a></i> (for 20th Century Fox, 1964) with <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Ford" title="Glenn Ford">Glenn Ford</a> and Suzanne Pleshette; <i><a href="/wiki/36_Hours_(1965_film)" title="36 Hours (1965 film)">36 Hours</a></i> (1964) with James Garner; <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Cassidy" title="Young Cassidy">Young Cassidy</a></i> (1965) with <a href="/wiki/Julie_Christie" title="Julie Christie">Julie Christie</a> and Maggie Smith; <i><a href="/wiki/The_Liquidator_(1965_film)" title="The Liquidator (1965 film)">The Liquidator</a></i> (1965) with <a href="/wiki/Jill_St._John" title="Jill St. John">Jill St. John</a>; and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Glass_Bottom_Boat" title="The Glass Bottom Boat">The Glass Bottom Boat</a></i> (1966) with <a href="/wiki/Doris_Day" title="Doris Day">Doris Day</a>.</p>
<p>He began to change his image toward the end of the decade to more tough-guy roles, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Chuka_(film)" title="Chuka (film)">Chuka</a></i> (1967), which he also produced, and starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_of_the_Sun" title="Dark of the Sun">Dark of the Sun</a></i> (or <i>The Mercenaries</i>, 1968) again with Yvette Mimieux; <i><a href="/wiki/Nobody_Runs_Forever" title="Nobody Runs Forever">Nobody Runs Forever</a></i> (1968) where he played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_South_Wales_Police" title="New South Wales Police">New South Wales Police</a> Sergeant <a href="/wiki/Scobie_Malone" title="Scobie Malone">Scobie Malone</a>, this Taylor's first starring feature film role as an Australian; and <i><a href="/wiki/Darker_than_Amber_(film)" title="Darker than Amber (film)">Darker than Amber</a></i> (1970) as <a href="/wiki/Travis_McGee" title="Travis McGee">Travis McGee</a>.</p>
<p>He was also reportedly up for the role of martial artist Roper in the <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Lee" title="Bruce Lee">Bruce Lee</a> vehicle <i><a href="/wiki/Enter_the_Dragon" title="Enter the Dragon">Enter the Dragon</a></i> (1973). The film was directed by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Clouse" title="Robert Clouse">Robert Clouse</a>, who had also directed Taylor in the film <i>Darker than Amber</i> (1970). Taylor was supposedly deemed too tall for the part, and the role instead went to <a href="/wiki/John_Saxon" title="John Saxon">John Saxon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rod_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1973, Taylor was cast in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Train_Robbers" title="The Train Robbers">The Train Robbers</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/John_Wayne" title="John Wayne">John Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ann-Margret" title="Ann-Margret">Ann-Margret</a>. The film was a box office success. Taylor also played in television roles and actually starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Bearcats!" title="Bearcats!">Bearcats!</a></i> (1971) on CBS and in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oregon_Trail_(TV_series)" title="The Oregon Trail (TV series)">The Oregon Trail</a></i> (1976) on NBC. He had a regular role in the short-lived spy drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Masquerade_(TV_series)" title="Masquerade (TV series)">Masquerade</a></i> (1983) and played one of the leads in the equally short-lived series, <i><a href="/wiki/Outlaws_(1986_TV_series)" title="Outlaws (1986 TV series)">Outlaws</a></i> (1986). From 1988 to 1990, Taylor appeared in the CBS drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Falcon_Crest" title="Falcon Crest">Falcon Crest</a></i> as Frank Agretti, playing opposite <a href="/wiki/Jane_Wyman" title="Jane Wyman">Jane Wyman</a>. In the mid 1990s, he appeared in several episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Murder,_She_Wrote" title="Murder, She Wrote">Murder, She Wrote</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Walker,_Texas_Ranger" title="Walker, Texas Ranger">Walker, Texas Ranger</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1993, he hosted the documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Machine:_The_Journey_Back" title="Time Machine: The Journey Back">Time Machine: The Journey Back</a></i>. At the end of the special, came a minisequel written by <a href="/wiki/David_Duncan_(writer)" title="David Duncan (writer)">David Duncan</a>, the screenwriter of the <a href="/wiki/George_Pal" title="George Pal">George Pal</a> film. Taylor recreated his role as George, reuniting him with Filby (<a href="/wiki/Alan_Young" title="Alan Young">Alan Young</a>).</p>
<p>Taylor returned to Australia several times over the years to make films, playing a 1920s traveling showman in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Picture_Show_Man" title="The Picture Show Man">The Picture Show Man</a></i> (1977) and a paid killer in <i><a href="/wiki/On_the_Run_(1982_film)" title="On the Run (1982 film)">On the Run</a></i> (1983). In the black comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_Woop_Woop" title="Welcome to Woop Woop">Welcome to Woop Woop</a></i> (1997), he played the foul-mouthed redneck Daddy-O.</p>
<p>By the late 1990s, he had moved into semiretirement. He appeared in the horror telemovie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KAW" title="KAW">KAW</a></i> in 2007 which revisits the idea of marauding birds turning on their human tormentors. In this version, however, the cause of the disturbance was discovered by Taylor who plays the town doctor. He appeared in <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Inglourious_Basterds" title="Inglourious Basterds">Inglourious Basterds</a></i> in 2009, portraying <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> in a cameo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miamih_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-miamih-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rod_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taylor married his third wife, Carol Kikumura, on 15 October 1980. He is the father of <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a> financial reporter <a href="/wiki/Felicia_Taylor" title="Felicia Taylor">Felicia Taylor</a> (born 1964) from his second marriage to model Mary Hilem (1 June 1963 – 18 September 1969). His first wife was model Peggy Williams (1951–1954).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rod_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taylor suffered a fatal <a href="/wiki/Myocardial_infarction" title="Myocardial infarction">heart attack</a> on 7 January 2015 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills" title="Beverly Hills">Beverly Hills</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> at the age of 84, four days short of his 85th birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rod_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_a_regular">As a regular</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rod_Taylor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: As a regular">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Taylor had several lead roles in television, from the early 1960s to the early first decade of the 21st century. Among his television shows as a regular are:</p>
<ul>
