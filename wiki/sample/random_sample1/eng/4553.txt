<p><b>Rodney G. Rowland</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (born February 20, 1964) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>. He is credited as <b>Rod Rowland</b> in more recent productions, given his predilection to being called Rod. Rowland's most noted appearances to date were as 1st Lieutenant Cooper Hawkes in 1995's <i><a href="/wiki/Space:_Above_and_Beyond" title="Space: Above and Beyond">Space: Above and Beyond</a></i> and P. Wiley in <i><a href="/wiki/The_6th_Day" title="The 6th Day">The 6th Day</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Schwarzenegger" title="Arnold Schwarzenegger">Arnold Schwarzenegger</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rodney_Rowland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Newport_Beach,_California" title="Newport Beach, California">Newport Beach, California</a>, Rowland is the youngest of four children and the son of a <a href="/wiki/Minister_(Christianity)" title="Minister (Christianity)">minister</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> After a brief time as a student on a watersport scholarship at <a href="/wiki/Pepperdine_University" title="Pepperdine University">Pepperdine University</a>, Rowland went to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and worked as a <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">model</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haute_Couture" title="Haute Couture">Haute Couture</a> labels like <a href="/wiki/Gucci" title="Gucci">Gucci</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gianni_Versace_S.p.A." title="Gianni Versace S.p.A.">Versace</a>, and <a href="/wiki/J.Crew" title="J.Crew">J.Crew</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On the suggestion of <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Weber_(photographer)" title="Bruce Weber (photographer)">Bruce Weber</a>, he began taking acting classes and took on roles in <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theater" title="Theater">theater</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rodney_Rowland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rowland's first big break was in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Wong_(producer)" title="James Wong (producer)">James Wong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glen_Morgan" title="Glen Morgan">Glen Morgan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">war</a> <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a>, <i>Space: Above and Beyond</i> in <a href="/wiki/1995_in_television" title="1995 in television">1995</a>. Despite auditions that were disastrous, Morgan and Wong brought Rowland on board to play the <a href="/wiki/Genetic_engineering" title="Genetic engineering">genetically engineered</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/InVitro" title="InVitro">InVitro</a> Cooper Hawkes. During the shoot for the episode "Who Monitors the Birds?", he became ill due to over work, succumbing to two bouts of <a href="/wiki/Fatigue_(medical)" title="Fatigue (medical)">exhaustion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bronchitis" title="Bronchitis">bronchitis</a>.</p>
<p>Rowland would later work with Wong and Morgan again on <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">The X-Files</a></i> in the season four episode "Never Again", appearing alongside <a href="/wiki/Gillian_Anderson" title="Gillian Anderson">Gillian Anderson</a>. His character, Ed Jerse, emotionally connects with Anderson's <a href="/wiki/Dana_Scully" title="Dana Scully">Dana Scully</a> and encourages her to have a small <a href="/wiki/Ouroboros" title="Ouroboros">Ouroboros</a> snake <a href="/wiki/Tattoo" title="Tattoo">tattooed</a> on the small of her back. Rowland would later date Anderson until 1998.</p>
<p>Rowland is well known for playing villainous roles due to his rugged looks. He appeared in a recurring role on <i><a href="/wiki/Veronica_Mars" title="Veronica Mars">Veronica Mars</a></i> portraying Liam Fitzpatrick, a lead character of the Irish gang and family of crime, the Fighting Fitzpatricks.</p>
