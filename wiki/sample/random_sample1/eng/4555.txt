<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Flag of Rhode Island</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Flag" title="Flag">flag</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">state of Rhode Island and Providence Plantations</a></b> is white and consists of a gold <a href="/wiki/Anchored_Cross" title="Anchored Cross">anchor</a> in the center (a symbol for hope) surrounded by thirteen gold stars (for the original <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/13_colonies" title="13 colonies">13 colonies</a> and Rhode Island's status as the 13th state to ratify the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Constitution" title="United States Constitution">Constitution</a>). A blue ribbon below the anchor bears the state's motto in gold: "<a href="/wiki/Hope" title="Hope">HOPE</a>." The flag is frequently depicted with golden fringe around the edges of the flag.</p>

<p>The present flag of the state of Rhode Island was formally adopted in 1897. As early as the 1640s, the anchor and "hope" were found on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_Seal" title="Rhode Island Seal">Rhode Island Seal</a>, and the seal's words and emblems were likely inspired by the biblical phrase "hope we have as an anchor of the soul," found in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hebrews_(Bible)" title="Hebrews (Bible)">Hebrews</a>, Verse 6:18-19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Rhode Island's earliest colonists were fleeing persecution in Massachusetts due to their religious beliefs.</p>
<p>In 2001, a survey conducted by the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Vexillological_Association" title="North American Vexillological Association">North American Vexillological Association</a> (NAVA) placed Rhode Island's flag 28th in design quality out of the 72 Canadian provincial, U.S. state and U.S. territorial flags ranked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Statute">Statute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Statute">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The state of Rhode Island and Providence Plantantions enacted legislation making this the official flag:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>§ 42-4-3 State flag. – The flag of the state shall be white, five feet and six inches (5′6″) fly and four feet and ten inches (4′10″) deep on the pike, bearing on each side in the center a gold anchor, twenty-two inches (22″) high, and underneath it a blue ribbon twenty-four inches (24″) long and five inches (5″) wide, or in these proportions, with the motto "Hope" in golden letters thereon, the whole surrounded by thirteen (13) golden stars in a circle. The flag shall be edged with yellow fringe. The pike shall be surmounted by a spearhead and the length of the pike shall be nine feet (9′), not including the spearhead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>

<p>Another commonly flown flag is the Flag of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_Regiment" title="Rhode Island Regiment">Rhode Island Regiment</a>. Many houses throughout the state display the flag alongside the current flag, or in lieu of the State Flag. The flag can also be seen flying at home football games of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Rhode_Island" title="University of Rhode Island">University of Rhode Island</a> Rams.</p>

<p>The flag of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor_of_Rhode_Island" title="Governor of Rhode Island">Governor of Rhode Island</a> is based on an earlier state flag. Like most U.S. governors' flags, there are four five-point stars at the corners of the field.</p>
