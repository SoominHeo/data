<p><b>Laura Elizabeth Carmichael</b> (born 16 July 1986)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> is an English actress, most widely known for her role as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Downton_Abbey_characters#Lady_Edith_Crawley" title="List of Downton Abbey characters">Lady Edith Crawley</a> in the TV period drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Downton_Abbey" title="Downton Abbey">Downton Abbey</a></i>. In 2016, Carmichael starred in a play production, <i>The Maids</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Uzo_Aduba" title="Uzo Aduba">Uzo Aduba</a> and Zawe Ashton.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laura_Carmichael&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Laura Elizabeth Carmichael was born in <a href="/wiki/Southampton" title="Southampton">Southampton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a>, England,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> the daughter of Sarah, a radiographer, and Andy Carmichael, a software consultant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dirtmail_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-dirtmail-2">[2]</a></sup> She has two sisters: Amy, who works for a software start-up company, and Olivia, a charity fundraiser with the NHS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JWoods_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-JWoods-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>One of her paternal great-grandfathers was aviator <a href="/wiki/Norman_Blackburn" title="Norman Blackburn">Norman Blackburn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dirtmail_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-dirtmail-2">[2]</a></sup> Carmichael was educated at Shirley Junior School, <a href="/wiki/The_Mountbatten_School" title="The Mountbatten School">The Mountbatten School</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Symonds_College" title="Peter Symonds College">Peter Symonds College</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lloyd_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lloyd-5">[5]</a></sup> She trained at the <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Old_Vic_Theatre_School" title="Bristol Old Vic Theatre School">Bristol Old Vic Theatre School</a>, graduating in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gilbert_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gilbert-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laura_Carmichael&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laura_Carmichael&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between 2010 and 2015 Carmichael played Lady Edith Crawley in <i><a href="/wiki/Downton_Abbey" title="Downton Abbey">Downton Abbey</a></i>, a part which brought her worldwide recognition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other television roles include parts in <i>The Heart of Thomas Hardy</i>, and <i>End of Our Street</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lloyd_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lloyd-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011 she appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Tinker_Tailor_Soldier_Spy_(film)" title="Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy (film)">Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy</a></i>, and she played Henriette in the 2014 film of <i><a href="/wiki/Madame_Bovary_(2014_film)" title="Madame Bovary (2014 film)">Madame Bovary</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lloyd_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Lloyd-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Carmichael plays Seph in the comedy film <i>Burn, Burn, Burn</i>, which was released in October 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Razaq_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Razaq-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>She plays Muriel, Ruth Williams' sister in <i>A United Kingdom</i>, and Maddy Stevenson in the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Marcella_(TV_series)" title="Marcella (TV series)">Marcella</a></i>, both released in 2016.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theatre">Theatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laura_Carmichael&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Theatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Carmichael made her West End debut in <i><a href="/wiki/Uncle_Vanya" title="Uncle Vanya">Uncle Vanya</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Anna_Friel" title="Anna Friel">Anna Friel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samuel_West" title="Samuel West">Samuel West</a> in October 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>She played Arabella in <i>The Fitzrovia Radio Hour</i> at The Underglobe Theatre in early 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-United_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-United-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2016, Carmichael will play Madam in Jamie Lloyd's production of <i>The Maids</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Trafalgar_Studios" title="Trafalgar Studios">Trafalgar Studios</a> in London's West End.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mayo_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mayo-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Carmichael has also appeared in <i>Plenty</i> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheffield_Crucible_Theatre" title="Sheffield Crucible Theatre">Sheffield Crucible Theatre</a>, and played Laura in <i>Reasons for Living</i> as part of the Scratch Festival at <a href="/wiki/Battersea_Arts_Centre" title="Battersea Arts Centre">Battersea Arts Centre</a>. In late 2009 she played Miranda in a touring production of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shakespeare" title="Shakespeare">Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Tempest_(play)" title="The Tempest (play)">The Tempest</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-United_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-United-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laura_Carmichael&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Laura Carmichael lives in Camden, north London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JWoods_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-JWoods-3">[3]</a></sup> She is close friends with <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Dockery" title="Michelle Dockery">Michelle Dockery</a>, who played her sister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mary_Crawley" title="Mary Crawley">Mary</a> in <i>Downton Abbey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JWoods_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-JWoods-3">[3]</a></sup></i></p>
<p>Prior to landing the role of Lady Edith in early 2010, Carmichael had worked as a secretary in a doctor's surgery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Woods_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Woods-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
