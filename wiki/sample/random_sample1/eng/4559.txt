<p><b>Laurasiatheria</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Superorder" title="Superorder">superorder</a> of <a href="/wiki/Placentalia" title="Placentalia">placental mammals</a> that originated on the northern supercontinent of <a href="/wiki/Laurasia" title="Laurasia">Laurasia</a> 99 million years ago. The superorder includes <a href="/wiki/Shrew" title="Shrew">shrews</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pangolin" title="Pangolin">pangolins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bats</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whales" title="Whales">whales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carnivora" title="Carnivora">carnivorans</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perissodactyl" title="Perissodactyl">odd-toed</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artiodactyl" title="Artiodactyl">even-toed ungulates</a>, among others.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification_and_phylogeny">Classification and phylogeny</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laurasiatheria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Classification and phylogeny">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Laurasiatheria was discovered on the basis of the <a href="/wiki/Molecular_phylogenetics" title="Molecular phylogenetics">similar gene sequences</a> shared by the mammals belonging to it; no <a href="/wiki/Anatomy" title="Anatomy">anatomical</a> features have yet been found that unite the group. Laurasiatheria is a <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">clade</a> usually discussed without a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biological_classification" title="Biological classification">Linnaean rank</a>, but has been assigned the rank of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cohort_(biology)" title="Cohort (biology)">cohort</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnorder" title="Magnorder">magnorder</a>, and superorder. The Laurasiatheria clade is based on DNA sequence analyses and <a href="/wiki/Retrotransposon" title="Retrotransposon">retrotransposon</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retrotransposon_Marker" title="Retrotransposon Marker">presence/absence data</a>. The name comes from the theory that these mammals evolved on the <a href="/wiki/Supercontinent" title="Supercontinent">supercontinent</a> of <a href="/wiki/Laurasia" title="Laurasia">Laurasia</a>, after it split from <a href="/wiki/Gondwana" title="Gondwana">Gondwana</a> when <a href="/wiki/Pangaea" title="Pangaea">Pangaea</a> broke up. It is a sister group to <a href="/wiki/Euarchontoglires" title="Euarchontoglires">Euarchontoglires</a> (or Supraprimates) with which it forms the clade <a href="/wiki/Boreoeutheria" title="Boreoeutheria">Boreoeutheria</a>. Laurasiatheria includes the following <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extant_taxon" title="Extant taxon">extant</a> taxa:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eulipotyphla" title="Eulipotyphla">Eulipotyphla</a>, having subsumed:
<ul>
<li>Erinaceomorpha: hedgehogs and gymnures (see <a href="/wiki/Erinaceidae" title="Erinaceidae">Erinaceidae</a>)</li>
<li>the remaining families of <a href="/wiki/Soricomorpha" title="Soricomorpha">Soricomorpha</a>: <a href="/wiki/Mole_(animal)" title="Mole (animal)">moles</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shrews" title="Shrews">shrews</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solenodon" title="Solenodon">solenodons</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_distribution" title="Cosmopolitan distribution">cosmopolitan distribution</a>)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiroptera" title="Chiroptera">Chiroptera</a>: bats (cosmopolitan)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perissodactyla" title="Perissodactyla">Perissodactyla</a>: odd-toed ungulates including horses, tapirs and rhinoceroses</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artiodactyla" title="Artiodactyla">Artiodactyla</a>: even-toed ungulates including camels, pigs, ruminants (giraffes, deer, antelopes, cattle, sheep, goats, etc.), hippopotamuses and cetaceans<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Groves2011_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Groves2011-3">[3]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cetacea" title="Cetacea">Cetacea</a>: whales, dolphins, and porpoises</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ferae" title="Ferae">Ferae</a> (unranked) containing the orders:
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pholidota" title="Pholidota">Pholidota</a>: pangolins ('scaly anteaters') (Africa, South Asia)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Carnivora" title="Carnivora">Carnivora</a>: cats, dogs, bears, seals, and others (cosmopolitan)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Uncertainty still exists regarding the <a href="/wiki/Phylogenetic_tree" title="Phylogenetic tree">phylogenetic tree</a> for extant laurasiatherians, primarily due to disagreement about the placement of Chiroptera and Perissodactyla. Based on morphological grounds, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiroptera" title="Chiroptera">Chiroptera</a> had long been classified in the superorder <a href="/wiki/Archonta" title="Archonta">Archonta</a> (e.g. along with <a href="/wiki/Treeshrew" title="Treeshrew">treeshrews</a> and the gliding <a href="/wiki/Colugo" title="Colugo">colugos</a>) until genetic research instead showed their kinship with the other laurasiatherians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The studies conflicted in terms of the exact placement of Chiroptera, however, with it being linked most closely to groups such as Eulipotyphla,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Ferae<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> or with Perissodactyla and Ferae in the <a href="/wiki/Pegasoferae" title="Pegasoferae">Pegasoferae</a> proposal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> A recent study (Zhou et al., 2011<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zhou_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Zhou-8">[8]</a></sup>) found that "trees reconstructed [...] for the 1,608-gene data set fully support [...] a basal position for Eulipotyphla and a more apical position for Chiroptera" (see <a href="/wiki/Cladogram" title="Cladogram">cladogram</a> below) and concluded that "Pegasoferae [...] does [sic] not appear to be a natural group." The most recent study (Nery et al., 2012<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nery_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nery-9">[9]</a></sup>) supports the conclusions of Zhou et al. using a large genomic dataset, placing Eulipotyphla as a basal order and Chiroptera as sister to Cetartiodactyla, with maximal support for all nodes of their phylogenetic tree. The exact position of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perissodactyla" title="Perissodactyla">Perissodactyla</a> remains less certain, with some studies linking it with Ferae into a proposed clade <a href="/wiki/Zooamata" title="Zooamata">Zooamata</a> while others unite it with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cetartiodactyla" title="Cetartiodactyla">Cetartiodactyla</a> into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euungulata" title="Euungulata">Euungulata</a>, a clade of 'true <a href="/wiki/Ungulate" title="Ungulate">ungulates</a>'; Zhou et al. found better (but not full) support for the latter, while Nery et al. found Perissodactyla to be sister to Carnivora.</p>

<p><br/>
Laurasiatheria is also posited to include several extinct orders and superorders. At least some of these are considered <a href="/wiki/Wastebasket_taxon" title="Wastebasket taxon">wastebasket taxons</a>, historically lumping together several lineages based on superficial attributes and assumed relations to modern mammals. In some cases, these orders have turned out to either be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paraphyletic" title="Paraphyletic">paraphyletic</a> assemblages, or to be composed of mammals now understood not to be laurasiatheres at all.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Meridiungulata" title="Meridiungulata">Meridiungulata</a> (Collagen sequences found in <i><a href="/wiki/Macrauchenia" title="Macrauchenia">Macrauchenia</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Toxodon" title="Toxodon">Toxodon</a></i> indicate that are now understood to be stem-perissodactyls)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Condylarthra" title="Condylarthra">Condylarthra</a> (paraphyletic in relation to true ungulates, possibly polyphyletic since some forms may be <a href="/wiki/Afrotheria" title="Afrotheria">afrotheres</a> or even non-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Placental" title="Placental">placental</a> <a href="/wiki/Eutheria" title="Eutheria">eutherians</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dinocerata" title="Dinocerata">Dinocerata</a> (natural clade closely related to perissodactyls and meridiungulates)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mesonychia" title="Mesonychia">Mesonychia</a> (natural clade, though several members, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Andrewsarchus" title="Andrewsarchus">Andrewsarchus</a></i>, are now thought to belong in other groups)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Creodonta" title="Creodonta">Creodonta</a> (polyphyletic, composed almost entirely of several unrelated clades of laurasiatheres and afrotheres)</li>
</ul>
