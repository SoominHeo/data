<p>The <b>Women's +75 kg</b> weightlifting event at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, took place at <a href="/wiki/ExCeL_London" title="ExCeL London">ExCeL London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Total score was the sum of the lifter's best result in each of the <a href="/wiki/Snatch_(weightlifting)" title="Snatch (weightlifting)">snatch</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Clean_and_jerk" title="Clean and jerk">clean and jerk</a>, with three lifts allowed for each lift. In case of a tie, the lighter lifter won; if still tied, the lifter who took the fewest attempts to achieve the total score won. Lifters without a valid snatch score did not perform the clean and jerk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Russia's <a href="/wiki/Tatiana_Kashirina" title="Tatiana Kashirina">Tatiana Kashirina</a> set a world record in snatch with a lift of 151 kilogrammes. China's <a href="/wiki/Zhou_Lulu" title="Zhou Lulu">Zhou Lulu</a> set a world record at total weightage with 333 kilogrammes, as well as an Olympic record in clean and jerk with a lift of 187 kilogrammes. Ukraine's <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Svitlana_Cherniavska&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Svitlana Cherniavska (page does not exist)">Svitlana Cherniavska</a> was on the start list but did not compete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
