<p><b>Lauren Lapkus</b> (born September 6, 1985) is an American actress and comedian, best known for portraying Dee Dee in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Are_You_There,_Chelsea%3F" title="Are You There, Chelsea?">Are You There, Chelsea?</a></i> and Susan Fischer in the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> original series <i><a href="/wiki/Orange_Is_the_New_Black" title="Orange Is the New Black">Orange Is the New Black</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She has also made appearances on such television shows as <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Middle_(TV_series)" title="The Middle (TV series)">The Middle</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_in_Cleveland" title="Hot in Cleveland">Hot in Cleveland</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/@midnight" title="@midnight">@midnight</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SO_Mag_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SO_Mag-3">[3]</a></sup> She is also known for her many podcast appearances, including <i><a href="/wiki/Comedy_Bang!_Bang!" title="Comedy Bang! Bang!">Comedy Bang! Bang!</a></i>, <i>improv4humans</i>, and her own podcast <i><a href="/wiki/With_Special_Guest_Lauren_Lapkus" title="With Special Guest Lauren Lapkus">With Special Guest Lauren Lapkus</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She was a main cast member on the TBS original sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Clipped_(TV_series)" title="Clipped (TV series)">Clipped</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Lapkus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lapkus was born in Evanston, Illinois, of half <a href="/wiki/Lithuanians" title="Lithuanians">Lithuanian</a> and half <a href="/wiki/Greeks" title="Greeks">Greek</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> At an early age she would film sketches with her older brother and later at <a href="/wiki/Evanston_Township_High_School" title="Evanston Township High School">Evanston Township High School</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup> she participated in the annual student revue and variety show YAMO.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>During her senior year of high school, she began taking improv classes at the <a href="/wiki/IO_Theater" title="IO Theater">iO Theater</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup> She would attend <a href="/wiki/DePaul_University" title="DePaul University">DePaul University</a> graduating in 2008 with a degree in English, while she continued performing improv most nights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup> Lapkus credits watching a lot of television sitcoms and <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i> while young with giving her the education to become a comedian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon graduation, she moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> for a year where she got involved with <a href="/wiki/Upright_Citizens_Brigade_Theatre" title="Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre">Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup> After living in New York for 15 months,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup> she relocated to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SO_Mag_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-SO_Mag-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Lapkus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After almost a year in Los Angeles, Lapkus began landing roles in a number of commercials, including <a href="/wiki/Snickers" title="Snickers">Snickers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_in_the_Box" title="Jack in the Box">Jack in the Box</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup> as well as a sketch on <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup> From these successes, she got herself representation and during pilot season landed a supporting role in the short lived <i><a href="/wiki/Are_You_There,_Chelsea%3F" title="Are You There, Chelsea?">Are You There, Chelsea?</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>After self-taping her audition, Lapkus was cast as security guard Susan Fischer on the first and second seasons of the <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orange_is_the_New_Black" title="Orange is the New Black">Orange is the New Black</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2015, Lapkus and the cast won the <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award" title="Screen Actors Guild Award">Screen Actors Guild Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award_for_Outstanding_Performance_by_an_Ensemble_in_a_Comedy_Series" title="Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Comedy Series">Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Comedy Series</a>.</p>
<p>In 2014, Lapkus launched her own podcast on the <a href="/wiki/Earwolf" title="Earwolf">Earwolf</a> network, called <i><a href="/wiki/With_Special_Guest_Lauren_Lapkus" title="With Special Guest Lauren Lapkus">With Special Guest Lauren Lapkus</a></i>. The show's premise is that each week her guest is the host of their own podcast, and she is appearing as a character on their show. Her guests have included <a href="/wiki/Paul_F._Tompkins" title="Paul F. Tompkins">Paul F. Tompkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Betsy_Sodaro" title="Betsy Sodaro">Betsy Sodaro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Daly" title="Andy Daly">Andy Daly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Kroll" title="Nick Kroll">Nick Kroll</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Scott_Aukerman" title="Scott Aukerman">Scott Aukerman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She has said that she "never expected to get into podcasts at all" and to her it is funny she has one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-9"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In mid-2014, Lapkus was cast in <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_World" title="Jurassic World">Jurassic World</a></i>, which she filmed in both <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-10"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup> Many of her scenes with <a href="/wiki/Jake_Johnson" title="Jake Johnson">Jake Johnson</a> were improvised.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BA_7-11"><a href="#cite_note-BA-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2015, she portrayed Joy the receptionist in <a href="/wiki/TBS_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="TBS (U.S. TV channel)">TBS</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Clipped_(TV_series)" title="Clipped (TV series)">Clipped.</a></i> In 2016, Lapkus wrote and starred in her own 30-minute episode of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sketch_show" title="Sketch show">sketch show</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Netflix_Presents:_The_Characters" title="Netflix Presents: The Characters">Netflix Presents: The Characters</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lapkus performs improv regularly. She is a cast member of <a href="/wiki/Upright_Citizens_Brigade_Theatre" title="Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre">UCB</a>'s flagship show Asssscat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Lapkus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lapkus married actor and improviser Chris Alvarado on May 3, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On February 14, 2016, Lapkus announced on social media that she and her husband had mutually agreed to end their marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
