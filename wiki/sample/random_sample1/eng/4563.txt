<p><b>Lauren Montgomery</b> (born May 1980) is an American <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> <a href="/wiki/Animation_director" title="Animation director">animation director</a> and <a href="/wiki/Storyboard_artist" title="Storyboard artist">storyboard artist</a>. She is noted for her storyboard work for animated television and DVD movie features. She directed the <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Woman" title="Wonder Woman">Wonder Woman</a> animated <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Woman_(2009_film)" title="Wonder Woman (2009 film)">film of the same name</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldsfinest_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-worldsfinest-1">[1]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Green_Lantern:_First_Flight" title="Green Lantern: First Flight">Green Lantern: First Flight</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Superman/Batman:_Apocalypse" title="Superman/Batman: Apocalypse">Superman/Batman: Apocalypse</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League:_Doom" title="Justice League: Doom">Justice League: Doom</a></i>, and co-directed with <a href="/wiki/Sam_Liu" title="Sam Liu">Sam Liu</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League:_Crisis_on_Two_Earths" title="Justice League: Crisis on Two Earths">Justice League: Crisis on Two Earths</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Year_One_(film)" title="Batman: Year One (film)">Batman:Year One</a></i>. She also worked on <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Justice_(TV_series)" title="Young Justice (TV series)">Young Justice</a></i> and provided storyboards for Book 3 of <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender" title="Avatar: The Last Airbender">Avatar: The Last Airbender</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a><a href="/wiki/Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender" title="Avatar: The Last Airbender">.</a> In 2012, Montgomery returned to Nickelodeon and the Avatar franchise as the supervising producer and storyboard artist for the sequel series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Korra" title="The Legend of Korra">The Legend of Korra</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She is now working as an executive producer and co-showrunner on the animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Voltron:_Legendary_Defender" title="Voltron: Legendary Defender">Voltron: Legendary Defender</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Joaquim_Dos_Santos" title="Joaquim Dos Santos">Joaquim Dos Santos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Montgomery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Montgomery became interested in drawing as a youngster and developed an interest in animation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-superherotimes_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-superherotimes-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1998 she enrolled in the animation program at <a href="/wiki/Loyola_Marymount_University" title="Loyola Marymount University">Loyola Marymount University</a>'s School of Film and Television where she graduated in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-linkedin_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-linkedin-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Montgomery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animation">Animation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Montgomery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Animation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Storyboards">Storyboards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Montgomery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Storyboards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>She got her start on the <i><a href="/wiki/He-Man_and_the_Masters_of_the_Universe_(2002_TV_series)" title="He-Man and the Masters of the Universe (2002 TV series)">He-Man and the Masters of the Universe</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-linkedin_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-linkedin-6">[6]</a></sup> animated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Young_Productions" title="Mike Young Productions">Mike Young Productions</a>. Montgomery has done storyboards for <a href="/wiki/Animated_cartoon" title="Animated cartoon">animated television series</a>' such as <i><a href="/wiki/Ben_10" title="Ben 10">Ben 10</a></i> from <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network_Studios" title="Cartoon Network Studios">Cartoon Network Studios</a> as well as storyboard work for the critically acclaimed television series <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League_Unlimited" title="Justice League Unlimited">Justice League Unlimited</a></i>, for which producer <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Timm" title="Bruce Timm">Bruce Timm</a> praised her work as being quite versatile.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lauren has also done storyboard work for the second <a href="/wiki/DC_Universe_Animated_Original_Movies" title="DC Universe Animated Original Movies">DC Universe film production</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League:_The_New_Frontier" title="Justice League: The New Frontier">Justice League: The New Frontier</a></i>, based on the <a href="/wiki/Eisner_Award" title="Eisner Award">Eisner Award</a>-winning <a href="/wiki/Graphic_novel" title="Graphic novel">graphic novel</a> by <a href="/wiki/Darwyn_Cooke" title="Darwyn Cooke">Darwyn Cooke</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/DC:_The_New_Frontier" title="DC: The New Frontier">DC: The New Frontier</a></i>. She also worked on the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Next_Avengers:_Heroes_of_Tomorrow" title="Next Avengers: Heroes of Tomorrow">Next Avengers: Heroes of Tomorrow</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hulk_Vs" title="Hulk Vs">Hulk Vs</a></i> animated features, <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a> properties by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lions_Gate_Entertainment" title="Lions Gate Entertainment">Lions Gate Entertainment</a>. Moreover, she worked on the <a href="/wiki/G.I._Joe" title="G.I. Joe">G.I. Joe</a> features for Reel FX Creative Studios and distributed by <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a>. She has also worked on the Emmy-winning series <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender" title="Avatar: The Last Airbender">Avatar: The Last Airbender</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>/<a href="/wiki/Nicktoons_(TV_channel)" title="Nicktoons (TV channel)">Nicktoons</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Directing">Directing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Montgomery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Directing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>She made her directorial debut by directing several episodes of the <i><a href="/wiki/Legion_of_Super_Heroes_(TV_series)" title="Legion of Super Heroes (TV series)">Legion of Super Heroes</a></i> series, and was one of the three directors for the <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">direct-to-video</a> feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Superman:_Doomsday" title="Superman: Doomsday">Superman: Doomsday</a></i>, directing the second act of the movie. In March, 2008, it was announced that she is the director for the direct-to-video <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Woman_(2009_film)" title="Wonder Woman (2009 film)"><i>Wonder Woman</i> film</a> from <a href="/wiki/Warner_Premiere" title="Warner Premiere">Warner Premiere</a>. The animated feature was released on March 3, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldsfinest_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-worldsfinest-1">[1]</a></sup> Furthermore, she is the director of <i><a href="/wiki/Green_Lantern:_First_Flight" title="Green Lantern: First Flight">Green Lantern: First Flight</a></i>, which was released on July 28, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Subsequently, Montgomery co-directed <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League:_Crisis_on_Two_Earths" title="Justice League: Crisis on Two Earths">Justice League: Crisis on Two Earths</a></i> (with <a href="/wiki/Sam_Liu" title="Sam Liu">Sam Liu</a>) and directed <i><a href="/wiki/Superman/Batman:_Apocalypse" title="Superman/Batman: Apocalypse">Superman/Batman: Apocalypse</a></i>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Producing">Producing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Montgomery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Producing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Montgomery served as a supervising producer on Nickelodeon's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Korra" title="The Legend of Korra">The Legend of Korra</a></i>, created by <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Konietzko" title="Bryan Konietzko">Bryan Konietzko</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dante_DiMartino" title="Michael Dante DiMartino">Michael Dante DiMartino</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Previously, she provided storyboard work on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avatar_book_3" title="Avatar book 3">Book 3</a> for the original series <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender" title="Avatar: The Last Airbender">Avatar: The Last Airbender</a></i>, also a Konietzko/DiMartino collaboration.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Illustration">Illustration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Montgomery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Illustration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Montgomery has released a sketchbook with comic book/animation artist Eric Canete entitled <i>Beauty and the Beast</i> published by Black Velvet Studios. She has also done various freelance illustration work such as illustrating a cover for <i>Nickleodeon Magazine</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and a press release illustration for the <i>Avatar</i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Style">Style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lauren_Montgomery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to Montgomery: "...I was definitely influenced by the Disney films. Those were the drawings I was tracing and sketching during my early years. I would study each and every one of the princesses and draw them until I had them all down by heart. My facial features are still influenced somewhat by Disney characters. As I got older, I was definitely influenced by Bruce's style in Batman (referring to Bruce Timm and <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_The_Animated_Series" title="Batman: The Animated Series">Batman: The Animated Series</a></i>), and I started getting into anime, and some of the more subtle styles in anime drawing. The clothing is a little more detailed than the typical American animation – it's more believable, yet still simplified. It's the way they draw bodies and cloth that I kind of incorporate into my drawings, as well as certain aspects of how they would draw hair. So I'd say I have a few different influences in my art style."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-superherotimes_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-superherotimes-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
