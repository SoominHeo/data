<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawrence_Makoare&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Makoare was a road construction builder who drifted into acting after he accompanied a girlfriend to a drama class and was picked out by the teacher to perform because of his impressive height. He began his career performing as a stuntman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Makoare is probably best known for his roles in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(film_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (film series)">The Lord of the Rings</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit_(film_series)" title="The Hobbit (film series)">The Hobbit</a></i> film trilogies. In <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring">The Fellowship of the Ring</a></i> he portrayed the <a href="/wiki/Uruk-hai" title="Uruk-hai">Uruk-hai</a> leader <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lurtz" title="Lurtz">Lurtz</a>, and in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Return_of_the_King" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King">The Return of the King</a></i> he portrayed the <a href="/wiki/Witch-king_of_Angmar" title="Witch-king of Angmar">Witch-king of Angmar</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Gothmog_(Third_Age)" title="Gothmog (Third Age)">Gothmog</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Orc_(Middle-earth)" title="Orc (Middle-earth)">Orc</a> commander at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Pelennor_Fields" title="Battle of the Pelennor Fields">Battle of the Pelennor Fields</a>. In <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_The_Desolation_of_Smaug" title="The Hobbit: The Desolation of Smaug">The Hobbit: The Desolation of Smaug</a></i> he portrayed the Orc commander <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Middle-earth_Orcs#Bolg" title="List of Middle-earth Orcs">Bolg</a>, son of Azog. Due to filming commitments on <i><a href="/wiki/Marco_Polo_(TV_series)" title="Marco Polo (TV series)">Marco Polo</a></i> Makoare was unavailable during the pick-ups shooting of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_The_Battle_of_the_Five_Armies" title="The Hobbit: The Battle of the Five Armies">The Hobbit: The Battle of the Five Armies</a></i>, in which Bolg is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/John_Tui" title="John Tui">John Tui</a> instead.</p>
<p>Makoare is also popular among <i><a href="/wiki/Xena:_Warrior_Princess" title="Xena: Warrior Princess">Xena: Warrior Princess</a></i> fans, having played two memorable characters in the third season: a Barbarian leader in episode "The Quill Is Mightier..."; and Maecanus (a minion of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aphrodite_(Xena_and_Hercules)" title="Aphrodite (Xena and Hercules)">Aphrodite</a>) in episode "Fins, Femmes and Gems". In 2002 he portrayed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mr._Kil" title="Mr. Kil">Mr. Kil</a> in the <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Die_Another_Day" title="Die Another Day">Die Another Day</a></i>. The director of <a href="/wiki/The_Dead_Lands" title="The Dead Lands">The Dead Lands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toa_Fraser" title="Toa Fraser">Toa Fraser</a>, was not enthusiastic about using Makoare as the warrior but was won over by the way he managed, after a few directing hints, to turn in a performance that made him weep.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
