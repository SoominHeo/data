<p><b>Loredana Dinu</b> (born <b>Iordăchioiu</b>; born 2 April 1984) is a Romanian épée fencer, twice World champion and twice European champion with Romania.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loredana_Dinu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dinu began fencing at age 11 after coaches from different sports did a presentation at her school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PS_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-PS-1">[1]</a></sup> Her first coaches were Dumitru Popescu and Radu Mitrăchioiu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PS_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-PS-1">[1]</a></sup> She took part in her first national championship at age 15; she was defeated in the final by <a href="/wiki/Ana_Maria_Br%C3%A2nz%C4%83" title="Ana Maria Brânză">Ana Maria Brânză</a>, with whom she would later fence for Romania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PS_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-PS-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dinu's first major results were a silver medal in the 2001 Junior World Championships and a bronze in the 2004 Junior World Championships. She won a gold medal with Romania in the <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2010 World Fencing Championships">2010</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2011 World Fencing Championships">2011 World Fencing Championships</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Simona_Alexandru" title="Simona Alexandru">Simona Alexandru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ana_Maria_Br%C3%A2nz%C4%83" title="Ana Maria Brânză">Ana Maria Brânză</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anca_M%C4%83roiu" title="Anca Măroiu">Anca Măroiu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She competed in the <a href="/wiki/Fencing_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_team_%C3%A9p%C3%A9e" title="Fencing at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's team épée">women's team épée</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, but Romania were defeated in quarter-finals against South Korea and missed the podium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She decided to take a break after the Olympics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2015 Dinu announced she would come back to competition, this time as a member of <a href="/wiki/CS_Dinamo_Bucure%C8%99ti" title="CS Dinamo București">CS Dinamo București</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In May she won a bronze medal in the Romanian national championship and reached the quarter-finals at the Rio de Janeiro Grand Prix.</p>
