<p><b>Lorraine Bracco</b> (born October 2, 1954)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American actress. She is best known for her performances as Dr. <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Melfi" title="Jennifer Melfi">Jennifer Melfi</a> on the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sopranos" title="The Sopranos">The Sopranos</a></i>, and as <a href="/wiki/Karen_Friedman_Hill" title="Karen Friedman Hill">Karen Friedman Hill</a> in the 1990 <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">Martin Scorsese</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Goodfellas" title="Goodfellas">Goodfellas</a></i>, for which she was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress</a>. She also appeared as Angela Rizzoli on the <a href="/wiki/TNT_(TV_channel)" title="TNT (TV channel)">TNT</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Rizzoli_%26_Isles" title="Rizzoli &amp; Isles">Rizzoli &amp; Isles</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorraine_Bracco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:180px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lorraine_Bracco_HS_Yearbook.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="247" data-file-width="178" height="247" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1b/Lorraine_Bracco_HS_Yearbook.jpeg" width="178"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>Bracco was born in the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Bay_Ridge,_Brooklyn" title="Bay Ridge, Brooklyn">Bay Ridge</a>. She is the daughter of Eileen (née Molyneux 1926-2010) and Salvatore Bracco, Sr. She has a sister, <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Bracco" title="Elizabeth Bracco">Elizabeth Bracco</a>, and brother, Salvatore Jr. Her mother was from <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and met her father during World War II. Eileen came with Salvatore to the United States as a <a href="/wiki/War_bride" title="War bride">War bride</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Bracco grew up in <a href="/wiki/Westbury,_New_York" title="Westbury, New York">Westbury</a>, on Maxwell Drive on <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>. She graduated from Hicksville High School in 1972. Her father was of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_American" title="Italian American">Italian</a> descent, while her mother was born in England to French parents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Bracco is fluent in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorraine_Bracco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1974, Bracco moved to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, where she became a fashion model for <a href="/wiki/Jean-Paul_Gaultier" title="Jean-Paul Gaultier">Jean-Paul Gaultier</a>. While there, she was introduced to the director and novelist <a href="/wiki/Lina_Wertm%C3%BCller" title="Lina Wertmüller">Lina Wertmüller</a>. In the early 1970s, Wertmüller recruited Bracco in a new film production titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camorra_(A_Story_of_Streets,_Women_and_Crime)" title="Camorra (A Story of Streets, Women and Crime)">Camorra</a></i>. "She dressed me up like an Italian woman of no means. A street woman clad in disheveled clothes, hair unkempt and all that, and threw me on the set. She was so creative. I mean, Lina accentuated my eyes with dark make-up, the way <a href="/wiki/Sophia_Loren" title="Sophia Loren">Sophia Loren</a> used to appear in those epic roles in the '60s. And talk about talent. She's so bright and perceptive. I mean, she's just fantastic. And yes, I learned a lot from her. She's a master of her profession, and I've been blessed not only with her, but also with so many masters," recounted Bracco to Daniel Simone during a 2007 interview.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bracco acted in several French-language films. She also once worked as a <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_Luxembourg_(French)" title="Radio Luxembourg (French)">Radio Luxembourg</a>. She appeared as <a href="/wiki/Paul_Guilfoyle" title="Paul Guilfoyle">Paul Guilfoyle</a>'s hostage in the first season <i><a href="/wiki/Crime_Story_(TV_series)" title="Crime Story (TV series)">Crime Story</a></i> episode, "Hide and Go Thief". Her sister <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Bracco" title="Elizabeth Bracco">Elizabeth</a> played a hostage in the series pilot. Her other films include <i><a href="/wiki/Someone_to_Watch_Over_Me_(film)" title="Someone to Watch Over Me (film)">Someone to Watch Over Me</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Riding_in_Cars_with_Boys" title="Riding in Cars with Boys">Riding in Cars with Boys</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Basketball_Diaries_(film)" title="The Basketball Diaries (film)">The Basketball Diaries</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Medicine_Man_(film)" title="Medicine Man (film)">Medicine Man</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Radio_Flyer_(film)" title="Radio Flyer (film)">Radio Flyer</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Hackers_(film)" title="Hackers (film)">Hackers</a></i>.</p>
<p>Bracco received her big career break when offered the role as the mobster-wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karen_Friedman" title="Karen Friedman">Karen Hill</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Goodfellas" title="Goodfellas">Goodfellas</a></i>, which earned her an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Best Supporting Actress</a> and was nominated for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_-_Motion_Picture" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress - Motion Picture">Best Supporting Actress – Motion Picture</a>. She won the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Film_Critics_Association_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Los Angeles Film Critics Association Award for Best Supporting Actress">Los Angeles Film Critics Association Award for Best Supporting Actress</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Film_Critics_Association_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Chicago Film Critics Association Award for Best Supporting Actress">Chicago Film Critics Association Award for Best Supporting Actress</a>.</p>
<p>Bracco is the owner of Bracco Wines, with association of Straight-Up Brands LLC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Her line of wines was featured on the Season 1 finale of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bravo_(US_TV_channel)" title="Bravo (US TV channel)">Bravo's</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Chef" title="Top Chef">Top Chef</a></i> in 2006. She appeared as a guest judge for the two-episode finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chef2011_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chef2011-8">[8]</a></sup> She also appeared as a special judge on <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Chef_(season_8)" title="Top Chef (season 8)">Top Chef: All Stars</a></i> in the episode titled "An Offer They Can't Refuse", which featured Italian cuisine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Bracco appeared on a season 6 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Throwdown!_with_Bobby_Flay" title="Throwdown! with Bobby Flay">Throwdown! with Bobby Flay</a></i> as a guest judge for the ravioli throwdown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the audition process for <i>The Sopranos</i>, David Chase wanted her to read for the lead female role of <a href="/wiki/Carmela_Soprano" title="Carmela Soprano">Carmela Soprano</a>. However, Bracco had read the script and was drawn to the part of <a href="/wiki/Psychiatrist" title="Psychiatrist">psychiatrist</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._Jennifer_Melfi" title="Dr. Jennifer Melfi">Dr. Jennifer Melfi</a>. Bracco felt strongly enough about her ability and desire to play this part that she arranged a meeting with David Chase and talked him into letting her have a chance as Dr. Melfi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It netted her three consecutive nominations for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emmy_Awards" title="Emmy Awards">Emmy Awards</a> in 1999, 2000 and 2001 and at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> awards for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Golden_Globe_Awards:_Television,_Best_Actress,_Drama" title="List of Golden Globe Awards: Television, Best Actress, Drama">Best TV Actress in a Drama</a> in 2000, 2001 and 2002. She lost out at the Emmys in 1999 and 2001 and at the Golden Globes in 2000 to her co-star <a href="/wiki/Edie_Falco" title="Edie Falco">Edie Falco</a>. She was nominated again at the 2007 Emmy Awards for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actress_-_Drama_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress - Drama Series">Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series</a>, thus being pitted against <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sopranos" title="The Sopranos">The Sopranos</a></i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Aida_Turturro" title="Aida Turturro">Aida Turturro</a> for the award.</p>
<p>In October 2015, it was announced that Bracco would guest star opposite <a href="/wiki/Adrien_Brody" title="Adrien Brody">Adrien Brody</a> on the upcoming Showtime comedy series <i>Dice</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorraine_Bracco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bracco's first marriage was to Daniel Guerard from the late 1970s to the early 1980s. They have one daughter, Margaux Guerard.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> She was in a 12-year relationship with actor <a href="/wiki/Harvey_Keitel" title="Harvey Keitel">Harvey Keitel</a>. They have one daughter, Stella Keitel (b. 1985). Bracco and Keitel fought a lengthy custody battle over Stella, resulting in Bracco's <a href="/wiki/Depression_(mood)" title="Depression (mood)">depression</a>, a loss of acting roles, and $2 million in legal fees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-14">[14]</a></sup> She married <a href="/wiki/Edward_James_Olmos" title="Edward James Olmos">Edward James Olmos</a> in 1994. They divorced in 2002.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Lorraine Bracco is a practitioner of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shotokan_Karate" title="Shotokan Karate">Shotokan Karate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2015 she authored a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self_help" title="Self help">self help</a> book <i>To the Fullest: The Clean Up Your Act Plan to Lose Weight, Rejuvenate, and Be the Best You Can Be</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
