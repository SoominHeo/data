<p><i><b>Lorenzo's Oil</b></i> is a 1992 American <a href="/wiki/Drama_(film_and_television)" title="Drama (film and television)">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/George_Miller_(director)" title="George Miller (director)">George Miller</a>. It is based on the true story of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Augusto_and_Michaela_Odone" title="Augusto and Michaela Odone">Augusto and Michaela Odone</a>, two parents in a relentless search for a cure for their son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Odone" title="Lorenzo Odone">Lorenzo</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Adrenoleukodystrophy" title="Adrenoleukodystrophy">adrenoleukodystrophy</a> (ALD). The film was nominated for two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Awards</a>. It was filmed primarily from September 1991 to February 1992 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The film had a limited release in North America on December 30, 1992, with a nationwide release two weeks later on January 15, 1993.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo%27s_Oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lorenzo (<a href="/wiki/Zack_O%27Malley_Greenburg" title="Zack O'Malley Greenburg">Zack O'Malley Greenburg</a>) is a bright and vibrant young boy living in the <a href="/wiki/Comoro_Islands" title="Comoro Islands">Comoro Islands</a>, as his father <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Augusto_and_Michaela_Odone" title="Augusto and Michaela Odone">Augusto</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nick_Nolte" title="Nick Nolte">Nick Nolte</a>) works for the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> and is stationed there. However, when his parents relocate to the United States, he begins to show neurological problems, such as loss of hearing, tantrums, etc. The boy is diagnosed as having <a href="/wiki/Adrenoleukodystrophy" title="Adrenoleukodystrophy">adrenoleukodystrophy</a> (ALD), which is fatal within two years. Failing to find a doctor capable of treating their son's rare disease, Augusto and his wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Augusto_and_Michaela_Odone" title="Augusto and Michaela Odone">Michaela</a> (<a href="/wiki/Susan_Sarandon" title="Susan Sarandon">Susan Sarandon</a>) set out on a mission to find a treatment to save their child. In their quest, the Odones clash with doctors, scientists, and support groups, who are skeptical that anything could be done about ALD, much less by laypeople. But they persist, setting up camp in medical libraries, reviewing animal experiments, enlisting the aid of Professor Gus Nikolais (<a href="/wiki/Peter_Ustinov" title="Peter Ustinov">Peter Ustinov</a>), badgering researchers, questioning top doctors all over the world, and even organizing an international symposium about the disease.</p>
<p>Despite research dead-ends, the horror of watching their son's health decline, and being surrounded by skeptics (including the coordinators of the support group they attend), they persist until they finally hit upon a therapy involving adding a certain kind of <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo%27s_oil" title="Lorenzo's oil">oil</a> (actually containing two specific <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_chain_fatty_acids" title="Long chain fatty acids">long chain fatty acids</a>, isolated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapeseed_oil" title="Rapeseed oil">rapeseed oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olive_oil" title="Olive oil">olive oil</a>) to their son's diet. They contact over 100 firms around the world until they find an elderly British chemist (Don Suddaby) working for <a href="/wiki/Croda_International" title="Croda International">Croda International</a> who is willing to take on the challenge of distilling the proper formula. The oil, <a href="/wiki/Erucic_acid" title="Erucic acid">erucic acid</a>, proves successful in normalizing the accumulation of the very long chain fatty acids in the brain that had been causing their son's steady decline, thereby halting the progression of the disease. There is still a great deal of neurological damage remaining which could not be reversed unless new treatments could be found to regenerate the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Myelin_sheath" title="Myelin sheath">myelin sheath</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Lipid" title="Lipid">lipid</a> insulator) around the nerves. The father is seen taking on the new challenge of organizing <a href="/wiki/The_Myelin_Project" title="The Myelin Project">biomedical efforts to heal myelin damage in patients</a>.</p>
<p>Finally, Lorenzo, at the age of 14, shows definite improvement (swallowing for himself and answering "yes" or "no" questions by blinking) but more medical research is still needed. Ultimately it is revealed that Lorenzo has also regained his sight and is learning to use a computer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo%27s_Oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Principal photography for <i>Lorenzo's Oil</i> began on September 9, 1991 in <a href="/wiki/Ben_Avon,_Pennsylvania" title="Ben Avon, Pennsylvania">Ben Avon, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Possibly to emphasize the "Everyman" aspect of the plot (the notion that a cure could affect families and individuals anywhere), many smaller roles were played by inexperienced actors or non-actors with unusual physical features and mannerisms. For example, the poet <a href="/wiki/James_Merrill" title="James Merrill">James Merrill</a> was noticed by a casting director at a New York public reading of his poetry. His rarefied speaking cadences were utilized in a symposium scene in which he played a questioning doctor.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo%27s_Oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film features <a href="/wiki/Miserere_(Allegri)" title="Miserere (Allegri)">Allegri's Miserere</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edward_Elgar" title="Edward Elgar">Edward Elgar</a>'s cello concerto, as well as <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Barber" title="Samuel Barber">Barber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Adagio_for_Strings" title="Adagio for Strings">Adagio for Strings</a> and Mozart's Ave verum corpus.</p>
<p>The opening song is "Kijana Mwana Mwali" ("Song about a Young Lady"), sung by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gonda_Traditional_Entertainers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gonda Traditional Entertainers (page does not exist)">Gonda Traditional Entertainers</a>.</p>
<p>A 1960 recording of <a href="/wiki/Maria_Callas" title="Maria Callas">Maria Callas</a> with the <a href="/wiki/La_Scala" title="La Scala">La Scala</a> orchestra and chorus is heard singing selections from Bellini's <i><a href="/wiki/Norma_(opera)" title="Norma (opera)">Norma</a></i> at several points.</p>
<p>The music for the Easter Midnight Mass scene is a <a href="/wiki/Russian_Orthodox_Church" title="Russian Orthodox Church">Russian Orthodox Church</a> hymn, "Bogoroditse Devo" (Rejoice, O Virgin) from "Three Choruses from 'Tsar Feodor Ioannovich'", taken from the album <i>Sacred Songs of Russia</i> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gloriae_Dei_Cantores" title="Gloriae Dei Cantores">Gloriae Dei Cantores</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo%27s_Oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo%27s_Oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Lorenzo's Oil</i> was acclaimed by critics. <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun_Times" title="Chicago Sun Times">Chicago Sun Times</a></i> gave it four out of four stars and called it an "immensely moving and challenging movie".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He added, "it was impossible not to get swept up in it" and <a href="/wiki/James_Berardinelli" title="James Berardinelli">James Berardinelli</a> of ReelViews gave it three out of four stars and claimed, "it was about the war for knowledge and the victory of hope through perseverance." <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">Review aggregation</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> retrospectively collected reviews from 32 critics to give the film a score of 95%, with an average rating of 7/10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Medical_response">Medical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo%27s_Oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Medical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Though the film seemed to accurately portray the events related to the boy's condition and his parents' efforts during the time period covered by the film, it was criticized for painting a picture of a miracle cure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC07212004_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC07212004-5">[5]</a></sup> Subsequent research with Lorenzo's oil has not clearly proven its long-term effectiveness in treating ALD after its onset,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oil_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-oil-6">[6]</a></sup> but the oil is highly effective if provided before onset.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC07212004_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC07212004-5">[5]</a></sup> The actual subject of the film, Lorenzo Odone, died of pneumonia in May 2008 at the age of 30, having lived two decades longer than originally predicted by doctors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hugo_Moser_(scientist)" title="Hugo Moser (scientist)">Hugo Moser</a>, whom Professor Nikolais was based on, was very angry at the way the film portrayed him.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo%27s_Oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While a critical success, the film was a financial disappointment taking in $7,286,388 domestically with a budget of around $30 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo%27s_Oil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Awards and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Lorenzo's Oil</i> was nominated twice at the <a href="/wiki/65th_Academy_Awards" title="65th Academy Awards">65th Academy Awards</a> for Best Actress in a Leading Role (Susan Sarandon) and Best Original Screenplay (George Miller &amp; Nick Enright).</p>
<p>Susan Sarandon was nominated for Best Actress at the <a href="/wiki/50th_Golden_Globe_Awards" title="50th Golden Globe Awards">50th Golden Globe Awards</a>.</p>
<p>The film was nominated for Best Original Screenplay Written Directly for the Screen at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WGA_Award" title="WGA Award">WGA Awards</a>.</p>
<p>Also, the film is recognized by <a href="/wiki/American_Film_Institute" title="American Film Institute">American Film Institute</a> in these lists:</p>
<ul>
<li>2006: <a href="/wiki/AFI%27s_100_Years...100_Cheers" title="AFI's 100 Years...100 Cheers">AFI's 100 Years...100 Cheers</a> – Nominated<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
