<p><i><b>Freeheld</b></i> is a 2015 American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sollett" title="Peter Sollett">Peter Sollett</a> and written by <a href="/wiki/Ron_Nyswaner" title="Ron Nyswaner">Ron Nyswaner</a>. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Julianne_Moore" title="Julianne Moore">Julianne Moore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Page" title="Ellen Page">Ellen Page</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Carell" title="Steve Carell">Steve Carell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luke_Grimes" title="Luke Grimes">Luke Grimes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Shannon" title="Michael Shannon">Michael Shannon</a>. It is based on the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Freeheld_(2007_film)" title="Freeheld (2007 film)">documentary short film of the same name</a> about police officer <a href="/wiki/Laurel_Hester" title="Laurel Hester">Laurel Hester</a>'s fight against the <a href="/wiki/Ocean_County,_New_Jersey" title="Ocean County, New Jersey">Ocean County, New Jersey</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Board_of_Chosen_Freeholders" title="Board of Chosen Freeholders">Board of Chosen Freeholders</a> to allow her pension benefits to be transferred to her domestic partner after being diagnosed with terminal cancer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeheld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film is based on the true story of <a href="/wiki/Laurel_Hester" title="Laurel Hester">Laurel Hester</a> (Moore), a police officer in <a href="/wiki/Ocean_County,_New_Jersey" title="Ocean County, New Jersey">Ocean County, New Jersey</a>. The story narrates the difficulties faced by a <a href="/wiki/Lesbian" title="Lesbian">lesbian</a> police <a href="/wiki/Detective" title="Detective">detective</a> and her domestic partner, Stacie Andree (Page). Following her diagnosis with terminal <a href="/wiki/Lung_cancer" title="Lung cancer">lung cancer</a> in 2005, Hester repeatedly appealed to the county's <a href="/wiki/Board_of_chosen_freeholders" title="Board of chosen freeholders">board of chosen freeholders</a> in an attempt to ensure her pension benefits could be passed on to her domestic partner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-app_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-app-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeheld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeheld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Screenwriter <a href="/wiki/Ron_Nyswaner" title="Ron Nyswaner">Ron Nyswaner</a> announced his intention in 2010 to write a feature-length adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Cynthia_Wade" title="Cynthia Wade">Cynthia Wade</a>'s 2007 Oscar-winning short film <i>Freeheld</i>, a documentary about New Jersey police officer <a href="/wiki/Laurel_Hester" title="Laurel Hester">Laurel Hester</a>'s fight against the <a href="/wiki/Ocean_County,_New_Jersey" title="Ocean County, New Jersey">Ocean County, New Jersey</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Board_of_Chosen_Freeholders" title="Board of Chosen Freeholders">Board of Chosen Freeholders</a> to pass on her pension benefits to her domestic partner, Stacie Andree, after being diagnosed with terminal cancer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Page_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Page-6">[6]</a></sup> At that time, <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Page" title="Ellen Page">Ellen Page</a> had already agreed to star as Andree; she stated in 2014 that she had been involved in the project's development for almost six years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Page_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Page-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She first became involved after two of the film's producers, <a href="/wiki/Stacey_Sher" title="Stacey Sher">Stacey Sher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Shamberg" title="Michael Shamberg">Michael Shamberg</a>, sent her a copy of Wade's documentary and asked if she was interested in starring in an adaptation, an offer which she accepted "right away".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Hardwicke" title="Catherine Hardwicke">Catherine Hardwicke</a> was attached to the project as director early in its development but later withdrew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hardwicke_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hardwicke-10">[10]</a></sup> Shortly after the film's financing was secured in August 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hardwicke_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hardwicke-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sollett" title="Peter Sollett">Peter Sollett</a> signed on to replace Hardwicke as director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Julianne_Moore" title="Julianne Moore">Julianne Moore</a> was cast as Hester in February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moore_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Moore-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Zach_Galifianakis" title="Zach Galifianakis">Zach Galifianakis</a> was cast as <a href="/wiki/Steven_Goldstein_(activist)" title="Steven Goldstein (activist)">Steven Goldstein</a>, the founder and then-Chair of <a href="/wiki/Garden_State_Equality" title="Garden State Equality">Garden State Equality</a>, but was later replaced by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Carell" title="Steve Carell">Steve Carell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-app_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-app-4">[4]</a></sup> Andree consulted with Page, Sollett and Nyswaner about the making of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeheld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although the film is set in New Jersey, it was filmed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> due to its more generous tax incentives for filmmakers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-app_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-app-4">[4]</a></sup> Filming began in October 2014 in <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In late October, a scene that was supposed to be filmed at <a href="/wiki/Salesian_High_School_(New_Rochelle)" title="Salesian High School (New Rochelle)">Salesian High School</a>, a Catholic boys' school in <a href="/wiki/New_Rochelle,_New_York" title="New Rochelle, New York">New Rochelle, New York</a>, had to be relocated after the school's principal reversed his decision to allow the crew to film on the school campus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Salesian_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Salesian-14">[14]</a></sup> The shoot was later relocated to the city hall of <a href="/wiki/Rye,_New_York" title="Rye, New York">Rye, New York</a>, which stood in for the Ocean County Administration Building<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-County_of_Ocean_Government_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-County_of_Ocean_Government-15">[15]</a></sup> in a scene where Hester and Andree apply for domestic partnership.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Salesian_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Salesian-14">[14]</a></sup> Another scene, in which Hester lies ill in a hospital room, was filmed in the community center of <a href="/wiki/Greenburgh,_New_York" title="Greenburgh, New York">Greenburgh, New York</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> while the town board room of <a href="/wiki/North_Hempstead,_New_York" title="North Hempstead, New York">North Hempstead, New York</a> stood in for the Ocean County Board of Freeholders' chambers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeheld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2015, <a href="/wiki/Lionsgate" title="Lionsgate">Lionsgate</a> acquired distribution rights to the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> It received a <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">limited release</a> on October 2, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeheld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Freeheld</i> received mixed reviews from critics. <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">Review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> reports a 48% rating based on 122 reviews, with an average rating of 5.6/10. The consensus states: "<i>Freeheld</i> certainly means well, but its cardboard characters and by-the-numbers drama undermine its noble intentions."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a 50 out of 100 rating based on 29 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
