<p><b>Loreena Isabel Irene McKennitt</b>, <span style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Order_of_Canada" title="Order of Canada">CM</a> <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Manitoba" title="Order of Manitoba">OM</a></span> (born February 17, 1957) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian" title="Canadian">Canadian</a> musician, composer, harpist, accordionist and pianist who writes, records and performs <a href="/wiki/World_music" title="World music">world music</a> with <a href="/wiki/Celtic_music" title="Celtic music">Celtic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Middle_Eastern_music" title="Middle Eastern music">Middle Eastern</a> themes. McKennitt is known for her refined and clear <a href="/wiki/Dramatic_soprano" title="Dramatic soprano">dramatic</a> <a href="/wiki/Soprano" title="Soprano">soprano</a> vocals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loreena_McKennitt_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Loreena_McKennitt-1">[1]</a></sup> She has sold more than 14 million records worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loreena_McKennitt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McKennitt was born in <a href="/wiki/Morden,_Manitoba" title="Morden, Manitoba">Morden, Manitoba</a>, of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> descent to parents Jack (died 1992) and Irene McKennitt (1931–2011). In 1981, she moved to <a href="/wiki/Stratford,_Ontario" title="Stratford, Ontario">Stratford, Ontario</a>, where she still resides.</p>
<p>When she was young McKennitt wanted to become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veterinarian" title="Veterinarian">veterinarian</a>; she would later state that music chose her rather than she it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Developing a passion for Celtic music, she learned to play the <a href="/wiki/Celtic_harp" title="Celtic harp">Celtic harp</a> and began <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Busking" title="Busking">busking</a> at various places, including <a href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_Market" title="St. Lawrence Market">St. Lawrence Market</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> in order to earn money to record her first album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loreena_McKennitt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McKennitt's first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Elemental_(Loreena_McKennitt_album)" title="Elemental (Loreena McKennitt album)">Elemental</a></i>, was released in 1985, followed by <i><a href="/wiki/To_Drive_the_Cold_Winter_Away" title="To Drive the Cold Winter Away">To Drive the Cold Winter Away</a></i> (1987), <i><a href="/wiki/Parallel_Dreams" title="Parallel Dreams">Parallel Dreams</a></i> (1989), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Visit_(Loreena_McKennitt_album)" title="The Visit (Loreena McKennitt album)">The Visit</a></i> (1991), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mask_and_Mirror" title="The Mask and Mirror">The Mask and Mirror</a></i> (1994),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inc.1994_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inc.1994-5">[5]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/A_Winter_Garden:_Five_Songs_for_the_Season" title="A Winter Garden: Five Songs for the Season">A Winter Garden</a></i> (1995), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Book_of_Secrets" title="The Book of Secrets">The Book of Secrets</a></i> (1997), <i><a href="/wiki/An_Ancient_Muse" title="An Ancient Muse">An Ancient Muse</a></i> (2006), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Midwinter_Night%E2%80%99s_Dream" title="A Midwinter Night’s Dream">A Midwinter Night’s Dream</a></i> (2008), and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wind_That_Shakes_the_Barley_(album)" title="The Wind That Shakes the Barley (album)">The Wind That Shakes the Barley</a></i> (2010). All of her work is released under her own label, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quinlan_Road" title="Quinlan Road">Quinlan Road</a>.</p>
<p>In 1990, McKennitt provided the music for the <a href="/wiki/National_Film_Board_of_Canada" title="National Film Board of Canada">National Film Board of Canada</a> documentary <i><a href="/wiki/The_Burning_Times" title="The Burning Times">The Burning Times</a></i>, a feminist revisionist account of the Early Modern European <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Witchcraft_trials" title="Witchcraft trials">witchcraft trials</a>. The main theme song would later be rerecorded by her and her band on her album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Visit_(Loreena_McKennitt_album)" title="The Visit (Loreena McKennitt album)">The Visit</a></i> under the title "Tango to Evora."</p>
<p>In 1993, she toured Europe supporting <a href="/wiki/Mike_Oldfield" title="Mike Oldfield">Mike Oldfield</a>. In 1995, her version of the traditional Irish song "<a href="/wiki/Bonny_Portmore" title="Bonny Portmore">Bonny Portmore</a>" was featured in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Highlander_(TV_series)" title="Highlander (TV series)">Highlander</a></i> series. McKennitt's single "<a href="/wiki/The_Mummers%27_Dance" title="The Mummers' Dance">The Mummers' Dance</a>" received airplay in North American markets during the spring of 1997, and was used as the theme song for the short-lived TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Legacy_(TV_series)" title="Legacy (TV series)">Legacy</a></i>. It also saw use in the trailer for a wide-release 1998 <a href="/wiki/Drew_Barrymore" title="Drew Barrymore">Drew Barrymore</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Ever_After" title="Ever After">Ever After</a>.</i></p>
<p>Her music appeared in the movies <i><a href="/wiki/The_Santa_Clause" title="The Santa Clause">The Santa Clause</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Soldier_(1998_American_film)" title="Soldier (1998 American film)">Soldier</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jade_(film)" title="Jade (film)">Jade</a></i>, <i>Holy Man</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Mists_of_Avalon_(TV_miniseries)" title="The Mists of Avalon (TV miniseries)">The Mists of Avalon</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Tinker_Bell_(film)" title="Tinker Bell (film)">Tinkerbell</a></i>; and in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Roar_(TV_series)" title="Roar (TV series)">Roar</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Due_South" title="Due South">Due South</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Full_Circle_(Women_and_Spirituality)" title="Full Circle (Women and Spirituality)">Full Circle (Women and Spirituality)</a></i>.</p>
<p>On November 30, 2012, McKennitt lent her support to <a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Golden_Hat_Foundation" title="Golden Hat Foundation">Golden Hat Foundation</a> together with <a href="/wiki/Tim_Janis" title="Tim Janis">Tim Janis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarah_McLachlan" title="Sarah McLachlan">Sarah McLachlan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Corr" title="Andrea Corr">Andrea Corr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hayley_Westenra" title="Hayley Westenra">Hayley Westenra</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sleepy_Man_Banjo_Boys" title="Sleepy Man Banjo Boys">Sleepy Man Banjo Boys</a>, Dawn Kenney, <a href="/wiki/Jana_Mashonee" title="Jana Mashonee">Jana Mashonee</a>, Amy Petty and a choir etc performing on "The American Christmas Carol" concert in <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loreena_McKennitt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 1998, McKennitt's fiancé Ronald Rees, his brother Richard and their close friend Gregory Cook drowned in a boating accident on <a href="/wiki/Georgian_Bay" title="Georgian Bay">Georgian Bay</a>. She was deeply affected by the event and subsequently founded the <a href="/wiki/Cook-Rees_Memorial_Fund" title="Cook-Rees Memorial Fund">Cook-Rees Memorial Fund</a> for Water Search and Safety in the same year.</p>
<p>At the time of the incident, she was working on a live album of two performances called <i><a href="/wiki/Live_in_Paris_and_Toronto" title="Live in Paris and Toronto">Live in Paris and Toronto</a></i>. The proceeds from this album were donated to the newly created memorial fund, totaling some three million dollars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> After the release of the live album, McKennitt decided to substantially reduce the number of her public performances and did not release any new recordings until the studio album <i><a href="/wiki/An_Ancient_Muse" title="An Ancient Muse">An Ancient Muse</a></i> in 2006.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loreena_McKennitt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Genre_and_work">Genre and work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loreena_McKennitt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Genre and work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>McKennitt's music has generally been classified as <a href="/wiki/World_music" title="World music">World</a> or <a href="/wiki/Celtic_music" title="Celtic music">Celtic music</a> even though it contains aspects and characteristics of music from around the globe and is sometimes classified as <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk music</a> in record stores.</p>
<p>Before McKennitt composes any music, she engages in considerable research on a specific subject which then forms the general concept of the album. Before creating <i>Elemental</i> and <i>Parallel Dreams</i>, she travelled to <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> for inspiration from the country's history, <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">folklore</a>, geography and culture. The album <i>The Mask and Mirror</i> was preceded by research in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> where she engaged in studying <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a>, a Celtic section of Spain, along with its abundant Arabic roots.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The result was an album that included elements of Celtic and Arabic music. According to the jacket notes, her album <i>An Ancient Muse</i> was inspired by travels among and reading about the various cultures along the <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a>.</p>
<p>McKennitt is occasionally compared to <a href="/wiki/Enya" title="Enya">Enya</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> but McKennitt's music is more grounded in traditional and classical invocations, using literary works as sources of lyrics and springboards for interpretation such as "<a href="/wiki/The_Lady_of_Shalott" title="The Lady of Shalott">The Lady of Shalott</a>" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfred_Tennyson,_1st_Baron_Tennyson" title="Alfred Tennyson, 1st Baron Tennyson">Lord Tennyson</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Prospero" title="Prospero">Prospero</a>'s Speech" (the final <a href="/wiki/Soliloquy" title="Soliloquy">soliloquy</a> in <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tempest" title="The Tempest">The Tempest</a></i>), "Snow" by <a href="/wiki/Archibald_Lampman" title="Archibald Lampman">Archibald Lampman</a>, "Dark Night of the Soul" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._John_of_the_Cross" title="St. John of the Cross">St. John of the Cross</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dante" title="Dante">Dante</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dante%27s_Inferno" title="Dante's Inferno"><i>Inferno</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Blake" title="William Blake">William Blake</a>'s "Lullaby", <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Butler_Yeats" title="William Butler Yeats">Yeats'</a> "<a href="/wiki/The_Stolen_Child" title="The Stolen Child">The Stolen Child</a>", "The English Ladye and the Knight" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Walter_Scott" title="Sir Walter Scott">Sir Walter Scott</a> and "<a href="/wiki/The_Highwayman_(poem)" title="The Highwayman (poem)">The Highwayman</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Noyes" title="Alfred Noyes">Alfred Noyes</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Court_case">Court case</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loreena_McKennitt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Court case">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/McKennitt_v_Ash" title="McKennitt v Ash">McKennitt v Ash</a></div>
<p>In 2005, McKennitt was involved in an acrimonious court case in England when her former friend and employee, Niema Ash, published a book that contained intimate details of their friendship. McKennitt argued that much of the book contained confidential personal information that Ash had no right to publish. The English courts found that there had indeed been a breach of confidence and a misuse of McKennitt's private information, and the case is likely to set important precedents in the law of England and Wales on the privacy of public figures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a> affirmed the lower court's decisions in 2007.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2006_and_later">2006 and later</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loreena_McKennitt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2006 and later">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 2006, McKennitt performed live at the <a href="/wiki/Alhambra" title="Alhambra">Alhambra</a>. The performance premiered on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Broadcasting_Service" title="Public Broadcasting Service">PBS</a> and in August 2007 was released on a three-disc DVD/CD set titled <i><a href="/wiki/Nights_from_the_Alhambra" title="Nights from the Alhambra">Nights from the Alhambra</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2008, McKennitt wrote and composed a song she titled "To The Fairies They Draw Near" as the theme song for Disney's direct-to-video animated film <i><a href="/wiki/Tinker_Bell_(film)" title="Tinker Bell (film)">Tinker Bell</a></i>. She also provided the narration for the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2008, she returned to <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gabriel" title="Peter Gabriel">Peter Gabriel</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Real_World_Studios" title="Real World Studios">Real World Studios</a> to record <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Midwinter_Night%E2%80%99s_Dream" title="A Midwinter Night’s Dream">A Midwinter Night’s Dream</a></i>, an extended version of her 1995 mini-album <i><a href="/wiki/A_Winter_Garden:_Five_Songs_for_the_Season" title="A Winter Garden: Five Songs for the Season">A Winter Garden: Five Songs for the Season</a></i>. The album was released on October 28, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since the release of <i>An Ancient Muse</i>, McKennitt has toured consistently, with a European and North American tour in 2007 and another extensive tour across Canada and United States later in 2007, a tour of Europe in 2008 and a Mediterranean tour in 2009 with stops in Greece,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Turkey, Cyprus, Lebanon, Hungary and Italy.</p>
<p>On September 17, 2009, McKennitt announced that she planned to release a two-disc album titled <i>A Mediterranean Odyssey</i>. The first CD, "From Istanbul to Athens", consisted of 10 new live recordings made during her 2009 Mediterranean tour, including songs she had never before recorded in concert. The second CD, "The Olive and the Cedar", had a Mediterranean theme which McKennitt herself curated. It contained previously released studio recordings created between 1994 and 2006.</p>
<p>November 16, 2010, saw the US release (November 12 for Europe) of McKennitt's latest studio album, <i>The Wind That Shakes the Barley</i>. Recorded at the Sharon Temple, Ontario, it consists of nine traditional Celtic songs. "Every once and again there is a pull to return to one's own roots or beginnings, with the perspective of time and experience, to feel the familiar things you once loved and love still", said McKennitt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>When McKennitt released <i>The Wind that Shakes the Barley</i> she visited several countries to help promote the album. During the promotional tour she performed an hour-long concert in the studios of German radio station SWR1, accompanied only by Brian Hughes (guitars) and <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Lavelle" title="Caroline Lavelle">Caroline Lavelle</a> (cello) who have long been part of her tours and recordings. This live concert was released on CD in 2011. Called <i>Troubadours on the Rhine</i>, the album was nominated for a 2012 Grammy for Best New Age Album.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Documentaries">Documentaries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loreena_McKennitt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Documentaries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Late in the 1990s, McKennitt created <i>No Journey's End</i>, a half-hour <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary</a>, for <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> in which she discussed the influences behind her music. <i>No Journey's End</i> contained excerpts from several songs from the albums <i><a href="/wiki/Parallel_Dreams" title="Parallel Dreams">Parallel Dreams</a>,</i> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Visit_(Loreena_McKennitt_album)" title="The Visit (Loreena McKennitt album)">The Visit</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mask_and_Mirror" title="The Mask and Mirror">The Mask and Mirror</a></i> It also shows live performances of the songs "The Lady of Shalott", "Santiago", and "The Dark Night of the Soul". It was later released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a href="/wiki/VHS" title="VHS">VHS</a>, the former also containing <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music videos</a> for "The Mummers' Dance" and "The Bonny Swans." A bonus copy of the DVD was included with the 2004 remastered versions of McKennitt's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CD" title="CD">CDs</a>.</p>
<p>In 2008, McKennitt released <i>A Moveable Musical Feast</i>, based on her 2007 <i>An Ancient Muse</i> tour. The DVD included interviews with McKennitt, her band, crew, fans and professional colleagues from the Canadian music industry.</p>

<p><i>Quinlan Road</i> is an <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent record label</a> founded in <a href="/wiki/1985_in_music" title="1985 in music">1985</a> and based in <a href="/wiki/Stratford,_Ontario" title="Stratford, Ontario">Stratford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It is maintained by its sole artist, Loreena McKennitt. Quinlan Road started out at McKennitt's home where she sold recordings by mail order. Today Quinlan Road music is distributed internationally by <a href="/wiki/Verve_Records" title="Verve Records">Verve Records</a>, <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keltia_Musique" title="Keltia Musique">Keltia Musique</a>, and <a href="/wiki/SPV_GmbH" title="SPV GmbH">SPV GmbH</a>.</p>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eileen_McGann" title="Eileen McGann">Eileen McGann</a> – fellow Irish-Canadian female Celtic folksinger. During McKennitt's early career McGann played many of the same venues, and they appeared together on several early compilation recordings.</li>
</ul>
