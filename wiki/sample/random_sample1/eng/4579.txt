<p><b>Roman Aleksandrovich Shishkin</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Роман Александрович Шишкин</span>; born 27 January 1987 in <a href="/wiki/Voronezh" title="Voronezh">Voronezh</a>) is an association footballer, who plays for <a href="/wiki/FC_Lokomotiv_Moscow" title="FC Lokomotiv Moscow">Lokomotiv Moscow</a> in <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">Russian Premier League</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Position">Position</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roman_Shishkin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Position">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shiskin plays as <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">fullback</a> on either flank or <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roman_Shishkin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Roman Shishkin was born in <a href="/wiki/Voronezh" title="Voronezh">Voronezh</a>. He is a son of Aleksandr Shishkin, a driver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roman_Shishkin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shishkin graduated from local football school and at age 15 secured his first professional contract with <a href="/wiki/FC_Fakel_Voronezh" title="FC Fakel Voronezh">FC Fakel</a>. After some time with Fakel he moved to Spartak Moscow.</p>
<p>He made his professional debut for Spartak in 2004 when <a href="/wiki/Nevio_Scala" title="Nevio Scala">Nevio Scala</a> started fielding a lot of young players. Roman eventually lost his position in the first team. In 2006 when Fedotov was appointed as a manager, Shishkin became a first team regular. Shishkin played as a right fullback.</p>
<p>On 12 September 2006 Shishkin made his European debut after he came in as a substitute in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayern_Munich" title="Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>. At the end of 2006 <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> named Shishkin among Europe's eight most prospective footballers and described him as having a good shot from distance and being useful from set-pieces<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009 <a href="/wiki/FC_Spartak_Moscow" title="FC Spartak Moscow">FC Spartak Moscow</a> sent Shiskin on loan to <a href="/wiki/FC_Krylia_Sovetov_Samara" title="FC Krylia Sovetov Samara">FC Krylia Sovetov Samara</a>.</p>
<p>On 6 July 2010, Shishkin signed with <a href="/wiki/FC_Lokomotiv_Moscow" title="FC Lokomotiv Moscow">Lokomotiv Moscow</a>. His attitude and performances for the club were rewarded in 2013 when he was named <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">vice-captain</a> by manager <a href="/wiki/Slaven_Bili%C4%87" title="Slaven Bilić">Slaven Bilić</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roman_Shishkin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Shiskin was invited by <a href="/wiki/Guus_Hiddink" title="Guus Hiddink">Guus Hiddink</a> to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">national team</a> training sessions. His debut for the national team took place on 24 March 2007 as he played the whole match during Russia's 2–0 victory over Estonia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a few years' absence from the squad, Shishkin forced his way back into contention following some good performances for Lokomotiv Moscow, and he would play a part in Russia's successful qualifying campaign for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_2012" title="Euro 2012">Euro 2012</a>. He was named in <a href="/wiki/Dick_Advocaat" title="Dick Advocaat">Dick Advocaat</a>'s provisional squad for the tournament itself, but had to withdraw due to a stomach ailment.</p>
<p>Shishkin did not appear for the national team again until late 2015, when he featured in friendlies against Portugal and Croatia. He was then included by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leonid_Slutsky_(footballer)" title="Leonid Slutsky (footballer)">Leonid Slutsky</a> in the Russian squad for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euro_2016" title="Euro 2016">Euro 2016</a>.</p>
