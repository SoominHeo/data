<p>The <b>women's 4 × 100 metres relay</b> competition at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Olympic Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> took place on 9–10 August at the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Stadium_(London)" title="Olympic Stadium (London)">Olympic Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The victorious United States team broke the world record by over half a second. The previous record had been set 27 years previously by <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East Germany</a>, a result that was widely considered suspicious due to East Germany's <a href="/wiki/Doping_in_East_Germany" title="Doping in East Germany">record of doping</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The Jamaican team, 6 metres back, missed the previous world record by .04, but set a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jamaican_records_in_athletics" title="Jamaican records in athletics">national record</a>. Another 7 metres back, Ukraine took the bronze, also setting a new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ukrainian_records_in_athletics" title="Ukrainian records in athletics">national record</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAAF_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-IAAF-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Final">Final</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Athletics_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_4_%C3%97_100_metres_relay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Final">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The American team had an often repeated history of relay failures, so most of the pre-race discussion focused on whether it could successfully get the baton around the track. At the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> in Beijing, both the men's and women's 4 x 100 metres teams had dropped the baton, leading one reporter to call it the "nadir in US relay history".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the first leg of the final, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianna_Madison" title="Tianna Madison">Tianna Madison</a> was able to hold her own against the double 100 metre gold medalist <a href="/wiki/Shelly-Ann_Fraser-Pryce" title="Shelly-Ann Fraser-Pryce">Shelly-Ann Fraser-Pryce</a>. <a href="/wiki/Allyson_Felix" title="Allyson Felix">Allyson Felix</a> put the USA into the lead, extended by <a href="/wiki/Bianca_Knight" title="Bianca Knight">Bianca Knight</a> around the turn. At the final handoff to individual 100 metre silver medalist <a href="/wiki/Carmelita_Jeter" title="Carmelita Jeter">Carmelita Jeter</a>, the team enjoyed a 3-metre lead. Secure in the handoffs, Jeter sped to the finish, noticing the time and pointing at the clock before the finish line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
