<p><b>Percy Romeo Miller, Jr.</b> (born August 19, 1989),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> better known by his stage name <b>Maserati Rome</b> or simply <b>Rome</b> (formerly <b>Lil' Romeo</b> and <b>Romeo</b>) or his acting/business name <b>Romeo Miller</b>, is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basketball_player" title="Basketball player">basketball player</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entrepreneur" title="Entrepreneur">entrepreneur</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">model</a>. Miller is the son of well known rap mogul <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a>. Miller has founded <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Guttar_Music_Entertainment" title="Guttar Music Entertainment">Guttar Music</a>, Take A Stand, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Next_Generation_Entertainment" title="The Next Generation Entertainment">The Next Generation</a> &amp; his current label <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/No_Limit_Forever" title="No Limit Forever">No Limit Forever</a>.</p>
<p>Miller gained fame and fortune in the early 2000s after signing with his father's popular and successful label at the time <a href="/wiki/No_Limit_Records" title="No Limit Records">No Limit Records</a> with the help of his debut single &amp; most successful single to date "<a href="/wiki/My_Baby_(Lil%27_Romeo_song)" title="My Baby (Lil' Romeo song)">My Baby</a>" which went on to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Platinum_certification" title="Platinum certification">certified platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA. In 2001 Miller released his debut &amp; most successful album to date <i><a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Romeo_(album)" title="Lil' Romeo (album)">Lil 'Romeo</a></i> it charted the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at number six &amp; went on to be certified gold, selling over 500,000 copies.</p>
<p>Miller performed the single "My Baby" with his father Master P for late pop singer Michael Jackson at Jackson's 30th Anniversary Celebration, which was also broadcast on television.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In the later 2000s, as <a href="/wiki/No_Limit_Records" title="No Limit Records">No Limit Records</a> popularity was slowly declining, so was Miller's. Miller then signed to his father's relaunch label <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_No_Limit_Records" title="New No Limit Records">New No Limit Records</a>. In 2002, Miller released his second album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Time" title="Game Time">Game Time</a></i>; it charted the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at number thirty-three. In 2004, Miller released his third album <i><a href="/wiki/Romeoland" title="Romeoland">Romeoland</a></i>; it charted on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at number seventy. In 2002, <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> offered Miller his own show titled <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo!" title="Romeo!">Romeo!</a></i>; the show was successful on the network and went on to run for three seasons.</p>
<p>In 2006, Miller released his digital album titled <i>Lottery</i> it was his first album released with his name changed to Romeo. In 2006, Miller released the <i><a href="/wiki/God%27s_Gift_(soundtrack)" title="God's Gift (soundtrack)">soundtrack</a></i> to his movie <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/God%27s_Gift_(film)" title="God's Gift (film)">God's Gift</a>; it was Miller's first album to be explicit. In 2007, Miller was offered and accepted a scholarship to play for the <a href="/wiki/USC_Trojans_men%27s_basketball" title="USC Trojans men's basketball">USC Trojans</a>; Miller would play for the team until he was let go before his junior season in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Miller starred in the successful film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jumping_the_Broom_(film)" title="Jumping the Broom (film)">Jumping the Broom</a></i>. In 2012 Miller would also star in <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Perry" title="Tyler Perry">Tyler Perry</a>'s successful film <i><a href="/wiki/Madea%27s_Witness_Protection" title="Madea's Witness Protection">Madea's Witness Protection</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Romeo_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Miller was born in <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>. He is the son of rapper and entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a> and former rapper <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonya_C" title="Sonya C">Sonya C</a>. He is the nephew of rappers <a href="/wiki/C-Murder" title="C-Murder">C-Murder</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Silkk_the_Shocker" title="Silkk the Shocker">Silkk the Shocker</a>, cousin of producer, singer &amp; rapper <a href="/wiki/Mo_B._Dick" title="Mo B. Dick">Mo B. Dick</a> &amp; the brother of singer and actress <a href="/wiki/Cymphonique_Miller" title="Cymphonique Miller">Cymphonique Miller</a>. Romeo was signed to <a href="/wiki/No_Limit_Records" title="No Limit Records">No Limit</a>'s subsidiary label Soulja Music Entertainment at the age of five, after he wrote a rap to his father.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Romeo_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001:_Lil.27_Romeo">2001: <i>Lil' Romeo</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Romeo_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2001: Lil' Romeo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He released his debut album seven years later, titled after his original alias <i><a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Romeo_(album)" title="Lil' Romeo (album)">Lil' Romeo</a></i>. This offering contained the hit single "<a href="/wiki/My_Baby_(Lil%27_Romeo_song)" title="My Baby (Lil' Romeo song)">My Baby</a>" that charted No. 1 . R&amp;B/Hip-hop Singles. The album peaked at No. 6 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and No. 5 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a>, selling more than 200,000 copies in its first week out soon making it certified Gold. The album is now Miller's highest selling album selling over 897,000 copies. Also in 2001 he was featured on his uncle <a href="/wiki/Silkk_the_Shocker" title="Silkk the Shocker">Silkk the Shocker</a>'s remix single of "<a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Romeo_(album)" title="Lil' Romeo (album)">That's Kool</a>", which was also featured on his own album.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002.E2.80.9303:_Game_Time">2002â03: <i>Game Time</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Romeo_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2002â03: Game Time">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2002, a year later after his debut album, Miller put out his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Game_Time" title="Game Time">Game Time</a></i>. It peaked at No. 33 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and No. 10 on the <a href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Albums</a> selling close to 80,000 copies the first week .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Romeo_biography_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Romeo_biography-1">[1]</a></sup> It spawned the billboard charting single "<a href="/wiki/2-Way" title="2-Way">2-Way</a>" that charted to No. 66 <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a>. It also spawned the album second single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/True_Love_(Lil%27_Romeo_song)" title="True Love (Lil' Romeo song)">True Love</a>" which charted at No. 16 under the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a>. Though not as successful on the charts as Miller's debut album it was successful in sales for selling 301,000 copies.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004:_Romeoland">2004: <i>Romeoland</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Romeo_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2004: Romeoland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Miller released his third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Romeoland" title="Romeoland">Romeoland</a></i> via <a href="/wiki/No_Limit_Records" title="No Limit Records">The New No Limit</a> &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Koch_Records" title="Koch Records">Koch</a>, it would be Miller's last studio album to feature himself billed as Lil' Romeo; it would not be as successful in sales as his last two studio albums selling only 30,000 the first week, but the album would still managed to sell over 250,000 copies in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Romeo_biography_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Romeo_biography-1">[1]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Romeoland" title="Romeoland">Romeoland</a></i> would chart at #70 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and at #29 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Rap_Albums" title="Top Rap Albums">Top Rap Albums</a> chart.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9307:_Collaborations.2C_name_change.2C_Lottery_.26_God.27s_Gift_OST">2006â07: Collaborations, name change, <i>Lottery</i> &amp; <i>God's Gift OST</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Romeo_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2006â07: Collaborations, name change, Lottery &amp; God's Gift OST">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005 Miller along with his father <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a> founded a new record label titeld <a href="/wiki/Guttar_Music_Entertainment" title="Guttar Music Entertainment">Guttar Music Entertainment</a>. Also in 2005 Miller along with his brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valentino_(rapper)" title="Valentino (rapper)">Valentino Miller</a> who was known as Young V at that time and their three cousins C-Los, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D_(rapper)" title="D (rapper)">Lil' D</a> &amp; Willie J would form the rap group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rich_Boyz" title="Rich Boyz">Rich Boyz</a> &amp; on November 22, 2005 they would release their debut album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Ballers:_The_Hood_Been_Good_to_Us" title="Young Ballers: The Hood Been Good to Us">Young Ballers: The Hood Been Good to Us</a></i> via Guttar Music.The group would later disband though.</p>
<p>On April 4, 2006, Miller released his debut digital album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lottery_(album)" title="Lottery (album)">Lottery</a></i> via <a href="/wiki/Guttar_Music_Entertainment" title="Guttar Music Entertainment">Guttar Music</a>, UrbanDigital &amp; <a href="/wiki/GoDigital_Media_Group" title="GoDigital Media Group">GoDigital Music Group</a>, it was Miller's first album released with his official name changed to Romeo. On December 12, 2006 Miller would release an album titled <i><a href="/wiki/God%27s_Gift_(soundtrack)" title="God's Gift (soundtrack)">God's Gift</a></i> via Guttar Music, UrbanDigital &amp; GoDigital Music Group, it would serve as the <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">soundtrack</a> to the same name independent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/God%27s_Gift_(film)" title="God's Gift (film)">film</a> Miller's father <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a> had directed &amp; produced, it would also serve as Miller's first album to be classified as <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/explicit" title="wikt:explicit">explicit</a>; the soundtrack would go on to sell over 96,000 copies to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Romeo_biography_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Romeo_biography-1">[1]</a></sup> Both albums were known for containing Miller's song titled "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U_Can%27t_Shine_Like_Me" title="U Can't Shine Like Me">U Can't Shine Like Me</a>" which was a direct response to fellow young rapper/actor at the time Bow Wow, who fans believed dissed Miller &amp; his father Master P in his song "<a href="/wiki/Fresh_Azimiz" title="Fresh Azimiz">Fresh Azimiz</a>".</p>
<p>In 2007 Miller &amp; his father <a href="/wiki/Master_P" title="Master P">Master P</a> would start up a non-explicit record label titled Take A Stand Records. Also in 2007 Miller along with his father Master P would form a new group together titled Miller Boyz, on September 4, 2007 they would release their debut album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Hip_Hop_History" title="Hip Hop History">Hip Hop History</a></i> via Take A Stand Records, UrbanDigital &amp; <a href="/wiki/GoDigital_Media_Group" title="GoDigital Media Group">GoDigital Music Group</a>; the album would go on to sell over 32,000 records worldwide.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Romeo_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Romeo_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Miller started his acting career with a cameo appearance in the 2001 film <i><a href="/wiki/Max_Keeble%27s_Big_Move" title="Max Keeble's Big Move">Max Keeble's Big Move</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> He later co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Alba" title="Jessica Alba">Jessica Alba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mekhi_Phifer" title="Mekhi Phifer">Mekhi Phifer</a> and Zachary Isaiah Williams in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Honey_(2003_film)" title="Honey (2003 film)">Honey</a></i> (2003). In <i>Honey</i>, his supporting character was Benny, a young boy looking for a break.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> In 2003, he voiced himself on an episode of the animated series, <i><a href="/wiki/Static_Shock" title="Static Shock">Static Shock</a></i>, to which he had performed the theme song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> He starred in another movie with Zachary Isaiah Williams, <i><a href="/wiki/God%27s_Gift" title="God's Gift">God's Gift</a></i>, which came out in 2006. He then went on to co-star with his dad in a movie called <i>Uncle P</i> in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> His next project in 2007, was a film called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ASL" title="ASL">ASL</a></i> in which he starred as himself, Romeo, alongside Forrest Lipton and Zachary Isaiah Williams who played the young Romeo; Williams had previously co-starred with Romeo in <i>Honey</i> and Nickelodeon's <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo!" title="Romeo!">Romeo!</a></i>. Recently, Miller made a cameo appearance in <i><a href="/wiki/Ned%27s_Declassified_School_Survival_Guide" title="Ned's Declassified School Survival Guide">Ned's Declassified School Survival Guide</a></i> as a rapping coach for the teachers. He then guest-starred as a rapper accused of murder in the 2010 <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Defenders_(2010_TV_series)" title="The Defenders (2010 TV series)">The Defenders</a></i> and acted in the highly successful film, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jumping_the_Broom_(film)" title="Jumping the Broom (film)">Jumping the Broom</a></i>, released in May 2011. In 2011, he also starred in TV commercials for McDonald's &amp; <a href="/wiki/ICDC_College" title="ICDC College">ICDC Colleges</a>. In 2012 Miller starred in Tyler Perry's film "Madea's Witness Protection". He played as Jake, a young man who is trying to get his money back from a ponzi scheme for his father's church and his retirement. In 2012, he participated in <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a>'s dating game show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Choice_(TV_series)" title="The Choice (TV series)">The Choice</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> He was a special guest star on the <a href="/wiki/Nick_News_with_Linda_Ellerbee" title="Nick News with Linda Ellerbee">Nick News with Linda Ellerbee</a> episode <i>If Only I Were An Only Child: The Top 10 Annoying Things My Siblings Do</i> which aired on <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> on May 13, 2013, on which Romeo (among others) talked about annoying things his siblings did, one of which was his brother took his shoes that Romeo was planning to wear, so he could show them to a girl he liked.</p>
<p>In 2013-2014, Romeo Miller appeared in advertisements for ICDC College, an online school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>

<p>Romeo was invited, in the summer of 2006, to the Reebok-sponsored ABCD Camp, which is considered the premier basketball camp in the United States. The <a href="/wiki/Teaneck,_New_Jersey" title="Teaneck, New Jersey">Teaneck, New Jersey</a>-based ABCD Camp is an invitation-only basketball showcase previously attended by high-profile players including <a href="/wiki/Kobe_Bryant" title="Kobe Bryant">Kobe Bryant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephon_Marbury" title="Stephon Marbury">Stephon Marbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tracy_McGrady" title="Tracy McGrady">Tracy McGrady</a>, <a href="/wiki/LeBron_James" title="LeBron James">LeBron James</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carmelo_Anthony" title="Carmelo Anthony">Carmelo Anthony</a>. Surrounded by future NBA players such as <a href="/wiki/O._J._Mayo" title="O. J. Mayo">O. J. Mayo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derrick_Rose" title="Derrick Rose">Derrick Rose</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Love" title="Kevin Love">Kevin Love</a>, Miller averaged less than 2 points a game throughout the camp. In the March 8th issue of the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i>, Sonny Vaccaro, the longtime director of the ABCD Camp, explained that "he invited Romeo Miller to the 2006 camp, primarily as a favor to Percy Miller, whom he knew from the club basketball circuit." In the article, Vaccaro indicated, "If you're looking for the profile of an athlete who plays basketball at USC, he's not it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wsj_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 13, 2007, Miller verbally committed to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a> (USC) and signed a letter of intent on November 19, 2007.</p>
<p>Miller, a 5'11" <a href="/wiki/Point_guard" title="Point guard">point guard</a>, played one year at Windward High School before he transferred and became a three-year starter at <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_High_School" title="Beverly Hills High School">Beverly Hills High School</a>. As a junior, during the 2005â2006 high school season, he averaged 13.9 points and 5.6 assists per game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USC-Trojans_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-USC-Trojans-61">[61]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frost_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-Frost-62">[62]</a></sup> As a senior, during the 2006â2007 high school season, he averaged 8.6 points and 9.0 assists per game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wsj_60-1"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USC-Trojans_61-1"><a href="#cite_note-USC-Trojans-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to <i>The Wall Street Journal</i>, it appears the decision to grant Miller a full scholarship at USC was largely driven by his relationship with friend and teammate <a href="/wiki/DeMar_DeRozan" title="DeMar DeRozan">DeMar DeRozan</a>, the 6-foot-6 All-American forward who was rated as the number five prospect in the country on Scout.com. <i>The Wall Street Journal</i> reported Coach <a href="/wiki/Tim_Floyd" title="Tim Floyd">Tim Floyd</a> as saying, "Last April...Percy Miller called while driving both players from a tournament in Fayetteville, Ark...Percy Miller said 'Demar and Romeo are ready to make their decision, and would you like to have them both on scholarship?'...'I said absolutely.'"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wsj_60-2"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-60">[60]</a></sup> Miller and DeRozan began playing in the 2008â2009 season.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dancing_with_the_Stars"><i>Dancing with the Stars</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Romeo_Miller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Dancing with the Stars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After originally withdrawing from <a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._season_2)" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 2)">season two</a> of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(US_TV_series)" title="Dancing with the Stars (US TV series)">Dancing with the Stars</a></i> due to an injury,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Romeo competed in <a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._season_12)" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 12)">season twelve</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> He was partnered with professional dancer, <a href="/wiki/Chelsie_Hightower" title="Chelsie Hightower">Chelsie Hightower</a>. They were eliminated from the show on May 10, 2011 and finished in the Top 5, one week before the semi-final.</p>
