<p><b>Georges Mouton</b>, <a href="/wiki/Count" title="Count">comte</a> <b>de <a href="/wiki/Lobau" title="Lobau">Lobau</a></b> (21 February 1770 – 27 November 1838) was a French soldier and political figure who rose to the rank of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marshal_of_France" title="Marshal of France">Marshal of France</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georges_Mouton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Phalsbourg" title="Phalsbourg">Phalsbourg</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lorraine_(r%C3%A9gion)" title="Lorraine (région)">Lorraine</a>, he enlisted in the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Army" title="French Revolutionary Army">French Revolutionary Army</a> in 1792. Serving in the early campaigns of the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars" title="French Revolutionary Wars">French Revolutionary Wars</a>, he by 1800 he was promoted to the rank of <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">colonel</a>.</p>
<p>Promoted to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brigadier_General" title="Brigadier General">général de brigade</a></i> in 1805, after the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/First_French_Empire" title="First French Empire">French Empire</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9n%C3%A9ral" title="Général">général de division</a></i> in 1807, Lobau distinguished himself in the battles of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Jena" title="Battle of Jena">Jena</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aspern" title="Battle of Aspern">Aspern-Essling</a>. In 1810, he was created <a href="/wiki/Count" title="Count">count</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lobau" title="Lobau">Lobau</a> in recognition of his role in the battle of Aspern.</p>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon%27s_Invasion_of_Russia" title="Napoleon's Invasion of Russia">Russian Campaign</a>, he acted as a senior <i><a href="/wiki/Aide-de-camp" title="Aide-de-camp">aide-de-camp</a></i> to Emperor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon I of France</a>. He then served with distinction during the 1813 campaign, seeing action at the Battles of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_L%C3%BCtzen_(1813)" title="Battle of Lützen (1813)">Lützen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bautzen" title="Battle of Bautzen">Bautzen</a>.</p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Dominique_Vandamme" title="Dominique Vandamme">Dominique Vandamme</a> was made prisoner during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kulm" title="Battle of Kulm">battle of Kulm</a>, Lobau commanded the retreat of the remnants of the corps. He served under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laurent,_Marquis_de_Gouvion_Saint-Cyr" title="Laurent, Marquis de Gouvion Saint-Cyr">Laurent Gouvion Saint-Cyr</a> when, upon the retreat after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leipzig" title="Battle of Leipzig">battle of Leipzig</a>, the latter was trapped in <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a> and after the surrender of these forces he became a prisoner of the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Empire" title="Austrian Empire">Austrian Empire</a> for the rest of the war.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Days" title="Hundred Days">Hundred Days</a>, Lobau rallied to Napoleon and was made commander of the VI Infantry Corps which he led in the battles of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ligny" title="Battle of Ligny">Ligny</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Waterloo" title="Battle of Waterloo">Waterloo</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Waterloo" title="Battle of Waterloo">Battle of Waterloo</a> he distinguished himself in the defense of <a href="/wiki/Plancenoit" title="Plancenoit">Plancenoit</a> against the Prussians.</p>
<p>After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Restoration" title="Second Restoration">Second Restoration</a>, Lobau was forced to go into exile until he was allowed to return to France in 1818. He was elected to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_National_Assembly" title="French National Assembly">House of Representatives</a> from 1828 to 1830 as a liberal, and, in 1830, he joined the <a href="/wiki/July_Revolution" title="July Revolution">July Revolution</a> as commander of the National Guard.</p>
<p>As a reward for his services to <a href="/wiki/List_of_French_monarchs" title="List of French monarchs">King</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis-Philippe_of_France" title="Louis-Philippe of France">Louis-Philippe</a> he was made a Marshal in 1831, the same year he was made a <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_France" title="Peerage of France">Peer of France</a>. In 1832 and 1834, Lobau was assigned to suppress insurrections, a task in which he was successful.</p>
<p>George Mouton, comte de Lobau died in Paris.</p>
