<p><b>Lobamba</b> is the traditional, spiritual, and legislative <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> city of <a href="/wiki/Swaziland" title="Swaziland">Swaziland</a>, seat of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Swaziland" title="Parliament of Swaziland">Parliament</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPA_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CPA-6">[6]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Ludzidzini_Royal_Village" title="Ludzidzini Royal Village">residence</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ntombi_of_Swaziland" title="Ntombi of Swaziland">Ntombi</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ndlovukati" title="Ndlovukati">Queen Mother</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-EB-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mswati_III" title="Mswati III">Mswati III</a> lives about 10 kilometres (6.2 mi) away at the Lozitha Palace. The King and Queen Mother participate in annual December and January <a href="/wiki/Incwala" title="Incwala">Incwala</a> ceremonies and August and September <a href="/wiki/Umhlanga_(ceremony)" title="Umhlanga (ceremony)">Reed Dancees</a> at the Royal <a href="/wiki/Kraal" title="Kraal">Kraal</a>.</p>
<p>Key attractions are the Parliament, National Museum of Swaziland, <a href="/wiki/Mlilwane_Wildlife_Sanctuary" title="Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary">Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary</a>, and the King Sobhuza II Memorial Park. The Embo State Palace, not open to visitors, was built by the British government for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polygamous" title="Polygamous">polygamous</a> King <a href="/wiki/Sobhuza_II" title="Sobhuza II">Sobhuza II</a>, whose family included 600 children. He led the movement for Swaziland's independence from the United Kingdom and was its first prime minister.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is located in the western part of the country<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haggett_p._2477_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Haggett_p._2477-9">[9]</a></sup> in the woodland "Valley of Heaven", or <a href="/wiki/Ezulwini_Valley" title="Ezulwini Valley">Ezulwini Valley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Armstrong_p._582_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Armstrong_p._582-10">[10]</a></sup> It is 16 kilometres (9.9 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Mbabane" title="Mbabane">Mbabane</a>, in the district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hhohho" title="Hhohho">Hhohho</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-EB-7">[7]</a></sup> and has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropical_climate" title="Subtropical climate">subtropical climate</a> with a summer rainy season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haggett_p._2477_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Haggett_p._2477-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1997 its population was 3,625.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-EB-7">[7]</a></sup> Its population in 2006 was 11,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Across the country, 84.3% of its people are <a href="/wiki/Swazi_people" title="Swazi people">Swazi</a> and 9.9% are <a href="/wiki/Zulu_people" title="Zulu people">Zulu</a>. The remainder are <a href="/wiki/Tsonga_people" title="Tsonga people">Tsonga</a> (2.5%), <a href="/wiki/Indian_people" title="Indian people">Indian</a> (1.6%) and others (1.7%). Its official languages are <a href="/wiki/Swazi_language" title="Swazi language">Swazi</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haggett_p._2477_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Haggett_p._2477-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two areas have been called Lobamba, the first now called "Old Lobamba" was established in 1750 in southern Swaziland. The subject of this article is a settlement that was created by <a href="/wiki/Sobhuza_II" title="Sobhuza II">Sobhuza II</a> in the northwest section of the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[nb 1]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:147px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sobhuza.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="219" data-file-width="145" height="219" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5a/Sobhuza.jpg" width="145"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sobhuza.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Sobhuza_II" title="Sobhuza II">Sobhuza II</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1903, following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boer_Wars" title="Boer Wars">Boer Wars</a>, the British government took control of Swaziland and it was then ruled by a <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regent</a>. In 1921 King <a href="/wiki/Sobhuza_II" title="Sobhuza II">Sobhuza II</a> became leader of Swaziland, which was still under the British government's control.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[16]</a></sup> Swaziland became independent of the British government on September 6, 1968, which was announced at a cattle <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byre" title="Byre">byre</a> in Lobamba by <a href="/wiki/Makhosini_Dlamini" title="Makhosini Dlamini">Prince Makhosini</a>. He was the country's first prime minister and the great-grandson of <a href="/wiki/Sobhuza_I" title="Sobhuza I">Sobhuza I</a>. With its independence, Swaziland was a member in its own right of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organization_of_African_Unity" title="Organization of African Unity">Organization of African Unity</a> (OAU), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Commonwealth" title="British Commonwealth">British Commonwealth</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Akyeampong_p._233_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Akyeampong_p._233-18">[17]</a></sup> It was made a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy" title="Constitutional monarchy">constitutional monarchy</a> under Sobhuza II,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Twentieth_Century_Africa_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Twentieth_Century_Africa-19">[18]</a></sup> who lived in the royal residence, or <a href="/wiki/Kraal" title="Kraal">kraal</a>, in Lobamba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Twentieth_Century_Africa_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Twentieth_Century_Africa-19">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Lobamba is the legislative seat of the <a href="/wiki/Swaziland" title="Swaziland">Swaziland</a> government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPA_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-CPA-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parliament_of_Swaziland">Parliament of Swaziland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Parliament of Swaziland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Swaziland" title="Parliament of Swaziland">Parliament of Swaziland</a></div>
<p>It was made a branch of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Parliamentary_Association" title="Commonwealth Parliamentary Association">Commonwealth Parliamentary Association</a> on January 1, 1965 and its date of independence was January 1, 1968. The constitution was signed by King <a href="/wiki/Mswati_III" title="Mswati III">Mswati III</a> on July 26, 2005. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Kings_of_Swaziland" title="List of Kings of Swaziland">king</a> appoints the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_heads_of_government_of_Swaziland" title="List of heads of government of Swaziland">prime minister</a> and the council. There are two chambers: the Senate and the House of Assembly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPA_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-CPA-6">[6]</a></sup> The parliament building is sometimes open to visitors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Murphy_p._592_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Murphy_p._592-20">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Embo_State_Palace">Embo State Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Embo State Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The royal Embo State Palace was built by the British to house the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polygamous" title="Polygamous">polygamous</a> <a href="/wiki/Sobhuza_II" title="Sobhuza II">Sobhuza II</a> and his family, including 600 children. It is not open to visitors and photographs are not allowed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Armstrong_p._582_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Armstrong_p._582-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Murphy_p._592_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-Murphy_p._592-20">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Royal_residences">Royal residences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Royal residences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>King <a href="/wiki/Mswati_III" title="Mswati III">Mswati III</a> lives at the Lozitha Palace, about 10 kilometres (6.2 mi) from the city. He visits the Royal Kraal, or Ludzidzini Royal Residence, during the <a href="/wiki/Umhlanga_(ceremony)" title="Umhlanga (ceremony)">Umhlanga dance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Incwala" title="Incwala">Incwala</a> ceremonies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Murphy_p._592_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-Murphy_p._592-20">[19]</a></sup> The royal village includes the queen mother's Royal Kraal, dwelling clusters, and a parade ground for ceremonies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Attractions_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Attractions-21">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure">Infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_enforcement">Law enforcement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Law enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Lobamba has a police station and is served by The Royal Swaziland Police Service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup> During the British colonial era <a href="/wiki/William_Palmer,_2nd_Earl_of_Selborne" title="William Palmer, 2nd Earl of Selborne">Lord Selborn</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_High_Commissioners_of_the_United_Kingdom_to_South_Africa" title="List of High Commissioners of the United Kingdom to South Africa">High Commissioner for South Africa</a> signed a proclamation in 1907 for what became the Swaziland Police Force. After Swaziland's independence in 1968, the force was renamed the Royal Swaziland Police Force. Its name was further changed making it a service, rather than a force.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Education is free, but is not required. It had low literacy rates, but they are rising.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haggett_p._2477_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-Haggett_p._2477-9">[9]</a></sup> Lobamba National High School is in Lobamba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_and_welfare">Health and welfare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Health and welfare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The government provides health facilities to manage <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endemic_disease" title="Endemic disease">endemic disease</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malnutrition" title="Malnutrition">malnutrition</a>. Retirement, disability and survivor pensions are available through its welfare system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haggett_p._2477_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-Haggett_p._2477-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many of the roads in Swaziland are unsurfaced, but there are good roads that connect principal towns,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haggett_p._2477_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-Haggett_p._2477-9">[9]</a></sup> including the <a href="/wiki/MR3_road" title="MR3 road">MR3</a> highway and MR103 road.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Armstrong_p._582_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Armstrong_p._582-10">[10]</a></sup> There are small local airstrips and a railroad that operates between Swaziland and Mozambique.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haggett_p._2477_9-6"><a href="#cite_note-Haggett_p._2477-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Matsapha_Airport" title="Matsapha Airport">Matsapha Airport</a> is 23 kilometres (14 mi) from Lobamba. The next closest domestic and international airport is <a href="/wiki/Maputo_International_Airport" title="Maputo International Airport">Maputo International Airport</a> in Mozambique, which is 216 kilometres (134 mi) away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>One of the tour operators in Swaziland is Swazi Trails, which has tours of the Lobamba royal village, nature reserves, game parks, and craft centres. Nabo Bashoa runs minibus tours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[25]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Museum_of_Swaziland">National Museum of Swaziland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: National Museum of Swaziland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The National Museum of Swaziland, located in Lobamba next to the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Swaziland" title="Parliament of Swaziland">Parliament building</a>, was built in 1972 and expanded in 1986 and 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAL_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-SAL-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SNTC_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-SNTC-27">[26]</a></sup> The museum was made a non-profit institution in 1974 by the <a href="/wiki/International_Council_of_Museums" title="International Council of Museums">International Council of Museums</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SNTC_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-SNTC-27">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The museum houses a memorial to the revered King <a href="/wiki/Sobhuza_II" title="Sobhuza II">Sobhuza II</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Murphy_p._592_20-3"><a href="#cite_note-Murphy_p._592-20">[19]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Swazi_people" title="Swazi people">Swazi</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South African</a> artifacts. It has a collection of photographs that include subjects of the <a href="/wiki/Mbabane" title="Mbabane">Mbabane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manzini_Region" title="Manzini Region">Manzini Regions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scramble_for_Africa" title="Scramble for Africa">British colonial</a> administrators. A 16th-century head of <a href="/wiki/Krishna" title="Krishna">Krishna</a>, discovered nearby, is located in the <a href="/wiki/Natural_history" title="Natural history">natural history</a> wing and provides evidence of trade with the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_world" title="Eastern world">east</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAL_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-SAL-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SNTC_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-SNTC-27">[26]</a></sup> The natural history wing includes <a href="/wiki/Highveld" title="Highveld">highveld</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lowveld" title="Lowveld">lowveld</a> <a href="/wiki/Diorama" title="Diorama">dioramas</a> to illustrate the diverse Swaziland ecosystems and feature rarely seen nocturnal animals. Its nature-centric exhibits integrate environmental and cultural impacts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Attractions_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Attractions-21">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>A recreation of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homestead_(small_African_settlement)" title="Homestead (small African settlement)">Swazi homestead</a> is located outside the museum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAL_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-SAL-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Somhlolo_stadium">Somhlolo stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Somhlolo stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Also located near the Parliament building is the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Somhlolo_stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Somhlolo stadium (page does not exist)">Somhlolo stadium</a> for football and other major events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAL_14-4"><a href="#cite_note-SAL-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mlilwane_Wildlife_Sanctuary">Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mlilwane_Wildlife_Sanctuary" title="Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary">Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary</a></div>
<p>Just outside Lobamba is the Mlilwane Wildlife Sanctuary that has horseback and hiking trails, guided mountain-bike tours, rustic trail camps and camping in caves. Throughout the park are opportunities to observe game,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAL_14-5"><a href="#cite_note-SAL-14">[14]</a></sup> including <a href="/wiki/Antelope" title="Antelope">antelope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giraffe" title="Giraffe">giraffe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zebra" title="Zebra">zebras</a>, and many types of birds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="King_Sobhuza_II_Memorial_Park">King Sobhuza II Memorial Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: King Sobhuza II Memorial Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A memorial park was established in the memory of King <a href="/wiki/Sobhuza_II" title="Sobhuza II">Sobhuza II</a>, who was the leader of the country's independence in 1968. The king's life is told through an exhibit of photographs. Three of the king's vintage cars are in the museum and his mausoleum is within the park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Attractions_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-Attractions-21">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Malkern_Valley">Malkern Valley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Malkern Valley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Malkerns Valley is an arts and crafts center located 7 kilometres (4.3 mi) south of Lobamba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ceremonies">Ceremonies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lobamba&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Ceremonies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lobamba is famous for two ceremonies that are held there: the <a href="/wiki/Umhlanga_(ceremony)" title="Umhlanga (ceremony)">Reed Dance</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-EB-7">[7]</a></sup> celebrated in August and September in honour of the Queen Mother, and the <a href="/wiki/Incwala" title="Incwala">Incwala</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-EB-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[29]</a></sup> in December and January in honour of the King. These ceremonies include dancing, singing, and celebrations with traditional attire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[31]</a></sup></p>

<li id="cite_note-16"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-16">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">In the mid 18th century ancestors of <a href="/wiki/Sobhuza_I" title="Sobhuza I">Sobhuza I</a> came to (old) Lobamba from the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> About 1750 Swazi was founded in (old) Lobamba by <a href="/wiki/House_of_Dlamini" title="House of Dlamini">Dlamini dynasty</a> chief <a href="/wiki/Ngwane_III" title="Ngwane III">Ngwane III</a>, according to local tradition. His son, <a href="/wiki/Ndvungunye" title="Ndvungunye">Ndvungunye</a>, died in 1815 and <a href="/wiki/Sobhuza_I" title="Sobhuza I">Sobhuza I</a> became the king and founder of modern Swaziland, ruling from his <a href="/wiki/Shiselweni_Region" title="Shiselweni Region">Shiselweni</a> royal residence beginning in 1818. The city of (old) Lobamba was built in 1820 as headquarters for Sobhuza I, who died in 1838.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAL_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-SAL-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Akyeampong_p._424_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Akyeampong_p._424-15">[15]</a></sup></span></li>
