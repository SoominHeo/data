<p><b>Francois Robert "Rob" de Castella</b> <span style="font-size:100%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Officer_of_the_Order_of_Australia" title="Officer of the Order of Australia">AO</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Member of the Order of the British Empire">MBE</a></span> (born 27 February 1957) is an Australian former world champion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marathon_(sport)" title="Marathon (sport)">marathon</a> runner.</p>
<p>De Castella is widely known as "<b>Deek</b>" or "<b>Deeks</b>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup> to the Australian public, and "Tree" to his competitors due to his thick legs and inner calm. He holds the <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceanian</a> record for the marathon.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>De Castella is of Swiss-French descent. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a>, the eldest of seven children. Sport was a way of life in his family – his father Rolet ran marathons in his fifties. His mother Anne was a state-level tennis player. His brother Nicholas, took part in four World Cross Country Championships, whereas brother Anthony competed in running at club level for more than 25 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Rob de Castella was an outstanding track athlete as a teenager and trained under <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pat_Clohessy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pat Clohessy (page does not exist)">Pat Clohessy</a> from the age of 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marathon_career">Marathon career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marathon career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1981_Fukuoka_Marathon">1981 Fukuoka Marathon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1981 Fukuoka Marathon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>De Castella first came to international attention when he won the 1981 Fukuoka Marathon in a time of 2:08:18, which was the fastest time recorded for an out-and-back course. It was not initially known to be a world best time, because his time was 5 seconds slower than the time set by <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Salazar" title="Alberto Salazar">Alberto Salazar</a> in the 1981 <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Marathon" title="New York City Marathon">New York City Marathon</a>. It later emerged that the New York course was about 148 metres short. De Castella's time was later ratified as the world record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1982_Commonwealth_Games_Marathon">1982 Commonwealth Games Marathon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1982 Commonwealth Games Marathon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>De Castella was the favourite to win the marathon at the <a href="/wiki/1982_Commonwealth_Games" title="1982 Commonwealth Games">1982 Commonwealth Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a>. At the start of the race, <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzanians</a> <a href="/wiki/Gidamis_Shahanga" title="Gidamis Shahanga">Gidamis Shahanga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juma_Ikangaa" title="Juma Ikangaa">Juma Ikangaa</a> raced to the lead and were 50 m ahead of the pack after five minutes. After the 20 km mark, this gap had widened to several hundred meters. By the 23 km mark, Ikangaa had taken the lead from his compatriot, with de Castella in the main pack some 250 m behind, but closing. de Castella passed the now tiring Shahanga at the 38 km mark, and in the next kilometre drew level with Ikangaa then took the lead. However Ikangaa was not done yet, and re-took the lead slightly. The duel continued for the remainder of the race. Eventually de Castella pulled away, and won by 80 m in a time of 2:09:18, 12 seconds ahead of Ikangaa. Briton <a href="/wiki/Mike_Gratton" title="Mike Gratton">Mike Gratton</a> finished third in 2:12:06, and Shahanga faded to finish 6th. The race finished on the streets of Brisbane, not in the main stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wilson_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wilson-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1983_IAAF_World_Championships">1983 IAAF World Championships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1983 IAAF World Championships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>De Castella won Australia's first <a href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="IAAF World Championships in Athletics">athletic World Championships</a> gold medal when he won the marathon in <a href="/wiki/1983_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1983 World Championships in Athletics">1983</a>, beating Ethiopian <a href="/wiki/Kebede_Balcha" title="Kebede Balcha">Kebede Balcha</a> by 24 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wolfgang_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wolfgang-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1983_Rotterdam_Marathon">1983 Rotterdam Marathon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1983 Rotterdam Marathon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>De Castella won the 1983 Rotterdam Marathon won in 2:08:37, defeating a deep field that included the previously unbeaten Alberto Salazar and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Lopes" title="Carlos Lopes">Carlos Lopes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup> The race was also televised live back to Australia.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> De Castella's time was, at that point, the fourth fastest in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1984_Olympic_Games">1984 Olympic Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1984 Olympic Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>De Castella was the favourite for the <a href="/wiki/1984_Summer_Olympics" title="1984 Summer Olympics">1984 Summer Olympics</a> marathon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup> He ran comfortably in the main group until about 33 kilometres, when he slowed down to drink water. At the same time, the leading runners picked up speed and de Castella suddenly found himself out of contention. He accelerated during the final kilometres to finish fifth.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1986_Commonwealth_Games">1986 Commonwealth Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 1986 Commonwealth Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>De Castella defended his Commonwealth Games title in <a href="/wiki/1986_Commonwealth_Games" title="1986 Commonwealth Games">Edinburgh</a>, winning in 2:10:15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-athletics.com.au_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-athletics.com.au-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cross_country_running">Cross country running</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cross country running">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to marathon running, de Castella was also an accomplished <a href="/wiki/Cross_country_running" title="Cross country running">cross country runner</a>. He won the Australian national title once as a junior and four times as a senior, along with five other podium finishes. He competed eight times at the <a href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Cross_Country_Championships" title="IAAF World Cross Country Championships">World Cross Country Championships</a> from 1977 to 1986, finishing in the top 20 five times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-athletics.com.au_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-athletics.com.au-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career_and_life">Later career and life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Later career and life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>De Castella failed to finish the marathon at the 1987 World Athletics Championships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-athletics.com.au_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-athletics.com.au-7">[7]</a></sup> and never again placed very well in a major marathon. He represented Australia at the 1988 Seoul and 1992 Barcelona Olympics, competing in four straight Olympic Games. He finished in the top ten in three Olympics, but never won a medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He retired from the sport in 1993 and lives in <a href="/wiki/Canberra" title="Canberra">Canberra</a>, together with his wife Theresa and four children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-img_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-img-8">[8]</a></sup> Previously he was married to the former champion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triathlete" title="Triathlete">triathlete</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gaylene_Clews&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gaylene Clews (page does not exist)">Gaylene Clews</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOTY1983_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-AOTY1983-9">[9]</a></sup> He lost his family home, along with many of his medals and other citations, in the <a href="/wiki/2003_Canberra_bushfires" title="2003 Canberra bushfires">2003 Canberra bushfires</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Choosing to move rather than rebuild, he now lives in a nearby suburb. He later helped with the taskforce established for the reconstruction of areas worst affected by the fires.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-img_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-img-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1990 to 1995, de Castella was the director of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Institute_of_Sport" title="Australian Institute of Sport">Australian Institute of Sport</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOTY1983_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-AOTY1983-9">[9]</a></sup> and subsequently became executive director of <i>Focus On You</i>, a company focusing on corporate and community health and fitness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOTY1983_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-AOTY1983-9">[9]</a></sup> He has also been actively involved in other organisations dedicated to encouraging healthy living for both children and adults.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AIS_Alumni_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-AIS_Alumni-11">[11]</a></sup> He also fronts the Indigenous Marathon Project. In 2014 he was made an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Officer_of_the_Order_of_Australia" title="Officer of the Order of Australia">Officer of the Order of Australia</a> at the Australia Day honours.</p>
<p>In 2003, de Castella launched "deeks", a specialist chain of grain and gluten free bakeries &amp; cafés.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2004, he earned a black belt in the traditional Okinawan <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goju_Ryu" title="Goju Ryu">Goju Ryu</a>; he has also been part of a long-running advertising campaign for the multi-vitamin tablet "Centrum".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-img_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-img-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><br/></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>1977 – won the Sydney <a href="/wiki/City2Surf_(Sydney)" title="City2Surf (Sydney)">City to Surf</a> in 41' 12"</li>
<li>1979 – won the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Victorian_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Victorian Championship (page does not exist)">Victorian Championship</a> in 2h 14'44" and the Australian marathon title in 2h 13'23"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>1980 – 10th in the <a href="/wiki/1980_Summer_Olympics" title="1980 Summer Olympics">1980 Summer Olympics</a> marathon in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, 2h 14'31"; 8th at the <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Marathon" title="Fukuoka Marathon">Fukuoka Marathon</a> in 2h 10'44"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>1981 – won the <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Marathon" title="Fukuoka Marathon">Fukuoka Marathon</a> in 2h 08'18" (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marathon_world_best_progression" title="Marathon world best progression">world record</a> from 1981 to 1984)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>1981 – won and set fastest time of 40'08" in City to Surf, Sydney. (this time bettered in 1991)</li>
<li>1982 – won the <a href="/wiki/1982_Commonwealth_Games" title="1982 Commonwealth Games">1982 Commonwealth Games</a> marathon in <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a> in 2h 09'18"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>1983 – won the <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam_Marathon" title="Rotterdam Marathon">Rotterdam Marathon</a> in 2h 08'37"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-10"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>1983 – won the <a href="/wiki/1983_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1983 World Championships in Athletics">1983 World Championships in Athletics</a> marathon in <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a> in 2h 10'03"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-11"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>1984 – 5th in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1984_Summer_Olympics_-_Men%27s_Marathon" title="Athletics at the 1984 Summer Olympics - Men's Marathon">Olympic Marathon</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>1984 – 3rd in the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Marathon" title="Chicago Marathon">Chicago Marathon</a> in 2h 09'09"</li>
<li>1985 – 3rd in the Chicago Marathon in 2h 08'48"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-12"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>1986 – won the <a href="/wiki/1986_Commonwealth_Games" title="1986 Commonwealth Games">1986 Commonwealth Games</a> marathon in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> in 2h 10'15"</li>
<li>1986 – won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston_marathon" title="Boston marathon">Boston marathon</a> in 2h 07'51" (personal best<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-13"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup>)</li>
<li>1987 – won the <a href="/wiki/Great_North_Run" title="Great North Run">Great North Run</a> in 1h 02'04"</li>
<li>1988 – 4th in the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_International_Marathon" title="Tokyo International Marathon">Tokyo International Marathon</a> in 2h 08'49"</li>
<li>1988 – 8th in the <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Summer Olympics</a> marathon in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b1_3-14"><a href="#cite_note-b1-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>1990 – 13th in the <a href="/wiki/1990_Commonwealth_Games" title="1990 Commonwealth Games">1990 Commonwealth Games</a> marathon<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-athletics.com.au_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-athletics.com.au-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>1991 – won the Rotterdam Marathon in 2h 09'42"</li>
<li>1992 – finished 26th in Olympic Marathon, Barcelona<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_de_Castella&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of May 2009, de Castella still held the following records:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-athletics.com.au_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-athletics.com.au-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Australian U20 10,000 m – 28'54"4 on 16 December 1976 in Melbourne</li>
<li>Australian 20 km Track – 58.37.2 on 17 Apr 1982 in Rome</li>
<li>Australian 1 hr – 20,516 m on 17 April 1982 in Rome</li>
<li>Australian All Comers Marathon – 2h 09'18" in Brisbane Commonwealth Games 1982</li>
<li>Australian Marathon – 2h 07'51" at the 1986 Boston Marathon</li>
</ul>
