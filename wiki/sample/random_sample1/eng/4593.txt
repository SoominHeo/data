<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Robert Reid (co-driver)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Robert Reid</b> (born 17 February 1966) is a retired <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Rallying" title="Rallying">rally</a> co-driver who partnered <a href="/wiki/Richard_Burns" title="Richard Burns">Richard Burns</a> to victory in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2001_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="2001 World Rally Championship season">2001 World Rally Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gomotorsport_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gomotorsport-1">[1]</a></sup> The partnership lasted from 1991 until 2003 during which time they entered into 103 world rallies, achieving 277 stage wins and 34 podium finishes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> The pair were runners-up in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1999_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="1999 World Rally Championship season">1999 World Rally Championship</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="2000 World Rally Championship season">2000 World Rally Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gomotorsport_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-gomotorsport-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Reid_(co-driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Years">Early Years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Reid_(co-driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early Years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Reids first stage rally was the 1984 <i>Hackle Rally</i>, a round of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Rally_Championship" title="Scottish Rally Championship">Scottish Rally Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rally_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-rally-3">[3]</a></sup> For seven years he competed in many different rallies around the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> navigating for many notable drivers including Robbie Head, <a href="/wiki/Colin_McRae" title="Colin McRae">Colin McRae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alister_McRae" title="Alister McRae">Alister McRae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rally_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-rally-3">[3]</a></sup> However, he is most well known for his long and successful partnership with Richard Burns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gomotorsport_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-gomotorsport-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Burns_and_Reid">Burns and Reid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Reid_(co-driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Burns and Reid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1991 Burns and Reid formed a new partnership and placed 16th in the British round of the <a href="/wiki/World_Rally_Championship" title="World Rally Championship">World Rally Championship</a> that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> The following year they finished in 7th place and in 1993 they won the <a href="/wiki/British_Rally_Championship" title="British Rally Championship">British Rally Championship</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Subaru" title="Subaru">Subaru</a> making Burns the youngest ever British champion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1994 and 1995 Burns and Reid competed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Pacific_Rally_Championship" title="Asia Pacific Rally Championship">Asia Pacific Rally Championship</a> and occasionally rounds of the World Rally Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> Their best result was third in the 1995 British round of the World Rally Championship driving a Subaru.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1996-1998_Mitsubishi">1996-1998 Mitsubishi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Reid_(co-driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1996-1998 Mitsubishi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between 1996 and 1998 Burns and Reid drove for <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi" title="Mitsubishi">Mitsubishi</a> with their results improving every year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1996 they placed fifth in the Australian round and fourth in the Argentinian round of the World Rally Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> The following year they finished fourth in the British, Australian, Indonesian, New Zealand and Greek rounds of the Championship as well as placing second in the Kenyan round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> Even further progress was made in 1998 with their first ever victory in the Kenyan round of the World Rally Championship and a first at the British round of the Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> They placed sixth overall in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1998_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="1998 World Rally Championship season">1998 World Rally Championship season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1999-2001_Subaru">1999-2001 Subaru</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Reid_(co-driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1999-2001 Subaru">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1999 Burns and Reid switched manufacturer and drove for Subaru.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> They won the Greek, Australian and British rounds of the Championship and finished second in the Argentinian, Finnish and Chinese rounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> Overall they finished runners-up in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1999_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="1999 World Rally Championship season">1999 World Rally Championship season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-12"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following year Burns and Reid would finish runners-up a second time even though they won four rounds of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="2000 World Rally Championship season">2000 World Rally Championship season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-13"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> They finished first in the Kenyan, Portuguese, Argentinian and British rounds but <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marcus_Gronholm" title="Marcus Gronholm">Marcus Gronholm</a> still managed to beat them at the end of the season by five points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-14"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2001_World_Rally_Champions">2001 World Rally Champions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Reid_(co-driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2001 World Rally Champions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2001 Burns and Reid would win their first and only <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2001_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="2001 World Rally Championship season">World Rally Championship</a> title finishing two points ahead of Colin McRae.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-15"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> They won the New Zealand round of the Championship and placed second at the Argentinian, Cypriot, Finnish and Australian rounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-16"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> Burns became the first English driver to become world champion.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002-2003_Peugeot">2002-2003 Peugeot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Reid_(co-driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2002-2003 Peugeot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The move to Peugeot did not prove to be as successful, though they did manage four second place finishes in the 2002 season at the Spanish, Cypriot, Finnish and German rounds of the Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-17"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> Marcus Gronholm won the title by thirty points from his nearest rival. Burns and Reid finished in fifth place overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-18"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Burns and Reid managed two second place and five third place positions in the 2003 Championship, giving them an outside chance of re-claiming the world title going into the <a href="/wiki/Wales_Rally_GB" title="Wales Rally GB">Wales Rally GB</a>, the final rally of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2003_World_Rally_Championship_season" title="2003 World Rally Championship season">2003 World Rally Championship season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rallyeinfo_2-19"><a href="#cite_note-rallyeinfo-2">[2]</a></sup> Whilst driving to the final event Richard Burns suffered a blackout at the wheel and was withdrawn from the event. Burns was diagnosed with a malignant brain tumour. Throughout Burns's illness, Reid spent two to three days per week with him and did not want to race with any other driver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-richardburns_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-richardburns-4">[4]</a></sup> On 25 November 2005 four years to the day since Burns and Reid had won the World Championship title, Richard Burns died.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Racing_Career">Post-Racing Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Reid_(co-driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-Racing Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since retiring from racing Reid has held several positions within the sport including colour commentator for WRC. He spent several years as the Performance Director of the UK Motor Sport Association establishing elite programmes to develop young driver talent in the United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fia_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-fia-5">[5]</a></sup> More recently he has been working alongside <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> to select and train emerging talent at the <a href="/wiki/FIA_Institute_Young_Driver_Excellence_Academy" title="FIA Institute Young Driver Excellence Academy">FIA Institute Young Driver Excellence Academy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fia_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-fia-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reid appears in the 2004 sim rally game <i><a href="/wiki/Richard_Burns_Rally" title="Richard Burns Rally">Richard Burns Rally</a></i> as the player's co-driver.</p>
