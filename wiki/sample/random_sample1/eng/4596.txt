<p><b>Robert Bridge Richardson</b> (born August 27, 1955) is an American <a href="/wiki/Cinematographer" title="Cinematographer">cinematographer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">[1]</a></sup> He has won the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Cinematography" title="Academy Award for Best Cinematography">Academy Award for Best Cinematography</a> three times, for his work on <i><a href="/wiki/JFK_(film)" title="JFK (film)">JFK</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Aviator_(2004_film)" title="The Aviator (2004 film)">The Aviator</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AC_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-AC-2">[2]</a></sup> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hugo_(2011_film)" title="Hugo (2011 film)">Hugo</a></i>. Richardson is and has been a frequent collaborator for several directors, including <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Stone" title="Oliver Stone">Oliver Stone</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Sayles" title="John Sayles">John Sayles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Errol_Morris" title="Errol Morris">Errol Morris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">Martin Scorsese</a>. He is one of three living persons who won the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Cinematography" title="Academy Award for Best Cinematography">Academy Award for Best Cinematography</a> three times, the others being <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Storaro" title="Vittorio Storaro">Vittorio Storaro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_Lubezki" title="Emmanuel Lubezki">Emmanuel Lubezki</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Richardson_(cinematographer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Richardson was born in <a href="/wiki/Hyannis,_Massachusetts" title="Hyannis, Massachusetts">Hyannis, Massachusetts</a>. He graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_School_of_Design" title="Rhode Island School of Design">Rhode Island School of Design</a> with a BFA in Film/Animation/Video and received his MFA from <a href="/wiki/AFI_Conservatory" title="AFI Conservatory">AFI Conservatory</a>. Richardson worked as a camera operator and 2nd unit photographer on such features as <a href="/wiki/Alex_Cox" title="Alex Cox">Alex Cox</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Repo_Man_(film)" title="Repo Man (film)">Repo Man</a></i>, Dorian Walker's <i><a href="/wiki/Making_the_Grade_(film)" title="Making the Grade (film)">Making the Grade</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Wes_Craven" title="Wes Craven">Wes Craven</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_Nightmare_on_Elm_Street" title="A Nightmare on Elm Street">A Nightmare on Elm Street</a></i> (all in 1984). He also served as <a href="/wiki/Cinematographer" title="Cinematographer">cinematographer</a> on TV documentaries and docudramas such as <i>America, America</i> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Disney_Channel" title="The Disney Channel">The Disney Channel</a>, <i>God's Peace</i> for the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> and <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a>' <i>The Front Line: El Salvador</i>. His work in <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a> led to his meeting <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Stone" title="Oliver Stone">Oliver Stone</a>, who hired him to "shoot" <i><a href="/wiki/Salvador_(film)" title="Salvador (film)">Salvador</a></i> (1986).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Stone" title="Oliver Stone">Oliver Stone</a>'s major motion picture debut was also Richardson's first film as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Director_of_photography" title="Director of photography">director of photography</a>. <i>Salvador</i> was also filmed the same year as Stone's <i><a href="/wiki/Platoon_(film)" title="Platoon (film)">Platoon</a></i>. <i>Platoon</i> would earn Richardson his first Oscar nomination for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Cinematography" title="Academy Award for Best Cinematography">Best Cinematography</a>. In 1987, Richardson reteamed with Stone on <i><a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_(1987_film)" title="Wall Street (1987 film)">Wall Street</a></i>. In 1988, he filmed <i><a href="/wiki/Eight_Men_Out" title="Eight Men Out">Eight Men Out</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/John_Sayles" title="John Sayles">John Sayles</a>. In 1989, he earned his second Best Cinematography Oscar nomination for Stone's <i><a href="/wiki/Born_on_the_Fourth_of_July_(film)" title="Born on the Fourth of July (film)">Born on the Fourth of July</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1991, Richardson won the first of his Best Cinematography Academy Awards for his work on Stone's <i><a href="/wiki/JFK_(film)" title="JFK (film)">JFK</a></i>; he also shot Stone's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Doors_(film)" title="The Doors (film)">The Doors</a></i> that same year. He worked with Sayles again in 1991 for <i><a href="/wiki/City_of_Hope_(film)" title="City of Hope (film)">City of Hope</a></i>. In 1992, he worked as director of photography on <a href="/wiki/Rob_Reiner" title="Rob Reiner">Rob Reiner</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Few_Good_Men_(film)" title="A Few Good Men (film)">A Few Good Men</a></i> and served as a 2nd unit photographer for <a href="/wiki/Haskell_Wexler" title="Haskell Wexler">Haskell Wexler</a> on <i>To the Moon, Alice</i>, a "Showtime 30-Minute Movie" (for which he was also credited as visual consultant). He began a long working relationship with <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">Martin Scorsese</a> in 1995, beginning with <i><a href="/wiki/Casino_(film)" title="Casino (film)">Casino</a></i>. In 1995, he was cinematographer on Stone's <i><a href="/wiki/Nixon_(film)" title="Nixon (film)">Nixon</a></i>. In 1997, Richardson photographed <a href="/wiki/Errol_Morris" title="Errol Morris">Errol Morris</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fast,_Cheap_and_Out_of_Control" title="Fast, Cheap and Out of Control">Fast, Cheap and Out of Control</a></i> as well as filming the majority of Stone's <i><a href="/wiki/U_Turn_(1997_film)" title="U Turn (1997 film)">U Turn</a></i> and serving as director of photography for <a href="/wiki/Barry_Levinson" title="Barry Levinson">Barry Levinson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Wag_the_Dog" title="Wag the Dog">Wag the Dog</a></i>.</p>
<p>Richardson has four children, Kanchan, Maya, Bibi and Madeleine. His family currently runs the Cape Cod Sea Camps situated on the Cape Cod Bay.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
