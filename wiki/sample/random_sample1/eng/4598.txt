<p><b>Robert A. Blust</b> (born 1940, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">白樂思</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Bái Lèsī</span></i>) is a prominent <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">linguist</a> in several areas, including <a href="/wiki/Historical_linguistics" title="Historical linguistics">historical linguistics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lexicography" title="Lexicography">lexicography</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ethnology" title="Ethnology">ethnology</a>. Blust specializes in the <a href="/wiki/Austronesian_languages" title="Austronesian languages">Austronesian languages</a> and has made major contributions to the field of Austronesian linguistics.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Blust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robert Blust was born in <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a>, Ohio and raised in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. He received a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> in <a href="/wiki/Anthropology" title="Anthropology">anthropology</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">PhD</a> in linguistics from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii_at_Manoa" title="University of Hawaii at Manoa">University of Hawai<span class="unicode">ʻ</span>i at Mānoa</a> in 1974. Currently, he is a <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> in the Department of Linguistics at the University of Hawai<span class="unicode">ʻ</span>i at Mānoa, and served as the department chair from 2005 to 2008.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Austronesian_Languages">Austronesian Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Blust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Austronesian Languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He also serves as the review editor for the <i><a href="/wiki/Oceanic_Linguistics" title="Oceanic Linguistics">Oceanic Linguistics</a></i>, an academic journal that covers the <a href="/wiki/Austronesian_languages" title="Austronesian languages">Austronesian languages</a>. Blust is best known for his work on Austronesian, including a large Austronesian comparative dictionary (Blust 1995c) and a <a href="/wiki/Thao_language" title="Thao language">Thao</a>-English dictionary (Blust 2003b). Another one of his well-known works is a 2009 work called <i>The Austronesian Languages</i>, which is the first single-authored book to cover all aspects (<a href="/wiki/Phonology" title="Phonology">phonology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syntax" title="Syntax">syntax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(linguistics)" title="Morphology (linguistics)">morphology</a>, sound changes, classification, etc.) of the Austronesian language family in its entirety.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Field_work">Field work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Blust&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Field work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Blust has done field work on 97 Austronesian languages spoken in locations such as <a href="/wiki/Sarawak" title="Sarawak">Sarawak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>. In Taiwan, he has performed field work on <a href="/wiki/Formosan_languages" title="Formosan languages">Formosan languages</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Thao_language" title="Thao language">Thao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kavalan_language" title="Kavalan language">Kavalan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pazeh_language" title="Pazeh language">Pazeh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amis_language" title="Amis language">Amis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paiwan_language" title="Paiwan language">Paiwan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saisiyat_language" title="Saisiyat language">Saisiyat</a>. His dictionary of the highly endangered Thao language is currently the most complete of any Formosan language dictionary, containing over 1100 pages. Blust also has an abiding interest in both linguistic and cultural aspects of <a href="/wiki/Rainbows_in_mythology" title="Rainbows in mythology">rainbows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragons</a>.</p>
