<p><b>Robert Stevenson</b> (31 March 1905 – 30 April 1986) was an English film writer and director. He was educated at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cambridge_University" title="Cambridge University">Cambridge University</a> where he became the president of both the Liberal Club and the Cambridge Union Society.</p>
<p>After directing a number of British films, including <i><a href="/wiki/King_Solomon%27s_Mines_(1937_film)" title="King Solomon's Mines (1937 film)">King Solomon's Mines</a></i> (1937), he was given a contract by <a href="/wiki/David_O._Selznick" title="David O. Selznick">David O. Selznick</a> and moved to Hollywood in the 1940s. He ended up directing 19 films for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Company" title="Walt Disney Company">Walt Disney Company</a> in the 1960s and 1970s. Stevenson is best remembered for directing the <a href="/wiki/Julie_Andrews" title="Julie Andrews">Julie Andrews</a> musical <i><a href="/wiki/Mary_Poppins_(film)" title="Mary Poppins (film)">Mary Poppins</a></i>, for which Andrews won the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Actress">Academy Award for Best Actress</a> and Stevenson was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Director" title="Academy Award for Best Director">Best Director</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> With Disney, he also directed the first two Herbie films, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Love_Bug" title="The Love Bug">The Love Bug</a></i> (1968) and <i><a href="/wiki/Herbie_Rides_Again" title="Herbie Rides Again">Herbie Rides Again</a></i> (1974), as well as <i><a href="/wiki/Bedknobs_and_Broomsticks" title="Bedknobs and Broomsticks">Bedknobs and Broomsticks</a></i> (1971). Three of his films featured English actor <a href="/wiki/David_Tomlinson" title="David Tomlinson">David Tomlinson</a>.</p>
<p>Stevenson divorced his first wife Cecilie and married English actress <a href="/wiki/Anna_Lee" title="Anna Lee">Anna Lee</a> in 1934. They lived on London's Bankside for five years, moving to Hollywood in 1939, where he remained for many years. They had two daughters, <a href="/wiki/Venetia_Stevenson" title="Venetia Stevenson">Venetia</a> and Caroline, before divorcing in March 1944.</p>
<p>He married Frances Holyoke Howard on 8 October 1944; they later divorced. They had one son, Hugh Howard Stevenson. Robert Stevenson's widow, Ursula Henderson, appeared as herself in the documentary <i>Locked in the Tower: The Men behind Jane Eyre</i> in 2007.</p>
