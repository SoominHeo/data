<p><b>Robert Reed Carradine</b> (born March 24, 1954) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>. The youngest of the <a href="/wiki/Carradine_family" title="Carradine family">Carradine</a> family of actors, he made his first appearances on television western series such as <i><a href="/wiki/Bonanza" title="Bonanza">Bonanza</a></i> and his older brother David's TV series, <i><a href="/wiki/Kung_Fu_(TV_series)" title="Kung Fu (TV series)">Kung Fu</a></i>. Carradine's first film role was in the 1972 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cowboys" title="The Cowboys">The Cowboys</a></i>, which starred <a href="/wiki/John_Wayne" title="John Wayne">John Wayne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_Lee_Browne" title="Roscoe Lee Browne">Roscoe Lee Browne</a>. Carradine also portrayed fraternity president "Lewis Skolnick" in the <i><a href="/wiki/Revenge_of_the_Nerds" title="Revenge of the Nerds">Revenge of the Nerds</a></i> series of comedy films and father <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sam_McGuire" title="Sam McGuire">Sam McGuire</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Lizzie_McGuire" title="Lizzie McGuire">Lizzie McGuire</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Carradine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carradine was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollywood,_Los_Angeles,_California" title="Hollywood, Los Angeles, California">Hollywood, California</a>, the son of actress and artist Sonia Sorel (née Henius) and actor <a href="/wiki/John_Carradine" title="John Carradine">John Carradine</a>. He is one of many actors in the <a href="/wiki/Carradine_family" title="Carradine family">Carradine family</a>. He is the brother of Christopher and <a href="/wiki/Keith_Carradine" title="Keith Carradine">Keith Carradine</a>, paternal half-brother of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bruce_Carradine" title="Bruce Carradine">Bruce</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Carradine" title="David Carradine">David Carradine</a>, and maternal half-brother of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bowen_(actor)" title="Michael Bowen (actor)">Michael Bowen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> His maternal great-grandfather was biochemist <a href="/wiki/Max_Henius" title="Max Henius">Max Henius</a>, and his maternal great-grandmother was the sister of historian <a href="/wiki/Johan_Ludvig_Heiberg_(historian)" title="Johan Ludvig Heiberg (historian)">Johan Ludvig Heiberg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Carradine's parents divorced when he was 2 years old. A bitter custody battle led to his father gaining custody of him and his brothers, Christopher and Keith. During the custody battle, the children had spent three months in a home for abused children as wards of the court.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rader_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rader-3">[3]</a></sup> His brother, Keith, said of the experience, "It was like being in jail. There were bars on the windows, and we were only allowed to see our parents through glass doors. It was very sad. We would stand there on either side of the glass door crying".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Carradine was raised primarily by his stepmother, his father's third wife, Doris Grimshaw, and believed her to be his mother until he was introduced to Sonia Sorel at a Christmas party when he was 14 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vernon_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vernon-5">[5]</a></sup> While still in high school, Robert lived with his half-brother, David, in Laurel Canyon, California. Under David's care he indulged in two of his major interests: race car driving and music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Endless_Highway_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Endless_Highway-6">[6]</a></sup> He and David belonged to a musical quartet that performed in small clubs in Los Angeles and San Francisco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vernon_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Vernon-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Carradine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Carradine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Carradine made his film debut in 1972 in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cowboys" title="The Cowboys">The Cowboys</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/John_Wayne" title="John Wayne">John Wayne</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He was also featured in a short-lived television series, of the same name, based on the movie. He made an appearance as a killer in the <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">Martin Scorsese</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Mean_Streets" title="Mean Streets">Mean Streets</a></i> shooting to death the character played by his brother, David.</p>
<p>During this time he worked with David on some independent projects including a biker film called <i>You and Me</i> (1975) and an unreleased musical called <i>A Country Mile</i>. He also did camera work for David's cult classic Vietnam War inspired <i><a href="/wiki/Americana_(film)" title="Americana (film)">Americana</a></i> which was not released until 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Endless_Highway_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Endless_Highway-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1976, Carradine had the opportunity to demonstrate on screen what he considered to be his "first ambition", car racing,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vernon_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Vernon-5">[5]</a></sup> when he played Jim Cantrell in <a href="/wiki/Paul_Bartel" title="Paul Bartel">Paul Bartel</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Cannonball_(film)" title="Cannonball (film)">Cannonball</a></i>. In the film Robert's character ironically wins the cross country road race, beating the favorite, Coy "Cannonball" Buckman, played by his brother, David. In 1977, Robert became a snack for the vengeful killer whale in the <i><a href="/wiki/Jaws_(film)" title="Jaws (film)">Jaws</a></i> imitation film <i><a href="/wiki/Orca_(film)" title="Orca (film)">Orca</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1978, Robert landed a demanding role in <a href="/wiki/Hal_Ashby" title="Hal Ashby">Hal Ashby</a>'s Oscar-winning Vietnam War drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Coming_Home_(1978_film)" title="Coming Home (1978 film)">Coming Home</a></i>, which starred <a href="/wiki/Jane_Fonda" title="Jane Fonda">Jane Fonda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jon_Voight" title="Jon Voight">Jon Voight</a>. His performance caused some speculation that he might be the best actor in his family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vernon_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Vernon-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Robert was instrumental in securing his brothers David and Keith to perform with him in one of the most unusual casting arrangements in movie history. Together the Carradines played the Younger brothers in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Long_Riders" title="The Long Riders">The Long Riders</a></i> (1980) along with three other sets of acting brothers: <a href="/wiki/Stacy_Keach" title="Stacy Keach">Stacy</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Keach" title="James Keach">James Keach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Quaid" title="Dennis Quaid">Dennis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Randy_Quaid" title="Randy Quaid">Randy Quaid</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Guest" title="Christopher Guest">Christopher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Guest" title="Nicholas Guest">Nicholas Guest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also in 1980, Carradine co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hamill" title="Mark Hamill">Mark Hamill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lee_Marvin" title="Lee Marvin">Lee Marvin</a> in <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Fuller" title="Samuel Fuller">Samuel Fuller</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Red_One" title="The Big Red One">The Big Red One</a></i> recounting Fuller's WW II experience. His character, who was based on Fuller himself, narrated the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1983, he and Cherie Curie starred in the science fiction movie <i><a href="/wiki/Wavelength_(1983_film)" title="Wavelength (1983 film)">Wavelength</a></i> in which he played a washed up rock star who helps extraterrestrials escape from a military base.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> For the film he performed his own compositions including one named after his daughter, Ever. Also in 1983, he starred in the music video for The Motels hit song "<a href="/wiki/Suddenly_Last_Summer_(song)" title="Suddenly Last Summer (song)">Suddenly Last Summer</a>" as lead singer Martha Davis' love interest.</p>
<p>Carradine's biggest film success to date came in 1984 when he starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Revenge_of_the_Nerds" title="Revenge of the Nerds">Revenge of the Nerds</a></i> as the lead nerd Lewis Skolnick. To prepare for the comedy, Carradine spent time at The University of Arizona, where the movie was filmed, participating in rush week. "No fraternity picked him, convincing Carradine that he was indeed right for the part of the nerd that nobody wanted to claim as their own."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Carradine reprised the role of Skolnick in three sequels, taking over as executive producer in the latter two.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Carradine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Carradine's first television appearance was in 1971, on the classic western series, <i><a href="/wiki/Bonanza" title="Bonanza">Bonanza</a></i>. He also appeared on his brother David's series, <i><a href="/wiki/Kung_Fu_(TV_series)" title="Kung Fu (TV series)">Kung Fu</a></i>, as Sunny Jim, the mute companion of Serenity Johnson, played by his father, John, in an episode called <i>Dark Angel</i> (1972). In 1979, he was alongside <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Sue_Anderson" title="Melissa Sue Anderson">Melissa Sue Anderson</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Survival_of_Dana" title="Survival of Dana">Survival of Dana</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Endless_Highway_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Endless_Highway-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1984, Carradine played Robert Cohn in the television mini-series version of <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Hemingway" title="Ernest Hemingway">Ernest Hemingway</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_Also_Rises_(1984_film)" title="The Sun Also Rises (1984 film)">The Sun Also Rises</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He appeared in the 1987 HBO mini-series, <i>Conspiracy: The Trial of the Chicago 8</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He was also a guest star in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Criminal_Intent" title="Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent">Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent</a></i> entitled <i>Gone</i> (2005, Season 4, Episode 11). He also took part in <a href="/wiki/Jane_Doe_(film_series)" title="Jane Doe (film series)">Jane Doe</a>, a TV series directed by <a href="/wiki/James_A._Contner" title="James A. Contner">James A. Contner</a>, in 2007.</p>
<p>One of Carradine's best known roles is that of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sam_McGuire" title="Sam McGuire">Sam McGuire</a>, the aloof father on the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a>'s hit TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Lizzie_McGuire" title="Lizzie McGuire">Lizzie McGuire</a></i>. The show, which starred <a href="/wiki/Hilary_Duff" title="Hilary Duff">Hilary Duff</a> as Lizzie, was widely popular among <i>tween-aged</i> girls: girls between the ages of 8-14. The show's realistic approach to the problems of a 13-year-old girl also appealed to parents. It attracted such guest stars as David Carradine and <i><a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a></i> lead singer, <a href="/wiki/Steven_Tyler" title="Steven Tyler">Steven Tyler</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2013, he and former <i>Revenge of the Nerds</i> co-star, <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Armstrong" title="Curtis Armstrong">Curtis Armstrong</a>, hosted <i><a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Nerds" title="King of the Nerds">King of the Nerds</a></i>, a reality TV series in which a group of nerds compete to find out which one is the nerdiest.</p>
<p>In January 2015, he and <a href="/wiki/Curtis_Armstrong" title="Curtis Armstrong">Curtis Armstrong</a>, repeated their series <i>King of the Nerds</i> on <a href="/wiki/TBS_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="TBS (U.S. TV channel)">TBS</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_car_crash">2015 car crash</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Carradine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2015 car crash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/TMZ" title="TMZ">TMZ</a> reported on March 12, 2015 that Carradine was involved in a serious car accident. He was rushed to the hospital. His wife was also injured in the crash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Carradine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carradine has two daughters, actress <a href="/wiki/Ever_Carradine" title="Ever Carradine">Ever Carradine</a> (with Susan Snyder), Marika Reed Carradine with his wife Edie Mani, and a son, Ian Alexander Carradine. He is also the uncle of actress <a href="/wiki/Martha_Plimpton" title="Martha Plimpton">Martha Plimpton</a>.</p>
