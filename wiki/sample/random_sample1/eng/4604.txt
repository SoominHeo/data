<p><b>Robert Joseph Pastorelli</b> (June 21, 1954 – March 8, 2004) was an American actor.</p>
<p>Having acquired a reputation as a skilled <a href="/wiki/Character_actor" title="Character actor">character actor</a> in the 1980s and 1990s, Pastorelli's career went into decline after the death of his girlfriend in mysterious circumstances at his home in 1999. He died of a narcotic overdose in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Pastorelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pastorelli was born in <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick,_New_Jersey" title="New Brunswick, New Jersey">New Brunswick, New Jersey</a>, the son of Ledo Pastorelli, an insurance salesman and Dotty, an artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was of Albanian<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> descent from Italy. His sister, Gwen Pastorelli, is an <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> singer and a real estate agent.</p>
<p>Pastorelli was raised in <a href="/wiki/Edison,_New_Jersey" title="Edison, New Jersey">Edison, New Jersey</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Edison_High_School_(New_Jersey)" title="Edison High School (New Jersey)">Edison High School</a> in 1972. He initially intended a career as a professional boxer, but had to abandon the sport due to injuries sustained in a near-fatal high-speed car crash at the age of 19 (he later claimed that he had a "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Near_death_experience" title="Near death experience">near death</a>" experience at this time, and that he had experienced himself looking down from above upon his body in a hospital bed with his father at the bedside overcome with grief).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Pastorelli acquired a narcotic habit in his early twenties prior to his acting career, which he overcame, but which he would relapse into throughout his later life.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theater_career">Theater career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Pastorelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Theater career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pastorelli entered acting via <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> theater in the late 1970s, after studying at the New York Academy of Theatrical Arts and the <a href="/wiki/Actors_Studio" title="Actors Studio">Actors Studio</a>, whilst financially maintaining himself working as a bar-tender. He made his stage debut in 1977 in a production of <i>Rebel Without a Cause</i>. He also performed in productions of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rainmaker_(play)" title="The Rainmaker (play)">The Rainmaker</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Death_of_a_Salesman" title="Death of a Salesman">Death of a Salesman</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Later in his career he performed at London's <a href="/wiki/South_Bank" title="South Bank">South Bank</a> theater in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Streetcar_Named_Desire_(play)" title="A Streetcar Named Desire (play)">A Streetcar Named Desire</a></i> in 2002.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hollywood">Hollywood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Pastorelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hollywood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1982 Pastorelli headed west to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> seeking opportunities in Hollywood. Spending the early 1980s employed in television <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bit-part" title="Bit-part">bit-part</a> appearances, he found a niche playing streetwise characters, appearing also in supporting roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Outrageous_Fortune_(film)" title="Outrageous Fortune (film)">Outrageous Fortune</a></i> (1987) and <i><a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_Cop_II" title="Beverly Hills Cop II">Beverly Hills Cop II</a></i> (1987). His first substantial film role came with <i><a href="/wiki/Dances_with_Wolves" title="Dances with Wolves">Dances with Wolves</a></i> (1990). His big break in television came in the role of the gruff but lovable house painter "Eldin Bernecky" on the series <i><a href="/wiki/Murphy_Brown" title="Murphy Brown">Murphy Brown</a></i>, and he stayed with the show for seven seasons from 1988 to 1994. <i>Murphy Brown</i> producer <a href="/wiki/Diane_English" title="Diane English">Diane English</a> was sufficiently impressed with his abilities that she worked with him to produce his first starring vehicle, the television sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Rush" title="Double Rush">Double Rush</a></i> which lasted one season in 1995. Two years later, he starred in the American adaptation of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> detective series <i><a href="/wiki/Cracker_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Cracker (U.S. TV series)">Cracker</a></i> (1997-1999).</p>
<p>As his television career gained momentum Pastorelli's opportunities in cinema films increased: <i><a href="/wiki/Sister_Act_2:_Back_in_the_Habit" title="Sister Act 2: Back in the Habit">Sister Act 2: Back in the Habit</a></i> (1993); a career defining performance playing a demented serial killer in the 1993 murder mystery thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Striking_Distance" title="Striking Distance">Striking Distance</a></i>, although the movie was not a commercial success; <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eraser_(1996_film)" title="Eraser (1996 film)">Eraser</a></i> (1996), <i><a href="/wiki/Michael_(1996_film)" title="Michael (1996 film)">Michael</a></i> (1996), and <i><a href="/wiki/Modern_Vampires" title="Modern Vampires">Modern Vampires</a></i> (1998).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charemon_Jonovich_shooting">Charemon Jonovich shooting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Pastorelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Charemon Jonovich shooting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the evening of March 15, 1999, during an incident at his Hollywood home, Pastorelli's 25-year-old girlfriend, Charemon Jonovich, was killed by a gunshot to the head. During the authorities' investigation that followed, Pastorelli testified that in the midst of an argument between the two of them, she suddenly produced a handgun and killed herself. The incident was investigated as an accident or suicide, and the Los Angeles Coroner's Office declared the cause of death undetermined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_years">Final years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Pastorelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Final years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pastorelli was exonerated of responsibility for Charemon Jonovich's death and received public expressions of sympathy within Hollywood and from the Los Angeles media, but his career went into noticeable decline afterwards. He appeared in two more cinema productions in small roles in the early 2000s as well as some minor supporting roles in television productions. He developed a friendship with the actress <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Close" title="Glenn Close">Glenn Close</a> towards the end of his career<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and appeared alongside her in the television films <i>The Ballad of Lucy Whipple</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/South_Pacific_(2001_film)" title="South Pacific (2001 film)">South Pacific</a></i> in 2001. The next year he again appeared alongside her at London's <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">Royal National Theatre</a> in a performance of Tennessee Williams' <i>A Streetcar Named Desire</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2002 he co-founded the <a href="/wiki/Garden_State_Film_Festival" title="Garden State Film Festival">Garden State Film Festival</a>. His posthumous final screen appearance was in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Be_Cool" title="Be Cool">Be Cool</a></i> (2005).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Pastorelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pastorelli was found dead at his home in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Hills" title="Hollywood Hills">Hollywood Hills</a> on March 8, 2004, from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narcotics" title="Narcotics">narcotics</a> overdose.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foxnews.com_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-foxnews.com-9">[9]</a></sup> At the time of his death, Pastorelli had been forewarned that authorities were planning on arresting him for further questioning. A review of the original evidence of the March 1999 shooting of his girlfriend Charemon Jonovich resulted in her cause of death being reclassified a homicide, and Pastorelli was identified as a prime suspect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foxnews.com_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-foxnews.com-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Coroner's Office reported Pastorelli died of a "fatal blood concentration of <a href="/wiki/Morphine" title="Morphine">morphine</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pastorelli's body was interred in the mausoleum at Saint Catherine's Cemetery in <a href="/wiki/Sea_Girt,_New_Jersey" title="Sea Girt, New Jersey">Sea Girt, New Jersey</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Pastorelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pastorelli had two daughters, Gianna Li Pastorelli (born February 6, 1998) with Charemon Jonovich, and Giannina Marie Pastorelli (born March 6, 2000) with his then girlfriend, Jalee Carder.</p>
