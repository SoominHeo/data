<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Robert Parry (politician)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Robert Parry</b> (8 January 1933 – 9 March 2000) was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a> politician who was <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Member of Parliament</a> in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> for 27 years.</p>
<p>In 1963 he was elected to Liverpool City Council for the central ward, one of the safest Labour wards in Liverpool.</p>
<p>Parry was elected to Parliament at the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_1970" title="United Kingdom general election, 1970">1970 election</a> for <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Exchange_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Liverpool Exchange (UK Parliament constituency)">Liverpool Exchange</a> and served until his retirement in 1997, from 1974 in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Scotland_Exchange_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Liverpool Scotland Exchange (UK Parliament constituency)">Liverpool Scotland Exchange</a>, then from 1983 for <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Riverside_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Liverpool Riverside (UK Parliament constituency)">Liverpool Riverside</a>. He retired from Parliament at the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_general_election,_1997" title="United Kingdom general election, 1997">1997 general election</a>.</p>
<p>He was known as a hard-line left-winger who opposed any policy moves which he saw as edging Labour away from pure <a href="/wiki/Socialism" title="Socialism">socialism</a>. He once branded <a href="/wiki/Neil_Kinnock" title="Neil Kinnock">Neil Kinnock</a> a "traitor" over the latter's denunciation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Militant_tendency" title="Militant tendency">Militant tendency</a> activists who dominated local government on <a href="/wiki/Merseyside" title="Merseyside">Merseyside</a>.</p>
<p>In 1992, Parry was arrested in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> when he and his colleagues unfurled a banner in <a href="/wiki/Tiananmen_Square" title="Tiananmen Square">Tiananmen Square</a> protesting the shootings which had taken place there in 1989.</p>
