<p><b>Robert Wallace Forster, Jr.</b> (born July 13, 1941) is an American actor. He is best known for his roles as news cameraman John Cassellis in <a href="/wiki/Haskell_Wexler" title="Haskell Wexler">Haskell Wexler</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Medium_Cool" title="Medium Cool">Medium Cool</a></i> (1969), Lebanese terrorist Abdul Rafai in the action-thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Delta_Force" title="The Delta Force">The Delta Force</a></i> (1986) and bail bondsman Max Cherry in <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jackie_Brown_(film)" title="Jackie Brown (film)">Jackie Brown</a></i> (1997), the last of which saw him nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor</a>.</p>
<p>Forster's varied filmography further includes titles such as <i><a href="/wiki/Reflections_in_a_Golden_Eye_(film)" title="Reflections in a Golden Eye (film)">Reflections in a Golden Eye</a></i> (1967), <i><a href="/wiki/Alligator_(film)" title="Alligator (film)">Alligator</a></i> (1980), <i><a href="/wiki/Psycho_(1998_film)" title="Psycho (1998 film)">Psycho</a></i> (1998), <i><a href="/wiki/Me,_Myself_%26_Irene" title="Me, Myself &amp; Irene">Me, Myself &amp; Irene</a></i> (2000), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mulholland_Dr._(film)" title="Mulholland Dr. (film)">Mulholland Drive</a></i> (2001), <i><a href="/wiki/Like_Mike" title="Like Mike">Like Mike</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Firewall_(film)" title="Firewall (film)">Firewall</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Lucky_Number_Slevin" title="Lucky Number Slevin">Lucky Number Slevin</a></i> (2006) and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Descendants_(film)" title="The Descendants (film)">The Descendants</a></i> (2011), plus <i><a href="/wiki/Olympus_Has_Fallen" title="Olympus Has Fallen">Olympus Has Fallen</a></i> (2013) and its sequel <i><a href="/wiki/London_Has_Fallen" title="London Has Fallen">London Has Fallen</a></i> (2016).</p>
<p>He has also had recurring roles in television series such as <i><a href="/wiki/Huff_(TV_series)" title="Huff (TV series)">Huff</a></i> (2004–05), <i><a href="/wiki/Heroes_(TV_series)" title="Heroes (TV series)">Heroes</a></i> (2007–08), <i><a href="/wiki/Alcatraz_(TV_series)" title="Alcatraz (TV series)">Alcatraz</a></i> (2012) and <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Man_Standing_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Last Man Standing (U.S. TV series)">Last Man Standing</a></i> (2012–15). He received a <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award" title="Saturn Award">Saturn Award</a> for his performance in the 2013 "<a href="/wiki/Granite_State_(Breaking_Bad)" title="Granite State (Breaking Bad)">Granite State</a>" episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Breaking_Bad" title="Breaking Bad">Breaking Bad</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Forster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Forster was born in <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a>, the son of Grace Dorothy (née Montanarella) and Robert Wallace Forster, Sr., who worked as an elephant trainer for the <a href="/wiki/Ringling_Bros._and_Barnum_%26_Bailey_Circus" title="Ringling Bros. and Barnum &amp; Bailey Circus">Ringling Bros. and Barnum &amp; Bailey Circus</a> and later as an executive for a baking supply company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His mother was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_American" title="Italian American">Italian American</a>, while his father was of <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The couple divorced in 1949.</p>
<p>As a tribute to his father, Forster hung one of his father's Barnum &amp; Bailey Circus posters in the office of his character in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jackie_Brown_(film)" title="Jackie Brown (film)">Jackie Brown</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He completed his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> in history in 1964 at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Rochester" title="University of Rochester">University of Rochester</a>, where he starred in student dramatic performances such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bye_Bye_Birdie_(musical)" title="Bye Bye Birdie (musical)">Bye Bye Birdie</a></i> and, after initially intending to study law, instead decided to become an actor.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Forster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After acclaimed supporting performances in two major Hollywood films, one as Private Williams in John Huston's <i><a href="/wiki/Reflections_in_a_Golden_Eye_(film)" title="Reflections in a Golden Eye (film)">Reflections in a Golden Eye</a></i> (1967), another as part-Indian Army scout Nick Tana in Robert Mulligan's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Stalking_Moon" title="The Stalking Moon">The Stalking Moon</a></i> (1968), Forster starred in the critically acclaimed 1969 film <i><a href="/wiki/Medium_Cool" title="Medium Cool">Medium Cool</a></i>. After starring roles in the TV shows <i><a href="/wiki/Banyon" title="Banyon">Banyon</a></i> (1972) and <i>Nakia</i> (1974), he played mostly supporting roles in action and horror films including Disney's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Hole" title="The Black Hole">The Black Hole</a></i> (1979). Forster had lead roles in cult B-movies in the 1980s like <i><a href="/wiki/Alligator_(film)" title="Alligator (film)">Alligator</a></i> (1980), <i><a href="/wiki/Vigilante_(film)" title="Vigilante (film)">Vigilante</a></i> (1983), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Delta_Force" title="The Delta Force">The Delta Force</a></i> (1986), and <i>The Banker</i> (1989).</p>
<p>Forster was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor</a> in 1997 for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jackie_Brown_(film)" title="Jackie Brown (film)">Jackie Brown</a></i>, which he credits with reviving his career. He has since had consistent work in the film industry, appearing in <i><a href="/wiki/Like_Mike" title="Like Mike">Like Mike</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mulholland_Drive_(film)" title="Mulholland Drive (film)">Mulholland Drive</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Me,_Myself,_%26_Irene" title="Me, Myself, &amp; Irene">Me, Myself, &amp; Irene</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Lucky_Number_Slevin" title="Lucky Number Slevin">Lucky Number Slevin</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Firewall_(film)" title="Firewall (film)">Firewall</a></i>, to name a few. He recently appeared in the made-for-television movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunt_for_the_BTK_Killer" title="The Hunt for the BTK Killer">The Hunt for the BTK Killer</a></i>, as the detective intent on capturing <a href="/wiki/Serial_killer" title="Serial killer">serial killer</a> <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Rader" title="Dennis Rader">Dennis Rader</a>. Forster also played the father of Van on the short-lived Fox series <i><a href="/wiki/Fastlane_(TV_series)" title="Fastlane (TV series)">Fastlane</a></i>.</p>
<p>Forster recorded a public service announcement for Deejay Ra's Hip-Hop Literacy campaign, encouraging reading of books by <a href="/wiki/Elmore_Leonard" title="Elmore Leonard">Elmore Leonard</a> (he starred in the movie adaption of Leonard's book <i><a href="/wiki/Rum_Punch" title="Rum Punch">Rum Punch</a></i>, filmed as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jackie_Brown_(film)" title="Jackie Brown (film)">Jackie Brown</a></i>). Forster appeared in the hit <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heroes(TV_Series)" title="Heroes(TV Series)">Heroes</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthur_Petrelli" title="Arthur Petrelli">Arthur Petrelli</a>, the father of Nathan and Peter Petrelli, as well as the Emmy award winning <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Breaking_Bad" title="Breaking Bad">Breaking Bad</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Walter_White_(Breaking_Bad)" title="Walter White (Breaking Bad)">Walter White</a>'s new identity specialist. Forster is also a motivational speaker.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
