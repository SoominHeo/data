<p><b>Robert James Hoffman III</b> (born September 21, 1980) is an American actor, dancer, and choreographer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Hoffman_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robert James Hoffman III was born in <a href="/wiki/Gainesville,_Florida" title="Gainesville, Florida">Gainesville, Florida</a>, the son of Charlotte and Robert Hoffman II, and moved with his family to <a href="/wiki/Madison,_Alabama" title="Madison, Alabama">Madison, Alabama</a> when he was seven. He has one younger brother, Chris, and two younger sisters, Ashley and Lauren. He attended <a href="/wiki/Bob_Jones_High_School" title="Bob Jones High School">Bob Jones High School</a>. He discovered his passion for dancing after seeing <i><a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson%27s_Thriller_(music_video)" title="Michael Jackson's Thriller (music video)">Michael Jackson's Thriller</a></i>. He briefly attended the <a href="/wiki/Alabama_School_of_Fine_Arts" title="Alabama School of Fine Arts">Alabama School of Fine Arts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Hoffman_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, Hoffman starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/You_Got_Served" title="You Got Served">You Got Served</a></i> as Max, a lead dancer in the antagonist's crew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He also starred in <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon" title="Nick Cannon">Nick Cannon</a>'s sketch comedy television series <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_%27n_Out" title="Wild 'n Out">Wild 'n Out</a></i>. In 2006, he co-starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Bynes" title="Amanda Bynes">Amanda Bynes</a> in the film <i><a href="/wiki/She%27s_the_Man" title="She's the Man">She's the Man</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, Hoffman played the role of Bluto in the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Shrooms_(film)" title="Shrooms (film)">Shrooms</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He also played the role of Clyde "Windmill" Wynorski in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/National_Lampoon%27s_Bag_Boy" title="National Lampoon's Bag Boy">National Lampoon's Bag Boy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Hoffman has guest starred in many shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/American_Dreams" title="American Dreams">American Dreams</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Quintuplets" title="Quintuplets">Quintuplets</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Vanished" title="Vanished">Vanished</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Miami" title="CSI: Miami">CSI: Miami</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Campus_Ladies" title="Campus Ladies">Campus Ladies</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Drop_Dead_Diva" title="Drop Dead Diva">Drop Dead Diva</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, he starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Step_Up_2:_The_Streets" title="Step Up 2: The Streets">Step Up 2: The Streets</a></i> as Chase Collins, a talented dancer at the Maryland School of the Arts (M.S.A).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2009, he guest starred in the ABC Family show <i><a href="/wiki/Greek_(TV_series)" title="Greek (TV series)">Greek</a></i> as Evan Chamber's older brother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On June 1, 2008, <i>Step Up 2: The Streets</i> earned Hoffman and co-star <a href="/wiki/Briana_Evigan" title="Briana Evigan">Briana Evigan</a> an <a href="/wiki/2008_MTV_Movie_Awards" title="2008 MTV Movie Awards">MTV Movie Award</a> for Best Kiss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2008, he and Evigan starred in <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Iglesias" title="Enrique Iglesias">Enrique Iglesias</a>'s music video for his single "<a href="/wiki/Push_(Enrique_Iglesias_song)" title="Push (Enrique Iglesias song)">Push</a>", where they reprised their <i>Step Up</i> roles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hoffman appeared in the 2009 film <i><a href="/wiki/Aliens_in_the_Attic" title="Aliens in the Attic">Aliens in the Attic</a></i> as Ricky.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2010, Hoffman played the role of Chad Bower in the comedy thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/Burning_Palms_(film)" title="Burning Palms (film)">Burning Palms</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Hoffman featured as Garth in the musical comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/BoyBand_(film)" title="BoyBand (film)">BoyBand</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He played the role of Tyler "Dance Machine" Jones in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Take_Me_Home_Tonight_(film)" title="Take Me Home Tonight (film)">Take Me Home Tonight</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was also featured in <a href="/wiki/Garfunkel_and_Oates" title="Garfunkel and Oates">Garfunkel and Oates</a>'s music video for their song "Present Face".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In late January 2012, Hoffman landed a recurring role in the <a href="/wiki/90210_(season_4)" title="90210 (season 4)">fourth season</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a>'s teen drama series <i><a href="/wiki/90210_(TV_series)" title="90210 (TV series)">90210</a></i> as Caleb Walsh, a student at <a href="/wiki/Seminary" title="Seminary">seminary</a> school studying to become a priest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Hoffman starred as Kevin Shepherd in the indie drama <i><a href="/wiki/Lap_Dance_(film)" title="Lap Dance (film)">Lap Dance</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Briana_Evigan" title="Briana Evigan">Briana Evigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ali_Cobrin" title="Ali Cobrin">Ali Cobrin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carmen_Electra" title="Carmen Electra">Carmen Electra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Hoffman guest starred as Thad Callahan in the TV show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Night_Shift_(TV_series)" title="The Night Shift (TV series)">The Night Shift</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In 2016, he had a role in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Amateur_Night_(2016_film)" title="Amateur Night (2016 film)">Amateur Night</a></i> directed by Lisa Addario and Joe Syracus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
