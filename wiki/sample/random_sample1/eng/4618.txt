<p><b>Robin Schulz</b> (born 28 April 1987)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> is a German musician, DJ and record producer. Most of his songs make use of manual guitar riffs. On 4 February 2014, he released the first single from his debut album, a remix of "<a href="/wiki/Waves_(Mr_Probz_song)" title="Waves (Mr Probz song)">Waves</a>" by Dutch hip hop artist <a href="/wiki/Mr_Probz" title="Mr Probz">Mr Probz</a>. This remix later received a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Remixed_Recording,_Non-Classical" title="Grammy Award for Best Remixed Recording, Non-Classical">Best Remixed Recording, Non-Classical</a> at the <a href="/wiki/57th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="57th Annual Grammy Awards">57th Annual Grammy Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His follow-up single, released 6 June 2014, was a remix of the <a href="/wiki/Lilly_Wood_and_the_Prick" title="Lilly Wood and the Prick">Lilly Wood and the Prick</a> track "<a href="/wiki/Prayer_in_C" title="Prayer in C">Prayer in C</a>" from their 2010 album <i>Invincible Friends</i>. Both remixes have charted in many European countries and the United States, leading to international recognition for Schulz. He co-founded Lausbuben Records with Daniel Bruns and Christopher Noble.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Schulz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314:_Prayer">2013–14: <i>Prayer</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Schulz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2013–14: Prayer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Prayer_(Robin_Schulz_album)" title="Prayer (Robin Schulz album)">Prayer (Robin Schulz album)</a></div>
<p>Robin Schulz remixed the song "<a href="/wiki/Waves_(Mr_Probz_song)" title="Waves (Mr Probz song)">Waves</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Mr_Probz" title="Mr Probz">Mr Probz</a> in a more up-tempo fashion, with the remix being released as a single on 4 February 2014 in North America and 7 February 2014 in Europe. The song became a worldwide hit, topping the charts in <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and peaking within the top five of the charts in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. An alternate version of the Schulz remix was released on 11 November 2014, featuring additional vocals from American rapper <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a> and American singer <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brown" title="Chris Brown">Chris Brown</a>. Schulz released a remix of the song "<a href="/wiki/Prayer_in_C" title="Prayer in C">Prayer in C</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Lilly_Wood_and_the_Prick" title="Lilly Wood and the Prick">Lilly Wood and the Prick</a> on 6 June 2014. The remix topped the charts in <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. This track also reached the top 10 in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and charted in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. He released "<a href="/wiki/Willst_Du#Robin_Schulz_version" title="Willst Du">Willst Du</a>" as the third single from the album on 22 August 2014. The song has charted in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. On 24 October 2014, "<a href="/wiki/Sun_Goes_Down_(Robin_Schulz_song)" title="Sun Goes Down (Robin Schulz song)">Sun Goes Down</a>" was released as the fourth single from the album, featuring vocals from British singer <a href="/wiki/Jasmine_Thompson" title="Jasmine Thompson">Jasmine Thompson</a>. The song has peaked to number 2 in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and number 3 in <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. The song has also charted in <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015:_Sugar">2015: <i>Sugar</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Schulz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2015: Sugar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sugar_(Robin_Schulz_album)" title="Sugar (Robin Schulz album)">Sugar (Robin Schulz album)</a></div>
<p>After the success of "Prayer in C" and "Waves", Schulz released the lead single from his second studio album titled "<a href="/wiki/Headlights_(Robin_Schulz_song)" title="Headlights (Robin Schulz song)">Headlights</a>" featuring vocals from American singer and songwriter Ilsey. The song was a mild success in Europe. Schulz later released "<a href="/wiki/Sugar_(Robin_Schulz_song)" title="Sugar (Robin Schulz song)">Sugar</a>" featuring vocals from Canadian singer <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Yates" title="Francesco Yates">Francesco Yates</a>. The song was a success in Europe as well, with moderate success in the US and Canada. Schulz released his second album of the same name on 25 September 2015. A third single titled "Show Me Love" featuring <a href="/wiki/Richard_Judge" title="Richard Judge">Richard Judge</a> was released with a music video.</p>
