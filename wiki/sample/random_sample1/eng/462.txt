<p>The <b>+Teamgeist</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈtiːmgaɪ̯st]</a></span>) ball was the official <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Association_football_ball" title="Association football ball">football</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plus_sign" title="Plus sign">plus sign</a> in its name was introduced for trademark purposes, since the regular <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> word <i>Teamgeist</i>, meaning "team spirit", could not be trademarked.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adidas_Teamgeist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ball was designed by the Adidas Innovation Team and the <a href="/wiki/Molten_Corporation" title="Molten Corporation">Molten Corporation</a> and is made by <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a>, which has provided the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_football_(soccer)_balls#FIFA_World_Cup" title="History of football (soccer) balls">balls</a> used in all <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> matches since the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_1970" title="FIFA World Cup 1970">1970 World Cup</a> when the <a href="/wiki/Adidas_Telstar" title="Adidas Telstar">Telstar</a> was introduced. The +Teamgeist ball differs from previous balls in having just 14 curved panels (making the ball topologically equivalent to a <a href="/wiki/Truncated_octahedron" title="Truncated octahedron">truncated octahedron</a>), rather than the 32 that have been standard since 1970. Like the 32 panel <a href="/wiki/Adidas_Roteiro" title="Adidas Roteiro">Roteiro</a> which preceded it, the +TeamGeist panels are bonded together, rather than stitched. It is claimed to be rounder and to perform more uniformly regardless of where it is hit, and being almost <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waterproof" title="Waterproof">waterproof</a>, it does not get heavier in wet weather.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Cup_match_balls">World Cup match balls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adidas_Teamgeist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World Cup match balls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Each of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_(qualification)" title="2006 FIFA World Cup (qualification)">32 qualified federations</a> received 40 match balls for training purposes.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Teamgeistgallery.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2048" data-file-width="3072" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Teamgeistgallery.jpg/220px-Teamgeistgallery.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Teamgeistgallery.jpg/330px-Teamgeistgallery.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Teamgeistgallery.jpg/440px-Teamgeistgallery.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Teamgeistgallery.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Teamgeist Gallery in <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> "world of football", <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a></div>
</div>

<p>Match balls for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> were personalized with the name of the stadium, the teams, the match date, and the kick-off time of each individual game, under a protective coating.</p>
<p>A special match ball was used for the final game — the <i>"+Teamgeist Berlin"</i>. The design is the same as the other match balls, but accented in gold, with black and white details. Both qualified federations (<a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a>) received 20 of these versions for training purposes.There is also a gold +Teamgeist ball.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technical_specification">Technical specification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adidas_Teamgeist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Technical specification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although it had been planned to include an electronic tracking system in the ball, this was abandoned after a trial at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_U-17_World_Championship_2005" title="FIFA U-17 World Championship 2005">2005 World Under-17 Championship</a> in <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>.</p>

<p>The Teamgeist was the first World Cup ball to not have the traditional 32 panels. Instead, the ball is made up of 14 panels, which means that the number of three-panel touch points is reduced by 60% (60 to 24) and the total length of the panel lines falls by over 15% (400.5 cm to 339.3 cm). Building on the introduction of thermal bonding technology in 2004, the Teamgeist ball is the first time Adidas has used this in a World Cup. <a href="/wiki/Loughborough_University" title="Loughborough University">Loughborough University</a> conducted extensive comparative testing on the ball, along with the Adidas football laboratory in <a href="/wiki/Scheinfeld" title="Scheinfeld">Scheinfeld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adidas_Teamgeist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Swiss</a> international <a href="/wiki/Johann_Vogel" title="Johann Vogel">Johann Vogel</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Beckham" title="David Beckham">David Beckham</a>, both sponsored by Adidas, and others were reported to be happy with the new ball, it was criticized by many top players before the World Cup. Players such as <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roberto_Carlos_(footballer)" title="Roberto Carlos (footballer)">Roberto Carlos</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paul_Robinson_(goalkeeper)" title="Paul Robinson (goalkeeper)">Paul Robinson</a> of <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> were among the critics of the new ball,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> claiming it was too light and had a vastly different performance when wet. The ball has fewer seams, reducing air resistance and thus altering flight patterns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i>"Wawa Aba"</i> ball of the Africa Cup of Nations was criticised by the player of the tournament, <a href="/wiki/Hosny_Abd_Rabo" title="Hosny Abd Rabo">Hosny Abd Rabo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Egypt_national_football_team" title="Egypt national football team">Egypt</a>, who said that ball was bad for passing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Teamgeist_2">Teamgeist 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adidas_Teamgeist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Teamgeist 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Teamgeist 2 was introduced by Adidas as an update of the ball during the 2007 Club World Cup in Japan. The ball was then formally introduced in 2008.</p>
