<p><i><b>Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves</b></i> is a 1991 American <a href="/wiki/Romance_film" title="Romance film">romantic</a> <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a> <a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">adventure film</a>. The film, an iteration of the legendary <a href="/wiki/Robin_Hood" title="Robin Hood">English folk tale</a>, was directed by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Reynolds_(director)" title="Kevin Reynolds (director)">Kevin Reynolds</a>. The film's principal cast includes <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Costner" title="Kevin Costner">Kevin Costner</a> as <a href="/wiki/Robin_Hood" title="Robin Hood">Robin Hood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Freeman" title="Morgan Freeman">Morgan Freeman</a> as Azeem, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Slater" title="Christian Slater">Christian Slater</a> as <a href="/wiki/Will_Scarlet" title="Will Scarlet">Will Scarlet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mary_Elizabeth_Mastrantonio" title="Mary Elizabeth Mastrantonio">Mary Elizabeth Mastrantonio</a> as <a href="/wiki/Maid_Marian" title="Maid Marian">Maid Marian Dubois</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Rickman" title="Alan Rickman">Alan Rickman</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Sheriff_of_Nottingham" title="Sheriff of Nottingham">Sheriff of Nottingham</a>.</p>
<p>The film grossed over $390 million worldwide, ranking as the <a href="/wiki/1991_in_film#Highest-grossing_films_.28international.29" title="1991 in film">second-highest-grossing film</a> of 1991. For his role as George, Sheriff of Nottingham, Rickman was accorded the <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Best_Actor_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="BAFTA Award for Best Actor in a Supporting Role">BAFTA Award for Best Actor in a Supporting Role</a>. The film's theme song, "<a href="/wiki/(Everything_I_Do)_I_Do_It_for_You" title="(Everything I Do) I Do It for You">(Everything I Do) I Do It for You</a>", by <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Adams" title="Bryan Adams">Bryan Adams</a>, was nominated for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Song" title="Academy Award for Best Song">Academy Award for Best Song</a> and won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Song_Written_for_Visual_Media" title="Grammy Award for Best Song Written for Visual Media">Grammy Award for Best Song Written Specifically for a Motion Picture or Television</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Hood:_Prince_of_Thieves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Robin_Hood" title="Robin Hood">Robin</a> of <a href="/wiki/Loxley,_South_Yorkshire" title="Loxley, South Yorkshire">Locksley</a> – an English nobleman who joined <a href="/wiki/Richard_I_of_England" title="Richard I of England">Richard the Lionheart, King of England</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Third_Crusade" title="Third Crusade">Third Crusade</a> – is imprisoned in <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> along with his comrade, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peter_Dubois" title="Peter Dubois">Peter Dubois</a>. Facing the amputation of his hand by the <a href="/wiki/Ayyubid_dynasty" title="Ayyubid dynasty">Ayyubid</a> prison guards, Robin escapes with Peter, saving the life of a <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moor</a> named Azeem in the process. Robin, Peter, and Azeem escape through a sewer and into an alley, but Peter is shot and mortally wounded by an archer. Before making his last stand against the approaching guards, he has Robin swear to protect his sister, Marian. Robin returns to <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> with Azeem, who has vowed to accompany him until Azeem's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Life-debt" title="Life-debt">life-debt</a> to Robin is repaid.</p>
<p>In England, with King Richard still away (in France), the cruel <a href="/wiki/Sheriff_of_Nottingham" title="Sheriff of Nottingham">Sheriff of Nottingham</a> rules over the land, aided by his cousin, <a href="/wiki/Guy_of_Gisbourne" title="Guy of Gisbourne">Guy of Gisbourne</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Witchcraft" title="Witchcraft">witch</a> Mortianna, and the corrupt <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Hereford_(Robin_Hood)" title="Bishop of Hereford (Robin Hood)">Bishop of Hereford</a> (<a href="/wiki/Harold_Innocent" title="Harold Innocent">Harold Innocent</a>). At Locksley Castle, Robin's father, who is loyal to <a href="/wiki/Richard_I_of_England" title="Richard I of England">King Richard</a>, is killed by the Sheriff's men after refusing to join them.</p>
<p>Robin returns to England to find his father dead, his home in ruins, and the Sheriff and his men oppressing the people. After telling Marian of Peter's demise, and while fleeing the Sheriff's forces afterwards, Robin and Azeem encounter a band of outlaws hiding in <a href="/wiki/Sherwood_Forest" title="Sherwood Forest">Sherwood Forest</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Little_John" title="Little John">Little John</a>. Among the band is <a href="/wiki/Will_Scarlet" title="Will Scarlet">Will Scarlet</a>, who holds a belligerent grudge against Robin. Robin ultimately assumes command of the group, encourages his men to fight against Nottingham, and trains them to defend themselves. They rob soldiers and convoys that pass through the forest, then distribute the stolen wealth among the poor. One of their early targets is <a href="/wiki/Friar_Tuck" title="Friar Tuck">Friar Tuck</a>, who subsequently joins these <a href="/wiki/Merry_Men" title="Merry Men">Merry Men</a>. Marian begins to sympathize with the band and renders Robin any aid she can muster. Robin's successes infuriate the Sheriff, who increases the mistreatment of the people, resulting in greater local support for Robin Hood.</p>
<p>The Sheriff kills Gisbourne for his failure to prevent the looting of several convoys, and hires <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celtic warriors</a> from <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> to assist his forces in assaulting the hideout. The Sheriff manages to locate the outlaws' hideout and launches an attack, destroying the forest refuge and capturing most of the outlaws. He confines Marian when she tries to summon help from France. In order to consolidate his claim to the throne, the Sheriff proposes to Marian (who is Richard's cousin), claiming that if she accepts he will spare the lives of the captured outlaws. Nevertheless, several of the rebels are due to be executed by hanging as part of the wedding celebration. Among the captured is Will Scarlet, who makes a deal with the Sheriff to find and kill Robin in exchange for his freedom.</p>
<p>Will meets back with Robin and a handful of his most trusted aides who survived the assault by the Celts. Instead of attacking Robin, Will informs him of the Sheriff's plans to marry Marian and execute Robin's men. Will continues to display anger against Robin, which motivates Robin to question why Will hates him so much. Will then reveals himself to be Robin's younger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legitimacy_(law)" title="Legitimacy (law)">illegitimate</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Half-brother" title="Half-brother">half-brother</a>; Will's mother was a peasant woman with whom Robin's father took comfort after Robin's mother had died. Robin's anger toward his father caused him to separate from her and leave Will fatherless. Despite his anger, Robin is overjoyed to learn that he has a brother, and reconciles with Will.</p>
<p>On the day of the wedding and hangings, Robin and his men infiltrate Nottingham Castle, freeing the prisoners. Although Robin's band originally planned to free their friends and retreat, Azeem reveals himself and his willingness to fight the Sheriff, inciting the peasants to revolt. After a fierce fight, Robin kills the Sheriff but is attacked by Mortianna, who charges with a spear. Azeem slays Mortianna, fulfilling his vow to repay his life debt. Tuck kills the Bishop, burdening him with treasure and throwing him out a window.</p>
<p>Robin and Marian profess their love for each other and marry in the forest. Their wedding is briefly interrupted by the return of King Richard, who blesses the marriage and thanks Robin for his deeds.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Hood:_Prince_of_Thieves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CRW_2684.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="359" data-file-width="480" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/CRW_2684.jpg/220px-CRW_2684.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/CRW_2684.jpg/330px-CRW_2684.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/CRW_2684.jpg/440px-CRW_2684.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CRW_2684.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Sycamore Gap at <a href="/wiki/Hadrian%27s_Wall" title="Hadrian's Wall">Hadrian's Wall</a>. Today known as the "Robin Hood Tree".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></div>
</div>

<p>Rickman turned down the role of the Sheriff twice before he was told he could have <i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/carte_blanche" title="wikt:carte blanche">carte blanche</a></i> with his interpretation of the character.</p>
<p>On the DVD commentary for <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Robin_of_Sherwood" title="Robin of Sherwood">Robin of Sherwood</a></i> television series, which ran from 1983 to 1986, writer and creator <a href="/wiki/Richard_Carpenter_(screenwriter)" title="Richard Carpenter (screenwriter)">Richard Carpenter</a> explains that the stunt coordinator from the <i>Robin of Sherwood</i> series, <a href="/wiki/Terry_Walsh_(actor)" title="Terry Walsh (actor)">Terry Walsh</a>, was hired to do stunt work for <i>Prince of Thieves</i>. While on set, Walsh noticed that there was a <a href="/wiki/Saracen" title="Saracen">Saracen</a> assassin character named Nasir in the film. That character was a creation of Carpenter and is exclusive to the <i>Robin of Sherwood</i> series. Once the creators of <i>Prince of Thieves</i> realized there was potential copyright infringement, they changed the character's name from Nasir to Azeem. Carpenter also explains that Costner and others involved in <i>Prince of Thieves</i> have admitted to watching <i>Robin of Sherwood</i> as inspiration for their film.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Hood:_Prince_of_Thieves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Principal exteriors were shot on location in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. A second unit filmed the medieval walls and towers of the town of <a href="/wiki/Carcassonne" title="Carcassonne">Carcassonne</a> in <a href="/wiki/Aude" title="Aude">Aude</a>, France, for the portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a> and its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nottingham_castle" title="Nottingham castle">castle</a>. Locksley Castle was <a href="/wiki/Wardour_Castle" title="Wardour Castle">Wardour Castle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire" title="Wiltshire">Wiltshire</a> – restored in an early shot using a <a href="/wiki/Matte_painting" title="Matte painting">matte painting</a>. Marian's manor was filmed at <a href="/wiki/Hulne_Priory" title="Hulne Priory">Hulne Priory</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northumberland" title="Northumberland">Northumberland</a>. Scenes set in <a href="/wiki/Sherwood_Forest" title="Sherwood Forest">Sherwood Forest</a> were filmed throughout England: <a href="/wiki/Burnham_Beeches" title="Burnham Beeches">Burnham Beeches</a> in <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a> was used for the outlaws' encampment, <a href="/wiki/Aysgarth_Falls" title="Aysgarth Falls">Aysgarth Falls</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> for the fight scene between Robin and Little John, and <a href="/wiki/Hardraw_Force" title="Hardraw Force">Hardraw Force</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a> was the location where Marian sees Robin bathing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-book_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-book-9">[9]</a></sup> Sycamore Gap on <a href="/wiki/Hadrian%27s_Wall" title="Hadrian's Wall">Hadrian's Wall</a> was used for the scene when Robin first confronts the Sheriff's men.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Chalk cliffs at <a href="/wiki/Seven_Sisters,_Sussex" title="Seven Sisters, Sussex">Seven Sisters, Sussex</a> were used as the locale for Robin's return to England from the Crusades.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Interior scenes were completed at <a href="/wiki/Shepperton_Studios" title="Shepperton Studios">Shepperton Studios</a> in <a href="/wiki/Surrey" title="Surrey">Surrey</a>, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-book_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-book-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Hood:_Prince_of_Thieves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves</i> was submitted for classification from the <a href="/wiki/British_Board_of_Film_Classification" title="British Board of Film Classification">British Board of Film Classification</a> and required 14 seconds to be cut from the film in order to obtain a PG rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbfc_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbfc-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Hood:_Prince_of_Thieves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span id="Critics"></span><span id="Critical_response"></span><span id="Critical_reception"></span> <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> critic <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> praised Freeman's performance as well as Rickman's, but ultimately decried the film as a whole, giving it two stars and stating, "<i>Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves</i> is a murky, unfocused, violent, and depressing version of the classic story... The most depressing thing about the movie is that children will attend it expecting to have a good time."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> gave the film a negative review, with <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Canby" title="Vincent Canby">Vincent Canby</a> writing that the movie is "a mess, a big, long, joyless reconstruction of the Robin Hood legend that comes out firmly for civil rights, feminism, religious freedom and economic opportunity for all."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> found the movie unsatisfactory as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Costner was criticised for not attempting an English accent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lanre Bakare, writing in <i>The Guardian</i> calls Rickman's Sheriff, for which he won a BAFTA, a "genuinely great performance".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Review aggregation website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> retrospectively collected reviews from 52 critics to give the film a score of 50%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Prince of Thieves</i> was nominated for two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award" title="Golden Raspberry Award">Golden Raspberry Awards</a>: Kevin Costner "won" the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award_for_Worst_Actor" title="Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Actor">Worst Actor</a> award for his performance as Robin Hood, while Christian Slater received a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Golden_Raspberry_Award_for_Worst_Supporting_Actor" title="Golden Raspberry Award for Worst Supporting Actor">Worst Supporting Actor</a> for his performances in this film and <i><a href="/wiki/Mobsters" title="Mobsters">Mobsters</a></i> but "lost" to <a href="/wiki/Dan_Aykroyd" title="Dan Aykroyd">Dan Aykroyd</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Nothing_but_Trouble_(1991_film)" title="Nothing but Trouble (1991 film)">Nothing but Trouble</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, the <a href="/wiki/American_Film_Institute" title="American Film Institute">American Film Institute</a> nominated this film for <a href="/wiki/AFI%27s_100_Years_of_Film_Scores" title="AFI's 100 Years of Film Scores">AFI's 100 Years of Film Scores</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Hood:_Prince_of_Thieves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite mixed critical reception, the film was a box office hit. It made $25 million in its opening weekend and $18.3 million in its second. The film would eventually make $390,493,908 at the global box office, making it the second-highest-grossing film of 1991, immediately behind <i><a href="/wiki/Terminator_2:_Judgment_Day" title="Terminator 2: Judgment Day">Terminator 2: Judgment Day</a></i>. It enjoyed the second-best opening for a non-sequel, at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Adaptations">Adaptations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Hood:_Prince_of_Thieves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Adaptations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A tie-in <a href="/wiki/Robin_Hood:_Prince_of_Thieves_(video_game)" title="Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves (video game)">video game of the same name</a> was released in 1991 for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Nintendo Entertainment System">Nintendo Entertainment System</a> and <a href="/wiki/Game_Boy" title="Game Boy">Game Boy</a>. Developed by Sculptured Software Inc. and Bits Studios, respectively, and published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virgin_Games,_Inc." title="Virgin Games, Inc.">Virgin Games, Inc.</a>, it was featured as the cover game for the July 1991 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Power" title="Nintendo Power">Nintendo Power</a></i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>A toy line was released by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kenner" title="Kenner">Kenner</a>, consisting of <a href="/wiki/Action_figure" title="Action figure">action figures</a> and playsets. Notably, all but one of the figures were slightly modified from Kenner's well known Super Powers line, while Friar Tuck, as well as the vehicles and playset, were modified from <i>Star Wars: Return of the Jedi</i> toys.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
