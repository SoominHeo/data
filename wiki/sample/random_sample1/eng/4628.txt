<p><b>Roschdy Zem</b> (born 27 September 1965) is a <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a> actor and filmmaker of <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Moroccan</a> descent. He shared the award for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Actor_Award_(Cannes_Film_Festival)" title="Best Actor Award (Cannes Film Festival)">Best Actor</a> for his role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Days_of_Glory_(2006_film)" title="Days of Glory (2006 film)">Days of Glory</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/2006_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2006 Cannes Film Festival">2006 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-festival-cannes.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-festival-cannes.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roschdy_Zem&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Versatile and determined to not be typecast in "<i><a href="/wiki/Beur" title="Beur">Beur</a></i>" roles, Roschdy Zem developed his range, playing a General of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napol%C3%A9on" title="Napoléon">Napoléon</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Monsieur_N." title="Monsieur N.">Monsieur N.</a></i> (2003), a Jewish father in <i><a href="/wiki/Live_and_Become" title="Live and Become">Va, vis et deviens</a></i> (<i>Live and Become</i>, 2005), and a transvestite in <i>Change moi ma vie</i> (<i>Change My Life</i>, 2001) alongside <a href="/wiki/Fanny_Ardant" title="Fanny Ardant">Fanny Ardant</a>. He also appeared in roles highlighting issues in mainstream French society as well as in films promoting aspects of French and North African history such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indig%C3%A8nes" title="Indigènes">Indigènes</a></i> (<i>Days of Glory</i>, 2006) and <i>Camping à la ferme</i> (2005), based on a script from <a href="/wiki/Azouz_Begag" title="Azouz Begag">Azouz Begag</a>.</p>
<p>In 2011, he directed the film <i><a href="/wiki/Omar_Killed_Me" title="Omar Killed Me">Omar Killed Me</a></i>, which was selected as the Moroccan entry for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Foreign_Language_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film">Best Foreign Language Film</a> at the <a href="/wiki/84th_Academy_Awards" title="84th Academy Awards">84th Academy Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84th_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-84th-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
