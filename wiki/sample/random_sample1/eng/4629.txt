<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochefort,_Charente-Maritime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In December 1665, Rochefort was chosen by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Colbert" title="Jean-Baptiste Colbert">Jean-Baptiste Colbert</a> as a place of "refuge, defense and supply" for the <a href="/wiki/French_Navy" title="French Navy">French Navy</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_de_Rochefort" title="Arsenal de Rochefort">Arsenal de Rochefort</a> served as a naval base and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dockyard" title="Dockyard">dockyard</a> until it closed in 1926.</p>
<p>In September 1757, Rochefort was the target of an ambitious <a href="/wiki/Raid_on_Rochefort" title="Raid on Rochefort">British raid</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War" title="Seven Years' War">Seven Years' War</a>.</p>
<p>Another infrastructure of early Rochefort from 1766 was its <i>bagne</i>, a high-security <a href="/wiki/Penal_colony" title="Penal colony">penal colony</a> involving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hard_labor" title="Hard labor">hard labor</a>. <i>Bagnes</i> were then common fixtures in military harbors and naval bases, such as <a href="/wiki/Toulon" title="Toulon">Toulon</a> or <a href="/wiki/Brest,_France" title="Brest, France">Brest</a>, because they provided free labor. During the <a href="/wiki/Jacobin" title="Jacobin">Jacobin</a> period of the French Revolution (1790â95), over 800 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">priests</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Clergy" title="Clergy">clergy</a> who refused to take the anti-<a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Papal</a> oath of the "Civil Constitution of the Clergy" were put aboard a fleet of prison ships in Rochefort harbor, where most died due to inhumane conditions.</p>
<p>Off Rochefort, from the island of <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele-d%27Aix" title="Ãle-d'Aix">Ãle-d'Aix</a> where he had spent several days hoping to flee to <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">America</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">Napoleon Bonaparte</a> surrendered to Captain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederick_Lewis_Maitland_(Rear_Admiral)" title="Frederick Lewis Maitland (Rear Admiral)">F. L. Maitland</a> aboard <a href="/wiki/HMS_Bellerophon_(1786)" title="HMS Bellerophon (1786)">HMS <i>Bellerophon</i></a>, on 17 July 1815, ending the "<a href="/wiki/Hundred_Days" title="Hundred Days">Hundred Days</a>".</p>
<p>Rochefort is a notable example of 17th-century "ville nouvelle" or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_town" title="New town">new town</a>, which means its design and building resulted from a political decree. The reason for building Rochefort was to a large extent that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_monarchy" title="French monarchy">royal power</a> could hardly depend on rebellious <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Protestant" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> <a href="/wiki/La_Rochelle" title="La Rochelle">La Rochelle</a>, which <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_Richelieu" title="Cardinal Richelieu">Cardinal Richelieu</a> had to <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_La_Rochelle" title="Siege of La Rochelle">besiege</a> a few decades earlier. Well into the 20th century, Rochefort remained primarily a <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a> town. The <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourist</a> industry, which had long existed due to the town's <a href="/wiki/Spa" title="Spa">spa</a>, gained emphasis in the 1990s.</p>
