<p><b>Lowestoft</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="/./ syllable break">.</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ɒ/ short 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span></span>/</a></span></span>, <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ɒ/ short 'o' in 'body'">ɒ</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span></span>/</a></span></span> or <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'f' in 'find'">f</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">town</a> in the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/County" title="County">county</a> of <a href="/wiki/Suffolk" title="Suffolk">Suffolk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OL40_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-OL40-2">[2]</a></sup> The town is on the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> coast and is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extreme_points_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Extreme points of the United Kingdom">most easterly settlement</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. It is 110 miles (177 km) north-east of <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, 38 miles (61 km) north-east of <a href="/wiki/Ipswich" title="Ipswich">Ipswich</a> and 22 miles (35 km) south-east of <a href="/wiki/Norwich" title="Norwich">Norwich</a>. It is situated on the edge of <a href="/wiki/The_Broads" title="The Broads">The Broads</a> system and is the major settlement within the district of <a href="/wiki/Waveney" title="Waveney">Waveney</a> with a population of 71,010 in 2011.</p>
<p>Some of the earliest evidence of settlement in Britain has been found in Lowestoft and the town has a long history. It is a <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Lowestoft" title="Port of Lowestoft">port town</a> which developed due to the <a href="/wiki/Fishing_industry" title="Fishing industry">fishing industry</a>, and a traditional <a href="/wiki/Seaside_resort" title="Seaside resort">seaside resort</a>. It has wide, sandy beaches, two piers and a number of other tourist attractions. Whilst its fisheries have declined, the development of <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_industry" title="Petroleum industry">oil and gas exploitation</a> in the southern North Sea in the 1960s led to the development of the town, along with nearby <a href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth" title="Great Yarmouth">Great Yarmouth</a>, as a base for the industry. This role has since declined and the town has begun to develop as a centre of the <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> industry within the East of England.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the discovery of flint tools in the cliffs at <a href="/wiki/Pakefield" title="Pakefield">Pakefield</a> in south Lowestoft in 2005, the human habitation of the Lowestoft area can be traced back 700,000 years. This establishes Lowestoft as one of the earliest known sites for human habitation in Britain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cba_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-cba-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The area was settled during the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Ages</a> and during the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">Roman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxons" title="Anglo-Saxons">Saxon</a> periods, with a Saxon cemetery producing a number of finds at Bloodmoor Hill in south Lowestoft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cau_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-cau-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wavlandscapehist_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-wavlandscapehist-5">[5]</a></sup> The settlement's name is derived from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viking" title="Viking">Viking</a> personal name <i>Hlothver</i> and <i>toft</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mills_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-mills-6">[6]</a></sup> a Viking word for 'homestead'. The town's name has been spelled variously: <i>Lothnwistoft</i>, <i>Lestoffe</i>, <i>Laistoe</i>, <i>Loystoft</i> and <i>Laystoft</i>.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday survey</a> the village was known as <i>Lothuwistoft</i> and was relatively small with a population of around 16 households comprising, in 1086, three families, ten smallholders and three slaves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-freeman_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-freeman-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ddmap_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ddmap-8">[8]</a></sup> The manor formed part of <a href="/wiki/William_the_Conqueror" title="William the Conqueror">the king's</a> holding within the <a href="/wiki/Mutford_and_Lothingland_Hundred" title="Mutford and Lothingland Hundred">Hundred of Lothingland</a> and was worth about four <a href="/wiki/Money" title="Money">geld</a> in tax income.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ddmap_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-ddmap-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ddbook_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ddbook-9">[9]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roger_Bigod,_1st_Earl_of_Norfolk" title="Roger Bigod, 1st Earl of Norfolk">Roger Bigod</a> was the tenant in chief of the village.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ddbook_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-ddbook-9">[9]</a></sup> The village of <a href="/wiki/Akethorpe" title="Akethorpe">Akethorpe</a> may have been located close to Lowestoft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ddmapake_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ddmapake-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> Lowestoft became an increasingly important fishing town. The industry grew quickly and the town grew to challenge its neighbour <a href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth" title="Great Yarmouth">Great Yarmouth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edplow_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-edplow-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poppy_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-poppy-12">[12]</a></sup> The trade, particularly fishing for <a href="/wiki/Herring" title="Herring">herring</a>, continued to act as the town's main identity until the 20th century.</p>
<p>In June 1665 the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lowestoft" title="Battle of Lowestoft">Battle of Lowestoft</a>, the first battle of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Anglo-Dutch_War" title="Second Anglo-Dutch War">Second Anglo-Dutch War</a>, took place 40 miles (64 km) off the coast of the town. The battle resulted in a significant victory for the English fleet over the Dutch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-battlepic_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-battlepic-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_Porcelain_Factory" title="Lowestoft Porcelain Factory">Lowestoft Porcelain Factory</a>, active from 1757 to 1802, was in production for longer than any English <a href="/wiki/Soft-paste_porcelain" title="Soft-paste porcelain">soft-paste porcelain</a> manufacturer other than <a href="/wiki/Royal_Worcester" title="Royal Worcester">Royal Worcester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Crown_Derby" title="Royal Crown Derby">Royal Crown Derby</a>, producing domestic wares such as pots, teapots and jugs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edplow_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-edplow-11">[11]</a></sup> The factory, built on the site of an existing pottery or brick kiln, was later used as a brewery and malt kiln. Most of its remaining buildings were demolished in 1955.</p>

<p>In the 19th century, the arrival of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samuel_Morton_Peto" title="Samuel Morton Peto">Sir Samuel Morton Peto</a> brought about a change in Lowestoft's fortunes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poppy_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-poppy-12">[12]</a></sup> Railway contractor Peto was contracted by the Lowestoft Railway &amp; Harbour Company to build a railway line between Lowestoft and Reedham. This stimulated the further development of the fishing industry and the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Lowestoft" title="Port of Lowestoft">Port of Lowestoft</a> in general.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lmmport_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-lmmport-14">[14]</a></sup> The development of the port boosted trade with the continent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lmmport_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-lmmport-14">[14]</a></sup> Peto's railway not only enabled the fishing industry to get its product to market, but assisted the development of other industries such as engineering and helped to establish Lowestoft as a flourishing <a href="/wiki/Seaside_resort" title="Seaside resort">seaside holiday resort</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poppy_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-poppy-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lmmport_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-lmmport-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, Lowestoft was <a href="/wiki/Bombardment_of_Yarmouth_and_Lowestoft" title="Bombardment of Yarmouth and Lowestoft">bombarded</a> by the German Navy on 24 April 1916 in conjunction with the <a href="/wiki/Easter_Rising" title="Easter Rising">Easter Rising</a>. The port was a significant naval base during the war, including for armed trawlers such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armed_trawler_%22Ethel_%26_Millie%22" title='Armed trawler "Ethel &amp; Millie"'>Ethel &amp; Millie</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Armed_trawler_Nelson" title="Armed trawler Nelson">Nelson</a></i> which were used to combat <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaiserliche_Marine" title="Kaiserliche Marine">German</a> <a href="/wiki/U-boat" title="U-boat">U-boat</a> actions in the North Sea such as the <a href="/wiki/Action_of_15_August_1917" title="Action of 15 August 1917">action of 15th August 1917</a>. In <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the town was heavily targeted for bombing by the <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a> due to its engineering industry and role as a naval base.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ww2bombing_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-ww2bombing-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hoseasonobit_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-hoseasonobit-16">[16]</a></sup> It is sometimes claimed that it became one of the most heavily bombed towns per head of population in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ww2bombing_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-ww2bombing-15">[15]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Naval_Patrol_Service" title="Royal Naval Patrol Service">Royal Naval Patrol Service</a>, formed primarily from trawlermen and fishermen from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Naval_Reserve" title="Royal Naval Reserve">Royal Naval Reserve</a>, was mobilised at Lowestoft in August 1939. The service had its central depot <i>HMS Europa</i>, also known as <i>Sparrow's Nest</i>, in the town. Many Lowestoft fishermen served in the patrol service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-memoriallist_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-memoriallist-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lowestoft is the major settlement in <a href="/wiki/Waveney" title="Waveney">Waveney District Council</a>. It is a former <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a>, having lost this status in 1974, although it retains a ceremonial mayor. The mayor of Lowestoft is elected by councillors annually.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mayor_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-mayor-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County_Council" title="Suffolk County Council">Suffolk County Council</a> is the local authority.</p>
<p>The town is part of the <a href="/wiki/Waveney_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Waveney (UK Parliament constituency)">Waveney parliamentary constituency</a>, represented at Westminster by <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/Peter_Aldous" title="Peter Aldous">Peter Aldous</a>. Former M.P.s include <a href="/wiki/Bob_Blizzard" title="Bob Blizzard">Bob Blizzard</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_Porter_(UK_politician)" title="David Porter (UK politician)">David Porter</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Prior,_Baron_Prior" title="James Prior, Baron Prior">Jim Prior</a>, a cabinet minister and <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Secretary of State for Northern Ireland">Secretary of State for Northern Ireland</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Premiership_of_Margaret_Thatcher" title="Premiership of Margaret Thatcher">Thatcher</a> governments, who also represented the former constituency of <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Lowestoft (UK Parliament constituency)">Lowestoft</a>. For European Union elections Lowestoft forms part of the <a href="/wiki/East_of_England_(European_Parliament_constituency)" title="East of England (European Parliament constituency)">East of England</a> constituency.</p>
<p>For district election purposes, Lowestoft is divided into ten electoral wards, with <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Colville" title="Carlton Colville">Carlton Colville</a> treated as a separate electoral area. Harbour, <a href="/wiki/Kirkley" title="Kirkley">Kirkley</a>, Normanston, <a href="/wiki/Pakefield" title="Pakefield">Pakefield</a>, St Margarets and Whitton wards elect three councillors each, with Carlton, Gunton and Corton, Oulton and Oulton Broad wards electing two district councillors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elecmap_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-elecmap-19">[19]</a></sup> Of the 48 council seats in the district, 26 represent wards within Lowestoft, with three more representing Carlton Colville. In 2010 the council changed to a Whole Council election process, with all seats on the council elected at one set of elections every four years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fouryearelec_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-fouryearelec-20">[20]</a></sup> The most recent district council elections were on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waveney_Council_election,_2011" title="Waveney Council election, 2011">5th May 2011</a> at which the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour party</a> won 19 of the Lowestoft seats, a gain of four seats, concentrated in the central areas of the town. The Conservative party won six seats with one Independent candidate retaining their seat in Oulton ward.</p>
<p>At Suffolk County Council, Lowestoft and its immediate surrounding area are represented by eight councillors, split equally between four electoral divisions–Gunton, Lowestoft South, Oulton and Pakefield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccelec_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ccelec-21">[21]</a></sup> For county council elections Pakefield division includes Carlton Colville. Elections take place every four years, with the most recent elections in <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County_Council_election,_2013" title="Suffolk County Council election, 2013">May 2013</a>. Five of Lowestoft's county councillors represent the Labour party and three the <a href="/wiki/UK_Independence_Party" title="UK Independence Party">UK Independence Party</a> after the Conservative party lost all seven of its seats in May 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eadt3may13_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-eadt3may13-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lowjournal3may13_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-lowjournal3may13-23">[23]</a></sup></p>

<p>Lowestoft is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extreme_points_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Extreme points of the United Kingdom">easternmost town in the United Kingdom</a>. The town lies on the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> coast and is located 110 miles (177 km) north-east of London, 38 miles (61 km) north-east of <a href="/wiki/Ipswich" title="Ipswich">Ipswich</a> and 22 miles (35 km) south-east of <a href="/wiki/Norwich" title="Norwich">Norwich</a>. The town is divided in two by <a href="/wiki/Lake_Lothing" title="Lake Lothing">Lake Lothing</a> which forms <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Lowestoft" title="Port of Lowestoft">Lowestoft Harbour</a> and provides access via <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Broad" title="Oulton Broad">Oulton Broad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Dyke" title="Oulton Dyke">Oulton Dyke</a> to the <a href="/wiki/River_Waveney" title="River Waveney">River Waveney</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Broads" title="The Broads">the Broads</a>.</p>
<p>Lowestoft is mainly low lying, although with areas of steep hills in the north of the town where the highest points are 20–30 metres above sea level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wavlandscape_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-wavlandscape-24">[24]</a></sup> The underlying rock is crag-sand with overlying sand and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glacial_till" title="Glacial till">glacial till</a> deposits with gravel, with the crag being exposed at coastal cliffs such as at <a href="/wiki/Pakefield" title="Pakefield">Pakefield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wavlandscape_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-wavlandscape-24">[24]</a></sup> Areas around Lake Lothing feature alluvium silt and some marshland remains west of Oulton Broad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wavlandscape_24-2"><a href="#cite_note-wavlandscape-24">[24]</a></sup> The beaches to the south of the harbour are sandy and have <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_flag_beach" title="Blue flag beach">Blue Flag</a> status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-northclaremont_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-northclaremont-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-southclaremont_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-southclaremont-26">[26]</a></sup> Towards the north of the harbour is an area of old sand dunes known locally as the Denes as well as more beaches and <a href="/wiki/Ness_Point" title="Ness Point">Ness Point</a>, the easternmost point of the U.K.</p>
<p>Lowestoft has been subject to periodic flooding, most notably in <a href="/wiki/North_Sea_flood_of_1953" title="North Sea flood of 1953">January 1953</a> when a North Sea swell driven by low pressure and a high tide swept away many of the older sea defences and deluged most of the southern town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc53_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc53-27">[27]</a></sup> Heavy rain caused flash flooding in the town in September 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc25sep06_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc25sep06-28">[28]</a></sup> December 2013 <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">storm surge</a> caused severe flooding <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc6dec13_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc6dec13-29">[29]</a></sup> of Lowestoft and its suburbs in December 2013 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-itv6dec13_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-itv6dec13-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lowestoft is in one of the driest areas of the United Kingdom and receives less than 600 mm of rainfall a year on average.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metoffice_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-metoffice-31">[31]</a></sup> Rainfall is fairly evenly distributed throughout the year. Summer temperatures will tend to reach 21 °C in August, when the town averages over 200 hours of sunshine, whilst in winter minimum temperatures average 2 °C.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metoffice_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-metoffice-31">[31]</a></sup> Significant snowfall is rare. Sea fog and cool onshore breezes can affect the town.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lowestoft is Suffolk's second largest town (second to Ipswich) with an estimated population of 58,560 in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wav2010_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-wav2010-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scccensuslow_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-scccensuslow-32">[32]</a></sup> Including the suburban areas of Oulton and <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Colville" title="Carlton Colville">Carlton Colville</a>, which are part of the wider urban area, brings this population to 71,010, up from 64,358 at the 2001 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wav2010_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-wav2010-1">[1]</a></sup> The town contains business and residential areas, with the main shopping centre lying just to the north of Lake Lothing. The wider Lowestoft urban area includes the suburbs of Carlton Colville, <a href="/wiki/Gunton,_Suffolk" title="Gunton, Suffolk">Gunton</a>, Pakefield, Oulton and Oulton Broad as well as the district of <a href="/wiki/Kirkley" title="Kirkley">Kirkley</a>. Outlying villages associated with Lowestoft include <a href="/wiki/Blundeston" title="Blundeston">Blundeston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corton,_Suffolk" title="Corton, Suffolk">Corton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gisleham" title="Gisleham">Gisleham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kessingland" title="Kessingland">Kessingland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Somerleyton" title="Somerleyton">Somerleyton</a>.</p>
<p>Around a tenth of the Lowestoft areas population of 64,358 at the 2001 census was aged 75 or over, whereas 20% was aged under 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scccensuslow_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-scccensuslow-32">[32]</a></sup> In general the population of a number of wards within the town is slightly skewed towards elderly people. The population is mainly classified as "white" with minority ethnic groups making up around 1.4% of the population of the town compared to around 8.7% nationally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guntonprofile_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-guntonprofile-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pakefieldprofile_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-pakefieldprofile-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oultonprofile_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-oultonprofile-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lowsouthprofile_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-lowsouthprofile-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wavprofile_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-wavprofile-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ONS_who_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-ONS_who-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the 2001 census there were 27,777 households with an average household size of 2.40.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scccensuslow_32-2"><a href="#cite_note-scccensuslow-32">[32]</a></sup> In total 8,430 households (30%) were classified as one person households while 26% included children aged 15 or under.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scccensuslow_32-3"><a href="#cite_note-scccensuslow-32">[32]</a></sup> The proportion of households without a car was 29% whilst 22% had two or more cars. In terms of housing tenure, 72% of households were owner occupied.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scccensuslow_32-4"><a href="#cite_note-scccensuslow-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally based on the fishing and engineering industries, the economy of Lowestoft has declined over the years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aldous_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-aldous-39">[39]</a></sup> Although the tourism sector has grown, the major employers in the town are in the wholesale and retail sector, making up 18% of employment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wavprofilewdc_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-wavprofilewdc-40">[40]</a></sup> Service industries, including health and social care and education are significant employers, whilst manufacturing employs around 10% of the workforce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wavprofilewdc_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-wavprofilewdc-40">[40]</a></sup> Employment in the town can vary seasonally due to the importance of tourism to the economy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wdcecon_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-wdcecon-41">[41]</a></sup> In early 2011 around 10% of the working population of the town claimed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jobseekers_Allowance" title="Jobseekers Allowance">Jobseekers Allowance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wdcecon_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-wdcecon-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditional_industries">Traditional industries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Traditional industries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Until the mid 1960s, fishing was perceived as Lowestoft's main industry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poppy_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-poppy-12">[12]</a></sup> although from the 1930s the percentage of those employed directly and in trades associated with fishing was actually only around 10% of the working population<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Fleets of <a href="/wiki/Drifter_(fishing_boat)" title="Drifter (fishing boat)">drifters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fishing_trawler" title="Fishing trawler">trawlers</a> caught fish such as <a href="/wiki/Herring" title="Herring">herring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cod" title="Cod">cod</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plaice" title="Plaice">plaice</a>. Catches have diminished since the 1960s<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcfilm_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcfilm-42">[42]</a></sup> and, although by the 1980s 100 boats remained, there are now only a few small boats operating out of Lowestoft, with no large trawlers remaining.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aldous_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-aldous-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcfish27dec07_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcfish27dec07-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcfish080630_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcfish080630-44">[44]</a></sup> By 2011 just three traders remained at the towns fish market which is under threat of closure due to the redevelopment of the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Lowestoft" title="Port of Lowestoft">port</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc30mar11_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc30mar11-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edp29mar11_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-edp29mar11-46">[46]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Centre_for_Environment,_Fisheries_and_Aquaculture_Science" title="Centre for Environment, Fisheries and Aquaculture Science">Centre for Environment, Fisheries and Aquaculture Science</a> (CEFAS), a large fisheries research centre, which is a part of <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Environment,_Food_and_Rural_Affairs" title="Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs">Defra</a> is still located in Lowestoft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aldous_39-2"><a href="#cite_note-aldous-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other major traditional employers included the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Coach_Works" title="Eastern Coach Works">Eastern Coach Works</a> and engineering and ship building companies clustered around the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Lowestoft" title="Port of Lowestoft">harbour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poppy_12-4"><a href="#cite_note-poppy-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aldous_39-3"><a href="#cite_note-aldous-39">[39]</a></sup> These included <a href="/wiki/Brooke_Marine" title="Brooke Marine">Brooke Marine</a> and Richards shipbuilding companies, who together employed over a thousand men but went out of business in the 1990s and Norwich based engineering company <a href="/wiki/Boulton_%26_Paul_Ltd" title="Boulton &amp; Paul Ltd">Boulton and Paul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poppy_12-5"><a href="#cite_note-poppy-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc03dec09_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc03dec09-47">[47]</a></sup> Some ship building and repair still goes on in the harbour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc28jun08_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc28jun08-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcwherry_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwherry-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_economy">Modern economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Modern economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Major local employers include <a href="/wiki/Birds_Eye" title="Birds Eye">Birds Eye</a> frozen foods which employs 700 workers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aldous_39-4"><a href="#cite_note-aldous-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc04feb10_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc04feb10-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc05oct10_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc05oct10-51">[51]</a></sup> The company has been located in the town for over 60 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc07nov03_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc07nov03-52">[52]</a></sup> Food processing company Wessex Foods closed its Lowestoft plant in 2010 after a major fire destroyed the factory and the company was unable to find alternative premises.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc29oct10_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc29oct10-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>A number of other local employers have had to make redundancies in recent years. The <a href="/wiki/Sanyo" title="Sanyo">Sanyo</a> plant in the town closed down in 2009 with the loss of 60 jobs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc12jan09_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc12jan09-54">[54]</a></sup> The plant once employed 800 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc17mar10_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc17mar10-55">[55]</a></sup> Timber company <a href="/wiki/Jeld-Wen" title="Jeld-Wen">Jeld-Wen</a> closed their factory in the town in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc03dec09_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc03dec09-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>From the mid 1960s to the late 1990s, the <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_industry" title="Petroleum industry">oil and gas industry</a> provided significant employment in the Lowestoft area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-offshore_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-offshore-56">[56]</a></sup> For many years the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">Shell</a> Southern Operations base on the north shore of <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Lowestoft" title="Port of Lowestoft">Lowestoft Harbour</a> was one of the town's largest employers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-offshore_56-1"><a href="#cite_note-offshore-56">[56]</a></sup> A decision to close the Shell base was finally made in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcshell_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcshell-57">[57]</a></sup> The oil and gas industry is still a significant industry within the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yartoft_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-yartoft-58">[58]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gymi_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-gymi-59">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abpoil_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-abpoil-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>The town has attempted to develop itself as a centre for the development of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> in the east of England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scctran11_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-scctran11-61">[61]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc25mar05_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc25mar05-62">[62]</a></sup> The non-profit Orbis Energy centre has been set up to attract business in the green energy sector to the town and features <a href="/wiki/Passive_solar_building_design" title="Passive solar building design">solar thermal heating</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc26feb07_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc26feb07-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc26dec07_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc26dec07-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sworksorbis_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-sworksorbis-65">[65]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orbis_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-orbis-66">[66]</a></sup> In April 2009, <a href="/wiki/Associated_British_Ports" title="Associated British Ports">Associated British Ports</a> announced that the harbour is to become the operations centre for the 500 megawatt <a href="/wiki/Greater_Gabbard_wind_farm" title="Greater Gabbard wind farm">Greater Gabbard offshore windfarm</a> which, when completed, will be the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Offshore_wind_power" title="Offshore wind power">offshore windfarm</a>. The turbines will be located 15 miles (24 km) off the Suffolk coast and Lowestoft's Outer Harbour is being used to house the necessary operational support facilities. Other developments in the renewable energy sector include a prototype <a href="/wiki/Tidal_power" title="Tidal power">tidal energy</a> generator being produced by local company 4NRG<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc02feb11_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc02feb11-67">[67]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Wave_power" title="Wave power">wave power</a> systems developed by Trident Energy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc02nov10_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc02nov10-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Saver_Anglia_Credit_Union" title="Rainbow Saver Anglia Credit Union">Rainbow Saver Anglia Credit Union</a>, a savings and loans <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Co-operative" title="Co-operative">co-operative</a> established in 1999 and operating throughout <a href="/wiki/East_Anglia" title="East Anglia">East Anglia</a>, is based in the town. Hoseasons, the self-catering, UK Holiday specialist are also a large employer in the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retailing">Retailing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Retailing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The town centre is the main shopping area within Waveney district.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retail_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-retail-70">[70]</a></sup> Major retailers <a href="/wiki/Marks_%26_Spencer" title="Marks &amp; Spencer">Marks &amp; Spencer</a> have a store in the town. Chadds independent department store was founded in 1907 and after nearly 100 years trading on the high street and was taken over in 2004 by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth" title="Great Yarmouth">Great Yarmouth</a> based Palmers group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-palmers_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-palmers-71">[71]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edp17jan07_72-0"><a href="#cite_note-edp17jan07-72">[72]</a></sup> Specialist shopping areas, branded as The Historic High Street and the Triangle Market Place, have been developed on the northern edge of the centre, while a number of retail parks have been developed in the town.</p>

<p>Lowestoft is a traditional <a href="/wiki/Seaside_resort" title="Seaside resort">seaside resort</a>, first developing as a bathing site in the 1760s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edplow_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-edplow-11">[11]</a></sup> The coast has been branded the "<a href="/wiki/The_Sunrise_Coast" title="The Sunrise Coast">Sunrise Coast</a>". The town's main beaches are to the south of the harbour where two <a href="/wiki/Pier" title="Pier">piers</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Claremont_Pier" title="Claremont Pier">Claremont</a> and South piers, provide tourist facilities and the East Point Pavilion is the site of the tourist information service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edplow_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-edplow-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcaug07_73-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcaug07-73">[73]</a></sup> The beach south of the Claremont Pier is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_flag_beach" title="Blue flag beach">Blue flag beach</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc22may13_74-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc22may13-74">[74]</a></sup> Lifeguard facilities are provided during the summer and watersports take place along the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcaug07_73-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbcaug07-73">[73]</a></sup> Tourism is a significant aspect of the town's economy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scctran11_61-1"><a href="#cite_note-scctran11-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pleasurewood_Hills" title="Pleasurewood Hills">Pleasurewood Hills</a> Theme Park is situated on the northern edge of the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hills_75-0"><a href="#cite_note-hills-75">[75]</a></sup> In the west at <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Broad" title="Oulton Broad">Oulton Broad</a> boat trips and watersports on the <a href="/wiki/The_Broads" title="The Broads">Broads</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Waveney" title="River Waveney">River Waveney</a> are attractions, with companies such as Hoseasons operating hire boats from Oulton Broad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yartoft_58-1"><a href="#cite_note-yartoft-58">[58]</a></sup> To the south <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Africa_Alive" title="Africa Alive">Africa Alive</a> at <a href="/wiki/Kessingland" title="Kessingland">Kessingland</a> is a major attraction whilst <a href="/wiki/Pontins" title="Pontins">Pontins</a> operates a holiday park at <a href="/wiki/Pakefield" title="Pakefield">Pakefield</a> where 160 jobs were created in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yartoft_58-2"><a href="#cite_note-yartoft-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>A major attraction in recent years was Lowestoft Airshow, founded in 1996. The two-day event, which took place in August, featured a wide range of aircraft includingthe <a href="/wiki/Red_Arrows" title="Red Arrows">Red Arrows</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Avro_Lancaster" title="Avro Lancaster">Lancaster bomber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Supermarine_Spitfire" title="Supermarine Spitfire">Spitfires</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Avro_Vulcan" title="Avro Vulcan">Avro Vulcan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcair_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcair-76">[76]</a></sup> The event, which was run by Lowestoft Seafront Air Festival Ltd, a not for profit company, since 2004, had financial difficulties and made a £40,000 loss in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lj25feb11_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-lj25feb11-77">[77]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-evnews26jan11_78-0"><a href="#cite_note-evnews26jan11-78">[78]</a></sup> Further financial difficulties, made worse by bad weather and low visitor numbers in 2012, mean that the 2012 airshow was the last to take place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc25jul12_79-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc25jul12-79">[79]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-anglia25jul12_80-0"><a href="#cite_note-anglia25jul12-80">[80]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc14mar14_81-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc14mar14-81">[81]</a></sup></p>
<p>Near the town centre is <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_Maritime_Museum" title="Lowestoft Maritime Museum">Lowestoft Maritime Museum</a>, which is open from late April to late October each year and which includes exhibits of maritime artefacts, an extensive collection of ship models and medals, marine art, fishing and the fishing industry, activities with the Royal Navy in WW II, and shipwrights and coopers tools.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Redevelopment">Redevelopment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Redevelopment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>Lowestoft is one of the more socially deprived areas in Suffolk, with <a href="/wiki/Kirkley" title="Kirkley">Kirkley</a> the most deprived ward in the county ranking 173rd most deprived in England (out of 32,486).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wavprofile_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-wavprofile-37">[37]</a></sup> The area has attracted <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> redevelopment funding. The Waveney Sunrise Scheme invested £14.7 million in the town, including the transport improvements and the development of tourist facilities such as fountains on Royal Plain, in an attempt to stimulate the local economy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fountains_82-0"><a href="#cite_note-fountains-82">[82]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sccsunrise_83-0"><a href="#cite_note-sccsunrise-83">[83]</a></sup> Regeneration company 1st East, which focussed on the Lowestoft and <a href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth" title="Great Yarmouth">Great Yarmouth</a> areas, closed in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc27jan11_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc27jan11-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth_and_Lowestoft_Enterprise_Zone" title="Great Yarmouth and Lowestoft Enterprise Zone">Great Yarmouth and Lowestoft Enterprise Zone</a> was announced in 2011 and launched in April 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edp12jan12_85-0"><a href="#cite_note-edp12jan12-85">[85]</a></sup> The zone, developed by New Anglia Local Enterprise Partnership, has six redevelopment sites across Lowestoft and <a href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth" title="Great Yarmouth">Great Yarmouth</a>. The bid for the zone in 2011 aimed to create 13,500 jobs by 2036.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edp17aug11_86-0"><a href="#cite_note-edp17aug11-86">[86]</a></sup> It involved the Norfolk and Suffolk Energy Alliance and focussed on developing the energy sector initially using tax incentives, simplified planning regulations and the provision of improved broadband internet services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edp17aug11_86-1"><a href="#cite_note-edp17aug11-86">[86]</a></sup> The sites in Lowestoft are Mobbs Way, Riverside Road and South Lowestoft Industrial Estate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edp12jan12_85-1"><a href="#cite_note-edp12jan12-85">[85]</a></sup></p>
<p>The harbour is the focus for redevelopment proposals for Lowestoft in the form of the Lake Lothing and Outer Harbour <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Area_Action_Plan" title="Area Action Plan">Area Action Plan</a> submitted in February 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aap_87-0"><a href="#cite_note-aap-87">[87]</a></sup> The plan focusses on the redevelopment of <a href="/wiki/Brownfield_land" title="Brownfield land">brownfield sites</a> in and around the harbour area to create jobs, particularly in the renewable energy and retailing sectors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scctran11_61-2"><a href="#cite_note-scctran11-61">[61]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aapwhat_88-0"><a href="#cite_note-aapwhat-88">[88]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edp14oct10_89-0"><a href="#cite_note-edp14oct10-89">[89]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_community">Culture and community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Culture and community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Churchyard,_St._Margaret,_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_910808.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Churchyard%2C_St._Margaret%2C_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_910808.jpg/220px-Churchyard%2C_St._Margaret%2C_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_910808.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Churchyard%2C_St._Margaret%2C_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_910808.jpg/330px-Churchyard%2C_St._Margaret%2C_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_910808.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Churchyard%2C_St._Margaret%2C_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_910808.jpg/440px-Churchyard%2C_St._Margaret%2C_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_910808.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The town has two theatres, the <a href="/wiki/Marina_Theatre" title="Marina Theatre">Marina Theatre</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Seagull_(theatre)" title="The Seagull (theatre)">The Seagull</a> community theatre. Operated as a Charitable Trust, the 800 seat Marina was substantially restored and refurbished in 2012 and its cinema was upgraded to digital in 2013. A small four screen cinema, the independently owned East Coast Cinema, underwent a modest refurbishment in late 2011 to upgrade facilities and allow 3D films to be shown. <a href="/wiki/The_Beach_(radio_station)" title="The Beach (radio station)">The Beach</a> radio station broadcasts to Lowestoft and the surrounding area as does <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Suffolk" title="BBC Radio Suffolk">BBC Radio Suffolk</a>. The local weekly paper is the Lowestoft Journal which is part of the <a href="/wiki/Archant" title="Archant">Archant</a> group. The Royal Philharmonic Orchestra has been resident at the Marina Theatre since 2005.</p>
<p>Lowestoft Museum, which holds a collection of Lowestoft Porcelain as well as artifacts describing the town's history, is in Nicholas Everett Park in <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Broad" title="Oulton Broad">Oulton Broad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edplow_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-edplow-11">[11]</a></sup> A number of small museums are located in Sparrow's Nest park in the north of the town, including the <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_War_Memorial_Museum" title="Lowestoft War Memorial Museum">Lowestoft War Memorial Museum</a>, the Maritime Museum and the Royal Naval Patrol Service Museum. The Heritage Workshop Centre is also located in the park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lowj11feb11_90-0"><a href="#cite_note-lowj11feb11-90">[90]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Mincarlo_(trawler)" title="Mincarlo (trawler)"><i>Mincarlo</i></a> is the last surviving <a href="/wiki/Fishing_trawler" title="Fishing trawler">sidewinder trawler</a> of the Lowestoft <a href="/wiki/Fishing_fleet" title="Fishing fleet">fishing fleet</a> and can be visited at Lowestoft Harbour. The <a href="/wiki/East_Anglia_Transport_Museum" title="East Anglia Transport Museum">East Anglia Transport Museum</a>, which holds a collection of buses, trams and trolleybuses is located in <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Colville" title="Carlton Colville">Carlton Colville</a>.</p>
<p>Lowestoft retains a number of narrow lanes with steps running steeply towards the sea, known locally as "scores". These were used by fishermen and smugglers in the past and are now the site of an annual race which raises money for charity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edplow_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-edplow-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scoresrace_91-0"><a href="#cite_note-scoresrace-91">[91]</a></sup> The borough church is dedicated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Margaret%27s_Church,_Lowestoft" title="St. Margaret's Church, Lowestoft">St Margaret</a> and is a <a href="/wiki/Grade_I_listed_buildings_in_Waveney" title="Grade I listed buildings in Waveney">Grade I listed building</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-suffchurch_92-0"><a href="#cite_note-suffchurch-92">[92]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-listchurch_93-0"><a href="#cite_note-listchurch-93">[93]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lowestoft library, located in the centre of the town, contains a local history section and a branch of the Suffolk Record Office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sro_94-0"><a href="#cite_note-sro-94">[94]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_Hospital" title="Lowestoft Hospital">Lowestoft Hospital</a> provides community care for the elderly as well as other services. The main burial grounds for the town are <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_Cemetery" title="Lowestoft Cemetery">Lowestoft Cemetery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kirkley_Cemetery" title="Kirkley Cemetery">Kirkley Cemetery</a>.</p>
<p>The town is twinned with the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plaisir" title="Plaisir">Plaisir</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Yvelines" title="Yvelines">Yvelines</a> department in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele-de-France_(region)" title="Île-de-France (region)">Île-de-France</a> to the west of <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. It was formerly twinned with <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> town of <a href="/wiki/Katwijk" title="Katwijk">Katwijk</a> which is due east from Lowestoft on the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> coast.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ness_Point" title="Ness Point">Ness Point</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extreme_points_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Extreme points of the United Kingdom">most easterly location in the United Kingdom</a>, is located in the town close to a 126 metre high <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">wind turbine</a> known locally as Gulliver. At the time it was completed the turbine was the tallest in the United Kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gulliver_95-0"><a href="#cite_note-gulliver-95">[95]</a></sup> At the most easterly point is a large compass rose, the <i>Euroscope</i>, set in the ground which gives the direction and distance to various cities in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ness_96-0"><a href="#cite_note-ness-96">[96]</a></sup></p>
<p>Belle Vue Park is the site of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Naval_Patrol_Service" title="Royal Naval Patrol Service">Royal Naval Patrol Service</a> memorial. The central depot for the service was in Lowestoft when it was mobilised in August 1939 on a site known as <i>Sparrow's Nest</i> adjacent to the memorial. The memorial has the names of the 2,385 members of the service who died in World War II.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-memoriallist_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-memoriallist-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lighthouse">Lighthouse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Lighthouse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_Lighthouse" title="Lowestoft Lighthouse">Lowestoft Lighthouse</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lowestoft_Denes_lighthouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_229057.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="542" data-file-width="640" height="186" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Lowestoft_Denes_lighthouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_229057.jpg/220px-Lowestoft_Denes_lighthouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_229057.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Lowestoft_Denes_lighthouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_229057.jpg/330px-Lowestoft_Denes_lighthouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_229057.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Lowestoft_Denes_lighthouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_229057.jpg/440px-Lowestoft_Denes_lighthouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_229057.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p><a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_Lighthouse" title="Lowestoft Lighthouse">Lowestoft Lighthouse</a>, located to the north of the town centre, was built in 1874 and stands 16 metres tall, 37 metres above sea level. The light, which has a range of 23 nautical miles (43 km), was automated in 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trinityhouse_97-0"><a href="#cite_note-trinityhouse-97">[97]</a></sup> It is the most easterly <a href="/wiki/Lighthouse" title="Lighthouse">lighthouse</a> in the United Kingdom.</p>
<p>The first two lighthouses in Lowestoft were built in 1609, on the foreshore and candlelit, to give warning of the dangerous sandbanks around the coast. These were the first lighthouses constructed by <a href="/wiki/Trinity_House" title="Trinity House">Trinity House</a>. The Low Light was discontinued in 1706 following sea encroachment, but re-established in 1730 in a form that could be easily moved in response to further changes to the Stamford Channel and shoreline. It was finally discontinued in August 1923. The 'High Light' tower was rebuilt as the present lighthouse in 1874<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lightlist_98-0"><a href="#cite_note-lightlist-98">[98]</a></sup> with the intention of displaying an electric light, but when opened paraffin oil was used instead; it was not until 1936 that it was electrified. The lighthouse, along with two cottages originally used by lighthouse keepers, is a Grade II <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lightlist_98-1"><a href="#cite_note-lightlist-98">[98]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lifeboat_station">Lifeboat station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Lifeboat station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_Lifeboat_Station" title="Lowestoft Lifeboat Station">Lowestoft Lifeboat Station</a></div>
<p>Lowestoft Lifeboat Station is located at the mouth of the outer harbour at the South pier. The station is one of the oldest in the British Isles, founded in 1801, and is open to visitors throughout the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rnli_99-0"><a href="#cite_note-rnli-99">[99]</a></sup> The lifeboat is <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=RNLB_Patsy_Knight_(ON_1312)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="RNLB Patsy Knight (ON 1312) (page does not exist)">Patsy Knight</a></i>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shannon_class_lifeboat" title="Shannon class lifeboat">Shannon class lifeboat</a> which replaced the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tyne_class_lifeboat" title="Tyne class lifeboat">Tyne class</a> boat <i><a href="/wiki/RNLB_Spirit_of_Lowestoft_(ON_1132)" title="RNLB Spirit of Lowestoft (ON 1132)">Spirit of Lowestoft</a></i> in 2014. A former Lowestoft lifeboat was used during the <a href="/wiki/Dunkirk_evacuation" title="Dunkirk evacuation">Dunkirk evacuation</a> of British forces from France in 1940.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rnli_99-1"><a href="#cite_note-rnli-99">[99]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/South_Broads_Lifeboat_Station" title="South Broads Lifeboat Station">South Broads Lifeboat Station</a>, an inland <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RNLI" title="RNLI">RNLI</a> station, operated at <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Broad" title="Oulton Broad">Oulton Broad</a> between 2001 and 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rnliob_100-0"><a href="#cite_note-rnliob-100">[100]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Town_Hall">Town Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Town Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Lowestoft Town Hall stands on the High Street. Various forms of local government have met or been based on this site since the establishment of a Town House and Chapel here in 1570. In 1698 a new Town House was built, incorporating a '<a href="/wiki/Market_cross" title="Market cross">corn cross</a>' on the ground floor with the meeting chamber and chapel above. This in turn was replaced by the present building, designed by architect J. L. Clemence, in 1857.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-halllist_101-0"><a href="#cite_note-halllist-101">[101]</a></sup> The building houses the town clock and the curfew bell, which dates from 1644 and is rung each evening at 8pm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-102"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup> The building is a <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">Grade II listed building</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wdc2015hall_103-0"><a href="#cite_note-wdc2015hall-103">[103]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012 Waveney District Council announced that it planned to leave the town hall, and share <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County_Council" title="Suffolk County Council">Suffolk County Council</a>'s offices on Riverside Road. This took place in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wdc2015hall_103-1"><a href="#cite_note-wdc2015hall-103">[103]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Diesel_Multiple_Units_stand_at_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1460645.jpg"><img alt="Image trains at railway station" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Diesel_Multiple_Units_stand_at_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1460645.jpg/220px-Diesel_Multiple_Units_stand_at_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1460645.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Diesel_Multiple_Units_stand_at_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1460645.jpg/330px-Diesel_Multiple_Units_stand_at_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1460645.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Diesel_Multiple_Units_stand_at_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1460645.jpg/440px-Diesel_Multiple_Units_stand_at_Lowestoft_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1460645.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p><a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_railway_station" title="Lowestoft railway station">Lowestoft railway station</a>, originally known as Lowestoft Central station, is centrally placed within the town, within walking distance of the beach and the town centre. It provides services to <a href="/wiki/Ipswich" title="Ipswich">Ipswich</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Suffolk_Line" title="East Suffolk Line">East Suffolk Line</a> and to <a href="/wiki/Norwich" title="Norwich">Norwich</a> along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wherry_Line" title="Wherry Line">Wherry Line</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eastsuff_104-0"><a href="#cite_note-eastsuff-104">[104]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wherry_105-0"><a href="#cite_note-wherry-105">[105]</a></sup> Both lines were originally part of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Eastern_Railway" title="Great Eastern Railway">Great Eastern Railway</a> and are operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abellio_Greater_Anglia" title="Abellio Greater Anglia">Abellio Greater Anglia</a>. The suburb of Oulton Broad has two stations: <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Broad_North_railway_station" title="Oulton Broad North railway station">Oulton Broad North station</a> lies on the line to <a href="/wiki/Norwich_railway_station" title="Norwich railway station">Norwich</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Broad_South_railway_station" title="Oulton Broad South railway station">Oulton Broad South</a> is on the line to <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_railway_station" title="Ipswich railway station">Ipswich</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_North_railway_station" title="Lowestoft North railway station">Lowestoft North railway station</a>, which was originally operated by the <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_and_Suffolk_Joint_Railway" title="Norfolk and Suffolk Joint Railway">Norfolk and Suffolk Joint Railway</a>, closed in 1970 when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yarmouth%E2%80%93Lowestoft_Line" title="Yarmouth–Lowestoft Line">Yarmouth to Lowestoft</a> line closed. The site is now occupied by Beeching Drive, a residential area.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Buses_in_Lowestoft" title="Buses in Lowestoft">Buses in Lowestoft</a> are mainly operated by <a href="/wiki/First_Norfolk_%26_Suffolk" title="First Norfolk &amp; Suffolk">First Norfolk &amp; Suffolk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anglian_Bus" title="Anglian Bus">Anglian Bus</a> with Lowestoft bus station as the hub for routes. Buses link the town with Norwich and Great Yarmouth and provide public transport within the town and to surrounding villages. The X1 service operates a direct link to <a href="/wiki/Peterborough" title="Peterborough">Peterborough</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express Coaches</a> stop in Lowestoft on the route from London to Great Yarmouth.</p>
<p>The main <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A12_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A12 road (Great Britain)">A12</a> road from London to Great Yarmouth passes through the centre of Lowestoft, crossing the harbour in the centre of the town on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bascule_Bridge" title="Bascule Bridge">Bascule Bridge</a>. The <a href="/wiki/A146_road" title="A146 road">A146</a> links Lowestoft with Beccles and Norwich, with a second road crossing of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Lothing" title="Lake Lothing">Lake Lothing</a> at Oulton Broad on the A1117.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scctran11_61-3"><a href="#cite_note-scctran11-61">[61]</a></sup> Both bridges can be raised if vessels need to pass through the harbour and Lake Lothing and this can cause congestion in the town and routes can become gridlocked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scctran11_61-4"><a href="#cite_note-scctran11-61">[61]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc12sep07_106-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc12sep07-106">[106]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-highways_107-0"><a href="#cite_note-highways-107">[107]</a></sup> A third crossing of Lowestoft Harbour is proposed<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-highways_107-1"><a href="#cite_note-highways-107">[107]</a></sup> but has yet to receive planning or funding, although a southern relief road diverts traffic away from the seafront to help reduce congestion<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sccsunrise_83-1"><a href="#cite_note-sccsunrise-83">[83]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc27jun06_108-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc27jun06-108">[108]</a></sup> and a pedestrian and cycle bridge is planned to provide an alternative crossing alongside the Bascule Bridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc13oct11_109-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc13oct11-109">[109]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lowestoft's cycle network is generally fairly well developed, with routes focussing on linking areas to the town centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scctran11_61-5"><a href="#cite_note-scctran11-61">[61]</a></sup> Around 12% of residents cycle to work and the town is considered to be "ideally suited" to cycling due to its relatively small size and flat landscape.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scctran11_61-6"><a href="#cite_note-scctran11-61">[61]</a></sup> Suffolk County Council is aiming to promote cycling in the town by working with employers and schools as well as through the funding of the pedestrian and cycle bridge in the town centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scctran11_61-7"><a href="#cite_note-scctran11-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lowestoft has a number of primary and high schools, including four 11–16 high schools: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Benjamin_Britten_High_School" title="The Benjamin Britten High School">The Benjamin Britten High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ormiston_Denes_Academy" title="Ormiston Denes Academy">Ormiston Denes Academy</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Point_Academy" title="East Point Academy">East Point Academy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pakefield_School" title="Pakefield School">Pakefield School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sccschools_110-0"><a href="#cite_note-sccschools-110">[110]</a></sup> Following a reorganisation of schools in Lowestoft, all eight middle schools in the town closed in 2011 and Pakefield High School opened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sccsor_111-0"><a href="#cite_note-sccsor-111">[111]</a></sup> Post–16 education is provided at <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_Sixth_Form_College" title="Lowestoft Sixth Form College">Lowestoft Sixth Form College</a>, which opened in September 2011 as part of the school reorganisation, and <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_College" title="Lowestoft College">Lowestoft College</a> which provides a range of academic and vocational courses.</p>
<p>Lowestoft College provides a small range of higher education courses through an affiliation to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_Campus_Suffolk" title="University Campus Suffolk">University Campus Suffolk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ucslowestoft_112-0"><a href="#cite_note-ucslowestoft-112">[112]</a></sup> Degrees are validated by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_East_Anglia" title="University of East Anglia">University of East Anglia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Essex" title="University of Essex">University of Essex</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ucsvalid_113-0"><a href="#cite_note-ucsvalid-113">[113]</a></sup> The college also runs courses in boatbuilding and courses designed to support the offshore and maritime industries which are important employers in the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maritimecourses_114-0"><a href="#cite_note-maritimecourses-114">[114]</a></sup> Other adult education courses are run by the County council from a base at the town library.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-adulted_115-0"><a href="#cite_note-adulted-115">[115]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport_and_leisure">Sport and leisure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Sport and leisure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lowestoft has sport clubs and facilities. <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_Town_F.C." title="Lowestoft Town F.C.">Lowestoft Town Football Club</a> play at Crown Meadow and <a href="/wiki/Kirkley_%26_Pakefield_F.C." title="Kirkley &amp; Pakefield F.C.">Kirkley &amp; Pakefield Football Club</a> play at Walmer Road. Lowestoft Cricket Club play at the Denes Oval sport ground.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wdcdenessg_116-0"><a href="#cite_note-wdcdenessg-116">[116]</a></sup> Other sport clubs include Waveney Gymnastics club<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gym_117-0"><a href="#cite_note-gym-117">[117]</a></sup> and Rookery Park Golf Club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rookery_118-0"><a href="#cite_note-rookery-118">[118]</a></sup></p>
<p>The town's main <a href="/wiki/Leisure_centre" title="Leisure centre">leisure centre</a>, the Waterlane Leisure Centre, was redeveloped at a cost of £8million in 2010–11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc27aug09_119-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc27aug09-119">[119]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edp27may13_120-0"><a href="#cite_note-edp27may13-120">[120]</a></sup> Facilities include a gym and climbing wall as well as a 25 metre swimming pool with a movable floor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc27aug09_119-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc27aug09-119">[119]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sentinel_121-0"><a href="#cite_note-sentinel-121">[121]</a></sup> Lowestoft has a number of parks and recreation grounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wdcparks_122-0"><a href="#cite_note-wdcparks-122">[122]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/The_Broads" title="The Broads">The Broads</a> national park extends to Lowestoft on <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Broad" title="Oulton Broad">Oulton Broad</a>. Water activities and boat tours can be taken here. <a href="/wiki/Powerboating" title="Powerboating">Powerboat racing</a> takes place throughout the summer period, mainly on Thursday evenings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-powerboats_123-0"><a href="#cite_note-powerboats-123">[123]</a></sup> Fixtures are organised by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft_and_Oulton_Broad_Motor_Boat_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lowestoft and Oulton Broad Motor Boat Club (page does not exist)">Lowestoft and Oulton Broad Motor Boat Club</a> and can attract up to 1500 spectators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-powerboats_123-1"><a href="#cite_note-powerboats-123">[123]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc21jun13_124-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc21jun13-124">[124]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Norfolk_and_Suffolk_Yacht_Club" title="Royal Norfolk and Suffolk Yacht Club">Royal Norfolk and Suffolk Yacht Club</a> has its club house in <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Lowestoft" title="Port of Lowestoft">Lowestoft harbour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rnsyc_125-0"><a href="#cite_note-rnsyc-125">[125]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lowestoft&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Elizabethan pamphleteer <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Nashe" title="Thomas Nashe">Thomas Nashe</a>, one of the fathers of modern journalism and a primary source for the literary milieux of <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>, was born in Lowestoft in 1567. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Potter_(1721-1804)" title="Robert Potter (1721-1804)">Robert Potter</a>, poet and translator of Greek drama, was vicar of Lowestoft until 1804. The 19th-century writer and traveller <a href="/wiki/George_Borrow" title="George Borrow">George Borrow</a> lived at <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Broad" title="Oulton Broad">Oulton Broad</a> for many years and wrote most of his books there. Lieutenant General Sir <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Alderson" title="Edwin Alderson">Edwin Alderson</a> also lived at Oulton Broad, on a houseboat, and died in 1927 at the since-demolished Royal Hotel in Lowestoft, where he had been staying for the last month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lj17dec27_126-0"><a href="#cite_note-lj17dec27-126">[126]</a></sup></p>
<p>Admiral <a href="/wiki/John_Ashby_(Royal_Navy_officer)" title="John Ashby (Royal Navy officer)">Sir John Ashby</a>, who commanded <a href="/wiki/HMS_Royal_James_(1675)" title="HMS Royal James (1675)">HMS <i>Victory</i></a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battles_of_Barfleur_and_La_Hogue" title="Battles of Barfleur and La Hogue">Battles of Barfleur and La Hogue</a> in 1692, grew up in Suffolk and is buried in Lowestoft. A memorial to him is sited in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Margaret%27s_Church,_Lowestoft" title="St. Margaret's Church, Lowestoft">St Margaret's church</a> in the town. Vice Admiral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Richard_Dacres_(1788%E2%80%931853)" title="James Richard Dacres (1788–1853)">James Dacres</a> fought in wars against America in the 19th century and was born in the town. Captain <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Crisp" title="Thomas Crisp">Thomas Crisp</a> <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Cross" title="Victoria Cross">V.C.</a>, Royal Navy officer, was born in the town – one of the town's main roads is named after him.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Robert_William_Hook" title="Robert William Hook">Robert William Hook</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coxswain" title="Coxswain">coxswain</a> at <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Lifeboat_Institution" title="Royal National Lifeboat Institution">RNLI</a> Lowestoft from 1853 to 1883 and who has been credited with saving more than 600 lives during his career, with Lowestoft RNLI and with private companies. He was born in Lowestoft, lived and worked there all his life, and is buried in <a href="/wiki/Lowestoft_Cemetery" title="Lowestoft Cemetery">Lowestoft Cemetery</a>.</p>
<p>Sir <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samuel_Morton_Peto" title="Samuel Morton Peto">Samuel Morton Peto</a>, bought <a href="/wiki/Somerleyton_Hall" title="Somerleyton Hall">Somerleyton Hall</a> in 1843, and has one of the town's main roads named after him. He was influential in developing the town's railway links and harbour. <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Cockerell" title="Christopher Cockerell">Sir Christopher Cockerell</a>, inventor of the <a href="/wiki/Hovercraft" title="Hovercraft">hovercraft</a>, lived at Oulton Broad, and tested craft in <a href="/wiki/Somerleyton" title="Somerleyton">Somerleyton</a>. Economist <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Robertson_(economist)" title="Dennis Robertson (economist)">Sir Dennis Holme Robertson</a> was born in Lowestoft in 1890. He was educated on a scholarship at Eton, and read Classics and Economics at Trinity College, Cambridge before teaching at Cambridge University, working closely with <a href="/wiki/John_Maynard_Keynes" title="John Maynard Keynes">Keynes</a>.</p>
<p>The philanthropist Howard Hollingsworth, co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Bourne_%26_Hollingsworth" title="Bourne &amp; Hollingsworth">Bourne &amp; Hollingsworth</a> Department Store, visited Lowestoft in 1908 and subsequently bought and renovated the burnt out Briar Clyffe House and grounds on Gunton Cliff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-local2006_127-0"><a href="#cite_note-local2006-127">[127]</a></sup> He became a Lowestoft benefactor and, on the death of his friend Nicholas Everitt, bought his estate at <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Broad" title="Oulton Broad">Oulton Broad</a> and gave it to Lowestoft to be used as a public park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-na_128-0"><a href="#cite_note-na-128">[128]</a></sup> He was made the first Freeman of the Borough of Lowestoft in 1929.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-local2006_127-1"><a href="#cite_note-local2006-127">[127]</a></sup> Roland Aubrey Leighton, the fiancé of Vera Brittain imortalised in her WW1 autobiography <i>Testament of Youth</i>, lived with his family at Heather Cliff on Gunton Cliff.</p>
<p>The composer <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Britten" title="Benjamin Britten">Benjamin Britten</a> was born in Lowestoft in 1913. He has been described as "without a doubt the greatest English classical composer of the last century"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britten_129-0"><a href="#cite_note-britten-129">[129]</a></sup> and "the only person of real celebrity to have emerged from darkest Lowestoft."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-darkness_130-0"><a href="#cite_note-darkness-130">[130]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Benjamin_Britten_High_School" title="The Benjamin Britten High School">The Benjamin Britten High School</a> and a small shopping centre in the town are named after him.</p>
<p>The children's author and illustrator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Foreman_(author_/_illustrator)" title="Michael Foreman (author / illustrator)">Michael Foreman</a> was born in 1938 and spent his childhood years in <a href="/wiki/Pakefield" title="Pakefield">Pakefield</a>, where his mother kept the grocer's shop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-darkness_130-1"><a href="#cite_note-darkness-130">[130]</a></sup> He went to Pakefield Primary School, and played on Hilly Green – stories of which are recorded in his book <i>War Boy</i>. Photographer <a href="/wiki/George_Davison_(photographer)" title="George Davison (photographer)">George Davison</a> was also born in Lowestoft. Author of the Inspector Allen mysteries, Jayne-Marie Barker, grew up at Oulton Broad and has used Lowestoft as an inspiration for her books.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lowjournal28sep12_131-0"><a href="#cite_note-lowjournal28sep12-131">[131]</a></sup> The artist <a href="/wiki/Mark_Burrell" title="Mark Burrell">Mark Burrell</a> (born Lowestoft, 1957) has a studio in the town and frequently features Lowestoft architectural landmarks and local people in his paintings. Burrell is also a leading member of the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea_Magical_Realists" title="North Sea Magical Realists">North Sea Magical Realists</a>. Author <a href="/wiki/Mark_Dawson_(writer)" title="Mark Dawson (writer)">Mark Dawson</a> was born in the town.</p>
<p>The comedian and actor <a href="/wiki/Karl_Theobald" title="Karl Theobald">Karl Theobald</a> was born in Lowestoft as was BBC Radio 4 newsreader and television presenter <a href="/wiki/Zeb_Soanes" title="Zeb Soanes">Zeb Soanes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Westwood" title="Tim Westwood">Tim Westwood</a>, DJ and BBC radio presenter. Three of the founder members of the rock band <a href="/wiki/The_Darkness_(band)" title="The Darkness (band)">The Darkness</a> were educated in <a href="/wiki/Kirkley" title="Kirkley">Kirkley</a> and some of their songs feature landmarks or stories from the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-darkness_130-2"><a href="#cite_note-darkness-130">[130]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Chris" title="Lil' Chris">Lil' Chris</a> featured in <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_School" title="Rock School">Rock School</a></i> programme filmed at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kirkley_High_School" title="Kirkley High School">Kirkley High School</a> and went on to have a musical career. <a href="/wiki/Leanne_Mitchell" title="Leanne Mitchell">Leanne Mitchell</a>, winner of the first series of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_UK" title="The Voice UK">The Voice UK</a></i>, lives in the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lj1jun12_132-0"><a href="#cite_note-lj1jun12-132">[132]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sportspeople associated with Lowestoft include former England football captain <a href="/wiki/Terry_Butcher" title="Terry Butcher">Terry Butcher</a> who was educated in Lowestoft. Others born in the town include former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town" title="Ipswich Town">Ipswich Town</a> goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Laurie_Sivell" title="Laurie Sivell">Laurie Sivell</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwich_City" title="Norwich City">Norwich City</a> defenders <a href="/wiki/Paul_Haylock" title="Paul Haylock">Paul Haylock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daryl_Sutch" title="Daryl Sutch">Daryl Sutch</a>, former football player and manager <a href="/wiki/Richard_Money" title="Richard Money">Richard Money</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Les_Rohr" title="Les Rohr">Les Rohr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic</a> Bronze medal-winning middleweight boxer <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Ogogo" title="Anthony Ogogo">Anthony Ogogo</a>.</p>
