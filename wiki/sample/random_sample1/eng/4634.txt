<p>The fourth season of the American <a href="/wiki/Serial_(radio_and_television)" title="Serial (radio and television)">serial drama</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">television series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i> commenced airing on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">American Broadcasting Company</a> Network in the United States,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-1">[1]</a></sup> and on <a href="/wiki/CTV_Television_Network" title="CTV Television Network">CTV</a> in Canada on January 31, 2008, and concluded on May 29, 2008. The <a href="/wiki/Television_program#Seasons.2Fseries" title="Television program">season</a> continues the stories of a group of over 40 people who have been stranded on a remote island in the South Pacific, after their airplane crashed there more than 90 days prior to the beginning of the season. According to <i>Lost</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> executive producers/writers/<a href="/wiki/Showrunner" title="Showrunner">showrunners</a> <a href="/wiki/Damon_Lindelof" title="Damon Lindelof">Damon Lindelof</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Cuse" title="Carlton Cuse">Carlton Cuse</a>, there are two main themes in the fourth season: "the castaways' relationship to the freighter folk"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and "who gets off the island and the fact that they need to get back".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The fourth season was acclaimed for its flash-forwards, pace and new characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The season was originally planned to contain 16 episodes; eight were filmed before the start of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%932008_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike" title="2007–2008 Writers Guild of America strike">2007–2008 Writers Guild of America strike</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Following the strike's resolution, it was announced that only five more episodes would be produced to complete the season;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> however, the season finale's script was so long that network executives approved the production of a 14th episode as part of a three-hour season finale split over two nights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The fourth season aired Thursdays at 9:00 pm from January 31 to March 20, 2008, and at 10:00 pm from April 24 to May 15, 2008. The two-hour finale aired at 9:00 pm on May 29, 2008. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buena_Vista_Home_Entertainment" title="Buena Vista Home Entertainment">Buena Vista Home Entertainment</a> released the season on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray Disc</a> under the title <i>Lost: The Complete Fourth Season – The Expanded Experience</i> on December 9, 2008, in <a href="/wiki/DVD_region_code" title="DVD region code">Region 1</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> however, it was released earlier—on October 20, 2008—in Region 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Crew">Crew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lost_(season_4)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Crew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fourth season was produced by <a href="/wiki/ABC_Studios" title="ABC Studios">ABC Studios</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Robot_Productions" title="Bad Robot Productions">Bad Robot Productions</a> and Grass Skirt Productions. Damon Lindelof and Carlton Cuse served as the season's show runners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The show was primarily filmed in Hawaii with <a href="/wiki/Post-production" title="Post-production">post-production</a> in Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Lindelof and Cuse's fellow executive producers were co-creator <a href="/wiki/J._J._Abrams" title="J. J. Abrams">J. J. Abrams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Burk" title="Bryan Burk">Bryan Burk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Bender" title="Jack Bender">Jack Bender</a>. The staff writers were Lindelof, Cuse, co-executive producers <a href="/wiki/Edward_Kitsis" title="Edward Kitsis">Edward Kitsis</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adam_Horowitz_(screenwriter)" title="Adam Horowitz (screenwriter)">Adam Horowitz</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Drew_Goddard" title="Drew Goddard">Drew Goddard</a>, supervising producer <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Sarnoff" title="Elizabeth Sarnoff">Elizabeth Sarnoff</a>, co-producer <a href="/wiki/Brian_K._Vaughan" title="Brian K. Vaughan">Brian K. Vaughan</a> and executive story editor <a href="/wiki/Christina_M._Kim" title="Christina M. Kim">Christina M. Kim</a>. The regular directors were Bender and co-executive producer <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Williams_(director)" title="Stephen Williams (director)">Stephen Williams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lost_(season_4)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fourth season featured 16 major roles with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billing_(film)" title="Billing (film)">star billing</a>. The show continues to chronicle the lives of the survivors of the crash of <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_Airlines" title="Oceanic Airlines">Oceanic Airlines</a> Flight 815, including their interactions with the island's original inhabitants, whom they refer to as "the Others", and an inauspicious team from a nearby <a href="/wiki/Cargo_ship" title="Cargo ship">freighter</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matthew_Fox_(actor)" title="Matthew Fox (actor)">Matthew Fox</a> stars as Dr. <a href="/wiki/Jack_Shephard" title="Jack Shephard">Jack Shephard</a>, the leader of the castaways. <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Garcia" title="Jorge Garcia">Jorge Garcia</a> plays unlucky millionaire and mentally unstable <a href="/wiki/Hugo_%22Hurley%22_Reyes" title='Hugo "Hurley" Reyes'>Hugo "Hurley" Reyes</a>, one of Jack's fellow survivors of 815. <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Mitchell" title="Elizabeth Mitchell">Elizabeth Mitchell</a> portrays fertility specialist <a href="/wiki/Juliet_Burke" title="Juliet Burke">Juliet Burke</a>, a woman recruited by the Others who joins the 815 survivors in the third season and becomes involved in a love square with Jack, Kate and Sawyer. <a href="/wiki/Evangeline_Lilly" title="Evangeline Lilly">Evangeline Lilly</a> stars as fugitive <a href="/wiki/Kate_Austen" title="Kate Austen">Kate Austen</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Davies" title="Jeremy Davies">Jeremy Davies</a> plays <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Faraday" title="Daniel Faraday">Daniel Faraday</a>, a socially awkward physicist from the freighter. <a href="/wiki/Terry_O%27Quinn" title="Terry O'Quinn">Terry O'Quinn</a> plays <a href="/wiki/John_Locke_(Lost)" title="John Locke (Lost)">John Locke</a>, an 815 survivor with a deep connection to the <a href="/wiki/Mythology_of_Lost" title="Mythology of Lost">island</a>. <a href="/wiki/Josh_Holloway" title="Josh Holloway">Josh Holloway</a> portrays the sardonic 815 survivor <a href="/wiki/James_%22Sawyer%22_Ford" title='James "Sawyer" Ford'>James "Sawyer" Ford</a>. <a href="/wiki/Naveen_Andrews" title="Naveen Andrews">Naveen Andrews</a> acts as 815 survivor <a href="/wiki/Sayid_Jarrah" title="Sayid Jarrah">Sayid Jarrah</a>, a former soldier of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iraqi_Republican_Guard" title="Iraqi Republican Guard">Iraqi Republican Guard</a>. <a href="/wiki/Emilie_de_Ravin" title="Emilie de Ravin">Emilie de Ravin</a> portrays single new mother <a href="/wiki/Claire_Littleton" title="Claire Littleton">Claire Littleton</a> of Flight 815. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Emerson" title="Michael Emerson">Michael Emerson</a> acts as <a href="/wiki/Ben_Linus" title="Ben Linus">Ben Linus</a>, the leader of the Others. <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_Mader" title="Rebecca Mader">Rebecca Mader</a> acts as anthropologist <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Lewis_(Lost)" title="Charlotte Lewis (Lost)">Charlotte Lewis</a> from the freighter. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Dae_Kim" title="Daniel Dae Kim">Daniel Dae Kim</a> plays the non-English speaking <a href="/wiki/Jin-Soo_Kwon" title="Jin-Soo Kwon">Jin Kwon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yunjin_Kim" title="Yunjin Kim">Yunjin Kim</a> plays his pregnant wife <a href="/wiki/Sun-Hwa_Kwon" title="Sun-Hwa Kwon">Sun</a>. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ian_Cusick" title="Henry Ian Cusick">Henry Ian Cusick</a> plays <a href="/wiki/Desmond_Hume" title="Desmond Hume">Desmond Hume</a>, a man who has been living on the island for three years and who has developed the ability to time travel, though this is beyond his control. <a href="/wiki/Ken_Leung" title="Ken Leung">Ken Leung</a> portrays <a href="/wiki/Miles_Straume" title="Miles Straume">Miles Straume</a>, an arrogant <a href="/wiki/Mediumship" title="Mediumship">medium</a> from the freighter. <a href="/wiki/Harold_Perrineau" title="Harold Perrineau">Harold Perrineau</a> acts as Flight 815 survivor <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dawson_(Lost)" title="Michael Dawson (Lost)">Michael Dawson</a>, who returns aboard the freighter undercover for Ben as a deckhand, after escaping the island in <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_2)" title="Lost (season 2)">Season 2</a>.</p>

<p>The show regularly features guest stars. <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Fahey" title="Jeff Fahey">Jeff Fahey</a> plays the freighter's helicopter pilot <a href="/wiki/Frank_Lapidus" title="Frank Lapidus">Frank Lapidus</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Durand" title="Kevin Durand">Kevin Durand</a> acts as <a href="/wiki/Martin_Keamy" title="Martin Keamy">Martin Keamy</a>, the sinister leader of a group of <a href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">mercenaries</a> from the freighter that included <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Azizi" title="Anthony Azizi">Anthony Azizi</a>'s character Omar. <a href="/wiki/L._Scott_Caldwell" title="L. Scott Caldwell">L. Scott Caldwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sam_Anderson" title="Sam Anderson">Sam Anderson</a> return as 815 married couple <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rose_Henderson" title="Rose Henderson">Rose Henderson</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bernard_Nadler" title="Bernard Nadler">Bernard Nadler</a>. <a href="/wiki/John_Terry_(actor)" title="John Terry (actor)">John Terry</a> appears as Jack and Claire's deceased father <a href="/wiki/Christian_Shephard" title="Christian Shephard">Christian Shephard</a>. <a href="/wiki/Marsha_Thomason" title="Marsha Thomason">Marsha Thomason</a> returns as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Characters_of_Lost#Other_Kahana_crew_members" title="Characters of Lost">Naomi Dorrit</a>, the first person from the freighter to appear on the island and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Vann" title="Marc Vann">Marc Vann</a> plays Ray, the ship's doctor. <a href="/wiki/Alan_Dale" title="Alan Dale">Alan Dale</a> acts as <a href="/wiki/Charles_Widmore" title="Charles Widmore">Charles Widmore</a>, the man responsible for sending the freighter to the island. <a href="/wiki/Mira_Furlan" title="Mira Furlan">Mira Furlan</a> portrays <a href="/wiki/Danielle_Rousseau" title="Danielle Rousseau">Danielle Rousseau</a>, a marooned island inhabitant of sixteen years, who is reunited with her sixteen-year-old daughter, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alex_(Lost)" title="Alex (Lost)">Alex</a>, played by <a href="/wiki/Tania_Raymonde" title="Tania Raymonde">Tania Raymonde</a>; <a href="/wiki/Blake_Bashoff" title="Blake Bashoff">Blake Bashoff</a> plays Alex's boyfriend <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Characters_of_Lost#The_Others" title="Characters of Lost">Karl</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nestor_Carbonell" title="Nestor Carbonell">Nestor Carbonell</a> is <a href="/wiki/Richard_Alpert_(Lost)" title="Richard Alpert (Lost)">Richard Alpert</a>, the ranking Other while Ben is held in captivity. <a href="/wiki/Grant_Bowler" title="Grant Bowler">Grant Bowler</a> acts as Gault, the captain of the freighter, <i>Kahana</i>. <a href="/wiki/Fisher_Stevens" title="Fisher Stevens">Fisher Stevens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zo%C3%AB_Bell" title="Zoë Bell">Zoë Bell</a> play <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Characters_of_Lost#Other_Kahana_crew_members" title="Characters of Lost">George Minkowski</a> and Regina, two crew members involved with communications. <a href="/wiki/Lance_Reddick" title="Lance Reddick">Lance Reddick</a>'s character <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Characters_of_Lost#Off-island_characters" title="Characters of Lost">Matthew Abaddon</a> is introduced as a mysterious man with connections to Naomi, Hurley and Locke.</p>
<p>Former regular cast members return for guest spots. <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_David_Kelley" title="Malcolm David Kelley">Malcolm David Kelley</a> reprises the role of <a href="/wiki/Walt_Lloyd" title="Walt Lloyd">Walt Lloyd</a>, Michael's son, in both flashbacks and flashforwards. <a href="/wiki/Dominic_Monaghan" title="Dominic Monaghan">Dominic Monaghan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cynthia_Watros" title="Cynthia Watros">Cynthia Watros</a> appear in hallucinations as deceased survivors <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Pace" title="Charlie Pace">Charlie Pace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Libby_(Lost)" title="Libby (Lost)">Libby</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lost_(season_4)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lost_(season_4)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_for_Lost" title="List of awards and nominations for Lost">List of awards and nominations for Lost</a></div>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:25em; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"With … a clear finish line in 2010, the creative team could now focus on telling their story without having to worry about how many episodes they had left to work with. Season four is the first to benefit and delivers a faster paced and leaner story that expands the <i>Lost</i> universe in some unexpected ways and delves into the mystery that was introduced at the end of last season."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">— Reviews website <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i> named <i>Lost</i> the seventh best television series of 2008 and praised the fourth season for "complicat[ing] [<i>Lost</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span>] time-and-space-travel story deliciously".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Don Williams of <a href="/wiki/BuddyTV" title="BuddyTV">BuddyTV</a> dubbed "The Beginning of the End" "the most anticipated season premiere of the year"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ausiello" title="Michael Ausiello">Michael Ausiello</a> later called the final hour of <i>Lost</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> fourth season "the most anticipated 60 minutes of television all year."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> American critics were sent screener DVDs of "The Beginning of the End" and "Confirmed Dead" on January 28, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> gave the season a Metascore—a weighted average based on the impressions of a select twelve critical reviews—of 87,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> earning the second highest Metascore in the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_United_States_network_television_schedule" title="2007–08 United States network television schedule">2007–2008 television season</a> after the <a href="/wiki/The_Wire_(season_5)" title="The Wire (season 5)">fifth and final season</a> of <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Wire_(TV_series)" title="The Wire (TV series)">The Wire</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In a survey conducted by <i><a href="/wiki/TVWeek" title="TVWeek">TVWeek</a></i> of professional critics, <i>Lost</i> was voted the best show on television in the first half of 2008 "by a wide margin", apparently "crack[ing] the top five on nearly every critic's submission" and receiving "nothing but praise".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The May 7, 2007 announcement of a 2010 series end date and the introduction of flashforwards were received favorably by critics,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> as were the season's new characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lost_(season_4)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The fourth season was <a href="/wiki/60th_Primetime_Emmy_Awards" title="60th Primetime Emmy Awards">nominated</a> for seven <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Awards</a>, with won one, for Outstanding Sound Mixing for a Comedy or Drama Series (One-Hour). The series was nominated for Outstanding Drama Series, its second nomination in that category since the first season, while <a href="/wiki/Michael_Emerson" title="Michael Emerson">Michael Emerson</a> received his second consecutive nomination for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series. It also received nominations for Outstanding Cinematography for a One-Hour Series, Outstanding Music Composition for a Series (Original Dramatic Score), Outstanding Single-Camera Picture Editing for a Drama Series, and Outstanding Sound Editing for a Series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The season earned <i>Lost</i> two <a href="/wiki/Television_Critics_Association" title="Television Critics Association">Television Critics Association</a> Award nominations for "Program of the Year" and "Outstanding Achievement in Drama".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The season also was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Awards_2008" title="Writers Guild of America Awards 2008">2008</a> <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Writers Guild of America Award">Writers Guild of America Award</a> in the category of Dramatic Series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratings">Ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lost_(season_4)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Throughout the fourth season, <i>Lost</i> continued to slip in the ratings. The season premiered with 16 million American viewers, giving <i>Lost</i> its highest ratings in 17 episodes; however, the size of the audience steadily decreased throughout the season. The eighth episode, which served as the mid-season finale as a result of the writers' strike, brought in 11 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-ew-26">[26]</a></sup> setting a new series low.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The next episode and midseason premiere climbed slightly to 12 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-april_24_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-april_24-28">[28]</a></sup> but the episode after that set the current record for lowest-rated episode in the United States with 11 million people watching.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-may_1_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-may_1-29">[29]</a></sup> The finale was seen by 12 million, reaching the most viewers since the midseason premiere and making it <i>Lost</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> lowest-rated finale yet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Despite the decline in viewers, <i>Lost</i> consistently ranked within the top 20 programs of the week with one exception. The finale topped the chart, due to its broadcast being over a week after the official end of the television season. Entertainment president Stephen McPherson commented that while he would "love to see the show grow … the reality is that the numbers are pretty good."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ew_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-ew-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
