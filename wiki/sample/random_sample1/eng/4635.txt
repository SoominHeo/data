<p><b>Lawson</b> are an English <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a> band, consisting of Andy Brown (lead vocals, guitar), Ryan Fletcher (bass guitar, backing vocals), Joel Peat (lead guitar, backing vocals) and Adam Pitts (drums). The band's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Chapman_Square" title="Chapman Square">Chapman Square</a></i>, was released on 22 October 2012 and reached number three on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>. To date, the band have achieved seven UK top 20 hit singles. They are named after Liverpool-based surgeon Dr. David Lawson who performed life-saving surgery on Brown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heart1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-heart1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawson_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9312:_Early_career_and_breakthrough">2009–12: Early career and breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawson_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2009–12: Early career and breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band was formed in late 2009 and was earlier known as "The Groves" but they later changed their name to "Lawson" when Dr. David Lawson performed a life-saving brain surgery on the lead singer Andy Brown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heart1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-heart1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-2">[2]</a></sup> Lawson began recording material in early 2010. Acoustic versions of their early original material as well as covers of artists such as <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Mars" title="Bruno Mars">Bruno Mars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a> built up an online fanbase. They soon began playing gigs around the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Wireless_Festival" title="Wireless Festival">Wireless Festival</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and the Ultrasound Festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 8 September 2011, it was announced that the band had been signed to <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">Polydor Records</a>, with a lucrative two-album deal on the cards. After signing to a major label, Lawson were tasked with opening for acts such as <a href="/wiki/The_Wanted" title="The Wanted">The Wanted</a>, <a href="/wiki/Will_Young" title="Will Young">Will Young</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Avril_Lavigne" title="Avril Lavigne">Avril Lavigne</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Jessie_J" title="Jessie J">Jessie J</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The band toured extensively at the beginning of 2012, fitting in a headline tour of the UK in January<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and another in May<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> as well as a one-off intimate show at Monto Water Rats in London<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> which was streamed worldwide in April. During this time the band worked on material for their debut album with writers and producers such as <a href="/wiki/Carl_Falk" title="Carl Falk">Carl Falk</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Shanks" title="John Shanks">John Shanks</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313:_Chapman_Square">2012–13: <i>Chapman Square</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawson_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2012–13: Chapman Square">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Chapman_Square" title="Chapman Square">Chapman Square</a></div>
<p>The band's first official single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/When_She_Was_Mine_(Lawson_song)" title="When She Was Mine (Lawson song)">When She Was Mine</a>", was released in May 2012 and reached number four on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Following the single release, the band undertook their second headline tour, selling out venues in London, Glasgow, Manchester, Leeds, Nottingham, Sheffield and Birmingham.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Student_Magazine_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Student_Magazine-13">[13]</a></sup> The band's second single, "<a href="/wiki/Taking_Over_Me" title="Taking Over Me">Taking Over Me</a>", was released on in August 2012, peaking at number three in the UK. In the summer of 2012, Lawson performed at <a href="/wiki/T_in_the_Park" title="T in the Park">T in the Park</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> V Festival<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and alongside <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a> at Hard Rock Calling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> They also announced a Hometown Tour,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> playing dates from October through November in their respective home towns and London, where they reside now. In October 2012, the band released their third official single, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standing_in_the_Dark_(Lawson_song)" title="Standing in the Dark (Lawson song)">Standing in the Dark</a>", which reached number six in the UK. The band later revealed that their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Chapman_Square" title="Chapman Square">Chapman Square</a></i>, would be released on 22 October. Guitarist Joel Peat said of the album: "We're all so excited to release our debut album, we've been building up to it for the past four years, and the album's title for us is special, because it is the first place that we played together as a band. Going out on tour to support the album is amazing too. Doing shows in our home towns feels like the perfect way to celebrate. Performing live is what we love the most, so to be able to play our biggest venues to date in the cities where we grew up is very special." Lawson cited many inspirations for their debut album, including <a href="/wiki/John_Mayer" title="John Mayer">John Mayer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On 22 October 2012, Lawson released <i><a href="/wiki/Chapman_Square" title="Chapman Square">Chapman Square</a></i>, peaking at number four in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>. The album was later certified gold. The band finished the year performing at the Oxford Street Christmas Lights switch on with Robbie Williams<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-capitalfm.com_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-capitalfm.com-19">[19]</a></sup> and at the Capital FM Jingle Bell Ball where their performance was watched by Taylor Swift.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-capitalfm.com_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-capitalfm.com-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the beginning of 2013, Lawson played their first headline shows in the US and Canada selling out New York's <a href="/wiki/Bowery_Ballroom" title="Bowery Ballroom">Bowery Ballroom</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Troubadour,_West_Hollywood" title="The Troubadour, West Hollywood">The Troubadour</a> in LA and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Mod_Club" title="The Mod Club">The Mod Club</a> in Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In February, they embarked on the Chapman Square tour, playing 18 sold out shows in the UK and Ireland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> While working on new tracks for the repack of Chapman Square they visited South East Asia<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and Australia<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> as well as announcing numerous festival dates including T in the Park,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> V Festival<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and Hard Rock Calling<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> for the second year running and <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight_Festival" title="Isle of Wight Festival">Isle of Wight Festival</a> for the first time.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013:_Chapman_Square_.2F_Chapter_II">2013: <i>Chapman Square / Chapter II</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawson_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013: Chapman Square / Chapter II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Brokenhearted_(Lawson_song)" title="Brokenhearted (Lawson song)">Brokenhearted</a>", featuring American rapper <a href="/wiki/B.o.B" title="B.o.B">B.o.B</a>, was released as the lead single from the repackaged version of <i>Chapman Square</i> in July 2013, reaching number six in the UK. B.o.B flew over to the UK to perform Brokenhearted with the band at Wembley Stadium for the Capital FM Summertime Ball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The band announced their second single, "<a href="/wiki/Juliet_(Lawson_song)" title="Juliet (Lawson song)">Juliet</a>", while on tour in the US for the second time in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The track was released on 13 October 2013. British model and actress <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Brook" title="Kelly Brook">Kelly Brook</a> stars as the titular character in the video. The band were included in the line-up of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITunes_Festival" title="ITunes Festival">iTunes Festival</a> 2013, opening for Jessie J.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Lawson began their biggest headline tour to date, the "Everywhere We Go Tour", in September 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The repackaged version of their debut album titled <i>Chapman Square / Chapter II</i> was released on 21 October 2013. The reissue featured all twelve of the songs from standard edition of the <i>Chapman Square</i> (though not the full sixteen of the deluxe edition), plus six new songs. The deluxe edition of <i>Chapter II</i> includes a second disc including acoustic songs and a short tour film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315:_.22Roads.22_and_Lawson_EP">2014–15: "Roads" and <i>Lawson EP</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawson_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title='Edit section: 2014–15: "Roads" and Lawson EP'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, according to the updates at Facebook by Ryan Fletcher, he wrote "Lawson have been in <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> to do a photo shoot for their new album, and maybe to film a video... More news soon!"</p>
<p>On 2 February 2015 Lawson announced via Twitter that their first gig (after releasing the new album) would be at live at Leeds on 2 May 2015.</p>
<p>On 10 March 2015, Lawson announced the name of the lead single of their second album "<a href="/wiki/Roads_(Lawson_song)" title="Roads (Lawson song)">Roads</a>" after being played for the first time on in demand radio. Lawson then released a lyric video to "Roads" straight after play being posted to there YouTube channel. "Roads" was released on 24 May 2015.</p>
<p>The band also announced that their album would be titled <i>Perspective</i>, and stated it was due to be released sometime in 2015. This was delayed after band member Andy Brown was taken to hospital with liver failure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Perspective is now due to release on 8 July 2016.</p>
<p>In October, Lawson released a self-titled <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> globally, except in the US and Asia. It included their singles "<a href="/wiki/Roads_(Lawson_song)" title="Roads (Lawson song)">Roads</a>" and "Under the Sun".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The Australian/New Zealand release of <i>Lawson</i> coincided with their support of <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Williams" title="Robbie Williams">Robbie Williams</a>' Australian and New Zealand tour, and features the four tracks from deluxe version of <i>Chapman Square</i>, which was not released in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.93present:_Perspective">2016–present: <i>Perspective</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawson_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2016–present: Perspective">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On Twitter in January 2016 Lawson announced that their single "Money" was issued on 29 January 2016. Lawson also announced a video for the single. The music video for "Money" was released on 4 February 2016 before the single was released on 18 March 2016.</p>
<p>Lawson also announced the release of their second album for the summer of 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The name of the album was officially revealed in May 2016 on the band's Twitter as <i>Perspective</i>. The record was released on 8 July 2016.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Members">Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawson_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>Andrew Christopher "Andy" Brown</b> (born 8 May 1987 in <a href="/wiki/Fazakerley" title="Fazakerley">Fazakerley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tumblr_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-tumblr-36">[36]</a></sup> Andy is the lead singer and guitarist of the band. He is the oldest member of group and previously dated <a href="/wiki/Mollie_King" title="Mollie King">Mollie King</a> from <a href="/wiki/The_Saturdays" title="The Saturdays">The Saturdays</a>. He used to be in the boy band <a href="/wiki/Avenue_(band)" title="Avenue (band)">Avenue</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Max_George_(singer)" title="Max George (singer)">Max George</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Wanted" title="The Wanted">The Wanted</a>. He attended St Bede's High School, Ormskirk and Winstanley College, Wigan. He proposed to his girlfriend, Joey McDowall, in the band's new music video <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%22Where_My_Love_Goes%22&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title='"Where My Love Goes" (page does not exist)'>"Where My Love Goes"</a>.</li>
<li><b>Adam James Pitts</b> (born 24 December 1990 in <a href="/wiki/Brighton" title="Brighton">Brighton</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tumblr_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-tumblr-36">[36]</a></sup> is the drummer of the band. Adam is the youngest member of Lawson.</li>
<li><b>Joel Gilchrist Peat</b> (born 27 June 1990 in <a href="/wiki/Mansfield" title="Mansfield">Mansfield</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tumblr_36-2"><a href="#cite_note-tumblr-36">[36]</a></sup> plays lead guitar. He and Ryan attended the same university.</li>
<li><b>Ryan Gary Fletcher</b> (born 9 January 1990 in <a href="/wiki/Chesterfield" title="Chesterfield">Chesterfield</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tumblr_36-3"><a href="#cite_note-tumblr-36">[36]</a></sup> is the bass guitarist of the band. He and Joel attended the same university (ACM). Engaged to Alternative Model Dayna Winchester.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Digital">Digital</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lawson_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Digital">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In December 2013, the first official mobile game was launched for the band by SoshiGames of Birmingham, UK. Available across <a class="external text" href="https://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/clue-the-music-lawson/id779436119?mt=8" rel="nofollow">iOS (Apple)</a>, <a class="external text" href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.soshigames.ctmlawson" rel="nofollow">Android (Google Play)</a> and Facebook, the trivia game tests the player's knowledge of the band, the members and their music.</p>
