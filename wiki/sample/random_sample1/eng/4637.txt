<p><b>Royal Excel Mouscron</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgian</a> football club located in the town of <a href="/wiki/Mouscron" title="Mouscron">Mouscron</a>, in the western province of <a href="/wiki/Hainaut_(province)" title="Hainaut (province)">Hainaut</a>. The club was formed as Royal Mouscron-Péruwelz in the spring of 2010 as a result of the merging of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bankrupt" title="Bankrupt">bankrupt</a> club <a href="/wiki/R.E._Mouscron" title="R.E. Mouscron">R.E. Mouscron</a> and <a href="/wiki/R.R.C._Peruwelz" title="R.R.C. Peruwelz">R.R.C. Peruwelz</a> and was renamed in the summer of 2016 to Royal Excel Mouscron. The team plays in the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_First_Division_A" title="Belgian First Division A">Belgian First Division A</a> after winning the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Second_Division" title="Belgian Second Division">Belgian Second Division</a> promotion play-offs in the 2013–14 season.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Excel_Mouscron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the liquidation and break-up of <a href="/wiki/R.E._Mouscron" title="R.E. Mouscron">R.E. Mouscron</a>, whose registration number was removed by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Belgian_Football_Association" title="Royal Belgian Football Association">Royal Belgian Football Association</a>, the city of <a href="/wiki/Mouscron" title="Mouscron">Mouscron</a> began negotiations with leaders of <a href="/wiki/R.R.C._Peruwelz" title="R.R.C. Peruwelz">R.R.C. Peruwelz</a> about a possible merge. After weeks of hesitation and uncertainty, an agreement was reached. The agreement was officially signed and announced on March 11, 2010. Debts of about <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>100,000 in R.R.C. Peruwelz's name were cleared while the city agreed to maintain the training center known as "Futurosport". The newly merged club, Royal Mouscron-Péruwelz, took on the history of R.R.C. Peruwelz, receiving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matricule" title="Matricule">matricule</a> number 216 and keeping red and blue as its main colors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In its first season, it participated in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Promotion_A" title="Belgian Promotion A">Belgian Promotion A</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Promotion" title="Belgian Promotion">4th level</a> of Belgian football. After a strong season, they were promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Third_Division" title="Belgian Third Division">Belgian Third Division</a> for the 2011–12 season, playing in <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Third_Division_A" title="Belgian Third Division A">Division A</a>. They had reached Round 6 of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Belgian_Cup" title="2011–12 Belgian Cup">2011–12 Belgian Cup</a> before losing narrowly to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Pro_League" title="Belgian Pro League">Belgian Pro League</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beerschot_AC" title="Beerschot AC">Beerschot AC</a> on penalties 4–3. They finished first in their division in league play, and thus were promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Second_Division" title="Belgian Second Division">Belgian Second Division</a>. The next season Mouscron-Péruwelz finished 2nd and qualified for the promotion play-offs but lost. In the 2014 play-offs Mouscron-Péruwelz wound up 4th and qualified yet again; this time they won the play-offs, promoting them to the highest division of Belgian football for the first time in their history, bringing back top tier football to <a href="/wiki/Mouscron" title="Mouscron">Mouscron</a> after a 5-year absence.</p>
