<p><b>Rougned Roberto Odor</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈruɣneð oˈðor]</a></span>; born February 3, 1994) is a Venezuelan <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Second_baseman" title="Second baseman">second baseman</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB). He played for the team's minor league affiliates from 2011 until he was called up to the MLB team in May 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_family">Early life and family</span></h2>
<p>Odor is from <a href="/wiki/Maracaibo" title="Maracaibo">Maracaibo</a>, Venezuela.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-laverdad_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-laverdad-1">[1]</a></sup> His first name is a combination of his grandfather's name, Douglas, and his grandmother's name, Nedia; in keeping with the family custom of giving boys names that begin with the letter "R", the "D" in Douglas was changed to an "R", yielding Rougned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He has a sister<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grace_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-grace-3">[3]</a></sup> and a younger brother, Rougned José Odor, who signed with the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a> on February 19, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His uncle, Rouglas Odor, played for eight seasons with the <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">Minor League</a> teams of Cleveland and Milwaukee,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grace_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-grace-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and is now a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batting_coach" title="Batting coach">hitting coach</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Akron_RubberDucks" title="Akron RubberDucks">Akron RubberDucks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sign_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-sign-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Odor played in the <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Youth_Baseball_Championship" title="2009 World Youth Baseball Championship">2009 World Youth Baseball Championship</a>. He was named to the tournament's all-star team as a <a href="/wiki/Second_baseman" title="Second baseman">second baseman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allstar_team_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-allstar_team-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor_leagues">Minor leagues</span></h3>
<p>Odor signed with the Texas Rangers as an international <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agent</a> in 2011, receiving a $425,000 <a href="/wiki/Signing_bonus" title="Signing bonus">signing bonus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sign_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-sign-6">[6]</a></sup> The Rangers aggressively assigned him to the <a href="/wiki/Spokane_Indians" title="Spokane Indians">Spokane Indians</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_A-Short_Season" title="Class A-Short Season">Class A-Short Season</a> <a href="/wiki/Northwest_League" title="Northwest League">Northwest League</a>. He was the youngest player in the Northwest League, and had a .262 <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">batting average</a> on the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dallas_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-dallas-8">[8]</a></sup> Odor was suspended for four games after starting a brawl during a game with the Vancouver Canadians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bull_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bull-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2012, Odor batted .259 with the <a href="/wiki/Hickory_Crawdads" title="Hickory Crawdads">Hickory Crawdads</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_A_(baseball)" title="Class A (baseball)">Class A</a> <a href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_League" title="South Atlantic League">South Atlantic League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dallas_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-dallas-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>Odor began the 2013 season with the <a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Beach_Pelicans" title="Myrtle Beach Pelicans">Myrtle Beach Pelicans</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_A-Advanced" title="Class A-Advanced">Class A-Advanced</a> <a href="/wiki/Carolina_League" title="Carolina League">Carolina League</a>, where he had a .303 <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">batting average</a> in 99 <a href="/wiki/Games_played" title="Games played">games played</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-laverdad_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-laverdad-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mlb_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-10">[10]</a></sup> In August 2013, Odor was promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Frisco_RoughRiders" title="Frisco RoughRiders">Frisco RoughRiders</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_AA" title="Class AA">Class AA</a> <a href="/wiki/Texas_League" title="Texas League">Texas League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Odor's emergence with the Rangers allowed them to trade <a href="/wiki/Utility_infielder" title="Utility infielder">utility infielder</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leury_Garcia" title="Leury Garcia">Leury Garcia</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a> for <a href="/wiki/All-star" title="All-star">all-star</a> <a href="/wiki/Right_fielder" title="Right fielder">right fielder</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alex_Rios" title="Alex Rios">Alex Rios</a> that month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mlb_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-mlb-10">[10]</a></sup> He finished the season with a .305 batting average with 41 <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">doubles</a>, six <a href="/wiki/Triple_(baseball)" title="Triple (baseball)">triples</a>, 11 <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home runs</a>, and 78 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Runs_batted_in" title="Runs batted in">runs batted in</a> in 130 games for Myrtle Beach and Frisco. The Rangers named Odor their minor league player of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-springtraining2014_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-springtraining2014-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Rangers invited Odor to <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a> in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-springtraining2014_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-springtraining2014-12">[12]</a></sup> He began the year with Frisco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-promoted_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-promoted-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_leagues">Major leagues</span></h3>
<p>The Rangers promoted Odor to the major leagues on May 8, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-promoted_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-promoted-14">[14]</a></sup> On May 12, 2014, against <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Brad_Peacock" title="Brad Peacock">Brad Peacock</a>, Odor hit a single for his first major league hit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He hit his first <a href="/wiki/Grand_slam_(baseball)" title="Grand slam (baseball)">grand slam</a> on August 27, 2014, in a 12-4 decision against the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a>. Odor was the youngest player in the MLB during his rookie season and helped the Rangers' second base problem even though they finished 67-95, the third worst record in baseball that year. He finished the year hitting a .259 batting average, 9 homeruns, and 48 RBIs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Odor began 2015 as the Rangers' starting second baseman, but he struggled mightily, hitting .144 with 1 HR, 9 RBI, and 25 strikeouts to go with just 7 walks in 29 games, and he was optioned to Triple-A <a href="/wiki/Round_Rock_Express" title="Round Rock Express">Round Rock</a> on May 11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> However, upon his demotion, Odor found his stroke, beating up Triple-A pitching to a .352/.426/.639 slash with 5 HR, 19 RBI, and only 10 strikeouts to 12 walks in roughly the same amount of appearances. On June 15, following an injury to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Delino_DeShields,_Jr." title="Delino DeShields, Jr.">Delino DeShields, Jr.</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Odor was recalled to the Rangers, and in his return, promply went 3-3 with 2 RBI against the Dodgers. Rougned proceeded to hit very hot in the month of June, hitting .391 with 2 HR and 9 RBI and hit .319 with 5 HR and 16 RBI in July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He ended the regular season hitting .261, with 16 home runs, 61 RBIs, and 54 runs scored.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 15, 2016, Odor started another on-field brawl by punching <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Bautista" title="José Bautista">Jose Bautista</a> in the face during the eighth inning of a game against the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a>, after Bautista made a hard slide into Odor at second base during a double play. Odor first pushed and then swung at Bautista, hitting him squarely in the face.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joey_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Joey-21">[21]</a></sup> It is possible that this incident spilled over from the bad blood that resulted from <a href="/wiki/2015_American_League_Division_Series#Game_5.2C_October_14_2" title="2015 American League Division Series">Game 5 of 2015's</a> <a href="/wiki/American_League_Division_Series" title="American League Division Series">American League Division Series</a> between the Rangers and the Blue Jays, a game that included fans throwing trash onto the field and Bautista flipping his bat emphatically after hitting the Blue Jays' go-ahead home run.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Joey_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Joey-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Odor received an eight-game suspension from MLB two days later for his actions that resulted in the brawl.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> After an appeal, Odor's suspension was revised, eventually finalized at seven games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 9, 2016, Odor was charged with a throwing error which allowed the Toronto Blue Jays to score the winning run in the 10th inning of Game 3 of the ALDS, thus eliminating the Rangers despite holding the best record in the AL in 2016.</p>
