<p><b>Roy Michael Joseph Dupuis</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ʁɔj dypɥi]</a></span>) (born April 21, 1963) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian" title="Canadian">Canadian</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> best known for his role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counterterrorism" title="Counterterrorism">counterterrorism</a> operative Michael Samuelle in the television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Femme_Nikita_(TV_series)" title="La Femme Nikita (TV series)">La Femme Nikita</a></i>. He portrayed <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Richard" title="Maurice Richard">Maurice Richard</a> on television and in film and <a href="/wiki/Rom%C3%A9o_Dallaire" title="Roméo Dallaire">Roméo Dallaire</a> in the 2007 film <i><a href="/wiki/Shake_Hands_with_the_Devil_(2007_film)" title="Shake Hands with the Devil (2007 film)">Shake Hands with the Devil</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Dupuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dupuis was born in <a href="/wiki/Temiskaming_Shores" title="Temiskaming Shores">New Liskeard, Ontario</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French-Canadian" title="French-Canadian">French-Canadian</a> parents. From early infancy until he was 11 years old, Dupuis lived in <a href="/wiki/Amos,_Quebec" title="Amos, Quebec">Amos</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abitibi_Regional_County_Municipality,_Quebec" title="Abitibi Regional County Municipality, Quebec">Abitibi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. The next three years he lived in <a href="/wiki/Kapuskasing" title="Kapuskasing">Kapuskasing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, where he learned to speak English. His father was a travelling salesman for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canada_Packers" title="Canada Packers">Canada Packers</a>; his mother was a piano teacher. He has a younger brother and an older sister. When he was 14, after his parents divorced, his mother moved the family to Sainte-Rose, <a href="/wiki/Laval,_Quebec" title="Laval, Quebec">Laval</a>, where he finished high school. After high school, he studied acting in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/National_Theatre_School_of_Canada" title="National Theatre School of Canada">National Theatre School of Canada</a> (<i>L'École nationale de théâtre du Canada</i>), from which he graduated in 1986.</p>
<p>He lives southeast of Montreal, in an 1840 farmhouse located on 50 acres (20 ha) of land which he bought in 1996 and which he has restored and renovated. He enjoys sports, particularly hockey, sky-diving, and golf. His hobbies include <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a> (his interests in high school). He learned to play the <a href="/wiki/Cello" title="Cello">cello</a> as a boy and, at times, still plays, sometimes in dramatic roles. For the past few years, between television and film projects, he has been occupied with learning to sail; he owns a couple of sailboats, and he is custom-outfitting the larger aluminum-keeled vessel in preparation for extended ocean voyages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hampson_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hampson-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RDJ_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-RDJ-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Dupuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:RoyDupuis07TIFF.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="716" data-file-width="534" height="295" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/RoyDupuis07TIFF.jpg/220px-RoyDupuis07TIFF.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/RoyDupuis07TIFF.jpg/330px-RoyDupuis07TIFF.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/RoyDupuis07TIFF.jpg/440px-RoyDupuis07TIFF.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Dupuis is a celebrity in French-speaking areas of Canada and is also well known in anglophone areas due to his English-language and bilingual projects. He has performed in many theater productions, movies, and television series.</p>
<p>Among the stage roles that he has performed so far are: Luc in <a href="/wiki/Michel_Marc_Bouchard" title="Michel Marc Bouchard">Michel Marc Bouchard</a>'s <i>Les Muses orphelines</i> (<i>The Orphan Muses</i>), directed by <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Brassard" title="André Brassard">André Brassard</a> in 1985; Roméo in a Québécois adaptation of <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s <i>Romeo and Juliet</i> (<i>Roméo et Juliette</i>), directed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Guillermo_de_Andrea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Guillermo de Andrea (page does not exist)">Guillermo de Andrea</a> in 1989; and Jay in <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marc_Dalp%C3%A9" title="Jean-Marc Dalpé">Jean-Marc Dalpé</a>'s <i>Le Chien</i> (<i>The Dog</i>), Adrien in <a href="/wiki/Jeanne-Mance_Delisle" title="Jeanne-Mance Delisle">Jeanne-Mance Delisle</a>'s <i>Un Oiseau vivant dans la gueule</i> (<i>A Live Bird in Its Jaws</i>), and Lee in a Québécois version of <a href="/wiki/Sam_Shepard" title="Sam Shepard">Sam Shepard</a>'s <i>True West,</i> all three productions directed by <a href="/wiki/Brigitte_Haentjens" title="Brigitte Haentjens">Brigitte Haentjens</a>, in 1987-89, 1990, and 1994, respectively.</p>
<p>Dupuis gained popularity in Quebec as Ovila Pronovost in the series <i><a href="/wiki/Les_Filles_de_Caleb" title="Les Filles de Caleb">Les Filles de Caleb</a></i> (also known as <i>Emilie</i>) when it premiered on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T%C3%A9l%C3%A9vision_de_Radio-Canada" title="Télévision de Radio-Canada">Radio-Canada</a> (1990–92), and he co-starred as the journalist Michel Gagné in four seasons of <i>Scoop</i> (1991–95). He was introduced to the American public on television as Oliva Dionne in <i><a href="/wiki/Million_Dollar_Babies" title="Million Dollar Babies">Million Dollar Babies</a></i> (1994)--<i>Les jumelles Dionne: La véritable histoire tragique des quintuplées Dionne</i> (<i>The Dionne "Twins": The True Tragic Story of the <a href="/wiki/Dionne_quintuplets" title="Dionne quintuplets">Dionne Quintuplets</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In the United States, he also debuted on the big screen in such film roles as Becker in <i><a href="/wiki/Screamers_(1995_film)" title="Screamers (1995 film)">Screamers</a></i> (1995) and as John Strauss in <i><a href="/wiki/Bleeders_(film)" title="Bleeders (film)">Bleeders</a></i> (1996), also known as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hemoglobin_(film)" title="Hemoglobin (film)">Hemoglobin</a></i> (1997) in the UK. In 1997 he began appearing as Michael Samuelle in the television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Femme_Nikita_(TV_series)" title="La Femme Nikita (TV series)">La Femme Nikita</a></i>, also known as <i>Nikita</i>. Recently, he won a <a href="/wiki/MetroStar_Award" title="MetroStar Award">MetroStar Award</a> for his role as Ross Desbiens in <i>Le Dernier Chapitre: La Vengeance</i> (2003), the sequel to <i>Le Dernier Chapitre</i> (2002), both filmed simultaneously in dual-language versions broadcast in French and English on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio-Canada" title="Radio-Canada">Radio-Canada</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Canadian Broadcasting Corporation">CBC</a>, respectively. Dupuis's first appearance on film was in a 1987 short experimental work inspired by the 1926 avant-garde film <i>Anémique Cinéma</i>, by <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Duchamp" title="Marcel Duchamp">Marcel Duchamp</a> and <a href="/wiki/Man_Ray" title="Man Ray">Man Ray</a>, featuring the same title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Among Dupuis' film performances are Yves, in <i><a href="/wiki/Being_at_Home_with_Claude" title="Being at Home with Claude">Being at Home with Claude</a></i> (1991; Cannes, <a href="/wiki/Un_Certain_Regard" title="Un Certain Regard">Un Certain Regard</a> 1992)--his first major screen role—directed by <a href="/wiki/Jean_Beaudin" title="Jean Beaudin">Jean Beaudin</a>, adapted from a screenplay by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Johanne_Boisvert&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Johanne Boisvert (page does not exist)">Johanne Boisvert</a> based on the 1986 stage play by <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9-Daniel_Dubois" title="René-Daniel Dubois">René-Daniel Dubois</a>; and Kevin Barlow, in <i><a href="/wiki/Manners_of_Dying" title="Manners of Dying">Manners of Dying</a></i> (2004), the first feature film directed by <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Peter_Allen" title="Jeremy Peter Allen">Jeremy Peter Allen</a>, adapted from his own screenplay based on the short story first published in the 1993 collection <i><a href="/wiki/The_Facts_Behind_the_Helsinki_Roccamatios" title="The Facts Behind the Helsinki Roccamatios">The Facts Behind the Helsinki Roccamatios</a> and Other Stories</i> by <a href="/wiki/Yann_Martel" title="Yann Martel">Yann Martel</a>. His performance as Alexandre Tourneur in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%A9moires_affectives" title="Mémoires affectives">Mémoires affectives</a></i> (2004), directed by <a href="/wiki/Francis_Leclerc" title="Francis Leclerc">Francis Leclerc</a>, who co-wrote the screenplay with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marcel_Beaulieu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marcel Beaulieu (page does not exist)">Marcel Beaulieu</a>, has recently received awards.</p>
<p>In <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maurice_Richard_(film)" title="Maurice Richard (film)">Maurice Richard</a></i> (<i>The Rocket</i>), directed by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Binam%C3%A9" title="Charles Binamé">Charles Binamé</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9raphin:_Heart_of_Stone" title="Séraphin: Heart of Stone">Séraphin: Heart of Stone</a></i>) and released in late November 2005, Dupuis stars as French-Canadian <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> icon <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Richard" title="Maurice Richard">Maurice "Rocket" Richard</a>, who played for the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Canadiens" title="Montreal Canadiens">Montreal Canadiens</a> from 1942 to 1960 and whom he portrayed previously on Canadian television in 1997 and 1999. Dupuis' own experience playing hockey and his ability to perform on the ice on authentic period hockey skates were useful for this film, in which several professional hockey players were cast in supporting roles. The film was nominated for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jutra_Award" title="Jutra Award">Jutra Award</a> 2006 in fourteen categories, including Dupuis for Best Actor, but he did not win it. Leading the nominations for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genie_Award" title="Genie Award">Genie Award</a> in thirteen categories, it won nine of the twenty-two awards on the night of Tuesday, 13 February 2007, at the Carlu Event Theatre in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, including Best Performance by an Actor in a Leading Role for Dupuis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2005, Dupuis completed filming <i><a href="/wiki/That_Beautiful_Somewhere" title="That Beautiful Somewhere">That Beautiful Somewhere</a>,</i> based on the 1992 novel <i>Loon,</i> by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bill_Plumstead&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bill Plumstead (page does not exist)">Bill Plumstead</a> its executive producer, and both set and filmed on location in <a href="/wiki/North_Bay,_Ontario" title="North Bay, Ontario">North Bay, Ontario</a>. The film, directed by Robert Budreau, is produced by Lumanity Productions. Its world premiere was on August 26, 2006, at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Festival_des_Films_du_Monde_-_Montr%C3%A9al" title="Festival des Films du Monde - Montréal">Montreal World Film Festival</a> (24 August to September 4, 2006); it was presented at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cin%C3%A9fest" title="Cinéfest">Cinéfest</a> <a href="/wiki/Greater_Sudbury" title="Greater Sudbury">Sudbury</a>: International Film Festival (16–24 September 2006), at the <a href="/wiki/Calgary_International_Film_Festival" title="Calgary International Film Festival">Calgary International Film Festival</a> (September 22-October 1, 2006), and at other film festivals, as well as broadcast on Canadian pay cable television, before it was released commercially in Canada in April 2007.</p>
<p>On location in <a href="/wiki/Kigali" title="Kigali">Kigali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a>, in mid-June 2006, Dupuis began filming the dramatic feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Shake_Hands_with_the_Devil_(2007_film)" title="Shake Hands with the Devil (2007 film)">Shake Hands with the Devil</a></i>, in which he performs the principal role of Lieutenant-General <a href="/wiki/Rom%C3%A9o_Dallaire" title="Roméo Dallaire">Roméo Dallaire</a>, head of the United Nations Assistance Mission for Rwanda (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNAMIR" title="UNAMIR">UNAMIR</a>) during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rwandan_Genocide" title="Rwandan Genocide">Rwandan Genocide</a>. The film is based on Dallaire's autobiographical book <i><a href="/wiki/Shake_Hands_with_the_Devil_(book)" title="Shake Hands with the Devil (book)">Shake Hands with the Devil: The Failure of Humanity in Rwanda</a></i>. After two months in Kigali, filming continued in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halifax_Urban_Area" title="Halifax Urban Area">Halifax, Nova Scotia</a>, in August 2006. Prior to its release, a "draft of the film" was screened as a courtesy by the producer, <a href="/wiki/Laszlo_Barna" title="Laszlo Barna">Laszlo Barna</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Paul_Kagame" title="Paul Kagame">Paul Kagame</a>, the President of Rwanda, and his cabinet, who found it emotionally very moving.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBCSHWD_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBCSHWD-7">[7]</a></sup> The film was a "special presentation" at the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto International Film Festival</a> on 9 September 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VSRSTIFF_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-VSRSTIFF-8">[8]</a></sup> and opened the 27th <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Film_Festival" title="Atlantic Film Festival">Atlantic Film Festival</a> on 13 September 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <i>Shake Hands with the Devil</i> opened in theaters on 28 September 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stone_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stone-10">[10]</a></sup> For his performance as Dallaire, Dupuis won his second Jutra Best Actor award; in accepting it, "Dupuis dedicated his award to his mother, who died recently, as well as to Dallaire and the people of Rwanda."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CP_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-CP-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2006, along with <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Byrne" title="Gabriel Byrne">Gabriel Byrne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Plummer" title="Christopher Plummer">Christopher Plummer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Max_von_Sydow" title="Max von Sydow">Max von Sydow</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Susan_Sarandon" title="Susan Sarandon">Susan Sarandon</a>, he filmed <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emotional_Arithmetic_(film)" title="Emotional Arithmetic (film)">Emotional Arithmetic</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Barzman" title="Paolo Barzman">Paolo Barzman</a> and adapted by Barzman and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jefferson_Lewis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jefferson Lewis (page does not exist)">Jefferson Lewis</a> from the novel by Canadian writer <a href="/wiki/Matt_Cohen_(writer)" title="Matt Cohen (writer)">Matt Cohen</a> (1942–1999), who had written several drafts of a screenplay adaptation himself before his death. Dupuis plays Benjamin Winters, the "embittered" son of Melanie Lansing Winters (Sarandon) and her husband, David Winters (Plummer).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The film closed the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto International Film Festival</a> on 15 September 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gala_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gala-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Strauss_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Strauss-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In winter 2007, he participated in the improvisational short film directed by <a href="/wiki/Francis_Leclerc" title="Francis Leclerc">Francis Leclerc</a>, entitled <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Revenir_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Revenir (film) (page does not exist)">Revenir</a></i> ("Return"), conceived, filmed, and screened during the 11th edition of Festival Regard, a festival of short films, held in <a href="/wiki/Saguenay,_Quebec" title="Saguenay, Quebec">Saguenay, Quebec</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later in 2007 and 2008, Dupuis began working on several new film projects, including: as Charles in <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Truffe_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Truffe (film) (page does not exist)">Truffe</a></i> ("Truffle"), directed by <a href="/wiki/Kim_Nguyen" title="Kim Nguyen">Kim Nguyen</a>, produced by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ren%C3%A9e_Gosselin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Renée Gosselin (page does not exist)">Renée Gosselin</a> and distributed by Christal Films, whose world premiere opens the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fantasia_Film_Festival" title="Fantasia Film Festival">Fantasia Film Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> on July 3, 2008;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KellyFant_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-KellyFant-17">[17]</a></sup> as Jean-Paul Mercier in "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=L%27Instinct_de_Mort_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="L'Instinct de Mort (film) (page does not exist)">L'Instinct de Mort</a>" ("Mesrine: Killer Instinct"), part 1 of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_Number_One" title="Public Enemy Number One">Public Enemy Number One</a></i>, a two-part <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature film</a> about notorious French gangster <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Mesrine" title="Jacques Mesrine">Jacques Mesrine</a>, played by <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Cassel" title="Vincent Cassel">Vincent Cassel</a>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Fran%C3%A7ois_Richet" title="Jean-François Richet">Jean-François Richet</a>; as Mr. Turcotte in "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Un_%C3%A9t%C3%A9_sans_point_ni_coup_s%C3%BBr_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Un été sans point ni coup sûr (film) (page does not exist)">Un été sans point ni coup sûr</a>" ("A No-Hit No-Run Summer"), a baseball <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature film</a> set at the beginning of the 1960s adapted from the novel of that title by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marc_Robitaille&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marc Robitaille (page does not exist)">Marc Robitaille</a>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Francis_Leclerc" title="Francis Leclerc">Francis Leclerc</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> as Scully in "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Timekeeper_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Timekeeper (film) (page does not exist)">The Timekeeper</a>", an English-language <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature film</a> directed by Louis Bélanger;,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> as Irishman Liam Hennessy in <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Forcier" title="André Forcier">André Forcier</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Je_me_souviens" title="Je me souviens">Je me souviens</a></i> and as another character named Charles in "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Les_doigts_croches_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Les doigts croches (film) (page does not exist)">Les doigts croches</a>" (2008), directed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ken_Scott_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ken Scott (director) (page does not exist)">Ken Scott</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PRCV_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-PRCV-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 18, 2008, after fourteen years, Dupuis returned to the stage for a limited run as Ian in a French translation of <i><a href="/wiki/Blasted" title="Blasted">Blasted</a></i>, the controversial first play by British playwright <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Kane" title="Sarah Kane">Sarah Kane</a> (1971–1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marc_Dalp%C3%A9" title="Jean-Marc Dalpé">Jean-Marc Dalpé</a>'s French version, <i>Blasté</i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Brigitte_Haentjens" title="Brigitte Haentjens">Brigitte Haentjens</a> for her company Sybillines Inc., also featured <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9line_Bonnier" title="Céline Bonnier">Céline Bonnier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Ahmarani" title="Paul Ahmarani">Paul Ahmarani</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civic_and_philanthropic_activities">Civic and philanthropic activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Dupuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Civic and philanthropic activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dupuis is co-founder and president of the <a href="/wiki/Rivers_Foundation_(Canada)" title="Rivers Foundation (Canada)">Rivers Foundation</a> (Fondation Rivières), an ecological organisation that protects the rivers of Quebec and their natural and cultural habitats from small hydroelectric dam projects and other environmental and economic threats, and to encourage, through education, the development of alternative energy sources.</p>
