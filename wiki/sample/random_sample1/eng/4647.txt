<p><b>Royce' Confect Co., Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">株式会社ロイズコンフェクト</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, also known as <b>E-Royce',</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Chocolate" title="Chocolate">chocolate</a> manufacturing company. The company's central offices are located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sapporo,_Hokkaido" title="Sapporo, Hokkaido">Sapporo</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_history">Company history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royce%27&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Company history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Royce' was first established in July 1983, with a total capital of ¥10.0 million. Royce's first head office building was located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hokkaid%C5%8D" title="Hokkaidō">Hokkaidō</a>, about 500 miles north of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyo,_Japan" title="Tokyo, Japan">Tokyo</a>. By November of that year, Royce' began to produce their first chocolates.</li>
<li>The company's first two years saw rather slow growth as it integrated into the Japanese economy. It was not until the 1990s that Royce' would become a significant player in the <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> candy industry. In September 1985, Royce's capital had increased to ¥30.5 million.</li>
<li>In September 1989, Royce' was finally able to add a chocolate molding line, allowing the company to further compete with other market brands.</li>
<li>The first Royce' shop was established in May 1993. It was named the "Higashi Naebo Main Shop". This shop, like others to come, would sell Royce' chocolates, as well as company and area-related souvenirs. Royce' <a href="/wiki/Cookie" title="Cookie">cookie</a> production also began in 1993.</li>
<li>1994 and 1995 saw major store expansion for Royce' in Japan. Outlets were established in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hiragishi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hiragishi (page does not exist)">Hiragishi</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Asabu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Asabu (page does not exist)">Asabu</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kotoni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kotoni (page does not exist)">Kotoni</a>.</li>
<li>A delivery center was established in 1996, as well as the "Nama" chocolate brand. The delivery center was served by a fleet of semi-<a href="/wiki/Truck" title="Truck">trucks</a> that took Royce's products to clients.</li>
<li>March 1997 was a relatively important year for Royce', as the company opened a sales shop in downtown Sapporo, thereby establishing its first contact with the city where they are currently headquartered. The Royce' sales shop was established inside a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mitsukoshi_Department_Stores&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mitsukoshi Department Stores (page does not exist)">Mitsukoshi department store</a>.</li>
<li>In June of the same year, with the Sapporo public already familiar with the Royce' brand, the company moved their headquarters to Sapporo, in the <a href="/wiki/Higashi-ku,_Sapporo" title="Higashi-ku, Sapporo">Higashi-ku</a> district. The Truffle chocolate brand was later established, and a new distribution center was inaugurated in November. Royce' began selling its chocolates in Hokkaidō area <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airports</a>, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chitose_Airport" title="Chitose Airport">Chitose Airport</a>.</li>
<li>By June 1998, Royce' had established an <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">internet</a> web page, using a <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computer</a> system named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LAN" title="LAN">LAN</a>. The next month, the company announced that its capital had risen to slightly more than twice the amount from the previous announcement, or to ¥61 million. Another store was opened, this time in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shopping_center" title="Shopping center">shopping center</a>.</li>
<li>A new manufacturing facility was inaugurated by Royce', in July 1999, at Futomi. Another store opened, this one located at the Sapporo Odori. Just one month before, Royce' had inaugurated a store at their Sapporo factory, naming it the "Sapporo Factory Shopping Center".</li>
<li>In September of that same year, the company moved its headquarters for the third time, but stayed within Sapporo.</li>
<li>In December 2000, Royce' launched an online store. By that year, the company had begun an advertisement campaign on <a href="/wiki/All_Nippon_Airways" title="All Nippon Airways">All Nippon Airways</a>' inflight magazine.</li>
<li>Three more Sapporo stores were inaugurated in July 2001, each located inside well known Japanese department stores.</li>
<li>Yet another store inside a Sapporo department store, the <a href="/wiki/Robinson_Department_Store" title="Robinson Department Store">Robinson Department Store</a> Sapporo branch, was opened in April 2002. By then, Royce' also began to sell <a href="/wiki/Bakery" title="Bakery">bakery</a> products.</li>
<li>Another shop is opened in Busan, feb. 2012</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_stores">International stores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royce%27&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International stores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Royce' Confect International PTE Ltd. was established in August 2001, with the purpose of serving international, albeit mostly Asian, customers. Royce's first international store opened that month, in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>.</p>
<p>Royce' currently operates over 40 international stores in 12 countries outside Japan, including in chronological order:</p>
<ul>
<li>Singapore - First opened 2001 in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Takashimaya_Shopping_Centre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Takashimaya Shopping Centre (page does not exist)">Takashimaya Shopping Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ngee_Ann_City" title="Ngee Ann City">Ngee Ann City</a>. Currently five outlets.</li>
<li>Hong Kong - First opened March 2002 in <a href="/wiki/Times_Square_(Hong_Kong)" title="Times Square (Hong Kong)">Times Square</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harbour_City_(Hong_Kong)" title="Harbour City (Hong Kong)">Harbour City</a>, after experimental sales at City'super Times Square. Currently six outlets, including three on the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Island" title="Hong Kong Island">Hong Kong Island</a>, and one each in <a href="/wiki/Kowloon" title="Kowloon">Kowloon</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Kowloon" title="New Kowloon">New Kowloon</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/New_Territories" title="New Territories">New Territories</a>.</li>
<li>Malaysia - First opened December 2005 in <a href="/wiki/Suria_KLCC" title="Suria KLCC">Suria KLCC</a>. Currently six outlets in the <a href="/wiki/Klang_Valley" title="Klang Valley">Klang Valley</a>.</li>
<li>Thailand - First opened March 2007 in <a href="/wiki/CentralWorld" title="CentralWorld">CentralWorld</a>. Currently four outlets in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>.</li>
<li>Philippines - First opened December 2008 in the Power Plant Mall at the <a href="/wiki/Rockwell_Center" title="Rockwell Center">Rockwell Center</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Makati_City" title="Makati City">Makati City</a> Currently six outlets in <a href="/wiki/Metro_Manila" title="Metro Manila">Metro Manila</a>.</li>
<li>China - Four outlets in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>. First shop opened in 2010 in Shanghai IFC.</li>
<li>Brunei - First opened August 2011 in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mabohai_Shopping_Complex&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mabohai Shopping Complex (page does not exist)">Mabohai Shopping Complex</a>. Currently three outlets.</li>
<li>Indonesia - First and only outlet opened October 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Plaza_Indonesia" title="Plaza Indonesia">Plaza Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>.</li>
<li>Russia - First opened November 2011 in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=City_Mall,_Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="City Mall, Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk (page does not exist)">City Mall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk" title="Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk">Yuzhno-Sakhalinsk</a>. Currently two other outlets in <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a>.</li>
<li>Taiwan - First opened December 2011 in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mall,_Taipei&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Mall, Taipei (page does not exist)">The Mall</a>. Currently three outlets in <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a> and one each in <a href="/wiki/Taichung" title="Taichung">Taichung</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hsinchu" title="Hsinchu">Hsinchu</a></li>
<li>South Korea - First opened January 2012 in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Metro_Heights_Jung-dong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Metro Heights Jung-dong (page does not exist)">Metro Heights Jung-dong</a>. Currently one outlet in Busan and two in Seoul.</li>
<li>United States - First opened December 2012 in <a href="/wiki/Madison_Avenue" title="Madison Avenue">Madison Avenue</a>. Currently three outlets in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.</li>
<li>India - First outlet opened July 2013 in <a href="/wiki/High_Street_Phoenix" title="High Street Phoenix">High Street Phoenix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> in Palladium Mall. There is another shop in JW Marriott Mumbai. It also operates a store in JW Marriott Bangalore, a store in Ambience Mall, New Delhi, and a final store in <a href="/wiki/Quest_Mall" title="Quest Mall">Quest Mall</a>, Kolkata.</li>
<li>Australia - First outlet opened December 2013 in <a href="/wiki/Westfield_Marion" title="Westfield Marion">Westfield Marion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adelaide" title="Adelaide">Adelaide</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Westfield_Chatswood" title="Westfield Chatswood">Westfield Chatswood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a> in 2015.</li>
<li>United Arab Emirates - First opened February 2014 inside Galeries Lafayette in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dubai_Mall" title="Dubai Mall">Dubai Mall</a> - <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>. After tremendous support from the chocolate lovers in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE" title="UAE">UAE</a>, the shop expands and now currently have 2 more outlets in Sunset Mall, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jumeirah_Beach_Road" title="Jumeirah Beach Road">Jumeirah Beach Road</a> and World Trade Centre Mall in <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> All chocolates sold from UAE branches are liquor-free.</li>
</ul>
<p>- Canada - The first store opened January 2016, now located in Richmond, British Columbia, specifically <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Richmond_Center_Mall&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Richmond Center Mall (page does not exist)">Richmond Center Mall</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diversification_in_other_business">Diversification in other business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royce%27&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Diversification in other business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2005, Royce' branched out into other businesses, such as selling other types of <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">foods</a> and drinking <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>, food and beverage processing machines, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liquor" title="Liquor">liquor</a> sales (importing and exporting), <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> brands, <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">restaurant</a> management, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arts" title="Arts">arts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Craft" title="Craft">craft</a> sales, and <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">insurance</a> sales.</p>
