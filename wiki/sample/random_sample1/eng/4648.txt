<p><b>Rozanna Purcell</b> (born September 3, 1990) is an <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> model, chef, charity worker and beauty pageant titleholder who won <a href="/wiki/Miss_Universe_Ireland" title="Miss Universe Ireland">Miss Universe Ireland</a> 2010 and represented <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a> at <a href="/wiki/Miss_Universe_2010" title="Miss Universe 2010">Miss Universe 2010</a> in <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">USA</a> where she placed Top 10.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Miss_Universe">Miss Universe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rozanna_Purcell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Miss Universe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rozanna Purcell competed in Miss Universe Ireland 2009 and was 1st Runner-up to Diana Donnelly. The following year, 2010, Purcell became the first Miss Universe Ireland to place since 1998, after being called among the Top 15. Purcell competed in the swimsuit segment and placed 4th with an average score of 8.784. Purcell then competed in evening gown with a score of 8.548. Despite being confirmed as a favourite of Pageant Experts and Blogs across the globe to become Ireland's first Miss Universe, Purcell failed to advance to the Top 5, finishing in 7th place.[1]</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_Miss_Universe">After Miss Universe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rozanna_Purcell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: After Miss Universe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Purcell was offered a contract with Trump Model Management after the <a href="/wiki/Miss_Universe_2010" title="Miss Universe 2010">Miss Universe 2010</a> competition ended, and was offered additional contracts in Mexico and Colombia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She was then invited to judge at the <a href="/wiki/Miss_Venezuela_2010" title="Miss Venezuela 2010">Miss Venezuela 2010</a> pageant. She later joined her mentor Andrea Roche's modeling agency in Ireland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has talked of her difficulties at temperatures of minus 25C on Kilimanjaro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was a judge at Miss Universe Ireland 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>She published her first cookbook <i>Natural Born Feeder</i> in January of 2016.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_work">Television work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rozanna_Purcell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She won <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Come_Dine_With_Me_(Ireland)" title="Come Dine With Me (Ireland)">Come Dine With Me (Ireland)</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/TV3_(Ireland)" title="TV3 (Ireland)">TV3</a> in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>She participated in <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Bainisteoir" title="Celebrity Bainisteoir">Celebrity Bainisteoir</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RT%C3%89" title="RTÉ">RTÉ</a> in 2012. She managed Newtown Cashel, in <a href="/wiki/Longford" title="Longford">Longford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has modeled extensively for <a href="/wiki/Newbridge_Silverware" title="Newbridge Silverware">Newbridge Silverware</a> and is one of the faces of Newbridge Silverware.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2015 She appeared as the guest Celebrity Chef on TV3’s The Restaurant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity_work">Charity work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rozanna_Purcell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Charity work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She has worked with the Children's Hospital of Puerto Rico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has also helped to raise awareness and funds for the Irish Cancer Society's 'Get Active For 2015' campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rozanna_Purcell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Purcell was dating Irish musician <a href="/wiki/Niall_Breslin" title="Niall Breslin">Niall Breslin</a> since 2013 although they announced the relationship had ended in April 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In October 2013 she started her own food blog <a class="external text" href="http://naturalbornfeeder.com" rel="nofollow">naturalbornfeeder.com</a> and plans to publish her first cookbook in December 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
