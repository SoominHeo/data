<p><b>Roger Dean Stadium</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baseball_stadium" title="Baseball stadium">baseball stadium</a> located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abacoa" title="Abacoa">Abacoa</a> community of the town of <a href="/wiki/Jupiter,_Florida" title="Jupiter, Florida">Jupiter, Florida</a>. The stadium was built in 1998, holds 6,871 people,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Roger_Dean_Stadium_seating_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Roger_Dean_Stadium_seating-2">[2]</a></sup> and features <a href="/wiki/Luxury_box" title="Luxury box">luxury sky-box seating</a>, two levels of permanent seating, parking and concessions. The Roger Dean Stadium Complex is the only stadium in the country to host four <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">minor league</a> teams: the <a href="/wiki/Jupiter_Hammerheads" title="Jupiter Hammerheads">Jupiter Hammerheads</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_Cardinals" title="Palm Beach Cardinals">Palm Beach Cardinals</a> of the Class A-Advanced Baseball <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_League" title="Florida State League">Florida State League</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast_Marlins" title="Gulf Coast Marlins">Gulf Coast Marlins</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast_Cardinals" title="Gulf Coast Cardinals">Gulf Coast Cardinals</a> of the Rookie-level <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast_League" title="Gulf Coast League">Gulf Coast League</a>.</p>
<p>Roger Dean Stadium is one of the only 2 stadiums in Florida to host two <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> teams annually for <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Marlins" title="Miami Marlins">Miami Marlins</a> and <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a> (the other will be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ballpark_of_The_Palm_Beaches" title="The Ballpark of The Palm Beaches">The Ballpark of The Palm Beaches</a>, hosting the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals" title="Washington Nationals">Washington Nationals</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> beginning in 2017). The teams share the main stadium where the games are played. However, the teams have their own practice fields, outdoor <a href="/wiki/Batting_cage" title="Batting cage">batting cages</a>, several <a href="/wiki/Baseball_field#Pitcher.27s_mound" title="Baseball field">pitching mounds</a>, and state-of-the-art conditioning rooms.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Dean_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Through 2002 the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a> shared the stadium with the Cardinals, until they swapped with the Marlins as part of the Marlins sale to <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Loria" title="Jeffrey Loria">Jeffrey Loria</a>. The Expos, now known as the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals" title="Washington Nationals">Washington Nationals</a>, then moved to <a href="/wiki/Space_Coast_Stadium" title="Space Coast Stadium">Space Coast Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Viera,_Florida" title="Viera, Florida">Viera, Florida</a>.</p>
<p>Roger Dean Stadium was badly damaged in <a href="/wiki/2004_in_baseball" title="2004 in baseball">2004</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricanes" title="Hurricanes">hurricanes</a> <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Frances" title="Hurricane Frances">Frances</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Jeanne" title="Hurricane Jeanne">Jeanne</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Cliff_Politte" title="Cliff Politte">Cliff Politte</a> threw the first pitch in the stadium's history in spring training, <a href="/wiki/1998_in_baseball" title="1998 in baseball">1998</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2012, the stadium hosted a qualifying round for the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2013 World Baseball Classic">2013 World Baseball Classic</a>. <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_baseball_team" title="Spain national baseball team">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/France_national_baseball_team" title="France national baseball team">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel_national_baseball_team" title="Israel national baseball team">Israel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_baseball_team" title="South Africa national baseball team">South Africa</a> took part in the qualifier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wbcqual_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-wbcqual-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
