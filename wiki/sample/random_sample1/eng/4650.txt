<p><b>Roger Bart</b> (born September 29, 1962) is an American actor and singer who has received <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tony_Awards" title="Tony Awards">Tony Awards</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_Desk_Awards" title="Drama Desk Awards">Drama Desk Awards</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Awards" title="Screen Actors Guild Awards">Screen Actors Guild Awards</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bart&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bart was born in <a href="/wiki/Norwalk,_Connecticut" title="Norwalk, Connecticut">Norwalk, Connecticut</a>, the son of a teacher and a chemical engineer, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Bernardsville,_New_Jersey" title="Bernardsville, New Jersey">Bernardsville, New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ref07_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ref07-2">[2]</a></sup> His uncle is journalist <a href="/wiki/Peter_Bart" title="Peter Bart">Peter Bart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ref07_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ref07-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Bart&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He made his <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> debut in <i><a href="/wiki/Big_River_(musical)" title="Big River (musical)">Big River</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Tom_Sawyer" title="Tom Sawyer">Tom Sawyer</a> in 1987. Additional theatre credits include Jonathan in the <a href="/wiki/Alan_Menken" title="Alan Menken">Alan Menken</a>/<a href="/wiki/Tim_Rice" title="Tim Rice">Tim Rice</a> musical <i><a href="/wiki/King_David_(musical)" title="King David (musical)">King David</a></i>, Harlequin in <i><a href="/wiki/Triumph_of_Love_(musical)" title="Triumph of Love (musical)">Triumph of Love</a></i>, Snoopy in the Broadway revival of <i><a href="/wiki/You%27re_a_Good_Man,_Charlie_Brown" title="You're a Good Man, Charlie Brown">You're a Good Man, Charlie Brown</a></i> (for which he won the <a href="/wiki/Drama_Desk_Award" title="Drama Desk Award">Drama Desk Award</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony</a>), Carmen Ghia and later Leo Bloom in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Producers_(musical)" title="The Producers (musical)">The Producers</a></i> (earning Drama Desk and Tony nominations), and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Frogs_(musical)" title="The Frogs (musical)">The Frogs</a></i> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincoln_Center" title="Lincoln Center">Lincoln Center</a>, which reunited him with fellow <i>Producers</i> star <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Lane" title="Nathan Lane">Nathan Lane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Susan_Stroman" title="Susan Stroman">Susan Stroman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1996 and 1997, Bart appeared as Bud Frump in the USA national tour of <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Succeed_in_Business_Without_Really_Trying" title="How to Succeed in Business Without Really Trying">How to Succeed in Business Without Really Trying</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On television, Bart played <a href="/wiki/George_Carlin" title="George Carlin">George Carlin</a>'s son on <i>The George Carlin Show</i> (1994), and on <i>Bram and Alice</i> (2002) he portrayed Bram's assistant, Paul Newman. He became widely known to viewers with his portrayal of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acquaintances_of_Bree_Van_de_Kamp#George_Williams" title="Acquaintances of Bree Van de Kamp">George Williams</a>, the unhinged homicidal pharmacist in love with <a href="/wiki/Bree_Van_de_Kamp" title="Bree Van de Kamp">Bree Van de Kamp</a> (<a href="/wiki/Marcia_Cross" title="Marcia Cross">Marcia Cross</a>), on <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i>, which earned him a SAG Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bart provided the singing voice for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hercules_(Disney_character)" title="Hercules (Disney character)">Hercules</a> in Disney's <i><a href="/wiki/Hercules_(1997_film)" title="Hercules (1997 film)">Hercules</a></i>, as well as the singing voice of Scamp in <i><a href="/wiki/Lady_and_the_Tramp_II:_Scamp%27s_Adventure" title="Lady and the Tramp II: Scamp's Adventure">Lady and the Tramp II: Scamp's Adventure</a></i>. He was featured in the 2004 remake of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Stepford_Wives_(2004_film)" title="The Stepford Wives (2004 film)">The Stepford Wives</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Kidman" title="Nicole Kidman">Nicole Kidman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Broderick" title="Matthew Broderick">Matthew Broderick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bette_Midler" title="Bette Midler">Bette Midler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Walken" title="Christopher Walken">Christopher Walken</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Close" title="Glenn Close">Glenn Close</a>, and in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Producers_(2005_film)" title="The Producers (2005 film)">The Producers</a></i> (2005), in which he reprised his role of "common-law assistant" Carmen Ghia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2006, Bart played Howard "The Weasel" Montague in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sci_Fi_Channel_(United_States)" title="Sci Fi Channel (United States)">Sci Fi Channel</a> miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lost_Room" title="The Lost Room">The Lost Room</a></i>. In June 2007, he starred as Stuart in <i><a href="/wiki/Hostel:_Part_II" title="Hostel: Part II">Hostel: Part II</a></i>, the sequel to 2006's <i><a href="/wiki/Hostel_(2005_film)" title="Hostel (2005 film)">Hostel</a></i>. In 2007 he had supporting roles in <i><a href="/wiki/American_Gangster_(film)" title="American Gangster (film)">American Gangster</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Spy_School" title="Spy School">Spy School</a></i>. In 2008, he appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Harold_%26_Kumar_Escape_from_Guantanamo_Bay" title="Harold &amp; Kumar Escape from Guantanamo Bay">Harold &amp; Kumar Escape from Guantanamo Bay</a></i>, the sequel to 2004's <i><a href="/wiki/Harold_%26_Kumar_Go_to_White_Castle" title="Harold &amp; Kumar Go to White Castle">Harold &amp; Kumar Go to White Castle</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midnight_Meat_Train" title="Midnight Meat Train">Midnight Meat Train</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bart also originated the lead role of Dr. Frederick Frankenstein in the musical adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Mel_Brooks" title="Mel Brooks">Mel Brooks</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Frankenstein_(musical)" title="Young Frankenstein (musical)">Young Frankenstein</a></i>, which opened on Broadway in November 2007, following a run in Seattle. He recently reprised the role alongside former Broadway co-stars <a href="/wiki/Shuler_Hensley" title="Shuler Hensley">Shuler Hensley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cory_English" title="Cory English">Cory English</a> in the United States national tour which launched in September 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He ended his run on August 8, 2010 and was succeeded by Christopher Ryan. Bart originally portrayed the role of Igor in the original workshop read through.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Brad_Oscar" title="Brad Oscar">Brad Oscar</a> and Bart reprised their roles as Max Bialystock and Leo Bloom, respectively, in a production of <i>The Producers</i> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starlight_Theatre_(Kansas_City)" title="Starlight Theatre (Kansas City)">Starlight Theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a>. The show ran from August 23–29, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bart returned to Broadway in the musical, <i><a href="/wiki/Disaster!_(musical)" title="Disaster! (musical)">Disaster!</a></i>, which began performances in February 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Look_Out.21_All-Star_Disaster.21_Will_Hit_Broadway.2C_Starring_Adam_Pascal.2C_Roger_Bart.2C_Faith_Prince_.26_More_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Look_Out.21_All-Star_Disaster.21_Will_Hit_Broadway.2C_Starring_Adam_Pascal.2C_Roger_Bart.2C_Faith_Prince_.26_More-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
