<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Vienna,_Georgia" title="Vienna, Georgia">Vienna, Georgia</a>, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletics_(sport)" title="Athletics (sport)">athlete</a> of note Kingdom excelled at the <a href="/wiki/High_jump" title="High jump">high jump</a> and <a href="/wiki/Discus_throw" title="Discus throw">discus</a> in his formative years as well as being a noteworthy <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> player. He attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pittsburgh" title="University of Pittsburgh">University of Pittsburgh</a> originally on a football scholarship but excelled on the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Panthers#Track_and_Field" title="Pittsburgh Panthers">school's track team</a> winning the NCAA outdoor national championship in the 110 meter hurdles in 1983 and the NCAA indoor national championship in the 55 meter hurdles in 1984. He still makes his home in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh</a> suburb of <a href="/wiki/Monroeville,_Pennsylvania" title="Monroeville, Pennsylvania">Monroeville, Pennsylvania</a>.</p>
<p>He had a long and distinguished career on the track in the 110 meter high hurdles and won two <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic</a> <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medals</a>. In 1988 he was unbeaten all season and was the favorite to retain his Olympic title in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul, South Korea</a>. In a stunning display of technique, power and speed he won by three meters, becoming the first man to run below the 13 second barrier in an Olympic final, running 12.98s. This record stood until 1996 when <a href="/wiki/Allen_Johnson" title="Allen Johnson">Allen Johnson</a> broke it at the Atlanta Games. Kingdom is only the second athlete to have successfully defended his 110 m hurdle Olympic title, after <a href="/wiki/Lee_Calhoun" title="Lee Calhoun">Lee Calhoun</a>, who won the gold medal in both 1956 and 1960.</p>
<p>Kingdom set a 110 m high hurdles <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Record" title="World Record">World Record</a> of 12.92 seconds in <a href="/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich" title="Zürich">Zürich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> in 1989. This stood until August 20, 1993 when it was beaten by one 1/100th of a second by <a href="/wiki/Colin_Jackson" title="Colin Jackson">Colin Jackson</a> of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, a subsequent record that stood for 13 years.</p>
<p>His progress was hampered some in 1991 when he underwent <a href="/wiki/Surgery" title="Surgery">surgery</a> to repair <a href="/wiki/Ligament" title="Ligament">ligament</a> damage and remove bone chips from his <a href="/wiki/Knee" title="Knee">knee</a>.</p>
<p>He is also a member of <a href="/wiki/Omega_Psi_Phi" title="Omega Psi Phi">Omega Psi Phi</a> fraternity.</p>
<p>He is the director of track and field and cross country as well as the men and women's head track and field coach for <a href="/wiki/California_University_of_Pennsylvania" title="California University of Pennsylvania">California University of Pennsylvania</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Division_II_(NCAA)" title="Division II (NCAA)">Division II</a> College.</p>
<p>On March 6, 2014, Kingdom was hired as the assistant strength and conditioning coach for the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Cardinals" title="Arizona Cardinals">Arizona Cardinals</a>. Kingdom will assist newly hired strength and conditioning coach Buddy Morris and his focus will be on improving the team's speed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
