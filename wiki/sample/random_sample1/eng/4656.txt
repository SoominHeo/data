<p><b>Roger Hunt</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">MBE</a> (born 20 July 1938) is an English former <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>. He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> team which won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_World_Cup_1966" title="Football World Cup 1966">1966 World Cup</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Hunt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Culcheth_and_Glazebury" title="Culcheth and Glazebury">Glazebury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-River_Mersey_1974_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-River_Mersey_1974-1">[1]</a></sup> Hunt played for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warrington_Town" title="Warrington Town">Stockton Heath</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bury_F.C." title="Bury F.C.">Bury</a>, Stockton for a second time, <a href="/wiki/Devizes_Town_F.C." title="Devizes Town F.C.">Devizes Town</a> and Stockton again before manager <a href="/wiki/Phil_Taylor_(footballer,_born_1917)" title="Phil Taylor (footballer, born 1917)">Phil Taylor</a> signed him for <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> on 29 July 1958. He made his debut and scored his first goal for the club on 9 September 1959 in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Division_Two" title="Football League Division Two">Second Division</a> fixture at <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a> against <a href="/wiki/Scunthorpe_United_F.C." title="Scunthorpe United F.C.">Scunthorpe United</a>; Hunt scored in the 64th minute to give the Reds a 2â0 victory. This goal was the first of many - he would go on to score 286 goals for the club, 245 of them in the league, which remains a club record.</p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Bill_Shankly" title="Bill Shankly">Bill Shankly</a> replaced Taylor, Shankly and his fellow '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boot_Room" title="Boot Room">Boot Room</a>' coaching staff embarked upon a clear out of 24 players. Hunt however was retained and was a major factor in the Reds success in the 1960s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bennett_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bennett-2">[2]</a></sup> Liverpool gained promotion to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Division_One" title="Football League Division One">First Division</a> in 1962,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bennett_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-bennett-2">[2]</a></sup> after the club had finished 3rd or 4th, and thus just outside the promotion spots for five consecutive years from 1956 to 1961.</p>
<p>Hunt appeared in 41 of the 42 league games and scored 41 goals in season 1961â1962, averaging one goal per game. His goals helped propel Liverpool to a comfortable eight point title win over runners-up <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a> and included five <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hat-tricks" title="Hat-tricks">hat-tricks</a>, coming against <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walsall_F.C." title="Walsall F.C.">Walsall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea Town</a>, former club Bury and <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>.</p>
<p>It was a similar story in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1963-64_in_English_football" title="1963-64 in English football">1963â64</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1965-66_in_English_football" title="1965-66 in English football">1965â66</a> as Liverpool were English League champions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bennett_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-bennett-2">[2]</a></sup> Hunt again the top scorer (as he was for eight straight seasons) scoring 31 goals from 41 games and 30 goals from 37 appearances respectively.</p>
<p>In between the two titles, in 1965 he was instrumental in the side winning the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> for the first time. Hunt scored four times in a cup run that saw <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stockport_County_F.C." title="Stockport County F.C.">Stockport County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> all defeated as Liverpool reached the final for the first time since 1950. In the <a href="/wiki/1965_FA_Cup_Final" title="1965 FA Cup Final">final</a>, after a goal-less 90 minutes, Hunt scored the opening goal in the 93rd minute and strike partner <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ian_St._John" title="Ian St. John">Ian St. John</a> scored the second as the Reds recorded a 2â1 victory over Leeds United at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1923)" title="Wembley Stadium (1923)">Wembley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bennett_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-bennett-2">[2]</a></sup> He would also score Liverpool's only goal in the final of the Cup Winners Cup the following year as they went down 2-1 after extra time to <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bennett_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-bennett-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 22 August 1964, Hunt scored against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> after 11 minutes in a 3â2 home win, the first ever goal seen on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="British Broadcasting Corporation">BBC</a>'s flagship football highlights programme <i><a href="/wiki/Match_of_the_Day" title="Match of the Day">Match of the Day</a></i>.</p>
<p>He became Liverpool's record goalscorer on 7 November 1967 in an <a href="/wiki/Inter-Cities_Fairs_Cup" title="Inter-Cities Fairs Cup">Inter-Cities Fairs Cup</a> tie against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TSV_1860_Munchen" title="TSV 1860 Munchen">TSV Munich</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>, in which he scored his 242nd goal for the club. His final tally for the club was 286 goals by the time he left the club in 1969 to join Bolton Wanderers, a record which was not broken until <a href="/wiki/Ian_Rush" title="Ian Rush">Ian Rush</a> 23 years later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Hunt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hunt was capped 34 times for his country, with his debut given to him by <a href="/wiki/Walter_Winterbottom" title="Walter Winterbottom">Walter Winterbottom</a> whilst he was still a Second Division player on 4 April 1962, in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Austria_national_football_team" title="Austria national football team">Austria</a> at Wembley. He scored on his international debut as <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> won 3-1. He then went to the 1962 World Cup finals in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, but was not selected to play.</p>
<p>England had been given the go ahead to host the <a href="/wiki/1966_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1966 FIFA World Cup">1966 FIFA World Cup</a> and Hunt, along with club mates <a href="/wiki/Ian_Callaghan" title="Ian Callaghan">Ian Callaghan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerry_Byrne_(footballer)" title="Gerry Byrne (footballer)">Gerry Byrne</a> were selected for the 22 man squad by manager <a href="/wiki/Alf_Ramsey" title="Alf Ramsey">Alf Ramsey</a>.</p>
<p>Hunt was one of three forwards selected for the tournament. He initially partnered <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur" title="Tottenham Hotspur">Tottenham Hotspur</a> striker <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Greaves" title="Jimmy Greaves">Jimmy Greaves</a> up front but following a leg injury to Greaves he played alongside <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Hurst" title="Geoff Hurst">Geoff Hurst</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Ham_United" title="West Ham United">West Ham United</a>.</p>
<p>Hunt played in all six games, scoring three times, as England went on to win the <a href="/wiki/Jules_Rimet" title="Jules Rimet">Jules Rimet</a> trophy after a 4â2 extra time win over <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">West Germany</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1966_World_Cup_Final" title="1966 World Cup Final">World Cup Final</a> at Wembley.</p>
<p>Roger Hunt is the player whom strike partner Hurst always mentions when discussing his controversial second goal in the final when the ball hit the crossbar and bounced down - Hurst always says that Hunt, the closest player to the ball, would have followed up to score himself if he'd been in any doubt, but he turned away in celebration of a certain goal.</p>
<p>Back in the domestic game, Shankly's Liverpool team of the 1960s was beginning to age and be replaced, and this included Hunt, who after 492 appearances and 245 goals was allowed to leave on 16 December 1969 to join <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a>, with whom he played 76 games and scored 24 goals.</p>
<p>Only <a href="/wiki/Ian_Rush" title="Ian Rush">Ian Rush</a> has since surpassed his goalscoring total for Liverpool, though Rush scored fewer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League" title="Football League">League</a> goals than Hunt, who still holds that particular Liverpool Football Club record.</p>
<p>After retiring from football in 1972, Hunt joined his family's haulage company and in 1975 became a sitting member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pools_Panel" title="Pools Panel">Pools Panel</a>, who predict the results of games affected due to adverse weather in order for pools participants to be still able to win the prizes available.</p>
<p>In 2000, Hunt joined fellow 1966 heroes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alan_Ball_(footballer)" title="Alan Ball (footballer)">Alan Ball</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Cohen" title="George Cohen">George Cohen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nobby_Stiles" title="Nobby Stiles">Nobby Stiles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ray_Wilson_(English_footballer)" title="Ray Wilson (English footballer)">Ray Wilson</a> in receiving the MBE, after a campaign by sections of the <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">media</a> surprised by the lack of recognition for their part in England's biggest day in football. Their other six teammates, plus manager <a href="/wiki/Alf_Ramsey" title="Alf Ramsey">Alf Ramsey</a>, had already received honours of one form or another.</p>
<p>Roger Hunt was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/English_Football_Hall_of_Fame" title="English Football Hall of Fame">English Football Hall of Fame</a> in 2006, recognising his achievements in the English game. He was voted at No.13 by Liverpool fans on the official Liverpool Football Club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Web_site" title="Web site">web site</a> (www.liverpoolfc.tv) in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/100_Players_Who_Shook_The_Kop" title="100 Players Who Shook The Kop">100 Players Who Shook The Kop</a> poll, also in 2006.</p>
<p>He continues amongst the Liverpool supporters to be known as "Sir" Roger Hunt, despite his lack of a knighthood from the reigning monarch.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Hunt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hunt married his wife, Patricia, in 1959. The marriage was dissolved in 1981. Hunt has two children, a son, David (b 1960), and a daughter, Julie (b 1965).</p>
<p><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He now lives, with his 2nd wife Rowan, in Warrington.</p>
