<p><b>Rogers</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Benton_County,_Arkansas" title="Benton County, Arkansas">Benton County, Arkansas</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, the city has a population of 55,964 making the state's-eighth largest city. Rogers is located in the northwest portion of the state in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fayetteville-Springdale-Rogers_Metropolitan_Area" title="Fayetteville-Springdale-Rogers Metropolitan Area">Fayetteville-Springdale-Rogers Metropolitan Area</a>, which is one of the fastest growing areas in the nation and is ranked 109th in terms of population in the United States with 465,776 in 2010 according to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Centennial_Caboose_in_Rogers_Arkansas.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3000" data-file-width="4000" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Centennial_Caboose_in_Rogers_Arkansas.JPG/220px-Centennial_Caboose_in_Rogers_Arkansas.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Centennial_Caboose_in_Rogers_Arkansas.JPG/330px-Centennial_Caboose_in_Rogers_Arkansas.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Centennial_Caboose_in_Rogers_Arkansas.JPG/440px-Centennial_Caboose_in_Rogers_Arkansas.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Centennial_Caboose_in_Rogers_Arkansas.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Frisco caboose at Centennial Park, Rogers Historical District downtown. The caboose is emblematic of Rogers' railroad history.</div>

<p>Rogers was named after Captain Charles Warrington Rogers, who was vice-president and general manager of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis_and_San_Francisco_Railway" title="St. Louis and San Francisco Railway">St. Louis and San Francisco Railway</a>, also known as the Frisco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The town was established in 1881, the year the Frisco line arrived; it was at this time the area residents honored Captain Rogers by naming it for him. The community was incorporated on June 6, 1881.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rogers_Arkansas_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rogers_Arkansas-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rogers was the location of the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wal-Mart" title="Wal-Mart">Wal-Mart</a> store, whose corporate headquarters is located in neighboring <a href="/wiki/Bentonville,_Arkansas" title="Bentonville, Arkansas">Bentonville, Arkansas</a>. <a href="/wiki/Daisy_Outdoor_Products" title="Daisy Outdoor Products">Daisy Outdoor Products</a>, known for its air rifles, has both its headquarters and its Air Rifle Museum in Rogers. In June 2007, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BusinessWeek" title="BusinessWeek">BusinessWeek</a> magazine ranked Rogers as 18th in its list of the 25 best affordable suburbs in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_South" title="American South">American South</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> And in 2010, CNN Money ranked Rogers # 10 on their list of 100 Best Places to Live.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_commerce">Historic commerce</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Historic commerce">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first retail business owned by the Stroud family was a store in Pea Ridge, Arkansas, which was co-owned by Allen Bryant Stroud (1831–1914) and his son Harlan Lafayette (H.L.) Stroud (1858–1950).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prark2004_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-prark2004-6">[6]</a></sup> That business was established prior to 1879 and Allen Stroud also served as postmaster at Pea Ridge for a time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prark2004_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-prark2004-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1884, H.L. Stroud sold his interest in the Stroud store in Pea Ridge and purchased a dry goods store at the corner of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walnut_Street_Historic_District_(Rogers,_Arkansas)" title="Walnut Street Historic District (Rogers, Arkansas)">First and Walnut Streets</a> in Rogers which he named <a href="/wiki/Stroud%27s_Mercantile" title="Stroud's Mercantile">Stroud's Mercantile</a>.</p>
<p>In 1887 he brought in his brother Evan Giesen (1868–1952) to serve as manager of his new business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rogersarkansas1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-rogersarkansas1-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1891 H.L. Stroud moved his business into a storefront on the north side of the 100 block of Walnut Street. Stroud's continued to prosper, and in 1899 H.L. built the brick building at 114–116 West Walnut Street.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rogersarkansas1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-rogersarkansas1-7">[7]</a></sup> Stroud's continued to be the leading retail business in Rogers up into the 1960s, when in 1962 Sam Walton opened the first location of what would become the retail giant Walmart just seven blocks away.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Walton's new store combined with the nationwide movement of retail centers from aged downtowns to malls and shopping centers slowly eroded Stroud's customer base, leading the locally beloved retailer to permanently close in 1993 after 109 years in business. In 1912 the city council formed a commission of local businessmen to facilitate the paving of downtown Rogers. Despite the constant complaints of dusty and muddy streets, and the enthusiastic support of prominent citizens such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coin_Harvey" title="Coin Harvey">Coin Harvey</a>, bickering over the cost and method of paving delayed the start of the project until July, 1924. The downtown area was paved with <a href="/wiki/Concrete" title="Concrete">concrete</a> and overlaid with bricks in rows, changing to a basket weave pattern at the intersections of streets. The work was completed in December, 1924, and the brick pavement remains today, with renovations done to the streets in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rogers is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Rogers%2C_Arkansas&amp;params=36_19_46_N_94_8_29_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">36°19′46″N</span> <span class="longitude">94°8′29″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">36.32944°N 94.14139°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">36.32944; -94.14139</span></span></span></a></span> (36.329388, −94.141372).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-9">[9]</a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 33.6 square miles (87 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 33.5 square miles (87 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 0.1 square miles (0.26 km<sup>2</sup>) (0.15%) is water.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The climate in this area is characterized by warm, humid summers and generally mild to cool winters. According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_Climate_Classification" title="Köppen Climate Classification">Köppen Climate Classification</a> system, Rogers has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a>, abbreviated "Cfa" on climate maps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>As of 2010<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> Rogers had a population of 55,964. The racial and ethnic composition of the population was 62.0% non-Hispanic white, 1.3% non-Hispanic black, 1.0% Native American, 1.0% Asian Indian, 1.6% other Asian, 0.3% Pacific Islander, 0.1% non-Hispanics of some other race, 3.0% from two or more races and 31.5% Hispanic or Latino.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-14">[14]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 38,829 people, 14,005 households, and 10,209 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,158.0 people per square mile (447.1/km²). There were 14,836 housing units at an average density of 442.4 per square mile (170.8/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 90.75% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a>, 0.47% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 1.05% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 1.43% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 0.07% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 9.43% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.80% from two or more races. 19.29% of the population are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> of any race.</p>
<p>There were 14,005 households out of which 39.4% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 58.4% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 10.1% had a female householder with no husband present, and 27.1% were non-families. 22.2% of all households were made up of individuals and 8.9% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.74 and the average family size was 3.21.</p>
<p>In the city the population was spread out with 29.4% under the age of 18, 9.0% from 18 to 24, 31.5% from 25 to 44, 18.3% from 45 to 64, and 11.8% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 32 years. For every 100 females there were 95.3 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 91.5 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the city was $40,474, and the median income for a family was $45,876. Males had a median income of $30,911 versus $22,020 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $19,761. About 9.4% of families and 12.8% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 17.6% of those under age 18 and 10.1% of those age 65 or over.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Retail">Retail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Retail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rogers has two <a href="/wiki/Shopping_mall" title="Shopping mall">shopping malls</a>: Frisco Station Mall and <a href="/wiki/Pinnacle_Hills_Promenade" title="Pinnacle Hills Promenade">Pinnacle Hills Promenade</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since <a href="/wiki/2007_LPGA_Tour" title="2007 LPGA Tour">2007</a>, Rogers has hosted the <a href="/wiki/Walmart_NW_Arkansas_Championship" title="Walmart NW Arkansas Championship">Walmart NW Arkansas Championship</a>, a women's professional <a href="/wiki/Golf" title="Golf">golf</a> tournament on the <a href="/wiki/LPGA" title="LPGA">LPGA Tour</a>. The 54-hole event is held at Pinnacle Country Club in late June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dock Wheeler Park in Rogers houses the largest softball program in the state of Arkansas,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> while Foerester Park is home to the largest soccer program in the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rogers is also host to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Susan_G._Komen_Race_for_the_Cure" title="Susan G. Komen Race for the Cure">Susan G. Komen Race for the Cure</a>, the most widely known, largest and best-funded <a href="/wiki/Breast_cancer" title="Breast cancer">breast cancer</a> organization in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Suliklogo_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Suliklogo-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>Rogers has five large sports parks, 14 neighborhood parks along with a swimming pool, a skateboard and splash park, 26 athletic fields, an activity center, a YMC, and two lakes, along with five golf courses and a trail system totaling over 23 miles (37 km). The NWA <a href="/wiki/Razorback_Regional_Greenway" title="Razorback Regional Greenway">Razorback Regional Greenway</a> is a 36-mile (58 km) primarily off-road shared-use trail that connects the region's trail systems to various community attractions. There is also the Rogers Family Aquatic Center.</p>
<p>Other recreational attractions in the surrounding area includes <a href="/wiki/Beaver_Lake_(Arkansas)" title="Beaver Lake (Arkansas)">Beaver Lake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hobbs_State_Park_%E2%80%93_Conservation_Area" title="Hobbs State Park – Conservation Area">Hobbs State Park – Conservation Area</a> and <a href="/wiki/War_Eagle_Mill" title="War Eagle Mill">War Eagle Mill</a> &amp; Cavern to the east of Rogers, as well as historical Civil War battlefield, <a href="/wiki/Pea_Ridge_National_Military_Park" title="Pea Ridge National Military Park">Pea Ridge National Military Park</a>, about 10 miles outside of Rogers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a> of Rogers since 2011 is Greg Hines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The previous mayor, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Womack" title="Steve Womack">Steve Womack</a>, won <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections,_2010" title="United States House of Representatives elections, 2010">election</a> to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a> in 2010 for the seat vacated by <a href="/wiki/John_Boozman" title="John Boozman">John Boozman</a>, now a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._senator" title="U.S. senator">U.S. senator</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Secondary_education">Secondary education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Secondary education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rogers is home to several public and private school districts and schools including:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rogers_School_District" title="Rogers School District">Rogers School District</a> - In 2012, 2013, &amp; 2014, both Rogers and Rogers Heritage high schools were recognized with Silver awards from <i>U.S. News &amp; World Report Top 1,000 High Schools in America</i> and were ranked among the top schools in the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Additionally, for many years, Rogers High School has been ranked by Newsweek magazine among the top 1,300 schools in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li>Rogers High School<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> (Home of the Mountaineers) was the first of the Rogers School District's three high schools, which also includes <a href="/wiki/Rogers_Heritage_High_School" title="Rogers Heritage High School">Rogers Heritage High School</a> and Rogers New Technology High School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rogers_New_Technology_High_School_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rogers_New_Technology_High_School-23">[23]</a></sup> RHS and HHS, each with about 2,000 students, are in the state's largest classification (7A).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rogers_Heritage_High_School" title="Rogers Heritage High School">Rogers Heritage High School</a> (Home of the War Eagles) is Rogers' second high school that opened in August 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The two traditional Rogers high schools offer the same courses and athletic opportunities. They both serve students in grades 9-12.</li>
<li>Rogers New Technology High School<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rogers_New_Technology_High_School_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rogers_New_Technology_High_School-23">[23]</a></sup> opened in 2013 and is part of the New Tech Network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Like more than 120 New Tech schools around the country, the Rogers school features an instructional approach centered on project-based learning and integrated technology in the classroom.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Arkansas_Arts_Academy_High_School" title="Arkansas Arts Academy High School">Arkansas Arts Academy High School</a> is a public charter school supported by the Arkansas Arts Academy district.</li>
<li>St. Vincent de Paul is a private Catholic school, the largest private school in Rogers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Postsecondary_education">Postsecondary education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Postsecondary education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city has local campuses of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Phoenix" title="University of Phoenix">the University of Phoenix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bryan_College" title="Bryan College">Bryan College</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/University_of_Arkansas" title="University of Arkansas">University of Arkansas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Harding_University" title="Harding University">Harding University</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/John_Brown_University" title="John Brown University">John Brown University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other nearby schools and campuses include the University of Arkansas (Fayetteville), <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Arkansas_Community_College" title="Northwest Arkansas Community College">Northwest Arkansas Community College</a> (Bentonville),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> and John Brown University (a Christian school in Siloam Springs).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Print">Print</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Print">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The daily paper in Rogers is the <i>Rogers Morning News</i>, with a special "A section" dedicated to news just for Rogers and surrounding cities. The rest of the newspaper is the Northwest Arkansas edition of the <i><a href="/wiki/Arkansas_Democrat-Gazette" title="Arkansas Democrat-Gazette">Arkansas Democrat-Gazette</a></i> (the "B section" is the regular <i>Democrat-Gazette</i> "A section," complete with front page and masthead).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Airports">Airports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rogers,_Arkansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Airports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rogers_Municipal_Airport" title="Rogers Municipal Airport">Rogers Municipal Airport</a> (ROG), also known as Carter Field, is home to Wal-Mart's air fleet. All commercial aviation, however, goes through the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Arkansas_Regional_Airport" title="Northwest Arkansas Regional Airport">Northwest Arkansas Regional Airport</a> (XNA), located about 15 miles west of Rogers in <a href="/wiki/Highfill,_Arkansas" title="Highfill, Arkansas">Highfill, Arkansas</a>.</p>
