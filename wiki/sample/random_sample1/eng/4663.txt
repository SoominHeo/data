<p><b>Rosemåling</b>, or <b>rosemaling</b> is the name of a traditional form of decorative <a href="/wiki/Folk_art" title="Folk art">folk art</a> that originated in the rural valleys of <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>. Rosemåling is a style of Norwegian decorative painting on wood that uses stylized flower ornamentation, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scrollwork" title="Scrollwork">scrollwork</a>, lining and geometric elements, often in flowing patterns. Landscape and architectural elements are also common. Many other decorative painting techniques were used such as glazing, spattering, marbelizing, manipulating the paint with the fingers or other objects.</p>
<p>Rosemåling is also common in rural <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> (Swedish: <i>rosmålning</i>) where it is also<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> called <i>kurbitsmålning</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> or simply <a href="/wiki/Kurbits" title="Kurbits">kurbits</a>, where <i>kurbits</i> refers to depictions of <a href="/wiki/Cucurbita" title="Cucurbita">Cucurbita</a>. As with its Norwegian counterpart, it was most popular from the latter half of the 18th century<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and until the 1860s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Illinois_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Illinois-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosem%C3%A5ling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Norwegian for "decorative painting", (applied decoration or embellishment, decorative, decorated [rosut, rosute, rosete, rosa] and "å måle, å male" to paint). "Rose" can be interpreted as a reference to the rose flower, but the floral elements are often so stylized that no specific flower is identifiable, and are absent in some designs.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_in_Norway">History in Norway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosem%C3%A5ling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History in Norway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rosemåling in Norway originated in the low-land areas of eastern <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> particularly in the <a href="/wiki/Telemark" title="Telemark">Telemark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hallingdal" title="Hallingdal">Hallingdal</a>, but also in <a href="/wiki/Numedal" title="Numedal">Numedal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Setesdal" title="Setesdal">Setesdal</a> and in other valleys in <a href="/wiki/Vest-Agder" title="Vest-Agder">Vest-Agder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hordaland" title="Hordaland">Hordaland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sogn_og_Fjordane" title="Sogn og Fjordane">Sogn og Fjordane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rogaland" title="Rogaland">Rogaland</a>. It came into existence around 1750, when <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">Baroque</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rococo" title="Rococo">Rococo</a>, artistic styles of the upper class, were introduced into Norway's rural culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Illinois_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Illinois-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rosemåling designs use C and S strokes and feature scroll and flowing lines, floral designs, and both subtle and vibrant colors. Script lettering, scenes, animal and human figures may also be included. Artists who specialized in rosemåling often came from poorer classes in the countryside. They would travel from county to county painting churches, homes and furnishings for a commission of either money or merely room and board. Thus rosemåling was carried over the mountains and toward Norway's western coast. Once farther away from the influence of the painters' guild, these artists tried new ideas and motifs. Rosemåling became widespread as amateur artists in rural areas often imitated this <a href="/wiki/Folk_art" title="Folk art">folk art</a>. Soon strong regional styles developed and today the three main styles are <a href="/wiki/Telemark" title="Telemark">Telemark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hallingdal" title="Hallingdal">Hallingdal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rogaland" title="Rogaland">Rogaland</a>, named after the regions in which each originated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rosemåling is, in a sense, the two-dimensional counterpart of <a href="/wiki/Acanthus_(ornament)" title="Acanthus (ornament)">acanthus</a> carving, since it is clear that the C and S curves in rosemåling take their inspiration from the acanthus carvings of Baroque and Rococo art and the acanthus carvings in the rural churches (e.g., the altar <a href="/wiki/Reredos" title="Reredos">reredoses</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pulpits" title="Pulpits">pulpits</a>) and homes (e.g., cupboards) were painted in the same bright colors as used in rosemåling. While in the cities these acanthus carvings were generally gilt, the rural artisans did not have ready access to <a href="/wiki/Gold_leaf" title="Gold leaf">gold leaf</a> as their urban counterparts and so painted their carvings in the bright colors popular in rural communities (e.g., Norwegian rural dress, Cf. <a href="/wiki/Bunad" title="Bunad">bunad</a>). Like rosemåling, acanthus carving has had a cultural revival in recent times as both a means of interior design (e.g., on furniture, picture frames, door and window frames, etc.) and as a personal hobby, although most modern day acanthus carving is left unpainted and unvarnished.</p>
<p>An anecdote about the <a href="/wiki/German_occupation_of_Norway" title="German occupation of Norway">Nazi occupation of Norway</a> (1940–1945) is that at a time when the public display of the Norwegian flag or the State Coat of Arms could bring imprisonment or even death, the Norwegians discovered that they could display the 'H' overlapping the '7' of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_cypher" title="Royal cypher">Royal cypher</a> of their exiled king, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haakon_VII" title="Haakon VII">Haakon VII</a>, at the center of a rosemåling design without the German occupation forces seeing anything but a colorful peasant design. Christmas cards with the Royal cypher at the center of a rosemåling design were especially popular; many have survived and their history documented.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_in_America">History in America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosem%C3%A5ling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History in America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian-American" title="Norwegian-American">Norwegian immigrants</a> brought the art of Rosemåling to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> since many of the immigrants came from parts of Norway where rosemåling was well established. They often carried their belongings in beautifully painted trunks. Some of the immigrants were Rosemåling painters. This generation, however, contributed little to the development of American rosemåling in the 20th century, because rosemåling began to go out of style starting in 1860.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rosemåling experienced its revival in America in the 20th century. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian-Americans" title="Norwegian-Americans">Norwegian-Americans</a> became interested in the rosemåling decorated possessions of their ancestors. American rosemåling began to develop and flourish. The revival of Rosemåling in the United States is often credited to <a href="/wiki/Per_Lysne" title="Per Lysne">Per Lysne</a> who was born in Norway and trained in the craft. He came to America in the early 20th Century and was employed as a wagon painter in <a href="/wiki/Stoughton,_Wisconsin" title="Stoughton, Wisconsin">Stoughton, Wisconsin</a>. When business slowed during the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>, he began Rosemåling again. Today Norwegian rosemåling is taught in many areas of the USA. Rosemåling associations sponsor classes and competitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
