<p><b>Rosa Maria</b> "<b>Rosie</b>" <b>Perez</b> (born September 6, 1964)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biocom_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-biocom-4">[4]</a></sup> is an American actress, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Activist" title="Activist">community activist</a>, talk show host, author, dancer, and choreographer. Her film breakthrough was in Spike Lee's <i><a href="/wiki/Do_the_Right_Thing" title="Do the Right Thing">Do the Right Thing</a></i> (1989), which she followed with <i><a href="/wiki/White_Men_Can%27t_Jump" title="White Men Can't Jump">White Men Can't Jump</a></i> (1992). Among many honors, she was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress</a> for her performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Fearless_(1993_film)" title="Fearless (1993 film)">Fearless</a></i> (1993). She was also nominated for three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emmy_Awards" title="Emmy Awards">Emmy Awards</a> for her work as a choreographer on <i><a href="/wiki/In_Living_Color" title="In Living Color">In Living Color</a></i>. In 2007, Perez starred on Broadway as Googie Gomez in a revival of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ritz_(play)" title="The Ritz (play)">The Ritz</a></i>, and she was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Independent_Spirit_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Female" title="Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Female">Indie Spirit</a> Award for her performance in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Take_(2007_film)" title="The Take (2007 film)">The Take</a></i>. Perez was a regular host on <i><a href="/wiki/The_View_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The View (U.S. TV series)">The View</a></i> and in 2015 she returned to Broadway to star in <i><a href="/wiki/Fish_in_the_Dark" title="Fish in the Dark">Fish in the Dark</a></i>, a new play written by <a href="/wiki/Larry_David" title="Larry David">Larry David</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN-Handbook-2014_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-Handbook-2014-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vogue-Career-2014_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vogue-Career-2014-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosie_Perez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Perez was born in the <a href="/wiki/Bushwick,_Brooklyn" title="Bushwick, Brooklyn">Bushwick</a> neighborhood of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a>, to mother Lydia Perez and Ismael Serrano, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merchant_marine" title="Merchant marine">merchant marine</a> seaman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes-Dali-2001_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-Dali-2001-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indypendent-YoSoy-2006_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Indypendent-YoSoy-2006-7">[7]</a></sup> Her parents, who were both from <a href="/wiki/Aguadilla,_Puerto_Rico" title="Aguadilla, Puerto Rico">Aguadilla, Puerto Rico</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Centro-YoSoy-2007_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Centro-YoSoy-2007-8">[8]</a></sup> were married to other people when they met—she is the product of their affair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN-Handbook-2014_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-Handbook-2014-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heavy-View-2014_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Heavy-View-2014-9">[9]</a></sup> She was born at the now-closed Greenpoint Hospital in the <a href="/wiki/Greenpoint,_Brooklyn" title="Greenpoint, Brooklyn">Greenpoint</a> neighborhood of Brooklyn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LarryKingNow-View-2014_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-LarryKingNow-View-2014-10">[10]</a></sup> Perez became a ward of the state when her mother took her from an aunt, who had been raising her. She was transferred to a group foster home at age three and lived in foster care in New York and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peekskill" title="Peekskill">Peekskill</a> until age eight, and was still legally considered a ward of the State of New York until age 12. Her mother and aunt frequently visited, and her father made an unsuccessful custody bid at one point.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN-Handbook-2014_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-Handbook-2014-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYDN-Handbook-2014_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYDN-Handbook-2014-11">[11]</a></sup> Perez has five brothers and sisters from her mother's marriage to her mother's first husband, Ventura Perez, but also has additional half-brothers and half-sisters (a total of 10 children).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYDN-Handbook-2014_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYDN-Handbook-2014-11">[11]</a></sup> These life problems left Perez with a speech impediment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biocom_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-biocom-4">[4]</a></sup> She eventually moved in with paternal aunt, Ana Dominga Otero Serrano-Roque,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biocom_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-biocom-4">[4]</a></sup> and attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grover_Cleveland_High_School_(New_York_City)" title="Grover Cleveland High School (New York City)">Grover Cleveland High School</a>, which is located in the <a href="/wiki/Ridgewood,_Queens" title="Ridgewood, Queens">Ridgewood</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CapitalNY-HighSchool-2012_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-CapitalNY-HighSchool-2012-12">[12]</a></sup> Her mother died of AIDS-related complications in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYDN-Handbook-2014_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYDN-Handbook-2014-11">[11]</a></sup> Perez is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_people" title="Puerto Rican people">Puerto Rican</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NPR-YoSoy-2006_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-NPR-YoSoy-2006-13">[13]</a></sup> and had a strict Catholic upbringing, which she has credited to the influence of the nuns during her childhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biocom_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-biocom-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LarryKingNow-View-2014_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-LarryKingNow-View-2014-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vibe-Fearless-1993-1994_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-Fearless-1993-1994-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosie_Perez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosie_Perez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At 19 years old,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Perez started her career in the early 1980s, as a dancer on <i><a href="/wiki/Soul_Train" title="Soul Train">Soul Train</a>.</i> As a college student in Los Angeles, majoring in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bio-chemistry" title="Bio-chemistry">bio-chemistry</a>, to relieve stress, she said she would go to nightclubs for ladies night. A talent scout from <i><a href="/wiki/Soul_Train" title="Soul Train">Soul Train</a></i> asked Perez to appear on the show. She was not a professional dancer, but loved it so much she dropped out of school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LarryKingNow-View-2014_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-LarryKingNow-View-2014-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1988, when she was 24 years old, Perez was noticed at the dance club Funky Reggae<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCM-Bio_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-TCM-Bio-16">[16]</a></sup> by <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>, who hired her for her first major acting role in <i><a href="/wiki/Do_the_Right_Thing" title="Do the Right Thing">Do the Right Thing</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewYork-DTRT-2008_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-NewYork-DTRT-2008-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Perez later choreographed <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music videos</a> by <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Brown" title="Bobby Brown">Bobby Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diana_Ross" title="Diana Ross">Diana Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Boys_(band)" title="The Boys (band)">The Boys</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vibe-Fearless-1993-1994_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Vibe-Fearless-1993-1994-14">[14]</a></sup> She was the choreographer for the dancing group the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fly_Girls_(dance_troupe)" title="Fly Girls (dance troupe)">Fly Girls</a> who were featured on the Fox television comedy program <i><a href="/wiki/In_Living_Color" title="In Living Color">In Living Color</a></i> and also worked as a segment producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ebert-Sundance-1999_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ebert-Sundance-1999-18">[18]</a></sup> She made her <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> debut in <a href="/wiki/Terrence_McNally" title="Terrence McNally">Terrence McNally</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Frankie_and_Johnny_in_the_Clair_de_Lune" title="Frankie and Johnny in the Clair de Lune">Frankie and Johnny in the Clair de Lune</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Playbill-Frankie-2003_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Playbill-Frankie-2003-19">[19]</a></sup> Perez had her third major role in the hit comedy <i><a href="/wiki/White_Men_Can%27t_Jump" title="White Men Can't Jump">White Men Can't Jump</a></i> co-starring <a href="/wiki/Wesley_Snipes" title="Wesley Snipes">Wesley Snipes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woody_Harrelson" title="Woody Harrelson">Woody Harrelson</a>.</p>
<p>Perez was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Oscar for Best Supporting Actress</a> for her role in <a href="/wiki/Peter_Weir" title="Peter Weir">Peter Weir</a>'s 1993 film <i><a href="/wiki/Fearless_(1993_film)" title="Fearless (1993 film)">Fearless</a></i>. She attended the ceremony with her father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes-66AcademyAwards-1994_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-66AcademyAwards-1994-20">[20]</a></sup> In 1997, she starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Perdita_Durango" title="Perdita Durango">Perdita Durango</a></i>, a controversial film in which many scenes of excessive violence, sex and nudity were edited out of the version released in the United States but remained intact in the version released throughout Latin America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SensesofCinema-Perdita-2000_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-SensesofCinema-Perdita-2000-21">[21]</a></sup> In 1999, Perez starred in <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Savoca" title="Nancy Savoca">Nancy Savoca</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_24_Hour_Woman" title="The 24 Hour Woman">The 24 Hour Woman</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes-24Hour-1999_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-24Hour-1999-2">[2]</a></sup> She provides the voices of Click, the camera, on <a href="/wiki/Nick_Jr._(block)" title="Nick Jr. (block)">Nick Jr.</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Go,_Diego,_Go!" title="Go, Diego, Go!">Go, Diego, Go!</a></i> and Chel, a beautiful native woman in the <a href="/wiki/DreamWorks_Animation" title="DreamWorks Animation">DreamWorks Animation</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Road_to_El_Dorado" title="The Road to El Dorado">The Road to El Dorado</a></i>. She played corrupt police officer Carol Brazier in the <a href="/wiki/Judd_Apatow" title="Judd Apatow">Judd Apatow</a>-produced film <i><a href="/wiki/Pineapple_Express_(film)" title="Pineapple Express (film)">Pineapple Express</a></i>, co-starring <a href="/wiki/Seth_Rogen" title="Seth Rogen">Seth Rogen</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Franco" title="James Franco">James Franco</a>. Perez appeared on an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> in October 2009 about pedophiles' rights. Executive producer <a href="/wiki/Neal_Baer" title="Neal Baer">Neal Baer</a> said the writers had Perez in mind when they wrote the role of a young sexual abuse victim's mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVGuide-SVU-2009_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVGuide-SVU-2009-22">[22]</a></sup> She suffered a serious injury during the filming of the episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFChron-neckscar-2010_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-SFChron-neckscar-2010-23">[23]</a></sup> Most recently, Perez is the subject of an album by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Felt_(hip_hop)" title="Felt (hip hop)">Felt</a> entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Felt_3:_A_Tribute_To_Rosie_Perez" title="Felt 3: A Tribute To Rosie Perez">Felt 3: A Tribute To Rosie Perez</a></i>. Perez was in a <a href="/wiki/Mike_Nichols" title="Mike Nichols">Mike Nichols</a> production of <i>The Play What I Wrote</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Injury">Injury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosie_Perez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Injury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rosie Perez injured her neck in 2009 while filming an episode of <i>Law &amp; Order: SVU</i> and underwent surgery to heal a <a href="/wiki/Spinal_disc_herniation" title="Spinal disc herniation">herniated disc</a>. One year after the accident, she appeared at the White House in a wheelchair, wearing a neck brace for a meeting with President Obama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday-SVU-2010_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday-SVU-2010-24">[24]</a></sup> In May 2011, Perez filed a lawsuit against the producers of the show,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYPost-SVUsuit-2011_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYPost-SVUsuit-2011-25">[25]</a></sup> claiming that the injury she incurred was the result of being "recklessly pulled, grabbed, yanked, wrenched and manhandled" during filming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DailyNews-SVUsettlement-2012_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-DailyNews-SVUsettlement-2012-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Boxing">Boxing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosie_Perez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Boxing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rosie Perez is a fan of the sport of <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a>. In June 2013, she served as the grand marshal for the International Boxing Hall of Fame parade in <a href="/wiki/Canastota,_New_York" title="Canastota, New York">Canastota, New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RingTV-2013_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-RingTV-2013-27">[27]</a></sup> uk</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Author">Author</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosie_Perez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Author">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2014, Rosie Perez published an autobiography titled <i>Handbook for an Unpredictable Life: How I Survived Sister Renata and My Crazy Mother, and Still Came Out Smiling...</i> She is also the reader of the audio CD of this book. Perez said that she didn't initially set out to write an autobiography, but rather a book that analyses the causes and effects of child abuse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LarryKingNow-View-2014_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-LarryKingNow-View-2014-10">[10]</a></sup> She said it wasn't until about 6 months after the book was published and she heard responses from others that she found the experience cathartic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LarryKingNow-View-2014_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-LarryKingNow-View-2014-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_View"><i>The View</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosie_Perez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The View">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 3, 2014, <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> announced Perez would join <i><a href="/wiki/The_View_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The View (U.S. TV series)">The View</a></i> as a new co-host alongside moderator <a href="/wiki/Whoopi_Goldberg" title="Whoopi Goldberg">Whoopi Goldberg</a>, newcomer <a href="/wiki/Nicolle_Wallace" title="Nicolle Wallace">Nicolle Wallace</a>, and returning co-host <a href="/wiki/Rosie_O%27Donnell" title="Rosie O'Donnell">Rosie O'Donnell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC-ViewAnnouncement-2014_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-ViewAnnouncement-2014-28">[28]</a></sup> The new season began on September 15, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-People-ViewReview-2014_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-People-ViewReview-2014-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW-View-2014_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-EW-View-2014-30">[30]</a></sup> Perez said she was initially hesitant about the job because "I didn't want to be on a show where people were just screaming at each other disrespectfully."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LarryKingNow-View-2014_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-LarryKingNow-View-2014-10">[10]</a></sup> She decided to join the cast when she learned that <a href="/wiki/Bill_Wolff_(television_executive)" title="Bill Wolff (television executive)">Bill Wolff</a>, whom she knew from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rachel_Maddow_Show" title="The Rachel Maddow Show">The Rachel Maddow Show</a></i>, was going to be the new executive producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LarryKingNow-View-2014_10-6"><a href="#cite_note-LarryKingNow-View-2014-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffPo-View-2014_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-HuffPo-View-2014-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Colorlines-Maddow-Romney-2014_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Colorlines-Maddow-Romney-2014-32">[32]</a></sup> On July 8, 2015, Perez announced she would be leaving <i><a href="/wiki/The_View_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The View (U.S. TV series)">The View</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosie_Perez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Perez is an <a href="/wiki/Activism" title="Activism">activist</a> for Puerto Rican rights:</p>
<ul>
<li>Her film <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yo_soy_Boricua,_pa%27que_t%C3%BA_lo_sepas!_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yo soy Boricua, pa'que tú lo sepas! (film) (page does not exist)">Yo soy Boricua, pa'que tu lo sepas!</a></i> (<i>I'm Puerto Rican, Just So You Know!</i>) documents her activism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Centro-YoSoy-2007_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Centro-YoSoy-2007-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes-YoSoy-2006_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-YoSoy-2006-34">[34]</a></sup></li>
<li>She starred in and directed the Spanish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIDS" title="AIDS">AIDS</a> PSA campaign "Join the Fight" for Cable Positive and Kismet Films. The campaign featured actor <a href="/wiki/Wilmer_Valderrama" title="Wilmer Valderrama">Wilmer Valderrama</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Entertainment_Television" title="Black Entertainment Television">BET</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Julissa_Bermudez" title="Julissa Bermudez">Julissa Bermudez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telenovela" title="Telenovela">Telenovela</a> actor <a href="/wiki/Erick_El%C3%ADas" title="Erick Elías">Erick Elías</a>, singer/actress <a href="/wiki/Lorena_Rojas" title="Lorena Rojas">Lorena Rojas</a>, 2006–2007 <a href="/wiki/Miss_Universe" title="Miss Universe">Miss Universe</a> <a href="/wiki/Zuleyka_Rivera" title="Zuleyka Rivera">Zuleyka Rivera</a> and actress <a href="/wiki/Judy_Marte" title="Judy Marte">Judy Marte</a>. An English-language campaign was also directed by <a href="/wiki/Liev_Schreiber" title="Liev Schreiber">Liev Schreiber</a>.</li>
<li>President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> appointed her to The Presidential Advisory Council on HIV/AIDS (PACHA). She was sworn in on February 2, 2010.</li>
<li>On January 6, 2000, she was arrested for <a href="/wiki/Disorderly_conduct" title="Disorderly conduct">disorderly conduct</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> following a rally to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Navy-Vieques_protests" title="Navy-Vieques protests">protest</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">U.S. Navy</a> air weapons training, as well as other forms of payload on the government training range owned at <a href="/wiki/Vieques,_Puerto_Rico" title="Vieques, Puerto Rico">Vieques</a>, an island off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.</li>
<li>Perez serves as the chair of the artistic board for <a class="external text" href="http://www.urbanarts.org/" rel="nofollow">Urban Arts Partnership</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UrbanArts-Board-2013_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-UrbanArts-Board-2013-35">[35]</a></sup> a New York City arts education nonprofit that uses arts integrated education programs to close the achievement gap.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosie_Perez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Perez suffered abuse during her childhood from both her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schizophrenic" title="Schizophrenic">schizophrenic</a> mother and from the nuns at the group home. As a result, she has suffered from high anxiety, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PTSD" title="PTSD">PTSD</a>, and a form of depression called <a href="/wiki/Dysthymia" title="Dysthymia">dysthymia</a>, but with hard work in therapy it has been greatly reduced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN-Handbook-2014_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-Handbook-2014-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LarryKingNow-View-2014_10-7"><a href="#cite_note-LarryKingNow-View-2014-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LatinPost-Madonna2Pac-2014_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-LatinPost-Madonna2Pac-2014-36">[36]</a></sup> Perez married artist <a href="/wiki/Eric_Haze" title="Eric Haze">Eric Haze</a> on September 15, 2013 in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BET-Marriage-2013_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-BET-Marriage-2013-37">[37]</a></sup> They had decided to get married the night before while attending the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Floyd_Mayweather_vs._Sa%C3%BAl_%C3%81lvarez" title="Floyd Mayweather vs. Saúl Álvarez">Floyd Mayweather vs. Saúl Álvarez</a> boxing match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HipHollywood-Marriage-2013_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-HipHollywood-Marriage-2013-38">[38]</a></sup> The couple slept over at the <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Las_Vegas" title="MGM Grand Las Vegas">MGM Grand</a> hotel-casino and were married in the morning. They live in <a href="/wiki/Clinton_Hill,_Brooklyn" title="Clinton Hill, Brooklyn">Clinton Hill, Brooklyn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYDN-Handbook-2014_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-NYDN-Handbook-2014-11">[11]</a></sup> Perez was previously married to filmmaker and playwright <a href="/wiki/Seth_Zvi_Rosenfeld" title="Seth Zvi Rosenfeld">Seth Zvi Rosenfeld</a> but the couple divorced in 2001 after ten years of marriage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BET-Marriage-2013_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-BET-Marriage-2013-37">[37]</a></sup> Perez stated on the <i><a href="/wiki/Pineapple_Express_(film)" title="Pineapple Express (film)">Pineapple Express</a></i> DVD commentary that she is allergic to dairy products. She was a friend of the late rapper and actor <a href="/wiki/Tupac_Shakur" title="Tupac Shakur">Tupac Shakur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LatinPost-Madonna2Pac-2014_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-LatinPost-Madonna2Pac-2014-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
