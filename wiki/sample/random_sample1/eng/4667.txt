<p><b>Roccamonfina</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> (municipality) in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Caserta" title="Province of Caserta">Province of Caserta</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> region <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">Campania</a>, located about 60 kilometres (37 mi) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a> and about 40 kilometres (25 mi) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Caserta" title="Caserta">Caserta</a>.</p>
<p>In the communal territory is the extinct <a href="/wiki/Roccamonfina_(volcano)" title="Roccamonfina (volcano)">volcano of Roccamonfina</a>. The fossil human footprints called <a href="/wiki/Ciampate_del_Diavolo" title="Ciampate del Diavolo">Ciampate del Diavolo</a> have been found there. The town is surrounded by dense groves of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sweet_chestnut" title="Sweet chestnut">sweet chestnut</a> trees.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roccamonfina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SantuarioMariaSSLattaniCapitelloChiesa.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1224" data-file-width="1432" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/SantuarioMariaSSLattaniCapitelloChiesa.jpg/220px-SantuarioMariaSSLattaniCapitelloChiesa.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/SantuarioMariaSSLattaniCapitelloChiesa.jpg/330px-SantuarioMariaSSLattaniCapitelloChiesa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/SantuarioMariaSSLattaniCapitelloChiesa.jpg/440px-SantuarioMariaSSLattaniCapitelloChiesa.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SantuarioMariaSSLattaniCapitelloChiesa.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Capital at the entrance of the Santa Maria dei Lattani sanctuary.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the territory of the volcano are traces of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ausoni" title="Ausoni">Ausonian</a> or <a href="/wiki/Aurunci" title="Aurunci">Auruncan</a> settlements, with remains of polygonal walls dating to the 6th-5th century BC. Findings of coins and inscriptions seems to confirm the presence of a settlement in the current town as early as the 3rd century BC, although it is mentioned only in the 10th century AD.</p>
<p>In the Middle Ages and the modern era it was a fief of Neapolitan and Papal noble families, while under the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Bourbon" title="House of Bourbon">Bourbon</a> rulers it was a direct possession of the crown of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Two_Sicilies" title="Kingdom of the Two Sicilies">Two Sicilies</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roccamonfina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cuisine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pizza Chiena AKA Pizza Rustica AKA Pizzagaina...</p>
<p>Pronounced by most Roccamonfinians as simply Pizza-Gain, this Italian Easter favorite was originally made to celebrate the breaking of the fast from meat observed by Catholics during lent. The pie consists of salami, prosciutto, capicola, pepperoni, mozzarella, ricotta and eggs. The pies have since been made famous in New Jersey by Mildred Vento (AKA Camilla Rocco) and her ambitious 34 pie recipe. The recipe is so labor-intensive that modern day families have resorted to an assembly line method in order to complete the task.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roccamonfina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Roccamonfina's main attraction is the sanctuary of <i>Maria Santissima dei Lattani</i>, founded in 1430 by St. <a href="/wiki/Bernardino_of_Siena" title="Bernardino of Siena">Bernardino of Siena</a> and St. <a href="/wiki/James_of_the_Marches" title="James of the Marches">James of the Marches</a> and enlarged to the current shape in 1448–1507. The façade has a large portico with the original wooden door from 1507. The interior has a nave with no aisles, and 15th and 17th centuries frescoes. The Chapel of the Virgin of the Lattani has a statue of "Madonna with Child", dating perhaps to the 9th century. The convent has a two-storey cloister.</p>
<p>The Collegiate church of <i>Santa Maria Maggiore</i> is of medieval origin, but its current appearance dates to the 1715 restoration. In the same square is the <i>Palazzo Colletta</i>.</p>
