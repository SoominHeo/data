<p>The <b>2013 Saravan earthquake</b> occurred with a moment magnitude of 7.7 at 15:14 pm <a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time_in_Iran" title="Daylight saving time in Iran">IRDT</a>(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC%2B4:30" title="UTC+4:30">UTC+4:30</a>) on 16 April. The shock struck a mountainous area between the cities of <a href="/wiki/Saravan,_Iran" title="Saravan, Iran">Saravan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Khash,_Iran" title="Khash, Iran">Khash</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sistan_and_Baluchestan_Province" title="Sistan and Baluchestan Province">Sistan and Baluchestan Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, close to the border with <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_Major_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_Major-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USGS_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-USGS-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> with a duration of about 25 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_Major_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_Major-1">[1]</a></sup> The earthquake occurred at an intermediate depth in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabian_plate" title="Arabian plate">Arabian plate</a> lithosphere, near the boundary between the subducting Arabian Plate and the overriding <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Plate" title="Eurasian Plate">Eurasian Plate</a> at a depth of about 80 km.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_Major_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_Major-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USGS_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-USGS-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was the strongest earthquake in Iran within the last 40 years, equal in magnitude to the one that <a href="/wiki/1978_Tabas_earthquake" title="1978 Tabas earthquake">shook Tabas</a> in 1978 killing 15,000,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and possibly the strongest in the last half-century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_Major_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_Major-1">[1]</a></sup> The earthquake followed a <a href="/wiki/2013_Bushehr_earthquake" title="2013 Bushehr earthquake">magnitude 6.3 event</a> near Bushehr. The town of <a href="/wiki/Mashkel" title="Mashkel">Mashkel</a> was close to the quake's epicentre, and around 85 percent of the city's buildings were demolished.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Thirty-five people were killed in the earthquake.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Primary_effects">Primary effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Saravan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Primary effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The earthquake struck 83 kilometres (52 mi) east of the city of <a href="/wiki/Saravan,_Iran" title="Saravan, Iran">Saravan, Iran</a>, and was felt throughout much of eastern Iran and southern Pakistan, and as far away as <a href="/wiki/Riyadh" title="Riyadh">Riyadh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manama" title="Manama">Manama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doha" title="Doha">Doha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muscat,_Oman" title="Muscat, Oman">Muscat</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_Major_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_Major-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Initial sources cited that close to 100 people lost their lives in Iran and that Iran had confirmed the deaths of 40,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> but these sources have been disputed; Iran has since officially denied death counts, only reporting injuries, as of 14:00 UTC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Although the earthquake was much stronger than previous ones in the area, the depth and the terrain/population density above the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Focus_(earthquake)" title="Focus (earthquake)">focus</a>, as well as predominating building construction being relatively light materials such as mud and wood, meant that there were relatively few casualties in Iran, although the number of casualties was higher in the neighbouring state of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, with at least 34 reported deaths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_Major_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_Major-1">[1]</a></sup> According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Crescent_Society_of_the_Islamic_Republic_of_Iran" title="Red Crescent Society of the Islamic Republic of Iran">Iranian Red Crescent</a>, all communications in the area have been cut, and rescue teams have been dispatched.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Buildings were evacuated as far away as <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a> and on the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>, and many buildings in Iran were completely destroyed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_Major_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_Major-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tectonics">Tectonics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Saravan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tectonics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:215px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arabian_tectonic_plate_overview_map.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="203" data-file-width="213" height="203" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/75/Arabian_tectonic_plate_overview_map.png" width="213"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arabian_tectonic_plate_overview_map.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Arabian plate boundaries with the Eurasian, African and Indian plates</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The quake reportedly occurred within the subducting <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Plate" title="Arabian Plate">Arabian Plate</a> below the overriding <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Plate" title="Eurasian Plate">Eurasian Plate</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Hormuz" title="Strait of Hormuz">Strait of Hormuz</a> separates two forms of plate collision. To the northwest, continental crust portions of the Arabian and Eurasian Plates are colliding, resulting in <a href="/wiki/Continental_collision" title="Continental collision">compressive faulting</a>. To the southeast, dense oceanic crust of the Arabian Plate is <a href="/wiki/Subduction" title="Subduction">subducting</a> underneath the Eurasian Plate at the <a href="/wiki/Makran_Trench" title="Makran Trench">Makran Trench</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Iran lies on the fault line between the Arabian plate and the Eurasian plate. The collision of these two plates cause most of the earthquakes that strike Iran. The specific area of the fault that lies below the <a href="/wiki/Sistan_and_Baluchestan_Province" title="Sistan and Baluchestan Province">Sistan and Baluchestan Province</a> is referred to as the Makran region. In this area the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Plate" title="Arabian Plate">Arabian Plate</a> is folding under, or <a href="/wiki/Subduction" title="Subduction">subducting</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eurasia_Plate" title="Eurasia Plate">Eurasia Plate</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Earthquake_activity">Earthquake activity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Saravan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Earthquake activity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There has been an increase in earthquake activity in not only <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> but also neighboring <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. In the entire 20th century Pakistan and Iran experienced a combined total of twenty-six earthquakes. Within the first thirteen years of the 21st century Pakistan and Iran have suffered a combined total of eighteen earthquakes, with the most recent <a href="/wiki/List_of_earthquakes_in_Iran" title="List of earthquakes in Iran">earthquake in Iran</a> occurring on April 16, 2013 and the most recent <a href="/wiki/List_of_earthquakes_in_Pakistan" title="List of earthquakes in Pakistan">earthquake in Pakistan</a> occurring on September 28, 2013. These earthquakes can range from a <a href="/wiki/Microearthquake" title="Microearthquake">Microearthquake</a>, earning less than a 2.0 on the <a href="/wiki/Richter_magnitude_scale" title="Richter magnitude scale">Richter Magnitude Scale</a>, to a major earthquake, earning a 7.0-7.9 on the Richter magnitude scale. These microearthquakes rank as less than a I on the Mercalli Intensity Scale<a href="/wiki/Mercalli_intensity_scale" title="Mercalli intensity scale">Mercalli Intensity Scale</a>, while the major earthquakes rank as a VIII or higher.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Subduction">Subduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Saravan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Subduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Subduction usually occurs at convergent fault lines. The underlying tectonic plate descends, or subducts, into the <a href="/wiki/Mantle_(geology)" title="Mantle (geology)">Earth's mantle</a>. Earthquakes occurring at these faults are very large in magnitude and scale. In this case the disappearing Arabian plate is part of the oceanic crust while the Eurasia plate is part of the continental crust.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Makran_region">The Makran region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Saravan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Makran region">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Makran" title="Makran">Makran Region</a> Of Pakistan and Iran is characterized by three mountain ranges. These mountain ranges, <a href="/wiki/Makran_Coastal_Range" title="Makran Coastal Range">the Makran Coastal Range</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_Makran_Range" title="Central Makran Range">the Central Makran Range</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Siahan_Range" title="Siahan Range">the Siahan Range</a>, are located in the southwestern area of the Baluchestan province of Pakistan. These ranges are the product of years of collision between <a href="/wiki/Indian_Plate" title="Indian Plate">the Indian Plate</a> and the Eurasia tectonic plate. The Makran Region is also the covering point of three tectonic plates. The Indian plate, Eurasia plate, and the Arabian plate meet and converge in this area.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scientific_preparedness">Scientific preparedness</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Saravan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Scientific preparedness">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There is no method to accurately predict an earthquake, however, there are systems which attempt to give <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earthquake_early_warning" title="Earthquake early warning">early warnings</a> when earthquakes occur. Currently the most all-inclusive system is <a href="/wiki/Earthquake_Early_Warning_(Japan)" title="Earthquake Early Warning (Japan)">Japan's Earthquake Early Warning</a> system. This system is monitored by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japanese Meteorological Agency">Japanese Meteorological Agency</a> The JMA sends out the warnings through the television, mobile devices, the internet, and the radio as soon as an earthquake is detected. Other countries have similar systems that are mostly limited to a particular region, for example, the United States' early warning system is in place only for the earthquake disposed west coast.</p>
<p>One study conducted in California, United States suggests there may be a new way to more precisely presage earthquakes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fault_fluids_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fault_fluids-15">[15]</a></sup> This group of scientists, composed of scientists form <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Institution_for_Science" title="Carnegie Institution for Science">the Carnegie Institution for Science</a> <a href="/wiki/Rice_University" title="Rice University">Rice University</a>,and <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">the University of California, Berkeley</a> monitored earthquake waves in the <a href="/wiki/San_Andreas_Fault" title="San Andreas Fault">San Andreas Fault</a> using seismometers for twenty years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fault_fluids_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fault_fluids-15">[15]</a></sup> This group discovered cracks in the fault filled with fluids.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fault_fluids_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-Fault_fluids-15">[15]</a></sup> The scientists noticed that these fluids shifted after being disrupted by seismic activity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fault_fluids_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-Fault_fluids-15">[15]</a></sup> Shifting fluids cause faults to weaken, making them more prone to earthquakes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fault_fluids_15-4"><a href="#cite_note-Fault_fluids-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Red_Crescent">The Red Crescent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Saravan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Red Crescent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/International_Red_Cross_and_Red_Crescent_Movement" title="International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement">The International Federation of the Red Cross and the Red Crescent Societies</a> is a nongovernmental organization founded at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1919_Paris_Peace_Conference" title="1919 Paris Peace Conference">1919 Paris Peace Conference</a> after World War I. The IFRC has seven fundamental principles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFRC.2_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-IFRC.2-16">[16]</a></sup> These principles include ensuring respect for all humans, making no discrimination as to nationality, religious beliefs, or political opinion, and remaining neutral in times of controversy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFRC.2_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-IFRC.2-16">[16]</a></sup> The IFRC also has a set of goals it aspires to achieve during each disaster recovery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFRC.2_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-IFRC.2-16">[16]</a></sup> These four goals are: reducing the deaths, inures, and impact caused by the disaster; decreasing the amount of death and illnesses caused by diseases and public health emergencies; increasing its ability to address the urgent situations; and promote respect for human dignity while reducing intolerance and discrimination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFRC.2_16-3"><a href="#cite_note-IFRC.2-16">[16]</a></sup> The IFRC has created stock procedure for disaster relief, however the specifics vary based on the type of disaster, the region the disaster affected, and the amount of destruction caused by the disaster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFRC.2_16-4"><a href="#cite_note-IFRC.2-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 9, 2013, the IFRC deployed seventy-five rescue teams to northwestern Iran following a <a href="/wiki/2013_Bushehr_earthquake" title="2013 Bushehr earthquake">6.3 magnitude earthquake</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Red_Cross_Art_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Red_Cross_Art-17">[17]</a></sup> The next day over 800 volunteers were in the disaster area helping those affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Red_Cross_Art_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Red_Cross_Art-17">[17]</a></sup> A week later a 7.8 magnitude earthquake struck southeastern Iran, the IFRC dispatched air assessment teams in addition to forty ambulances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Red_Cross_Art_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-Red_Cross_Art-17">[17]</a></sup> While the first earthquake was much more destructive, the IFRC was still able to aid both disaster areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Red_Cross_Art_17-3"><a href="#cite_note-Red_Cross_Art-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2013, the IFRC and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Crescent_Society_of_the_Islamic_Republic_of_Iran" title="Red Crescent Society of the Islamic Republic of Iran">the Red Crescent Society of the Islamic Republic of Iran</a> agreed to band together in an attempt to redouble their efforts to promote better building codes in disaster prone areas, like Iran.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFRC_and_Iran_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-IFRC_and_Iran-18">[18]</a></sup> These two groups will also work together to strethen each group's capacity to respond to disasters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFRC_and_Iran_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-IFRC_and_Iran-18">[18]</a></sup> Their goals include erecting resilient buildings, improving disaster preparedness, and further developing the IFRC's disaster response guiltiness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFRC_and_Iran_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-IFRC_and_Iran-18">[18]</a></sup> There may be similar partnerships between the IFRC and other countries to achieve similar goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IFRC_and_Iran_18-3"><a href="#cite_note-IFRC_and_Iran-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>The afternoon of April 16, 2013 rocked the Sistan and Baluchestan province of Iran and Pakistan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-19">[19]</a></sup> The earthquake, measuring 7.7 on the moment magnitude scale, was not only felt in this southeastern area of Iran and Pakistan but also as far away as India's capital city of Delhi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-20">[20]</a></sup> The rural city of Khash, the closest city to the epicenter, is part of Iran's most impoverished province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-19">[19]</a></sup> Most of the citizens of the Sistan and Baluchestan province live in either mud shacks or tents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-20">[20]</a></sup> The lack of actual structures contributed to the minimal damage which occurred due to this earthquake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-20">[20]</a></sup> Another contributing factor is the depth of the epicenter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_20-3"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-20">[20]</a></sup> The epicenter of this earthquake was located fifty-nine miles beneath the earth's surface causing damage that would normally be found after an earthquake measuring 4.0 magnitude on the Richter magnitude scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_20-4"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-20">[20]</a></sup> About 127 people were injured, however these injuries were mostly minor broken bones, and the deaths totaled thirty-five people on both the Iranian and Pakistani side of the earthquake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffPost_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-HuffPost-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
