<p><b>Mount Lokon</b>, together with <a href="/wiki/Mount_Empung" title="Mount Empung">Mount Empung</a>, is a twin volcano (2.2 km or 1.4 mi apart) in the northern <a href="/wiki/Sulawesi" title="Sulawesi">Sulawesi</a>, Indonesia, roughly 10 km (6 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Manado" title="Manado">Manado</a>. Both rise above the Tondano plain and are among active volcanoes of Sulawesi. Mount Lokon has a flat and craterless top.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gvp_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-gvp-1">[1]</a></sup> Its active crater is located on its foot, named "Tompaluan" crater.</p>
<p>Lokon formed during a period of andesitic volcanism on ring fractures resulting from the <a href="/wiki/Mount_Tondano" title="Mount Tondano">Tondano caldera</a>'s early to mid-Pleistocene collapse. Recently erupted material remains andesitic in composition <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lahendong_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-lahendong-2">[2]</a></sup> and consists of ash plumes and, less commonly, pyroclastic flows and lava domes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gvp_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-gvp-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The volcano erupted on 15 July 2011, forcing thousands of people to evacuate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The volcano again began showing signs of activity on 10 February 2012, and 19 September 2012 (11:01pm). An eruption occurred at 8:20, am<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> the same day sending an ash plume two miles into the sky. Local residents have been evacuated from a two and a half mile exclusion zone around the volcano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> An eruption occurred on 17 December 2012.</p>
<p>Indonesia has 129 volcanoes including Mount Lokon. The eruption of Mount Lokon in 1991 killed a Swiss hiker and forced thousands of people to flee their homes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
