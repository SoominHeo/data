<p><b>Locrinus</b> was a legendary king of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britons_(historic)" title="Britons (historic)">Britons</a>, as recounted by <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_of_Monmouth" title="Geoffrey of Monmouth">Geoffrey of Monmouth</a>. He was the oldest son of <a href="/wiki/Brutus_of_Troy" title="Brutus of Troy">Brutus</a> and a descendant of the <a href="/wiki/Troy" title="Troy">Trojans</a> through <a href="/wiki/Aeneas" title="Aeneas">Aeneas</a>. Following Brutus's death, Britain was divided amongst the three sons, with Locrinus receiving the portion roughly equivalent to England, Albanactus receiving Scotland (Albany), and Kamber receiving Wales (Cymru). He ruled a portion of <a href="/wiki/British_Iron_Age" title="British Iron Age">Britain</a> called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loegria" title="Loegria">Loegria</a>, named after him, which is roughly the boundaries of modern-day <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. He reigned 10 years, most of which were peaceful.</p>
<p>He avenged his brother <a href="/wiki/Albanactus" title="Albanactus">Albanactus</a>'s death at the hands of <a href="/wiki/Humber_the_Hun" title="Humber the Hun">Humber the Hun</a> by allying with his other brother, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamber" title="Kamber">Kamber</a>, and fighting Humber to the banks of a river where he drowned. The river was named <a href="/wiki/Humber" title="Humber">Humber</a> after this battle. Locrinus divided up the spoils of war with his allies, only keeping gold and silver found on their ships for himself. He also took the daughter of the king of the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estrildis" title="Estrildis">Estrildis</a>, whom the <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a> had captured. This angered <a href="/wiki/Corineus" title="Corineus">Corineus</a>, an ally of his father Brutus, who had arranged a marriage between Locrinus and his own daughter, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Gwendolen" title="Queen Gwendolen">Queen Gwendolen</a>. Locrinus submitted and married Gwendolen but still secretly loved Estrildis, whom he locked in a cave beneath <a href="/wiki/Trinovantum" title="Trinovantum">Trinovantum</a> (<a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>) for seven years.</p>
<p>Locrinus became the father of a girl, <a href="/wiki/Hafren" title="Hafren">Habren</a>, by Estrildis, and a boy, <a href="/wiki/Maddan" title="Maddan">Maddan</a>, by Gwendolen. Soon after Maddan's birth, Locrinus sent him off to Corineus, the child's grandfather. When Corineus finally died, Locrinus left Gwendolen and took Estrildis as his queen. Gwendolen went to <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> and assembled an army to harass Locrinus. The two armies met near the River Stour and there Locrinus was killed. His wife, Gwendolen, ruled after his death.</p>
