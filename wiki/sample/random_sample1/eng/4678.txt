<p>The <b>Rotterdam Metro</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <span lang="nl" xml:lang="nl"><i>Rotterdamse metro</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit</a> system operated in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a> and surrounding municipalities by <a href="/wiki/Rotterdamse_Elektrische_Tram" title="Rotterdamse Elektrische Tram">RET</a>. The first line, called <i>Noord – Zuidlijn</i> (North – South line) opened in 1968 and ran from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rotterdam_Centraal_railway_station" title="Rotterdam Centraal railway station">Centraal Station</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zuidplein_(Rotterdam_Metro)" title="Zuidplein (Rotterdam Metro)">Zuidplein</a>, crossing the river <a href="/wiki/Nieuwe_Maas" title="Nieuwe Maas">Nieuwe Maas</a> in a tunnel. It was the first <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">metro</a> system to open in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. At the time it was also one of the shortest metro lines in the world with a length of only 5.9 km (3.7 mi).</p>
<p>In 1982 a second line was opened, the <i>Oost – Westlijn</i> (East – West line), running between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capelsebrug_(Rotterdam_Metro)" title="Capelsebrug (Rotterdam Metro)">Capelsebrug</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coolhaven_(Rotterdam_Metro)" title="Coolhaven (Rotterdam Metro)">Coolhaven</a> stations. In the late 1990s, the lines were named after two historic <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Citizens" title="Citizens">citizens</a>, the Erasmus Line (North – South) after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Desiderius_Erasmus" title="Desiderius Erasmus">Desiderius Erasmus</a> and the Caland Line (East – West) after <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Caland" title="Pieter Caland">Pieter Caland</a>. As of December 2009, these names were dropped again in favour of a combination of letters and colours, to emphasize and clarify the difference between the separate branches, especially of the former East – West line.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines_A_and_B">Lines A and B</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rotterdam_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lines A and B">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Metro_Delfshaven.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1071" data-file-width="1098" height="215" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Metro_Delfshaven.jpg/220px-Metro_Delfshaven.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Metro_Delfshaven.jpg/330px-Metro_Delfshaven.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Metro_Delfshaven.jpg/440px-Metro_Delfshaven.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Metro_Delfshaven.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rotterdam Metro sign at <a href="/wiki/Delfshaven_metro_station" title="Delfshaven metro station">Delfshaven</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the northeast of Rotterdam, Lines A and B branch to <a href="/wiki/Binnenhof_metro_station" title="Binnenhof metro station">Binnenhof</a> (Line A) and to <a href="/wiki/Nesselande_metro_station" title="Nesselande metro station">Nesselande</a> (Line B). The latter has been extended since September 2005; before that date, this line terminated at <a href="/wiki/De_Tochten_metro_station" title="De Tochten metro station">De Tochten</a>.</p>
<p>North of <a href="/wiki/Capelsebrug_metro_station" title="Capelsebrug metro station">Capelsebrug station</a>, with the exception of the De Tochten-Nesselande section, Lines A and B have some <a href="/wiki/Level_crossing" title="Level crossing">level crossings</a> (with priority), and could therefore be called <a href="/wiki/Light_rail" title="Light rail">light rail</a> instead of metro. These sections also have <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overhead_wires" title="Overhead wires">overhead wires</a>, while most of the system has a <a href="/wiki/Third_rail" title="Third rail">third rail</a> (the other exception is Line E (<a href="/wiki/RandstadRail" title="RandstadRail">RandstadRail</a>) to The Hague). However, the term 'light rail' is not used in Rotterdam; most people just call these branches 'metro'.</p>
<p>As of 2017, lines A and B will be connected to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Schiedam%E2%80%93Hoek_van_Holland_railway&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Schiedam–Hoek van Holland railway (page does not exist)">Schiedam–Hoek van Holland railway</a> line, connecting the metro network to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hoek_van_Holland" title="Hoek van Holland">Hoek van Holland</a>.</p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam_Metro#Future_extensions" title="Rotterdam Metro">Rotterdam Metro § Future extensions</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Line_C">Line C</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rotterdam_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Line C">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At Capelsebrug, line C branches off the main East-West section to <a href="/wiki/De_Terp_metro_station" title="De Terp metro station">De Terp</a> in <a href="/wiki/Capelle_aan_den_IJssel" title="Capelle aan den IJssel">Capelle aan den IJssel</a>. Until November 2002, the Calandlijn (now lines A, B and C) terminated in the west of Rotterdam, at <a href="/wiki/Marconiplein_metro_station" title="Marconiplein metro station">Marconiplein</a>. On 4 November 2002 an extension through the city of <a href="/wiki/Schiedam" title="Schiedam">Schiedam</a> towards <a href="/wiki/Spijkenisse" title="Spijkenisse">Spijkenisse</a> was opened. The extension included four new stations in Schiedam (including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schiedam_Centrum_railway_station" title="Schiedam Centrum railway station">Schiedam Centrum railway station</a>) and one in <a href="/wiki/Pernis,_Netherlands" title="Pernis, Netherlands">Pernis</a>. Line C joins Line D at the new <a href="/wiki/Tussenwater_metro_station" title="Tussenwater metro station">Tussenwater station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hoogvliet" title="Hoogvliet">Hoogvliet</a>. Line A and B trains still terminate at Schiedam Centrum, while Line C trains continue and, like those on the Line D, terminate at <a href="/wiki/De_Akkers_metro_station" title="De Akkers metro station">De Akkers station</a> in Spijkenisse.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Line_D">Line D</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rotterdam_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Line D">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Line D runs from <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam_Centraal_station" title="Rotterdam Centraal station">Rotterdam Centraal</a> via <a href="/wiki/Beurs_metro_station" title="Beurs metro station">Beurs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slinge_metro_station" title="Slinge metro station">Slinge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhoon_metro_station" title="Rhoon metro station">Rhoon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tussenwater_metro_station" title="Tussenwater metro station">Tussenwater</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Spijkenisse_Centrum_metro_station" title="Spijkenisse Centrum metro station">Spijkenisse Centrum</a> towards <a href="/wiki/De_Akkers_metro_station" title="De Akkers metro station">De Akkers</a>.</p>
<p>Line D intersects with Lines A-B-C at <i>Beurs</i> station, the only underground interchange between metrolines in the Netherlands. Before the connection with Line E at Rotterdam Centraal was realized in December 2011, some Line D trains terminated at Slinge during rush hours.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Line_E">Line E</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rotterdam_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Line E">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When the <a href="/wiki/Hofpleinlijn" title="Hofpleinlijn">Hofplein Line</a> was converted from a railway line to a rapid transit line in 2006, the old <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam_Hofplein_railway_station" title="Rotterdam Hofplein railway station">Hofplein station</a> was temporarily kept as the line's southern terminus. On 17 August 2010 however a new tunnel opened, which connected the metro station at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rotterdam_Centraal_railway_station" title="Rotterdam Centraal railway station">Rotterdam Centraal</a> via a new tunnel and new <a href="/wiki/Blijdorp_RandstadRail_station" title="Blijdorp RandstadRail station">Blijdorp station</a> with the existing tracks near <a href="/wiki/Melanchthonweg_RandstadRail_station" title="Melanchthonweg RandstadRail station">Melanchthonweg station</a>.</p>
<p>For the next year, work was in progress to connect Line D to Line E at Rotterdam Centraal station. Since the completion of this project in December 2011, all trains coming from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Hague_Centraal_railway_station" title="The Hague Centraal railway station">The Hague</a> terminate at <a href="/wiki/Slinge_metro_station" title="Slinge metro station">Slinge</a> (these are line E trains), while line D continues in service between <a href="/wiki/De_Akkers_metro_station" title="De Akkers metro station">De Akkers</a> and Rotterdam Centraal.</p>

In 2013, RET announced that it ordered 16 additional vehicles of SG3 stock to run on the Hoekse Line extension. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_extensions">Future extensions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rotterdam_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Future extensions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 2017, the metro network will be extended westward to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hoek_van_Holland" title="Hoek van Holland">Hoek van Holland</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Rotterdamse_Elektrische_Tram" title="Rotterdamse Elektrische Tram">RET</a> will take over passenger transportation services on the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Schiedam%E2%80%93Hoek_van_Holland_railway&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Schiedam–Hoek van Holland railway (page does not exist)">Schiedam–Hoek van Holland railwayline</a> from <a href="/wiki/Nederlandse_Spoorwegen" title="Nederlandse Spoorwegen">NS</a>. The new line will connect to the existing network at <a href="/wiki/Schiedam_Centrum_station" title="Schiedam Centrum station">Schiedam Centrum</a>. From that moment on, line A trains will start at <a href="/wiki/Vlaardingen_West_railway_station" title="Vlaardingen West railway station">Vlaardingen West</a> rather than <a href="/wiki/Schiedam_Centrum_station" title="Schiedam Centrum station">Schiedam Centrum</a>, whereas line B will start at a newly built station near the current <a href="/wiki/Hoek_van_Holland_Strand_railway_station" title="Hoek van Holland Strand railway station">Hoek van Holland Strand</a> station, replacing the latter. The project will also include the construction of a new station at Maassluis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>As part of the "Stadionpark" project, a new line between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zuidplein_(Rotterdam_Metro)" title="Zuidplein (Rotterdam Metro)">Zuidplein</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kralingse_Zoom_(Rotterdam_Metro)" title="Kralingse Zoom (Rotterdam Metro)">Kralingse Zoom</a> is planned. This line will bypass the existing transfer at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beurs_(Rotterdam_Metro)" title="Beurs (Rotterdam Metro)">Beurs</a> and will provide better public transport connections for residential areas on the South bank of Rotterdam.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traction_power">Traction power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rotterdam_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Traction power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Trains run on 750 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volts" title="Volts">volts</a> <a href="/wiki/Direct_current" title="Direct current">DC power</a> which is supplied through a bottom-contact <a href="/wiki/Third_rail" title="Third rail">third rail</a> throughout most of the system. There are multiple <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spring-loaded" title="Spring-loaded">spring-loaded</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contact_shoe" title="Contact shoe">contact shoes</a> on both sides of the vehicle, which are loaded and unloaded automatically due to the slanted edges of third rail ends. This allows the rail to be installed on either side of the track, a necessity around <a href="/wiki/Railroad_switch" title="Railroad switch">points</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Station_platform" title="Station platform">station platforms</a>. There is sufficient overlap between the two rails on either end to avoid a "gapped" train, a situation where none of the shoes are in contact with the live rail. To reduce the risk of <a href="/wiki/Electric_shock" title="Electric shock">electrocution</a>, the rail consists of a sturdy yellow <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_insulation" title="Electrical insulation">insulating</a> material, with the live current carried on a thick metal strip on the bottom side. This also guards against grime (such as from fallen autumn <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaves</a>) reducing or preventing electrical contact.</p>
<p>Three lines do however have sections that use <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overhead_wires" title="Overhead wires">overhead wires</a>. These are lines A (towards <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Binnenhof_(Rotterdam_Metro)" title="Binnenhof (Rotterdam Metro)">Binnenhof</a>), B (towards <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nesselande_(Rotterdam_Metro)" title="Nesselande (Rotterdam Metro)">Nesselande</a>), and E (towards <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Den_Haag_Centraal" title="Den Haag Centraal">Den Haag Centraal</a>). On lines A and B, trains raise or lower their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pantograph_(rail)" title="Pantograph (rail)">pantographs</a> while the vehicle is in motion just east of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capelsebrug_(Rotterdam_Metro)" title="Capelsebrug (Rotterdam Metro)">Capelsebrug station</a>, while on line E this happens while stationary at <a href="/wiki/Melanchthonweg_RandstadRail_station" title="Melanchthonweg RandstadRail station">Melanchtonweg station</a> (this leads to the only level crossing with third rail in the country being at the Kleiweg just outside the tunnel heading to <a href="/wiki/Blijdorp_RandstadRail_station" title="Blijdorp RandstadRail station">Blijdorp station</a>). Note that Line B trains switch back to third rail for the final leg of the journey, from the penultimate station <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/De_Tochten_(Rotterdam_Metro)" title="De Tochten (Rotterdam Metro)">De Tochten</a> to Nesselande. The western extension of lines A and B to Hoek van Holland will also use overhead power as they will be converted directly from the existing railway line.</p>
<p>The sections of the metro that use overhead wires are called <i>sneltram</i> (light rail) by locals, as they include several protected <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Level_crossings" title="Level crossings">level crossings</a> at street level, which trains pass through with priority, as in a conventional railway line. For this reason, trains with a pantograph (series 5200 and 5400) are equipped with <a href="/wiki/Automotive_lighting#Turn_signals" title="Automotive lighting">turn signals</a> just like any road vehicle. This makes it easy to see the difference between series 5300 and series 5400 Bombardier-built trains. Series 5500 and 5600 trains are also equipped, although the former is normally used on line E only as they carry the RandstadRail branding and livery while the latter carries that of RET.</p>
