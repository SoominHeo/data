<p><b>Roxanne Joy Seeman</b> (born in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lyricist" title="Lyricist">lyricist</a>. She is best known for her songs by <a href="/wiki/Billie_Hughes" title="Billie Hughes">Billie Hughes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philip_Bailey" title="Philip Bailey">Philip Bailey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bette_Midler" title="Bette Midler">Bette Midler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earth,_Wind_%26_Fire" title="Earth, Wind &amp; Fire">Earth, Wind &amp; Fire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Sisters_of_Mercy" title="The Sisters of Mercy">The Sisters of Mercy</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Jacksons" title="The Jacksons">The Jacksons</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Cheung" title="Jacky Cheung">Jacky Cheung</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career_and_personal_life">Musical career and personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roxanne_Seeman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career and personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the age of 10, Seeman pursued private piano lessons, learning chords and melody. She took up violin at the Saddle Rock Elementary School. Seeman picked up guitar at 16, while spending a summer in Ithaca, New York.</p>
<p>During Seeman's high school years, she was an avid fine artist. Seeman applied to Carnegie-Mellon University, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania with the intention of pursuing a career in art. In the practice rooms of Carnegie-Mellon, Seeman found a piano teacher and studied classical piano.</p>
<p>At Carnegie-Mellon, Seeman was on the School Activities Board, booking bands to perform on campus. With a passionate interest in both jazz and art, Seeman attended classes offered at the University of Pittsburgh (by reciprocal agreement between the two Universities) that would shape her career. One of these classes was taught by Dr. <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Davis_(saxophonist)" title="Nathan Davis (saxophonist)">Nathan Davis</a>, bebop jazz saxophonist and Director of Jazz Studies at the University of Pittsburgh. Seeman learned to play jazz classics such as "Giant Steps", "Moment's Notice", "Round Midnight", and others.</p>
<p>Wishing to learn Chinese calligraphy, Seeman attended a class in Chinese language where she learned to write Chinese characters. This motivated Seeman to apply as a transfer student to Columbia University in New York, where she would pursue Oriental Studies and be in the mix of the music world in New York City. Over the summers on Long Island, Seeman studied piano with <a href="/wiki/Tony_Aless" title="Tony Aless">Tony Aless</a>, jazz pianist who played with Woody Herman, Charlie Parker, among others. Seeman also took several lessons from Sir <a href="/wiki/Roland_Hanna" title="Roland Hanna">Roland Hanna</a>.</p>
<p>Seeman is a graduate of Columbia University, with a B.A. in Oriental Studies, Chinese Arts and Language. She studied Chinese, Japanese and Indian literature, art, and took a class on Chinese music from Professor <a href="/wiki/Chou_Wen-chung" title="Chou Wen-chung">Chou Wen-chung</a>.</p>
<p>Seeman spent nights in New York jazz clubs while she worked as a temporary secretary at Atlantic Records and Warner Communication in NY. Seeman then began writing lyrics for jazz instrumentals. She developed a close friendship with <a href="/wiki/David_Lasley" title="David Lasley">David Lasley</a>, famed session singer who toured with James Taylor. Seeman produced recorded demos of <a href="/wiki/David_Lasley" title="David Lasley">David Lasley</a> singing her lyrics to the instrumentals.</p>
<p>Seeman relocated to Los Angeles when she was offered a position at ABC Records, soon to be sold to MCA Records. Seeman's career turned to professional songwriting when <a href="/wiki/Dee_Dee_Bridgewater" title="Dee Dee Bridgewater">Dee Dee Bridgewater</a> recorded the Ramsey Lewis instrumental "Tequila Mockingbird" with Seeman's lyrics. <a href="/wiki/Larry_Dunn" title="Larry Dunn">Larry Dunn</a> of <a href="/wiki/Earth,_Wind_%26_Fire" title="Earth, Wind &amp; Fire">Earth, Wind &amp; Fire</a> wrote the tune and <a href="/wiki/George_Duke" title="George Duke">George Duke</a> produced the record. This led to Roxanne's collaboration with Philip Bailey of Earth, Wind &amp; Fire, with whom she wrote, along with <a href="/wiki/Maurice_White" title="Maurice White">Maurice White</a> and Eduardo del Barrio, "Sailaway", for Earth, Wind &amp; Fire's <i>Faces</i> album. During this time, Carmine Coppola and Italia Pennino asked Roxanne to collaborate with them in developing themes from Carmine's movie scores into songs. She co-wrote lyrics and produced song demos for the themes from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Black_Stallion_(film)" title="The Black Stallion (film)">The Black Stallion</a></i>, <i>The Outsiders</i>, and <i>Napoleon</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Seeman co-wrote original songs with David Lasley and then other co-writing partners, producing 24-track recordings at the ABC Recording Studios and other LA studios. Some of the singers and musicians that played for Seeman's recordings include David Lasley, David Benoit, Bobby Watson, Eduardo del Barrio, David Garibaldi, Doug Rodrigues, Terry Reid, Hubert Laws, Debra Laws, Eloise Laws, Sylvia St. James, Marty Walsh, Arnold McCuller and Charlotte Crossley. The early recordings were engineered by Al Schmidt, Jr. and Zoli Osaze. One day walking down the hallway at the ABC Studios looking to book time to mix a session, Seeman heard one of her songs coming out of the production room. She walked in to find Gerry Brown, a young recording engineer of 17, unbeknownst to her, had taken her 24-track 2" inch tape from the shelf over the weekend and mixed it. Brown was listening to it so enthusiastically, she told him he could mix all the others.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Working_with_Billie_Hughes">Working with Billie Hughes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roxanne_Seeman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Working with Billie Hughes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Seeman's partnership with recording artist and songwriter/composer <a href="/wiki/Billie_Hughes" title="Billie Hughes">Billie Hughes</a>, which began in 1983, marked the beginning of a long and successful career of recording, producing and songwriting. Their efforts achieved popularity in Japan in 1991; the couple wrote and produced Hughes' "Welcome to the Edge" #1 single and album. "Welcome to the Edge" remained on Billboard's Japan Top 10 chart for four months, selling 520,000 copies. Seeman and Hughes received #1 International Single of the Year at the NHK Japan Grand Prix Awards, the equivalent of the Grammys.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Working_at_20th_Century_Fox_and_Casting">Working at 20th Century Fox and Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roxanne_Seeman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Working at 20th Century Fox and Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Seeman held the position of Executive Assistant to <a href="/wiki/Scott_Rudin" title="Scott Rudin">Scott Rudin</a>, President of Production for 20th Century Fox in the mid 1980's. In 1987, she worked on the film <i><a href="/wiki/Off_Limits_(1988_film)" title="Off Limits (1988 film)">Off Limits</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Willem_Dafoe" title="Willem Dafoe">Willem Dafoe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Hines" title="Gregory Hines">Gregory Hines</a>, on location in Bangkok and is credited for Thai casting.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Songwriting_career">Songwriting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roxanne_Seeman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Songwriting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Walking_on_the_Chinese_Wall" title="Walking on the Chinese Wall">Walking on the Chinese Wall</a>" is one of Seeman and Hughes' most novel songs, resulting in the title track, chart single and video for Philip Bailey's <i><a href="/wiki/Chinese_Wall_(album)" title="Chinese Wall (album)">Chinese Wall</a></i> album produced by <a href="/wiki/Phil_Collins" title="Phil Collins">Phil Collins</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a> recorded Seeman's "<a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Start_Right_Now" title="Let's Start Right Now">Let's Start Right Now</a>", a Brazilian song with her original English lyric. "Let's Start Right Now" appeared as a bonus CD single in a limited edition of Streisand's <i>A Love Like Ours</i> album.</p>
<p>In 2003, <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Jackson" title="Jermaine Jackson">Jermaine Jackson</a> performed "<a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Start_Right_Now" title="Let's Start Right Now">Let's Start Right Now</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/The_View_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The View (U.S. TV series)">The View</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1991, <a href="/wiki/Bette_Midler" title="Bette Midler">Bette Midler</a> released Seeman &amp; Hughes' "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Night_and_Day_(Roxanne_Seeman_and_Billie_Hughes_song)" title="Night and Day (Roxanne Seeman and Billie Hughes song)">Night And Day</a>", a chart single and music video.</p>
<p>In 1993, <a href="/wiki/The_Sisters_of_Mercy" title="The Sisters of Mercy">The Sisters of Mercy</a> released "<a href="/wiki/Under_the_Gun_(The_Sisters_of_Mercy_song)" title="Under the Gun (The Sisters of Mercy song)">Under the Gun</a>", a chart single and music video, written by Billie Hughes, Roxanne Seeman, and Andrew Eldritch, produced by Andrew Eldritch and Billie Hughes.</p>
<p>In collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Brightman" title="Sarah Brightman">Sarah Brightman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Peterson" title="Frank Peterson">Frank Peterson</a>, Seeman wrote "<a href="/wiki/Harem" title="Harem">Harem</a>", new original English lyrics for "Cancao do Mar", a Portuguese fado made famous by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amalia_Rodrigues" title="Amalia Rodrigues">Amalia Rodrigues</a>. Sarah Brightman's <i>Harem</i> album stayed in the Billboard Top 10 Crossover Classical chart for over 80 weeks.</p>
<p>In December 2008, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daniel_Lindstrom" title="Daniel Lindstrom">Daniel Lindstrom</a> released the single "Caught In That Feeling" in Sweden, written by Seeman, Lindstrom, and Samsson.</p>
<p>In March 2009, Seeman met Philipp Steinke, from Berlin, while he was staying in Los Angeles, and began a writing collaboration. Seeman and Steinke wrote "Amor En Suspenso (Crocodile Tears)" which was recorded by <a href="/wiki/Alejandra_Guzm%C3%A1n" title="Alejandra Guzmán">Alejandra Guzmán</a>, Mexico's "Queen Of Rock", for Guzman's <i><a href="/wiki/%C3%9Anico_(album)" title="Único (album)">Único</a></i> album, released by EMI Latin. Guzman wrote the Spanish lyrics with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Osorio" title="Fernando Osorio">Fernando Osorio</a>. Seeman and Steinke continued to write songs for <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Cheung" title="Jacky Cheung">Jacky Cheung</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lin_Yu_Chun" title="Lin Yu Chun">Lin Yu Chun</a>.</p>
<p>February 2013, Austrian blues-rock singer <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lu" title="Saint Lu">Saint Lu</a> released "Falling For Your Love", written by <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lu" title="Saint Lu">Saint Lu</a>, Roxanne Seeman, and Jimmy Messer, as part of her Warner Germany album "2". The song was written in Venice and Santa Monica. An acoustic recording appears on an EP of acoustic songs from "2". "Falling For Your Love", was premiered on YouTube.</p>
<p>October 21, 2014, Earth, Wind &amp; Fire released "Holiday", their first-ever holiday album, including their recording of Seeman's song "<a href="/wiki/Everyday_Is_Christmas" title="Everyday Is Christmas">Everyday Is Christmas</a>".</p>
<p>December 2015, Eduardo Bosio, Argentinian tenor, recorded Seeman's English lyrics "Part Of Me", adapting Beethoven's "Moonlight Sonata" composition into a new song.</p>
<p>October 28, 2016, <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Nils_Landgren" title="Nils Landgren">Nils Landgren</a>, Swedish R&amp;B funk and jazz trombone player, also known as The Man With The Red Horn or Mr. Redhorn, released "Everyday Is Christmas" as part of his "Christmas With My Friends V" album. Following the release, Nils Landgren toured Germany for a month. The "Christmas With My Friends" collection achieved gold status during this time, selling 125,000 albums.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roxanne_Seeman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Seeman began writing for <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Cheung" title="Jacky Cheung">Jacky Cheung</a>, known as one of the "Four Heavenly Kings" of Canto-Pop. Cheung, nicknamed "God of Songs", has been awarded "Top Asian Pop Artist" by Billboard multiple times. From Seeman's travels to Asia, she was familiar with Cheung and soon developed a close collaborative relationship with Cheung's producer, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Tuason" title="Andrew Tuason">Andrew Tuason</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Cheung" title="Jacky Cheung">Jacky Cheung</a>.</p>
<p>Cheung wanted to try a different style, jazz, for his new album which he called <i>Private Corner</i>, because Cheung says it is a personal expression, something he wanted to do for himself. The first song, "<a href="/wiki/Everyday_Is_Christmas" title="Everyday Is Christmas">Everyday Is Christmas</a>", Seeman co-wrote with <a href="/wiki/Philipp_Steinke" title="Philipp Steinke">Philipp Steinke</a>. Cheung liked "<a href="/wiki/Everyday_Is_Christmas" title="Everyday Is Christmas">Everyday Is Christmas</a>" so much, he asked Seeman and Steinke to write another song and they composed "<a href="/wiki/Which_Way,_Robert_Frost%3F" title="Which Way, Robert Frost?">Which Way, Robert Frost?</a>". Seeman continued to write songs for Cheung in the new "Canto-jazz" style, a phrase Cheung coined to describe the music of his <i>Private Corner</i> album. "不只有緣 (<a href="/wiki/Lucky_in_Love_(song)" title="Lucky in Love (song)">Lucky in Love</a>)", "<a href="/wiki/Double_Trouble_(Jacky_Cheung_song)" title="Double Trouble (Jacky Cheung song)">Double Trouble</a>", "Let It Go", "<a href="/wiki/Which_Way,_Robert_Frost%3F" title="Which Way, Robert Frost?">Which Way, Robert Frost?</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Everyday_Is_Christmas" title="Everyday Is Christmas">Everyday Is Christmas</a>", were co-written with European collaborators and all of the songs were adapted into Cantonese, except "Everyday Is Christmas". Cheung said he tried having the lyrics for "<a href="/wiki/Everyday_Is_Christmas" title="Everyday Is Christmas">Everyday Is Christmas</a>" adapted into Cantonese, but he liked the meaning of the lyrics in English so much that he decided to record it in English. Nokia’s music download service website (Ovi.com) announced that "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Everyday_is_Christmas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Everyday is Christmas (page does not exist)">Everyday is Christmas</a>" was the 10th most downloaded Christmas song in the world in 2010, joining classic hits such as Wham’s ‘Last Christmas’ and Mariah Carey’s "All I Want for Christmas is You". Jacky is the only Chinese language singer to make it into the Top Ten.</p>
<p>In 2009, the newly formed South Korean girl group <a href="/wiki/4Minute" title="4Minute">4Minute</a> with <a href="/wiki/Hyuna" title="Hyuna">Kim Hyun-Ah</a> of The <a href="/wiki/Wonder_Girls" title="Wonder Girls">Wonder Girls</a>, recorded "Tick Tock". When 4 Minute did not release "Tick Tock", it was recorded by Rainie Yang (Sony Taiwan) in Mandarin. "<a href="/wiki/Tick_Tock_(Rainie_Yang_song_Qing_Chun_Dou)" title="Tick Tock (Rainie Yang song Qing Chun Dou)">Tick Tock (Rainie Yang song Qing Chun Dou)</a>" by Rainie Yang was featured in the hit Taiwanese TV drama <i><a href="/wiki/Hi_My_Sweetheart" title="Hi My Sweetheart">Hi My Sweetheart</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Rainie_Yang" title="Rainie Yang">Rainie Yang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Show_Luo" title="Show Luo">Show Luo</a>. "Tick Tock" is co-written with Kine L. Fossheim and Olav Fossheim.</p>
<p>Seeman returned to Asia in August 2009 for five weeks. Since then, her songs have been recorded and released including "All Pumped Up" by <a href="/wiki/Evan_Yo" title="Evan Yo">Evan Yo</a> (Sony Taiwan), "Cha Cha Cha" by <a href="/wiki/Linda_Chung" title="Linda Chung">Linda Chung</a> (Star Entertainment Hong Kong) produced by <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Tuason" title="Andrew Tuason">Andrew Tuason</a>, When You Hear This Song" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allen_Su" title="Allen Su">Allen Su</a> (Sony Beijing), "Arrest Me" by <a href="/wiki/Amber_Kuo" title="Amber Kuo">Amber Kuo</a> (Warner Taiwan), "No One Knows" by <a href="/wiki/Stephy_Tang" title="Stephy Tang">Stephy Tang</a> (Gold Typhoon), and "Hui Bu Hui (Will We)" by <a href="/wiki/Yang_Kun" title="Yang Kun">Yang Kun</a>. Music Videos include Jacky Cheung "<a href="/wiki/Double_Trouble_(Jacky_Cheung_song)" title="Double Trouble (Jacky Cheung song)">Double Trouble</a>", Rainie Yang "Tick Tock", and Stephy Tang "No One Knows".</p>
<p>On New Year's Eve 2010, Jacky Cheung embarked on the Jacky Cheung 1/2 Century Tour, with 25 musicians and 18 dancers, opening at the Mercedes Benz Arena, Shanghai, China. "<a href="/wiki/Double_Trouble_(Jacky_Cheung_song)" title="Double Trouble (Jacky Cheung song)">Double Trouble</a>" is a highlight song in Cheung's stadium world tour. "<a href="/wiki/Double_Trouble_(Jacky_Cheung_song)" title="Double Trouble (Jacky Cheung song)">Double Trouble</a>" is co-written with Kine L. Fossheim and Olav Fossheim.</p>
<p>In 2011, <a href="/wiki/Yang_Kun" title="Yang Kun">Yang Kun</a> recorded Seeman's "Hui Bu Hui (Will We)", co-written with Fredrik Samsson and Tobias Forsberg. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Yadong" title="Zhang Yadong">Zhang Yadong</a>. It is the theme song and promo music video for the Mainland China hit thriller "Lost In Panic Cruise".</p>
<p>In November 2011, <a href="/wiki/Lin_Yu-chun" title="Lin Yu-chun">Lin Yu-chun</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_people" title="Taiwanese people">Taiwanese</a> singer, who gained fame by appearing on a Taiwanese talent show, <i><a href="/wiki/One_Million_Star" title="One Million Star">One Million Star</a></i> (超級星光大道), singing "<a href="/wiki/I_Will_Always_Love_You" title="I Will Always Love You">I Will Always Love You</a>" (written and originally sung by <a href="/wiki/Dolly_Parton" title="Dolly Parton">Dolly Parton</a>) in the style of <a href="/wiki/Whitney_Houston" title="Whitney Houston">Whitney Houston</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover version</a>.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> released "Saving Grace", co-written by Steinke with Roxanne Seeman and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Finn_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Finn Martin (page does not exist)">Finn Martin</a>, on his <i>Endlessly</i> album.</p>
<p>Seeman returned to Hong Kong in May 2012 for the finale concerts of Jacky Cheung's 1/2 Century Tour. From there, Seeman went to Suzhou to meet Han Xue, who recorded "Lonely Kiss" for her Gold Typhoon Mainland China release. She continued to Shanghai, Beijing, where she met Ai Dai, also recording Seeman's songs, and Tokyo.</p>
<p>An original English version of "不只有緣 (<a href="/wiki/Lucky_in_Love_(song)" title="Lucky in Love (song)">Lucky in Love</a>)" was recorded by <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Onesa" title="Paolo Onesa">Paolo Onesa</a>, Top 8 contestant from the first season of <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_of_the_Philippines" title="The Voice of the Philippines">The Voice of the Philippines</a>. It was released digitally as a single on September 23, 2013 by MCA Music, as part of The Voice complitation album. Onesa went on to include the song along with "Which Way, Robert Frost" on his debut album, <i>Pop Goes Standards</i>, released February 14, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Paolo Onesa was awarded twice at the AWIT awards, for Best Performance by A Male Recording Artist and Best Performance by A New Male Recording Artist, both for the song “<a href="/wiki/Lucky_in_Love_(song)" title="Lucky in Love (song)">Lucky in Love</a>.” The AWIT Awards, equivalent of the Grammy Award in The Philippines, occurred on December 12, 2014. Ten years earlier, in 2004, Seeman's song "Goodnite But Not Goodbye" by Nina won an AWIT Award for Best Engineered Recording.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jason_Dy" title="Jason Dy">Jason Dy</a>, winner of <i>The Voice of The Philippines</i> (season 2) performed “Caught In That Feeling” live during the All Star Cast Finale Episode on March 7, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Dy recorded a new version of the song as the first single, released March 30, 2015, from his upcoming album release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>August 2015, <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Onesa" title="Paolo Onesa">Paolo Onesa</a> was nominated for Best Performance by a male recording artist for the song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Which_Way,_Robert_Frost" title="Which Way, Robert Frost">Which Way, Robert Frost</a>?" for the 28th Awit Awards.</p>
<p>August 7, 2015 MCA Music Universal Philippines released Jason Dy's self-titled album "Jason Dy" with Seeman's songs "Caught In That Feeling", "Turn Out The Night" and "When You Hear This Song".</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Zendee_Rose_Tenerefe" title="Zendee Rose Tenerefe">Zendee Rose Tenerefe</a>, aka Zendee, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">Filipina</a> singer who rose to prominence after a video of her singing a <a href="/wiki/Karaoke" title="Karaoke">karaoke</a> version of <a href="/wiki/Whitney_Houston" title="Whitney Houston">Whitney Houston</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/I_Will_Always_Love_You" title="I Will Always Love You">I Will Always Love You</a>" was put on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inquirer_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inquirer-7">[7]</a></sup> recorded "When Love Calls Your Name" and "Watch This!". Both tracks are included on Zendee's album "Z", released on August 7, 2015 by MCA Music Universal Philippines.</p>
<p>September 22, 2016, Kyle Echarri, 13 year old contestant of The Voice Of The Philippines, season 2, released Seeman's song "Our Moment", co-written with Philip Doron Bailey, jr., Jens Hoy, and Rasmus Rudolph Soegaard.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_.26_television">Film &amp; television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roxanne_Seeman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Film &amp; television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Seeman's works have been incorporated in film, television, video and records. Her "So Hard To Know" was written by request for <a href="/wiki/Chet_Baker" title="Chet Baker">Chet Baker</a>. It appears in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Oscar</a>-nominated documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Get_Lost_(1988_film)" title="Let's Get Lost (1988 film)">Let's Get Lost</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Weber_(photographer)" title="Bruce Weber (photographer)">Bruce Weber</a>'s tribute to <a href="/wiki/Chet_Baker" title="Chet Baker">Chet Baker</a>'s life and music, which was re-released in 2008.</p>
<p>The February 19, 1994, <i>The Jackson Family Honors</i> live <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> telecast from <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a> at the <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Las_Vegas" title="MGM Grand Las Vegas">MGM Grand Hotel</a> featured Seeman, Hughes and <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Jackson" title="Jermaine Jackson">Jermaine Jackson</a>'s song "<a href="/wiki/If_You%27d_Only_Believe" title="If You'd Only Believe">If You'd Only Believe</a>" thematically, with a finale performance including <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>, guest artists <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a> and others.</p>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_the_Edge" title="Welcome to the Edge">Welcome to the Edge</a>" received an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emmy_Awards" title="Emmy Awards">Emmy nomination</a> for Best Original Song in the TV drama <i><a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara_(TV_series)" title="Santa Barbara (TV series)">Santa Barbara</a></i> in 1991. In 1994, Seeman and Hughes received a second Emmy nomination for Best Original Song for "Dreamlove" in the TV drama <i><a href="/wiki/Another_World_(TV_series)" title="Another World (TV series)">Another World</a></i>. In 1996, Seeman received Special Recognition for Musical Contribution, Daytime Drama Guiding Light, for her work with, and the Emmy awarded to, Music Director, Jonathan Firstenberg.</p>
<p>With Earth, Wind &amp; Fire, Seeman co-wrote "Cruisin'" for <a href="/wiki/Spike_Lee" title="Spike Lee">Spike Lee</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Get_On_The_Bus" title="Get On The Bus">Get On The Bus</a></i>. A collaboration with Eric Levi of ERA and Philip Bailey of Earth, Wind &amp; Fire resulted in "People And Places", the end-title song of the French film <i>La Vengeance d'Une Blonde</i>.</p>
<p>Seeman co-wrote "Hold On To The Good Things" for <i><a href="/wiki/Stuart_Little_2" title="Stuart Little 2">Stuart Little 2</a></i>, recorded by Grammy-winning artist <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Colvin" title="Shawn Colvin">Shawn Colvin</a>. "Hold On To The Good Things" appears as the second end-credit song.</p>
<p>From <a href="/wiki/William_Ross_(composer)" title="William Ross (composer)">William Ross</a>' theme for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_Black_Stallion" title="The Young Black Stallion">The Young Black Stallion</a></i>, Roxanne and <a href="/wiki/Gavin_Greenaway" title="Gavin Greenaway">Gavin Greenaway</a> developed and produced the song "Born To Ride" sung by Biana Tamimi, the 11-year-old actress from the film, for the DVD release December 2004.</p>
<p>Seeman co-wrote "不只有緣 (<a href="/wiki/Lucky_in_Love_(song)" title="Lucky in Love (song)">Lucky in Love</a>)" with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Nitt" title="Daniel Nitt">Daniel Nitt</a> for <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Cheung" title="Jacky Cheung">Jacky Cheung</a>. "不只有緣 (<a href="/wiki/Lucky_in_Love_(song)" title="Lucky in Love (song)">Lucky in Love</a>)" appears over the end-credits of <i><a href="/wiki/Crossing_Hennessy" title="Crossing Hennessy">Crossing Hennessy</a></i>, a Hong Kong movie produced by <a href="/wiki/William_Kong" title="William Kong">William Kong</a>. "<a href="/wiki/Crossing_Hennessy" title="Crossing Hennessy">Crossing Hennessy</a>", starring <a href="/wiki/Tang_Wei" title="Tang Wei">Tang Wei</a> (Lust, Caution) and Jacky Cheung, premiered as the opening night film for the 34th Hong Kong International Film Festival March 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-1">[1]</a></sup> The track is produced by <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Tuason" title="Andrew Tuason">Andrew Tuason</a>.</p>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Arif_Mardin" title="Arif Mardin">Arif Mardin</a>'s <i>All My Friends Are Here</i> album and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Greatest_Ears_in_Town:_The_Arif_Mardin_Story" title="The Greatest Ears in Town: The Arif Mardin Story">The Greatest Ears in Town: The Arif Mardin Story</a></i> companion documentary, Seeman wrote lyrics for Mardin's composition "So Blue". "So Blue" features <a href="/wiki/Chaka_Khan" title="Chaka Khan">Chaka Khan</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Sanborn" title="David Sanborn">David Sanborn</a>. <i>The Greatest Ears In Town</i> was screened during the 29th International Istanbul Film Festival in April 2010. <i>All My Friends Are Here</i> was released on June 15, 2010. <i>The Greatest Ears in Town: The Arif Mardin Story</i> was nominated for a Grammy in 2011 for Best Long Form Video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NY_Times_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-NY_Times-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-imdb2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Hui Bu Hui (Will We)", recorded by Mainland China recording artist and actor Yang Kun, appears as the theme song of <i>Lost In Panic Cruise</i>, the sequel to <i>Lost In Panic Room</i>. The movie premiered on Mainland China October 27, 2011. Yangkun, actor and singer, appears in the movie and the music video.</p>
<p>Seeman's songs have been appearing in the Nickelodeon television series <i><a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Heights_(TV_series)" title="Hollywood Heights (TV series)">Hollywood Heights</a></i> since June 20, 2012.</p>
<p>Roxanne Seeman and Riccardo Cocciante wrote "<a href="/wiki/When_Love_Calls_Your_Name" title="When Love Calls Your Name">When Love Calls Your Name</a>" for The Voice of Italy finalist <a href="/wiki/Elhaida_Dani" title="Elhaida Dani">Elhaida Dani</a>. A videoclip of the recording session for "When Love Calls Your Name" by Elhaida Dani with Riccardo Cocciante, "Coach" (Judge") and producer, was posted at the Rai Television site, and the song was made available at iTunes Italy for voters. During the May 30, 2013 finale show of The Voice of Italy, Elhaida Dani performed "When Love Calls Your Name". When it was announced that Elhaida Dani wins as The Voice of Italy, Riccardo Cocciante joins Dani on stage and she sings, with Riccardo Cocciante alongside, an emotional reprise performance of "<a href="/wiki/When_Love_Calls_Your_Name" title="When Love Calls Your Name">When Love Calls Your Name</a>".</p>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Tick_Tock_(Rainie_Yang_song_Qing_Chun_Dou)" title="Tick Tock (Rainie Yang song Qing Chun Dou)">Qing Chun Dou</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Rainie_Yang" title="Rainie Yang">Rainie Yang</a> appears in Netflix Marvel's Daredevils, season 2, episode 1. "<a href="/wiki/Tick_Tock_(Rainie_Yang_song_Qing_Chun_Dou)" title="Tick Tock (Rainie Yang song Qing Chun Dou)">Qing Chun Dou</a>" is the Mandarin language version of "<a href="/wiki/Tick_Tock_(Rainie_Yang_song_Qing_Chun_Dou)" title="Tick Tock (Rainie Yang song Qing Chun Dou)">Tick Tock (Beat The Clock)</a>", written by Seeman - Ludvigsen - Fossheim.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_theatre">Musical theatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roxanne_Seeman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Musical theatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For "Jambalaya, The Musical", Roxanne Seeman and Kennard Ramsey, Music Director and Composer, wrote the song "Put It In The Pot". "Jambalaya, The Musical" premiered November 30th at The Orpheum Theatre, New Orleans, for a four night run with a three night return run on December 21, 2016. "Jambalaya, The Musical" is written and directed by Nancy Gregory.</p>
