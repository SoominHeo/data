<p><b>Rockstar</b> (branded <b>ROCKST★R</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Energy_drink" title="Energy drink">Energy Drink</a> created in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-busweek_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-busweek-1">[1]</a></sup> With 14% of the US market in 2008, Rockstar is a leading energy drink brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters200209_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-reuters200209-2">[2]</a></sup> Rockstar is based in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>. As of January 2013, Rockstar Energy Drink is available in more than twenty flavors, and is in more than thirty countries.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockstar_(drink)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Founded in 1998 by <a href="/wiki/Russell_Weiner" title="Russell Weiner">Russell Weiner</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-busweek_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-busweek-1">[1]</a></sup> the son of conservative talkshow host <a href="/wiki/Michael_Savage" title="Michael Savage">Michael Savage</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Rockstar launched into the fastest-growing segment of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US" title="US">US</a> beverage market at the time: <a href="/wiki/Energy_drink" title="Energy drink">energy drinks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt040404_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt040404-4">[4]</a></sup> In addition to featuring different ingredients, which it claimed were "scientifically formulated to speed the recovery time of those who lead active and exhausting lifestyles—from athletes to rock stars,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Rockstar sought to differentiate itself from the market leader, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull" title="Red Bull">Red Bull</a>, by using a 16 oz can size (against Red Bull's 8 oz can) and marketing itself as "twice the size of Red Bull for the same price!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt040404_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-nyt040404-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>By 2007, Rockstar was one of the top three energy drink brands in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, (having enjoyed a 155% growth in sales in 2004,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> reaching $48 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>) and had sold over a billion cans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It had 14% of the US energy drink market in 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters200209_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-reuters200209-2">[2]</a></sup> and as of 2009 was available in over 20 countries, in addition to the United States. Rockstar switched distributors from <a href="/wiki/The_Coca-Cola_Company" title="The Coca-Cola Company">The Coca-Cola Company</a> to <a href="/wiki/PepsiCo" title="PepsiCo">PepsiCo</a> in the summer of 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockstar_(drink)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like its competitor <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull" title="Red Bull">Red Bull</a>, Rockstar sponsors a range of action sports and music events, including the <a href="/wiki/Mayhem_Festival" title="Mayhem Festival">Mayhem Festival</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metal_music" title="Metal music">metal</a> and rock festival touring the United States in July and August; the <a href="/wiki/Uproar_Festival" title="Uproar Festival">Uproar Festival</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">rock</a> festival touring the United States in September and October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Both the Mayhem and Uproar events were discontinued in 2015.</p>

<p>Rockstar products in the US have two levels of <a href="/wiki/Caffeine" title="Caffeine">Caffeine</a> content - either 10 mg of caffeine per ounce, or 15 mg of caffeine per ounce. Rockstar Energy Drink Original contains 160 mg of caffeine per 16 ounce can, while the Rockstar Punched energy drink contain 360 mg of caffeine per 24 ounce can.</p>
<p>Rockstar energy gum was introduced in 2010. There are ten pieces in a pack, and one pack contains the same amount of caffeine as five 8 oz cans. It is available in two flavors: iced mint and iced mint orange.</p>
<p>Besides caffeine and <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a>, Rockstar Energy Drink contains a variety of herbs, including <a href="/wiki/Panax_ginseng" title="Panax ginseng">panax ginseng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ginkgo_biloba" title="Ginkgo biloba">ginkgo biloba</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milk_thistle" title="Milk thistle">milk thistle</a> extract, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guarana" title="Guarana">guarana</a> seed. The amount of guarana used to be higher, but "after being criticized for including guarana once health concerns about the herb were publicized, the amount in the drink was significantly reduced."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-legalzoom_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-legalzoom-13">[13]</a></sup> It also includes 1000 mg of <a href="/wiki/Taurine" title="Taurine">taurine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Side_effects">Side effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rockstar_(drink)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Side effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As with all energy drinks, Rockstar can cause jitteriness, head aches, <a href="/wiki/Anxiety" title="Anxiety">anxiety</a>, and high <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blood_sugar_levels" title="Blood sugar levels">blood sugar levels</a>. If mixed with alcohol it may also mask the level of <a href="/wiki/Alcohol_intoxication" title="Alcohol intoxication">alcohol intoxication</a>. There are no known side-effects if used in moderation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Several alcoholic versions of Rockstar are available in Canada; an alcoholic version in the US was discontinued in 2007, possibly in response to criticism that young people were confusing the alcoholic version with the regular one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rockstar Original was named Worst Energy Drink by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Men%27s_Health_(magazine)" title="Men's Health (magazine)">Men's Health</a></i> magazine for having 280 calories due to 67.5 grams of sugar. <a href="/wiki/Monster_Energy" title="Monster Energy">Monster Energy</a> Lo-Carb, with 20 calories, was suggested as a replacement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rockstar has introduced a line of low calorie energy drinks that include electrolytes called Rockstar Recovery, partially in response to these concerns.</p>

<p>Members of the <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> community have supported a boycott because the company's founder and <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a> is the son of controversial radio personality <a href="/wiki/Michael_Savage" title="Michael Savage">Michael Savage</a>, and because its former <a href="/wiki/Chief_financial_officer" title="Chief financial officer">CFO</a>, Janet Weiner, is Savage's wife and also CFO of Savage Productions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edge_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-edge-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gayw040609_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-gayw040609-20">[20]</a></sup> The boycott was motivated by allegations that Savage had made comments perceived to be homophobic, racist, and discriminatory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alternet_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-alternet-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In May 2009 San Francisco mayor <a href="/wiki/Gavin_Newsom" title="Gavin Newsom">Gavin Newsom</a> returned a $25,000 donation which Rockstar had made to his 2010 re-election campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gayw040609_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-gayw040609-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>During an interview with the San Francisco Chronicle, Eric Jaye of the campaign, stated the money was returned because "there was some statements made during (Weiner's) 1998 campaign in the GOP primary which conflicted with the mayor's position and we're returning the check," he said.</p>
<p>In a later interview Weiner told the SF Gate: "I still wish Gavin well. I always will." But he said that with the donation rejected, "I'm taking this money and I'm donating it to charity. We're telling them to name a charity of their choosing, and if they don't want it, we'll donate it to <a href="/wiki/Project_Open_Hand" title="Project Open Hand">Project Open Hand</a>."</p>
<p>Rockstar CFO Janet Weiner had stepped down from her role as CFO of Savage Productions "as an apparent statement of solidarity with equality advocates" which had not been requested as part of the agreement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rockstar, saying that the "truthaboutrockstarenergydrink.com" domain name was registered and used in bad faith, obtained control of that site under a decision from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Arbitration_Forum" title="National Arbitration Forum">National Arbitration Forum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
