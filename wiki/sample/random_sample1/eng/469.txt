<p>The <b>2013 United Kingdom local elections</b> took place on Thursday 2 May 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-communities_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-communities-5">[4]</a></sup> Elections were held in 35 English councils: all 27 <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_county" title="Non-metropolitan county">non-metropolitan county councils</a> and eight <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">unitary authorities</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Anglesey_County_Council" title="Isle of Anglesey County Council">one Welsh unitary authority</a>. Direct mayoral elections took place in <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Doncaster" title="Metropolitan Borough of Doncaster">Doncaster</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Tyneside" title="North Tyneside">North Tyneside</a>. These elections last took place on the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_local_elections,_2009" title="United Kingdom local elections, 2009">4 June 2009</a> at the same time as the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2009_(United_Kingdom)" title="European Parliament election, 2009 (United Kingdom)">2009 European Parliament Elections</a>, except for County Durham, Northumberland and the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Anglesey_County_Council" title="Isle of Anglesey County Council">Anglesey</a> where elections last took place in 2008.</p>
<p>On the same day a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_United_Kingdom_by-elections_(1979%E2%80%93present)" title="List of United Kingdom by-elections (1979–present)">parliamentary by-election</a> took place in the <a href="/wiki/North_East_England" title="North East England">North East</a> constituency of <a href="/wiki/South_Shields_by-election,_2013" title="South Shields by-election, 2013">South Shields</a> following the departure of <a href="/wiki/David_Miliband" title="David Miliband">David Miliband</a>, with the Labour Party retaining the seat.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria_to_vote">Criteria to vote</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_local_elections,_2013&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Criteria to vote">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All <a href="/wiki/Elections_in_the_United_Kingdom#Registration_procedure" title="Elections in the United Kingdom">registered electors</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_citizen" title="British citizen">British</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_citizen" title="Irish citizen">Irish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_citizen" title="Commonwealth citizen">Commonwealth</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> citizens) aged 18 or over were entitled to vote in the local elections. Those temporarily away from their ordinary address (for example, away working, on holiday, in student accommodation or in hospital) were also entitled to vote in the local elections,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup> as were those who had moved abroad and registered as overseas electors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>Labour and UKIP made substantial gains, but the Conservatives won the most councillors and retained control of most councils. The BBC published projected national vote shares, adjusting for which regions are holding local elections and extrapolating to the national situation. These were Labour 29%, Conservatives 25%, UKIP 23% and the Liberal Democrats 14%. This is the lowest figure for the Conservatives since 1982 and the lowest ever figure for the Liberal Democrats. It is also the first time that none of the three main parties in the Commons has scored 30% or more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-10">[9]</a></sup> Rallings &amp; Thrasher separately calculated a projected national vote share of Labour 29%, Conservatives 26%, UKIP 22% and the Liberal Democrats 13%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The actual votes received were Conservative 34.3%, Labour 21.1%, UKIP 19.9%, Lib Dem 13.8% and Green 3.5%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Overall, in England, the Conservatives saw 1116 councillors elected (down 335), Labour 538 (up 291), the Liberal Democrats 352 (down 124), independents 165 (up 24), UKIP 147 (up 139), the Greens 22 (up 5), Residents Associations 12 (up 2), <a href="/wiki/Mebyon_Kernow" title="Mebyon Kernow">Mebyon Kernow</a> 4 (up 1), the Liberal Party 3 (up 1) and <a href="/wiki/Independent_Community_and_Health_Concern" title="Independent Community and Health Concern">Independent Community and Health Concern</a> 2 (no change). The BNP won no seats (down 3).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-metropolitan_county_councils">Non-metropolitan county councils</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_local_elections,_2013&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Non-metropolitan county councils">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

These were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First-past-the-post" title="First-past-the-post">first-past-the-post</a> elections in a mixture of single-member and multi-member electoral divisions.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unitary_authorities">Unitary authorities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_Kingdom_local_elections,_2013&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Unitary authorities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Eight single-tier <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">unitary authorities</a> held elections. Seven of these had all of their seats up for election, whilst one, <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Bristol" title="Politics of Bristol">Bristol</a>, elected a third of its seats. These are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First-past-the-post" title="First-past-the-post">first-past-the-post</a> elections in a mixture of single-member and multi-member wards.</p>
