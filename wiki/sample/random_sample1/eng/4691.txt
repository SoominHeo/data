<p><b>Ronald Earle "Ron" Glass</b> (July 10, 1945 – November 25, 2016) was an American <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>. He was known for his roles as literary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ron_Harris_(detective)" title="Ron Harris (detective)">Det. Ron Harris</a> in the television <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Barney_Miller" title="Barney Miller">Barney Miller</a></i> (1975–1982), and as the spiritual Shepherd <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Derrial_Book" title="Derrial Book">Derrial Book</a> in the 2002 science fiction series <i><a href="/wiki/Firefly_(TV_series)" title="Firefly (TV series)">Firefly</a></i> and its sequel film <i><a href="/wiki/Serenity_(film)" title="Serenity (film)">Serenity</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ron_Glass&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Glass was born in <a href="/wiki/Evansville,_Indiana" title="Evansville, Indiana">Evansville, Indiana</a>, the son of Lethia and Crump Glass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Graduating from <a href="/wiki/St._Francis_Seminary_(Ohio)" title="St. Francis Seminary (Ohio)">Saint Francis Seminary</a> in 1964, Glass attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Evansville" title="University of Evansville">University of Evansville</a>, where he received a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a>, double majoring in <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Literature" title="Literature">literature</a>. Years later, the university awarded him its Medal of Honor. Glass said in 2007 he knew while he was in college that he wanted to act. With a teacher's encouragement, he performed in a play and went on to an acting career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>Glass made his <a href="/wiki/Stage_(theatre)" title="Stage (theatre)">stage</a> debut at the <a href="/wiki/Guthrie_Theater" title="Guthrie Theater">Guthrie Theater</a> in <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis, Minnesota</a> before moving to <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a>. His earliest TV appearances include episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Sanford_and_Son" title="Sanford and Son">Sanford and Son</a></i> in 1972, an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Five-O" title="Hawaii Five-O">Hawaii Five-O</a></i> in 1973, an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/All_in_the_Family" title="All in the Family">All in the Family</a></i> in 1973, an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bob_Newhart_Show" title="The Bob Newhart Show">The Bob Newhart Show</a>,</i> and episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Times" title="Good Times">Good Times</a></i> in 1974. In 1975, he landed the role of Det. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ron_Harris_(detective)" title="Ron Harris (detective)">Ron Harris</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Barney_Miller" title="Barney Miller">Barney Miller</a>,</i> which ran until 1982. The following season, Glass also co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Demond_Wilson" title="Demond Wilson">Demond Wilson</a> on television in the short-lived remake of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Odd_Couple_(TV_series)" title="The Odd Couple (TV series)">The Odd Couple</a>,</i> called <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Odd_Couple" title="The New Odd Couple">The New Odd Couple</a></i>. On December 13, 1985, he played a soul-collecting devil opposite Sherman Hemsley's mathematics professor in an episode of the revived <i>Twilight Zone</i> series. In 1992, he co-starred in the short-lived <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Rhythm_%26_Blues_(TV_series)" title="Rhythm &amp; Blues (TV series)">Rhythm and Blues</a></i>, a kind of "black <i><a href="/wiki/WKRP_in_Cincinnati" title="WKRP in Cincinnati">WKRP</a></i>," playing "the fifth <a href="/wiki/Four_Tops" title="Four Tops">Top</a>" opposite <a href="/wiki/Roger_Kabler" title="Roger Kabler">Roger Kabler</a>. In 1996, Glass was cast as uptight history teacher Roland Felcher in NBC sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Rhodes" title="Mr. Rhodes">Mr. Rhodes</a></i> opposite comedian <a href="/wiki/Tom_Rhodes" title="Tom Rhodes">Tom Rhodes</a>. In 1999, he appeared in an episode of <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a></i> as Ross Geller's divorce lawyer, Russell.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After that, Glass appeared in dozens of television series, including sitcoms such as <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Matters" title="Family Matters">Family Matters</a></i> and the series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teen_Angel_(TV_series)" title="Teen Angel (TV series)">Teen Angel</a></i>, where he played God's cousin Rod. He had a guest appearance in the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Voyager" title="Star Trek: Voyager">Star Trek: Voyager</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Nightingale_(Star_Trek:_Voyager)" title="Nightingale (Star Trek: Voyager)">Nightingale</a>". He was a series regular in the <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Firefly_(television_series)" title="Firefly (television series)">Firefly</a></i> (2002) and the sequel film <i><a href="/wiki/Serenity_(film)" title="Serenity (film)">Serenity</a></i> (2005), in which he played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Derrial_Book" title="Derrial Book">Derrial Book</a>, a Christian<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bookbio_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-bookbio-3">[3]</a></sup> "Shepherd" with a mysterious past. Glass provided the voice of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_All_Grown_Up!_characters" title="List of All Grown Up! characters">Randy Carmichael</a> for the Nickelodeon series <i><a href="/wiki/All_Grown_Up!" title="All Grown Up!">All Grown Up!</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rugrats" title="Rugrats">Rugrats</a></i>, and the character Garth in the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Fable_II" title="Fable II">Fable II</a></i>. In 2008 he appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Lakeview_Terrace" title="Lakeview Terrace">Lakeview Terrace</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Samuel_L._Jackson" title="Samuel L. Jackson">Samuel L. Jackson</a>, and starred in the 2010 version of <i><a href="/wiki/Death_at_a_Funeral_(2010_film)" title="Death at a Funeral (2010 film)">Death at a Funeral</a></i> as Duncan.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
