<p><b>Ron Moody</b> (born <b>Ronald Moodnick</b>; 8 January 1924 – 11 June 2015) was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, singer, composer and writer best known for his portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Fagin" title="Fagin">Fagin</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Oliver!_(film)" title="Oliver! (film)">Oliver!</a></i> (1968) and its <a href="/wiki/Oliver!#1983_London_and_Broadway_revivals" title="Oliver!">1983 Broadway revival</a>. Moody earned a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a> and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> nomination for the film, as well as a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> nomination for the stage production. Other notable projects include <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mouse_on_the_Moon" title="The Mouse on the Moon">The Mouse on the Moon</a></i> (1963), <a href="/wiki/Mel_Brooks" title="Mel Brooks">Mel Brooks</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twelve_Chairs_(1970_film)" title="The Twelve Chairs (1970 film)">The Twelve Chairs</a></i> (1970) and <i><a href="/wiki/Flight_of_the_Doves" title="Flight of the Doves">Flight of the Doves</a></i> (1971), in which Moody shared the screen with <i>Oliver!</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Jack_Wild" title="Jack Wild">Jack Wild</a>.</p>
<p>Moody holds the distinction of having portrayed the wizard <a href="/wiki/Merlin" title="Merlin">Merlin</a> in two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a> films, <i><a href="/wiki/Unidentified_Flying_Oddball" title="Unidentified Flying Oddball">Unidentified Flying Oddball</a></i> (1979) and <i><a href="/wiki/A_Kid_in_King_Arthur%27s_Court" title="A Kid in King Arthur's Court">A Kid in King Arthur's Court</a></i> (1995).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ron_Moody&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moody was born in <a href="/wiki/Tottenham" title="Tottenham">Tottenham</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_London" title="North London">north London</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> England, the son of Kate (née Ogus; 1898-1980) and Bernard/Barnett Moodnick (1896-1964), a studio executive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmr_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmr-3">[3]</a></sup> His father was a <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Russia" title="History of the Jews in Russia">Russian Jew</a> and his mother was a <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_Jews" title="Lithuanian Jews">Lithuanian Jew</a>; said Moody, "I'm 100% <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jewish</a>—totally kosher!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He was a cousin of <a href="/wiki/Television_director" title="Television director">director</a> <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Moody" title="Laurence Moody">Laurence Moody</a> and actress <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clare_Lawrence" title="Clare Lawrence">Clare Lawrence</a>. His surname was legally changed to Moody in 1930.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmr_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-filmr-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ron_Moody&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moody was educated at <a href="/wiki/Southgate_School" title="Southgate School">Southgate County School</a>, which at the time was a state grammar school, and based in <a href="/wiki/Palmers_Green" title="Palmers Green">Palmers Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middlesex" title="Middlesex">Middlesex</a>, followed by the <a href="/wiki/London_School_of_Economics" title="London School of Economics">London School of Economics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Central_London" title="Central London">Central London</a>, where he trained to become an economist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup> During World War II he enlisted in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> (RAF) and became a radar technician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ron_Moody&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite training to be an economist, Moody began appearing in theatrical shows and later decided to become a professional actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:28%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"My proudest moment was the number "Reviewing the Situation". I suspect that, because I gave my all to the role, and because I was working with such a fine team of people, it inhibited my future career. I turned down quite a few offers afterwards because I thought the people didn't come close to those I'd worked with on <i><a href="/wiki/Oliver!_(film)" title="Oliver! (film)">Oliver!</a></i>—which in retrospect was a mistake."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">—Moody on his acclaimed role as Fagin and subsequent career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>Moody worked in a variety of genres, but is perhaps best known for his starring role as <a href="/wiki/Fagin" title="Fagin">Fagin</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Bart" title="Lionel Bart">Lionel Bart</a>'s stage and film musical <i><a href="/wiki/Oliver!" title="Oliver!">Oliver!</a></i> based on <i><a href="/wiki/Oliver_Twist" title="Oliver Twist">Oliver Twist</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Dickens" title="Charles Dickens">Charles Dickens</a>. He created the role in the original <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a> production in 1960, and reprised it in the 1984 <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> revival, garnering a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> nomination for Best Actor in a Musical. For his performance in the 1968 film <i><a href="/wiki/Oliver!_(film)" title="Oliver! (film)">Oliver!</a></i>, he received the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a> for Best Actor (Musical/Comedy), the Best Actor award at the <a href="/wiki/6th_Moscow_International_Film_Festival" title="6th Moscow International Film Festival">6th Moscow International Film Festival</a> and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> nomination in the same category.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moscow1969_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Moscow1969-7">[7]</a></sup> Reflecting on the role, Moody states: "Fate destined me to play Fagin. It was the part of a lifetime. That summer of 1967 [during filming] was one of the happiest times of my life".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup> He reprised his role as Fagin at the 1985 <a href="/wiki/Royal_Variety_Performance" title="Royal Variety Performance">Royal Variety Performance</a> in <a href="/wiki/Theatre_Royal,_Drury_Lane" title="Theatre Royal, Drury Lane">Theatre Royal, Drury Lane</a> before Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">Duke of Edinburgh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Moody appeared in several children's television series, including the voice of Badger and Toad in the TV Adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Colin_Dann" title="Colin Dann">Colin Dann</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Animals_of_Farthing_Wood_(TV_series)" title="The Animals of Farthing Wood (TV series)">The Animals of Farthing Wood</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Noah%27s_Island" title="Noah's Island">Noah's Island</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telebugs" title="Telebugs">Telebugs</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Labyrinth_(TV_series)" title="Into the Labyrinth (TV series)">Into the Labyrinth</a></i>, and the <i><a href="/wiki/Discworld" title="Discworld">Discworld</a></i> series.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Among his better known roles was that of Prime Minister Rupert Mountjoy in the comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mouse_on_the_Moon" title="The Mouse on the Moon">The Mouse on the Moon</a></i> (1963), alongside <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Rutherford" title="Margaret Rutherford">Margaret Rutherford</a>, with whom he appeared again the following year in <i><a href="/wiki/Murder_Most_Foul" title="Murder Most Foul">Murder Most Foul</a></i> (1964), one of Rutherford's Miss Marple films. He played French entertainer and <a href="/wiki/Mime_artist" title="Mime artist">mime artist</a> The Great Orlando in the 1963 <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Richard" title="Cliff Richard">Cliff Richard</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_Holiday_(1963_film)" title="Summer Holiday (1963 film)">Summer Holiday</a></i>. He acted again with former <i>Oliver!</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Jack_Wild" title="Jack Wild">Jack Wild</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Flight_of_the_Doves" title="Flight of the Doves">Flight of the Doves</a></i> (1971).</p>
<p>In 1969, Moody was offered, but declined, the lead role in <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i>, following the departure of <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Troughton" title="Patrick Troughton">Patrick Troughton</a> from the part.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stevens_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stevens-9">[9]</a></sup> He later told many people (including <i>Doctor Who</i> companion <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_Sladen" title="Elisabeth Sladen">Elisabeth Sladen</a>) that declining the role was a decision he subsequently regretted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup> He played Ippolit Vorobyaninov alongside <a href="/wiki/Frank_Langella" title="Frank Langella">Frank Langella</a> (as <a href="/wiki/Ostap_Bender" title="Ostap Bender">Ostap Bender</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Mel_Brooks" title="Mel Brooks">Mel Brooks</a>' version of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twelve_Chairs_(1970_film)" title="The Twelve Chairs (1970 film)">The Twelve Chairs</a></i> (1970). In 2003, he starred in the black comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Paradise_Grove" title="Paradise Grove">Paradise Grove</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Rula_Lenska" title="Rula Lenska">Rula Lenska</a>, and played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edwin_Caldecott" title="Edwin Caldecott">Edwin Caldecott</a>, an old nemesis of <a href="/wiki/Jim_Branning" title="Jim Branning">Jim Branning</a> on the BBC soap <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-6"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2005, he acted in the <a href="/wiki/Big_Finish_Productions" title="Big Finish Productions">Big Finish Productions</a> <i>Doctor Who</i> audio play <i><a href="/wiki/Other_Lives" title="Other Lives">Other Lives</a></i>, playing the <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Wellesley,_1st_Duke_of_Wellington" title="Arthur Wellesley, 1st Duke of Wellington">Duke of Wellington</a>. He made several appearances in BBC TVs long running variety show, The Good Old Days, enacting pastiche/comic Victorian melodramas.</p>
<p>In 2004, the British <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITV1" title="ITV1">ITV1</a> nostalgia series <i>After They Were Famous</i> hosted a documentary of the surviving cast of the film <i>Oliver!</i> Several of the film's musical numbers were reenacted. Moody, then 80 but still spry, and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Wild" title="Jack Wild">Jack Wild</a> (seriously ill with oral cancer at the time) recreated their dance from the closing credits of the film.</p>
<p>Moody appeared in an episode of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC1" title="BBC1">BBC1</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Casualty_(TV_series)" title="Casualty (TV series)">Casualty</a></i> (aired on 30 January 2010) as a Scottish patient who had served with the <a href="/wiki/Black_Watch" title="Black Watch">Black Watch</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-7"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup> On 30 June 2010, Moody appeared on stage at the end of a performance of <a href="/wiki/Cameron_Mackintosh" title="Cameron Mackintosh">Cameron Mackintosh</a>'s revival of <i>Oliver!</i> and made a humorous speech about the show's 50th anniversary. He then reprised the "<a href="/wiki/You%27ve_Got_to_Pick_a_Pocket_or_Two" title="You've Got to Pick a Pocket or Two">Pick a Pocket or Two</a>" number with the cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6-8"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ron_Moody&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moody married a <a href="/wiki/Pilates" title="Pilates">Pilates</a> teacher, Therese Blackbourn, in 1985. The couple had six children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ron_Moody&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
