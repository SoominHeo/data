<p><b>Rolando Bianchi</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[roˈlando ˈbjaŋki]</a></span>; born 15 February 1983) is an Italian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/A.C._Perugia_Calcio" title="A.C. Perugia Calcio">Perugia</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>
<p>With 77 total goals, he is <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a>'s 10th-highest all-time goal scorer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atalanta">Atalanta</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Atalanta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A product of the Atalanta youth system, Bianchi made his Serie A debut at the age of 18 during the <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Serie_A" title="2000–01 Serie A">2000–01 Serie A</a> season. He played the final 10 minutes in a 2–1 loss to Juventus on 17 June 2001. His only appearance during the season. The following <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_Serie_A" title="2001–02 Serie A">season</a> he made an additional 3 appearances for the <i>bergamaschi</i>, while during the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Serie_A" title="2002–03 Serie A">2002–03 Serie A</a> season he made 16 appearances. Despite being a prolific scorer in the youth ranks, he played 21 games for the first team without scoring any goals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cagliari">Cagliari</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cagliari">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He was sold in co-ownership to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> team <a href="/wiki/Cagliari_Calcio" title="Cagliari Calcio">Cagliari</a> in January 2004, where he played 14 matches and scored two goals helping them gain promotion to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a>. The <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Serie_A" title="2004–05 Serie A">2004–05</a> season, for which he remained at Cagliari, saw him score 2 goals in 25 matches.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reggina">Reggina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reggina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bianchi was promptly sold to Reggina in 2005, where his first season was hampered by a serious knee injury suffered early on in the season. He recovered to play 9 games late in the season, scoring one goal.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Serie_A" title="2006–07 Serie A">2006–07</a> season saw Bianchi score 18 goals in 37 games. Due to their indictment for sporting fraud, the club had started the season with an 11-point deduction and seemed destined for relegation. They managed to save themselves, however, with Bianchi's performances considered a key part of Reggina's Serie A survival. He finished the season 4th in the league's goal scoring table.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_City">Manchester City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Manchester City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His form for Reggina attracted the attention of clubs throughout Europe, and in July 2007 Bianchi moved to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> for a reported fee of £8.8 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (€13 million), where he was given the number 10 shirt. Bianchi scored on his debut for <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">City</a> against <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham</a>, slotting home from close range following a run and pass from <a href="/wiki/Elano" title="Elano">Elano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Bianchi's second goal came in a 2–1 victory in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">Football League Cup</a> 2nd round against <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City F.C.</a>. Bianchi then scored his third goal in English football in a 2–1 defeat against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur" title="Tottenham Hotspur">Tottenham</a> on 9 December 2007. Then on the following Saturday he scored the first goal against Bolton in a 4–2 victory. Later that month, Bianchi criticised English cuisine and alcohol culture, and stated how he could never become a full international unless he returned to Serie A.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Lazio">Loan to Lazio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Loan to Lazio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In January 2008, Manchester City manager <a href="/wiki/Sven-G%C3%B6ran_Eriksson" title="Sven-Göran Eriksson">Sven-Göran Eriksson</a> informed Bianchi that he would be allowed to leave the club. On 23 January 2008 Bianchi finalised a loan move to <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a> after not being able to settle in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In his debut with Lazio on 27 January, Bianchi was sent off after picking up two yellow cards in just under five minutes, as the capital club drew 0–0 with Torino.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for Lazio against former club Reggina from a penalty kick in a 1–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In spite of Bianchi's hope that Lazio would make his move permanent, he had in fact been asked to return to Manchester City.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Torino">Torino</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Torino">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 August 2008, after a summer of much speculation about a possible move, it was officially announced by Manchester City that Bianchi had signed for Italian club <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a> on a five-year deal,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> for a fee of €5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 August 2012, Bianchi scored two goals in Torino's 4–2 defeat of <a href="/wiki/U.S._Lecce" title="U.S. Lecce">U.S. Lecce</a> in the Third Round of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Coppa_Italia" title="2012–13 Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a>, Torino's first competitive game of the new season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He scored two goals in the 5–1 defeat of <a href="/wiki/Atalanta_B.C." title="Atalanta B.C.">Atalanta</a> on 30 September, reaching 70 goals with Torino.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> With these strikes, he entered into the Top 10 scorers for the club, tied with club legend, <a href="/wiki/Ezio_Loik" title="Ezio Loik">Ezio Loik</a>. Bianchi helped the club to a 3–2 victory over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Siena" title="AC Siena">Siena</a> on 13 January 2013, heading in a cross from <a href="/wiki/Valter_Birsa" title="Valter Birsa">Valter Birsa</a> in the later stages of the first-half.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bologna">Bologna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Bologna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 July 2013, Bianchi was signed by <a href="/wiki/Bologna_F.C._1909" title="Bologna F.C. 1909">Bologna</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Bologna relegated at the end of season. Bianchi wore no.9 shirt that season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 5 August 2014, Bianchi returned to Atalanta in a temporary deal, with <a href="/wiki/Rub%C3%A9n_Bentancourt" title="Rubén Bentancourt">Rubén Bentancourt</a> moved to opposite direction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On the same day Bologna signed <a href="/wiki/Daniele_Cacia" title="Daniele Cacia">Daniele Cacia</a>.</p>
<p>On 8 August 2015, he was released by Bologna.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mallorca">Mallorca</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mallorca">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 27 August 2015, Bianchi joined <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> After contributing with only two goals in 16 matches, he rescinded his contract and moved to <a href="/wiki/A.C._Perugia_Calcio" title="A.C. Perugia Calcio">Perugia</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Perugia">Perugia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Perugia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bianchi signed a 1½year contract with <a href="/wiki/A.C._Perugia_Calcio" title="A.C. Perugia Calcio">Perugia</a> on 20 January 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolando_Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the summer of 2006, Bianchi was called up to the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy U-21</a> squad for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_U-21_Championship_2006" title="UEFA U-21 Championship 2006">2006 U-21 Championships</a> in Portugal, where he appeared 3 times and scored 1 goal.</p>
