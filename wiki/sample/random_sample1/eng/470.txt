<p>The <b>championship round of the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2013 World Baseball Classic">2013 World Baseball Classic</a></b> was held at <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Park" title="AT&amp;T Park">AT&amp;T Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> from March 17 to 19, 2013.</p>
<p>The championship round was a <a href="/wiki/Single-elimination_tournament" title="Single-elimination tournament">single-elimination tournament</a>. In the final, the team with the higher winning percentage of games in the tournament were to be the home team. If the teams competing in the final had identical winning percentages in the tournament, then World Baseball Classic, Inc. (WBCI) would conduct a coin flip or draw to determine the home team.</p>
<p>The Dominican Republic defeated the Netherlands and Puerto Rico to win their first World Baseball Classic championship. <a href="/wiki/Robinson_Can%C3%B3" title="Robinson Canó">Robinson Canó</a> was named the tournament's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Most_Valuable_Player" title="Most Valuable Player">Most Valuable Player</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cano_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cano-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>Two time <a href="/wiki/World_Baseball_Classic" title="World Baseball Classic">World Baseball Classic</a> defending champions Japan faces off against surprising upstart Puerto Rico in the first semi-final matchup. Puerto Rico scores a run from the first inning and scored two additional runs from a home run by <a href="/wiki/Alex_R%C3%ADos" title="Alex Ríos">Alex Ríos</a> as the Japanese team struggle to catch up. Japan, however, only managed to score at least a run; <a href="/wiki/Seiichi_Uchikawa" title="Seiichi Uchikawa">Seiichi Uchikawa</a> made a baserun snafu in an effort to steal second but was caught up between first and second bases as catcher <a href="/wiki/Yadier_Molina" title="Yadier Molina">Yadier Molina</a> tags Uchikawa for a second out. Despite the efforts, Japan eventually loses to Puerto Rico 3-1 as their championship streak from two previous World Baseball Classic games has ended and the Japanese team gracefully bows out to the stands and the Puerto Rican baseball team after their defeat.</p>

<p>In the final, <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Deduno" title="Samuel Deduno">Samuel Deduno</a> started for the Dominican Republic, while <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Alvarado" title="Giancarlo Alvarado">Giancarlo Alvarado</a> started for Puerto Rico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aol_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-aol-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> 35,703 fans attended the game at <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Park" title="AT&amp;T Park">AT&amp;T Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>. An additional 50,000 Dominican fans watched the game at <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Quisqueya" title="Estadio Quisqueya">Estadio Quisqueya</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo,_Dominican_Republic" title="Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic">Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aol_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-aol-2">[2]</a></sup> In Puerto Rico, the final was the most watched sporting event for the past year with nearly three-fourths of all households tuning in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESPN_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Edwin_Encarnaci%C3%B3n" title="Edwin Encarnación">Edwin Encarnación</a> hit a two-run <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">double</a> in the first inning, giving the Dominican Republic the lead. <a href="/wiki/Erick_Aybar" title="Erick Aybar">Erick Aybar</a> had the game's third <a href="/wiki/Run_batted_in" title="Run batted in">run batted in</a> for the Dominican Republic. Deduno recorded five <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">strikeouts</a> in five scoreless <a href="/wiki/Innings_pitched" title="Innings pitched">innings pitched</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Rodney" title="Fernando Rodney">Fernando Rodney</a> completed the game with a <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)" title="Save (baseball)">save</a>, his seventh save of the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aol_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-aol-2">[2]</a></sup> Four Dominican relief pitchers combined for 4 scoreless innings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_York_Times_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_York_Times-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>This was the third time in the tournament that the Dominican Republic defeated Puerto Rico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aol_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-aol-2">[2]</a></sup> The Dominican Republic completed the tournament with an 8–0 record, becoming the first undefeated team to win the World Baseball Classic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESPN_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robinson_Can%C3%B3" title="Robinson Canó">Robinson Canó</a> was named the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Most_Valuable_Player" title="Most Valuable Player">Most Valuable Player</a> of the Classic after he batted 15-for-32 (.469), the most hits in tournament history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cano_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-cano-1">[1]</a></sup> After the match the team was congratulated immediately for their victory by Dominican president <a href="/wiki/Danilo_Medina" title="Danilo Medina">Danilo Medina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESPN_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-ESPN-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
