<p><b>Roller hockey</b> (in <a href="/wiki/British_English" title="British English">British English</a>), <b>rink hockey</b> (in <a href="/wiki/American_English" title="American English">American English</a>) or <b>quad hockey</b> is a team sport that enjoys significant popularity in a number of Latin countries.</p>
<p>Two five-man teams (four skaters and one goalkeeper) try to drive the ball with their sticks into the opponents' goal. The ball can only be put in motion by a stick, not the skate, otherwise a foul will be stated. The game has two 25-minute halves (for adults), with the clock stopping when the ball becomes dead. Each team has a one-minute timeout in each half. Each team has a minimum of six players (a backup goalie is required) and a maximum of ten.</p>
<p>Players â including the goalie â use <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quad_skates" title="Quad skates">quad skates</a>, whereas <a href="/wiki/Inline_skates" title="Inline skates">inline skates</a> are used in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inline_hockey" title="Inline hockey">inline hockey</a>. Excessive contact between players is forbidden in rink hockey, unlike inline hockey.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Roller_hockey_at_the_1992_Summer_Olympics" title="Roller hockey at the 1992 Summer Olympics">Roller Hockey</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Demonstration_sport" title="Demonstration sport">demonstration rollersport</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>. There have been 42 editions of the <a href="/wiki/FIRS_Roller_Hockey_World_Cup" title="FIRS Roller Hockey World Cup">FIRS Roller Hockey World Cup</a>, with Latin countries dominating the sport since the 1940s: <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_roller_hockey_team" title="Spain national roller hockey team">Spain</a> (16 World titles), <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_roller_hockey_team" title="Portugal national roller hockey team">Portugal</a> (15 World titles), <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_roller_hockey_team" title="Argentina national roller hockey team">Argentina</a> (5 World titles) and <a href="/wiki/Italy_men%27s_national_roller_hockey_team" title="Italy men's national roller hockey team">Italy</a> (4 World titles). Other countries, such as <a href="/wiki/France_national_roller_hockey_team" title="France national roller hockey team">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_roller_hockey_team" title="Brazil national roller hockey team">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_roller_hockey_team" title="Germany national roller hockey team">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_roller_hockey_team" title="Switzerland national roller hockey team">Switzerland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andorra_national_roller_hockey_team" title="Andorra national roller hockey team">Andorra</a> and <a href="/wiki/England_national_roller_hockey_team" title="England national roller hockey team">England</a> are regular international competitors, but rarely overcome the traditional powers.</p>
<p>Roller Hockey is a very fast sport, which may create a problem for TV transmissions, and new rinks are built using blue or white pavement to make the ball more visible on TV.</p>
<p>The most important clubs in Europe (and, arguably, the world) are <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica_(roller_hockey)" title="S.L. Benfica (roller hockey)">SL Benfica</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Porto_(rink_hockey)" title="F.C. Porto (rink hockey)">FC Porto</a> from Portugal, <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona_Hoquei" title="FC Barcelona Hoquei">FC Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/HC_Liceo_La_Coru%C3%B1a" title="HC Liceo La CoruÃ±a">Liceo La CoruÃ±a</a> from Spain. In terms of trophies won <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona_Hoquei" title="FC Barcelona Hoquei">FC Barcelona</a> is the most successful team having won 21 <a href="/wiki/CERH_European_League" title="CERH European League">European Cups</a>.</p>
<p>SL Benfica is the oldest roller hockey club in the world, having played its first game in 1917.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2013_FIRS_Men%27s_Roller_Hockey_World_Cup" title="2013 FIRS Men's Roller Hockey World Cup">2013 Roller Hockey World Cup</a> took place in <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, for the first time on the African continent, won by <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> being the country with most victories in history.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roller_hockey_(quad)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Roller Hockey (Quad) was referred to as Hardball Hockey in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> until November 2008 when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USOC" title="USOC">USOC</a> adopted the sport's more common name, Rink Hockey. <p>The rink has usually a polished wooden surface, but any flat, non-abrasive and non-slippery material such as treated cement is acceptable. Likewise, it is allowed for rink owners to put advertisements in the playing area, as long as they don't interfere with ball or skate motion, which includes both physically (must be at exactly the same level as the remaining area) and visually (dark colours or any other pattern which can mask the ball).</p>
<p>It can have one out of three standard sizes (a minimum of 34x17 meters, an average of 40x20 and a maximum of 44x22) or any size between the minimum and maximum values that has a 2:1 size ratio with a 10% margin of error.</p>
<p>The rink has rounded corners (1 m radius) and is surrounded by a 1 m wall. The wall also has a wooden base 2Â cm wide and at least 20Â cm high. Behind the goals there is a 4 m high net, even if there are no stands (to avoid the ball bouncing back from a wall and hitting a player). If the ball hits the net, it's considered to be out of bounds.</p>
<p>The markings are simple. The halfway line divides the rink into halves, and 22 m from the end wall an "anti-play" line is painted. The area is a 9 X 5.40 m rectangle, placed from 2.7 to 3.3 m ahead of the end table. It has a protection area for goalkeepers, a half-circle with 1.5 m radius. All markings are 8Â cm in width. The goal (painted in fluorescent orange) is 105Â cm high by 170Â cm wide. Inside the goal there is a thick net and a bar close to ground to trap the ball inside (before, two extra referees stayed behind the goal to judge goal decisions), and 92Â cm deep. While not attached to the ground, it is extremely heavy to prevent movement.</p>

<li>The clothing is similar to that used in <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Association football</a>âsocks up to the knee, shorts and a shirt.</li>
<li>Sticks are different for skaters and goalkeepers. They can be of any material approved by the CIRH (although wooden sticks are still most often used), with a minimum length of 90Â cm and maximum of 115Â cm. They cannot be wider than 5Â cm or weigh over 500 g.</li>
<li>The ball is made of <a href="/wiki/Vulcanization" title="Vulcanization">vulcanized</a> rubber, is air pressurized, has a 23Â cm in circumference, and weighs 155 g.</li>
<li>The skates must have two pairs of wheels, with a minimum diameter of 3Â cm. Players are allowed to use brakes in the front of the skate, with a diameter or larger side not larger than 5Â cm.</li>
<li>Protective material includes shin guards, knee caps, jock strap and gloves. Specifications for helmets and elbow caps vary from federation to federation.</li>
<li>Goalkeepers (or netminders) use protective padding on the torso (plus shoulders) (the maximum amount is being regulated, since, as in <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a>, many goalkeepers have been using massive protection to make them larger and broader), neck guard, large shin guards (not longer than 75Â cm), gloves protecting the whole forearm and a helmet with either a grid or unbreakable transparent material. Unlike the Roller Hockey (Inline) Goalie who uses a Catch Glove to catch the shot on goal, the Roller hockey (Quad) Goalie uses a flat batting glove that provides rebound characteristics when blocking a shot on goal.</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roller_hockey_(quad)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first recorded Hardball Roller Hockey game was played in 1878 at the Denmark Rink in London, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rollerskatingmuseum.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-rollerskatingmuseum.com-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usarollersports.org_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-usarollersports.org-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It was first known as âroller poloâ due to the introduction of <a href="/wiki/Polo" title="Polo">Polo</a> in 1876, skaters took polo to the rinks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usarollersports.org_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-usarollersports.org-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The sport was introduced into the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in 1882 with the formation of the National Roller Polo League in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dayton" title="Dayton">Dayton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, with teams in seven cities. Roller Polo League<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usarollersports.org_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-usarollersports.org-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1884 the Massachusetts Roller Polo league was operating with 14 teams<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usarollersports.org_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-usarollersports.org-2">[2]</a></sup> Organized roller skating sports developed as the popularity of roller skates increased in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Roller hockey teams were playing throughout Europe as early as 1901.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jtaa.org_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-jtaa.org-5">[5]</a></sup> Roller Hockey was played by the famous silent film stars, Stan Laurel and Charlie Chaplin, in the early 1900s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The first World Championships in roller hockey were held in 1936 in <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jtaa.org_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-jtaa.org-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rink Hockey as it was called in Europe was not organized by the RSROA in the United States until 1959 and name roller hockey<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usarollersports.org_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-usarollersports.org-2">[2]</a></sup> The sport debuted at the US National Championships in 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Games" title="Pan American Games">Pan American Games</a> introduced roller skating as a sport in 1979 and debuted roller hockey the same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rollerskatingmuseum.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-rollerskatingmuseum.com-1">[1]</a></sup> It was one of the <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Games_sports" title="Pan American Games sports">Pan American Games sports</a> in 1979, 1987, 1991 and 1995. It has since been discontinued. Roller hockey was <a href="/wiki/Roller_hockey_at_the_1992_Summer_Olympics" title="Roller hockey at the 1992 Summer Olympics">present</a> as an exhibition sport at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
