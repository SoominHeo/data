<p><b>Robert Arthur "Rob" Knox</b> (21 August 1989 – 24 May 2008) was an English actor who portrayed the character of Marcus Belby in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i> (2009),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newkids_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-newkids-1">[1]</a></sup> and had signed to appear in the planned film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_(films)#Film" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (films)">Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</a></i> (2010).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-times-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Having studied at <a href="/wiki/Beths_Grammar_School" title="Beths Grammar School">Beths Grammar School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bexley" title="Bexley">Bexley</a>, Knox began acting aged 11, gaining small roles in British television shows like <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bill" title="The Bill">The Bill</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/After_You%27ve_Gone_(TV_series)" title="After You've Gone (TV series)">After You've Gone</a></i> and <i>Trust Me I'm a Teenager</i>. His first cinematic appearance was as an extra in <i><a href="/wiki/King_Arthur_(2004_film)" title="King Arthur (2004 film)">King Arthur</a></i> (2004).</p>
<p>Knox died after being <a href="/wiki/Stabbing" title="Stabbing">stabbed</a> outside a bar in <a href="/wiki/Sidcup" title="Sidcup">Sidcup</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_London" title="South London">Southeast London</a>, in May 2008, when he intervened in a fight to protect his brother. His murder attracted widespread attention in the British press, and his assailant, Karl Norman Bishop, was subsequently sentenced to <a href="/wiki/Life_imprisonment" title="Life imprisonment">life imprisonment</a>, with a minimum 50-year non-parole period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> An annual film festival, the Rob Knox Film Festival, was created in Knox's name to commemorate him, by the Rob Knox Foundation which helps fund training for young local people in the arts.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Knox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Knox was born to Colin and Sally Knox in 1989. He was a pupil at <a href="/wiki/Beths_Grammar_School" title="Beths Grammar School">Beths Grammar School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bexley" title="Bexley">Bexley</a>. He had been acting since the age of 11, and his first credited role was a small part in an episode of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITV_Network" title="ITV Network">ITV</a> police drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bill" title="The Bill">The Bill</a></i> and he also appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> reality show <i>Trust Me I'm a Teenager</i>, and the BBC comedy <i><a href="/wiki/After_You%27ve_Gone_(TV_series)" title="After You've Gone (TV series)">After You've Gone</a></i>. He had previously appeared as an extra in a number of productions. The first film Knox appeared in was <i><a href="/wiki/King_Arthur_(2004_film)" title="King Arthur (2004 film)">King Arthur</a></i> in 2004. He also appeared on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tonight_with_Trevor_McDonald" title="Tonight with Trevor McDonald">Tonight with Trevor McDonald</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-times-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Harry_Potter">Harry Potter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Knox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Harry Potter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007, casting began for the role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marcus_Belby" title="Marcus Belby">Marcus Belby</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (film)">Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</a></i>, the film adaptation of British author <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.K._Rowling" title="J.K. Rowling">J.K. Rowling</a>'s best-selling <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Half-Blood_Prince" title="Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince">novel</a> and the sixth instalment of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_films" title="Harry Potter films"><i>Harry Potter</i> film series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Knox posthumously starred in <i>Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince</i> (2009).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newkids_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-newkids-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although his character does not appear in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows">seventh novel</a>, Knox had signed on to reprise his role as Marcus in the final instalment of the <i>Harry Potter</i> film series, <i>Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-times-2">[2]</a></sup> The film was divided into two parts for financial and scripting reasons;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-times2-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-empireheyman_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-empireheyman-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_%E2%80%93_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 1">Part 1</a> was released in November 2010, with <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_%E2%80%93_Part_2" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 2">the second part</a> in July 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Knox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Murder">Murder</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Knox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Murder">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Knox died at the age of 18 after he was stabbed outside the Metro Bar in <a href="/wiki/Sidcup" title="Sidcup">Sidcup</a>, South East London, on 24 May 2008. He had intervened in a fight to protect his 17-year-old brother Jamie, who was being threatened by two men, one of whom was armed with two <a href="/wiki/Knife" title="Knife">knives</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a> released a statement concerning his death: "We are all shocked and saddened by this news and at this time our sympathies are with his family".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arrest_and_trial_of_Karl_Bishop">Arrest and trial of Karl Bishop</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Knox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Arrest and trial of Karl Bishop">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Karl Norman Bishop (born 1987 in <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Lewisham" title="London Borough of Lewisham">Lewisham</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/South_London" title="South London">Southeast London</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> from Carlton Road, Sidcup, was charged with the murder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-times-2">[2]</a></sup> He was taken into custody, and remained there until his trial, which began in February 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Bishop was found guilty of murder on 4 March 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and received a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Life_sentence" title="Life sentence">life sentence</a> with a minimum of fifty years before being considered for parole.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Police reported that he showed no remorse for the crime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
