<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Friend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Friend was born 23 January 1981 in <a href="/wiki/Rosetown" title="Rosetown">Rosetown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>, Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Growing up in <a href="/wiki/Kelowna" title="Kelowna">Kelowna</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, he was the first Kelowna-based player to make the British Columbia Provincial team, a collection of top youth players, and was named captain for the 1997 team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmuannounce_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-wmuannounce-3">[3]</a></sup> Friend was exposed to senior level play early, appearing for <a href="/wiki/Okanagan_Challenge" title="Okanagan Challenge">Okanagan Challenge</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Premier_Development_League" title="Premier Development League">Premier Development Soccer League</a> as well as appearing in an exhibition for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vancouver_86ers" title="Vancouver 86ers">Vancouver 86ers</a> of the <a href="/wiki/A-League_(1995%E2%80%932004)" title="A-League (1995–2004)">A-League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmuannounce_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-wmuannounce-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite training with English club <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra_F.C." title="Crewe Alexandra F.C.">Crewe Alexandra F.C.</a>, Friend decided to attend <a href="/wiki/Western_Michigan_University" title="Western Michigan University">Western Michigan University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmuannounce_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-wmuannounce-3">[3]</a></sup> He played <a href="/wiki/College_soccer" title="College soccer">college soccer</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Western_Michigan_Broncos" title="Western Michigan Broncos">Western Michigan Broncos</a> from 1999 to 2000, having appeared in 40 games and scoring 9 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Friend transferred to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Santa_Barbara" title="University of California, Santa Barbara">University of California, Santa Barbara</a> and finished his collegiate career with the <a href="/wiki/UC_Santa_Barbara_Gauchos_men%27s_soccer" title="UC Santa Barbara Gauchos men's soccer">UC Santa Barbara Gauchos men's soccer</a> team. As a senior, Friend scored in 12 consecutive matches which ranks fifth in <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Division_I" title="NCAA Division I">NCAA Division I</a> history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He appeared in 40 games for the Gauchos, scoring 31 goals and assisting on 9 more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He was named as a 2002 All-American, becoming the first Gaucho in 32 years to receive the honor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ucsbborussia_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ucsbborussia-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Friend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Norway">Norway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Friend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Norway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After graduation from UCSB, Friend went on trial with <a href="/wiki/Moss_FK" title="Moss FK">Moss FK</a> in December 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> After a positive showing, he signed a two-year deal in December 2002 for Moss FK's upcoming <a href="/wiki/2003_Norwegian_First_Division" title="2003 Norwegian First Division">2003 Norwegian First Division</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Despite his contract with Moss FK, Friend was drafted in the 4th Round (35th Overall) of the <a href="/wiki/2003_MLS_SuperDraft" title="2003 MLS SuperDraft">2003 MLS SuperDraft</a> by <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Fire_Soccer_Club" title="Chicago Fire Soccer Club">Chicago Fire</a> making him the first player from UCSB drafted to MLS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ucsbborussia_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-ucsbborussia-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Friend made 41 league appearances and scored 16 league goals for Moss FK over his two season span with the club before being transferred to <a href="/wiki/Molde_FK" title="Molde FK">Molde FK</a> in August 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fotballno_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-fotballno-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> With Molde, he won the <a href="/wiki/2005_Norwegian_Football_Cup" title="2005 Norwegian Football Cup">2005 Norwegian Football Cup</a> and scored a goal in the <a href="/wiki/2005_Norwegian_Football_Cup_Final" title="2005 Norwegian Football Cup Final">Final</a>. He remained with the Molde FK until 2006, making 40 league appearances and scoring 14 league goals for the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fotballno_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-fotballno-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Netherlands">The Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Friend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Early 2006 saw interest in Friend rise with <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace F.C.</a> and <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A5lerenga_Fotball" title="Vålerenga Fotball">Vålerenga Fotball</a> among teams looking to purchase him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> It was announced in late March 2006 that Friend was sold to <a href="/wiki/SC_Heerenveen" title="SC Heerenveen">SC Heerenveen</a> ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Eredivisie" title="2006–07 Eredivisie">2006–07 Eredivisie</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrk_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrk-18">[18]</a></sup> Per terms of the agreement, his last game for Molde FK was in July and a portion of the transfer fee went to his original club Moss FK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrk_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-nrk-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>After making 19 appearances with Heerenveen and scoring five goals, he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/Heracles_Almelo" title="Heracles Almelo">Heracles Almelo</a> in January 2007 to conclude the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Eredivisie" title="2006–07 Eredivisie">2006–07 Eredivisie</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Despite his contributions, Heerenveen deemed him surplus to requirements and transferred him on to <a href="/wiki/Borussia_M%C3%B6nchengladbach" title="Borussia Mönchengladbach">Borussia Mönchengladbach</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Friend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_2._Bundesliga" title="2007–08 2. Bundesliga">2007–08 season</a> Friend scored 18 goals in 33 matches, making him <a href="/wiki/Borussia_M%C3%B6nchengladbach" title="Borussia Mönchengladbach">Borussia Mönchengladbach</a>'s top scorer and the second top scorer in Bundesliga 2. His team finished first, and were promoted to the Bundesliga.</p>
<p>On 17 August 2008, Friend scored in his very first game in the Bundesliga, against VfB Stuttgart. After three years in Mönchengladbach, including two in the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>, Friend was sold from the club and was bought by the newly relegated <a href="/wiki/Hertha_BSC" title="Hertha BSC">Hertha BSC</a> for €1.8 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<p>Friend made his debut and scored his first goal for Berlin on 14 August 2010 in a <a href="/wiki/DFB-Pokal" title="DFB-Pokal">DFB-Pokal</a> cup match versus <a href="/wiki/SC_Pfullendorf" title="SC Pfullendorf">Pfullendorf</a>, the game ended as a 2–0 victory. Six days later he made his league debut for the team in a 3–2 victory over <a href="/wiki/Rot-Wei%C3%9F_Oberhausen" title="Rot-Weiß Oberhausen">Rot-Weiß Oberhausen</a>. On 12 September, Friend had a man of the match performance scoring 2 goals in a 3–1 home victory over <a href="/wiki/Arminia_Bielefeld" title="Arminia Bielefeld">Arminia Bielefeld</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Stadium_(Berlin)" title="Olympic Stadium (Berlin)">Olympic Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Friend finished his first season with Hertha having scored 6 goals in 27 appearances in the 2. Bundesliga and DFB-Pokal. He helped Hertha win the <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a> gaining automatic promotion back to top flight football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On the final day of the 2011 summer transfer window, Friend returned to the second division, joining the newly relegated <a href="/wiki/Eintracht_Frankfurt" title="Eintracht Frankfurt">Eintracht Frankfurt</a>. He was close to re-joining Major League Soccer in the United States in January 2013, but despite interest from <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Whitecaps_FC" title="Vancouver Whitecaps FC">Vancouver Whitecaps FC</a> Friend saw his contract to completion in Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Friend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 13 January 2014, Friend made the move to <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Galaxy" title="Los Angeles Galaxy">Los Angeles Galaxy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> After one season with Los Angeles, which included winning the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_MLS_Cup" title="2014 MLS Cup">2014 MLS Cup</a>, Friend announced his retirement on 10 December 2014 due to issues stemming from concussions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timescol_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-timescol-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Friend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Friend has represented <a href="/wiki/Canada_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="Canada men's national soccer team">Canada</a> at the U-20, U-23, and senior levels. He saw action in the <a href="/wiki/2001_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="2001 FIFA World Youth Championship">2001 FIFA World Youth Championship</a>, as well as the 2004 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CONCACAF_Men_Pre-Olympic_Tournament" title="CONCACAF Men Pre-Olympic Tournament">CONCACAF Men Pre-Olympic Tournament</a>.</p>
<p>Friend made his debut with the senior international team on 18 January 2003 in a friendly with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States</a>. Since then, and as of February 2013, he has been capped 32 times by his country, scoring twice. He has represented Canada in five <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="FIFA World Cup qualification">FIFA World Cup qualification</a> matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Friend&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Friend was inducted into the UCSB Intercollegiate Athletics Hall of Fame in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In August 2014, Friend was named as the inaugural sports ambassador for the Academy for Canadians in Sports and Entertainment – Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>Friend is married to Marisa Lauren "Mari" Bell, a former UC Santa Barbara women's volleyball player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timescol_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-timescol-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The couple have 3 children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timescol_27-2"><a href="#cite_note-timescol-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
