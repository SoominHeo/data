<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/World_Baseball_Classic_qualification" title="World Baseball Classic qualification">Qualifying Round</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2013 World Baseball Classic">2013 World Baseball Classic</a></b> was held from September 19 to November 19, 2012. Although the <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2006 World Baseball Classic">2006</a> and <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2009 World Baseball Classic">2009</a> editions of the World Baseball Classic were contested by the same pre-selected field of 16 teams, for the 2013 tournament only the 12 teams that won at least one game in 2009 were guaranteed a berth in the main tournament. The other four (<a href="/wiki/Canada_national_baseball_team" title="Canada national baseball team">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei_national_baseball_team" title="Chinese Taipei national baseball team">Chinese Taipei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Panama_national_baseball_team" title="Panama national baseball team">Panama</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_baseball_team" title="South Africa national baseball team">South Africa</a>) contested the qualifying round along with 12 additional teams invited by the <a href="/wiki/International_Baseball_Federation" title="International Baseball Federation">International Baseball Federation</a> (IBAF).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IBAF1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-IBAF1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The qualifiers were organized as four independent <a href="/wiki/Double-elimination_tournament" title="Double-elimination tournament">modified double-elimination</a> tournaments featuring four teams each. The final game was winner-take-all, even if won by the team emerging from the loser's bracket. That is, the team emerging from the winner's bracket might be eliminated despite losing only one game (as, in fact, happened to Israel in Qualifier 1).</p>
<p>Canada and Chinese Taipei dominated in Qualifiers 2 and 4 to advance easily. In Qualifier 1, <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_baseball_team" title="Spain national baseball team">Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Israel_national_baseball_team" title="Israel national baseball team">Israel</a> both won in the first round, then Israel beat Spain in the winner's bracket. Spain then eliminated <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_baseball_team" title="South Africa national baseball team">South Africa</a> (the only team in the qualifier to have previously contested the WBC) to earn a rematch with Israel, which was without its several Major League players due to the qualifier taking place during the MLB season. Spain won the winner-take-all final game, 9–7 in 10 innings, to advance to the main tournament.</p>
<p>In Qualifier 3, favorites <a href="/wiki/Panama_national_baseball_team" title="Panama national baseball team">Panama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua_national_baseball_team" title="Nicaragua national baseball team">Nicaragua</a> lost in the first round, and Panama then eliminated Nicaragua. After both teams defeated <a href="/wiki/Colombia_national_baseball_team" title="Colombia national baseball team">Colombia</a>, underdog <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_baseball_team" title="Brazil national baseball team">Brazil</a> defeated Panama 1–0 to advance to the main tournament.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Players">Players</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_World_Baseball_Classic_%E2%80%93_Qualification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Players">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Under the Classic's rules, Team Israel was entitled to have non-Israeli citizens of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> heritage play for the team. Such players included major leaguers catcher <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Lavarnway" title="Ryan Lavarnway">Ryan Lavarnway</a>, first baseman <a href="/wiki/Ike_Davis" title="Ike Davis">Ike Davis</a>, second basemen <a href="/wiki/Ian_Kinsler" title="Ian Kinsler">Ian Kinsler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Satin" title="Josh Satin">Josh Satin</a>, third basemen <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Youkilis" title="Kevin Youkilis">Kevin Youkilis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Valencia" title="Danny Valencia">Danny Valencia</a>, outfielders <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Braun" title="Ryan Braun">Ryan Braun</a> (whose father is Israeli), <a href="/wiki/Sam_Fuld" title="Sam Fuld">Sam Fuld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Kalish" title="Ryan Kalish">Ryan Kalish</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gabe_Kapler" title="Gabe Kapler">Gabe Kapler</a>, and pitchers <a href="/wiki/Jason_Marquis" title="Jason Marquis">Jason Marquis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Feldman" title="Scott Feldman">Scott Feldman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Craig_Breslow" title="Craig Breslow">Craig Breslow</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Grabow" title="John Grabow">John Grabow</a>, as well as what were then recent major leaguers catcher <a href="/wiki/Brad_Ausmus" title="Brad Ausmus">Brad Ausmus</a> and pitcher <a href="/wiki/Scott_Schoeneweis" title="Scott Schoeneweis">Scott Schoeneweis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes1-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Kinsler said:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"Wow, I would be happy to play for Team Israel.... The truth is that if a proposal comes from Team USA to play for them, I will have a very difficult decision to make. Yuk [Kevin Youkilis], Braun [Ryan Braun], and I could make a fantastic team. I am sure that I'll talk it over with Yuk – we always laugh about things like this."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Outfielder <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Green" title="Shawn Green">Shawn Green</a>, who retired in 2007, was also eligible because he is Jewish. In early June 2011 he said it "would be an honor" and he "would love to" play for Israel in the Classic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because they were held in September, while the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> season was still in progress, Qualifiers 1 and 2 could not feature major league players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Youkilis" title="Kevin Youkilis">Kevin Youkilis</a> announced that he would play for the team if they made it past the qualifying round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The highest-level players involved in Qualifiers 1 and 2 were minor-league prospects ranked among the top 20 in their respective organizations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Team Israel, managed by former major league All Star <a href="/wiki/Brad_Ausmus" title="Brad Ausmus">Brad Ausmus</a>, included minor league pitchers Eric Berger (1–0) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Lorin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brett Lorin (page does not exist)">Brett Lorin</a>, first baseman <a href="/wiki/Nate_Freiman" title="Nate Freiman">Nate Freiman</a> (.417; 4 HR in 12 AB), second baseman Josh Satin (.273), shortstops <a href="/wiki/Jake_Lemmerman" title="Jake Lemmerman">Jake Lemmerman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ben_Orloff" title="Ben Orloff">Ben Orloff</a>, and outfielders <a href="/wiki/Adam_Greenberg_(baseball)" title="Adam Greenberg (baseball)">Adam Greenberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Guez" title="Ben Guez">Ben Guez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joc_Pederson" title="Joc Pederson">Joc Pederson</a> (.308), and <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Widlansky" title="Robbie Widlansky">Robbie Widlansky</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldbaseballclassic1_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-worldbaseballclassic1-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldbaseballclassic2_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-worldbaseballclassic2-16">[16]</a></sup> Retired major leaguer <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Green" title="Shawn Green">Shawn Green</a> played for Israel (.333).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldbaseballclassic1_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-worldbaseballclassic1-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Similarly, <a href="/wiki/Joey_Votto" title="Joey Votto">Joey Votto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justin_Morneau" title="Justin Morneau">Justin Morneau</a> were unable to play for Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> They were led by minor league first baseman <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Van_Ostrand" title="Jimmy Van Ostrand">Jimmy Van Ostrand</a> (.538; 10 RBIs in 13 at-bats).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldbaseballclassic2_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-worldbaseballclassic2-16">[16]</a></sup> Although Canada advanced easily despite the unavailability of its Major Leaguers, Israel was eliminated after narrowly losing the final game to Spain.</p>
<p>By contrast, all four teams in Qualifier 3 boasted Major League players, as it took place in November and did not conflict with the MLB season. Established Major Leaguers included <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Ruiz_(baseball)" title="Carlos Ruiz (baseball)">Carlos Ruiz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rub%C3%A9n_Tejada" title="Rubén Tejada">Rubén Tejada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manny_Acosta" title="Manny Acosta">Manny Acosta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manny_Corpas" title="Manny Corpas">Manny Corpas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Lee" title="Carlos Lee">Carlos Lee</a> of Panama; and <a href="/wiki/Everth_Cabrera" title="Everth Cabrera">Everth Cabrera</a> and <a href="/wiki/J._C._Ram%C3%ADrez" title="J. C. Ramírez">J. C. Ramírez</a> of Nicaragua. Major League rookies and prospects included <a href="/wiki/Erasmo_Ram%C3%ADrez_(right-handed_pitcher)" title="Erasmo Ramírez (right-handed pitcher)">Erasmo Ramírez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cheslor_Cuthbert" title="Cheslor Cuthbert">Cheslor Cuthbert</a> of Nicaragua; <a href="/wiki/Yan_Gomes" title="Yan Gomes">Yan Gomes</a> of Brazil; and <a href="/wiki/Luis_Martinez_(catcher)" title="Luis Martinez (catcher)">Luis Martinez</a> and brothers <a href="/wiki/Donovan_Solano" title="Donovan Solano">Donavan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jhonatan_Solano" title="Jhonatan Solano">Jhonatan Solano</a> of Colombia. Retired Major Leaguers included <a href="/wiki/Ramiro_Mendoza" title="Ramiro Mendoza">Ramiro Mendoza</a> of Panama and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edgar_Renter%C3%ADa" title="Edgar Rentería">Edgar Rentería</a> of Colombia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although Qualifier 4 also took place in November, it did not feature any current MLB players, as both <a href="/wiki/Wei-Yin_Chen" title="Wei-Yin Chen">Wei-Yin Chen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chien-Ming_Wang" title="Chien-Ming Wang">Chien-Ming Wang</a> of Chinese Taipei elected to skip the qualifier. Chinese Taipei did feature Japanese major leaguer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yao-Hsun_Yang" title="Yao-Hsun Yang">Yao-Hsun Yang</a> as well as Taiwanese league players such as <a href="/wiki/Peng_Cheng-min" title="Peng Cheng-min">Peng Cheng-min</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lin_Chih-sheng" title="Lin Chih-sheng">Lin Chih-sheng</a>, and qualified easily. Both New Zealand and the Philippines included minor league players, while Thailand included former Major Leaguer <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Damon" title="Johnny Damon">Johnny Damon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Additional_rules">Additional rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_World_Baseball_Classic_%E2%80%93_Qualification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Additional rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A pitcher threw no more than 85 pitches per game in the Qualifying Round unless the pitcher needed more to complete a batter's plate appearance.</p>
<p>A pitcher must:</p>
<ul>
<li>Not pitch until a minimum of four days have passed since he last pitched, if he threw 50 or more pitches when he last pitched;</li>
<li>Not pitch until a minimum of one day has passed since he last pitched, if he threw 30 or more pitches when he last pitched;</li>
<li>Not pitch until a minimum of one day has passed since any second consecutive day on which the pitcher pitched;</li>
</ul>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Mercy_rule" title="Mercy rule">mercy rule</a> came into effect when one team led by either fifteen runs after five innings, or ten runs after seven innings.</p>
<p>An alternative version of the IBAF's <a href="/wiki/Extra_innings#Baseball" title="Extra innings">extra inning rule</a> was also used. If after 12 innings the score was still tied, each half inning thereafter would have started with runners on second and first base. The runners would have been the eighth and ninth hitters due in that inning respectively. For example, if the number five hitter was due to lead off the inning, the number three hitter would have been on second base, and the number four hitter on first base. However, this rule was never actually employed in this year's qualifiers, as the two extra-inning games in the qualifying round ended prior to a 13th inning.</p>
