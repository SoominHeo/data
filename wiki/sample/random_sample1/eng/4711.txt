<p><b>Rokkō Island</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">六甲アイランド</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Rokkō Airando</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is the second major artificial island in <a href="/wiki/Higashinada-ku,_Kobe" title="Higashinada-ku, Kobe">Higashinada-ku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kobe" title="Kobe">Kobe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> located in the southeast region at <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Kobe" title="Port of Kobe">Port of Kobe</a>. The island was constructed from <a href="/wiki/Land_reclamation" title="Land reclamation">reclaimed land</a> between 1973 and 1992. It has a 3.4 km by 2 km rectangular shape, and covers 5.80 km². The island was one of the hardest hit areas in Kobe during the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hanshin_earthquake" title="Great Hanshin earthquake">Great Hanshin earthquake</a>. Rokkō Island contains<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> hotels, sports and conventions facilities, markets, a water amusement park, sea view apartment buildings, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kobe_Fashion_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kobe Fashion Museum (page does not exist)">Kobe Fashion Museum</a>, two international schools, container yards, wharves, and port facilities. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rokko_Liner" title="Rokko Liner">Rokko Liner</a> connects the island to JR <a href="/wiki/Sumiyoshi_Station_(JR_West)" title="Sumiyoshi Station (JR West)">Sumiyoshi Station</a>, stopping at several points along the way.</p>

<p>The Rokkō Liner divides the apartments to two different sections: east and west.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rokk%C5%8D_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The City of Kobe convinced <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a> Far East, Inc. to move its Asia headquarters to Rokko Island in March 1993; previously the P&amp;G Asia offices were in Osaka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stewartp41_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stewartp41-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Facilities:</p>
<ul>
<li>Hotels (KOBE Bay <a href="/wiki/Sheraton_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Sheraton Hotels and Resorts">Sheraton</a> HOTEL and so on)</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kobe_Fashion_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kobe Fashion Museum (page does not exist)">Kobe Fashion Museum</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P%26G" title="P&amp;G">P&amp;G</a> Japan Head Office</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Morozoff_Ltd." title="Morozoff Ltd.">Morozoff Ltd.</a> Head Office</li>
<li>Ferry Berths RF1 - RF3</li>
<li>Container Berths RC1 - RC7</li>
<li>Area 480 ha</li>
</ul>
<p>See also <a href="/wiki/Port_Island" title="Port Island">Port Island</a>, another artificial island used for business, residential, and commercial purposes in the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Kobe" title="Port of Kobe">Port of Kobe</a>.</p>
