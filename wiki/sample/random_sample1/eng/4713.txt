<p><i><b>Longmire</b></i> is an American modern Western <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crime_drama" title="Crime drama">crime drama</a> television series that premiered on June 3, 2012, on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%26E_(TV_channel)" title="A&amp;E (TV channel)">A&amp;E</a> network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The series was developed by John Coveny and Hunt Baldwin, and is based on the <i>Walt Longmire Mysteries</i> series of mystery novels written by best-selling author <a href="/wiki/Craig_Johnson_(author)" title="Craig Johnson (author)">Craig Johnson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The show centers on <a href="/wiki/Walt_Longmire" title="Walt Longmire">Walt Longmire</a>, a sheriff in fictional Absaroka County, <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a>, who returns to full-time work after his wife's death. Assisted by his friends and his daughter, Walt investigates major crimes within his jurisdiction, while campaigning for re-election against one of his own deputies. Following the conclusion of the third season in August 2014, A&amp;E announced it would not renew the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Television#Warner_Horizon_Television" title="Warner Bros. Television">Warner Horizon Television</a> shopped it to other networks,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and on November 19, 2014, <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> picked up the show, starting with Season 4. In late 2016, Seasons 1 to 5 were available for viewing online via Netflix in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On November 2, 2016, Netflix announced that it had renewed the show for a sixth and final season of ten episodes, to air in 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Longmire_episodes" title="List of Longmire episodes">List of Longmire episodes</a></div>
<p>Sheriff <a href="/wiki/Walt_Longmire" title="Walt Longmire">Walt Longmire</a> (<a href="/wiki/Robert_Taylor_(Australian_actor)" title="Robert Taylor (Australian actor)">Robert Taylor</a>), is the sheriff of <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a>'s fictional Absaroka County (pronounced <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i>ab-sə-<b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">ROH</span></span></b>-kah</i></a></span> [<span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i>ab-sə-<b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">RAH</span></span></b>-kə</i></a></span> by many Wyoming natives] and named after the Native American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Absaroke" title="Absaroke">Absaroke</a> people. On a map and in many scenes, Absaroka county seat Durant is similar in many respects to <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_Wyoming" title="Buffalo, Wyoming">Buffalo</a> in <a href="/wiki/Johnson_County,_Wyoming" title="Johnson County, Wyoming">Johnson County</a> in northeast Wyoming).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_1">Season 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Season 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Walt's adult daughter Cady (<a href="/wiki/Cassidy_Freeman" title="Cassidy Freeman">Cassidy Freeman</a>) is concerned that her father refuses to move on after the death of her mother. While preparing to run for re-election, Walt has delegated most police duties to deputies Branch Connally (<a href="/wiki/Bailey_Chase" title="Bailey Chase">Bailey Chase</a>) and "The Ferg" (Adam Bartley). Branch has also entered the election, to unseat Walt, and he is secretly dating Cady. Victoria "Vic" Moretti (<a href="/wiki/Katee_Sackhoff" title="Katee Sackhoff">Katee Sackhoff</a>), a transplanted Philadelphia homicide detective, arrived in Wyoming six months prior and is now one of Walt's deputies. Walt's longtime friend Henry Standing Bear (<a href="/wiki/Lou_Diamond_Phillips" title="Lou Diamond Phillips">Lou Diamond Phillips</a>) assists with any dealings with the local <a href="/wiki/Indian_reservation" title="Indian reservation">Indian reservation</a>.</p>
<p>In random <a href="/wiki/Flashback_(narrative)" title="Flashback (narrative)">flashback</a> scenes, Walt and Henry travel separately to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" title="Denver, Colorado">Denver, Colorado</a>, where Walt attacks someone in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meth_house" title="Meth house">meth house</a>. Denver Police Homicide Detective Fales (<a href="/wiki/Charles_S._Dutton" title="Charles S. Dutton">Charles S. Dutton</a>) later arrives in Wyoming to talk to Walt and Cady about her mother's death. Cady is shocked to learn her mother was murdered, as Walt had told her she died of cancer. Fales tells Walt they found the man suspected of killing his wife buried in a shallow grave. Walt denies killing the man; however, Detective Fales suspects that if Walt did not commit the murder, then Henry did.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_2">Season 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Season 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cady arrives in Denver to speak to Detective Fales, who gives her the details of her mother's murder and informs her the suspected murderer has also been killed. Fales prods her for information, and she offers that Walt confides in Henry, inadvertently giving Fales a new target of investigation. Henry tells Walt that he killed the murderer because Walt was unable to do so. Vic encounters Ed Gorski (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Tergesen" title="Lee Tergesen">Lee Tergesen</a>), a retired cop from Philadelphia. They discuss the suicide of Gorski's former partner, whom Vic implicated in a corruption scandal that prompted an internal affairs investigation. Gorski blames Vic for his partner's death, leading to his stalking Vic. Frightened by Gorski's manner, Vic asks Henry for help, and Walt visits Gorski to warn him away. Gorski is subsequently beaten severely. Hector, a Cheyenne mercenary believed to have assaulted Gorski, is protected by Walt. Henry confesses to Walt that he hired Hector to kill Miller Beck, the murderer of Walt's wife, but Hector explains that he only beat him up and took his teeth as trophies. Fales and his team find the teeth at Henry's bar and arrest him. Walt wins the election, and Cady is hospitalized when she is hit by a car after hers is sabotaged by an unknown assailant. Branch investigates, which leads to trouble with the local Cheyenne reservation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_3">Season 3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Season 3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As Branch recovers from being shot, he believes David Ridges, who was thought to have committed suicide and was cremated, was the one who shot him. Since Walt and the others do not believe Branch, he conducts his own investigation. In prison, Henry gets abused by fellow natives, led by former reservation police chief Malachi Strand (<a href="/wiki/Graham_Greene_(actor)" title="Graham Greene (actor)">Graham Greene</a>), for his friendship with Walt and assisting in his arrest. When Malachi makes it difficult for Henry to obtain an attorney, Cady decides to represent him. Henry gets released on bail and sets out to prove his innocence. However, Malachi is also released and begins working as security for Jacob Nighthorse. Both Branch and Henry uncover clues that point to Nighthorse's involvement in each of their situations, with Ridges as a key figure. Even Walt believes Nighthorse could have had something to do with his own wife's murder, years ago. Branch's dangerous obsession causes him to be suspended from the force and into the care of his father, Barlow. Walt and Henry learn that Ridges's murderous past leads back to his killing Miller Beck. Walt learns of Ridges's location and is forced to kill him when attacked. Fales drops all charges against Henry. However, neither Walt nor Branch believe their investigations are over. As Walt prepares to go after Nighthorse, Branch questions Barlow, who admits to having paid Nighthorse to hire Ridges to kill Walt's wife in hopes of helping Branch become sheriff. A tense moment between father and son leads to a gunshot.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_4">Season 4</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Season 4">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Walt, Vic and Ferg set out to search for Branch, after finding a typewritten note on his home computer. Walt finds him dead in a river from what appears to be a self-inflicted shotgun wound. Walt refuses to rule Branch's death a suicide after he finds dirt in the shotgun shell, signifying that the gun had been fired, the shell ejected and put back into the gun. Walt maintains that Jacob Nighthorse is behind the murder, until a drunken Barlow Connally confesses to Walt about killing Branch for finding out that Barlow had Walt's wife killed. Barlow then causes Walt to shoot and kill him, furthering the suspicion around Walt. Meanwhile, Walt and his department join forces with Mathias (<a href="/wiki/Zahn_McClarnon" title="Zahn McClarnon">Zahn McClarnon</a>), chief of the tribal police, to investigate the rape of a Cheyenne woman, Gabriella (<a href="/wiki/Julia_Jones" title="Julia Jones">Julia Jones</a>) by several oil field workers. The men are never prosecuted, after the case gets mired in governmental red tape and Walker (<a href="/wiki/Callum_Keith_Rennie" title="Callum Keith Rennie">Callum Keith Rennie</a>), the oil foreman, buys off Gabriella's family. Henry takes up Hector's role of avenger and plans to exact justice on the rapists. One turns up dead, and Gabriella shoots the other as Henry is threatening him. Shot in the dark by Walt, Henry must then take Gabriella into the <a href="/wiki/Crow_Nation" title="Crow Nation">Crow Nation</a> to protect her. Both Walt and Walker set out to find her, ending up in a stand-off. Later, Mathias catches up with Henry and takes him into custody. Thinking the recent events are over, Walt relaxes at home with his new love interest Dr. Donna Sue Monaghan (<a href="/wiki/Ally_Walker" title="Ally Walker">Ally Walker</a>), but an unknown intruder interrupts and shoots them.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_5">Season 5</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Season 5">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Walt conducts an intense search for the person who kidnapped Donna and seriously injured him. Walt and Donna continue their relationship. Jacob becomes increasingly suspicious of Malachi's illegal activities. Mathias figures out Henry has taken over Hector's duties as vigilante and uses that to his advantage. A heroin operation by the Irish mob is discovered in Walt's jurisdiction. Walt defends a wrongful death suit; if he loses, he will lose everything he owns and probably, also his job. Vic is pregnant and she is assaulted. Walt ends his relationship with Donna, suggesting some future possibility of a relationship with Vic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Henry gives a ride home to a presumably drunk woman; Malachi and his men kidnap him. He is taken to a remote area of the neighboring Crow reservation, staked to the ground, and left for dead.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast_and_characters">Cast and characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cast and characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_cast">Main cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Main cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Robert_Taylor_(Australian_actor)" title="Robert Taylor (Australian actor)">Robert Taylor</a> as <a href="/wiki/Walt_Longmire" title="Walt Longmire">Walt Longmire</a>: the long-time sheriff of Absaroka County. Laconic and introspective with a strong sense of duty and justice, his character is a throwback to the iconic lone hero of classical Westerns. As the stories build, Walt is shown to have a knack for finding the truth behind the various crimes that have been committed. Walt's wife died a year before the series started. Almost everyone believes her death was due to cancer, but a few people know the truth: she was the victim of a murder in Denver. Walt's grief over her death fuels his isolation and self-recrimination. (Season 1-)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Katee_Sackhoff" title="Katee Sackhoff">Katee Sackhoff</a> as Victoria "Vic" Moretti: a sheriff's deputy and a former <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Police_Department" title="Philadelphia Police Department">Philadelphia Police Department</a> homicide detective. She moved to Wyoming with Sean, her husband, following an incident with her superior officer in Philadelphia. She has four brothers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> (Season 1-)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lou_Diamond_Phillips" title="Lou Diamond Phillips">Lou Diamond Phillips</a> as Henry Standing Bear: a <a href="/wiki/Cheyenne" title="Cheyenne">Cheyenne</a> who is Walt's best friend and confidant. Their friendship goes back to their early school days together. He is the proprietor of the Red Pony, a local tavern and restaurant, and an expert tracker. His name is described in Season 3 as a bear who protects those he loves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Initially a low-key character, Henry becomes much more assertive and involved in the plot by Season 4. (Season 1-)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cassidy_Freeman" title="Cassidy Freeman">Cassidy Freeman</a> as Cady Longmire: Walt's daughter, an attorney who works for a local law firm, and later for Jacob Nighthorse, providing representation to residents on the reservation. She was romantically involved with Branch Connally for a time. (Season 1-)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bailey_Chase" title="Bailey Chase">Bailey Chase</a> as Branch Connally: an ambitious deputy who comes from a wealthy local family; his uncle was Walt's predecessor as sheriff, and Branch also hopes to be sheriff one day. He was romantically involved with Cady Longmire. He was shot in "Bad Medicine" by David Ridges, and spent most of the next season recovering and investigating his assault. He was suspended from the force at the end of Season 3 for kidnapping and drugging Sam Poteet and is later shot and killed by his father, Barlow Connally. (Season 1-3)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adam_Bartley" title="Adam Bartley">Adam Bartley</a> as Jim "The Ferg" Ferguson: an awkward but hard-working young deputy who often feels unappreciated. His eclectic knowledge and skills have helped solve cases, justifying Walt's intuition in hiring him. Despite this, he continues to feel overlooked, even after he becomes a more senior deputy following Branch's death. (Season 1-)</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Longmire</i> received a pilot order on December 14, 2010. The pilot was written by John Coveny and Hunt Baldwin, and directed by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Chulack" title="Christopher Chulack">Christopher Chulack</a>. Coveny, Baldwin, and Chulack serve as executive producers alongside <a href="/wiki/Greer_Shephard" title="Greer Shephard">Greer Shephard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_M._Robin" title="Michael M. Robin">Michael M. Robin</a>, and the production companies <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Horizon_Television" title="Warner Horizon Television">Warner Horizon Television</a> and the Shephard/Robin Company. The series is an adaptation of the Longmire mystery novels written by best-selling author <a href="/wiki/Craig_Johnson_(author)" title="Craig Johnson (author)">Craig Johnson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> While the story is supposed to take place in northern Wyoming, the series' various seasons have been filmed in several locations in <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a> including <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_New_Mexico" title="Las Vegas, New Mexico">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe,_New_Mexico" title="Santa Fe, New Mexico">Santa Fe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Nest,_New_Mexico" title="Eagle Nest, New Mexico">Eagle Nest</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Red_River,_New_Mexico" title="Red River, New Mexico">Red River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Casting announcements began in February 2011, with <a href="/wiki/Robert_Taylor_(Australian_actor)" title="Robert Taylor (Australian actor)">Robert Taylor</a> first cast in the lead role of Walt Longmire, the sheriff of Absaroka County, Wyoming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Katee_Sackhoff" title="Katee Sackhoff">Katee Sackhoff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bailey_Chase" title="Bailey Chase">Bailey Chase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cassidy_Freeman" title="Cassidy Freeman">Cassidy Freeman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lou_Diamond_Phillips" title="Lou Diamond Phillips">Lou Diamond Phillips</a> followed, with Sackhoff cast in the role of Victoria "Vic" Moretti, Chase cast as Branch Connally, Freeman cast as Cady Longmire, and Phillips cast as Henry Standing Bear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 26, 2011, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%26E_(TV_channel)" title="A&amp;E (TV channel)">A&amp;E</a> picked up <i>Longmire</i> for a 10-episode first season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Following early success as the highest-rated summer scripted drama debut, and as A&amp;E's highest-rated scripted drama, A&amp;E renewed <i>Longmire</i> for a second season on June 29, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On November 25, 2013, A&amp;E renewed <i>Longmire</i> for a third season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, massive wildfires scorched the <a href="/wiki/Valles_Caldera#Valles_Caldera_National_Preserve" title="Valles Caldera">Valles Caldera National Preserve</a>, which disrupted the series production. In a three-week period, three fires started up (two at the same time). The <a href="/wiki/Prescott_Fire_Department" title="Prescott Fire Department">Prescott Fire Department</a>'s Granite Mountain Hotshots assisted in preventing the fiery destruction of the area around where Walt's house is filmed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LocationFire_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-LocationFire-21">[21]</a></sup> Nineteen of the Hotshots' 20 members later died battling an <a href="/wiki/Yarnell_Hill_Fire" title="Yarnell Hill Fire">Arizona</a> wildfire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The second season's finale honored the firefighters in the closing credits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LocationFire_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-LocationFire-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Production for the third season began in early 2014. Filming of the interiors took place in New Mexico at Garson Studios, on the campus of <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe_University_of_Art_and_Design" title="Santa Fe University of Art and Design">Santa Fe University of Art and Design</a>. Exteriors were filmed in and around Santa Fe and at Garson Studios, according to the New Mexico State Film Office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 28, 2014, A&amp;E announced Longmire was canceled after completing its third season, despite consistently strong viewership. Three months later, Netflix confirmed Longmire would resume on its service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The ten-episode fourth season, which continued to be filmed on location in New Mexico,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> was made available for viewing in North America and Oceania on September 10, 2015.</p>
<p>On October 30, 2015, Netflix announced Longmire would return for a fifth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Season 5 location filming, scheduled for late March to late June, 2016, was also primarily in Las Vegas, New Mexico, the actual town visible in the series. Other locations included towns in the surrounding area. The studio work was again completed at Garson Studios at <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe_University_of_Art_and_Design" title="Santa Fe University of Art and Design">Santa Fe University of Art and Design</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The ten-episode fifth season was made available for viewing on September 23, 2016.</p>
<p>According to Eric Witt, director of the Santa Fe Film Office, season 6 filming was scheduled to begin in March 2017 in Santa Fe, Pecos, Los Alamos and Las Vegas, New Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_broadcasts">International broadcasts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: International broadcasts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

Season two began airing in Australia on July 31, 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> and in Italy on <a href="/wiki/Rete_4" title="Rete 4">Rete 4</a> on July 4, 2013, at 9:00 pm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In Ireland, the series is shown on <a href="/wiki/RT%C3%89_One" title="RTÉ One">RTÉ One</a>, and in Germany on <a href="/wiki/RTL_Nitro" title="RTL Nitro">RTL Nitro</a> as of January 10, 2014, at 10:05 pm. The series made its Canadian debut in English in January 2014 on <a href="/wiki/Aboriginal_Peoples_Television_Network" title="Aboriginal Peoples Television Network">APTN</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> and in French on May 26, 2014, on <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9ries%2B" title="Séries+">Séries+</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> International broadcasters retain the rights to new Netflix-commissioned seasons of the show until their licenses expire.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media_release">Home media release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Home media release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first season of <i>Longmire</i> was released on May 28, 2013. It featured a documentary about filming in New Mexico, when the setting is in Wyoming, a gallery of photographic stills, and some unaired scenes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The second season was released on May 13, 2014. The three-disc set included an extended directors' cut version of the seventh episode, "Sound and Fury", as well as the season finale, "Bad Medicine". A bonus featurette, "Testing Courage: The Storm Defines the Man" was also included.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> The third season, including a bonus featurette, "The Ghost in the Storm," was released on March 3, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> The first three seasons were added to <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> in the United States in June 2015. The fourth season was released on September 16, 2016, in a three-disc, 10-episode set.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S4_DVD_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-S4_DVD-46">[46]</a></sup> The Blu-ray releases of the series were handled by the <a href="/wiki/Warner_Archive_Collection" title="Warner Archive Collection">Warner Archive Collection</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S4_DVD_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-S4_DVD-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Reception has generally been positive. During Season 1, Nancy DeWolf Smith of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> called the series "the best of two worlds: a modern crime drama with dry wit and sometimes heart-wrenching emotion that's also got a glorious setting under the big sky of Wyoming." She added: "If it weren't for a few modern conveniences, like cellphones and trucks, it might as well be 1875, so rugged and unspoiled does the scenery look."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Newsday" title="Newsday">Newsday</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Verne Gay stated: "<i>Longmire</i> arrives as silently as a dust devil kicked up by a high wind on the Wyoming plains. With little in the way of fanfare and a lead actor unacquainted with household name status, it must instead rely on a quiet fortitude, much like its namesake." He added: "Unassuming <i>Longmire</i> doesn't shout 'LOVE ME!' but instead works its charms subtly, quietly. There's promise here."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alan_Sepinwall" title="Alan Sepinwall">Alan Sepinwall</a> of <a href="/wiki/HitFix" title="HitFix">HitFix</a> said of Season 1, "there's a sense of place to the show that makes it feel unlike every other cop show on television" and that he would "like to see the mysteries grow more engaging as the series moves along, but <i>Longmire</i> at least starts with a good foundation in Walt, his sidekicks, and the wide, open spaces they travel."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> There was also some negative commentary during Season 1, including one from the <i><a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Chronicle" title="San Francisco Chronicle">San Francisco Chronicle</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s David Wiegand who wrote that the series "has the look and feel of a show cooked up by a bunch of bored TV industry types while they were waiting for the valet to bring their car to them at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills" title="Beverly Hills">Beverly Hills</a> <a href="/wiki/Chuck_E._Cheese%27s" title="Chuck E. Cheese's">Chuck E. Cheese</a>." He added: "There's very little drama, and the pilot episode lumbers along like an overfed elk."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>Three years later, after viewing the first three episodes of Season 4, a top critic on the <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> site wrote the following in his review. "It sometimes sounds like faint praise to describe a series like you would a reliable car but <i>Longmire</i> is just a sturdy show. It is well-constructed all around—confidently made, well-acted, and the writing is much smarter than many shows like it. It hums, each episode these three feeling more well-paced than the one before."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> At the same point in the series, Mike Hale of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> also filed a favorable review, with particular praise for actor <a href="/wiki/Robert_Taylor_(Australian_actor)" title="Robert Taylor (Australian actor)">Robert Taylor</a>, described as a "modern day Gary Cooper or Joel McCrae".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the aggregate, <i>Longmire</i> has received favorable reviews on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>. For example, Season 4 received an average rating of 8.5/10 based on 7 reviews on Rotten Tomatoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Neither site has adequate data yet for Season 5 to provide a rating.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Longmire_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, the pilot episode of <i>Longmire</i>, teleplay by Hunt Baldwin and John Coveny, was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Award" title="Edgar Award">Edgar Award</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Mystery_Writers_of_America" title="Mystery Writers of America">Mystery Writers of America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> The series also won a Bronze <a href="/wiki/Key_Art_Awards" title="Key Art Awards">Key Art Award</a> for its season two <a href="/wiki/Television_advertisement" title="Television advertisement">television advertisement</a>, titled "The Oath".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> The season two episode, "Party's Over", was also nominated by the Entertainment Industries Council for a PRISM Award for Best Drama Series Episode – Substance Use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Also in 2013, the series won the <a href="/wiki/National_Cowboy_%26_Western_Heritage_Museum" title="National Cowboy &amp; Western Heritage Museum">National Cowboy &amp; Western Heritage Museum</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Wrangler" title="Bronze Wrangler">Bronze Wrangler</a> for Fictional Television Drama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> In 2014, <a href="/wiki/Steve_La_Porte" title="Steve La Porte">Steve La Porte</a> was nominated for a Makeup Artists and Hairstylist Guild Award for Best Special Makeup Effects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>Over the years, the series has also received two Key Art Awards and a Red Nation Film of Excellence Award, the latter for Supporting Actor <a href="/wiki/A_Martinez" title="A Martinez">A Martinez</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
