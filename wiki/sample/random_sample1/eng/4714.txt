<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Long Biên District</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Long Biên District</b> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Districts_of_Vietnam" title="Districts of Vietnam">urban district</a> (<i><span lang="vi" xml:lang="vi">quận</span></i>) of <a href="/wiki/Hanoi" title="Hanoi">Hanoi</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>. The district is named for the formerly separate settlement of <a href="/wiki/Long_Bi%C3%AAn" title="Long Biên">Long Biên</a> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <i>Longbian</i>), which served as the capital of <a href="/wiki/Jiaozhou_(region)" title="Jiaozhou (region)">Jiaozhou</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jiaozhi_Commandery" title="Jiaozhi Commandery">Jiaozhi</a> in imperial China and as the capital of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ly_Bi" title="Ly Bi">Ly Bi</a>'s kingdom of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Van_Xuan" title="Van Xuan">Van Xuan</a>. It is Hanoi's only urban district on the east side of the <a href="/wiki/Red_River_(Asia)" title="Red River (Asia)">Red River</a>. The district includes <a href="/wiki/Long_Bi%C3%AAn_Bridge" title="Long Biên Bridge">Long Biên Bridge</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_Airlines" title="Vietnam Airlines">Vietnam Airlines</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Authority_of_Vietnam" title="Civil Aviation Authority of Vietnam">Civil Aviation Authority of Vietnam</a> (CAAV),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Pham_Nhat_Vuong" title="Pham Nhat Vuong">VinGroup</a>.</p>
