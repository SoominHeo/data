<p><b>Long An</b> (<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Long_An.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/bc/Long_An.ogg" title="Long An.ogg">listen</a></span></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Vietnam" title="Provinces of Vietnam">province</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mekong_Delta" title="Mekong Delta">Mekong Delta</a> region of southern <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>. The provincial capital is <a href="/wiki/T%C3%A2n_An,_Long_An" title="Tân An, Long An">Tân An</a>, and other major cities include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B%E1%BA%BFn_L%E1%BB%A9c" title="Bến Lức">Bến Lức</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BB%A9c_H%C3%B2a" title="Đức Hòa">Đức Hòa</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%E1%BB%99c_H%C3%B3a" title="Mộc Hóa">Mộc Hóa</a>. There are 13 districts within the province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Under the <a href="/wiki/Khmer_Empire" title="Khmer Empire">Khmer Empire</a>, before the Vietnamese annexation of the region, the province was known as <span lang="km">ឈ្មោះថ្មី</span>, <i><span lang="km-Latn">Chmu Thmey</span></i> ("New Name") or <span lang="km">កំពង់គោ</span>, <i><span lang="km-Latn">Kompong Kô</span></i> ("Cow Shore").</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Long_An_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Long An is situated in an advantageous position in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Southern_Key_Economic_Region_of_Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Southern Key Economic Region of Vietnam (page does not exist)">Southern Key Economic Region of Vietnam</a>. It serves as a bridge between <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City" title="Ho Chi Minh City">Ho Chi Minh City</a> in the north and 12 provinces in the <a href="/wiki/Mekong_Delta" title="Mekong Delta">Mekong Delta</a> in the south. The province also has Cambodia to its west and the East Sea (<a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a>) to its east.</p>
<p>Long An is a low-lying coastal region, and therefore some areas of it are subject to flooding during the rainy season, which lasts from the beginning of August until November.</p>
<p>The province has numerous rivers. Two of the main ones are the <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A0m_C%E1%BB%8F_%C4%90%C3%B4ng_River" title="Vàm Cỏ Đông River">Vàm Cỏ Đông</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/V%C3%A0m_C%E1%BB%8F_Tay" title="Vàm Cỏ Tay">Vàm Cỏ Tay</a>, which connect with the <a href="/wiki/Ti%E1%BB%81n_River" title="Tiền River">Tiền</a> to form a larger river system. Another important river in the region is the <a href="/wiki/So%C3%A0i_R%E1%BA%A1p" title="Soài Rạp">Soài Rạp</a>.</p>

<p>They are further subdivided into 14 commune-level towns (or townlets), 166 communes, and 12 wards.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_change_concerns">Climate change concerns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Long_An_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate change concerns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Being a low-lying coastal region, Long An is particularly susceptible to floods resulting from rises in sea level due to climate change. The Climate Change Research Institute at <a href="/wiki/Can_Tho_University" title="Can Tho University">Can Tho University</a> (<i><span lang="vi">Trường Đại học Cần Thơ</span></i>), in studying the possible consequences of climate change, has predicted that 49% of Long An province is expected to be flooded if sea levels rise by 1 meter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vnnnet-sealevel_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-vnnnet-sealevel-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="University">University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Long_An_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Long An is home to two large universities:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://daihoclongan.edu.vn" rel="nofollow">Long An University of Economics and Industry</a> (<i><span lang="vi">Trường Đại Học Kinh Tế Công Nghiệp Long An</span></i>)</li>
<li><a class="external text" href="http://ttu.edu.vn" rel="nofollow">Tan Tao University</a> (<i><span lang="vi">Trường Đại Học Tân Tạo</span></i>)</li>
</ul>
