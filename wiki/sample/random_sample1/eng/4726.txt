<p>The national <b>flag of Romania</b> (<a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian</a>: <span lang="ro"><i>drapelul Rom√¢niei</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Tricolour_(flag)" title="Tricolour (flag)">tricolor</a> with vertical stripes, beginning from the flagpole: blue, yellow and red. It has a width-length ratio of 2:3.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Romania" title="Constitution of Romania">Constitution of Romania</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> provides that "The flag of Romania is tricolor; the colors are arranged vertically in the following order from the flagpole: blue, yellow, red". The proportions, shades of color as well as the flag protocol were established by law in 1994<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and extended in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The flag is coincidentally very similar to the civil <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Andorra" title="Flag of Andorra">flag of Andorra</a> and the state <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Chad" title="Flag of Chad">flag of Chad</a>. The similarity with Chad's flag, which differs only in having a darker shade of blue (indigo rather than cobalt), has caused international discussion. In 2004, Chad asked the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> to examine the issue, but then-president of Romania <a href="/wiki/Ion_Iliescu" title="Ion Iliescu">Ion Iliescu</a> announced no change would occur to the flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Moldova" title="Flag of Moldova">flag of Moldova</a> is related to the Romanian tricolor, except it has a 1:2 ratio, a lighter shade of blue, a slightly different tint of yellow, and the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Moldova" title="Coat of arms of Moldova">Moldovan coat of arms</a> in the middle. The <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Belgium" title="Flag of Belgium">civil ensign of Belgium</a> uses black rather than blue.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_significance_of_the_colors">History and significance of the colors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Romania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History and significance of the colors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_flags_of_Romania" title="History of the flags of Romania">History of the flags of Romania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_and_coat_of_arms_of_Moldavia" title="Flag and coat of arms of Moldavia">Flag and coat of arms of Moldavia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Historical_coat_of_arms_of_Transylvania" title="Historical coat of arms of Transylvania">Historical coat of arms of Transylvania</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tricolore1848.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="926" data-file-width="710" height="287" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Tricolore1848.jpg/220px-Tricolore1848.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Tricolore1848.jpg/330px-Tricolore1848.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Tricolore1848.jpg/440px-Tricolore1848.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tricolore1848.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

</div>
</div>
<p>During the 1970s and 1980s, with <a href="/wiki/Protochronism" title="Protochronism">Protochronism</a> receiving official endorsement, it was claimed that red, yellow and blue were found on late 16th-century royal grants of <a href="/wiki/Michael_the_Brave" title="Michael the Brave">Michael the Brave</a>, as well as shields and banners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Contemporary descriptions and later reconstructions indicate the flag of Wallachia during Michael's reign was made of <a href="/wiki/Damask" title="Damask">damask</a>, originally yellow-white but later faded to white. It featured a black eagle on a green juniper branch, with a cross in its beak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wallachian_uprising_of_1821" title="Wallachian uprising of 1821">Wallachian uprising of 1821</a>, the colors were present, among many others, on the canvas of the revolutionaries' flag (a religious image) and in its fringes; much later historiography attributed to them the following meanings: "Liberty (sky-blue), Justice (field yellow), Fraternity (blood red)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The tricolor was first adopted in <a href="/wiki/Wallachia" title="Wallachia">Wallachia</a> in 1834, when the reforming <i><a href="/wiki/Domnitor" title="Domnitor">domnitor</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Alexandru_II_Ghica" title="Alexandru II Ghica">Alexandru II Ghica</a> submitted naval and military colors designs for the approval of Sultan <a href="/wiki/Mahmud_II" title="Mahmud II">Mahmud II</a>. The latter was a "flag with a red, blue and yellow face, also having stars and a bird's head in the middle".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Soon, the order of colors was changed, with yellow appearing in the center.</p>
<p>In 1848, the flag adopted for Wallachia by <a href="/wiki/Wallachian_Revolution_of_1848" title="Wallachian Revolution of 1848">the revolutionaries</a> was a blue-yellow-red tricolor (with blue above, in line with the meaning "Liberty, Justice, Fraternity"). Already on 26 April, according to <i><a href="/wiki/Gazeta_de_Transilvania" title="Gazeta de Transilvania">Gazeta de Transilvania</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Romanian students in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> were hailing the new government with a blue, gold and red national flag, "as a symbol of union between Moldavians and <a href="/wiki/Muntenia" title="Muntenia">Muntenians</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-C.C4.83z.C4.83ni.C8.99teanu_1967.2C_p._36_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-C.C4.83z.C4.83ni.C8.99teanu_1967.2C_p._36-11">[11]</a></sup> Decree no. 1 of 14/26 June 1848 of the provisional government mentioned that "the National Flag will bear three colors: blue, yellow, red", emblazoned with the words "DPE–üTATE –§P–™–¶IE" (<i>Dreptate, FrƒÉ»õie</i> or "Justice, Fraternity"). It differed from earlier tricolors in that the blue stripe was on top, the princely monogram was eliminated from the corners, as was the crown atop the eagle at the end of the flagpole, while a motto was now present.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Wallachian_Revolution_of_1848,_vertical_stripes.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="600" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Flag_of_Wallachian_Revolution_of_1848%2C_vertical_stripes.svg/220px-Flag_of_Wallachian_Revolution_of_1848%2C_vertical_stripes.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Flag_of_Wallachian_Revolution_of_1848%2C_vertical_stripes.svg/330px-Flag_of_Wallachian_Revolution_of_1848%2C_vertical_stripes.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Flag_of_Wallachian_Revolution_of_1848%2C_vertical_stripes.svg/440px-Flag_of_Wallachian_Revolution_of_1848%2C_vertical_stripes.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Wallachian_Revolution_of_1848,_vertical_stripes.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The 1848 Tricolor with vertical stripes</div>

</div>
<p>Nevertheless, decree no. 252 of 13/25 July 1848, issued because "it has not been understood [yet] how the national flags should be designed", defined the flag as three <i>vertical</i> stripes, possibly influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_France" title="Flag of France">French model</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-N.C4.83sturel_1900.2F1901.2C_p._255_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-N.C4.83sturel_1900.2F1901.2C_p._255-13">[13]</a></sup> The shades were "dark blue, light yellow and carmine red"; as for order, "near the wood comes blue, then yellow and then red fluttering".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Petre Vasiliu-NƒÉsturel observes that from a heraldic point of view, on the French as well as the revolutionary Wallachian flag, the middle stripe represents a <a href="/wiki/Tincture_(heraldry)" title="Tincture (heraldry)">heraldic metal</a> (<a href="/wiki/Argent" title="Argent">argent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Or_(heraldry)" title="Or (heraldry)">or</a> respectively), thus, the two flags could be related.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-N.C4.83sturel_1900.2F1901.2C_p._255_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-N.C4.83sturel_1900.2F1901.2C_p._255-13">[13]</a></sup> Other historians believe that the tricolor was not an imitation of the French flag, instead embodying an old Romanian tradition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dogaru_1978.2C_p._868_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dogaru_1978.2C_p._868-16">[16]</a></sup> This theory is supported by a note from the revolutionary minister of foreign affairs to Emin Pasha: "the colors of the band that we, the leaders, wear, as well as all our followers, are not of modern origin. We have had our flags since an earlier time. When we received the tricolor insignia and bands we did not follow the spirit of imitation or fashion".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-C.C4.83z.C4.83ni.C8.99teanu_1967.2C_p._36_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-C.C4.83z.C4.83ni.C8.99teanu_1967.2C_p._36-11">[11]</a></sup> The same minister assured the extraordinary envoy of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottoman_Porte" title="Ottoman Porte">Porte</a>, Suleiman Pasha, that the flag's three colors had existed "for a long time; our ancestors bore them on their standard and their flags. So they are not a borrowing or an imitation from the present or a threat for the future".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dogaru_1978.2C_p._868_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dogaru_1978.2C_p._868-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the revolution was quelled, the old flags were restored and the revolutionaries punished for having worn the tricolor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dogaru_1978.2C_p._868_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-Dogaru_1978.2C_p._868-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbcaption">

Flag of the United Principalities of Romania</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>From 1859 until 1866, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Principalities_of_Wallachia_and_Moldavia" title="United Principalities of Wallachia and Moldavia">United Principalities of Wallachia and Moldavia</a> had a red-yellow-blue Romanian tricolor, with horizontal stripes, as national flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The flag was described properly in <i>Almanahul rom√¢n din 1866</i>: "a tricolor flag, divided in three stripes, red, yellow and blue and laid out horizontally: red above, blue below and yellow in the middle".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Although the Ottoman Empire did not allow the United Principalities to have their own symbols, the new flag gained a degree of international recognition. Relating prince Cuza's May‚ÄìJune 1864 journey to <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>, doctor <a href="/wiki/Carol_Davila" title="Carol Davila">Carol Davila</a> observed: "The Romanian flag was raised on the great mast, the Sultan's kayaks awaited us, the guard was armed, the Grand Vizier at the door... The Prince, quiet, dignified, concise in his speech, spent 20 minutes with the Sultan, who then came to review us‚Ä¶ Once again, the Grand Vizier led the Prince to the main gate and we returned to the Europe Palace, the Romanian flag still fluttering on the mast...".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Article 124 of the <a href="/wiki/1866_Constitution_of_Romania" title="1866 Constitution of Romania">1866 Constitution of Romania</a> provided that "the colors of the United Principalities will be Blue, Yellow and Red".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The order and placement of the colors were decided by the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_(Romania)" title="Chamber of Deputies (Romania)">Assembly of Deputies</a> in its session of 26 March 1867. Thus, following a proposal by <a href="/wiki/Nicolae_Golescu" title="Nicolae Golescu">Nicolae Golescu</a>, they were placed just as in 1848:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> vertically and in the following order: blue hoist, yellow in the middle and red fly. The country's coat of arms was placed only on army and princely flags, in the center; civilian flags remained without a coat of arms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Velcu_1938.2C_p._81_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Velcu_1938.2C_p._81-22">[22]</a></sup> The same distinction was made between flags of the Navy and those of the civil and merchant ships.</p>
<p>The rapporteur <a href="/wiki/Mihail_Kog%C4%83lniceanu" title="Mihail KogƒÉlniceanu">Mihail KogƒÉlniceanu</a>, who also conveyed the opinion of <a href="/wiki/Cezar_Bolliac" title="Cezar Bolliac">Cezar Bolliac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dimitrie_Br%C4%83tianu" title="Dimitrie BrƒÉtianu">Dimitrie BrƒÉtianu</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Constantin_Grigorescu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Constantin Grigorescu (page does not exist)">Constantin Grigorescu</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ion_Leca&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ion Leca (page does not exist)">Ion Leca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicolae_Golescu" title="Nicolae Golescu">Nicolae Golescu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gheorghe_Grigore_Cantacuzino" title="Gheorghe Grigore Cantacuzino">Gheorghe Grigore Cantacuzino</a>, said: "The tricolor flag as it is today is not (as the minister claims) the flag of the United Principalities. It is much more: it is itself the flag of the Romanian nation in all lands inhabited by Romanians".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The "Law for modifying the country's arms" of 11/23 March 1872 did not change these provisions,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Velcu_1938.2C_p._81_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Velcu_1938.2C_p._81-22">[22]</a></sup> only the design of the coat of arms. This design of the national flag lasted until 1948.</p>

<p>On 30 December 1947, Romania was proclaimed a <a href="/wiki/Socialist_state" title="Socialist state">people's republic</a> and all the ex-kingdom's symbols were outlawed, including the coat of arms and the tricolor flags that showed it. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist_Romania" title="Communist Romania">communist era in Romania</a>, the state flag had the emblem of the country in the middle of the yellow stripe, and for the first time the 2:3 proportion was regulated by law. By 1989, the coat of arms had been changed no less than four times.</p>

<p>Starting on 17 December 1989, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romanian_Revolution_of_1989" title="Romanian Revolution of 1989">revolution</a> at <a href="/wiki/Timi%C8%99oara" title="Timi»ôoara">Timi»ôoara</a>, the protesters began waving flags with the Communist coat of arms cut out of the middle. The coat of arms was perceived as a symbol of <a href="/wiki/Nicolae_Ceau%C8%99escu" title="Nicolae Ceau»ôescu">Nicolae Ceau»ôescu</a>'s dictatorial regime. These flags were called "the flag with the hole" (<i>drapelul cu gaurƒÉ</i>).</p>
<p>Decree-Law no. 2 of 27 December 1989 regarding the membership, organization and functioning of the Council of the National Salvation Front and of the territorial councils of the <a href="/wiki/National_Salvation_Front_(Romania)" title="National Salvation Front (Romania)">National Salvation Front</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> provided at article 1, among other matters, that "the national flag is the traditional tricolor of Romania, with the colors laid out vertically, in the following order, starting from the flagpole: blue, yellow, red".</p>
<p>In May 2013, a Romanian flag that was the largest in the world was unfurled at <a href="/wiki/Clinceni" title="Clinceni">Clinceni</a>. The flag, weighing some 5000¬†kg and with a size of 349.425¬†x¬†226.917¬†m, had an official surface area of 79,290.39¬†m<sup>2</sup>. This area surpassed the previous world record of 65,975¬†m<sup>2</sup>, held by a <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Lebanon" title="Flag of Lebanon">flag of Lebanon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>

<p>Law no. 75/1994 establishes the protocol for the flag of Romania. Its provisions are extended by the Governmental Decision no. 1157/2001<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> which approves the Regulations regarding the display of the Romanian flag, the singing of the national anthem and the use of insignia containing the Romanian coat of arms. Protocol for military flags and standards is fixed by internal regulation. The law contains the following provisions:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>The flag of Romania is always to be hoisted on the buildings and in the headquarters of public authorities and institutions, at the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Romania" title="List of political parties in Romania">political parties</a>, <a href="/wiki/Category:Trade_unions_in_Romania" title="Category:Trade unions in Romania">unions</a>, of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romanian_educational_system" title="Romanian educational system">educational</a> and <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Romania" title="Culture of Romania">cultural</a> institutions, on <a href="/wiki/Category:Border_crossings_of_Romania" title="Category:Border crossings of Romania">border crossings</a> and in <a href="/wiki/List_of_airports_in_Romania" title="List of airports in Romania">international airports</a>. As <a href="/wiki/Ensign" title="Ensign">ensign</a>, it is permanently hoisted on ships of any kind and other vessels that navigate under the Romanian flag. According to customary protocol, the flag of Romania is hoisted at the headquarters of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diplomatic_missions_of_Romania" title="Diplomatic missions of Romania">diplomatic missions and consular offices of Romania</a>, as well as at the residences of the chiefs of diplomatic missions and consular offices. Likewise, the flag of Romania is used as a standard on vehicles transporting chiefs of Romanian diplomatic missions and consular offices, in their official travels, according to the same customs.</p>

<p>Temporarily, on the <a href="/wiki/Great_Union_Day" title="Great Union Day">national day of Romania</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Romania" title="Public holidays in Romania">national holidays</a>, the flag of Romania may be hoisted in public places decided upon by the local authorities; and for official festivals and ceremonies with a local, national and international character, in the locations where these take place. Likewise, it must be raised for official visits undertaken in Romania by heads of state and of government, as well as by high political personalities representing the principal international intergovernmental bodies, at <a href="/wiki/List_of_airports_in_Romania" title="List of airports in Romania">airports</a>, <a href="/wiki/C%C4%83ile_Ferate_Rom%C3%A2ne" title="CƒÉile Ferate Rom√¢ne">rail stations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Category:Ports_and_harbours_of_Romania" title="Category:Ports and harbours of Romania">ports</a> and on their various routes. The flag is also hoisted at <a href="/wiki/Sport_in_Romania" title="Sport in Romania">sporting competitions</a>, at <a href="/wiki/List_of_football_stadiums_in_Romania" title="List of football stadiums in Romania">stadiums</a> and other sporting grounds, and during <a href="/wiki/Elections_in_Romania" title="Elections in Romania">election campaigns</a>, at the headquarters of electoral commissions and polling stations. During military ceremonies, the flag is hoisted according to military regulations.</p>
<p>The flag of Romania may be raised without restrictions by individuals at their domicile or residence, or by legal entities at their headquarters.</p>
<p>The Government is the only official body that fixes days of national <a href="/wiki/Mourning" title="Mourning">mourning</a>, on which the flag of Romania is lowered at half-staff.</p>
<p>The flags of other states may be hoisted on Romanian territory only together with the national flag and only on the occasion of visits with an official state character, international festivities and meetings, on official buildings and in public places specified in Law no. 75/1994. In such cases, the flag of Romania is hoisted in the place of honor, that is in the center, if the number of flags is odd, or to the right of the flag with which it occupies the center if the number of flags is even. In such cases, all flags must have the same dimensions (but not proportions, which are fixed by each respective country).</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_European_Union" title="Flag of the European Union">flag of the European Union</a> is raised next to the flag of Romania, to the left of the latter.</p>
<p>The raising of the flag of Romania at events that take place under the aegis of international organizations is done according to international regulations and customs.</p>

<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_colors" title="Military colors">military colors</a> are removed from its display case for the solemn occasion of its presentation, at the ceremony for taking the military <a href="/wiki/Oath" title="Oath">oath</a>, at parades of troops and reviews on the front, at the giving or taking of command by the respective unit, at the granting of military honors during military funerals, or on other occasions if required.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Military_colors_of_Romania_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Military_colors_of_Romania-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>When in formation and standing, the color bearer keeps the military colors near his foot, holding his right hand down on the rod and his left hand on the rod, at his chest level. The rod's low end must be in front of his right foot. When <a href="/wiki/Salute" title="Salute">saluting</a> from this position, the military colors are bowed at horizontal for the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Romania" title="President of Romania">Romanian President</a> and other heads of state and at 45¬†<a href="/wiki/Degree_(angle)" title="Degree (angle)">degrees</a> for the other civil and military staff. When marching, the color bearer holds the military colors vertically. If the unit is walking more than 100¬†m, the rod is introduced inside the scarf's muff. When traveling by vehicle, the color bearer with the military color stands inside the unit commander's car. During the march, the color bearer salutes by bowing the military colors at 45¬†degrees, regardless the person. When two military units cross each other (either one or both of them are marching in formation) the military colors are bowed for salute at 45¬†degrees. In case of raining, snowing or strong winds, the military colors are protected by a transparent plastic cover.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Military_colors_of_Romania_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-Military_colors_of_Romania-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ensign" title="Ensign">ensign</a> of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romanian_Navy" title="Romanian Navy">Navy</a> vessel must be raised daily on the stern flagpole at 8¬†<a href="/wiki/12-hour_clock" title="12-hour clock">a.m.</a>, and on holidays at 9¬†a.m. If the vessel is in motion, the ensign remains raised permanently where the <a href="/wiki/Boom_(sailing)" title="Boom (sailing)">boom</a> meets the mast. Usually, the hoisting of a vessel's ensign takes place in the presence of the entire crew, which is not the case at the lowering, daily at sunset.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Military_colors_of_Romania_32-2"><a href="#cite_note-Military_colors_of_Romania-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Penalties">Penalties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Romania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Penalties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to Governmental Decision no. 1157/2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> which details the rules of hoisting the flag of Romania, the citizens must show respect to the Romanian flag and never offend it.</p>
<p>The hoisting of a Romanian flag of another shape, dimension, model or color than those regulated by law, or having an improper condition, is a contravention and is punishable with a fine of between 500 and 1500 <a href="/wiki/Romanian_leu" title="Romanian leu">lei</a>.</p>
<p>Not raising the national flag by public authorities and institutions, or in the mandatory situations stated by the law, the improper hoisting of the flag and the hoisting of the flag of another country outside the situations regulated by law, or with improper dimensions, represents contravention and is punishable with a fine of between 2500 and 5000 lei.</p>
<p>The contraventions are ascertained and sanctioned by mandataries of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Interior_and_Administrative_Reform_(Romania)" title="Ministry of Interior and Administrative Reform (Romania)">Minister of Public Administration</a>, by the <a href="/wiki/Prefect" title="Prefect">Prefect</a> or his mandataries, and are applied to the director of the public authority or institution, to the <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a>, to the <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Romania" title="Counties of Romania">President of the county's council</a>, or to the private individual or juridical person that committed the contraventions.</p>
<p>Until 2011, article 236 of the <a href="/wiki/Penal_Code_of_Romania" title="Penal Code of Romania">Penal Code of Romania</a> stated that any display of contempt against the symbols of Romania was punishable by detention between 6 months and 3 years in prison. Article 344 of the same Penal Code provided that, in times of war, lowering the vessel's ensign during a battle in order to serve the enemy's cause is punishable by life in prison or detention between 15 and 25 years in prison and civil penalties. A new penal code adopted that year eliminates the provisions of article 236 and reduces the punishment provided by article 344 (renamed article 420) to between 10 and 20 years' imprisonment and civil penalties.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flag_Day">Flag Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Romania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Flag Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Law no. 96 of 20 May 1998<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> proclaimed 26 June as the Day of the National Flag of Romania. It was on this day in 1848 that Decree no. 1 of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1848_Wallachian_Revolution#Provisional_Government" title="1848 Wallachian Revolution">Wallachian Provisional Government</a> was issued, making the red-yellow-blue tricolor the national flag.</p>
<p>On Flag Day, public authorities and other state institutions are obliged by law to organize cultural/educational programs and events, with a patriotic or scientific character, devoted to Romanian history, as well as specific military ceremonies, organized within units of the Ministry of National Defense and the Ministry of the Internal Affairs.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_colors_and_ensigns">Military colors and ensigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Romania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Military colors and ensigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Between 1995 and 1998, the Romanian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naval_jack" title="Naval jack">naval jack</a> was similar to the rank flags of Navy officers. Afterward, it was replaced with a 1:1 national flag with two crossed white anchors in the center of the blue stripe, similarly to the naval jack used between 1966 and 1989.</p>
<p>The standard of the Chief of the General Staff is a square Romanian tricolor with four white stars, one beneath the other, in the center of the blue stripe. It can be used both as car standard or as rank flag on Navy vessels.</p>
<p>The ships' pennant is a horizontal piece of canvas in the shape of an isosceles triangle, with a 1:10 ratio, on which is printed the Romanian national tricolor.</p>
<p>The <i>Album des pavillons nationaux et des marques distinctives</i> (2000)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Album_des_pavillons....2C_2000_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-Album_des_pavillons....2C_2000-35">[35]</a></sup> also depicts the rank flags of navy officers. These flags indicate that a commanding or leadership officer is on board. But one exception, they are rectangular light blue 2:3 canvases, on which are found a blue anchor, the Romanian flag in the canton and a number of five-pointed yellow stars, according to rank: four for the Chief of the Naval Forces Staff, three for the Deputy Chief of the Naval Staff, two for fleet or flotilla Commander and one for a major Naval unit Commander. The flag of a regular Navy unit Commander is, by exception, triangular and it lacks stars.</p>
