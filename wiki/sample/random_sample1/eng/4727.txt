<p><b>Rumoi Subprefecture</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">留萌振興局</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Rumoi-shinkō-kyoku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Subprefectures_of_Hokkaido" title="Subprefectures of Hokkaido">subprefecture</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 2011, it had a population of 52,627 and an area of 4,019.97 square kilometres (1,552.12 sq mi). The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of the subprefecture, 13 people per km<sup>2</sup>, is very low compared to the rest of Japan. The population of Rumoi Subprefecture represents 0.96% of the overall population of Hokkaido.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rumoi_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rumoi-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivers">Rivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rumoi_Subprefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rivers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Rumoi_River" title="Rumoi River">Rumoi River</a> 44 kilometres (27 mi) flows from <a href="/wiki/Mount_Poroshiri" title="Mount Poroshiri">Mount Poroshiri</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hidaka_Mountains" title="Hidaka Mountains">Hidaka Mountain</a> range to the Sea of Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rumoi_Subprefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mashike_Subprefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mashike Subprefecture (page does not exist)">Mashike Subprefecture</a> was established as part of the administrative reforms of Hokkaido during the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a> (1868 – 1912). The capital was moved to Rumoi in 1914, and the subprefecture was renamed to Rumoi Subprefecture in the same year. <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi,_Hokkaido" title="Toyotomi, Hokkaido">Toyotomi Town</a>) in Teshio District transferred to <a href="/wiki/S%C5%8Dya_Subprefecture" title="Sōya Subprefecture">Sōya Subprefecture</a> in 1948, and <a href="/wiki/Horonobe,_Hokkaido" title="Horonobe, Hokkaido">Horonobe</a> was transferred to Sōya Subprefecture in 2010.</p>
