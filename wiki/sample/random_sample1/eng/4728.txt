<p><b>Rumonge Province</b> is one of the eighteen <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Burundi" title="Provinces of Burundi">provinces</a> of <a href="/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assembly_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-assembly-2">[2]</a></sup> It was created on 26 March 2015 by combining the communes of Burambi, Buyengero and Rumonge, previously part of <a href="/wiki/Bururi_Province" title="Bururi Province">Bururi Province</a>, with the Bugarama and Muhuta communes previously belonging to <a href="/wiki/Bujumbura_Rural_Province" title="Bujumbura Rural Province">Bujumbura Rural Province</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-law_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-law-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rumonge_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The capital is at <a href="/wiki/Rumonge" title="Rumonge">Rumonge</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-law_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-law-3">[3]</a></sup> on the shores of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Tanganyika" title="Lake Tanganyika">Lake Tanganyika</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Rumonge_Nature_Reserve" title="Rumonge Nature Reserve">Rumonge Nature Reserve</a>, a semi-deciduous forest, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kigwena_Nature_Reserve&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kigwena Nature Reserve (page does not exist)">Kigwena Nature Reserve</a>, a lowland tropical forest, are located in the province.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rumonge_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The province's first governor, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Juv%C3%A9nal_Bigirimana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Juvénal Bigirimana (page does not exist)">Juvénal Bigirimana</a>, was sworn in on 17 April 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In June 2015 it <a href="/wiki/Burundian_legislative_election,_2015" title="Burundian legislative election, 2015">elected four deputies</a> to Burundi's <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(Burundi)" title="National Assembly (Burundi)">National Assembly</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and in July it chose its first two <a href="/wiki/Senate_(Burundi)" title="Senate (Burundi)">senators</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jean-Pierre_Ndayahundwa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jean-Pierre Ndayahundwa (page does not exist)">Jean-Pierre Ndayahundwa</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tharcisse_Rutomo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tharcisse Rutomo (page does not exist)">Tharcisse Rutomo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Communes">Communes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rumonge_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Communes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
