<p><b>Ruben Santiago-Hudson</b> (born November 24, 1956) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Playwright" title="Playwright">playwright</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Theatre_director" title="Theatre director">director</a> who has won national awards for his work in all three categories. From 2009 to 2011, he played Captain Roy Montgomery in ABC's <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_(TV_series)" title="Castle (TV series)">Castle</a></i>. In November 2011 he appeared on <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lydia_R._Diamond" title="Lydia R. Diamond">Lydia R. Diamond</a>'s play <i>Stick Fly</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2013 he starred in the TV series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_Winter_Sun" title="Low Winter Sun">Low Winter Sun</a></i>, a police drama set in <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruben_Santiago-Hudson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ruben Hudson was born in 1956 in <a href="/wiki/Lackawanna,_New_York" title="Lackawanna, New York">Lackawanna, New York</a>, the son of Alean Hudson and Ruben Santiago, a railroad worker. He later adopted his mother's maiden name as part of his compound surname.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FilmRef_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FilmRef-3">[3]</a></sup> His father was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_American" title="Puerto Rican American">Puerto Rican</a> and his mother was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">African American</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DanaR_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-DanaR-4">[4]</a></sup> He went to Lackawanna High school, earned his bachelor's degree from <a href="/wiki/Binghamton_University" title="Binghamton University">Binghamton University</a>, his master's degree from <a href="/wiki/Wayne_State_University" title="Wayne State University">Wayne State University</a> and received an honorary Doctor of Humane Letters from <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_State_College" title="Buffalo State College">Buffalo State College</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DanaR_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-DanaR-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2003, he was the reader in Volume 13 of the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> film, <i>Unchained Memories: Readings from the Slave Narratives</i>. The series was narrated by <a href="/wiki/Whoopi_Goldberg" title="Whoopi Goldberg">Whoopi Goldberg</a>. He wrote <i><a href="/wiki/Lackawanna_Blues" title="Lackawanna Blues">Lackawanna Blues</a></i> (2001), an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autobiographical" title="Autobiographical">autobiographical</a> play in which he portrayed himself and some twenty different characters from his past, which was produced in New York at the Joseph Papp Theatre in 2001. He adapted it for a highly acclaimed, award-winning 2005 <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> film, in which the parts were played by different people. He won the <a href="/wiki/Humanitas_Prize" title="Humanitas Prize">Humanitas Prize</a> and earned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emmy_Awards" title="Emmy Awards">Emmy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America" title="Writers Guild of America">Writers Guild of America</a> Award nominations.</p>
<p>Santiago-Hudson appeared on Broadway in <i><a href="/wiki/Jelly%27s_Last_Jam" title="Jelly's Last Jam">Jelly's Last Jam</a></i> (1992), written by <a href="/wiki/George_C._Wolfe" title="George C. Wolfe">George C. Wolfe</a>. He received the 1996 Tony for his performance in <a href="/wiki/August_Wilson" title="August Wilson">August Wilson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Seven_Guitars" title="Seven Guitars">Seven Guitars</a></i>. Santiago-Hudson's screen credits include <i><a href="/wiki/Coming_to_America" title="Coming to America">Coming to America</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Domestic_Disturbance" title="Domestic Disturbance">Domestic Disturbance</a></i>.</p>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> he has appeared on the daytime dramas <i><a href="/wiki/Another_World_(TV_series)" title="Another World (TV series)">Another World</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/All_My_Children" title="All My Children">All My Children</a></i>. His work in primetime series have included <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cosby_Mysteries" title="The Cosby Mysteries">The Cosby Mysteries</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Undercover" title="New York Undercover">New York Undercover</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/NYPD_Blue" title="NYPD Blue">NYPD Blue</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Touched_by_an_Angel" title="Touched by an Angel">Touched by an Angel</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_West_Wing" title="The West Wing">The West Wing</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Third_Watch" title="Third Watch">Third Watch</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> and five episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i> (which coincidentally stars <i>Lackawanna Blues</i> star <a href="/wiki/S._Epatha_Merkerson" title="S. Epatha Merkerson">S. Epatha Merkerson</a>), among others. He starred as New York City Police Captain Roy Montgomery in the ABC series <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_(TV_series)" title="Castle (TV series)">Castle</a></i> until his character's death occurred in the third season finale. In 2007 he starred in a <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">Public Broadcasting Service</a> <a href="/wiki/Nova_(TV_series)" title="Nova (TV series)">Nova documentary</a> about the life of chemist <a href="/wiki/Percy_Lavon_Julian" title="Percy Lavon Julian">Percy Lavon Julian</a>.</p>
<p>In 2013, Santiago-Hudson won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucille_Lortel_Award" title="Lucille Lortel Award">Lucille Lortel Award</a> for Outstanding Director,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> an <a href="/wiki/Obie_Award" title="Obie Award">Obie Award</a> for Direction,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Drama_Desk_Award_for_Outstanding_Director_of_a_Play" title="Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Director of a Play">Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Director of a Play</a> for his work in the <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> production of August Wilson's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Piano_Lesson" title="The Piano Lesson">The Piano Lesson</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
