<p><b>Ruben Sosa Arzaiz</b> (born April 25, 1966 in <a href="/wiki/Montevideo" title="Montevideo">Montevideo</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguayan</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a>. He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay national team</a> and a plethora of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_club" title="Football club">clubs</a> around the world.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rub%C3%A9n_Sosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sosa started his career in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danubio" title="Danubio">Danubio</a> at the age of 15, being one of the youngest footballers to play in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_Uruguaya" title="Primera División Uruguaya">Uruguayan First Division</a>. He played for Danubio from 1982 to 1985, when he was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Real_Zaragoza" title="Real Zaragoza">Real Zaragoza</a>. With this club, Ruben Sosa won the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> in <a href="/wiki/1985%E2%80%9386_Copa_del_Rey" title="1985–86 Copa del Rey">1986</a>, scoring in the final against <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After playing for Zaragoza, Ruben Sosa was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>'s <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">S.S. Lazio</a>, staying for four years before being sold to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internazionale_Milano_F.C." title="Internazionale Milano F.C.">Internazionale</a>, where he reached his greatest form as a football player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_Rush_to_Stardom_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-A_Rush_to_Stardom-2">[2]</a></sup> He was Inter's leading goal scorer in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1992%E2%80%9393_F.C._Internazionale_Milano_season" title="1992–93 F.C. Internazionale Milano season">1992–93</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%9394_F.C._Internazionale_Milano_season" title="1993–94 F.C. Internazionale Milano season">1993–94</a> seasons, winning the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%9394_UEFA_Cup" title="1993–94 UEFA Cup">1994</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> However, the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Bergkamp" title="Dennis Bergkamp">Dennis Bergkamp</a> in the Summer of 1993, led to splits within the Inter camp, and as a result Sosa left Serie A in the summer of 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After years of success in Uruguay, Spain and Italy, Ruben Sosa played for <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>´s <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>, winning the <a href="/wiki/List_of_German_football_champions" title="List of German football champions">Bundesliga title</a> in <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_Bundesliga" title="1995–96 Bundesliga">1995–96</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>When he left Borussia Dortmund, he returned to Spain to play for <a href="/wiki/CD_Logro%C3%B1%C3%A9s" title="CD Logroñés">CD Logroñés</a>. After a couple of months playing for the team, Ruben Sosa decided to leave in order to make his dream come true: he wanted to play for his favourite team in Uruguay, famous <a href="/wiki/Club_Nacional_de_Football" title="Club Nacional de Football">Nacional</a>. At Nacional, Sosa won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_Uruguaya" title="Primera División Uruguaya">Uruguayan League</a> in <a href="/wiki/1998_Uruguayan_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1998 Uruguayan Primera División">1998</a>, <a href="/wiki/2000_Uruguayan_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2000 Uruguayan Primera División">2000</a> and <a href="/wiki/2001_Uruguayan_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2001 Uruguayan Primera División">2001</a>, becoming one of the fans' heroes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002, he left Nacional to play in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shanghai_Shenhua" title="Shanghai Shenhua">Shanghai Shenhua</a>. In 2003, Shanghai won the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Jia-A_League" title="Chinese Jia-A League">Chinese Jia-A League</a> title, but the club was stripped of the title in 2013 for <a href="/wiki/Match_fixing" title="Match fixing">match fixing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004, he returned to Nacional, this time as assistant coach, winning the <a href="/wiki/2005_Uruguayan_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2005 Uruguayan Primera División">2005 league title</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team">National team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rub%C3%A9n_Sosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: National team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With the <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay national team</a>, Sosa won the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Copa América">Copa América</a> in <a href="/wiki/1987_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1987 Copa América">1987</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_Rush_to_Stardom_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-A_Rush_to_Stardom-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/1995_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1995 Copa América">1995</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal-1">[1]</a></sup> and he played at the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 FIFA World Cup</a> in Italy;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-From_Italia_90_to_Italy_now_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-From_Italia_90_to_Italy_now-5">[5]</a></sup> he also managed a runners-up medal at the <a href="/wiki/1989_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1989 Copa América">1989 Copa América</a>, where he was named the tournament's best player,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-From_Italia_90_to_Italy_now_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-From_Italia_90_to_Italy_now-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and later took part at the <a href="/wiki/1993_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1993 Copa América">1993 Copa América</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rub%C3%A9n_Sosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nicknamed <i>El Principito</i> (The Little Prince) by Uruguayan fans, Sosa was a quick, creative, talented, agile, and powerful left-footed forward, with good skills, control, and explosive acceleration. A diminutive footballer with a stocky physique, he was usually deployed as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Second_striker" title="Forward (association football)">second striker</a>, although he was also capable of playing as a main <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_Rush_to_Stardom_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-A_Rush_to_Stardom-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ricordate_Ruben_Sosa.3F_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ricordate_Ruben_Sosa.3F-8">[8]</a></sup> as he was capable of both scoring and creating goals. He was known in particular for his spectacular efforts and thunderous <a href="/wiki/Direct_free_kick" title="Direct free kick">free kicks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-Rub.C3.A9n_Sosa_.E2.80.93_Uruguay.E2.80.99s_Little_Prince.2C_a_Poet_of_the_Goal-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ricordate_Ruben_Sosa.3F_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ricordate_Ruben_Sosa.3F-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> A complete forward, who could shoot, volley, dribble, pass with precision, and hold up the ball to create chances for team-mates, his attributes made him one of the best strikers in Europe during his prime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_Rush_to_Stardom_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-A_Rush_to_Stardom-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ricordate_Ruben_Sosa.3F_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ricordate_Ruben_Sosa.3F-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He is considered by many to be one of the best Uruguayan forwards of the last thirty years, alongside <a href="/wiki/Luis_Su%C3%A1rez" title="Luis Suárez">Luis Suárez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Francescoli" title="Enzo Francescoli">Enzo Francescoli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Aguilera" title="Carlos Aguilera">Carlos Aguilera</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Recoba" title="Álvaro Recoba">Álvaro Recoba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Fonseca" title="Daniel Fonseca">Daniel Fonseca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diego_Forl%C3%A1n" title="Diego Forlán">Diego Forlán</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fabian_O%27Neill" title="Fabian O'Neill">Fabian O'Neill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rub%C3%A9n_Paz" title="Rubén Paz">Rubén Paz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-From_Italia_90_to_Italy_now_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-From_Italia_90_to_Italy_now-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-retirement">Post-retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rub%C3%A9n_Sosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nowadays, Ruben Sosa works for <a href="/wiki/Club_Nacional_de_Football" title="Club Nacional de Football">Nacional</a> as assistant coach, but he also played for a <a href="/wiki/Uruguayan_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Uruguayan Segunda División">Second Division</a> team in Uruguay, <a href="/wiki/Racing_Club_de_Montevideo" title="Racing Club de Montevideo">Racing Club de Montevideo</a> in 2006.</p>
