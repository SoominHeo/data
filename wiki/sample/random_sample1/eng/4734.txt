<p>The <i><b>Rubis</b></i> type is a class of first-generation <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_submarine" title="Nuclear submarine">nuclear</a> attack <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarines</a> of the <a href="/wiki/French_Navy" title="French Navy">French Navy</a>. The class comprises six vessels and they are the most compact nuclear attack submarines to date. All submarines of the class (except for <a href="/wiki/French_submarine_Casabianca_(S603)" title="French submarine Casabianca (S603)"><i>Casabianca</i></a>) are named after <a href="/wiki/Gemstone" title="Gemstone">gemstones</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rubis-class_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the <i>Rubis</i> class belonged to the same generation as the <a href="/wiki/Redoutable-class_submarine_(1967)" title="Redoutable-class submarine (1967)"><i>Redoutable</i></a>, due to President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_De_Gaulle" title="Charles De Gaulle">Charles De Gaulle</a>'s insistence on acquiring a nuclear deterrent for France, the <i>Rubis</i> program was started only in 1974, after the <a href="/wiki/Ballistic_missile_submarine" title="Ballistic missile submarine">ballistic missile submarine</a> program. The first <i>Rubis</i> hull was laid down in December 1976 and launched in 1979.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_Canada-class">Proposed <i>Canada</i>-class</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rubis-class_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Proposed Canada-class">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1987, the Canadian White Paper on Defence recommended the purchase of 10 to 12 <i>Rubis</i> or <a href="/wiki/Trafalgar-class_submarine" title="Trafalgar-class submarine"><i>Trafalgar</i>-class submarines</a> under technology transfer, which would be known as the <i><a href="/wiki/Canada-class_submarine" title="Canada-class submarine">Canada</a></i>-class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> with the choice of the type of submarine due to be confirmed before Summer 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The goal was to build up a three-ocean navy and to assert Canadian sovereignty over Arctic waters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i>Rubis</i>-class as designed failed to meet the Canadian Statement of Requirement (SOR) as it was noisy underwater and slow. It also came with the caveat that the first 4-5 submarines would have to be built in France. Unlike the British <i>Trafalgar</i>-class, the <i>Rubis</i> design did not require USA permission to transfer the nuclear propulsion technology, as the Americans were certain to invoke their veto of the sale to Canada. The French brought back a revision to their design, added an "ice pick" so the submarine could operate under ice and were developing a modification for their torpedo tubes which were too short to use the Mark 48 torpedoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The purchase was finally abandoned in April 1989 due to high costs, particularly with the end of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AMETHYSTE_rebuild">AMETHYSTE rebuild</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rubis-class_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: AMETHYSTE rebuild">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The initial design of the <i>Rubis</i> proved to be problematic with unexpectedly high noise levels. This led to the <i>Améthyste</i> silencing program (<b>AMÉ</b>lioration <b>T</b>actique <b>HY</b>drodynamique <b>S</b>ilence <b>T</b>ransmission <b>E</b>coute, literally <i>Silent Acoustic Transmission Tactical Hydrodynamic Amelioration</i>) which was applied to the fifth (S605 <i>Améthyste</i>) and sixth (S606 <i>Perle</i>) hulls.</p>
<p><i>Améthyste</i> and <i>Perle</i> were both longer that the original <i>Rubis</i>, 73.6 metres (241 ft) as compared with 72 metres (236 ft) and the program included upgrades to the sonar, reshaping of the hull form and bow to improve silencing and additional upgrades of the electronics. With the upgrades tested and proven, the original 4 boats were rebuilt to the same standards between 1989 and 1995.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rubis-class_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>They have a central computer system for submarine detection, processing of information, and firing of weapons. The hull is made of 80 HLES high elasticity steel. The sonar dome and the <a href="/wiki/Conning_tower" title="Conning tower">conning tower</a> are made of composite materials. The submarines have two crews, "Blue" and "Red", who man the ships every three months in turn.</p>
<p>They will be succeeded by the 2nd-generation <a href="/wiki/French_Barracuda-class_submarine" title="French Barracuda-class submarine">Barracuda class</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operational_history">Operational history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rubis-class_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Operational history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 20 August 1993, <i>Rubis</i> collided with the oil tanker <i>Lyria</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 30 March 1994, <i>Émeraude</i> had a steam leakage, suffering 10 casualties.</p>
<p>During the <i>Péan</i> inter-allied manoeuvres of 1998, <i>Casabianca</i> managed to "sink" the <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carrier</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USS_Dwight_D._Eisenhower_(CVN-69)" title="USS Dwight D. Eisenhower (CVN-69)">USS <i>Dwight D. Eisenhower</i></a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ticonderoga-class_cruiser" title="Ticonderoga-class cruiser"><i>Ticonderoga</i>-class</a> cruiser that was escorting her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>During COMPTUEX 2015, an exercise led by the US Navy, <i>Saphir</i> successfully defeated the aircraft carrier <a href="/wiki/USS_Theodore_Roosevelt" title="USS Theodore Roosevelt">USS <i>Theodore Roosevelt</i></a> and its escort, managing to "sink" the US carrier. This was widely advertised by the French Navy but unmentioned by the US Navy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
