<p><b>Lucy Punch</b> (born 30 December 1977)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an English actress. She has appeared in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_Fuzz" title="Hot Fuzz">Hot Fuzz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_Teacher" title="Bad Teacher">Bad Teacher</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/You_Will_Meet_a_Tall_Dark_Stranger" title="You Will Meet a Tall Dark Stranger">You Will Meet a Tall Dark Stranger</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dinner_for_Schmucks" title="Dinner for Schmucks">Dinner for Schmucks</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Woods_(film)" title="Into the Woods (film)">Into the Woods</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucy_Punch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Punch was born in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, the daughter of Johanna and Michael Punch, who ran a market research company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She was educated privately at <a href="/wiki/Godolphin_School" title="Godolphin School">Godolphin School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Salisbury" title="Salisbury">Salisbury, Wiltshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latymer_Upper_School" title="Latymer Upper School">Latymer Upper School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hammersmith" title="Hammersmith">Hammersmith, London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMail2186286_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-DMail2186286-4">[4]</a></sup> She performed with the <a href="/wiki/National_Youth_Theatre" title="National Youth Theatre">National Youth Theatre</a> from 1993 to 1997, and began a course at <a href="/wiki/University_College_London" title="University College London">University College London</a> before dropping out to become an actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucy_Punch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bel_et_sombre_Cannes_2010.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="402" data-file-width="450" height="214" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Bel_et_sombre_Cannes_2010.jpg/240px-Bel_et_sombre_Cannes_2010.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Bel_et_sombre_Cannes_2010.jpg/360px-Bel_et_sombre_Cannes_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e1/Bel_et_sombre_Cannes_2010.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bel_et_sombre_Cannes_2010.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Punch (left) with the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/You_Will_Meet_a_Tall_Dark_Stranger" title="You Will Meet a Tall Dark Stranger">You Will Meet a Tall Dark Stranger</a></i> in 2010</div>

<p>Punch made her acting debut in a 1998 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Adventures_of_Robin_Hood" title="The New Adventures of Robin Hood">The New Adventures of Robin Hood</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Her other TV credits include the naive daughter of <a href="/wiki/Alison_Steadman" title="Alison Steadman">Alison Steadman</a>'s character in the short-lived series <i><a href="/wiki/Let_Them_Eat_Cake_(TV_series)" title="Let Them Eat Cake (TV series)">Let Them Eat Cake</a></i> which starred <a href="/wiki/French_and_Saunders" title="French and Saunders">French and Saunders</a>. She starred as a football player in the kids TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Renford_Rejects" title="Renford Rejects">Renford Rejects</a></i>, and played the role of victim Melissa Townsend in the 19th episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Midsomer_Murders" title="Midsomer Murders">Midsomer Murders</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2000, Punch appeared in the film Greenfingers.. Punch made her stage debut as Elaine in <a href="/wiki/Terry_Johnson_(dramatist)" title="Terry Johnson (dramatist)">Terry Johnson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a> adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Graduate" title="The Graduate">The Graduate</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She has worked at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Court_Theatre" title="Royal Court Theatre">Royal Court</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bush_Theatre" title="Bush Theatre">Bush</a> theatres, both in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004, Punch played receptionist Elaine Denham on <i><a href="/wiki/Doc_Martin" title="Doc Martin">Doc Martin</a></i>. She left the television show <i>The Class</i> after appearing in 11 of the first 12 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2006 she won the Best Actress award at the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_International_Film_Festival" title="Monaco International Film Festival">Monaco International Film Festival</a> for her performance in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Are_You_Ready_For_Love%3F" title="Are You Ready For Love?">Are You Ready For Love?</a></i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Punch starred in <a href="/wiki/Woody_Allen" title="Woody Allen">Woody Allen</a>'s 2010 film <i><a href="/wiki/You_Will_Meet_a_Tall_Dark_Stranger" title="You Will Meet a Tall Dark Stranger">You Will Meet a Tall Dark Stranger</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In August 2010, she appeared in <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a>'s three part police comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/Vexed" title="Vexed">Vexed</a></i>. In 2011, Punch appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_Teacher" title="Bad Teacher">Bad Teacher</a></i>, portraying sanctimonious school teacher Amy Squirrel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Punch was cast as Deena Pilgrim, the female lead in the TV pilot <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Powers_(FX_TV_series)" title="Powers (FX TV series)"><i>Powers</i></a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but the role was recast.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucy_Punch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Punch resides in <a href="/wiki/West_Hollywood,_California" title="West Hollywood, California">West Hollywood, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMail2186286_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-DMail2186286-4">[4]</a></sup> Her first child, a son named Rex, was born in July 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
