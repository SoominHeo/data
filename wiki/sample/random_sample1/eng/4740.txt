<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabi%C3%A1n_Monz%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Boca_Juniors">Boca Juniors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabi%C3%A1n_Monz%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Boca Juniors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Granadero_Baigorria" title="Granadero Baigorria">Granadero Baigorria</a>, Monzón began his professional career with <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> in 2007 after being promoted out of the club's youth academy. He made his Boca Juniors debut during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torneo_Clausura_2008#Torneo_Clausura" title="Torneo Clausura 2008">2008 Clausura</a> tournament in Boca Juniors, and was a surprise choice for the Argentina squad for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Olympic Games</a> later that year. This doubt was put to rest as Monzón started every match as the team went on to win gold.</p>
<p>Unsurprisingly, after Monzón's performance at the Olympics he moved to Europe to play for <a href="/wiki/Real_Betis" title="Real Betis">Real Betis</a> in <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> for a reported €9 million after being linked with a move to German side, Hamburg since the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Games_2008" title="Olympic Games 2008">Olympics</a> had begun. It was later revealed that Monzón had moved on a loan deal with the option to purchase the player outright in the summer of 2009. Monzón scored his first goal with Betis on his debut against <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Camp_Nou" title="Camp Nou">Camp Nou</a> on 24 September 2008, however, Real Betis eventually lost the game 3–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Monzón returned to Boca after his loan deal with <a href="/wiki/Real_Betis" title="Real Betis">Betis</a> ran out in July 2009. Upon his return he revealed his remarkably low wage compared to other stars at Boca.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: left; clear: left; margin: 0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0; width:30%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"The contract can't be changed, because before going to Betis I signed a contract for five years, and I have to respect it. I earn 2,000 pesos [€376], and from what has been spoken, they don't want to increase it, not even a little bit. But I will still stay at Boca."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;"><small>Luciano Fabián Monzón.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></small></cite></div>
<p>In his first season back with Boca, Monzón earned a regular playing time for his native club, playing in 23 league matches, with 2 goals. In August 2010, after his first season back in Argentina, he had two failed transfer attempts. First, he failed to move to <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukrainian</a> side <a href="/wiki/FC_Dnipro_Dnipropetrovsk" title="FC Dnipro Dnipropetrovsk">Dnipro</a> due to a congenital heart condition and a few days later a second transfer was cancelled, this time to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> team <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a>, admittedly by differences in payment method.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Despite the failed transfer attempts, Monzón continued to feature for the club, making 20 league appearances during the 2010-11 campaign, which ultimately would be his last at the club, before finally completing a permanent transfer to Europe with French side, <a href="/wiki/OGC_Nice" title="OGC Nice">OGC Nice</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="OGC_Nice">OGC Nice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabi%C3%A1n_Monz%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: OGC Nice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 July 2011 Monzón signed a 4-year contract with <a href="/wiki/OGC_Nice" title="OGC Nice">OGC Nice</a> in the French <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Monzón made his debut for Nice on 6 August 2011, in a 1-3 home loss against <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Olymipique_Lyonnais&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Olymipique Lyonnais (page does not exist)">Lyon</a>. During his one and only season in <a href="/wiki/Nice" title="Nice">Nice</a>, he was a regular feature in the club's starting lineup, managing 34 league appearances, marked with 8 goals, a great performance for a left defender. Monzón was set to begin the 2012-13 season with Nice, before completing a late transfer to Lyon on 26 August 12. His final match for Nice came on the opening matchday of the 2012-13 season: a 0-1 home defeat to Ajaccio on 11 August 2012.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympique_Lyonnais">Olympique Lyonnais</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabi%C3%A1n_Monz%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Olympique Lyonnais">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 26 August 2012, Monzón officially was unveiled as a new signining for 7-time French champions <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Olympique Lyonnais</a> on a 4-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Monzón was purchased by the club to replace <a href="/wiki/Aly_Cissokho" title="Aly Cissokho">Aly Cissokho</a> who signed for <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia</a> earlier in the summer.<br/>
The transfer fee was worth €3 million with bonus variables of up to €1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> With Lyon, the player began the season as a first team regular before losing his place in the team sheet after just 5 league appearances. Goal.com named Monzón as the worst transfer signing of the 2012-13 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 January 2013, Monzón signed a 6-month loan deal with Brazilian national champions <a href="/wiki/Fluminense_FC" title="Fluminense FC">Fluminense</a> in order to replace <a href="/wiki/Thiago_Carleto" title="Thiago Carleto">Thiago Carleto</a> who returned to <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_FC" title="São Paulo FC">São Paulo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He returned to Lyon on 16 July 2013 before being sold to Serie A side <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Catania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Calcio_Catania">Calcio Catania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabi%C3%A1n_Monz%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Calcio Catania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 16 July 2013, Monzón officially signed for <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Calcio Catania</a> in the Italian <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> on a four-year contract<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> for €3.3 million, while a further €300,000 could be paid on performance-related bonuses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Boca_Juniors">Return to Boca Juniors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabi%C3%A1n_Monz%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to Boca Juniors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2015, Monzón returned to his first club, Boca Juniors.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Universidad_de_Chile">Universidad de Chile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabi%C3%A1n_Monz%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Universidad de Chile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 20 January 2016, Monzón signed for <a href="/wiki/Club_Universidad_de_Chile" title="Club Universidad de Chile">Club Universidad de Chile</a> on a free transfer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fabi%C3%A1n_Monz%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Monzón was a member of the Argentine squad for the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2008 Beijing Olympics</a>, which also were figures like <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Messi" title="Lionel Messi">Lionel Messi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Ag%C3%BCero" title="Sergio Agüero">Sergio Agüero</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%81ngel_Di_Mar%C3%ADa" title="Ángel Di María">Ángel Di María</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Rom%C3%A1n_Riquelme" title="Juan Román Riquelme">Juan Román Riquelme</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diario_Publicable_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Diario_Publicable-17">[17]</a></sup> Argentina went on to win his second consecutive gold medal in those Olympics.</p>
<p>He has been sporadically nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina national football team</a>, accumulating seven appearances since 2009.</p>
