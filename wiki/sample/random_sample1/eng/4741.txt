<p><b>Lucija Zaninović</b> (born 26 June 1987 in <a href="/wiki/Split,_Croatia" title="Split, Croatia">Split</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatian</a> <a href="/wiki/Taekwondo" title="Taekwondo">taekwondo</a> practitioner. She is the twin sister of taekwondo practitioner <a href="/wiki/Ana_Zaninovi%C4%87" title="Ana Zaninović">Ana Zaninović</a>.</p>

<p>Zaninović won the gold medal in the women's flyweight class at the <a href="/wiki/2010_European_Taekwondo_Championships" title="2010 European Taekwondo Championships">2010 European Taekwondo Championships</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Zaninović qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> after winning the silver medal in the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Taekwondo_Olympic_Qualification_Tournament_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_49_kg" title="2011 World Taekwondo Olympic Qualification Tournament – Women's 49 kg">women's 49 kg</a> class at the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Taekwondo_Olympic_Qualification_Tournament" title="2011 World Taekwondo Olympic Qualification Tournament">2011 World Taekwondo Olympic Qualification Tournament</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Baku" title="Baku">Baku</a>. She withdrew in the final match against Olympic champion <a href="/wiki/Wu_Jingyu" title="Wu Jingyu">Wu Jingyu</a> and was the only European to qualify through the Qualification Tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2012, she defended the European title in the women's flyweight class after defeating Kristina Kim at the <a href="/wiki/2012_European_Taekwondo_Championships" title="2012 European Taekwondo Championships">2012 European Taekwondo Championships</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Taekwondo_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_49_kg" title="Taekwondo at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's 49 kg">2012 Summer Olympics</a>, she defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seulki_Kang" title="Seulki Kang">Seulki Kang</a> and Carola Malvina López before losing to Wu Jingyu in the semi-finals. She won the bronze medal after defeating <a href="/wiki/Jannet_Alegr%C3%ADa" title="Jannet Alegría">Jannet Alegría</a> in the sudden death round of the bronze medal match. This was the third medal for <a href="/wiki/Croatia_at_the_Olympics" title="Croatia at the Olympics">Croatia at the Olympics</a> in taekwondo, after <a href="/wiki/Martina_Zub%C4%8Di%C4%87" title="Martina Zubčić">Martina Zubčić</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sandra_%C5%A0ari%C4%87" title="Sandra Šarić">Sandra Šarić</a> who also won the bronze four years before in <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">Beijing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
