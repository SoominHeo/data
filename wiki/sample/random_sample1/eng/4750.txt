<p><b>Luis Fernando Garrido</b> (born 5 November 1990) is a Honduran <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played for <a href="/wiki/Houston_Dynamo" title="Houston Dynamo">Houston Dynamo</a>. Most often deployed as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>, he also plays for the <a href="/wiki/Honduras_national_football_team" title="Honduras national football team">Honduran national team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Garrido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juticalpa" title="Juticalpa">Juticalpa</a>, Garrido started playing for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juticalpa_FC" title="Juticalpa FC">Juticalpa FC</a> in the secondary division when he was 17 years old. By the end of 2007, after his performance in the <a href="/wiki/2007_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup" title="2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup">2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup</a> he signed a 7-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Club_Deportivo_Olimpia" title="Club Deportivo Olimpia">Olimpia</a>. He made his top-league debut in the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Honduran_Liga_Nacional" title="2007–08 Honduran Liga Nacional">2007–08 Honduran Liga Nacional</a>. He played 6 consecutive seasons with Olimpia winning 5 national titles. He spent part of 2011 playing on loan at <a href="/wiki/Deportes_Savio" title="Deportes Savio">Deportes Savio</a>.</p>
<p>On January 25, 2013, he agreed to a six-month loan at <a href="/wiki/Red_Star_Belgrade" title="Red Star Belgrade">Red Star Belgrade</a> for the spring <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Serbian_SuperLiga" title="2012–13 Serbian SuperLiga">2013</a> half-season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On February 1, 2013, he was promoted by Red Star Belgrade after he inked his contract for a 6-month loan, with an option to sign a subsequent contract in June 2013 to stay with the club permanently.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> However at the end of the season Red Star was unable to fulfill the financial demands of Olimpia, so his first experience in Europe came to an end, and subsequently Garrido returned to Olimpia. He played 10 matches in the <a href="/wiki/Serbian_SuperLiga" title="Serbian SuperLiga">Serbian SuperLiga</a> and Red Star finished the season runner-up.</p>
<p>On July 25, 2014, he agreed to a loan deal with <a href="/wiki/Houston_Dynamo" title="Houston Dynamo">Houston Dynamo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Garrido&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a youth, he was also a "sub" in a 2007 international game against the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and later that year played a game for the Honduran national under-17 team against the USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He played at the <a href="/wiki/2007_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup" title="2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup">2007 FIFA U-17 World Cup</a> and at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>.</p>
<p>He made his senior début for <a href="/wiki/Honduras_national_football_team" title="Honduras national football team">Honduras</a> in an October 2012 <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="FIFA World Cup qualification">FIFA World Cup qualification</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Panama_national_football_team" title="Panama national football team">Panama</a>. Ever since he became progressively a key player in the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="FIFA World Cup qualification">FIFA World Cup qualification</a> matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 November 2015, he sustained an horrific knee injury while playing for Honduras in a <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CONCACAF)" title="2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONCACAF)">FIFA World Cup qualification</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a>, resulting in the rupture of both <a href="/wiki/Posterior_cruciate_ligament" title="Posterior cruciate ligament">PCL</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament" title="Anterior cruciate ligament">ACL</a> ligaments of his right Knee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daily_Mail_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Daily_Mail-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
