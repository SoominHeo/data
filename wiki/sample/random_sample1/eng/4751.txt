<p><b>Luís Carlos Novo Neto</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[ˈlwiʒ ˈnɛtu]</a></span>; born 26 May 1988) is a Portuguese professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for Russian club <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">FC Zenit Saint Petersburg</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lu%C3%ADs_Neto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/P%C3%B3voa_de_Varzim" title="Póvoa de Varzim">Póvoa de Varzim</a>, Neto joined hometown <a href="/wiki/Varzim_S.C." title="Varzim S.C.">Varzim SC</a>'s youth ranks at the age of ten. With the club he played exclusively in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Segunda_Liga" title="Segunda Liga">second division</a>, being a backup or third-choice in four of his five seasons; his official debut came on 10 February 2007 in a 2–1 home win against <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a> for <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="2006–07 Taça de Portugal">the campaign</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="Taça de Portugal">Portuguese Cup</a> (two minutes played).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2011, Neto signed with <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">top level</a> side <a href="/wiki/C.D._Nacional" title="C.D. Nacional">C.D. Nacional</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Free_transfer_(association_football)" title="Free transfer (association football)">free transfer</a>. He immediately made an impact for his new team, attracting interest from the likes of <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> during the following off-season;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> however, Italy's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Siena" title="A.C. Siena">A.C. Siena</a> eventually won the bid to sign the player, for <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>1.7 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Neto was an undisputed starter during the first half of <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Serie_A" title="2012–13 Serie A">the season</a>, scoring his first goal in <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> in a 1–3 home defeat against <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">A.S. Roma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 1 February 2013, however, he moved teams and countries again, joining compatriots <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Alves" title="Bruno Alves">Bruno Alves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danny_(footballer)" title="Danny (footballer)">Danny</a> at <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">FC Zenit Saint Petersburg</a> in Russia. He scored his first goal for his new club on 19 May, netting his team's first in an eventual 3–1 home win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Volga_Nizhny_Novgorod" title="FC Volga Nizhny Novgorod">FC Volga Nizhny Novgorod</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lu%C3%ADs_Neto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Neto appeared with the <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_under-21_football_team" title="Portugal national under-21 football team">Portuguese under-21 team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Lusophony_Games" title="2009 Lusophony Games">2009 Lusophony Games</a>. He played all four matches during <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2009_Lusophony_Games" title="Football at the 2009 Lusophony Games">the tournament</a> as the side collected the <a href="/wiki/Silver_medal" title="Silver medal">silver medal</a>.</p>
<p>On 4 October 2012, Neto was called up for the first time to <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">the full team</a>, for <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_UEFA_Group_F" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group F">qualifiers</a> against <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national football team">Northern Ireland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He only made his debut on 6 February of the following year, however, partnering Zenit teammate Alves in a 2–3 <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly</a> loss with <a href="/wiki/Ecuador_national_football_team" title="Ecuador national football team">Ecuador</a> in <a href="/wiki/Guimar%C3%A3es" title="Guimarães">Guimarães</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 May 2014, Neto was named in the final 23-man squad for the tournament in Brazil,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> being an unused member in an eventual group stage exit.</p>
