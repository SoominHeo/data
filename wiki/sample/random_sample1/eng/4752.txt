<p><b>Luis Felipe Monti</b> (May 15, 1901 – September 9, 1983) was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Argentine" title="Italian Argentine">Italian Argentine</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> and an Olympian. Monti has the distinction of having played in two <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a> final matches with two different national teams. He played the first of these finals with his native <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> in <a href="/wiki/1930_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1930 FIFA World Cup">1930</a>, which was lost to <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a>; and the second with <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> as one of their <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oriundi" title="Oriundi">Oriundi</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/1934_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1934 FIFA World Cup">1934</a>, thanks to his <a href="/wiki/Romagna" title="Romagna">Romagnol</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This second time Monti was on the winning side in a 2–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia_national_football_team" title="Czechoslovakia national football team">Czechoslovakia</a>.</p>
<p>Monti was a rugged and ruthless player, but had the technical skills to go with his strong tackling. He played as an attacking <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre half</a> in the old-fashioned <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metodo" title="Metodo">Metodo</a></i> system: a position roughly equivalent to the <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">defensive central midfield</a> position of today. As such he would mark the opposing <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Centre_forward" title="Forward (association football)">centre forward</a> when his team were defending, but would be the main <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfield</a> <a href="/wiki/Playmaker" title="Playmaker">playmaker</a> when his team were on the attack, due to his passing and creativity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was nicknamed <i>doble ancho</i> (double wide) due to his coverage of the pitch.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Monti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Argentina">Argentina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Monti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Argentina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Luismonti_1925casla.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="757" data-file-width="540" height="280" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Luismonti_1925casla.jpeg/200px-Luismonti_1925casla.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Luismonti_1925casla.jpeg/300px-Luismonti_1925casla.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Luismonti_1925casla.jpeg/400px-Luismonti_1925casla.jpeg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Luismonti_1925casla.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Monti in 1925 while playing at <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo_de_Almagro" title="San Lorenzo de Almagro">San Lorenzo</a>.</div>

<p>Monti started his career in 1921 with <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Hurac%C3%A1n" title="Club Atlético Huracán">Huracán</a>, where he won the first of his many championships. The following year he signed with <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> but left without playing a game. He joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_San_Lorenzo_de_Almagro" title="Club Atlético San Lorenzo de Almagro">San Lorenzo</a> where he won a further three Argentine championships. All of Monti's honours in Argentina were recorded during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amateur_Era_in_Argentine_football" title="Amateur Era in Argentine football">Amateur Era</a>.</p>
<p>Monti was first called up to represent the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentine national team</a> in 1924. He won the <a href="/wiki/1927_South_American_Championship" title="1927 South American Championship">1927 South American Championship</a> and the Silver Medal at the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1928_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_team_squads#Mexico" title="Football at the 1928 Summer Olympics – Men's team squads">1928 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> With Monti as a key player, Argentina cruised to the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> final in <a href="/wiki/1930_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1930 FIFA World Cup">1930</a>, defeating <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States</a>. Monti scored two goals along the way, and injured opponents with his tackling. Some sources speculate that Monti was carrying an injury, but whatever the truth, and despite a death threat,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> he had a quiet game as <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a> triumphed 4–2.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Monti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1931 Monti was signed by the Italian club <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>, as he had Italian citizenship. As he was overweight and out of condition, he had a month's solitary training. Monti was back to top form helping Juventus to four consecutive <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> titles (1932 to 1935), also serving as the club's <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a>. Monti went on to play 225 matches and score 19 goals in Italy.</p>
<p>He was also called up, within a year, to play for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italian national team</a> as an <i><a href="/wiki/Oriundo" title="Oriundo">oriundo</a></i>. Hosts Italy won their way to the <a href="/wiki/1934_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1934 FIFA World Cup">1934 World Cup</a> final and defeated <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia_national_football_team" title="Czechoslovakia national football team">Czechoslovakia</a> 2–1.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Battle_of_Highbury">The Battle of Highbury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Monti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Battle of Highbury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Highbury" title="Battle of Highbury">Battle of Highbury</a> is a match that took place between Italy and <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> on November 14, 1934 at <a href="/wiki/Highbury" title="Highbury">Highbury</a>, the home ground of <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>. Monti was playing centre half for Italy, but as early as the second minute he broke a bone in his foot after a clash with England centre forward <a href="/wiki/Ted_Drake" title="Ted Drake">Ted Drake</a>. Down to 10 men, in the days before <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Substitute_(soccer)" title="Substitute (soccer)">substitutes</a>, Italy succumbed 2–3. Monti was only to play twice more for Italy.</p>
<p>In total Monti won 16 caps (5 goals) for Argentina between 1924 and 1931, and 18 caps (1 goal) for Italy between 1932 and 1936.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_football">After football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Monti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: After football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Monti became <a href="/wiki/Manager_(association_football)" title="Manager (association football)">manager</a> after retiring. He died in 1983 aged 82.</p>
