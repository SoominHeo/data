<p>The <b>Luis Aparicio Award</b> is given annually to a <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuelan</a> player in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) who is judged to have recorded the best individual performance in that year. The winner of the award is determined by a vote conducted by Venezuelan sports journalists and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish-language</a> media around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bigtime_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bigtime-1">[1]</a></sup> It is named after former MLB <a href="/wiki/Shortstop" title="Shortstop">shortstop</a> <a href="/wiki/Luis_Aparicio" title="Luis Aparicio">Luis Aparicio</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bigtime_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bigtime-1">[1]</a></sup> who is the only player from Venezuela to be inducted into the <a href="/wiki/National_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame_and_Museum" title="National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum">National Baseball Hall of Fame</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The award was first presented in 2004, and was created in order to honour Aparicio's major league career and to commemorate his father, who died thirteen years before his son was elected into the Hall of Fame.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sabr_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sabr-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Johan_Santana" title="Johan Santana">Johan Santana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Cabrera" title="Miguel Cabrera">Miguel Cabrera</a> are the only players to win the Luis Aparicio Award more than once, with the latter having won the award five times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Almanac_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Almanac-4">[4]</a></sup> Cabrera won the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Most_Valuable_Player_Award" title="Major League Baseball Most Valuable Player Award">MLB Most Valuable Player (MVP) Award</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hank_Aaron_Award" title="Hank Aaron Award">Hank Aaron Award</a> alongside the Luis Aparicio Award in 2012 and 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-winners_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-winners-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> becoming the first and—as of 2013—only Venezuelan to win the MLB MVP Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Santana, the 2004 and 2006 recipient, also won the <a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">Cy Young Award</a> in those two years,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-winners_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-winners-5">[5]</a></sup> winning by a unanimous vote on each occasion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Santana (2006) and Cabrera (2012) are the only award winners to also earn the pitching and batting <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_Triple_Crown" title="Major League Baseball Triple Crown">Triple Crown</a> respectively in the same season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In accomplishing the feat, Cabrera became the first player in 45 years to achieve a Triple Crown in batting since <a href="/wiki/Carl_Yastrzemski" title="Carl Yastrzemski">Carl Yastrzemski</a> in 1967,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> while Santana became the first pitcher since <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Gooden" title="Dwight Gooden">Dwight Gooden</a> in 1985 to secure a "Major League Triple Crown" by leading all of MLB in <a href="/wiki/Win%E2%80%93loss_record_(pitching)#Winning_pitcher" title="Win–loss record (pitching)">wins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">earned run average</a> and <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">strikeouts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(Venezuelan_pitcher)" title="Francisco Rodríguez (Venezuelan pitcher)">Francisco Rodríguez</a> compiled a major league record of 62 <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)#Most_in_a_single_season" title="Save (baseball)">saves in a single season</a> in 2008 and went on to win the <a href="/wiki/Rolaids_Relief_Man_Award" title="Rolaids Relief Man Award">Rolaids Relief Man Award</a> in the same year as the Luis Aparicio Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Three winners—Cabrera, <a href="/wiki/Magglio_Ord%C3%B3%C3%B1ez" title="Magglio Ordóñez">Magglio Ordóñez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Gonz%C3%A1lez_(baseball)" title="Carlos González (baseball)">Carlos González</a>—were <a href="/wiki/List_of_Major_League_Baseball_batting_champions" title="List of Major League Baseball batting champions">batting champions</a> in their respective leagues in the same year they won the award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The award is presented annually before a baseball game hosted by the local team, <a href="/wiki/%C3%81guilas_del_Zulia" title="Águilas del Zulia">Águilas del Zulia</a>, on November 18 in Aparicio's hometown of <a href="/wiki/Maracaibo" title="Maracaibo">Maracaibo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zulia" title="Zulia">Zulia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Almanac_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Almanac-4">[4]</a></sup> The date marks both the feast of the <a href="/wiki/Our_Lady_of_the_Rosary_of_Chiquinquir%C3%A1" title="Our Lady of the Rosary of Chiquinquirá">Virgin of Chiquinquirá</a>—the patron saint of Zulia<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>—and the anniversary of Aparicio's professional debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sabr_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-sabr-3">[3]</a></sup> As of 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Luis_Aparicio_Award&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the most recent recipient of the award is <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Altuve" title="José Altuve">José Altuve</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altuve2016_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-altuve2016-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
