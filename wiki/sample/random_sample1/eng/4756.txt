<div class="hatnote" role="note">This name uses <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>: the first or paternal <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">family name</a> is <i>Cubilla</i> and the second or maternal family name is <i>Almeida</i>.</div>
<p><b>Luis Alberto Cubilla Almeida</b> (28 March 1940 – 3 March 2013) was a <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguayan</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player and coach. He had a successful playing career winning 16 major titles. He then went on to become one of the most successful managers in South American football with 17 major titles.</p>

<p>Also known as "El Negro", Cubilla was born in <a href="/wiki/Paysand%C3%BA" title="Paysandú">Paysandú</a> and started his playing career in the youth team of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Col%C3%B3n_de_Paysandu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Colón de Paysandu (page does not exist)">Colón de Paysandu</a>. In 1957 he joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C.A._Pe%C3%B1arol" title="C.A. Peñarol">Peñarol</a> where he was part of the team that won four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n_Uruguaya" title="Primera División Uruguaya">Uruguayan league</a> championships, two <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a> and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copa_Intercontinental" title="Copa Intercontinental">Copa Intercontinental</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_highlights">Career highlights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Cubilla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career highlights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1962 he joined <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> of Spain, where he was part of the team that won the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> in 1963. He played 49 games and scored 12 goals with Barça.</p>
<p>Cubilla returned to South America in 1964 to play for <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a> of Argentina. In 1969 he returned to Uruguay joining <a href="/wiki/Club_Nacional_de_Football" title="Club Nacional de Football">Nacional</a> where he won 4 more Uruguayan league titles, another <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Copa_Interamericana" title="Copa Interamericana">Copa Interamericana</a> and another <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copa_Intercontinental" title="Copa Intercontinental">Copa Intercontinental</a>.</p>
<p>In the last years of his career he played for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_de_Deportes_Santiago_Morning" title="Club de Deportes Santiago Morning">Santiago Morning</a> of Chile and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defensor_Sporting_Club" title="Defensor Sporting Club">Defensor Sporting</a> of Uruguay where he helped the club to win their first league championship and break the complete dominance of the league by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C.A._Pe%C3%B1arol" title="C.A. Peñarol">Peñarol</a> and <a href="/wiki/Club_Nacional_de_Football" title="Club Nacional de Football">Nacional</a>.</p>
<p>Between 1959 and 1974 Cubilla played 38 games for the <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay national team</a> in which he scored 11 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He played in three <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cups</a> in 1962, 1970 and 1974.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_career">Managerial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luis_Cubilla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Managerial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a coach, Cubilla achieved enormous success with <a href="/wiki/Club_Olimpia" title="Club Olimpia">Olimpia Asunción</a> of Paraguay, winning 7 international titles and several national championships. He also coached Nacional, Peñarol, Defensor Sporting, <a href="/wiki/Danubio_F.C." title="Danubio F.C.">Danubio</a> (all of Uruguay), <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Nacional" title="Atlético Nacional">Atlético Nacional</a> of Colombia, <a href="/wiki/Newell%27s_Old_Boys" title="Newell's Old Boys">Newell's Old Boys</a> and River Plate of Argentina and <a href="/wiki/Cerro_Porte%C3%B1o" title="Cerro Porteño">Cerro Porteño</a> and <a href="/wiki/Club_Libertad" title="Club Libertad">Club Libertad</a>, both from Paraguay.</p>
<p>Between 1991–1993 Luis Cubilla was the head coach of the Uruguay National Team and worked together with his older brother <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Cubilla" title="Pedro Cubilla">Pedro Cubilla</a> as his assistant coach and Alejandro Riccino as the physical trainer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>During 1994 he coached the famous Argentinean club <a href="/wiki/Racing_Club_de_Avellaneda" title="Racing Club de Avellaneda">Racing Club de Avellaneda</a>.</p>
<p>In February 2007, Cubilla signed with the Ecuadorian team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona_de_Guayaquil" title="Barcelona de Guayaquil">Barcelona de Guayaquil</a>.</p>
<p>In 2010, he returned once again as a coach for <a href="/wiki/Club_Olimpia" title="Club Olimpia">Olimpia Asunción</a> of Paraguay. He died, aged 72, in <a href="/wiki/Asunci%C3%B3n" title="Asunción">Asunción</a>.</p>
