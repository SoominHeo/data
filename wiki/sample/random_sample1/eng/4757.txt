<p><b>Luiz Fernando Pereira da Silva</b> (born 25 November 1985), commonly known as <b>Fernandinho</b>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brazilian_people" title="Brazilian people">Brazilian</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">winger</a> for <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%AAmio_Foot-Ball_Porto_Alegrense" title="Grêmio Foot-Ball Porto Alegrense">Grêmio</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fernandinho_(footballer,_born_1985)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 28, 2010, he became the São Paulo's striker by assisting 4 goals on his debut, in only 45 minutes. At the age of 5 he was recognised as one of Brazil's future stars. Fernandinho, however, left brazilian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Futebol_Clube" title="São Paulo Futebol Clube">São Paulo</a> and transferred to <a href="/wiki/Al_Jazira_Club" title="Al Jazira Club">Al-Jazira</a>, from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE" title="UAE">UAE</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_real" title="Brazilian real">R$</a>11M.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his presentation at <a href="/wiki/Al_Jazira_Club" title="Al Jazira Club">Al-Jazira</a>, on July 26, 2012, Fernandinho signed a three-year contract and said that believes in a good partnership along with his friend <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Oliveira" title="Ricardo Oliveira">Ricardo Oliveira</a>. It was confirmed that he will wear the nº12 shirt, previously worn by Sami Rubaiya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 7, 2013, Fernandinho was contracted to <a href="/wiki/Clube_Atl%C3%A9tico_Mineiro" title="Clube Atlético Mineiro">Atlético Mineiro</a> and, consequently, came back to Brazilian football. He signed a one-year contract and is one of main players to <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_Club_World_Cup" title="2013 FIFA Club World Cup">2013 FIFA Club World Cup</a> for <a href="/wiki/Clube_Atl%C3%A9tico_Mineiro" title="Clube Atlético Mineiro"><i>Galo</i></a> after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bernard_An%C3%ADcio_Caldeira_Duarte" title="Bernard Anício Caldeira Duarte">Bernard</a>'s selling to Ukrainian football. As Fernandinho is linked to <a href="/wiki/Desportivo_Brasil" title="Desportivo Brasil">Desportivo Brasil</a>, his contraction, even after closing of international window, could be considered a national one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 July 2014, Fernandinho was transferred from Al-Jazira by €2 million to <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%AAmio_Foot-Ball_Porto_Alegrense" title="Grêmio Foot-Ball Porto Alegrense">Grêmio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
