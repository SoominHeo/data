<p><b>Louise Bond-Williams</b> (born 14 May 1982) is a British <a href="/wiki/Sabre" title="Sabre">sabre</a> <a href="/wiki/Fencing" title="Fencing">fencer</a>. She represented Great Britain at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004</a> and <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louise_Bond-Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bond-Williams took up fencing at <a href="/wiki/Chipping_Campden_School" title="Chipping Campden School">Chipping Campden School</a>. She quickly showed promise, winning the West Midlands championships for her age class when she was twelve. She was then coached by David Kirby at Shakespeare's Swords, the fencing club at <a href="/wiki/King_Edward_VI_School,_Stratford-upon-Avon" title="King Edward VI School, Stratford-upon-Avon">King Edward VI School, Stratford-upon-Avon</a>.</p>
<p>In 1999, first year where women's sabre was allowed at top-level competitions, she earned a silver medal at the Cadet World Championships in Dijon and posted a last-16 finish at the <a href="/wiki/1999_World_Fencing_Championships" title="1999 World Fencing Championships">Senior World Championships</a> in Seoul. The following year she won a silver medal at Budapest and a gold medal at Havana in the Junior World Cup circuit. She went on to take a silver medal at the 2002 Junior World Championships in Antalya and reached the quarter-finals at the <a href="/wiki/2002_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2002 World Fencing Championships">senior championships</a> in Lisbon.</p>
<p>At a World Cup event in Spain Bond-Williams was noticed by <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University" title="Ohio State University">Ohio State</a> coach <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Nazlymov" title="Vladimir Nazlymov">Vladimir Nazlymov</a> who offered to train her in the United States and arranged for an athletic scholarship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She accepted the offer as the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Birmingham" title="University of Birmingham">University of Birmingham</a>, where she had been studying for a year, did not offer her the facilities to train properly at her level. She fenced for the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_Buckeyes" title="Ohio State Buckeyes">Ohio State Buckeyes</a> from 2002 to 2004, taking All-America honours each season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OSB_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-OSB-2">[2]</a></sup> Ohio State would induct her into their Athletics Hall of Fame in 2012.</p>
<p>She made her Olympic début at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a>, the first Games allowing women's sabre, after reaching the final in the European qualifying event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In the first round of the <a href="/wiki/Fencing_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_sabre" title="Fencing at the 2004 Summer Olympics – Women's sabre">individual event</a> she saw off Germany's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Susanne_Koenig" title="Susanne Koenig">Susanne Koenig</a>, but lost afterwards to Russia's <a href="/wiki/Yelena_Nechayeva" title="Yelena Nechayeva">Yelena Nechayeva</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sports-reference_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sports-reference-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the Games she resumed her history degree at Ohio State while serving as undergraduate assistant coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OSB_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-OSB-2">[2]</a></sup> She posted two quarter-finals finishes in the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2004–05 Fencing World Cup">2004–05 season</a> and finished World No.22, a career best. She climbed her first senior <a href="/wiki/Fencing_World_Cup" title="Fencing World Cup">World Cup</a> podium with a bronze medal at Orléans in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She missed out however for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>. She was severely injured in a car crash in late 2008 and sustained several operations which left her out for a full season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>She <a href="/wiki/Fencing_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Qualification" title="Fencing at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Qualification">qualified</a> to the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> through host option, being the British top-ranked female sabreur at the time. She lost in the first round to the World No.4, Greece's <a href="/wiki/Vassiliki_Vougiouka" title="Vassiliki Vougiouka">Vassiliki Vougiouka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She retired after the Games to manage the Truro Fencing Club.</p>
