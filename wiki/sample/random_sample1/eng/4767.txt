<p><i><b>The Losers</b></i> is a 2010 American <a href="/wiki/Action_film#Subgenres" title="Action film">action comedy film</a> based on the adaptation of the <a href="/wiki/Vertigo_(DC_Comics)" title="Vertigo (DC Comics)">Vertigo</a> comic book <a href="/wiki/The_Losers_(Vertigo)" title="The Losers (Vertigo)">series of the same name</a> by Andy Diggle and Jock. Directed by <a href="/wiki/Sylvain_White" title="Sylvain White">Sylvain White</a>, the film features an <a href="/wiki/Ensemble_cast" title="Ensemble cast">ensemble cast</a> that includes <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Dean_Morgan" title="Jeffrey Dean Morgan">Jeffrey Dean Morgan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zoe_Saldana" title="Zoe Saldana">Zoe Saldana</a>.</p>
<p>The film received mixed reviews from critics and drew comparisons to <i><a href="/wiki/The_A-Team_(film)" title="The A-Team (film)">The A-Team</a></i>, a remake of which was released shortly after <i>The Losers</i> premiered.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Losers_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Losers are an elite <a href="/wiki/Black_operation" title="Black operation">black ops</a> team of <a href="/wiki/Special_Forces_(United_States_Army)" title="Special Forces (United States Army)">United States Special Forces</a> operatives, led by Clay (<a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Dean_Morgan" title="Jeffrey Dean Morgan">Jeffrey Dean Morgan</a>) and formed by Roque (<a href="/wiki/Idris_Elba" title="Idris Elba">Idris Elba</a>), Pooch (<a href="/wiki/Columbus_Short" title="Columbus Short">Columbus Short</a>), Jensen (<a href="/wiki/Chris_Evans_(actor)" title="Chris Evans (actor)">Chris Evans</a>), and Cougar (<a href="/wiki/%C3%93scar_Jaenada" title="Óscar Jaenada">Óscar Jaenada</a>), who are sent to <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Search_and_destroy" title="Search and destroy">search and destroy</a> mission on a compound run by a drug lord. While <a href="/wiki/Laser_designator" title="Laser designator">painting a target</a> for an upcoming air strike, the Losers spot slave children in the compound and try to call off the attack, but their superior, codenamed "Max" (<a href="/wiki/Jason_Patric" title="Jason Patric">Jason Patric</a>), ignores their pleas.</p>
<p>With no other option, the Losers enter the compound, successfully rescue the children and kill the drug lord in the process. As a helicopter arrives to pick them up, Max, convinced that they know too much, orders it to be destroyed, unaware that they decided to rescue the children first. The Losers watch as a missile destroys the helicopter and kills 25 innocents. Knowing that the attack was meant to kill them, they fake their deaths and become stranded in Bolivia, determined to get revenge on the mysterious Max.</p>
<p>Four months later, Clay is approached by Aisha (<a href="/wiki/Zoe_Saldana" title="Zoe Saldana">Zoe Saldana</a>), a mysterious woman who offers him the chance to kill Max, against whom she wants revenge. Clay accepts and Aisha arranges for the Losers to return to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, where they proceed to attack a convoy supposedly carrying Max, only to discover that they were tricked by Aisha into stealing a hard drive with Max's secrets.</p>
<p>Unable to access the files, Jensen infiltrates the company that made the drive and steals an algorithm that allows him to crack the code, discovering that the drive contains credits for a $400 million transfer in Max's name, which he received for selling "Snukes"—eco-friendly bombs with the potency of a nuclear warhead, but no fall-out—to international terrorists. Tracing the money flow to the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Los_Angeles" title="Port of Los Angeles">International Port Of Entry</a>, which the Losers deduce is Max's base, a plan is formed to attack it and kill Max.</p>
<p>While studying the drive, Jensen discovers that their mission in Bolivia was a cover so Max could eliminate the drug lord—who had discovered his plan—and that Aisha is the man's daughter, seeking revenge for his death. After her cover is blown, Aisha shoots Jensen and escapes. Believing that she might betray them, the Losers decide to speed up their attack on Max's base, only to be betrayed by Roque and captured by Max and his right-hand man and chief of security, Wade (<a href="/wiki/Holt_McCallany" title="Holt McCallany">Holt McCallany</a>).</p>
<p>As the Losers are lined up to be executed, Aisha returns and ambushes Max's team. In the ensuing fight, Clay confirms that he killed Aisha's father. Roque attempts to steal Max's plane, loaded with his money, and tries to escape. As Roque's jet heads down the runway, Wade takes a motorcycle and goes after him to retrieve Max's money. Cougar shoots the motorcycle's engine, causing Wade to be hurled into the jet's engine and the flaming motorcycle to be hurled into the cockpit of the plane, which explodes, killing Roque.</p>
<p>As Jensen, Cougar and Aisha help Pooch, who has been shot in both legs by one of Max's security guards, Clay pursues Max to a crane, where Max says that he has activated a Snuke that will destroy Los Angeles, and Clay will have to choose between de-activating it or killing Max. Clay chooses the former and Max escapes, but Clay affirms that he now knows what Max looks like and will soon find him.</p>
<p>Max escapes on a bus and is robbed by two thugs. His fate after this is unknown.</p>
<p>Shortly thereafter, the Losers help Pooch reach the hospital where his pregnant wife is giving birth to their son and attend Jensen's 8-year-old niece's soccer game.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Losers_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Losers_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tim_Story" title="Tim Story">Tim Story</a> was initially announced as the film's director in 2007. <i>The Losers</i> was thought to be a notable departure from Story's previous work, as he spent considerable time directing the American superhero films <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four_(2005_film)" title="Fantastic Four (2005 film)">Fantastic Four</a></i> (2005) and <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four:_Rise_of_the_Silver_Surfer" title="Fantastic Four: Rise of the Silver Surfer">Fantastic Four: Rise of the Silver Surfer</a></i> (2007).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-variety-8">[8]</a></sup> An ardent fan of the <a href="/wiki/War_film" title="War film">war film</a> genre, Story sought influences from the war drama <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Hawk_Down_(film)" title="Black Hawk Down (film)">Black Hawk Down</a></i> (2001) and the television miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Band_of_Brothers_(miniseries)" title="Band of Brothers (miniseries)">Band of Brothers</a></i> (2001). "I was looking for a vehicle that would have that edge," he affirmed, "but I didn't want to lose my personality, which is a bit tongue-in-cheek, where the characters have a little fun with each other."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-variety-8">[8]</a></sup> Coinciding with Story's arrival, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Berg" title="Peter Berg">Peter Berg</a> was declared as a producer for <i>The Losers</i>. Berg was originally responsible for conceiving the film's script—ultimately having intentions to direct the film—but later concluded his endeavors in lieu of creating <i><a href="/wiki/Hancock_(film)" title="Hancock (film)">Hancock</a></i> (2008).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-variety-8">[8]</a></sup> Alongside Akiva Goldsman and Kerry Foster, Berg collaborated with longtime partners Sarah Aubrey and John Cameron.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-variety_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-variety-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sylvain_White" title="Sylvain White">Sylvain White</a> replaced Story as director in October 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Prior to the revelation, White was developing an adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Ronin_(DC_Comics)" title="Ronin (DC Comics)">Ronin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-collider3_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-collider3-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>A native of <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, White asserted that his fascination of <i>The Losers</i> became evident after drawing parallels to the graphic novels he read as a child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV-SplashPage_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-SplashPage-11">[11]</a></sup> Unlike the majority of superhero films, White appreciated the realistic nature of the installment. "What appealed to me about <i>The Losers</i> was that it wasn't the typical superhero-with-superpowers thing. It was based on real characters—realistic characters—and based in reality, like a lot of the European graphic novels that I had grown up reading."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV-SplashPage_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-SplashPage-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jock_(cartoonist)" title="Jock (cartoonist)">Jock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andy_Diggle" title="Andy Diggle">Andy Diggle</a>, the creators of the <a href="/wiki/The_Losers_(Vertigo)" title="The Losers (Vertigo)">Vertigo comics of the same name</a>, worked closely with White creating for <i>The Losers</i>; the former in particular was sought after by White for help. "From the beginning, even when I was working on the screenplay, I had a really tight dialogue with them — even in trying to visualize some of the scenes. I would talk to Jock often, because he's obviously got an amazing eye, and they came down during production. I'm actually talking to them almost every day right now, talking to Jock in terms of design. We included some of his design work into the design of the movie. I'm not going to give it away, but it's certain little design elements that we use to introduce the characters or transition from one location to the next."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV-SplashPage_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-SplashPage-11">[11]</a></sup> The film adapts the first two volumes of the comic book, "Ante Up"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and "Double Down"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and tells the story in a more linear way than in the comic books. Elements of the story have been left out instead of trying to squeeze the whole story into one film and the director would like to tell the rest of the story if the film does well at the box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV-SplashPage2_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-SplashPage2-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2009, it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Dean_Morgan" title="Jeffrey Dean Morgan">Jeffrey Dean Morgan</a> would headline the upcoming adaptation playing Clay. In March 2009, it was confirmed that <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Short" title="Columbus Short">Columbus Short</a> will play Pooch, <a href="/wiki/Idris_Elba" title="Idris Elba">Idris Elba</a> will play Roque and <a href="/wiki/Zoe_Saldana" title="Zoe Saldana">Zoe Saldana</a> will play Aisha, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Evans_(actor)" title="Chris Evans (actor)">Chris Evans</a> playing Jensen, and <a href="/wiki/%C3%93scar_Jaenada" title="Óscar Jaenada">Óscar Jaenada</a> playing Cougar. In August 2009, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Jason_Patric" title="Jason Patric">Jason Patric</a> will play Max.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The release date for <i>The Losers</i> was changed on two occasions; it was originally delayed to June 2010—as it roughly coincided with the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Clash_of_the_Titans_(2010_film)" title="Clash of the Titans (2010 film)">Clash of the Titans</a></i> (2010)—ultimately reverting to an April release. This occurrence was partially attributed to investment purposes as well as to accommodate reshoots for the film—although reshoots were not needed for the film. "So I think the marketing department decided, 'Let's maybe move to the June date,' but then the April date opened up again and they went back to it. I'm not quite sure how the science of these things work, that's not really my department and I trust the marketing at Warner Bros. to make the best decision for the movie."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV-SplashPage_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-SplashPage-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Losers_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arecibo_Observatory_Aerial_View.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <a href="/wiki/Arecibo_Observatory" title="Arecibo Observatory">Arecibo Observatory</a> in Puerto Rico; its surroundings depicted Bolivian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainforests" title="Rainforests">rainforests</a> in the film.</div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> commenced over a period of four months from July to October 2009. Despite the diverse array of settings featured in the film—which included cities such as <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>—most of principal photography transpired in <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-film_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-film-16">[16]</a></sup> White found its infrastructure and locales to be ideal and convenient, giving them the ability to "cheat" because they had "a variety of terrains within a relatively small distance".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-film_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-film-16">[16]</a></sup> "It was staggering what we found when we came to Puerto Rico to scout," recalled Foster. "There is a city, obviously gorgeous beaches, a close approximation of a desert, and a rainforest that was ideal to double for Bolivia."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-film_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-film-16">[16]</a></sup> Filming locations in the area include the <a href="/wiki/Arecibo_Observatory" title="Arecibo Observatory">Arecibo Observatory</a>, which <a href="/wiki//Film" title="/Film">/Film</a>'s Peter Scrietta felt that producers utilized the tropical environment to their advantage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-film_16-3"><a href="#cite_note-film-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Color was strongly emphasized in crafting the visuals of the film. Production designer Aaron Osbourne attempted to enhance the color scheme of the comics, which he noticed that only two to three colors were used to convey the tone of each setting. "Every chapter of the comic books is a new environment distinguished by different color palettes," explained White. "I really wanted to do that in the movie as well–where every time we are in a new city or country, the aesthetic of the film changes completely, so there is no homogeneous look. It’s very eclectic."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-film_16-4"><a href="#cite_note-film-16">[16]</a></sup> This meticulous approach was echoed by cinematographer Scott Kevan, who White collaborated with in breaking down the images of various scenes. "We charted the whole movie with certain color schemes for each location and different shades as we moved from day to night."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-film_16-5"><a href="#cite_note-film-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Losers_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Promotional artwork for the film was released at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">Comic Con</a>, the poster was drawn in the style of the comic book by series artist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jock_(comics)" title="Jock (comics)">Jock</a>, and was later recreated photographically with the cast from the film and used as the theatrical release poster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>A four-minute preview of the film was shown at <a href="/wiki/WonderCon" title="WonderCon">WonderCon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>A special "double volume" <a href="/wiki/The_Losers_(Vertigo)#Collected_editions" title="The Losers (Vertigo)">collected edition</a> graphic novel was released to tie in with the film adaptation collecting including the volumes <i>Ante Up</i> and <i>Double Down</i>. A second book to collect the rest of the series was also released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Losers_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In June 2009, Warner Bros. set a tentative release date of April 9, 2010 for the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The release date was then pushed back to June 4, 2010, to avoid going up against <i><a href="/wiki/Clash_of_the_Titans_(2010_film)" title="Clash of the Titans (2010 film)">Clash of the Titans</a></i> also from Warner Bros.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The trailer for the film was released online January 29, 2010, and was shown in theaters with <i><a href="/wiki/Edge_of_Darkness_(2010_film)" title="Edge of Darkness (2010 film)">Edge of Darkness</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> An official photo for the film was released online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The release date was subsequently moved up to April 23, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Losers_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Losers_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span id="Critics"></span> The film received mixed reviews. <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gives the film an approval rating of 49% based on 161 reviews, with the site's consensus stating "<i>The Losers</i> is loud, fast, and unrelentingly violent -- but it's also funny and well-acted, which will make all the difference for some action fans."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Review aggregation website <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> assigned the film a weighted average score of 44/100 based on 32 reviews from selected critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Peter Debruge of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> criticized the film as "the sort of pyro-heavy exercise parodied in <i><a href="/wiki/Tropic_Thunder" title="Tropic Thunder">Tropic Thunder</a></i>". He notes that casting against type helps make the team more memorable but complains that despite the polished production, the film offers only a hollow "junk-food high."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> John Anderson describes the film as a good idea pushed to excess, and with all "the freshness of last week's salad bar."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Scott Tobias of <a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a> complains about the lack of "humility or self-deprecation" in the heroes despite their title. He notes how the film tries so strenuously to be cool and describes the film as nothing more than "style for its own sake".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AVClub_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-AVClub-30">[30]</a></sup> Kyle Smith of the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i> lambasts the film, giving it half a star out of 4. He describes Zoe Saldana as a "<i><a href="/wiki/Femme_fatale" title="Femme fatale">femme banale</a></i>", saying actors Jeffrey Dean Morgan and Idris Elba deserved better. He describes the film as one that "tries to do so little, and yet falls so short."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Phelim O'Neill of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> gave the film 3 stars out of 5. He notes similarities to <i><a href="/wiki/The_A-Team_(film)" title="The A-Team (film)">The A-Team</a></i> and criticizes the film for being full of action movie cliches. He praises the film for the lighter comedic touches, and overall describes it as "big dumb fun".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> His colleague Philip French of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Observer" title="The Observer">The Observer</a></i> described the film as being in "<i>A-Team</i> territory" with the action sequences being well enough put together but that "it was all done far better in Walter Hill's <i><a href="/wiki/Extreme_Prejudice_(film)" title="Extreme Prejudice (film)">Extreme Prejudice</a></i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Observer_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Observer-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Director Quentin Tarantino offered a positive reaction to the film, calling it "dumb fun".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Losers_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film played in 2,936 theaters and earned $9,406,348 on its opening weekend at the box office at #4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mojo-Weekend_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mojo-Weekend-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-4">[4]</a></sup> The film went on to earn $23.5 million in the United States and more than $5 million internationally for a worldwide total of over $29 million. Added to this, it made over $8.2 million on DVD sales in the USA alone, giving it a minimum earning of $37.6 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mojo_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Mojo-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
