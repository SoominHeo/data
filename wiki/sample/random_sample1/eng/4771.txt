<p><b>Luca Antonelli</b> (born 11 February 1987) is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">left back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Antonelli was born in <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>. He is the son of <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Antonelli" title="Roberto Antonelli">Roberto Antonelli</a>, a former footballer who played for <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> from 1977 until 1982 as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After spending a few seasons in the <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan_Primavera" title="A.C. Milan Primavera">A.C. Milan youth teams</a>, Antonelli made his first team debut in a <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> game against <a href="/wiki/Brescia_Calcio" title="Brescia Calcio">Brescia</a> on 8 November 2006. On 23 December, he made also his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut, in a match against <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a>.</p>
<p>In the summer of 2007 Antonelli was loaned out to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S._Bari" title="A.S. Bari">Bari</a>, but he impressed so much that during the January transfer window <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma</a> secured him on loan from <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> for the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> season. He made his first appearance for the <i>Gialloblu</i> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Livorno_Calcio" title="Livorno Calcio">Livorno</a> on 16 March 2008.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parma">Parma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Parma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2008, Parma had acquired half of the contractual rights from Milan, for €750,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Milan2009bilancio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Milan2009bilancio-2">[2]</a></sup> While the following summer, he was fully transferred for another €2.5 million, which Milan made a financial income of €1.75 million as the value of the retained half had increased from €0.75 million to €2.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Milan2009bilancio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Milan2009bilancio-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genoa">Genoa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Genoa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2011, Antonelli was transferred to Genoa for €7 million. Genoa also signed <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Paloschi" title="Alberto Paloschi">Alberto Paloschi</a> for €4.35 million and sold <a href="/wiki/Raffaele_Palladino" title="Raffaele Palladino">Raffaele Palladino</a> (€3M) and <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Modesto" title="Francesco Modesto">Francesco Modesto</a> (€2.5M) to Parma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The club soon sold <a href="/wiki/Domenico_Criscito" title="Domenico Criscito">Domenico Criscito</a> as Antonelli had succeeded Criscito as full back.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_A.C._Milan">Return to A.C. Milan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Return to A.C. Milan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2015, Antonelli moved back to his debut club <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a>, for a fee of €4.5 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> signing a three-and-a-half-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On his debut for his return to <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> on 7 February 2015, Antonelli scored a goal from a corner kick off a header to tie the match with <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> at 1–1, which did not prove decisive, as Milan eventually lost the match 3–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
