<p><b>Luca Antonini</b> (born 4 August 1982) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_people" title="Italian people">Italian</a> former <a href="/wiki/Professional_sports#Association_football" title="Professional sports">professional</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who last played for Italian club <a href="/wiki/A.C._Prato" title="A.C. Prato">Prato</a>. He was a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">full-back</a> who is equally proficient on either flank.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Antonini started his career at <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a>, originally as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">winger</a>, playing for 11 years in their <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan_Primavera" title="A.C. Milan Primavera">youth teams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interview_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Interview-1">[1]</a></sup> At the start of the 2001–02 season, he was <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loaned</a> out to <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Seconda_Divisione" title="Lega Pro Seconda Divisione">Serie C2</a> side <a href="/wiki/A.C._Prato" title="A.C. Prato">Prato</a>, where he made his professional debut, totalling 26 appearances and scoring 3 <a href="/wiki/Goal_(sport)" title="Goal (sport)">goals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-2">[2]</a></sup> The following year, he made 17 appearances and scored one goal in his first <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> season, during a loan spell at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Ancona" title="A.C. Ancona">Ancona</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-2">[2]</a></sup> The team got promoted to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> and Antonini's contribution was significant, as he became a regular in the second half of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sampdoria_.282003.E2.80.932004.29">Sampdoria (2003–2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sampdoria (2003–2004)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2003 he moved to <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Co-ownership_(football)" title="Co-ownership (football)">co-ownership</a> deal with the <i>Rossoneri</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> for <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>2 million. In exchange, <a href="/wiki/Ikechukwu_Kalu" title="Ikechukwu Kalu">Ikechukwu Kalu</a> moved to Milan in co-ownership deal for €1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He did not get much playing time, as he served mainly as a backup winger for <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Gasbarroni" title="Andrea Gasbarroni">Gasbarroni</a>. However, he made his Serie A debut on 14 December, in an away match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perugia_Calcio" title="Perugia Calcio">Perugia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Various_loan_spells_.282004.E2.80.932007.29">Various loan spells (2004–2007)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Various loan spells (2004–2007)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The following season he returned to play in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>, being first loaned out to <a href="/wiki/Modena_F.C." title="Modena F.C.">Modena</a> and then switching, during the January <a href="/wiki/Transfer_window" title="Transfer window">transfer window</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Delfino_Pescara_1936" title="Delfino Pescara 1936">Pescara</a>. Still seeking for more playing time, for the 2005–06 season he moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Arezzo" title="A.C. Arezzo">Arezzo</a> on another loan spell, where he finally managed to play regularly throughout the whole season, making 39 appearances and scoring 3 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the beginning of the 2006–07 season, the then 24-year-old midfielder was sent to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Siena" title="A.C. Siena">Siena</a> on a fourth consecutive loan spell, and faced his second <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> season, which proved much more successful than the first one. He made 32 appearances and scored 3 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Profile-2">[2]</a></sup> In particular, his very first goal in the top-tier came on 26 November, in a home game against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C.F._Fiorentina" title="A.C.F. Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Empoli_.282007.E2.80.932008.29">Empoli (2007–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Empoli (2007–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to the start of the 2007–08 season, Milan reclaimed Antonini for €1 million and sent him to <a href="/wiki/Empoli_F.C." title="Empoli F.C.">Empoli</a> also for €1 million, along with his future teammate <a href="/wiki/Ignazio_Abate" title="Ignazio Abate">Ignazio Abate</a> (the 50<a href="/wiki/Percentage" title="Percentage">%</a> valued €0.9 million), in a new <a href="/wiki/Co-ownership_(football)" title="Co-ownership (football)">co-ownership</a> deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> With Empoli, Antonini made his debut in <a href="/wiki/UEFA#Competitions" title="UEFA">European competitions</a> on 20 September 2007, in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> first-round game against <a href="/wiki/FC_Z%C3%BCrich" title="FC Zürich">Zürich</a>, also scoring a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Despite the team being relegated, he had an extremely good season, also showing versatility when coach <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Malesani" title="Alberto Malesani">Alberto Malesani</a> started to employ him as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full_back" title="Defender (association football)">full-back</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interview_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Interview-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Milan_.282008.E2.80.932013.29">Return to Milan (2008–2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to Milan (2008–2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 9 June 2008, Antonini was brought back by Milan and included in their squad<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> for €2.75 million plus €150,000 other fee, in a pure player swap. (Antonini and Abate to Milan for a total fee of €4.75 million, <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Pozzi" title="Nicola Pozzi">Pozzi</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lino_Marzoratti" title="Lino Marzoratti">Marzoratti</a> to Empoli for a total fee of €4.75 million)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p>In his first season with the <i>Rossoneri</i>, he did not get much playing time though, making only 11 league appearances. For the 2009–10 campaign, Milan signed new head coach <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Ara%C3%BAjo" title="Leonardo Araújo">Leonardo</a>, who initially did not seem to take Antonini into much consideration. However, as the season progressed, <a href="/wiki/Sports_injury" title="Sports injury">injuries</a> to first-string players such as <a href="/wiki/Gianluca_Zambrotta" title="Gianluca Zambrotta">Zambrotta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marek_Jankulovski" title="Marek Jankulovski">Jankulovski</a> prompted the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> to put him in the line-up. Antonini seized his chance and earned Leonardo's trust by constantly performing well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>His valuable performances also persuaded the club to offer him a <a href="/wiki/Association_football_contracts" title="Association football contracts">contract</a> extension until 2014, which was signed on 11 February 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He finished his second year at Milan on a high, scoring his first goal for the <i>Rossoneri</i> in the last match of the season, a home game against <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>, on 15 May 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>When new coach Massimiliano Allegri was appointed in the summer of 2010, he kept Antonini at the left back position and Luca was a regular in the starting XI for the whole of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Serie_A" title="2010–11 Serie A">2010–11</a> season, helping Milan win their 18th Scudetto. He continued to be a regular for the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_A" title="2011–12 Serie A">2011–12</a> season, where Milan disappointingly finished second to Juventus, but managed to win the <a href="/wiki/Supercoppa_Italiana" title="Supercoppa Italiana">Supercoppa Italiana</a> in the summer of 2011.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genoa_.282013.E2.80.932015.29">Genoa (2013–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Genoa (2013–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Serie_A" title="2013–14 Serie A">2013–14 season</a>, Antonini left Milan to join <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a> as part of a deal which saw midfielder <a href="/wiki/Valter_Birsa" title="Valter Birsa">Valter Birsa</a> move the other way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He made 26 appearances in Serie A, scoring two goals, in his first season with the Grifone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The following season, he made just seven appearances and was then released by the club.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ascoli_and_Livorno_loan_.282015.E2.80.932016.29">Ascoli and Livorno loan (2015–2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ascoli and Livorno loan (2015–2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Antonini joined <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> side <a href="/wiki/Ascoli_Picchio_F.C._1898" title="Ascoli Picchio F.C. 1898">Ascoli</a> on 4 September 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On 1 February 2016, he moved to <a href="/wiki/A.S._Livorno_Calcio" title="A.S. Livorno Calcio">Livorno</a> on a half-season loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Prato_.282016.E2.80.93present.29">Return to Prato (2016–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Return to Prato (2016–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 July 2016, Antonini signed with <a href="/wiki/A.C._Prato" title="A.C. Prato">Prato</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro" title="Lega Pro">Lega Pro</a>, the team with which he originally made his professional debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 23 November 2016, Prato has announced that Antonini's contract was mutually terminated following his willingness to end his playing career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luca_Antonini_announces_retirement_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Luca_Antonini_announces_retirement-25">[25]</a></sup> As revealed in the press release,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Antonini will be appointed as a member of staff in the youth sector of the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luca_Antonini_announces_retirement_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-Luca_Antonini_announces_retirement-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Antonini made up a total of 5 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> with <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-17_football_team" title="Italy national under-17 football team">Italy U-17</a> and U-18, between 1999 and 2001. However, he did not receive any further call-up at higher levels of youth international football.</p>
<p>Nine years later, Antonini got his first call-up for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">senior team</a>; newly appointed coach <a href="/wiki/Cesare_Prandelli" title="Cesare Prandelli">Cesare Prandelli</a> named him in the squad for a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Ivoire_national_football_team" title="Côte d'Ivoire national football team">Côte d'Ivoire</a>, to be played on 10 August 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> However, he was left as an unused <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luca_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Antonini is a versatile footballer who has been employed either as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">winger</a> or as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">full-back</a>, playing both on the left or the right side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Right_Spirit_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Right_Spirit-29">[29]</a></sup> His speed and stamina also allows him to attack and fall back to his position with ease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Right_Spirit_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Right_Spirit-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
