<p><b>Lukas Graham</b> is a Danish pop and <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a> band. It currently consists of lead vocalist <a href="/wiki/Lukas_Forchhammer" title="Lukas Forchhammer">Lukas Forchhammer</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Polit01_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Polit01-1">[1]</a></sup> drummer Mark Falgren, bassist Magnus Larsson, and keyboardist <a href="/wiki/Morten_Ristorp" title="Morten Ristorp">Morten Ristorp</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB01_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-EB01-2">[2]</a></sup> The band released their first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Lukas_Graham_(2012_album)" title="Lukas Graham (2012 album)">Lukas Graham</a></i>, with labels <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen_Records" title="Copenhagen Records">Copenhagen Records</a> and Then We Take the World in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GAFFA_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-GAFFA-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB02_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-EB02-4">[4]</a></sup> The album peaked at number one on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tracklisten" title="Tracklisten">Danish charts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Danish_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Danish-5">[5]</a></sup> Their <a href="/wiki/Lukas_Graham_(Blue_Album)" title="Lukas Graham (Blue Album)">second album</a> was released in 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT01_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT01-6">[6]</a></sup> and earned international attention with singles like "<a href="/wiki/Mama_Said_(Lukas_Graham_song)" title="Mama Said (Lukas Graham song)">Mama Said</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/7_Years_(Lukas_Graham_song)" title="7 Years (Lukas Graham song)">7 Years</a>", the latter of which peaked at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> list (among other countries' charts).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Globe01_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Globe01-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard100_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard100-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBCCharts_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBCCharts-9">[9]</a></sup> The self-titled global debut album was officially released in the United States by <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a> on 1 April 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-USA-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gum_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gum-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312:_Career_beginnings">2011–12: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lukas_Graham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2011–12: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band formed in Denmark in 2011 and initially uploaded homemade videos of the songs "<a href="/wiki/Drunk_in_the_Morning" title="Drunk in the Morning">Drunk in the Morning</a>" and "Criminal Mind".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB01_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-EB01-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Polit02_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Polit02-12">[12]</a></sup> The songs were also shared on Facebook and accrued several hundred thousand views.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GAFFA2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-GAFFA2-13">[13]</a></sup> The band earned a contract with Copenhagen Records in late 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GAFFA_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-GAFFA-3">[3]</a></sup> Prior to officially releasing any music, the band had sold 17,000 tickets for an upcoming tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Polit02_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Polit02-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Their first release, available only in Europe and select other nations,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Polit01_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Polit01-1">[1]</a></sup> would go on to become certified five-times platinum in Denmark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Platinum_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Platinum-14">[14]</a></sup> It also featured four charting songs in "Drunk in the Morning", "<a href="/wiki/Better_Than_Yourself_(Criminal_Mind_Pt_2)" title="Better Than Yourself (Criminal Mind Pt 2)">Better Than Yourself (Criminal Mind Pt 2)</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Things_(song)" title="Ordinary Things (song)">Ordinary Things</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DanishSongs_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-DanishSongs-15">[15]</a></sup> Over the course of 2012, Lukas Graham played 107 concerts throughout Europe, selling 40,000 tickets in Denmark alone. They sold 80,000 albums and 150,000 singles while also earning 5 million YouTube views and 27 million streams. Their tour took them across Europe to countries like England, Netherlands, Norway, Sweden, Germany, Austria, France, and Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Polit01_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Polit01-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314:_Continued_touring_and_Warner_Bros._Records_deal">2013–14: Continued touring and Warner Bros. Records deal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lukas_Graham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2013–14: Continued touring and Warner Bros. Records deal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, the band continued to tour throughout Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BZ_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-BZ-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Focus_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Focus-17">[17]</a></sup> In the summer of 2013, the band was added to the bill of the <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%B8n_Koncert" title="Grøn Koncert">Grøn Koncert</a> (Green Concert) tour which is held in support of <a href="/wiki/Muscular_dystrophy" title="Muscular dystrophy">muscular dystrophy</a> research.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fyens_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fyens-18">[18]</a></sup> In October 2013, Lukas Graham won the <a href="/wiki/European_Border_Breakers_Award" title="European Border Breakers Award">European Border Breakers Award</a> (EBBA) for their international tours in Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PolitEBBA_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-PolitEBBA-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the fall of 2013, the band signed to Warner Bros. Records. The record company signed them with the intent of bringing their music to the United States. Starting in early 2014, the band spent extended periods of time in Los Angeles writing and recording what would become their official U.S. debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EBWarner_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-EBWarner-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PolitWarner_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-PolitWarner-21">[21]</a></sup> The band is still represented by Copenhagen Records in the <a href="/wiki/Nordic_countries" title="Nordic countries">Nordics</a>, Germany, Switzerland, Austria, and France<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ally_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ally-22">[22]</a></sup> and is managed by Then We Take The World.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BT_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-BT-23">[23]</a></sup> In March 2014, Lukas himself contributed vocals to <a href="/wiki/Hedegaard" title="Hedegaard">Hedegaard</a>'s Danish number-one single "<a href="/wiki/Happy_Home_(song)" title="Happy Home (song)">Happy Home</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DR_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-DR-24">[24]</a></sup> Later in 2014, the band released their first single, "Mama Said", off their upcoming album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GAFFAMama_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-GAFFAMama-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.93present:_Worldwide_debut.2C_.227_Years.22_and_global_success">2015–present: Worldwide debut, "7 Years" and global success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lukas_Graham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title='Edit section: 2015–present: Worldwide debut, "7 Years" and global success'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band released their next record (often referred to as the "Blue Album") on Copenhagen Records and Then We Take The World solely in Denmark and several other mostly European nations in 2015. It peaked at number one in Denmark and made it onto several other European charts as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> It featured the singles "Mama Said", "<a href="/wiki/Strip_No_More" title="Strip No More">Strip No More</a>" and "7 Years", the last of which peaked at number one on the charts for Denmark, Italy, Austria, Belgium, and Sweden.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Danish7_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Danish7-27">[27]</a></sup> The band made their United States television debut performing "7 Years" on a 10 December 2015 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Conan_(talk_show)" title="Conan (talk show)">Conan</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indie_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Indie-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Conan_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Conan-29">[29]</a></sup> and the song peaked at number two on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard100_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard100-8">[8]</a></sup> In March 2016, Lukas Graham performed "7 Years" on <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kimmel_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kimmel-30">[30]</a></sup> The band has also performed the song on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Seth_Meyers" title="Late Night with Seth Meyers">Late Night with Seth Meyers</a>,</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meyers_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Meyers-31">[31]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a>,</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ellen_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ellen-32">[32]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Late_Late_Show_with_James_Corden" title="The Late Late Show with James Corden">The Late Late Show with James Corden</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>By the end of March 2016, "7 Years" had achieved around 225 million total listens on <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a> worldwide with 17.2 million listeners per month, making the band the 20th most popular artist on the service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ally_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ally-22">[22]</a></sup> On 1 April 2016, the self-titled global debut was officially released in the United States by Warner Bros. Records. The US release of the album received new artwork, a reworked track listing, and a wider global audience in countries including, Canada, Australia, the United Kingdom, and New Zealand. The album reached No. 3 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, No. 2 in the UK and New Zealand, No. 1 in Australia and Canada, and No. 5 in Ireland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-USA-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBCCharts_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBCCharts-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stapleton_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stapleton-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AUSNZ_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-AUSNZ-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IRMA_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-IRMA-37">[37]</a></sup> The band also embarked on a two-month long tour throughout the United States and Canada at the end of March 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BWW_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-BWW-38">[38]</a></sup> Most dates on the tour have already sold out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-USA-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DH_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-DH-39">[39]</a></sup> As of July 2016, "7 Years" has over 480 million Spotify streams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMNY_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMNY-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2016, keyboardist Kasper Daugaard left the band as "touring life wasn't right for (him)". He was replaced by the band's producer and former keyboardist, Morten Ristorp.</p>
<p>In December 2016, Lukas Graham was nominated for three <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a> including Record of the Year and Song of the Year for <i>7 Years</i> and Best Pop Duo/Group Performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimesBrown_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-LATimesBrown-41">[41]</a></sup> <i>7 Year</i> was also nominated for Song of the Year at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/BBC_Music_Awards" title="BBC Music Awards">BBC Music Awards</a> where the band performed the song live on December 12th, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lukas_Graham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lukas Graham's music has been described as a pop-soul hybrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Polit01_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Polit01-1">[1]</a></sup> In a review of the band's new album, journalist <a href="/wiki/Jon_Pareles" title="Jon Pareles">Jon Pareles</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> described their sound as the place "where pop meets R&amp;B".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT01_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT01-6">[6]</a></sup> Patrick Ryan of <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> wrote that the band's songs "effortlessly blend elements of hip hop and folk". Lyrically, the songs often deal with relatable experiences like growing up poor ("Mama Said") or drinking ("Drunk in the Morning").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-USA-10">[10]</a></sup> The band's most popular song, "7 Years", describes growing up and aging at specific points in life (from seven years old to 60).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Huff_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-Huff-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lukas grew up in a self-governing artistic community in the middle of <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> called <a href="/wiki/Freetown_Christiania" title="Freetown Christiania">Christiania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-USA-10">[10]</a></sup> The area is known for both its "utopian", creative atmosphere, relative poverty, and crime. His experiences growing up in the community have shaped both the sound of his music and the lyrics he writes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indie_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-Indie-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meyers_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-Meyers-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
