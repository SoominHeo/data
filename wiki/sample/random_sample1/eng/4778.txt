<p><b>Lucas Hedges</b> (born December 12, 1996) is an American actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is best known for his roles in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Moonrise_Kingdom" title="Moonrise Kingdom">Moonrise Kingdom</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_the_Messenger_(2014_film)" title="Kill the Messenger (2014 film)">Kill the Messenger</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Manchester_by_the_Sea_(film)" title="Manchester by the Sea (film)">Manchester by the Sea</a></i>, for which he garnered a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor</a>, becoming the eighth youngest nominee in that category's history.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_education">Personal life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucas_Hedges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hedges was born in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Heights" title="Brooklyn Heights">Brooklyn Heights</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> the second child of poet and actress Susan Bruce (Titman) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Oscar</a>-nominated screenwriter and director <a href="/wiki/Peter_Hedges" title="Peter Hedges">Peter Hedges</a>. He has an older brother, Simon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He grew up in Brooklyn Heights and Cobble Hill, frequently visiting his father's film sets. A graduate of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Ann%27s_School_(New_York_City)" title="Saint Ann's School (New York City)">Saint Ann's School</a>, he is currently on a break from studying theatre at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina_School_of_the_Arts" title="University of North Carolina School of the Arts">University of North Carolina School of the Arts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucas_Hedges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.932015:_Early_roles">2007–2015: Early roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucas_Hedges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007–2015: Early roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a child, Hedges' made his feature film debut as an extra in his father's film <i><a href="/wiki/Dan_in_Real_Life" title="Dan in Real Life">Dan in Real Life</a></i> (2007). His line in the film was cut in post-production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He starred in a middle-school play production, when the casting director for <a href="/wiki/Wes_Anderson" title="Wes Anderson">Wes Anderson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Moonrise_Kingdom" title="Moonrise Kingdom">Moonrise Kingdom</a></i> (2012) was in the audience and eventually cast Hedges in the film, his first role as Redford. He later appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Arthur_Newman_(film)" title="Arthur Newman (film)">Arthur Newman</a></i> (2013), <a href="/wiki/Jason_Reitman" title="Jason Reitman">Jason Reitman</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Labor_Day_(film)" title="Labor Day (film)">Labor Day</a></i> (2013) and <a href="/wiki/Terry_Gilliam" title="Terry Gilliam">Terry Gilliam</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Zero_Theorem" title="The Zero Theorem">The Zero Theorem</a></i> (2013). In 2014, Hedges made a cameo in <a href="/wiki/Wes_Anderson" title="Wes Anderson">Wes Anderson</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Grand_Budapest_Hotel" title="The Grand Budapest Hotel">The Grand Budapest Hotel</a></i> and appeared in <a href="/wiki/Michael_Cuesta" title="Michael Cuesta">Michael Cuesta</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_the_Messenger_(2014_film)" title="Kill the Messenger (2014 film)">Kill the Messenger</a></i>, for which he received positive reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.E2.80.93present:_Breakthrough">2016–present: Breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucas_Hedges&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2016–present: Breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2015, Hedges joined the cast of <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Lonergan" title="Kenneth Lonergan">Kenneth Lonergan</a>'s third feature film, <i><a href="/wiki/Manchester_by_the_Sea_(film)" title="Manchester by the Sea (film)">Manchester by the Sea</a></i> (2016). In it, he plays Patrick Chandler, a 16-year old dealing with the recent death of his father and who is left in the care of his unwilling and troubled uncle. The film had its world premiere on January 23, 2016, at the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> where Hedges received rave reviews for his performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> For his performance, Hedges has been nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Spirit_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Male" title="Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Male">Independent Spirit Award for Best Supporting Male</a>, <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award_for_Outstanding_Performance_by_a_Male_Actor_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Male Actor in a Supporting Role">Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Male Actor in a Supporting Role</a> and <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor</a>, making him one of the eighth youngest actor to ever be nominated in that category.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2017, he will star in <a href="/wiki/Greta_Gerwig" title="Greta Gerwig">Greta Gerwig</a>'s directorial debut <i><a href="/wiki/Lady_Bird_(film)" title="Lady Bird (film)">Lady Bird</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_McDonagh" title="Martin McDonagh">Martin McDonagh</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/Three_Billboards_Outside_Ebbing,_Missouri" title="Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri">Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He will also make his stage debut as the lead in the <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> play called <i>Yen</i>. The Trip Cullman-directed play will start its run on January 30, 2017 and is set through February 19 at the <a href="/wiki/Lucille_Lortel_Theatre" title="Lucille Lortel Theatre">Lucille Lortel Theatre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
