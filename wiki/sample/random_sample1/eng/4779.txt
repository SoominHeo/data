<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Lukeni lua Nimi</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Lukeni lua Nimi</b> (also <i>Ntinu Nimi a Lukeni</i>; c. 1380–1420) was the first king and founder of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kongo" title="Kingdom of Kongo">Kingdom of Kongo</a> Dia Ntotila. The name Nimi a Lukeni appeared in later oral traditions and some modern historians, notably <a href="/wiki/Jean_Cuvelier" title="Jean Cuvelier">Jean Cuvelier</a>, popularized it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He conquered the kingdom of Mwene.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lukeni_lua_Nimi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was the son of his father Nimi and the Mwene Mbata's daughter, according to traditions recorded by <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Cavazzi_da_Montecuccolo" title="Giovanni Cavazzi da Montecuccolo">Giovanni Cavazzi da Montecuccolo</a> in the mid 17th century. Although he probably ruled in the valley of the Kwilu River in modern day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of Congo">Democratic Republic of Congo</a>, he is credited with conquering the region of modern day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mbanza_Kongo" title="Mbanza Kongo">Mbanza Kongo</a>, displacing a local ruler named Mwene Mpangala and building his capital there. He probably ruled in the late 14th century.</p>
