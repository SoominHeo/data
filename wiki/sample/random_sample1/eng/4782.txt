<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luke_Davies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Davies studied Arts at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Sydney" title="University of Sydney">University of Sydney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-steg_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-steg-1">[1]</a></sup> His first poetry collection <i>Four Plots for Magnets</i> was published in 1982 by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=S._K._Kelen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="S. K. Kelen (page does not exist)">S. K. Kelen</a> at Glandular Press. Long out of print, it was republished (with additional poetry and prose) by <a href="/wiki/Pitt_Street_Poetry" title="Pitt Street Poetry">Pitt Street Poetry</a> in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pitt_Street_Poetry_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pitt_Street_Poetry-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He co-wrote the screenplay for the 2006 film <i><a href="/wiki/Candy_(2006_film)" title="Candy (2006 film)">Candy</a></i> with director <a href="/wiki/Neil_Armfield" title="Neil Armfield">Neil Armfield</a>, based on his 1997 novel <i><a href="/wiki/Candy:_A_Novel_of_Love_and_Addiction" title="Candy: A Novel of Love and Addiction">Candy</a></i>. The film stars <a href="/wiki/Heath_Ledger" title="Heath Ledger">Heath Ledger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abbie_Cornish" title="Abbie Cornish">Abbie Cornish</a> as struggling heroin addicts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Davies himself overcame <a href="/wiki/Heroin" title="Heroin">heroin</a> addiction in 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-steg_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-steg-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>His other works include the novels <i>Isabelle the Navigator</i> and <i>God of Speed</i>, and several volumes of poetry – <i>Four Plots for Magnets</i>, <i>Absolute Event Horizon</i>, <i>Running With Light</i>, <i>Totem</i> and <i>Interferon Psalms</i> – as well as the chatbooks <i>The Entire History of Architecture ... and other love poems</i> (Vagabond Press, 2001)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vagabond_Press_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vagabond_Press-4">[4]</a></sup> and <i>The Feral Aphorisms</i> (Vagabond Press, 2011).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Davies is the film critic for <i>The Monthly</i>, and occasional book reviewer and essayist for other magazines and newspapers. His children’s book, <i>Magpie</i>, was published by ABC Books in 2010.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luke_Davies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>1995: Turnbull Fox Phillips Poetry Prize Shortlisted for <i>Absolute Event Horizon</i></li>
<li>1998: A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney_Morning_Herald" title="Sydney Morning Herald">Sydney Morning Herald</a> Young Writer of the Year</li>
<li>2000: <a href="/wiki/Queensland_Premier%27s_Literary_Awards#Poetry_Collection_.E2.80.93_Arts_Queensland_Judith_Wright_Calanthe_Award" title="Queensland Premier's Literary Awards">Queensland Premier's Literary Awards, Judith Wright Poetry Prize</a> for <i>Running With Light</i></li>
<li>2004: <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Age_Poetry_Book_of_the_Year&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Age Poetry Book of the Year (page does not exist)">The Age Poetry Book of the Year</a> for <i>Totem</i></li>
<li>2004: Overall <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Age_Book_of_the_Year" title="Age Book of the Year">Age Book of the Year</a> for <i>Totem</i></li>
<li>2004: <a href="/wiki/Grace_Leven_Prize_for_Poetry" title="Grace Leven Prize for Poetry">Grace Leven Prize for Poetry</a> for <i>Totem</i></li>
<li>2004: <a href="/wiki/Philip_Hodgins" title="Philip Hodgins">Philip Hodgins Memorial Medal</a> at the Mildura Writer's Festival<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hodgin_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hodgin-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>2006: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Australian_Premier%27s_Awards" title="South Australian Premier's Awards">South Australian Premier's Awards</a>, John Bray Poetry Award for <i>Totem</i></li>
<li>2012: <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister%27s_Literary_Awards" title="Prime Minister's Literary Awards">Prime Minister's Literary Awards</a> Poetry Winner for <i>Interferon Psalms</i></li>
</ul>
