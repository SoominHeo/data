<p><b>Luke Cunningham Wilson</b> (born September 21, 1971) is an American actor known for his roles in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Idiocracy" title="Idiocracy">Idiocracy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Old_School_(film)" title="Old School (film)">Old School</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bottle_Rocket" title="Bottle Rocket">Bottle Rocket</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Tenenbaums" title="The Royal Tenenbaums">The Royal Tenenbaums</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Streak_(film)" title="Blue Streak (film)">Blue Streak</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bongwater_(film)" title="Bongwater (film)">Bongwater</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Legally_Blonde" title="Legally Blonde">Legally Blonde</a></i>. He is the brother of actors <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Wilson_(actor)" title="Andrew Wilson (actor)">Andrew</a> and <a href="/wiki/Owen_Wilson" title="Owen Wilson">Owen Wilson</a>. He was a member of the cast of the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Enlightened_(TV_series)" title="Enlightened (TV series)">Enlightened</a></i> (2011–2013).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luke_Wilson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wilson was born in <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, a son of Robert Andrew Wilson, an advertising executive and previously an executive at KERA,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup> a public television station, and <a href="/wiki/Laura_Wilson_(photographer)" title="Laura Wilson (photographer)">Laura (Cunningham) Wilson</a>, a photographer. His family, originally from <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, is of Irish Catholic descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is the younger brother of actors <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Wilson_(actor)" title="Andrew Wilson (actor)">Andrew Wilson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Owen_Wilson" title="Owen Wilson">Owen Wilson</a>. All three boys attended <a href="/wiki/St._Mark%27s_School_of_Texas" title="St. Mark's School of Texas">St. Mark's School of Texas</a>. According to his brother Owen, Luke was voted class president the first year he attended St. Mark's.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luke_Wilson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Luke_Wilson.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="343" data-file-width="250" height="302" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Luke_Wilson.jpg/220px-Luke_Wilson.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a0/Luke_Wilson.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Wilson's acting career began with the lead role in the short film <i><a href="/wiki/Bottle_Rocket" title="Bottle Rocket">Bottle Rocket</a></i> in 1994, which was co-written by his older brother <a href="/wiki/Owen_Wilson" title="Owen Wilson">Owen Wilson</a> and director <a href="/wiki/Wes_Anderson" title="Wes Anderson">Wes Anderson</a>. It was remade as a feature-length film in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup> After moving to Hollywood with his two brothers, he was cast opposite <a href="/wiki/Calista_Flockhart" title="Calista Flockhart">Calista Flockhart</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Telling_Lies_in_America" title="Telling Lies in America">Telling Lies in America</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup> and made a <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearance</a> in the film-within-the-film of <i><a href="/wiki/Scream_2" title="Scream 2">Scream 2</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup> both in 1997. Wilson filmed back-to-back romantic films in 1998, opposite <a href="/wiki/Drew_Barrymore" title="Drew Barrymore">Drew Barrymore</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Best_Men" title="Best Men">Best Men</a></i>, about a group of friends who pull off a heist on their way to a wedding,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_Fries" title="Home Fries">Home Fries</a></i>, about two brothers interested in the same woman for different reasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup> He played the male nurse beau of a schoolteacher in <i><a href="/wiki/Rushmore_(film)" title="Rushmore (film)">Rushmore</a></i> (also 1998), also directed by Anderson and co-written by brother Owen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1999, <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Streak_(film)" title="Blue Streak (film)">Blue Streak</a></i> was released featuring Wilson as detective Carlson. He later starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Reese_Witherspoon" title="Reese Witherspoon">Reese Witherspoon</a> in the 2001 comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Legally_Blonde" title="Legally Blonde">Legally Blonde</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> which was followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Old_School_(film)" title="Old School (film)">Old School</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Tenenbaums" title="The Royal Tenenbaums">The Royal Tenenbaums</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup> Wilson also had a role on <i><a href="/wiki/That_%2770s_Show" title="That '70s Show">That '70s Show</a></i>, as Michael Kelso's older brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Casey_Kelso" title="Casey Kelso">Casey Kelso</a>, appearing sporadically from 2002 through 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, Wilson starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Idiocracy" title="Idiocracy">Idiocracy</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Judge" title="Mike Judge">Mike Judge</a>'s first film since 1999's <i><a href="/wiki/Office_Space" title="Office Space">Office Space</a></i>. He portrayed an ordinary serviceman chosen for a cryogenics project. He awakens after hundreds of years in an America which is significantly less intelligent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2007, Wilson starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Kate_Beckinsale" title="Kate Beckinsale">Kate Beckinsale</a> in the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Vacancy_(film)" title="Vacancy (film)">Vacancy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In July 2007, he worked on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_Poole_is_Here" title="Henry Poole is Here">Henry Poole is Here</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/La_Mirada,_California" title="La Mirada, California">La Mirada</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, which was released in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup> He starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Tenure_(film)" title="Tenure (film)">Tenure</a></i> in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2010, he appeared in films <i><a href="/wiki/Death_at_a_Funeral_(2010_film)" title="Death at a Funeral (2010 film)">Death at a Funeral</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Middle_Men_(film)" title="Middle Men (film)">Middle Men</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Luke_Wilson_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-Luke_Wilson-1">[1]</a></sup> From 2011 to 2013 he starred in the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Enlightened_(TV_series)" title="Enlightened (TV series)">Enlightened</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wilson and brother Owen have co-written a <a href="/wiki/Wright_brothers" title="Wright brothers">Wright Brothers</a> biopic, in which they also plan to star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
