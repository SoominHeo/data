<p><b>Luke Jacobz</b> (born <b>Luke Jacob Ashwood</b>; 14 February 1981, Sydney) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian" title="Australian">Australian</a> actor and television presenter.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luke_Jacobz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He appeared in a high school production of <i>The Man of Steel</i> in 1995. He came to public attention when he starred as Zac Croft for two years on the Australian teen drama television series <i><a href="/wiki/Heartbreak_High" title="Heartbreak High">Heartbreak High</a></i>. He later moved on to hosting television programs such as the now cancelled television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Arvo" title="The Big Arvo">The Big Arvo</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Popstars_Live" title="Popstars Live">Popstars Live</a></i>.</p>
<p>Jacobz made a return to acting in <i><a href="/wiki/McLeod%27s_Daughters" title="McLeod's Daughters">McLeod's Daughters</a></i> as Patrick Brewer, appearing on the show between 2005 and 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He also appeared in a six-month recurring role as <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Rosetta" title="Angelo Rosetta">Angelo Rosetta</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Home_and_Away" title="Home and Away">Home and Away</a></i>, where after becoming a viewer favourite, he was then upgraded to series regular. In 2008, Jacobz was crowned <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(Australian_TV_series)" title="Dancing with the Stars (Australian TV series)">Dancing with the Stars</a></i> champion along with dancer and mentor <a href="/wiki/Luda_Kroitor" title="Luda Kroitor">Luda Kroitor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 23 August 2010, Jacobz was announced as the new host of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(Australia)" title="The X Factor (Australia)">The X Factor</a></i>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Newton" title="Matthew Newton">Matthew Newton</a>, who stepped down from the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Jacobz also began talks to appear in a new Australian drama series <i>Wild Boys</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jacobz returned as host for <i>The X Factor</i> in the fourth series,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and finished hosting the seventh series on 24 November 2015.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luke_Jacobz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2010, Jacobz was nominated for the Silver <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logie_Award" title="Logie Award">Logie Award</a> for the most popular actor for his role on <i><a href="/wiki/Home_and_Away" title="Home and Away">Home and Away</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luke_Jacobz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Jacobz" is a screen name, derived from adding a 'z' to the actor's middle name of Jacob. Jacobz dated his <i>Dancing with the Stars</i> partner <a href="/wiki/Luda_Kroitor" title="Luda Kroitor">Luda Kroitor</a> in 2009; the relationship ended some time later. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jacobz was charged with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drink_driving" title="Drink driving">drink driving</a> on 31 May 2015 in Sydney, returning a 0.116 reading, well above the 0.05 limit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In court, he pleaded guilty and lost his licence for 12 months and was fined AU$700.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 September 2015, Jacobz jokingly came out as gay/bisexual by asking a contestant <a href="/wiki/Cyrus_Villanueva" title="Cyrus Villanueva">Cyrus Villanueva</a> on <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(Australian_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (Australian TV series)">The X Factor (Australian TV series)</a> whether he would be his boyfriend. Cyrus awkwardly replied, 'Yours?'.</p>
