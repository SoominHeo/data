<p><i><b>Rookies</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ルーキーズ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Rūkīzu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dnen" title="Shōnen">shōnen</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Masanori_Morita" title="Masanori Morita">Masanori Morita</a>. The series was featured on <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Jump" title="Weekly Shōnen Jump">Weekly Shōnen Jump</a></i> during its original run from 1998 to 2003. The manga has been adapted into a television series which was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">Tokyo Broadcasting System</a> between April 19, 2008 and July 26, 2008. The series was later released on DVD by TC Entertainment, and the soundtrack released on June 28, 2008. The finale of the TV series was in the form of a film, called <i>Rookies: Graduation</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">Rｏｏｋｉｅｓ－卒業－</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Rookies -Sotsugyō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> which premiered on Japanese cinemas on May 31, 2009.</p>
<p><i>Rookies</i> is the story of an amateur teacher, Koichi Kawato, who is faced with the challenge of guiding a high school baseball club composed of delinquents. The students are wary of him and often threaten Kawato, but he refuses to abandon them and continuously speaks highly of recognizing their dreams. Eventually, the team is assembled and they dedicate their time to achieve their dream of going to the <a href="/wiki/Koshien_Stadium" title="Koshien Stadium">Koshien</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rookies_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koichi Kawato is the new Japanese teacher at the ill-famed Futakotamagawa high school, whose baseball club is composed of thugs and bullies who have been suspended for a year from all school competitions, for causing a brawl during an official match. The newly appointed teacher finds that the club members left are only interested in women, smoking and doing nothing good until, under Kawato's guidance, they recognize that baseball is what they truly love doing. Kawato also teaches them that they should follow their dreams, and for them, being able to reach the national high school tournament finals in Koshien (Hyogo prefecture) is what they have always wished for. However, reaching Koshien is far from easy as many obstacles await them.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rookies_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Teachers">Teachers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rookies_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Teachers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Koichi Kawato</dt>
<dd>Koichi Kawato is a young, optimistic, and highly honorable teacher who strongly believes in following dreams. He also tries to build more personal relationships with his students, even memorizing all their names from the class list. After he promises a student that he would protect the school's baseball team, he becomes the team's advisor. It is later found out he resigned from his last school after getting in a fight with a student, and he vowed never to hit anyone again. Despite this, he was near universally loved at his last school and they try (unsuccessfully) to get him to come back.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Vice Principal Ikebe</dt>
<dd>Vice Principal Ikebe is a former member of the baseball team who had once made it to <a href="/wiki/Japanese_High_School_Baseball_Championship" title="Japanese High School Baseball Championship">Koshien</a>. Though he seemed uptight and strict at first, often lecturing Kawato over the smallest details, he later warms up to Kawato. When the team starts playing again, Ikebe becomes their coach, lending his expertise.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Principal Murayama</dt>
<dd>Principal Murayama is the former captain of the baseball team from 43 years ago, when the team went to Koshien. He harbors a resentment for the current baseball team as he views them as tarnishing the legacy he left behind, and is willing to do anything to get them all expelled. However, after witnessing their first game against Yoga Daiichi, he starts to regard the baseball team more positively. When Yoga Daiichi's coach threatens to press charges of assault against Kawato for hitting him during their game, Murayama takes the blame for Kawato and resigns.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baseball_team">Baseball team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rookies_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Baseball team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>Toru Mikoshiba</dt>
<dd>Toru Mikoshiba is the team's captain. Though non-violent and intelligent, he is often affected by nervousness and insecurity. He does, however, know when to take a stand and lead the team. He was originally the gofer when they were still a team of thugs.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Keiichi Aniya</dt>
<dd>Keiichi Aniya is the team's primary pitcher. He was well known in middle school, but lost his motivation after a pitcher who had struck him out was unable to strike anyone out at <a href="/wiki/Koshien_Stadium" title="Koshien Stadium">Koshien Stadium</a>. Despite fooling with other girls, he is in love with his childhood friend, Toko Yagi. He once stated that the reason he played around so much was because she wouldn't go out with him. However, after seriously beginning to train for baseball, he has stopped dating so frequently.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Shuta Sekikawa</dt>
<dd>Shuta Sekikawa is the team's fastest member and sports a <a href="/wiki/Mohawk_hairstyle" title="Mohawk hairstyle">mohawk</a> that is "rock hard" due to the amount of styling products he uses. When he lets his hair down (washes the styling products out), his speed increases greatly. He was the second one to be convinced by Kawato to officially join the team, after Mikoshiba.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Kei Shinjo</dt>
<dd>Kei Shinjo was originally angry at the other members for starting to leave gang-life behind, and proceeded to heavily injure them. He is later revealed to care deeply about his friendship with the team, but was reluctant to rejoin due to his actions. He later rejoins and works twice as hard to make up for his actions. He has great batting abilities, being able to hit a 150 meter line drive. His swing is described as being able to cut through the wind.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Yuya Okada</dt>
<dd>Yuya Okada is a rational member who keeps his hair in long <a href="/wiki/Dreadlocks" title="Dreadlocks">dreadlocks</a>. He is by far the calmest and wisest among the group, though perhaps not the most mature. It has been mentioned that he gets good grades, along with Mikoshiba and Shinjo.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Tetsuro Yufune</dt>
<dd>Tetsuro Yufune is a nervous player who uses his batting technique "Cat Swing", in which he screams "<a href="/wiki/Meow" title="Meow">Meow</a>" before he hits the ball, to achieve better accuracy. He also has a crush on the English teacher, Rie Mayumi, but he is unable to confess his feelings to her, choosing to jump off a balcony (yelling "MEOW" like he does when he's nervous) rather than do so.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Kiyooki Hiyama</dt>
<dd>Kiyooki Hiyama is one of the team's worst batters, often mocked by other teams as "the bearded strikeout king." Although he is short-tempered, he refused to fight against a few boys from Megurogawa attacking him to avoid team disqualification. However, after constant practice, he managed to improve his hitting.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Tomochika Wakana</dt>
<dd>Tomochika Wakana is the only player able to consistently catch Aniya's pitches, though he originally was unable to keep his eyes open as the ball came to him. With help from Hiyama, Wakana was able to overcome this obstacle. He is considered to be the mood maker of the team.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Taira Hiratsuka</dt>
<dd>Taira Hiratsuka is a strong member who has almost no skill in baseball. However, he is able to achieve incredible hits by using survival instinct, particularly when a ball is thrown at his head. He is often ignored by his teammates and even Kawato, not out of spite but because they simply don't notice him. He has a huge crush on Toko Yagi after she slapped him for making fun of Shinjo.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Shinobu Imaoka</dt>
<dd>Shinobu Imaoka is the team's <a href="/wiki/Ambidexterity" title="Ambidexterity">ambidextrous</a> player and considered strange among several of his teammates. He has surprising talent for the sport, having great baseball sense and being able to bat and pitch well. He is close friends with Hiratsuka and refers to him by the nickname "Hiracchi."</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Shouji Akaboshi</dt>
<dd>Shouji Akaboshi is one of two new first years who join after the main group of ten. He a skilled player who was also well known in middle school, and dreamed of being able to compete in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>. At first he seemed arrogant, claiming he only needed high school to learn English and refusing to join the baseball team as he viewed them beneath him. However, he was practicing with a university team in secret, something Yagi and Aniya accidentally found out. When he was convinced to join the team, he discovered they were all as or even more talented than he was, leading him to work much harder for his spot on the team.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Hamanaka Taiyou</dt>
<dd>Hamanaka Taiyou is one of two new first years who join after the main group of ten, being a full year younger than the rest. At first, he was a bully who beat up old men for money. After an encounter with Hiratsuka, he began to model himself after Hiratsuka, who he believed to be a brilliant player. After realizing the truth, Hamanaka still joined the baseball team, though he is deemed useless.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Toko Yagi</dt>
<dd>Toko Yagi is the baseball team manager and childhood friend of Aniya, whom she has romantic feelings for. She has great baseball sense and managing capabilities, to the point where she and Mikoshiba lost everyone during their baseball talk.</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rookies_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rookies_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Rookies</i> is written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Masanori_Morita" title="Masanori Morita">Masanori Morita</a>. It was serialized in <a href="/wiki/Shueisha" title="Shueisha">Shueisha</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Weekly_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Jump" title="Weekly Shōnen Jump">Weekly Shōnen Jump</a></i> during its original run from 1998 to 2003 from the tenth to 39th issue of <i>Weekly Shōnen Jump</i>. <a href="/wiki/Shueisha" title="Shueisha">Shueisha</a> released the manga in 24 <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes between June 4, 1998 and November 4, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was licensed in France by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Editions_Tonkam" title="Editions Tonkam">Editions Tonkam</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and in Taiwan by Da Ran Culture before it went bankrupt. The Taiwan license was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Tong_Li_Publishing" title="Tong Li Publishing">Tong Li Publishing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In Italy, <i>Rookies</i> was licensed by <a href="/wiki/Star_Comics_(Italy)" title="Star Comics (Italy)">Star Comics</a> and serialized in the magazine <i>Express</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_drama">TV drama</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rookies_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: TV drama">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The manga was adapted into a live-action <a href="/wiki/Japanese_television_drama" title="Japanese television drama">Japanese television drama</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The series' script was written by Yoshihiro Izumi and was directed by Yuichiro Hirakawa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TBS_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-TBS-7">[7]</a></sup> It was produced by Tsuru Masaaki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TBS_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-TBS-7">[7]</a></sup> Its 11 episodes were broadcast on <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">Tokyo Broadcasting System</a> between April 19, 2008 and July 26, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The series' theme song was "<a href="/wiki/Kiseki_(Greeeen_song)" title="Kiseki (Greeeen song)">Kiseki</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Greeeen" title="Greeeen">Greeeen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Academy_Awards_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Academy_Awards-10">[10]</a></sup> The first three episodes of the drama were released as a <i>Rookies Omote Box</i> by TC Entertainment on July 18, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Episodes four to eleven were released in a <i>Rookies Ura Box</i> on October 8, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The soundtrack to the drama was released on a CD on June 28, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rookies_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A live-action film, <i>Rookies: Graduation</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">Rｏｏｋｉｅｓ－卒業－</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Rookies -Sotsugyō-</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, was adapted from the manga. Created by <a href="/wiki/Toho" title="Toho">Toho</a> and directed by Yūichirō Hirakawa, the film premiered on May 31, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Box_Office_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Box_Office-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rookies_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Rookies</i> was ranked the 9th top-selling manga in Japan in 2008 with 2,765,163 copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The last episode of <i>Rookies</i>, broadcast on July 26, 2008, was rated 19.5 in the average household ratings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The television special episode of <i>Rookies</i> garnered a 10.0 rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The <i>Rookies</i> Ura Box for the live-action television adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Masanori_Morita" title="Masanori Morita">Masanori Morita</a>'s <i>Rookies</i> baseball manga sold 5,595 copies during the October 15–21, 2008 weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <i>Rookies</i> TV drama DVDs was ranked the top having the most copies sold in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> First episode of <i>Rookies</i> was rated a 9.5 when it re-ran on December 31, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <i>Rookies</i> Ura Box was ranked 24th on the Oricon charts with 1,997 copies sold between June 1–7, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-June_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-June-22">[22]</a></sup> <i>Rookies</i> Omote Box was ranked 30th on the Oricon charts with 1,891 copies sold between June 8–14, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-June2_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-June2-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Rookies</i> drama series was awarded five of the possible nine awards at the 58th Television Drama Academy Awards in 2008. <i>Rookies</i> was awarded best drama; Ryuta Sato was awarded best actor; Hayato Ichihara was awarded best supporting actor; "Kiseki" by <a href="/wiki/Greeeen" title="Greeeen">Greeeen</a> was awarded best theme song and Nikogaku Nine was awarded the special award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Academy_Awards_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Academy_Awards-10">[10]</a></sup> <i>Rookies</i> was highly commended as one of the best drama series at the 2008 Asian Television Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Document of Rookies ~Sotsugyō</i> debut at 15th on the Oricon charts between April 21–28, 2009 with 9,141 copies sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> It sold 3,092 copies during June 1–7, 2009 to be ranked 10th.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-June_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-June-22">[22]</a></sup> It sold 2,648 copies the following week and dropped to rank 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-June2_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-June2-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Rookies: Graduation</i> topped the Japanese box office chart when it premiered on May 30, 2009, earning the equivalent of <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>12,703,775 over the May 30–31 weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Box_Office_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Box_Office-15">[15]</a></sup> It continued to top the Japanese box office chart the following weekend, earning US$12,703,775 that weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> It was ranked second on the box office chart to <i><a href="/wiki/Terminator_Salvation" title="Terminator Salvation">Terminator Salvation</a></i>, earning US$8,278,440 that weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> It rose back to top the box office charts on the June 20–21 weekend, earning US$6,404,066 that weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On the fifth weekend, the movie dropped to second place to <i><a href="/wiki/Evangelion:_2.0_You_Can_(Not)_Advance" title="Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance">Evangelion: 2.0 You Can (Not) Advance</a></i>, earning US$4,290,266 that weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On its sixth weekend on the box office charts, it stayed at second place, earning US$2,933,634 that weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In its seventh weekend, <i>Rookies: Graduation</i> fell to 4th place and earned US$1,894,007, and has grossed US$82 million as of July 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In its eighth weekend, <i>Rookies: Graduation</i>, fell to 8th place and earned US$1,129,349.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In its ninth weekend, <i>Rookies: Graduation</i> rose to 6th place and earned US$748,804.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
