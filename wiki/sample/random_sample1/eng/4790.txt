<p><i><b>Luther</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Television_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Television in the United Kingdom">British</a> crime drama programme<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> starring <a href="/wiki/Idris_Elba" title="Idris Elba">Idris Elba</a> as the title character, <a href="/wiki/Police_ranks_of_the_United_Kingdom#Rank_insignia" title="Police ranks of the United Kingdom">DCI</a> John Luther. Written by <a href="/wiki/Neil_Cross" title="Neil Cross">Neil Cross</a>, the first series, comprising six episodes, ran in May and June 2010. A second series of four episodes aired on <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> in June and July 2011. A third series was commissioned in 2012;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> comprising four episodes aired in July 2013. A two-episode fourth series was broadcast in December 2015.</p>
<p>Elba has received critical acclaim for the role of John Luther, and has been awarded a <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Television_Award" title="Critics' Choice Television Award">Critics’ Choice Television Award</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a>, <a href="/wiki/NAACP_Image_Award" title="NAACP Image Award">NAACP Image Award</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award" title="Screen Actors Guild Award">Screen Actors Guild Award</a> for his performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The series has also received eleven <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Award</a> nominations in various categories, including four nominations of Elba for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Lead_Actor_in_a_Miniseries_or_a_Movie" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Miniseries or a Movie">Outstanding Lead Actor in a Miniseries or Movie</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/List_of_Luther_characters#John_Luther" title="List of Luther characters">John Luther</a> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Inspector#United_Kingdom" title="Chief Inspector">Detective Chief Inspector</a> (DCI) working for the Serious Crime Unit in series one, and the new Serious and Serial Crime Unit in series two and three.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Profile_Luther_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Profile_Luther-6">[6]</a></sup> A dedicated police officer, Luther is obsessive, possessed, and sometimes violent. However, Luther has paid a heavy price for his dedication; he has never been able to prevent himself from being consumed by the darkness of the crimes with which he deals.</p>
<p>For Luther, the job always comes first. His dedication is a curse and a blessing, both for him and those close to him. In the very first episode of the series he investigates brilliant psychopath and murderer <a href="/wiki/List_of_Luther_characters#Alice_Morgan" title="List of Luther characters">Alice Morgan</a>. Ultimately he is unable to arrest her due to lack of evidence, but as the programme progresses, she becomes both his nemesis and unlikely companion. As she pursues her infatuation with him, he gradually relents as he is able to glean insight from her about some of the cunning criminals he pursues.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inspiration">Inspiration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Inspiration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Creator <a href="/wiki/Neil_Cross" title="Neil Cross">Neil Cross</a> has said that Luther is influenced by both <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbo" title="Columbo">Columbo</a>; the nature of Luther's intellect and its application to solving crimes is comparable to Holmes', whereas the show's use of the <a href="/wiki/Inverted_detective_story" title="Inverted detective story">inverted detective</a> format was inspired by Columbo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first series was filmed in and around London, England, and produced by <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC Drama Productions</a>. <a href="/wiki/Brian_Kirk" title="Brian Kirk">Brian Kirk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Miller" title="Sam Miller">Sam Miller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Schwartz" title="Stefan Schwartz">Stefan Schwartz</a> each directed two episodes and show creator <a href="/wiki/Neil_Cross" title="Neil Cross">Neil Cross</a> wrote all six of the episodes. Leila Kirkpatrick was the line producer for the entire program and Katie Swinden was the producer for a number of episodes. Tim Fleming provided cinematography for two episodes. Katie Weiland and Victoria Boydell were involved in film editing, with Weiland editing two episodes. Andy Morgan was responsible for the all casting, Paul Cross provided production design and Adam A. Makin was behind the show's art direction.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Locations">Locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The show is filmed in various locations around <a href="/wiki/Central_London" title="Central London">Central</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_London" title="North London">North</a> and <a href="/wiki/East_London" title="East London">East London</a> including the <a href="/wiki/Barbican_Estate" title="Barbican Estate">Barbican Estate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elephant_and_Castle" title="Elephant and Castle">Elephant and Castle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cranbrook_Estate" title="Cranbrook Estate">Cranbrook Estate</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bethnal_Green" title="Bethnal Green">Bethnal Green</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waterloo_Station" title="Waterloo Station">Waterloo Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holloway,_London" title="Holloway, London">Holloway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shoreditch" title="Shoreditch">Shoreditch</a> and <a href="/wiki/London_Docklands" title="London Docklands">the Docklands</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_1_.282010.29">Series 1 (2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Series 1 (2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_Luther_episodes#Series_1_.282010.29" title="List of Luther episodes">first series</a> of Luther aired in Summer 2010 and received positive reviews from critics, getting an average of 5.9 million viewers per episode.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_2_.282011.29">Series 2 (2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Series 2 (2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2010, the BBC announced that it had commissioned a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Luther_episodes#Series_2_.282011.29" title="List of Luther episodes">second series</a> for 2011. Filming started in late September/early October 2010. Originally planned to be broadcast as two two-hour episodes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC29Aug2010_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC29Aug2010-9">[9]</a></sup> it was shown as four one-hour episodes. The first episode was shown on BBC One on 14 June 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_3_.282013.29">Series 3 (2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Series 3 (2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2011, the BBC One controller announced that a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Luther_episodes#Series_3_.282013.29" title="List of Luther episodes">third series</a> had been commissioned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Filming of the four-episode series started in November 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sienna_Guillory" title="Sienna Guillory">Sienna Guillory</a> was cast as Luther's new love interest. Other guest stars included <a href="/wiki/Lucian_Msamati" title="Lucian Msamati">Lucian Msamati</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ned_Dennehy" title="Ned Dennehy">Ned Dennehy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The third series began airing on 2 July 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and concluded on 23 July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_4_.282015.29">Series 4 (2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Series 4 (2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 19 November 2014, it was announced that a two-episode special would be aired on the BBC in autumn 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Filming began in March 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and ended April 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> BBC Home Entertainment later confirmed that the <a href="/wiki/DVD_region_code" title="DVD region code">Region 1</a> version of the Series 4 DVD would be released on 15 December 2015, coinciding with the air date of the fourth series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On 24 October 2015, <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> confirmed via their <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> page that series 4 of <i>Luther</i> would air in December 2015 on BBC One.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2015, it was announced that Luther was confirmed to air in the United States on BBC America for a three-hour one-night event on 17 December 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first episode of the fourth series aired on BBC One on 15 December 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future">Future</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Future">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, Idris Elba expressed his enthusiasm for making a <a href="/wiki/Film_adaptation" title="Film adaptation">film adaptation</a> of the show, saying, "That's where the ultimate <i>Luther</i> story will unfold, is in the big silver screen — London as a huge backdrop and a very menacing, horrible character to play against."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> During a <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a> interview in March 2012, co-creator Neil Cross revealed that plans were being made for a <i>Luther</i> film that would begin production at the conclusion of the third series, the show's last.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In May 2012, Elba stated in an interview that the third series would serve as the <a href="/wiki/Lead-in" title="Lead-in">lead-in</a> for the planned film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Neil Cross previously had confirmed there would not be a fourth series but did not rule out the possibility of a feature film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In September 2012, Cross announced that he was in the process of creating a <a href="/wiki/Spin-off_(media)" title="Spin-off (media)">spin-off</a> of <i>Luther</i> centred around the character <a href="/wiki/List_of_Luther_characters#Alice_Morgan" title="List of Luther characters">Alice Morgan</a>, starring <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Wilson" title="Ruth Wilson">Ruth Wilson</a>. Cross stated, "The BBC is very interested in the project. The only real question would be how many and how often we would do it - whether it would be a one-off miniseries or a returning miniseries, a co-production or not."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2014, in an interview with TVLine at the 2014 66th Primetime Emmy Awards and Zap2It while promoting his movie <i><a href="/wiki/No_Good_Deed_(2014_film)" title="No Good Deed (2014 film)">No Good Deed</a></i> (2014), Idris Elba promised that "the series isn't over", which kept the possibility for future series or a feature film of Luther open.</p>
<p>In a September 2014 <a href="/wiki/Reddit" title="Reddit">Reddit</a> AMA, Elba addressed the possibility of future plans for <i>Luther</i> and said there were "talks at the moment to figure out a way to bring more Luther back to the TV, but the focus is to try and make a film from it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an August 2016 Q&amp;A session on his Twitter, Elba responded to a question about the show's future by stating that there was a "big chance" of a new fifth series of <i>Luther</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="American_version">American version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: American version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2014, <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> was developing an American TV series version of Luther, with Cross writing and executive producing the series, and Elba, Peter Chernin (of the Chernin company), Katherine Pope, and BBC Worldwide Prods' Jane Tranterand Julie Gartner on board as executive producers. The project hails from 20th Century Fox TV, studio-based Chernin Entertainment, and BBC Worldwide Prods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 March 2015, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> reported that the network had delayed the series, as the busy pilot season had limited the number of available actors to play lead character John Luther. While Fox had at one point considered <a href="/wiki/Marlon_Wayans" title="Marlon Wayans">Marlon Wayans</a>, they have since decided to search out another actor for the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Russian_version">Russian version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Russian version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Klim_(TV_series)" title="Klim (TV series)">Klim (TV series)</a></div>
<p>In 2014, <a href="/wiki/Channel_One_Russia" title="Channel One Russia">Channel One Russia</a> was developing a Russian TV series version of Luther, with <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Lavronenko" title="Konstantin Lavronenko">Konstantin Lavronenko</a>, starring as Klim. The series was first broadcast on 31 January 2016.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Series one was met with positive reviews, according to aggregate review site <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, where it holds a 95% approval rating, based on 19 reviews. The site's consensus reads: "Gritty and ambiguous, Luther is a captivating drama bolstered by a powerhouse performance from Idris Elba in the title role."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> It holds a <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> score of 82 out of 100, based on 11 collected reviews, indicating "universal acclaim".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> However, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Stuart Heritage was initially critical of the show,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> and compared it to the American series <i><a href="/wiki/FlashForward" title="FlashForward">FlashForward</a></i>, in that both series arrived with a large amount of hype but delivered an anticlimactic end product. However, by the fifth episode, Heritage had changed his views and called it Idris Elba's best work since <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wire" title="The Wire">The Wire</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>The show's second series was met with equally positive reviews and holds a 100% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, based on 12 reviews. The site's consensus reads: "Luther delves into darker territory in its second series, and Idris Elba's outstanding performance makes this a police procedural of uncommon weight and heft."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> It holds a <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> score of 78 out of 100, based on 9 collected reviews, indicating "Generally favorable reviews."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> admiringly highlighted the darker tone, Elba's performance, and the production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In the third quarter of 2011, the top-rated shows on <a href="/wiki/BBC_America" title="BBC America">BBC America</a> were <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(2002_TV_series)" title="Top Gear (2002 TV series)">Top Gear</a></i>, and <i>Luther</i>, which together gained the network its highest quarterly ratings ever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Series three was again met with positive reviews and holds an 84% approval rating on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, based on 19 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> It holds a <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> score of 76 out of 100, based on 19 collected reviews, indicating "Generally favorable reviews."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_broadcast">International broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: International broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first series of the show premiered in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> on <a href="/wiki/ABC_(Australian_TV_channel)" title="ABC (Australian TV channel)">ABC1</a> on 15 October 2010 and in the United States on <a href="/wiki/BBC_America" title="BBC America">BBC America</a> two days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Australian_airdate_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-Australian_airdate-44">[44]</a></sup> It was also broadcast in Germany (Series 1: September/October 2011; Series 2: March 2012) where it was dubbed and cut, in France on Canal+ and in Poland on Ale Kino+ channel. The third series ran in the United States on BBC America on four consecutive nights beginning 3 September 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luther_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Paul_Englishby" title="Paul Englishby">Paul Englishby</a> composed <i>Luther'</i>s soundtrack. He incorporated many pop and rock songs into the soundtrack, using these generally in the end credits. A CD titled <i>Luther - Songs and Score From Series 1, 2 and 3</i> was released on 19 August 2013 and contains many of the themes and songs used in the television series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Amazon.co.uk_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-Amazon.co.uk-50">[50]</a></sup> The opening theme song is a shorter version of Paradise Circus by <a href="/wiki/Massive_Attack" title="Massive Attack">Massive Attack</a> from the album <i><a href="/wiki/Heligoland_(album)" title="Heligoland (album)">Heligoland</a></i> (2010).</p>
