<p><b>Rupert William Penry-Jones</b> (born 22 September 1970) is an English actor, best known for his roles as <a href="/wiki/Adam_Carter" title="Adam Carter">Adam Carter</a> in the <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> spy drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Spooks_(TV_series)" title="Spooks (TV series)">Spooks</a></i>, Clive Reader QC in the BBC One legal drama <i><a href="/wiki/Silk_(TV_series)" title="Silk (TV series)">Silk</a></i>, policeman DI Joseph Chandler in the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> murder mystery series <i><a href="/wiki/Whitechapel_(TV_series)" title="Whitechapel (TV series)">Whitechapel</a></i>, and Mr. Quinlan in the <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a> horror series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Strain_(TV_series)" title="The Strain (TV series)">The Strain</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rupert_Penry-Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Penry-Jones was born in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, the son of <a href="/wiki/Welsh_people" title="Welsh people">Welsh</a> actor <a href="/wiki/Peter_Penry-Jones" title="Peter Penry-Jones">Peter Penry-Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> actress <a href="/wiki/Angela_Thorne" title="Angela Thorne">Angela Thorne</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-1">[1]</a></sup> His brother, Laurence Penry-Jones, is an actor turned ambulance driver who is married to actress <a href="/wiki/Polly_Walker" title="Polly Walker">Polly Walker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>On BBC One's <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Do_You_Think_You_Are%3F_(UK_TV_series)" title="Who Do You Think You Are? (UK TV series)">Who Do You Think You Are?</a></i>, broadcast in August 2010, it was revealed that Penry-Jones' maternal grandfather, William, had served with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Army_Medical_Corps" title="Indian Army Medical Corps">Indian Army Medical Corps</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Monte_Cassino" title="Battle of Monte Cassino">Battle of Monte Cassino</a> and that his earlier ancestors had a long-standing connection with the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Army" title="Indian Army">Indian Army</a>. Penry-Jones also discovered that he had <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Indian" title="Anglo-Indian">Anglo-Indian</a> ancestry from the early 19th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Penry-Jones was educated at <a href="/wiki/Dulwich_College" title="Dulwich College">Dulwich College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dulwich" title="Dulwich">Dulwich, London</a>, until age 17 when he was enrolled at <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Old_Vic_Theatre_School" title="Bristol Old Vic Theatre School">Bristol Old Vic Theatre School</a>, only to be expelled in his second year for being a bad influence. His bad influence was a result of a broken relationship, which Rupert stated he tried to recover from by "shagging everything in sight". Being dyslexic, he struggled at school, eventually leaving without A-levels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rupert_Penry-Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1995 he appeared with his mother on television in <i><a href="/wiki/Cold_Comfort_Farm_(film)" title="Cold Comfort Farm (film)">Cold Comfort Farm</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He made his London stage debut at the <a href="/wiki/Hackney_Empire" title="Hackney Empire">Hackney Empire</a> theatre in 1995 playing Fortinbras to <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Fiennes" title="Ralph Fiennes">Ralph Fiennes</a>'s Hamlet in an <a href="/wiki/Almeida_Theatre" title="Almeida Theatre">Almeida</a> production of <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i>.</p>
<p>He was cast as Richard in the premiere staging of <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Poliakoff" title="Stephen Poliakoff">Stephen Poliakoff</a>'s <i>Sweet Panic</i> at <a href="/wiki/Hampstead_Theatre" title="Hampstead Theatre">Hampstead Theatre</a> in 1996. The following year he appeared in both <i>The Paper Husband</i> at Hampstead Theatre and as the upper-class Pip Thompson in a revival of <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Wesker" title="Arnold Wesker">Arnold Wesker</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Chips_with_Everything" title="Chips with Everything">Chips with Everything</a></i> on the Lyttelton stage at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">Royal National Theatre</a>.</p>
<p>In 1998, he created the role of the Boy in <a href="/wiki/Edward_Albee" title="Edward Albee">Edward Albee</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Play_About_the_Baby" title="The Play About the Baby">The Play About the Baby</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Almeida_Theatre" title="Almeida Theatre">Almeida Theatre</a>. In 1999, he joined the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Shakespeare_Company" title="Royal Shakespeare Company">Royal Shakespeare Company</a> at <a href="/wiki/Stratford-upon-Avon" title="Stratford-upon-Avon">Stratford-upon-Avon</a>, playing the title role in <i><a href="/wiki/Don_Carlos_(play)" title="Don Carlos (play)">Don Carlos</a></i> at <a href="/wiki/The_Other_Place_(theatre)" title="The Other Place (theatre)">The Other Place</a> theatre and Alcibiades in <i><a href="/wiki/Timon_of_Athens" title="Timon of Athens">Timon of Athens</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Shakespeare_Theatre" title="Royal Shakespeare Theatre">Royal Shakespeare Theatre</a>. Both productions transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Barbican_Centre" title="Barbican Centre">Barbican Centre</a> in London in 2000, where his performance as Don Carlos won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ian_Charleson_Award" title="Ian Charleson Award">Ian Charleson Award</a>.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Playhouse" title="West Yorkshire Playhouse">West Yorkshire Playhouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, in 2001 he was cast as Robert Caplan in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.B._Priestley" title="J.B. Priestley">J.B. Priestley</a>'s thriller "time-play" <i><a href="/wiki/Dangerous_Corner" title="Dangerous Corner">Dangerous Corner</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Dervla_Kirwan" title="Dervla Kirwan">Dervla Kirwan</a>, who played Olwen Peel. The production then transferred for a four-month run at the <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Theatre" title="Garrick Theatre">Garrick Theatre</a> in London's West End.</p>
<p>From July to October 2003 at the National's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cottesloe_Theatre" title="Cottesloe Theatre">Cottesloe Theatre</a> he played the leading role of Louis XIV in <a href="/wiki/Nick_Dear" title="Nick Dear">Nick Dear</a>'s historical drama <i>Power</i>.</p>
<p>He returned to the theatre at the end of 2009 playing the role of Carl in Michael Wynne's new play <i><a href="/wiki/The_Priory_(play)" title="The Priory (play)">The Priory</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Court_Theatre" title="Royal Court Theatre">Royal Court Theatre</a>, London, from 19 November 2009 to 16 January 2010.</p>
<p>On television, he has played barrister Alex Hay in C4's ten-part serial <i><a href="/wiki/North_Square" title="North Square">North Square</a></i> in 2000; Donald McLean in the BBC's four-part production of <i><a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Spies" title="Cambridge Spies">Cambridge Spies</a></i> in 2003; and Grimani in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russell_T._Davies" title="Russell T. Davies">Russell T. Davies</a>' production of <i><a href="/wiki/Casanova_(2005_TV_serial)" title="Casanova (2005 TV serial)">Casanova</a></i> in 2005.</p>
<p>In 2004, he joined the cast in series 3 of the BBC's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a>-winning series <i><a href="/wiki/Spooks_(TV_series)" title="Spooks (TV series)">Spooks</a></i>. He played the lead role of section leader <a href="/wiki/Adam_Carter" title="Adam Carter">Adam Carter</a> for four series before leaving the show in 2008. He won <a href="/wiki/Crime_Thriller_Awards" title="Crime Thriller Awards">ITV3 Crime Thriller Awards</a> for his role in <i>Spooks</i> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He also went on to play the role of Captain Wentworth in ITV's adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Persuasion_(2007_film)" title="Persuasion (2007 film)">Persuasion</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2008, he starred with <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Whitford" title="Bradley Whitford">Bradley Whitford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neve_Campbell" title="Neve Campbell">Neve Campbell</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Burn_Up_(miniseries)" title="Burn Up (miniseries)">Burn Up</a></i> playing an oil executive who becomes embroiled in the politics surrounding global warming and oil stocks.</p>
<p>He played <i><a href="/wiki/Richard_Hannay" title="Richard Hannay">Richard Hannay</a></i> in the BBC adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/The_39_Steps_(2008_film)" title="The 39 Steps (2008 film)">The 39 Steps</a></i> which was screened at Christmas 2008.</p>
<p>In 2009, he was cast as the lead in the unaired ABC pilot <i><a href="/wiki/The_Forgotten_(TV_series)" title="The Forgotten (TV series)">The Forgotten</a></i> but was unceremoniously replaced when the pilot was picked up and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Christian_Slater" title="Christian Slater">Christian Slater</a>. Penry-Jones was apparently devastated and proceeded to give a number of interviews in the UK in which he attacked the US television industry. He has since described American television as a "factory" and the producers as "disgusting".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In February 2009, he took the lead in an ITV drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Whitechapel_(TV_series)" title="Whitechapel (TV series)">Whitechapel</a></i>, a three-part thriller based on the <a href="/wiki/Copycat_crime" title="Copycat crime">copycat killings</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jack_the_Ripper" title="Jack the Ripper">Jack the Ripper</a>. <i>Whitechapel</i> was the highest-performing new drama in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> A second series of the show based around the <a href="/wiki/Kray_twins" title="Kray twins">Kray twins</a> was broadcast in autumn 2010; the third series began in January 2012. The fourth and last series aired in September 2013, and no further series were commissioned due to poor ratings.</p>
<p>He was scheduled to appear alongside other celebrities in <a href="/wiki/Soccer_Aid#2010:_Series_3" title="Soccer Aid">Soccer Aid 2010</a>, but broke a bone in his knee during training, putting him in a plaster cast and ruling him out of the final match on 6 June 2010.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Penry-Jones was also cast opposite <a href="/wiki/Maxine_Peake" title="Maxine Peake">Maxine Peake</a> in a legal drama <i><a href="/wiki/Silk_(TV_series)" title="Silk (TV series)">Silk</a></i> created by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Moffat" title="Peter Moffat">Peter Moffat</a>. The show revolves around two barristers, played by Penry-Jones and Peake who are competing to become <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Counsel" title="Queen's Counsel">QCs</a>. Series 2 aired in 2012 and Series 3 premiered on 24 February 2014.</p>
<p>He also joined the cast of the film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Little_Chaos" title="A Little Chaos">A Little Chaos</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a> as Antoine. The film was directed by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Rickman" title="Alan Rickman">Alan Rickman</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Penry-Jones is known to be very critical of the British television and film industry. He stated that <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i> is a "very good children's show... but has low production values...and is a pantomime". He also said the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a> films are "shit". He admitted walking out of the first three films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Red_Magazine.27_2010_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Red_Magazine.27_2010-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rupert_Penry-Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Penry-Jones married Irish actress <a href="/wiki/Dervla_Kirwan" title="Dervla Kirwan">Dervla Kirwan</a> in August 2007, following a three-year engagement. They met in a theatre production, <i><a href="/wiki/Dangerous_Corner" title="Dangerous Corner">Dangerous Corner</a></i>, in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-1">[1]</a></sup> They have two children, Peter and Florence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
