<p><b>Luhansk Oblast</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Луганська область</span>, <a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Ukrainian" title="Romanization of Ukrainian">translit.</a> <i>Luhanśka oblastj</i>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Луганская область</span>, <a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Russian" title="Romanization of Russian">translit.</a> <i>Luganskaja oblastj</i>; also referred to as <b>Luhanshchyna</b> – <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Луганщина, <i>Luhanščyna</i></span>) is the easternmost <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Ukraine" title="Administrative divisions of Ukraine">oblast</a> (province) of <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">administrative center</a> is <a href="/wiki/Luhansk" title="Luhansk">Luhansk</a>. The oblast was established in 1938 and bore the name <i>Voroshilovgrad Oblast</i> (until 1958 and again 1970 to 1990) in honor of <a href="/wiki/Kliment_Voroshilov" title="Kliment Voroshilov">Kliment Voroshilov</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">2,256,551 (2013 est.)</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ua2013estimate_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ua2013estimate-9">[9]</a></sup>.</p>
<p>Important cities within the oblast include: <a href="/wiki/Alchevsk" title="Alchevsk">Alchevsk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antratsyt" title="Antratsyt">Antratsyt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brianka" title="Brianka">Brianka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirovsk,_Luhansk_Oblast" title="Kirovsk, Luhansk Oblast">Kirovsk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Krasnyi_Luch" title="Krasnyi Luch">Krasnyi Luch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Krasnodon" title="Krasnodon">Krasnodon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lysychansk" title="Lysychansk">Lysychansk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luhansk" title="Luhansk">Luhansk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pervomaisk,_Luhansk_Oblast" title="Pervomaisk, Luhansk Oblast">Pervomaisk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rovenky" title="Rovenky">Rovenky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rubizhne" title="Rubizhne">Rubizhne</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sverdlovsk,_Ukraine" title="Sverdlovsk, Ukraine">Sverdlovsk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sievierodonetsk" title="Sievierodonetsk">Sievierodonetsk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stakhanov,_Ukraine" title="Stakhanov, Ukraine">Stakhanov</a>.</p>
<p>Due to the <a href="/wiki/War_in_Donbass" title="War in Donbass">War in Donbass</a>, the administrative center of the oblast was relocated to <a href="/wiki/Sievierodonetsk" title="Sievierodonetsk">Sievierodonetsk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luhansk_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lugansk_province_physical_map.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="791" data-file-width="531" height="328" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Lugansk_province_physical_map.svg/220px-Lugansk_province_physical_map.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Lugansk_province_physical_map.svg/330px-Lugansk_province_physical_map.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Lugansk_province_physical_map.svg/440px-Lugansk_province_physical_map.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lugansk_province_physical_map.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Geographic map</div>

<p>Luhansk Oblast is located in <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Ukraine" title="Eastern Ukraine">eastern Ukraine</a>. The area of the oblast (26,700 km²), comprises about 4.42% of the total area of the country.</p>
<p>Its longitude from north to south is 250 km, from east to west – 190 km. The oblast has the longest segment of the Ukrainian international border with Russia among other regions (see <a href="/wiki/State_Border_of_Ukraine" title="State Border of Ukraine">State Border of Ukraine</a>) consisting of 746 km (464 mi). It borders the <a href="/wiki/Belgorod_Oblast" title="Belgorod Oblast">Belgorod</a> and <a href="/wiki/Voronezh_Oblast" title="Voronezh Oblast">Voronezh Oblasts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> on the north, while the <a href="/wiki/Rostov_Oblast" title="Rostov Oblast">Rostov Oblast</a> is located to the east and the south. Among Ukrainian regions, the oblast borders the <a href="/wiki/Kharkiv_Oblast" title="Kharkiv Oblast">Kharkiv</a> and <a href="/wiki/Donetsk_Oblast" title="Donetsk Oblast">Donetsk Oblasts</a> to its west.</p>
<p>The region is located in the valley of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siversky_Donets" title="Siversky Donets">Siversky Donets</a> which splits the region approximately in half. The southern portion of the region is elevated by the Donetsk Ridge which is located closer to the southern border. The highest point is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mohyla_Mechetna&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mohyla Mechetna (page does not exist)">Mohyla Mechetna</a> (367 m (1,204 ft)) which is the highest point of Donetsk Ridge.</p>
<p>The left bank of Siversky Donets is part of the Starobilsk Plain which to the north transforms into the <a href="/wiki/Central_Russian_Upland" title="Central Russian Upland">Central Russian Upland</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luhansk_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Wild_Fields" title="Wild Fields">Wild Fields</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sloboda_Ukraine" title="Sloboda Ukraine">Sloboda Ukraine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slavo-Serbia" title="Slavo-Serbia">Slavo-Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yekaterinoslav_Governorate" title="Yekaterinoslav Governorate">Yekaterinoslav Governorate</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Donetsk_Oblast" title="Donetsk Oblast">Donetsk Oblast</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Don_Cossacks_monument_Luhansk.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4000" data-file-width="3000" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Don_Cossacks_monument_Luhansk.JPG/220px-Don_Cossacks_monument_Luhansk.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Don_Cossacks_monument_Luhansk.JPG/330px-Don_Cossacks_monument_Luhansk.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Don_Cossacks_monument_Luhansk.JPG/440px-Don_Cossacks_monument_Luhansk.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Don_Cossacks_monument_Luhansk.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A monument to <a href="/wiki/Don_Cossacks" title="Don Cossacks">Don Cossacks</a> in Luhansk. "To the sons of glory and freedom"</div>

<p>The oblast originated in 1938 as Voroshylovhrad (Russian: Voroshilovgrad) Oblast after the Donetsk Oblast was split between Voroshylovhrad and Stalino (today <a href="/wiki/Donetsk_Oblast" title="Donetsk Oblast">Donetsk Oblast</a>) oblasts. After the invasion by <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> in 1941 the region came under a German military administration, due to its proximity to frontlines.</p>
<p>During the Soviet times the Oblast bore its current name between 1958 and 1970. In the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_independence_referendum,_1991" title="Ukrainian independence referendum, 1991">1991 referendum</a>, 83.86% of votes in the oblast were in favor of the <a href="/wiki/Declaration_of_Independence_of_Ukraine" title="Declaration of Independence of Ukraine">Declaration of Independence of Ukraine</a>.</p>
<p>In 1994 a referendum took place in the <a href="/wiki/Donetsk_Oblast" title="Donetsk Oblast">Donetsk Oblast</a> and the Luhansk Oblast, with around 90% supporting the <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian language</a> gaining status of an official language alongside <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>, and for the <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian language</a> to be an official language on a regional level; however, the referendum was annulled by the <a href="/wiki/Kiev" title="Kiev">Kiev</a> government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 8, 2014, following the <a href="/wiki/Annexation_of_Crimea_by_the_Russian_Federation" title="Annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation">annexation of Crimea by Russia</a>, pro-Russian separatists occupying the Luhansk Oblast administrative building planned to declare the independence of the region as the Luhansk Parliamentary Republic, after other pro-Russian separatists declared <a href="/wiki/Donetsk_People%27s_Republic" title="Donetsk People's Republic">Donetsk People's Republic</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Donetsk_Oblast" title="Donetsk Oblast">Donetsk Oblast</a> (April 7, 2014). When the Luhansk Parliamentary Republic ceased to exist, the separatists declared the <a href="/wiki/Luhansk_People%27s_Republic" title="Luhansk People's Republic">Luhansk People's Republic</a> (April 27, 2014) and held a disputed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Donetsk_and_Luhansk_status_referendums,_2014" title="Donetsk and Luhansk status referendums, 2014">referendum</a> on separating from Ukraine on May 11, 2014. The legitimacy of the referendums was not recognized by any government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Referendum_on_joining_Russia_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Referendum_on_joining_Russia-13">[13]</a></sup> Ukraine does not recognize the referendum, while the EU and US said the polls were illegal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Subsequently, the <a href="/wiki/War_in_Donbass" title="War in Donbass">War in Donbass</a> started.</p>
<p>As a result of the <a href="/wiki/War_in_Donbass" title="War in Donbass">War in Donbass</a>, Luhansk insurgents control the southern third of the oblast, which includes the city of <a href="/wiki/Luhansk" title="Luhansk">Luhansk</a>, the region's most populous city as well as the capital of the oblast. Due to this, most oblast government functions have moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Severodonetsk" title="Severodonetsk">Severodonetsk</a>, which forces of the Government of Ukraine recaptured in July 2014. Many universities located in the occupied areas have moved to government-controlled cities such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Severodonetsk" title="Severodonetsk">Severodonetsk</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starobelsk" title="Starobelsk">Starobelsk</a> or <a href="/wiki/Rubizhne" title="Rubizhne">Rubizhne</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> A survey conducted in December 2014 by the Kyiv International Institute of Sociology found 5.7% of the oblast's population supported their region joining Russia, 84.1% did not support the idea, and the rest were undecided or did not respond; insurgent-controlled areas were not polled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT150103_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-DT150103-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<p>Like the rest of the provinces in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, Luhansk Oblast has double jurisdiction. The oblast is predominantly administrated by the Luhansk Oblast State Administration headed by the <a href="/wiki/Chief_of_Local_State_Administration_(Ukraine)" title="Chief of Local State Administration (Ukraine)">governor of the oblast</a> and appointed by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ukraine" title="President of Ukraine">President of Ukraine</a>. The province also has a representative body, the provincial council, which is headed by its chairman and elected by popular vote.</p>
<p>The province is primarily divided into 18 <i><a href="/wiki/Raion" title="Raion">raions</a></i> (districts), and 37 cities, including 14 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cities_of_regional_significance_(Ukraine)" title="Cities of regional significance (Ukraine)">cities of regional significance</a>. The administrative center is <a href="/wiki/Luhansk" title="Luhansk">Luhansk</a>. These are listed below with their areas and populations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The province's secondary division consists of various municipalities. Those municipalities may consist of one or more populated places. The municipalities are administratively subordinate to the raion in which they are located, with the exception of 14 cities subordinated directly to the oblast. In addition, the city of Luhansk is subdivided into its own four city-districts (boroughs).</p>
<p>All subdivisions are governed by their respective councils (<i>radas</i>).</p>

<p>The population is largely <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>-speaking, although ethnic <a href="/wiki/Ukrainians" title="Ukrainians">Ukrainians</a> constitute a majority (58.0%). Among the minorities are native Russians (39.1%), Belarusians (0.8%), and others (1.4%). Ukrainians constitute the majority in all raions except for <a href="/wiki/Stanytsia-Luhanska_Raion" title="Stanytsia-Luhanska Raion">Stanytsia-Luhanska Raion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Krasnodon_Raion" title="Krasnodon Raion">Krasnodon Raion</a>, both of which are east of Luhansk. Ethnic <a href="/wiki/Russians" title="Russians">Russians</a> also constitute the majority in regionally significant cities, such as <a href="/wiki/Krasnodon" title="Krasnodon">Krasnodon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sverdlovsk,_Ukraine" title="Sverdlovsk, Ukraine">Sverdlovsk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Krasnyi_Luch" title="Krasnyi Luch">Krasnyi Luch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stakhanov,_Ukraine" title="Stakhanov, Ukraine">Stakhanov</a>.</p>
<p>According to the 2001 Ukrainian Census, more than 68.8% of the population consider themselves Russian speakers, while 30.0% consider themselves Ukrainian speakers. The Russophone population predominates in the southern portion of the region and around the city of Luhansk, while the northern region is less populated, mostly agricultural and Ukrainophone.</p>
<p>Its population (as of 2004) of 2,461,506 constitutes 5.13% of the overall Ukrainian population. The Luhansk Oblast rates fifth in Ukraine by the number of its inhabitants, having an average population density of 90.28/km². About 87% of the population lives in urban areas, while the remaining 13% reside in agricultural areas. According to the national census, 54% of the population are Ukrainians and 42% are Russians.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luhansk_Oblast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Through the region pass two major European routes <a href="/wiki/European_route_E50" title="European route E50"><img alt="" data-file-height="130" data-file-width="210" height="20" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Tabliczka_E50.svg/33px-Tabliczka_E50.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Tabliczka_E50.svg/49px-Tabliczka_E50.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Tabliczka_E50.svg/65px-Tabliczka_E50.svg.png 2x" width="33"/></a> and <a href="/wiki/European_route_E40" title="European route E40"><img alt="" data-file-height="130" data-file-width="210" height="20" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Tabliczka_E40.svg/33px-Tabliczka_E40.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Tabliczka_E40.svg/49px-Tabliczka_E40.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Tabliczka_E40.svg/65px-Tabliczka_E40.svg.png 2x" width="33"/></a>. There are 24 Russo-Ukrainian international <a href="/wiki/Russia%E2%80%93Ukraine_border#Luhansk_.E2.80.93_Belgorod" title="Russia–Ukraine border">border checkpoints</a> of various entry.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/European_route_E50" title="European route E50"><img alt="" data-file-height="130" data-file-width="210" height="20" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Tabliczka_E50.svg/33px-Tabliczka_E50.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Tabliczka_E50.svg/49px-Tabliczka_E50.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Tabliczka_E50.svg/65px-Tabliczka_E50.svg.png 2x" width="33"/></a> within the Luhansk Oblast uses highway <a href="/wiki/Highway_M03_(Ukraine)" title="Highway M03 (Ukraine)"><img alt="" data-file-height="76" data-file-width="156" height="15" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/M03-UA.svg/30px-M03-UA.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/M03-UA.svg/45px-M03-UA.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/M03-UA.svg/60px-M03-UA.svg.png 2x" width="30"/></a> that starts from <a href="/wiki/Debaltseve" title="Debaltseve">Debaltseve</a> (<a href="/wiki/Donetsk_Oblast" title="Donetsk Oblast">Donetsk Oblast</a>), passes through the city of <a href="/wiki/Krasnyi_Luch" title="Krasnyi Luch">Krasnyi Luch</a>, and enters the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federation" title="Russian Federation">Russian Federation</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Dovzhansky_(border_checkpoint)" title="Dovzhansky (border checkpoint)">border checkpoint "Dovzhansky"</a> (settlement Dovzhanske, town of Biryukove).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/European_route_E40" title="European route E40"><img alt="" data-file-height="130" data-file-width="210" height="20" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Tabliczka_E40.svg/33px-Tabliczka_E40.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Tabliczka_E40.svg/49px-Tabliczka_E40.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Tabliczka_E40.svg/65px-Tabliczka_E40.svg.png 2x" width="33"/></a> within the Luhansk Oblast uses highway <a href="/wiki/Highway_M04_(Ukraine)" title="Highway M04 (Ukraine)"><img alt="" data-file-height="76" data-file-width="156" height="15" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/M04-UA.svg/30px-M04-UA.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/M04-UA.svg/45px-M04-UA.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/M04-UA.svg/60px-M04-UA.svg.png 2x" width="30"/></a> that starts from <a href="/wiki/Debaltseve" title="Debaltseve">Debaltseve</a> (<a href="/wiki/Donetsk_Oblast" title="Donetsk Oblast">Donetsk Oblast</a>), passes through the city of <a href="/wiki/Luhansk" title="Luhansk">Luhansk</a>, and enters the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federation" title="Russian Federation">Russian Federation</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Izvaryne_(border_checkpoint)" title="Izvaryne (border checkpoint)">border checkpoint "Izvaryne"</a> (town of Izvaryne).</li>
<li>There is also another highway <a href="/wiki/Highway_H21_(Ukraine)" title="Highway H21 (Ukraine)"><span style="padding: 1px .5em; color: white; background-color: #0057b8; font-weight: bold;">H21</span></a> that runs from north to south and connects <a href="/wiki/Starobilsk" title="Starobilsk">Starobilsk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luhansk" title="Luhansk">Luhansk</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Krasnyi_Luch" title="Krasnyi Luch">Krasnyi Luch</a> with <a href="/wiki/Donetsk" title="Donetsk">Donetsk</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Rail transportation is administered by the Donetsk Railway.</p>
<p>There is also its regional airport <a href="/wiki/Luhansk_International_Airport" title="Luhansk International Airport">Luhansk International Airport</a> with its own carrier.</p>
