<p>The <b>Luxembourg Grand Prix</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Großer Preis von Luxemburg</i></span>) was the name given to two races of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIA" title="FIA">FIA</a> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One World Championship</a>, held in <a href="/wiki/1997_Formula_One_season" title="1997 Formula One season">1997</a> and <a href="/wiki/1998_Formula_One_season" title="1998 Formula One season">1998</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIA" title="FIA">FIA</a> rulings for <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> stipulate that no country be allowed more than one race. However, the FIA has got around this ruling in the past by running <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_motor_racing" title="Grand Prix motor racing">Grands Prix</a> under another name; although the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Imola circuit</a> is not in <a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">San Marino</a>, races held there have been run under the title of the <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a> as the circuit is nearby.</p>
<p>In 1997, there were two Grands Prix in Spain and two in Germany. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Barcelona</a> hosted the <a href="/wiki/1997_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1997 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> whilst <a href="/wiki/Circuito_de_Jerez" title="Circuito de Jerez">Jerez</a> hosted the <a href="/wiki/1997_European_Grand_Prix" title="1997 European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a>; in Germany, <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheim</a> hosted the <a href="/wiki/1997_German_Grand_Prix" title="1997 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> and a second race was planned for the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>. The FIA decided to name the race the Luxembourg Grand Prix as the circuit was located some 80 kilometres (50 miles) from the Germany-Luxembourg border, thereby circumventing the ban on two races having the same name. The 1998 race was also known as the Luxembourg Grand Prix despite no European Grand Prix taking place; the rights to the European Grand Prix had been revoked from the organisers of the race after an incident on the podium in 1997, and they refused to allow the Nürburgring to use the title. From <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_season" title="1999 Formula One season">1999</a> on, the Nürburgring hosted its race under the title of the European Grand Prix. From 2008–2012 it was no longer called "The European Grand Prix" as it was replaced by the race being held at the Valencia Street Circuit, returning to its main title "The German Grand Prix".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1997">1997</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1997">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1997_Luxembourg_Grand_Prix" title="1997 Luxembourg Grand Prix">1997 Luxembourg Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>As it was, the Luxembourg Grand Prix provided a moment in history, as <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> powered cars took the first four places at the finish with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> (<a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>) taking first place. The race was also Villeneuve's final F1 victory.</p>
<p>For a long time it looked as if <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> would take his first F1 win as he pulled away at the front from his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team-mate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>. However in the space of one lap both McLarens had pulled out of the race with blown engines allowing Villeneuve to move close to an eventual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Championship" title="World Championship">World Championship</a>. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s race was over by the end of the first lap after brother <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> collided with his team-mate at the first corner causing suspension damage and retirement to the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1998">1998</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luxembourg_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1998">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1998_Luxembourg_Grand_Prix" title="1998 Luxembourg Grand Prix">1998 Luxembourg Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>1998 saw Mika Häkkinen gain revenge for his engine failure at the previous race by taking victory at this one, with Michael Schumacher second despite qualifying on pole, and team-mate Coulthard third. Häkkinen went on to win the World Championship in the <a href="/wiki/1998_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1998 Japanese Grand Prix">final race</a> at <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a>; interestingly, this means that every winner of the Luxembourg GP went on to win that year's World Championship.</p>
