<p>The <b>National Division</b> (<a href="/wiki/Luxembourgish_language" title="Luxembourgish language">Luxembourgish</a>: <span lang="lb" xml:lang="lb"><i>Nationaldivisioun</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Division Nationale</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Nationaldivision</i></span>) is the highest <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> league in Luxembourg. Until 2011, it was known as the <b>BGL Ligue</b>, after the <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_Football_Federation" title="Luxembourg Football Federation">Luxembourg Football Federation</a> managed to seal a sponsorship deal with <a href="/wiki/Fortis_(finance)" title="Fortis (finance)">Fortis</a>. Before 2006, it contained twelve teams, but it expanded to fourteen for the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Luxembourg_National_Division" title="2006â07 Luxembourg National Division">2006â07 season</a>, and it has maintained this number since. The current champions are <a href="/wiki/F91_Dudelange" title="F91 Dudelange">F91 Dudelange</a>.</p>
<p>The competition was first held in 1910, and has been held every year since, with the exceptions of 1913 and four seasons during the Second World War. The competition was called in Luxembourgish and French <i>LÃ«tzebuerger Championnat / Championnat Luxembourgeois</i> until 1913â14. From 1914â15 until 1931â32, the title was <i>Ãischt Divisioun / PremiÃ¨re Division</i>. It was called <i>Ãirendivisioun / Division d'Honneur</i> from 1932â33 to 1956â57. Since the 1957â58 season, the competition has been called the <i>Nationaldivisioun / Division Nationale</i>.</p>
