<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Teresa,_Grand_Duchess_of_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maria Teresa, Grand Duchess of Luxembourg, was born in <a href="/wiki/Marianao" title="Marianao">Marianao</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_Habana_Province" title="La Habana Province">Havana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, to José Antonio Mestre y Álvarez (<a href="/wiki/Vedado" title="Vedado">Vedado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>, 1926 - 1993) and wife (m. <a href="/wiki/Vedado" title="Vedado">Vedado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>, 1951) María Teresa Batista y Falla de Mestre (<a href="/wiki/Vedado" title="Vedado">Vedado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>, 1928–1988), both from <a href="/wiki/Bourgeoisie" title="Bourgeoisie">bourgeois</a> families of Spanish descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Mestre family (descended in the female line from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinand_I_of_Le%C3%B3n_and_Castile" title="Ferdinand I of León and Castile">Ferdinand I of León and Castile</a>) have as their patriarch Arnau Mestre, born <a href="/wiki/Landorthe" title="Landorthe">Landorthe</a>, who married in 1625 in San Pedro de Ribas. One of his descendants, Francisco Mestre y Roig Benaprés (born in 1787 in Sitges) travelled to Cuba in 1830 where he married Josefa Dominguez y Morales (born in 1764, daughter of Andres Domínguez Bencomo and Manuela Morales Ponce de León), being the founder of the Mestre family in Cuba. The Grand Duchess also descends through her father from the Spanish <a href="/wiki/Espinosa_de_los_Monteros" title="Espinosa de los Monteros">Espinosa de los Monteros</a> noble family.</p>
<p>Her maternal grandparents were Don Agustín Batista y González de Mendoza (Batista family member with possessions in the town of Puerto de Santa María del Príncipe and no family relationship with <a href="/wiki/Fulgencio_Batista" title="Fulgencio Batista">Fulgencio Batista</a>), Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Trust Company of Cuba and Doña María Teresa Falla Bonet, daughter of the Spanish tycoon Laureano Falla Gutierrez, millionaire businessman whose fortune was made up of several sugar mills, two banks (one of which is "The Trust Company of Cuba") and other goods which were confiscated by the government of the Revolution.</p>
<p>In October 1959, at the time of the <a href="/wiki/Cuban_Revolution" title="Cuban Revolution">revolution</a>, Maria Teresa Mestre left Cuba with her parents. The family settled in New York City, where, as a young girl, she was a pupil at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marymount_School,_New_York" title="Marymount School, New York">Marymount School</a>. From 1961 she carried on her studies at the <a href="/wiki/Lyc%C3%A9e_Fran%C3%A7ais_de_New_York" title="Lycée Français de New York">Lycée Français de New York</a>. In her childhood, Maria Teresa Mestre took <a href="/wiki/Ballet" title="Ballet">ballet</a> and singing courses. She practices <a href="/wiki/Skiing" title="Skiing">skiing</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ice-skating" title="Ice-skating">ice-skating</a> and water sports. She lived in New York City, <a href="/wiki/Santander,_Spain" title="Santander, Spain">Santander, Spain</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>, where she met her future husband.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1980 she graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Graduate_Institute_of_International_and_Development_Studies" title="Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies">Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies</a> in Geneva with a degree in <a href="/wiki/Political_science" title="Political science">political sciences</a>. Miss Mestre met Prince Henri at this university. For four years they both pursued similar studies, working sometimes in the same study groups.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_and_humanitarian_interests">Social and humanitarian interests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Teresa,_Grand_Duchess_of_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Social and humanitarian interests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the same period she took an interest in social and human problems, following the family tradition which had allegedly brought her grandparents to devote themselves to <a href="/wiki/Philanthropy" title="Philanthropy">philanthropy</a> and cultural tasks in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Besides her studies, Miss Mestre was a member of a group in Geneva taking interest in the well-being of residents of retirement homes. She also taught children in a Geneva classroom.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>As a Cuban, the Grand Duchess's first language is Spanish but she is fluent in French and English, having been raised in the United States and attended a <i>Lycée Français</i> and also studied in Switzerland. After her marriage she learned <a href="/wiki/Luxembourgish_language" title="Luxembourgish language">Luxembourgish</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and she also speaks German and Italian.</p>
<p>In 2006, the Grand Duchess received the <i>Path to Peace Award</i> from a <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a> Foundation for her "relentless contribution to humanitarian causes."</p>
<p>She is the President of the <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_Red_Cross" title="Luxembourg Red Cross">Luxembourg Red Cross</a>.</p>
<p>On 19 April 2007, the Grand Duchess was appointed UNICEF Eminent Advocate for Children,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in which role she has visited Brazil (2007),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> China (2008),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and Burundi (2009).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is a member of the Honorary Board of the <a href="/wiki/International_Paralympic_Committee" title="International Paralympic Committee">International Paralympic Committee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Grand Duchess is afraid of flying. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Teresa,_Grand_Duchess_of_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles_and_styles">Titles and styles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Teresa,_Grand_Duchess_of_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Titles and styles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From her marriage until her husband's accession on 7 October 2000 she was styled <i>Her Royal Highness</i> The Hereditary Grand Duchess of Luxembourg. Since then, she has been known as <i>Her Royal Highness</i> The Grand Duchess of Luxembourg.</p>
