<p>The <b>flag of <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Luxembourgish_language" title="Luxembourgish language">Luxembourgish</a>: <span lang="lb"><i>Lëtzebuerger Fändel</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de"><i>Flagge Luxemburgs</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>Drapeau du Luxembourg</i></span>) consists of three horizontal stripes, red, white and blue, and can be in 1:2 or 3:5 ratio. It was first used between 1845 and 1848 and officially adopted on June 23, 1972.</p>
<p>Luxembourg had no flag until 1830, when patriots were urged to display the national colors. The flag was defined as a horizontal tricolor of red, white, and blue in 1848, but it was not officially adopted until 1972. The tricolor flag is almost identical to <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_Netherlands" title="Flag of the Netherlands">that of the Netherlands</a>, except that it is longer and its blue stripe is a lighter shade. The red, white, and blue coloring was derived from the coat of arms of the county of the Ardennes, renamed county (1339 duchy, 1815 grand-duchy) of Luxembourg after the creation of its identical named capital city in 963 A.D. by then count Siegfried.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The colours of the flag of Luxembourg were first adopted around 1830 during the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Revolution" title="Belgian Revolution">Belgian Revolution</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> They were probably derived from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_of_Luxembourg" title="County of Luxembourg">counts, dukes and later grand-dukes of Luxembourg</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Luxembourg" title="Coat of arms of Luxembourg">coat of arms</a> which in turn was derived from the combination of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Limburg" title="Duchy of Limburg">dukes of Limbourg</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lion_(heraldry)" title="Lion (heraldry)">Lion</a> and the supposed striped <a href="/wiki/Banner" title="Banner">banner</a> of the early counts of Luxembourg.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The three-colored horizontal design was fixed on June 12, 1845.</p>
<p>It took until June 23, 1972 before a law was passed regulating the flag of Luxembourg. The same law also prescribed ensign and roundel for aircraft and ships registered in Luxembourg.</p>
<p>One important clarification brought by this law was that the color blue was defined as being a very bright blue, in contrast to the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_Netherlands" title="Flag of the Netherlands">flag of the Netherlands</a> (exactly the same design, but the Dutch flag uses dark blue and a less <a href="/wiki/Rectangle" title="Rectangle">oblong</a> shape).</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">heraldric</a> <a href="/wiki/Blazon" title="Blazon">blazon</a> for the flag is <i>per fess Gules and Azure, a fess Argent</i></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ensign">Ensign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ensign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The red lion is the official ensign for ships registered in Luxembourg. It is used for river and sea shipping, as well as for <a href="/wiki/Civil_air_ensign" title="Civil air ensign">aviation</a>. This flag is based on the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Luxembourg" title="Coat of arms of Luxembourg">coat of arms of Luxembourg</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Banner_of_arms" title="Banner of arms">banner of arms</a>) and is used as the ensign to avoid the possibility of Luxembourg's ensign being confused for that of the Netherlands. Ten white and blue stripes serve as the field for a red lion with a yellow tongue, claws and crown. The proper ratio for this ensign is 5:7.</p>
<p>The <i>Réglement Grand-Ducal</i> of July 27, 1993 defined the ensign's colors as:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Red: Pantone 032 c</li>
<li>White:</li>
<li>Blue: Pantone 299 c</li>
<li>Yellow: Pantone 116c</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Roundel">Roundel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roundel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="floatright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Roundel_of_Luxembourg.svg"><img alt="Roundel of Luxembourg.svg" data-file-height="304" data-file-width="304" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Roundel_of_Luxembourg.svg/140px-Roundel_of_Luxembourg.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Roundel_of_Luxembourg.svg/210px-Roundel_of_Luxembourg.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Roundel_of_Luxembourg.svg/280px-Roundel_of_Luxembourg.svg.png 2x" width="140"/></a></div>
<p>The roundel of Luxembourg is seen on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Airborne_Early_Warning_and_Control" title="Airborne Early Warning and Control">AWACS</a> aircraft used by <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flag_change_debate">Flag change debate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Flag change debate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The resemblance of the Luxembourgish flag with the Dutch flag has given rise to a national debate to change it.</p>
<p>On October 5, 2006, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">MP</a> <a href="/wiki/Michel_Wolter" title="Michel Wolter">Michel Wolter</a> introduced a legislative proposition<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> to replace the current red-white-blue national flag with the red lion ensign. He argued that the current flag was commonly confused with that of the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_Netherlands" title="Flag of the Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and that the red lion on the other hand was more popular, more aesthetic and of greater historic value. Wolter also claimed he had personally discussed the matter with some three hundred people, most of whom expressed their support for his initiative. On the other hand, many national politicians (including leading members of Wolter's own <a href="/wiki/Christian_Social_People%27s_Party" title="Christian Social People's Party">CSV</a>)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (January 2010)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> and VIPs<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (January 2010)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> have expressed astonishment in the local media concerning both the timing and necessity for such a change.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The only political party to express support for Wolter's initiative was the nationalist and populist <a href="/wiki/Alternative_Democratic_Reform_Party" title="Alternative Democratic Reform Party">ADR</a>.</p>
<p>On October 24, 2006, a local initiative called <i>Initiativ Roude Léiw</i> ("Red Lion Initiative") held a press conference explaining their intention to support Wolter's project on a non-partisan basis.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Their first actions would include distribution of red lion bumper stickers, a petition and a poll. During sporting events like the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a> supporters for Luxembourg participants now overwhelmingly use the Ensign instead of the Flag.</p>
<p>In 2015, a citizen submitted a formal petition to the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_(Luxembourg)" title="Chamber of Deputies (Luxembourg)">Chamber of Deputies</a> to replace the current national flag with the <i>Roude Léiw</i>. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.wort.lu/en/politics/petition-change-the-luxembourg-flag-55ba15ab0c88b46a8ce5d86e" rel="nofollow">[1]</a> <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.chd.lu/wps/portal/public/PetitionDetail?action=doPetitionDetail&amp;id=510" rel="nofollow">[2]</a></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Partitions_of_Luxembourg" title="Partitions of Luxembourg">For historic reasons</a>, the adjacent <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_(Belgium)" title="Luxembourg (Belgium)">Belgian province of Luxembourg</a> uses a flag that also features a <a href="/wiki/Lion_(heraldry)" title="Lion (heraldry)">lion</a> superimposed on <a href="/wiki/Bar_(heraldry)" title="Bar (heraldry)">horizontal stripes</a>. Elsewhere, similar designs can also be found, for instance in the Belgian city of <a href="/wiki/Bruges" title="Bruges">Bruges</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_state" title="German state">German state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hesse" title="Hesse">Hesse</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Two_Civil_official_flags">Two Civil official flags</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Luxembourg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Two Civil official flags">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 6, 2007, the Government stated that the <i>Roude Léiw</i> would be accepted as a Civil flag only within the territory of the Grand-Duchy along with the National flag keeping the same proportions, i.e. 1:2 or 3:5 as the National flag to avoid confusion with the Civil ensign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
