<p><b>Lunda Sul</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Angola" title="Provinces of Angola">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>. It has an area of 77,637 km² and a 2014 census population of 516,077.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GH_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-GH-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Saurimo" title="Saurimo">Saurimo</a> is the capital of the province.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunda_Sul_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lunda Sul province is located in the extreme east of Angola, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Saurimo" title="Saurimo">Saurimo</a> located 946 kilometres (588 mi) by road east of the capital <a href="/wiki/Luanda" title="Luanda">Luanda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GM_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GM-2">[2]</a></sup> It is bordered to the north by <a href="/wiki/Lunda_Norte_Province" title="Lunda Norte Province">Lunda Norte Province</a>, to the east by the Democratic Republic of the Congo, to the south by <a href="/wiki/Moxico_Province" title="Moxico Province">Moxico Province</a> and southwest by <a href="/wiki/Bi%C3%A9_Province" title="Bié Province">Bié Province</a>, and to the west by <a href="/wiki/Malanje_Province" title="Malanje Province">Malanje Province</a>. Municipalities include <a href="/wiki/Cacolo" title="Cacolo">Cacolo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dala,_Angola" title="Dala, Angola">Dala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muconda" title="Muconda">Muconda</a>, and Saurimo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GH_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-GH-1">[1]</a></sup> The main road from Luanda to <a href="/wiki/Lubumbashi" title="Lubumbashi">Lubumbashi</a> traverses the province from west to east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GM_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GM-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The province is dominated by dry <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Savannah" title="Savannah">savannah</a> land; only in the <a href="/wiki/Kasai_River" title="Kasai River">Kasai River</a> valley are there remnants of tropical rainforest. The Kasai forms the eastern and southern frontier of Lunda Sul and is the main river of the province. The <a href="/wiki/Kwango_River" title="Kwango River">Kwango River</a> is also a major river of the province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Africa2009_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Africa2009-3">[3]</a></sup> The climate of the province is predominantly tropical.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy_and_social_issues">Economy and social issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunda_Sul_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy and social issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Economically the province is dominated by <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">peanut</a> cultivation, which is operated primarily in the Saurimo area. In the south of the province <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corn" title="Corn">corn</a> production is major contributing factor. Other agricultural products include <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">cassava</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cereal" title="Cereal">cereals</a>. This region is rich with diamonds,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kolapo2009_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kolapo2009-4">[4]</a></sup> manganese and iron which are exploited; Catoca mine in Lunda Sul Province is the fourth largest diamond mine in the world. Due to warfare in the region, at times mining has been disrupted by attacks form the <a href="/wiki/UNITA" title="UNITA">UNITA</a>, especially in 1999-2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weigert2011_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Weigert2011-5">[5]</a></sup> In spring 1999, UNITA destroyed the bridge on the Kasai River at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biula" title="Biula">Biula</a>, affecting transport communications in the region between Lunda Sul and Moxico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kukkuk2004_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kukkuk2004-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Like in many other areas of Angola, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landmines" title="Landmines">landmines</a> are a serious problem, and it is described as being "severely mined". As of 1995, some 30 major or strategic bridges and 58 secondary bridges had been destroyed in the province according to <a href="/wiki/Oxfam" title="Oxfam">Oxfam</a> reports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RobertsWilliams1995_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-RobertsWilliams1995-7">[7]</a></sup> "Forced return and restrictions to freedom of movement" by people has been noted in the province, and the <a href="/wiki/Huambo_Province" title="Huambo Province">Huambo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cuando_Cubango_Province" title="Cuando Cubango Province">Cuando Cubango</a> provinces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Watch2003_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Watch2003-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lunda_Sul_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The origin of the province's population belongs to the ethnic groups of the <a href="/wiki/Lunda_people" title="Lunda people">Lunda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chokwe_people" title="Chokwe people">Chokwe</a> people, which are closely connected historically. They are mainly involved in agriculture. There are also many immigrants from other parts of the country, working for the <a href="/wiki/Endiama" title="Endiama">Endiama</a> or as independent diamond miners. The struggle between the Lunda and Chokwe to obtain wealth from the diamond industry has increased tension in the province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hodges2001_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hodges2001-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
