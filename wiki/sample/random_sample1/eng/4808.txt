<p><b>Room to Read</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-profit" title="Non-profit">non-profit</a> organization for improving <a href="/wiki/Literacy" title="Literacy">literacy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gender_equality" title="Gender equality">gender equality</a> in education in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developing_world" title="Developing world">developing world</a>. Headquartered in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, and founded on the belief that "World Change Starts With Educated Children," the organization focuses on working in collaboration with local communities, partner organizations and governments. Room to Read develops literacy skills and the habit of reading among primary school children, and supports girls in completing secondary school with the relevant <a href="/wiki/Life_skills" title="Life skills">life skills</a> to succeed in school and beyond.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_Us_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-About_Us-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Room to Read is serving communities in ten countries in Asia and Africa: <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Room_to_Read&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Wood_(Room_to_Read)" title="John Wood (Room to Read)">John Wood</a>, founder and board co-chair, launched Room to Read in 1999 after a trek through Nepal where he visited several local schools. He was amazed by the warmth and enthusiasm of the students and teachers, but saddened by the shocking lack of resources. Driven to help, John quit his senior executive position with <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> and built a global team to work with rural villages to build sustainable solutions to their education challenges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_Us_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-About_Us-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (July 2016)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Beginning in Nepal, John and his Nepali Co-Founder, Dinesh Shrestha, started by working with rural communities to build schools (School Room) and establish libraries (Reading Room). John and Dinesh quickly recognized the need to expand the scope of work beyond libraries, and wanted to address the fact that many girls in the developing world are overlooked in the educational system due to cultural bias. To that end, in 2000, Room to Read began the Girls' Education program, which targets young girls and provides a long-term commitment to their education.</p>
<p>In 2001, co-founder and CEO Erin Keown Ganju spearheaded Room to Read’s expansion into <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Since then, Room to Read's operations have expanded to include Bangladesh, Cambodia, India, Laos, South Africa, Sri Lanka and Zambia. In 2010, Room to Read celebrated its "Year of Tens," marking its ten-year anniversary with the opening of its 10,000th library in Nepal, along with the construction of its 1,000th school and support of its 10,000th girl through the Girls' Education Program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (July 2016)">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chapter_network">Chapter network</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Room_to_Read&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chapter network">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Room to Read has all-volunteer fundraising chapters in:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> United States: Aspen Valley, CO; Atlanta, GA; Austin, TX; Boston, MA; Central NJ; Charlotte, NC; Chicago, IL; Cincinnati, OH; Columbus, OH; Dallas, TX; Denver / Boulder, CO; Fort Lauderdale, FL; Greenwich, CT; Houston, TX; Los Angeles, CA; Manchester, NH; Minneapolis / St. Paul, MN; Nashville, TN; New York, NY; Portland, OR; Salt Lake City/Wasatch, UT; San Diego, CA; San Francisco, CA; Seattle, WA; St. Louis, MO; Washington, DC; and Westchester, NY.</p>
<p>Canada: Calgary, AB; Edmonton, AB; Toronto, ON; Montreal, QC; and Vancouver, BC.</p>
<p>Asia: Hong Kong, China; Seoul, South Korea; Tokyo, Japan; Mumbai, India; Singapore.</p>
<p>Middle East: United Arab Emirates</p>
<p>Australasia: Adelaide, Auckland, Brisbane, Canberra, Melbourne, Perth, Sydney.</p>
<p>Europe: Edinburgh, Scotland; London, England; Brussels, Belgium; Paris, France; Munich, Germany; Rhein-Main, Germany; Amsterdam, The Netherlands; Zurich, Switzerland.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Room_to_Read&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since its inception in 2000, Room to Read has impacted the lives of over 10 million children in the developing world by:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Constructing 1,450 schools;</li>
<li>Establishing 12,522 libraries;</li>
<li>Publishing 591 new local language children's titles;</li>
<li>Distributing over 10,4 million children's books;</li>
<li>Funding 13,662 long-term girls' scholarships.</li>
</ul>
