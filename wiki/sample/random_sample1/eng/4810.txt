<p><b>Rumphi</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Malawi" title="Districts of Malawi">district</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Region,_Malawi" title="Northern Region, Malawi">Northern Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Malawi" title="Malawi">Malawi</a>. The capital is <a href="/wiki/Rumphi" title="Rumphi">Rumphi</a>. The district covers an area of 4,769 km.² and has a population of 128,360.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_administrative_divisions">Government and administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rumphi_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Government and administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Malawi" title="National Assembly of Malawi">National Assembly</a> constituencies in Rumphi:</p>
<ul>
<li>Rumphi - East</li>
<li>Rumphi - North</li>
<li>Rumphi - West</li>
</ul>
<p>Since the <a href="/wiki/Malawian_general_election,_2009" title="Malawian general election, 2009">2009 election</a> all of these constituencies have been held by members of an independent party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_residents">Notable residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rumphi_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notable residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rumphi has produced some notable residents like:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Edgar_Chibaka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Edgar Chibaka (page does not exist)">Edgar Chibaka</a>, founder and Managing Editor of <a class="external free" href="http://www.nyasatimes.com" rel="nofollow">http://www.nyasatimes.com</a> one of Malawi's top online and print newspaper.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chakufwa_Chihana" title="Chakufwa Chihana">Chakufwa Chihana</a> 'the father of Malawian Democracy', founder of AFORD</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dindi_Gowa_Nyasulu" title="Dindi Gowa Nyasulu">Dindi Gowa Nyasulu</a>, Malawian engineer and former President of AFORD</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kamlepo_Kalua" title="Kamlepo Kalua">Kamlepo Kalua</a>, president of an opposition party</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dennis_Nkhwazi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dennis Nkhwazi (page does not exist)">Dennis Nkhwazi</a>, a former member of the cabinet</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mwiza_Munthali" title="Mwiza Munthali">Mwiza Munthali</a>, talk show host and activist</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Thindwa" title="Kenneth Thindwa">Kenneth Thindwa</a>, pharmacist, entrepreneur and politician</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Moses_Chirambo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Moses Chirambo (page does not exist)">Moses Chirambo</a>, ophthalmologist, entrepreneur, and politician</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Robert_Ng%27ambi" title="Robert Ng'ambi">Robert Ng'ambi</a>, soccer player.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gabadhinyo_Mhango&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gabadhinyo Mhango (page does not exist)">Gabadhinyo Mhango</a>, soccer player</li>
</ul>
