<p><b>Luocheng Mulao Autonomous County</b> (<a href="/wiki/Standard_Zhuang" title="Standard Zhuang">zhuang</a>: <span lang="za">Lozcwngz Bouxmohlaujcuz Swci Yen</span>,<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">罗城仫佬族自治县</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">羅城仫佬族自治縣</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Luóchéng Mùlǎozú Zìzhìxiàn</span></i>) is a county of northern <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a>, China. It is under the administration of <a href="/wiki/Hechi" title="Hechi">Hechi</a> City.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luocheng_Mulao_Autonomous_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are 7 towns and 4 townships in the county:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Towns:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dongmen,_Luocheng_County&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dongmen, Luocheng County (page does not exist)">Dongmen</a> (东门镇), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Long%27an,_Luocheng_County&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Long'an, Luocheng County (page does not exist)">Long'an</a> (龙岸镇), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Huangjin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Huangjin (page does not exist)">Huangjin</a> (黄金镇), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Xiaochang%27an&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Xiaochang'an (page does not exist)">Xiaochang'an</a> (小长安镇), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Siba,_Guangxi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Siba, Guangxi (page does not exist)">Siba</a> (四把镇), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tianhe,_Guangxi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tianhe, Guangxi (page does not exist)">Tianhe</a> (天河镇), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Huaiqun&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Huaiqun (page does not exist)">Huaiqun</a> (怀群镇)</li>
</ul>
<p>Townships:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Baotan_Township&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Baotan Township (page does not exist)">Baotan Township</a> (宝坛乡), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Qiaoshan_Township&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Qiaoshan Township (page does not exist)">Qiaoshan Township</a> (乔善乡), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Naweng_Township&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Naweng Township (page does not exist)">Naweng Township</a> (纳翁乡), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jian%27ai_Township&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jian'ai Township (page does not exist)">Jian'ai Township</a> (兼爱乡)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luocheng_Mulao_Autonomous_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mulam_language" title="Mulam language">Mulao language</a> (or Mulam) is spoken by the <a href="/wiki/Mulao_people" title="Mulao people">Mulao people</a> of Luocheng County.</p>
<p>At least seven varieties of <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a> are spoken in Luocheng County (Edmondson 1992:137).</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Putonghua" title="Putonghua">Putonghua</a></b> is the national language of China.</li>
<li><b>Gui-Liu Hua</b> is a local <a href="/wiki/Pinghua" title="Pinghua">Pinghua</a> variety spoken in <a href="/wiki/Guilin" title="Guilin">Guilin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liuzhou" title="Liuzhou">Liuzhou</a>. It was spoken by government officials formerly sent to administer the region, and also used to be spoken in the provincial court.</li>
<li><b>Tuguai Hua</b> is the local <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_Mandarin" title="Southwestern Mandarin">Southwestern Mandarin</a> vernacular spoken in Luocheng, <a href="/wiki/Rongshui_Miao_Autonomous_County" title="Rongshui Miao Autonomous County">Rongshui</a>, and other nearby counties.</li>
<li><b>Magai</b> is a Cantonese variety introduced by migrating merchants from <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a>.</li>
<li><b>Ngai</b> is a form of Southern Chinese with seven tones. It is spoken by 20,000 Han peasant agriculturalists.</li>
<li><b>Yangsan</b> is an archaic form of Chinese now spoken by only 300 people. It retains voiced stops and ten tones.</li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Min_language" title="Min language">Southern Min</a></b> was introduced by recent migrants from <a href="/wiki/Fujian" title="Fujian">Fujian</a>.</li>
</ul>
