<p><b>Luohu District</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_character" title="Simplified Chinese character">Simplified</a>: 罗湖; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_character" title="Traditional Chinese character">Traditional</a>: 羅湖; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">Pinyin</a>: Luōhú, <a href="/wiki/Hakka_Chinese" title="Hakka Chinese">Hakka Chinese</a>:lo2 fu2; <a href="/wiki/Cantonese" title="Cantonese">Cantonese</a> <a href="/wiki/Jyutping" title="Jyutping">Jyutping</a>: Lo4 Wu4 Keoi1) is a district of <a href="/wiki/Shenzhen" title="Shenzhen">Shenzhen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> Province, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luohu_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The size of Luohu district is about 78.9 km<sup>2</sup> (30 sq mi) and contains 10 <a href="/wiki/Subdistrict" title="Subdistrict">subdistricts</a>.</p>
<p>Luohu is located in the southern part of Shenzhen, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Futian" title="Futian">Futian</a> district on its west, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yantian" title="Yantian">Yantian</a> district on its east, and <a href="/wiki/Longgang_District,_Shenzhen" title="Longgang District, Shenzhen">Longgang</a> district on its north. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shenzhen_River" title="Shenzhen River">Shenzhen River</a>, which separates the district from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_District,_Hong_Kong" title="North District, Hong Kong">North District, Hong Kong</a> forms its southern limits.</p>
<p>It is also one of the six administrative districts of the <a href="/wiki/Shenzhen" title="Shenzhen">Shenzhen</a> Special Economic Zone (SEZ) of Shenzhen City of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>.</p>
<p>Luohu has an uneven geography and contains a number of natural fresh water sources, including Donghu (東湖/东湖), Honghu (洪湖), and Xianhu (仙湖), among others. Shuiku (水庫/水库), or "Water Reservoir", is also located in Luohu and is an important source of water for both Shenzhen and Hong Kong.</p>
<p>Shenzhen's highest peak, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wutong_Mountain" title="Wutong Mountain">Wutong Mountain</a> (944m) (梧桐山) is in Luohu District.</p>
<p>The district's west boundary with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Futian" title="Futian">Futian</a> is Hongling Road, and its east boundary with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yantian" title="Yantian">Yantian</a> is Wutong Mountain.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Immigration_Control_Point">Immigration Control Point</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luohu_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Immigration Control Point">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lo_Wu_Border_Crossing.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="225" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Lo_Wu_Border_Crossing.jpg/300px-Lo_Wu_Border_Crossing.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Lo_Wu_Border_Crossing.jpg/450px-Lo_Wu_Border_Crossing.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Lo_Wu_Border_Crossing.jpg/600px-Lo_Wu_Border_Crossing.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lo_Wu_Border_Crossing.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Luohu Immigration Control Point</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Luohu_Port" title="Luohu Port">Luohu Port</a></div>
<p>Luohu serves as an important immigration control between <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">Mainland China</a>. Two immigration control points, Luohu and <a href="/wiki/Man_Kam_To" title="Man Kam To">Man Kam To</a>, are located in the Luohu district. The Luohu immigration point is the busiest land boundary patrol connecting China and <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>,it consist of the <a href="/wiki/Luohu_Port" title="Luohu Port">Luohu Port</a> on the Shenzhen side <a href="/wiki/Lo_Wu_Control_Point" title="Lo Wu Control Point">Lo Wu Control Point</a> on the Hong Kong side.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luohu_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Shenzhen" title="Shenzhen">Shenzhen</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Special_Economic_Zone" title="Special Economic Zone">Special Economic Zone</a>(SEZ), an original town called Shenzhen (or Shum Chun) Hui (深圳墟, lit. Shenzhen market) was located within the current Luohu district. It was of size 350,000 m<sup>2</sup> and has a population of little less than 30,000. The town centred at the present-day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongmen_Subdistrict,_Shenzhen" title="Dongmen Subdistrict, Shenzhen">Dongmen</a>, where a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tin_Hau_Temple" title="Tin Hau Temple">Tin Hau Temple</a> once stood.</p>
<p>The name Shenzhen was first mentioned in 1410<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>though the town was only first documented in 1688<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> but was believed to be inhabited long before this.The market town prospered and expanded out of its boundaries in the early 20th century,following the completion of the Kowloon-Canton Railway,which the town was a stopping station.The town was occupied by Japanese forces between 1942 and 1945.It was promoted from market town to an official town status in 1950 .In 1953,the administrative centre of <a href="/wiki/Bao%27an_County" title="Bao'an County">San On County</a> was moved to Shum Chun from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nantou,_Shenzhen_(historical)" title="Nantou, Shenzhen (historical)">Nantou</a>.</p>
<p>After Shenzhen was promoted to city status in October 1979,Luohu district was established as the first district in Shenzhen. Several hills were flattened to facilitate its infrastructure during initial construction phases. In November 1997, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yantian" title="Yantian">Yantian</a> area became an independent administrative district.</p>
<p>Some of the tallest and most recognizable buildings in Shenzhen, including <a href="/wiki/Shun_Hing_Square" title="Shun Hing Square">Shun Hing Square</a>, <a href="/wiki/KK100" title="KK100">KK100</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Guomao_Building" title="Guomao Building">Guomao Building</a> has are located within the district.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shopping">Shopping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luohu_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Shopping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shenzhen002.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="3888" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Shenzhen002.JPG/220px-Shenzhen002.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Shenzhen002.JPG/330px-Shenzhen002.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Shenzhen002.JPG/440px-Shenzhen002.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

Luohu District</div>

<p>Luohu is known in Hong Kong and <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> for shopping. Most one-day visitors from Hong Kong limit their shopping to <a href="/wiki/Luohu_Commercial_City" title="Luohu Commercial City">Luohu Commercial City</a> (羅湖商業城/罗湖商业城), located right outside the Luohu Immigration Control Point, but the areas of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongmen_Subdistrict,_Shenzhen" title="Dongmen Subdistrict, Shenzhen">Dongmen</a> (東門/东门), Guomao (國貿/国贸), and Diwang (地王) are also important shopping districts.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nightlife">Nightlife</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luohu_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Nightlife">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Luohu district is also known for its nightlife. Though most expatriates residing in Shenzhen live in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shekou_Industrial_Zone" title="Shekou Industrial Zone">Shekou</a>, visitors to Shenzhen from Hong Kong and other places typically frequent bars, KTV (<a href="/wiki/Karaoke" title="Karaoke">karaoke</a>) lounges, and night clubs in the Luohu area.</p>
