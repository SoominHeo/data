<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryu_Deok-hwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ryu Deok-hwan began his career as a <a href="/wiki/Child_actor" title="Child actor">child actor</a> on stage at age 6. Among his early <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">TV drama</a> appearances was a role on the popular <i>Lifetime in the Country</i> which he played for 8 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>While he had played minor roles in several films, it was Ryu's breakout lead performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Like_a_Virgin_(film)" title="Like a Virgin (film)">Like a Virgin</a></i> that earned him critical praise and industry recognition. He gained 28kg in three months to play the role of a <a href="/wiki/Transgender" title="Transgender">transgender</a> teenager who joins the <a href="/wiki/Ssireum" title="Ssireum">ssireum</a> team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other notable roles include a high schooler desperate for a pair of <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nikes</a> in <i>No Comment</i>, a <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korean</a> soldier in <i><a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_Dongmakgol" title="Welcome to Dongmakgol">Welcome to Dongmakgol</a></i>, a boy meeting his estranged father in <i><a href="/wiki/My_Son_(film)" title="My Son (film)">My Son</a></i>, a serial killer in <i><a href="/wiki/Our_Town_(2007_film)" title="Our Town (2007 film)">Our Town</a></i>, a medical student in <i><a href="/wiki/Private_Eye_(film)" title="Private Eye (film)">Private Eye</a></i>, a top neurosurgeon and forensic examiner in <i><a href="/wiki/God%27s_Quiz" title="God's Quiz">God's Quiz</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Gongmin_of_Goryeo" title="Gongmin of Goryeo">King Gongmin</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Faith_(2012_TV_series)" title="Faith (2012 TV series)">Faith</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He has also acted in the stage plays <i><a href="/wiki/Equus_(play)" title="Equus (play)">Equus</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Jang_Jin" title="Jang Jin">Jang Jin</a>'s <i>Clumsy People</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012 he directed the <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short film</a> <i>Waiting for <a href="/wiki/Jang_Joon-hwan" title="Jang Joon-hwan">Jang Joon-hwan</a></i> for the Olleh Smartphone Film Festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
