<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="February_referendums">February referendums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss_referendums,_2014&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: February referendums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Swiss_immigration_referendum,_February_2014" title="Swiss immigration referendum, February 2014">Swiss immigration referendum, February 2014</a></div>
<p>Three referendums were held on 9 February on abortion, immigration and the rail network. The abortion referendum proposed that abortions would no longer be funded through health insurance, but should be paid for privately by the mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The anti-immigration proposal was supported by the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_People%27s_Party" title="Swiss People's Party">Swiss People's Party</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and opposes the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freedom_of_movement_for_workers" title="Freedom of movement for workers">free movement of workers</a> between the EU and Switzerland, which was introduced following a <a href="/wiki/Swiss_referendums,_2000" title="Swiss referendums, 2000">2000 referendum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The rail network proposal combined a number of existing federal and cantonal funds into a single permanent rail infrastructure fund, and defined a programme of upgrade projects of about 42 billion francs over 40 years, with a mechanism for the federal council to present regular updates to the programme. The initial programme includes a number of speed increases to intercity rail routes and S-Bahn expansion around Bern, Basel, and Geneva.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss_referendums,_2014&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The abortion referendum, which would have dropped abortion coverage from public health insurance, failed by a large margin, with about 70% of participating voters rejecting the proposal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The immigration restriction proposal passed by a narrow margin, with 50.3% of participating voters supporting the measure; the proposal was also approved by the required majority of cantons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-SW-6">[6]</a></sup> The immigration measure requires the Swiss government to either renegotiate the Swiss-EU agreement of free movement of people within three years, or to revoke the agreement. The proposal mandates re-introduction of strict quotas for various immigration categories, and imposes limits on the ability of foreigners to bring in their family members to live in Switzerland, to access Swiss social security benefits, and to request asylum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPost_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-WPost-7">[7]</a></sup> Opinion polls ahead of the vote showed the lead for the opponents of the immigration measure, but that lead began to close as the day of the referendum approached.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPost_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-WPost-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The referendum on public rail passed with 62% of votes in favour.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss_referendums,_2014&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Due to the passing of the initiative to limit immigration, Switzerland has effectively rejected granting Croatia free movement of persons. As retaliation, the EU has excluded Switzerland from the <a href="/wiki/Erasmus_Programme" title="Erasmus Programme">Erasmus Programme</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horizon_2020" title="Horizon 2020">Horizon 2020</a>, the largest educational and research European program ever.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="May_referendums">May referendums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss_referendums,_2014&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: May referendums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four referendums were held in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> on 18 May 2014. An initiative on minimum wage proposed that the government should promote the adoption of minimum wages in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collective_agreements" title="Collective agreements">collective agreements</a>, and set a national minimum wage of 22 <a href="/wiki/Swiss_franc" title="Swiss franc">francs</a> an hour,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> which would have been the highest minimum wage in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The other referendums included changes to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primary_health_care" title="Primary health care">primary health care</a>, a lifetime ban on convicted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pedophiles" title="Pedophiles">pedophiles</a> working with children, and the procurement of the <a href="/wiki/Saab_JAS_39_Gripen" title="Saab JAS 39 Gripen">JAS 39 Gripen</a> fighter aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="September_referendums">September referendums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss_referendums,_2014&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: September referendums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two referendums were held on 28 September, with voters asked whether they supported the creation of a unified health insurance fund and the "End to VAT discrimination in the hospitality industry" <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Popular_initiative" title="Popular initiative">popular initiative</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-SR-11">[11]</a></sup> The health insurance proposal was supported by the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Switzerland" title="Social Democratic Party of Switzerland">Social Democratic Party</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Switzerland" title="Green Party of Switzerland">Green Party</a>, together with several user and consumer organisations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-SR-11">[11]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Value-added_tax" title="Value-added tax">VAT</a> initiative was launched by GastroSuisse, and sought to reduce the level of VAT paid in restaurants from the standard 8% to 2.5%, the same level paid at food stands. However, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Council_(Switzerland)" title="Federal Council (Switzerland)">Federal Council</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Assembly_(Switzerland)" title="Federal Assembly (Switzerland)">Federal Assembly</a> both opposed the proposal, claiming it would reduce tax take by around <a href="/wiki/Swiss_franc" title="Swiss franc">CHF</a> 750 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-SR-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Both proposals were rejected by voters.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="November_referendums">November referendums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss_referendums,_2014&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: November referendums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A further three referendums were held on 30 November on the country's gold reserves, abolishing the flat tax and limiting immigration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nov_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nov-12">[12]</a></sup> All three were rejected by voters.</p>
<p>The gold reserves proposal would have required the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_National_Bank" title="Swiss National Bank">Swiss National Bank</a> to have gold reserves of at least 20% of the value of the assets of the Swiss National Bank, and see all Swiss gold currently held in the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Reserve_Bank_of_New_York" title="Federal Reserve Bank of New York">Federal Reserve Bank of New York</a> returned to Switzerland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Popular_initiative_(Switzerland)" title="Popular initiative (Switzerland)">popular initiative</a> was started by <a href="/wiki/Swiss_People%27s_Party" title="Swiss People's Party">Swiss People's Party</a> MP <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Luzi_Stamm&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Luzi Stamm (page does not exist)">Luzi Stamm</a> and two other MPs, with the 100,000 signatures required for a referendum obtained by early 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GS_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-GS-14">[14]</a></sup> The proposal was opposed by the Swiss National Bank and the Swiss government, as it would limit the SNB's ability to print money.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GS_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-GS-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The flat tax abolition proposal called for residents and non-working foreigners to be taxed based only on their income and their assets, <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nov_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nov-12">[12]</a></sup> whilst the immigration cap proposal put forward by <a href="/wiki/Ecopop" title="Ecopop">Ecopop</a> called for preserving natural resources by limiting immigration to Switzerland to 0.2% of the population per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nov_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Nov-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
