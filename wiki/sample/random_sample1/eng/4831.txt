<p><b>Liu Xiang</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%88%98" title="wikt:刘">刘</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%BF%94" title="wikt:翔">翔</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%8A%89" title="wikt:劉">劉</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%BF%94" title="wikt:翔">翔</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Liú Xiáng</span></i>; born July 13, 1983 in <a href="/wiki/Putuo_District,_Shanghai" title="Putuo District, Shanghai">Putuo District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/110_metres_hurdles" title="110 metres hurdles">110 meter</a> <a href="/wiki/Hurdling" title="Hurdling">hurdler</a>. Liu is an <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic</a> Gold medalist and <a href="/wiki/2007_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2007 World Championships in Athletics">World Champion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> His 2004 Olympic gold medal<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="per WP:CIRCULAR (August 2016)">better source needed</span></a></i>]</sup> was the first in a men's <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a> event for China.</p>
<p>Liu is one of China's most successful athletes and has emerged as a <a href="/wiki/Cultural_icon" title="Cultural icon">cultural icon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He is the only male athlete in history to have achieved the "triple crown" in the event of 110-metre hurdles: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Record" title="World Record">World Record</a> Holder, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Champion" title="World Champion">World Champion</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Champion" title="Olympic Champion">Olympic Champion</a>. He was the favorite to win another gold in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_110_metres_hurdles" title="Athletics at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's 110 metres hurdles">110 metre hurdles</a> at the <a href="/wiki/China_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="China at the 2008 Summer Olympics">Beijing Olympics</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_News_2008-05-24_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_News_2008-05-24-4">[4]</a></sup> but he had to withdraw from competition at the last moment after a false start and aggravation to a previously unrevealed injury. Again a gold medal favourite in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_110_metres_hurdles" title="Athletics at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's 110 metres hurdles">110 metre hurdles</a> at the <a href="/wiki/China_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="China at the 2012 Summer Olympics">London Olympics</a> he pulled his Achilles tendon attempting to clear the first hurdle in the heats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On April 7, 2015, he made a retirement announcement on his Sina Weibo. He would end his sport career officially.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liu_Xiang_(hurdler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liu_Xiang_(hurdler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In May 2001, he won at the <a href="/wiki/East_Asian_Games" title="East Asian Games">East Asian Games</a> in Osaka, Japan with a time of 13.42 seconds. In August 2001, he won at the <a href="/wiki/Universiade" title="Universiade">Universiade</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> with a time of 13.33 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He also won at the <a href="/wiki/2001_National_Games_of_China" title="2001 National Games of China">2001 National Games of China</a> that same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002 he set an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_records_in_athletics" title="Asian records in athletics">Asian record</a> time at the <a href="/wiki/Athletissima" title="Athletissima">Athletissima</a> meeting, completing the event in 13.12 seconds. This also broke <a href="/wiki/Renaldo_Nehemiah" title="Renaldo Nehemiah">Renaldo Nehemiah</a>'s long standing and <a href="/wiki/List_of_world_junior_records_in_athletics" title="List of world junior records in athletics">world junior record</a>, which had stood for almost 25 years. The following year he secured bronze medals in the <a href="/wiki/60_metres_hurdles" title="60 metres hurdles">60 metres hurdles</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2003_IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="2003 IAAF World Indoor Championships">2003 IAAF World Indoor Championships</a> and the 110 m hurdles at the <a href="/wiki/2003_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2003 World Championships in Athletics">2003 World Championships in Athletics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Focus_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Focus-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_Olympics">2004 Olympics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liu_Xiang_(hurdler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004 Olympics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In May 2004 at an IAAF Grand Prix race in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, Liu managed to beat <a href="/wiki/Allen_Johnson" title="Allen Johnson">Allen Johnson</a> with a personal best record time of 13.06 seconds<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. He improved even further at the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Athens Olympics</a>. Although he was not considered a favourite for the event<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, he won the Olympic final by some distance to take the gold medal in a <a href="/wiki/List_of_world_records_in_athletics" title="List of world records in athletics">world record</a>-equalling time of 12.91 seconds, matching the feat of <a href="/wiki/Colin_Jackson" title="Colin Jackson">Colin Jackson</a>. This was a new <a href="/wiki/List_of_Olympic_records_in_athletics" title="List of Olympic records in athletics">Olympic record</a> and was almost three tenths of a second faster than the runner-up <a href="/wiki/Terrence_Trammell" title="Terrence Trammell">Terrence Trammell</a>. The performance had Liu the sixth man to run under 13 seconds for the event and was China's first men's Olympic gold medal in a <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a> event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Focus_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Focus-9">[9]</a></sup> On top of this, it defied the traditional thinking that Asian athletes could not compete in sprint events at the top level<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. He said that his gold medal "changes the opinion that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_countries" title="Asian countries">Asian countries</a> don't get good results in <a href="/wiki/Sprint_(running)" title="Sprint (running)">sprint races</a>. I want to prove to all the world that Asians can run very fast."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Liu, a 21-year-old student at <a href="/wiki/East_China_Normal_University" title="East China Normal University">East China Normal University</a> at the time of his Athens victory, became the object of a bidding war between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commercial_sponsor" title="Commercial sponsor">commercial sponsors</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_Track_and_Field_Association" title="Chinese Track and Field Association">Chinese Track and Field Association</a> restricted him to four such deals.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Liu finished the season with four of the year's ten fastest clockings<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Reaching 17 finals in the 60 m indoor hurdles and the 110 metre hurdles, he lost just two, both to American <a href="/wiki/Allen_Johnson" title="Allen Johnson">Allen Johnson</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_and_2007_World_Championships">2005 and 2007 World Championships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liu_Xiang_(hurdler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2005 and 2007 World Championships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2005, Liu won a silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="IAAF World Championships in Athletics">IAAF World Championships in Athletics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, finishing 13.08 seconds, 0.01 seconds after champion <a href="/wiki/Ladji_Doucour%C3%A9" title="Ladji Doucouré">Ladji Doucouré</a> from France. In November 2005, he won at <a href="/wiki/East_Asian_Games" title="East Asian Games">East Asian Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>, China with 13.21 seconds.</p>
<p>Off track, in May, he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Laureus_World_Sports_Awards" title="Laureus World Sports Awards">Laureus World Sports Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laureus_World_Sports_Award_for_Newcomer_of_the_Year" title="Laureus World Sports Award for Newcomer of the Year">Newcomer of the Year</a> for his breakthrough performance at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>.</p>
<p>Liu set a new world record in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/110_metre_hurdles" title="110 metre hurdles">110 metre hurdles</a>, at the Super Grand Prix in <a href="/wiki/Lausanne" title="Lausanne">Lausanne</a> on July 11, 2006, with a time of 12.88 seconds (+1.1 m/s tailwind). The record was ratified by the IAAF.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In that same race, American <a href="/wiki/Dominique_Arnold" title="Dominique Arnold">Dominique Arnold</a> had also beaten the previous record with a time of 12.90 seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In September, he won gold at <a href="/wiki/2006_IAAF_World_Athletics_Final" title="2006 IAAF World Athletics Final">IAAF World Athletics Final</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>, Germany with 12.93 seconds.</p>
<p>On August 31, 2007 at the <a href="/wiki/2007_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2007 World Championships in Athletics">World Athletics Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, Japan, Liu won gold in the 110 metre hurdles with 12.95 seconds to become the World Champion for the first time.</p>
<p>On May 23, Liu participated in a test event at the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_National_Stadium" title="Beijing National Stadium">Beijing National Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-13">[13]</a></sup> He pulled out of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reebok_Grand_Prix" title="Reebok Grand Prix">Reebok Grand Prix</a> in New York on May 31, citing hamstring problems. On June 8, he false-started at the <a href="/wiki/Prefontaine_Classic" title="Prefontaine Classic">Prefontaine Classic</a> at <a href="/wiki/Eugene,_Oregon" title="Eugene, Oregon">Eugene, Oregon</a>. Liu skipped the entire European circuit, preferring to train for the Olympics in China instead.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beijing_Olympics">Beijing Olympics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liu_Xiang_(hurdler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Beijing Olympics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Leading up to the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, China, Liu bore national expectations of a repeat victory on home soil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indeexspendent_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Indeexspendent-14">[14]</a></sup> On August 18, Liu withdrew from the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_110_metres_hurdles" title="Athletics at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's 110 metres hurdles">Olympic 110 metre hurdles</a>. He walked off the track after a false start by another runner in his first-round heat,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indeexspendent_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Indeexspendent-14">[14]</a></sup> leaving the crowd at the Beijing National Stadium in stunned silence,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBC-15">[15]</a></sup> confusion,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_News_2008-08-18_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_News_2008-08-18-16">[16]</a></sup> and tears.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indeexspendent_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-Indeexspendent-14">[14]</a></sup> According to Jeré Longman of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a>, "<i>China's greatest hope had been dashed</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to China's <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Athletic_Association" title="Chinese Athletic Association">track and field association</a>, Liu suffered from a recurrence of chronic inflammation in his right <a href="/wiki/Achilles_tendon" title="Achilles tendon">Achilles tendon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-13">[13]</a></sup> Liu's coach, Sun Haiping addressed the media during a press conference and stated that the hurdler had been hampered by a tendon injury for six or seven years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBC-15">[15]</a></sup> He commented on the situation, saying "We worked hard every day, but the result was as you see and it is really hard to take."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-CBC-15">[15]</a></sup> Sun, who was in tears for most of the press conference, stated that Liu would be unable to compete for the remainder of 2008. Liu made a public apology to the Chinese media the following day, saying he could "do nothing but pull out of the race" because of his foot injury. He believed that the injury would not prevent him from future competitions and vowed to "come back" for the next Olympics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT2_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT2-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Liu's injury was significant and also ruled him out of the following year's major competition, the <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2009 World Championships in Athletics">2009 World Championships in Athletics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> However, coach Sun Haiping was confident that he would return in time for the Chinese national championships and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_Asian_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2009 Asian Championships in Athletics">2009 Asian Championships in Athletics</a> in November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.932011:_Return_from_injury">2009–2011: Return from injury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liu_Xiang_(hurdler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2009–2011: Return from injury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a 13-month absence because of his injury, Liu finally returned to competition at the Shanghai Golden Grand Prix. Liu recorded a time of 13.15, tied with Terrence Trammell, but finished 0.01s behind and was awarded second place. However, Liu said he was happy with his performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Nearing the end of the year, he competed at a number of major events on home turf. He won gold medals at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Asian_Athletics_Championships" title="2009 Asian Athletics Championships">2009 Asian Athletics Championships</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2009_East_Asian_Games" title="Athletics at the 2009 East Asian Games">East Asian Games</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2009_National_Games_of_China" title="Athletics at the 2009 National Games of China">11th Chinese National Games</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2010_IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="2010 IAAF World Indoor Championships">2010 IAAF World Indoor Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Doha" title="Doha">Doha</a>, admitting that his right foot has yet to fully recover,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Liu was able to finish in the finals of the 60 m hurdles, but managed only seventh place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> His sole appearance on the <a href="/wiki/2010_IAAF_Diamond_League" title="2010 IAAF Diamond League">2010 IAAF Diamond League</a> circuit came at the Shanghai Grand Prix and he lost to national rival Shi Dongpeng for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Following a six-month break, he marked his return to form at the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2010_Asian_Games" title="Athletics at the 2010 Asian Games">2010 Asian Games</a>. A crowd of 70,000 gathered at the <a href="/wiki/Guangdong_Olympic_Stadium" title="Guangdong Olympic Stadium">Guangdong Olympic Stadium</a> to see him in the final and he easily won his third consecutive title at the competition, breaking the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Asian_Games_records_in_athletics" title="List of Asian Games records in athletics">Games record</a> with a run of 13.09 seconds – making him the third fastest athlete that season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Shanghai Golden Grand Prix in May 2011 saw Liu make a return to a world class level: he defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_Oliver_(athlete)" title="David Oliver (athlete)">David Oliver</a> (the fastest hurdler in 2010) with a world-leading mark of 13.07 seconds to take his first win on the <a href="/wiki/2011_IAAF_Diamond_League" title="2011 IAAF Diamond League">2011 IAAF Diamond League</a>. Liu showed he had accomplished a transition in his technique, as he reduced his number of starting steps before the first hurdle from eight to seven, using his left leg for hurdling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 29, 2011, Liu Xiang competed in the men's 110 m hurdles final in the IAAF World Championships in Daegu, South Korea. Liu finished the race in third place, but he eventually won the silver medal, as the winner Dayron Robles was disqualified for entering Liu's lane and pulling him back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_season">2012 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liu_Xiang_(hurdler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2012 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Liu's first competition of 2012, he was matched up against Dayron Robles at the <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_Indoor_Grand_Prix" title="Birmingham Indoor Grand Prix">Birmingham Indoor Grand Prix</a> and this time he won cleanly with an Asian record time of 7.41 seconds for the 60 m hurdles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> He was the favourite for the title at the <a href="/wiki/2012_IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="2012 IAAF World Indoor Championships">2012 IAAF World Indoor Championships</a>, but was beaten into second place by <a href="/wiki/Aries_Merritt" title="Aries Merritt">Aries Merritt</a> and left with the silver medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In the outdoor season he set a 110 m hurdles meet record at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Grand_Prix_Kawasaki" title="Golden Grand Prix Kawasaki">Golden Grand Prix Kawasaki</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> then ran 12.97 seconds to win at his home nation <a href="/wiki/2012_IAAF_Diamond_League" title="2012 IAAF Diamond League">2012 IAAF Diamond League</a> meet, the <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_Golden_Grand_Prix" title="Shanghai Golden Grand Prix">Shanghai Golden Grand Prix</a>. This was his first run under 13 seconds since 2007 and he beat Americans David Oliver and Jason Richardson by some distance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> He followed this with a run of 12.87 seconds to win at the <a href="/wiki/Prefontaine_Classic" title="Prefontaine Classic">Prefontaine Classic</a>, matching the world record time albeit with wind-assistance of 2.4 m/s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_110_metres_hurdles" title="Athletics at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's 110 metres hurdles">110 metre hurdles</a> at the <a href="/wiki/China_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="China at the 2012 Summer Olympics">London Olympics</a> in 2012 he pulled his Achilles tendon while taking off and attempting to clear the first hurdle, instead crashing straight into it. Liu hopped the full 110 metre stretch, was helped by a few of his fellow competitors and was put into a wheel chair and led away. He kissed the last hurdle before he left the track. <a href="/wiki/Colin_Jackson" title="Colin Jackson">Colin Jackson</a> described it as a "very sad sight indeed" for the sport. Liu's loss echoed strongly in the Chinese press but also sparked a lot of controversies. Some voices expressed support while others wondered why Liu chose to participate in spite of his injury. Liu Xiang even earned a nickname "Liu RunRun" because of pullbacks in two consecutive Olympic Games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> As per reports Liu Xiang is to have surgery on his Achilles tendon in Britain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liu_Xiang_(hurdler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Liu is known for his low-profile appearance, but he has become one of the most popular athletes in China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Liu Xiang was on <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i> magazine Asian edition's cover of the 2008 Summer Olympic Games titled "Liu Xiang &amp; 99 More Athletes to Watch."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Liu donated approximately 2,500,000 yuan (364,000 USD) to <a href="/wiki/2008_Sichuan_earthquake" title="2008 Sichuan earthquake">2008 Sichuan earthquake</a> relief efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Liu married <a href="/wiki/Ge_Tian" title="Ge Tian">Ge Tian</a>, a post-90s generation actress on September 7, 2014, after officially dating the actress for two years prior to their marriage. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> They divorced in 2015. On January 9, 2016, Liu Xiang announced a new relationship with pole vaulter <a href="/wiki/Wu_Sha" title="Wu Sha">Wu Sha</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wu_Sha2016_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wu_Sha2016-43">[43]</a></sup> in his Sina Weibo, a Chinese equivalent to Twitter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wu_Sha2016_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wu_Sha2016-43">[43]</a></sup> Wu is</p>
<p>Liu's athletic gear is sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is also a spokesperson for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coca_Cola" title="Coca Cola">Coca Cola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cadillac" title="Cadillac">Cadillac</a>.</p>
