<p><b>Liu Yuan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">刘源</span>; born 22 February 1951) is a retired general of the Chinese <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> and a former politician. He served as the last political commissar of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PLA_General_Logistics_Department" title="PLA General Logistics Department">PLA General Logistics Department</a> and prior to that, political commissar of the <a href="/wiki/PLA_Academy_of_Military_Science" title="PLA Academy of Military Science">PLA Academy of Military Science</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Page_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Page-1">[1]</a></sup> Before his military career, he served as vice mayor of <a href="/wiki/Zhengzhou" title="Zhengzhou">Zhengzhou</a> and vice governor of <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> Province. He is the son of <a href="/wiki/Liu_Shaoqi" title="Liu Shaoqi">Liu Shaoqi</a>, the former <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="President of the People's Republic of China">President of China</a> who was persecuted to death during the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liu_Yuan_(general)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Liu Yuan was born in 1951 in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, the son of <a href="/wiki/Liu_Shaoqi" title="Liu Shaoqi">Liu Shaoqi</a>, a Communist revolutionary and former <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="President of the People's Republic of China">President of China</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wang_Guangmei" title="Wang Guangmei">Wang Guangmei</a>, a multilingual interpreter who also worked for the party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Page_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Page-1">[1]</a></sup> He graduated from the No. 2 Experimental School in Beijing in 1964, and entered a regiment on the <a href="/wiki/Central_Security_Bureau_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="Central Security Bureau of the Communist Party of China">Central Security Bureau</a> to undergo military training during his summer vacation.</p>
<p>In 1966, Mao launched the <a href="/wiki/Cultural_Revolution" title="Cultural Revolution">Cultural Revolution</a>, and targeted Liu Shaoqi through the euphemistic "<a href="/wiki/Bombard_the_Headquarters" title="Bombard the Headquarters">Bombard the Headquarters</a>" big-character poster that Mao penned himself and ordered circulated all over the country. Liu Yuan, perhaps not initially realizing the real target of the poster was his own father, answered Mao's call-to-arms to usher in a brave new world by joining a Red Guard regiment at <a href="/wiki/Beijing_No._4_High_School" title="Beijing No. 4 High School">Beijing No. 4 High School</a>. In September 1967, after his father had been forcibly removed from the capital, Liu Yuan and his two sisters escaped the <a href="/wiki/Zhongnanhai" title="Zhongnanhai">Zhongnanhai</a> compound by themselves but were left homeless. They found temporary shelter at the No. 4 Middle School.</p>
<p>Liu Shaoqi fell into political disgrace and was later killed during the Cultural Revolution. However, he was later rehabilitated after the Cultural Revolution ended. Liu Yuan was therefore allowed to participate in politics again.</p>
<p>In 1985, Liu Yuan became the vice mayor of <a href="/wiki/Zhengzhou" title="Zhengzhou">Zhengzhou</a>, capital of <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a> province. He was promoted to vice governor of Henan in 1988. Since 1992, he had served in <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Armed_Police" title="People's Armed Police">People's Armed Police</a> for years. In 2003, he became a deputy political commissar of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PLA_General_Logistics_Department" title="PLA General Logistics Department">PLA General Logistics Department</a>, and was made lieutenant general. He was appointed as political commissar of the <a href="/wiki/PLA_Academy_of_Military_Science" title="PLA Academy of Military Science">PLA Academy of Military Science</a> in 2005. On 20 July 2009, Liu was promoted to general.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, Liu wrote the preface to a friend's book titled <i>Changing Our View of Culture and History</i>, which has aroused notice for criticizing recent Party leadership and calls for the rejection of foreign models and a return to a supposed upright military heritage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Page_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Page-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CITIC_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CITIC-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (December 2013)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> believe Liu is politically close to other "<a href="/wiki/Princelings" title="Princelings">princelings</a>", especially <a href="/wiki/Xi_Jinping" title="Xi Jinping">Xi Jinping</a>, the <a href="/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="General Secretary of the Communist Party of China">General Secretary of the Communist Party of China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Page_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Page-1">[1]</a></sup> However, he has also had close ties to the disgraced "princeling" <a href="/wiki/Bo_Xilai" title="Bo Xilai">Bo Xilai</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and this, coupled with Liu's outspokenness about corruption in the PLA, may have denied Liu a seat on the <a href="/wiki/Central_Military_Commission" title="Central Military Commission">Central Military Commission</a>. However, <a href="/wiki/Xi_Jinping" title="Xi Jinping">Xi Jinping</a> was said to be moving to promote Liu to the Central Military Commission after 'accusations (by Liu) in 2012 paved the way for the corruption charges against' senior military offices General <a href="/wiki/Xu_Caihou" title="Xu Caihou">Xu Caihou</a> and lieutenant general <a href="/wiki/Gu_Junshan" title="Gu Junshan">Gu Junshan</a>, as part of his plan to tackle <a href="/wiki/Corruption" title="Corruption">corruption</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-4">[4]</a></sup> Despite media speculation that he would take on the post of the Central Military Commission's new Discipline Inspection Commission, Liu Yuan retired in December 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retired_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-retired-5">[5]</a></sup> Regarding his retirement, Liu said, "I will be the last political commissar of the Logistics Department... I will absolutely obey the military reforms [of Xi Jinping]."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retired_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-retired-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Liu was named deputy chair of the <a href="/wiki/National_People%27s_Congress_Financial_and_Economic_Affairs_Committee" title="National People's Congress Financial and Economic Affairs Committee">National People's Congress Financial and Economic Affairs Committee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Liu is a member of the <a href="/wiki/17th_Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="17th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">17th</a> and the <a href="/wiki/18th_Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="18th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">18th</a> Central Committees of the Communist Party of China.</p>
