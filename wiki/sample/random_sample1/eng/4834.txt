<p>The <b>University of the Ryukyus</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">琉球大学</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ryūkyū Daigaku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, abbreviated to <i>Ryūdai</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">琉大</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is a national university of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Okinawa_Prefecture" title="Okinawa Prefecture">Okinawa Prefecture</a>. It is located in the Senbaru neighborhood of the town of <a href="/wiki/Nishihara,_Okinawa" title="Nishihara, Okinawa">Nishihara</a>, with its campus bordering both the village of <a href="/wiki/Nakagusuku,_Okinawa" title="Nakagusuku, Okinawa">Nakagusuku</a> and the city of <a href="/wiki/Ginowan,_Okinawa" title="Ginowan, Okinawa">Ginowan</a>. It is the westernmost national university of Japan and the largest public university in Okinawa Prefecture.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_the_Ryukyus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The University of the Ryukyus was established under the guidance of <a href="/wiki/Michigan_State_University" title="Michigan State University">Michigan State University</a>. It has since developed its own traditions of contributing to and advancing the position of the local community, of conducting international exchange, and of broadening the knowledge base of the people of Okinawa through academic and educational activities. Under the auspices of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Civil_Administration_of_the_Ryukyu_Islands" title="United States Civil Administration of the Ryukyu Islands">United States Civil Administration of the Ryukyu Islands</a>, the University of the Ryukyus was founded, as a territorial university, on the site of the historic <a href="/wiki/Shuri_Castle" title="Shuri Castle">Shuri Castle</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naha,_Okinawa" title="Naha, Okinawa">Naha</a> on May 22, 1950. It was placed under the jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Ryukyu_Islands" title="Government of the Ryukyu Islands">Government of the Ryukyu Islands</a> in 1966.</p>
<p><i>Ryūdai</i> became a Japanese national university on May 15, 1972, upon Okinawa's return to Japan. The university had been situated on the site of the Shuri Castle in Naha, but it had moved to its current locations during 1975 and 1984 during the restoration of the castle.</p>
<p>The university was a state-run university from 1972 until 2004, when it was reclassified as a 'national university corporation' under the Japanese government's changes to the national university system.</p>
<p>Since 1988, Ryudai and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii" title="University of Hawaii">University of Hawaii</a> have had a "sister-university" relationship, and have opened up centers for Okinawan studies at both universities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2013, the University of the Ryukyus began a research exchange program with <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mokpo_National_University" title="Mokpo National University">Mokpo National University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
