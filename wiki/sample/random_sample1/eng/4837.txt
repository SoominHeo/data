<p><b>Ryu Hwayoung</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">류화영</span>; born April 22, 1993), better known by the <a href="/wiki/Mononymous_person" title="Mononymous person">mononym</a> <b>Hwayoung</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> rapper and actress. She is a former member of South Korean girl group <a href="/wiki/T-ara" title="T-ara">T-ara</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryu_Hwa-young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ryu Hwayoung was born on 22 April 1993 in <a href="/wiki/Gwangju" title="Gwangju">Gwangju</a>, South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-birth_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-birth-1">[1]</a></sup> Her older twin sister, <a href="/wiki/Ryu_Hyo-young" title="Ryu Hyo-young">Ryu Hyo-young</a>, is also a rapper and former member of the group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coed_School" title="Coed School">Coed School</a> and its sub-group <a href="/wiki/F-ve_Dolls" title="F-ve Dolls">F-ve Dolls</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2010, Hwayoung's sister Hyoyoung placed first in a nationwide beauty pageant, "<a href="/wiki/Namwon#Chunhyang_Festivals" title="Namwon">Miss Chunhyang</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hyoyoung and Hwayoung made a guest appearance on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a>'s variety show <i><a href="/wiki/Star_King_(TV_series)" title="Star King (TV series)">Star King</a></i> in May 2010. They were both subsequently scouted and recruited by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Core_Contents_Media" title="Core Contents Media">Core Contents Media</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryu_Hwa-young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9312:_T-ara_and_Solo_activities">2010–12: T-ara and Solo activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryu_Hwa-young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010–12: T-ara and Solo activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/T-ara" title="T-ara">T-ara</a></div>
<p>Core Contents Media originally slated to add Hyoyoung (Hwayoung's twin sister) into T-ara as the seventh member, but she recommended her sister, Hwayoung. In July 2010, it was announced that Hwayoung would be added into T-ara as lead rapper while Hyoyoung would be debuting in the newly formed Coed School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Hwayoung began promotions as part of T-ara in November 2010 by appearing on the third season of <i><a href="/wiki/Hello_Baby#Season_3:_T-ara.27s_Hello_Baby" title="Hello Baby">Hello Baby</a></i> and promoting their first <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">mini album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Temptastic" title="Temptastic">Temptastic</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> T-ara held their first performance with Hwayoung on December 3, through <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i>. During her time in the group, she participated in the collaboration between Davichi and T-ara for "We Were In Love" (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">우리 사랑했잖아</span>) in December 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She also participated in the writing of the rap lyrics of "Love is All the Same" (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">사랑은 다 그런거래요</span>) by fellow labelmate <a href="/wiki/Yangpa" title="Yangpa">Yangpa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 28, 2012, several members of T-ara posted <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweets</a> that were purportedly seen to be derisive references to Hwayoung.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> An official announcement regarding these tweets and a "major announcement about T-ara" was made July 30, which revealed Hwayoung would no longer be a member of the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rachellee_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-rachellee-10">[10]</a></sup> Following the announcement of her abrupt contract termination with Core Contents Media, rumors of her experiencing severe bullying by other members of T-ara surfaced throughout the web and the media, which sparked intensive public interest and controversy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Some members of the public strongly believed that Hwayoung was mistreated by the others members of T-ara and her agency. Outcry by T-ara's fan community led to the scheduling of a shut down,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> as well as the suspension of the members' activities,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> resulting in a huge blow to the group's popularity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite rumours of a possible return to T-ara,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> on September 6, 2012, Hwayoung announced under her via Twitter that she would be returning to the music industry with new image.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On September 10, Hwayoung later participated in the hip hop and rap event "Open Freestyle Day 2012", announcing that she was looking forward to studying music and eventually returning to the industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present:_Solo_career">2013–present: Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryu_Hwa-young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–present: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2013, it was reported that composer, <a href="/wiki/Shinsadong_Tiger" title="Shinsadong Tiger">Shinsadong Tiger</a> set up a meeting between talent agency Wellmade Yedang (now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imagine_Asia_Co.,_Ltd." title="Imagine Asia Co., Ltd.">Imagine Asia</a>) and Hwayoung when he found out that the agency was looking to produce new content.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On December 15, It was announced that Hwayoung signed an exclusive contract with Wellmade Yedang and prepare debuted as an actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On December 31, Hwayoung made an appearance at the "2014 Miller Countdown Party" as a DJ, alongside <a href="/wiki/Clon_(duo)" title="Clon (duo)">DJ Koo</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Park_Myung-soo" title="Park Myung-soo">Park Myung-soo</a> and TATA, becoming the youngest female DJ to ever participate in the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 2, 2014, Hwayoung was spotted filming outside the <a href="/wiki/Korea_International_Exhibition_Center" title="Korea International Exhibition Center">Korea International Exhibition Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Goyang" title="Goyang">Goyang</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and it was later revealed that she was to be the lead actress in singer, <a href="/wiki/Zia_(singer)" title="Zia (singer)">Zia</a>'s new music video for "Have You Ever Cried" (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">울어본 적 있나요</span>), which was released on January 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>After debuting as an actress in the two-episode drama special <i>Mother’s Choice</i>, Hwa-young successfully made a transition into acting after receiving praises for her roles in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TvN" title="TvN">tvN</a>'s romantic comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Ex-Girlfriend_Club" title="Ex-Girlfriend Club">Ex-Girlfriend Club</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/JTBC" title="JTBC">JTBC</a>'s coming-of-age drama <i><a href="/wiki/Age_of_Youth" title="Age of Youth">Age of Youth</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
