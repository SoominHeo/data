<div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">family name</a> is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liu_(surname)" title="Liu (surname)">Liu</a>.</div>
<p><b>Liu Hongcai</b> (born June 1955) is a Chinese diplomat, currently the deputy head of the <a href="/wiki/International_Liaison_Department_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="International Liaison Department of the Communist Party of China">International Liaison Department of the Communist Party of China</a>; he is the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ambassadors_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China#Current_PRC_Ambassadors" title="Ambassadors of the People's Republic of China">Chinese Ambassador to North Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CV_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CV-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Liu was born in 1955 in <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>. He studied Japanese at <a href="/wiki/Beijing_International_Studies_University" title="Beijing International Studies University">Beijing Second Foreign Language Institute</a> from 1972 to 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CV_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-CV-1">[1]</a></sup> In his final year, Liu joined the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> and was assigned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Department_of_the_Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="International Department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">International Department</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">Central Committee</a> upon graduation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-book_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-book-2">[2]</a></sup> Between the years of 1989 to 1992, he served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_Secretary" title="First Secretary">First Secretary</a> at the Chinese Embassy in Tokyo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CV_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-CV-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2003, he was appointed Vice Minister of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Department_of_the_Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="International Department of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">International Department</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-book_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-book-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2010, he succeeded <a href="/wiki/Liu_Xiaoming" title="Liu Xiaoming">Liu Xiaoming</a> as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ambassadors_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China#Current_PRC_Ambassadors" title="Ambassadors of the People's Republic of China">Chinese Ambassador to North Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CV_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-CV-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
