<p><b>René Girard</b> (born 4 April 1954 in <a href="/wiki/Vauvert" title="Vauvert">Vauvert</a>, Gard) is a French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manager_(football)" title="Manager (football)">football manager</a> and former player. He was most recently the manager of <a href="/wiki/FC_Nantes" title="FC Nantes">Nantes</a>, replacing the departing <a href="/wiki/Michel_Der_Zakarian" title="Michel Der Zakarian">Michel Der Zakarian</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ren%C3%A9_Girard_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Girard won seven caps, scoring one goal for <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> and was a member of the squad that finished fourth at the <a href="/wiki/1982_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1982 FIFA World Cup">1982 World Cup</a>. Playing for <a href="/wiki/FC_Girondins_de_Bordeaux" title="FC Girondins de Bordeaux">Bordeaux</a>, Girard won three <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> titles – in <a href="/wiki/1983%E2%80%9384_French_Division_1" title="1983–84 French Division 1">1983–84</a>, <a href="/wiki/1984%E2%80%9385_French_Division_1" title="1984–85 French Division 1">1984–85</a> and <a href="/wiki/1986%E2%80%9387_French_Division_1" title="1986–87 French Division 1">1986–87</a>, as well as two <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_France" title="Coupe de France">Coupes de France</a>, over <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1986_Coupe_de_France_Final" title="1986 Coupe de France Final">1986</a> and <a href="/wiki/1987_Coupe_de_France_Final" title="1987 Coupe de France Final">1987</a> editions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ren%C3%A9_Girard_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Girard has managed <a href="/wiki/N%C3%AEmes_Olympique" title="Nîmes Olympique">Nîmes</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RC_Strasbourg" title="RC Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pau_FC" title="Pau FC">Pau FC</a> and multiple <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France youth teams</a>. He coached the <a href="/wiki/France_national_under-21_football_team" title="France national under-21 football team">France national under-21 football team</a> from 2004–08. On 3 June 2009, he became the new head coach of <a href="/wiki/Montpellier_HSC" title="Montpellier HSC">Montpellier</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Rolland_Courbis" title="Rolland Courbis">Rolland Courbis</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Ligue_1" title="2011–12 Ligue 1">2011–12 season</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montpellier_HSC" title="Montpellier HSC">Montpellier</a> shocked the footballing world by winning its first <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1 title</a>, finishing the season with 82 points, three more than <a href="/wiki/Runner-up" title="Runner-up">runner-up</a> <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a>. He said after his team won it. "I think our triumph is a real shot in the arm for <a href="/wiki/Football_in_France" title="Football in France">French football</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> "It just goes to show that everyone can beat everyone and that money isn't the be-all and end-all. We're a club of mates, a <a href="/wiki/Montpellier_HSC" title="Montpellier HSC">club</a> that brings young players through and gives them a chance. "At the end of the day, it's worked out well for us. We played some great football, with a well-balanced team and I'm overwhelmed."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He signed for <a href="/wiki/Lille_OSC" title="Lille OSC">Lille</a> in July 2013, replacing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rudi_Garcia" title="Rudi Garcia">Rudi Garcia</a> who left for <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
