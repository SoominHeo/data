<p><b>Renesas Electronics Corporation</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ルネサス エレクトロニクス株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Runesasu Erekutoronikusu Kabushiki Gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">TYO</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://www2.tse.or.jp/tseHpFront/StockSearch.do?callJorEFlg=1&amp;method=&amp;topSearchStr=6723" rel="nofollow">6723</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor_industry" title="Semiconductor industry">semiconductor</a> manufacturer headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>. It has manufacturing, design and sales operations in around 20 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is the world's largest auto semiconductor maker in 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> one of the world's largest makers of semiconductor systems for <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">mobile phones</a>, the world's largest maker of <a href="/wiki/Microcontroller" title="Microcontroller">microcontrollers</a>, and the second largest maker of application processors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It also makes LCD drivers, RF ICs, <a href="/wiki/Mixed-signal_integrated_circuit" title="Mixed-signal integrated circuit">mixed-signal integrated circuits</a> and <a href="/wiki/System_on_a_chip" title="System on a chip">system on a chip</a> semiconductors.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renesas_Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Renesas" is an invented name and a contraction of Renaissance Semiconductor for Advanced Solutions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Renesas_Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Renesas Electronics started operation in April 2010, through the integration of <a href="/wiki/NEC" title="NEC">NEC Electronics</a> Corporation and Renesas Technology Corporation. NEC Electronics was established in November 2002 by a spin-off of the semiconductor operations of <a href="/wiki/NEC" title="NEC">NEC</a>. Renesas Technology was established on April 1, 2003, as a joint venture of <a href="/wiki/Hitachi" title="Hitachi">Hitachi</a>, Ltd. (55%) and <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi" title="Mitsubishi">Mitsubishi Electric</a> (45%).</p>
<p>In April 2009, Renesas Technology and NEC Electronics reached a basic agreement to merge by around April 2010. On April 1, 2010 NEC Electronics and Renesas Technology merged, forming Renesas Electronics which became the fourth largest semiconductor company according to iSuppli published data.</p>
<p>In December 2010 Renesas Mobile Corporation (RMC) was created by integrating the Mobile Multimedia Business Unit of Renesas with the acquired <a href="/wiki/Nokia" title="Nokia">Nokia Wireless Modem Business Unit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Renesas Electronics was adversely affected by the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and flooding in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>. In 2012, the company decided to restructure its business, including the sale and consolidation of its Japanese domestic plants, to get profitable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In December 2012, <a href="/wiki/INCJ" title="INCJ">INCJ</a>, Japan’s government-backed fund, and several key clients decided to invest in the company.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Through the investment, Renesas aimed to secure 150 billion yen as fresh capital by September 2013 and use it for realizing the Smart Society<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (February 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> through investment in the microcontroller and Analog &amp; Power semiconductor development, plant improvements, automotive and industrial semiconductor solutions and corporate acquisitions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In September 2013, <a href="/wiki/Broadcom" title="Broadcom">Broadcom</a> acquired most of Renesas Mobile Communication.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2014, Renesas Electronics introduced 5 new product versions in the RMLV0816B and RMLV0808B series of Advanced Low-Power SRAM (Advanced LP SRAM), the company’s flagship <a href="/wiki/Static_random-access_memory" title="Static random-access memory">static random-access memory</a> (SRAM) devices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
