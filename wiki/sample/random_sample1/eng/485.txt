<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Garda phone recordings scandal</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">Not to be confused with the <a href="/wiki/Irish_phone_tapping_scandal" title="Irish phone tapping scandal">Irish phone tapping scandal</a>, revealed in 1982.</div>
<p>The <b>Garda phone recordings scandal</b> refers to the widespread practice of recording phone calls in Ireland to and from <a href="/wiki/Garda_S%C3%ADoch%C3%A1na" title="Garda Síochána">Garda Síochána</a> police stations from the 1980s to November 2013, which was revealed in March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-it-inquiry-into-tapped-phones_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-it-inquiry-into-tapped-phones-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ii-major-revelations-on-recording-of-conversations_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ii-major-revelations-on-recording-of-conversations-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc-concerns-over-Gardai-secret-phone-recordings_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-concerns-over-Gardai-secret-phone-recordings-3">[3]</a></sup> The scandal unfolded while <a href="/wiki/Fine_Gael" title="Fine Gael">Fine Gael</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Ireland)" title="Labour Party (Ireland)">Labour Party</a> were in office, though the time period involved also covered successive governments during which <a href="/wiki/Fianna_F%C3%A1il" title="Fianna Fáil">Fianna Fáil</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_(Ireland)" title="Green Party (Ireland)">Green Party</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Progressive_Democrats" title="Progressive Democrats">Progressive Democrats</a> were <a href="/wiki/Irish_cabinets_since_1919" title="Irish cabinets since 1919">all in power</a>. Among those implicated in the scandal are the <a href="/wiki/Taoiseach" title="Taoiseach">Taoiseach</a> (prime minister) <a href="/wiki/Enda_Kenny" title="Enda Kenny">Enda Kenny</a>, justice minister <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shatter" title="Alan Shatter">Alan Shatter</a> (since resigned), Secretary General of the Department of Justice <a href="/wiki/Brian_Purcell" title="Brian Purcell">Brian Purcell</a>, Secretary General of the Department of the Taoiseach <a href="/wiki/Martin_Fraser" title="Martin Fraser">Martin Fraser</a>, attorney general <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1ire_Whelan" title="Máire Whelan">Máire Whelan</a>, Garda Commissioner <a href="/wiki/Martin_Callinan" title="Martin Callinan">Martin Callinan</a> (since resigned), as well as <a href="/wiki/Independent_News_%26_Media" title="Independent News &amp; Media">Independent News &amp; Media</a>, Ireland's largest and most powerful media organisation.</p>
<p>The term <a href="/wiki/GUBU" title="GUBU">GUBU</a> (grotesque, unbelievable, bizarre and unprecedented), first used in 1982 to describe the arrest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malcolm_MacArthur" title="Malcolm MacArthur">Malcolm MacArthur</a> in the home of Attorney General <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Connolly" title="Patrick Connolly">Patrick Connolly</a> was invoked to describe the Garda controversy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent-2014-03-26_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-2014-03-26-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Shane Phelan, Public Affairs Editor at the <i><a href="/wiki/Irish_Independent" title="Irish Independent">Irish Independent</a></i> commented "it is now safe to say we have finally reached GUBU territory",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent-2014-03-26_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-2014-03-26-4">[4]</a></sup> while Michael Clifford of the <i><a href="/wiki/Irish_Examiner" title="Irish Examiner">Irish Examiner</a></i> observed "More than 30 years after the original GUBU moment, here was another one."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garda_phone_recordings_scandal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Irish_phone_tapping_scandal" title="Irish phone tapping scandal">1980s Irish phone tapping scandal</a></div>
<p>While debating the question of penalty points on state television with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_(politician)" title="Independent (politician)">independent</a> deputy <a href="/wiki/Mick_Wallace" title="Mick Wallace">Mick Wallace</a> in May 2013, justice minister <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shatter" title="Alan Shatter">Alan Shatter</a> divulged hitherto publicly unknown personal information to viewers about Wallace in an attempt to undermine his opponent. Shatter later admitted receiving his information from <a href="/wiki/Garda_Commissioner" title="Garda Commissioner">Garda Commissioner</a> <a href="/wiki/Martin_Callinan" title="Martin Callinan">Martin Callinan</a> but claimed he was not "in the business of collecting secret files on politicians".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Callinan subsequently referred to whistleblowers, such as Maurice McCabe and John Wilson, as "disgusting" at a Public Accounts Committee in 2014 (see also <a href="/wiki/Garda_whistleblower_scandal" title="Garda whistleblower scandal">Garda whistleblower scandal</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garda_phone_recordings_scandal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2014 revelations of surveillance came on Tuesday 25 March, the same day that Martin Callinan resigned as Garda Commissioner over his remarks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-it-inquiry-into-tapped-phones_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-it-inquiry-into-tapped-phones-1">[1]</a></sup> On that day it was revealed that the former commissioner had written to the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Justice_and_Equality" title="Department of Justice and Equality">Department of Justice and Equality</a> about the recordings over two weeks before the revelations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department-9">[9]</a></sup> He wrote that he had established a working group to deal with the recordings and that they would report to him once they had consulted the <a href="/wiki/Attorney_General_of_Ireland" title="Attorney General of Ireland">Attorney General</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department-9">[9]</a></sup> He wrote that he had spoken to the Attorney General and that he expected to talk to the <a href="/wiki/Data_Protection_Commissioner" title="Data Protection Commissioner">Data Protection Commissioner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department-9">[9]</a></sup> He wrote that the Attorney General had advised that the recordings be collected and an inventory be made identifying them by station, date and condition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department-9">[9]</a></sup> He also wrote that he had ordered all recording of non-999 calls to Garda stations cease and that this happened on 23 November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department-9">[9]</a></sup> He wrote that <a href="/wiki/Minister_for_Justice_and_Equality" title="Minister for Justice and Equality">Minister for Justice and Equality</a> <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shatter" title="Alan Shatter">Alan Shatter</a> be informed of details of the recordings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-rte-callinan-wrote-to-department-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 March, <a href="/wiki/Taoiseach" title="Taoiseach">Taoiseach</a> <a href="/wiki/Enda_Kenny" title="Enda Kenny">Enda Kenny</a> informed the Dáil that he had sent <a href="/wiki/Brian_Purcell" title="Brian Purcell">Brian Purcell</a>, the Secretary General of the Department of Justice, to Callinan the day before the Garda Commissioner's departure. <a href="/wiki/Fianna_F%C3%A1il" title="Fianna Fáil">Fianna Fáil</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Miche%C3%A1l_Martin" title="Micheál Martin">Micheál Martin</a> said this meant Kenny had effectively "sacked" Callinan. Kenny also said that he had been personally briefed on Garda surveillance by the Attorney General as the AG did not wish to speak of the matter over the telephone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Response">Response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garda_phone_recordings_scandal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was soon clear the revelation that telephone calls were recorded <i>en masse</i> would have a major impact on court cases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-it-inquiry-into-tapped-phones_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-it-inquiry-into-tapped-phones-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ii-major-revelations-on-recording-of-conversations_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ii-major-revelations-on-recording-of-conversations-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc-concerns-over-Gardai-secret-phone-recordings_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-concerns-over-Gardai-secret-phone-recordings-3">[3]</a></sup> This has indeed been the case; the first trial to be affected was that of two men suspected of IRA membership on 26 March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Irish_Council_for_Civil_Liberties" title="Irish Council for Civil Liberties">Irish Council for Civil Liberties</a> said: ”For weeks, the ICCL has been calling for the creation of a proper statutory inquiry with the fact-finding powers necessary to restore public trust in the accountability of An Garda Síochána. Instead, the Government has chosen to refer each successive crisis to a different non-judicial mechanism, none of which has been granted the power comprehensively to establish the truth. Today's revelations regarding the recording of calls to and from Garda stations may have grave ramifications for the administration of justice and this is the day when the rot must stop”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bugged_letterkenny_garda_station_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-bugged_letterkenny_garda_station-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In response to Callinan's resignation, his Deputy Commissioner Noirín O'Sullivan was selected as provisional Garda Commissioner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The government also set up a statutory <a href="/wiki/Commission_of_investigation_(Ireland)" title="Commission of investigation (Ireland)">commission of investigation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-it-inquiry-into-tapped-phones_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-it-inquiry-into-tapped-phones-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ii-major-revelations-on-recording-of-conversations_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-ii-major-revelations-on-recording-of-conversations-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc-concerns-over-Gardai-secret-phone-recordings_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-concerns-over-Gardai-secret-phone-recordings-3">[3]</a></sup> The "Commission of Investigation (Certain Matters relative to An Garda Síochána and other persons)" is commonly called the <a href="/wiki/Fennelly_Commission" title="Fennelly Commission">Fennelly Commission</a>, after its sole member, <a href="/wiki/Nial_Fennelly" title="Nial Fennelly">Nial Fennelly</a>, a former <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Ireland" title="Supreme Court of Ireland">Supreme Court</a> justice.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Locations">Locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Garda_phone_recordings_scandal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to cabinet minister <a href="/wiki/Simon_Coveney" title="Simon Coveney">Simon Coveney</a>, 2,400 physical tape recordings exist from before 2008, as well as an unknown number of digital recordings made after this date. Coveney was unable to identify the listeners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Telephone calls at all 26 divisional headquarters of the Garda Síochána were tapped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Garda stations at the following locations were confirmed to have had all calls into and out of them tapped for at least three decades:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bray" title="Bray">Bray</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Letterkenny" title="Letterkenny">Letterkenny</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bugged_letterkenny_garda_station_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-bugged_letterkenny_garda_station-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Waterford" title="Waterford">Waterford</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
