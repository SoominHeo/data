<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ruanda-Urundi franc</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Ruanda-Urundi franc</b> was a currency issued for the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_colonial_empire" title="Belgian colonial empire">Belgian mandate territory</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ruanda-Urundi" title="Ruanda-Urundi">Ruanda-Urundi</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burundi" title="Burundi">Burundi</a>) in <a href="/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">East Africa</a> between 1960 and 1964. The currency replaced the <a href="/wiki/Congolese_franc" title="Congolese franc">Belgian Congo franc</a> which had also circulated in Ruanda-Urundi from 1916 until 1960 when the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Congo" title="Belgian Congo">Belgian Congo</a> became independent, leaving Ruanda-Urundi as the sole Belgian colonial possession in Africa. With the independence of Rwanda and Burundi in 1962, the shared Ruanda-Urundi franc continued to circulate until 1964 when it was eventually replaced by two separate national currencies.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruanda-Urundi_franc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The franc became the currency of Rwanda and Burundi in 1916, when the two countries were occupied by Belgium and the <a href="/wiki/Congolese_franc" title="Congolese franc">Belgian Congo franc</a> replaced the <a href="/wiki/German_East_African_rupie" title="German East African rupie">German East African rupie</a>. In 1960, the Belgian Congo franc was replaced by the Ruanda-Urundi franc, issued by the Banque d’Emission du Rwanda et du Burundi (Issuing Bank of Rwanda and Burundi) or BERB. This circulated after independence until January 1964,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> when Rwanda and Burundi introduced their own currencies, the <a href="/wiki/Burundian_franc" title="Burundian franc">Burundian franc</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rwandan_franc" title="Rwandan franc">Rwandan franc</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coins">Coins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruanda-Urundi_franc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A single denomination was issued, the 1 franc, between 1960 and 1964.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Banknotes">Banknotes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruanda-Urundi_franc&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Banknotes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1960 to 1963, the BERB issued notes in denominations of 5, 10, 20, 50, 100, 500 and 1000 francs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1964, Burundi overprinted all of these denominations for use in Burundi, whilst Rwanda overprinted all but the 5 and 10 franc notes for use in Rwanda.</p>
