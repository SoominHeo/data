<p>The <b>seal of <a href="/wiki/Rwanda" title="Rwanda">Rwanda</a></b> is the national symbol and used by the government. It was restyled in 2001 to match the color scheme of the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Rwanda" title="Flag of Rwanda">new national flag</a>. The text reads "Republic of Rwanda - Unity, Work, Patriotism" in <a href="/wiki/Kinyarwanda" title="Kinyarwanda">Kinyarwanda</a>. The central tribal devices are surmounted on a <a href="/wiki/Gear" title="Gear">cog</a> <a href="/wiki/Wheel" title="Wheel">wheel</a> and encircled by a <a href="/wiki/Reef_knot" title="Reef knot">square knot</a>.</p>
<p>The details of the seal are laid out in Article 3(2) of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Rwanda" title="Constitution of Rwanda">Constitution of Rwanda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The previous emblem dated from the 1960s-the colors green, yellow and red represented peace; the nation's hope for future development; and the people. The device and the flag itself were changed because they had become associated with the brutality of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rwandan_Genocide" title="Rwandan Genocide">Rwandan Genocide</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
