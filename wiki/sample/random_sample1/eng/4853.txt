<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Lludd Llaw Eraint</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Lludd Llaw Ereint</b>, "Lludd of the Silver Hand", son of <a href="/wiki/Beli_Mawr" title="Beli Mawr">Beli Mawr</a>, is a legendary hero from <a href="/wiki/Welsh_mythology" title="Welsh mythology">Welsh mythology</a>. As <b>Nudd Llaw Ereint</b> (the earlier form of his name, <a href="/wiki/Cognate" title="Cognate">cognate</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_mythology" title="Irish mythology">Irish</a> <a href="/wiki/Nuada_Airgetl%C3%A1m" title="Nuada Airgetlám">Nuada Airgetlám</a>, derived from the pre-Roman Celtic god <a href="/wiki/Nodens" title="Nodens">Nodens</a>) he is the father of <a href="/wiki/Gwyn_ap_Nudd" title="Gwyn ap Nudd">Gwyn ap Nudd</a>. He is probably the source of king <a href="/wiki/Lud_son_of_Heli" title="Lud son of Heli">Lud</a> from <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_of_Monmouth" title="Geoffrey of Monmouth">Geoffrey of Monmouth</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_the_Kings_of_Britain" title="History of the Kings of Britain">History of the Kings of Britain</a>.</p>
<p>In the <i><a href="/wiki/Mabinogion" title="Mabinogion">Mabinogion</a></i> tale of <i><a href="/wiki/Lludd_and_Llefelys" title="Lludd and Llefelys">Lludd and Llefelys</a></i>, which seems heavily influenced by Geoffrey of Monmouth's work, he is the ruler of <a href="/wiki/Prehistoric_Britain" title="Prehistoric Britain">Britain</a> while his brother <a href="/wiki/Llefelys" title="Llefelys">Llefelys</a> ruled <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a>. Lludd calls on Llefelys to rid Britain of three plagues then afflicting the kingdom. According to legend, there was once a memorial to Lludd at the site of <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="St Paul's Cathedral">St Paul's Cathedral</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Ludgate" title="Ludgate">Ludgate</a>, which is named after him.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_plagues_of_Lludd.E2.80.99s_reign">The plagues of Lludd’s reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lludd_Llaw_Eraint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The plagues of Lludd’s reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The first plague was that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coranian" title="Coranian">Coranians</a>.</li>
<li>The second was that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_Dragon" title="White Dragon">white</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Y_Ddraig_Goch" title="Y Ddraig Goch">red dragons</a>. One of the dragons represented the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brython" title="Brython">Brythons</a>, while the other represented the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon" title="Anglo-Saxon">Anglo-Saxon</a> invaders of Britain. On the eve of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beltaine" title="Beltaine">May Day</a>, the two dragons would begin to fight. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_Dragon" title="White Dragon">White Dragon</a> would strive to overcome the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Y_Ddraig_Goch" title="Y Ddraig Goch">Red Dragon</a>, making the Red cry out a fearful shriek which was heard over every Brythonic hearth. This shriek went through people’s hearts, scaring them so much that the men lost their hue and their strength, women lost their children, young men and the maidens lost their senses, and all the animals and trees and the earth and the waters, were left barren. The plague was finally eradicated by catching the dragons and burying both of them in a rock pit at <a href="/wiki/Dinas_Emrys" title="Dinas Emrys">Dinas Emrys</a> in <a href="/wiki/Snowdonia" title="Snowdonia">Snowdonia</a>, north <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, the securest place in Britain at that time. The dragons were caught by digging out a pit under the exact point where the dragons would fall down exhausted after fighting. This place was at <a href="/wiki/Oxford" title="Oxford">Oxford</a>, which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lludd" title="Lludd">Lludd</a> found to be the exact centre of the island when he measured the island of <a href="/wiki/Prydain" title="Prydain">Britain</a>. The pit had a satin covering over it and a cauldron of mead in it at the bottom. First, the dragons fought by the pit in the form of terrific animals. Then they began to fight in the air over the pit in the form of dragons. Then exhausted with the fighting, they fell down on the pit in the form of pigs and sank into the pit drawing the satin covering under them into the cauldron at the bottom of the pit whereupon they drank the mead and fell asleep. The dragons were then wrapped up in the satin covering and placed in the pit to be buried at Dinas Emrys.</li>
<li>The third plague was the plunder committed by a giant who wore strong, heavy armour and carried a hamper. His nocturnal entrance was heralded by soporific illusions and musical sounds which lulled the members of Lludd’s Court to sleep. Once the court was asleep, he would put all the food and provisions of meat and drink of Lludd’s Court into his commodious hamper and take it away with him. This recurring theft constituted the third plague of Lludd’s reign. Lludd was only able to stop the recurring theft by confronting the intruder. He was able to avoid falling asleep to the soporific illusions and musical sounds by frequently dipping his head in a vessel of cold water by his side. Upon confronting the magician, a fierce encounter ensued in which glittering fire flew out from their arms until Lludd overcame the magician. Thereupon, Lludd granted him mercy and made him his loyal vassal.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lludd_Llaw_Eraint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Nudd</i>, <a href="/wiki/Cognate" title="Cognate">cognate</a> with the Irish <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuada" title="Nuada">Nuada</a></i> and related to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romano-British" title="Romano-British">Romano-British</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Nodens" title="Nodens">Nodens</a></i>, probably derives from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proto-Celtic" title="Proto-Celtic">Celtic</a> <a href="/wiki/Word_stem" title="Word stem">stem</a> *<i>noudont-</i> or <i>*noudent-</i>, which <a href="/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien" title="J. R. R. Tolkien">J. R. R. Tolkien</a> suggested was related to a <a href="/wiki/Germanic_languages" title="Germanic languages">Germanic</a> root meaning "acquire, have the use of", earlier "to catch, entrap (as a hunter)". Making the connection with Nuada and Lludd's hand, he detected "an echo of the ancient fame of the magic hand of Nodens the Catcher".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Similarly, <a href="/wiki/Julius_Pokorny" title="Julius Pokorny">Julius Pokorny</a> derives the name from a <a href="/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language" title="Proto-Indo-European language">Proto-Indo-European</a> root <i>*neu-d-</i> meaning "acquire, utilise, go fishing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
