<p><b>Lee E. McMahon</b> (1931–1989) was an American <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a>. He was a graduate of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis_University" title="St. Louis University">St. Louis University</a> and obtained a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ph.D." title="Ph.D.">Ph.D.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">Psychology</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harvard" title="Harvard">Harvard</a>. He worked for <a href="/wiki/Bell_Labs" title="Bell Labs">Bell Labs</a> from 1963 until 1989, and is best known for his contributions to early versions of the <a href="/wiki/Unix" title="Unix">Unix</a> operating system, in particular the <a href="/wiki/Sed" title="Sed">sed</a> stream editor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> McMahon contributed to the development of <a href="/wiki/Comm" title="Comm">comm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qsort" title="Qsort">qsort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grep" title="Grep">grep</a>, index, cref, <a href="/wiki/Tip_(Unix_utility)" title="Tip (Unix utility)">cu</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Datakit" title="Datakit">Datakit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reader_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-reader-2">[2]</a></sup> and devised the <a href="/wiki/McMahon_system_tournament" title="McMahon system tournament">McMahon system tournament</a>.</p>
