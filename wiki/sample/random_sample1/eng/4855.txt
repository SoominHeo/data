<p><b>Lý Cao Tông</b> (1176–1210), born <b>Lý Long Trát</b> or <b>Lý Long Cán</b>, was the seventh emperor of the <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_dynasty" title="Lý dynasty">Lý dynasty</a>, ruling for 35 years. Some consider his reign to begin the falling into decay of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_Dynasty" title="Lý Dynasty">Lý Dynasty</a> because of his erroneous determinations. However, many historians agree that he had an important influence in Vietnamese history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BD_Cao_T%C3%B4ng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="infobox" style="width: 22em">

<th scope="row" style="font-weight:normal;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ch%E1%BB%AF_H%C3%A1n" title="Chữ Hán">Chữ Hán</a></th>
<td><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%9D%8E" title="wikt:李">李</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%AB%98" title="wikt:高">高</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%AE%97" title="wikt:宗">宗</a></td>

<p>He was the sixth son of Lý Anh Tông. His mother was Đỗ Thụy Châu who later became Empress Dowager Đỗ. He was born Lý Long Cán (or Trát) on May 25, 1173 according to the Eastern calendar. He was enthroned when he was very young, at the age of three, as one of the youngest Emperors in Vietnam's history. Prior to this, his predecessor Emperor Lý Anh Tông disposed the former Crown Prince Long Xưởng and replaced Prince Cán with this title. <a href="/wiki/T%C3%B4_Hi%E1%BA%BFn_Th%C3%A0nh" title="Tô Hiến Thành">Tô Hiến Thành</a> was trusted to become the regent to help the young Emperor run the court and administer his nation. It is often noted that his age and inexperience proved to be a minimal disadvantage in his ability to govern.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Regent_T.C3.B4_Hi.E1.BA.BFn_Th.C3.A0nh">The Regent Tô Hiến Thành</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BD_Cao_T%C3%B4ng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Regent Tô Hiến Thành">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/T%C3%B4_Hi%E1%BA%BFn_Th%C3%A0nh" title="Tô Hiến Thành">Tô Hiến Thành</a> was considered a talent and competent official when he was conferred the title Regent. After the death of Anh Tông, his uppermost consort (now became Empress Dowager <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chi%C3%AAu_Linh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chiêu Linh (page does not exist)">Chiêu Linh</a>) wanted to dethrone her son <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Long_X%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Fng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Long Xưởng (page does not exist)">Long Xưởng</a>, who was the former Crown Prince. But thanks to Tô Hiến Thành's peremptoriness, Cán eventually became the successor of the throne.</p>
<p>Tô Hiến Thành fell ill not long after. At death's door, he recommended Empress Dowager Đỗ an official named <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tr%E1%BA%A7n_Trung_T%C3%A1&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Trần Trung Tá (page does not exist)">Trần Trung Tá</a>. She said a good word for his recommendation but it was merely something to please the dying people. After he died in 1179, she chose <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%97_An_Di&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đỗ An Di (page does not exist)">Đỗ An Di</a> to become the Regent for her son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1181, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BD_Long_X%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Fng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lý Long Xưởng (page does not exist)">Lý Long Xưởng</a> led his army in a mutiny and looted the capital. One year later, Empress Dowager Đỗ appointed Lý Kính Tu to teach her son. He was a capable official tutoring the young emperor in literature and morality. Thereafter, Empress Dowager Chiêu Linh and his son gave up the plot to mutiny.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Epicurean_Emperor">Epicurean Emperor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BD_Cao_T%C3%B4ng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Epicurean Emperor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Emperor was meek and lenient while young, but after he grew and directly administered his reign, he became more and more cruel and sent the nation into turmoil. He loved to hunt and built himself large palaces at his people's expense. The codes and rules of his reign were obscure and immoral, leading the people to conflict with the local administration and rebel from the injustice and drudgery. At worst, they devastated buildings and looted villages.</p>
<p>In March 1189, Cao Tông took a trip around the nation and built pagoda or temples wherever a deity or spirit was said to reside. Nine years later, he built Nghênh Thiềm palace. The apex of his indulgence was in 1203, when he had numerous palaces built. By 1208, there were multiple famines and many people died of starvation. Despite the state of his country, the Emperor continued to indulge in pleasure and personal building projects.</p>
<p>An official named <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cao_%C4%90%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Dng_Long&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cao Đường Long (page does not exist)">Cao Đường Long</a> observed a strange-bird nesting on the roof of the unfinished palace Kính Thiên (means Respecting the Heaven)and predicted that a new and more powerful dynasty would soon supersede the current dynasty. He advised the Emperor to stop the injurious building projects, but the Emperor ignored him and instead listened to a eunuch named Phạm Bỉnh Di. The Emperor also ignored the looting and robberies outside the citadel.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Turbulent_era">Turbulent era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BD_Cao_T%C3%B4ng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Turbulent era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Some_rebellions">Some rebellions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BD_Cao_T%C3%B4ng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Some rebellions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At that time, the affairs of the state were abandoned. Civil belief was replaced by disgust. Many revolts and secessions exploded amongst the peasantry and small local nobles. The court tried to suppress the rebellions, but it was to no actual effect.</p>
<p>In 1192, the civilians from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=C%E1%BB%95_Ho%E1%BA%B1ng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cổ Hoằng (page does not exist)">Cổ Hoằng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thanh_H%C3%B3a_Province" title="Thanh Hóa Province">Thanh Hóa</a> rioted. The rebellions of Câu Diễn and Đinh Khả followed in 1198, who professed to be the descendants of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C4%90inh_Ti%C3%AAn_Ho%C3%A0ng" title="Đinh Tiên Hoàng">Đinh Tiên Hoàng</a>.</p>
<p>In August 1203, the king of <a href="/wiki/Champa" title="Champa">Champa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vidyanandana" title="Vidyanandana">Vidyanandana</a>, entered <a href="/wiki/C%E1%BB%ADa_L%C3%B2" title="Cửa Lò">Cửa Lò</a> seeking asylum from a Khmer invasion. Vidyanandana killed the governor of <a href="/wiki/Ngh%E1%BB%87_An_Province" title="Nghệ An Province">Nghệ An</a>, Pham Gieng. Vidyanandana then fled, never to be heard from again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maspero_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Maspero-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:79–80</sup></p>
<p>There was a potent force rebelling in September 1203 under the leading of two people from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i_Ho%C3%A0ng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đại Hoàng (page does not exist)">Đại Hoàng</a> River. Many years ago they had accused <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%C3%A0m_D%C4%A9_M%C3%B4ng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đàm Dĩ Mông (page does not exist)">Đàm Dĩ Mông</a> of bleeding monetary and civil property. They used the turbulent society and lack of a central power to establish a force to oppose the court. All generals sent to suppress them failed. The first generals were Trần Lệnh Hinh and Từ Anh Nhữ, a minister, who were both killed, and the last general was Đỗ Kính Tu.</p>
<p>In 1207, in the mountainous area of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T%E1%BA%A3n_Vi%C3%AAn" title="Tản Viên">Tản Viên</a> ( now <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A0_T%C3%A2y_Province" title="Hà Tây Province">Hà Tây</a> ) there has a revolt of some minorities, which was quite prestigious.</p>
<p>In addition to the internal conflict and constant hunger during this time, the Song Dynasty invaded with their Northern Army and the Vietnamese living in the areas tried to flee.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_shifty_official_Ph.E1.BA.A1m_Du">The shifty official Phạm Du</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BD_Cao_T%C3%B4ng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The shifty official Phạm Du">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>March 1207, two squires in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=H%E1%BB%93ng_Ch%C3%A2u&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hồng Châu (page does not exist)">Hồng Châu</a> area ( now <a href="/wiki/H%E1%BA%A3i_D%C6%B0%C6%A1ng_Province" title="Hải Dương Province">Hải Dương</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haiphong" title="Haiphong">Hải Phòng</a>) <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90o%C3%A0n_Th%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đoàn Thượng (page does not exist)">Đoàn Thượng</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90o%C3%A0n_Ch%E1%BB%A7&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đoàn Chủ (page does not exist)">Đoàn Chủ</a> rioted, built fortifications, and conferred the title Lord on themselves. Cao Tông dispatched a great amount of soldiers, dividing them on some corps to conquest the rebellion. There were 4 main corps from 4 directions: the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i_Th%C3%B4ng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đại Thông (page does not exist)">Đại Thông</a> corps of Đàm Dĩ Mông, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kh%E1%BA%A3_Li%E1%BB%85u&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Khả Liễu (page does not exist)">Khả Liễu</a> corps of <a href="/wiki/Ph%E1%BA%A1m_B%E1%BB%89nh_Di" title="Phạm Bỉnh Di">Phạm Bỉnh Di</a>, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ph%C3%B9_%C4%90%C3%A1i&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Phù Đái (page does not exist)">Phù Đái</a> corps of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tr%E1%BA%A7n_Hinh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Trần Hinh (page does not exist)">Trần Hinh</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nam_S%C3%A1ch" title="Nam Sách">Nam Sách</a> corps of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=B%E1%BA%A3o_Trinh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bảo Trinh (page does not exist)">Bảo Trinh</a>; they all would assemble to suppress <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90o%C3%A0n_Th%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đoàn Thượng (page does not exist)">Đoàn Thượng</a>'s army. Recognizing that confronting Du with such a large army would kill them all, Thượng bribed Du with costly furniture and willingly followed him, establishing a coalition between Du and Thượng. Thanks to Du's efforts to implore Cao Tông for forgiving Thượng, Thượng saved his neck.</p>
<p>Du went to <a href="/wiki/Ngh%E1%BB%87_An_Province" title="Nghệ An Province">Nghệ An</a> to manage the army in 1209. This province underwent a catastrophic starvation whereby a large amount of people died of hunger. The survivors left their homes and wandered, penniless. Most of them became beggars. Hence Phạm Du told the king that: "The society now is chaotic, and the pillaging and the rebellions are uncontrolled. Please allow me to recruit soldier for establishing a self-defense army ...."</p>
<p>Cao Tông agreed with him. Du recruited the local people to blockade the transport system, both on land and water. Cao Tông identified his army as rebellious and sent Phạm Bỉnh Di with an army from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BA%B1ng_Ch%C3%A2u&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đằng Châu (page does not exist)">Đằng Châu</a> <a href="/wiki/H%C6%B0ng_Y%C3%AAn_Province" title="Hưng Yên Province">Hưng Yên</a> to conquer him. Du retreated to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=C%E1%BB%95_Mi%E1%BB%87t&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cổ Miệt (page does not exist)">Cổ Miệt</a> and united with the army of Đòan Thương and Đoàn Chủ from Hồng Châu. Pham Bỉnh Di was defeated in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BA%B1ng_Ch%C3%A2u&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đằng Châu (page does not exist)">Đằng Châu</a>. In February 1209, Bỉnh Di fought again with an army from Đằng Châu and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kho%C3%A1i_Ch%C3%A2u" title="Khoái Châu">Khoái Châu</a> and defeated Du, who fled in retreat. He confiscated all of Du's property and set it on fire.</p>
<p>Đoàn Thượng's army was overthrown in April 1209. Phạm Du bribed high-ranking officials in the capital and accused Bỉnh Di of a ferocious massacre. Cao Tông allowed Trần Hinh to convoke Phạm Du to come to the court and also called Bỉnh Di back. Phạm Du returned to the capital first and told the king false rumors about Bỉnh Di. When Bỉnh Di arrived, Cao Tông sent both him and his son to prison.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="General_Qu.C3.A1ch_B.E1.BB.91c">General Quách Bốc</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BD_Cao_T%C3%B4ng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: General Quách Bốc">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Qu%C3%A1ch_B%E1%BB%91c" title="Quách Bốc">Quách Bốc</a></div>
<p>After being told of Bỉnh Di's imprisonment, one of his subordinates named <a href="/wiki/Qu%C3%A1ch_B%E1%BB%91c" title="Quách Bốc">Quách Bốc</a> led his army to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i_Thanh&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đại Thanh (page does not exist)">Đại Thanh</a> Gate of the citadel and overthrew it to save *. . Phạm Du and Phạm Kinh killed Bỉnh Di and his son and then escaped the citadel with Cao Tông.</p>
<p>Quách Bốc occupied the citadel and enthroned the young prince Lý Thầm. Cao Tông fled to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tam_N%C3%B4ng" title="Tam Nông">Tam Nông</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ph%C3%BA_Th%E1%BB%8D_Province" title="Phú Thọ Province">Phú Thọ</a> and lodged at the residence of Hà Vạn, who was a minority leader holding a substantial force. The Crown Prince Sảm, who later became the succeeding king <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_Hu%E1%BB%87_T%C3%B4ng" title="Lý Huệ Tông">Lý Huệ Tông</a>, fled to Hải Ấp, <a href="/wiki/Th%C3%A1i_B%C3%ACnh_Province" title="Thái Bình Province">Thái Bình</a> with his mother, Noble Consort Đoàn and his two younger sisters. Sảm stayed at the residence of Trần Lý and married his daughter Trần Thị Dung. Then he conferred the title Minh Tự on Trần Lý, and the title Commander of Anterior Citadel on Trần Lý's brother-in-law <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=T%C3%B4_Trung_T%E1%BB%AB&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tô Trung Từ (page does not exist)">Tô Trung Từ</a>. <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_Th%C3%A1i_T%E1%BB%95" title="Lý Thái Tổ">Lý Thái Tổ</a> also held the title of Commander of the Anterior Citadel during the time he was an official of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anterior_L%C3%AA_Dynasty" title="Anterior Lê Dynasty">Anterior Lê Dynasty</a> ). They recruited soldiers from the surrounding area and fought against Quách Bốc.</p>
<p>When that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_S%E1%BA%A3m" title="Lý Sảm">Lý Sảm</a> established his own court and arbitrarily conferred titles, Cao Tông sought to suppress his military power. He sent Phạm Du to associate with Đoàn Thượng, but Du missed the meeting with Đoàn Thượng because he spent time with the Princess Thiên Cực. Du was killed by the army of the landlord of <a href="/wiki/B%E1%BA%AFc_Giang_Province" title="Bắc Giang Province">Bắc Giang</a> when crossing over Ma Lãng to go to another meeting.</p>
<p>Trần Lý and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=T%C3%B4_Trung_T%E1%BB%B1&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tô Trung Tự (page does not exist)">Tô Trung Tự</a> led their army to the capital to defeat Quách Bốc. At the end of 1209, they suppressed the rebellion Cao Tông was once again king. Trần Lý died in battle. Because Phạm Du was dead and Tô Trung Từ held the most power in the court, Cao Tông was forced to work with him. Even though Đàm Dĩ Mông worked with the rebel Quách Bốc when he occupied the citadel, he became the Thái úy – a rather high official.</p>
<p>There is no document confirming how Quách Bốc and Lý Thầm died.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_ending_of_the_reign_and_some_comments">The ending of the reign and some comments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=L%C3%BD_Cao_T%C3%B4ng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The ending of the reign and some comments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>October 28, 1210, Cao Tông died at the age of 38 at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Th%C3%A1nh_Th%E1%BB%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thánh Thọ (page does not exist)">Thánh Thọ</a> palace. The affairs of the court were committed to Đỗ Kính Tu who became the Regent. The Crown Prince Sảm acceded to the throne and became the Emperor <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_Hu%E1%BB%87_T%C3%B4ng" title="Lý Huệ Tông">Lý Huệ Tông</a>. The society was more and more turbulent. Ten years later the Lý Dynasty was replaced by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BA%A7n_Dynasty" title="Trần Dynasty">Trần Dynasty</a></p>
<p>Historians criticize Cao Tông about his incompetence leading to the Lý Dynasty being lost. There were some comments given by Vietnamese ancient historians:</p>
<ul>
<li>Cao Tông was an epicurean Emperor, who was lost in lust, hunting, spirits, music. He also wasted much money in building his own palace, pagoda ....</li>
<li>He had many competent officials such as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C4%90%E1%BB%97_K%C3%ADnh_Tu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Đỗ Kính Tu (page does not exist)">Đỗ Kính Tu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ph%E1%BA%A1m_B%E1%BB%89nh_Di" title="Phạm Bỉnh Di">Phạm Bỉnh Di</a> but he didn’t hear them, per contra, he killed Phạm Bỉnh Di and heard <a href="/wiki/Ph%E1%BA%A1m_Du" title="Phạm Du">Phạm Du</a>, who was a foxy and deceitful official.</li>
<li>Quách Bốc's force could not be very powerful but instead of confronting and oppressing him, Cao Tông and his subordinates fled cowardly.</li>
<li>Đàm Dĩ Mông was a disloyal official, but instead of killing him, Cao Tông conferred a high-rank on him.</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/L%C3%AA_V%C4%83n_H%C6%B0u" title="Lê Văn Hưu">Lê Văn Hưu</a> (1230–1322), a historian of the next generation, criticized Lý Cao Tông for presuming himself to be a Buddha.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
