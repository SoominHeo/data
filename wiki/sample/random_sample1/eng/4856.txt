<p><b>Caroline Lee Radziwill</b> (née <b>Bouvier</b>; born March 3, 1933) is an American <a href="/wiki/Socialite" title="Socialite">socialite</a>, public relations executive, interior decorator, and former actress. She is the younger sister of the late <a href="/wiki/First_Lady" title="First Lady">First Lady</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacqueline_Kennedy_Onassis" title="Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis">Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis</a>, and sister-in-law of President <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>. Her niece <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caroline_Bouvier_Kennedy" title="Caroline Bouvier Kennedy">Caroline Bouvier Kennedy</a> is named after her. She was named to the <a href="/wiki/International_Best_Dressed_List" title="International Best Dressed List">International Best Dressed List</a> Hall of Fame in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_ancestry">Early life and ancestry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Radziwill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and ancestry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Caroline Lee Bouvier was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southampton_(town),_New_York" title="Southampton (town), New York">Southampton, New York</a> to <a href="/wiki/Stockbroker" title="Stockbroker">stockbroker</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Vernou_Bouvier_III" title="John Vernou Bouvier III">John Vernou Bouvier III</a> and socialite <a href="/wiki/Janet_Lee_Bouvier" title="Janet Lee Bouvier">Janet Norton Lee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-obit1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriages_and_children">Marriages and children</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Radziwill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marriages and children">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Radziwill has been married three times. Her first marriage, in April 1953, was to <a href="/wiki/Kiki_Preston#Illegitimate_child_allegations" title="Kiki Preston">Michael Temple Canfield</a>, a publishing executive who had been adopted as an infant by the American publisher <a href="/wiki/Cass_Canfield" title="Cass Canfield">Cass Canfield</a>. Canfield's biological mother was the American socialite <a href="/wiki/Kiki_Preston" title="Kiki Preston">Kiki Preston</a>. It was rumored that his biological father was <a href="/wiki/Prince_George,_Duke_of_Kent" title="Prince George, Duke of Kent">Prince George, Duke of Kent</a>, a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Royal_Family" title="British Royal Family">British Royal Family</a>; if so, then Canfield would be a first cousin of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">the present Queen</a>. Caroline and Canfield divorced in 1959, and the marriage was annulled by the Roman Catholic Church in November 1962.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-annul_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-annul-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her second marriage, on March 19, 1959, was to the Polish former prince <a href="/wiki/Stanis%C5%82aw_Albrecht_Radziwi%C5%82%C5%82" title="Stanisław Albrecht Radziwiłł">Stanisław Albrecht Radziwiłł</a>, who divorced his second wife, the former Grace Maria Kolin,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and received a Roman Catholic annulment of his first marriage to marry the former Mrs. Canfield (his second marriage had never been acknowledged by the Roman Catholic Church, so no annulment was necessary).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-annul_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-annul-4">[4]</a></sup> Their marriage ended in divorce in 1974.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> They had two children, Anthony and Christina "Tina".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 23, 1988, Radziwill became the second wife of American film director and choreographer <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Ross" title="Herbert Ross">Herbert Ross</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ross_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ross-8">[8]</a></sup> They divorced in 2001, shortly before his death. She reverted her surname to that of her second husband.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_and_fame">Career and fame</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Radziwill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career and fame">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the 1960s, Radziwill attempted to forge a career as an actress. Her acting attempt was unsuccessful if highly publicized. She received dismal reviews in the 1967 production of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Philadelphia_Story_(play)" title="The Philadelphia Story (play)">The Philadelphia Story</a></i>, starring as spoiled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Main_Line" title="Pennsylvania Main Line">Main Line</a> heiress Tracy Lord. The play was staged at the Ivanhoe Theatre in Chicago, and Radziwill's performance was widely panned. A year later, she appeared in a television adaptation of the Hollywood film <i><a href="/wiki/Laura_(1944_film)" title="Laura (1944 film)">Laura</a></i>, which was also badly received.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Radziwill discontinued her acting work.</p>
<p>She visited India and Pakistan along with her elder sister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jacqueline_Kennedy" title="Jacqueline Kennedy">Jacqueline Kennedy</a> (then <a href="/wiki/First_Lady_of_the_United_States" title="First Lady of the United States">First Lady of the United States</a>) in March 1962.</p>
<p>A London townhouse and a manor Turville Grange in <a href="/wiki/Turville" title="Turville">Turville</a> that she shared with her second husband, both of which had been decorated by Italian stage designer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renzo_Mongiardino" title="Renzo Mongiardino">Renzo Mongiardino</a>, were greatly admired and frequently photographed by <a href="/wiki/Cecil_Beaton" title="Cecil Beaton">Cecil Beaton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horst_P._Horst" title="Horst P. Horst">Horst P. Horst</a>. She herself worked briefly as an interior decorator in a style much influenced by her association with Mongiardino. Her clientele were the wealthy; she once decorated a house "for people who would not be there more than three days a year".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_York_Magazine_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_York_Magazine-10">[10]</a></sup> She frequented celebrity company, including travelling with the Rolling Stones during <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones_American_Tour_1972" title="The Rolling Stones American Tour 1972">The Rolling Stones American Tour 1972</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> during which she was accompanied by <a href="/wiki/Truman_Capote" title="Truman Capote">Truman Capote</a>.</p>
<p>For some years, Radziwill was a public relations executive for <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Armani" title="Giorgio Armani">Giorgio Armani</a>, the Italian fashion designer.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>She received the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%A9gion_d%27honneur" title="Légion d'honneur">Légion d'honneur</a> from the French government in 2008.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Her Paris and Manhattan apartments were featured in the April 2009 issue of <i>Elle Décor</i> magazine. She was listed as one of the fifty best-dressed over 50s by the <i>Guardian</i> in March 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was interviewed by producer <a href="/wiki/Sofia_Coppola" title="Sofia Coppola">Sofia Coppola</a> for a June 2013 blog article about Coppola's film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bling_Ring" title="The Bling Ring">The Bling Ring</a></i> and about the loss of privacy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References_within_popular_culture">References within popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Radziwill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: References within popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1973, Radziwill hired documentary filmmakers <a href="/wiki/Albert_and_David_Maysles" title="Albert and David Maysles">Albert and David Maysles</a> to work on a film about the Bouvier family. At the outset, the brothers filmed two eccentric and reclusive members of the extended family: <a href="/wiki/Edith_Ewing_Bouvier_Beale" title="Edith Ewing Bouvier Beale">Edith Ewing Bouvier Beale</a> ("Big Edie") and her daughter <a href="/wiki/Edith_Bouvier_Beale" title="Edith Bouvier Beale">Edith Bouvier Beale</a> ("Little Edie"), who were Radziwill's aunt and cousin respectively. The Beales lived in a rambling, decaying home in <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Easthampton,_New_York" title="Easthampton, New York">Easthampton, New York</a>, and were supported by other members of the family. Radziwill's original film project was not completed, and Radziwill kept the footage that had been shot of the Beales. But the Maysles brothers were fascinated by the strange life the two women led, and after raising funds for film and equipment on their own they returned and filmed 70 more hours of footage with Big Edie and Little Edie. The resulting film was released in 1976, and titled <i><a href="/wiki/Grey_Gardens" title="Grey Gardens">Grey Gardens</a></i> after the name of the Beales' <a href="/wiki/Grey_Gardens_(estate)" title="Grey Gardens (estate)">home</a>. <i>Grey Gardens</i> is widely considered a masterpiece of the documentary genre. It was later turned into a 2006 <a href="/wiki/Grey_Gardens_(musical)" title="Grey Gardens (musical)">musical</a> of the same name in which the characters of Lee and Jackie Bouvier appear as visiting children in retrospect. <a href="/wiki/Grey_Gardens_(2009_film)" title="Grey Gardens (2009 film)">An HBO television movie</a> based upon the Grey Gardens story appeared in 2009 and featured both Radziwill and her sister Jackie as children and as adults who later assisted their aunt and cousin to refurbish their dilapidated, condemned home.</p>
