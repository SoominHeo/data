<p><b>Lee Beachill</b> (born 28 November 1977 in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, United Kingdom) is a former World No. 1 <a href="/wiki/Squash_(sport)" title="Squash (sport)">squash</a> player from England.</p>
<p>Beachill reached the World No. 1 ranking in October 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He also finished runner-up at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Open_(squash)" title="World Open (squash)">World Open</a> that year.</p>
<p>Beachill was part of the English team which won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Team_Squash_Championships" title="World Team Squash Championships">World Team Squash Championships</a> in 2005. He has also won Gold Medals for England in the men's doubles at the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Games" title="Commonwealth Games">Commonwealth Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/2002_Commonwealth_Games" title="2002 Commonwealth Games">2002</a> and <a href="/wiki/2006_Commonwealth_Games" title="2006 Commonwealth Games">2006</a>, partnering <a href="/wiki/Peter_Nicol" title="Peter Nicol">Peter Nicol</a> on both occasions.</p>
<p>Beachill has won the <a href="/wiki/British_National_Squash_Championships" title="British National Squash Championships">British National Squash Championships</a> three times – in 2001, 2002 and 2005.</p>
<p>Beachill announced his retirement from the game in February 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> after undergoing <a href="/wiki/Hernia" title="Hernia">hernia</a> surgery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a junior player, Beachill helped England win the World Junior Team Championship in 1997, and was the British champion at under-12, under-14, under-17 and under-19 levels.</p>
<p>Lee first played the game at the Skelmanthorpe Squash Club in Yorkshire under the guidance of coach Chris Beck.</p>
<p>He attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horbury_School" title="Horbury School">Horbury School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Horbury" title="Horbury">Horbury</a>.</p>
