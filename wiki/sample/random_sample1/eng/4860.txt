<p><b>Lee Oskar</b> (born 24 March 1948, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copenhagen,_Denmark" title="Copenhagen, Denmark">Copenhagen, Denmark</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seattle_Post-Intelligencer_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Seattle_Post-Intelligencer-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danish_people" title="Danish people">Danish</a> <a href="/wiki/Harmonica" title="Harmonica">harmonica</a> player,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Houston_Press_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Houston_Press-2">[2]</a></sup> notable for his contributions to the sound of the rock-funk fusion group <a href="/wiki/War_(band)" title="War (band)">War</a>, which he formed with <a href="/wiki/Eric_Burdon" title="Eric Burdon">Eric Burdon</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ashland_Daily_Tidings_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ashland_Daily_Tidings-3">[3]</a></sup> his <a href="/wiki/Solo_(music)#Jazz" title="Solo (music)">solo work</a>, and as a <a href="/wiki/Harmonica" title="Harmonica">harmonica</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manufacturer" title="Manufacturer">manufacturer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Music_in_Southern_California_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Music_in_Southern_California-4">[4]</a></sup> He currently plays with 3 other original WAR band members, Harold Brown, Howard Scott and B.B. Dickerson, under the name LowRider Band.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Oskar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>, Denmark in 1948, Oskar was six years old when a family friend gave him his first harmonica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC_Metro_Music_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBC_Metro_Music-5">[5]</a></sup> "I came from an area where every kid on the block had a harmonica", he remembers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deseret_News_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Deseret_News-6">[6]</a></sup> He grew up listening to Danish radio, enjoying all types of music and cites <a href="/wiki/Ray_Charles" title="Ray Charles">Ray Charles</a> as the biggest influence from that period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Los_Angeles_Blues_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Los_Angeles_Blues-7">[7]</a></sup> When he was 17, Oskar decided that the United States was where a harmonica player should make his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deseret_News_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Deseret_News-6">[6]</a></sup> So he moved to New York at the age of 18 with little more than a <a href="/wiki/Harmonica" title="Harmonica">harmonica</a> in his pocket.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC_Metro_Music_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBC_Metro_Music-5">[5]</a></sup> With no money, Oskar played harmonica in the streets of New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deseret_News_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Deseret_News-6">[6]</a></sup> Eventually arriving in Los Angeles, via Toronto and San Francisco, Oskar soon met and joined forces with <a href="/wiki/Eric_Burdon" title="Eric Burdon">Eric Burdon</a> who had recently disbanded <a href="/wiki/The_Animals" title="The Animals">The Animals</a> and was searching for new collaborators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Sacramento_Bee_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Sacramento_Bee-8">[8]</a></sup> Together, the harmonica-playing Dane (born Lee Oskar Levitin<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Malay_Mail_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Malay_Mail-9">[9]</a></sup>) and the British blues-rock singer made the rounds of the L.A. clubs, eventually hooking up with the soon-to-be members of <a href="/wiki/War_(band)" title="War (band)">War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_All_Music_Guide_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTV_All_Music_Guide-10">[10]</a></sup> Burdon agreed to the novel idea of pairing up Oskar's harmonica with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Miller_(musician)" title="Charles Miller (musician)">Charles Miller</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Saxophone" title="Saxophone">saxophone</a> to form a <a href="/wiki/Horn_section" title="Horn section">horn section</a>. This team-up set War apart from the start, giving Oskar room to display the full spectrum of his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Improvisational" title="Improvisational">improvisational</a> prowess. Oskar's harmonica magic was always a vital element in War's music and performances. Oskar continued with War for 24 years non-stop. At the end of 1992, during the time of dispute over the WAR trademark, Oskar took a few years to continue his <a href="/wiki/Solo_(music)#Jazz" title="Solo (music)">solo</a> career and to focus on his Lee Oskar Harmonica manufacturing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_All_Music_Guide_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-MTV_All_Music_Guide-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="War">War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Oskar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee Oskar who has been described as "a virtuoso,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chicago_Tribune_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chicago_Tribune-11">[11]</a></sup> "the harmonica whiz"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_New_York_Times_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_New_York_Times-12">[12]</a></sup> "a war hero,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-On_Disc_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-On_Disc-13">[13]</a></sup> "legendary,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBC_Metro_Music_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-CBC_Metro_Music-5">[5]</a></sup> a master of "musical wizardry,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seattle_Post-Intelligencer_Camp_Article_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Seattle_Post-Intelligencer_Camp_Article-14">[14]</a></sup> and considered, "...among the best rock-blues-soul harmonica players."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Letters_to_the_Editor_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Letters_to_the_Editor-15">[15]</a></sup> His role as a founding member and former lead harmonica player of the pioneer funk-jazz group <a href="/wiki/War_(band)" title="War (band)">War</a> won him international renown for over two and a half decades (1969–1993). Oskar's signature solos helped to define the War sound from the band's beginning in 1969, adding dashes of color to its R&amp;B, jazz, rock, and Latin influences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seattle_Post-Intelligencer_Camp_Article_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Seattle_Post-Intelligencer_Camp_Article-14">[14]</a></sup> Oskar's position with War was a prominent one from its early days with singer <a href="/wiki/Eric_Burdon" title="Eric Burdon">Eric Burdon</a> onward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Independent_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Independent-16">[16]</a></sup> "My playing has become more aggressive over the years", he says. "In the beginning, my role was playing horn lines. Today, it's evolved to the point where I'm playing a lead instrument. If I'm not doing a solo, I'm playing counterlines — I try to paint within certain spaces in the music to help create the overall picture."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_career">Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Oskar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The eclectic, multicultural nature of War's music is also evident in Oskar's solo projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chicago_Sun-Times_.E2.80.93_Oskar_keeps_blowin.E2.80.99_his_harp_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chicago_Sun-Times_.E2.80.93_Oskar_keeps_blowin.E2.80.99_his_harp-17">[17]</a></sup> Three well regarded albums released between 1976 and 1981 (and recently rereleased on CD) brought critical and popular acclaim<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Sunday_Independent_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Sunday_Independent-18">[18]</a></sup> including being voted No. 1 Instrumental Artist of the Year for 1976 in <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cashbox_(magazine)" title="Cashbox (magazine)">Cashbox</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Record_World" title="Record World">Record World</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loose_Blues_News_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Loose_Blues_News-19">[19]</a></sup> The albums, like Oskar's live performances, show the diverse influences of his musicality. A composer, his compositions have been featured on movie sound tracks and television commercials. He has been the recipient of many Gold and Platinum recordings and honored with special <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ASCAP" title="ASCAP">ASCAP</a> Writing Awards. His music has more recently influenced the song "<a href="/wiki/Timber_(Pitbull_song)" title="Timber (Pitbull song)">Timber</a>", which was performed by rapper <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kesha" title="Kesha">Kesha</a>; which was inspired by Oskar's melodic harmonica playing within his song "San Francisco Bay".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lee_Oskar_harmonica">Lee Oskar harmonica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Oskar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lee Oskar harmonica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1983 Oskar formed a company to manufacture high-quality harmonicas. His company, Lee Oskar Harmonica, sells harmonicas suited to many different styles of music, including the most common <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">blues</a>, <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> and <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country</a> but Oskar's altered tunings also allows players to explore other genres such as Hip Hop, Reggae, Ska, Latin, Gypsy, Yiddish, Eastern European, Asian, and many other types of music. The harmonicas themselves are manufactured by Tombo of Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seattle_Post-Intelligencer_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Seattle_Post-Intelligencer-1">[1]</a></sup> Oskar's harmonica company celebrated its 25th anniversary in 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/NAMM_Show" title="NAMM Show">NAMM Show</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and has now surpassed his 30th anniversary.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Oskar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several modern musicians, such as Mike Tetrault, cited Oskar as an important influence.
