<p><b>Lee Grinner Pace</b> (born March 25, 1979) is an American actor. Pace has been featured in film, stage and television. He currently stars as protagonist Joe MacMillan in <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Halt_and_Catch_Fire_(TV_series)" title="Halt and Catch Fire (TV series)">Halt and Catch Fire</a></i>. He also played Roy Walker/the Masked Bandit in the 2006 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fall_(2006_film)" title="The Fall (2006 film)">The Fall</a></i>. He has appeared in film series, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_Breaking_Dawn_%E2%80%93_Part_2" title="The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 2">The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 2</a></i> as Garrett and <a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit_(film_series)" title="The Hobbit (film series)"><i>The Hobbit</i> trilogy</a> as <a href="/wiki/Thranduil" title="Thranduil">Thranduil</a>. He played villain <a href="/wiki/Ronan_the_Accuser" title="Ronan the Accuser">Ronan the Accuser</a> in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Galaxy_(film)" title="Guardians of the Galaxy (film)">Guardians of the Galaxy</a></i>, and starred as <a href="/wiki/Ned_(Pushing_Daisies)" title="Ned (Pushing Daisies)">Ned</a> in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Pushing_Daisies" title="Pushing Daisies">Pushing Daisies</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-1">[1]</a></sup> for which he was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a> and <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Lead_Actor_in_a_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series</a> in 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Pace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pace was born in <a href="/wiki/Chickasha,_Oklahoma" title="Chickasha, Oklahoma">Chickasha, Oklahoma</a>, the son of Charlotte (née Kloeckler), a schoolteacher, and James Roy Pace, an engineer. He has two siblings, a sister named Sally and a younger brother named Willam Pace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As a child, Pace spent several years in <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, where his father worked in the oil business; the family later moved to <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, Texas. Pace attended <a href="/wiki/Klein_High_School" title="Klein High School">Klein High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spring,_Texas" title="Spring, Texas">Spring, Texas</a>, a suburb of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">Houston</a>, with future fellow actor <a href="/wiki/Matt_Bomer" title="Matt Bomer">Matt Bomer</a>. Pace temporarily quit high school to act at Houston's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Alley_Theatre" title="The Alley Theatre">Alley Theatre</a> before returning to graduate. At the Alley, he appeared in productions of <i>The Spider's Web</i> and <i>The Greeks</i>. In 1997, Pace was accepted by the <a href="/wiki/Juilliard_School" title="Juilliard School">Juilliard School</a>'s Drama Division as a member of <i>Group 30</i> (1997–2001), which also included actors <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Mackie" title="Anthony Mackie">Anthony Mackie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tracie_Thoms" title="Tracie Thoms">Tracie Thoms</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> While there, he acted in several plays, including <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romeo_Montague" title="Romeo Montague">Romeo</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Richard_III_(play)" title="Richard III (play)">Richard III</a></i> in the title role, and <i><a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar_(play)" title="Julius Caesar (play)">Julius Caesar</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Cassius_Longinus" title="Gaius Cassius Longinus">Cassius</a>. He graduated from Juilliard with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Fine_Arts" title="Bachelor of Fine Arts">Bachelor of Fine Arts</a> degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Pace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theater">Theater</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Pace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Theater">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After graduation, Pace starred in several <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">off-Broadway</a> plays, including <i>The Credeaux Canvas</i> and <i>The Fourth Sister</i>. He also starred in a production of <a href="/wiki/Craig_Lucas" title="Craig Lucas">Craig Lucas</a>'s <i>Small Tragedy</i>, for which he was nominated for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucille_Lortel_Award" title="Lucille Lortel Award">Lucille Lortel Award</a> as Outstanding Actor. In 2006, Pace starred in the two-character play <i><a href="/wiki/Guardians_(play)" title="Guardians (play)">Guardians</a></i> by Peter Morris, which earned Pace his second nomination for a Lortel Award as Outstanding Actor.</p>
<p>April 19, 2011 marked Pace's Broadway debut, when <i><a href="/wiki/The_Normal_Heart" title="The Normal Heart">The Normal Heart</a></i> began its previews; it opened April 27 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Theatre" title="Golden Theatre">Golden Theatre</a>. The show ran for a total of 96 performances, with its closing night on July 10, 2011. Pace played the character Bruce Niles in the show.</p>
<p>Pace has been cast as composer <a href="/wiki/Vincenzo_Bellini" title="Vincenzo Bellini">Vincenzo Bellini</a> in <i>Golden Age</i>, which began previews November 15, 2012. The play, written by <a href="/wiki/Terrence_McNally" title="Terrence McNally">Terrence McNally</a>, began its official run at the <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Theatre_Club" title="Manhattan Theatre Club">Manhattan Theatre Club</a> December 4, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The previews were originally scheduled for November 13, 2012 but two shows were cancelled as a result of Hurricane Sandy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Pace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Pace first gained recognition for his role in the 2003 film <i><a href="/wiki/Soldier%27s_Girl" title="Soldier's Girl">Soldier’s Girl</a></i>, based on real events, in which he played the central role of <a href="/wiki/Calpernia_Addams" title="Calpernia Addams">Calpernia Addams</a>. Of the role, Pace has said</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Not even my excellent training at Juilliard prepared me for my first movie role, where I played a transsexual who falls in love with a military guy in <i>Soldier’s Girl</i>. Here I was, this 6′3″, 190 pound, lanky kid from <a href="/wiki/Chickasha,_Oklahoma" title="Chickasha, Oklahoma">Chickasha, Oklahoma</a>, not knowing how to begin being a woman. So I saw documentaries about transsexuals, I lost twenty-five pounds, and I put on prosthetic boobs and hips to become that character. There were times I’d look in the mirror and wonder, ‘What am I doing to my life here? My dad is going to <i>kill</i> me!’ But the reason I went into acting was to be able to play parts as complicated and important as this one. In playing a transsexual, I got the chance to help change people’s perspective about other people, and that is a powerful thing. I’m playing a swashbuckling bandit in my next film, but I’ll always be proud of <i>Soldier’s Girl</i> ”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interviewmag_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-interviewmag-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Pace won a <a href="/wiki/Gotham_Awards" title="Gotham Awards">Gotham Award</a> for Breakthrough Actor and was nominated for several other awards, including a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a>, for his work in this film.</p>
<p>He starred in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Pettigrew_Lives_for_a_Day" title="Miss Pettigrew Lives for a Day">Miss Pettigrew Lives for a Day</a></i> and in <a href="/wiki/Tarsem_Singh" title="Tarsem Singh">Tarsem Singh</a>’s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fall_(2006_film)" title="The Fall (2006 film)">The Fall</a></i>, both released in 2008. <i>The Fall</i> was very well received amongst critics and viewers alike and where Pace starred in one of his most artistic roles. His next film was the movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Possession_(2008_film)" title="Possession (2008 film)">Possession</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Michelle_Gellar" title="Sarah Michelle Gellar">Sarah Michelle Gellar</a>.</p>
<p>Pace also played supporting roles in <i><a href="/wiki/The_White_Countess" title="The White Countess">The White Countess</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Infamous_(film)" title="Infamous (film)">Infamous</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/When_in_Rome_(2010_film)" title="When in Rome (2010 film)">When in Rome</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Shepherd_(film)" title="The Good Shepherd (film)">The Good Shepherd</a></i>. In 2010, Pace appeared as Phil Winslow in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Marmaduke" title="Marmaduke">Marmaduke</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2012, Pace starred as Garrett, the nomadic <a href="/wiki/Vampire" title="Vampire">vampire</a>, in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_Breaking_Dawn_%E2%80%93_Part_2" title="The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 2">The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 2</a></i>. Pace was admittedly aware of the reputation linked to the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga_(film_series)" title="The Twilight Saga (film series)">Twilight</a></i> franchise, and revealed that he "went in to this a little like ‘You know what you’re getting into, just do what you can.’<small> </small>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> However, he enjoyed the experience and only had praise for the director <a href="/wiki/Bill_Condon" title="Bill Condon">Bill Condon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheaterMania_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheaterMania-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stephenie_Meyer" title="Stephenie Meyer">Stephenie Meyer</a>, the author of the <i><a href="/wiki/Twilight_(novel_series)" title="Twilight (novel series)">Twilight</a></i> saga, was very satisfied with Pace’s performance as Garrett, since he "stood out as someone who really was just so much fun and really looked the part."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The movie was met with a mixed reception by critics,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> however, some critics, such as Betsy Sharkey of the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a>, whose only issue was that he was introduced too late in the series: "Why, oh, why didn’t they introduce him sooner?."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Meanwhile, Sara Stewart of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a> simply described him as a “standout”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Pace appreciates the support of the fans, and said that he felt their support while he was appearing in <i>Golden Age</i> on <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a>: "a lot of them come to the play, which is nice, really nice. I just appreciate that <i>Twilight</i> really does bring a Community of people together."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 30, 2011, it was announced that Pace had been cast as the king of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mirkwood_Elves" title="Mirkwood Elves">Mirkwood Elves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thranduil" title="Thranduil">Thranduil</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">Peter Jackson</a>'s film adaptation of <a href="/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien" title="J. R. R. Tolkien">J. R. R. Tolkien</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit" title="The Hobbit">The Hobbit</a></i>. The announcement was made by Peter Jackson himself, who revealed on his <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> page that Pace had been his favorite for the part, ever since he saw his performance in <i>The Fall</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The character had previously been mentioned in Jackson's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring">The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring</a></i>, and had previously been portrayed in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit_(1977_film)" title="The Hobbit (1977 film)">The Hobbit</a></i>, voiced by the Oscar–nominated <a href="/wiki/Otto_Preminger" title="Otto Preminger">Otto Preminger</a>, and in the 1968 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_radio" title="BBC radio">BBC radio</a> series, voiced by the British actor <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Fenton" title="Leonard Fenton">Leonard Fenton</a>. Pace made three trips to <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, and called it a “fantastic experience”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheaterMania_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-TheaterMania-10">[10]</a></sup> The character appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Prologue" title="Prologue">prologue</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_An_Unexpected_Journey" title="The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey">The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey</a></i> released in December 2012, and had a larger role in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_The_Desolation_of_Smaug" title="The Hobbit: The Desolation of Smaug">The Hobbit: The Desolation of Smaug</a></i> released in December 2013 and in the last film of the series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_The_Battle_of_the_Five_Armies" title="The Hobbit: The Battle of the Five Armies">The Hobbit: The Battle of the Five Armies</a></i>, released on December 17, 2014.</p>
<p>On July 28, 2011, it was announced that Pace had been cast in <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincoln_(2012_film)" title="Lincoln (2012 film)">Lincoln</a></i>, as the one-time <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> mayor <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Wood" title="Fernando Wood">Fernando Wood</a>, an early Confederate supporter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The events in the movie takes place in 1865, when Fernando Wood served in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">House of Representatives</a>. However, at the time, Wood was 53 years old, which is 20 years senior to Pace’s age when he portrayed him. In a Q&amp;A, Spielberg revealed that he decided to offer Pace the part, after he saw his performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Ceremony_(film)" title="Ceremony (film)">Ceremony</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Of the experience of the film, Pace said that “it was a real pinnacle of what I’ve done as an actor.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheaterMania_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-TheaterMania-10">[10]</a></sup> The movie was nominated for 12 Academy Awards,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> including <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Picture" title="Academy Award for Best Picture">Best Picture</a>, and was nominated for Best Ensemble at the <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award" title="Screen Actors Guild Award">Screen Actors Guild Award</a>. However, Pace was not included among the <i>Lincoln</i> ensemble’s nomination, and several bloggers were annoyed by this, including Katey Rich of Cinemablend.com and Nathaniel Rogers of The Film Experience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pace played the villain, <a href="/wiki/Ronan_the_Accuser" title="Ronan the Accuser">Ronan the Accuser</a>, in the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Studios" title="Marvel Studios">Marvel Studios</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Guardians_of_the_Galaxy_(film)" title="Guardians of the Galaxy (film)">Guardians of the Galaxy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GotG_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-GotG-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Pace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pace played Aaron Tyler in the acclaimed but short-lived 2004 television series <i><a href="/wiki/Wonderfalls" title="Wonderfalls">Wonderfalls</a></i>, which was co-created by <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Fuller" title="Bryan Fuller">Bryan Fuller</a>. Later, Fuller cast Pace in the lead role of <a href="/wiki/Ned_(Pushing_Daisies)" title="Ned (Pushing Daisies)">Ned</a> in the series <i><a href="/wiki/Pushing_Daisies" title="Pushing Daisies">Pushing Daisies</a></i> which debuted on ABC in October 2007 and returned for its second and final season on October 1, 2008. For his role as Ned, Pace was nominated for several awards including: Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series, Golden Globe Award for Best Actor in a Television Series, Musical or Comedy, also for a Satellite Award for Best Actor in a Television Series, Musical or Comedy, and a Saturn Award for Best Actor on Television.</p>
<p>Pace has also portrayed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Child_molestation" title="Child molestation">child molester</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> episode "Guilt".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In 2010, Pace was cast in the unsold <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> pilot <i>The Miraculous Year</i> directed by <a href="/wiki/Kathryn_Bigelow" title="Kathryn Bigelow">Kathryn Bigelow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2013, Pace was cast as Joe MacMillan in the <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> pilot <i><a href="/wiki/Halt_and_Catch_Fire_(TV_series)" title="Halt and Catch Fire (TV series)">Halt and Catch Fire</a></i>, which was directed by <a href="/wiki/Juan_J._Campanella" title="Juan J. Campanella">Juan J. Campanella</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
