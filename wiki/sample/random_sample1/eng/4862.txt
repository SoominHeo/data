<p><b>Lee Haney</b> (born November 11, 1959) is an American former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_BodyBuilders" title="International Federation of BodyBuilders">IFBB</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Bodybuilding" title="Bodybuilding">bodybuilder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KrasniewiczBlitz2006_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-KrasniewiczBlitz2006-1">[1]</a></sup> Haney shares the all-time record for most <a href="/wiki/Mr._Olympia" title="Mr. Olympia">Mr. Olympia</a> titles at eight with <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Coleman" title="Ronnie Coleman">Ronnie Coleman</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Haney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Haney grew up in <a href="/wiki/Spartanburg,_South_Carolina" title="Spartanburg, South Carolina">Spartanburg, South Carolina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Spartanburg_Methodist_College" title="Spartanburg Methodist College">Spartanburg Methodist College</a>, where he earned a degree in <a href="/wiki/Child_and_Youth_Care" title="Child and Youth Care">youth counseling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He and his wife, Shirley, reside in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a>. They have two children.</p>
<p>President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> appointed Haney chairman of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President%27s_Council_on_Physical_Fitness_and_Sports" title="President's Council on Physical Fitness and Sports">President's Council on Physical Fitness and Sports</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> A devout <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Born_again_Christianity" title="Born again Christianity">born again</a> <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a>, Haney has his own program on the <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Broadcasting_Network" title="Trinity Broadcasting Network">Trinity Broadcasting Network</a> called "TotaLee Fit with Lee Haney," where he is usually joined by famous Christian sportsmen, and on occasion his wife. On the show he teaches the importance of both physical and spiritual growth. One of his most famous quotes is that we should "train to stimulate, not annihilate."</p>
