<p><b>Lough Ree</b> (<a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <i>Loch Rí</i> or <i>Loch Ríbh</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lake</a> in the midlands of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>, the second of the three major lakes on the <a href="/wiki/River_Shannon" title="River Shannon">River Shannon</a>. Lough Ree is the second largest lake on the Shannon after <a href="/wiki/Lough_Derg_(Shannon)" title="Lough Derg (Shannon)">Lough Derg</a>. The other two major lakes are <a href="/wiki/Lough_Allen" title="Lough Allen">Lough Allen</a> to the north, and Lough Derg to the south, there are also several minor lakes along the length of the river. The lake serves as a border between the counties of <a href="/wiki/County_Longford" title="County Longford">Longford</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Westmeath" title="County Westmeath">Westmeath</a> (both in the province of <a href="/wiki/Leinster" title="Leinster">Leinster</a>) on the eastern side and <a href="/wiki/County_Roscommon" title="County Roscommon">County Roscommon</a> in the province of <a href="/wiki/Connacht" title="Connacht">Connacht</a> on the western side. The lake is popular for fishing and boating. The lake supports a small commercial eel fishery and is locally famous for its <i>eels on wheels</i> truck. The town of <a href="/wiki/Athlone" title="Athlone">Athlone</a> is situated at the southern end of the lake, and has a harbour for boats going out on the lake. The small town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lanesborough-Ballyleague" title="Lanesborough-Ballyleague">Lanesboro</a> is at the northern end of the lake.</p>
<p>The island of <a href="/wiki/Inchcleraun" title="Inchcleraun">Inchcleraun</a> (<i>Inis Cloithreann</i>) in the northern part of the lake is the site of a monastery founded in the early Christian era and contains the remains of several ancient churches. In Irish legends, it was on this island that <a href="/wiki/Medb" title="Medb">Queen Maeve</a> was killed. The Viking <a href="/wiki/Turgesius" title="Turgesius">Turgesius</a> controlled a <a href="/wiki/Ringfort" title="Ringfort">ringfort</a> on the shores until his death by drowning in <a href="/wiki/Lough_Owel" title="Lough Owel">Lough Owel</a>.</p>
<p>Families lived on some of the islands in Lough Ree including Inchcleraun, (Walsh &amp; Farrell) The Black Islands (Hanly &amp; O'Hara) Inchmore (Tiernan, Quigley, Nolan &amp; Keefe), Hare Island (Duffy), Inchbofin (Connell), Inch Turk (Ganly, Slevin &amp; Walsh) until the 1950s, when they were rehoused ashore. Like several other Irish loughs, Lough Ree has been the scene of claimed sightings of a <a href="/wiki/Lake_monster" title="Lake monster">lake monster</a> over the years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Geographical_centre_of_Ireland" title="Geographical centre of Ireland">Geographical centre of Ireland</a> is in the townland of Carnagh East, Co. Roscommon on the western shore of Lough Ree, opposite the Cribby Islands<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Also Hodson pillar which is located on an island on the lake is said to be the most central point in Ireland.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lough_Ree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lough Ree has been designated a <a href="/wiki/Special_Area_of_Conservation" title="Special Area of Conservation">Special Area of Conservation</a>. The main habitat, by area, is the lake itself, but there are other habitats including bog.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ie_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ie-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lough Ree site has been designated a <a href="/wiki/Special_Protection_Area" title="Special Protection Area">Special Protection Area</a> on the basis of its migratory waterfowl, with nationally important numbers of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tachybaptus_ruficollis" title="Tachybaptus ruficollis">Tachybaptus ruficollis</a></i>. It is also an <a href="/wiki/Important_Bird_Area" title="Important Bird Area">Important Bird Area</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BI_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BI-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lough_Ree&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jim O'Connor was the first person to solo swim the length of Lough Ree in 2012. The 33.91 kilometres swim lasted 13 hours and 34 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Jim completed this swim to raise funds for South Westmeath Hospice.</p>
