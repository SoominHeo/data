<p>The <b>Riga–Lugaži Railway</b> is a 166 kilometres (103 mi) long,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ldz_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ldz-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/5_ft_and_1520_mm_gauge_railways" title="5 ft and 1520 mm gauge railways"><span class="nowrap">1,524 mm</span></a> (<span class="nowrap">5 ft</span>) gauge <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway" title="Railway">railway</a> in <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>. It was built in the late 19th century (commencing in 1886) to connect the cities of <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a> via <a href="/wiki/Valga,_Estonia" title="Valga, Estonia">Valga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pskov" title="Pskov">Pskov</a>. The railway was opened in 1889.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railmus_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-railmus-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The final station in Latvia is <a href="/wiki/Luga%C5%BEi" title="Lugaži">Lugaži</a> (near <a href="/wiki/Valka" title="Valka">Valka</a>). The railway was last used for the Riga–Saint Petersburg service in 1998, after which St Petersburg trains operated via <a href="/wiki/R%C4%93zekne" title="Rēzekne">Rēzekne</a>. For several years there were no cross-border services, and trains terminated at Lugaži, but since 2008 a regular passenger service again operates from Riga to <a href="/wiki/Valga,_Estonia" title="Valga, Estonia">Valga</a> in <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, with an onward connection to <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a>.</p>
