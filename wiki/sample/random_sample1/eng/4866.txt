<p><b>Rigatoni</b> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[riÉ¡aËtoËni]</a></span> are a form of tube-shaped <a href="/wiki/Pasta" title="Pasta">pasta</a> of varying lengths and diameters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thenibble1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-thenibble1-1">[1]</a></sup> They are larger than <a href="/wiki/Penne" title="Penne">penne</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ziti" title="Ziti">ziti</a>, and sometimes slightly curved, though nowhere near as curved as elbow <a href="/wiki/Macaroni" title="Macaroni">macaroni</a>. Rigatoni characteristically have ridges down their length, sometimes spiraling around the tube. And unlike penne, rigatoni's ends are cut square (<a href="/wiki/Perpendicular" title="Perpendicular">perpendicular</a>) to the tube walls instead of <a href="/wiki/Diagonal" title="Diagonal">diagonally</a>.</p>
<p>The word <i>rigatoni</i> comes from the <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> word <i>rigato</i> (<i>rigatone</i> being the augmentative and <i>rigatoni</i> the plural form), which means "<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stripe" title="wikt:stripe">ridged</a>" or "lined", and is associated with the cuisine of southern and central <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thenibble1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-thenibble1-1">[1]</a></sup> Rigatoncini are a smaller version, close to the size of penne. Their name takes on the diminutive suffix <i>-ino</i> (pluralized <i>-ini</i>) denoting their relative size. Rigatoni is a particular favorite pasta shape in the south of Italy, especially in <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>. Its namesake ridges make better adhesive surfaces for sauces and grated cheese than smooth-sided pasta like ziti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vita2009_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vita2009-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
