<p>The <b>Battle of Liegnitz</b> on 15 August 1760 saw <a href="/wiki/Frederick_the_Great" title="Frederick the Great">Frederick the Great</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussian</a> Army defeat the Austrian army under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ernst_Gideon_Freiherr_von_Laudon" title="Ernst Gideon Freiherr von Laudon">Ernst von Laudon</a>.</p>
<p>The armies collided around the town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liegnitz" title="Liegnitz">Liegnitz</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Legnica" title="Legnica">Legnica</a>, Poland) in Lower Silesia. Laudon's Austrian cavalry attacked the Prussian position in the early morning but were beaten back by <a href="/wiki/Hans_Joachim_von_Zieten" title="Hans Joachim von Zieten">General Zieten</a>'s Hussars. An artillery duel emerged which was eventually won for the Prussians when a grenade hit an Austrian powder wagon. The Austrian infantry then proceeded to attack the Prussian line, but was met with concentrated artillery fire. A Prussian infantry counter-attack led by the Regiment Anhalt-Bernburg on the left forced the Austrians into retreat.</p>
<p>Shortly after dawn the major action was over but Prussian artillery fire continued to harass the Austrians. General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leopold_Josef_Graf_Daun" title="Leopold Josef Graf Daun">Leopold von Daun</a> arrived and, learning of Laudon's defeat, decided not to attack despite his soldiers being fresh.</p>
