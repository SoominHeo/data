<p>The <b>Korean League Cup</b> was a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> competition. The competition was firstly played in 1992 and was abolished after the 2012 season. The predecessor competition, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professional_Football_Championship_(Korea)" title="Professional Football Championship (Korea)">Professional Football Championship</a>, was played in 1986.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Names">Names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Korean_League_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1992 to 2011, the <b>League Cup</b> had been named after its sponsor, giving it the following names:</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Adidas_Cup" title="Adidas Cup">Adidas Cup</a></b> (1992–2002), sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Samsung_Hauzen_Cup" title="Samsung Hauzen Cup">Samsung Hauzen Cup</a></b> (2004–2008), sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Electronics" title="Samsung Electronics">Samsung Electronics</a></li>
<li><b>Peace Cup Korea</b> (2009), sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Peace_Cup" title="Peace Cup">Peace Cup</a> Organizing Committee</li>
<li><b>Posco Cup</b> (2010), sponsored by <a href="/wiki/POSCO" title="POSCO">POSCO</a></li>
<li><b>Rush &amp; Cash Cup</b> (2011), sponsored by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=A%26P_Financial&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="A&amp;P Financial (page does not exist)">A&amp;P Financial</a></li>
</ul>
<p>From 1997 to 2000, there were supplementary <b><a href="/wiki/Korean_League_Cup_(Supplementary_Cup)" title="Korean League Cup (Supplementary Cup)">League Cups</a></b>:</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Korean_League_Cup_(Supplementary_Cup)" title="Korean League Cup (Supplementary Cup)">Prospecs Cup</a></b> (1997), sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Prospecs" title="Prospecs">LS Networks Company</a></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Korean_League_Cup_(Supplementary_Cup)" title="Korean League Cup (Supplementary Cup)">Phillip Morris Korea Cup</a></b> (1998), sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Philip_Morris_International" title="Philip Morris International">Phillip Morris Korea</a></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Korean_League_Cup_(Supplementary_Cup)" title="Korean League Cup (Supplementary Cup)">Daehanhwajae Cup</a></b> (1999–2000), sponsored by the now <a href="/wiki/Lotte_Insurance" title="Lotte Insurance">Lotte Insurance</a></li>
</ul>
