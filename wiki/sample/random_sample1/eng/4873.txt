<p><b>Rignano sull'Arno</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> (municipality) in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Florence" title="Province of Florence">province of Florence</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> region <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a>, located about 20 kilometres (12 mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rignano_sull%27Arno&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Pieve" title="Pieve">Pieve</a></i> of San Lorenzo a Miransù</li>
<li><i>Pieve</i> di San Leonino, with a terracotta hexagonal attributed to <a href="/wiki/Santi_Buglioni" title="Santi Buglioni">Santi Buglioni</a></li>
<li>Monastery of Santa Maria a Rosano, allegedly founded in 780 but known from the 11th century. The church has kept some structures from the 12th century.</li>
<li>Church of <i>San Pietro a Perticaia</i>. It houses a wooden crucifix of the Florentine school (late 15th-early 16th century)</li>
<li>Church of <i>San Michele</i> a Volognano, included in a castle which is now a neo-Gothic villa. The church houses several artworks, included a late 14th century <i>Madonna with Child</i> by <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_di_Bicci" title="Lorenzo di Bicci">Lorenzo di Bicci</a>, an altarpiece by <a href="/wiki/Mariotto_Albertinelli" title="Mariotto Albertinelli">Mariotto Albertinelli</a> (1514) and the <i>Madonna of the Cintola</i> (c. 1520), attributed to the workshop of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_del_Sarto" title="Andrea del Sarto">Andrea del Sarto</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Domenico_Puligo" title="Domenico Puligo">Domenico Puligo</a> or to <a href="/wiki/Rosso_Fiorentino" title="Rosso Fiorentino">Rosso Fiorentino</a>.</li>
</ul>
