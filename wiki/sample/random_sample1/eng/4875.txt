<p><b>Linosa</b> (<a href="/wiki/Sicilian_language" title="Sicilian language">Sicilian</a>: <span lang="scn" xml:lang="scn"><i>Linusa</i></span>) is one of the <a href="/wiki/Pelagie_Islands" title="Pelagie Islands">Pelagie Islands</a> in the Sicily Channel of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>.</p>
<p>The island is a part of the <a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lampedusa_e_Linosa" title="Lampedusa e Linosa">Lampedusa e Linosa</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Agrigento" title="Province of Agrigento">province of Agrigento</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>. It has a population of 430.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island is cited first as <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> Aethusa "Αἰθοῦσσα" by <a href="/wiki/Strabo" title="Strabo">Strabo</a> and Algusa "Ἀλγοῦσσα" by <a href="/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder" title="Pliny the Elder">Pliny the Elder</a> in his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naturalis_Historia" title="Naturalis Historia">Naturalis Historia</a>. The name "Lenusa" appeared first during the 16th century in the writing of <a href="/wiki/Tommaso_Fazello" title="Tommaso Fazello">Tommaso Fazello</a> while the modern one date back to 1845 used by the Knight Bernardo Maria Sanvinsente.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island has an area of 5.45 square kilometres (2.10 sq mi) and is of volcanic origin. It is formed by a series of craters of which Monte Vulcano, 195 metres (640 feet) high, is the most important. The closest land to Linosa is the island of Lampedusa, which lies 43 km (27 mi) to the south. Linosa is situated 119 kilometres (74 miles) west of <a href="/wiki/Gozo" title="Gozo">Gozo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, 121 km (75 miles) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Pantelleria" title="Pantelleria">Pantelleria</a>, 163 km (101 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a> and 165 km (103 mi) east of Cape Mahida in <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Punic_Wars" title="Punic Wars">Punic Wars</a> it was used by the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Romans</a> as a base; the 150 water cisterns remaining are from this period. Roman domination was followed by <a href="/wiki/Saracen" title="Saracen">Saracen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Norman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Capetian_House_of_Anjou" title="Capetian House of Anjou">Angevin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon" title="Crown of Aragon">Aragonese</a> control. The island remained deserted until 1843 when <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_the_Two_Sicilies" title="Ferdinand II of the Two Sicilies">Ferdinand II of the Two Sicilies</a> ordered Knight Bernardo Maria Sanvinsente, captain of frigate, to colonize the island. The first thirty colonists (artisans from <a href="/wiki/Ustica" title="Ustica">Ustica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agrigento" title="Agrigento">Agrigento</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pantelleria" title="Pantelleria">Pantelleria</a>) with the addition of a major, a priest and a doctor, landed on 25 April 1845.</p>
<p>During World War II, a small Italian garrison surrendered<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to a British force from <a href="/wiki/HMS_Nubian_(F36)" title="HMS Nubian (F36)">HMS <i>Nubian</i></a> on the morning of 13 June 1943.</p>
<p>Few were interested in the island during the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Kingdom of Italy</a> and only in the 1970s did the island begin to change, with technological innovations and the development of tourism. The first telephone exchange was installed in 1963, the first power station in 1967, and a new school with a nursery in 1968. In 1983 the building of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Desalinization" title="Desalinization">desalinization plant</a> provided a constant supply of potable water.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linosa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Linosa's population subsists on fishing, agriculture and tourism.</p>
