<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Linolenic acid</h1>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Linoleic_acid" title="Linoleic acid">linoleic acid</a>.</div>
<p><b>Linolenic acid</b> is a type of <a href="/wiki/Fatty_acid" title="Fatty acid">fatty acid</a>. It can refer to either of two <a href="/wiki/Octadecatrienoic_acid" title="Octadecatrienoic acid">octadecatrienoic acids</a> (i.e. with an 18-carbon chain and three double bonds, which are found in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cis-trans_isomerism" title="Cis-trans isomerism">cis</a></i> configuration), or a mixture of the two. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Linolenate" title="Linolenate">Linolenate</a> (in the form of esters of linolenic acid) is often found in vegetable oils; traditionally, such fatty acylates are reported as the fatty acids:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alpha-Linolenic_acid" title="Alpha-Linolenic acid">α-Linolenic acid</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Omega-3_fatty_acid" title="Omega-3 fatty acid">omega-3</a> (n-3) fatty acid</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gamma-Linolenic_acid" title="Gamma-Linolenic acid">γ-Linolenic acid</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Omega-6_fatty_acid" title="Omega-6 fatty acid">omega-6</a> (n-6) fatty acid</li>
</ul>
