<p><b>Leduc</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/uː/ long 'oo' in 'food'">uː</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span></span>/</a></span></span> is a city in the province of <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It is located 33 kilometres (21 mi) south of the provincial capital of <a href="/wiki/Edmonton" title="Edmonton">Edmonton</a> and is part of the <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_Capital_Region" title="Edmonton Capital Region">Edmonton Capital Region</a> metropolitan area.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leduc,_Alberta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Leduc was first established in 1899, when <a href="/wiki/Robert_Telford" title="Robert Telford">Robert Telford</a>, a settler, bought land near a lake which would later bear his name. It was on that piece of land where the new settlement would take root. Telford previously served as an officer for the <a href="/wiki/North-West_Mounted_Police" title="North-West Mounted Police">North-West Mounted Police</a>, and later became Leduc's first postmaster, first general merchantman and first justice of the peace. The establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Calgary_and_Edmonton_Railway" title="Calgary and Edmonton Railway">Calgary and Edmonton Railway</a>, later acquired by the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Pacific_Railway" title="Canadian Pacific Railway">Canadian Pacific Railway</a>, opened the region to settlement. The first train stopped at Leduc in July 1891.</p>
<p>There are two versions of how Leduc got its name. According to popular local legend, the city's name was decided in 1890 when a settler (McKinlay) setting up a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telegraph" title="Telegraph">telegraph</a> office needed a name for the new settlement and decided that it would be named after the first person who came through the door of the telegraph office. That person was <b>Father Hippolyte Leduc</b>, a priest who had served the area since 1867. In another, more official, version, the Lieutenant Governor of the North-West Territories, <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Dewdney" title="Edgar Dewdney">Edgar Dewdney</a> (1835–1916) decided that Telford Place should be renamed at the time the railroad terminal was established in 1891, and picked the name of the missionary priest.</p>
<p>Leduc was first incorporated as a village in 1899, and went on to become officially a town in 1906. It became a city in 1983. By that time its population had reached 12,000.</p>
<p>The town continued to grow quietly over the decades and Alberta's historical oil strike on February 13, 1947, was made near the town at the <a href="/wiki/Leduc_No._1" title="Leduc No. 1">Leduc No. 1</a> oil well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>The population of the City of Leduc according to its <a href="/wiki/Alberta_municipal_censuses,_2016" title="Alberta municipal censuses, 2016">2016 municipal census</a> is 30,496, a <span class="sortkey" style="display:none">7000410009999999999♠</span>4.1% change from its <a href="/wiki/Alberta_municipal_censuses,_2015" title="Alberta municipal censuses, 2015">2015 municipal census</a> population of 29,304.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leduc2016census_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Leduc2016census-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Canada_2011_Census" title="Canada 2011 Census">2011 Census</a>, the City of Leduc had a population of 24,279 living in 9,290 of its 9,789 total dwellings, a 43.1% change from its 2006 population of 16,967. With a land area of 36.97 km<sup>2</sup> (14.27 sq mi), it had a population density of 656.7/km<sup>2</sup> (1,700.9/sq mi) in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011censusABmunis_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-2011censusABmunis-3">[3]</a></sup> The 2011 census also indicated that Leduc was ranked as the municipality with the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_100_largest_municipalities_in_Canada_by_population#Highest_growth.2C_2006.E2.80.932011" title="List of the 100 largest municipalities in Canada by population">ninth-highest</a> population growth between 2006 and 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011highest_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-2011highest-31">[31]</a></sup> Following its 2014 annexation, Statistics Canada adjusted Leduc's 2011 population by an additional 25 people to 24,304.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2014censusadj_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-2014censusadj-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the 2011 municipal census, males and females each accounted for 50% of the population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011census_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-2011census-33">[33]</a></sup> The average age was 35, while residents aged 19 or younger represented 27% of the population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011census_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-2011census-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, Leduc had a population of 16,967 living in 6,718 <a href="/wiki/House" title="House">dwellings</a>, a 12.9% increase from its 2001 population of 15,032.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-statcan2006_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-statcan2006-34">[34]</a></sup> The city has a land area of 36.97 km<sup>2</sup> (14.27 sq mi) and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 458.9/km<sup>2</sup> (1,189/sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-statcan2006_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-statcan2006-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leduc,_Alberta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The City of Leduc is a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/Leduc-Nisku_Economic_Development_Association" title="Leduc-Nisku Economic Development Association">Leduc-Nisku Economic Development Association</a>, an economic development partnership that markets <a href="/wiki/Leduc-Nisku_Economic_Development_Association#Alberta.27s_International_Region" title="Leduc-Nisku Economic Development Association">Alberta's International Region</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AboutEDA_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-AboutEDA-35">[35]</a></sup> in proximity to the <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_International_Airport" title="Edmonton International Airport">Edmonton International Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ExploreEDA_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-ExploreEDA-36">[36]</a></sup> The city forms part of this international transportation and economic region. It is located on the <a href="/wiki/CANAMEX_Corridor" title="CANAMEX Corridor">CANAMEX Trade Corridor</a> at the intersection of two <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Pacific_Railway" title="Canadian Pacific Railway">Canadian Pacific Railway</a> lines and is adjacent to the Edmonton International Airport. These provide transportation links to the petrochemical activities in <a href="/wiki/Alberta%27s_Industrial_Heartland" title="Alberta's Industrial Heartland">Alberta's Industrial Heartland</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Fort_McMurray" title="Fort McMurray">Fort McMurray</a> area, and other economic hubs.</p>
<p>The oil and gas industry has long been the base of Leduc's economy. The Leduc Business Park, which is located in the northern portion of the city, contains more than 1,400 businesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nisku_Industrial_Park" title="Nisku Industrial Park">Nisku Industrial Park</a>, located to the north within Leduc County, also contains many businesses.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arts_and_culture">Arts and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leduc,_Alberta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Arts and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Leduc is home to The Maclab Centre for the Performing Arts, a preeminent performing arts facility, including a 460-seat theatre.</p>
<p>In fall 2009, the Leduc Recreation Centre officially opened. The 309,000-square-foot facility includes three NHL-sized arenas, an aquatic centre and a curling rink.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leduc,_Alberta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Leduc is served by a local newspaper, the <i>Leduc Representative</i> (currently branded the <i>Leduc Rep</i>), as well as a regional newspaper, the <i>Leduc-Wetaskiwin Pipestone Flyer</i>.</p>
<p>Leduc's first FM radio station, <a href="/wiki/CJLD-FM" title="CJLD-FM">CJLD-FM</a>, began in 2013 and is known on-air as "93.1 The One".</p>
<p>An internet based community radio station, branded "Leduc Radio" since 2008, also serves the city.</p>
<p>Due to its location within the Edmonton region, all <a href="/wiki/Media_in_Edmonton" title="Media in Edmonton">major Edmonton media</a> (newspapers, radio and television) also serve Leduc and its surrounding area.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emergency_services">Emergency services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leduc,_Alberta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Emergency services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The City of Leduc has its own fire services and <a href="/wiki/Emergency_medical_services" title="Emergency medical services">emergency management</a> departments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Led by a fire chief,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> the Fire Services Department comprises full and part-time members providing fire, ambulance and patient transportation services to the city and portions of <a href="/wiki/Leduc_County" title="Leduc County">Leduc County</a> to the west, south and east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fire_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-fire-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Policing for Leduc is provided by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Mounted_Police" title="Royal Canadian Mounted Police">Royal Canadian Mounted Police</a> (RCMP), with additional law enforcement is provided by the city's Enforcement Services Department consisting of peace offices appointed by the Alberta Solicitor General.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
