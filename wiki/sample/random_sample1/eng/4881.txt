<p><b>Redondo Beach</b> is one of the three <a href="/wiki/Beach_Cities" title="Beach Cities">Beach Cities</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_County,_California" title="Los Angeles County, California">Los Angeles County</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The population was 66,748 at the 2010 census, up from 63,261 at the 2000 census. The city is located in the <a href="/wiki/South_Bay,_Los_Angeles" title="South Bay, Los Angeles">South Bay</a> region of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Los_Angeles_Area" title="Greater Los Angeles Area">greater Los Angeles area</a>.</p>
<p>Redondo Beach was originally part of the 1785 <a href="/wiki/Rancho_San_Pedro" title="Rancho San Pedro">Rancho San Pedro</a> <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> land grant that later became the South Redondo area. The city's territory has an unusual shape including an area along the beach (South Redondo Beach) and another strip inland from Manhattan Beach and Hermosa Beach (North Redondo Beach). The primary attractions include <a href="/wiki/Redondo_Beach_pier" title="Redondo Beach pier">Municipal Pier</a> and the sandy beach, popular with tourists and a variety of sports enthusiasts. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Redondo_Beach_(Los_Angeles_Metro_station)" title="Redondo Beach (Los Angeles Metro station)">western terminus</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LACMTA_Green_Line" title="LACMTA Green Line">Metro Rail Green Line</a> is in North Redondo Beach.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:RedondoBeach-1906.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="590" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/RedondoBeach-1906.jpg/220px-RedondoBeach-1906.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/RedondoBeach-1906.jpg/330px-RedondoBeach-1906.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/RedondoBeach-1906.jpg/440px-RedondoBeach-1906.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:RedondoBeach-1906.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Redondo Beach, 1906</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Chowigna,_California" title="Chowigna, California">Chowigna Indians</a> used the site of today's Hopkins Wilderness Park, formerly Nike missile site LA-57 from 1956 to 1963, in Redondo Beach, California, as a lookout place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The wetlands located at the site of today's AES power plant in Redondo Beach were a source of foods including halibut, lobster, and sea bass, and also of salt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In the 1700s, the Chowigna bartered salt from the old Redondo Salt Lake, "a spring-fed salt lake about 200 yards wide and 600 yards long situated about 200 yards from the ocean", with other tribes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sjmerc_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-sjmerc-12">[12]</a></sup> Their village by the lake was called "Onoova-nga", or "Place of Salt." The Chowigna were relocated to missions in 1854, when Manuel Dominguez sold 215 acres of <a href="/wiki/Rancho_San_Pedro" title="Rancho San Pedro">Rancho San Pedro</a>, including the lake, to Henry Allanson and William Johnson for the Pacific Salt Works.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sjmerc_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-sjmerc-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Building_a_Better_Redondo_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Building_a_Better_Redondo-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbmuseum_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-rbmuseum-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Moonstone_Beach" title="Moonstone Beach">Moonstone Beach</a> was a tourist attraction from the late 1880s to the early 1920s. Tourists gathered <a href="/wiki/Moonstone_(gemstone)" title="Moonstone (gemstone)">moonstones</a> from the many mounds that had washed ashore during storms.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 6.2 square miles (16.1 km²), over 99% of it land.</p>
<p>Redondo Beach was originally part of the 1784 Rancho San Pedro Spanish land grant of the 43,000-acre (170 km<sup>2</sup>) Dominguez Rancho that later became the ten-mile (16 km) Ocean frontage of <a href="/wiki/Rancho_Sausal_Redondo" title="Rancho Sausal Redondo">Rancho Sausal Redondo</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Neighborhoods">Neighborhoods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Neighborhoods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The ocean side of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_Highway_(US)" title="Pacific Coast Highway (US)">Pacific Coast Highway</a> (PCH) has restaurants and boating activities while inland of PCH is largely residential. Redondo Breakwall is a well-known surf spot in the South Bay. Bordering the Marina is the massive AES power plant, which has been the source of substantial political debate in the city over the last decade, largely centered over what to do with the land once the plant becomes inactive over the next 25 years (subject to change and compromise).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Building_a_Better_Redondo_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Building_a_Better_Redondo-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> This power plant sports <a href="/wiki/List_of_Whaling_Walls" title="List of Whaling Walls">Whaling Wall</a> number 31, a 586 ft (179 m) × 95 ft (29 m) whale mural by world-famous artist <a href="/wiki/Robert_Wyland" title="Robert Wyland">Robert Wyland</a> titled "Gray Whale Migration".</p>

<p>Redondo Beach has a distinct division between the north and south sections of the city with 190th, Anita, and Herondo streets forming its east-west boundary line. South Redondo is along the beachfront with the <a href="/wiki/Redondo_Beach_pier" title="Redondo Beach pier">pier</a> and marina/harbor complex. The small business district near the pier and marina was revived in the 1990s by beachgoers and new residents who wanted to sell beachwear and surfing accessories. That district was once focused on fishing and canning when the pier was used to transport fish-based foodstuffs and canned fish to American and Asian consumers, but that industry had an economic downfall in the 1970s and 1980s. South Redondo has wide streets, wide sand beaches and laid-back feel make it a prime destination for those seeking a "bike to the grocery store" community. Several close-knit neighborhoods exist; South Broadway hosts street parties in the summer where children play on jumping gyms and the local fire department judges the best dessert contest while kids climb their pumper truck. The main library is located in the Civic Center.</p>
<p>North Redondo, north of 190th Street, is an inland community separated from the beachfront by Hermosa Beach and Manhattan Beach. While primarily residential, North Redondo contains some of the city's major industrial and commercial space, including the inland aerospace and engineering firms that are part of Southern California's long space legacy. It is also home to the <a href="/wiki/South_Bay_Galleria" title="South Bay Galleria">South Bay Galleria</a> shopping center and a revitalized Artesia Boulevard. North Redondo is the home of the Redondo Beach Performing Arts Center, one of the South Bay's premier cultural facilities, and home to the Los Angeles Ballet, Distinguished Speaker Series, Kala Koa Entertainment, and a diverse range of other cultural and entertainment events. The North Branch of the Redondo Beach Library serves this area. North Redondo is home to nearly two-thirds of the children in Redondo Beach.</p>
<p>Many original homes still stand in Redondo Beach neighborhoods, but many small Arts and Crafts style homes have been demolished and replaced with new modern houses. Zoning allows properties within two to three blocks of the beach to be developed as large, two to three-unit luxury townhomes; inland areas are more likely to have single-family homes. There is a citywide height limit of 32 ft (9.8 m) for new homes but rooftop living spaces and decks are allowed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>A revitalized downtown area affectionately termed "Riviera Village" (named so after the Hollywood Riviera, the area in which it is located) provides locals an opportunity to eat, shop and commune in a quiet atmosphere. Since 2004 several new or newly renovated restaurants have made a positive impact on local options for an on-the-town experience. Downtown also supports a number of independent boutiques and shops specializing in clothing and fashion, as well as at least three wine-tasting galleries. This downtown area is in South Redondo west of PCH between Avenue I and Palos Verdes Blvd.</p>
<p>The Marina, Harbor and <a href="/wiki/Redondo_Beach_pier" title="Redondo Beach pier">Pier</a> complexes are large, planned centers of activity that host Korean seafood restaurants, touristy bars, smaller shops and a games arcade. The pier is a common spot for anglers to cast for a local catch; many residents of inland Los Angeles drive to Redondo Beach to take advantage of the long and unique shape of this pier. The adjacent Marina, located just north of the Pier, is home to another half-dozen restaurants, a seafood shop, and a pub with almost 100 beers on tap. The large, monolithic concrete structures that make up the pier and harbor area stand in stark contrast to the venerable cozy feel of the rest of the South Bay's neighborhood-style street front architecture; the city continues to consider options for the area as new entertainment and dining chooses to locate in other areas of the Beach Cities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lifestyle">Lifestyle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lifestyle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:RedondoBeach-1890a.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="660" height="133" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/cf/RedondoBeach-1890a.jpg/220px-RedondoBeach-1890a.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/cf/RedondoBeach-1890a.jpg/330px-RedondoBeach-1890a.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/cf/RedondoBeach-1890a.jpg/440px-RedondoBeach-1890a.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:RedondoBeach-1890a.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Redondo Beach, 1890</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Much of the Redondo Beach lifestyle is a blend of the neighborhoods, activities and people of the three <a href="/wiki/Beach_Cities" title="Beach Cities">Beach Cities</a> of <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a>'s <a href="/wiki/South_Bay,_Los_Angeles" title="South Bay, Los Angeles">South Bay</a>. Like its sister cities of <a href="/wiki/Hermosa_Beach,_California" title="Hermosa Beach, California">Hermosa Beach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Beach,_California" title="Manhattan Beach, California">Manhattan Beach</a>, Redondo's draw is the beach that links the three cities. Redondo was described as "The Gem Of The Continent" in the <i>Los Angeles Daily Herald</i> in 1887.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Los_Angeles_Daily_Herald_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Los_Angeles_Daily_Herald-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="The provided source is a picture of a newspaper posted to Pinterest. Certainly, a readable copy exists. (September 2015)">better source needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The beach starts below the bluffs of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palos_Verdes" title="Palos Verdes">Palos Verdes</a> in the south (after <a href="/wiki/Torrance,_California" title="Torrance, California">Torrance</a> Beach) and carries north to the <a href="/wiki/Redondo_Beach_pier" title="Redondo Beach pier">Redondo Pier</a>. The area of the beach that starts in Palos Verdes is known as "RAT (Right After Torrance) Beach." The <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Braude_Bike_Trail" title="Marvin Braude Bike Trail">Marvin Braude Bike Trail</a> runs from Torrance through South Redondo, north to Hermosa Beach, Manhattan Beach, and eventually to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Monica" title="Santa Monica">Santa Monica</a>. The path is broken by the Redondo Beach King Harbor Marina and Pier complex, where it veers away from water and onto dedicated lanes of surface streets for about a mile before again turning to the ocean in Hermosa Beach. Continuing north from Manhattan Beach, the path stretches to Marina Del Rey.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">Surfing</a> is an element of the South Bay lifestyle year-round. Winter storms in the Pacific Ocean sometimes turn typically placid and rolling South Bay waves into large and occasionally dangerous waves, a draw for surfers. Wave heights in December 2005 were some of the largest on record at 15 feet (4.6 m) to 20 feet (6.1 m).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Beach_volleyball" title="Beach volleyball">Beach volleyball</a> is another aspect of Redondo Beach's lifestyle. The wide and flat sand beaches provide the perfect venue for the sport and permanent poles and nets are placed and maintained by the city year-round. Professional tournaments managed by the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Volleyball_Professionals" title="Association of Volleyball Professionals">AVP</a> take place in neighboring Hermosa and Manhattan Beach. Redondo Beach is home to Gold Medalist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kerri_Walsh" title="Kerri Walsh">Kerri Walsh</a> and AVP Pro <a href="/wiki/Casey_Jennings" title="Casey Jennings">Casey Jennings</a>.</p>
<p>In 2006, TV crews for <i><a href="/wiki/Medium_Productions" title="Medium Productions">Medium</a></i> were seen shooting at a local coffee shop. Fox's <i><a href="/wiki/The_O.C." title="The O.C.">The O.C.</a></i> was also seen filming at <a href="/wiki/Redondo_Union_High_School" title="Redondo Union High School">Redondo Union High School</a>, Redondo Beach Pier, and local parks. Also, Redondo Beach was home to the filming of the classic television series, <i><a href="/wiki/Baywatch" title="Baywatch">Baywatch</a></i>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> reported that Redondo Beach had a population of 66,748. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 10,751.1 people per square mile (4,151.0/km²). The racial makeup of Redondo Beach was 49,805 (74.6%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a> (65.2% Non-Hispanic White),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quickfacts.census.gov_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-quickfacts.census.gov-7">[7]</a></sup> 1,852 (2.8%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 291 (0.4%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 8,004 (12.0%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 199 (0.3%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 2,725 (4.1%) from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 3,872 (5.8%) from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 10,142 persons (15.2%).</p>
<p>The Census reported that 66,317 people (99.4% of the population) lived in households, 367 (0.5%) lived in non-institutionalized group quarters, and 64 (0.1%) were institutionalized.</p>
<p>There were 29,011 households, out of which 7,825 (27.0%) had children under the age of 18 living in them, 12,507 (43.1%) were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">opposite-sex married couples</a> living together, 2,515 (8.7%) had a female householder with no husband present, 1,207 (4.2%) had a male householder with no wife present. There were 1,904 (6.6%) <a href="/wiki/POSSLQ" title="POSSLQ">unmarried opposite-sex partnerships</a>, and 179 (0.6%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Same-sex_partnerships" title="Same-sex partnerships">same-sex married couples or partnerships</a>. 9,252 households (31.9%) were made up of individuals and 2,145 (7.4%) had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.29. There were 16,229 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Family_(U.S._Census)" title="Family (U.S. Census)">families</a> (55.9% of all households); the average family size was 2.94.</p>
<p>The population was spread out with 12,887 people (19.3%) under the age of 18, 4,198 people (6.3%) aged 18 to 24, 23,149 people (34.7%) aged 25 to 44, 19,532 people (29.3%) aged 45 to 64, and 6,982 people (10.5%) who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 39.3 years. For every 100 females there were 99.1 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 97.2 males.</p>
<p>There were 30,609 housing units at an average density of 4,930.2 per square mile (1,903.6/km²), of which 14,917 (51.4%) were owner-occupied, and 14,094 (48.6%) were occupied by renters. The homeowner vacancy rate was 0.9%; the rental vacancy rate was 5.3%. 36,796 people (55.1% of the population) lived in owner-occupied housing units and 29,521 people (44.2%) lived in rental housing units.</p>
<p>According to the 2010 United States Census, Redondo Beach had a median household income of $99,496, with 5.4% of the population living below the federal poverty line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quickfacts.census.gov_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-quickfacts.census.gov-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000">2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2000">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-21">[21]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 63,261 people, 28,566 households, and 15,254 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 10,065.4 inhabitants per square mile (3,889.4/km²). There were 29,543 housing units at an average density of 4,700.6 per square mile (1,816.3/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 78.6% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 9.1% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 2.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 4.4% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 4.6,% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 13.5% of the population.</p>
<p>There were 28,566 households out of which 23.3% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 40.6% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 9.0% had a female householder with no husband present, and 46.6% were non-families. 33.1% of all households were made up of individuals and 5.9% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.21 and the average family size was 2.87.</p>
<p>In the city the population was spread out with 18.8% under the age of 18, 6.1% from 18 to 24, 43.1% from 25 to 44, 23.6% from 45 to 64, and 8.5% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 37 years. For every 100 females there were 101.5 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 99.8 males.</p>
<p>According to a 2007 estimate, the median income for a household in the city was $93,274, and the median income for a family was $108,753.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Males had a median income of $56,796 versus $45,204 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $38,305. About 4.0% of families and 5.9% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 6.2% of those under age 18 and 6.1% of those age 65 or over.</p>

<p>Before the beach city housing boom in the 1990s, Redondo Beach's population mirrored much of older communities around Los Angeles. Redondo Beach had generally large numbers of <a href="/wiki/Asian_Americans" title="Asian Americans">Asian Americans</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islanders" title="Pacific Islanders">Pacific Islanders</a> (esp. <a href="/wiki/Native_Hawaiians" title="Native Hawaiians">Hawaiians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samoans" title="Samoans">Samoans</a>) for cities with an excess of 50,000 residents. The city has a sizable <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> community, due to the fact that numerous participants in the <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Indian_Affairs" title="Bureau of Indian Affairs">Bureau of Indian Affairs</a>' relocation programs chose the South Bay area of Los Angeles. Nearby is the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=El_Nido,_Los_Angeles_County,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="El Nido, Los Angeles County, California (page does not exist)">El Nido</a> section, briefly a <a href="/wiki/Chicano" title="Chicano">Chicano</a> area before the 1970s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In the early 1900s, the developing community attracted immigrants, often fishermen and maritime workers of <a href="/wiki/Dutch_people" title="Dutch people">Dutch</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_people" title="Greek people">Greek</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_people" title="Italian people">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_people" title="Portuguese people">Portuguese</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_people" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> descent, who used to have neighborhoods on the city's southern end known as "Clifton" (the county area was incorporated into Redondo Beach in 1976). The community also had its share of Asian American and <a href="/wiki/Latino" title="Latino">Latino</a> (Mexican or other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_American" title="Latin American">Latin American</a>) residents. <a href="/wiki/African_Americans" title="African Americans">African Americans</a> encountered few problems on moving into the mixed-race community, especially after the 1950s.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cost_of_living">Cost of living</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cost of living">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to public data from the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i>, real estate prices increased almost 20% per year between 1999 and 2005. Properties within walking distance of the ocean routinely sell for over $1 million. <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Money_Magazine" title="Money Magazine">Money Magazine</a></i> ranks communities in the area as some of the most expensive places to live in the U.S. The average three-bed, two-bath home costs about $1,000,000 in South Redondo as of 2006 and $875,000 in North Redondo. The 2007 "credit meltdown" has affected home values in the area to a lesser extent than the rest of <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Municipal_government">Municipal government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Municipal government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the city’s 2012 Comprehensive Annual Financial Report, the city’s various funds had $109.5 million in Revenues, $106.8 million in expenditures, $283.1 million in total assets, $66.3 million in total liabilities, and $79.5 million in cash and investments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-City_of_Redondo_Beach_CAFR_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-City_of_Redondo_Beach_CAFR-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Beach_Cities_Health_District" title="Beach Cities Health District">Beach Cities Health District</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> one of 78 California Health Districts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> is located in Redondo Beach. Created in 1955, as South Bay Hospital, to provide health and wellness services to the residents of <a href="/wiki/Hermosa_Beach,_California" title="Hermosa Beach, California">Hermosa Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Beach,_California" title="Manhattan Beach, California">Manhattan Beach</a>, and Redondo Beach. The district took on its current name in 1993. In 2002, Beach Cities Health District opened AdventurePlex,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> a Manhattan Beach fitness facility for youth and their families of the three beach cities. AdventurePlex has mazes, tunnels, outdoor <a href="/wiki/Rock_climbing" title="Rock climbing">rock climbing</a> walls, complex <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ropes_courses" title="Ropes courses">ropes courses</a>, and an indoor gymnasium.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="State_and_federal_representation">State and federal representation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: State and federal representation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/California_State_Legislature" title="California State Legislature">California State Legislature</a>, Redondo Beach is in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_26th_State_Senate_district" title="California's 26th State Senate district">the 26th Senate District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Democratic_Party" title="California Democratic Party">Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/Ben_Allen" title="Ben Allen">Ben Allen</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_66th_State_Assembly_district" title="California's 66th State Assembly district">the 66th Assembly District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Democratic_Party" title="California Democratic Party">Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/Al_Muratsuchi" title="Al Muratsuchi">Al Muratsuchi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swd_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-swd-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a>, Redondo Beach is in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_33rd_congressional_district" title="California's 33rd congressional district">California's 33rd congressional district</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/Ted_Lieu" title="Ted Lieu">Ted Lieu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2008" title="United States presidential election, 2008">2008 presidential election</a> <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> won 59% of the vote and <a href="/wiki/John_McCain" title="John McCain">John McCain</a> won 37% of the vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Redondo_Beach_Unified_School_District" title="Redondo Beach Unified School District">Redondo Beach Unified School District</a> serves the city. <a href="/wiki/Redondo_Union_High_School" title="Redondo Union High School">Redondo Union High School</a> is the zoned high school, with the adjoining campus of Redondo Shores High School located to the west of the Redondo Union High School main grounds serving as a continuation school. The Redondo Beach Learning Academy day school for grades 9-12 is also located on the Redondo Shores High School premises, and the campus is the designated independent studies program of the Redondo Beach Unified School District.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Additionally, Redondo Beach has two middle schools, Adams Middle School and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parras_Middle_School" title="Parras Middle School">Parras Middle School</a>. Adams Middle School, located in North Redondo Beach, primarily serves 6th to 8th grade students in the North Redondo area, with Parras Middle School being the designated middle school of South Redondo Beach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The city also has eight established elementary schools: Alta Vista, Beryl Heights, Birney, Jefferson, Lincoln, Madison, Tulita and Washington.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> All twelve schools are evenly divided throughout the North and South areas of Redondo Beach, with five elementary schools and one middle school located in North/Central Redondo Beach; and four elementary schools, one middle school, and the singular designated high school placed in South Redondo Beach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The Redondo Beach Educational Foundation was originally founded in 1992 and revitalized in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Valor_Christian_Academy" title="Valor Christian Academy">Valor Christian Academy</a> (formerly Coast Christian School) is in Redondo Beach.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Redondo_Beach_Police_Department">Redondo Beach Police Department</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Redondo Beach Police Department">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Redondo Beach Police Department was established in the 1920s. The Police Department consists of 110 sworn members (officers) plus 57 non-sworn members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Redondo_Beach_Public_Library">Redondo Beach Public Library</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Redondo_Beach,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Redondo Beach Public Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first library in Redondo Beach began as a reading room in 1895. The first five-member Library Commission for the city was formed in November 1908. The library moved into the then City Hall's west wing in 1909 and remained there until 1917 when it filled the entire west wing at 301 Emerald Street. In 1928 the Chamber of Commerce recommended a new library be built on the site previously occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Redondo" title="Hotel Redondo">Hotel Redondo</a> in what is now Veterans Park. The Veterans Park Library is a Spanish/Dutch colonial building designed by architect Lovel Bearse Pemberton and opened on July 2, 1930. It was placed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1981. After serving as the Main Library for the city for 60 years, a site adjacent to City Hall was identified for a new, modern Main Library building. The new Main Library for the City of Redondo Beach opened on July 8, 1995 at 303 N. Pacific Coast Highway. A North Branch Library was also established in 1930 and started at the Grant Community Hall, it then moved to its current location at 2000 Artesia Boulevard in 1949. A new North Branch Library was constructed on the site in 2009 and opened its doors on September 28, 2010. The North Branch Library is the first City owned Green building and received Gold <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LEED_certification" title="LEED certification">LEED certification</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
