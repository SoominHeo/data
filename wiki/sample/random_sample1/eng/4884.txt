<p><b>RELX Group</b> (known as <b>Reed Elsevier</b> between 1992 and February 2015) is a <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> information and analytics company based in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, in the United Kingdom. It operates in four market segments: scientific, technical and medical; risk and business analytics; legal; and exhibitions.</p>
<p>It is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dual-listed" title="Dual-listed">dual-listed</a> company. RELX PLC, the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a> listed shareholding vehicle, holds 52.9% of the shares in RELX Group. RELX NV, the <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Stock_Exchange" title="Amsterdam Stock Exchange">Amsterdam Stock Exchange</a> listed shareholding vehicle, holds 47.1% of the shares in RELX Group. The shares are traded on the London Stock Exchange, Amsterdam Stock Exchange and <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> using the following ticker symbols: London: REL, Amsterdam: REN, New York: RELX and RENX. The company is one of the constituents of the <a href="/wiki/FTSE_100_Index" title="FTSE 100 Index">FTSE 100 Index</a>, <a href="/wiki/Financial_Times_Global_500" title="Financial Times Global 500">Financial Times Global 500</a> and <a href="/wiki/Euronext_100" title="Euronext 100">Euronext 100</a> Index.</p>
<p>The company operates in 40 countries and serves customers in over 180 nations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ar_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-ar-1">[1]</a></sup> About 54% of the company’s revenues are generated from the US, with 26 per cent from Europe and 20 per cent from the rest of the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ar_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-ar-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2000, two-thirds of revenues were from print and only a small percentage was digital; by 2015, those numbers had been reversed - print now generates just 15 per cent of revenues at RELX Group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ar_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-ar-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>RELX Group</b> was previously known as Reed Elsevier. The company announced the change of the operating company's name to RELX Group in February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rename_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rename-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reed Elsevier came into being in 1992, following the merger of Reed International, a British trade book and magazine publisher, and <a href="/wiki/Elsevier" title="Elsevier">Elsevier</a>, the Netherlands-based scientific publisher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reed_International">Reed International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reed International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1895, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Edwin_Reed" title="Albert Edwin Reed">Albert E. Reed</a> established a <a href="/wiki/Newsprint" title="Newsprint">newsprint</a> manufacturing operation at <a href="/wiki/Loose_Stream" title="Loose Stream">Tovil Mill</a> near <a href="/wiki/Maidstone" title="Maidstone">Maidstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1965 Reed Group, as it was then known, became a <a href="/wiki/Conglomerate_(company)" title="Conglomerate (company)">conglomerate</a>, creating its Decorative Products Division with the purchase of <a href="/wiki/Crown_Paints" title="Crown Paints">Crown Paints</a>, Polycell and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Sanderson_%26_Sons" title="Arthur Sanderson &amp; Sons">Sanderson</a>'s wallpaper and DIY decorating interests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sand_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-sand-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1970, Reed Group merged with the <a href="/wiki/Time_Inc._UK" title="Time Inc. UK">International Publishing Corporation</a> and the company name was changed to Reed International Limited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> The company continued to grow by merging with other publishers and produced high quality trade journals as IPC Business Press Ltd and women's and other consumer magazines as IPC magazines Ltd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> The original family owners, the Reeds, were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methodist" title="Methodist">Methodists</a> and encouraged good working conditions for their staff in the then-dangerous print trade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1985 the company decided to rationalise its operations, focusing on publishing and selling off its other interests. Sanderson was sold to <a href="/wiki/WestPoint_Home" title="WestPoint Home">WestPoint Pepperell, Inc.</a> of <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, that year,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sand_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-sand-5">[5]</a></sup> while Crown Paint and Polycell were sold to <a href="/wiki/Williams_Holdings" title="Williams Holdings">Williams Holdings</a> in 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The company's paper and packaging production operations were bundled together to form Reedpack and sold to <a href="/wiki/Private_equity" title="Private equity">private equity</a> firm <a href="/wiki/Cinven" title="Cinven">Cinven</a> in 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsevier_NV">Elsevier NV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Elsevier NV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1880, Jacobus George Robbers started a publishing company called NV Uitgeversmaatschappij Elsevier (Elsevier Publishing Company NV) to publish literary classics and the encyclopedia <i><a href="/wiki/Winkler_Prins" title="Winkler Prins">Winkler Prins</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> Robbers named the company after the old Dutch printers family <a href="/wiki/House_of_Elzevir" title="House of Elzevir">Elzevir</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> which, for example, published the works of <a href="/wiki/Erasmus" title="Erasmus">Erasmus</a> in 1587. Elsevier NV originally was based in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a> but moved to <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> in the late 1880s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Up to the 1930s, Elsevier remained a small family-owned publisher, with no more than ten employees. After the war it launched the weekly <i><a href="/wiki/Elsevier_(magazine)" title="Elsevier (magazine)">Elsevier</a></i> magazine, which turned out to be very profitable. A rapid expansion followed. Elsevier Press Inc. started in 1951 in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, Texas, USA, and in 1962 publishing offices were opened in London and New York. Multiple mergers in the 1970s led to name changes, settling at "Elsevier Scientific Publishers" in 1979. In 1991, two years before the merger with Reed, Elsevier acquired <a href="/wiki/Pergamon_Press" title="Pergamon Press">Pergamon Press</a> in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 21st century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the past three years, the value of the group's acquisitions has averaged about £300m a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft.com_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ft.com-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Significant_acquisitions">Significant acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Significant acquisitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The company bought the Official Airline Guides Inc, a publisher of airline schedules, for $425 million in August 1993,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> LexisNexis, an on-line information business, for $1.5 billion in October 1994<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and MDL Information Systems Inc, a US software systems and information database developer, for $320 million in March 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company then acquired Chilton Business Group, a US business information publishing company, for $447 million in June 1997,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Matthew Bender &amp; Company Inc, a US publisher of legal information, for $1.65 billion in April 1998<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Harcourt_(publisher)" title="Harcourt (publisher)">Harcourt</a>, an education publishing business, for US$4.5 billion plus debt in October 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004, LexisNexis acquired Seisint of <a href="/wiki/Boca_Raton,_Florida" title="Boca Raton, Florida">Boca Raton, Florida</a> for $775 million, which provided the company with access to HPCC Systems for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2005, it bought Medimedia, a medical publisher, for $270 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2008, shareholders of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choicepoint" title="Choicepoint">Choicepoint</a> voted in favor of acquisition by the company for $4.1 billion. The acquisition was completed in September 2008. Choicepoint was a spinoff of Equifax's Insurance Services Group in August 1997. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2011, Reed Business Information, a RELX Group business, acquired Ascend, a London-based civil aviation data analytics company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2011 Reed Elsevier purchased US online-data business Accuity Holdings Inc. from investment firm <a href="/wiki/Investcorp" title="Investcorp">Investcorp</a> for £343 million ($530.1 million) in cash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2012, LexisNexis Legal &amp; Professional, a RELX Group company, acquired <a href="/wiki/Law360" title="Law360">Law360</a>, a US-based online provider of legal information and analysis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2013, Elsevier, a RELX Group company, acquired <a href="/wiki/Mendeley" title="Mendeley">Mendeley</a>, a London-based desktop and web program for managing and sharing research papers, discovering research data and collaborating online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2013, LexisNexis Risk Solutions, a RELX Group company, acquired Mapflow, a Dublin-based group that helps insurance companies assess geographic risk, in particular in relation to flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2014, LexisNexis Risk Solutions acquired Tracesmart, a UK-based provider of tracing, identity verification, fraud prevention and anti-money laundering solutions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2014, LexisNexis Risk Solutions acquired Wunelli, a telematics data business which uses driving data for insurers, enabling them to reduce risk exposure and deliver discounts to safer drivers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2014, Accuity, a RELX business, acquired Fircosoft, a Paris-based anti-money laundering company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft.com_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ft.com-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2014, LexisNexis Risk Solutions bought Health Market Science (HMS), a supplier of high quality data about US healthcare professionals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2015, LexisNexis Risk Solutions acquired BAIR Analytics, a US-based law enforcement data company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2015, LexisNexis Legal &amp; Professional, a RELX Group company, acquired MLex, a media organization providing exclusive analysis and commentary on regulatory risk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2015, LexisNexis Legal &amp; Professional, a RELX Group company, acquired <a href="/wiki/Lex_Machina" title="Lex Machina">Lex Machina</a>, a US-based online provider of legal analytics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Significant_disposals">Significant disposals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Significant disposals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In February 2007, the company announced its intention to sell Harcourt, its educational publishing division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> On 4 May 2007 <a href="/wiki/Pearson_PLC" title="Pearson PLC">Pearson</a>, the international education and information company, announced that it had agreed to acquire <a href="/wiki/Harcourt_Assessment" title="Harcourt Assessment">Harcourt Assessment</a> and Harcourt Education International from Reed Elsevier for $950m in cash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In July 2007, Reed Elsevier announced its agreement to sell the remaining Harcourt Education business, including international imprint <a href="/wiki/Heinemann_(publisher)" title="Heinemann (publisher)">Heinemann</a>, to Houghton Mifflin Riverdeep Group for $4 billion in cash and stock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2009, Reed Elsevier announced its intention to sell most of its North American trade publications, including <i><a href="/wiki/Publishers_Weekly" title="Publishers Weekly">Publishers Weekly</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Broadcasting_%26_Cable" title="Broadcasting &amp; Cable">Broadcasting &amp; Cable</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Multichannel_News" title="Multichannel News">Multichannel News</a></i>, although it planned to retain <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2010, Reed Elsevier announced that it had sold 21 US magazines to other owners in recent months, and that an additional 23 US trade magazines, including <i>Restaurants &amp; Institutions</i>, <i>Hotels</i>, and <i>Trade Show Week</i> would cease publication. The closures were mostly due to the weak economy including an advertising slump.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Variety, the company's last remaining North American title, was sold in October 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Reed Business Information sold BuyerZone, an online marketplace; emedia, an American provider of research for IT buyers and vendors; and a majority stake in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reed_Construction_Data" title="Reed Construction Data">Reed Construction Data</a>, a provider of construction data.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>RELX Group operates in four market segments:</p>
<ul>
<li>Scientific, Technical &amp; Medical, under the Elsevier brand</li>
<li>Risk &amp; Business Analytics, under the LexisNexis Risk Solutions and Reed Business Information brands</li>
<li>Legal, under the LexisNexis brand</li>
<li>Exhibitions, under the Reed Exhibitions brand</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scientific.2C_Technical_.26_Medical">Scientific, Technical &amp; Medical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Scientific, Technical &amp; Medical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>RELX Group's Scientific, Technical &amp; Medical business provides information, analytics and tools that help investors make decisions that improve scientific and healthcare outcomes. It operates under the name of Elsevier and generated revenues in the year to 31 December 2015 of £2 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/ScienceDirect" title="ScienceDirect">ScienceDirect</a>, an online database of primary research, contains 13 million documents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Scopus" title="Scopus">Scopus</a> is a bibliographic database containing abstracts and citations for academic journal articles. It contains more than 50 million items in more 20,000 titles from 5,000 publishers worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mendeley" title="Mendeley">Mendeley</a> is a desktop and web program for managing and sharing research papers, discovering research data and collaborating online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Elsevier is the world's largest publisher of academic articles with 16 per cent market share, according to the Financial Times. It publishes 400,000 articles a year in about 2,500 journals. Its best-known titles are The Lancet and Cell. In 1995, Forbes Magazine (wrongly) predicted Elsevier would be "the first victim of the internet" as it was disrupted and disintermediated by the world wide web.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ftnov15_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-ftnov15-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Elsevier has been criticized for its high prices; excessive profitability; and limiting the diffusion of innovation by putting scientific research behind paywalls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016 Elsevier signed a landmark agreement with the UK’s research institutions, granting academics access to globally-published research at costs lower than the industry average.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Risk_.26_Business_Analytics">Risk &amp; Business Analytics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Risk &amp; Business Analytics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Risk &amp; Business Analytics provides decision-making tools which help banks spot money launderers and insurance companies weed out fraudulent claims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>The business claims to have saved the state of Florida more than $60 million a year by preventing benefit fraud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Leading brands include <a href="/wiki/Flightglobal" title="Flightglobal">Flightglobal</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Farmers_Weekly" title="Farmers Weekly">Farmers Weekly</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/New_Scientist" title="New Scientist">New Scientist</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In 2015, Forbes Magazine voted LexisNexis, a RELX Group company, one of the top 10 employers in the US Technology Sector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2000, LexisNexis Risk Solutions technology has helped trace 155 missing children through its ADAM Programme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FD-dec15_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-FD-dec15-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal">Legal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Legal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>RELX Group's legal business operates under the <a href="/wiki/LexisNexis" title="LexisNexis">LexisNexis</a> brand. Many of LexisNexis' brands date back to the nineteenth century or earlier. These include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Butterworths" title="Butterworths">Butterworths</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tolley_(company)" title="Tolley (company)">Tolley</a> in the UK and JurisClasseur in France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Exhibitions">Exhibitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Exhibitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>RELX Group’s exhibitions business is called <a href="/wiki/Reed_Exhibitions" title="Reed Exhibitions">Reed Exhibitions</a>. It is the world’s largest exhibitions company, running 500 shows for 140,000 exhibitors and 7m visitors..<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FD-dec15_52-1"><a href="#cite_note-FD-dec15-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2013, shortly after the Sandy Hook Elementary School Shooting when 20 children and six adult staff members were killed, Reed Exhibitions caused an uproar at the Eastern Sports and Outdoor Show when it decided to not allow assault weapons and high-capacity magazines at the show, also known as AR-15s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> This decision caused a huge backlash and subsequent boycott that eventually shut the show down for that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Also the National Shooting Sports Foundation (NSSF) terminated their management of the SHOT show held in Las Vegas every year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, Reed Exhibitions sold its defense exhibitions business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_Social_Responsibility">Corporate Social Responsibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Corporate Social Responsibility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>The Reed Elsevier Environmental Challenge</b> awards grants to projects advancing access to safe water and sanitation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>The Elsevier Foundation</b> supports libraries in developing countries, women scientists and nursing facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<p>LexisNexis Legal &amp; Professional has worked with the Atlantic Council to launch the first draft of the <b>Global Rule of Law Business Principles</b> which will help businesses, law firms and NGOs promote and uphold the rule of law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>LexisNexis Legal &amp; Professional has worked with the International Bar Association to launch an application called <b>eyeWitness to Atrocities</b>. The app is designed to capture GPS coordinates, date and time stamps, sensory and movement data, and the location of nearby objects such as Wi-Fi networks. The technology also creates a secure chain of custody to help verify that the images and video has not been edited or digitally manipulated. The goal is to create content that can be used in a court of law to prosecute perpetrators of atrocities and human rights abuses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>LexisNexis Risk Solutions continues to operate the <b>ADAM</b> (Automated Delivery of Alerts on Missing Children) programme in the US, developed by employees. The programme assists in the recovery of missing children through its system of targeted alerts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> LexisNexis Risk Solutions has also launched <b>Social Media Monitor</b> to help law enforcement officials investigate serious crimes such as drug dealing and human trafficking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016 Elsevier's not-for-profit Elsevier Foundation committed $1m a year, for 3 years, to programmes encouraging diversity in science, technology and medicine and promoting science research in developing countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Elsevier#Criticism_and_controversies" title="Elsevier">Elsevier § Criticism and controversies</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Boycott">Boycott</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Boycott">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Reed Elsevier has been criticised for the high prices of its journals and services, especially those published by Elsevier. It has also supported <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SOPA" title="SOPA">SOPA</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Personal_Information_Protection_Act" title="Personal Information Protection Act">PIPA</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Research_Works_Act" title="Research Works Act">Research Works Act</a>, although it no longer supports the last. Because of this, members of the scientific community have boycotted Elsevier journals. In January 2012, the boycott gained an online pledge and petition (<a href="/wiki/The_Cost_of_Knowledge" title="The Cost of Knowledge">The Cost of Knowledge</a>) initiated by mathematician and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fields_medal" title="Fields medal">Fields medalist</a> Sir <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Gowers" title="Timothy Gowers">Timothy Gowers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> The movement has received support from noted science bloggers, such as biologist <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Eisen" title="Jonathan Eisen">Jonathan Eisen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> Between 2012 and November 2015, about 15,391 scientists signed The Cost of Knowledge boycott. In 2014, Elsevier received article submissions from 1.8m authors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ftnov15_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-ftnov15-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Privacy">Privacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Privacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Reed Elsevier collected, used, and sold data on millions of consumers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> It has settled a Federal Trade Commission action taken against it, for failure to provide reasonable security for a database containing names, current and prior addresses, dates of birth, drivers license numbers and Social Security numbers, amongst other data. This information was obtained from credit reporting agencies and other sources, and made available to purchasers through a password-secured website which permitted easy-to-guess passwords, allowing identity thieves to steal records on over quarter of a million people. This breach occurred in 2005 through a recently purchased subsidiary, Seisint.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Defence_exhibitions">Defence exhibitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RELX_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Defence exhibitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between 2005 and 2007, members of the medical and scientific communities, which purchase and use many journals published by Reed Elsevier, agitated for the company to cut its links to the arms trade. Two UK academics, Dr Tom Stafford of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheffield_University" title="Sheffield University">Sheffield University</a> and Dr Nick Gill, launched petitions calling on Reed Elsevier to stop organising arms fairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> A subsidiary, Spearhead, organised defence shows, including an event where it was reported that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cluster_bombs" title="Cluster bombs">cluster bombs</a> and extremely powerful <a href="/wiki/Riot_control" title="Riot control">riot control</a> equipment were offered for sale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2007, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Smith_(editor)" title="Richard Smith (editor)">Richard Smith</a>, former editor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Medical_Journal" title="British Medical Journal">British Medical Journal</a>, published an editorial in the <a href="/wiki/Journal_of_the_Royal_Society_of_Medicine" title="Journal of the Royal Society of Medicine">Journal of the Royal Society of Medicine</a>, arguing that Reed Elsevier's involvement in both the arms trade and medical publishing constituted a conflict of interest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 June 2007, Reed Elsevier announced that they would be exiting the Defence Exhibition business during the second half of 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
