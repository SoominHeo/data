<p><i><b>Lillia and Treize</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">リリアとトレイズ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Riria to Toreizu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novel</a> adventure series by <a href="/wiki/Keiichi_Sigsawa" title="Keiichi Sigsawa">Keiichi Sigsawa</a>, with illustrations by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kohaku_Kuroboshi" title="Kohaku Kuroboshi">Kohaku Kuroboshi</a>, starring the children of the characters from <i><a href="/wiki/Allison_(novel_series)" title="Allison (novel series)">Allison</a></i>. There are six light novels in the series, published by <a href="/wiki/MediaWorks_(publisher)" title="MediaWorks (publisher)">MediaWorks</a> under their <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Bunko" title="Dengeki Bunko">Dengeki Bunko</a></i> label. The first novel was released on March 10, 2005, and the last novel was published on April 10, 2007; there are three stories in the six volumes, with each story taking up two volumes. The series will be followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Meg_and_Seron" title="Meg and Seron">Meg and Seron</a></i>. A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hiroki_Haruse&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hiroki Haruse (page does not exist)">Hiroki Haruse</a> started serialization in the <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo" title="Shōjo">shōjo</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manga_magazine" title="Manga magazine">manga magazine</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic_Sylph" title="Comic Sylph">Comic Sylph</a></i> on December 9, 2006, also published by MediaWorks. An <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> adaptation aired between April and October 2008 based on both the <i>Allison</i> and <i>Lillia and Treize</i> novels, known as <i><a href="/wiki/Allison_%26_Lillia" title="Allison &amp; Lillia">Allison &amp; Lillia</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lillia_and_Treize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dt>Lillia <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">リリア</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Riria</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>The female protagonist of the series. Lillia is the 15-year-old (16 in the third arc) daughter of <a href="/wiki/Allison_(novel_series)" title="Allison (novel series)">Allison and Wil</a>. She is feisty and outspoken with a childish streak. She prefers to be called Lillia because her full name (Lilliane Acacia Corazon Whittington Schultz) is too long. She is bilingual and able to fly a plane and has known Treize since childhood.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dt>Treize <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">トレイズ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Toreizu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd>The 16 (17 in the third arc) year-old son of <a href="/wiki/Allison_(novel_series)" title="Allison (novel series)">Carr Benedict</a>, formerly of Sous-Beil, and <a href="/wiki/Allison_(novel_series)" title="Allison (novel series)">Fiona</a>, also known as Queen Franchesca of Ixtova. Has a twin sister named Merielle whom he argues frequently with (especially regarding firstborn claims) and rarely wins against. He takes after his mother in looks. He is thoughtful and calm by nature and is quite skilled at many things (like cooking, hunting, flying planes and languages), but he is also somewhat dorky. Because of the Ixtovan royal family's "single child" rule, his rank as prince is not publicly known. His royal family crest is the hawk. He and Lillia have known each other since childhood. Through the years, he appears to develop feelings for her, but he is unable to act (effectively) on them. Of all the major characters in this part of the series, only Lillia is unaware of his real identity.</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lillia_and_Treize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Light_novels">Light novels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lillia_and_Treize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Light novels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Lillia and Treize</i> began as a series of <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novels</a> written by <a href="/wiki/Keiichi_Sigsawa" title="Keiichi Sigsawa">Keiichi Sigsawa</a> and drawn by <a href="/wiki/Kouhaku_Kuroboshi" title="Kouhaku Kuroboshi">Kouhaku Kuroboshi</a>. The novels are published by <a href="/wiki/MediaWorks_(publisher)" title="MediaWorks (publisher)">MediaWorks</a> under their <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Bunko" title="Dengeki Bunko">Dengeki Bunko</a></i> publishing label. There are six novels which encompass three stories split between two volumes each. The first novel was released on March 10, 2005, and the final novel was published on April 10, 2007. The series was followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Meg_and_Seron" title="Meg and Seron">Meg and Seron</a></i>. The light novels have sold over 1.1 million copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lillia_and_Treize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation started serialization in the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Djo" title="Shōjo">shōjo</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manga_magazine" title="Manga magazine">manga magazine</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic_Sylph" title="Comic Sylph">Comic Sylph</a></i> on December 9, 2006, published by <a href="/wiki/MediaWorks_(publisher)" title="MediaWorks (publisher)">MediaWorks</a> and ended on November 22, 2008. The manga takes its story from the light novels that preceded it, and is illustrated by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hiroki_Haruse&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hiroki Haruse (page does not exist)">Hiroki Haruse</a>. The first <a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">bound volume</a> was published by <a href="/wiki/ASCII_Media_Works" title="ASCII Media Works">ASCII Media Works</a> on April 26, 2008 under their <i><a href="/wiki/Dengeki_Comics" title="Dengeki Comics">Dengeki Comics</a></i> label. Two volumes in total were published.</p>
