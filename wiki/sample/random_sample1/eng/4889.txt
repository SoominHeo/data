<p><b>Mizuki Kawashita</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">河下 水希</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kawashita Mizuki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born August 30, 1971, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shizuoka_prefecture" title="Shizuoka prefecture">Shizuoka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>)</span> is a female Japanese <a href="/wiki/Mangaka" title="Mangaka">manga artist</a>, best known for her romantic comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Strawberry_100%25" title="Strawberry 100%">Strawberry 100%</a></i> which was published by <a href="/wiki/Shueisha" title="Shueisha">Shueisha</a> in <i>Weekly Shonen Jump</i> from 2002 to 2005 and was published in 19 volumes, as well as an anime and OAV television adaptation. During the early part of her career, she wrote and illustrated under the <a href="/wiki/Pen_name" title="Pen name">pen name</a> <b>Mikan Momokuri</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">桃栗 みかん</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Momokuri Mikan</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. Her first public work was a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doujinshi" title="Doujinshi">doujinshi</a></i> called <i>Innocent</i> in 1993.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Another series, <i><a href="/wiki/First_Love_Limited" title="First Love Limited">First Love Limited</a></i>, was adapted into a 12-episode anime television series which ran in 2009. Other works include <i>Lilim Kiss</i>, <i>Akane-chan overdrive</i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ane_Doki" title="Ane Doki">Ane Doki</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
