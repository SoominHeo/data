<p><b>Rimush</b> (or <b>Rimuš</b>) was the second king of the <a href="/wiki/Akkadian_Empire" title="Akkadian Empire">Akkadian Empire</a>. He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Sargon_of_Akkad" title="Sargon of Akkad">Sargon of Akkad</a> and Queen <a href="/wiki/Tashlultum" title="Tashlultum">Tashlultum</a>. He was succeeded by his brother <a href="/wiki/Manishtushu" title="Manishtushu">Manishtushu</a>, and was an uncle of <a href="/wiki/Naram-Sin_of_Akkad" title="Naram-Sin of Akkad">Naram-Sin of Akkad</a>.</p>
<p>According to his inscriptions, he faced widespread revolts, and had to reconquer the cities of <a href="/wiki/Ur" title="Ur">Ur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Umma" title="Umma">Umma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adab_(city)" title="Adab (city)">Adab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lagash" title="Lagash">Lagash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Der_(Sumer)" title="Der (Sumer)">Der</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kazallu" title="Kazallu">Kazallu</a> from rebellious <i>ensis</i>. There are also records of victorious campaigns against <a href="/wiki/Elam" title="Elam">Elam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marhasi" title="Marhasi">Barakhshe</a>. A number of his votive offerings have been found in excavated temples in several Mesopotamian cities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Sumerian_King_List" title="Sumerian King List">Sumerian King List</a></i>, his reign lasted 9 years (though variant copies read 7 or 15 years.) There is one surviving year-name for an unknown year in his reign: "Year in which <a href="/wiki/Adab_(city)" title="Adab (city)">Adab</a> was destroyed". Tradition gives that he was assassinated, as the <a href="/wiki/B%C4%81r%C3%BBtu" title="Bārûtu">Bārûtu</a>, “art of the diviner”, a first millennium compendium of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extispicy" title="Extispicy">extispicy</a>, records “Omen of king Rimuš, whom his courtiers killed with their seals”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was succeeded by his "elder" brother <a href="/wiki/Manishtushu" title="Manishtushu">Manishtushu</a>, leading to speculation by <a href="/wiki/Mario_Liverani" title="Mario Liverani">Mario Liverani</a> that the two were twins,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> as in: <i>man ištuśu? rïmuš!</i> “Who is with him? His beloved!”, as apparently the second born was thought to be the first conceived.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
