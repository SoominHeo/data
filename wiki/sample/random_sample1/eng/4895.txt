<p><b>Livadeia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Λιβαδειά</span> <i>Livadiá</i>, <small>Greek pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Greek" title="Help:IPA for Greek">[livaˈðʝa]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a>: Λεβάδεια, <i>Lebadeia</i>) is a town in central <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>. It is the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Boeotia" title="Boeotia">Boeotia</a> <a href="/wiki/Regional_units_of_Greece" title="Regional units of Greece">regional district</a>. Livadeia lies 90 km (56 mi) north-west of <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, 64 km (40 mi) west of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chalkida" title="Chalkida">Chalkida</a>, 63 km (39 mi) south-east of <a href="/wiki/Lamia_(city)" title="Lamia (city)">Lamia</a>, 44 km (27 mi) east-south-east of <a href="/wiki/Amfissa" title="Amfissa">Amfissa</a>, and 91 km (57 mi) east-north-east of <a href="/wiki/Nafpaktos" title="Nafpaktos">Nafpaktos</a>. The town lies some five kilometres (3.1 mi) west of <a href="/wiki/Greek_National_Road_3" title="Greek National Road 3">Greek National Road 3</a>, to which it is linked by <a href="/wiki/Greek_National_Road_48" title="Greek National Road 48">National Road 48</a>.</p>
<p>The area around Livadeia is mountainous, with farming activities mainly confined to the valleys. The area has traditionally been associated with the production and processing of cotton and tobacco, as well as the cultivation of cereal crops and the raising of livestock.</p>
<p>Livadeia is home to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Levadiakos_FC" title="Levadiakos FC">Levadiakos FC</a>, members of the <a href="/wiki/Superleague_Greece" title="Superleague Greece">Greek Superleague</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Livadeia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The municipality of Livadeia covers an area of 694.016 km<sup>2</sup> (267.961 sq mi), the municipal unit of Livadeia 166.691 km<sup>2</sup> (64.360 sq mi) and the community 139.614 km<sup>2</sup> (53.905 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stat01_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-stat01-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Municipality">Municipality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Livadeia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Municipality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The municipality Livadeia was formed at the 2011 local government reform by the merger of the following 5 former municipalities, that became municipal units (constituent communities in brackets):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kallikratis_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kallikratis-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chaironeia" title="Chaironeia">Chaironeia</a> (Agios Vlasios, <a href="/wiki/Akontio" title="Akontio">Akontio</a>, Anthochori, Chaironeia, Profitis Ilias, Prosili, Thouri, Vasilika)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Davleia" title="Davleia">Davleia</a> (Davleia, Mavroneri, <a href="/wiki/Parori,_Boeotia" title="Parori, Boeotia">Parori</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Koroneia,_Boeotia" title="Koroneia, Boeotia">Koroneia</a> (Agia Anna, Agia Triada, Agios Georgios, <a href="/wiki/Alalkomenes,_Boeotia" title="Alalkomenes, Boeotia">Alalkomenes</a>, Koroneia)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kyriaki" title="Kyriaki">Kyriaki</a></li>
<li>Livadeia (Lafystio, Livadeia, Romaiiko)</li>
</ul>
<p>The community Livadeia consists of the following outlying settlements (besides the town itself): Agios Nikolaos Livadeias (pop: 25), Analypsis Livadeias (pop: 184), Elikon (pop: 43), Perachora (pop: 279), Stathmos (pop: 3), Tzimeika (pop: 25) and <a href="/wiki/Tsoukalades,_Boeotia" title="Tsoukalades, Boeotia">Tsoukalades</a> (pop: 149).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Antiquities">Antiquities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Livadeia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Antiquities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pausanias_(geographer)" title="Pausanias (geographer)">Pausanias</a>, writing in the 2nd century AD, reported that the original name of the city was Mideia, and that it took its name Lebadeia from Lebados of Athens, who moved the city from high to low ground,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> to its current location on the banks of the Herkyna river. The sacred protector of the city was the hero/god <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trophonios" title="Trophonios">Trophonios</a>, whose oracle, involving a harrowing descent into an underground chamber, was famous beyond the borders of Greece. At the springs of the Herkyna river are shallow grottos with niches and marble remnants said to be the site of the oracle. On the hill above is a small medieval castle, mostly the work of the <a href="/wiki/Catalan_Company" title="Catalan Company">Catalan Company</a> during the 14th century, accessible by foot. Further west, commanding a dramatic view from the hill of Profitis Ilias, are the remains of a large temple of <a href="/wiki/Zeus" title="Zeus">Zeus</a> <a href="/wiki/Basileus" title="Basileus">Basileus</a>, perhaps begun in the 3rd century BC but never completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The cathedral church of St. George houses an important relic, a head of St. George, translated to <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a> in the 15th century but restored to Livadeia as a gesture of interfaith cooperation in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In medieval times the river was lined by a series of water mills, one of which is preserved.</p>
<p>During the Byzantine times Livadeia, enters a period of decline, only in the 9th century some economic growth was depicted. During the Frankish Livadeia came back on track, then it passes in Catalan sovereignty. The Turkish domination begins in 1458,when economic and administrative privileges granted to residents, contribute to industry and trade.Today the city is the financial, industrial, commercial center of the law.Finally,Livadeia is also developed in craft, folk art and industry. An important source for the economy is also the tourism.</p>
