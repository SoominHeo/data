<p>The <b>Libertador Bolivarian Municipality</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <span lang="es" xml:lang="es"><i>Municipio Bolivariano Libertador</i></span>) is the only <a href="/wiki/Administrative_division" title="Administrative division">administrative division</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venezuelan_Capital_District" title="Venezuelan Capital District">Venezuelan Capital District</a> and along with the municipalities of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baruta_Municipality" title="Baruta Municipality">Baruta</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chacao_Municipality" title="Chacao Municipality">Chacao</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Hatillo_Municipality" title="El Hatillo Municipality">El Hatillo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sucre_Municipality,_Miranda" title="Sucre Municipality, Miranda">Sucre</a> forms the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_District_of_Caracas" title="Metropolitan District of Caracas">Metropolitan District of Caracas</a>. It is also known as <b>West Caracas</b>, because its localization in the western portion of <a href="/wiki/Caracas" title="Caracas">Caracas</a>. It is landlocked by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vargas_State" title="Vargas State">Vargas State</a> and also borders <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miranda_State" title="Miranda State">Miranda State</a> on the east and south. The municipality is one of a number in Venezuela named "<a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Libertador_Municipality_(disambiguation)" title="Libertador Municipality (disambiguation)">Libertador Municipality</a>", in honour of Venezuelan independence hero <a href="/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar" title="Simón Bolívar">Simón Bolívar</a>.</p>
<p>Libertador is one of the smallest municipalities in Venezuela, with a total area of 438 square kilometres (169 sq mi). It is the largest in terms of population, with approximately 2.1 million inhabitants.</p>
<p>In this municipality is home to the <a href="/wiki/Miraflores_Palace" title="Miraflores Palace">presidential palace</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Venezuela" title="National Assembly of Venezuela">federal legislative palace</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Electoral_Council_(Venezuela)" title="National Electoral Council (Venezuela)">national electoral council</a>, the main offices of <a href="/wiki/PDVSA" title="PDVSA">PDVSA</a> and <a href="/wiki/CANTV" title="CANTV">CANTV</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Central_Bank_of_Venezuela" title="Central Bank of Venezuela">Central Bank of Venezuela</a> and the public ministry.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Libertador_Bolivarian_Municipality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It was first established in 1901 under the name of Libertador Department; with the <a href="/wiki/Vargas_(state)#Municipality" title="Vargas (state)">Department of Vargas</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Vargas_(state)#Municipality" title="Vargas (state)">Vargas Municipality</a>, it formed the <i>Federal District of Venezuela</i>.</p>
<p>In 1986, its name was changed to Libertador District. Following the creation of the Vargas State in 1998, it became the sole administrative division of the Federal District.</p>
<p>Under the 1999 constitution, the Federal District ceased to exist and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venezuelan_Capital_District" title="Venezuelan Capital District">Capital District of Venezuela</a> was created. Under the new terms of administrative divisions, Libertador became a municipality, consisting of 22 <a href="/wiki/Parish_(administrative_division)" title="Parish (administrative division)">parishes</a>.</p>
<p>In 2000, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_District_of_Caracas" title="Metropolitan District of Caracas">Metropolitan District of Caracas</a> was formed from Libertador and four other municipalities: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baruta_Municipality" title="Baruta Municipality">Baruta</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chacao_Municipality" title="Chacao Municipality">Chacao</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Hatillo_Municipality" title="El Hatillo Municipality">El Hatillo</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sucre_Municipality,_Miranda" title="Sucre Municipality, Miranda">Sucre</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Libertador_Bolivarian_Municipality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Libertador Municipality is located in the mid-north of the country surrounded by the mountain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cerro_El_Avila" title="Cerro El Avila">El Ávila</a> which is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coastal_Range,_Venezuela" title="Coastal Range, Venezuela">Venezuelan central range</a>. It borders by the north with the Vargas State, by the south and east with the Baruta Municipality of the Miranda State and by the west with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aragua_State" title="Aragua State">Aragua State</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Libertador_Bolivarian_Municipality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The climate is characteristically tropical with the temperature varying according to the altitude with an annual temperature of 24 to −28 °C (75 to −18 °F) being the warmest of the five municipalities that forms Caracas.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Libertador_Bolivarian_Municipality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The vast majority of the population works for the public sector. It is also the most important commercial centre of the country since the Central Bank of Venezuela is located there. Tourism is the next largest economic activity in the municipality as the historical centre of the city is there. Informal commerce is also predominant in the area.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Libertador_Bolivarian_Municipality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Libertador Municipality represents only 0.22% of the countrywide territory but is home to more than 2 million people and represents half of the total population of Caracas and a density of 4,816.36/km2. On the other hand, the entire area is urbanized.</p>
<p>As of 2006, Libertador was estimated to contain the world's second-largest mega-<a href="/wiki/Slum" title="Slum">slum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_and_government">Law and government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Libertador_Bolivarian_Municipality&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Law and government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The Venezuelan constitution specifies that the municipal governments are divided by <a href="/wiki/Executive_(government)" title="Executive (government)">executive</a>; governed by the mayor, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legislative" title="Legislative">legislative</a> branches; governed by the municipal council comprising thirteen members, both offices are elected through universal and secret suffrage.</p>
<p>The Libertador Municipality does not belong to any state therefore it doesn't have a governor but instead a Head of Government of the Capital District which is appointed by the President of the Republic.</p>
<p>Since August 2009 the municipality publishes a newspaper, <i><a href="/wiki/Ciudad_CCS" title="Ciudad CCS">Ciudad CCS</a></i>.</p>
