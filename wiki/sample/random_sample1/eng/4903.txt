<p><b>Rebecca Rittenhouse</b> (born <b>Rebecca Rittenhouse Meaders</b>; November 30, 1988) is an American actress. She is starred as Cody LeFever in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> prime time soap opera, <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_%26_Oil" title="Blood &amp; Oil">Blood &amp; Oil</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_Rittenhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rittenhouse was born in Los Angeles, California. She attended <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">University of Pennsylvania</a> and later studied acting at the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Theater_Company" title="Atlantic Theater Company">Atlantic Theater Company</a> in New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rt_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-rt-1">[1]</a></sup> She made her Off-Broadway debut opposite <a href="/wiki/Blythe_Danner" title="Blythe Danner">Blythe Danner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Jessica_Parker" title="Sarah Jessica Parker">Sarah Jessica Parker</a> in <i>Commons of Pensacola</i> written by <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Peet" title="Amanda Peet">Amanda Peet</a> at <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Theatre_Club" title="Manhattan Theatre Club">Manhattan Theatre Club</a> during 2013–14 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, she made her television debut, appearing in the pilot episode of <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> drama series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Affair_(TV_series)" title="The Affair (TV series)">The Affair</a></i>. Later in that year, Rittenhouse was cast in the series regular role as Brittany Dobler in the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> comedy-drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Band_Society" title="Red Band Society">Red Band Society</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The series was canceled after one season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Rittenhouse was originally considered for the female lead role in the superhero film <i><a href="/wiki/Deadpool_(film)" title="Deadpool (film)">Deadpool</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Schilling" title="Taylor Schilling">Taylor Schilling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Reed" title="Crystal Reed">Crystal Reed</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Morena_Baccarin" title="Morena Baccarin">Morena Baccarin</a>, but lost the part to Baccarin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Rittenhouse was cast in the female lead character in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> prime time soap opera, <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_%26_Oil" title="Blood &amp; Oil">Blood &amp; Oil</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> She plays Cody LeFever, the series' protagonist and <a href="/wiki/Chace_Crawford" title="Chace Crawford">Chace Crawford</a>'s character's wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
