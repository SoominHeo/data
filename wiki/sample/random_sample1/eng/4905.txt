<p><b>Rebecca Jean Fink</b> (<a href="/wiki/Given_name#Name_at_birth" title="Given name">née</a> <b>Smallbone</b>; born 26 July 1977), known professionally as <b>Rebecca Jean</b> or <b>Rebecca St. James</b> is an Australian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_pop_rock" title="Christian pop rock">Christian pop rock</a> singer, songwriter, musician, author, and actress. She began performing in Australia in the late 1980s and released her first <a href="/wiki/Refresh_My_Heart" title="Refresh My Heart">full-length studio album</a> in 1991. She was signed to <a href="/wiki/ForeFront_Records" title="ForeFront Records">ForeFront Records</a> in 1993 and released her major label <a href="/wiki/Rebecca_St._James_(album)" title="Rebecca St. James (album)">debut</a> the following year.</p>
<p>St. James rose to fame in the late 1990s with her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Gold</a> albums <i><a href="/wiki/God_(Rebecca_St._James_album)" title="God (Rebecca St. James album)">God</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Pray_(Rebecca_St._James_album)" title="Pray (Rebecca St. James album)">Pray</a></i>, the latter of which won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> in 2000 for Best Rock Gospel Album. The albums spawned multiple singles, including "<a href="/wiki/God_(Rebecca_St._James_song)" title="God (Rebecca St. James song)">God</a>", "Pray", and "Yes, I Believe in God". Since then she has established herself as one of the most prominent musical artists in <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_Christian_music" title="Contemporary Christian music">Contemporary Christian music</a> (CCM), with four additional full-length studio albums: <i><a href="/wiki/Transform_(Rebecca_St._James_album)" title="Transform (Rebecca St. James album)">Transform</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Worship_God" title="Worship God">Worship God</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/If_I_Had_One_Chance_to_Tell_You_Something" title="If I Had One Chance to Tell You Something">If I Had One Chance to Tell You Something</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/I_Will_Praise_You" title="I Will Praise You">I Will Praise You</a></i>. Staple songs such as "<a href="/wiki/Wait_for_Me_(Rebecca_St._James_song)" title="Wait for Me (Rebecca St. James song)">Wait for Me</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reborn_(Rebecca_St._James_song)" title="Reborn (Rebecca St. James song)">Reborn</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Wait_for_Me/Song_of_Love" title="Wait for Me/Song of Love">Song of Love</a>", "I Thank You", "<a href="/wiki/Alive_(Rebecca_St._James_song)" title="Alive (Rebecca St. James song)">Alive</a>", and "Shine Your Glory Down", have all been derived from these releases. She has sold two million albums since starting her career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emicmgdistribution.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-emicmgdistribution.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>St. James is also an accomplished author and actress. To date, she has released nine published books and starred in five films, a <a href="/wiki/!Hero" title="!Hero">musical stage show</a>, and a <i><a href="/wiki/VeggieTales" title="VeggieTales">VeggieTales</a></i> episode ("<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/An_Easter_Carol" title="An Easter Carol">An Easter Carol</a>"). Her ninth book, <i>What Is He Thinking?</i>, was released on 26 September 2011. She has starred in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Unidentified" title="Unidentified">Unidentified</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sarah%27s_Choice" title="Sarah's Choice">Sarah's Choice</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rising_Stars_(film)" title="Rising Stars (film)">Rising Stars</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Frontier_Boys" title="The Frontier Boys">The Frontier Boys</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Suing_the_Devil" title="Suing the Devil">Suing the Devil</a></i>, <i>A Strange Brand of Happy</i>, and <i>Faith of Our Fathers</i>. She is also an outspoken <a href="/wiki/Sexual_abstinence" title="Sexual abstinence">sexual abstinence</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pro-life" title="Pro-life">pro-life</a> advocate, a spokesperson for <a href="/wiki/Compassion_International" title="Compassion International">Compassion International</a>, the sister of Joel and Luke Smallbone, who comprise the band <a href="/wiki/For_King_%26_Country_(band)" title="For King &amp; Country (band)">for King &amp; Country</a>, and the wife of <a href="/wiki/Foster_the_People" title="Foster the People">Foster the People</a>'s former bassist Jacob "Cubbie" Fink.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_St._James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rebecca St. James was born Rebecca Jean Smallbone on 26 July 1977 in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, Australia to parents David and Helen Smallbone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jfh_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-jfh-1">[1]</a></sup> She moved to <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>, after her father's job relocation, in 1991, where she was raised with a sister, Libby, and five brothers, Ben, Dan, Joel, Josh, and Luke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_St._James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_beginnings_.281990.E2.80.931995.29">Musical beginnings (1990–1995)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_St._James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Musical beginnings (1990–1995)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1990, at twelve years of age, St. James opened shows for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Contemporary_Music" title="Christian Contemporary Music">CCM</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Carman_(singer)" title="Carman (singer)">Carman</a> during his Australian tour. The following year she released an <a href="/wiki/Independent_music" title="Independent music">independent</a> album, <i><a href="/wiki/Refresh_My_Heart" title="Refresh My Heart">Refresh My Heart</a></i>, in Australia under the stage name "Rebecca Jean".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cm.com_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-cm.com-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Soon after its release, her family moved to the United States where her father received a job offer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cm.com_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-cm.com-8">[8]</a></sup> She signed with <a href="/wiki/ForeFront_Records" title="ForeFront Records">ForeFront Records</a> and took her stage name at the label's request. In 1994 she released her major label debut titled <i><a href="/wiki/Rebecca_St._James_(album)" title="Rebecca St. James (album)">Rebecca St. James</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She also released an EP titled <i><a href="/wiki/Rebecca_St._James:_Extended_Play_Remixes" title="Rebecca St. James: Extended Play Remixes">Rebecca St. James: Extended Play Remixes</a></i> in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="God_and_Pray_.281996.E2.80.931999.29"><i>God</i> and <i>Pray</i> (1996–1999)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_St._James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: God and Pray (1996–1999)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 June 1996, St. James released her second major album <i><a href="/wiki/God_(Rebecca_St._James_album)" title="God (Rebecca St. James album)">God</a></i>, led by the <a href="/wiki/God_(Rebecca_St._James_song)" title="God (Rebecca St. James song)">title track</a>. The album took her music in a new direction, focusing more on rock. It debuted to positive reviews <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AM197_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-AM197-12">[12]</a></sup> and debuted at 200, and peaked at 168 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It also charted at No. 10 on the <i>Billboard</i> Heatseekers chart and No. 6 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Christian_Adult_Contemporary#Christian" title="Hot Christian Adult Contemporary"><i>Billboard'</i>s Contemporary Christian chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AM197_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-AM197-12">[12]</a></sup> In 1997, she was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Rock_Gospel_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Rock Gospel Album">Grammy Award for Best Rock Gospel Album</a> for <i>God</i> and in 2005 the album was <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">RIAA</a> <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">certified gold</a> for selling over 500,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaa.com_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-riaa.com-14">[14]</a></sup> To promote the album, St. James released a devotion book titled <i>40 Days with God: A Devotional Journey</i> in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1997, St. James released the sequel to her devotional book, titled <i><a href="/wiki/You%27re_the_Voice:_40_More_Days_with_God" title="You're the Voice: 40 More Days with God">You're the Voice: 40 More Days with God</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On 7 October of the same year, she released her first holiday album, simply titled <i><a href="/wiki/Christmas_(Rebecca_St._James_album)" title="Christmas (Rebecca St. James album)">Christmas</a></i>. The album charted at No. 12 on <i>Billboard'</i>s <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" title="Top Heatseekers">Top Heatseekers</a> chart and No. 14 on the Top Contemporary Christian chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AM095_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-AM095-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 20 October 1998 St. James released her third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Pray_(Rebecca_St._James_album)" title="Pray (Rebecca St. James album)">Pray</a></i>, which debuted to mixed reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The album managed to chart at No. 168 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, and No. 5 on both the Heatseekers Chart and the Contemporary Christian Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-18">[18]</a></sup> The album won a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy" title="Grammy">Grammy</a> in 1999 for Best Rock/Gospel Album,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-18">[18]</a></sup> and in 2006 it was RIAA certified gold for selling over 500,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaa.com_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-riaa.com-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1999 St. James released a song titled "<a href="/wiki/Pray_(Rebecca_St._James_album)#No_Secrets_VHS.2F.22Yes.2C_I_Believe_In_God.22_Single" title="Pray (Rebecca St. James album)">Yes, I Believe In God</a>" to radio only, in memory of the lives lost at the <a href="/wiki/Columbine_High_School_massacre" title="Columbine High School massacre">Columbine shooting</a>. The song was later released on the album <i><a href="/wiki/Wait_for_Me:_The_Best_from_Rebecca_St._James" title="Wait for Me: The Best from Rebecca St. James">Wait for Me: The Best from Rebecca St. James</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Also in 1999, St. James released a video on VHS titled <i><a href="/wiki/Pray_(Rebecca_St._James_album)#No_Secrets_VHS.2F.22Yes.2C_I_Believe_In_God.22_Single" title="Pray (Rebecca St. James album)">No Secrets</a></i> featuring interviews of her and her family, behind-the-scenes footage and the music video for the song "Pray".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-21">[21]</a></sup> In addition to her own projects, St. James took part in a CD release titled <i>Heaven &amp; Earth: A Tapestry of Worship</i>, which featured female Christian artists such as <a href="/wiki/Nichole_Nordeman" title="Nichole Nordeman">Nichole Nordeman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Knapp" title="Jennifer Knapp">Jennifer Knapp</a>. The album was released in November 1999 and features two songs by St. James; "As We Wait" and "River of Life".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transform.2C_Worship_God_and_Wait_for_Me:_The_Best_from_Rebecca_St._James_.282000.E2.80.932004.29"><i>Transform</i>, <i>Worship God</i> and <i>Wait for Me: The Best from Rebecca St. James</i> (2000–2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_St._James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transform, Worship God and Wait for Me: The Best from Rebecca St. James (2000–2004)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 24 October 2000, St. James released a brand new album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Transform_(Rebecca_St._James_album)" title="Transform (Rebecca St. James album)">Transform</a></i>. The album charted at No. 166 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, No. 7 on the Heatseekers Chart and No. 14 on the Contemporary Christian Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-23">[23]</a></sup> The album garnered positive reviews <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and featured the songs "<a href="/wiki/Wait_for_Me_(Rebecca_St._James_song)" title="Wait for Me (Rebecca St. James song)">Wait for Me</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reborn_(Rebecca_St._James_song)" title="Reborn (Rebecca St. James song)">Reborn</a>". Also in 2000, St. James made a <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo</a> in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Left_Behind:_The_Movie" title="Left Behind: The Movie">Left Behind: The Movie</a></i>. A year later, the <a href="/wiki/Bible_study_(Christian)" title="Bible study (Christian)">devotional book</a>, <i>40 Days with God</i> was re-released with a new layout and five new devotions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002, to promote the single "<a href="/wiki/Wait_for_Me_(Rebecca_St._James_song)" title="Wait for Me (Rebecca St. James song)">Wait For Me</a>" from <i>Transform</i>, St. James released the book <i><a href="/wiki/Wait_for_Me:_Rediscovering_the_Joy_of_Purity_in_Romance" title="Wait for Me: Rediscovering the Joy of Purity in Romance">Wait for Me: Rediscovering the Joy of Purity in Romance</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> which went on to sell over 100,000 copies and spawn a <a href="/wiki/Journal" title="Journal">journal</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Study_guide" title="Study guide">study guide</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The song and book promotes <a href="/wiki/Sexual_abstinence" title="Sexual abstinence">sexual abstinence</a> before marriage, and St. James has since become a major spokesperson for the subject.</p>
<p>On 26 February 2002, St. James released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Worship_God" title="Worship God">Worship God</a></i>. The album debuted to extremely positive reviews <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceC_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceC-30">[30]</a></sup> and charted at No. 94 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, marking St. James' first Top 100 album, and No. 5 on the Contemporary Christian chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceC_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceC-30">[30]</a></sup> She released a DVD to promote the album 19 November 2002 that featured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_videos" title="Music videos">music videos</a>, interviews, etc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 March 2003, after 10 years with <a href="/wiki/ForeFront_Records" title="ForeFront Records">ForeFront Records</a>, St. James released her first compilation project, <i><a href="/wiki/Wait_for_Me:_The_Best_from_Rebecca_St._James" title="Wait for Me: The Best from Rebecca St. James">Wait for Me: The Best from Rebecca St. James</a></i>, which features 16 of her most popular songs and two new ones,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-32">[32]</a></sup> including "I Thank You" which managed to peak at No. 2 on <i>Billboard'</i>s Hot Christian Songs chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The album failed to make the <i>Billboard</i> 200, but charted at No. 16 on the Contemporary Christian chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 February 2004, St. James released her very first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live_album" title="Live album">live album</a> titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live_Worship:_Blessed_Be_Your_Name" title="Live Worship: Blessed Be Your Name">Live Worship: Blessed Be Your Name</a></i> which features 7 new songs and 2 studio recorded songs. The album charted at No. 187 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 and No. 12 on the Top Christian Albums chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Later that year, St. James released a compilation album titled <i>The Best of Rebecca St. James</i>, and her book <i>SHE: Safe, Healthy, Empowered: The Woman You're Made to Be</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Also in 2004, St. James starred in the stage musical <i><a href="/wiki/!Hero" title="!Hero">!Hero</a></i> as a modern day <a href="/wiki/Mary_Magdalene" title="Mary Magdalene">Mary Magdalene</a> aka "Maggie".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> St. James later lent her voice to the <a href="/wiki/VeggieTales" title="VeggieTales">VeggieTales</a> episode <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/An_Easter_Carol" title="An Easter Carol">An Easter Carol</a></i> as Hope the Music Box Angel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-21">[21]</a></sup> She also took part in a pop/rock <a href="/wiki/VeggieTales" title="VeggieTales">VeggieTales</a> album titled <i>Veggie Rocks!</i>. She covered "The VeggieTales Theme Song" for the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="If_I_Had_One_Chance_to_Tell_You_Something.2C_film_debut.2C_and_aLIVE_in_Florida_.282005.E2.80.932007.29"><i>If I Had One Chance to Tell You Something</i>, film debut, and <i>aLIVE in Florida</i> (2005–2007)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_St._James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: If I Had One Chance to Tell You Something, film debut, and aLIVE in Florida (2005–2007)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>After taking a <a href="/wiki/Recess_(break)" title="Recess (break)">hiatus</a> from recording music, St. James returned to the <a href="/wiki/Recording_studio" title="Recording studio">studio</a> in early 2005 to record new songs. On 24 October 2005, the first single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Alive_(Rebecca_St._James_song)" title="Alive (Rebecca St. James song)">Alive</a>", was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The song managed to chart at No. 3 on R&amp;R's CHR Chart <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emicmgdistribution.com_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-emicmgdistribution.com-3">[3]</a></sup> and No. 13 on <i>Billboard</i>s Hot Christian Songs Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> The new album, titled <i>If I Had One Chance To Tell You Something</i> was released on 22 November 2005. The album debuted to fairly positive reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-41">[41]</a></sup> It charted at No. 14 on <i>Billboard'</i>s Top Christian Albums Chart, but failed to make the <i>Billboard</i> 200.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 July 2005 St. James released a Teen Edition of her book, <i>SHE</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> and on 1 October 2005, she released another book titled <i>Sister Freaks: Stories of Women Who Gave Up Everything For God</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2006, St. James embarked on her <i>If I Had One Chance To Tell You Something Tour</i> with fellow Christian group <a href="/wiki/BarlowGirl" title="BarlowGirl">BarlowGirl</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> She also recorded the theme song for the <a href="/wiki/National_Day_of_Prayer" title="National Day of Prayer">National Day of Prayer</a>. The song was titled "America" and was released to <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> on 2 May 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> She also recorded a cover of <a href="/wiki/Chris_Tomlin" title="Chris Tomlin">Chris Tomlin</a>'s song "Forever" for the album <i><a href="/wiki/WOW_Worship:_Aqua" title="WOW Worship: Aqua">WOW Worship: Aqua</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> The same year, ForeFront Records put together a compilation album titled <i>The Early Years</i>, that covered ten songs from her earliest releases: <i>Rebecca St. James</i>, <i>GOD</i> and <i>Pray</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Aside from music, St. James made her major character film debut in <i>Unidentified</i> as Colleen in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, ForeFront Records took live footage and recordings from the <i>If I Had One Chance... Tour</i> and released a CD/DVD collection on 20 March titled <i><a href="/wiki/ALIVE_in_Florida" title="ALIVE in Florida">aLIVE in Florida</a></i>. The album features 14 live songs and an exclusive remix of "You Are Loved".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-49">[49]</a></sup> The album charted at No. 43 on <i>Billboard'</i>s Top Christian Albums Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-49">[49]</a></sup> At the time of the album's release, it was announced that St. James has sold over 1.8 million albums to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emicmgdistribution.com_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-emicmgdistribution.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_hiatus_and_film_career_launch_.282008.E2.80.932010.29">Musical hiatus and film career launch (2008–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_St._James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Musical hiatus and film career launch (2008–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the midst of a musical hiatus, ForeFront Records put together a two-disc compilation album titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Collection_(Rebecca_St._James_album)" title="The Ultimate Collection (Rebecca St. James album)">The Ultimate Collection</a></i> which was released 11 March 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Another compilation titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Rebecca_St._James_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Rebecca St. James album)">Greatest Hits</a></i> was released later that year on 28 October 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> On 3 September 2008 St. James released another book titled <i>Pure: A 90-Day Devotional for the Mind, Body, &amp; Spirit</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 2008, St. James announced she would star as the lead role in a new film titled <i><a href="/wiki/Sarah%27s_Choice" title="Sarah's Choice">Sarah's Choice</a></i>, which was released 17 November 2009 to DVD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> The film also features a song by St. James titled "Little One".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> The song was released almost two years later on 2 September 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-littleonesingle_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-littleonesingle-55">[55]</a></sup> The film received good reviews from Christian movie critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although on musical hiatus, on 16 April 2009 St. James released a new song titled "You're Alive" to iTunes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> as part of an album titled <i>Resurrection Worship: Songs of Hope</i>. Then, in June 2009, she released another new song titled "Wish" to her MySpace page.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Aside from these brief musical endeavors, St. James' book <i>Loved: Stories of Forgiveness</i> was released on 1 September 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 August 2009, Christian Cinema reported that St. James had wrapped up filming a new movie titled <i><a href="/wiki/Rising_Stars_(film)" title="Rising Stars (film)">Rising Stars</a></i>, which was released on 22 October 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> On 28 April 2010, it was announced that St. James is working on another film titled <i>Frontier Boys</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> and a book titled <i>What Is He Thinking?</i>, both to be released in 2011.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The text near this tag is dated.">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> It was also later announced that she will star in a film titled <i><a href="/wiki/Suing_the_Devil" title="Suing the Devil">Suing the Devil</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> which was released in August 2011.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Precise_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The text near this tag is dated.">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="I_Will_Praise_You_and_film_career_.282011.E2.80.932012.29"><i>I Will Praise You</i> and film career (2011–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_St._James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: I Will Praise You and film career (2011–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 19 October 2010 St. James released her version of "<a href="/wiki/O_Little_Town_of_Bethlehem" title="O Little Town of Bethlehem">O Little Town of Bethlehem</a>" on the album <i>The Essential Christmas Collection</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> On 18 November 2010, St. James announced that she had parted ways with <a href="/wiki/ForeFront_Records" title="ForeFront Records">ForeFront Records</a> and would be releasing a new worship album in April 2011 via <a href="/wiki/Beach_Street_Records" title="Beach Street Records">Beach Street</a>/<a href="/wiki/Reunion_Records" title="Reunion Records">Reunion Records</a>. Her ninth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Will_Praise_You" title="I Will Praise You">I Will Praise You</a></i>, was released on 5 April 2011. It was preceded by the single "Shine Your Glory Down", which was released to Christian radio on 11 February. The album was met with positive reviews from Christian music critics and was highly successful, debuting at No. 18 on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>'</i>s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Christian_Albums" title="Hot Christian Albums">Hot Christian Albums</a> chart and later peaking at No. 9. It also peaked at No. 153 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, her highest charting effort after <i>Worship God</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-billboard_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-66">[66]</a></sup> Her ninth book, <i>What Is He Thinking?</i>, hit shelves on 26 September 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forefrontrecords1_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-forefrontrecords1-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 16 June 2011 it was announced that St. James would be starring in a new romantic comedy film titled <i>A Strange Brand of Happy</i>, which was released on 13 September 2013. The film revolves around a single Christian life coach Joyce who falls for an agnostic client. The movie began filming on 15 August 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rebelpilgrim_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-rebelpilgrim-69">[69]</a></sup> and was released on 13 September 2013.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_novel_and_music_retirement_.282013-present.29">First novel and music retirement (2013-present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_St._James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: First novel and music retirement (2013-present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 12 March 2013 St. James announced via Facebook that was currently publishing her first Christian novel titled <i>The Merciful Scar</i>, co-authored with Nancy Rue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSJFB_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-RSJFB-70">[70]</a></sup> It was released on 10 September 2013. She also narrated the documentary <i>Mother India: Life Through the Eyes of the Orphan</i>, which was released on 23 April 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-motherindia_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-motherindia-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<p>ForeFront Records released a new compilation album on 7 January 2014 titled <i>Icon</i> (titled <i>Best of Rebecca St. James</i> in the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> store).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> Her second novel, <i>Sarah's Choice</i>, an adaptation of the 2009 film of the same name in which St. James starred, was released on 27 May 2014, while her third novel, <i>One Last Thing</i>, is due on 10 March 2015. Both books are co-authored by Nancy Rue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Faith of Our Fathers</i>, released in July 2015, features St. James portraying a car-stealing Australian hitchhiker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_St._James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>St. James is originally from Australia and moved with her family to the United States at age 14. She currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> Two of her brothers, Joel and Luke Smallbone, formed the band <a href="/wiki/For_King_%26_Country_(band)" title="For King &amp; Country (band)">For King &amp; Country</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Air_1Brothers_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-Air_1Brothers-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 3 January 2011 she announced her engagement to <a href="/wiki/Foster_the_People" title="Foster the People">Foster the People</a>'s former bassist Jacob "Cubbie" Fink, a Colorado native and a missionary to South Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> Fink proposed on Christmas Day 2010 at St. James' family's farm in <a href="/wiki/Franklin,_Tennessee" title="Franklin, Tennessee">Franklin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forefrontrecords1_67-1"><a href="#cite_note-forefrontrecords1-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> They married on 23 April 2011 at the Junípero Serra Museum in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marriagepic_80-0"><a href="#cite_note-marriagepic-80">[80]</a></sup> On 4 October 2013, St. James announced that they were expecting their first child<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> and on 18 February 2014, she gave birth to their daughter Gemma Elena Fink.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></p>
