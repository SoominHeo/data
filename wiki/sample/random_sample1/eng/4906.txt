<p><b>Rebecca Pidgeon</b> (born October 10, 1965; some sources cite October 25, 1965 as her date of birth)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a British-American actress and singer-songwriter. She has maintained a recording career while also acting on stage and in feature films. She is married to American writer and director <a href="/wiki/David_Mamet" title="David Mamet">David Mamet</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_Pidgeon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pidgeon was born to <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> parents in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge, Massachusetts</a>, while her father, Carl R. Pidgeon, was a visiting professor at <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">MIT</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyts_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyts-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her mother, Elaine, is a yoga teacher. Her paternal grandmother, <a href="/wiki/Monica_Pidgeon" title="Monica Pidgeon">Monica Pidgeon</a>, the editor of <i><a href="/wiki/Architectural_Design" title="Architectural Design">Architectural Design</a></i>, was the sister of artist <a href="/wiki/Olga_Lehmann" title="Olga Lehmann">Olga Lehmann</a> and academic <a href="/wiki/Andrew_George_Lehmann" title="Andrew George Lehmann">Andrew George Lehmann</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She moved to <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> in 1970, with her parents. She holds dual <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a>/<a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Multiple_citizenship" title="Multiple citizenship">citizenship</a>. She graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Dramatic_Art" title="Royal Academy of Dramatic Art">Royal Academy of Dramatic Art</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Clive_Owen" title="Clive Owen">Clive Owen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liza_Tarbuck" title="Liza Tarbuck">Liza Tarbuck</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_Pidgeon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1986-90, Pidgeon was the lead singer of the British folk/pop band <a href="/wiki/Ruby_Blue" title="Ruby Blue">Ruby Blue</a>. She left Ruby Blue shortly after the band signed to a major record label. She appeared in her first feature film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dawning" title="The Dawning">The Dawning</a>,</i> in 1988 and then starred in <a href="/wiki/David_Mamet" title="David Mamet">David Mamet</a>'s plays and films, beginning with the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Homicide_(1991_film)" title="Homicide (1991 film)">Homicide</a></i> and the play <i><a href="/wiki/Oleanna_(play)" title="Oleanna (play)">Oleanna</a></i>, a part Mamet wrote for her. Pidgeon composed the music for the film version, which starred Debra Eisenstadt in her role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Returning to music, she released the album <i>The Raven</i> (1994), followed by <i>The New York Girls' Club</i> (1996), and <i>The Four Marys</i> (1998), a collection of traditional <a href="/wiki/Celtic_music" title="Celtic music">Celtic</a> folk songs. <i>Tough on Crime</i> (2005) featured <a href="/wiki/Walter_Becker" title="Walter Becker">Walter Becker</a> of <a href="/wiki/Steely_Dan" title="Steely Dan">Steely Dan</a> on guitar and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Preston" title="Billy Preston">Billy Preston</a> on keyboards. <i><a href="/wiki/Behind_the_Velvet_Curtain:_Songs_from_the_Motion_Picture_Redbelt" title="Behind the Velvet Curtain: Songs from the Motion Picture Redbelt">Behind the Velvet Curtain: Songs from the Motion Picture Redbelt</a></i> (2008) included a cover version of the <a href="/wiki/The_Beach_Boys" title="The Beach Boys">Beach Boys</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Wouldn%27t_It_Be_Nice" title="Wouldn't It Be Nice">Wouldn't It Be Nice</a>?" and a duet with <a href="/wiki/Luciana_Souza" title="Luciana Souza">Luciana Souza</a>. <i>Slingshot</i> was released in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pidgeon has had roles in several of Mamet's films, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Spanish_Prisoner" title="The Spanish Prisoner">The Spanish Prisoner</a></i> (1997), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Winslow_Boy_(1999_film)" title="The Winslow Boy (1999 film)">The Winslow Boy</a></i> (1999), <i><a href="/wiki/State_and_Main" title="State and Main">State and Main</a></i> (2000), <i><a href="/wiki/Heist_(2001_film)" title="Heist (2001 film)">Heist</a></i> (2001), and <i><a href="/wiki/Red_(2010_film)" title="Red (2010 film)">Red</a></i> (2010). She had a small role in <i><a href="/wiki/Redbelt" title="Redbelt">Redbelt</a></i> (2008), for which she performed the music. In the 2013 television movie <i><a href="/wiki/Phil_Spector_(film)" title="Phil Spector (film)">Phil Spector</a></i> she sang "<a href="/wiki/Spanish_Harlem_(song)" title="Spanish Harlem (song)">Spanish Harlem</a>" over the closing credits. She appeared in the U.S. television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Unit" title="The Unit">The Unit</a></i>, playing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlotte_Ryan" title="Charlotte Ryan">Charlotte Ryan</a>, and in the 2007 television film <i><a href="/wiki/Jesse_Stone:_Sea_Change" title="Jesse Stone: Sea Change">Jesse Stone: Sea Change</a>,</i> playing Leeann Lewis, a murder-bank robbery suspect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebecca_Pidgeon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pidgeon is married to the American writer and director <a href="/wiki/David_Mamet" title="David Mamet">David Mamet</a>. She met Mamet while acting in his play <i><a href="/wiki/Speed-the-Plow" title="Speed-the-Plow">Speed-the-Plow</a></i> during its run at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">National Theatre, London</a>. Although married at the time to actress <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Crouse" title="Lindsay Crouse">Lindsay Crouse</a>, Mamet began a relationship with Pidgeon. Mamet divorced Crouse in 1990 and married Pidgeon in 1991.</p>
<p>Pidgeon and Mamet have two children, actress <a href="/wiki/Clara_Mamet" title="Clara Mamet">Clara</a> and Noah. Pidgeon, who was born to a non-practicing <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> family, has <a href="/wiki/Conversion_to_Judaism" title="Conversion to Judaism">converted</a> to Mamet's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judaism_in_the_United_States" title="Judaism in the United States">Jewish</a> faith.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
