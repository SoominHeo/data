<p><br/>
The <b>North Fire</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a> that occurred in the <a href="/wiki/Mojave_Desert" title="Mojave Desert">Mojave Desert</a> near the towns of <a href="/wiki/Victorville,_California" title="Victorville, California">Victorville</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hesperia,_California" title="Hesperia, California">Hesperia</a>, north of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Bernardino" title="San Bernardino">San Bernardino</a> and south of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bakersfield" title="Bakersfield">Bakersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. The fire began on July 17, 2015. The areas most impacted were adjacent to <a href="/wiki/Interstate_15_in_California" title="Interstate 15 in California">Interstate 15</a>, where the <a href="/wiki/Cajon_Pass" title="Cajon Pass">Cajon Pass</a> passes through the <a href="/wiki/San_Bernardino_National_Forest" title="San Bernardino National Forest">San Bernardino National Forest</a>. The fire spread to 4,250 acres, and burned homes and other buildings, as well as numerous vehicles stranded on the interstate. Seventy-four passenger vehicles and trucks were burned along the highway or in neighboring communities due to the fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contained_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-contained-3">[3]</a></sup> The fire closed <a href="/wiki/Interstate_15_in_California" title="Interstate 15 in California">Interstate 15</a>, the main highway connecting <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas, Nevada</a>, during the first day of the blaze.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_New_York_Times_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_New_York_Times-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>One-thousand fire fighters battled the blaze during the height of the fire, which as of the evening of July 17 was five percent contained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Mandatory evacuations were ordered for the towns of <a href="/wiki/Phelan,_California" title="Phelan, California">Phelan</a> and Baldy Mesa, which were threatened or impacted by the fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Two-hundred-four people utilized an emergency shelter set up at <a href="/wiki/Serrano_High_School" title="Serrano High School">Serrano High School</a>, in Phelan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Damage_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Damage-1">[1]</a></sup> The evacuation orders were lifted as crews worked to contain the fire. On the evening of July 18, the fire was reported to be 45% contained, after a rain storm from the remnant moisture of <a href="/wiki/2015_Pacific_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Dolores" title="2015 Pacific hurricane season">Hurricane Dolores</a> brought cooler temperatures to the <a href="/wiki/Chaparral" title="Chaparral">chaparral</a> fueled fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> By the following morning, the fire was reported to be 60% contained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KESQ_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-KESQ-8">[8]</a></sup> On Monday, July 20, the fire was reported to be 75% contained, but had also grown from 3,500 to 4,250 acres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The fire was fully contained on July 21, and fire fighters remained on scene to douse hot spots in the burn area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-contained_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-contained-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The presence of <a href="/wiki/Unmanned_aerial_vehicle" title="Unmanned aerial vehicle">unmanned aerial vehicles</a> (UAVs/drones) in the area that interfered with fire fighting efforts has renewed discussion of legislation to limit the operation of drones in the United States. In response to the drone activity over active wildfires, San Bernardino County, the <a href="/wiki/California_State_Legislature" title="California State Legislature">California State Legislature</a> and the Congress of the United States have all proposed restrictions on privately owned drones.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fire_fighting_complications">Fire fighting complications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Fire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fire fighting complications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Efforts at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_suppression" title="Fire suppression">fire suppression</a> were complicated by the presence of <a href="/wiki/Unmanned_aerial_vehicle" title="Unmanned aerial vehicle">drones</a> (UAVs) in the air space being used by fire-fighting helicopters and airplanes, and by a shooting at the command center.</p>
<p>On Sunday, July 19, aerial suppression of the fire was temporarily halted by the presence of drones flying in the vicinity. Three of the five drones detected in the area left the scene, but the presence of two others interfered with the fire fighting operation. After a delay, the fire fighting operation resumed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On Monday, July 20, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Forest_Service" title="US Forest Service">US Forest Service</a> officer fired his weapon outside the headquarters of the fire fighting effort, located at the fair grounds in Victorville. A man allegedly became "uncooperative" and tried to flee, dragging the officer with his car. The officer fired several rounds into the vehicle, but there were no injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Fire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Representative" title="United States Representative">United States Representative</a> <a href="/wiki/Paul_Cook_(politician)" title="Paul Cook (politician)">Paul Cook</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a>-<a href="/wiki/Yucca_Valley,_California" title="Yucca Valley, California">Yucca Valley</a>, introduced legislation known as the Wildfire Airspace Protection Act of 2015, (H.R. 3025) one week prior to the North Fire which would make it a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_offense" title="Federal offense">federal offense</a> to fly a UAV in a manner that interferes with fire fighters in the vicinity of a wildfire on federal lands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The legislation has garnered renewed interest in the aftermath of the North Fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drones_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-drones-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The San Bernardino County Board of Supervisors discussed UAV activity in the county at its meeting on July 28, 2015. Prior to the meeting, Board Chairman James Ramos stated, regarding UAVs in the area of fire fighting operations, “When you are inhibiting the response of the first responders, then you infringe on the safety of the residents of San Bernardino County.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drones_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-drones-13">[13]</a></sup> At the meeting, the county approved monetary rewards of US$25,000 for each incident for information leading to the arrest of individuals who piloted UAVs over recent wildfires in the county.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-legislate_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-legislate-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Both houses of the California State Legislature have either introduced legislation or otherwise acted regarding UAVs operating over state wildfires. Senate Bill 168 would give immunity to first responders who damage or destroy privately owned UAVs operating over wildfires. And some members of the California House of Representatives are urging the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">Federal Aviation Administration</a> to consider <a href="/wiki/Geo-fence" title="Geo-fence">geo fencing</a> of commercially available UAVs, which could be used by authorities to keep them out of restricted areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-legislate_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-legislate-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October, 2015, the US <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">Federal Aviation Administration</a> announced that UAVs flying within the United States must register their aircraft with the federal government. This new policy will apply to both new vehicles, as well as UAVs already owned and operated. The new policy imposes penalties on UAV owners who do not comply.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cause">Cause</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_Fire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cause">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Authorities have not determined the cause of the fire. The first call firefighters received was of a vehicle in flames on Interstate 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KESQ_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-KESQ-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
