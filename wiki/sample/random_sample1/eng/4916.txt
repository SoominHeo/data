<p><b>Lisa Nilsson</b> (born August 13, 1970, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tyres%C3%B6" title="Tyresö">Tyresö</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a>. She was discovered by the producer William (Billy) Butt and she is perhaps best known in Sweden for her 1992 hit, <i><a href="/wiki/Himlen_runt_h%C3%B6rnet" title="Himlen runt hörnet">Himlen runt hörnet</a></i>, written by <a href="/wiki/Mauro_Scocco" title="Mauro Scocco">Mauro Scocco</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johan_Ekelund" title="Johan Ekelund">Johan Ekelund</a>. It was released in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> in 1995, titled <i>Ticket to Heaven</i>. She has released four subsequent albums, <i>Till Morelia</i> in 1995, <i>Viva</i> in 2000, <i>Små rum</i> in 2002 and <i>Hotel Vermont 609</i> in 2006, all in <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>. A greatest hits compilation, <i>Samlade sånger</i> followed in 2003 and contained two new songs. Prior to <i>Himlen runt hörnet</i> she had released two English language albums, <i>Lean On Love</i> in 1989 and <i>Indestructible</i> in 1991, both released by William Butt. She also participated in <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen_1989" title="Melodifestivalen 1989">Melodifestivalen 1989</a>.</p>
<p>Nilsson's style ranges from out and out pop music to soul-inflected ballads and dance tracks on her earlier albums to a more mature jazz feel in her more recent work, perhaps reflecting familial connections as her father Gösta Nilsson is a jazz pianist.</p>
<p>In 2002 she moved into acting with appearances in the Swedish TV sitcom, <i>Cleo</i> and a role in the Swedish film <i>Paradiset</i> made by British-born director <a href="/wiki/Colin_Nutley" title="Colin Nutley">Colin Nutley</a>.</p>
<p>In February 2007 she gave birth to a daughter.</p>
