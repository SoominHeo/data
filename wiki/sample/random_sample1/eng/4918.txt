<p><b>Lisa Lois</b> (born 22 June 1987 as <b>Lisa Hordijk</b>) is a Dutch singer who rose to prominence after winning the second season of talent show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/X_Factor_(Netherlands)" title="X Factor (Netherlands)">X Factor</a></i>. In 2009, she changed her stage name to Lisa Lois<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and released her debut album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smoke_(Lisa_Hordijk_album)" title="Smoke (Lisa Hordijk album)">Smoke</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Hordijk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lois was born in <a href="/wiki/Wageningen" title="Wageningen">Wageningen</a>, Netherlands. She studied at the Rock Academy in <a href="/wiki/Tilburg" title="Tilburg">Tilburg</a> and at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Utrecht_(city)" title="Utrecht (city)">Utrecht</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Herman_Brood" title="Herman Brood">Herman Brood</a> Academy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2009_-_Present">2009 - Present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Hordijk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2009 - Present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After winning the X-Factor, she released her debut single, a cover of <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Cohen" title="Leonard Cohen">Leonard Cohen</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Hallelujah_(Leonard_Cohen_song)" title="Hallelujah (Leonard Cohen song)">Hallelujah</a>" as 'Lisa'. The single went straight to number one in the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Top_40" title="Dutch Top 40">Dutch Top 40</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> "Hallelujah" went <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">double platinum</a> within one month, selling over 100,000 copies as of today and becoming the best-selling single of 2009 in the Netherlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Her second single "No Good for Me" written by <a href="/wiki/Pixie_Lott" title="Pixie Lott">Pixie Lott</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phil_Thornalley" title="Phil Thornalley">Phil Thornalley</a> was released on 13 November 2009. Two weeks later her debut album <i>Smoke</i> was released. Lisa's third single from the album is "Promises, Promises" written by Pixie Lott, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Sutton" title="Martin Sutton">Martin Sutton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Neill" title="Chris Neill">Chris Neill</a>.</p>
<p>In August 2012 Lisa announced by Facebook and Twitter she will join Allen Stone with his European tour.</p>
<p>Lisa planned her second studio album in winter 2012. The first single from this album was released in the beginning of January 2013. In short time it reached number 4 in the Dutch iTunes Charts.</p>
