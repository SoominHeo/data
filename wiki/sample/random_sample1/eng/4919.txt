<p><b>Lisa Michelle Bonet</b> (born November 16, 1967)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Westbrook_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Westbrook-2">[2]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a> actress. She is best known for her role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denise_Huxtable%E2%80%93Kendall" title="Denise Huxtable–Kendall">Denise Huxtable–Kendall</a> on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> sitcom, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cosby_Show" title="The Cosby Show">The Cosby Show</a></i> (1984–92) and originally starring in its <a href="/wiki/Spin-off_(media)" title="Spin-off (media)">spinoff</a> comedy, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Different_World" title="A Different World">A Different World</a></i> for its first season (1987–88).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Bonet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bonet was born in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco, California</a>.<sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_12"><a class="external autonumber" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lisa_Bonet#endnote_12">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African-American" title="African-American">African-American</a> father, Allen Bonet (1945-2008), was an <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> singer and was born in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>; her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> mother, Arlene (née Litman) (1940-1998), was a teacher. She has several younger half-siblings. Bonet attended <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_High_School" title="Birmingham High School">Birmingham High School</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Van_Nuys,_California" title="Van Nuys, California">Van Nuys, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She also attended the Celluloid Actor's Studio in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Hollywood" title="North Hollywood">North Hollywood</a> where she majored in acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Bonet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After being in beauty pageants and appearing in guest spots on television series as a child, Bonet landed the role of Denise Huxtable on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cosby_Show" title="The Cosby Show">The Cosby Show</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Bill_Cosby" title="Bill Cosby">Bill Cosby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phylicia_Rashad" title="Phylicia Rashad">Phylicia Rashad</a>. In 1987, she briefly left <i>The Cosby Show</i> to star in the spin-off series <i><a href="/wiki/A_Different_World" title="A Different World">A Different World</a></i>, which focused on Denise Huxtable's life at college. That year, Bonet, then 19, accepted the role of 17-year-old Epiphany Proudfoot in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Angel_Heart" title="Angel Heart">Angel Heart</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Rourke" title="Mickey Rourke">Mickey Rourke</a>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Parker" title="Alan Parker">Alan Parker</a>. In the film, several seconds of an explicit scene she shared with Rourke were edited to ensure an R-rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It was followed by a topless centerspread in <i><a href="/wiki/Interview_(magazine)" title="Interview (magazine)">Interview</a></i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> After announcing her pregnancy during the run of <i>A Different World</i>, Bonet left the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The following year, she returned to <i>The Cosby Show</i>, but was fired in 1991 because of "creative differences".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <i>The Cosby Show</i>, Bonet began to accept jobs on <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">straight-to-video</a> releases and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_movie" title="Television movie">made-for-TV movies</a>. In 1998 she had a supporting role in <i><a href="/wiki/Enemy_of_the_State_(film)" title="Enemy of the State (film)">Enemy of the State</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a>. In 2000, she appeared in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/High_Fidelity_(film)" title="High Fidelity (film)">High Fidelity</a></i>. In 2003, she played the role of Queenie in <i><a href="/wiki/Biker_Boyz" title="Biker Boyz">Biker Boyz</a></i>, which reunited her with former co-star <a href="/wiki/Kadeem_Hardison" title="Kadeem Hardison">Kadeem Hardison</a> of <i>A Different World</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bonet_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bonet-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bonet also co-starred in the 2006 film <i>Whitepaddy</i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Sherilyn_Fenn" title="Sherilyn Fenn">Sherilyn Fenn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hill_Harper" title="Hill Harper">Hill Harper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Debra_Wilson" title="Debra Wilson">Debra Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karen_Black" title="Karen Black">Karen Black</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kareem_Abdul-Jabbar" title="Kareem Abdul-Jabbar">Kareem Abdul-Jabbar</a>. Two years later she appeared in the US adaptation of the British television series, <i><a href="/wiki/Life_on_Mars_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Life on Mars (U.S. TV series)">Life on Mars</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Bonet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On November 16, 1987, her 20th birthday, she <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elopement_(marriage)" title="Elopement (marriage)">eloped</a> with <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">singer</a> <a href="/wiki/Lenny_Kravitz" title="Lenny Kravitz">Lenny Kravitz</a> in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a>. Bonet recalled of their relationship:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">It was interesting when we were first finding out about each other, that our backgrounds were so similar. When I first told him my mom was Jewish, and he said 'So's my dad,' I thought that was both unusual and enchanting. I felt like, 'Okay, here's someone who really knows how it is.' And I think I trusted him a little more with my feelings and let him inside a little more than I ordinarily would have.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>She gave birth to their daughter <a href="/wiki/Zo%C3%AB_Kravitz" title="Zoë Kravitz">Zoë Isabella</a> on December 1, 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> She and Kravitz separated in 1993. In 1995, Bonet legally changed her name to Lilakoi Moon, although she still uses the name Lisa Bonet professionally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Westbrook_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Westbrook-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, she began a relationship with actor <a href="/wiki/Jason_Momoa" title="Jason Momoa">Jason Momoa</a>, and they married in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-horse_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-horse-3">[3]</a></sup> On July 23, 2007, Bonet gave birth to her second child, Lola Iolani Momoa, her first with Momoa. On December 15, 2008, the couple had a son named Nakoa-Wolf Manakauapo Namakaeha Momoa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
