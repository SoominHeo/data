<p><b>Lisa Irene Chappell</b> (born 18 October 1968) is an actress and musician from <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. She is best known for playing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claire_McLeod" title="Claire McLeod">Claire McLeod</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Nine_Network" title="Nine Network">Nine Network</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/McLeod%27s_Daughters" title="McLeod's Daughters">McLeod's Daughters</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Chappell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chappell was born and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. Two years before joining <i>McLeod's Daughters</i>, she completed "The Journey" program at the Actors Centre Australia in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aca-alumni-news_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-aca-alumni-news-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Chappell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Chappell has acted in numerous roles in New Zealand, including <i><a href="/wiki/Gloss_(TV_series)" title="Gloss (TV series)">Gloss</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Shortland_Street" title="Shortland Street">Shortland Street</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hercules:_The_Legendary_Journeys" title="Hercules: The Legendary Journeys">Hercules: The Legendary Journeys</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/City_Life_(TV_series)" title="City Life (TV series)">City Life</a></i>. Starting in 2000, Chappell played Claire McLeod in the Australian show <i>McLeod's Daughters</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> For this portrayal, she <a href="/wiki/Logie_Awards_of_2004" title="Logie Awards of 2004">won</a> a Most Popular Actress <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logie_Award" title="Logie Award">Logie Award</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and was nominated for a Silver Logie in 2003. She moved on from the show in 2003, when her character was killed in a car accident.</p>
<p>She has also appeared in the third installment of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Small_Claims" title="Small Claims">Small Claims</a>, 'The Reunion', and starred in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stingers" title="Stingers">Stingers</a> as Megan Walsh (Season 8).</p>
<p>Chappell starred in a stage adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Educating_Rita" title="Educating Rita">Educating Rita</a> in 2007, which toured Australia. She also stars in the Australian film <i><a href="/wiki/Coffin_Rock" title="Coffin Rock">Coffin Rock</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Singing">Singing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Chappell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Singing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Chappell's current<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (January 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> focus is her music career. Her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/When_Then_Is_Now" title="When Then Is Now">When Then Is Now</a></i>, was released on 1 May 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-release-5">[5]</a></sup> She did some minor touring in Australia and New Zealand to support the albums' release.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theatre">Theatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Chappell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Theatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1997, Chappell played Ann Deever in <i><a href="/wiki/All_My_Sons" title="All My Sons">All My Sons</a></i>, at the Maidment Theatre. In 2007, she starred in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Homecoming" title="The Homecoming">The Homecoming</a></i> as Ruth, at the <a href="/wiki/Auckland_Festival" title="Auckland Festival">Auckland Festival</a> AK07, and in the same year she toured Australia with the play <i><a href="/wiki/Educating_Rita" title="Educating Rita">Educating Rita</a></i>, where she played Rita.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Chappell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2004, Chappell became an ambassador for The Humor Foundation. She wanted to help raise money to keep The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clown_doctor" title="Clown doctor">Clown Doctors</a> in Australian hospitals. She has also written a children's book, in which she collaborated with her husband Chris to produce, which was illustrated by Chris.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lisa_Chappell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chappell married her husband Chris Taylor in 2001 after they met at her co-star <a href="/wiki/Rachael_Carpani" title="Rachael Carpani">Rachael Carpani</a>'s 21st birthday party, they later divorced in 2005. She made the decision to become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vegetarian" title="Vegetarian">vegetarian</a> after joining the cast of <i>McLeod's Daughters</i>. Chappell also supports donating blood and encourages everyone to give blood. She loves to write lyrics and other texts.</p>
