<p><b>Li Xiaopeng</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">李小鹏</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">李小鵬</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Lǐ Xiǎopéng</span></i>; July 27, 1981 in <a href="/wiki/Changsha" title="Changsha">Changsha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a>) is a male <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/Gymnastics" title="Gymnastics">gymnast</a>, who specializes in <a href="/wiki/Parallel_bars" title="Parallel bars">parallel bars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vault_(gymnastics)" title="Vault (gymnastics)">vault</a>. He currently holds 16 world titles, more than any other gymnasts in China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On 29 August 2009, he was the torch bearer for the torch relay of the <a href="/wiki/East_Asian_Games" title="East Asian Games">East Asian Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>. He retired from the sport in late 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cri_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-cri-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_Xiaopeng_(gymnast)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He began gymnastics training at <a href="/wiki/Changsha" title="Changsha">Changsha</a> Spare-time Sports School in <a href="/wiki/Hunan" title="Hunan">Hunan</a> Province at the age of 6 and was a member of the Hunan provincial team at 12. Li's diligence and skill soon set him apart from his peers, which could be proved by his several provincial titles. At the age of 15, he was selected into the national team.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team">National team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_Xiaopeng_(gymnast)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: National team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At 16, Li Xiaopeng became China's youngest world (team) gymnastics champion ever when he and his teammates won the men's team final at the <a href="/wiki/1997_World_Artistic_Gymnastics_Championships" title="1997 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships">1997 Lausanne World Championships</a>. At the same event, Li also received a silver medal for parallel bars, just second to his teammate <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zhang_Jingjin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zhang Jingjin (page does not exist)">Zhang Jingjin</a>, and a bronze for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Floor_exercise" title="Floor exercise">floor exercise</a>.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/1999_World_Artistic_Gymnastics_Championships" title="1999 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships">1999 Tianjin World Championships</a>, Li was part of the Chinese team to become men's team champion. Individually, he won his first gold medal in vault. However, his failure in parallel bars left something to be desired, which pushed him to train harder for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney_Olympics" title="Sydney Olympics">Sydney Olympics</a>.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Gymnastics_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics" title="Gymnastics at the 2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Sydney Olympic Games</a>, Li and his teammates gave an outstanding performance, earning them the gold medal at the prestigious men's team event, a first for Chinese gymnasts. Li also won an individual gold medal in parallel bars.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2003_World_Artistic_Gymnastics_Championships" title="2003 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships">2003 Anaheim World Championships</a>, Li Xiaopeng was the only person to win three gold medals, namely for men's team final, vault and parallel bars. Thus he was voted as the 2003 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=World_Gymnast_of_the_Year&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="World Gymnast of the Year (page does not exist)">World Gymnast of the Year</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, due to a severe foot injury, Li's performance at the <a href="/wiki/Gymnastics_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Gymnastics at the 2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Athens Olympic Games</a> was unsatisfactory. China finished 5th at the men's team final, and Li could only get a bronze in parallel bars, after being undefeated in major competitions since 2000. He was the fourth athlete to compete in the vault event finals, but unfortunately shuffled during the initial sprint and fell on the first jump.</p>
<p>Li had been suffering from a chronic ankle injury and had an operation in 2005. He rested for that whole year, refraining from participating in any competitions.</p>
<p>In 2006, he made a successful comeback by winning a gold in parallel bars at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2006_S%C3%A3o_Paulo_World_Cup_Series_Final&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2006 São Paulo World Cup Series Final (page does not exist)">São Paulo World Cup Series Final</a>. By then he had tied with his idol, China's gymnastic legend <a href="/wiki/Li_Ning" title="Li Ning">Li Ning</a>, by winning a record of 14 world titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, after suffering from toe injury, he came back and won titles for parallel bars at the two gymnastics World Cups, Cottbus and Tianjin.</p>
<p>After winning two gold medals for men's team and parallel bars at the <a href="/wiki/Gymnastics_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Gymnastics at the 2008 Summer Olympics">Beijing Olympics</a>, he surpassed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Li_Ning_(gymnast)" title="Li Ning (gymnast)">Li Ning</a> by holding 16 world titles, more than any other gymnasts in China. He retired in late 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cri_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-cri-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_Xiaopeng_(gymnast)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_Gymnastique" title="Fédération Internationale de Gymnastique">Fédération Internationale de Gymnastique</a> has named two routines after Li. They are "Li Xiaopeng hop" in vault, and "Li Xiaopeng arm hang" in parallel bars.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_Xiaopeng_(gymnast)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He met his future wife a Chinese-American girl <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Li_%22Angel%22_Anqi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title='Li "Angel" Anqi (page does not exist)'>Li "Angel" Anqi</a> daughter of former gymnasts <a href="/wiki/Li_Xiaoping" title="Li Xiaoping">Li Xiaoping</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wen_Jia_(gymnast)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wen Jia (gymnast) (page does not exist)">Wen Jia</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2003_World_Artistic_Gymnastics_Championships" title="2003 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships">2003 World Championship</a> in <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Anaheim, California</a> where her father served as a host and interpreter for the Chinese team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-intl_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-intl-5">[5]</a></sup> They became engaged in 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and married in LA on June 5, 2010. The couple held a second marriage ceremony in Beijing on July 11, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-intl_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-intl-5">[5]</a></sup> Due to a problem with the visa of Li's parents, they were unable to attend the LA wedding ceremony. On January 12, 2012, they had their first child called Olivia (Li Xinqi). His second child, a son, was born in June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_shows">Television shows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Li_Xiaopeng_(gymnast)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Television shows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Li Xiaopeng and his daughter have been regular cast on the show "Dad came back" (version of Korean reality-variety show" Return of Superman")on which dads have to take care of their child/children without help of anyone (include their wives) in 48 hours. After the first season was aired, his daughter has gained a lot of love from public and fans. He also appears on other television shows, such as the <a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Race_1_(China)" title="The Amazing Race 1 (China)">first season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Race_China" title="The Amazing Race China">The Amazing Race China</a></i>, and the 2016 <i>Race the World</i> (非凡搭檔), which he had to quit before the finale due to the expected birth of his second child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
