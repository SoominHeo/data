<p>The <b>Lycée Louis-le-Grand</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[lise lwi lə gʁɑ̃]</a></span>) is a public <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. Founded in 1563 as the <i>Collège de Clermont</i>, it was renamed in King <a href="/wiki/Louis_XIV_of_France" title="Louis XIV of France">Louis XIV of France</a>'s honor after he extended his direct patronage to it in 1682. It offers both a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sixth-form_college" title="Sixth-form college">sixth-form college</a> curriculum (as a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lyc%C3%A9e" title="Lycée">lycée</a></i> with 800 pupils), and a post-secondary-level curriculum (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CPGE" title="CPGE">classes préparatoires</a></i> with 900 students), preparing students for entrance to the <a href="/wiki/Elite" title="Elite">elite</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grandes_%C3%89coles" title="Grandes Écoles">Grandes Écoles</a> (such as the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_Normale_Sup%C3%A9rieure" title="École Normale Supérieure">École Normale Supérieure</a>, the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_Polytechnique" title="École Polytechnique">École Polytechnique</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centrale_Paris" title="Centrale Paris">Centrale Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/HEC_Paris" title="HEC Paris">HEC Paris</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ESSEC" title="ESSEC">ESSEC Business School</a>). Students at the Lycée Louis-le-Grand are called <i>magnoludoviciens</i>.</p>
<p>Louis-le-Grand, founded in 1563, is located in the heart of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quartier_Latin" title="Quartier Latin">Quartier Latin</a>, the traditional student's area of Paris. Rich in history, architecture, culture, this area is home to some of the oldest and most prestigious educational establishments in France including the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Paris" title="University of Paris">Sorbonne</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Coll%C3%A8ge_de_France" title="Collège de France">Collège de France</a>. The <i>lycée</i> is situated close to the <i>place du <a href="/wiki/Panth%C3%A9on" title="Panthéon">Panthéon</a></i>, which is the location of its historical rival, the <a href="/wiki/Lyc%C3%A9e_Henri-IV" title="Lycée Henri-IV">Lycée Henri-IV</a>. These two lycées are home to the oldest preparatory classes in France, which are commonly viewed as the most selective in the country.</p>
<p>Because of this, Louis-le-Grand is considered to play an important role in the education of French elites. Many of its former pupils have become statesmen, diplomats, prelates, marshals of France, members of the <a href="/wiki/Acad%C3%A9mie_fran%C3%A7aise" title="Académie française">Académie française</a>, and men and women of letters. "The Jesuit College of Paris", wrote Élie de Beaumont in 1862, "has for a long time been a state nursery, the most fertile in great men". Indeed, former students have included writers <a href="/wiki/Moli%C3%A8re" title="Molière">Molière</a>, <a href="/wiki/Voltaire" title="Voltaire">Voltaire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victor_Hugo" title="Victor Hugo">Victor Hugo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Baudelaire" title="Charles Baudelaire">Charles Baudelaire</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">revolutionaries</a> <a href="/wiki/Maximilien_Robespierre" title="Maximilien Robespierre">Maximilien Robespierre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louis_Antoine_de_Saint-Just" title="Louis Antoine de Saint-Just">Louis Antoine de Saint-Just</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marquis_de_Sade" title="Marquis de Sade">Marquis de Sade</a> and <a href="/wiki/Camille_Desmoulins" title="Camille Desmoulins">Camille Desmoulins</a>, former French presidents <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Poincar%C3%A9" title="Raymond Poincaré">Raymond Poincaré</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Deschanel" title="Paul Deschanel">Paul Deschanel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Millerand" title="Alexandre Millerand">Alexandre Millerand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Poher" title="Alain Poher">Alain Poher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georges_Pompidou" title="Georges Pompidou">Georges Pompidou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Val%C3%A9ry_Giscard_d%27Estaing" title="Valéry Giscard d'Estaing">Valéry Giscard d'Estaing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Chirac" title="Jacques Chirac">Jacques Chirac</a> as well as many other ministers and prime ministers, philosophers such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emile_Durkheim" title="Emile Durkheim">Emile Durkheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denis_Diderot" title="Denis Diderot">Denis Diderot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Paul_Sartre" title="Jean-Paul Sartre">Jean-Paul Sartre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Derrida" title="Jacques Derrida">Jacques Derrida</a>, scientists <a href="/wiki/%C3%89variste_Galois" title="Évariste Galois">Évariste Galois</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henri_Poincar%C3%A9" title="Henri Poincaré">Henri Poincaré</a> and <a href="/wiki/Laurent_Schwartz" title="Laurent Schwartz">Laurent Schwartz</a>, and artists <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_Delacroix" title="Eugène Delacroix">Eugène Delacroix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Degas" title="Edgar Degas">Edgar Degas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georges_M%C3%A9li%C3%A8s" title="Georges Méliès">Georges Méliès</a>. Renowned foreign students of the <i>lycée</i> include King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicholas_I_of_Montenegro" title="Nicholas I of Montenegro">Nicholas I of Montenegro</a>, <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A9opold_S%C3%A9dar_Senghor" title="Léopold Sédar Senghor">Léopold Sédar Senghor</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Francis_de_Sales" title="Saint Francis de Sales">Saint Francis de Sales</a>.</p>
<p>Admission to Louis-Le-Grand is very competitive; the strict selection process is based on academic grades, drawing from middle schools throughout France. The education is highly rated and the conditions of work are optimal due to the diverse qualities of the teachers.Louis-Le-Grand students generally achieve high marks and results for the baccalauréat, with a pass rate of 100% in most years, against a national pass rate of about 80%. The Concours général (national competition for brilliant students) and the examinations to enter the grandes écoles are considered more difficult. Overall,Louis-le-Grand provides a high level of education due to its rigorous selection process and the quality of its teachers.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Abu_Dhabi_Section"><a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a> Section</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lyc%C3%A9e_Louis-le-Grand&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Abu Dhabi Section">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 2008, LLG and the <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_Education_Council" title="Abu Dhabi Education Council">Abu Dhabi Education Council</a> launched the Advanced Math and Science Pilot Class. There is a class designed for girls (20 girls) and another for boys (20 boys). Classes will be taught by professors sent from France, exceptionally the classes are taught in English.</p>
<p>The students who make up the Advanced Math and Science Pilot Class graduate at the end of the 12th grade and are awarded with a certificate of academic recognition by LLG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
