<p><b>Reece Brown</b> (born 1 November 1991) is an English <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> for <a href="/wiki/EFL_League_One" title="EFL League One">EFL League One</a> side <a href="/wiki/Bury_F.C." title="Bury F.C.">Bury</a>. He began his career with <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>, and has played on loan at <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_A.F.C." title="Bradford City A.F.C.">Bradford City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_Rovers_F.C." title="Doncaster Rovers F.C.">Doncaster Rovers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oldham_Athletic_A.F.C." title="Oldham Athletic A.F.C.">Oldham Athletic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlisle_United_F.C." title="Carlisle United F.C.">Carlisle United</a>. His elder brother, <a href="/wiki/Wes_Brown" title="Wes Brown">Wes</a>, also played for Manchester United from 1998 to 2011.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reece_Brown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brown joined <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> at the age of nine, having previously played for Fletcher Moss Rangers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> After making his way through United's youth teams he was awarded his first professional contract by the club in the summer of 2009.</p>
<p>On 29 September 2010, he joined <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_A.F.C." title="Bradford City A.F.C.">Bradford City</a> on a month's loan along with fellow Manchester United defender <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Gill" title="Oliver Gill">Oliver Gill</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bantams_land_United_pair_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bantams_land_United_pair-4">[4]</a></sup> Brown, like Gill, made his debut on 2 October 2010 in a 0–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Morecambe_F.C." title="Morecambe F.C.">Morecambe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On 25 October 2010, the pair returned to Manchester United after the expiration of their month's loan despite Bradford's willingness to extend their stay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 16 August 2011, he joined <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_Rovers_F.C." title="Doncaster Rovers F.C.">Doncaster Rovers</a> on a month's loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He made his debut on the same day, coming on as an 85th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Kyle_Bennett_(footballer)" title="Kyle Bennett (footballer)">Kyle Bennett</a> in Doncaster's 1–0 home defeat by <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest_F.C." title="Nottingham Forest F.C.">Nottingham Forest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Doncaster_0.E2.80.931_Nott.27m_Forest_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Doncaster_0.E2.80.931_Nott.27m_Forest-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brown returned from his loan spell at Doncaster on 10 September 2011 and after a couple of impressive displays in the reserves, he was included in the first-team squad for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Football_League_Cup#Third_round" title="2011–12 Football League Cup">third round</a> tie against <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a>, however he was an unused substitute on the night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 2 March 2012, Manchester United agreed for Brown to spend a one-month loan spell at <a href="/wiki/Oldham_Athletic_A.F.C." title="Oldham Athletic A.F.C.">Oldham Athletic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 3 April 2012, Brown extended his loan deal with the club until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 20 July 2012, he went out on loan again, this time to <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a> for the duration of the 2012–13 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He made his debut on 11 August in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> in a 1–0 win away to <a href="/wiki/Dagenham_%26_Redbridge_F.C." title="Dagenham &amp; Redbridge F.C.">Dagenham &amp; Redbridge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He made his league debut in a 1–1 draw away to <a href="/wiki/Yeovil_Town_F.C." title="Yeovil Town F.C.">Yeovil Town</a> on 18 August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On 21 August 2012, he made his home debut in a 1–1 draw to <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Although the loan was intended to last the entire season, Manchester United recalled Brown in January 2013 after he fell down the pecking order at Coventry;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> his last appearance came in the league against <a href="/wiki/Oldham_Athletic_A.F.C." title="Oldham Athletic A.F.C.">Oldham Athletic</a> in September 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 February 2013, Manchester United agreed for Brown to spend the rest of the season on loan to <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> At the end of the 2012–13 season, he was released by Manchester United.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brown completed a move to <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a>, signing a two-year deal on 22 July 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He made his league debut as a substitute away to <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a> on 17 August 2013.</p>
<p>On 11 March 2014, Brown joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a> side <a href="/wiki/Carlisle_United_F.C." title="Carlisle United F.C.">Carlisle United</a> on a one-month loan until 12 April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On 15 April 2014, Brown extended his loan stay with the <i>Cumbrians</i> until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Football_League_One" title="2013–14 Football League One">2013–14</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite still being contracted to Watford, Brown went on trial at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a> side <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a> in July 2014 playing in some of their pre-season fixtures. On 31 July 2014, Brown duly signed for Barnsley from Watford for an undisclosed fee on a one-year deal with the option of a second.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a spell with <a href="/wiki/Bury_F.C." title="Bury F.C.">Bury</a>, he linked up with <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a> in September 2016 following a successful trial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> However, after making just two league appearances in 2016–17 for Sheffield United he departed the club in January 2017 to rejoin former club Bury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
