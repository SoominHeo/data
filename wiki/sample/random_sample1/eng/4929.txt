<p>The <b>University of Lisbon</b> (<b>UL</b>) (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>Universidade de Lisboa</i></span>, <small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[univɨɾsiˈdad(ɨ) dɨ liʒˈboɐ]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>Universitas Olisiponensis</i>) was a <a href="/wiki/Public_university" title="Public university">public university</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>. It was founded in 1911 after the fall of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese_monarchy" title="Portuguese monarchy">Portuguese monarchy</a> and was later integrated in the new <a href="/wiki/University_of_Lisbon" title="University of Lisbon">University of Lisbon</a> along with the former <a href="/wiki/Technical_University_of_Lisbon" title="Technical University of Lisbon">Technical University of Lisbon</a>. The history of a university in Lisbon dates back to the 13th century.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Lisbon_(1911%E2%80%932013)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first Portuguese university school was founded in 1290 by <a href="/wiki/Denis_of_Portugal" title="Denis of Portugal">King Dinis</a> in Lisbon, and was called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studium_Generale" title="Studium Generale">Studium Generale</a> (<i>Estudo Geral</i>). In the following 247 years, this first university school was moved several times between Lisbon and <a href="/wiki/Coimbra" title="Coimbra">Coimbra</a>. In 1537, during the reign of <a href="/wiki/John_III_of_Portugal" title="John III of Portugal">João III</a>, the university moved definitively to Coimbra. The entire university institution, including the teaching staff and all the books from its library, were moved to <a href="/wiki/Coimbra" title="Coimbra">Coimbra</a> where the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Coimbra" title="University of Coimbra">University of Coimbra</a> was definitively installed. Lisbon became a university city again in 1911 when the current University of Lisbon was founded, through the union of newly created and older schools, like the 19th century Polytechnic School (<i>Escola Politécnica</i>), the Royal Medical School of Lisbon (<i>Real Escola Médico-Cirúrgica de Lisboa</i>) and the Letters Higher Studies (<i>Curso Superior de Letras</i>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculties">Faculties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Lisbon_(1911%E2%80%932013)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Faculties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculty_of_Law">Faculty of Law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Lisbon_(1911%E2%80%932013)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Faculty of Law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Faculty of Law (Portuguese: <i>Faculdade de Direito</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> was officially created by a Decree of March 22, 1911 as <i>Faculdade de Ciências Económicas e Políticas</i>, but was only installed in 1913, and was given its current designation later in 1918. It was originally located at the Valmor Building (<i>Edifício Valmor</i>) at the <i>Campo dos Mártires da Pátria</i>. It was transferred to its current campus at the University City (<i>Cidade Universitária</i>) in 1957-1958. A new building, housing the Faculty's library, was built in the late 1990s.</p>
<p>The only graduation given is <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">law</a>, and the specialised post-graduate studies available include several branches of the same area.</p>
<p>Among the many distinguished graduates from the faculty of law are the former <a href="/wiki/President_of_Portugal" title="President of Portugal">Presidents of Portugal</a> <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Sampaio" title="Jorge Sampaio">Jorge Sampaio</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1rio_Soares" title="Mário Soares">Mário Soares</a>, Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_Caetano" title="Marcelo Caetano">Marcelo Caetano</a>, the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_European_Commission" title="President of the European Commission">President of the European Commission</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Manuel_Dur%C3%A3o_Barroso" title="José Manuel Durão Barroso">José Manuel Durão Barroso</a>, Portuguese statesman, deputy, and professor <a href="/wiki/Adriano_Moreira" title="Adriano Moreira">Adriano Moreira</a> and businessman and former <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Portugal" title="List of Prime Ministers of Portugal">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Pinto_Balsem%C3%A3o" title="Francisco Pinto Balsemão">Francisco Pinto Balsemão</a>. Current President <a href="/wiki/Marcelo_Rebelo_de_Sousa" title="Marcelo Rebelo de Sousa">Marcelo Rebelo de Sousa</a> was a full professor there for many years, before his election in 2016. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portuguese_First_Republic" title="Portuguese First Republic">First Republic</a> political leader and several times Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Afonso_Costa" title="Afonso Costa">Afonso Costa</a> was a teacher at the faculty and its founder and first dean. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculty_of_Sciences">Faculty of Sciences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Lisbon_(1911%E2%80%932013)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Faculty of Sciences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Faculty of Sciences (Portuguese: <i>Faculdade de Ciências</i>, usually abbreviated <i>FCUL</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> was created on April 19, 1911, by the transformation of the former Polytechnic School (<i>Escola Politécnica</i>). From that date until 1985 (when it moved to its current grounds, at Campo Grande) it was established on the former Polytechnic School building. Those former installations are now used as museum, now and then.</p>
<p>Its current grounds comprise a built area of 75662 square meters, corresponding to 8 buildings (labeled C1 through C8, where <i>C</i> stands for <i>Ciências</i> — Sciences) which host the classrooms, offices, cafeterias, libraries, book shop and leisure areas. The faculty population, as of the 2009/2010 school year, consisted of (in parentheses, the numbers as of the 2008/2009 school year):</p>
<ul>
<li>3055 graduation students (2964);</li>
<li>418 Joint degree (B.Sc.+M.Sc.) students (327)</li>
<li>1008 M.Sc. students (1218);</li>
<li>412 Ph.D. students (552);</li>
<li>388 teachers, about 95.3% hold a Ph.D. (417, 96.6%);</li>
<li>22 hired research staff (23)</li>
<li>186 non-teaching workers (204).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>The computer science department has been granted several honours, namely a finalist position in the <a href="/wiki/Descartes_Prize" title="Descartes Prize">Descartes Prize</a> and two <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> Scientific Awards.</p>
<p>The faculty's campus also comprises the <i><a class="external text" href="http://ibeb.fc.ul.pt/" rel="nofollow">Instituto de Biofísica e Engenharia Biomédica (IBEB)</a></i>, the <i><a class="external text" href="http://www.io.fc.ul.pt/" rel="nofollow">Instituto de Oceanografia</a></i> and the <i><a class="external text" href="http://www.icat.fc.ul.pt/" rel="nofollow">Instituto de Ciência Aplicada e Tecnologia (ICAT)</a></i>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculty_of_Medicine">Faculty of Medicine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Lisbon_(1911%E2%80%932013)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Faculty of Medicine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Faculty_of_Letters">Faculty of Letters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Lisbon_(1911%E2%80%932013)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Faculty of Letters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Faculty of Letters (Portuguese: <i>Faculdade de Letras</i>), FLUL, <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.fl.ul.pt/" rel="nofollow">[4]</a> was created in 1911, although it's predated by the Superior Studies in Letters (<i>Curso Superior de Letras</i>), created in 1859 by King <a href="/wiki/Pedro_V_of_Portugal" title="Pedro V of Portugal">Pedro V</a>, from which all students and professors were transferred.</p>
<p>It remained on the grounds of the Superior Studies, an annex to the Academy of Science until 1957, when it changed to the current building, in the University City (<i>Cidade Universitária</i>). In 1975, a new pavilion was built to accommodate the large influx of students who arrived after the democratization of Superior Education in Portugal, a consequence of the <a href="/wiki/Carnation_Revolution" title="Carnation Revolution">Carnation Revolution</a>. The pavilion, theoretically provisional, still stands today. In 2001, two new buildings were finished: one to accommodate new classrooms and the Computer Room, and the Library Building, which is now the second biggest library in Portugal.</p>
<p>Although the faculty's graduation with most studies is modern languages and literatures (<i>Línguas e Literaturas Modernas</i>) (which has a number of variants, including studies in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>), it also offers <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a>, <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a> (and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archeology" title="Archeology">archeology</a>), <a href="/wiki/African_studies" title="African studies">African studies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asian_studies" title="Asian studies">Asian studies</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_studies" title="European studies">European studies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cultural_studies" title="Cultural studies">cultural studies</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Classical_studies" title="Classical studies">classical studies</a> (the degree itself is named classic languages and literatures). It is also the former home of the degree in <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">psychology</a>. In the mid-1980s a new Faculty of Psychology <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.fpce.ul.pt" rel="nofollow">[5]</a> was created to accommodate it.</p>
<p>Famous professors at the faculty include the second President of the Portuguese Republic, <a href="/wiki/Te%C3%B3filo_Braga" title="Teófilo Braga">Teófilo Braga</a>, and writers <a href="/wiki/Vitorino_Nem%C3%A9sio" title="Vitorino Nemésio">Vitorino Nemésio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Urbano_Tavares_Rodrigues" title="Urbano Tavares Rodrigues">Urbano Tavares Rodrigues</a>.</p>
<p>The most famous former student, who only attended for less than a year, is the poet <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Pessoa" title="Fernando Pessoa">Fernando Pessoa</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fialho_Gouveia" title="Fialho Gouveia">Fialho Gouveia</a>, a noted Portuguese television presenter, attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romance_Languages" title="Romance Languages">Romance</a> <a href="/wiki/Philology" title="Philology">Philology</a> course at the <i>Faculdade de Letras</i> but dropped out in order to follow a successful career in radio and television. The writer <a href="/wiki/Luiz_Pacheco" title="Luiz Pacheco">Luiz Pacheco</a> was a student at FLUL before dropping out. The actress <a href="/wiki/Alexandra_Lencastre" title="Alexandra Lencastre">Alexandra Lencastre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moonspell" title="Moonspell">Moonspell</a> frontman <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fernando_Ribeiro_(music)" title="Fernando Ribeiro (music)">Fernando Ribeiro</a> also attended the philosophy course but did not graduate. Famous musician and composer <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Lopes-Gra%C3%A7a" title="Fernando Lopes-Graça">Fernando Lopes-Graça</a> also dropped out of FLUL. Football player and manager <a href="/wiki/Artur_Jorge_(footballer)" title="Artur Jorge (footballer)">Artur Jorge</a> graduated by FLUL after has been a student at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Coimbra" title="University of Coimbra">University of Coimbra</a>'s FLUC.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Lisbon_(1911%E2%80%932013)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Instituto_de_Medicina_Molecular" title="Instituto de Medicina Molecular">Instituto de Medicina Molecular</a> of the University of Lisbon, a research institute in <a href="/wiki/Molecular_medicine" title="Molecular medicine">molecular medicine</a>, is one of the most noted biosciences research institutions in Portugal.</p>
<p>The Instituto Geofisico do Infante Dom Luiz exists since 1853 and is a research and operational unit that maintains the longest meteorological series of Portugal. Research is organized and funded through CGUL, the leading Portuguese geophysical research unit, and Associated Laboratory (with LATTEX) of the Portuguese Ministry of Science and Technology.</p>
<p>Researchers of <a href="/wiki/LaSIGE" title="LaSIGE">LaSIGE</a>, a research laboratory for large-scale information systems (integrated into the Department of Computer Science), have received several honors, namely an IBM Scientific Award, an <a href="/wiki/Ordem_dos_Engenheiros" title="Ordem dos Engenheiros">Order of Engineers</a> distinction and a place among the eight finalists of the <a href="/wiki/Descartes_Prize" title="Descartes Prize">Descartes Prize</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Instituto_de_Ci.C3.AAncias_Sociais_.28Institute_of_Social_Sciences.29">Instituto de Ciências Sociais (Institute of Social Sciences)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Lisbon_(1911%E2%80%932013)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Instituto de Ciências Sociais (Institute of Social Sciences)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Instituto de Ciências Sociais (ICS), an associated state laboratory, is a university institution devoted to research and advanced training in the social sciences.</p>
<p>The mission of the ICS is to study contemporary societies, with a special emphasis on Portugal and the societies and cultures with which Portugal has a historical relationship, either in Europe or in other regions of the world.</p>
<p>Bringing together researchers from several different disciplines, the ICS focuses its research on five main subject areas: the formation of the contemporary world; the study of citizenship and democratic institutions; the problems of sustainability, linking the environment, risk and space; social changes and individual action in the context of the family, lifestyles and schooling; and issues concerning identity, migration and religion. The main subjects represented at the institute are: social and cultural anthropology, political science, economics, human geography, history, social psychology and sociology.</p>
<p>Researchers and the ideas they propose are the driving force of the ICS. The high number of postgraduates engaged in research in a wide range of disciplines and the vitality acquired over the years make the ICS a key institution in this field of knowledge in both Portugal and Europe. In terms of methodology, ICS research involves a broad range of procedures, ranging from the study of documents, ethnographic methods, qualitative studies, studies involving representative samples and experimental methods.</p>
<p>Having originated in 1962 as the independent Social Science Research Group (GIS), founded by Adérito Sedas Nunes, the ICS became an autonomous institute of the University of Lisbon in 1982 and acquired the status of associated state laboratory in 2002, an achievement that puts it on a par with other Portuguese institutions of excellence.</p>
<p>The central activities of the ICS include: publishing research in book form and in articles in Portuguese and international journals of reference; advanced education to the level of master's degrees and doctorates; maintaining a dialogue with the international scientific community; and spreading knowledge in the wider community—an increasingly important activity to ensure the necessary interaction between science and citizenship.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/An%C3%A1lise_Social" title="Análise Social">Análise Social</a> (Social Analysis), Portugal's oldest and most prestigious peer-reviewed social science journal, together with the ICS's own publishing house, Imprensa de Ciências Sociais (Social Science Press) are the most visible manifestations of the institute's activities.</p>
<p>The ICS is located at the University of Lisbon campus in a central area of the city next to the national library (<a class="external text" href="http://www.bnportugal.pt/" rel="nofollow">Biblioteca Nacional</a>). Its new offices, opened in 2003, were especially designed for the institute's research activities and postgraduate courses. The Library, which has 40,000 books and subscribes to 313 periodicals, is also home to the fast-growing Social History Archive (AHS).</p>
<p>In an open and informal atmosphere, the ICS has succeeded in creating an attractive environment for intellectual activity, and has developed strong ties with the academic world in Portugal and internationally. Over time, the institute has established an internal culture of independence and creativity, but also of exacting standards and responsibility.</p>
<p>Currently (2008), the institute has about 70 researchers and 100 postgraduate students and is engaged in about 200 research projects. Nearly 70 per cent of its activities are financed from its own funds, which are obtained competitively.</p>
