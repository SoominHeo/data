<p>On 17 February 2016, in <a href="/wiki/Ankara" title="Ankara">Ankara</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, at least 30 people died and 60 were injured in a bombing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnnturk_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-cnnturk-5">[5]</a></sup> According to Turkish authorities, the attack targeted a convoy of shuttles carrying both civilian and military personnel working at the military headquarters during the evening rush hour<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardFeb1716_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-GuardFeb1716-7">[7]</a></sup> as the vehicles were stopped at traffic lights at an intersection with <a href="/wiki/%C4%B0smet_%C4%B0n%C3%B6n%C3%BC_Boulevard_(Ankara)" title="İsmet İnönü Boulevard (Ankara)">İsmet İnönü Boulevard</a> close to <a href="/wiki/K%C4%B1z%C4%B1lay,_Ankara" title="Kızılay, Ankara">Kızılay neighborhood</a>. Several ministries, the headquarters of the army and the Turkish Parliament are located in the neighbourhood where the attack occurred.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Freedom_Falcons" title="Kurdistan Freedom Falcons">Kurdistan Freedom Falcons</a> (TAK) took responsibility for the attack and said they targeted security forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ntv1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ntv1-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardiantak_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardiantak-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=February_2016_Ankara_bombing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Civil_War" title="Syrian Civil War">Syrian Civil War</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurdish_rebellions_in_Turkey" title="Kurdish rebellions in Turkey">Kurdish rebellions in Turkey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kurdish%E2%80%93Turkish_conflict_(2015%E2%80%93present)" title="Kurdish–Turkish conflict (2015–present)">Kurdish–Turkish conflict (2015–present)</a></div>
<p>In October 2015, a <a href="/wiki/2015_Ankara_bombings" title="2015 Ankara bombings">bombing at a peace rally in Ankara</a> against a crackdown on Kurds in the country amidst the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PKK_rebellion_(2015%E2%80%93present)" title="PKK rebellion (2015–present)">renewed PKK rebellion</a> following a breakdown of the ceasefire killed over 100 people. On 13 February, Turkish shelling of Kurdish positions in response to "incoming Kurdish fire" and against the backdrop of <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Protection_Units" title="People's Protection Units">YPG</a> territorial gains in northern Syria<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> led, at the request of Russia, to a UN briefing in which the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_Nations_Security_Council" title="President of the United Nations Security Council">president of the United Nations Security Council</a> <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Ram%C3%ADrez_(Venezuela)" title="Rafael Ramírez (Venezuela)">Rafael Carreno</a> said that all members during the closed-door meeting expressed their concern at Turkey's actions and called on the country to "comply with international law".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the UNSC vote and just hours before the bombing, Turkey's President, <a href="/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan" title="Recep Tayyip Erdoğan">Recep Tayyip Erdoğan</a>, referred to <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Union_Party_(Syria)" title="Democratic Union Party (Syria)">PYD</a> as a "terrorist organisation" akin to the <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Workers%27_Party" title="Kurdistan Workers' Party">Kurdistan Workers' Party</a> (PKK), <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant</a> (ISIL), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DHKP-C" title="DHKP-C">DHKP-C</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Nusra_Front" title="Al-Nusra Front">Al-Nusra Front</a> and stressed that the attacks against PYD's armed wing, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YPG" title="YPG">YPG</a>, will continue until it stops alleged threats against Turkey's national security.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup>[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Pages_needing_translation_into_English" title="Wikipedia:Pages needing translation into English">source needs translation</a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bombings">Bombings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=February_2016_Ankara_bombing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bombings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Car_bomb" title="Car bomb">car bomb</a> detonated at 18:31 local time, while army buses carrying military personnel were waiting at traffic lights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ntv1_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ntv1-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The attack site was next to a residential block for high-ranking military personnel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DailyMail_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-DailyMail-20">[20]</a></sup> Some Turkish news channels showed images of a raging fire that engulfed military vehicles after the explosion, heard several kilometres away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Casualties">Casualties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=February_2016_Ankara_bombing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Casualties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At least 30 people, including the perpetrator, died and 60 were injured as a result of the blast. The initial dead included the perpetrator, 12 soldiers, 12 civilian employees of the military and a journalist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Another civilian died from his wounds in hospital on 23 February 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> A list of the first dead was published on 18 February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Assailant">Assailant</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=February_2016_Ankara_bombing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Assailant">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Initially, Turkish government declared the name of assailant as Salih Necar, a Syrian Kurd who was trained by YPG, PKK and Syrian government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Kurdistan Freedom Falcons (TAK) claimed responsibility and named the suicide bomber as Abdülbaki Sönmez and released a photo of him, which was later claimed to be an edited image of a Turkish blogger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardiantak_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guardiantak-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later, DNA reports confirmed that perpetrator was Turkish-born Abdulbaki Sönmez, not Syrian-born Salih Necar who was shown as perpetrator to the world by Turkish prime minister Ahmet Davutoglu. Also, Syrian Kurdish authorities announced that a person named Salih Necar from Hasakah doesn't exist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-haberturk_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-haberturk-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>After DNA reports Deputy Prime Minister of Turkey <a href="/wiki/Numan_Kurtulmu%C5%9F" title="Numan Kurtulmuş">Numan Kurtulmuş</a> said that "the name of the perpetrator may be different but it does not change the reality of this matter. This person has entered Turkey from the PYD region and there is record of him assuming the given identity.".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Turkish government is claiming that YPG, taking orders from PKK, is responsible from the massacre, and TAK is used as a way to "exonerate" the YPG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Turkish government is also worried about NATO allies of support of YPG, prime minister Davutoğlu said “We cannot excuse any <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> ally, including the US, of having links with a terrorist organisation [YPG] that strikes us in the heart of Turkey”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Turkish authorities claimed that the perpetrator was trained as a suicide bomber in Syrian Rojava by YPG and entered Turkey as a refugee. Turkey claims that the attack was planned by the YPG, PKK, PYD and the <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syrian</a> government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph12162418_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph12162418-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JPost445383_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-JPost445383-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>The suicide bomber was eventually confirmed as Abdülbaki Sönmez, a citizen of Turkey.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic">Domestic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=February_2016_Ankara_bombing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Domestic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan issued a statement. Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Ahmet_Davuto%C4%9Flu" title="Ahmet Davutoğlu">Ahmet Davutoğlu</a> cancelled planned trips to Belgium and Azerbaijan following the attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardFeb1716_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-GuardFeb1716-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Spokesman for the ruling <a href="/wiki/Justice_and_Development_Party_(Turkey)" title="Justice and Development Party (Turkey)">Justice and Development Party</a> <a href="/wiki/%C3%96mer_%C3%87elik" title="Ömer Çelik">Ömer Çelik</a> said he condemned the attacks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardFeb1716_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-GuardFeb1716-7">[7]</a></sup> Erdogan vowed retaliation against the perpetrators of the attack while Deputy Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Numan_Kurtulmu%C5%9F" title="Numan Kurtulmuş">Numan Kurtulmuş</a> called it an attack on the "nation as a whole".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Oppositional <a href="/wiki/Peoples%27_Democratic_Party_(Turkey)" title="Peoples' Democratic Party (Turkey)">Peoples' Democratic Party</a> (HDP) co-leader <a href="/wiki/Selahattin_Demirta%C5%9F" title="Selahattin Demirtaş">Selahattin Demirtaş</a> condemned the bombing as a "merciless attack" and wished "God’s mercy upon those who lost their lives".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Grand_National_Assembly_of_Turkey" title="Grand National Assembly of Turkey">Turkish Parliament</a> failed to make a unanimous declaration. The pro-Kurdish and pro-minority HDP party condemned the bombing, with parliamentary chair <a href="/wiki/%C4%B0dris_Baluken" title="İdris Baluken">İdris Baluken</a> referring to it as a "loathsome attack"; the party disagreed with the unilaterally drafted text. The declaration was finally released by the ruling <a href="/wiki/Justice_and_Development_Party_(Turkey)" title="Justice and Development Party (Turkey)">Justice and Development Party</a> (AKP), and opposition <a href="/wiki/Republican_People%27s_Party_(Turkey)" title="Republican People's Party (Turkey)">CHP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nationalist_Movement_Party" title="Nationalist Movement Party">MHP</a> parties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=February_2016_Ankara_bombing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An EU summit in Brussels that was to focus on the issues of migrants was cancelled following the bombing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> World leaders, including those from Germany, the United Kingdom and United States condemned the attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-45">[45]</a></sup> In a joint statement, the EU's High Representative for Foreign Affairs <a href="/wiki/Federica_Mogherini" title="Federica Mogherini">Federica Mogherini</a> and European Enlargement Commissioner <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Hahn" title="Johannes Hahn">Johannes Hahn</a>, termed the bombing "a terrible attack".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-45">[45]</a></sup> NATO Secretary-General <a href="/wiki/Jens_Stoltenberg" title="Jens Stoltenberg">Jens Stoltenberg</a> condemned the attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_45-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
