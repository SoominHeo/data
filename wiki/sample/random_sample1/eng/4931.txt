<p><b>Leah Marie Remini</b> (born June 15, 1970)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American actress, producer, author, and comedian. She is best known for her roles as <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Heffernan" title="Carrie Heffernan">Carrie Heffernan</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_King_of_Queens" title="The King of Queens">The King of Queens</a></i> (1998–2007) and Lara in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Old_School_(film)" title="Old School (film)">Old School</a></i>, as well as a co-hosting <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Talk_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Talk (U.S. TV series)">The Talk</a></i> from 2010–2011. In 2015, she starred in the show <i><a href="/wiki/Leah_Remini:_It%27s_All_Relative" title="Leah Remini: It's All Relative">Leah Remini: It's All Relative</a></i>, and released <i><a href="/wiki/Troublemaker:_Surviving_Hollywood_and_Scientology" title="Troublemaker: Surviving Hollywood and Scientology">Troublemaker: Surviving Hollywood and Scientology</a></i>, her memoir about her experience with <a href="/wiki/Scientology" title="Scientology">Scientology</a> and reasons for leaving it. In 2016, she followed up her memoir with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Documentary_television_series" title="Documentary television series">documentary television series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Leah_Remini:_Scientology_and_the_Aftermath" title="Leah Remini: Scientology and the Aftermath">Leah Remini: Scientology and the Aftermath</a></i> to highlight other former Scientologists' experiences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leah_Remini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Remini was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York_City" title="Brooklyn, New York City">Brooklyn, New York City</a>, to George Remini, who owned an <a href="/wiki/Asbestos" title="Asbestos">asbestos</a> removal company, and his wife, Vicki Marshall, a schoolteacher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmr_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmr-3">[3]</a></sup> Her mother is of <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Austria" title="History of the Jews in Austria">Austrian Jewish</a> descent, while her father has <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italian</a> ancestry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ref03_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ref03-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-5">[5]</a></sup> Remini has an older sister named Nicole and four half-sisters: Christine, Stephanie (died 2013),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Elizabeth, and Shannon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Leah was nine years old when her mother joined the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Scientology" title="Church of Scientology">Church of Scientology</a>, and thereafter she was raised as a Scientologist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-People7.29.13_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-People7.29.13-8">[8]</a></sup> At 13 years old, Remini moved to Los Angeles with her mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ref03_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ref03-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leah_Remini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One of Remini's early television roles was on <i><a href="/wiki/Who%27s_the_Boss%3F" title="Who's the Boss?">Who's the Boss?</a></i> as Charlie Briscoe, which led to a spin-off series entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Living_Dolls" title="Living Dolls">Living Dolls</a></i>, in which Remini starred with <a href="/wiki/Halle_Berry" title="Halle Berry">Halle Berry</a>. The show premiered in late 1989 and ran for 12 episodes before being cancelled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Remini appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Saved_by_the_Bell" title="Saved by the Bell">Saved by the Bell</a></i>, playing Stacey Carosi. She then appeared in two series, <i><a href="/wiki/First_Time_Out" title="First Time Out">First Time Out</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Fired_Up_(TV_series)" title="Fired Up (TV series)">Fired Up</a></i>. In 1993, she appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Cheers" title="Cheers">Cheers</a></i> as the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Carla_Tortelli" title="Carla Tortelli">Carla</a> and Nick Tortelli (<a href="/wiki/Rhea_Perlman" title="Rhea Perlman">Rhea Perlman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dan_Hedaya" title="Dan Hedaya">Dan Hedaya</a>). In 1994, Remini auditioned for the role of <a href="/wiki/Monica_Geller" title="Monica Geller">Monica Geller</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a></i>, but the role went to <a href="/wiki/Courteney_Cox" title="Courteney Cox">Courteney Cox</a>. Remini later appeared in the 1995 <i>Friends</i> episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_One_with_the_Birth" title="The One with the Birth">The One with the Birth</a>" in which she played a pregnant woman. In 1998, Remini landed the role of Carrie Heffernan on the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> sitcom <i>The King of Queens</i>. The series was successful, and ran for nine seasons.</p>
<p>During her time on the show, she had a supporting role in the <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Old_School_(film)" title="Old School (film)">Old School</a></i> (2003). Remini also starred in her own reality show, which aired on VH1. <i>The King of Queens</i> ran for nine seasons, from September 21, 1998, to May 14, 2007. Since <i>The King of Queens</i> ended, Remini has starred in nine-episode webisodes of <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Motherhood" title="In the Motherhood">In the Motherhood</a></i>, along with <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Handler" title="Chelsea Handler">Chelsea Handler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenny_McCarthy" title="Jenny McCarthy">Jenny McCarthy</a>. She has made two guest appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Lately" title="Chelsea Lately">Chelsea Lately</a></i>. On December 15, 2009, Remini appeared as Carrie Heffernan on <i><a href="/wiki/Lopez_Tonight" title="Lopez Tonight">Lopez Tonight</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/George_Lopez" title="George Lopez">George Lopez</a> as <a href="/wiki/Doug_Heffernan" title="Doug Heffernan">Doug Heffernan</a> in an episode reuniting the cast of the <i><a href="/wiki/George_Lopez_(TV_series)" title="George Lopez (TV series)">George Lopez</a></i> sitcom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Remini and <a href="/wiki/Holly_Robinson_Peete" title="Holly Robinson Peete">Holly Robinson Peete</a> appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i> on July 28, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2011, it was announced that Remini signed a talent development deal at <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> and <a href="/wiki/ABC_Studios" title="ABC Studios">ABC Studios</a> that required the network and the studio to develop a comedy project for Remini to star in and produce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In March 2012, it was announced that Remini would star in an ABC comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Tools" title="Family Tools">Family Tools</a></i>, a remake of the UK comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/White_Van_Man_(TV_series)" title="White Van Man (TV series)">White Van Man</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The series premiered on May 1, 2013, and was not renewed owing to low ratings, ending its run on July 10, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 4 that year, it was announced publicly on <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i> that Remini would compete as a contestant on <a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._season_17)" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 17)">season 17</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. TV series)">Dancing with the Stars</a></i>, in which she was partnered with professional dancer <a href="/wiki/Tony_Dovolani" title="Tony Dovolani">Tony Dovolani</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The couple made it to the 10th week of competition and reached 5th place. Remini later returned in <a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._season_19)" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 19)">season 19</a> as a guest co-host on week 6. She returned as guest co-host on <a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._season_21)" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. season 21)">season 21</a> during weeks 6 and 7.</p>
<p>On November 22, 2013, it was announced that Remini joined the cast of the <a href="/wiki/TV_Land" title="TV Land">TV Land</a> comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Exes" title="The Exes">The Exes</a></i>, filling a recurring role starting in the third season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Remini and her husband Angelo starred in a reality television series titled <i><a href="/wiki/Leah_Remini:_It%27s_All_Relative" title="Leah Remini: It's All Relative">Leah Remini: It's All Relative</a></i>. The show focuses on Remini's family life. It premiered on <a href="/wiki/TLC_(TV_network)" title="TLC (TV network)">TLC</a> on July 10, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> According to Remini, the purpose of the show is to highlight the fact that she's a normal person just like everyone else and that celebrity doesn't change that.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Remini developed an eight-episode series for <a href="/wiki/A%26E_Networks" title="A&amp;E Networks">A&amp;E</a> focusing on ex-<a href="/wiki/Scientology" title="Scientology">Scientologists</a> speaking candidly about their experiences entitled <i>Leah Remini: Scientology and the Aftermath</i>. The show premiered November 29, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In a statement released by the network Leah said,</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>For too long, this multi-billion-dollar organization bullied victims and journalists to prevent the truth from being told. It is my hope that we shed light on information that makes the world aware of what is really going on and encourages others to speak up so the abuses can be ended forever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Talk"><i>The Talk</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leah_Remini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Talk">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Remini was a co-host on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Talk_(TV_series)" title="The Talk (TV series)">The Talk</a></i>, a CBS talk show which premiered October 18, 2010. <a href="/wiki/Julie_Chen" title="Julie Chen">Julie Chen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sara_Gilbert" title="Sara Gilbert">Sara Gilbert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holly_Robinson_Peete" title="Holly Robinson Peete">Holly Robinson Peete</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Osbourne" title="Sharon Osbourne">Sharon Osbourne</a> were her co-hosts. The show is similar to <i><a href="/wiki/The_View_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The View (U.S. TV series)">The View</a></i> and seeks to address motherhood and contemporary issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Remini drew criticism within the first week of the show's debut, accused of being too loud, brash, and obtrusive. It was stated on one episode that she had received a significant amount of negative Twitter feedback on this subject. Remini addressed the issue, stating she refused to change who she is and asserting that thousands of fans like her for who she is. Her co-hosts supported her response.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> After the first season ended, Remini was not asked to return to the show. Just weeks after Remini was let go, Holly Robinson Peete was also informed that her contract was not being renewed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>A refusal to disclose why Remini and Peete were fired brought on a great deal of criticism of the show and protests from the stars' fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> While there was much speculation that their former <i>Talk</i> co-host, Julie Chen, had involvement in the decision, as she is married to CBS President/CEO <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Moonves" title="Leslie Moonves">Leslie Moonves</a>, it was Sharon Osbourne who stated, regarding their dismissals, in December 2011 on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Howard_Stern_Show" title="The Howard Stern Show">The Howard Stern Show</a></i>: "Some people don't really know who they are. And you have to know who you are when you're in something like this. You can't pretend to be something you're not. You have to know your brand. You can't be all things to everyone."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eonline.com_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-eonline.com-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chen_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chen-28">[28]</a></sup> Osbourne replied to criticism about the refusal to inform Remini and Peete why they were let go, rhetorically stating, "Why should we call them to discuss?"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eonline.com_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-eonline.com-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chen_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-Chen-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2012, a heavily publicized Twitter dispute ignited between Remini and Osbourne when Remini fired back at Osbourne for her comments on <i>The Howard Stern Show</i>. In response to questions from her Twitter followers, Remini tweeted:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Ask Sharon. She had us fired...Sharon thought me and Holly were too 'Ghetto'. (her words) we were not funny, awkward and didn't know ourselves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSN_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-MSN-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffingtonPost_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-HuffingtonPost-30">[30]</a></sup> Haters Gon' Hate. True. But, Haters have the balls to say they 'Hate'...not call themselves your friend. She had us fired all the while calling me and Holly her friend. Heartbreaking. Yes. She had us fired she told Howard Stern. Explains why she never called us back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSN_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-MSN-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In response, Osbourne tweeted, "I had absolutely nothing to do with her departure from the show and have no idea why she continues to take to Twitter to spread this false gossip."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSN_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-MSN-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Leah knows that I have never been in the position to hire or fire anyone on the show. That being said, my only wish is that Leah would just stop all this negative, unprofessional and childish behavior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffingtonPost_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-HuffingtonPost-30">[30]</a></sup> It's been seven months. It's time to move on. Leah is a very talented actress and I only wish her happiness and success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSN_29-3"><a href="#cite_note-MSN-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffingtonPost_30-2"><a href="#cite_note-HuffingtonPost-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> I know that better things are to come if she can just get beyond this negativity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSN_29-4"><a href="#cite_note-MSN-29">[29]</a></sup> Remini tweeted a challenge to Osbourne to establish in a court of law what statements she (Remini) had made that were untrue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSN_29-5"><a href="#cite_note-MSN-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>While co-hosting <i>The Talk</i>, Remini turned down various parts on situation comedies, stating that any new role would make her feel as if she were cheating on Doug Heffernan, her character's husband on <i>The King of Queens</i>. She added that she needs to spend more time with her child and family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leah_Remini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Remini met her husband, Angelo Pagán, at a Cuban restaurant in 1996. He has three sons from previous relationships. They were married on July 19, 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Their daughter, Sofia, was born on June 16, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beginning at age nine, Remini was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Scientology" title="Church of Scientology">Church of Scientology</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-People7.29.13_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-People7.29.13-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abcnews.go.com_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-abcnews.go.com-36">[36]</a></sup> In December 2005, she helped promote the gala opening of the <a href="/wiki/Citizens_Commission_on_Human_Rights" title="Citizens Commission on Human Rights">Citizens Commission on Human Rights</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Psychiatry:_An_Industry_of_Death" title="Psychiatry: An Industry of Death">Psychiatry: An Industry of Death</a></i> museum on <a href="/wiki/Sunset_Boulevard" title="Sunset Boulevard">Sunset Boulevard</a> in Hollywood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Responding to criticism of Scientology during an interview on <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a>, Remini said, "If somebody is going to get turned off about something because of what they read or heard, then that person's not smart enough to even enter a church. If you're really against something, then know what you're against."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2013, Remini left the Church of Scientology,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usmagazine.com_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-usmagazine.com-38">[38]</a></sup> owing to policies that forbid members from questioning the management of church leader <a href="/wiki/David_Miscavige" title="David Miscavige">David Miscavige</a>, which she believed was corrupt; the reported abuse of members of its <a href="/wiki/Sea_Org" title="Sea Org">Sea Org</a> religious order; its policy of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disconnection_(Scientology)" title="Disconnection (Scientology)">disconnection</a>"; and its practice of branding those who have left the church of their own accord as "<a href="/wiki/Suppressive_Person" title="Suppressive Person">Suppressive Persons</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nypost-0712_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-nypost-0712-39">[39]</a></sup> According to former high-ranking Sea Org member <a href="/wiki/Mike_Rinder" title="Mike Rinder">Mike Rinder</a>, Remini's problems with the Church began when she asked about the whereabouts of Miscavige's wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michelle_Miscavige" title="Michelle Miscavige">Shelly</a> at the 2006 wedding of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Cruise" title="Tom Cruise">Tom Cruise</a> and <a href="/wiki/Katie_Holmes" title="Katie Holmes">Katie Holmes</a>, and was told profanely that she lacked the rank to do so. Remini then filed a "knowledge report" that was critical of Miscavige, Cruise, and other senior Scientology members and behavior on the part of members that was inconsistent with Church rules. She was subsequently "subjected to years of 'interrogations' and 'thought modification'" that led to her being blacklisted within the Church, while fellow parishioners with whom she had been friends for decades wrote internal reports about her, resulting in a Church investigation into her family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nypost-0712_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-nypost-0712-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday-0712_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-0712-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>During a September 9, 2013, appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a></i>, Remini discussed her departure from the Church and the loss of friends who are still in the Church and, according to Remini, are not permitted to have contact with her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usmagazine.com_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-usmagazine.com-38">[38]</a></sup> Following her departure from the Church, she publicly expressed her appreciation for those who supported her departure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Remini's sister, Nicole, who had earlier left the Church herself,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> revealed that the rest of their family left the Church along with her to avoid being split up by the Church's disconnection policy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Writer and director <a href="/wiki/Paul_Haggis" title="Paul Haggis">Paul Haggis</a>, who had previously been the most famous person to publicly disavow Scientology, wrote an open letter, published by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i>, thanking Remini for standing by him after he left the Church and praised her "enormous amount of integrity and compassion".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2013, it was disclosed that Remini had filed a missing person report with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Police_Department" title="Los Angeles Police Department">Los Angeles Police Department</a> concerning the disappearance of Shelly Miscavige.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> After the report was filed, the Los Angeles Police Department looked into the matter, allegedly met with Shelly in person, closed the investigation, and said Remini's report was "unfounded". The Church said in a statement that the whole affair was simply harassment and a publicity stunt for Remini.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2013, it was reported that Remini had been subpoenaed to testify in a <a href="/wiki/Comal_County,_Texas" title="Comal County, Texas">Comal County, Texas</a> lawsuit against the Church of Scientology and its leader David Miscavige, regarding acts of alleged harassment and surveillance against the wife of a former member, Monique Rathbun (who was married to ex-Scientology executive <a href="/wiki/Mark_Rathbun" title="Mark Rathbun">Mark Rathbun</a>). Rathbun's attorney, Ray Jeffery, says he wants Remini, a former Scientologist, to give a <a href="/wiki/Deposition_(law)" title="Deposition (law)">deposition</a> in the hopes she could testify that Miscavige has vast influence over the operations of the church and had to have known about the alleged harassment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abcnews.go.com_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-abcnews.go.com-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>Remini released her memoir <i>Troublemaker: Surviving Hollywood and Scientology</i> on November 3, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> In a 2015 interview with <i><a href="/wiki/People_(magazine)" title="People (magazine)">People</a></i> magazine, Remini stated that she was embracing <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholicism</a> and found comfort in the religion's practices, contrasting her experiences with Scientology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmag_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmag-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
