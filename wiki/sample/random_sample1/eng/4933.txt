<p><b>Lyantonde District</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Uganda" title="Districts of Uganda">district</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Central_Region,_Uganda" title="Central Region, Uganda">Central Uganda</a>. It is named after the 'chief town' of the district, <a href="/wiki/Lyantonde" title="Lyantonde">Lyantonde</a>, where the district headquarters are located.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lyantonde_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lyantonde District is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Sembabule_District" title="Sembabule District">Sembabule District</a> to the north and northeast, <a href="/wiki/Lwengo_District" title="Lwengo District">Lwengo District</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/Rakai_District" title="Rakai District">Rakai District</a> to the south, and <a href="/wiki/Kiruhura_District" title="Kiruhura District">Kiruhura District</a> to the west.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The 'chief town' of the district, <a href="/wiki/Lyantonde" title="Lyantonde">Lyantonde</a>, is located approximately 78 kilometres (48 mi), by road, west of the city of <a href="/wiki/Masaka" title="Masaka">Masaka</a>, the largest metropolitan area in the sub-region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The coordinates of the district are: 00 25S, 31 10E.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lyantonde_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district is composed on one county, <i>Kabula County</i>. Prior to 2007, <i>Kabula County</i> was part of <a href="/wiki/Rakai_District" title="Rakai District">Rakai District</a>. In 2007, the county was split off of Rakai District and given autonomous district status.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lyantonde_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1991, the national population census estimated the population of the district at about 53,100. During the next census in 2002, the population of Lyantonde District was estimated at about 66,000 with an annual population growth rate of 1.9%. In 2012 the district population was estimated at about 80,200.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_activities">Economic activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lyantonde_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economic activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Cattle are the main livestock kept by farmers in Lyantonde. It is estimated that there are 83,700 of cattle in the District. Other animals that are raised include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goats" title="Goats">goats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheep" title="Sheep">sheep</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pigs" title="Pigs">pigs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poultry" title="Poultry">poultry</a>.</p>
