<p><b>Lianne Charlotte Barnes</b> (born 23 August 1989),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stevenson_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stevenson-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Lianne La Havas</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>-born British singer, songwriter and multi-instrumentalist. Her career began after being introduced to various musicians and singer <a href="/wiki/Paloma_Faith" title="Paloma Faith">Paloma Faith</a>, for whom she sang backing vocals. In 2010, La Havas signed to <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>, spending two years developing her songwriting skills before releasing any music publicly. La Havas' debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Is_Your_Love_Big_Enough%3F" title="Is Your Love Big Enough?">Is Your Love Big Enough?</a></i> (2012), was released to positive reviews from critics and earned her a nomination for the BBC's <a href="/wiki/Sound_of..." title="Sound of...">Sound of 2012</a> poll and awards for the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> Album of The Year 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-so12_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-so12-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianne_La_Havas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>La Havas was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London,_England" title="London, England">London, England</a>, to a <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> father and <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaican</a> mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She was raised in <a href="/wiki/Tooting" title="Tooting">Tooting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Streatham" title="Streatham">Streatham</a>, spending the majority of her time with her grandparents after her parents separated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thisislondon.co.uk_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-thisislondon.co.uk-6">[6]</a></sup> La Havas began singing at seven, and cites her parents' diverse musical tastes as having the biggest influence on her music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Her father, an accomplished multi-instrumentalist, taught her the basics of guitar and piano. Lianne wrote her first song at the age of 11, but did not learn to play the guitar until she was 18 years old. Lianne sang in her school choir.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/Norbury_Manor_Business_and_Enterprise_College_for_Girls" title="Norbury Manor Business and Enterprise College for Girls">Norbury Manor Business and Enterprise College for Girls</a> in <a href="/wiki/Thornton_Heath" title="Thornton Heath">Thornton Heath</a> where she studied art <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A-level" title="A-level">A-level</a>, and had planned to take an art foundation course before she decided to leave college to pursue a career in music full-time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thisislondon.co.uk_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-thisislondon.co.uk-6">[6]</a></sup> Although born Lianne Barnes, her stage name is an adaptation derived from her Greek father Henry Vlahavas's surname. She lives in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stevenson_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stevenson-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianne_La_Havas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginnings_and_debut">Beginnings and debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianne_La_Havas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Beginnings and debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lianne_La_Havas_2012.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="612" data-file-width="542" height="226" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Lianne_La_Havas_2012.jpg/200px-Lianne_La_Havas_2012.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Lianne_La_Havas_2012.jpg/300px-Lianne_La_Havas_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Lianne_La_Havas_2012.jpg/400px-Lianne_La_Havas_2012.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>Whilst attending sixth-form in Croydon, a friend of La Havas', the singer and songwriter Allan Rose (who had attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brit_School" title="Brit School">Brit School</a>), introduced her to other musicians who would assist La Havas in the recording of her first demos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thisislondon.co.uk_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-thisislondon.co.uk-6">[6]</a></sup> Through that same friend La Havas was also introduced to British singer <a href="/wiki/Paloma_Faith" title="Paloma Faith">Paloma Faith</a>; she later sang backing vocals on tour for Faith. La Havas was discovered on Myspace in 2008. Duncan Ellis, owner of Scruffy Bird Artist Management, first heard of La Havas through colleague Alex Hardee of the Coda Music Agency, and it was love at first scroll as Ellis skimmed the material on her Myspace page.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup> When Ellis first saw La Havas perform, she was in the duo the Paris Parade, collaborating with future Elephant member Christian Pinchebeck (who designed the artwork for <i>Lost &amp; Found</i> and is also now part of the duo Memphis Industries duo Elephant); their short-lived career would kick off La Havas' career in commercial music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2010, Lianne signed to <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a>, spending two years developing her songwriting skills before releasing any music publicly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thisisfakediy.co.uk_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-thisisfakediy.co.uk-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her first <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i>Lost &amp; Found</i> was released on 21 October 2011 on the Labour of Love label,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> featuring <a href="/wiki/Willy_Mason" title="Willy Mason">Willy Mason</a> on the opening track "No Room For Doubt".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> That same month, La Havas released the live EP <i>Live From LA</i>, which was made available for free download on her website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> La Havas made her television debut on 21 October 2011 broadcast of <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Later%E2%80%A6_with_Jools_Holland" title="Later… with Jools Holland">Later… with Jools Holland</a></i>, a programme that also featured Wisconsin folk band <a href="/wiki/Bon_Iver" title="Bon Iver">Bon Iver</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> soon after this on 25 October 2011 it was announced that La Havas would be the supporting act for <a href="/wiki/Bon_Iver" title="Bon Iver">Bon Iver</a>'s December 2011 North American tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her official debut single "Lost &amp; Found" was released in the UK on 30 April 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Is_Your_Love_Big_Enough%3F" title="Is Your Love Big Enough?">Is Your Love Big Enough?</a></i> was released on 9 July 2012 on Warner Brothers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thisisfakediy.co.uk_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-thisisfakediy.co.uk-10">[10]</a></sup> At the end of 2012, <i>Is Your Love Big Enough?</i> was named iTunes Album of the Year. On 24 September 2012, September La Havas supported <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a> at MTV 'Crashes' Manchester, a live performance in front of 1,000 people in Manchester Cathedral, which was broadcast in 164 countries. On 31 December 2012, she appeared on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a>'s New Year's Eve show <i>Jools' Annual Hootenanny</i> singing <i>Cow Cow Boogie</i>. On 9 June 2013, La Havas played at the <a href="/wiki/RockNess" title="RockNess">RockNess</a> music festival in <a href="/wiki/Inverness" title="Inverness">Inverness</a>, Scotland,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STV_rockness_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-STV_rockness-19">[19]</a></sup> and on 30 June 2013 La Havas performed at the <a href="/wiki/Glastonbury_Festival_2013" title="Glastonbury Festival 2013">Glastonbury Festival 2013</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph_glast13_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph_glast13-20">[20]</a></sup> La Havas also performed at the Isle of Wight Festival in June 2013.</p>
<p>In early 2014, <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a> played a gig in her living room in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> She also appeared on <a href="/wiki/Alt-J" title="Alt-J">Alt-J</a>'s 2014 album <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_All_Yours" title="This Is All Yours">This Is All Yours</a></i> on the track "Warm Foothills". On 30 September Prince released <i><a href="/wiki/Art_Official_Age" title="Art Official Age">Art Official Age</a></i>, featuring La Havas heavily through the album, singing in "Clouds" and providing vocals on "Affirmation I&amp;II", "Way Back Home" and "Affirmation III".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> La Havas performed with <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a> on an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i> as a featured vocalist on 3 November 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> She collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Aqualung_(musician)" title="Aqualung (musician)">Aqualung</a> on the song "Eggshells" from his album <i>10 Features</i>, released on 19 January 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015:_Blood">2015: <i>Blood</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lianne_La_Havas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015: Blood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After touring was completed for her first album, La Havas and her mother travelled to Jamaica. During the trip, La Havas was inspired to write new material, which would eventually become part of her second album. She gave a live performance for her family with producer, <a href="/wiki/Di_Genius" title="Di Genius">Stephen McGregor</a>, who would later co-produce her forthcoming album. Many of the songs were inspired by La Havas' reconnection to her Greek and Jamaican heritage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The first single, "Unstoppable", from this album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_(Lianne_La_Havas_album)" title="Blood (Lianne La Havas album)">Blood</a></i>, premiered for streaming on 1 April 2015. The single, which was co-written with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Epworth" title="Paul Epworth">Paul Epworth</a>, was officially released in the UK on 1 June 2015. Its music video premiered on 12 May 2015 on La Havas' official YouTube channel. <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_(Lianne_La_Havas_album)" title="Blood (Lianne La Havas album)">Blood</a></i> will be released on 31 July 2015 for physical and digital formats. La Havas will embark on a tour in the UK and Europe in support of album from mid-May to September 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> La Havas features twice on <a href="/wiki/Rudimental" title="Rudimental">Rudimental</a>'s album, <i><a href="/wiki/We_the_Generation" title="We the Generation">We the Generation</a></i> (2015), on the songs "Needn't Speak" and "Breath". She appeared on the popular "The Late Show with Stephen Colbert" on CBS in the United States on October 27, 2015, singing with Colbert's regular band, New Orleans jazz bandleader <a href="/wiki/Jon_Batiste" title="Jon Batiste">Jon Batiste</a> and Stay Human.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>La Havas plays a hollow-bodied 1964 <a href="/wiki/Harmony_Company" title="Harmony Company">Harmony</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stratotone" title="Stratotone">Alden Stratotone</a> guitar, which is put through a <a href="/wiki/Fender_Musical_Instruments_Corporation" title="Fender Musical Instruments Corporation">Fender</a> Hot Rod Deluxe amp on stage with her band. For solo gigs she uses a SansAmp DI'd, and a <a href="/wiki/Roland_CUBE" title="Roland CUBE">Roland CUBE</a> amp is used for practice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
