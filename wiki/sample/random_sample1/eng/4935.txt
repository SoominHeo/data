<p><b>Lija</b> (<a href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese</a>: <span lang="mt" xml:lang="mt"><i>Ħal Lija</i></span>) is a small village in the <a href="/wiki/Central_Region,_Malta" title="Central Region, Malta">Central Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>. It forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Three_villages_of_Malta" title="Three villages of Malta">Three villages of Malta</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Attard" title="Attard">Attard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Balzan" title="Balzan">Balzan</a>. Lija has a baroque <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a> and seven other small chapels. The parish church is dedicated to Our Saviour and is currently undergoing an extensive restoration project in order to save priceless art work and <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Cal%C3%AC" title="Giuseppe Calì">Calì</a> paintings that are found surrounding the church's ceilings. Lija became a parish in 1594, after the small community detached itself from the neighbouring town, <a href="/wiki/Birkirkara" title="Birkirkara">Birkirkara</a>. It has a population of 3,070 as of March 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>Lija is mostly known for its <a href="/wiki/Fireworks" title="Fireworks">fireworks</a> displays that attract thousands of locals and tourists during the festa period held in the first week of August. These displays are very well renowned and claimed to be the best around the island. The Lija fireworks team also won an international fireworks competition held in <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a> back in 1980. In 2006, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lija_fireworks_factory&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lija fireworks factory (page does not exist)">Lija fireworks factory</a> placed second in a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fireworks_festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fireworks festival (page does not exist)">Fireworks festival</a> organised at the Grand Harbour in Malta's capital, <a href="/wiki/Valletta" title="Valletta">Valletta</a>.</p>
<p>Lija also hosts the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Maltese_Citrus_festival&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Maltese Citrus festival (page does not exist)">Maltese Citrus festival</a>, held each year.</p>
<p>Lija has several old <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=House_of_character&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="House of character (page does not exist)">houses of character</a> and large <a href="/wiki/Citrus" title="Citrus">citrus</a> gardens. The <a href="/wiki/Lija_Belvedere_Tower" title="Lija Belvedere Tower">Lija Belvedere Tower</a> and <a href="/wiki/Villa_Francia" title="Villa Francia">Villa Francia</a> are a main attraction. The official University Residence of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Malta" title="University of Malta">University of Malta</a> is also situated in this village.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lija_Athletic_F.C." title="Lija Athletic F.C.">Lija Athletic F.C.</a> is the village's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_club" title="Football club">football club</a>. This club has been promoted to Malta's Premier (highest) Division for three times in its history. Considering the small size of the village and the resource and financial limitations, this was deemed to be a huge success by local sport enthusiasts. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lija_Athletic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lija Athletic (page does not exist)">Lija Athletic</a>, with the collaboration of the Local Council inaugurated a new synthetic pitch on July 6, 2007 (<i>Jum Ħal Lija</i> or "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lija_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lija Day (page does not exist)">Lija Day</a>"). This pitch is situated in the village's primary school grounds. This year, 2009, the Club is celebrating its 60th Anniversary since its foundation.</p>
<p>British author <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Burgess" title="Anthony Burgess">Anthony Burgess</a>, whose works include <i><a href="/wiki/A_Clockwork_Orange_(novel)" title="A Clockwork Orange (novel)">A Clockwork Orange</a></i>, resided in Lija for three years (1968-1970). At Lija there is also <a href="/wiki/Villa_Parisio" title="Villa Parisio">Villa Parisio</a>, where politician <a href="/wiki/Mabel_Strickland" title="Mabel Strickland">Mabel Strickland</a>, daughter of Lord <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gerald_Strickland" title="Gerald Strickland">Gerald Strickland</a>, lived.</p>
