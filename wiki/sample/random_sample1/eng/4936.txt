<p><b>Leon Julian Brendan Best</b> (born 19 September 1986) is a professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> for <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a>. He has played for the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland national football team</a>. Best represented Ireland at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_under-21_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland under-21 national football team">under-21 level</a> and won his first full international cap against <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a> on 29 May 2009. Best had a youth career at <a href="/wiki/Notts_County_F.C." title="Notts County F.C.">Notts County</a>. His first professional club was then Premier League club <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a>. He has also played for <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> before his 2012 move to <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Best was born on 19 September 1986 in <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. He opted to play football for his mother's country, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>, while in his teens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Southampton">Southampton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Southampton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a young player, Best started his career at <a href="/wiki/Notts_County_F.C." title="Notts County F.C.">Notts County</a> before being signed as a trainee by then <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a>. He made his professional debut against <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> on his 18th birthday in 2004. A loan spell at <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> followed later in the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_in_English_football" title="2004–05 in English football">2004–05</a> season.</p>
<p>He was a member of Southampton's youth team that reached the final of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FA_Youth_Cup_Final_Squads#2004.2F05:_Ipswich_Town_v._Southampton_.281-0.2C_2-2.29" title="FA Youth Cup Final Squads">FA Youth Cup in 2005</a>, losing on aggregate to <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a>.</p>
<p>Best also had two loan spells at <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005-06_in_English_football" title="2005-06 in English football">2005–06</a> season, scoring his first professional goal and Wednesday's first of the season on 9 August 2005 at <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_Stadium" title="Hillsborough Stadium">Hillsborough</a> against <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>. The first loan spell was cut short in the same match when Best broke his foot and returned to <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Stadium" title="St Mary's Stadium">St. Mary's</a> after only 5 days in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>. After recovering with Southampton, Best then returned to Hillsborough at the end of the January transfer window (though there was also speculation he would go to <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a>) where he remained until the end of the season, scoring Wednesday's last goal of the season at <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> on 30 April 2006.</p>
<p>On 3 August 2006 Best joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">Football League One</a> club <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a> on a month-long loan deal with an option to extend his stay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He returned to Southampton at the end of October at the end of the maximum 3-month loan spell, having made 15 league appearances, scoring 3 goals.</p>
<p>On 23 November 2006, he joined his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Martin_Cranie" title="Martin Cranie">Martin Cranie</a> on loan to <a href="/wiki/Yeovil_Town_F.C." title="Yeovil Town F.C.">Yeovil Town</a> until 22 February 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After his loan spells, Best returned to play a role in Southampton's qualification for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Play-Offs" title="Football League Play-Offs">play-offs</a>, where they faced <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a>. He came on as a substitute in the first leg at St Marys, which Southampton lost 2–1. In the second leg Best started alongside on-loan striker <a href="/wiki/Marek_Saganowski" title="Marek Saganowski">Marek Saganowski</a>. The game ended in a tie on aggregate, with Best scoring an <a href="/wiki/Own_goal" title="Own goal">own goal</a> which levelled the score at 2–2 on the night. Southampton lost 4–3 in the ensuing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout_(association_football)" title="Penalty shootout (association football)">penalty shootout</a>, with Best missing the opening penalty.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coventry_City">Coventry City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Coventry City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 5 July 2007, he joined <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> for a fee of £650,000 decided by a tribunal,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> with the fee increasing were Coventry to gain promotion to the Premiership by 2010. Following a cheekbone fracture at the end of 2008, Best took his place in the team wearing a protective mask. His return led him scoring three goals in successive matches and consequently gaining a reputation for wearing a "Besty" mask, even after he had recovered from his injury and earning the nickname "Zorro".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> His distinctive mask became somewhat of a talisman for Coventry City fans, many of whom took to wearing similar masks in the stands as a tribute to Best.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On 3 October 2009 he was named Championship Player of the Month for September.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newcastle_United">Newcastle United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Newcastle United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He signed for <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> on 1 February 2010 for a fee of £1,500,000 on a three and a half year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Best made his debut against <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> at <a href="/wiki/St_James%27_Park" title="St James' Park">St James' Park</a> on 5 February 2010. Best failed to make an impact in his first season at Newcastle as he failed to find a goal and was put behind on form strike duo <a href="/wiki/Andy_Carroll" title="Andy Carroll">Andy Carroll</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_L%C3%B8venkrands" title="Peter Løvenkrands">Peter Løvenkrands</a>.</p>
<p>Best netted his first goal for Newcastle in a pre-season friendly against <a href="/wiki/Carlisle_United_F.C." title="Carlisle United F.C.">Carlisle United</a> on 17 July 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> followed by his second goal for the club in the pre-season <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Bobby_Robson" title="Sir Bobby Robson">Sir Bobby Robson</a> tribute match against <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a> on 31 July 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> However Best then received a <a href="/wiki/Cruciate_ligament" title="Cruciate ligament">cruciate</a> injury during pre-season causing him to miss the first few months of the 2010–11 season.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He was back in light training by the end of September and was on the bench for Newcastle's 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> on 28 November, but did not make an appearance. He made his comeback in Newcastle's 1–0 victory against <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> on 2 January 2011, appearing as a late substitute for <a href="/wiki/Shola_Ameobi" title="Shola Ameobi">Shola Ameobi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Best made his first <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> start for Newcastle in a home 5–0 win against <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> on 5 January 2011, scoring a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Premier_League_hat-tricks" title="List of Premier League hat-tricks">hat-trick</a> in the process, his first league goals for the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He was injured prior to the <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke</a> away game on 19 March and was ruled out until the end of the season. However, Best had scored six goals in 10 starts.</p>
<p>On 28 August 2011, he scored two goals against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> to give Newcastle a 2–1 victory followed by the equalizer in the 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>, extending the Magpies unbeaten run to eight games in the English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Best then ended a run of 12 games without a goal by scoring the winner in Newcastle's 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blackburn_Rovers">Blackburn Rovers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Blackburn Rovers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 July 2012, Best signed for <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> on a four-year contract for a fee of more than £3 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Less than a month after signing for Blackburn Rovers, he suffered an injury to his anterior cruciate ligaments, ruling him out for at least six months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Best scored his first league goal in the 89th minute, when he volleyed in a <a href="/wiki/Chris_Taylor_(footballer,_born_1986)" title="Chris Taylor (footballer, born 1986)">Chris Taylor</a> cross for Blackburn when he came on as a first-half substitute for the injured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruben_Rochina" title="Ruben Rochina">Ruben Rochina</a> in a 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> at <a href="/wiki/Pride_Park" title="Pride Park">Pride Park</a> on 4 August 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Best was released by Rovers on 8 July 2015 by mutual consent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_spells">Loan spells</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Loan spells">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 14 February 2014, he was loaned out by Rovers to their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">SkyBet Championship</a> rivals <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a>, this was to be his third spell with the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> club having previously been on loan at <a href="/wiki/Hillsborough_Stadium" title="Hillsborough Stadium">Hillsborough</a> in 2005 and 2006. He scored his first goal in his third spell at the club in a 2–1 <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> defeat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic" title="Charlton Athletic">Charlton Athletic</a> on 24 February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On 14 March 2014, Best extended his loan with the <i>Owls</i> until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_in_English_football" title="2013–14 in English football">2013–14</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Best scored his first league goal of his third spell with the owls on 15 March 2014, helping with a 4–1 victory over Birmingham City.</p>
<p>On 4 August 2014, Best was sent to <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> on a season-long loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Derby_County_Sign_Striker_Leon_Best_On_Loan_From_Blackburn_Rovers_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Derby_County_Sign_Striker_Leon_Best_On_Loan_From_Blackburn_Rovers-24">[24]</a></sup> He returned to Blackburn at the end of his loan spell with Derby before joining <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a> on loan. He made thirteen appearances without scoring for Brighton before returning to Blackburn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rotherham_United">Rotherham United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Rotherham United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Best joined <a href="/wiki/Rotherham_United_F.C." title="Rotherham United F.C.">Rotherham United</a> on 16 November 2015 until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 March 2016, Best led an astonishing fight back against promotion chasing <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> at <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stadium" title="New York Stadium">New York Stadium</a>. Coming on as a second-half substitute with his team losing 3–0, he saw his team mate <a href="/wiki/Danny_Ward_(footballer,_born_1990)" title="Danny Ward (footballer, born 1990)">Danny Ward</a> score in the 82nd minute then scored two goals in the 88th and 90th minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On 3 June 2016, Best left the club, having failed to agree terms on a new contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ipswich_Town">Ipswich Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ipswich Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Best joined <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a> on 30 August 2016 as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Daryl_Murphy" title="Daryl Murphy">Daryl Murphy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> He struggled to keep his place in the team, and following his performance on 17 January 2017 in an <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> tie against non-league <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_City_F.C." title="Lincoln City F.C.">Lincoln City</a>, where Ipswich were beaten 1–0,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mick_McCarthy" title="Mick McCarthy">Mick McCarthy</a> stated that Best would never play for Ipswich Town again. “I told him after the Lincoln game that he is not part of my plans here. He’s still with us and may have to have something done with his groin. We will see but he won’t be playing for us.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Best qualifies to play for the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a> through his mother, who is from <a href="/wiki/Bluebell,_Dublin" title="Bluebell, Dublin">Bluebell, Dublin</a>. He also lived in Dublin from the age of seven, playing for Lourdes Celtic before returning to Nottingham.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Having already represented the Republic of Ireland firstly at <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_under-17_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national under-17 football team">under-17</a> level in 2002 and later at <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national under-21 football team">under-21</a> level in 2008, Best was called up to the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a> senior squad for the first time for the games against <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a> and then the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2010 FIFA World Cup qualifier</a> against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a> in May 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He made his debut on 29 May against Nigeria in a match that finished in a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leon_Best&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Best's sister had a baby with <a href="/wiki/Jamaica_national_football_team" title="Jamaica national football team">Jamaica</a> international footballer, <a href="/wiki/Wes_Morgan" title="Wes Morgan">Wes Morgan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leon_Best_roars:_Bring_on_Nottingham_Forest_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leon_Best_roars:_Bring_on_Nottingham_Forest-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
