<p>The <i><b>Himno de Riego</b></i> ("Anthem of <a href="/wiki/Rafael_del_Riego" title="Rafael del Riego">Riego</a>") is a song dating from the <a href="/wiki/Trienio_Liberal" title="Trienio Liberal">Liberal Triennium</a> and named in honour of Colonel <a href="/wiki/Rafael_del_Riego" title="Rafael del Riego">Rafael del Riego</a>, composed by <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Melchor_Gomis" title="José Melchor Gomis">José Melchor Gomis</a>. It was the <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> of Spain during the <a href="/wiki/Trienio_Liberal" title="Trienio Liberal">Trienio Liberal</a> (1820–1823) and the <a href="/wiki/First_Spanish_Republic" title="First Spanish Republic">First</a> (1873–1874) and <a href="/wiki/Second_Spanish_Republic" title="Second Spanish Republic">Second Spanish Republics</a> (1931–1939).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2003_Davis_Cup_controversy">2003 Davis Cup controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Himno_de_Riego&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 2003 Davis Cup controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Davis_Cup" title="Davis Cup">Davis Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> finals held in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> in 2003, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Morrison_(musician)" title="James Morrison (musician)">James Morrison</a> performed <i>El himno de Riego</i> instead of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>'s current national anthem, the <a href="/wiki/Marcha_Real" title="Marcha Real">Marcha Real</a> ("Royal march"). Australian officials claimed there was an error on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CD" title="CD">CD</a> provided to the musician, but Spanish sport authorities still issued an official protest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Satirical_lyrics">Satirical lyrics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Himno_de_Riego&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Satirical lyrics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This alternative version was extremely popular amongst Spaniards, particularly in <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>. The rudeness of the lyrics reflects the dislike of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Second_Republic" title="Spanish Second Republic">Republican Spain</a> for the church and the monarchy. After the war, these lyrics continued to be sung, by <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Franco</a>'s detractors and enemies. Such was the popularity of these lyrics that even today many people, particularly younger ones, think they are in fact the official ones.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
