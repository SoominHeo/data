<p><b>Riek Machar Teny Dhurgon</b> (born 1953) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudanese</a> politician who served as the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_South_Sudan" title="Vice President of South Sudan">Vice President of South Sudan</a>, from its independence in 2011 until his dismissal in 2013. Between April and July 2016 Machar served as the <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_South_Sudan" title="Vice President of South Sudan">First Vice President of South Sudan</a>. He now leads the rebel faction opposing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salva_Kiir" title="Salva Kiir">Salva Kiir</a> known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SPLM-IO" title="SPLM-IO">SPLM-IO</a>.</p>
<p>Machar obtained a PhD in strategic planning in 1984 and then joined the rebel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sudan_People%27s_Liberation_Movement/Army" title="Sudan People's Liberation Movement/Army">Sudan People's Liberation Movement/Army</a> (SPLM/A) during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sudanese_Civil_War" title="Second Sudanese Civil War">Second Sudanese Civil War</a> (1983–2005). Riek fell out with the SPLM/A leader <a href="/wiki/John_Garang" title="John Garang">John Garang</a> in 1991 and formed a splinter group, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SPLM/A-Nasir" title="SPLM/A-Nasir">SPLM/A-Nasir</a>. In 1997 Riek made a treaty with the Government of Sudan and became head of the government-backed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Sudan_Defense_Force" title="South Sudan Defense Force">South Sudan Defense Force</a> (SSDF). In 2000 he left the SSDF and formed a new militia, the <a href="/wiki/Sudan_People%27s_Defense_Forces/Democratic_Front" title="Sudan People's Defense Forces/Democratic Front">Sudan People's Defense Forces/Democratic Front</a> (SPDF), and in 2002 rejoined the SPLA as a senior commander. After the death of John Garang in July 2005, Riek Machar became vice-president of the autonomous Southern Sudan. He became vice-president of South Sudan on 9 July 2011 when the country became independent, but was dismissed from office by President <a href="/wiki/Salva_Kiir_Mayardit" title="Salva Kiir Mayardit">Salva Kiir Mayardit</a> on 23 July 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riek_Machar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Riek Machar Teny Dhurgon was born in <a href="/wiki/Leer,_South_Sudan" title="Leer, South Sudan">Leer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unity_State" title="Unity State">Unity State</a> in 1953, the 26th son of the chief of <a href="/wiki/Ayod" title="Ayod">Ayod</a> and Leer. He was brought up as a member of the Presbyterian church.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEManyang2005_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEManyang2005-2">[2]</a></sup> Machar belongs to the Dok section of the <a href="/wiki/Nuer_people" title="Nuer people">Nuer people</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchleeWatson200944_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchleeWatson200944-4">[4]</a></sup> He trained as an engineer at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khartoum_University" title="Khartoum University">Khartoum University</a>, and obtained a PhD in Philosophy and strategic planning from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bradford" title="University of Bradford">University of Bradford</a> in 1984.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArcuil2006_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArcuil2006-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEEdgerton2004128_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEdgerton2004128-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Machar has been called a <i>tuut dhoali/Doth in English</i>, which may be translated "adult boy", meaning uninitiated and literate. He has tried to transcend tribal divisions, and at one time attempted to ban initiation marks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFeyissa2011170_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFeyissa2011170-7">[7]</a></sup> However, in his struggle with <a href="/wiki/John_Garang" title="John Garang">John Garang</a> he exploited ethnic rivalries between the Nuer and <a href="/wiki/Dinka_people" title="Dinka people">Dinka</a> people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFeyissa2011204_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFeyissa2011204-8">[8]</a></sup> Machar married <a href="/wiki/Emma_McCune" title="Emma McCune">Emma McCune</a>, a British aid worker. She died in a car accident in <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a> in 1993 at the age of 29, while pregnant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirkus_Reviews_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirkus_Reviews-9">[9]</a></sup> Machar's second wife, <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Teny" title="Angelina Teny">Angelina Teny</a>, is one of the leading women politicians in South Sudan. She was state minister of Energy and Mining in the transitional government (2005–2010).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEAngelina_Teny..._10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAngelina_Teny...-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Machar was a rebel leader with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sudan_People%27s_Liberation_Army/Movement" title="Sudan People's Liberation Army/Movement">Sudan People's Liberation Army/Movement</a> (SPLM/A) headed by <a href="/wiki/John_Garang" title="John Garang">John Garang</a> from 1984 until he fell out with Garang in 1991. As Zonal Commander of Western Upper Nile, in 1986 he entered into an agreement with <a href="/wiki/Baggara" title="Baggara">Baggara</a> chiefs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone200315_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone200315-11">[11]</a></sup> Machar led forces that attacked and overran <a href="/wiki/Melut" title="Melut">Melut</a> in 1989. That year he was able to visit his family, which was based in Britain, for the first time since the civil war started. In 1990 Machar was based at Leer. Later he was appointed SPLA Regional Commander for a region that extended from the Ethiopian border in the east to <a href="/wiki/Renk,_South_Sudan" title="Renk, South Sudan">Renk</a> in the north and to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ayod,_South_Sudan" title="Ayod, South Sudan">Ayod</a> and <a href="/wiki/Waat" title="Waat">Waat</a> in the south.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEManyang2005_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEManyang2005-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Machar disagreed with the SPLA leader John Garang over objectives. Where John Garang at first wanted a secular and democratic but united Sudan in which the southerners would have full representation, Machar wanted a fully independent South Sudan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELittle2007192_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELittle2007192-12">[12]</a></sup> In August 1991 Riek Machar, <a href="/wiki/Lam_Akol" title="Lam Akol">Lam Akol</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gordon_Kong" title="Gordon Kong">Gordon Kong</a> announced that John Garang had been ejected from the SPLM.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJohnson2003202_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohnson2003202-13">[13]</a></sup> Kong Chuol is from the Eastern Jikany Nuer and Lam Akol is from the <a href="/wiki/Shilluk_people" title="Shilluk people">Shilluk people</a>. The breakaway faction, based in <a href="/wiki/Nasir,_South_Sudan" title="Nasir, South Sudan">Nasir</a> until 1995 and then in Waat and Ayod, was called the SPLM/A-Nasir faction from 1991 to 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone20038.E2.80.939_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone20038.E2.80.939-14">[14]</a></sup> As part of <a href="/wiki/SPLA-Nasir" title="SPLA-Nasir">SPLA-Nasir</a>, he was involved in the <a href="/wiki/Bor_massacre" title="Bor massacre">Bor massacre</a>, where 2000 mostly civilians were killed in Bor in 1991 while tens of thousands died in the following years from the resulting famine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The Bul Nuer Anyanya-2 militia at <a href="/wiki/Mayom,_South_Sudan" title="Mayom, South Sudan">Mayom</a> under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paulino_Matip" title="Paulino Matip">Paulino Matip</a> and the Lou Nuer Anyanya-2 militia at <a href="/wiki/Doleib_Hill" title="Doleib Hill">Doleib Hill</a> under <a href="/wiki/Yohannes_Yual" title="Yohannes Yual">Yohannes Yual</a> declared for Riek.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJohnson2003202_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohnson2003202-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kerubino_Kuanyin" title="Kerubino Kuanyin">Kerubino Kuanyin</a> and Faustino Atem Gualdit, Dinkas from Bahr el-Ghazal, had been among the founders of the SPLM but had fallen out with John Garang and had been jailed. They escaped and joined Machar in 1993, with their forces making an important addition to the formerly Nuer-dominated SPLA-Nasir. Kerubino became deputy Commander in Chief.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone1996318.E2.80.93319_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone1996318.E2.80.93319-16">[16]</a></sup> After this addition by forces from other ethnic groups, Riek's movement and force was called the SPLA-United from 1993 to 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone20038.E2.80.939_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone20038.E2.80.939-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 1993, President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daniel_Arap_Moi" title="Daniel Arap Moi">Daniel Arap Moi</a> of Kenya held separate talks with Garang and Riek Machar. In October 1993 the US Congress hosted a meeting between Garang and Machar. The two seemed to agree about various subjects related to a cease fire and reconciliation between the two factions, self-determination and opposition to the Khartoum regime, but Machar disputed Garang's authority and refused to sign a joint declaration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJohnson2003204_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohnson2003204-17">[17]</a></sup> Machar dismissed Lam Akol from the SPLA-United in February 1994. Lam Akol returned to <a href="/wiki/Kodok" title="Kodok">Kodok</a> in the government-held region of <a href="/wiki/Upper_Nile_(state)" title="Upper Nile (state)">Upper Nile</a> state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJohnson2003205_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohnson2003205-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1994 to 1997 Machar's movement was known as the South Sudan Independence Movement/Army (SSIM/A).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone200315_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone200315-11">[11]</a></sup> Although seeking independence for South Sudan, the group received covert support from the Government of Sudan as it fought the SPLA between 1991 and 1999 in attacks that became increasingly violent and ethnically motivated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone200316_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone200316-19">[19]</a></sup> Early in 1995 hostilities between the SSIM and SPLA, which had taken several thousands of civilian lives, were temporarily suspended. Machar dismissed Kerubino Kuanyin and Commander <a href="/wiki/William_Nyuon_Bany" title="William Nyuon Bany">William Nyuon Bany</a> from the SSIM on the basis that they had signed military and political agreements with the government of Sudan late in the previous year, and that they had attempted to form a government-supported faction in the SSIM.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone1996318_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone1996318-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the 1990s Machar skillfully developed support among the eastern Nuer, the Jikany and the Lou, taking advantage of SPLA unpopularity with the Jikany and drawing on prophetic tradition to make his case.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFeyissa2011206_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFeyissa2011206-21">[21]</a></sup> In 1996 Machar signed a Political Charter and in 1997 the <a href="/wiki/Khartoum_Peace_Agreement_of_1997" title="Khartoum Peace Agreement of 1997">Khartoum Peace Agreement</a> with the government. Under this agreement he was assistant to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omar_el-Bashir" title="Omar el-Bashir">Omar el-Bashir</a>, President of Sudan, and President of the Southern States Coordinating Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone200316_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone200316-19">[19]</a></sup> He was also made commander in chief of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Sudan_Defense_Force" title="South Sudan Defense Force">South Sudan Defense Force</a> (SSDF), which included most of the ex-rebels who had signed the Khartoum agreement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone200316_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone200316-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_SPLM">Return to SPLM</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riek_Machar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Return to SPLM">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There was growing tension between Riek Machar and Paulino Matip's South Sudan Unity Movement (SSUM), which became engaged in forcibly removing civilians from the <a href="/wiki/Block_5A,_South_Sudan" title="Block 5A, South Sudan">Block 5A</a> oil concession area, and assisting in clearances from other oil blocks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone200317_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone200317-22">[22]</a></sup> In 1998–1999, Matip's fighters and government troops clashed several times with Machar's SSDF forces in a struggle for control of the Unity state oilfields. Matip's fighters forced <a href="/wiki/Tito_Biel" title="Tito Biel">Tito Biel</a>, a high-ranking SSDF commander, to evacuate <a href="/wiki/Leer,_South_Sudan" title="Leer, South Sudan">Leer</a> early in 1999. Tito Biel later went over to the SPLA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPeace_from_within_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPeace_from_within-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Riek Machar's failure to prevent the government from forcibly displacing civilians from the oil-producing areas of <a href="/wiki/Unity_State" title="Unity State">Unity State</a> turned the Nuer against his leadership.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone200316_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone200316-19">[19]</a></sup> Machar's SSDF began to receive ammunition from the SPLA as of June 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEOil-Caused_Realignment_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEOil-Caused_Realignment-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2000 at a meeting of leaders in <a href="/wiki/Koch,_South_Sudan" title="Koch, South Sudan">Koch</a> he finally resigned from the government of Sudan and created a fresh militia named the <a href="/wiki/Sudan_People%27s_Defense_Forces/Democratic_Front" title="Sudan People's Defense Forces/Democratic Front">Sudan People's Defense Forces/Democratic Front</a> (SPDF).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone200316_19-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone200316-19">[19]</a></sup> At risk in his own homeland of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dok&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dok (page does not exist)">Dok</a> Nuer, Riek moved his base of operations to the eastern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jikany" title="Jikany">Jikany</a> area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchleeWatson200944_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchleeWatson200944-4">[4]</a></sup> In January 2002 he signed an agreement with John Garang to merge the SPDF into the SPLA, and was given command of the Dok Nuer within the SPLA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERone200316_19-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERone200316-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>The civil war ended in January 2005. In August Machar became Vice President of the Government of Southern Sudan and SPLM Co-Chair of the Joint Executive Political Committee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMiraya_FM_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMiraya_FM-25">[25]</a></sup> When South Sudan became independent, in July 2011 he was appointed first Vice President of the new republic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESoSaNews_13_July_2011_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESoSaNews_13_July_2011-26">[26]</a></sup> On 15 July 2011 Machar represented South Sudan at the ceremony when his country's flag was raised outside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_headquarters" title="United Nations headquarters">United Nations headquarters</a> in New York.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESudan_Tribune_15_July_2011_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESudan_Tribune_15_July_2011-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Sudan_politics">South Sudan politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riek_Machar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: South Sudan politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/South_Sudanese_independence_referendum,_2011" title="South Sudanese independence referendum, 2011">independence of South Sudan</a>, Machar was the vice president of the country. By February 2013 Machar publicly stated his intentions to challenge President Kiir. In July 2013, he, and the entire cabinet, was dismissed from office. Machar said that Kiir's move was a step towards dictatorship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> These events in turn led to the <a href="/wiki/South_Sudanese_Civil_War" title="South Sudanese Civil War">South Sudanese Civil War</a>.</p>
<p>In 2012, he publicly apologized for his part in the Bor massacre as he prepared to pave way for taking the helm of SPLM.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riek_Machar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Machar already known for his high degree of political and leadership ambition has revived links with the Government of Sudan in Khartoum for the purpose of receiving adequate resource and political support to achieve his political objectives. British monitors have confirmed the delivery of several payloads of heavy military hardware to Machar's loyalists. The presence of specific military equipment (Norinco weaponry) is attributed almost exclusively to Khartoum's support lines. Approximately 40,000 automatic rifles, 5,000 RPG units and some 5,000 heavy machine guns of various designations alongside truckloads of ammunition have been logged at border crossings and along the northern perimeter of South Sudan.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_vice-presidency_and_second_sacking">Return to vice-presidency and second sacking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riek_Machar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Return to vice-presidency and second sacking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late August 2015, a peace agreement was signed between the government and Machar's rebels. The agreement would make Riek Machar the vice-president again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-irin_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-irin-31">[31]</a></sup> In April 2016, as part of the peace deal, Machar returned to Juba and was sworn in as vice-president.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Machar fled the capital after <a href="/wiki/2016_Juba_clashes" title="2016 Juba clashes">renewed fighting</a> between the Kiir loyalists and his own loyalists Juba in July 2016. After a 48-hour ultimatum given by the president to him for returning to Juba to progress with the peace agreement talks passed, the SPLA-IO in Juba appointed lead negotiator <a href="/wiki/Taban_Deng_Gai" title="Taban Deng Gai">Taban Deng Gai</a> to replace Machar and the government accepted him as acting vice-president. Machar said any talks would be illegal because he had fired Gai.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gai_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gai-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
