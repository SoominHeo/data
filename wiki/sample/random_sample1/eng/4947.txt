<p>The <b>Lyonnais</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_France" title="Provinces of France">historical province of France</a> which owes its name to the city of <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a>.</p>
<p>The geographical area known as the <i>Lyonnais</i> became part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Burgundy" title="Kingdom of Burgundy">Kingdom of Burgundy</a> after the division of the <a href="/wiki/Carolingian_Empire" title="Carolingian Empire">Carolingian Empire</a>. The disintegration of Imperial control, especially after the fall of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hohenstaufens" title="Hohenstaufens">Hohenstaufens</a> in 1254, led to French encroachment and eventual acquisition by King <a href="/wiki/Philip_IV_of_France" title="Philip IV of France">Philip IV of France</a> in 1313. Lyonnais now often simply refers to the area around the city of <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a>.</p>
<p>The local speech-form known as <i>Lyonnais</i> is a dialect of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francoproven%C3%A7al_language" title="Francoprovençal language">Francoprovençal language</a> that is spoken in the region, but its use is marginal.</p>
