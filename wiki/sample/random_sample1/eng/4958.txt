<p><b>Licia Maglietta</b> (16 November 1954) is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> actress and stage director, known for her role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Bread_and_Tulips" title="Bread and Tulips">Bread and Tulips</a></i> (2000).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Licia_Maglietta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>, after enrolling several acting courses, Maglietta started her professional career in the avantgarde stage company "Falso Movimento" (later renamed "Teatri Uniti") directed by <a href="/wiki/Mario_Martone" title="Mario Martone">Mario Martone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATTRICI_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ATTRICI-1">[1]</a></sup> She also worked several times with the theatrical company held by <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Cecchi" title="Carlo Cecchi">Carlo Cecchi</a> and directed several stage plays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATTRICI_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ATTRICI-1">[1]</a></sup> She made her film debut in 1992, in Martone's <i><a href="/wiki/Death_of_a_Neapolitan_Mathematician" title="Death of a Neapolitan Mathematician">Death of a Neapolitan Mathematician</a></i>, and had her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Breakout_role" title="Breakout role">breakout</a> in 2000 thanks to the role of Rosalba Barletta in <a href="/wiki/Silvio_Soldini" title="Silvio Soldini">Silvio Soldini</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Bread_and_Tulips" title="Bread and Tulips">Bread and Tulips</a></i>, which got her a <a href="/wiki/David_di_Donatello" title="David di Donatello">David di Donatello</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Nastro_d%27Argento" title="Nastro d'Argento">Nastro d'Argento</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Ciak_d%27oro" title="Ciak d'oro">Ciak d'oro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ATTRICI_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-ATTRICI-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
