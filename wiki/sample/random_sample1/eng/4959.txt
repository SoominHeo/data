<p><b>Richard Kruse</b> (born 30 July 1983 in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>) is a British <a href="/wiki/Fencing" title="Fencing">fencer</a> who specialises in the <a href="/wiki/Foil_(fencing)" title="Foil (fencing)">foil</a> discipline. He has represented Great Britain at the <a href="/wiki/Summer_Olympic_Games" title="Summer Olympic Games">Summer Olympics</a> three times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sref_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sref-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2015, he was part of the Great Britain team that shocked Olympic champions Italy to win the first <a href="/wiki/European_Games" title="European Games">European Games</a> gold in team foil, the first British gold medal in a team fencing event at World or European level for fifty years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Kruse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kruse won his first international medal, a silver, at the 2001 European Under 20 Championships and in 2002 won gold in at the same event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He made his Olympic debut at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, Greece. Competing in the <a href="/wiki/Fencing_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_foil" title="Fencing at the 2004 Summer Olympics – Men's foil">men's individual foil</a>. He received a bye in the first round, beat <a href="/wiki/China_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="China at the 2004 Summer Olympics">China's</a> <a href="/wiki/Wang_Haibin" title="Wang Haibin">Wang Haibin</a> in round two and <a href="/wiki/Dan_Kellner" title="Dan Kellner">Dan Kellner</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="United States at the 2004 Summer Olympics">United States</a> in the third round before being defeated 8–15 by <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Cassar%C3%A0" title="Andrea Cassarà">Andrea Cassarà</a> in the quarter-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sref_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sref-1">[1]</a></sup> His eighth-place finish was the best by a British fencer since the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> in Barcelona, when <a href="/wiki/Fiona_McIntosh_(fencer)" title="Fiona McIntosh (fencer)">Fiona McIntosh</a> was eighth in the women's foil,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fie_1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-fie_1-3">[3]</a></sup> and the best by a British man since the <a href="/wiki/1964_Summer_Olympics" title="1964 Summer Olympics">1964 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Bill_Hoskyns" title="Bill Hoskyns">Bill Hoskyns</a> won the silver medal in the individual épée.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fie_2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-fie_2-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2006 Kruse won the silver medal in the men's foil event at the <a href="/wiki/2006_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2006 European Fencing Championships">European Fencing Championships</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britishfencing_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-britishfencing-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, China, Kruse again participated in the <a href="/wiki/Fencing_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_foil" title="Fencing at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's foil">men's individual foil event</a>. He defeated <a href="/wiki/Virgil_S%C4%83li%C8%99can" title="Virgil Sălișcan">Virgil Sălișcan</a> of <a href="/wiki/Romania_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Romania at the 2008 Summer Olympics">Romania</a> in the round of 32 but was eliminated at the last 16 stage after a 9–10 loss to <a href="/wiki/Germany_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Germany at the 2008 Summer Olympics">Germany's</a> <a href="/wiki/Peter_Joppich" title="Peter Joppich">Peter Joppich</a>. He finished 14th.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sref_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-sref-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009 Kruse had his most successful season to date. He won a gold medal at the World Cup event in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> and a silver medal in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, then gold in Venice before winning a silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/2009_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2009 European Fencing Championships">2009 European Fencing Championships</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Plovdiv" title="Plovdiv">Plovdiv</a>, Bulgaria. Kruse finished the season ranked fourth in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the 2011 World Cup event in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, South Korea, Kruse won the silver medal in the men's foil. he won five matches, including a 15–11 defeat of compatriot <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Halsted" title="Laurence Halsted">Laurence Halsted</a> in the last eight to reach the final against <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Baldini" title="Andrea Baldini">Andrea Baldini</a> of Italy. Baldini won the final by a single point, 15–14 to take the gold medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kruse is coached by former Polish Olympian turned British national <a href="/wiki/Ziemowit_Wojciechowski" title="Ziemowit Wojciechowski">Ziemowit Wojciechowski</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-britishfencing_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-britishfencing-5">[5]</a></sup> He is also a coach for his ZFW fencing club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ZFW_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ZFW-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Richard Kruse won bronze at the Wakayama Grand Prix. Only 2 months later he claimed another bronze at the European Championships in Legnano, having been seeded 45th after the poule stages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">London Olympics</a> later that year, he finished 17th in the men's individual event, losing to <a href="/wiki/Artur_Akhmatkhuzin" title="Artur Akhmatkhuzin">Artur Akhmatkhuzin</a> in the last 32.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He was part of the British team that came 6th, losing to Italy in the quarter-finals and then beating France and losing to Russia in the ranking rounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
