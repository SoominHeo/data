<p><b>Richard Speight, Jr.</b> (born September 4, 1970) is an American actor, director, screenwriter and producer who is known for a variety of roles including <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Jericho_(2006_TV_series)" title="Jericho (2006 TV series)">Jericho</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Agency_(2001_TV_series)" title="The Agency (2001 TV series)">The Agency</a></i>, and the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> miniseries, <i><a href="/wiki/Band_of_Brothers_(miniseries)" title="Band of Brothers (miniseries)">Band of Brothers</a></i>. Speight played a recurring role, Archangel <a href="/wiki/Gabriel" title="Gabriel">Gabriel</a>, originally thought to be a "Trickster" or <a href="/wiki/Loki" title="Loki">Loki</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">WB</a>/<a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">CW</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Supernatural (U.S. TV series)">Supernatural</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Speight,_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born September 4, 1970, Speight was raised in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>, the son of Barbara and Richard Speight. He has two older sisters, Barby and Lindy. Speight attended <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_Bell_Academy" title="Montgomery Bell Academy">Montgomery Bell Academy</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California's School of Theater</a>, graduating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cum_laude" title="Cum laude">cum laude</a>. While at USC, he met and befriended writer and director <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Chbosky" title="Stephen Chbosky">Stephen Chbosky</a>. Speight appeared in Chbosky’s senior showcase piece<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Years later, the two teamed up on the CBS series <i><a href="/wiki/Jericho_(2006_TV_series)" title="Jericho (2006 TV series)">Jericho</a></i> which Chbosky co-created.</p>
<p>In 2003, Speight married Jaci Kathryn Hays, a <a href="/wiki/Dot-com_company" title="Dot-com company">dot-com</a> executive in California.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Speight,_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Speight,_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Speight has starred in commercials for such brands as <a href="/wiki/Got_Milk%3F" title="Got Milk?">Got Milk?</a>; <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>; <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">Pepsi</a>; and <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a>. He has also directed several commercials for Pepsi as well as for other major brands.</p>
<p>Speight played Sgt. <a href="/wiki/Warren_Muck" title="Warren Muck">Warren "Skip" Muck</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a>-winning World War II miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Band_of_Brothers_(miniseries)" title="Band of Brothers (miniseries)">Band of Brothers</a></i>, produced for HBO by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a>. Speight appeared in several television series, such as <i>The Agency</i>; <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>; <i>Jericho</i>; <i><a href="/wiki/Party_of_Five" title="Party of Five">Party of Five</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/JAG_(TV_series)" title="JAG (TV series)">JAG</a></i>. He had a recurring role on the drama <i>Supernatural</i> from 2007 to 2014, where he played 'The Trickster'/Loki (later revealed to be the <a href="/wiki/Gabriel" title="Gabriel">Archangel Gabriel</a>).</p>
<p>He is starring as a guest on Criminal Minds Beyond Borders Season 2, airing mid-March 2017.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Speight,_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Speight's earlier work included a role in the film, <i><a href="/wiki/Ernest_Goes_to_Camp" title="Ernest Goes to Camp">Ernest Goes to Camp</a></i>; and a regular role on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> Saturday morning sitcom, <i><a href="/wiki/Running_the_Halls" title="Running the Halls">Running the Halls</a></i>. He had a brief appearance in the film, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thank_You_for_Smoking_(film)" title="Thank You for Smoking (film)">Thank You for Smoking</a></i>, directed and written by fellow USC alumnus Jason Reitman. Speight appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Speed_2:_Cruise_Control" title="Speed 2: Cruise Control">Speed 2: Cruise Control</a></i> and <i>The Last Big Attraction</i>. He had a starring role in Jason Reitman's short film, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=In_God_We_Trust_(film_short)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="In God We Trust (film short) (page does not exist)">In God We Trust</a></i>.</p>
<p>His more recent productions include "La Musica Provata", "The Week (2016)", "Mucho Dinero", "Brick Madness", "Bad, Bad Men", and "Death House (2016)". Speight plays a lead role as an FBI agent in a thriller, "The Evil Gene (2016)".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Web_series">Web series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Speight,_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Web series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Speight is a close friend of actor <a href="/wiki/Rob_Benedict" title="Rob Benedict">Rob Benedict</a>. The two of them often appear at conventions in tandem. Together, they created the comedy web-series "Kings of Con" based on their experiences at conventions for <i><a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Supernatural (U.S. TV series)">Supernatural</a></i>, a TV show in which they both were featured actors and beloved characters. Speight is co-writer, producer, director, and actor in "Kings of Con," and plays a "heightened version of himself" as character Richard Slate. With the support of a successful <a href="/wiki/Indiegogo" title="Indiegogo">Indiegogo</a> campaign, the series launched on November 15, 2016 at Comic Con Headquarters in the U.S., and internationally through multiple platforms such as GooglePlay.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Directing_and_writing">Directing and writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Speight,_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Directing and writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Speight's directing careeer began with co-directing/co-starring in the independent feature <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=North_Beach_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="North Beach (film) (page does not exist)">North Beach</a></i>.</p>
<p>Speight wrote and directed the satirical social comedy <i>America 101</i>. The festival darling and award winning 10-minute short stars <i><a href="/wiki/Band_of_Brothers_(miniseries)" title="Band of Brothers (miniseries)">Band of Brothers</a></i> veteran, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Gomez" title="Rick Gomez">Rick Gomez</a>; and co-stars <a href="/wiki/Amy_Gumenick" title="Amy Gumenick">Amy Gumenick</a> (a veteran of "Supernatural") and <i>North Beach</i> alums Jayden Lund and Jim Hanna. The short feature is described thusly: “One man’s life becomes as the lesson of the day as he takes a frenetic ride through his personal vision of the American experience.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is now available on iTunes.</p>
<p>The success of " America 101" launched Speight into the world of commercial directing. He has helmed several spots for Pepsi among other national brands.</p>
<p>Following his success in commercial directing and rigorous preparation in <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a> TV Director's Workshop, Speight made debut as TV show director on <a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(season_11)" title="Supernatural (season 11)">Supernatural (season 11)</a> Episode 8 "Just My Imagination" – the first episode to have been nominated for Hugo Awards 2016 in the show's 11-year history.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Podcast">Podcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Speight,_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Podcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Speight has participated in several podcasts including <a href="/wiki/Thrilling_Adventure_Hour" title="Thrilling Adventure Hour">Thrilling Adventure Hour</a> (Episodes 148, 159, 165, 171, 195, 196).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Speight,_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Speight plays the <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass</a>. He has been in several bands, including college years in the band Strange Neighbors with actor/harmonica player <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tim_Omundson" title="Tim Omundson">Tim Omundson</a>, as well as in the band Fugitive Pope (1995) as the guitarist. Speight has two songs released as a guest on singer songwriter Jason Manns albums – "Christmas with Friends (2014)" and "Covers with Friends (2016).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conventions">Conventions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Speight,_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Conventions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While he only appeared in five episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Supernatural (U.S. TV series)">Supernatural</a></i>, Speight's character is a fan favorite, enabling him to stay in close contact with the current cast and crew through his steady attendance at fan conventions (including <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con" title="San Diego Comic-Con">San Diego Comic-Con</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Comic-Con" title="New York Comic-Con">New York Comic-Con</a> and several in Europe). As of May 2016, he has participated in over 70 fan conventions.</p>
<p>His most notable contribution to the convention world is at the "Salute To Supernatural", held annually in many cities across North America. He has made appearances since 2008 and has become the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_of_Ceremonies" title="Master of Ceremonies">Master of Ceremonies</a> in 2013 – hosting for the entire three days of convention weekend, 13 weekends a year. Along with <a href="/wiki/Matt_Cohen_(actor)" title="Matt Cohen (actor)">Matt Cohen</a>, Speight is known as the Karaoke Kings, a fan favorite event where the actors show up in costumes to sing karaoke with fans (which Speight and Cohen started with fewer than 10 attendees and has now grown to a crowd of over 1,500). Speight also participates in Saturday Night Special concert with the band Louden Swain as a singer and bass player.</p>
<p>In a 2012 interview for "The Examiner"<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (November 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, he said that Gabriel has been his favorite role, “bar none;” due to the duality and depth that the character offered.</p>
