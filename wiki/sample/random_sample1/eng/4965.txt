<p><b>Richard Earl Thomas</b> (born June 13, 1951) is an American actor. He is best known for his leading role as budding author <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John-Boy_Walton" title="John-Boy Walton">John-Boy Walton</a> in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Waltons" title="The Waltons">The Waltons</a></i>, where he won one <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a>, and received nominations for another Emmy Award and two <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Awards</a>.</p>
<p>From 2013 to 2016, he starred in the FX drama series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Americans_(2013_TV_series)" title="The Americans (2013 TV series)">The Americans</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Thomas_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thomas was born in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, the son of Barbara (<i>née</i> Fallis) and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Thomas_(dancer)" title="Richard Thomas (dancer)">Richard S. Thomas</a>, in 1951.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> His parents were dancers with the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Ballet" title="New York City Ballet">New York City Ballet</a> and owned the New York School of Ballet. He attended The Allen Stevenson School and the <a href="/wiki/McBurney_School" title="McBurney School">McBurney School</a> in Manhattan. Thomas was seven when he made his Broadway debut in <i>Sunrise at Campobello</i> (1958) playing <a href="/wiki/John_Aspinwall_Roosevelt" title="John Aspinwall Roosevelt">John Roosevelt</a>, son of future U.S. President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franklin_Delano_Roosevelt" title="Franklin Delano Roosevelt">Franklin Delano Roosevelt</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Thomas_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thomas soon began his television career. In 1959, he appeared in the presentation of <a href="/wiki/Henrik_Ibsen" title="Henrik Ibsen">Ibsen</a>’s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Doll%27s_House_(1959_TV_drama)" title="A Doll's House (1959 TV drama)">A Doll’s House</a></i> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julie_Harris_(American_actress)" title="Julie Harris (American actress)">Julie Harris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Plummer" title="Christopher Plummer">Christopher Plummer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hume_Cronyn" title="Hume Cronyn">Hume Cronyn</a>. He then began acting in daytime TV, appearing in soap operas such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Edge_of_Night" title="The Edge of Night">The Edge of Night</a></i> (as Ben Schultz, 1961) and <i><a href="/wiki/As_the_World_Turns" title="As the World Turns">As the World Turns</a></i> (as <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hughes_(As_the_World_Turns)" title="Tom Hughes (As the World Turns)">Tom Hughes</a>, 1966–67), which were <a href="/wiki/Broadcasting" title="Broadcasting">broadcast</a> from his native <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.</p>
<p>Thomas received his first major film roles, appearing in <i><a href="/wiki/Winning" title="Winning">Winning</a></i> (1969) with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Newman" title="Paul Newman">Paul Newman</a>, about auto racing, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Last_Summer" title="Last Summer">Last Summer</a></i> (also 1969) with <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Davison" title="Bruce Davison">Bruce Davison</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Hershey" title="Barbara Hershey">Barbara Hershey</a>, a summer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coming-of-age" title="Coming-of-age">coming-of-age</a> movie. In 1971 he starred in the Universal Pictures Hal Wallis Production <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Sky_at_Morning_(1971_film)" title="Red Sky at Morning (1971 film)">Red Sky at Morning</a></i>, which was a financial failure.</p>

<p>He became nationally recognized for his portrayal of John “John-Boy” Walton, Jr., in the 1970s TV series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Waltons" title="The Waltons">The Waltons</a></i>, which was based on the real life of writer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earl_Hamner,_Jr." title="Earl Hamner, Jr.">Earl Hamner, Jr.</a> He appeared in the 1971 pilot <i>The Homecoming</i>, and then played the role continuously in 122 episodes until March 17, 1977. Thomas left the series and his role was taken over by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Wightman" title="Robert Wightman">Robert Wightman</a>, but Thomas returned to the role in three Waltons TV movies, 1993–97. Thomas won an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emmy" title="Emmy">Emmy</a> for Best Actor in a Dramatic Series in 1973. He enrolled in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Columbia_College_of_Columbia_University" title="Columbia College of Columbia University">Columbia College of Columbia University</a> as a member of the class of 1973 but left after his junior year.</p>
<p>In 1972, he played against type as murderer and rapist Kenneth Kinsolving in <i><a href="/wiki/You%27ll_Like_My_Mother" title="You'll Like My Mother">You’ll Like My Mother</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Patty_Duke" title="Patty Duke">Patty Duke</a>. He played the lead roles of Private Henry Fleming in the 1974 TV movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Red_Badge_of_Courage" title="The Red Badge of Courage">The Red Badge of Courage</a></i>, and Paul Baumer in the 1979 TV movie <i><a href="/wiki/All_Quiet_on_the_Western_Front_(1979_film)" title="All Quiet on the Western Front (1979 film)">All Quiet on the Western Front</a></i>. In further TV movies, he played Col. Warner’s younger son Jim in <i><a href="/wiki/Roots:_The_Next_Generations" title="Roots: The Next Generations">Roots: The Next Generations</a></i> (the sequel to the Emmy-winning <i><a href="/wiki/Roots_(1977_miniseries)" title="Roots (1977 miniseries)">Roots</a></i>); the title role in the biopic <i>Living Proof: The Hank Williams, Jr., Story</i> (1983); Will Mossup in <i>Hobson’s Choice</i> (1983); Henry Durrie in <i>The Master of Ballantrae</i> (1984); Martin Campbell in <i>Final Jeopardy</i> (1985); and the adult Bill Denbrough in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/It_(1990_film)" title="It (1990 film)">Stephen King’s It</a></i> (1990).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Thomas_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1980, Thomas made his first Broadway appearance in more than 12 years when he stepped in as a replacement in <a href="/wiki/Lanford_Wilson" title="Lanford Wilson">Lanford Wilson</a>’s <i><a href="/wiki/Fifth_of_July" title="Fifth of July">Fifth of July</a></i>. In 1987, he appeared on stage in Philadelphia and Washington DC in the one-man tour-de-force "Citizen Tom Paine". In 1993, he played the title role in a stage production of <i><a href="/wiki/Richard_II_(play)" title="Richard II (play)">Richard II</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1981, his book of poetry "Glass" was published as a letterpress limited edition by Kenward Elmslie's Z Press.</p>
<p>In 1990, he played the adult version of main character Bill in the TV adaptation of Stephen King's novel "It".</p>
<p>Thomas starred with <a href="/wiki/Maureen_O%27Hara" title="Maureen O'Hara">Maureen O’Hara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Annette_O%27Toole" title="Annette O'Toole">Annette O’Toole</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hallmark_Channel" title="Hallmark Channel">Hallmark Channel</a> movie, <i>The Christmas Box</i>, in 1995. O'Toole and Thomas starred in <i>It</i> five years earlier as the adult Beverly Marsh and adult Bill Denbrough.</p>
<p>Thomas has appeared in a quartet of performances at the Hartford Stage in Connecticut: <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i> (1987), <i><a href="/wiki/Peer_Gynt" title="Peer Gynt">Peer Gynt</a></i> (1989), <i><a href="/wiki/Richard_III_(play)" title="Richard III (play)">Richard III</a></i> (1994), and <i><a href="/wiki/Tiny_Alice" title="Tiny Alice">Tiny Alice</a></i> (1996).</p>
<p>In 1997 and 1998, he appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Touched_by_an_Angel" title="Touched by an Angel">Touched by an Angel</a></i>.</p>
<p>In the early 2000s, he appeared in a London production of <a href="/wiki/Yasmina_Reza" title="Yasmina Reza">Yasmina Reza</a>’s <i>Art</i> with <a href="/wiki/Judd_Hirsch" title="Judd Hirsch">Judd Hirsch</a> (2001); on the New York stage in The Public Theater’s production of <i><a href="/wiki/As_You_Like_It" title="As You Like It">As You Like It</a></i> (2005); <a href="/wiki/Michael_Frayn" title="Michael Frayn">Michael Frayn</a>’s <i>Democracy</i> on Broadway (2004); and the Primary Stages’ production of <a href="/wiki/Terrence_McNally" title="Terrence McNally">Terrence McNally</a>’s <i>The Stendhal Syndrome</i> (2004).</p>
<p>He has served as national chairman of the Better Hearing Institute, hosted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAX_TV" title="PAX TV">PAX TV</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_a_Miracle_(TV_series)" title="It's a Miracle (TV series)">It’s a Miracle</a></i>, and starred in the series <a href="/wiki/Just_Cause_(TV_series)" title="Just Cause (TV series)"><i>Just Cause</i></a> for the same network.</p>
<p>In 2006, Thomas began a national tour of Reginald Rose’s acclaimed play <i><a href="/wiki/Twelve_Angry_Men_(play)" title="Twelve Angry Men (play)">Twelve Angry Men</a></i>, along with <a href="/wiki/George_Wendt" title="George Wendt">George Wendt</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Shubert_Theatre_(New_Haven)" title="Shubert Theatre (New Haven)">Shubert Theater</a> in New Haven, Connecticut, playing the pivotal role of Juror Eight opposite Wendt’s Juror One.</p>
<p>Thomas has provided <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voiceover" title="Voiceover">voiceovers</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a>, <a href="/wiki/BB%26T" title="BB&amp;T">BB&amp;T</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aleve" title="Aleve">Aleve</a> commercials. In the summer of 2008, Thomas made commercials for the <a href="/wiki/Zaxby%27s" title="Zaxby's">Zaxby’s</a> restaurant chain.</p>
<p>In 2009–2010, Richard Thomas was featured on Broadway in <i><a href="/wiki/Race_(play)" title="Race (play)">Race</a></i>, a play by <a href="/wiki/David_Mamet" title="David Mamet">David Mamet</a>. The production was directed by Mamet and included <a href="/wiki/James_Spader" title="James Spader">James Spader</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Alan_Grier" title="David Alan Grier">David Alan Grier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kerry_Washington" title="Kerry Washington">Kerry Washington</a>. In February and March 2011, he starred at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Public_Theater" title="New York Public Theater">New York Public Theater</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Timon_of_Athens" title="Timon of Athens">Timon of Athens</a></i>.</p>
<p>Thomas has a supporting role in the <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX Network</a> <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Americans_(2013_TV_series)" title="The Americans (2013 TV series)">The Americans</a></i>, which debuted in January 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He plays Frank Gaad, an FBI counterintelligence supervisor helping to investigate KGB <a href="/wiki/Sleeper_agent" title="Sleeper agent">sleeper agents</a> in early 1980s America.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Thomas_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thomas married Alma Gonzales on February 14, 1975. They had one son, Richard Francisco, born in 1976, and triplet daughters Barbara Ayala, Gweneth Gonzales and Pilar Alma, born August 26, 1981. They divorced in 1993.</p>
<p>Thomas married Georgiana Bischoff on November 20, 1994. They have one son, Montana James Thomas, born July 28, 1996. At the time she married Thomas, Bischoff had two daughters from previous marriages, Brooke Murphy and Kendra Kneifel.</p>
