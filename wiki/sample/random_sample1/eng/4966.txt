<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Richard Foster (theologian)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Richard James Foster</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Christian_theology" title="Christian theology">Christian theologian</a> and author in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quaker" title="Quaker">Quaker</a> tradition. His writings speak to a broad Christian audience. Born in 1942, in New Mexico, Foster has been a professor at <a href="/wiki/Friends_University" title="Friends University">Friends University</a> and pastor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelical_Friends_International" title="Evangelical Friends International">Evangelical Friends</a> churches. Foster resides in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denver,_Colorado" title="Denver, Colorado">Denver, Colorado</a>. He earned his undergraduate degree at <a href="/wiki/George_Fox_University" title="George Fox University">George Fox University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Pastoral_Theology" title="Doctor of Pastoral Theology">Doctor of Pastoral Theology</a> at <a href="/wiki/Fuller_Theological_Seminary" title="Fuller Theological Seminary">Fuller Theological Seminary</a>,and received an honorary doctorate from <a href="/wiki/Houghton_College" title="Houghton College">Houghton College</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Foster is best known for his 1978 book <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Celebration_of_Discipline&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Celebration of Discipline (page does not exist)">Celebration of Discipline</a></i>, which examines the inward disciplines of <a href="/wiki/Prayer" title="Prayer">prayer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fasting" title="Fasting">fasting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meditation" title="Meditation">meditation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bible_study_(Christian)" title="Bible study (Christian)">study</a> in the Christian life, the outward disciplines of <a href="/wiki/Simplicity" title="Simplicity">simplicity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solitude" title="Solitude">solitude</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Submission" title="Submission">submission</a>, and service, and the corporate disciplines of <a href="/wiki/Confession_(religion)" title="Confession (religion)">confession</a>, <a href="/wiki/Worship" title="Worship">worship</a>, guidance, and celebration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It has sold over one million copies. It was named by <i><a href="/wiki/Christianity_Today" title="Christianity Today">Christianity Today</a></i> as one of the top ten books of the twentieth century. A work described as a sequel to <i>Celebration</i> is Foster's 1985 <i>Money, Sex &amp; Power</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and associated study guide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He also published <i>Freedom of Simplicity</i> in 1981, which further explores the discipline of simple, <a href="/wiki/Simple_living" title="Simple living">intentional living</a>, <i>Freedom of Simplicity</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>Prayer: Finding the Heart's True Home</i> (1992, <a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0060628464">ISBN 0-06-062846-4</a>), which explores 21 different types of Christian prayer, edited <i>Devotional Classics</i> (1993, <a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0060669667">ISBN 0-06-066966-7</a>), a devotional guide featuring Christian wisdom through the ages, and <i>Streams of Living Water</i> (2001, <a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0060628227">ISBN 0-06-062822-7</a>), which examines the place of the different spiritual traditions - Contemplative: The Prayer-Filled Life; Holiness: The Virtuous Life ; Charismatic: The Spirit-Empowered Life; Social Justice: The Compassionate Life; Evangelical: The Word-Centered Life; and Incarnational: The Sacramental Life - in Christianity.</p>
<p>Foster (along with several others) also wrote the <a href="/wiki/Bible_study_(Christian)" title="Bible study (Christian)">devotional</a> <i>Renovaré Spiritual Formation Bible</i> (now published as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_with_God_Study_Bible" title="The Life with God Study Bible">The Life with God Study Bible</a></i>). Two editions of this <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NRSV" title="NRSV">NRSV</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Study_Bible" title="Study Bible">study Bible</a> exist, one with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apocryphal" title="Apocryphal">apocryphal</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deuterocanonical" title="Deuterocanonical">deuterocanonical</a> texts and one without.</p>
<p>In 2008, he co-authored with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gayle_Beebe" title="Gayle Beebe">Gayle Beebe</a>, the book <i>Longing for God</i>.</p>
<p>In 1988 Foster founded <i><a href="/wiki/Renovar%C3%A9" title="Renovaré">Renovaré</a></i>, a Christian renewal para-Church organization.</p>
<p>In recent years he has allowed his hair to grow, wearing it in a long pony tail. He says this is his way of honoring the portion of his heritage which is <a href="/wiki/Ojibwe" title="Ojibwe">Ojibwe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
