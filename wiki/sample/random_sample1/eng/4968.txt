<p><b>Ritchie Ria Alfons De Laet</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[ˈrɪtʃi də ˈlaːt]</a></span>, born 28 November 1988) is a Belgian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> club <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ritchie_De_Laet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stoke_City">Stoke City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ritchie_De_Laet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stoke City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Antwerp" title="Antwerp">Antwerp</a>, De Laet began his career at Belgian club <a href="/wiki/Royal_Antwerp_F.C." title="Royal Antwerp F.C.">Royal Antwerp</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugman2010-11_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hugman2010-11-1">[1]</a></sup> On 17 August 2007, he signed a three-year contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> club <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> for an initial £100,000 fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In July 2008, De Laet joined <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a> on trial and played his first game in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a>, a 4–1 defeat. However, he later returned to Stoke. In October 2008, he joined <a href="/wiki/Wrexham_A.F.C." title="Wrexham A.F.C.">Wrexham</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conference_National" title="Conference National">Conference National</a> on a one-month <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan</a> deal, making his debut in a 2–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Lewes_F.C." title="Lewes F.C.">Lewes</a> in the league. He made three appearances during his time at the club before his loan spell was terminated in order for him to undergo a hernia operation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_United">Manchester United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ritchie_De_Laet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Manchester United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>On 8 January 2009, De Laet was signed by <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> champions <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> on a three-year contract. The fee to be paid by Manchester United depended on appearances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After joining United, De Laet played exclusively for the club's reserve team. In March 2009, De Laet was named as one of five over-age players in the Manchester United under-18 squad for the Torneo Calcio Memorial Claudio Sassi-Sassuolo; he played in three of the five matches, and scored a penalty in the 5–3 semi-final <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">shootout</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Modena_F.C." title="Modena F.C.">Modena</a> to take the Red Devils into the final, in which they beat <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> 1–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> De Laet made his first appearance for the Manchester United first team on 24 May 2009, when he was named at left back for the last game of the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Premier_League" title="2008–09 Premier League">2008–09 Premier League</a> season against <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the 2009–10 season, De Laet featured in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>, coming on as a substitute against <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a> in the Third and Fourth Rounds and starting against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in the Fifth. Despite a defensive injury crisis in November 2009, Alex Ferguson preferred to bring midfielder Michael Carrick on for Gary Neville ahead of De Laet, after the United captain suffered a groin strain in the next league match away to West Ham on 5 December. He then played his first league game of the 2009–10 season on 15 December against <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a>. He played his second league game against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> on 19 December which ended in a 3–0 defeat. On 4 May 2010, he was voted as the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy#Players_of_the_Year" title="Manchester United F.C. Reserves and Academy">Reserve Team Player of the Year</a>, beating Oliver Gill and <a href="/wiki/Magnus_Wolff_Eikrem" title="Magnus Wolff Eikrem">Magnus Eikrem</a> to the award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2010, De Laet joined Championship side <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a> on a 30-day emergency loan deal after the Blades suffered a defensive injury crisis, with both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_Morgan_(English_footballer)" title="Chris Morgan (English footballer)">Chris Morgan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kozluk" title="Robert Kozluk">Robert Kozluk</a> ruled out for several weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> De Laet returned to Old Trafford at the end of his month having made six appearances for the Blades.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blades_exit_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-blades_exit-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 17 November 2010, De Laet joined Championship team <a href="/wiki/Preston_North_End_F.C." title="Preston North End F.C.">Preston North End</a> on a 28-day loan after an injury to first-team defender <a href="/wiki/Callum_Davidson" title="Callum Davidson">Callum Davidson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 14 January 2011, De Laet joined <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> of the Championship on loan until the end of the 2010–11 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Norwich_City_loan">Norwich City loan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ritchie_De_Laet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Norwich City loan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 17 June 2011, De Laet joined newly promoted Premier League side <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a> on loan until the end of the 2011–12 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He made his debut in a 1–1 draw at <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a>, conceding the penalty for the Wigan goal. However, he redeemed himself with a number of excellent blocks to retain the scoreline. On 21 August, he scored his first goal for Norwich against former club <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> in a 1–1 draw. This was also De Laet's first senior goal. De Laet was sidelined with a back injury whilst at Norwich. He made his return from injury on 27 December 2011 against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in a 2–0 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deleatnorreturn_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-deleatnorreturn-14">[14]</a></sup> On 18 January 2012, he returned to United after Norwich cancelled his loan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deleatnorreturn_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-deleatnorreturn-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leicester_City">Leicester City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ritchie_De_Laet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Leicester City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 14 May 2012, De Laet signed a three-year deal with <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a>. He was joined at Leicester by fellow United player <a href="/wiki/Matty_James" title="Matty James">Matty James</a>, who signed on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agreement_reached_for_United_Duo_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Agreement_reached_for_United_Duo-15">[15]</a></sup> De Laet scored his first goal for Leicester shortly before half time in the 6–1 thrashing of Huddersfield Town on 1 January 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He scored his second just four days later, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burton_Albion" title="Burton Albion">Burton Albion</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FA_Cup_2012-13" title="FA Cup 2012-13">FA Cup</a> 1st Round. De Laet ended his first season with Leicester having played 46 games and scoring twice in all competitions, helping City into the <a href="/wiki/2013_Football_League_play-offs" title="2013 Football League play-offs">2012–13 Championship Play-offs</a>. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013-14_Football_League_Championship" title="2013-14 Football League Championship">2013–14</a> season, he helped Leicester gain promotion back to the top flight playing regularly in the first team once more. He featured 35 times scoring 2 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Middlesbrough_loan">Middlesbrough loan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ritchie_De_Laet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Middlesbrough loan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 1 February 2016, De Laet joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> on loan for the remainder of the 2015–16 season. He won promotion with the team on the final day of the season, after a 1–1 draw with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brighton_and_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton and Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton and Hove Albion</a>. This meant that De Laet achieved the unique feat of winning promotion from the Championship and then winning the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Premier_League" title="2015–16 Premier League">Premier League</a> title (courtesy of his 12 league appearances for Leicester) in the same season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aston_Villa">Aston Villa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ritchie_De_Laet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aston Villa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 August 2016, De Laet signed a three-year deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> club <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ritchie_De_Laet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A few days after his Manchester United debut, De Laet was called up for <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kirin_Cup" title="Kirin Cup">Kirin Cup</a> matches against <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He made his début in the match against Chile on 29 May 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He was a regular for the under-21s in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship_qualification" title="2011 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification">2011 European Championship qualifying</a>.</p>
