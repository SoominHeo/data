<p>The <b>Richmond Renegades</b> was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SPHL" title="SPHL">SPHL</a> <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> team in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a>. The team, owned by Allan B. Harvie Jr., the founder of the former <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a> Renegades franchise, began play in October 2006 at the <a href="/wiki/Richmond_Coliseum" title="Richmond Coliseum">Richmond Coliseum</a>. The team's first head coach was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Brophy_(hockey)" title="John Brophy (hockey)">John Brophy</a>. After the Renegades loss in the first round of the 2007 playoffs, Brian Goudie replaced Brophy as head coach.</p>
<p>The new Renegades replaced the <a href="/wiki/United_Hockey_League" title="United Hockey League">United Hockey League</a> <a href="/wiki/Richmond_RiverDogs" title="Richmond RiverDogs">Richmond RiverDogs</a>, who relocated to suburban <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" title="Chicago, Illinois">Chicago</a> for the 2006-07 season to become the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Hounds" title="Chicago Hounds">Chicago Hounds</a>. The most recent Renegades were the second team with that name; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richmond_Renegades_(ECHL)" title="Richmond Renegades (ECHL)">the first</a> was an <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a> team that played from 1990-2003.</p>
<p>The Richmond Renegades ceased operations at the end of the 2008-2009 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
