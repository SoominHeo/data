<p><b>Richmond Yiadom Boakye</b> (<small><a href="/wiki/Akan_language" title="Akan language">Akan</a>: <i><span lang="ak" xml:lang="ak"><small>pronunciation:</small></span></i></small> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/ʊ/ short 'oo' in 'foot'">ʊ</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/tʃ/ 'ch' in 'china'">tʃ</span><span title="/iː/ long 'e' in 'seed'">iː</span></span>/</a></span></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i>buuah-<b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">CHEE</span></span></b></i></a></span>; born 28 January 1993) is a Ghanaian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> for <a href="/wiki/U.S._Latina_Calcio" title="U.S. Latina Calcio">Latina</a>. He also represents the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana national football team</a>.</p>

<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Agogo,_Ghana" title="Agogo, Ghana">Agogo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ashanti_Region" title="Ashanti Region">Ashanti</a>, Boakye began his career with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bechem_United" title="Bechem United">Bechem United</a> in his native country of <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He eventually moved to <a href="/wiki/Agogo,_Ghana" title="Agogo, Ghana">Agogo</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asante_Akim_North_District" title="Asante Akim North District">Asante Akim North District</a> based club, D.C. United<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and remained until 2008 when he was scouted by <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa C.F.C.</a> following a tournament in <a href="/wiki/Vicenza" title="Vicenza">Vicenza</a>, Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scout_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-scout-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Genoa_C.F.C.">Genoa C.F.C.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richmond_Boakye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Genoa C.F.C.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following his transfer to <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a> in 2009, Boakye spent the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Serie_A" title="2009–10 Serie A">2009–10 Serie A</a> season, playing in the <a href="/wiki/Campionato_Nazionale_Primavera" title="Campionato Nazionale Primavera">Campionato Nazionale Primavera</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Liguria" title="Liguria">Ligurian</a> club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On 3 April 2010, Boakye made his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut, in a match against <a href="/wiki/A.S._Livorno_Calcio" title="A.S. Livorno Calcio">Livorno</a>. He came off the bench as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/David_Suazo" title="David Suazo">David Suazo</a> in the 13th minute and scored the opening goal, and the match ended in a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 2 May, he played in his second match, replacing <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Sculli" title="Giuseppe Sculli">Giuseppe Sculli</a> as a 71st minute <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a>.</p>
<p>On 21 June 2011, Boakye officially joined <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> outfit <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Sassuolo_Calcio" title="US Sassuolo Calcio">Sassuolo</a> on a one-year <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan deal</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> that proved very successful for the Ghanaian; he managed to score 12 goals in 34 appearances for the club that missed promotion to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> via a promotion play-off loss on aggregate to <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">U.C. Sampdoria</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Juventus">Juventus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richmond_Boakye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Juventus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His performances for the <i>Neroverde</i> did not go unnoticed, as the player was eventually signed by <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_A" title="2011–12 Serie A">reigning Serie A champions</a> <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus F.C.</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Co-ownership_(football)" title="Co-ownership (football)">co-ownership</a> deal from <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a> in July 2012. After joining the club, however, he returned to Sassuolo on a second season-long loan deal with the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Juventus paid nothing in cash but cancelled the transfer debt owed to Genoa with the sale of <a href="/wiki/Ciro_Immobile" title="Ciro Immobile">Ciro Immobile</a> on a similar co-owenrship deal.</p>
<p>Boakye returned to Juventus on 30 June 2013 but was later loaned to <a href="/wiki/Elche_CF" title="Elche CF">Elche</a> in Spain for the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_La_Liga" title="2013–14 La Liga">2013–14 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He finished the season having scored 6 goals in 31 league appearances (12 starts) for the Spanish club, and returned again to Juventus on 30 June 2014, after the co-ownership with Genoa was renewed just ten days prior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atalanta">Atalanta</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richmond_Boakye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Atalanta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 July 2014 Genoa sold half of Boakye's 50% registration rights to Atalanta<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> for €1.3 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> with Juventus retained the other half. On 23 June 2015 Atalanta bought Boakye outright from Juventus for €1.6 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 31 August 2015 Boakye left for Dutch club <a href="/wiki/Roda_JC_Kerkrade" title="Roda JC Kerkrade">Roda JC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Latina">Latina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richmond_Boakye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Latina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2016 Boakye was sold to <a href="/wiki/U.S._Latina_Calcio" title="U.S. Latina Calcio">Latina</a> in a temporary deal, with an obligation to sign outright at the end of season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richmond_Boakye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ghana_national_team">Ghana national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richmond_Boakye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ghana national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Boakye received an International <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly match</a> call-up by the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team#Personnel" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana national football team technical staff</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana national football team</a> vs. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China_PR_national_football_team" title="China PR national football team">China PR national football team</a> friendly match at the <a href="/wiki/Tianhe_Stadium" title="Tianhe Stadium">Tianhe Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou, Guangdong</a>, China on 15 August 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Boakye scored his first goal for the Ghana national team, an equaliser against China in the 80th minute of the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-China_1_-_1_Ghana_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-China_1_-_1_Ghana-17">[17]</a></sup>He played for Ghana at the Fifa U20 World Cup</p>
