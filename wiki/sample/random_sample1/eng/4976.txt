<p><b>Richmond Kickers Soccer Club</b> is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">soccer</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a>. Founded in 1993, the team plays in the <a href="/wiki/United_Soccer_League" title="United Soccer League">United Soccer League</a>, part of the second tier of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_soccer_league_system" title="American soccer league system">American soccer league system</a>. The team is one of the oldest continuously run soccer clubs in the United States.</p>
<p>The team's home field is the 22,000-seat <a href="/wiki/City_Stadium_(Richmond)" title="City Stadium (Richmond)">City Stadium</a>, where the club has played since 1995. The team's president is Richmond native, and former Kickers player, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rob_Ukrop" title="Rob Ukrop">Rob Ukrop</a>, who is also an heir to the area's <a href="/wiki/Ukrop%27s_Food_Group" title="Ukrop's Food Group">Ukrop's Food Group</a> chain. The team is coached by Englishman <a href="/wiki/Leigh_Cowlishaw" title="Leigh Cowlishaw">Leigh Cowlishaw</a>, who has been the team's head coach since 2000.</p>
<p>The Kickers had two developmental teams: <a href="/wiki/Richmond_Kickers_Future" title="Richmond Kickers Future">Richmond Kickers Future</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richmond_Kickers_Destiny" title="Richmond Kickers Destiny">Richmond Kickers Destiny</a>. Future played in the men's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USL_Premier_Development_League" title="USL Premier Development League">USL Premier Development League</a> until 2008, while the Destiny left the women's <a href="/wiki/USL_W-League" title="USL W-League">USL W-League</a> after the 2009 season.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richmond_Kickers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Richmond Kickers were founded in 1993 and played their inaugural season in the <a href="/wiki/United_Soccer_Leagues" title="United Soccer Leagues">United States Interregional Soccer League</a> (USISL), which, at the time, represented the third division of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_soccer_pyramid" title="American soccer pyramid">American soccer pyramid</a>. The team originally played their home matches on the campus of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Richmond" title="University of Richmond">University of Richmond</a> and targeted players specifically from Virginia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> After a poor season in 1994, the club self-relegated to the newly formed fourth-tier <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USL_Premier_Development_League" title="USL Premier Development League">USISL Premier League</a>, now known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USL_PDL" title="USL PDL">Premier Development League</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/1995_USISL_Premier_League_season" title="1995 USISL Premier League season">1995 season</a>, the Kickers battled to a 15–3 winning season and went on to win the first ever USISL Premier League championship, defeating the now-defunct <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_Expos" title="Cocoa Expos">Cocoa Expos</a> in the championship. That same season, Richmond won their first, and to date only, <a href="/wiki/Lamar_Hunt_U.S._Open_Cup" title="Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup">Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup</a> championship. The following year, Richmond chose to rejoin the third division, where they reached the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USISL_Select_League" title="USISL Select League">USISL Select League</a> championship, only to lose to the <a href="/wiki/California_Jaguars" title="California Jaguars">California Jaguars</a> in the finals.</p>
<p>For the next nine seasons, Richmond played in the second tier of the American soccer pyramid, earning the Commissioner's Cup twice, in 1998 and 2001, as well as earning two conference championships. However, the club was never able to secure a division two league championship, their closest coming in 2005, when the Kickers fell to the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Sounders_(1994%E2%80%932008)" title="Seattle Sounders (1994–2008)">Seattle Sounders</a> in the 2005 USL First Division Championship. After the 2005 season, the Kickers ownership self-relegated the team back into the third division, citing possible financial problems for the club if they remained in the second tier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kickers_realign_into_USL2_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kickers_realign_into_USL2-5">[5]</a></sup> Since joining the third division, the Kickers have had tremendous success, making it to at least the playoff semifinals each season they have played in the third division. Additionally, the Kickers have won two third division premierships and two third division championships during their spell.</p>
<p>Richmond Kickers have not only experienced league success, but have been perennial contenders in the Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup tournament. In the <a href="/wiki/2007_Lamar_Hunt_U.S._Open_Cup" title="2007 Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup">2007 U.S. Open Cup</a>, the Kickers defeated the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Galaxy" title="Los Angeles Galaxy">Los Angeles Galaxy</a> of the first division, <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a>, 1–0 in the third round of competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kickers_oust_LA_Galaxy_in_Open_Cup_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kickers_oust_LA_Galaxy_in_Open_Cup-6">[6]</a></sup> The Kickers won their First Round match of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Lamar_Hunt_U.S._Open_Cup" title="2008 Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup">2008 U.S. Open Cup</a>, beating the <a href="/wiki/Fredericksburg_Gunners" title="Fredericksburg Gunners">Fredericksburg Gunners</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USL_Premier_Development_League" title="USL Premier Development League">PDL</a> with a final scoreline of 3–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kickers_disarm_Gunners_in_3-0_win_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kickers_disarm_Gunners_in_3-0_win-7">[7]</a></sup> The Kickers won their second round game over the <a href="/wiki/Western_Mass_Pioneers" title="Western Mass Pioneers">Western Mass Pioneers</a> 2–1 before being eliminated from the Cup in the third round by MLS side, and defending USOC champion, <a href="/wiki/New_England_Revolution" title="New England Revolution">New England Revolution</a>, 3–0. In the 2011 US Open Cup the Kickers made a cinderalla run, defeating MLS clubs <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Columbus_Crew" title="Columbus Crew">Columbus Crew</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Kansas_City" title="Sporting Kansas City">Sporting Kansas City</a> to reach the semi-finals, before falling to the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Fire_Soccer_Club" title="Chicago Fire Soccer Club">Chicago Fire</a>.</p>
<p>In September 2005, Rob Ukrop's #6 jersey was retired. He was a well-liked soccer player on and off of the field, and particularly notable since he is the son of the influential <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ukrop%27s" title="Ukrop's">Ukrop's</a> grocery store CEO Robert "Bobby" Ukrop.</p>
<p>On January 24, 2013, it was announced that the Kickers had signed a multi-year deal to become the USL Pro affiliate of <a href="/wiki/D.C._United" title="D.C. United">D.C. United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kickersdcunited_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-kickersdcunited-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Colors_and_badge">Colors and badge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richmond_Kickers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Colors and badge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Kickers primary colors are red and white, having been so since the beginning of the 2001 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Their kits are manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a>, as they have been since at least 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In March 2012, the Kickers revealed a new logo to celebrate 20 years since their inception. Along with the new crest, the club also announced they would be using a third uniform with the colors from their first season in 1993, green and blue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> They also have a black kit they have used during the 2013 season.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="James_River_Cup">James River Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richmond_Kickers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: James River Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The James River Cup was an annual competition held between the Richmond Kickers and the <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach_Mariners" title="Virginia Beach Mariners">Virginia Beach Mariners</a> (formerly Hampton Roads Mariners) which the team with the most points at the conclusion of all scheduled matches between the two teams would win. The Cup was held every year since 1996 with the exception of 1997 and 2001 when Virginia Beach did not field a team. In 2007, the Virginia Beach team was disbanded.</p>
<p>For the 2008 season, the James River Cup was contested between the Kickers' organization and the <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Roads_Piranhas" title="Hampton Roads Piranhas">Hampton Roads Piranhas</a> organization. The cup went to the organization that had the most points in games between their PDL and W-League teams. The series ended up tied 2–2–1, with the Piranhas winning the Cup on <a href="/wiki/Goal_difference" title="Goal difference">goal difference</a>.</p>
