<p><b>Riccardo Montolivo</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[rikˈkardo montoˈlivo]</a></span>; born 18 January 1985) is an Italian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a>, where he is the <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a>, and internationally for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italian national team</a>.</p>
<p>A versatile and creative cone, Montolivo started his career with <a href="/wiki/Atalanta_B.C." title="Atalanta B.C.">Atalanta</a> in 2003 before being transferred to <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> in 2005. He went on to make more than 250 appearances for the club in seven years. In 2012, he signed for <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> on a free transfer and was given the club's captaincy the season following the departure of <a href="/wiki/Massimo_Ambrosini" title="Massimo Ambrosini">Massimo Ambrosini</a>.</p>
<p>Montolivo made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italian senior team</a> in 2007 against <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_football_team" title="South Africa national football team">South Africa</a> and has since then represented the country in two <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="FIFA Confederations Cup">FIFA Confederations Cups</a> (<a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2009 FIFA Confederations Cup">2009</a> and <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2013 FIFA Confederations Cup">2013</a>, winning a bronze medal in the latter edition of the tournament) as well as in the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>, where he played as a starter in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012_Final" title="UEFA Euro 2012 Final">final</a>; he also took part at the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2008 Summer Olympics</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riccardo_Montolivo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atalanta">Atalanta</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riccardo_Montolivo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Atalanta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Montolivo is a graduate of the <a href="/wiki/Atalanta_B.C." title="Atalanta B.C.">Atalanta</a> youth academy. He made his debut for the club in a <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piacenza_Calcio" title="Piacenza Calcio">Piacenza</a> coming on the field as an 81st-minute substitute for Michele Marcoliniego.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> His first starting match came in a 2–1 win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona" title="Hellas Verona">Hellas Verona</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> From that match onwards, he became frequent member of the starting line-up<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and scored his first goal against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S._Bari" title="A.S. Bari">Bari</a> in a 2–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Atalanta placed fifth and was promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> through playoffs.</p>
<p>He missed the first matches of his debut season in the Serie A because he was out with an injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He made his Serie A debut in a 2–2 draw against <a href="/wiki/U.S._Lecce" title="U.S. Lecce">Lecce</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He scored goals in consecutive matches against <a href="/wiki/Cagliari_Calcio" title="Cagliari Calcio">Cagliari</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 23 January 2005, he got the first red card of his career in a match against Lecce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Atalanta finished last in the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Serie_A" title="2004–05 Serie A">2004–05 Serie A</a> and was relegated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Montolivo won 32 caps and scored three goals in his debut season in the Serie A.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fiorentina">Fiorentina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riccardo_Montolivo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fiorentina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Riccardo_Montolivo_(19_02_2011).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="427" data-file-width="640" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Riccardo_Montolivo_%2819_02_2011%29.jpg/220px-Riccardo_Montolivo_%2819_02_2011%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Riccardo_Montolivo_%2819_02_2011%29.jpg/330px-Riccardo_Montolivo_%2819_02_2011%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Riccardo_Montolivo_%2819_02_2011%29.jpg/440px-Riccardo_Montolivo_%2819_02_2011%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>In the summer of 2005, <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> signed Montolivo in a co-ownership deal for €3.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Archiviostorico_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Archiviostorico-16">[16]</a></sup> He penned a five-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Archiviostorico_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Archiviostorico-16">[16]</a></sup> He was given the jersey number 18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Serie_A" title="2005–06 Serie A">2005–06 season</a>, Montolivo's first-team chances were limited, partly due to the fact that he was still an inexperienced player and partly because manager <a href="/wiki/Cesare_Prandelli" title="Cesare Prandelli">Cesare Prandelli</a> had a relatively stable and reliable squad of players at his disposal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Riccardo_Montolivo_.E2.80.93_Profile_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Riccardo_Montolivo_.E2.80.93_Profile-18">[18]</a></sup> The young man's talents did not go unrecognised though. He became a fixture in <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy's under-21 team</a>, coached by <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Casiraghi" title="Pierluigi Casiraghi">Pierluigi Casiraghi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Riccardo_Montolivo_.E2.80.93_Profile_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Riccardo_Montolivo_.E2.80.93_Profile-18">[18]</a></sup> He made his debut in Fiorentina's colours in a 2–1 loss against <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Internazionale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<p>Despite being associated with the <a href="/wiki/2006_Italian_football_scandal" title="2006 Italian football scandal">Calciopoli scandal</a>, Fiorentina signed Montolivo on a permanent basis the following season for €2 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He played a total of 36 matches for Fiorentina, scoring two goals. He also declared that he will continue playing for the <i>Viola</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Serie_A" title="2007–08 Serie A">2007–08 season</a> started well for the <i>Viola</i> midfielder; in the first match day, he was as one of the best on the field against <a href="/wiki/Empoli_F.C." title="Empoli F.C.">Empoli</a>, against which he also scored a splendid goal with a lob.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Riccardo_Montolivo_.E2.80.93_Azzurri_profile_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Riccardo_Montolivo_.E2.80.93_Azzurri_profile-22">[22]</a></sup> The season followed with ups and downs, but during its course Montolivo confirmed himself as one of the best talents in Italian football.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>His good form earned him a spot for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. As he sustained an injury while playing in the competition, he missed the first round of <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Serie_A" title="2008–09 Serie A">2008–09 Serie A</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juventus_FC" title="Juventus FC">Juventus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He scored a crucial brace as Fiorentina defeated <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a> 4–2 after being down 0–1 in the first half.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Montolivo scored two more goals against <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> and <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a> as he ended the season with two goals, two more than his previous season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> On 17 January 2009, he captained Fiorentina for the first time in a 1–0 defeat to <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Serie_A" title="2009–10 Serie A">2009–10 season</a> he was made <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">club-captain</a> following the departures of the then captain <a href="/wiki/Dario_Dainelli" title="Dario Dainelli">Dario Dainelli</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Igol_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Igol-27">[27]</a></sup> He also expressed that the departure of Dainelli and <a href="/wiki/Martin_J%C3%B8rgensen" title="Martin Jørgensen">Martin Jørgensen</a> had weakened the team, but with <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Frey" title="Sébastien Frey">Sébastien Frey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marco_Donadel" title="Marco Donadel">Marco Donadel</a>, he formed a strong midfield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Igol_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-Igol-27">[27]</a></sup> Despite gaining attention from big clubs he said that Fiorentina was his house and he would not change his club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Again, the next season started with injury for Montolivo—he had a small problem with his ankle. However, he was operated and it was remedied.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> His form saw him included in Italian squad for <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He scored goals against <a href="/wiki/U.S._Citt%C3%A0_di_Palermo" title="U.S. Città di Palermo">Palermo</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/A.C._Cesena" title="A.C. Cesena">Cesena</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> as he ended his account scoring two goals in 29 matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Statistics_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Statistics-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the beginning of his last season, <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_A" title="2011–12 Serie A">2011–12</a>, Montolivo said he would leave Fiorentina after his contract expired, with an intent to play in a team of a higher caliber.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> As a result, he was stripped from his captainship and the armband was given to <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Gamberini" title="Alessandro Gamberini">Alessandro Gamberini</a> with <a href="/wiki/Stevan_Joveti%C4%87" title="Stevan Jovetić">Stevan Jovetić</a> as the vice-captain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> He made his 200th appearance for the club in a 2–0 loss against Palermo on 27 November 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Milan">Milan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riccardo_Montolivo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Milan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kevin-Prince_Boateng_taking_photos_of_Yankee_Stadium.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2694" data-file-width="2466" height="240" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Kevin-Prince_Boateng_taking_photos_of_Yankee_Stadium.jpg/220px-Kevin-Prince_Boateng_taking_photos_of_Yankee_Stadium.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Kevin-Prince_Boateng_taking_photos_of_Yankee_Stadium.jpg/330px-Kevin-Prince_Boateng_taking_photos_of_Yankee_Stadium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Kevin-Prince_Boateng_taking_photos_of_Yankee_Stadium.jpg/440px-Kevin-Prince_Boateng_taking_photos_of_Yankee_Stadium.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>In May 2012, Milan head coach <a href="/wiki/Massimiliano_Allegri" title="Massimiliano Allegri">Massimiliano Allegri</a> announced that Montolivo would join the club on a free transfer when his <a href="/wiki/Association_football_contracts" title="Association football contracts">contract</a> with Fiorentina expired on 30 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FI1_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-FI1-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> He penned a four-year contract with the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> "Next season, Montolivo will arrive and he is a very talented player with great technique," said Allegri to <a href="/wiki/Milan_Channel" title="Milan Channel">Milan Channel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FI1_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-FI1-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> He chose to wear the number 18 jersey which he had worn before and which was last worn by <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Aquilani" title="Alberto Aquilani">Alberto Aquilani</a> in Milan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Derby_della_Madonnina" title="Derby della Madonnina">Milan derby</a> though he was highly praised for his performance, the referee disallowed a stunning 30-yard <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volley_(football)" title="Volley (football)">volley</a> by Montolivo due to a challenge committed by <a href="/wiki/Urby_Emanuelson" title="Urby Emanuelson">Urby Emanuelson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> On 30 October, Montolivo scored his first goal for Milan, coming in the 69th minute after Milan were down 2–0 to <a href="/wiki/U.S._Citt%C3%A0_di_Palermo" title="U.S. Città di Palermo">Palermo</a>. Milan eventually drew the match 2–2, salvaging a point.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 November, against <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>, a match won 1–0 by Milan, he played his first match as Milan's captain as <a href="/wiki/Daniele_Bonera" title="Daniele Bonera">Daniele Bonera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Massimo_Ambrosini" title="Massimo Ambrosini">Massimo Ambrosini</a> were suspended and <a href="/wiki/Christian_Abbiati" title="Christian Abbiati">Christian Abbiati</a> was injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> He ended the first season at the club by becoming one of the two (the other being <a href="/wiki/Stephan_El_Shaarawy" title="Stephan El Shaarawy">Stephan El Shaarawy</a>) most used players of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> On 11 June 2013, Milan Vice-President <a href="/wiki/Adriano_Galliani" title="Adriano Galliani">Adriano Galliani</a> stated that Montolivo would be the next captain for Milan, after previously announcing that current club captain Massimo Ambrosini would not be extending his contract with the <i>Rossoneri</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>After getting captainship, he opened his account by scoring against <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Catania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> He then scored in a 4–3 defeat to <a href="/wiki/U.S._Sassuolo_Calcio" title="U.S. Sassuolo Calcio">Sassulo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> On 13 April 2014, Montolivo scored an astonishing long-range strike in the 23rd minute against Catania, which extended Milan's winning streak to four matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> After breaking his leg in a pre-World Cup friendly against the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a> on 31 May, Montolivo was ruled out for six months and made his return in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a> on 30 November.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riccardo_Montolivo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Junior_teams">Junior teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riccardo_Montolivo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Junior teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Montolivo represented <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> at <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-16_football_team" title="Italy national under-16 football team">under-16</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-18_football_team" title="Italy national under-18 football team">under-18</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-19_football_team" title="Italy national under-19 football team">under-19</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">under-21</a> levels before receiving his first <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">cap</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">national team</a> in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIGC_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-FIGC-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>Montolivo scored the winning goal when Italy's under-21 team defeated <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">Spain</a> 2–1 and qualified for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship</a> on 10 October 2006. One year and seven days later, he made his debut for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_national_football_team" title="Italian national football team">senior team</a> in a 2–0 friendly win against <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_football_team" title="South Africa national football team">South Africa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIGC_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-FIGC-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NFT_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-NFT-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Despite being ineligible for the 2007–08 season of the under-21 team, Montolivo capped once for the <i>de facto</i> Olympic team against the Dutch Olympic team in February 2008, where he was eligible for Beijing as underage player.</p>
<p>Montolivo was included in Italy's provisional squad for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a> by head coach <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Donadoni" title="Roberto Donadoni">Roberto Donadoni</a>. He was, however, omitted from the final squad of 23 players. The same summer, <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Casiraghi" title="Pierluigi Casiraghi">Pierluigi Casiraghi</a> called-up Montolivo to participate in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Olympic_Games" title="2008 Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. He scored one goal in four appearances as Italy were eliminated in the quarter-finals by Belgium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIGC_50-2"><a href="#cite_note-FIGC-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior_team">Senior team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riccardo_Montolivo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Senior team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2007, Montolivo was called by <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Donadoni" title="Roberto Donadoni">Roberto Donadoni</a> to face <a href="/wiki/Georgia_national_football_team" title="Georgia national football team">Georgia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying">UEFA Euro qualifiers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> He did not play in that match but made his debut in a 2–1 win in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_football_team" title="South Africa national football team">South Africa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> In May 2008, Montolivo was included in <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a>'s preliminary squad for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> but he was omitted from the 23-man final squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> However, Donadoni said that Montolivo would be a key part of Italy's midfield in the future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> His second appearance came over a year after the first, against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2010 FIFA World Cup qualification</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2009, he was selected to play in the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2009 FIFA Confederations Cup">2009 FIFA Confederations Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Italy were eliminated at the group stage. Montolivo started the match against <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> but played the other two matches as a substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> In 2010, Montolivo was named in <a href="/wiki/Marcello_Lippi" title="Marcello Lippi">Marcello Lippi</a>'s squad for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Due to the injury sustained by <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Pirlo" title="Andrea Pirlo">Andrea Pirlo</a>, he started Italy's first two group matches against <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguay</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_national_football_team" title="New Zealand national football team">New Zealand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> In the third match, Montolivo was again in the starting line-up but was replaced by Pirlo in the 56th minute, as the <i>Azzurri</i> lost 3–2 to <a href="/wiki/Slovakia_national_football_team" title="Slovakia national football team">Slovakia</a> and were eliminated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<p>Montolivo scored his first senior international goal in a 2–1 friendly win against <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bari" title="Bari">Bari</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> Montolivo made four appearances at the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>, playing as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#False-attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">false attacking midfielder</a> in Prandelli's 4–3–1–2 formation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Italy_squad_under_the_spotlight_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-Italy_squad_under_the_spotlight-69">[69]</a></sup> as Italy progressed to the final, where they lost 4–0 to Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faces_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-faces-71">[71]</a></sup> In the semi-final match against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>, he assisted <a href="/wiki/Mario_Balotelli" title="Mario Balotelli">Mario Balotelli</a>'s second goal of the match with a long ball, as the Italians advanced to the final after a 2–1 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>Montolivo was named in Italy's 30-man preliminary squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> under <a href="/wiki/Cesare_Prandelli" title="Cesare Prandelli">Cesare Prandelli</a>, but was ruled out of the tournament after suffering a broken leg in a friendly match against the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a> at <a href="/wiki/Craven_Cottage" title="Craven Cottage">Craven Cottage</a> on 31 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>After initially being named to <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Conte" title="Antonio Conte">Antonio Conte</a>'s 30-man provisional squad for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>, on 31 May 2016, Montolivo was omitted from the final 23-man squad due to injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> He returned to the national team under Conte's successor, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giampiero_Ventura" title="Giampiero Ventura">Giampiero Ventura</a>, making a substitute appearance in a 3–1 friendly home defeat to <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> on 1 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> However, in Italy's second <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2018 World Cup Qualifying</a> match on 6 October, a 1–1 home draw against Spain, he was forced off with yet another injury; it was later revealed that he suffered an anterior cruciate ligament injury to his left knee, which would rule him out for six months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riccardo_Montolivo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Montolivo has been described as a versatile player capable of playing anywhere in the <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> His main and favoured role is in the <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">centre</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Deep-lying_playmaker" title="Midfielder">deep-lying playmaker</a>, which best utilises his technical ability, creativity, vision, and range of passing, although he is also capable of playing as an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfielder</a>, even though this is not his preferred position;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> he also possesses an accurate and powerful shot from outside the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> With the Italian national team, under Cesare Prandelli, Montolivo also played in a new role as <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#False-attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">false attacking midfielder</a> in a 4–3–1–2 formation, in particular at Euro 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Italy_squad_under_the_spotlight_69-1"><a href="#cite_note-Italy_squad_under_the_spotlight-69">[69]</a></sup> In recent years, he has also been praised for his work-rate, and for his defensive attributes, including his ability to break down possession and subsequently start attacking plays,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> which led manager <a href="/wiki/Sini%C5%A1a_Mihajlovi%C4%87" title="Siniša Mihajlović">Siniša Mihajlović</a> to deploy him as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Defensive_midfielder" title="Midfielder">ball-winner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> Montolivo has also drawn criticism at times, however, for his lack of pace, and has been accused of being inconsistent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riccardo_Montolivo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Montolivo was born to a <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> mother and an Italian father in Milan but the couple soon relocated to <a href="/wiki/Caravaggio,_Lombardy" title="Caravaggio, Lombardy">Caravaggio</a> where he spent most of his childhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fiorentina_interview_86-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fiorentina_interview-86">[86]</a></sup> His brother, Luca, who is four years older than him, is a lawyer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Archiovio_87-0"><a href="#cite_note-Archiovio-87">[87]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to holding an <a href="/wiki/Italian_passport" title="Italian passport">Italian passport</a>; Montolivo also holds a <a href="/wiki/German_passport" title="German passport">German passport</a>—his mother is from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ascheberg_(Holstein)" title="Ascheberg (Holstein)">Ascheberg</a>, and his grandparents are from <a href="/wiki/Kieler_F%C3%B6rde#Locations" title="Kieler Förde">Heidkate</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Kieler_F%C3%B6rde" title="Kieler Förde">Kieler Förde</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a> coast, where he spent all of his summers as a youth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fiorentina_interview_86-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fiorentina_interview-86">[86]</a></sup> He looks upon <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Totti" title="Francesco Totti">Francesco Totti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zinedine_Zidane" title="Zinedine Zidane">Zinedine Zidane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Lampard" title="Frank Lampard">Frank Lampard</a> as his idols.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Archiovio_87-1"><a href="#cite_note-Archiovio-87">[87]</a></sup></p>
<p>Montolivo speaks fluent <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>.</p>
