<p><b>Ricardo Iván Rodríguez Araya</b> (born 25 August 1992) is a Swiss professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full_back" title="Defender (association football)">left back</a> for <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">Vfl Wolfsburg</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>.</p>
<p>Born to a Spanish father and a Chilean mother, Rodríguez began his professional career at local club <a href="/wiki/FC_Z%C3%BCrich" title="FC Zürich">FC Zürich</a> in 2010, having spent eight years in the youth setup. After breaking through into the first team at Zürich, Rodríguez was sold to <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">VfL Wolfsburg</a> in January 2012 for £7.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He has been described as an accomplished defender and is noted for his strong heading ability,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky_Sports_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sky_Sports-3">[3]</a></sup> and ability to deliver pinpoint crosses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIFA_Profile_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FIFA_Profile-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FFT_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FFT-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bleacher_Report_Scouting_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bleacher_Report_Scouting-6">[6]</a></sup> Rodríguez is also noted for his speciality in dead-ball situations and for penalty-taking abilities, emerging as Wolfsburg's first-choice penalty taker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bundesliga_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bundesliga-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Four_Two_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Four_Two-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodríguez has earned caps at every level of the Swiss national team setup, winning the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_U-17_World_Cup" title="FIFA U-17 World Cup">FIFA U17 World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup" title="2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup">2009</a>. A full international for <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a> since 2011, Rodríguez has earned over 40 caps for the country. He was part of the Swiss team at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> and later that year was voted <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Footballer_of_the_Year" title="Swiss Footballer of the Year">Swiss Footballer of the Year</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FC_Z.C3.BCrich">FC Zürich</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: FC Zürich">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rodríguez began playing football at the highly regarded youth club FC Schwamendingen in 2001 before joining the youth setup of <a href="/wiki/FC_Z%C3%BCrich" title="FC Zürich">FC Zürich</a> as an 11-year-old in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky_Sports_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sky_Sports-3">[3]</a></sup> He was promoted to the senior squad in 2009 at age 16, making the bench for Zürich's 2–3 defeat to <a href="/wiki/NK_Maribor" title="NK Maribor">NK Maribor</a> in the first leg of their <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Champions_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round#First_leg_3" title="2009–10 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round">third qualifying round</a> Champions League tie on 29 July 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky_Sports_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Sky_Sports-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>He made his Zürich debut as a 17-year-old in the 25th round of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Swiss_Super_League" title="2009–10 Swiss Super League">Swiss Super League</a> on 21 March 2010, replacing the injured <a href="/wiki/Hannu_Tihinen" title="Hannu Tihinen">Hannu Tihinen</a> in the first-half of the 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/AC_Bellinzona" title="AC Bellinzona">Bellinzona</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> His full debut came in the <a href="/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich_Derby" title="Zürich Derby">Zürich Derby</a> against <a href="/wiki/Grasshopper_Club_Z%C3%BCrich" title="Grasshopper Club Zürich">Grasshoppers</a> on 5 April, playing the entire 3–2 win for the hosts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>He made only his second start in the first match of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Swiss_Super_League" title="2010–11 Swiss Super League">Swiss Super League</a> season on 20 July 2010, playing the full 90 minutes in a 2–3 defeat to <a href="/wiki/FC_Basel#Supporters_and_rivalries" title="FC Basel">rivals</a> <a href="/wiki/FC_Basel" title="FC Basel">FC Basel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 28 April 2011, he scored his first professional goal for the club, opening the scoring for the hosts as they ran out 3–0 winners over <a href="/wiki/Neuch%C3%A2tel_Xamax" title="Neuchâtel Xamax">Neuchâtel Xamax</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Letzigrund" title="Letzigrund">Letzigrund</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodríguez made his <a href="/wiki/UEFA_competitions" title="UEFA competitions">European</a> debut in the first-leg of their <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round#First_leg_3" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round">third qualifying round</a> tie against <a href="/wiki/Standard_Li%C3%A8ge" title="Standard Liège">Standard Liège</a> on 27 July 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He played the entire match and provided the cross for fellow Zürich youth team graduate <a href="/wiki/Admir_Mehmedi" title="Admir Mehmedi">Admir Mehmedi</a> as the Swiss secured a 1–1 draw at the <a href="/wiki/Stade_Maurice_Dufrasne" title="Stade Maurice Dufrasne">Stade Maurice Dufrasne</a> in Belgium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In the second leg, Rodríguez missed from an open goal from close range but Zürich won 1–0 to earn a spot in the play-off round against <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He played the entirety of both <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League_qualifying_phase_and_play-off_round#Matches_4" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round">play-off matches</a> against Bayern,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> but the Swiss side fell 0–3 on aggregate and dropped into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011-12_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2011-12 UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 October 2011, Rodríguez scored his second goal for the club, netting his side's second from the penalty spot, in a 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/FC_Thun" title="FC Thun">FC Thun</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The club's final <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Swiss_Super_League" title="2011–12 Swiss Super League">Super League</a> match before the winter break on 10 December 2011, turned out to be Rodríguez's last in a Zürich shirt, playing the entire 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/FC_Sion" title="FC Sion">FC Sion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 11 January 2012, Zürich officials announced that the club had accepted a bid for Rodríguez by German side <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">Wolfsburg</a> and that he was on the verge of completing the transfer, subject to a medical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Despite being sold mid-way through the season, Rodríguez was voted as the Fans' Player of the Season on 4 June 2012, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Buff" title="Oliver Buff">Oliver Buff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Henrique_Konzen" title="Pedro Henrique Konzen">Pedro Henrique</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCZ_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-FCZ-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="VfL_Wolfsburg">VfL Wolfsburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: VfL Wolfsburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">VfL Wolfsburg</a> purchased Rodríguez as a 19-year-old on 13 January 2012 for £7.5 million with the player signing a four-and-a-half-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Four_Two_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Four_Two-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He quickly asserted himself into the first-team lineup, making his Bundesliga debut the next day against <a href="/wiki/1._FC_K%C3%B6ln" title="1. FC Köln">1. FC Köln</a>, a 1–0 victory for the Wolves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Rodríguez went on to play every game of the season from that point on without being substituted, becoming a favorite on the left of the defense for manager <a href="/wiki/Felix_Magath" title="Felix Magath">Felix Magath</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky_Sports_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Sky_Sports-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Through the first ten league games of the following campaign, Rodríguez was once again an ever-present in the squad, featuring in all the outings, starting eight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> But Magath was sacked after a poor run of form in the league and when <a href="/wiki/Lorenz-G%C3%BCnther_K%C3%B6stner" title="Lorenz-Günther Köstner">Lorenz-Günther Köstner</a> was installed as interim manager, the more experienced <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Sch%C3%A4fer" title="Marcel Schäfer">Marcel Schäfer</a> was preferred at left back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky_Sports_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Sky_Sports-3">[3]</a></sup> Once <a href="/wiki/Dieter_Hecking" title="Dieter Hecking">Dieter Hecking</a> was appointed manager though, Rodríguez won his place back in the first team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky_Sports_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-Sky_Sports-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314_season">2013–14 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013–14 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 9 November 2013, he scored his first goal for Wolfsburg, from a direct <a href="/wiki/Direct_free_kick" title="Direct free kick">free kick</a> in a home Bundesliga match against <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The match ended in a 2–1 win for Wolfsburg, although they were losing at half-time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> His second goal for Wolfsburg came from the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty spot</a> against <a href="/wiki/Hamburger_SV" title="Hamburger SV">Hamburg</a> on 29 November, earning his side a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> He opened the scoring in their Bundesliga match on 14 December, curling the ball past keeper <a href="/wiki/Sven_Ulreich" title="Sven Ulreich">Sven Ulreich</a>, as Wolfsburg won 3–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>He finished the 2013–14 season with five league goals, in addition to nine assists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Four_Two_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Four_Two-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He failed to make it into the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Bundesliga" title="2013–14 Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> Team of the Season, but was included in a list of the Best Defenders for the 2013–14 campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Rodríguez's nine assists also meant that he created more goals than any other full-back in Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FFT_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-FFT-5">[5]</a></sup> His impressive displays during the season included 2.3 key passes per game, the same number as <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Pirlo" title="Andrea Pirlo">Andrea Pirlo</a>, the two-time reigning <a href="/wiki/Serie_A_Footballer_of_the_Year" title="Serie A Footballer of the Year">Serie A Footballer of the Year</a>, and 2.6 successful dribbles per game, more than <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_Ronaldo" title="Cristiano Ronaldo">Cristiano Ronaldo</a>, the reigning <a href="/wiki/2013_FIFA_Ballon_d%27Or" title="2013 FIFA Ballon d'Or">Ballon d'Or</a> winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>He scored his first ever goal in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_competitions" title="UEFA competitions">European football</a> for Wolfsburg on 18 September 2014, netting directly from a free-kick after having previously scored an own-goal in a 4–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">English</a> side <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Three days later, Rodriguez scored twice more for Wolfsburg, once from the penalty spot and a volley from a <a href="/wiki/Kevin_De_Bruyne" title="Kevin De Bruyne">Kevin De Bruyne</a> corner, as Wolfsburg defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayer_Leverkusen" title="Bayer Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a> 4–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> He scored his third <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Bundesliga" title="2014–15 Bundesliga">league</a> goal of the season against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Werder_Bremen" title="Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a> on 27 September, converting a De Bruyne cross to put Wolfsburg up 1–0 in an eventual 2–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2014, Rodríguez was ruled out for a number of weeks with a troubled patella tendon and thigh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On 31 October, his agent, Roger Wittman, confirmed that Rodríguez and Wolfsburg had opened contract talks to extend his current deal beyond 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wolfsburg_ESPN_Rodriguez_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wolfsburg_ESPN_Rodriguez-40">[40]</a></sup> Rodríguez scored twice in Wolfsburg's 3–0 win away to <a href="/wiki/Lille_OSC" title="Lille OSC">Lille</a> on 11 December, a result which advanced his side into the Europa League knockout stage instead of their opponents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> In January 2015, he signed a new contract up to June 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> He scored the only goal of the game from the penalty spot on 7 April, as Wolfsburg defeated <a href="/wiki/SC_Freiburg" title="SC Freiburg">SC Freiburg</a> to reach the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/DFB-Pokal" title="DFB-Pokal">DFB-Pokal</a>, also making a goalline clearance later on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2015_DFB-Pokal_Final" title="2015 DFB-Pokal Final">the final</a> on 30 May, he played the full 90 minutes as Wolfsburg won their first cup, defeating <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> 3–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pokal_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-pokal-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.9316_season">2015–16 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2015–16 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Rodríguez played the full 90 minutes of Wolfsburg's victory over Bayern Munich in the <a href="/wiki/2015_DFL-Supercup" title="2015 DFL-Supercup">2015 DFL-Supercup</a> on 1 August, scoring their first attempt in the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout" title="Penalty shootout">penalty shootout</a> after a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supercup_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-supercup-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 April 2016, as Wolfsburg hosted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Madrid" title="Real Madrid">Real Madrid</a> in the first leg of the quarter-finals of <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League">the season's</a> Champions League, Rodríguez scored a penalty to open a surprise 2–0 victory. By sending <a href="/wiki/Keylor_Navas" title="Keylor Navas">Keylor Navas</a> the wrong way, he was the first player to score against Real's goalkeeper in the tournament for 738 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In 2009, he was part of the <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_under-17_football_team" title="Switzerland national under-17 football team">Swiss U17 team</a> who won the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup" title="2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup">2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bundesliga_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bundesliga-7">[7]</a></sup> His first goal of the tournament came in Switzerland's second <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup#Group_B" title="2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup">group game</a> against <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_under-17_football_team" title="Japan national under-17 football team">Japan</a> on 27 October 2009, netting the winning goal as the match ended 4–3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> He opened the scoring for the Swiss in the 35th minute against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_youth_football_team#Germany_national_under-17_squad" title="Germany national youth football team">Germany</a> in the Round of 16 as Switzerland eventually won 4–3 in <a href="/wiki/Association_football#Duration_and_tie-breaking_methods" title="Association football">added extra time</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIFA_U-17_World_Cup#Semi-finals" title="2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup">semi-finals</a> against <a href="/wiki/Colombia_national_under-17_football_team" title="Colombia national under-17 football team">Colombia</a>, he scored Switzerland's final goal of their 4–0 win to reach their first final of a <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> competition since a 3–0 loss to <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1924_Summer_Olympics#Gold_medal_match" title="Football at the 1924 Summer Olympics">1924 Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> He played the entire final against <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_under-17_football_team" title="Nigeria national under-17 football team">Nigeria</a> on 15 November, helping Switzerland to a 1–0 victory to become just the third European nation to lift the trophy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodríguez made his debut for <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a> in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">Euro 2012</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012_qualifying_Group_G" title="UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying Group G">qualifying match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales</a> on 7 October 2011, replacing <a href="/wiki/Xherdan_Shaqiri" title="Xherdan Shaqiri">Xherdan Shaqiri</a> in the second half of the 0–2 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIFA_Profile_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FIFA_Profile-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> In his full debt four days later, Rodríguez played the entire match as the Swiss defeated <a href="/wiki/Montenegro_national_football_team" title="Montenegro national football team">Montenegro</a> 2–0 to end their qualification campaign on a high, despite missing out on a playoff spot to the Balkan side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> In a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game#International_football" title="Exhibition game">friendly</a> match against the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> on 11 November, he played the entire match as the Swiss kept a clean sheet in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>He played all three matches for Switzerland at the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2012 Olympics</a>, in which they were eliminated in the group stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rodríguez featured in nine of ten <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_UEFA_Group_E" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group E">World Cup qualifying matches</a> for the Swiss, totaling 810 minutes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> as they qualified for the World Cup with a game to spare, thanks to a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania</a> on 11 October 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> On 13 May 2014, Rodríguez was named in Switzerland's squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> In their opening group game against <a href="/wiki/Ecuador_national_football_team" title="Ecuador national football team">Ecuador</a> on 15 June, he took the corner which was headed by <a href="/wiki/Admir_Mehmedi" title="Admir Mehmedi">Admir Mehmedi</a> for the equaliser, as well as crossing for <a href="/wiki/Haris_Seferovi%C4%87" title="Haris Seferović">Haris Seferović</a> to score the winner in a 2–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> During the tournament finals in Brazil, he averaged 5 tackles and 2.5 interceptions per game, earning plaudits for his strong tackling as well as his ability to read the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Four_Two_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Four_Two-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>At <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a> in France, Rodríguez played every minute as Switzerland reached the last 16. They were eliminated on penalties by <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saint-%C3%89tienne" title="Saint-Étienne">Saint-Étienne</a>, although Rodríguez scored his attempt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> On 8 October, he scored his first international goal on his 43rd cap, putting the Swiss into the lead in a 3–2 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_qualifying" title="2018 FIFA World Cup qualifying">2018 FIFA World Cup qualifying</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Hungary_national_football_team" title="Hungary national football team">Hungary</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Groupama_Arena" title="Groupama Arena">Groupama Arena</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_style">Playing style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Playing style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rodríguez is known for being equally adept at defending as he is going forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Experts consider him as an accomplished defender and note his strong heading ability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky_Sports_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-Sky_Sports-3">[3]</a></sup> As well as considering him a continual attacking threat and noting his exceptional stamina and pace which allow for his constant, buccaneering runs up the wing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky_Sports_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-Sky_Sports-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FFT_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-FFT-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Four_Two_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Four_Two-8">[8]</a></sup> Conversely, he has been criticised for giving too much room to opposition attackers, taking up poor positioning and lacking in defensive discipline,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Four_Two_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Four_Two-8">[8]</a></sup> and other analysts have criticised his concentration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>He has become known for his pinpoint crossing and accurate left foot and has been compared to <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> and <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> left back <a href="/wiki/Leighton_Baines" title="Leighton Baines">Leighton Baines</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIFA_Profile_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-FIFA_Profile-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FFT_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-FFT-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bleacher_Report_Scouting_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bleacher_Report_Scouting-6">[6]</a></sup> He is also noted for his speciality in dead ball situations and for penalty taking abilities, emerging as Wolfsburg's first-choice penalty taker during the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_VfL_Wolfsburg_season" title="2013–14 VfL Wolfsburg season">2013–14 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bundesliga_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bundesliga-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Four_Two_8-6"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Four_Two-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2014, his manager <a href="/wiki/Dieter_Hecking" title="Dieter Hecking">Dieter Hecking</a> praised Rodríguez as "the best left-back in the Bundesliga".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wolfsburg_ESPN_Rodriguez_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wolfsburg_ESPN_Rodriguez-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rodríguez was born in <a href="/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich" title="Zürich">Zürich</a> to a Spanish father and a Chilean mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bundesliga_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-Bundesliga-7">[7]</a></sup> As a result, he is fluent in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> and holds <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> nationalities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky_Sports_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-Sky_Sports-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bundesliga_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-Bundesliga-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> He is the younger brother of <a href="/wiki/FC_Z%C3%BCrich" title="FC Zürich">FC Zürich</a> <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roberto_Rodriguez_(Swiss_footballer)" title="Roberto Rodriguez (Swiss footballer)">Roberto Rodríguez</a> and the older brother of <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_under-19_football_team" title="Switzerland national under-19 football team">Switzerland U19</a> midfielder <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francisco_Rodriguez_(footballer,_born_1995)" title="Francisco Rodriguez (footballer, born 1995)">Francisco Rodríguez</a>, who also plays for VfL Wolfsburg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Four_Two_8-7"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Four_Two-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
