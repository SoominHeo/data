<p><b>Carlos Enrique "Ricky" Nolasco</b> (born December 13, 1982) is an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels_of_Anaheim" title="Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim">Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB). He is listed as 6'2" and 220, and made his major league debut on April 5, <a href="/wiki/2006_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2006 Major League Baseball season">2006</a>. He has also played for the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Marlins" title="Miami Marlins">Florida/Miami Marlins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a>. He is of Mexican descent.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Nolasco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nolasco attended <a href="/wiki/Rialto_High_School" title="Rialto High School">Rialto High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rialto,_California" title="Rialto, California">Rialto, California</a>. He graduated in 2001 and was drafted out of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_school" title="High school">high school</a> in the fourth round of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2001 Major League Baseball draft">2001 Major League Baseball draft</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Nolasco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chicago_Cubs">Chicago Cubs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Nolasco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chicago Cubs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nolasco began his professional career in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rookie-level" title="Rookie-level">Rookie-level</a> <a href="/wiki/Arizona_League" title="Arizona League">Arizona League</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_League_Cubs" title="Arizona League Cubs">Arizona League Cubs</a>. In five games with the team, including four starts, he recorded a 1-0 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Win-loss_record" title="Win-loss record">win-loss record</a> with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earned-run_average" title="Earned-run average">earned-run average</a> (ERA) of 1.50. He spent the 2002 season with the <a href="/wiki/Boise_Hawks" title="Boise Hawks">Boise Hawks</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_A-Short_Season" title="Class A-Short Season">Class A-Short Season</a> <a href="/wiki/Northwest_League" title="Northwest League">Northwest League</a>, earning a record of 7-2 with an ERA of 2.48 in 15 starts. The next season, Nolasco was assigned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daytona_Cubs" title="Daytona Cubs">Daytona Cubs</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_A-Advanced" title="Class A-Advanced">Class A-Advanced</a> <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_League" title="Florida State League">Florida State League</a>, where he went 11-5 with a 2.96 ERA in 26 starts.</p>
<p>Nolasco started the 2004 season in the <a href="/wiki/Double-A_(baseball)" title="Double-A (baseball)">Double-A</a> (AA) <a href="/wiki/Southern_League_(baseball)" title="Southern League (baseball)">Southern League</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Tenn_Diamond_Jaxx" title="West Tenn Diamond Jaxx">West Tenn Diamond Jaxx</a>. A month into the season, on May 16, he was called up to the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">Triple-A</a> <a href="/wiki/Iowa_Cubs" title="Iowa Cubs">Iowa Cubs</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a>. He made nine starts for Iowa, accumulating a record of 2-3 with an ERA of 9.30 before being sent back down to AA on June 28. Nolasco spent the rest of the season there; he finished the season with a record of 6-4 and an ERA of 3.70 in 19 starts throughout both stints with the Diamond Jaxx during the 2004 season. Nolasco then spent the entire 2005 season in AA as he achieved an impressive 14-3 record with an ERA of 2.89 in 27 starts.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Florida.2FMiami_Marlins">Florida/Miami Marlins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Nolasco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Florida/Miami Marlins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_Marlins" title="Florida Marlins">Florida Marlins</a> acquired him, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Mitre" title="Sergio Mitre">Sergio Mitre</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Renyel_Pinto" title="Renyel Pinto">Renyel Pinto</a> from the Cubs for <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pierre" title="Juan Pierre">Juan Pierre</a> on December 7, 2005.</p>
<p>He made the opening day roster in 2006 and made his debut with 3 scoreless innings of relief on April 5 against the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>. He recorded his first win with 2 innings of relief against the Cubs on April 26. He made his first start on May 22, also against the Cubs, and allowed 1 run in 7 innings to get the win.</p>
<p>Nolasco missed most of the 2007 season with an injury and then started the 2008 season as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_reliever" title="Middle reliever">middle reliever</a>, but quickly found his way to the top of the rotation as Florida's top starting pitcher. On August 19, 2008, he pitched a two-hitter against the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>. With one out in the 9th inning, he gave up a <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">double</a>; the only other <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hit</a> given up by him was a ball deflected off the glove of <a href="/wiki/First_baseman" title="First baseman">first baseman</a> <a href="/wiki/Mike_Jacobs_(first_baseman)" title="Mike Jacobs (first baseman)">Mike Jacobs</a>. Nolasco also had a two-run double in the game. Nolasco won 15 games in 2008, putting him in the top 5 in wins in the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Nolasco was named the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Opening_Day" title="Opening Day">Opening Day</a> starter, throwing in six innings and win against the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals" title="Washington Nationals">Washington Nationals</a>. On September 30, 2009, Nolasco struck out 16 batters, breaking <a href="/wiki/A._J._Burnett" title="A. J. Burnett">A. J. Burnett</a>'s record for 14 strikeouts in a single game for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_Marlins" title="Florida Marlins">Florida Marlins</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2010, Nolasco agreed to a contract extension with the Marlins to remain with the team through 2013.</p>
<p>On August 23, 2011, Nolasco became the Marlins franchise leader in strikeouts, overtaking <a href="/wiki/Dontrelle_Willis" title="Dontrelle Willis">Dontrelle Willis</a>. A year later on May 22, 2012 he won his 69th game, overtaking Willis' club record of 68 wins with the Marlins. He finished the season with a 4.48 ERA in 191 innings pitched, with 47 walks, 125 strikeouts, a 1.37 WHIP, and a 12-13 record.</p>

<div class="thumb tright">

<p>On July 6, 2013, he was traded to the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> for minor league pitchers <a href="/wiki/Josh_Wall" title="Josh Wall">Josh Wall</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steven_Ames" title="Steven Ames">Steven Ames</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%81ngel_S%C3%A1nchez_(pitcher)" title="Ángel Sánchez (pitcher)">Ángel Sánchez</a>. He made 15 starts for the Dodgers and was 8-3 with a 3.52 ERA.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minnesota_Twins">Minnesota Twins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Nolasco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Minnesota Twins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 27, 2013, Nolasco agreed to terms with the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a> on a 4-year, $49 million deal, pending completion of a physical examination. The deal became official on December 3, 2013. In his first season as a Twin, Nolasco posted a disappointing 5.38 ERA in 27 starts. Nolasco's 2015 season was cut short due to injury, recording a 6.75 ERA in 8 starts while also appearing in one game out of the bullpen. For the 2016 season, Nolasco was competing for a rotation spot with <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Duffey" title="Tyler Duffey">Tyler Duffey</a>. Duffey ended up being sent down to AAA before the season began, anointing Nolasco the 5th starter. He continued his regression from his previous two seasons, registering an ERA of 5.13 in 21 starts for the Twins.</p>
<p>The deal was largely seen as a massive overpay for a National League pitcher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Nolasco ended his three seasons with the Twins with a 15-22 record.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Los_Angeles_Angels_of_Anaheim">Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Nolasco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 1, 2016, the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a> traded Nolasco with <a href="/wiki/Alex_Meyer_(baseball)" title="Alex Meyer (baseball)">Alex Meyer</a> plus cash in exchange for Angels pitcher <a href="/wiki/Hector_Santiago" title="Hector Santiago">Hector Santiago</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pitching_style">Pitching style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Nolasco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Pitching style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nolasco throws five pitches: a <a href="/wiki/Four-seam_fastball" title="Four-seam fastball">four-seam fastball</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Sinker_(baseball)" title="Sinker (baseball)">sinker</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Split-finger_fastball" title="Split-finger fastball">split-finger fastball</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Slider" title="Slider">slider</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Knuckle_curve" title="Knuckle curve">knuckle curve</a> .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
