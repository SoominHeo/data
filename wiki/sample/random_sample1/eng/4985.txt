<p><b>Ricard</b> "<b>Ricky</b>" <b>Rubio i Vives</b> (born October 21, 1990) is a Spanish professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Timberwolves" title="Minnesota Timberwolves">Minnesota Timberwolves</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA). Rubio became the youngest player ever to play in the <a href="/wiki/Liga_ACB" title="Liga ACB">Spanish ACB League</a> on October 15, 2005, at age 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He made his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euroleague" title="Euroleague">Euroleague</a> debut on October 24, 2006, at age 16, becoming the first player born in the 1990s to play in a Euroleague game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On June 25, 2009, he was drafted with the fifth pick in the first round of the <a href="/wiki/2009_NBA_draft" title="2009 NBA draft">2009 NBA draft</a> by the Timberwolves,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> making him the first player born in the 1990s to be drafted by the NBA. The Timberwolves had an agreement in principle with his former Spanish team, <a href="/wiki/Joventut_Badalona" title="Joventut Badalona">DKV Joventut</a>, to buy out his contract, but Rubio backed out of the deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Backing_out_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Backing_out-5">[5]</a></sup> On August 31, 2009, Joventut traded the rights to Rubio to FC Barcelona,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rubio_to_Bar.C3.A7a_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rubio_to_Bar.C3.A7a-6">[6]</a></sup> and Rubio signed a six-year contract with <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona_B%C3%A0squet" title="FC Barcelona Bàsquet">FC Barcelona</a> the following day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bar.C3.A7a_signing_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bar.C3.A7a_signing-7">[7]</a></sup> On May 31, 2011, Rubio reached an agreement with Minnesota to join the Timberwolves for the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_NBA_season" title="2011–12 NBA season">2011–12 NBA season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wojnarowski1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-wojnarowski1-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Player_profile">Player profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Rubio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Player profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:RickyRubio1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2475" data-file-width="2294" height="183" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/RickyRubio1.jpg/170px-RickyRubio1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/RickyRubio1.jpg/255px-RickyRubio1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/RickyRubio1.jpg/340px-RickyRubio1.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:RickyRubio1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Rubio playing in Spain</div>

</div>
<p>Rubio is regarded by some as the best European guard prospect ever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Rubio has been compared by some to <a href="/wiki/Pete_Maravich" title="Pete Maravich">Pete Maravich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009_NBA_draft">2009 NBA draft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Rubio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2009 NBA draft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 24, 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Timberwolves" title="Minnesota Timberwolves">Minnesota Timberwolves</a> acquired the 5th pick in the <a href="/wiki/2009_NBA_draft" title="2009 NBA draft">2009 NBA draft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Etan_Thomas" title="Etan Thomas">Etan Thomas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Darius_Songaila" title="Darius Songaila">Darius Songaila</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oleksiy_Pecherov" title="Oleksiy Pecherov">Oleksiy Pecherov</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Wizards" title="Washington Wizards">Washington Wizards</a>, in exchange for <a href="/wiki/Randy_Foye" title="Randy Foye">Randy Foye</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Miller_(basketball_player)" title="Mike Miller (basketball player)">Mike Miller</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> With the 5th pick, Minnesota selected Rubio, making him the first player born in the 1990s to be drafted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The Timberwolves had a total of four first round picks in the 2009 NBA draft. They used the 6th and 18th picks in the first round and chose two other point guards, <a href="/wiki/Jonny_Flynn" title="Jonny Flynn">Jonny Flynn</a> from <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_Orange_men%27s_basketball" title="Syracuse Orange men's basketball">Syracuse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ty_Lawson" title="Ty Lawson">Ty Lawson</a> from <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Tar_Heels_men%27s_basketball" title="North Carolina Tar Heels men's basketball">North Carolina</a>, surprising many in the NBA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Knicks.27_interest_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Knicks.27_interest-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skeets_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Skeets-14">[14]</a></sup> Lawson's draft rights were soon traded to the <a href="/wiki/Denver_Nuggets" title="Denver Nuggets">Denver Nuggets</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skeets_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Skeets-14">[14]</a></sup> The Timberwolves then used the 28th pick to select <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Ellington" title="Wayne Ellington">Wayne Ellington</a>, a shooting guard from <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Tar_Heels_men%27s_basketball" title="North Carolina Tar Heels men's basketball">North Carolina</a>.</p>
<p>The following day, Rubio's father told a Spanish media outlet that his son might return to Spain for at least one more season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Knicks.27_interest_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Knicks.27_interest-13">[13]</a></sup> Rubio did not attend an introductory press conference by the Timberwolves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rubio_won.27t_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rubio_won.27t-15">[15]</a></sup> Rubio had a buyout clause in his Joventut contract reported by various media outlets as ranging from $6.6 million to $8.2 million, and Timberwolves general manager <a href="/wiki/David_Kahn_(sports_executive)" title="David Kahn (sports executive)">David Kahn</a> admitted that Rubio would probably have to play one more season in Spain to reduce or avoid the buyout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rubio_won.27t_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rubio_won.27t-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_draft">After the draft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Rubio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: After the draft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 22, the <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> reported that Rubio was seeking to leave Joventut, regardless of whether he was able to come to the NBA. The report indicated that ACB powers <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona_B%C3%A0squet" title="FC Barcelona Bàsquet">FC Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_Baloncesto" title="Real Madrid Baloncesto">Real Madrid</a> were both interested in signing Rubio if he could not immediately come to the NBA. The main stumbling block to Rubio's NBA move was a clause in the league's collective bargaining agreement that prohibited a team from paying more than $500,000 toward a player's contract buyout. Barça and Real, as ACB clubs, were not subject to this rule and could pay far more toward a buyout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In another development, the <i><a href="/wiki/St._Paul_Pioneer_Press" title="St. Paul Pioneer Press">St. Paul Pioneer Press</a></i> reported on August 4 that Rubio's representatives had been actively seeking endorsement deals in Minnesota to help finance his buyout (the NBA buyout rules are binding on teams, but not on third parties).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, on August 31, 2009, <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> reported that Rubio would not come to the NBA until 2011 at the earliest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rubio_to_Bar.C3.A7a_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rubio_to_Bar.C3.A7a-6">[6]</a></sup> Joventut expressed a willingness to reduce the buyout, and the Timberwolves apparently had a deal to bring him to the NBA, but in the end Rubio did not feel ready to come across the Atlantic. Rubio issued the following statement:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Backing_out_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Backing_out-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>The reason leading me to take this next step is to have a period of preparation to better take the challenge of the NBA in better conditions as a player. The Minnesota Timberwolves continue to be my first option and I wish to play with them in the near future.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Kahn believed that Rubio and his family were being pressured by the Spanish media, his national teammates, and people in his hometown to stay in Spain, adding, "It's just been a tough summer. From an 18-year-old's perspective and his family's perspective, it was very nerve-racking".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Backing_out_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Backing_out-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>ESPN reported that his original buyout with Joventut was <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>5.7 million ($8.1 million) in both 2009 and 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rubio_to_Bar.C3.A7a_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rubio_to_Bar.C3.A7a-6">[6]</a></sup> Barça bought out Rubio's contract for €3.5 million ($5.0 million), and announced on September 1 that he had signed a six-year deal with the club. The contract gave him the option to go to the NBA after the 2010–11 season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCB_release_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FCB_release-18">[18]</a></sup> with ESPN reporting that the buyout at that time would be a more manageable $1.4 million (of which the Timberwolves could pay $500,000).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Backing_out_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Backing_out-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Rubio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Rubio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Camiseta_autografata_de_Ricky_Rubio_(2009).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Camiseta_autografata_de_Ricky_Rubio_%282009%29.jpg/250px-Camiseta_autografata_de_Ricky_Rubio_%282009%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Camiseta_autografata_de_Ricky_Rubio_%282009%29.jpg/375px-Camiseta_autografata_de_Ricky_Rubio_%282009%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Camiseta_autografata_de_Ricky_Rubio_%282009%29.jpg/500px-Camiseta_autografata_de_Ricky_Rubio_%282009%29.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>

<p>Rubio debuted in the <a href="/wiki/Liga_ACB" title="Liga ACB">Spanish ACB League</a> in the 2005–06 season with <a href="/wiki/Joventut_Badalona" title="Joventut Badalona">DKV Joventut</a>. He won the <a href="/wiki/EuroChallenge" title="EuroChallenge">EuroChallenge</a> championship with Joventut that year. He led the Spanish ACB League in steals during the 2006–07 season. He also won the Spanish ACB League's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ACB_Rising_Star_Award" title="ACB Rising Star Award">Rising Star Award</a> that season. Rubio was also named the 2007, 2008, and 2009 <a href="/wiki/FIBA_Europe_Young_Men%27s_Player_of_the_Year_Award" title="FIBA Europe Young Men's Player of the Year Award">FIBA Europe Young Player of the Year</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He made his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euroleague" title="Euroleague">Euroleague</a> debut on October 24, 2006, versus <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_BC" title="Panathinaikos BC">Panathinaikos</a> of <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> as Joventut's backup point guard for <a href="/wiki/Elmer_Bennett" title="Elmer Bennett">Elmer Bennett</a>. Rubio averaged 2.8 assists per game in his first season of Euroleague play. He won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ULEB_Cup" title="ULEB Cup">Eurocup</a> championship with Joventut in 2008. He was also voted the Spanish ACB League's best point guard in 2008 and 2010. He won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euroleague" title="Euroleague">Euroleague</a> 2009–2010 with FC Barcelona.</p>
<p>Despite his notability, he was carefully shielded. <a href="/wiki/Joventut_Badalona" title="Joventut Badalona">DKV Joventut</a> and Rubio's parents agreed not to make him available for interviews until his 18th birthday on 21 October 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sweet16_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sweet16-20">[20]</a></sup> However, the restrictions were ended a few months early once he was selected for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spain_national_basketball_team" title="Spain national basketball team">Spanish national team</a> that would play at the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Basketball at the 2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-La_Pistola_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-La_Pistola-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rubio won the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Mr._Europa" title="Mr. Europa">Mr. Europa Award</a>, which is given out yearly by the Italian <a href="/wiki/Superbasket_Magazine" title="Superbasket Magazine">Superbasket Magazine</a>. In 2009, his club Badalona raised the amount of money that his contract paid per year from 80,000 euros <a href="/wiki/Net_income" title="Net income">net income</a> to 300,000 euros net income.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> He was named the Defensive Player of the Year for the Spanish ACB League 2008–09 season, and he also led the league in steals that season. Rubio declared himself eligible for the <a href="/wiki/2009_NBA_draft" title="2009 NBA draft">2009 NBA draft</a> on April 20, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Rubio's agent is <a href="/wiki/Dan_Fegan" title="Dan Fegan">Dan Fegan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minnesota_Timberwolves_.282011.E2.80.93present.29">Minnesota Timberwolves (2011–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Rubio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Minnesota Timberwolves (2011–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ricky_Rubio-MN_v_DET.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2848" data-file-width="2886" height="217" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Ricky_Rubio-MN_v_DET.jpg/220px-Ricky_Rubio-MN_v_DET.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Ricky_Rubio-MN_v_DET.jpg/330px-Ricky_Rubio-MN_v_DET.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Ricky_Rubio-MN_v_DET.jpg/440px-Ricky_Rubio-MN_v_DET.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>On June 1, 2011, sources reported that Rubio had "reached an agreement with Minnesota to join the Timberwolves" for the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_NBA_season" title="2011–12 NBA season">2011–12 NBA season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wojnarowski1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-wojnarowski1-8">[8]</a></sup> Rubio arrived off a plane in Minnesota to a barrage of fans waiting to welcome him to America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sbnation_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-sbnation-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On June 17, 2011, Rubio held a press conference announcing that he had signed with Minnesota.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sportingnews_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-sportingnews-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Becoming the 10th Spanish NBA player,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Rubio made his regular season debut on December 26, 2011, to a sellout crowd at the <a href="/wiki/Target_Center" title="Target Center">Target Center</a>, the team's first sellout crowd since the 2007–08 season. Rubio recorded 6 points, 5 rebounds and 6 assists in 26 minutes a 4-point loss to the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_Thunder" title="Oklahoma City Thunder">Oklahoma City Thunder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He was named Western Conference <a href="/wiki/NBA_Rookie_of_the_Month_Award" title="NBA Rookie of the Month Award">Rookie of the Month</a> after the first month of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbslocal_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbslocal-31">[31]</a></sup> In February, Rubio and teammate <a href="/wiki/Derrick_Williams_(basketball)" title="Derrick Williams (basketball)">Derrick Williams</a> were selected to participate in the 2012 Rising Stars challenge. However, Rubio was drafted to Team <a href="/wiki/Shaquille_O%27Neal" title="Shaquille O'Neal">Shaq</a>, while Williams was drafted to Team <a href="/wiki/Charles_Barkley" title="Charles Barkley">Chuck</a>. Rubio scored 22 points on February 19, 2012 in a win over the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In a loss to the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lakers" title="Los Angeles Lakers">Los Angeles Lakers</a> on March 9, 2012, Rubio tore his <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament" title="Anterior cruciate ligament">ACL</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Fibular_collateral_ligament" title="Fibular collateral ligament">lateral collateral ligament</a> defending <a href="/wiki/Kobe_Bryant" title="Kobe Bryant">Kobe Bryant</a>. He missed the rest of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-go_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-go-34">[34]</a></sup> Rubio finished second in Rookie of the Year voting, trailing only <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a> rookie <a href="/wiki/Kyrie_Irving" title="Kyrie Irving">Kyrie Irving</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foxsportsnorth_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-foxsportsnorth-35">[35]</a></sup> Rubio had averaged 10.6 points, 8.2 assists and 2.2 steals in 41 games.</p>
<p>Rubio returned from his injury on December 15, 2012, in an overtime victory over the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Mavericks" title="Dallas Mavericks">Dallas Mavericks</a>, playing for 19 minutes and totaling 9 assists and 8 points.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> He then recorded his first career triple-double with 21 points, 13 rebounds and 12 assists in a win over the San Antonio Spurs on March 12, 2013. Rubio almost recorded his second career triple-double in a loss to the Memphis Grizzlies on March 30 with 23 points, 10 rebounds and 9 assists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Then on April 13, he recorded a career-high 24 points, 10 assists, 5 rebounds and 5 steals in a win over the Phoenix Suns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Rubio finished the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_NBA_season" title="2012–13 NBA season">2012–13 season</a> with averages of 10.7 points, 7.3 assists and 2.4 steals per game. He also finished second in steals per game, behind <a href="/wiki/Chris_Paul" title="Chris Paul">Chris Paul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 11, 2013, Rubio recorded his second career triple-double with 12 points, 14 assists and 10 rebounds in a 113–90 win over the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Lakers" title="Los Angeles Lakers">Los Angeles Lakers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 19, 2014, Rubio recorded a career-high 17 assists, along with 6 points, 7 rebounds and 2 steals, in a 104–91 win over the Indiana Pacers. Rubio also tied the Timberwolves' franchise record with his 17 assists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> On March 19, 2014, Rubio recorded his third career triple-double with 22 points, 15 assists and 10 rebounds in a 123–122 overtime win over the Dallas Mavericks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> On March 28, 2014, Rubio tied <a href="/wiki/Tyrone_Corbin" title="Tyrone Corbin">Tyrone Corbin</a> for the Wolves season-record in steals at 175 with his one steal in a 143–107 win over the Los Angeles Lakers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Two days later, he passed that record when he recorded 2 steals against the Brooklyn Nets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 31, 2014, Rubio signed a four-year, $56 million contract extension with the Timberwolves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> On November 8, 2014, he was ruled out indefinitely after he severely sprained his left ankle in the November 7 game loss to the Orlando Magic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Rubio returned to action on February 2, 2015 against the Dallas Mavericks, recording 10 points and 4 assists in a 100–94 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> On March 2, 2015, he recorded his fourth career triple-double with 18 points, 11 assists and 12 rebounds in a loss to the Los Angeles Clippers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> He later underwent surgery on his ankle in April 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 28, 2015, Rubio scored a career-high 28 points in a season opening win over the Los Angeles Lakers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> He later missed four games with a sore left knee, returning to action on November 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> On December 16, he recorded a near quadruple-double with 12 assists, 10 rebounds, 9 points and 8 steals in a loss to the New York Knicks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> On December 30, he tied a career high with 17 assists in a 94–80 win over the Utah Jazz. Having recorded 14 assists two nights prior in a loss to the San Antonio Spurs, Rubio became the first Wolves player with at least 14 assists in consecutive games since <a href="/wiki/Terrell_Brandon" title="Terrell Brandon">Terrell Brandon</a> did so in April 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> On March 11, 2016, Rubio's three-pointer with 0.2 seconds remaining lifted the Timberwolves to a 99–96 win over the Oklahoma City Thunder. He finished with 13 points and 12 assists for his eighth double-double of the season, as the Timberwolves snapped a 10-game losing streak against the Thunder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> On March 14 against the Phoenix Suns, for the fourth time in his career, Rubio had a 17-assist effort.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 11, 2017, in a win over the Houston Rockets, Rubio had a 17-assist effort for the fifth time in his career. Rubio also passed <a href="/wiki/Sam_Mitchell_(basketball)" title="Sam Mitchell (basketball)">Sam Mitchell</a> to move into fourth place on the team's career games played list with 292.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish_national_team">Spanish national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Rubio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Spanish national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Under-16_national_team">Under-16 national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Rubio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Under-16 national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2006, Rubio led the rising junior Spanish national team to the <a href="/wiki/FIBA_Europe_Under-16_Championship" title="FIBA Europe Under-16 Championship">FIBA Europe Under-16 Championship</a>. During the tournament Rubio achieved two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triple-double" title="Triple-double">triple-doubles</a> and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quadruple-double" title="Quadruple-double">quadruple-double</a>. In the 110–106 double overtime finale victory over Russia, Rubio scored 51 points, the first player to do so in <a href="/wiki/FIBA" title="FIBA">FIBA</a> tournaments since <a href="/wiki/Luol_Deng" title="Luol Deng">Luol Deng</a> (in 2001), grabbed 24 rebounds, made 12 assists, and stole the ball 7 times. He also forced the first overtime with a three-point, buzzer-beating shot from mid-court. Rubio was then named the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Most_Valuable_Player" title="Most Valuable Player">Most Valuable Player</a> of the tournament after leading it in points, rebounds, assists and steals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior_national_team">Senior national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ricky_Rubio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Senior national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The young Spaniard was selected to take part in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spain_national_basketball_team" title="Spain national basketball team">Spanish National Team</a> which participated at the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Basketball at the 2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Olympics Basketball Tournament</a> in Beijing, China. The team was composed of current and former NBA players such as <a href="/wiki/Pau_Gasol" title="Pau Gasol">Pau Gasol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Calder%C3%B3n_(basketball)" title="José Calderón (basketball)">José Calderón</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rudy_Fern%C3%A1ndez_(basketball)" title="Rudy Fernández (basketball)">Rudy Fernández</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gasol" title="Marc Gasol">Marc Gasol</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_L%C3%B3pez" title="Raúl López">Raúl López</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Garbajosa" title="Jorge Garbajosa">Jorge Garbajosa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Carlos_Navarro_(basketball)" title="Juan Carlos Navarro (basketball)">Juan Carlos Navarro</a>, among others.</p>
<p>He played in the 2008 Olympics Basketball Tournament's gold medal game against the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_basketball_team" title="United States men's national basketball team">USA's</a> <a href="/wiki/2008_United_States_men%27s_Olympic_basketball_team" title="2008 United States men's Olympic basketball team">"Redeem Team"</a>, and thus at 17 became the youngest basketball player to ever play in an Olympic basketball final. Rubio shared the Spanish national team's silver medal after his team suffered a 118–107 loss. The next year he was chosen to play for Spain at the <a href="/wiki/EuroBasket_2009" title="EuroBasket 2009">EuroBasket 2009</a> in Poland, where Spain won the gold medal by defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_national_basketball_team" title="Serbian national basketball team">Serbia</a> 85–63. During the tournament, Rubio averaged 22.7 minutes, 5.9 points, 2.2 rebounds, 3.9 assists and 1.4 steals per game.</p>
