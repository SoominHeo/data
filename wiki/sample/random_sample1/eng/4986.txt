<p><b>Liquiçá</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tetum" title="Tetum">Tetum</a>: <i>Likisá</i>) is a coastal city in <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">East Timor</a>, 32 km to the west of <a href="/wiki/Dili" title="Dili">Dili</a>, the national capital. Liquiçá is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Liqui%C3%A7%C3%A1_District" title="Liquiçá District">Liquiçá District</a>. The city has a population of 5,005 inhabitants.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liqui%C3%A7%C3%A1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A part of the colonial administration of East Timor was arrested in Maubara and Liquiçá during the Japanese occupation of East Timor from 1942 - 1945. As a consequence the two towns where spared from the air raids which devasted the rest of the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On 6 April 1999, in the campaign of intimidation and violence that preceded the referendum for East Timorese independence, about 200 persons were killed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liqui%C3%A7%C3%A1_Church_Massacre" title="Liquiçá Church Massacre">Liquiçá Church Massacre</a>, when members of the <a href="/wiki/Besi_Merah_Putih" title="Besi Merah Putih">Besi Merah Putih</a> militia, supported by <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesian</a> soldiers and police, attacked the parish church <i>Igreja de São João de Brito</i>. (The number of casualties is, not surprisingly, disputed by Indonesia).</p>
<p>During the leadup to the referendum on independence, most of the buildings in the city were destroyed. Only a few buildings from the Portuguese and Indonesian times remain. Starting in 1999, it became the district headquarters for the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=International_Police&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="International Police (page does not exist)">International Police</a>, assigned there by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNTAET" title="UNTAET">UNTAET</a>, under the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>. It also was the home base of operations for the <a href="/wiki/UNTAET_Crime_Scene_Detachment" title="UNTAET Crime Scene Detachment">UNTAET Crime Scene Detachment</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liqui%C3%A7%C3%A1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most of the inhabitants speak <a href="/wiki/Tocodede_language" title="Tocodede language">Tocodede</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights_and_Architecture">Sights and Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Liqui%C3%A7%C3%A1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sights and Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The former Headquarters of the Administrator de Liquiçá, built in 1938 in a <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Neoclassic" title="Neoclassic">neoclassical</a> style with a large porch with ten columns measuring 5 metres in height each, are one of the most impressive buildings in town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Nowerdays the building is used as an administration building of the municipality. The Primary School opposite the headquarters was built in the 1940s with four classrooms and one administration building.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The former Residence of the Administrator of Liquiçá was probably built before 1910 in a neoclassical style with an impressive staircase in front and a large garden with a pool behind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> During the Indonesian occupation, it served as a residence of the Indonesian administrator. Opposite the building a small park was laid out with several pavilions.</p>
<p>The Office of the Deputy Secretary of the Administrator was built in a colonial style in carved stone in 1936.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The building was renovated in 2016. It is currently used by the Ministry of Public Works. The former Municipal Market which is close by was built in the 1930s featuring a square plan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It had a corridor surrounded by small distribution outlets.</p>
<p>The former Municipal School, built before 1910 with stone masonry walls featuring two volumes, was the first school to be built in the district.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It served as a military pharmacy during the Indonesian occupation.</p>
<p>Hotel Tokodede was probably built between 1930 and 1950 in a privileged area of the town where colonial officers overnighted. Its terrace still offers a beautiful view of the coconut tree-lined sandy beach of Liquiçá. The building is still used for social events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Parish Church of Liquiçá <i>Igreja de São João de Brito</i> which became famous because of the Liquiçá Church Massacre was built in 1946.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The clock tower was added when the church was renovated in 2016.</p>
<p>The Customs House is a rectangular building of portuguese origin whose exact date of construction remains unknown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It might have been built around the middle of the 20th century. The building consisting of two halls which is close to the beach was destroyed in 1999.</p>
