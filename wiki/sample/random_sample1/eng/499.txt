<p>At 03:57 <a href="/wiki/Time_in_Taiwan" title="Time in Taiwan">local time</a> (19:57 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a>) on 6 February 2016, an <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">earthquake</a> with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moment_magnitude" title="Moment magnitude">moment magnitude</a> of 6.4 struck 28 km (17 mi) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Pingtung_City" title="Pingtung City">Pingtung City</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Meinong_District" title="Meinong District">Meinong District</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kaohsiung" title="Kaohsiung">Kaohsiung</a>. The earthquake struck at a depth of around 23 km (14 mi). Its comparatively shallow depth caused more intense reverberations on the surface.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The earthquake had a maximum intensity of VII (<i>Very strong</i>) on the <a href="/wiki/Mercalli_intensity_scale" title="Mercalli intensity scale">Mercalli intensity scale</a>, causing widespread damage and 117 deaths. Almost all of the deaths were caused by a collapsed residential building, named Weiguan Jinlong in <a href="/wiki/Yongkang_District" title="Yongkang District">Yongkang District</a>, except two others, who were killed in <a href="/wiki/Guiren_District" title="Guiren District">Guiren District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usgs-summary_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-usgs-summary-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc._Eart_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc._Eart-3">[3]</a></sup> Sixty-eight <a href="/wiki/Aftershock" title="Aftershock">aftershocks</a> have occurred.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-focustaiwan.tw1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-focustaiwan.tw1-8">[8]</a></sup> The earthquake was the deadliest earthquake in Taiwan since the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/921_earthquake" title="921 earthquake">921 earthquake</a> in 1999.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Taiwan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Taiwan is located in the <a href="/wiki/Ring_of_Fire" title="Ring of Fire">Ring of Fire</a>, making it prone to intense earthquakes. However the February 2016 event was particularly destructive and deadly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Taiwan lies on the boundary between the <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Plate" title="Eurasian Plate">Eurasian Plate</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Sea_Plate" title="Philippine Sea Plate">Philippine Sea Plate</a>, which are converging at 80 mm per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usgs-summary_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-usgs-summary-1">[1]</a></sup> The island is the result of uplift caused by the collision between the northern end of the Luzon <a href="/wiki/Volcanic_arc" title="Volcanic arc">Arc</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Continental_margin" title="Continental margin">continental margin</a> of China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lin_et_al_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lin_et_al-10">[10]</a></sup> The earthquake's <a href="/wiki/Focal_mechanism" title="Focal mechanism">focal mechanism</a> indicates oblique <a href="/wiki/Thrust_fault" title="Thrust fault">thrust faulting</a> in the mid to upper <a href="/wiki/Crust_(geology)" title="Crust (geology)">crust</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usgs-summary_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-usgs-summary-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The earthquake's epicenter was in the <a href="/wiki/Meinong_District" title="Meinong District">Meinong District</a> in Kaohsiung City. The earthquake waves traveled northwest from the epicenter to <a href="/wiki/Tainan" title="Tainan">Tainan</a> through the soft soil in the <a href="/wiki/Chianan_Plain" title="Chianan Plain">Chianan Plain</a>. Due to the soft soil nature of Tainan, surface ground motion due to the traveling wave was strongly amplified, thus creating more devastating effects in Tainan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Damage">Damage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Taiwan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Damage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The worst affected city was <a href="/wiki/Tainan" title="Tainan">Tainan</a>, where numerous buildings reportedly collapsed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> including at least one 17-story residential building in <a href="/wiki/Yongkang_District" title="Yongkang District">Yongkang District</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc._Eart_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-bbc._Eart-3">[3]</a></sup> with hundreds of people trapped in the collapsed buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dail_BREA_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-dail_BREA-15">[15]</a></sup> 115 people have died inside the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Weiguan_Jinlong_building&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Weiguan Jinlong building (page does not exist)">Weiguan Jinlong building</a> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%B6%AD%E5%86%A0%E9%87%91%E9%BE%8D%E5%A4%A7%E6%A8%93%E5%80%92%E5%A1%8C%E4%BA%8B%E6%95%85" title="zh:維冠金龍大樓倒塌事故">zh</a>) in Tainan City, including a six-month-old <a href="/wiki/Infant" title="Infant">infant</a> who died a few hours later in a hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Officials reported that 397 people were rescued, with 104 of them taken to a hospital. More than 500 people in total were injured from a disaster that struck during the most important family holiday in the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_calendar" title="Chinese calendar">Chinese calendar</a> — the <a href="/wiki/Lunar_New_Year" title="Lunar New Year">Lunar New Year</a> holiday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>A total of 34 historical buildings around Taiwan were damaged, in which 23 of them are located in Tainan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Public Work Bureau of <a href="/wiki/Kaohsiung_City_Government" title="Kaohsiung City Government">Kaohsiung City Government</a> discovered 314 broken bridges in the city and 5 of them deemed unusable anymore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Power_Company" title="Taiwan Power Company">Taiwan Power Company</a> initially reported that 168,000 households experienced <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">power outage</a> after the quake, but since then has restored the electricity to most of the houses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/National_Fire_Agency" title="National Fire Agency">National Fire Agency</a> reported that about 400,000 households were left without water supply.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taipeitimes.com_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-taipeitimes.com-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Taiwan_High_Speed_Rail" title="Taiwan High Speed Rail">Taiwan High Speed Rail</a> (THSR) cancelled all train services between <a href="/wiki/THSR_Taichung_Station" title="THSR Taichung Station">Taichung Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xinzuoying_Station" title="Xinzuoying Station">Zuoying Station</a> starting Saturday due to damage to the train power systems and extensive damage to its tracks north of Tainan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Later in the day, THSR made an announcement that the service between Taichung and <a href="/wiki/THSR_Chiayi_Station" title="THSR Chiayi Station">Chiayi Station</a> resumed after successful repair works.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/TSMC" title="TSMC">Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company</a> reported that the <a href="/wiki/Wafer_(electronics)" title="Wafer (electronics)">silicon wafers</a> at its Tainan factory were damaged, although it did not affect much of its shipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taipeitimes.com2_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-taipeitimes.com2-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/United_Microelectronics_Corporation" title="United Microelectronics Corporation">United Microelectronics Corporation</a> said that the earthquake triggered its plant safety measures to shut down the machines and that they would need recalibrating afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taipeitimes.com2_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-taipeitimes.com2-26">[26]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Interior_(Taiwan)" title="Ministry of the Interior (Taiwan)">Ministry of the Interior</a> immediately set up the <a href="/wiki/Central_Emergency_Operation_Center" title="Central Emergency Operation Center">Central Emergency Operation Center</a> (CEOC) shortly after 4 a.m. once the earthquake had occurred. From the CEOC, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="President of the Republic of China">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Ma_Ying-jeou" title="Ma Ying-jeou">Ma Ying-jeou</a> coordinated the rescue efforts and is expected to head to Tainan. <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Tainan" title="Mayor of Tainan">Tainan Mayor</a> <a href="/wiki/William_Lai" title="William Lai">William Lai</a> also set up emergency response minutes after the quake. <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Premier of the Republic of China">Premier</a> <a href="/wiki/Chang_San-cheng" title="Chang San-cheng">Chang San-cheng</a> has cancelled his original itinerary and is expected to head to Tainan as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Health_and_Welfare_(Taiwan)" title="Ministry of Health and Welfare (Taiwan)">Ministry of Health and Welfare</a> had launched six regional emergency operation centers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taipeitimes.com1_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-taipeitimes.com1-28">[28]</a></sup> The last survivors, a woman and her niece, were found 60 hours after the earthquake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Defense_(Republic_of_China)" title="Ministry of National Defense (Republic of China)">Ministry of National Defense</a> confirmed that <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_Army" title="Republic of China Army">army units</a> were dispatched for the rescue efforts consisting of 810 personnel, 11 medical teams, 24 search and rescue teams and 38 vehicles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taipeitimes.com_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-taipeitimes.com-22">[22]</a></sup> The Army Command Headquarters based in <a href="/wiki/Guiren_District" title="Guiren District">Guiren District</a> sent two helicopters to survey the damaged areas. Kaohsiung Armed Forces General Hospital dispatched 30 medical personnel to Tainan. <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_Armed_Forces" title="Republic of China Armed Forces">Armed Forces Chief of the General Staff</a> <a href="/wiki/Yen_Teh-fa" title="Yen Teh-fa">Yen Teh-fa</a> was planned to be stationed at the joint military operations command center to oversee the rescue efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> A total of 1,200 beds in four locations were prepared by the military for people who lost their homes due to the earthquake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tainan_Airport" title="Tainan Airport">Tainan Air Force Base</a> had been turned into a temporary shelter that could accommodate up to 1,400 people left homeless by the quake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taipeitimes.com1_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-taipeitimes.com1-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rescue teams from <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> provided advice on the search and rescue efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Rescuers used sensors to detect signs of life and avoided using heavy machinery for fear of further collapsing the rubble and potentially trapping any survivors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> On 13 February 2016, Tainan Mayor <a href="/wiki/William_Lai" title="William Lai">William Lai</a> declared the search and rescue mission for the earthquake victims was over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Investigation">Investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Taiwan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Investigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 February 2016, Tainan District Court approved prosecutors' request to detain three executives of a now-defunct Weiguan developer company that built the collapsed Weiguan Jinlong apartment complex. The prosecutors detained Lin Ming-hui (林明輝), former chairman of the Weiguan company, as well as two other former executives Chang Kui-an (張奎安) and Cheng Chin-kui (鄭進貴), on charges of <a href="/wiki/Professional_negligence_in_English_law" title="Professional negligence in English law">professional negligence</a> resulting in death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Reuters reported that witnesses saw large rectangular cooking-oil cans packed inside wall cavities of the collapsed apartment building, apparently having been used as building material, while Taiwan media reported the presence of <a href="/wiki/Polystyrene" title="Polystyrene">polystyrene</a> in supporting beams, mixed in with concrete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Taiwan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p><a href="/wiki/Taipei_101" title="Taipei 101">Taipei 101</a> put on a lighting display on Saturday night to pay tribute to the earthquake victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Tennis athletes <a href="/wiki/Chan_Yung-jan" title="Chan Yung-jan">Chan Yung-jan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chan_Hao-ching" title="Chan Hao-ching">Chan Hao-ching</a> dedicated their <a href="/wiki/Fed_Cup" title="Fed Cup">Fed Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>/<a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a> Group I victory to the earthquake victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>During her visit to Tainan, <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="President of the Republic of China">President</a>-elect <a href="/wiki/Tsai_Ing-wen" title="Tsai Ing-wen">Tsai Ing-wen</a> said that safety checks of old buildings and urban renewal will be the top priority of her administration, later stating that Taiwan has become a developed society that demands improved public infrastructure and better quality of life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> When meeting with the quake victims treated at hospital, Tsai promised that rescue efforts will continue and that rescue teams will not give up hope.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Donations">Donations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Taiwan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Donations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>To assist with post-earthquake relief and reconstruction efforts, Japan donated US$1 million, United States pledged US$500,000, and People's Republic of China donated 2 million yuan (approx. 321156.75 USD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tsai_Ing-wen" title="Tsai Ing-wen">Tsai Ing-wen</a> donated NT$1 million to the earthquake relief efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Taipei" title="New Taipei">New Taipei</a> <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_New_Taipei" title="Mayor of New Taipei">Mayor</a> <a href="/wiki/Eric_Chu" title="Eric Chu">Eric Chu</a> also donated NT$1 million to earthquake victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Changhua_County" title="Changhua County">Changhua County</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_county_magistrates_of_Changhua" title="List of county magistrates of Changhua">Magistrate</a> <a href="/wiki/Wei_Ming-ku" title="Wei Ming-ku">Wei Ming-ku</a> donated one month of his salary to the earthquake victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taipeitimes.com_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-taipeitimes.com-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Yulon" title="Yulon">Yulon Group</a> pledged NT$10 million while <a href="/wiki/Acer_Inc." title="Acer Inc.">Acer</a> donated NT$1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Foxconn" title="Foxconn">Foxconn</a> and Yunglin Healthcare Foundation donated NT$100 million each.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-focustaiwan.tw_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-focustaiwan.tw-46">[46]</a></sup> Lin Rung San Foundation of Culture and Social Welfare pledged NT$100 million for the victims. <a href="/wiki/Formosa_Plastics_Corp" title="Formosa Plastics Corp">Formosa Plastics Corp</a> pledged NT$50 million to help the rescue efforts. <a href="/wiki/Taishin_Financial_Holdings" title="Taishin Financial Holdings">Taishin Financial Holdings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Himax" title="Himax">Himax</a> each pledged NT$10 million to the <a href="/wiki/Tainan_City_Government" title="Tainan City Government">Tainan City Government</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Tzu_Chi" title="Tzu Chi">Buddhist Compassion Relief Tzu Chi Foundation</a> sent more than 1,000 volunteers to 15 locations in Tainan with blankets, winter bedding, clothes and food for the earthquake survivors. <a href="/wiki/Red_Cross_Society_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Red Cross Society of the Republic of China">Taiwan's Red Cross Society</a> mobilized more than 100 employees and volunteers to help with the relief efforts, while the <a href="/wiki/Red_Cross_Society_of_China" title="Red Cross Society of China">Red Cross Society of China</a> and Macau Red Cross have pledged US$303,030 and US$50,000 respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-focustaiwan.tw_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-focustaiwan.tw-46">[46]</a></sup> In addition, some Taiwanese and foreign celebrities, like <a href="/wiki/Jay_Chou" title="Jay Chou">Jay Chou</a> or <a href="/wiki/Huang_Xiaoming" title="Huang Xiaoming">Huang Xiaoming</a>, donated to the relief efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hotels around Tainan provided temporary free rooms for earthquake survivors to stay in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-focustaiwan.tw_46-2"><a href="#cite_note-focustaiwan.tw-46">[46]</a></sup> Restaurant owners setup temporary kitchens to provide hot foods for rescue workers and relatives of the people who are still trapped under the building rubble. <a href="/wiki/Heating_pad" title="Heating pad">Heating pads</a> and hand warmers were also distributed to the traffic police around the collapsed building.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reconstruction">Reconstruction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Taiwan_earthquake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reconstruction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Republic_of_China" title="Government of the Republic of China">government</a> plans to allocate NT$25 billion for the reconstruction after the damaged caused by the earthquake. The government will also provide rent assistance and banks will provide interest-free long-term loans. The government will also release report on land around Taiwan affected by <a href="/wiki/Soil_liquefaction" title="Soil liquefaction">soil liquefaction</a> and submersion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
