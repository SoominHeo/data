<p><i><b>Roccella tinctoria</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fungus" title="Fungus">fungus</a> in the genus <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roccella_(genus)" title="Roccella (genus)">Roccella</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homotypic_synonym" title="Homotypic synonym">homotypic synonym</a> of <i>Lecanora tinctoria</i> (DC.) Czerwiak., 1849. It was first described by <a href="/wiki/Augustin_Pyramus_de_Candolle" title="Augustin Pyramus de Candolle">Augustin Pyramus de Candolle</a> in 1805. It has the following <a href="/wiki/Variety_(botany)" title="Variety (botany)">varieties</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>R. t.</i> var. <i>portentosa</i></li>
<li><i>R. t.</i> var. <i>subpodicellata</i></li>
<li><i>R. t.</i> var. <i>tinctoria</i></li>
</ul>
<p>and <a href="/wiki/Form_(botany)" title="Form (botany)">formae</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>R. t.</i> f. <i>complanata</i></li>
<li><i>R. t.</i> f. <i>tinctoria</i></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roccella_tinctoria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is used to make <a href="/wiki/Litmus" title="Litmus">litmus</a>, a mixture of several organic compounds.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Orcinol" title="Orcinol">Orcinol</a>, a natural phenolic organic compound, occurs in many species of <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichens</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> including <i>R. tinctoria</i>.</p>
