<p><b>Lee Wai Tong</b> or <b>Li Huitang</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">李惠堂</span>; 16 October 1905 – 4 July 1979<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gb.cri.cn_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-gb.cri.cn-2">[2]</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a> international <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> player and <a href="/wiki/Head_coach" title="Head coach">head coach</a>. He is often regarded as the greatest Chinese footballer due to his accomplishments in winning several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Far_Eastern_Games" title="Far Eastern Games">Far Eastern Games</a> titles with the national team as well as captaining the national football squad to their first ever <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1936_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1936 Summer Olympics">Olympic tournament</a> in 1936, which was held in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sina_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sina-3">[3]</a></sup> This was also followed by having an extremely successful club career where he predominantly played for <a href="/wiki/South_China_AA" title="South China AA">South China</a> and helped establish the club in being the most successful team in Hong Kong history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-South_China_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-South_China-4">[4]</a></sup> After his retirement, he moved into management where he guided <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei_national_football_team" title="Chinese Taipei national football team">Republic of China</a> to win the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1954_Asian_Games" title="Football at the 1954 Asian Games">1954 Asian Games</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Wai_Tong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee Wai Tong was born in Tai Hang, Hong Kong as the third child to a construction firm owner before he moved back to his parents home of <a href="/wiki/Wuhua_County" title="Wuhua County">Wuhua County</a> in <a href="/wiki/Meizhou" title="Meizhou">Meizhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a>, China at the age of four. It was there that he was informally taught how to play football until to moved back to Hong Kong and was formally trained at <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_College,_Hong_Kong" title="Queen's College, Hong Kong">Queen's College, Hong Kong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sina_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sina-3">[3]</a></sup> He left school early initially to help his fathers construction company before he joined <a href="/wiki/South_China_AA" title="South China AA">South China AA</a> as a youth player in 1922. By 1923 he would graduate to the senior team where he quickly showed himself as being a highly talented <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>, which soon saw him called up to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_national_football_team" title="Chinese national football team">Chinese national football team</a> to represent them for the <a href="/wiki/1923_Far_Eastern_Championship_Games" title="1923 Far Eastern Championship Games">1923 Far Eastern Championship Games</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sina_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Sina-3">[3]</a></sup> Within the tournament China won the championship and Lee was considered the young stand out star of the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lee's reputation significantly grew when he returned to club football and he won the <a href="/wiki/1923%E2%80%9324_Hong_Kong_First_Division_League" title="1923–24 Hong Kong First Division League">1923–24 Hong Kong First Division League</a> title with South China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-South_China_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-South_China-4">[4]</a></sup> His international reputation was solidified when he was able to retain the Far Eastern Championship Games Gold medal in the <a href="/wiki/1925_Far_Eastern_Championship_Games" title="1925 Far Eastern Championship Games">1925 championship</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. These successful tournaments saw him reported as the greatest Chinese footballer at the time by the Chinese media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-news.ifeng.com_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-news.ifeng.com-7">[7]</a></sup> After these successes Lee returned to China and joined <a href="/wiki/Fudan_University" title="Fudan University">Fudan University</a> in Shanghai as an athletic director. This allowed him the opportunity to join Loh Hwa, a gregarious team who were built from <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s_University,_Shanghai" title="St. John's University, Shanghai">St. John's University, Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University" title="Shanghai Jiao Tong University">Shanghai Jiao Tong University</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Shanghai" title="University of Shanghai">University of Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jinan_University" title="Jinan University">Jinan University</a> and his own Fudan University sports team where they predominantly played in local and regional championships because they offered the only annual competitions within China during the amateur era.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1931 he returned to South China and won the <a href="/wiki/1932%E2%80%9333_Hong_Kong_First_Division_League" title="1932–33 Hong Kong First Division League">1932–33 Hong Kong First Division League</a> title for the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> This would soon be followed by winning the <a href="/wiki/1934%E2%80%9335_Hong_Kong_First_Division_League" title="1934–35 Hong Kong First Division League">1934–35</a> and <a href="/wiki/1935%E2%80%9336_Hong_Kong_First_Division_League" title="1935–36 Hong Kong First Division League">1935–36</a> league titles, which was the first time ever they were able to retain the title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> This was followed by China's first ever football participation in the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1936_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1936 Summer Olympics">1936 Summer Olympics</a> making them the joint first Asian nation along with Japan to take part in that tournament. Lee would, however struggle to see his nation participate in their first truly worldwide tournament when the Chinese government could only give 170,000 from the required 220,000 Yuan the team needed to get to the Berlin Olympics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-news.ifeng.com_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-news.ifeng.com-7">[7]</a></sup> To get the nessary funds China played a series of exhibition games against <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_national_football_team" title="Vietnam national football team">Vietnam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore_national_football_team" title="Singapore national football team">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia_national_football_team" title="Indonesia national football team">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_national_football_team" title="Malaysia national football team">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Myanmar_national_football_team" title="Myanmar national football team">Myanmar</a> and <a href="/wiki/India_national_football_team" title="India national football team">India</a> to gain the necessary funds for the trip. The team would reach their target and Lee would captain the side against <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_Olympic_football_team" title="Great Britain Olympic football team">Great Britain</a> in the last 16 within the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the Olympic games Lee's football career would be cut short by the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War" title="Second Sino-Japanese War">Second Sino-Japanese War</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Initially Lee continued with his club career with South China until Hong Kong was also <a href="/wiki/Japanese_occupation_of_Hong_Kong" title="Japanese occupation of Hong Kong">occupied by the Japanese</a>. Lee was able to escape to Guangdong and joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_Army" title="Chinese Army">Chinese Army</a> where he spent the war playing exhibition games to raise money for the war effort.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gb.cri.cn_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-gb.cri.cn-2">[2]</a></sup> He was promoted to Major General within the sports division before returning to South China after the war. After spending several seasons with the club he retired in 1948 at the age of 43.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Wai_Tong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee Wai Tong's first coaching experience came while he was still a player and he took a job with Fudan University while he played for Loh Hwa. With him also captaining the Chinese side and the team not having any permanent coach Lee would manage the side for the <a href="/wiki/1934_Far_Eastern_Championship_Games" title="1934 Far Eastern Championship Games">1934 Far Eastern Championship Games</a>, which he also played in as China won the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1948 the Chinese national team reappointed Lee as coach, this time for the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1948_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1948 Summer Olympics">Football at the 1948 Summer Olympics</a>. On a self-financed training course in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> he took the team away for three mouths while they prepared for the <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> Olympics. At the tournament China faced <a href="/wiki/Turkey_national_football_team" title="Turkey national football team">Turkey</a> in the last 16 but were defeated 4-0 within the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> After the defeat Lee returned to China in the middle of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a> and did not coach until after the conflict. By then his team had been split into two, Republic of China national football team (later renamed Chinese Taipei) and <a href="/wiki/China_national_football_team" title="China national football team">China national football team</a>. On 1954 Lee decided to join the Republic of China national football team as their first permanent coach and lead them to win the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1954_Asian_Games" title="Football at the 1954 Asian Games">1954 Asian Games</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>After winning the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1958_Asian_Games" title="Football at the 1958 Asian Games">Football at the 1958 Asian Games</a> Lee would move away from coaching and he became vice-chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei_Football_Association" title="Chinese Taipei Football Association">Chinese Taipei Football Association</a>. In 1965 he became vice-president of <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>, being the first Chinese to reach that position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
