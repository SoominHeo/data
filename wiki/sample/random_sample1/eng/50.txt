<p><b>November 21</b> is the 325th day of the year (326th in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 40 days remaining until the end of the year. This date is slightly more likely to fall on a Monday, Wednesday or Saturday (58 in 400 years each) than on Thursday or Friday (57), and slightly less likely to occur on a Tuesday or Sunday (56).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=November_21&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/164_BC" title="164 BC">164 BC</a> – <a href="/wiki/Judas_Maccabeus" title="Judas Maccabeus">Judas Maccabeus</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/Mattathias" title="Mattathias">Mattathias</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hasmonean_family" title="Hasmonean family">Hasmonean family</a>, restores the <a href="/wiki/Temple_in_Jerusalem" title="Temple in Jerusalem">Temple in Jerusalem</a>. This event is commemorated each year by the festival of <a href="/wiki/Hanukkah" title="Hanukkah">Hanukkah</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/235" title="235">235</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Anterus" title="Pope Anterus">Pope Anterus</a> succeeds <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pontian" title="Pope Pontian">Pontian</a> as the nineteenth <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pope</a>. During the persecutions of emperor <a href="/wiki/Maximinus_Thrax" title="Maximinus Thrax">Maximinus Thrax</a> he is <a href="/wiki/Martyr" title="Martyr">martyred</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1009" title="1009">1009</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_C%C3%B4ng_U%E1%BA%A9n" title="Lý Công Uẩn">Lý Công Uẩn</a> is enthroned as emperor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i_C%E1%BB%93_Vi%E1%BB%87t" title="Đại Cồ Việt">Đại Cồ Việt</a>, founding the <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BD_dynasty" title="Lý dynasty">Lý dynasty</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1386" title="1386">1386</a> – <a href="/wiki/Timur" title="Timur">Timur of Samarkand</a> <a href="/wiki/Timur%27s_invasions_of_Georgia" title="Timur's invasions of Georgia">captures and sacks</a> the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Georgia" title="Kingdom of Georgia">Georgian</a> capital of <a href="/wiki/Tbilisi" title="Tbilisi">Tbilisi</a>, taking King <a href="/wiki/Bagrat_V_of_Georgia" title="Bagrat V of Georgia">Bagrat V of Georgia</a> captive.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1620" title="1620">1620</a> – <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Colony" title="Plymouth Colony">Plymouth Colony</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mayflower_passengers" title="List of Mayflower passengers">settlers</a> sign the <a href="/wiki/Mayflower_Compact" title="Mayflower Compact">Mayflower Compact</a> (<a href="/wiki/November_11" title="November 11">November 11</a>, <a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">O.S.</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1676" title="1676">1676</a> – The Danish <a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">astronomer</a> <a href="/wiki/Ole_R%C3%B8mer" title="Ole Rømer">Ole Rømer</a> presents the first quantitative <a href="/wiki/Measurement" title="Measurement">measurements</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Speed_of_light" title="Speed of light">speed of light</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1783" title="1783">1783</a> – In Paris, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Fran%C3%A7ois_Pil%C3%A2tre_de_Rozier" title="Jean-François Pilâtre de Rozier">Jean-François Pilâtre de Rozier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Laurent_d%27Arlandes" title="François Laurent d'Arlandes">François Laurent d'Arlandes</a>, make the first untethered <a href="/wiki/Hot_air_balloon" title="Hot air balloon">hot air balloon</a> flight.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1789" title="1789">1789</a> – <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> ratifies the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Constitution" title="United States Constitution">United States Constitution</a> and is admitted as the 12th <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1832" title="1832">1832</a> – <a href="/wiki/Wabash_College" title="Wabash College">Wabash College</a> is founded in <a href="/wiki/Crawfordsville,_Indiana" title="Crawfordsville, Indiana">Crawfordsville, Indiana</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1861" title="1861">1861</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederate</a> President <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Davis" title="Jefferson Davis">Jefferson Davis</a> appoints <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judah_Benjamin" title="Judah Benjamin">Judah Benjamin</a> Secretary of War.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1877" title="1877">1877</a> – <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Edison" title="Thomas Edison">Thomas Edison</a> announces his invention of the <a href="/wiki/Phonograph" title="Phonograph">phonograph</a>, a machine that can record and play sound.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1894" title="1894">1894</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_Arthur,_China" title="Port Arthur, China">Port Arthur, China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lushunkou" title="Battle of Lushunkou">falls to the Japanese</a>, a decisive victory of the <a href="/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War" title="First Sino-Japanese War">First Sino-Japanese War</a>, after which Japanese troops are accused of the <a href="/wiki/Port_Arthur_massacre_(China)" title="Port Arthur massacre (China)">massacre</a> of the remaining inhabitants of the city.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1902" title="1902">1902</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Athletics_(NFL)" title="Philadelphia Athletics (NFL)">Philadelphia Football Athletics</a> defeated the <a href="/wiki/Kanaweola_Athletic_Club" title="Kanaweola Athletic Club">Kanaweola Athletic Club</a> of <a href="/wiki/Elmira,_New_York" title="Elmira, New York">Elmira, New York</a>, 39–0, in the first ever professional <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> <a href="/wiki/Night_game" title="Night game">night game</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1905" title="1905">1905</a> – <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Albert Einstein</a>'s paper, "Does the Inertia of a Body Depend Upon Its Energy Content?", is published in the journal <i>Annalen der Physik</i>. This paper reveals the relationship between energy and mass. This leads to the <a href="/wiki/Mass%E2%80%93energy_equivalence" title="Mass–energy equivalence">mass–energy equivalence</a> formula, <i>E</i> = <i>mc</i>².</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1910" title="1910">1910</a> – Sailors on board Brazil's most powerful military units, including the brand-new warships <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_battleship_Minas_Geraes" title="Brazilian battleship Minas Geraes"><i>Minas Geraes</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_battleship_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Brazilian battleship São Paulo"><i>São Paulo</i></a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_cruiser_Bahia" title="Brazilian cruiser Bahia"><i>Bahia</i></a>, violently rebel in what is now known as the <i>Revolta da Chibata</i> (<a href="/wiki/Revolt_of_the_Lash" title="Revolt of the Lash">Revolt of the Lash</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1916" title="1916">1916</a> – Mines from <a href="/wiki/SM_U-73" title="SM U-73">SM U-73</a> sink the <a href="/wiki/HMHS_Britannic" title="HMHS Britannic">HMHS <i>Britannic</i></a>, the largest ship lost in the First World War.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1918" title="1918">1918</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Estonia" title="Flag of Estonia">Flag of Estonia</a>, previously used by pro-independence activists, is formally adopted as the national flag of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Estonia" title="Republic of Estonia">Republic of Estonia</a>.</li>
<li>1918 – <a href="/wiki/Lw%C3%B3w_pogrom_(1918)" title="Lwów pogrom (1918)">A pogrom</a> takes place in Lwów (now <a href="/wiki/Lviv" title="Lviv">Lviv</a>); over three days, at least 50 Jews and 270 Ukrainian Christians are killed by Poles.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1920" title="1920">1920</a> – <a href="/wiki/Irish_War_of_Independence" title="Irish War of Independence">Irish War of Independence</a>: In <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, 31 people are killed in what became known as "<a href="/wiki/Bloody_Sunday_(1920)" title="Bloody Sunday (1920)">Bloody Sunday</a>". This included eleven British intelligence agents, fifteen Irish civilians, two <a href="/wiki/Irish_Republican_Army" title="Irish Republican Army">Irish Republican Army</a> prisoners, and three men caught in the crossfires.
