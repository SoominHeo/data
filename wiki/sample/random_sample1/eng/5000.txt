<p><b>Rick Remender</b> (born February 6, 1973) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People_of_the_United_States" title="People of the United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Comic_book" title="Comic book">comic book</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic_book_creator" title="Comic book creator">writer and artist</a> who resides in Los Angeles, California. He is best known for his work on Image Comics' "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Deadly_Class&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Deadly Class (page does not exist)">Deadly Class</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Fear_Agent" title="Fear Agent">Fear Agent</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Black_Science_(comics)" title="Black Science (comics)">Black Science</a>" as well as Marvel Comics' <i><a href="/wiki/Uncanny_X-Force" title="Uncanny X-Force">Uncanny X-Force</a></i></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rick_Remender&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While in the early stages of his comic book career, Remender worked in animation on such films as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Iron_Giant" title="The Iron Giant">The Iron Giant</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Anastasia_(1997_film)" title="Anastasia (1997 film)">Anastasia</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Titan_A.E." title="Titan A.E.">Titan A.E.</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Rocky_and_Bullwinkle" title="The Adventures of Rocky and Bullwinkle">The Adventures of Rocky and Bullwinkle</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1998 he teamed up with Harper Jaten and Rory Hensly to create the absurdist humor comic <i>Captain Dingleberry'</i>. After four issues self-publishing the series was picked up by SLG Publishing.</p>
<p>His next series, Black Heart Billy, was also at SLG with collaborator Kieron Dwyer.</p>
<p>In 1999, while producing Black Heart Billy, Remender produced the horror comic <i>Doll and Creature</i> with John Heebink and Mike Manley.</p>
<p>From 2000-2003 Remender went on to do co-create and direct an animated series <i>Swing Town</i> for Wild Brian Animation. He also produced comics and album covers for Fat Wreck Chords, a Bay Area punk label. During this time he taught comics, animation, and storyboards at the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Art_University" title="Academy of Art University">Academy of Art University</a>, inked The Avengers, and penciled numerous issues of the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles.</p>
<p>From 2004 to 2005 Remender launched <i>Fear Agent</i>, <i>Sea of Red</i> and <i>Strange Girl</i> with Image Comics, <i>Night Mary</i> at IDW, and he illustrated the comic book adaptation of the movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Man_With_the_Screaming_Brain" title="Man With the Screaming Brain">Man With the Screaming Brain</a></i>, by <a href="/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics" title="Dark Horse Comics">Dark Horse Comics</a>. A year later he drew the series <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last_Christmas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Last Christmas (page does not exist)">The Last Christmas</a></i>, which was written by <a href="/wiki/Brian_Posehn" title="Brian Posehn">Brian Posehn</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gerry_Dugan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gerry Dugan (page does not exist)">Gerry Dugan</a>.</p>
<p>During this time he would team up with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People_of_France" title="People of France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Artist" title="Artist">artist</a> <a href="/wiki/Paul_Renaud" title="Paul Renaud">Paul Renaud</a> for a <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Sonja" title="Red Sonja">Red Sonja</a></i> <a href="/wiki/One-shot_(comics)" title="One-shot (comics)">one-shot</a> published by <a href="/wiki/Dynamite_Entertainment" title="Dynamite Entertainment">Dynamite Entertainment</a> titled "Vacant Shell".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is also working on the horror line <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crawl_Space_(comics)" title="Crawl Space (comics)">Crawl Space</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Kieron_Dwyer" title="Kieron Dwyer">Kieron Dwyer</a>, which will include stories like <i><a href="/wiki/XXXombies" title="XXXombies">XXXombies</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He had previously worked with Dwyer on <i><a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Red" title="Sea of Red">Sea of Red</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Night_Mary" title="Night Mary">Night Mary</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Remender was working on the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics" title="Dark Horse Comics">Dark Horse Comics</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_End_League" title="The End League">The End League</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> as well as launching a giant robot series <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gigantic_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gigantic (comics) (page does not exist)">Gigantic</a></i>,.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He also relaunched <i>Fear Agent</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and started, for <a href="/wiki/Image_Comics" title="Image Comics">Image Comics</a>, a new horror miniseries called <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sorrow_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sorrow (comics) (page does not exist)">Sorrow</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>He became co-author of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punisher_War_Journal" title="Punisher War Journal">Punisher War Journal</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Matt_Fraction" title="Matt Fraction">Matt Fraction</a> from issue #19 to 25 and then was the main writer on the new and eighth <i><a href="/wiki/Punisher_(2009_series)" title="Punisher (2009 series)">Punisher</a></i> series. <a href="/wiki/IDW_Publishing" title="IDW Publishing">IDW Publishing</a> also released a "master edition" of <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Heart_Billy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Black Heart Billy (page does not exist)">Black Heart Billy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In April 2009, Remender signed an exclusive writing contract with <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> but was still able to release the series <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Last_Days_of_American_Crime&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Last Days of American Crime (page does not exist)">The Last Days of American Crime</a></i> through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radical_Comics" title="Radical Comics">Radical Comics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In late 2010 he launched the new title <i><a href="/wiki/Uncanny_X-Force" title="Uncanny X-Force">Uncanny X-Force</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>He served as a writer on Electronic Art's game <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dead_Space_(video_game)" title="Dead Space (video game)">Dead Space</a></i> and was the lead writer for Epic Games' <i><a href="/wiki/Bulletstorm" title="Bulletstorm">Bulletstorm</a></i>, which was released on February 22, 2011.</p>
<p>He has also done work for some punk CDs, including the <a href="/wiki/SideOneDummy_Records" title="SideOneDummy Records">SideOneDummy Records</a> 1999 compilation <i>Start Your Engines</i>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=NO_USE_FOR_A_NAME&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="NO USE FOR A NAME (page does not exist)">NO USE FOR A NAME</a>'s 2001 "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Live_in_a_Dive_album&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Live in a Dive album (page does not exist)">Live in a Dive album</a>", <a href="/wiki/Lagwagon" title="Lagwagon">Lagwagon</a>'s 2001 "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Live_in_a_Dive_album&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Live in a Dive album (page does not exist)">Live in a Dive album</a>", <a href="/wiki/NOFX" title="NOFX">NOFX</a>'s 2006 EP <i><a href="/wiki/Never_Trust_a_Hippy" title="Never Trust a Hippy">Never Trust a Hippy</a></i> and promo art for <a href="/wiki/3_Inches_of_Blood" title="3 Inches of Blood">3 Inches of Blood</a>.</p>
