<p><b>Richard Edward Bauer</b> (born January 10, 1977) is an American former <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a>, who played for the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB). He also played for the <a href="/wiki/LG_Twins" title="LG Twins">LG Twins</a> of the <a href="/wiki/KBO_League" title="KBO League">KBO League</a>.</p>
<p>Bauer is a 1995 graduate of <a href="/wiki/Centennial_High_School_(Idaho)" title="Centennial High School (Idaho)">Centennial High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Meridian,_Idaho" title="Meridian, Idaho">Meridian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Idaho" title="Idaho">Idaho</a> and played two seasons for <a href="/wiki/Treasure_Valley_Community_College" title="Treasure Valley Community College">Treasure Valley Community College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ontario,_Oregon" title="Ontario, Oregon">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RangersMediaGuide_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-RangersMediaGuide-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bauer was selected by the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a> in the fifth round of the <a href="/wiki/1997_in_baseball" title="1997 in baseball">1997</a> <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="Major League Baseball draft">amateur draft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RangersMediaGuide_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-RangersMediaGuide-1">[1]</a></sup> He was a <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">starting pitcher</a> in the Orioles <a href="/wiki/Minor_League_Baseball" title="Minor League Baseball">minor league</a> system from 1997 through <a href="/wiki/2001_in_baseball" title="2001 in baseball">2001</a>, compiling a 41-39 record in 119 games (114 starts) with a 4.39 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">ERA</a> in 666<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> <a href="/wiki/Innings_pitched" title="Innings pitched">innings pitched</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bauer made his major league debut in a start against the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a> on September 2, 2001, a 1-0 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loss_(baseball)" title="Loss (baseball)">loss</a> in which he surrendered just one <a href="/wiki/Run_(baseball)" title="Run (baseball)">run</a> on three <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hits</a> and two <a href="/wiki/Base_on_balls" title="Base on balls">walks</a> in 6<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> <a href="/wiki/Innings_pitched" title="Innings pitched">innings</a>. He pitched primarily in relief for Baltimore from 2001 through <a href="/wiki/2005_in_baseball" title="2005 in baseball">2005</a>, making 116 relief appearances and nine starts and compiling an 8-13 record with a 4.58 ERA in 240 innings pitched.</p>
<p>Bauer was released by the Orioles after the 2005 season and signed a minor league contract with <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas</a> in November 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_baseball_(C)#called_up" title="Glossary of baseball (C)">called up</a> to the Rangers April 7, <a href="/wiki/2006_in_baseball" title="2006 in baseball">2006</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and remained with the team throughout the 2006 season. In 57 relief appearances and one start, he pitched 71 innings and posted a 3-1 record with two <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)" title="Save (baseball)">saves</a> and a career-best 3.55 ERA. He was not available to pitch the final week of the season because of <a href="/wiki/Tendinitis" title="Tendinitis">tendinitis</a> in his pitching shoulder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January <a href="/wiki/2007_in_baseball" title="2007 in baseball">2007</a>, the Rangers avoided salary arbitration with Bauer when they signed him to a $730,000, one-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> But after a poor <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a>, he was <a href="/wiki/Designated_for_assignment" title="Designated for assignment">designated for assignment</a> and <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_transactions#Waivers" title="Major League Baseball transactions">waived</a> by the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He signed a minor league deal with the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Phillies</a> April 6, 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> but was released by their <a href="/wiki/International_League" title="International League">International League</a> affiliate, the <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Lynx" title="Ottawa Lynx">Ottawa Lynx</a>, June 20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He signed another minor league contract with the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Dodgers</a> on July 1 playing for their <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a> affiliate, the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_51s" title="Las Vegas 51s">Las Vegas 51s</a>. Bauer signed a minor league contract with an invitation to spring training with the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a> on November 30, 2007. On July 4, <a href="/wiki/2008_in_baseball" title="2008 in baseball">2008</a>, Bauer was designated for assignment by the Indians; he refused a minor league assignment and was released.</p>
<p>On July 11, 2008, Bauer signed a minor league contract with the Toronto Blue Jays and became a free agent at the end of the season. In November 2008, he re-signed with the Blue Jays. However, he was released at the end of spring training.</p>
<p>Bauer had signed with the <a href="/wiki/York_Revolution" title="York Revolution">York Revolution</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_League_of_Professional_Baseball" title="Atlantic League of Professional Baseball">Atlantic League</a> for the 2009 season, but May 13, 2009, signed with the <a href="/wiki/LG_Twins" title="LG Twins">LG Twins</a> in South Korea. He was released from the Twins with an injury on July 22, 2009.</p>
<p>On March 28, 2010, Bauer signed a minor league contract with the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a>.</p>
<p>On April 12, 2010, Bauer signed a contract with the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Ducks" title="Long Island Ducks">Long Island Ducks</a> of the Atlantic League of Professional Baseball. He was released, and on July 14 signed a contract with the <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_Barnstormers" title="Lancaster Barnstormers">Lancaster Barnstormers</a> of the Atlantic League.</p>
<p>Feb 21st 2012 signed a minor league contract with Los Angeles Dodgers but he was released during spring training.</p>
