<p><b>Richard</b> "<b>Rick</b>" <b>Savage</b> (born 2 December 1960) is the bass guitarist and one of the founding members of the English rock band, <a href="/wiki/Def_Leppard" title="Def Leppard">Def Leppard</a>. He is called "<b>Sav</b>" to avoid confusion between him and the other Rick in the band, drummer <a href="/wiki/Rick_Allen_(drummer)" title="Rick Allen (drummer)">Rick Allen</a>. Savage and lead singer <a href="/wiki/Joe_Elliott" title="Joe Elliott">Joe Elliott</a> are the only remaining original members of the band. The two of them, plus Allen, are also the only members of the band who have performed on every album.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rick_Savage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Richard Savage was born in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a>, England. Savage was educated at Tapton Secondary school in Sheffield. In his youth, he learned to play guitar along with his older brother. They played songs like <a href="/wiki/Rod_Stewart" title="Rod Stewart">Rod Stewart</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Maggie_May" title="Maggie May">Maggie May</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Don_McLean" title="Don McLean">Don McLean</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/American_Pie_(song)" title="American Pie (song)">American Pie</a>." Savage also pursued a career as a professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player with <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_United_F.C." title="Sheffield United F.C.">Sheffield United</a>, despite his devotion to rivals <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a>. Savage ultimately chose music and formed a band with a few schoolmates including <a href="/wiki/Tony_Kenning" title="Tony Kenning">Tony Kenning</a> and eventually <a href="/wiki/Pete_Willis" title="Pete Willis">Pete Willis</a>. As <a href="/wiki/Atomic_Mass_(band)" title="Atomic Mass (band)">Atomic Mass</a> they mostly played <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slade" title="Slade">Slade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deep_Purple" title="Deep Purple">Deep Purple</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimi_Hendrix" title="Jimi Hendrix">Jimi Hendrix</a>, and other popular <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">covers</a> of the time.</p>
<p>At one point, they decided that Willis was the better guitar player so Savage picked up the bass guitar. Needing a singer, the band auditioned an acquaintance of Pete's, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Elliott" title="Joe Elliott">Joe Elliott</a>. They subsequently picked up guitarist Steve Clark and changed their name to <a href="/wiki/Def_Leppard" title="Def Leppard">Def Leppard</a>.</p>
<p>Savage contracted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bell%27s_Palsy" title="Bell's Palsy">Bell's Palsy</a> around 1994. This causes his facial muscles to weaken and become partially paralyzed. He has recovered, but effects of the disease are still visible, especially when he's fatigued.</p>
<p>From an interview by Gordon Shearer on DefLeppard.com:</p>
<dl>
<dd>Shearer: "What about when you developed your <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bell%27s_Palsy" title="Bell's Palsy">Bell's Palsy</a>? Did that change the way you dealt with things? Lots of gossip, lots of comments. I could imagine going from the position that you were in – from ultimate rock sex god to...."</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Savage: "It was hard to come to terms with and to understand why. I found it was easy to get into the 'why me?' syndrome. Partly because of the disability. It was more not being able to eat properly, or not being able to sleep without putting a patch on your eye because your eye wouldn't close, and things like that. The effect that it has on your everyday life was the hardest thing. In comparison, it was a very minor debilitating disease. It keeps you humble seeing what Rick Allen went through and how he had to deal with things. So, I would rather have not got the disease, but I did and you make the best of what you got".</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rick_Savage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Savage is married to Paige, with whom he has three children (one from Paige's prior relationship with Robbie Fox).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rick_Savage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Savage currently uses custom basses made by <a href="/wiki/Jackson_Guitars" title="Jackson Guitars">Jackson</a> after endorsing <a href="/wiki/Washburn_Guitars" title="Washburn Guitars">Washburn</a> XB920 (4-string) and XB925 (5-string) bass guitars from 1999–2009. He formerly used <a href="/wiki/Hamer_Guitars" title="Hamer Guitars">Hamer</a> 4 and 5-string bass guitars for over 17 years, starting with their <a href="/wiki/Gibson_Guitar_Corporation" title="Gibson Guitar Corporation">Gibson</a> Explorer-inspired "Blitz" model during the <i><a href="/wiki/High_%27n%27_Dry" title="High 'n' Dry">High 'n' Dry</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Pyromania_(album)" title="Pyromania (album)">Pyromania</a></i> albums from 1982–1985 and <a href="/wiki/Hamer_Guitars" title="Hamer Guitars">Hamer</a>'s modernistic take on the Explorer shape, the "Scarab" model of which he mainly used a white model during the <i><a href="/wiki/Hysteria_(Def_Leppard_album)" title="Hysteria (Def Leppard album)">Hysteria</a></i> recording sessions and tour (although Savage continued to use a black-colored "Blitz" downtuned C#ADG for the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Pour_Some_Sugar_on_Me" title="Pour Some Sugar on Me">Pour Some Sugar on Me</a>" which can be seen on their <i><a href="/wiki/Live:_In_the_Round,_in_Your_Face" title="Live: In the Round, in Your Face">Live: In the Round, in Your Face</a></i> live video/DVD, and a Scarab model in black with a reverse headstock was seen in the "Rocket" video and their performance at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="MTV Video Music Awards">MTV Video Music Awards</a> in 1989) from 1985–1989. At the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Adrenalize" title="Adrenalize">Adrenalize</a></i> he used an array of Chaparral Max 5-string models in various colours including a black &amp; white "Granite" finish, one with a variation of the <i>Adrenalize</i> album artwork custom painted on it, and a black-painted model from 1990–1999. Savage switched endorsements to Washburn basses in the middle of the promotion cycle for the <i><a href="/wiki/Euphoria_(Def_Leppard_album)" title="Euphoria (Def Leppard album)">Euphoria</a></i> album in 1999, and a Natural Flame XB925 given to him by Washburn in 1999 has been his main instrument since then.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However the custom painted <a href="/wiki/Union_Jack" title="Union Jack">Union Jack</a> XB925 is his most well known bass,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> followed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_George%27s_Cross" title="Saint George's Cross">Saint George's Cross</a> XB925, with both paint schemes reproduced on his new Jackson basses in 2009. He acquired two more of the Jackson basses, one in a grey-toned Union Jack scheme and the other painted in the colors of Sheffield Wednesday with the team's logo. He used these on the 2011 Mirrorball tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Savage never has been a <a href="/wiki/Fingerstyle_guitar" title="Fingerstyle guitar">fingerstyle guitarist</a>, relying entirely on a <a href="/wiki/Plectrum" title="Plectrum">plectrum</a>, or "<a href="/wiki/Plectrum" title="Plectrum">pick</a>". In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Bass_Guitar_(magazine)" title="Bass Guitar (magazine)">Bass Guitar</a></i> magazine, he stated: "I'm a terrible player with my fingers and i always play with a pick. I love to have consistency within the sound no matter where you are on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fretboard" title="Fretboard">fretboard</a>. I've found that when I play with fingers, notes jump out and others are too muted. It would be a nightmare for the sound engineer just trying to compress the thing to make sure there's an equal balance coming off the stage."</p>
