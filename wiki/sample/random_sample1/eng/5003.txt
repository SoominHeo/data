<p><b>Richard Paul "Rick" Astley</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span></span> <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born 6 February 1966) is an English singer, songwriter, musician, and radio personality. His 1987 song, "<a href="/wiki/Never_Gonna_Give_You_Up" title="Never Gonna Give You Up">Never Gonna Give You Up</a>" was a No. 1 hit single in 25 countries, and won the 1988 <a href="/wiki/Brit_Awards" title="Brit Awards">Brit Award</a> for Best British Single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup> By the time of his retirement in 1993, Astley had sold approximately 40 million records worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Astley made a comeback in 2007, becoming an <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">Internet phenomenon</a> when his video "Never Gonna Give You Up" became integral to the meme known as "<a href="/wiki/Rickrolling" title="Rickrolling">rickrolling</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Astley was voted "Best Act Ever" by Internet users at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_Europe_Music_Awards_2008" title="MTV Europe Music Awards 2008">MTV Europe Music Awards 2008</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc1-7">[7]</a></sup> His 2016 album <i><a href="/wiki/50_(album)" title="50 (album)">50</a></i> debuted in the UK at No. 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50no1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-50no1-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Astley was born on 6 February 1966 in <a href="/wiki/Newton-le-Willows" title="Newton-le-Willows">Newton-le-Willows</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>, the fourth child of his family. His parents divorced when he was five, and Astley was brought up by his father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-9">[9]</a></sup> His musical career started when he was ten, singing in the local church choir.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LookStudio_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-LookStudio-10">[10]</a></sup> During his schooldays, Astley formed and played the drums in a number of local bands, where he met guitarist <a href="/wiki/David_Morris_(Conservative_politician)" title="David Morris (Conservative politician)">David Morris</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC12523570_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC12523570-11">[11]</a></sup> After leaving school at sixteen, Astley was employed during the day as a driver in his father's market-gardening business and played drums on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_club_circuit" title="Northern club circuit">Northern club circuit</a> at night in bands such as Give Way – specialising in covering <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beatles" title="Beatles">Beatles</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Shadows" title="The Shadows">Shadows</a> songs – and FBI, which won several local talent competitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LookStudio_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-LookStudio-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Signing_with_Stock_Aitken_Waterman">Signing with Stock Aitken Waterman</span></h3>
<p>In 1985, Astley was performing as a drummer with a soul band named FBI, with Morris on guitar. They were a well-known local band writing and performing their own music, gigging in pubs and clubs. When FBI's lead singer left the band, and Morris left to concentrate on his career in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hairdressing" title="Hairdressing">hairdressing</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC12523570_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC12523570-11">[11]</a></sup> Astley offered to be the lead vocalist. This was when he was noticed by the record producer <a href="/wiki/Pete_Waterman" title="Pete Waterman">Pete Waterman</a>, who persuaded him to come to London to work at the <a href="/wiki/Pete_Waterman_Entertainment" title="Pete Waterman Entertainment">Pete Waterman Limited</a> (PWL) recording studio,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> publishing his records. Under the tutelage of the production team of <a href="/wiki/Mike_Stock_(musician)" title="Mike Stock (musician)">Mike Stock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Aitken" title="Matt Aitken">Matt Aitken</a> and Pete Waterman, known as <a href="/wiki/Stock_Aitken_Waterman" title="Stock Aitken Waterman">Stock Aitken Waterman</a> (SAW), Astley was taught about the <a href="/wiki/Sound_recording_and_reproduction" title="Sound recording and reproduction">recording</a> process and groomed for his future career, supposedly starting off as the recording studio "<a href="/wiki/Gofer" title="Gofer">tea boy</a>". The reason for Astley to be hired as a "tape op" was to overcome his shyness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> SAW also hired most of FBI, including Morris as a guitarist/songwriter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC12523570_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC12523570-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Success">Success</span></h3>
<p>His first single was the little-known "When You Gonna", released as a collaboration with Lisa Carter, with little promotion. It did not chart. His first solo offering was "<a href="/wiki/Never_Gonna_Give_You_Up" title="Never Gonna Give You Up">Never Gonna Give You Up</a>", recorded on New Year's Day 1987, and released eight months later, in August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NGGYU_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-NGGYU-13">[13]</a></sup> Astley's distinctive rich, deep voice combined with dance-pop, made the song an immediate success,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> spending five weeks at the top of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Official_UK_Charts_Company" title="The Official UK Charts Company">British charts</a> and becoming the year's highest-selling single. The song was also a worldwide number one hit, topping the charts in 24 other countries, including the U.S., Australia, and Germany. It would become the first of 13 (worldwide) top 30 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chart-topper" title="Chart-topper">hit</a> singles for him. "Never Gonna Give You Up" won Best British single at the 1988 BPI awards (now called the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BRIT_Awards" title="BRIT Awards">BRIT Awards</a></i>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-talktalk_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-talktalk-15">[15]</a></sup> and he performed the hit in front of a global audience of 100 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>His next single was "<a href="/wiki/Whenever_You_Need_Somebody_(song)" title="Whenever You Need Somebody (song)">Whenever You Need Somebody</a>", which was released in October. The single was a recycled Stock, Aitken, Waterman song, originally recorded by <a href="/wiki/O%27Chi_Brown" title="O'Chi Brown">O'Chi Brown</a> in 1985. It became a successful European hit, reaching No. 1 in seven countries, including Germany and <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, following up the success of his debut single. It also reached No. 3 in the UK. It was not released in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 1987, the album <i><a href="/wiki/Whenever_You_Need_Somebody" title="Whenever You Need Somebody">Whenever You Need Somebody</a></i>, containing four tracks written by Astley, also reached <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chart-topper" title="Chart-topper">number one</a> in the UK and Australia, and No. 10 in the U.S. It was certified 4x Platinum in the UK and Canada, and 2x Platinum in the US. Overall <i>Whenever You Need Somebody</i> sold 15.2 million copies worldwide, making him the top-selling British act of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 1987, Astley released a cover version of the <a href="/wiki/Nat_King_Cole" title="Nat King Cole">Nat King Cole</a> classic "<a href="/wiki/When_I_Fall_in_Love" title="When I Fall in Love">When I Fall in Love</a>". This single is mainly remembered for a closely fought contest for UK <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christmas_Number_1" title="Christmas Number 1">Christmas Number 1</a>. Rivals <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a>, hoping to see their act the <a href="/wiki/Pet_Shop_Boys" title="Pet Shop Boys">Pet Shop Boys</a> reach No. 1, re-released the version by Nat King Cole.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> This led to a slowdown of purchases of Astley's version, allowing the Pet Shop Boys to reach the coveted top spot. Despite selling over 200,000 copies and gaining a Silver certification from the BPI,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> it peaked in the UK at No. 2 for two weeks. The re-release of Nat King Cole's version reached No. 4. The B side was a dance number "<a href="/wiki/My_Arms_Keep_Missing_You" title="My Arms Keep Missing You">My Arms Keep Missing You</a>", which was successful in mainland Europe.</p>
<p>Astley's fourth single release would be "<a href="/wiki/Together_Forever_(song)" title="Together Forever (song)">Together Forever</a>" in 1988, reaching No. 2 in the UK. It was denied the top spot by <i><a href="/wiki/Neighbours" title="Neighbours">Neighbours</a></i> sensation <a href="/wiki/Kylie_Minogue" title="Kylie Minogue">Kylie Minogue</a>'s debut "<a href="/wiki/I_Should_Be_So_Lucky" title="I Should Be So Lucky">I Should Be So Lucky</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> "Together Forever" was more successful in the U.S., topping the charts, making it his second U.S. chart topper. In 1989 he was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for Best New Artist, but lost to <a href="/wiki/Tracy_Chapman" title="Tracy Chapman">Tracy Chapman</a>.</p>
<p>His fifth and final release from his debut album was "<a href="/wiki/It_Would_Take_a_Strong_Strong_Man" title="It Would Take a Strong Strong Man">It Would Take a Strong Strong Man</a>". It was a more soulful song, when compared to his other releases, and was mainly intended for the North American market. Thus, it was not released in Britain. It was another hit for Astley, reaching No. 10 on the U.S. <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a></i>, and No. 1 in Canada. During the period between his debut release and his fifth single, Astley outsold every other artist in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In the UK, he was in the Top 40 every week for the first 6 months of his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A fire in the PWL studios destroyed much of Astley's new material,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup> causing a delay in the release of his second album. <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hold_Me_in_Your_Arms_(Rick_Astley_album)" title="Hold Me in Your Arms (Rick Astley album)">Hold Me in Your Arms</a></i> was released in January 1989, containing five singles, and reached No. 8 in the UK and No. 19 in the U.S., being certified platinum in the UK and gold in the U.S.</p>

Rick Astley was presented by <a href="/wiki/Stock_Aitken_Waterman" title="Stock Aitken Waterman">Stock Aitken Waterman</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Boy_next_door" title="Boy next door">boy next door</a> type act. After leaving PWL, Astley changed his image to a more mature and moody musician.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Astley's relationship with British media deteriorated significantly after the release of <i>Whenever You Need Somebody</i>, with the media calling him a "puppet" of Stock Aitken Waterman,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-talktalk_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-talktalk-15">[15]</a></sup> although Astley had written five of his new album's tracks. The negative press inevitably affected the sales of his singles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The first single from the album to be released was "<a href="/wiki/She_Wants_to_Dance_with_Me" title="She Wants to Dance with Me">She Wants to Dance with Me</a>", a number written by Astley. It was another successful single, reaching No. 6 on both the UK and US charts. "<a href="/wiki/Take_Me_to_Your_Heart_(Rick_Astley_song)" title="Take Me to Your Heart (Rick Astley song)">Take Me to Your Heart</a>" was the next single to be released from the album. It reached No. 8 in the UK and was not released in the U.S. "<a href="/wiki/Hold_Me_in_Your_Arms_(Rick_Astley_song)" title="Hold Me in Your Arms (Rick Astley song)">Hold Me in Your Arms</a>", a ballad written by Astley, reached No. 10 in the UK and was also not released in North America. The next two singles released from the album were intended for the North American market. "<a href="/wiki/Giving_Up_on_Love" title="Giving Up on Love">Giving Up on Love</a>" and a cover of <a href="/wiki/The_Temptations" title="The Temptations">The Temptations</a> song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ain%27t_Too_Proud_To_Beg" title="Ain't Too Proud To Beg">Ain't Too Proud To Beg</a>" charted at No. 38 and No. 89 in the U.S. respectively.</p>
<p>In December 1989, Astley set off on his first world tour, touring 15 countries including the UK, U.S., Australia, and Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup> By the end of the tour, he was tiring of the negative press, and wanted to explore alternative paths as a musician. Thus, he left his producers Stock Aitken Waterman, and RCA records bought out his contract with PWL.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Switching_to_soul_and_adult_contemporary">Switching to soul and adult contemporary</span></h3>
<p>By 1990, Astley had parted company with Stock Aitken Waterman. He also decided to leave his dance-pop days behind him, moving towards his passion, soul. This shift in musical genre led him to change his image too, ditching the <a href="/wiki/Boy_next_door" title="Boy next door">boy next door</a> look, presenting himself as a mature and passionate musician. His third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Free_(Rick_Astley_album)" title="Free (Rick Astley album)">Free</a></i>, was released in 1991 containing collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-talktalk_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-talktalk-15">[15]</a></sup> The album contained three singles, and reached No. 9 in the UK and No. 31 in the US.</p>
<p>He achieved one more major success with the 1991 <a href="/wiki/Ballad" title="Ballad">ballad</a> "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cry_for_Help_(song)" title="Cry for Help (song)">Cry for Help</a>", which reached No. 7 in both the UK and the US. The other two singles from <i>Free</i> were not as successful. "<a href="/wiki/Move_Right_Out" title="Move Right Out">Move Right Out</a>" reached No. 58 in the UK and No. 81 in the US; and "<a href="/wiki/Never_Knew_Love" title="Never Knew Love">Never Knew Love</a>" reached No. 70 in the UK and did not chart in the US. <i>Free</i> would mark the end of Astley's successful period, and "Cry for Help" would be the last Astley single to make the Top 10 in either the UK or US to date.</p>
<p>His next album, <i><a href="/wiki/Body_%26_Soul_(Rick_Astley_album)" title="Body &amp; Soul (Rick Astley album)">Body and Soul</a></i>, was released in 1993, and was largely an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adult_Contemporary" title="Adult Contemporary">Adult Contemporary</a> album. By the time the album was released, Astley had decided to retire from the <a href="/wiki/Music_industry" title="Music industry">music industry</a>. As a result, the album did not get a lot of promotion,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup> not charting in the UK but managing to make the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, peaking at No. 182. The two singles, "<a href="/wiki/The_Ones_You_Love" title="The Ones You Love">The Ones You Love</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Hopelessly" title="Hopelessly">Hopelessly</a>", performed very well on the U.S. adult contemporary chart, peaking at No. 19 and No. 4 respectively. "The Ones You Love" peaked at No. 48 in the UK but did not chart in the U.S. "Hopelessly" also crossed over and peaked at No. 28 on the US <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_100" title="Billboard 100">Billboard 100</a>, staying in the US top 40 for five weeks, and No. 33 in the UK. It was named as one of the most performed songs at the 1994 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadcast_Music_Incorporated" title="Broadcast Music Incorporated">BMI Awards</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and is one of the few songs to achieve BMI 'Million-Air' status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span></h3>
<p>After 1993, Astley retired from the music industry at the age of 27, deciding that family life was more important. During Astley's time out of the music business, he raised his daughter Emilie, born in 1992. Thus, for much of the 1990s and early 2000s, Astley remained largely out of the spotlight. Astley would later state that this was due to his growing frustration with business side of things.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surprisecomeback_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-surprisecomeback-26">[26]</a></sup> During this period he co-wrote "Mission Statement", a track for former <a href="/wiki/Marillion" title="Marillion">Marillion</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Fish_(singer)" title="Fish (singer)">Fish</a>'s 1999 solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Raingods_with_Zippos" title="Raingods with Zippos">Raingods with Zippos</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_singing">Return to singing</span></h3>
<p>Almost 10 years after <i>Body and Soul</i>, Astley returned to the music industry, signing a co-publishing deal with <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">Polydor</a> and releasing <i><a href="/wiki/Keep_It_Turned_On" title="Keep It Turned On">Keep It Turned On</a></i> in 2001. The album featured the single "<a href="/wiki/Sleeping_(Rick_Astley_song)" title="Sleeping (Rick Astley song)">Sleeping</a>", which became a minor club hit, thanks to a set of remixes from U.S. <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a> producer <a href="/wiki/Todd_Terry" title="Todd Terry">Todd Terry</a>. <i>Keep It Turned On</i> was only released in <a href="/wiki/Continental_Europe" title="Continental Europe">Continental Europe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Astley's first compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Rick_Astley_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Rick Astley album)">Greatest Hits</a></i>, was released in 2002, and reached No. 16 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>. With no promotion from Astley, it sold over 100,000 copies and was certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">British Phonographic Industry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2003 he charted at No. 10 in the UK as a songwriter with "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shakespeare%27s_(Way_With)_Words" title="Shakespeare's (Way With) Words">Shakespeare's (Way With) Words</a>" performed by short-lived boy band <a href="/wiki/One_True_Voice" title="One True Voice">One True Voice</a>.</p>
<p>In 2004, Astley toured for the first time in 14 years, which led him to a record contract with <a href="/wiki/Sony_BMG" title="Sony BMG">Sony BMG</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2005, Astley released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Portrait_(Rick_Astley_album)" title="Portrait (Rick Astley album)">Portrait</a></i>, in which he <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">covered</a> many classic standards such as "<a href="/wiki/Vincent_(song)" title="Vincent (song)">Vincent</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nature_Boy_(song)" title="Nature Boy (song)">Nature Boy</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/(They_Long_to_Be)_Close_to_You" title="(They Long to Be) Close to You">Close to You</a>". Astley and Sony BMG were unhappy with the result so the album was poorly promoted,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-12"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup> yet it managed to reach No. 26 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>.</p>
<p>In April 2008, the album <i>The Ultimate Collection: Rick Astley</i> was released by Sony BMG, and by early May it had reached #17 on the UK Top 40 Albums Chart,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> again with no promotion by Astley.</p>
<p>In September 2008, Astley was nominated for the 'Best Act Ever' award at the <a href="/wiki/MTV_Europe_Music_Awards" title="MTV Europe Music Awards">MTV Europe Music Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc1-7">[7]</a></sup> The push to make Astley the winner of the award continued after the announcement, as did efforts to encourage MTV to invite Astley to the awards ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> On 7 November, following a massive Internet campaign by fans, Astley won the award in Liverpool, but was not there in person to receive it. <a href="/wiki/Perez_Hilton" title="Perez Hilton">Perez Hilton</a> collected the prize on his behalf.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> On the back of this, "Never Gonna Give You Up" returned to the UK charts, over 21 years after it was released, peaking at No. 73 during the Christmas period.</p>

<p>During the late 2000s, Astley continued touring across the globe, touring with various other 1980s acts, such as <a href="/wiki/Boy_George" title="Boy George">Boy George</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belinda_Carlisle" title="Belinda Carlisle">Belinda Carlisle</a>, in the <i><a href="/wiki/Here_and_Now_Tour" title="Here and Now Tour">Here and Now Tour</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RickBio_2-13"><a href="#cite_note-RickBio-2">[2]</a></sup> In April 2009, he wrote an article for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Time_Magazine" title="Time Magazine">Time</a></i> about <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moot_(4chan)" title="Moot (4chan)">moot</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Astley was a special guest throughout <a href="/wiki/Peter_Kay" title="Peter Kay">Peter Kay</a>'s new tour, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tour_That_Doesn%27t_Tour_Tour...Now_On_Tour" title="The Tour That Doesn't Tour Tour...Now On Tour">The Tour That Doesn't Tour Tour...Now On Tour</a></i>, from 27 April to 22 May 2010. To mark the occasion, Rick Astley released a new single "<a href="/wiki/Lights_Out_(Rick_Astley_song)" title="Lights Out (Rick Astley song)">Lights Out</a>" on his own label on 7 June 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> This was Astley's first release in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> in 17 years. It was well received by radio, peaking No. 15 on the UK Airplay Charts, but it failed to become a commercial hit, reaching only No. 97 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>.</p>
<p>During the summer of 2010, Astley became a radio DJ for London's <a href="/wiki/Magic_Radio" title="Magic Radio">Magic FM</a>, presenting a Sunday show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The initial contract was for eight weeks, but he proved popular with listeners and his contract was extended till the end of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In December 2010, Astley co-hosted the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_Evans_Breakfast_Show" title="Chris Evans Breakfast Show">Chris Evans Breakfast Show</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2" title="BBC Radio 2">BBC Radio 2</a> with Peter Kay,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> and in March 2011 appeared in <a href="/wiki/Comic_Relief" title="Comic Relief">Comic Relief</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Red_Nose_Day_2011" title="Red Nose Day 2011">Red Nose Day</a> telethon on the BBC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2016 Astley released "<a href="/wiki/Keep_Singing" title="Keep Singing">Keep Singing</a>", from his forthcoming album, <i><a href="/wiki/50_(album)" title="50 (album)">50</a></i>. Interviewed by <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Holden" title="Amanda Holden">Amanda Holden</a> on the <i><a href="/wiki/Lorraine_(TV_programme)" title="Lorraine (TV programme)">Lorraine</a></i> show on 7 April, Astley explained that it was turning 50 which had prompted him to release the single. He said, "It was a big milestone. I got back in the studio and friends were telling me the material I was working on was pretty good. So I decided to go for it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surprisecomeback_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-surprisecomeback-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> On 31 May, the release date for Astley's <i>50</i> was revealed to be 10 June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The album reached number one on the Official UK Album Sales charts in the week of 17 June 2016 to 23 June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50no1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-50no1-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2016 Astley released the single "Dance", together with an official video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Rickrolling" title="Rickrolling">Rickrolling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Never_Gonna_Give_You_Up" title="Never Gonna Give You Up">Never Gonna Give You Up</a></div>
<p>In 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Date_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-Date-45">[45]</a></sup> Rick Astley became the subject of a <a href="/wiki/Viral_phenomenon" title="Viral phenomenon">viral</a> <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">Internet meme</a> known as <a href="/wiki/Rickrolling" title="Rickrolling">rickrolling</a>. In this prank, Web users are tricked into seeing Rick Astley's video "Never Gonna Give You Up" when they follow a link that claims to be something else.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Views of this video on various websites are now in the hundreds of millions. The phenomenon became so popular that on <a href="/wiki/April_Fools%27_Day" title="April Fools' Day">1 April 2008</a>, YouTube pranked its users by making every single featured video on its front page a rickroll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 November 2008, Astley participated in a live rickroll during the <i><a href="/wiki/Macy%27s_Thanksgiving_Day_Parade" title="Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade">Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade</a></i>, while the <i><a href="/wiki/Foster%27s_Home_for_Imaginary_Friends" title="Foster's Home for Imaginary Friends">Foster's Home for Imaginary Friends</a></i> characters were singing "Best Friend", the theme from the 1970s TV series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Courtship_of_Eddie%27s_Father" title="The Courtship of Eddie's Father">The Courtship of Eddie's Father</a></i>. Midway through the song, Astley emerged from the float and began to <a href="/wiki/Lip_sync" title="Lip sync">lip sync</a> his signature hit. At the end of Astley's performance, Cheese (a character from <i>Foster's</i>) shouted out "I like rickrolling!".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>Although the video garnered millions of hits on YouTube, Astley has earned almost no money from the meme. By 2010 he had received only US$12 in royalties from YouTube for his performance share.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Rick Astley is married to Lene Bausager, and the couple have a daughter, Emilie, who was born in 1992. Astley met Lene when she was working as a promoter with RCA in 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-9">[9]</a></sup> Lene is a film producer, and was nominated for the 2006 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Live_Action_Short_Film" title="Academy Award for Live Action Short Film">Best Live Action Short Film</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Sean_Ellis" title="Sean Ellis">Sean Ellis</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Cashback_(film)" title="Cashback (film)">Cashback</a></i>, where she was the main producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> The family lives in the London suburb of <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_London" title="Richmond, London">Richmond</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
