<p><b>Lynne Ann Cheney</b> (née <b>Vincent</b>; born August 14, 1941) is an author, scholar, and former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Talk-show" title="Talk-show">talk-show</a> host. She is the wife of the 46th <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_United_States" title="Vice President of the United States">Vice President of the United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dick_Cheney" title="Dick Cheney">Dick Cheney</a> and served as the <a href="/wiki/Second_Lady_of_the_United_States" title="Second Lady of the United States">Second Lady of the United States</a> from 2001 to 2009.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Childhood_and_education">Childhood and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lynne_Cheney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Childhood and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lynne Ann Vincent was born on August 14, 1941 in <a href="/wiki/Casper,_Wyoming" title="Casper, Wyoming">Casper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a>. Her mother, Edna Lolita (née Lybyer), became a deputy <a href="/wiki/Sheriff" title="Sheriff">sheriff</a>, and her father, Wayne Edwin Vincent, was an <a href="/wiki/Engineer" title="Engineer">engineer</a>. A descendant of <a href="/wiki/Mormon_pioneers" title="Mormon pioneers">Mormon pioneers</a>, and with roots in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deseret_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-deseret-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> she was raised <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presbyterian" title="Presbyterian">Presbyterian</a> and became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methodist" title="Methodist">Methodist</a> upon her marriage to Dick Cheney.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deseret_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-deseret-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cheney received her <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> degree in <a href="/wiki/English_literature" title="English literature">English literature</a> with highest <a href="/wiki/Honors_student" title="Honors student">honors</a> from <a href="/wiki/Colorado_College" title="Colorado College">Colorado College</a>. She continued her education with a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">Master of Arts</a> degree from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Colorado" title="University of Colorado">University of Colorado</a> in <a href="/wiki/Boulder,_Colorado" title="Boulder, Colorado">Boulder</a>, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PhD" title="PhD">PhD</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/19th_century_British_literature" title="19th century British literature">19th century British literature</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wisconsin%E2%80%93Madison" title="University of Wisconsin–Madison">University of Wisconsin–Madison</a>. (Her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dissertation" title="Dissertation">dissertation</a> was entitled "<a href="/wiki/Matthew_Arnold" title="Matthew Arnold">Matthew Arnold</a>'s Possible Perfection: A Study of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kantian" title="Kantian">Kantian</a> Strain in Arnold's Poetry".)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lynne_Cheney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lynne_Cheney_2007Apr17.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="713" data-file-width="515" height="208" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Lynne_Cheney_2007Apr17.jpg/150px-Lynne_Cheney_2007Apr17.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Lynne_Cheney_2007Apr17.jpg/225px-Lynne_Cheney_2007Apr17.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Lynne_Cheney_2007Apr17.jpg/300px-Lynne_Cheney_2007Apr17.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>

<p>Cheney served as the sixth <a href="/wiki/Chair_of_the_National_Endowment_for_the_Humanities" title="Chair of the National Endowment for the Humanities">Chair</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Endowment_for_the_Humanities" title="National Endowment for the Humanities">National Endowment for the Humanities</a> from 1986 to 1993. In 1995, she founded <a href="/wiki/American_Council_of_Trustees_and_Alumni" title="American Council of Trustees and Alumni">American Council of Trustees and Alumni</a>, a think tank devoted to reforming higher education.</p>
<p>She is a senior fellow in education and culture at the <a href="/wiki/American_Enterprise_Institute" title="American Enterprise Institute">American Enterprise Institute for Public Policy Research</a>. She also serves as a <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/director" title="wikt:director">director</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reader%27s_Digest_Association,_Inc" title="Reader's Digest Association, Inc">Reader's Digest Association, Inc</a>. From 1995 to 1998, Cheney served as the co-host of the Sunday edition of <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Crossfire_(TV_series)" title="Crossfire (TV series)">Crossfire</a></i>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Tony_Snow" title="Tony Snow">Tony Snow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cheney served on <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Corporation" title="Lockheed Corporation">Lockheed Corporation</a>'s board of directors from 1994 to 2001. She gave up the $120,000-a-year position shortly before her husband's inauguration. She had served on the Lockheed board's Finance, and Nominating and Corporate Governance committees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2000, she was mentioned as a possible conservative female pick for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Republican_Party" title="U.S. Republican Party">Republican</a> Vice Presidential nominee on the <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> ticket.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The appointed head of the nominating committee was her husband, Dick Cheney, then the CEO of <a href="/wiki/Halliburton" title="Halliburton">Halliburton</a>, who eventually emerged as Bush's choice.</p>
<p>As Second Lady she repeatedly spoke out against violent and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sexually_explicit" title="Sexually explicit">sexually explicit</a> <a href="/wiki/Lyrics" title="Lyrics">lyrics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">popular music</a>, including those of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, picking up on an issue that was originally made famous by former Vice President <a href="/wiki/Al_Gore" title="Al Gore">Al Gore</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Tipper_Gore" title="Tipper Gore">Tipper</a>. She also <a href="/wiki/Video_game_controversies" title="Video game controversies">criticized</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">developers</a> for similar content.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On an October 10, 2007, episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Show" title="The Daily Show">The Daily Show</a></i>, Cheney stated her opposition to a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_amendment" title="Constitutional amendment">constitutional amendment</a> banning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gay_marriage" title="Gay marriage">gay marriage</a>. Her daughter <a href="/wiki/Mary_Cheney" title="Mary Cheney">Mary</a> is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Openly_lesbian" title="Openly lesbian">openly lesbian</a> and Lynne Cheney, like her husband Dick, also supports gay marriage.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lynne_Cheney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cheney has one brother, Mark Vincent, who lives in Wyoming with his wife, Linda. She has been married to Richard "Dick" Cheney since 1964. They have two daughters and seven grandchildren. Their daughters are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Cheney" title="Elizabeth Cheney">Elizabeth Cheney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mary_Cheney" title="Mary Cheney">Mary Cheney</a>. Elizabeth, also known as Liz, was born July 28, 1966, and is married to <a href="/wiki/Philip_Perry" title="Philip Perry">Philip Perry</a>, the former general counsel for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_Homeland_Security" title="Department of Homeland Security">Department of Homeland Security</a>. They have five children. She graduated from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago" title="University of Chicago">University of Chicago</a> <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago_Law_School" title="University of Chicago Law School">Law School</a> in 1996 and has worked as an <a href="/wiki/International_law" title="International law">international law</a> attorney, consultant, and for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_Department" title="State Department">State Department</a>'s Near East Affairs Bureau. Mary was born March 14, 1969. Openly lesbian, she lives with her partner, Heather Roan Poe (born April 11, 1961), in <a href="/wiki/Great_Falls,_Virginia" title="Great Falls, Virginia">Great Falls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>.</p>
<p>Mary Cheney married Poe on June 22, 2012, in Washington, D.C. She gave birth to their first child, Samuel David Cheney, in May 2007, and to their second child, daughter Sarah Lynne Cheney, on November 18, 2009. She is one of her father's top campaign aides and closest confidantes. In July 2003, Mary became the director of vice presidential operations for the Bush-Cheney 2004 presidential re-election campaign. She was a vital part of the campaign. Until May 2000, she was the lesbian/gay corporate relations manager for the <a href="/wiki/Coors_Brewing_Company" title="Coors Brewing Company">Coors Brewing Company</a>. In 2006, she wrote a book about working with her father.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wyoming_U.S._Senate_seat_vacancy">Wyoming U.S. Senate seat vacancy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lynne_Cheney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Wyoming U.S. Senate seat vacancy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cheney was considered a possible contender to complete the term of <a href="/wiki/Craig_L._Thomas" title="Craig L. Thomas">Craig L. Thomas</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Senator" title="U.S. Senator">U.S. Senator</a> from Wyoming following his death in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> A spokesman stated<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (March 2014)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> that she was considering the post but she never signed an application to become a candidate. If she had won the seat, she would have become the first former "Second Lady" to serve in the Senate, or any public office,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> since <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muriel_Humphrey" title="Muriel Humphrey">Muriel Humphrey</a> was appointed Senator from Minnesota after her husband's death in 1978.</p>
