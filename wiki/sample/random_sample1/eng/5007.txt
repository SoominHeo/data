<p><b>Linda Perry</b> (born April 15, 1965)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Nominees" title="Grammy Nominees">Grammy nominated</a> <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>. She first became known as the lead singer and primary songwriter of <a href="/wiki/4_Non_Blondes" title="4 Non Blondes">4 Non Blondes</a> and has since founded two record labels and composed and produced hit songs for several other artists. They include: "<a href="/wiki/Beautiful_(Christina_Aguilera_song)" title="Beautiful (Christina Aguilera song)">Beautiful</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a>; "<a href="/wiki/What_You_Waiting_For%3F" title="What You Waiting For?">What You Waiting For?</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Gwen_Stefani" title="Gwen Stefani">Gwen Stefani</a>; and "<a href="/wiki/Get_the_Party_Started" title="Get the Party Started">Get the Party Started</a>" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P!nk" title="P!nk">P!nk</a>. Perry has also contributed to albums by <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Courtney_Love" title="Courtney Love">Courtney Love</a>, as well as signing and distributing <a href="/wiki/James_Blunt" title="James Blunt">James Blunt</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitquarters.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters.com-2">[2]</a></sup> in the United States. Perry was also inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Songwriters_Hall_of_Fame" title="Songwriters Hall of Fame">Songwriters Hall of Fame</a> in 2015.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linda_Perry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Perry was born in <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Massachusetts" title="Springfield, Massachusetts">Springfield, Massachusetts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">[1]</a></sup> Growing up in an artistic and musical household, Perry displayed musical talent and interest from an early age. Despite struggling with kidney disease and then drug addiction, she still focused on music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">[1]</a></sup> Her father is <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Americans" title="Brazilian Americans">Brazilian</a> and her mother is <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Americans" title="Portuguese Americans">Portuguese</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linda_Perry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1987.E2.80.9389:_Early_career">1987–89: Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linda_Perry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1987–89: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early 1986, at the age of 21, Perry moved to San Francisco. As a teenager in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, she had pursued an interest in music, playing the <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a> and auditioning for all-girl cover bands. Her arrival in San Francisco would mark the beginning of a career in music, though not before waiting tables, working coat-check, and working at a pizzeria.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In San Francisco, Perry lived in a small, windowless room, and would play her guitar and sing her own songs on city streets. Eventually, people began to recognize her as "that chick with the big voice."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1989.E2.80.9395:_4_Non_Blondes">1989–95: 4 Non Blondes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linda_Perry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1989–95: 4 Non Blondes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After some time spent paying her dues as a solo singer at Bay Area (San Francisco) clubs and coffeehouses, Perry composed her first professional song, called "Down On Your Face," and was recruited into the band <a href="/wiki/4_Non_Blondes" title="4 Non Blondes">4 Non Blondes</a> by its founder <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christa_Hillhouse" title="Christa Hillhouse">Christa Hillhouse</a> in the middle of <a href="/wiki/1989_in_music" title="1989 in music">1989</a>. After several years of playing locally and negotiating with various <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Record_companies" title="Record companies">record companies</a>, the band finally signed with <a href="/wiki/Interscope_Records" title="Interscope Records">Interscope Records</a> and released its debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bigger,_Better,_Faster,_More!" title="Bigger, Better, Faster, More!">Bigger, Better, Faster, More!</a></i> on October 9, 1992. The album, featuring Perry as lead singer and dominated by her compositions, was an immediate success and spawned a hit single, the Perry-penned "<a href="/wiki/What%27s_Up%3F_(song)" title="What's Up? (song)">What's Up?</a>" (often erroneously called "What's Going On?" after its prominent chorus lyric).</p>
<p>In February 1994, Perry had a solo appearance in <a href="/wiki/Roger_Daltrey" title="Roger Daltrey">Roger Daltrey</a>'s production, album and video <i><a href="/wiki/A_Celebration:_The_Music_of_Pete_Townshend_and_The_Who" title="A Celebration: The Music of Pete Townshend and The Who">A Celebration: The Music of Pete Townshend and The Who</a></i>. She shared the stage with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juilliard" title="Juilliard">Juilliard</a> Orchestra and members of <a href="/wiki/The_Who" title="The Who">The Who</a> as she sang "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_Jimmy" title="Doctor Jimmy">Doctor Jimmy</a>,""<a href="/wiki/Acid_Queen" title="Acid Queen">Acid Queen</a>," "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Free_(The_Who_song)" title="I'm Free (The Who song)">I'm Free</a>" (in a duet with Roger Daltrey), and the encore for "<a href="/wiki/Join_Together_(song)" title="Join Together (song)">Join Together</a>" with original The Who members <a href="/wiki/John_Entwistle" title="John Entwistle">John Entwistle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pete_Townshend" title="Pete Townshend">Pete Townshend</a>. In 1994, a CD and a VHS video of the shows were issued with the songs "Doctor Jimmy" and "I'm Free," while in 1998 a DVD was released with the song "I'm Free" only.</p>
<p>In June 1994, 4 Non Blondes guitarist <a href="/wiki/Roger_Rocha" title="Roger Rocha">Roger Rocha</a> was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Concrete_Blonde" title="Concrete Blonde">Concrete Blonde</a> member <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jim_Mankey" title="Jim Mankey">Jim Mankey</a>, who had just played for <a href="/wiki/Van_Halen" title="Van Halen">Van Halen</a>'s cover "<a href="/wiki/Van_Halen_(album)" title="Van Halen (album)">I'm the One</a>" recorded as the theme from the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Airheads" title="Airheads">Airheads</a></i>. During sessions at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interscope" title="Interscope">Interscope</a> studios in Los Angeles, the band struggled to come up with a followup to <i>Bigger</i>, and Perry left the band during the attempt to pursue a solo career. The band recorded one last video, for a cover of <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Misty_Mountain_Hop" title="Misty Mountain Hop">Misty Mountain Hop</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Dave_Navarro" title="Dave Navarro">Dave Navarro</a> on guitar.</p>
<p>In a 2011 <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> interview, while promoting her most recent band, <a href="/wiki/Deep_Dark_Robot" title="Deep Dark Robot">Deep Dark Robot</a>, Perry was quoted saying, "I wasn't really a big fan of my band" due to the "fluffy polished bullshit" sound of <i>Bigger, Better, Faster, More!</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>With the band in disarray and the recording contract unfulfilled, Interscope reluctantly allowed Perry to make a solo CD, dropping the rest of the band. Interscope insisted on choosing the album's producer; the production team selected was <a href="/wiki/Bill_Bottrell" title="Bill Bottrell">Bill Bottrell</a> and members of the <i><a href="/wiki/Tuesday_Night_Music_Club" title="Tuesday Night Music Club">Tuesday Night Music Club</a></i>, who had recently produced <a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Crow" title="Sheryl Crow">Sheryl Crow</a>'s debut. Released in <a href="/wiki/1996_in_music" title="1996 in music">1996</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/In_Flight_(Linda_Perry_album)" title="In Flight (Linda Perry album)">In Flight</a></i> was moody, mellow and, at times, dark, far removed from the 4 Non Blondes sound.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The release received positive notices, but was a poor seller. Perry joined Red Fish, Blue Fish for her world tour, supporting such acts as The Who.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> She promoted her CD with her own funds, including an appearance on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Howard_Stern_Show" title="The Howard Stern Show">The Howard Stern Show</a></i>, during which she participated in "lesbian dial-a-date" and performed her former band's only hit single, "What's Up?" Perry also hosted the 1997 and 1998 Bammies (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bay_Area_Music_Awards" title="Bay Area Music Awards">Bay Area Music Awards</a>).</p>
<p>In 1997, Perry produced a film, <i>Pink as the Day She Was Born</i>, featuring cameos by <a href="/wiki/Les_Claypool" title="Les Claypool">Les Claypool</a> of <a href="/wiki/Primus_(band)" title="Primus (band)">Primus</a> and comedian <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Cho" title="Margaret Cho">Margaret Cho</a>. She also launched her own record label, Rockstar Records, for the primary purpose of releasing a CD of band Stone Fox. In addition, she signed another local San Francisco band, 2 Lane Blacktop. The year 1998 saw Perry appearing and performing on <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a> as part of a special on music and the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>—how independent artists are cutting out the major labels. In 1999, she released her second solo album on Rockstar Records, <i><a href="/wiki/After_Hours_(Linda_Perry_album)" title="After Hours (Linda Perry album)">After Hours</a></i>. For the rest of 1999, she opened for <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Adams" title="Bryan Adams">Bryan Adams</a>, reuniting with 4 Non Blondes' bassist Christa Hillhouse, then toured behind <i>After Hours</i>, with Hillhouse and San Francisco-based drummer Claudia Page.</p>
<p>In 2001, Perry again returned to music and touring. She wrote a handful of songs, two of which, "Beautiful" and "Cruz," would eventually be recorded and appear on Christina Aguilera's hit album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stripped_(2002_album)" title="Stripped (2002 album)">Stripped</a></i>. In 2001, she closed most of her shows with "Beautiful," a song she hoped would be her own comeback hit.</p>
<p>In 2002, Perry made a rare live performance at the <a href="/wiki/Knitting_Factory" title="Knitting Factory">Knitting Factory</a> in Los Angeles, where she performed a set of original music and a set of Zeppelin covers. Also in 2002, she made an appearance on a solo record of <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Gano" title="Gordon Gano">Gordon Gano</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hitting_the_Ground" title="Hitting the Ground">Hitting the Ground</a></i>, performing the song "So It Goes."</p>
<p>Perry made a live appearance at a <a href="/wiki/DKNY" title="DKNY">DKNY</a> benefit show in Los Angeles, where she joined <a href="/wiki/Slash_(musician)" title="Slash (musician)">Slash</a> on stage for a version of Led Zeppelin's "<a href="/wiki/Whole_Lotta_Love" title="Whole Lotta Love">Whole Lotta Love</a>."</p>
<p>In 2005, Perry was a performer at ASCAP's showcase at the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>. In August 2006, she played piano on Christina Aguilera's performance of "Hurt" at the MTV Video Music Awards.</p>
<p>Perry has signed with the independent label <a href="/wiki/Kill_Rock_Stars" title="Kill Rock Stars">Kill Rock Stars Records</a>, who re-released her solo album <i>In Flight</i> in October 2005. Featuring new artwork, the re-release includes original videos for "Fill Me Up" and "Freeway". In 2008, Perry played guitar in the video for Christina Aguilera's "Save Me From Myself." Perry made a live appearance at "Hope For Haiti Now" playing piano while Aguilera premiered a stripped-down version of their song "Lift Me Up." She also made guest appearances on Hole's "Nobody's Daughter" tour and on a concert of the Perry-signed band <a href="/wiki/Little_Fish_(band)" title="Little Fish (band)">Little Fish</a> in London. In May 2010, Perry again hosted the "Evening with Women" at L.A.'s Gay and Lesbian Centre, where she reunited with P!nk for a live duet of Perry's song "What's Up?" Christina Aguilera invited Perry to her VH1 Storytellers concert in May 2010. Perry played piano to Aguilera's "Beautiful" and talked about the song.</p>
<p>On November 5, 2010, Perry appeared live in San Francisco with 4 Non Blondes guitar player Roger Rocha at TwentyFifty (formerly CELLSpace). The appearance celebrated the release of Rocha's album with his band The Golden Hearts. It was the first time in 20 years the two had appeared together live. Perry performed a solo set of cover tunes, including <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Creep_(Radiohead_song)" title="Creep (Radiohead song)">Creep</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Soundgarden" title="Soundgarden">Soundgarden's</a> "<a href="/wiki/Black_Hole_Sun" title="Black Hole Sun">Black Hole Sun</a>." Perry and Roger Rocha performed one song together, Led Zeppelin's "<a href="/wiki/Since_I%27ve_Been_Loving_You" title="Since I've Been Loving You">Since I've Been Loving You</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001.E2.80.932010:_Production_and_songwriting">2001–2010: Production and songwriting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linda_Perry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2001–2010: Production and songwriting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, Perry was contacted by pop rock singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P!nk" title="P!nk">Pink</a>, seeking production and songwriting assistance on her second album. Perry commented on the experience in an interview with <a href="/wiki/HitQuarters" title="HitQuarters">HitQuarters</a>: "She was this white girl singing R&amp;B music, and it made absolutely no sense to me why she would call me. So when I got together with her my instinct said, 'Don’t go and be an artist again, don’t get another record deal, find out what you can do with her.' My manager freaked out when I called her and said, cancel all the showcases!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hitquarters.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-hitquarters.com-2">[2]</a></sup> Perry co-wrote and produced much of Pink's successful album <i><a href="/wiki/Missundaztood" title="Missundaztood">Missundaztood</a></i>, which brought Perry back into the spotlight as a music producer. Perry took full writing credits for the songs "Get the Party Started" and "Lonely Girl." Perry next provided <a href="/wiki/Christina_Aguilera" title="Christina Aguilera">Christina Aguilera</a> the hit "<a href="/wiki/Beautiful_(Christina_Aguilera_song)" title="Beautiful (Christina Aguilera song)">Beautiful</a>."</p>
<p>Since then, she has gone on to work with many artists, including <a href="/wiki/Jewel_(singer)" title="Jewel (singer)">Jewel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Courtney_Love" title="Courtney Love">Courtney Love</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gwen_Stefani" title="Gwen Stefani">Gwen Stefani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blaque" title="Blaque">Blaque</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sugababes" title="Sugababes">Sugababes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lillix" title="Lillix">Lillix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Williams" title="Robbie Williams">Robbie Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Etheridge" title="Melissa Etheridge">Melissa Etheridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Swan" title="Sierra Swan">Sierra Swan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solange_Knowles" title="Solange Knowles">Solange Knowles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gavin_Rossdale" title="Gavin Rossdale">Gavin Rossdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juliette_and_the_Licks" title="Juliette and the Licks">Juliette and the Licks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Marie_Presley" title="Lisa Marie Presley">Lisa Marie Presley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fischerspooner" title="Fischerspooner">Fischerspooner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unwritten_Law" title="Unwritten Law">Unwritten Law</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L.P._(singer)" title="L.P. (singer)">L.P.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Osbourne" title="Kelly Osbourne">Kelly Osbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Blunt" title="James Blunt">James Blunt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheap_Trick" title="Cheap Trick">Cheap Trick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Jelen" title="Ben Jelen">Ben Jelen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Iglesias" title="Enrique Iglesias">Enrique Iglesias</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giusy_Ferreri" title="Giusy Ferreri">Giusy Ferreri</a>.</p>
<p>The 2002 <a href="/wiki/Faith_Hill" title="Faith Hill">Faith Hill</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Cry_(Faith_Hill_album)" title="Cry (Faith Hill album)">Cry</a></i> has a song co-written by Perry and Alecia B. Moore (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P!nk" title="P!nk">Pink</a>).</p>
<p>In 2003, Perry won two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ASCAP" title="ASCAP">ASCAP</a> awards for her songwriting, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a> nomination for her song "Beautiful" as a contender for "Song of the Year"; the song received the award for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Female_Pop_Vocal_Performance" title="Best Female Pop Vocal Performance">Best Female Pop Vocal Performance</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_Grammy_Awards" title="2004 Grammy Awards">2004 Grammy Awards</a>.</p>
<p>With actress <a href="/wiki/Gina_Gershon" title="Gina Gershon">Gina Gershon</a>, she wrote the song "StupidStar" for the movie <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prey_for_Rock_and_Roll" title="Prey for Rock and Roll">Prey for Rock and Roll</a>.</i> With <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> rocker <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L.P._(singer)" title="L.P. (singer)">L.P.</a>, she co-wrote "The Darkside."</p>
<p>For most of 2004, Perry remained behind the scenes, making another appearance with Camp Freddy, this time at Los Angeles' Live 103's Birthday Concert. She joined Lisa Marie Presley on stage for songs they wrote together. The year 2004 also saw the release of Courtney Love's solo <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Sweetheart_(album)" title="America's Sweetheart (album)">America's Sweetheart</a></i>, to which Perry heavily contributed.</p>
<p>A song called "Girls &amp; Boys", which was originally intended for Pink but later recorded by <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>, was released as a bonus track on Spears' European DVD release. The track marked Perry's foray into more <a href="/wiki/Electroclash" title="Electroclash">electroclash</a>-inspired tunes.</p>
<p>She collaborated with art-dance duo Fischerspooner on a few songs for their upcoming release and ended the year with a few co-writing credits on Gwen Stefani's debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Love._Angel._Music._Baby." title="Love. Angel. Music. Baby.">Love. Angel. Music. Baby.</a></i>, including the album's first single, "<a href="/wiki/What_You_Waiting_For%3F" title="What You Waiting For?">What You Waiting For?</a>" Additionally, her Atlantic-distributed label, <a href="/wiki/Custard_Records" title="Custard Records">Custard Records</a>, was launched to promote two new acts, Sunshine and James Blunt. She produced and played guitar on the recording of Blunt's song "No Bravery".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The year ended with Perry co-writing "Save Me" for the southern California punk rock band <a href="/wiki/Unwritten_Law" title="Unwritten Law">Unwritten Law</a>.</p>
<p>Releases for 2005 included Kelly Osbourne's second album, which was written and produced by Perry. The lead track from Osbourne's second album, "<a href="/wiki/One_Word" title="One Word">One Word</a>," reached the top of the UK and US dance charts.</p>
<p>Perry contributed to Christina Aguilera's third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Back_to_Basics_(Christina_Aguilera_album)" title="Back to Basics (Christina Aguilera album)">Back to Basics</a></i> (2006). Perry and Aguilera co-wrote every song.</p>
<p>2007 saw Perry working with Vanessa Carlton on her third studio album, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heroes_and_Thieves" title="Heroes and Thieves">Heroes and Thieves</a></i>. She also co-wrote and did session work on many of the songs on <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Swan" title="Sierra Swan">Sierra Swan</a>'s solo album, <i>Ladyland</i>. She continued work with Courtney Love, <a href="/wiki/Kelis" title="Kelis">Kelis</a>, Cheap Trick, Ziggy Marley, Ben Jelen, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deborah_Dyer" title="Deborah Dyer">Skin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Iglesias" title="Enrique Iglesias">Enrique Iglesias</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Format" title="The Format">The Format</a> on the song "Dead End," and "Darker Side of The Moon" on Bay Area artist <a href="/wiki/Goapele" title="Goapele">Goapele</a>'s <i>Change It All</i> album.</p>
<p>Perry won an award from the San Francisco chapter of the Recording Academy for her contribution to the world of music. The years 2007 and 2008 saw more tunes written or produced by Linda Perry: The Section Quartet, Alicia Keys, Vanessa Carlton, and Gina Gershon.</p>
<p>Perry signed the group <a href="/wiki/Little_Fish_(band)" title="Little Fish (band)">Little Fish</a> to her label in 2008 and began producing its album,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Baffled_and_Beat" title="Baffled and Beat">Baffled and Beat</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> which was released in August 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>She wrote and produced "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=A_Loaded_Smile&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="A Loaded Smile (page does not exist)">A Loaded Smile</a>" for the 2009 debut album <i><a href="/wiki/For_Your_Entertainment_(album)" title="For Your Entertainment (album)">For Your Entertainment</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a>. Aguilera's fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bionic_(Christina_Aguilera_album)" title="Bionic (Christina Aguilera album)">Bionic</a></i>, released in 2010, included another track, written and produced solely by Perry, "Lift Me Up."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.93present:_Recent_career">2010–present: Recent career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linda_Perry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2010–present: Recent career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Perry worked with <a href="/wiki/KT_Tunstall" title="KT Tunstall">KT Tunstall</a> and wrote a few songs with her, some of which would later be featured on Tunstall's third studio album <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Suit" title="Tiger Suit">Tiger Suit</a>, released in September of that year.</p>
<p>Perry announced on her Facebook and Twitter profiles in June that she was "going to make an album" with her new band <a href="/wiki/Deep_Dark_Robot" title="Deep Dark Robot">Deep Dark Robot</a>, which she founded with Tony Tornay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The album, <i><a href="/wiki/8_Songs_About_a_Girl" title="8 Songs About a Girl">8 Songs About a Girl</a></i>, was promoted with the single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Won%27t_You_Be_My_Girl%3F" title="Won't You Be My Girl?">Won't You Be My Girl?</a>" Perry imagined the name Deep Dark Robot as part of an ad-libbed song lyric—"deep dark robot falling in love"—and decided to hang onto the name for her next musical project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The band released the album in March 2011 and began touring.</p>
<p>Perry also worked with <a href="/wiki/Rivers_Cuomo" title="Rivers Cuomo">Rivers Cuomo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Weezer" title="Weezer">Weezer</a> on their song "Brave New World."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2010 and 2011, Perry, along with Fox Entertainment Group, was the Presenting Sponsor of the Los Angeles talent show STARFEST, benefiting The L.A.C.E.R. Afterschool Programs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Perry appeared in the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> reality television show <i>Make or Break: The Linda Perry Project</i>, in which she worked with up-and-coming musicians, like <a href="/wiki/VanJess" title="VanJess">VanJess</a> and winner Hemming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Perry is scheduled to appear as the <a href="/wiki/House_band" title="House band">house band</a> in a special series of late night editions of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Talk_(TV_series)" title="The Talk (TV series)">The Talk</a></i> airing the week of January 12, 2015 in the 12:34 am time slot of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Late_Late_Show_(CBS_TV_series)" title="The Late Late Show (CBS TV series)">The Late Late Show</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2015, Perry was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Songwriters_Hall_of_Fame" title="Songwriters Hall of Fame">Songwriters Hall of Fame</a> at a ceremony in New York.</p>
<p>In 2015, Perry wrote the theme song for the film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freeheld_(2015_film)" title="Freeheld (2015 film)">Freeheld</a></i> titled "Hands of Love," performed by <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a>.</p>
<p>Perry co-wrote a song with <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a> for her 2015 album <i><a href="/wiki/25_(Adele_album)" title="25 (Adele album)">25</a></i> titled "C<i>an't Let Go"</i> which was included as a bonus track on the <i>Adele 25 - Target Deluxe Edition</i>. Perry played piano, produced and engineered the track.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="iPhone_sessions">iPhone sessions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linda_Perry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: iPhone sessions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In July 2011, Perry started to publish a set of acoustic cover songs that she recorded with her iPhone,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> including "<a href="/wiki/Mad_World" title="Mad World">Mad World</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Tears_for_Fears" title="Tears for Fears">Tears for Fears</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Just_What_I_Needed" title="Just What I Needed">Just What I Needed</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Cars" title="The Cars">The Cars</a>, and "<a href="/wiki/Creep_(Radiohead_song)" title="Creep (Radiohead song)">Creep</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Radiohead" title="Radiohead">Radiohead</a>, among many others. On her <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> account, she wrote: "<a href="/wiki/IPhone" title="IPhone">iPhone</a> sessions are me sitting at my piano an recording song into iPhone voice memo. Then I post it. Very simple enjoy :)"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<p>Perry is openly lesbian. She famously displayed the slang word <a href="/wiki/Dyke_(slang)" title="Dyke (slang)">"dyke"</a> on her guitar for a performance with 4 Non Blondes at the 1994 Billboard Music Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Perry was in a relationship with actress <a href="/wiki/Clementine_Ford" title="Clementine Ford">Clementine Ford</a> from 2009 to 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-advocate_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-advocate-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Perry began a relationship with actress <a href="/wiki/Sara_Gilbert" title="Sara Gilbert">Sara Gilbert</a> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> They announced their engagement in April 2013 and married on March 30, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Gilbert gave birth to their son Rhodes Emilio Gilbert Perry, on February 28, 2015. Perry is stepmother to Gilbert's son and daughter from a previous relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
