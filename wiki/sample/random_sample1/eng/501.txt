<p>The <b>2016 Winter Youth Olympics</b> (Norwegian: <i>Vinter-OL for ungdom 2016</i>), officially known as the <b>II Winter Youth Olympic Games</b>, took place in and around <a href="/wiki/Lillehammer" title="Lillehammer">Lillehammer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, between 12 February and 21 February 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> They were the fourth <a href="/wiki/Youth_Olympic_Games" title="Youth Olympic Games">Youth Olympic Games</a> and the second winter edition. Lillehammer was awarded the games on 7 December 2011 as the only candidate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The games <a href="/wiki/Venues_of_the_2016_Winter_Youth_Olympics" title="Venues of the 2016 Winter Youth Olympics">reused venues</a> from the <a href="/wiki/1994_Winter_Olympics" title="1994 Winter Olympics">1994 Winter Olympics</a>. In addition to Lillehammer, sports were contested in <a href="/wiki/Hamar" title="Hamar">Hamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gj%C3%B8vik" title="Gjøvik">Gjøvik</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%98yer" title="Øyer">Øyer</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Host_selection">Host selection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Winter_Youth_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Host selection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lillehammer_bid_for_the_2016_Winter_Youth_Olympics" title="Lillehammer bid for the 2016 Winter Youth Olympics">Lillehammer bid for the 2016 Winter Youth Olympics</a></div>
<div class="floatright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lillehammer_Youth_Olympics_2016.jpg" title="Initial logo of the Games"><img alt="Initial logo of the Games" data-file-height="186" data-file-width="534" height="87" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3c/Lillehammer_Youth_Olympics_2016.jpg/250px-Lillehammer_Youth_Olympics_2016.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3c/Lillehammer_Youth_Olympics_2016.jpg/375px-Lillehammer_Youth_Olympics_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/3c/Lillehammer_Youth_Olympics_2016.jpg/500px-Lillehammer_Youth_Olympics_2016.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a></div>
<p>Lillehammer was the only city to bid for the games. The Norwegian Olympic Committee talked with Norwegian and regional authorities to investigate a bid and ultimately submitted a bid to the IOC. Upon the deadline for bidding, they were the only city to bid. Lillehammer hosted the <a href="/wiki/1994_Winter_Olympics" title="1994 Winter Olympics">1994 Winter Olympics</a>. They bid for the <a href="/wiki/2012_Winter_Youth_Olympics" title="2012 Winter Youth Olympics">2012 Winter Youth Olympics</a>, but failed to become a candidate. <a href="/wiki/Lake_Placid,_New_York" title="Lake Placid, New York">Lake Placid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lucerne" title="Lucerne">Lucerne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia</a> all expressed interest in bidding but ultimately failed to submit any bids.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On December 7, 2011, the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> selected Lillehammer as the host city of the 2016 Winter Youth Olympics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Winter_Youth_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2012, <a href="/wiki/Siri_Hatlen" title="Siri Hatlen">Siri Hatlen</a> was appointed as head of the Lillehammer 2016 Organizing Committee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> At the Closing ceremony of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Winter_Youth_Olympics" title="2012 Winter Youth Olympics">2012 Winter Youth Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Innsbruck" title="Innsbruck">Innsbruck</a>, Lillehammer was handed the Olympic Flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tomas_Holmestad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tomas Holmestad (page does not exist)">Tomas Holmestad</a> (33) is CEO of Lillehammer 2016, which holds office at <a href="/wiki/Oppland" title="Oppland">Oppland Fylkeskommune</a>. In August 2014, Lillehammer Organizing Committee counts 20 employees, and this number is expected to rise to 70-80 employees in January 2016.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Venues">Venues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Winter_Youth_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Venues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Venues_of_the_2016_Winter_Youth_Olympics" title="Venues of the 2016 Winter Youth Olympics">Venues of the 2016 Winter Youth Olympics</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lillehammer_Ski_Jump.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1704" data-file-width="2272" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Lillehammer_Ski_Jump.jpg/220px-Lillehammer_Ski_Jump.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Lillehammer_Ski_Jump.jpg/330px-Lillehammer_Ski_Jump.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Lillehammer_Ski_Jump.jpg/440px-Lillehammer_Ski_Jump.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lillehammer_Ski_Jump.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Like the rest of the competition venues, <a href="/wiki/Lysg%C3%A5rdsbakken" title="Lysgårdsbakken">Lysgårdsbakken</a> was built ahead of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Winter_Olympics" title="1994 Winter Olympics">1994 Winter Olympics</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Nine competition and eleven non-competition venues were used, with all except the Youth Olympic Village in Lillehammer being existing venues. The games were held in four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Norway" title="Municipalities of Norway">municipalities</a>: Lillehammer, <a href="/wiki/Hamar" title="Hamar">Hamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gj%C3%B8vik" title="Gjøvik">Gjøvik</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%98yer" title="Øyer">Øyer</a>. The former three are located nearby the lake of <a href="/wiki/Mj%C3%B8sa" title="Mjøsa">Mjøsa</a> and each have about 27,000 residents, while Øyer has 5,000 residents and is located in the valley of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gudbrandsdalen" title="Gudbrandsdalen">Gudbrandsdalen</a>. There were five competition venues in Lillehammer, two in Hamar and one in Gjøvik and Øyer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Lillehammer, the twin <a href="/wiki/Ski_jumping_hill" title="Ski jumping hill">ski jumping hill</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lysg%C3%A5rdsbakken" title="Lysgårdsbakken">Lysgårdsbakken</a> has a spectator capacity of 35,000. Lysgårdsbakken has a <a href="/wiki/Hill_size" title="Hill size">hill size</a> of 138 and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K-point" title="K-point">K-point</a> of 120, while the normal hill has a hill size of 100 and a K-point of 90.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lysgardsbakken_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-lysgardsbakken-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Birkebeineren_Ski_Stadium" title="Birkebeineren Ski Stadium">Birkebeineren Ski Stadium</a> hosted <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Cross-country skiing at the Winter Olympics">cross-country skiing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biathlon_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Biathlon at the Winter Olympics">biathlon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nordic_combined_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Nordic combined at the Winter Olympics">Nordic combined</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nif10_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-nif10-14">[14]</a></sup> with the stadium itself having a capacity for 31,000 spectators during cross-country skiing and 13,500 during biathlon. In addition, spectators could watch from along the tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kanthaugen_Freestyle_Arena" title="Kanthaugen Freestyle Arena">Kanthaugen Freestyle Arena</a> has a capacity for 15,000 spectators and hosted <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_skiing_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Freestyle skiing at the Winter Olympics">freestyle skiing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snowboarding_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Snowboarding at the Winter Olympics">half-pipe snowboarding</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nif8_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-nif8-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lillehammer_Olympic_Bobsleigh_and_Luge_Track" title="Lillehammer Olympic Bobsleigh and Luge Track">Lillehammer Olympic Bobsleigh and Luge Track</a> is located at <a href="/wiki/Hunderfossen" title="Hunderfossen">Hunderfossen</a> and is the only <a href="/wiki/Bobsleigh_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Bobsleigh at the Winter Olympics">bobsleigh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luge_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Luge at the Winter Olympics">luge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skeleton_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Skeleton at the Winter Olympics">skeleton</a> track in the Nordic Countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kristins_Hall" title="Kristins Hall">Kristins Hall</a> hosted both <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Ice hockey at the Winter Olympics">ice hockey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curling_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Curling at the Winter Olympics">curling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nif12_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-nif12-18">[18]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gj%C3%B8vik_Olympic_Cavern_Hall" title="Gjøvik Olympic Cavern Hall">Gjøvik Olympic Cavern Hall</a> is located in a man-made cave and featured the <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Short track speed skating at the Winter Olympics">short track speed skating</a> events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nif32_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-nif32-19">[19]</a></sup> In Hamar, <a href="/wiki/Vikingskipet" title="Vikingskipet">Vikingskipet</a> hosted <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Speed skating at the Winter Olympics">long track speed skating</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamar_Olympic_Amphitheatre" title="Hamar Olympic Amphitheatre">Hamar Olympic Amphitheatre</a> hosted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Figure_skating_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Figure skating at the Winter Olympics">figure skating</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nif24_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-nif24-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Alpine skiing at the Winter Olympics">Alpine skiing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snowboarding_at_the_Winter_Olympics" title="Snowboarding at the Winter Olympics">slopestyle snowboarding</a> were undertaken at <a href="/wiki/Hafjell" title="Hafjell">Hafjell</a> in Øyer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nif16_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-nif16-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stampesletta" title="Stampesletta">Stampesletta</a>, a multi-sports complex next to Kristins Hall, hosted the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games_ceremony" title="Olympic Games ceremony">opening and closing ceremonies</a>. The medal ceremonies took place in the town plaza. Athletes and leader accommodation were provided at two <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Village" title="Olympic Village">Olympic Villages</a>, one in Lillehammer for the Lillehammer and Øyer-based events, and one in Hamar for the Hamar and Gjøvik-based events. The Lillehammer village consisted of student apartments in combination with a hotel and apartment resort. They used <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A5kons_Hall" title="Håkons Hall">Håkons Hall</a> for dining. The Hamar village was Hotel Scandic Hamar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ceremonies_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-ceremonies-22">[22]</a></sup> In addition, there are five designated cultural venues in Lillehammer: Kulturhuset Banken, <a href="/wiki/Lillehammer_Art_Museum" title="Lillehammer Art Museum">Lillehammer Art Museum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lillehammer_University_College" title="Lillehammer University College">Lillehammer University College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maihaugen" title="Maihaugen">Maihaugen</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nansen_Academy" title="Nansen Academy">Nansen Academy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The Main Media Centre was located at <a href="/wiki/Mesna_Upper_Secondary_School" title="Mesna Upper Secondary School">Mesna Upper Secondary School</a>, which is adjacent to Stampesletta.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nif47_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-nif47-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>All the competition venues were built ahead of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Winter_Olympics" title="1994 Winter Olympics">1994 Winter Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-l14_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-l14-25">[25]</a></sup> Kristins Hall is the only venue not used during those Games,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> while Håkons Hall and <a href="/wiki/Kvitfjell" title="Kvitfjell">Kvitfjell</a> were used. During Lillehammer 2016 Youth Olympic Games, Håkonshall was the venue for the Learn &amp; Share program, whilst Kristins Hall was the official venue for ice hockey and curling. Kvitfjell was not used, and Hafjell was the main venue for downhill skiing, snowboard, and friskiing instead.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mascot">Mascot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Winter_Youth_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mascot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Lillehammer organizing committee launched an international mascot design competition in March and April 2014. The competition required that the design proposals would be on an animal (ordinary animal or a fantasy one), look youthful, be kind and open, sporty, and represent the look of Lillehammer 2016 Winter Youth Olympic Games. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=LYOGOC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="LYOGOC (page does not exist)">LYOGOC</a> received over 50 proposals from all over the world, and a jury consisting of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Birgit_Skarstein&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Birgit Skarstein (page does not exist)">Birgit Skarstein</a>, Julie Strømsvåg, Simen Staalnacke, and Marianne Aagotnes, selected three finalists. The final proposals were presented on the official Facebook page of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lillehammer_2016" title="Lillehammer 2016">Lillehammer 2016</a>, where fans could vote on their favorite. It was the Lynx that won the competition, designed by 19-year-old Line Ansethmoen.</p>
