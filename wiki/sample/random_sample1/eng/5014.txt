<p><b>Lyndsy Marie Fonseca</b> (born January 7, 1987) is an American actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvguide_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-1">[1]</a></sup> She began her career by appearing as <a href="/wiki/Colleen_Carlton" title="Colleen Carlton">Colleen Carlton</a> on the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> daytime soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i>, on which she starred between 2001 and 2005. Thereafter, she had a series of other recurring roles, including <a href="/wiki/List_of_How_I_Met_Your_Mother_characters#Penny_and_Luke_Mosby" title="List of How I Met Your Mother characters">Penny Mosby</a> on the CBS sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Met_Your_Mother" title="How I Met Your Mother">How I Met Your Mother</a></i>, Donna on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_Box_Office" title="Home Box Office">HBO</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Love" title="Big Love">Big Love</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Desperate_Housewives_characters#Dylan_Mayfair" title="List of Desperate Housewives characters">Dylan Mayfair</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives_(season_4)" title="Desperate Housewives (season 4)">fourth season</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i>.</p>
<p>From 2010 to 2013, Fonseca starred as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexandra_(Nikita_character)" title="Alexandra (Nikita character)">Alexandra "Alex" Udinov</a> on <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Nikita_(TV_series)" title="Nikita (TV series)">Nikita</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Agent_Carter_characters#Angie_Martinelli" title="List of Agent Carter characters">Angie Martinelli</a> on ABC's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marvel%27s_Agent_Carter" title="Marvel's Agent Carter">Marvel's Agent Carter</a></i> from 2015 to 2016. Fonseca has also appeared in a variety of film roles, including Jenny Steadmeyer in <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_Tub_Time_Machine" title="Hot Tub Time Machine">Hot Tub Time Machine</a></i> (2010) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Katie_Deauxma" title="Katie Deauxma">Katie Deauxma</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Kick-Ass_(film)" title="Kick-Ass (film)">Kick-Ass</a></i> (2010) and its <a href="/wiki/Kick-Ass_2_(film)" title="Kick-Ass 2 (film)">2013 sequel</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lyndsy_Fonseca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fonseca was born in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>, the daughter of Lima Lynn (née Bergmann)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="WP:BLPPRIMARY (January 2017)">better source needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and James Victor Fonseca.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvguide_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-1">[1]</a></sup> She is partly of <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Americans" title="Portuguese Americans">Portuguese</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GirlsLife_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-GirlsLife-3">[3]</a></sup> Her mother later married attorney Reid Dworkin, Fonseca's stepfather, and she has a half-sister, Hannah Leigh Dworkin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvguide_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-1">[1]</a></sup> Fonseca graduated from <a href="/wiki/Barbizon_Modeling_and_Acting_School" title="Barbizon Modeling and Acting School">Barbizon Modeling and Acting School</a> in San Francisco<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and relocated to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> at age 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GirlsLife_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-GirlsLife-3">[3]</a></sup> She soon made her screen debut as Colleen Connelly-Carlton on the <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvguide_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lyndsy_Fonseca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2005, Fonseca played a recurring role as Ted Mosby's future daughter on <i><a href="/wiki/How_I_Met_Your_Mother" title="How I Met Your Mother">How I Met Your Mother</a></i> and as Donna on <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Love" title="Big Love">Big Love</a></i>. She starred in the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Hallmark_Channel" title="Hallmark Channel">Hallmark</a> TV film <i><a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Miracles" title="Ordinary Miracles">Ordinary Miracles</a></i>, as a 16-year-old juvenile delinquent who goes to live with a judge (<a href="/wiki/Jaclyn_Smith" title="Jaclyn Smith">Jaclyn Smith</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In fall 2007, Fonseca played Dawn in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Remember_the_Daze" title="Remember the Daze">Remember the Daze</a></i>. She joined the cast of <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i> as the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Katherine_Mayfair" title="Katherine Mayfair">Katherine Mayfair</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dana_Delany" title="Dana Delany">Dana Delany</a>), a character moving onto <a href="/wiki/Wisteria_Lane" title="Wisteria Lane">Wisteria Lane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2008 she was nominated for the Actor award at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Awards" title="Screen Actors Guild Awards">Screen Actors Guild Awards</a> for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Comedy Series for <i>Desperate Housewives</i> (2004), shared with the cast.</p>
<p>Fonseca played Katie Deauxma in the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Superhero_film" title="Superhero film">superhero film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Kick-Ass_(film)" title="Kick-Ass (film)">Kick-Ass</a></i>, which also featured her <i>Nikita</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Xander_Berkeley" title="Xander Berkeley">Xander Berkeley</a> in the cast. She appeared in three more US films in 2010, including <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_Tub_Time_Machine" title="Hot Tub Time Machine">Hot Tub Time Machine</a></i>. In 2010, she was cast in the <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">CW</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Nikita_(TV_series)" title="Nikita (TV series)">Nikita</a></i> as Alex, a new recruit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-collider_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-collider-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fonseca was listed at No. 62 on the 2010 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maxim_Hot_100" title="Maxim Hot 100"><i>Maxim</i> Hot 100</a> and No. 89 on the 2011 list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lyndsy_Fonseca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fonseca married artist and writer Matthew Smiley in April 2009. The couple split in July 2012 and Fonseca filed for divorce in 2013, citing irreconcilable differences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In February 2016, she announced her engagement to actor <a href="/wiki/Noah_Bean" title="Noah Bean">Noah Bean</a>, whom she has been dating since 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> She and Bean married on October 2, 2016 in <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
