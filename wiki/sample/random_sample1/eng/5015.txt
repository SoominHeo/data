<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Linton Garner</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><b>Linton Garner</b> (March 25, 1915 in <a href="/wiki/Greensboro,_North_Carolina" title="Greensboro, North Carolina">Greensboro, North Carolina</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Grove_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Grove-3">[3]</a></sup> – March 6, 2003 in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>) was a jazz pianist. He was <a href="/wiki/Erroll_Garner" title="Erroll Garner">Erroll Garner</a>'s older brother. As a youngster he was keener to play cornet than piano, but due to problems with his teeth, was forced to concentrate on the keyboard. From the age of 8 till 10 he had piano lessons, as did his three sisters, unlike his brother Erroll.</p>
<p>He was arranger and pianist with <a href="/wiki/Fletcher_Henderson" title="Fletcher Henderson">Fletcher Henderson</a>'s band before the war, then spent 1943 to 1946 in the army, where he played both piano and trumpet in different bands. Afterwards he was pianist and arranger for a number of distinguished bands including those of <a href="/wiki/Billy_Eckstine" title="Billy Eckstine">Billy Eckstine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dizzy_Gillespie" title="Dizzy Gillespie">Dizzy Gillespie</a>. He also accompanied <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Vaughan" title="Sarah Vaughan">Sarah Vaughan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nat_King_Cole" title="Nat King Cole">Nat King Cole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carmen_McRae" title="Carmen McRae">Carmen McRae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Della_Reese" title="Della Reese">Della Reese</a>. He also composed both melodies and lyrics, including "You're the One For Me." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He moved to Canada in 1963. In 1974, Arni May invited him to Vancouver to accompany him at the opening of the Richmond Inn Hotel. Linton stayed in Vancouver and worked in many venues. He particularly enjoyed 7 years resident pianist at the Four Seasons Hotels and Resorts. Later (in the 1990s) he was playing in The Three Greenhorns in Vancouver. He also sang and played the piano in Rossini's restaurant in Kitsilano.</p>
