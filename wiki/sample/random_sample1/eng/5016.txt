<div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">family name</a> is <a href="/wiki/Lin_(surname)" title="Lin (surname)">Lam</a>.</div>
<p><b>Raymond Lam</b> (born 8 December 1979) is a Hong Kong actor and singer. He is best known for his roles in the television dramas <i><a href="/wiki/A_Step_into_the_Past" title="A Step into the Past">A Step into the Past</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Moonlight_Resonance" title="Moonlight Resonance">Moonlight Resonance</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Highs_and_Lows" title="Highs and Lows">Highs and Lows</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Line_Walker" title="Line Walker">Line Walker</a></i>, as well as being the "King of <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Chok" title="Chok">Chok</a>" for his role in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mysteries_of_Love" title="The Mysteries of Love">The Mysteries of Love</a></i>.</p>

<p>Raymond Lam was born into a wealthy family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-singca_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-singca-1">[1]</a></sup> His father, Lam Kwok-Wah (林國華), is a real-estate property developer in the mainland referred to as "<a href="/wiki/Xiamen" title="Xiamen">Xiamen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Li_Ka-shing" title="Li Ka-shing">Li Ka-shing</a>" (廈門李嘉誠) who sits on the board of seven real estate and construction companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-singca_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-singca-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jay1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-jay1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>His birthname was Lam Wui-man (林匯文), which later changed to Lam Yat-fung (林逸峯), and stage name is Raymond Lam Fung. He calls himself Raymond as the pronunciation of it is similar to his birthname 'Wui-man'. The name was changed to give him a "<i><a href="/wiki/Feng_shui" title="Feng shui">feng shui</a></i> advantage".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>While his first languages are Cantonese and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hokkien_dialect" title="Hokkien dialect">Hokkien</a>, Lam studied in <a href="/wiki/Kiangsu_and_Chekiang_Primary_School" title="Kiangsu and Chekiang Primary School">Kiangsu and Chekiang Primary School</a>, a primarily Mandarin-speaking primary school in pre-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Handover_of_Hong_Kong" title="Handover of Hong Kong">Handover</a> Hong Kong and is thus also fluent in Mandarin and English. He studied in <a href="/wiki/St._Paul%27s_College,_Hong_Kong" title="St. Paul's College, Hong Kong">St. Paul's College, Hong Kong</a> for Secondary School. Following that, he studied <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a> in <a href="/wiki/Xiamen_University" title="Xiamen University">Xiamen University</a> for 1st Year, and then completed the rest at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a>, majoring in architecture and minoring in music.</p>
<p>Raymond Lam's childhood dream was to become a professional dancer. He has a younger sister and a younger brother. His paternal great-grandmother is Spanish, making Raymond 1/16th Spanish.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raymond_Lam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Success_in_Hong_Kong">Success in Hong Kong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raymond_Lam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Success in Hong Kong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lam attended the 13th TVB artists training class in 1998 as an auditing student and was offered a contract with TVB upon graduation in September 1998. In the earlier part of his career, Lam appeared as a walk-on in numerous television series and as a host for TVB8. Lam rose to fame for his portrayal of the ruthless <a href="/wiki/Qin_Shi_Huang" title="Qin Shi Huang">Ying Ching</a> in the historical/science fiction drama <i><a href="/wiki/A_Step_into_the_Past" title="A Step into the Past">A Step into the Past</a></i> (2001). He was then offered his first lead role in <i><a href="/wiki/Eternal_Happiness" title="Eternal Happiness">Eternal Happiness</a></i> (2002). In 2003, Lam won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TVB_Anniversary_Award" title="TVB Anniversary Award">TVB Anniversary Award</a> for "Most Improved Male Artiste" for his performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor%27s_Law" title="Survivor's Law">Survivor's Law</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2004, Lam gained fame in China for his role as Kou Chong in <i><a href="/wiki/Twin_of_Brothers" title="Twin of Brothers">Twin of Brothers</a></i>. He won the "Audience's Favourite Character" award at the TVB Anniversary Award, "Newcomer with the Most Potential" award at the 2nd Enlight Media Awards as well as "Favourite Actor Award" in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> at the Astro Wah Lai Toi Drama Awards.</p>
<p>After a one year hiatus in 2005 due to inner conflicts in TVB, Lam made his comeback in <i><a href="/wiki/La_Femme_Desperado" title="La Femme Desperado">La Femme Desperado</a></i> (2006), which became the highest rated drama of the year.</p>
<p>In 2007, Lam was cast in the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Drive_of_Life" title="The Drive of Life">The Drive of Life</a></i>, a grand production by TVB to commemorate the 10th anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Transfer_of_sovereignty_over_Hong_Kong" title="Transfer of sovereignty over Hong Kong">Hong Kong reunification</a>. He was also invited to guest star in <i><a href="/wiki/Heart_of_Greed" title="Heart of Greed">Heart of Greed</a></i>. Though initially slated to appear for about 10 episodes in <i>Heard of Greed</i>, his appearance was extended to 30 episodes due to popularity. That year, he received the "Favourite Hong Kong TV Actor" award from QQ Entertainment Awards in mainland China.</p>
<p>The following year, Lam returned to star in the <i><a href="/wiki/Moonlight_Resonance" title="Moonlight Resonance">Moonlight Resonance</a></i>, the sequel to <i>Heart of Greed</i>. The series was met with critical acclaim, and subsequently became the highest rated Hong Kong drama since 1991. Lam won the "Grand Popularity" award at the 41st TVB Anniversary Awards, as well as the "Favourite Male Character" award for his role as Kam Wing-ho. He was also crowned "Best Actor" at the <a href="/wiki/StarHub_TVB_Awards" title="StarHub TVB Awards">StarHub TVB Awards</a> held in Singapore and Astro Wah Lai Toi Drama Awards.</p>
<p>Also in 2008, Lam starred in the wuxia drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Four_(2008_TV_series)" title="The Four (2008 TV series)">The Four</a></i>. His portrayal of the character "Wu Qing" was met with critical acclaim from fans of the original novel by <a href="/wiki/Woon_Swee_Oan" title="Woon Swee Oan">Woon Swee Oan</a>. This was followed by a starring role in the martial arts series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Master_of_Tai_Chi_(TV_series)" title="The Master of Tai Chi (TV series)">The Master of Tai Chi</a></i>, for which he won "Best Actor" at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_Television_Awards" title="Asian Television Awards">Asian Television Awards</a>. Lam was also given the "Best Performance" award at the New York Festivals International Television &amp; Film Awards, becoming the first Hong Kong actor to win the award.</p>
<p>In 2010, Lam starred in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mysteries_of_Love" title="The Mysteries of Love">The Mysteries of Love</a></i>, where he portrayed a genius <a href="/wiki/Scientist" title="Scientist">physics professor</a>. Lam's portrayal of a professor was described by audience using the slang word "<a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Chok" title="Chok">Chok</a>", which means "cool or handsome, to the point of electrifying". Lam stated that he thinks the word means "professional/serious" instead. Subsequently, the term went viral and Lam was named as the "King of Chok". The same year, Lam also made his foray into films, starring in <i><a href="/wiki/Perfect_Wedding" title="Perfect Wedding">Perfect Wedding</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Miriam_Yeung" title="Miriam Yeung">Miriam Yeung</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jade_and_the_Pearl" title="The Jade and the Pearl">The Jade and the Pearl</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Charlene_Choi" title="Charlene Choi">Charlene Choi</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breaking_into_China">Breaking into China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raymond_Lam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Breaking into China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, Lam starred in the Chinese film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sorcerer_and_the_White_Snake" title="The Sorcerer and the White Snake">The Sorcerer and the White Snake</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Jet_Li" title="Jet Li">Jet Li</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huang_Shengyi" title="Huang Shengyi">Huang Shengyi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charlene_Choi" title="Charlene Choi">Charlene Choi</a>. The film was one of the top 5 grossing Chinese films of the year, and Lam was ranked on <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> China Celebrity List for the first time.</p>
<p>In 2012, Lam played a policeman for the first time in <i><a href="/wiki/Highs_and_Lows" title="Highs and Lows">Highs and Lows</a></i>. <i>High and Lows</i> was the most watched Hong Kong drama in China that year, and Lam won "Best Actor" at the <a href="/wiki/Huading_Awards" title="Huading Awards">Huading Awards</a>.</p>
<p>In 2013, Lam starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Saving_General_Yang" title="Saving General Yang">Saving General Yang</a></i>, portraying the fifth son Yang Yande. The same year, he was chosen along with <a href="/wiki/Andy_Lau" title="Andy Lau">Andy Lau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chow_Yun-fat" title="Chow Yun-fat">Chow Yun-fat</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tony_Leung_Chiu_Wai" title="Tony Leung Chiu Wai">Tony Leung Chiu Wai</a> as Hong Kong's "ideal man".</p>
<p>In 2014, Lam's contract with <a href="/wiki/TVB" title="TVB">TVB</a> ended and he did not renew. The same year, he portrayed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Han_Wudi" title="Han Wudi">Han Wudi</a> in the drama <i>The Virtuous Queen of Han</i>, which won him the "Top 10 Most Popular Actor" award at the 9th Chinese TV Audience Festival. He also starred the Hong Kong series <i><a href="/wiki/Line_Walker" title="Line Walker">Line Walker</a></i>. His character "Bao Seed" was praised by critics for being the "soul/essence" of the drama. <i>Line Walker</i> achieved an average rating of 30.5 becoming the highest-rated Hong Kong drama of the year. It also surpassed 24 billion views, becoming the only Hong Kong drama to join the "20 billion view" category for television dramas. Lam won the "Best Actor" award at the 15th <a href="/wiki/Huading_Awards" title="Huading Awards">Huading Awards</a> for his performance both dramas.</p>
<p>At the end of 2014, Lam signed with <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Entertainment_Group" title="Emperor Entertainment Group">Emperor Entertainment Group</a> (EEG) for a reported sum of 100 million.</p>
<p>In 2015, Lam portrayed <a href="/wiki/Lu_Xiaofeng" title="Lu Xiaofeng">Lu Xiaofeng</a> in the wuxia drama <i>Detectives and Doctors</i>. He is the fourth Chinese actor to portray the character, and was praised by critics for being the most oustanding Lu Xiaofeng.</p>
<p>Lam has been cast in the upcoming dramas <i>Rule The World</i>, playing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huang_Taiji" title="Huang Taiji">Huang Taiji</a> as well as <i>The Legends of Monkey King</i>, where he will play the titular <a href="/wiki/Sun_Wukong" title="Sun Wukong">Sun Wukong</a>.</p>

<p>Apart from his acting career, Lam also performed some of the theme songs for the television series he acted in. In 2007, Lam signed on with <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Entertainment_Group" title="Emperor Entertainment Group">EEG</a> under its Music Plus Label, and released his debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Love_in_Memories" title="Finding Love in Memories">Love is Finding You in Memories</a></i>, on 23 November. The title track of the album served as one of the theme songs for <a href="/wiki/TVB" title="TVB">TVB</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Drive_of_Life" title="The Drive of Life">The Drive of Life</a></i>. More than 20,000 copies were sold a week after the album was released. In addition, the album received the IFPI Gold Disk Award and Lam won the "Most Popular Newcomer" award at the Jade Solid Gold Awards Presentation.</p>
<p>On September 10, 2008, Lam released his second album <i><a href="/wiki/Your_Love_(Raymond_Lam_album)" title="Your Love (Raymond Lam album)">Your Love</a></i>, and a month after the release, the album secured a double IFPI Platinum Award for reaching sales of more than 60,000 copies after 3 weeks. A song from the album, <i>Love With No Regrets</i>, became the theme song of <i><a href="/wiki/Moonlight_Resonance" title="Moonlight Resonance">Moonlight Resonance</a></i>, and won many awards including the "Golden Tunes" award from the Top Ten Chinese Gold Songs Award and Jade Solid Gold Awards Presentation.</p>
<p>In 2009, Lam released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Get_Wet" title="Let's Get Wet">Let's Get Wet</a></i> and stepped on the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Coliseum" title="Hong Kong Coliseum">Hong Kong Coliseum</a> for his first concert, held on 17 and 18 June, where both nights were sold out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> EEG and TVB showed strong support for his concert and a billboard advertisement was featured at the entrance of Cross Harbour Tunnel to publicize the event. The tour continued to <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> in July, and then to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/America" title="America">America</a> in December. Lam was awarded "Best Selling Male Singer" at the IPFI Hong Kong Top Sales Music Award, "Outstanding Trendy Singer" at the Top Ten Chinese Gold Songs Award as well as "Asia-Pacific's Most Popular Male Singer" at the Jade Solid Gold Awards Presentation. Lam became the first singer to receive the award in three years, the youngest winner and the first to sing a Cantonese song when winning this award. However, the Hong Kong media criticized TVB for giving him that award out of commercial point.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 17, 2010, Lam released his fourth album <i><a href="/wiki/Come_2_Me" title="Come 2 Me">Come 2 Me</a></i>. From 27 to 30 July, Lam held his second concert at the Hong Kong Coliseum. He also held mini concerts at <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. Both the concert DVD as well as the album secured a IFPI Thriple Platinum Award. The concerts continued over to Singapore, Malaysia, Macau and Guangzhou in early 2011.</p>
<p>On March 4, 2011, Lam released his first Mandarin and fifth album <i><a href="/wiki/First_(Raymond_Lam_album)" title="First (Raymond Lam album)">First</a></i>. The album achieved IFPI Quadruple Platinum Award. On July 22, 2011, Lam released his sixth album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LF_(Raymond_Lam_album)" title="LF (Raymond Lam album)">LF</a></i>, which includes the music videos of several songs from <i>First</i>. From 29–31 July 2011, Lam held his third concert, <i>Light Up My Live</i>, at HKC on four-sided stage. <i>Light Up My Live</i> was also his first world tour. Lam held an autograph session on November 26, 2011 where it was announced that both <i>LF</i> and the concert DVD sales reached Quadruple Platinum. The single "CHOK", from the <i>LF</i> album, was also named as the "Best Song" at the Jade Solid Gold Awards Presentation.</p>
<p>From March to June 2012, Lam was busy with his tour which had stops at <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. On August 17, 2012, Lam released his seventh album <i><a href="/wiki/Self_Portrait_(Raymond_Lam_album)" title="Self Portrait (Raymond Lam album)">Self-Portrait</a></i>. The title song, also named "Self Portrait" was written by the team behind <a href="/wiki/Eason_Chan" title="Eason Chan">Eason Chan</a>'s single "What's Going On…?"; and the music video was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>. The album topped the YesAsia charts, and reached Double Platinum. Lam also won "Most Popular Male Singer" at the Jade Solid Gold Awards Presentation.</p>
<p>On 26 January 2013, Lam held his fourth concert and world tour, <i>A Time 4 You</i>, with his eighth album of the same name released one day before the concert. The DVD album of the concert ranked first on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>' Top World Music Albums Charts. For the 3rd time, Lam won "Best Selling Male Singer" at the IPFI Hong Kong Top Sales Music Award. He also won several other awards.</p>
<p>In 2016, Lam released his seventh album, <i>Trap</i>. From 29 April until 1 May 2016, Lam held his fifth concert, <i>Heart Attack LF Live In HK</i>.</p>
