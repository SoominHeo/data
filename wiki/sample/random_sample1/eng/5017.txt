<p><b>Leland Jones Orser</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>. Orser is primarily a <a href="/wiki/Character_actor" title="Character actor">character actor</a>, and has appeared in small roles in a wide variety of films and television series, including Chief of Surgery <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucien_Dubenko#Lucien_Dubenko" title="Lucien Dubenko">Dr. Lucien Dubenko</a> on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leland_Orser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Orser made his television debut in 1991 on the series <i><a href="/wiki/Gabriel%27s_Fire" title="Gabriel's Fire">Gabriel's Fire</a></i>. His next roles were small roles on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Golden_Girls" title="The Golden Girls">The Golden Girls</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cheers" title="Cheers">Cheers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/L.A._Law" title="L.A. Law">L.A. Law</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">The X-Files</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/NYPD_Blue" title="NYPD Blue">NYPD Blue</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1999, he played Dr. Arthur Zeller in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Outer_Limits_(1995_TV_series)" title="The Outer Limits (1995 TV series)">The Outer Limits</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Descent_(The_Outer_Limits)" title="Descent (The Outer Limits)">Descent</a>". He appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Seven_(1995_film)" title="Seven (1995 film)">Seven</a></i> (1995) as the man who was involuntarily recruited as the punisher for the <a href="/wiki/Lust" title="Lust">lust</a> sin. He was credited as "Crazed Man in Massage Parlour". He played Larry Purvis in <i><a href="/wiki/Alien:_Resurrection" title="Alien: Resurrection">Alien: Resurrection</a></i> (1997).</p>
<p>He played the antagonist Richard Thompson in the <a href="/wiki/Denzel_Washington" title="Denzel Washington">Denzel Washington</a> thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bone_Collector" title="The Bone Collector">The Bone Collector</a></i> (1999). In 2001, he had a small role in <i><a href="/wiki/Pearl_Harbor_(film)" title="Pearl Harbor (film)">Pearl Harbor</a></i> as an injured man saved by <a href="/wiki/Kate_Beckinsale" title="Kate Beckinsale">Kate Beckinsale</a>'s character. In 2003, he appeared as Wesley in the comic-turned-film <i><a href="/wiki/Daredevil_(film)" title="Daredevil (film)">Daredevil</a></i>. He appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Saving_Private_Ryan" title="Saving Private Ryan">Saving Private Ryan</a></i> as the traumatized pilot of a crashed glider.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Orser also appeared in various parts in the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a></i> franchise, among them playing a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Changelings_(Star_Trek)" title="Changelings (Star Trek)">Changeling</a> posing as the <a href="/wiki/Romulan" title="Romulan">Romulan</a> <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">Colonel</a> Lovok in the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Deep_Space_Nine" title="Star Trek: Deep Space Nine">Star Trek: Deep Space Nine</a></i> episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Die_is_Cast_(Star_Trek:_Deep_Space_Nine)" title="The Die is Cast (Star Trek: Deep Space Nine)">The Die is Cast</a>" and in the episode "<a href="/wiki/Sanctuary_(Star_Trek:_Deep_Space_Nine)" title="Sanctuary (Star Trek: Deep Space Nine)">Sanctuary</a>" playing a bit part as a member of the Skrreean race. He also played a homicidal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hologram" title="Hologram">hologram</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Voyager" title="Star Trek: Voyager">Star Trek: Voyager</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Revulsion_(Star_Trek:_Voyager)" title="Revulsion (Star Trek: Voyager)">Revulsion</a>". In the franchise's most recent series <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Enterprise" title="Star Trek: Enterprise">Star Trek: Enterprise</a></i>, he played a low-life in the time-travel episode "<a href="/wiki/Carpenter_Street_(Star_Trek:_Enterprise)" title="Carpenter Street (Star Trek: Enterprise)">Carpenter Street</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He played Ansel in the drama thriller film <a href="/wiki/Faults_(film)" title="Faults (film)">Faults</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> From 2004-09, he played Chief of Surgery Dr. Lucien Dubenko, a recurring character, on <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>. Most recently he has appeared in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taken_(film_series)" title="Taken (film series)">all three of the <i>Taken</i> film series</a> as "Sam".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leland_Orser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Orser was born in San Francisco, California. He married Irish actress <a href="/wiki/Roma_Downey" title="Roma Downey">Roma Downey</a> in 1987; they divorced in 1989. In 2000, he married <a href="/wiki/Jeanne_Tripplehorn" title="Jeanne Tripplehorn">Jeanne Tripplehorn</a>; they have one son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfgate_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sfgate-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
