<p>On July 30, 2016, sixteen people were killed when the <a href="/wiki/Hot_air_balloon" title="Hot air balloon">hot air balloon</a> they were riding in struck power lines, crashed and caught fire in the <a href="/wiki/Unincorporated_area" title="Unincorporated area">unincorporated community</a> of <a href="/wiki/Maxwell,_Texas" title="Maxwell, Texas">Maxwell</a> near <a href="/wiki/Lockhart,_Texas" title="Lockhart, Texas">Lockhart, Texas</a>, a city 30 miles (48 km) south of the state capital <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austin" title="Austin">Austin</a>. It is the deadliest ballooning disaster in the United States, and the second-deadliest worldwide, surpassed only by <a href="/wiki/2013_Luxor_hot_air_balloon_crash" title="2013 Luxor hot air balloon crash">a hot air balloon crash in Egypt in 2013</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BNO_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BNO-1">[1]</a></sup> It is also the deadliest aviation incident on U.S. soil since <a href="/wiki/Colgan_Air_Flight_3407" title="Colgan Air Flight 3407">Colgan Air Flight 3407</a> crashed in 2009.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aircraft">Aircraft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Lockhart_hot_air_balloon_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Aircraft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The aircraft involved was a <a href="/wiki/Kubicek_Balloons" title="Kubicek Balloons">Kubicek BB85Z</a> <a href="/wiki/Hot_air_balloon" title="Hot air balloon">hot air balloon</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KXAN310716_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-KXAN310716-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_registration" title="Aircraft registration">registration</a> N2469L.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASN188936_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ASN188936-3">[3]</a></sup> The balloon was operated by the Heart of Texas Balloon Ride company, which serves people in the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Austin" title="Greater Austin">Greater Austin</a> area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC36933150_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC36933150-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accident">Accident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Lockhart_hot_air_balloon_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Accident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The balloon departed from <a href="/wiki/Fentress,_Texas" title="Fentress, Texas">Fentress Airpark</a> at 07:09 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC-05:00" title="UTC-05:00">local time</a> (12:09 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>) on Saturday, July 30, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KXAN310716_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-KXAN310716-2">[2]</a></sup> It was carrying the pilot and fifteen passengers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN310716_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-CNN310716-5">[5]</a></sup> At 07:42,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KXAN310716_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-KXAN310716-2">[2]</a></sup> the aircraft struck power lines and crashed into a field near <a href="/wiki/Lockhart,_Texas" title="Lockhart, Texas">Lockhart, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> All sixteen people on board were killed. The emergency services were alerted at 07:44 about a "possible vehicle accident", and arrived at the scene to find the basket of the balloon on fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky10517549_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sky10517549-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>A witness described hearing two "pops" which was thought to be a gun going off. Reports said that the balloon lost contact about half an hour into the scheduled one-hour flight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC36933150_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC36933150-4">[4]</a></sup> It is thought that the balloon collided with the power lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LPR_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-LPR-9">[9]</a></sup> The envelope of the balloon landed about <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>⁄<sub>4</sub></span> mile (1,320 yd; 1,210 m) northeast of the burned-out gondola. The flight had covered a distance of about 8 nautical miles (15 km).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KXAN310716_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-KXAN310716-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Investigation">Investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Lockhart_hot_air_balloon_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Investigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">Federal Aviation Administration</a> sent investigators to the scene. The <a href="/wiki/National_Transportation_Safety_Board" title="National Transportation Safety Board">National Transportation Safety Board</a> (NTSB) will take over the investigation from them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC36933150_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC36933150-4">[4]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Federal_Bureau_of_Investigation" title="Federal Bureau of Investigation">Federal Bureau of Investigation</a> (FBI) were at the scene securing evidence for the NTSB's investigation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN300716_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-CNN300716-10">[10]</a></sup> The event was designated a "major accident" by the NTSB.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC41015210_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABC41015210-11">[11]</a></sup> Fourteen personal electronic devices (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cellphone" title="Cellphone">cellphones</a>, an <a href="/wiki/IPad" title="IPad">iPad</a>, and cameras) were recovered from the wreckage. These were turned over to the FBI for the recovery of evidence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KXAN310716_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-KXAN310716-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Texas_Department_of_Public_Safety" title="Texas Department of Public Safety">Texas Department of Public Safety</a> said in a "preliminary working theory" that investigators believe the hot air balloon struck power lines and caught fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN300716_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-CNN300716-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Lockhart_hot_air_balloon_crash&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On August 1, Heart of Texas Hot Air Balloon Rides, whose owner died in the incident, announced that it would be suspending operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
