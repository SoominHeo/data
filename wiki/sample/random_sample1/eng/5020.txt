<p><b>Lily Chloe Ninette Thomson</b> (born 5 April 1989), better known as <b>Lily James</b>, is an English actress. She is best known for her role as Lady Rose Aldridge in the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> period drama <i><a href="/wiki/Downton_Abbey" title="Downton Abbey">Downton Abbey</a></i>, and the title role in the 2015 Disney film <i><a href="/wiki/Cinderella_(2015_Disney_film)" title="Cinderella (2015 Disney film)">Cinderella</a></i>.</p>
<p>James began her professional acting career as Ethel Brown in the 2010 BBC production <i><a href="/wiki/Just_William_(2010_TV_series)" title="Just William (2010 TV series)">Just William</a></i>. In 2011 and 2012, she earned warm reviews in several London theatre productions. She began to appear in Hollywood films in 2012, with roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Wrath_of_the_Titans" title="Wrath of the Titans">Wrath of the Titans</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fast_Girls_(film)" title="Fast Girls (film)">Fast Girls</a></i>, and was seen in further television roles, including the character of <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Rostova" title="Natasha Rostova">Natasha Rostova</a> in the 2016 <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> historical drama series <i><a href="/wiki/War_%26_Peace_(2016_TV_series)" title="War &amp; Peace (2016 TV series)">War &amp; Peace</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lily_James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lily James was born in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>,the daughter of Ninette (<a href="/wiki/Given_name#Name_at_birth" title="Given name">née</a> Mantle), an actress, and musician James "Jamie" Thomson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Her grandmother, Helen Horton, was an American actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She began her education at the <a href="/wiki/Tring_Park_School_for_the_Performing_Arts" title="Tring Park School for the Performing Arts">Tring Park School for the Performing Arts</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and subsequently went on to study acting at the <a href="/wiki/Guildhall_School_of_Music_and_Drama" title="Guildhall School of Music and Drama">Guildhall School of Music and Drama</a> in London, graduating in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lily_James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, James played Taylor at the <a href="/wiki/Young_Vic" title="Young Vic">Young Vic Theatre</a> in Tanya Ronder's stage adaptation of the novel <i><a href="/wiki/Vernon_God_Little" title="Vernon God Little">Vernon God Little</a></i> directed by <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Norris" title="Rufus Norris">Rufus Norris</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Nina in Russell Bolam's modern adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Seagull" title="The Seagull">The Seagull</a></i> at <a href="/wiki/Southwark_Playhouse" title="Southwark Playhouse">Southwark Playhouse</a>, and Desdemona in <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Evans_(actor)" title="Daniel Evans (actor)">Daniel Evans</a>'s production of <i>Othello</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Crucible_Theatre" title="Crucible Theatre">Crucible Theatre, Sheffield</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Dominic_West" title="Dominic West">Dominic West</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clarke_Peters" title="Clarke Peters">Clarke Peters</a>. <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Letts" title="Quentin Letts">Quentin Letts</a> wrote in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Daily_Mail" title="The Daily Mail">The Daily Mail</a></i>: "We may have a new star actress on our hands. ... She left drama school only last year, yet she practically sweeps all before her as Desdemona in this <i>Othello</i>. Poise, diction, allure – she has them all. ... Her character’s love affair with a much older husband is completely believable".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2012, James played the role of Katrina in <i>Play House</i> and Marijka in <i>Definitely the Bahamas</i> in a double-bill written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Martin_Crimp" title="Martin Crimp">Martin Crimp</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Orange_Tree_Theatre" title="Orange Tree Theatre">Orange Tree Theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_London" title="Richmond, London">Richmond, London</a> as part of the theatre's fortieth anniversary. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Spencer_(journalist)" title="Charles Spencer (journalist)">Charles Spencer</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Telegraph</a></i> wrote that "it is performed with a persuasive mixture of mischief and deeper feeling by Obi Abili and Lily James, the latter combining a mixture of neuroticism and glowing sexual allure that proves extraordinarily potent."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also in 2012, James played the role of Korrina in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Brothers" title="Warner Brothers">Warner Brothers</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Wrath_of_the_Titans" title="Wrath of the Titans">Wrath of the Titans</a></i> and starred in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fast_Girls_(film)" title="Fast Girls (film)">Fast Girls</a></i>, written by <a href="/wiki/Noel_Clarke" title="Noel Clarke">Noel Clarke</a>, centered around a group of young female athletes competing in the World Championships. Her television credits include Ethel Brown in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Just_William_(2010_TV_serial)" title="Just William (2010 TV serial)">2010 BBC production</a> of <a href="/wiki/Richmal_Crompton" title="Richmal Crompton">Richmal Crompton</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Just_William" title="Just William">Just William</a></i>, Poppy in the fourth series of ITV's <i><a href="/wiki/Secret_Diary_of_a_Call_Girl" title="Secret Diary of a Call Girl">Secret Diary of a Call Girl</a></i> (2011), and the rebellious Lady Rose in the last episode of series three of <i><a href="/wiki/Downton_Abbey" title="Downton Abbey">Downton Abbey</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Lady Rose later became a main character in the fourth and fifth series of the programme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> James also appeared as Lady Rose in the series finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>James played the <a href="/wiki/Cinderella_(Disney_character)" title="Cinderella (Disney character)">title character</a> in the 2015 live-action <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Cinderella_(2015_Disney_film)" title="Cinderella (2015 Disney film)">Cinderella</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The film was released on 13 March 2015. James, as Cinderella, was photographed by <a href="/wiki/Annie_Leibovitz" title="Annie Leibovitz">Annie Leibovitz</a>, in the blue gown that she wears to the ball, for the December 2014 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Vogue_(magazine)" title="Vogue (magazine)">Vogue</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> James also made her singing debut in the film's end credits, covering "<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Dream_is_a_Wish_Your_Heart_Makes" title="A Dream is a Wish Your Heart Makes">A Dream is a Wish Your Heart Makes</a></i>" from the <a href="/wiki/Cinderella_(1950_film)" title="Cinderella (1950 film)">1950 animated film</a>.</p>
<p>In 2016, she returned to television in the BBC's War &amp; Peace drama adoption, which received rave reviews. She played the character of <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Rostova" title="Natasha Rostova">Natasha Rostova</a> in the 2016 <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> historical drama series <i><a href="/wiki/War_%26_Peace_(2016_TV_series)" title="War &amp; Peace (2016 TV series)">War &amp; Peace</a></i>. She also starred in her second major film, playing the role of Elizabeth Bennett in the action-<a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Pride_and_Prejudice_and_Zombies_(film)" title="Pride and Prejudice and Zombies (film)">Pride and Prejudice and Zombies</a></i>, a parody of Jane Austen's <a href="/wiki/Pride_and_Prejudice" title="Pride and Prejudice">Pride and Prejudice</a>. It received mixed reviews and failed to break even at the box office.</p>
<p>James is scheduled to play the role of Juliet in the <a href="/wiki/Garrick_Theatre" title="Garrick Theatre">Garrick Theatre</a>'s production of <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a></i>. The directors are <a href="/wiki/Rob_Ashford" title="Rob Ashford">Rob Ashford</a> and actor-director <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Branagh" title="Kenneth Branagh">Kenneth Branagh</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lily_James&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008, James' father died from cancer, which made filming father/daughter scenes in <i>Cinderella</i> very emotional for her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> She began dating <a href="/wiki/Matt_Smith_(actor)" title="Matt Smith (actor)">Matt Smith</a> in 2015, announcing their relationship when they appeared together at the <i>Cinderella</i> premiere in February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
