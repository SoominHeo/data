<p><b>Lilián Annette Garcia</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> (born August 19, 1966)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> is a Spanish retired ring announcer and singer best known for her time with the <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a>.</p>
<p>During her first tenure in WWE, she became the first <a href="/wiki/WWE_Diva" title="WWE Diva">WWE Diva</a> to remain with the <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_promotion" title="Professional wrestling promotion">promotion</a> for ten years.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lilian_Garcia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>García was born on August 19, 1966,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She spent the first eight years of her life in Spain due to her father's employment with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diplomatic_missions_of_the_United_States" title="Diplomatic missions of the United States">American Embassy</a>, and was educated on an American <a href="/wiki/Military_base" title="Military base">military base</a>, leading her to describe herself as a "<a href="/wiki/Military_brat_(U.S._subculture)" title="Military brat (U.S. subculture)">military brat</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spain_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spain-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Waldman_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Waldman-11">[11]</a></sup> She is fluent in English and Spanish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garcia_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Garcia-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Upon returning to the United States, Garcia graduated from <a href="/wiki/Irmo_High_School" title="Irmo High School">Irmo High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Columbia,_South_Carolina" title="Columbia, South Carolina">Columbia, South Carolina</a>. She went on to attend the <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Carolina" title="University of South Carolina">University of South Carolina</a>, graduating <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cum_laude" title="Cum laude">cum laude</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garcia_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Garcia-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>García was a top ten finalist in the <a href="/wiki/Miss_South_Carolina" title="Miss South Carolina">Miss South Carolina</a> beauty pageant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garcia_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Garcia-3">[3]</a></sup> Garcia was also one half of a popular morning show on radio station WYYS,"YES 97" in Columbia with Chuck Finley in the early '90s. She was a <a href="/wiki/VJ_(media_personality)" title="VJ (media personality)">VJ</a> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta, Georgia</a>, where she also had a drive-time afternoon radio show. She also served as karaoke host at the Nitelites nightclub in the Embassy Suites Hotel in Columbia, South Carolina, and later at the West Columbia Ramada.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singing_career">Singing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lilian_Garcia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Singing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>García began singing when she was a child in Madrid, Spain as she participated in singing contests with her sister starting when she was five.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Medalis_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Medalis-12">[12]</a></sup> In her early teens – by then Garcia's family had relocated to Columbia, South Carolina – she was singing with a band at clubs around the area; her mom chaperoned so she could get in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WWE_bio_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-WWE_bio-13">[13]</a></sup> She appeared in the 1990 film <i>Modern Love</i> as a singer.</p>
<p>In addition to her broadcasting duties with World Wrestling Entertainment, García sang the <a href="/wiki/The_Star-Spangled_Banner" title="The Star-Spangled Banner">National Anthem</a> of the United States of America before most <i>Monday Night Raw</i> tapings from February 14, 2000 until her departure on September 21, 2009. WWE Chairman <a href="/wiki/Vince_McMahon" title="Vince McMahon">Vince McMahon</a> asked her to sing <a href="/wiki/America_the_Beautiful" title="America the Beautiful">America the Beautiful</a> at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_2000" title="WrestleMania 2000">WrestleMania 2000</a> and <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_21" title="WrestleMania 21">WrestleMania 21</a>, joining a renowned list of legendary recording artists that includes <a href="/wiki/Aretha_Franklin" title="Aretha Franklin">Aretha Franklin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ray_Charles" title="Ray Charles">Ray Charles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reba_McEntire" title="Reba McEntire">Reba McEntire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gladys_Knight" title="Gladys Knight">Gladys Knight</a> and <a href="/wiki/Willie_Nelson" title="Willie Nelson">Willie Nelson</a>. Garcia is one of two singers to have sung the opening song at <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania" title="WrestleMania">WrestleMania</a> more than once, Franklin being the other.</p>
<p>García is a regular fixture in professional sports with annual performances for the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a> and <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>. She has also performed at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">1996 Summer Olympics</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Macy%27s_Thanksgiving_Day_Parade" title="Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade">Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade</a>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live_with_Regis_and_Kelly" title="Live with Regis and Kelly">Live with Regis and Kathie Lee</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars" title="Dancing with the Stars">Dancing with the Stars</a></i> and as an opening act for <a href="/wiki/James_Brown" title="James Brown">James Brown</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p>García's first single, "Shout", debuted at No. 69 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a></i> in October 2002. She recalled her reaction to the news years later, commenting, "When my single came out years ago, and it was just a single, I remember the very first week it charted No. 69, and I was screaming as I grabbed Billboard, running down the streets of New York, holding it up and screaming, ‘I made Billboard! I made Billboard!' I'm dreaming for that moment again".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quiero_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-quiero-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garcia recorded fellow Diva <a href="/wiki/Torrie_Wilson" title="Torrie Wilson">Torrie Wilson</a>'s theme song, "Need A Little Time", for <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Anthology" title="WWE Anthology">WWE Anthology</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation album</a> released by World Wrestling Entertainment in 2002. She co-wrote <i>You Just Don't Know Me At All</i> with producers Doug Kistner and Anthony Krizan, which was released in January 2004 on the <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Originals" title="WWE Originals">WWE Originals</a></i> album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-originals_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-originals-16">[16]</a></sup> It became her theme song at WWE live events.</p>
<p>García began work on a solo album with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy" title="Grammy">Grammy</a> award winning record producers <a href="/wiki/George_Noriega" title="George Noriega">George Noriega</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Mitchell" title="Tim Mitchell">Tim Mitchell</a> in 2005, who have also worked with multi <a href="/wiki/Platinum" title="Platinum">platinum</a> artists such as <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Martin" title="Ricky Martin">Ricky Martin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>. Her album <i>¡Quiero Vivir!</i>, which is Spanish for <i>I Wanna Live</i>, was released on October 9, 2007, via Universal Music Latino. Garcia co-wrote eleven songs on the album, including three with <a href="/wiki/Jon_Secada" title="Jon Secada">Jon Secada</a>, who she duets with on "Adonde". The album contains ten Spanish language tracks, and two English translations; "Under in Love" ("Desenamorada") and "Where Did Love Go?" ("Adonde").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-track_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-track-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williams_III_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Williams_III-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following her departure from WWE in September 2009, García began work on a follow-up album. The new project will be predominantly English speaking, with possible Spanish nuances included. Garcia reunited with "¡Quiero Vivir!" producers George Noriega and Tim Mitchell to record the first song in Miami, Florida in February 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> She also recorded tracks with Grammy-nominated producer <a href="/wiki/Oh,_Hush!" title="Oh, Hush!">Oh, Hush!</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garcia appeared at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/53rd_Grammy_Awards" title="53rd Grammy Awards">53rd annual Grammy Awards</a>, held on February 13, 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Staples_Center" title="Staples Center">Staples Center</a> in Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Her appearance was featured the next evening on <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 27, 2014, Garcia announced that she and her band, The Fuse, will be performing every Saturday night at Club Madrid in The Sunset Station Casino in Henderson, Nevada, right next to Las Vegas. Her first show was performed on June 14, 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_wrestling_career">Professional wrestling career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lilian_Garcia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Professional wrestling career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wrestling_Federation.2FEntertainment">World Wrestling Federation/Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lilian_Garcia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: World Wrestling Federation/Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Ring_announcing_and_storyline_involvements_.281999.E2.80.932008.29">Ring announcing and storyline involvements (1999–2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lilian_Garcia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ring announcing and storyline involvements (1999–2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>García was hired by the World Wrestling Federation as a ring announcer in 1999. She made her debut on the August 23, 1999, episode of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw is War</a></i> in Ames, Iowa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zimmerman_August-99_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmerman_August-99-24">[24]</a></sup> She was involved in her first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angle_(professional_wrestling)" title="Angle (professional wrestling)">angle</a> on the September 13 episode of <i>Raw is War</i>, when <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Jarrett" title="Jeff Jarrett">Jeff Jarrett</a> applied a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_holds#Figure_four_leglock" title="Professional wrestling holds">figure four leglock</a> to her after she angered him by announcing his loss by disqualification to <a href="/wiki/Luna_Vachon" title="Luna Vachon">Luna Vachon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zimmerman_September-99_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Zimmerman_September-99-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Garcia's second angle occurred on the December 13, 1999, episode of <i>Raw</i>. Lilian introduced <a href="/wiki/Stacy_Carter" title="Stacy Carter">Miss Kitty</a> for a <a href="/wiki/WWE_Women%27s_Championship_(1956%E2%80%932010)" title="WWE Women's Championship (1956–2010)">Women's Championship</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mud_wrestling#Variations" title="Mud wrestling">chocolate pudding pool match</a>. After Lilian announced her, Miss Kitty grabbed the mic and informed Lilian that she was now to be known as "The Kat". Her opponent, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terri_Poch" title="Terri Poch">Tori</a>, came out for their match and right after the two women began, <a href="/wiki/Sean_Waltman" title="Sean Waltman">X-Pac</a> came poolside in a skin diving suit complete with snorkel, flippers, and goggles. As he was adjusting his goggles Tori jumped on his back causing both of them to fall into the pool with X-Pac landing up on top of Tori, giving Miss Kitty an easy pinfall. Lilian announced the winner as "Miss Kitty", which infuriated The Kat, who threw Lilian into the pool of pudding.</p>
<p>García sang in the WWF for the first time on February 14, 2000, at a <a href="/wiki/House_show" title="House show">house show</a>, and began singing "<a href="/wiki/The_Star-Spangled_Banner" title="The Star-Spangled Banner">The Star-Spangled Banner</a>" at television tapings later that month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Medalis_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Medalis-12">[12]</a></sup> Her first televised performance was on April 2, 2000, where she opened <a href="/wiki/WrestleMania_2000" title="WrestleMania 2000">WrestleMania 2000</a>. Following the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a>, Garcia sang "The Star-Spangled Banner" on the September 13, 2001, episode of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown!</a></i>, receiving a standing ovation. In the course of the evening, Garcia commented on the attacks, saying "Well, to say the least, this has been a real roller coaster of a week. And especially because I live in New York City. I have loved ones there, and I'm just very fortunate and very glad that a specific one didn't get to go to his meeting at 8:30 in the morning that he was supposed to be at the Twin Towers, and so I thank God for that, and I still don't know about a lot of my friends. I have, we'll be finding out, I guess, little by little. I pray for everybody there..."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-September-01_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-September-01-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In mid-2002, García briefly <a href="/wiki/Feud_(professional_wrestling)" title="Feud (professional wrestling)">feuded</a> with rival announcer <a href="/wiki/Howard_Finkel" title="Howard Finkel">Howard Finkel</a> after Finkel left her to be decimated by <a href="/wiki/3-Minute_Warning" title="3-Minute Warning">3-Minute Warning</a>, culminating in an "evening gown versus tuxedo match" that Garcia won with the assistance of <a href="/wiki/Stacy_Keibler" title="Stacy Keibler">Stacy Keibler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trish_Stratus" title="Trish Stratus">Trish Stratus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Raw_2.2F26.2F02_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Raw_2.2F26.2F02-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the Memorial Day episode of <i>Raw</i> on May 26, 2003, Garcia was singing "America the Beautiful" to the crowd when she was interrupted by <a href="/wiki/La_R%C3%A9sistance_(professional_wrestling)" title="La Résistance (professional wrestling)">La Résistance</a> members <a href="/wiki/Sylvain_Grenier" title="Sylvain Grenier">Sylvain Grenier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Dupr%C3%A9e" title="René Duprée">René Duprée</a> as they insult America saying they should've been more like the French and they mock the American people by calling them "fat and ugly". Just then, Garcia's friend, Co-General Manager <a href="/wiki/Stone_Cold_Steve_Austin" title="Stone Cold Steve Austin">Stone Cold Steve Austin</a> came down to the ring and tossed Grenier out of the ring and delivered a Stunner to Duprée. After the two Frenchmen walked out of the ring, Austin sang America the Beautiful with Lilian and the two celebrated with a beer bash.</p>

<p>In June 2005, García began an on-screen romance with wrestler <a href="/wiki/Viscera_(wrestler)" title="Viscera (wrestler)">Viscera</a>. The angle saw García <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proposal_of_marriage" title="Proposal of marriage">propose</a> to Viscera, stating that she wanted to marry him during the <a href="/wiki/Vengeance_(2005)" title="Vengeance (2005)">Vengeance</a> <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> on June 26. The angle ended at <i>Vengeance</i> when Viscera rejected Garcia in favor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles_Wright_(wrestler)" title="Charles Wright (wrestler)">The Godfather's</a> <a href="/wiki/Prostitution" title="Prostitution">Hos</a>. The angle was briefly resurrected eleven months later when Viscera proposed to Garcia on the May 22, 2006, episode of <i>Raw</i>, but was interrupted by <a href="/wiki/Umaga_(wrestler)" title="Umaga (wrestler)">Umaga</a> before receiving an answer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tywalk_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tywalk-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>García took part in her first major <a href="/wiki/WWE_Diva" title="WWE Diva">Diva</a> publication in 2005 with the release of the <i>Divas 2005</i> swimsuit magazine and related DVD <i>Viva Las Divas of the WWE</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Waldman_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Waldman-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the June 5, 2006, episode of <i>Raw</i>, Garcia (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legit_(professional_wrestling)" title="Legit (professional wrestling)">legitimately</a>) suffered a sprained wrist when wrestler <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Haas" title="Charlie Haas">Charlie Haas</a> inadvertently knocked her to the ground as she stood on the ring apron. One week later, the accident was metamorphosed into a storyline, with Haas incurring the wrath of Viscera. The storyline ended on the July 10, 2006, episode of <i>Raw</i>, when Viscera accidentally performed a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_throws#Samoan_drop" title="Professional wrestling throws">Samoan drop</a> on Garcia while confronting Haas. Garcia was removed from television for several weeks in order to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sell_(professional_wrestling)" title="Sell (professional wrestling)">sell</a> the Samoan drop.</p>
<p>García took part in her second major Diva publication in 2007 with the release of <i>WWE Divas Do San Antonio</i>, in honor of the WWE being in San Antonio for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Rumble_(2007)" title="Royal Rumble (2007)">Royal Rumble</a>.</p>
<p>She returned to announcing on the July 2, 2007, episode of <i>Raw</i> after taking time off to recover from a skiing injury in which she tore the ACL in her right knee. She was not fully recovered as was evident from her only announcing the first two matches from the ring and the rest from the floor.</p>
<p>On the August 6, 2007, episode of <i>Raw</i>, Garcia announced her new album entitled "Quiero Vivir". Lilian then started a feud with <a href="/wiki/Jillian_Hall" title="Jillian Hall">Jillian Hall</a>, who claimed to be a better singer than Lilian. The next week, she competed in <i>WWE Idol</i>, a parody of <a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a>, in which she sang "New York, New York" by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Sinatra" title="Frank Sinatra">Frank Sinatra</a> before being interrupted by <a href="/wiki/Santino_Marella" title="Santino Marella">Santino Marella</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement_and_part-time_appearances_.282009.E2.80.932010.29">Retirement and part-time appearances (2009–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lilian_Garcia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Retirement and part-time appearances (2009–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In addition to announcing on <i>Raw</i> and <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> matches, Garcia also announced on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Superstars" title="WWE Superstars">WWE Superstars</a></i>, making her first appearance on April 23, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Superstars_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Superstars-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the last week of August 2009, Garcia celebrated ten years with WWE, becoming the organization's first and only "Decade Diva".</p>
<p>On the September 21, 2009, episode of <i>Raw</i> from <a href="/wiki/Little_Rock,_Arkansas" title="Little Rock, Arkansas">Little Rock, Arkansas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Lawler" title="Jerry Lawler">Jerry Lawler</a> announced to the live audience that it was Garcia's final night with WWE. Garcia then made an emotional speech in the ring, thanking the fans and all of her colleagues. The final match she announced live featured <a href="/wiki/Randy_Orton" title="Randy Orton">Randy Orton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Jericho" title="Chris Jericho">Chris Jericho</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Big_Show" title="Big Show">Big Show</a> taking on the team of <a href="/wiki/John_Cena" title="John Cena">John Cena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montel_Vontavious_Porter" title="Montel Vontavious Porter">Montel Vontavious Porter</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Henry" title="Mark Henry">Mark Henry</a>, although a match between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eddie_Col%C3%B3n" title="Eddie Colón">Primo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Masters" title="Chris Masters">Chris Masters</a> was taped earlier that evening and aired three days later on <i>WWE Superstars</i>, marking her final television appearance.</p>
<p>Garcia made a special appearance before the <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a> audience on November 16, 2009, singing <a href="/wiki/The_Star-Spangled_Banner" title="The Star-Spangled Banner">The Star-Spangled Banner</a> prior to the evening's <i>Raw</i> telecast. Garcia filled in for <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a> ring announcer <a href="/wiki/Justin_Roberts" title="Justin Roberts">Justin Roberts</a> on April 19, 2010, due to being stranded in Europe with other WWE Superstars and crew as a result of the <a href="/wiki/2010_eruptions_of_Eyjafjallaj%C3%B6kull" title="2010 eruptions of Eyjafjallajökull">Eyjafjallajökull volcanic eruption</a>. Her appearance caused a surge in online searches, resulting in the term "Lilian Garcia" becoming the eighth most searched-for term on <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> that evening and into the next day.</p>

<p>On December 5, 2011, WWE announced that Garcia would be returning to ring announcing full-time, appearing on <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> She returned at the December 6 tapings of the show. She also served as the ring announcer for <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Main_Event" title="WWE Main Event">Main Event</a></i> and pay-per-views.</p>
<p>Garcia suffered accidents prior to <i>SmackDown</i> tapings in 2012. While heading to the ring prior to the March 13 taping in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a>, the strap on her left shoe broke. One month later, during the April 17 taping in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London,_England" title="London, England">London, England</a>, she tripped over pyrotechnic equipment on the top of the stage and fell down on the ramp, landing on her knees. On the April 19, 2013, episode of <i>SmackDown</i>, Garcia was instructed dance steps by <a href="/wiki/Fandango_(wrestler)" title="Fandango (wrestler)">Fandango</a> and insulted.</p>
<p>On the June 6, 2014, episode of <i>SmackDown</i>, she was involved in a confrontation with <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Fox" title="Alicia Fox">Alicia Fox</a> after Fox lost a match to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natalya_(wrestler)" title="Natalya (wrestler)">Natalya</a>. On October 20, 2014, after <a href="/wiki/Justin_Roberts" title="Justin Roberts">Justin Roberts</a> was released from WWE, Garcia returned as the full-time ring announcer for <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> In April 2015, Garcia underwent knee surgery, leaving her off television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Lilian returned from knee surgery on May 31 at <a href="/wiki/Elimination_Chamber_(2015)" title="Elimination Chamber (2015)">Elimination Chamber</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 1, 2016, Garcia announced her departure from WWE due to wanting to care for her ailing father, who was battling two different unidentified types of <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> She made a one-night return on December 14, 2016, at WWE Tribute to the Troops where she sang the American national anthem. On December 26, Garcia announced that her father had died on Christmas Day.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lilian_Garcia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>García was previously married, but has since divorced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ross_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ross-37">[37]</a></sup> Garcia married her second husband, Chris Joseph, on September 28, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leaving_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leaving-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 17, 2007, García twisted her knee in heavy snow while skiing in <a href="/wiki/Killington,_Vermont" title="Killington, Vermont">Killington, Vermont</a>, incurring a <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament_injury" title="Anterior cruciate ligament injury">torn</a> <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament" title="Anterior cruciate ligament">anterior cruciate ligament</a>. She underwent surgery on her knee on April 5, 2007, in New York City under Dr. <a href="/wiki/Armin_Tehrany" title="Armin Tehrany">Armin Tehrany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> She returned to <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i> on July 2.</p>
<p>García's first album, <i><a href="/wiki/%C2%A1Quiero_Vivir!" title="¡Quiero Vivir!">¡Quiero Vivir!</a></i>, was released on October 9, 2007.</p>
<p>On November 6, 2009, Garcia almost got robbed during the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>' <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Series" title="2009 World Series">2009 World Series</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ticker-tape_parade" title="Ticker-tape parade">ticker-tape parade</a>. She said that a "punk kid" came up to her and tried to steal her phone. When Garcia wouldn't relinquish the phone, the assailant threw her to the ground and ran away. Garcia suffered a scraped elbow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 26, 2012, Garcia was struck by a car in Los Angeles, causing multiple contusions and lacerations on the left side of her body. Doctors immobilized Garcia's neck, after which she was said to be in a stable condition. She was released from the hospital two days later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
