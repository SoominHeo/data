<p><b>Limpopo</b> (<small>UK: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/lɪmˈpəʊpəʊ/</a></span>) is the northernmost <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_South_Africa" title="Provinces of South Africa">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>. It is named after the <a href="/wiki/Limpopo_River" title="Limpopo River">Limpopo River</a>, which forms the province's western and northern borders. The name "Limpopo" has its etymological origin in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Sotho" title="Northern Sotho">Northern Sotho</a> language word <i>diphororo tša meetse</i>, meaning "strong gushing waterfalls".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The capital is <a href="/wiki/Polokwane" title="Polokwane">Polokwane</a>, formerly called <i>Pietersburg</i>.</p>
<p>The province was formed from the northern region of <a href="/wiki/Transvaal_Province" title="Transvaal Province">Transvaal Province</a> in 1994, and was initially named <b>Northern Transvaal</b>. The following year, it was renamed <b>Northern Province</b>, which remained the name until 2003, when it was <a href="/wiki/Geographical_renaming" title="Geographical renaming">formally changed</a> to Limpopo after deliberation by the provincial government and amendment of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_South_Africa" title="Constitution of South Africa">South African Constitution</a>. An alternate name considered for the province was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mapungubwe" title="Mapungubwe">Mapungubwe</a>, the area where its most ancient gold-using civilization had been discovered a few years earlier.</p>
<p>Limpopo has the highest level of poverty of any South African province, with 78.9% of the population living below the national poverty line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2011, 74.4% of local dwellings were located in a tribal or traditional area, compared to a national average of 27.1%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supweb1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-supweb1-5">[5]</a></sup> The Northern Sotho language is spoken by more than half of Limpopo's population.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limpopo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Limpopo Province shares international borders with districts and provinces of three countries: <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Central_District_(Botswana)" title="Central District (Botswana)">Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kgatleng_District" title="Kgatleng District">Kgatleng</a> districts to the west and northwest respectively, <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matabeleland_South" title="Matabeleland South">Matabeleland South</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masvingo_(province)" title="Masvingo (province)">Masvingo</a> provinces to the north and northeast respectively, and <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Gaza_Province" title="Gaza Province">Gaza Province</a> to the east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Limpopo is the link between South Africa and countries further afield in sub-Saharan Africa. On its southern edge, from east to west, it shares borders with the South African provinces of <a href="/wiki/Mpumalanga" title="Mpumalanga">Mpumalanga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gauteng" title="Gauteng">Gauteng</a>, and <a href="/wiki/North_West_(South_African_province)" title="North West (South African province)">North West</a>. Its border with Gauteng includes that province's <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>-<a href="/wiki/Pretoria" title="Pretoria">Pretoria</a> axis, the most industrialized metropole on the continent. The province is at the centre of regional, national, and international developing markets.</p>
<p>Limpopo contains much of the <a href="/wiki/Waterberg_Biosphere" title="Waterberg Biosphere">Waterberg Biosphere</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Massif" title="Massif">massif</a> of approximately 15,000 km<sup>2</sup> (5,800 sq mi) which is the first region in the northern part of South Africa to be named a <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biosphere_Reserve" title="Biosphere Reserve">Biosphere Reserve</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Massif" title="Massif">massif</a> was shaped by hundreds of millions of years of riverine erosion yielding diverse bluff and <a href="/wiki/Butte" title="Butte">butte</a> <a href="/wiki/Landform" title="Landform">landforms</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hogan_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hogan-7">[7]</a></sup> The Waterberg ecosystem can be characterized as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dry_deciduous_forest" title="Dry deciduous forest">dry deciduous forest</a> or <a href="/wiki/Bushveld" title="Bushveld">Bushveld</a>. Within the Waterberg, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaeological" title="Archaeological">archaeological</a> finds date to the <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone Age</a>. Nearby are early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evolutionary" title="Evolutionary">evolutionary</a> finds related to the origin of humans.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limpopo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The province is a typical developing area, exporting primary products and importing manufactured goods and services. It is also one of the poorest regions of South Africa with a big gap between poor and rich residents, especially in rural areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> However, Limpopo's economy and standard of living have shown great improvement. A recent border shift with Limpopo's wealthier neighbour, <a href="/wiki/Mpumalanga" title="Mpumalanga">Mpumalanga</a>, was effected to try and bring some wealth into the province.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limpopo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The bushveld is <a href="/wiki/Beef_cattle" title="Beef cattle">beef cattle</a> country, where extensive ranching operations are often supplemented by controlled hunting. About 80% of South Africa's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Game_hunting" title="Game hunting">game hunting</a> industry is found in Limpopo.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunflower" title="Sunflower">Sunflowers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maize" title="Maize">maize</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">peanuts</a> are cultivated in the <a href="/wiki/Bela-Bela" title="Bela-Bela">Bela-Bela</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modimolle" title="Modimolle">Modimolle</a> areas. Modimolle is also known for its table <a href="/wiki/Grape" title="Grape">grapes</a>. Tropical fruit, such as <a href="/wiki/Banana" title="Banana">bananas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lychee" title="Lychee">litchis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pineapple" title="Pineapple">pineapples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mango" title="Mango">mangoes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Papaya" title="Papaya">pawpaws</a>, as well as a variety of <a href="/wiki/Nut_(fruit)" title="Nut (fruit)">nuts</a>, are grown in the <a href="/wiki/Tzaneen" title="Tzaneen">Tzaneen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Trichardt" title="Louis Trichardt">Louis Trichardt</a> areas. Tzaneen is also at the centre of extensive <a href="/wiki/Citrus" title="Citrus">citrus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">tea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> plantations, and a major <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a> industry.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mining">Mining</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limpopo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mining">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Limpopo's rich mineral deposits include the platinum group metals, iron ore, chromium, high- and middle-grade <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coking_coal" title="Coking coal">coking coal</a>, diamonds, <a href="/wiki/Antimony" title="Antimony">antimony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phosphate" title="Phosphate">phosphate</a>, and copper, as well as mineral reserves like gold, emeralds, <a href="/wiki/Scheelite" title="Scheelite">scheelite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magnetite" title="Magnetite">magnetite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vermiculite" title="Vermiculite">vermiculite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silicon" title="Silicon">silicon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mica" title="Mica">mica</a>. Commodities such as <a href="/wiki/Black_granite" title="Black granite">black granite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corundum" title="Corundum">corundum</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Feldspar" title="Feldspar">feldspar</a> are also found. Mining contributes to over a fifth of the provincial economy.</p>
<p>Limpopo has the largest <a href="/wiki/Platinum" title="Platinum">platinum</a> deposit in South Africa.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a href="/wiki/Waterberg_Coalfield" title="Waterberg Coalfield">Waterberg Coalfield</a>, the eastern extension of <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mmamabula" title="Mmamabula">Mmamabula</a> coalfields, is estimated to contain 40% of South Africa's coal reserves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limpopo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tourism is one of the three pillars of the Limpopo economy along with mining and agribusiness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ledet.gov.za_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ledet.gov.za-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2008, the Province accounted for 5% of all foreign tourist bed nights in South Africa, numbers which are showing strong annual growth. The R 93 million Provincial tourism budget for 2010/11 represents 11% of Limpopo's total budget.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ledet.gov.za_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ledet.gov.za-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-search.gov.za_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-search.gov.za-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Near <a href="/wiki/Modjadjiskloof" title="Modjadjiskloof">Modjadjiskloof</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Sunland_Baobab" title="Sunland Baobab">Sunland Baobab</a> farms, there is a large Baobab tree which has been fashioned into a rather spacious pub.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Limpopo Department of Economic Development, Environment and Tourism has targeted the province as a preferred <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eco-tourism" title="Eco-tourism">eco-tourism</a> destination. Its Environment and Tourism Programme encompasses tourism, protected areas and community environment development to achieve sustainable economic growth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-search.gov.za_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-search.gov.za-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>While Limpopo is one of South Africa's poorest provinces, it is rich in wildlife, which gives it an edge in attracting tourists. Both the private and public sectors are investing in tourism development.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation_and_communications">Transportation and communications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limpopo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Transportation and communications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The province has excellent road, rail, and air links. The N1 route from <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>, which extends the length of the province, is the busiest overland route in Africa in terms of cross-border trade in raw materials and beneficiated goods. The port of <a href="/wiki/Durban" title="Durban">Durban</a>, South Africa’s busiest, is served directly by the province, as are the ports of <a href="/wiki/Richards_Bay" title="Richards Bay">Richards Bay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maputo" title="Maputo">Maputo</a>. <a href="/wiki/Polokwane_International_Airport" title="Polokwane International Airport">Polokwane International Airport</a> is situated just north of <a href="/wiki/Polokwane" title="Polokwane">Polokwane</a>.</p>

<p>The Department of Education is charged with the responsibility of effecting quality education and training for all. The Department has to coordinate all professional development and support. Policies, systems, and procedures had to be developed.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limpopo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Association football</a>: <a href="/wiki/Polokwane" title="Polokwane">Polokwane</a> was one of South Africa's host cities for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>, with matches being played at the <a href="/wiki/Peter_Mokaba_Stadium" title="Peter Mokaba Stadium">Peter Mokaba Stadium</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rugby_union_in_South_Africa" title="Rugby union in South Africa">Rugby union</a>: Limpopo has no provincial rugby team of its own; it is represented in the domestic <a href="/wiki/Currie_Cup" title="Currie Cup">Currie Cup</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Pretoria" title="Pretoria">Pretoria</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Blue_Bulls" title="Blue Bulls">Blue Bulls</a>. The Blue Bulls also operate a <a href="/wiki/Super_Rugby" title="Super Rugby">Super Rugby</a> franchise, known simply as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bulls_(rugby)" title="Bulls (rugby)">Bulls</a>. Limpopo nonetheless produces its share of top players. Most notably, the two most-<a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">capped</a> forwards in the history of <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_rugby_union_team" title="South Africa national rugby union team">the country's national team</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Smit" title="John Smit">John Smit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victor_Matfield" title="Victor Matfield">Victor Matfield</a>, are both natives of Polokwane.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">Basketball</a>: The province is home to <a href="/wiki/Limpopo_Pride" title="Limpopo Pride">Limpopo Pride</a>, a professional team that plays in South Africa's top basketball division, the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_National_League" title="Basketball National League">Basketball National League</a>.</li>
</ul>

<p>The population of Limpopo consists of several ethnic groups distinguished by culture, language and race. 97.3% of the population is <a href="/wiki/Black_people" title="Black people">Black</a>, 2.4% is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whites" title="Whites">White</a>, 0.2% is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coloured" title="Coloured">Coloured</a>, and 0.1% is <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a>/<a href="/wiki/Asian_people" title="Asian people">Asian</a>. The province has the smallest percentage and second smallest total number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_South_Africans" title="White South Africans">white South Africans</a> in the country. It also has the highest Black percentage out of all the provinces.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Sotho_people" title="Northern Sotho people">Northern Sotho people</a> (of which the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bapedi" title="Bapedi">Bapedi</a> are a part) make up the largest percentage of the black population, being 52% of the province. The <a href="/wiki/Tsonga_people" title="Tsonga people">Tsonga people</a> comprise about 17.0% of the province; the Tsonga also comprise about 11.5% of <a href="/wiki/Mpumalanga" title="Mpumalanga">Mpumalanga</a> province since the southern part of their homeland, <a href="/wiki/Gazankulu" title="Gazankulu">Gazankulu</a>, was cut off from Limpopo and allocated to Mpumalanga. The <a href="/wiki/Venda_people" title="Venda people">Venda</a> make up about 16.7%. <a href="/wiki/Afrikaners" title="Afrikaners">Afrikaners</a> make up the majority of Limpopo's white population, about 95,000 people; <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>-speaking whites number just over 20,000. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vhembe" title="Vhembe">Vhembe</a> district has the smallest share of white people in Limpopo, about 5,000 total, while the <a href="/wiki/Waterberg_District_Municipality" title="Waterberg District Municipality">Waterberg</a> district has the largest share of whites, with more than 60,000 whites residing there. <a href="/wiki/Coloureds" title="Coloureds">Coloureds</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asians" title="Asians">Asians</a>/<a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indians</a> make up a very small percentage of the province's total population.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="HIV_.2F_Aids">HIV / Aids</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Limpopo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: HIV / Aids">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At 18.5% (2007), Limpopo has a fairly high incidence of <a href="/wiki/HIV" title="HIV">HIV</a> compared to other South African provinces. Cases rose from 14.5% to 21.5% between 2001 and 2005, with a slight fall between 2005 and 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
