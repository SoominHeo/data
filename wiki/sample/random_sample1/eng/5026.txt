<p><b>Lipps, Inc.</b> (pronounced like "<a href="/wiki/Lip_sync" title="Lip sync">lip sync</a>") was a <a href="/wiki/Musical_ensemble" title="Musical ensemble">band</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minneapolis,_Minnesota,_USA" title="Minneapolis, Minnesota, USA">Minneapolis, Minnesota, USA</a>. The group was best known for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Hot_100_number-one_singles_of_1980_(U.S.)" title="List of Hot 100 number-one singles of 1980 (U.S.)">chart-topping</a> 1980 worldwide hit single "<a href="/wiki/Funkytown" title="Funkytown">Funkytown</a>", which hit No. 1 in 28 different countries around the world and was certified as double-<a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">platinum</a> in sales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group originally consisted of lead vocalist <a href="/wiki/Cynthia_Johnson" title="Cynthia Johnson">Cynthia Johnson</a> and a changing lineup of <a href="/wiki/Session_musician" title="Session musician">session musicians</a>, including guitarist <a href="/wiki/David_Z_(music_producer)" title="David Z (music producer)">David Rivkin</a>, guitarist Tom Riopelle and bassist Terry Grant. <a href="/wiki/Steven_Greenberg_(musician)" title="Steven Greenberg (musician)">Steven Greenberg</a>, the creator of the act, <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">wrote</a> and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">produced</a> most of the group's music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-funkytown.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-funkytown.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lipps_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lipps Inc.'s first release was a 1979 single, "Rock It", on Greenberg's own Flight imprint. The act released its debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Mouth_to_Mouth_(Lipps_Inc._album)" title="Mouth to Mouth (Lipps Inc. album)">Mouth to Mouth</a></i> in late <a href="/wiki/1979_in_music" title="1979 in music">1979</a>.</p>
<p>The second single from this album, "<a href="/wiki/Funkytown" title="Funkytown">Funkytown</a>", spent four weeks at #1 on both the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Music/Club_Play" title="Hot Dance Music/Club Play">Hot Dance Music/Club Play</a> charts in the United States. The 7" single sold over two million copies within a few months and was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">platinum record</a> within the same year of its release .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allmusic_awards_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic_awards-4">[4]</a></sup> It reached #2 in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and was a hit throughout the world.</p>
<p>Lipps Inc.'s later singles failed to match the band's initial success (the only other Hot 100 entry was "Rock It", which peaked at #64); however, more dance hits were released throughout the 1980s, including "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/How_Long%3F" title="How Long?">How Long?</a>" in 1981 (originally a hit for <a href="/wiki/Ace_(band)" title="Ace (band)">Ace</a>), which reached #4 on the U.S. dance chart. After the third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Designer_Music" title="Designer Music">Designer Music</a></i>, Johnson left the band and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Margie_Cox" title="Margie Cox">Margie Cox</a> and <a class="external text" href="http://www.melanierosales.com" rel="nofollow">Melanie Rosales</a>. Lipps Inc. released its final album, <i>4</i>, in 1983 before disbanding two years later.</p>
