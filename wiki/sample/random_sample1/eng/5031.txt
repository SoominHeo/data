<p><b>Lincoln City Football Club</b> is a professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> club based in the city of <a href="/wiki/Lincoln,_England" title="Lincoln, England">Lincoln</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a>. The club participates in the <a href="/wiki/National_League_(division)" title="National League (division)">National League</a>, the fifth tier of English football.</p>
<p>The club plays at the 10,312-capacity <a href="/wiki/Sincil_Bank" title="Sincil Bank">Sincil Bank</a>, and are nicknamed <i>the Imps</i> after the legend of the <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Imp" title="Lincoln Imp">Lincoln Imp</a>. They have also been known as the Red Imps. Traditionally they play in red and white striped shirts with black shorts and red and white socks. Their most recent championship win was the <a href="/wiki/National_League_(English_football)" title="National League (English football)">Football Conference</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/1987%E2%80%9388_in_English_football" title="1987–88 in English football">1987–88</a> season. This season saw the club set an all-time record attendance for a Conference match, attracting 9,432 spectators in a 2–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Wycombe_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wycombe Wanderers F.C.">Wycombe Wanderers</a>, on 2 May 1988, the last game of the season (this record has since been broken by <a href="/wiki/Oxford_United_F.C." title="Oxford United F.C.">Oxford United</a>). The game also decided the championship, as beforehand Lincoln had not occupied the top spot at any point in the season.</p>
<p>The club's highest-ever position is fifth in the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Second_Division" title="Football League Second Division">Second Division</a> in <a href="/wiki/1901%E2%80%9302_in_English_football" title="1901–02 in English football">1901–02</a>. They have not been higher than the third tier since 1960–61, and they hold the record for the most seasons in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">the Football League</a> without reaching the top tier (104), and the most demotions from the League (five, in 1908, 1911, 1920, 1987 and 2011 – though in all but the last case, they returned to the League the following season).</p>
<p>The club has reached the last 16 in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> three times, most recently in 1901–02. Their best performance in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> came in 1967–68, when they reached the fourth round before losing 0–3 at home to <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> in a replay.</p>
<p>In recent history, Lincoln have reached the play-offs of the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Third_Division" title="Football League Third Division">Third Division</a>/League Two in five consecutive seasons, from 2002–03 to 2006–07, losing in the final twice (2002–03 and 2004–05) and the semi-finals three times. This failure to succeed in five consecutive play-off competitions is also a record.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_City_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_City_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Having formed officially as an amateur association in 1884 after the disbanding of Lincoln Rovers (formerly Lincoln Recreation), football in the city of Lincoln had been prominent since the 1860s (although not strictly connected to the modern day club). The first game Lincoln played as an amateur team at the <a href="/wiki/John_O%27Gaunts" title="John O'Gaunts">John O'Gaunts</a> Ground, a ground that wealthy local brewer Robert Dawber provided and rented out to the club, was an emphatic 9–1 victory over local rivals Sleaford, on 4 October 1884. George Hallam set two records for the club that day. He scored the first ever goal for the club, and also the first ever hat-trick. Their first competitive game at home also ended in an emphatic manner, beating Boston Excelsior 11–0, with Edwin Teesdale scoring four goals. It was at this time, before the club gained entry into the Football League and professional status, that the County Cup was their main priority. They won it for the first time in the 1886–87 season with a 2–0 replay victory over neighbours <a href="/wiki/Grimsby_Town_F.C." title="Grimsby Town F.C.">Grimsby Town F.C.</a>, after the initial match had finished 2–2.</p>
<p>Lincoln soon helped to form what was then the Second Division in 1892–93 season, as an increasing number of clubs wished to join the Football League. Their first game in the Football League was a 4–2 away defeat to Sheffield United on 3 September 1892.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Their first home game was also against Sheffield United, this time, however, Lincoln won 1–0. The first game at Sincil Bank in 1895, after moving from the John O'Gaunts Ground due to Dawber's death, was a 0–0 friendly draw with local rivals, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gainsborough_Trinity" title="Gainsborough Trinity">Gainsborough Trinity</a>. The first competitive fixture at the ground was against Arsenal, the game ended 1–1.</p>
<p>In January 1907 The Imps knocked Chelsea out of the FA Cup after a replay. Managed by <a href="/wiki/David_Calderhead" title="David Calderhead">David Calderhead</a>, two late goals salvaged a home draw in the first leg. In the replay in London, an injury time goal by <a href="/wiki/Norrie_Fairgray" title="Norrie Fairgray">Norrie Fairgray</a> took Lincoln through. Chelsea returned at the end of the season to poach Calderhead to become their manager.</p>
<p>Up until the 1920s Lincoln spent most of their time swinging between the Second Division and the more localised leagues, the Midland and also the Central league. After then, however, in the 1921–22 season, Lincoln, along with several other clubs from the Central and Midland leagues, founded the Third Division (North). The newly founded league and the Second Division would take turns in becoming Lincoln's home up until the early 1960s where they would drop a further division to the Fourth Division in the 1962–63 season. Formed in 1884 as an amateur association, Lincoln turned professional in the <a href="/wiki/1891%E2%80%9392_in_English_football" title="1891–92 in English football">1891–92</a> season. Originally they played at the John O'Gaunts ground, however, in 1895 they moved to their current ground, Sincil Bank.</p>
<p>Their championship honours include three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_winners_of_English_Football_League_Two_and_predecessors" title="List of winners of English Football League Two and predecessors">Division 3 (North)</a> championships in <a href="/wiki/1931%E2%80%9332_in_English_football" title="1931–32 in English football">1931–32</a>, <a href="/wiki/1947%E2%80%9348_in_English_football" title="1947–48 in English football">1947–48</a> and <a href="/wiki/1951%E2%80%9352_in_English_football" title="1951–52 in English football">1951–52</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_winners_of_English_Football_League_Two_and_predecessors" title="List of winners of English Football League Two and predecessors">Division 4</a> (now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/League_Two" title="League Two">League Two</a>) championship in <a href="/wiki/1975%E2%80%9376_in_English_football" title="1975–76 in English football">1975–76</a> (when they were managed by future <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graham_Taylor_(football_manager)" title="Graham Taylor (football manager)">Graham Taylor</a>).</p>
<p>It was the <a href="/wiki/1975%E2%80%9376_in_English_football" title="1975–76 in English football">1975–76</a> season where the club broke the record for most points for a whole season when 2 instead of 3 points were awarded for a win with 74 points in total (this was and still is the record amount of points achieved under the 2-point system); the record of winning the most games (32) and losing the fewest (4), was also set.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> City also become the first club in nearly a decade to score over 100 league goals (111 in total). They also won 21 out of 23 home league games in this season (the other 2 were drawn) and also won 11 games away from home, another impressive bout from the club. It was the season where, Graham Taylor recalls, "teams were petrified of coming to Sincil Bank".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s_and_1990s">1980s and 1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_City_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1980s and 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1982 and again in 1983, Lincoln narrowly missed out on promotion to the Second Division. In 1985, Lincoln were the opposition at <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_A.F.C." title="Bradford City A.F.C.">Bradford City</a> when the <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_stadium_fire" title="Bradford City stadium fire">Bradford City stadium fire</a> claimed the lives of 56 spectators – two of them, Bill Stacey and Jim West, were Lincoln fans, and subsequently these fans had the Stacey West stand named after them.</p>
<p>Lincoln were relegated on the last day of the <a href="/wiki/1985%E2%80%9386_in_English_football#Third_Division" title="1985–86 in English football">following season</a>, and the year after that they became the first team to suffer automatic relegation from the Football League. This was a dramatic decline for a club who had almost reached the Second Division four years earlier and has been linked to the trauma arising from the disaster. This marked the fourth occasion on which Lincoln were demoted from the Football League, a record that still stands. They regained their Football League place automatically via promotion as champions of the Conference (beforehand it was done by re-election) at the first attempt with a long ball game devised by eccentric manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colin_Murphy_(footballer_born_1950)" title="Colin Murphy (footballer born 1950)">Colin Murphy</a> and held on to it until the end of season 2010–11. On 8 September 1990, Lincoln were the opposition when <a href="/wiki/David_Longhurst" title="David Longhurst">David Longhurst</a> suffered a fatal heart attack during the first half of a game against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/York_City" title="York City">York City</a> at Bootham Crescent. The game was abandoned at halftime.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_difficulties_.26_Play-off_attempts">Financial difficulties &amp; Play-off attempts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_City_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Financial difficulties &amp; Play-off attempts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With Lincoln entering administration at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_in_English_football" title="2001–02 in English football">2001–02</a> season, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Buckley" title="Alan Buckley">Alan Buckley</a> was relieved of his duties as manager on financial grounds<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Keith_Alexander_(footballer)" title="Keith Alexander (footballer)">Keith Alexander</a> placed in charge of all football matters. On 3 May 2002 Lincoln successfully petitioned to go into administration<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> but the financial crisis would leave the first team squad bereft of players as the day saw five senior players – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jason_Barnett_(footballer)" title="Jason Barnett (footballer)">Jason Barnett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grant_Brown" title="Grant Brown">Grant Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Cameron_(footballer)" title="Dave Cameron (footballer)">David Cameron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Holmes_(footballer)" title="Steve Holmes (footballer)">Steve Holmes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justin_Walker_(footballer)" title="Justin Walker (footballer)">Justin Walker</a> – released at the end of their contracts<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> with a sixth, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Thorpe" title="Lee Thorpe">Lee Thorpe</a>, departing for <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> A hectic day finished with confirmation of Alexander's official appointment as team manager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_in_English_football" title="2002–03 in English football">2002–03</a>, Alexander was given the task of keeping the team in the football league, he proved the many pundits and fans who believed that Lincoln would be relegated and sent out of business due to financial irregularities wrong. With a team made up of cheap ex-non-league players and the lower paid members of the previous season's squad he managed to take them to the play-off final which they lost 5–2 to <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a>. The team were rewarded with a civil reception in Lincoln, and an open-top bus ride through Lincoln, an event usually preserved for the winners of such competitions, but was awarded to the team because of the massive achievement.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_in_English_football" title="2003–04 in English football">2003–04</a> Alexander again confounded the critics by coaching the Imps to another play-off position, this time losing to eventual winners <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> in the semi-finals. Alexander, one of the very few black managers in the Football League, had a very serious brain injury (a cerebral aneurysm) halfway through the season, but made a full recovery. In the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_in_English_football" title="2004–05 in English football">2004–05</a> season they again qualified for the play-offs, for a third year running, and in the semi-finals Lincoln beat <a href="/wiki/Macclesfield_Town_F.C." title="Macclesfield Town F.C.">Macclesfield Town</a> 2–1 on aggregate over two legs but lost in the final against <a href="/wiki/Southend_United_F.C." title="Southend United F.C.">Southend United</a> 2–0 after extra time.</p>

<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_in_English_football" title="2005–06 in English football">2005–06</a> season Lincoln City lost narrowly to then <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> in the second round of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005-06_Football_League_Cup" title="2005-06 Football League Cup">2005–06 League Cup</a>, taking the match to extra-time before the London side won 5–4 in the final minute. In the league, Lincoln again reached the play-offs after many fans and critics believed that they would finish in the mid-table after losing many of their first team regulars from the previous three campaigns. In January both Alexander and former Assistant Manager, Gary Simpson, were put on gardening leave by the board. Alexander was soon after reinstated, however, Simpson did not return. Shortly after, over a disagreement with other board members over the way the club was being run and certain personnel, two prominent board members, <a href="/wiki/Ray_Trew" title="Ray Trew">Ray Trew</a> and Keith Roe departed from the club. Lincoln brushed this saga to the side though, and finished 7th in League 2 after only losing 3 games since the new year. Lincoln were to face local neighbours <a href="/wiki/Grimsby_Town_F.C." title="Grimsby Town F.C.">Grimsby Town</a> in the play-offs, a side they had beaten 5–0 at Sincil Bank earlier in the season. However, once again it was not to be, as Lincoln lost 3–1 on aggregate to become the first team ever to lose four consecutive play-off competitions.</p>
<p>After speculation that he would take up the vacant managerial role at <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_United_F.C." title="Peterborough United F.C.">Peterborough United</a>, Keith Alexander left his position as manager of Lincoln City by mutual consent on 24 May 2006 stating that he could take the club no further, and shortly after on 15 June John Schofield was appointed his successor, with <a href="/wiki/John_Deehan" title="John Deehan">John Deehan</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Director_of_Football" title="Director of Football">Director of Football</a>. When John Deehan was the Director of Football, the club enjoyed a close link with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FA_Premier_League" title="FA Premier League">Premier League</a> outfit <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>. As well as Villa hosting a behind closed-doors friendly with City earlier in the 2006–07 season, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paul_Green_(footballer_born_1987)" title="Paul Green (footballer born 1987)">Paul Green</a>, a promising youngster, made a permanent move to the club, whilst goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Olejnik" title="Bobby Olejnik">Bobby Olejnik</a> featured several times on the substitute bench during his loan spell at City. Deehan also brought in <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Amoo" title="Ryan Amoo">Ryan Amoo</a>, a youth player who he worked with at Villa, who has since left the club since his contract expired. For the fifth year in a row, under a different manager, however, Lincoln City reached the League Two play-offs after finishing 5th in the league (the highest position that they have qualified for the play-offs in). Once again, however, they lost, this time to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bristol_Rovers" title="Bristol Rovers">Bristol Rovers</a> in the semi-finals courtesy of a 2–1 defeat away and a 3–5 defeat at home. The failure to succeed in five successive Play-off competitions is a record for any club.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demise_and_relegation_from_the_Football_League">Demise and relegation from the Football League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_City_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Demise and relegation from the Football League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The team started the 2007–08 campaign poorly, managing just two wins before a winless streak that lasted from 25 August to 24 November. During this winless streak the Managerial team of <a href="/wiki/John_Schofield" title="John Schofield">John Schofield</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Deehan" title="John Deehan">John Deehan</a> were sacked, and replaced with former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town" title="Huddersfield Town">Huddersfield Town</a> manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson_(footballer_born_1961)" title="Peter Jackson (footballer born 1961)">Peter Jackson</a>. Jackson quickly earned the nickname "Lord of the Imps" due to his shared name with <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">Peter Jackson</a> the director who made the <i>Lord of the Rings</i> films. Jackson parted company with the club on 2 September 2009 due to poor home form in the previous season and a poor start to the 2009/10 season.</p>
<p>On 28 September 2009, the Lincoln hotseat was handed to former <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> and <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> striker <a href="/wiki/Chris_Sutton" title="Chris Sutton">Chris Sutton</a>. His assistant was named as <a href="/wiki/Ian_Pearce" title="Ian Pearce">Ian Pearce</a>, another former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FA_Premier_League" title="FA Premier League">Premier League</a> player. The club had been managed by coach Simon Clark following the sacking of Peter Jackson and his assistant <a href="/wiki/Iffy_Onuora" title="Iffy Onuora">Iffy Onuora</a>. It was announced that Sutton would take the reins from Clark on 30 September. Sutton led Lincoln City to the Third round of the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a>, after beating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northwich_Victoria" title="Northwich Victoria">Northwich Victoria</a> in a second round game televised live on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITV1" title="ITV1">ITV1</a>. City were drawn with Premier League side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers" title="Bolton Wanderers">Bolton Wanderers</a> in the third round. The tie was played on 2 January 2010 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reebok_Stadium" title="Reebok Stadium">Reebok Stadium</a>, with Lincoln losing the game 4–0 and crashing out respectfully to the Premier League side. League form improved in January, with the team profiting from new loan and permanent signings. Loan signing <a href="/wiki/Davide_Somma" title="Davide Somma">Davide Somma</a> became an instant hero, scoring 9 goals in his 14 games on loan and ending up being Lincoln's top goalscorer for the season.</p>
<p>Sutton resigned in September 2010, citing personal reasons. However, he later revealed it was due to disagreements over spending with the club's board. On 15 October, the Imps hired <a href="/wiki/Steve_Tilson" title="Steve Tilson">Steve Tilson</a> as the club's new manager. Under new management, things looked up for the Imps and by Christmas, Tilson's side were 11th. The good run ended abruptly, and Lincoln started to slip down the table. After a run of nine losses and a draw in the final ten games, Lincoln City were relegated from League Two on the last day of the end of the 2010–2011 season. They needed a win in their final game against <a href="/wiki/Aldershot_Town_F.C." title="Aldershot Town F.C.">Aldershot Town</a> to survive, but lost 3–0. With relegation rivals <a href="/wiki/Barnet_F.C." title="Barnet F.C.">Barnet</a> winning their final game, Lincoln finished 23rd and were relegated. Almost 8,000 supporters watched the game, an attendance that had not been seen at the club in years.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conference_Premier">Conference Premier</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_City_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Conference Premier">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lincoln City have played in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conference_Premier" title="Conference Premier">Conference Premier</a> since the season 2011–12. Following relegation, Tilson released all but three members of the squad, telling them they had no future at <a href="/wiki/Sincil_Bank" title="Sincil Bank">Sincil Bank</a>. By early October, Lincoln were one point above relegation zone and the management were coming under-fire after a run of one win in four; Tilson was sacked as manager on the 10 October 2011 following a 4–0 defeat at <a href="/wiki/Tamworth_F.C." title="Tamworth F.C.">Tamworth</a>. Following the duo's sacking, <a href="/wiki/Grant_Brown" title="Grant Brown">Grant Brown</a> was put in temporary charge.</p>
<p>Brown remained in charge for four games, winning the first but none of the subsequent three, before former <a href="/wiki/Mansfield_Town_F.C." title="Mansfield Town F.C.">Mansfield Town</a> manager <a href="/wiki/David_Holdsworth" title="David Holdsworth">David Holdsworth</a> was confirmed as manager. Holdsworth managed the Imps to safety but only by 8 points; furthermore, Lincoln lost to <a href="/wiki/Isthmian_League" title="Isthmian League">Isthmian League</a> outfit <a href="/wiki/Carshalton_Athletic_F.C." title="Carshalton Athletic F.C.">Carshalton Athletic</a> in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Trophy" title="FA Trophy">FA Trophy</a> and suffered an earliest <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> exit since 1924–25.</p>
<p>Lincoln were one game away from facing <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> third round the following season, but were denied by a second round replay defeat to <a href="/wiki/Mansfield_Town_F.C." title="Mansfield Town F.C.">Mansfield Town</a>. On 17 February 2013, <a href="/wiki/David_Holdsworth" title="David Holdsworth">David Holdsworth</a> left the club by mutual consent following twelve games without a win. On 27 February 2013, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gary_Simpson_(footballer_born_1961)" title="Gary Simpson (footballer born 1961)">Gary Simpson</a>, a former assistant of <a href="/wiki/Keith_Alexander_(footballer)" title="Keith Alexander (footballer)">Keith Alexander</a> during his time at the club, was appointed manager until the end of the season. Safety was secured on the final day with a 5–1 away win against <a href="/wiki/Hyde_United_F.C." title="Hyde United F.C.">Hyde</a>.</p>
<p>After a good start to the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Lincoln_City_F.C._season" title="2013–14 Lincoln City F.C. season">2013–14</a> season, Lincoln went on a run of just two wins in seventeen games saw the Imps embroiled in relegation trouble once more. From the start of February to the end of the season, Lincoln lost just three games, and finished 14th in the league, their best placing since relegation. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gary_Simpson_(footballer_born_1961)" title="Gary Simpson (footballer born 1961)">Gary Simpson</a> was placed on gardening leave on 3 November 2014. Assistant manager <a href="/wiki/Chris_Moyses" title="Chris Moyses">Chris Moyses</a> was placed in temporary charge and then appointed permanently on 8 December 2014. Lincoln finished 15th that season. 2015-16 would prove to be largely a season of mid-table stability, eventually culminating in a 13th place finish. Just before the season ended, Moyses announced that he would leave the club in order to focus on his business interests outside of football, and was subsequently replaced by <a href="/wiki/Braintree_Town_F.C." title="Braintree Town F.C.">Braintree Town</a> manager Danny Cowley.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_City_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sincil_Bank" title="Sincil Bank">Sincil Bank</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>The club have played at Sincil Bank since 1895. Previously, Lincoln City had played at the nearby <a href="/wiki/John_O%27Gaunts" title="John O'Gaunts">John O'Gaunts</a> ground since the club's 1884 inception. Sincil Bank has an overall capacity of 10,120<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-os_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-os-1">[1]</a></sup> and is colloquially known to fans as "The Bank". It is overlooked by <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Cathedral" title="Lincoln Cathedral">Lincoln Cathedral</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Former Lincoln City chairman <a href="/wiki/John_Reames" title="John Reames">John Reames</a> re-purchased the ground from the local council in 2000 at a cost of £175,000. The club had sold it in 1982 for £225,000 to fend off the threat of eviction, arranging a 125-year lease.</p>
<p>Sincil Bank hosted <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>'s 2–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Victory_Shield" title="2008 Victory Shield">Victory Shield</a> on 28 November 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Martin_Peters" title="Martin Peters">Martin Peters</a> paraded the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_Trophy" title="FIFA World Cup Trophy">FIFA World Cup Trophy</a> at the ground in March 2010 as part of its global tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/FA_WSL" title="FA WSL">FA WSL</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincoln_Ladies_F.C." title="Lincoln Ladies F.C.">Lincoln Ladies</a> played home games at Sincil Bank in their <a href="/wiki/2011_FA_WSL" title="2011 FA WSL">2011</a> season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The Ladies' club had previously hosted <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_L.F.C." title="Arsenal L.F.C.">Arsenal Ladies</a> there in an <a href="/wiki/FA_Women%27s_Cup" title="FA Women's Cup">FA Women's Cup</a> semi-final in March 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mascot">Mascot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_City_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Mascot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lincoln City mascot is called Poacher The Imp, after the old song 'The Lincolnshire Poacher'. He has been portrayed by Gary Hutchinson since he was first introduced to Imps fans in July 1999. In 2003, Poacher finished 12th in the <a href="/wiki/Mascot_Grand_National" title="Mascot Grand National">Mascot Grand National</a>, representing his best finish.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> However following claims that the event at Huntingdon allows professional athletes to compete in the guise of mascots, Poacher has decided to boycott the event in the future. Gary retired as Poacher after 16 seasons, with his last match being a FA Cup 1st Round game at home against Plymouth Argile which finished 0–0 on 9 November 2013. Poacher is currently inactive.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivals">Rivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_City_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Rivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire_derby" title="Lincolnshire derby">Lincolnshire derby</a></div>
<p>Lincoln City are one of three professional football clubs playing inside <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a>. Football clubs in Lincoln's geographical region include <a href="/wiki/Scunthorpe_United_F.C." title="Scunthorpe United F.C.">Scunthorpe United</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grimsby_Town_F.C." title="Grimsby Town F.C.">Grimsby Town</a>.</p>
<p>Other local rivalries include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gainsborough_Trinity" title="Gainsborough Trinity">Gainsborough Trinity</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston_United" title="Boston United">Boston United</a> – both former league clubs who like Lincoln and, until their 2016 promotion, Grimsby Town have slipped out of the Football League.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Peterborough_United_F.C." title="Peterborough United F.C.">Peterborough United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mansfield_Town_F.C." title="Mansfield Town F.C.">Mansfield Town</a> and <a href="/wiki/York_City_F.C." title="York City F.C.">York City</a> are all clubs who have had some sort of local rivalry with The Imps in the past. The two other clubs in the City of Lincoln, <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_United_F.C." title="Lincoln United F.C.">Lincoln United</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Moorlands_Railway_F.C." title="Lincoln Moorlands Railway F.C.">Lincoln Moorlands Railway</a> are further down the footballing pyramid and are not considered rivals.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crest">Crest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lincoln_City_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Crest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Up until fairly recently the club's logo was very simple in design, with the letters 'L.C.F.C.' inscribed on the historic city's heraldic shield, and a ribbon displaying the club's number directly below it. Since, however, Lincoln have changed their logo twice – once in 2001 to a similar design with the club's Imp mascot and nickname added, and then again in 2014 to a simpler design, depicting only a red-and-white version of the Imp and a banner with the club name underneath. This second change was made to mark the club's 130th anniversary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<p>Traditionally, the colours and design of the Lincoln City strip have been a red and white striped shirt along with black shorts and red socks. This has varied, however, as in the late 1960s and early 70s, the club opted to field a predominantly red strip with white shorts, and also in the 2000–01 season where the shirt was quartered red and white with white shorts. Their away kit has never retained any single pattern or design, and has varied vastly throughout the seasons.</p>
<p>In the 2006–07 season, Lincoln have retained their traditional home kit colours and style, and have a predominantly black away strip with red side stripes, also with red shorts and black socks. This has been the away strip of choice for two seasons now. Their current sponsors are Starglaze (home) and Haart (away), with their kit manufacturer being Uhlsport. Dennis Horton &amp; Son, also sponsor Lincoln, with their logo appearing at the top centre on the reverse of their home kit.</p>
<p>A brand new set of kits was introduced for the 2007–08 season due to the club changing kit supplier from <a href="/wiki/Uhlsport" title="Uhlsport">Uhlsport</a> to <a href="/wiki/Umbro" title="Umbro">Umbro</a>. The home shirt retains the traditional red and white stripes to this day. Their away kit is now Lincoln Green in colour.</p>
