<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">E (musical note)</h1>

<p><b>E</b> is the third note of the <a href="/wiki/C_major" title="C major">C major</a> scale, and <b>mi</b> in <a href="/wiki/Solf%C3%A8ge#Fixed_do_solf.C3.A8ge" title="Solfège">fixed-do solfège</a>. It has <a href="/wiki/Enharmonic" title="Enharmonic">enharmonic</a> equivalents of <b>F♭</b> (which is by definition a diatonic semitone above E<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♭</span>) and <b>D<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:DoubleSharp.svg" title="double sharp"><img alt="double sharp" data-file-height="7" data-file-width="6" height="8" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/DoubleSharp.svg/7px-DoubleSharp.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/DoubleSharp.svg/10px-DoubleSharp.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/DoubleSharp.svg/14px-DoubleSharp.svg.png 2x" width="7"/></a></span></b>, amongst others.</p>
<p>When calculated in <a href="/wiki/Equal_temperament" title="Equal temperament">equal temperament</a> with a reference of A above middle C as 440 <a href="/wiki/Hertz" title="Hertz">Hz</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Frequency" title="Frequency">frequency</a> of <b>Middle E</b> (E<sub>4</sub>) is approximately 329.628 Hz. See <a href="/wiki/Pitch_(music)" title="Pitch (music)">pitch (music)</a> for a discussion of historical variations in frequency.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="F_flat">F flat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E_(musical_note)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: F flat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>F<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♭</span></b> is a common <a href="/wiki/Enharmonic" title="Enharmonic">enharmonic</a> equivalent of E, but is not regarded as the same note. F<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♭</span> is commonly found after E<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♭</span> in the same measure in pieces where E<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♭</span> is in the <a href="/wiki/Key_signature" title="Key signature">key signature</a>, in order to represent a diatonic, rather than a chromatic semitone; writing an E<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♭</span> with a following E<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;">♮</span> is regarded as a chromatic alteration of one scale degree.</p>
