<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ma Jianzhong</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">family name</a> is <a href="/wiki/Ma_(surname)" title="Ma (surname)">Ma</a>.</div>
<p><b>Ma Jianzhong</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">馬建忠</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Mǎ Jiànzhōng</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade–Giles">Wade–Giles</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile" xml:lang="zh-Latn-wadegile">Ma Chien-chung</span></i>; 1845–1900), <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_name" title="Courtesy name">courtesy name</a> <b>Meishu</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">眉叔</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Méishū</span></i>), also known as <b>Ma Kié-Tchong</b> in French, was a Chinese official and scholar in the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a>.</p>
<p>Ma was born in <a href="/wiki/Dantu_District" title="Dantu District">Dantu</a> (丹徒), <a href="/wiki/Jiangsu" title="Jiangsu">Jiangsu</a> province to a prominent Chinese Catholic family. After studies at a French Catholic school in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, Ma went to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in 1876 to study international law. He became the first Chinese to obtain a <a href="/wiki/Baccalaur%C3%A9at" title="Baccalauréat">baccalauréat</a> and in 1879 he obtained a diploma in law (<i>licence de droit</i>) from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%89cole_Libre_des_Sciences_Politiques" title="École Libre des Sciences Politiques">École Libre des Sciences Politiques</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.</p>
<p>Following his return to China in 1880, Ma became a member of <a href="/wiki/Li_Hongzhang" title="Li Hongzhang">Li Hongzhang's</a> secretariat, where Ma's knowledge of international law became a useful asset. Among other things, Ma helped to carry out Qing policy in <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> in 1880-82 and he took part in the arrest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daewon-gun" title="Daewon-gun">Taewŏn'gun</a>. The same year, Ma reportedly suggested the design of the Korean national flag <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taeguk" title="Taeguk">Taeguk</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> which is still in use. In 1884, he also became involved in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China_Merchants%27_Steam_Navigation_Company" title="China Merchants' Steam Navigation Company">China Merchants' Steam Navigation Company</a>, where he worked closely with <a href="/wiki/Tong_King-sing" title="Tong King-sing">Tong King-sing</a>.</p>
<p>Ma is the author of <i><a href="/wiki/Mashi_Wentong" title="Mashi Wentong">Mashi Wentong</a></i> (馬氏文通 "Basic principles for writing clearly and coherently by Mister Ma"), the first textbook of <a href="/wiki/Chinese_grammar" title="Chinese grammar">Chinese grammar</a> written by a Chinese (there were already several grammars written by Westerners), published in 1898.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Today most scholars believe that Ma's older brother <a href="/wiki/Ma_Xiangbo" title="Ma Xiangbo">Ma Xiangbo</a>, a famous educator and co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Fudan_University" title="Fudan University">Fudan University</a>, also contributed to the work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
