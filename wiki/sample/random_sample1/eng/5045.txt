<p><b>Manazuru</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">真鶴町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Manazuru-machi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Ashigarashimo_District,_Kanagawa" title="Ashigarashimo District, Kanagawa">Ashigarashimo District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanagawa_Prefecture" title="Kanagawa Prefecture">Kanagawa Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of June 1 2012, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 7,926 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 1,130 persons per km². The total area was 7.02 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manazuru,_Kanagawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Manazuru is located on a small Manazuru peninsula on <a href="/wiki/Sagami_Bay" title="Sagami Bay">Sagami Bay</a> in the far southwestern corner of Kanagawa Prefecture.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manazuru,_Kanagawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As with the rest of Ashigarashimo District, the area now comprising modern Manazuru was part of <a href="/wiki/Sagami_Province" title="Sagami Province">Sagami Province</a> under control of the <a href="/wiki/Late_H%C5%8Dj%C5%8D_clan" title="Late Hōjō clan">late Hōjō clan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sengoku_period" title="Sengoku period">Sengoku period</a>, and part of <a href="/wiki/Odawara_Domain" title="Odawara Domain">Odawara Domain</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. During this time, it was known for high quality stones for construction. After the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>, Manazuru was established as a village in 1889. On October 1, 1927 Manazuru village was elevated to town status. On September 30, 1956, neighboring Iwa village was merged into Manazuru. A proposal to merge Manazuru into neighboring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yugawara,_Kanagawa" title="Yugawara, Kanagawa">Yugawara</a> was overwhelmingly rejected by a citizen's <a href="/wiki/Referendum" title="Referendum">referendum</a> in 2005.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manazuru,_Kanagawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Manazuru is based on tourism, and it also functions as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bedroom_community" title="Bedroom community">bedroom community</a> for nearby Odawara. The hilly, stony peninsula is not suited for agriculture, but cultivation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mikan" title="Mikan">mikan</a> as well as commercial fishing also contribute to the local economy.</p>
