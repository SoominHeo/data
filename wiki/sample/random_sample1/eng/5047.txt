<p><b>Manuel García-Rulfo</b> (born February 25, 1981) is a Mexican actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He is best known for roles in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Bless_Me,_Ultima_(film)" title="Bless Me, Ultima (film)">Bless Me, Ultima</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cake_(2014_film)" title="Cake (2014 film)">Cake</a></i>, as well as the television program <i><a href="/wiki/From_Dusk_till_Dawn:_The_Series" title="From Dusk till Dawn: The Series">From Dusk till Dawn: The Series</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He headlined as one of the titular characters in the 2016 remake <i><a href="/wiki/The_Magnificent_Seven_(2016_film)" title="The Magnificent Seven (2016 film)">The Magnificent Seven</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rulfo_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-rulfo-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Garcia-Rulfo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>García-Rulfo was born in <a href="/wiki/Guadalajara" title="Guadalajara">Guadalajara</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jalisco,_Mexico" title="Jalisco, Mexico">Jalisco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. He grew up on a ranch in <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">Jalisco</a>, where he learned to ride <a href="/wiki/Horse" title="Horse">horses</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rulfo_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-rulfo-3">[3]</a></sup> At some point in his life he went to <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> to study English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He went to <a href="/wiki/Universidad_del_Valle_de_Atemajac" title="Universidad del Valle de Atemajac">Universidad del Valle de Atemajac</a> where he majored in communications before he realized his passion for <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">filmmaking</a>. He studied at the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Film_Academy" title="New York Film Academy">New York Film Academy</a>, but decided to return to Mexico to continue his acting career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Garcia-Rulfo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He made his first major American film with <i><a href="/wiki/Bless_Me,_Ultima_(film)" title="Bless Me, Ultima (film)">Bless Me, Ultima</a></i>, as Uncle Pedro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2016, he played the outlaw Vasquez, one of the title characters, in <a href="/wiki/The_Magnificent_Seven_(2016_film)" title="The Magnificent Seven (2016 film)"><i>The Magnificent Seven</i></a> remake. The role required him to take up gun training, which he thought was difficult, though he stated, "I got blisters, but it was hilarious."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
