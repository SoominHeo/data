<p><b>Manuel Lanzini</b> (born 15 February 1993) is an Argentine <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Attacking_midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfielder</a> for English club <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a>. He has previously played for <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fluminense_FC" title="Fluminense FC">Fluminense</a> and international football for <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_under-20_football_team" title="Argentina national under-20 football team">Argentina U20</a>. He is described as possessing phenomenal dribbling ability, as well as fantastic acceleration and vision and the ability to ghost past defenders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He is nicknamed "the jewel" ("la joya") and holds an <a href="/wiki/Italian_passport" title="Italian passport">Italian passport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uaesign_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-uaesign-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Italy_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Italy-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Lanzini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="River_Plate">River Plate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Lanzini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: River Plate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Ituzaing%C3%B3,_Buenos_Aires" title="Ituzaingó, Buenos Aires">Ituzaingó, Buenos Aires</a>, Lanzini made his professional debut for <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_River_Plate" title="Club Atlético River Plate">River Plate</a> as a 17-year-old on August 8, 2010, in a 1–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Tigre" title="Club Atlético Tigre">Tigre</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> playing 45 minutes.</p>
<p>Lanzini spent the 2011–12 season on-loan with <a href="/wiki/Fluminense_FC" title="Fluminense FC">Fluminense</a> in Brazil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uaesign_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-uaesign-4">[4]</a></sup> He scored his debut goal on 1 September 2011, in a 2–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_FC" title="São Paulo FC">São Paulo</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_do_Morumbi" title="Estádio do Morumbi">Estádio do Morumbi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He went on to play 37 games in all competitions, scoring five goals and won the <a href="/wiki/2012_Campeonato_Carioca" title="2012 Campeonato Carioca">2012</a> <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Carioca" title="Campeonato Carioca">Carioca</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Soccerway_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Soccerway-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>He returned from a loan in the summer of 2012 and took the number 10 shirt. He scored his first goal for the club in a 1–2 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Club_Atl%C3%A9tico_Belgrano" title="Club Atlético Belgrano">Belgrano</a>, in the first game of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Al_Jazira">Al Jazira</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Lanzini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Al Jazira">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014 Lanzini became the youngest foreign player, at 21, to play in the <a href="/wiki/UAE_Arabian_Gulf_League" title="UAE Arabian Gulf League">UAE Arabian Gulf League</a> when he signed a four-year deal for <a href="/wiki/Al_Jazira_Club" title="Al Jazira Club">Al Jazira Club</a>. He had been courted by clubs in England, Spain, Italy and Turkey, and was signed as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a>-bound <a href="/wiki/Abdelaziz_Barrada" title="Abdelaziz Barrada">Abdelaziz Barrada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uaesign_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-uaesign-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="West_Ham_United">West Ham United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Lanzini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: West Ham United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 July 2015, Lanzini signed for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> on a season-long loan, with an option to make the move permanent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He made his debut on 6 August in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> third qualifying round second leg away to <a href="/wiki/FC_Astra_Giurgiu" title="FC Astra Giurgiu">FC Astra Giurgiu</a>, starting the match and scoring in the third minute, but the team lost 2–1 (4–3 on aggregate) and were eliminated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> His league debut came on 15 August as a 76th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Cheikhou_Kouyat%C3%A9" title="Cheikhou Kouyaté">Cheikhou Kouyaté</a> in a 1–2 home defeat to <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 29 August, he scored his first league goal within three minutes of kick-off away to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>, and assisted a <a href="/wiki/Mark_Noble" title="Mark Noble">Mark Noble</a> goal after exploiting poor defending by <a href="/wiki/Dejan_Lovren" title="Dejan Lovren">Dejan Lovren</a>, as the Hammers won 3–0 for their first victory at <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a> since 1963.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In March 2016, a permanent deal to sign Lanzini with effect from 1 July 2016 was announced by West Ham.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Lanzini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 2016, Lanzini was named in <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>'s provisional 35-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Olympics</a> in Rio de Janeiro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He withdrew from the squad in July 2016 following a knee injury sustained while training in Miami, Florida. He returned to London for treatment and training with West Ham.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Lanzini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lanzini has an older brother, <a href="/wiki/Tom%C3%A1s_Lanzini" title="Tomás Lanzini">Tomás Lanzini</a>, who plays for Chilean club <a href="/wiki/%C3%91ublense" title="Ñublense">Ñublense</a>.</p>
<p>He is also of <a href="/wiki/Italian_Argentines" title="Italian Argentines">Italian</a> origin and holds an Italian passport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
