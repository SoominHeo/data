<p><b>Manuel 'Manolo' Sanchís Hontiyuelo</b> (born 23 May 1965) is a Spanish retired <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Sweeper_.28libero.29" title="Defender (association football)">sweeper</a>.</p>
<p>A part of the famous <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quinta_del_Buitre" title="Quinta del Buitre">Quinta del Buitre</a></i> which stemmed from the <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> youth ranks, he was the only of its five members to play his <a href="/wiki/List_of_one-club_men" title="List of one-club men">entire career</a> with the club. His father, <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Sanch%C3%ADs_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Manuel Sanchís Martínez">Manuel Sanchís Martínez</a>, also played for Real Madrid, and both were <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spanish internationals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FANS_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FANS-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sanchís appeared in more than 700 official games for his only club, and represented the national team in one <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> and one <a href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Championship" title="UEFA European Championship">European Championship</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Sanch%C3%ADs_Hontiyuelo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A product of <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>'s prolific youth system, <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>-born Sanchís made his debut with the first team on 4 December 1983, scoring the game's only goal at <a href="/wiki/Real_Murcia" title="Real Murcia">Real Murcia</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FANS_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FANS-1">[1]</a></sup> and finishing <a href="/wiki/1983%E2%80%9384_La_Liga" title="1983–84 La Liga">his debut season</a> with a further 17 appearances (and two goals) for the main squad.</p>
<p>During the following 15 years, Sanchís only appeared in less than 30 matches in two, and was instrumental in the <i>Merengues'</i> several conquests, including six <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> championships, two <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> and two <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cups</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_La_Liga" title="1999–2000 La Liga">1999–2000 campaign</a> he featured sparingly, but still helped to the team's <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_UEFA_Champions_League" title="1999–2000 UEFA Champions League">eighth European Cup</a>, playing 11 minutes in <a href="/wiki/2000_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2000 UEFA Champions League Final">the final</a> against fellow Spanish side <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a> in a 3–0 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FANS_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FANS-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sanchís retired in 2001 at the age of 36, having appeared in 710 overall games for his only club – 523 of those in the league – while also <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captaining</a> it during 13 years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Sanch%C3%ADs_Hontiyuelo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After excelling with the <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">Spanish under-21s</a>, with which he won the <a href="/wiki/1986_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="1986 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">1986 UEFA European Championship</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Sanchís made his <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">senior</a> debut on 12 November of that year in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1988" title="UEFA Euro 1988">UEFA Euro 1988</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1988_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 1988 qualifying">qualifier</a> against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a> (1–0 win). He went on to gain 48 full <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a>, appearing at both Euro 1988 and the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>Sanchís' last match was a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly</a> with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States</a>, on 11 March 1992.</p>
