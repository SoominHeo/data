<p><b>Manuel Jesús Plaza Reyes</b> (March 17, 1900 – February 9, 1969) was a long-distance runner from <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>. He competed in the <a href="/wiki/Marathon" title="Marathon">marathon</a> at the 1924 and 1928 Olympics and won a silver medal in 1928, becoming the first Olympic medalist from Chile. He placed sixth in 1924,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-sr-1">[1]</a></sup> and served as the flag bearer for Chile at both Olympics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sr2-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
