<p><b>Manuel Carlos Valls Galfetti</b> (<small>French: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[ma.nɥɛl vals]</a></span>, <small>Catalan: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Catalan" title="Help:IPA for Catalan">[mənuˈɛl ˈβaʎs]</a></span>, <small>Spanish: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[maˈnwel ˈβals]</a></span>; born 13 August 1962) is a French <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a> who was the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_France" title="Prime Minister of France">Prime Minister of France</a> from 2014 until 2016. He was previously <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_the_Interior_(France)" title="Minister of the Interior (France)">Minister of the Interior</a> from 2012 to 2014. He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_(France)" title="Socialist Party (France)">Socialist Party</a>, and a candidate in <a href="/wiki/French_Socialist_Party_presidential_primary,_2017" title="French Socialist Party presidential primary, 2017">their primary</a> for the <a href="/wiki/French_presidential_election,_2017" title="French presidential election, 2017">2017 presidential election</a>.</p>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> father and a <a href="/wiki/Swiss_people" title="Swiss people">Swiss</a> mother, Valls was <a href="/wiki/Mayor_(France)" title="Mayor (France)">Mayor</a> of <a href="/wiki/%C3%89vry,_Essonne" title="Évry, Essonne">Évry</a> from 2001 to 2012 and was first elected to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_France" title="National Assembly of France">National Assembly of France</a> in 2002. He is regarded as belonging to the Socialist Party's <a href="/wiki/Social_liberalism" title="Social liberalism">social liberal</a> wing, sharing common orientations with <a href="/wiki/Blairism" title="Blairism">Blairism</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_family">Early life and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Valls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Valls' paternal grandfather was the editor-in-chief of a <a href="/wiki/Second_Spanish_Republic" title="Second Spanish Republic">Republican</a> newspaper in Spain. During the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a>, he sheltered priests who were fleeing from the <a href="/wiki/Red_Terror_(Spain)" title="Red Terror (Spain)">Red Terror</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LePoint_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-LePoint-1">[1]</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Francisco Franco</a>'s victory, he was forced out of his job as editor. Valls' father was the Barcelona-born painter <a href="/wiki/Xavier_Valls" title="Xavier Valls">Xavier Valls</a> (1923–2006).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the late 1940s, Xavier Valls moved to Paris and met his future wife, Luisangela Galfetti, a <a href="/wiki/Ticino" title="Ticino">Ticino</a>-born <a href="/wiki/Swiss_people" title="Swiss people">Swiss</a> citizen, the sister of architect <a href="/wiki/Aurelio_Galfetti" title="Aurelio Galfetti">Aurelio Galfetti</a>. In 1955, he won the prize for best still life in the third Spanish-American Art Biennial inaugurated by Franco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Valls was born in Barcelona while his parents were there on holiday but grew up in France.</p>

<p>In 1980, aged 17, Valls joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_Socialist_Party" title="French Socialist Party">French Socialist Party</a> (PS) to support <a href="/wiki/Michel_Rocard" title="Michel Rocard">Michel Rocard</a>. Within the PS, he defended the 'Second left' (La Deuxième gauche), rather than the more pragmatic left of François Mitterrand. (The Second left could be compared to the 1960s 'New Left' – opposed to party lines and bureaucracy, anti-statist, supportive of anti-colonialist and anti-imperialist movements worldwide, favoring direct action politics.) While studying history at the <a href="/wiki/Pantheon-Sorbonne_University" title="Pantheon-Sorbonne University">Pantheon-Sorbonne University</a>, Tolbiac campus, he was a member of the UNEF-ID, a progressive students' union.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1980, he met two other student supporters of Rocard with whom he became close friends: <a href="/wiki/Alain_Bauer" title="Alain Bauer">Alain Bauer</a> (Bauer is the godfather of Valls' second son), and Stéphane Fouks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1983-86, Valls was a parliamentary attaché for the member for Ardèche, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Chapuis" title="Robert Chapuis">Robert Chapuis</a>. In 1986 he was elected to the regional Council for the Île-de-France and served until 1992. In 1988, he became head of the Socialist Party in Argenteuil-Bezons and deputy mayor. From 1988-91 he was responsible for the functioning of the prime minister's cabinet. From 1991-93 he was an inter-ministerial delegate to the <a href="/wiki/1992_Winter_Olympics" title="1992 Winter Olympics">1992 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Albertville" title="Albertville">Albertville</a>. In 1995, he became the Secretary of Communications for the national Socialist Party and in 1997 communications and media relations chief for the prime minister's Cabinet. In 1998 he was elected vice president of the regional council for Île-de-France, a post which he held until 2002. While vice president of the regional Council, he was also elected <a href="/wiki/Mayor_(France)" title="Mayor (France)">mayor</a> of Évry in 2001, a post he held until 2012. In 2002, he became the deputy for the First Electoral District in Essonne and in 2008, the president of the tri-city jurisdiction of Évry-Centre-Essonne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 2008 elections to choose the head of the Socialist Party, Valls supported the former presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9gol%C3%A8ne_Royal" title="Ségolène Royal">Ségolène Royal</a> over her former partner <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Hollande" title="François Hollande">François Hollande</a>; Hollande eventually won.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On 13 June 2009, Valls announced his intention to run in the <a href="/wiki/French_Socialist_Party_presidential_primary,_2011" title="French Socialist Party presidential primary, 2011">Socialist presidential primary</a> in 2011 for the 2012 election. On 30 June 2009 he founded a political organization with the slogan "The Left Needs Optimism," to provide legal and financial support the Socialist Primary candidates.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On 7 June 2011, he confirmed his candidacy for the Socialist primary. On the evening of the first primary round, 9 October 2011, Valls achieved only 6% of the vote, just behind <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9gol%C3%A8ne_Royal" title="Ségolène Royal">Ségolène Royal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He was therefore eliminated. On the night of his defeat, he endorsed <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Hollande" title="François Hollande">François Hollande</a> for the second round. Valls was appointed <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_the_Interior_(France)" title="Minister of the Interior (France)">Minister of the Interior</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ayrault_Cabinet" title="Ayrault Cabinet">Ayrault Cabinet</a> in May 2012.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prime_Minister_and_critics">Prime Minister and critics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Valls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Prime Minister and critics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dmitry_Medvedev_said_at_the_Munich_Conference_2016_02.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="540" data-file-width="960" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Dmitry_Medvedev_said_at_the_Munich_Conference_2016_02.png/220px-Dmitry_Medvedev_said_at_the_Munich_Conference_2016_02.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Dmitry_Medvedev_said_at_the_Munich_Conference_2016_02.png/330px-Dmitry_Medvedev_said_at_the_Munich_Conference_2016_02.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Dmitry_Medvedev_said_at_the_Munich_Conference_2016_02.png/440px-Dmitry_Medvedev_said_at_the_Munich_Conference_2016_02.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>In March 2014, following major losses to centre-right and extreme-right political parties in French municipal elections, President <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Hollande" title="François Hollande">François Hollande</a> appointed Valls to the post of Prime Minister. He replaced <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marc_Ayrault" title="Jean-Marc Ayrault">Jean-Marc Ayrault</a> who had resigned earlier that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valls_Cabinet" title="Valls Cabinet">Valls Cabinet</a> was formed on 2 April 2014, consisting of 16 ministers from the Socialist Party and one minister from the <a href="/wiki/Radical_Party_of_the_Left" title="Radical Party of the Left">Radical Party of the Left</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/2016_Nice_attack" title="2016 Nice attack">2016 Nice attack</a>, he was criticised for saying that "France will have to live with terrorism."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ft1-12">[12]</a></sup> French citizens booed him when he joined the memorial for the victims, yelling "murderer" and "resign" at him before the minute of silence for the dead began.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Valls left office on 6 December 2016 to run in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_(France)_presidential_primary,_2017" title="Socialist Party (France) presidential primary, 2017">primaries to be the Socialist candidate</a> in the <a href="/wiki/French_presidential_election,_2017" title="French presidential election, 2017">2017 presidential election</a>. He was replaced by Minister of the Interior <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Cazeneuve" title="Bernard Cazeneuve">Bernard Cazeneuve</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He came in second during the first round of the primary on 22 January, behind his ex-<a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Education_(France)" title="Ministry of National Education (France)">Minister of National Education</a> <a href="/wiki/Beno%C3%AEt_Hamon" title="Benoît Hamon">Benoît Hamon</a>. The two candidates advanced to the second round, scheduled for 29 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_beliefs">Political beliefs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Valls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Political beliefs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Valls is on the "right wing" of the Socialist Party, with a similar approach to the German and Dutch Social Democratic Parties. During the 2011 presidential primary, he defined himself as "<a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">Blairiste</a>" or "<a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Clintonien</a>", and described his position as "in the tradition of <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Mend%C3%A8s_France" title="Pierre Mendès France">Pierre Mendès France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Jospin" title="Lionel Jospin">Lionel Jospin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michel_Rocard" title="Michel Rocard">Michel Rocard</a>". As prime minister he openly said that he liked the comparison with the new Italian premier, <a href="/wiki/Matteo_Renzi" title="Matteo Renzi">Matteo Renzi</a>, another <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_way" title="Third way">thirdway-er</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-europe2_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-europe2-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Valls advocates an "economically realistic" political speech without "demagoguery". He voices his dissent in the party by his vision of <a href="/wiki/Moral_responsibility" title="Moral responsibility">individual responsibilities</a> ("The new hope that the Left must carry is individual self-realization: to allow everyone to become that which they are"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>) and his positions against a system where some people live only from national solidarity. Describing himself as "reformist rather than revolutionary," he wants to "reconcile the left to the liberal approach".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-europe2_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-europe2-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Immigration">Immigration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Valls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Immigration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In his book <i>To Put the Old Socialism to Rest ... And Finally be Left-Wing</i>, he declared support for immigration "quotas".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On Sunday 9 June 2009, while visiting a market in Évry, of which he was then mayor, he was caught on camera suggesting that the presence of more white people would give a better image of the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2013, his stance in the <a href="/wiki/Dibrani_case" title="Dibrani case">Dibrani case</a> met with high public approval, with a global approval rate of 74% (57% approval rate from the left, and 89% from the right).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement_age">Retirement age</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Valls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Retirement age">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Valls supported the extension of the years of required pension-contribution to 41, as advocated and achieved by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarkozy" title="Sarkozy">Sarkozy</a> administration. The extension means that due to the maximum mandatory <a href="/wiki/French_special_retirement_plan" title="French special retirement plan">retirement age</a> of 62, only immigrants receiving the right to legally work around the age of 21 would be allowed to receive the pension to which they would have contributed throughout their careers. "The role of the Left is not to deny democratic changes, nor to hide the size of deficits ... The Left can advocate an à la carte pension system and increasing the pay-in period."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Views_on_religion">Views on religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Valls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Views on religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2002, as <a href="/wiki/Mayor_(France)" title="Mayor (France)">mayor</a> of Évry, he opposed a branch of the national grocery store chain <a href="/wiki/Franprix" title="Franprix">Franprix</a>, located in a predominately Muslim neighborhood, deciding to sell only <a href="/wiki/Halal" title="Halal">halal</a>-certified meat/products and products that do not contain alcohol.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>As parliamentarian and interior minister, he took strong stances on secularism, supported crackdowns on the wearing of <a href="/wiki/Niq%C4%81b" title="Niqāb">niqābs</a> in public and defended a nursery which sacked an employee for demanding to wear one at work. He had harsh words for anti-gay marriage protesters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> When Catholics protested against "<a href="/wiki/Golgota_Picnic" title="Golgota Picnic">Golgota Picnic</a>", he supported the theatre director in the name of <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_speech" title="Freedom of speech">freedom of speech</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>When <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dieudonn%C3%A9_M%27Bala_M%27Bala" title="Dieudonné M'Bala M'Bala">Dieudonné</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Quenelle_(gesture)" title="Quenelle (gesture)">quenelle gesture</a> became viral in 2013, Valls said he would consider "all legal means" to ban Dieudonné's "public meetings", given that he "addresses in an obvious and insufferable manner the memory of victims of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holocaust" title="Holocaust">Holocaust</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cannabis">Cannabis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Valls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cannabis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 12 October 2009, Valls expressed "total disagreement" with a proposal by <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Vaillant" title="Daniel Vaillant">Daniel Vaillant</a> for decriminalization or legalization of cannabis. The plan involved depriving traffickers of a source of income. Valls argued, "The question of drugs that produce considerable damage in some neighbourhoods and nourish the underground economy, cannot be handled this way. There is a certain number of rules that cannot be removed."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Terrorism">Terrorism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Valls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Terrorism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Valls said after the <a href="/wiki/November_2015_Paris_attacks" title="November 2015 Paris attacks">2015 Paris attacks</a> that French society needed a "general mobilisation" against the appeal of "deadly" doctrines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/2016_Nice_attack" title="2016 Nice attack">2016 Nice attack</a> Valls said "Times have changed, and France is going to have to live with terrorism, and we must face this together and show our collective <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sang-froid" title="Sang-froid">sang-froid</a>. France is a great country and a great democracy and we will not allow ourselves to be destabilized."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft1_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-ft1-12">[12]</a></sup> The comments on the Nice attack provoked criticism in France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Valls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1987, Valls married Nathalie Soulié, with whom he had 4 children before divorcing. On 1 July 2010, he married<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Anne_Gravoin" title="Anne Gravoin">Anne Gravoin</a>, a violinist and winner of the <a href="/wiki/Conservatoire_de_Paris" title="Conservatoire de Paris">Conservatoire de Paris</a>' prestigious Premier Prix for Violin and Chamber Orchestra.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Owing to his family background, Manuel Valls is fluent in French, Spanish, <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a> and Italian,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and is distantly related to the <a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marquesado_del_Bosch_de_Ar%C3%A9s" title="es:Marquesado del Bosch de Arés">Marquesses del Bosch de Arés</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
