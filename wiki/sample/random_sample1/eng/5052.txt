<p><b>Manuel Friedrich</b> (born 13 September 1979) is a German retired <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>.</p>
<p>He amassed <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> totals of 258 games and 16 goals over the course of 12 seasons, representing in the competition <a href="/wiki/SV_Werder_Bremen" title="SV Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a>, <a href="/wiki/1._FSV_Mainz_05" title="1. FSV Mainz 05">Mainz 05</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Friedrich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mainz_.2F_Werder_Bremen">Mainz / Werder Bremen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Friedrich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mainz / Werder Bremen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Bad_Kreuznach" title="Bad Kreuznach">Bad Kreuznach</a>, Friedrich started playing football at SG Guldental 07, making his professional debuts with <a href="/wiki/1._FSV_Mainz_05" title="1. FSV Mainz 05">1. FSV Mainz 05</a> which he had joined in 1995 – the club was then in the <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">second division</a>. However, he had to wait until 26 February 2000 to make his competition debut, in a home match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Energie_Cottbus" title="FC Energie Cottbus">FC Energie Cottbus</a>.</p>
<p>In the following two seasons, Friedrich was a defensive stalwart, scoring an astonishing eight goals – no <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalties</a> – in <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_2._Bundesliga" title="2001–02 2. Bundesliga">the latter</a> as Mainz finished fourth, just one point shy of promotion.</p>
<p>Those stellar performances attracted attention from the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>, and Friedrich signed with <a href="/wiki/SV_Werder_Bremen" title="SV Werder Bremen">SV Werder Bremen</a>, but his time there would be anything but successful as he only appeared once in the league in two years combined, the final seven minutes of the 4–1 home triumph against <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">FC Schalke 04</a> on 23 August 2003. In that second season he also featured for <a href="/wiki/SV_Werder_Bremen_II" title="SV Werder Bremen II">the reserve squad</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Regionalliga_Nord" title="Regionalliga Nord">Regionalliga Nord</a>; <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Bundesliga" title="2002–03 Bundesliga">2002–03</a> had already been totally lost to an <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament" title="Anterior cruciate ligament">anterior cruciate ligament</a> injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late January 2004, Friedrich returned to Mainz, still contributing with 17 matches <i>en route</i> to <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_2._Bundesliga" title="2003–04 2. Bundesliga">top level promotion</a>. On 23 May he scored one of four goals in the league, netting the second in the decisive 3–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/SV_Eintracht_Trier_05" title="SV Eintracht Trier 05">SV Eintracht Trier 05</a> on the final day of the campaign (promotion attained on <a href="/wiki/Goal_difference" title="Goal difference">goal difference</a>). In the following three seasons combined he rarely missed a game, adding four goals, but Mainz would be relegated in <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Bundesliga" title="2006–07 Bundesliga">2007</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bayer_Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Friedrich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bayer Leverkusen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2007, Friedrich moved to <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer 04 Leverkusen</a>, continuing to be an everpresent defensive unit and contributing – early in <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Bundesliga" title="2007–08 Bundesliga">his first year</a> – to consecutive home wins against <a href="/wiki/Karlsruher_SC" title="Karlsruher SC">Karlsruher SC</a> (3–0) and <a href="/wiki/VfL_Bochum" title="VfL Bochum">VfL Bochum</a> (2–0).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He scored ten league goals in his first three seasons combined.</p>
<p>Friedrich could only appear in 19 games in <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Bundesliga" title="2010–11 Bundesliga">2010–11</a> due to injury, with Leverkusen finishing in second position and <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League">qualifying</a> to the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>. In that competition, on 23 November 2011, he headed home in the <a href="/wiki/Last-minute_goal" title="Last-minute goal">last-minute</a> to help the hosts come from behind against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> to win it 2–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Borussia_Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Friedrich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Borussia Dortmund">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After an unsuccessful trial with <a href="/wiki/Bangkok_Glass_F.C." title="Bangkok Glass F.C.">Bangkok Glass F.C.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Friedrich joined <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Free_transfer_(association_football)" title="Free transfer (association football)">free transfer</a> on 20 November 2013, following injuries to fellow stoppers <a href="/wiki/Mats_Hummels" title="Mats Hummels">Mats Hummels</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neven_Suboti%C4%87" title="Neven Subotić">Neven Subotić</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He made his debut for his new team three days later, playing the full 90 minutes in a 0–3 home loss to <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">FC Bayern Munich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Friedrich scored his first goal for BVB on 8 February 2014, netting his team's third in a 5–1 away demolition of former club Werder. It was his 16th goal in the German top division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mumbai_City">Mumbai City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Friedrich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mumbai City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 August 2014, Friedrich signed with <a href="/wiki/Mumbai_City_FC" title="Mumbai City FC">Mumbai City FC</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Super_League" title="Indian Super League">Indian Super League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Friedrich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 2006, Friedrich was called up to <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>'s squad for a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly</a> against the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States</a>, thus becoming the first Mainz player to ever represent the national side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> However, he did not receive any playing time in the match, and was not part of the 23-man squad at the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> either.</p>
<p>Friedrich made his full debut on 16 August 2006, playing the entire second half of another friendly, the 3–0 with <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gelsenkirchen" title="Gelsenkirchen">Gelsenkirchen</a>. He subsequently featured the full 90 minutes in the German team's opening two <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying">Euro 2008 qualifiers</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_national_football_team" title="San Marino national football team">San Marino</a>, scoring his first international goal in the latter, the 12th in a 13–0 victory over the group underdogs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
