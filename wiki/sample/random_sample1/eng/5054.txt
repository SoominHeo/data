<p><b>Manishtushu</b> (or <b>Maništušu</b>) was a king of the <a href="/wiki/Akkadian_Empire" title="Akkadian Empire">Akkadian Empire</a> from 2270 to 2255 BC (<a href="/wiki/Middle_Chronology" title="Middle Chronology">Middle Chronology</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manishtushu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Manishtushu was the third king of the <a href="/wiki/Akkadian_Empire" title="Akkadian Empire">Akkadian Empire</a>. He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Sargon_of_Akkad" title="Sargon of Akkad">Sargon of Akkad</a> and Queen <a href="/wiki/Tashlultum" title="Tashlultum">Tashlultum</a>, brother of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/En-hedu-ana" title="En-hedu-ana">En-hedu-ana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rimush" title="Rimush">Rimush</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Shu-Enlil" title="Shu-Enlil">Shu-Enlil</a>, and the father of <a href="/wiki/Naram-Sin_of_Akkad" title="Naram-Sin of Akkad">Naram-Sin</a>. He became king in c. 2270 BC after the death of his brother <a href="/wiki/Rimush" title="Rimush">Rimush</a>. Manishtushu, freed of the rebellions of his brother's reign, led campaigns to distant lands. According to a passage from one of his inscriptions, he led a fleet down the Persian Gulf where 32 kings allied to fight him. Manishtushu was victorious and consequently looted their cities and silver mines, along with other expeditions to kingdoms along the Persian Gulf.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He also sailed a fleet down the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tigris_River" title="Tigris River">Tigris River</a> that eventually traded with 37 other nations, conquered the city of Shirasum in <a href="/wiki/Elam" title="Elam">Elam</a>,and rebuilt the destroyed temple of <a href="/wiki/Inanna" title="Inanna">Inanna</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nineveh" title="Nineveh">Nineveh</a> in c. 2260 BC. In c. 2255 BC he died, assassinated by members of his own court, and was succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Naram-Sin_of_Akkad" title="Naram-Sin of Akkad">Naram-Sin</a>. A pyramidal stele erected by Manishtushu bearing a long cuneiform inscription in Akkadian is featured in the <a href="/wiki/Louvre" title="Louvre">Louvre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
