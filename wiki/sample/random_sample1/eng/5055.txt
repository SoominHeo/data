<p>The <b>Battle of Manila Bay</b> took place on 1 May 1898, during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish–American War">Spanish–American War</a>. The American <a href="/wiki/Asiatic_Squadron" title="Asiatic Squadron">Asiatic Squadron</a> under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commodore_(USN)" title="Commodore (USN)">Commodore</a> <a href="/wiki/George_Dewey" title="George Dewey">George Dewey</a> engaged and destroyed the Spanish Pacific Squadron under <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">Admiral</a> <a href="/wiki/Patricio_Montojo" title="Patricio Montojo">Patricio Montojo</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Battle" title="Battle">battle</a> took place in <a href="/wiki/Manila_Bay" title="Manila Bay">Manila Bay</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, and was the first major engagement of the Spanish–American War. The battle was one of the most decisive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naval_battles" title="Naval battles">naval battles</a> in history and marked the end of the Spanish colonial period in Philippine history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SeaHistory_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-SeaHistory-8">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prelude">Prelude</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Manila_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Prelude">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Americans living on the <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="West Coast of the United States">West Coast of the United States</a> feared a Spanish attack at the outbreak of the Spanish–American War. Only a few U.S. Navy warships, led by the <a href="/wiki/USS_Olympia_(C-6)" title="USS Olympia (C-6)">USS <i>Olympia</i></a>, stood between them and a powerful Spanish fleet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SeaHistory_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-SeaHistory-8">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Admiral Montojo, a career <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_people" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Navy_(Armada_Espa%C3%B1ola)" title="Spanish Navy (Armada Española)">naval</a> officer who had been dispatched rapidly to the Philippines, was equipped with a variety of obsolete vessels. Efforts to strengthen his position amounted to little. The strategy adopted by the Spanish bureaucracy suggested they could not win a war and saw resistance as little more than a face-saving exercise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nofi_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nofi-9">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:59</sup> Administration actions worked against the effort, sending explosives meant for <a href="/wiki/Naval_mine" title="Naval mine">naval mines</a> to civilian construction companies while the Spanish fleet in <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a> was seriously undermanned by inexperienced sailors who had not received any training for over a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Koenig_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Koenig-10">[9]</a></sup> Reinforcements promised from Madrid resulted in only two poorly-armored scout <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruisers</a> being sent while at the same time the authorities transferred a squadron from the Manila fleet under Admiral <a href="/wiki/Pascual_Cervera_y_Topete" title="Pascual Cervera y Topete">Pascual Cervera</a> to reinforce the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>. Admiral Montojo had originally wanted to confront the Americans at <a href="/wiki/Subic_Bay" title="Subic Bay">Subic Bay</a> but abandoned that idea when he learned the planned mines and coastal defensives were lacking and the <i>Castilla</i> started to leak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nofi_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nofi-9">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:69</sup> Montojo compounded his difficulties by placing his ships outside the range of Spanish <a href="/wiki/Coastal_artillery" title="Coastal artillery">coastal artillery</a> (which might have evened the odds) and choosing a relatively shallow anchorage. His intent seems to have been to spare Manila from bombardment and to allow any survivors of his fleet to swim to safety. The harbor was protected by six shore batteries and three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort" title="Fort">forts</a> whose fire during the battle proved to be ineffective. Only Fort San Antonio Abad had guns with enough range to reach the American fleet, but Dewey never came within their range during the battle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-navy_history_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-navy_history-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Koenig_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Koenig-10">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle">Battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Manila_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At 7 p.m. on 30 April, Montojo was informed that Dewey's ships had been seen in <a href="/wiki/Subic_Bay" title="Subic Bay">Subic Bay</a> that morning. As <a href="/wiki/Manila_Bay" title="Manila Bay">Manila Bay</a> was considered unnavigable at night by foreigners, Montojo expected an attack the following morning. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_F._Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Oscar F. Williams (page does not exist)">Oscar F. Williams</a>, the United States Consul in Manila, had provided Dewey with detailed information on the state of the Spanish defenses and the lack of preparedness of the Spanish fleet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williams1898_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Williams1898-11">[10]</a></sup> Based in part upon this intelligence, Dewey—embarked aboard <i><a href="/wiki/USS_Olympia_(C-6)" title="USS Olympia (C-6)">USS <i>Olympia</i></a></i>—led his squadron into Manila Bay at midnight on 1 May.</p>
<p>Passing the entrance, two Spanish mines exploded but were ineffective as they were well below the draft of any of the ships due to the depth of the water. Inside the bay, ships normally used the north channel between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corregidor_Island" title="Corregidor Island">Corregidor Island</a> and the northern coast, and this was the only channel mined. Dewey instead used the unmined south channel between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Fraile_Island" title="El Fraile Island">El Fraile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caballo_Island" title="Caballo Island">Caballo</a> Islands. The El Fraile battery fired a few rounds but the range was too great. The <a href="/wiki/USS_McCulloch_(1897)" title="USS McCulloch (1897)"><i>McCulloch</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Nanshan_(AG-3)" title="USS Nanshan (AG-3)"><i>Nanshan</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/USS_Zafiro_(1884)" title="USS Zafiro (1884)"><i>Zafiro</i></a> were then detached from the line and took no further part in the fighting. At 5:15 a.m. on 1 May, the squadron was off Manila and the <a href="/wiki/Cavite" title="Cavite">Cavite</a> battery fired ranging shots. The shore batteries and Spanish fleet then opened fire but all the shells fell short as the fleet was still out of range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Koenig_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Koenig-10">[9]</a></sup> At 5:41 with the now famous phrase, "You may fire when ready, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Vernon_Gridley" title="Charles Vernon Gridley">Gridley</a>,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup> the <i>Olympia</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s captain was instructed to begin the destruction of the Spanish <a href="/wiki/Flotilla" title="Flotilla">flotilla</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dewey_account_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-dewey_account-13">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The U.S. squadron swung in front of the Spanish ships and forts in <a href="/wiki/Line_of_battle" title="Line of battle">line ahead</a>, firing their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_(nautical)" title="Port (nautical)">port</a> guns. They then turned and passed back, firing their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starboard" title="Starboard">starboard</a> guns. This process was repeated five times, each time closing the range from 5,000 yards to 2,000 yards. The Spanish forces had been alerted, and most were ready for action, but they were heavily outgunned. Eight Spanish ships, the land batteries, and the forts returned fire for two and a half hours although the range was too great for the guns on shore. Five other small Spanish ships were not engaged.</p>
<p>Montojo accepted that his cause was hopeless and ordered his ships to ram the enemy if possible. He then slipped the <i>Cristina's</i> cables and charged. Much of the American fleet's fire was then directed at her and she was shot to pieces. Of the crew of 400, more than 200, including Montojo, were casualties and only two men remained who were able to man her guns. The ship managed to return to shore and Montojo ordered it to be scuttled. The <i>Castilla</i>, which only had guns on the port side, had her forward cable shot away causing her to swing about, presenting her weaponless starboard side. The captain then ordered her sunk and abandoned. The <i>Ulloa</i> was hit by a shell at the waterline that killed her captain and disabled half the crew. The <i>Luzon</i> had three guns out of action but was otherwise unharmed. The <i>Duero</i> lost an engine and had only one gun left able to fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Koenig_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-Koenig-10">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>At 7:45 a.m., after Captain Gridley messaged Dewey that only 15 rounds of 5" ammunition remained per gun, he ordered an immediate withdrawal. To preserve morale, he informed the crews that the halt in the battle was to allow the crews to have breakfast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dewey_account_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-dewey_account-13">[12]</a></sup> According to an observer on the <i>Olympia</i>, "At least three of his (Spanish) ships had broken into flames but so had one of ours. These fires had all been put out without apparent injury to the ships. Generally speaking, nothing of great importance had occurred to show that we had seriously injured any Spanish vessel". Montojo took the opportunity to now move his remaining ships into <a href="/wiki/Bacoor_Bay" title="Bacoor Bay">Bacoor Bay</a> where they were ordered to resist for as long as possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Koenig_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-Koenig-10">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>A captains' conference on the <i>Olympia</i> revealed little damage and no men killed. It was discovered that the original ammunition message had been garbled—instead of only 15 rounds of ammunition per gun remaining, the message had meant to say only 15 rounds of ammunition per gun had been expended. During the conference reports arrived that sounds of exploding ammunition had been heard and fires sighted on the <i>Cristina</i> and <i>Castilla</i>. At 10:40 AM action was resumed but the Spanish offered little resistance and Montojo issued orders for the remaining ships to be scuttled and the <a href="/wiki/Breechblock" title="Breechblock">breechblocks</a> of their guns taken ashore. The <i>Olympia</i>, <i>Baltimore</i> and <i>Boston</i> then fired on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sangley_Point" title="Sangley Point">Sangley Point</a> battery putting it out of action and followed up by sinking the <i>Ulloa</i>. The <i>Concord</i> fired on the transport <i>Mindanao</i>, whose crew immediately abandoned ship. The <a href="/wiki/USS_Petrel_(PG-2)" title="USS Petrel (PG-2)"><i>Petrel</i></a> fired on the government offices next to the arsenal and a white flag was raised over the building after which all firing ceased.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Koenig_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-Koenig-10">[9]</a></sup> The Spanish colors were <a href="/wiki/Striking_the_colors" title="Striking the colors">struck</a> at 12:40 PM.</p>
<p>According to American sources, Dewey won the battle<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dewey_account_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-dewey_account-13">[12]</a></sup> with seven men very slightly wounded,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup> a total of nine injured, and only a single fatality among his crew: Francis B. Randall, Chief Engineer on the <i>McCulloch</i>, from a heart attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcsherry_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-mcsherry-15">[14]</a></sup> On the other hand, the Spanish naval historian Agustín Ramón Rodríguez González suggests that Dewey suffered heavier losses, though still much lower than those of the Spanish squadron.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RodriguezG_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-RodriguezG-7">[6]</a></sup> Rodríguez notes that Spanish officials estimated the American casualties at 13 crewmen killed and more than 30 wounded based on reliable information collected by the Spanish consulate in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RodriguezG_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-RodriguezG-7">[6]</a></sup> According to Rodríguez, Dewey may have concealed the deaths and injuries by including the numbers amongst the 155 men who reportedly deserted during the campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RodriguezG_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-RodriguezG-7">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subsequent_action">Subsequent action</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Manila_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Subsequent action">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A Spanish attempt to attack Dewey with the naval task force known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camara%27s_Flying_Relief_Column" title="Camara's Flying Relief Column">Camara's Flying Relief Column</a> came to naught, and the naval war in the Philippines devolved into a series of <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_boat" title="Torpedo boat">torpedo boat</a> hit-and-run attacks for the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Military_campaign" title="Military campaign">campaign</a>. While the Spanish scored several hits, there were no American fatalities directly attributable to Spanish gunfire.</p>
<p>On 2 May, Dewey landed a force of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Marines" title="United States Marines">Marines</a> at <a href="/wiki/Cavite" title="Cavite">Cavite</a>. They completed the destruction of the Spanish fleet and batteries and established a guard for the protection of the Spanish hospitals. The resistance of the forts was weak. The <i>Olympia</i> turned a few guns on the Cavite arsenal, detonating its <a href="/wiki/Magazine_(artillery)" title="Magazine (artillery)">magazine</a>, and ending the fire from the Spanish batteries.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Manila_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In recognition of George Dewey's leadership during the Battle of Manila Bay a special medal known as the <a href="/wiki/Dewey_Medal" title="Dewey Medal">Dewey Medal</a> was presented to the officers and sailors under Admiral Dewey's command. Dewey was later honored with promotion to the special rank of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Admiral_of_the_Navy_(U.S.)" title="Admiral of the Navy (U.S.)">Admiral of the Navy</a>; a rank that no one has held before or since in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>. Building on his popularity, Dewey briefly <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_1900" title="United States presidential election, 1900">ran for president in 1900</a>, but withdrew and endorsed <a href="/wiki/William_McKinley" title="William McKinley">William McKinley</a>, the incumbent, who won.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dewey_Square" title="Dewey Square">Dewey Square</a> in Boston is named after Commodore Dewey. <a href="/wiki/Union_Square,_San_Francisco" title="Union Square, San Francisco">Union Square, San Francisco</a> features a 97 ft (30 m) tall <a href="/wiki/Monument" title="Monument">monument</a> to Admiral George Dewey's victory at the Battle of Manila Bay.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Order_of_battle">Order of battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Manila_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Order of battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Manila_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Engaged Vessels:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/USS_Olympia_(C-6)" title="USS Olympia (C-6)">USS <i>Olympia</i></a>, flagship, <a href="/wiki/Protected_cruiser" title="Protected cruiser">protected cruiser</a> of 5,870 tons. Twin 8-inch guns mounted in turrets fore and aft, ten 5-inch guns and six torpedo tubes. Top speed 20 knots. She is now a museum ship at the <a href="/wiki/Independence_Seaport_Museum" title="Independence Seaport Museum">Independence Seaport Museum</a>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/USS_Baltimore_(C-3)" title="USS Baltimore (C-3)">USS <i>Baltimore</i></a>, protected cruiser of 4,600 tons. Single 8-inch guns mounted fore and aft, two 8-inch and two 6-inch guns aiming <a href="/wiki/Coaxial" title="Coaxial">axially</a> and three 6-inch guns aiming on each broadside. Top speed 20 knots.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/USS_Raleigh_(C-8)" title="USS Raleigh (C-8)">USS <i>Raleigh</i></a>, protected cruiser of 3,200 tons. One 6-inch and two 5-inch guns aiming forward, four 5-inch guns aiming astern and two 5-inch guns aiming on each broadside. Top speed 19 knots.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/USS_Boston_(1884)" title="USS Boston (1884)">USS <i>Boston</i></a>, protected cruiser of 3,200 tons. Single 8-inch guns mounted in barbettes fore and aft with 6-inch axial firing guns mounted beside each. Four additional 6-inch guns. Top speed 13 knots.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/USS_Concord_(PG-3)" title="USS Concord (PG-3)">USS <i>Concord</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Gunboat" title="Gunboat">gunboat</a> of 1,710 tons with six 6-inch guns. Top speed 17 knots.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/USS_Petrel_(PG-2)" title="USS Petrel (PG-2)">USS <i>Petrel</i></a>, gunboat of 867 tons with four 6-inch guns. Top speed 12 knots.</li>
</ul>
<p>Despite the superiority of the American artillery, the success rate of their guns was minimal, a total of 5,859 shells were expended during the battle. Excluding shells fired at land targets and the unengaged vessels, only 145 hit the seven Spanish engaged vessels. The <i>Reina Cristina</i> and <i>Castilla</i> suffered 81 hits between them, the <i>Don Antonio de Ulloa</i> was hit 33 times, the <i>Don Juan de Austria</i> 13, the <i>Marques del Duero</i> 10, the <i>Isla de Cuba</i> five and the <i>Isla de Luzón</i> was hit three times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RodriguezG_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-RodriguezG-7">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Unengaged vessels:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Revenue_Cutter_Service" title="United States Revenue Cutter Service">Revenue Cutter</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_McCulloch_(1897)" title="USS McCulloch (1897)"><i>McCulloch</i></a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collier_(ship_type)" title="Collier (ship type)">collier</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Nanshan_(AG-3)" title="USS Nanshan (AG-3)"><i>Nanshan</i></a> and the <a href="/wiki/Steamboat" title="Steamboat">steamer</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Zafiro_(1884)" title="USS Zafiro (1884)"><i>Zafiro</i></a> (a supply vessel) were directed to keep out of the main action because of their light armament and lack of armor. The <i>McCulloch</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s chief engineer died of a heart attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcsherry_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-mcsherry-15">[14]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spain">Spain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Manila_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Spain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Engaged Vessels:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spanish_cruiser_Reina_Cristina" title="Spanish cruiser Reina Cristina"><i>Reina Cristina</i></a>, flagship, <a href="/wiki/Unprotected_cruiser" title="Unprotected cruiser">unprotected cruiser</a> of 3,042 tons, with six 6.4-inch guns. The fastest Spanish vessel with a top speed of 16 knots.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spanish_cruiser_Castilla" title="Spanish cruiser Castilla"><i>Castilla</i></a>, unprotected cruiser of 3,289 tons, with four 5.9-inch and two 4.7-inch guns. The vessel's 8-inch guns had been removed to equip the shore batteries. The ship was used as a floating battery as the temporary repair of the leaks had immobilized her propeller shaft.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spanish_cruiser_Don_Antonio_de_Ulloa" title="Spanish cruiser Don Antonio de Ulloa"><i>Don Antonio de Ulloa</i></a>, unprotected cruiser of 1,152 tons, with two 4.7-inch guns on the starboard side. Under repair with her engines ashore. Her entire port side armament had been removed to equip the shore batteries.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spanish_cruiser_Don_Juan_de_Austria" title="Spanish cruiser Don Juan de Austria"><i>Don Juan de Austria</i></a>, unprotected cruiser of 1,152 tons, with four 4.7-inch guns. Top speed 13 knots.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spanish_cruiser_Isla_de_Cuba" title="Spanish cruiser Isla de Cuba"><i>Isla de Cuba</i></a>, protected cruiser of 1,030 tons, with six 4.7-inch guns. Top speed 14 knots.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_cruiser_Isla_de_Luzon" title="Spanish cruiser Isla de Luzon"><i>Isla de Luzon</i></a>, protected cruiser of 1,030 tons, with six 4.7-inch guns. Top speed 14 knots.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spanish_gunboat_Marques_del_Duero" title="Spanish gunboat Marques del Duero"><i>Marques del Duero</i></a>, gunboat of 492 tons, with one 6.4-inch and two 4.7-inch guns. Top speed 10 knots.</li>
</ul>
<p>Engaged vessels ranged in size from 5,870 tons (<i>Olympia</i>) to 492 tons (<i>Marques del Duero</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ships_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ships-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Unengaged Vessels:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><i>Mindanao</i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transport_ship" title="Transport ship">transport ship</a> of 1,900 tons, with 2 secondary rapid fire guns. 77 men.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spanish_cruiser_Velasco" title="Spanish cruiser Velasco"><i>Velasco</i></a>, unprotected cruiser of 1152 tons. Her boilers were ashore being repaired. All her guns were apparently removed to the Caballo Island Battery. 145 men.</li>
<li><i>El Coreo</i>, gunboat of 560 tons, with three 4.7-inch guns, three secondary rapid-fire guns, and 1 torpedo tube. 115 men.</li>
<li><i>General Lezo</i>, gunboat of 520 tons, with two 4.7-inch guns which were apparently removed to El Fraile Island, 2 secondary rapid-fire guns, and 1 torpedo tube. 115 men.</li>
<li><i>Argos</i>, gunboat of 508 tons, with one 3.5-inch gun. 87 men.</li>
</ul>
<p>The Spanish vessels had 19 torpedo tubes between them but no serviceable torpedoes.</p>
<p><b>Shore Defenses</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Fort San Antonio Abad: Built 1584. Located in <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a>. Various guns with only the 9.4-inch having enough range to reach Dewey's ships at their closest approach.</li>
<li>Fort San Felipe: Built 1609. A small castle built on a <a href="/wiki/Shoal" title="Shoal">sandbar</a> protected by a <a href="/wiki/Breakwater_(structure)" title="Breakwater (structure)">breakwater</a> and separated from <a href="/wiki/Cavite_City" title="Cavite City">Cavite City</a> by a <a href="/wiki/Moat" title="Moat">moat</a>.</li>
<li>Cavite Fort: Fortified naval base and shipyard in Cavite City located adjacent to Fort San Felipe.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Corregidor" title="Corregidor">Corregidor</a> battery: Entrance to <a href="/wiki/Manila_Bay" title="Manila Bay">Manila Bay</a>. Did not fire.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Caballo_Island" title="Caballo Island">Caballo</a> battery: Entrance to Manila Bay. Did not fire.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Fraile_Island" title="El Fraile Island">El Fraile</a> battery: Entrance to Manila Bay. Fired three rounds before <i>Raleigh</i> silenced it after hitting the battery with a single shell.</li>
<li>Cañacao battery: Located in the town of <a href="/wiki/Ca%C3%B1acao_Bay" title="Cañacao Bay">Cañacao</a>. Armed with a single 4.7-inch gun. Did not fire.</li>
<li>Sangley Point battery: Located at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Naval_Station_Sangley_Point" title="U.S. Naval Station Sangley Point">Sangley Point Naval Base</a>. Armed with three 64-lb <a href="/wiki/Muzzleloader" title="Muzzleloader">muzzleloading cannon</a> and two 5.9-inch guns (which were the only ones to fire.)</li>
<li>Malate battery: Located in the Manila district of <a href="/wiki/Malate,_Manila" title="Malate, Manila">Malate</a>. Did not fire.</li>
</ul>
<p>The batteries were supplemented with the guns removed from Montojo's fleet. The Corregidor, Caballo and El Fraile batteries had a combined total of 17 guns.</p>
