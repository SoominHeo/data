<p>On 9 November 2016, a <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">tram</a> operated by <a href="/wiki/Tramlink" title="Tramlink">Tramlink</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Light_rail" title="Light rail">light rail</a> <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">tram</a> system serving <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Croydon" title="London Borough of Croydon">Croydon</a> and surrounding areas in <a href="/wiki/South_London" title="South London">South London</a>, England, <a href="/wiki/Derailment" title="Derailment">derailed</a> and overturned on a sharp bend approaching a junction. There were seven fatalities with 58 other people injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITV091116_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ITV091116-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-G101116_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-G101116-2">[2]</a></sup> It was the first tram incident in the United Kingdom in which passengers were killed since 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITV091116_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ITV091116-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The tram was running from <a href="/wiki/New_Addington_tram_stop" title="New Addington tram stop">New Addington</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wimbledon_railway_station" title="Wimbledon railway station">Wimbledon</a> via Croydon, and was on the approach to <a href="/wiki/Sandilands_tram_stop" title="Sandilands tram stop">Sandilands tram stop</a> soon after 6:00 a.m.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RAIB_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-RAIB-3">[3]</a></sup> A preliminary report issued a week after the accident indicated that although the speed limit approaching the junction was 20 km/h (12 mph), the tram had been travelling at 70 kilometres per hour (43 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Report_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Report-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accident">Accident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Croydon_tram_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Accident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The accident took place in the dark and during heavy rain at 06:07,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Report_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Report-4">[4]</a></sup> on a sharp left curve approach­ing the <a href="/wiki/Railroad_switch" title="Railroad switch">points</a> where <a href="/wiki/Tramlink_route_3" title="Tramlink route 3">route 3</a> from <a href="/wiki/New_Addington" title="New Addington">New Addington</a> (on which the tram was operating) converges with the line from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beckenham_Junction" title="Beckenham Junction">Beckenham Junction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elmers_End" title="Elmers End">Elmers End</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RAIB_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-RAIB-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The curve is located in a <a href="/wiki/Cut_(earthmoving)" title="Cut (earthmoving)">cutting</a>, which comes almost immediately after the line emerges from a series of three tunnels on a 1-mile (1.6 km) straight section of track after leaving <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Park_tram_stop" title="Lloyd Park tram stop">Lloyd Park tram stop</a>. It has been described as a "sharp bend",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37919658_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37919658-7">[7]</a></sup> and has a 20 km/h (12 mph) speed restriction.</p>
<p>The tram entered the curve at a speed of 70 kilometres per hour (43 mph),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Report_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Report-4">[4]</a></sup> and overturned onto its right side, falling outside the curve on which it was travelling and trapping several people inside. The tram was a <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_CR4000" title="Bombardier CR4000">Bombardier CR4000</a>, No. 2551, which is a two-car <a href="/wiki/Articulated_vehicle" title="Articulated vehicle">articulated</a> unit, and has a maximum design speed of 80 km/h (50 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Report_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Report-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The emergency services confirmed that 51 people were injured, and initially stated that five had been killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37919658_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37919658-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Standard1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Standard1-8">[8]</a></sup> The death toll later rose to seven.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BTP_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BTP-9">[9]</a></sup> The victims, six men and a woman, were between 19 and 63 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37952302_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37952302-10">[10]</a></sup> Eight of the survivors were described as having serious or life-threatening injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37932744_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37932744-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was the deadliest <a href="/wiki/Tram_accident" title="Tram accident">tram accident</a> in the United Kingdom since <a href="/wiki/Dover_Corporation_Tramways#Accidents" title="Dover Corporation Tramways">an accident at Dover</a> in 1917 killed eleven and injured 60,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dover5_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dover5-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ES3391846_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-ES3391846-13">[13]</a></sup> and the first in the United Kingdom in which passengers were killed since three people died in an accident in Glasgow in 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITV091116_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-ITV091116-1">[1]</a></sup> It is also the deadliest accident on any rail network in the UK since the 2001 <a href="/wiki/Great_Heck_rail_crash" title="Great Heck rail crash">Great Heck rail crash</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ES3391846_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-ES3391846-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Croydon_tram_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The injured were taken to <a href="/wiki/St_George%27s_Hospital" title="St George's Hospital">St George's Hospital</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tooting" title="Tooting">Tooting</a>, and to <a href="/wiki/Croydon_University_Hospital" title="Croydon University Hospital">Croydon University Hospital</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37919658_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37919658-7">[7]</a></sup> As well as the 51 people taken to hospital, seven more made their own way to hospital for treatment. Twenty-two ambulances,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-G101116_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-G101116-2">[2]</a></sup> eight fire engines, and over 70 firefighters from the <a href="/wiki/London_Fire_Brigade" title="London Fire Brigade">London Fire Brigade</a> were sent to the scene.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37947408_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37947408-14">[14]</a></sup> Assistance was given by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Croydon_Council" title="Croydon Council">Croydon Council</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salvation_Army" title="Salvation Army">Salvation Army</a> and railway <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chaplains" title="Chaplains">chaplains</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rail814p6-7_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rail814p6-7-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/FirstGroup" title="FirstGroup">FirstGroup</a>, which operates the tram service on behalf of <a href="/wiki/Transport_for_London" title="Transport for London">Transport for London</a> (TfL), said it was "shocked and saddened by what happened."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37947408_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37947408-14">[14]</a></sup> An extra minute was added to the <a href="/wiki/Two-minute_silence" title="Two-minute silence">two-minute</a> <a href="/wiki/Armistice_Day" title="Armistice Day">Armistice Day</a> silence at <a href="/wiki/Croydon_Cenotaph" title="Croydon Cenotaph">Croydon Cenotaph</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37947408_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37947408-14">[14]</a></sup> TfL later made an offer to cover the funeral expenses of the victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37988749_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37988749-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the night of 10–11 November, the tram was righted in preparation for removal from the accident site,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ES3393116_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ES3393116-17">[17]</a></sup> and it was removed on the morning of 12 November. The tram had suffered serious damage to its right side, onto which it had overturned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37960541_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37960541-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC38003934_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC38003934-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the accident, no services operated on the line between <a href="/wiki/East_Croydon_station" title="East Croydon station">East Croydon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Addington_Village_Interchange" title="Addington Village Interchange">Addington Village</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrington_Road_tram_stop" title="Harrington Road tram stop">Harrington Road</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elmers_End_railway_station" title="Elmers End railway station">Elmers End</a>. Partial tram services ran between East Croydon and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wimbledon_railway_station" title="Wimbledon railway station">Wimbledon</a>, between <a href="/wiki/New_Addington_tram_stop" title="New Addington tram stop">New Addington</a> and Addington Village, and between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beckenham_Junction_railway_station" title="Beckenham Junction railway station">Beckenham Junction</a> and Harrington Road. Full services were reinstated on 18 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TFLLTT_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-TFLLTT-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC38025241_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC38025241-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Investigations">Investigations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Croydon_tram_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Investigations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Transport_Police">British Transport Police</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Croydon_tram_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: British Transport Police">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 42-year-old tram driver was arrested by the <a href="/wiki/British_Transport_Police" title="British Transport Police">British Transport Police</a> on suspicion of <a href="/wiki/Manslaughter" title="Manslaughter">manslaughter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BTP_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-BTP-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky10651841_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sky10651841-22">[22]</a></sup> After questioning, he was released on bail until May 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky10651841_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sky10651841-22">[22]</a></sup> According to <i><a href="/wiki/Metro_(British_newspaper)" title="Metro (British newspaper)">metro.co.uk</a></i>, one aspect of the police investigation is whether or not the tram driver fell asleep; and <i>The Guardian</i> reports that some passengers said the driver had blacked out at the controls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>A former driver suggested, to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i>, that a blackout was a possibility, due to the erratic <a href="/wiki/Shift_work" title="Shift work">shift patterns</a> that the drivers had to adhere to. He also said that the vending machine at the tram depot was stocked only with <a href="/wiki/Energy_drink" title="Energy drink">energy drinks</a>, and that "Nobody is ever fully awake; I was always in a bit of a daze and that is because the way the shifts work doesn't allow the drivers to get a regular sleep pattern."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Following this, multiple sources reported on a video apparently showing a different driver struggling to stay awake at the controls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The driver concerned was suspended pending an investigation into the matter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Office_of_Rail_and_Road">Office of Rail and Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Croydon_tram_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Office of Rail and Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Rail_and_Road" title="Office of Rail and Road">Office of Rail and Road</a> opened its own investigation into the accident, concentrating on whether or not safety rules were followed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37988749_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37988749-16">[16]</a></sup> They have confirmed that British trams are not fitted with any safety protection systems that would <a href="/wiki/Automatic_train_protection" title="Automatic train protection">apply the brakes automatically</a> if they are going too fast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37919658_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37919658-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sky1_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sky1-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail_Accident_Investigation_Branch">Rail Accident Investigation Branch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Croydon_tram_derailment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Rail Accident Investigation Branch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Rail_Accident_Investigation_Branch" title="Rail Accident Investigation Branch">Rail Accident Investigation Branch</a> (RAIB) is also investigating the accident, with data from the tram's <a href="/wiki/Train_event_recorder" title="Train event recorder">on-board event recorder</a> being analysed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The RAIB state that initial indications suggest that the tram was travelling at a significantly higher speed than permitted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RAIB_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-RAIB-3">[3]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rail_Magazine" title="Rail Magazine">Rail</a></i> magazine reported that the tram's <a href="/wiki/Track_brake" title="Track brake">electro-magnetic track brakes</a> had not been activated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rail814p3_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rail814p3-31">[31]</a></sup> Following the accident, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> reported that on 31 October there had been passenger allegations made on <i>Facebook</i> of a tram travelling round the curve at excessive speed. The <i><a href="/wiki/London_Evening_Standard" title="London Evening Standard">Evening Standard</a></i> reports an earlier passenger complaint describing the tram as "tipping" on the curve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-G101116_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-G101116-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ES3391846_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-ES3391846-13">[13]</a></sup> The driver of the tram involved in the incident of 31 October was not the one involved in the accident on 9 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC37979121_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC37979121-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>The interim report was released a week later on 16 November 2016. At the time of the accident it was dark and it was raining heavily. There was no evidence of any track defects, or obstructions on the track, that could have contributed to the derailment. Initial investigation did not indicate any malfunction of the tram's braking system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Report_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Report-4">[4]</a></sup> Initial findings were that the tram, with about 60 people on board, was travelling at approximately 70 kilometres per hour (43 mph) at the time of the accident – far more than the speed restriction of 20 kilometres per hour (12 mph) – with the brakes having been applied late.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardianInterim_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianInterim-33">[33]</a></sup> A recommendation was made that a further speed limit should be introduced prior to the one for the curve at Sandilands Junction before the line reopened to traffic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Report_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-Report-4">[4]</a></sup> The recommendation was accepted and three further speed restrictions were put in place before the line reopened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC38003934_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC38003934-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC38025241_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC38025241-21">[21]</a></sup> In November 2016, <i>Rail</i> magazine called for the RAIB to complete its investigation and release the final report "much more rapidly than has become the norm".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rail814p3_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rail814p3-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
