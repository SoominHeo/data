<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Fernande Grudet</b> (6 July 1923 – 19 December 2015), also known as <b>Madame Claude</b>, was a French brothel keeper. In the 1960s she was the head of a French network of call girls who worked especially for dignitaries and civil servants.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Madame_Claude&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born on 6 July 1923 in <a href="/wiki/Angers" title="Angers">Angers</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, Fernande Grudet was reared in a convent by nuns. After acting as an agent of the <a href="/wiki/French_Resistance" title="French Resistance">Resistance</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_occupation_of_France_during_World_War_II" title="German occupation of France during World War II">German Occupation of France</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and doing several other jobs, she created her exclusive prostitution network in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> during the 1960s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>At this time she ran a brothel in the expensive 16th arrondissement of Paris. "There are two things that people will always pay for: food and sex. I wasn't any good at cooking", she is reputed to have said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mulholland_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mulholland-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her wealthy clientele included not only political figures, but also members of the Mafia, and her status as an informant to the police ensured she was protected. Her address book, Grudet claimed, had included the names of the <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Reza_Pahlavi" title="Mohammad Reza Pahlavi">Shah of Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Agnelli" title="Gianni Agnelli">Gianni Agnelli</a>, the one-time head of <a href="/wiki/Fiat_Automobiles" title="Fiat Automobiles">Fiat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VF2014_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-VF2014-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dailymail_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dailymail-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1976, the judge <a href="/wiki/Jean-Louis_Brugui%C3%A8re" title="Jean-Louis Bruguière">Jean-Louis Bruguière</a> began dismantling Grudet's organization. She was being pursued for unpaid taxes, amounting to 11m francs (around £4.9m), and fled to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, but returned to France in 1986, serving a four-month jail sentence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VF2014_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-VF2014-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Willsher_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Willsher-4">[4]</a></sup> After her release, she attempted to set up a new prostitution organization, but in 1992 she was sentenced to a term in <a href="/wiki/Fleury-M%C3%A9rogis_Prison" title="Fleury-Mérogis Prison">Fleury-Mérogis Prison</a> for procuring.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The history of Madame Claude has inspired many writers. Her life was the basis of the feature film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_French_Woman" title="The French Woman">Madame Claude</a></i> (1977), directed by <a href="/wiki/Just_Jaeckin" title="Just Jaeckin">Just Jaeckin</a>, and starring <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7oise_Fabian" title="Françoise Fabian">Françoise Fabian</a>. Grudet died in <a href="/wiki/Nice" title="Nice">Nice</a> on 19 December 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
