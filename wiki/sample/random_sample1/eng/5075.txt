<p>The <b>Maranhão red-handed howler</b> (<i>Alouatta ululata</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered</a> species of <a href="/wiki/Howler_monkey" title="Howler monkey">howler monkey</a> <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to forests (for example <a href="/wiki/Maranh%C3%A3o_Baba%C3%A7u_forests" title="Maranhão Babaçu forests">Babaçu forests</a>) in the northeastern <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> states of <a href="/wiki/Cear%C3%A1" title="Ceará">Ceará</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maranh%C3%A3o" title="Maranhão">Maranhão</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piau%C3%AD" title="Piauí">Piauí</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">[1]</a></sup> It was previously thought to be a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Red-handed_howler" title="Red-handed howler">red-handed howler</a>, but unlike that species, the Maranhão red-handed howler is strongly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sexually_dichromatic" title="Sexually dichromatic">sexually dichromatic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
