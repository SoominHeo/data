<p><b>Maroua</b> is the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Far_North_Region_(Cameroon)" title="Far North Region (Cameroon)">Far North Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>, stretching along the banks of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ferngo_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ferngo River (page does not exist)">Ferngo</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kaliao_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kaliao River (page does not exist)">Kaliao Rivers</a>, "in the foothills of the <a href="/wiki/Mandara_Mountains" title="Mandara Mountains">Mandara Mountains</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city had 201,371 inhabitants at the 2005 Census, and is a centre of <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a> industry. The city also has <a href="/wiki/Salak_Airport" title="Salak Airport">an airport located near the town of Salak</a>, an agricultural school, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethnographic" title="Ethnographic">ethnographic</a> museum. To the North of the city is the famous <a href="/wiki/Waza_National_Park" title="Waza National Park">Waza National Park</a>. To the South is a large military base home to the <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><abbr title="Bataillon d’Intervention Rapide">BIR</abbr></span> rapid intervention force. Mount Maroua dominates the skyline of the city. The predominant religion is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sufi" title="Sufi">Sufi</a> Islam. The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maroua" title="University of Maroua">University of Maroua</a> is based in the city.</p>
<p>It is well known for its huge fields of ginger, and for the famous liquor of ginger. Inhabitants believe that this liquor is good of sexual health, and gives "lion's power" to men who drink it.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maroua&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Maroua_Declaration" title="Maroua Declaration">Maroua Declaration</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Maritime_boundary" title="Maritime boundary">maritime boundary</a> agreement between <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> and Cameroon, was signed in Maroua on 1 June 1975.</p>
<p>In 2013, a <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">malaria</a> outbreak occurred, with "more than 10,000 cases ... reported in the Town of Maroua alone".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 4, 2014 two Italian priests and a Canadian nun were kidnapped from the parish of Maroua, in the village of Tchéré.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> They "were released in the night of May 31 to June 1, 2014."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2014, "a gendarme [military] raid on the Maroua Central Market" resulted in 40 arrests of suspected <a href="/wiki/Boko_Haram" title="Boko Haram">Boko Haram</a> militants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maroua&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The climate is Sahelian, being hot and dry for most of the year. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen-Geiger_climate_classification_system" title="Köppen-Geiger climate classification system">Köppen-Geiger climate classification system</a> classifies its climate as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_wet_and_dry_climate" title="Tropical wet and dry climate">tropical wet and dry</a> (Aw).</p>
