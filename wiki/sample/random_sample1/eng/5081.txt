<p><b>Márcio Amoroso dos Santos</b> (born 5 July 1974 in <a href="/wiki/Bras%C3%ADlia" title="Brasília">Brasília</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Brazil" title="Football in Brazil">Brazilian footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> or on occasion as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attacking_midfielder" title="Attacking midfielder">attacking midfielder</a>. He played for several teams in Japan, Italy, Germany, Spain and Greece while representing <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil at international</a> level, winning the <a href="/wiki/1999_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1999 Copa América">1999 Copa América</a>. In his prime, he was a very talented striker with great dribbling skills and goal scoring ability, who was also capable of creating chances for team-mates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Amoroso.2C_the_fading_star_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Amoroso.2C_the_fading_star-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1rcio_Amoroso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amoroso started his career at homeland club <a href="/wiki/Guarani_FC" title="Guarani FC">Guarani FC</a> at 1992. In July 1992, he was loaned to a Japanese outfit <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Verdy" title="Tokyo Verdy">Verdy Kawasaki</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J._League_Division_1" title="J. League Division 1">J. League Division 1</a>), winning two J-League titles,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lequipe.fr_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-lequipe.fr-3">[3]</a></sup> and returned to Guarani FC two years later, finishing the <a href="/wiki/1994_Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="1994 Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">1994 Campeonato Brasileiro Série A</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A_top_scorers" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A top scorers">season's top scorer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Amoroso.2C_the_fading_star_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Amoroso.2C_the_fading_star-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1996, he transferred to <a href="/wiki/Clube_de_Regatas_do_Flamengo" title="Clube de Regatas do Flamengo">Flamengo</a>, but he came to prominence playing in the Italian <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> for unfashionable <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a> in the late-1990s. There he starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Bierhoff" title="Oliver Bierhoff">Oliver Bierhoff</a> in a side which played an adventurous 3–4–3 formation, finishing his first season with the club in third place in <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_Serie_A" title="1997–98 Serie A">Serie A</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sport.tiscali.it_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-sport.tiscali.it-5">[5]</a></sup> When the league's top scorer Oliver Bierhoff left the club for <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a> in 1998, many thought Udinese Calcio would struggle to repeat their success, but that very <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_1998%E2%80%9399" title="Serie A 1998–99">next season</a> Amoroso himself became the focus of the team, and was the <a href="/wiki/Capocannoniere" title="Capocannoniere">top scorer in Serie A</a> with 22 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sport.tiscali.it_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-sport.tiscali.it-5">[5]</a></sup> The following season, he transferred to the defending UEFA Cup and Coppa Italia champions <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_F.C." title="Parma F.C.">Parma</a> for an astounding €30 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off-4">[4]</a></sup> Although the team started the season strongly, winning the <a href="/wiki/1999_Supercoppa_Italiana" title="1999 Supercoppa Italiana">1999 Supercoppa Italiana</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sport.tiscali.it_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-sport.tiscali.it-5">[5]</a></sup> Parma never quite fulfilled their potential to win the league title, and Amoroso was not able to match the form he managed with Udinese due to recurring injury problems;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-calcioweb.eu_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-calcioweb.eu-6">[6]</a></sup> the club did manage to reach the <a href="/wiki/2001_Coppa_Italia_Final" title="2001 Coppa Italia Final">2001 Coppa Italia final</a>, however.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>After two seasons, Amoroso was soon on the move again, this time to <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> in Germany, for 50 million <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Mark" title="Deutsche Mark">Deutsche Mark</a> (€25 million),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dortmund_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dortmund-9">[nb 1]</a></sup> a German record at that time,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> and as of 2013, still a fourth highest signing after <a href="/wiki/Javi_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Javi Martínez">Javi Martínez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mario_G%C3%B3mez" title="Mario Gómez">Mario Gómez</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mario_G%C3%B6tze" title="Mario Götze">Mario Götze</a>. Amoroso won the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> title during the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_Bundesliga" title="2001–02 Bundesliga">2001–02 season</a>, and was also the league's top scorer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off-4">[4]</a></sup> He helped the club to the <a href="/wiki/2002_UEFA_Cup_Final" title="2002 UEFA Cup Final">2002 UEFA Cup Final</a>, where his goal (a penalty) could not prevent the team from losing 3–2 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feyenoord_Rotterdam" title="Feyenoord Rotterdam">Feyenoord</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> During his next two seasons with the club, his appearances were more limited however, due to recurring injury problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off-4">[4]</a></sup> Amoroso played for <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga_CF" title="Málaga CF">Málaga</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_La_Liga" title="2004–05 La Liga">2004–05 season</a>, although he was mainly used as a substitute, scoring only 5 goals in 29 appearances, as Málaga finished the season in 10th place in the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Amoroso moved to <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_FC" title="São Paulo FC">São Paulo</a> in the summer of <a href="/wiki/2005_Copa_Libertadores" title="2005 Copa Libertadores">2005</a> and immediately helped them to the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Libertadores" title="Copa Libertadores">Copa Libertadores</a>, the most prestigious club prize in South America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup> In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_2005%E2%80%9306" title="Serie A 2005–06">January 2006</a>, after having won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_Club_World_Championship" title="FIFA Club World Championship">FIFA Club World Championship</a>, finishing the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIFA_Club_World_Championship" title="2005 FIFA Club World Championship">tournament</a> as top scorer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> he returned to Italy, signing an 18-month contract for A.C. Milan as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Christian_Vieri" title="Christian Vieri">Christian Vieri</a>, who had transferred to <a href="/wiki/AS_Monaco_FC" title="AS Monaco FC">Monaco</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>After an unsuccessful spell, Amoroso agreed to cancel his contract with A.C. Milan on 1 September 2006, and immediately signed a new contract with <a href="/wiki/Sport_Club_Corinthians_Paulista" title="Sport Club Corinthians Paulista">Corinthians</a>. Amoroso quickly received the <a href="/wiki/Squad_number_(association_football)" title="Squad number (association football)">no. 10</a> jersey from Corinthians as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Tevez" title="Carlos Tevez">Carlos Tevez</a> (who left SC Corinthians Paulista and moved to <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup> But there he could not show the football that he was capable of, having his contract resigned in April 2007, signing in for <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%AAmio_Foot-Ball_Porto_Alegrense" title="Grêmio Foot-Ball Porto Alegrense">Grêmio</a>. Since August, Amoroso did not play for Grêmio, having his contract resigned due to lack of form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[15]</a></sup> In January 2008, he signed a one-and-a-half year contract with <a href="/wiki/Aris_Thessaloniki_F.C." title="Aris Thessaloniki F.C.">Aris Thessaloniki</a>. However, he spent only six months in Thessaloniki. On 29 December 2008, Amoroso returned to Guarani for the 2009 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guarani2009_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-guarani2009-17">[16]</a></sup> He retired at the end of the season, at the age of 34, due to injury struggles, despite not making an appearance for the club that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-calcioweb.eu_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-calcioweb.eu-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1rcio_Amoroso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amoroso scored 9 goals in 19 appearances for <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> between 1995 and 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NFT_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-NFT-18">[17]</a></sup> He made his debut in a 5–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a>, and was later a member of the squad that won the <a href="/wiki/1999_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1999 Copa América">1999 Copa América</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Amoroso.2C_the_fading_star_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Amoroso.2C_the_fading_star-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Individual">Individual</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1rcio_Amoroso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Individual">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aris Thessaloniki was Amoroso’s 12th club in six different countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[18]</a></sup> He won 20 trophies and personal awards, including the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Copa América">Copa América</a> with Brazil and both the FIFA Club World Championship and Copa Libertadores with São Paulo. He has also played for Verdy Kawasaki, Flamengo, Udinese, Parma, Borussia Dortmund, Málaga, Milan, Corinthians, Grêmio and Guarani which was his last club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-Marcio_Amoroso.2C_a_one_off-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Amoroso was the top scorers in three different national championships, and broke the Bundesliga transfer record when he moved to Borussia Dortmund from Parma in the summer of 2001.</p>
