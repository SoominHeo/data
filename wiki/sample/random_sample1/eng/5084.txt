<p><b>Marco Davide Faraoni</b> (born 25 October 1991) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_people" title="Italian people">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">full back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Davide_Faraoni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_career">Youth career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Davide_Faraoni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Youth career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Bracciano" title="Bracciano">Bracciano</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Rome" title="Province of Rome">Province of Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lazio" title="Lazio">Lazio</a>, Faraoni started his career at <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">S.S. Lazio</a>, which he could play right or centre-back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-profile-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lazio_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lazio-2">[2]</a></sup> He missed a few months in 2009–10 season due to right leg <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament" title="Anterior cruciate ligament">anterior cruciate ligament</a> injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Faraoni had played for Lazio's <i>Giovanissimi regionali</i> team<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> to <a href="/wiki/Campionato_Nazionale_Primavera" title="Campionato Nazionale Primavera"><i>Primavera</i> team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lazio_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lazio-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 July 2010, he was signed by <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Internazionale</a> in four-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> on reported free transfer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He was a member of <a href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano_Primavera" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano Primavera">Inter's <i>Primavera</i> under-20 team</a> in 2010–11 season (<i>de facto</i> U-19 team as the team had a handful players born 1991: Faraoni, <a href="/wiki/Denis_Alibec" title="Denis Alibec">Alibec</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sebastian_Carlsen" title="Sebastian Carlsen">Carlsen</a>). With the team, he also won <i><a href="/wiki/Torneo_di_Viareggio" title="Torneo di Viareggio">Torneo di Viareggio</a></i> (Carnival Cup) which held during <a href="/wiki/Carnival_of_Viareggio" title="Carnival of Viareggio">carnival</a> 2011 (days before <a href="/wiki/Lent" title="Lent">Lent</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carnival_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-carnival-7">[7]</a></sup> He played various positions for the team, from right-back, left-back<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> to right wing-forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carnival_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-carnival-7">[7]</a></sup> Under new coach <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Ara%C3%BAjo" title="Leonardo Araújo">Leonardo</a>, he was named in the first team squad several times<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but never made his first team debut in <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Serie_A" title="2010–11 Serie A">2010–11 season</a>.</p>
<p>Faraoni did not play in the reserve league playoff round, as coach <a href="/wiki/Fulvio_Pea" title="Fulvio Pea">Fulvio Pea</a> and the technical team chose to rest Faraoni and Alibec, two of the three senior players of the reserve.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Internazionale">Internazionale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Davide_Faraoni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Internazionale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Faraoni graduated from the youth team in 2011. Under new coach <a href="/wiki/Gian_Piero_Gasperini" title="Gian Piero Gasperini">Gian Piero Gasperini</a>, he played in pre-season friendlies, usually as a midfielder (wing-back) in 3–4–3 formation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and made his competitive debut in <a href="/wiki/2011_Supercoppa_Italiana" title="2011 Supercoppa Italiana">2011 Supercoppa Italiana</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. That match Gasperini used his symbolic 3-5-2 formation in the first half but changed to 4–4–1–1 in the second;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supercup_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-supercup-20">[20]</a></sup> Faraoni substituted midfielder <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ricardo_Gabriel_%C3%81lvarez" title="Ricardo Gabriel Álvarez">Ricardo Álvarez</a> in the 63rd minute, as a left-midfielder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supercup_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-supercup-20">[20]</a></sup> He made his Serie A debut 3 months later, under new coach <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Ranieri" title="Claudio Ranieri">Claudio Ranieri</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Moreira" title="Jonathan Moreira">Jonathan</a> at the last minutes. That match Inter won <a href="/wiki/Cagliari_Calcio" title="Cagliari Calcio">Cagliari</a> 2–1. He was the only backup right-back of the team in <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League">UCL</a> matchday 5 as <a href="/wiki/Maicon_Sisenando" title="Maicon Sisenando">Maicon</a> had not yet recovered. He made his European debut in that match, replacing Ricky Álvarez on 22 November 2011, a 1–1 draw with <a href="/wiki/Trabzonspor" title="Trabzonspor">Trabzonspor</a>.</p>
<p>Faraoni played five out of possible six matches for Inter in December, all as right midfielder. The first match was his first start in professional career, losing to <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a> 0–1 on 3 December. In the next game, Faraoni played his first European start, losing to <a href="/wiki/PFC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PFC CSKA Moscow">CSKA</a>, but Inter already qualified as group winner before that match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He then played 3 out of 4 successive league matches, as midfielder, which Inter won all 4 matches and climbed from bottom half to 5th before the winter break. Faraoni was highly rated<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> in the first match (v. <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a>) but he was substituted at half time in the second (v. Genoa, by Álvarez, who made a winning assist) and third one (v. Lecce, by Cambiasso). In those 4 league appearances, <a href="/wiki/Javier_Zanetti" title="Javier Zanetti">Zanetti</a> moved from right midfielder to right-back twice (Udinese &amp; Genoa, Maicon was injured/rested), suspended (v. Fiorentina), and as central midfielder (v. Lecce, <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Cambiasso" title="Esteban Cambiasso">Cambiasso</a> on the bench) respectively; while his right wing-back competitor <a href="/wiki/Yuto_Nagatomo" title="Yuto Nagatomo">Nagatomo</a> was as left-back to replace injured <a href="/wiki/Cristian_Chivu" title="Cristian Chivu">Chivu</a> in latter 3 match. He missed the match against <a href="/wiki/A.C._Cesena" title="A.C. Cesena">Cesena</a> on 18 December as Zanetti and Maicon restored to their usual position.</p>
<p>Despite being returned to a 'backup' role on 7 January 2012 against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parma_FC" title="Parma FC">Parma</a>, Faraoni came on as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> and scored his first ever <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> goal, in the 77th minute. He hit a stunning volley from the edge of the area which lobbed the Parma <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Mirante" title="Antonio Mirante">goalkeeper</a> and crowned a 5-0 win. On 12 January 2012, Inter announced that Faraoni had signed a new 4½-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> A week later, Faraoni made his <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Italian cup</a> debut in a 2-1 win over <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Faraoni returned to starting XI on 17 February, played three successive Serie A match. Since late January Inter suffered from injury crisis (e.g., Alvarez, Maicon and then Chivu again), poor form and the departure of main player (Thiago Motta) and did not won any game with only two draw. The coach had changed the formation occasionally to fit recovered <a href="/wiki/Wesley_Sneijder" title="Wesley Sneijder">Wesley Sneijder</a> but no improvement. Faraoni had played as a right wing forward in 4–2–3–1 formation against Bologna (as coach had a handful choice of winger since injury of Alvarez) and then starting right-back (due to injury of Maicon and Chivu, Nagatomo had to play left-back) for the first time in the 4–3–1–2 formation against Napoli. In the third match saw the return of 4–4–2 formation with Faraoni as right midfielder. However Faraoni was substituted by Sneijder at half time after finished the first half in 0–2 against Catania. Eventually Inter 2–2 draw with Catania, a point since January.</p>
<p>After the appointment of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Stramaccioni" title="Andrea Stramaccioni">Andrea Stramaccioni</a>, Faraoni was left out from the starting XI completely.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Udinese">Udinese</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Davide_Faraoni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Udinese">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 July 2012, <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese Calcio</a> claimed half the rights of Faraoni due to them being transferred as part of the deal that saw <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samir_Handanovi%C4%8D" title="Samir Handanovič">Samir Handanovič</a> go the other way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He made his debut on 25 August 2012, in the match against <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a>. On June 2013, Udinese redeemed the other half of Faraoni.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Watford">Watford</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Davide_Faraoni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Watford">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 6 July 2013, Faraoni's agent confirmed to <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a> that he would be joining <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> on a five-year deal, in his first season he was given the number 19. He made his Watford debut away to <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a> on 3 August 2013. Faraoni scored his first Watford goal away to <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a> on 17 August 2013, heading home a corner in the second half. He scored his first cup goal against <a href="/wiki/Norwich" title="Norwich">Norwich</a> in a 3-2 loss. After one season and 43 appearances for Watford (38 in the league), Faraoni announced on Twitter he would be heading back to Italy in July 2014.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Udinese">Return to Udinese</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Davide_Faraoni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to Udinese">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After one season with Watford, Faraoni re-signed for <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a> in July 2014. On 3 September 2015 Faraoni was signed by <a href="/wiki/Novara_Calcio" title="Novara Calcio">Novara Calcio</a> in a temporary deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Davide_Faraoni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Faraoni was a regular member of <i>Azzurrini</i> (Young <a href="/wiki/Azure_(color)" title="Azure (color)">azure</a>, the nickname of Italian youth teams). After made his U-16 debut, he was named in the squad of 2006 U16 international <a href="/wiki/Val-de-Marne" title="Val-de-Marne">Val-de-Marne</a> tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Faraoni was a member of U17 for 2008 Minsk youth tournament, however no lineup was known.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>

<p>He started two out of three matches of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship_elite_qualification" title="2008 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship elite qualification">2008 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship elite qualification</a>, which <i>Azzurrini</i> finished as the third and eliminated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> As U-19 (born 1990) team also eliminated from the 2009 edition of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship" title="UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship">UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship</a>, Faraoni promoted to U-19 directly and made his debut against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_under-19_football_team" title="Romania national under-19 football team">Romania</a> in December 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> FIGC also organized a U-18 friendly for the same age group in January and Faraoni played, winning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denmark_national_under-18_football_team" title="Denmark national under-18 football team">Denmark</a> 3–0. Missed a game in March against <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_under-19_football_team" title="Norway national under-19 football team">Norway</a>, he played the match against <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_under-19_football_team" title="Ukraine national under-19 football team">Ukraine U-19 (born 1990) team</a> in April,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> and played all four matches in a U-18 Slovakia Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Missed the match against <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_under-19_football_team" title="Denmark national under-19 football team">Denmark U-19</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship_qualification" title="2010 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship qualification">qualification</a> and two more friendlies due to injury, Faraoni returned to the U-19 team in April 2010, winning <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_under-19_football_team" title="Switzerland national under-19 football team">Switzerland</a> 1-0. He then played all 3 matches of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship_elite_qualification" title="2010 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship elite qualification">2010 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship elite qualification</a>.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> but dropped from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship" title="2010 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship">final squad</a> by the coach Massimo Piscedda.</p>
<p>In November 2010, he received his first <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">U-21</a> call-up from the new coach <a href="/wiki/Ciro_Ferrara" title="Ciro Ferrara">Ciro Ferrara</a>; on 17 November he made his debut in the friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Turkey_national_under-21_football_team" title="Turkey national under-21 football team">Turkey</a> played in <a href="/wiki/Fermo" title="Fermo">Fermo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> That match Inter had a dominated 5 members (out of 23-men) in the squad, namely forward <a href="/wiki/Mattia_Destro" title="Mattia Destro">Mattia Destro</a>, defender <a href="/wiki/Giulio_Donati" title="Giulio Donati">Giulio Donati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luca_Caldirola" title="Luca Caldirola">Luca Caldirola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_Biraghi" title="Cristiano Biraghi">Cristiano Biraghi</a>. (which the latter two remains in <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_A" title="2011–12 Serie A">2011–12</a> Inter squad). Except missed one in February (played for U-20 instead),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> he played three successive games (despite not call-up to <a href="/wiki/2011_Toulon_Tournament" title="2011 Toulon Tournament">2011 Toulon Tournament</a>) and benched on 10 August,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> became one of the regular member of <i>Azzurrini</i> along with Inter team-mate <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Bardi" title="Francesco Bardi">Francesco Bardi</a>, Caldirola, Donati and <a href="/wiki/Davide_Santon" title="Davide Santon">Davide Santon</a> (1 keeper, 1 centre-back and 2 fullbacks) He was the third choice right-back for <i>Azzurrini</i> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship_qualification" title="2013 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification">2013 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> behind Santon (a Newcastle player since late August) and Donati (who later swapped the role with Santon). He made his debut in that tournament in round 5 on 15 November 2011, replacing winger <a href="/wiki/Antonino_Ragusa" title="Antonino Ragusa">Antonino Ragusa</a>. However, after lack of chance under <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Stramaccioni" title="Andrea Stramaccioni">Andrea Stramaccioni</a>, Faraoni was dropped out from U21 squad.</p>
