<p><b>Marco Materazzi</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation:Â </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[Ëmarko mateËrattsi]</a></span>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Merit_of_the_Italian_Republic" title="Order of Merit of the Italian Republic">Ufficiale OMRI</a>; born 19 August 1973) is an Italian former professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Association_football_manager" title="Association football manager">manager</a> of <a href="/wiki/Indian_Super_League" title="Indian Super League">Indian Super League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Chennaiyin_FC" title="Chennaiyin FC">Chennaiyin</a>.</p>
<p>At the beginning of his career, Materazzi played with various Italian teams in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C" title="Serie C">Serie C</a>, and with <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>. He spent two periods with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perugia_Calcio" title="Perugia Calcio">Perugia</a> (1995â98 and 1999â2001) and signed for <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Internazionale</a>, in 2001 for â¬10Â million. At club level, he won a number of major honors with Inter including five <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> league titles in a row from 2006 to 2010, one <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>, one <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Club_World_Cup" title="FIFA Club World Cup">FIFA Club World Cup</a>, four <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> titles, and the <a href="/wiki/Supercoppa_Italiana" title="Supercoppa Italiana">Supercoppa Italiana</a> four times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Materazzi earned 41 caps for Italy from his debut in 2001 until 2008, playing in two World Cups and two European Championships. He was one of the key players in the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="2006 FIFA World Cup Final">2006 FIFA World Cup Final</a> against France, as he scored Italy's goal and later, in extra time, he received a headbutt from <a href="/wiki/Zinedine_Zidane" title="Zinedine Zidane">Zinedine Zidane</a>, who was punished with a red card. Italy then went on to win the World Cup in a penalty shoot-out, during which Materazzi scored again.</p>
<p>A controversial and provocative figure in football, he was known for his very physical and aggressive style of play as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a>, as well as his tight marking and strong, harsh tackling, which led him to receive more than 60 yellow cards and 25 red cards throughout his playing career. Due to his temper, and his commitment to rash, heavy challenges, he has been involved in several altercations with other players during matches,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Materazzi_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Materazzi-2">[2]</a></sup> drawing comparisons with retired defender <a href="/wiki/Pasquale_Bruno" title="Pasquale Bruno">Pasquale Bruno</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> placed Materazzi at number 45 in their list of the 50 hardest football players in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Serie_A" title="2006â07 Serie A">2006â07 season</a>, Materazzi was voted the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A_Defender_of_the_Year" title="Serie A Defender of the Year">Serie A Defender of the Year</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Materazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marco Materazzi was born in <a href="/wiki/Lecce" title="Lecce">Lecce</a>, where his father, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Materazzi" title="Giuseppe Materazzi">Giuseppe</a>, a professional footballer, was playing for <a href="/wiki/U.S._Lecce" title="U.S. Lecce">U.S. Lecce</a>. Giuseppe was also a former football coach and manager of teams such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pisa_Calcio" title="Pisa Calcio">Pisa</a>, <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting CP</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianjin_Teda_F.C." title="Tianjin Teda F.C.">Tianjin Teda</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Materazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Messina_Peloro">Messina Peloro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Materazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Messina Peloro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He began his footballing career with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Messina_Peloro" title="F.C. Messina Peloro">Messina Peloro</a> youth team from 1990 to 1991. He spent his early career in the lower divisions of Italian football, with amateur side Tor di Quinto (1991â92), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C" title="Serie C">Serie C</a>2 team <a href="/wiki/S.C._Marsala_1912" title="S.C. Marsala 1912">Marsala</a> (1993â94), and Serie C1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S.D._Trapani_Calcio" title="A.S.D. Trapani Calcio">Trapani</a> (1994â95), where he narrowly missed a historic promotion to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> after losing a promotion playoff to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S.S._Gualdo" title="S.S. Gualdo">Gualdo</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Perugia">Perugia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Materazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Perugia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> squad <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perugia_Calcio" title="Perugia Calcio">Perugia Calcio</a> signed Materazzi for the first time in 1995, but spent a part of the 1996â97 season in Serie C with <a href="/wiki/Carpi_F.C._1909" title="Carpi F.C. 1909">Carpi</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Everton">Everton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Materazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Everton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He then spent 1998â99 with <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>, where he was sent off three times in just 27 games, and scored twice, against <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> in the league<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_F.C." title="Huddersfield Town F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gazzetta_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-gazzetta-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Perugia">Return to Perugia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Materazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to Perugia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He then returned to Perugia in 1999 and scored 12 goals in the 2000â01 season, breaking <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Passarella" title="Daniel Passarella">Daniel Passarella</a>'s Serie A record of most goals by a defender in one season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gazzetta_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-gazzetta-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>Materazzi was then signed by <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Internazionale</a> for â¬10Â million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He finished the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Serie_A" title="2006â07 Serie A">2006â07 season</a> as the top scoring defender of Serie A with 10 goals,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> including a double away to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Siena" title="AC Siena">Siena</a> on 23 April which won the club's 15th league title with five games remaining.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Materazzi was an injury-time substitute for Inter in the <a href="/wiki/2010_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2010 UEFA Champions League Final">2010 UEFA Champions League Final</a> against <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Diego_Milito" title="Diego Milito">Diego Milito</a> who scored both of their goals in the 2â0 victory in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chennaiyin_FC">Chennaiyin FC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Materazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Chennaiyin FC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 September 2014, Materazzi was signed as the player-manager of <a href="/wiki/Chennaiyin_FC" title="Chennaiyin FC">Chennaiyin FC</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2014_Indian_Super_League_season" title="2014 Indian Super League season">inaugural season</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Super_League" title="Indian Super League">Indian Super League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Materazzi signed a two-season contract with Chennaiyin for $1 million (USD) every season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Materazzi did not choose himself to play in their opening match, a 2â1 victory at <a href="/wiki/FC_Goa" title="FC Goa">FC Goa</a> on 15 October courtesy of a free kick from marquee player and former Brazil international <a href="/wiki/Elano" title="Elano">Elano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Six days later in his first home game, Chennaiyin defeated the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kerala_Blasters" title="Kerala Blasters">Kerala Blasters</a> 2â1, but four days after that he lost for the first time, 1â4 to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Delhi_Dynamos" title="Delhi Dynamos">Delhi Dynamos</a>. In the club's fourth match of the season, he selected himself to play for the first time, starting in a 5â1 win over <a href="/wiki/Mumbai_City_FC" title="Mumbai City FC">Mumbai City FC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On 28 November, he brought his former international defensive partner <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nesta" title="Alessandro Nesta">Alessandro Nesta</a> out of retirement to play for Chennaiyin until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Chennaiyin finished the 14-game regular season in first place in the league, with Materazzi having made 6 appearances. In the <a href="/wiki/2014_Indian_Super_League_finals" title="2014 Indian Super League finals">end-of-season play-offs</a>, the team were eliminated in extra time in the semi-finals by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kerala_Blasters" title="Kerala Blasters">Kerala Blasters</a>.</p>
<p>At the end of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Indian_Super_League_season" title="2015 Indian Super League season">2015 season</a>, in which Materazzi lead Chennaiyin to the Indian Super League championship, his contract ended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Materazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Marco_Materazzi.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="828" data-file-width="797" height="260" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Marco_Materazzi.jpg/250px-Marco_Materazzi.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Marco_Materazzi.jpg/375px-Marco_Materazzi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Marco_Materazzi.jpg/500px-Marco_Materazzi.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>

<p>Materazzi made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy national team</a> on 25 April 2001, in a 1â0 friendly match victory against <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_football_team" title="South Africa national football team">South Africa</a>. He made two appearances in the qualifying campaign against Georgia and Hungary. In the final tournament of the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a>, Materazzi was used as a reserve player to back up Alessandro Nesta and Fabio Cannavaro. He made only one appearance by coming on as a substitute for Nesta in the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_Group_G" title="2002 FIFA World Cup Group G">2â1 loss</a> to <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_football_team" title="Croatia national football team">Croatia</a> in the group stage but soon in the 90th minute Materazzi played a floating ball over the top from just over half way to <a href="/wiki/Filippo_Inzaghi" title="Filippo Inzaghi">Inzaghi</a> but everyone missed the ball and rolled in the back of the net but the goal was disallowed after referee <a href="/wiki/Graham_Poll" title="Graham Poll">Graham Poll</a> claimed that Inzaghi grabbed an opponent's shirt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Materazzi was later criticised for his defending on both of Croatia's goals during the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two years later at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2004" title="UEFA Euro 2004">UEFA Euro 2004</a>, Materazzi was on the bench for Italy's first two games but started in the final <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2004_Group_C#Italy_vs_Bulgaria" title="UEFA Euro 2004 Group C">group game</a> against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a> in place of the suspended <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Cannavaro" title="Fabio Cannavaro">Fabio Cannavaro</a>, as Italy came from behind to win 2â1; in the first half, Materazzi was judged to have allegedly fouled <a href="/wiki/Dimitar_Berbatov" title="Dimitar Berbatov">Dimitar Berbatov</a> in the area, conceding a penalty, which <a href="/wiki/Martin_Petrov" title="Martin Petrov">Martin Petrov</a> subsequently converted. Despite the win, Italy were eliminated in the first round on direct encounters, following a three-way five point tie with <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_football_team" title="Denmark national football team">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<p>In his third major tournament, Materazzi started the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> as a reserve player, but after <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nesta" title="Alessandro Nesta">Alessandro Nesta</a> suffered an injury in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_-_Group_E#Czech_Republic_vs_Italy" title="2006 FIFA World Cup - Group E">group match</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic_national_football_team" title="Czech Republic national football team">Czech Republic</a>, Materazzi came on as his replacement and made an impact by scoring a goal, and was named Man of the Match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He received a red card in the round of 16 match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_association_football_team" title="Australia national association football team">Australia</a> for a foul on <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bresciano" title="Mark Bresciano">Mark Bresciano</a>, which ended in a 1â0 win to the Italians, and was suspended for the quarter-final against <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_football_team" title="Ukraine national football team">Ukraine</a>, which Italy won 3â0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="2006 FIFA World Cup Final">the final</a> against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>, Materazzi fouled <a href="/wiki/Florent_Malouda" title="Florent Malouda">Florent Malouda</a> to concede a dubious penalty, which <a href="/wiki/Zinedine_Zidane" title="Zinedine Zidane">Zinedine Zidane</a> subsequently scored. He made another impact by scoring a goal to level the score, a header from a right sided corner by <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Pirlo" title="Andrea Pirlo">Andrea Pirlo</a>. After the match went to extra-time, Materazzi and Zidane were involved in a confrontation in the 110th minute, where Materazzi verbally insulted Zinedine Zidane alongside tugging his shirt while Zidane attempted to walk away, which ended with Zidane head-butting Materazzi and receiving a red card. The game then continued to penalties. Materazzi scored Italy's second penalty as they defeated France 5â3 to claim their fourth FIFA World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> After the final, the confrontation resulted in a major controversy as Zidane accused Materazzi of insulting his sister and mother. Additionally, Materazzi claimed that after he had grabbed Zidane's jersey, Zidane sarcastically said to him <i>"If you want my shirt, I will give to you afterwards"</i>. Materazzi then revealed that he replied, <i>"Preferisco la puttana di tua sorella<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-26">[26]</a></sup>"</i> (<i>I would prefer your whore of a sister<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-26">[26]</a></sup></i>), which resulted in the head-butt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Three British <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tabloid_newspaper" title="Tabloid newspaper">tabloid newspapers</a>, the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daily_Star_(United_Kingdom)" title="Daily Star (United Kingdom)">Daily Star</a></i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mail" title="Daily Mail">Daily Mail</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sun_(United_Kingdom)" title="The Sun (United Kingdom)">The Sun</a></i>, alleged that Materazzi had called Zidane <i>"the son of a terrorist whore."</i> Materazzi took legal action against all three newspapers and the allegations were later withdrawn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> FIFA later issued a <a href="/wiki/Swiss_franc" title="Swiss franc">CHF</a> 5,000 fine and a two-match ban against Materazzi. Alongside striker <a href="/wiki/Luca_Toni" title="Luca Toni">Luca Toni</a>, Materazzi was Italy's top scorer throughout the tournament with two goals; he also won 14 challenges throughout the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-simpatia_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-simpatia-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Under Roberto Donadoni, Materazzi appeared consistently during the Euro 2008 qualifying campaign and became a starter after Alessandro Nesta's retirement. In the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a> tournament, he started in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008_Group_C#Netherlands_vs_Italy" title="UEFA Euro 2008 Group C">3â0 loss</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> but was substituted in the 54th minute in what would later prove to be his final appearance for Italy, as he was replaced later in the tournament by <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Chiellini" title="Giorgio Chiellini">Giorgio Chiellini</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_style">Playing style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Materazzi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Playing style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An aggressive, physical, and hard-tackling <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre-back</a>, who was also known for his tight marking of opponents,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Materazzi_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Materazzi-2">[2]</a></sup> Materazzi was considered to be one of the best defenders of his generation, and was highly regarded by two of the world's most respected coaches, <a href="/wiki/Marcello_Lippi" title="Marcello Lippi">Marcello Lippi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mourinho" title="JosÃ© Mourinho">JosÃ© Mourinho</a>, later becoming close friends with both managers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Materazzi was lauded by his managers in particular for being a goal threat as a defender, due to his outstanding aerial ability, which made him dangerous during set pieces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gazzetta_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-gazzetta-7">[7]</a></sup> His prolific goalscoring allowed him to capture the record for most goals in a Serie A season by a defender, which was broken during the <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Serie_A" title="2000â01 Serie A">2000â01 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gazzetta_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-gazzetta-7">[7]</a></sup> He was also an accurate set piece and penalty kick taker, with a powerful shot from distance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gazzetta_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-gazzetta-7">[7]</a></sup> In addition to these attributes, he had reliable technique and distribution skills, and was known for frequently playing long balls to the strikers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Materazzi_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Materazzi-2">[2]</a></sup> During his time with Internazionale, he developed the nickname <i>Matrix</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
