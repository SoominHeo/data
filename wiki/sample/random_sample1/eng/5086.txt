<p><b>Marco Motta</b> (born 14 May 1986) is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">right back</a>.</p>
<p>Motta is a former <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> international and made his only senior international appearance in 2010; at youth level, he participated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_-_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics - Men's tournament">2008 Summer Olympics</a> with Italy.</p>

<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Merate" title="Merate">Merate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a>, Motta began his professional career with Lombardy side <a href="/wiki/Atalanta_B.C." title="Atalanta B.C.">Atalanta B.C.</a> in 2005, following his promotion from the club's youth setup. He was a member of the 2004–05 <a href="/wiki/Campionato_Nazionale_Primavera" title="Campionato Nazionale Primavera">Primavera Under-20 squad</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and made his Serie A debut on 9 January 2005, replacing <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Montolivo" title="Riccardo Montolivo">Riccardo Montolivo</a> in the 65th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On 13 January he was a starter in the <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a>, ahead <a href="/wiki/Damiano_Zenoni" title="Damiano Zenoni">Damiano Zenoni</a>, a match in which <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> was eliminated in a 5–3 aggregate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Motta made 22 appearances (3 in the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Coppa_Italia" title="2004–05 Coppa Italia">2004–05 Coppa Italia</a>) in the remainder of the season to replace the left of Zenoni who left on 1 February, but due to the club's relegation, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese Calcio</a> in a co-ownership deal, for €2.05 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Atalanta2006bilancio_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Atalanta2006bilancio-4">[4]</a></sup> prior to the start of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_A_2005-06" title="Serie A 2005-06">2005–06</a> <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> season. Udinese also signed several players from <a href="/wiki/Bergamo" title="Bergamo">Bergamo</a>, namely <a href="/wiki/Fausto_Rossini" title="Fausto Rossini">Fausto Rossini</a> (50% for €450,000),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Atalanta2006bilancio_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Atalanta2006bilancio-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cesare_Natali" title="Cesare Natali">Cesare Natali</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and young players <a href="/wiki/Massimo_Gotti" title="Massimo Gotti">Massimo Gotti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Piermario_Morosini" title="Piermario Morosini">Piermario Morosini</a> (50%),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Rinaldi_(footballer,_born_1987)" title="Michele Rinaldi (footballer, born 1987)">Michele Rinaldi</a> (loan); Simultaneously sending <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Manfredini" title="Thomas Manfredini">Thomas Manfredini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antonino_D%27Agostino" title="Antonino D'Agostino">Antonino D'Agostino</a> (50%) to Bergamo as part of the deal for a total of €2 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Atalanta2006bilancio_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Atalanta2006bilancio-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Atalanta2008bilancio_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Atalanta2008bilancio-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Udinese">Udinese</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Motta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Udinese">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese Calcio</a> officially signed the wing back during the summer transfer window of 2005, and in his first season with the club, Motta was very sparingly used. An understudy of <a href="/wiki/Damiano_Zenoni" title="Damiano Zenoni">Damiano Zenoni</a> once again, he made six league appearances and netted one goal. He also made his European debut for Udinese, on 2 November 2005, substituting <a href="/wiki/David_Di_Michele" title="David Di Michele">David Di Michele</a> in the 73rd minute in a 3–4 defeat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Werder_Bremen" title="Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 30 January 2006, he was injured and ruled out for the remainder of season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UEFAinjury_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-UEFAinjury-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>He remained in <a href="/wiki/Udine" title="Udine">Udine</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Serie_A" title="2006–07 Serie A">2006–07 Serie A</a> season, as well, and he made 16 additional league appearances (all as starting XI) with his club. In June 2007, Udinese bought the remainder of his contract from <a href="/wiki/Atalanta_B.C." title="Atalanta B.C.">Atalanta</a> for an additional €800,000,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> but the player did not remain in <a href="/wiki/Friuli" title="Friuli">Friuli</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Serie_A" title="2007–08 Serie A">2007-08</a> campaign. Atalanta also signed <a href="/wiki/Zlatan_Muslimovi%C4%87" title="Zlatan Muslimović">Zlatan Muslimović</a> from Udine as part-exchange deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Atalanta2008bilancio_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Atalanta2008bilancio-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 28 July 2007, <a href="/wiki/Torino_F.C." title="Torino F.C.">Torino</a> announced the one-year loan signing of Motta from Udinese, re-joining former teammate <a href="/wiki/Cesare_Natali" title="Cesare Natali">Cesare Natali</a>, who was sold to Turin weeks earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Mainly an understudy to <a href="/wiki/Gianluca_Comotto" title="Gianluca Comotto">Gianluca Comotto</a>, he made 24 league appearances (14 as starting XI) for his club, also scoring his second career <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> goal.</p>
<p>Following a successful loan period, the player returned to Udinese ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Serie_A" title="2008–09 Serie A">2008–09 Serie A</a> season, due to the injury of <a href="/wiki/Cristi%C3%A1n_Zapata" title="Cristián Zapata">Cristián Zapata</a> (right-back or central back). At Udine, he competed the starting line-up with <a href="/wiki/Damiano_Ferronetti" title="Damiano Ferronetti">Damiano Ferronetti</a> (who can play left or right back), <a href="/wiki/Aleksandar_Lukovi%C4%87" title="Aleksandar Luković">Aleksandar Luković</a> (who can play left or central back) and <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Pasquale" title="Giovanni Pasquale">Giovanni Pasquale</a> (left-back or winger). He played eight league matches as a starter out of fourteen Serie A appearances that season. But at <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_UEFA_Cup" title="2008–09 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>, he was the starting right back at the group stage and played all four matches, three as starter, ahead of Ferronetti who played the first round. Udinese finished first in Group D.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since Udinese had signed <a href="/wiki/Du%C5%A1an_Basta" title="Dušan Basta">Dušan Basta</a> for the next season (who can play winger or right-back) and since Zapata recovered, he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">A.S. Roma</a>, agreed a ½+3-year deal, which he earned a gross salary (pre-tax income) of €300,000 for the remaining six months of 2008–09 season and increased to €660,000 in the 2009–10 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Romaloan_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Romaloan-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roma">Roma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Motta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Roma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Motta made his debut for Roma on 8 February 2009, coming on as a substitute in the 31st minute in Roma's 3–0 victory against Genoa. He impressed both his new club's supporters and his new coach, <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Spalletti" title="Luciano Spalletti">Luciano Spalletti</a>. In his various post-match press conferences with Roma Channel and Sky Italia, Spalletti said "Motta gets my compliments for that performance. It's not easy to enter onto the field at the Olimpico for the first time in front of a crowd like ours, with whom it's difficult to exhibit tranquility. He has great speed, force and technical quality, and he was very good in the defensive phase of the game. He played with great personality."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Motta then earned a starting position under Spalletti, beating off competition from the likes of <a href="/wiki/Cicinho" title="Cicinho">Cicinho</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cristian_Panucci" title="Cristian Panucci">Cristian Panucci</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Marco_Cassetti" title="Marco Cassetti">Marco Cassetti</a>. He also played both legs of <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2008–09 UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> round of 32 matches that lost to <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the loan deal, the capital club obtained the option to for the right to buy half of the player's contract in June for €3.5million or outright for €7million in three installments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Romaloan_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Romaloan-15">[15]</a></sup> On 24 June 2009 Roma exercised their right to sign half of Motta's contract for pre-agreed price.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Following the resignation of Spalletti, and the appointment of <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Ranieri" title="Claudio Ranieri">Claudio Ranieri</a>, Motta lost his place in the Roma first team, and failed to become a regular starter and made just 16 appearances during the entire 2009–10 <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> season. At the conclusion of the season, the two clubs failed to agree price on him to negotiate the co-ownership and on 25 June 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> the deal went to a blind auction (submit the bid by envelope). Udinese repurchased Motta after winning the auction on 26 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> for €1.45M.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Juventus">Juventus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Motta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Juventus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 July 2010, <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> agreed a loan deal for Motta with Udinese for the 2010–2011 season. Juventus have the option to sign Motta permanently at the end of the season, as Juventus was seeking a new right back to replace <a href="/wiki/Mart%C3%ADn_C%C3%A1ceres" title="Martín Cáceres">Martín Cáceres</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Zebina" title="Jonathan Zebina">Jonathan Zebina</a>. Motta then joined the Juventus squad which they would left from <a href="/wiki/Vinovo" title="Vinovo">Vinovo</a>, Juventus's facility located to <a href="/wiki/Pinzolo" title="Pinzolo">Pinzolo</a>, the camp site for pre-season training, in although the deal was pending a formal finalization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 5 July, Juventus announced the deal was completed, which cost €1.25 million for the loan with option to make it permanently for a further €3.75 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He made his official Juventus debut on 29 July 2010 in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2010–11 UEFA Europa League">2010–11 UEFA Europa League</a> qualifier against <a href="/wiki/Shamrock_Rovers_F.C." title="Shamrock Rovers F.C.">Shamrock Rovers</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Zden%C4%9Bk_Grygera" title="Zdeněk Grygera">Zdeněk Grygera</a> as right back. The Old Lady won the Ireland side 3–0 in aggregate. Grygera alter regained his starting place and Motta had to appear as unused substitute on the bench for much of the 2010–11 Serie A campaign.</p>
<p>On 22 June 2011 Juventus exercised the rights to sign Motta along with <a href="/wiki/Simone_Pepe" title="Simone Pepe">Simone Pepe</a> for a pre-agreed price. Following his permanent transfer to the club, he became a surplus to requirements under new coach Antonio Conte and failed to make any appearances for the club alongside the likes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amauri_Carvalho_de_Oliveira" title="Amauri Carvalho de Oliveira">Carvalho Amauri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luca_Toni" title="Luca Toni">Luca Toni</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vincenzo_Iaquinta" title="Vincenzo Iaquinta">Vincenzo Iaquinta</a>, all of which were released during the 2012 winter transfer market on either loan deals or permanent transfers.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_deals">Loan deals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Motta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Loan deals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Motta joined <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Catania" title="Calcio Catania">Calcio Catania</a> on a loan from <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> on 30 January, one-day before the end of the winter transfer period. His second league match for the club was coincidentally against his parent club, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juventus" title="Juventus">Juventus</a>, and he was ironically sent off 56 minutes into the match for a rash challenge on former teammate <a href="/wiki/Paolo_De_Ceglie" title="Paolo De Ceglie">Paolo De Ceglie</a>, having already been booked. Catania eventually lost the match 3-1. He returned to Juventus on 30 June 2012 and was loaned to <a href="/wiki/Bologna_F.C._1909" title="Bologna F.C. 1909">Bologna</a> on 19 July from <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus F.C.</a> with an option to buy 50% of the player. Bologna did not exercise their option to purchase the play's registration rights and he returned to Juventus once more. On 23 January 2014, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a> on loan for the remainder of the 2013–14 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Watford">Watford</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Motta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Watford">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Having left Juventus, Motta completed a move to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> on 26 February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He made his debut in a 1-0 win against Fulham on 3 March 2015.</p>
<p>However, following Watford's promotion to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barclays_Premier_League" title="Barclays Premier League">Barclays Premier League</a>, Motta was released by the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Charlton">Charlton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Motta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Charlton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2015 numerous reports stated that Motta was on the verge of a transfer to <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a>.On 12 February 2016 Motta signed for Championship side <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C." title="Charlton Athletic F.C.">Charlton Athletic</a> on a deal until the end of the 2015-16 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He was released at the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Motta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2005, Motta has been a member of the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy U-21 national team</a>. In 2007, after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2007 Euro U-21</a>, he was named <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captain</a> of the side.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He also represented Italy at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_-_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics - Men's tournament">2008 Summer Olympics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2003_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="2003 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">2003 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italy/Germany/Switzerland_under-20_Four_Nations_Tournament" title="Italy/Germany/Switzerland under-20 Four Nations Tournament">2006–07 Under-20 Four Nations Tournament</a>, <a href="/wiki/2008_Toulon_Tournament" title="2008 Toulon Tournament">2008 Toulon Tournament</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2009 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship</a>, he missed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2006 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2006 edition</a> due to injury,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UEFAinjury_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-UEFAinjury-11">[11]</a></sup> where he played 4<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> out of possible 8<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> in qualifying since made his U21 debut.</p>
<p>On 22 March 2009, Motta received his first call-up to the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italian national team</a> for the games against <a href="/wiki/Montenegro_national_football_team" title="Montenegro national football team">Montenegro</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> but remained an unused substitute.</p>
<p>He made his debut with the senior team on 10 August 2010, under new head coach <a href="/wiki/Cesare_Prandelli" title="Cesare Prandelli">Cesare Prandelli</a>, in the friendly match lost 1–0 against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Ivoire_national_football_team" title="Côte d'Ivoire national football team">Côte d'Ivoire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
