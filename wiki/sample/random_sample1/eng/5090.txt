<p><b>MARCOS</b>, previously named as <b>Marine Commando Force (MCF)</b>, is the <a href="/wiki/Special_Forces_of_India" title="Special Forces of India">special forces unit</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Navy" title="Indian Navy">Indian Navy</a>. created for conducting special operations such as <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_warfare" title="Amphibious warfare">Amphibious warfare</a>, <a href="/wiki/Close_quarters_combat" title="Close quarters combat">Close Quarter Combat</a> <a href="/wiki/Counter-terrorism" title="Counter-terrorism">Counter-terrorism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Direct_action" title="Direct action">Direct action</a>, <a href="/wiki/Special_reconnaissance" title="Special reconnaissance">Special reconnaissance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unconventional_warfare" title="Unconventional warfare">Unconventional warfare</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hostage_rescue" title="Hostage rescue">Hostage rescue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Personnel_recovery" title="Personnel recovery">Personnel recovery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Combat_search_and_rescue" title="Combat search and rescue">Combat search and rescue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asymmetric_warfare" title="Asymmetric warfare">Asymmetric warfare</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_internal_defence" title="Foreign internal defence">Foreign internal defence</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counterproliferation" title="Counterproliferation">Counterproliferation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_reconnaissance" title="Amphibious reconnaissance">Amphibious reconnaissance</a> including <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_reconnaissance" title="Amphibious reconnaissance">Hydrographic reconnaissance</a>. The MCF is specially organised, trained and equipped for the conduct of special operations in a maritime environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiannavy.nic.in_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-indiannavy.nic.in-2">[2]</a></sup> <i>MARCOS</i> is short for <i>Marine Commandos</i>. The force has gradually acquired experience and a reputation for professionalism over the three decades it has been in existence. The MARCOS are capable of undertaking operations in all types of terrain, but are specialised in maritime operations in <a href="/wiki/Jammu_and_Kashmir" title="Jammu and Kashmir">Jammu and Kashmir</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Jhelum" title="Jhelum">Jhelum</a> River and <a href="/wiki/Wular_Lake" title="Wular Lake">Wular Lake</a>, a 65 square kilometre freshwater lake. Some MARCOS personnel are also attached with the Army special forces units conducting counter-terrorism operations in the area. MARCOS are widely feared among the terrorists, who call them "Dadiwala fauj",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibn_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ibn-1">[1]</a></sup> meaning the "Bearded army" because of their bearded disguise in civil areas.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MARCOS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Indian_Armed_Forces" title="Indian Armed Forces">Indian Armed Forces</a> wanted to establish an amphibious warfare and maritime special operations force. During the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Pakistani_War_of_1971" title="Indo-Pakistani War of 1971">Indo-Pakistani War of 1971</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Navy" title="Indian Navy">Indian Navy</a> supported the landing operations against the Pakistani base of Cox Bazar, <a href="/wiki/Operation_Jackpot" title="Operation Jackpot">Operation Jackpot</a>, landing an army brigade using Polnochny class landing ships without significant resistance. Before this operation, Indian navy divers provided basic training in underwater demolition to Bengali insurgents. However, this did not yield expected results. Later, different Indian army units were assigned amphibious roles in exercises. In 1983, the 340th Army Independent Brigade based at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trivandrum" title="Trivandrum">Trivandrum</a>, was converted to an amphibious assault unit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sp.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sp.com-3">[3]</a></sup> Since then, the navy has conducted various exercises to demonstrate its amphibious abilities, notably the <a href="/wiki/Andaman_Islands" title="Andaman Islands">Andaman Islands</a> in 1984, and <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a> in 1986. These assault operations included both airborne and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphibious_forces" title="Amphibious forces">amphibious forces</a>. In April 1986, the Indian Navy started planning for a maritime special force which could conduct amphibious reconnaissance, raids and counter-terrorist operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gs_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-gs-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The force was initially trained by the other special forces of the country, including those under the Home Ministry and the Army. Three officers were then sent to undergo a hard training schedule with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Navy_SEALs" title="US Navy SEALs">US Navy SEALs</a> and further training was then acquired through exchanges with the British <a href="/wiki/Special_Air_Service" title="Special Air Service">Special Air Service</a>. The officers formed the first nucleus of the Indian Marine Special Force (IMSF) which was officially established in February 1987. The first batch of marine commandos qualified in February 1987. It was later renamed as the <i>Marine Commando Force</i> (MCF) in 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sp.com_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-sp.com-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>The MARCOS are capable of undertaking operations in all types of terrain, but are specialised in maritime operations. The force has undertaken numerous joint exercises with <a href="/wiki/Special_forces" title="Special forces">special forces</a> from around the world. The MARCOS presently has approximately two thousand personnel, though its actual strength remains classified. Operations undertaken by MARCOS usually remains classified though some of the known operations are:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finet.com_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-finet.com-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>MARCOS are known to be very secretive, not revealing their actual identity as specialised, elite soldiers. In their operations, they are sometimes assisted by <a href="/wiki/Westland_Sea_King" title="Westland Sea King">Westland WS-61 Sea King</a> transport helicopters, <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9rospatiale_Alouette_III" title="Aérospatiale Alouette III">Chetak helicopters</a> and two-man submarines. MARCOS can be launched from ships, aircraft and submarines in full battle gear.</p>

<p>The exclusivity of the unit lies in its competence to operate in all three mediums namely Sea, Air and Land. The MCF is specially organized, trained and equipped for the conduct of special operations in a maritime environment. Personnel from unit are deployed round the clock from Kashmir for CI/CT ops to GoA, Somalia for anti-piracy operations. The unit has always been at forefront in providing diving / civil assistance during times of natural disaster. The core tasking of MARCOS include:-</p>
<ul>
<li>Conduct clandestine attack against enemy ships,offshore installations and other vital assets behind enemy lines.</li>
<li>To support amphibious operations including pre-assault ops.</li>
<li>To conduct unconventional warfare.</li>
<li>Conduct of surveillance and recce missions in support of military operations.</li>
<li>Conduct of clandestine diving operations.</li>
<li>To conduct hostage rescue operations in maritime environment.</li>
<li>Combating terrorism in a maritime environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiannavy.nic.in_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-indiannavy.nic.in-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Selection_and_Training">Selection and Training</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MARCOS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Selection and Training">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>All MARCOS personnel are males selected from the Indian Navy. They are selected when they are in their early 20s and have to go through a stringent selection process and training. The selection standards are extremely high. The training is a continuous process. American and British special forces assisted in the initial training, which now consists of a two-year course for new recruits. The training regimen includes: airborne operations, combat diving courses, <a href="/wiki/Counter-terrorism" title="Counter-terrorism">counter-terrorism</a>, anti-<a href="/wiki/Aircraft_hijacking" title="Aircraft hijacking">hijacking</a>, anti-<a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">piracy</a> operations, <a href="/wiki/Direct_action" title="Direct action">direct action</a>, <a href="/wiki/Infiltration_tactics" title="Infiltration tactics">infiltration</a> and <a href="/wiki/Extraction_(military)" title="Extraction (military)">exfiltration</a> tactics, <a href="/wiki/Special_reconnaissance" title="Special reconnaissance">special reconnaissance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Unconventional_warfare" title="Unconventional warfare">unconventional warfare</a> training etc. Majority of the training is conducted at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=INS_Abhimanyu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="INS Abhimanyu (page does not exist)">INS Abhimanyu</a> which is also the home base of MARCOS.</p>
<p>All MARCOS personnel are freefall qualified (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HALO/HAHO" title="HALO/HAHO">HALO/HAHO</a>). A few also qualify to operate the Cosmos CE-2F/X100 two-man subs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sp.com_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-sp.com-3">[3]</a></sup> MARCOS train along with the <a href="/wiki/Special_Forces_of_India" title="Special Forces of India">Special Forces officers of the Indian Army</a> like the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Para_Commandos_(India)" title="Para Commandos (India)">Para Commandos</a> at the Indian <a href="/wiki/Indian_Special_Forces_Training_School_(SFTS)" title="Indian Special Forces Training School (SFTS)">Special Forces Training School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nahan" title="Nahan">Nahan</a> and Army's other schools for <a href="/wiki/Unconventional_warfare" title="Unconventional warfare">Unconventional warfare</a> These include the Junior Leaders' Commando Training Camp in Belgaum, Karnataka, the Parvat Ghatak School (for high altitude mountain warfare) in Tawang Arunachal Pradesh, Desert warfare school in Rajasthan, the <a href="/wiki/High_Altitude_Warfare_School" title="High Altitude Warfare School">High Altitude Warfare School</a> (HAWS) in Sonamarg, Kashmir and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counter-insurgency_and_Jungle_Warfare_School" title="Counter-insurgency and Jungle Warfare School">Counter-insurgency and Jungle Warfare School</a> (CIJWS) in Vairengte, Mizoram. These elite schools routinely host students from other countries. MARCOS are then trained at various training agencies within the navy. They are skilled combat divers and parachutists and most of the training deals with counter-terrorist, anti-hijacking and <a href="/wiki/Piracy#Anti-piracy_measures" title="Piracy">anti-piracy</a> operations.</p>
<p>Generally, the pre-training selection process is made of two parts. Any Indian Navy personnel who wants to join this outfit must first undergo a three-day long, physical fitness test and aptitude test. Within this process, 80% of the application are screened out. Further screening process is known as 'hell's week' which is similar to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy_SEAL_selection_and_training" title="United States Navy SEAL selection and training">United States Navy SEAL</a> hell’s week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16_Impressive_Things_Indian_Navy_Marine_Commandos_Do_In_Training_That_Deserve_Our_Respect_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-16_Impressive_Things_Indian_Navy_Marine_Commandos_Do_In_Training_That_Deserve_Our_Respect-17">[17]</a></sup> This involves high degree of physical exercises and sleep deprivation. It is only after this process that actual training begins<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-no_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-no-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-red_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-red-19">[19]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MARCOS_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-MARCOS-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The total duration of training of MARCOS is between two and half to three years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The basic training lasts six months. The first two months is the weeding out phase. The first phase of which lasts one month, in which they undergo many rigorous physical tests of which only 50% pass. Then for the next nine months, they are taught how to use different types of weapons, conduct special warfare techniques and how to gather intelligence from the enemy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The cadets start with basic diving and commando tactics and skills. Those that go on are trained in every aspect of modern warfare and in every situation. This includes firing while lying down, standing, running full-sprint, even backwards and looking into a mirror – with a reaction time of 0.27 seconds. This is followed by a year of training of specialised skills. They are trained in various types of close combat including <a href="/wiki/Krav_Maga" title="Krav Maga">Krav Maga</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Different forms of warfare training is imparted through field operations in <a href="/wiki/Counter-insurgency" title="Counter-insurgency">counter-insurgency</a> and anti-terrorist operations within the country and are trained to operate in any kind of environment like beaches, deep sea, on the surface of the ocean, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jungles" title="Jungles">jungles</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ravines" title="Ravines">ravines</a> and for situations like hostage rescue, urban combat and piracy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sp.com_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-sp.com-3">[3]</a></sup> A notably rigorous training program is the "death crawl" – an 800-metre struggle through thigh-high mud, loaded with 25 kg of gear and after a 2.5 km obstacle course that most soldiers would fail. After that, when the trainee is exhausted and sleep-deprived, he has to undergo the final test – shooting a target 25 metres away, with a partner standing next to it.</p>
<p>The MARCOS are trained in every kind of weapon and instruments, from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knives" title="Knives">knives</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crossbows" title="Crossbows">crossbows</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sniper_rifles" title="Sniper rifles">sniper rifles</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Handguns" title="Handguns">handguns</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assault_rifles" title="Assault rifles">assault rifles</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Submachine_guns" title="Submachine guns">submachine guns</a> and bare hands. Being divers, they can reach hostile shores swimming underwater.</p>
<p>The further training includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Open and closed circuit diving</li>
<li>Basic commando skills including advanced weapon skills, demolitions, endurance training and martial arts</li>
<li>Para training</li>
<li>Intelligence training</li>
<li>Operation of submersible craft</li>
<li>Offshore operations</li>
<li>Anti-terrorism operations</li>
<li>Operations from submarines</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skydiving" title="Skydiving">Skydiving</a></li>
<li>Various special skills such as language training, insertion methods, etc.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Explosive_ordnance_disposal" title="Explosive ordnance disposal">Explosive ordnance disposal</a> techniques</li>
</ul>
<p>They are also trained to parachute into open water with full combat load.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fotn_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-fotn-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2013, the MARCOS have introduced a larger duck-drop system which will be fitted on <a href="/wiki/Ilyushin_Il-76" title="Ilyushin Il-76">Ilyushin Il-76</a> aircraft. Each system (two boats) can accommodate 32 commandos, their weapons and fuel for the boats. Duck-drop system that marcos operate in the past is smaller in size which is fitted on An-32 aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> This force-multiplier was developed by the Agra-based Aerial Delivery Research &amp; Development Establishment. Once para-dropped from the aircraft, it allows for the commandos to assemble inflatable motorised boats within ten minutes, and quickly reach ships in distress. On reaching the target, they can dismantle the boats and travel underwater to mount a surprise attack. Such rescue missions can be mounted by the commandos deployed within an hour unlike in the past, when commandos took up to 48 hours to reach the targets as it involved travelling on-board ships and then being deployed on motorised boats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dc6f_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-dc6f-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Marcos are also preparing for urban warfare and have begun practicing on 3D virtual models of offshore installations to ensure a swift response during a terrorist attack. The marine commandos are undergoing regular training sessions in this computer-generated programme to be well-prepared for a strike similar to the 26/11 attack. Reliance Industries Limited (RIL) has provided a virtual model of its facility in Krishna-Godavari basin in the east, for the marine commandos at <a href="/wiki/INS_Kalinga" title="INS Kalinga">INS Kalinga</a>, the training base for Marcos in Visakhapatnam.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>MARCOS will carry out amphibious operations at Frasersgunj in the Sunderbans as part of their routine training. Frasersgunj, with its beach and proximity to the estuarine delta, is an ideal location for such exercises, the source added. The Coast Guard is in the process of setting up a base at Frasersgunj and station hovercraft there. More importantly, the elite force will get a feel of the Sunderbans, which is considered at risk from infiltrators and terrorists. There are so many waterways running through the Sunderbans delta that it is not possible to maintain physical vigil on all movement there. Security experts believe that the threat from terrorists moving in from the sea still remains. With so many vessels passing through the Sunderbans, it may be a route for terror outfits to send in armed men to carry out an attack like that on Mumbai. In case of any eventuality, the Macros are most likely to be called in as it is a marine environment. A major part of their training is kept under wraps. Frasersgunj will be an ideal location where secrecy can be maintained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Indian Navy <a href="/wiki/Indian_Navy_Swimmer_Delivery_Vehicle" title="Indian Navy Swimmer Delivery Vehicle">initiated the process of procuring five midget submarines</a> for Marine Commandos (MARCOS). These vessels would have the capacity of carrying four to six personnel on board. The submarines would have a diving depth of around 400 meters, and will strengthen the MARCOS ability to carry out special underwater operations in high seas. The midgets will have the capability of carrying out both manned and unmanned operations and will be equipped with a number of weapons including torpedoes. In 2009, the navy initiated the process of procuring these vessels and issued a Request for Proposal to Indian shipyards including Hindustan Shipyards Limited, ABG and Pipavav shipyards. Due to their faster speed and smaller size, midgets are able to escape the enemy Sound Navigation and Ranging (SONAR) probes making them useful in carrying out covert operations, and help troops to penetrate into hostile territory without getting noticed by enemies. In 2013 other noteworthy development, Vizag-based Hindustan Shipyards Ltd won the contract for building four 500-tonne mini-submarines, which were designed back in the previous decade by Larsen &amp; Toubro. The mini-submarines, to be delivered in the latter half of this decade, will be used exclusively by the Indian Navy’s MARCOS. The combat management systems have been designed and built by TATA Power SED, while Riva Calzoni will be supplying the periscopes and other masts that will host a SATCOM communications systems and LPI navigation radar. The sonar suite is likely to be supplied by ATLAS Elektronik.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>The average MARCOS training drop-out rate is more than 90%. The force has its own training facility, first as an adjunct of the operational company at INS Abhimanyu, in Bombay,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sp.com_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-sp.com-3">[3]</a></sup> later as the Naval Special Warfare Tactical Training Centre. For combat diving training, the commandos are sent to the Naval Diving School, Kochi. There are plans to move the Naval Special Warfare Tactical Training Centre to the erstwhile Naval Academy facility in Goa where it will be set up with focus on jungle warfare as well as counter insurgency operations. The new facility will be modelled on the lines of CIJWS of the Indian Army in Mizoram.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-no_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-no-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-red_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-red-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_Developments">Future Developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MARCOS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Future Developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To strengthen its capabilities to carry out special operations, the Navy is planning to procure advanced Integrated Combat System (ICS) for the MARCOS. The Navy wants the ICS for effective command, control and information sharing to maximise capabilities of individuals and groups of the MARCOS while engaging enemies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jagran_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-jagran-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>The ICS will provide enhanced capabilities such as tactical awareness, ability to fight in hostile environment and can enable Group Commanders to remotely monitor and control operations. It would help in integrating an individual sailor's capability of day and night surveillance, ballistic protection, communication and firepower through an integrated network at individual and group level. Initiating the procurement process through a Request for Information (RFI), Navy's Directorate of Special Operations and Diving has sought details from global vendors about the ICS, which should have gear for both individual and group uses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jagran_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-jagran-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>The individual equipment required by the Navy in the ICS includes light weight helmets, head-mounted displays, tactical and soft <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ballistic_vest" title="Ballistic vest">ballistic vests</a> along with communication equipment. The group-level gear requirements include command and control and surveillance systems along with high speed communication equipment. The devices would have sight for the sniper, laser range finder and long range thermal imager (medium and long range) and near IR laser pointer for a combat group to undertake surveillance, reconnaissance and targeting. The ICS would be compatible with the assault rifles and close-quarter combat weapons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jagran_32-2"><a href="#cite_note-jagran-32">[32]</a></sup> The Navy has recently started inducting the Israeli <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IMI_Tavor_TAR-21" title="IMI Tavor TAR-21">IMI Tavor TAR-21</a> for the MARCOS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jagran_32-3"><a href="#cite_note-jagran-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bases">Bases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MARCOS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The MCF currently operates out of the naval bases at <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Visakhapatnam" title="Visakhapatnam">Visakhapatnam</a>, Goa, <a href="/wiki/Kochi" title="Kochi">Kochi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Port_Blair" title="Port Blair">Port Blair</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fotn_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-fotn-10">[10]</a></sup> There are plans to shift the current training facility at the Naval Special Warfare Training and Tactical Centre to a new facility to be set up at the erstwhile Naval Academy in <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a>.</p>
<p>On July 12, 2016, the naval base INS Karna was commissioned near <a href="/wiki/Visakhapatnam" title="Visakhapatnam">Visakhapatnam</a> as a permanent base for the unit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
