<p><b>Marcus Berg</b> (<small>Swedish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Swedish" title="Help:IPA for Swedish">[ˈmarkɵs bærj]</a></span>; born 17 August 1986 in <a href="/wiki/Torsby" title="Torsby">Torsby</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> club <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_F.C." title="Panathinaikos F.C.">Panathinaikos F.C.</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Swedish national team</a>. In Sweden he is nicknamed <i>Svarte-Marcus</i> ("Black Marcus") due to scoring a lot of goals in the junior teams, in reference to <a href="/wiki/Filip_Johansson" title="Filip Johansson">Filip <i>"Svarte-Filip"</i> Johansson</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Berg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_years">Youth years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Berg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Youth years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Berg started to play for a local club <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Torsby_IF&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Torsby IF (page does not exist)">Torsby IF</a> in 2002. After that, he moved to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=IFK_Velen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="IFK Velen (page does not exist)">IFK Velen</a> in 2003.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="IFK_G.C3.B6teborg">IFK Göteborg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Berg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: IFK Göteborg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After spending two years in the youth team, in the season 2005, Berg was promoted to the regular squad and played for IFK Göteborg in the <a href="/wiki/Allsvenskan" title="Allsvenskan">Allsvenskan</a>. During the time playing for IFK Göteborg, he played alongside his older brother <a href="/wiki/Jonatan_Berg" title="Jonatan Berg">Jonatan</a>.</p>
<p>Berg played for IFK Göteborg until the 2007 summer and put forth an attracting performance throughout his final season, as he maintained a high scoring efficiency throughout the league matches. Göteborg won the league that season and Berg received a standing ovation upon leaving the field after the 5–0 away victory over <a href="/wiki/Kalmar_FF" title="Kalmar FF">Kalmar FF</a> where Berg scored, which was his last for the club because of his suspension for the game against <a href="/wiki/Djurg%C3%A5rdens_IF_Fotboll" title="Djurgårdens IF Fotboll">Djurgårdens IF</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Groningen">Groningen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Berg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Groningen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 10 August 2007, Dutch side <a href="/wiki/FC_Groningen" title="FC Groningen">FC Groningen</a> signed Berg for a reported <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>2.5 million<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> to replace the departing <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">AFC Ajax</a>-bound <a href="/wiki/Luis_Su%C3%A1rez" title="Luis Suárez">Luis Suárez</a>.</p>
<p>Berg had a good debut season in the Netherlands. Although he was not the top scorer in the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> in <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Eredivisie" title="2007–08 Eredivisie">2007–08</a>, he scored 18 goals for Groningen, helping the Dutch outfit to a seventh-place finish.</p>
<p>In the 2008–09 season, Groningen remained top of the Eredivisie table after five games played. Berg had a good display as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He managed to score 13 goals in 15 matches. He also scored four goals in a December game against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roda_JC" title="Roda JC">Roda JC</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hamburg">Hamburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Berg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hamburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 17 July 2009, <a href="/wiki/Hamburger_SV" title="Hamburger SV">Hamburger SV</a> confirmed that Berg had signed a five-year contract with the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The official FC Groningen website mentioned it was a record fee paid for a Groningen player. <i>Dagblad van het Noorden</i>, a Dutch newspaper, stated that Berg swapped clubs for €10.5 million. The contract also mentioned that a friendly match will be played between Groningen and Hamburg in the summer of 2010 or 2011 in the <a href="/wiki/Euroborg" title="Euroborg">Euroborg</a>, the main stadium of Groningen. Although initially the fee of €9.5 million was too high for Hamburg, Berg's desire of moving to <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> persuaded Groningen to complete the transfer. In his first Bundesliga game for Hamburg, playing at home against <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>, Berg scored his first goal for the club in the 72nd minute, just 182 seconds after coming off the bench, a record in the club's history. Despite the promising start, his debut season in the Bundesliga would not become a success, with Berg only scoring three additional goals in 30 appearances.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PSV_Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Berg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: PSV Eindhoven">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 17 July 2010, <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hamburger_SV" title="Hamburger SV">Hamburger SV</a> agreed a loan deal<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> with the result that Berg played for <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a> the 2010–11 season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Hamburg">Return to Hamburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Berg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to Hamburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After returning from his loan spell at PSV, Berg only made 13 appearances the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Bundesliga" title="2011–12 Bundesliga">2011–12 Bundesliga</a> due to injuries and continued lack of form, scoring once. On 9 November 2012, Hamburger SV Athletic supervisor <a href="/wiki/Frank_Arnesen" title="Frank Arnesen">Frank Arnesen</a> stated that the club had to sell five players to raise funds after having invested heavily in new players in the previous summer (including Dutch star <a href="/wiki/Rafael_van_der_Vaart" title="Rafael van der Vaart">Rafael van der Vaart</a>). One of these five players was said to be Marcus Berg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After these news, Berg was linked with a return to his former club IFK Göteborg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Berg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Panathinaikos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 4 July 2013, it was revealed that Berg had travelled to Athens, in order to sign with Panathinaikos. On 8 July 2013, Panathinaikos announced that Berg had signed a contract with the team for four years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 18 August 2013, in his debut appearance, Berg scored his first goal with Panathinaikos, as his team won the game against <a href="/wiki/Panetolikos_F.C." title="Panetolikos F.C.">Panetolikos</a>. On the first derby of the year against <a href="/wiki/PAOK_FC" title="PAOK FC">PAOK FC</a>, Berg scored Panathinaikos' goal and lost a penalty, as his team lost with a score of 2–1. On 2 March 2014, Berg was the MVP in the 3–0 away victory against <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C." title="Olympiacos F.C.">Olympiacos</a> with one goal and one assist for <a href="/wiki/Danijel_Pranji%C4%87" title="Danijel Pranjić">Danijel Pranjić</a>. This was the biggest away victory in the history of the Eternal Rivals. The following week, he scored a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a> when Panathinaikos defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aris_F.C._(Thessaloniki)" title="Aris F.C. (Thessaloniki)">Aris</a> 4–1 at home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 26 April 2014, in the Greek Cup final against rivals PAOK, Berg scored a hat-trick, leading Panathinaikos to a 4–1 victory, who won the Greek Cup as they had done in 2010. On 11 January 2015, Berg scored his second hat-trick in 5-0 home win against <a href="/wiki/Ergotelis_F.C." title="Ergotelis F.C.">Ergotelis</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Superleague_Greece" title="Superleague Greece">Superleague Greece</a>. On 26 January 2015, Marcus signed a one-year extension contract, until 2018<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 8 March, at Panathinaikos-<a href="/wiki/PAOK_FC" title="PAOK FC">PAOK</a> meeting, Marcus scored his third hat-trick in 4-3 home win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 30 March 2015, after 2 and a half years, Marcus scores his 7th international goal on friendly match against Iran.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 31 August 2015, Panathinaikos rivals <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAOK" title="PAOK">PAOK</a> offered to €2 million for Berg, but the "Greens" turned it down as <i>SDNA</i> reported.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos" title="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</a> rejected a bid from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAOK" title="PAOK">PAOK</a>, however <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Marseille</a> also tried to complete a move for the Swedish striker on the deadline date. According to <i>SDNA</i> sources, Marseille enquiried Panathinaikos over Berg, however they were put off by the high <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos" title="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</a>'s demands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 September 2015, Berg was left frustrated as he did not start in the line-up for the game against <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a> lost 1-0 at the hands of Russia with Berg making his appearance for only 30 minutes on the pitch as he started from the bench. Swedish press criticised this decision and Berg demanded first team football in the National Team after the final whistle.<i>"<a href="/wiki/Ola_Toivonen" title="Ola Toivonen">Ola Toivonen</a> is a kind of a different player than me and this decision has been taken. But it's pretty clear that I want to play as I'm in a decent form,"</i> the Panathinaikos striker underlined straight after the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On 7 November 2015, he scored the winning goal in the 10th day of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_Super_League" title="Greek Super League">Greek Super League</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Atromitos_F.C." title="Atromitos F.C.">Atromitos</a>, It was his 50th goal with the club in all competitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On 24 January 2016, almost 2,5 months since his last goal in <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a>, Berg scored leading his club to away 2-0 victory against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Levadiakos" title="Levadiakos">Levadiakos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On 6 March 2016, Swedish striker and fans of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos" title="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</a>' favorite Marcus Berg reached 100 official appearances with the Greek club at tonight's home game against <a href="/wiki/Atromitos_F.C." title="Atromitos F.C.">Atromitos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On 19 March 2016, he scored a brace against <a href="/wiki/Iraklis_1908_Thessaloniki_F.C." title="Iraklis 1908 Thessaloniki F.C.">Iraklis</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On 17 April 2016, in the last matchday for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2015-16_Superleague_Greece" title="2015-16 Superleague Greece">2015-16 season</a>, Berg scored five times (23', 41', 45+2', 51', 62') in his team's 6-1 victory against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panthrakikos" title="Panthrakikos">Panthrakikos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On 18 August 2016,Berg was the scorer of two goals, helping <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panathinaikos" title="Panathinaikos">Panathinaikos</a> won his first leg of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Europa_League" title="Europa League">Europa League</a> play offs and is set to qualify for the group stage, as the Greens crushed Danish <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%B8ndby_IF" title="Brøndby IF">Brøndby IF</a> 3-0 at Leoforos stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 September 2016,Berg stepped into retired <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> national team captain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zlatan_Ibrahimovic" title="Zlatan Ibrahimovic">Zlatan Ibrahimovic</a>'s vacant boots to open the scoring in Stockholm just two minutes before the break against <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a>, as he capitalised on <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Strootman" title="Kevin Strootman">Kevin Strootman</a>'s defensive mishap to smash in from outside the box into the top left corner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 18 September 2016, was the scorer of two goals, helping the club, to achieve a 4-0 home win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PAS_Giannina" title="PAS Giannina">PAS Giannina</a>. It was his first goals for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016-17_Superleague_Greece" title="2016-17 Superleague Greece">2016-17 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> On 23 September 2016, Berg extended his three-year contract for an annual fee of €1.1 million and with a €10 million release clause.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Panathinaikos have the 55% of Berg's rights, <a href="/wiki/Hamburger_SV" title="Hamburger SV">Hamburger SV</a> has 35% and the rest 10% belongs to the player. On 19 January, was the scorer of two goals, helping the club, to achieve a 2-1 home win against <a href="/wiki/Platanias_F.C." title="Platanias F.C.">Platanias</a>, helping the club climbing in the third place of <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Berg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Berg is renowned for his heading ability, first touches, and being able to shoot and pass with both his right and left foot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He is also known for his tactical dribbles and scoring from unusual angles. Berg scored 15 goals in his first season in the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a>, having only played 25 matches due to having a back-strain injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In his second season for Groningen, he scored 13 goals in the first 15 matches and was by then the second highest goalscorer in the league. He also showed his scoring ability in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2009 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship</a>, which he scored a record-breaking seven goals in four matches. His excellent performance earned him the player of the tournament award.</p>
