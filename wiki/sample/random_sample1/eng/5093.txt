<p><b>Marki</b> <span class="IPA" style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Polish" title="Help:IPA for Polish">[ˈmarkʲi]</a></span> is a city in central <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, just to the north-east of the Polish capital <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>.</p>
<p>Marki, a Warsaw suburb, was incorporated in 1967. While by Polish standards Marki is a relatively young town, with approximately 31,000 residents, it is one of the fastest growing cities in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masovia" title="Masovia">Masovian</a> province. Marki consists of three districts: Marki proper, Pustelnik, and Struga. Each district has its own post office, with an assigned zip code.</p>
<p>Marki has been long associated with industry, and while manufacturing still takes place, the service sector is booming. Marki has an extensive public education system served with six elementary schools, two preschools, and high school/trade school. While there is currently no hospital, there are a few public and private clinics available to the residents of the town.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marki is surrounded by forests, and pastures. Tourist attractions include numerous man-made ponds, lakes, and hiking trails. Marki is home to two hotels, one in Marki and one in Struga. North-east of Marki, there is a 100-metre high mast used for radio relay links by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telekomunikacja_Polska" title="Telekomunikacja Polska">Telekomunikacja Polska</a> and Centertel.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mikalojus_Konstantinas_.C4.8Ciurlionis">Mikalojus Konstantinas Čiurlionis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mikalojus Konstantinas Čiurlionis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On Christmas Eve of 1909, <a href="/wiki/Lithuanians" title="Lithuanians">Lithuanian</a> <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painter</a> and composer <a href="/wiki/Mikalojus_Konstantinas_%C4%8Ciurlionis" title="Mikalojus Konstantinas Čiurlionis">Mikalojus Konstantinas Čiurlionis</a> fell into a profound depression and at the beginning of 1910 was hospitalized in a psychiatric hospital "Czerwony Dwór" (Red Manor) in Pustelnik, now incorporated into Marki. While a patient there he died of pneumonia in 1911 at 35 years of age. A plaque on the side of this building commemorates the passing of one of the most influential figures of modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lithuanian_culture" title="Lithuanian culture">Lithuanian culture</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marecka_Kolej_Dojazdowa">Marecka Kolej Dojazdowa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marecka Kolej Dojazdowa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Marecka_Kolej_Dojazdowa" title="Marecka Kolej Dojazdowa">Marecka Kolej Dojazdowa</a> (English: Marki Commuter Railway) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narrow_gauge_railway" title="Narrow gauge railway">narrow gauge railway</a> in <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> connecting <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a> with Marki and <a href="/wiki/Radzymin" title="Radzymin">Radzymin</a> active from 1896 to 1974.</p>
