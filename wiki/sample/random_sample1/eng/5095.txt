<p><b>Martin Picandet</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[maʁtɛ̃ pikɑ̃dɛ]</a></span>; born 22 September 1976),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mtv.com-1">[1]</a></sup> better known by his <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> <b>Martin Solveig</b>, is a French DJ, singer, songwriter and record producer. He also hosts a weekly radio show called <i>C'est La Vie</i> on radio stations worldwide, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FG_DJ_Radio" title="FG DJ Radio">FG DJ Radio</a> in his homeland. His label is called Mixture Stereophonic. Solveig had been ranked number 29 in the 2011 <i><a href="/wiki/DJ_Magazine" title="DJ Magazine">DJ Mag</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He has collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Dragonette" title="Dragonette">Dragonette</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kele_Okereke" title="Kele Okereke">Kele</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bloc_Party" title="Bloc Party">Bloc Party</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Solveig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a child, Martin Solveig joined the choir of The Paris Boys Choir also known in France as <a href="/wiki/Petits_chanteurs_de_Sainte-Croix_de_Neuilly" title="Petits chanteurs de Sainte-Croix de Neuilly">Les petits chanteurs de Sainte-Croix de Neuilly</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> where he had a classical music training and became soprano soloist under the direction of François Polgár.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At the age of 18, he became a DJ and then started to work in famous Parisian nightclubs: L'Enfer, <a href="/wiki/Le_Queen" title="Le Queen">Le Queen</a>, Les Bains Douches.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Solveig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002.E2.80.9307:_Sur_la_Terre_and_Hedonist">2002–07: <i>Sur la Terre</i> and <i>Hedonist</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Solveig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2002–07: Sur la Terre and Hedonist">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 17 June 2002, Solveig released his debut studio album <i>Sur la Terre</i>, the album failed to chart. The album includes the singles "Madan", "Rocking Music" and "I'm a Good Man". On 12 September 2005, he released his second studio album <i>Hedonist</i>, the album peaked to number 43 on the <a href="/wiki/Syndicat_National_de_l%27%C3%89dition_Phonographique" title="Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique">French Albums Chart</a>. The album includes the singles "Everybody", "Jealousy", "Something Better" and "Rejection".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008.E2.80.9309:_C.27est_la_Vie">2008–09: <i>C'est la Vie</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Solveig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2008–09: C'est la Vie">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/C%27est_la_Vie_(Martin_Solveig_album)" title="C'est la Vie (Martin Solveig album)">C'est la Vie (Martin Solveig album)</a></div>
<p>On 2 June 2008, he released his third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/C%27est_la_Vie_(Martin_Solveig_album)" title="C'est la Vie (Martin Solveig album)">C'est la Vie</a></i>, the album peaked to number 16 on the <a href="/wiki/Syndicat_National_de_l%27%C3%89dition_Phonographique" title="Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique">French Albums Chart</a>. "C'est la Vie" was released as the lead single from the album on 21 January 2008. "<a href="/wiki/I_Want_You_(Martin_Solveig_song)" title="I Want You (Martin Solveig song)">I Want You</a>" was released as the second single from the album on 15 September 2008. "One 2.3 Four" was released as the third single from the album on 22 December 2008. "<a href="/wiki/Boys_%26_Girls_(Martin_Solveig_song)" title="Boys &amp; Girls (Martin Solveig song)">Boys &amp; Girls</a>" was released as the fourth single from the album on 28 September 2009.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9312:_Smash">2010–12: <i>Smash</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Solveig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–12: Smash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Smash_(Martin_Solveig_album)" title="Smash (Martin Solveig album)">Smash (Martin Solveig album)</a></div>
<p>On 6 September 2010, he released the single "<a href="/wiki/Hello_(Martin_Solveig_song)" title="Hello (Martin Solveig song)">Hello</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Dragonette" title="Dragonette">Dragonette</a> as the lead single from his fifth studio album. It is Solveig's most successful single to date, peaking at number one in <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> (<a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a>), <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, while charting within the top 10 in ten other countries. The song also peaked to number 13 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. "<a href="/wiki/Ready_2_Go" title="Ready 2 Go">Ready 2 Go</a>" was released as the second single from the album on 28 March 2011. He released his fifth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Smash_(Martin_Solveig_album)" title="Smash (Martin Solveig album)">Smash</a></i> on 6 June 2011, peaking at number 18 on the <a href="/wiki/Syndicat_National_de_l%27%C3%89dition_Phonographique" title="Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique">French Albums Chart</a>. In 2011, Solveig was officially named as one of the producers for <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>'s new studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/MDNA_(album)" title="MDNA (album)">MDNA</a></i>. On 24 October 2011, "<a href="/wiki/Big_in_Japan_(Martin_Solveig_song)" title="Big in Japan (Martin Solveig song)">Big in Japan</a>" was released as the third single from the album. On 2 April 2012, "<a href="/wiki/The_Night_Out" title="The Night Out">The Night Out</a>" was released as the fourth single from the album. Solveig was also made the in house DJ for the 2012 MTV Movie Awards where he paid homage to the classic themes from movies such as <a href="/wiki/Pulp_Fiction" title="Pulp Fiction">Pulp Fiction</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present">2013–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Solveig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 28 May 2013, Solveig released the single "<a href="/wiki/Hey_Now_(Martin_Solveig_song)" title="Hey Now (Martin Solveig song)">Hey Now</a>" with <a href="/wiki/The_Cataracs" title="The Cataracs">The Cataracs</a> featuring vocals from Kyle, peaking at number 55 on the <a href="/wiki/Syndicat_National_de_l%27%C3%89dition_Phonographique" title="Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique">French Singles Chart</a>. The song has also charted in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. On 6 January 2014, he released the single "Blow" with Laidback Luke. The song charted in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. On 23 February 2015, he released the single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intoxicated_(Martin_Solveig_song)" title="Intoxicated (Martin Solveig song)">Intoxicated</a>" with GTA. The song has peaked to number 15 on the <a href="/wiki/Syndicat_National_de_l%27%C3%89dition_Phonographique" title="Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique">French Singles Chart</a>. On 6 July 2015, he released the single "<a href="/wiki/%2B1_(song)" title="+1 (song)">+1</a>" featuring vocals from Sam White. In 2016, Solveig released the single "Do It Right" with Australian singer <a href="/wiki/Tkay_Maidza" title="Tkay Maidza">Tkay Maidza</a>.</p>
