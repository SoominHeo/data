<p><b>Martí Riverola Bataller</b> (born 26 January 1991) is a Spanish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for Italian club <a href="/wiki/Foggia_Calcio" title="Foggia Calcio">Foggia Calcio</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">central midfielder</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mart%C3%AD_Riverola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Barcelona">Barcelona</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mart%C3%AD_Riverola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Barcelona">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, Riverola joined <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>'s youth academy in 1999 at the age of 8, and started his career as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a>, being reconverted into a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for the 2004–05 season. In spite of this change he retained his goalscoring touch, being the under-19's second top scorer in 2010 with 13 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 2009, still registered with the juniors, Riverola made his debut for Barcelona's <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona_B" title="FC Barcelona B">B-team</a>, in a 2–0 away win against neighbours <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol_B" title="RCD Espanyol B">RCD Espanyol B</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The reserves eventually <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="2009–10 Segunda División B">returned</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">second division</a> after 11 years, but the player only appeared once more in the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2011, Riverola joined <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vitesse_Arnhem" title="Vitesse Arnhem">Vitesse Arnhem</a> on a six-month-long loan, as the Dutch were managed by countryman <a href="/wiki/Albert_Ferrer" title="Albert Ferrer">Albert Ferrer</a>, himself a former Barcelona player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In July he returned to Barcelona and its reserves, alongside <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Cuenca" title="Isaac Cuenca">Isaac Cuenca</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and scored five goals in 32 games throughout <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2011–12 Segunda División">the campaign</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> to help the B's finish eighth.</p>
<p>On 6 December 2011, Riverola made his first and only official appearance for the main squad, coming on as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> for <a href="/wiki/Sergi_Roberto" title="Sergi Roberto">Sergi Roberto</a> in the dying minutes of a <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League">4–0 home success</a> over <a href="/wiki/FC_BATE_Borisov" title="FC BATE Borisov">FC BATE Borisov</a> for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bologna">Bologna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mart%C3%AD_Riverola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bologna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 7 June 2012, Riverola was presented as player of <a href="/wiki/Bologna_F.C._1909" title="Bologna F.C. 1909">Bologna F.C. 1909</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> having already agreed to a four-year contract in late January<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>– FIFA later ordered the Italian side to pay Barcelona €535,000 as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Training_compensation" title="Training compensation">training compensation</a>, although he was a free agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In August of the following year, he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a> in his country's second level<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and, in January 2015, in the same predicament, joined <a href="/wiki/SC_Rheindorf_Altach" title="SC Rheindorf Altach">SC Rheindorf Altach</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Football_Bundesliga" title="Austrian Football Bundesliga">Austrian Football Bundesliga</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his spell at the <i><a href="/wiki/Stadio_Renato_Dall%27Ara" title="Stadio Renato Dall'Ara">Stadio Renato Dall'Ara</a></i>, Riverola only appeared in three competitive games, his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> input consisting of 50 minutes in a 1–2 loss at <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a> on 20 January 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foggia">Foggia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mart%C3%AD_Riverola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Foggia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 17 July 2015, Riverola was signed by <a href="/wiki/Foggia_Calcio" title="Foggia Calcio">Foggia Calcio</a> on a two-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
