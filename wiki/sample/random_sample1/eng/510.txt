<p><b><a href="/wiki/Taekwondo" title="Taekwondo">Taekwondo</a></b> at the <b><a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a> took place from 17 to 20 August at the <a href="/wiki/Carioca_Arena_3" title="Carioca Arena 3">Carioca Arena 3</a> inside the <a href="/wiki/Barra_Olympic_Park" title="Barra Olympic Park">Barra Olympic Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barra_da_Tijuca" title="Barra da Tijuca">Barra da Tijuca</a>. Around 128 taekwondo fighters competed in eight weight categories; four for men, and four for women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualification">Qualification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taekwondo_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Taekwondo_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Qualification" title="Taekwondo at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Qualification">Taekwondo at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Qualification</a></div>
<p>Taekwondo competition at these Games featured a total of 128 athletes, 64 in each gender, and 16 in each of the eight weight categories. Each <a href="/wiki/National_Olympic_Committee" title="National Olympic Committee">National Olympic Committee</a> (NOC) was allowed to enter up to a maximum of eight competitors, four of each gender, based on the <a href="/wiki/World_Taekwondo_Federation" title="World Taekwondo Federation">World Taekwondo Federation</a> (WTF) Olympic rankings, such that an athlete per NOC must be among the top six in each weight category. If an NOC had qualified only two female and male athletes through ranking, it could not participate in the respective Continental Qualification Tournament unless it had relinquishing the places obtained through ranking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wtf-qual_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-wtf-qual-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Four places had been reserved to the host nation <a href="/wiki/Brazil_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="Brazil at the 2016 Summer Olympics">Brazil</a>, and another four had been invited by the Tripartite Commission. The remaining 120 places were allocated through a qualification process in which athletes had won quota place for their respective NOC. 48 taekwondo fighters, 24 in each gender and the top 6 in each weight category, were eligible to compete through the ITTF Olympic rankings, while the rest through the five Continental Qualification Tournaments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wtf-qual_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-wtf-qual-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>If an NOC having qualified through a Qualification Tournament relinquishing a quota place, it would be allocated to the nation of the next highest placed athlete in the respective weight category of that tournament as long as the addition of the place did not exceed the maximum quota for that nation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wtf-qual_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-wtf-qual-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
