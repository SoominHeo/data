<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Gropius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gropius-tomb.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="939" data-file-width="1200" height="141" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Gropius-tomb.JPG/180px-Gropius-tomb.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Gropius-tomb.JPG/270px-Gropius-tomb.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Gropius-tomb.JPG/360px-Gropius-tomb.JPG 2x" width="180"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gropius-tomb.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Gropius' grave in Berlin</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Gropius studied at the <a href="/wiki/Bauakademie" title="Bauakademie">Bauakademie</a> in Berlin and after graduation worked as a private architect. He received artistic direction from <a href="/wiki/Karl_Friedrich_Schinkel" title="Karl Friedrich Schinkel">Karl Friedrich Schinkel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karl_B%C3%B6tticher" title="Karl Bötticher">Karl Bötticher</a> and continued his studies with prolonged trips through <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.</p>
<p>In 1856 Gropius was appointed to a professorship at the Academy of Applied Art and was later a member of the <a href="/wiki/Berlin-Brandenburg_Academy_of_Sciences_and_Humanities" title="Berlin-Brandenburg Academy of Sciences and Humanities">Berlin-Brandenburg Academy of Sciences and Humanities</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Austrian Academy of Sciences">Austrian Academy of Sciences</a>. Until his death he worked with <a href="/wiki/Heino_Schmieden" title="Heino Schmieden">Heino Schmieden</a> to develop <i>Fa. Gropius &amp; Schmieden</i>, one of the largest architecture firms in Berlin.</p>
<p>Martin Gropius was the great-uncle of architect and <a href="/wiki/Bauhaus" title="Bauhaus">Bauhaus</a> founder <a href="/wiki/Walter_Gropius" title="Walter Gropius">Walter Gropius</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Berlin_Martin_Gropiusbau_Front.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Berlin_Martin_Gropiusbau_Front.jpg/220px-Berlin_Martin_Gropiusbau_Front.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Berlin_Martin_Gropiusbau_Front.jpg/330px-Berlin_Martin_Gropiusbau_Front.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Berlin_Martin_Gropiusbau_Front.jpg/440px-Berlin_Martin_Gropiusbau_Front.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Berlin_Martin_Gropiusbau_Front.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Applied Arts Museum (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_Gropius_Bau" title="Martin Gropius Bau">Martin Gropius Bau</a>), Berlin, 1877-81</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The present-day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_Gropius_Bau" title="Martin Gropius Bau">Martin Gropius Bau</a> in Berlin was built in 1881 based on plans by Martin Gropius and Heino Schmieden as an applied art museum (the <a href="/wiki/Kunstgewerbemuseum_Berlin" title="Kunstgewerbemuseum Berlin">Kunstgewerbemuseum Berlin</a>). It was constructed in the style of the Italian <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> and has a central atrium. <a href="/wiki/Mosaic" title="Mosaic">Mosaics</a> with allegories from various ages and the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coats of arms</a> of German states decorate the spaces between windows. After <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> the Bau housed the Museum of Pre- and Early History as well as the oriental art collection. In the last weeks of the Second World War the building was bombed. Reconstruction of the building began in 1978 after it was placed under protection for historic preservation in 1966. Another restoration took place in 1999/2000. Today the Martin Gropius Bau is an important space for special exhibits of all kinds.</p>
<p>Martin Gropius is buried at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Friedh%C3%B6fe_vor_dem_Halleschen_Tor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Friedhöfe vor dem Halleschen Tor (page does not exist)">Dreifaltigkeitsfriedhof 2</a> in the Kreuzberg neighborhood of Berlin.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Works">Works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Gropius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Along with representative buildings (e.g. the University in Kiel and the Gewandhaus in Leipzig), many clinics and hospitals were built in Berlin and Brandenburg based on Gropius's designs.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Martin_Gropius_Krankenhaus" title="Martin Gropius Krankenhaus">Martin Gropius Krankenhaus</a> (Neuro-Psychiatric Hospital) in Neustadt-<a href="/wiki/Eberswalde" title="Eberswalde">Eberswalde</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ungern-Sternberg" title="Ungern-Sternberg">Ungern-Sternberg</a> palace in <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a>, nowadays the main building of <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Estonian Academy of Sciences">Estonian Academy of Sciences</a> (1865–1868)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Friedrichshain" title="Friedrichshain">Friedrichshain</a> Hospital in Berlin (1868–74), with <a href="/wiki/Heino_Schmieden" title="Heino Schmieden">Heino Schmieden</a></li>
<li>Hospital in <a href="/wiki/Wiesbaden" title="Wiesbaden">Wiesbaden</a></li>
<li>University Building in <a href="/wiki/Kiel" title="Kiel">Kiel</a> (1873–76)</li>
<li>Military Hospital in <a href="/wiki/Tempelhof" title="Tempelhof">Tempelhof</a>, Berlin (1875–77)</li>
<li>Manor House in <a href="/wiki/Neuruppin" title="Neuruppin">Neuruppin</a>-Gentzrode (1876–1877)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Martin-Gropius-Bau" title="Martin-Gropius-Bau">Applied Art Museum</a> (Martin Gropius Bau) in <a href="/wiki/Kreuzberg" title="Kreuzberg">Kreuzberg</a>, Berlin (1877–81), with Heino Schmieden</li>
<li>Second <a href="/wiki/Gewandhaus" title="Gewandhaus">Gewandhaus</a> in <a href="/wiki/Leipzig" title="Leipzig">Leipzig</a> (1882–84), completed by Heino Schmieden after Gropius's death.</li>
<li>Old Library at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Greifswald" title="University of Greifswald">University of Greifswald</a></li>
<li>Bureau of Mines in <a href="/wiki/Saarbr%C3%BCcken" title="Saarbrücken">Saarbrücken</a> (1877–1880)</li>
</ul>
