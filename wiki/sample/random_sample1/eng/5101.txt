<p><b>Martin Max</b> (born 7 August 1968) is a retired German <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>, who played as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>
<p>One of the oldest winners of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fu%C3%9Fball-Bundesliga" title="Fußball-Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>'s top scorer crown, at the age of 32 and 34, he represented four teams in his country of adoption.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Max&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Max&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tarnowskie_Gory" title="Tarnowskie Gory">Tarnowskie Gory</a> in <a href="/wiki/Upper_Silesia" title="Upper Silesia">Upper Silesia</a>, Martin Max started to play football in the youth of <i>Rodło Górniki Bytom</i> and in 1982 he emigrated with his parents as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aussiedler" title="Aussiedler">ethnic Germans</a> (<i>Aussiedler</i>) from Poland and went to <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, where the family settled in <a href="/wiki/Recklinghausen" title="Recklinghausen">Recklinghausen</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruhrgebiet" title="Ruhrgebiet">Ruhr region</a> (Ruhrgebiet). In Recklinghausen, he joined the junior from <i>Blau Weiß Post Recklinghausen</i> and 1985 he transferred to the offspring of the <i>1. FC Recklinghausen</i>.</p>
<p>At Schalke, Max revived his career, netting 23 goals in his first two seasons combined, adding three in the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_UEFA_Cup" title="1996–97 UEFA Cup">victorious</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> campaign, and his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout_(association_football)" title="Penalty shootout (association football)">penalty shootout</a> attempt in <a href="/wiki/1997_UEFA_Cup_Final" title="1997 UEFA Cup Final">the final</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">F.C. Internazionale Milano</a>.</p>
<p>He joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TSV_1860_M%C3%BCnchen" title="TSV 1860 München">TSV 1860 München</a> in 1999, and was crowned league topscorer in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_Fu%C3%9Fball-Bundesliga" title="1999–2000 Fußball-Bundesliga">his first season</a>, with 19; in 2001–02, he added 18 for a second individual accolade, tied with <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1rcio_Amoroso" title="Márcio Amoroso">Márcio Amoroso</a>.</p>
<p>Already at 35, Max moved to <a href="/wiki/F.C._Hansa_Rostock" title="F.C. Hansa Rostock">F.C. Hansa Rostock</a>, and contributed massively to the former <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East Germany</a> side's comfortable league position, as he netted 20 goals and ranked third in the goal charts, retiring at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Fu%C3%9Fball-Bundesliga" title="2003–04 Fußball-Bundesliga">the season</a>'s end with a total of 396 matches, with 126 first division goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After retiring, Max began running a soccer camp for youths.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Max&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Courtesy of his stellar TSV performances, Max earned his only cap for <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>, on 17 April 2002 (aged almost 34), coming on as a substitute during the 84th minute in a 1–0 loss in a friendly in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gottlieb-Daimler-Stadion" title="Gottlieb-Daimler-Stadion">Gottlieb-Daimler-Stadion</a> (today <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz-Arena" title="Mercedes-Benz-Arena">Mercedes-Benz-Arena</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a> against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
