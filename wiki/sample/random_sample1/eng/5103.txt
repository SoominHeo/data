<p><b>Magdalena Johanna Maria "Marleen" Veldhuis</b> (born 29 June 1979) is a retired <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swimmer" title="Swimmer">swimmer</a> from the Netherlands. She was <a href="/wiki/List_of_world_records_in_swimming" title="List of world records in swimming">world record holder</a> in four events (one individual event and three relay events). Veldhuis won eight world championships gold medals and 20 European championships gold medals. In the Olympics, she won a bronze medal in London 2012 in the 50 m freestyle, as well as three relay medals: bronze in Athens 2004, gold in Beijing 2008, and silver in London 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Veldhuis was born on 29 June 1979 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Borne_(Overijssel)" title="Borne (Overijssel)">Borne</a> in the Netherlands. She was brought up in the eastern part of the Netherlands. Veldhuis had a daughter in June 2010, causing her to miss the <a href="/wiki/2010_European_Aquatics_Championships" title="2010 European Aquatics Championships">2010 European Aquatics Championships</a>, and she returned to swimming for the 2011 and 2012 seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Swimming_career">Swimming career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Swimming career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Marleen Veldhuis is a former <a href="/wiki/Water_polo" title="Water polo">water polo</a> player which she combined with swimming. After she decided to focus completely on swimming, Veldhuis made her international debut during the <a href="/wiki/2002_European_Aquatics_Championships" title="2002 European Aquatics Championships">2002 European Aquatics Championships</a> in Berlin, at the relatively late age of 23, where she won a bronze medal in the 4×100 m freestyle as part of a team with <a href="/wiki/Manon_van_Rooijen" title="Manon van Rooijen">Manon van Rooijen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chantal_Groot" title="Chantal Groot">Chantal Groot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wilma_van_Hofwegen" title="Wilma van Hofwegen">Wilma van Hofwegen</a>. At the end of the year, she competed in <a href="/wiki/Riesa" title="Riesa">Riesa</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Short_Course_Swimming_Championships_2002" title="European Short Course Swimming Championships 2002">European Short Course Swimming Championships 2002</a>. There she won bronze medals in both relays, 4×50 m freestyle and 4×50 m medley.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breakthrough">Breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2003 she joined Topzwemmen Amsterdam (TZA) to train as a full-time professional under the guidance of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fedor_Hes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fedor Hes (page does not exist)">Fedor Hes</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/2003_World_Aquatics_Championships" title="2003 World Aquatics Championships">2003 World Aquatics Championships</a> it turned out to be a great decision as she reached two individual finals in the 50 m freestyle ending 7th, and in the 100 m freestyle finishing 8th. In <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> she also reached the final in all relay events but did not win a medal. In December she participated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Short_Course_Swimming_Championships_2003" title="European Short Course Swimming Championships 2003">European Short Course Swimming Championships 2003</a> where she won her first international titles in the 50 m freestyle and the 4×50 m freestyle. In <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a> she also won silver in the 100 m freestyle and bronze in the 4×50 m medley. 2004 started off with the <a href="/wiki/2004_European_Aquatics_Championships" title="2004 European Aquatics Championships">2004 European Aquatics Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>. There Veldhuis won two silver medals in the 4×100 m freestyle, together with Chantal Groot, <a href="/wiki/Annabel_Kosten" title="Annabel Kosten">Annabel Kosten</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inge_Dekker" title="Inge Dekker">Inge Dekker</a>, and individually in the 100 m freestyle. At the end of the week there was a bronze medal in the 4×100 m medley relay alongside <a href="/wiki/Stefanie_Luiken" title="Stefanie Luiken">Stefanie Luiken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madelon_Baans" title="Madelon Baans">Madelon Baans</a> and Chantal Groot.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_Olympics">2004 Olympics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2004 Olympics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a> Veldhuis made her Olympic debut at the age of 25 and won the bronze medal in the 4×100 m freestyle, together with <a href="/wiki/Inge_de_Bruijn" title="Inge de Bruijn">Inge de Bruijn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inge_Dekker" title="Inge Dekker">Inge Dekker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chantal_Groot" title="Chantal Groot">Chantal Groot</a>. Her individual results at the Olympics were slightly disappointing with the 9th place in the 50 m freestyle and the 11th place in the 100 m freestyle. She was the anchor lady in the 4×100 m medley, she took over from de Bruijn who swam the butterfly leg, finishing 6th. At the end of 2004 she became <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Sportsman_of_the_year" title="Amsterdam Sportsman of the year">Amsterdam Sportswoman of the year</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Athens_season">Post-Athens season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-Athens season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2004 she avenged her failed individual Olympic campaign by winning the gold medal in the 50 m freestyle at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_FINA_Short_Course_World_Championships" title="2004 FINA Short Course World Championships">2004 FINA Short Course World Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">Indianapolis</a> by beating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Libby_Lenton" title="Libby Lenton">Libby Lenton</a> from Australia and <a href="/wiki/Therese_Alshammar" title="Therese Alshammar">Therese Alshammar</a> from Sweden. In the 100 m freestyle she received a bronze medal behind Lenton and <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Josefin_Lillhage" title="Josefin Lillhage">Josefin Lillhage</a>. In December she successfully defended her 50 m freestyle at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Short_Course_Swimming_Championships_2004" title="European Short Course Swimming Championships 2004">European Short Course Swimming Championships 2004</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> she also won gold in both 4×50 m freestyle and 4×50 m medley, there was a silver medal for Veldhuis in the 100 m freestyle behind French <a href="/wiki/Malia_Metella" title="Malia Metella">Malia Metella</a>. She achieved her best long-course result so far by winning the silver medal in the 50 m freestyle at the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Aquatics_Championships" title="2005 World Aquatics Championships">2005 World Aquatics Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, which was the only medal for her country during these World Championships. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Short_Course_Swimming_Championships_2005" title="European Short Course Swimming Championships 2005">European Short Course Swimming Championships 2005</a> were very successful for Veldhuis. In <a href="/wiki/Trieste" title="Trieste">Trieste</a> she defended all her titles from the year before, but now she won also the gold medal in the 100 m freestyle.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006">2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2006 alongside Chantal Groot, <a href="/wiki/Hinkelien_Schreuder" title="Hinkelien Schreuder">Hinkelien Schreuder</a> and Inge Dekker, Veldhuis set a world record and won the gold medal in the 4×100 m freestyle relay at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_FINA_Short_Course_World_Championships" title="2006 FINA Short Course World Championships">2006 FINA Short Course World Championships</a>. She failed to defend her 50 m freestyle title ending third. She finished second behind long-time rival Libby Lenton in the 100 m freestyle. In the summer she competed in the <a href="/wiki/2006_European_Aquatics_Championships" title="2006 European Aquatics Championships">2006 European Aquatics Championships</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, Hungary where she ended second in the 100 m freestyle, behind <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German's</a> <a href="/wiki/Britta_Steffen" title="Britta Steffen">Britta Steffen</a> who broke the world record, and third in the 50 m freestyle. In the 4×100 m freestyle relay she received a silver medal together with Chantal Groot, Inge Dekker and newcomer <a href="/wiki/Ranomi_Kromowidjojo" title="Ranomi Kromowidjojo">Ranomi Kromowidjojo</a>. After these Championships she split up with her coach, Fedor Hes, and started to work with <a href="/wiki/Jacco_Verhaeren" title="Jacco Verhaeren">Jacco Verhaeren</a>, who at the time was also working with triple Olympic champion <a href="/wiki/Pieter_van_den_Hoogenband" title="Pieter van den Hoogenband">Pieter van den Hoogenband</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a> she successfully defended her 50 m and 100 m freestyle titles at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Short_Course_Swimming_Championships_2006" title="European Short Course Swimming Championships 2006">European Short Course Swimming Championships 2006</a>, but lost her relay titles when her team ended second in the 4×50 m freestyle and did not start in the 4×50 m medley. Just before this championships she had bettered <a href="/wiki/Annemarie_Verstappen" title="Annemarie Verstappen">Annemarie Verstappen</a>'s long standing 200 m freestyle national record.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007">2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the spring of 2007 she took part in the <a href="/wiki/2007_World_Aquatics_Championships" title="2007 World Aquatics Championships">2007 World Aquatics Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, Australia. There she won the silver medal behind Libby Lenton. Veldhuis won the bronze medal in the 50 m freestyle despite being the favourite before the final. Alongside Inge Dekker, Ranomi Kromowidjojo, and another young talent <a href="/wiki/Femke_Heemskerk" title="Femke Heemskerk">Femke Heemskerk</a>, she won bronze in the 4×100 m freestyle relay. In November at the Berlin leg of the <a href="/wiki/2007_FINA_Swimming_World_Cup" title="2007 FINA Swimming World Cup">2007 FINA Swimming World Cup</a> series, Veldhuis broke Swede <a href="/wiki/Therese_Alshammar" title="Therese Alshammar">Therese Alshammar</a>'s 50 m freestyle world record. Alshammar's world record had stood at 23.59 since March 2000. The new standard is 23.58 seconds. One month later she qualified for the Beijing Olympics with personal bests in the 50 and 100 m freestyle during the Dutch Open Swim Cup 2007 and competed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Short_Course_Swimming_Championships_2007" title="European Short Course Swimming Championships 2007">European Short Course Swimming Championships 2007</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Debrecen" title="Debrecen">Debrecen</a> she won the 50 m freestyle for the fifth time in a row but lost her 100 m freestyle title to Britta Steffen due to a bad turn, finishing second. She also won gold in the 4×50 m freestyle. A few days after the tournament she was elected <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Sportsman_of_the_year" title="Dutch Sportsman of the year">Dutch Sportswoman of the Year</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spring_2008">Spring 2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Spring 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/2008_European_Aquatics_Championships" title="2008 European Aquatics Championships">2008 European Aquatics Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven" title="Eindhoven">Eindhoven</a>, she won the 100 m and 50 m freestyle. In the latter event she broke the eight-year-old world record by since 2004 retired <a href="/wiki/Inge_de_Bruijn" title="Inge de Bruijn">Inge de Bruijn</a> with a time of 24.09.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She also broke the 4×100 m freestyle relay world record, together with Inge Dekker, Ranomi Kromowidjojo and Femke Heemskerk, and won the gold medal. The new standard was 3:33.62.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Veldhuis also won a bronze medal in 4×100 m medley alongside <a href="/wiki/Hinkelien_Schreuder" title="Hinkelien Schreuder">Hinkelien Schreuder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jolijn_van_Valkengoed" title="Jolijn van Valkengoed">Jolijn van Valkengoed</a> and Inge Dekker. In April's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_FINA_Short_Course_World_Championships" title="2008 FINA Short Course World Championships">2008 FINA Short Course World Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> Veldhuis won four gold medals. Individually in the 50 m freestyle, lowering her own world record to 23.25, and the 100 m freestyle beating home favourite <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francesca_Halsall" title="Francesca Halsall">Francesca Halsall</a>. Furthermore, she won gold in the 4×200 m freestyle with Dekker, Heemskerk and Kromowidjojo, bettering the world record. With Hinkelien Schreuder replacing Kromowidjojo, the Dutch team also lowered their own world record in the 4×100 m freestyle.</p>

<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> she won the gold medal in the 4×100 m freestyle relay together with <a href="/wiki/Inge_Dekker" title="Inge Dekker">Inge Dekker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ranomi_Kromowidjojo" title="Ranomi Kromowidjojo">Ranomi Kromowidjojo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Femke_Heemskerk" title="Femke Heemskerk">Femke Heemskerk</a>, they swam just 0.14 seconds outside their own world record. Individually she did not achieve what she expected to, with the sixth place in the 100 m freestyle and the fifth in the 50 m freestyle.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fall_2008">Fall 2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Fall 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Veldhuis kicked off her new season at the Swim Cup Eindhoven 2008. She qualified for the 50 m and 100 m butterfly at the <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Aquatics_Championships" title="2009 World Aquatics Championships">2009 World Aquatics Championships</a>, for the 50 m and 100 m freestyle she had already qualified at the Olympics. The next week, she won four gold medals at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Short_Course_Swimming_Championships_2008" title="European Short Course Swimming Championships 2008">European Short Course Swimming Championships 2008</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rijeka" title="Rijeka">Rijeka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>, in the 50 m and 100 m freestyle and 4×50 m freestyle and 4×50 m medley. In the 100 m freestyle she lowered her own European record during the heats, she also finished fourth in the 100 m butterfly.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009">2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the Amsterdam Swim Cup Veldhuis broke the <a href="/wiki/World_record_progression_50_metres_butterfly" title="World record progression 50 metres butterfly">50 metre butterfly world record</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_record_progression_50_metres_freestyle" title="World record progression 50 metres freestyle">50 metre freestyle world record</a> on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Aquatics_Championships" title="2009 World Aquatics Championships">2009 World Aquatics Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> she won the gold medal in the 4×100 m freestyle alongside Inge Dekker, Ranomi Kromowidjojo and Femke Heemskerk.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011">2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011 Veldhuis won a gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Aquatics_Championships" title="2011 World Aquatics Championships">2011 World Aquatics Championships</a> as part of the 4 × 100 m freestyle relay alongside <a href="/wiki/Inge_Dekker" title="Inge Dekker">Inge Dekker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ranomi_Kromowidjojo" title="Ranomi Kromowidjojo">Ranomi Kromowidjojo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Femke_Heemskerk" title="Femke Heemskerk">Femke Heemskerk</a> in a time of 3:33.96.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marleen_Veldhuis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Swimming at the 2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> she won a bronze medal in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_50_metre_freestyle" title="Swimming at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's 50 metre freestyle">50 m freestyle</a> (her first Olympic medal in an individual event, in a time of 24.39) and a silver medal in the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_4_%C3%97_100_metre_freestyle_relay" title="Swimming at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's 4 × 100 metre freestyle relay">4 × 100 m freestyle</a>.</p>
