<p><b>Marie of Anjou</b> (14 October 1404 – 29 November 1463) was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_of_France" title="Queen of France">Queen of France</a> as the spouse of <a href="/wiki/Charles_VII_of_France" title="Charles VII of France">King Charles VII</a> from 1422 to 1461. She served as regent and presided over the council of state several times during the absence of the king.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marie_of_Anjou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marie was the eldest daughter of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_II_of_Anjou" title="Louis II of Anjou">Louis II of Anjou</a>, titular King of Naples, titular King of Sicily, and <a href="/wiki/Yolande_of_Aragon" title="Yolande of Aragon">Yolande of Aragon</a>, titular Queen of Aragon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Green190_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Green190-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Marie was betrothed to her second cousin Charles, fifth son of Charles VI of France and Isabeau of Bavaria, in 1413.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Green190_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Green190-2">[2]</a></sup> The wedding took place in April 1422 at <a href="/wiki/Bourges" title="Bourges">Bourges</a>. The wedding made her Queen of France, but as far as it is known, she was never crowned. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her spouse's victory in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hundred_Years_War" title="Hundred Years War">Hundred Years War</a> owed a great deal to the support he received from Marie's family, notably from her mother <a href="/wiki/Yolande_of_Aragon" title="Yolande of Aragon">Yolande of Aragon</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Queen">Queen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marie_of_Anjou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Queen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Queen Marie presided over the Council of state several times in the absence of the king, during which she had power of attorney as regent and signed acts in the position of "lieutenant of the king" (April 1434). <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She made several pilgrimages, such as Puy with the king in 1424, and Mount St Michel by herself in 1447.</p>
<p>Marie and Charles had fourteen children, but her spouse's affection was primarily directed towards his mistress, <a href="/wiki/Agn%C3%A8s_Sorel" title="Agnès Sorel">Agnès Sorel</a>, originally Marie's lady in waiting, who became official mistress to the king in 1444 and played a dominant role at court until her death in 1450, somewhat eclipsing the queen. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Blondel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Robert Blondel (page does not exist)">Robert Blondel</a> composed the allegorical Treatise of the <i>"Twelve Perils of Hell"</i> for queen Marie in 1455.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Queen_dowager">Queen dowager</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marie_of_Anjou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Queen dowager">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1461, Charles VII died and was succeeded by their son Louis XI, making Marie queen dowager. She was granted the Chateau of Amboise and the income from Brabant by her son.</p>
<p>During the winter of 1462-63, Marie of Anjou made a pilgrimage to St Jacques de Compostela. It has been speculated that she had a mission in Spain as secret ambassador for her son, due to the political situation at the time and the fact that she made the pilgrimage during winter time, when the roads were so bad that such trips were normally avoided if possible. She died at the age of 59 on 29 November 1463 at the Cistercian Abbaye de Chateliers-en-Poitou (<a href="/wiki/Poitou-Charentes" title="Poitou-Charentes">Poitou-Charentes</a> region) on her return. She is buried in the basilica of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint-Denis_Basilica" title="Saint-Denis Basilica">Saint-Denis</a> alongside her spouse.</p>
