<p><b>Marie Louise Coleiro Preca</b>, <small><a href="/wiki/National_Order_of_Merit_(Malta)" title="National Order of Merit (Malta)">KUOM</a></small> (born 7 December 1958) is a <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Maltese</a> politician and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_Malta" title="List of Presidents of Malta">9th</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_Malta" title="President of Malta">President of Malta</a> since her appointment on 4 April 2014. Previously, as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Malta)" title="Labour Party (Malta)">Labour Party</a>, she was a Member of Parliament in the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Malta" title="Parliament of Malta">Parliament of Malta</a> from 1998 to 2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and served as the Minister of the Family and Social Solidarity from 11 March 2013 to 29 March 2014.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marie_Louise_Coleiro_Preca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Qormi" title="Qormi">Qormi</a>, Coleiro Preca studied at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Malta" title="University of Malta">University of Malta</a> where she graduated with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">BA</a> in Legal and Humanistic Studies (International Studies) and a <a href="/wiki/Notary_public" title="Notary public">notary public</a> diploma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Malta)" title="Labour Party (Malta)">Labour Party</a>, Coleiro Preca served as a member of the National Executive, the Assistant General Secretary,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and as the General-Secretary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She was the only woman to have served in such a senior post of a Maltese political party.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In addition to these posts, Coleiro Preca was also a member of the National Bureau of Socialist Youths (now the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Youth_Forum" title="Labour Youth Forum">Labour Youth Forum</a>), President of the Women's Section of the Party (1996-2001), founding member of the <a href="/wiki/%C4%A0u%C5%BC%C3%A8_Ellul_Mercer" title="Ġużè Ellul Mercer">Ġużè Ellul Mercer</a> Foundation and publisher of the Party's weekly newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Il-Helsien" title="Il-Helsien">Il-Ħelsien</a></i> (now defunct).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>She served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">MP</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Malta" title="Parliament of Malta">Maltese Parliament</a> from 1998 to 2014. In the 2008 general election she was the first elected MP by the. As an Opposition MP Coleiro Preca served as Shadow Minister for Social Policy and as member of the Parliamentary Permanent Committee for Social Affairs beginning in 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Sant" title="Alfred Sant">Alfred Sant</a> resigned as Leader of the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Malta)" title="Labour Party (Malta)">Labour Party</a> in 2008, Coleiro Preca unsuccessfully contested the Leadership election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Presidency">Presidency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marie_Louise_Coleiro_Preca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Presidency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On 1 March 2014, Coleiro Preca accepted the nomination for President. Succeeding <a href="/wiki/George_Abela" title="George Abela">George Abela</a>, she was sworn in as President on 4 April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Coleiro Preca is the youngest person to assume the office of President, sworn in at the age of 55, and is the second woman to hold the post after <a href="/wiki/Agatha_Barbara" title="Agatha Barbara">Agatha Barbara</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014 Coleiro Preca announced the formation of <a href="/wiki/The_President%27s_Foundation_for_the_Wellbeing_of_Society" title="The President's Foundation for the Wellbeing of Society">The President's Foundation for the Wellbeing of Society</a>, a non-profit consultative and research entity advising the President of Malta on initiatives to help improve social inclusion and standards of living.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<li><span class="flagicon"><img alt="" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="1200" height="12" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/ae/Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom.svg/23px-Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/ae/Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom.svg/35px-Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/ae/Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom.svg/46px-Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png 2x" width="23"/> </span><a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>: Honorary Knight Grand Cross of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_St_Michael_and_St_George" title="Order of St Michael and St George">Order of St Michael and St George</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>In September 2016, President Coleiro Preca received the 'Agent of Change' award, conferred by <a href="/wiki/UN_Women" title="UN Women">UN Women</a>, in recognition of her work as a champion of gender equality and women's empowerment in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean_region" title="Mediterranean region">Mediterranean region</a>. UN Women executive director <a href="/wiki/Lakshmi_Puri" title="Lakshmi Puri">Lakshmi Puri</a> described Coleiro Preca as "a true champion for gender equality not just in Malta but in the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
