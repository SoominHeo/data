<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Marie_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marie Cavallier was born in Paris, France. She is the only child of Alain Cavallier and Françoise Grassiot (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Married_and_maiden_names" title="Married and maiden names">née</a> Moreau). She moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geneva,_Switzerland" title="Geneva, Switzerland">Geneva, Switzerland</a> with her mother following the divorce of her parents.</p>
<p>She has two older, maternal half-brothers: Benjamin and Gregory Grandet, both born in Switzerland; and two younger, paternal half-brothers: Charles and Edouard Cavallier, both born in Paris.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_career">Education and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Marie_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After her parents divorced, Marie was sent to the <a href="/wiki/Coll%C3%A8ge_Alpin_International_Beau_Soleil" title="Collège Alpin International Beau Soleil">Collège Alpin International Beau Soleil</a> boarding school in Switzerland <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.beausoleil.ch/s_en/" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>), where members of the Luxembourg ruling house have also studied. She attended <a href="/wiki/Babson_College" title="Babson College">Babson College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wellesley,_Massachusetts" title="Wellesley, Massachusetts">Wellesley, Massachusetts</a>, for a brief time, to study international business and economics and then went on to study economics in Genève. Marie earned a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> at <a href="/wiki/Marymount_Manhattan_College" title="Marymount Manhattan College">Marymount Manhattan College</a>. During her years in college, Marie worked for <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A9e_Lauder_Companies" title="Estée Lauder Companies">Estée Lauder</a>, as assistant to the public relations manager in 1994, and as an assistant to the managing director of ING Numismatic Group SA in Genève.</p>
<p>After graduating, she started working for DoubleClick Inc, an international advertising agency, in New York as international marketing coordinator. Back in France, she worked for advertising agency Media Marketing. She then worked for Reuters financial news agency Radianz in Switzerland, took a position with REInvest in Geneva and worked as executive secretary in ING Numismatic Group SA until the engagement.</p>
<p>Princess Marie's mother tongue is French. In addition she speaks English, Spanish and Italian. In connection to her wedding with <a href="/wiki/Prince_Joachim_of_Denmark" title="Prince Joachim of Denmark">Prince Joachim</a> she started taking lessons in Danish.</p>

<p>Marie first came to public attention when she was photographed on a private holiday in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avignon,_France" title="Avignon, France">Avignon, France</a>, with Prince Joachim in August 2005. She celebrated the New Year 2006/2007 with Prince Joachim, his former wife <a href="/wiki/Alexandra,_Countess_of_Frederiksborg" title="Alexandra, Countess of Frederiksborg">Alexandra</a>, their sons, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Nikolai_of_Denmark" title="Prince Nikolai of Denmark">Nikolai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Felix_of_Denmark" title="Prince Felix of Denmark">Felix</a>, as well as close friends.</p>
<p>In January 2007, Marie accompanied Prince Joachim and his children on a ski holiday in Switzerland. Later that year, Marie joined the royal family for Easter at <a href="/wiki/Marselisborg_Palace" title="Marselisborg Palace">Marselisborg Palace</a>, where she met Joachim's mother, <a href="/wiki/Margrethe_II_of_Denmark" title="Margrethe II of Denmark">Queen Margrethe II</a>, for the first time. Marie increasingly made weekend visits from Geneva to Denmark in 2007.</p>
<p>On 3 October 2007, it was officially announced that Marie Cavallier was engaged to Prince Joachim.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On 21 November 2007, the Royal Court announced that the wedding would be held on 24 May 2008.</p>
<p>The first wedding took place on 24 May 2008 in Møgeltønder Church. Upon her marriage to Joachim, Marie's title is <b>Her Royal Highness Princess Marie of Denmark, Countess of Monpezat</b>. Marie, who had been a French citizen and a member of the Roman Catholic Church, became, in connection with the marriage, a Danish citizen and a member of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Denmark" title="Church of Denmark">Evangelical-Lutheran Church of Denmark</a>. Marie's wedding gown was designed by Spanish-Italian fashion house Arasa Morelli,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and was sewn by a Danish woman working for the company.</p>
<p>The couple have two children:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prince_Henrik_of_Denmark" title="Prince Henrik of Denmark">Prince <i>Henrik</i> Carl Joachim Alain</a> (born 4 May 2009)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Princess_Athena_of_Denmark" title="Princess Athena of Denmark">Princess <i>Athena</i> Marguerite Françoise Marie</a> (born 24 January 2012)</li>
</ul>
<p>Marie is also the stepmother to Prince Joachim's sons from his first marriage, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Nikolai_of_Denmark" title="Prince Nikolai of Denmark">Prince Nikolai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Felix_of_Denmark" title="Prince Felix of Denmark">Prince Felix</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_role">Public role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Marie_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Public role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marie started her role as Princess of Denmark shortly after her wedding, while attending with her husband events and activities from his patronages. She gave an interview to Danish magazine <i>Billed Bladet</i>, in which she explained that she was in the process of making a decision on her areas of interest in September 2008 and mentioned her desire to have children.</p>
<p>Her first engagement and trip abroad was to <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a> on 28 October 2008, when she handed out <a href="/wiki/Lego" title="Lego">Lego</a> toys to orphanage children in <a href="/wiki/Rabat" title="Rabat">Rabat</a>. After this trip abroad, the Princess accompanied Prince Joachim to Russia.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Patronages">Patronages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Marie_of_Denmark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Patronages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marie's first patronage came shortly after her son <a href="/wiki/Prince_Henrik_of_Denmark" title="Prince Henrik of Denmark">Prince Henrik of Denmark</a>'s birth, when her patronage of Tønder Festival was announced. Tønder Festival is an international folk music festival near her official residence Shackenborg Manor in Møgeltønder, where the Princess resides with her family. Shortly afterwards, Marie took over one of the Prince Consort's duties as Patron of the Annual Literature Prize that awards an annual prize for French-language literature in Copenhagen. Marie also took the patronage of ambassador to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syddansk_Universitet" title="Syddansk Universitet">Syddansk Universitet</a> in Odense in September 2009.</p>
<p>Princess Marie was presented on 17 November 2009 as patron of "The Danish National Commission for <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>.</p>
<p>The Princess' third official trip was to Hong Kong and China together with Prince Joachim in November 2009.</p>
<p>In January 2010, the Danish Ski Federation named Princess Marie official patron.</p>
<p>In March 2010, Marie undertook her fourth official visit abroad to Mexico City with Prince Joachim.</p>
<p>In April 2010, Marie paid a working visit to the UNESCO Headquarters in Paris as patron for the organisation.</p>
<p>She is a patron of the Danish Epilepsy Association and Kattegatcentret.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is a patron of the The AIDS Foundation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She is a patron of Autism Denmark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
