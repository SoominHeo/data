<p><b>Marinos Satsias</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el">Μαρίνος Σατσιάς</span>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cypriot</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who spent his entire 19-year career playing for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APOEL_F.C." title="APOEL F.C.">APOEL</a> in Cyprus.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marinos_Satsias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Satsias was a right-footed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defensive_midfielder" title="Defensive midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>. He made his debut for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APOEL_F.C." title="APOEL F.C.">APOEL</a> in 1995 at the age of seventeen. He was known for his passion and discipline in the game.</p>
<p>During his career with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APOEL_F.C." title="APOEL F.C.">APOEL</a>, Marinos won 8 <a href="/wiki/Cypriot_First_Division" title="Cypriot First Division">Championships</a>, 6 <a href="/wiki/Cypriot_Cup" title="Cypriot Cup">Cups</a>, 8 <a href="/wiki/Cypriot_Super_Cup" title="Cypriot Super Cup">Super Cups</a> and appeared in three official <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Champions_League_group_stage" title="2009–10 UEFA Champions League group stage">2009–10 UEFA Champions League group stage</a> matches in APOEL's first <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> participation. He also appeared in one <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League">2011–12 UEFA Champions League</a> match for APOEL (against <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> at <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Bernab%C3%A9u_Stadium" title="Santiago Bernabéu Stadium">Santiago Bernabéu Stadium</a>), in the club's surprising run to the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League_knockout_phase#Quarter-finals" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League knockout phase">quarter-finals</a> of the competition.</p>
<p>In his last season (2013–14) as a player, APOEL's captain managed to lift all the season's trophies in Cyprus, the <a href="/wiki/Cypriot_First_Division" title="Cypriot First Division">Cypriot League</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Cypriot_Cup" title="2013–14 Cypriot Cup">Cypriot Cup</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Cypriot_Super_Cup" title="Cypriot Super Cup">Cypriot Super Cup</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> retiring as a proud domestic <a href="/wiki/Treble_(association_football)" title="Treble (association football)">treble</a> winner.</p>
