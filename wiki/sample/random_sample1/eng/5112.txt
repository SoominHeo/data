<p>Countess <b>Marisa Allasio</b> (born <b>Maria Luisa Lucia Allasio</b> on 14 July 1936) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a> of the 1950s. She appeared in nearly 20 films between 1952 and 1958.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was considered a typical sex symbol during her film career,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> which she abandoned in 1958 after marrying Count Pier Francesco Calvi di Bergolo (born 22 December 1933), son of <a href="/wiki/Princess_Yolanda_of_Savoy" title="Princess Yolanda of Savoy">Princess Iolanda di Savoia</a>, first-born of <a href="/wiki/Victor_Emmanuel_III_of_Italy" title="Victor Emmanuel III of Italy">Vittorio Emanuele III</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elena_of_Montenegro" title="Elena of Montenegro">Elena del Montenegro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> They had two children, Carlo Giorgio Dmitri Drago Maria Laetitia dei Conti Calvi di Bergolo (born 1959, <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>) and Anda Federica Angelica Maria dei Conti Calvi di Bergolo (born 1962, Rome).</p>
