<p><b>Marie-Chantal, Crown Princess of Greece, Princess of Denmark</b> (<i>née</i> <b>Miller</b>; born 17 September 1968 in London, England), is the wife of <a href="/wiki/Pavlos,_Crown_Prince_of_Greece" title="Pavlos, Crown Prince of Greece">Pavlos, Crown Prince of Greece</a>, son of the deposed <a href="/wiki/Constantine_II_of_Greece" title="Constantine II of Greece">King Constantine II of Greece</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marie-Chantal,_Crown_Princess_of_Greece&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marie-Chantal Miller was born in London, England, to Marie Chantal Pesantes, an Ecuadorian, and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Warren_Miller" title="Robert Warren Miller">Robert Warren Miller</a>, an American-born British businessman. She has an older sister, <a href="/wiki/Pia_Getty" title="Pia Getty">Pia</a>, ex-wife of Christopher <a href="/wiki/Getty_family" title="Getty family">Getty</a>, and a younger sister, <a href="/wiki/Alexandra_von_F%C3%BCrstenberg" title="Alexandra von Fürstenberg">Alexandra</a>, ex-wife of Prince <a href="/wiki/Alexander_von_F%C3%BCrstenberg" title="Alexander von Fürstenberg">Alexander von Fürstenberg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HttpwwwgreekroyalfamilyorgprofilecfmItemIDgetfamily_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-HttpwwwgreekroyalfamilyorgprofilecfmItemIDgetfamily-1">[1]</a></sup> She was baptized in the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a> at <a href="/wiki/St._Patrick%27s_Cathedral_(Manhattan)" title="St. Patrick's Cathedral (Manhattan)">St. Patrick's Cathedral</a> in New York City by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_O%27Connor_(archbishop_of_New_York)" title="John O'Connor (archbishop of New York)">Cardinal John O’Connor, Archbishop of New York</a> with <a href="/wiki/Donatella_Flick" title="Donatella Flick">Princess Donatella Missikoff Flick</a> as her godmother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Marie-Chantal was raised in Hong Kong where she attended <a href="/wiki/Peak_School" title="Peak School">The Peak School</a> until she was 9 years-old when she went to board at <a href="/wiki/Institut_Le_Rosey" title="Institut Le Rosey">Institut Le Rosey</a> in Switzerland. In 1982 she transferred to the Ecole Active Bilingue in Paris until her senior year which she took at <a href="/wiki/Masters_School" title="Masters School">The Masters School</a> in New York which was then girls only. After graduating she attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_of_Arts" title="Academy of Arts">Academy of Arts</a> for one year. She began a degree in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_Art" title="History of Art">History of Art</a> at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a> in 1993 but dropped out a year later after <a href="/wiki/Pavlos,_Crown_Prince_of_Greece" title="Pavlos, Crown Prince of Greece">Pavlos, Crown Prince of Greece</a>, proposed to her on a skiing holiday in <a href="/wiki/Gstaad" title="Gstaad">Gstaad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, at Christmas.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marie-Chantal,_Crown_Princess_of_Greece&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marie-Chantal is the Founder and Creative Director of Marie-Chantal, an international childrenswear brand founded in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mariechantal_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mariechantal-3">[3]</a></sup> She is a trustee of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Arts" title="Royal Academy of Arts">Royal Academy Trust</a> and a board director of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Warren_Miller#Duty_Free_Shoppers_.28DFS.29" title="Robert Warren Miller">DFS Group Ltd</a>. DFS is a luxury retailer catering to the travelling public and is a division of LVMH.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_issue">Marriage and issue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marie-Chantal,_Crown_Princess_of_Greece&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriage and issue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_Pavlos,_Crown_Prince_of_Greece,_and_Marie-Chantal_Miller" title="Wedding of Pavlos, Crown Prince of Greece, and Marie-Chantal Miller">Wedding of Pavlos, Crown Prince of Greece, and Marie-Chantal Miller</a></div>
<p>In 1995, Marie-Chantal became engaged to <a href="/wiki/Pavlos,_Crown_Prince_of_Greece" title="Pavlos, Crown Prince of Greece">Pavlos, Crown Prince of Greece</a>, son of the deposed <a href="/wiki/Constantine_II_of_Greece" title="Constantine II of Greece">King Constantine II</a>. She converted from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</a> to <a href="/wiki/Greek_Orthodox_Church" title="Greek Orthodox Church">Greek Orthodoxy</a> on 22 May 1995 in a private <a href="/wiki/Chrismation" title="Chrismation">Chrismation</a> ceremony held at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Chapel" title="St Paul's Chapel">St Paul's Chapel</a> in New York. Her engagement ring was a <a href="/wiki/Cabochon" title="Cabochon">cabochon</a>-cut sapphire and heart-shaped diamond engagement ring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The wedding was planned by Lady Elizabeth Anson and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Isabell" title="Robert Isabell">Robert Isabell</a>. Marie-Chantal wore a <a href="/wiki/Valentino_SpA" title="Valentino SpA">Valentino</a> dress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The ceremony was held on 1 July 1995 at <a href="/wiki/St_Sophia%27s_Cathedral,_London" title="St Sophia's Cathedral, London">St Sophia's Cathedral</a> in London. Attendees included many members of European royal families and ten members of the Greek political party <a href="/wiki/New_Democracy_(Greece)" title="New Democracy (Greece)">New Democracy</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<li><b>Prince Constantine-Alexios of Greece and Denmark</b> (named after his grandfather; born 29 October 1998 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cornell_Medical_Center" title="Cornell Medical Center">Cornell Medical Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>)</li>
<li><b>Prince Achileas Andreas of Greece and Denmark</b> (born on 12 August 2000 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cornell_Medical_Center" title="Cornell Medical Center">Cornell Medical Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>)</li>
<li><b>Prince Odysseus-Kimon of Greece and Denmark</b> (born on 17 September 2004 in <a href="/wiki/Portland_Hospital" title="Portland Hospital">Portland Hospital</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>)</li>
<li><b>Prince Aristidis-Stavros of Greece and Denmark</b> (born on 29 June 2008 in <a href="/wiki/Cedars-Sinai_Medical_Center" title="Cedars-Sinai Medical Center">Cedars-Sinai Medical Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>)</li>
