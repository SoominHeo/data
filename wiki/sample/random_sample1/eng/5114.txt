<p><b>Mariestella Cañedo Racal</b> (born September 22, 1997; better known as <b>Maris Racal</b>) is a Filipina actress, singer and dancer who won 2nd Big Placer on <a href="/wiki/Pinoy_Big_Brother:_All_In" title="Pinoy Big Brother: All In">Pinoy Big Brother (PBB) All In</a>. She entered the PBB House at the age of 16 and was dubbed as the "Singing Sunshine ng Davao".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She gained a fan base known as "Mariestellers" during the course of the show.</p>
<p>Maris grew up in a musically inclined family and learned to sing and play instruments at a young age. She can play the guitar, piano, ukulele and beatbox, some of which she learned on her own by watching various videos online. Aside from being a musician and singer, she also excels in painting, song/poem writing, acting and dancesports. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After her stint in PBB, Maris became a <a href="/wiki/Star_Magic" title="Star Magic">Star Magic</a> artist. In February 2016, Maris was introduced as one of the members of all-girl dance group, Girltrends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 22, 2017, Maris was officially launched as one of the members of <a href="/wiki/ASAP_(variety_show)" title="ASAP (variety show)">ASAP's</a> newest girl squad, BFF5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maris_Racal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After PBB, Maris Racal was launched as part of Star Magic Circle 2014 and had been quickly molded as a budding actress, starting off on TV show <a href="/wiki/Hawak_Kamay_(TV_series)" title="Hawak Kamay (TV series)">Hawak Kamay</a> followed by several more stints on TV shows like <a href="/wiki/Maalaala_Mo_Kaya" title="Maalaala Mo Kaya">Maalaala Mo Kaya</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ipaglaban_Mo" title="Ipaglaban Mo">Ipaglaban Mo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oh_My_G!" title="Oh My G!">Oh My G!</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ningning" title="Ningning">Ningning</a>, and on films like <a href="/wiki/Halik_sa_Hangin" title="Halik sa Hangin">Halik sa Hangin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Just_The_Way_You_Are" title="Just The Way You Are">Just The Way You Are</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Breakup_Playlist" title="The Breakup Playlist">The Breakup Playlist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She also starred with Roberto "Fourth" Solomon Pagotan IV in the 'Dear MOR' episode, the “Kate and Red Story”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Maris released her first single "Tanong Mo Sa Bituin" in April 2015 as part of <a href="/wiki/Star_Music" title="Star Music">Star Record</a>'s compilation album <a href="/wiki/2015_in_Philippine_music" title="2015 in Philippine music">"OPM Fresh"</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2015, Maris was cast in her first main lead role on big screen as Elena in Tandem Entertainment's film "Stars Versus Me", starring also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manolo_Pedrosa" title="Manolo Pedrosa">Manolo Pedrosa</a> as her leading man.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>By February 2016, Maris was introduced as one of the members of all-girl dance group "Girltrends" in the noontime show <a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_(variety_show)" title="It's Showtime (variety show)">It's Showtime</a>. She was then tagged as "Magnificent Girltrend Maris" and became the group's Team Young member.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Maris subsequently played Jenny Bonanza in the teleserye <a href="/wiki/We_Will_Survive" title="We Will Survive">We Will Survive</a> later in the month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2016, Maris performed in her first OneMusicPH's Digital Concert dubbed as "Double Hearts" (together with co-performers <a href="/wiki/Loisa_Andalio" title="Loisa Andalio">Loisa Andalio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sue_Ramirez" title="Sue Ramirez">Sue Ramirez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kristel_Fulgar" title="Kristel Fulgar">Kristel Fulgar</a>), where she debuted her original song Take It All Away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In the same month, Maris appeared in the film <a href="/wiki/The_Third_Party" title="The Third Party">The Third Party</a> as Joan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>By December 2016, Maris became notable as Maxine in the <a href="/wiki/2016_Metro_Manila_Film_Festival" title="2016 Metro Manila Film Festival">2016 Metro Manila Film Festival</a>'s box office entry film <a href="/wiki/Vince_and_Kath_and_James" title="Vince and Kath and James">Vince and Kath and James</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>At present (2017), Maris is one of the <a href="/wiki/ASAP_(variety_show)" title="ASAP (variety show)">ASAP (variety show)</a> girl group, known as BFF5, together with <a href="/wiki/Ylona_Garcia" title="Ylona Garcia">Ylona Garcia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Brillantes" title="Andrea Brillantes">Andrea Brillantes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loisa_Andalio" title="Loisa Andalio">Loisa Andalio</a> and Kira Balinger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> She continues as one of <a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_(variety_show)" title="It's Showtime (variety show)">It's Showtime's</a> Girltrends. She's also one of the cast in the upcoming horror thriller film <a href="/wiki/Bloody_Crayons_(film)" title="Bloody Crayons (film)">Bloody Crayons</a>, as Richalaine Alcantara.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maris_Racal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Pinoy_Big_Brother:_All_In" title="Pinoy Big Brother: All In">PBB All In</a> housemates including Maris Racal released their "Pinoy Big Brother: All In - Originals" album, composed of three songs (Teen Love Song, Turtle Song, and Bibimbap) originally written and sung by them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 28, 2014, Maris uploaded her cover of <a href="/wiki/Ed_Sheeran" title="Ed Sheeran">Ed Sheeran</a>‘s “<a href="/wiki/Thinking_Out_Loud" title="Thinking Out Loud">Thinking Out Loud</a>” song on her official <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> account. The hashtag ‘Thinking Out Loud Cover by Maris Racal’ immediately trended worldwide on Twitter as her fans expressed their delight with her cover song. The video has already garnered almost two million of hits on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> since it was uploaded. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February-March 2015, Maris shared her video blogs on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Youtube" title="Youtube">Youtube</a> known as “Maris My Song For You” episodes (with the direction of Alco Guerrero) where she gave her fans an opportunity to send her their messages and ask her to breathe new life to their favorite song. Her MMSFY episodes started from her cover of songs <a href="/wiki/In_My_Life" title="In My Life">In My Life</a> and <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/True_Colors" title="True Colors">True Colors</a> followed by more cover songs like Firework, Yellow, <a href="/wiki/Lost_Stars" title="Lost Stars">Lost Stars</a>, <a href="/wiki/%27Til_There_Was_You" title="'Til There Was You">'Til There Was You</a> and Ordinary People.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>By April 2015, Maris released her first single, "Tanong Mo Sa Bituin", part of <a href="/wiki/Star_Music" title="Star Music">Star Record</a>'s compilation album, <a href="/wiki/2015_in_Philippine_music" title="2015 in Philippine music">"OPM Fresh"</a>. This song became the official soundtrack of the film "Stars Versus Me". <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2015, Maris covered <a href="/wiki/Norah_Jones" title="Norah Jones">Norah Jones</a>' hit song "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Know_Why" title="Don't Know Why">Don't Know Why</a>" during the #HappyBirthdayWish1075 celebration. Her exclusive performance made her won the awards "Wishers’ Choice for Wishclusive Performance of the Year" and "Wishers’ Choice for Young Artist of the Year" in the 2016 Wish 107.5 Music Awards dated January 26, 2016 held at Smart Araneta Coliseum,.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 2, 2016, Maris was one of the four singers/performers (the others being <a href="/wiki/Loisa_Andalio" title="Loisa Andalio">Loisa Andalio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sue_Ramirez" title="Sue Ramirez">Sue Ramirez</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kristel_Fulgar" title="Kristel Fulgar">Kristel Fulgar</a>) who headlined OneMusicPH's Double Hearts Digital Concert where she debuted her original song, Take It All Away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_Life">Personal Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maris_Racal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mariestella C. Racal grew up in <a href="/wiki/Davao_del_Norte" title="Davao del Norte">Davao del Norte</a> and graduated high school from St. Mary’s College in <a href="/wiki/Tagum" title="Tagum">Tagum City</a>. Maris is the 5th of 6 siblings and they are all musically-inclined in the family. In school, she used to sing the responsorial psalm, sing in mass and sing intermission numbers. She also did cover songs on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Maris Racal dreams of becoming a doctor and plans to juggle acting, singing, dancing &amp; studying. She is currently attending <a href="/wiki/Trinity_University_of_Asia" title="Trinity University of Asia">Trinity University of Asia</a> taking up <a href="/wiki/Health_technology" title="Health technology">Medical Technology degree</a>. She's also active in her social media accounts: Instagram (mariesteller), Twitter (MissMarisRacal), Facebook (Mariestella Racal &amp; Maris Racal), Snapchat (marisracal) and Youtube (Maris Racal).</p>
