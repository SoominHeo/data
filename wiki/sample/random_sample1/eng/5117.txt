<p><b>Maria Sadowska</b> (born June 27, 1976 in <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmpolski_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmpolski-1">[1]</a></sup> also credited as <b>Marysia Sadowska</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-topclub_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-topclub-2">[2]</a></sup> or simply as <b>Marysia</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish_people" title="Polish people">Polish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pop_singer" title="Pop singer">pop singer</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-topclub_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-topclub-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_producer" title="Music producer">music producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmf_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-rmf-4">[4]</a></sup> and film director.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Sadowska&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Sadowska&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sadowska was born in the capital of <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmpolski_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-filmpolski-1">[1]</a></sup> to her father, a composer, and her mother, a <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dm7_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-dm7-5">[5]</a></sup> She debuted as a singer at the age of 14, and produced a musical, at the age of 17, where she wrote and composed its theme music and played the main role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dm8_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-dm8-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>Sadowska released her first album <i>Jutro</i> in 1995. Two years later, in 1997, she released her second and third albums, <i>Lucky Star</i> and <i>Crazy</i>, exclusively in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmf_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-rmf-4">[4]</a></sup> The song "Crazy" from the third album was featured on <a href="/wiki/Dancemania" title="Dancemania">Dancemania</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Dancemania_7" title="Dancemania 7">seventh issue</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dm7_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-dm7-5">[5]</a></sup> and two songs from the second album, "Maybe Baby" and "Lucky Star", were featured respectively on <i><a href="/wiki/Dancemania_8" title="Dancemania 8">Dancemania 8</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Dancemania_9" title="Dancemania 9">Dancemania 9</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dm8_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-dm8-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Sadowska&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, Sadowska released her fourth, jazz-<a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a>-orientated album, <i>Marysia Sadowska</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmf_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-rmf-4">[4]</a></sup> which reached number 38 on the Polish <a href="/wiki/OLiS" title="OLiS">OLiS</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her fifth album, <i>Tribute to Komeda</i>, was literally a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tribute_album" title="Tribute album">tribute album</a> to <a href="/wiki/Krzysztof_Komeda" title="Krzysztof Komeda">Krzysztof Komeda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmf_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-rmf-4">[4]</a></sup> In its release year of 2006, the album reached #11 on OLiS' chart and was certified <a href="/wiki/List_of_music_recording_certifications" title="List of music recording certifications">gold</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The next year, 2007, she appeared on the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_on_Ice" title="Dancing on Ice">Dancing on Ice</a></i>, and released her sixth album <i>Gwiazda dla każdego</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmf_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-rmf-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2009 album <i>Spis treści</i> featured her tenth single "Jest dobrze". <i>Spis treści</i> was her first completely self-produced album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmf_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-rmf-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Sadowska&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Her debut feature film <i>Women's Day</i> (<i>Dzień kobiet</i>) was released in 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-10">[10]</a></sup> and received the main award at the 22nd Film Festival Cottbus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sadowska was the judge of the <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_of_Poland_(season_3)" title="The Voice of Poland (season 3)">third</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_of_Poland_(season_4)" title="The Voice of Poland (season 4)">fourth</a> season of Polish TV show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_of_Poland" title="The Voice of Poland">The Voice of Poland</a></i>, with participants from her team winning twice. She returned to judging for the show's <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_of_Poland_(season_6)" title="The Voice of Poland (season 6)">sixth</a> season, and won again.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Sadowska&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sadowska is the daughter of composer Krzysztof Sadowski and singer Liliana Urbańska.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmpolski_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-filmpolski-1">[1]</a></sup> She earned a second degree in <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fryderyk_Chopin_Academy_of_Music_in_Warsaw" title="Fryderyk Chopin Academy of Music in Warsaw">Fryderyk Chopin Academy of Music in Warsaw</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-topclub_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-topclub-2">[2]</a></sup> soon after the releases of <i>Lucky Star</i> and <i>Crazy</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rmf_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-rmf-4">[4]</a></sup> She also graduated from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lodz_Film_School" title="Lodz Film School">Lodz Film School</a> in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmpolski_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-filmpolski-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
