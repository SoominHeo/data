<p><b>Maria Elena Kyriakou</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Μαρία Έλενα Κυριάκου</span>, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Greek" title="Help:IPA for Greek">[marˈia ˈelena cirˈʝaku]</a></span>; born 11 January 1984) is a <a href="/wiki/Greek_Cypriots" title="Greek Cypriots">Greek Cypriot</a> singer best known for winning the <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_of_Greece_(season_1)" title="The Voice of Greece (season 1)">first season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_of_Greece" title="The Voice of Greece">The Voice of Greece</a></i> under the mentorship of <a href="/wiki/Despina_Vandi" title="Despina Vandi">Despina Vandi</a>. She <a href="/wiki/Greece_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2015" title="Greece in the Eurovision Song Contest 2015">represented Greece</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2015" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2015">Eurovision Song Contest 2015</a> with her song "<a href="/wiki/One_Last_Breath_(Maria_Elena_Kyriakou_song)" title="One Last Breath (Maria Elena Kyriakou song)">One Last Breath</a>".</p>

<p>Maria Elena Kyriakou was born on 11 January 1984 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Larnaca,_Cyprus" title="Larnaca, Cyprus">Larnaca, Cyprus</a> and has lived there since then. She has three siblings; one brother <a href="/wiki/Kyriacos_Pavlou" title="Kyriacos Pavlou">Kyriacos Pavlou</a> and two sisters. At the age of 13, her mother registered her at a <a href="/wiki/Music_school" title="Music school">music school</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-biography1-1">[1]</a></sup> Years later, she studied philosophy at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Ioannina" title="University of Ioannina">University of Ioannina</a> and during the first year she got a proposition from Giannis Ploutarchos which she did not accept as she preferred to concentrate on her studying.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-biography1-1">[1]</a></sup> After 2 years in the University of Ioannina she decided to move back to her home country. She then continued her studies in the University of Cyprus. After her graduation she opened her own <a href="/wiki/Tutorial" title="Tutorial">tutorial class</a>. In 2011, her mother applied for Maria Elena to audition for <i><a href="/wiki/Greek_Idol" title="Greek Idol">Greek Idol</a></i> but did not go to the auditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-biography2-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014:_The_Voice_of_Greece">2014: <i>The Voice of Greece</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Elena_Kyriakou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014: The Voice of Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kyriakou auditioned for <i>The Voice of Greece</i> at the age of twenty-nine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mariaelenaprofile_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mariaelenaprofile-3">[3]</a></sup> Appearing on the third of the eight blind auditions, she auditioned with "<a href="/wiki/Because_of_You_(Kelly_Clarkson_song)" title="Because of You (Kelly Clarkson song)">Because of You</a>" originally by <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a>—with all four coaches electing for her to join their teams; from which Kyriakou selected <a href="/wiki/Despina_Vandi" title="Despina Vandi">Despina Vandi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blinds3_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-blinds3-4">[4]</a></sup> During the battles, she performed "<a href="/wiki/Hero_(Mariah_Carey_song)" title="Hero (Mariah Carey song)">Hero</a>" against Eva Kanata–with Despina crowning her as the winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-battles1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-battles1-5">[5]</a></sup> In the second live show, she performed "<a href="/wiki/I_Have_Nothing" title="I Have Nothing">I Have Nothing</a>" and was saved by the public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-live2-6">[6]</a></sup> In the third live show, she performed "Den Eisai Edo" and was once again saved by the public along with Stelios Mayalios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live3_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-live3-7">[7]</a></sup> In the fourth live show, she performed "<a href="/wiki/Hurt_(Christina_Aguilera_song)" title="Hurt (Christina Aguilera song)">Hurt</a>" and was saved by the public to proceed to the semi-final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live4_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-live4-8">[8]</a></sup> In the semi-final, she performed "<a href="/wiki/Bleeding_Love" title="Bleeding Love">Bleeding Love</a>" for her solo performance and "<a href="/wiki/Listen_(Beyonc%C3%A9_song)" title="Listen (Beyoncé song)">Listen</a>" for her duet with <a href="/wiki/Mando_(singer)" title="Mando (singer)">Mando</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live5_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-live5-9">[9]</a></sup> Kyriakou proceeded to the final after getting 60 points from her coach and 80 points from the public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live5_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-live5-9">[9]</a></sup> Her song "Dio Egoismoi" was available on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> the day after the semi-final, on 3 May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finalists_songs_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-finalists_songs-10">[10]</a></sup> In the final she performed her original song "Dio Egoismoi" and a duet with her coach with the song "<a href="/wiki/No_More_Tears_(Enough_Is_Enough)" title="No More Tears (Enough Is Enough)">No More Tears (Enough Is Enough)</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live6_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-live6-11">[11]</a></sup> As she advanced to the second round along with Kosmidou and Kintatos, she performed "<a href="/wiki/Because_of_You_(Kelly_Clarkson_song)" title="Because of You (Kelly Clarkson song)">Because of You</a>", the song she performed during the blind auditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-live6_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-live6-11">[11]</a></sup> Kyriakou won the series and a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Minos_EMI" title="Minos EMI">Minos EMI</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015:_Eurovision_Song_Contest">2015: Eurovision Song Contest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Elena_Kyriakou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015: Eurovision Song Contest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Maria Elena Kyriakou won the <a href="/wiki/Greece_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2015" title="Greece in the Eurovision Song Contest 2015">Greek national final</a> and represented Greece in the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2015" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2015">Eurovision Song Contest 2015</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maria_Elena_Kyriakou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kyriakou is one of the four children in her family among them the known Cypriot <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> <a href="/wiki/Kyriacos_Pavlou" title="Kyriacos Pavlou">Kyriacos Pavlou</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography2_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-biography2-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In 2006, she married Cypriot <a href="/wiki/Sports_journalism" title="Sports journalism">sports journalist</a> Gregoris Gregoriou, with whom she has three children; Kyriakou and Gregoriou divorced in 2011. She also has a relative in England called Charlie Barnard who is a right nutty geezer, so much so there is a video of his ballsack on the YouTube<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gg_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-gg-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gg_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-gg-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
