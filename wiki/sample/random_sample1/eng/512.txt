<p><b><a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a></b> competed at the <b><a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, Brazil, from 5 to 21 August 2016. This was the territory's sixteenth appearance at the Summer Olympics, since its debut as a British colony in <a href="/wiki/1952_Summer_Olympics" title="1952 Summer Olympics">1952</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sports_Federation_and_Olympic_Committee_of_Hong_Kong,_China" title="Sports Federation and Olympic Committee of Hong Kong, China">Sports Federation and Olympic Committee of Hong Kong, China</a> sent a team of 37 athletes, 13 men and 24 women, to compete in nine sports at the Games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The nation's full roster in Rio de Janeiro was smaller by five athletes than in London, and also, featured more female athletes than males for only the second time in its Olympic history.</p>
<p>Eleven athletes from Hong Kong previously competed at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_2012" title="London 2012">London 2012</a>, including bronze medalist <a href="/wiki/Lee_Wai_Sze" title="Lee Wai Sze">Lee Wai Sze</a> in track cycling, badminton player <a href="/wiki/Yip_Pui_Yin" title="Yip Pui Yin">Yip Pui Yin</a> in the women's singles, mountain biker <a href="/wiki/Chan_Chun_Hing" title="Chan Chun Hing">Chan Chun Hing</a>, and 37-year-old table tennis player <a href="/wiki/Tie_Ya_Na" title="Tie Ya Na">Tie Ya Na</a>, who attended her fourth Olympics as the oldest and most experienced member. Other notable athletes on the Hong Kong roster also featured Youth Olympians <a href="/wiki/Doo_Hoi_Kem" title="Doo Hoi Kem">Doo Hoi Kem</a> (table tennis) and 18-year-old <a href="/wiki/Siobhan_Haughey" title="Siobhan Haughey">Siobhan Haughey</a> (swimming), Asia's top-ranked épée fencer <a href="/wiki/Vivian_Kong" title="Vivian Kong">Vivian Kong</a>, and backstroke swimmer and three-time Olympian <a href="/wiki/Stephanie_Au" title="Stephanie Au">Stephanie Au</a>, who was selected by the committee to carry the Hong Kong flag at the opening ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flag-bearer_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-flag-bearer-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unlike the previous Games, Hong Kong failed to win a single medal in Rio de Janeiro. The special administrative region's best medal hope, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Wai_Sze" title="Lee Wai Sze">Lee Wai Sze</a> missed out on a place in the finals of her respective track cycling events, following her involvement in the controversial clash in the women's keirin, and her quarterfinal defeat to Germany's eventual champion <a href="/wiki/Kristina_Vogel" title="Kristina Vogel">Kristina Vogel</a> in the women's sprint.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Meanwhile, windsurfer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Cheng" title="Michael Cheng">Michael Cheng</a> wrapped up his Olympic campaign with one of Hong Kong's most unexpected outcomes at the Games, finishing eighth overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Swimming">Swimming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hong_Kong_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Swimming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="Swimming at the 2016 Summer Olympics">Swimming at the 2016 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Hong Kong swimmers have so far achieved qualifying standards in the following events (up to a maximum of 2 swimmers in each event at the Olympic Qualifying Time (OQT), and potentially 1 at the Olympic Selection Time (OST)):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Table_tennis">Table tennis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hong_Kong_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Table tennis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Table_tennis_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="Table tennis at the 2016 Summer Olympics">Table tennis at the 2016 Summer Olympics</a></div>
<p>Hong Kong has fielded a team of six athletes into the table tennis competition at the Games. <a href="/wiki/Tang_Peng" title="Tang Peng">Tang Peng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wong_Chun_Ting" title="Wong Chun Ting">Wong Chun Ting</a>, 2014 Youth Olympic runner-up <a href="/wiki/Doo_Hoi_Kem" title="Doo Hoi Kem">Doo Hoi Kem</a>, and Lee Ho Ching were automatically selected among the top 22 eligible players each in their respective singles events based on the ITTF Olympic Rankings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ho Kwan Kit and three-time Olympian <a href="/wiki/Tie_Ya_Na" title="Tie Ya Na">Tie Ya Na</a> were each awarded the third spot to build the men's and women's teams for the Games by virtue of a top 10 national finish in the ITTF Olympic Rankings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
