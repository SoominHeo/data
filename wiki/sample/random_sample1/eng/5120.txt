<p><b>Mariana Avitia Martínez</b> (born September 18, 1993 in <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">Monterrey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Nuevo León">Nuevo León</a>) is an athlete from <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> who competes in <a href="/wiki/Archery" title="Archery">archery</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_Summer_Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariana_Avitia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 2008 Summer Olympics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> Avitia finished her ranking round with a total of 641 points. This gave her the 20th seed for the final competition bracket in which she faced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Son_Hye-Yong" title="Son Hye-Yong">Son Hye-Yong</a> in the first round, beating the archer from <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> with 112-107. In the second round she was too strong for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malgorzata_Cwienczek" title="Malgorzata Cwienczek">Malgorzata Cwienczek</a> with 110-109 and via <a href="/wiki/Khatuna_Narimanidze" title="Khatuna Narimanidze">Khatuna Narimanidze</a> (109-108) she achieved her place in the quarter final. There she was eliminated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kwon_Un-Sil" title="Kwon Un-Sil">Kwon Un-Sil</a> with 105-99.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beijing_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-beijing-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_Summer_Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mariana_Avitia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2012 Summer Olympics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the 2012 Summer Olympics in London Avitia won the bronze medal in the women's individual archery event. Teammate <a href="/wiki/A%C3%ADda_Rom%C3%A1n" title="Aída Román">Aída Román</a> won the silver medal. Together they were the first ever to win an archery Olympic medal for Mexico.</p>
