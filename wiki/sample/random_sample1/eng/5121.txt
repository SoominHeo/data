<p><b>Marian Lupu</b> (<small>Romanian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Romanian" title="Help:IPA for Romanian">[mariˈan ˈlupu]</a></span>; born 20 June 1966) is a <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldovan</a> politician who was the President of the Parliament of Moldova between 2010 and 2013. From this position he served as Acting President of the Republic from 2010 until 2012.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_education">Background and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marian_Lupu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marian Lupu was born on 20 June 1966 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B%C4%83l%C5%A3i" title="Bălţi">Bălţi</a>, but he and his family moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chi%C5%9Fin%C4%83u" title="Chişinău">Chişinău</a> when he was 6 years old. His parents gave him the nickname Marcel, because they liked the Romanian <a href="/wiki/Accordion" title="Accordion">accordion</a> musician <a href="/wiki/Marcel_Budal%C4%83" title="Marcel Budală">Marcel Budală</a> (1926–1989). His father, Ilie Lupu (b. 1938), was a mathematics professor at the <a href="/wiki/Moldova_State_University" title="Moldova State University">Moldova State University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> His mother taught <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French language</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicolae_Testemi%C5%A3anu_State_University_of_Medicine_and_Pharmacy" title="Nicolae Testemiţanu State University of Medicine and Pharmacy">Nicolae Testemiţanu State University of Medicine and Pharmacy</a>. He had been a member of <a href="/wiki/Komsomol" title="Komsomol">Komsomol</a> from 1980 until 1988 and a Member of <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Communist Party of the Soviet Union">Communist Party of the Soviet Union</a> from 1988 to 1991.</p>
<p>Until 1983, Lupu studied at "Gheorghe Asachi" High School of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chi%C5%9Fin%C4%83u" title="Chişinău">Chişinău</a>. He studied Economics at <a href="/wiki/Moldova_State_University" title="Moldova State University">Moldova State University</a> (until 1987) and at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plekhanov_Russian_Economic_University" title="Plekhanov Russian Economic University">Plekhanov Moscow Institute of the National Economy</a> (1987–1991) in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> where he obtained his Ph.D. in Economics. Lupu also attended stages at the Institute of the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a> in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> (1994) and <a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Organization" title="World Trade Organization">World Trade Organization</a> in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a> (1996).</p>
<p>Besides speaking his native Romanian, Lupu speaks <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>. Lupu was married in 1992 and has two children, Alexandra and Cristian.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marian_Lupu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was named as Vice Minister of the Economy in the new <a href="/wiki/Party_of_Communists_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova" title="Party of Communists of the Republic of Moldova">Communist</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vasile_Tarlev_Cabinet_(1)" title="Vasile Tarlev Cabinet (1)">Tarlev cabinet</a> in June 2001. Two years later in August 2003, Lupu was promoted to full Minister of the Economy (5 August 2003 - 24 March 2005).</p>
<p>After the Communists won <a href="/wiki/Moldovan_parliamentary_election,_2005" title="Moldovan parliamentary election, 2005">re-election in 2005</a>, Lupu was once again promoted, this time as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_Moldovan_Parliament" title="Speaker of the Moldovan Parliament">Speaker of the Moldovan Parliament</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Marian Lupu was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_Moldovan_Parliament" title="Speaker of the Moldovan Parliament">Speaker</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova" title="Parliament of the Republic of Moldova">Moldovan Parliament</a> from March 2005 to May 2009. But in December 2008, Lupu was not included by president <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Voronin" title="Vladimir Voronin">Vladimir Voronin</a> in the composition of the Supreme Security Council. Lupu was a high-ranking member of the <a href="/wiki/Party_of_Communists_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova" title="Party of Communists of the Republic of Moldova">Party of Communists of the Republic of Moldova</a> and was considered to be a leading candidate to become the next <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Moldova" title="Prime Minister of Moldova">Prime Minister</a> after <a href="/wiki/Zinaida_Grecean%C3%AEi" title="Zinaida Greceanîi">Zinaida Greceanîi</a> stepped down; however, shortly before the second (unsuccessful) attempt to elect the next president on 3 June 2009, he left the PCRM, stating that it was not possible to reform the PCRM from within. Subsequently he was offered membership and even leadership in the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Moldova" title="Democratic Party of Moldova">Democratic Party of Moldova</a>, which had not cleared the electoral threshold in the first elections in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radio_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Radio-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Moldovan_parliamentary_election,_July_2009" title="Moldovan parliamentary election, July 2009">July 2009 parliamentary election</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Vlad_Filat" title="Vlad Filat">Vlad Filat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mihai_Ghimpu" title="Mihai Ghimpu">Mihai Ghimpu</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Serafim_Urechean" title="Serafim Urechean">Serafim Urechean</a>, Marian Lupu signed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alliance_For_European_Integration" title="Alliance For European Integration">Alliance For European Integration</a> in a press-conference on 8 August 2009.</p>
<p>He was the candidate of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alliance_For_European_Integration" title="Alliance For European Integration">Alliance For European Integration</a> for <a href="/wiki/President_of_Moldova" title="President of Moldova">President of Moldova</a> up until the election of current president <a href="/wiki/Nicolae_Timofti" title="Nicolae Timofti">Nicolae Timofti</a> on 16 March 2012.</p>
<p>On 25 April 2013 Marian Lupu was sacked from position of President of Moldovan Parliament with 76 of 101 votes (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partidul_Comuni%C8%99tilor_din_Republica_Moldova" title="Partidul Comuniștilor din Republica Moldova">communists</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partidul_Liberal_Democrat_din_Moldova" title="Partidul Liberal Democrat din Moldova">liberal-democrats</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partidul_Sociali%C8%99tilor_din_Republica_Moldova" title="Partidul Socialiștilor din Republica Moldova">socialists</a> and some liberal-reformators).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Controversy_over_ethnic_and_linguistic_identity_in_Moldova" title="Controversy over ethnic and linguistic identity in Moldova">controversy over ethnic and linguistic identity in Moldova</a>, Marian Lupu identifies himself as Moldovan ethnic and supported retention of "<a href="/wiki/Moldovan_language" title="Moldovan language">Moldovan language</a>" sytagma in <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Moldova" title="Constitution of Moldova">Constitution</a> as state language of Moldova.</p>
<p>Though in 2010, in TV talk-show „În profunzime” on <a href="/wiki/ProTV_Chi%C8%99in%C4%83u" title="ProTV Chișinău">ProTV Chișinău</a>, Marian Lupu stated: "<i>From scientifical point of view I speak Romanian, from political point of view - Moldovan!</i>”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After two years, in the same talk-show Lupu stated: „<i>I changed my mind. Scientifically is not Romanian anymore, as I said previously, but Moldovan [language].</i>”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Lupu supports idea of existence of the Moldovan people and Moldovan nation and the idea that Moldova is a distinct entity apart of Romania.</p>
<p>In 2010 Lupu stated „<i>It is a pride for Moldova that its soldiers marched on the <a href="/wiki/Red_Square" title="Red Square">Red Square</a></i>” in Moscow on the <a href="/wiki/Victory_Day_(9_May)" title="Victory Day (9 May)">Victory Day (9 May)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
