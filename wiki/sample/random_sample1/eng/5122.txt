<p><b>Marianne Ragipcien Jean-Baptiste</b> (born 26 April 1967) is an English actress, singer-songwriter, composer and director, best known for her roles as Hortense Cumberbatch in <i><a href="/wiki/Secrets_%26_Lies_(film)" title="Secrets &amp; Lies (film)">Secrets &amp; Lies</a></i> (1996), for which she was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress</a>, and as <a href="/wiki/Vivian_Johnson" title="Vivian Johnson">Vivian Johnson</a> on the American television series <i><a href="/wiki/Without_a_Trace" title="Without a Trace">Without a Trace</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2015, she starred as Sharon Bishop QC in the ITV crime series <i><a href="/wiki/Broadchurch_(series_2)" title="Broadchurch (series 2)">Broadchurch</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marianne_Jean-Baptiste&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jean-Baptiste was born in London to a mother from <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a> and a father from <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">Saint Lucia</a>, growing up in <a href="/wiki/Peckham" title="Peckham">Peckham</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hattenstone_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hattenstone-2">[2]</a></sup> She was classically trained at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Dramatic_Art" title="Royal Academy of Dramatic Art">Royal Academy of Dramatic Art</a> in London and performed at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">Royal National Theatre</a>. She was nominated for a 1994 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ian_Charleson_Award" title="Ian Charleson Award">Ian Charleson Award</a> for her performance in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Measure_For_Measure" title="Measure For Measure">Measure For Measure</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Cheek_by_Jowl" title="Cheek by Jowl">Cheek by Jowl</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marianne_Jean-Baptiste&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jean-Baptiste gained international success from the <a href="/wiki/Mike_Leigh" title="Mike Leigh">Mike Leigh</a>-directed social drama <i><a href="/wiki/Secrets_%26_Lies_(film)" title="Secrets &amp; Lies (film)">Secrets &amp; Lies</a></i> (1996), receiving both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress</a> nominations for her performance, becoming the first black British actress to be nominated for an Academy Award, and the second black Briton to be nominated, succeeding <a href="/wiki/Jaye_Davidson" title="Jaye Davidson">Jaye Davidson</a>. She collaborated with director <a href="/wiki/Mike_Leigh" title="Mike Leigh">Mike Leigh</a> onstage in <i>It's a Great Big Shame</i> (1993).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She caused some controversy when she accused the film industry of racism. She noted that leading actors had been asked to attend <a href="/wiki/Cannes_Film_Festival" title="Cannes Film Festival">Cannes</a>, but despite her success she was not invited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bourne2005_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bourne2005-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>A writer and composer, Jean-Baptiste has recorded an album of blues songs and composed the musical score for Leigh's 1997 film <i><a href="/wiki/Career_Girls" title="Career Girls">Career Girls</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1999, she performed in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> in a French-language production by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Brook" title="Peter Brook">Peter Brook</a> of <i>The Suit</i> (<i>Le Costume</i>), a one-act play by <a href="/wiki/Barney_Simon" title="Barney Simon">Barney Simon</a> and Mothobi Mutloatse, based on the short story by <a href="/wiki/Can_Themba" title="Can Themba">Can Themba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She was also acclaimed for her role as <a href="/wiki/Doreen_Lawrence" title="Doreen Lawrence">Doreen Lawrence</a> in <i>The Murder of Stephen Lawrence</i> (1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hattenstone_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hattenstone-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jean-Baptiste relocated for work reasons to the US and has been living for some years in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> with her husband and two daughters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hattenstone_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hattenstone-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She honed her American accent and starred in the American television series <i><a href="/wiki/Without_a_Trace" title="Without a Trace">Without a Trace</a></i> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FBI" title="FBI">FBI</a> agent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vivian_Johnson_(Without_a_Trace)" title="Vivian Johnson (Without a Trace)">Vivian Johnson</a>. More recently the actress has appeared in such films as <i><a href="/wiki/Takers" title="Takers">Takers</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Secrets_in_the_Walls" title="Secrets in the Walls">Secrets in the Walls</a></i> (2010) and <i><a href="/wiki/Harry%27s_Law" title="Harry's Law">Harry's Law</a></i> (2012). She played Detroit Chief of Police, Karen Dean, in <i><a href="/wiki/RoboCop_(2014_film)" title="RoboCop (2014 film)">RoboCop</a></i> (2014).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was much praised for her stage performance in the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">National Theatre</a> production of <a href="/wiki/James_Baldwin" title="James Baldwin">James Baldwin</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amen_Corner" title="The Amen Corner">The Amen Corner</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Norris" title="Rufus Norris">Rufus Norris</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a friend to <a href="/wiki/Angela_Bassett" title="Angela Bassett">Angela Bassett</a>, Marianne Jean-Baptiste attended <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Image_Network_Awards" title="Women's Image Network Awards">Women's Image Network Awards</a> and picked up an award, reading Bassett's poetic acceptance speech for her winning role in the film <i>Betty &amp; Coretta</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
