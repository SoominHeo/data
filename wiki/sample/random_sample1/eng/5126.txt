<p><i><b>Mario Kart Arcade GP</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">マリオカート アーケードグランプリ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Mario Kāto Ākēdo Guran Puri</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade</a>-only iteration of <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Kart" title="Mario Kart">Mario Kart</a></i> series, developed by <a href="/wiki/Namco" title="Namco">Namco</a>. Designed for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triforce_(arcade_system_board)" title="Triforce (arcade system board)">Triforce arcade board</a> (also used for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F-Zero_AX" title="F-Zero AX">F-Zero AX</a></i>), players can race as one of eleven characters on twenty-four tracks. On certain machines, for an additional fee, a player's data can be saved on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnetic_card" title="Magnetic card">magnetic card</a> which can be inserted into the machine again later to retain unlocked items and records. Nintendo's limited role of development was mainly isolated to ensuring that it was a worthy title for the established franchise.</p>
<p>This game's arcade cabinet houses a <a href="/wiki/Camera" title="Camera">camera</a> that will take a <a href="/wiki/Photograph" title="Photograph">photograph</a> of the player's face. The photo can be customized and then will be displayed above the player's character during multiplayer races.</p>
<p>The game is noted for being the first <i>Mario Kart</i> to feature playable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_crossover" title="Fictional crossover">crossover</a> characters: Namco's <a href="/wiki/Pac-Man_(character)" title="Pac-Man (character)">Pac-Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ms._Pac-Man" title="Ms. Pac-Man">Ms. Pac-Man</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ghosts_(Pac-Man)#Blinky" title="Ghosts (Pac-Man)">Blinky</a>. The game's sequel also features Mametchi, a character from the <a href="/wiki/Tamagotchi" title="Tamagotchi">Tamagotchi</a> games, and the third installment includes Don from the <i><a href="/wiki/Taiko_no_Tatsujin" title="Taiko no Tatsujin">Taiko no Tatsujin</a></i> franchise.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mario_Kart_Arcade_GP&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are a total of six stages, with four tracks in each for a grand total of twenty-four. After a track is completed, the player must insert additional credits to continue the game, even if first place is achieved. After finishing all four races from each stage, a "challenge game" comes up, in which the player is required to complete a certain task; each of them requires the player to drive to a goal under a certain amount of time while under a certain condition, such as driving backwards through a field of banana peels.</p>
<p>When a player hits one of the item boxes scattered throughout the tracks, the player is given one of the three selected items chosen at the start of a race. <i>Mario Kart Arcade GP</i> features items new and old. Unique to this game is a lock-on feature for the items.</p>
<p>Also unique to this game is a temporary shield that is produced while powersliding which protects players from items thrown at them by the other racers.</p>
<p>Most of the game's sound effects came from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mario_Kart:_Double_Dash%E2%80%BC" title="Mario Kart: Double Dash‼">Mario Kart: Double Dash‼</a></i> including the <a href="/wiki/Time_limit_(video_gaming)" title="Time limit (video gaming)">pre-race countdown timer</a> to commence racing.</p>

<p><i><b>Mario Kart Arcade GP 2</b></i> is the second game in the <i>Mario Kart Arcade GP</i> <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade game</a> series. The game features the "Nam Cam" camera features of its predecessor, and 50cc, 100cc, and 150cc difficulty modes, as well as new items. During the races, there is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Color_commentary" title="Color commentary">color commentary</a>, which can be toggled on or off at any time prior to starting the race. In the Japanese version of the game, this is done by the prominent <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting_in_Japan" title="Voice acting in Japan">Japanese voice actor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tarento" title="Tarento">on-screen personality</a> <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dichi_Yamadera" title="Kōichi Yamadera">Kōichi Yamadera</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> All eleven characters from <i>Mario Kart Arcade GP</i> return, alongside new characters Waluigi from the <i><a href="/wiki/Mario" title="Mario">Mario</a></i> series and Mametchi from the <i><a href="/wiki/Tamagotchi" title="Tamagotchi">Tamagotchi</a></i> series.</p>

<p><i><b>Mario Kart Arcade GP DX</b></i> is the third <i>Mario Kart Arcade GP</i> title, which was released in Japanese arcade in July 25, 2013, and in North American and European arcade in 2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indonesia_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-indonesia-2">[2]</a></sup> (previously announced for end of 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>), but the game was available in all <a href="/wiki/Dave_%26_Buster%27s" title="Dave &amp; Buster's">Dave &amp; Buster's</a> restaurants from February 16, 2014 until April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The game features ten redesigned courses, as well as features introduced in <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Kart_7" title="Mario Kart 7">Mario Kart 7</a></i> such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hang_glider" title="Hang glider">gliders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Underwater" title="Underwater">underwater</a> racing. Along with Grand Prix and Battle mode, the game introduces two new modes; Alter-Ego and Team. Alter-Ego mode uses online functionality to allow players to race against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ghost_(video_gaming)" title="Ghost (video gaming)">ghosts</a> set by other players similar to Ghost Versus Battle mode in <i><a href="/wiki/Wangan_Midnight" title="Wangan Midnight">Wangan Midnight Maximum Tune</a></i> series. Team mode allows two players to face against two computer-controlled opponents. The two players can combine their karts to form a more powerful kart, with one player driving and the other serving as the gunner (similar to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucky_and_Wild" title="Lucky and Wild">Lucky and Wild</a></i>). The playable characters in this game are Mario, Luigi, Peach, <a href="/wiki/Rosalina_(character)" title="Rosalina (character)">Rosalina</a>, Toad, Yoshi, Bowser, Dry Bowser, Wario, Waluigi, <a href="/wiki/Bowser_Jr." title="Bowser Jr.">Bowser Jr.</a>, Donkey Kong, Baby Mario, Baby Peach, Metal Mario, and Gold Mario from the <i>Mario</i> series, Pac-Man (using the design from <i><a href="/wiki/Pac-Man_and_the_Ghostly_Adventures" title="Pac-Man and the Ghostly Adventures">Pac-Man and the Ghostly Adventures</a></i>) from the <i>Pac-Man</i> series, and Don from the <i><a href="/wiki/Taiko_no_Tatsujin" title="Taiko no Tatsujin">Taiko no Tatsujin</a></i> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The racing commentary returns in this game and this time, it is voiced by <a href="/wiki/Rica_Matsumoto" title="Rica Matsumoto">Rica Matsumoto</a>, who also voices <a href="/wiki/Ash_Ketchum" title="Ash Ketchum">Ash Ketchum</a> in the Japanese version of the <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_(anime)" title="Pokémon (anime)"><i>Pokémon</i></a> anime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
