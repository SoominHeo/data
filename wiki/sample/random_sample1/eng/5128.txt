<p><b>Marion Jeanne Caroline Maréchal-Le Pen</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[maʁjɔ̃ maʁeʃal ləˈpɛn]</a></span>; born 10 December 1989) is a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> politician, granddaughter of <i><a href="/wiki/National_Front_(France)" title="National Front (France)">Front national</a></i> (FN) founder <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marie_Le_Pen" title="Jean-Marie Le Pen">Jean-Marie Le Pen</a>, and niece of current FN president <a href="/wiki/Marine_Le_Pen" title="Marine Le Pen">Marine Le Pen</a>.</p>
<p>Maréchal-Le Pen is, like her aunt, also a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Front_(France)" title="National Front (France)">National Front</a> and has been the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_France" title="National Assembly of France">MP</a> for <a href="/wiki/Vaucluse%27s_3rd_constituency" title="Vaucluse's 3rd constituency">Vaucluse's 3rd constituency</a> since <a href="/wiki/French_legislative_election,_2012" title="French legislative election, 2012">2012</a>. 22 years old at the time of her election, she became France's youngest MP in modern political history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT2MMLP_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-DT2MMLP-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_background">Family background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She was born on 10 December 1989 in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Germain-en-Laye" title="Saint-Germain-en-Laye">Saint-Germain-en-Laye</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yvelines" title="Yvelines">Yvelines</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele-de-France" title="Île-de-France">Île-de-France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MMLPANB_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-MMLPANB-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her grandfather Jean-Marie Le Pen, who was the honorary chairman of the FN prior to his expulsion in August 2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BMMLP_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BMMLP-6">[6]</a></sup> founded this French nationalist party on 5 October 1972. Her aunt Marine Le Pen has been the president of the Front National since 16 January 2011. Her mother Yann Le Pen, Jean-Marie Le Pen's second daughter, does not carry out any official duties within the FN. Her father Samuel Maréchal had been the leader of the Front National Youth movement (FNJ) for seven years (1992–1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GMMLP_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-GMMLP-7">[7]</a></sup> She featured with her grandfather in a campaign poster at the age of two.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charm_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-charm-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a book entitled <i>The Conquerors</i> (<i>Les Conquérantes</i>) launched on 18 November 2013, the French journalist Christine Clerc (<a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christine_Clerc" title="fr:Christine Clerc">fr</a>) revealed that Samuel Maréchal is not her biological father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Expbio_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Expbio-9">[9]</a></sup> On 7 November 2013, the French weekly <a href="/wiki/News_magazine" title="News magazine">news magazine</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%27Express_(France)" title="L'Express (France)">L'Express</a></i> disclosed the identity of her birth father, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Auque" title="Roger Auque">Roger Auque</a>, a Mossad agent and investigative journalist who died in September 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Expbio_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Expbio-9">[9]</a></sup> On 8 November, Marion Maréchal-Le Pen officially announced that she had asked her lawyer to sue <i>L'Express</i> for a "serious invasion of her privacy".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Maréchal-Le Pen married businessman Matthieu Decosse on 29 July 2014, at the <a href="/wiki/Saint-Cloud" title="Saint-Cloud">Saint-Cloud</a> town hall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Their daughter was born that September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> They divorced in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_studies">Academic studies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academic studies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Until 2012, she was enrolled in <a href="/wiki/Panth%C3%A9on-Assas_University" title="Panthéon-Assas University">Panthéon-Assas University</a>'s masters of public business law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On 14 November 2012, she wrote in an official statement that she had decided to put aside her studies in order to dedicate herself to her office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career_.282008.E2.80.932013.29">Political career (2008–2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political career (2008–2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>About her early interest in politics, she explained: "Contrary to what everyone thinks, in my family we didn’t talk about politics at home and we were free to make our own choices. I became interested in politics around 15 or 16 and in various approaches, not necessarily FN". As a teenager, she once went to a meeting addressed by <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Sarkozy" title="Nicolas Sarkozy">Nicolas Sarkozy</a> "out of curiosity" because he "intrigued" her. She added: "I very quickly came down to earth." At the age of 18, she became a member of the FN.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT1MMLP_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-DT1MMLP-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career_.282008.E2.80.932010.29">Early career (2008–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early career (2008–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/French_municipal_elections,_2008" title="French municipal elections, 2008">French municipal elections, 2008</a></div>
<p>In the 2008 municipal elections, she was a candidate in seventh position on the FN list in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Cloud" title="Saint-Cloud">Saint-Cloud</a>, Hauts-de-Seine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> She was not elected since the FN list only got 6.29% with one municipal councillor elected from the first round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/French_regional_elections,_2010" title="French regional elections, 2010">French regional elections, 2010</a></div>
<p>In the 2010 regional elections, she figured in second position on the FN departmental list in the <a href="/wiki/Yvelines" title="Yvelines">Yvelines</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele-de-France_(region)" title="Île-de-France (region)">Île-de-France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Marie-Christine_Arnautu" title="Marie-Christine Arnautu">Marie-Christine Arnautu</a>'s FN list, which polled 9.29% in the whole of Île-de-France in the first round,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> could not take part in the run-off given that a list must cross a threshold of 10% of the valid votes at a regional level. Because of the process of elimination, she was not elected in the Île-de-France's regional council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TIMEMMLP_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-TIMEMMLP-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youngest_MP_in_modern_history_.282012.29">Youngest MP in modern history (2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Youngest MP in modern history (2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Her parliamentary candidacy in Vaucluse's 3rd constituency was publicly confirmed on 25 April 2012, in between the first round and run-off of the <a href="/wiki/French_presidential_election,_2012" title="French presidential election, 2012">presidential election</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> After her candidacy was made official by the FN nomination committee, she then campaigned in this constituency which includes the southern part of <a href="/wiki/Carpentras" title="Carpentras">Carpentras</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GMMLP_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-GMMLP-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT1MMLP_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-DT1MMLP-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TIMEMMLP_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-TIMEMMLP-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPMMLP_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-HPMMLP-25">[25]</a></sup> In the first round of the presidential elections, Marine Le Pen had achieved her highest national performance in Vaucluse (27.03%)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and most notably in this constituency (31.50%) where she outdistanced the UMP incumbent president Nicolas Sarkozy (27.60%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GMMLP_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-GMMLP-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TIMEMMLP_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-TIMEMMLP-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPMMLP_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-HPMMLP-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Two-round_system" title="Two-round system">run-off</a> on 17 June 2012, she defeated the incumbent MP <a href="/wiki/Jean-Michel_Ferrand" title="Jean-Michel Ferrand">Jean-Michel Ferrand</a> who had continuously sat in the National Assembly for twenty-six years (<a href="/wiki/Rally_for_the_Republic" title="Rally for the Republic">Rally for the Republic</a>: 1986–2002, <a href="/wiki/Union_for_a_Popular_Movement" title="Union for a Popular Movement">Union for a Popular Movement</a>: 2002–2012).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> At the age of 22, she became the youngest citizen to enter the French Parliament in modern political history (<a href="/wiki/Louis_Antoine_de_Saint-Just" title="Louis Antoine de Saint-Just">Louis Antoine de Saint-Just</a> was at 24 years old the former youngest MP in 1791).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GMMLP_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-GMMLP-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is the only member of the National Front since 1997 to serve in the National Assembly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DT2MMLP_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-DT2MMLP-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> A member of the FN from 1972 to 2005 and an MP for Vaucluse under the eighth legislature (1986–1988), <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Bompard" title="Jacques Bompard">Jacques Bompard</a>, was also elected as an MP for Vaucluse's 4th constituency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Collard" title="Gilbert Collard">Gilbert Collard</a>, a member of the <a href="/wiki/Rassemblement_bleu_Marine" title="Rassemblement bleu Marine">Rassemblement bleu Marine</a>, a political association who supports Marine Le Pen, was also elected as an MP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<p>In early July 2012 she became a member of the National Front's executive board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On 23 September 2012, she made her first public speech in front of 1,000 participants at the FN summer school in <a href="/wiki/La_Baule-Escoublac" title="La Baule-Escoublac">La Baule-Escoublac</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_politics:_Sorgues_.282013.E2.80.932014.29">Local politics: Sorgues (2013–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Local politics: Sorgues (2013–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/French_municipal_elections,_2014" title="French municipal elections, 2014">French municipal elections, 2014</a></div>
<p>During a press conference held on 30 October 2013, she officially announced her appearance as a fellow candidate on a municipal list at <a href="/wiki/Sorgues" title="Sorgues">Sorgues</a>, a town of 18,000 inhabitants located to the north of <a href="/wiki/Avignon" title="Avignon">Avignon</a> in the western part of her constituency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> She decided to figure in tenth position on this local list led by Gérard Gérent, then an independent councillor belonging to the UMP municipal majority and a former deputy mayor of Sorgues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the first round of the 2012 presidential elections, Marine Le Pen had polled 36.02% at Sorgues<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> whereas Marion Maréchal-Le Pen got there 37.65% in the first round and 44.36% in the run-off of the following legislative elections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the first round on 23 March 2014, the FN list led by Gérard Gérent, which was defeated by the one of the UMP incumbent mayor Thierry Lagneau, came second with 33.80% (2,861 votes) with the election of five municipal councillors and two community councillors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Consequently, she was not elected as a municipal councillor at Sorgues.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_candidacy_in_Provence-Alpes-C.C3.B4te_d.E2.80.99Azur_.282015.29">Regional candidacy in Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur (2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Regional candidacy in Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur (2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In April 2015, Marion Maréchal-Le Pen was chosen by her party to be the leading FN candidate in the southeastern region of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provence-Alpes-C%C3%B4te_d%E2%80%99Azur" title="Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur">Provence-Alpes-Côte d’Azur</a> in that year's regional elections, after her grandfather was expelled for his remarks on the Holocaust.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> She did not support his expulsion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alduy_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-alduy-43">[43]</a></sup> The elections came a month after <a href="/wiki/November_2015_Paris_attacks" title="November 2015 Paris attacks">an Islamist terror attack</a> which killed 130 people in Paris. Maréchal-Le Pen reacted on television by declaring that "Today, we can see that immigration has become favorable terrain for the development of Islamism".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the first round of voting, she won 40.55% of the vote, becoming one of six FN candidates to lead a region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Socialist candidate Christophe Castaner then withdrew, to avoid splitting the vote for <a href="/wiki/The_Republicans_(France)" title="The Republicans (France)">The Republicans</a>' mayor of <a href="/wiki/Nice" title="Nice">Nice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Estrosi" title="Christian Estrosi">Christian Estrosi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> In the second round of voting, no FN candidate won a region, with Maréchal-Le Pen losing to Estrosi by 54.78% to 45.22%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> She got the best result for a National Front candidate, Marine Le Pen obtaining 42% in the <a href="/wiki/Hauts-de-France" title="Hauts-de-France">Nord-Pas-de-Calais-Picardie</a> region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_views">Political views</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Political views">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Her political, cultural and foreign policy views reflect the general consensus of her party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MMLPEPravda_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-MMLPEPravda-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_positions">Social positions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Social positions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She asserts that her party has supported the defense of the <a href="/wiki/Family" title="Family">family</a> for a very long time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPTP_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-MPTP-51">[51]</a></sup> Along with <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Collard" title="Gilbert Collard">Gilbert Collard</a> and other FN senior executives, she took part in the mass demonstrations against <a href="/wiki/Same-sex_marriage_in_France" title="Same-sex marriage in France">same-sex marriage</a> organized in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> by La Manif Pour Tous movement in the first half of the year 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MMLPEPravda_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-MMLPEPravda-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MPTP_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-MPTP-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>Maréchal-Le Pen believes that Muslims can only be French if they follow the Christian culture, saying that "In our country, we don’t wear djellaba clothing, we don’t wear a veil and we don’t impose cathedral-sized mosques".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alduy_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-alduy-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is opposed to the reinstatement of <a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment" title="Capital punishment">capital punishment</a>: "In a private capacity, I am against the reinstatement of capital punishment, since this would impose an extremely difficult choice on judges. And whatever happens, the horrifying possibility of a <a href="/wiki/Miscarriage_of_justice" title="Miscarriage of justice">miscarriage of justice</a> is ever-present, no matter how minimally. I prefer the alternative of <a href="/wiki/Life_imprisonment" title="Life imprisonment">life imprisonment</a> without the possibility of <a href="/wiki/Parole" title="Parole">parole</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>Academic Cécile Alduy described Maréchal-Le Pen as a "paradoxical character" who dresses and speaks in a modern way while promoting social conservatism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alduy_43-2"><a href="#cite_note-alduy-43">[43]</a></sup> Conservative American former vice presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Palin" title="Sarah Palin">Sarah Palin</a> praised Maréchal-Le Pen for her societal beliefs, and compared her to <a href="/wiki/Joan_of_Arc" title="Joan of Arc">Joan of Arc</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> Latest <a href="/wiki/Counselor_to_the_President" title="Counselor to the President">Counselor to the President</a> and former executive chair of <a href="/wiki/Breitbart_News" title="Breitbart News">Breitbart News</a> <a href="/wiki/Steve_Bannon" title="Steve Bannon">Steve Bannon</a> also praised her, referring to her as "the new rising star"; afterwards Maréchal Le-Pen said on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> that she was willing to work with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_policy_and_EU_issues">Foreign policy and EU issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Foreign policy and EU issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She is a member of the parliamentary friendship groups France–<a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> and France−<a href="/wiki/Ivory_Coast" title="Ivory Coast">Ivory Coast</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 December 2012, she took part in an international parliamentary forum organized at <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> by the <a href="/wiki/State_Duma" title="State Duma">State Duma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> On 22 January 2013, she was present in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reichstag_(building)" title="Reichstag (building)">Reichstag</a> at the commemoration of the fiftieth anniversary of the signing of the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89lys%C3%A9e_Treaty" title="Élysée Treaty">Élysée Treaty</a> by French President <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Gaulle" title="Charles de Gaulle">Charles de Gaulle</a> and German Chancellor <a href="/wiki/Konrad_Adenauer" title="Konrad Adenauer">Konrad Adenauer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> In a written statement, she said that the treaty was originally based on the cooperation and partnership between two sovereign states and denounced the "forced march towards a German federal Europe".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 September 2013, she attended a political event organized by the <a href="/wiki/Vlaams_Belang" title="Vlaams Belang">Vlaams Belang</a> in <a href="/wiki/Boom,_Belgium" title="Boom, Belgium">Boom</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Antwerp" title="Antwerp">Antwerp</a>. On this occasion, she explained: "It is important that a front of patriotic and euro-critical parties form in sight of <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2014" title="European Parliament election, 2014">European elections</a>, which is the case, and get some good results in order to lead resistance to <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Globalism" title="Globalism">globalism</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parliamentary_career">Parliamentary career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marion_Mar%C3%A9chal-Le_Pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Parliamentary career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Along with Gilbert Collard, she introduced on 7 December 2012 a constitutional <a href="/wiki/Private_member%27s_bill" title="Private member's bill">private member's bill</a> concerning the appointment of the members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constitutional_Council_of_France" title="Constitutional Council of France">Constitutional Council of France</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the beginning of the fourteenth legislature, she cosigned four private member's bills<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MMLPANB_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-MMLPANB-5">[5]</a></sup> including one constitutional forbidding marriage between same-sex persons<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> and one organic which aims at enforcing the article 68 of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_France" title="Constitution of France">Constitution of France</a> establishing a process of <a href="/wiki/Impeachment" title="Impeachment">impeachment</a> for the <a href="/wiki/President_of_France" title="President of France">President of the Republic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to the rules of the National Assembly, an unregistered MP can ask orally the government every eight sessions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> She had asked three oral questions for the beginning of the legislature: in 2013, to <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Valls" title="Manuel Valls">Manuel Valls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_the_Interior_(France)" title="Minister of the Interior (France)">Minister of the Interior</a> about the policy regarding <a href="/wiki/Romani_people" title="Romani people">Romani people</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> and to <a href="/wiki/Marisol_Touraine" title="Marisol Touraine">Marisol Touraine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Health_(France)" title="Minister of Health (France)">Minister of Health</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Social_Affairs_(France)" title="Minister of Social Affairs (France)">Social Affairs</a> about the fight against <a href="/wiki/Welfare_fraud" title="Welfare fraud">welfare fraud</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> in 2014, to <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Bricq" title="Nicole Bricq">Nicole Bricq</a>, Minister for Foreign Trade about the <a href="/wiki/Transatlantic_Trade_and_Investment_Partnership" title="Transatlantic Trade and Investment Partnership">Transatlantic Trade and Investment Partnership</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a written parliamentary question addressed in May 2013 to <a href="/wiki/Val%C3%A9rie_Fourneyron" title="Valérie Fourneyron">Valérie Fourneyron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Youth_Affairs_and_Sports_(France)" title="Minister of Youth Affairs and Sports (France)">Minister of Sports, Youth Affairs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Popular_Education" title="Popular Education">Popular Education</a> and Community life, she drew her attention to the poor treatment <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_in_France" title="Rugby league in France">rugby league</a> receives in France from the government and the media, regretting the banning of this sport during the Vichy regime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2015, because of his intense anti-FN campaign in the <a href="/wiki/French_departmental_elections,_2015" title="French departmental elections, 2015">departmental elections</a>, Maréchal-Le Pen criticized the "cretin contempt" ("mépris crétin") of Socialist Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Valls" title="Manuel Valls">Manuel Valls</a> in parliament. Valls responded with a trembling hand and an apparent fury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> After it had become a viral video, she explained it was a reference to <a href="/wiki/Michel_Onfray" title="Michel Onfray">Michel Onfray</a> who has also called Valls a "crétin" when the Prime Minister has accused him of "losing his landmarks".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charm_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-charm-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>

<ul>
<li>Member of the standing committee for cultural affairs and education, 28 June 2012 – 30 September 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MMLPANB_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-MMLPANB-5">[5]</a></sup> — Member of the standing committee on Foreign Affairs, 1 October 2013–present<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></li>
<li>Member of the study groups <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">Heritage</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> — Policies on rurality<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> — <a href="/wiki/Shale_gas" title="Shale gas">Shale gas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></li>

<p>One of the six youngest MPs of the new assembly, she served on 26 June 2012 as a secretary during the opening of the fourteenth legislature presided over by the most senior MP <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Scellier" title="François Scellier">François Scellier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has been a Non-Attached MP since 20 June 2012. Her bench (number 67) is located between the ones of <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Collard" title="Gilbert Collard">Gilbert Collard</a> (number 66, on her "right") and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Bompard" title="Jacques Bompard">Jacques Bompard</a> (number 68, on her "left").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> The National Assembly has included eight unregistered MPs since 30 August 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup></p>
