<p><b>Marinko Galič</b> (born 22 April 1970, in <a href="/wiki/Koper" title="Koper">Koper</a>) is a former <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">footballer</a> who has represented his country at the two major tournaments for which they have qualified (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Football_Championship" title="UEFA European Football Championship">Euro 2000</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_World_Cup_2002" title="Football World Cup 2002">Football World Cup 2002</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marinko_Gali%C4%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He began his career playing for <a href="/wiki/FC_Koper" title="FC Koper">Koper</a> in 1991. In 1993 he joined <a href="/wiki/NK_Maribor" title="NK Maribor">Maribor</a> where he played three seasons. For the first time, he moved abroad in 1996 for <a href="/wiki/NK_Zagreb" title="NK Zagreb">NK Zagreb</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dinamo_Zagreb" title="Dinamo Zagreb">Dinamo Zagreb</a> spending two seasons in Croatia before moving to <a href="/wiki/NK_Mura" title="NK Mura">Mura Murska Sobota</a>. In 1998 he returned to Maribor for another three seasons and then he signed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rudar_Velenje" title="Rudar Velenje">Rudar Velenje</a> before he returned for another two years for the club he started at, NK Koper. He left his country again in 2003 to play for the Chinese team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shandong_Luneng" title="Shandong Luneng">Shandong Luneng</a> and for the Cypriot team <a href="/wiki/Apollon_Limassol" title="Apollon Limassol">Apollon Limassol</a>. After a short spell on Cyprus he returned home and played one season in the <a href="/wiki/Slovenian_Third_League" title="Slovenian Third League">third league</a> for <a href="/wiki/NK_Male%C4%8Dnik" title="NK Malečnik">Malečnik</a>. He finished his professional career with a short spell at <a href="/wiki/NK_Interblock" title="NK Interblock">NK Interblock</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marinko_Gali%C4%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Galič was capped 66 times for <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia</a>. He was a participant at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_UEFA_European_Championship" title="2000 UEFA European Championship">Euro 2000</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_World_Cup_2002" title="Football World Cup 2002">World Cup 2002</a>.</p>
