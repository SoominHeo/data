<p><b>Mamadou Niang</b> (born 13 October 1979) is a <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegalese</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Striker_(association_football)" title="Striker (association football)">striker</a> for <a href="/wiki/AC_Arles-Avignon" title="AC Arles-Avignon">Arles-Avignon</a>. He has represented <a href="/wiki/Senegal_national_football_team" title="Senegal national football team">Senegal</a> at international level, participating in the <a href="/wiki/2004_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="2004 African Cup of Nations">2004</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="2006 African Cup of Nations">2006</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="2008 African Cup of Nations">2008</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_Cup_of_Nations" title="African Cup of Nations">African Cup of Nations</a>. He is the older brother of <a href="/wiki/Papa_Niang" title="Papa Niang">Papa Niang</a>, who currently plays at <a href="/wiki/FF_Jaro" title="FF Jaro">FF Jaro</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> <a href="/wiki/Ykk%C3%B6nen" title="Ykkönen">Ykkönen</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Troyes">Troyes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamadou_Niang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Troyes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After beginning his career with the <a href="/wiki/Le_Havre_AC" title="Le Havre AC">Le Havre</a> youth team, Niang turned professional at 18 years of age with <a href="/wiki/Troyes_AC" title="Troyes AC">Troyes</a>. He had a mixed beginning to his career with his first season featuring ten <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> starts, all from the bench, the following season would show little improvement with 17 starts and only 3 league goals.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Metz">Loan to Metz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamadou_Niang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Loan to Metz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A loan period with <a href="/wiki/Ligue_2" title="Ligue 2">second division</a> <a href="/wiki/FC_Metz" title="FC Metz">Metz</a> allowed him to hone his skills, contributing 5 goals in 12 appearances and helping Metz return to top flight French football for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ligue_1_season_2003%E2%80%9304" title="Ligue 1 season 2003–04">2003–04</a> season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Strasbourg">Strasbourg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamadou_Niang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Strasbourg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jean_Fernandez" title="Jean Fernandez">Jean Fernandez</a>, the Metz manager, tried to make the loan deal into a permanent move but failed and Niang left Troyes for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RC_Strasbourg" title="RC Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a>. The arrival of <a href="/wiki/Danijel_Ljuboja" title="Danijel Ljuboja">Danijel Ljuboja</a>, to Strasbourg at the same time allowed Niang to form an important strike partnership until Ljuboja moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_FC" title="Paris Saint-Germain FC">Paris Saint-Germain</a>. Niang didn't score again for the remainder of the second half of that season.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ligue_1_season_2004%E2%80%9305" title="Ligue 1 season 2004–05">2004–05</a> season saw <a href="/wiki/Micka%C3%ABl_Pagis" title="Mickaël Pagis">Mickaël Pagis</a> arrive at Strasbourg, and a fruitful new partnership was formed, with the pair linking up for 27 goals in the league. This partnership helped Strasbourg make it to the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_la_Ligue" title="Coupe de la Ligue">Coupe de la Ligue</a> final, where victory over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SM_Caen" title="SM Caen">Caen</a> gave Strasbourg their second Coupe de la Ligue trophy.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympique_Marseille">Olympique Marseille</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamadou_Niang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Olympique Marseille">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, Niang moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympique_Marseille" title="Olympique Marseille">Marseille</a> for a reported fee of €7 million. He was joined six months later by Mickaël Pagis. Niang finished the season as Marseille's top scorer with ten goals but Marseille lost the <a href="/wiki/Coupe_de_France" title="Coupe de France">Coupe de France</a> final to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_FC" title="Paris Saint-Germain FC">Paris Saint-Germain</a>.</p>
<p>Niang would go on to finish the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ligue_1_2009-10" title="Ligue 1 2009-10">2009–10</a> season with 18 goals in Ligue 1, making him the highest scorer in the league and Marseille's highest scorer with 28 goals in all competitions.</p>
<p>Niang was selected <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympique_Marseille" title="Olympique Marseille">OM</a> player of the month for May 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fenerbah.C3.A7e">Fenerbahçe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamadou_Niang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fenerbahçe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He signed a 3+1 year deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fenerbah%C3%A7e_S.K._(football_team)" title="Fenerbahçe S.K. (football team)">Fenerbahçe</a> on 14 August 2010 for a fee of reported €8 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Mamadou Niang had made a great start with Fenerbahçe SK when he scored 7 goals in his first 6 league appearances. Niang scored his first hat-trick for Fenerbahçe on his 5th appearance when they won 6–2 against <a href="/wiki/Kas%C4%B1mpa%C5%9Fa_S.K." title="Kasımpaşa S.K.">Kasımpaşa</a>. Niang helped his side win the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="2010–11 Süper Lig">2010–11 Süper Lig</a>, scoring 16 goals in 29 appearances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Al_Sadd">Al Sadd</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamadou_Niang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Al Sadd">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 6 September 2011 he was sold to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sadd_Sports_Club" title="Sadd Sports Club">Al-Sadd</a> for €7.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 October 2011, at the <a href="/wiki/2011_AFC_Champions_League" title="2011 AFC Champions League">2011 AFC Champions League</a> Semi-final match against <a href="/wiki/Suwon_Samsung_Bluewings" title="Suwon Samsung Bluewings">Suwon Samsung Bluewings</a>, he scored the controversial,un-sportsmanlike second goal for his team. Al Sadd should've sent the ball back to the Bluewings since the play was suspended when the home team's player was injured. However, while Suwon's defense stood stil thinking their goalkeeper will get the ball back, Niang stole the ball and went past the goalkeeper to score. The goal induced an intrusion of a home fan and a huge melee of both players and bench. The controversy got bigger when Al Sadd's player Keita took a swing at a home fan. Even though his goal was against general notion of fair-play, Niang argued that there was no problem with his goal, and that it was Suwon who played without manners, inducing melee between two teams. After all, AFC did not conclude this issue justly, by giving disciplinary actions to Suwon only, Al Sadd getting absolutely nothing, not even Niang or Keita.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He then got himself sent-off in injury time for kicking the ball away when he was flagged offside, receiving another yellow card in addition to his previous, meaning he could not participate in the second leg in <a href="/wiki/Doha" title="Doha">Doha</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Be.C5.9Fikta.C5.9F_.28loan.29">Beşiktaş (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamadou_Niang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Beşiktaş (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 31 January 2013, he moved to Turkish side Beşiktaş on loan until the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 3 March 2013, he scored his first goal and contributed with an assist in a 3–2 win against his old club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fenerbah%C3%A7e_S.K._(football_team)" title="Fenerbahçe S.K. (football team)">Fenerbahçe</a> securing 3 points for his team in the final derby ever on <a href="/wiki/BJK_%C4%B0n%C3%B6n%C3%BC_Stadium" title="BJK İnönü Stadium">İnönü Stadium</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arles-Avignon">Arles-Avignon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamadou_Niang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Arles-Avignon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 28 August 2014, Niang returned to France to sign for <a href="/wiki/AC_Arles-Avignon" title="AC Arles-Avignon">AC Arles-Avignon</a>, having been overseas for the last four years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
