<p><i><b>Mamorukun Curse!</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">まもるクンは呪われてしまった!</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mamoru Has Been Cursed!</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Shoot_%27em_up" title="Shoot 'em up">shoot 'em up</a> <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade game</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/G.rev" title="G.rev">G.rev</a> and Gulti. The game was released for arcade in Japan on July 30, 2008. Mamorukun Curse! was later ported and released for the <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a> in Japan on June 25, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mamoru-kun_wa_Norowarete_Shimatta.21_Official_Page_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mamoru-kun_wa_Norowarete_Shimatta.21_Official_Page-1">[1]</a></sup> An enhanced port of game was released exclusively for the PlayStation 3 in Japan on March 31, 2011, offering full high-definition widescreen support with two Xbox 360 DLC characters included for free, and a new "Meikai Katsugeki" mode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mamoru-kun_PS3_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mamoru-kun_PS3-3">[3]</a></sup> UFO Interactive Games localized and released the PS3 version as a digital download on July 16, 2013. Sony made this version available to play on additional platforms through its <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Now" title="PlayStation Now">PlayStation Now</a> streaming service on March 17, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamorukun_Curse!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The game puts the player in control of Mamoru in an overhead shooter. Different from most shooters, the scrolling is not forced. The player is able to move about as they please, although most movements end up being up and down.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mamoru-kun_set_for_Xbox_360_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mamoru-kun_set_for_Xbox_360-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The "curse" part of the title comes from a special "curse bomb" attack which uses a separate button from the standard shot. These bombs can be used both to destroy the enemies' bullets, and to "curse" them, making them more powerful but opening up chances for a higher score.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mamoru-kun_set_for_Xbox_360_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mamoru-kun_set_for_Xbox_360-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
