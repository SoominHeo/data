<p><b>Maborikaigan Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">馬堀海岸駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Maborikaigan-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keikyu_Main_Line" title="Keikyu Main Line">Keikyu Main Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yokosuka,_Kanagawa" title="Yokosuka, Kanagawa">Yokosuka, Kanagawa</a>, Japan, operated by the private railway operator <a href="/wiki/Keikyu" title="Keikyu">Keikyu</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maborikaigan_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maborikaigan Station is served by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keikyu_Main_Line" title="Keikyu Main Line">Keikyu Main Line</a>, and is located 54.2 km from the starting point of the line at <a href="/wiki/Shinagawa_Station" title="Shinagawa Station">Shinagawa Station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maborikaigan_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maborikaigan Station is an elevated station with dual opposed <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platforms</a> serving two tracks.</p>
