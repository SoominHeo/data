<p><b>MBK Center</b>, also known as <b>Mahboonkrong</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th" xml:lang="th">à¸¡à¸²à¸à¸¸à¸à¸à¸£à¸­à¸</span>), is a large <a href="/wiki/Shopping_mall" title="Shopping mall">shopping mall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MBK1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MBK1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MBK2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-MBK2-2">[2]</a></sup> At eight stories high, the center contains around 2,000 shops, restaurants and service outlets, including the 4-story Tokyu department store.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MBK2_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-MBK2-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The MBK Center management reports daily visitor numbers of more than 100,000, half of whom are young Thai people and a third foreign visitors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MBKFaq1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MBKFaq1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The MBK Center is popular with tourists, although the majority of shoppers are Bangkok residents. Many stores selling authentic merchandise are also available. MBK Center is connected to the Siam Discovery and Siam Paragon shopping mall by elevated walkways, both of which are more upscale and have only authentic goods.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MBK_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MBK was the largest shopping mall in Asia when it opened in 1985. It is located on land leased from the adjacent <a href="/wiki/Chulalongkorn_University" title="Chulalongkorn University">Chulalongkorn University</a>. This lease was renewed in 2002. MBK was named after the parents of the developer Sirichai Bulakul, Mah &amp; Boonkrong, whose statues are located on the ground floor.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MBK_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MBK Center is located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pathum_Wan" title="Pathum Wan">Pathum Wan</a> district, on the southwest corner of the intersection of Rama I Road and <a href="/wiki/Phaya_Thai_Road" title="Phaya Thai Road">Phaya Thai Road</a>. It is close to <a href="/wiki/Siam_Square" title="Siam Square">Siam Square</a>, which can be reached from the second floor via a covered pedestrian bridge over Phaya Thai Road, and <a href="/wiki/Siam_Center" title="Siam Center">Siam Center</a> and <a href="/wiki/Siam_Paragon" title="Siam Paragon">Siam Paragon</a>, which are across Rama I Road from Siam Square.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AreaMap_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-AreaMap-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MBK_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bangkok_Skytrain" title="Bangkok Skytrain">Bangkok Skytrain</a> (BTS) - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Stadium_Station" title="National Stadium Station">National Stadium Station</a>; also within walking distance of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siam_Skytrain_Station" title="Siam Skytrain Station">Siam station</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khlong_Saen_Saeb" title="Khlong Saen Saeb">Khlong Saen Saeb</a> - Hua Chang pier is within walking distance.</li>
</ul>
