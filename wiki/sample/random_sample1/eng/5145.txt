<p><b>Hanako, Princess Hitachi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">正仁親王妃華子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Masahito Shinnōhi Hanako</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, born <b>Hanako Tsugaru</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">津軽華子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tsugaru Hanako</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> on 19 July 1940, is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_House_of_Japan" title="Imperial House of Japan">Japanese Imperial Family</a> as the wife of <a href="/wiki/Masahito,_Prince_Hitachi" title="Masahito, Prince Hitachi">Masahito, Prince Hitachi</a>, who is the younger son of <a href="/wiki/Hirohito" title="Hirohito">Emperor Shōwa</a> and the only brother of the current emperor, <a href="/wiki/Akihito" title="Akihito">Akihito</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hanako,_Princess_Hitachi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She is the fourth daughter of Count <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yoshitaka_Tsugaru&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yoshitaka Tsugaru (page does not exist)">Yoshitaka Tsugaru</a>, the last representative of the <a href="/wiki/Tsugaru_clan" title="Tsugaru clan">Tsugaru clan</a> and adopted son of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimyo" title="Daimyo">daimyo</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hirosaki_Domain" title="Hirosaki Domain">Tsugaru Domain</a> (present day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hirosaki,_Aomori" title="Hirosaki, Aomori">Hirosaki, Aomori</a>). Yoshitaka Tsugaru was originally from the <a href="/wiki/Gosanke" title="Gosanke">Owari branch</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_clan" title="Tokugawa clan">Tokugawa clan</a>. He was also a member of the aristocracy created by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meiji_restoration" title="Meiji restoration">Meiji restoration</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Kazoku" title="Kazoku">kazoku</a></i>).</p>
<p>Her mother, Hisako Mōri, was a descendant of the <a href="/wiki/M%C5%8Dri_clan" title="Mōri clan">Mōri clan</a> and also of the former daimyo of <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_Domain" title="Chōshū Domain">Chōshū Domain</a> in the former province of <a href="/wiki/Nagato_Province" title="Nagato Province">Nagato</a> (present day <a href="/wiki/Yamaguchi_Prefecture" title="Yamaguchi Prefecture">Yamaguchi</a>).</p>
<p>Princess Hitachi is descendant from the old feudal aristocracy. She is a second cousin to a niece of <a href="/wiki/Kikuko,_Princess_Takamatsu" title="Kikuko, Princess Takamatsu">Kikuko, Princess Takamatsu</a> who was, like her father, a descendant of the Tokugawa clan. She is also a second cousin to Takamasa Ikeda, former head of the <a href="/wiki/Ikeda_clan" title="Ikeda clan">Ikeda clan</a> and husband of her sister-in-law, <a href="/wiki/Atsuko_Ikeda" title="Atsuko Ikeda">Atsuko Ikeda</a>.</p>
<p>Hanako Tsugaru attended the prestigious <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gakushuin" title="Gakushuin">Gakushuin</a> School for her primary, junior high, and high school education, a school for Peers which its purpose was to educate the children of the imperial family and the imperial aristocracy (<a href="/wiki/Kuge" title="Kuge">kuge</a>). She graduated from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gakushuin_Women%27s_Junior_College" title="Gakushuin Women's Junior College">Gakushuin Women's Junior College</a> in 1961.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage">Marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hanako,_Princess_Hitachi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hanako met her future husband, <a href="/wiki/Masahito,_Prince_Hitachi" title="Masahito, Prince Hitachi">Prince Masahito</a>, during her studies at Gakushuin. The <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Household_Council" title="Imperial Household Council">Imperial Household Council</a> announced the engagement of Prince Masahito and Hanako Tsugaru on 28 February 1964 and the engagement ceremony was held on 14 April 1964. The wedding ceremony took place on 30 September 1964. Upon marriage, Prince Masahito received the title Prince Hitachi (Hitachi-no-miya – strictly "Prince Hitachi") and authorization from the Imperial Household Economy Council to form a new branch of the Imperial Family. As of tradition dictates, upon her entry into the imperial family and like other members, she received a personal <a href="/wiki/Emblem" title="Emblem">emblem</a> (<i>o-shirushi</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">お印</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>): <a href="/wiki/Rhododendron" title="Rhododendron">rhododendron</a> (<i>Tsutsuji</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ツツジ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>). They have no children.</p>
<p>Since December 1976, Prince Hitachi and Princess Hitachi have their official residence in a palace in large grounds off Komazawadori in <a href="/wiki/Higashi,_Shibuya" title="Higashi, Shibuya">Higashi</a> in the district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shibuya,_Tokyo" title="Shibuya, Tokyo">Shibuya</a> in Tokyo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>Princess Hitachi, like her husband, was elected on 5 September 2007 by the other members of the imperial family to be one of their main representatives (there are two in total) to the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Household_Council" title="Imperial Household Council">Imperial Household Council</a> as a member.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Both the members and reserve members, including Princess Hitachi, were re-elected on 7 September 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Princess Hitachi is president of various organizations that concern themselves with welfare and the arts. She has also translated various children's books from English into Japanese.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Translation">Translation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hanako,_Princess_Hitachi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Translation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Princess Hitachi has translated various children books from <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> into <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li><i>The Eighty-Ninth Kitten</i> by Eleanor Nilsson (published in 1987)</li>
<li><i>The Most Obedient Dog in the World</i>, by Anita Jeram (published in 1996)</li>
<li><i>It was Jake</i>, by Anita Jeram (published in 1997)</li>
<li><i>A Guide Dog Puppy Grows Up</i>, by Caroline Arnold (published in 2001)</li>
</ul>
