<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macenta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Macenta is the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Macenta_Prefecture" title="Macenta Prefecture">Macenta Prefecture</a> in southeastern Guinea is located in the <a href="/wiki/Guinea_Highlands" title="Guinea Highlands">Guinea Highlands</a> (at 620 metres (2,030 ft)) on the road from <a href="/wiki/Nz%C3%A9r%C3%A9kor%C3%A9" title="Nzérékoré">Nzérékoré</a> to <a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%A9ck%C3%A9dou" title="Guéckédou">Guéckédou</a>. The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nianda_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nianda River (page does not exist)">Nianda River</a> joins the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Makonda_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Makonda River (page does not exist)">Makonda River</a> near Macenta.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>French colonial and settlement influx influenced by Liberians circa 2000. It is the source of the 2014 African Ebola outbreak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macenta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Macenta is the major trading market town for <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">tea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">cassava</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shea_butter" title="Shea butter">shea butter</a> extract, <a href="/wiki/Kola_nut" title="Kola nut">kola nuts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palm_oil" title="Palm oil">palm oil</a>, and kernels grown in the surrounding agricultural area. A tea processing plant was built in Macenta in 1968, and the town has an agricultural research station, a sawmill, and several secondary schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> A trade in <a href="/wiki/Smuggling" title="Smuggling">smuggling</a> is believed to have developed in Macenta.</p>
<p>The town is served by <a href="/wiki/Macenta_Airport" title="Macenta Airport">Macenta Airport</a>.</p>
