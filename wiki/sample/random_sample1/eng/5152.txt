<p><b>Hiroya Masuda</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">増田 寛也</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Masuda Hiroya</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born December 20, 1951)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> politician who was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minister_of_Internal_Affairs_and_Communications_(Japan)" title="Minister of Internal Affairs and Communications (Japan)">Minister of Internal Affairs and Communications</a> from August 2007 to September 2008.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Yoichi_Masuzoe_Yasuo_Fukuda_and_Hiroya_Masuda_20071019.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="262" data-file-width="380" height="138" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Yoichi_Masuzoe_Yasuo_Fukuda_and_Hiroya_Masuda_20071019.jpg/200px-Yoichi_Masuzoe_Yasuo_Fukuda_and_Hiroya_Masuda_20071019.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Yoichi_Masuzoe_Yasuo_Fukuda_and_Hiroya_Masuda_20071019.jpg/300px-Yoichi_Masuzoe_Yasuo_Fukuda_and_Hiroya_Masuda_20071019.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c5/Yoichi_Masuzoe_Yasuo_Fukuda_and_Hiroya_Masuda_20071019.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

with <a href="/wiki/Yasuo_Fukuda" title="Yasuo Fukuda">Yasuo Fukuda</a> and <a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%9B%BD%E5%8B%99%E5%A4%A7%E8%87%A3" title="ja:国務大臣">Ministers of State</a></div>

<p>A native of <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, he graduated from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tokyo" title="University of Tokyo">University of Tokyo</a> and served as governor of <a href="/wiki/Iwate_Prefecture" title="Iwate Prefecture">Iwate Prefecture</a> from 1995 to 2007. While serving as Iwate's governor, Masuda developed a reputation as a reformist by cutting spending through personnel cuts and took the initiative in introducing an industrial waste tax system. Masuda was reappointed as Minister of Internal Affairs when Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Yasuo_Fukuda" title="Yasuo Fukuda">Yasuo Fukuda</a> reshuffled the cabinet on August 1, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In the Cabinet of Prime Minister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taro_Aso" title="Taro Aso">Taro Aso</a>, appointed on September 24, 2008, Masuda was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Kunio_Hatoyama" title="Kunio Hatoyama">Kunio Hatoyama</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Masuda wrote a 2013 article in <i>Chuo Koron</i> magazine arguing that growth in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> was leading to population decay in other regions of Japan, further describing Tokyo as a "population black hole" due to the difficulty of raising children there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Political and corporate leaders in Japan were jolted by the conclusions of a 2014 book by Masuda called <i>Local Extinctions</i>, a detailed report of population changes that used the latest official figures from the National Institution of Population and Social Security Research to show that 896 cities, towns and villages throughout Japan were facing extinction by 2040. At first glance, the book simply repeated what earlier reports had concluded; however, it also included the percentages by which child-bearing women between the ages of 20 and 40 were expected to decline in each and every city, town and village.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Masuda was approached in 2016 by twenty-one of the twenty-three <a href="/wiki/Special_wards_of_Tokyo" title="Special wards of Tokyo">Tokyo ward</a> mayors to run for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor_of_Tokyo" title="Governor of Tokyo">Governor of Tokyo</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_gubernatorial_election,_2016" title="Tokyo gubernatorial election, 2016">July 2016 election</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The Tokyo branch of the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democratic_Party_(Japan)" title="Liberal Democratic Party (Japan)">Liberal Democratic Party</a> threw its official support behind Masuda, warning members that they would be reprimanded if they supported rival <a href="/wiki/Yuriko_Koike" title="Yuriko Koike">Yuriko Koike</a> (also an LDP member).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
