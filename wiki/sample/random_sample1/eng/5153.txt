<p><b>Masjed Soleyman</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">مسجدسليمان</span>‎‎, <a href="/wiki/Luri_language" title="Luri language">Lurish</a>: مس‌سلیموو <i>Mas-seleymoo</i>; also <a href="/wiki/Romanization" title="Romanization">Romanized</a> as <b>Masjed Soleymān</b>, <b>Masjed-e Soleymān</b>, <b>Masjed Soleiman</b>, and <b>Masjid-i-Sulaiman</b>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a city in and the capital of <a href="/wiki/Masjed_Soleyman_County" title="Masjed Soleyman County">Masjed Soleyman County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khuzestan_Province" title="Khuzestan Province">Khuzestan Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. At the 2006 census, its population was 206,121, in 51,530 families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Masjed Soleiman is a historical city which is located among the <a href="/wiki/Zagros_Mountains" title="Zagros Mountains">Zagros Mountains</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Masjed_Soleyman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Masjed Soleiman's most interesting parish is Tembi, and historical and ancient site is Sarmasjed Temple in the northeast. Sarmasjed temple is on the verge of Sarmasjed terrace, in ancient time fire had been continuously flaming inside it. And according to historians was the capital of Achaemenian Reign. Among other Masjed Soleiman ancient relics, we can suggest the ancient Temple of Bard-e-Neshande.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_history_and_etymology">Ancient history and etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Masjed_Soleyman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ancient history and etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to historical scripts, In elamite empire the name of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masjed" title="Masjed">masjed</a> soleyman was Assak and after following the Persian invasion this name change to parsomash. They founded the city of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Parsumash&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Parsumash (page does not exist)">Parsumash</a> in memory of their motherland near the city of Anshan which is the fatherland of <a href="/wiki/Pasargadae" title="Pasargadae">Pasargadae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Persepolis" title="Persepolis">Persepolis</a>.</p>
<p>A French archaeological expedition under the supervision of Prof. Roman Ghirshman made some important discoveries. Ghirshman believes Parsomash (present-day Masjed Soleyman) was the oldest capital of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Achaemenian" title="Achaemenian">Achaemenian</a> Kingdom.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Contemporary_history">Contemporary history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Masjed_Soleyman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Contemporary history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Masjed Soleyman's importance today is in oil exploration. <a href="/wiki/William_Knox_D%27Arcy" title="William Knox D'Arcy">William Knox D'Arcy</a>, by contract with the Emir of Mohammerah, <a href="/wiki/Khaz%27al_Khan_al-Kaabi" title="Khaz'al Khan al-Kaabi">Sheikh Khaz'al Khan al-Kaabi</a>, obtained permission to explore for oil for the first time in the Middle East, near this city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> This event changed the history of the Middle East. The oil discovery led to petrochemical industry development and also the establishment of industries that strongly depended on oil.</p>
