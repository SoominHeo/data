<p><b>Massimo Corey Luongo</b> (born 25 September 1992) is an Australian international <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for English club <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_soccer_team" title="Australia national soccer team">Australian national team</a>.</p>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, Luongo played youth football for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APIA_Leichhardt_Tigers" title="APIA Leichhardt Tigers">APIA Leichhardt Tigers</a> before moving to England to play for <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>, where he started his professional career. Following a loan spell at <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a>, he played on loan at <a href="/wiki/Swindon_Town_F.C." title="Swindon Town F.C.">Swindon Town</a>, a move which was eventually made permanent. He joined current club <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> in 2015.</p>
<p>Luongo has played for the <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_soccer_team" title="Australia national soccer team">Australia national team</a> since 2014 and has made eighteen appearances. He was a member of the squad at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> and played a central role in Australia winning the <a href="/wiki/2015_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2015 AFC Asian Cup">2015 AFC Asian Cup</a>, where he scored in the <a href="/wiki/2015_AFC_Asian_Cup_Final" title="2015 AFC Asian Cup Final">final</a> and was named player of the tournament.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Massimo_Luongo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Luongo was born in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>. His father Mario is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Australian" title="Italian Australian">of Italian heritage</a> and mother Ira Luongo is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indonesian_Australian" title="Indonesian Australian">of Indonesian heritage</a>. He attended <a href="/wiki/Waverley_College" title="Waverley College">Waverley College</a> where he won the championship in 2009. He is the youngest of three children; he has a sister Angela and a brother Tiziano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In addition to holding an <a href="/wiki/Australian_passport" title="Australian passport">Australian passport</a>, Luongo also has an <a href="/wiki/Italian_passport" title="Italian passport">Italian passport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Massimo_Luongo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tottenham_Hotspur">Tottenham Hotspur</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Massimo_Luongo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tottenham Hotspur">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Luongo signed for <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in January 2011 after impressing on trial, and went on to make nine appearances for the <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy" title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C. Reserves and Academy">under-18 team</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Premier_Academy_League" title="2010–11 Premier Academy League">2010–11 Premier Academy League</a> season, scoring three goals. He made his only appearance for the first team on 20 September 2011 in a 7–6 <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout" title="Penalty shootout">penalty shootout</a> loss to fellow <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Football_League_Cup#Third_round" title="2011–12 Football League Cup">third round</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>, replacing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sandro_Raniere" title="Sandro Raniere">Sandro</a> after 70 minutes. Luongo had his penalty attempt saved by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_S%C3%B8rensen" title="Thomas Sørensen">Thomas Sørensen</a>, resulting in the defeat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 February 2012, he was called up to a league match for the first time, remaining an unused substitute as Tottenham earned a goalless draw away to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Thirteen days later he was included in the squad for the last time, again unused in a goalless <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> fifth round match away to League One team <a href="/wiki/Stevenage_F.C." title="Stevenage F.C.">Stevenage</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ipswich_Town_.28loan.29">Ipswich Town (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Massimo_Luongo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ipswich Town (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 July 2012, Luongo joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a> on a season-long loan for the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Football_League_Championship" title="2012–13 Football League Championship">2012–13 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He made his debut on 14 August in the first round of the League Cup, playing the entirety of a 3–1 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Two" title="Football League Two">League Two</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bristol_Rovers" title="Bristol Rovers">Bristol Rovers</a> at <a href="/wiki/Portman_Road" title="Portman Road">Portman Road</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Four days later he played his first professional league game, starting in a 1–1 home draw against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers" title="Blackburn Rovers">Blackburn Rovers</a> and making way for <a href="/wiki/Andy_Drury" title="Andy Drury">Andy Drury</a> after 70 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 28 August, in the second round of the League Cup against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carlisle_United" title="Carlisle United">Carlisle United</a> at <a href="/wiki/Brunton_Park" title="Brunton Park">Brunton Park</a>, he scored from outside the penalty area to put Ipswich ahead with his first professional goal, but Carlisle scored a late equaliser and won 2–1 after extra time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The loan was terminated on 9 November after new Ipswich manager <a href="/wiki/Mick_McCarthy" title="Mick McCarthy">Mick McCarthy</a> said that he wanted a 'different type of player'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Swindon_Town">Swindon Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Massimo_Luongo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Swindon Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 28 March 2013, Luongo signed for <a href="/wiki/Swindon_Town_F.C." title="Swindon Town F.C.">Swindon Town</a> on loan along with fellow Spurs trainees <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Byrne" title="Nathan Byrne">Nathan Byrne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dean_Parrett" title="Dean Parrett">Dean Parrett</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The very next day he went straight into the squad to face <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oldham_Athletic" title="Oldham Athletic">Oldham Athletic</a> and play the full 90 minutes in a 1–1 draw at the <a href="/wiki/County_Ground_(Swindon)" title="County Ground (Swindon)">County Ground</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On 16 April, Luongo scored his first goal for Swindon in a 4–1 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra" title="Crewe Alexandra">Crewe Alexandra</a>, heading in <a href="/wiki/Gary_Roberts_(footballer,_born_1984)" title="Gary Roberts (footballer, born 1984)">Gary Roberts</a>' cross.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He opened the scoring on 4 May in the 70th minute of the first leg of the play-off semi-final against <a href="/wiki/Brentford_F.C." title="Brentford F.C.">Brentford</a>, but in added time conceded a penalty by fouling <a href="/wiki/Harry_Forrester_(footballer)" title="Harry Forrester (footballer)">Harry Forrester</a>; <a href="/wiki/Kevin_O%27Connor_(footballer,_born_1982)" title="Kevin O'Connor (footballer, born 1982)">Kevin O'Connor</a> converted it for a 1–1 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Swindon eventually lost the tie in a penalty shootout.</p>
<p>Luongo signed a season-long loan deal with Swindon Town on 2 July 2013 and was handed the number 4 shirt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> However, on 31 August 2013, Swindon signed Luongo on a permanent three-year contract having agreed a fee of £400,000 with Spurs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He scored six goals in 44 league appearances <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Football_League_One" title="2013–14 Football League One">that season</a>, including a first professional brace in a 5–2 home win over <a href="/wiki/Port_Vale_F.C." title="Port Vale F.C.">Port Vale</a> on 2 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In the following campaign, he got just as many goals but in 34 matches, as Swindon lost the <a href="/wiki/2015_Football_League_One_play-off_Final" title="2015 Football League One play-off Final">play-off final</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preston_North_End" title="Preston North End">Preston North End</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Queens_Park_Rangers">Queens Park Rangers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Massimo_Luongo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Queens Park Rangers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 28 May 2015, Luongo joined <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Swindon_Town_F.C." title="Swindon Town F.C.">Swindon Town</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Ben_Gladwin" title="Ben Gladwin">Ben Gladwin</a>, both signing a three-year deal for an undisclosed fee reported to be £3.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> QPR head coach <a href="/wiki/Chris_Ramsey_(footballer)" title="Chris Ramsey (footballer)">Chris Ramsey</a> was Luongo's youth coach at Tottenham.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He made his debut in the first game of the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Football_League_Championship" title="2015–16 Football League Championship">Championship season</a> on 8 August, playing the full 90 minutes of a 2–0 defeat at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic" title="Charlton Athletic">Charlton Athletic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Luongo played 30 league games in his first season – 32 overall – He provided 6 league assists but did not score.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> However, his performances earned him a place on the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Ballon_d%27Or" title="FIFA Ballon d'Or">FIFA Ballon d'Or</a> longlist for the <a href="/wiki/2015_FIFA_Ballon_d%27Or#FIFA_Ballon_d.27Or" title="2015 FIFA Ballon d'Or">2015 campaign</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Massimo_Luongo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Luongo represented the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_under-20_association_football_team" title="Australia national under-20 association football team">Australian under-20 team</a> twice but was not selected for the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>He made his debut for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Socceroos" title="Socceroos">Socceroos</a> on 6 March 2014 as a second-half substitute for captain <a href="/wiki/Mile_Jedinak" title="Mile Jedinak">Mile Jedinak</a> in the 3–4 loss to <a href="/wiki/Ecuador_national_football_team" title="Ecuador national football team">Ecuador</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_New_Den" title="The New Den">The New Den</a> in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> He was selected for the 23-man <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australia_national_association_football_team" title="Australia national association football team">Australia squad</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> by manager <a href="/wiki/Ange_Postecoglou" title="Ange Postecoglou">Ange Postecoglou</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> but did not feature in any of their three matches as they were eliminated in the group stage.</p>
<p>Luongo was also selected in the 23-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2015_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2015 AFC Asian Cup">Asia Cup</a> to be played on home soil in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Swindon team mate <a href="/wiki/Yaser_Kasim" title="Yaser Kasim">Yaser Kasim</a> was also called up for his home nation <a href="/wiki/Iraq_national_football_team" title="Iraq national football team">Iraq</a>, meaning that the club would have to compete in their regular <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Football_League_One" title="2014–15 Football League One">League One season</a> without the two central midfielders for a month. He went on to score in Australia's 4–1 <a href="/wiki/2015_AFC_Asian_Cup_Group_A#Australia_vs_Kuwait" title="2015 AFC Asian Cup Group A">win</a> over <a href="/wiki/Kuwait_national_football_team" title="Kuwait national football team">Kuwait</a> in the opening game of the tournament, in addition to providing the assist that led to <a href="/wiki/Tim_Cahill" title="Tim Cahill">Tim Cahill</a> scoring Australia's first goal of the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kerr_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-kerr-28">[28]</a></sup> At the end of the game, he was named as man of the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Luongo also started in Australia's second group game against <a href="/wiki/Oman_national_football_team" title="Oman national football team">Oman</a>, providing the assist for <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Kruse" title="Robbie Kruse">Robbie Kruse</a> to score Australia's second goal in an eventual 4–0 win. He played in the <a href="/wiki/2015_AFC_Asian_Cup_Final" title="2015 AFC Asian Cup Final">2015 AFC Asian Cup Final</a> against <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_football_team" title="South Korea national football team">South Korea</a>, scoring the first goal from outside the box in a 2–1 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afc_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-afc-30">[30]</a></sup> He was named as Most Valuable Player of the tournament after scoring two goals and assisting four throughout the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mvp_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-mvp-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
