<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Matsudaira Tadanao</h1>

<p><b>Matsudaira Tadanao</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">松平 忠直</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 16 July 1595 – 5 October 1650)</span> was a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Daimy%C5%8D" title="Daimyō">daimyō</a></i> in the early <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. He was the head of the <a href="/wiki/Fukui_Domain" title="Fukui Domain">Fukui Domain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matsudaira_Tadanao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When Tadanao was born, his name was Matsudaira Senchiyo. He was the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%ABki_Hideyasu" title="Yūki Hideyasu">Yūki Hideyasu</a>, by his concubine Lady Nakagawa.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mature_years">Mature years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matsudaira_Tadanao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mature years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When Hideyasu died in 1607, Tadanao became clan leader and head of his father's domain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-papinot30_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-papinot30-2">[2]</a></sup> Four years later he married his cousin <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Katsuhime&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Katsuhime (page does not exist)">Katsuhime</a>, the daughter of the 2nd shogun <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Hidetada" title="Tokugawa Hidetada">Hidetada</a>. He led Echizen troops in battle at the Osaka Winter Campaign, but suffered a high casualty rate, for which he was scolded by his grandfather, the retired shogun <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Ieyasu</a>. He took part in the next year's Summer Campaign, taking the head of <a href="/wiki/Sanada_Yukimura" title="Sanada Yukimura">Sanada Yukimura</a>, and leading his forces at the very tip of the Tokugawa advance into <a href="/wiki/Osaka_Castle" title="Osaka Castle">Osaka Castle</a>. However, he received no reward for his efforts, and his court rank remained at the comparatively low <i>jusanmi-sangi</i> (従三位参議; junior 3rd rank, councilor), though his father had been <i>chūnagon</i> (中納言; Middle Councilor). Tadanao was so upset at this that in 1621 he feigned illness and did not make his required trip to Edo; in 1622 he even plotted the death of his wife (who was saved at the last moment by one of her maids taking her place). He even led his own soldiers on rampages through the homes of retainers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1622, he was banished to Ogiwara in <a href="/wiki/Bungo_Province" title="Bungo Province">Bungo Province</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-papinot30_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-papinot30-2">[2]</a></sup> Tadanao also entered the Buddhist priesthood, taking the name Ippaku (一伯). He died in 1650 at age 56.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Tadanao's son <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Matsudaira_Mitsunaga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Matsudaira Mitsunaga (page does not exist)">Mitsunaga</a> was transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Takada_Domain" title="Takada Domain">Takada Domain</a> in <a href="/wiki/Echigo_Province" title="Echigo Province">Echigo Province</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Tadanao's brother <a href="/wiki/Matsudaira_Tadamasa" title="Matsudaira Tadamasa">Tadamasa</a> was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Fukui_Domain" title="Fukui Domain">Fukui</a>, The clan continued to hold the fief until the end of the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
