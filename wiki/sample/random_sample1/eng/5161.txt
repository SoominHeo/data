<div class="hatnote" role="note">In this <a href="/wiki/Japanese_name" title="Japanese name">Japanese name</a>, the family name is Matsumae.</div>
<p><b>Matsumae Takahiro</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">松前 崇広</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, December 10, 1829 – June 9, 1866)</span> was a Japanese daimyo of the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, who ruled the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Matsumae_Domain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Matsumae Domain (page does not exist)">Matsumae Domain</a>. Though he was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tozama_daimyo" title="Tozama daimyo">tozama daimyo</a>, he served in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Shogunate" title="Tokugawa Shogunate">Tokugawa Shogunate</a> as a <a href="/wiki/R%C5%8Dj%C5%AB" title="Rōjū">rōjū</a>. His court title was <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kokushi_(officials)" title="Kokushi (officials)">Izu no kami</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matsumae_Takahiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Takahiro, whose childhood name was Tamekichi, was born at Fukuyama Castle in <a href="/wiki/Ezo" title="Ezo">Ezo</a>. He was the 6th son of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Matsumae_Akihiro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Matsumae Akihiro (page does not exist)">Matsumae Akihiro</a>, the 9th lord of Matsumae. At age 4, he was sent to the family estate in <a href="/wiki/Edo" title="Edo">Edo</a>. In an act most unusual for a daimyo's son, his education included the study of the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a>.</p>
<p>He succeeded to lordship of Matsumae in 1849.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rise_to_Power_as_R.C5.8Dj.C5.AB">Rise to Power as Rōjū</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matsumae_Takahiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rise to Power as Rōjū">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Takahiro was appointed as <a href="/wiki/R%C5%8Dj%C5%AB" title="Rōjū">rōjū</a> in November 1864; this was a post that was unheard of for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tozama_daimyo" title="Tozama daimyo">tozama daimyo</a>. The following year, he and his fellow rōjū Abe Masato were responsible for the opening of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hy%C5%8Dgo" title="Hyōgo">Hyōgo</a> port to foreign trade. However, as they did so against the wishes of the imperial court, the court issued orders calling for their dismissal from office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Both men lost their offices, court rank, and titles, and were forced to yield lordship of their domains. Takahiro retired in favor of his son <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Matsumae_Norihiro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Matsumae Norihiro (page does not exist)">Norihiro</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matsumae_Takahiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Takahiro returned to Matsumae in the spring of 1866. Soon after, he contracted a fever and died at age 36. A few years after Takahiro's death, his grandson Nagahiro petitioned the imperial court for a pardon, which was granted, along with a restoration of his court rank and titles.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matsumae_Takahiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Takahiro has appeared infrequently in fictional depictions of the bakumatsu era. Most recently he appeared as a minor character in the <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Kaze_Hikaru" title="Kaze Hikaru">Kaze Hikaru</a></i>.</p>
