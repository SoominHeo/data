<p><b>Matsubushi</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">松伏町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Matsubushi-machi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Kita-Katsushika_District,_Saitama" title="Kita-Katsushika District, Saitama">Kitakatsushika District</a> <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>, in the central <a href="/wiki/Kant%C5%8D_region" title="Kantō region">Kantō region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 1 February 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Matsubushi,_Saitama&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 29,946 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 1850 persons per km². Its total area was 16.20 square kilometres (6.25 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matsubushi,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Located in the <a href="/wiki/Shim%C5%8Dsa_Plateau" title="Shimōsa Plateau">Shimōsa Plateau</a> of far eastern Saitama Prefecture, Matsubushi is on the central reaches of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edogawa_River" title="Edogawa River">Edogawa River</a> and borders upon Chiba Prefecture to the east.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matsubushi,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of Matsubushi was part of <a href="/wiki/Shim%C5%8Dsa_Province" title="Shimōsa Province">Shimōsa Province</a> prior to the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a>. The village of Matsubushiryo was created within <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitakatsushika_District,_Saitama" title="Kitakatsushika District, Saitama">Kitakatsushika District, Saitama</a> with the establishment of the municipalities system on April 1, 1889. It was renamed Matsubushi on April 1, 1956 and elevated to town status on April 1, 1969.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matsubushi,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Matsubushi was formerly a center of <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a> production, but farmland is giving way to <a href="/wiki/Urbanization" title="Urbanization">urbanization</a>. The town now produces eggs, <i>hakusai</i> <a href="/wiki/Chinese_cabbage" title="Chinese cabbage">Chinese cabbage</a> and <i>negi</i>, the Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Welsh_onion" title="Welsh onion">welsh onion</a>. The town is home to the Matsubushi Campus of <a href="/wiki/Taisho_University" title="Taisho University">Taisho University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matsubushi,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Matsubushi has three elementary schools, two middle schools and one high school.</p>
