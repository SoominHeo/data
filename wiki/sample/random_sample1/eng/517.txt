<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">2018 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group F</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>2018 FIFA World Cup qualification UEFA Group F</b> is one of the nine <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2018 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">UEFA groups</a> for <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="2018 FIFA World Cup qualification">2018 FIFA World Cup qualification</a>. The group consists of six teams: <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovakia_national_football_team" title="Slovakia national football team">Slovakia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_national_football_team" title="Lithuania national football team">Lithuania</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a>.</p>
<p>The draw for the first round (group stage) was held as part of the 2018 FIFA World Cup Preliminary Draw on 25 July 2015, starting 18:00 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moscow_Standard_Time" title="Moscow Standard Time">MSK</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B03:00" title="UTC+03:00">UTC+3</a>), at the Konstantinovsky Palace in <a href="/wiki/Strelna" title="Strelna">Strelna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>, Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-draw_format_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-draw_format-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group winners will qualify directly for the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 FIFA World Cup</a>. Among the nine group runners-up, the eight best runners-up will advance to the play-offs, where they will be drawn into four home-and-away ties to determine the other four qualifiers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-draw_format_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-draw_format-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Goal_difference" title="Goal difference">Goal difference</a></li>
<li>Goals scored</li>
<li>Points in matches between tied teams</li>
<li>Goal difference in matches between tied teams</li>
<li>Goals scored in matches between tied teams</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Away_goals_rule" title="Away goals rule">Away goals</a> scored in matches between tied teams (if the tie is only between two teams in home-and-away league format)</li>
<li>Fair play points
<ul>
<li>first yellow card: minus 1 point</li>
<li>indirect red card (second yellow card): minus 3 points</li>
<li>direct red card: minus 4 points</li>
<li>yellow card and direct red card: minus 5 points</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Drawing of lots by the FIFA Organising Committee</li>
</ol>

<p>A player is automatically suspended for the next match for the following offences:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-regulations_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Receiving a red card (red card suspensions may be extended for serious offences)</li>
<li>Receiving two yellow cards in two different matches (yellow card suspensions are carried forward to the play-offs, but not the finals or any other future international matches)</li>
</ul>
