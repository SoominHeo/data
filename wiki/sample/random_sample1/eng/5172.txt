<p><b>Maeda Toshimasu</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">前田 利益</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, 1543? - 1612)</span>, better known as <b>Maeda Keiji <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">前田慶次</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></b> or <b>Keijirō</b> (慶次郎), was a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sengoku_Period" title="Sengoku Period">Sengoku Period</a> through early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edo_Period" title="Edo Period">Edo Period</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maeda_Toshimasu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Toshimasu was born in the village of Arako (present-day <a href="/wiki/Nakagawa-ku,_Nagoya" title="Nakagawa-ku, Nagoya">Nakagawa-ku, Nagoya</a>), Toshimasu was born to the Takigawa Clan, originally the son of <a href="/wiki/Takigawa_Kazumasu" title="Takigawa Kazumasu">Takigawa Kazumasu</a>. He was adopted by Maeda Toshihisa, the older brother of <a href="/wiki/Maeda_Toshiie" title="Maeda Toshiie">Maeda Toshiie</a>. Toshimasu served under <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobunaga" title="Oda Nobunaga">Oda Nobunaga</a> along with his uncle. Toshimasu was originally intended to inherit Maeda family headship; however, after <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobunaga" title="Oda Nobunaga">Oda Nobunaga</a> replaced Toshihisa with Toshiie as Maeda family head, he lost this position. Perhaps because of this loss of inheritance, Toshimasu is well known for quarreling with his uncle.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1581, Toshimasu was under the command of his uncle, and made a reputation for himself through conflicts within <a href="/wiki/Noto_Province" title="Noto Province">Noto Province</a> and was rewarded with 5,000 koku. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Komaki-Nagakute" title="Battle of Komaki-Nagakute">Battle of Komaki-Nagakute</a> three years later, Toshimasu went to <a href="/wiki/Sassa_Narimasa" title="Sassa Narimasa">Sassa Narimasa</a>'s rescue when he was attacked at <a href="/wiki/Suemori_Castle" title="Suemori Castle">Suemori Castle</a>. While in <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a>, Toshimasu met and befriended <a href="/wiki/Naoe_Kanetsugu" title="Naoe Kanetsugu">Naoe Kanetsugu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uesugi_Kagekatsu" title="Uesugi Kagekatsu">Uesugi Kagekatsu</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Kar%C5%8D" title="Karō">karō</a></i>. The two became close friends, and Toshimasu agreed to join Kanetsugu in the Uesugi clan's invasion of <a href="/wiki/Aizu" title="Aizu">Aizu</a>. The invasion failed, and Keiji was appointed to led the rear guard during the retreat. Riding his horse Matsukaze into battle and brandishing a two-pronged spear, he made a splendid show of force. Due in part to Toshimasu's actions, the Uesugi forces were able to retreat largely intact.</p>
<p>After this, Toshimasu returned to the capital and devoted himself to arts and literature. Keiji was barred from <a href="/wiki/Toyotomi_clan" title="Toyotomi clan">Toyotomi’s</a> Kyushu campaign for his wild ways. When the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_clan" title="Tokugawa clan">Tokugawa</a> challenged the <a href="/wiki/Uesugi" title="Uesugi">Uesugi</a> in 1600, he once again fought with Uesugi’s army. In the battle against the <a href="/wiki/Mogami_clan" title="Mogami clan">Mogami</a>, legend says he broke through the enemy lines with only eight riders, and shattered their formation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> After the Uesugi clan's move to the <a href="/wiki/Yonezawa_Domain" title="Yonezawa Domain">Yonezawa Domain</a>, Toshimasu remained as a retainer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Matsukaze">Matsukaze</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maeda_Toshimasu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Matsukaze">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Matsukaze</b> ("wind in the pines") was Toshimasu's famous mount. According to the legend, he was bred of only the finest horses, but he refused to let anyone ride him and eventually ran away. Toshimasu managed to tame the wild horse, possibly due to Toshimasu's own wild personality. Since then, the two were never seen apart.</p>
<p>Matsukaze was said to be a horse of immense strength, able to carry his master's large frame for days. Supposedly, Matsukaze ran away after his master's death, never seen again.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Depictions_in_fiction">Depictions in fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maeda_Toshimasu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Depictions in fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/People_of_the_Sengoku_period_in_popular_culture#Mazda_Hiroshima" title="People of the Sengoku period in popular culture">People of the Sengoku period in popular culture § Mazda Hiroshima</a></div>
<p>Toshimasu is a playable character from the Western Army in the original <i><a href="/wiki/Kessen" title="Kessen">Kessen</a></i> and in the <i><a href="/wiki/Samurai_Warriors" title="Samurai Warriors">Samurai Warriors</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Warriors_Orochi" title="Warriors Orochi">Warriors Orochi</a></i> under the name Keiji, and <i><a href="/wiki/Sengoku_Basara" title="Sengoku Basara">Sengoku BASARA</a></i> series, as "Meiji Damaged". He is a playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Conquest" title="Pokémon Conquest">Pokémon Conquest</a></i> (Pokémon + Nobunaga's Ambition in Japan), with his partner <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon" title="Pokémon">Pokémon</a> being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bastiodon" title="Bastiodon">Bastiodon</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terrakion" title="Terrakion">Terrakion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pok.C3.A9mon_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pok.C3.A9mon-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
