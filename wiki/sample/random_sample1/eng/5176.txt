<p><b>Maebashi Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">前橋駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Maebashi-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Ry%C5%8Dm%C5%8D_Line" title="Ryōmō Line">Ryōmō Line</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maebashi,_Gunma" title="Maebashi, Gunma">Maebashi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gunma_Prefecture" title="Gunma Prefecture">Gunma Prefecture</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East). It is one of two main railway stations of Maebashi; the other is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D_Maebashi_Station" title="Chūō Maebashi Station">Chūō Maebashi Station</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Private_railway" title="Private railway">private railway</a> operator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J%C5%8Dm%C5%8D_Electric_Railway" title="Jōmō Electric Railway">Jōmō Electric Railway</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maebashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maebashi Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/Ry%C5%8Dm%C5%8D_Line" title="Ryōmō Line">Ryōmō Line</a>, and is located 81.9 kilometers from the starting point of the line at <a href="/wiki/Oyama_Station" title="Oyama Station">Oyama Station</a>. Some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dnan_Shinjuku_Line" title="Shōnan Shinjuku Line">Shōnan Shinjuku Line</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ueno-Tokyo_Line" title="Ueno-Tokyo Line">Ueno-Tokyo Line</a> services also originate at this station.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maebashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maebashi Station has two elevated <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platforms</a> serving three tracks, with the station building underneath. The station has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midori_no_Madoguchi" title="Midori no Madoguchi">Midori no Madoguchi</a> ticket office.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maebashi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maebashi Station was opened by Ryōmō Railway on November 20, 1889. From August 20, 1884, <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Railway" title="Nippon Railway">Nippon Railway</a> operated another Maebashi Station on the other side of the <a href="/wiki/Tone_River" title="Tone River">Tone River</a>, but this station was closed on December 26, 1889 when a bridge across the river was completed and Nippon Railway trains began sharing the Ryōmō Railway station. Ryōmō Railway was merged into Nippon Railway on January 1, 1897 and Nippon Railway was <a href="/wiki/Railway_Nationalization_Act" title="Railway Nationalization Act">nationalized</a> on November 1, 1906.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
