<p><b>Mayenne</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[majɛn]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a> in northwest <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> named after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayenne_River" title="Mayenne River">Mayenne River</a>. Mayenne is part of the current region of <a href="/wiki/Pays_de_la_Loire" title="Pays de la Loire">Pays de la Loire</a> and is surrounded by the departments of <a href="/wiki/Manche" title="Manche">Manche</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orne" title="Orne">Orne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarthe" title="Sarthe">Sarthe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maine-et-Loire" title="Maine-et-Loire">Maine-et-Loire</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ille-et-Vilaine" title="Ille-et-Vilaine">Ille-et-Vilaine</a>.</p>
<p>Mayenne is one of the original 83 departments created during the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a> on March 4, 1790. The northern two thirds correspond to the western part of the <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_France" title="Provinces of France">former province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Maine_(province)" title="Maine (province)">Maine</a>. The southern third of Mayenne corresponds to the northern portion of the old province of <a href="/wiki/Anjou#In_the_Kingdom_of_France" title="Anjou">Anjou</a>. The inhabitants of the department are called <i>Mayennais</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayenne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like 82 other departments, Mayenne was created on March 4, 1790 during the early stages of the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a> by order of the <a href="/wiki/National_Constituent_Assembly" title="National Constituent Assembly">National Constituent Assembly</a>. The new departments were to be uniformly administered and approximately equal to one another in size and population. The former province of Maine was partitioned into two, Upper Maine, centred on <a href="/wiki/Le_Mans" title="Le Mans">Le Mans</a>, became the new department of Sarthe, and Lower Maine, centred on Laval became the new department of Mayenne. <a href="/wiki/Anjou" title="Anjou">Anjou</a>, to the south, being too big to form a single department, was reduced in size and became Maine-et-Loire. In this partition, Sarthe received the region of <a href="/wiki/La_Fl%C3%A8che" title="La Flèche">La Flèche</a>, and Mayenne received <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau-Gontier" title="Château-Gontier">Château-Gontier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Craon,_Mayenne" title="Craon, Mayenne">Craon</a>. <a href="/wiki/Flax" title="Flax">Flax</a> was a feature of the Mayenne economy, and the southern limit for the cultivation of flax was used to determine the new border between Mayenne and Maine-et-Loire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayenne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mayenne is a department in northwestern France and is part of the region of <a href="/wiki/Pays_de_la_Loire" title="Pays de la Loire">Pays de la Loire</a>. The department does not have a sea coast, but about thirty kilometres to the northwest is <a href="/wiki/Mont_Saint-Michel_Bay" title="Mont Saint-Michel Bay">Mont Saint-Michel Bay</a>. The capital and largest town is <a href="/wiki/Laval,_Mayenne" title="Laval, Mayenne">Laval</a> in the centre of the department. To the north lies the department of <a href="/wiki/Orne" title="Orne">Orne</a>, to the east lies <a href="/wiki/Sarthe" title="Sarthe">Sarthe</a>, to the south lies <a href="/wiki/Maine-et-Loire" title="Maine-et-Loire">Maine-et-Loire</a>, to the west lies <a href="/wiki/Ille-et-Vilaine" title="Ille-et-Vilaine">Ille-et-Vilaine</a> and to the northwest lies <a href="/wiki/Manche" title="Manche">Manche</a>. The department forms a roughly rectangular shape, being 90 km (56 mi) long by 77 km (48 mi) wide, with a total area of about 5,175 km<sup>2</sup> (1,998 sq mi). The <a href="/wiki/Mayenne_(river)" title="Mayenne (river)">River Mayenne</a> flows centrally through it from north to south, passing through the towns of <a href="/wiki/Mayenne,_Mayenne" title="Mayenne, Mayenne">Mayenne</a>, Laval and Château-Gontier. After leaving the department, the river joins the <a href="/wiki/Sarthe_(river)" title="Sarthe (river)">River Sarthe</a> to form the <a href="/wiki/Maine_(river)" title="Maine (river)">River Maine</a> which later joins the <a href="/wiki/Loire" title="Loire">River Loire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Philips_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Philips-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The department is varied in <a href="/wiki/Topography" title="Topography">topography</a>. Much of it is largely flat, but there are also hilly areas, some with steep-sided valleys and ravines. Of the total area of 1,275,532 acres (516,189 ha), some 875,000 acres (354,100 ha) are arable, 170,000 acres (68,800 ha) are grassland, 65,000 acres (26,300 ha) are forests and woodland and 50,000 acres (20,200 ha) are heathland and moorland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dictionary_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dictionary-3">[3]</a></sup> To the north lies the <a href="/wiki/Armorican_Massif" title="Armorican Massif">Armorican Massif</a>, a plateau that has been eroded over time, the highest summit of which, the Mont des Avaloirs, is the highest point in the department at 417 m (1,368 ft) above sea level. A branch range to the south of this plateau forms the ridge that divides the Mayenne Valley from the <a href="/wiki/Vilaine" title="Vilaine">Vilaine Valley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dictionary_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dictionary-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The department is subdivided into three arrondissements, Mayenne, Laval and Château-Gontier, and along with the department of Sarthe, Mayenne forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_see" title="Episcopal see">episcopal see</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Le_Mans" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Le Mans">Roman Catholic Diocese of Le Mans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dictionary_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Dictionary-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayenne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mayenne has a diversity of habitat types such as forest, heathland, bog and farmland. Some 1445 species of plants, 63 species of mammals, 280 species of birds, 16 species of amphibians and 11 species of reptiles have been recorded, as well as thousands of species of <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>. The peat-lands and bogs are often fringed with woodlands of alder and ash, and in some places carnivorous plants such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sundew" title="Sundew">sundew</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Butterwort" title="Butterwort">butterwort</a> flourish, <a href="/wiki/Fritillaria_meleagris" title="Fritillaria meleagris">fritillaries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Comarum_palustre" title="Comarum palustre">marsh cinquefoil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eriophorum_angustifolium" title="Eriophorum angustifolium">cottongrass</a> grow and butterflies, dragonflies and spiders abound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exposition_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Exposition-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The woodlands are mostly small with the deciduous trees dominated by <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oak</a>. Here <a href="/wiki/Roe_deer" title="Roe deer">roe deer</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_badger" title="European badger">badger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fire_salamander" title="Fire salamander">fire salamander</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aesculapian_snake" title="Aesculapian snake">Aesculapian snake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middle_spotted_woodpecker" title="Middle spotted woodpecker">middle spotted woodpecker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Little_owl" title="Little owl">little owl</a> and <a href="/wiki/Limenitis_camilla" title="Limenitis camilla">white admiral</a> can be found and uncommon plants present including <a href="/wiki/Aquilegia_vulgaris" title="Aquilegia vulgaris">European columbine</a> and wild <a href="/wiki/Russet_apple" title="Russet apple">russet apple</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exposition_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Exposition-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The dry grasslands, which cover the limestone and sandstone soils, are also rich in fauna and flora. They house the snake <i><a href="/wiki/Vipera_aspis" title="Vipera aspis">Vipera aspis</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Large_blue" title="Large blue">large blue butterfly</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Oedipoda_caerulescens" title="Oedipoda caerulescens">blue-winged grasshopper</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bee_orchid" title="Bee orchid">bee orchid</a>. The heathland in the north of Mayenne is populated by <a href="/wiki/Ulex_minor" title="Ulex minor">dwarf gorse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Erica_tetralix" title="Erica tetralix">cross-leaved heath</a> and there are plenty of spiders, <a href="/wiki/Nightjar" title="Nightjar">nightjars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warbler" title="Warbler">warblers</a>. The old quarries are the refuge of bats, amphibians, the <a href="/wiki/Geranium_lucidum" title="Geranium lucidum">shining cranesbill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Platanthera_chlorantha" title="Platanthera chlorantha">greater butterfly orchid</a>. Rivers and ponds are home to <a href="/wiki/Eel" title="Eel">eel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_crested_newt" title="Northern crested newt">northern crested newt</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_otter" title="European otter">European otter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingfisher" title="Kingfisher">kingfisher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grass_snake" title="Grass snake">grass snake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Common_moorhen" title="Common moorhen">common moorhen</a> and plants such as <a href="/wiki/Ranunculus" title="Ranunculus">spearwort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iris_pseudacorus" title="Iris pseudacorus">yellow flag</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sagittaria_sagittifolia" title="Sagittaria sagittifolia">arrowhead</a> and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Isopyrum_thalictroides&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Isopyrum thalictroides (page does not exist)">Isopyrum thalictroides</a></i>, a small poisonous plant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exposition_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Exposition-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayenne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The department is largely rural with about 80% being used for agriculture, 8% being urban area and the remainder forest, heath and plantations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Livestock farming predominates, with the breeding of cattle, horses and pigs, and also bee-keeping being important.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Everyman_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Everyman-6">[6]</a></sup> The soil is generally poor, but it is of better quality around Laval and Château-Gontier. In these parts corn is cultivated and there are plantings of hemp, flax, fruits and vines. There are many apple orchards and large quantities of <a href="/wiki/Cider" title="Cider">cider</a> are made.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dictionary_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Dictionary-3">[3]</a></sup> The department is rich in mineral resources; iron and coal are mined and there are quarries for <a href="/wiki/Marble" title="Marble">marble</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slate" title="Slate">slate</a>, building stone, limestone and flint; the white sand deposits are used in the manufacture of glass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dictionary_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Dictionary-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Industries include the manufacture of linen, paper and hemp, and cider-making is traditionally carried on in the department.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Everyman_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Everyman-6">[6]</a></sup> Office furniture is manufactured in Château-Gontier,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and Laval is active in the industrial sector, with dairy products, electronics and chemicals in a modern <a href="/wiki/Science_park" title="Science park">science park</a>.</p>
