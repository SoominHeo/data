<p><b>Maurizio Pollini</b> (born January 5, 1942) is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">classical</a> <a href="/wiki/Pianist" title="Pianist">pianist</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maurizio_Pollini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pollini was born in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> to the Italian rationalist architect <a href="/wiki/Gino_Pollini" title="Gino Pollini">Gino Pollini</a>, who has been said to have been the first to bring Modernist architecture to Italy in the 1930s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC_Classic_FM_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABC_Classic_FM-1">[1]</a></sup> Pollini studied piano first with Carlo Lonati, until the age of 13, then with <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Vidusso" title="Carlo Vidusso">Carlo Vidusso</a>, until he was 18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He received a diploma from the <a href="/wiki/Milan_Conservatory" title="Milan Conservatory">Milan Conservatory</a> and won both the <a href="/wiki/International_Ettore_Pozzoli_Piano_Competition" title="International Ettore Pozzoli Piano Competition">International Ettore Pozzoli Piano Competition</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seregno" title="Seregno">Seregno</a> (<a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>) in 1959 and the <a href="/wiki/International_Chopin_Piano_Competition" title="International Chopin Piano Competition">International Chopin Piano Competition</a> in <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a> in 1960.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Rubinstein" title="Arthur Rubinstein">Arthur Rubinstein</a>, who led the jury, declared Pollini the winner of the competition, allegedly saying: "that boy can play the piano better than any of us".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AR_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-AR-4">[4]</a></sup> Soon afterwards, he recorded Chopin's Concerto No. 1 in E minor with the <a href="/wiki/Philharmonia_Orchestra" title="Philharmonia Orchestra">Philharmonia Orchestra</a> under the Polish conductor <a href="/wiki/Paul_Kletzki" title="Paul Kletzki">Paul Kletzki</a> for <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a>, and taped performances of Chopin's etudes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> When the Philharmonia offered Pollini a series of concerts, he experienced what EMI producer <a href="/wiki/Peter_Andry" title="Peter Andry">Peter Andry</a> has called "an apparent crisis of confidence".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peter_andry_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-peter_andry-6">[6]</a></sup> After this, he studied with <a href="/wiki/Arturo_Benedetti_Michelangeli" title="Arturo Benedetti Michelangeli">Arturo Benedetti Michelangeli</a>, from whom he is said to have acquired "a precise technique and emotional restraint",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> although some have expressed a concern that Michelangeli's influence resulted in Pollini's playing becoming "mannered and cold".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peter_andry_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-peter_andry-6">[6]</a></sup> During the early 1960s, Pollini limited his concertizing, preferring to spend these years studying by himself and expanding his repertoire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since the mid-1960s, he has given recitals and appeared with major orchestras in Europe, the United States, and the Far East. He made his American debut in 1968 and his first tour of Japan in 1974.</p>
<p>During the 1960s and 1970s, Pollini was a left-wing political activist. He collaborated with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luigi_Nono_(composer)" title="Luigi Nono (composer)">Luigi Nono</a> in such works as <i>Como una ola de fuerza y luz</i> (1972), which was to mourn the assassination of Luciano Cruz, a leader of the Chilean Revolutionary Front. He performed with <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Abbado" title="Claudio Abbado">Claudio Abbado</a> at La Scala in a cycle of concerts for students and workers, in an attempt to build a new public as they believed that art should be for everybody.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AR2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-AR2-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1985, on the occasion of <a href="/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach" title="Johann Sebastian Bach">Johann Sebastian Bach</a>'s tricentenary, he performed the complete first book of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Well-Tempered_Clavier" title="The Well-Tempered Clavier">The Well-Tempered Clavier</a></i>. In 1987 he played the complete piano concertos of <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_van_Beethoven" title="Ludwig van Beethoven">Ludwig van Beethoven</a> in New York with the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Philharmonic" title="Vienna Philharmonic">Vienna Philharmonic</a> under Claudio Abbado and received on this occasion the orchestra's Honorary Ring. In 1993-94 he played his first complete Beethoven Piano Sonata cycles in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> and later also in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/Salzburg_Festival" title="Salzburg Festival">Salzburg Festival</a> in 1995 he inaugurated the "Progetto Pollini", a series of concerts in which old and new works are juxtaposed. An analogous series took place at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a> in 2000-01 with "Perspectives: Maurizio Pollini" and at London's <a href="/wiki/Royal_Festival_Hall" title="Royal Festival Hall">Royal Festival Hall</a> in 2010-11 with the "Pollini Project", a series of five concerts with programmes ranging from Bach to Stockhausen.</p>
<p>In March 2012 it was announced that Pollini had cancelled all his forthcoming appearances in the USA for health reasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Pollini played on a tour including the <a href="/wiki/Salzburg_Festival" title="Salzburg Festival">Salzburg Festival</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and his debut at the <a href="/wiki/Rheingau_Musik_Festival" title="Rheingau Musik Festival">Rheingau Musik Festival</a>, playing in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurhaus_Wiesbaden" title="Kurhaus Wiesbaden">Kurhaus Wiesbaden</a> Chopin's <i><a href="/wiki/Preludes_(Chopin)" title="Preludes (Chopin)">Preludes</a></i> (Op. 28) and Book 1 of Debussy's <i><a href="/wiki/Pr%C3%A9ludes_(Debussy)" title="Préludes (Debussy)">Preludes</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blum_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blum-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pollini is father of the pianist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Daniele_Pollini&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Daniele Pollini (page does not exist)">Daniele Pollini</a> (born 1978).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recordings">Recordings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maurizio_Pollini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recordings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His first recordings for <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Grammophon" title="Deutsche Grammophon">Deutsche Grammophon</a> in 1971 included <a href="/wiki/Igor_Stravinsky" title="Igor Stravinsky">Stravinsky</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Trois_mouvements_de_Petrouchka" title="Trois mouvements de Petrouchka">Trois mouvements de Petrouchka</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Prokofiev" title="Sergei Prokofiev">Prokofiev</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Piano_Sonata_No._7_(Prokofiev)" title="Piano Sonata No. 7 (Prokofiev)">Seventh Sonata</a> and are still considered a landmark of twentieth century piano discography. Since then he has been one of Deutsche Grammophon's leading pianists. His recording of <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Chopin" title="Frédéric Chopin">Chopin</a>'s <a href="/wiki/%C3%89tudes_(Chopin)" title="Études (Chopin)">Etudes, Opp. 10 and 25</a>, also under Deutsche Grammophon, won Pollini international acclaim. In 2002, Deutsche Grammophon released a 13-CD commemorative edition to celebrate the pianist's 60th birthday.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Repertoire_and_technique">Repertoire and technique</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maurizio_Pollini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Repertoire and technique">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pollini is especially noted for his performances of <a href="/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach" title="Johann Sebastian Bach">Bach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_van_Beethoven" title="Ludwig van Beethoven">Beethoven</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franz_Schubert" title="Franz Schubert">Schubert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Chopin" title="Frédéric Chopin">Chopin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Schumann" title="Robert Schumann">Schumann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Brahms" title="Johannes Brahms">Brahms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Schoenberg" title="Arnold Schoenberg">Schoenberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anton_Webern" title="Anton Webern">Webern</a> and for championing modern composers such as <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Boulez" title="Pierre Boulez">Pierre Boulez</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luigi_Nono_(composer)" title="Luigi Nono (composer)">Luigi Nono</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karlheinz_Stockhausen" title="Karlheinz Stockhausen">Karlheinz Stockhausen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giacomo_Manzoni" title="Giacomo Manzoni">Giacomo Manzoni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salvatore_Sciarrino" title="Salvatore Sciarrino">Salvatore Sciarrino</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Maderna" title="Bruno Maderna">Bruno Maderna</a>. Important modern works have been composed for Pollini, notably Nono's <i>…sofferte onde serene…</i>, Manzoni's <i>Masse: omaggio a Edgard Varèse</i> and Sciarrino's Fifth Sonata.</p>
<p>While known for possessing an exceptional technique, Pollini is sometimes accused of emotional conservatism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He has conducted both <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orchestra" title="Orchestra">orchestral</a> music, sometimes leading the orchestra from the keyboard in <a href="/wiki/Piano_concerto" title="Piano concerto">concertos</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_recognition">Awards and recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maurizio_Pollini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards and recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1996, he received the <a href="/wiki/Ernst_von_Siemens_Music_Prize" title="Ernst von Siemens Music Prize">Ernst von Siemens Music Prize</a>. In 2001, his recording of Beethoven's <i><a href="/wiki/Diabelli_Variations" title="Diabelli Variations">Diabelli Variations</a></i> won the Diapason d'or. In 2007, Pollini received the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Instrumental_Soloist_Performance_(without_orchestra)" title="Grammy Award for Best Instrumental Soloist Performance (without orchestra)">Best Instrumental Soloist Performance (without orchestra)</a> for his <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Grammophon" title="Deutsche Grammophon">Deutsche Grammophon</a> recording of <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Chopin" title="Frédéric Chopin">Chopin</a> <a href="/wiki/Nocturne" title="Nocturne">nocturnes</a>. He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Praemium_Imperiale" title="Praemium Imperiale">Praemium Imperiale</a> in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PI_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-PI-14">[14]</a></sup> He entered the <a href="/wiki/Gramophone_(magazine)" title="Gramophone (magazine)">Gramophone</a> Hall of Fame in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
