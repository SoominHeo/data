<p><i><b>My Girl</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">마이걸</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Mai Geol</span></i>) is a 2005 <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">South Korean television series</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Lee_Da-hae" title="Lee Da-hae">Lee Da-hae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Dong-wook" title="Lee Dong-wook">Lee Dong-wook</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Joon-gi" title="Lee Joon-gi">Lee Joon-gi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Park_Si-yeon" title="Park Si-yeon">Park Si-yeon</a>. It aired on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a> from December 14, 2005 to February 2, 2006 on Wednesdays and Thursdays at 21:55 for 16 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy" title="Romantic comedy">romantic comedy</a> series was a hit during its run—it placed number one in its timeslot and reached a peak viewership rating of 24.9%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It also catapulted actors Lee Da-hae, Lee Dong-wook and Lee Joon-gi into <a href="/wiki/Korean_Wave" title="Korean Wave">Korean Wave</a> stardom.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Girl_(2005_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A native of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeju_Island" title="Jeju Island">Jeju Island</a>, Joo Yoo-rin lives with her father, who is addicted to gambling. Because of her father's debts, Yoo-rin has become particularly skilled at lying and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grifting" title="Grifting">grifting</a>. When her father escapes the island to hide from his debtors, Yoo-rin is determined to support herself and clear up the debts. One day, she meets Seol Gong-chan, the sole heir to the L'Avenue Hotel fortune.</p>
<p>To grant his grandfather's dying wish, Gong-chan employs Yoo-rin to act as his grandfather's long-lost granddaughter. Offering a monthly salary plus a bonus, he asks her to do what she does best—to put on an act. Not wishing to lie to a dying man, yet desperate for the money, Yoo-rin takes the offer and starts playing the long-lost granddaughter. Through a strange twist of fate, however, finding his granddaughter makes the grandfather so happy that he makes a complete recovery. Since they have claimed to be cousins, Gong-chan and Yoo-rin are forced to live together under one roof, and as time passes, the attraction between them grows. However, <a href="/wiki/Cousin_marriage#Far_East" title="Cousin marriage">love is forbidden</a> for these two, who must pass as cousins.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_characters">Main characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Girl_(2005_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Main characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lee_Da-hae" title="Lee Da-hae">Lee Da-hae</a> as Joo Yoo-rin</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>A tour guide in charge of running mini tours in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeju_Island" title="Jeju Island">Jeju Island</a>. Her father is a gambling addict, but despite all the debts that he runs up, Yoo-rin always finds a way to help repay them. She leads a below-average life but is happy as long as she has her father with her. Because of her father's gambling ways, she has had to move from country to country whenever her father runs from one place to another to avoid their debtors. This has in turn brought her the ability to converse fluently in Chinese and Japanese, which are of great help as a tour guide whenever she has to entertain tourists from China and Japan.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lee_Dong-wook" title="Lee Dong-wook">Lee Dong-wook</a> as Seol Gong-chan</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>The only heir of the owner of L'Avenuel Hotel, which is one of the top hotels in Korea. His grandfather, Seol Woong, charges him with the duty of finding his granddaughter, who is Gong-chan's cousin.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lee_Joon-gi" title="Lee Joon-gi">Lee Joon-gi</a> as Seo Jung-woo<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>The son of one of the major shareholders of L'Avenuel Hotel, and Gong-chan's best friend. Unlike Gong-chan who is responsible and hardworking, Jung-woo is a flirt who is known to have many girlfriends.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Park_Si-yeon" title="Park Si-yeon">Park Si-yeon</a> as Kim Se-hyun<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Gong-chan's ex-girlfriend. A rising star in the tennis world, she returns to Korea hoping to reconcile with Gong-chan.</dd>
</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_broadcast">International broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Girl_(2005_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series aired on <a href="/wiki/Gala_Television" title="Gala Television">GTV</a> in <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> in June 2006, followed by a <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> airing in July 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and airings in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> on <a href="/wiki/8TV_(Malaysia)" title="8TV (Malaysia)">8TV</a> and <a href="/wiki/Animax_Asia" title="Animax Asia">Animax Asia</a> that same year.</p>
<p>It began airing in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> on <a href="/wiki/TV_Tokyo" title="TV Tokyo">TV Tokyo</a> on September 6, 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and was re-aired on cable channel KNTV from April 9 to March 16, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>It aired in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> on <a href="/wiki/ABS-CBN" title="ABS-CBN">ABS-CBN</a> from May 29 to August 18, 2006. It was re-aired from July 14 to August 15, 2014 on the network's "Kapamilya Gold" afternoon block.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, it aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swarnawahini" title="Swarnawahini">Swarnawahini</a> under the title <i>Thatu Ahimi Samanali</i>.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, it aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBTV_Channel_7" title="BBTV Channel 7">Channel 7</a> under the tite <i>rạk h̄md cı yạy kal̀xn</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remake">Remake</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=My_Girl_(2005_TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Remake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There have been three <a href="/wiki/Remake" title="Remake">remakes</a> of <i>My Girl</i>, produced in Indonesia, the Philippines and Taiwan.</p>
<p>The Indonesian remake <i><a class="extiw" href="https://id.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benci_Jadi_Cinta" title="id:Benci Jadi Cinta">Benci Jadi Cinta</a></i> starred <a href="/wiki/Nia_Ramadhani" title="Nia Ramadhani">Nia Ramadhani</a> as Alya, <a class="extiw" href="https://id.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonathan_Frizzy" title="id:Jonathan Frizzy">Jonathan Frizzy</a> as Reihan, <a class="extiw" href="https://id.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al_Fathir_Muchtar" title="id:Al Fathir Muchtar">Al Fathir Muchtar</a> as Irfan, and <a class="extiw" href="https://id.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donita" title="id:Donita">Donita</a> as Tasha. It aired on <a href="/wiki/RCTI" title="RCTI">RCTI</a> in 2006.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/ABS-CBN" title="ABS-CBN">ABS-CBN</a> aired the Philippine remake, also titled <i><a href="/wiki/My_Girl_(2008_TV_series)" title="My Girl (2008 TV series)">My Girl</a></i>, from May 26 to September 5, 2008. It starred <a href="/wiki/Kim_Chiu" title="Kim Chiu">Kim Chiu</a> as Jasmine Estocapio, <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Anderson" title="Gerald Anderson">Gerald Anderson</a> as Julian Abueva, <a href="/wiki/Enchong_Dee" title="Enchong Dee">Enchong Dee</a> as Nico Legaspi, and <a href="/wiki/Ni%C3%B1a_Jose" title="Niña Jose">Niña Jose</a> as Anika Ramirez.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Taiwanese network <a href="/wiki/Jiangsu_Television" title="Jiangsu Television">JSTV</a> aired a remake from October 19 to November 1, 2011. Retitled <i><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Sweetheart" title="Rainbow Sweetheart">Rainbow Sweetheart</a></i>, it starred <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Lin" title="Jimmy Lin">Jimmy Lin</a> as Shao Feng, <a href="/wiki/Cherrie_Ying" title="Cherrie Ying">Cherrie Ying</a> as Peng Xiao Qian, Zou Ting Wei as Xu Kai, and Hou Shu as Luo Ying Ying.</p>
