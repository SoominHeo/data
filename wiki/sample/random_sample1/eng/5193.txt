<p><b>Mainichi Broadcasting System, Inc.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">株式会社毎日放送</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kabushiki-gaisha" title="Kabushiki-gaisha">Kabushiki-gaisha</a> Mainichi Hōsō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, Mainichi Broadcasting System Stock‑Company)</span> is a radio and television broadcasting company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, affiliated with <a href="/wiki/Japan_Radio_Network" title="Japan Radio Network">Japan Radio Network (JRN)</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Radio_Network_(Japan)" title="National Radio Network (Japan)">National Radio Network (NRN)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan_News_Network" title="Japan News Network">Japan News Network (JNN)</a> and TBS Network, serving in the Kansai region.</p>
<p>It is a parent company of a television station named <a href="/wiki/MBS_TV" title="MBS TV">MBS TV</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">MBSテレビ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and a radio station named <a href="/wiki/MBS_Radio_(Japan)" title="MBS Radio (Japan)">MBS Radio</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">MBSラジオ</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. MBS is also one of the major stockholders of the following networks : <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">Tokyo Broadcasting System Holdings, Inc. (TBSHD)</a>, <a href="/wiki/RKB_Mainichi_Broadcasting" title="RKB Mainichi Broadcasting">RKB Mainichi Broadcasting Corporation</a>, <a href="/wiki/BS-TBS" title="BS-TBS">BS-TBS, Incorporated</a>, <a href="/wiki/I-Television" title="I-Television">i-Television Inc.</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=TV-U_Fukushima&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="TV-U Fukushima (page does not exist)">TV-U Fukushima Co., Ltd.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hiroshima_Home_Television" title="Hiroshima Home Television">Hiroshima Home Television Co., Ltd.</a>, <a href="/wiki/WOWOW" title="WOWOW">WOWOW Inc.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/FM802" title="FM802">FM 802 Co., Ltd.</a></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_MBS">History of MBS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mainichi_Broadcasting_System&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History of MBS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>December 27, 1950: <b>New Japan Broadcasting Company</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">新日本放送株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shin-Nippon Hōsō Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, NJB)</span> was founded.</li>
<li>September 1, 1951: NJB started radio broadcasting from <a href="/wiki/Hankyu_Department_Store" title="Hankyu Department Store">Hankyu Department Store</a> as the second commercial one in Japan.</li>
<li>December 1, 1956: NJB founded <b>Osaka Television Co., Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">大阪テレビ放送株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ōsaka Terebi Hōsō Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, OTV)</span> with <a href="/wiki/Asahi_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Asahi Broadcasting Corporation">Asahi Broadcasting Corporation</a> (ABC).</li>
<li>June 1, 1958: NJB was renamed "<b>Mainichi Broadcasting System, Inc.</b>"</li>
<li>March 1, 1959: After selling all stocks of OTV to ABC, MBS started analog terrestrial television broadcasting independently from OTV on, and made a network with Nippon Educational Television Co., Ltd. (NET, the predecessor of <a href="/wiki/TV_Asahi" title="TV Asahi">TV Asahi</a> Corporation).</li>
<li>1960: A broadcasting studio was completed in Senri.</li>
<li>1964: MBS formed a radio network with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TBS_Radio_%26_Communications" title="TBS Radio &amp; Communications">TBS Radio</a> and <a href="/wiki/RKB_Mainichi_Broadcasting" title="RKB Mainichi Broadcasting">RKB Radio</a>, which evolved into <a href="/wiki/Japan_Radio_Network" title="Japan Radio Network">Japan Radio Network</a> (JRN) in 1965.</li>
<li>1974: MBS joined a television news network called <a href="/wiki/All-Nippon_News_Network" title="All-Nippon News Network">All-Nippon News Network</a>　(ANN).</li>
<li>March 31, 1975: MBS changed to a news network called <a href="/wiki/Japan_News_Network" title="Japan News Network">Japan News Network</a> (JNN) because the president of the <a href="/wiki/Asahi_Shimbun" title="Asahi Shimbun">Asahi Shimbun</a> in those days ordered ABC to change the flagship station to NET.</li>
<li>May 15, 1977: The frequency of <a href="/wiki/MBS_Radio_(Japan)" title="MBS Radio (Japan)">MBS Radio</a> changed from 1210 kHz to 1180 kHz.</li>
<li>5 a.m., November 23, 1978: the frequency of <a href="/wiki/MBS_Radio_(Japan)" title="MBS Radio (Japan)">MBS Radio</a> changed again from 1180 kHz to 1179 kHz.</li>
<li>1990: New headquarters and new broadcasting studio was completed in Chayamachi, Kita-ku, Osaka for the 40th anniversary of starting broadcasting. MBS moved and merged the headquarters and studio on September 1; the registered headquarters from the <a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Shimbun" title="Mainichi Shimbun">Mainichi Shimbun</a> Osaka Head Office, and the broadcasting studio from Senri.</li>
<li>September 1, 1991: 40th anniversary of MBS's starting broadcasting</li>
<li>September 29, 2000: MBS Now airs for the last time. It was replaced by Voice on October 2.</li>
<li>March 31, 2001: A broadcasting studio called "MBS Studio in USJ" was opened at Universal Studios Japan.</li>
<li>September 1, 2001: 50th anniversary of MBS's starting broadcasting</li>
<li>11 a.m., December 1, 2003: MBS started digital terrestrial TV broadcasting.</li>
<li>September 1, 2006: 55th anniversary of MBS's starting broadcasting</li>
<li>May 15, 2010: MBS started the Internet protocol simulcast radio service via "radiko" together with ABC, OBC, FM 802, FM Osaka, and FM Cocolo.</li>
<li>March, 2011: The construction of the new building started in the north of the head office, completed on September 4, 2013.</li>
<li>noon, July 24, 2011: MBS terminated analog terrestrial TV broadcasting.</li>
<li>September 1, 2011: The corporate logo of MBS was renewed for the 60th anniversary of the station's starting broadcasting. The new MBS logo is in use for the TV program credits.</li>
<li>September 4, 2013: The construction of the new building was completed and named "B Building". The headquarters building was named "M Building".</li>
<li>October 1, 2013: Takaishi Solar Plant was situated in the area of MBS Takaishi Radio Transmitter. MBS sells electricity to the Kansai Electric Power Company, Inc. for 20 years.</li>
<li>11:07:09, April 4, 2014: The B Building opened.</li>
</ul>
