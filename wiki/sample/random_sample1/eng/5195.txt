<p><b>Mysore State</b> was a separate state within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_India" title="Union of India">Union of India</a> from 1948 until 1956<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Mysore" title="Mysore">Mysore</a> as its capital. The state was considerably enlarged in 1956 when it became a linguistically homogeneous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kannada_language" title="Kannada language">Kannada</a>-speaking state in 1956, within the Union of India. It was subsequently incorporated into the state of Karnataka.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mysore_State&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Mysore" title="Kingdom of Mysore">Kingdom of Mysore</a> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/aɪ/ long 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/ɔər/ 'ore' in 'bore'">ɔər</span></span>/</a></span></span> was one of the three largest <a href="/wiki/Princely_state" title="Princely state">princely states</a> within the former British <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">Empire of India</a>. Upon <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> gaining its independence in 1947, the <a href="/wiki/Maharaja_of_Mysore" title="Maharaja of Mysore">Maharaja of Mysore</a> signed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Instrument_of_accession" title="Instrument of accession">instrument of accession</a> incorporating his realm with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_India" title="Union of India">Union of India</a> on 15 August 1947. The territories of the erstwhile princely state of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Mysore" title="Kingdom of Mysore">Mysore</a> were then reconstituted into a state within the Union of India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Karnataka_1956_Reorg.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="748" data-file-width="693" height="237" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Karnataka_1956_Reorg.svg/220px-Karnataka_1956_Reorg.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Karnataka_1956_Reorg.svg/330px-Karnataka_1956_Reorg.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Karnataka_1956_Reorg.svg/440px-Karnataka_1956_Reorg.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>In 1956, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">Government of India</a> effected a comprehensive re-organisation of provincial boundaries, based upon the principle of shared language. As a result of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/States_Reorganisation_Act" title="States Reorganisation Act">States Reorganisation Act</a> on 1 November 1956, the Kannada-speaking districts of <a href="/wiki/Belgaum_district" title="Belgaum district">Belgaum</a> (except <a href="/wiki/Kolhapur#Chandgad" title="Kolhapur">Chandgad taluk</a>), <a href="/wiki/Bijapur_district,_Karnataka" title="Bijapur district, Karnataka">Bijapur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dharwad_district" title="Dharwad district">Dharwar</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uttara_Kannada" title="Uttara Kannada">North Canara</a> were transferred from <a href="/wiki/Bombay_State" title="Bombay State">Bombay State</a> to Mysore State.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sorg1956_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sorg1956-3">[3]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bellary_district" title="Bellary district">Bellary district</a> was transferred from <a href="/wiki/Andhra_State" title="Andhra State">Andhra State</a>. <a href="/wiki/South_Canara" title="South Canara">South Canara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Udupi_district" title="Udupi district">Udupi districts</a> were transferred from <a href="/wiki/Madras_State" title="Madras State">Madras State</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Koppal_district" title="Koppal district">Koppal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raichur_district" title="Raichur district">Raichur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gulbarga_district" title="Gulbarga district">Gulbarga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bidar_district" title="Bidar district">Bidar districts</a> from <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad_State_(1948%E2%80%9356)" title="Hyderabad State (1948–56)">Hyderabad State</a>. Also small <a href="/wiki/Coorg_State" title="Coorg State">Coorg State</a> was merged, becoming a district of Mysore State.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
