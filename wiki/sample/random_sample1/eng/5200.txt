<p><b>MAIET Entertainment</b> was a South Korean <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">video game developer</a>, best known for creating the third-person shooter game <i><a href="/wiki/GunZ:_The_Duel" title="GunZ: The Duel">GunZ: The Duel</a></i> in 2004. They also developed <a href="/wiki/GunZ_2" title="GunZ 2">GunZ 2</a> in 2011 and adapted it into English in 2013.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MAIET_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the official site, MAIET is 'TEAM' spelled backwards with the letter 'I' added in, standing for 'innovation'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maiet.co.kr_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-maiet.co.kr-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MAIET_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company started out as a small group of five researchers which eventually grew into a company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maiet.co.kr_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-maiet.co.kr-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2015 they closed their doors after selling all of their game rights to Masangsoft Inc. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maietgames.com_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-maietgames.com-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MAIET_Entertainment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/GunZ:_The_Duel" title="GunZ: The Duel">GunZ: The Duel</a> has servers in Korea and Brazil, and has all evolved from the original International server in many ways, such as having "cash items". MAIET's first developed video game was the aerial strategy game <i>AceSaga</i>, which was terminated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
