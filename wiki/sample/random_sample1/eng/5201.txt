<p><b>Maiori</b> (<a href="/wiki/Neapolitan_language" title="Neapolitan language">Neapolitan</a>, <i>Majure</i>; originally <i>Reghinna Maior</i>) is a town and <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> on the <a href="/wiki/Amalfi_Coast" title="Amalfi Coast">Amalfi coast</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Salerno" title="Province of Salerno">province of Salerno</a> (<a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">Campania</a>, Italy). It has been a popular tourist resort since <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> times, with the longest unbroken stretch of <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">beach</a> on the Amalfi coastline.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maiori&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The origins of the town are unclear but the original name of the town was Reghinna Maior, in contrast to the neighbouring town, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minori,_Italy" title="Minori, Italy">Minori</a>, Reghinna Minor. All places along the coast were formed by alternating conquerors - such as the Etruscans or the Romans.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Italy_Maiori_Beach.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Italy_Maiori_Beach.JPG/250px-Italy_Maiori_Beach.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Italy_Maiori_Beach.JPG/375px-Italy_Maiori_Beach.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Italy_Maiori_Beach.JPG/500px-Italy_Maiori_Beach.JPG 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Italy_Maiori_Beach.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Beach of Maiori.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Between 830 and 840, the places of the coast united to form a confederation of Amalfi States. In this Amalfi Sea Republic, the places between Lettere and Tramonti and between Cetara and Positano, along with the island of Capri, were united; and their inhabitants were all called Amalfitaner. At that time, each city retained its own name and administrative autonomy, but had a specific role in this federation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Salerno" title="Principality of Salerno">Principality of Salerno</a>, and then of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Naples" title="Kingdom of Naples">Kingdom of Naples</a>, of which it followed the history until the 19th century.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maiori&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Collegiate Church of Santa Maria a Mare. The colourful maiolica tiled dome looks like Maiori's crown, whose jewels are treasured inside the Sacred Art Museum just next to the Church.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Santa Maria de Olearia in Maiori. The ruins of the XI Benedictine Abbey just above Maiori, is a great way to enjoy the most ancient part of town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Falerzio Mount and Avvocata Church. The highest peak of the Amalfi Coast and the Sanctuary on the plain above Mount Mirteto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_film">In film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maiori&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the mid-20th century, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Rossellini" title="Roberto Rossellini">Roberto Rossellini</a> filmed some of his films here: <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pais%C3%A0" title="Paisà">Paisà</a></i> (1946); "Il Miracolo" ("The Miracle"), the second episode of the movie <i><a href="/wiki/L%27Amore_(film)" title="L'Amore (film)">L'Amore</a></i> (<i>Ways of Love</i>, 1948); <i>La macchina ammazzacattivi</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Machine_to_Kill_Bad_People" title="Machine to Kill Bad People">Machine to Kill Bad People</a></i>, 1952); and <i>Il viaggio in Italia</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Journey_to_Italy" title="Journey to Italy">Journey to Italy</a></i>, 1953). A film festival is held every November at which the <i>Premio Internazionale Roberto Rossellini</i> is awarded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
