<p><b>Maika Monroe</b> (born <b>Dillon Monroe Buckley</b>; May 29, 1993) is an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a> actress and professional <a href="/wiki/Kitesurfing" title="Kitesurfing">kiteboarder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She made her acting debut in the 2012 film <i><a href="/wiki/At_Any_Price_(film)" title="At Any Price (film)">At Any Price</a></i>. In 2013, she appeared alongside <a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a> in the drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Labor_Day_(film)" title="Labor Day (film)">Labor Day</a></i>. She became recognized as a "<a href="/wiki/Scream_queen" title="Scream queen">scream queen</a>" after her performances in the critically acclaimed 2014 films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guest_(film)" title="The Guest (film)">The Guest</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/It_Follows" title="It Follows">It Follows</a></i>. She also played Patricia Whitmore in <i><a href="/wiki/Independence_Day:_Resurgence" title="Independence Day: Resurgence">Independence Day: Resurgence</a></i> (2016), replacing <a href="/wiki/Mae_Whitman" title="Mae Whitman">Mae Whitman</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maika_Monroe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Monroe was born Dillon Monroe Buckley in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara,_California" title="Santa Barbara, California">Santa Barbara, California</a>, the daughter of Dixie, a sign language interpreter, and Jack Buckley, who works in construction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She later changed her first name to "Maika", by which she had already been publicly called for most of her life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-undmag1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-undmag1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2012, Monroe signed up for the drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Labor_Day_(film)" title="Labor Day (film)">Labor Day</a></i>, an adaptation of the <a href="/wiki/Labor_Day_(novel)" title="Labor Day (novel)">novel of the same name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She played Mandy, a farm girl who romances with a young man and eventually becomes his wife and the mother of his child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In June 2013, she joined the cast of the psychological thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guest_(film)" title="The Guest (film)">The Guest</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2014, she starred as Jay in the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/It_Follows" title="It Follows">It Follows</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> which became a cult hit and secured Monroe's status as a <a href="/wiki/Scream_queen" title="Scream queen">scream queen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2016, she co-starred in the invasion thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/The_5th_Wave_(film)" title="The 5th Wave (film)">The 5th Wave</a></i>, based on the <a href="/wiki/The_5th_Wave_(novel)" title="The 5th Wave (novel)">novel of the same name</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Monroe also co-starred in the 2016 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Independence_Day:_Resurgence" title="Independence Day: Resurgence">Independence Day: Resurgence</a></i>, the sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(1996_film)" title="Independence Day (1996 film)">Independence Day</a></i> (1996), playing former First Daughter Patricia Whitmore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
