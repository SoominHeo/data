<p><b>Maicon Pereira Roque</b> (born 14 September 1988), known simply as <b>Maicon</b>, is a Brazilian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_FC" title="São Paulo FC">São Paulo FC</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maicon_Pereira_Roque&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_.2F_Porto">Early years / Porto</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maicon_Pereira_Roque&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years / Porto">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barretos,_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Barretos, São Paulo">Barretos, São Paulo</a>, Maicon started off his career with <a href="/wiki/Cruzeiro_Esporte_Clube" title="Cruzeiro Esporte Clube">Cruzeiro Esporte Clube</a>. He moved the following season to modest <a href="/wiki/Associa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Desportiva_Cabofriense" title="Associação Desportiva Cabofriense">Associação Desportiva Cabofriense</a>.</p>
<p>Maicon had his first abroad adventure in the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Primeira_Liga" title="2008–09 Primeira Liga">2008–09 campaign</a>, being a defensive mainstay for <a href="/wiki/Madeira" title="Madeira">Madeira</a>'s <a href="/wiki/C.D._Nacional" title="C.D. Nacional">C.D. Nacional</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> which again <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2009–10 UEFA Europa League">qualified</a> for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">European competition</a>. Courtesy of those solid performances, on 4 June 2009, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a> champions <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a>, in a contract running until 2014;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> he appeared in only four games in <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Primeira_Liga" title="2009–10 Primeira Liga">his first year</a>.</p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Alves" title="Bruno Alves">Bruno Alves</a> left for <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">FC Zenit Saint Petersburg</a>, Maicon became a first-team regular in <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Primeira_Liga" title="2010–11 Primeira Liga">2010–11</a>, playing in 37 official games as Porto won <a href="/wiki/Treble_(association_football)" title="Treble (association football)">the treble</a> (including eight in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2010–11 UEFA Europa League">victorious run</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a>). In <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Primeira_Liga" title="2011–12 Primeira Liga">the following season</a> – under new manager <a href="/wiki/V%C3%ADtor_Pereira_(football_manager)" title="Vítor Pereira (football manager)">Vítor Pereira</a> – after <a href="/wiki/Cristian_S%C4%83punaru" title="Cristian Săpunaru">Cristian Săpunaru</a>'s injury, he beat competition from <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Fucile" title="Jorge Fucile">Jorge Fucile</a> and began being often utilized as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">right back</a>.</p>
<p>On 2 March 2012, after a <a href="/wiki/Indirect_free_kick" title="Indirect free kick">free kick</a>, Maicon headed a late offside goal in a 3–2 away win against <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He scored five goals from 35 appearances in two seasons combined, helping his team to back-to-back national championships.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="S.C3.A3o_Paulo">São Paulo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maicon_Pereira_Roque&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: São Paulo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 September 2015, Maicon headed home the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2015–16 UEFA Champions League">2–1 winner</a> over <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>, in what was his third competitive goal of <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_FC_Porto_season" title="2015–16 FC Porto season">the campaign</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On 14 February of the following year, however, having been deemed surplus to requirements by new manager <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Peseiro" title="José Peseiro">José Peseiro</a>, he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_FC" title="São Paulo FC">São Paulo FC</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> stating upon arriving that the move was "a dream come true".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 28 June 2016, Maicon signed a permanent deal for <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_real" title="Brazilian real">R$</a>22 million, as Porto also ceded 50% of the sporting rights to fellow defenders Inácio and <a href="/wiki/Luc%C3%A3o_(footballer)" title="Lucão (footballer)">Lucão</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
