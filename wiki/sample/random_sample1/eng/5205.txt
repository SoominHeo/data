<p><b>Michael Ryan Pritchard</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nobody_Likes_You_Page_12_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nobody_Likes_You_Page_12-1">[1]</a></sup> (born May 4, 1972),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nobody_Likes_You_Page_12_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nobody_Likes_You_Page_12-1">[1]</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Mike Dirnt</b>, is an American musician, songwriter and composer. He is best known as the co-founder, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bass_guitarist" title="Bass guitarist">bassist</a>, backing vocalist and occasional lead vocalist of American punk rock band <a href="/wiki/Green_Day" title="Green Day">Green Day</a>. He has also played in several other bands, including <a href="/wiki/The_Frustrators" title="The Frustrators">The Frustrators</a>. His stage name was originally a nickname from his friends at grade school, as he would constantly play "air bass" and make a "dirnt, dirnt, dirnt" noise while pretending to pluck the strings.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Dirnt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a child, his father was often away obtaining a degree, while his mother stayed at home to care for him and his sister Myla.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz13_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz13-2">[2]</a></sup> Dirnt excelled in school despite missing classes, often as a result of various illnesses believed to be caused by his biological mother's drug use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz13_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz13-2">[2]</a></sup> After an argument between his parents resulted in a call to the police, the couple divorced. His mother and sister moved to <a href="/wiki/Rodeo,_California" title="Rodeo, California">Rodeo</a>, while he stayed in El Sobrante with his father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz13_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz13-2">[2]</a></sup> However, missing his mother, he eventually moved in with her and Myla. Having been previously described as bright and fearless, Dirnt became sullen and withdrawn after the divorce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz13_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz13-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the cafeteria in Carquinez Middle School, ten-year-old Dirnt met <a href="/wiki/Billie_Joe_Armstrong" title="Billie Joe Armstrong">Billie Joe Armstrong</a>, with whom he bonded instantly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz13_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz13-2">[2]</a></sup> Armstrong began to play guitar with Mike, and the two spent much time in Armstrong's house and they played songs by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ramones" title="The Ramones">The Ramones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Van_Halen" title="Van Halen">Van Halen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz14_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz14-3">[3]</a></sup> At the age of 15, they formed a group named "Sweet Children" where Billie playing lead guitar and Mike playing rhythm guitar.</p>
<p>After attending <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salesian_High_School_(Richmond)" title="Salesian High School (Richmond)">Salesian High School</a>, an all-boys Catholic school for his first year, Dirnt transferred to <a href="/wiki/Pinole_Valley_High_School" title="Pinole Valley High School">Pinole Valley High School</a>, where Armstrong had also recently transferred from <a href="/wiki/John_Swett_High_School" title="John Swett High School">John Swett High School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz16_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz16-4">[4]</a></sup> Dirnt's family struggled with financial troubles; in an effort to help out, he worked as a chef at Nantucket, a seafood restaurant in <a href="/wiki/Crockett,_California" title="Crockett, California">Crockett</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz20_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz20-5">[5]</a></sup> He eventually saved up enough money to purchase a used pickup truck that he and Armstrong often drove to Berkeley to attend shows at <a href="/wiki/924_Gilman_Street" title="924 Gilman Street">924 Gilman Street</a>, an influential <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DIY" title="DIY">DIY</a> <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a> club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz20_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz20-5">[5]</a></sup> The pair got jobs at the club as security guards, despite their small physiques. Dirnt recalled, "We lived and died for that place. At that time, it meant everything."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz35_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz35-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Dirnt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While at Pinole Valley High School, Dirnt, Armstrong, and classmate Billie Joe Armstrong formed a band called Sweet Children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz16_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz16-4">[4]</a></sup> Dirnt's mother struggled to provide for him and his sister as a single parent, and eventually had to leave Rodeo in 1987 to look for work. Unwilling to leave Sweet Children, Armstrong and Dirnt convinced each other's parents to allow Dirnt to move into Armstrong's garage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz42_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz42-7">[7]</a></sup> After drummer <a href="/wiki/John_Kiffmeyer" title="John Kiffmeyer">John Kiffmeyer</a>, who was also known as Al Sobrante, joined the group, Sweet Children began performing at 924 Gilman Street. The band performed several well-received sets, which encouraged Armstrong to drop out of high school to focus his energy on music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz46_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz46-8">[8]</a></sup> Dirnt, however, worried that he needed a backup plan and continued his studies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz47_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz47-9">[9]</a></sup> Hughes, who was not as dedicated to the group as the other members, left Sweet Children, leaving Dirnt to play bass. Dirnt often brought his bass to school, and the plucking noise of the unamplified strings led classmates to jokingly call him by the <a href="/wiki/Onomatopoeia" title="Onomatopoeia">onomatopoeia</a> "Dirnt".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz4950_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz4950-10">[10]</a></sup> After changing its name to <a href="/wiki/Green_Day" title="Green Day">Green Day</a>, the band recorded its debut album <i><a href="/wiki/39/Smooth" title="39/Smooth">39/Smooth</a></i> over the 1989 Christmas holiday break and went on its first van tour in June 1990, leaving the day that Dirnt graduated from high school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spitz67_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spitz67-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>While performing at the 1998 <a href="/wiki/KROQ_Weenie_Roast" title="KROQ Weenie Roast">KROQ Weenie Roast</a> in Irvine, California, <a href="/wiki/Third_Eye_Blind" title="Third Eye Blind">Third Eye Blind</a> bassist Arion Salazar ran onstage and "<a href="/wiki/Bear_hug" title="Bear hug">bear-hugged</a>" Dirnt, who was caught off-guard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meyer153154_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Meyer153154-12">[12]</a></sup> The incident escalated into an on-stage scuffle before security took Salazar away. After the performance, Dirnt confronted Salazar backstage, and as the two argued, a beer bottle struck Dirnt in the head, causing a small fracture in his skull.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meyer153154_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Meyer153154-12">[12]</a></sup> Eyewitnesses later attributed the bottle throwing to a fan of Third Eye Blind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meyer153154_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Meyer153154-12">[12]</a></sup> Salazar and the band's management soon released a statement: "I am sorry that my attempt at doing something I thought would be funny escalated into Mike getting hurt. That was never my intention. I simply had too much to drink and made a very bad decision. If I had been in Mike's place, I am sure I would have acted similarly. My heart goes out to him and I hope he recovers quickly."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meyer153154_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-Meyer153154-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

</div>
<p>In the live album, <i><a href="/wiki/Bullet_in_a_Bible" title="Bullet in a Bible">Bullet in a Bible</a>,</i> Armstrong calls Dirnt "The best bass player in the history of punk rock."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Songwriting_and_lead_vocals">Songwriting and lead vocals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Dirnt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Songwriting and lead vocals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although Armstrong is Green Day's main lyricist, Dirnt has written lyrics for "Emenius Sleepus," "<a href="/wiki/J.A.R." title="J.A.R.">J.A.R. (Jason Andrew Relva)</a>", "Scumbag", "Ha Ha You're Dead," the subtrack "Nobody Likes You" from the medley "Homecoming" and the b-side "Governator," He also co-wrote the lyrics to "Best Thing in Town," "The One I Want," and "Panic Song" with Armstrong, and writes all the bass lines for Green Day songs. Mike also composed the intro to "Desensitized" from <i>Shenanigans</i> using a baseball bat and an old cathode ray tube television.</p>
<p>Dirnt sings lead vocals on "Governator," part of the third verse of "Outsider" on the <i><a href="/wiki/Shenanigans_(album)" title="Shenanigans (album)">Shenanigans</a></i> album, the "Nobody Likes You" subtrack of "Homecoming." He also sings the subtrack "Modern World" from the medley "American Eulogy" from <i><a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Breakdown" title="21st Century Breakdown">21st Century Breakdown</a></i> and sings lead for exactly 10 seconds in "Sex, Drugs, and Violence" from <i><a href="/wiki/%C2%A1Tr%C3%A9!" title="¡Tré!">¡Tré!</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Dirnt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dirnt married his first wife, Anastasia Serman in 1996 and the couple divorced in 1999. Anastasia and Dirnt had a daughter together, Estelle Desiree, who was born on December 20, 1996. He won full custody of her in the summer of 2008 and took her to live in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland</a>. In 2004, he married his girlfriend Sarah Garrity in a private villa in Puerto Vallarta, Mexico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The two divorced that same year. On March 14, 2009, he married Brittney Cade in a private ceremony in Brittney's hometown of <a href="/wiki/Ojai,_California" title="Ojai, California">Ojai, California</a>. Dirnt has two children with Cade: a son, Brixton Michael (born October 11, 2008), and a daughter, Ryan Ruby Mae (born November 29, 2010).</p>
<p>Dirnt is a <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i> fan; he stated in an interview that he bases "most of his religious beliefs" on <i>Star Wars</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He is a part owner of <a href="/wiki/Rudy%27s_Can%27t_Fail_Cafe" title="Rudy's Can't Fail Cafe">Rudy's Can't Fail Cafe</a>, a diner in <a href="/wiki/Emeryville,_California" title="Emeryville, California">Emeryville</a> and Oakland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gda_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-gda-15">[15]</a></sup> The diner was named after a song by punk rock band <a href="/wiki/The_Clash" title="The Clash">The Clash</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Rudie_Can%27t_Fail" title="Rudie Can't Fail">Rudie Can't Fail</a>", from their album <i><a href="/wiki/London_Calling" title="London Calling">London Calling</a></i>. The menu item names are loosely based on punk themes such as "God Save the Chicken", a reference to "<a href="/wiki/God_Save_the_Queen_(Sex_Pistols_song)" title="God Save the Queen (Sex Pistols song)">God Save the Queen</a>" by the <a href="/wiki/Sex_Pistols" title="Sex Pistols">Sex Pistols</a>, and "Give 'Em Enough Meatloaf", a reference to <i><a href="/wiki/Give_%27Em_Enough_Rope" title="Give 'Em Enough Rope">Give 'Em Enough Rope</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/The_Clash" title="The Clash">The Clash</a>.</p>
<p>Dirnt was adopted; his biological mother died on January 9, 2013. Dirnt only managed to meet her one month before her death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2014, it was announced that Dirnt would co-produce the Indie film <i>Crickets</i>, with Green Day manager, Pat Magnarella.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2015, Dirnt along with Billie Joe Armstrong, announced that they would be launching a coffee company, <i><a href="/wiki/Oakland_Coffee" title="Oakland Coffee">Oakland Coffee</a> Works</i>. The company will sell organic coffee beans and is said to be the first company to use exclusively 100% compostable bags and pods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dirnt supported <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Sanders" title="Bernie Sanders">Bernie Sanders</a> during the 2016 Presidential Campaign.</p>
