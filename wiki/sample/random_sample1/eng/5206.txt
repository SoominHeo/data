<p><b>Michael John</b> "<b>Mike</b>" <b>Myers</b> (born May 25, 1963) is a <a href="/wiki/Canadian_nationality_law" title="Canadian nationality law">Canadian</a>-born actor, comedian, screenwriter, and film producer, who also holds <a href="/wiki/British_nationality_law" title="British nationality law">UK</a> and <a href="/wiki/Citizenship_of_the_United_States" title="Citizenship of the United States">US</a> citizenship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harrison2005_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harrison2005-1">[1]</a></sup> He is known for his run as a featured performer on <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i> from 1989 to 1995, and for playing the title roles in the <i><a href="/wiki/Wayne%27s_World_(film)" title="Wayne's World (film)">Wayne's World</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers_(film_series)" title="Austin Powers (film series)">Austin Powers</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shrek_(series)" title="Shrek (series)">Shrek</a></i> films. He also directed the documentary film <i><a href="/wiki/Supermensch:_The_Legend_of_Shep_Gordon" title="Supermensch: The Legend of Shep Gordon">Supermensch: The Legend of Shep Gordon</a></i>, and had a small role in <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Inglourious_Basterds" title="Inglourious Basterds">Inglourious Basterds</a></i> in 2009.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Myers was born on May 25, 1963<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-2">[2]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_Toronto" title="Scarborough, Toronto">Scarborough, Ontario</a>, the son of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English-born</a> parents Eric Myers (1922–1991), an insurance agent, and his wife, Alice E. (née Hind; born 1926), an office supervisor and a veteran of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tiscali1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tiscali1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Both his parents were from <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> and he has two older brothers, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Myers_(musician)" title="Paul Myers (musician)">Paul</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a>, broadcaster and author, and Peter, a Sears Canada employee.</p>
<p>The family is of <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> ancestry. Myers was raised <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Protestant" title="Protestant">Protestant</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Myers reportedly holds Canadian, U.S. and British citizenship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harrison2005_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Harrison2005-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Myers grew up in <a href="/wiki/North_York" title="North York">North York</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_Toronto" title="Scarborough, Toronto">Scarborough</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> where he attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_John_A._MacDonald_Collegiate_Institute" title="Sir John A. MacDonald Collegiate Institute">Sir John A. MacDonald Collegiate Institute</a> and then graduated from <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Leacock_Collegiate_Institute" title="Stephen Leacock Collegiate Institute">Stephen Leacock Collegiate Institute</a> in 1982.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span></h3>
<p>Myers began performing in commercials at the age of two. At the age of ten, he made a commercial for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Columbia_Hydro" title="British Columbia Hydro">British Columbia Hydro</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Gilda_Radner" title="Gilda Radner">Gilda Radner</a> playing his mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tiscali1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-tiscali1-3">[3]</a></sup> At the age of 12, he made a guest appearance as Ari on the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/King_of_Kensington" title="King of Kensington">King of Kensington</a></i>.</p>
<p>After graduating from high school, Myers was accepted into the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Second_City" title="The Second City">Second City</a></i> Canadian touring company. He later moved to the United Kingdom, where in 1985 he was one of the founding members of <a href="/wiki/The_Comedy_Store_Players" title="The Comedy Store Players">The Comedy Store Players</a>, an improvisational group based at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Comedy_Store,_London" title="The Comedy Store, London">The Comedy Store</a> in London.</p>
<p>The next year, he starred in the British children's TV program <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wide_Awake_Club_(television)" title="Wide Awake Club (television)"><i>Wide Awake Club</i></a>, parodying the show's normal exuberance with his own "Sound Asleep Club", in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Neil_Mullarkey" title="Neil Mullarkey">Neil Mullarkey</a>.</p>
<p>He returned to <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Second_City" title="The Second City">Second City</a> in 1986 as a cast member in the Second City's Toronto main stage show, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_City_Theatre" title="Second City Theatre">Second City Theatre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ott_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ott-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1988, he moved from Second City in Toronto to <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>. In Chicago, he trained, performed, and taught at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Improv_Olympic" title="Improv Olympic">Improv Olympic</a>.</p>
<p>Myers made many appearances, including as Wayne Campbell, on Toronto's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Citytv" title="Citytv">Citytv</a> in the early 1980s, on the alternative video show <i>City Limits</i> hosted by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Ward_(songwriter)" title="Christopher Ward (songwriter)">Christopher Ward</a>. Myers also appeared as Wayne Campbell in the music video for Ward's Canadian hit "Boys and Girls".</p>
<p>The Wayne Campbell character was featured extensively in the 1986 summer series <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Only_Rock_%26_Roll_(TV_series)" title="It's Only Rock &amp; Roll (TV series)">It's Only Rock &amp; Roll</a></i>, produced by Toronto's Insight Production Company for <a href="/wiki/CBC_Television" title="CBC Television">CBC Television</a>. Wayne appeared both in studio and in a series of location sketches directed and edited by <a href="/wiki/Allan_Novak" title="Allan Novak">Allan Novak</a>. Myers wrote another sketch, "Kurt and Dieter", co-starring with Second City's <a href="/wiki/Dana_Andersen" title="Dana Andersen">Dana Andersen</a> and also directed by Novak, which would later turn into the popular "<a href="/wiki/Sprockets_(Saturday_Night_Live)" title="Sprockets (Saturday Night Live)">Sprockets</a>" sketch on <i>Saturday Night Live</i>.</p>
<p>In 2011 Myers returned to The Comedy Store in London to perform a one-time reprisal of his role with The Comedy Store Players. The UK comedy website Chortle praised his performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mike_Myers_HWoF_Star.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2304" data-file-width="3072" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Mike_Myers_HWoF_Star.jpg/220px-Mike_Myers_HWoF_Star.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Mike_Myers_HWoF_Star.jpg/330px-Mike_Myers_HWoF_Star.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Mike_Myers_HWoF_Star.jpg/440px-Mike_Myers_HWoF_Star.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>In 1992, Myers and <a href="/wiki/Dana_Carvey" title="Dana Carvey">Dana Carvey</a> adapted <i><a href="/wiki/Wayne%27s_World_(film)" title="Wayne's World (film)">Wayne's World</a></i> into a full-length motion picture based on the <i>SNL</i> sketch. It was among the most successful movies of the year and the following year a sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Wayne%27s_World_2" title="Wayne's World 2">Wayne's World 2</a></i>, was released. That year Myers also starred in <i><a href="/wiki/So_I_Married_an_Axe_Murderer" title="So I Married an Axe Murderer">So I Married an Axe Murderer</a></i>, which garnered a cult following.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He then took a hiatus from television.</p>
<p>Myers returned to acting in 1997 with the film <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers:_International_Man_of_Mystery" title="Austin Powers: International Man of Mystery">Austin Powers: International Man of Mystery</a></i>, then a sequel in 1999, <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers:_The_Spy_Who_Shagged_Me" title="Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me">Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me</a></i>, and finally <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers_in_Goldmember" title="Austin Powers in Goldmember">Austin Powers in Goldmember</a></i> in 2002. Myers played both the title role (<a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers_(character)" title="Austin Powers (character)">Austin Powers</a>) and the villain (<a href="/wiki/Dr._Evil" title="Dr. Evil">Dr. Evil</a>), as well as other characters, in all three Austin Powers films.</p>
<p>In 1998, Myers played one of his rare non-comedic roles in the film <i><a href="/wiki/54_(film)" title="54 (film)">54</a></i>: <a href="/wiki/Steve_Rubell" title="Steve Rubell">Steve Rubell</a>, proprietor of New York City's famous <a href="/wiki/Studio_54" title="Studio 54">Studio 54</a>, a 1970s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Discotheque" title="Discotheque">discotheque</a>. The film was moderately successful, and Myers's performance was widely praised.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Myers later parodied the club as "Studio 69" in <i>Goldmember</i>.</p>
<p>In June 2000, Myers was sued by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Pictures" title="Universal Pictures">Universal Pictures</a> for <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$</span>3.8 million for backing out of a contract to play Dieter, the SNL character, in a feature film. Myers said he refused to honor the <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$</span>20 million contract because he did not want to cheat moviegoers with an unacceptable script—one that he himself had written.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Myers countersued, and a settlement was reached after several months where Myers agreed to make another film with Universal. That film was <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cat_in_the_Hat_(film)" title="The Cat in the Hat (film)">The Cat in the Hat</a></i>, which was released in November 2003 and starred Myers as the title character.</p>
<p>In 2001, Myers provided the voice of <a href="/wiki/Shrek_(character)" title="Shrek (character)">Shrek</a> in the <a href="/wiki/DreamWorks_Animation" title="DreamWorks Animation">DreamWorks</a> animated film <a href="/wiki/Shrek" title="Shrek">of the same name</a>, having taken over the role after original planned voice actor <a href="/wiki/Chris_Farley" title="Chris Farley">Chris Farley</a> died in December 1997. He reprised this role in <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek_4-D" title="Shrek 4-D">Shrek 4-D</a></i> (a theme park ride) in 2003, <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek_2" title="Shrek 2">Shrek 2</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek_the_Third" title="Shrek the Third">Shrek the Third</a></i>, and the Christmas special <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shrek_The_Halls" title="Shrek The Halls">Shrek The Halls</a></i>, both in 2007.</p>
<p>In 2008, Myers co-wrote, co-produced and starred in <a href="/wiki/The_Love_Guru" title="The Love Guru">The Love Guru</a>. In 2009 he played the part of British general Ed Fenech, in <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Inglourious_Basterds" title="Inglourious Basterds">Inglourious Basterds</a></i>. In 2010 Myers returned for the latest film in the <i>Shrek</i> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Shrek_Forever_After" title="Shrek Forever After">Shrek Forever After</a></i>.</p>
<p>Myers made a cameo appearance in <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>' music video <i><a href="/wiki/Boys_(Britney_Spears_song)" title="Boys (Britney Spears song)">Boys</a></i> as his film character <a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers_(character)" title="Austin Powers (character)">Austin Powers</a>.</p>
<p>Myers is a member of the band <a href="/wiki/Ming_Tea" title="Ming Tea">Ming Tea</a> along with <a href="/wiki/The_Bangles" title="The Bangles">The Bangles</a>' guitarist and vocalist <a href="/wiki/Susanna_Hoffs" title="Susanna Hoffs">Susanna Hoffs</a> and musician <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Sweet" title="Matthew Sweet">Matthew Sweet</a>. They performed the songs "BBC" and "Daddy Wasn't There" from the <i>Austin Powers</i> movies.</p>
<p>In a 2005 poll to find The Comedian's Comedian, he was voted among the top 50 comedy acts ever by fellow comedians and comedy insiders.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> During a CBS interview in 2007, he noted that he normally takes three years between films. He spends one year "living his life" and then writes various screenplays, develops characters, practices them in front of live audiences, and then selects one of the screenplays to film.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Myers received the MTV Generation Award in June 2007, making him the second Canadian to win the award (following <a href="/wiki/Jim_Carrey" title="Jim Carrey">Jim Carrey</a> in 2006), for bringing his unique style of comedy to small and big screens alike. His directorial debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Supermensch:_The_Legend_of_Shep_Gordon" title="Supermensch: The Legend of Shep Gordon">Supermensch: The Legend of Shep Gordon</a></i>, was selected to be screened in the Gala Presentation section at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2013 Toronto International Film Festival">2013 Toronto International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TIFF_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-TIFF-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Myers began dating actress and comedy writer Robin Ruzan in the late 1980s after meeting at a hockey game in <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, during which Myers caught a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puck_(sports)" title="Puck (sports)">puck</a> and used the incident as an icebreaker to strike up a conversation with Ruzan. The couple married on 22 May 1993, and Myers later referred to Ruzan as "his muse".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The couple filed for divorce in December 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, café owner Kelly Tisdale confirmed reports that she and Myers were dating. Myers and Tisdale wed in New York in a secret ceremony in the fall of 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> They have three children: son Spike Alan (b. 2011) and daughters, Sunday Molly (b. 2014) and Paulina Kathleen (b. 2015).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Myers is a <i><a href="/wiki/Dungeons_%26_Dragons" title="Dungeons &amp; Dragons">Dungeons &amp; Dragons</a></i> player<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and was one of several celebrities to have participated in the <a href="/wiki/Worldwide_Dungeons_%26_Dragons_Game_Day" title="Worldwide Dungeons &amp; Dragons Game Day">Worldwide Dungeons &amp; Dragons Game Day</a> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>He supports the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Maple_Leafs" title="Toronto Maple Leafs">Toronto Maple Leafs</a> of the National Hockey League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He named two characters in the first <i>Austin Powers</i> movie Commander Gilmour and General Borschevsky, after then-Maple Leafs players <a href="/wiki/Doug_Gilmour" title="Doug Gilmour">Doug Gilmour</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Borschevsky" title="Nikolai Borschevsky">Nikolai Borschevsky</a>.</p>
<p>Myers has played for <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_United_F.C." title="Hollywood United F.C.">Hollywood United F.C.</a>, a celebrity soccer team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He played in the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Soccer_Aid" title="Soccer Aid">Soccer Aid</a> for <a href="/wiki/UNICEF_UK" title="UNICEF UK">UNICEF UK</a> football match, England vs. R.O.W (Rest of the World) and scored his penalty during a sudden death shootout after the game ended 2–2 (June 6, 2010). The Rest of the World team beat England for the first time since the tournament started. Myers is a fan of <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool F.C.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016 Myers published a book, <i>Canada</i>, about the country's history and pop culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ott_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-ott-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Four <a href="/wiki/American_Comedy_Awards" title="American Comedy Awards">American Comedy Awards</a> (Best Film Performance (Male) and Best Writing in 2003 and 2000, for <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers_in_Goldmember" title="Austin Powers in Goldmember">Austin Powers in Goldmember</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers:_The_Spy_Who_Shagged_Me" title="Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me">Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me</a></i>, respectively)</li>
<li>The 2000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Comedy_Award" title="American Comedy Award">American Comedy Award</a> for Best Lead Actor for <i><a href="/wiki/Austin_Powers:_The_Spy_Who_Shagged_Me" title="Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me">Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me</a></i>.</li>
<li>An <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> in 1989 for <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Writing_for_a_Variety_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Variety Series">Outstanding Writing in a Variety or Music Program</a>, for <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>. He has also been nominated for two other <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a>.</li>
<li>He has won seven <a href="/wiki/MTV_Movie_Awards" title="MTV Movie Awards">MTV Movie Awards</a>.</li>
<li>In 2003 he was inducted into <a href="/wiki/Canada%27s_Walk_of_Fame" title="Canada's Walk of Fame">Canada's Walk of Fame</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
<li>Nominated for the Worst Actor and Worst Screen Couple <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Razzie_Awards" title="Razzie Awards">Razzie Awards</a> in 2004, for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cat_in_the_Hat_(film)" title="The Cat in the Hat (film)">The Cat in the Hat</a></i>.</li>
<li>For contributions to the motion picture industry, Mike Myers was honoured with a star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a> at 7042 <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Boulevard" title="Hollywood Boulevard">Hollywood Boulevard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HWOFDB_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-HWOFDB-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li>He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Lucille_Ball" title="Lucille Ball">Lucille Ball</a> Legacy of Laughter Award at the 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Land_Awards" title="TV Land Awards">TV Land Awards</a>. His award was presented to him by <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a>, his co-star in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Love_Guru" title="The Love Guru">The Love Guru</a></i>.</li>
<li>Two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Golden_Raspberry_Awards" title="2008 Golden Raspberry Awards">Razzie Awards in 2008</a>, for Worst Actor and along with co-writer Graham Gordy Worst Screenplay for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Love_Guru" title="The Love Guru">The Love Guru</a></i>. The film also won Worst Film and secured 4 other nominations.</li>
<li>In 2015, his face was put on a stamp by <a href="/wiki/Canada_Post" title="Canada Post">Canada Post</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
