<p><b>Michael Christopher "Mike" Moustakas</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/uː/ long 'oo' in 'food'">uː</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born September 11, 1988) is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Third_baseman" title="Third baseman">third baseman</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB). He was drafted second overall in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_Major_League_Baseball_Draft" title="2007 Major League Baseball Draft">2007 Major League Baseball Draft</a> by the Royals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> During games he is often greeted by calls of <i>"Moose"</i>, a nickname given by fans and the media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Duber_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Duber-2">[2]</a></sup> The team encourages the nickname by selling foam <a href="/wiki/Moose" title="Moose">moose</a> antlers in the <a href="/wiki/Kauffman_Stadium" title="Kauffman Stadium">Kauffman Stadium</a> gift shop, as well as a giveaway night in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Duber_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Duber-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="High_school_career">High school career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: High school career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moustakas attended <a href="/wiki/Chatsworth_High_School" title="Chatsworth High School">Chatsworth High School</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chatsworth,_Los_Angeles,_California" title="Chatsworth, Los Angeles, California">Chatsworth, California</a>, where he was a four-year starter in baseball. During that time he helped Chatsworth to a 124–11 record, including four West Valley League championships, and two CIF city titles. Moustakas was voted "Player of the Year" by Cal-Hi Sports in both his sophomore and junior years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chatsworth_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chatsworth-3">[3]</a></sup> His 52 career home runs in high school are a California prep state record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Moustakas attempted to be a two-sport player for Chatsworth, but a broken ankle suffered in his first game as quarterback for the Chancellors ended his high school football career, and nearly ended his baseball career as well. However, after three months of rehabilitation Moustakas was able to resume playing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Times-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2006 Moustakas was a member of <a href="/wiki/USA_Baseball" title="USA Baseball">USA Baseball</a>'s Junior National Team, competing in the World Junior Championships in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>. He topped off his prep career by being named <i><a href="/wiki/Baseball_America" title="Baseball America">Baseball America</a></i> 's <a href="/wiki/Baseball_America_High_School_Player_of_the_Year_Award" title="Baseball America High School Player of the Year Award">High School Player of the Year</a> in 2007, an honor previously bestowed on MLB stars like <a href="/wiki/Joe_Mauer" title="Joe Mauer">Joe Mauer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Homer_Bailey" title="Homer Bailey">Homer Bailey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Hamilton" title="Josh Hamilton">Josh Hamilton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Moustakas was also selected to the <a href="/wiki/USA_Today_All-USA_high_school_baseball_team" title="USA Today All-USA high school baseball team">USA Today All-USA high school baseball team</a> in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-USA-7">[7]</a></sup> He was offered a scholarship to <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">USC</a>, but turned it down and decided to enter the 2007 MLB Draft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Times-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor_Leagues">Minor Leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Minor Leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>Moustakas was drafted by the Kansas City Royals with the second overall pick of the 2007 amateur draft. He signed with the team on August 15, <a href="/wiki/2007_in_baseball" title="2007 in baseball">2007</a>, receiving a reported signing bonus of $4 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Fellow Chatsworth teammate <a href="/wiki/Matt_Dominguez_(baseball)" title="Matt Dominguez (baseball)">Matt Dominguez</a> was chosen by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_Marlins" title="Florida Marlins">Florida Marlins</a> with the 12th overall pick of the amateur draft. The pair became just the sixth set of high school teammates to be selected in the first round of the same draft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLBbio_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-MLBbio-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Entering 2009, Moustakas was widely regarded as one of the top baseball prospects in all of minor league baseball. The <a href="/wiki/Sporting_News" title="Sporting News">Sporting News</a> ranked Moustakas as the 13th-best prospect overall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> while MLB.com listed Moustakas as their 11th-best prospect in all of baseball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Moustakas, however, had trouble adjusting to the Carolina League in 2009. While his power remained, his ability to hit for a high average went down significantly. In 2010, Moustakas' power and average increased significantly. As of July 13, 2010, Moustakas was hitting .352 with 21 homers and 76 runs batted in in AA Northwest Arkansas. On July 15, 2010, he was promoted to Triple-A Omaha.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On August 30, 2010, Moustakas was named the 2010 Texas League Player of the Year and on the same night, he set an Omaha Royals record with three home runs and 11 RBIs in one game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Moustakas won the <a href="/wiki/Joe_Bauman_Home_Run_Award" title="Joe Bauman Home Run Award">Joe Bauman Home Run Award</a> for most home runs in minor league baseball, winning a tiebreaker with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Trumbo" title="Mark Trumbo">Mark Trumbo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On May 22, 2014, he was demoted to Triple-A Omaha after batting just .152 through 139 plate appearances.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kansas_City_Royals">Kansas City Royals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Kansas City Royals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2011">2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Moustakas had his contract purchased by Kansas City on June 9, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He made his debut the following night against the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels_of_Anaheim" title="Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim">Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim</a>, going 1–3 with a walk and a run scored. His first major league hit came in his third plate appearance against <a href="/wiki/Ervin_Santana" title="Ervin Santana">Ervin Santana</a>, where he would eventually score in the 4–2 win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Moustakas went on to play in 89 games for Kansas City in 2011, finishing the season with five home runs, thirty RBIs, and a season batting average of .263.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Duber_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Duber-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On February 18, 2012 the Royals announced they had signed Moustakas to a one-year contract for the 2012 season. No financial terms of the deal were released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> During the 2012 season Moustakas set a new club record for K.C., playing 47 straight games without an error. He also made 41 double plays far besting the previous mark of 34 <a href="/wiki/Mark_Teahen" title="Mark Teahen">Mark Teahen</a> set in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Duber_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Duber-2">[2]</a></sup> Moustakas finished the year batting .242 with a .296 OBP with 20 home runs and 73 RBIs, also hitting 34 doubles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Duber_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Duber-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2013, Moustakas' locker was assigned next to <a href="/wiki/George_Kottaras" title="George Kottaras">George Kottaras</a>, who is also of Greek descent. "I don't know how many times you've had two Greek guys in the same clubhouse on the same team," Kottaras said. "And they put us next to each other, which makes it even better."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greek1_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Greek1-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, he batted .233 with a .287 on base percentage, 12 home runs, and 42 RBI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baseball-reference1_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-baseball-reference1-19">[19]</a></sup> He also led all American League third basemen with 16 errors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baseball-reference1_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-baseball-reference1-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Moustakas struggled early in the 2014 season. On May 22, 2014, he was demoted to Triple-A Omaha after batting just .152 through 139 plate appearances, though he led the team in homers with 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On June 1, Moustakas was recalled from AAA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He began to hit more consistently after this. He finished the season batting .212, while hitting 15 homers and driving in 54 runs. Moustakas tore through the 2014 Playoffs, hitting 5 homeruns, including a go-ahead solo shot in the 11th inning of game 1 against the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels_of_Anaheim" title="Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim">Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim</a>. He led all 2014 Postseason players in homers. The Royals would then lose to the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2014_World_Series" title="2014 World Series">2014 World Series</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2015, Moustakas carried over his success from the postseason and had a batting average at or above .300 right up until the All-Star break. He was selected to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2015_MLB_All-Star_Game" title="2015 MLB All-Star Game">2015 All-Star Game</a> via the All-Star Game Final Vote. Moustakas was placed on the family emergency list twice during the first half of the 2015 season. On September 12, Moustakas set a new Royals franchise record against the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a>, with 9 RBI, eclipsing the previous mark of seven set by <a href="/wiki/Billy_Butler_(baseball)" title="Billy Butler (baseball)">Billy Butler</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Brett" title="George Brett">George Brett</a>. He hit a grand slam, three-run home run and a two-run single in the 14-6 win. In 147 games, Moustakas batted .284 with 22 home runs and 82 RBI. The Royals clinched an AL Central Pennant and went to the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Series" title="2015 World Series">World Series</a> again, this time to beat the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a>. It was the Royals first championship title in 30 years.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016">2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On May 7, 2016, Moustakas was placed on the 15-day disabled list due to a fractured left thumb. On May 22, Moustakas suffered a right knee injury while colliding with teammate Alex Gordon in an attempt to fetch a pop up in foul territory. The next day, an MRI revealed that there was a torn ACL in the knee, ending Moustakas' 2016 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Moustakas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The son of Connie and Mike Moustakas, a former college football player for <a href="/wiki/UCLA_Bruins_football" title="UCLA Bruins football">UCLA</a>, Mike was born in Los Angeles on September 11, 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chatsworth_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Chatsworth-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-USA-7">[7]</a></sup> He is of Greek descent, as his grandmother was born in Greece, and flies a Greek flag above his locker in the Royals' clubhouse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greek1_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Greek1-18">[18]</a></sup> He is the nephew of author, MLB player and former <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> hitting coach <a href="/wiki/Tom_Robson" title="Tom Robson">Tom Robson</a>, who gave Moustakas hitting tips growing up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dick_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dick-23">[23]</a></sup> He grew up in the Los Angeles area. Moustakas' mother died after a long illness in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>When not playing baseball Moustakas enjoys golf and fishing. He claims his golf game is much like hunting, however, as he spends most of his time in the woods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ortiz_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ortiz-25">[25]</a></sup> Asked by <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> what career he would enjoy other than baseball, Moustakas expressed an interest in being a firefighter. He owns an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_bulldog" title="English bulldog">English bulldog</a> named Molly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ortiz_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ortiz-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Moustakas has been married to his wife, Stephanie, since January 2014. They had their first child, Mila Constance Moustakas, on Aug. 30, 2016. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
