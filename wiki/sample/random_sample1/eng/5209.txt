<p><b>Michael Joseph Porcaro</b> (May 29, 1955 – March 15, 2015) was an American <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass</a> player known for his work with <a href="/wiki/Toto_(band)" title="Toto (band)">Toto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He retired from touring in 2007 as a result of being diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Amyotrophic_lateral_sclerosis" title="Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis">amyotrophic lateral sclerosis</a> (ALS), also known as <a href="/wiki/Lou_Gehrig" title="Lou Gehrig">Lou Gehrig's</a> disease or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motor_Neurone_Disease" title="Motor Neurone Disease">Motor Neurone Disease</a>.</p>
<p>He was the middle brother of Toto members <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Porcaro" title="Jeff Porcaro">Jeff Porcaro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steve_Porcaro" title="Steve Porcaro">Steve Porcaro</a>. Their father is <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> <a href="/wiki/Drummer" title="Drummer">drummer</a>-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Percussionist" title="Percussionist">percussionist</a> <a href="/wiki/Joe_Porcaro" title="Joe Porcaro">Joe Porcaro</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Porcaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Porcaro worked as a session bass player before replacing <a href="/wiki/Toto_(band)" title="Toto (band)">Toto</a> original bass player <a href="/wiki/David_Hungate" title="David Hungate">David Hungate</a> in 1982 shortly after the band completed recording the <i><a href="/wiki/Toto_IV" title="Toto IV">Toto IV</a></i> album. Porcaro played cello on a track for the album and subsequently appeared in the band's videos and performed as a full band member on the world tour in support of the album. He remained with the band until 2007.</p>
<p>Along with Toto, Porcaro played numerous sessions in Los Angeles, and toured with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Franks_(musician)" title="Michael Franks (musician)">Michael Franks</a> on his first tour. He then toured with <a href="/wiki/Seals_and_Crofts" title="Seals and Crofts">Seals and Crofts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Larry_Carlton" title="Larry Carlton">Larry Carlton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Boz_Scaggs" title="Boz Scaggs">Boz Scaggs</a>. He appears in the video for the song "JoJo" by Scaggs. <a href="/wiki/In_Flames" title="In Flames">In Flames</a> bass player <a href="/wiki/Peter_Iwers" title="Peter Iwers">Peter Iwers</a> said that his bass playing style was influenced by Porcaro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amyotrophic_lateral_sclerosis">Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Porcaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Porcaro stopped performing with Toto in 2007 after a growing numbness in his fingers that made it increasingly difficult for him to play. He was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Leland_Sklar" title="Leland Sklar">Leland Sklar</a> for the remainder of the tour and Toto disbanded in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On February 26, 2010, it was announced via official press release that Mike Porcaro was suffering from <a href="/wiki/Amyotrophic_lateral_sclerosis" title="Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis">amyotrophic lateral sclerosis</a> and that former band members of Toto, including <a href="/wiki/Steve_Porcaro" title="Steve Porcaro">Steve Porcaro</a>, would reform and do a short tour through Europe in support of him in the summer of 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The regrouped Toto continued to tour and perform for Mike's benefit during 2011. <a href="/wiki/Nathan_East" title="Nathan East">Nathan East</a> was the guest bass player for the 2010, 2011, 2012 and 2013 tours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2012, it was reported in <i><a href="/wiki/Classic_Rock_(magazine)" title="Classic Rock (magazine)">Classic Rock</a></i> magazine that he was doing as well as could be expected with his disease, but he was in a wheelchair as the disease was progressing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 15, 2015, Porcaro died in his sleep at his home in Los Angeles from the disease. He was 59.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He is interred at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forest_Lawn_Memorial_Park,_Hollywood_Hills" title="Forest Lawn Memorial Park, Hollywood Hills">Forest Lawn Memorial Park</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Hills" title="Hollywood Hills">Hollywood Hills</a> of Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
