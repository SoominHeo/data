<p><b>Microsoft Office Accounting (formerly Small Business Accounting)</b> is a discontinued <a href="/wiki/Accounting" title="Accounting">accounting</a> software application from <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> targeted towards <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Small_businesses" title="Small businesses">small businesses</a> that had between 1 and 25 employees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The last version, Microsoft Office Accounting 2009, was available in both <i>Express</i> and <i>Professional</i> editions. It included several new features, including tax preparation add-ins, 20 new reports and a <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish language</a> pack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 16 November 2009, Microsoft discontinued its distribution of the Microsoft Office Accounting product line in the US and UK. Licensees are to receive mainstream and extended product support from Microsoft or one of its partners through January 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>Microsoft Office Accounting Express was the <a href="/wiki/Freeware" title="Freeware">freeware</a> version of Accounting, made available for download and also being distributed in certain editions of <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Office_2007" title="Microsoft Office 2007">Microsoft Office 2007</a>, including <i>Professional</i>, <i>Small Business</i> and <i>Ultimate</i> editions. This free version did not support local requirements outside of UK and USA.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Microsoft_Office_Accounting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Microsoft Office Accounting has its roots in the a 2006 release called Microsoft Small Business Accounting. A new iteration was released in 2007 with the same name. In 2008, however, the name was changed to Microsoft Office Accounting and it retained that name in 2009, which was its last iteration.</p>
