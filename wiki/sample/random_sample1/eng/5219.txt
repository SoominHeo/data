<p><b>Mycroft Holmes</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">fictional character</a> appearing in stories written by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Arthur_Conan_Doyle" title="Sir Arthur Conan Doyle">Sir Arthur Conan Doyle</a>. He is the elder brother (by seven years) of detective <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a>. He is described as having abilities of deduction and knowledge exceeding even those of his brother, though their practical use is limited by his poor physique and dislike of fieldwork.</p>
<p>The character has been adapted in various pieces of literature and media, including television series, movies, radio, and comics. He is also popular in culture, being mentioned by many works, which mostly reference his job, personality, or his relationship with Sherlock Holmes.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Profile">Profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mycroft_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Possessing deductive powers exceeding even those of his younger brother, Mycroft is nevertheless incapable of performing detective work similar to that of Sherlock as he is unwilling to put in the physical effort necessary to bring cases to their conclusions. In "<a href="/wiki/The_Adventure_of_the_Greek_Interpreter" title="The Adventure of the Greek Interpreter">The Adventure of the Greek Interpreter</a>", Arthur Conan Doyle has Sherlock Holmes say:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>...he has no ambition and no energy. He will not even go out of his way to verify his own solutions, and would rather be considered wrong than take the trouble to prove himself right. Again and again I have taken a problem to him, and have received an explanation which has afterwards proved to be the correct one. And yet he was absolutely incapable of working out the practical points...</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Though Sherlock initially tells <a href="/wiki/Dr._Watson" title="Dr. Watson">Watson</a> that Mycroft audits books for some government departments, he later reveals that Mycroft's true role is more substantial. While Conan Doyle's stories leave unclear what Mycroft Holmes' exact position is in the British government, Sherlock Holmes says that "Occasionally he <i>is</i> the British government [...] the most indispensable man in the country." He apparently serves as a sort of human computer, as stated in "<a href="/wiki/The_Adventure_of_the_Bruce-Partington_Plans" title="The Adventure of the Bruce-Partington Plans">The Bruce-Partington Plans</a>":</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>He has the tidiest and most orderly brain, with the greatest capacity for storing facts, of any man living. The same great powers which I have turned to the detection of crime he has used for this particular business. The conclusions of every department are passed to him, and he is the central exchange, the clearinghouse, which makes out the balance. All other men are specialists, but his specialism is omniscience. We will suppose that a minister needs information as to a point which involves the Navy, India, Canada and the bimetallic question; he could get his separate advices from various departments upon each, but only Mycroft can focus them all, and say offhand how each factor would affect the other. They began by using him as a short-cut, a convenience; now he has made himself an essential. In that great brain of his everything is pigeon-holed and can be handed out in an instant.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Mycroft appears or is mentioned in four stories by Doyle: "<a href="/wiki/The_Adventure_of_the_Greek_Interpreter" title="The Adventure of the Greek Interpreter">The Greek Interpreter</a>", "<a href="/wiki/The_Final_Problem" title="The Final Problem">The Final Problem</a>", "<a href="/wiki/The_Adventure_of_the_Empty_House" title="The Adventure of the Empty House">The Empty House</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/The_Adventure_of_the_Bruce-Partington_Plans" title="The Adventure of the Bruce-Partington Plans">The Bruce-Partington Plans</a>". While he does occasionally exert himself in these stories on behalf of his brother, he on the whole remains a sedentary problem-solver, providing solutions based on seemingly no evidence and trusting Sherlock to handle any of the practical details. In fact, Mycroft's own lack of practicality is a severe handicap despite his inductive talents: in "The Greek Interpreter", his blundering approach to the case nearly costs the client his life.</p>
<p>Mycroft resembles Sherlock, but is described in "The Greek Interpreter" as being "a much larger and stouter man". In "The Bruce-Partington Plans", the following description is given:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Heavily built and massive, there was a suggestion of uncouth physical inertia in the figure, but above this unwieldy frame there was perched a head so masterful in its brow, so alert in its steel-grey, deep-set eyes, so firm in its lips, and so subtle in its play of expression, that after the first glance one forgot the gross body and remembered only the dominant mind.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Mycroft spends most of his time at the <a href="/wiki/Diogenes_Club" title="Diogenes Club">Diogenes Club</a>, which he co-founded.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media">In other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mycroft_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mycroft Holmes has been portrayed many times in film, television, and radio adaptations of the Holmes stories.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio">Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mycroft_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>In the 1950s radio series starring <a href="/wiki/John_Gielgud" title="John Gielgud">John Gielgud</a> as Sherlock Holmes, Gielgud's own brother, <a href="/wiki/Val_Gielgud" title="Val Gielgud">Val Gielgud</a>, played the part.</li>
<li>In the BBC radio dramatisations with <a href="/wiki/Carleton_Hobbs" title="Carleton Hobbs">Carleton Hobbs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norman_Shelley" title="Norman Shelley">Norman Shelley</a>, Mycroft was played at various times by Malcolm Graeme, Keith Williams, Felix Felton and – in "The Empty House" – by Carleton Hobbs himself.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio" title="BBC Radio">BBC Radio</a> broadcasts starring <a href="/wiki/Clive_Merrison" title="Clive Merrison">Clive Merrison</a> as Sherlock and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Williams_(actor)" title="Michael Williams (actor)">Michael Williams</a> as Watson, John Hartley played Mycroft in "The Greek Interpreter" on October 21, 1992, "The Bruce-Partington Plans" on January 24, 1994 and "The Retired Colourman" on March 29, 1995.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mycroft_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The first film appearance of Mycroft Holmes was in the 1922 film <i>The Bruce Partington Plans</i>, where he was played by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Gilbert_(actor)" title="Lewis Gilbert (actor)">Lewis Gilbert</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> broadcast <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_(1965_TV_series)" title="Sherlock Holmes (1965 TV series)">two <i>Sherlock Holmes</i> series in 1965 and 1968</a> which starred <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Wilmer" title="Douglas Wilmer">Douglas Wilmer</a> (1965) and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Cushing" title="Peter Cushing">Peter Cushing</a> (1968) as Sherlock and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Stock_(actor)" title="Nigel Stock (actor)">Nigel Stock</a> as Watson. Mycroft appeared twice, once in 1965 in <i>The Bruce-Partington Plans</i> and played by <a href="/wiki/Derek_Francis" title="Derek Francis">Derek Francis</a> and in 1968 in <i>The Greek Interpreter</i> and played by <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Adam_(actor)" title="Ronald Adam (actor)">Ronald Adam</a>.</li>
<li>In the 1965 film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Study_in_Terror" title="A Study in Terror">A Study in Terror</a></i>, Mycroft is played by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Morley" title="Robert Morley">Robert Morley</a>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Billy_Wilder" title="Billy Wilder">Billy Wilder</a>-directed film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Private_Life_of_Sherlock_Holmes" title="The Private Life of Sherlock Holmes">The Private Life of Sherlock Holmes</a></i> (1970), which starred <a href="/wiki/Robert_Stephens" title="Robert Stephens">Robert Stephens</a> as Sherlock, Mycroft was played by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Lee" title="Christopher Lee">Christopher Lee</a> (who also played Sherlock Holmes in other productions before and since). In this film, which purports to show the 'real' people behind Watson's dramatised accounts, Mycroft is nearly unrecognisable: whippet-thin and not notably indolent. He is also depicted as either the head or at least a senior operative of the British <a href="/wiki/Secret_service" title="Secret service">secret service</a>, for which the Diogenes Club is a front.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Charles_Gray_(actor)" title="Charles Gray (actor)">Charles Gray</a> assumed the character in both the 1976 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Seven-Per-Cent_Solution_(film)" title="The Seven-Per-Cent Solution (film)">The Seven-Per-Cent Solution</a></i> and four episodes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Granada_Television" title="Granada Television">Granada Television</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_(1984_TV_series)" title="Sherlock Holmes (1984 TV series)">The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes</a></i> series in the late 1980s and early 1990s. Gray's first two television appearances were adaptations of the two stories in which Mycroft actually appears (<i>The Greek Interpreter</i> and <i>The Bruce-Partington Plans</i>). In the two other appearances, the character was used to replace another for various reasons:
<ul>
<li><i>The Golden Pince-Nez</i> – Mycroft was used in place of Watson due to <a href="/wiki/Edward_Hardwicke" title="Edward Hardwicke">Edward Hardwicke</a> being unavailable.</li>
<li><i>The Mazarin Stone</i> – Mycroft was used in place of Sherlock owing to <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Brett" title="Jeremy Brett">Jeremy Brett</a>'s ill health.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>The 1975 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Adventure_of_Sherlock_Holmes%27_Smarter_Brother" title="The Adventure of Sherlock Holmes' Smarter Brother">The Adventure of Sherlock Holmes' Smarter Brother</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Gene_Wilder" title="Gene Wilder">Gene Wilder</a> as Holmes' <i>younger</i> brother "Sigerson Holmes," was inspired by Mycroft (he is mentioned, but doesn't appear except as a photograph of the three brothers as children).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Boris_Klyuyev" title="Boris Klyuyev">Boris Klyuyev</a> played Mycroft Holmes in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Sherlock_Holmes_and_Dr._Watson" title="The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes and Dr. Watson">The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes and Dr. Watson</a></i>, a Soviet TV series. Klyuyev was nine years younger than <a href="/wiki/Vasily_Livanov" title="Vasily Livanov">Vasily Livanov</a>, who played Sherlock Holmes. According to Sherlock, Mycroft is married and has a son.</li>
<li>He is also briefly mentioned in the 1985 film, <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Sherlock_Holmes" title="Young Sherlock Holmes">Young Sherlock Holmes</a></i>; when Sherlock is expelled from boarding school, he tells Watson that he plans to stay at his brother Mycroft's for a few days.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peter_Jeffrey" title="Peter Jeffrey">Peter Jeffrey</a> played Mycroft in the 1990 film <i>Hands of a Murderer</i> which starred <a href="/wiki/Edward_Woodward" title="Edward Woodward">Edward Woodward</a> as Sherlock, <a href="/wiki/John_Hillerman" title="John Hillerman">John Hillerman</a> as Watson and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Andrews" title="Anthony Andrews">Anthony Andrews</a> as <a href="/wiki/Professor_Moriarty" title="Professor Moriarty">Professor Moriarty</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jerome_Willis" title="Jerome Willis">Jerome Willis</a> played Mycroft in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_and_the_Leading_Lady_(1992_TV_film)" title="Sherlock Holmes and the Leading Lady (1992 TV film)">Sherlock Holmes and the Leading Lady</a></i>, a 1992 made-for-TV film which starred Christopher Lee as Holmes and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Macnee" title="Patrick Macnee">Patrick Macnee</a> as Watson.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/R._H._Thomson" title="R. H. Thomson">R. H. Thomson</a> played Mycroft in the 2001 made-for-TV film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Scandal" title="The Royal Scandal">The Royal Scandal</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Matt_Frewer" title="Matt Frewer">Matt Frewer</a>'s Sherlock.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Richard_E._Grant" title="Richard E. Grant">Richard E. Grant</a> played him as a semi-crippled young man – following a bad trip after he was injected with drugs by Moriarty – in <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock:_Case_of_Evil" title="Sherlock: Case of Evil">Sherlock: Case of Evil</a></i> (2002).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mark_Gatiss" title="Mark Gatiss">Mark Gatiss</a> plays Mycroft in the 2010 BBC television series <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_(TV_series)" title="Sherlock (TV series)">Sherlock</a></i> of which he is the co-creator. In this contemporary version, Sherlock and Mycroft exhibit smouldering animosity toward each other (which Dr. Watson characterises as "sibling rivalry" and Mycroft himself refers to as a "childish feud"). Mycroft is part of the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_Office" title="Cabinet Office">Cabinet Office</a>, and is so powerful that he can use <a href="/wiki/Mass_surveillance_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Mass surveillance in the United Kingdom">mass surveillance</a> to track Sherlock.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stephen_Fry" title="Stephen Fry">Stephen Fry</a> played Mycroft in the <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie" title="Guy Ritchie">Guy Ritchie</a>-directed <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes:_A_Game_of_Shadows" title="Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows">Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows</a></i>, released in December 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rhys_Ifans" title="Rhys Ifans">Rhys Ifans</a> played Mycroft Holmes in another modern adaptation, <i><a href="/wiki/Elementary_(TV_series)" title="Elementary (TV series)">Elementary</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In this series, Mycroft is introduced as a London <a href="/wiki/Restaurateur" title="Restaurateur">restaurateur</a> who later turns out to work for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MI6" title="MI6">MI6</a>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_(2013_TV_series)" title="Sherlock Holmes (2013 TV series)">Russian TV adaptation</a> from 2013, <a href="/wiki/Igor_Petrenko" title="Igor Petrenko">Igor Petrenko</a> played Mycroft Holmes, as a twin brother of Sherlock, who is serving <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">The Queen</a>.</li>
<li>In the NHK puppetry <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_(puppetry)" title="Sherlock Holmes (puppetry)">Sherlock Holmes</a></i>, Mycroft is a fat young man who is in the sixth grade of <a href="/wiki/Beeton_School" title="Beeton School">Beeton School</a>. He is in the position of managing the pupils of Dealer house in which he lives, the head of the pupil council and member of <a href="/wiki/Diogenes_Club" title="Diogenes Club">Diogenes Club</a> in his house. Though he has deductive powers superior to Sherlock, he is more calculating than his younger brother.</li>
<li>In the 2015 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mr_Holmes" title="Mr Holmes">Mr Holmes</a></i>, set in 1947, though it is revealed that Mycroft died a year or so earlier, he appears briefly, played by <a href="/wiki/John_Sessions" title="John Sessions">John Sessions</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Novels_and_short_stories">Novels and short stories</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mycroft_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Novels and short stories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The character has been used many times in works that are not adaptations of Holmes stories:</p>
<ul>
<li>The NBA Hall of Famer and All Time Leading Scorer, <a href="/wiki/Kareem_Abdul-Jabbar" title="Kareem Abdul-Jabbar">Kareem Abdul-Jabbar</a> and Anna Waterhouse authored <i>Mycroft Holmes</i>, released September 2015. Abdul-Jabbar and novelist <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Obstfeld" title="Raymond Obstfeld">Raymond Obstfeld</a> are writing a five-issue comic book series titled <i>Mycroft Holmes and the Apocalypse Handbook</i>, to be released in Fall 2016.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Jasper_Fforde" title="Jasper Fforde">Jasper Fforde</a>'s series of books about <a href="/wiki/Thursday_Next" title="Thursday Next">Thursday Next</a>, Mycroft is revealed to be Thursday's uncle, having escaped into fiction and taken up residence in the <i>Sherlock Holmes</i> series to escape the evil Goliath Corporation.</li>
<li>He was the main character in a series of mystery novels by the author <a href="/wiki/Quinn_Fawcett" title="Quinn Fawcett">Quinn Fawcett</a>.</li>
<li>He was also the main character in a series of mystery novels by the author <a href="/wiki/David_Dickinson" title="David Dickinson">David Dickinson</a>.</li>
<li>He is a recurring character in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mary_Russell_(fictional)" title="Mary Russell (fictional)">Mary Russell</a> mystery series by <a href="/wiki/Laurie_R._King" title="Laurie R. King">Laurie R. King</a>, which feature a retired Sherlock Holmes as a major character. Mycroft is portrayed as a senior figure in the British Secret Service, who occasionally calls on Russell and Holmes for assistance in specific cases.</li>
<li>A young Mycroft Holmes is the protagonist of a mystery-adventure "edited" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_P._Hodel" title="Michael P. Hodel">Michael P. Hodel</a> and Sean M. Wright, <i>Enter the Lion: A Posthumous Memoir of Mycroft Holmes</i> (published in hardcover by Hawthorn Books in 1979 in the U.S. and by JM Dent &amp; Sons Ltd. in 1980 in London (<a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0460044834">ISBN 0-460-04483-4</a>) and in paperback by Playboy Press in 1980). The action takes place in 1875, ten years after the end of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, at the time when Mycroft Holmes was a minor official in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_Office" title="Foreign Office">Foreign Office</a>. Mycroft is aided by his younger brother Sherlock, Victor Trevor (who appears in Doyle's tale "<a href="/wiki/The_Adventure_of_the_Gloria_Scott" title="The Adventure of the Gloria Scott">The Adventure of the Gloria Scott</a>"), and an adventurer known as "Captain Jericho", a mysterious former slave. They band together in an effort to prevent an attempt by former Confederate officers to involve the British government in a scheme to overthrow the United States government. The story also provides an explanation as to the antagonism between Sherlock Holmes and <a href="/wiki/Professor_Moriarty" title="Professor Moriarty">Professor Moriarty</a>.</li>
<li>Mycroft has a small but extremely important role in Ray Walsh's novel <i>The Mycroft Memoranda</i>, published in London by Andre Deutsch, 1984 (<a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0233975829">ISBN 0-233-97582-9</a>), in which Sherlock Holmes, at the request of Major Henry Smith, Acting Commissioner for the City of London, becomes involved in the hunt for <a href="/wiki/Jack_the_Ripper" title="Jack the Ripper">Jack the Ripper</a>.</li>
<li>Mycroft and the Diogenes Club play an important part in <a href="/wiki/Kim_Newman" title="Kim Newman">Kim Newman</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Dracula_series#Anno_Dracula" title="Anno Dracula series">Anno Dracula</a></i>.</li>
<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i> novel <i><a href="/wiki/All-Consuming_Fire" title="All-Consuming Fire">All-Consuming Fire</a></i> featured Sherlock and Mycroft Holmes, as well as the apocryphal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sherrinford_Holmes" title="Sherrinford Holmes">Sherringford Holmes</a>. The Doctor's <a href="/wiki/Companion_(Doctor_Who)" title="Companion (Doctor Who)">companion</a> <a href="/wiki/Bernice_Summerfield" title="Bernice Summerfield">Bernice Summerfield</a> was then reunited with Mycroft in the 2008 audio play <i><a href="/wiki/The_Adventure_of_the_Diogenes_Damsel" title="The Adventure of the Diogenes Damsel">The Adventure of the Diogenes Damsel</a></i> where he was voiced by <a href="/wiki/David_Warner_(actor)" title="David Warner (actor)">David Warner</a>.</li>
<li>The novel <i>Oscar Wilde and the Candlelight Murders</i> by <a href="/wiki/Gyles_Brandreth" title="Gyles Brandreth">Gyles Brandreth</a> suggests that <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Wilde" title="Oscar Wilde">Oscar Wilde</a>'s friendship with <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Conan_Doyle" title="Arthur Conan Doyle">Arthur Conan Doyle</a> led Doyle to create Mycroft as a caricature of Wilde: mentally brilliant, but indolent and lazy.</li>
<li>The award-winning short story by <a href="/wiki/Robert_J._Sawyer" title="Robert J. Sawyer">Robert J. Sawyer</a>, "You See But You Do Not Observe", portrays Mycroft Holmes' namesake involved in pulling Sherlock and Watson into the year 2096 to solve a scientific mystery.</li>
<li>He appears in the novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Italian_Secretary" title="The Italian Secretary">The Italian Secretary</a></i> (2005) by <a href="/wiki/Caleb_Carr" title="Caleb Carr">Caleb Carr</a>.</li>
<li>In the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enola_Holmes" title="Enola Holmes">Enola Holmes</a></i> series, Mycroft is the official legal guardian of their much younger sister, Enola, after the mysterious departure of their mother on her daughter's 14th birthday. Rather than submit to his wish for her to be sent to <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school" title="Boarding school">boarding school</a> to conform to contemporary feminine social mores, Enola instead runs away to secretly become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Private_detective" title="Private detective">private detective</a> in London while eluding her brothers. Through the series, Mycroft is steadfastly determined to capture her while Sherlock gradually grows to respect her considerable talents and begins to understand her reasons for her defiance. However, it is Mycroft who suspects that Enola may well be determined to become an adult colleague in his brother's profession, a notion Sherlock finds difficult to accept.</li>
<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Sherlock_Holmes_(books)" title="Young Sherlock Holmes (books)">Young Sherlock Holmes</a></i> series by <a href="/wiki/Andy_Lane" title="Andy Lane">Andrew Lane</a> features Mycroft Holmes.</li>
<li>In the story "Whitechapel Rose", by <a href="/wiki/Lorelei_Shannon" title="Lorelei Shannon">Lorelei Shannon</a>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jordan_K._Weisman" title="Jordan K. Weisman">Jordan K. Weisman</a>'s anthology of short stories set in the Shadowrun Universe Into the Shadows, Mycroft is revealed to be legendary among deckers (an in game term for futuristic hackers).</li>
<li>He is a recurring character in the <i>Amelia Watson</i> series of novels and short stories by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Mallory" title="Michael Mallory">Michael Mallory</a>, which recast him as a close confidant of King Edward VII and the head of England's fledgling secret service bureau.</li>
<li>When Mannie, the lead character and a computer engineer in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Heinlein" title="Robert Heinlein">Robert Heinlein</a> science fiction novel <a href="/wiki/The_Moon_Is_a_Harsh_Mistress" title="The Moon Is a Harsh Mistress">The Moon Is a Harsh Mistress</a>, realizes that the Holmes IV, the Lunar colony's super computer, has become self-aware. he names it Mike after Mycroft Holmes.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comics">Comics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mycroft_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Comics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Mycroft is depicted as a violent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychopath" title="Psychopath">psychopath</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/2000_AD_(comics)" title="2000 AD (comics)">2000 AD</a></i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Canon_Fodder" title="Canon Fodder">Canon Fodder</a></i>, issues #861–867) by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Millar" title="Mark Millar">Mark Millar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Weston" title="Chris Weston">Chris Weston</a>.</li>
<li>In Issue #6, Volume 1 of <a href="/wiki/Alan_Moore" title="Alan Moore">Alan Moore</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Extraordinary_Gentlemen" title="The League of Extraordinary Gentlemen">The League of Extraordinary Gentlemen</a></i>, Mycroft Holmes becomes the leader of British intelligence and uses the code-name "M" – a nod to the <a href="/wiki/M_(James_Bond)" title="M (James Bond)">fictional head of MI6</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ian_Fleming" title="Ian Fleming">Ian Fleming</a>'s <a href="/wiki/James_Bond_(literary_character)" title="James Bond (literary character)">James Bond</a> novels. It is hinted that he and his brother are not on the best of terms as a mention of Sherlock sending his regards are met by Mycroft with laughter and regarded as a joke. (Mycroft also appears as the Bond M in <i>Son of Holmes</i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anno_Dracula_(novel)" title="Anno Dracula (novel)">Anno Dracula</a></i>).</li>
<li>Mycroft Holmes is the head of "The Committee" in the comic book <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Limited_series" title="Limited series">miniseries</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Predator_(comics)" title="Predator (comics)">Predator: Nemesis</a></i>, by <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Rennie" title="Gordon Rennie">Gordon Rennie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colin_MacNeil" title="Colin MacNeil">Colin MacNeil</a> and published by <a href="/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics" title="Dark Horse Comics">Dark Horse Comics</a>. He hires the main character, Captain Edward Soames, to hunt down <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spring-Heeled_Jack" title="Spring-Heeled Jack">Spring-Heeled Jack</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Predator_(alien)" title="Predator (alien)">predator</a> hunting in the East End.</li>
<li>Mycroft Holmes appears at least twice in the <a href="/wiki/Italian_comics" title="Italian comics">Italian comic book</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Martin_Myst%C3%A8re" title="Martin Mystère">Martin Mystère</a></i> and spin-off series <i>Storie di Altrove/Stories from Elsewhere</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>The comic book series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Muppet_Show_(comics)#Fairy_Tale_Issues" title="The Muppet Show (comics)">Muppet Sherlock Holmes</a></i> features <a href="/wiki/Rowlf_the_Dog" title="Rowlf the Dog">Rowlf the Dog</a> as Mycroft Holmes.</li>
<li>In the popular manga <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History%27s_Strongest_Disciple" title="History's Strongest Disciple">History's Strongest Disciple</a></i>, Mycroft is a fictional martial artist who has worked with Sakaki Shio and Christopher Eclair.</li>
<li>Mycroft is featured as the main character of the comic <i>Mycroft Holmes and the Apocalypse Handbook</i>, published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Titan_Comics" title="Titan Comics">Titan Comics</a> and written by <a href="/wiki/Kareem_Abdul-Jabbar" title="Kareem Abdul-Jabbar">Kareem Abdul-Jabbar</a> and Raymon Obstfeld.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mycroft_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Mycroft has a minor role in the 1987 <a href="/wiki/Infocom" title="Infocom">Infocom</a> game <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock:_The_Riddle_of_the_Crown_Jewels" title="Sherlock: The Riddle of the Crown Jewels">Sherlock: The Riddle of the Crown Jewels</a></i></li>
<li>He plays a central role as the victim of an assassination attempt in the PC game <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lost_Files_of_Sherlock_Holmes" title="The Lost Files of Sherlock Holmes">The Lost Files of Sherlock Holmes</a>: The Case of the Rose Tattoo</i>.</li>
<li>In the 2009 PC and Xbox game <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_vs._Jack_the_Ripper" title="Sherlock Holmes vs. Jack the Ripper">Sherlock Holmes vs. Jack the Ripper</a></i>, the younger Holmes receives assistance on a case from his brother.</li>
<li>Mycroft appears as a minor character in the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Focus_Home_Interactive" title="Focus Home Interactive">Focus Home Interactive</a> game <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes:_Crimes_%26_Punishments" title="Sherlock Holmes: Crimes &amp; Punishments">Sherlock Holmes: Crimes &amp; Punishments</a></i>, seeking and providing occasional assistance; in one case, Sherlock has the option to call on him to apprehend the suspects.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References_in_popular_culture">References in popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mycroft_Holmes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: References in popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mycroft_AI&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mycroft AI (page does not exist)">Mycroft AI</a> is named after Mycroft Holmes.</li>
<li>Mycroft was parodied in the <i><a href="/wiki/Solar_Pons" title="Solar Pons">Solar Pons</a></i> series with a character named Bancroft Stoneham Pons, who was also seven years older than the leading protagonist.</li>
<li>Mycroft Holmes was the inspiration for the name of the silent assistant quiz master of BBC Radio 4's programme <i><a href="/wiki/Brain_of_Britain" title="Brain of Britain">Brain of Britain</a></i>. The phrase "Mycroft is shaking his head" became well known to listeners. <a href="/wiki/Ian_Gillies" title="Ian Gillies">Ian Gillies</a> (who was known as Mycroft) died in 2002 and was replaced by a character known as "Jorkins".</li>
<li>Mycroft was the inspiration for the name of a character in <a href="/wiki/Robert_A._Heinlein" title="Robert A. Heinlein">Robert A. Heinlein</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Moon_Is_a_Harsh_Mistress" title="The Moon Is a Harsh Mistress">The Moon Is a Harsh Mistress</a></i>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mycroft_Holmes_(computer)" title="Mycroft Holmes (computer)">"Mycroft" a.k.a. Mike</a>, a H.O.L.M.E.S. (<i>"High-Optional, Logical, Multi-Evaluating Supervisor"</i>) Mark 4, a sentient computer. At one point in the story, Mike indicates Sherlock is indeed his brother.</li>
<li>First series of <i><a href="/wiki/SeaQuest_DSV" title="SeaQuest DSV">seaQuest DSV</a></i>, in the episode "Photon Bullet", a reformed computer hacker used the <a href="/wiki/User_(computing)" title="User (computing)">handle</a> "Mycroft" while at an underwater telecommunications node.</li>
<li>British writer Colin Dexter, author of the Inspector Morse series of books, wrote a Sherlock Holmes short story "A Case of Mis-Identity", part of a collection of short stories published under the title "Morse's Greatest Mystery", in which Watson's practical knowledge of the circumstances of a case outwits both Sherlock and Mycroft.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/John_Dickson_Carr" title="John Dickson Carr">John Dickson Carr</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Sir_Henry_Merrivale" title="Sir Henry Merrivale">Sir Henry Merrivale</a>" novels, the brilliant, overweight Military Intelligence chief is compared to Mycroft Holmes, much to his annoyance.</li>
<li>At one point it was planned for <a href="/wiki/Gregory_House" title="Gregory House">Gregory House</a> (who is based on Sherlock Holmes and also lives at 221B) to have an elder brother who was based on Mycroft. <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Fry" title="Stephen Fry">Stephen Fry</a> (who was the comedic partner of <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Laurie" title="Hugh Laurie">Hugh Laurie</a>) was to play him but was unable, due to other commitments. Fry would later go on to portray Mycroft in the 2011 film "<a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes:_A_Game_of_Shadows" title="Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows">Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows</a>"</li>
<li>In the TV series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Numb3rs_(TV_series)" title="Numb3rs (TV series)">Numb3rs</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Angels_and_Devils_(Numbers)" title="Angels and Devils (Numbers)">Angels and Devils</a>", <a href="/wiki/Larry_Fleinhardt" title="Larry Fleinhardt">Larry Fleinhardt</a>, played by <a href="/wiki/Peter_MacNicol" title="Peter MacNicol">Peter MacNicol</a>, says: "I have rather always fancied myself more as a Mycroft than a Dr. Watson." He expands upon this reference in the series finale when he assumes the role of math/science expert for the FBI in place of <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Eppes" title="Charlie Eppes">Charlie Eppes</a> saying, "...like Sherlock's brother Mycroft Holmes, I prefer to do the conceptualizing, leaving the grunt work to others."</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Nobuhiro_Watsuki" title="Nobuhiro Watsuki">Nobuhiro Watsuki</a>'s manga series <i><a href="/wiki/Embalming_(manga)" title="Embalming (manga)">Embalming -The Another Tale of Frankenstein-</a></i>, Asuhit Richter goes to the <a href="/wiki/Diogenes_Club" title="Diogenes Club">Diogenes Club</a> in London to meet one of the club's founders and his client "Mike Roft", a play on Mycroft, who is also a high-standing government official. Mike remarks that "if you are looking for someone, my younger brother is quite good at that type of thing" and has him locate Dr. Peabody and Fury Flatliner. Only the younger brother's silhouette is shown, but it is obviously that of Sherlock Holmes.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Honor_Harrington" title="Honor Harrington">Honor Harrington</a> novel <i><a href="/wiki/A_Rising_Thunder" title="A Rising Thunder">A Rising Thunder</a></i>, the name Mycroft is used as the code designation for a new Manticoran missile fire control system to be deployed for system defence, based somewhat upon the Havenites' 'Moriarty' system (the name of which is a reference to <a href="/wiki/Professor_Moriarty" title="Professor Moriarty">Professor Moriarty</a>).</li>
<li>In the American TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Monk_(TV_series)" title="Monk (TV series)">Monk</a></i>, Adrian Monk has an older brother called Ambrose Monk. Ambrose, like his brother Adrian, possesses uncanny powers of deduction and memory. Unfortunately, he suffers from a severe form of <a href="/wiki/Agoraphobia" title="Agoraphobia">agoraphobia</a>. As of 2003, he had not left the home he grew up in since 1971.</li>
</ul>
