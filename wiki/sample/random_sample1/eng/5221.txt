<p><i><b>Mike's New Car</b></i> is a 2002 <a href="/wiki/Pixar" title="Pixar">Pixar</a> <a href="/wiki/Computer_animation" title="Computer animation">computer animated short</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a>, starring the two main characters from <i><a href="/wiki/Monsters,_Inc." title="Monsters, Inc.">Monsters, Inc.</a></i>, Sulley and Mike. Directed by <a href="/wiki/Pete_Docter" title="Pete Docter">Pete Docter</a> and Roger L. Gould, it is the first Pixar short to use dialogue and the first to take characters and situations from a previously established work.</p>
<p>The short premiered on September 17, 2002, included in the <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a href="/wiki/VHS" title="VHS">VHS</a> release of <i><a href="/wiki/Monsters,_Inc." title="Monsters, Inc.">Monsters, Inc.</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVD_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-DVD-1">[1]</a></sup> It was nominated for a 2002 Academy Award for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Animated_Short_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film">Best Animated Short Film</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike%27s_New_Car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mike is obsessed with his new <a href="/wiki/Six-wheel_drive" title="Six-wheel drive">six-wheel drive</a> <a href="/wiki/Car" title="Car">car</a>, and insists on showing it off to Sulley. Unfortunately for Mike, <a href="/wiki/Murphy%27s_law" title="Murphy's law">anything that can go wrong does go wrong</a>. Sulley plays with the <a href="/wiki/Power_seat" title="Power seat">power seat</a> until an annoyed Mike asks him to stop. Mike starts the <a href="/wiki/Engine" title="Engine">engine</a> and hears the seatbelt reminder tone sound off. Although Sulley manages to get his seatbelt on easily, Mike finds his seatbelt stuck and accidentally locks himself out of the car while trying to unstick it. Sulley, trying to be helpful, but confused by the massive amount of buttons on the dashboard, pops the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hood_(vehicle)" title="Hood (vehicle)">hood</a> open. When Mike goes over to close it, Sulley ends up closing the hood on Mike's fingers, causing Mike to scream, until Sulley ends up trapping Mike in the engine compartment. Mike manages to escape, re-enters the car, and is exasperated by the continuous seatbelt reminder tone. Sulley reaches for another button on the dash, and when Mike manages to put his seat-belt on, he turns the windshield wipers on by accident. Mike shouts, "Don't touch anything! I'll do it." and pushes a button that launches the entire car into chaotic malfunction, including <a href="/wiki/Conga_(music)" title="Conga (music)">conga</a> music playing loudly on the car's stereo system. Mike finally ends the chaos by pulling the key out of the ignition, but as Sulley adds insult to injury when, in an attempt to realign the rearview mirror, he breaks it off. Mike gets angry, forces Sulley out of the car, and speeds away, wrecking the car completely. Sulley mutters, "Huh, that's weird, the <a href="/wiki/Airbag" title="Airbag">airbag</a> didn't go off." Right on cue, the airbag inflates, and its force sends Mike flying back up the street. Sulley catches Mike, who mourns for his old car before agreeing to walk to work while the credits roll.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike%27s_New_Car&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
