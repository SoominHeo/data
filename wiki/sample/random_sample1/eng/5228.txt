<p><b>Thomas Michael Bond</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">CBE</a> (born 13 January 1926) is an English author, best known for his <a href="/wiki/Paddington_Bear" title="Paddington Bear">Paddington Bear</a> series of books. Bond was awarded a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CBE" title="CBE">CBE</a> in the Queen's Birthday Honours 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Bond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Michael_Bond,_Saint_Mary%27s_Square,_Paddington.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3264" data-file-width="2448" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Michael_Bond%2C_Saint_Mary%27s_Square%2C_Paddington.jpg/220px-Michael_Bond%2C_Saint_Mary%27s_Square%2C_Paddington.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Michael_Bond%2C_Saint_Mary%27s_Square%2C_Paddington.jpg/330px-Michael_Bond%2C_Saint_Mary%27s_Square%2C_Paddington.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Michael_Bond%2C_Saint_Mary%27s_Square%2C_Paddington.jpg/440px-Michael_Bond%2C_Saint_Mary%27s_Square%2C_Paddington.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Bond was born in <a href="/wiki/Newbury,_Berkshire" title="Newbury, Berkshire">Newbury</a> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Berkshire" title="Reading, Berkshire">Reading, Berkshire</a>, where his visits to Reading Station to watch the <i>Cornish Riviera Express</i> go steaming through started a love of trains. His father was a manager for the post office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Guardian_28_November_2014_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Guardian_28_November_2014-2">[2]</a></sup> He was educated at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presentation_College,_Reading" title="Presentation College, Reading">Presentation College</a>, a school in Reading, Berkshire. His time there was unhappy. He told UK newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> in November 2014 that his parents had chosen the school "for the simple reason my mother liked the colour of the blazers. She didn’t make many mistakes in life but that was one of them". Consequently, he left education aged fourteen, despite his parents’ wishes for him to go to university.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Guardian_28_November_2014_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Guardian_28_November_2014-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> was under way and he went to work in a <a href="/wiki/Solicitor" title="Solicitor">solicitor's office</a> for a year and then as an engineer's assistant for the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>.</p>
<p>In February 1943, Michael Bond survived an air raid in Reading. The building in which he was working collapsed under him, killing 41 people and injuring many more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Shortly afterwards he volunteered for <a href="/wiki/Aircrew" title="Aircrew">aircrew</a> service in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> as a 17-year-old but he was discharged after suffering from acute <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_sickness" title="Air sickness">air sickness</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrank_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-jrank-5">[5]</a></sup> He then served in the Middlesex Regiment of the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> until 1947.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc2014_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc2014-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Author">Author</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Bond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Author">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bond began writing in 1945 whilst stationed with the army in <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a> and sold his first short story to the magazine <i>London Opinion</i>. He was paid seven guineas, and thought he "wouldn't mind being a writer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Guardian_28_November_2014_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-The_Guardian_28_November_2014-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1958, after producing a number of plays and short stories and while working as a BBC television cameraman (where he worked on <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Peter" title="Blue Peter">Blue Peter</a></i> for a time), his first book, <i>A Bear Called Paddington</i>, was published. This was the start of Bond's series of books recounting tales of a bear from "Darkest <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>", whose Aunt Lucy sends him to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, carrying a jar of marmalade; the Brown family found the bear at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paddington_Station" title="Paddington Station">Paddington Station</a>, and adopted him, naming the bear after the railway station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc2014_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc2014-6">[6]</a></sup> By 1967, Bond was able to give up his BBC job to work full-time as a writer.</p>
<p>Paddington's adventures have sold over 35 million books, have been published in nearly twenty countries, in over forty languages, and have inspired pop bands, race horses, plays, hot air balloons, a movie and television series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc2014_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-bbc2014-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Bond stated in December 2007 that he did not plan to continue the adventures of Paddington Bear in further volumes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> However, in April 2014 it was reported a new book, titled <i>Love From Paddington</i>, would be published that autumn. In a film, <i><a href="/wiki/Paddington_(film)" title="Paddington (film)">Paddington</a></i> (2014), based on the books, Bond has a credited cameo as the Kindly Gentleman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bond has also written another series of children's books, the adventures of a <a href="/wiki/Guinea_pig" title="Guinea pig">guinea pig</a> named <a href="/wiki/Olga_da_Polga" title="Olga da Polga">Olga da Polga</a>, named after the Bond family's pet,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Guardian_28_November_2014_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-The_Guardian_28_November_2014-2">[2]</a></sup> as well as the animated BBC television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Herbs" title="The Herbs">The Herbs</a></i> (1968). Bond also writes culinary mystery stories for adults featuring Monsieur Pamplemousse and his faithful <a href="/wiki/Bloodhound" title="Bloodhound">bloodhound</a>, Pommes Frites.</p>
<p>Bond wrote a <i>Reflection on the Passing of the Years</i> shortly after his 90th birthday. The piece was read by <a href="/wiki/David_Attenborough" title="David Attenborough">David Attenborough</a>, who also turned 90 in 2016, at the national service of thanksgiving to commemorate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_II" title="Queen Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a>'s 90th birthday at <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_Cathedral" title="St Paul's Cathedral">St Paul's Cathedral</a> in June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Bond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1997, Bond was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">Order of the British Empire</a> (OBE) Commander of the British Empire (CBE) for services to children's literature. On 6 July 2007 the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Reading" title="University of Reading">University of Reading</a> awarded him an <a href="/wiki/Honorary_degree" title="Honorary degree">Honorary</a> <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Letters" title="Doctor of Letters">Doctor of Letters</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Bond&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bond is married, has two adult children and lives in London, not far from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paddington_Station" title="Paddington Station">Paddington Station</a>, the place that inspired many of his books.</p>
