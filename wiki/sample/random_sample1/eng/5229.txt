<p><b>Michael Bisping</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'p' in 'pie'">p</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="/ŋ/ 'ng' in 'sing'">ŋ</span></span>/</a></span></span>, <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i><b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">BIS</span></span></b>-ping</i></a></span>; born 28 February 1979) is an English <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_artist" title="Mixed martial artist">mixed martial artist</a>. He fights in the <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Fighting_Championship" title="Ultimate Fighting Championship">Ultimate Fighting Championship</a> (UFC), and is the current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Middleweight_Championship" title="UFC Middleweight Championship">UFC Middleweight Champion</a>. He is a former <a href="/wiki/List_of_Cage_Rage_champions#World_Light_Heavyweight_Championship" title="List of Cage Rage champions">Cage Rage Light Heavyweight Champion</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Fighter_3" title="The Ultimate Fighter 3">The Ultimate Fighter 3</a></i> Light Heavyweight Tournament winner. He was the first English fighter in a UFC main event at <a href="/wiki/UFC_78" title="UFC 78">UFC 78</a> and the first British fighter to win a UFC championship at <a href="/wiki/UFC_199" title="UFC 199">UFC 199</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Bisping was born on a <a href="/wiki/British_Forces_Cyprus" title="British Forces Cyprus">British military base</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nicosia" title="Nicosia">Nicosia</a>, Cyprus, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Clitheroe" title="Clitheroe">Clitheroe, Lancashire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> His <a href="/wiki/Poles" title="Poles">Polish</a> paternal grandfather, Andrew (Andrzej), fled with his family from Poland to England after the <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Poland" title="Invasion of Poland">German invasion</a> in 1939. The Bisping family is of noble descent in Poland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Bisping's father Jan was in the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> while his mother is <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He began training <a href="/wiki/Jujutsu" title="Jujutsu">Jujutsu</a> at the age of eight. In 1994, at the age of 15, he competed as an amateur in Britain's first "no holds barred" competition, a precursor to modern MMA, called Knock Down Sport Budo (KSBO).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the age of 18, Bisping decided to abandon his martial arts training "to pursue real life".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Less than a year later, Bisping began training in <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Karate" title="Karate">karate</a> on the advice of Allan Clarkin, owner of Black Knights Kickboxing, who saw potential in him. Bisping enjoyed a short but successful kickboxing career, winning the North West Area title and later the Pro British light heavyweight kickboxing title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-9">[9]</a></sup> After again briefly quitting competition in 1998, Bisping returned to kickboxing to take the Pro British light heavyweight title for a second time. Soon after winning his second kickboxing title, he was forced to abandon his full-time training for a "real job". He worked at factories, slaughterhouses, and demolition companies; he was an upholsterer, postman, tiler, plasterer, and salesman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mixed_martial_arts_career">Mixed martial arts career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span></h3>
<p>Bisping made his professional mixed martial arts debut at <i>Pride &amp; Glory 2: Battle of the Ages</i> on 4 April 2004, taking a 0:38 submission victory over Steve Mathews. Just one month later, Bisping scored his first <a href="/wiki/Knockout" title="Knockout">knockout</a> against John Weir at <i>UK MMA Challenge 7: Rage &amp; Fury</i>. In his third MMA match, Bisping became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cage_Rage" title="Cage Rage">Cage Rage</a> light heavyweight champion at Cage Rage 7, defeating Mark Epstein by technical knockout. Bisping went on to defend his championship title in a rematch against Epstein at Cage Rage 9 in a knockout victory that solidified Bisping as one of the top light heavyweight fighters in England. It also earned him the moniker "The Great British Hope", by UFC.com.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>At <i>The Ultimate Fight Club UK: Natural Instinct</i> on 29 January 2005, Bisping made his cage kickboxing debut against David Brown in a light heavyweight contest. With Brown badly cut, Bisping picked up the win via doctor stoppage in round 2.</p>
<p>Bisping made his debut for the promotion at <i>Ultimate Force</i> on 30 April 2005, defeating Dave Radford to win the vacant Cage Warriors light heavyweight title. Bisping then competed in another light heavyweight cage kickboxing contest, against <a href="/wiki/Cyrille_Diabat%C3%A9" title="Cyrille Diabaté">Cyrille Diabaté</a> at <i>CWFC: Strike Force 1</i> on 21 May 2005, losing to Diabaté via decision after the end of the first extra round. He captured the FX3 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light-heavyweight" title="Light-heavyweight">light-heavyweight</a> title on 18 June 2005 and was reigning on the UK's major federations. In his first Cage Warriors title defence, Bisping defeated Miika Mehmet at <i>CWFC: Strike Force 2</i>, on 16 July 2005.</p>
<p>In September 2005, Cage Rage stripped the light heavyweight title from Bisping due to "management issues",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> though Cage Warriors stated that "Bisping was willing to defend his title but is being punished by Cage Rage due to his Wolfslair and Cage Warriors links".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Towards the end of 2005, Bisping again successfully defended the Cage Warriors title, this time against Jakob Lovstad<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ross_Pointon" title="Ross Pointon">Ross Pointon</a> in the <i>CWFC: Strike Force</i> series of events, leading to a record of 10 wins and no losses.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ultimate_Fighting_Championship">Ultimate Fighting Championship</span></h3>
<p>In early 2006 Bisping was featured on the UFC's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Fighter" title="The Ultimate Fighter">The Ultimate Fighter</a></i> reality television series as a contestant training under <a href="/wiki/Tito_Ortiz" title="Tito Ortiz">Tito Ortiz</a>. He won a preliminary bout against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kristian_Rothaermel" title="Kristian Rothaermel">Kristian Rothaermel</a> by TKO, followed by a semi-final win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ross_Pointon" title="Ross Pointon">Ross Pointon</a> by submission after landing a flying knee and a series of strikes. In the finals, Bisping finished <a href="/wiki/Josh_Haynes" title="Josh Haynes">Josh Haynes</a> by TKO at 4:14 into the second round, making Bisping the second Light Heavyweight winner of <i>The Ultimate Fighter</i> television series.</p>
<p>Five months after his victory in the <i>TUF 3</i> finals, Bisping was slated to fight <a href="/wiki/Eric_Schafer" title="Eric Schafer">Eric Schafer</a> at <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Fighter_4" title="The Ultimate Fighter 4">The Ultimate Fighter 4</a></i> finale, but withdrew because of problems acquiring his visa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The fight was re-scheduled and held on 30 December at <i><a href="/wiki/UFC_66" title="UFC 66">UFC 66</a></i>. Bisping defeated Schafer by TKO at 4:24 in the first round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 21 April 2007, Bisping scored a TKO win over <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Sinosic" title="Elvis Sinosic">Elvis Sinosic</a> at <i><a href="/wiki/UFC_70" title="UFC 70">UFC 70</a></i> in Manchester, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Sinosic came out fighting and Bisping eagerly obliged, firing back before throwing the Australian to the canvas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ufc.com_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ufc.com-19">[19]</a></sup> Bisping then launched a vicious ground and pound attack, opening a cut on Sinosic's forehead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In the second round Bisping was knocked down from a knee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Then Sinosic almost secured a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kimura_lock" title="Kimura lock">kimura</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ufc.com_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-ufc.com-19">[19]</a></sup> before Bisping reversed and pounded away until Steve Mazzagatti stopped the bout at 1:40 of the second round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ufc.com_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-ufc.com-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping was a special guest referee at the Cage Warriors events <i>Enter The Wolfslair</i> on 5 March 2005 and <i>CWFC: Strike Force 6</i> on 27 May 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 8 September 2007, Bisping faced former <i>Ultimate Fighter 3</i> rival <a href="/wiki/Matt_Hamill" title="Matt Hamill">Matt Hamill</a> in London, England and won a controversial split decision after 3 rounds. Hamill pushed the pace and took Bisping down numerous times in the first two rounds, with Bisping hitting solid strikes and blocking numerous takedown attempts towards the end of the second and in the third round. Both American judges scored the bout 29–28 for Bisping, the lone British judge voted 30–27 for Hamill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping's next match at <a href="/wiki/UFC_78" title="UFC 78">UFC 78</a> against fellow <i>Ultimate Fighter</i> winner <a href="/wiki/Rashad_Evans" title="Rashad Evans">Rashad Evans</a> resulted in his first loss, via split decision with the scores being 29–28 for Bisping, and 29–28 (twice) for Evans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Move_down_to_middleweight">Move down to middleweight</span></h4>
<p>Since the start of his MMA career, people had been advising Bisping that he was a little too small for light heavyweight and would do better at middleweight, and after his loss to Evans, Bisping decided to drop down a weight division, with UFC president <a href="/wiki/Dana_White" title="Dana White">Dana White</a>'s support.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> On 19 April 2008 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_83" title="UFC 83">UFC 83</a>, Bisping made his middleweight debut against <a href="/wiki/Charles_McCarthy_(fighter)" title="Charles McCarthy (fighter)">Charles McCarthy</a>. Bisping won by TKO, as McCarthy was unable to continue after the first round due to a forearm injury he sustained after receiving an unanswered series of knees and uppercuts from Bisping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping's next fight at middleweight was scheduled to be on 7 June 2008 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_85" title="UFC 85">UFC 85</a> in London, against <a href="/wiki/Chris_Leben" title="Chris Leben">Chris Leben</a>. However, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Day_(fighter)" title="Jason Day (fighter)">Jason Day</a> stepped in after Leben was sentenced to jail for 35 days and unable to compete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MMAWeekly.com_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-MMAWeekly.com-28">[28]</a></sup> Bisping won the fight in dominant fashion in the first round, securing two takedowns and applying high paced pressure; it was his second 1st round TKO in a row.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping finally fought <a href="/wiki/Chris_Leben" title="Chris Leben">Chris Leben</a> in the headliner of <a href="/wiki/UFC_89" title="UFC 89">UFC 89</a> on 18 October 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MMAWeekly.com_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-MMAWeekly.com-28">[28]</a></sup> Bisping won by unanimous decision. The judges scored the fight 30–27 (twice), and 29–28 for Bisping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Bisping used effective counter punching in the fight to keep the aggressive Leben at bay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> After the fight Leben tested positive for <a href="/wiki/Stanozolol" title="Stanozolol">Stanozolol</a> and was suspended for nine months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the fight with Leben, Bisping was confirmed as one of the coaches on the ninth season of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Fighter" title="The Ultimate Fighter">The Ultimate Fighter</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The other coach was The Former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PRIDE_Fighting_Championships" title="PRIDE Fighting Championships">PRIDE</a> 205lb &amp; 185lb Champion <a href="/wiki/Dan_Henderson" title="Dan Henderson">Dan Henderson</a>. After the show's conclusion Bisping fought Henderson at <a href="/wiki/UFC_100" title="UFC 100">UFC 100</a> on 11 July 2009 at <a href="/wiki/Mandalay_Bay" title="Mandalay Bay">Mandalay Bay</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The fight was believed to be an eliminator for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Middleweight_Championship" title="UFC Middleweight Championship">UFC Middleweight Championship</a> top contender spot. Bisping was knocked out by Henderson with a flush overhand right while he was circling to his left. Henderson followed up with a strike after Bisping had fallen to the ground before the fight was stopped. It marked the first occasion Bisping had ever conceded a knockout loss.</p>
<p>Bisping faced Spirit MC and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PRIDE_Fighting_Championships" title="PRIDE Fighting Championships">PRIDE</a> veteran <a href="/wiki/Denis_Kang" title="Denis Kang">Denis Kang</a> at <a href="/wiki/UFC_105" title="UFC 105">UFC 105</a> on 14 November 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Bisping TKO'd Kang in the 2nd round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-38">[38]</a></sup> Kang dropped Bisping with a right hand in the first round which stunned the Briton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Kang followed Bisping to the floor and attempted submissions, but Bisping defended well and neutralised Kang's attacks despite Kang briefly having full mount.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-38">[38]</a></sup> In the 2nd round Bisping secured a single-leg takedown and the complexion of the fight changed. He unleashed a ground and pound attack on the Canadian, which Kang failed to recover from. The fight was awarded "Fight of the Night" giving both Kang and Bisping a $40,000 bonus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping next faced former <a href="/wiki/Pride_Fighting_Championships" title="Pride Fighting Championships">PRIDE</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pride_Middleweight_Championship" title="Pride Middleweight Championship">Middleweight Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Wanderlei_Silva" title="Wanderlei Silva">Wanderlei Silva</a> at <a href="/wiki/UFC_110" title="UFC 110">UFC 110</a> on 21 February 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Wanderlei Silva's ability to flurry in the final seconds of the second and third rounds, two knockdowns, and an attempted guillotine choke contributed to his victory by a clear unanimous decision (29–28, 29–28, 29–28).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping defeated <a href="/wiki/Dan_Miller_(fighter)" title="Dan Miller (fighter)">Dan Miller</a> by unanimous decision on 29 May 2010 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_114" title="UFC 114">UFC 114</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping faced and defeated <a href="/wiki/Yoshihiro_Akiyama" title="Yoshihiro Akiyama">Yoshihiro Akiyama</a> on 16 October 2010 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_120" title="UFC 120">UFC 120</a>, winning 30–27 on all three judges' scorecards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Though Bisping was rocked early in the fight by Akiyama, he regained his composure and landed combos repeatedly on Akiyama to sweep all three judges scorecards. The fight was awarded Fight of the Night honours.</p>
<p>Bisping faced off in an emotionally charged fight against <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Rivera_(fighter)" title="Jorge Rivera (fighter)">Jorge Rivera</a> on 26 February 2011 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_127" title="UFC 127">UFC 127</a>. Both fighters had trash talked each other before the fight, having to be restrained at the weigh-in during a verbal argument. Bisping dominated the first round scoring a few takedowns until he delivered an illegal knee to the head of Rivera. A point was taken from Bisping and, after a lengthy halt to the action, the fight continued. The fight ended with a TKO from Bisping in the second round. After the fight Bisping spat at Rivera's corner and confronted Rivera on pre-fight comments that Bisping thought disparaged his family. Rivera denied the comments and tried to mend things but Bisping told him to "Go home" and called him a "loser." He immediately apologised in the post match interview.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> In the aftermath of the incident Bisping faced "disciplinary actions" and was fined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Multiple fighters have since come forward saying they want to fight Bisping in light of the incident including UFC middleweight notables <a href="/wiki/Chael_Sonnen" title="Chael Sonnen">Chael Sonnen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vitor_Belfort" title="Vitor Belfort">Vitor Belfort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demian_Maia" title="Demian Maia">Demian Maia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Belcher" title="Alan Belcher">Alan Belcher</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nate_Marquardt" title="Nate Marquardt">Nate Marquardt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 May 2011, it was revealed that Bisping would be one of the coaches of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Fighter_14" title="The Ultimate Fighter 14">The Ultimate Fighter 14</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Jason_Miller_(fighter)" title="Jason Miller (fighter)">Jason Miller</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> There were concerns that Miller had spies in Bisping's training camp, but Bisping was confident that was not true.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Bisping defeated <a href="/wiki/Jason_Miller_(fighter)" title="Jason Miller (fighter)">Jason Miller</a> via third round TKO on 3 December 2011 at <i>The Ultimate Fighter 14</i> finale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping was expected to face <a href="/wiki/Demian_Maia" title="Demian Maia">Demian Maia</a> on 28 January 2012 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_on_Fox_2" title="UFC on Fox 2">UFC on Fox 2</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stupp1_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-stupp1-53">[53]</a></sup> However, an injury forced <a href="/wiki/Mark_Mu%C3%B1oz" title="Mark Muñoz">Mark Muñoz</a> out of his bout on the same card with <a href="/wiki/Chael_Sonnen" title="Chael Sonnen">Chael Sonnen</a> and Bisping instead fought Sonnen for a chance to challenge <a href="/wiki/Anderson_Silva" title="Anderson Silva">Anderson Silva</a> for the Middleweight title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> Sonnen defeated Bisping via a controversial unanimous decision.</p>
<p>Bisping was expected to face <a href="/wiki/Tim_Boetsch" title="Tim Boetsch">Tim Boetsch</a> on 21 July 2012 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_149" title="UFC 149">UFC 149</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> However, Bisping was forced out of the bout with an injury and replaced by promotional newcomer <a href="/wiki/Hector_Lombard" title="Hector Lombard">Hector Lombard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping faced <a href="/wiki/Brian_Stann" title="Brian Stann">Brian Stann</a> on 22 September 2012 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_152" title="UFC 152">UFC 152</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> Bisping won the fight via unanimous decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping faced former UFC Light Heavyweight Champion <a href="/wiki/Vitor_Belfort" title="Vitor Belfort">Vitor Belfort</a> on 19 January 2013 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_on_FX:_Belfort_vs._Bisping" title="UFC on FX: Belfort vs. Bisping">UFC on FX: Belfort vs. Bisping</a> in a fight which, if won, would have given Bisping a title shot. However, he lost the fight via second-round TKO after getting hit flush by a head kick.</p>
<p>Bisping went on to face <a href="/wiki/Alan_Belcher" title="Alan Belcher">Alan Belcher</a> on 27 April 2013 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_159" title="UFC 159">UFC 159</a>. In the first round Bisping managed to out-<a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">box</a> his opponent, score a brief trip take-down, and land a very accurate, clean and extremely solid knee. Bisping then noticeably stepped up the pace of the fight in round 2, landing some heavy kicks and multiple combinations of solid punches. The fight was then stopped at 4:29 of round 3 as Bisping inadvertently poked Belcher in the eye, rendering Belcher unable to continue. Bisping won the bout via unanimous technical decision. The judges' scores in Bisping's favour read 30–27, 30–27, and 29–28.</p>
<p>Bisping was expected to face <a href="/wiki/Mark_Mu%C3%B1oz" title="Mark Muñoz">Mark Muñoz</a> on 26 October 2013 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Fight_Night_30" title="UFC Fight Night 30">UFC Fight Night 30</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> However, Bisping pulled out of the bout with a <a href="/wiki/Retinal_detachment" title="Retinal detachment">detached retina</a> eye injury and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Lyoto_Machida" title="Lyoto Machida">Lyoto Machida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> The detached retina required surgery, including full removal of the eyeball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>After nearly a year away from the sport due to his eye injury, Bisping returned to face <a href="/wiki/Tim_Kennedy_(fighter)" title="Tim Kennedy (fighter)">Tim Kennedy</a> on 16 April 2014 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Fighter_Nations_Finale:_Bisping_vs._Kennedy" title="The Ultimate Fighter Nations Finale: Bisping vs. Kennedy">The Ultimate Fighter Nations Finale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> He lost the fight via unanimous decision.</p>
<p>In April 2014, it was reported that both Bisping and <a href="/wiki/Brad_Tavares" title="Brad Tavares">Brad Tavares</a> were interested in a potential fight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> However, in May 2014, it was announced that Bisping would face <a href="/wiki/Cung_Le" title="Cung Le">Cung Le</a> on 23 August 2014 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Fight_Night_48" title="UFC Fight Night 48">UFC Fight Night 48</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Bisping won the one-sided fight via TKO in the fourth round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> The win also earned Bisping his first <i>Performance of the Night</i> bonus award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> After the bout, it was revealed that Le tested positive for elevated levels of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HGH" title="HGH">HGH</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> There has been some dispute about the abnormal test, as the lab in question was not WADA-approved, did not do the appropriate HGH test,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> and destroyed the blood sample before confirmatory tests could be done.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> Ultimately, on 21 October, the UFC reversed their decision and rescinded Le's suspension. This decision was made after several flaws were found in the testing process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping faced <a href="/wiki/Luke_Rockhold" title="Luke Rockhold">Luke Rockhold</a> on 8 November 2014 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Fight_Night_55" title="UFC Fight Night 55">UFC Fight Night 55</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> He lost the fight in the second round after being dropped by a head kick and then submitted with a guillotine choke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping faced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C.B._Dollaway" title="C.B. Dollaway">C.B. Dollaway</a> on 25 April 2015 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_186" title="UFC 186">UFC 186</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> Bisping won the fight by unanimous decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping faced <a href="/wiki/Thales_Leites" title="Thales Leites">Thales Leites</a> on 18 July 2015 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Fight_Night_72" title="UFC Fight Night 72">UFC Fight Night 72</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> He won the back-and-forth fight by split decision (47–48, 49–46, and 48–47).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping was expected to face <a href="/wiki/Robert_Whittaker_(fighter)" title="Robert Whittaker (fighter)">Robert Whittaker</a> on 15 November 2015 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_193" title="UFC 193">UFC 193</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> However, it was announced on 30 September 2015 that Bisping withdrew from the bout citing an elbow injury and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Uriah_Hall" title="Uriah Hall">Uriah Hall</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping was briefly linked to a fight with <a href="/wiki/Gegard_Mousasi" title="Gegard Mousasi">Gegard Mousasi</a> on 27 February 2016 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UFC_Fight_Night_84" title="UFC Fight Night 84">UFC Fight Night 84</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> However, on 24 December, Bisping was pulled from the bout in favor of a matchup with <a href="/wiki/Anderson_Silva" title="Anderson Silva">Anderson Silva</a> at the same event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> Bisping won the back and forth fight via unanimous decision (48–47, 48–47, and 48–47).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> Both participants were awarded <i>Fight of the Night</i> honors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="UFC_Middleweight_Champion">UFC Middleweight Champion</span></h4>
<p>With an injury to <a href="/wiki/Chris_Weidman" title="Chris Weidman">Chris Weidman</a> forcing him out of the competition, Bisping stepped up with only 17 days notice to face champion <a href="/wiki/Luke_Rockhold" title="Luke Rockhold">Luke Rockhold</a> for a second time on 4 June 2016 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_199" title="UFC 199">UFC 199</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> He won via knockout in the first round to become the UFC Middleweight Champion and the first British UFC champion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> He was awarded <i>Performance of the Night</i> honors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> The result was considered by many MMA media as one of the biggest upsets in the UFC title fight history, as Bisping took the fight on short notice and was submitted in their first encounter 18 months earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bisping faced <a href="/wiki/Dan_Henderson" title="Dan Henderson">Dan Henderson</a> in a rematch on 8 October 2016 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_204" title="UFC 204">UFC 204</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UFC204_88-0"><a href="#cite_note-UFC204-88">[88]</a></sup> Bisping won the fight via unanimous decision (48-47, 48-47, 49-46) and retained the UFC Middleweight championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup> Both fighters were awarded <i>Fight of the Night</i> for their performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span></h2>
<p>On 6 April 2016, Bisping announced that he had landed a role in the upcoming film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/XXx:_The_Return_of_Xander_Cage" title="XXx: The Return of Xander Cage">xXx: The Return of Xander Cage</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup> Later that month, Bisping revealed that he is a fan of the 1990s television series <i><a href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks" title="Twin Peaks">Twin Peaks</a></i> and that he had gained a small role in <a href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks_(2017_TV_series)" title="Twin Peaks (2017 TV series)">the upcoming continuation of the series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Bisping has three children with his Australian wife, Rebecca.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup> Their third child was born just before Bisping set off to the USA for final preparation in his fight against <a href="/wiki/Dan_Miller_(fighter)" title="Dan Miller (fighter)">Dan Miller</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup> Bisping has family around the globe, including in Germany, Ireland, the United Kingdom, and the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-11">[11]</a></sup> He resides in <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_California" title="Orange County, California">Orange County, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stupp1_53-1"><a href="#cite_note-stupp1-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
