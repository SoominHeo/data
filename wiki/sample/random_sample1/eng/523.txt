<p>The <b>2021 FIFA Confederations Cup</b> is scheduled to be the 11th edition of the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="FIFA Confederations Cup">FIFA Confederations Cup</a>, a quadrennial international men's <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> tournament organized by <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>. It is expected to be held in 2021, as a prelude to the <a href="/wiki/2022_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2022 FIFA World Cup">2022 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Host_selection">Host selection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2021_FIFA_Confederations_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Host selection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As is customary, the tournament was originally to be held in <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a> — host country of the <a href="/wiki/2022_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2022 FIFA World Cup">2022 FIFA World Cup</a>, as announced on 2 December 2010 after the country was <a href="/wiki/2018_and_2022_FIFA_World_Cup_bids" title="2018 and 2022 FIFA World Cup bids">awarded the hosting rights</a> of the 2022 World Cup. However, concerns arose surrounding Qatar's high temperatures during the summer period (which also led to calls for the World Cup to be moved from its traditional June/July scheduling to November/December). This is the first edition since the <a href="/wiki/2003_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2003 FIFA Confederations Cup">2003 FIFA Confederations Cup</a> that the tournament host is not hosting the next <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a> and the first edition immediately prior to a World Cup tournament that won't be held at the same host country.</p>
<p>On 25 February 2015, this resulted in FIFA officially announcing that it would move the 2021 Confederations Cup to another country of the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Football_Confederation" title="Asian Football Confederation">Asian Football Confederation</a>, so it could still be held during the traditional window of June/July 2021, without interrupting domestic leagues. As compensation, another FIFA tournament, potentially the 2021 <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Club_World_Cup" title="FIFA Club World Cup">FIFA Club World Cup</a>, would be held in Qatar in November/December 2021, as the test event for the 2022 World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbc-qatar2021moved_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbc-qatar2021moved-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fifa_task_force_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-fifa_task_force-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualified_teams">Qualified teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2021_FIFA_Confederations_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualified teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The eight competing teams will be the hosts, the reigning World Cup champions, and the six holders of the <a href="/wiki/Football_continental_championships" title="Football continental championships">FIFA confederation championships</a>. If any team qualifies for multiple berths (e.g. if the World Cup champions also won their continental championship), the next best-placed team from their continental championship qualifies.</p>
