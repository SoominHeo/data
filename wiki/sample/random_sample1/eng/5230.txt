<p><b>Michael Robert Socha</b> (born 13 December 1987)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is an English actor, known for his roles in the films <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_England" title="This Is England">This Is England</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_(2008_film)" title="Summer (2008 film)">Summer</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup> and the television series <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_England_%2786" title="This Is England '86">This Is England '86</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_England_%2788" title="This Is England '88">'88</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_England_%2790" title="This Is England '90">'90</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Human_(UK_TV_series)" title="Being Human (UK TV series)">Being Human</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Broke_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Broke-4">[4]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_in_Wonderland" title="Once Upon a Time in Wonderland">Once Upon a Time in Wonderland</a></i> and the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Three" title="BBC Three">BBC Three</a> miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Our_World_War_(TV_series)" title="Our World War (TV series)">Our World War</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Socha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Socha was born in <a href="/wiki/Derby" title="Derby">Derby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, to Kathleen Lyons (nicknamed "Kath") and Robert Socha, on 13 December 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OurFamily_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-OurFamily-5">[5]</a></sup> He is the older brother of actress <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Socha" title="Lauren Socha">Lauren Socha</a>, star of the <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/Misfits_(TV_series)" title="Misfits (TV series)">Misfits</a></i>. Michael was brought up in <a href="/wiki/Littleover" title="Littleover">Littleover</a>, a suburb of Derby,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup> and attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Benedict_Catholic_School_and_Performing_Arts_College" title="Saint Benedict Catholic School and Performing Arts College">St Benedict Catholic School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OurFamily_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-OurFamily-5">[5]</a></sup> Socha was a rebellious pupil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup> He often skipped school, but when forced to attend he tried to make it fun by doing the things he was most interested in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GayTimes_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-GayTimes-6">[6]</a></sup> At the age of 11 Michael unsuccessfully auditioned for the lead role in a school <a href="/wiki/Musical_theatre" title="Musical theatre">musical play</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williams_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Williams-7">[7]</a></sup> When Michael was 12, his mother read in a local newspaper about a play being cast by the <a href="/wiki/Chellaston" title="Chellaston">Chellaston</a> Youth Players. She asked her son and daughter if they wanted to try out for it, and both did.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YouFancy_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-YouFancy-8">[8]</a></sup> Michael's motivation for auditioning was his anger at being denied a role in the musical and his desire to prove his teachers wrong about his acting skills.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup> He won the lead role of <a href="/wiki/Bugsy_Malone" title="Bugsy Malone">Bugsy Malone</a> in the play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williams_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Williams-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TouchofGlamour_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-TouchofGlamour-9">[9]</a></sup> He acted in several plays for the group, but by his own admission did not take acting very seriously.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TouchofGlamour_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-TouchofGlamour-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Through teachers at Michael's school the family learned about film director Ian Smith's Central Television Junior Workshop,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williams_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Williams-7">[7]</a></sup> a free actors' workshop for young people from the <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a> area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OurFamily_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-OurFamily-5">[5]</a></sup> He enrolled in the course when he was 14,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup> but was almost refused admission as he had failed to learn the monologue for his audition and was put in the "reserve group". His second audition went the same way, but the programme relented and allowed him in after a time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup> Michael says the workshop had a strong impact on him, and he began working hard at acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TouchofGlamour_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-TouchofGlamour-9">[9]</a></sup> His first professional role came when he was cast in a small part in a short film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup> He was quickly cast in several small parts subsequently, and hired an agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TouchofGlamour_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-TouchofGlamour-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Socha's father had a long history of alcohol abuse, and his parents separated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2004, Robert Socha died of an alcohol-related heart attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OurFamily_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-OurFamily-5">[5]</a></sup> According to Kathleen,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup> the death of Michael's father made her son very independent. "Michael was walking the streets of London at 16 going to auditions. He had to because I was working. He'd go off with a map and Tube fare."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OurFamily_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-OurFamily-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Michael liked drama, English and history courses, however, and received his <a href="/wiki/General_Certificate_of_Secondary_Education" title="General Certificate of Secondary Education">GCSEs</a> in those three subjects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-10"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup> He credits his teacher, Mrs Urquhart-Hughes, as someone who watched over him, motivated him, and got him to do his homework after school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GayTimes_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-GayTimes-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>His first job after leaving school was in a manufacturing factory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GayTimes_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-GayTimes-6">[6]</a></sup> He disliked it and quit, and enrolled at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burton_College" title="Burton College">Burton College</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Further_education" title="Further education">further education</a> school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He worked at two other factories after leaving school, as well as at a <a href="/wiki/Car_wash" title="Car wash">car wash</a>. He also worked as a labourer for his uncle's bricklaying firm, mixing mortar for the bricklayers. But as he was constantly taking time off work at the last moment to attend auditions he quit working full-time and dedicated himself to acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GayTimes_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-GayTimes-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Socha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Socha's breakthrough role came early in his acting career. In 2006, he was cast as the bully Harvey in <a href="/wiki/Shane_Meadows" title="Shane Meadows">Shane Meadows</a><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> <a href="/wiki/Skinhead" title="Skinhead">skinhead</a> <a href="/wiki/Subculture" title="Subculture">subculture</a> film, <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_England" title="This Is England">This Is England</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TouchofGlamour_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-TouchofGlamour-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, he was cast in Kenneth Glenaan's <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Scotland" title="BAFTA Scotland">BAFTA Scotland</a>-award winning film <i><a href="/wiki/Summer_(2008_film)" title="Summer (2008 film)">Summer</a></i>, where he acted opposite <a href="/wiki/Robert_Carlyle" title="Robert Carlyle">Robert Carlyle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TouchofGlamour_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-TouchofGlamour-9">[9]</a></sup> The same year, he appeared in <a href="/wiki/Duane_Hopkins" title="Duane Hopkins">Duane Hopkins</a><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> film <i><a href="/wiki/Better_Things_(film)" title="Better Things (film)">Better Things</a></i> and the independent small-budget comedy <i>Dogging: A Love Story</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TouchofGlamour_9-6"><a href="#cite_note-TouchofGlamour-9">[9]</a></sup> He also appeared in three episodes of the <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> medical drama <i><a href="/wiki/Casualty_(TV_series)" title="Casualty (TV series)">Casualty</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TouchofGlamour_9-7"><a href="#cite_note-TouchofGlamour-9">[9]</a></sup> In February 2009, Socha made his stage debut at <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Playhouse" title="Nottingham Playhouse">Nottingham Playhouse</a> in <i>Glamour</i>, a comedy by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Lowe_(playwright)" title="Stephen Lowe (playwright)">Stephen Lowe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scott_3-11"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TouchofGlamour_9-8"><a href="#cite_note-TouchofGlamour-9">[9]</a></sup> The same year, he appeared in an episode of the science fiction <a href="/wiki/Police_procedural" title="Police procedural">police drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Paradox_(TV_series)" title="Paradox (TV series)">Paradox</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> and in the made-for-television film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Unloved" title="The Unloved">The Unloved</a></i> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Channel_Four" title="Channel Four">Channel Four</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WerewolfRole_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-WerewolfRole-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite his initial success, Socha was "penniless" and "waiting for work" for much of 2009 and early 2010, sitting at home and watching daytime television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Broke_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Broke-4">[4]</a></sup> His next big break came when Shane Meadows asked him to reprise the role of Harvey the bully for a four-part television series for Channel 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Broke_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Broke-4">[4]</a></sup> The series, <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_England_%2786" title="This Is England '86">This Is England '86</a></i>, follows the lives of the film's characters three years after the events originally depicted. It aired in September 2010. That same year, Socha appeared in an episode of the hit television comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Married_Single_Other" title="Married Single Other">Married Single Other</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Broke_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Broke-4">[4]</a></sup> as well as the film <i><a href="/wiki/Bonded_by_Blood_(film)" title="Bonded by Blood (film)">Bonded by Blood</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WerewolfRole_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-WerewolfRole-11">[11]</a></sup> He also appeared in the <i>This Is England</i> Christmas special, and in a music video with American <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul music</a> singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lauren_Pritchard_(singer)" title="Lauren Pritchard (singer)">Lauren Pritchard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WerewolfRole_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-WerewolfRole-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Socha appeared in <a href="/wiki/BBC_Three" title="BBC Three">BBC Three</a><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> hit supernatural series <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Human_(UK_TV_series)" title="Being Human (UK TV series)">Being Human</a></i> in 2011, receiving good critical notice as the innocent young <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a> Tom McNair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YouFancy_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-YouFancy-8">[8]</a></sup> He almost did not appear on the show. He missed his audition after a taxicab <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hit_and_run_(vehicular)" title="Hit and run (vehicular)">hit him and fled the scene</a> while he was helping a friend move furniture across a road in <a href="/wiki/Normanton,_Derby" title="Normanton, Derby">Normanton</a>. Although only bruised, he missed his audition and had to plead for a second chance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WerewolfRole_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-WerewolfRole-11">[11]</a></sup> He won the role of Tom, a young werewolf who has been brought up in the wild by his father and taught to hunt vampires.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GayTimes_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-GayTimes-6">[6]</a></sup> During his time on <i>Being Human</i>, Socha says he became good friends with <a href="/wiki/Russell_Tovey" title="Russell Tovey">Russell Tovey</a> and co-star <a href="/wiki/Robson_Green" title="Robson Green">Robson Green</a>. Socha has admitted that he can get carried away while performing some of his more physically demanding scenes. On another occasion, acting out a werewolf transformation in a metal cage, he almost injured himself after his physical acting became too extreme. He has received at least seven minor injuries requiring medical treatment and reporting to authorities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WerewolfRole_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-WerewolfRole-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Socha was upgraded from a recurring role to the main cast for the fourth series of <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Human_(UK_TV_series)" title="Being Human (UK TV series)">Being Human</a></i>, after the previous main cast, including Tovey, left the show. The fourth series started airing in February 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He also appeared in the music video to the <a href="/wiki/Jake_Bugg" title="Jake Bugg">Jake Bugg</a> song "Seen It All" and in the music video to the Lauren Pritchard song "Stuck".</p>
<p>In 2013, Socha portrayed the <a href="/wiki/Knave_of_Hearts_(Alice%27s_Adventures_in_Wonderland)" title="Knave of Hearts (Alice's Adventures in Wonderland)">The Knave of Hearts</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_in_Wonderland" title="Once Upon a Time in Wonderland">Once Upon a Time in Wonderland</a></i>, a spin-off to the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> fantasy series <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_(TV_series)" title="Once Upon a Time (TV series)">Once Upon a Time</a></i>. He also starred in Chris Coghill's film <i>Spike Island</i>, based upon The Stone Roses gig of the same name. After <i>Once Upon a Time in Wonderland'</i>s cancellation, it was confirmed that Socha would be a main cast member in the fourth season of <i>Once Upon A Time</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SochaSeason4_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-SochaSeason4-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>He also starred in the film <i>Svengali</i> in which he played a guitarist in a band and in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Twenty8k" title="Twenty8k">Twenty8k</a></i> in which he played a drug dealer.</p>
<p>2016 saw Michael take the lead in the E4 series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Aliens_(TV_series)" title="The Aliens (TV series)">The Aliens</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and he has starred in two films currently in production (<i>Double Date</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-15">[15]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Papillon_(2017_film)" title="Papillon (2017 film)">a remake</a> of the 1973 film <i><a href="/wiki/Papillon_(film)" title="Papillon (film)">Papillon</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-16">[16]</a></sup>) with 2017 release dates to be confirmed.</p>
