<p><b>Michael Finlay Parkhurst</b> (born January 24, 1984) is an American soccer <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> who currently plays for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta_United" title="Atlanta United">Atlanta United</a> in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a>. The 2005 <a href="/wiki/MLS_Rookie_of_the_Year_Award" title="MLS Rookie of the Year Award">MLS Rookie of the Year</a> and 2007 <a href="/wiki/MLS_Defender_of_the_Year_Award" title="MLS Defender of the Year Award">MLS Defender of the Year Award</a>, he is capable of playing both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centre_back" title="Centre back">center back</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Full_back_(association_football)" title="Full back (association football)">right back</a>. Parkhurst has played for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States men's national team</a>, including being a member of two <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="CONCACAF Gold Cup">CONCACAF Gold Cup</a> squads and participating in the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2008 Summer Olympics</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_and_college">Youth and college</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Parkhurst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Youth and college">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a>, Parkhurst attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IMG_Academies" title="IMG Academies">Bradenton Soccer Academy</a> in Florida.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He played <a href="/wiki/College_soccer" title="College soccer">college soccer</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wake_Forest_University" title="Wake Forest University">Wake Forest University</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Wake_Forest_Demon_Deacons" title="Wake Forest Demon Deacons">Demon Deacons</a> from 2002 to 2004, where he started all sixty-two games the team played during his tenure, scoring one goal and registering two assists. Parkhurst was named a second-team All-American as a sophomore and junior and graduated from the university with a degree in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He then signed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Generation_adidas" title="Generation adidas">Generation adidas</a> contract with MLS following the 2004 season.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Parkhurst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_England_Revolution">New England Revolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Parkhurst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: New England Revolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Parkhurst, who grew up in Rhode Island and played for CLCF Soccer and Bayside United, was in attendance at the inaugural home game for the <a href="/wiki/New_England_Revolution" title="New England Revolution">New England Revolution</a>, in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-futureisnow_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-futureisnow-3">[3]</a></sup> Nine years later, on January 14, 2005, he was selected ninth overall in the <a href="/wiki/2005_MLS_SuperDraft" title="2005 MLS SuperDraft">2005 MLS SuperDraft</a> by his hometown Revolution. He was ever-present for the Revs in his first season and was named <a href="/wiki/MLS_Rookie_of_the_Year_Award" title="MLS Rookie of the Year Award">MLS Rookie of the Year</a>.</p>
<p>He scored his first career MLS goal on October 20, 2007, in a 2–2 draw between the Revolution and <a href="/wiki/Toronto_FC" title="Toronto FC">Toronto FC</a>. The goal was scored from sixty yards out, having caught Toronto's keeper <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Stamatopoulos" title="Kenny Stamatopoulos">Kenny Stamatopoulos</a> off-guard.</p>
<p>Parkhurst won the <a href="/wiki/MLS_Defender_of_the_Year_Award" title="MLS Defender of the Year Award">MLS Defender of the Year Award</a> in 2007, the <a href="/wiki/MLS_Fair_Play_Award" title="MLS Fair Play Award">MLS Fair Play Award</a> in 2007 and 2008 and was also twice named the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MLS_Humanitarian_of_the_Year" title="MLS Humanitarian of the Year">MLS Humanitarian of the Year</a> in 2006 and 2008.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="F.C._Nordsj.C3.A6lland">F.C. Nordsjælland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Parkhurst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: F.C. Nordsjælland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After his contract with the Revolution expired after the 2008 season, Parkhurst signed a three-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Danish_Superliga" title="Danish Superliga">Danish Superliga</a> side <a href="/wiki/FC_Nordsj%C3%A6lland" title="FC Nordsjælland">Nordsjælland</a> on December 9, 2008, as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_agent_(football)" title="Free agent (football)">free agent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> March 1, 2009, Parkhurst made his FCN league debut in a 1–1 away draw against <a href="/wiki/Vejle_Boldklub" title="Vejle Boldklub">Vejle</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Danish_Superliga" title="2009–10 Danish Superliga">2009–10</a> season, with the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Andreas_Bjelland" title="Andreas Bjelland">Andreas Bjelland</a> and injury to <a href="/wiki/Henrik_Kildentoft" title="Henrik Kildentoft">Henrik Kildentoft</a>, Parkhurst was inserted into a variety of new positions, including <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">full back</a> and <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">centre midfielder</a>, becoming adept at playing at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rightback" title="Rightback">right back</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Parkhurst would help the Wild Tigers to win the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Danish_Cup" title="2009–10 Danish Cup">Danish Cup</a>, the club's first silverware in their history, and his first trophy in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>.</p>
<p>The following season, Parkhurst was hampered by numerous injury set backs throughout the campaign, eventually losing his place to <a href="/wiki/Jores_Okore" title="Jores Okore">Jores Okore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He would make his return in time for Nordsjælland's <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Danish_Cup" title="2010–11 Danish Cup">Danish Cup</a> final, winning the cup with a 3–2 victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Midtjylland" title="FC Midtjylland">F.C. Midtjylland</a>, lifting the trophy for the second consecutive year.</p>
<p>With less than a year remaining on his contract, there was speculation on Michael Parkhurst's future at FCN at the start of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Danish_Superliga" title="2011–12 Danish Superliga">2011–12</a> season, with the emergence of Bjelland and Okore's partnership in <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre_back" title="Defender (association football)">central defense</a>, Parkhurst was forced to play out of position, often at right back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Helping the club to their best ever start to a season, ending the winter break in December 2011 at a club record second in the league, Parkhurst put rumors to rest when he signed a one-year contract extension with F.C. Nordsjælland, despite financially better offers from other clubs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Michael Parkhust later this season helped F.C. Nordsjælland win the Danish Superliga for the first time in club history, securing the team automatic qualification for 2012–13 season's Champions League group stage.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Augsburg">Augsburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Parkhurst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Augsburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 19, 2012, it was announced that Parkhurst joined <a href="/wiki/FC_Augsburg" title="FC Augsburg">FC Augsburg</a> on a free transfer. The struggling <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> side finished 15th for the 2012–13 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He played his first competitive match in Germany on February 10, 2013 – a 1–1 draw at home against <a href="/wiki/1._FSV_Mainz_05" title="1. FSV Mainz 05">1. FSV Mainz 05</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Columbus_Crew">Columbus Crew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Parkhurst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Columbus Crew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The MLS rights to Parkhurst were traded by New England to <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Crew_SC" title="Columbus Crew SC">Columbus Crew SC</a> on January 12, 2014 in exchange for the Crew’s first-round selection in the <a href="/wiki/2014_MLS_SuperDraft" title="2014 MLS SuperDraft">2014 MLS SuperDraft</a> and allocation money. Parkhurst made 33 appearances in his first season with Columbus and was named captain of the club. In summer 2014, Parkhurst became the first Columbus player in four years named to the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer_All-Star_Game" title="Major League Soccer All-Star Game">MLS All-Star Team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlanta_United_FC">Atlanta United FC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Parkhurst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Atlanta United FC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 11, Parkhurst was traded to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_United_FC" title="Atlanta United FC">Atlanta United FC</a> in exchange for General Allocation Money.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Parkhurst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Parkhurst is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish-American" title="Irish-American">Irish-American</a> and thus holds an <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Irish</a> passport. He had declared that he would be willing to play for the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Irish national team</a> if his American ambitions never came to fruition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-futureisnow_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-futureisnow-3">[3]</a></sup> Parkhurst was called up by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">United States national team</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2007_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="2007 CONCACAF Gold Cup">2007 Gold Cup</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF_Gold_Cup" title="CONCACAF Gold Cup">2013 Gold Cup</a>. He earned his first cap against <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_national_football_team" title="Trinidad and Tobago national football team">Trinidad and Tobago</a> on June 9, 2007, thus eliminating any further possibility of playing for the Republic of Ireland. He's been included in many friendlies for the US under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jurgen_Klinsmann" title="Jurgen Klinsmann">Jurgen Klinsmann</a>. He was included on Klinsmann's 30-man preliminary roster for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
