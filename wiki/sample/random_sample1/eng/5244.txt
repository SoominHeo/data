<p><b>Milo Parker</b> is a British actor, known for his roles as Connor in <i><a href="/wiki/Robot_Overlords" title="Robot Overlords">Robot Overlords</a></i>, Roger Munro in <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Holmes" title="Mr. Holmes">Mr. Holmes</a></i> and Hugh Apiston in <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Peregrine%27s_Home_for_Peculiar_Children_(film)" title="Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children (film)">Miss Peregrine's Home for Peculiar Children</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_work">Life and work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milo_Parker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Parker trained at Youngblood Theatre Company and made his feature film debut in the British independent science fiction film <i><a href="/wiki/Robot_Overlords" title="Robot Overlords">Robot Overlords</a></i>, before starring alongside <a href="/wiki/Ian_McKellen" title="Ian McKellen">Ian McKellen</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Holmes" title="Mr. Holmes">Mr. Holmes</a></i>, for which he earned a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Movie_Award_for_Best_Young_Performer" title="Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Young Performer">Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Young Performer</a> and <a href="/wiki/42nd_Saturn_Awards" title="42nd Saturn Awards">42nd Saturn Awards</a> for <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award_for_Best_Performance_by_a_Younger_Actor" title="Saturn Award for Best Performance by a Younger Actor">Best Performance by a Younger Actor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-holmes_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-holmes-1">[1]</a></sup> In an interview for <a href="/wiki/The_Lady_(magazine)" title="The Lady (magazine)"><i>The Lady</i></a>, McKellen praised his young co-star, saying "Milo was full of the esprit of a young person. He had no fear of the camera or of doing exactly what the director wanted when required." He attends <a href="/wiki/Farlingaye_High_School" title="Farlingaye High School">Farlingaye High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Woodbridge,_Suffolk" title="Woodbridge, Suffolk">Woodbridge, Suffolk</a>, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
