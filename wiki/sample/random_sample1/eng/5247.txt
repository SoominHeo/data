<p><b>Mile End</b> is a district in <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East London</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, 3.6 miles (5.8 km) east-northeast of <a href="/wiki/Charing_Cross" title="Charing Cross">Charing Cross</a>. On the London to <a href="/wiki/Colchester" title="Colchester">Colchester</a> road, it was one of the earliest suburbs of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_London" title="City of London">City of London</a> and became part of the metropolitan area of London in 1855. In 2011, Mile End had a population of 28,544.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mile_End&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toponymy">Toponymy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mile_End&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Toponymy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mile End is recorded in 1288 as <i>La Mile ende</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mills_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mills-2">[2]</a></sup> It is formed from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a> 'mile' and 'ende' and means 'the hamlet a mile away'. The mile distance was in relation to <a href="/wiki/Aldgate" title="Aldgate">Aldgate</a> in the <a href="/wiki/City_of_London" title="City of London">City of London</a>, reached by the London to Colchester road.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mills_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mills-2">[2]</a></sup> In around 1691 Mile End became known as Mile End Old Town because a new unconnected settlement to the west and adjacent to <a href="/wiki/Spitalfields" title="Spitalfields">Spitalfields</a> had taken the name <a href="/wiki/Mile_End_New_Town" title="Mile End New Town">Mile End New Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mills_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-mills-2">[2]</a></sup> This data combines the ethnicity data for Mile End's two wards (Mile End And Globe Town and Mile End East). <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginnings">Beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mile_End&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Whilst there are many references to settlements in the area, excavations have suggested there were very few buildings before 1300.</p>
<p>Mile End Road is an ancient route from London to the East, and was moved to its present-day alignment after the foundation of <a href="/wiki/Bow,_London#Bridges_at_Bowe" title="Bow, London">Bow Bridge</a> in 1110. In the medieval period it was known as ‘Aldgatestrete’, as it led to the eastern entrance to the City of London at <a href="/wiki/Aldgate" title="Aldgate">Aldgate</a>. The area running alongside Mile End Road was known as Mile End Green, and became known as a place of assembly for Londoners, reflected in the name of Assembly Passage. For most of the medieval period, this road was surrounded by open fields on either side, but speculative developments existed by the end of the 16th century and continued throughout the 18th century.</p>
<p>The Stepney Green Conservation Area was designated in January 1973, covering the area previously known as Mile End Old Town. It is a large Conservation Area with an irregular shape that encloses buildings around Mile End Road, Assembly Passage, Louisa Street and Stepney Green itself. It is an area of exceptional architectural and historic interest, with a character and appearance worthy of protection and enhancement. It is situated just north of the medieval village of Stepney, which was clustered around St. Dunstan’s Church.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Peasants.27_Revolt">Peasants' Revolt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mile_End&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Peasants' Revolt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Peasants%27_Revolt" title="Peasants' Revolt">Peasants' Revolt</a></div>
<p>In 1381, an uprising against the tax collectors of <a href="/wiki/Brentwood,_Essex" title="Brentwood, Essex">Brentwood</a> quickly spread first to the surrounding villages, then throughout the South-East of England, but it was the rebels of <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>, led by a priest named <a href="/wiki/Jack_Straw_(rebel_leader)" title="Jack Straw (rebel leader)">Jack Straw</a>, and the men of <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Wat_Tyler" title="Wat Tyler">Wat Tyler</a>, who marched on London. On 12 June, the Essex rebels, comprising 100,000 men, camped at Mile End and on the following day the men of Kent arrived at <a href="/wiki/Blackheath,_London" title="Blackheath, London">Blackheath</a>. On 14 June, the young king <a href="/wiki/Richard_II_of_England" title="Richard II of England">Richard II</a> rode to Mile End where he met the rebels and signed their charter. The king subsequently had the leaders and many rebels executed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Birth_of_London.27s_Yiddish_theatre">Birth of London's Yiddish theatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mile_End&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Birth of London's Yiddish theatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1883, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jacob_P._Adler" title="Jacob P. Adler">Jacob P. Adler</a> arrived in London with a troupe of refugee professional actors. He enlisted the help of local amateurs, and the <i>Russian Jewish Operatic Company</i> made their debut at the Beaumont Hall, close to <a href="/wiki/Stepney_Green_tube_station" title="Stepney Green tube station">Stepney Green tube station</a>. Within two years they were able to establish their own theatre in <a href="/wiki/Brick_Lane" title="Brick Lane">Brick Lane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="People.27s_Palace">People's Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mile_End&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: People's Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Novelist and social commentator <a href="/wiki/Walter_Besant" title="Walter Besant">Walter Besant</a> proposed a <i>Palace of Delight</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> with concert halls, reading rooms, picture galleries, an art school and various classes, social rooms and frequent fêtes and dances. This coincided with a project by the philanthropist businessman Edmund Hay Currie to use the money from the winding up of the <i>Beaumont Trust</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> together with subscriptions, to build a <i>People's Palace</i> in the East End. Five acres of land were secured on the Mile End Road, and the <i>Queen's Hall</i> was opened by <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> on 14 May 1887. The complex was completed with a library, swimming pool, gymnasium and winter garden by 1892, providing an eclectic mix of populist entertainment and education. A peak of 8,000 tickets were sold for classes in 1892, and by 1900, a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">Bachelor of Science</a> degree awarded by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a> was introduced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1931, the building was destroyed by fire, but the <a href="/wiki/Worshipful_Company_of_Drapers" title="Worshipful Company of Drapers">Draper's Company</a>, major donors to the original scheme, invested more to rebuild the technical college and create <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Mary,_University_of_London" title="Queen Mary, University of London">Queen Mary College</a> in December 1934.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> A new <i>People's Palace</i> was constructed, in 1937, by the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Stepney" title="Metropolitan Borough of Stepney">Metropolitan Borough of Stepney</a>, in St Helen's Terrace. This finally closed in 1954.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_World_War">Second World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mile_End&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Second World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Besides suffering heavily in earlier blitzes, Mile End was hit by the first <a href="/wiki/V-1_flying_bomb" title="V-1 flying bomb">V-1 flying bomb</a> to strike London. On 13 June 1944, this 'doodlebug' impacted next to the railway bridge on Grove Road, an event now commemorated by a plaque. Eight civilians were killed, 30 injured and 200 made homeless by the blast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The area remained derelict for many years, until cleared to extend Mile End park. Before demolition in 1993, local artist <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Whiteread" title="Rachel Whiteread">Rachel Whiteread</a> made a cast of the inside of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/193_Grove_Road" title="193 Grove Road">193 Grove Road</a>. Despite attracting controversy, the exhibit won her the <a href="/wiki/Turner_Prize" title="Turner Prize">Turner Prize</a> for 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2007 during building work, a live <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> bomb weighing 200 kg was found north of Mile End station near Grove and Roman Roads. Approximately 100 local residents were evacuated and stayed with friends and family or the Mile End Leisure Centre until the bomb could be deactivated and removed.</p>

<p>Mile End formed a hamlet within the large ancient parish of <a href="/wiki/Stepney_(parish)" title="Stepney (parish)">Stepney</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Tower_division" title="Tower division">Tower division</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ossulstone" title="Ossulstone">Ossulstone</a> hundred of <a href="/wiki/Middlesex" title="Middlesex">Middlesex</a>. It was grouped into the Stepney <a href="/wiki/Poor_law_union" title="Poor law union">poor law union</a> in 1836, becoming a single parish for poor law purposes in 1857. It formed part of the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Police_District" title="Metropolitan Police District">Metropolitan Police District</a> from 1830. Upon the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Board_of_Works" title="Metropolitan Board of Works">Metropolitan Board of Works</a> in 1855 the vestry of Mile End Old Town became an electing authority. The vestry hall was located on Bancroft Road. The parish became part of the <a href="/wiki/County_of_London" title="County of London">County of London</a> in 1889 and in 1900 it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Stepney" title="Metropolitan Borough of Stepney">Metropolitan Borough of Stepney</a>. In 1965 Stepney was incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Tower_Hamlets" title="London Borough of Tower Hamlets">London Borough of Tower Hamlets</a>. There was a <a href="/wiki/Mile_End_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Mile End (UK Parliament constituency)">Mile End Parliament constituency</a> from 1885 to 1950 which was notable for being one of two constituencies in the UK to elect a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist_Party" title="Communist Party">Communist Party</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">MP</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mile_End&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Under the <a href="/wiki/Metropolis_Management_Act_1855" title="Metropolis Management Act 1855">Metropolis Management Act 1855</a> any parish that exceeded 2,000 <a href="/wiki/Rates_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Rates in the United Kingdom">ratepayers</a> was to be divided into wards; as such the incorporated vestry of the Hamlet of Mile End Old Town within the parish of St Dunstan Stepney was divided into five wards (electing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vestrymen" title="Vestrymen">vestrymen</a>): No. 1 or North Ward (15), No. 2 or East Ward (12), No. 3 or West Ward (18), No. 4 or Centre Ward (21) and No. 5 or South Ward (18). <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1894 as its population had increased the incorporated vestry was re-divided into eight wards (electing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vestrymen" title="Vestrymen">vestrymen</a>): No. 1 (9), No. 2 (9), No. 3 (12), No. 4 (12), No. 5 (12), No. 6 (9), No. 7 (12) and No. 8 (15).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mile_End&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mile End is in a part of London known as the <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End</a> and home to the main campus of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Mary,_University_of_London" title="Queen Mary, University of London">Queen Mary, University of London</a>. Parts of <a href="/wiki/Barts_and_The_London_School_of_Medicine_and_Dentistry" title="Barts and The London School of Medicine and Dentistry">Barts and The London School of Medicine and Dentistry</a> are also based on this campus. The main student halls of residence are also now located on this campus.</p>
<p>The area also boasts an unusual landmark, the "Green Bridge". This structure (designed by CZWG Architects, and opened in 2000) allows <a href="/wiki/Mile_End_Park" title="Mile End Park">Mile End Park</a> to cross over the Mile End Road and makes an interesting contrast with the more usual approach of building bridges for cars. It contains garden and water features and some shops and restaurant space built in below.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ragged_School_Museum" title="Ragged School Museum">Ragged School Museum</a>, opened in 1990 in three canal side former warehouses in Copperfield Road, facing the western edge of the park, south of Mile End Road. The buildings previously housed <a href="/wiki/Thomas_John_Barnardo" title="Thomas John Barnardo">Dr Barnado</a>'s Cooperfield Road <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ragged_School" title="Ragged School">Ragged School</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mile_End_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Mile End (UK Parliament constituency)">Mile End</a> as a parliamentary constituency had a reputation as a <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a> stronghold, but also sent <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Great_Britain" title="Communist Party of Great Britain">Communist</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> (MP) <a href="/wiki/Phil_Piratin" title="Phil Piratin">Phil Piratin</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_House_of_Commons" title="British House of Commons">House of Commons</a> between 1945 and 1950. At that time, it had a large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> population. The area now is covered by the <a href="/wiki/Bethnal_Green_and_Bow_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Bethnal Green and Bow (UK Parliament constituency)">Bethnal Green and Bow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poplar_and_Limehouse_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Poplar and Limehouse (UK Parliament constituency)">Poplar and Limehouse</a> constituencies.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport_and_leisure">Sport and leisure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mile_End&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sport and leisure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mile End has a <a href="/wiki/Non-League_football" title="Non-League football">Non-League football</a> club <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Bengal_United_F.C." title="Sporting Bengal United F.C.">Sporting Bengal United F.C.</a> who play at <a href="/wiki/Mile_End_Stadium" title="Mile End Stadium">Mile End Stadium</a>.</p>

<p>The neighbourhood was immortalised (humorously but unfavourably) in the pop band <a href="/wiki/Pulp_(band)" title="Pulp (band)">Pulp</a>'s song, "Mile End," which was featured on the <i><a href="/wiki/Trainspotting_(film)" title="Trainspotting (film)">Trainspotting</a></i> soundtrack. The song describes a group of squatters taking up residence in an abandoned 15th-floor apartment in a run-down apartment tower.</p>
<p>A man pushed in front of a train at Mile End underground station recently attracted media attention and has resulted in an acclaimed play, called "Mile End," devised and performed by <a href="/wiki/Analogue_(theatre_company)" title="Analogue (theatre company)">Analogue</a> Theatre.</p>
<p>In 2009, the music video for "<a href="/wiki/Confusion_Girl" title="Confusion Girl">Confusion Girl</a>" by electropop musician <a href="/wiki/Frankmusik" title="Frankmusik">Frankmusik</a> was filmed in Mile End Park and Clinton Road, Mile End.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, the music video for "<a href="/wiki/Heart_Skips_a_Beat" title="Heart Skips a Beat">Heart Skips a Beat</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Olly_Murs" title="Olly Murs">Olly Murs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rizzle_Kicks" title="Rizzle Kicks">Rizzle Kicks</a> was filmed in Mile End's skate park.</p>
<p>The artistic director of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics_opening_ceremony" title="2012 Summer Olympics opening ceremony">2012 Summer Olympics opening ceremony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Boyle" title="Danny Boyle">Danny Boyle</a>, lives in Mile End. Boyle is also the director of <a href="/wiki/Trainspotting_(film)" title="Trainspotting (film)">Trainspotting</a>.</p>

<p>The nearest <a href="/wiki/List_of_London_Underground_stations" title="List of London Underground stations">London Underground station</a> is <a href="/wiki/Mile_End_tube_station" title="Mile End tube station">Mile End</a> on the <a href="/wiki/District_line" title="District line">District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hammersmith_%26_City_line" title="Hammersmith &amp; City line">Hammersmith &amp; City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_line_(London_Underground)" title="Central line (London Underground)">Central</a> lines.</p>
<p>There are major bus interchanges at Mile End Gate – for buses north towards Hackney; and near Mile End tube station – for buses north towards Hackney and south towards <a href="/wiki/Canary_Wharf" title="Canary Wharf">Canary Wharf</a>.</p>
