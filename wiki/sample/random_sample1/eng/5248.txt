<p><b>Miles Prower</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">マイルス・パウアー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mairusu Pauā</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, more commonly known by his <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nickname</a> <b>Tails</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">テイルス</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Teirusu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">fictional character</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sega" title="Sega">Sega</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(series)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (series)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i> series. He is a significant main character after the title character Sonic, who is also his best friend. Tails also appears in his own spin-off series in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comic_books" title="Comic books">comic books</a>, <a href="/wiki/Animated_cartoon" title="Animated cartoon">cartoons</a>, as well as in <a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(OVA)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (OVA)">an OVA</a>.</p>
<p>The name "Miles Prower" is a <a href="/wiki/Pun" title="Pun">pun</a> on "<a href="/wiki/Miles_per_hour" title="Miles per hour">miles per hour</a>", a reference to the famed speed of <a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(character)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (character)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a>. He is an 8-year-old<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fox" title="Fox">fox</a> with 2 tails,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trophy_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-trophy-2">[2]</a></sup> hence the nickname. He is known to be Sonic's <a href="/wiki/Sidekick" title="Sidekick">sidekick</a> and adopted younger brother as well as Sonic's best friend<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trophy_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-trophy-2">[2]</a></sup> as well as a <a href="/wiki/Mechanic" title="Mechanic">mechanical</a> <a href="/wiki/Genius" title="Genius">genius</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trophy_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-trophy-2">[2]</a></sup> He is able to use his two tails to propel himself into the air like a <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> for a limited time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trophy_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-trophy-2">[2]</a></sup> He debuted on October 16, 1992, with the release of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_2_(8-bit)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog 2 (8-bit)">8-bit version of <i>Sonic the Hedgehog 2</i></a>; the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_2_(16-bit)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog 2 (16-bit)">16-bit version</a> was released that November.</p>
<p>When he was first introduced in <i>Sonic the Hedgehog 2</i>, Tails's fur was orange. The color was changed to yellow-orange for <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Adventure" title="Sonic Adventure">Sonic Adventure</a></i>, and later to light yellow for <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Heroes" title="Sonic Heroes">Sonic Heroes</a></i>. In 1993 and 1995 he starred in his own games: <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tails_and_the_Music_Maker" title="Tails and the Music Maker">Tails and the Music Maker</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/Sega_Pico" title="Sega Pico">Pico</a>; <i><a href="/wiki/Tails_Adventure" title="Tails Adventure">Tails Adventure</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Tails%27_Skypatrol" title="Tails' Skypatrol">Tails' Skypatrol</a></i> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sega_Game_Gear" title="Sega Game Gear">Game Gear</a>. Tails is the third most popular character of the series, behind <a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(character)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (character)">Sonic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shadow_the_Hedgehog" title="Shadow the Hedgehog">Shadow</a>, according to an official poll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the video games, Tails was voiced by young males from 1998 to 2004. By 2005, old females have since taken over being the voice of the character.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept_and_creation">Concept and creation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept and creation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Yasushi_Yamaguchi" title="Yasushi Yamaguchi">Yasushi Yamaguchi</a>, originally the main artist and zone designer for <a href="/wiki/Sega" title="Sega">Sega</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sonic_Team" title="Sonic Team">Sonic Team</a>, designed Tails for an internal competition for a <a href="/wiki/Sidekick" title="Sidekick">sidekick</a> to the speedy hedgehog. The character was meant to hold a "deep admiration for Sonic."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Naka_interview_96_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Naka_interview_96-4">[4]</a></sup> His entry won, but Sonic Team decided to change his character's name from "Miles" to "Tails".</p>
<p>Yamaguchi ended up with a compromise, using "Miles" in as the character's real first name; "Tails" would be his nickname, giving the character the full name of Miles "Tails" Prower. The name "Miles Prower" is a pun on the phrase "miles per hour".</p>
<p>The character debuted as Sonic's tag-along partner in the franchise's second game, and has remained an important player since. However the character's uniqueness was not established until <i>Sonic the Hedgehog 3</i> when players were given the power to control his flying (although the AI would make Tails fly when he would get off-screen).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_smash_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN_smash-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tails was redesigned (along with all of the other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_from_Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(games)" title="List of characters from Sonic the Hedgehog (games)">Sonic characters</a>) by Yuji Uekawa for <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Adventure" title="Sonic Adventure">Sonic Adventure</a></i>. His fur changed color to a more yellow shade as opposed to the orange in his early appearances, and he gained blue <a href="/wiki/Iris_(anatomy)" title="Iris (anatomy)">irises</a>.</p>
<p>In the American <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(comic_book)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (comic book)">Sonic</a></i> comic book, along with the <i><a href="/wiki/Adventures_of_Sonic_the_Hedgehog" title="Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog">Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(TV_series)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (TV series)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i> cartoons, he was originally brown, but the comic artists and cartoon artists eventually decided to change this to match his in-game appearance.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The character of Tails is portrayed as a very sweet-natured<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tails_Sonic_Adventure_Profile_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tails_Sonic_Adventure_Profile-6">[6]</a></sup> and humble fox<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sonic_Heroes_profile_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sonic_Heroes_profile-7">[7]</a></sup> who, before he met Sonic, was often picked on because of his twin tails.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-profile-1">[1]</a></sup> As a long time friend,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tails_Sonic_Adventure_Profile_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tails_Sonic_Adventure_Profile-6">[6]</a></sup> he admires Sonic<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sonic_Adventure_2_profile_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sonic_Adventure_2_profile-8">[8]</a></sup> and dreams of being just like him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sonic_Advance_2_profile_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sonic_Advance_2_profile-9">[9]</a></sup> Though he lacks courage,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-profile-1">[1]</a></sup> he wants to prove he can be counted on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sonic_Central_profile_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sonic_Central_profile-10">[10]</a></sup> He loves <a href="/wiki/Mint_(candy)" title="Mint (candy)">mint candy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sonic_Jam_profile_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sonic_Jam_profile-11">[11]</a></sup> tinkering with machines, and flying mechas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-profile-1">[1]</a></sup> Tails has <a href="/wiki/Astraphobia" title="Astraphobia">a fear of lightning</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sonic_Jam_profile_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sonic_Jam_profile-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tails has been described as a mechanical prodigy who rivals Dr. Eggman but he has not yet realized his full potential.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sonic_Adventure_2_profile_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sonic_Adventure_2_profile-8">[8]</a></sup> Additionally, by spinning his two tails like a <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> rotor, he has the unique ability to push himself through the air<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tails_Sonic_Adventure_Profile_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Tails_Sonic_Adventure_Profile-6">[6]</a></sup> to catch up with Sonic. However, when he takes to flight he tires quickly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tails_Sonic_Adventure_Profile_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Tails_Sonic_Adventure_Profile-6">[6]</a></sup> Unlike Sonic, Tails has the ability to swim. Like Sonic, he can transform into Super Tails, but he requires Super Emeralds to transform. This was seen first as what seemed a one-time appearance in <i>Sonic 3 &amp; Knuckles</i> but then again in <i>Sonic Heroes</i> where alongside Team Sonic members Sonic and Knuckles, would transform themselves into their 'super' forms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tails_Sonic_Advance_1_Actions_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tails_Sonic_Advance_1_Actions-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<p>Tails debuted in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_2_(8-bit)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog 2 (8-bit)"><i>Sonic 2</i> for the Sega Master System</a> before being made playable in its <a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_2" title="Sonic the Hedgehog 2">Genesis version</a>, playing the part of Sonic's sidekick. He was a playable character from the second controller and could be chosen as player one for the main game. <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Chaos" title="Sonic Chaos">Sonic Chaos</a></i> (1993), on the Game Gear/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sega_Master_System" title="Sega Master System">Master System</a> was the second game where the player could control Tails and the first time the player could control his flight.</p>
<p>Tails made his third major appearance in the 1994 game, <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_3" title="Sonic the Hedgehog 3">Sonic 3 (&amp; Knuckles)</a></i>, with the ability to pick up Sonic and use his tails to fly him to other areas, rotating his tails like a helicopter. He also gained the ability to swim under water, something Sonic has never been able to do.</p>
<p>Tails has also starred in games without Sonic, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Tails%27_Skypatrol" title="Tails' Skypatrol">Tails' Skypatrol</a></i>, which is a side-scrolling score attack like game for the Game Gear released exclusively in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. This was followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Tails_Adventure" title="Tails Adventure">Tails Adventures</a></i> later the same year, which is a <i><a href="/wiki/Metroid" title="Metroid">Metroid</a></i>-esque platformer with <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_game" title="Role-playing game">RPG</a> elements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Tails is also the star of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tails_and_the_Music_Maker" title="Tails and the Music Maker">Tails and the Music Maker</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/Sega_Pico" title="Sega Pico">Sega Pico</a>.</p>
<p>In later games, Tails had roles that require unique modes of play including <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Adventure" title="Sonic Adventure">Sonic Adventure</a></i>, where he appears as one of the six playable characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> His gameplay is based around standard platforming stages, but the goal of each stage is to get to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chaos_Emeralds" title="Chaos Emeralds">Chaos Emerald</a> before Sonic, or to the missile dud in the final race against Eggman. In <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Adventure_2" title="Sonic Adventure 2">Sonic Adventure 2</a></i>, he is featured in <a href="/wiki/Third-person_shooter" title="Third-person shooter">third-person shooting</a> segments, seated in his "Cyclone" mech.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> These stages, along with Dr. Eggman's shooting levels in the same game, were very similar to the E-102 Gamma levels of <i>Sonic Adventure</i>. In <i>Sonic Adventure</i>, he was given a theme song "Believe In Myself", of which another version appeared in <i>Sonic Adventure 2</i>.</p>
<p>Tails also appeared either as a playable character or in a supporting role in many later <i>Sonic</i> titles and still often resumes his role flying other characters around, such as in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Heroes" title="Sonic Heroes">Sonic Heroes</a></i>, where Tails appears on <a href="/wiki/Sonic_Heroes#Team_Sonic" title="Sonic Heroes">Team Sonic</a> as their flight-type character, being capable of carrying both Sonic and <a href="/wiki/Knuckles_the_Echidna" title="Knuckles the Echidna">Knuckles the Echidna</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He also appears as an 'amigo' character in the 2006 <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(2006_video_game)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (2006 video game)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i> game.</p>
<p>Tails is seen in the background of Green Hill Zone along with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silver_the_Hedgehog" title="Silver the Hedgehog">Silver</a> and Knuckles in <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Brawl" title="Super Smash Bros. Brawl">Super Smash Bros. Brawl</a></i>, and also appears as a trophy. He is the third character the player unlocks in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Chronicles:_The_Dark_Brotherhood" title="Sonic Chronicles: The Dark Brotherhood">Sonic Chronicles: The Dark Brotherhood</a></i>, where he acts as a team medic.</p>
<p>Tails appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Unleashed" title="Sonic Unleashed">Sonic Unleashed</a></i>, where he helps Sonic restore the shattered planet, largely by flying Sonic from continent to continent in the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tails takes a supporting role in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_and_the_Black_Knight" title="Sonic and the Black Knight">Sonic and the Black Knight</a></i>, portrayed as a local blacksmith who helps players craft goods from the items Sonic collects.</p>
<p>He appears as a playable character in all of the <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_Mario_sports_games#Mario_.26_Sonic_titles" title="List of Mario sports games">Mario &amp; Sonic</a></i> titles, as well as in <i><a href="/wiki/Sega_Superstars_Tennis" title="Sega Superstars Tennis">Sega Superstars Tennis</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_%26_Sega_All-Stars_Racing" title="Sonic &amp; Sega All-Stars Racing">Sonic &amp; Sega All-Stars Racing</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Sonic_%26_All-Stars_Racing_Transformed" title="Sonic &amp; All-Stars Racing Transformed">Transformed</a>. He also appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Colors" title="Sonic Colors">Sonic Colors</a></i> as a non-playable character.</p>
<p>Tails celebrates Sonic's birthday along with his friends by setting up a party in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Generations" title="Sonic Generations">Sonic Generations</a></i>, but when the Time Eater appears, it warps them through various time holes, sending Tails to Green Hill. After Sonic frees him, Tails meets his classic counterpart (Classic Tails), and concludes that they were traveling through time and space. They accompany both Sonics throughout the game, later discovering that Eggman and Robotnik are controlling the Time Eater. Both Classic and Modern Tails, along with all of Sonic's friends, help motivate the 2 Sonics to defeat the Time Eater.</p>
<p>The feature to play as Tails was added to the 2011 enhanced port of <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_CD" title="Sonic the Hedgehog CD">Sonic CD</a></i>. He appeared as a co-op character in the <a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_4:_Episode_II" title="Sonic the Hedgehog 4: Episode II">second episode of <i>Sonic 4</i></a>.</p>
<p>Tails was added as a playable character in the enhanced port of <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(1991_video_game)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (1991 video game)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i> released in 2013 for mobile devices. The port also has the option to play the "Sonic and Tails" mode as well.</p>
<p>Sonic and Tails end up facing a group of villains allied with Dr. Eggman, called the Deadly Six when they were shot down while chasing him in the Tornado in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Lost_World" title="Sonic Lost World">Sonic Lost World</a></i>. They run into Eggman and notice him wielding a Cacophonic Conch to control the Deadly Six. Despite Tails' warnings, Sonic knocks away the conch, causing the Deadly Six to betray him. Eggman reluctantly teams up with Sonic and Tails to stop them. This causes numerous conflict between Tails and Eggman, and increases tension with his friendship with Sonic, since Sonic believes Eggman is their only hope in defeating the Deadly Six. Eventually, Tails gets captured in a trap intended for Sonic, and the Deadly Six plan to use this to their advantage by turning Tails into a robot. However, Tails manages to reprogram the computer that would control his mind. The seemingly-roboticized Tails confronts Sonic at the game's final stage, but by retaining his free will, he takes the Deadly Six by surprise and attacks them. Later on, after Sonic defeats Doctor Eggman at the game's climax, Sonic apologizes to Tails for doubting him. Tails forgives him and the two return home.</p>
<p>Tails appears as a playable character in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Boom:_Rise_of_Lyric" title="Sonic Boom: Rise of Lyric">Sonic Boom: Rise of Lyric</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Boom:_Shattered_Crystal" title="Sonic Boom: Shattered Crystal">Shattered Crystal</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Boom:_Fire_%26_Ice" title="Sonic Boom: Fire &amp; Ice">Fire &amp; Ice</a></i>. He will be playable in the upcoming <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Mania" title="Sonic Mania">Sonic Mania</a></i>.</p>
<p>Tails also was featured in the Sonic the Hedgehog Level Pack of <i><a href="/wiki/Lego_Dimensions" title="Lego Dimensions">Lego Dimensions</a></i>. He is also playable if the player uses the Tornado as Sonic to fly around. In the story mode for the Sonic level titled "Sonic Dimensions", Tails assists Sonic using the Tornado and his technological knowledge. In the hub world, Tails has a side quest for the player to aid him in disabling all of Eggman's roboticizers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media">In other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tails has appeared in four animated television shows: <i><a href="/wiki/Adventures_of_Sonic_the_Hedgehog" title="Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog">Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(TV_series)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (TV series)">Sonic the Hedgehog (SatAM)</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_X" title="Sonic X">Sonic X</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Boom_(TV_series)" title="Sonic Boom (TV series)">Sonic Boom</a></i>. He has also appeared in several Sonic series of comic books and a feature film.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adventures_of_Sonic_the_Hedgehog"><i>Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He is supposedly significantly younger than in the games; he's "only 4 and a half" years old in <i><a href="/wiki/Adventures_of_Sonic_the_Hedgehog" title="Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog">Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i>. While in the games, he's 8. As with the game canon, his real name is Miles Prower. However, his fur is brown in the cartoon as opposed to orange in the games. Sonic's relationship with Tails is quite close in this series. Before Tails met Sonic, he was apparently an orphan who thought he was a bird because of his ability to fly; it was his being kicked from a nest that introduced the two and led to Miles receiving his nickname "Tails".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>While Tails is indeed useful to Sonic in many situations, he can be a hindrance. Because he is very young and cannot defend himself, he is incredibly susceptible to being kidnapped by Robotnik and used as bait for Sonic. Sonic appears to know of this flaw and does his best to protect him, but his protection methods are often the reason why Tails gets kidnapped in the first place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He also hates his real name and does not want anyone to know about it, a fact that Sonic occasionally exploits to <a href="/wiki/Blackmail" title="Blackmail">blackmail</a> him out of danger.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sonic_the_Hedgehog_.28SatAM.29"><i>Sonic the Hedgehog</i> (SatAM)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sonic the Hedgehog (SatAM)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tails' role in the Saturday morning <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(TV_series)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (TV series)">Sonic the Hedgehog (SatAM)</a></i> cartoon had him in a supporting role, quite low-key compared to the games and <i><a href="/wiki/Adventures_of_Sonic_the_Hedgehog" title="Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog">Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i> cartoon series. He had an inherently clever nature and high intellect, but was too combat inexperienced to be a Freedom Fighter. Instead, he was simply another inhabitant of the refugee village called Knothole, where the Freedom Fighters operated from. Tails is also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princess_Sally_Acorn" title="Princess Sally Acorn">Princess Sally Acorn</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mentorship" title="Mentorship">protégé</a>, and was in training to become a Freedom Fighter in season 1. He became a member of the Freedom Fighters near the end of Season 2, after finally proving himself capable. His first full role was in the pilot episode, however, as he went tagging along with on a dangerous mission with Sonic.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sonic_the_Hedgehog_OVA_.281996.29"><i>Sonic the Hedgehog</i> OVA (1996)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sonic the Hedgehog OVA (1996)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 2-part episode <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(OVA)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (OVA)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Original_Video_Animation" title="Original Video Animation">OVA</a>, Sonic and Tails were occasionally shown to be at odds with one another but were still best friends as in all other Sonic media. The two reluctantly agreed to help Dr. Robotnik outwit Metal Robotnik, who kicked Robotnik out of the city of Robotropolis, and deactivate the Robot Generator which will otherwise explode at sunrise. However, it was all a ruse by Robotnik in order to kidnap the <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> and his daughter Sara, a strange <a href="/wiki/Catgirl" title="Catgirl">cat girl</a>, and activate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metal_Sonic" title="Metal Sonic">Metal Sonic</a>. With the assistance of Knuckles, the trio prevailed in rescuing them both and in a climatic showdown, Metal Sonic was ultimately destroyed and melted in a pool of lava.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sonic_the_Hedgehog_.28comic.29"><i>Sonic the Hedgehog</i> (comic)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sonic the Hedgehog (comic)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Archie_Comics" title="Archie Comics">Archie Comics</a>-produced comic series, <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(comics)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (comics)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i>, Tails is the son of Rosemary and Amadeus Prower; Sonic was present at the time of Tails' birth, and the two immediately bonded. After being separated from them by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._Robotnik_(Archie_character)" title="Dr. Robotnik (Archie character)">Dr. Robotnik</a>, he grows up in Knothole Village with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(Archie_character)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (Archie character)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a> and the other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freedom_Fighters_(Sonic_the_Hedgehog)" title="Freedom Fighters (Sonic the Hedgehog)">Freedom Fighters</a>. He joins the Freedom Fighters, though he is unable to work in some of the more dangerous missions; when he does work in them, he mostly serves to pilot the group's escape vehicle and stays on board during that time. He eventually leaves on an adventure where he learns from Athair that he is the "Chosen One," a great hero that will bring about "The Great Harmony." He eventually becomes the target of Mammoth Mogul, and the two do battle on various occasions. After Robotnik is overthrown, Tails begins living with Sonic and his parents.</p>
<p>Tails later completes part of the Great Harmony by sealing away the Chaos Emeralds in a special dimension after being captured by A.D.A.M. Sonic begins dating Fiona Fox, Tails' crush, which causes a rift between them. When the two return Tails' parents to Mobius, the rift widens when the two get involved in a political schism started by Amadeus, Tails supporting his father and Sonic remaining loyal to the House of Acorn. Eventually, after an argument, the two reconcile after Sonic apologizes and explains that he had dated Fiona in the hopes that it would help Tails get over his feelings for her. He later helps in the fight against his fellow Freedom Fighters anti-counterparts once again. He then helped in the recent battle against Dr. Eggman, which results in Eggman descending into complete <a href="/wiki/Insanity" title="Insanity">insanity</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sonic_the_Comic"><i>Sonic the Comic</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sonic the Comic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tails also appears in the British <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Comic" title="Sonic the Comic">Sonic the Comic</a></i>, published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fleetway" title="Fleetway">Fleetway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Here, he first met Sonic when the latter saved him from drowning in a swamp. In this series Tails was older than his other incarnations, and was frequently the butt of many of Sonic's cruel jokes, most often being called "pixel brain". He is initially portrayed as a bit of a push-over, who constantly allows <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(Sonic_the_Comic)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (Sonic the Comic)">Sonic</a> to insult him, gets flustered in dangerous situations, and had a significant <a href="/wiki/Inferiority_complex" title="Inferiority complex">inferiority complex</a>. As time went on, Tails became more competent and heroic. On occasion he would return to his home land 'The Nameless Zone' where he would get a suit of silver armour and a short sword. When the comic began adapting Sonic Adventure, Tails' eyes remained brown while Knuckles and Sonic had their eyes changed to match their in-game counterparts.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sonic_X"><i>Sonic X</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sonic X">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Throughout most of the <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_X" title="Sonic X">Sonic X</a></i>, Tails mirrors his game role in various stories and game adaptations, though he often plays a smaller role in the series due to the presence of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_Thorndyke" title="Chris Thorndyke">Chris Thorndyke</a>. He quickly befriends fellow inventor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_from_Sonic_X#Chuck_Thorndyke" title="List of characters from Sonic X">Chuck Thorndyke</a>, who built the X-Tornado jet with him. In a tournament for a Chaos Emerald, Tails shows that he is stronger than he looks, but is defeated by Rouge the Bat when she kisses him.</p>
<p>In the third season, Tails is the captain of the Blue Typhoon, and over the series develops leadership skills. Tails has a crush on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmo_(Sonic_the_Hedgehog)" title="Cosmo (Sonic the Hedgehog)">Cosmo</a>, a plant-like female alien introduced during this season. Cosmo starts to show signs of affection towards Tails as the season progresses. Their relationship is confirmed when they fall into a love trap placed by Amy that was meant to catch Sonic. He emotionally broke down in tears at the conclusion when Cosmo asked him to kill her to defeat the Metarex, but kept one of the seeds she released. In the original Japanese version, Tails even confessed his love for Cosmo, before shooting her.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sonic_Boom"><i>Sonic Boom</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sonic Boom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2013, Sega announced a new <a href="/wiki/Computer_animation" title="Computer animation">CG-animated</a> television series called <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Boom_(TV_series)" title="Sonic Boom (TV series)">Sonic Boom</a></i>, released on November 8, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The series, along with two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonic_Boom_(2014_video_games)" title="Sonic Boom (2014 video games)">accompanying video games</a>, was officially unveiled in February 2014, with Tails playing a major role in the series alongside Sonic, Knuckles, and Amy, with new character Sticks. Unlike the other main characters, his personality and design is left mostly the same as his mainstream counterpart, but is shown to be more blunt with Sonic and willing to show skepticism to others.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wreck-It_Ralph"><i>Wreck-It Ralph</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Wreck-It Ralph">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While not physically appearing in the Disney animated film <i><a href="/wiki/Wreck-It_Ralph" title="Wreck-It Ralph">Wreck-It Ralph</a></i>, a drawing of Tails can be seen at the <a href="/wiki/Tapper" title="Tapper">Tapper</a> bar. Also, he can possibly be seen when Ralph is exiting Pac Man.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_and_legacy">Reception and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tails_(character)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Reception and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="rquote floatright" role="presentation" style="border-collapse:collapse; border-style:none; float:right; margin:0.5em 0.75em; width:33%;">
<tr style="text-align:left; vertical-align:top;">
<td style="color:#b2b7f2; font-size:3.3em; font-family:'Times New Roman',serif; font-weight:bold; padding:4px 2px 2px; width:0.5em; line-height: 1em;">“</td>
<td style="padding:0 10px;">Tails holds a place in the hearts of the kids of the Nineties as the very definition of ‘side-kick’. Players of <i>Sonic the Hedgehog 2</i> will remember the option to play as Sonic &amp; Tails at the same time, with Player 2 being able to control the two-tailed fox.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wc_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-wc-22">[22]</a></sup></td>
<td style="color:#b2b7f2; font-size:3.3em; font-family:'Times New Roman',serif; font-weight:bold; padding:4px 2px 2px; text-align:right; vertical-align:bottom; width:0.5em; line-height: 1em;">”</td>

<td class="rquotecite" colspan="3" style="padding-top:10px; font-size:smaller; line-height:1em; text-align:right;"><cite>— <i>20 Top Video Game Sidekicks Of All Time</i>, WhatCulture.com</cite></td>

<p>Reception to Tails has been generally positive. He was awarded "Best New Character" in <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Gaming_Monthly" title="Electronic Gaming Monthly">Electronic Gaming Monthly</a>'s 1992 video game awards, stating "not only is he as cute as Sonic, but he actually serves a major purpose in the game."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-egmbuyersguide1993_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-egmbuyersguide1993-23">[23]</a></sup> IGN editor Lucas M. Thompson listed Tails as one of the <i>Sonic the Hedgehog</i> characters who should be in <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Brawl" title="Super Smash Bros. Brawl">Super Smash Bros. Brawl</a></i>, citing his importance in the series and his abilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> IGN editor Levi Buchanan stated that the fan response to Tails' introduction was favorable, which led to further introductions such as Knuckles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_wrong_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN_wrong-25">[25]</a></sup> Unlike most <i>Sonic</i> characters, Tails has received consistently positive opinions from the <i>Sonic</i> fanbase over the years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duos_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-duos-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tails is remembered for symbolizing sidekicks and two-player gameplay in the 1990s,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wc_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-wc-22">[22]</a></sup> and as helping <i>Sonic 2</i> become the second highest-selling game for the Genesis through allowing a second player to join the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He has been featured on many "Top Sidekicks" lists. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ScrewAttack" title="ScrewAttack">ScrewAttack</a> listed him as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ScrewAttack%27s_Top_10" title="ScrewAttack's Top 10">greatest sidekick in gaming</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Additionally, ScrewAttack's web series <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_Battle" title="Death Battle">Death Battle</a> determined that he would win in a fight against <a href="/wiki/Mario" title="Mario">Mario</a>'s sidekick <a href="/wiki/Luigi" title="Luigi">Luigi</a>, due to his ability to fly and his extremely high IQ.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Maximum PC listed him as their third greatest sidekick,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> WhatCulture listed Tails as the 10th greatest sidekick out of a field of 20,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wc_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-wc-22">[22]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Machinima.com" title="Machinima.com">Machinima.com</a> ranked him fifth,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Maxim_(magazine)" title="Maxim (magazine)">Maxim</a> listed him as the eighth most underrated sidekick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dorkly" title="Dorkly">Dorkly</a> listed him as the greatest sidekick in gaming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Ranking him as the sixth greatest, <a href="/wiki/Mashable" title="Mashable">Mashable</a> stated that Tails "pretty much embodies the definition of 'sidekick'".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Sonic and Tails were together ranked as IGN's ninth greatest gaming duo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-duos_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-duos-26">[26]</a></sup> Morgan Sleeper of NintendoLife called Tails "one of Sega's most beloved mascots".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> staff writer Levi Buchanan stated that when Sega noticed the popularity of Tails, and later, Knuckles, they "just kept stuffing new faces and names into the game, pulling attention away from their hero."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_wrong_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-IGN_wrong-25">[25]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/GameDaily" title="GameDaily">GameDaily</a> listed the "annoying sidekick" in their top 25 video game archetypes list, citing Tails as an example of this.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cracked_(magazine)" title="Cracked (magazine)">Cracked</a> ranked him in <i>Sonic 2</i> as the 14th most annoying video game characters in otherwise great video games, stating that his ambition far outweighed his abilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GamesRadar" title="GamesRadar">GamesRadar</a> listed him as number one on their list of cutesy characters they wanted to beat up, stating that while he started out as interesting, he led to the creation of other characters who "choked the life out the franchise." They cited him being a "know it all" in later games as to why they hate him so much.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Official_Nintendo_Magazine" title="Official Nintendo Magazine">Official Nintendo Magazine</a></i> listed him as the second best <i>Sonic</i> character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
