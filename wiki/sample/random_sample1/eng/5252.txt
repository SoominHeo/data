<p><b>Mageia</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Linux_kernel" title="Linux kernel">Linux</a> based <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating system</a>, distributed as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_and_open_source_software" title="Free and open source software">free and open source software</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Fork_(software_development)" title="Fork (software development)">forked</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Mandriva_Linux" title="Mandriva Linux">Mandriva Linux</a> <a href="/wiki/Linux_distribution" title="Linux distribution">distribution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theinquirer-1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-theinquirer-1-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-osnews-1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-osnews-1-5">[5]</a></sup> Mageia was the first Linux distribution where <a href="/wiki/MariaDB" title="MariaDB">MariaDB</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Oracle_Corporation" title="Oracle Corporation">Oracle</a>'s <a href="/wiki/MySQL" title="MySQL">MySQL</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> term <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Greek transliteration"><i>mageía</i></span> (<span lang="el">μαγεία</span>) means enchantment, fascination, glamour, wizardry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-myEtymology-1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-myEtymology-1-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first release of the software distribution, Mageia 1, took place in June 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muktware-2_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-muktware-2-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clubic_heuillard_2011_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-clubic_heuillard_2011-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mageia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mageia was created in 2010 as a <a href="/wiki/Fork_(software_development)" title="Fork (software development)">fork</a> of Mandriva Linux,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theinquirer-1_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-theinquirer-1-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-osnews-1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-osnews-1-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-clubic_belfiore_2010_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-clubic_belfiore_2010-10">[10]</a></sup> by a group of former employees of <a href="/wiki/Mandriva" title="Mandriva">Mandriva S.A.</a> and several other developers and users and supporters of the Mandriva community.</p>
<p>On September 2, 2010, Edge IT, one of the subsidiaries of Mandriva, was placed under liquidation process by the <a href="/wiki/Tribunal_de_commerce" title="Tribunal de commerce">Tribunal de commerce</a> in Paris;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-societe-1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-societe-1-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-channelnews-1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-channelnews-1-12">[12]</a></sup> effective by September 17, all assets were liquidated and employees were let go.</p>
<p>The next day, on September 18, 2010, some of these former employees, who were mostly responsible for the development and maintenance of the Mandriva Linux distribution, and several community members announced the creation of Mageia, with the support of many members of the community of developers, users and employees of Mandriva Linux.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mga-announce-1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-mga-announce-1-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Desktop_environment">Desktop environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mageia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Desktop environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like many other distributions, Mageia uses all major desktop environments. Similar to Mandrake/Mandriva Linux, <a href="/wiki/KDE" title="KDE">KDE</a> is the main and the most used environment. End-users can choose from KDE and <a href="/wiki/GNOME" title="GNOME">GNOME</a> for the small installation DVD edition, any environment in full DVD edition and <a href="/wiki/Xfce" title="Xfce">Xfce</a> on the dual-arch CD. It uses <a href="/wiki/Drakconf" title="Drakconf">Mageia Control Center</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mageia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mageia is planned to be released on a 9-month release cycle, with each release to be supported for 18 months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muktware-1_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-muktware-1-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The latest stable version is Mageia 5.1, released in December, 2016.</p>
