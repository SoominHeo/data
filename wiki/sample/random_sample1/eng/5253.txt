<p><b>Magellan's Cross</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Christian_cross" title="Christian cross">Christian cross</a> planted by <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> explorers as ordered by <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a> upon arriving in <a href="/wiki/Cebu" title="Cebu">Cebu</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> on (depending on source) March 15 , 1521.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cebunetwork_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cebunetwork-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asiarooms_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-asiarooms-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>This cross is housed in a <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapel</a> next to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basilica_Minore_del_Santo_Ni%C3%B1o" title="Basilica Minore del Santo Niño">Basilica Minore del Santo Niño</a> on Magallanes Street (Magalhaes is the Portuguese name of Magellan), just in front of the city center of <a href="/wiki/Cebu_City" title="Cebu City">Cebu City</a>. A sign below the cross describes the original cross is encased inside the wooden cross that is found in the center of the chapel. This is to protect the original cross from people who chipped away parts of the cross for souvenir purposes or in the belief that the cross possesses miraculous powers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Some people, however, believe that the original cross had been destroyed or had disappeared after Magellan's death, and the cross is a replica that was planted there by the Spaniards after they successfully Christianized the Philippines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cebunetwork_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-cebunetwork-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asiarooms_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-asiarooms-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
