<p><b>Majuro</b>, <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="/dʒ/ 'j' in 'jam'">dʒ</span><span title="/ər/ 'er' in 'finger'">ər</span><span title="/oʊ/ long 'o' in 'code'">oʊ</span></span>/</a></span></span> (<a href="/wiki/Marshallese_language" title="Marshallese language">Marshallese</a>: <b><span lang="mh" xml:lang="mh">Mājro</span></b>, <span class="IPA" style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Marshallese" title="Help:IPA for Marshallese">[mʲæzʲ(ɛ͡ʌ)rˠɤ͡oo̯]</a></span>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is the <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a> and largest city of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Marshall_Islands" title="Republic of the Marshall Islands">Republic of the Marshall Islands</a>. It is also a large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coral_atoll" title="Coral atoll">coral atoll</a> of 64 islands in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>. It forms a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legislative_district" title="Legislative district">legislative district</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ratak_Chain" title="Ratak Chain">Ratak (Sunrise) Chain</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a>. The atoll has a land area of 9.7 square kilometres (3.7 sq mi) and encloses a lagoon of 295 square kilometres (114 sq mi). As with other atolls in the Marshall Islands, Majuro consists of narrow land masses.</p>
<p>The main population center, named <a href="/wiki/Delap-Uliga-Djarrit" title="Delap-Uliga-Djarrit">Delap-Uliga-Djarrit</a>, DUD - three contiguous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motu_(geography)" title="Motu (geography)">motus</a>, had 20,301 people as of 2012<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>. Majuro has a port, shopping district, hotels, <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands_International_Airport" title="Marshall Islands International Airport">and an international airport</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Majuro_Atoll-USA-P-Gilberts-15.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="509" data-file-width="800" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Majuro_Atoll-USA-P-Gilberts-15.jpg/220px-Majuro_Atoll-USA-P-Gilberts-15.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Majuro_Atoll-USA-P-Gilberts-15.jpg/330px-Majuro_Atoll-USA-P-Gilberts-15.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Majuro_Atoll-USA-P-Gilberts-15.jpg/440px-Majuro_Atoll-USA-P-Gilberts-15.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Majuro_Atoll-USA-P-Gilberts-15.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Schematic overview of Majuro</div>

<p>At the western end of the atoll, about 50 kilometres (30 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Delap-Uliga-Djarrit" title="Delap-Uliga-Djarrit">D–U–D</a> by road, is the island community of <a href="/wiki/Laura,_Marshall_Islands" title="Laura, Marshall Islands">Laura</a>, a growing residential area with a popular beach. Laura has the highest elevation point on the atoll, estimated at less than 3 metres (10 feet) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" title="Above mean sea level">above sea level</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Djarrit" title="Djarrit">Djarrit</a> is mostly residential.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lagoon_side_with_native_dwellings_-_Majuro.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="303" data-file-width="700" height="87" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/10/Lagoon_side_with_native_dwellings_-_Majuro.jpg/200px-Lagoon_side_with_native_dwellings_-_Majuro.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/10/Lagoon_side_with_native_dwellings_-_Majuro.jpg/300px-Lagoon_side_with_native_dwellings_-_Majuro.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/10/Lagoon_side_with_native_dwellings_-_Majuro.jpg/400px-Lagoon_side_with_native_dwellings_-_Majuro.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lagoon_side_with_native_dwellings_-_Majuro.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lagoon side with native dwellings</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Humans have inhabited the <a href="/wiki/Atoll" title="Atoll">atoll</a> for at least 2,000 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Majuro Atoll was claimed by the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> with the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a> in 1884, and the Germans established a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Trading_outpost&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Trading outpost (page does not exist)">trading outpost</a>. As with the rest of the Marshalls, Majuro was captured by the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a> in 1914 during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and mandated to the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a> by the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> in 1920. The island then became a part of the Japanese mandated territory of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nanyo_(Japanese_mandated_territory)" title="Nanyo (Japanese mandated territory)">Nanyo</a>; although the Japanese had established a government in Nanyo, local affairs were mostly left in the hands of traditional local leaders until the start of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:US_fleet_at_Majuro_Atoll_1944.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1995" data-file-width="2957" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/US_fleet_at_Majuro_Atoll_1944.jpg/220px-US_fleet_at_Majuro_Atoll_1944.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/US_fleet_at_Majuro_Atoll_1944.jpg/330px-US_fleet_at_Majuro_Atoll_1944.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/US_fleet_at_Majuro_Atoll_1944.jpg/440px-US_fleet_at_Majuro_Atoll_1944.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:US_fleet_at_Majuro_Atoll_1944.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
U.S. Fifth Fleet at Majuro Atoll 1944.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On January 30, 1944, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States troops</a> invaded, but found that Japanese forces had evacuated their fortifications to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kwajalein" title="Kwajalein">Kwajalein</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enewetak" title="Enewetak">Enewetak</a> about a year earlier. A single Japanese <a href="/wiki/Warrant_officer" title="Warrant officer">warrant officer</a> had been left as a caretaker. With his capture, the islands were secured. This gave the U.S. Navy use of one of the largest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anchorage" title="Anchorage">anchorages</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Pacific_Area" title="Central Pacific Area">Central Pacific</a>. The lagoon became a large forward naval base of operations and was the largest and most active <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">port</a> in the world until the war moved westward when it was supplanted by <a href="/wiki/Ulithi" title="Ulithi">Ulithi</a> (<a href="/wiki/Yap" title="Yap">Yap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Federated_States_of_Micronesia" title="Federated States of Micronesia">Federated States of Micronesia</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following World War II, Majuro came under the control of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Trust_Territory_of_the_Pacific_Islands" title="Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands">Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands</a>. It supplanted <a href="/wiki/Jaluit_Atoll" title="Jaluit Atoll">Jaluit Atoll</a> as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of the Marshall Islands, a status that it retains after the independence of the Marshall Islands in 1986.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The major population centers are the D–U–D communities: the islets of <a href="/wiki/Delap" title="Delap">Delap</a>–<a href="/wiki/Uliga" title="Uliga">Uliga</a>–<a href="/wiki/Djarrit" title="Djarrit">Djarrit</a> (listed from south to north, on the eastern edge of the atoll).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> As of 2008<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Majuro had a population of 27,797.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-undata_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-undata-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most of the population is Christian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-state-depart_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-state-depart-7">[7]</a></sup> The majority follows the <a href="/wiki/United_Church_of_Christ" title="United Church of Christ">United Church of Christ</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Cathedral_of_the_Assumption,_Majuro" title="Cathedral of the Assumption, Majuro">Cathedral of the Assumption</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Apostolic_Prefecture_of_the_Marshall_Islands" title="Roman Catholic Apostolic Prefecture of the Marshall Islands">Roman Catholic Apostolic Prefecture of the Marshall Islands</a> is located in Majuro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCatholic_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GCatholic-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Islamic influence has been increasing. There is a sizable number of <a href="/wiki/Ahmadiyya" title="Ahmadiyya">Ahmadi</a> Muslims. The first mosque opened in Majuro in September 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are also Mormon churches, Baptist churches, SDA churches, and the Salvation Army.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Majuro's economy is driven by the service sector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-state-depart_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-state-depart-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 15, 2007, Witon Barry, of the Tobolar Copra processing plant in the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a>' capital of Majuro, said power authorities, private companies and entrepreneurs had been experimenting with <a href="/wiki/Coconut_oil" title="Coconut oil">coconut oil</a> as an alternative to <a href="/wiki/Diesel_fuel" title="Diesel fuel">diesel fuel</a> for vehicles, <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">power generators</a> and ships. Coconut trees abound in the Pacific's tropical islands. Copra from 6 to 10 coconuts makes 1 litre of oil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Air_Marshall_Islands" title="Air Marshall Islands">Air Marshall Islands</a> has its headquarters in Majuro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Housed in Uliga are the <a href="/wiki/College_of_the_Marshall_Islands" title="College of the Marshall Islands">College of the Marshall Islands</a>, Assumption High School, and Uliga Elementary School where English is taught to all students.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands_High_School" title="Marshall Islands High School">Marshall Islands High School</a> is near the north end of Majuro.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_South_Pacific" title="University of South Pacific">University of South Pacific</a> has a presence on Majuro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-state-depart_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-state-depart-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Delap has the Seventh Day Adventist High School and Elementary School where English is taught to all students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Laura has the Seventh Day Adventist Elementary school where English is taught to all students.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Majuro Hospital has 81 beds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It is the main <a href="/wiki/Hospital" title="Hospital">hospital</a> for Majuro, as well as many of the outer islands.</p>
<p>In 2006, <a href="/wiki/Infant_mortality" title="Infant mortality">infant mortality</a> was 3.0%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-state-depart_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-state-depart-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Average_life_expectancy" title="Average life expectancy">Average life expectancy</a> at birth is 59 and 60 years for males and females respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure">Infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_and_Sewage">Water and Sewage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Water and Sewage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Majuro Water and Sewer Company obtains water from a catchment on the International runway. It supplies 140,000,000 US gallons (530,000,000 l; 120,000,000 imp gal) a year or 14 US gallons (53 l; 12 imp gal) per person per day. This compares with New York City's 118 US gallons (450 l; 98 imp gal) per person per day. Water is supplied 12 hours daily. The threat of drought is commonplace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Air">Air</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Air">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands_International_Airport" title="Marshall Islands International Airport">Marshall Islands International Airport</a>, offering domestic and international services, is on Majuro Atoll. It is served by four passenger airlines: <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines" title="United Airlines">United Airlines</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Our_Airline" title="Our Airline">Our Airline</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_Marshall_Islands" title="Air Marshall Islands">Air Marshall Islands</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Asia_Pacific_Airlines_(PNG)" title="Asia Pacific Airlines (PNG)">Asia Pacific Airlines</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Air Marshall Islands flies to most of the Marshalls' inhabited <a href="/wiki/Atoll" title="Atoll">atolls</a> once a week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> It offers daily service between Majuro and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kwajalein" title="Kwajalein">Kwajalein</a> except Thursdays and Sundays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Boat">Boat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Boat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Majuro Lagoon is an <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Active_port&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Active port (page does not exist)">active port</a>. The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marshall_Islands_Shipping_Corporation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marshall Islands Shipping Corporation (page does not exist)">Marshall Islands Shipping Corporation</a> operates three ships (<i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Aeaman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Aeaman (page does not exist)">Aeaman</a></i>, <i>Langdrik</i>, <i>Ribuukae</i>) and a landing craft (<i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jelejeletae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jelejeletae (page does not exist)">Jelejeletae</a></i>). These vessels are the main link for transporting people and supplies to and from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Outer_islands&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Outer islands (page does not exist)">outer islands</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Additionally, the <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoon</a> acts as a harbor for <a href="/wiki/Commercial_fishing" title="Commercial fishing">commercial fishing</a> vessels, <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruisers</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sport_fishing" title="Sport fishing">sport fishing</a> boats, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Outrigger_sailing_canoe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Outrigger sailing canoe (page does not exist)">outrigger sailing canoes</a> and the occasional <a href="/wiki/Luxury_yacht" title="Luxury yacht">luxury yacht</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads">Roads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Roads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Most of the roadway from <a href="/wiki/Djarrit" title="Djarrit">Djarrit</a> to <a href="/wiki/Laura,_Marshall_Islands" title="Laura, Marshall Islands">Laura</a> is a single two-lane paved road.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majuro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Majuro was initially scheduled to host the <a href="/wiki/2010_Micronesian_Games" title="2010 Micronesian Games">seventh edition</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Micronesian_Games" title="Micronesian Games">Micronesian Games</a>, in 2010. It subsequently renounced its hosting rights, citing a lack of adequate infrastructure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RNZI_39650_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-RNZI_39650-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>There is an olympic committee in Majuro. Weightlifter Mattie Langtor Sasser participated in the Rio 2016 summer olympics, competing in the Women’s 58Kg category on August 8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> She was competing for the Marshall Islands.</p>
