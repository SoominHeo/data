<p><b>Majid Jordan</b> is a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> duo, composed of Majid Al Maskati and Jordan Ullman. They are signed to <a href="/wiki/OVO_Sound" title="OVO Sound">OVO Sound</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a> co-founded by rapper <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>, producer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Noah_%2240%22_Shebib" title='Noah "40" Shebib'>Noah "40" Shebib</a> and Oliver El-Khatib.</p>
<p>Jordan, originally from <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, and Majid, arriving in Toronto by way of <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>, met as students at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a> in 2011. Working between Ullman's dorm room and his parents' basement, they launched their first joint <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> titled <i>Afterhours</i> on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a> under the pseudonym Good People.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majid_Jordan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present:_A_Place_Like_This_and_Majid_Jordan">2013–present: <i>A Place Like This</i> and <i>Majid Jordan</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Majid_Jordan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2013–present: A Place Like This and Majid Jordan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Majid Jordan co-produced and were credited to be featured on Drake's track "<a href="/wiki/Hold_On,_We%27re_Going_Home" title="Hold On, We're Going Home">Hold On, We're Going Home</a>", which was included from his platinum-selling album <i><a href="/wiki/Nothing_Was_the_Same" title="Nothing Was the Same">Nothing Was the Same</a></i>. "Hold On, We're Going Home" went on and then become Drake's most successful single-charting in each countries; including <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.</p>
<p>Majid and Jordan first met each other at Majid's birthday party at a bar in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toronto,_Canada" title="Toronto, Canada">Toronto, Canada</a>, and then later that week, they began working together in Jordan's dorm, releasing their first official single, entitled "Hold Tight", in just one day, which they posted on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soundcloud" title="Soundcloud">Soundcloud</a> anonymously. Producer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Noah_%2240%22_Shebib" title='Noah "40" Shebib'>Noah "40" Shebib</a> was later impressed by their EP, titled <i>afterhours</i>, and he later signed them to Drake's OVO Sound <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 7, 2014, Majid Jordan released their first official single as a duo through SoundCloud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On July 17, 2014, 10 ten days later, they released their official debut EP, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Place_Like_This_(EP)" title="A Place Like This (EP)">A Place Like This</a></i>, which was digitally through OVO Sound, the record label co-founded by rapper Drake. They went on to release music videos for three of the songs from the EP, including the title track "<a href="/wiki/A_Place_Like_This_(song)" title="A Place Like This (song)">A Place Like This</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> "Her"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and "Forever".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 10, 2015, Drake premiered their first single, titled "My Love" for their upcoming debut studio album on <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a>'s Beats 1 radio, during an interview with radio host <a href="/wiki/Zane_Lowe" title="Zane Lowe">Zane Lowe</a>, their label boss is also featured on the track. After its premiere it was released straight to <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a> and the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 17, 2016, the duo revealed their first-ever North American tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Majid_Jordan_to_kick_off_tour_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Majid_Jordan_to_kick_off_tour-8">[8]</a></sup> It starts off March 14 in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>. Shows are scheduled for <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Majid_Jordan_to_kick_off_tour_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Majid_Jordan_to_kick_off_tour-8">[8]</a></sup> Earlier in February, the duo released its self-titled debut by OVO sound, featuring a song with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drake_(rapper)" title="Drake (rapper)">Drake</a>, called "My Love.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Majid_Jordan_to_kick_off_tour_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Majid_Jordan_to_kick_off_tour-8">[8]</a></sup>"</p>
