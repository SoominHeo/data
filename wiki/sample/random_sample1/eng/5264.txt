<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Anesthesiology</h1>

<p><b>Anesthesiology</b>, <b>anaesthesia</b> or <b>anaesthetics</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Specialty_(medicine)" title="Specialty (medicine)">medical speciality</a> that focuses on <a href="/wiki/Perioperative_medicine" title="Perioperative medicine">perioperative medicine</a> and the administration of <a href="/wiki/Anesthesia" title="Anesthesia">anesthesia</a>. The American Society of Anesthesiologists define anesthesiology as "the practice of medicine dedicated to the relief of pain and total care of the surgical patient before, during and after surgery."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Terminology varies between countries. In North America, the medical speciality is called <i>anesthesiology</i>, a doctor practising it is termed an <i><a href="/wiki/Anesthesiologist" title="Anesthesiologist">anesthesiologist</a></i>, and the treatment delivered is referred to as <i>anesthesia</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASA_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ASA-2">[2]</a></sup> By contrast, in the United Kingdom and other countries following the British tradition, both the medical speciality and the treatment delivered are referred to as <i>anaesthesia</i> or <i>anaesthetics</i>, and the physician who performs them is termed an <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anaesthetist" title="Anaesthetist">anaesthetist</a></i> (in North America, the word <i>anesthetist</i> indicates a certified anesthesiologist assistant who delivers anesthesia under the supervision of a physician) or certified registered nurse anesthetist who in most states in the U.S. require physician supervision.</p>
<p>One of the fundamental practices of anesthesiologists is that of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_anesthesia" title="General anesthesia">general anesthesia</a> in which a person is placed in a medical <a href="/wiki/Coma" title="Coma">coma</a>. This is performed to permit <a href="/wiki/Surgery" title="Surgery">surgery</a> without the individual responding to pain (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Analgesia" title="Analgesia">analgesia</a>) during surgery or remembering the surgery.</p>
<p>If general anesthesia is not necessary, then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_anesthesia" title="Regional anesthesia">regional anesthesia</a> can be performed to induce analgesia in a region of the body. For example, <a href="/wiki/Epidural_administration" title="Epidural administration">epidural administration</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Local_anesthetic" title="Local anesthetic">local anesthetic</a> is commonly performed on the mother during <a href="/wiki/Childbirth" title="Childbirth">childbirth</a> to reduce the pain while permitting the mother to be awake and active in labor &amp; delivery (general anesthesia would not permit this).</p>
<p>Anesthesiologists often also undertake non-surgical pain relief and critical care management that includes working in an <a href="/wiki/Intensive_care_unit" title="Intensive care unit">intensive care unit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ASA_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ASA-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Training">Training</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anesthesiology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Training">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United States, anesthesiologists attend four years of <a href="/wiki/Medical_school" title="Medical school">medical school</a> and then follow it with four years of <a href="/wiki/Residency_(medicine)" title="Residency (medicine)">residency</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nurse_anesthetist" title="Nurse anesthetist">Nurse anesthetists</a> are <a href="/wiki/Registered_nurse" title="Registered nurse">registered nurses</a> with additional post-graduate training in anesthesia with at least one year of work experience in an <a href="/wiki/Intensive_care_unit" title="Intensive care unit">intensive care unit</a>, who work either under the supervision of an anesthesiologist or independently depending on state law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Knowledge">Knowledge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anesthesiology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Knowledge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Effective practice of anesthesiology requires several areas of knowledge by the practitioner, some of which are:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pharmacology" title="Pharmacology">Pharmacology</a> of commonly used drugs including <a href="/wiki/Inhalational_anaesthetic" title="Inhalational anaesthetic">inhalational anaesthetics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Topical_anesthetic" title="Topical anesthetic">topical anesthetics</a>, &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vasopressors" title="Vasopressors">vasopressors</a> as well as numerous other drugs used in association with anesthetics (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Ondansetron" title="Ondansetron">ondansetron</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glycopyrrolate" title="Glycopyrrolate">glycopyrrolate</a>)</li>
<li>Monitors: <a href="/wiki/Electrocardiography" title="Electrocardiography">electrocardiography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electroencephalography" title="Electroencephalography">electroencephalography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electromyography" title="Electromyography">electromyography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Entropy_monitoring" title="Entropy monitoring">entropy monitoring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neuromuscular_monitoring" title="Neuromuscular monitoring">neuromuscular monitoring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cortical_stimulation_mapping" title="Cortical stimulation mapping">cortical stimulation mapping</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neuromorphology" title="Neuromorphology">neuromorphology</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mechanical_ventilation" title="Mechanical ventilation">Mechanical ventilation</a></li>
<li>Anatomical knowledge of the <a href="/wiki/Nervous_system" title="Nervous system">nervous system</a> for nerve blocks, etc.</li>
<li>Other areas of medicine (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Cardiology" title="Cardiology">cardiology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pulmonology" title="Pulmonology">pulmonology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Obstetrics" title="Obstetrics">obstetrics</a>) to assess the risk of anesthesia to adequately have <a href="/wiki/Informed_consent" title="Informed consent">informed consent</a>, and knowledge of anesthesia regarding how it affects certain age groups (neonates, pediatrics, geriatrics)</li>
</ul>
