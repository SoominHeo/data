<p><b>Mats Julian Hummels</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈmats ˈhʊml̩s]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:De-Mats_Hummels.oga" title="File:De-Mats Hummels.oga"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e7/De-Mats_Hummels.oga" title="De-Mats Hummels.oga">listen</a>)</small>; born 16 December 1988) is a German professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a> for <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">FC Bayern Munich</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">German national team</a>.</p>
<p>He came through the <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> youth academy before joining <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> on loan in January 2008 and officially signing for Dortmund in February 2009 for €4 million. His honours at the club include two league titles and finishing as runner up in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2012–13 UEFA Champions League">2012–13 UEFA Champions League</a>. In May 2016, Hummels signed a contract with former side Bayern, which took effect on 1 July 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hummels has been a full international since 2010, earning 53 caps till date and representing Germany at <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>, <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mats_Hummels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bayern_Munich:_Youth.2C_and_2006.E2.80.932008">Bayern Munich: Youth, and 2006–2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mats_Hummels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bayern Munich: Youth, and 2006–2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hummels is a product of <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayern_Munich_Junior_Team" title="Bayern Munich Junior Team">youth academy</a>, first entering the club as a six-year-old. He signed his first professional contract on 19 December 2006, until 2010. On 19 May 2007, in <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Bundesliga" title="2006–07 Bundesliga">the season</a>'s final match, he played his first <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> match with the first team in a 5–2 home routing of <a href="/wiki/1._FSV_Mainz_05" title="1. FSV Mainz 05">1. FSV Mainz 05</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Borussia_Dortmund:_2008.E2.80.932016">Borussia Dortmund: 2008–2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mats_Hummels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Borussia Dortmund: 2008–2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In January 2008, Hummels joined <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>, initially on loan. During his <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Bundesliga" title="2008–09 Bundesliga">first full season</a>, he quickly established himself as first-choice, as he often partnered newly signed <a href="/wiki/Neven_Suboti%C4%87" title="Neven Subotić">Neven Subotić</a>, but also missed a great part of its final months due to injury. In February 2009, he was fully signed to the club<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> for a fee of €4 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Borussia_Dortmund_season" title="2010–11 Borussia Dortmund season">2010–11 season</a> brought increased success for Hummels. He was the regular first-choice center-back, again paired with Subotić. The duo helped Dortmund to the best defensive record in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Bundesliga" title="2010–11 Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>, as the team won the <a href="/wiki/List_of_German_football_champions" title="List of German football champions">league title</a>. Hummels' performances that season displayed great quality in <a href="/wiki/Sliding_tackle" title="Sliding tackle">tackling</a>, positioning, passing and composure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He has attracted praise from pundits and coaches alike, and he is considered one of the best defenders in the Bundesliga, and in Europe.</p>
<p>Hummels won the Bundesliga for a second time in <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Bundesliga" title="2011–12 Bundesliga">2011–12</a> as Borussia set a Bundesliga record with the most points in a Bundesliga season with 81 points. Hummels scored a goal in Borussia's 5–2 win against Bayern in the <a href="/wiki/2012_DFB-Pokal_Final" title="2012 DFB-Pokal Final">2012 DFB-Pokal Final</a> as the team completed the league and cup <a href="/wiki/Double_(association_football)" title="Double (association football)">double</a>. On 3 June 2012, Hummels signed a new contract that will keep him at Dortmund until the summer of 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 May 2013, Hummels was in the Borussia Dortmund line-up that was defeated 2–1 by Bayern Munich at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2013 UEFA Champions League Final">2013 UEFA Champions League Final</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 July 2013, Hummels was in the Borussia Dortmund line-up that won 4–2 against Bayern Munich at BVB's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Signal_Iduna_Park" title="Signal Iduna Park">Signal Iduna Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dortmund" title="Dortmund">Dortmund</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/2013_DFL-Supercup" title="2013 DFL-Supercup">2013 DFL-Supercup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>On 10 May 2016, it was confirmed that Hummels would re-join Bayern Munich from 1 July 2016, signing a five-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He made his 2016 debut for Bayern on 14 August 2016 in the <a href="/wiki/2016_DFL-Supercup" title="2016 DFL-Supercup">2016 DFL-Supercup</a> final, against his old team and winning 2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>Hummels was called by the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_under-21_football_team" title="Germany national under-21 football team">German under-21 national team</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">2009 European Championship</a>; after getting only minimal playing time during the initial four matches, he started (and performed well) in the final, a 4–0 success against <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England</a>.</p>
<p>He made his <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">senior team</a> debut in a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly</a> against <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a> on 13 May 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Aachen" title="Aachen">Aachen</a>. He came on as a 46th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Serdar_Tasci" title="Serdar Tasci">Serdar Tasci</a>, taking part in a 3–0 win at <a href="/wiki/New_Tivoli" title="New Tivoli">New Tivoli</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ramos_quits_Sevilla_to_join_Spurs_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ramos_quits_Sevilla_to_join_Spurs-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hummels was in the starting line-up for Germany's opening <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> and helped them to a 1–0 victory. He subsequently received high praise for his performance. Hummels went on to play all 450 minutes in Germany's Euro campaign, along with teammates <a href="/wiki/Philipp_Lahm" title="Philipp Lahm">Philipp Lahm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sami_Khedira" title="Sami Khedira">Sami Khedira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bastian_Schweinsteiger" title="Bastian Schweinsteiger">Bastian Schweinsteiger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holger_Badstuber" title="Holger Badstuber">Holger Badstuber</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Neuer" title="Manuel Neuer">Manuel Neuer</a>.</p>
<p>Hummels scored the second goal in Germany's 4–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> in their first game of the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> on 16 June, heading a corner from <a href="/wiki/Toni_Kroos" title="Toni Kroos">Toni Kroos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> After missing the team's <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_knockout_stage#Round_of_16" title="2014 FIFA World Cup knockout stage">round of 16</a> match due to illness, Hummels returned to the team for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_knockout_stage#Quarter-finals" title="2014 FIFA World Cup knockout stage">quarter-final</a> against <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>, where he scored the winning goal in a 1–0 victory at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_do_Maracan%C3%A3" title="Estádio do Maracanã">Estádio do Maracanã</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 July 2014, Hummels was named on the 10-man shortlist for FIFA's <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup#Golden_Ball" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">Golden Ball</a> award for the tournament's best player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mats_Hummels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Regarded as one of the best <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defenders</a> in the world,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theguardian.com_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian.com-16">[16]</a></sup> Hummels is a large, consistent, and physically strong player, known for his powerful tackling and strength in the air, as well as his ability to read the game and intercept loose balls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> A technically gifted and tactically versatile <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>, he is also capable of playing as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defensive_midfielder" title="Defensive midfielder">defensive midfielder</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theguardian.com_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian.com-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> his composure, elegance, ball playing ability, and confidence in possession have led him to be compared to compatriot <a href="/wiki/Franz_Beckenbauer" title="Franz Beckenbauer">Franz Beckenbauer</a>, and Spanish defender <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Piqu%C3%A9" title="Gerard Piqué">Gerard Piqué</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mats_Hummels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hummels was born in <a href="/wiki/Bergisch_Gladbach" title="Bergisch Gladbach">Bergisch Gladbach</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>. His father, Hermann Hummels, was a professional footballer and manager. His father worked as youth coordinator at <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> until he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Stephan_Beckenbauer" title="Stephan Beckenbauer">Stephan Beckenbauer</a>, the son of <a href="/wiki/Franz_Beckenbauer" title="Franz Beckenbauer">Franz Beckenbauer</a>, on 30 March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> His younger brother <a href="/wiki/Jonas_Hummels" title="Jonas Hummels">Jonas</a> is also a footballer, currently playing for <a href="/wiki/SpVgg_Unterhaching" title="SpVgg Unterhaching">SpVgg Unterhaching</a>.</p>
<p>His wife, Cathy Fischer, was named as Germany's <a href="/wiki/WAGs" title="WAGs">WAG</a> of the year for 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Fischer and Hummels married in June 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
