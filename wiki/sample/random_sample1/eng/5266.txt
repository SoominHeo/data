<p><b>Ryuhei Matsuda</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">松田 龍平</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Matsuda Ryūhei</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born 9 May 1983)</span> is a Japanese film and television actor. Matsuda's best known film roles include the young and desirable samurai Sōzaburō Kanō in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taboo_(1999_film)" title="Taboo (1999 film)">Taboo</a></i> and the rock star Ren Honjo in <i><a href="/wiki/Nana_(2005_film)" title="Nana (2005 film)">Nana</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryuhei_Matsuda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Matsuda was born on 9 May 1983 in Tokyo to <a href="/wiki/Yusaku_Matsuda" title="Yusaku Matsuda">Yusaku Matsuda</a>, a Japanese actor of partial <a href="/wiki/Koreans_in_Japan" title="Koreans in Japan">Korean</a> ancestry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Miyuki_Matsuda" title="Miyuki Matsuda">Miyuki Matsuda</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N%C3%A9e" title="Née">née</a> Kumagai), a Japanese actress. He has two younger siblings, a younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Shota_Matsuda" title="Shota Matsuda">Shota Matsuda</a>, and a younger sister by his parents' marriage and one older half-sister by his father's first marriage. His father died from <a href="/wiki/Bladder_cancer" title="Bladder cancer">bladder cancer</a> in 1989, when Ryuhei was 6 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Horikoshi_High_School" title="Horikoshi High School">Horikoshi High School</a>, a Japanese high school that caters to celebrity students, but did not graduate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryuhei_Matsuda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the age of 15, Matsuda was offered the role of the desirable young <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a> Kanō Sōzaburō in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nagisa_%C5%8Cshima" title="Nagisa Ōshima">Nagisa Ōshima</a>'s 1999 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taboo_(1999_film)" title="Taboo (1999 film)">Taboo</a></i>. The role helped boost him from an entirely unknown actor to a film star, earning him a Japanese Academy award "Newcomer of the Year", as well as "Blue Ribbon", "Kinema Junpo", and "Yokohama Film Festival" Awards for the "Best New Actor".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since appearing in <i>Taboo</i>, Matsuda has played a wide range of roles, from the high school student Kujo in the 2001 film <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Spring_(film)" title="Blue Spring (film)">Blue Spring</a></i> to the rock star Ren Honjo in the 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/Nana_(2005_film)" title="Nana (2005 film)">Nana</a></i>. In February 2013, it was revealed that Matsuda would play the part of a Japanese gangster in an upcoming sequel to the 2012 Indonesian film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Raid:_Redemption" title="The Raid: Redemption">The Raid</a></i>, named <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berandal_(film)" title="Berandal (film)">Berandal</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryuhei_Matsuda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 11 January 2009, Matsuda married Lina Ohta <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">太田 莉菜</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ōta Rina</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, a Russian-Japanese model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tokyograph_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-tokyograph-6">[6]</a></sup> Their first child, a baby girl, was born on 4 July 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tokyograph_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-tokyograph-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryuhei_Matsuda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
