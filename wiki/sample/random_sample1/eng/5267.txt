<p><b>Rica Matsumoto</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">松本 梨香</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Matsumoto Rika</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born November 30, 1968)</span> is a Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voice_actress" title="Voice actress">voice actress</a>, and <a href="/wiki/J-pop" title="J-pop">J-pop</a> singer who was born in <a href="/wiki/Yokohama" title="Yokohama">Yokohama</a>, Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Doi1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Doi1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Her name is also sometimes <a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Japanese" title="Romanization of Japanese">romanized</a> as <b>Rika Matsumoto</b>. She was a founding member of vocal supergroup <a href="/wiki/JAM_Project" title="JAM Project">JAM Project</a>.</p>
<p>After early stage work, she began both voice acting and singing careers. The most popular role by Rica is Satoshi (<a href="/wiki/Ash_Ketchum" title="Ash Ketchum">Ash Ketchum</a> in English translations), the main character of the <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_(anime)" title="Pokémon (anime)">Pokémon</a></i>. She also had a role in the <i>Yu-Gi-Oh</i> series as Ryo Bakura and Yami Bakura. As such, Matsumoto has taken on roles for young boys. Matsumoto also performs the songs for many of the openings for the Japanese <i>Pokémon</i> anime.</p>
<p>Matsumoto has had lead or supporting roles in dozens of series. She has also had a radio show in Japan and does some dubbing work for translations of American films and TV series into Japanese. Matsumoto was also a founding member of <a href="/wiki/JAM_Project" title="JAM Project">JAM Project</a> in 2000. She announced in April 2008 she was taking time off from the group and her solo work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
