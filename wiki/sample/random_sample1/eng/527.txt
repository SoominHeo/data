<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">2026 FIFA World Cup</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>2026 FIFA World Cup</b> will be the 23rd <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">FIFA World Cup</a>, the quadrennial international men's <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> championship contested by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_men%27s_national_association_football_teams" title="List of men's national association football teams">national teams</a> of the member associations of <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>.</p>
<p>The bidding process was due to start in 2015, with the appointment of hosts previously scheduled for the FIFA Congress on 10 May 2017 in <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fifa20150320_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-fifa20150320-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On 10 June 2015, it was announced the bidding process has been postponed, and the bidding process will resume in 2020, amid <a href="/wiki/Garcia_Report" title="Garcia Report">corruption allegations around the previous tournaments</a>, due to be held in <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018</a> (Russia), as well as in <a href="/wiki/2022_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2022 FIFA World Cup">2022</a> (Qatar).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The tournament will be the first to feature 48 teams, after FIFA approved expansion from 32 teams in January 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48teams_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-48teams-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Format">Format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2026_FIFA_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Then-<a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> head <a href="/wiki/Michel_Platini" title="Michel Platini">Michel Platini</a> had suggested in January 2015 an expansion of the tournament to 40 teams,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> an idea FIFA president <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Infantino" title="Gianni Infantino">Gianni Infantino</a> also suggested in March 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> A desire to increase the number of participants in the tournament from the previous 32 team format was announced 4 October 2016. Four expansion options were considered:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heraldlive_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-heraldlive-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Expand to 40 teams (8 groups of 5 teams) – 88 matches</li>
<li>Expand to 40 teams (10 groups of 4 teams) – 76 matches</li>
<li>Expand to 48 teams (opening 32-team playoff round) – 80 matches</li>
<li><b>Expand to 48 teams (16 groups of 3 teams) – 80 matches</b></li>
</ul>
<p>On 10 January 2017, the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Council" title="FIFA Council">FIFA Council</a> voted unanimously to expand to a 48 team tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48teams_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-48teams-5">[5]</a></sup> The tournament will open with a group stage consisting of 16 groups of 3 teams, with the top 2 teams progressing from each group to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elimination_tournament" title="Elimination tournament">knockout tournament</a> starting with a round of 32 teams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The number of games played overall will increase from 64 to 80, but the number of games played by finalists remains at 7, the same as with 32 teams, except that one group match will be replaced by a knockout match. The tournament will also be completed within 32 days, same as previous 32-team tournaments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The proposal for expansion has been opposed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Clubs_Association" title="European Clubs Association">European Clubs Association</a> and its member clubs, saying that the number of games was already at an "unacceptable" level and they urged the governing body to reconsider its idea of increasing the number of teams that qualify.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> German national team coach <a href="/wiki/Joachim_L%C3%B6w" title="Joachim Löw">Joachim Löw</a> warned that expansion, as had occurred for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">Euro 2016</a>, would dilute the value of the world tournament because players have already reached their physical and mental limit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Another criticism of the new format is that with 3-team groups, the risk of collusion between the two teams playing in the last round will increase compared with 4-team groups (where simultaneous kick-offs have been employed). One suggestion by President Infantino is that group matches that end in draws will be decided by penalty shootouts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Host_selection">Host selection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2026_FIFA_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Host selection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:World_Map_FIFA2.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="415" data-file-width="940" height="177" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/World_Map_FIFA2.svg/400px-World_Map_FIFA2.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/World_Map_FIFA2.svg/600px-World_Map_FIFA2.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/World_Map_FIFA2.svg/800px-World_Map_FIFA2.svg.png 2x" width="400"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:World_Map_FIFA2.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map of the World with the six confederations</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The FIFA Executive Committee (now the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Council" title="FIFA Council">FIFA Council</a>) decided on 30 May 2015 that any country could bid for a World Cup provided that their <a href="/wiki/FIFA#Six_confederations_and_211_national_associations" title="FIFA">continental confederation</a> had not hosted the preceding World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> For the 2026 World Cup, this meant that bids from the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Football_Confederation" title="Asian Football Confederation">Asian Football Confederation</a> (which is to host the <a href="/wiki/2022_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2022 FIFA World Cup">2022 World Cup</a> in Qatar) would not be allowed. Later, in October 2016, the FIFA Council approved the general principle that member associations from continental confederations of the last two hosts of the FIFA World Cup (i.e. the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Football_Confederation" title="Asian Football Confederation">AFC</a> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a>, the latter being due to host the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 World Cup</a> in Russia) will be ineligible to host the 2026 FIFA World Cup; however, the FIFA Council will have the power to grant eligibility to member associations of the confederation of the second-to-last host of the FIFA World Cup (i.e. <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a>) and open the bidding process to any interested MAs from this confederation in the event that none of the received bids fulfil the strict technical and financial requirements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fifacouncil_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-fifacouncil-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap-cbc_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap-cbc-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was also approved that co-hosting of the 2026 FIFA World Cup will be permitted, not limited to a specific number, but evaluated on a case-by-case basis, and that for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, the FIFA general secretariat, after consultation with the Competitions Committee, will have the power to exclude bidders who do not meet the minimum technical requirements to host the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fifacouncil_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-fifacouncil-19">[19]</a></sup> Joint bids had been banned by FIFA after the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>Therefore, the 2026 World Cup will be hosted by one of the remaining four confederations: <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF" title="CONCACAF">CONCACAF</a> (last hosted in <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1994 FIFA World Cup">1994</a>), <a href="/wiki/Confederation_of_African_Football" title="Confederation of African Football">CAF</a> (last hosted in <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010</a>), <a href="/wiki/CONMEBOL" title="CONMEBOL">CONMEBOL</a> (last hosted in <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014</a>), or <a href="/wiki/Oceania_Football_Confederation" title="Oceania Football Confederation">OFC</a> (never hosted before), or potentially by <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> in case no bid from those four meets the requirements.</p>
<p>Under the initial decision, there would be a change from FIFA's previous policy, which applied for the <a href="/wiki/2018_and_2022_FIFA_World_Cup_bids" title="2018 and 2022 FIFA World Cup bids">2018 and 2022 bidding process</a>, and allowed any country to bid provided that its confederation had not hosted either of the previous two World Cups. However, the revised decision effectively means that this policy remains the same, except for the chance for potential eligibility of the second-to-last hosting confederation.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bid_process">Bid process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2026_FIFA_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bid process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bidding for the 2026 FIFA World Cup was postponed due to the <a href="/wiki/2015_FIFA_corruption_case" title="2015 FIFA corruption case">2015 FIFA corruption case</a> and the subsequent resignation of <a href="/wiki/Sepp_Blatter" title="Sepp Blatter">Sepp Blatter</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> then restarted following the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Council" title="FIFA Council">FIFA Council</a> meeting on 10 May 2016, wherein the bidding process will consist of four phases:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-process_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-process-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>May 2016 – May 2017: a new strategy and consultation phase</li>
<li>June 2017 – Dec 2018: enhanced phases for bid preparation</li>
<li>January 2019 – February 2020: bid evaluation</li>
<li>May 2020: final decision</li>
</ul>
<p>The consultation phase will focus on four areas:</p>
<ul>
<li>The inclusion of human rights requirements, sustainable event management, environmental protection in the bidding</li>
<li>Principle of exclusion of bidders that do not meet technical requirements</li>
<li>Review of the current stance on joint bids</li>
<li>Number of teams</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Confirmed_interest_in_bidding">Confirmed interest in bidding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2026_FIFA_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Confirmed interest in bidding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CONCACAF">CONCACAF</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2026_FIFA_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: CONCACAF">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CONCACAF member federations (Canada, Mexico, United States) are favoured to host the 2026 World Cup for the first time since 1994 World Cup. Also, FIFA's president Infantino will consider the option for triple co-hosting by Canada, Mexico and the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap-cbc_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap-cbc-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 14 October 2016, FIFA said it will accept CONCACAF triple tournament-sharing bid by Canada, Mexico and the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to CONCACAF president, <a href="/wiki/Victor_Montagliani" title="Victor Montagliani">Victor Montagliani</a>, there would be a possibility of a joint bid between the United States, Canada and Mexico to host the 2026 FIFA World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>

<dd>In July 2012, <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Soccer_Association" title="Canadian Soccer Association">Canadian Soccer Association</a> president <a href="/wiki/Victor_Montagliani" title="Victor Montagliani">Victor Montagliani</a> confirmed plans for a Canadian bid, saying: "We have verbally told FIFA that when the bid process begins for the next available World Cup, which would be the 2026 World Cup, that the CSA will be one of the countries putting in a formal proposal".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> At the time the bid was announced, Canada had hosted the men's <a href="/wiki/1987_FIFA_U-16_World_Championship" title="1987 FIFA U-16 World Championship">1987 Under-16 World Championship</a> and the U-20 World Cups for both <a href="/wiki/2007_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2007 FIFA U-20 World Cup">men</a> and <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_U-19_Women%27s_World_Championship" title="2002 FIFA U-19 Women's World Championship">women</a>; the country has since hosted the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_U-20_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA U-20 Women's World Cup">2014 FIFA U-20 Women's World Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="FIFA Women's World Cup">FIFA Women's World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/2015_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="2015 FIFA Women's World Cup">2015</a>. In October 2013, Montagliani confirmed Canada's intention to bid for the 2026 tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On 23 January 2014, the Canadian Soccer Association confirmed that it is bidding for the 2026 World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_to_bid_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Canada_to_bid-28">[28]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>

<dd>In September 2012, <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Football_Federation" title="Mexican Football Federation">Mexican Football Federation</a> President Justino Compeán confirmed plans for a Mexican bid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Mexico has hosted two previous World Cups in <a href="/wiki/1970_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1970 FIFA World Cup">1970</a> and <a href="/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1986 FIFA World Cup">1986</a> (Colombia had been chosen to host the 1986 tournament, but due to economic problems, Mexico was awarded as the new host). In October 2013, Liga MX President said that Mexico is interested in joining forces with the U.S. to co-host a bid for the 2026 World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> On 9 December 2014, the Mexican Football Federation confirmed that it is bidding for the 2026 World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mexico_to_bid_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mexico_to_bid-31">[31]</a></sup> If the campaign is successful, Mexico will be the first nation to have hosted the World Cup three times.</dd>

<dl>
<dd>On 13 May 2016 at the FIFA Congress in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, USSF board member John Motta told <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> "whatever happens, we will bid for the 2026 World Cup -- either jointly (with Mexico) or we will go it alone."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Previously, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Soccer_Federation" title="United States Soccer Federation">United States Soccer Federation</a> president <a href="/wiki/Sunil_Gulati" title="Sunil Gulati">Sunil Gulati</a> stated the United States would only seriously consider bidding for the 2026 World Cup if the bidding process is more transparent and fair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, FIFA Secretary General <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_Valcke" title="Jérôme Valcke">Jérôme Valcke</a> said that he felt there was interest in the United States for hosting the 2026 Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> FIFA President <a href="/wiki/Sepp_Blatter" title="Sepp Blatter">Sepp Blatter</a> said, “Perhaps there’s a big commercial opportunity arising now in the United States because of the tremendous television audiences that are booming and that the World Cup has also encouraged in its domestic game as well. We did well with football when it first went to the United States but the opportunities are bigger now."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The United States hosted the <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1994 FIFA World Cup">1994 FIFA World Cup</a> and unsuccessfully <a href="/wiki/United_States_2022_FIFA_World_Cup_bid" title="United States 2022 FIFA World Cup bid">bid</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2022_World_Cup" title="2022 World Cup">2022 World Cup</a>, which was won by <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a> in 2010. On 18 April 2015, Brazilian legend <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9" title="Pelé">Pelé</a> stated that the United States should host the 2026 World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The U.S. hosting the 2026 World Cup would coincide with the country's 250th anniversary of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Declaration_of_Independence" title="American Declaration of Independence">American Declaration of Independence</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CONMEBOL">CONMEBOL</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2026_FIFA_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: CONMEBOL">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><span class="flagicon"><img alt="" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="450" height="15" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Flag_of_Colombia.svg/23px-Flag_of_Colombia.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Flag_of_Colombia.svg/35px-Flag_of_Colombia.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Flag_of_Colombia.svg/45px-Flag_of_Colombia.svg.png 2x" width="23"/> </span><a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>In March 2010, Colombian President <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Uribe" title="Álvaro Uribe">Álvaro Uribe</a> confirmed plans for a Colombian bid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a> final, <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> president <a href="/wiki/Sepp_Blatter" title="Sepp Blatter">Sepp Blatter</a> said "Colombia is ready for a World Cup".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Colombia was chosen to host the <a href="/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1986 FIFA World Cup">1986 FIFA World Cup</a> in 1974, but due to financial problems, was later awarded to <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> in 1983. The country has since hosted the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2011 Under-20 World Cup</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Games_2013" title="World Games 2013">World Games 2013</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2016_FIFA_Futsal_World_Cup" title="2016 FIFA Futsal World Cup">2016 FIFA Futsal World Cup</a>. In July 2010, Peru also suggested co-hosting with Colombia and Ecuador, and President Uribe reacted positively, saying "every positive proposition has to be welcomed."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The bid would also form part of the country's National Development Plan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> On 8 March 2015, Senator Jorge Espinoza called on President <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Santos" title="Juan Manuel Santos">Juan Manuel Santos</a> to begin a "diplomatic offensive" for the bidding process for their right to host the 2026 edition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Potential_bids">Potential bids</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2026_FIFA_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Potential bids">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AFC.2FOFC">AFC/OFC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2026_FIFA_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: AFC/OFC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>AFC member federations will be able to bid to host the 2026 World Cup only if none of the eligible candidates are able to fulfil the necessary criteria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap-cbc_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-ap-cbc-20">[20]</a></sup> OFC member federations do not face the same restriction, but it is not clear how joint bids involving both AFC and OFC countries would be received.</p>
<ul>
<li><span class="flagicon"><img alt="" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="1280" height="12" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b9/Flag_of_Australia.svg/23px-Flag_of_Australia.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b9/Flag_of_Australia.svg/35px-Flag_of_Australia.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b9/Flag_of_Australia.svg/46px-Flag_of_Australia.svg.png 2x" width="23"/> </span><a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <span class="flagicon"><img alt="" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="1200" height="12" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Flag_of_New_Zealand.svg/23px-Flag_of_New_Zealand.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Flag_of_New_Zealand.svg/35px-Flag_of_New_Zealand.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Flag_of_New_Zealand.svg/46px-Flag_of_New_Zealand.svg.png 2x" width="23"/> </span><a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>On 13 April 2015, former <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Cricket" title="New Zealand Cricket">New Zealand Cricket</a> CEO and head of the Tourism Industry Association NZ, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Snedden" title="Martin Snedden">Martin Snedden</a> proposed a possible New Zealand and Australia joint bid for either the 2026 or 2030 FIFA World Cup. Australia had also unsuccessfully <a href="/wiki/Australia_2022_FIFA_World_Cup_bid" title="Australia 2022 FIFA World Cup bid">bid</a> for the 2022 World Cup.</dd>
<dd>Snedden’s vision was for both the <a href="/wiki/Asian_Football_Confederation" title="Asian Football Confederation">Asian Football Confederation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oceania_Football_Confederation" title="Oceania Football Confederation">Oceania Football Confederation</a> working together to achieve the event. Snedden recognized there would be plenty of hurdles to leap. On the idea of a potential bid, New Zealand Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/John_Key" title="John Key">John Key</a> said the decision on such a bid would be "a long way away".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> However, <a href="/wiki/Football_Federation_Australia" title="Football Federation Australia">Football Federation Australia</a> promptly dismissed the idea of a joint bid, saying Australia was not currently interested in bidding. Nonetheless, conversations with the New Zealand government would continue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CAF">CAF</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2026_FIFA_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: CAF">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><span class="flagicon"><img alt="" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="900" height="15" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Flag_of_Morocco.svg/23px-Flag_of_Morocco.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Flag_of_Morocco.svg/35px-Flag_of_Morocco.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Flag_of_Morocco.svg/45px-Flag_of_Morocco.svg.png 2x" width="23"/> </span><a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Then minister of Youth and Sports, <a href="/wiki/Moncef_Belkhayat" title="Moncef Belkhayat">Moncef Belkhayat</a>, said to the French daily <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Figaro" title="Le Figaro">Le Figaro</a></i>: "The <a href="/wiki/2015_Africa_Cup_of_Nations" title="2015 Africa Cup of Nations">African Cup of Nations 2015</a> will be the first indicator of our ability to host a great event. Then we can confidently consider us as a candidate to host the World Cup 2026".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> However, in November 2014, Morocco refused to host the African Cup of Nations due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ebola_virus_epidemic_in_West_Africa" title="Ebola virus epidemic in West Africa">Ebola virus epidemic in West Africa</a>. Morocco lost bids to host the World Cup in <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1994 FIFA World Cup">1994</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1998 FIFA World Cup">1998</a>, <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006</a>, and <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010</a> to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> respectively. This makes Morocco the country to have launched the most bids without ever gaining the hosting rights.</dd>
</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting_rights">Broadcasting rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2026_FIFA_World_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Broadcasting rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><span class="flagicon"><img alt="" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="1000" height="12" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/cf/Flag_of_Canada.svg/23px-Flag_of_Canada.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/cf/Flag_of_Canada.svg/35px-Flag_of_Canada.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/cf/Flag_of_Canada.svg/46px-Flag_of_Canada.svg.png 2x" width="23"/> </span><a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> – <a href="/wiki/CTV_Television_Network" title="CTV Television Network">CTV</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Sports_Network" title="The Sports Network">TSN</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9seau_des_sports" title="Réseau des sports">RDS</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canadarights_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-canadarights-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cangloberights_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-cangloberights-53">[53]</a></sup></li>
<li><span class="flagicon"><img alt="" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="650" data-file-width="1235" height="12" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Flag_of_the_United_States.svg/23px-Flag_of_the_United_States.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Flag_of_the_United_States.svg/35px-Flag_of_the_United_States.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Flag_of_the_United_States.svg/46px-Flag_of_the_United_States.svg.png 2x" width="23"/> </span><a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> – <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telemundo" title="Telemundo">Telemundo</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foxrights_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-foxrights-54">[54]</a></sup></li>

<p>FIFA has come in for criticism for the way Fox was awarded the rights: there was no tender process, the network receiving the rights in order to placate it regarding the move of the 2022 World Cup (which it has the rights to) from summer to winter time, during the last few weeks of the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a> regular season. Due to the lack of a tender, FIFA lost revenue. According to the BBC's sports editor Dan Roan, "As ever, it seemed, FIFA was looking after itself."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fall-out_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fall-out-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
