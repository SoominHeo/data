<p><b>Macau Tower Convention &amp; Entertainment Centre</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">澳門旅遊塔會展娛樂中心</span>; <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <span lang="pt" xml:lang="pt"><i>Centro de Convenções e Entretenimento da Torre de Macau</i></span>), also known as <b>Macau Tower</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Tower" title="Tower">tower</a> located in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9,_Macau" title="Sé, Macau">Sé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The tower measures 338 m (1,109 ft) in height from ground level to the highest point. An observation deck with panoramic views, restaurants, theaters, shopping malls and the Skywalk X, a thrilling walking tour around the outer rim. It offers the best view of Macau and in recent years has been used for a variety of adventurous activities. At 233 meters, the Macau Tower's tethered "skyjump" and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bungee_jump" title="Bungee jump">Bungee jump</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_J_Hackett" title="A J Hackett">AJ Hackett</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> from the tower's outer rim, is the highest commercial skyjump in the world (233 meters), and the second highest commercial decelerator descent facility in the world, after Vegas' Stratosphere skyjump at 252 meters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The tower was created by the architecture firm of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Craig_Craig_Moller&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Craig Craig Moller (page does not exist)">Craig Craig Moller</a>.</p>
<p>The tower is one of the members of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=World_Federation_of_Great_Towers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="World Federation of Great Towers (page does not exist)">World Federation of Great Towers</a>. Besides being used for observation and entertainment, the <a href="/wiki/Radio_masts_and_towers" title="Radio masts and towers">tower</a> is also used for telecommunications and broadcasting.</p>

<p>On a visit to <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a>, New Zealand, Hong Kong casino billionaire <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Ho" title="Stanley Ho">Stanley Ho Hung-Sun</a> was so impressed by the <a href="/wiki/Sky_Tower_(Auckland)" title="Sky Tower (Auckland)">Sky Tower</a> in Auckland that he commissioned a similar one to be built in Macau. The tower was designed by New Zealand engineering firm <a href="/wiki/Beca_Group" title="Beca Group">Beca Group</a> and Gordon Moller of Craig Craig Moller architects for <a href="/wiki/Sociedade_de_Turismo_e_Divers%C3%B5es_de_Macau" title="Sociedade de Turismo e Diversões de Macau">Sociedade de Turismo e Diversões de Macau</a>. Construction work of the tower started in 1998, and the tower was officially opened on December 19, 2001.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macau_Tower&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On December 17, 2006, the father of contemporary <a href="/wiki/Bungee_jumping" title="Bungee jumping">bungee jumping</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_J_Hackett" title="A J Hackett">A J Hackett</a>, and popular artist <a href="/wiki/Edison_Chen" title="Edison Chen">Edison Chen</a> broke two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Record" title="Guinness World Record">Guinness World Records</a> at the Macau Tower. A J Hackett, broke his own Guinness World Record of "The Highest Bungee Jump from a Building" achieved in 1987 from the <a href="/wiki/Eiffel_Tower" title="Eiffel Tower">Eiffel Tower</a>. Edison Chen represented Macau Tower in the inaugural jump to bid for "The World's Highest Bungee Jump Facility".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macau_Tower&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jack_Osbourne" title="Jack Osbourne">Jack Osbourne</a> has bungee-jumped off the tower as well, as part of the third series of <i><a href="/wiki/Jack_Osbourne:_Adrenaline_Junkie" title="Jack Osbourne: Adrenaline Junkie">Jack Osbourne: Adrenaline Junkie</a></i>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Anthony_Bourdain" title="Anthony Bourdain">Anthony Bourdain</a> bungee-jumped from the top floor of the building in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Anthony_Bourdain:_No_Reservations" title="Anthony Bourdain: No Reservations">Anthony Bourdain: No Reservations</a></i>.</p>
<p>It served as the venue of photoshoot of Episode 10, <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Next_Top_Model" title="America's Next Top Model">America's Next Top Model</a></i> Cycle 18: British Invasion.</p>
<p>The tower was featured in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_An_Idiot_Abroad_episodes#ep19" title="List of An Idiot Abroad episodes">third episode</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/An_Idiot_Abroad" title="An Idiot Abroad">An Idiot Abroad 3</a></i>, where <a href="/wiki/Karl_Pilkington" title="Karl Pilkington">Karl Pilkington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warwick_Davis" title="Warwick Davis">Warwick Davis</a> perform the Skywalk which involves walking around the perimeter of one of the highest floors. Davis then proceeded to take a controlled descent to the ground.</p>
<p>It was used on the South Korean variety show <i><a href="/wiki/Running_Man_(TV_series)" title="Running Man (TV series)">Running Man</a></i> for episode 133. The cast (not including Kim Jongkook) and their guests, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Dong-wook" title="Lee Dong-wook">Lee Dong-wook</a> and <a href="/wiki/Han_Hye-jin" title="Han Hye-jin">Han Hye-jin</a>, were given three missions - sky jump, mast climb, and sky walk - to be completed on the tower to acquire the three characters they needed to continue onto the next mission.</p>
<p>The tower was also used as a <a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Race#Roadblock" title="The Amazing Race">"roadblock"</a> in an episode of US version <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Race_11" title="The Amazing Race 11">The Amazing Race: All-Stars</a></i> that originally aired on April 22, 2007, on <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> as well as a roadblock in the <a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Race_Canada_2" title="The Amazing Race Canada 2">Amazing Race Canada 2</a> in 2014. The tower also appeared in one roadblock each on two episodes of <a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Race_Asia_3" title="The Amazing Race Asia 3">The Amazing Race Asia 3</a>.</p>
<p>It was also used on the Chinese variety show <i><span class="ILHItem"><span class="ILHChinese"><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Up_Idol_(season_2)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Up Idol (season 2) (page does not exist)">Up Idol (season 2)</a></span><span class="ILHComment" style="display:none;">（<span style="font-size:smaller;"><span class="ILHLang">Chinese</span>：</span><span class="ILHOriginal" lang="zh" style="font-size:smaller;" xml:lang="zh">-{<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%88%91%E4%BB%AC%E6%9D%A5%E4%BA%86" title="zh:我们来了">我们来了</a>}-</span>）</span></span></i> for episode 1. The two team captains, <a href="/wiki/Wang_Han_(host)" title="Wang Han (host)">Wang Han</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuan_Hong_(actor)" title="Yuan Hong (actor)">Yuan Hong</a>, with their members, were given a mission - sky relay - to be completed on the tower as short a time as possible. Finally, Yuan Hong's team won the game, and Wang Han's team was given a punishment, <a href="/wiki/Karen_Mok" title="Karen Mok">Karen Mok</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ming_Xi" title="Ming Xi">Ming Xi</a> were going to bungee-jump off the tover. <a href="/wiki/Xie_Na" title="Xie Na">Xie Na</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Chen" title="Joe Chen">Joe Chen</a> replaced them to accept the punishment at last.</p>
