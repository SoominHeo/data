<p><b>Marcus Oliver Johnstone Mumford</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BMIDATA_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BMIDATA-1">[1]</a></sup> (born 31 January 1987) is an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a>-born <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> singer, songwriter, musician and producer best known as the lead singer of the band <a href="/wiki/Mumford_%26_Sons" title="Mumford &amp; Sons">Mumford &amp; Sons</a>. He also plays a number of instruments with the group, including guitar, drums and mandolin.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Mumford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marcus was born on 31 January 1987, in <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Orange County</a>, California,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-familysearch.org_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-familysearch.org-4">[4]</a></sup> to English parents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-5">[5]</a></sup> As a result, he has held both British and <a href="/wiki/United_States_nationality_law" title="United States nationality law">US</a> citizenship from birth. Mumford has an older brother, James.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph.co.uk_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph.co.uk-6">[6]</a></sup> His aunt is also an academic in law at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bristol" title="University of Bristol">University of Bristol</a>.</p>
<p>Mumford's family moved back to their native England when Marcus was six months old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-5">[5]</a></sup> He grew up in Wimbledon Chase, southwest London, and attended <a href="/wiki/King%27s_College_School" title="King's College School">King's College School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wimbledon,_London" title="Wimbledon, London">Wimbledon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> There he met future fellow band member <a href="/wiki/Ben_Lovett_(British_musician)" title="Ben Lovett (British musician)">Ben Lovett</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He returned to London to focus on his music career after his first year of study at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a>.</p>
<p>It was in Edinburgh that he penned most of Mumford &amp; Sons' debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Sigh_No_More_(Mumford_%26_Sons_album)" title="Sigh No More (Mumford &amp; Sons album)">Sigh No More</a></i>. He began his musical career playing drums for <a href="/wiki/Laura_Marling" title="Laura Marling">Laura Marling</a> on tour, along with the other current members of Mumford &amp; Sons. It was through touring with Marling and gaining experience playing gigs, as well as experimenting with his early writing that they decided to form the band in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-independent.co.uk_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-independent.co.uk-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mumford was included in the <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> 30 Under 30 list of music stars in December 2012 for his achievements with Mumford &amp; Sons. Forbes commented of the list which also included acts such as <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>: "In sum they represent the entrepreneurial, creative and intellectual best of their generation. Individually, they are engaging, surprising and incredibly hardworking."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mumford sang a <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover</a> of "<a href="/wiki/Dink%27s_Song" title="Dink's Song">Dink's Song</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Isaac" title="Oscar Isaac">Oscar Isaac</a> for the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Coen_brothers" title="Coen brothers">Coen brothers</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Inside_Llewyn_Davis" title="Inside Llewyn Davis">Inside Llewyn Davis</a></i>. Although Mumford is not seen in the film, he voices the protagonist's musical partner, Mike, who has committed suicide before the starting point of the film.</p>
<p>Mumford is featured on the 2014 collaborative album <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_on_the_River:_The_New_Basement_Tapes" title="Lost on the River: The New Basement Tapes">Lost on the River: The New Basement Tapes</a></i>, with other artists including <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Costello" title="Elvis Costello">Elvis Costello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhiannon_Giddens" title="Rhiannon Giddens">Rhiannon Giddens</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taylor_Goldsmith" title="Taylor Goldsmith">Taylor Goldsmith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_James" title="Jim James">Jim James</a>, Jay Bellerose, and <a href="/wiki/T_Bone_Burnett" title="T Bone Burnett">T Bone Burnett</a>. Mumford takes co-writing credits on tracks, "Kansas City," "When I Get My Hands on You," "The Whistle Is Blowing," "Stranger," and "Lost on the River #20."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 23 September 2014, the video for "When I Get My Hands on You" was released and features Mumford on lead vocals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band's third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Wilder_Mind" title="Wilder Mind">Wilder Mind</a>,</i> was released on 4 May, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_work">Production work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Mumford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2014 Mumford produced <i>Hold Fast</i> by Christian Letts (<a href="/wiki/Edward_Sharpe_and_the_Magnetic_Zeros" title="Edward Sharpe and the Magnetic Zeros">Edward Sharpe and the Magnetic Zeros</a>), released February 2015. Mumford co-wrote 4 tracks from the record - "Copper Bells", "La Mer", "Emeralds" and "Matches".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mumford produced the album <i>Gamble for a Rose</i> by <a href="/wiki/King_Charles_(musician)" title="King Charles (musician)">King Charles</a>, released January 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Mumford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mumford's parents, John and Eleanor (née Weir-Breen),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> are former national leaders of the <a href="/wiki/Vineyard_Churches_UK_and_Ireland" title="Vineyard Churches UK and Ireland">Vineyard Church in the UK and Ireland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mumford formerly dated <a href="/wiki/Laura_Marling" title="Laura Marling">Laura Marling</a> from whom he separated in late 2010. In April 2012 Mumford married English actress <a href="/wiki/Carey_Mulligan" title="Carey Mulligan">Carey Mulligan</a> in Somerset, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> They were childhood <a href="/wiki/Pen_pal" title="Pen pal">pen pals</a> who lost touch and reconnected as adults. They married a few weeks after wrapping production on the Coen brothers film <i>Inside Llewyn Davis</i>, in which they were both involved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> They had a daughter, named Evelyn Grace Mumford, in September 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
