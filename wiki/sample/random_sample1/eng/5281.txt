<p><b>Mark Nicholas Davies</b> (born 18 February 1988) is an English <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Davies_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wolverhampton_Wanderers">Wolverhampton Wanderers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Davies_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Wolverhampton Wanderers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Willenhall" title="Willenhall">Willenhall</a>, Davies was a former student of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pool_Hayes_Arts_and_Community_School" title="Pool Hayes Arts and Community School">Pool Hayes Community School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Willenhall" title="Willenhall">Willenhall</a>. He joined <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> Youth Academy at the age of nine. He signed his first professional contract at Wolves in February 2005, aged 17.</p>
<p>In the 2005–06 season, Davies broke into the Wolves first team under then manager <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Hoddle" title="Glenn Hoddle">Glenn Hoddle</a>, making his league debut as a substitute against <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a> on 20 August 2005, losing the game 2–0 at <a href="/wiki/Elland_Road" title="Elland Road">Elland Road</a>. He made his first league start for Wolves against <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> on 20 September 2005, losing the game 2–1. Davies scored his first professional league goal against <a href="/wiki/Luton_Town_F.C." title="Luton Town F.C.">Luton Town</a> on 13 January 2006, winning the game 2–1 at <a href="/wiki/Molineux_Stadium" title="Molineux Stadium">Molineux</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He went on to frequently feature for Wolves throughout the season, and becoming a vital member of the team, making 20 appearances for Wolves in his first season. Former Wolves Academy manager <a href="/wiki/Chris_Evans_(footballer)" title="Chris Evans (footballer)">Chris Evans</a> said Davies was the best prospect to come out the youth system since players <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Keane" title="Robbie Keane">Robbie Keane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joleon_Lescott" title="Joleon Lescott">Joleon Lescott</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The start of the 2006–07 season was a blow to Davies as he suffered a cartilage problem in pre-season. However, he returned to fitness in January 2007, making 10 substitute appearances for Wolves, and also went on to feature for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-19_football_team" title="England national under-19 football team">England U-19’s</a>.</p>
<p>In the 2007–08 season, Davies picked up a shoulder injury and other niggling injuries which kept him out for the whole season, limiting him to only a few reserve outings.</p>
<p>The 2008–09 season started brightly for Davies as he had a successful pre-season for Wolves. His first and final game for Wolves ended with him coming off the bench to assist and score the winning goal against <a href="/wiki/Accrington_Stanley_F.C." title="Accrington Stanley F.C.">Accrington Stanley</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a>, winning the game 3–2 at Molineux. This was Davies last appearance for Wolves as on 27 November 2008 he joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a> club <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> on a one-month <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan</a> deal.</p>
<p>In late January 2009, Davies handed in a transfer request at Wolves. Wolves did offer him a new three-and-a-half year contract, but he declined the offer. Wolves went on to receive a bid from League One side Leicester City, for around £650,000, but this was declined by the club. Wolves then later received another bid from <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a>, believed to be in the region of £1m, rising to £2m, and accepted the offer, with Wolves adding a hefty sell on clause in part of the deal. Davies went on to sign for Bolton Wanderers on 26 January 2009, ending his ten-year stay with the club.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_spell_at_Leicester_City">Loan spell at Leicester City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Davies_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Loan spell at Leicester City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 27 November 2008, Davies joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a> side <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> on a one-month loan deal in order to gain full match fitness after almost two years on the sidelines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_Deadline_Day_Deal_Done_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Davies_Deadline_Day_Deal_Done-4">[4]</a></sup> He made his debut for Leicester as a substitute against <a href="/wiki/Southend_United_F.C." title="Southend United F.C.">Southend United</a> on 6 December 2008, winning the game 3–0 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walkers_Stadium" title="Walkers Stadium">Walkers Stadium</a>. He made his first start for Leicester against <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_United_F.C." title="Peterborough United F.C.">Peterborough United</a> on 20 December 2008, thrashing them 4–0 at the Walkers Stadium. He scored his only goal for the club against <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a> on 10 January 2009, as Leicester won 3-0 at the Walkers Stadium. Davies impressed during his short loan spell, causing Leicester to extend it to 1 February 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bolton_Wanderers">Bolton Wanderers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Davies_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bolton Wanderers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 26 January 2009, Davies joined <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a> on a four-and-a-half-year contract, for an undisclosed fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He extended his contract by another year on 15 February 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He made his debut two days after signing when he came on as a substitute in the 2–2 draw with local rivals <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> at <a href="/wiki/Ewood_Park" title="Ewood Park">Ewood Park</a>. He made his first start for his new club three days later against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> on the 31 January 2009, winning the game 3–2 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reebok_Stadium" title="Reebok Stadium">Reebok Stadium</a>. He scored his first goal for Bolton at the start of the 2009–10 season, in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/Tranmere_Rovers_F.C." title="Tranmere Rovers F.C.">Tranmere Rovers</a> on the 25 August 2009, winning the game 1–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He scored his second goal for the club in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_City_F.C." title="Lincoln City F.C.">Lincoln City</a> on 2 January 2010, winning the game 4–0 at the Reebok Stadium. Davies scored his first league goal for Bolton against <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a> on 27 November 2010, earning his side a 2–2 draw at the Reebok Stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> This goal was voted the club's goal of the 2010–11 season at the official Player of the Year dinner, beating <a href="/wiki/Johan_Elmander" title="Johan Elmander">Johan Elmander</a>'s individual effort against Wolverhampton Wanderers.</p>
<p>Davies his first goal of the 2011–12 season at <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> on 20 December as Bolton won 2–1. His second came in a 3–1 home win against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> on 21 January 2012. He also scored on 11 February in the 2–1 loss to <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> with a half volley from twenty yards out. He scored on the final day of the 2011–12 season, 13 May, in a 2–2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> but the result wasn't enough to keep Bolton in the Premier League as they needed a win to stay up.</p>
<p>On 23 October 2012, in an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> at Molineux, Davies scored a 90th-minute equaliser against his former club with a left-foot volley from the edge of the box into the far top corner to earn Bolton a 2–2 draw.</p>
<p>On 27 February 2013, Davies signed a new contract with Bolton, keeping at the club until 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Two days later Davies was ruled out with injury for up to ten months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> His return came as he came on as a late substitute against <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan</a> ten months later in December 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He missed the final two months of that season with injury, as well as all of pre season and the first month of the following season. In November 2014, Davies was again injured, this time for up to four months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Davies_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_teams">Youth teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Davies_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Youth teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Davies represented <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-17_football_team" title="England national under-17 football team">England U17's</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="2004 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship</a> in 2004. He scored two goals for his country, his first against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_under-17_football_team" title="Portugal national under-17 football team">Portugal U17's</a> in the group stages, and his second against Portugal in the Third-Place Play-Off, drawing the game 4–4, but losing on penalties. Davies captained the England U17's team in the UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship Qualifier against <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national football team">Northern Ireland U17's</a> at <a href="/wiki/Molineux_Stadium" title="Molineux Stadium">Molineux</a> in March 2005. He also went on to represent the England U17's in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_UEFA_European_Under-17_Football_Championship" title="2005 UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship">UEFA European Under-17 Football Championship</a> in May 2005, but they did not make it out of the group stages.</p>
<p>Davies was called up to the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England U21's</a> squad to face <a href="/wiki/Ecuador_national_football_team" title="Ecuador national football team">Ecuador U21's</a> on 16 February 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but had to pull out due to the ankle injury he picked up against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On 14 March 2011, Davies received his second call up to the England Under 21's,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> but pulled out due to injury. He received a third call up, for the England Under-21 Championships provisional squad,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> but once again pulled out due to the same injury from his second call up and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jay_Spearing" title="Jay Spearing">Jay Spearing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Davies_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His younger brother <a href="/wiki/Richard_Davies_(footballer)" title="Richard Davies (footballer)">Richard Davies</a> is a professional footballer and currently plays for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conference_National" title="Conference National">Conference National</a> side <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Telford_United" title="A.F.C. Telford United">AFC Telford United</a>.</p>
