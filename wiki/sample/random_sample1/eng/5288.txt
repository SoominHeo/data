<p><b>Mark Allen</b> (born 22 February 1986) is a <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Irish</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Snooker" title="Snooker">snooker</a> player. He won the <a href="/wiki/IBSF_World_Snooker_Championship" title="IBSF World Snooker Championship">World Amateur Championship</a> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-World_Amateur_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-World_Amateur-11">[11]</a></sup> The following year he entered the Main Tour and took only three seasons to reach the elite top 16. As a prolific break-builder, Allen has compiled more than 300 <a href="/wiki/Century_break" title="Century break">century breaks</a> in professional competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-centurybreaks_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-centurybreaks-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Allen has reached five ranking semi-finals: the <a href="/wiki/2007_Northern_Ireland_Trophy" title="2007 Northern Ireland Trophy">2007 Northern Ireland Trophy</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2008_Bahrain_Championship" title="2008 Bahrain Championship">2008 Bahrain Championship</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2009 World Snooker Championship">2009 World Championship</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2010_China_Open_(snooker)" title="2010 China Open (snooker)">2010 China Open</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2010_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2010 UK Championship (snooker)">2010 UK Championship</a>. In June 2009, he won his first professional title, the invitational <a href="/wiki/2009_Jiangsu_Classic" title="2009 Jiangsu Classic">Jiangsu Classic</a> in China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-First_Title_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-First_Title-12">[12]</a></sup> He reached his first ranking event final at the <a href="/wiki/2011_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2011 UK Championship (snooker)">2011 UK Championship</a>.</p>
<p>A left-hander, Allen's highest break is 147 the maximum points a snooker player can score, which he has achieved in the <a href="/wiki/2016_UK_Championship" title="2016 UK Championship">2016 UK Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-World_Snooker_.282016.2C_Mark_Allen.29_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-World_Snooker_.282016.2C_Mark_Allen.29-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_history">Career history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Allen_(snooker_player)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Allen_(snooker_player)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He began his professional career by playing <a href="/wiki/Challenge_Tour_(snooker)" title="Challenge Tour (snooker)">Challenge Tour</a> in 2003, at the time the second-level professional tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Before entering Main Tour for the <a href="/wiki/Snooker_season_2005/2006" title="Snooker season 2005/2006">2005/2006 season</a>, Allen won the European Championship and the IBSF World Championship, plus Northern Ireland Championship at under-14, under-16, and under-19 levels. His early career was aided by <a href="/wiki/National_Lottery_(United_Kingdom)" title="National Lottery (United Kingdom)">National Lottery</a> funding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>By chance, an invitational Northern Ireland Trophy was staged shortly after Allen turned professional.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Northern_Ireland_Trophy_2005_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Northern_Ireland_Trophy_2005-16">[16]</a></sup> As a local player, he was invited and made an immediate impact, defeating <a href="/wiki/Steve_Davis" title="Steve Davis">Steve Davis</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Higgins_(snooker_player)" title="John Higgins (snooker player)">John Higgins</a> to reach the quarter-finals, before losing to <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Hendry" title="Stephen Hendry">Stephen Hendry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Northern_Ireland_Trophy_2005_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Northern_Ireland_Trophy_2005-16">[16]</a></sup> In his first year on the tour, he reached the last 32 of the <a href="/wiki/2005_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2005 UK Championship (snooker)">2005 UK Championship</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2006_Welsh_Open_(snooker)" title="2006 Welsh Open (snooker)">2006 Welsh Open</a>, losing 2–5 to the then World Champion <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Murphy_(snooker_player)" title="Shaun Murphy (snooker player)">Shaun Murphy</a> after leading 2–0. He also got to the final qualifying round of the <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2006 World Snooker Championship">2006 World Championship</a>, losing 7–10 to <a href="/wiki/Andy_Hicks" title="Andy Hicks">Andy Hicks</a>, after leading 7–4.</p>
<p>In March 2007 he qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2007_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2007 World Snooker Championship">World Championship</a> for the first time, winning 3 matches culminating in a 10–4 win over <a href="/wiki/Robert_Milkins" title="Robert Milkins">Robert Milkins</a>. In April 2007 he beat former world champion <a href="/wiki/Ken_Doherty" title="Ken Doherty">Ken Doherty</a> 10–7 in the first round held at the <a href="/wiki/Crucible_Theatre" title="Crucible Theatre">Crucible Theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> but lost to <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Stevens" title="Matthew Stevens">Matthew Stevens</a> 9–13 in the second round. This was his first run to the last 16 of a tournament and helped him into the top 32 of the rankings (at no. 29).</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2007_Grand_Prix_(snooker)" title="2007 Grand Prix (snooker)">2007 Grand Prix</a>, in a match with <a href="/wiki/Ken_Doherty" title="Ken Doherty">Ken Doherty</a>, he was involved in an incident that led to his opponent branding him 'a disgrace'. Unhappy that the black would not go back on its spot after potting it, Allen struck the side cushion with his fist. The referee told him that he would be warned if he did that again. Allen was not warned, and ended up winning the match. Doherty said, "He was a disgrace. For such a relative newcomer to the pro game, he's got a serious attitude problem". Mark later commented: "It was entirely my own fault and if there are going to be any repercussions then so be it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Allen did not reach the knockout stage of the tournament.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2007_Northern_Ireland_Trophy" title="2007 Northern Ireland Trophy">2007 Northern Ireland Trophy</a> he gave his home supporters plenty to shout about, by beating <a href="/wiki/Graeme_Dott" title="Graeme Dott">Graeme Dott</a> 5–3 and <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Day" title="Ryan Day">Ryan Day</a> 5–3 to reach his first ever quarter-final. In it he defeated <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Greene" title="Gerard Greene">Gerard Greene</a> 5–3 to reach the semi-final, where he lost 3–6 to <a href="/wiki/Fergal_O%27Brien" title="Fergal O'Brien">Fergal O'Brien</a>. In the following <a href="/wiki/2007_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2007 UK Championship (snooker)">UK Championship</a> he defeated Stephen Hendry in the last 32. He opened his last-16 match against <a href="/wiki/Mark_Williams_(snooker_player)" title="Mark Williams (snooker player)">Mark Williams</a> with two centuries in the first three frames, building a 5–1 lead, but Williams fought back and he lost 5–9. He then reached the quarter-finals in the <a href="/wiki/2008_China_Open_(snooker)" title="2008 China Open (snooker)">2008 China Open</a> before losing to Shaun Murphy. At the <a href="/wiki/2008_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2008 World Snooker Championship">2008 World Championship</a> he led Stephen Hendry 6–3, 7–4 and 9–7 before losing 9–10. However, first-round defeats for all his rivals for a top 16 place ensured that he finished the season at number 16 in the rankings.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.932011_season">2009–2011 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Allen_(snooker_player)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–2011 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a consistent season, Allen was back at the Crucible the following year where he beat <a href="/wiki/Martin_Gould" title="Martin Gould">Martin Gould</a> in his opening match. He faced <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_O%27Sullivan" title="Ronnie O'Sullivan">Ronnie O'Sullivan</a> in the second round and confident and unaffected by his opponent's reputation, Allen beat the defending champion, 13–11 for a quarter-final place in the <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2009 World Snooker Championship">2009 World Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He then defeated Ryan Day by the same scoreline to reach the semi-finals,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> where he lost 13–17 to John Higgins, despite making a determined fightback from 3–13 down.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Soon after his run in the World Championship, Allen won his first professional tournament, the <a href="/wiki/2009_Jiangsu_Classic" title="2009 Jiangsu Classic">2009 Jiangsu Classic</a> beating home favourite <a href="/wiki/Ding_Junhui" title="Ding Junhui">Ding Junhui</a> 6–0 in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen_cruises_to_first_pro_win_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allen_cruises_to_first_pro_win-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2010_Masters_(snooker)" title="2010 Masters (snooker)">2010 Masters</a>, Allen beat the World Champion, John Higgins, by 6–3 in the last 16. He lost out 5–6 to eventual champion Mark Selby in the quarter-finals.</p>
<p>On the first day of the <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2010 World Snooker Championship">2010 World Championship</a> on 17 April 2010, Allen came close to recording his first ever maximum break in his first round match against <a href="/wiki/Tom_Ford_(snooker_player)" title="Tom Ford (snooker player)">Tom Ford</a> after potting 15 reds with blacks, and the yellow, before breaking down on the green.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Five days later, Allen made the first 146 break in <a href="/wiki/World_Snooker_Championship" title="World Snooker Championship">World Championship</a> history, and the second of his career after defeating <a href="/wiki/Mark_Davis_(snooker_player)" title="Mark Davis (snooker player)">Mark Davis</a> 13–5 in the second round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He was defeated 12–13 in the quarter-final by <a href="/wiki/Graeme_Dott" title="Graeme Dott">Graeme Dott</a>, having led 12–10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2010_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2010 UK Championship (snooker)">2010 UK Championship</a>, Allen reached the semi-finals for the first time, where he was beaten 9–5 by eventual champion <a href="/wiki/John_Higgins_(snooker_player)" title="John Higgins (snooker player)">John Higgins</a>. In the Masters, Allen again reached the semi-finals, and led <a href="/wiki/Marco_Fu" title="Marco Fu">Marco Fu</a> 4–1 before Fu reeled off five frames in a row to win 6–4. At the 2011 World Championship, Allen entered as the 11th seed and played <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Stevens" title="Matthew Stevens">Matthew Stevens</a> in the first round, recovering from 9–6 down and seeing Stevens miss a pot on the final pink to win the match 10–7, before winning 10–9. In the second round, Allen defeated <a href="/wiki/Barry_Hawkins" title="Barry Hawkins">Barry Hawkins</a> 13–12. He reached the quarter-finals for the second year in a row but lost to Mark Williams 5–13.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.2F2012_season">2011/2012 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Allen_(snooker_player)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011/2012 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mark_Allen_PHC_2012-3.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1564" data-file-width="2284" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Mark_Allen_PHC_2012-3.jpg/220px-Mark_Allen_PHC_2012-3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Mark_Allen_PHC_2012-3.jpg/330px-Mark_Allen_PHC_2012-3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Mark_Allen_PHC_2012-3.jpg/440px-Mark_Allen_PHC_2012-3.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>The season began with Allen ranked <a href="/wiki/Snooker_world_rankings_2011/2012" title="Snooker world rankings 2011/2012">world number 12</a> and he began it at the inaugural <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Goldfields_Open" title="2011 Australian Goldfields Open">Australian Goldfields Open</a>, where he beat <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Day" title="Ryan Day">Ryan Day</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Campbell" title="Marcus Campbell">Marcus Campbell</a>. Into the quarter finals, Allen was beaten by his rival <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Bingham" title="Stuart Bingham">Stuart Bingham</a> 5–3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-26">[26]</a></sup> The next ranking event was the <a href="/wiki/2011_Shanghai_Masters" title="2011 Shanghai Masters">Shanghai Masters</a> where Allen reached the second round and held a 4–2 advantage over <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Murphy_(snooker_player)" title="Shaun Murphy (snooker player)">Shaun Murphy</a> before losing the next 3 frames and being edged out of the match 5–4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> His steady start to the season meant that he maintained his world ranking of 12 after the <a href="/wiki/Snooker_world_ranking_points_2011/2012" title="Snooker world ranking points 2011/2012">first cut-off point</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Allen made it to his first ranking event final at the <a href="/wiki/2011_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2011 UK Championship (snooker)">2011 UK Championship</a> by beating <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Gunnell" title="Adrian Gunnell">Adrian Gunnell</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allister_Carter" title="Allister Carter">Ali Carter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marco_Fu" title="Marco Fu">Marco Fu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Walden" title="Ricky Walden">Ricky Walden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011.2F12_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-2011.2F12-29">[29]</a></sup> It was his first success in a ranking event semi-final, after having lost in all five prior attempts. In the final he played <a href="/wiki/Judd_Trump" title="Judd Trump">Judd Trump</a> with whom he held a 2–1 advantage in the previous meetings between the pair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Allen opened up a 3–1 lead early in the best of 19 frames match, but subsequently lost the next seven frames to trail 3–8. However, such a deficit brought out the best in Allen as he won five of the next six frames, which included three centuries. The comeback was not quite completed though, as Trump secured the frame he required to take an 8–10 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Allen said after the final, "I knew it was going to be hard the way he was playing, he scores so heavy and so quickly and I didn't feel I was playing too bad but Judd played so well and it was hard to compete."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Allen made five centuries during the tournament, the most of anyone in the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Allen lost the last four frames in the first round of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Masters_(snooker)" title="2012 Masters (snooker)">Masters</a> to <a href="/wiki/Neil_Robertson_(snooker_player)" title="Neil Robertson (snooker player)">Neil Robertson</a> having led 3–2 and stated afterwards that he had "completely lost interest" in the match. He accused the Australian of employing slow tactics and said that at times he didn't want to watch him play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> He then exited the <a href="/wiki/2012_German_Masters" title="2012 German Masters">German Masters</a> in the second round and lost to <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Murphy_(snooker_player)" title="Shaun Murphy (snooker player)">Shaun Murphy</a> in the quarter-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Welsh_Open_(snooker)" title="2012 Welsh Open (snooker)">Welsh Open</a>.</p>
<p>In March, Allen won his first ever ranking event as he captured the <a href="/wiki/2012_World_Open_(snooker)" title="2012 World Open (snooker)">World Open</a> title in <a href="/wiki/Haikou" title="Haikou">Haikou</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. He beat qualifier <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Robertson_(snooker_player)" title="Jimmy Robertson (snooker player)">Jimmy Robertson</a> in the first round, before exacting revenge over Judd Trump for his defeat in York, by coming back from 0–3 down to triumph 5–4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> He comfortably beat <a href="/wiki/Mark_King_(snooker_player)" title="Mark King (snooker player)">Mark King</a> 5–1 in the quarter-finals, before producing another comeback from 2–5 down against world number 1 <a href="/wiki/Mark_Selby" title="Mark Selby">Mark Selby</a> to win 6–5 and reach his second ranking final of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Allen played <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Lee" title="Stephen Lee">Stephen Lee</a> in the final and dominated the encounter from start to finish as he won by 10 frames to 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> His season finished in disappointment though, as he exited both the <a href="/wiki/2012_China_Open_(snooker)" title="2012 China Open (snooker)">China Open</a> and <a href="/wiki/2012_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2012 World Snooker Championship">World Championship</a> in the first round,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011.2F12_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-2011.2F12-29">[29]</a></sup> to end the year where he started it, ranked world number 12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.2F2013_season">2012/2013 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Allen_(snooker_player)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012/2013 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Allen began the <a href="/wiki/Snooker_season_2012/2013" title="Snooker season 2012/2013">season</a> with second round losses to <a href="/wiki/Mark_Williams_(snooker_player)" title="Mark Williams (snooker player)">Mark Williams</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Wuxi_Classic" title="2012 Wuxi Classic">Wuxi Classic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Judd_Trump" title="Judd Trump">Judd Trump</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Shanghai_Masters" title="2012 Shanghai Masters">Shanghai Masters</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012.2F13_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-2012.2F13-39">[39]</a></sup> His first title of the year soon followed at the minor-ranking <a href="/wiki/European_Tour_2012/2013_%E2%80%93_Event_3" title="European Tour 2012/2013 – Event 3">Antwerp Open</a>, by making three centuries in a 4–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Mark_Selby" title="Mark Selby">Mark Selby</a> in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Allen then beat <a href="/wiki/Robert_Milkins" title="Robert Milkins">Robert Milkins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cao_Yupeng" title="Cao Yupeng">Cao Yupeng</a> both 6–2 in the inaugural <a href="/wiki/2012_International_Championship" title="2012 International Championship">International Championship</a>, before being edged out 5–6 by Trump in the quarter-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> Before his first round match with <a href="/wiki/Marco_Fu" title="Marco Fu">Marco Fu</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2012_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2012 UK Championship (snooker)">UK Championship</a>, Allen reiterated his claims that Fu has cheated in the past. Fu denied the accusations and went on to beat Allen 6–3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2013_Masters_(snooker)" title="2013 Masters (snooker)">Masters</a> Allen came past <a href="/wiki/Mark_Davis_(snooker_player)" title="Mark Davis (snooker player)">Mark Davis</a> 6–2, but was then narrowly beaten 5–6 by <a href="/wiki/Neil_Robertson_(snooker_player)" title="Neil Robertson (snooker player)">Neil Robertson</a> in a high quality encounter in the quarter-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2013_Snooker_Shoot-Out" title="2013 Snooker Shoot-Out">Snooker Shoot-Out</a>, the tournament where each match is decided by a 10-minute frame, Allen won through to the final where he lost to <a href="/wiki/Martin_Gould" title="Martin Gould">Martin Gould</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012.2F13_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-2012.2F13-39">[39]</a></sup> He suffered successive second round defeats at the <a href="/wiki/2013_German_Masters" title="2013 German Masters">German Masters</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2013_Welsh_Open_(snooker)" title="2013 Welsh Open (snooker)">Welsh Open</a> to <a href="/wiki/Barry_Hawkins" title="Barry Hawkins">Barry Hawkins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ding_Junhui" title="Ding Junhui">Ding Junhui</a> respectively, before he travelled to China in an attempt to defend his <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Open_(snooker)" title="2013 World Open (snooker)">World Open</a> title from 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012.2F13_39-2"><a href="#cite_note-2012.2F13-39">[39]</a></sup> Allen comfortably won every match he played at the event as he beat <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Day" title="Ryan Day">Ryan Day</a> 5–2, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Milkins" title="Robert Milkins">Robert Milkins</a> 5–2, <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Walden" title="Ricky Walden">Ricky Walden</a> 5–1, <a href="/wiki/John_Higgins_(snooker_player)" title="John Higgins (snooker player)">John Higgins</a> 6–2 and <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Stevens" title="Matthew Stevens">Matthew Stevens</a> 10–4 in the final to capture his second ranking title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Allen's Antwerp Open win from earlier in the season helped him finish eighth on the <a href="/wiki/Players_Tour_Championship_2012/2013" title="Players Tour Championship 2012/2013">Players Tour Championship</a> Order of Merit to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/Players_Tour_Championship_2012/2013_%E2%80%93_Finals" title="Players Tour Championship 2012/2013 – Finals">Finals</a> where he lost 3–4 in the quarter-finals to Ding, who made a 147 and two further centuries during the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Robertson beat Allen 5–1 in the second round of the <a href="/wiki/2013_China_Open_(snooker)" title="2013 China Open (snooker)">China Open</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012.2F13_39-3"><a href="#cite_note-2012.2F13-39">[39]</a></sup> and then Allen was the victim of a first round shock at the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2013 World Snooker Championship">World Championship</a> for a second year in a row as he lost to world number 30 <a href="/wiki/Mark_King_(snooker_player)" title="Mark King (snooker player)">Mark King</a> 8–10, having led 8–6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Despite this, he climbed five spots in the rankings during the year to finish it ranked world number seven.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.2F2014_season">2013/2014 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Allen_(snooker_player)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2013/2014 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>After losing in the first round of the opening two ranking events of the year, Allen won the minor-ranking <a href="/wiki/European_Tour_2013/2014_%E2%80%93_Event_5" title="European Tour 2013/2014 – Event 5">Ruhr Open</a> in <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BClheim" title="Mülheim">Mülheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, by beating <a href="/wiki/Ding_Junhui" title="Ding Junhui">Ding Junhui</a> 4–1 in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2013.2F14_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-2013.2F14-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> He also won the next European Tour event, the <a href="/wiki/European_Tour_2013/2014_%E2%80%93_Event_6" title="European Tour 2013/2014 – Event 6">Kay Suzanne Memorial Cup</a> by battling past former world champions <a href="/wiki/Neil_Robertson_(snooker_player)" title="Neil Robertson (snooker player)">Neil Robertson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Graeme_Dott" title="Graeme Dott">Graeme Dott</a> in the quarter-finals and semis respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2013.2F14_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-2013.2F14-48">[48]</a></sup> He beat <a href="/wiki/Judd_Trump" title="Judd Trump">Judd Trump</a> 4–1 in the final to become the first player to win back-to-back events since they were introduced in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In the last 16 of the <a href="/wiki/2013_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2013 UK Championship (snooker)">UK Championship</a>, Allen and <a href="/wiki/Judd_Trump" title="Judd Trump">Judd Trump</a> both struggled for consistency during their match, with Allen giving Trump a hug when the pair missed a succession of easy balls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Allen went on to win 6–4 to reach his first major quarter-final of the season, where he lost 6–2 to <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Walden" title="Ricky Walden">Ricky Walden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> Allen came close to recording a hat-trick of <a href="/wiki/2014_World_Open_(snooker)" title="2014 World Open (snooker)">World Open</a> titles as he won through to the semi-finals, but was beaten 6–4 by <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Murphy_(snooker_player)" title="Shaun Murphy (snooker player)">Shaun Murphy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> His two titles earlier in the season meant Allen was the number one seed for the <a href="/wiki/Players_Tour_Championship_2013/2014_%E2%80%93_Finals" title="Players Tour Championship 2013/2014 – Finals">PTC Finals</a> where he lost in the quarter-finals 4–2 against <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Greene" title="Gerard Greene">Gerard Greene</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Allen was 9–7 behind against <a href="/wiki/Neil_Robertson_(snooker_player)" title="Neil Robertson (snooker player)">Neil Robertson</a> after the second session of his last 16 match at the <a href="/wiki/2014_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2014 World Snooker Championship">World Championship</a> and lost all four frames in the next session to be defeated 13–7. Afterwards, Allen tipped Robertson for the title saying he had faced perfect snooker from the Australian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>

<p>In August 2014, he reached the final of the <a href="/wiki/European_Tour_2014/2015_%E2%80%93_Event_1" title="European Tour 2014/2015 – Event 1">Riga Open</a> but lost 4–3 to <a href="/wiki/Mark_Selby" title="Mark Selby">Mark Selby</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> Two weeks later Allen won the <a href="/wiki/European_Tour_2014/2015_%E2%80%93_Event_2" title="European Tour 2014/2015 – Event 2">Paul Hunter Classic</a> defeating <a href="/wiki/Judd_Trump" title="Judd Trump">Judd Trump</a> 4–2 in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> He advanced to the final of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Shanghai_Masters_(snooker)" title="2014 Shanghai Masters (snooker)">Shanghai Masters</a> where he was beaten 10–3 by <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Bingham" title="Stuart Bingham">Stuart Bingham</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2014_International_Championship" title="2014 International Championship">International Championship</a>, Allen led <a href="/wiki/Mark_Williams_(snooker_player)" title="Mark Williams (snooker player)">Mark Williams</a> 7–4 by producing some of his best snooker which included a total of eight breaks above 50. However, Williams fought back to lead 8–7 before Allen came from 71–0 down and requiring two snookers to steal the next frame 73–71. Williams missed a tricky final red in the decider to allow Allen in to reach the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> He faced <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Walden" title="Ricky Walden">Ricky Walden</a> with there never being more than two frames between the players until from 7–7 Walden raced away to take the final three frames and condemn Allen to a second ranking event final defeat of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Despite taking a 3–0 lead over <a href="/wiki/Rod_Lawler" title="Rod Lawler">Rod Lawler</a> with two centuries, Allen was knocked out 6–4 in the third round of the <a href="/wiki/2014_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2014 UK Championship (snooker)">UK Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>He eliminated <a href="/wiki/John_Higgins_(snooker_player)" title="John Higgins (snooker player)">John Higgins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Perry_(snooker_player)" title="Joe Perry (snooker player)">Joe Perry</a> both 6–4 to play in the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Masters_(snooker)" title="2015 Masters (snooker)">Masters</a> for the second time. Allen won the opening two frames against <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Murphy_(snooker_player)" title="Shaun Murphy (snooker player)">Shaun Murphy</a>, but then lost six frames in a row.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> The rest of the season would prove to be disappointing for Allen as he couldn't advance beyond the last 16 of any event and after he lost the last five frames against <a href="/wiki/Barry_Hawkins" title="Barry Hawkins">Barry Hawkins</a> in the second round of the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2015 World Snooker Championship">World Championship</a> to be beaten 13–11, he stated that the match had summed up his year as he was great in patches but overall came up short. He had not been coached by <a href="/wiki/Terry_Griffiths" title="Terry Griffiths">Terry Griffiths</a> this season and Allen said that he was hopeful his form would improve next year by working with him again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.2F2016_season">2015/2016 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Allen_(snooker_player)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2015/2016 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Allen reached the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Shanghai_Masters" title="2015 Shanghai Masters">Shanghai Masters</a> with a 5–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Mark_Davis_(snooker_player)" title="Mark Davis (snooker player)">Mark Davis</a>, but was beaten 6–1 by <a href="/wiki/Kyren_Wilson" title="Kyren Wilson">Kyren Wilson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> In the quarter-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2015_International_Championship" title="2015 International Championship">International Championship</a> he compiled a 118 break to send the match into a deciding frame, but <a href="/wiki/Thepchaiya_Un-Nooh" title="Thepchaiya Un-Nooh">Thepchaiya Un-Nooh</a> won it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Allen swept to the final of the <a href="/wiki/European_Tour_2015/2016_%E2%80%93_Event_4" title="European Tour 2015/2016 – Event 4">Bulgarian Open</a> by whitewashing <a href="/wiki/Mark_Williams_(snooker_player)" title="Mark Williams (snooker player)">Mark Williams</a> 4–0 and did not drop a frame against <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Day" title="Ryan Day">Ryan Day</a> either as he took home the title, his first in 15 months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> The win qualified Allen for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Champion_of_Champions" title="2015 Champion of Champions">Champion of Champions</a> and he knocked out <a href="/wiki/Barry_Hawkins" title="Barry Hawkins">Barry Hawkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Maguire" title="Stephen Maguire">Stephen Maguire</a> and Wilson to reach another final, where he lost 10–5 to <a href="/wiki/Neil_Robertson_(snooker_player)" title="Neil Robertson (snooker player)">Neil Robertson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> Robertson was also the victor when the pair met in the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Welsh_Open_(snooker)" title="2016 Welsh Open (snooker)">Welsh Open</a>, this time 6–4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p>Allen came from 4–2 down against <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Murphy_(snooker_player)" title="Shaun Murphy (snooker player)">Shaun Murphy</a> to win 6–4 and reach his first ranking event final of the season at the <a href="/wiki/Players_Tour_Championship_2015/2016_%E2%80%93_Finals" title="Players Tour Championship 2015/2016 – Finals">PTC Finals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> He was 3–1 down to <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Walden" title="Ricky Walden">Ricky Walden</a>, but a crucial run of six successive frames would be key as Allen won 10–6. He became the first Northern Irishman to win a ranking event in the UK since <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Taylor" title="Dennis Taylor">Dennis Taylor</a> won the world title in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> Allen lost the opening seven frames against <a href="/wiki/Kyren_Wilson" title="Kyren Wilson">Kyren Wilson</a> in the second round of the <a href="/wiki/2016_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2016 World Snooker Championship">World Championship</a> and was also 11–5 behind. He closed the gap to 11–9, but had left himself too much to do as he lost 13–9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> Allen called the season a waste as he had not become the world champion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivalry">Rivalry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Allen_(snooker_player)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Rivalry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Allen has a rivalry with <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Bingham" title="Stuart Bingham">Stuart Bingham</a>. After Bingham squandered a 12–9 lead in the second round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2011 World Snooker Championship">2011 World Snooker Championship</a> to lose 13–12 against <a href="/wiki/Ding_Junhui" title="Ding Junhui">Ding Junhui</a>, Allen stated that Bingham had "no bottle" and admitted that there was history between the players and that they did not get on. Bingham responded by calling Allen an idiot and the tension between the two was clearly high as they were drawn to play each other in the second round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Goldfields_Open" title="2011 Australian Goldfields Open">2011 Australian Goldfields Open</a>. Before the match, Bingham said that he couldn't wait to play and that he had been waiting for it for a long time. He also stated that he didn't care what Allen thought and it would give him more pleasure to beat him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> Bingham fulfilled his pre-match words by defeating Allen 5–3 and also went on to win the tournament by coming back from 8–5 down to beat <a href="/wiki/Mark_Williams_(snooker_player)" title="Mark Williams (snooker player)">Mark Williams</a> 9–8 in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> When the draw for the <a href="/wiki/2011_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2011 UK Championship (snooker)">2011 UK Championship</a> was made it revealed that there was a potential quarter-final clash between the two. Upon anticipating the meeting Bingham said that the "feud was not over" and that he would be staying away from Allen and not be speaking to him until they next played.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> The match-up was avoided, however, when Bingham lost in the first round to <a href="/wiki/Marco_Fu" title="Marco Fu">Marco Fu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> Allen announced before the start of the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2013 World Snooker Championship">2013 World Championship</a> that there was no problem between himself and Bingham since Bingham had proved him wrong by winning tournaments, and the two have had drinks and dinner together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> After Bingham won the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2015 World Snooker Championship">2015 World Championship</a> he said "Thanks to Mark Allen. He said I had no bottle and since then things have changed".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p>
<p>Allen also has a rivalry with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Joyce" title="Mark Joyce">Mark Joyce</a> and had made no secret of his dislike for Joyce, stating that: “I don’t like Mark Joyce, basically he is a dick, on and off the table, I doubt even his mum likes him." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> He went on to accuse Joyce of <a href="/wiki/Gamesmanship" title="Gamesmanship">gamesmanship</a> during their match at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Northern_Ireland_Open" title="2016 Northern Ireland Open">2016 Northern Irish Open</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Allen_(snooker_player)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a post-match press conference after his first round win in the <a href="/wiki/2011_UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="2011 UK Championship (snooker)">2011 UK Championship</a>, Allen called for chairman of the <a href="/wiki/World_Professional_Billiards_and_Snooker_Association" title="World Professional Billiards and Snooker Association">World Professional Billiards and Snooker Association</a> (WPBSA), <a href="/wiki/Barry_Hearn" title="Barry Hearn">Barry Hearn</a>, to resign. Allen's reasoning for this was based upon his belief that Hearn had promised not to alter the structure of any of the major snooker tournaments in the calendar (the <a href="/wiki/World_Snooker_Championship" title="World Snooker Championship">World Snooker Championships</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_Championship_(snooker)" title="UK Championship (snooker)">UK Championships</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Masters_(snooker)" title="Masters (snooker)">Masters</a>) when he was appointed chairman in June 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> whereas in fact the UK Championship had seen matches decided on a best of 11 frames basis, rather than the best of 17 frames used in previous years. Allen also stated his perception that the crowd atmosphere within snooker was turning into that of one usually associated with darts and that the tradition of snooker was "going to pot".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup> Allen then swore when talking about Hearn at a press conference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> The WPBSA announced that Allen would be facing a disciplinary committee for swearing when talking about Hearn and that he could be charged with bringing the game into disrepute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> Hearn himself responded by saying he was "far too busy to worry about silly little boys making silly little comments".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> Upon learning that Hearn had been on holiday for the entirety of the event, Allen said it was a "joke" and only confirmed the views which he had expressed earlier in the week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> The pair held a meeting in January 2012, to settle their differences ahead of the forthcoming <a href="/wiki/2012_Masters_(snooker)" title="2012 Masters (snooker)">Masters</a> tournament. Hearn said after the meeting that the way he was running the game may not suit everyone, but his concern was the game of snooker in general. Hearn added: "Mark's prize money this year is probably double what he's earned in the last couple of years so I must be doing something right. We had a very frank and friendly exchange of views and in the end I think he saw my point of view, but time will tell."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> Allen was fined <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>250 for his actions by the WPBSA later in the month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup></p>
<p>Just two months later while playing in the <a href="/wiki/2012_World_Open_(snooker)" title="2012 World Open (snooker)">World Open</a> in <a href="/wiki/Haikou" title="Haikou">Haikou</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, Allen described the conditions on his <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> page as "horrendous", following up with: "Journey a nightmare. People are ignorant. Place stinks. Arena's rubbish, tables poor, food is horrendous. Other than that I love China", although this tweet was later deleted. Answering critics of that particular tweet, he said they should "get a life".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mark_Allen.27s_Twitter_feed_90-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mark_Allen.27s_Twitter_feed-90">[90]</a></sup> World Snooker described his remarks as very disappointing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> It was later revealed that Allen was fined £1,000 for his criticism and he went on to close his Twitter account in April.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen_controv_92-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allen_controv-92">[92]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following his defeat at the <a href="/wiki/2012_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2012 World Snooker Championship">2012 World Snooker Championship</a> to Chinese qualifier <a href="/wiki/Cao_Yupeng" title="Cao Yupeng">Cao Yupeng</a>, he openly accused his opponent of cheating and unsporting behaviour for not owning up to what he perceived to be a foul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup> Allen attributed the deception to his perceived cultural differences between British and Chinese players, purportedly with incidents involving Chinese professional <a href="/wiki/Liang_Wenbo" title="Liang Wenbo">Liang Wenbo</a> and Hong Kong's <a href="/wiki/Marco_Fu" title="Marco Fu">Marco Fu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> Barry Hearn confirmed that Allen would once again face a disciplinary process and said that the player's actions had left him "speechless".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen_controv_92-1"><a href="#cite_note-Allen_controv-92">[92]</a></sup> Allen was fined a total of £11,000 and warned he would be suspended from the tour for 3 months if he breached the rules again in the following six months. He was also required to undergo media training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup></p>
<p>Prior to the commencement of the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Snooker_Championship" title="2013 World Snooker Championship">2013 World Snooker Championship</a>, Allen renewed his criticisms of Barry Hearn. Allen voiced concerns that the <a href="/wiki/World_Snooker_Tour" title="World Snooker Tour">World Snooker Tour</a>'s increasing emphasis on events in China was pricing players out of competing, pointing out that since Hearn took over the running of the game players now have to pay for their own flights, which can incur extra expenses of £10,000–£15,000 per season. Allen, who earned £278,000 over the course of the previous two years, further added he did not believe he was being fairly compensated for his services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Allen_(snooker_player)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Allen had a relationship with women's snooker world champion <a href="/wiki/Reanne_Evans" title="Reanne Evans">Reanne Evans</a>. In 2006, they had a daughter together called Lauren.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2011, Allen admitted he was suffering from depression, due to the amount of travelling and hours spent alone in hotel rooms associated with the life of a professional snooker player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup></p>
<p>Allen married Kyla McGuigan on 10 May 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup></p>
