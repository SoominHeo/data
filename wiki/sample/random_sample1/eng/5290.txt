<p><b>Mark Williams</b> (born 22 August 1959) is an <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> actor, screenwriter and presenter. He is best known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(films)" title="Harry Potter (films)"><i>Harry Potter</i> films</a>, and as one of the stars of the popular BBC sketch show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fast_Show" title="The Fast Show">The Fast Show</a></i>. He also played Brian Williams (father of <a href="/wiki/Rory_Williams" title="Rory Williams">Rory Williams</a>) in the BBC series <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i>, and Olaf Petersen in <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Dwarf" title="Red Dwarf">Red Dwarf</a></i>. More recently he has appeared as <a href="/wiki/Father_Brown" title="Father Brown">the title character</a> in the BBC series <i><a href="/wiki/Father_Brown_(2013_TV_series)" title="Father Brown (2013 TV series)">Father Brown</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Williams_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Williams was educated at <a href="/wiki/North_Bromsgrove_High_School" title="North Bromsgrove High School">North Bromsgrove High School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brasenose_College,_Oxford" title="Brasenose College, Oxford">Brasenose College, Oxford</a>. He performed with <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Dramatic_Society" title="Oxford University Dramatic Society">Oxford University Dramatic Society</a> (OUDS). Having made a career as a theatre actor and working for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Shakespeare_Company" title="Royal Shakespeare Company">Royal Shakespeare Company</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">Royal National Theatre</a> along the way, he came to wider public attention through his appearances on the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> television <a href="/wiki/Sketch_comedy" title="Sketch comedy">sketch</a> programmes <i><a href="/wiki/Alexei_Sayle%27s_Stuff" title="Alexei Sayle's Stuff">Alexei Sayle's Stuff</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fast_Show" title="The Fast Show">The Fast Show</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-belfast_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-belfast-2">[2]</a></sup> Williams has described the huge popularity of the latter show as a "double-edged sword" as it has led to his being seen by the public as a comedian rather than as an actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-belfast_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-belfast-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Williams_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Williams made his film début alongside fellow débutants <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Grant" title="Hugh Grant">Hugh Grant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Imogen_Stubbs" title="Imogen Stubbs">Imogen Stubbs</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Film_Foundation" title="Oxford University Film Foundation">Oxford University Film Foundation</a> production <i><a href="/wiki/Privileged_(1982_film)" title="Privileged (1982 film)">Privileged</a></i> in 1982.</p>
<p>His most famous cinema role is as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthur_Weasley" title="Arthur Weasley">Arthur Weasley</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)">Harry Potter film series</a>, which began in 2002. Other high-profile appearances include the film adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Neil_Gaiman" title="Neil Gaiman">Neil Gaiman</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Stardust_(2007_film)" title="Stardust (2007 film)">Stardust</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Pfeiffer" title="Michelle Pfeiffer">Michelle Pfeiffer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_De_Niro" title="Robert De Niro">Robert De Niro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Claire_Danes" title="Claire Danes">Claire Danes</a> in 2007 and a 2012 role in <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i> as Brian Williams, father of <a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Doctor" title="Eleventh Doctor">the Doctor</a>'s companion, <a href="/wiki/Rory_Williams" title="Rory Williams">Rory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2013, he has appeared as the lead role in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> costume drama <i><a href="/wiki/Father_Brown_(2013_TV_series)" title="Father Brown (2013 TV series)">Father Brown</a></i>. Williams also featured in the first series of <i><a href="/wiki/Blandings_(TV_series)" title="Blandings (TV series)">Blandings</a></i>, the BBC TV adaptation of the <a href="/wiki/P._G._Wodehouse" title="P. G. Wodehouse">P. G. Wodehouse</a> Blandings Castle stories, broadcast in 2013, in which he played Beach, the Emsworth's tipsy butler; his performance was described as "a delight" by <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Letts" title="Quentin Letts">Quentin Letts</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mail" title="Daily Mail">Daily Mail</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> but he did not return for the show's second season, for which he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Vine" title="Tim Vine">Tim Vine</a>.</p>
<p>In 2014 and 2015, he presented the BBC daytime game show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Link_(game_show)" title="The Link (game show)">The Link</a></i>. The show ran for two series.</p>
<p>Aside from his acting work, Williams has also presented several documentary programmes: <i>Mark Williams' Big Bangs</i> on the history of explosives, a follow-up to previous series <i>Mark Williams on the Rails</i>, <i>Industrial Revelations</i> and <i>More Industrial Revelations</i>.</p>
<p>He is a supporter of <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa F.C.</a> although, living in <a href="/wiki/Brighton" title="Brighton">Lewes,</a> he also supports <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Interviewed in 2014 by the <i><a href="/wiki/Lancashire_Evening_Post" title="Lancashire Evening Post">Lancashire Evening Post</a></i>, when asked if some people still saw him as a comedy actor, Williams replied, "Well, it’s only a few people in the BBC. In America, they see me as a major British character actor, but unfortunately, the BBC is pretty parochial and people are institutionalised here."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
