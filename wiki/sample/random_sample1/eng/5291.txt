<p><b>Mark Kassen</b> (born 1971)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes3_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes3-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American actor, director and producer. He has appeared in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Growing_Up_Brady_(film)" title="Growing Up Brady (film)">Growing Up Brady</a></i> (2000), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Good_Student" title="The Good Student">The Good Student</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Puncture_(film)" title="Puncture (film)">Puncture</a></i> (2011), and <i><a href="/wiki/Jobs_(film)" title="Jobs (film)">Jobs</a></i> (2013).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Kassen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theatre">Theatre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Kassen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Theatre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1994, he made his theatre debut in a New York Off Broadway play titled <i>Judy at the Stonewall Inn</i>. A few years later, he was cast in a play called <i>Things You Shouldn't Say Past Midnight</i> which he appeared on stage totally nude.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes3_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes3-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2006, he appeared as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hitler" title="Hitler">Hitler</a>'s nephew <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Patrick_Hitler" title="William Patrick Hitler">William Patrick Hitler</a> in the play <i>Little Willy</i>, which he also wrote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes3_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes3-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Kassen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kassen has had small parts in television films and TV series such as <i><a href="/wiki/Another_World_(TV_series)" title="Another World (TV series)">Another World</a></i> in 1994, <i><a href="/wiki/Cybill" title="Cybill">Cybill</a></i> in 1997,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes3_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes3-1">[1]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Third_Watch" title="Third Watch">Third Watch</a></i> 1999. In 2006, he produced the television movie <i><a href="/wiki/Bernard_and_Doris" title="Bernard and Doris">Bernard and Doris</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Susan_Sarandon" title="Susan Sarandon">Susan Sarandon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Fiennes" title="Ralph Fiennes">Ralph Fiennes</a>, which earned him, along with the other producers, nominations for a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Miniseries_or_Television_Film" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Miniseries or Television Film">Golden Globe</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Made_for_Television_Movie" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Made for Television Movie">Primetime Emmy Award</a> for Best Television Film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, he appeared in the drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Puncture_(film)" title="Puncture (film)">Puncture</a></i> based off the true story of <a href="/wiki/Michael_David_Weiss" title="Michael David Weiss">Michael David Weiss</a> played by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Evans_(actor)" title="Chris Evans (actor)">Chris Evans</a> where Kassen played Weiss's law partner and best friend Paul Danziger. Kassen also directed and produced the film with his brother <a href="/wiki/Adam_Kassen" title="Adam Kassen">Adam Kassen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced in December 2013 that Kassen would star in the upcoming indie film <i>Alone</i> where he will play a 35-year-old veteran battling <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PTSD" title="PTSD">PTSD</a> alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sophie_Turner_(actress)" title="Sophie Turner (actress)">Sophie Turner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ray_Liotta" title="Ray Liotta">Ray Liotta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_company">Production company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Kassen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kassen runs an independent film production company with his younger brother <a href="/wiki/Adam_Kassen" title="Adam Kassen">Adam Kassen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes3_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes3-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
