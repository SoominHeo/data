<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Consuelos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Consuelos<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbcbio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nbcbio-1">[1]</a></sup> was born in Zaragoza, Spain, to an Italian mother, Camilla, and a Mexican father, Saul Consuelos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He is the youngest of three children and has a brother who is a doctor and a sister who is a lawyer. Consuelos has lived in Italy and the United States since childhood. He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Lebanon,_Illinois" title="Lebanon, Illinois">Lebanon, Illinois</a>, and later in <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">Tampa, Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbcbio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nbcbio-1">[1]</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Bloomingdale_High_School" title="Bloomingdale High School">Bloomingdale High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Valrico,_Florida" title="Valrico, Florida">Valrico, Florida</a>, and then went to <a href="/wiki/University_of_Notre_Dame" title="University of Notre Dame">University of Notre Dame</a> before transferring to and graduating from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Florida" title="University of South Florida">University of South Florida</a> with a degree in marketing in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abcbio_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-abcbio-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Consuelos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Consuelos had a starring role in the educational serial <i><a href="/wiki/Connect_With_English" title="Connect With English">Connect With English</a></i>, which aired on public television stations as part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annenberg/CPB_Project" title="Annenberg/CPB Project">Annenberg/CPB Project</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In February 1995, Consuelos had his breakthrough when he landed the role of <a href="/wiki/Mateo_Santos" title="Mateo Santos">Mateo Santos</a> on the soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/All_My_Children" title="All My Children">All My Children</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbcbio_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-nbcbio-1">[1]</a></sup> There, he fell in love with his on-screen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_interest" title="Love interest">love interest</a> <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Ripa" title="Kelly Ripa">Kelly Ripa</a>, and secretly married her in Las Vegas, in May 1996. At the time, he lived on co-star <a href="/wiki/Winsor_Harmon" title="Winsor Harmon">Winsor Harmon</a>'s sofa; Harmon was one of the few to know that co-stars Ripa and Consuelos were not only dating, but married.</p>
<p>Ripa and Consuelos continued to tape episodes of <i>All My Children</i> until 2002, when Ripa wished to focus more on her other job: taking over for <a href="/wiki/Kathie_Lee_Gifford" title="Kathie Lee Gifford">Kathie Lee Gifford</a> as host of what then became <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live_with_Regis_and_Kelly" title="Live with Regis and Kelly">Live! with Regis and Kelly</a></i> (later known as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live!_with_Kelly" title="Live! with Kelly">Live! with Kelly</a></i>, and subsequently as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live!_with_Kelly_and_Michael" title="Live! with Kelly and Michael">Live! with Kelly and Michael</a></i>). Consuelos is a regular guest co-host.</p>
<p>Since leaving <i>All My Children</i>, Consuelos has starred in the feature film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Raid" title="The Great Raid">The Great Raid</a></i>, which debuted in theatres in 2005. In 2006, he appeared in the movies <i><a href="/wiki/My_Super_Ex-Girlfriend" title="My Super Ex-Girlfriend">My Super Ex-Girlfriend</a></i>, as Steve, and <i><a href="/wiki/Wedding_Daze" title="Wedding Daze">Wedding Daze</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbcbio_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-nbcbio-1">[1]</a></sup> He has been seen on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lifetime_Television" title="Lifetime Television">Lifetime</a> series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1-800-Missing" title="1-800-Missing">Missing</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Vivica_A._Fox" title="Vivica A. Fox">Vivica A. Fox</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbcbio_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-nbcbio-1">[1]</a></sup> He also had a part in <i><a href="/wiki/Ugly_Betty" title="Ugly Betty">Ugly Betty</a></i>.</p>
<p>Consuelos hosted two reality dating shows, <i><a href="/wiki/Age_of_Love_(TV_series)" title="Age of Love (TV series)">Age of Love</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbcbio_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-nbcbio-1">[1]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Science_of_Love" title="Science of Love">Science of Love</a></i>, both airing on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> in 2007. He guest-starred on <i><a href="/wiki/Third_Watch" title="Third Watch">Third Watch</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a></i>, <i>American Family,</i> <i>Fortune Hunter</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SeaQuest" title="SeaQuest">SeaQuest</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Hope_%26_Faith" title="Hope &amp; Faith">Hope &amp; Faith</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbcbio_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-nbcbio-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>A weekly live episode of the <i>Oprah Winfrey Show</i> premiered in the show's 23rd season with a panel consisting of Mark Consuelos, Ali Wentworth, Oprah Winfrey, and Gayle King. The panel discussed the week's news and highlighted events in the media and on the show. In the 2009–10 season, Winfrey hosted this segment on her own. Fridays Live did not return for the show's 25th season.</p>
<p>In 2010 Mark Consuelos attended the <i><a href="/wiki/Cop_Out_(2010_film)" title="Cop Out (2010 film)">Cop Out</a></i> premiere in New York where he had a pleasant conversation with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bryan_Johnson_(filmmaker)" title="Bryan Johnson (filmmaker)">Bryan Johnson</a> in view of a spying <a href="/wiki/Walt_Flanagan" title="Walt Flanagan">Walt Flanagan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012 Consuelos guest-starred on an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> titled "<a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit_(season_13)#ep17" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit (season 13)">Justice Denied</a>". He played Michael, the prime suspect in a brutal rape during <a href="/wiki/Fleet_Week" title="Fleet Week">Fleet Week</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Consuelos is not new to the <a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order_(franchise)" title="Law &amp; Order (franchise)"><i>Law &amp; Order</i> franchise</a>, he had guest-starred in the sixth-season episode "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albatross_(Law_%26_Order:_Criminal_Intent)" title="Albatross (Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent)">Albatross</a>" of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Criminal_Intent" title="Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent">Law &amp; Order: Criminal Intent</a></i> as the U.S. Attorney involved in a case where the judge's life is threatened.</p>
<p>Consuelos portrayed Spivey, a recurring character, in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story_(season_2)" title="American Horror Story (season 2)">American Horror Story: Asylum</a></i>, the second season of the anthology horror series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Consuelos co-starred as Florida Senator Andy Guzman in Amazon's <i><a href="/wiki/Alpha_House" title="Alpha House">Alpha House</a></i>, a political comedy written by <i><a href="/wiki/Doonesbury" title="Doonesbury">Doonesbury</a></i> creator <a href="/wiki/Garry_Trudeau" title="Garry Trudeau">Garry Trudeau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Along with <a href="/wiki/John_Goodman" title="John Goodman">John Goodman</a>, Consuelos plays one of four Republican senators living together in a house on Capitol Hill. He has spent the summer of 2014 filming the second season.</p>
<p>In 2016, Consuelos landed a role on a new FOX series, <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_(TV_series)" title="Pitch (TV series)">Pitch</a></i>, as Oscar Arguella.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Consuelos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1995, Consuelos met <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Ripa" title="Kelly Ripa">Kelly Ripa</a>, his co-star on <i><a href="/wiki/All_My_Children" title="All My Children">All My Children</a></i>. The two eloped on May 1, 1996. The couple have three children: Michael Joseph Consuelos (born June 2, 1997), Lola Grace Consuelos (born June 16, 2001), and Joaquin Antonio Consuelos (born February 24, 2003).</p>
<p>On October 3, 2008 he performed the marriage ceremony for <a href="/wiki/Howard_Stern" title="Howard Stern">Howard Stern</a> and model/actress <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beth_Ostrosky" title="Beth Ostrosky">Beth Ostrosky</a> at the Le Cirque restaurant in New York City. The two couples had grown close, which was why Stern asked Consuelos to perform the wedding ceremony. Consuelos agreed, and took it upon himself to seek <a href="/wiki/Ordination" title="Ordination">ordination</a> to make it an official ceremony for Stern and Ostrosky. The guest list for the wedding included <a href="/wiki/Joan_Rivers" title="Joan Rivers">Joan Rivers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Walters" title="Barbara Walters">Barbara Walters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billy_Joel" title="Billy Joel">Billy Joel</a> and wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Katie_Lee_(food_critic)" title="Katie Lee (food critic)">Katie Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel" title="Jimmy Kimmel">Jimmy Kimmel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Silverman" title="Sarah Silverman">Sarah Silverman</a>, and Stern's radio co-host <a href="/wiki/Robin_Quivers" title="Robin Quivers">Robin Quivers</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Consuelos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1998 and 1999, Consuelos won the American Latino Media Arts Award for "Outstanding Actor in a Daytime Soap Opera." Consuelos won "Hottest Romance" along with Ripa in 1997 at the Soap Opera Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abcbio_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-abcbio-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
