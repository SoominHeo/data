<p><b>Marc Jason Kroon</b> (born April 2, 1973) is an American former right-handed <a href="/wiki/Relief_pitcher" title="Relief pitcher">relief pitcher</a>. He served as the <a href="/wiki/Closer_(baseball)" title="Closer (baseball)">closer</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Yomiuri_Giants" title="Yomiuri Giants">Yomiuri Giants</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Japan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Central_League" title="Central League">Central League</a>.</p>
<p>He was drafted 72nd overall by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991_in_baseball" title="1991 in baseball">1991</a>. After playing for various teams including the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a>, he joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yokohama_BayStars" title="Yokohama BayStars">Yokohama BayStars</a> in <a href="/wiki/2005_in_baseball" title="2005 in baseball">2005</a>. That year, he set the record for the fastest pitch ever in Japanese baseball, at 161 km/h (100 mph). He threw fastballs at that speed several times while playing in Yokohama. After the <a href="/wiki/2007_in_baseball" title="2007 in baseball">2007</a> season, Kroon and the BayStars failed to come to terms on a new contract, and ended up being a <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agent</a>. Kroon was later signed by the <a href="/wiki/Yomiuri_Giants" title="Yomiuri Giants">Yomiuri Giants</a> along with two other major non-Japanese free agents: former <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Yakult_Swallows" title="Tokyo Yakult Swallows">Tokyo Yakult Swallows</a> standouts <a href="/wiki/Seth_Greisinger" title="Seth Greisinger">Seth Greisinger</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alex_Ramirez" title="Alex Ramirez">Alex Ramirez</a>.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2008_in_baseball" title="2008 in baseball">2008</a>, Kroon led the <a href="/wiki/Central_League" title="Central League">Central League</a> in <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)" title="Save (baseball)">saves</a> with 41.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He also broke his own record of pitching to 162 km/h (101 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kroon signed a minor league contract with an invitation for spring training with the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Kroon was reassigned to triple-A <a href="/wiki/Fresno_Grizzlies" title="Fresno Grizzlies">Fresno Grizzlies</a> at the end of spring training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kroon was featured in the <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> television production <i><a href="/wiki/The_Franchise_(TV_series)" title="The Franchise (TV series)">The Franchise</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 8, 2012, Kroon retired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
