<p><b>Mark Ross Pellegrino</b> (born April 9, 1965) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> actor of film and television. A prolific actor, he is best known for his work as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucifer_(Supernatural)" title="Lucifer (Supernatural)">Lucifer</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Supernatural (U.S. TV series)">Supernatural</a></i>, Paul Bennett in <i><a href="/wiki/Dexter_(TV_series)" title="Dexter (TV series)">Dexter</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Jacob_(Lost)" title="Jacob (Lost)">Jacob</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i> and as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Being_Human_(North_American_TV_series)_characters#James_Bishop" title="List of Being Human (North American TV series) characters">Bishop</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Being_Human_(North_American_TV_series)" title="Being Human (North American TV series)">Being Human</a></i>. He also starred as Jedikiah Price in <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tomorrow_People_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Tomorrow People (U.S. TV series)">The Tomorrow People</a></i> and as Clayton Haas in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Quantico_(TV_series)" title="Quantico (TV series)">Quantico</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<p>Pellegrino appeared in season 7, episode 3 of <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">The X-Files</a></i> in November 1999. In 2001, he played an incompetent hitman in <a href="/wiki/David_Lynch" title="David Lynch">David Lynch</a>'s critically acclaimed <a href="/wiki/Mulholland_Drive_(film)" title="Mulholland Drive (film)"><i>Mulholland Drive</i></a>. Pellegrino starred as Rita's abusive ex-con former husband <a href="/wiki/List_of_Dexter_characters#Paul_Bennett" title="List of Dexter characters">Paul</a> on <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Dexter_(TV_series)" title="Dexter (TV series)">Dexter</a></i>; and later as Lucifer on <i>Supernatural</i> and as the mysterious near-immortal <a href="/wiki/Jacob_(Lost)" title="Jacob (Lost)">Jacob</a> on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i>. He has appeared on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Northern_Exposure" title="Northern Exposure">Northern Exposure</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Without_a_Trace" title="Without a Trace">Without a Trace</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/NYPD_Blue" title="NYPD Blue">NYPD Blue</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Crime_Scene_Investigation" title="CSI: Crime Scene Investigation">CSI: Crime Scene Investigation</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Knight_Rider_(2008_TV_series)" title="Knight Rider (2008 TV series)">Knight Rider</a></i>. He also is well known as the "Blond Thug" who stuffs The Dude's head down the toilet and drops his bowling ball, breaking the floor tile, in the cult film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Lebowski" title="The Big Lebowski">The Big Lebowski</a></i>. He also is featured in <a href="/wiki/National_Treasure_(film)" title="National Treasure (film)"><i>National Treasure</i></a> as the background FBI agent known as Agent Johnson. In 2005, Pellegino appeared in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> winning film <i><a href="/wiki/Capote_(film)" title="Capote (film)">Capote</a></i>, playing murderer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dick_Hickock" title="Dick Hickock">Dick Hickock</a>.</p>
<p>Upon his numerous cable and network television shows, he portrayed terrorist bomber Bobby James on <i>The Beast</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2009, Pellegrino was cast on the ABC series <i>Lost</i> for an appearance in the final episode of Season 5, to play the role of the mysterious <a href="/wiki/Jacob_(Lost)" title="Jacob (Lost)">Jacob</a>. Although the press release for the episode refers to his character simply as "Man No. 1", the episode revealed that Pellegrino portrayed Jacob, a mysterious character pivotal to the show's plot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On June 26, 2009 it was also announced that Pellegrino was cast in a recurring role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucifer_(Supernatural)" title="Lucifer (Supernatural)">Lucifer</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(season_5)" title="Supernatural (season 5)">fifth season</a> of the CW series <i><a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Supernatural (U.S. TV series)">Supernatural</a></i> until he was returned to his prison, returning in the <a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(season_7)" title="Supernatural (season 7)">seventh season</a> as a hallucination of protagonist <a href="/wiki/Sam_Winchester" title="Sam Winchester">Sam Winchester</a> due to his mental damage after he spent a year in the Cage, until Sam was cured of his insanity. Pellegrino reprised his role as Lucifer in <a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(season_11)" title="Supernatural (season 11)">season 11's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/O_Brother_Where_Art_Thou%3F_(Supernatural)" title="O Brother Where Art Thou? (Supernatural)">O Brother Where Art Thou?</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Devil_in_the_Details" title="The Devil in the Details">The Devil in the Details</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Hell%27s_Angel_(Supernatural)" title="Hell's Angel (Supernatural)">Hell's Angel</a></i>.</p>
<p>He portrayed the vampire "Bishop" in the SyFy horror series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Being_Human_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Being Human (U.S. TV series)">Being Human</a></i> in the first season, and also reprises the role in season 2 and 3 as both flashbacks and hallucinations.</p>
<p>He starred as Jedikiah Price in <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a> series <a href="/wiki/The_Tomorrow_People_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Tomorrow People (U.S. TV series)"><i>The Tomorrow People</i></a>.</p>
<p>In 2015, Pellegrino joined the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> thriller series <i><a href="/wiki/Quantico_(TV_series)" title="Quantico (TV series)">Quantico</a></i> playing the recurring role of FBI Deputy Director Clayton Haas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mark_Pellegrino.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="397" height="332" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Mark_Pellegrino.jpg/220px-Mark_Pellegrino.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Mark_Pellegrino.jpg/330px-Mark_Pellegrino.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9a/Mark_Pellegrino.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mark_Pellegrino.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Mark Pellegrino at Calgary Expo 2015</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Pellegrino was born in Los Angeles, California. He is an adherent of <a href="/wiki/Objectivism_(Ayn_Rand)" title="Objectivism (Ayn Rand)">Objectivism</a>, a philosophy created by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian-American" title="Russian-American">Russian-American</a> writer <a href="/wiki/Ayn_Rand" title="Ayn Rand">Ayn Rand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Pellegrino does not identify as a <a href="/wiki/Libertarianism" title="Libertarianism">libertarian</a>. Though he considers this description to be the closest to his political views, he considers Libertarianism to be an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anarchist" title="Anarchist">anarchist</a> political ideology and has distanced himself from this ideology, describing himself solely as an Objectivist, stating that he sees a place for government in society.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-radicalcapitalist_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-radicalcapitalist-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He has described his political views, instead, as <a href="/wiki/Classical_liberalism" title="Classical liberalism">classical liberalism</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and has also described himself as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_market_capitalism" title="Free market capitalism">radical capitalist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-radicalcapitalist_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-radicalcapitalist-6">[6]</a></sup> He is married to Tracy Aziz and is the stepfather of her daughter Tess.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pellegrino is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atheist" title="Atheist">atheist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
