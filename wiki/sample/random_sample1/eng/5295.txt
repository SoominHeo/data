<p><b>Mark Lewis "Roman" Polansky</b> (born June 2, 1956 in <a href="/wiki/Paterson,_New_Jersey" title="Paterson, New Jersey">Paterson, New Jersey</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> aerospace engineer and research pilot and a former <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">astronaut</a>. Polansky received the nickname "Roman" as a joke, because he shares a last name with director <a href="/wiki/Roman_Polanski" title="Roman Polanski">Roman Polanski</a>. He flew on three <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a> missions: <a href="/wiki/STS-98" title="STS-98">STS-98</a>, <a href="/wiki/STS-116" title="STS-116">STS-116</a>, and <a href="/wiki/STS-127" title="STS-127">STS-127</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_L._Polansky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Polansky's father, Irving, was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a>. His mother, Edith, is originally from Hawaii and is of <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Korean</a> descent. Polansky was raised as a Jew, and honored his father's heritage by taking a teddy bear from the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum along for the ride on <a href="/wiki/STS-116" title="STS-116">STS-116</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He has one daughter and one son.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_L._Polansky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Polansky graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_P._Stevens_High_School" title="John P. Stevens High School">John P. Stevens High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edison,_New_Jersey" title="Edison, New Jersey">Edison, New Jersey</a> in 1974, and received a bachelor of science degree in aeronautical and astronautical engineering, and a master of science degree in aeronautics and astronautics, from <a href="/wiki/Purdue_University" title="Purdue University">Purdue University</a>, both in 1978.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_career">Military career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_L._Polansky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Military career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Polansky received an Air Force commission upon graduation from Purdue University in 1978. He earned his pilot wings in January 1980 at <a href="/wiki/Vance_Air_Force_Base" title="Vance Air Force Base">Vance AFB</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>. From 1980 to 1983, he was assigned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Langley_AFB,_Virginia" title="Langley AFB, Virginia">Langley AFB, Virginia</a>, where he flew the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F-15_Eagle" title="F-15 Eagle">F-15</a> aircraft. In 1983, Polansky transitioned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F-5_Freedom_Fighter" title="F-5 Freedom Fighter">F-5E</a> aircraft and served as an Aggressor Pilot, where he trained tactical aircrews to defeat enemy aircraft tactics. He was assigned in this capacity to Clark Air Base, Republic of the Philippines, and <a href="/wiki/Nellis_Air_Force_Base" title="Nellis Air Force Base">Nellis AFB, Nevada</a>, until he was selected to attend USAF Test Pilot School, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edwards_AFB,_California" title="Edwards AFB, California">Edwards AFB, California</a>, in 1986. Upon graduation, he was assigned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eglin_AFB" title="Eglin AFB">Eglin AFB</a>, Florida, where he conducted weapons and systems testing in the F-15, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F-15E" title="F-15E">F-15E</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A-10_Thunderbolt_II" title="A-10 Thunderbolt II">A-10</a> aircraft. Polansky left active duty in 1992 to pursue a career at NASA. He has logged over 5,000 flight hours in over 30 different aircraft.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="NASA_career">NASA career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_L._Polansky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: NASA career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Polansky joined NASA in August 1992, as an aerospace engineer and research pilot. He was assigned to the Aircraft Operations Division of the <a href="/wiki/Johnson_Space_Center" title="Johnson Space Center">Johnson Space Center</a>. His primary responsibilities involved teaching the astronaut pilots <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">Space Shuttle</a> landing techniques in the Shuttle Trainer Aircraft and instructing astronaut pilots and mission specialists in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T-38_Talon" title="T-38 Talon">T-38</a> aircraft. Polansky also conducted flight testing of the NASA T-38 avionics upgrade aircraft.</p>
<p>Selected by NASA in April 1996, Polansky reported to the Johnson Space Center in August 1996. Having completed two years of training and evaluation, he was initially assigned as a member of the Astronaut Support Personnel team at the Kennedy Space Center, supporting Space Shuttle launches and landings. He served as pilot on <a href="/wiki/STS-98" title="STS-98">STS-98</a> (2001) and has logged over 309 hours in space. He was next assigned as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capsule_communicator" title="Capsule communicator">CAPCOM</a>. Polansky was Chief of the CAPCOM Branch from April 2002 to December 2002. He served as Chief Instructor Astronaut from April 2003 to January 2004. He has also served as Chief of the Return to Flight and Orbiter Repair Branches. Polansky's last shuttle flight as Commander of Mission <a href="/wiki/STS-127" title="STS-127">STS-127</a>, an assembly flight to the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>.</p>
<p>Polansky retired from the Astronaut Corps in June 30, 2012. "Mark is a remarkably talented individual," said <a href="/wiki/Peggy_Whitson" title="Peggy Whitson">Peggy Whitson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chief_of_the_Astronaut_Office" title="Chief of the Astronaut Office">Chief of the Astronaut Office</a>. "His skills as an aviator coupled with his engineering expertise were a valuable contribution to our team. We wish him well in his future endeavors."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spaceflights">Spaceflights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_L._Polansky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Spaceflights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="STS-98_Atlantis_.28February_9.E2.80.9320.2C_2001.29">STS-98 <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Atlantis" title="Space Shuttle Atlantis">Atlantis</a> (February 9–20, 2001)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_L._Polansky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: STS-98 Atlantis (February 9–20, 2001)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The STS-98 crew continued the task of building and enhancing the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a> by delivering the U.S. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Destiny_Laboratory_Module" title="Destiny Laboratory Module">Destiny Laboratory Module</a>. The Shuttle spent seven days docked to the station while Destiny was attached and three spacewalks were conducted to complete its assembly. The crew also relocated a docking port, and delivered supplies and equipment to the resident <a href="/wiki/Expedition_1" title="Expedition 1">Expedition 1</a> crew. Mission duration was 12 days, 21 hours, 20 minutes.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="STS-116_Discovery_.28December_9.E2.80.9322.2C_2006.29">STS-116 <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Discovery" title="Space Shuttle Discovery">Discovery</a> (December 9–22, 2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_L._Polansky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: STS-116 Discovery (December 9–22, 2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The seven-member crew on this 12-day mission continued construction of the ISS outpost by adding the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P5_Truss_Segment" title="P5 Truss Segment">P5 Truss Segment</a> during the first of four spacewalks. The next two spacewalks rewired the station’s power system, preparing it to support the addition of European and Japanese science modules by future shuttle crews. The fourth spacewalk was added to allow the crew to coax and retract a stubborn solar panel to fold up accordion-style into its box. Discovery also delivered a new crew member and more than two tons of equipment and supplies to the station. Almost two tons of items no longer needed on the station returned to Earth with STS-116. Mission duration was 12 days, 20 hours and 45 minutes.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="STS-127_Endeavour_.28July_15.E2.80.9331.2C_2009.29">STS-127 <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">Endeavour</a> (July 15–31, 2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_L._Polansky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: STS-127 Endeavour (July 15–31, 2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Polansky was the commander of the <a href="/wiki/STS-127" title="STS-127">STS-127</a> mission, which launched aboard <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">Space Shuttle Endeavour</a> on July 15, 2009 from the Kennedy Space Center in Florida. During this 16-day mission, the seven-member crew installed the Japanese Exposed Facility onto the ISS, and perform five spacewalks. Polansky returned to Earth with his crew on July 31, 2009. Following fellow astronaut <a href="/wiki/Michael_J._Massimino" title="Michael J. Massimino">Michael J. Massimino</a>'s lead, Mark began posting public updates and replies to <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> on May 7, 2009 during his training for STS-127, and continued to do so throughout the mission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organizations">Organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_L._Polansky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Organizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Member of the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Experimental_Test_Pilots" title="Society of Experimental Test Pilots">Society of Experimental Test Pilots</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Institute_of_Aeronautics_and_Astronautics" title="American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics">American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_Owners_and_Pilots_Association" title="Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association">Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_L._Polansky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Awards and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Distinguished Graduate of the USAF Test Pilot School (1987). Distinguished Graduate of USAF <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Undergraduate_Pilot_Training" title="Undergraduate Pilot Training">Undergraduate Pilot Training</a> (1980). Recipient of the USAF Flying Training Award (1980). Awarded <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Force_Meritorious_Service_Medal" title="Air Force Meritorious Service Medal">Air Force Meritorious Service Medal</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Force_Commendation_Medal" title="Air Force Commendation Medal">Air Force Commendation Medal</a> with two Oak Leaf Clusters.</p>
<p>In his hometown of Edison, NJ, a park is named for him on Grove Ave., across the street from his alma mater of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J._P._Stevens_High_School" title="J. P. Stevens High School">J. P. Stevens High School</a>. Recently in June 2006 the township officials unveiled a sign for Mark Polansky Park while his friends and family were there with him for this presentation. He frequently returns to Edison for programs at the local library for the kids.</p>
