<p><a href="/wiki/Captain_(British_Army_and_Royal_Marines)" title="Captain (British Army and Royal Marines)">Captain</a> <b>Mark Anthony Peter Phillips</b> <span style="font-size:85%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander_of_the_Royal_Victorian_Order" title="Commander of the Royal Victorian Order">CVO</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Personal_Aide-de-Camp" title="Personal Aide-de-Camp">ADC(P)</a></span> (born 22 September 1948) is an English <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold-medal" title="Gold-medal">gold-medal</a>-winning horseman for Great Britain and former husband of <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Princess_Royal" title="Anne, Princess Royal">Anne, Princess Royal</a>, with whom he has two children. He remains a leading figure in British equestrian circles, a noted eventing course designer, and a columnist in <i><a href="/wiki/Horse_%26_Hound" title="Horse &amp; Hound">Horse &amp; Hound</a></i> magazine.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_background_and_early_life">Family background and early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Phillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family background and early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mark Phillips is the son of Major Peter William Garside Phillips (1920–1998),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and Anne Patricia Phillips (née Tiarks;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> 1926–1988). Anne was educated at <a href="/wiki/Downe_House_School" title="Downe House School">Downe House</a> and served in the Women's Royal Naval Service in World War II.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her father was John Gerhard Edward Tiarks (1896–1962), who served in World War I and II, achieving the rank of <a href="/wiki/Brigadier" title="Brigadier">Brigadier</a>. John Gerhard was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aide-de-Camp" title="Aide-de-Camp">Aide-de-Camp</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_George_VI" title="King George VI">King George VI</a> from 1947–50. On 1 June 1922 he married Evelyn Florence, the youngest daughter of Percy Roland Cripps, of Winford Manor, Somerset. Actively interested in fox hunting, John was killed in an accident when hunting with the Gloucestershire Berkeley Hunt. His daughter, Anne, had married Major Peter William Garside Phillips in 1946.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mark Phillips's sister Sarah died in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Phillips was educated at <a href="/wiki/Stouts_Hill" title="Stouts Hill">Stouts Hill</a> Preparatory School near <a href="/wiki/Uley" title="Uley">Uley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Marlborough_College" title="Marlborough College">Marlborough College</a>, then the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Military_Academy_Sandhurst" title="Royal Military Academy Sandhurst">Royal Military Academy Sandhurst</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-sr-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>Upon passing out from Sandhurst, Phillips was commissioned as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Lieutenant" title="Second Lieutenant">Second Lieutenant</a> into the <a href="/wiki/1st_The_Queen%27s_Dragoon_Guards" title="1st The Queen's Dragoon Guards">Queen's Dragoon Guards</a> in July 1969,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After the expected period, he was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_(British_Army_and_Royal_Marines)" title="Lieutenant (British Army and Royal Marines)">Lieutenant</a> in January 1971.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> By the time of his wedding to Princess Anne in November 1973, Phillips was an acting <a href="/wiki/Captain_(British_Army_and_Royal_Marines)" title="Captain (British Army and Royal Marines)">Captain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In January 1974, Phillips was appointed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Personal_Aide-de-Camp" title="Personal Aide-de-Camp">Personal Aide-de-Camp</a> to <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Phillips was substantively promoted to Captain in July 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Phillips retired from the Army on 30 March 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>He continued to style himself Captain Mark Phillips,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> as captains are customarily allowed to use this honorific rank in civilian life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equestrian_career">Equestrian career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Phillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Equestrian career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Phillips was a reserve member of the British equestrian team for the 1968 Olympics. He was part of the British three-day event teams that won a world title in 1970, a European title in 1971 and Olympic gold medals in 1972; individually he finished in 35th place in 1972. At the 1988 Olympics his horse sustained a pulled muscle and could not complete the individual three-day event, but Phillips won a silver medal with the British team. Phillips was a four-time champion at the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_Horse_Trials" title="Badminton Horse Trials">Badminton Horse Trials</a>, in 1971–72 on <i>Great Ovation</i>, in 74 on <i>Colombus</i> and 1981 on <i>Lincoln</i>. It was through his equestrian activities that he met <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Princess_Royal" title="Anne, Princess Royal">Princess Anne</a>, the only daughter of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh</a>. Their daughter Zara won a silver medal in the three-day event with the British team in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sr_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-sr-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1998, Phillips designed the cross-country venue for the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hills_Horse_Trials" title="Red Hills Horse Trials">Red Hills Horse Trials</a>, a qualifying event for the <a href="/wiki/Summer_Olympic_Games" title="Summer Olympic Games">Olympics</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Tallahassee,_Florida" title="Tallahassee, Florida">Tallahassee, Florida</a>, United States. He is now a regular columnist in <i><a href="/wiki/Horse_%26_Hound" title="Horse &amp; Hound">Horse &amp; Hound</a></i> magazine. He also remains a leading figure in British equestrian circles, and serves as <i>Chef d'Equipe</i> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USEA" title="USEA">United States Eventing Team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mark_Phillips&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Wedding_of_Princess_Anne_and_Mark_Phillips" title="Wedding of Princess Anne and Mark Phillips">Wedding of Princess Anne and Mark Phillips</a></div>
<p>Phillips married Princess Anne on 14 November 1973, at <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Abbey" title="Westminster Abbey">Westminster Abbey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_British_Monarchy_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_British_Monarchy-16">[16]</a></sup> They have two children:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peter_Phillips" title="Peter Phillips">Peter Mark Andrew Phillips</a>, born 1977</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Zara_Tindall" title="Zara Tindall">Zara Anne Elizabeth Phillips</a>, born 1981</li>
</ul>
<p>The Queen is believed to have offered him a <a href="/wiki/Peerage" title="Peerage">peerage</a> on his wedding day, which he declined. As female-line grandchildren of the Sovereign, Princess Anne's children were never eligible for the style "Royal Highness" or the title "Prince/Princess" under the terms of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_V_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George V of the United Kingdom">George V</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Letters_patent" title="Letters patent">letters patent</a> of 1917. If Phillips held a peerage, however, his children with <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Princess_Royal" title="Anne, Princess Royal">Princess Anne</a> would have been entitled to be addressed as "The Honourable", or as "Lord" or "Lady".</p>
<p>The Queen bought the couple <a href="/wiki/Gatcombe_Park" title="Gatcombe Park">Gatcombe Park</a> near <a href="/wiki/Minchinhampton" title="Minchinhampton">Minchinhampton</a> as a wedding present.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Prince of Wales</a> is believed to have originated the nickname "Foggy" on the grounds that he was "thick and wet".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Langley_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Langley-18">[18]</a></sup> In August 1985, Phillips fathered a daughter, Felicity, as a result of an <a href="/wiki/Extramarital_sex" title="Extramarital sex">extramarital</a> <a href="/wiki/Affair" title="Affair">affair</a> with New Zealand art teacher Heather Tonkin. Phillips was confirmed as the father as a result of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DNA_testing" title="DNA testing">DNA testing</a> during a paternity suit in 1991. In 1989 the Princess Royal and Mark Phillips announced their intention to separate, as the marriage had been under strain for a number of years. The couple divorced on 23 April 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-father_again_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-father_again-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 February 1997, Phillips married <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Pflueger" title="Sandy Pflueger">Sandy Pflueger</a>, an American Olympic <a href="/wiki/Dressage" title="Dressage">dressage</a> rider. Their daughter Stephanie was born on 2 October 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-father_again_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-father_again-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 3 May 2012, it was confirmed by Phillips' solicitors that Phillips and Pflueger had separated, intending to divorce. Phillips is reported to be in a relationship with US <a href="/wiki/Show_jumping" title="Show jumping">show jumping</a> rider Lauren Hough. The couple are reported to be planning to <a href="/wiki/Cohabitation" title="Cohabitation">live together</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wellington,_Florida" title="Wellington, Florida">Wellington, Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheTelegraph_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-TheTelegraph-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Phillips' personal wealth is thought to be in the range of £15–20 million, although he gained only a small settlement when his marriage to Anne, Princess Royal ended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Langley_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Langley-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
