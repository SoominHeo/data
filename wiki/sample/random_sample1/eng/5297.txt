<p><i><b>Macross Frontier</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">マクロスF (フロンティア)</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Makurosu Furontia</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">Japanese animated</a> <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> space drama that aired in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> on <a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Broadcasting_System" title="Mainichi Broadcasting System">MBS</a> from April 3, 2008 to September 25, 2008. It is the third Japanese <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series set in the <i><a href="/wiki/Macross" title="Macross">Macross</a></i> universe.</p>
<p><i>Macross Frontier</i> is the story of a <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a> space colony fleet trying to find a habitable planet near the center of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milky_Way_Galaxy" title="Milky Way Galaxy">Milky Way Galaxy</a>. The story focuses on three young adults (a famed pop singer, a private military pilot, and a rising pop singer) and the events that occur around them as the fleet faces a crisis of <a href="/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life" title="Extraterrestrial life">alien</a> origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newtype_USA_7_2_11_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Newtype_USA_7_2_11-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macross_Frontier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Macross_Frontier_characters" title="List of Macross Frontier characters">List of Macross Frontier characters</a></div>
<p>The series features the 25th New Macross-class Colonial Fleet, dubbed the <i>Macross Frontier</i>, en route to the galactic center. This heavily populated interstellar fleet (consisting of numerous civilian vessels and their military escorts) contains a makeup of both human occupants and their <a href="/wiki/Zentradi" title="Zentradi">Zentradi</a> allies. As such, many of the Macross Frontier's companion vessels appear to merge more metallic Human designs with organic <a href="/wiki/Zentradi" title="Zentradi">Zentradi</a> aesthetics. <i>Macross Frontier</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'s</span> plot explores a combination of action/political intrigue/space drama more than previous <i><a href="/wiki/Macross" title="Macross">Macross</a></i> series have done in the past.</p>
<p>As the series begins, during a mission to an unexplored asteroid belt a reconnaissance New <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.N._Spacy" title="U.N. Spacy">U.N. Spacy</a> (N.U.N.S.) <a href="/wiki/Variable_fighter#Variable_Fighters_.28official_Macross_continuity.29" title="Variable fighter">VF-171</a> is destroyed by extremely powerful and fast insectoid biomechanical alien <a href="/wiki/Mecha" title="Mecha">mecha</a> known as the "Vajra" which immediately begin their attack on the rest of the fleet. Unable to stop the new enemy threat, the N.U.N.S. Colonial Defense Forces authorizes the deployment of a <a href="/wiki/Private_military_company" title="Private military company">private military provider</a> organization called S.M.S. (Strategic Military Services) which utilizes the new VF-25 <i>Messiah</i> variable fighter to combat the alien menace.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Macross_Fufonfia"><i>Macross Fufonfia</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macross_Frontier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Macross Fufonfia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><i>Macross Fufonfia</i> is a series of <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Flash" title="Adobe Flash">flash</a>-based 90-second promotional shorts developed by <a href="/wiki/Satelight" title="Satelight">Satelight</a> and sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Broadcasting_System" title="Mainichi Broadcasting System">Mainichi Broadcasting System</a> where characters from <i>Macross Frontier</i> are portrayed as <a href="/wiki/Office_lady" title="Office lady">office ladies</a> and employees at the "Frontier Software Company". While not exactly standard anime, more a sort of paper cut-out animation, these <a href="/wiki/Original_net_animation" title="Original net animation">ONA</a> shorts are a fun spoof of the original series intended to promote the show. The series was aired in 2007 and featured 20 episodes.</p>

<p>A theatrical version of the <i>Macross Frontier</i> anime television series was announced in Japan during the broadcast of the 25th and final episode <i>Your Sound</i> ("Anata no Oto") on September 25, 2008. According to interviews with <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dji_Kawamori" title="Shōji Kawamori">Shōji Kawamori</a> the movie was to be an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_adaptation" title="TV adaptation">adaptation</a> of the story from the T.V. series. Japanese <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> magazine <i>Animedia</i> had originally announced that the film was to be released during the summer of 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-macross_frontier_movie_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-macross_frontier_movie-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Other reports later placed the premiere in the fall of 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-movie_in_fall_2009_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-movie_in_fall_2009-6">[6]</a></sup> That report was confirmed by an <i><a href="/wiki/Itasha" title="Itasha">itasha</a></i> (vehicle decorated with character art) mini-van outside the "Macross: The Super Dimension Space Launching Ceremony" event that took place on February 22, 2009 in Japan. However, at that point, the creator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shoji_Kawamori" title="Shoji Kawamori">Shoji Kawamori</a> and the cast members Yuuichi Nakamura (Alto Saotome), Aya Endo (Sheryl Nome), and Megumi Nakajima (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranka_Lee" title="Ranka Lee">Ranka Lee</a>) noted on the actual event, that the new date and the title were still subjected to changes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The theatrical film adaptation of the Macross Frontier opened in Japan on November 21, 2009 under the name <i>Macross Frontier the Movie: The False Songstress</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">劇場版 マクロスF 虚空歌姫 〜イツワリノウタヒメ〜</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gekijōban Makurosu Furontia Itsuwari no Utahime</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. The Kadokawa Cineplex and Shochiku Multiplex theaters' websites revealed the title and date, and the second issue of Kadokawa Shoten's Macross Ace magazine eventually published the details on June 26, 2009. The teaser trailer for the film premiered in Japanese theaters on June 27, 2009 (the same day ticket pre-sales began in Japan).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The first movie retells the events of the anime from episodes 1 to 13, with significant alterations to the story and timeline of the television series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray Disc</a> release of the movie, titled <i>Macross Frontier The Movie: The False Songstress Hybrid Pack</i>, was released on October 7, 2010. The Blu-ray release contains the Blu-ray jacket, the hybrid disc, a 48-page artwork booklet, a theatrical release archive, and a card featuring frames from the movie. The pack also included a code that allowed the owner to be entered into a contest to attend the Macross F Christmas Live event that year. A PS3 game titled "Macross Trial Frontier" is included in the Hybrid Pack, playable on the PlayStation 3. A standard DVD was also released in the same day.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="All_That_VF_-_Macross_25th_Anniversary_Air_Show_.28Frontier_Edition.29">All That VF - Macross 25th Anniversary Air Show (Frontier Edition)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macross_Frontier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: All That VF - Macross 25th Anniversary Air Show (Frontier Edition)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Coinciding with both the 25th Anniversary of Macross and the Blu-ray releases of volume one of both <a href="/wiki/Macross_Zero" title="Macross Zero">Macross Zero</a> and Macross Frontier, the official website of Macross Frontier posted two newly animated short films featuring Air Show style demonstrations for both series. People who purchased the first pressings of either Macross Zero or Macross Frontier received a unique code to enter into the website and therefore be able to watch the short film of the respective anime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macross_Frontier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a tribute to the popularity of the series, Japanese <a href="/wiki/Champion" title="Champion">champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Cosplay" title="Cosplay">cosplay</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kickboxer" title="Kickboxer">kickboxer</a> <a href="/wiki/Yuichiro_Nagashima" title="Yuichiro Nagashima">Yuichiro Nagashima</a> ("Jienotsu") <a href="/wiki/Crossplay" title="Crossplay">crossplayed</a> as the character Ranka Lee during one of his tournaments and also in a promotional event for the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cosplayer_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cosplayer-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first DVD volume that has been released in Japan by <a href="/wiki/Bandai_Visual" title="Bandai Visual">Bandai Visual</a> sustained a third position in the sales chart for 2 consecutive weeks, prior to its release. Namely, from 21 July 2008 to 6 August 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> It is revealed that about 45,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray Discs</a> and 55,000 DVDs are shipped by Bandai Visual for the first volume of the series. This marks the highest Blu-ray Disc pressing among all Bandai Visual releases. <i>Macross Frontier</i> is also reported to be the first anime television series to ship simultaneously on both disc formats in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Music wise, the first opening single, "<i>Triangular</i>" performed by <a href="/wiki/Maaya_Sakamoto" title="Maaya Sakamoto">Maaya Sakamoto</a> made the Oricon's weekly top 10 chart for all Japanese singles. "Triangular" debuted at #3 and sustained for 3 consecutive weeks before falling to #6. Similarly, the first ending single "<i>Diamond Crevasse</i>" performed by <a href="/wiki/May%27n" title="May'n">May'n</a> also debuted at #3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Original_Soundtrack" title="Original Soundtrack">Original Soundtrack</a> for the series titled, "<i>Macross Frontier O.S.T.1 Nyan FRO</i>" made it to #3 on <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a>'s next weekly chart of all albums sold in Japan. The album sold over 72,000 copies becoming the first anime album in 11 Years to rank in Japan's Top 3. The only anime album which managed to rivalled the sales of this is <a href="/wiki/The_End_of_Evangelion" title="The End of Evangelion">The End of Evangelion</a> soundtrack, which was first released on 26 September 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The second opening single "<i>Lion</i>", which also features the second ending theme "<i>Northern Cross</i>" has sold about 56,000 copies in its first week and thus reached #3 on Oricon's weekly chart for all CD singles. All four of the Macross Frontier singles that have been released have debuted at #5 or higher. This brings the total sales of the Macross Frontier CD singles to 500,000, marking the start of a "new anime song boom" in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
