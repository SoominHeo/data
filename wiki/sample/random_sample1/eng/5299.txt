<p><b>Macchiagodena</b> is a town and <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Isernia" title="Province of Isernia">province of Isernia</a>, (south central <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>) located high in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apennines" title="Apennines">Apennines</a> mountains in the region of <a href="/wiki/Molise" title="Molise">Molise</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macchiagodena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The present name of the city appeared for the first time in a document of the year 964 CE as <i>Maccia de Godena</i>. It was a garrison post between the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombard</a> countdoms of <a href="/wiki/Isernia" title="Isernia">Isernia</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boiano" title="Boiano">Boiano</a>, and later a sighting post along the Pescasseroli-Candela <i>tratturo</i> (shepherds' track). A great number of feudal lords came one after the other to take control of the castle (due to the high vantage point overlooking the valley), including the Pandoni and the Mormile.</p>
<p>Many of the early to mid 20th Century inhabitants emigrated to all parts of the world, to Australia, Argentina, Brazil, Canada and USA.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macchiagodena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Castle of Macchiagodena, with a polygonal plant, was constructed on one limestone cliff spur. Of the castle today, only the basements of the old wall and two towers remain. The Castello (Castle) was put up for sale and was purchased in November 2009 by the Regione of Molise. The Regione decided to purchase the castle for its preservation for public use and part of it may be used as a regional historical museum. Also noteworthy is the Church of <i>Santa Maria Assunta</i>, with a statue made by Francesco Saverio Citarelli from Naples, and a precious processional cross.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macchiagodena&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Macchiagodena mostly consists of farmers with a tightly knit community spirit. Every year during the 3rd Sunday in May a fine large fair is held to celebrate the feast day of San Nicola, the patron saint of the town.</p>
<p>A very peculiar tradition is still maintained by some of the population: known as <i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/rapimento" title="wiktionary:rapimento">rapimento</a></i> ("kidnapping"), it consists in the kidnapping of the future bride some time before the wedding is celebrated. Another fine tradition is a local dance called "tuzacula".</p>
