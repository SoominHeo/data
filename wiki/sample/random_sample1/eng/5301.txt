<p>The <b>mata mata</b>, <b>mata-mata</b>, or <b>matamata</b> (<i>Chelus fimbriata</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ttwg_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-ttwg-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> is a freshwater <a href="/wiki/Turtle" title="Turtle">turtle</a> found in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, primarily in the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_basin" title="Amazon basin">Amazon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orinoco" title="Orinoco">Orinoco</a> basins. It is the only <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extant_taxon" title="Extant taxon">extant</a> species in the genus <i><b>Chelus</b></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mata_mata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mata mata was described for the first time by French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naturalist" title="Naturalist">naturalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Barr%C3%A8re" title="Pierre Barrère">Pierre Barrère</a> in 1741 as a "large land turtle with spiky and ridged scales" (translation).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Espenshade_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Espenshade-8">[8]</a></sup> It was first classified as <i>Testudo fimbriata</i> by <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> naturalist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johann_Gottlob_Schneider" title="Johann Gottlob Schneider">Johann Gottlob Schneider</a> in 1783. It was renamed 14 different times in two centuries, finally being renamed <i>Chelus fimbriata</i> in 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ttwg_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-ttwg-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Espenshade_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Espenshade-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Anatomy_and_morphology">Anatomy and morphology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mata_mata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Anatomy and morphology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mata_Mata_1911.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="490" data-file-width="489" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Mata_Mata_1911.jpg/220px-Mata_Mata_1911.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Mata_Mata_1911.jpg/330px-Mata_Mata_1911.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Mata_Mata_1911.jpg/440px-Mata_Mata_1911.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mata_Mata_1911.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Top view of the mata mata turtle</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The mata mata is a large, sedentary turtle with a large, triangular, flattened head characterized with many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tubercle_(anatomy)" title="Tubercle (anatomy)">tubercles</a> and flaps of skin, and a "horn" on its long and tubular snout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Espenshade_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Espenshade-8">[8]</a></sup> Three <a href="/wiki/Barbel_(anatomy)" title="Barbel (anatomy)">barbels</a> occur on the chin and four additional filamentous barbels at the upper jaw, which is neither hooked nor notched.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bartlett_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bartlett-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The mata mata's <a href="/wiki/Brown" title="Brown">brown</a> or <a href="/wiki/Black" title="Black">black</a>, oblong <a href="/wiki/Carapace" title="Carapace">carapace</a> can measure up to 45 cm (18 in) at adult age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-description_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-description-12">[12]</a></sup> The full adult weight is 15 kg (33 lb).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-description_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-description-12">[12]</a></sup> The mata mata's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plastron" title="Plastron">plastron</a> is reduced, narrowed, hingeless, shortened towards the front, and deeply notched at the rear with narrow bridges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bartlett_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bartlett-11">[11]</a></sup> These may be meant to allow the turtle to resemble a piece of bark, camouflaging it from possible predators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The plastron and bridges are cream to <a href="/wiki/Yellow" title="Yellow">yellow</a> or brown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bartlett_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bartlett-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The head, neck, tail, and limbs are grayish brown on adults.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bartlett_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-Bartlett-11">[11]</a></sup> The neck is longer than the vertebra under its carapace and is fringed with small skin flaps along both sides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bartlett_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-Bartlett-11">[11]</a></sup> Hatchlings show a pink to reddish tinge in the underside edge of their carapaces and plastrons that gradually disappear as they grow.</p>
<p>Each fore foot has five webbed claws. Males have concave plastrons and longer, thicker tails than females.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bartlett_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-Bartlett-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mata_mata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mata mata inhabits slow moving, blackwater streams, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stagnant_(water)" title="Stagnant (water)">stagnant</a> pools, marshes, and swamps ranging into northern <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>, eastern <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>, eastern <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guianas" title="Guianas">Guianas</a>, and northern and central <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. The mata mata is strictly an aquatic species but it prefers standing in shallow water where its snout can reach the surface to breathe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rosenfeld_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rosenfeld-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behavior">Behavior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mata_mata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behavior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chelus_fimbriatus_close.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="941" data-file-width="800" height="259" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Chelus_fimbriatus_close.jpg/220px-Chelus_fimbriatus_close.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Chelus_fimbriatus_close.jpg/330px-Chelus_fimbriatus_close.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Chelus_fimbriatus_close.jpg/440px-Chelus_fimbriatus_close.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The appearance of the mata mata's shell resembles a piece of bark, and its head resembles fallen leaves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cogger_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cogger-15">[15]</a></sup> As it remains motionless in the water, its <a href="/wiki/Skin" title="Skin">skin</a> flaps enable it to blend into the surrounding vegetation until a fish comes close.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cogger_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cogger-15">[15]</a></sup> The mata mata thrusts out its head and opens its large mouth as wide as possible, creating a low-pressure vacuum that sucks the prey into its mouth, known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suction_feeding" title="Suction feeding">suction feeding</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cogger_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cogger-15">[15]</a></sup> The mata mata snaps its mouth shut, the water is slowly expelled, and the fish is swallowed whole; the mata mata cannot chew due to the way its mouth is constructed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cogger_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-Cogger-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mata_mata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Males display for females by extending their limbs, lunging their heads toward the females with mouths agape, and moving the lateral flaps on their heads. Nesting occurs from October through December in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upper_Amazon" title="Upper Amazon">Upper Amazon</a>. The 12 to 28 brittle, spherical, 35 mm-diameter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are deposited in a clutch.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mata_mata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mata mata is carnivorous, feeding exclusively upon aquatic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invertebrates" title="Invertebrates">invertebrates</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Espenshade_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-Espenshade-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cogger_15-4"><a href="#cite_note-Cogger-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_captivity">In captivity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mata_mata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: In captivity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mata_mata_NC.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3240" data-file-width="4320" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Mata_mata_NC.jpg/220px-Mata_mata_NC.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Mata_mata_NC.jpg/330px-Mata_mata_NC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Mata_mata_NC.jpg/440px-Mata_mata_NC.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mata_mata_NC.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Mata mata turtles on exhibit at the <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Museum_of_Natural_Sciences" title="North Carolina Museum of Natural Sciences">North Carolina Museum of Natural Sciences</a> in <a href="/wiki/Raleigh,_North_Carolina" title="Raleigh, North Carolina">Raleigh, North Carolina</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Mata mata turtles are readily available in the exotic pet trade and are quite expensive to obtain. Due to their unique appearance, they make interesting display animals. They also grow quite large. However, mata matas are not active hunters, so, like the <a href="/wiki/Alligator_snapping_turtle" title="Alligator snapping turtle">alligator snapping turtle</a>, they need less space than a large, active species.</p>
<p>As with all aquatic turtles, water quality is one of the keys to keeping this species successfully in captivity. Warm, acidic water is the best type used with a high <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tannin-stained_waters" title="Tannin-stained waters">tannin content</a> that should be maintained all year round. Moderate to heavy filtration is recommended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
