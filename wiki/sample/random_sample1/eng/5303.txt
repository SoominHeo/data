<p><b>Mateja Kežman</b> (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_Cyrillic_alphabet" title="Serbian Cyrillic alphabet">Serbian Cyrillic</a>: Матеја Кежман; born 12 April 1979) is a <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbian</a> former professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>
<p>Upon playing in his homeland, Kežman went on to play top-flight football in the Netherlands, England, Spain, Turkey, France, Russia, Hong Kong and Belarus. He is arguably best known for his career at <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a>, earning numerous awards and worldwide recognition as having the potential to be one of the greatest strikers of his generation, being named <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Footballer_of_the_Year" title="Dutch Footballer of the Year">Dutch Footballer of the Year</a> in 2003. Kežman was subsequently labeled as a flop after his spell at <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>, as there were extremely high expectations of him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> By the end of his illustrious career, he had amassed a record amount of championship medals in the top-tier competitions of five different countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Internationally, Kežman represented <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro_national_football_team" title="Serbia and Montenegro national football team">FR Yugoslavia / Serbia and Montenegro</a> in one <a href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Championship" title="UEFA European Championship">European Championship</a> and one <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>After finishing his professional playing career, Kežman became a <a href="/wiki/Sports_agent" title="Sports agent">sports agent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He also served as a <a href="/wiki/Director_of_football" title="Director of football">director of football</a> at <a href="/wiki/FK_Vojvodina" title="FK Vojvodina">Vojvodina</a> in 2013.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_and_personal_life">Early and personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early and personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kežman's father Zlatko was also a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a>, spending the majority of his career with <a href="/wiki/FK_Zemun" title="FK Zemun">Zemun</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>His early career at Partizan was disrupted by <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Belgrade" title="Bombing of Belgrade">airstrikes on Belgrade</a> in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brodkin_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-brodkin-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2003, he took protection when a Yugoslav criminal threatened to kidnap him and hold PSV to ransom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brodkin_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-brodkin-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kežman is a devout member of the <a href="/wiki/Serbian_Orthodox_Church" title="Serbian Orthodox Church">Serbian Orthodox Church</a>. During his time in the Netherlands, he prayed in the German city of <a href="/wiki/Dortmund" title="Dortmund">Dortmund</a> to retain his privacy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brodkin_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-brodkin-6">[6]</a></sup> Although he considers his addiction to tattoos to be a vice, his tattoos are of a religious theme, and while playing he wore an undershirt with a printed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Icon_(art)" title="Icon (art)">icon</a> of Jesus; this drew media attention during his spell in Turkey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He pledged to become a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orthodox_monasticism" title="Orthodox monasticism">monk in the church</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lewis_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-lewis-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Partizan">Partizan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Partizan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the age of 19, Kežman joined <a href="/wiki/FK_Partizan" title="FK Partizan">Partizan</a>. In his two seasons at the club, Kežman made a name for himself as a prolific striker and became a favourite among the fans of the club. He scored a total of 33 league goals in 54 appearances and was the top scorer of the national championship in the <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_First_League_of_FR_Yugoslavia" title="1999–2000 First League of FR Yugoslavia">1999–2000 season</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PSV">PSV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: PSV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the summer of 2000, Kežman completed his move to Dutch club <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In his <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Eredivisie" title="2000–01 Eredivisie">debut season</a>, he was the league's top scorer, achieving 24 goals in 33 games as PSV won the title. He also picked up the nickname 'Batman', because the famous theme song was played at <a href="/wiki/Philips_Stadion" title="Philips Stadion">Philips Stadion</a> every time he scored. He formed a successful partnership with <a href="/wiki/Arjen_Robben" title="Arjen Robben">Arjen Robben</a>, and they gained the epithet 'Batman and Robben', a pun on <a href="/wiki/Batman_(comic_strip)" title="Batman (comic strip)">Batman and Robin</a>.</p>
<p>Kežman went to score 81 goals over the following three seasons with PSV. His best season in terms of both goals and appearances was <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Eredivisie" title="2002–03 Eredivisie">2002–03</a> when he played 33 out of 34 of PSV's matches and scored 35 goals to help them win the title.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chelsea">Chelsea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chelsea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kežman spent the 2004–05 season at <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> and scored seven goals in 40 appearances in all competitions. He was signed for the club on 13 July 2004 for £5.3 million by <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mourinho" title="José Mourinho">José Mourinho</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He wore the number 9 shirt left vacant by <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Floyd_Hasselbaink" title="Jimmy Floyd Hasselbaink">Jimmy Floyd Hasselbaink</a>'s transfer to <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>.</p>
<p>He played 24 league games and scored four goals. The first goal came on 4 December 2004 in a 4–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> at home, with an injury time penalty after coming on as a substitute in the 61st minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He did not score again until 11 March 2005 in a 3–1 away victory over <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a>, who were relegated at the end of the season. Eight days later he scored twice at home against another team that would be relegated, <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a>, as Chelsea won 4–1. At the end of the season Chelsea won their first league title since <a href="/wiki/1954%E2%80%9355_Chelsea_F.C._season" title="1954–55 Chelsea F.C. season">1954–55</a>.</p>
<p>His first ever goal for Chelsea was in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> third round at home to <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> on 27 October 2004. It was the only goal of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He scored a goal in extra time (112th minute) of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Football_League_Cup_Final" title="2005 Football League Cup Final">2005 League Cup Final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>. Chelsea won 3–2 after it finished 1–1 after 90 minutes.</p>
<p>Kežman scored a goal in the 3rd round of <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_FA_Cup" title="2004–05 FA Cup">that season's FA Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/Scunthorpe_United_F.C." title="Scunthorpe United F.C.">Scunthorpe United</a> as Chelsea won 3–1 at home.</p>
<p>In 2011, Kežman revealed to <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a> that his time at Chelsea was the best he has ever had in football, despite failing to make an impact for the club: <i>"Chelsea was the best thing in my career. That was the climax of my career, for sure. Living and playing in London was something that I will never forget. I don't know if I made a mistake because I moved away so quickly. Maybe I do and maybe I don't regret it, but I always believe in my decisions. But the transfer to Chelsea made my dreams come true. That was the best thing that ever happened to me. Maybe I could achieve more and I could have been more successful at <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Bridge_(stadium)" title="Stamford Bridge (stadium)">Stamford Bridge</a>, but I don't regret anything."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atl.C3.A9tico_Madrid">Atlético Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Atlético Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kežman left Chelsea for <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> of Spain's <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">Primera División</a> on 29 June 2005 for £5.3 million, the same price as he had joined Chelsea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He soon suffered a knee injury, which he did not recover from until after New Year's in 2006. Initially, the appointment of <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Murcia" title="José Murcia">José Murcia</a> as coach allegedly boosted Kežman's confidence, but by the end of his spell in Madrid, Kežman actually scored more goals (six) under <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Bianchi" title="Carlos Bianchi">Carlos Bianchi</a> than with Murcia (with whom he scored a total of two goals in the remainder of the season).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Atlético ended <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_La_Liga" title="2005–06 La Liga">the season</a> in 10th and were knocked out by <a href="/wiki/Real_Zaragoza" title="Real Zaragoza">Real Zaragoza</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Copa_del_Rey" title="2005–06 Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> round of 16. Kežman scored a total of eight goals in 30 league matches, often in a strike partnership with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Torres" title="Fernando Torres">Fernando Torres</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fenerbah.C3.A7e">Fenerbahçe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Fenerbahçe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In the summer of 2006, Kežman was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Fenerbah%C3%A7e_S.K._(football)" title="Fenerbahçe S.K. (football)">Fenerbahçe</a> on a four-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He played a central role in Fenerbahçe winning the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="2006–07 Süper Lig">2006–07 Süper Lig</a> in their centennial year. In the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2007–08 UEFA Champions League">2007–08 UEFA Champions League</a>, he appeared in over half of Fenerbahçe's group stage matches against the likes of his former club PSV, <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Internazionale</a> and <a href="/wiki/PFC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PFC CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a>. Ironically, he even played against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> over both legs of the 2008 Champions League quarter-finals; the first of which he started and showed off with a close <a href="/wiki/Bicycle_kick" title="Bicycle kick">bicycle kick</a> in a 2–1 win,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> while in the second leg he was subbed in during the dying minutes of his team's 2–0 loss at <a href="/wiki/Stamford_Bridge_(stadium)" title="Stamford Bridge (stadium)">Stamford Bridge</a>, where Fenerbahçe was eliminated in the longest-lasting Champions League campaign in the club's history. As a result of this and the preceding regular season, in which Kežman scored almost a goal every other game by statistical average, <a href="/wiki/Fenerbah%C3%A7e_S.K._supporters" title="Fenerbahçe S.K. supporters">Fenerbahçe S.K. supporters</a> revered him even in the years after he left <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> in his journeyman years.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Paris_Saint-Germain">Paris Saint-Germain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Paris Saint-Germain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 19 August 2008, Kežman signed for <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a>, a one-year loan with a buyout clause.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He appeared in 21 league matches, mostly as substitute, and scored three goals.</p>
<p>On 30 August 2009, it was announced that Kežman would be joining <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">Zenit Saint Petersburg</a> from Paris Saint-Germain on a season-long loan, with a purchase option for an undisclosed fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The next day, the transfer had been officially confirmed when the player passed medical examinations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He scored two goals for Zenit helping the team <a href="/wiki/2009_Russian_Premier_League" title="2009 Russian Premier League">to finish 3rd</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Premier_League" title="Russian Premier League">Russian Premier League</a>, but the Russian side decided not to retain his services, and he returned to Paris in January 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 3 November 2010, Kežman's contract with PSG was terminated by mutual consent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_China">South China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: South China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 20 January 2011, it was announced that Kežman had signed to play for <a href="/wiki/South_China_AA" title="South China AA">South China</a> for the rest of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Hong_Kong_First_Division_League" title="2010–11 Hong Kong First Division League">2010–11 Hong Kong First Division League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Kežman made his debut on 29 January 2011 versus <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NT_Realty_Wofoo_Tai_Po" title="NT Realty Wofoo Tai Po">NT Realty Wofoo Tai Po</a> which lost 1–0; he was substituted by <a href="/wiki/Leung_Chun_Pong" title="Leung Chun Pong">Leung Chun Pong</a> in the 64th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 February 2011, in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Hong_Kong_League_Cup" title="2010–11 Hong Kong League Cup">League Cup</a> semi-final against league leader <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitchee" title="Kitchee">Kitchee</a>, Kežman scored his first goal for the club in the 32nd minute and his second goal seven minutes before the end.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> South China beat Kitchee by 4–2 and entered the finals.</p>
<p>On 13 April 2011, in the <a href="/wiki/2011_AFC_Cup" title="2011 AFC Cup">2011 AFC Cup</a> group H match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingfisher_East_Bengal" title="Kingfisher East Bengal">Kingfisher East Bengal</a>, Kežman scored his first AFC Cup goal in the 69th minute with a header, assisted by <a href="/wiki/Au_Yeung_Yiu_Chung" title="Au Yeung Yiu Chung">Au Yeung Yiu Chung</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> His goal helped South China gain victory and moved up to 3rd position in the group, but he also received a yellow card in the match, his second yellow card in the tournament and he missed the away game to East Bengal.</p>
<p>In May 2011, after his team failed to progress into the <a href="/wiki/2011_AFC_Cup_knockout_stage" title="2011 AFC Cup knockout stage">2011 AFC Cup knockout stage</a>, following 2–4 loss to Indonesian outfit <a href="/wiki/Persipura_Jayapura" title="Persipura Jayapura">Persipura Jayapura</a>, Kežman said to the media that the <a href="/wiki/AFC_Cup" title="AFC Cup">AFC Cup</a> is a strange competition and kind of stupid. He blamed the hot weather and long travel to go to <a href="/wiki/Jayapura" title="Jayapura">Jayapura</a> as one of the factors why his team could not progress further.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the final match of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Hong_Kong_FA_Cup" title="2010–11 Hong Kong FA Cup">2010–11 Hong Kong FA Cup</a>, Kežman managed to take advantage of an opponent's defensive error and scored an outstanding goal in extra time to give his team a 2–1 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NT_Realty_Wofoo_Tai_Po" title="NT Realty Wofoo Tai Po">NT Realty Wofoo Tai Po</a> on 29 May 2011. The next day, Kežman finished his stint with South China after his four-month contract expired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BATE">BATE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: BATE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 August 2011, Kežman joined Belarusian side <a href="/wiki/FC_BATE_Borisov" title="FC BATE Borisov">BATE Borisov</a> as a free agent until the end of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He was seen as a reinforcement for upcoming <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> group stage games and as a potential replacement for seriously injured striker <a href="/wiki/Vitali_Rodionov" title="Vitali Rodionov">Vitali Rodionov</a>. Ultimately, Kežman played six games in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Belarusian_Premier_League" title="2011 Belarusian Premier League">Belarusian League</a> and 5 games in the Champions League without scoring any goals. He parted ways with BATE after the last Champions League game against <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Hong_Kong_and_retirement">Return to Hong Kong and retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Return to Hong Kong and retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2012, Kežman returned to South China to play at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Asian_Challenge_Cup" title="2012 Asian Challenge Cup">Asian Challenge Cup</a>. He played his last professional match on 26 January 2012, missing the last penalty in the shoot-out series against <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou_R%26F_F.C." title="Guangzhou R&amp;F F.C.">Guangzhou R&amp;F</a>, in the third-place match.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mateja_Ke%C5%BEman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kežman marked his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia_national_football_team" title="Federal Republic of Yugoslavia national football team">senior</a> international debut for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">FR Yugoslavia</a> during March 2000 with a goal against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China_PR_national_football_team" title="China PR national football team">China</a> in a friendly match as part of the preparations for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000" title="UEFA Euro 2000">UEFA Euro 2000</a>. Head coach <a href="/wiki/Vujadin_Bo%C5%A1kov" title="Vujadin Boškov">Vujadin Boškov</a> included him in the final 23-man roster that he took to <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. Despite being the fourth attacking option behind <a href="/wiki/Predrag_Mijatovi%C4%87" title="Predrag Mijatović">Predrag Mijatović</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savo_Milo%C5%A1evi%C4%87" title="Savo Milošević">Savo Milošević</a> and <a href="/wiki/Darko_Kova%C4%8Devi%C4%87" title="Darko Kovačević">Darko Kovačević</a>, 21-year-old Kežman got his chance as substitute late in the group match versus <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a>. However, after being on the pitch for about 90 seconds, he was sent off following a rash tackle on <a href="/wiki/Erik_Mykland" title="Erik Mykland">Erik Mykland</a>.</p>
<p>In February 2003, after embarrassing performances of the team during <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2004_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2004 qualifying">UEFA Euro 2004 qualifying</a>, Kežman announced that he retired from the national jersey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> However, Kežman returned to the team few months later, after <a href="/wiki/Ilija_Petkovi%C4%87" title="Ilija Petković">Ilija Petković</a> was appointed as the new coach.</p>
<p>Kežman led <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro_national_football_team" title="Serbia and Montenegro national football team">Serbia and Montenegro</a>'s scoring during the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="2006 FIFA World Cup qualification">2006 FIFA World Cup qualifications</a> with five goals, including the only goal in the final game against <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> which in turn allowed Serbia and Montenegro to qualify directly, having come first in <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_UEFA_Group_7" title="2006 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group 7">their group</a>. During <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">the competition</a>, he was sent off in a group match against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> by referee <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Rosetti" title="Roberto Rosetti">Roberto Rosetti</a>.</p>
<p>After his sending off against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>, Kežman did not receive any call up for his <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_football_team" title="Serbia national football team">country's national team</a>.</p>
<p>During his six-year career in the national team, Kežman scored 17 goals in 49 appearances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
