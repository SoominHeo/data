<p><b>Matteo Bianchetti</b> (born 17 March 1993) is an Italian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona_F.C." title="Hellas Verona F.C.">Verona</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matteo_Bianchetti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Internazionale_.28youth.29">Internazionale (youth)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matteo_Bianchetti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Internazionale (youth)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Como" title="Como">Como</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a>, Bianchetti joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">Inter</a> from Corsico, a local team from the town of Corsico, the Province of Milan, Lombardy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He played for Inter in every ranks from U15, 16, 17 to the reserve in 2011 (U20 by regulation until 2011–12, but Inter changed to U19 internally). He also spent one season with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.S._Varese_1910" title="A.S. Varese 1910">Varese</a> reserve in 2010–11 season<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> as Inter did not had its own U18 team in <a href="/wiki/Campionato_Nazionale_Dante_Berretti" title="Campionato Nazionale Dante Berretti">Berretti League</a> as wild card until 2011. Bianchetti won 2012 <a href="/wiki/Campionato_Nazionale_Primavera" title="Campionato Nazionale Primavera">Campionato Nazionale Primavera</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_NextGen_Series" title="2011–12 NextGen Series">2011–12 NextGen Series</a> with the U19 team. Bianchetti also included in the first team occasionally but failed to play in competitive games. He was included in <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League">2011–12 UEFA Champions League</a> List B squad as no.44, a routine move for reserve players.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ListB_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ListB-6">[nb 1]</a></sup> In summer 2012 <a href="/wiki/Lega_Serie_A" title="Lega Serie A">Lega Serie A</a> changed the age limit of the reserve league to U19, and Inter's reserve transformed to a more age specific teams: U19 and U18. However, Bianchetti remained in Inter, just likes his predecessors <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Davide_Faraoni" title="Davide Faraoni">Davide Faraoni</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samuele_Longo" title="Samuele Longo">Samuele Longo</a> who was one of the three age 20 "overage" player for Inter U19 reserve in 2010–11 season and 2011–12 season respectively. But in the other hand, Bianchetti was a real "overage" player as the regulation changed, thus Bianchetti did not had much chance for Inter U19 team in 2012–13 season, along with former captain <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Roman%C3%B2" title="Andrea Romanò">Andrea Romanò</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Bessa" title="Daniel Bessa">Daniel Bessa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Raffaele_Di_Gennaro" title="Raffaele Di Gennaro">Raffaele Di Gennaro</a>, despite the latter two were forced to remain in Inter due to injury (Bianchetti also missed a few month since January 2012 and recovered in pre-season 2012). Bianchetti did not play in 2012 <a href="/wiki/Supercoppa_Primavera" title="Supercoppa Primavera">Supercoppa Primavera</a>, the conclusion of last season, where Inter also fielded overage players <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Duncan" title="Alfred Duncan">Alfred Duncan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marko_Livaja" title="Marko Livaja">Marko Livaja</a>. Bianchetti remained in Inter's European squad, but promoted to List A, along with Romanò.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[6]</a></sup> However it was due to Inter did not had enough youth product to fill the 4 specific quota and the outcome was indifference. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ListA_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ListA-8">[nb 2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Verona">Verona</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matteo_Bianchetti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Verona">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As Bianchetti was lack of play time, despite he received call-up from <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy national under-21 football team</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[7]</a></sup> he did not debut yet. On 23 January 2013 he left for <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona_F.C." title="Hellas Verona F.C.">Hellas Verona F.C.</a> of Serie B.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[9]</a></sup> Verona also signed former U20 player <a href="/wiki/Michelangelo_Albertazzi" title="Michelangelo Albertazzi">Michelangelo Albertazzi</a> from Milan in summer 2012 but he failed to make a significant impact and did not receive any call-up since January due to injury. Bianchetti immediately entered the squad and made his competitive debut against <a href="/wiki/Spezia_Calcio" title="Spezia Calcio">Spezia</a> as a last minute substitute for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emanuel_Benito_Rivas" title="Emanuel Benito Rivas">Emanuel Benito Rivas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[10]</a></sup> He wore no.14 shirt previously owned by <a href="/wiki/Simone_Calvano" title="Simone Calvano">Simone Calvano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[11]</a></sup> After the recovery of Albertazzi, Bianchetti was fall from the first priority on the bench, until regained the bench position since round 30 (missed round 33 for international duty), made 5 substitute appearances for <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_%C3%81ngel_Crespo" title="José Ángel Crespo">Crespo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raphael_Martinho" title="Raphael Martinho">Raphael Martinho</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luca_Ceccarelli_(footballer_born_in_Massa)" title="Luca Ceccarelli (footballer born in Massa)">Ceccarelli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vangelis_Moras" title="Vangelis Moras">Moras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Domenico_Maietta" title="Domenico Maietta">Maietta</a> respectively; Bianchetti made his only start on 22 April,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[12]</a></sup> due to the absent of Ceccarelli.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2013 Verona bought <a href="/wiki/Co-ownership_(football)" title="Co-ownership (football)">half of the registration rights</a> of Bianchetti for €350,000 in 3-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[14]</a></sup> Bianchetti made his Serie A debut on 15 September 2013. In total he made 3 Serie A appearances before leaving for Spezia in January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[15]</a></sup> In June 2014 the co-ownership deal between Verona and Inter was renewed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 January 2015 Spezia re-signed Bianchetti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 25 June 2015 Inter announced the Co-Ownership resolved in their favor, and they are sending the player to Verona in a definitive deal on 1 July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matteo_Bianchetti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bianchetti played twice in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_UEFA_European_Under-19_Football_Championship_qualification" title="2012 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship qualification">2012 UEFA European Under-19 Football Championship qualification</a>. He missed the elite round due to injury. In September 2012 he played for <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-20_football_team" title="Italy national under-20 football team">Italy national under-20 football team</a> in Four Nations. In October 2012 received he call-up to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship_qualification_play-offs" title="2013 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification play-offs">2013 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship qualification play-offs</a>. In January 2013 made he his debut for U21 team in a non-competitive triangular tournament between U21, 20 and 19. He was partnered with 2 other Inter youth products <a href="/wiki/Luca_Caldirola" title="Luca Caldirola">Luca Caldirola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_Biraghi" title="Cristiano Biraghi">Cristiano Biraghi</a> in that 45-minute match against U20;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[20]</a></sup> rested against U19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>His debut for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy U-21</a> team was in a friendly game against the Germany U-21 side on 6 February 2013, playing 90 minutes.</p>
<p>He took part at both the <a href="/wiki/2013_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2013 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2013</a> and <a href="/wiki/2015_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2015</a> editions of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="UEFA European Under-21 Championship">UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a>.</p>
