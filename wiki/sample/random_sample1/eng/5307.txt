<p><b>Matthias Lukas Ginter</b> (born 19 January 1994) is a German <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthias_Ginter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ginter began his career with SV March before he moved to the youth squad of <a href="/wiki/SC_Freiburg" title="SC Freiburg">SC Freiburg</a> for the 2005–06 season. With the A-youth squad, he won the Junior <a href="/wiki/DFB-Pokal" title="DFB-Pokal">DFB-Pokal</a>.</p>
<p>In January 2012, Ginter trained with Freiburg's first team due, in part, to a loss of several players from the roster during the winter transfer window. On 21 January 2012, Ginter made his professional debut when he was substituted in for <a href="/wiki/Anton_Putsila" title="Anton Putsila">Anton Putsila</a> in the 70th minute against fellow relegation battlers <a href="/wiki/FC_Augsburg" title="FC Augsburg">FC Augsburg</a>. In the 88th minute of the game, he scored the winning goal from a free-kick by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Lumb_(footballer)" title="Michael Lumb (footballer)">Michael Lumb</a> for his team in the 1–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The goal, which came two days after Ginter's 18th birthday, made him SCF's youngest Bundesliga goalscorer in the club's history. The record was previously held by <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Aogo" title="Dennis Aogo">Dennis Aogo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Two days later, on 23 January, Ginter signed a contract to join the first team of SC Freiburg.</p>
<p>On 17 July 2014 Ginter signed for <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a> on a five-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He made his debut on 13 August, playing the full match as they won the <a href="/wiki/DFL-Supercup" title="DFL-Supercup">DFL-Supercup</a> 2–0 against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayern_Munich" title="Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Westfalenstadion" title="Westfalenstadion">Westfalenstadion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthias_Ginter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ginter is a German senior international having also represented his nation at U18, U19 and <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_under-21_football_team" title="Germany national under-21 football team">U21</a> levels.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthias_Ginter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He represented the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_under-21_football_team" title="Germany national under-21 football team">under-21 team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2015_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2015 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2015 European Championship</a> in the Czech Republic, starting all 4 matches. In their second group match at the <a href="/wiki/Eden_Arena" title="Eden Arena">Eden Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a> against <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_under-21_football_team" title="Denmark national under-21 football team">Denmark</a>, following a <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Volland" title="Kevin Volland">Kevin Volland</a> brace, Ginter rounded off the scoring by heading in <a href="/wiki/Amin_Younes" title="Amin Younes">Amin Younes</a>' cross in the 53rd minute, leading to a 3-0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior">Senior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthias_Ginter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Senior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 5 March 2014, he earned his first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cap_(association_football)" title="Cap (association football)">cap</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">senior national team of his country</a> after coming on as a 90th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Mesut_%C3%96zil" title="Mesut Özil">Mesut Özil</a> in the 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a> in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friendly_match" title="Friendly match">friendly match</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Olympiastadion_(Berlin)" title="Olympiastadion (Berlin)">Olympiastadion in Berlin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He became the 900th player to be capped by the German national team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June, he was named as the youngest player in Germany's 23-man squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> which went on to win the tournament, although he did not enter the field of play at any point.</p>
<p>He was part of the squad for the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2016 Summer Olympics</a>, where Germany won the silver medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
