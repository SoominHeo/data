<p><b>Matías Nicolás Rodríguez</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[maˈti.as niˈkolas roˈðɾiɣes]</a></span>, born 14 April 1986) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentinian_people" title="Argentinian people">Argentine</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">right back</a> or <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for Chilean club <a href="/wiki/Club_Universidad_de_Chile" title="Club Universidad de Chile">Universidad de Chile</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mat%C3%ADas_Rodr%C3%ADguez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After years spending in <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> academy, he moved <a href="/wiki/Ecuadorian_Serie_A" title="Ecuadorian Serie A">Ecuadorian Serie A</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sociedad_Deportiva_Aucas" title="Sociedad Deportiva Aucas">Aucas</a> in mid-2006 (after fail joined on trial to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona_de_Guayaquil" title="Barcelona de Guayaquil">Barcelona de Guayaquil</a>), where scored three goals in 13 league matches, which his transfer fee was appraised in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">US$</a>1 million, because their well performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rodr.C3.ADguez_in_Ecuador_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rodr.C3.ADguez_in_Ecuador-2">[2]</a></sup> Following his success, he returned to Boca and had an horrible 2007, after have a seriously <a href="/wiki/Tibia" title="Tibia">tibia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fibula" title="Fibula">fibula</a> injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rodr.C3.ADguez_in_Ecuador_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rodr.C3.ADguez_in_Ecuador-2">[2]</a></sup> Finally, after one-and-half year without play, he joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nacional_Montevideo" title="Nacional Montevideo">Nacional Montevideo</a> in June 2008.</p>
<p>In January 2010, following <a href="/wiki/Gerardo_Pelusso" title="Gerardo Pelusso">Gerardo Pelusso</a>'s departure (coach of Rodríguez at Nacional) to <a href="/wiki/Club_Universidad_de_Chile" title="Club Universidad de Chile">Universidad de Chile</a>, he also joined Chilean club,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> where he won three league titles and the <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Sudamericana" title="2011 Copa Sudamericana">2011 Copa Sudamericana</a> title with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santiago,_Chile" title="Santiago, Chile">Santiago</a>-based club. However, all of these title was under manager <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Sampaoli" title="Jorge Sampaoli">Jorge Sampaoli</a>, who replaced former coach Pelusso after bad results in 2010. Their most important goals with <i>The Blues</i> were in two derbies against <a href="/wiki/Colo-Colo" title="Colo-Colo">Colo-Colo</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and in a <a href="/wiki/2010_Copa_Libertadores" title="2010 Copa Libertadores">2010 Copa Libertadores</a> game too with <a href="/wiki/Clube_de_Regatas_do_Flamengo" title="Clube de Regatas do Flamengo">Flamengo</a>, where he scored a last-minute equalizer goal that sealed a 2–2 away draw at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_do_Maracan%C3%A3" title="Estádio do Maracanã">Maracanã</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>He also had serious possibilities of a move to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Europe_(continent)" title="Europe (continent)">Europe</a> because his performances, being heavily linked with Portugal's <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> in January 2012 after reach Copa Sudamericana title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> However, on 29 January 2013, Rodríguez joined <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Italian Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> in a transfer fee a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">US$</a>4.2 million (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%E2%82%AC" title="€">€</a>3.28 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sampdoria2013bilancio_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sampdoria2013bilancio-11">[11]</a></sup> He signed a 4½ year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sampdoria2013bilancio_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sampdoria2013bilancio-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 May 2014, Rodríguez was loaned from Serie A side Sampdoria to <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%AAmio_Foot-Ball_Porto_Alegrense" title="Grêmio Foot-Ball Porto Alegrense">Grêmio</a> until June 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 19 July 2015 Rodríguez was re-signed by <a href="/wiki/Club_Universidad_de_Chile" title="Club Universidad de Chile">Universidad de Chile</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mat%C3%ADas_Rodr%C3%ADguez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012, Matías Rodríguez was selected by <a href="/wiki/Alejandro_Sabella" title="Alejandro Sabella">Alejandro Sabella</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina national team</a> as part of the squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CONMEBOL)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)">2014 FIFA World Cup qualification</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Ecuador_national_football_team" title="Ecuador national football team">Ecuador</a>. Later, he was selected again for the match played against <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He did not play in either of the two matches.</p>
