<p><b>Mattias Hargin</b> (born 7 October 1985) is a Swedish <a href="/wiki/FIS_Alpine_Ski_World_Cup" title="FIS Alpine Ski World Cup">World Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing" title="Alpine skiing">alpine ski racer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Born in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, he competes mainly in <a href="/wiki/Slalom_skiing" title="Slalom skiing">slalom</a>, and is the younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Janette_Hargin" title="Janette Hargin">Janette Hargin</a> (b. 1977), who also raced for Sweden. Hargin was married to Swedish alpine free-skier <a href="/wiki/Matilda_Rapaport" title="Matilda Rapaport">Matilda Rapaport</a>, who died in an avalanche in 2016.</p>
<p>He finished fifth in the <a href="/wiki/FIS_Alpine_World_Ski_Championships_2009_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_slalom" title="FIS Alpine World Ski Championships 2009 – Men's slalom">slalom</a> at the <a href="/wiki/FIS_Alpine_World_Ski_Championships_2009" title="FIS Alpine World Ski Championships 2009">2009 World Championships</a> and competed for <a href="/wiki/Sweden_at_the_2010_Winter_Olympics" title="Sweden at the 2010 Winter Olympics">Sweden at the 2010 Winter Olympics</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Alpine_skiing_at_the_2010_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_slalom" title="Alpine skiing at the 2010 Winter Olympics – Men's slalom">slalom</a>, finishing in 14th place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January <a href="/wiki/2011_Alpine_Skiing_World_Cup" title="2011 Alpine Skiing World Cup">2011</a>, Hargin made a strong comeback in a World Cup slalom in <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a> to finish third. He was the last qualifier at 30th after the first run, but had the best time in the second run to attain his first World Cup podium. Hargin was the runner-up in a World Cup slalom at <a href="/wiki/Val-d%27Is%C3%A8re" title="Val-d'Isère">Val-d'Isère</a> in December <a href="/wiki/2014_Alpine_Skiing_World_Cup" title="2014 Alpine Skiing World Cup">2013</a>, his best World Cup result to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His first World Cup win came in January <a href="/wiki/2015_Alpine_Skiing_World_Cup" title="2015 Alpine Skiing World Cup">2015</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hahnenkamm,_Kitzb%C3%BChel" title="Hahnenkamm, Kitzbühel">Kitzbühel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hohohs_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-hohohs-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
