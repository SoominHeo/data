<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Lanig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_squads">Youth squads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Lanig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Youth squads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lanig began playing for SV Königshofen. Later he played for TSV Tauberbischofsheim and <a href="/wiki/1._FC_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="1. FC Nürnberg">1. FC Nürnberg</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FV_Lauda_.2F_Hoffenheim_.2F_Greuther_F.C3.BCrth">FV Lauda / Hoffenheim / Greuther Fürth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Lanig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: FV Lauda / Hoffenheim / Greuther Fürth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lanig started his career with lower division team FV Lauda in 2002. The next year he moved to the <a href="/wiki/Regionalliga_S%C3%BCd" title="Regionalliga Süd">Regionalliga Süd</a> club <a href="/wiki/TSG_1899_Hoffenheim" title="TSG 1899 Hoffenheim">TSG 1899 Hoffenheim</a> and in 2006 he signed with the <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a> club <a href="/wiki/SpVgg_Greuther_F%C3%BCrth" title="SpVgg Greuther Fürth">SpVgg Greuther Fürth</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="VfB_Stuttgart_.2F_FC_K.C3.B6ln_.2F_Eintracht_Frankfurt">VfB Stuttgart / FC Köln / Eintracht Frankfurt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Lanig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: VfB Stuttgart / FC Köln / Eintracht Frankfurt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2008, he transferred from <a href="/wiki/SpVgg_Greuther_F%C3%BCrth" title="SpVgg Greuther Fürth">Greuther Fürth</a> to <a href="/wiki/VfB_Stuttgart" title="VfB Stuttgart">VfB Stuttgart</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> where he had the opportunity to play for the first time in the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>. Lanig scored his first goal for VfB Stuttgart on 5 October 2008 against <a href="/wiki/SV_Werder_Bremen" title="SV Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a>.</p>
<p>In July 2010, he moved to <a href="/wiki/1._FC_K%C3%B6ln" title="1. FC Köln">1. FC Köln</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and two years later he transferred to <a href="/wiki/Eintracht_Frankfurt" title="Eintracht Frankfurt">Eintracht Frankfurt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="APOEL">APOEL</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Lanig&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: APOEL">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 January 2015, aged 30, Lanig moved abroad for the first time and signed an 18-month contract with <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cypriot</a> club <a href="/wiki/APOEL_FC" title="APOEL FC">APOEL FC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He made his debut on 31 January 2015, playing the full 90 minutes in <a href="/wiki/APOEL_FC" title="APOEL FC">APOEL's</a> 0–1 away victory against <a href="/wiki/Nea_Salamis_Famagusta_FC" title="Nea Salamis Famagusta FC">Nea Salamina</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Cypriot_First_Division" title="2014–15 Cypriot First Division">Cypriot First Division</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He scored his first official goal for APOEL on 7 February 2015, opening the scoreline in his team's 4–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Ethnikos_Achna_FC" title="Ethnikos Achna FC">Ethnikos Achna</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Cypriot_First_Division" title="Cypriot First Division">Cypriot First Division</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In his first season at APOEL, Lanig managed to win his first two career titles, as his team won both the Cypriot <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Cypriot_First_Division" title="2014–15 Cypriot First Division">championship</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Cypriot_Cup" title="2014–15 Cypriot Cup">cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 16 June 2015, APOEL announced that Lanig's contract with the club was terminated by mutual consent, as the player wanted to return to Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> APOEL was the last stop of his playing career, as a few months later, he announced his retirement from football at age 31, due to multiple and serious injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
