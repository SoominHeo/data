<p><b>Martin Harris</b> (born December 31, 1981) is a British <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a> better known by his <a href="/wiki/Ring_name" title="Ring name">ring name</a> <b>Martin Stone</b>. Stone works in the British independent circuit with promotions such as <a href="/wiki/Insane_Championship_Wrestling" title="Insane Championship Wrestling">Insane Championship Wrestling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frontier_Wrestling_Alliance" title="Frontier Wrestling Alliance">Frontier Wrestling Alliance</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_Pro_Wrestling:_United_Kingdom" title="International Pro Wrestling: United Kingdom">International Pro Wrestling: United Kingdom</a>. Between 2011 and 2014, he was signed with <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_promotion" title="Professional wrestling promotion">professional wrestling promotion</a> <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> and worked in its <a href="/wiki/Farm_team" title="Farm team">developmental territory</a>, <a href="/wiki/WWE_NXT" title="WWE NXT">WWE NXT</a>, under the ring name <b>Danny Burch</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WWEbio_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-WWEbio-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_wrestling_career">Professional wrestling career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Stone_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional wrestling career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_independent_circuit_.282003.E2.80.932012.29">European independent circuit (2003–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Stone_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: European independent circuit (2003–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Harris trained in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FWA_Academy" title="FWA Academy">FWA Academy</a> and Dropkixx Academy in 2003/04; competing under the ring name <b>Joe Riot</b> in various shows, including a match for All Star Wrestling in Croydon in Summer 2003;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> making his first appearance at FWA:A's <i>Revenge – Chapter IV</i> in losing effort to Leroy Kincaide on 29 November 2003 in <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth" title="Portsmouth">Portsmouth</a>. Harris made his first main roster appearance under the name "Martin Stone" on 4 September 2004, in a losing effort to Jack Xavier at a <i>Live in <a href="/wiki/Morecambe" title="Morecambe">Morecambe</a></i> show. Stone would soon be paired with fellow FWA:A trainee Stixx and <a href="/wiki/Manager_(professional_wrestling)" title="Manager (professional wrestling)">manager</a> "Twisted Genius" Dean Ayass to form the <a href="/wiki/Tag_team" title="Tag team">tag team</a> Stixx and Stone.</p>
<p>While still in the FWA, Stone travelled all over the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, competing in several other promotions including: IPW:UK, <a href="/wiki/One_Pro_Wrestling" title="One Pro Wrestling">One Pro Wrestling</a>, LDN Wrestling, and <a href="/wiki/Real_Quality_Wrestling" title="Real Quality Wrestling">Real Quality Wrestling</a>. Stone won several top titles in these promotions the most notable of which would be his 15-month title reign as <a href="/wiki/IPW:UK_Championship" title="IPW:UK Championship">IPW:UK Champion</a>.</p>
<p>Back in the FWA, Stixx and Stone would quickly capture the <a href="/wiki/FWA_Tag_Team_Championship" title="FWA Tag Team Championship">FWA Tag Team Championships</a> from the team of Hampton Court (Duke of Danger and <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Simmons" title="Andrew Simmons">Simmons</a>) on 18 June 2005 at <i>FWA <a href="/wiki/Pro_Wrestling_Noah" title="Pro Wrestling Noah">NOAH</a> Limits</i>. Stone would be stripped of the title 16 months later after being unable to make a mandatory title defence on 19 November 2006 due to commitments with IPW:UK.</p>
<p>The tag title strip happened at the height of FWA's "Civil War" with IPW:UK, which would quickly see then-IPW:UK champion Stone officially leave FWA to stay on the side of IPW:UK. Stone would lead the war against the FWA and is in preparation to face semi-retired FWA star <a href="/wiki/Alex_Shane" title="Alex Shane">Alex Shane</a> in a Promotion v Promotion – Winner Takes All Match at <a href="/wiki/Broxbourne" title="Broxbourne">Broxbourne</a> on 16 March 2007. This match however ended up taking place in Orpington several weeks later, with Flash Barker replacing an injured Alex Shane.</p>
<p>In late April 2007, Stone represented Real Quality Wrestling in the prestigious <a href="/wiki/King_of_Europe_Cup" title="King of Europe Cup">King of Europe Cup</a> where he was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Go_Shiozaki" title="Go Shiozaki">Go Shiozaki</a> in the first round.</p>
<p>During this time Stone would compete in and win an RQW tournament at <i>Not Just For Christmas</i> to become RQW's Heavyweight Champion. Stone defeated Iceman and Aviv Maayan in the first two rounds before defeating <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Neville" title="Adrian Neville">Pac</a> in the final to win the vacant championship.</p>
<p>In September 2007, Stone began to compete regularly for the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> promotion westside Xtreme wrestling. On 26 July 2008, Stone won the wXw Tag Team Championship alongside <a href="/wiki/Doug_Williams_(wrestler)" title="Doug Williams (wrestler)">Doug Williams</a> when the pair beat AbLas (Absolute Andy and Steve Douglas) at wXw Broken Rulz VIII in <a href="/wiki/Oberhausen" title="Oberhausen">Oberhausen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2008, Stone competed <a href="/wiki/Chikara_(professional_wrestling)" title="Chikara (professional wrestling)">Chikara</a>'s <a href="/wiki/King_of_Trios#2008" title="King of Trios">King of Trios</a> tournament in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, teaming with <a href="/wiki/The_Kartel" title="The Kartel">The Kartel</a> as Team IPW:UK, but were defeated in the second round of the tournament by the Golden Trio (<a href="/wiki/Delirious_(wrestler)" title="Delirious (wrestler)">Delirious</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hallowicked" title="Hallowicked">Hallowicked</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ricochet_(wrestler)" title="Ricochet (wrestler)">Helios</a>). On 28 August 2008, Stone defeated Eamon O'Neill and <a href="/wiki/James_Tighe" title="James Tighe">James Tighe</a> to win the Premier Promotions Worthing Trophy.</p>
<p>On 13 February 2010 at the British Uproar event in Broxbourne, Martin Stone defeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andy_Simmons" title="Andy Simmons">Andy Simmonz</a> in the tournament finals to become the first FWA World Heavyweight Champion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uproar_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-uproar-4">[4]</a></sup> In a speech after the match he turned against the fans and claimed that he was only using British wrestling as a stepping stone to a big-money contract in the US. A month later he became the leader of "The Agenda", a faction of FWA wrestlers with aims along the same lines.</p>
<p>On 14 March 2010 at PW101 Unstoppable at The Hubs in Sheffield. Martin Stone defeated 11 other men in the 101 Championship Rush match last defeating Martin Kirby to become Pro Wrestling 101's first and only Heavyweight Champion. Upon returning to Britain in 2014, Stone challenged for Jack Jester's <a href="/wiki/ICW_World_Heavyweight_Championship_(Scotland)" title="ICW World Heavyweight Championship (Scotland)">ICW Heavyweight Championship</a> but was defeated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="WWE">WWE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Stone_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: WWE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="NXT_.282011.E2.80.932014.29">NXT (2011–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Stone_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: NXT (2011–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In late 2011, Harris signed with WWE and reported to its developmental territory in June 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He was given the ring name Danny Burch,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and he made his televised debut for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NXT_Wrestling" title="NXT Wrestling">NXT Wrestling</a> on 15 May 2013, episode of <i>NXT</i> with a loss to <a href="/wiki/Bray_Wyatt" title="Bray Wyatt">Bray Wyatt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> From then on, Burch was used sporadically as enhancement talent until it was reported on 30 April 2014 that he was released by WWE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Total_Nonstop_Action_Wrestling_.282014.E2.80.932015.29">Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (2014–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Stone_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (2014–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014 Harris, under his Martin Stone name, appeared on the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/TNA_British_Boot_Camp" title="TNA British Boot Camp">TNA British Boot Camp</a></i>. On 16 February 2015 Harris competed at the <a href="/wiki/TNA_One_Night_Only" title="TNA One Night Only">TNA One Night Only</a>: <i><a href="/wiki/TNA_One_Night_Only#TNA_Gut_Check" title="TNA One Night Only">Gut Check</a></i>, where he defeated <a href="/wiki/Jessie_Godderz" title="Jessie Godderz">Jessie Godderz</a> to qualify for the final match later that night. He then competed in a five-way elimination match where the winner of that match would earn an appearance on the next episode of <i>Impact Wrestling</i>, though he was unsuccessful as the match was won by <a href="/wiki/Camacho_(wrestler)" title="Camacho (wrestler)">Tevita Fifita</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_WWE_.282015.E2.80.93present.29">Return to WWE (2015–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Stone_(wrestler)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to WWE (2015–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 16, 2015, though not re-signed to WWE, Stone made an appearance on an <i>NXT</i> television taping, where he lost to <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Owens" title="Kevin Owens">Kevin Owens</a>. He made a second appearance on the August 13, 2015 taping of <i>NXT</i>, where he lost to <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Crews" title="Apollo Crews">Apollo Crews</a>. He made a third appearance on 16 September, where he was referred to as his old <i>NXT</i> name Danny Burch, losing to <a href="/wiki/Tye_Dillinger" title="Tye Dillinger">Tye Dillinger</a>. He made a fourth appearance on the October 21, 2015 taping of <i>NXT</i>, where he lost to <a href="/wiki/James_Storm" title="James Storm">James Storm</a>. Burch made a further appearance on <i>NXT</i> on January 13, 2016 in a losing effort against <a href="/wiki/Tommaso_Ciampa" title="Tommaso Ciampa">Tommaso Ciampa</a>. He again faced Ciampa and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Gargano" title="Johnny Gargano">Johnny Gargano</a>, teaming up with Rob Ryzin on the May 18 taping of NXT, losing again. On September 14, 2016, during the <a href="/wiki/Cruiserweight_Classic#Finale" title="Cruiserweight Classic">Cruiserweight Classic Finale</a> he teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Sean_Maluta" title="Sean Maluta">Sean Maluta</a> and lost to <a href="/wiki/The_Bollywood_Boyz" title="The Bollywood Boyz">The Bollywood Boyz</a>, Gurv Sihra and Harv Sihra, in a dark match. On January 6, 2017, it was announced by <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a> that Burch will be a participant in the 16 man <a href="/wiki/WWE_United_Kingdom_Championship_Tournament" title="WWE United Kingdom Championship Tournament">WWE United Kingdom Championship Tournament</a>. Burch was eliminated in the first round by <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Devlin" title="Jordan Devlin">Jordan Devlin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UKTitle01142017_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-UKTitle01142017-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
