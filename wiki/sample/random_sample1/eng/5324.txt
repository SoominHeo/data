<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Mache (unit)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Mache</b> (symbol <b>ME</b> from German Mache-Einheit, plural Maches) is an obsolete unit of volumic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radioactivity" title="Radioactivity">radioactivity</a> named for the Austrian physicist <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Mache" title="Heinrich Mache">Heinrich Mache</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was defined as the quantity of <a href="/wiki/Radon" title="Radon">radon</a> (ignoring its daughters; in practice, mostly radon-222) per litre of air which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ionises" title="Ionises">ionises</a> a sustained current of 0.001 <a href="/wiki/Electrostatic_units" title="Electrostatic units">esu</a> (0.001 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/StatAmpere" title="StatAmpere">StatAmpere</a>).</p>
<p>1 ME = 3.64 Eman = 3.64×10<sup>−10</sup> <a href="/wiki/Curie" title="Curie">Ci</a>/L = 13.4545 <a href="/wiki/Becquerel" title="Becquerel">Bq</a>/L.</p>
