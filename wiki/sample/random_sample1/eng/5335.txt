<p><b>Victorio Maximiliano</b> "<b>Maxi</b>" <b>Pereira Páez</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[ˈmaksi peˈrejra]</a></span>; born 8 June 1984) is a Uruguayan professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">right back</a> for Portuguese club <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay national team</a>.</p>
<p>He played with <a href="/wiki/Defensor_Sporting" title="Defensor Sporting">Defensor</a> in Uruguay, joining <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> in 2007 and going on to remain with the club for eight seasons, appearing in 333 official games and winning eleven major titles.</p>
<p>A Uruguayan international on more than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_Century_Club" title="FIFA Century Club">100 occasions</a>, Pereira represented the nation in two <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cups</a> and four <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="Copa América">Copa América</a> tournaments, winning the <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2011 Copa América">2011 edition</a> of the latter tournament.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maxi_Pereira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Defensor">Defensor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maxi_Pereira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Defensor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Montevideo" title="Montevideo">Montevideo</a>, Pereira started playing professionally with <a href="/wiki/Defensor_Sporting" title="Defensor Sporting">Defensor Sporting</a> as an 18-year-old, early in the 2002–03 season. He took no time to adjust to the first team, being a crucial part alongside <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Rafael_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="Álvaro Rafael González">Álvaro González</a> and scoring a career-best 12 goals in the <a href="/wiki/Uruguayan_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Uruguayan Primera División">Primera División</a> of <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Uruguayan_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2005–06 Uruguayan Primera División">2005–06</a>.</p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Uruguayan_Primera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2006–07 Uruguayan Primera División">2006–07 campaign</a>, Defensor lost both gems as González was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Boca_Juniors" title="Boca Juniors">Boca Juniors</a> and Pereira went to <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a>, accompanied by countryman <a href="/wiki/Cristian_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Cristian Rodríguez">Cristian Rodríguez</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the latter club signed the player for €3 million and 70% of his rights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Benfica">Benfica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maxi_Pereira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Benfica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Zen-Benf_(2).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="365" data-file-width="298" height="269" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Zen-Benf_%282%29.jpg/220px-Zen-Benf_%282%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d7/Zen-Benf_%282%29.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Zen-Benf_(2).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Pereira playing for <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> in 2014</div>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Primeira_Liga" title="2007–08 Primeira Liga">his first year</a> in Portugal, Pereira was initially played as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Wide_midfielder" title="Midfielder">right midfielder</a>, failing to adjust to that position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He did score in two consecutive home games in November 2007, in a 6–1 league thrashing of <a href="/wiki/Boavista_F.C." title="Boavista F.C.">Boavista F.C.</a> and in <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2007–08 UEFA Champions League">the season</a>'s <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a>, where he netted with his left foot from outside the box.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Primeira_Liga" title="2008–09 Primeira Liga">The following campaign</a>, Pereira switched to right-back after <a href="/wiki/N%C3%A9lson_Marcos" title="Nélson Marcos">Nélson Marcos</a> moved to <a href="/wiki/Real_Betis" title="Real Betis">Real Betis</a>, and became an everpresent fixture during their campaign. In <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Primeira_Liga" title="2009–10 Primeira Liga">2009–10</a>, as Benfica won the league after five years – <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Ta%C3%A7a_da_Liga" title="2009–10 Taça da Liga">adding</a> the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%A7a_da_Liga" title="Taça da Liga">Taça da Liga</a> – he appeared in a total of 40 matches, scoring five goals in 2,932 minutes of action.</p>
<p>On 17 December 2011, Pereira renewed his contract until 2015 and the club paid €2.7 million for the remaining 30% of his rights, still in possession of Defensor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He netted two goals in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2011–12 UEFA Champions League">2011–12 Champions League</a>, both coming against <a href="/wiki/FC_Zenit_Saint_Petersburg" title="FC Zenit Saint Petersburg">FC Zenit Saint Petersburg</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Single-elimination_tournament" title="Single-elimination tournament">round-of-16</a> (2–3 away loss, 2–0 home win, both through tap-ins)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> as his team eventually reached the quarter-finals of the competition, where he was <a href="/wiki/Ejection_(sports)" title="Ejection (sports)">sent off</a> in the first half of the second leg against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> after two bookable offenses (1–1 away draw, 1–2 aggregate loss).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 2 October 2014, Pereira reached the 300-game milestone at Benfica, in a <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2014–15 UEFA Champions League">Champions League match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer 04 Leverkusen</a>, becoming the second foreigner behind <a href="/wiki/Luis%C3%A3o" title="Luisão">Luisão</a> to achieve this feat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 2 May of the following year, he scored twice in a 5–0 win at <a href="/wiki/Gil_Vicente_F.C." title="Gil Vicente F.C.">Gil Vicente FC</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> contributing with a further three goals as the team <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Primeira_Liga" title="2014–15 Primeira Liga">renewed their domestic supremacy</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Porto">Porto</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maxi_Pereira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Porto">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 June 2015, Pereira left Benfica following the expiration of his contract. Fifteen days later, he joined rivals FC Porto as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>-bound <a href="/wiki/Danilo_(footballer,_born_1991)" title="Danilo (footballer, born 1991)">Danilo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> His competitive debut occurred on 15 August, in a 3–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%B3ria_S.C." title="Vitória S.C.">Vitória S.C.</a> where he provided two <a href="/wiki/Assist_(football)" title="Assist (football)">assists</a> for teammates <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Aboubakar" title="Vincent Aboubakar">Vincent Aboubakar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Silvestre_Varela" title="Silvestre Varela">Silvestre Varela</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The following 6 March, he scored his first goal for the team, a late equaliser in a 1–3 loss at <a href="/wiki/S.C._Braga" title="S.C. Braga">S.C. Braga</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 22 May 2016, in <a href="/wiki/2016_Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal_Final" title="2016 Taça de Portugal Final">the final</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="Taça de Portugal">Taça de Portugal</a>, also against Braga, Pereira's attempt in the <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shootout</a> was saved by <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Coentr%C3%A3o_Marafona" title="José Carlos Coentrão Marafona">José Carlos Marafona</a>, in an eventual 2–4 defeat (<a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="2015–16 Taça de Portugal">2–2 after 120 minutes</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maxi_Pereira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>A <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguayan international</a> since making his debut on 26 October 2005 in a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly</a> loss, away against <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a>, Pereira quickly became a regular for his country, and represented it at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2007 Copa América">2007 Copa América</a>. He was selected for the squad at the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, playing all the games and minutes for the eventual semfinalists: in the quarterfinals against <a href="/wiki/Ghana_national_football_team" title="Ghana national football team">Ghana</a>, he missed his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout_(association_football)" title="Penalty shootout (association football)">penalty shootout</a> attempt but Uruguay eventually qualified for the next stage, where he scored his first goal as an international in a 2–3 loss against the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a>, finding the net in the 91st minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pereira was again an undisputed starter for Uruguay in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2011 Copa América">2011 Copa América</a> held in Argentina, with the national team winning its 15th continental tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He played 14 times <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CONMEBOL)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (CONMEBOL)">in qualification</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 World Cup</a>, netting once in a 4–2 home win over <a href="/wiki/Peru_national_football_team" title="Peru national football team">Peru</a> on 10 June 2012<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and opening the scoring as Uruguay won 5–0 away to <a href="/wiki/Jordan_national_football_team" title="Jordan national football team">Jordan</a> in <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(AFC%E2%80%93CONMEBOL_play-off)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC–CONMEBOL play-off)">a play-off</a> to qualify for the tournament, on 13 November of the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pereira was the first player sent off in the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">finals in Brazil</a>, receiving his marching orders in injury time of the 1–3 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica_national_football_team" title="Costa Rica national football team">Costa Rica</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fortaleza" title="Fortaleza">Fortaleza</a> after kicking <a href="/wiki/Joel_Campbell" title="Joel Campbell">Joel Campbell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> He played a further two games in the tournament, which ended for the <i>Charrúas</i> at the last-16 stage.</p>
<p>On 28 March 2015, Pereira earned his <a href="/wiki/List_of_footballers_with_100_or_more_caps" title="List of footballers with 100 or more caps">100th cap</a> in a 1–0 friendly victory over <a href="/wiki/Morocco_national_football_team" title="Morocco national football team">Morocco</a> in <a href="/wiki/Agadir" title="Agadir">Agadir</a>, becoming only the second Uruguayan after <a href="/wiki/Diego_Forl%C3%A1n" title="Diego Forlán">Diego Forlán</a> to reach the milestone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He played every minute of the campaign at <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="2015 Copa América">that year's Copa América</a>, <a href="/wiki/Captain_(association_football)" title="Captain (association football)">captaining</a> the team in their final <a href="/wiki/2015_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Group_B" title="2015 Copa América Group B">group</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Paraguay_national_football_team" title="Paraguay national football team">Paraguay</a> in place of the suspended <a href="/wiki/Diego_God%C3%ADn" title="Diego Godín">Diego Godín</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 June 2016, Pereira surpassed Forlán as Uruguay's most capped player by earning his 113th in the team's final group game at the <a href="/wiki/Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_Centenario" title="Copa América Centenario">Copa América Centenario</a> in the United States;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> the 1–0 loss to <a href="/wiki/Venezuela_national_football_team" title="Venezuela national football team">Venezuela</a> eliminated the nation.</p>
