<p><i><b>Codonopsis pilosula</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh"><a href="/wiki/Shangdang" title="Shangdang">党</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%8F%82" title="wiktionary:参">参</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">dǎngshēn</span></i>), also known as <b>dang shen</b> or <b>poor man's ginseng</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Perennial_plant" title="Perennial plant">perennial</a> species of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plant</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Campanulaceae" title="Campanulaceae">bellflower family</a>. It is native to Asia, where it grows in forests, meadows, and <a href="/wiki/Shrubland" title="Shrubland">scrub</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-china_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-china-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Codonopsis_pilosula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The plant produces twining stems up to 2 meters long. It has lateral branches with alternately arranged leaves and small branchlets with oppositely arranged leaves. The ovate leaves are up to 7.3 centimeters long and are usually coated with short hairs. Solitary flowers occur at the branch tips. The bell-shaped flower is about 2 centimeters long and wide and is yellow-green with purple spots inside. The fruit capsule is up to 2.4 centimeters long.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-china_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-china-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditional_uses">Traditional uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Codonopsis_pilosula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Traditional uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The roots of <i>C. pilosula</i> are used in <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_medicine" title="Traditional Chinese medicine">traditional Chinese medicine</a>. They are carrot-shaped or cylindrical, sometimes branched, and up to 30 centimeters long by 3 wide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-china_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-china-1">[1]</a></sup> They are a constituent of Radix Codonopsis, an herbal mix used to improve appetite and energy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-li_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-li-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
