<p><b>Mansour bin Zayed bin Sultan bin Zayed bin Khalifa Al Nahyan</b> (born 20 November 1970), commonly known as <b>Sheikh Mansour</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smguard1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-smguard1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraphapril2013_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraphapril2013-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-indep-3">[3]</a></sup> is the deputy prime minister of the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>, minister of presidential affairs and member of the ruling family of Abu Dhabi. He is the half brother of the current President of UAE, <a href="/wiki/Khalifa_bin_Zayed_Al_Nahyan" title="Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan">Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cabinet_Members_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cabinet_Members-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is also the chairman of the ministerial council for services, the Emirates Investment Authority and the Emirates Racing Authority. He sits on the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Petroleum_Council" title="Supreme Petroleum Council">Supreme Petroleum Council</a> and the boards of numerous investment companies including the <a href="/wiki/International_Petroleum_Investment_Company" title="International Petroleum Investment Company">International Petroleum Investment Company</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_Investment_Council" title="Abu Dhabi Investment Council">Abu Dhabi Investment Council</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cabinet_Members_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cabinet_Members-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mansour also owns stakes in a number of business ventures, including <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Galactic" title="Virgin Galactic">Virgin Galactic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sky_News_Arabia" title="Sky News Arabia">Sky News Arabia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He is also the owner of the privately held <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_United_Group" title="Abu Dhabi United Group">Abu Dhabi United Group</a> (ADUG), a specialist investment company that successfully acquired <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City Football Club</a> in September 2008, and which has overseen a significant transformation at the Club since that time,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> most notably, the club have won two top flight league titles for the first time since 1968, City's first <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> titles. On May 21, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a> of the United States announced that its second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_City_Metropolitan_Area" title="New York City Metropolitan Area">New York City Metropolitan Area</a> club, to be called <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_FC" title="New York City FC">New York City FC</a>, would begin play in the 2015 season, to be majority-owned by Mansour bin Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, in association with brothers <a href="/wiki/Hal_Steinbrenner" title="Hal Steinbrenner">Hal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hank_Steinbrenner" title="Hank Steinbrenner">Hank Steinbrenner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mansour_bin_Zayed_Al_Nahyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mansour was born in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_(emirate)" title="Abu Dhabi (emirate)">Abu Dhabi emirate</a> on 20 November 1970, the fifth son of the Emir of Abu Dhabi <a href="/wiki/Zayed_bin_Sultan_Al_Nahyan" title="Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan">HH Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-indep-3">[3]</a></sup> His mother is Sheikha <a href="/wiki/Fatima_bint_Mubarak_Al_Ketbi" title="Fatima bint Mubarak Al Ketbi">Fatima bint Mubarak Al Ketbi</a> and he has five full-brothers: <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_bin_Zayed_Al_Nahyan" title="Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan">Crown Prince Mohammed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamdan_bin_Zayed_bin_Sultan_Al_Nahyan" title="Hamdan bin Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan">Hamdan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hazza_bin_Zayed_Al_Nahyan" title="Hazza bin Zayed Al Nahyan">Hazza</a>, Tahnoun, and <a href="/wiki/Abdullah_bin_Zayed_Al_Nahyan" title="Abdullah bin Zayed Al Nahyan">Abdullah</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wleaks004_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-wleaks004-9">[9]</a></sup> They are known as Bani Fatima or sons of Fatima.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft5may_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ft5may-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mansour attended <a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara_City_College" title="Santa Barbara City College">Santa Barbara Community College</a> as an English student in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wleaks22nov_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-wleaks22nov-11">[11]</a></sup> He is a graduate of <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates_University" title="United Arab Emirates University">United Arab Emirates University</a> where he received a bachelor's degree in international affairs in 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wleaks22nov_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-wleaks22nov-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mansour_bin_Zayed_Al_Nahyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1997, Mansour bin Zayed was appointed chairman of the presidential office, which his father Zayed II is the first and by-then president of UAE. After the death of his father, he was appointed by his eldest half brother, <a href="/wiki/Khalifa_bin_Zayed_Al_Nahyan" title="Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan">Khalifa II</a>, as first minister of presidential affairs of the United Arab Emirates, which is the merger of the presidential office and presidential court. He also served in a number of positions in Abu Dhabi to support his brother, Crown Prince <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_bin_Zayed_Al_Nahyan" title="Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan">Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan</a>.</p>
<p>He was appointed chairman of the ministerial council for services, which is considered a ministerial entity attached to the Cabinet, comprising a number of ministers heading the services departments. Since 2000 he chaired <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Center_for_Documentation_and_Research" title="National Center for Documentation and Research">National Center for Documentation and Research</a>. In 2004 reshuffle, he became minister for presidential affairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wleaks22nov_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-wleaks22nov-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2005, he became the deputy chairman of the Abu Dhabi education council (ADEC), chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Emirates_Foundation" title="Emirates Foundation">Emirates Foundation</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Food_Control_Authority&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Abu Dhabi Food Control Authority (page does not exist)">Abu Dhabi Food Control Authority</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_Fund_for_Development" title="Abu Dhabi Fund for Development">Abu Dhabi Fund for Development</a>. In 2006, he was named the chairman of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Judicial_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Abu Dhabi Judicial Department (page does not exist)">Abu Dhabi Judicial Department</a>. In 2007, he was appointed chairman of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Khalifa_bin_Zayed_Charity_Foundation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Khalifa bin Zayed Charity Foundation (page does not exist)">Khalifa bin Zayed Charity Foundation</a>.</p>
<p>Mansour served as the chairman of <a href="/wiki/First_Gulf_Bank" title="First Gulf Bank">First Gulf Bank</a> until 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and as a member of the board of trustees of the Zayed charitable and humanitarian foundation. Mansour has established scholarship programs for U.A.E students to study abroad. He is also chairman of the Emirates horse racing authority (EHRA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-indep-3">[3]</a></sup> On 11 May 2009, he was appointed deputy prime minister, retaining his cabinet post of minister of presidential affairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gnews11may_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-gnews11may-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<p>Mansour heads the <a href="/wiki/International_Petroleum_Investment_Company" title="International Petroleum Investment Company">International Petroleum Investment Company</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_BusinessWeek_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_BusinessWeek-14">[14]</a></sup> which owns 71% of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aabar_Investments_PJSC" title="Aabar Investments PJSC">Aabar Investments</a> and is used as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Investment_vehicle" title="Investment vehicle">investment vehicle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thesun_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-thesun-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, he was appointed as member of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ADNOC" title="ADNOC">Supreme Petroleum Council</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_BusinessWeek_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_BusinessWeek-14">[14]</a></sup> In the same year he chaired the board of directors for <a href="/wiki/International_Petroleum_Investment_Company" title="International Petroleum Investment Company">International Petroleum Investment Company</a> and became the board member of <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_Investment_Authority" title="Abu Dhabi Investment Authority">Abu Dhabi Investment Authority</a> (ADIA). In 2007, he was appointed chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Emirates_Investment_Authority" title="Emirates Investment Authority">Emirates Investment Authority</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_wealth_fund" title="Sovereign wealth fund">sovereign wealth fund</a> of UAE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_BusinessWeek_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_BusinessWeek-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mansour has a 32% stake in <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Galactic" title="Virgin Galactic">Virgin Galactic</a> after investing $280m in the project through <a href="/wiki/Aabar_Investments" title="Aabar Investments">Aabar</a> in July 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Aabar also has a 9.1% stake in <a href="/wiki/Daimler_AG" title="Daimler AG">Daimler</a> after purchasing the stake for $2.7 billion in March 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and it was reported that Aabar wishes to increase its stake to 15% in August 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He owns the Abu Dhabi Media Investment Corporation (ADMIC) which partnered with British Sky Broadcasting to establish Sky News Arabia â a new Arabic-language news channel headquartered in the Emirate of Abu Dhabi.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mansour_bin_Zayed_Al_Nahyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mansour is an accomplished horse rider and has won a number of endurance racing tournaments held in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, and is chairman of the Emirates horse racing authority. He is a strong supporter of Arabian horse racing through the Sheik Mansour global Arabian flat racing festival with races on 5 continents. He is the patron of an annual road <a href="/wiki/Running" title="Running">running</a> competition in the Abu Dhabi, the <a href="/wiki/Zayed_International_Half_Marathon" title="Zayed International Half Marathon">Zayed International Half Marathon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is also the chairman of the Al Jazira sports company and was a leading figure in the Emirate of Abu Dhabiâs successful bid to host the FIFA Club World Cup in the UAE in both 2009 and 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The company owns <a href="/wiki/Al_Jazira_Club" title="Al Jazira Club">Al Jazira Club</a>, which plays football, <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_handball" title="Team handball">handball</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The football club achieved the Presidentâs Cup in 2010-2011 and 2011-2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sheikh Mansour owns City Football Group, which consists of Manchester City F.C, Melbourne City F.C and New York City F.C. In 2009, HH Sheikh Mansour bought the City Football Group for 265 Million British Pounds. Approximately one year ago, HH Sheikh Mansour sold only 13% of City Football Group for the same price.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mansour_bin_Zayed_Al_Nahyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mansour has two wives and five children. He married Sheikha Alia bint Mohammed bin Butti Al Hamed in the mid-1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wleaks22nov_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-wleaks22nov-11">[11]</a></sup> They have one son, Zayed. He married Sheikha <a href="/wiki/Manal_bint_Mohammed_bin_Rashid_Al_Maktoum" title="Manal bint Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum">Manal bint Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum</a>, president of the Dubai women establishment in May 2005, and his second cousin once removed. They have four children, two daughters and two sons:</p>
<ul>
<li>Fatima (born 9 June 2006).</li>
<li>Mohammed (born 4 December 2007).</li>
<li>Hamdan (born 21 June 2011).</li>
<li>Latifa (born 23 January 2014).</li>
</ul>
