<p><br/>
<i><b>Researches on Manchu Origins</b></i>, also known as <i><b>Manzhou Yuanliu Kao</b></i>, is an important history book published by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qing_Dynasty" title="Qing Dynasty">Qing Dynasty</a> government in 1777. The <a href="/wiki/Qianlong_Emperor" title="Qianlong Emperor">Qianlong Emperor</a> sponsored its compilation with the goal of legitimizing Qing rule, as well as identifying the Qing as a successor to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jinn_Dynasty" title="Jinn Dynasty">Jinn Dynasty</a> (1115–1234).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Manzhou Yuanliu Kao also bolstered Qianlong's conception of the <a href="/wiki/Manchu_people" title="Manchu people">Manchu people</a> as a <i><a href="/wiki/Wen_and_wu" title="Wen and wu">wu</a></i>, or <a href="/wiki/Martial_race" title="Martial race">martial race</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It consists of 4 parts: Manchu tribes, territory, topography (mountains and rivers), and culture. <a href="/wiki/Pamela_Kyle_Crossley" title="Pamela Kyle Crossley">Pamela Kyle Crossley</a> analyses it as the apex of the Qing Dynasty's attempt at "documentary institutionalisation" of Manchu heritage and from it, Manchu ethnic identity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Researches on Manchu Origins contained a list of corrections of transcribed <a href="/wiki/Jurchen_language" title="Jurchen language">Jurchen language</a> words found in the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Jin" title="History of Jin">History of Jin</a> in Chapter 135, using the <a href="/wiki/Manchu_language" title="Manchu language">Manchu language</a> to correct them, in Chapter 18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>It claimed that the <a href="/wiki/Samhan" title="Samhan">Samhan</a> were three different groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
