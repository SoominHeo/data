<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Language">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay language</a> or Bahasa Melayu, a major Austronesian language spoken in Malaysia, Indonesia, Brunei, and Singapore
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Malay_language#Old_Malay" title="History of the Malay language">History of the Malay language#Old Malay</a>, the Malay language from the 4th to the 14th century</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malayan_languages" title="Malayan languages">Malayan languages</a>, a group of closely related languages in the Malay Archipelago</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malay_trade_and_creole_languages" title="Malay trade and creole languages">Malay trade and creole languages</a>, a set of pidgin languages throughout the Malay Peninsula and Malay Archipelago</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Brunei_Malay" title="Brunei Malay">Brunei Malay</a>, an unofficial national language of Brunei distinct from standard Malay</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kedah_Malay" title="Kedah Malay">Kedah Malay</a>, a variety of the Malaya languages spoken in Malaysia and Thailand</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Creole_Malay" title="Sri Lankan Creole Malay">Sri Lankan Creole Malay</a>, a language spoken by the Malay ethnic minority in Sri Lanka</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian language</a>, the official form of the Malay language in Indonesia</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnic_groups">Ethnic groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ethnic groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malay_race" title="Malay race">Malay race</a>, a racial category used in the late 19th and early 20th century to describe Austronesian peoples</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malays_(ethnic_group)" title="Malays (ethnic group)">Malays (ethnic group)</a>, the ethnic group located primarily in the Malay peninsula, and parts of Sumatra and Borneo</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bruneian_Malay_people" title="Bruneian Malay people">Bruneian Malay people</a>, ethnic Malays in Brunei Darussalam</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cape_Malays" title="Cape Malays">Cape Malays</a>, an ethnic group or community in South Africa</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cocos_Malays" title="Cocos Malays">Cocos Malays</a>, the predominant ethnic group of the Cocos (Keeling) Islands, now part of Australia</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kedahan_Malay_people" title="Kedahan Malay people">Kedahan Malay people</a>, a large subgroup of ethnic Malays</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Malay" title="Malaysian Malay">Malaysian Malay</a>, a constitutionally defined group of Muslim Malaysian citizens</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malay_Singaporeans" title="Malay Singaporeans">Malay Singaporeans</a>, a broad ethnic group defined by the Singaporean government</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Malay_Indonesian" title="Malay Indonesian">Malay Indonesian</a>, ethnic Malays in Indonesia</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Overseas_Malays" title="Overseas Malays">Overseas Malays</a>, people of Malay ancestry living outside ethnic Malay home areas</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Malays" title="Sri Lankan Malays">Sri Lankan Malays</a>, an ethnic group in Sri Lanka of Indonesian and Malaysian ancestry</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thai_Malays" title="Thai Malays">Thai Malays</a>, ethnic Malays in Thailand</li>
</ul>
