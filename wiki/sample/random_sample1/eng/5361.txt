<p>The <b>Maloelap Atoll</b> (<a href="/wiki/Marshallese_language" title="Marshallese language">Marshallese</a>: <b><span lang="mh" xml:lang="mh">M̧aļoeļap</span></b>, <span class="IPA" style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Marshallese" title="Help:IPA for Marshallese">[mˠɑɫʌ͡ɔɔ̯-ɛ̯ɛ͡ʌɫɑ͡æpʲ]</a></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) (also spelled Maleolap) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coral_atoll" title="Coral atoll">coral atoll</a> of 71 islands in the Pacific Ocean, and forms a legislative district of the <a href="/wiki/Ratak_Chain" title="Ratak Chain">Ratak Chain</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a>. Its land area is only 9.8 square kilometres (3.8 sq mi), but that encloses a lagoon of 972 square kilometres (375 sq mi). It is located 18 kilometres (11 mi) north of the atoll of <a href="/wiki/Aur_Atoll" title="Aur Atoll">Aur</a>. In 1999 the population of the islands of the atoll was 856.</p>
<p>The largest of the islands that make up the atoll are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taroa" title="Taroa">Taroa</a> (the administrative center of the atoll), in the northeast, and <a href="/wiki/Kaben" title="Kaben">Kaben</a> in the northwest. Only three of the other islands in the atoll are inhabited: Airuk, Wolot and Jang. The island is served by <a href="/wiki/Air_Marshall_Islands" title="Air Marshall Islands">Air Marshall Islands</a> via <a href="/wiki/Maloelap_Airport" title="Maloelap Airport">Maloelap Airport</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maloelap_Atoll&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maloelap Atoll was claimed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empire_of_Germany" title="Empire of Germany">Empire of Germany</a> along with the rest of the Marshall Islands in 1884, and the Germans established a trading outpost. After World War I, the island came under the <a href="/wiki/South_Pacific_Mandate" title="South Pacific Mandate">South Pacific Mandate</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a>.</p>
<p>In 1939, the Japanese built a seaplane base and landplane <a href="/wiki/Taroa_Airfield" title="Taroa Airfield">Taroa Airfield</a> with two runways (4800' + 4100') and support buildings and facilities, including a <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">radar</a> station. During World War II the Japanese garrison of 2,940 navy men and 389 army men was commanded by Rear Admiral Shoichi Kamada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Takizawa_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Takizawa-2">[2]</a></sup> The perimeter of the island was heavily fortified with 12 heavy coastal artillery and 10 heavy anti-aircraft guns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Takizawa_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Takizawa-2">[2]</a></sup> The island was attacked by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> beginning in February 1942 starting with carrier-based aircraft and shelling by warships. The attacks grew in frequency and severity after <a href="/wiki/Majuro" title="Majuro">Majuro</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kwajalein" title="Kwajalein">Kwajalein</a> had fallen to the Americans. Of the 3097-man Japanese garrison (1772 <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a>, 368 <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a>, and 957 civilians) only 1041 (34%) survived the war. Several Marshallese were also killed. A large number of war relics, including plane wrecks, mainly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_A6M" title="Mitsubishi A6M">Mitsubishi A6M</a> Zero fighters and <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_G4M" title="Mitsubishi G4M">Mitsubishi G4M</a> Betty bombers remain scattered about.</p>
<p>Following the end of World War II, the island came under the control of the United States as part of the <a href="/wiki/Trust_Territory_of_the_Pacific_Islands" title="Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands">Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands</a> until the independence of the Marshall Islands in 1986. Maloelap Atoll was the first to ratify the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a>' constitution.</p>
