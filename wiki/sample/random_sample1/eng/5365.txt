<p><b>Malik Bendjelloul</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">مالك بن جلول</span>‎‎; 14 September 1977 – 13 May 2014) was a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> documentary filmmaker, journalist and former child actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfdb_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sfdb-3">[3]</a></sup> He directed the 2012 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Searching_for_Sugar_Man" title="Searching for Sugar Man">Searching for Sugar Man</a></i>, which won an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Documentary_Feature" title="Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature">Academy Award</a> and a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Best_Documentary" title="BAFTA Award for Best Documentary">BAFTA Award</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malik_Bendjelloul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bendjelloul was born in <a href="/wiki/Ystad" title="Ystad">Ystad</a> in Sweden, 55 kilometres (34 mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6" title="Malmö">Malmö</a>, the son of Algerian-born physician Hacène Bendjelloul and Swedish translator and painter Veronica Schildt Bendjelloul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-2">[2]</a></sup> He was the brother of journalist <a href="/wiki/Johar_Bendjelloul" title="Johar Bendjelloul">Johar Bendjelloul</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sveri_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sveri-4">[4]</a></sup> and the nephew of actors <a href="/wiki/Peter_Schildt" title="Peter Schildt">Peter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johan_Schildt" title="Johan Schildt">Johan Schildt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v.C3.A4rldsstj.C3.A4rna_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-v.C3.A4rldsstj.C3.A4rna-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Bendjelloul grew up in central and southern Sweden (<a href="/wiki/%C3%84ngelholm" title="Ängelholm">Ängelholm</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.C3.A4ngelholm_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-.C3.A4ngelholm-7">[7]</a></sup> and during the 1990s acted in the <a href="/wiki/Sveriges_Television" title="Sveriges Television">SVT</a> TV series <i><a class="extiw" href="https://sv.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ebba_och_Didrik" title="sv:Ebba och Didrik">Ebba och Didrik</a></i> as Philip Clavelle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sveri_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-sveri-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inde_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-inde-8">[8]</a></sup> The episodes were directed by his uncle, Peter Schildt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v.C3.A4rldsstj.C3.A4rna_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-v.C3.A4rldsstj.C3.A4rna-5">[5]</a></sup> Bendjelloul was educated at the Rönne Gymnasium in Ängelholm, where he entered the social science programme. He graduated in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v.C3.A4rldsstj.C3.A4rna_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-v.C3.A4rldsstj.C3.A4rna-5">[5]</a></sup> He then attended <a href="/wiki/Linnaeus_University" title="Linnaeus University">Kalmar University</a>, where he studied journalism and media production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inde_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-inde-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malik_Bendjelloul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bendjelloul started his television career as a reporter on Swedish public television (SVT), where he worked as a <a href="/wiki/Freelancer" title="Freelancer">freelancer</a> and journalist for <i><a href="/wiki/Kobra_(television_programme)" title="Kobra (television programme)">Kobra</a></i>. His career also included working as a host for the breakfast television programme <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gomorron_Sverige&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gomorron Sverige (page does not exist)">Gomorron Sverige</a></i> as well as for the morning radio programme <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=P1-morgon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="P1-morgon (page does not exist)">P1-morgon</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Sveriges_Radio" title="Sveriges Radio">Sveriges Radio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v.C3.A4rldsstj.C3.A4rna_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-v.C3.A4rldsstj.C3.A4rna-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.C3.A4ngelholm_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-.C3.A4ngelholm-7">[7]</a></sup> Prior to working for SVT, he also worked for an independent production company, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Barracuda_Film_%26_TV&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Barracuda Film &amp; TV (page does not exist)">Barracuda Film &amp; TV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-v.C3.A4rldsstj.C3.A4rna_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-v.C3.A4rldsstj.C3.A4rna-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Subsequently, he left the job to direct documentaries on musicians including <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rod_Stewart" title="Rod Stewart">Rod Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">Björk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kraftwerk" title="Kraftwerk">Kraftwerk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inde_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-inde-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bendjelloul's documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Searching_for_Sugar_Man" title="Searching for Sugar Man">Searching for Sugar Man</a></i> won the <a href="/wiki/85th_Academy_Awards" title="85th Academy Awards">2013 Academy Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Documentary_Feature#2010s" title="Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature">Best Documentary Feature</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oscars2013_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oscars2013-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Bendjelloul also won the 2013 <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Film_Awards" title="British Academy Film Awards">BAFTA Award</a>, <a href="/wiki/Directors_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Directors Guild of America Award">Directors Guild of America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Producers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Producers Guild of America Award">Producers Guild of America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Writers Guild of America Award">Writers Guild of America</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Cinema_Editors" title="American Cinema Editors">American Cinema Editors</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance</a> audience and special jury accolades<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and the 2012 <a href="/wiki/International_Documentary_Association" title="International Documentary Association">International Documentary Association</a> awards. Eventually, the documentary achieved commercial success as well and made $3.6 million (£2.7 million) at the box office. The film documents the revival of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sixto_Rodriguez" title="Sixto Rodriguez">Sixto Rodriguez</a>'s musical career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inde_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-inde-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Bendjelloul was invited to host a show on the Swedish radio show <i><a href="/wiki/Sommar_(radio_program)" title="Sommar (radio program)">Sommar i P1</a></i>, where he told the listeners about the process behind <i>Searching for Sugar Man</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sveri_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-sveri-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malik_Bendjelloul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Malik Bendjelloul committed suicide on 13 May 2014 after struggling with <a href="/wiki/Depression_(mood)" title="Depression (mood)">depression</a>, as reported by his brother Johar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> At the time of his death, he was working on a film project based on <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Anthony" title="Lawrence Anthony">Lawrence Anthony</a>'s book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Elephant_Whisperer" title="The Elephant Whisperer">The Elephant Whisperer</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
