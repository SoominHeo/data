<p><b>Malin Crépin</b> (born 22 August 1978 in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>), is a Swedish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a>. She has starred in several movies. She became best known for her role in the Swedish film series <i>A Case for <a href="/wiki/Annika_Bengtzon" title="Annika Bengtzon">Annika Bengtzon</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malin_Cr%C3%A9pin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Malin Crépin was born in Stockholm in 1978. From 1998 to 2002, she received her training at the <a href="/wiki/Lund_University#Campus" title="Lund University">Malmö Theatre Academy</a>. Since then, she has been active in the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_City_Theatre" title="Stockholm City Theatre">Stockholm City Theatre</a> and at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dramatic_Theatre" title="Royal Dramatic Theatre">Swedish National Theater</a>, in Stockholm.</p>
<p>Crépin has also played in films and television series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Crépin embodied in 2012, the main role of the journalist Annika Bengtzon from the books by the Swedish novelist <a href="/wiki/Liza_Marklund" title="Liza Marklund">Liza Marklund</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
