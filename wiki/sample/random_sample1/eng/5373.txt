<p>The <b>malbrouck</b> (<i>Chlorocebus cynosuros</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Primate" title="Primate">primate</a> from <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> that belongs to the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Chlorocebus" title="Chlorocebus">Chlorocebus</a></i>. The species is sometimes classified as a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Vervet_monkey" title="Vervet monkey">vervet monkey</a> (<i>C. pygerythrus</i>), or of the widespread <a href="/wiki/Grivet" title="Grivet">grivet</a> (<i>C. aethiops</i>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_characteristics">Physical characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malbrouck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Physical characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The malbrouck is a slim, agile primate with long limbs and a long tail. The fur is olive-grey. The breast and underparts are white, as well as the cheeks and eyebrows, which surround the bald, pale-blotched face.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The eyes are brown. The genitals are brightly colored; the <a href="/wiki/Scrotum" title="Scrotum">scrotum</a> of the male is blue, the <a href="/wiki/Penis" title="Penis">penis</a> is red. Males are about 20% larger than females.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malbrouck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The malbrouck is found in central and south-central Africa, ranging from the <a href="/wiki/Albertine_Rift" title="Albertine Rift">Albertine Rift</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DR_Congo" title="DR Congo">DR Congo</a> west to the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> coast and south to northern <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a> west of the <a href="/wiki/Luangwa_River" title="Luangwa River">Luangwa River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MSW3_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-MSW3-1">[1]</a></sup> It lives in marshy forest, savanna-forest and montane forest up to an altitude of 4,500 m (14,750Â ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behavior">Behavior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malbrouck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behavior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The malbrouck is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diurnal_animal" title="Diurnal animal">diurnal animal</a> living in large groups, consisting of six to more than 50 animals. The number of males and females is usually about the same. Each group has its own <a href="/wiki/Territory_(animal)" title="Territory (animal)">territory</a>, the size of which depends on the amount of available food. The malbrouck uses a wide range of sounds and gestures to communicate with other members of a group.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malbrouck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The diet of this <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omnivorous" title="Omnivorous">omnivorous</a> species consists of fruit, seeds, flowers, gum, <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egg_(food)" title="Egg (food)">eggs</a>, chicks and <a href="/wiki/Lizard" title="Lizard">lizards</a>. Vegetable food forms the largest part of the diet in most areas.</p>
