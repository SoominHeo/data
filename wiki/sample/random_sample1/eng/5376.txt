<p><i><b>Mamma Mia!</b></i> (promoted as <i><b>Mamma Mia! The Musical</b></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Jukebox_musical" title="Jukebox musical">jukebox musical</a> written by <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Playwright" title="Playwright">playwright</a> <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Johnson_(playwright)" title="Catherine Johnson (playwright)">Catherine Johnson</a>, based on the songs of <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a>, composed by <a href="/wiki/Benny_Andersson" title="Benny Andersson">Benny Andersson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Ulvaeus" title="Björn Ulvaeus">Björn Ulvaeus</a>, former members of the band. The title of the musical is taken from the group's 1975 chart-topper "<a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia_(song)" title="Mamma Mia (song)">Mamma Mia</a>". Ulvaeus and Andersson, who composed the original music for ABBA, were involved in the development of the show from the beginning. <a href="/wiki/Anni-Frid_Lyngstad" title="Anni-Frid Lyngstad">Anni-Frid Lyngstad</a> has been involved financially in the production and she has also been present at many of the premieres around the world.</p>
<p>The musical includes such hits as "<a href="/wiki/Super_Trouper_(song)" title="Super Trouper (song)">Super Trouper</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Lay_All_Your_Love_on_Me" title="Lay All Your Love on Me">Lay All Your Love on Me</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_Queen" title="Dancing Queen">Dancing Queen</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Knowing_Me,_Knowing_You" title="Knowing Me, Knowing You">Knowing Me, Knowing You</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Take_a_Chance_on_Me" title="Take a Chance on Me">Take a Chance on Me</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Thank_You_for_the_Music" title="Thank You for the Music">Thank You for the Music</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Money,_Money,_Money" title="Money, Money, Money">Money, Money, Money</a>", "<a href="/wiki/The_Winner_Takes_It_All" title="The Winner Takes It All">The Winner Takes It All</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Voulez-Vous_(song)" title="Voulez-Vous (song)">Voulez-Vous</a>", "<a href="/wiki/SOS_(ABBA_song)" title="SOS (ABBA song)">SOS</a>" and the title track. Over 60 million people have seen the show, which has grossed $2 billion worldwide since its 1999 debut. A <a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!_(film)" title="Mamma Mia! (film)">film adaptation</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Meryl_Streep" title="Meryl Streep">Meryl Streep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colin_Firth" title="Colin Firth">Colin Firth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pierce_Brosnan" title="Pierce Brosnan">Pierce Brosnan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Seyfried" title="Amanda Seyfried">Amanda Seyfried</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christine_Baranski" title="Christine Baranski">Christine Baranski</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stellan_Skarsg%C3%A5rd" title="Stellan Skarsgård">Stellan Skarsgård</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julie_Walters" title="Julie Walters">Julie Walters</a> was released in July 2008.</p>
<p>As of 2016, the show has productions in London's <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a>, where it is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_longest-running_West_End_shows" title="List of the longest-running West End shows">eighth longest-running show in West End history</a>, as well as various international productions. Its Broadway incarnation closed in September 2015 after a 14-year run, making it the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_longest-running_Broadway_shows" title="List of the longest-running Broadway shows">eighth longest-running show in Broadway history</a>. The show had its first regional production on 27 May 2016 at the <a href="/wiki/Barter_Theatre" title="Barter Theatre">Barter Theatre</a> in Abingdon, Virginia. A typical performance of <i>Mamma Mia!</i> runs for approximately two hours and 30 minutes, plus a 15-minute intermission.</p>

<p><i>Mamma Mia!</i> is based on the songs of <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a>, a Swedish <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a>/<a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> group active from 1972–1982 and one of the most popular international pop groups of all time, topping the charts again and again in Europe, North and South America and Australia. Following the premiere of the musical in London in 1999, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold:_Greatest_Hits_(ABBA_album)" title="Gold: Greatest Hits (ABBA album)"><i>ABBA Gold</i></a> topped the charts in the United Kingdom again. This musical was the brainchild of producer <a href="/wiki/Judy_Craymer" title="Judy Craymer">Judy Craymer</a>. She met songwriters Björn Ulvaeus and Benny Andersson in 1983 when they were working with <a href="/wiki/Tim_Rice" title="Tim Rice">Tim Rice</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Chess_(musical)" title="Chess (musical)">Chess</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Craymer_2004_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Craymer_2004-2">[2]</a></sup> It was the song "The Winner Takes It All" that suggested to her the theatrical potential of their pop songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Craymer_2004_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Craymer_2004-2">[2]</a></sup> The songwriters were not enthusiastic, but they were not completely opposed to the idea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Craymer_2004_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Craymer_2004-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1997, Craymer commissioned <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Johnson_(playwright)" title="Catherine Johnson (playwright)">Catherine Johnson</a> to write the book for the musical. In 1998, <a href="/wiki/Phyllida_Lloyd" title="Phyllida Lloyd">Phyllida Lloyd</a> became the director for the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Craymer_2004_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Craymer_2004-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>The musical opened in the <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Theatre" title="Prince Edward Theatre">Prince Edward Theatre</a> on April 6, 1999, and transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales_Theatre" title="Prince of Wales Theatre">Prince of Wales Theatre</a> on June 9, 2004, where it played until September 2012, when it moved to the <a href="/wiki/Novello_Theatre" title="Novello Theatre">Novello Theatre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Directed by <a href="/wiki/Phyllida_Lloyd" title="Phyllida Lloyd">Phyllida Lloyd</a> with choreography by <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Van_Laast" title="Anthony Van Laast">Anthony Van Laast</a>, the original cast featured Siobhan McCarthy, Lisa Stokke, and Hilton McRae.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>Prior to the musical's Broadway engagement, it opened in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Alexandra_Theatre" title="Royal Alexandra Theatre">Royal Alexandra Theatre</a> in May 2000, where it played for five years. Its US debut was in San Francisco, California, at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orpheum_Theatre_(San_Francisco,_California)" title="Orpheum Theatre (San Francisco, California)">Orpheum Theatre</a> from November 17, 2000 to February 17, 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> moving next to Los Angeles, California, at the <a href="/wiki/Shubert_Theatre_(Los_Angeles)" title="Shubert Theatre (Los Angeles)">Shubert Theatre</a> from February 26, 2001 to May 12, 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and finally to Chicago, Illinois, at the <a href="/wiki/Cadillac_Palace_Theatre" title="Cadillac Palace Theatre">Cadillac Palace Theatre</a> from May 13, 2001 to August 12, 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="New_York_City_run">New York City run</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamma_Mia!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: New York City run">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The musical opened on <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Garden_Theatre" title="Winter Garden Theatre">Winter Garden Theatre</a> on October 18, 2001, after beginning previews on October 5. The director is Phyllida Lloyd with choreography by Anthony Van Laast. As of January 2017, it is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_longest-running_Broadway_shows" title="List of the longest-running Broadway shows">eighth longest-running Broadway show</a> and the longest-running <a href="/wiki/Jukebox_musical" title="Jukebox musical">jukebox musical</a> in Broadway history. On April 18, 2013, it was announced that <i>Mamma Mia!</i> would transfer from its home at the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Garden_Theatre" title="Winter Garden Theatre">Winter Garden Theatre</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Broadhurst_Theatre" title="Broadhurst Theatre">Broadhurst Theatre</a> later that year to make way for the musical adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Rocky_the_Musical" title="Rocky the Musical">Rocky</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The show played its final performance at the Winter Garden Theatre on October 19, 2013 and began performances at the Broadhurst Theatre on November 2, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced on April 9, 2015 that the show would close on September 5, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On April 21, 2015, it was then announced that show would play an additional week and would instead close on September 12, 2015. <i>Mamma Mia!</i> played 5,773 performances on Broadway before closing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<p>The first city to produce the show after London was <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, where it ran from May 22, 2000 to May 22, 2005, being the North America premiere of <i>Mamma Mia!</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The original cast included Louise Pitre as Donna and Tina Maddigan as Sophie, who both of them later reprised their roles in first US Tour and Broadway premiere.</p>
<p><i>Mamma Mia!</i> played in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>, opening at the <a href="/wiki/Mandalay_Bay" title="Mandalay Bay">Mandalay Bay</a> in February 2003 and closing on January 4, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In June 2005, <i>Mamma Mia!</i> played its 1000th performance in Las Vegas, becoming the longest-running West End/Broadway musical in Las Vegas. <p>The North American tour started in <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a> in February 2002, and has since played more than 120 cities as of its 6th anniversary in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>The first non-English version of the show debuted in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Operettenhaus" title="Operettenhaus">Operettenhaus</a>, where it ran from November 3, 2002 to September 8, 2007. With the productions of Stuttgart (2004) and Essen (2007), <i>Mamma Mia!</i> became the first major musical to play concurrently in three German cities.</p>
<p>The show has had (and in some cases, still has) permanent productions in London, Toronto, Melbourne, New York, Hamburg, Tokyo (later transferred to Osaka, Fukuoka and Nagoya), Las Vegas, Utrecht, Seoul (later transferred to Seongnam and Daegu), Stuttgart, Madrid (later transferred to Barcelona), Stockholm (later transferred to Gothenburg), Antwerp, Moscow, Essen, Berlin, Oslo, Mexico City, Milan (later transferred to Rome), Copenhagen (later transferred to Aarhus), Paris, São Paulo, Shanghai, Buenos Aires, Vienna and Helsinki.</p>
<p>Since its premiere in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a> on September 9, 2004 (with Helen Hobson as Donna) the international tour has visited more than 74 cities in 35 countries and has been seen by 5 million people. In addition there have been several touring productions worldwide, including Australia/Asia (2002–2005), Australia (2009-2010), China, France (2012-2013), Germany, Japan, Netherlands (2009-2010), North America (since 2002), Spain (2009-2011 and 2016-2017), South Africa (2010-2011), South Korea, United Kingdom (2016-2017), and United States (2000-2001).</p>

It was seen by 250,000 people across six venues, with a total of 190 performances. Shadow Zen played the role of Donna.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamma_Mia!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Act_I">Act I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamma_Mia!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Act I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before the curtain rises, the orchestra starts playing the overture, which is a montage of instrumental versions of some of ABBA's hit songs.</p>
<p>On the Greek island of Kalokairi, 20-year-old Sophie is preparing to marry her fiancé, Sky. She wants her father to walk her down the aisle ("<a href="/wiki/Prologue" title="Prologue">Prologue</a>") but does not know who he is. Sophie discovers her mother's old diary and finds entries which describe intimate dates with three men (Sam Carmichael, Bill Austin (Anderson in the film version), and Harry Bright) ("<a href="/wiki/Honey,_Honey" title="Honey, Honey">Honey, Honey</a>"). Sophie believes one of these men is her father and, three months prior to the wedding, sends each an invitation to her wedding, writing in the name of her mother, Donna, without letting the unsuspecting mother know.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>The day before the wedding, Donna begins receiving guests at her <a href="/wiki/Taverna" title="Taverna">taverna</a>. The first to arrive are her old best friends, Tanya, a rich woman who has been married and divorced three times, and Rosie, an unmarried, carefree woman. The trio used to be a <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> called "Donna and the Dynamos". The three women catch up and talk about how their lives have been ("<a href="/wiki/Money,_Money,_Money" title="Money, Money, Money">Money, Money, Money</a>").</p>
<p>Later that day, Sophie's three possible fathers arrive: Sam (an American architect), Harry (a British banker), and Bill (originally an Australian writer and adventurer, Swedish in the film, and currently American in New York and on tour). Sophie convinces them not to tell Donna that she invited them ("<a href="/wiki/Thank_You_for_the_Music" title="Thank You for the Music">Thank You for the Music</a>"). Donna is surprised to see her ex-lovers ("<a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia_(song)" title="Mamma Mia (song)">Mamma Mia</a>") and leaves in tears. Donna, crying, explains to Tanya and Rosie the situation, and they cheer her up ("<a href="/wiki/Chiquitita" title="Chiquitita">Chiquitita</a>"). Tanya and Rosie try to convince Donna that she can still be the girl that she once was ("<a href="/wiki/Dancing_Queen" title="Dancing Queen">Dancing Queen</a>").</p>
<p>Sophie had hoped she would know her father the moment she saw him, but is now only confused. She tries to tell her fiancé, Sky, how she feels without confessing what she has done. Sky tells her he will be the only man she ever needs ("<a href="/wiki/Lay_All_Your_Love_on_Me" title="Lay All Your Love on Me">Lay All Your Love on Me</a>").</p>
<p>At Sophie's <a href="/wiki/Bachelorette_party" title="Bachelorette party">hen party</a>, Donna and the Dynamos don their old costumes and perform a song, "<a href="/wiki/Super_Trouper_(song)" title="Super Trouper (song)">Super Trouper</a>". Sam, Bill, and Harry accidentally walk in on the party, and the guests persuade them to stay ("<a href="/wiki/Gimme!_Gimme!_Gimme!_(A_Man_After_Midnight)" title="Gimme! Gimme! Gimme! (A Man After Midnight)">Gimme! Gimme! Gimme! (A Man After Midnight)</a>"). Sophie first pulls Sam out of the room to talk to him. After he asks why he is here, she is overcome with guilt and goes to talk to Harry instead. But Harry asks if Sophie's father is at the party, and she tells him the whole truth. Lastly, she draws Bill aside to talk with him. She learns that Bill has an aunt <i>Sophia</i> who left all her money to Donna's family. Bill learns that Donna built the taverna with money she inherited from a friend she lived with when Sophie was a baby; that friend was Bill's aunt. They both think this means he is her father.</p>
<p>Sophie asks Bill to walk her down the aisle, but Bill wants to discuss it first with Donna. This has been her secret, after all. But no one knows yet that even Donna does not know who the father is, because she slept with the three men in such swift succession. Sophie insists they must not tell Donna anything ("<a href="/wiki/The_Name_of_the_Game_(ABBA_song)" title="The Name of the Game (ABBA song)">The Name of the Game</a>") and finally, Bill agrees.</p>
<p>Afterward, everybody crashes the hen party (including the guys from the <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_party" title="Bachelor party">stag party</a>). Eddie, a <a href="/wiki/Stripper" title="Stripper">stripper</a>, has an erotic yet tasteful <a href="/wiki/Nudity" title="Nudity">nude scene</a>. During the striptease, Sam pulls Sophie aside and tells her he has figured out why she invited him. He knows he is her father and promises to walk her down the aisle the next day. Then, Harry approaches Sophie, apologizing for being so slow on the uptake; he is also convinced that she is his daughter and promises to walk her down the aisle. Sophie leaves the party, hopelessly confused; she does not want to turn any of them down ("<a href="/wiki/Voulez-Vous_(song)" title="Voulez-Vous (song)">Voulez-Vous</a>").</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Act_II">Act II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamma_Mia!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Act II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mamma_mia.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="748" data-file-width="1598" height="103" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mamma_mia.JPG/220px-Mamma_mia.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mamma_mia.JPG/330px-Mamma_mia.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mamma_mia.JPG/440px-Mamma_mia.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>(<i>Entr'acte</i>) Sophie's having a nightmare, involving her three possible fathers all fighting for the right to walk her down the aisle and wakes up despairing ("<a href="/wiki/Under_Attack" title="Under Attack">Under Attack</a>").</p>
<p>Sophie's upset, and Donna assumes that Sophie wants to cancel the wedding and offers to handle all the details. Sophie is offended and vows that her children won't grow up not knowing who their father is. As Sophie storms out of the room, Sam enters and tries to tell Donna that Sophie may not be all she seems, but Donna won't listen ("<a href="/wiki/One_of_Us_(ABBA_song)" title="One of Us (ABBA song)">One of Us</a>"). She hates Sam; at the end of their affair, she said she never wanted to see him again. But it seems that Sam was the man Donna cared about the most, and both of them wish they could go back to the start ("<a href="/wiki/SOS_(ABBA_song)" title="SOS (ABBA song)">SOS</a>"').</p>
<p>At the beach, Harry asks Tanya what the father of the bride ought to be doing for Sophie's wedding. Tanya explains that for her part, her father gave her his advice and then paid. Pepper, one of the guys who works at Donna's taverna, makes advances to Tanya, but she rebuffs him ("<a href="/wiki/Does_Your_Mother_Know" title="Does Your Mother Know">Does Your Mother Know</a>").</p>
<p>Sky finds out what Sophie has done in inviting Sam, Harry and Bill to the wedding. He accuses her of wanting a big white wedding only so that she can find out who her father is. He's very hurt that she kept this plan a secret from him. He storms off just as Sam walks in. Sam tries to give Sophie some fatherly advice by describing his failed marriage ("<a href="/wiki/Knowing_Me,_Knowing_You" title="Knowing Me, Knowing You">Knowing Me, Knowing You</a>"), but Sophie isn't consoled.</p>
<p>Harry offers to Donna to pay for the wedding, and they reminisce about their fling ("<a href="/wiki/Our_Last_Summer" title="Our Last Summer">Our Last Summer</a>"). Sophie arrives and Donna helps her get dressed. She cannot believe her daughter is going to be a bride ("<a href="/wiki/Slipping_Through_My_Fingers" title="Slipping Through My Fingers">Slipping Through My Fingers</a>"). Donna admits that her own mother disowned her when she learned that she was pregnant. They reconcile and Sophie asks her mother if she will walk her down the aisle. Sam arrives and tries to speak to Donna again, but she does not want to see him, and asks him to leave. He refuses, and a bitter confrontation ensues. Donna tells Sam that he broke her heart, presumably when she found out he was engaged ("<a href="/wiki/The_Winner_Takes_It_All" title="The Winner Takes It All">The Winner Takes It All</a>"). It emerges that the two still love each other dearly, albeit against Donna's better judgment.</p>
<p>Rosie is making final preparations in the taverna when Bill arrives. He's upset because he has received a note that Donna will be walking Sophie down the aisle. Bill reaffirms his commitment to the single life, but Rosie has become attracted to him, and urges him to reconsider ("<a href="/wiki/Take_a_Chance_on_Me" title="Take a Chance on Me">Take a Chance on Me</a>"). They are about to have sex in the taverna, but the guests arrive, leaving Rosie quite stunned.</p>
<p>The wedding begins, with Donna walking Sophie down the aisle. Before the priest has a chance to begin the ceremonies, Donna acknowledges to everyone that Sophie's father is present. Sophie tells her mother that she knows about her father. Donna realizes that Sophie invited them to the wedding for that very reason. The issue of Sophie's parentage is left unsettled, as none of them have any idea whether they are actually her father. Everyone involved agrees that it does not matter which one of them her biological parent is, as Sophie loves all three and they are all happy to be "one-third of a father" and a part of her life at last. Finally, Harry, who has made frequent references to his "other half" throughout the show, is revealed to be in a committed gay relationship.</p>
<p>Suddenly, Sophie calls a halt to the proceedings. She is not ready to get married and Sky agrees with Sophie about not getting married. Sam seizes his chance and proposes to Donna in order to prevent the wedding preparations from going to waste. He explains that he loved her, even when he left to get married. It is revealed that he called off the wedding with his fiancée and came back to the island, only to be told that Donna was going out with another man (Bill). He went back, married his fiancée and had children but he got divorced. Surprisingly, Donna accepts ("<a href="/wiki/I_Do,_I_Do,_I_Do,_I_Do,_I_Do" title="I Do, I Do, I Do, I Do, I Do">I Do, I Do, I Do, I Do, I Do</a>"). In the end, Sam and Donna are married, and at the end of the night, Sophie and Sky depart on a round-the-world tour ("<a href="/wiki/I_Have_a_Dream_(song)" title="I Have a Dream (song)">I Have a Dream</a>") .</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Finale_and_Encore">Finale and Encore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamma_Mia!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Finale and Encore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After their <a href="/wiki/Curtain_call" title="Curtain call">final bows</a> to the audience, which are set to an instrumental version of "<a href="/wiki/Does_Your_Mother_Know" title="Does Your Mother Know">Does Your Mother Know</a>", the ensemble performs a reprise of "<a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia_(song)" title="Mamma Mia (song)">Mamma Mia</a>". A reprise of "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_Queen" title="Dancing Queen">Dancing Queen</a>" follows, during which Donna, Tanya, Rosie are revealed wearing brightly colored ABBA inspired costumes. The cast ends the finale with "<a href="/wiki/Waterloo_(ABBA_song)" title="Waterloo (ABBA song)">Waterloo</a>", during which Sam, Bill, and Harry join the rest of the cast onstage in brightly colored costumes that match the leading ladies. The Dynamos and the Dads often invite the audience to clap, dance, and sing along during the finale.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes_on_the_music">Notes on the music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamma_Mia!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Notes on the music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the preview period in London, the musical had the song "<a href="/wiki/Summer_Night_City" title="Summer Night City">Summer Night City</a>" just after the prologue. The "Summer Night City" scene was a wedding rehearsal and during the song, Ali, Lisa, Tanya, and Rosie arrived on the island. Now, part of the song is used as underscoring to connect the end of "The Winner Takes It All" and "Take a Chance on Me". You can also hear a couple of lines of "Summer Night City" in the 'Entr'acte' (the most noticeable line is: 'Time to breathe and time to live').</p>
<p>Donna hums a few lines of "<a href="/wiki/Fernando_(song)" title="Fernando (song)">Fernando</a>", when she repairs the doors of the taverna just before she sees her three former lovers. In the Mexican production Donna sings a few lines of ABBA's original Spanish version of the song.</p>
<p>In the German production, Sky (Bernhard Forcher) sang a few lines of "<a href="/wiki/King_Kong_Song" title="King Kong Song">King Kong Song</a>" before starting "Lay All Your Love on Me". Niklas Riesbeck uses the same song in the Swedish production. Andrey Kozhan sang a few lines of <a href="/wiki/Summer_Night_City" title="Summer Night City">Summer Night City</a> in the Russian production. In the international tour version, Sky (David Roberts) sings a few lines of "<a href="/wiki/She%27s_My_Kind_of_Girl" title="She's My Kind of Girl">She's My Kind of Girl</a>", a Björn &amp; Benny song. In the 2015 International tour version, Sky (Justin Thomas) sings a few lines of "<a href="/wiki/Dum_Dum_Diddle" title="Dum Dum Diddle">Dum Dum Diddle</a>".</p>
<p>The wedding march that is played as Sophie walks down the aisle is a slower arrangement of "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_Queen" title="Dancing Queen">Dancing Queen</a>".</p>
<p>The creators also intended to include "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Just_Like_That_(song)" title="Just Like That (song)">Just Like That</a>", an unreleased ABBA song recorded in 1982. The song was apparently dropped just before the first public previews in March 1999, though it was listed in the program available during the preview period.</p>
<p>Originally the outro after the encore was a 5-minute instrumental medley (in the style of the Ouverture and Entr'Acte) featuring <a href="/wiki/Gimme!_Gimme!_Gimme!_(A_Man_After_Midnight)" title="Gimme! Gimme! Gimme! (A Man After Midnight)">Gimme! Gimme! Gimme! (A Man After Midnight)</a>, <a href="/wiki/SOS_(ABBA_song)" title="SOS (ABBA song)">SOS</a>, <a href="/wiki/Voulez-Vous_(song)" title="Voulez-Vous (song)">Voulez-Vous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Does_Your_Mother_Know" title="Does Your Mother Know">Does Your Mother Know</a>, <a href="/wiki/Money,_Money,_Money" title="Money, Money, Money">Money, Money, Money</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dancing_Queen" title="Dancing Queen">Dancing Queen</a>. It was later replaced with the short Dancing Queen instrumental, still being used today.</p>
<p>The last three songs, performed as an encore/finale by the whole cast are: "<a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia_(song)" title="Mamma Mia (song)">Mamma Mia</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_Queen" title="Dancing Queen">Dancing Queen</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Waterloo_(ABBA_song)" title="Waterloo (ABBA song)">Waterloo</a>". The version of "<a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia_(song)" title="Mamma Mia (song)">Mamma Mia</a>" used in the encore/finale is sung by the Company. The finale song Mamma Mia has been extended in theatres where Donna and the Dynamos had to go downstairs for taking the stage lift in Dancing Queen. Then, during the intro of "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_Queen" title="Dancing Queen">Dancing Queen</a>", Donna, Tanya and Rosie join the Company (as the girl-power band "Donna and the Dynamos", wearing the ABBA's 70's colorful and flashy costumes). Sam, Bill and Harry join them during Waterloo, wearing male versions of the girls' ABBA's costumes. The Encore is included on the 5th Anniversary Cast Recording (commemorating said anniversary for the Broadway production).</p>
<p>In the Brazilian production, all three songs from the encore/finale were kept in English. Though "<a href="/wiki/Waterloo_(ABBA_song)" title="Waterloo (ABBA song)">Waterloo</a>" was adapted into Brazilian Portuguese, this version was never used and the lyrics are unknown.</p>
<p>In the Russian production the song "Happy New Year" is sung at the encore on New Year holidays shows.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!_(film)" title="Mamma Mia! (film)">Mamma Mia! (film)</a></div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!_The_Movie_Soundtrack" title="Mamma Mia! The Movie Soundtrack">Mamma Mia! The Movie Soundtrack</a></div>
<p><i>Mamma Mia!</i> was <a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!_(film)" title="Mamma Mia! (film)">adapted as a film</a>, produced by Judy Craymer and Gary Goetzman, written by Catherine Johnson directed by Phyllida Lloyd and with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rita_Wilson" title="Rita Wilson">Rita Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Ulvaeus" title="Björn Ulvaeus">Björn Ulvaeus</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Benny_Andersson" title="Benny Andersson">Benny Andersson</a> as executive producers. <a href="/wiki/Meryl_Streep" title="Meryl Streep">Meryl Streep</a> stars as Donna Sheridan and <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Seyfried" title="Amanda Seyfried">Amanda Seyfried</a> as Sophie and <a href="/wiki/Pierce_Brosnan" title="Pierce Brosnan">Pierce Brosnan</a> as Sam Carmichael. The movie also features <a href="/wiki/Christine_Baranski" title="Christine Baranski">Christine Baranski</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominic_Cooper" title="Dominic Cooper">Dominic Cooper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colin_Firth" title="Colin Firth">Colin Firth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stellan_Skarsg%C3%A5rd" title="Stellan Skarsgård">Stellan Skarsgård</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julie_Walters" title="Julie Walters">Julie Walters</a>. It premiered July 18, 2008, in the US. Most of the songs remained intact with the exceptions of "Under Attack", "One of Us", "Knowing Me, Knowing You", and "Thank You for the Music". "Knowing Me, Knowing You" was used as the wedding music and "Thank You for the Music" is sung during the second half of the end credits. The first half is occupied with a "Dancing Queen" reprise and "Waterloo", with a "Mamma Mia" reprise and "I Have a Dream" sung before the credits (though the latter is sung as Sophie mails the invitations at the start of the film). "When All Is Said and Done", a song not used in the musical, was added for the film sung by Sam, Donna, and the company. "Our Last Summer" was used earlier in the movie, with Sophie, Bill, Sam, and Harry singing it. "The Name of the Game", while filmed, was subsequently edited out of the film for the final cut. The song is included in full on the <a href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia!_The_Movie_Soundtrack" title="Mamma Mia! The Movie Soundtrack">motion picture soundtrack</a> - an abbreviated version of the song and scene appear on the DVD/Blu-ray as a supplement. In addition, "Thank You for the Music" is used as a <a href="/wiki/Hidden_track" title="Hidden track">hidden track</a>, and is performed by Amanda Seyfried.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Response">Response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamma_Mia!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office_and_business">Box office and business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamma_Mia!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Box office and business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On any given day, there are at least seven performances of <i>Mamma Mia!</i> being performed around the globe. On May 15, 2005, <i>Mamma Mia!</i> surpassed the original Broadway runs of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sound_of_Music" title="The Sound of Music">The Sound of Music</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_King_and_I" title="The King and I">The King and I</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Damn_Yankees" title="Damn Yankees">Damn Yankees</a></i> with 1,500 performances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Craymer_2006_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Craymer_2006-17">[17]</a></sup> On March 6, 2014, it surpassed <i><a href="/wiki/Rent_(musical)" title="Rent (musical)">Rent</a></i> to become the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_longest-running_Broadway_shows" title="List of the longest-running Broadway shows">9th longest-running Broadway musical</a> of all time with 5,124 performances. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Later that year, it surpassed <i><a href="/wiki/Beauty_and_the_Beast_(musical)" title="Beauty and the Beast (musical)">Beauty and the Beast</a></i> to become Broadway's eighth longest-running show on Broadway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> When <i>Mamma Mia!</i> opened in Russia, it received a very positive response.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> As of January 2008, <i>Mamma Mia!</i> became the longest daily running show in the history of Russian theatre.</p>
