<p><i><b>Erigeron canadensis</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Synonym_(taxonomy)" title="Synonym (taxonomy)">synonym</a> <i>Conyza canadensis</i>) is an annual plant native throughout most of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. It is also widely naturalized in <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-belanna_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-belanna-2">[2]</a></sup> Common names include <b>horseweed</b>, <b>Canadian horseweed</b>, <b>Canadian fleabane</b>, <b>coltstail</b>, <b>marestail</b> and <b>butterweed</b>. It was the first weed to have developed <a href="/wiki/Glyphosate" title="Glyphosate">glyphosate</a> resistance, reported in 2001 from <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erigeron_canadensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Erigeron canadensis</i> is an <a href="/wiki/Annual_plant" title="Annual plant">annual plant</a> growing to 1.5 m (60 inches) tall, with sparsely hairy stems. The <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaves</a> are unstalked, slender, 2–10 cm long and up to 1 cm (0.4 inches) across, with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leaf_shape" title="Leaf shape">coarsely toothed margin</a>. They grow in an alternate spiral up the stem and the lower ones wither early. The <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">flowers</a> are produced in dense <a href="/wiki/Inflorescence" title="Inflorescence">inflorescences</a> 1 cm in diameter. Each individual flower has a ring of white or pale purple <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ray_florets" title="Ray florets">ray florets</a> and a centre of yellow <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disc_floret" title="Disc floret">disc florets</a>. The fruit is a <a href="/wiki/Asteraceae#Fruits_and_seeds" title="Asteraceae">cypsela</a> tipped with dirty white down. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NatureGate_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NatureGate-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Erigeron canadensis</i> can easily be confused with <i><a href="/wiki/Conyza_sumatrensis" title="Conyza sumatrensis">Conyza sumatrensis</a></i>, which may grow to a height of 2 m, and the more hairy <i><a href="/wiki/Erigeron_bonariensis" title="Erigeron bonariensis">Erigeron bonariensis</a></i> which does not exceed 1 m (40 inches). <i>Erigeron canadensis</i> is distinguished by <a href="/wiki/Bract" title="Bract">bracts</a> that have a brownish inner surface and no red dot at the tip, and are free (or nearly free) of the hairs found on the bracts of the other species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erigeron_canadensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Horseweed originated in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and is very widespread there,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-belanna_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-belanna-2">[2]</a></sup> but has spread to inhabited areas of most of the temperate zone of Asia,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Europe,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NatureGate_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-NatureGate-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It is found in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a> from northern <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> growing as a weed of arable land. It is not invasive of any natural or semi-natural habitats.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Weed_status">Weed status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erigeron_canadensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Weed status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Horseweed is commonly considered a weed, and in <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> it has been declared a <a href="/wiki/Noxious_weed" title="Noxious weed">noxious weed</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It can be found in fields, meadows, and gardens throughout its native range. Horseweed infestations have reduced <a href="/wiki/Soybean" title="Soybean">soybean</a> yields by as much as 83%.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is an especially problematic weed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/No-till_agriculture" title="No-till agriculture">no-till agriculture</a>, as it is often resistant to <a href="/wiki/Glyphosate" title="Glyphosate">glyphosate</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herbicides" title="Herbicides">herbicides</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Farmers are advised to include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2,4-D" title="2,4-D">2,4-D</a> or <a href="/wiki/Dicamba" title="Dicamba">dicamba</a> in a burndown application prior to planting to control horseweed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Erigeron_canadensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Zuni_people" title="Zuni people">Zuni people</a> insert the crushed flowers of <i>E. canadensis</i> var. <i>canadensis</i> into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nostrils" title="Nostrils">nostrils</a> to cause <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sneezing" title="Sneezing">sneezing</a>, relieving <a href="/wiki/Rhinitis" title="Rhinitis">rhinitis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> A tincture can be made from the dried flowering tops of the plants.</p>
<p>Horseweed is a preferable material for use in the hand drill method of making <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friction_fire" title="Friction fire">friction fire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NatureGate_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-NatureGate-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">
