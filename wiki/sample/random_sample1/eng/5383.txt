<p>The <b>malleus</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span></span>/</a></span></span> or <b>hammer</b> is a hammer-shaped small bone or <a href="/wiki/Ossicles" title="Ossicles">ossicle</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_ear" title="Middle ear">middle ear</a> which connects with the <a href="/wiki/Incus" title="Incus">incus</a> and is attached to the inner surface of the <a href="/wiki/Eardrum" title="Eardrum">eardrum</a>. The word is Latin for <i>hammer</i> or <i>mallet</i>. It transmits the sound vibrations from the <a href="/wiki/Eardrum" title="Eardrum">eardrum</a> to the <i><a href="/wiki/Incus" title="Incus">incus</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malleus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Ossicles" title="Ossicles">Ossicles</a></div>
<p>The malleus is a bone situated in the middle ear. It is the first of the three <a href="/wiki/Ossicles" title="Ossicles">ossicles</a>, and attached to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tympanic_membrane" title="Tympanic membrane">tympanic membrane</a>. The <i>head of the malleus</i> is the large protruding section, which attaches to the <a href="/wiki/Incus" title="Incus">incus</a>. The head connects to the <i>neck of malleus</i>, and the bone continues as the <i>handle of malleus</i>, which connects to the tympanic membrane. Between the neck and handle of the malleus, <i>lateral</i> and <i>anterior processes</i> emerge from the bone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRAYS2005_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GRAYS2005-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The malleus is unique to mammals, and <a href="/wiki/Evolution_of_mammalian_auditory_ossicles" title="Evolution of mammalian auditory ossicles">evolved from a lower jaw bone</a> in basal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amniota" title="Amniota">amniotes</a> called the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Articular" title="Articular">articular</a>, which still forms part of the jaw joint in reptiles and birds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malleus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Embryologically it is derived from the first <a href="/wiki/Pharyngeal_arch" title="Pharyngeal arch">pharyngeal arch</a> along with the rest of the bones of mastication, such as the <a href="/wiki/Maxilla" title="Maxilla">maxilla</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_mandible" title="Human mandible">mandible</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Function">Function</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malleus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Function">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hearing" title="Hearing">Hearing</a></div>
<p>The malleus is one of three <a href="/wiki/Ossicles" title="Ossicles">ossicles</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_ear" title="Middle ear">middle ear</a> which transmit sound from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ear_drum" title="Ear drum">tympanic membrane (ear drum)</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Inner_ear" title="Inner ear">inner ear</a>. The malleus receives vibrations from the tympanic membrane and transmits this to the incus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRAYS2005_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GRAYS2005-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malleus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several sources attribute the discovery of the malleus to the anatomist and philosopher <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Achillini" title="Alessandro Achillini">Alessandro Achillini</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The first brief written description of the malleus was by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berengario_da_Carpi" title="Berengario da Carpi">Berengario da Carpi</a> in his <i>Commentaria super anatomia Mundini</i> (1521).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Niccolo_Massa" title="Niccolo Massa">Niccolo Massa</a>'s <i>Liber introductorius anatomiae</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> described the malleus in slightly more detail and likened both it and the <a href="/wiki/Incus" title="Incus">incus</a> to little hammers terming them <i>malleoli</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
