<p><b>Maggie Rose Durante</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> singer. In 2009, Durante signed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Republic" title="Universal Republic">Universal Republic</a> as <b>Margaret Durante</b> and released a cover of <a href="/wiki/Kings_of_Leon" title="Kings of Leon">Kings of Leon</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Use_Somebody" title="Use Somebody">Use Somebody</a>". A year later, she left Universal Republic and signed to independent Emrose Records, an imprint that used the services of <a href="/wiki/James_Stroud" title="James Stroud">James Stroud</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stroudavarious_Records" title="Stroudavarious Records">Stroudavarious Records</a>. She charted two singles for Emrose and released her digital EP, <i>Maybe Tonight</i>. Maggie also recorded two songs that were featured in episodes of the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Shake_It_Up_(TV_series)" title="Shake It Up (TV series)">Shake It Up</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Luck_Charlie" title="Good Luck Charlie">Good Luck Charlie</a></i> television series, and were included on the <i><a href="/wiki/Shake_It_Up:_Break_It_Down" title="Shake It Up: Break It Down">Shake It Up: Break It Down</a></i> soundtrack album that was released on July 12, 2011. Durante changed her recording name to <b>Maggie Rose</b> in 2012 after signing with <a href="/wiki/Scott_Siman" title="Scott Siman">Scott Siman</a>'s RPM Management. When Siman expanded RPM to include a mainstream country label, he launched the album with her as the flagship artist with the first single being "<a href="/wiki/I_Ain%27t_Your_Mama" title="I Ain't Your Mama">I Ain't Your Mama</a>".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maggie_Rose&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maggie Rose Durante was born in <a href="/wiki/Potomac,_Maryland" title="Potomac, Maryland">Potomac, Maryland</a>. She graduated from <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_Visitation_Preparatory_School" title="Georgetown Visitation Preparatory School">Georgetown Visitation Preparatory School</a>. She began singing at age sixteen and performed frequently with The B Street Band, a <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a> cover band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/Clemson_University" title="Clemson University">Clemson University</a> and was a member of <a href="/wiki/Kappa_Kappa_Gamma" title="Kappa Kappa Gamma">Kappa Kappa Gamma</a> but withdrew in her sophomore year in order to pursue a career in country music in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nashville,_TN" title="Nashville, TN">Nashville, TN</a> upon receiving a phone call from producer <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Mottola" title="Tommy Mottola">Tommy Mottola</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Durante signed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Republic" title="Universal Republic">Universal Republic</a> and released a cover of <a href="/wiki/Kings_of_Leon" title="Kings of Leon">Kings of Leon</a>'s 2008 single "<a href="/wiki/Use_Somebody" title="Use Somebody">Use Somebody</a>" Engine 145 reviewer Karlie Justus was critical of the choice of a cover for her debut single, saying that she did not think that the cover showed Durante's potential as a singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The cover was made into a music video which aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Country_Music_Television" title="Country Music Television">Country Music Television</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>A year later, she left Universal Republic and signed to independent Emrose Records, an imprint utilizing the services of <a href="/wiki/James_Stroud" title="James Stroud">James Stroud</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stroudavarious_Records" title="Stroudavarious Records">Stroudavarious Records</a>, later rebranded as R&amp;J Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Her first release for the label was "Mississippi's Crying", which entered the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> charts in December 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On April 19, 2011, Emrose/Stroudavarious released her digital EP, <i>Maybe Tonight</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2011, Durante's music video<i>"Maybe Tonight"</i> debuted in <a href="/wiki/Great_American_Country" title="Great American Country">Great American Country</a>'s Top 20 Country Countdown videos at No. 1, making her the first ever artist signed to an independent label to do so in the history of the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>Durante sang guest lead vocals on two songs they were recording for the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Shake_It_Up_(TV_series)" title="Shake It Up (TV series)">Shake It Up</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Both "Watch Me" and "Breakout" were featured in early episodes of the show, and "Breakout" was also featured in the <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Luck_Charlie" title="Good Luck Charlie">Good Luck Charlie</a></i> crossover episode "Charlie Shakes It Up" that premiered on June 5, 2011 on the Disney Channel. Both songs are included on the <i><a href="/wiki/Shake_It_Up:_Break_It_Down" title="Shake It Up: Break It Down">Shake It Up: Break It Down</a></i> soundtrack album that was released on July 12, 2011. "Watch Me" was covered by <a href="/wiki/Zendaya" title="Zendaya">Zendaya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bella_Thorne" title="Bella Thorne">Bella Thorne</a>, who star in the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Amazon_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Amazon-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, she began performing as Maggie Rose, and signed with RPM Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Her first single under this name, "<a href="/wiki/I_Ain%27t_Your_Mama" title="I Ain't Your Mama">I Ain't Your Mama</a>", was released in May 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It peaked at number 29 on the country airplay chart. "<a href="/wiki/Better_(Maggie_Rose_song)" title="Better (Maggie Rose song)">Better</a>" followed in early 2013. Both songs appear on her first RPM album, <i><a href="/wiki/Cut_to_Impress" title="Cut to Impress">Cut to Impress</a></i>. She also collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Comcast_SportsNet_Mid-Atlantic" title="Comcast SportsNet Mid-Atlantic">Comcast SportsNet Mid-Atlantic</a> to write "Get Ya Game On" and produce the song's music video for the network's coverage of the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Ravens" title="Baltimore Ravens">Baltimore Ravens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Washington_Redskins" title="Washington Redskins">Washington Redskins</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2013_NFL_season" title="2013 NFL season">2013 NFL season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The singer visited with US troops in the middle east before joining <a href="/wiki/Gary_Allan" title="Gary Allan">Gary Allan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Crow" title="Sheryl Crow">Sheryl Crow</a> on tour in the fall of 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Country Aircheck magazine reported Rose as the 6th most played female on country radio in their December 2013 year end edition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Cut_to_Impress" title="Cut to Impress">Cut to Impress</a></i> landed on quite a few "Best Of 2013" lists. It appeared on Roughstock.com's Best Albums Of 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> CoutryMusicRocks.com's Top Albums Of 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and was one of only 4 country albums to land on Amazon.com's Best Albums Of 2013 List.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2014, Rose was a guest on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bobby_Bones_Show" title="Bobby Bones Show">Bobby Bones Show</a> and performed a cover of <a href="/wiki/A_Great_Big_World" title="A Great Big World">A Great Big World</a>'s "Say Something", which was met with rave reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Looking Back Now", the third single from <i>Cut to Impress</i>, shipped to country radio in January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Later in the year, she released "Girl in Your Truck Song". Maggie Rose left RPM in 2015.</p>
