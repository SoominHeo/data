<p><b>Massachusetts Bay</b> is a bay on the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> that forms part of the coastline of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Massachusetts" title="Commonwealth of Massachusetts">Commonwealth of Massachusetts</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Massachusetts_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bay extends from <a href="/wiki/Cape_Ann" title="Cape Ann">Cape Ann</a> on the north to <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Harbor" title="Plymouth Harbor">Plymouth Harbor</a> on the south, a distance of about 42 miles (68 km). Its northern and southern shores incline toward each other through the entrance to <a href="/wiki/Boston_Harbor" title="Boston Harbor">Boston Harbor</a>, where they are about five miles apart. The depth from the base of the triangle to Boston Harbor is about 21 miles (34 km). The westmost point of the bay is at the city of <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>.</p>
<p>The northern shore of Massachusetts Bay is rocky and irregular, but the southern shore low, marshy, and sandy. Along the shores are a number of capes and headlands, and off the coast a number of small islands, especially in the entrance to Boston Harbor. The principal inlets are: on the north coast, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gloucester,_MA" title="Gloucester, MA">Gloucester</a> Harbor, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nahant,_MA" title="Nahant, MA">Nahant</a> Bay, <a href="/wiki/Salem_Harbor" title="Salem Harbor">Salem Harbor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marblehead_Harbor" title="Marblehead Harbor">Marblehead Harbor</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lynn,_MA" title="Lynn, MA">Lynn</a> Harbor, and on the west, <a href="/wiki/Boston_Harbor" title="Boston Harbor">Boston Harbor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dorchester_Bay_(Boston_Harbor)" title="Dorchester Bay (Boston Harbor)">Dorchester Bay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Quincy_Bay" title="Quincy Bay">Quincy Bay</a> (the two latter being part of the Outer Boston Harbor), and on the south coast, <a href="/wiki/Hingham_Bay" title="Hingham Bay">Hingham Bay</a>. Massachusetts Bay is itself part of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Maine" title="Gulf of Maine">Gulf of Maine</a>, which extends from <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> south to <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod_Bay" title="Cape Cod Bay">Cape Cod Bay</a>. Cape Cod Bay is sometimes considered to be part of Massachusetts Bay. Under this interpretation, the name "Massachusetts Bay" denotes the entire rectangular area of ocean between Cape Ann and <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">Cape Cod</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Massachusetts_Bay_Disposal_Site">Massachusetts Bay Disposal Site</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Massachusetts_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Massachusetts Bay Disposal Site">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Massachusetts Bay Disposal Site in deep water off the coast has been used for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ocean_dumping" title="Ocean dumping">ocean dumping</a>, to dispose of munitions, dredged material, rock and construction debris and sunken vessels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usgs_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-usgs-1">[1]</a></sup> Between 1919 and 1970 <a href="/wiki/Chemical_warfare" title="Chemical warfare">chemical warfare</a> munitions were dumped, and after World War II hundreds of thousands of tons of surplus artillery and munitions, which are the majority of munitions washed up on shore. Most of them are inert <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UXO" title="UXO">UXO</a> but occasionally they are live. Fishermen have brought a torpedo into <a href="/wiki/Provincetown,_Massachusetts" title="Provincetown, Massachusetts">Provincetown, Massachusetts</a>, a depth charge into <a href="/wiki/Gloucester,_Massachusetts" title="Gloucester, Massachusetts">Gloucester, Massachusetts</a> and mustard munitions into <a href="/wiki/New_Bedford,_Massachusetts" title="New Bedford, Massachusetts">New Bedford, Massachusetts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wbgh_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-wbgh-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Massachusetts_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Massachusetts Bay gave its name to the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay_Colony" title="Massachusetts Bay Colony">Massachusetts Bay Colony</a>, which is one of the two predecessor colonies of the current state of Massachusetts.</p>
<p>Massachusetts Bay is one of five bays besides Quincy Bay, <a href="/wiki/Narragansett_Bay" title="Narragansett Bay">Narragansett Bay</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buzzards_Bay_(bay)" title="Buzzards Bay (bay)">Buzzards Bay</a>, and Cape Cod Bay that give the state the nickname "the Bay State".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
