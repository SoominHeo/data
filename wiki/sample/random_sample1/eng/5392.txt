<p><b>Matthew D. Emmons</b> (born April 5, 1981 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mount_Holly_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Mount Holly Township, New Jersey">Mount Holly Township, New Jersey</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shooting_sports" title="Shooting sports">sport shooter</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthew_Emmons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Emmons started out as a successful junior and has been a holder of the junior world record in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/50_metre_rifle_three_positions" title="50 metre rifle three positions">50 metre rifle three positions</a>. He won both the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2002_ISSF_World_Cup_Final" title="2002 ISSF World Cup Final">2002 ISSF World Cup Final</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2004_ISSF_World_Cup_Final" title="2004 ISSF World Cup Final">2004 ISSF World Cup Final</a> in this event.</p>
<p>At the great championships however his successes have come in another event: that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/50_metre_rifle_prone" title="50 metre rifle prone">prone position</a>. He won both the <a href="/wiki/2002_ISSF_World_Shooting_Championships" title="2002 ISSF World Shooting Championships">2002 ISSF World Shooting Championships</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Shooting at the 2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a> in this event. In <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, he was very close to winning a historic double, but in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics_-_Men%27s_50_metre_rifle_three_positions" title="Shooting at the 2004 Summer Olympics - Men's 50 metre rifle three positions">three positions competition</a>, he accidentally cross-fired his last shot and finished eighth.</p>
<p>Emmons's gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Shooting at the 2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/50_metre_rifle_prone" title="50 metre rifle prone">prone position</a> came while using a borrowed rifle. In April 2004, just prior to the Olympic Team Trials, Emmons discovered his rifle had been severely sabotaged in the supposedly secure locker room at the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Olympic_Training_Center" title="United States Olympic Training Center">United States Olympic Training Center</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juneauempire.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-juneauempire.com-1">[1]</a></sup> The precisely tuned barrel and action were heavily damaged by what appeared to be a screwdriver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juneauempire.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-juneauempire.com-1">[1]</a></sup> "I unpacked my gun and I noticed that something wasn't right," Emmons said. "Sure enough, somebody had done something to it. I shot it and I couldn't get the shell out. I said, 'Something's wrong here'."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juneauempire.com_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-juneauempire.com-1">[1]</a></sup> Emmons said it could not have been an accident: "Oh no, no," Emmons said. "Somebody took a screwdriver and went in." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juneauempire.com_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-juneauempire.com-1">[1]</a></sup> Emmons went on to the <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Shooting at the 2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a>, and his gold medal in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/50_metre_rifle_prone" title="50 metre rifle prone">prone position</a> event, using his former <a href="/wiki/University_of_Alaska_Fairbanks" title="University of Alaska Fairbanks">University of Alaska Fairbanks</a> teammate, Amber Darland's, .22 rifle. He never found out who the saboteur was, but said "I'd like to know so I could shake their hand and say thanks."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juneauempire.com_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-juneauempire.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Shooting at the 2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>, Emmons won the silver medal in the <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_50_metre_rifle_prone" title="Shooting at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's 50 metre rifle prone">prone competition</a>. In his second event, <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_50_metre_rifle_three_positions" title="Shooting at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's 50 metre rifle three positions">men's 50 metre rifle three positions</a>, Emmons finished the qualification round in second place, 1 point behind the leader. In the ten shot final, Emmons overtook the leader after just the first shot. Over the next eight shots, Emmons extended his lead to 3.3 points. Then, in a shocking turn of events, his 3.3 point lead vanished when he posted a 4.4 on his final shot. He finished off the podium in 4th place. Emmons called the last shot a "freak of nature." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> "The way I come into a target is I start above the target and come down from 12 o'clock and get into the bullseye," he told reporters. "And as I get down into the bullseye is when I start to get on the trigger (with my finger) and as I was starting to get on the trigger, the gun just went off. I guess I just set it off. I got on the trigger a little too hard. I didn't feel my trigger finger shaking but I guess it was. It just hit the trigger, the gun went off and I was like 'uh, that's not going to be good - I hope it hit the black'. It hit the black, but a little high," he said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>"It's just not meant to be," said Kateřina Emmons, laughing. "He's definitely the best standing shooter out there. If he can still end up fourth with a 4.4 on the last shot, that's a hell of a shooter." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap.google.com_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap.google.com-4">[4]</a></sup> "He smoked everybody else [until the last shot]," Kateřina Emmons said.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap.google.com_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap.google.com-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Things happen for a reason," Emmons said. "I said that last time, and it's the truth. Maybe last time, the reason was Katy. This time I don't know yet, but I'm sure something good's going to come from it." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap.google.com_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-ap.google.com-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Shooting at the 2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>, Emmons won the bronze medal in the <a href="/wiki/Shooting_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_50_metre_rifle_three_positions" title="Shooting at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's 50 metre rifle three positions">three positions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matthew_Emmons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Emmons has been a resident of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mount_Holly_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Mount Holly Township, New Jersey">Mount Holly Township, New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He holds a degree in management and finance from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Alaska_Fairbanks" title="University of Alaska Fairbanks">University of Alaska Fairbanks</a> and is a graduate at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Colorado_at_Colorado_Springs" title="University of Colorado at Colorado Springs">University of Colorado at Colorado Springs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Springs,_Colorado" title="Colorado Springs, Colorado">Colorado Springs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-juneauempire.com_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-juneauempire.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Emmons married <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech</a> sport shooter and Olympic champion <a href="/wiki/Kate%C5%99ina_Emmons" title="Kateřina Emmons">Kateřina Kůrková</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> in Pilsen, Czech Republic on June 30, 2007. They met at the 2004 Olympic Games in Athens when Kateřina came to console Matt after his gold medal blunder. They both live and train at the Colorado Springs, Colo. Olympic Training Center. Their daughter, Julia, was born in 2009. Matthew Emmons was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Thyroid_cancer" title="Thyroid cancer">thyroid cancer</a> in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
