<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">MagicGate</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>MagicGate</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Digital_rights_management" title="Digital rights management">copy-protection</a> technology introduced by <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> in 1999 as part of the <a href="/wiki/Secure_Digital_Music_Initiative" title="Secure Digital Music Initiative">Secure Digital Music Initiative</a> (SDMI). It works by encrypting the content on the device and using MagicGate chips in both the storage device and the reader to enforce control over how files are copied.</p>
<p>MagicGate encryption is used in the memory cards of the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> and, as of 2004<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MagicGate&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, has been introduced into all of Sony's <a href="/wiki/Memory_Stick" title="Memory Stick">Memory Stick</a> products. Some devices, such as Sony's <a href="/wiki/Walkman#Network_Walkman" title="Walkman">Network Walkman</a>, will only accept Memory Sticks which use MagicGate technology.</p>
<p>All <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Memory_Stick_Duo" title="Memory Stick Duo">Memory Stick Duo</a> cards equipped with MagicGate can be identified by a notch located on the rear end of the card.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Support">Support</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MagicGate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Support">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Few (<a href="/wiki/USB" title="USB">USB</a> or <a href="/wiki/PC_Card" title="PC Card">PC Card</a>) Memory Stick-compatible <a href="/wiki/Card_reader" title="Card reader">card readers</a> support secure MagicGate. Although manufacturers list Memory Stick Pro/Pro Duo (MG) compatibility, where MG means MagicGate, they may not support the security portion of MagicGate. This becomes important when purchasing a card reader/writer for use with <a href="/wiki/SonicStage" title="SonicStage">SonicStage</a> and Sony CONNECT as even Sony itself does not fully support MagicGate on certain readers. Non-MagicGate memory card reader/writers do not show up in SonicStage, with the notable exception of Sony Ericsson phones.</p>
<p>Current Sony devices that support MagicGate DRM as of summer 2007:</p>
<ul>
<li>Most Memory Stick capable Sony VAIO notebooks and desktops</li>
<li>PlayStation Portable – The PlayStation Portable in service mode will only boot to Memory Sticks with MagicGate enabled</li>
<li>PlayStation 3</li>
<li>MSAC-US40 USB Memory Stick Card Reader/Writer.</li>
<li>PlayStation 2</li>
</ul>
<p>Current Sony Devices that do not support MagicGate DRM as of summer 2007:</p>
<ul>
<li>All U.S. Sony Ericsson phones – most Sony Ericsson phones should show up in SonicStage for MP3/AAC transfer</li>
<li>VGP-MCA10 PCMCIA Card Reader/Writer – does not sport the MagicGate logo</li>
<li>Sony 17 in 1 Card Reader/Writer MRW62E/S1/181</li>
<li>Sony <a href="/wiki/AVCHD" title="AVCHD">AVCHD</a> camcorders such as HDR-UX5/UX7</li>
</ul>
<p>Current Sony Devices that do not support MagicGate DRM as of summer 2007, but the support may be possible via future firmware updates:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mylo_(Sony)" title="Mylo (Sony)">Mylo</a> Personal Communicator – Mylo's 1GB internal memory can be used with SonicStage and supports OpenMG</li>
<li>Sony Portable e-Reader PRS-500 &amp; PRS-600 – may show up in SonicStage for MP3/AAC transfer.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="State_of_DRM">State of DRM</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MagicGate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: State of DRM">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2009 Sony Electronics announced they were phasing out the Sony CONNECT service in favor of Windows Media Audio &amp; Windows Media DRM, though they have not officially announced plans to continue a Windows Media DRM music service, either. As of June 2009, the future of MagicGate DRM was unknown. MagicGate slots were still a part of the default Vaio configuration as of May 2010. However, the continued development of the SonicStage software past version 4.3 and support for MagicGate DRM are also as yet unknown.</p>
