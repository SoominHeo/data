<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Mackenzie Basin</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p>The <b>Mackenzie Basin</b> (popularly and traditionally known as the <b>Mackenzie Country</b>) is an elliptical <a href="/wiki/Intermontane" title="Intermontane">intermontane</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_basin" title="River basin">basin</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Mackenzie_District" title="Mackenzie District">Mackenzie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Waitaki_District" title="Waitaki District">Waitaki Districts</a>, near the centre of the <a href="/wiki/South_Island" title="South Island">South Island</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. It is the largest such basin in New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Historically famous mainly for <a href="/wiki/Sheep_farming" title="Sheep farming">sheep farming</a>, the sparsely populated area is now also a popular tourism destination.</p>
<p>The basin was named in the 1850s by and after <a href="/wiki/James_Mckenzie_(outlaw)" title="James Mckenzie (outlaw)">James Mckenzie</a> (or in his native <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic_language" title="Scottish Gaelic language">Scottish Gaelic</a>: <span lang="gd" xml:lang="gd"><i>Seumas MacCoinneach</i></span>), a shepherd and <a href="/wiki/Cattle_raiding" title="Cattle raiding">sheep thief</a> of Scottish origin,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> who herded his stolen flocks in what was then an area almost totally empty of any human habitation, though <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">Māori</a> previously lived there intermittently.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> After his capture, the area was soon divided up amongst new sheep pasture stations in 1857.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mackenzie_Basin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>The basin extends approximately 100 kilometres (62 mi) north to south, and 40 kilometres (25 mi) east to west. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Alps_(New_Zealand)" title="Southern Alps (New Zealand)">Southern Alps</a> constitute its western edge. The majority of the Mackenzie Basin is within <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canterbury_Region" title="Canterbury Region">Canterbury</a>, although the area to the south of the <a href="/wiki/Waitaki_River" title="Waitaki River">Waitaki River</a> is in <a href="/wiki/Otago" title="Otago">Otago</a>.</p>
<p>Using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_Highway_8_(New_Zealand)" title="State Highway 8 (New Zealand)">State Highway 8</a>, it can be accessed via <a href="/wiki/Burkes_Pass" title="Burkes Pass">Burkes Pass</a> (elevation 709m / 2,326 ft) from the north and the <a href="/wiki/Lindis_Pass" title="Lindis Pass">Lindis Pass</a> (elevation 965m / 3,166 ft) from the south, or via <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_State_Highway_83" title="New Zealand State Highway 83">State Highway 83</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Waitaki_River" title="Waitaki River">Waitaki Valley</a> from the east. Aside from these passes, the terrain is generally highest at the northern end and gradually descends in a southward direction.</p>
<p>The basin is drained by the <a href="/wiki/Waitaki_River" title="Waitaki River">Waitaki River</a>. Prominent rivers crossing the Mackenzie Basin include the <a href="/wiki/Ahuriri_River" title="Ahuriri River">Ahuriri</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hakataramea_River" title="Hakataramea River">Hakataramea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tekapo_River" title="Tekapo River">Tekapo</a> Rivers. Lakes <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ohau" title="Lake Ohau">Ohau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lake_Pukaki" title="Lake Pukaki">Pukaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lake_Alexandrina_(New_Zealand)" title="Lake Alexandrina (New Zealand)">Alexandrina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Tekapo" title="Lake Tekapo">Tekapo</a> lie within the Mackenzie Basin, as do the artificial hydroelectric lakes of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ruataniwha" title="Lake Ruataniwha">Ruataniwha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lake_Benmore" title="Lake Benmore">Benmore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Aviemore" title="Lake Aviemore">Aviemore</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Settlement_and_activities">Settlement and activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mackenzie_Basin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Settlement and activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sparsely populated, and with only five settlements (<a href="/wiki/Lake_Tekapo_(town)" title="Lake Tekapo (town)">Lake Tekapo</a>, population &lt;500; <a href="/wiki/Mount_Cook_Village" title="Mount Cook Village">Mount Cook Village</a>, population &lt;150; <a href="/wiki/Twizel" title="Twizel">Twizel</a>, population &lt;1,000; <a href="/wiki/Omarama" title="Omarama">Omarama</a>, population &lt;400 and Fairlie, population &lt;800), the Mackenzie Country comprises an area of huge glacial lakes and snow-capped mountains, particularly favoured by tourists and skiers.</p>
<p>The Ohau skifield near Omarama, and Roundhill and Mount Dobson Ski Areas at Lake Tekapo, are small commercial skifields popular amongst many living in Canterbury and Otago.</p>
<p>The Mackenzie Country is frequently utilized as the principal and second-unit location for television commercials, documentaries, and motion pictures, including much of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">Peter Jackson</a>'s epic <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(film_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (film series)"><i>The Lord of the Rings</i> film trilogy</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit_(film_series)" title="The Hobbit (film series)"><i>The Hobbit</i> film trilogy</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Gliding" title="Gliding">Gliding</a> is another common activity within the Mackenzie Basin, and the area was host to a Gliding World Cup event in 1995, as well as being the home of several gliding clubs, airfields, and numerous private <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailplane" title="Sailplane">glider</a> pilots, of both amateur and professional status.</p>
<p>Due to its clean, dry and dark sky, the Mackenzie Basin serves as an important area for New Zealand-based <a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">astronomy</a>, with a number of related facilities located there, including the nation's premier astronomical observatory, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Canterbury" title="University of Canterbury">University of Canterbury</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mount_John_University_Observatory" title="Mount John University Observatory">Mount John University Observatory</a>, and several amateur observatories. Astronomy-related tourism is an increasing contributor to the area's economy, with more astro-tourism ventures in development near Lake Tekapo, Mount Cook village (planetarium) and Omarama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Each June the annual mid winter star party is held at new moon on the Omarama airfield.</p>
<p>Plans are currently well-progressed to have the Mackenzie Basin designated as a Dark Sky Reserve, possibly the World's first,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ODT_32364_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ODT_32364-6">[6]</a></sup> that will probably result in strengthening local ordinances to prevent <a href="/wiki/Light_pollution" title="Light pollution">light pollution</a>. A recent World Heritage Committee meeting in Seville in 2009 was positive about the proposed project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NZ_Herald_10583861_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-NZ_Herald_10583861-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_issues">Environmental issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mackenzie_Basin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Environmental issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tekapo-Pukaki_canal.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2304" data-file-width="3072" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Tekapo-Pukaki_canal.jpg/220px-Tekapo-Pukaki_canal.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Tekapo-Pukaki_canal.jpg/330px-Tekapo-Pukaki_canal.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Tekapo-Pukaki_canal.jpg/440px-Tekapo-Pukaki_canal.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tekapo-Pukaki_canal.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Many canals have been built in the Basin, diverting water to generate hydroelectricity.</div>

<p>The original vegetation cover prior to human settlement has been completely transformed in the basin and indeed most of the surrounding <a href="/wiki/Canterbury-Otago_tussock_grasslands" title="Canterbury-Otago tussock grasslands">Canterbury-Otago tussock grasslands</a> ecoregion, initially due to fires lit by Maori and European settlers, and in more recent times by farming activity and by pests. A relatively weakly settled area, in recent times (late 2000s), numerous proposals for new farming operations have locals fearing that the agriculture will be transformed from often family-held farms to large <a href="/wiki/Agribusiness" title="Agribusiness">agribusiness</a> operations causing increased local ecologic damage and siphoning off capital overseas. The effluent from proposed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Factory_farming" title="Factory farming">factory farming</a> schemes, one proposed set of which would release sewage the equivalent of the city of Christchurch, is also feared to put further pressure on the ecosystems of local lakes and rivers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TREASURE_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-TREASURE-8">[8]</a></sup> Over 5,000 submissions have been received in opposition to the dairy farming proposals, including from tourism associations, which fear that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_agriculture" title="Industrial agriculture">industrial agriculture</a> will damage the reputation of the area as well as its ecosystems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The extensive network of canals for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroelectric" title="Hydroelectric">hydroelectric</a> schemes posed a threat to the <a href="/wiki/Black_stilt" title="Black stilt">black stilt</a> (or kaki), an endangered river wading bird. A captive breeding programme was set up and it is administered by the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Conservation_(New_Zealand)" title="Department of Conservation (New Zealand)">Department of Conservation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The MacKenzie Basin is one of the areas where <a href="/wiki/Wilding_conifer" title="Wilding conifer">wilding conifers</a> proliferate. These weed trees cover large areas sometimes to a very high density and therefore excluding native vegetation and reducing the amount of available pasture. Some areas have control measures in place to prevent the trees from spreading.</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shaun_muckle_mtcook.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1720" data-file-width="2580" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Shaun_muckle_mtcook.jpg/220px-Shaun_muckle_mtcook.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Shaun_muckle_mtcook.jpg/330px-Shaun_muckle_mtcook.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Shaun_muckle_mtcook.jpg/440px-Shaun_muckle_mtcook.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shaun_muckle_mtcook.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mount_Cook" title="Mount Cook">Mount Cook</a> from the southern shore of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Pukaki" title="Lake Pukaki">Lake Pukaki</a>.</div>

<p>Rabbits are a common <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive species</a> in the area affect both agricultural production and <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a>. Rabbit numbers dropped after the introduction of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rabbit_haemorrhagic_disease_virus" title="Rabbit haemorrhagic disease virus">rabbit haemorrhagic disease virus</a> (RCD) but are now increasing. The Department of Conservation has plans to drop <a href="/wiki/Sodium_fluoroacetate" title="Sodium fluoroacetate">sodium fluoroacetate</a> (1080) laced poison bait to control the rabbit numbers. This is seen as necessary due to the disproportionally high number of threatened plant species in the Mackenzie Basin. The plan attracted opposition at submission hearings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>There is currently a high demand for water to irrigate the MacKenzie Basin, with 126 <a href="/wiki/Resource_consent" title="Resource consent">resource consents</a> from 36 applicants before <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environment_Canterbury" title="Environment Canterbury">Environment Canterbury</a> as of mid-2009. The water would be used to irrigate an area of 27,125 hectares (67,030 acres), but the schemes are opposed by many locals and the Department of Conservation for the potential ecological effects, and since it may clash with a proposed MacKenzie Basin Drylands Park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Royal_Forest_and_Bird_Protection_Society_of_New_Zealand" title="Royal Forest and Bird Protection Society of New Zealand">Forest and Bird</a> are also opposed to the irrigation for a variety of <a href="/wiki/Conservation_(ethic)" title="Conservation (ethic)">conservation</a> reasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
