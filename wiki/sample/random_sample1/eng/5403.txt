<p>The <b>Mack Titan</b> is a heavy duty <a href="/wiki/Truck" title="Truck">truck</a> produced by <a href="/wiki/Mack_Trucks" title="Mack Trucks">Mack Trucks</a>. There are two different variants of the Titan, one for the <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> market, that was first introduced in 1995 aimed at the heavy <a href="/wiki/Road_train" title="Road train">road train</a> operators of Australia, and a 2008 model that was introduced in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. The Titan can haul loads up to 200 tonnes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gross_Combination_Mass" title="Gross Combination Mass">GCM</a> and comes with many heavy duty options that are not usually found on highway trucks.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Australian_variant">Australian variant</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mack_Titan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Australian variant">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation">
<tr>
<td class="mbox-image">

<ul>
<li>A raised cab and taller hood allows for larger radiators to cool the engine more quickly in the hot <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian" title="Australian">Australian</a> climates.</li>
<li>Heavy duty double and triple frame rails handle the high loads and stress of driving on unpaved dirt roads.</li>
<li>Rear axles offered include tri-drive options and planetary hub reduction axles from <a href="/wiki/Renault_Trucks" title="Renault Trucks">Renault Trucks</a>.</li>
<li>Air brake system has high flow air compressor and large air tanks to provide air for 2 or more trailers and the air starter (if fitted).</li>
<li>Integrated vertical air intake snorkels keep dust and dirt out of the filters prolonging their life.</li>
<li>High <a href="/wiki/Power_(physics)" title="Power (physics)">power</a> engines from <a href="/wiki/Cummins" title="Cummins">Cummins</a> (ISX &amp; Signature) and <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar_Inc." title="Caterpillar Inc.">Caterpillar</a> (C-16) provide the necessary power. Up until 2000/2001, the Mack 16.4 litre E9 V8 was offered at 455 kW (610 hp) and 2780 Nm (2050 ft·lbf) of torque.</li>
<li>The Mack 18 speed transmission is standard with an Eaton 18 speed option. Eaton two and four speed auxiliary transmissions are also available to provide extra gears and an optional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_Tower_(pto)" title="Power Tower (pto)">Power Tower</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mack_Titan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 2008, <i>Titan</i> was introduced in <i>Bulldog</i> magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The target markets are the heavy-haul applications of: construction, logging, mining, oil field, and heavy equipment hauling. A Heavy Duty model uses the 16-liter big-block MP10, the largest ever 6-cylinder engine from Mack, with 515, 565, and 605 horsepower (451 kW) models. The 605 has a torque rating of 2,060 ft·lbf (2,790 N·m). at 1,200 RPMs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It will replace the now retired CL model which served as Mack's "big bore" truck which was powered by the <a href="/wiki/Cummins" title="Cummins">Cummins</a> ISX. It will feature the MP10 which is Mack's version of the Volvo D16C.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
