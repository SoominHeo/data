<p>The <b>MacBook</b> is a line of <a href="/wiki/Macintosh" title="Macintosh">Macintosh</a> portable computers introduced in March 2015 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apple_Inc" title="Apple Inc">Apple Inc</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2015-03-09_PR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-2015-03-09_PR-1">[1]</a></sup> The MacBook has a similar appearance to the MacBook Air, but is thinner and lighter,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and is available in space grey, silver, gold, and rose gold. It offers a high-resolution <a href="/wiki/Retina_Display" title="Retina Display">Retina Display</a>, a Force Touch trackpad, a redesigned keyboard, and only two ports: a headphone jack and a <a href="/wiki/USB_3.1" title="USB 3.1">USB 3.1</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USB_Type-C" title="USB Type-C">Type-C</a> port for charging, data transfer and video output.</p>
<p>In the MacBook product line, the MacBook sits below the <a href="/wiki/MacBook_Pro" title="MacBook Pro">MacBook Pro</a> in terms of average specifications. The price is higher than the Macbook Air, which is compensated for by the Retina Display, thin body, and higher storage and memory. However, the processor and graphic performance is inferior to the MacBook Air. Many reviewers have also criticized its keyboard for being less comfortable than that of the MacBook Air.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_MacBook_Review:_Stupidly_Thin_Gizmodo_review_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_MacBook_Review:_Stupidly_Thin_Gizmodo_review-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Macbook_review_Six_Colors_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Macbook_review_Six_Colors-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Macbook:_hands_on_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Macbook:_hands_on-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Retina_MacBook.E2.80.99s_.E2.80.98butterfly.E2.80.99_keyboard_feels_a_bit_buggy_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Retina_MacBook.E2.80.99s_.E2.80.98butterfly.E2.80.99_keyboard_feels_a_bit_buggy-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 19, 2016, Apple updated the Retina MacBook with new sixth-generation Intel Core M processors, Intel HD 515 graphics, faster RAM, longer battery life, faster storage<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Apple_Refreshes_MacBook_Line_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Apple_Refreshes_MacBook_Line-7">[7]</a></sup> and a new rose gold finish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2016-04-19_PR_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-2016-04-19_PR-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MacBook_(Retina)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Apple_Macbook12_Sideview2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1944" data-file-width="2592" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Apple_Macbook12_Sideview2.jpg/220px-Apple_Macbook12_Sideview2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Apple_Macbook12_Sideview2.jpg/330px-Apple_Macbook12_Sideview2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Apple_Macbook12_Sideview2.jpg/440px-Apple_Macbook12_Sideview2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Apple_Macbook12_Sideview2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A side view of the MacBook</div>
</div>

<p>The MacBook is designed with thinness in mind; the aluminum enclosure is 13.1 mm at its thickest point at the end of the computer next to the place where it pivots.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The MacBook includes Intel <a href="/wiki/List_of_Intel_Core_M_microprocessors" title="List of Intel Core M microprocessors">Core M</a> processors (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intel_Skylake" title="Intel Skylake">Skylake</a> architecture) which requires no fans or heat pipe, allowing the logic board to be much smaller than the one of the MacBook Air, however, storage upgradeability is no longer possible. The computer still features a full-sized keyboard and trackpad.</p>
<p>The battery was redesigned to a custom shape to fill the available space in the smaller enclosure. It uses a new 39.7 watt-hour lithium-polymer terraced battery cell which is said to provide "all day" battery life. It is said to last up to 9 hours for Internet browsing or 10 hours of iTunes movie playback. The battery was improved in the 2016 refresh, with Apple claiming to provide 10 hours for Internet browsing, and 11 hours for iTunes movie playback.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Apple_MB_2016_design_1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Apple_MB_2016_design_1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The MacBook does not include any <a href="/wiki/Beryllium" title="Beryllium">beryllium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brominated_flame_retardant" title="Brominated flame retardant">BFR</a>s, or <a href="/wiki/Polyvinyl_chloride" title="Polyvinyl chloride">PVC</a>s in its construction. The display is made of <a href="/wiki/Arsenic" title="Arsenic">arsenic</a>-free glass and contains no <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(element)" title="Mercury (element)">mercury</a>. It is made of recyclable materials such as aluminum and glass, meets <a href="/wiki/Energy_Star" title="Energy Star">Energy Star</a> 6.1 standards, and is rated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EPEAT" title="EPEAT">EPEAT</a> Gold.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Keyboard_and_trackpad">Keyboard and trackpad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MacBook_(Retina)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Keyboard and trackpad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The MacBook has a new keyboard and trackpad. Compared to previous models, the individual keys have a larger surface area. Apple replaced the scissor-like mechanism with a butterfly mechanism, making the keyboard thinner overall and, as Apple claims, individual keys more stable. The keyboard's backlight no longer consists of a row of LEDs and a light guide panel, but instead uses a single LED for each key. The letters on the Macbook's keyboard are set in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_(2014_typeface)" title="San Francisco (2014 typeface)">San Francisco</a>, whereas previous MacBooks used <a href="/wiki/VAG_Rounded" title="VAG Rounded">VAG Rounded</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2015 model of this computer was the first to introduce the Force Touch trackpad (<a href="/wiki/MacBook_Pro" title="MacBook Pro">MacBook Pro</a> was the second computer to have this technology), allowing for pressure sensitivity, and replicating a click with haptic feedback. The trackpad was later brought to the <a href="/wiki/Magic_Trackpad" title="Magic Trackpad">Magic Trackpad</a>. A similar technology was used in the <a href="/wiki/Apple_Watch" title="Apple Watch">Apple Watch</a> and <a href="/wiki/IPhone_6S" title="IPhone 6S">iPhone 6S</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ports">Ports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MacBook_(Retina)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:USB_Type-C_macbook.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2003" data-file-width="4724" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/USB_Type-C_macbook.png/220px-USB_Type-C_macbook.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/USB_Type-C_macbook.png/330px-USB_Type-C_macbook.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/USB_Type-C_macbook.png/440px-USB_Type-C_macbook.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:USB_Type-C_macbook.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
USB Type-C on MacBook</div>
</div>

<p>To achieve its thin chassis, the Macbook only contains a single, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USB_Type-C" title="USB Type-C">USB Type-C</a> port, and a 3.5 mm headphone jack. The vertically symmetrical port supports transmission speeds of up to 5 Gbit/s, and can be used for charging, output and input of data, and output of video and audio. Apple markets an adapter that can provide a full-size USB connector, and a "Digital AV Multiport Adapter" with a charging pass-through, full-size USB port, and <a href="/wiki/HDMI" title="HDMI">HDMI</a> output.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pcw-6things_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-pcw-6things-12">[12]</a></sup> Thunderbolt devices cannot function, however, since there is no built-in support for Thunderbolt data. Other devices have Thunderbolt 3 support via USB-C.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Shortly after the MacBook’s introduction, various companies began announcing cables and adapters for the USB Type-C port.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MR_Belkin_adaptors_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-MR_Belkin_adaptors-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MacBook_(Retina)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reception for the new MacBook has been generally positive, with critics praising the design and overall quality of the product. However, several described it as a limited first-generation product to avoid for extensive use until the category reached greater maturity, and its price dropped enough for mainstream adoption.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Concise_.E2.80.9CNew_Retina_Macbook.E2.80.9D_Review_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Concise_.E2.80.9CNew_Retina_Macbook.E2.80.9D_Review-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The low-power but slow performance Intel Core M processor was regarded as the new MacBook's main deficiency, relative to the cheaper and faster MacBook Air and Pro which were not much bulkier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Among the other criticisms of the new MacBook are the lack of various popular ports, particularly USB type-A ports, which limits data transfer and overall convenience without the use of an <a href="/wiki/Adapter_(computing)" title="Adapter (computing)">adapter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>The keyboard also received criticism as being poor for long-term use: developer <a href="/wiki/Marco_Arment" title="Marco Arment">Marco Arment</a> described the laptop's build and small size as "absolutely amazing, revolutionary, and mind-blowing… until you need to use the keyboard for something."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Quick_impressions_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Quick_impressions-22">[22]</a></sup> He also criticised the trackpad as worse than on previous MacBooks, and said that he would be returning the model he had bought.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mistake_One_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mistake_One-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Soon after the launch, the 2015 MacBook became the object of a humorous <i>Ratones Coloraos</i> interview meme, featuring Spanish comedian <a href="/wiki/El_Risitas" title="El Risitas">El Risitas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Quintero" title="Jesús Quintero">Jesús Quintero</a>'s TV show. In the meme, a Spanish-language interview was given subtitles claiming to present an engineer who worked on the prototype of this MacBook, laughing at how limited its features were. Within a month of its uploading, the video received over five million views on YouTube.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-europapress_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-europapress-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Apple's introduction of a gold style has been cited as an example of positioning itself as a luxury-style brand. The choice follows the introduction of the gold <a href="/wiki/IPhone_5S" title="IPhone 5S">iPhone 5S</a> after Apple found that gold was seen as a popular sign of a luxury product among <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> customers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cook_Says_Chinese_Tastes_Considered_in_Apple_Product_Designs_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cook_Says_Chinese_Tastes_Considered_in_Apple_Product_Designs-25">[25]</a></sup> Noting the appeal of Apple introducing gold laptops, IT and science journalist Casey Johnston commented, "I'm marrying one."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Twitter_post_Johnston_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Twitter_post_Johnston-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
