<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max_Borenstein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Borenstein wrote, edited, and directed the 2003 film <i>Swordswallowers and Thin Men</i> while a senior at <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>. The film starred Peter Cellini, <a href="/wiki/Zoe_Kazan" title="Zoe Kazan">Zoe Kazan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fran_Kranz" title="Fran Kranz">Fran Kranz</a> and Graham Norris, and featured <i><a href="/wiki/Army_Wives" title="Army Wives">Army Wives</a></i> star <a href="/wiki/Sally_Pressman" title="Sally Pressman">Sally Pressman</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Midnight%27s_Children_(film)" title="Midnight's Children (film)">Midnight's Children</a></i> lead <a href="/wiki/Satya_Bhabha" title="Satya Bhabha">Satya Bhabha</a>. The film won Best Feature and Best Screenplay at the New York Independent Film Festival and was named Best First Feature 2003 by <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a> critic <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Thomas_(film_critic)" title="Kevin Thomas (film critic)">Kevin Thomas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Borenstein's 2008 screenplay <i>What Is Life Worth?</i>, based on <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Feinberg" title="Kenneth Feinberg">Kenneth Feinberg</a>'s memoir of the same name, was honored with inclusion on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Black_List_(survey)" title="The Black List (survey)">The Black List</a>, an annual list compiled by Hollywood executives of their favorite unproduced screenplays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His 2009 screenplay <i>Jimi</i>, commissioned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legendary_Pictures" title="Legendary Pictures">Legendary Pictures</a> and based on the life of guitarist <a href="/wiki/Jimi_Hendrix" title="Jimi Hendrix">Jimi Hendrix</a>, was also included on The Black List.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Borenstein wrote additional projects for Legendary, including <i><a href="/wiki/Godzilla_(2014_film)" title="Godzilla (2014 film)">Godzilla</a></i>. For future projects, Borenstein will write the sequel for <i>Godzilla</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Kong:_Skull_Island" title="Kong: Skull Island">Kong: Skull Island</a></i> for Legendary,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>Paladin</i> for <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Pictures">Walt Disney Pictures</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and <i>Mona</i> for <a href="/wiki/Regency_Enterprises" title="Regency Enterprises">New Regency</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In April 2016, Borenstein was announced as an executive producer for HBO's Vinyl's second season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
