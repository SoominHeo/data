<p><b>Manchester Futsal Club</b> is an English <a href="/wiki/Futsal" title="Futsal">futsal</a> club, based in Greater Manchester. They have competed in the National <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FA_Futsal_League" title="FA Futsal League">FA Futsal League</a> since its inception and are one of the most prominent and established <a href="/wiki/Futsal" title="Futsal">futsal</a> clubs in Great Britain.</p>
<p>The club was created in March 2006 and was founded on a desire to create a sustainable club structure that would produce a futsal team that would represent the city and compete regularly at the highest level, whilst giving opportunities to individuals to go on to the highest levels of the sport, both nationally and internationally. Fundamental to the club, is also the desire to be pioneers of the game; through a commitment to introduce and accelerate the games development, knowledge, competition and popularity within the region and also at a national and worldwide level.</p>
<p>It was the first futsal club to receive the FA's Charter Standard in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> As well as working with Manchester College, the city's University futsal teams and neighbouring football clubs in the Greater Manchester area, the club also delivers a number of Manchester FA Futsal 5's leagues and futsal specific coaching sessions in local schools, sports clubs and various community projects within the Greater Manchester area.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Team_History">First Team History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: First Team History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006_-_2013">2006 - 2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2006 - 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Upon being established the club competed in the Manchester FA Regional Futsal league from September 2006. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> captained the team to league success in March 2007 with the team scoring more than eighty goals in the fourteen fixtures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> 2007 also saw the club compete in its first tournament at the Tees Valley Futsal Cup. The team won all its group and subsequent knockout games before losing a tiring final in extra time and finished runners up to Perth Futsal Club. Towards the latter part of 2007 the FA created the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FA_Futsal_League" title="FA Futsal League">FA Futsal League</a>, complimenting the longer standing annual <a href="/wiki/FA_Futsal_Cup" title="FA Futsal Cup">FA Futsal Cup</a>. The team was accepted into the Northern Conference, tying in with the club being one of the very first futsal clubs in the country to be accredited at FA Charter Standard.</p>
<p>The club started its first game in the newly formed FA National Northern Conference with a 3-2 loss, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doug_Reed_(futsal)" title="Doug Reed (futsal)">Doug Reed</a> eventually led the team to a second-place finish, secured by a win over Sheffield Hallamshire Futsal. Many of the club's players reached double figures, with the club's vice captain <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ilya_Ovechkin_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ilya Ovechkin (futsal) (page does not exist)">Ilya Ovechkin</a> leading the club and northern conference in goals scored. Midway through the season the club appointed the Brazilian tactician <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rodrigo_Freitas_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rodrigo Freitas (futsal) (page does not exist)">Rodrigo Freitas</a> in a dual club manager/coach role, with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Wright_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Simon Wright (futsal) (page does not exist)">Simon Wright</a> moving up to a general manager position.</p>
<p>A second-place finish meant they qualified for the National Play-off Finals, the club faced a three-way group, matched against the Midland teams of Ipswich Wolves and Birmingham Tigers. Despite being down 4-0 in their first game, Manchester managed to finish their game with the heavily tipped and reigning champions Ipswich Wolves, 4-4. The second game saw Manchester face Birmingham Tigers, with the fatigue of the first game evident, the Manchester team succumbed to an 8-5 loss, which knocked the club out of the winners brackets. A depleted Ipswich Wolves team later lost in the overall play off final to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Helvecia_Futsal" title="Helvecia Futsal">Helvecia Futsal</a>, with Manchester finishing 6th overall.</p>
<p>The second season in the National League saw a string of squad changes, with a number of established players leaving the club for various reasons and a number of subsequent new players coming in. The team suffered various team changes due to injuries and the time taken to bed new players to the game, into the team. They finished in third place, missing out on a play-off place by two points, a controversial 5-4 loss to Loughborough University being the most crucial result of the season. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> topped the club's goal scoring, with seven goals, which highlighted the difference between the relative success of 2008's campaign contrasted with the flat 2009 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doug_Reed_(futsal)" title="Doug Reed (futsal)">Doug Reed</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ross_Farran_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ross Farran (futsal) (page does not exist)">Ross Farran</a> broke into the England F-30 squad, with Reed later moving on to represent and establish himself within the England international team. Furthermore, Reed went on to sign a one-year deal with Spanish second division team <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lanzarote_Tias_Yaiza&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lanzarote Tias Yaiza (page does not exist)">Lanzarote Tias Yaiza</a> at the end of the season, becoming one of the very first English players to compete professionally in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Farran became one of four winners of the Nike "Most Wanted" Accolades, the winners selected by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester_United" title="Manchester United">Manchester United</a>'s Sir Alex Ferguson. He shortly became a part of the Nike Academy squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After being invited by Gothenburg Futsal Club, the club competed in its first game on international soil. Manchester ended the tournament in third place, competing against other clubs from Sweden and Uruguay.</p>

<p>The 2010 campaign started off disappointingly, but under the captaincy of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Anthony_Haralambous_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Anthony Haralambous (futsal) (page does not exist)">Anthony Haralambous</a>, the team remained unbeaten from their opening day defeat, battling for the Northern Conference title with Sheffield throughout the season. Ultimately, the penultimate game of the season between the two clubs decided the championship, with Sheffield romping to an emphatic 6-1 victory that effectively sealed the title. Manchester remained in second place and in strong contention for a playoff spot, which they secured with a final day 3-2 victory over third-placed Tranmere Victoria.</p>
<p>At the national finals, despite having the majority of the play, Manchester lost their first game to an efficient Team United Birmingham, 3-2. In a closely fought second match, Manchester lost 5-4 to Spartans. Overall results placed Manchester 5th nationally. Helvecia were crowned as national champions for a third time in a row, after beating Spartans in an exhilarating final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The team also qualified and competed in the FA Futsal Cup 2010, topping their qualifying group unbeaten and in the process avenging their previous years National Finals loss to Team United Birmingham. The team were eventually knocked out in the quarter finals via a controversial "ghost goal" 1-0 loss to Northern rivals Sheffield. Helvecia were again the eventual winners of the tournament, beating rivals Spartans in an extra time final.</p>
<p>At the start of the year the club's top scorer <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> was selected for the England training squad and later made his full international debut for the National team in October 2010, vs. Macedonia.</p>
<p>Manchester's manager and coach, Rodrigo Freitas, announced that the FA Futsal Cup would be his last in charge. In October, the club announced <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Periklies_Antoniou_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Periklies Antoniou (futsal) (page does not exist)">Periklies Antoniou</a> as the new coach of the squad, together with the introduction of assistant coach <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Berdejo-del-Fresno_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Daniel Berdejo-del-Fresno (futsal) (page does not exist)">Daniel Berdejo-del-Fresno</a> to complete the new management line-up. Over the course of the pre-season the new management evaluated the team and the final squad for the National League 2011 was selected in November.</p>
<p>After the previous season's achievements, the club started as strongly tipped contenders for the Northern title. The first game of the season suggested that winning that elusive title would be no guarantee as they drew at home to a strengthened Leeds team. After dropping these two points, the team went on a six-game winning streak, including a 6-4 win over Sheffield, that put the club in pole position midway through the season. Sheffield, the reigning Northern champions, again proved the club's closest rivals come the season's end and after an incredibly tight 25 minutes of action in the returning tie; Sheffield showed a final third ruthless streak that ended in a 7-2 loss for Manchester. The title was still a two horse race, but Manchester held the advantage and only needed to win their remaining fixtures to take the top spot. In the club's final game, a resilient Middlesbrough held on to spring a surprising 3-2 victory over the club, allowed Sheffield to pip the club, with a final fixtures victory over Tranmere Victoria.</p>
<p>For the third successive year, the club qualified for the National Finals. In their first group game, vs Helvecia, the clubs were quite evenly matched throughout large parts of the game, but Helvecia's experience and ability to make scoring opportunities count shone through and they ran out as 4-1 winners. In the second group game the team faced a Chippenham side that was threadbare in squad size. Manchester showed an extreme ruthlessness that carried ten different scorers and finished 20-1, a playoff record. Helvecia successfully defended their hold of the national championship, beating Sheffield in the Grand Final.</p>
<p>This left the FA Futsal Cup as the final tournament of the season for the club. In similar fashion to the club's previous manager, this would also be <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Periklies_Antoniou_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Periklies Antoniou (futsal) (page does not exist)">Periklies Antoniou</a> final weekend as the club head coach. The team topped their group beating Team Baltic to second place due to a greater goal difference. This was helped by the 18-1 thrashing over Team Northumbria, which set the record for the largest FA Futsal Cup finals win.</p>
<p>After beating Passlona in the quarter finals, the team faced the FA Futsal Cup holders and National Champions, Helvecia. With the game closely poised and looking to go into extra time, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ilya_Ovechkin_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ilya Ovechkin (futsal) (page does not exist)">Ilya Ovechkin</a> scored a breakaway goal with minutes left and the club saw the game out, setting up a final with Team Baltic. In the final, a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stuart_Cook_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stuart Cook (futsal) (page does not exist)">Stuart Cook</a> hat trick and goals from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doug_Reed_(futsal)" title="Doug Reed (futsal)">Doug Reed</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> sealed a 5-2 victory in the final, confirming the club as 2011 FA Futsal Cup champions. Additionally, some of the club's players received individual awards based on their performances. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doug_Reed_(futsal)" title="Doug Reed (futsal)">Doug Reed</a> was named player of the tournament and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Haralambous_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Daniel Haralambous (futsal) (page does not exist)">Daniel Haralambous</a> the tournaments top scorer.</p>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marcos_Leon_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marcos Leon (futsal) (page does not exist)">Marcos Leon</a> took charge of the first team and the campaign started with an emphatic opening day 11-1 victory over Tranmere Rovers, but then the club shortly succumbed to their biggest loss in recent years, losing 5-0 at home to Middlesbrough. The magnitude of the loss appeared to galvanise the players and from that point onwards, they remained unbeaten for the remainder of the season.</p>
<p>Crucial last minute goals to earn a vital win away at Sheffield and a draw away at Middlesbrough, meant the team remained in poll position for the majority of the season and they went on to claim their first National League North conference title. In the process they scored 118 goals in the 12 game campaign, whilst only conceding 27 and went on to record the leagues largest recorded victory margin, with a 27-0 massacre over Hull Futsal at the Velodrome. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> finished as both the club's and the league's top scorer with 30 goals.</p>
<p>In the first game of the National finals, the team were defeated 9-4 by Oxford Lions who managed to punish Manchester's use of playing with a flying goal keeper for the majority of the game. After Genesis beat Oxford Lions in the second group game, Manchester still had an outside chance to qualify, but they were only able to beat Genesis 7-3. Oxford Lions qualified from the group on goal difference and subsequently lost to Helvecia in the Grand Final. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> finished as the first English golden boot winner at the Grand Finals, with 5 goals.</p>
<p>This campaign was to be <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Berdejo-del-Fresno_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Daniel Berdejo-del-Fresno (futsal) (page does not exist)">Daniel Berdejo-del-Fresno</a> last with the club, as he moved on to be head coach of Sheffield and a part of the newly adjusted England national coaching set up.</p>
<p>At the beginning of the year <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stuart_Cook_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stuart Cook (futsal) (page does not exist)">Stuart Cook</a> returned to the England national squad after a long absence out due to a knee injury and gained his return call up in a friendly match vs. France. At the end of the year he scored his first goal for his country, in a 3-2 victory away against Denmark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the same year <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ross_Bond_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ross Bond (futsal) (page does not exist)">Ross Bond</a> made the progression from the England development squad to the full team and later on in the year made his full international debut vs. Turkey.</p>
<p>In 2013, the team again remained toe to toe all the way to the final game of the season and despite scoring over 100 goals and remaining unbeaten until the final game of the season, a Sheffield victory resigned the club to second position in the North. In the Super League playoffs, they comfortably dispatched Team Birmingham twice, but lost to Helveica who took the one and only finals spot, with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stuart_Cook_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stuart Cook (futsal) (page does not exist)">Stuart Cook</a> finishing the season as the club's top goal scorer with 22 goals. Subsequently, at the end of this season, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Frank_Chiarella_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Frank Chiarella (futsal) (page does not exist)">Frank Chiarella</a> took over the reigns of first team manager.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_-_2016">2014 - 2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014 - 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 2014 season the team fully focused it's attention on returning the title back to Manchester. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stuart_Cook_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stuart Cook (futsal) (page does not exist)">Stuart Cook</a> led the team to romp through to a historical unbeatean North campaign league win, in which the team scored 96 goals and conceded only 27 goals in 14 games on its way to the Super League Finals.</p>
<p>After drawing Loughborough in the semi-finals, an <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ilya_Ovechkin_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ilya Ovechkin (futsal) (page does not exist)">Ilya Ovechkin</a> winner saw the team go through to the finals via a 5-4 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and face the high spending Baku United outfit in the final. The final was extremely close, but with 10 minutes left and the game on a knife's edge at 2-1, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stuart_Cook_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stuart Cook (futsal) (page does not exist)">Stuart Cook</a> was controversially sent off for a second caution and the team was unable to recover from the goal that was scored immediately following this, with Baku eventually running out 5-1 winners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The club also competed in the FA Futsal Cup finals, which was the first time it returned since they won the competition in 2011. After comfortable progression through their group and smashing Loughborough 6-1 in the semi finals, thanks in part to a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> hat trick, the club faced Baku United again in the cup final, a repeat of the Grand Final. Despite another close game, Baku were again able to show their strength in depth and ran out 2-0 winners, to complete a double.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Notably, due to various circumstances, the team was never able to produce a squad that included all of Cook, Richardson, Bond, Quintana, Ovechkin and King in any game. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> regained his top goal scorer title, with 29 goals in total.</p>
<p>2015 saw a reformatted FA Futsal Cup and FA Super League, that produced a more congested fixture list for the club. At the beginning of the season, a number of the experienced players left the club and they were replaced with several youngsters with high potential, but whom were relatively inexperienced and this dramatically changed the dynamic of the squad and the focus of the campaign.</p>
<p>Despite the adjustment, the club again went unbeaten throughout their North campaign and retained their title, this time winning every game they played in. Similarly, they comfortably qualified for the FA Futsal Cup finals, finishing runners up in their qualification group behind Baku. In the Super League group, the relatively inexperienced team struggled to find consistency and ended up finishing just outside the qualification spots. Within the season campaign, it was decided that <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chris_Vernon_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chris Vernon (futsal) (page does not exist)">Chris Vernon</a> would take over first team responsibilities from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Frank_Chiarella_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Frank Chiarella (futsal) (page does not exist)">Frank Chiarella</a> going forward.</p>
<p>With the focus on regaining their FA Futsal Cup title, the club managed to again comfortably win their group and beat Genesis 5-2 in the semi final, helped in no part to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Stapleton(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Danny Stapleton(futsal) (page does not exist)">Danny Stapleton</a> who scored in every game up to the final. In the final, they met an even stronger Baku United than previously faced and this was proven with a 3-0 victory to the Azerbaijan backed club, meaning they regained the prestigious cup trophy once again. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> finished as the clubs top goal scorer, with 40 goals in total and this set a new season club record.</p>
<p>The 2016 campaign, saw the FA National League's top regional competitions absorb into two main leagues; North and South. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> took over the captaincy, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stuart_Cook_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stuart Cook (futsal) (page does not exist)">Stuart Cook</a> moved to London Helvecia and England International <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jordan_Parker_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jordan Parker (futsal) (page does not exist)">Jordan Parker</a> moved on to pursue other interest. Due to the squad make-up, there club focused on developing some of the younger players for the longer term benefit of the club. However, the club remained relatively competitive throughout the season and still finished a comfortable second, behind a strong Team Birmingham that had recently brought in a number of experienced overseas players as their core.</p>
<p>The club drew London Helvecia in the Super League playoffs, and similarly to the games against Birmingham earlier on in the season, it competed well in its first game until the final quarter, where the game quickly ran away from Manchester's control and ultimately the difference in experience saw Helvecia win. Helvecia comfortably won the return game, but ended their losing to Oxford Lions on penalties, in the Grand Finals. In the FA Futsal Cup, the club made it to the Quarter Finals of the competition, but surprisingly lost to Midlands Division 2 team Cambridge United, in a game that was controversially re-arranged. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Raducio_King(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Raducio King(futsal) (page does not exist)">Raducio King</a> who were the joint top scorers over the season with 18 goals each, were both unavailable due to the re-arrangement. Oxford Lions beat Genesis in the finals of the FA Futsal Cup, to complete a season double.</p>
<p>The development focus of the current first team proved fruitful, exemplified by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Danny_Stapleton_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Danny Stapleton (futsal) (page does not exist)">Danny Stapleton</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ben_Seol_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ben Seol (futsal) (page does not exist)">Ben Seol</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Giles_Collier_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Giles Collier (futsal) (page does not exist)">Giles Collier</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jordan_Edge_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jordan Edge (futsal) (page does not exist)">Jordan Edge</a> all moving up to the England U23's squad and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yussef_Abdullahi_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yussef Abdullahi (futsal) (page does not exist)">Yussef Abdullahi</a> being selected for England U19's. Furthermore, recent addition to the squad <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Ward(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Richard Ward(futsal) (page does not exist)">Richard Ward</a>, represented the England national squad in the Main Round of UEFA's European Championships qualifier's.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_Futsal_Academy">Manchester Futsal Academy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Manchester Futsal Academy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Starting from the 2016 season, the club has an additional team represented in the FA National League North (Div 2), which is currently the country's second tier of elite competition. Specifically set up with the longer term aim of giving the clubs younger players an opportunity to play and experience games in a high level of competition. The squad is primarily made up of U21 players, alongside experienced players who act as mentors to the younger players and also players that require additional development time. The academy team is headed by the management team of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Karl_Burns_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Karl Burns (futsal) (page does not exist)">Karl Burns</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Russell_Fryer_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Russell Fryer (futsal) (page does not exist)">Russell Fryer</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="MFC_Elite_Development">MFC Elite Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: MFC Elite Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the beginning of 2012 the club created an Elite Development Squad, for its U21 players. It continues to train and recruit players, preparing them for the transition into the first team squad and help adapt those that have been brought up in a with a football environment. It has a direct relationship with both the senior team and academy team, supporting the underlining values of a player's pathway and development that is core to the club's values. The squad currently competes in the Manchester Futsal 5's League and across the country in various cup competitions and friendlies and is coached by first team captain <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Samuel_Richardson_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Samuel Richardson (futsal) (page does not exist)">Samuel Richardson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="MFC_Youth_Academy">MFC Youth Academy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: MFC Youth Academy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Youth Director <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ilya_Ovechkin_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ilya Ovechkin (futsal) (page does not exist)">Ilya Ovechkin</a> heads up and develops the club's youth academy program to specifically produce futsal players for the club's academy alongside a team of experienced coaches. Through the use of development centres and the production of teams ranging from u8's through to u16's, with a clear and defined pathway for players to compete right through to the highest standards of the game once they reach 16.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_Team_Trips">Youth Team Trips</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Youth Team Trips">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Fundamental to the youth development programme is the provision of opportunities for the club to go abroad and visit various cities and countries of the world, to heighten and increase the experience for players, coaches and the management team as a whole, particularly in regions of the world where the sport is more firmly established. Most notably, the youth teams have visited Murcia in Spain, for the last few years, playing games in the region and linking up with LNFS team ElPozo Murcia FS and CD Murcia FS.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_Futsal_Club_Women">Manchester Futsal Club Women</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Manchester Futsal Club Women">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012, the club established a women's team, that competes both regionally and nationally, in the FA Futsal Women's league and FA Futsal Cup. The program is currently headed by manager Abdallah Mohammad and is assisted by Leanne Skarratt, who is also the club's captain and driver of the women's programme.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_International_Futsal_Tournaments.5B12.5D">Manchester International Futsal Tournaments<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Manchester International Futsal Tournaments[12]">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An arm of the club that is involved in the running of local, national and international competitions at all ages and levels, individual and team tours, coaching seminars and futsal specific promotions. A multi-aged Manchester International Futsal Club tournament ran in 2015, at the Velodrome. Over 2016, a series of one day youth specific competitions were held, to promote the game and allow teams familiar and unfamiliar to the sport an opportunity to develop and compete.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_Projects">Club Projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Club Projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among the clubs desire to produce a nationally competitive team on the court, the club also heavily values its role in helping increase the popularity and participation of the sport. It looks to host FA Futsal Level 1 Coaching courses, alongside organising guest speakers and coaching seminars from around the world.</p>
<p>The club has helped introduce and develop teams from the surrounding College and University infrastructures. It currently holds a development partnership with the University of Manchester Futsal team, whilst in the past it has introduced and assisted a Manchester Metropolitan University team in University competition. Similarly, it helped prepare Manchester College for its British College National Futsal Championship, which saw them win the tournament, beating Queen Mary's College in the 2009 final held at the @Futsal Centre in Swindon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The club maintain a strong social media presence on Twitter,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Facebook<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and Instagram and a majority of the club's home games are recorded and highlights are posted on its "MFC TV" YouTube channel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FA_Futsal_5.27s_Leagues">FA Futsal 5's Leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: FA Futsal 5's Leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Local teams in the Greater Manchester region compete with each other within an 8 team league competition at various venues across the region, with subsequent winners going on to have a chance to represent themselves in the FA Futsal Cup. Currently, the league opportunities are available at both men's and women's open age.</p>

<p>With the imminent arrival of the Professional Futsal League in 2017, a promotional exhibition game between Brazil's Falcao and Portugals' Ricardinho, two of the world's most famous and celebrated futsal players, alongside a number of other professional players was launched. After a worldwide competition was set up to find some players to fill out the respective teams, the club's <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Raducio_King_(futsal)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Raducio King (futsal) (page does not exist)">Raducio King</a> was selected by the panel and chosen by Ricardinho to participate on his team in the game.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="10_Year_Anniversary">10 Year Anniversary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 10 Year Anniversary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2016, the club celebrated its 10-year anniversary at the Velodrome, by hosting a number of its youth teams playing against the comparative team from CD Murcia FS. For the main event and to celebrate the history of the club, a "Team Ovechkin" vs "Team Richardson" game was set up that brought together two squads of past and current players.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kevin_Davies_All_Stars_Showcase_Game">Kevin Davies All Stars Showcase Game</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Kevin Davies All Stars Showcase Game">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the summer of 2015, the club linked up with current Bolton and Bury league's ambassador and ex-Bolton Wanderers legend Kevin Davies to help create a promotional showcase game. Hosted at the Velodrome, the first team faced an exhibition team, with the two squads made up of some of the world's best freestyles; Edward Van Gils, Sean Garnier, Issy Hitman and Steve "STR" Roberts, alongside familiar and established futsal and ex-pro footballers, that ran as the main event, either side of the finals of the Bolton and Bury youth futsal competitions.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tiger_Street_Football">Tiger Street Football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Tiger Street Football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The club embarked on a unique Far Eastern trip and competed in the ESPN Star 2011 Tiger Street Football Tournament, held in Singapore, the first of its kind to be staged in Asia, which was a hybrid of predominately street football and cage football, with a futsal ball. The club were invited by ESPN Star to be the UK's representatives in 2011 and 2013, competing alongside the best international street football teams from Brazil, Vietnam, Singapore, Netherlands, Argentina, France, Cambodia, Australia and Thailand, as well as the best local teams at each stage. The team finished 5th in Singapore, 2nd in Malaysia, 1st in the Mongolian leg and finished 5th place overall in the Vietnam Grand Finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_City_FC_.26_CITC_Links">Manchester City FC &amp; CITC Links</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Manchester City FC &amp; CITC Links">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The club has helped support the preparation for the academy teams that MCFC put forward in the Premier League futsal competitions. Furthermore, it has also forged close ties with Manchester City's "City in the Community", hosting a number of exhibition games between the clubs players versus ex-pro players from Manchester City and the CITC coaches to raise the profile of the game. Most recently, the club hosted a CITC team that played the MFC team in a friendly in support of a CITC initiative. The CITC team included ex-professionals such as Patrick Vieria, Terry Phelan and Nigel Gleghorn alongside CITC coaches and was used as an exhibition to raise the profile of the sport.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Events_Delivered">Events Delivered</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Futsal_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Events Delivered">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>Umbro International Futsal Tournament 2008 -</b> In 2008 the Umbro International Futsal Tournament made its debut, and it consisted of eight teams from all over the nation, competing over two days. The overall winners of the tournament were Kickers Futsal Club, who comfortably beat London Brazil 6-0 in the final. Futsal Gold/Manchester Maccabi took home the plate competition, beating Ardwick Futsal 6-4 in the final, after extra time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Umbro International Futsal Tournament 2009 -</b> The tournament's second year saw a bigger and more competitive tournament, including the addition of two international teams. The semi-finals of the competition were highly energized and presented a good standard of Futsal. KPP Baraberi from Slovakia advanced after a close affair against the returning champions of London-based Kickers Futsal. Norwegian team, Boenes Aires defeated the hosting Manchester Futsal Club in an equally tight game, 3-1. In the final, KPP Baraberi's ability to use the flying goalkeeper and take their chances meant they held the edge over the Norwegian team and ended up winning the final and the overall tournament 4-2. Ardwick Futsal Club took the plate home after an entertaining final against the newly formed Liverpool Futsal Club, with an 8-5 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Sports Vest -</b> staff from the club delivered an international student futsal tournament. The tournament took place in Lloret De Mar near Barcelona as part of Sportvest 2011, where thousands of students from around the world came together to compete in a range of sports. SportsVest has a huge following as students take time out from their studies and head to the sunshine over the Easter Break and this was the first futsal competition ran at the event.</li>
<li><b>Railsport International Futsal Tournament -</b> delivered by the club at the Velodrome, for railway workers from across Europe. The team from the Czech Republic ended up as the eventual winners of the tournament.</li>
</ul>
