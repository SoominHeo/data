<p><b>Alliance Manchester Business School</b> (<b>Alliance MBS</b>) is the <a href="/wiki/Business_school" title="Business school">business school</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Manchester" title="University of Manchester">University of Manchester</a> in Manchester, England.</p>
<p>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">Financial Times</a></i> 2011 Global MBA Rankings, its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M.B.A" title="M.B.A">M.B.A</a> programme is ranked 29th in the world and its Post Graduate and Doctoral program (PhD and DBA) is ranked 1st in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> MBS "Marketing" has been ranked 9th in the world and its "International Business" has been ranked 10th in the world as per the Financial Times 2012 ranking.</p>
<p>It includes departments from both the former <a href="/wiki/Victoria_University_of_Manchester" title="Victoria University of Manchester">Victoria University of Manchester</a>'s Faculty of Business Administration, and from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UMIST" title="UMIST">UMIST</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Business_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>The "new" Manchester Business School was formed in 2004 as a result of the merger of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UMIST" title="UMIST">UMIST</a>'s Manchester School of Management, the Institute of Innovation Research (IoIR), the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_University_of_Manchester" title="Victoria University of Manchester">Victoria University of Manchester</a>'s School of Accounting and Finance, and the "old" Manchester Business School. Prior to merger the constituent parts of new MBS formed, from 1994,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THE_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-THE-2">[2]</a></sup> the "Manchester Federal School of Business and Management" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and occupied nearby buildings either side of Oxford Road<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THE_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-THE-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The foundation of the School dates back to 1918 when the Manchester Municipal College of Technology (as UMIST was then called) pioneered academic training in management, with the formation of a Department of Industrial Administration<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Byng_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Byng-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> funded by an endowment from <a href="/wiki/Asbestos" title="Asbestos">asbestos</a> magnate <a href="/wiki/Turner_%26_Newall" title="Turner &amp; Newall">Sir Samuel Turner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/London_School_of_Economics" title="London School of Economics">London School of Economics</a> and Manchester Municipal College of Technology were the first higher education institutions in the UK to follow the US trend of offering postgraduate management programmes in the 1930s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The Department of Industrial Administration was heavily influenced by management science pioneer <a href="/wiki/Charles_Garonne_Renold" title="Charles Garonne Renold">Charles Garonne Renold</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Robbins_Report" title="Robbins Report">Robbins Report</a> in the 1960s recommended that two national centres for postgraduate business education be created, and the <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Franks,_Baron_Franks" title="Oliver Franks, Baron Franks">Franks Report</a> subsequently suggested that one would be in London centred on the <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a>, and one in Manchester centred on the (Victoria) University and UMIST.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> As a result, Manchester was one of the first two business schools in the UK offering MBA degrees.</p>
<p>On 21 September 2015, Manchester Business School changed its name to Alliance Manchester Business School,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> following a donation from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_Alliance" title="Lord Alliance">Lord Alliance</a> of Manchester and his fellow Trustees of the Alliance Family Foundation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Business_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The school comes under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Humanities_(University_of_Manchester)" title="Faculty of Humanities (University of Manchester)">Faculty of Humanities of the University of Manchester</a>. As of 2008<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Business_School&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> with over 200 teaching staff it is the largest campus-based business school in the country, and has an international student composition as three quarters of its student body is from outside the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Manchester offers <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ph.D" title="Ph.D">Ph.D</a> (full and part-time); <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Business_Administration" title="Doctor of Business Administration">D.B.A</a> - Doctor of Business Administration (executive part-time); <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M.B.A" title="M.B.A">M.B.A</a> - Master of Business Administration (full-time, executive part-time, and blended learning); several specialist masters programmes as well as undergraduate degrees. Entry requirements for undergraduate and postgraduate degrees are both highly competitive. For popular undergraduate courses such as Accounting and/or Finance there are just 70 places available for approximately 1600 applicants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> As of 2006, a minimum work experience of 3 years and a good <a href="/wiki/Graduate_Management_Admission_Test" title="Graduate Management Admission Test">GMAT</a> is required for admission to the MBA programs.</p>
<p>The 18 months full-time MBA program is known for its "Manchester Method" which puts emphasis on learning by doing throughout the entire duration of the programme, including significant real life projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In addition, the school offers the so-called "Manchester Gold" programme, a mentoring scheme to provide students with opportunities to meet regularly with professionals from a wide variety of sectors and industries. Last year's mentors, for example, came from high-profile companies such as <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Stanley" title="Morgan Stanley">Morgan Stanley</a>, <a href="/wiki/PricewaterhouseCoopers" title="PricewaterhouseCoopers">PricewaterhouseCoopers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">Royal Dutch Shell</a> Group, <a href="/wiki/IBM_Global_Services" title="IBM Global Services">IBM Global Services</a> and <a href="/wiki/GlaxoSmithKline" title="GlaxoSmithKline">GlaxoSmithKline</a> among many others.</p>
<p>As of 2013 the Head of MBS is Professor <a href="/wiki/Fiona_Devine" title="Fiona Devine">Fiona Devine</a> OBE. The school's advisory board is chaired by businessman <a href="/wiki/Tony_De_Nunzio" title="Tony De Nunzio">Tony De Nunzio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thestreet_kkr_senior_advisor_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-thestreet_kkr_senior_advisor-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accreditation_and_reputation">Accreditation and reputation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Business_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Accreditation and reputation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among the bodies validating MBS programmes, the <a href="/wiki/British_Psychological_Society" title="British Psychological Society">British Psychological Society</a> validates its Organizational Psychology masters, and the <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Institute_of_Personnel_and_Development" title="Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development">Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development</a> its HRM MSc degree. Its MBA is one of a select band worldwide which receive <a href="/wiki/Triple_accreditation" title="Triple accreditation">triple accreditation</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AACSB" title="AACSB">AACSB</a> International, <a href="/wiki/Association_of_MBAs" title="Association of MBAs">AMBA</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EQUIS" title="EQUIS">EQUIS</a>. It is also a program partner of the <a href="/wiki/CFA_Institute" title="CFA Institute">CFA Institute</a>.</p>
<p>The Financial Times Global MBA Ranking 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> survey ranks MBS MBA 5th in the UK, 12th in Europe and 35th in the world.</p>
<p>In 2011, the school was ranked among the top 30 business schools in the world for its MBA program, and 1st in the world for its doctoral program (both Ph.D and D.B.A).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rankings1_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-rankings1-17">[17]</a></sup> It is placed 11th in Europe and 4th in the UK, while being 6th in Europe for percentage salary increase of its graduates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rankings1_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-rankings1-17">[17]</a></sup> Regarding international experience, MBS is 17th in the world and 7th in the Europe as well as is ranked 13th in the world for the career progress of its alumni.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rankings1_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-rankings1-17">[17]</a></sup> The average salary of Manchester's MBA graduates after three years is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">US$</a>116,100 - the 12th highest of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> business schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rankings1_17-3"><a href="#cite_note-rankings1-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other rankings, such as the most recent <i>Forbes</i> survey, which lists business schools on their "return on investment" via a survey of alumni salaries, put Manchester 2nd in the UK and 5th in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In the <i>Which MBA?</i> survey MBS reached the 5th place in the UK, 8th in Europe and 30th in the world. The <i>MBA Career Guide</i> of international MBA recruiters placed Manchester 2nd in the UK, 7th in Europe and 15th in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In the 2009 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/QS_Global_200_Business_Schools_Report" title="QS Global 200 Business Schools Report">QS Global 200 Business Schools Report</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> the school was ranked 14th in Europe.</p>
<p>In the UK government Research Assessment Exercise 2008 (RAE 2008),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> MBS is ranked first of all UK universities for research power in Business and Management and Finance and Accounting. The Financial Times ranks MBS PhD programme 1st in the World (Jan 2012).</p>
<p>Furthermore, Manchester was selected by <a href="/wiki/UK_Border_Agency" title="UK Border Agency">UK Border Agency</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Highly_Skilled_Migrant_Programme" title="Highly Skilled Migrant Programme">Highly Skilled Migrant Programme</a> which gives MBA-graduates from the world's top 50 business schools extra qualification points and helps them meet visa requirements to work in the UK. The <a href="/wiki/Quality_Assurance_Agency_for_Higher_Education" title="Quality Assurance Agency for Higher Education">Quality Assurance Agency for Higher Education</a> (QAA) assessed the school's teaching quality with 24 out of 24 points.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_MBA_programme">Global MBA programme</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Business_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Global MBA programme">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MBS offers a unique part-time option for Master of Business Administration students called the Part-time Global MBA. This programme provides online instruction along with the ability for students to study at any of six global centres, while receiving the same degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Students are able to take classes in Manchester, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>. Students spend one week per semester attending workshops at one of the seven centres, and ten nights' hotel stay is included with the cost of tuition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Each centre has its own team of staff and facilities and all of them together support over 3,500 students from more than 100 countries around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The same faculty members teach across the centres representing forty different countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Buildings">Buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Business_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The older part of MBS, called "MBS West" is part of the University Precinct Centre complex on the southwest corner of Oxford Road and Booth Street West. The building houses lecture and conference suites and restaurant facilities including a 101-room hotel that is opening in 2017. It is also the home of the MBA programme. The complex was designed by Hugh Wilson and Lewis Womersley and dates from the 1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The newer part "MBS East" lies on the northeast corner of Oxford Road and Booth Street East. It was completed in 1997 at the time when the UMIST departments co-located to form the Federal School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THE_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-THE-2">[2]</a></sup> The building cost £7m and was designed by London architects ORMS. MBS East currently houses undergraduate services and some of the Research Centres of MBS.</p>
<p>Manchester Accounting and Finance Group (MAFG) of MBS is located in Crawford House, which is situated opposite MBS East on the southwest corner of Oxford Road and Booth Street West and is linked via a pedestrian walkway. The <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Enterprise_Centre" title="Manchester Enterprise Centre">Manchester Enterprise Centre</a>, pioneer of the <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Enterprise" title="Master of Enterprise">Master of Enterprise</a> degree, is located in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zochonis_Building&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zochonis Building (page does not exist)">Zochonis Building</a>, Brunswick Street.</p>
<p>As announced on 15 August 2014, The MBS West is now approved for £50m refurbishment. This strategic development forms part of the University’s ten-year, £1 billion Campus Masterplan to create some of the most modern campus facilities in the world along the southern gateway to the city, known as the Manchester Corridor. Subject to planning consent, work on the retail redevelopment will begin in 2015, with the development set to open in mid-2016. The wider scheme of MBS refurbishment works will be completed in phases with the final phase due for completion in early 2018. The plans reconfigure the existing precinct centre to create up to 14 units with double-height glazed frontages onto Oxford Road. The variety of units will create a mixed offer of retail, food and leisure for students, staff and visitors to the University and Business School. The offer will complement the new hotel and enhanced Manchester Business School Executive Education Centre, both of which are being developed as part of phase one, which was granted planning consent in April 2013 with construction due to commence later in 2014. In addition to the retail and leisure, phase two includes the removal of a link bridge across Oxford Road to improve views and light levels in the area. Public realm improvements such as pavement widening will enhance the area for pedestrians, cyclists and public transport users. The phase two redevelopment also includes the refurbishment of circa 200,000 sq ft of MBS facilities to create significantly improved teaching and administrative accommodation, a new Learning Library and Enterprise Zone.</p>

<p>In the previous 2001 <a href="/wiki/Research_Assessment_Exercise" title="Research Assessment Exercise">Research Assessment Exercise</a> (pre-merger) the 'old' Manchester Business School and Manchester School of Management were both rated 5. Manchester School of Accounting and Finance is one of only two accounting and finance schools in the UK rated as 6* by the RAE (i.e. with RAE 5* rating for two consecutive periods). According to a survey published by <i>Accounting and Finance</i> in 2008, MBS is ranked as the "world number one for accounting research".</p>
<p>Manchester has several research centres of international standing. These include those in the areas of decision sciences, employment and labour studies, technology management and innovation, and <a href="/wiki/Critical_management_studies" title="Critical management studies">critical management studies</a>. Among its Professors with World-ranked reputations are <a href="/wiki/Fiona_Devine" title="Fiona Devine">Fiona Devine</a> (Sociology), <a href="/wiki/Luke_Georghiou" title="Luke Georghiou">Luke Georghiou</a> (Policy), Ian Miles (Technological Innovation and Social Change), <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Dosi" title="Giovanni Dosi">Giovanni Dosi</a> (Innovation Economics), Paul Jackson (Corporate Communications), <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stiglitz" title="Joseph Stiglitz">Joseph Stiglitz</a> (Economics), Thomas Kirchmaier (Corporate Strategy/Governance), <a href="/wiki/Colin_Talbot" title="Colin Talbot">Colin Talbot</a> (Public Policy and Management), Jikyeong Kang (Marketing), John Hassard (Organization Studies), Mick Marchington (HRM), Jill Rubery FBA (Employment Studies), Trevor Wood-Harper (Information Systems), Peter Kawalek (Information Systems and Strategy), Maria Nedeva (Innovation Management), Andrew Stark (Finance), and Richard Whitley (Business Systems). Karel Williams is currently co-heading a major new centre for the research of social change called CRESC.</p>
