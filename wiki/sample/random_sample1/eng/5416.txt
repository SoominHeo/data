<p><b>Matthew Edward "Matt" Harvey</b> (born March 27, 1989) is an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB). Every time Harvey pitches, Mets fans refer to it as "Harvey Day".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Harvey previously played at <a href="/wiki/Fitch_Senior_High_School" title="Fitch Senior High School">Fitch Senior High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Groton,_Connecticut" title="Groton, Connecticut">Groton, Connecticut</a>. After being drafted in the third round of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2007 Major League Baseball draft">2007 MLB draft</a> by the [[Los Angeles Angels of He was drafted again in the <a href="/wiki/2010_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2010 Major League Baseball draft">2010 MLB draft</a> by the Mets as the seventh overall pick. In his major league debut on July 26, <a href="/wiki/2012_New_York_Mets_season" title="2012 New York Mets season">2012</a>, against the <a href="/wiki/2012_Arizona_Diamondbacks_season" title="2012 Arizona Diamondbacks season">Arizona Diamondbacks</a>, Harvey set a new club record with 11 <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">strikeouts</a> while earning his first career victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_Debut_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-MLB_Debut-2">[2]</a></sup> Harvey had a breakout season in 2013, being selected to play in his first <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="Major League Baseball All-Star Game">Major League Baseball All-Star Game</a>. Harvey then missed the entire 2014 season due to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tommy_John_surgery" title="Tommy John surgery">Tommy John surgery</a> but returned to top form in 2015, leading the Mets to the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Series" title="2015 World Series">pennant</a>.</p>
<p>After a 2013 magazine cover compared him to <a href="/wiki/Batman" title="Batman">Batman</a>, Harvey and his fans have embraced "<b>The Dark Knight</b>" as his nickname.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harvey was born in <a href="/wiki/New_London,_Connecticut" title="New London, Connecticut">New London, Connecticut</a>; he is the only son and youngest of three children of Ed and Jackie Harvey, both teachers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mitrosilis_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mitrosilis-3">[3]</a></sup> He is of Irish and Italian descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Harvey was raised in <a href="/wiki/Mystic,_Connecticut" title="Mystic, Connecticut">Mystic, Connecticut</a>, with his two older sisters, Jessica and Jocelyn. His father was a standout athlete at <a href="/wiki/Groton,_Connecticut" title="Groton, Connecticut">Groton, Connecticut</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fitch_Senior_High_School" title="Fitch Senior High School">Fitch Senior High School</a> and attended the <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Huskies" title="Connecticut Huskies">University of Connecticut</a> where he played both <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Huskies_baseball" title="Connecticut Huskies baseball">baseball</a> (as a centerfielder)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tumblr_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tumblr-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Huskies_football" title="Connecticut Huskies football">football</a>, even appearing in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1972_College_World_Series" title="1972 College World Series">1972 College World Series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mitrosilis_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-mitrosilis-3">[3]</a></sup> After UConn, he eventually returned to Groton to coach his former high school baseball team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maxpreps_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-maxpreps-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Harvey grew up as a <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> fan, especially admiring <a href="/wiki/Paul_O%27Neill_(baseball)" title="Paul O'Neill (baseball)">Paul O'Neill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Jeter" title="Derek Jeter">Derek Jeter</a>, whom he has described as a childhood idol of his.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heavy_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-heavy-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-childhoodteam_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-childhoodteam-8">[8]</a></sup> At the beginning of every elementary school year, when asked to write about his life goals, Harvey would write that he wanted to play professional baseball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mitrosilis_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-mitrosilis-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>At Fitch Senior High School, Harvey played both baseball (where he was coached by his dad) and <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>. He was teammates with future Major League pitcher <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Hahn" title="Jesse Hahn">Jesse Hahn</a> on both teams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hahn_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-hahn-9">[9]</a></sup> As a high school freshman, he was able on a few occasions to throw as fast as 90 miles per hour (140 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bodyissue_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-bodyissue-10">[10]</a></sup> Harvey also played summer baseball for numerous travel teams across the country, including the South Florida Bandits, the Midland Redskins, and the East Coast Grays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-perfectgame_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-perfectgame-11">[11]</a></sup> As a high school senior, he was selected as a <a href="/wiki/Rawlings_(company)" title="Rawlings (company)">Rawlings</a> First Team All-American and named to their Northeast All-Region First Team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-perfectgame_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-perfectgame-11">[11]</a></sup> He was grouped with <a href="/wiki/Madison_Bumgarner" title="Madison Bumgarner">Madison Bumgarner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rick_Porcello" title="Rick Porcello">Rick Porcello</a> as one of the best pitchers in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_MLB_draft" title="2007 MLB draft">2007 MLB draft</a> and a likely first round pick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mitrosilis_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-mitrosilis-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maxpreps_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-maxpreps-6">[6]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Baseball_America" title="Baseball America">Baseball America</a></i> ranked him the best high school prospect in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> However, he fell to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Angels" title="Los Angeles Angels">Los Angeles Angels</a> in the third round with the 118th overall pick. As the Angels offered only a $1 million signing bonus, Harvey took the advice of his advisors, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Caudill" title="Bill Caudill">Bill Caudill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scott_Boras" title="Scott Boras">Scott Boras</a>, and opted to sign with the <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Tar_Heels_baseball" title="North Carolina Tar Heels baseball">UNC Tar Heels</a> instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mitrosilis_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-mitrosilis-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: College career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harvey would go on to pitch three seasons at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina_at_Chapel_Hill" title="University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill">University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill</a>, where he majored in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sports_administration" title="Sports administration">sports administration</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mets_signing_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mets_signing-14">[14]</a></sup> During his collegiate career, Harvey went 22–7 with 3.73 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">earned run average</a> (ERA) in <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">238<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> <a href="/wiki/Innings_pitched" title="Innings pitched">innings pitched</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mets_signing_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mets_signing-14">[14]</a></sup> He ranks ninth all-time in UNC history in strikeouts (263) and 10th in wins (22).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mets_signing_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-Mets_signing-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Harvey spent the summers of ’08 and ’09 pitching for the Chatham Anglers of the Cape Cod Baseball League. Harvey was a key component of the Anglers’ 2008 bullpen, completing the season with a 0.83 ERA, the lowest on the team, pitching <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">21<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings and striking out 29 of 92 batters faced. Harvey returned to Chatham in 2009 after a difficult sophomore year on the mound. As Harvey explains, he had lost some of the mechanics and flexibility that had made him such a great pitcher throughout high school and the beginning of his college career. Although his ’09 summer was not as impressive as the previous one, his time on the Cape helped him return to the basics and set him on the road to becoming the seventh overall draft pick in the 2010 first year player draft. According to his pitching coach at UNC, Scott Forbes, Harvey returned from the 2009 Cape League season with "a more professional approach."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harvey was selected as the seventh overall pick by the New York Mets in the <a href="/wiki/2010_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2010 Major League Baseball draft">2010 Major League Baseball First-Year Player Draft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Harvey was listed at 6' 4" and 210 lbs, batting and throwing right-handed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor_leagues">Minor leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Minor leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, Harvey's first professional season in the Mets <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minor-league_baseball" title="Minor-league baseball">minor-league</a> system, he split time between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Single-A_(baseball)" title="Single-A (baseball)">single-A</a> <a href="/wiki/St._Lucie_Mets" title="St. Lucie Mets">St. Lucie Mets</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Double-A_(baseball)" title="Double-A (baseball)">Double-A</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Binghamton_Mets" title="Binghamton Mets">Binghamton Mets</a>. With St. Lucie in the <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_League" title="Florida State League">Florida State League</a> (FSL), he went 8–2 with a 2.37 ERA and recorded 92 strikeouts in 76 innings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MiLB.com_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-MiLB.com-18">[18]</a></sup> His performance garnered him two FSL Pitcher of the Week awards and he was selected as a FSL Mid-Season All-Star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MiLB.com_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-MiLB.com-18">[18]</a></sup> Although selected to appear in the FSL All-Star game, Harvey did not pitch because he was promoted to Double-A Binghamton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_League_(baseball)" title="Eastern League (baseball)">Eastern League</a> with Binghamton, he went 5–3 with a 4.53 ERA and 64 strikeouts in 59.2 innings. Harvey also pitched in the <a href="/wiki/All-Star_Futures_Game" title="All-Star Futures Game">2011 All-Star Futures Game</a>, recording a <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)" title="Save (baseball)">save</a> for the winning U.S. team over the World team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Harvey was ranked as the Mets organization's second best <a href="/wiki/Prospect_(sports)" title="Prospect (sports)">prospect</a> in 2012 and the 34th overall best prospect by MLB.com.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He was invited to <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a> by the Mets that year but did not make the team. Instead, he was promoted to the club's <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">Triple-A</a> affiliate, the <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Bisons" title="Buffalo Bisons">Buffalo Bisons</a> of the <a href="/wiki/International_League" title="International League">International League</a> (IL).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the first half of his 2012 season at Triple-A, Harvey went 7–4 with a 3.39 ERA in 18 starts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Promotion_Speculation_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Promotion_Speculation-23">[23]</a></sup> That performance earned him <a href="/wiki/International_League" title="International League">IL Mid-Season All-Star</a> honors. His strong pitching, plus injuries to major leaguers <a href="/wiki/Mike_Pelfrey" title="Mike Pelfrey">Mike Pelfrey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dillon_Gee" title="Dillon Gee">Dillon Gee</a>, put him in contention for the fifth spot in the Mets rotation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Promotion_Speculation_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Promotion_Speculation-23">[23]</a></sup> Despite spending more time pitching at Triple-A than other top draft picks — 105 innings, recording a 3.34 ERA and striking out over a batter per inning through mid-July<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> — the Mets front office (headed by general manager <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Alderson" title="Sandy Alderson">Sandy Alderson</a>) did not want to promote Harvey until his consistency and control were better.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_leagues">Major leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Major leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:DSC_0163_Matt_Harvey.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2220" data-file-width="1370" height="356" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/DSC_0163_Matt_Harvey.jpg/220px-DSC_0163_Matt_Harvey.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/DSC_0163_Matt_Harvey.jpg/330px-DSC_0163_Matt_Harvey.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/DSC_0163_Matt_Harvey.jpg/440px-DSC_0163_Matt_Harvey.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:DSC_0163_Matt_Harvey.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Harvey with the Mets in 2012</div>

<p>After an injury to <a href="/wiki/Ace_(baseball)" title="Ace (baseball)">staff ace</a> <a href="/wiki/Johan_Santana" title="Johan Santana">Johan Santana</a> and replacements to the Mets' major-league rotation failed to turn in quality starts, general manager Sandy Alderson and Mets manager <a href="/wiki/Terry_Collins" title="Terry Collins">Terry Collins</a> backtracked and decided to promote Harvey to the majors, ending his stay with the Bisons with a 7–5 record and 3.68 ERA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Harvey stayed in the rotation for the remainder of the season as the fifth starter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Harvey's debut, a July 26 start against the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a> at <a href="/wiki/Chase_Field" title="Chase Field">Chase Field</a> in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>, he pitched <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">5<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings, giving up three hits and three walks while recording 11 strikeouts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_Debut_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-MLB_Debut-2">[2]</a></sup> Harvey recorded his first major-league strikeout against the first batter he faced, <a href="/wiki/Gerardo_Parra" title="Gerardo Parra">Gerardo Parra</a> of the Diamondbacks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_Debut_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-MLB_Debut-2">[2]</a></sup> He then had his first major league hit, a two-out double off of <a href="/wiki/Wade_Miley" title="Wade Miley">Wade Miley</a> in the top of the following inning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_Debut_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-MLB_Debut-2">[2]</a></sup> Harvey set a Mets franchise record for strikeouts in a pitching debut (11) and became the first player in modern baseball history (since 1900) to strike out 10 or more batters and get two hits in his major-league debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_Debut_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-MLB_Debut-2">[2]</a></sup> After the game, Mets manager Terry Collins said:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_Debut_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-MLB_Debut-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p><i>"I haven't seen 98 out of a starting pitcher in quite some time. He's lived up to exactly what everybody's talked about."</i></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In his second major-league start, against the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>, Harvey pitched six innings, gave up two earned runs, three walks and struck out seven in his first loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harvey_Impressive_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harvey_Impressive-28">[28]</a></sup> His 18 total strikeouts were a Mets record for a rookie over his first two games of his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harvey_Impressive_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-Harvey_Impressive-28">[28]</a></sup> After three straight losses, Harvey was able to earn his second win against the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a> on August 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In his next two starts, Harvey got a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Decision_(baseball)" title="Decision (baseball)">no-decision</a> and a win against the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Rockies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Phillies</a>, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Both were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quality_starts" title="Quality starts">quality starts</a> and he struck out 15 combined in the games. Over his first 15 at-bats in seven starts, he posted impressive batting numbers with a .462 average, two <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">doubles</a> and three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Runs_batted_in" title="Runs batted in">runs batted in</a> (RBIs).</p>
<p>Harvey then went on to lose his next two starts and record a no-decision in his last outing of the season on September 19. Despite more opportunities to pitch, Mets management ended his season due to an innings-pitched limit. He finished his inaugural season with a 3–5 record, a 2.73 ERA over 10 starts in which he pitched <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">59<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings and recorded 70 strikeouts. He surrendered 42 hits and 26 walks.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Harveyallstar.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3264" data-file-width="4928" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Harveyallstar.jpg/220px-Harveyallstar.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Harveyallstar.jpg/330px-Harveyallstar.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Harveyallstar.jpg/440px-Harveyallstar.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Harveyallstar.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Harvey pitching in the <a href="/wiki/2013_Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="2013 Major League Baseball All-Star Game">2013 Major League Baseball All-Star Game</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Harvey continued to garner accolades for his arm strength and control in 2013. New York sports radio host <a href="/wiki/Mike_Francesa" title="Mike Francesa">Mike Francesa</a> has compared Harvey to standouts like <a href="/wiki/Justin_Verlander" title="Justin Verlander">Justin Verlander</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Pettitte" title="Andy Pettitte">Andy Pettitte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Curt_Schilling" title="Curt Schilling">Curt Schilling</a>. After watching Harvey's first two starts of the 2013 season, during which he struck out 19 in 14 innings, former Mets manager <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Valentine" title="Bobby Valentine">Bobby Valentine</a> said Harvey had the potential to be "the best Met pitcher to ever wear the uniform." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> His April performance garnered him <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pitcher_of_the_Month" title="Pitcher of the Month">Pitcher of the Month</a> honors after he posted a 1.56 ERA with 46 strikeouts in 40.1 innings. Opposing batters hit .153 against him. <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Gooden" title="Dwight Gooden">Dwight Gooden</a> gave him the nickname The Real Deal after he saw him pitch live.</p>
<p>On April 13, Harvey had a <a href="/wiki/No-hitter" title="No-hitter">no-hit</a> bid through 6 2/3 innings against the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a> until <a href="/wiki/Justin_Morneau" title="Justin Morneau">Justin Morneau</a> hit a solo home run in the bottom of the seventh inning. On May 7, while pitching with a severe <a href="/wiki/Nosebleed" title="Nosebleed">nosebleed</a> Harvey retired the first 20 <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a> batters he faced until <a href="/wiki/Alex_R%C3%ADos" title="Alex Ríos">Alex Ríos</a> broke up the <a href="/wiki/Perfect_game" title="Perfect game">perfect game</a> with an infield single. Harvey left the game after nine innings, having surrendered only the one hit, as the Mets won in 10 innings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He was subsequently featured on the cover of the May 20, 2013 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Sports_Illustrated" title="Sports Illustrated">Sports Illustrated</a></i> magazine, dubbed "The Dark Knight of Gotham."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foxknight_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-foxknight-34">[34]</a></sup> On June 18, Harvey took another no-hitter into the seventh inning, but was stymied by an infield single off the bat of the Atlanta Braves' Jason Heyward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-june182013_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-june182013-35">[35]</a></sup> Harvey notched a career-high 13 strikeouts in the game, giving up three hits over seven innings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-june182013_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-june182013-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the mid-season <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MLB_All-Star_Game" title="MLB All-Star Game">All-Star</a> break approached, team management talked about limiting Harvey's innings to ensure his pitching health. Harvey had thrown 117 innings in 17 starts at the time, which put him on a season-long pace for 240–250 innings. Mets manager <a href="/wiki/Terry_Collins" title="Terry Collins">Terry Collins</a> said Harvey would not be allowed to pitch more than 215–220 innings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>Harvey was the starting pitcher for the <a href="/wiki/2013_Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="2013 Major League Baseball All-Star Game">2013 MLB All-Star Game</a>, which took place at the Mets' home ballpark, <a href="/wiki/Citi_Field" title="Citi Field">Citi Field</a> in which he pitched the first two innings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> On August 7, Harvey pitched his first career <a href="/wiki/Complete_game" title="Complete game">complete game</a> <a href="/wiki/Shutouts_in_baseball" title="Shutouts in baseball">shutout</a>, giving up four hits and striking out six in a 5–0 win over the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> On August 26, Harvey was diagnosed with a partial tear of the <a href="/wiki/Ulnar_collateral_ligament_of_elbow_joint" title="Ulnar collateral ligament of elbow joint">ulnar collateral ligament</a> in his right elbow and was placed on the disabled list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> He had logged 178 1/3 innings at that point.</p>
<p>On September 17, Harvey said he would try <a href="/wiki/Physical_therapy" title="Physical therapy">rehab</a> before opting for surgery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> But the Mets announced on October 4 that Harvey would have <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tommy_John_surgery" title="Tommy John surgery">Tommy John surgery</a> to repair his right elbow. Because of the procedure, Harvey was expected to miss the entire 2014 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Harvey finished this past season 9–5 with a 2.27 ERA in 26 starts with 191 strikeouts in 178 1/3 innings. It was later announced that Harvey had finished tied for 4th in the <a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">Cy Young Award</a>, losing to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Clayton_Kershaw" title="Clayton Kershaw">Clayton Kershaw</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On October 22, 2013, Harvey underwent successful Tommy John surgery to replace the partially torn ulnar collateral ligament in his right elbow. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._James_Andrews" title="Dr. James Andrews">Dr. James Andrews</a> performed the operation in <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Breeze,_Florida" title="Gulf Breeze, Florida">Gulf Breeze, Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Exactly 4 months after his Tommy John surgery Harvey was throwing a baseball for the first time since his injury occurred. He threw 20 times at a distance of 60 feet at the Mets spring training site <a href="/wiki/Port_St._Lucie,_Florida" title="Port St. Lucie, Florida">Port St. Lucie, Florida</a>, with the Mets goal being that he would be ready for the start of the 2015 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March, Harvey dismissed the idea that he would not pitch until the start of the 2015 season by posting on Twitter that "2014 Harvey Day will happen".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> He also told reporters around the same time that he was looking to return around September 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> But in June of that same year the Mets officially declared that Harvey would not be pitching until the beginning of the 2015 season. Harvey had been rehabbing at such an accelerated pace that they thought it would be better to take a more cautious approach. After talking with Mets doctors, <a href="/wiki/General_manager" title="General manager">general manager</a> <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Alderson" title="Sandy Alderson">Sandy Alderson</a> decided that slowing Harvey's path would be best. Another factor was that the Mets at the time were out of playoff contention, and him pitching in meaningless games was not worth the risk of being injured again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Harvey was elected the team's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MLB_Players_Association" title="MLB Players Association">MLB Players Association</a> representative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>Without Harvey for the 2014 season, the Mets finished with a record of 79–83 (second in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NL_East" title="NL East">NL East</a>) with the pitching staff having an ERA of 3.49, good for 6th in the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>On April 9, Harvey returned from surgery, and allowed no runs in six innings while striking out nine against the Washington Nationals. After the game, Nationals outfielder <a href="/wiki/Bryce_Harper" title="Bryce Harper">Bryce Harper</a> said: "He's going to be a <a href="/wiki/Cy_Young_Award" title="Cy Young Award">Cy Young</a> one day and everybody knows that. He's one of the toughest at-bats I've ever had."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> On May 4, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baseball_Hall_of_Fame" title="Baseball Hall of Fame">Hall of Fame</a> pitcher and former Mets ace <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Pedro Martínez">Pedro Martínez</a> said that he believed that Harvey could have a better career than his own. He added, "I think he has more talent than I do."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 18, Harvey pitched 8 innings, giving up no runs against the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a> while striking out 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> On May 23, Harvey had the worst start of his career, surrendering 7 runs to the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> in four innings. Due to this poor outing, Harvey's ERA jumped from 1.98 to 2.91.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> On July 11, Harvey hit his first career home run against the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a>' <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Corbin" title="Patrick Corbin">Patrick Corbin</a>. Harvey also pitched 7 innings, striking out 9, and got his 8th win on the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup></p>

<p>In September, Harvey's agent, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Boras" title="Scott Boras">Scott Boras</a>, publicly expressed concern with the Mets' stated plans to allow Harvey to pitch around 190 innings in the regular season, and also pitch "a reasonable amount" in the postseason. Boras suggested that better medical advice, allegedly given by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dr._James_Andrews" title="Dr. James Andrews">Dr. James Andrews</a>, was to cap the innings at 180, and no postseason activity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fanrage_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-fanrage-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Harvey initially appeared to agree with Boras,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fanrage_55-1"><a href="#cite_note-fanrage-55">[55]</a></sup> in contrast with his cultivated image of toughness and desire to compete and win at all costs, including having previously objected to efforts by the Mets to both proceed cautiously in his recovery with respect to the timetable for his return (in 2014 Harvey expressed a desire to come back from the injury early, while the Mets followed a conventional recovery timetable<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup>), and curtail his innings in 2015 by employing a six-man rotation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> After backlash against Harvey's initial comments from Mets fans and the media,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fanrage_55-2"><a href="#cite_note-fanrage-55">[55]</a></sup> Harvey wrote in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Players%27_Tribune" title="The Players' Tribune">The Players' Tribune</a></i> that the innings limit only applied to the regular season and that he would pitch in the playoffs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 12, 2015, Harvey pitched against the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> in Game 3 of the <a href="/wiki/2015_National_League_Division_Series" title="2015 National League Division Series">NLDS</a>. Though he struggled a bit he was still able to get the win, he gave up 3 runs (2 earned), 7 hits &amp; 2 walks, he also struck out 7 in the Mets' 13-7 win.</p>
<p>Harvey pitched well in Game 1 of the <a href="/wiki/2015_National_League_Championship_Series" title="2015 National League Championship Series">National League Championship Series</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a>. He started Game 1 of the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Series" title="2015 World Series">2015 World Series</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a>, giving up 3 earned runs through 6 innings in a no-decision. Kansas City won the game, 5-4 in 14 innings, to take the series lead.</p>
<p>In Game 5 of the World Series against the Royals, Harvey appeared to be on his way to one of the greatest pitching performances in MLB Postseason history. Harvey entered the mound with a 2-0 lead in the top of the ninth inning per his own request, to try and finish the game despite having thrown over 100 pitches. After allowing a leadoff walk, followed by an RBI double, Harvey was pulled. The game-tying run eventually scored later in the inning on a throwing error, resulting in a no-decision for Harvey in a game the Mets would eventually go on to lose in 12 innings. This loss made Kansas City the 2015 World Series Champions.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2016">2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After making the Mets' opening day roster, Harvey began the 2016 regular season on a sour note, losing his first two starts before gaining traction. During his first start on Opening Day against the reigning World Series champions, the Kansas City Royals he allowed 4 runs (3 earned) and did not seem his typical self, earning a few amount of strikeouts and surrendering a large amount of hits. After observing Harvey pitch, it seemed that his typical velocity and command had disappeared while striking out few batters. Throughout the beginning of the season, Harvey seemed to be improving minimally and inconsistently. However, Harvey had a few quality starts, including one where he went 7 complete innings while striking out 7 against the Chicago White Sox, and one against the San Diego Padres where Harvey struck out a season high 10 and surrendered only 2 runs throughout 6 innings. However Harvey was not pitching as successfully as he was his previous season. Mets pitching coach <a href="/wiki/Dan_Warthen" title="Dan Warthen">Dan Warthen</a> said early in the season that Harvey had been struggling with a mechanical problem with his slider during his first two starts.</p>

<p>Throughout the 2016 season it was believed Harvey was struggling mechanically, but it turned out he, after beginning the year 4–10, was placed on the 15-day disabled list with shoulder pain. Harvey later opted to have season-ending surgery to resolve a condition called <a href="/wiki/Thoracic_outlet_syndrome" title="Thoracic outlet syndrome">thoracic outlet syndrome</a>. Matt Harvey commented on his season-ending surgery, "I'm disappointed in the way I pitched, and hope this cures me and we get back on track."</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pitching_style">Pitching style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Pitching style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harvey is a power pitcher with a 6-foot-4-inch (1.93 m), 225-pound (102 kg) frame that is suitable for heavy workloads and can generate great arm speed. He has a four-pitch repertoire that includes a fastball, slider, curveball and change-up.</p>
<p>He throws his <a href="/wiki/Fastball" title="Fastball">fastball</a> in both four-seam and two-seam varieties, where it is consistently in the range of 93–96 miles per hour (150–154 km/h) (tops out at 98 miles per hour (158 km/h)) with movement; it is considered a <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_baseball_(P)#plus_pitch" title="Glossary of baseball (P)">plus pitch</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLB_scouting_report_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-MLB_scouting_report-63">[63]</a></sup> Harvey usually relies on his <a href="/wiki/Slider" title="Slider">slider</a>, which comes in at 88–92 miles per hour (142–148 km/h) and at times is a plus pitch. It gets good rotation and tilts when thrown well, but will occasionally flatten out. In high school, Harvey's <a href="/wiki/Curveball" title="Curveball">curveball</a> was an overhand power curve that had plus potential, but he doesn't throw it frequently in the majors because he favors the slider. Harvey's fourth option is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Change-up" title="Change-up">change-up</a>, which he commands well in the range of 86–88 miles per hour (138–142 km/h). His change-up is considered an average pitch.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harvey is a passionate <a href="/wiki/New_York_Rangers" title="New York Rangers">New York Rangers</a> fan and frequently attends their games at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a>, sometimes with teammate <a href="/wiki/Jon_Niese" title="Jon Niese">Jon Niese</a>. He has been described as perhaps their most prominent and identifiable fan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> However, his experience as a hockey player is limited to casual <a href="/wiki/Roller_hockey" title="Roller hockey">roller hockey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> at a cousin's backyard rink in <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> He is also a fan of the <a href="/wiki/New_England_Patriots" title="New England Patriots">New England Patriots</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heavy_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-heavy-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Harvey has a high profile in the media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-media_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-media-66">[66]</a></sup> He is known to live a flashy lifestyle, drives an expensive <a href="/wiki/Maserati" title="Maserati">Maserati</a> sports car,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tumblr_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-tumblr-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> has been described as a "lothario" due to his record of dating fashion models, and is frequently mentioned in celebrity <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gossip_columns" title="Gossip columns">gossip columns</a> in New York media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-media_66-1"><a href="#cite_note-media-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ania_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-ania-68">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enjoy_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-enjoy-69">[69]</a></sup> In May 2013, Matt Harvey began dating model <a href="/wiki/Anne_Vyalitsyna" title="Anne Vyalitsyna">Anne Vyalitsyna</a> after meeting her at a New York Rangers game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> They broke up in February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> In 2015, he began dating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish_people" title="Polish people">Polish</a> model Ania Cywinska.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ania_68-1"><a href="#cite_note-ania-68">[68]</a></sup> He also appeared nude in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Body_Issue" title="The Body Issue">The Body Issue</a></i> of <i><a href="/wiki/ESPN_The_Magazine" title="ESPN The Magazine">ESPN The Magazine</a></i> in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bodyissue_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-bodyissue-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Harvey appeared on the cover of the May 20, 2013 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Sports_Illustrated" title="Sports Illustrated">Sports Illustrated</a></i> magazine, dubbed "The <a href="/wiki/Dark_Knight_(nickname)" title="Dark Knight (nickname)">Dark Knight</a> of Gotham,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-foxknight_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-foxknight-34">[34]</a></sup> a play on <a href="/wiki/Batman" title="Batman">Batman</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Gotham_City" title="Gotham City">home city</a>, its association with New York City and the recent <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_Trilogy" title="The Dark Knight Trilogy">Dark Knight</a></i> film trilogy. Harvey, who had been a Batman fan since childhood,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> as well as teammates, fans, the Mets and other media sources immediately embraced the comparison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enjoy_69-1"><a href="#cite_note-enjoy-69">[69]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> Harvey even had "Dark Knight" carved into the knobs of his <a href="/wiki/Baseball_bat" title="Baseball bat">bats</a> to begin the 2015 season before replacing it with a personalized hybrid Harvey-Batman logo, versions of which had previously appeared on his locker and on T-shirts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> Early in the 2013 season, fans, media and teammates also began referring to any day on which Harvey is scheduled to start as "Harvey Day."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
