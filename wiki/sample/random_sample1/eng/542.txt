<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2065">2065</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2060s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2065">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>November 11 – <a href="/wiki/Transit_of_Mercury" title="Transit of Mercury">Transit of Mercury</a></li>
<li>November 22 12:45 UTC — <a href="/wiki/Venus" title="Venus">Venus</a> will <a href="/wiki/Occultation" title="Occultation">occult</a> <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a>. It will be very difficult to observe from <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>, because the elongation of Venus and Jupiter from the Sun at this time will be only 7 degrees. This event will be the first occultation of a planet by another since January 3, 1818; however the next will occur less than two years later, on July 15, 2067.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2067">2067</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2060s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2067">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>February 15 – Assuming no further extensions to the <a href="/wiki/Copyright_law_of_the_United_States#Duration_of_copyright" title="Copyright law of the United States">term of copyrights</a> become law in the interim, all <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sound_recordings" title="Sound recordings">sound recordings</a> fixed before February 15, 1972 will enter the public domain in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>July 15 11:56 UTC — <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(planet)" title="Mercury (planet)">Mercury</a> will occult <a href="/wiki/Neptune" title="Neptune">Neptune</a>. This rare event will be very difficult to observe from Earth.</li>
<li>October – A <a href="/wiki/Active_SETI" title="Active SETI">METI</a> message <a href="/wiki/Cosmic_Call" title="Cosmic Call">Cosmic Call 1</a> sent from the 70-meter <a href="/wiki/Yevpatoria" title="Yevpatoria">Eupatoria</a> Planetary Radar arrives at its destination, star <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HD_178428&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HD 178428 (page does not exist)">HD 178428</a>.</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2060_3">2060</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2060s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2060">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
