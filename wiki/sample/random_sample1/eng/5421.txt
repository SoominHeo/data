<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Margaret Island</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<p><b>Margaret Island</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <span lang="hu" xml:lang="hu"><i>Margit-sziget</i></span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Hungarian" title="Help:IPA for Hungarian">[ˈmɒrɡit ˈsigɛt]</a></span>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Margareteninsel</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i>Kızadası</i></span>) is a 2.5 km (1.6 mi) long island, 500 metres (550 yards) wide, (0.965 km<sup>2</sup> (238 acres) in area) in the middle of the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> in central <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. The island is mostly covered by landscape parks, and is a popular recreational area. Its medieval ruins are reminders of its importance in the Middle Ages as a religious centre. The island spans the area between the <i><a href="/wiki/Margaret_Bridge" title="Margaret Bridge">Margaret Bridge</a></i> (south) and the <i><a href="/wiki/%C3%81rp%C3%A1d_Bridge" title="Árpád Bridge">Árpád Bridge</a></i> (north). Before the 14th century the island was called <i>Insula leporum</i> (Island of Rabbits). Administratively Margaret Island used to belong to the 13th district until 2013. Now it is directly under the control of the city.</p>
<p>Today's appearance was developed through the connection of three separate islands, the Festő (<i>Painter</i>), the Fürdő (<i>Bath</i>) and the Nyulak (<i>Rabbits</i>), during the end of the 19th century, to control the flow of the Danube. Originally, the island was 102.5 metres above sea level, but now has been built up to 104.85 metres above sea level to control flooding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Margaret_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island was called Insula leporum before being named after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Margaret_of_Hungary" title="Saint Margaret of Hungary">Saint Margaret</a> (1242–1270) in the 14th century. Margaret was the daughter of <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9la_IV_of_Hungary" title="Béla IV of Hungary">Béla IV of Hungary</a>, and she lived in the Dominican convent on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WDL1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-WDL1-2">[2]</a></sup> Other names of the island were during 13th century: <i>Nyulak szigete</i>, <i>Nagyboldogasszony-sziget</i>, <i>Úr-sziget</i>, <i>Budai-sziget</i>, <i>Dunai-sziget</i>, during the 1790s: <i>Nádor-sziget</i>, <i>Palatinus-sziget</i>. (The names mean Island of Rabbits, Island of Our Lady, Island of Nobles, Buda Island, Danube Island, Palatine Island.)</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Margaret_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">Knights of St. John</a> settled on the island in the 12th century. Among the present historical monuments of the island are the 13th century ruins of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franciscan" title="Franciscan">Franciscan</a> church and a <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Order" title="Dominican Order">Dominican</a> church and convent, as well as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Premonstratensian" title="Premonstratensian">Premonstratensian</a> church from the 12th century. Members of the <a href="/wiki/Augustinians" title="Augustinians">Augustinian</a> order also lived on the island.</p>
<p>The island was dominated by nunneries, churches and cloisters until the 16th century. During the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> wars the monks and nuns fled and the buildings were destroyed. In the 18th century it was chosen to be the resort of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palatine_(Kingdom_of_Hungary)" title="Palatine (Kingdom of Hungary)">palatines</a>. It was declared a public garden in 1908.</p>

<p>Since the 1980s, entry by cars has been limited; only a single bus line and taxis, alongside the service traffic of local stores and restaurants are allowed to enter. On the northern end of the island a car park houses the cars of hotel guests.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_and_recreation">Sports and recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Margaret_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sports and recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island houses various sports establishments, like the Palatinus <a href="/wiki/Water_park" title="Water park">water park</a> (the largest open-air swimming complex in Budapest), the <a href="/wiki/Alfr%C3%A9d_Haj%C3%B3s" title="Alfréd Hajós">Alfréd Hajós</a> sports pool (where the <a href="/wiki/1958_European_Aquatics_Championships" title="1958 European Aquatics Championships">European LC Championships 1958</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2006_European_Championships_in_Aquatics" title="2006 European Championships in Aquatics">2006</a> and <a href="/wiki/2010_European_Aquatics_Championships" title="2010 European Aquatics Championships">2010</a> took place), a tennis stadium and an athletics centre.</p>
<p>The island has a rubber-coated running track measuring <b>5.35</b> kilometers (3.33 miles), and is popular among both locals and tourists because of its superb quality and easy access.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Margaret_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sights in the island include:</p>
<ul>
<li>the Centennial Memorial of 1973, commemorating the hundredth anniversary of the city's unification;</li>
<li>a small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Garden" title="Japanese Garden">Japanese Garden</a> with a mildly thermal fish pond;</li>
<li>a tiny zoo featuring a wide range of exotic <a href="/wiki/Waterfowl" title="Waterfowl">waterfowl</a> among other animals;</li>
<li>the "Music Well" (<i>Zenélő kút</i>), a small pavilion, which was originally built for open-air concerts (it is close to Árpád bridge);</li>
<li>the "Music Fountain" (<i>Zenélő szökőkút</i>), a fountain near which music is played and light shows are performed in summer (it is close to Margaret bridge). The water springs out according to music, so that the fountain seems to <i>dance</i> at the various classical themes reproduced. The last piece played is <i>Con te partirò</i> sung by Andrea Bocelli;</li>
<li>an octagonal Water Tower of 57 m (built in <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a> style in 1911, today functioning as a lookout tower and an exhibition hall, see picture).</li>
</ul>
<p>The Music Fountain and the Water Tower are protected <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Educational,_Scientific_and_Cultural_Organization" title="United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization">UNESCO</a> sites.</p>
<p>Two hotels provide accommodation: the <a href="/wiki/Fin_de_si%C3%A8cle" title="Fin de siècle">fin de siècle</a> Grand Hotel Margitsziget, and the modern Thermal Hotel Margitsziget with thermal spa and various medical services. There is also an open-air theatre accommodating an audience of 3500, and several clubs and restaurants. For exploration and pastime, four-person cycle cars or small electric cars can be rented for use on the area of the island.</p>
