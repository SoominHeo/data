<p><b>Murray Jonathan Gold</b> (born 28 February 1969) is a five-time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> nominated English <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a> for stage, film, and television and a dramatist for both theatre and radio. He is best known as the sole composer of all music for the 2005 revival of British TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span></h2>
<p>Gold has been nominated for a BAFTA five times in the category Best Original Television Music, for <i>Vanity Fair</i> (1999), <i><a href="/wiki/Queer_as_Folk_(UK_TV_series)" title="Queer as Folk (UK TV series)">Queer as Folk</a></i> (2000), <i><a href="/wiki/Casanova_(2005_TV_serial)" title="Casanova (2005 TV serial)">Casanova</a></i> (2006) and twice for <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i> (2009 &amp; 2014). His score for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> winning film <i>Kiss of Life</i> was awarded the 'Mozart Prize of the 7th Art' by a French jury at Aubagne in 2003. He has also been nominated four times by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Television_Society" title="Royal Television Society">Royal Television Society</a> in categories relating to music for television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manners_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Manners-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He has worked with <a href="/wiki/Russell_T_Davies" title="Russell T Davies">Russell T Davies</a>, the former writer and executive producer of <i>Doctor Who</i>, many times in the past on projects such as <i><a href="/wiki/Casanova_(2005_TV_serial)" title="Casanova (2005 TV serial)">Casanova</a></i> (starring <a href="/wiki/David_Tennant" title="David Tennant">David Tennant</a>), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Second_Coming_(TV_serial)" title="The Second Coming (TV serial)">The Second Coming</a></i> (starring <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Eccleston" title="Christopher Eccleston">Christopher Eccleston</a>) and <i>Queer as Folk</i> 1 &amp; 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manners_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Manners-3">[3]</a></sup> He has also provided the incidental music for the 2000s version of <i><a href="/wiki/Randall_%26_Hopkirk_(Deceased)_(2000_TV_series)" title="Randall &amp; Hopkirk (Deceased) (2000 TV series)">Randall &amp; Hopkirk (Deceased)</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> composer <a href="/wiki/David_Arnold" title="David Arnold">David Arnold</a>, who provided the theme tune.</p>
<p>He wrote the theme tune for the <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Shameless_(UK_TV_series)" title="Shameless (UK TV series)">Shameless</a></i> and scored the period drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Devil%27s_Whore" title="The Devil's Whore">The Devil's Whore</a></i>. More recently Gold scored another <a href="/wiki/David_Tennant" title="David Tennant">David Tennant</a> series, in BBC1's <i><a href="/wiki/Single_Father_(TV_series)" title="Single Father (TV series)">Single Father</a></i>. In this, Gold opted for a more popular music style ensemble rather than writing for orchestra.</p>
<p>Since 2012 Murray Gold has composed the musical score for the drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Tango_in_Halifax" title="Last Tango in Halifax">Last Tango in Halifax</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OrdinaryFellow_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-OrdinaryFellow-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2014, Gold scored the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Musketeers" title="The Musketeers">The Musketeers</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Doctor_Who_and_related_series"><i>Doctor Who</i> and related series</span></h3>
<p>Since 2005, Gold has served as musical director for the new series of <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>. In this capacity, he created a new arrangement of the show's theme (originally composed by <a href="/wiki/Ron_Grainer" title="Ron Grainer">Ron Grainer</a>) and also composes the show's incidental music. Silva Screen released a compilation of Gold's <i>Doctor Who</i> incidental music from the <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_(series_1)" title="Doctor Who (series 1)">first</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_(series_2)" title="Doctor Who (series 2)">second</a> series, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who:_Original_Television_Soundtrack" title="Doctor Who: Original Television Soundtrack">Doctor Who: Original Television Soundtrack</a></i>, on 11 December 2006. A second CD, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_Who:_Original_Television_Soundtrack_%E2%80%93_Series_3" title="Doctor Who: Original Television Soundtrack – Series 3">Doctor Who: Original Television Soundtrack – Series 3</a></i>, was released on 5 November 2007 and a third, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_Who:_Original_Television_Soundtrack_%E2%80%93_Series_4" title="Doctor Who: Original Television Soundtrack – Series 4">Doctor Who: Original Television Soundtrack – Series 4</a></i>, was released in November 2008. He has also been seen very briefly in the show itself, making a cameo appearance (and wearing a false moustache) in the 2007 Christmas special "<a href="/wiki/Voyage_of_the_Damned_(Doctor_Who)" title="Voyage of the Damned (Doctor Who)">Voyage of the Damned</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DWFactFile_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-DWFactFile-5">[5]</a></sup> Also, music from the 2008–2010 specials was released on 4 October 2010, entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_Who:_Original_Television_Soundtrack_%E2%80%93_Series_4:_The_Specials" title="Doctor Who: Original Television Soundtrack – Series 4: The Specials">Doctor Who: Original Television Soundtrack – Series 4: The Specials</a></i>, and on 8 November music from Series 5, entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_Who:_Original_Television_Soundtrack_%E2%80%93_Series_5" title="Doctor Who: Original Television Soundtrack – Series 5">Doctor Who: Original Television Soundtrack – Series 5</a></i>, was released.</p>
<p>Gold's initial arrangement of the <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_theme_music" title="Doctor Who theme music"><i>Doctor Who</i> theme</a> did not include "<a href="/wiki/Bridge_(music)" title="Bridge (music)">middle eight</a>" portion originally used in the theme, although he later reinstated it for a rearrangement of the theme introduced in the series' 2005 <a href="/wiki/The_Christmas_Invasion" title="The Christmas Invasion">Christmas episode</a> and subsequently used in the 2006 series of the programme.</p>
<p>Gold has created many themes to be associated with various elements of the show, creating two themes for The Doctor ("The Doctor's Theme" and "The Doctor Forever"), <a href="/wiki/Rose_Tyler" title="Rose Tyler">Rose Tyler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martha_Jones" title="Martha Jones">Martha Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donna_Noble" title="Donna Noble">Donna Noble</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gallifrey" title="Gallifrey">Gallifrey</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_(Doctor_Who)" title="Master (Doctor Who)">The Master</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astrid_Peth" title="Astrid Peth">Astrid Peth</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cybermen" title="Cybermen">Cybermen</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daleks" title="Daleks">Daleks</a>.</p>
<p>Gold re-arranged the <i>Doctor Who</i> opening theme in 2010 for Series 5. With the 2010 series, Gold also created two new musical identities for the <a href="/wiki/Eleventh_Doctor" title="Eleventh Doctor">Eleventh Doctor</a> ("I Am The Doctor" and "A Madman With A Box," replacing themes previously associated with the Ninth and Tenth Doctors), a theme for <a href="/wiki/Amy_Pond" title="Amy Pond">Amy Pond</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silurians" title="Silurians">Silurians</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daleks" title="Daleks">Daleks</a>. He also continued to use the theme for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cybermen" title="Cybermen">Cybermen</a>, as well as several action cues such as "Corridors and Fire Escapes" and "All the Strange, Strange Creatures."</p>
<p>Although his music for the 2005 series of <i>Doctor Who</i> relied largely on sampled sounds, his later arrangements for the show, beginning with "<a href="/wiki/The_Christmas_Invasion" title="The Christmas Invasion">The Christmas Invasion</a>," have been more orchestral, often being recorded by the BBC National Orchestra of Wales, supplemented by vocal performances with <a href="/wiki/Melanie_Pappenheim" title="Melanie Pappenheim">Melanie Pappenheim</a> and others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inside_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Inside-6">[6]</a></sup> One of the most well-known orchestral numbers is 'Abigail's Song', sung by Katherine Jenkins, from the 2010 Christmas special <a href="/wiki/A_Christmas_Carol_(Doctor_Who)" title="A Christmas Carol (Doctor Who)">'A Christmas Carol'</a>, whose soundtrack was released in March 2011. The orchestral scoring (partly reflecting a larger budget) contrasts strongly with music for the classic 1963-1989 series of <i>Doctor Who</i>, as produced by the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radiophonic_Workshop" title="BBC Radiophonic Workshop">BBC Radiophonic Workshop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Ayres" title="Mark Ayres">Mark Ayres</a> <i>et al.</i>, which generally had an electronic feel, with innovative instrumentation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Machina_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Machina-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gold also wrote the theme tunes for <i>Doctor Who</i> spin-offs <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sarah_Jane_Adventures" title="The Sarah Jane Adventures">The Sarah Jane Adventures</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Torchwood" title="Torchwood">Torchwood</a></i>, and composes music for the latter series alongside <a href="/wiki/Ben_Foster_(orchestrator)" title="Ben Foster (orchestrator)">Ben Foster</a>. A selection of their compositions, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Torchwood:_Original_Television_Soundtrack" title="Torchwood: Original Television Soundtrack">Torchwood: Original Television Soundtrack</a></i>, was made available during August 2008. He arranged the theme tunes to <i><a href="/wiki/Totally_Doctor_Who" title="Totally Doctor Who">Totally Doctor Who</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_Confidential" title="Doctor Who Confidential">Doctor Who Confidential</a></i>, both of which are variations on the <i>Doctor Who</i> theme.</p>
<p>Gold has created, arranged and orchestrated three special live concerts for the music from <i>Doctor Who</i>. The first, "<a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who:_A_Celebration" title="Doctor Who: A Celebration">Doctor Who: A Celebration</a>", was played at the <a href="/wiki/Wales_Millennium_Centre" title="Wales Millennium Centre">Millennium Centre</a> in Cardiff in 2006; the second, the <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_Prom_(2008)" title="Doctor Who Prom (2008)">2008 Doctor Who Prom</a>, was part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_Proms" title="BBC Proms">BBC Proms</a> on 24 July 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> the third, the <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who_Prom_(2010)" title="Doctor Who Prom (2010)">2010 Doctor Who Prom</a>, was part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_Proms" title="BBC Proms">BBC Proms</a> hold on 24 and 25 July 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2010, his Doctor Who soundtrack entered UK radio station <a href="/wiki/Classic_FM_(UK)" title="Classic FM (UK)">Classic FM's</a> Hall of Fame as that year's second highest new entry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2011, it remained in the Hall of Fame, but three places lower at number 228 out of 300.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film.2C_stage_and_radio">Film, stage and radio</span></h2>
<p>Gold has scored a number of British and American films, including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a>-winning <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_of_Life_(film)" title="Kiss of Life (film)">Kiss of Life</a></i> directed by Emily Young, <i><a href="/wiki/Death_at_a_Funeral_(2007_film)" title="Death at a Funeral (2007 film)">Death at a Funeral</a></i> directed by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Oz" title="Frank Oz">Frank Oz</a> and <i>Mischief Night</i>, directed by Penny Woolcock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manners_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Manners-3">[3]</a></sup> Other projects include <a href="/wiki/Ant_%26_Dec" title="Ant &amp; Dec">Ant &amp; Dec</a>'s 2006 film <i><a href="/wiki/Alien_Autopsy_(film)" title="Alien Autopsy (film)">Alien Autopsy</a></i> and 2009 drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Veronika_Decides_to_Die_(film)" title="Veronika Decides to Die (film)">Veronika Decides to Die</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2001, his radio play <i>Electricity</i> was given the Imison Award<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>—named after former BBC radio drama script editor <a href="/wiki/Richard_Imison" title="Richard Imison">Richard Imison</a>—for best new play after its broadcast on Radio 3 in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It subsequently transferred to the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Playhouse" title="West Yorkshire Playhouse">West Yorkshire Playhouse</a> in 2004 and was performed with <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Eccleston" title="Christopher Eccleston">Christopher Eccleston</a> in the lead role. Others of his plays include <i>50 Revolutions</i> performed by the <a href="/wiki/Headlong_(theatre_company)" title="Headlong (theatre company)">Oxford Stage Company</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whitehall_Theatre" title="Whitehall Theatre">Whitehall Theatre</a>, London in 2000 and <i>Resolution</i> at <a href="/wiki/Battersea_Arts_Centre" title="Battersea Arts Centre">Battersea Arts Centre</a> in 1994.</p>
<p>Gold also wrote the radio play <i>Kafka the Musical</i>, broadcast on Easter Sunday 2011 on BBC Radio 3, starring <a href="/wiki/David_Tennant" title="David Tennant">David Tennant</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> It won the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Tinniswood_Award" title="Tinniswood Award">Tinniswood Award</a> for the Best Original Radio Drama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
