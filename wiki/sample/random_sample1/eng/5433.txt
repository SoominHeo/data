<p><b>Melissa Suzanne McBride</b> (born May 23, 1965)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buddytv_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-buddytv-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American actress and former <a href="/wiki/Casting_(performing_arts)#Casting_director" title="Casting (performing arts)">casting director</a>, best known for her role as <a href="/wiki/Carol_Peletier" title="Carol Peletier">Carol Peletier</a> on the <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i>. McBride has garnered critical acclaim and received multiple awards and nominations for her role on the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_McBride&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McBride was born in <a href="/wiki/Lexington,_Kentucky" title="Lexington, Kentucky">Lexington, Kentucky</a> to parents John Leslie McBride and Suzanne Lillian (née Sagley).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her father owned his own business, and her mother studied at the historic <a href="/wiki/Pasadena_Playhouse" title="Pasadena Playhouse">Pasadena Playhouse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_McBride&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McBride began her acting career in 1991, appearing in several television commercials for clients such as <a href="/wiki/Rooms_To_Go" title="Rooms To Go">Rooms To Go</a>; she was also a spokeswoman for <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rdtvt_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-rdtvt-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-6">[6]</a></sup> She made her series television debut in a 1993 episode of <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> legal drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Matlock_(TV_series)" title="Matlock (TV series)">Matlock</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-6">[6]</a></sup> and later guest-starred in several other television drama series, including <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Heat_of_the_Night_(TV_series)" title="In the Heat of the Night (TV series)">In the Heat of the Night</a></i>; <i><a href="/wiki/American_Gothic_(1995_TV_series)" title="American Gothic (1995 TV series)">American Gothic</a></i>; <i><a href="/wiki/Profiler_(TV_series)" title="Profiler (TV series)">Profiler</a></i>; <i><a href="/wiki/Walker,_Texas_Ranger" title="Walker, Texas Ranger">Walker, Texas Ranger</a></i>; and <i><a href="/wiki/Dawson%27s_Creek" title="Dawson's Creek">Dawson's Creek</a></i>. In the latter, she played Nina – a film buff who charms Dawson after his breakup with Jen – in the Season 1 episode "Road Trip" (1998) - and in 2003 returned to show in the series finale playing a different character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wet_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-wet-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 1990s, McBride had supporting roles in several made-for-television movies, such as <i>Her Deadly Rival</i> (1995) opposite <a href="/wiki/Annie_Potts" title="Annie Potts">Annie Potts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Hamlin" title="Harry Hamlin">Harry Hamlin</a>, <i>Close to Danger</i> (1997) with <a href="/wiki/Rob_Estes" title="Rob Estes">Rob Estes</a>, <i>Any Place But Home</i> (1997), and <i><a href="/wiki/Pirates_of_Silicon_Valley" title="Pirates of Silicon Valley">Pirates of Silicon Valley</a></i> (1999). In 1996, she appeared on the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/A_Season_in_Purgatory" title="A Season in Purgatory">A Season in Purgatory</a></i>, based on <a href="/wiki/Dominick_Dunne" title="Dominick Dunne">Dominick Dunne</a>'s eponymous 1993 novel. From 2000 to 2010, she worked as a film and commercial casting director in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and starred in several short films. In 2007, director <a href="/wiki/Frank_Darabont" title="Frank Darabont">Frank Darabont</a> cast McBride as the "woman with the kids at home" in the ensemble-cast science-fiction horror film <i>The Mist</i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jane" title="Thomas Jane">Thomas Jane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laurie_Holden" title="Laurie Holden">Laurie Holden</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Marcia_Gay_Harden" title="Marcia Gay Harden">Marcia Gay Harden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LATimes_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-6">[6]</a></sup> McBride was in contention for a bigger role in the film but did not want to take a significant amount of time away from her job as a casting director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rdtvt_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-rdtvt-5">[5]</a></sup> The following year, she appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> television movie <i><a href="/wiki/Living_Proof_(film)" title="Living Proof (film)">Living Proof</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Walking_Dead"><i>The Walking Dead</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_McBride&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Walking Dead">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:160px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Melissa_McBride_40th_Saturn_Awards_(cropped).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2446" data-file-width="1728" height="224" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Melissa_McBride_40th_Saturn_Awards_%28cropped%29.jpg/158px-Melissa_McBride_40th_Saturn_Awards_%28cropped%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Melissa_McBride_40th_Saturn_Awards_%28cropped%29.jpg/237px-Melissa_McBride_40th_Saturn_Awards_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Melissa_McBride_40th_Saturn_Awards_%28cropped%29.jpg/316px-Melissa_McBride_40th_Saturn_Awards_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" width="158"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Melissa_McBride_40th_Saturn_Awards_(cropped).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>McBride's earlier relationship with Darabont led him to cast her as <a href="/wiki/Carol_Peletier" title="Carol Peletier">Carol Peletier</a> in the AMC television drama series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i> – her biggest role to date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Peletier is a middle-aged widow and caring mother to elementary-school-aged Sophia, fighting to survive in a violent post-apocalyptic world populated with flesh-eating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zombies" title="Zombies">zombies</a> and the few surviving humans, some of whom are diabolical and even more dangerous than the zombies themselves. McBride did not audition for role, which she thought was temporary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rdtvt_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-rdtvt-5">[5]</a></sup> She was a recurring cast member in Season 1 and was promoted to series regular for Season 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> McBride's name appeared in the opening credits sequence beginning with the first episode of Season 4. Carol was supposed to have been killed off in the episode "<a href="/wiki/Killer_Within" title="Killer Within">Killer Within</a>", but the producers eventually decided against it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>As the series progresses, McBride's character develops from a weak, dependent woman into a strong, loyal warrior. The direction of her character is contrasted between the two media. In the comic series, Carol is much younger and exhibits a neurotic, self-centered, and naive demeanor. Throughout her time in the comics, she grows increasingly unstable to the point of being self-destructive. The television show differs in these regards, as she is shown to be a stern, pragmatic, and compassionate individual who has been gradually building inner strength. Producers of the series, <a href="/wiki/Scott_M._Gimple" title="Scott M. Gimple">Scott M. Gimple</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kirkman" title="Robert Kirkman">Robert Kirkman</a>, said in 2014 that "Carol is her own unique character; it would be a disservice to Melissa McBride to say she's evolved into the Carol from comics. The Carol in the TV show is a wholly original creation that we'll continue to explore on the show to great effect. Everyone in writers' room loves that character, and we're thrilled with what Melissa has brought to the table. She has definitely become a character that is one to watch, and there's some really exciting stuff ahead for her."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>McBride has received critical acclaim for her performance as Carol and won positive reviews from critics during Seasons 3, 4, and 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Many critics praised McBride's performance in the Season 4 episode centered on her character, "<a href="/wiki/The_Grove_(The_Walking_Dead)" title="The Grove (The Walking Dead)">The Grove</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Others singled out Carol's actions in the Season 5 premiere, <a href="/wiki/No_Sanctuary_(The_Walking_Dead)" title="No Sanctuary (The Walking Dead)">"No Sanctuary"</a>, which earned critical praise and positive fan reception. Despite the praise of some critics<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and a fan campaign,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> McBride did not receive a nomination for the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Drama_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> However, she won the 40th Annual <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_on_Television" title="Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress on Television">Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress on Television</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and was nominated for the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Television_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Drama_Series" title="Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Drama Series">Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Drama Series</a> for her performance in Season 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In March 2015, McBride was nominated for an Fangoria Chainsaw Award for Best Leading Actress in a Television series, for her role as Carol.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> She once again won the <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_on_Television" title="Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress on Television">Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress on Television</a> at the <a href="/wiki/41st_Saturn_Awards" title="41st Saturn Awards">41st Saturn Awards</a>, for the second year in a row.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_McBride&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the mid-1980s, McBride moved to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, where she still lives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rdtvt_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-rdtvt-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
