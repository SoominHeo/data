<p><b>Melissa Errico</b> is an American actress, singer, recording artist and writer. She is perhaps best known for her leading Broadway musical roles such as Eliza Doolittle in "<a href="/wiki/My_Fair_Lady" title="My Fair Lady">My Fair Lady</a>" and Kurt Weill's "<a href="/wiki/One_Touch_of_Venus" title="One Touch of Venus">One Touch of Venus</a>," and as an interpreter of the work of Stephen Sondheim and Michel Legrand.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_Errico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Melissa Errico was born in <a href="/wiki/New_York_Hospital" title="New York Hospital">New York Hospital</a> and lived in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> for five years while her New Jersey-raised father completed medical residency and her Brooklyn-native mother was a schoolteacher in Harlem. Both of first-generation Italian descent, her parents relocated the family to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manhasset" title="Manhasset">Manhasset</a>, Long Island where her father established a medical practice in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orthopedics" title="Orthopedics">Orthopedics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Starting her professional career at the age of 12, Melissa appeared on national television in the <a href="/wiki/Sunbow_Entertainment" title="Sunbow Entertainment">Sunbow Entertainment</a> production, syndicated children's TV series "The Great Space Coaster" (created by <a href="/wiki/Kermit_Love" title="Kermit Love">Kermit Love</a> (original Muppet designer for <a href="/wiki/Jim_Henson" title="Jim Henson">Jim Henson</a>) and Jim Martin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Errico's maternal grandmother was an operatic, lyric <a href="/wiki/Soprano" title="Soprano">soprano</a> and her grandmother's sister was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ziegfeld" title="Ziegfeld">Ziegfeld</a> Follies girl.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_Errico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Errico is a graduate of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yale" title="Yale">Yale</a> University. During her freshman year, Errico was asked to audition for the role of <a href="/wiki/Cosette" title="Cosette">Cosette</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-antonnews1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-antonnews1-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-broadwayworld1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-broadwayworld1-6">[6]</a></sup> and was cast. Errico toured for over a year in the First National Company of "Les Misérables," then returned and graduated from Yale University with an honors degree <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-antonnews1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-antonnews1-5">[5]</a></sup> in Art History &amp; Philosophy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> While a student in New Haven, Errico played Irina in "The Three Sisters" with <a href="/wiki/Edward_Norton" title="Edward Norton">Edward Norton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She was accepted into the Yale Graduate School of Drama but dropped out to star in "<a href="/wiki/Anna_Karenina" title="Anna Karenina">Anna Karenina</a>" as Princess Kitty on Broadway at The Circle In The Square. Errico is a graduate of the BADA program at Oxford University where she studied acting Shakespeare and Chekhov.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Theater">Theater</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_Errico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Theater">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Errico played Cosette in "Les Miserables" (National Tour) and Eliza Doolittle in "My Fair Lady" on Broadway. Other Broadway starring roles include "High Society," "Anna Karenina," "Dracula, The Musical," "Amour," and "Irving Berlin's White Christmas." She has six Drama Desk Award nominations including Best Actress in a Play for "Candida" and is the recipient of a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> nomination for Best Leading Actress in a Musical (<a href="/wiki/Michel_Legrand" title="Michel Legrand">Michel Legrand</a>'s "Amour"), four Outer Critics Circle Awards, five Drama League Honors, two Helen Hayes nominations and the <a href="/wiki/Lucille_Lortel" title="Lucille Lortel">Lucille Lortel</a> Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in "<a href="/wiki/One_Touch_of_Venus" title="One Touch of Venus">One Touch of Venus</a>." She is the first, and only, recipient in the history of The Lortel Awards to be recognized as Outstanding Lead Actress in a Musical and is the only performer in the over-twenty-year history of The City Center Encores! series to win an award for their work. In 2016, she returned to City Center for another triumph in the Rodgers-Sondheim musical "Do I Hear A Waltz?" winning rave reviews, such as "an exquisite interpretation"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_roles">Notable roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_Errico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notable roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>Eliza Doolittle</b>: previewing from 26 November 1993, opening 9 December, running until May 1, 1994, Errico appeared as Eliza opposite <a href="/wiki/Richard_Chamberlain" title="Richard Chamberlain">Richard Chamberlain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julian_Holloway" title="Julian Holloway">Julian Holloway</a> (who recreated his father's role as Alfred P. Doolittle) at the Virginia Theater on Broadway. In 2003, Errico returned to Eliza Doolittle, with <a href="/wiki/John_Lithgow" title="John Lithgow">John Lithgow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roger_Daltrey" title="Roger Daltrey">Roger Daltrey</a> for a production of "<a href="/wiki/My_Fair_Lady" title="My Fair Lady">My Fair Lady</a>" at The Hollywood Bowl in Los Angeles.</li>
<li><b>Venus</b>: From 28 – 30 March 1996, Errico made an appearance at The City Center Encores! Directed by <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Foglia" title="Leonard Foglia">Leonard Foglia</a> (Master Class), she played the titular role in Kurt Weill's "<a href="/wiki/One_Touch_of_Venus" title="One Touch of Venus">One Touch of Venus</a>." Errico was named Outstanding Actress in a Musical (1996 Lucille Lortel Award). The first-ever complete recording of the score, starring Errico, was released 13 May 2014 on Jay Records. On October 7, 2013, Errico sang the role in a filmed concert at Symphony Space with Le Train Bleu Orchestra conducted by James Holmes.</li>
<li><b>Dot/Marie</b>: The production ran at the Eisenhower Theater, 31 May – 28 June and Errico's work was recognized with a Helen Hayes Award nomination for Leading Actress in a Musical.</li>
<li><b>Candida</b> – Directed by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Walton" title="Tony Walton">Tony Walton</a>, Errico took on the title role in the Irish Repertory Theater's revival of <a href="/wiki/George_Bernard_Shaw" title="George Bernard Shaw">George Bernard Shaw</a>'s play. The production ran 24 February – 25 April in 2010. Errico achieved a Drama Desk Award nomination for her work.</li>
<li><b>Annie Savoy</b> – Errico played the role of Annie in the world premiere of the new musical, "<a href="/wiki/Bull_Durham" title="Bull Durham">Bull Durham</a>" which played 3 September – 5 October 2014 at the Alliance theater in Atlanta, Georgia.</li>
<li><b>Leona Samish</b> – Ms Errico played the lead role of Leona in "Do I Hear A Waltz?" at New York, City Center from 11–15 May 2016.</li>
<li><b>Sharon McLonergan</b> – Errico returned to the role of Sharon in the 2016 production of "<a href="/wiki/Finian%27s_Rainbow" title="Finian's Rainbow">Finian's Rainbow</a>" an experience she chronicled (as a writer) <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>in the NY Times.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_Errico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Errico made a strong impression with the recurring guest role of June on Showtime Television's "Billions," leading Variety Magazine to write her up as a contender for a 2016 Emmy Award Nomination. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> She played the recurring role of Catherine on Season One of Stephen Soderbergh's "The Knick," and had a comedic guest turn as Gloria, mother to Michael Ian Black's character, on "The Jim Gaffigan Show." She has been cast to star in pilots produced by Jon Favreau and Larry Charles ("Smog"), Ed Burns ("Fighting Fitzgeralds"), Tom Fontana and Spike Lee ("Mony" opposite Bobby Cannavale) and Kelsey Grammar ("Neurotic Tendencies"). She was a series regular on Darren Star's "Central Park West" and has made recent guest appearances in "Blue Bloods" and "The Good Wife".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concerts">Concerts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_Errico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Concerts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Her appearances include concerts at The Kennedy Center Opera House, Avery Fisher/David Geffen Music Hall, Wolf Trap, Severance Hall, 54 Below, Joe's Pub at The Public Theater, Jazz at Lincoln Center and Café Carlyle. She reprised her performance as Venus at City Center Encores! Broadway Bash! <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> in November 2001. Errico made her London debut in May 2008 singing in "Jerry Herman's Broadway" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/London_Palladium" title="London Palladium">London Palladium</a>. She has taken part in three Symphony Space Wall to Wall events to commemorate <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Sondheim" title="Stephen Sondheim">Stephen Sondheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurt_Weill" title="Kurt Weill">Kurt Weill</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Richard_Rodgers" title="Richard Rodgers">Richard Rodgers</a> in NYC. In November 2015, she debuted her new one-woman show at Joe's Pub, New York co-written with close collaborator <a href="/wiki/Adam_Gopnik" title="Adam Gopnik">Adam Gopnik</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Errico has sung with such symphonies as The Cleveland Orchestra, The National Symphony Orchestra, The London Philharmonic, The Utah Symphony, The Brussels Philharmonic, and has toured with conductors Marvin Hamlisch <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>(NSO) and Michael Tilson Thomas (New World Symphony). She appeared in March 2016 at Lincoln Center in a Judy Garland concert<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> moderated by Adam Gopnik.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_Errico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She has 2 siblings – <a href="/wiki/Mike_Errico" title="Mike Errico">Mike Errico</a> and Melanie Errico. She met her husband, former tennis professional and ESPN sports commentator <a href="/wiki/Patrick_McEnroe" title="Patrick McEnroe">Patrick McEnroe</a> when they were in grade school together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longislandpress1_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-longislandpress1-13">[13]</a></sup> They married 19 December 1998 at the Holy Trinity Church on W. 82nd Street.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The couple has three daughters.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing">Writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_Errico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Errico has written lyrics – "Gentle Child" appears on her 2008 solo recording, "Lullabies and Wildflowers" and authored "Musings" – which details the chronology of her album <i>Legrand Affair</i>. She was principal speaker at the Annual Broadway Blessing in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melissa_Errico&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Errico founded the original Bowery Babes in 2005 and maintains her seat on the Board of Directors for this New York based non-profit 501-c3 organization. Errico sang on June 1, 2015 at Merkin Concert Hall in support of The Ali Forney Center, the nation's largest and most comprehensive organization dedicated to homeless LGBT youth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
