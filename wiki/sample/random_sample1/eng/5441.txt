<p><b>Mengshan County</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">蒙山县</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">蒙山縣</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Méngshān Xiàn</span></i>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_(People%27s_Republic_of_China)" title="County (People's Republic of China)">county</a> under the administration of <a href="/wiki/Wuzhou" title="Wuzhou">Wuzhou</a> City in northeastern <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a>, China. Its seat is located in Mengshan Town.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mengshan_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tianshu Canyon (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">天书峡谷</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">天書峽谷</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">San tiān shū xiá gǔ</span></i>) is located 5 kilometers east of Mengshan town. Tianshu Canyon has a has impressive stone formations and natural waterfalls.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mengshan_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Battles were fought in Mengshan during the <a href="/wiki/Taiping_Rebellion" title="Taiping Rebellion">Tai Ping Rebellion</a> in 1851 and 1852.</p>
<p>Taiping soldiers captured the walled city of Yongan(<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">永安</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">yǒng ān</span></i>)(now Mengshan Town) from Qing forces on September 25, 1851. The remains of sections of wall, replica cannons, other artifacts are preserved and now form a tourist attraction on the northern end of Mengshan Town.</p>

<p>Located 13 kilometers east of Mengshan Town is San Chong Historic Battle Ground (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">三冲古战场</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">三衝古戰場</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">San Chong Gu Zhan Chang</span></i>) The site of a battle on April 8, 1852 during the Tai Ping Rebellion.</p>
<p>In Mengshan county in 1968 during the cultural revolution Cannibalism occurred. 500 people were killed in a five-day period in mid-June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Many Middle Schools were closed in Mengshan in 1968 and remained closed until 1974.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mengshan_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mengshan does not have an airport or railway. The closest airports to Mengshan are <a href="/wiki/Guilin_Liangjiang_International_Airport" title="Guilin Liangjiang International Airport">Guilin Liangjiang International Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wuzhou" title="Wuzhou">Wuzhou Airport</a>. The main road which runs North-South through Mengshan is the G321 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China_National_Highway" title="China National Highway">National Highway</a> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">国道G321</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">國道G321</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Guódào G sān èr yāo</span></i>). Mengshan town is 145 km by road south of Guilin, 82 km by road south of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yangshuo" title="Yangshuo">Yangshuo</a>, and 187 km by road north of Wuzhou. The G321 is a single carriageway with 2 lanes.</p>
<p>There are direct bus services from Mengshan town to Guilin, Wuzhou and many cities in the Guangdong Pearl River Delta including Guangzhou and Zhongshan.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mengshan_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although similar to Guilin and Yangshuo with attractive karst scenery, potential for outdoor activities, such as caving, rafting, climbing and mountain biking, Mengshan does not have any significant tourist industry or infrastructure.</p>
<p>Agriculture and Forestry are the main Industries in Mengshan. Rice is grown primarily for domestic consumption. Crops and fruit such as butter tea, citrus fruit, apple, chestnut, pear, persimmon, passion fruit, star anise, sugar cane and ginger are grown for sale. Silkworms are also an important source of income for many villages. Vanilla is grown in Changping <a href="/wiki/Yao_people" title="Yao people">Yao</a> Minority Village (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">长坪瑶族乡</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">長坪瑤族鄉</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Cháng Píng Yáo Zú Xiāng</span></i>) and in other parts of Mengshan.</p>
<p>Tea is grown in most villages for domestic use, as well as for sale. Mengshan's climate is ideal for growing tea. Coffee mainly of the <a href="/wiki/Coffea_arabica" title="Coffea arabica">Arabica</a> variety grows well in Mengshan county in frost free areas but planting is on small scale.</p>
<p>Mengshan has productive vinyards, and further vinyards are being developed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Remittance" title="Remittance">Remittances</a> from rural migrant workers significantly contribute to the local economy.</p>
