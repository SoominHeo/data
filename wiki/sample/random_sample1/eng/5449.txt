<p><b>Megan Sullivan McInerney</b> (born 6 July 1990), known by her stage name <b>Meg Mac</b>, is an Australian singer-songwriter and musician. She signed to littleBIGMAN records in 2014, locally, and <a href="/wiki/300_Entertainment" title="300 Entertainment">300 Entertainment</a> in United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meg_Mac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Meg Mac started developing her singing as soon as she could speak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She started writing songs as a teen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meg_Mac&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Musical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2014 Meg Mac announced her first national headline tour to promote her debut extended play, <i><a href="/wiki/MegMac" title="MegMac">MegMac</a></i>, which was issued on 12 September that year on littleBIGMAN Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moskovitch_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Moskovitch-4">[4]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Triple_J" title="Triple J">Triple J</a>, the national youth radio station, she was announced as their "Unearthed Artist of 2014",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> while <i>Marie Claire Australia</i> chose her as an "Artist to Watch" for 2015<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and she received a nomination for <i>Rolling Stone Australia</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s "Best New Talent" Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was reviewed by <i>The New York Times</i>, BuzzFeed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and Daytrotter. Mac toured supporting <a href="/wiki/Clean_Bandit" title="Clean Bandit">Clean Bandit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 August 2015 Mac achieved her first top 50 chart appearance with "<a href="/wiki/Never_Be" title="Never Be">Never Be</a>", which entered the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">ARIA Singles Chart</a> at No. 39.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Singles_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Singles-10">[10]</a></sup> It out-peaked her first top 100 single, "<a href="/wiki/Roll_Up_Your_Sleeves_(song)" title="Roll Up Your Sleeves (song)">Roll Up Your Sleeves</a>", which had reached No. 80 in August 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Singles_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Singles-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARIA_Report_1280_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ARIA_Report_1280-11">[11]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Music_Awards_of_2015" title="ARIA Music Awards of 2015">ARIA Music Awards of 2015</a> she was nominated for <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Award_for_Best_Female_Artist" title="ARIA Award for Best Female Artist">Best Female Artist</a> for <i>MEGMAC</i> and <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Award_for_Breakthrough_Artist_-_Release" title="ARIA Award for Breakthrough Artist - Release">Breakthrough Artist</a> for "<a href="/wiki/Never_Be" title="Never Be">Never Be</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARIA_List_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ARIA_List-12">[12]</a></sup> On 26 January 2016 "Never Be" made it to No.11 in Triple J's Hottest 100.</p>
