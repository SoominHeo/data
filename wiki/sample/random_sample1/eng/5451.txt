<p><b>Meghna Naidu</b> is an <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> actress and dancer. Her first major appearance was in the music video for UMI10's "Kaliyon Ka Chaman" (2000),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> an official remix of <a href="/wiki/Lata_Mangeshkar" title="Lata Mangeshkar">Lata Mangeshkar</a>'s 1981 song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thoda_Resham_Lagta_Hai_(Mangeshkar_song)" title="Thoda Resham Lagta Hai (Mangeshkar song)">Thoda Resham Lagta Hai</a>" (in turn, the 2002 song "<a href="/wiki/Addictive_(song)" title="Addictive (song)">Addictive</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Truth_Hurts" title="Truth Hurts">Truth Hurts</a> was inspired by the remix). She also featured in <a href="/wiki/Saru_Maini" title="Saru Maini">Saru Maini</a>'s video "Dil De Diya Tha (Sutta Mix)",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> before starting to work in <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_India" title="Cinema of India">Indian films</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meghna_Naidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Naidu was born on 19 September 1980 in <a href="/wiki/Vijayawada" title="Vijayawada">Vijayawada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">Andhra Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-idlebrain1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-idlebrain1-1">[1]</a></sup> Her father Ethiraj works for <a href="/wiki/Air_India" title="Air India">Air India</a> and was a <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> coach and her mother Purnima, a half-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bengali_people" title="Bengali people">Bengali</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff2005_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-rediff2005-6">[6]</a></sup> was a school teacher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiatimes1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-indiatimes1-7">[7]</a></sup> She has one younger sister, Sona.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> She told that she requested her mother to quit her job and accompany her to shooting locations, after her sister who used to travel with her got a job abroad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-idlebrain1_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-idlebrain1-1">[1]</a></sup> She grew up in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maharashtra" title="Maharashtra">Maharashtra</a>, and considers herself "more of a Mumbaiite".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-idlebrain1_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-idlebrain1-1">[1]</a></sup> She studied at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bhavan%27s_College,_Andheri" title="Bhavan's College, Andheri">Bhavan's College, Andheri</a>, Mumbai,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-rediff1-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and graduated with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B.Com" title="B.Com">B.Com</a> degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff2005_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-rediff2005-6">[6]</a></sup> She learned classical <a href="/wiki/Bharatanatyam" title="Bharatanatyam">bharatanatyam</a> dance for seven years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiatimes1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-indiatimes1-7">[7]</a></sup> She stated that she also was coaching tennis in the US for four years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff1_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-rediff1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the age of 18, she met a model co-ordinator at the wedding of her cousin in <a href="/wiki/Chennai" title="Chennai">Chennai</a>, who called her for a screen test for a feature film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deccanherald1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-deccanherald1-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff3_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-rediff3-13">[13]</a></sup> She secured a role in the Telugu film <i>Prema Sakshi</i> (1999), following which she appeared in one more Telugu and two Kannada films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deccanherald1_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-deccanherald1-12">[12]</a></sup> She then appeared in UMI10's music video "Kaliyon Ka Chaman", directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radhika_Rao_(director)" title="Radhika Rao (director)">Radhika Rao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vinay_Sapru" title="Vinay Sapru">Vinay Sapru</a>, which she says happened by accident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-idlebrain1_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-idlebrain1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff3_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-rediff3-13">[13]</a></sup> She was accompanying her friend to the audition for the video, when she was approached by the team. She was asked to send in her photographs and within few days, she was selected for the video. "Kaliyon Ka Chaman" became a grand success and gained her recognition. She subsequently went on to do many stage performances in and outside of India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-idlebrain1_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-idlebrain1-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deccanherald1_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-deccanherald1-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>She made her <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a> debut with the <a href="/wiki/B_movie" title="B movie">B-grade film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hawas_(2004_film)" title="Hawas (2004 film)">Hawas</a></i> (2004),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff4_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-rediff4-14">[14]</a></sup> which dealt with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extramarital_affair" title="Extramarital affair">extramarital affair</a>. It was followed by lead roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Classic_Dance_of_Love" title="Classic Dance of Love">Classic Dance of Love</a></i> (2005), which featured her as a dancer, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rain:_The_Terror_Within..." title="Rain: The Terror Within...">Rain: The Terror Within...</a></i> (2005), in which she played a blind author.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff3_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-rediff3-13">[13]</a></sup> In <i>Mashooka</i>, she played a character with negative shades. In her latter career, she mostly made special appearances in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Item_numbers" title="Item numbers">item numbers</a> in South Indian films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timesofindia.indiatimes.com_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-timesofindia.indiatimes.com-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>She also participated in the television shows <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fear_Factor_-_Khatron_Ke_Khiladi" title="Fear Factor - Khatron Ke Khiladi">Fear Factor - Khatron Ke Khiladi</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_Queen_(TV_series)" title="Dancing Queen (TV series)">Dancing Queen</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rediff4_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-rediff4-14">[14]</a></sup> She played the role of Benazir in <i><a href="/wiki/Jodha_Akbar_(TV_series)" title="Jodha Akbar (TV series)">Jodha Akbar</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Naidu has been in a relationship with tennis player Luis Miguel Reis since 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
