<p><b>Medny Island</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">о́стров Ме́дный</span>), also spelled <i>Mednyy</i> or <i>Mednyi</i>, sometimes called <b>Copper Island</b> in English, is the smaller (after <a href="/wiki/Bering_Island" title="Bering Island">Bering Island</a>) of the two main <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">islands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Commander_Islands" title="Commander Islands">Commander Islands</a> in the North <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, east of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kamchatka" title="Kamchatka">Kamchatka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. (The other fifteen are better described as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islets" title="Islets">islets</a> and rocks.) These islands belong to the <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka_Krai" title="Kamchatka Krai">Kamchatka Krai</a> of the Russian Federation.</p>
<p>The island was uninhabited until the early 20th century, when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aleut_people" title="Aleut people">Aleuts</a> came from <a href="/wiki/Attu_Island" title="Attu Island">Attu Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands" title="Aleutian Islands">Aleutian Islands</a> to Medny Island.</p>
<p>The island is 56 km long and between 5 and 7 km wide and its area is 186 km². Its maximum elevation is 640 m and the average annual temperature is +2.8 <a href="/wiki/Celsius" title="Celsius">°C</a>. About 100 meters off the northwestern end of the islands are the <i>Beaver Stones</i> (Бобровые камни in <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>), two islets connected by an <a href="/wiki/Isthmus" title="Isthmus">isthmus</a>, with a combined length of 1 km.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Medny_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island was sighted by on 5 November 1741 by <a href="/wiki/Vitus_Bering" title="Vitus Bering">Bering</a> and his crew while returning from the expedition during which he found America from the west, but he did not land on this island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Russian naval officer and historian Vasily Berch believed that Yemelyan Basov reached this island in 1745, during his second of four expeditions to the Commander Islands between 1743 and 1749, and that most of the furs he brought back to Kamchatka in 1745 were hunted on this island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Towards the end of the 19th century, the settlement of <a href="/wiki/Preobrazhenskoye,_Kamchatka_Oblast" title="Preobrazhenskoye, Kamchatka Oblast">Preobrazhenskoye</a> was established by Aleuts who moved there from <a href="/wiki/Attu_Island" title="Attu Island">Attu Island</a>.</p>
<p>In 1970, all citizens of the island were moved to neighboring <a href="/wiki/Bering_Island" title="Bering Island">Bering Island</a>. Until 2001, the island was occupied as a frontier post. Since then, the island has been completely uninhabited. Scientific studies of the fauna and flora are conducted annually.</p>
