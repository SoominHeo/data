<p><b>Medenine</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">مدنين</span>‎‎ <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="DIN 31635 Arabic">Mednīn</span></i>) is one of the 24 governorates (provinces) of <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Medenine_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The govenerate encompasses the south-easternmost coastal strip, totalling 9167 km² and had a population of 479,520 at the 2014 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tun_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tun-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The capital is <a href="/wiki/Medenine" title="Medenine">Medenine</a>. The governorate includes the country's largest island, <a href="/wiki/Djerba" title="Djerba">Djerba</a>, which is connected by bridge and has approximately a quarter of the total population and its own airport.</p>
<p>The area is generally lowlands. It extends in two projections at its extreme ends a maximum of 60 km from the coast, otherwise the neighbouring division of <a href="/wiki/Tataouine_Governorate" title="Tataouine Governorate">Tataouine</a> is 30 km from the coast. The northern projection abuts <a href="/wiki/Kebili_Governorate" title="Kebili Governorate">Kebili Governorate</a> and includes part of a long escarpment which extends from the south of Gabes Governorate (adjoining Medenine to the north) to beyond Tripoli in Libya and which is dotted with settlements. Precipitation is low and reaches a maximum between October and January, the far north of the govenorate and western hill range benefiting from this most. Key settlements are those associated with ports, tourism, fishing, farming in more fertile parts within the north-east and oases.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Medenine_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The governorate is divided into nine delegations (with their populations at the 2004 Census):</p>
<ul>
<li>Medenine Nord (48,102)</li>
<li>Medenine Sud (48,087)</li>
<li>Beni Khedech (28,586)</li>
<li>Ben Guerdane (70,907)</li>
<li>Zarzis (73,549)</li>
<li>Djerba Houmet Souk (64,919)</li>
<li>Djerba Midoun (50,459)</li>
<li>Djerba Ajim (24,166)</li>
<li>Sidi Makhloulf (23,728)</li>
</ul>
