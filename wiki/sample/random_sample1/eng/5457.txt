<p><b>Yoshikazu Mera</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">米良 美一</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mera Yoshikazu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, born May 21, 1971, in <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki,_Miyazaki" title="Miyazaki, Miyazaki">Miyazaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Countertenor" title="Countertenor">countertenor</a>. His range is three and a half <a href="/wiki/Octave" title="Octave">octaves</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bach_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bach-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Originally wanting to become a <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> singer, Mera now primarily sings <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">classical music</a> from the West but also classical Japanese music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bach_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bach-1">[1]</a></sup> He appears frequently as a soloist with the <a href="/wiki/Bach_Collegium_Japan" title="Bach Collegium Japan">Bach Collegium Japan</a>, which under its conductor <a href="/wiki/Masaaki_Suzuki" title="Masaaki Suzuki">Masaaki Suzuki</a> performs <a href="/wiki/Baroque_music" title="Baroque music">Baroque music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bach_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-bach-1">[1]</a></sup> His 1998 recital disc "Nightingale" on Sweden's <a href="/wiki/BIS_Records" title="BIS Records">BIS Records</a> was a major success in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was revealed in a television documentary entitled "米良美一の實話" ("Yoshikazu Mela's true story") that he was born with congenital <a href="/wiki/Osteogenesis_imperfecta" title="Osteogenesis imperfecta">osteogenesis imperfecta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-true_story_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-true_story-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
