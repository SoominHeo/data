<p><b>Meredith Louise Vieira</b> (born December 30, 1953) is an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a>, talk show and <a href="/wiki/Game_show_host" title="Game show host">game show host</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> She is known for serving as the original moderator of the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a href="/wiki/Talk_show" title="Talk show">talk show</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_View_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The View (U.S. TV series)">The View</a></i> (1997–2006), and for co-hosting the long-running <a href="/wiki/NBC_News" title="NBC News">NBC News</a> <a href="/wiki/Breakfast_television" title="Breakfast television">morning news program</a>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Today_(NBC_program)" title="Today (NBC program)">Today</a></i> (2006–2011).</p>
<p>Vieira was the original host of the United States <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_syndication" title="Broadcast syndication">syndicated</a> version of <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Wants_to_Be_a_Millionaire_(U.S._game_show)" title="Who Wants to Be a Millionaire (U.S. game show)">Who Wants to Be a Millionaire</a></i>. She has been a contributor to <i><a href="/wiki/Dateline_NBC" title="Dateline NBC">Dateline NBC</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Center_with_Brian_Williams" title="Rock Center with Brian Williams">Rock Center with Brian Williams</a></i>, and was the presenter on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lifetime_Television" title="Lifetime Television">Lifetime Television</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Intimate_Portrait" title="Intimate Portrait">Intimate Portrait</a></i> series. She hosted a syndicated talk show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Meredith_Vieira_Show" title="The Meredith Vieira Show">The Meredith Vieira Show</a></i>, from 2014 to 2016.</p>
<p>She is a special correspondent for <a href="/wiki/NBC_News" title="NBC News">NBC News</a> and a contributor to <i><a href="/wiki/Today_(U.S._TV_program)" title="Today (U.S. TV program)">Today</a></i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/NBC_Nightly_News" title="NBC Nightly News">NBC Nightly News</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Dateline_NBC" title="Dateline NBC">Dateline NBC</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meredith_Vieira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vieira was born in <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-1">[1]</a></sup> and raised in nearby <a href="/wiki/East_Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="East Providence, Rhode Island">East Providence</a>, the daughter of Mary Elsie Rosa, a homemaker, and Edwin Vieira, a medical doctor,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> both <a href="/wiki/Immigrant_generations#first_generation" title="Immigrant generations">first-generation</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luso_American#Portuguese" title="Luso American">Portuguese Americans</a>. She is the youngest of four children, with three older brothers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GH_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-GH-3">[3]</a></sup> All four of Vieira’s grandparents came from the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>—three from <a href="/wiki/Faial_Island" title="Faial Island">Faial Island</a>, one of the nine islands in the <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a>. The family name <a href="/wiki/Vieira" title="Vieira">Vieira</a> means "scallop" in Portuguese. They <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emigrated" title="Emigrated">emigrated</a> to <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> in the late-19th and early-20th centuries, settling around <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a>. Vieira was raised in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> faith, but she has stated in recent interviews that she has "spirituality, not a religion."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GH2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GH2-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vieira attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincoln_School,_Rhode_Island" title="Lincoln School, Rhode Island">Lincoln School</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quaker" title="Quaker">Quaker</a> all-girls school in Providence, graduating in 1971.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She graduated with a degree in English from <a href="/wiki/Tufts_University" title="Tufts University">Tufts University</a> in 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meredith_Vieira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginnings_on_radio_and_local_TV_.281975.E2.80.931982.29">Beginnings on radio and local TV (1975–1982)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meredith_Vieira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Beginnings on radio and local TV (1975–1982)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vieira began her broadcasting career in 1975 as a news announcer for <a href="/wiki/WORC_(AM)" title="WORC (AM)">WORC</a> radio in <a href="/wiki/Worcester,_Massachusetts" title="Worcester, Massachusetts">Worcester, Massachusetts</a>, doing afternoon drive news during the <i>B. J. Dean Show</i>. She began a career in television working as a local reporter and anchor at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WJAR-TV" title="WJAR-TV">WJAR-TV</a> in Providence, eventually making her way into the newsroom at <a href="/wiki/WCBS-TV" title="WCBS-TV">WCBS-TV</a> in New York City where she was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Investigative_reporter" title="Investigative reporter">investigative reporter</a> from 1979 to 1982.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CBS_News_.281982.E2.80.931993.29">CBS News (1982–1993)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meredith_Vieira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: CBS News (1982–1993)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vieira first gained national recognition as a <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> reporter based in its Chicago bureau from 1982 to 1984. She later became a correspondent for nationwide news-magazine shows including <i><a href="/wiki/West_57th_(TV_series)" title="West 57th (TV series)">West 57th</a></i> (1985–89) and <i><a href="/wiki/60_Minutes" title="60 Minutes">60 Minutes</a></i> (1989–91). Her final assignment at CBS was as co-anchor of the <i><a href="/wiki/CBS_Morning_News" title="CBS Morning News">CBS Morning News</a></i> (1992–93).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Move_to_ABC.2C_Turning_Point.3B_Intimate_Portrait.3B_The_View_.281994.E2.80.932006.29">Move to ABC, <i>Turning Point</i>; <i>Intimate Portrait</i>; <i>The View</i> (1994–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meredith_Vieira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Move to ABC, Turning Point; Intimate Portrait; The View (1994–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vieira moved to ABC initially as one of six regular correspondents for the news-magazine show <i><a href="/wiki/Turning_Point_(TV_series)" title="Turning Point (TV series)">Turning Point</a></i> (1994–99),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complete_Directory_of_Television_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Complete_Directory_of_Television-7">[7]</a></sup> and was also the host of the <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime Network</a>'s show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intimate_Portrait_(TV_series)" title="Intimate Portrait (TV series)">Intimate Portrait</a></i>, which debuted on January 3, 1995, and ran until August 28, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Complete_Directory_of_Television_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Complete_Directory_of_Television-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vieira served as the original moderator and co-host of <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s daytime talk show <i><a href="/wiki/The_View_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The View (U.S. TV series)">The View</a></i> from its debut on August 11, 1997, until June 9, 2006. As moderator, she was responsible for opening and closing each of the show's live episodes, introducing "Hot Topics," guiding conversations, and breaking to commercials. On her final episode of <i>The View</i>, Vieira's co-hosts gave her a roast to commemorate her departure.</p>
<p>Vieira explained what led her to become <i>The View'</i>s moderator in an interview with the <i><a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Post-Gazette" title="Pittsburgh Post-Gazette">Pittsburgh Post-Gazette</a></i>, by making the following statement:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">Once I realized I was a reporter who didn't want to report because it required a tremendous amount of travel, nobody was too interested in having me work for them. I had to reinvent myself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oscar_Pre-Show_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oscar_Pre-Show-8">[8]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>In August 2006, Vieira told <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i> that she hasn't watched <i>The View</i> since she left the show, except the episode when <a href="/wiki/Star_Jones" title="Star Jones">Star Jones</a> announced she was leaving. She said it was "very sad" what's happened to it: "I'm proud of the work we did there, but it's not a good time in the history of the show... It's hard to watch. It sort of became a joke."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On August 29, 2006, Vieira told the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a></i> that she didn't mean that <i>The View</i> was a joke. She said the interview was taken out of context. "I felt that the media was turning [<i>The View</i>] into a joke, not that the show was a joke," she says. <i>Time</i> added a clarification to its website, saying "[Vieira] assures <i>Time</i> that in no way were her comments meant to be insensitive or derogatory..."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Who_Wants_to_Be_a_Millionaire_.282002.E2.80.932013.29"><i>Who Wants to Be a Millionaire</i> (2002–2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meredith_Vieira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Who Wants to Be a Millionaire (2002–2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vieira became the first host of the American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_syndication" title="Television syndication">syndicated</a> version of <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Wants_to_Be_a_Millionaire_(U.S._game_show)" title="Who Wants to Be a Millionaire (U.S. game show)">Who Wants to Be a Millionaire</a></i> on September 16, 2002;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> prior to that, American audiences had known it as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime" title="Primetime">primetime</a> show on ABC hosted by <a href="/wiki/Regis_Philbin" title="Regis Philbin">Regis Philbin</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rosie_O%27Donnell" title="Rosie O'Donnell">Rosie O'Donnell</a> (who would later succeed Vieira on <i>The View</i>) was originally offered to host the syndicated version, but rejected it almost immediately.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Vieira won two <a href="/wiki/Daytime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Game_Show_Host" title="Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Game Show Host">Daytime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Game Show Host</a> for her hosting duties on <i>Millionaire</i> (one in 2005, the other in 2009);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> as such, she is the second woman ever to win an Emmy Award in this category (after <a href="/wiki/Betty_White" title="Betty White">Betty White</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Just_Men!" title="Just Men!">Just Men!</a></i> in 1983), and the first to win multiple times. In addition to hosting the show, Vieira also served as its co-executive producer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-futon_critic.E2.80.94rules_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-futon_critic.E2.80.94rules-14">[14]</a></sup> a title that she would hold from 2005 until her departure from the show.</p>
<p>ABC originally offered Vieira hosting duties on the syndicated <i>Millionaire</i> to sweeten one of her re-negotiations for <i>The View</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vieira_leaves_Millionaire_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vieira_leaves_Millionaire-15">[15]</a></sup> When the show was honored by <a href="/wiki/Game_Show_Network" title="Game Show Network">GSN</a> on its 2007 <i>Gameshow Hall of Fame</i> special, one of the show's executive producers, Leigh Hampton, said that when the syndicated version was being developed, the production team felt that it was not feasible for Philbin to continue hosting, as the show recorded four episodes in a single day, and that the team was looking for qualities in a new host: it had to be somebody who would love the contestants and be willing to root for them. After O'Donnell declined the opportunity to host the syndicated version, Vieira was the one that the team settled on, because she had the above-mentioned qualities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gameshow_Hall_of_Fame_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gameshow_Hall_of_Fame-16">[16]</a></sup> On the special, Vieira herself gave the following explanation for why she decided to host the syndicated <i>Millionaire</i>:</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">I did the show because I fell in love with the show, and really, first and foremost, as a parent, [I feel that] there aren't that many shows on television that you can watch as a family. And when [the U.S. version's executive producer] <a href="/wiki/Michael_Davies_(television_producer)" title="Michael Davies (television producer)">Michael Davies</a> approached me and said, "Would you be interested in hosting the syndicated version?", I said, "Just point me toward the contract! I am so there!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gameshow_Hall_of_Fame_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gameshow_Hall_of_Fame-16">[16]</a></sup></td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>

<p>Prior to hosting the syndicated version of <i>Millionaire</i>, Vieira was a celebrity contestant in a special tournament on the third season of the original primetime version, winning $250,000 for her selected charitable organization, the <a href="/wiki/Windows_of_Hope_Family_Relief_Fund" title="Windows of Hope Family Relief Fund">Windows of Hope Family Relief Fund</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Seven and three-quarter years later, she would even turn the tables on Philbin himself with her surprise appearance on the finale of the show's 10th anniversary primetime revival on August 23, 2009—hosting its final segment while giving him the opportunity to answer one question in order to win $50,000 for his selected organization, <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_Hayes_High_School" title="Cardinal Hayes High School">Cardinal Hayes High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 10, 2013, Vieira announced that after eleven seasons with the syndicated <i>Millionaire</i>, throughout which she had hosted more than 1,800 episodes and offered a vast multitude of contestants a combined total of over $70,000,000,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> she would be leaving the show as part of an effort to focus on other projects in her career. She finalized taping of her last episodes with the show in November 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vieira_leaves_Millionaire_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Vieira_leaves_Millionaire-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Today_and_The_Meredith_Vieira_Show_.282006.E2.80.93present.29"><i>Today</i> and <i>The Meredith Vieira Show</i> (2006–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meredith_Vieira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Today and The Meredith Vieira Show (2006–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Vieira accepted an offer to succeed <a href="/wiki/Katie_Couric" title="Katie Couric">Katie Couric</a> as co-anchor of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Today_(NBC_program)" title="Today (NBC program)">Today</a></i> on April 6, 2006, the day after Couric announced that she would depart the show to become anchor of the <i><a href="/wiki/CBS_Evening_News" title="CBS Evening News">CBS Evening News</a></i>. The following day, Vieira announced on <i>The View</i> that she would be leaving the show to be co-anchor of <i>Today</i>, a role <i>View</i> co-host and <a href="/wiki/ABC_News" title="ABC News">ABC News</a> journalist <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Walters" title="Barbara Walters">Barbara Walters</a> had filled nearly four decades earlier during her tenure at NBC during the 1960s and 1970s. Vieira hosted the show with Matt Lauer from September 13, 2006, through June 8, 2011, also becoming a contributing anchor for <i><a href="/wiki/Dateline_NBC" title="Dateline NBC">Dateline NBC</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vieira continued as host of <i>Millionaire</i> while appearing on <i>Today</i>. As part of her contract with <i>Millionaire</i>, Vieira agreed not to appear on <i>Today</i> during hours that would conflict with the airing of the game show on competing stations. As a result, she rarely appeared on the third or fourth hours of <i>Today</i>. Her first appearance during the third hour came on June 25, 2008, for "<i>Today</i> Throws a Wedding". She also appeared during the entire third hour during the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">Beijing Olympics</a>, <a href="/wiki/2010_Winter_Olympics" title="2010 Winter Olympics">Vancouver Olympics</a> and the <a href="/wiki/First_inauguration_of_Barack_Obama" title="First inauguration of Barack Obama">Barack Obama inauguration</a> on January 20, 2009.</p>
<p>Vieira announced on May 9, 2011, that she would depart as co-host in the following month, but would remain with <i>Today</i> in the role of special correspondent. Her last appearance as regular co-host was on June 8; she returned to the show for the Halloween broadcast on October 31, 2011, and again as special co-host with Matt Lauer in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, United Kingdom for the <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Jubilee_of_Elizabeth_II" title="Diamond Jubilee of Elizabeth II">Diamond Jubilee of Queen Elizabeth II</a>. She has remained with NBC in a minimized role and became a contributor to <i>Dateline NBC</i> and a correspondent on <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Center_with_Brian_Williams" title="Rock Center with Brian Williams">Rock Center with Brian Williams</a></i>. In January 2013, she filled in for <a href="/wiki/Kathie_Lee_Gifford" title="Kathie Lee Gifford">Kathie Lee Gifford</a> on the fourth hour of <i>Today</i>, appearing alongside <a href="/wiki/Hoda_Kotb" title="Hoda Kotb">Hoda Kotb</a>. In January 2013, she reported the story <i>Inconceivable</i> on <i><a href="/wiki/Dateline_NBC" title="Dateline NBC">Dateline NBC</a></i>.</p>
<p>Vieira was one of the hosts for NBC's coverage of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Olympics</a>. She and co-host <a href="/wiki/Matt_Lauer" title="Matt Lauer">Matt Lauer</a> were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympics_on_NBC#2012_Opening_Ceremony" title="Olympics on NBC">criticized for their NBC coverage</a> of the opening ceremony for the 2012 London Summer Olympics Games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#Bundling_citations" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This claim has too many footnotes for reading to be smooth.">excessive citations</span></a></i>]</sup> Vieira also provided some <i>Today</i> Olympics coverage during the games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vieira co-hosted the opening ceremony of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_Winter_Olympic_Games" title="2014 Winter Olympic Games">2014 Winter Olympic Games</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Matt_Lauer" title="Matt Lauer">Matt Lauer</a> and was slated to provide coverage during the two weeks of the games. On February 14, 2014, she became the first woman to solo anchor NBC's Olympics prime time coverage, alternated with Lauer while covering for an ailing <a href="/wiki/Bob_Costas" title="Bob Costas">Bob Costas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2013, Vieira announced that she would begin hosting a syndicated afternoon daytime talk show in September 2014, produced by <a href="/wiki/NBCUniversal_Television_Distribution" title="NBCUniversal Television Distribution">NBCUniversal Television Distribution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On January 4, 2016, it was announced that the show would be ending after two seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meredith_Vieira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vieira married <a href="/wiki/CBS_News" title="CBS News">CBS News</a> journalist <a href="/wiki/Richard_M._Cohen" title="Richard M. Cohen">Richard M. Cohen</a> on June 14, 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-larry_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-larry-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> They reside with their three children in <a href="/wiki/Irvington,_New_York" title="Irvington, New York">Irvington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Westchester_County,_New_York" title="Westchester County, New York">Westchester County, New York</a>. Cohen has had <a href="/wiki/Multiple_sclerosis" title="Multiple sclerosis">multiple sclerosis</a> since he was 25 years old, and has had two bouts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colon_cancer" title="Colon cancer">colon cancer</a>, one in 1999 and one a year later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lhj_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-lhj-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Vieira is a vegan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Their younger son, Gabe Cohen, is a reporter for <a href="/wiki/KOMO-TV" title="KOMO-TV">KOMO-TV</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seattle,_Washington" title="Seattle, Washington">Seattle, Washington</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meredith_Vieira_Is_Moved_to_Tears_After_Mystery_Guest_Delivers_Mother.27s_Day_Surprise.E2.80.94Watch_the_Emotional_Clip_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Meredith_Vieira_Is_Moved_to_Tears_After_Mystery_Guest_Delivers_Mother.27s_Day_Surprise.E2.80.94Watch_the_Emotional_Clip-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Balancing_family_and_career">Balancing family and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meredith_Vieira&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Balancing family and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vieira joined <i><a href="/wiki/60_Minutes" title="60 Minutes">60 Minutes</a></i> in 1989, following the birth of her first child. <a href="/wiki/Don_Hewitt" title="Don Hewitt">Don Hewitt</a>, executive producer of <i>60 Minutes</i>, allowed her to work part-time for two seasons so she could care for her child. After that, it was arranged that she would work full-time. But after two years, she became pregnant again and asked to continue the part-time arrangement. Hewitt declined her request, firing her, and deciding instead to hire someone who would work full-time. Her departure from the show garnered headlines, as a national debate started to take place about whether women could balance both family and career. She turned down opportunities to co-host CBS's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Early_Show" title="The Early Show">The Early Show</a></i> and ABC's <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i> in the mid-1990s when her children were young.</p>
<p>Vieira discusses her family and career decisions in the book <i>Divided Lives: The Public and Private Struggles of Three American Women</i> by Elsa Walsh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
