<p><b>Mary Elizabeth Winstead</b> (born November 28, 1984) is an American actress and singer, best known for her <a href="/wiki/Scream_queen" title="Scream queen">scream queen</a> roles in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horror_films" title="Horror films">horror films</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Destination_3" title="Final Destination 3">Final Destination 3</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Christmas_(2006_film)" title="Black Christmas (2006 film)">Black Christmas</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Proof" title="Death Proof">Death Proof</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Thing_(2011_film)" title="The Thing (2011 film)">The Thing</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln:_Vampire_Hunter" title="Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter">Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter</a></i> (2012), and <i><a href="/wiki/10_Cloverfield_Lane" title="10 Cloverfield Lane">10 Cloverfield Lane</a></i> (2016). She appeared as <a href="/wiki/John_McClane" title="John McClane">John McClane</a>'s daughter Lucy in <i><a href="/wiki/Live_Free_or_Die_Hard" title="Live Free or Die Hard">Live Free or Die Hard</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/A_Good_Day_to_Die_Hard" title="A Good Day to Die Hard">A Good Day to Die Hard</a></i>, the fourth and fifth films in the <a href="/wiki/Die_Hard_(film_series)" title="Die Hard (film series)"><i>Die Hard</i> franchise</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ramona_Flowers" title="Ramona Flowers">Ramona Flowers</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Scott_Pilgrim_vs._the_World" title="Scott Pilgrim vs. the World">Scott Pilgrim vs. the World</a></i>, and Kate Hannah, an alcoholic struggling with sobriety in the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Smashed_(film)" title="Smashed (film)">Smashed</a></i>.</p>
<p>Her singing career started in 2013, with her formation of the music duo <a href="/wiki/Got_a_Girl" title="Got a Girl">Got a Girl</a> with <a href="/wiki/Dan_the_Automator" title="Dan the Automator">Dan the Automator</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Elizabeth_Winstead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The youngest of five children<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-snmag_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-snmag-1">[1]</a></sup> of Betty Lou (Knight) Winstead and James Ronald Winstead, Mary Elizabeth Winstead was born in <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mount,_North_Carolina" title="Rocky Mount, North Carolina">Rocky Mount, North Carolina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackchristmas_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-blackchristmas-3">[3]</a></sup> Her grandfather was a cousin of actress <a href="/wiki/Ava_Gardner" title="Ava Gardner">Ava Gardner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> When she was five years old, her family moved to <a href="/wiki/Sandy,_Utah" title="Sandy, Utah">Sandy, Utah</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City_metropolitan_area" title="Salt Lake City metropolitan area">Salt Lake City suburb</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackchristmas_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-blackchristmas-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-5">[5]</a></sup> She attended Peruvian Park Elementary,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> where she took advanced classes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-5">[5]</a></sup> A talented dancer and singer, Winstead studied dance in a <a href="/wiki/Joffrey_Ballet" title="Joffrey Ballet">Joffrey Ballet</a> summer program in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" title="Chicago, Illinois">Chicago, Illinois</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-5">[5]</a></sup> and sang in the International Children's Choir.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tribute_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-tribute-7">[7]</a></sup> During her youth, Winstead initially hoped to pursue a career as a ballerina and appeared in local ballet productions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-5">[5]</a></sup> As she entered her teens Winstead was forced to quit ballet due to her height. She later stated: "I realized pretty early on that I was already too tall by the time I was 13... You know, your body has to stay that way for your entire life, and it's pretty hard on your muscles and your bones".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackchristmas_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-blackchristmas-3">[3]</a></sup> Realizing that a dance career was unlikely, Winstead turned to acting;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blackchristmas_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-blackchristmas-3">[3]</a></sup> as she was still a teenager at the time, this required her to be <a href="/wiki/Homeschooling" title="Homeschooling">homeschooled</a> through most of high school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Elizabeth_Winstead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1997.E2.80.932005:_Early_acting_credits">1997–2005: Early acting credits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Elizabeth_Winstead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1997–2005: Early acting credits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Winstead appeared in a production of <i><a href="/wiki/Joseph_and_the_Amazing_Technicolor_Dreamcoat" title="Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat">Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Donny_Osmond" title="Donny Osmond">Donny Osmond</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gq_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-gq-10">[10]</a></sup> After her brief performance in that show, Winstead began making appearances in various television series and earned guest roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Touched_by_an_Angel" title="Touched by an Angel">Touched by an Angel</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Promised_Land_(1996_TV_series)" title="Promised Land (1996 TV series)">Promised Land</a></i>. She gained her first major role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Passions_characters#B" title="List of Passions characters">Jessica Bennett</a> in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Passions" title="Passions">Passions</a></i>, from 1999 to 2000. She left to pursue other interests, later stating, "I really had one of the smallest roles on <i>Passions</i>, so I didn't get too involved... I was able to leave pretty easily".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Winstead's next significant television role was in the short-lived <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Wolf_Lake" title="Wolf Lake">Wolf Lake</a></i> (2001–02), as the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Tim_Matheson" title="Tim Matheson">Tim Matheson</a>'s character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deseretnews_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-deseretnews-12">[12]</a></sup> The series was canceled after ten episodes.</p>
<p>In 2004, Winstead played a supporting role in <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Made-for-television" title="Made-for-television">made-for-television</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Monster_Island_(film)" title="Monster Island (film)">Monster Island</a></i>. She was offered a role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Cinderella_Story" title="A Cinderella Story">A Cinderella Story</a></i>, but turned it down as she had just graduated from school and was going on a cruise with friends (where she met Riley Stearns, her future husband).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In 2005 she had a minor role in the <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">indie</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy" title="Comedy">comedy</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Checking_Out_(2005_film)" title="Checking Out (2005 film)">Checking Out</a></i>, then a larger role as the main antagonist of the successful <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Pictures">Walt Disney Pictures</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Sky_High_(2005_film)" title="Sky High (2005 film)">Sky High</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.932011:_Mainstream_attention">2006–2011: Mainstream attention</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Elizabeth_Winstead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006–2011: Mainstream attention">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Winstead then began working with filmmakers <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Wong_(producer)" title="James Wong (producer)">James Wong</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glen_Morgan" title="Glen Morgan">Glen Morgan</a>, previously known for their contributions to <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">The X-Files</a></i>. She starred in the 2006 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horror_movie" title="Horror movie">horror movie</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Destination_3" title="Final Destination 3">Final Destination 3</a></i> (which Wong directed and Morgan produced) as lead character <a href="/wiki/Wendy_Christensen" title="Wendy Christensen">Wendy Christensen</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> the "beleaguered heroine who experiences the premonition" that sets the story in motion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The production was a commercial success, but received a mixed response from critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Winstead's performance fared positively with reviewers;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/James_Berardinelli" title="James Berardinelli">James Berardinelli</a> stated she "does as competent a job as one could expect in these dire circumstances,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> while Felix Gonzalez, Jr found her "likeable" in her role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Winstead would collaborate again with Morgan and Wong later that year, in the slasher-film <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Christmas_(2006_film)" title="Black Christmas (2006 film)">Black Christmas</a></i>. The movie, a loose remake of the 1974 <a href="/wiki/Black_Christmas_(1974_film)" title="Black Christmas (1974 film)">film of the same name</a>, follows a group of sorority sisters who are stalked and murdered by the house's former inhabitants during a winter storm. It received poor reviews,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> but earned her a nomination for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scream_Queen" title="Scream Queen">Scream Queen</a> at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Scream_Awards" title="Scream Awards">Scream Awards</a>. Winstead got a chance to lampoon horror scream queens when <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">Tonight Show</a></i> host <a href="/wiki/Jay_Leno" title="Jay Leno">Jay Leno</a>, unaware of who she was, knocked on her front door and included her in a comedy segment spoofing horror films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>She appeared in <a href="/wiki/Emilio_Estevez" title="Emilio Estevez">Emilio Estevez</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Bobby_(2006_film)" title="Bobby (2006 film)">Bobby</a></i>, a 2006 film depicting the last hours of <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert F. Kennedy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bobby_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-bobby-22">[22]</a></sup> Winstead became interested in <i>Bobby</i> after learning that <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hopkins" title="Anthony Hopkins">Anthony Hopkins</a> would appear in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bobby_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-bobby-22">[22]</a></sup> A moderate <a href="/wiki/Box_office" title="Box office">box office</a> success in selected theaters, <i>Bobby</i> received mixed reviews<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> with many criticisms directed at the film's script.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The film's cast was nominated for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award_for_Best_Cast_in_a_Motion_Picture" title="Screen Actors Guild Award for Best Cast in a Motion Picture">Screen Actors Guild Award for Best Cast in a Motion Picture</a>, but won the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Film_Festival" title="Hollywood Film Festival">Hollywood Film Festival Award</a> for Best Ensemble Cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>

<p>2007 saw Winstead appear in a pair of high-profile event films. <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">Quentin Tarantino</a> cast her as a well-intentioned but vapid and naïve actress in <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Proof" title="Death Proof">Death Proof</a></i>, his high-speed segment of the double–feature <a href="/wiki/Exploitation_film" title="Exploitation film">exploitation</a> thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Grindhouse_(film)" title="Grindhouse (film)">Grindhouse</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> She appeared alongside <a href="/wiki/Rosario_Dawson" title="Rosario Dawson">Rosario Dawson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tracie_Thoms" title="Tracie Thoms">Tracie Thoms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zo%C3%AB_Bell" title="Zoë Bell">Zoë Bell</a>, and Kurt Russell in the movie' part, which followed a <a href="/wiki/Psychopathy" title="Psychopathy">psychopathic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stunt_man" title="Stunt man">stunt man</a>, played by Russell, stalking and murdering young women. It is the second film to feature Winstead with Russell (after <i><a href="/wiki/Sky_High_(2005_film)" title="Sky High (2005 film)">Sky High</a></i>), although she only filmed scenes with Dawson, Thoms, and Bell. The production under-performed commercially but attracted significant media buzz and critical acclaim,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo-grindhouse_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-grindhouse-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> magazine noted that <i>Death Proof</i> "proves its worth as a stand-alone feature" and found Winstead's "emergence" to be "one agreeable plus [to the movie]".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Her next film appearance of the year was opposite <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Willis" title="Bruce Willis">Bruce Willis</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Live_Free_or_Die_Hard" title="Live Free or Die Hard">Live Free or Die Hard</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-snmag_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-snmag-1">[1]</a></sup> portraying <a href="/wiki/John_McClane" title="John McClane">John McClane</a>'s estranged daughter, <a href="/wiki/John_McClane#Lucy_Gennero-McClane" title="John McClane">Lucy</a>. The movie grossed US$383.5 million and was highly acclaimed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo-d4_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-d4-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>She starred in a lead role in <i><a href="/wiki/Make_It_Happen_(film)" title="Make It Happen (film)">Make It Happen</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Dance_in_film" title="Dance in film">dance film</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-makeithappen_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-makeithappen-33">[33]</a></sup> The film went <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Direct-to-DVD" title="Direct-to-DVD">straight to DVD</a> in the United States, and fared poorly in its UK release. Nevertheless, it proved a delight for Winstead, who once dreamed of being a dancer. Critics agreed that she was the film's best asset. Reviewer Mike Martin wrote, "Winstead infuses every moment with an amazing amount of charm".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Matthew Turner of <i>ViewLondon</i> wrote, "[Winstead] compensates for the generally poor direction."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Winstead co-starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Michael_Cera" title="Michael Cera">Michael Cera</a> in the film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scott_Pilgrim_vs._The_World" title="Scott Pilgrim vs. The World">Scott Pilgrim vs. The World</a></i>, an adaptation of the comic-book <a href="/wiki/Scott_Pilgrim" title="Scott Pilgrim">Scott Pilgrim</a>, under the direction of <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Wright" title="Edgar Wright">Edgar Wright</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Her role was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ramona_Flowers" title="Ramona Flowers">Ramona Flowers</a>, a mysterious delivery girl and Scott's love interest. Winstead went through fight training for two months and performed most of her own stunts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Filming occurred from March<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> to August 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> and the film was released in late 2010, to critical acclaim but poor box office returns. Winstead's performance was well received generally, and earned her a <a href="/wiki/Teen_Choice_Awards" title="Teen Choice Awards">Teen Choice Awards</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/2011_Teen_Choice_Awards#Choice_Movie" title="2011 Teen Choice Awards">Choice Actress Action</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Winstead was cast as the lead female in the 2011 <a href="/wiki/The_Thing_(2011_film)" title="The Thing (2011 film)">prequel</a> film to 1982's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Thing_(1982_film)" title="The Thing (1982 film)">The Thing</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thing_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-thing-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> which followed a group of scientists who discover an alien buried deep in the ice of Antarctica, realizing too late that it is still alive. Winstead portrayed paleontologist Dr. Kate Lloyd, a character she based on her sister, a neurologist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aintitcool_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-aintitcool-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cinemablend_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-cinemablend-44">[44]</a></sup> The production received a U.S. theatrical release on October 14, 2011, garnering a mixed critical reception and little commercial interest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Critics singled out Winstead for praise in her performance,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> with <i><a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Weekly" title="Las Vegas Weekly">Las Vegas Weekly</a></i> asserting that she "makes for an appealing protagonist, and Kate is portrayed as competent without being thrust into some unlikely action-hero role".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.932014:_Indie-film_route">2012–2014: Indie-film route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Elizabeth_Winstead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–2014: Indie-film route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Winstead appeared opposite <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Paul" title="Aaron Paul">Aaron Paul</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Smashed_(film)" title="Smashed (film)">Smashed</a></i>, an <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/James_Ponsoldt" title="James Ponsoldt">James Ponsoldt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smashed_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-smashed-49">[49]</a></sup> She said that the film was shot in 19 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smashedinter_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-smashedinter-50">[50]</a></sup> Screened during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Sundance_Film_Festival" title="2012 Sundance Film Festival">2012 Sundance Film Festival</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Winstead's performance earned her rave reviews,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> with <i><a href="/wiki/JoBlo.com" title="JoBlo.com">JoBlo.com</a></i> calling it: "the type of performance that could be Award-worthy if given the right kind of build-up by whichever studio picks it up".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> The film was released theatrically on October 12, 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smashedrelease3_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-smashedrelease3-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> and saw Winstead snagging the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_International_Film_Festival" title="Dallas International Film Festival">Dallas International Film Festival Award</a> for Best Actress. During a promotional interview for the movie with website <a href="/wiki/Collider.com" title="Collider.com">Collider.com</a>, she expressed pride in working on an independent project: "It's something I've been trying to do for years and years [...] It's almost like <a href="/wiki/Checking_Out_(2005_film)" title="Checking Out (2005 film)">my first movie</a> in a weird way, cause it's my first movie in this world, which is a world I've been trying to break into. [I've wanted] to be around filmmakers that are trying new things and not part of the system, so to speak, and they're doing things on their own terms."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Winstead played the role of <a href="/wiki/Mary_Todd_Lincoln" title="Mary Todd Lincoln">Mary Todd Lincoln</a> opposite <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Walker_(actor)" title="Benjamin Walker (actor)">Benjamin Walker</a> in the film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln:_Vampire_Hunter_(film)" title="Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter (film)">Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hunter_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-hunter-57">[57]</a></sup> The film was also given a 2012 release, to a mixed response,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> but critics praised Winstead's performance. The <a href="/wiki/San_Jose_Mercury_News" title="San Jose Mercury News">San Jose Mercury News</a> called Winstead "a standout",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Illinois_Times" title="Illinois Times">Illinois Times</a> film critic wrote, "Winstead humanizes Mary [Todd Lincoln] by giving her a fiery wit and sense of resolve in the face of considerable adversity".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Winstead was commended for scenes opposite Benjamin Walker, with <i><a href="/wiki/Little_White_Lies_(magazine)" title="Little White Lies (magazine)">Little White Lies</a></i> writing that they shared: "a sweet chemistry that gives their handful of scenes an endearing warmth".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>Winstead was also cast in <a href="/wiki/Roman_Coppola" title="Roman Coppola">Roman Coppola</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_Glimpse_Inside_the_Mind_of_Charles_Swan_III" title="A Glimpse Inside the Mind of Charles Swan III">A Glimpse Inside the Mind of Charles Swan III</a></i>, as Kate, "the best friend of [Charlie Sheen's] girlfriend who just broke up with him. She is not so supportive of their relationship."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-insidemovies.ew.com_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-insidemovies.ew.com-62">[62]</a></sup> The film reunited her with <a href="/wiki/Jason_Schwartzman" title="Jason Schwartzman">Jason Schwartzman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aubrey_Plaza" title="Aubrey Plaza">Aubrey Plaza</a> her collaborators on <i>Scott Pilgrim vs. the World</i>. Winstead was cast in <i><a href="/wiki/A.C.O.D." title="A.C.O.D.">A.C.O.D.</a></i> as Lauren Stinger, "long-time girlfriend of Carter (Scott) and the rock in his increasingly chaotic life."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-acod_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-acod-63">[63]</a></sup> She appeared with <a href="/wiki/Adam_Scott_(actor)" title="Adam Scott (actor)">Adam Scott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Jenkins" title="Richard Jenkins">Richard Jenkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jane_Lynch" title="Jane Lynch">Jane Lynch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Alba" title="Jessica Alba">Jessica Alba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clark_Duke" title="Clark Duke">Clark Duke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amy_Poehler" title="Amy Poehler">Amy Poehler</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Catherine_O%27Hara" title="Catherine O'Hara">Catherine O'Hara</a>. Although the film received mixed reviews,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Winstead's acting was singled out by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_Post" title="Washington Post">Washington Post</a></i>: “Winstead brings surprising depth to a small role, in which she has little to do except wait for her boyfriend to grow up, or to at least let go of his cynicism about love.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> <i>ScreenRant</i> critic Ben Kendrick wrote: “Mary Elizabeth Winstead and Jessica Alba also deliver in their contributions – though both of their characters are mainly designed to be mirrors for Carter to examine his own life and choices.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> The movie premiered at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a> and was later given a limited release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012 Winstead and <a href="/wiki/Topher_Grace" title="Topher Grace">Topher Grace</a> appeared in <i><a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/The_Beauty_Inside" title="The Beauty Inside">The Beauty Inside</a></i>, an "<a href="/wiki/Social_film" title="Social film">interactive social film</a>". It was broken into six filmed episodes interspersed with interactive storytelling, all on Alex (the main character)'s <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> timeline. He awakes each day with a different appearance; Winstead appeared as Leah, Alex's love interest. The <a href="/wiki/Web_television" title="Web television">web series</a> served as an advertising campaign for <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toshiba" title="Toshiba">Toshiba</a>; it ran from 16 August through 20 September 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> Winstead collaborated again with James Ponsoldt in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Spectacular_Now" title="The Spectacular Now">The Spectacular Now</a></i> as Holly, the sister of <a href="/wiki/Miles_Teller" title="Miles Teller">Miles Teller</a>'s lead character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> She appeared with <a href="/wiki/Brie_Larson" title="Brie Larson">Brie Larson</a>, her co-star in <i>Scott Pilgrim vs. the World</i>, and with <a href="/wiki/Shailene_Woodley" title="Shailene Woodley">Shailene Woodley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyle_Chandler" title="Kyle Chandler">Kyle Chandler</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Odenkirk" title="Bob Odenkirk">Bob Odenkirk</a>. The film, based on the novel of the same name by Tim Tharp, opened to favorable reviews and good results in its limited release with a total gross of $6 million over a production budget of $2.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-specnow_71-0"><a href="#cite_note-specnow-71">[71]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> Next, Winstead reprised her role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Good_Day_to_Die_Hard" title="A Good Day to Die Hard">A Good Day to Die Hard</a></i>, shooting her scenes in only one day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smashedinter_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-smashedinter-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-agdtdh1_75-0"><a href="#cite_note-agdtdh1-75">[75]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-agdtdh2_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-agdtdh2-76">[76]</a></sup> Winstead stated in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a></i> that she was not expecting another sequel but "[had fun reprising] the father-daughter rapport."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoointerview_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoointerview-77">[77]</a></sup> Winstead's scene was only available in the theatrical version, and was cut from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unrated_films" title="Unrated films">unrated version</a>.</p>
<p>In 2013 Winstead was given the title role in <i><a href="/wiki/Alex_of_Venice" title="Alex of Venice">Alex of Venice</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Messina" title="Chris Messina">Chris Messina</a> and penned by <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Goldberg" title="Jessica Goldberg">Jessica Goldberg</a>. She played "an environmental lawyer who is left to raise her 12-year-old son alone after her husband bolts."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faults_78-0"><a href="#cite_note-faults-78">[78]</a></sup> Premiering at the 57th <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_International_Film_Festival" title="San Francisco International Film Festival">San Francisco International Film Festival</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> reviews for the film were mostly positive, with many critics praising Winstead's part in it;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> magazine called her performance "extraordinary", saying the film: "belongs to Winstead, whose minor-key thesping proves as compelling as her heavy lifting in “<i>Smashed</i>. "Alex’s gradual metamorphosis into a richer, more fully realized young woman is accomplished in hundreds of tiny emotional brushstrokes, flitting across her girl-next-door wholesomeness in ever-shifting patterns".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> Website <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twitch_Film" title="Twitch Film">Twitch Film</a></i> wrote:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>[Winstead] has matured from her early roles in horror films, to more impressive fare such as <i>Smashed</i>. She has grace, courage, knows how to move the audience and the camera loves her. While her resume up 'til now is a mix of blockbusters (<i>Final Destination 3</i>, <i>Live Free or Die Hard</i>) and indie favorites (<i>Scott Pilgrim vs. the World</i>), this will change as she continues to grow into her profoundly blossoming career. This is an actress ready to do great things. As Alex, she always wears a look of determination, even when she's exhausted and losing the new battles thrown at her: learning how to be alone, taking care of her family and keeping the job she's worked her entire life for. Alex is a tender soul but does her damnedest to keep it together. It's an admirable trait not many can do, let alone be challenged with.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In July 2013 Winstead was reported to be starring in Stearns' debut feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Faults_(film)" title="Faults (film)">Faults</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faults_78-1"><a href="#cite_note-faults-78">[78]</a></sup> Winstead and co-star <a href="/wiki/Leland_Orser" title="Leland Orser">Leland Orser</a> were praised for their performances, with <i><a href="/wiki/Film_School_Rejects" title="Film School Rejects">Film School Rejects</a></i> calling it an "exceptional performance"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indiewire" title="Indiewire">Indiewire</a></i> saying "[Winstead's] inscrutable expression epitomizes this unique movie's enigmatic appeal", and even "ranks among her best".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> Winstead was next cast alongside <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Renner" title="Jeremy Renner">Jeremy Renner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rosemarie_DeWitt" title="Rosemarie DeWitt">Rosemarie DeWitt</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_the_Messenger_(2014_film)" title="Kill the Messenger (2014 film)">Kill the Messenger</a></i>. The thriller tells the <a href="/wiki/List_of_films_based_on_actual_events" title="List of films based on actual events">true story</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Investigative_journalist" title="Investigative journalist">investigative journalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Gary_Webb" title="Gary Webb">Gary Webb</a>. Winstead portrayed Dawn Garcia, Webb's editor at the <a href="/wiki/San_Jose_Mercury_News" title="San Jose Mercury News">San Jose Mercury News</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-messenger_86-0"><a href="#cite_note-messenger-86">[86]</a></sup> It was given a <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">limited release</a> on October 10, 2014, to favorable reviews but little interest in its theatrical run.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.E2.80.93present:_Television_and_film_roles">2015–present: Television and film roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Elizabeth_Winstead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2015–present: Television and film roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By January 2015, Winstead had been already cast as a series regular on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%26E_(TV_channel)" title="A&amp;E (TV channel)">A&amp;E</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Returned_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Returned (U.S. TV series)">US remake</a> of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_French_television_series" title="List of French television series">French</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Les_Revenants_(TV_series)" title="Les Revenants (TV series)">The Returned</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Sandrine_Holt" title="Sandrine Holt">Sandrine Holt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Agnes_Bruckner" title="Agnes Bruckner">Agnes Bruckner</a>. The adapted show followed residents in a small town whose lives are disrupted when people who have been dead for many years begin reappearing. It premiered on March 9, 2015, for a 10-episode run, to mostly critical acclaim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-returned_89-0"><a href="#cite_note-returned-89">[89]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup> The series was cancelled after its first season though, due to low ratings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup> The same year, she also made a guest-appearance on the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Comedy_Bang!_Bang!_(TV_series)_episodes" title="List of Comedy Bang! Bang! (TV series) episodes">August 6 episode</a> of <a href="/wiki/IFC_(U.S._TV_network)" title="IFC (U.S. TV network)">IFC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Comedy_Bang!_Bang!_(TV_series)" title="Comedy Bang! Bang! (TV series)">Comedy Bang! Bang!</a></i> ("Mary Elizabeth Winstead Wears an A-Line Skirt and Pointy Black Boots").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup> Winstead next portrayed the leading role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mary_Phinney" title="Mary Phinney">Mary Phinney</a> in <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a>' <a href="/wiki/Civil_rights_dramas" title="Civil rights dramas">civil rights drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Mercy_Street_(TV_series)" title="Mercy Street (TV series)">Mercy Street</a></i>. The six-part series, about two volunteer nurses on opposing sides of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">Civil War</a>, premiered on-demand on January 14, 2016, and made its broadcast debut three days later, on January 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mercy_93-0"><a href="#cite_note-mercy-93">[93]</a></sup> It rated favorably with reviewers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup> who also showed praise of Winstead's performance; <i>Variety</i> magazine described her as the "smart, capable center around which <i>Mercy Street</i> pivots" and therefore noted that she was "so good that it’s hard not to wish the show had pared down the sheer number of storylines it attempts to service in its six installments".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup> The show has since been renewed for a second season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup> due for release on January 22, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup></p>
<p>Winstead appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Swiss_Army_Man" title="Swiss Army Man">Swiss Army Man</a></i>, an indie comedy-drama directed by Daniel Kwan and Daniel Scheinert. The movie, co-starring <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Radcliffe" title="Daniel Radcliffe">Daniel Radcliffe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Dano" title="Paul Dano">Paul Dano</a>, follows a hopeless man stranded on an island who befriends an apparent dead body as he tries to get back home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-swiss_98-0"><a href="#cite_note-swiss-98">[98]</a></sup> It premiered at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a> and was released in selected theaters on June 24, to a positive reception.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup> Winstead also had a role in <a href="/wiki/John_Krasinski" title="John Krasinski">John Krasinski</a>'s dramedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollars" title="The Hollars">The Hollars</a></i>, opposite Richard Jenkins, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Kendrick" title="Anna Kendrick">Anna Kendrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mary_Kay_Place" title="Mary Kay Place">Mary Kay Place</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Margo_Martindale" title="Margo Martindale">Margo Martindale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollars_100-0"><a href="#cite_note-hollars-100">[100]</a></sup> She played the ex-girlfriend of Krasinski's struggling New York City <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graphic_novelist" title="Graphic novelist">graphic novelist</a>, who returns to his hometown after learning his mother has fallen ill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup> As <i>Swiss Army Man</i>, the movie was screened at the 2016 Sundance Film Festival, and is scheduled to receive a limited US theatrical release on August 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-102"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup></p>
<p>Winstead headlined the psychological thriller <i><a href="/wiki/10_Cloverfield_Lane" title="10 Cloverfield Lane">10 Cloverfield Lane</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cloverfieldlane_103-0"><a href="#cite_note-cloverfieldlane-103">[103]</a></sup> appearing as Michelle, a woman held in a shelter with two men, who claim the outside world is affected by a widespread chemical attack. The film, directed by <a href="/wiki/Dan_Trachtenberg" title="Dan Trachtenberg">Dan Trachtenberg</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cellar_104-0"><a href="#cite_note-cellar-104">[104]</a></sup> was released on March 11, 2016, to a widely positive reception from critics who, according to website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, agreed that the movie "makes the most of its confined setting and outstanding cast".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-105"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Express" title="Daily Express">Daily Express</a></i> found Winstead "sympathetic" and remarked that she "creates a character who is smart and resourceful",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup> while writer James Berardinelli called her a "good choice" to play the heroine as she is "strong yet feminine".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-107"><a href="#cite_note-107">[107]</a></sup> <i>10 Cloverfield Lane</i> had the highest debut at the box office for Winstead in a leading role, with over US$24 million grossed on its opening weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-108"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup></p>
<p>Winstead appears in CBS' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Political_film" title="Political film">political</a> comic thriller <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BrainDead_(TV_series)" title="BrainDead (TV series)">BrainDead</a></i>, as leading character Laurel, "the daughter of a Democratic political dynasty who left <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> to become a documentary filmmaker, but is pulled back into the family business when her brother needs political help".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-109"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup> The series' debuted on June 13, 2016 and its first season will consist of 13 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-braindead_110-0"><a href="#cite_note-braindead-110">[110]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-braindead2_111-0"><a href="#cite_note-braindead2-111">[111]</a></sup> The series received mixed to positive reviews by critics,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-112"><a href="#cite_note-112">[112]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-113"><a href="#cite_note-113">[113]</a></sup> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Magazine" title="New York Magazine">New York Magazine</a> calling Winstead's casting the "smartest decision the makers made",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-114"><a href="#cite_note-114">[114]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a> says she "makes an eminently watchable, formidable heroine".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-115"><a href="#cite_note-115">[115]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2016, it was reported that Winstead will be starring in Stearns' next feature film, <i>The Art of Self-Defense</i>. According to Stearns, it is a "karate movie".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kungfu_116-0"><a href="#cite_note-kungfu-116">[116]</a></sup> In September, it was announced that Winstead will be joining the third season of <i><a href="/wiki/Fargo_(TV_series)" title="Fargo (TV series)">Fargo</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-117"><a href="#cite_note-117">[117]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singing_career">Singing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Elizabeth_Winstead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Singing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Winstead has expressed her interest in singing, but initially did not plan on pursuing it as a career. "I wasn't ever really going to be a singer, but it's just something I've always loved."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-118"><a href="#cite_note-118">[118]</a></sup> For her part in 2007's <i>Death Proof</i>, Tarantino had Winstead sing an <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> cover of <a href="/wiki/The_Shirelles" title="The Shirelles">The Shirelles</a>' hit recording "<a href="/wiki/Baby_It%27s_You" title="Baby It's You">Baby It's You</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-radiofree_119-0"><a href="#cite_note-radiofree-119">[119]</a></sup> She was asked without warning to perform the song and the cast were reportedly "gob-smacked" by her singing. Winstead and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_producer" title="Music producer">music producer</a> Thai Long Ly eventually co-wrote a song, called "Warmth of Him".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-120"><a href="#cite_note-120">[120]</a></sup> Although first rumored to be a <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">pre-release single</a>, Winstead confirmed that she was just exploring her interest and did not plan at the time on releasing any <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_albums" title="Music albums">music albums</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Got_a_Girl">Got a Girl</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Elizabeth_Winstead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Got a Girl">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Got_a_Girl" title="Got a Girl">Got a Girl</a></div>
<p>In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Complex_(magazine)" title="Complex (magazine)">Complex</a></i> magazine for the June/July 2012 issue, Winstead revealed she was working with <a href="/wiki/Dan_the_Automator" title="Dan the Automator">Dan the Automator</a> and was indeed planning on releasing a music album. The duo formed the band <a href="/wiki/Got_a_Girl" title="Got a Girl">Got a Girl</a> the following year, and released their demo “You &amp; Me” on May 21, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-121"><a href="#cite_note-121">[121]</a></sup> The album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/I_Love_You_but_I_Must_Drive_Off_This_Cliff_Now" title="I Love You but I Must Drive Off This Cliff Now">I Love You but I Must Drive Off This Cliff Now</a></i>, was released on July 22, 2014 via Bulk Recordings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-122"><a href="#cite_note-122">[122]</a></sup> The first single, "Did We Live Too Fast" premiered on June 3,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-123"><a href="#cite_note-123">[123]</a></sup> and its music video, directed by <a href="/wiki/Hope_Larson" title="Hope Larson">Hope Larson</a>, premiered online on June 16.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-124"><a href="#cite_note-124">[124]</a></sup> The album features heavy influences of <a href="/wiki/French_pop_music" title="French pop music">French pop music</a>, with Winstead detailing that the inspiration behind the project came from "French ’60s pop—<a href="/wiki/Jane_Birkin" title="Jane Birkin">Jane Birkin</a>, and stuff like that. It’s kind of married with Dan’s sensibility, which is his beats and a little bit of that low-key hip-hop vibe. So it makes for something that’s very unique; it’s very lounge-y and light. It's got a little bit of a French quality."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-complex.com_125-0"><a href="#cite_note-complex.com-125">[125]</a></sup> All lyrics were written by Winstead while all music was composed by Dan the Automator.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guest-appearances">Guest-appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Elizabeth_Winstead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Guest-appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Winstead was also featured on the songs "The Agony" and "Look Across The Sky" on <a href="/wiki/Deltron_3030" title="Deltron 3030">Deltron 3030</a>'s second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Event_2" title="Event 2">Event 2</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-126"><a href="#cite_note-126">[126]</a></sup> released on September 30, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-127"><a href="#cite_note-127">[127]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2016, Honus Honus from <a href="/wiki/Experimental_music" title="Experimental music">experimental</a> band <a href="/wiki/Man_Man" title="Man Man">Man Man</a> released a song on <a href="/wiki/SoundCloud" title="SoundCloud">SoundCloud</a>, "Santa Monica" that features Winstead. In December 2016, <a href="/wiki/Portugal._The_Man" title="Portugal. The Man">Portugal. The Man</a> released the single and video for "Noise Pollution (Version A, Vocal Up Mix 1.3)" from their upcoming album, featuring Winstead and Zoe Manville.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-128"><a href="#cite_note-128">[128]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Elizabeth_Winstead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Winstead has been married since 2010 to Riley Stearns, a film director and television writer from <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-129"><a href="#cite_note-129">[129]</a></sup> whom she met at age 18 on an ocean cruise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Phoebe_Reilly_130-0"><a href="#cite_note-Phoebe_Reilly-130">[130]</a></sup> Winstead starred in and produced Stearns' debut feature film, <i><a href="/wiki/Faults_(film)" title="Faults (film)">Faults</a></i> in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faults_78-2"><a href="#cite_note-faults-78">[78]</a></sup> She is set to appear in Stearns' second feature film, <i>The Art of Self-Defense</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kungfu_116-1"><a href="#cite_note-kungfu-116">[116]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was one of the victims of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/August_2014_celebrity_photo_leaks" title="August 2014 celebrity photo leaks">2014 celebrity pictures hack</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-131"><a href="#cite_note-131">[131]</a></sup></p>
