<p><b>Mary Queeny</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar"><big>ماري كويني</big></span>‎‎; 1913–2003) was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egyptian_people" title="Egyptian people">Egyptian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a> and <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">film producer</a>.</p>
<p>She was born Mary Boutros Younis in 1913 to a Christian family in <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>. Her mother's cousin was Asaad Dagher, a writer and journalist at the <i><a href="/wiki/Al-Ahram" title="Al-Ahram">Al-Ahram</a></i> newspaper. In 1923 she moved to <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a> with her aunt, actress and film producer <a href="/wiki/Assia_Dagher" title="Assia Dagher">Assia Dagher</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Obit_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Obit-1">[1]</a></sup> She took the name Mary Queeny when she was 12. She married Egyptian film actor and director <a href="/wiki/Ahmed_Galal" title="Ahmed Galal">Ahmed Galal</a> in 1940. Their son is film director <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nader_Galal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nader Galal (page does not exist)">Nader Galal</a>. Until her retirement in 1982, she produced all of the films he directed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hillauer_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hillauer-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Queeny became a popular actor and producer in a pioneering age of <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Egypt" title="Cinema of Egypt">Egyptian cinema</a>. Her first role was in 1929 in the film <i>Ghadat al-sahara</i> (<i>The Desert Beauty</i>). She went on to star in all of her aunt's subsequent films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hillauer_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hillauer-2">[2]</a></sup> She appeared in 20 films and was among the first women in Egypt to appear on screen without a <a href="/wiki/Veil" title="Veil">veil</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Obit_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Obit-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>With her husband she founded Galal Films in 1942; in 1944 it became Gala Studios. During the Golden Age of Egyptian film, it was one of the five largest studios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hillauer_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hillauer-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1958 she established a film colour processing laboratory, which in 1963 she sold to the Misr Company, now Misr International, which was later acquired by <a href="/wiki/Youssef_Chahine" title="Youssef Chahine">Youssef Chahine</a> and his niece, Marianne Khoury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Obit_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Obit-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Queeny died on 23 November 2003 in <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a> of a heart attack. She was 90.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Obit_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Obit-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
