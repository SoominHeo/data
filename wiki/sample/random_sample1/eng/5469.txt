<p><b>Mary B. Timony</b> (born October 17, 1970) is an American <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a>, guitarist, keyboardist, and <a href="/wiki/Viola" title="Viola">violist</a>. She has been a member of the bands <a href="/wiki/Helium_(band)" title="Helium (band)">Helium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Autoclave_(band)" title="Autoclave (band)">Autoclave</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wild_Flag" title="Wild Flag">Wild Flag</a>, and currently fronts <a href="/wiki/Ex_Hex_(band)" title="Ex Hex (band)">Ex Hex</a>.</p>
<p>Timony's music is often heavy and dark, frequently using drones, beats, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Musical_mode" title="Musical mode">modal</a> melodies reminiscent of European <a href="/wiki/Medieval_music" title="Medieval music">Medieval music</a>. She uses a number of alternate guitar tunings, most prominent of which is DADGAE, which allows for the execution of melodic passages.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Timony&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Timony was born to James and Joan Timony of <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, and raised in the neighborhoods of <a href="/wiki/Glover_Park" title="Glover Park">Glover Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wesley_Heights" title="Wesley Heights">Wesley Heights</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swartz2013_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Swartz2013-1">[1]</a></sup> As a teenager, she attended the <a href="/wiki/Duke_Ellington_School_of_the_Arts" title="Duke Ellington School of the Arts">Duke Ellington School of the Arts</a> in <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_(Washington,_D.C.)" title="Georgetown (Washington, D.C.)">Georgetown</a> where she played guitar in the jazz band and also studied <a href="/wiki/Viola" title="Viola">viola</a>. Her guitar teacher Tom Newman recalled to interviewers: “She came to us a prodigy. You can’t teach what she has.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swartz2013_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Swartz2013-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1990–91 Timony played guitar and shared lead vocals in the Washington, D.C.-based band <a href="/wiki/Autoclave_(band)" title="Autoclave (band)">Autoclave</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ankeny_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ankeny-2">[2]</a></sup> She later relocated to <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, where she graduated from <a href="/wiki/Boston_University" title="Boston University">Boston University</a> with a degree in English literature and formed the band <a href="/wiki/Helium_(band)" title="Helium (band)">Helium</a> in the summer of 1992, recording two albums and three EPs with the group between 1994 and 1997. Helium disbanded in 1998, whereupon Timony embarked on her solo career, recording albums in 2000 and 2002 (<i>Mountains</i> and <i>The Golden Dove</i>).</p>
<p>In the mid-2000s, Timony moved back to D.C. In 2005, Timony joined with drummer Devin Ocampo; her album <i>Ex Hex</i>, released on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lookout!_Records" title="Lookout! Records">Lookout! Records</a> label, features the two performing together as a duo. In the same year, she contributed vocals to <a href="/wiki/Team_Sleep" title="Team Sleep">Team Sleep</a>'s self-titled album on the tracks "Tomb of Liegia" and "King Diamond."</p>
<p>Her most recent solo album, <i>The Shapes We Make</i>, was released on the <a href="/wiki/Kill_Rock_Stars" title="Kill Rock Stars">Kill Rock Stars</a> label on May 8, 2007. A <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for "Sharp Shooter" was produced by the art collective <a href="/wiki/Paper_Rad" title="Paper Rad">Paper Rad</a>.</p>
<p>In early 2009, Mary Timony formed a new band, Pow Wow, with Jonah R. Takagi and Winston H. Yu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> As of June 2009 the group added T. J. Lipple and changed its name to Soft Power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2010, Mary Timony and members of <a href="/wiki/Sleater-Kinney" title="Sleater-Kinney">Sleater-Kinney</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Minders" title="The Minders">The Minders</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Quasi" title="Quasi">Quasi</a> announced that they were working on a new album under the moniker <a href="/wiki/Wild_Flag" title="Wild Flag">Wild Flag</a>. It was released on <a href="/wiki/Merge_Records" title="Merge Records">Merge Records</a> on September 13, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>After Wild Flag's breakup, Timony formed <a href="/wiki/Ex_Hex_(band)" title="Ex Hex (band)">Ex Hex</a> with Fire Tapes bassist Betsy Wright, and <a href="/wiki/The_Aquarium_(band)" title="The Aquarium (band)">The Aquarium</a> drummer Laura Harris. Their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Rips_(album)" title="Rips (album)">Rips</a></i> was released on <a href="/wiki/Merge_Records" title="Merge Records">Merge Records</a> in October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her brother, Patrick Timony, is the keyboard player for the band <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Picture_is_Dead" title="The Picture is Dead">The Picture is Dead</a>.</p>
<p>There is a reference to Mary Timony in the lyrics to the song "Your Bruise" by the American indie rock band <a href="/wiki/Death_Cab_for_Cutie" title="Death Cab for Cutie">Death Cab for Cutie</a>, from their 1998 debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Something_About_Airplanes" title="Something About Airplanes">Something About Airplanes</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Side_projects">Side projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Timony&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Side projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to her work with Autoclave, Helium, and the Mary Timony Band, Timony has occasionally collaborated or recorded with other groups. In 1999, Timony recorded a four-song vinyl EP with <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Brownstein" title="Carrie Brownstein">Carrie Brownstein</a> of the band <a href="/wiki/Sleater-Kinney" title="Sleater-Kinney">Sleater-Kinney</a>, in a duo called The Spells. In 2000, she recorded a six-song CD with Anna Johansson and Erin Maclean, entitled <i>Green 4</i>.</p>
<p>In 1995, Timony recorded the song "All Dressed Up In Dreams" with <a href="/wiki/Stephin_Merritt" title="Stephin Merritt">Stephin Merritt</a>, on <a href="/wiki/The_6ths" title="The 6ths">The 6ths</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/Wasps%27_Nests" title="Wasps' Nests">Wasps' Nests</a></i>. She also recorded vocals for a one-off alt-country project called Lincoln '65. This project's sole output was a 7" single released by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Slow_River_Records&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Slow River Records (page does not exist)">Slow River Records</a> in 1996, labeled simply <i>Lincoln '65</i>, and contained two tracks, "Dreams" and "Jellyfish".</p>
<p>Timony collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Team_Sleep" title="Team Sleep">Team Sleep</a> on Tomb of Liegia and King Diamond on their 2005 self-titled album.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Films">Films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mary_Timony&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Timony had a role in the 1997 <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/All_Over_Me_(film)" title="All Over Me (film)">All Over Me</a></i> as lead singer/guitarist of the fictional girl rock band Coochie Pop, along with another real-life singer/guitarist, <a href="/wiki/Leisha_Hailey" title="Leisha Hailey">Leisha Hailey</a>. They performed the Helium song "Hole in the Ground" in the movie.</p>
<p>She also appeared in a short film by the name Dream Machine in 2000, directed by Brett Vapnek.</p>
