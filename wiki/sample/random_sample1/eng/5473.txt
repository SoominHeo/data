<p><b>Merrin Dungey</b> (born August 6, 1971) is an American film and television actress, known for her roles on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_King_of_Queens" title="The King of Queens">The King of Queens</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Alias_(TV_series)" title="Alias (TV series)">Alias</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Summerland_(TV_series)" title="Summerland (TV series)">Summerland</a></i>. She also appeared as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ursula_(The_Little_Mermaid)" title="Ursula (The Little Mermaid)">Ursula</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_(TV_series)" title="Once Upon a Time (TV series)">Once Upon a Time</a></i>. In October 2016, Dungey began playing series regular Maxine Bohen on the ABC legal drama <i><a href="/wiki/Conviction_(2016_TV_series)" title="Conviction (2016 TV series)">Conviction</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Merrin_Dungey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dungey was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MDungeyRaised-UncoveringAliasBook_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MDungeyRaised-UncoveringAliasBook-1">[1]</a></sup> the younger daughter of Don Dungey, a general services manager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> As a child, she was active in ballet and dance as well as piano. Dungey was also an accomplished ice skater. Dungey graduated in 1989 from <a href="/wiki/Rio_Americano_High_School" title="Rio Americano High School">Rio Americano High School</a> in Sacramento, California. She earned a degree of <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UCLA" title="UCLA">UCLA</a> in 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Dungey has one sister, <a href="/wiki/Channing_Dungey" title="Channing Dungey">Channing Dungey</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Emmy_2014-06_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Emmy_2014-06-4">[4]</a></sup> who was named president of <a href="/wiki/ABC_Entertainment" title="ABC Entertainment">ABC Entertainment</a> in February 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Merrin_Dungey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dungey had small roles in Hollywood films such as <i><a href="/wiki/EDtv" title="EDtv">EDtv</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Deep_Impact_(film)" title="Deep Impact (film)">Deep Impact</a></i> and on television series such as <i><a href="/wiki/Martin_(TV_series)" title="Martin (TV series)">Martin</a>,</i> before landing the recurring role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Of_Queens#Recurring_characters" title="King Of Queens">Kelly Palmer</a> on the CBS television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_King_of_Queens" title="The King of Queens">The King of Queens</a></i>, in which she starred from 1999 to 2007. She appeared in every season in the series except for the 2002-03 season. Dungey is also well known for her role as <a href="/wiki/Francie_Calfo" title="Francie Calfo">Francie Calfo</a> on the drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Alias_(TV_series)" title="Alias (TV series)">Alias</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW_Ursula_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-EW_Ursula-6">[6]</a></sup> in which she starred from 2001 to 2003, as well as an appearance in the 2006 <a href="/wiki/Series_finale" title="Series finale">series finale</a> episode <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alias_episodes_(Season_5)#All_the_Time_in_the_World" title="Alias episodes (Season 5)">All the Time in the World</a></i>. She also had a recurring role on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">FOX</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Malcolm_in_the_Middle" title="Malcolm in the Middle">Malcolm in the Middle</a></i> as Stevie's mother, Kitty Kernaban. At one point in 2001, Dungey was appearing in all three shows (<i>The King of Queens</i>, <i>Malcolm in the Middle</i>, and <i>Alias</i>) at the same time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Emmy_2014-06_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Emmy_2014-06-4">[4]</a></sup> Dungey then portrayed Susannah in the WB drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Summerland_(TV_series)" title="Summerland (TV series)">Summerland</a></i> from 2004 to 2005.</p>
<p>She has also guest-starred on various TV series including <i><a href="/wiki/Living_Single" title="Living Single">Living Single</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Murphy_Brown" title="Murphy Brown">Murphy Brown</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Seinfeld" title="Seinfeld">Seinfeld</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friends_(TV_series)" title="Friends (TV series)">Friends</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_West_Wing" title="The West Wing">The West Wing</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Babylon_5_(TV_series)" title="Babylon 5 (TV series)">Babylon 5</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Curb_Your_Enthusiasm" title="Curb Your Enthusiasm">Curb Your Enthusiasm</a></i>. Dungey appeared in two episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i> that served as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Backdoor_pilot" title="Backdoor pilot">backdoor pilot</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Private_Practice_(TV_series)" title="Private Practice (TV series)">Private Practice</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Private_Practice_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Private_Practice-7">[7]</a></sup> but was replaced by actress <a href="/wiki/Audra_McDonald" title="Audra McDonald">Audra McDonald</a> for the actual series.</p>
<p>Most recently, Dungey guest-starred on the ABC sitcoms <i><a href="/wiki/Surviving_Suburbia" title="Surviving Suburbia">Surviving Suburbia</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Bob_Saget" title="Bob Saget">Bob Saget</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Better_Off_Ted" title="Better Off Ted">Better Off Ted</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Portia_de_Rossi" title="Portia de Rossi">Portia de Rossi</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_(TV_series)" title="Castle (TV series)">Castle</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Fillion" title="Nathan Fillion">Nathan Fillion</a> and HBO's <i><a href="/wiki/Hung_(TV_series)" title="Hung (TV series)">Hung</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jane" title="Thomas Jane">Thomas Jane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She portrayed Ellie on the <a href="/wiki/Nick_at_Nite" title="Nick at Nite">Nick at Nite</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Heights_(TV_series)" title="Hollywood Heights (TV series)">Hollywood Heights</a></i> in 2012 and the recurring role of Alissa Barnes on ABC's <i><a href="/wiki/Betrayal_(TV_series)" title="Betrayal (TV series)">Betrayal</a></i> in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Emmy_2014-06_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Emmy_2014-06-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2014 she appeared in a featured role opposite <a href="/wiki/Pierce_Brosnan" title="Pierce Brosnan">Pierce Brosnan</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Kind_of_Beautiful" title="Some Kind of Beautiful">Some Kind of Beautiful</a></i>. Also in 2014 she began portraying the recurring role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ursula_(The_Little_Mermaid)" title="Ursula (The Little Mermaid)">Ursula</a> on ABC's <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_(TV_series)" title="Once Upon a Time (TV series)">Once Upon a Time</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW_Ursula_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-EW_Ursula-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beginning October 3, 2016, Dungey began playing investigator Maxine Bohen on the ABC legal drama <i><a href="/wiki/Conviction_(2016_TV_series)" title="Conviction (2016 TV series)">Conviction</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DL_Conviction_Casting_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-DL_Conviction_Casting-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DL_Conviction_Pickup_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-DL_Conviction_Pickup-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Merrin_Dungey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dungey married Matthew Drake in a <a href="/wiki/Mariachi" title="Mariachi">Mariachi</a> style wedding ceremony in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cancun,_Mexico" title="Cancun, Mexico">Cancun, Mexico</a>, in May 2007. She gave birth to their first daughter in June 2008, and a second daughter in 2011.</p>
