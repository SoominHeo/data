<p><b>Memory Alpha</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">wiki</a> encyclopedia for topics related to the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a></i> fictional universe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Conceived by Harry Doddema and Dan Carlson in September 2003 and officially launched on December 5 of that year, it uses the <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">wiki</a> model and is hosted by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikia,_Inc." title="Wikia, Inc.">Wikia, Inc.</a> on the <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki" title="MediaWiki">MediaWiki</a> software. Memory Alpha contains over 40,000 articles in its <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> edition alone as of June 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Memory_Alpha&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, making it one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_wikis" title="List of wikis">largest wiki projects</a>. The site is also available in several other languages,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> including <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalan_language" title="Catalan language">Catalan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esperanto_language" title="Esperanto language">Esperanto</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memory_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Memory Alpha aims to create a comprehensive database for all fans, but was not conceived as a wiki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Two concerns spurred its creation: many <i>Star Trek</i> references of the time were incomplete, and the most promising would shut down regularly, like the site TrekPulse, which closed inexplicably in late 2005 to be reborn the next year as TrekCore. Doddema and Carlson christened their project Memory Alpha, after the Federation's largest information archive, from the original series episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Lights_of_Zetar" title="The Lights of Zetar">The Lights of Zetar</a>".</p>
<p>The two decided on a wiki format, which allowed for more collaboration than other formats available. As Carlson said in the Charlotte Observer, "The idea I latched onto with the wiki concept is you can spread the work around. Everyone can pitch in and go in on their own special interest." After experimenting with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TikiWiki" title="TikiWiki">TikiWiki</a> software, they switched to the <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki" title="MediaWiki">MediaWiki</a> platform, finding it less cumbersome. The platform of choice for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikimedia" title="Wikimedia">Wikimedia</a> proved to be, in their opinion, more stable and efficient, and they brought a testsite online on November 11, 2003. Memory Alpha officially launched on December 5 that year.</p>
<p>The site gained momentum in the following months, aided by a mention on the <i>Star Trek</i> fan site "TrekNation" on December 23. Memory Alpha reached 1,000 articles by January 12, 2004, but on March 23, the site's <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">database</a> was accidentally erased during an upgrade of the MediaWiki <a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a>. Although this caused six weeks of work to be lost, the project expanded to include Dutch and German versions on April 10 and May 14 respectively. It remained stable until the following year, when the fees associated with hosting the site became more than the founders could afford.</p>
<p>In February 2005, Memory Alpha switched hosting servers and joined <a href="/wiki/Wikia" title="Wikia">Wikia</a>, a free for-profit wiki-hosting company started by <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">Wikimedia Foundation</a> board members <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Wales" title="Jimmy Wales">Jimmy Wales</a> and Angela Beesley. The site remained stable on Wikia, opening a Swedish site on May 5 and a French one on November 5. It also received several distinctions that year, such as the <i>Ex Astris Excellentia</i> award from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ex_Astris_Scientia" title="Ex Astris Scientia">Ex Astris Scientia</a>, a <i>Star Trek</i> reference site, in September 2005, and it was featured as the <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Sci-Fi Channel's</a> Site of the Week for October 10, 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scifi_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-scifi-8">[8]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Voyager" title="Star Trek: Voyager">Star Trek: Voyager</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Enterprise" title="Star Trek: Enterprise">Star Trek: Enterprise</a></i> writer/producer Mike Sussman joined the community that year as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Technical issues led the MediaWiki software to believe Memory Alpha was started on November 23, 2004, and despite the inaccuracy, this date was adopted <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ex_post_facto" title="Ex post facto">ex post facto</a></i> as Memory Alpha's "birthday".</p>
<p>The latter part of 2005 and early 2006 saw several new features added to the site. Among these was a <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer review</a> process, implemented on September 21, 2005 in response to questions about the process by which articles become featured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On November 20 of that year, Memory Alpha began a "Babel" program, inspired by and modeled after that of the <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Commons" title="Wikimedia Commons">Wikimedia Commons</a>, to help users who speak the same language. Other recent innovations include an area for user projects, sometimes referred to as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject">WikiProjects</a> on other wikis, and coverage of <a href="/wiki/Fan_film" title="Fan film">fan films</a>.</p>
<p>The site has remained popular since its inception, although its growth has slowed in recent times. For instance, it was the largest project on Wikia<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> until October 2005, when its article count was surpassed by <a href="/wiki/Wookieepedia" title="Wookieepedia">Wookieepedia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uncyclopedia" title="Uncyclopedia">Uncyclopedia</a>. Today,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (June 2010)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> it has approximately 1300 registered users (80 of whom are active) and upwards of 17 million page views since its move to Wikia. As of February 2006<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Memory_Alpha&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, it receives on average, slightly more visitors and page views per day than the official <i>Star Trek</i> website according to <a href="/wiki/Alexa_Internet" title="Alexa Internet">Alexa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Memory Alpha has influenced the design of other wikicities dedicated to information about television franchises, including <a class="extiw" href="http://www.wikia.com/wiki/c:x-files" title="wikicities:c:x-files">The X-Files Wiki</a> <a class="external autonumber" href="http://x-files.wikia.com/wiki/X-Files_Wiki:About" rel="nofollow">[1]</a> and the <a class="extiw" href="http://www.wikia.com/wiki/c:24" title="wikicities:c:24">24 Wiki</a> <a class="external autonumber" href="http://24.wikia.com/wiki/Wiki_24:About" rel="nofollow">[2]</a>. It is a resource used by mainstream journalists for information on <i>Star Trek</i> related issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Influential edublogger <a href="/wiki/Will_Richardson" title="Will Richardson">Will Richardson</a> hailed the site in his 2006 book <i>Blogs, Wikis, Podcasts, and Other Powerful Web Tools for Classrooms</i> as "one of the most impressive [wikis] out there".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 12, 2007, Memory Alpha reached a milestone of 25,000 articles with the creation of the article <a class="extiw" href="http://memory-alpha.org/wiki/Robert_Iscove" title="memoryalpha:Robert Iscove">Robert Iscove</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> named Memory Alpha one of the 25 Essential Fansites in 2007. In comparing it to other <i>Star Trek</i> sites, the reviewer wrote, "Memory Alpha wins out for its handsome, intuitive presentation and its overwhelming mass".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was revealed in April 2016 that actor and writer for <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_Beyond" title="Star Trek Beyond">Star Trek Beyond</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Pegg" title="Simon Pegg">Simon Pegg</a>, used Memory Alpha as a canon resource in writing of the film, even asking the community's leaders to name and give etymology for a device in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memory_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several aspects of Memory Alpha set it apart from other reference works, such as its method of citing sources. All information must be cited from a valid source (see <a href="/wiki/Memory_Alpha#Canon_policy" title="Memory Alpha">Canon</a> section below), but rather than a "Works Cited" or "References" list, Memory Alpha prefers stand-alone inline citations, which are placed in parentheses after the sentence or section in question. For television episodes, this consists of an abbreviation for the series from which the information came (e.g. DS9 for <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Deep_Space_Nine" title="Star Trek: Deep Space Nine">Star Trek: Deep Space Nine</a></i>), followed by the name of the episode in double quotation marks. So, to cite information from the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Next_Generation" title="Star Trek: The Next Generation">Star Trek: The Next Generation</a></i> (TNG) pilot "<a href="/wiki/Encounter_at_Farpoint" title="Encounter at Farpoint">Encounter at Farpoint</a>", one would add: (TNG: "Encounter at Farpoint"). The same rule applies for films, without the series prefix and with italics in place of quotation marks. The same method of notation is also used in the printed <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Star_Trek_Encyclopedia" title="Star Trek Encyclopedia">Star Trek Encyclopedia</a></i>, which is unrelated to the Memory Alpha wiki.</p>
<p>Articles on Memory Alpha are written from two points of view: "in-universe", which are written as if the reader is a part of the <i>Star Trek</i> universe, and "production", which speak from a real-world perspective. For "in-universe" articles, behind-the-scenes information is not included in the main body of the article; rather, it is placed in a separate background section or included indented and italicized to separate it from the in-universe perspective. The latter method is used in cases where either the information is particularly important (such as conflicting information from two canon sources) or there is not enough background to justify a separate section. In most cases, the background method is preferred and italics are used sparingly.</p>
<p>Like many wikis, Memory Alpha has a section for "featured articles", those believed to represent the best the community has to offer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scifi_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-scifi-8">[8]</a></sup> The criteria for this distinction are that an article must be well written, comprehensive (which includes citing sources), accurate and undisputed – criteria any article could hypothetically fulfill. This has caused some conflict over the criteria involved (see <a href="/wiki/Memory_Alpha#Current_issues" title="Memory Alpha">Current issues</a> section). To be featured, an article must be nominated by a user and unanimously supported by at least five other users; any objections must be fixable and may be invalidated if deemed irrational or unreasonable. Each week, one of the site's featured articles becomes the "Article of the Week" to be displayed on the project home page.</p>
<p>Several methods of communication are available beyond conventional talk pages. The "community portal" section of the website is named after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ten_Forward" title="Ten Forward">Ten Forward</a>, a locale frequented by characters on <i>The Next Generation</i>. Issues discussed there range from disputes between users to new ideas on how to improve the site to upcoming projects. A separate area, the <a href="/wiki/Reference_desk" title="Reference desk">reference desk</a>, exists for discussions and questions related to what is considered canon, discrepancies between sources, and other such topics. However, "meta-Trek" topics (a term used for <i>Star Trek</i>-related topics that do not pertain in any way to Memory Alpha) are not discussed on the wiki; a separate <a href="/wiki/Internet_Relay_Chat" title="Internet Relay Chat">IRC</a> chatroom exists for these discussions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Canon_policy">Canon policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memory_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Canon policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The question of canon is a complex one and has plagued fans since <i>Star Trek</i> began in 1966.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The general policy of <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a> is that anything outside live-action television episodes and movies is <a href="/wiki/Apocrypha" title="Apocrypha">apocryphal</a>, or non-canon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> However, grey areas in this policy, especially in relation to the canonical status of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Animated_Series" title="Star Trek: The Animated Series">Star Trek: The Animated Series</a></i> (TAS), further complicate the matter and have led to many debates among fans. In light of this, Memory Alpha crafted its own unique definition of canon in relation to what may be used as a "valid resource". <i>The Animated Series</i> is included as valid, or canonical, for a number of reasons, such as the fact that <i>Star Trek</i> creator <a href="/wiki/Gene_Roddenberry" title="Gene Roddenberry">Gene Roddenberry</a> and most of the cast of <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Original_Series" title="Star Trek: The Original Series">the original series</a> were involved with it and the existence of several references to TAS events in later series.</p>
<p>Information taken from the <i>Star Trek Encyclopedia</i> and <i>Star Trek Chronology</i> is mostly accepted on Memory Alpha as well, to the extent that it does not break from established on-screen facts. Content from these sources is an acknowledged grey area of Memory Alpha's canon policy and is disregarded if deemed speculative or contradictory. Thus, in some ways they hold the same weight as novels and other publications do for <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_canon" title="Star Wars canon">Star Wars canon</a>: a "second tier" of canonicity, which is subservient to primary (on-screen) sources.</p>
<p>Other sources such as books and computer games are not included as canon, but are covered by Memory Alpha in a way which sets it apart from other <i>Trek</i> resources: books, comics, and other products are included as articles about the products (i.e. from a "production point of view"), but "in-universe" information unique or new to them is covered on the product page. For example, in the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_New_Frontier" title="Star Trek: New Frontier">Star Trek: New Frontier</a></i> line of books, a new host of characters is introduced to the Trek universe, and their vessel is known as the USS <i>Excalibur</i>. The characters, ships and information from <i>New Frontier</i> books do not receive pages of their own, but they are covered on the pages about the books. In this way, Memory Alpha remains all-inclusive while attempting to distinguish canon from apocrypha.</p>
<p>Non-canon characters and topics are instead covered at <a class="extiw" href="http://www.wikia.com/wiki/c:memory-beta:Main_Page" title="wikia:memory-beta:Main Page">Memory Beta</a>, and fan-films and other fan-created material at Star Trek Expanded Universe; both are hosted by Wikia. <a class="external text" href="http://stowiki.org/" rel="nofollow">The Star Trek Online Wiki</a>, hosted by curse.com, covers the <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_Online" title="Star Trek Online">Star Trek Online</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MMORPG" title="MMORPG">MMORPG</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Issues">Issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memory_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Organizational">Organizational</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Memory_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Organizational">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Several issues face the Memory Alpha community, one of which is the question of which articles should be given "featured" status. Under Memory Alpha's current policies, both major and minor topics are eligible; however, the question has been raised of whether the criteria for featuring should be more subjective, i.e., if a topic is not significant enough, it should not be featured regardless of how comprehensive its article is.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> There is no consensus in the Memory Alpha community about the topic, although there has been no change in featuring policy.</p>
<p>The question of what content Memory Alpha should cover has plagued the project since its creation. On the project's home page, it describes itself as "a collaborative project to create the most definitive, accurate, and accessible encyclopedia and reference for everything related to <i>Star Trek</i>", but fans often have different interpretations of what "everything" means. Sometimes, one or more users may add content about a subject – such as a website, fan club or parody – that does not fit existing pages, necessitating a community decision about whether to keep the content and, if so, what to do with it. Such incidents led to the creation of an article on <i>Star Trek</i> parodies after several questions were raised about where to put information related to, but not a part of, the <i>Star Trek</i> franchise. An example of the reverse case is "The Sunspots", a musical group consisting of <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Stewart" title="Patrick Stewart">Patrick Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Frakes" title="Jonathan Frakes">Jonathan Frakes</a>, <a href="/wiki/LeVar_Burton" title="LeVar Burton">LeVar Burton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dorn" title="Michael Dorn">Michael Dorn</a>. Although the actors involved were all prominent parts of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Next_Generation" title="Star Trek: The Next Generation">Star Trek: The Next Generation</a></i>, several articles on the group have been deleted because the community deemed the topic not directly related to the franchise. As of 2006<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Memory_Alpha&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, information about The Sunspots can be found on the different actors' pages rather than a single, separate article.</p>
<p>Perhaps the most heated issue on Memory Alpha in recent years is the controversy surrounding their canon policy. Aside from issues of length and excessive use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legalese" title="Legalese">legalese</a>, this policy has been put into question by several of the site's regular users because of its rule that articles and information which were not seen on-screen but were derived from official production sources are not considered canon on Memory Alpha. The site's archivists are divided as to exactly whether such information should be allowed as canon; some want only to allow what was seen on-screen while others wish to include that which was originally intended to be used on-screen but never made it, such as information from deleted scenes, early script drafts, etc. The latter archivists believe that such information can be included so long as it does not contradict what was seen on-screen and so long as it states where the source of the information comes from. As of July 2006<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Memory_Alpha&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, this debate has died down significantly, with most having accepted the terms of the new policy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite early concern about how to incorporate information from the 2009 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_(film)" title="Star Trek (film)">Star Trek</a></i>, which is considered a semi-reboot of the original series, Memory Alpha members found a solution to the issue. All information from this film is recorded as separate articles, which are titled according to their subject with the term "alternate reality" included in the title. This is from the term used in the movie itself to describe the new continuity. For example, the article <i>Nyota Uhura</i> discusses the character as portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Nichelle_Nichols" title="Nichelle Nichols">Nichelle Nichols</a>, while the article <i>Nyota Uhura (alternate reality)</i> discusses the character as played by <a href="/wiki/Zoe_Saldana" title="Zoe Saldana">Zoe Saldana</a>.</p>
