<p><b>Meskheti</b> (<a href="/wiki/Georgian_language" title="Georgian language">Georgian</a>: <span lang="ka" xml:lang="ka">მესხეთი</span>), also known as <b>Samtskhe</b> (<a href="/wiki/Georgian_language" title="Georgian language">Georgian</a>: <span lang="ka" xml:lang="ka">სამცხე</span>), is in a mountainous area of <a href="/wiki/Moschia" title="Moschia">Moschia</a> in southwestern <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meskheti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ancient tribes known as the <a href="/wiki/Mushki" title="Mushki">Mushki</a> (or Moschi) and <a href="/wiki/Mossynoeci" title="Mossynoeci">Mosiniks</a> (or Mossynoeci) were the first known inhabitants of the area of the modern Samtskhe-Javakheti region. Some scholars credit the Mosiniks with the invention of iron metallurgy.</p>
<p>Between the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium_BC" title="2nd millennium BC">2nd millennium BCE</a> and the 4th century BCE, Meskheti was part of the Georgian Kingdom of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diaokhi" title="Diaokhi">Diaokhi</a>. It was subsequently, until the 6th century, part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caucasian_Iberia" title="Caucasian Iberia">Kingdom of Iberia</a>.</p>
<p>During the 10th-15th centuries, this region was a part of the united Georgian Kingdom. In the 16th century it was the independent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Principality_of_Meskheti" title="Principality of Meskheti">Principality of Meskheti</a> until it was occupied and annexed by the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>.</p>
<p>In 1829-1917 the region was a part of <a href="/wiki/Tbilisi" title="Tbilisi">Tbilisi</a> Governorate (<i>Tiflisskaia Gubernia</i>), and then briefly (1918-1921) part of the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Georgia" title="Democratic Republic of Georgia">Democratic Republic of Georgia</a>. Between 1921-1990 it was a part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgian_SSR" title="Georgian SSR">Georgian SSR</a>.</p>
<p>Meskheti is now part of the <a href="/wiki/Samtskhe-Javakheti" title="Samtskhe-Javakheti">Samtskhe-Javakheti</a> region, together with <a href="/wiki/Javakheti" title="Javakheti">Javakheti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tori_(Georgia)" title="Tori (Georgia)">Tori</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meskheti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnic_groups">Ethnic groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meskheti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ethnic groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Georgians">Georgians</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meskheti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Georgians">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><i>Meskhetians</i> or <i>Meskhs</i> (Meskhi) are a subgroup of <a href="/wiki/Georgians" title="Georgians">Georgians</a>, the indigenous population of Meskheti. Meskhetians speak the <a href="/wiki/Georgian_dialects" title="Georgian dialects">Meskhetian dialect</a> and are mainly Georgian Orthodox Christians in religion, while part of them are Catholics.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Meskhetian_Turks">Meskhetian Turks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meskheti&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Meskhetian Turks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Meskhetian_Turks" title="Meskhetian Turks">Meskhetian Turks</a> are the former Georgian inhabitants of the Meskheti region of <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> along the border with <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. They were deported to <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a> during November 15–25, 1944 by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a> and settled within <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyzstan" title="Kyrgyzstan">Kyrgyzstan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>. Of the 120,000 forcibly deported in cattle-trucks a total of 10,000 perished.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Today they are dispersed over a number of other countries of the former <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>. There are 500,000 to 700,000 Meskhetian Turks in exile in <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Most <a href="/wiki/Meskhetian_Turks" title="Meskhetian Turks">Meskhetian Turks</a> are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunni" title="Sunni">Sunni</a> <a href="/wiki/Hanafi" title="Hanafi">Hanafi</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> and a minority <a href="/wiki/Twelver" title="Twelver">Twelver</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shiite" title="Shiite">Shiite</a> Muslims.</p>
