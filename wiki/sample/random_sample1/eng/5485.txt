<p><b>Globular Cluster M107</b> (also known as <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Messier_Object" title="Messier Object">Messier Object</a> 107</b> or <b>NGC 6171</b>) is the last globular cluster in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Messier_Catalogue" title="Messier Catalogue">Messier Catalogue</a>. It is a very loose <a href="/wiki/Globular_cluster" title="Globular cluster">globular cluster</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> <a href="/wiki/Ophiuchus" title="Ophiuchus">Ophiuchus</a>. It was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Pierre_M%C3%A9chain" title="Pierre Méchain">Pierre Méchain</a> in April 1782 and independently by <a href="/wiki/William_Herschel" title="William Herschel">William Herschel</a> in 1793. It wasn't until 1947 that <a href="/wiki/Helen_Sawyer_Hogg" title="Helen Sawyer Hogg">Helen Sawyer Hogg</a> added it and three other objects discovered by Méchain to the list of Messier objects.</p>
<p>M107 is close to the <a href="/wiki/Galactic_plane" title="Galactic plane">galactic plane</a> at a distance of about 20,900 <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> from <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apj742_1_51_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-apj742_1_51-3">[3]</a></sup> There are 25 known <a href="/wiki/Variable_star" title="Variable star">variable stars</a> in this cluster.</p>
