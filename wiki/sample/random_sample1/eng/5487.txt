<p><b>Messier 59</b>, also known as <b>M59</b> or <b>NGC 4621</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Elliptical_galaxy" title="Elliptical galaxy">elliptical galaxy</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> <a href="/wiki/Virgo_(constellation)" title="Virgo (constellation)">Virgo</a>. It is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Virgo_Cluster" title="Virgo Cluster">Virgo Cluster</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messier_59&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Messier 59 and the nearby elliptical galaxy <a href="/wiki/Messier_60" title="Messier 60">Messier 60</a> were both discovered by <a href="/wiki/Johann_Gottfried_Koehler" title="Johann Gottfried Koehler">Johann Gottfried Koehler</a> in April 1779 during observations of a <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">comet</a> in the same part of the sky.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jonesbook1991_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-jonesbook1991-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Charles_Messier" title="Charles Messier">Charles Messier</a> listed both in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Messier_Catalogue" title="Messier Catalogue">Messier Catalogue</a> about three days after Koehler's discovery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jonesbook1991_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-jonesbook1991-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>One supernova (1939B) has been recorded in M59; it reached a peak <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">magnitude</a> of 11.9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Messier_59&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Messier 59's core contains a <a href="/wiki/Supermassive_black_hole" title="Supermassive black hole">supermassive black hole</a>, with a mass that has been estimated to be 270 million times the <a href="/wiki/Solar_mass" title="Solar mass">mass of the Sun</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wrobel2008_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wrobel2008-6">[6]</a></sup> and counter-rotates with respect of the rest of the galaxy, being bluer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wernli2001_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wernli2001-7">[7]</a></sup> This galaxy also has an inner disk of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stars" title="Stars">stars</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mizuno1996_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mizuno1996-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Krajnovi.C4.872004_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Krajnovi.C4.872004-9">[9]</a></sup> and is very rich in <a href="/wiki/Globular_cluster" title="Globular cluster">globular clusters</a>, with a population of them that has been estimated in around 2200.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harris1991_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harris1991-10">[10]</a></sup> It has a satellite, the <a href="/wiki/Dwarf_galaxy#Ultra-compact_dwarfs" title="Dwarf galaxy">Ultra Compact Dwarf</a> galaxy <a href="/wiki/M59-UCD3" title="M59-UCD3">M59-UCD3</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
