<p><b>Messier 62</b> (also known as <b>M62</b> or <b>NGC 6266</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Globular_cluster" title="Globular cluster">globular cluster</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> <a href="/wiki/Ophiuchus" title="Ophiuchus">Ophiuchus</a>. It was discovered in 1771 by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Messier" title="Charles Messier">Charles Messier</a>.</p>
<p>M62 is at a distance of about 22,500 <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> from <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> and measures some 100 light-years across. From studies conducted in the 1970s it is known that M62 contains the high number of 89 <a href="/wiki/Variable_star" title="Variable star">variable stars</a>, many of them of the <a href="/wiki/RR_Lyrae_variable" title="RR Lyrae variable">RR Lyrae</a> type. It also contains several <a href="/wiki/Astrophysical_X-ray_source" title="Astrophysical X-ray source">X-ray sources</a>, thought to be close <a href="/wiki/Binary_star" title="Binary star">binary star</a> systems, as well as <a href="/wiki/Millisecond_pulsar" title="Millisecond pulsar">millisecond pulsars</a> in binary systems.</p>
