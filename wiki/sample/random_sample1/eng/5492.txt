<p><b>Mary Megan</b> "<b>Mare</b>" <b>Winningham</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/eɪ/ long 'a' in 'base'">eɪ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born May 16, 1959) is an American actress and singer-songwriter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emmys_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-emmys-1">[1]</a></sup> An eight-time <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> nominee, she won <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Miniseries_or_a_Movie" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie">Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or Movie</a> for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amber_Waves" title="Amber Waves">Amber Waves</a></i> in 1980 and <i><a href="/wiki/George_Wallace_(film)" title="George Wallace (film)">George Wallace</a></i> in 1998. She was also nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress</a> for the 1995 film <i><a href="/wiki/Georgia_(1995_film)" title="Georgia (1995 film)">Georgia</a></i>.</p>
<p>Winningham's other film and TV roles include <i><a href="/wiki/St._Elmo%27s_Fire_(film)" title="St. Elmo's Fire (film)">St. Elmo's Fire</a></i> (1985), <i><a href="/wiki/Miracle_Mile_(film)" title="Miracle Mile (film)">Miracle Mile</a></i> (1988), <i><a href="/wiki/Turner_%26_Hooch" title="Turner &amp; Hooch">Turner &amp; Hooch</a></i> (1989), <i><a href="/wiki/The_War_(1994_film)" title="The War (1994 film)">The War</a></i> (1994), <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dandelion_(film)" title="Dandelion (film)">Dandelion</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Swing_Vote_(2008_film)" title="Swing Vote (2008 film)">Swing Vote</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/Brothers_(2009_film)" title="Brothers (2009 film)">Brothers</a></i> (2009), <i><a href="/wiki/Mildred_Pierce_(miniseries)" title="Mildred Pierce (miniseries)">Mildred Pierce</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Hatfields_%26_McCoys_(miniseries)" title="Hatfields &amp; McCoys (miniseries)">Hatfields &amp; McCoys</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Coven" title="American Horror Story: Coven">American Horror Story: Coven</a></i> (2013) and <i><a href="/wiki/American_Horror_Story:_Hotel" title="American Horror Story: Hotel">American Horror Story: Hotel</a></i> (2015). She made her New York stage debut in the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> musical <i>10 Million Miles</i>, for which she received a <a href="/wiki/Drama_Desk_Award" title="Drama Desk Award">Drama Desk Award</a> nomination. and her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadway_(theatre)" title="Broadway (theatre)">Broadway</a> debut in the 2013 revival of <i><a href="/wiki/Picnic_(play)" title="Picnic (play)">Picnic</a></i>. In 2014, she was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award_for_Best_Featured_Actress_in_a_Play" title="Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Play">Tony Award for Best Featured Actress in a Play</a> for the original Broadway production of <i><a href="/wiki/Casa_Valentina" title="Casa Valentina">Casa Valentina</a></i>.</p>

<p>Winningham was born in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>, and raised in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northridge,_California" title="Northridge, California">Northridge, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-2">[2]</a></sup> She is the daughter of Marilyn Jean (née Maloney) and Sam Neal Winningham.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She has three brothers and one sister. Her father was the chairman of the Department of Physical Education at <a href="/wiki/California_State_University,_Northridge" title="California State University, Northridge">California State University, Northridge</a> (CSUN) and her mother was an English teacher and college counselor at Monroe High School. She credits her first interest in acting to seeing an interview with <a href="/wiki/Kym_Karath" title="Kym Karath">Kym Karath</a> (who played Gretl in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sound_of_Music" title="The Sound of Music">The Sound of Music</a></i>) on <a href="/wiki/Art_Linkletter" title="Art Linkletter">Art Linkletter</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_Party_(1960s_TV_Show)" title="House Party (1960s TV Show)">House Party</a></i> when she was five or six years old.</p>
<p>Winningham attended Andasol Ave. Elementary School, where her favorite activities included drama and playing the guitar and drums. She took the extended drama option at Patrick Henry Junior High School and continued to study over her summer vacations at CSUN's Teenage Drama Workshop. It was at this time that she adopted the nickname "Mare". Her mother arranged for her to go to <a href="/wiki/Chatsworth_High_School" title="Chatsworth High School">Chatsworth High School</a>. In grade 12, Winningham starred in a production of <i>The Sound of Music</i>, playing the part of Maria, opposite classmate <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Spacey" title="Kevin Spacey">Kevin Spacey</a> as Captain Von Trapp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She graduated co-valedictorian (with Spacey) of her high school class in 1977.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mare_Winningham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mare_Winningham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Winningham began her career as a singer-songwriter. In 1976 and 1977, she got her break singing <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Here,_There_and_Everywhere" title="Here, There and Everywhere">Here, There and Everywhere</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Gong_Show" title="The Gong Show">The Gong Show</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Though Winningham received no record contracts as result of the appearance, she was signed to an acting contract by <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a> agent Meyer Mishkin, and received her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Screen_Actor%27s_Guild" title="Screen Actor's Guild">Screen Actor's Guild</a> card for doing three lines in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/James_at_15" title="James at 15">James at 15</a></i>. That year she was offered a role on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Young_Pioneers_(film)" title="Young Pioneers (film)">Young Pioneers</a></i> and <i>Young Pioneers Christmas</i>, pilots for the short-lived 1978 drama <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Young_Pioneers_(TV_series)" title="The Young Pioneers (TV series)">The Young Pioneers</a></i>. Though the series ended with just three episodes being broadcast, a number of television projects followed, including parts on <i><a href="/wiki/Police_Woman_(TV_series)" title="Police Woman (TV series)">Police Woman</a></i> in 1978 and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starsky_and_Hutch" title="Starsky and Hutch">Starsky and Hutch</a></i> in 1979. Later that same year, she played the role of teenage outcast Jenny Flowers in the <a href="/wiki/Television_film" title="Television film">made-for-TV film</a> <i>The Death of Ocean View Park</i>.</p>
<p>In 1980, Winningham starred in <i>Off the Minnesota Strip</i> playing a young prostitute. She then won an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> for <i>Best Supporting Actress In A Miniseries Or A Movie</i> for her role in the critically acclaimed <i>Amber Waves</i>, a television film about a rough farmer (<a href="/wiki/Dennis_Weaver" title="Dennis Weaver">Dennis Weaver</a>) who finds he is dying of <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emmys_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-emmys-1">[1]</a></sup> In that year, she also broke into feature films with <i><a href="/wiki/One-Trick_Pony_(film)" title="One-Trick Pony (film)">One Trick Pony</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simon" title="Paul Simon">Paul Simon</a>. In 1983, Winningham was nominated for a Canadian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genie_Award" title="Genie Award">Genie Award</a> for her work in the futuristic 1981 drama <i><a href="/wiki/Threshold_(1981_film)" title="Threshold (1981 film)">Threshold</a></i>, and appeared in the 1983 epic miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/The_Thorn_Birds" title="The Thorn Birds">The Thorn Birds</a></i>, in which she played Justine O'Neill. In 1984, she starred as <a href="/wiki/Helen_Keller" title="Helen Keller">Helen Keller</a> in <i>Helen Keller: The Miracle Continues</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Winningham achieved greater fame co-starring in <i><a href="/wiki/St._Elmo%27s_Fire_(film)" title="St. Elmo's Fire (film)">St. Elmo's Fire</a></i> (1985), alongside the other original "<a href="/wiki/Brat_Pack_(actors)" title="Brat Pack (actors)">brat pack</a>" alumni.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-2">[2]</a></sup> Despite the film's success, she failed to cash in on her teen idol status, and returned to television in the <a href="/wiki/Hallmark_Hall_of_Fame" title="Hallmark Hall of Fame">Hallmark Hall of Fame</a> movie, <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Is_Never_Silent" title="Love Is Never Silent">Love Is Never Silent</a></i>, for which she received an Emmy nomination. Another well-known and well-received performance was as a homeless young mother in the television movie <i><a href="/wiki/God_Bless_the_Child_(film)" title="God Bless the Child (film)">God Bless the Child</a></i>. Winningham finished the 1980s with two Hollywood films: the nuclear disaster drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Miracle_Mile_(film)" title="Miracle Mile (film)">Miracle Mile</a></i> (1988), for which she received an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_Spirit_Award" title="Independent Spirit Award">Independent Spirit Award</a> nomination in 1989, and the <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">Tom Hanks</a> vehicle <i><a href="/wiki/Turner_%26_Hooch" title="Turner &amp; Hooch">Turner &amp; Hooch</a></i> in 1989. In 1988, Winningham also starred in the Los Angeles stage production of <i><a href="/wiki/Hurlyburly" title="Hurlyburly">Hurlyburly</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Sean_Penn" title="Sean Penn">Sean Penn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Aiello" title="Danny Aiello">Danny Aiello</a>.</p>
<p>In the early 1990s, she returned to film for 1994's all-star <i><a href="/wiki/Wyatt_Earp_(film)" title="Wyatt Earp (film)">Wyatt Earp</a></i> and the family drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_War_(1994_film)" title="The War (1994 film)">The War</a></i>, both starring <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Costner" title="Kevin Costner">Kevin Costner</a>. 1995 brought <i><a href="/wiki/Georgia_(1995_film)" title="Georgia (1995 film)">Georgia</a></i>, a thoughtful character study of two sisters (Winningham and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Jason_Leigh" title="Jennifer Jason Leigh">Jennifer Jason Leigh</a>), which earned Winningham <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award" title="Screen Actors Guild Award">Screen Actors Guild</a> and Academy Award nominations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-2">[2]</a></sup> Two years later, she starred opposite <a href="/wiki/Gary_Sinise" title="Gary Sinise">Gary Sinise</a> in <i>George Wallace</i>, for which she garnered her first Golden Globe Award nomination<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and won an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emmys_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-emmys-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>She made acclaimed appearances on the series <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, as well as appearances in the 2001 television project <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sally_Hemmings" title="Sally Hemmings">Sally Hemmings</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Sam_Neill" title="Sam Neill">Sam Neill</a> and the short-lived <a href="/wiki/David_E._Kelley" title="David E. Kelley">David E. Kelley</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Brotherhood_of_Poland,_New_Hampshire" title="The Brotherhood of Poland, New Hampshire">The Brotherhood of Poland, New Hampshire</a></i>. Also in 2001, she appeared in the made-for-TV movie <i>Snap Decision</i> with <a href="/wiki/Felicity_Huffman" title="Felicity Huffman">Felicity Huffman</a>. She also appeared in the independent film <i>Dandelion</i>, which was a staple of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_festivals" title="Film festivals">film festivals</a> worldwide between 2003 and 2004 and had a limited American release in October 2005.</p>
<p>In 2006, she landed the role of Susan Grey on the ABC drama <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i> where she played the stepmother of one of the main characters, Dr. Meredith Grey. Her character was killed off in May 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2006, Winningham voiced the audio version of <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Lisey%27s_Story" title="Lisey's Story">Lisey's Story</a></i>. In 2007, she voiced Alice Hoffman's <i>Skylight Confessions</i>. In 2010, Winningham starred in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Cold_Case" title="Cold Case">Cold Case</a></i> as main character <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lilly_Rush" title="Lilly Rush">Lilly Rush</a>'s stepmother, Celeste Cooper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2011 she appeared in the fourth episode of <a href="/wiki/Torchwood:_Miracle_Day" title="Torchwood: Miracle Day">Torchwood: Miracle Day</a> as character <a href="/wiki/List_of_Torchwood_characters#Ellis_Hartley_Monroe" title="List of Torchwood characters">Ellis Hartley Monroe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dwm435_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-dwm435-11">[11]</a></sup> She also starred in miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Mildred_Pierce_(miniseries)" title="Mildred Pierce (miniseries)">Mildred Pierce</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Hatfields_%26_McCoys_(miniseries)" title="Hatfields &amp; McCoys (miniseries)">Hatfields &amp; McCoys</a></i> and garnered another two Emmy nominations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-emmys_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-emmys-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2012, she appeared <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> as Beth, the mother in an intellectual, though dysfunctional, British family, in the award-winning comic-drama <i><a href="/wiki/Tribes_(play)" title="Tribes (play)">Tribes</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Nina_Raine" title="Nina Raine">Nina Raine</a>.</p>

<p>Winningham has alternated her film career with a music career, and has used some of her films as a way to showcase her singing. She can be heard singing a few bars of "<a href="/wiki/Me_and_Bobby_McGee" title="Me and Bobby McGee">Me and Bobby McGee</a>" in <i><a href="/wiki/One-Trick_Pony_(film)" title="One-Trick Pony (film)">One Trick Pony</a></i>. In 1981, she played a teenage runaway with an aspiration to become a singer in the TV film <i>Freedom</i>, and sang six songs in the film, all written by <a href="/wiki/Janis_Ian" title="Janis Ian">Janis Ian</a>. She appeared as a club singer in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Teresa%27s_Tattoo" title="Teresa's Tattoo">Teresa's Tattoo</a></i>, and sang three songs in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Georgia_(1995_film)" title="Georgia (1995 film)">Georgia</a></i>.</p>
<p>Winningham has recorded four albums:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoo_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-yahoo-2">[2]</a></sup> <i>What Might Be</i> (1992) on the Bay Cities label, <i><a href="/wiki/Lonesomers" title="Lonesomers">Lonesomers</a></i> (1998) produced by <a href="/wiki/Carla_Olson" title="Carla Olson">Carla Olson</a> on the Razor and Tie label, and <i>Refuge Rock Sublime</i> (2007) on the Craig &amp; Co. label. <i>Lonesomers</i> is a folksy album dealing with relationship issues. The country/bluegrass/Jewish/folk songs on <i>Refuge Rock Sublime</i> deal mostly with her recent conversion to Judaism, and include the tracks, "What Would David Do," "A Convert Jig" and the Israeli <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hatikva" title="Hatikva">Hatikva</a>". Winningham released her fourth album <i>What's Left Behind</i> independently on digital outlets in March 2014.</p>

<p>Winningham was raised a Roman Catholic. In November 2001, on a friend's recommendation, she took a class given by <a href="/wiki/Rabbi" title="Rabbi">Rabbi</a> Neal Weinberg at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Judaism" title="University of Judaism">University of Judaism</a> (now the <a href="/wiki/American_Jewish_University" title="American Jewish University">American Jewish University</a>) in Los Angeles. On March 3, 2003, she <a href="/wiki/Conversion_to_Judaism" title="Conversion to Judaism">converted</a> to <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Judaism</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> She has been married three times: to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A._Martinez" title="A. Martinez">A. Martinez</a> (1981–81), William Mapel (1981–96) and Jason Trucco (2008–12).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
