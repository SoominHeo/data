<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Maid café</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><i><b>Maid cafés</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">メイド喫茶 / メイドカフェ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Meido kissa / Meido kafe</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> are a subcategory of <a href="/wiki/Cosplay_restaurant" title="Cosplay restaurant">cosplay restaurants</a> found predominantly in Japan. In these cafés, waitresses dressed in maid costumes act as servants, and treat customers as masters (and mistresses) in a private home, rather than as café patrons. The first permanent<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> maid café, Cure Maid Café, was established in <a href="/wiki/Akihabara" title="Akihabara">Akihabara</a>, Tokyo, Japan, in March 2001,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> but maid cafés are becoming increasingly popular. As they have done so, the increased competition has made use of some unusual tactics in order to attract customers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> They have also expanded overseas to countries like China,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> South Korea, Taiwan, Thailand, Australia, Hungary, the Czech Republic, France, the Netherlands, Mexico, Canada and the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Costume_and_appearance">Costume and appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maid_caf%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Costume and appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The maid costume varies from café to café but most are based upon the costume of <a href="/wiki/French_maid" title="French maid">French maids</a>, often composed of a dress, a <a href="/wiki/Petticoat" title="Petticoat">petticoat</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Pinafore" title="Pinafore">pinafore</a>, a matching hair accessory (such as a frill or a bow), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stockings" title="Stockings">stockings</a>. Often, employees will also <a href="/wiki/Cosplay" title="Cosplay">cosplay</a> as <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pwg_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-pwg-6">[6]</a></sup> Sometimes, employees wear animal ears with their outfits to add more appeal.</p>
<p>Waitresses in maid cafés are often chosen on the basis of their appearance; most are young, attractive and innocent-looking women. Applicants are sometimes tested to determine whether they can sufficiently portray a given character that they will be cosplaying. In order to maintain the cosplay fantasy, some employees may be contractually obligated to not reveal personal information to patrons, to slip out of character or to allow patrons to see them out of costume.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pwg_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-pwg-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Some maid cafés also have crossdressing males as maids.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clientele">Clientele</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maid_caf%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Clientele">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Maiden_Diner_Maid_Cafe_20131105.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1272" data-file-width="1272" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Maiden_Diner_Maid_Cafe_20131105.jpg/220px-Maiden_Diner_Maid_Cafe_20131105.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Maiden_Diner_Maid_Cafe_20131105.jpg/330px-Maiden_Diner_Maid_Cafe_20131105.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Maiden_Diner_Maid_Cafe_20131105.jpg/440px-Maiden_Diner_Maid_Cafe_20131105.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Maid cafés were originally designed primarily to cater to the fantasies of male <a href="/wiki/Otaku" title="Otaku">otaku</a>, fans of anime, <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Video_games" title="Video games">video games</a>. They have been analogized as the otaku's equivalent of <a href="/wiki/Host_and_hostess_clubs" title="Host and hostess clubs">hostess bars</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kotaku_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-kotaku-8">[8]</a></sup> The image of the <a href="/wiki/Uniform_fetishism#French_maid_uniform" title="Uniform fetishism">maid</a> is one that has been popularized and <a href="/wiki/Fetishism" title="Fetishism">fetishized</a> in many manga and anime series, as well as in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishojo_game" title="Bishojo game">gal games</a>. Important to the otaku attraction to maid cafés is the Japanese concept of <i><a href="/wiki/Moe_(slang)" title="Moe (slang)">moe</a></i>, which is a character trope in otaku media which describes young, innocent-looking female characters. People who have moe fetishes (especially a specific subcategory known as maid moe) are therefore attracted to an establishment in which they can interact with real-life manifestations (both physically and in demeanor) of the fictional maid characters that they have fetishized. Cafés may also employ a <i><a href="/wiki/Tsundere" title="Tsundere">tsundere</a></i> theme - another character trope which is a subset of the moe phenomenon and refers to a character who is initially cold or hostile before revealing feelings of warmth or affection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pwg_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-pwg-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-matador_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-matador-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Around 2003 and 2004, maid cafés became more common and popular in Japan as otaku culture became increasingly mainstream.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kotaku_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-kotaku-8">[8]</a></sup> As a result, there has been a diversification of themes and services at the restaurants but they are ultimately still predominantly colored by anime and video games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pwg_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-pwg-6">[6]</a></sup> Today, the maid café phenomenon attracts more than just male otaku, but also couples, tourists, and women.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Menu">Menu</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maid_caf%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Menu">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most maid cafés offer menus similar to those of more typical cafés. Customers can order coffee, other beverages, and a wide variety of entrées and desserts. However, in maid cafés, waitresses will often decorate a customer’s order with cute designs at his or her table. Syrup can be used to decorate desserts, and <a href="/wiki/Omurice" title="Omurice">omelette rice</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">オムライス</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Omu-raisu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, a popular entrée, is typically decorated using ketchup. This service adds to the image of the waitress as an innocent but pampering maid.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rituals.2C_etiquette_and_additional_services">Rituals, etiquette and additional services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maid_caf%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rituals, etiquette and additional services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are many rituals and additional services offered at many maid cafés. Maids greet customers with "Welcome home, Master (Mistress, My lady)" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">お帰りなさいませ、ご主人様(お嬢様)！</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Okaerinasaimase, goshujinsama (ojousama)</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and offer them wipe towels and menus. Maids will also kneel by the table to stir cream and sugar into a customer's coffee, and some cafés even offer spoon-feeding services to customers. Increasingly, maid cafés offer grooming services, such as ear cleanings and leg, arm, and back massages (provided the customer remains fully clothed), for an additional fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Customers can also sometimes play <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock-paper-scissors" title="Rock-paper-scissors">rock-paper-scissors</a>, card games, board games, and video games with maids as well as prepare <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arts_and_crafts" title="Arts and crafts">arts and crafts</a> and sing <a href="/wiki/Karaoke" title="Karaoke">karaoke</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kotaku_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-kotaku-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-matador_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-matador-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Customers are also expected to follow basic rules when they are at a maid café. One Tokyo maid café recently published a list of ten rules that customers should follow in a maid café.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-code_of_conduct_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-code_of_conduct-11">[11]</a></sup> For example, customers should not touch a maid's body, ask for a maid's personal contact information, or otherwise invade her personal privacy (by <a href="/wiki/Stalking" title="Stalking">stalking</a>). One common rule in a maid café is that photographs of maids or the café interior are forbidden. However, customers may have the option of paying an extra fee in order to get his or her photograph taken with a maid, possibly hand-decorated by the maid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-code_of_conduct_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-code_of_conduct-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-matador_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-matador-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
