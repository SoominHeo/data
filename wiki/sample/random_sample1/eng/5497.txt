<p>The <b>Meishin Expressway</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">名神高速道路</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Meishin Kōsoku-dōro</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (=193.9 km) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toll_(road)" title="Toll (road)">toll</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Expressway_in_Japan" title="Expressway in Japan">expressway in Japan</a>. It runs from a junction with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tomei_Expressway" title="Tomei Expressway">Tomei Expressway</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nagakute,_Aichi" title="Nagakute, Aichi">Nagakute, Aichi</a> (outside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nagoya,_Aichi" title="Nagoya, Aichi">Nagoya</a>) west to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nishinomiya,_Hy%C5%8Dgo" title="Nishinomiya, Hyōgo">Nishinomiya, Hyōgo</a> (between <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kobe" title="Kobe">Kobe</a>). It is the main road link between Osaka and Nagoya, and, along with the Tōmei Expressway, forms the main road link between Osaka and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>. East of the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%ABgoku_Expressway" title="Chūgoku Expressway">Chūgoku Expressway</a> near Osaka, it is part of <a href="/wiki/Asian_Highway_Network" title="Asian Highway Network">Asian Highway Network</a> (<a href="/wiki/AH1" title="AH1"><img alt="" data-file-height="446" data-file-width="535" height="25" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/AH1-JPN.png/30px-AH1-JPN.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/AH1-JPN.png/45px-AH1-JPN.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/AH1-JPN.png/60px-AH1-JPN.png 2x" width="30"/></a>).</p>
<p>The part east of interchange 29 (<a href="/wiki/Y%C5%8Dkaichi,_Shiga" title="Yōkaichi, Shiga">Yōkaichi</a>) is owned by the <a href="/wiki/Central_Nippon_Expressway_Company" title="Central Nippon Expressway Company">Central Nippon Expressway Company</a>; the rest is owned by the <a href="/wiki/West_Nippon_Expressway_Company" title="West Nippon Expressway Company">West Nippon Expressway Company</a>.</p>
<p>The Meishin Expressway was the first expressway in Japan, with a section near Osaka and Kyoto opening July 16, 1963.</p>
<p>The Meishin Expressway parallels the old <a href="/wiki/Nakasend%C5%8D" title="Nakasendō">Nakasendō</a> between <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a> and Nagoya, now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Route_8_(Japan)" title="Route 8 (Japan)">Route 8</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Route_21_(Japan)" title="Route 21 (Japan)">Route 21</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meihan_National_Highway" title="Meihan National Highway">Meihan National Highway</a> is a mostly non-tolled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freeway" title="Freeway">freeway</a> between Osaka and Nagoya, lying further south and built to lower standards. The <a href="/wiki/Shin-Meishin_Expressway" title="Shin-Meishin Expressway">Shin-Meishin Expressway</a> is an under-construction route between Osaka and Nagoya, lying between the two other high-speed roads, that will connect to the <a href="/wiki/Shin-T%C5%8Dmei_Expressway" title="Shin-Tōmei Expressway">Shin-Tōmei Expressway</a> (via the <a href="/wiki/Isewangan_Expressway" title="Isewangan Expressway">Isewangan Expressway</a>) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sany%C5%8D_Expressway" title="Sanyō Expressway">Sanyō Expressway</a>. It roughly parallels the old <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkaid%C5%8D_(road)" title="Tōkaidō (road)">Tōkaidō</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Route_1_(Japan)" title="Route 1 (Japan)">Route 1</a>).</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hanshin_earthquake" title="Great Hanshin earthquake">Great Hanshin earthquake</a> of January 17, 1995, the Meishin Expressway was lightly damaged but could only be used by <a href="/wiki/Emergency_vehicle" title="Emergency vehicle">emergency vehicles</a> for weeks after the earthquake. It was the only remaining link between Osaka and Kobe for some time after the quake.</p>
