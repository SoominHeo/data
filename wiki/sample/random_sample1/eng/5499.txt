<p><b>Major League Gaming Inc.</b> (<b>MLG</b>) is a professional <a href="/wiki/ESports" title="ESports">eSports</a> organization. MLG is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> and was founded in 2002 by <a href="/wiki/Sundance_DiGiovanni" title="Sundance DiGiovanni">Sundance DiGiovanni</a> and Mike Sepso. MLG has held official video game tournaments throughout the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. The <i><a href="/wiki/Boost_Mobile_MLG_Pro_Circuit" title="Boost Mobile MLG Pro Circuit">Boost Mobile MLG Pro Circuit</a></i> was a television broadcast of <i>Halo 2</i> MLG tournaments in 2006 and 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leagueofown_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-leagueofown-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bigCNET_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-bigCNET-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/ESPN.com" title="ESPN.com">ESPN.com</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and other broadband sites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLG_on_ESPN360.com_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-MLG_on_ESPN360.com-9">[9]</a></sup> The company has also been involved in television production,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leagueofown_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-leagueofown-6">[6]</a></sup> and game development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLGAgora_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-MLGAgora-10">[10]</a></sup> MLG's aim is to elevate computer and console game tournaments to viable competitive and spectator events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bigCNET_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-bigCNET-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2016, video game publisher <a href="/wiki/Activision_Blizzard" title="Activision Blizzard">Activision Blizzard</a> announced its acquisition of Major League Gaming. The company, whose own eSports division is led by MLG co-founder Mike Sepso, stated that it intended to leverage the purchase as part of its plans to build an eSports-focused <a href="/wiki/Television_network" title="Television network">television network</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pro_Circuit">Pro Circuit</span></h2>
<p>The MLG Pro Circuit roster currently includes <i>Starcraft II</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/League_of_Legends" title="League of Legends">League of Legends</a></i> for the PC. <i><a href="/wiki/Mortal_Kombat_(2011_video_game)" title="Mortal Kombat (2011 video game)">Mortal Kombat</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soul_Calibur_V" title="Soul Calibur V">Soul Calibur V</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Fighters_XIII" title="King of Fighters XIII">King of Fighters XIII</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Melee" title="Super Smash Bros. Melee">Super Smash Bros. Melee</a></i> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nintendo_Gamecube" title="Nintendo Gamecube">Nintendo Gamecube</a> are the only console games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <i>Starcraft II</i> is currently commentated by DJ Wheat, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Day_9" title="Day 9">Day 9</a>, Rob Simpson, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Plott" title="Nick Plott">Tasteless</a>, <a href="/wiki/Artosis" title="Artosis">Artosis</a>, JP, dApollo, and <a href="/wiki/TotalBiscuit" title="TotalBiscuit">TotalBiscuit</a>, with other personalities coming on time to time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Fighting games are commentated by Juicebox Abel, Tom Brady, and Bibulus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> No <i>League of Legends</i> casters have been announced yet.</p>
<p>Major League Gaming also hosts a series of online qualifier ladders for the online-only pro circuit titles leading to the national championship. In the past, MLG hosted <i>Super Smash Bros. Melee</i> tournaments during the 2004 through 2006 MLG Circuit and other games such as <i><a href="/wiki/Halo:_Combat_Evolved" title="Halo: Combat Evolved">Halo: Combat Evolved</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_2" title="Halo 2">Halo 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tekken_5" title="Tekken 5">Tekken 5</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gears_of_War" title="Gears of War">Gears of War</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tom_Clancy%27s_Rainbow_Six:_Vegas" title="Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six: Vegas">Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six: Vegas</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Shadowrun" title="Shadowrun">Shadowrun</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tom_Clancy%27s_Rainbow_Six:_Vegas_2" title="Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six: Vegas 2">Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six: Vegas 2</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty_4" title="Call of Duty 4">Call of Duty 4</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Gears_of_War_2" title="Gears of War 2">Gears of War 2</a></i>.</p>
<p>Each team/player must purchase a team pass to compete. These passes normally go on sale several weeks in advance of the next Pro Circuit event. Passes are limited, so participants are encouraged to purchase a pass as soon as they go on sale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<p>The MLG Pro League was a <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty" title="Call of Duty">Call of Duty</a></i> league that ran in 2014 and 2015 for <i>Ghosts</i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Advanced_Warfare" title="Advanced Warfare">Advanced Warfare</a></i> respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> There were three seasons per year, and 16 teams competing per season. The regular season was played online in a <a href="/wiki/Round-robin_tournament" title="Round-robin tournament">round-robin tournament</a> format over the course of two months. At the end of each season, the bottom four teams were sent to a relegation tournament and the top eight teams were invited to an offline playoff tournament.</p>
<p>There was also a <i><a href="/wiki/Dota_2" title="Dota 2">Dota 2</a></i> Pro League sponsored by joinDota.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:322px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Major_League_Gaming_(logo).svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="169" data-file-width="429" height="126" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2c/Major_League_Gaming_%28logo%29.svg/320px-Major_League_Gaming_%28logo%29.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2c/Major_League_Gaming_%28logo%29.svg/480px-Major_League_Gaming_%28logo%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2c/Major_League_Gaming_%28logo%29.svg/640px-Major_League_Gaming_%28logo%29.svg.png 2x" width="320"/></a>

<p>Major League Gaming was founded in 2002<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> by Sundance DiGiovanni<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLGCanada_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-MLGCanada-17">[17]</a></sup> and Mike Sepso.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leagueofown_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-leagueofown-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLGexec_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-MLGexec-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2006, MLG became the first televised <a href="/wiki/Video_game_console" title="Video game console">video game console</a> gaming league in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, with their <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_2" title="Halo 2">Halo 2</a></i> Pro Series being broadcast by <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Boost_Mobile_MLG_Pro_Circuit" title="Boost Mobile MLG Pro Circuit">Boost Mobile MLG Pro Circuit</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> It moved into the <a href="/wiki/3_Park_Avenue" title="3 Park Avenue">3 Park Avenue</a> sometime after its founding.</p>
<p>In February 2009, it was announced that MLG got $10 Million in financing from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ritchie_Capital_Management" title="Ritchie Capital Management">Ritchie Capital Management</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On February 6, 2009, MLG Commissioner John Nelson addressed the MLG community about changing the format for the 2009 pro circuit. Semi-pro teams now have the opportunity to gain pro status. The rolling rank points system and the championship bracket were also modified.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On August 18, 2009, Major League Gaming acquired <a href="/wiki/Agora_Games" title="Agora Games">Agora Games</a>; CEO Matthew Bromberg explained that "we already operate the largest online competitive gaming property in the world. Agora is the leading developer of multi-player communities in the world. Coming together with Agora allows us to double-down on our biggest strength."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MLGAgora_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-MLGAgora-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2010, it was announced that fighting games would return to the pro circuit, with <i><a href="/wiki/Tekken_6" title="Tekken 6">Tekken 6</a></i> exclusively on the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a>, and the return of the <i>Smash Bros.</i> competition with <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Brawl" title="Super Smash Bros. Brawl">Super Smash Bros. Brawl</a></i>. These two games appeared in the season opener in Orlando, along with the league's flagship, <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_3" title="Halo 3">Halo 3</a></i>, which entered its third season with the league.</p>
<p><i>Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2</i> made its debut on the Online Pro Circuit on MLG's GameBattles website for PlayStation 3. Originally, the game was on both the <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a> and PlayStation 3. Due to excessive hacking on the Xbox 360 console via <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360_system_software#2.0.8498.0" title="Xbox 360 system software">JTAG hacks</a>, it was stripped of its "Pro Circuit" branding. Prize payouts remain the same on both consoles. PlayStation 3 players are eligible to accumulate Pro Points. Those who have enough Pro Points at the end of the 3rd season of the Online Pro Circuit are eligible to compete live at the MLG Nationals held in Dallas. Those competing on the Xbox 360 do not earn pro points and will have championships held online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On July 30, 2010, it was announced that StarCraft II was to be added to the Pro Circuit. It made its official debut at MLG Raleigh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2011 Circuit featured four titles: <i><a href="/wiki/Halo:_Reach" title="Halo: Reach">Halo: Reach</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starcraft_2" title="Starcraft 2">Starcraft 2</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty:_Black_Ops" title="Call of Duty: Black Ops">Call of Duty: Black Ops</a></i> and the mid-season addition of <i>League of Legends</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Also returning, something that the MLG Pro Circuit hasn't seen since the 2005, is Pool Play. The top 16 teams were seeded in 4 pools of 5 teams, where the 5th team would play an undefeated amateur team. The team with the best record throughout pool play advanced to the winner's bracket semi-finals, securing themselves a Top 6 finish.</p>
<p>The 2012 MLG competitions saw many title changes in the Pro Circuit. <i>Starcraft II</i> was brought on as the league's main title. Fighting games for the PS3 and <i>League of Legends</i> were announced as additional titles. <i>Halo: Reach</i> is no longer on the Pro Circuit, nor is <i>Call of Duty: Black Ops</i>. Call of Duty was dropped from the circuit due to the lack of funding MLG received from PlayStation to put the title on the circuit. The 2012 tournament format has also drastically changed, with the introduction of seasonal events. The new format features 4 quarterly seasons; within each season are 2 Arenas and a Championship. The Championship features all Pro Circuit titles, and has a free SD broadcast option. The Arenas are <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">Pay-per-view</a> (PPV) events and for now only feature <i>Starcraft II</i>, and are broadcast in <a href="/wiki/High-definition_video" title="High-definition video">high definition</a> (HD) from MLG's Studio in New York. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also in 2012 came many new partnerships for MLG. So far, MLG has partnered with <a href="/wiki/CBS_Interactive" title="CBS Interactive">CBS Interactive</a> (CBSi) to increase its broadcast capabilities and to integrate with CBSi's website, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GameSpot.com" title="GameSpot.com">GameSpot.com</a>. This new partnership hopes to increase exposure of MLG to a larger more casual audience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>During 2012, MLG has also partnered with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KeSPA" title="KeSPA">KeSPA</a> (Korean eSports Association) in a multi-year agreement. This agreement allows MLG exclusive access to KeSPA's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starcraft:_Brood_War" title="Starcraft: Brood War">Starcraft: Brood War</a></i> players. The partnership will see KeSPA Brood War pros come to the US to compete in MLG events throughout the year. The participants will not be allowed to compete at any other foreign tournaments without MLG's approval. The deal took effect in June, when KeSPA Brood War pros participated in an exhibition event at MLG Anaheim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beginning on November 2, 2012, with the commencement of the MLG Fall Championship in <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <i>Halo</i> was reintroduced to the pro circuit. <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_4" title="Halo 4">Halo 4</a></i>, which was publicly released on November 6, was one of the five games scheduled for competitive play at MLG Dallas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2013, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty:_Black_Ops_2" title="Call of Duty: Black Ops 2">Call of Duty: Black Ops 2</a></i> was introduced into the Pro Circuit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cod_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-cod-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 14, 2013, <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty:_Ghosts" title="Call of Duty: Ghosts">Call of Duty: Ghosts</a></i> was announced to be MLG's featured <a href="/wiki/First-person_shooter" title="First-person shooter">first-person shooter</a> game for the MLG Columbus and 2014 season events. Through June 8–10, 2014 MLG hosted a tournament at the <a href="/wiki/X_Games" title="X Games">X Games</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty:_Ghosts" title="Call of Duty: Ghosts">Call of Duty: Ghosts</a></i> with the eventual winners being <a href="/wiki/OpTic_Gaming" title="OpTic Gaming">OpTic Gaming</a> including fan favorites <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matt_%22Nadeshot%22_Haag" title='Matt "Nadeshot" Haag'>Matt "Nadeshot" Haag</a> and Seth "Scump" Abner.</p>
<p>In April 2014, MLG announced that it partnering with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lai_Fung_Holdings_Limited" title="Lai Fung Holdings Limited">Lai Fung Holdings Limited</a> (Lai Fung) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ESun_Holdings_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ESun Holdings Limited (page does not exist)">eSun Holdings Limited</a> in building the MLG Arena on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hengqin_Island" title="Hengqin Island">Hengqin Island</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The arena, which is scheduled to be completed in 2017, is part of the "Creative Culture City" development planned on Hengqin.</p>
<p>In October 2014, MLG opened the 14,000 square feet (1,300 m<sup>2</sup>) MLG.tv Arena in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a> .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arena_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-arena-31">[31]</a></sup> It is located near the <a href="/wiki/Easton_Town_Center" title="Easton Town Center">Easton Town Center</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The first event held at the arena was the Season 3 Call of Duty playoffs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arena_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-arena-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 19, 2013, MLG announced it would be organizing the <a href="/wiki/MLG_Major_Championship:_Columbus" title="MLG Major Championship: Columbus">MLG Major Championship: Columbus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Global_Offensive" title="Counter-Strike: Global Offensive">Counter-Strike: Global Offensive</a></i> Major on March 29 – April 3, 2016 in <a href="/wiki/Nationwide_Arena" title="Nationwide Arena">Nationwide Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_by_Activision_Blizzard">Acquisition by Activision Blizzard</span></h3>
<p>On December 31, 2015, it was reported that "substantially all" of MLG's assets had been acquired by <a href="/wiki/Activision_Blizzard" title="Activision Blizzard">Activision Blizzard</a> for $46 million, and that CEO Sundance DiGiovanni would be replaced by MLG's former CFO Greg Chisholm. Activision Blizzard operates its own in-house e-sports division, Activision Blizzard Media Networks, led by veteran sports television executive <a href="/wiki/Steve_Bornstein" title="Steve Bornstein">Steve Bornstein</a>, MLG co-founder Mike Sepso, and its acquisition of assets from the defunct <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IGN_Pro_League" title="IGN Pro League">IGN Pro League</a>. Activision Blizzard owns the <i>Call of Duty</i> and <i>Starcraft</i> franchises—which have been popular as e-sports. Reports indicated that MLG was to be shuttered, and that the majority of the purchase price would go towards paying off the company's debt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ars-mlgshuttered_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-ars-mlgshuttered-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Activision Blizzard confirmed the purchase on January 4, 2016. Activision CEO <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kotick" title="Robert Kotick">Robert Kotick</a> explained that the main target of the acquisition was MLG's streaming operation MLG.tv. Kotick explained to <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> that their eventual goal was to "build the <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> of video games"—a television <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cable_channel" title="Cable channel">cable channel</a> that would be devoted to e-sports coverage and analysis with "premium" in-house productions that could attract more major advertisers, either produced by Activision's staff or by outside producers. Despite the acquisition, MLG will continue to host events relating to games that are not published by Activision Blizzard's subsidiaries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt-mlgbuy_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-mlgbuy-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2016, MLG announced "Enhanced Viewing Experience", a new streaming player design that integrates live data and statistic displays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired-revamp_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-wired-revamp-37">[37]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="MLG.tv">MLG.tv</span></h2>
<p>MLG.tv is Major League Gaming's <a href="/wiki/Streaming_media" title="Streaming media">streaming media</a> service. Several professional <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty" title="Call of Duty">Call of Duty</a></i> players including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matt_%22Nadeshot%22_Haag" title='Matt "Nadeshot" Haag'>Matt "Nadeshot" Haag</a> have signed exclusivity contracts with the streaming service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> At the end of the first quarter of 2015, MLG announced that mlg.tv saw in increase in viewership of 253% <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span></h2>
<p>"Montage parodies" are a type of <a href="/wiki/Montage_(filmmaking)" title="Montage (filmmaking)">montage video</a> that parodies <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty" title="Call of Duty">Call of Duty</a></i> sniping videos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Such videos commonly employ sound effects such as an <a href="/wiki/Air_horn" title="Air horn">air horn</a>, gun sound effects for the <i>Call of Duty</i> games, the <a href="/wiki/Nate_Dogg" title="Nate Dogg">Nate Dogg</a> line "smoke weed everyday" from <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s single "<a href="/wiki/The_Next_Episode" title="The Next Episode">The Next Episode</a>", and sounds from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wombo_Combo" title="Wombo Combo">Wombo Combo</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Melee" title="Super Smash Bros. Melee">Super Smash Bros. Melee</a></i> video. In particular the MLG brand is used in exaggerated effect for humor. "MLG no-scope" in particular is a satirical term used in association with such videos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Doritos" title="Doritos">Doritos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mountain_Dew" title="Mountain Dew">Mountain Dew</a> as brands have been associated Major League Gaming group. They are often parodied in such videos.</p>
<p>The most popular MLG parody is "mlg <a href="/wiki/Teletubbies" title="Teletubbies">teletubbies</a>" by Pyrocynical. As of December 2016, it has gained over 11 million views.</p>
