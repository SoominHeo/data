<p><b>Meiktila</b> (<a href="/wiki/Burmese_language" title="Burmese language">Burmese</a>: <span style="font-family:'Myanmar Text','Noto Sans Myanmar', Myanmar3, Myanmar2, Myanmar1, Padauk, mm3web, TharLon, 'Masterpiece Uni Sans', Parabaik, Yunghkio, Thanlwin, 'Win Uni Innwa', 'MyMyanmar Unicode', 'WinUni Innwa';">မိတ္ထီလာမြို့</span>; <a href="/wiki/MLC_Transcription_System" title="MLC Transcription System">MLCTS</a>: <i><span title="Transcription in Myanmar Language Commission Transcription System">mit thi la mrui.</span></i>; <small>Burmese pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Burmese" title="Help:IPA for Burmese">[meɪʔtʰìlà mjo̰]</a></span>) is a city in central <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Burma</a> on the banks of <a href="/wiki/Meiktila_Lake" title="Meiktila Lake">Meiktila Lake</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mandalay_Region" title="Mandalay Region">Mandalay Region</a> at the junctions of the <a href="/wiki/Bagan" title="Bagan">Bagan</a>-<a href="/wiki/Taunggyi" title="Taunggyi">Taunggyi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yangon" title="Yangon">Yangon</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mandalay" title="Mandalay">Mandalay</a> and Meiktila-<a href="/wiki/Myingyan" title="Myingyan">Myingyan</a> highways. Because of its strategic position, Meiktila is home to <a href="/wiki/Myanmar_Air_Force" title="Myanmar Air Force">Myanmar Air Force</a>'s central command and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Meiktila_Air_Force_Base&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Meiktila Air Force Base (page does not exist)">Meiktila Air Force Base</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afb_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-afb-1">[1]</a></sup> The country's main aerospace engineering university, <a href="/wiki/Myanmar_Aerospace_Engineering_University" title="Myanmar Aerospace Engineering University">Myanmar Aerospace Engineering University</a> is also located in Meiktila.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meiktila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_British_Army_in_Burma_1945_SE3302.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="792" height="222" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/The_British_Army_in_Burma_1945_SE3302.jpg/220px-The_British_Army_in_Burma_1945_SE3302.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/The_British_Army_in_Burma_1945_SE3302.jpg/330px-The_British_Army_in_Burma_1945_SE3302.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/The_British_Army_in_Burma_1945_SE3302.jpg/440px-The_British_Army_in_Burma_1945_SE3302.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The name Meiktila comes from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mithila_(ancient)" title="Mithila (ancient)">Mithila</a>, the name of an Indian kingdom in India.</p>
<p>The history of Meiktila is closely tied to that of central Myanmar's dry zone, home of the <a href="/wiki/Bamar_people" title="Bamar people">Bamar people</a>. The region had been part of various Bamar kingdoms at least from 11th century CE to 19th century CE before the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> took over all of Upper Myanmar in 1885.</p>
<p>Meiktila was part of the 1945 <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Meiktila_and_Mandalay" title="Battle of Meiktila and Mandalay">Battle of Meiktila and Mandalay</a>, during which the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied forces</a> under <a href="/wiki/William_Slim,_1st_Viscount_Slim" title="William Slim, 1st Viscount Slim">William Slim</a> defeated the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japanese forces</a>. Soon after Burma's independence from Britain in 1948, Meiktila briefly fell under the insurgent <a href="/wiki/Karen_people" title="Karen people">Karen forces</a>.</p>
<p>On 22 March 2013, a state of emergency was imposed in the town after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013_Burma_anti-Muslim_riots" title="2013 Burma anti-Muslim riots">two days of anti-Muslim riots</a> after dozens of people were killed and more than 12,000 were forced from their homes, the overwhelming majority of whom were Muslims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This violence later spread to other places in Burma such as <a href="/wiki/Okpho_Township" title="Okpho Township">Okpho</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gyobingauk_Township" title="Gyobingauk Township">Gyobingauk Townships</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meiktila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Meiktila district is the most easterly of the districts in Myanmar's central dry zone. It lies between <a href="/wiki/Wundwin" title="Wundwin">Wundwin</a>, Myingyan, <a href="/wiki/Yamethin" title="Yamethin">Yamethin</a>, and touches <a href="/wiki/Shan_State" title="Shan State">Shan State</a> on the east. The chief feature of the region <a href="/wiki/Meiktila_Lake" title="Meiktila Lake">Lake Meiktila</a>, an artificial <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_reservoir" title="Water reservoir">water reservoir</a>, 7 miles (11 km) long, 0.5 miles (0.80 km) wide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eb_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-eb-5">[5]</a></sup> Mondai dam supplies water to the lake.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meiktila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Meiktila has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_savanna_climate" title="Tropical savanna climate">tropical savanna climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Aw</i>). Temperatures are very warm throughout the year, and the months before the monsoon (March to May) are especially hot, with average maxima around 36 °C (97 °F). There is a winter dry season (November–April) and a summer wet season (May–October).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meiktila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city's well-known pagodas are Shwemyintin, Shweyinmi (asa Hteethonesint), Nagayon, Yele, Sutaung Pyi and Phaungdawoo.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meiktila&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Meiktila is home to a national university in <a href="/wiki/Myanmar_Aerospace_Engineering_University" title="Myanmar Aerospace Engineering University">Myanmar Aerospace Engineering University</a>, and regional universities and colleges such as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Meiktila_University&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Meiktila University (page does not exist)">Meiktila University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meiktila_Institute_of_Economics" title="Meiktila Institute of Economics">Meiktila Institute of Economics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_University,_Meiktila" title="Computer University, Meiktila">Computer University, Meiktila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Technological_University,_Meiktila" title="Technological University, Meiktila">Technological University, Meiktila</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Meiktila_Education_College&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Meiktila Education College (page does not exist)">Meiktila Education College</a>.</p>
<p>Of the city's six public <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_schools_in_Burma" title="High schools in Burma">Basic Education High Schools</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BEHS_1_Meiktila" title="BEHS 1 Meiktila">BEHS 1 Meiktila</a> or <b>Royal High School</b> is considered the most selective.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> No (4), Basic Education High School(Meiktila) (No.4,BEHS, Meiktila) is located at the side of highways of Meiktila- Kyaukpataung.</p>
