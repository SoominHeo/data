<p><i><b>MapleStory</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">메이플스토리</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Meipeul Seutori</span></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_free_massively_multiplayer_online_games" title="List of free massively multiplayer online games">free-to-play</a>, <a href="/wiki/2D_computer_graphics" title="2D computer graphics">2D</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Side-scrolling" title="Side-scrolling">side-scrolling</a> <a href="/wiki/Massively_multiplayer_online_role-playing_game" title="Massively multiplayer online role-playing game">massively multiplayer online role-playing game</a>, developed by the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> company <a href="/wiki/Wizet" title="Wizet">Wizet</a>. Several versions of the game are available for specific countries or regions, and each is published by various companies such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NEXON_Korea_Corporation" title="NEXON Korea Corporation">Nexon</a>. Although one may play the game for free, one can only purchase some character appearances and gameplay enhancements from the Cash Shop with real money.</p>
<p>In the game, players travel the "Maple World", defeating <a href="/wiki/Mob_(video_gaming)" title="Mob (video gaming)">monsters</a> and developing their characters' <a href="/wiki/Statistic_(role-playing_games)#Skills" title="Statistic (role-playing games)">skills</a> and abilities as is typical in <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_game" title="Role-playing game">role-playing games</a>. Players can interact with others in many ways, such as through <a href="/wiki/Instant_messaging" title="Instant messaging">chatting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trading</a> and playing <a href="/wiki/Minigame" title="Minigame">minigames</a>. Groups of players can band together in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Party_(role_playing_games)" title="Party (role playing games)">parties</a> to hunt monsters and share the rewards and can also collaborate forming <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clan_(video_gaming)" title="Clan (video gaming)">guilds</a> to interact more easily with each other.</p>
<p>In July 2010, the Korean version of the game was completely revised in a patch named the "Big Bang". Other versions followed suit and have since received the Big Bang update. Later in the year, the Korean version received the Chaos update which introduces <a href="/wiki/Player_versus_player" title="Player versus player">PvP</a> (player-versus-player) and professions to the game. Its sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/MapleStory_2" title="MapleStory 2">MapleStory 2</a></i>, was released in July 2015 and features updated 3D graphics and a similar storyline.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p>Players assume the roles of citizens in the Maple World with no specific objective to win the game, but to work on improving their characters' skill and status over time. The antagonist of the game, the Black Mage, was sealed away years ago by six heroes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aran_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Aran-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sealed_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sealed-2">[2]</a></sup> Fearing his resurrection, Empress Cygnus enlisted the Maple World to join her Cygnus Knights in preventing his return.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> However, the Black Wings were able to return the Black Mage to reality for a moment, causing a cataclysm known as the Big Bang.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> With the seal on the Black Mage weakening, the five heroes return to the world in a weakened state. The player's character starts a quest to restore the Black Mage's seal but instead, witness his revival. The Black Mage then begins assimilating Grandis with the Maple World in order to rule both worlds.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span></h2>
<p><i>MapleStory</i> is a 2D scrolling <a href="/wiki/Platform_game" title="Platform game">platform game</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Intro_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Intro-5">[5]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Computer_keyboard#Usage" title="Computer keyboard">controls</a> for the game are executed using the <a href="/wiki/Computer_keyboard" title="Computer keyboard">keyboard</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mouse_(computing)" title="Mouse (computing)">mouse</a>. The keyboard is used for many game functions, and much of it can be rearranged to suit users' needs. The mouse is mainly used to trigger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-player_characters" title="Non-player characters">NPCs</a> and manipulate items. Like most <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MMORPG" title="MMORPG">MMORPGs</a>, gameplay centers on venturing into dungeons and combating monsters in real-time. The players combat monsters and complete <a href="/wiki/Quest_(video_gaming)" title="Quest (video gaming)">quests</a>, in the process acquiring in-game currency called "Mesos", <a href="/wiki/Experience_point" title="Experience point">experience points</a> and various items.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Intro_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Intro-5">[5]</a></sup> Players are able to acquire in-game professions, allowing them to craft equipment, accessories or potions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Players have a variety of options when interacting with others online: They can form parties where loot and experience are distributed from a monster;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Party_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Party-7">[7]</a></sup> in-game <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marriages" title="Marriages">marriages</a> where the game will recognize the two players as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heterosexual" title="Heterosexual">heterosexual</a> couple;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marriage_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Marriage-8">[8]</a></sup> guilds which is indicated with a guild's name listed under their character name;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guild_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guild-9">[9]</a></sup> or families, an architecture gathering of juniors and seniors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Family_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Family-10">[10]</a></sup> Players are also able to raise or lower the in-game popularity stat entitled Fame of other players and compete with other players in <a href="/wiki/Minigame" title="Minigame">minigames</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Gomoku" title="Gomoku">Gomoku</a> or <a href="/wiki/Concentration_(game)" title="Concentration (game)">Concentration</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Minigames_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Minigames-11">[11]</a></sup> They may also engage in trading activities for "Mesos" or other in-game items (though subject to <a href="/wiki/Virtual_tax" title="Virtual tax">virtual tax</a>).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span></h3>
<p><i>MapleStory</i> <a href="/wiki/Player_character" title="Player character">characters</a> exist in worlds or servers. Players are able to create multiple characters in each world, though the characters in different worlds can not interact with each other.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Each world is similar in content with each other. The worlds are divided into channels, among which characters are allowed to freely switch. The ability to transfer entire characters between worlds was added in December 2007 to Global <i>MapleStory</i>, with restrictions placed on items.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In May 2012, this option was removed as it caused a negative impact on character gameplay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>When creating a new character, there are six branches of <a href="/wiki/Character_class" title="Character class">classes</a> currently available, with two other branches with one class each: Explorers, Cygnus Knights, Heroes, Resistance, Nova and Sengoku. Beast Tamer and Child of God have one class in both branches. Some classes are currently not available in other regions.</p>
<p>Explorer characters start on <a href="#World">Maple Island</a> and begin as a Beginner class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beginner_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beginner-13">[13]</a></sup> The Beginner is able to advance into one of the five classes: <a href="/wiki/Warrior_(character_class)" title="Warrior (character class)">Warrior</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bowmen" title="Bowmen">Bowmen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wizard_(character_class)" title="Wizard (character class)">Magician</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thief_(character_class)" title="Thief (character class)">Thief</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pirate" title="Pirate">Pirate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Explorers_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Explorers-14">[14]</a></sup> After choosing their class, further class progression is only allowed within the scope of the first class advancement with advancements at level 30, 70 and 120. Three separate classes, under the adventurer branch, are created differently from normal Adventurers: Dual Blade (<a href="/wiki/Thief_(character_class)" title="Thief (character class)">Thief</a>), Kinesis (<a href="/wiki/Wizard_(character_class)" title="Wizard (character class)">Magician</a>) and Cannoneers (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pirate" title="Pirate">Pirate</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adventurers_2_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Adventurers_2-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cygnus Knight characters begin as Noblesse class and begin their journey on Ereve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Noblesse_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Noblesse-17">[17]</a></sup> At level 10, they are allowed to class advance into five Cygnus Knight classes, each one parallels to the Explorer class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cygnus_Knights_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cygnus_Knights-18">[18]</a></sup> Upon reaching the level limit of 120, Cygnus Knights are able to change into a level 50 explorer with benefits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Six Hero classes are currently available: Aran, Evan, Mercedes, Phantom, Luminous and Shade. The Hero classes are characters with background stories related to the antagonist of the game, the Black Mage. Aran has the Combo System, a unique feature to the class, allowing them to generate combo counts by hitting monsters, giving them buffs and allowing them to cast combo-consuming skills.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aran_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Aran-1">[1]</a></sup> Evan is similar to Magicians, but uses a <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragon</a>, which fights alongside them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Evan_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Evan-20">[20]</a></sup> Mercedes is an elven archer who uses Dual Bowguns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mercedes_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mercedes-21">[21]</a></sup> Phantom is a Thief character with the ability to mimic skills from Adventurer characters. Luminous is a magician of light who can also use dark magic which they absorbed from the Black Mage. Shade is the sixth and forgotten hero, whose existence was erased during the sealing of the Black Mage. They use spirits to aid them in battle.</p>
<p>Resistance characters begin in Edelstein, a town taken over by the Black Mage's followers; the Black Wings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Resistance_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Resistance-22">[22]</a></sup> The characters created under the Resistance branch begin as Citizens and have four available job advancements: Battle Mage, Wild Hunter, Blaster and Mechanic. A Battle Mage uses a staff for physical attacks, a Wild Hunter rides a <a href="/wiki/Jaguar" title="Jaguar">jaguar</a> while wielding a crossbow, a Blaster uses an arm cannon and a Mechanic uses a robotic suit to fight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Resistance_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-Resistance-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Demon, a separate class, under the resistance branch, is created differently from the normal Resistance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Demon_Slayer_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Demon_Slayer-24">[24]</a></sup> A Demon character can become a Demon Slayer, who uses one-handed blunt weapons and axes, or the Demon Avenger, who uses a specialised weapon called the Desperado. Xenon, another separate resistance character, is a human-turned-cyborg who escaped from the Black Wings' laboratories who uses Whip Blades in combat.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World">World</span></h3>
<p>There are three main continents common to all versions in the <i>MapleStory</i> world: Maple Island, Victoria Island and Ossyria. Maple Island is where adventurers start off their characters. Victoria Island contains eight cities, and is where most classes begin. Ossyria features seven distinct regions with varying environments: El Nath Mountains, Ludus Lake, Aqua Road, Minar Forest, Mu Lung Garden, Nihal Desert and Temple of Time. Ereve and Rien are separated from another land which is the beginning of another job of the game. The World Tour regions contain in-game versions of real life locations, such as <a href="/wiki/Names_of_Japan#Jipangu" title="Names of Japan">Zipangu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cash_Shop">Cash Shop</span></h3>
<p>The Cash Shop is an in-game shop where players use real-life currency to purchase unique <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virtual_good" title="Virtual good">virtual goods</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Avatar_(computing)" title="Avatar (computing)">avatars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Digital_pet" title="Digital pet">digital pets</a> and other in-game items.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Most Cash Shop items expire after a period of time. The Cash Shop also offers a shop permit, allowing players to set up a store in the in-game market location called the Free Market.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<p><i>MapleStory</i> was developed and released in South Korea and was localized to ten different regions as of 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> As other publishers license the game for their region, proxy blocks are put in place (excluding the Korean version, it requires a <a href="/wiki/Resident_registration_number" title="Resident registration number">KSSN</a>), forcing players to play their localized version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Proxy_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Proxy-27">[27]</a></sup> On July 7, 2011, LevelUp! Games announced that they will not be renewing the contract for localization in Brazil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_updates">Notable updates</span></h3>
<p>An update entitled <i>Big Bang</i> was released in Korean <i>MapleStory</i> in July 2010 with other versions following suit.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Big Bang changed the game's core coding, and changed many aspects of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Big_Bang_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Big_Bang-29">[29]</a></sup> An update entitled <i>Chaos</i> features the introduction of professions, and <a href="/wiki/Player_versus_player" title="Player versus player">PvP</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chaos_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chaos-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 4, 2015, Nexon announced a new update for MapleStory's North American server, entitled Black Heaven. Players that registered prior to the update and before March 9 received a free Ignition Pack if they logged in before March 14 and after the update went live. Black Heaven was released on March 11, 2015.</p>
<p>On December 1, 2015, Nexon released the Maplestory Reboot update for its North American server, which allows access to the Reboot world. This world has disabled trading, increased meso drop rates and weapon/equipment drops that are specific to only your class. Reboot has the same monsters as other worlds, but with increased stats and EXP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_adaptations">Media adaptations</span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/MapleStory_DS" title="MapleStory DS">MapleStory DS</a></i> is a game based on <i>MapleStory</i> and features a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Single-player" title="Single-player">single-player</a> experience. The game was released on April 15, 2010 in Korea.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a>, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/MapleStory_(anime)" title="MapleStory (anime)">MapleStory</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">メイプルストーリー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Meipuru Sutōrī</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, was adapted of the game and produced by <a href="/wiki/Madhouse_(company)" title="Madhouse (company)">Madhouse</a>. It was aired on <a href="/wiki/TV_Tokyo" title="TV Tokyo">TV Tokyo</a> between October 7, 2007 and March 30, 2008, spanning 25 episodes. The plot revolves around a warrior as he journeys to revive the world tree.</p>
<p>The North American publisher, <a href="/wiki/Nexon" title="Nexon">Nexon</a>, released a <i>MapleStory</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trading_card_game" title="Trading card game">trading card game</a> on November 6, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Nexon Korea and Japan have since released similar trading card games, while Nexon North America has discontinued this service.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Two adaptations of <i>MapleStory</i> have been released for <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a> by Nexon Mobile:<i>MapleStory: Thief Edition</i> and <i>MapleStory: Cygnus Knights Edition</i>. The <i>Thief Edition</i> has only one job, Thief, while the other edition features two jobs: a Soul Master and a Fire Wizard. The games do not have multiplayer.</p>
<p>On July 27, 2011, Nexon released a public beta of a <i><a href="/wiki/MapleStory_Adventures" title="MapleStory Adventures">MapleStory Adventures</a></i> as an adaptation for <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> gaming format. The game consist of mainly single-player, but incorporates the main goals and themes of the full <i>MapleStory</i> game, which includes creating an avatar, fighting monsters and completing quests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> This version has been discontinued as of July 31, 2013.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_reception">Financial reception</span></h2>
<p>In 2006, <a href="/wiki/Wizet" title="Wizet">Wizet</a> revealed they earned over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">US$</a> 300 million from <i>MapleStory</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In Global <i>MapleStory</i> (GMS), more than a million players have bought items in the Cash Shop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CS_Mil_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-CS_Mil-34">[34]</a></sup> In 2006, Nexon announced <i>MapleStory</i> had a combined total of 39 million user accounts worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stats_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stats-35">[35]</a></sup> <i>MapleStory</i> Global has over 8 million users as of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-America_Stats_2011_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-America_Stats_2011-36">[36]</a></sup> As of 2014, it was among the top ten MMOs by worldwide revenue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span></h2>
<p>A sequel for <i><a href="/wiki/MapleStory_2" title="MapleStory 2">MapleStory 2</a></i> has been confirmed and a Beta testing was conducted in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Unlike its predecessor, <i><a href="/wiki/MapleStory_2" title="MapleStory 2">MapleStory 2</a></i> takes place on a 3D voxel-based plane at an often <a href="/wiki/Isometric_projection" title="Isometric projection">isometric</a> angle.</p>
