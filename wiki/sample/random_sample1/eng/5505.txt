<p>The <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flags_of_the_U.S._states" title="Flags of the U.S. states">flag</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">state of Maine</a></b> features the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Maine" title="Coat of arms of Maine">state coat of arms</a> on a blue field. In the center of the shield, a moose rests under a tall <a href="/wiki/Pine" title="Pine">pine</a> tree. A farmer and seaman represent the traditional reliance on <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> and the sea by the state. The <a href="/wiki/Polaris" title="Polaris">North Star</a> represents the state motto: <i>Dirigo</i> ("I Lead").</p>
<p>There are no official colors for the coat of arms, so variations in coloration can be seen in flags from different manufacturers. The blue field, however, is specified to be the same blue as in the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_United_States" title="Flag of the United States">flag of the United States</a>. According to the official description, the flag should have a fringe of yellow silk and should have a blue and white silk cord attached at the spearhead. These embellishments are very rarely observed.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/North_American_Vexillological_Association" title="North American Vexillological Association">North American Vexillological Association</a> conducted a survey in 2001 that ranked Maine's current flag as one of the worst in design. Amongst the 72 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_State" title="US State">U.S. state</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Territory" title="US Territory">U.S. territorial</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_province" title="Canadian province">Canadian provincial</a> flags, Maine's flag ranked 60th (13th worst).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> NAVA criticized the unoriginality of "[state] seal on blue bed-sheet" design, which is currently used by almost half of the flags of U.S. states.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Original_Flag">Original Flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Original Flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Maine_(1901-1909).svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="1200" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Flag_of_Maine_%281901-1909%29.svg/220px-Flag_of_Maine_%281901-1909%29.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Flag_of_Maine_%281901-1909%29.svg/330px-Flag_of_Maine_%281901-1909%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Flag_of_Maine_%281901-1909%29.svg/440px-Flag_of_Maine_%281901-1909%29.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Maine_(1901-1909).svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Digital reproduction of the 1901 flag</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Originally, the state flag consisted of a green pine tree, symbol of <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> in the center, with a blue "North Star", all on a buff-colored background.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The image to the right contains a digital reproduction of the 1901 Flag. The reproduction uses the same pine tree used in the Merchant Marine Flag of Maine. Also, the size of the tree, set to 1/3 the fly and 2/3 the hoist, is guesswork.</p>
<table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">The State Flag is hereby declared to be buff charged with the emblem of the State, a pine tree proper in the center and the polar star (a mullet of five points), in blue in the upper corner. The star to be equidistant from the hoist and upper border of the flag, the distance from the two borders to the center of the star equal to about one quarter the hoist. This distance and the size of the star being proportionate to the size of the flag.</td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>The Maine legislature approved the current flag on February 23, 1909.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ensign">Ensign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ensign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Naval_Ensign_of_Maine.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1800" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Naval_Ensign_of_Maine.svg/220px-Naval_Ensign_of_Maine.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Naval_Ensign_of_Maine.svg/330px-Naval_Ensign_of_Maine.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Naval_Ensign_of_Maine.svg/440px-Naval_Ensign_of_Maine.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Naval_Ensign_of_Maine.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Merchant and Marine Flag of Maine</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Maine is also one of only two states with a separate <a href="/wiki/Ensign" title="Ensign">ensign</a>, which is rarely seen (the other is <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Massachusetts" title="Flag of Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>). It features symbols from the current flag and the older one, with a white field and green pine tree. The green pine tree has the seaman's anchor, and the words "MAINE" and "DIRIGO" around it.</p>
