<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bal%C3%A1zs_Megyeri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ferencv.C3.A1ros">Ferencváros</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bal%C3%A1zs_Megyeri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ferencváros">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, Megyeri began his career with <a href="/wiki/Ferencv%C3%A1rosi_TC" title="Ferencvárosi TC">Ferencvárosi TC</a> and played a short time on youthside for <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City</a>, before returning to his previous club in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On 23 August 2009, he began a short trial with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hertha_BSC_Berlin" title="Hertha BSC Berlin">Hertha BSC Berlin</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> but nothing came of it.</p>
<p>Megyeri made his <a href="/wiki/Nemzeti_Bajnoks%C3%A1g_I" title="Nemzeti Bajnokság I">Nemzeti Bajnokság I</a> debut on 26 August, starting in a 0–0 away draw against <a href="/wiki/Szombathelyi_Halad%C3%A1s" title="Szombathelyi Haladás">Szombathelyi Haladás</a>. He appeared in 24 league matches during <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Nemzeti_Bajnoks%C3%A1g_I" title="2009–10 Nemzeti Bajnokság I">the campaign</a>, with his side finishing seventh.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympiacos">Olympiacos</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bal%C3%A1zs_Megyeri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Olympiacos">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 June 2010, Megyeri signed a three-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C." title="Olympiacos F.C.">Olympiacos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Olympiakos_announcement_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Olympiakos_announcement-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Olympiakos_Contract_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Olympiakos_Contract-5">[5]</a></sup> He took part in the international match "8th Match Against Poverty" on 14 December at the <a href="/wiki/Karaiskakis_Stadium" title="Karaiskakis Stadium">Karaiskakis Stadium</a>, as the club's All Star team goalkeeper.</p>
<p>Megyeri made his debut for the club on 3 April 2011, playing the full 90 minutes in a 3–1 home win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kavala_F.C." title="Kavala F.C.">Kavala</a>. He appeared in only three matches during <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Superleague_Greece" title="2010–11 Superleague Greece">the campaign</a>, being a third-choice behind <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antonis_Nikopolidis" title="Antonis Nikopolidis">Antonis Nikopolidis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Urko_Pardo" title="Urko Pardo">Urko Pardo</a>.</p>
<p>After Nikopolidis' retirement and Pardo's departure to <a href="/wiki/APOEL_FC" title="APOEL FC">APOEL</a>, Megyeri was challenged by new signings <a href="/wiki/Franco_Costanzo" title="Franco Costanzo">Franco Costanzo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iosif_Daskalakis" title="Iosif Daskalakis">Iosif Daskalakis</a>. After Constanzo's poor performances, he was elected as first-choice; despite the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Roy_Carroll" title="Roy Carroll">Roy Carroll</a> in January 2012, he continued to appear regularly, contributing with 22 matches.</p>
<p>Megyeri battled for the starting spot with Carroll in <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Superleague_Greece" title="2012–13 Superleague Greece">2012–13</a>, but was later overtaken by new signing <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Jim%C3%A9nez_Gago" title="Roberto Jiménez Gago">Roberto</a> in <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Superleague_Greece" title="2013–14 Superleague Greece">the following season</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Getafe">Getafe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bal%C3%A1zs_Megyeri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Getafe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 July 2015 Megyeri signed a three-year deal with <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> side <a href="/wiki/Getafe_CF" title="Getafe CF">Getafe CF</a>, after his contract with Olympiacos expired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bal%C3%A1zs_Megyeri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After representing <a href="/wiki/Hungary_national_football_team" title="Hungary national football team">Hungary</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hungary_national_under-21_football_team" title="Hungary national under-21 football team">under-21 level</a>, Megyeri was called up to the main squad on 16 May 2012, for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exhibition_match" title="Exhibition match">friendly</a> against <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic_national_football_team" title="Czech Republic national football team">Czech Republic</a>. However, he was only an unused substitute during the match. Megyeri was named in Hungary's provisional squad for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a> but was cut from the final squad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
