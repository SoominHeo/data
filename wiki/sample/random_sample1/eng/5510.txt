<p>The <b>Grand Duchy of Mecklenburg-Strelitz</b> was a territory in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Germany" title="Northern Germany">Northern Germany</a>, held by the younger line of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Mecklenburg" title="House of Mecklenburg">House of Mecklenburg</a> residing in <a href="/wiki/Neustrelitz" title="Neustrelitz">Neustrelitz</a>. Like the neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Mecklenburg-Schwerin" title="Grand Duchy of Mecklenburg-Schwerin">Grand Duchy of Mecklenburg-Schwerin</a>, it was a sovereign member state of the <a href="/wiki/German_Confederation" title="German Confederation">German Confederation</a> and became a <a href="/wiki/Federated_state" title="Federated state">federated state</a> of the <a href="/wiki/North_German_Confederation" title="North German Confederation">North German Confederation</a> and finally of the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> upon the <a href="/wiki/Unification_of_Germany" title="Unification of Germany">unification</a> of 1871. After <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and the <a href="/wiki/German_Revolution_of_1918%E2%80%9319" title="German Revolution of 1918–19">German Revolution of 1918–19</a> it was succeeded by the <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" title="Free State of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">Free State of Mecklenburg-Strelitz</a>.</p>

<p>It consisted of two detached parts of the <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg" title="Mecklenburg">Mecklenburg</a> region: the larger <a href="/wiki/Lordship_of_Stargard" title="Lordship of Stargard">Lordship of Stargard</a> with the residence of Neustrelitz to the southeast of the Grand Duchy of Mecklenburg-Schwerin and the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Ratzeburg" title="Principality of Ratzeburg">Principality of Ratzeburg</a> on the west. The first was bounded by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Prussian</a> provinces of <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Pomerania_(1815%E2%80%931945)" title="Province of Pomerania (1815–1945)">Pomerania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Brandenburg" title="Province of Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a>, the second bordered on the <a href="/wiki/Saxe-Lauenburg" title="Saxe-Lauenburg">Duchy of Lauenburg</a> (incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Schleswig-Holstein" title="Province of Schleswig-Holstein">Province of Schleswig-Holstein</a> in 1876) and the territory of the <a href="/wiki/Free_City_of_L%C3%BCbeck" title="Free City of Lübeck">Free City of Lübeck</a>. Major towns beside Neustrelitz included <a href="/wiki/Neubrandenburg" title="Neubrandenburg">Neubrandenburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Friedland,_Mecklenburg-Vorpommern" title="Friedland, Mecklenburg-Vorpommern">Friedland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woldegk" title="Woldegk">Woldegk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burg_Stargard" title="Burg Stargard">Stargard</a>, <a href="/wiki/F%C3%BCrstenberg/Havel" title="Fürstenberg/Havel">Fürstenberg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wesenberg,_Mecklenburg-Vorpommern" title="Wesenberg, Mecklenburg-Vorpommern">Wesenberg</a>. The Grand Duchy also comprised the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commandry_(feudalism)" title="Commandry (feudalism)">commandries</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">Knights Hospitaller</a> in <a href="/wiki/Commandry_of_Mirow" title="Commandry of Mirow">Mirow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commandry_of_Nemerow" title="Commandry of Nemerow">Nemerow</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grand_Duchy_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" title="Duchy of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">Duchy of Mecklenburg-Strelitz</a>, established according to the dynastic <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Hamburg_(1701)" title="Treaty of Hamburg (1701)">Treaty of Hamburg</a> in 1701, adopted the <a href="/wiki/Estates_of_the_realm" title="Estates of the realm">corporative</a> constitution of the sister <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Mecklenburg-Schwerin" title="Duchy of Mecklenburg-Schwerin">Duchy of Mecklenburg-Schwerin</a> by an act of September 1755.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChisholm19111020_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChisholm19111020-1">[1]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a> it was spared the infliction of a <a href="/wiki/First_French_Empire" title="First French Empire">French</a> occupation through the good offices of King <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I_Joseph_of_Bavaria" title="Maximilian I Joseph of Bavaria">Maximilian I Joseph of Bavaria</a> and his minister <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_von_Montgelas" title="Maximilian von Montgelas">Maximilian von Montgelas</a>; Duke <a href="/wiki/Charles_II,_Grand_Duke_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" title="Charles II, Grand Duke of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">Charles II of Mecklenburg-Strelitz</a> declared neutrality in 1806 and joined the <a href="/wiki/Confederation_of_the_Rhine" title="Confederation of the Rhine">Confederation of the Rhine</a> in 1808, however, he withdrew in 1813 on the eve of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_campaign_(Napoleonic_Wars)" title="German campaign (Napoleonic Wars)">German campaign</a> in favor of an alliance against <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>. He joined the <a href="/wiki/German_Confederation" title="German Confederation">German Confederation</a> established after the 1815 <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a> to succeed the dissolved Holy Roman Empire; he and his cousin <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Francis_I,_Grand_Duke_of_Mecklenburg-Schwerin" title="Frederick Francis I, Grand Duke of Mecklenburg-Schwerin">Frederick Francis I of Mecklenburg-Schwerin</a> both assumed the title of <a href="/wiki/Grand_duke" title="Grand duke">grand duke</a> (<i>Großherzog von Mecklenburg</i>).</p>

<p>Though Grand Duke <a href="/wiki/Frederick_William,_Grand_Duke_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" title="Frederick William, Grand Duke of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">Frederick William</a> openly rejected the Prussian annexation of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hanover" title="Kingdom of Hanover">Kingdom of Hanover</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Prussian_Army" title="Prussian Army">Prussian Army</a> had been aided by soldiers from Mecklenburg-Strelitz in the <a href="/wiki/Austro-Prussian_War" title="Austro-Prussian War">Austro-Prussian War</a> of 1866. Thereupon, the Grand Duchy joined the <a href="/wiki/North_German_Confederation" title="North German Confederation">North German Confederation</a> and the reconstituted <i><a href="/wiki/Zollverein" title="Zollverein">Zollverein</a></i>. Also in the <a href="/wiki/Franco-Prussian_War" title="Franco-Prussian War">Franco-Prussian War</a> of 1870/71, the Kingdom of Prussia received valuable assistance from Mecklenburg-Strelitz.</p>
<p>In 1871 both Mecklenburg-Schwerin and Mecklenburg-Strelitz became <a href="/wiki/States_of_the_German_Empire" title="States of the German Empire">States of the German Empire</a>. Mecklenburg-Strelitz returned one member to the <a href="/wiki/Bundesrat_of_Germany" title="Bundesrat of Germany">Bundesrat</a> chamber of states. However, the Grand Duke was still styled <a href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Mecklenburg" title="List of rulers of Mecklenburg">Prince of the Wends</a> and the internal government of Mecklenburg-Strelitz remained unmodernized. Mocked by Chancellor <a href="/wiki/Otto_von_Bismarck" title="Otto von Bismarck">Otto von Bismarck</a> as a safe haven in the face of threatening apocalypse "as everything there happens 50 years later", the Grand Duchy had always been a government of feudal character. The Grand Dukes exercised absolute power through their ministers, with an antiquated type of diet representing social classes. It met for a short session each year, and at other times was represented by a committee consisting of the proprietors of knights' estates (<span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Rittergüter</i></span>), known as the <i>Ritterschaft</i>, and of the <i>Landschaft</i>, which was composed of burgomasters of selected towns.</p>
<p>There was now a renewal of agitation for a more democratic constitution, and the German Reichstag gave some countenance to this movement. In 1904 <a href="/wiki/Adolphus_Frederick_V,_Grand_Duke_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" title="Adolphus Frederick V, Grand Duke of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">Adolphus Frederick V</a>, a son of Grand Duke <a href="/wiki/Frederick_William,_Grand_Duke_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" title="Frederick William, Grand Duke of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">Frederick William</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Princess_Augusta_of_Cambridge" title="Princess Augusta of Cambridge">Princess Augusta of Cambridge</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Adolphus,_Duke_of_Cambridge" title="Prince Adolphus, Duke of Cambridge">Prince Adolphus</a>, became grand duke of Mecklenburg-Strelitz. In 1907, the grand duke promised a constitution to the duchy's subjects, but this was met with opposition from the nobility.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grand_Duchy_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Mecklenburg-Strelitz dynasty ended just prior to the loss of the monarchy in developments associated with <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. At that time, there existed only two surviving recognized male dynasts of Strelitz, the young Grand Duke <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adolphus_Frederick_VI,_Grand_Duke_of_Mecklenburg" title="Adolphus Frederick VI, Grand Duke of Mecklenburg">Adolphus Frederick VI</a>, and his cousin <a href="/wiki/Charles_Michael,_Duke_of_Mecklenburg" title="Charles Michael, Duke of Mecklenburg">Charles Michael</a>, who was in <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian</a> service, being a son of Grand Duchess <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchess_Catherine_Mikhailovna_of_Russia" title="Grand Duchess Catherine Mikhailovna of Russia">Catherine Mikhailovna</a>. In 1914, before the proclamation of war between Germany and Russia, Duke Charles Michael renounced his Mecklenburgish citizenship. On 23 February 1918, Grand Duke Adolf Frederick VI committed suicide, leaving his cousin Charles Michael as heir to the Strelitz throne. Being in Russia, however, Charles Michael did not assume the throne, and in 1918 he wrote to Grand Duke <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederick_Francis_IV,_Grand_Duke_of_Mecklenburg" title="Frederick Francis IV, Grand Duke of Mecklenburg">Frederick Francis IV of Mecklenburg-Schwerin</a>, who was acting as regent in Strelitz, stating that he wished to renounce his rights of succession to Strelitz, though the letter was only received by Frederick Francis in 1919 after the end of the German monarchies, so the issue of succession could not be resolved at the time.</p>
<p>The House of Mecklenburg-Strelitz survives to this day, descending from Duke <a href="/wiki/George,_Duke_of_Mecklenburg" title="George, Duke of Mecklenburg">George</a>, the morganatic son of Duke George Alexander with Countess Natalia Carlow and nephew of Duke Charles Michael, who adopted him in 1928. George subsequently assumed the title "Duke of Mecklenburg" (<a href="/wiki/Serene_Highness" title="Serene Highness">Serene Highness</a>) which was acknowledged by Grand Duke Frederick Francis IV of Mecklenburg-Schwerin. He was later given the <a href="/wiki/Style_(manner_of_address)" title="Style (manner of address)">style</a> of "Highness" by the House of Mecklenburg-Schwerin. George's grandson <a href="/wiki/Borwin,_Duke_of_Mecklenburg" title="Borwin, Duke of Mecklenburg">Borwin</a> is the present head of the House of Mecklenburg-Strelitz.</p>
<p>The county of <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg_County,_North_Carolina" title="Mecklenburg County, North Carolina">Mecklenburg</a> in the U.S. state of North Carolina, which includes the city of <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte</a>, is named after the duchy. The City of Charlotte, known as "The Queen City" was named for Queen Charlotte, wife of King George III of Great Britain. Queen Charlotte was Princess <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" title="Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz</a>, born on 19 May 1744. She was the youngest daughter of Duke Charles Louis Frederick of Mecklenburg-Strelitz, Prince of Mirow and his wife Princess Elizabeth Albertine of Saxe-Hildburghausen.</p>
