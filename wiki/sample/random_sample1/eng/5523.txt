<p><b>Mehmed Orhan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Turkish_language" title="Ottoman Turkish language">Ottoman Turkish</a>محمد اورخان 1) July 1909 – 5/12 March 1994) was the 42nd head of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_dynasty" title="Ottoman dynasty">Ottoman dynasty</a> from 1983 to 1994. He succeeded as head of the Ottoman dynasty on 9 December 1983, following the death of <a href="/wiki/Ali_V%C3%A2sib" title="Ali Vâsib">Ali Vâsib</a>. If reigning, he would have been styled as <b>Grand Sultan Orhan II</b>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mehmed_Orhan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born at Serencebey Palace or at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=K%C4%B1z%C4%B1ltoprak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kızıltoprak (page does not exist)">Kızıltoprak</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Minor" title="Asia Minor">Asia Minor</a> or according to <a href="/wiki/Hamide_Ay%C5%9Fe_Sultan" title="Hamide Ayşe Sultan">Hamide Ayşe Sultan</a> in Naime Sultan Palace. He was the son of Prince <a href="/wiki/%C5%9Eehzade_Mehmed_Abd%C3%BClkadir" title="Şehzade Mehmed Abdülkadir">Şehzade Mehmed Abdülkadir</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captain_(land_and_air)" title="Captain (land and air)">Captain</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottoman_Army" title="Ottoman Army">Ottoman Army</a>, by his third wife Mihriban Hanım and grandson of <a href="/wiki/Abdul_Hamid_II" title="Abdul Hamid II">Abdul Hamid II</a> by his fourth wife Empress <a href="/wiki/Bidar_Kad%C4%B1n" title="Bidar Kadın">Bidar Kadın</a>.</p>
<p>Mehmed Orhan worked as a shipbuilder in Brazil, a taxi driver in <a href="/wiki/Beirut" title="Beirut">Beirut</a> and <a href="/wiki/Damascus" title="Damascus">Damascus</a>, a cemetery attendant in USA and an aide to King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zog_of_Albania" title="Zog of Albania">Zog of Albania</a>. He died in <a href="/wiki/Nice" title="Nice">Nice</a> and was buried there.</p>
<p>In a 1990 feature in <i>Life</i> magazine, he said his legacy is "both sacred and laughable," and said, "To be Ottoman is to know how to breathe with time."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriages_and_issue">Marriages and issue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mehmed_Orhan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marriages and issue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mehmed Orhan first married Nafiye Yeghen (<a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>, 1913 – ?) in January 1933, annulled in 1947. In 1944, he married a young pregnant French American Actress <a href="/wiki/Marguerite_Irma_Fournier" title="Marguerite Irma Fournier">Marguerite Irma Fournier</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. He had a daughter by his first wife and a stepson by his second wife:</p>
<ul>
<li>Fatma Necla Sultan (born <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>, 14 September 1933, died 2010 at Zurich) married twice and had two sons.</li>
<li>Mehmed Selim Orhan (born <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, 3 October 1943) stepson of a <a href="/wiki/Morganatic_marriage" title="Morganatic marriage">morganatic marriage</a></li>
</ul>
