<p><i><b>Men at Work</b></i> is an American <a href="/wiki/Comedy" title="Comedy">comedy</a> series that aired on <a href="/wiki/TBS_(U.S._TV_channel)" title="TBS (U.S. TV channel)">TBS</a>. The series was created by <a href="/wiki/Breckin_Meyer" title="Breckin Meyer">Breckin Meyer</a> and stars <a href="/wiki/Danny_Masterson" title="Danny Masterson">Danny Masterson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Cassidy_(actor)" title="Michael Cassidy (actor)">Michael Cassidy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adam_Busch" title="Adam Busch">Adam Busch</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Lesure" title="James Lesure">James Lesure</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Meredith_Hagner" title="Meredith Hagner">Meredith Hagner</a>. The series premiered on May 24, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 11, 2014, TBS canceled <i>Men at Work</i> after three seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cancellation_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-cancellation-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Men_at_Work_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series follows Milo (<a href="/wiki/Danny_Masterson" title="Danny Masterson">Danny Masterson</a>), a man who recently ended his relationship with his girlfriend, Lisa, and tries to re-enter the dating scene with the help of his three best friends/co-workers, Tyler, Gibbs, and Neal (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Cassidy_(actor)" title="Michael Cassidy (actor)">Michael Cassidy</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Lesure" title="James Lesure">James Lesure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adam_Busch" title="Adam Busch">Adam Busch</a>). Neal is the only one who has a girlfriend, Amy (<a href="/wiki/Meredith_Hagner" title="Meredith Hagner">Meredith Hagner</a>). Together, the four friends help each other navigate through relationships, friendship and working together in New York City at the same magazine, "Full Steam". The gang mainly hang out at the diner or at the workplace.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_production">Development and production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Men_at_Work_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Development and production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">pilot</a> appeared on TBS's development slate in May 2011. The series was created by <a href="/wiki/Breckin_Meyer" title="Breckin Meyer">Breckin Meyer</a>, who also serves as executive producer alongside <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Tarses" title="Jamie Tarses">Jamie Tarses</a> and Julia Franz, and the production companies, <a href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures_Television" title="Sony Pictures Television">Sony Pictures Television</a> and FanFare Productions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On August 26, 2011, TBS placed a pilot order for <i>Men at Work</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2011, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Masterson" title="Danny Masterson">Danny Masterson</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Lesure" title="James Lesure">James Lesure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adam_Busch" title="Adam Busch">Adam Busch</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Cassidy_(actor)" title="Michael Cassidy (actor)">Michael Cassidy</a> were cast as the four leads, with Masterson playing the role of Milo, the recently dumped guy whose friends try to help him get back in the dating scene; Lesure playing the role of Gibbs, a ladies' man and photographer at the magazine; Busch playing the role of Neal, a reporter at the magazine and the only one in the group with a girlfriend; and Cassidy playing the role of Tyler, a stylish feature writer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Meredith_Hagner" title="Meredith Hagner">Meredith Hagner</a> was the final addition to the cast as Amy, Neal's girlfriend whose father owns the magazine.</p>
<p>On January 6, 2012, TBS picked up <i>Men at Work</i> for a first season of 10 episodes that premiered on May 24, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Production on the first season began in April 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 20, 2013, TBS picked up <i>Men at Work</i> for a third season of 10 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Season 3 premiered on January 15, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 11, 2014, TBS canceled <i>Men at Work</i> after three seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cancellation_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-cancellation-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
