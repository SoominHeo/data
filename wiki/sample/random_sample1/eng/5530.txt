<p><b>Mendy López Aude</b> (born October 15, 1973 in <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">Santo Domingo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Utility_player" title="Utility player">utility player</a> who has played for many professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> teams including in the United States, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%A9xico" title="México">México</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>. He currently plays for the <a href="/wiki/Rieleros_de_Aguascalientes" title="Rieleros de Aguascalientes">Rieleros de Aguascalientes</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_League" title="Mexican League">Mexican League</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_summary">Career summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mendy_L%C3%B3pez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>López was signed as an amateur <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agent</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a> on February 26, <a href="/wiki/1992_in_baseball" title="1992 in baseball">1992</a>. He is often referred to as a "<a href="/wiki/Journeyman_(sports)" title="Journeyman (sports)">journeyman</a>", a title bestowed upon those in the sports world who play on several different teams within a short amount of time. Lopez has played for four <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> teams over the span of his 7-year career: the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a> (<a href="/wiki/1998_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1998 Major League Baseball season">1998</a>-<a href="/wiki/1999_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1999 Major League Baseball season">1999</a>, <a href="/wiki/2003_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2003 Major League Baseball season">2003</a>-<a href="/wiki/2004_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2004 Major League Baseball season">2004</a>), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_Marlins" title="Florida Marlins">Florida Marlins</a> (<a href="/wiki/2000_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2000 Major League Baseball season">2000</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> (<a href="/wiki/2001_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2001 Major League Baseball season">2001</a>), and the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> (2001-<a href="/wiki/2002_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2002 Major League Baseball season">2002</a>).</p>
<p>On July 9, 2004, the Kansas City Royals sold his contract to the <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Lions" title="Samsung Lions">Samsung Lions</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Baseball_Organization" title="Korea Baseball Organization">Korea Baseball Organization</a>.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2007_in_baseball" title="2007 in baseball">2007</a>, López played for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sultanes_Monterrey" title="Sultanes Monterrey">Sultanes Monterrey</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_League_(baseball)" title="Mexican League (baseball)">Mexican League</a>. He <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">hit</a> .304 with 21 <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home runs</a> and 67 <a href="/wiki/Run_batted_in" title="Run batted in">RBI</a>. In <a href="/wiki/2008_in_baseball" title="2008 in baseball">2008</a>, he again played for Monterrey batting .321 with 22 home runs and 97 RBI. He was named to the midseason All-Star team in <a href="/wiki/2006_in_baseball" title="2006 in baseball">2006</a>, 2007, and 2008. He played for <a href="/wiki/%C3%81guilas_Cibae%C3%B1as" title="Águilas Cibaeñas">Águilas Cibaeñas</a> in the Dominican Winter League in 2008.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Claim_to_fame">Claim to fame</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mendy_L%C3%B3pez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Claim to fame">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>López is probably best known for his dramatic <a href="/wiki/Pinch_hitter" title="Pinch hitter">pinch-hit</a>, three-run, game-tying home run against <a href="/wiki/Chicago_White_Sox" title="Chicago White Sox">Chicago White Sox</a> <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1maso_Marte" title="Dámaso Marte">Dámaso Marte</a> with 1 out in the bottom of the ninth inning during the Kansas City Royals' first game of the 2004 MLB season. The Royals went on to win the game 9–7.</p>
