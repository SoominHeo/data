<p><b>Menlo Park</b> is a city at the eastern edge of <a href="/wiki/San_Mateo_County,_California" title="San Mateo County, California">San Mateo County</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area" title="San Francisco Bay Area">San Francisco Bay Area</a> of California, in the United States. It is bordered by <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay" title="San Francisco Bay">San Francisco Bay</a> on the north and east; <a href="/wiki/East_Palo_Alto,_California" title="East Palo Alto, California">East Palo Alto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stanford,_California" title="Stanford, California">Stanford</a> to the south; <a href="/wiki/Atherton,_California" title="Atherton, California">Atherton</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Fair_Oaks,_California" title="North Fair Oaks, California">North Fair Oaks</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Redwood_City,_California" title="Redwood City, California">Redwood City</a> to the west. Menlo Park is one of the most educated cities in the state of California and the United States, with nearly 70% of its residents having earned a degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-California.27s_Most_Educated_Cities_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-California.27s_Most_Educated_Cities-7">[7]</a></sup> Menlo Park had 32,026 inhabitants according to the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Menlo_Park,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 17.4 square miles (45 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 9.8 square miles (25 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 7.6 square miles (20 km<sup>2</sup>) is water. The total area is 43.79% water. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Main_street" title="Main street">main street</a> in downtown Menlo Park is Santa Cruz Avenue, with the <i>Menlo Center</i> situated at its intersection with <a href="/wiki/El_Camino_Real_(California)" title="El Camino Real (California)">El Camino Real</a>. The Menlo Park <a href="/wiki/Civic_center" title="Civic center">Civic center</a> is bounded by Ravenswood Avenue, Alma Street, Laurel Street and Burgess Drive. It contains the <a href="/wiki/City_council" title="City council">council</a> offices, library, police station and <i>Burgess Park</i> which has various recreational facilities.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Menlo_Park,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:MenloParkTrainStation.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="510" data-file-width="800" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/MenloParkTrainStation.png/220px-MenloParkTrainStation.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/MenloParkTrainStation.png/330px-MenloParkTrainStation.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/MenloParkTrainStation.png/440px-MenloParkTrainStation.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:MenloParkTrainStation.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Menlo Park Station, ca. 1918</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the nineteenth century two Irish immigrants, Dennis J. Oliver and his brother-in-law D. C. McGlynn, purchased a 1,700-acre (6.9 km<sup>2</sup>) tract of land on the former <a href="/wiki/Rancho_de_las_Pulgas" title="Rancho de las Pulgas">Rancho de las Pulgas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hoover_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-hoover-9">[9]</a></sup> In the 1850s they erected a gate with a wooden arch bearing the inscription "Menlo Park" at the entrance to their property (now the intersection of Middle Ave and El Camino Real).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-durham_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-durham-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stanger.2C_1963._pg._109_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stanger.2C_1963._pg._109-11">[11]</a></sup> The word "Menlo" derived from the owners' former home of <a href="/wiki/Menlo,_County_Galway" title="Menlo, County Galway">Menlo</a> in County Galway, Ireland, and is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglicized" title="Anglicized">Anglicized</a> version of the original Irish name of the place, <i>Mionloch</i>, meaning "middle lake".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hoover_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-hoover-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1863, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Francisco_and_San_Jose_Rail_Road" title="San Francisco and San Jose Rail Road">San Francisco and San Jose Rail Road</a> named a nearby station "Menlo Park" after the sign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hoover_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-hoover-9">[9]</a></sup> The 1867 station building still stands on the platform of the current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Menlo_Park_(Caltrain_station)" title="Menlo Park (Caltrain station)">Caltrain station</a>, used by the local Chamber of Commerce. The town of Menlo Park grew up around this station, becoming a popular home for San Francisco businessmen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hoover_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-hoover-9">[9]</a></sup> A post office arrived in 1870, and the city was incorporated in 1874.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-durham_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-durham-10">[10]</a></sup> The original arch which gave its name to the stations and ultimately the city survived until 1922, when it was destroyed in an automobile accident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hoover_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-hoover-9">[9]</a></sup> The origin of the name of <i>Menlo Park, California</i> (<i>ca</i> 1850)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stanger.2C_1963._pg._109_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stanger.2C_1963._pg._109-11">[11]</a></sup> pre-dates any work done by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Edison" title="Thomas Edison">Thomas Edison</a> (<i>ca</i> 1876) in <a href="/wiki/Menlo_Park,_New_Jersey" title="Menlo Park, New Jersey">Menlo Park</a>, New Jersey.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Menlo_Park,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> reported that Menlo Park had a population of 32,026. The population density was 3,271.3 people per square mile (1,263.1/km²). The racial makeup of Menlo Park was 22,494 (70.2%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 1,551 (4.8%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 156 (0.5%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 3,157 (9.9%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 454 (1.4%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 2,776 (8.7%) from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1,438 (4.5%) from two or more races. There were 5,902 people (18.4%) of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> background, of any race. Among the Hispanic population, 4,303 were of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_American" title="Mexican American">Mexican</a> ancestry.</p>
<p>The Census reported that 31,181 people (97.4% of the population) lived in households, 599 (1.9%) lived in non-institutionalized group quarters, and 246 (0.8%) were institutionalized.</p>
<p>There were 12,347 households, out of which 4,112 (33.3%) had children under the age of 18 living in them, 6,163 (49.9%) were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">opposite-sex married couples</a> living together, 1,039 (8.4%) had a female householder with no husband present, 371 (3.0%) had a male householder with no wife present. There were 642 (5.2%) <a href="/wiki/POSSLQ" title="POSSLQ">unmarried opposite-sex partnerships</a>, and 102 (0.8%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Same-sex_partnerships" title="Same-sex partnerships">same-sex married couples or partnerships</a>. 3,672 households (29.7%) were made up of individuals and 1,371 (11.1%) had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.53. There were 7,573 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Family_(U.S._Census)" title="Family (U.S. Census)">families</a> (61.3% of all households); the average family size was 3.20.</p>
<p>In terms of age 7,805 residents (24.4%) were under the age of 18, 1,817 people (5.7%) aged 18 to 24, 9,563 people (29.9%) aged 25 to 44, 8,263 people (25.8%) aged 45 to 64, and 4,578 people (14.3%) who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 38.7 years. For every 100 females there were 93.7 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 91.5 males.</p>
<p>There were 13,085 housing units at an average density of 1,336.6 per square mile (516.1/km²), of which 6,927 (56.1%) were owner-occupied, and 5,420 (43.9%) were occupied by renters. The homeowner vacancy rate was 1.1%; the rental vacancy rate was 5.2%. 18,972 people (59.2% of the population) lived in owner-occupied housing units and 12,209 people (38.1%) lived in rental housing units.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000">2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Menlo_Park,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2000">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the census<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-16">[16]</a></sup> of 2009, there were 33,690 people, 12,543 households, and 7,248 families residing in the city. The population density was 3,441.3 inhabitants per square mile (1,328.7/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 14,026 housing units at an average density of 1,432.7 per square mile (553.2/km²). 64.2% spoke English, 19.5% Spanish, other <a href="/wiki/Indo-European_languages" title="Indo-European languages">Indo-European</a> 4.2%, 4.6% Chinese or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standard_Mandarin" title="Standard Mandarin">Mandarin</a>, and other language 0.7%, as their first language from estimate census 2009.</p>
<p>There were 12,543 households out of which 25.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 41.5% were married couples living together, 10.8% had a female householder with no husband present, and 45.67% were non-families. 35.1% of all households were made up of individuals and 11.1% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.46 and the average family size was 3.15.</p>
<p>In the city the population was spread out with 25.8% under the age of 18, 9.1% from 18 to 24, 31.5% from 25 to 44, 20.4% from 45 to 64, and 14.2% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 32 years. For every 100 females there were 94.0 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 90.6 males.</p>
<p>As of the 2000 estimate census, the median income for a household in the city was $82,609. Males had a median income of $77,766 versus $59,101 for females. The per capita income for the city was $51,341. About 5.9% of families and 7.3% of the population were below the poverty line, including 9.8% of those under age 18 and 8.7% of those over age 64. As of 2009 the median income for a family was $123,251.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Menlo_Park,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Government and politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>City Council members are elected at-large to staggered, 4-year terms, in non-partisan municipal elections held every two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="County.2C_state.2C_and_federal_representation">County, state, and federal representation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Menlo_Park,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: County, state, and federal representation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the <a href="/wiki/Board_of_supervisors" title="Board of supervisors">San Mateo County Board of Supervisors</a>, Menlo Park is split between Supervisorial District 3 and Supervisorial District 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> represented by Don Horsley and Warren Slocum, respectively.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/California_State_Legislature" title="California State Legislature">California State Legislature</a>, Menlo Park is in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_13th_State_Senate_district" title="California's 13th State Senate district">the 13th Senate District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Democratic_Party" title="California Democratic Party">Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Hill_(politician)" title="Jerry Hill (politician)">Jerry Hill</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_24th_State_Assembly_district" title="California's 24th State Assembly district">the 24th Assembly District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Democratic_Party" title="California Democratic Party">Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/Marc_Berman" title="Marc Berman">Marc Berman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a>, Menlo Park is split between California's <a href="/wiki/California%27s_14th_congressional_district" title="California's 14th congressional district">14th</a> and <a href="/wiki/California%27s_18th_congressional_district" title="California's 18th congressional district">18th</a> congressional districts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> represented by <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Speier" title="Jackie Speier">Jackie Speier</a> (<a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">D</a>–<a href="/wiki/Hillsborough,_California" title="Hillsborough, California">Hillsborough</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Anna_Eshoo" title="Anna Eshoo">Anna Eshoo</a> (<a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">D</a>–<a href="/wiki/Atherton,_California" title="Atherton, California">Atherton</a>), respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Menlo_Park,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For primary schools, the central portions of Menlo Park are served by the <a href="/wiki/Menlo_Park_City_School_District" title="Menlo Park City School District">Menlo Park City School District</a>, while the Belle Haven neighborhood and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Veterans_Affairs" title="United States Department of Veterans Affairs">VA</a> hospital are served by the <a href="/wiki/Ravenswood_City_School_District" title="Ravenswood City School District">Ravenswood City School District</a>, and the Sharon Heights and Stanford Hills neighborhoods served by the <a href="/wiki/Las_Lomitas_Elementary_School_District" title="Las Lomitas Elementary School District">Las Lomitas Elementary School District</a>. For high school, Menlo Park is part of the <a href="/wiki/Sequoia_Union_High_School_District" title="Sequoia Union High School District">Sequoia Union High School District</a>, with all of Menlo Park falling into the boundaries of <a href="/wiki/Menlo-Atherton_High_School" title="Menlo-Atherton High School">Menlo-Atherton High School</a>.</p>
<p>Catholic parochial schools are St. Raymond Catholic Elementary School and Nativity Elementary School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peninsula_School_(U.S.)" title="Peninsula School (U.S.)">Peninsula School</a> is a private progressive primary school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Trinity_School_(Menlo_Park)" title="Trinity School (Menlo Park)">Trinity School</a> is an Episcopalian elementary school. The German-American International School with about 315 students, K-12, is also in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>For higher education, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Patrick%27s_Seminary_and_University" title="Saint Patrick's Seminary and University">Saint Patrick's Seminary and University</a> is a Catholic seminary in Menlo Park. <a href="/wiki/Fuller_Theological_Seminary" title="Fuller Theological Seminary">Fuller Theological Seminary</a>, an evangelical Christian school, has a branch campus in Menlo Park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fullerbayarea_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-fullerbayarea-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Menlo_College" title="Menlo College">Menlo College</a> and <a href="/wiki/Menlo_School" title="Menlo School">Menlo School</a>, which is a prep school, despite their names are in neighboring <a href="/wiki/Atherton,_California" title="Atherton, California">Atherton</a>.</p>
<p>There are two libraries within the city, the Main branch and the Belle Haven branch. These libraries are part of the <a href="/wiki/Peninsula_Library_System" title="Peninsula Library System">Peninsula Library System</a> and share many resources with other nearby libraries.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Menlo_Park,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Much of Menlo Park's economy revolves around the companies on <a href="/wiki/Sand_Hill_Road" title="Sand Hill Road">Sand Hill Road</a>, consisting of venture capital, private equity, financial services, law firms, and other professional service companies and investment vehicles focusing on technology. <a href="/wiki/Geron_Corporation" title="Geron Corporation">Geron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Half_International" title="Robert Half International">Robert Half International</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exponent_(consulting_firm)" title="Exponent (consulting firm)">Exponent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sunset_(magazine)" title="Sunset (magazine)"><i>Sunset</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/SRI_International" title="SRI International">SRI International</a> are among the companies based in Menlo Park. Facebook moved its headquarters to the former campus of <a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems" title="Sun Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</a> in Menlo Park in December 2011.</p>
