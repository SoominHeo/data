<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maine-et-Loire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>See also: <a href="/wiki/Anjou" title="Anjou">Anjou</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_Maine-et-Loire&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="History of Maine-et-Loire (page does not exist)">History of Maine-et-Loire</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Histoire_de_Maine-et-Loire" title="fr:Histoire de Maine-et-Loire">fr</a>)</span></i><br/>
Maine-et-Loire is one of the original 83 departments created during the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a> on March 4, 1790. Originally it was called Mayenne-et-Loire, but its name was changed to Maine-et-Loire in 1791. It was created from part of the former province of <a href="/wiki/Anjou" title="Anjou">Anjou</a>. Its present name is drawn from the former province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maine_(province_of_France)" title="Maine (province of France)">Maine</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loire_River" title="Loire River">Loire River</a>, which runs through it.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maine-et-Loire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maine-et-Loire is part of the current region of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pays-de-la-Loire" title="Pays-de-la-Loire">Pays-de-la-Loire</a> and is surrounded by the departments of <a href="/wiki/Ille-et-Vilaine" title="Ille-et-Vilaine">Ille-et-Vilaine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayenne" title="Mayenne">Mayenne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarthe" title="Sarthe">Sarthe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indre-et-Loire" title="Indre-et-Loire">Indre-et-Loire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vienne" title="Vienne">Vienne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deux-S%C3%A8vres" title="Deux-Sèvres">Deux-Sèvres</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vend%C3%A9e" title="Vendée">Vendée</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Loire-Atlantique" title="Loire-Atlantique">Loire-Atlantique</a>.</p>
<p>It has a varied landscape, with forested ranges of hills in the south and north separated by the valley of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loire_River" title="Loire River">Loire</a>. The highest point is Colline des Gardes (689 feet/210m).</p>
<p>The area has many navigable rivers such as the Loire, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarthe_River" title="Sarthe River">Sarthe</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayenne_River" title="Mayenne River">Mayenne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loir" title="Loir">Loir</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Authion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Authion (page does not exist)">Authion</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maine-et-Loire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The inhabitants of Maine-et-Loire have no official qualifier. They are sometimes known as <i>Angevins</i>, from the former province of Anjou, or <i>Mainéligériens</i>, from the name of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D%C3%A9partement" title="Département">département</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
