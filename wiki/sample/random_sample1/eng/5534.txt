<p><b>Melvyn Desmond "Mel" Collins</b> (born 5 September 1947, <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>) is a British saxophonist, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flautist" title="Flautist">flautist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Session_musician" title="Session musician">session musician</a>.</p>
<p>Collins is perhaps best known for his work in <a href="/wiki/Progressive_rock" title="Progressive rock">progressive rock</a>, having been a member of <a href="/wiki/King_Crimson" title="King Crimson">King Crimson</a> on two separate occasions (the first from 1970 to 1972 and the second from 2013 to the present day) and having played with <a href="/wiki/Camel_(band)" title="Camel (band)">Camel</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alan_Parsons_Project" title="Alan Parsons Project">Alan Parsons Project</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Squire" title="Chris Squire">Chris Squire</a>. He has also worked in a wide variety of contexts ranging from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blues_rock" title="Blues rock">blues rock</a> to <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mel_Collins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Collins was born in a family of musicians. His mother was a singer while his father was a saxophonist and session musician who toured with <a href="/wiki/Judy_Garland" title="Judy Garland">Judy Garland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Bassey" title="Shirley Bassey">Shirley Bassey</a>.</p>
<p>Collins has worked with a large number of notable recording artists,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> including <a href="/wiki/10cc" title="10cc">10cc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Korner" title="Alexis Korner">Alexis Korner</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clannad_(musical_group)" title="Clannad (musical group)">Clannad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Clapton" title="Eric Clapton">Eric Clapton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Company" title="Bad Company">Bad Company</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dire_Straits" title="Dire Straits">Dire Straits</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Ferry" title="Bryan Ferry">Bryan Ferry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Chapman" title="Roger Chapman">Roger Chapman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marianne_Faithfull" title="Marianne Faithfull">Marianne Faithfull</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Waters" title="Roger Waters">Roger Waters</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gerry_Rafferty" title="Gerry Rafferty">Gerry Rafferty</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tears_For_Fears" title="Tears For Fears">Tears For Fears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Go_West_(band)" title="Go West (band)">Go West</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joan_Armatrading" title="Joan Armatrading">Joan Armatrading</a>.</p>
<p>He was a member of <a href="/wiki/Progressive_rock" title="Progressive rock">progressive rock</a> bands <a href="/wiki/King_Crimson" title="King Crimson">King Crimson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Camel_(band)" title="Camel (band)">Camel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Alan_Parsons_Project" title="The Alan Parsons Project">The Alan Parsons Project</a>. For King Crimson he was <a href="/wiki/Ian_McDonald_(musician)" title="Ian McDonald (musician)">Ian McDonald</a>'s replacement, playing on the <a href="/wiki/Lizard_(album)" title="Lizard (album)"><i>Lizard</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Islands_(King_Crimson_album)" title="Islands (King Crimson album)"><i>Islands</i></a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Earthbound_(King_Crimson_album)" title="Earthbound (King Crimson album)">Earthbound</a></i> albums, and was a session musician on the <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Wake_of_Poseidon" title="In the Wake of Poseidon">In the Wake of Poseidon</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Red_(King_Crimson_album)" title="Red (King Crimson album)"><i>Red</i></a> albums. He played on the <a href="/wiki/Crimson_Jazz_Trio" title="Crimson Jazz Trio">Crimson Jazz Trio</a>'s second album, <i>The King Crimson Songbook, Volume Two</i>, released in 2009.</p>
<p>Collins played the saxophone solo on <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a> 1978 single, "<a href="/wiki/Miss_You_(The_Rolling_Stones_song)" title="Miss You (The Rolling Stones song)">Miss You</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and played with Dire Straits on their live album, <i><a href="/wiki/Alchemy:_Dire_Straits_Live" title="Alchemy: Dire Straits Live">Alchemy</a></i>. He was one of the members of <a href="/wiki/Kokomo_(band)" title="Kokomo (band)">Kokomo</a> with <a href="/wiki/Tony_O%27Malley_(musician)" title="Tony O'Malley (musician)">Tony O'Malley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neil_Hubbard" title="Neil Hubbard">Neil Hubbard</a>, Paddy McHugh, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frank_Collins_(composer-singer)" title="Frank Collins (composer-singer)">Frank Collins</a>, Dyan Birch, and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Spenner" title="Alan Spenner">Alan Spenner</a>, and is a frequent performer with Tony O'Malley.</p>
<p>In 1984, Mel Collins toured with <a href="/wiki/Roger_Waters" title="Roger Waters">Roger Waters</a> to support the album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pros_and_Cons_of_Hitch_Hiking" title="The Pros and Cons of Hitch Hiking">The Pros and Cons of Hitch Hiking</a></i>. In 1985 Collins was part of the supergroup 'Willie and the Poor Boys' appearing in their video with <a href="/wiki/Bill_Wyman" title="Bill Wyman">Bill Wyman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Page" title="Jimmy Page">Jimmy Page</a>. He toured again with Waters on a second leg of the 'Pros and Cons' tour. That same year, he also appeared on the album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Songs_From_The_Big_Chair" title="Songs From The Big Chair">Songs From The Big Chair</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Tears_for_Fears" title="Tears for Fears">Tears for Fears</a>, playing saxophone on "The Working Hour". In 1986 he was a musician on the animated film <i><a href="/wiki/When_the_Wind_Blows_(1986_film)" title="When the Wind Blows (1986 film)">When the Wind Blows</a></i>. He worked again with Waters in 1987, appearing on both the album <i><a href="/wiki/Radio_K.A.O.S." title="Radio K.A.O.S.">Radio K.A.O.S.</a></i> and the subsequent tour.</p>
<p>In 2006 a band member on <i><a href="/wiki/Die_Harald_Schmidt_Show" title="Die Harald Schmidt Show">Die Harald Schmidt Show</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2002–07 Collins was a member of the <a href="/wiki/King_Crimson" title="King Crimson">King Crimson</a> "revival" group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/21st_century_Schizoid_Band" title="21st century Schizoid Band">21st century Schizoid Band</a>, with other former Crimson members.</p>
<p>In May 2008 Kokomo was reformed temporarily. With Collins were Tony O'Malley, Neil Hubbard, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_Smith_(musician_-_producer)" title="Mark Smith (musician - producer)">Mark Smith</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adam_Phillips_(composer_musician)" title="Adam Phillips (composer musician)">Adam Phillips</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Hamilton_(pop_musician)" title="Andy Hamilton (pop musician)">Andy Hamilton</a>, Paddy McHugh, Dyan Birch, Frank Collins, Bernie Holland, and Glen Le Fleur.</p>
<p>Collins played woodwinds on the 2011 <a href="/wiki/King_Crimson" title="King Crimson">King Crimson</a> ProjeKct 7, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Scarcity_of_Miracles" title="A Scarcity of Miracles">A Scarcity of Miracles</a></i>, appearing on a King Crimson related album for the first time since 1974. In September 2013, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Fripp" title="Robert Fripp">Robert Fripp</a> confirmed that Mel Collins would be a member of King Crimson again, the band being referred to as King Crimson VIII.</p>
<p>Collins was also a member of <a href="/wiki/Pete_Haycock" title="Pete Haycock">Pete Haycock</a>'s reformation of the <a href="/wiki/Climax_Blues_Band" title="Climax Blues Band">Climax Blues Band</a> in 2013, prior to Haycock's death in October 2013.</p>
