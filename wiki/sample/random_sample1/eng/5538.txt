<p><b>Bank Mellat</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa">Ø¨Ø§ÙÚ© ÙÙØª</span>ââ, lit. <i>People's Bank</i>) is a private <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banking_in_Iran" title="Banking in Iran">Iranian bank</a>. Its name means "Bank of the Nation". Bank Mellat was established in 1980, with a paid capital of Rials 33.5 billion as a merger of ten pre-revolution private banks, comprising Tehran, Dariush, Pars, Etebarat Taavoni &amp; Tozie, Iran &amp; Arab, Bein-al-melalie-Iran, Omran, Bimeh Iran, Tejarat Khareji Iran and Farhangian.</p>
<p>Currently, the bank's capital amounts to Rls 13,100 billion and it is one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Commercial_bank" title="Commercial bank">commercial banks</a> in the Islamic Republic of Iran, ranking among the top 1000 banks of the world.</p>
<p>Bank Mellat's logo is derived from <a class="external text" href="http://www.citynationalcm.com" rel="nofollow">City National Bank of Florida</a> (of CAJA MADRID Group).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The Bank Mellat London branch was merged with the Bank Tejarat branch to form <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persia_International_Bank_PLC" title="Persia International Bank PLC">Persia International Bank PLC</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sanctions_and_court_actions">Sanctions and court actions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bank_Mellat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sanctions and court actions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_the_Treasury" title="United States Department of the Treasury">United States Department of the Treasury</a> has mentioned Bank Mellat and Persia International in their watchlist of Iranian banks which may be trading in violation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN_Security_Council_Resolution_1803" title="UN Security Council Resolution 1803">UN Security Council Resolution 1803</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 29 January 2013, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_General_Court" title="European General Court">European General Court</a> in Luxembourg ruled to annul the European Union sanctions in place since 2010 against Bank Mellat on grounds of supporting the Iranian nuclear and missile programs, stating that the basic rights of the bank had been denied and there was no evidence supporting the claim. Bank Mellat intends to sue for damages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On 18 February 2016, the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Justice_of_the_European_Union" title="Court of Justice of the European Union">Court of Justice of the European Union</a> concurred with the General Court decision that the reasons given for sanctions were too vague.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>A related action in the British courts was taken to the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Supreme Court of the United Kingdom</a> in March 2013, causing the court to hold a closed hearing for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In June 2013, the Supreme Court ruled that the UK government's sanctions on the bank had been unlawful.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Bank Mellat intends to claim for Â£500 million of damages from the UK government for loss of business between 2009 and 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph-20130818_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-20130818-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
