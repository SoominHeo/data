<p><b>Melanie Ann Sykes</b> (born 7 August 1970) is an English television and radio presenter, and model. She is best known for co-hosting <i><a href="/wiki/Today_with_Des_and_Mel" title="Today with Des and Mel">Today with Des and Mel</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Des_O%27Connor" title="Des O'Connor">Des O'Connor</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Do_Lunch_with_Gino_%26_Mel" title="Let's Do Lunch with Gino &amp; Mel">Let's Do Lunch</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Gino_D%27Acampo" title="Gino D'Acampo">Gino D'Acampo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FHM_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FHM-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melanie_Sykes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sykes was born in <a href="/wiki/Ashton-under-Lyne" title="Ashton-under-Lyne">Ashton-under-Lyne</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> to an English father and a Catholic Anglo-Indian mother, Wendy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/Mossley_Hollins_High_School" title="Mossley Hollins High School">Mossley Hollins High School</a> and studied <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A-level" title="A-level">A-level</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Religious_Studies" title="Religious Studies">Religious Studies</a> at <a href="/wiki/Ashton_Sixth_Form_College" title="Ashton Sixth Form College">Ashton Sixth Form College</a>. Sykes was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Ashton-under-Lyne" title="Ashton-under-Lyne">Ashtonian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brass_Band" title="Brass Band">Brass Band</a> along with her father, mother and two sisters, playing the <a href="/wiki/Baritone_horn" title="Baritone horn">baritone horn</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melanie_Sykes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sykes first came to public attention as the <a href="/wiki/Bikini" title="Bikini">bikini</a>-clad girl in the <a href="/wiki/Boddingtons_Brewery" title="Boddingtons Brewery">Boddingtons Brewery</a> beer advertisements with the broad "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_England_English" title="Northern England English">Northern</a>" <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Accent_(dialect)" title="Accent (dialect)">accent</a> in the mid-1990s. Amongst other work, Sykes also modelled for <a href="/wiki/Berlei" title="Berlei">Berlei</a> <a href="/wiki/Lingerie" title="Lingerie">lingerie</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MailOnline2009_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MailOnline2009-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melanie_Sykes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After first reporting for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Big_Breakfast" title="The Big Breakfast">The Big Breakfast</a></i>, Sykes has continued her television career, including presenting stints on <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Celebrity...Get_Me_Out_of_Here!_(UK_TV_series)" title="I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! (UK TV series)">I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!</a></i> and as presenter of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EastEnders_Revealed" title="EastEnders Revealed">EastEnders Revealed</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1999, Sykes presented <i>Melanie Sykes' Southall Stories</i>, a documentary for <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a> on <a href="/wiki/Asian_people" title="Asian people">Asian</a> culture in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. She has also hosted a variety of awards ceremonies, including <a href="/wiki/Miss_World" title="Miss World">Miss World</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA" title="BAFTA">BAFTA Awards</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Q_Awards" title="Q Awards">Q Awards</a>.</p>
<p>Sykes' television career stalled for a period, although she made a successful comeback as host of <i><a href="/wiki/Today_with_Des_and_Mel" title="Today with Des and Mel">Today with Des and Mel</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Des_O%27Connor" title="Des O'Connor">Des O'Connor</a> in 2003. On 12 May 2006, <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> announced that the show would be one of a number to be axed in a "painful, but utterly necessary" move.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sykes' other work for <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> has included hosting shows <i><a href="/wiki/The_Vault_(game_show)" title="The Vault (game show)">The Vault</a></i> (2003–2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrities_Under_Pressure" title="Celebrities Under Pressure">Celebrities Under Pressure</a></i> (2003–2004) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_British_Soap_Awards" title="The British Soap Awards">The British Soap Awards</a></i> (2003).</p>
<p>Sykes appeared as a guest panellist on <i><a href="/wiki/Loose_Women" title="Loose Women">Loose Women</a></i> in October and November 2005 and later returned as a guest anchor in October 2008 and May 2009.</p>
<p>From 2006 to 2009, Sykes regularly filled in for <a href="/wiki/Paul_O%27Grady" title="Paul O'Grady">Paul O'Grady</a> as presenter of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Paul_O%27Grady_Show" title="The Paul O'Grady Show">The Paul O'Grady Show</a></i> when O'Grady was unable to appear. She presented the show on eleven occasions.</p>
<p>Sykes was the presenter of the <a href="/wiki/Daytime_television" title="Daytime television">daytime</a> series <i>Gene Detectives</i> for <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, Sykes was a judge on 'The Sofa Factor', an item for <i><a href="/wiki/GMTV" title="GMTV">GMTV</a></i>, where viewers sent in short clips and the winner got to present TV Pick of the Day and win a trip to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas</a>.</p>
<p>In August 2011, Sykes returned to daytime television, co-hosting <i><a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Do_Lunch_with_Gino_%26_Mel" title="Let's Do Lunch with Gino &amp; Mel">Let's Do Lunch with Gino &amp; Mel</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Let%27s_Do_Christmas_with_Gino_%26_Mel" title="Let's Do Christmas with Gino &amp; Mel">Let's Do Christmas with Gino &amp; Mel</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Gino_D%27Acampo" title="Gino D'Acampo">Gino D'Acampo</a> on ITV. The show was axed in 2014.</p>
<p>On 13 September 2011, Sykes co-hosted the three-part series <i>Missing Millions</i> with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Heiney" title="Paul Heiney">Paul Heiney</a> on ITV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2014, <a href="/wiki/BT_Sport" title="BT Sport">BT Sport</a> announced that Sykes would become part of their <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_motorcycle_racing" title="Grand Prix motorcycle racing">MotoGP</a> team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Due to other work commitments, she left the channel in May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Sykes took part in the <a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Celebrity...Get_Me_Out_of_Here!_(UK_series_14)" title="I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! (UK series 14)">fourteenth series</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Celebrity...Get_Me_Out_of_Here!_(UK_TV_series)" title="I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here! (UK TV series)">I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!</a></i> where she finished in third place, behind runner-up <a href="/wiki/Jake_Quickenden" title="Jake Quickenden">Jake Quickenden</a> and winner <a href="/wiki/Carl_Fogarty" title="Carl Fogarty">Carl 'Foggy' Fogarty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Sykes co-presented <i>Humble Pie</i>, a cookery series for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watch_(TV_channel)" title="Watch (TV channel)">Watch</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Marco_Pierre_White" title="Marco Pierre White">Marco Pierre White</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio">Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melanie_Sykes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sykes presented <i>The A List</i>, a national radio chart show based on total sales, produced by Unique the Production Company at the studios of London's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heart_106.2" title="Heart 106.2">Heart 106.2</a>. She left the show towards the end of 2006 and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Gail_Porter" title="Gail Porter">Gail Porter</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Sykes was heard on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2" title="BBC Radio 2">BBC Radio 2</a> with <a href="/wiki/Aled_Jones" title="Aled Jones">Aled Jones</a> sitting in for <i><a href="/wiki/Steve_Wright_in_the_Afternoon" title="Steve Wright in the Afternoon">Steve Wright in the Afternoon</a></i> on 22, 23 and 24 December 2008. In May 2010, she began co-hosting the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2" title="BBC Radio 2">BBC Radio 2</a> show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Going_Out_With_Alan_Carr" title="Going Out With Alan Carr">Going Out With Alan Carr</a></i> after <a href="/wiki/Emma_Forbes" title="Emma Forbes">Emma Forbes</a>' resignation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Sykes continued on the show until its end in March 2012. She returned on Boxing Day 2015 with <a href="/wiki/Alan_Carr" title="Alan Carr">Alan Carr</a> for a one-off show on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2" title="BBC Radio 2">BBC Radio 2</a>.</p>
<p>For a time, Sykes presented her own radio show on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galaxy_Radio" title="Galaxy Radio">Capital FM</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melanie_Sykes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sykes has been the face of <a href="/wiki/Head_%26_Shoulders" title="Head &amp; Shoulders">Head &amp; Shoulders</a> shampoo in Britain, appearing in print and television advertising and the high street chain <a href="/wiki/Matalan" title="Matalan">Matalan</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> She has also appeared in advertisements for <a href="/wiki/Morrisons" title="Morrisons">Morrisons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wynsors_World_of_Shoes" title="Wynsors World of Shoes">Wynsors World of Shoes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Churchill_Insurance" title="Churchill Insurance">Churchill Insurance</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2006, the book <i>Blooming Beautiful: My Plan for Looking Great, Being Healthy and Surviving Hormonal Havoc, Throughout Pregnancy and as a New Mum</i>, co-written by Sykes with Hilary Boyd, was published by <a href="/wiki/Pearson_PLC" title="Pearson PLC">Michael Joseph</a>.</p>
<p>Sykes posed naked for the December 2011 edition of men's magazine <a href="/wiki/Esquire_(magazine)" title="Esquire (magazine)">Esquire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Esquire_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Esquire-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 24 December 2013, on an episode of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Let%27s_Do_Christmas_with_Gino_%26_Mel" title="Let's Do Christmas with Gino &amp; Mel">Let's Do Christmas with Gino &amp; Mel</a></i>, Sykes and D'Acampo set the new Guinness World Record for "The most jumpers put on in one minute". They managed to put on 11 jumpers in a minute.</p>
<p>Sykes has her own line of underwear and lingerie with high street chain and retailer <a href="/wiki/Debenhams" title="Debenhams">Debenhams</a>.</p>
<p>In June 2016 Melanie Sykes launched a lifestyle website at <a class="external text" href="http://melaniesykes.com" rel="nofollow">melaniesykes.com</a> which features fitness, food and lifestyle content as well as celebrity interviews and competitions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melanie_Sykes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sykes married actor Daniel Caltagirone in January 2001, and the couple had two children: Roman (born 2002) and Valentino Luca (born 21 July 2004), who was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Autism" title="Autism">autism</a> in 2006. In July 2008, it was reported that the couple had split up, with Sykes having "grown apart" from Caltagirone, who had moved out of their <a href="/wiki/Hampstead" title="Hampstead">Hampstead</a>, London home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SadSplit_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-SadSplit-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SadSplit_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-SadSplit-15">[15]</a></sup> They divorced in June 2009. Sykes married her boyfriend Jack Cockings on 18 May 2013 at <a href="/wiki/Sherborne_Castle" title="Sherborne Castle">Sherborne Castle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FHM_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FHM-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MailOnline2009_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-MailOnline2009-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2013, Sykes was arrested and cautioned by police over an assault against Cockings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Soon after the domestic assault charges, after less than one year of marriage, Sykes began the process of filing for divorce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On 20 August 2014, the common assault caution was removed from police records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
