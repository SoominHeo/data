<p><b>Melanie Ann Amaro</b> (born June 26, 1992) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Isles" title="British Virgin Isles">Virgin Islander</a>-American recording artist who won the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(U.S._season_1)" title="The X Factor (U.S. season 1)">first season</a> of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(U.S.)" title="The X Factor (U.S.)">The X Factor USA</a></i> in 2011, securing a $5 million recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Syco_Music" title="Syco Music">Syco Music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>. Amaro was also the youngest contestant to win the competition during the show's run (2011-2013).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melanie_Amaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amaro was born in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Lauderdale,_Florida" title="Fort Lauderdale, Florida">Fort Lauderdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early3_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Early3-1">[1]</a></sup> and raised on <a href="/wiki/Tortola" title="Tortola">Tortola</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">British Virgin Islands</a> since the age of three.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Amaro was sent to the British Virgin Islands to live with her grandmother Catherine, after her parents Hipolito Amaro and Debra Sylvester Amaro felt they did not have the means to provide for her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Early1-3">[3]</a></sup> Amaro would only see her parents during summer vacations and Christmas breaks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early3_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Early3-1">[1]</a></sup> She also has two brothers, Mark and Mike, and a younger sister named Maya.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Early1-3">[3]</a></sup> Her mother Debra recalls that when her daughter was about six months old, she would get up at around two o'clock in the morning and would start singing in her crib.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early3_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Early3-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Early2-4">[4]</a></sup> Furthermore, from an early age, Amaro would sing around her house using a hairbrush as a microphone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early1_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Early1-3">[3]</a></sup> Melanie realized she fell in love with singing and acting at around age eleven, and credits her mother for pushing her to follow her singing dreams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early3_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Early3-1">[1]</a></sup> Amaro never entered any singing competitions as a child, but performed at weddings, churches, and other events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Early2-4">[4]</a></sup> Melanie attended the Althea Scatliffe Primary School and later went on to Elmore Stout high school, formerly known as BVI high school. Melanie moved to Florida when she was 15, and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Plantation_High_School" title="Plantation High School">Plantation High School</a> in 2010. In March 2012, her high school chorus teacher, Gary Rivenbark, whom Amaro credits with the help of shaping her voice, died of <a href="/wiki/Lymphoma" title="Lymphoma">lymphoma</a> at age 48.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melanie_Amaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011:_The_X_Factor">2011: <i>The X Factor</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melanie_Amaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011: The X Factor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2011, Amaro was persuaded by her mother<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early2_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Early2-4">[4]</a></sup> to audition for the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(U.S._season_1)" title="The X Factor (U.S. season 1)">first season</a> of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(U.S.)" title="The X Factor (U.S.)">The X Factor USA</a></i>. She auditioned in front of judges <a href="/wiki/Simon_Cowell" title="Simon Cowell">Simon Cowell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paula_Abdul" title="Paula Abdul">Paula Abdul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Scherzinger" title="Nicole Scherzinger">Nicole Scherzinger</a> and <a href="/wiki/L.A._Reid" title="L.A. Reid">L.A. Reid</a>, singing "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Listen_(Beyonc%C3%A9_Knowles_song)" title="Listen (Beyoncé Knowles song)">Listen</a>" by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9_Knowles" title="Beyoncé Knowles">Beyoncé Knowles</a>. She previously tried auditioning at her <a href="/wiki/WSVN" title="WSVN">local Fox affiliate</a> for a "fast pass", but was not well received by producers there and subsequently auditioned with the general public at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BankUnited_Center" title="BankUnited Center">BankUnited Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Coral_Gables,_Florida" title="Coral Gables, Florida">Coral Gables</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early3_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Early3-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Early1_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Early1-3">[3]</a></sup> After becoming one of the Top 32 finalists, she performed <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Will_You_Be_There" title="Will You Be There">Will You Be There</a>" in the judges' house, in front of her mentor Cowell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Amaro was initially not chosen to be part of the Top 16 and was eliminated from the girls' category.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top17_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-top17-7">[7]</a></sup> However, after 2 weeks Cowell surprised her at her Florida home, inviting her back to the competition after deciding he had made "a huge mistake" in not including her.</p>
<p>Amaro progressed to the live shows in Cowell's girls' category, along with <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Crow" title="Rachel Crow">Rachel Crow</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drew_(singer)" title="Drew (singer)">Drew</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiah_Tolliver" title="Tiah Tolliver">Tiah Tolliver</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simone_Battle" title="Simone Battle">Simone Battle</a>. The addition of Amaro took the total number of qualifiers for the first live show to 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top17_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-top17-7">[7]</a></sup> On December 15, Amaro won a place in the final along with <a href="/wiki/Chris_Rene" title="Chris Rene">Chris Rene</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Krajcik" title="Josh Krajcik">Josh Krajcik</a>. The following week, she was declared the winner. Amaro was also declared to be the show's youngest contestant to win at age 19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-XFWin1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-XFWin1-8">[8]</a></sup> Her prize was a $5 million recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Syco_Music" title="Syco Music">Syco Music</a> and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music</a> label, which is the largest guaranteed prize in television history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Shortly after winning, Sony Music label <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a> announced that they had officially signed Amaro, who will be working alongside <a href="/wiki/L.A._Reid" title="L.A. Reid">L.A. Reid</a> at the label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In addition to the contract, Amaro also appeared in a <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">Pepsi</a> commercial alongside Sir <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, which aired during <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVI" title="Super Bowl XLVI">Super Bowl XLVI</a> on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-XFWin1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-XFWin1-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.93present:_Truly.2C_label_conflict_and_broadway_ventures">2012–present: <i>Truly</i>, label conflict and broadway ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melanie_Amaro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–present: Truly, label conflict and broadway ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Amaro was scheduled to release her debut album in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It will feature music styles different from what people are used to her performing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-XFWin2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-XFWin2-13">[13]</a></sup> Amaro's <a href="/wiki/Pepsi" title="Pepsi">Pepsi</a> commercial aired during <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVI" title="Super Bowl XLVI">Super Bowl XLVI</a> on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> as part of her X Factor prize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The commercial features Amaro singing a <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> cover of "<a href="/wiki/Respect_(song)#Covers" title="Respect (song)">Respect</a>", alongside <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flavor_Flav" title="Flavor Flav">Flavor Flav</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Annie_Ilonzeh" title="Annie Ilonzeh">Annie Ilonzeh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The song was released as a <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">music download</a> on February 2, 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-debut_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-debut-16">[16]</a></sup> although Amaro confirmed that the song does not serve as her debut single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> As of March 29, 2012, Melanie's cover of "Respect" has reached the #3 spot on the Billboard Dance/Club Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On August 1, Amaro released her debut single "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Fail_Me_Now" title="Don't Fail Me Now">Don't Fail Me Now</a>", written by <a href="/wiki/Livvi_Franc" title="Livvi Franc">Livvi Franc</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Rodney_Jerkins" title="Rodney Jerkins">Rodney Jerkins</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Debutsingle1_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Debutsingle1-19">[19]</a></sup> Amaro's album was set for release on December 4, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On 19 October 2012 Amaro premiered the music video for "Don't Fail Me Now" on her official <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VEVO" title="VEVO">VEVO</a> account. The song failed to chart and the album was pushed back to 2013. She performed a new single, "Long Distance", live on the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(U.S.)" title="The X Factor (U.S.)">The X Factor</a></i> on December 6, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The song was released on December 3, 2012. Since then rumors began to circulate that Amaro had been dropped from Epic Records after it appeared that her name had been removed from the label's website, but a rep from the label confirmed that the website didn't always contain a full roster of artists and that Amaro was still part of Epic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> <i>Truly</i> was rescheduled for March 2013, but was never released. While season 2 winner <a href="/wiki/Tate_Stevens" title="Tate Stevens">Tate Stevens</a> debut album was released in April 2013 under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RCA_Nashville" title="RCA Nashville">RCA Nashville</a>, Amaro's label has stated that her album has no confirmed release date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2013, Amaro performed in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> for <a href="/wiki/RCTI" title="RCTI">RCTI</a>'s 24th anniversary <a href="/wiki/Television_special" title="Television special">television special</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/X_Factor_Around_the_World" title="X Factor Around the World">X Factor Around the World</a></i> along with <a href="/wiki/Samantha_Jade" title="Samantha Jade">Samantha Jade</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jahm%C3%A9ne_Douglas" title="Jahméne Douglas">Jahméne Douglas</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Collective_(band)" title="The Collective (band)">The Collective</a>, <a href="/wiki/Novita_Dewi" title="Novita Dewi">Novita Dewi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fatin_Shidqia" title="Fatin Shidqia">Fatin Shidqia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Amaro performed her new single "Long Distance" and then "<a href="/wiki/The_World%27s_Greatest" title="The World's Greatest">The World's Greatest</a>" as a duet with <a href="/wiki/Fatin_Shidqia" title="Fatin Shidqia">Fatin Shidqia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-X_Factor_Around_the_World_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-X_Factor_Around_the_World-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 4, 2013, Amaro made her musical theater debut in the national tour of "You're Never Alone".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 19, 2014 she premiered the lead single "Fuel My Fire" on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soundcloud" title="Soundcloud">Soundcloud</a> from her upcoming EP of the same name. Amaro described the song as being about "her frustration with the lack of music released by her label." The song was released independently as a digital download on June 26, 2014, without Epic or Sony being credited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of September 2014, Amaro's album is believed to have been scrapped and will not be released. Months later "Fuel My Fire" was taken off of iTunes for unknown reasons.</p>
<p>On May 6, 2015, Amaro released a new song <i>Dust</i> featuring American hip-hop recording artist, <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>. It is the second independent release of the artist ever since her departure from <a href="/wiki/Syco_Music" title="Syco Music">Syco</a> <a href="/wiki/Ampersand" title="Ampersand">&amp;</a> <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>. "Dust" was finally released to digital retail stores in September 2015, a solo version without Fabolous was also released.</p>
<p>On Feb 17, 2016, Amaro released a new song <i>The One</i>.</p>
