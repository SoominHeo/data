<p><b>Melina Eleni Kanakaredes Constantinides</b> (born April 23, 1967) is an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a> actress. She is widely known for her roles in U.S. primetime television dramas as Dr. Sydney Hansen in <i><a href="/wiki/Providence_(TV_series)" title="Providence (TV series)">Providence</a></i> (1999–2002) and as Detective <a href="/wiki/Stella_Bonasera" title="Stella Bonasera">Stella Bonasera</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_NY" title="CSI: NY">CSI: NY</a></i> (2004–10), and on the American daytime television drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Guiding_Light" title="Guiding Light">Guiding Light</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Eleni_Andros_Cooper" title="Eleni Andros Cooper">Eleni Andros Cooper</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melina_Kanakaredes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kanakaredes was born in <a href="/wiki/Akron,_Ohio" title="Akron, Ohio">Akron, Ohio</a>, the daughter of Connie (née Temo), a candy company owner, and Harry Kanakaredes, an insurance salesman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ref102_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ref102-2">[2]</a></sup> She is a second-generation <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek-American" title="Greek-American">Greek-American</a> and speaks <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Speaks_Greek_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Speaks_Greek-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uakron_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-uakron-4">[4]</a></sup> Her two maternal uncles own and run a candy store in Akron called "Temo's Candy Company", a chocolate store established in its current location by Kanakaredes's grandfather, Christ Temo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abj_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-abj-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>At age 8, Kanakaredes made her stage debut in a production of <i>Tom Sawyer</i> at Weathervane Playhouse in Akron, Ohio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Firestone_High_School" title="Firestone High School">Firestone High School</a> in Akron.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uakron_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-uakron-4">[4]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/Ohio_State_University" title="Ohio State University">Ohio State University</a> for a short time, and then transferred to <a href="/wiki/Point_Park_University" title="Point Park University">Point Park College</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a>, where she performed roles in the <a href="/wiki/Theatre_in_Pittsburgh" title="Theatre in Pittsburgh">professional theatre scene</a> there, including the role of Mary Magdalene in <i><a href="/wiki/Jesus_Christ_Superstar" title="Jesus Christ Superstar">Jesus Christ Superstar</a></i> at <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Musical_Theater" title="Pittsburgh Musical Theater">Pittsburgh Musical Theater</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She graduated from Point Park in 1989 with a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> degree in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theater_arts" title="Theater arts">theater arts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melina_Kanakaredes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melina_Kanakaredes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kanakaredes's first television role was on the daytime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Guiding_Light" title="Guiding Light">Guiding Light</a></i>, originating the role of <a href="/wiki/Eleni_Andros_Cooper" title="Eleni Andros Cooper">Eleni Andros Cooper</a> from 1991 to 1995. She was nominated twice for a Daytime Emmy Award for her portrayal of Eleni.</p>
<p>Her most high-profile roles on television were as the series protagonist Dr. Sydney Hansen on <i><a href="/wiki/Providence_(TV_series)" title="Providence (TV series)">Providence</a></i>, which ran for five seasons between 1999 and 2002, and as Det. <a href="/wiki/Stella_Bonasera" title="Stella Bonasera">Stella Bonasera</a> on the CBS drama series <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_NY" title="CSI: NY">CSI: NY</a></i>. Kanakaredes left <i>CSI: NY</i> in 2010, after six seasons on the program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leavecsiny_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-leavecsiny-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her other television credits include <i><a href="/wiki/NYPD_Blue" title="NYPD Blue">NYPD Blue</a></i> (in a recurring role as reporter Benita Alden during that show's second season), <i><a href="/wiki/Due_South" title="Due South">Due South</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Oz_(TV_series)" title="Oz (TV series)">Oz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Practice" title="The Practice">The Practice</a></i>, MTV's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ben_Stiller_Show" title="The Ben Stiller Show">The Ben Stiller Show</a></i>, and a <a href="/wiki/Hallmark_Hall_of_Fame" title="Hallmark Hall of Fame">Hallmark Hall of Fame</a> adaptation of the <a href="/wiki/Anne_Tyler" title="Anne Tyler">Anne Tyler</a> novel <i><a href="/wiki/Saint_Maybe" title="Saint Maybe">Saint Maybe</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melina_Kanakaredes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kanakaredes's film appearances include <i><a href="/wiki/The_Long_Kiss_Goodnight" title="The Long Kiss Goodnight">The Long Kiss Goodnight</a></i> as Trin and <i><a href="/wiki/15_Minutes" title="15 Minutes">15 Minutes</a></i> as Nicolette Karas, the girlfriend of <a href="/wiki/Robert_De_Niro" title="Robert De Niro">Robert De Niro</a>'s character. She was considered for the lead role in <i><a href="/wiki/My_Big_Fat_Greek_Wedding" title="My Big Fat Greek Wedding">My Big Fat Greek Wedding</a></i> but had to turn it down due to her pregnancy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Big_Fat_Greek_Wedding_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-My_Big_Fat_Greek_Wedding-10">[10]</a></sup> She played the role of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_Goddess" title="Greek Goddess">Greek Goddess</a> <a href="/wiki/Athena" title="Athena">Athena</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Percy_Jackson_and_the_Olympians:_The_Lightning_Thief" title="Percy Jackson and the Olympians: The Lightning Thief">Percy Jackson and the Olympians: The Lightning Thief</a></i> and starred in the 2013 drama <i><a href="/wiki/Snitch_(film)" title="Snitch (film)">Snitch</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Dwayne_Johnson" title="Dwayne Johnson">Dwayne (The Rock) Johnson</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melina_Kanakaredes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kanakaredes married Peter Constantinides<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usat_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-usat-11">[11]</a></sup> on September 6, 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Together, they have two daughters: Zoe (b. 2000) and Karina Eleni (b. 2003).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usat_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-usat-11">[11]</a></sup> She and her husband, a former restaurant consultant,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usat_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-usat-11">[11]</a></sup> owned the "Tria Greek Kuzina" in <a href="/wiki/Powell,_Ohio" title="Powell, Ohio">Powell, Ohio</a>, but it has since closed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
