<p><b>Immunotherapy</b> is the "<a href="/wiki/Therapy" title="Therapy">treatment</a> of <a href="/wiki/Disease" title="Disease">disease</a> by inducing, enhancing, or suppressing an immune response".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Immunotherapies designed to elicit or amplify an immune response are classified as <b>activation immunotherapies,</b> while immunotherapies that reduce or suppress are classified as <b><a href="/wiki/Immunosuppression" title="Immunosuppression">suppression immunotherapies</a>.</b></p>
<p>Immunomodulatory regimens often have fewer side effects than existing drugs, including less potential for creating resistance when treating microbial disease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid11727500_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid11727500-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Cell-based immunotherapies are effective for some cancers. Immune effector cells such as <a href="/wiki/Lymphocyte" title="Lymphocyte">lymphocytes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macrophage" title="Macrophage">macrophages</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dendritic_cell" title="Dendritic cell">dendritic cells</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_killer_cell" title="Natural killer cell">natural killer cells</a> (NK Cell), <a href="/wiki/Cytotoxic_T_cell" title="Cytotoxic T cell">cytotoxic T lymphocytes</a> (CTL), etc., work together to defend the body against cancer by targeting abnormal antigens expressed on the surface of tumor cells.</p>
<p>Therapies such as <a href="/wiki/Granulocyte_colony-stimulating_factor" title="Granulocyte colony-stimulating factor">granulocyte colony-stimulating factor</a> (G-CSF), <a href="/wiki/Interferon" title="Interferon">interferons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Imiquimod" title="Imiquimod">imiquimod</a> and cellular membrane fractions from <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacteria</a> are licensed for medical use. Others including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interleukin-2" title="Interleukin-2">IL-2</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interleukin-7" title="Interleukin-7">IL-7</a>, <a href="/wiki/Interleukin_12" title="Interleukin 12">IL-12</a>, various <a href="/wiki/Chemokine" title="Chemokine">chemokines</a>, synthetic cytosine phosphate-guanosine (CpG) oligodeoxynucleotides and <a href="/wiki/Glucan" title="Glucan">glucans</a> are involved in clinical and preclinical studies.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activation_immunotherapies">Activation immunotherapies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activation immunotherapies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cancer">Cancer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cancer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cancer_immunotherapy" title="Cancer immunotherapy">Cancer immunotherapy</a></div>
<p>Cancer immunotherapy attempts to stimulate the <a href="/wiki/Immune_system" title="Immune system">immune system</a> to destroy tumors. A variety of strategies are in use or are undergoing research and testing. Randomized controlled studies in different cancers resulting in significant increase in survival and disease free period have been reported<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid9816009_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid9816009-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid9210707_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid9210707-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid11022927_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid11022927-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid12060615_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid12060615-6">[6]</a></sup> and its efficacy is enhanced by 20â30% when cell-based immunotherapy is combined with conventional treatment methods.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The extraction of <a href="/wiki/Granulocyte_colony-stimulating_factor" title="Granulocyte colony-stimulating factor">G-CSF</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lymphocytes" title="Lymphocytes">lymphocytes</a> from the blood and expanding in vitro against a tumour antigen before reinjecting the cells<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid15654904_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid15654904-7">[7]</a></sup> with appropriate stimulatory <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cytokines" title="Cytokines">cytokines</a>. The cells then destroy the tumor cells that express the <a href="/wiki/Antigen" title="Antigen">antigen</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bacillus_Calmette-Gu%C3%A9rin" title="Bacillus Calmette-GuÃ©rin">BCG</a> immunotherapy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19395154_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19395154-8">[8]</a></sup> for early stage (non-invasive) <a href="/wiki/Bladder_cancer" title="Bladder cancer">bladder cancer</a> instills attenuated live <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacteria</a> into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bladder" title="Bladder">bladder</a> and is effective in preventing recurrence in up to two thirds of cases.</p>
<p>Topical immunotherapy utilizes an immune enhancement cream (<a href="/wiki/Imiquimod" title="Imiquimod">imiquimod</a>) which produces <a href="/wiki/Interferon" title="Interferon">interferon</a>, causing the recipient's killer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T_cells" title="T cells">T cells</a> to destroy <a href="/wiki/Wart" title="Wart">warts</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid18385498_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid18385498-9">[9]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actinic_keratoses" title="Actinic keratoses">actinic keratoses</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basal_cell_cancer" title="Basal cell cancer">basal cell cancer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vaginal_intraepithelial_neoplasia" title="Vaginal intraepithelial neoplasia">vaginal intraepithelial neoplasia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid17051086_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid17051086-10">[10]</a></sup> squamous cell cancer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19395154_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19395154-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19442565_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19442565-11">[11]</a></sup> cutaneous lymphoma,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19273388_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19273388-12">[12]</a></sup> and superficial malignant melanoma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19464602_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19464602-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Injection immunotherapy ("intralesional" or "intratumoral") uses mumps, candida, the HPV vaccine<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19473507_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19473507-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19353471_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19353471-15">[15]</a></sup> or <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Trichophytin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Trichophytin (page does not exist)">trichophytin</a> <a href="/wiki/Antigen" title="Antigen">antigen</a> injections to treat warts (HPV induced tumors).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Adoptive_cell_transfer" title="Adoptive cell transfer">Adoptive cell transfer</a> has been tested on <a href="/wiki/Lung_cancer" title="Lung cancer">lung</a> and other cancers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19471115_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19471115-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Dendritic_cell-based_pump-priming">Dendritic cell-based pump-priming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dendritic cell-based pump-priming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dendritic_cells" title="Dendritic cells">Dendritic cells</a> can be stimulated to activate a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cytotoxic" title="Cytotoxic">cytotoxic</a> response towards an <a href="/wiki/Antigen" title="Antigen">antigen</a>. Dendritic cells, a type of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antigen_presenting_cell" title="Antigen presenting cell">antigen presenting cell</a>, are harvested from the person needing the immunotherapy. These cells are then either pulsed with an antigen or tumor lysate<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Transfection" title="Transfection">transfected</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Viral_vector" title="Viral vector">viral vector</a>, causing them to display the antigen. Upon transfusion into the person, these activated cells present the antigen to the effector lymphocytes (<a href="/wiki/Cytotoxic_T_cell" title="Cytotoxic T cell">CD4+ helper T cells</a>, cytotoxic <a href="/wiki/Cytotoxic_T_cell" title="Cytotoxic T cell">CD8+ T cells</a> and <a href="/wiki/B_cell" title="B cell">B cells</a>). This initiates a cytotoxic response against tumor cells expressing the antigen (against which the adaptive response has now been primed).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19483655_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19483655-18">[18]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Cancer_vaccine" title="Cancer vaccine">cancer vaccine</a> <a href="/wiki/Sipuleucel-T" title="Sipuleucel-T">Sipuleucel-T</a> is one example of this approach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid21606971_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid21606971-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="T-cell_adoptive_transfer">T-cell adoptive transfer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: T-cell adoptive transfer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Adoptive_cell_transfer" title="Adoptive cell transfer">Adoptive cell transfer</a> <i><a href="/wiki/In_vitro" title="In vitro">in vitro</a></i> cultivates autologous, extracted T cells for later transfusion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rosenberg_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rosenberg-20">[20]</a></sup> The T cells may already target tumor cells. Alternatively, they may be genetically engineered to do so. These T cells, referred to as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tumor-infiltrating_lymphocyte" title="Tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte">tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes</a> (TIL), are multiplied using high concentrations of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interleukin-2" title="Interleukin-2">Interleukin-2</a>, anti-CD3 and allo-reactive feeder cells. These T cells are then transferred back into the person along with administration of IL-2 to further boost their anti-cancer activity.</p>
<p>Before reinfusion, lymphodepletion of the recipient is required to eliminate regulatory T cells as well as unmodified, endogenous lymphocytes that compete with the transferred cells for homeostatic cytokines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rosenberg_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rosenberg-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Antony_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Antony-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gattinoni_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gattinoni-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dummer_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dummer-23">[23]</a></sup> Lymphodepletion can be achieved by total body irradiation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dudley08_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dudley08-24">[24]</a></sup> Transferred cells multiplied <i>in vivo</i> and persisted in peripheral blood in many people, sometimes representing levels of 75% of all CD8<sup>+</sup> T cells at 6â12 months after infusion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dudley02_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dudley02-25">[25]</a></sup> As of 2012, clinical trials for metastatic melanoma were ongoing at multiple sites.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid22996367_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid22996367-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Immune_enhancement_therapy">Immune enhancement therapy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Immune enhancement therapy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p><a href="/wiki/Autologous_immune_enhancement_therapy" title="Autologous immune enhancement therapy">Autologous immune enhancement therapy</a> use a person's own peripheral blood-derived <a href="/wiki/Natural_killer_cell" title="Natural killer cell">natural killer cells</a>, cytotoxic T lymphocytes and other relevant immune cells are expanded <i>in vitro</i> and then reinfused.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manjunath_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Manjunath-27">[27]</a></sup> The therapy has been tested against <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_C" title="Hepatitis C">Hepatitis C</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Doskali_M_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Doskali_M-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Li_Y_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Li_Y-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Terunuma_H_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Terunuma_H-30">[30]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chronic_fatigue_syndrome" title="Chronic fatigue syndrome">Chronic fatigue syndrome</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-See_DM_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-See_DM-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ojo-Amaize_EA_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ojo-Amaize_EA-32">[32]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HHV6" title="HHV6">HHV6</a> infection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kida_K_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kida_K-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Genetically_engineered_T_cells">Genetically engineered T cells</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Genetically engineered T cells">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genetically_engineered_T_cell" title="Genetically engineered T cell">Genetically engineered T cells</a> are created by harvesting T cells and then infecting the T cells with a <a href="/wiki/Retrovirus" title="Retrovirus">retrovirus</a> that contains a copy of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T_cell_receptor" title="T cell receptor">T cell receptor</a> (TCR) gene that is specialised to recognise tumour antigens. The virus integrates the receptor into the T cells' <a href="/wiki/Genome" title="Genome">genome</a>. The cells are expanded non-specifically and/or stimulated. The cells are then reinfused and produce an immune response against the tumour cells.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morgan_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Morgan-34">[34]</a></sup> The technique has been tested on refractory stage IV metastatic melanomas<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rosenberg_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rosenberg-20">[20]</a></sup> and advanced <a href="/wiki/Skin_cancer" title="Skin cancer">skin cancer</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hunder_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hunder-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-urlCancer_Research_Institute_-_2008_Symposium_Program_.26_Speakers_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-urlCancer_Research_Institute_-_2008_Symposium_Program_.26_Speakers-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Immune_recovery">Immune recovery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Immune recovery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Another potential use of immunotherapy is to restore the immune system of people with immune deficiencies. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cytokines" title="Cytokines">Cytokines</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interleukin-7" title="Interleukin-7">Interleukin-7</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interleukin-2" title="Interleukin-2">Interleukin-2</a> have been tested in clinical trials.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vaccination">Vaccination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Vaccination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Antimicrobial" title="Antimicrobial">Antimicrobial</a> immunotherapy, which includes <a href="/wiki/Vaccination" title="Vaccination">vaccination</a>, involves activating the <a href="/wiki/Immune_system" title="Immune system">immune system</a> to respond to an infectious agent.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Suppression_immunotherapies">Suppression immunotherapies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Suppression immunotherapies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Immune_suppression" title="Immune suppression">Immune suppression</a> dampens an abnormal <a href="/wiki/Immune_response" title="Immune response">immune response</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autoimmune_diseases" title="Autoimmune diseases">autoimmune diseases</a> or reduces a normal <a href="/wiki/Immune_response" title="Immune response">immune response</a> to prevent <a href="/wiki/Transplant_rejection" title="Transplant rejection">rejection</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organ_transplant" title="Organ transplant">transplanted</a> organs or cells.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Immunosuppressive_drugs">Immunosuppressive drugs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Immunosuppressive drugs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Immunosuppressive_drug" title="Immunosuppressive drug">Immunosuppressive drugs</a> help manage organ transplantation and autoimmune disease. Immune responses depend on lymphocyte proliferation. <a href="/wiki/Immunosuppressive_drug#Cytostatics" title="Immunosuppressive drug">Cytostatic drugs</a> are immunosuppressive. <a href="/wiki/Immunosuppressive_drug#Glucocorticoids" title="Immunosuppressive drug">Glucocorticoids</a> are somewhat more specific inhibitors of lymphocyte activation, whereas inhibitors of <a href="/wiki/Immunosuppressive_drug#Drugs_acting_on_immunophilins" title="Immunosuppressive drug">immunophilins</a> more specifically target T lymphocyte activation. <a href="/wiki/Immunosuppressive_drug#Antibodies" title="Immunosuppressive drug">Immunosuppressive antibodies</a> target steps in the immune response. <a href="/wiki/Immunosuppressive_drug#Other_drugs" title="Immunosuppressive drug">Other drugs</a> modulate immune responses.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Immune_tolerance">Immune tolerance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Immune tolerance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Immune_tolerance" title="Immune tolerance">Immune tolerance</a></div>
<p>The body naturally does not launch an immune system attack on its own tissues. Immune tolerance therapies seek to reset the immune system so that the body stops mistakenly attacking its own organs or cells in <a href="/wiki/Autoimmune_disease" title="Autoimmune disease">autoimmune disease</a> or accepts foreign tissue in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organ_transplant" title="Organ transplant">organ transplantation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid12110811_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid12110811-38">[38]</a></sup> Creating immunity reduces or eliminates the need for lifelong immunosuppression and attendant side effects. It has been tested on transplantations, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Type_1_diabetes" title="Type 1 diabetes">type 1 diabetes</a> or other autoimmune disorders.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Allergies">Allergies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Allergies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Allergen_immunotherapy" title="Allergen immunotherapy">Allergen immunotherapy</a></div>
<p>Immunotherapy is used to treat <a href="/wiki/Allergy" title="Allergy">allergies</a>. While allergy treatments (such as <a href="/wiki/Antihistamine" title="Antihistamine">antihistamines</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corticosteroids" title="Corticosteroids">corticosteroids</a>) treat allergic symptoms, immunotherapy can reduce sensitivity to <a href="/wiki/Allergen" title="Allergen">allergens</a>, lessening its severity.</p>
<p>Immunotherapy may produce long-term benefits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid10441602_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid10441602-39">[39]</a></sup> Immunotherapy is partly effective in some people and ineffective in others, but it offers allergy sufferers a chance to reduce or stop their symptoms.</p>
<p>The therapy is indicated for people who are extremely allergic or who cannot avoid specific <a href="/wiki/Allergen" title="Allergen">allergens</a>. Immunotherapy is generally not indicated for food or medicinal allergies. This therapy is particularly useful for people with <a href="/wiki/Allergic_rhinitis" title="Allergic rhinitis">allergic rhinitis</a> or <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">asthma</a>.</p>
<p>The first dose contain tiny amounts of the allergen or antigen. Dosages increase over time, as the person becomes desensitized. This technique has been tested on infants to prevent peanut allergies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Helminthic_therapies">Helminthic therapies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Immunotherapy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Helminthic therapies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whipworm" title="Whipworm">Whipworm</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ovum" title="Ovum">ova</a> (<i>Trichuris suis</i>) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hookworm" title="Hookworm">Hookworm</a> (<i>Necator americanus</i>) have been tested for immunological diseases and allergies. <a href="/wiki/Helminthic_therapy" title="Helminthic therapy">Helminthic therapy</a> has been investigated as a treatment for relapsing remitting <a href="/wiki/Multiple_sclerosis" title="Multiple sclerosis">multiple sclerosis</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid17230481_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid17230481-41">[41]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Crohn%27s_disease" title="Crohn's disease">Crohnâs</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid16344586_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid16344586-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19050918_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19050918-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid18619749_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid18619749-44">[44]</a></sup> allergies and asthma.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid16965287_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid16965287-45">[45]</a></sup> The mechanism of how the helminths modulate the immune response, is unknown. Hypothesized mechanisms include re-polarisation of the Th1 / Th2 response<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid15603764_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid15603764-46">[46]</a></sup> and modulation of dendritic cell function.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19308259_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19308259-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19120496_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19120496-48">[48]</a></sup> The helminths down regulate the pro-inflammatory Th1 cytokines, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interleukin-12" title="Interleukin-12">Interleukin-12</a> (IL-12), Interferon-Gamma (IFN-Î³) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tumour_Necrosis_Factor" title="Tumour Necrosis Factor">Tumour Necrosis Factor-Alpha</a> (TNF-Î¬), while promoting the production of regulatory Th2 cytokines such as <a href="/wiki/Interleukin_10" title="Interleukin 10">IL-10</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interleukin-4" title="Interleukin-4">IL-4</a>, <a href="/wiki/Interleukin_5" title="Interleukin 5">IL-5</a> and <a href="/wiki/Interleukin_13" title="Interleukin 13">IL-13</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid15603764_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-pmid15603764-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid19468064_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid19468064-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>Co-evolution with helminths has shaped some of the genes associated with <a href="/wiki/Interleukin" title="Interleukin">Interleukin</a> expression and immunological disorders, such <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crohn%27s" title="Crohn's">Crohn's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ulcerative_colitis" title="Ulcerative colitis">ulcerative colitis</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celiac_Disease" title="Celiac Disease">Celiac disease</a>. Helminth's relationship to humans as hosts should be classified as mutualistic or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Symbionts" title="Symbionts">symbiotic</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
