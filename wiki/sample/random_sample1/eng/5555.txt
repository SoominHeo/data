<p>The <b>Myeongryang Strait</b> (also known as <b>Uldolmok Strait</b>; meaning <b>Screaming Strait</b>), just off the southwest corner of <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, separates <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jindo_Island" title="Jindo Island">Jindo Island</a> from the mainland. It also separates the administrative district of <a href="/wiki/Haenam_County" title="Haenam County">Haenam County</a> (on the mainland) from <a href="/wiki/Jindo_County" title="Jindo County">Jindo County</a>. At its narrowest point, it is about 293 meters across.</p>
<p>The strait has proved important at several points in Korean history. The seclusion it provided allowed the <a href="/wiki/Sambyeolcho_Rebellion" title="Sambyeolcho Rebellion">Sambyeolcho Rebellion</a> to take refuge here in 1271. In 1597, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hideyoshi%27s_invasions_of_Korea" title="Hideyoshi's invasions of Korea">Seven Year War</a>, the Korean admiral <a href="/wiki/Yi_Sun-sin" title="Yi Sun-sin">Yi Sun-sin</a> achieved a decisive victory here against the Japanese fleet, in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Myeongnyang" title="Battle of Myeongnyang">Battle of Myeongnyang</a>.</p>
<p>Today, the strait is spanned by the <a href="/wiki/Jindo_(island)" title="Jindo (island)">Jindo (island)</a> bridge, South Korea's longest <a href="/wiki/Suspension_bridge" title="Suspension bridge">suspension bridge</a>. There is also a memorial to Yi Sun-sin on the coast.</p>
<p>The Myeongryang Strait, during the lifetime of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yi_Sun-Shin" title="Yi Sun-Shin">Yi Sun-Shin</a>, was also known as 'Uldolmok,' or 'the Roaring Channel,' most likely due to the powerful tidal forces occupying and traversing the channel, which were especially loud during springtime.</p>
