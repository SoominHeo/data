<p>The <b>20th century</b> was a century that began on January 1, 1901<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and ended on December 31, 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It was the tenth and final century of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd millennium</a>. It is distinct from the century known as the <b>1900s</b> (sometimes written as <a href="/wiki/19XX" title="19XX">19XX</a>), which began on January 1, 1900 and ended on December 31, 1999.</p>
<p>The 20th century was dominated by a chain of events that heralded significant changes in world history as to redefine the era: <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_power" title="Nuclear power">nuclear power</a> and <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">space exploration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">nationalism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Decolonization" title="Decolonization">decolonization</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> and Post-Cold War conflicts; <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organization" title="Intergovernmental organization">intergovernmental organizations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cultural_homogenization" title="Cultural homogenization">cultural homogenization</a> through developments in <a href="/wiki/Emerging_technologies" title="Emerging technologies">emerging</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transportation" title="Transportation">transportation</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communications_technology" title="Communications technology">communications technology</a>; <a href="/wiki/Poverty_reduction" title="Poverty reduction">poverty reduction</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">world population</a> growth, awareness of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">environmental degradation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecological_extinction" title="Ecological extinction">ecological extinction</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wilson_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wilson-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and the birth of the <a href="/wiki/Digital_Revolution" title="Digital Revolution">Digital Revolution</a>. It saw great advances in communication and medical technology that by the late 1980s allowed for <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">near-instantaneous worldwide computer communication</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genetic_modification" title="Genetic modification">genetic modification</a> of life. The term "<a href="/wiki/Short_twentieth_century" title="Short twentieth century">short twentieth century</a>" was coined to represent the events from 1914 to 1991.</p>
<p>Global <a href="/wiki/Total_fertility_rate" title="Total fertility rate">total fertility rates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sea_level_rise" title="Sea level rise">sea level rise</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ecological_collapse" title="Ecological collapse">ecological collapses</a> skyrocketed exponentially; the resulting competition for land and dwindling resources accelerated <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a>, water depletion and the <a href="/wiki/Extinction_event" title="Extinction event">mass extinction</a> of half the <a href="/wiki/Holocene_extinction" title="Holocene extinction">world's wildlife population</a>; consequences which are now being dealt with.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It took all of human history up to 1804 for the world's population to reach 1 billion;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_population_milestones" title="World population milestones">world population</a> reached 2 billion estimates in 1927; by late 1999, the global population reached 6 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Global <a href="/wiki/Literacy" title="Literacy">literacy</a> averaged 80%; global lifespan-averages exceeded 40+ years for the first time in history, with over half achieving 70+ years (three decades <i>longer</i> than it was a century ago);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and income earnings of $10 or more per day outnumbered those earning $1 or less.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Globally approximately 45% of those who were married and able to have children used contraception;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dar2010p10_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dar2010p10-14">[14]</a></sup> 40% of pregnancies were <a href="/wiki/Unintended_pregnancy" title="Unintended pregnancy">unplanned</a>; half of unplanned pregnancies were <a href="/wiki/Abortion" title="Abortion">aborted</a>.</p>

<p>The century had the first global-scale <a href="/wiki/Total_war" title="Total war">total wars</a> between world powers across continents and oceans in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">Nationalism</a> became a major political issue in the world in the 20th century, acknowledged in <a href="/wiki/International_law" title="International law">international law</a> along with the right of nations to <a href="/wiki/Self-determination" title="Self-determination">self-determination</a>, official decolonization in the mid-century, and nationalist-influenced armed regional-conflicts.</p>
<p>The century saw a major shift in the way that many people lived, with changes in politics, ideology, economics, society, culture, science, technology, and medicine. The 20th century may have seen more technological and scientific progress than all the other centuries combined since the dawn of civilization. Terms like <i>ideology</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/World_war" title="World war">world war</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Genocide" title="Genocide">genocide</a>,</i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_war" title="Nuclear war">nuclear war</a></i> entered common usage. Scientific discoveries, such as the <a href="/wiki/Theory_of_relativity" title="Theory of relativity">theory of relativity</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quantum_physics" title="Quantum physics">quantum physics</a>, profoundly changed the foundational models of physical science, forcing scientists to realize that the universe was more complex than previously believed, and dashing the hopes (or fears) at the end of the 19th century that the last few details of scientific knowledge were about to be filled in. It was a century that started with horses, simple automobiles, and <a href="/wiki/Cargo_ship" title="Cargo ship">freighters</a> but ended with <a href="/wiki/High-speed_rail" title="High-speed rail">high-speed rail</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cruise_ships" title="Cruise ships">cruise ships</a>, global <a href="/wiki/Aviation" title="Aviation">commercial air travel</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_shuttle" title="Space shuttle">space shuttle</a>. Horses, Western society's basic form of personal transportation for thousands of years, were replaced by automobiles and buses within a few decades. These developments were made possible by the exploitation of <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">fossil fuel</a> resources, which offered energy in an easily portable form, but also caused concern about pollution and long-term impact on the <a href="/wiki/Environment_(biophysical)" title="Environment (biophysical)">environment</a>. Humans explored <a href="/wiki/Outer_space" title="Outer space">space</a> for the first time, taking their first footsteps on the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a>.</p>

<p>Mass media, telecommunications, and information technology (especially computers, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paperback_book" title="Paperback book">paperback books</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_education" title="Public education">public education</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>) made the world's knowledge more widely available. Advancements in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical_technology" title="Medical technology">medical technology</a> also improved the health of many people: the global life expectancy increased from 35 years to 65 years. Rapid technological advancements, however, also allowed warfare to reach unprecedented levels of destruction. World War II alone killed over 60 million people, while <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapons" title="Nuclear weapons">nuclear weapons</a> gave humankind the means to annihilate itself in a short time. However, these same wars resulted in the destruction of the Imperial system. For the first time in human history, empires and their wars of expansion and colonization ceased to be a factor in international affairs, resulting in a far more globalized and cooperative world. The last time major powers clashed openly was in 1945, and since then, violence has seen an unprecedented decline.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>The world also became more <a href="/wiki/Monoculturalism" title="Monoculturalism">culturally homogenized</a> than ever with developments in transportation and communications technology, <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">popular music</a> and other influences of Western culture, international corporations, and what was arguably a true <a href="/wiki/Economic_globalization" title="Economic globalization">global economy</a> by the end of the 20th century.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Technological advancements during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> changed the way war was fought, as new inventions such as <a href="/wiki/Tank" title="Tank">tanks</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemical_weapons" title="Chemical weapons">chemical weapons</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> modified tactics and strategy. After more than four years of <a href="/wiki/Trench_warfare" title="Trench warfare">trench warfare</a> in western Europe, and 20 million dead, the powers that had formed the <a href="/wiki/Triple_Entente" title="Triple Entente">Triple Entente</a> (<a href="/wiki/French_Third_Republic" title="French Third Republic">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">Britain</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russia</a>, later replaced by the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and joined by <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Italy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Romania" title="Kingdom of Romania">Romania</a>) emerged victorious over the <a href="/wiki/Central_Powers" title="Central Powers">Central Powers</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bulgaria" title="Kingdom of Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>). In addition to annexing much of the colonial possessions of the vanquished states, the Triple Entente exacted punitive restitution payments from them, plunging Germany in particular into economic depression. The regime of Tsar <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicholas_II" title="Nicholas II">Nicholas II</a> was overthrown during the conflict, Russia became the first communist state, and the Austro-Hungarian and Ottoman empires were dismantled at the war's conclusion.</p>

<p>At the beginning of the period, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">Britain</a> was the world's most powerful nation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ferguson_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ferguson-17">[17]</a></sup> having <a href="/wiki/Pax_Britannica" title="Pax Britannica">acted as the world's policeman</a> for the past century. Fascism, a movement which grew out of post-war <a href="/wiki/Angst" title="Angst">angst</a> and which accelerated during the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> of the 1930s, gained momentum in Italy, Germany and Spain in the 1920s and 1930s, culminating in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, sparked by <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a>'s aggressive expansion at the expense of its neighbors. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japan</a> had rapidly transformed itself into a technologically advanced industrial power. Its military expansion into eastern Asia and the Pacific Ocean culminated in <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">a surprise attack</a> on the United States, <a href="/wiki/Military_history_of_the_United_States_during_World_War_II" title="Military history of the United States during World War II">bringing it</a> into World War II. After some years of dramatic military success, Germany was <a href="/wiki/German_Instrument_of_Surrender" title="German Instrument of Surrender">defeated</a> in 1945, having been <a href="/wiki/Western_Allied_invasion_of_Germany" title="Western Allied invasion of Germany">invaded</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and Poland from the east and by the United States, the United Kingdom, Canada, and <a href="/wiki/Free_France" title="Free France">Free France</a> from the west. The war ended with the <a href="/wiki/Atomic_bombings_of_Hiroshima_and_Nagasaki" title="Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki">dropping of two atomic bombs</a> on Japan. Japan later became a western ally with an economy based on the manufacture of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consumer_goods" title="Consumer goods">consumer goods</a> and trade. Germany <a href="/wiki/Allied-occupied_Germany" title="Allied-occupied Germany">was divided</a> between the Western powers (<a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>; all areas recaptured by the Soviet Union (<a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East Germany</a> and eastward) became Soviet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puppet_states" title="Puppet states">puppet states</a> under communist rule. Meanwhile, Western European countries were influenced by the American <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Plan" title="Marshall Plan">Marshall Plan</a> and made a quick <a href="/wiki/Economic_recovery" title="Economic recovery">economic recovery</a>, becoming major allies of the United States under capitalist economies and relatively democratic governments.</p>
<p>World War II left about 60 million people dead. When the conflict ended in 1945, the United States and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> emerged as the major world powers. Allies during the war, they soon became hostile to one another as the competing ideologies of communism and <a href="/wiki/Democratic_capitalism" title="Democratic capitalism">democratic capitalism</a> occupied Europe, divided by the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Curtain" title="Iron Curtain">Iron Curtain</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Wall" title="Berlin Wall">Berlin Wall</a>. The military alliances headed by these nations (<a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> in North America and Western Europe; the <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Pact" title="Warsaw Pact">Warsaw Pact</a> in Eastern Europe) threatened each other with total war in what was called the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> (1947–91). The period was marked by a new arms race, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapons" title="Nuclear weapons">nuclear weapons</a> were produced in the tens of thousands, sufficient to end most human life on the planet had a large-scale nuclear exchange ever occurred. The size of the nuclear arsenals is believed by many historians to have staved off war between the two, as the consequences were too great to bear. The policy of massive nuclear attack, knowing a similar counterattack would be forthcoming, was called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mutually_assured_destruction" title="Mutually assured destruction">mutually assured destruction</a> (MAD). However, several <a href="/wiki/Proxy_war" title="Proxy war">proxy wars</a>, such as the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> (1950–1953) and the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a> (1957–1975), were waged as the United States implemented its worldwide "<a href="/wiki/Containment" title="Containment">containment</a>" policy against communism.</p>

<p>After World War II, most of the <a href="/wiki/History_of_colonialism" title="History of colonialism">European-colonized world</a> in Africa and Asia gained independence in a process of <a href="/wiki/Decolonization" title="Decolonization">decolonization</a>. Meanwhile, the wars empowered several nations, including the UK, USA, Russia, China and Japan, to exert a strong influence over many world affairs. <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_the_United_States" title="Culture of the United States">American culture</a> spread around the world with the advent of the <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_States" title="Cinema of the United States">Hollywood motion picture industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Broadway_(Manhattan)" title="Broadway (Manhattan)">Broadway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock and roll</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fast_food" title="Fast food">fast food</a>, <a href="/wiki/Big-box_store" title="Big-box store">big-box stores</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hip-hop" title="Hip-hop">hip-hop</a> lifestyle. Britain continued to influence world <a href="/wiki/Culture" title="Culture">culture</a>, including the <i><a href="/wiki/British_Invasion" title="British Invasion">British Invasion</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">The Rolling Stones</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>) into <a href="/wiki/Music_of_the_United_States" title="Music of the United States">American music</a>, leading many <a href="/wiki/Band_(rock_and_pop)" title="Band (rock and pop)">rock bands</a> from other countries (such as Swedish <a href="/wiki/ABBA" title="ABBA">ABBA</a>) to sing in English. The western world and parts of Asia enjoyed a <a href="/wiki/Post%E2%80%93World_War_II_economic_expansion" title="Post–World War II economic expansion">post–World War II economic expansion</a>. After the Soviet Union <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">collapsed</a> under internal pressure in 1991, the communist governments of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_bloc" title="Eastern bloc">Eastern bloc</a> were dismantled, followed by awkward transitions into market economies.</p>
<p>Following World War II, the United Nations, successor to the League of Nations, was established as an international forum in which the world's nations could discuss issues diplomatically. It enacted <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_resolution" title="United Nations resolution">resolutions</a> on such topics as the conduct of warfare, environmental protection, international <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">sovereignty</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a>. <a href="/wiki/Peacekeeping" title="Peacekeeping">Peacekeeping</a> forces consisting of troops provided by various countries, with various United Nations and other aid agencies, helped to relieve famine, disease, and poverty, and to suppress some local armed conflicts. Europe slowly united, economically and, in some ways, politically, to form the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, which consisted of 15 European countries by the end of the 20th century.</p>
<p>In the last third of the century, concern about humankind's impact on the Earth's <a href="/wiki/Environment_(biophysical)" title="Environment (biophysical)">environment</a> made environmentalism popular. In many countries, especially in Europe, the movement was channeled into politics through <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">Green parties</a>. Increasing awareness of <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> began in the 1980s, commencing decades of social and political debate.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_nature_of_innovation_and_change">The nature of innovation and change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The nature of innovation and change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Due to continuing industrialization and expanding trade, many significant changes of the century were, directly or indirectly, economic and technological in nature. Inventions such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_bulb" title="Light bulb">light bulb</a>, the automobile, and the telephone in the late 19th century, followed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supertanker" title="Supertanker">supertankers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Airliner" title="Airliner">airliners</a>, motorways, radio, television, <a href="/wiki/Antibiotics" title="Antibiotics">antibiotics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frozen_food" title="Frozen food">frozen food</a>, computers and <a href="/wiki/Microcomputer" title="Microcomputer">microcomputers</a>, the Internet, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mobile_telephone" title="Mobile telephone">mobile telephones</a> affected people's <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">quality of life</a> across the developed world. Scientific research, engineering professionalization and technological development drove changes in everyday life.</p>

<p>At the beginning of the century, strong discrimination based on race and sex was significant in general society. Although the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_slave_trade" title="Atlantic slave trade">Atlantic slave trade</a> had ended in the 19th century, the fight for equality for non-white people in white-dominated societies of North America, Europe, and South Africa continued. During the century, the social taboo of <a href="/wiki/Sexism" title="Sexism">sexism</a> fell. By the end of the 20th century, women had the same legal rights as men in many parts of the world, and racism had come to be seen as abhorrent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Attitudes towards <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality" title="Homosexuality">homosexuality</a> also began to change in the later part of the century.</p>

<p>The number of people killed during the century by government actions was in the hundreds of millions. This includes deaths caused by wars, genocide, politicide and mass murders. The deaths from acts of war during the two world wars alone have been estimated at between 50 and 80 million<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="also contradicted by figure of 100m by Tilly, below (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Political scientist <a href="/wiki/Rudolph_Rummel" title="Rudolph Rummel">Rudolph Rummel</a> estimated 262,000,000 deaths caused by <a href="/wiki/Democide" title="Democide">democide</a>, which excludes those killed in war battles, civilians unintentionally killed in war and killings of rioting mobs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> According to <a href="/wiki/Charles_Tilly" title="Charles Tilly">Charles Tilly</a>, "Altogether, about 100 million people died as a direct result of action by organized military units backed by one government or another over the course of the century. Most likely a comparable number of civilians died of war-induced disease and other indirect effects."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> It is estimated that approximately 70 million Europeans died through war, violence and famine between 1914 and 1945.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>After gaining political rights in the United States and much of Europe in the first part of the century, and with the advent of new <a href="/wiki/Birth_control" title="Birth control">birth control</a> techniques, <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_suffrage" title="Women's suffrage">women became more independent</a> throughout the century.</li>
<li>Rising nationalism and increasing national awareness were among the many causes of World War I (1914–1918), the first of two wars to involve many major world powers including Germany, France, Italy, Japan, Russia/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a>, the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. World War I led to <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_of_World_War_I#Territorial_gains_and_losses" title="Aftermath of World War I">the creation of many new countries</a>, especially in Eastern Europe. At the time, it was said by many to be the "<a href="/wiki/The_war_to_end_war" title="The war to end war">war to end war</a>".</li>
<li>Civil wars occurred in many nations. A violent <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">civil war</a> broke out in Spain in 1936 when General <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Francisco Franco</a> rebelled against the <a href="/wiki/Second_Spanish_Republic" title="Second Spanish Republic">Second Spanish Republic</a>. Many<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (July 2013)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> consider this war as a testing battleground for World War II, as the fascist armies bombed some Spanish territories.</li>
<li>The economic and political <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_of_World_War_I" title="Aftermath of World War I">aftermath of World War I</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> in the 1930s led to the rise of <a href="/wiki/Fascism" title="Fascism">Fascism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nazism" title="Nazism">Nazism</a> in Europe, and subsequently to World War II (1939–1945). This war also involved <a href="/wiki/Pacific_War" title="Pacific War">Eastern Asia and the Pacific</a>, in the form of Japanese aggression against <a href="/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War" title="Second Sino-Japanese War">China</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">United States</a>. Civilians also suffered greatly in World War II, due to the <a href="/wiki/Aerial_bombing_of_cities" title="Aerial bombing of cities">aerial bombing of cities</a> on both sides, and the German <a href="/wiki/Genocide" title="Genocide">genocide</a> of the Jews and others, known as <a href="/wiki/The_Holocaust" title="The Holocaust">the Holocaust</a>. In August 1945, <a href="/wiki/Atomic_bombings_of_Hiroshima_and_Nagasaki" title="Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki">Hiroshima and Nagasaki were bombed with nuclear weapons</a>. And then, <a href="/wiki/Surrender_of_Japan" title="Surrender of Japan">Japan surrendered</a>, ending the war.</li>
<li>During World War I, in the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Revolution" title="Russian Revolution">Russian Revolution</a> of 1917, 300 years of <a href="/wiki/House_of_Romanov" title="House of Romanov">Romanov</a> reign were ended and the <a href="/wiki/Bolsheviks" title="Bolsheviks">Bolsheviks</a>, under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin" title="Vladimir Lenin">Vladimir Lenin</a>, established the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_SFSR" title="Russian SFSR">world's first</a> <a href="/wiki/Socialist_state" title="Socialist state">socialist state</a>, precipitating the founding of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and the rise of <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communism</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union_in_World_War_II" title="Soviet Union in World War II">Soviet Union's involvement in World War II</a>, communism became a major force in <a href="/wiki/Global_politics" title="Global politics">global politics</a>, notably in Eastern Europe, China, <a href="/wiki/Indochina" title="Indochina">Indochina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Communist_party" title="Communist party">communist parties</a> gained near-absolute power. This led to the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> and <a href="/wiki/Proxy_war" title="Proxy war">proxy wars</a> with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_bloc" title="Western bloc">Western bloc</a>, including wars in <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korea</a> (1950–1953) and <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam</a> (1957–1975).</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>The Cold War had caused an <a href="/wiki/Arms_race" title="Arms race">arms race</a> and increasing competition between the two major players in the world: the Soviet Union and the United States. This competition included the development and improvement of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">nuclear weapons</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Space_Race" title="Space Race">Space Race</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Human_rights_in_the_Soviet_Union" title="Human rights in the Soviet Union">Soviet</a> authorities caused the deaths of millions of their own citizens in order to eliminate domestic opposition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> More than 18 million people passed through the <a href="/wiki/Gulag" title="Gulag">Gulag</a>, with a further 6 million being <a href="/wiki/Population_transfer_in_the_Soviet_Union#Timeline" title="Population transfer in the Soviet Union">exiled</a> to remote areas of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/African-American_Civil_Rights_Movement_(1954%E2%80%931968)" title="African-American Civil Rights Movement (1954–1968)">Civil Rights Movement</a> in the United States and the movement against <a href="/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">apartheid</a> in South Africa challenged racial segregation in those countries.</li>
<li>The two <a href="/wiki/World_war" title="World war">world wars</a> led to efforts to increase <a href="/wiki/Multilateralism" title="Multilateralism">international cooperation</a>, notably through the founding of the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> after World War I, and its successor, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>, after World War II.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">Nationalist movements</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">subcontinent</a> led to the independence and partition of India and Pakistan.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mahatma_Gandhi" title="Mahatma Gandhi">Gandhi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nonviolence" title="Nonviolence">nonviolence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indian_independence_movement" title="Indian independence movement">civil disobedience against</a> the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> influenced many <a href="/wiki/Political_movement" title="Political movement">political movements</a> around the world, including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African-American_Civil_Rights_Movement_(1954%E2%80%9368)" title="African-American Civil Rights Movement (1954–68)">African American Civil Rights Movement</a> in the U.S., and freedom movements in South Africa and Burma.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Declaration_of_Independence" title="Israeli Declaration of Independence">creation</a> in 1948 of <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Jewish_state" title="Jewish state">Jewish state</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Mandate_of_Palestine" title="British Mandate of Palestine">British Mandate of Palestine</a> fueled many <a href="/wiki/Arab%E2%80%93Israeli_conflict" title="Arab–Israeli conflict">regional conflicts</a>. These were also influenced by the vast <a href="/wiki/Oil_field" title="Oil field">oil fields</a> in many of the other countries of the mostly Arab region.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Decolonization" title="Decolonization">The end</a> of <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colonialism</a> led to the independence of many African and Asian countries. During the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>, many of these aligned with the United States, the USSR, or China for defense.</li>
<li>After a long period of civil wars and <a href="/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion" title="Boxer Rebellion">conflicts with western powers</a>, China's <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">last imperial dynasty</a> <a href="/wiki/Xinhai_Revolution" title="Xinhai Revolution">ended</a> in 1912. The resulting <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912%E2%80%9349)" title="Republic of China (1912–49)">republic</a> was replaced, after <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">another civil war</a>, by a communist <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic" title="People's Republic">People's Republic</a> in 1949. At the end of the 20th century, though still ruled by a communist party, China's economic system had <a href="/wiki/Chinese_economic_reform" title="Chinese economic reform">largely transformed</a> to <a href="/wiki/Capitalism" title="Capitalism">capitalism</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Great_Chinese_Famine" title="Great Chinese Famine">Great Chinese Famine</a> was a direct cause of the death of tens of millions of Chinese peasants between 1959 and 1962. It is thought to be the largest <a href="/wiki/Famine" title="Famine">famine</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_history" title="Human history">human history</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bmj.com_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-bmj.com-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a> caused <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War_casualties" title="Vietnam War casualties">two million deaths</a>, changed the dynamics between the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Bloc" title="Eastern Bloc">Eastern</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Bloc" title="Western Bloc">Western Blocs</a>, and altered <a href="/wiki/North%E2%80%93South_divide" title="North–South divide">North-South relations</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_War_in_Afghanistan" title="Soviet War in Afghanistan">Soviet War in Afghanistan</a> caused one million deaths and contributed to the downfall of the Soviet Union.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bmj.com_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-bmj.com-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Revolutions_of_1989" title="Revolutions of 1989">revolutions of 1989</a> released Eastern and Central Europe from <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Empire" title="Soviet Empire">Soviet supremacy</a>. Soon thereafter, the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_Czechoslovakia" title="Dissolution of Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Breakup_of_Yugoslavia" title="Breakup of Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a> dissolved, the latter violently over several years, into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Successor_state" title="Successor state">successor states</a>, many rife with <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_nationalism" title="Ethnic nationalism">ethnic nationalism</a>. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a> <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">were reunified in 1990</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Tiananmen_Square_protests_of_1989" title="Tiananmen Square protests of 1989">Tiananmen Square protests of 1989</a>, culminating in the deaths of hundreds of civilian protesters, were a series of demonstrations in and near Tiananmen Square in Beijing, China. Led mainly by students and intellectuals, the protests occurred in a year that saw the collapse of a number of communist governments around the world.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/European_integration" title="European integration">European integration</a> began in earnest in the 1950s, and eventually led to the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, a political and economic union that comprised 15 countries at the end of the 20th century.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_entertainment">Culture and entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Culture and entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<ul>
<li>As the century began, Paris was the artistic capital of the world, where both French and foreign writers, composers and visual artists gathered. By the end of the century <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> had become the artistic capital of the world.</li>
<li>Theater, films, music and the media had a major influence on fashion and trends in all aspects of life. As many films and much music originate from the United States, American culture spread rapidly over the world.</li>
<li>1953 saw the <a href="/wiki/Coronation_of_Queen_Elizabeth_II" title="Coronation of Queen Elizabeth II">coronation of Queen Elizabeth II</a>, an iconic figure of the century.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Visual_culture" title="Visual culture">Visual culture</a> became more dominant not only in films but in comics and television as well. During the century a new skilled understanding of narrativist imagery was developed.</li>
<li>In theater, sometimes referred to as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Broadway_theater" title="Broadway theater">Broadway</a> or <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> or <a href="/wiki/Off-Off-Broadway" title="Off-Off-Broadway">Off-Off-Broadway</a> playwrights like <a href="/wiki/Eugene_O%27Neill" title="Eugene O'Neill">Eugene O'Neill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Beckett" title="Samuel Beckett">Samuel Beckett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Albee" title="Edward Albee">Edward Albee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Miller" title="Arthur Miller">Arthur Miller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tennessee_Williams" title="Tennessee Williams">Tennessee Williams</a> introduced innovative language and ideas to the idiom. In musical theater, figures like <a href="/wiki/Rodgers_and_Hammerstein" title="Rodgers and Hammerstein">Rodgers and Hammerstein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lerner_and_Loewe" title="Lerner and Loewe">Lerner and Loewe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Irving_Berlin" title="Irving Berlin">Irving Berlin</a> had an enormous impact on both film and the culture in general.

</li>
<li>Computer games and internet surfing became new and popular form of entertainment during the last 25 years of the century.</li>
<li>In Literature, science fiction, <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">fantasy</a> (with well-developed fictional worlds, rich in detail), and <a href="/wiki/Alternate_history" title="Alternate history">alternative history</a> fiction gained unprecedented popularity. <a href="/wiki/Detective_fiction" title="Detective fiction">Detective fiction</a> gained unprecedented popularity in the <a href="/wiki/Interwar_period" title="Interwar period">interwar period</a>. In the United States in 1961 <a href="/wiki/Grove_Press" title="Grove Press">Grove Press</a> published <i><a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer_(novel)" title="Tropic of Cancer (novel)">Tropic of Cancer</a></i> a novel by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Miller" title="Henry Miller">Henry Miller</a> redefining pornography and censorship in publishing in America.</li>

<ul>
<li>In music <a href="/wiki/Igor_Stravinsky" title="Igor Stravinsky">Igor Stravinsky</a> revolutionized classical composition. <a href="/wiki/Argentine_tango" title="Argentine tango">Tango</a> was created in Argentina and became extremely popular in the rest of the Americas and Europe. <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">Blues</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> music became popularized during the 1910s and 1920s in the United States. Blues went on to influence <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock and roll</a> in the 1950s, which along with <a href="/wiki/American_folk_music_revival" title="American folk music revival">Folk Music</a>, increased in popularity with the <a href="/wiki/British_Invasion" title="British Invasion">British Invasion</a> of the mid-to-late 1960s. Rock soon branched into many different genres, including <a href="/wiki/Folk_rock" title="Folk rock">Folk rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> and became the dominant genre of <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">popular music</a>. This was challenged with the rise of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> in the 1980s and 1990s. Other genres such as <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a>, <a href="/wiki/Techno" title="Techno">techno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a> all developed during the latter half of the century and went through various periods of popularity. The world's most popular / famous music artists of the 20th century include : <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Parker" title="Charlie Parker">Charlie Parker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miles_Davis" title="Miles <li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_Dance" title="Modern Dance">Modern Dance</a> is born in America as a 'rebellion' against centuries-old European ballet. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dancers" title="Dancers">Dancers</a> and choreographers <a href="/wiki/Isadora_Duncan" title="Isadora Duncan">Isadora Duncan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vaslav_Nijinsky" title="Vaslav Nijinsky">Vaslav Nijinsky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ruth_St._Denis" title="Ruth St. Denis">Ruth St. Denis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martha_Graham" title="Martha Graham">Martha Graham</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jose_Limon" title="Jose Limon">Jose Limon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doris_Humphrey" title="Doris Humphrey">Doris Humphrey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merce_Cunningham" title="Merce Cunningham">Merce Cunningham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Taylor_(choreographer)" title="Paul Taylor (choreographer)">Paul Taylor</a> re-defined movement, struggling to bring it back to its 'natural' roots and along with Jazz, created a solely American art form.</li>
<li>In classical music, <a href="/wiki/Musical_composition" title="Musical composition">composition</a> branched out into many completely new domains, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dodecaphony" title="Dodecaphony">dodecaphony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aleatoric_music" title="Aleatoric music">aleatoric</a> (chance) music, and <a href="/wiki/Minimalism" title="Minimalism">minimalism</a>.</li>

<li>The art world experienced the development of new styles and explorations such as <a href="/wiki/Fauvism" title="Fauvism">fauvism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Expressionism" title="Expressionism">expressionism</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dadaism" title="Dadaism">Dadaism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cubism" title="Cubism">cubism</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/De_stijl" title="De stijl">de stijl</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surrealism" title="Surrealism">surrealism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abstract_expressionism" title="Abstract expressionism">abstract expressionism</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Color_field" title="Color field">color field</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_art" title="Pop art">pop art</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minimal_art" title="Minimal art">minimal art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lyrical_abstraction" title="Lyrical abstraction">lyrical abstraction</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Conceptual_art" title="Conceptual art">conceptual art</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Modern_art" title="Modern art">modern art</a> movement revolutionized art and culture and set the stage for both <a href="/wiki/Modernism" title="Modernism">Modernism</a> and its counterpart <a href="/wiki/Postmodern_art" title="Postmodern art">postmodern art</a> as well as other <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_art" title="Contemporary art">contemporary art</a> practices.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a> began as advanced architecture and design but fell out of fashion after World War I. The style was dynamic and inventive but unsuited to the depression of the Great War.</li>
<li>In Europe, <a href="/wiki/Modern_architecture" title="Modern architecture">modern architecture</a> departed from the decorated styles of the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian era</a>. Streamlined forms inspired by machines became commonplace, enabled by developments in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Building_materials" title="Building materials">building materials</a> and technologies. Before World War II, many European architects moved to the United States, where modern architecture continued to develop.</li>
<li>The automobile increased the mobility of people in the Western countries in the early-to-mid-century, and in many other places by the end of the 20th century. <a href="/wiki/Urban_planning" title="Urban planning">City design</a> throughout most of the West became focused on transport via car.</li>
<li>The popularity of sport increased considerably—both as an activity for all, and as entertainment, particularly on television.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_and_mathematics">Science and mathematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Science and mathematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alan_Turing_portr%C3%A9.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="309" data-file-width="280" height="210" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Alan_Turing_portr%C3%A9.jpg/190px-Alan_Turing_portr%C3%A9.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/07/Alan_Turing_portr%C3%A9.jpg 1.5x" width="190"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alan_Turing_portr%C3%A9.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The pioneer of <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Turing" title="Alan Turing">Alan Turing</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Science advanced dramatically during the century. There were new and radical developments in the <a href="/wiki/Outline_of_physical_science" title="Outline of physical science">physical</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Life_sciences" title="Life sciences">life</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_science" title="Human science">human</a> sciences, building on the progress made in the 19th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Big_Science" title="Big Science">Big Science</a> flourished, especially after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, as funding for science increased. Mathematics became ever more specialized and abstract.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mathematics">Mathematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Mathematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>New fields of <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> studying the <a href="/wiki/Theory_of_computation" title="Theory of computation">theory of computation</a> were developed. The work of <a href="/wiki/Alan_Turing" title="Alan Turing">Alan Turing</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Turing_machine" title="Turing machine">Turing machine</a>, and of <a href="/wiki/John_von_Neumann" title="John von Neumann">John von Neumann</a> was seminal.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6del%27s_incompleteness_theorems" title="Gödel's incompleteness theorems">Gödel's incompleteness theorems</a> were formulated and proven.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fermat%27s_Last_Theorem" title="Fermat's Last Theorem">Fermat's Last Theorem</a> was proved after 358 years (proof published in 1995).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Physics">Physics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Physics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>New areas of <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a>, like <a href="/wiki/Special_relativity" title="Special relativity">special relativity</a>, <a href="/wiki/General_relativity" title="General relativity">general relativity</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Quantum_mechanics" title="Quantum mechanics">quantum mechanics</a>, were developed during the first half of the century. In the process, the internal structure of <a href="/wiki/Atom" title="Atom">atoms</a> came to be clearly understood, followed by the discovery of <a href="/wiki/Elementary_particle" title="Elementary particle">elementary particles</a>.</li>
<li>It was found that all the known <a href="/wiki/Force" title="Force">forces</a> can be traced to only four <a href="/wiki/Fundamental_interaction" title="Fundamental interaction">fundamental interactions</a>. It was discovered further that two forces, <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetism" title="Electromagnetism">electromagnetism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Weak_interaction" title="Weak interaction">weak interaction</a>, can be merged in the <a href="/wiki/Electroweak_interaction" title="Electroweak interaction">electroweak interaction</a>, leaving only three different fundamental interactions.</li>
<li>Discovery of <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_reaction" title="Nuclear reaction">nuclear reactions</a>, in particular <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_fusion" title="Nuclear fusion">nuclear fusion</a>, finally revealed the source of <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">solar</a> energy.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Radiocarbon_dating" title="Radiocarbon dating">Radiocarbon dating</a> was invented, and became a powerful technique for determining the age of <a href="/wiki/Prehistory" title="Prehistory">prehistoric</a> animals and plants as well as <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">historical</a> objects.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Astronomy">Astronomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Astronomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>A much better understanding of the evolution of the <a href="/wiki/Universe" title="Universe">universe</a> was achieved, its <a href="/wiki/Age_of_the_universe" title="Age of the universe">age</a> (about 13.75 billion years) was determined, and the <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang" title="Big Bang">Big Bang</a> theory on its origin was proposed and generally accepted.</li>
<li>The age of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_system" title="Solar system">solar system</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>, was determined, and it turned out to be much older than believed earlier: more than 4 billion years, rather than the 20 million years suggested by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_Kelvin" title="Lord Kelvin">Lord Kelvin</a> in 1862.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></li>
<li>The planets of the solar system and their moons were closely observed via numerous <a href="/wiki/Space_probe" title="Space probe">space probes</a>. <a href="/wiki/Pluto" title="Pluto">Pluto</a> was discovered in 1930 on the edge of the solar system, although in the early 21st century, it was reclassified as a <a href="/wiki/Plutoid" title="Plutoid">plutoid</a> instead of a planet proper, leaving eight planets.</li>
<li>No trace of <a href="/wiki/Life" title="Life">life</a> was discovered on any of the other planets in our solar system (or elsewhere in the universe), although it remained undetermined whether some forms of primitive life might exist, or might have existed, somewhere. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrasolar_planets" title="Extrasolar planets">Extrasolar planets</a> were observed for the first time.</li>
<li>In 1969, <a href="/wiki/Apollo_11" title="Apollo 11">Apollo 11</a> was launched towards the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neil_Armstrong" title="Neil Armstrong">Neil Armstrong</a> became the first person from <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a> to walk on another celestial body.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Biology">Biology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Biology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Genetics" title="Genetics">Genetics</a> was unanimously accepted and significantly developed. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Structure_of_DNA" title="Structure of DNA">structure of DNA</a> was determined in 1953 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_D._Watson" title="James D. Watson">James Watson</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nobelmedicine1962_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-nobelmedicine1962-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chemheritageFranklin_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-chemheritageFranklin-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Francis_Crick" title="Francis Crick">Francis Crick</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nobelmedicine1962_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-nobelmedicine1962-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chemheritageFranklin_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-chemheritageFranklin-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rosalind_Franklin" title="Rosalind Franklin">Rosalind Franklin</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chemheritageFranklin_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-chemheritageFranklin-29">[29]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Wilkins" title="Maurice Wilkins">Maurice Wilkins</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nobelmedicine1962_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-nobelmedicine1962-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chemheritageFranklin_29-3"><a href="#cite_note-chemheritageFranklin-29">[29]</a></sup> following by developing techniques which allow to read DNA sequences and culminating in starting the <a href="/wiki/Human_Genome_Project" title="Human Genome Project">Human Genome Project</a> (not finished in the 20th century) and <a href="/wiki/Dolly_(sheep)" title="Dolly (sheep)">cloning the first mammal</a> in 1996.</li>
<li>The role of <a href="/wiki/Sexual_reproduction" title="Sexual reproduction">sexual reproduction</a> in evolution was understood, and <a href="/wiki/Bacterial_conjugation" title="Bacterial conjugation">bacterial conjugation</a> was discovered.</li>
<li>The convergence of various sciences for the formulation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Modern_evolutionary_synthesis" title="Modern evolutionary synthesis">modern evolutionary synthesis</a> (produced between 1936 and 1947), providing a widely accepted account of <a href="/wiki/Evolution" title="Evolution">evolution</a>.</li>
</ul>

<p>One of the prominent traits of the 20th century was the dramatic growth of technology. Organized research and practice of science led to advancement in the fields of communication, engineering, travel, medicine, and war.</p>
<ul>
<li>The number and types of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_appliances" title="Home appliances">home appliances</a> increased dramatically due to advancements in technology, electricity availability, and increases in wealth and leisure time. Such basic appliances as <a href="/wiki/Washing_machine" title="Washing machine">washing machines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clothes_dryer" title="Clothes dryer">clothes dryers</a>, furnaces, <a href="/wiki/Exercise_machine" title="Exercise machine">exercise machines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Refrigerator" title="Refrigerator">refrigerators</a>, freezers, <a href="/wiki/Electric_stove" title="Electric stove">electric stoves</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vacuum_cleaner" title="Vacuum cleaner">vacuum cleaners</a> all became popular from the 1920s through the 1950s. The <a href="/wiki/Microwave_oven" title="Microwave oven">microwave oven</a> became popular during the 1980s and have become a standard in all homes by the 1990s. Radios were popularized as a form of entertainment during the 1920s, which extended to television during the 1950s. Cable and satellite television spread rapidly during the 1980s and 1990s. Personal computers began to enter the home during the 1970s–1980s as well. The age of the portable music player grew during the 1960s with the development of the <a href="/wiki/Transistor_radio" title="Transistor radio">transistor radio</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stereo_8" title="Stereo 8">8-track</a> and <a href="/wiki/Compact_Cassette" title="Compact Cassette">cassette</a> tapes, which slowly began to replace <a href="/wiki/Gramophone_record" title="Gramophone record">record</a> players These were in turn replaced by the CD during the late 1980s and 1990s. The proliferation of the Internet in the mid-to-late 1990s made <a href="/wiki/Digital_distribution" title="Digital distribution">digital distribution</a> of music (mp3s) possible. <a href="/wiki/Videocassette_recorder" title="Videocassette recorder">VCRs</a> were popularized in the 1970s, but by the end of the 20th century, DVD players were beginning to replace them, making the VHS obsolete by the end of the first decade of the 21st century.</li>
<li>The first airplane was flown in 1903. With the engineering of the faster <a href="/wiki/Jet_engine" title="Jet engine">jet engine</a> in the 1940s, mass <a href="/wiki/Air_travel" title="Air travel">air travel</a> became commercially viable.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Assembly_line" title="Assembly line">assembly line</a> made mass production of the automobile viable. By the end of the 20th century, billions of people had automobiles for personal transportation. The combination of the automobile, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motor_boat" title="Motor boat">motor boats</a> and air travel allowed for unprecedented personal mobility. In western nations, motor vehicle accidents became the greatest cause of death for young people. However, expansion of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Divided_highway" title="Divided highway">divided highways</a> reduced the death rate.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Triode" title="Triode">triode tube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transistor" title="Transistor">transistor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit" title="Integrated circuit">integrated circuit</a> successively revolutionized electronics and computers, leading to the proliferation of the personal computer in the 1980s and cell phones and the public-use Internet in the 1990s.</li>
<li>New materials, most notably <a href="/wiki/Stainless_steel" title="Stainless steel">stainless steel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Velcro" title="Velcro">Velcro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silicone" title="Silicone">silicone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polytetrafluoroethylene" title="Polytetrafluoroethylene">teflon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Plastic" title="Plastic">plastics</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Polystyrene" title="Polystyrene">polystyrene</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polyvinyl_chloride" title="Polyvinyl chloride">PVC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polyethylene" title="Polyethylene">polyethylene</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nylon" title="Nylon">nylon</a> came into widespread use for many various applications. These materials typically have tremendous performance gains in strength, temperature, chemical resistance, or mechanical properties over those known prior to the 20th century.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aluminum" title="Aluminum">Aluminum</a> became an inexpensive metal and became second only to iron in use.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">Semiconductor</a> materials were discovered, and methods of production and purification developed for use in electronic devices. <a href="/wiki/Silicon" title="Silicon">Silicon</a> became one of the purest substances ever produced.</li>
<li>Thousands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemical" title="Chemical">chemicals</a> were developed for industrial processing and home use.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Space_Race" title="Space Race">Space Race</a> between the United States and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> gave a peaceful outlet to the political and military tensions of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>, leading to the first <a href="/wiki/Human_spaceflight" title="Human spaceflight">human spaceflight</a> with the Soviet Union's <i><a href="/wiki/Vostok_1" title="Vostok 1">Vostok 1</a></i> mission in 1961, and man's first landing on another world—the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a>—with America's <i><a href="/wiki/Apollo_11" title="Apollo 11">Apollo 11</a></i> mission in 1969. Later, the first <a href="/wiki/Space_station" title="Space station">space station</a> was launched by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_space_program" title="Soviet space program">Soviet space program</a>. The United States developed the first (and to date only) reusable spacecraft system with the <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_program" title="Space Shuttle program">Space Shuttle program</a>, first launched in 1981. As the century ended, a permanent manned presence in space was being founded with the ongoing construction of the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>.</li>
<li>In addition to human spaceflight, unmanned space probes became a practical and relatively inexpensive form of exploration. The first orbiting space probe, <i><a href="/wiki/Sputnik_1" title="Sputnik 1">Sputnik 1</a></i>, was launched by the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> in 1957. Over time, a massive system of artificial satellites was placed into orbit around Earth. These satellites greatly advanced navigation, communications, military intelligence, geology, climate, and numerous other fields. Also, by the end of the 20th century, unmanned probes had visited the Moon, <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(planet)" title="Mercury (planet)">Mercury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venus" title="Venus">Venus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">Mars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">Saturn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uranus" title="Uranus">Uranus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neptune" title="Neptune">Neptune</a>, and various asteroids and comets. The <a href="/wiki/Hubble_Space_Telescope" title="Hubble Space Telescope">Hubble Space Telescope</a>, launched in 1990, greatly expanded our understanding of the Universe and brought brilliant images to TV and computer screens around the world.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Medicine">Medicine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Medicine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Faroe_stamp_079_europe_(fleming).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="280" data-file-width="327" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Faroe_stamp_079_europe_%28fleming%29.jpg/220px-Faroe_stamp_079_europe_%28fleming%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8a/Faroe_stamp_079_europe_%28fleming%29.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Faroe_stamp_079_europe_(fleming).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A stamp commemorating <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Fleming" title="Alexander Fleming">Alexander Fleming</a>. His discovery of <a href="/wiki/Penicillin" title="Penicillin">penicillin</a> had changed the world of modern medicine by introducing the age of antibiotics.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Placebo" title="Placebo">Placebo</a>-<a href="/wiki/Scientific_control" title="Scientific control">controlled</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Random_sample" title="Random sample">randomized</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blind_experiment" title="Blind experiment">blinded</a> <a href="/wiki/Clinical_trial" title="Clinical trial">clinical trials</a> became a powerful tool for testing new medicines.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Antibiotics" title="Antibiotics">Antibiotics</a> drastically reduced mortality from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bacterial_disease" title="Bacterial disease">bacterial diseases</a> and their <a href="/wiki/Prevalence" title="Prevalence">prevalence</a>.</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Vaccine" title="Vaccine">vaccine</a> was developed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polio" title="Polio">polio</a>, ending a worldwide epidemic. Effective vaccines were also developed for a number of other serious infectious diseases, including <a href="/wiki/Influenza_vaccine" title="Influenza vaccine">influenza</a>, <a href="/wiki/DPT_vaccine" title="DPT vaccine">diphtheria, pertussis (whooping cough), tetanus</a>, <a href="/wiki/MMR_vaccine" title="MMR vaccine">measles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mumps_vaccine" title="Mumps vaccine">mumps</a>, <a href="/wiki/MMR_vaccine" title="MMR vaccine">rubella (German measles)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Varicella_vaccine" title="Varicella vaccine">chickenpox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_A_vaccine" title="Hepatitis A vaccine">hepatitis A</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_B_vaccine" title="Hepatitis B vaccine">hepatitis B</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Epidemiology" title="Epidemiology">Epidemiology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vaccination" title="Vaccination">vaccination</a> led to the eradication of the <a href="/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">smallpox</a> virus in humans.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">X-rays</a> became powerful diagnostic tool for wide spectrum of diseases, from bone fractures to cancer. In the 1960s, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computerized_tomography" title="Computerized tomography">computerized tomography</a> was invented. Other important diagnostic tools developed were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonography" title="Sonography">sonography</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_resonance_imaging" title="Magnetic resonance imaging">magnetic resonance imaging</a>.</li>
<li>Development of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vitamins" title="Vitamins">vitamins</a> virtually eliminated <a href="/wiki/Scurvy" title="Scurvy">scurvy</a> and other vitamin-deficiency diseases from industrialized societies.</li>
<li>New psychiatric drugs were developed. These include <a href="/wiki/Antipsychotic" title="Antipsychotic">antipsychotics</a> for treating <a href="/wiki/Hallucination" title="Hallucination">hallucinations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Delusion" title="Delusion">delusions</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Antidepressant" title="Antidepressant">antidepressants</a> for treating depression.</li>
<li>The role of <a href="/wiki/Tobacco_smoking" title="Tobacco smoking">tobacco smoking</a> in the causation of cancer and other diseases was proven during the 1950s (see <a href="/wiki/British_Doctors_Study" title="British Doctors Study">British Doctors Study</a>).</li>
<li>New methods for cancer treatment, including <a href="/wiki/Chemotherapy" title="Chemotherapy">chemotherapy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radiation_therapy" title="Radiation therapy">radiation therapy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Immunotherapy" title="Immunotherapy">immunotherapy</a>, were developed. As a result, cancer could often be <a href="/wiki/Cure" title="Cure">cured</a> or placed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Remission_(medicine)" title="Remission (medicine)">remission</a>.</li>
<li>The development of <a href="/wiki/Blood_type" title="Blood type">blood typing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blood_bank" title="Blood bank">blood banking</a> made <a href="/wiki/Blood_transfusion" title="Blood transfusion">blood transfusion</a> safe and widely available.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Invention" title="Invention">invention</a> and development of <a href="/wiki/Immunosuppressive_drug" title="Immunosuppressive drug">immunosuppressive drugs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tissue_typing" title="Tissue typing">tissue typing</a> made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organ_transplant" title="Organ transplant">organ and tissue transplantation</a> a clinical reality.</li>
<li>New methods for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heart_surgery" title="Heart surgery">heart surgery</a> were developed, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacemaker" title="Pacemaker">pacemakers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Artificial_heart" title="Artificial heart">artificial hearts</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">Cocaine</a>/<a href="/wiki/Crack_cocaine" title="Crack cocaine">crack</a> and heroin were found to be dangerous addictive drugs, and their wide usage had been outlawed; mind-altering drugs such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LSD" title="LSD">LSD</a> and <a href="/wiki/MDMA" title="MDMA">MDMA</a> were discovered and later outlawed. In many countries, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_on_drugs" title="War on drugs">war on drugs</a> caused prices to soar 10x-20x higher, leading to profitable <a href="/wiki/Black_market" title="Black market">black market</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drugdealer" title="Drugdealer">drugdealing</a>, and to prison inmate sentences being 80% related to drug use by the 1990s.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contraceptive" title="Contraceptive">Contraceptive</a> drugs were developed, which reduced population growth rates in industrialized countries, as well as decreased the taboo of <a href="/wiki/Premarital_sex" title="Premarital sex">premarital sex</a> throughout many western countries.</li>
<li>The development of medical <a href="/wiki/Insulin" title="Insulin">insulin</a> during the 1920s helped raise the life expectancy of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diabetes" title="Diabetes">diabetics</a> to three times of what it had been earlier.</li>
<li>Vaccines, hygiene and clean water improved health and decreased mortality rates, especially among infants and the young.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_diseases">Notable diseases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Notable diseases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>An <a href="/wiki/Influenza" title="Influenza">influenza</a> pandemic, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_Flu" title="Spanish Flu">Spanish Flu</a>, killed anywhere from 20 to 100 million people between 1918 and 1919.</li>
<li>A new <a href="/wiki/Virus" title="Virus">viral</a> disease, called the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_Immunodeficiency_Virus" title="Human Immunodeficiency Virus">Human Immunodeficiency Virus</a>,or HIV, arose in Africa and subsequently killed millions of people throughout the world. HIV leads to a syndrome called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acquired_Immunodeficiency_Syndrome" title="Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome">Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome</a>, or AIDS. Treatments for HIV remained inaccessible to many people living with AIDS and HIV in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developing_countries" title="Developing countries">developing countries</a>, and a cure has yet to be discovered.</li>
<li>Because of increased <a href="/wiki/Life_expectancy" title="Life expectancy">life spans</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Prevalence" title="Prevalence">prevalence</a> of cancer, <a href="/wiki/Alzheimer%27s_disease" title="Alzheimer's disease">Alzheimer's disease</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parkinson%27s_disease" title="Parkinson's disease">Parkinson's disease</a>, and other diseases of <a href="/wiki/Old_age" title="Old age">old age</a> increased slightly.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sedentary_lifestyle" title="Sedentary lifestyle">Sedentary lifestyles</a>, due to labor-saving devices and technology, along with the increase in home entertainment and technology such as television, video games, and the internet contributed to an "epidemic" of <a href="/wiki/Obesity" title="Obesity">obesity</a>, at first in the rich countries, but by the end of the 20th century spreading to the developing world.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_and_the_environment">Energy and the environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Energy and the environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:365px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Oilfields_California.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="180" data-file-width="363" height="180" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/66/Oilfields_California.jpg" width="363"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption"><a href="/wiki/Oil_field" title="Oil field">Oil field</a> in California, 1938. The first modern oil well was drilled in 1848 by Russian engineer F.N. Semyonov, on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apsheron_Peninsula" title="Apsheron Peninsula">Apsheron Peninsula</a> north-east of <a href="/wiki/Baku" title="Baku">Baku</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>The dominant use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fossil_source" title="Fossil source">fossil sources</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_power" title="Nuclear power">nuclear power</a>, considered the conventional energy sources.</li>
<li>Widespread use of petroleum in industry—both as a chemical precursor to plastics and as a fuel for the automobile and <a href="/wiki/Airplane" title="Airplane">airplane</a>—led to the geopolitical importance of petroleum resources. The Middle East, home to many of the world's oil deposits, became a center of geopolitical and military tension throughout the latter half of the century. (For example, oil was a factor in Japan's decision to go to war against the United States in 1941, and the oil cartel, <a href="/wiki/OPEC" title="OPEC">OPEC</a>, used an oil embargo of sorts in the wake of the <a href="/wiki/Yom_Kippur_War" title="Yom Kippur War">Yom Kippur War</a> in the 1970s).</li>
<li>The increase in <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">fossil fuel</a> consumption also fueled a major scientific controversy over its effect on air pollution, <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>, and global <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pesticide" title="Pesticide">Pesticides</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbicide" title="Herbicide">herbicides</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Toxicity" title="Toxicity">toxic</a> <a href="/wiki/Chemical_substance" title="Chemical substance">chemicals</a> accumulated in the environment, including in the bodies of humans and other animals.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Human_overpopulation" title="Human overpopulation">Overpopulation</a> and worldwide <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a> diminished the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_quality" title="Environmental quality">quality of the environment</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_world_at_the_end_of_the_20th_century">The world at the end of the 20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=20th_century&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: The world at the end of the 20th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Communications and information technology, transportation technology, and medical advances had radically altered daily lives. Europe appeared to be at a sustainable peace for the first time in recorded history. The people of the Indian subcontinent, a sixth of the world population at the end of the 20th century, had attained an indigenous independence for the first time in centuries. China, an ancient nation comprising a fifth of the world population, was finally open to the world in a new and powerful synthesis of west and east, creating a new state after the near-complete destruction of the old cultural order. With the end of colonialism and the Cold War, nearly a billion people in Africa were left in new nation states after centuries of foreign domination.</p>
<p>The world was undergoing its second major period of <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">globalization</a>; the first, which started in the 18th century, having been terminated by World War I. Since the US was in a dominant position, a major part of the process was <a href="/wiki/Americanization" title="Americanization">Americanization</a>. The influence of China and India was also rising, as the world's largest populations were rapidly integrating with the world economy.</p>
<p>Terrorism, dictatorship, and the spread of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapons" title="Nuclear weapons">nuclear weapons</a> were some issues requiring attention. The world was still blighted by small-scale wars and other violent conflicts, fueled by competition over resources and by ethnic conflicts. <a href="/wiki/Despotism" title="Despotism">Despots</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jong-il" title="Kim Jong-il">Kim Jong-il</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> continued to lead their nations toward the development of nuclear weapons.</p>
<p>Disease threatened to destabilize many regions of the world. New viruses such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SARS" title="SARS">SARS</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_Nile_virus" title="West Nile virus">West Nile</a> continued to spread. <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">Malaria</a> and other diseases affected large populations. Millions were infected with HIV, the virus which causes AIDS. The virus was becoming an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIDS_in_Africa" title="AIDS in Africa">epidemic in southern Africa</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Global_Warming_Predictions_Map.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="408" data-file-width="559" height="204" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Global_Warming_Predictions_Map.jpg/280px-Global_Warming_Predictions_Map.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Global_Warming_Predictions_Map.jpg/420px-Global_Warming_Predictions_Map.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/ad/Global_Warming_Predictions_Map.jpg 2x" width="280"/></a>

In this figure, the globally averaged warming corresponds to 3.0 °C (5.4 °F).</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Based on research done by climate scientists, the majority of the scientific community consider that in the long term environmental problems may threaten the planet's habitability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> One argument is that of <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> occurring, and that it may be due (at least partially) to human-caused emission of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gases</a>, particularly <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> produced by the burning of <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">fossil fuels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> This prompted many nations to negotiate and sign the <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol" title="Kyoto Protocol">Kyoto treaty</a>, which set mandatory limits on carbon dioxide emissions.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">World population</a> increased from about 1.6 billion people in 1901 to 6.1 billion at the century's end.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
