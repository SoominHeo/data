<p><b>Shift_JIS art</b> is artwork created from characters within the <a href="/wiki/Shift_JIS" title="Shift JIS">Shift JIS</a> character set, a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Industrial_Standards" title="Japanese Industrial Standards">Japanese Industrial Standards</a> (JIS) superset of <a href="/wiki/ASCII" title="ASCII">ASCII</a> intended for Japanese usage. Naturally there are many similarities between Shift_JIS artwork and <a href="/wiki/ASCII_art" title="ASCII art">ASCII art</a>.</p>
<p>Shift_JIS has become very popular on <a href="/wiki/Internet_forum" title="Internet forum">web-based bulletin boards</a>, notably <a href="/wiki/2channel" title="2channel">2channel</a>, and has even made its way into mainstream media and commercial advertising in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>Within the Japanese community, Shift_JIS art is sometimes abbreviated as <i>SJIS art</i>, but is most commonly referred to as "<b>AA</b>" meaning ASCII art, although it rarely restricts itself to the 95 printable characters within the ASCII standard.</p>
<p>As with <a href="/wiki/ANSI_art" title="ANSI art">ANSI art</a>, SJIS art is sometimes used for animation. However, due to technical advances, SJIS art also appears in the form of <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Flash" title="Adobe Flash">Adobe Flash</a> files and animated <a href="/wiki/GIF" title="GIF">GIFs</a>.</p>
<p>Unlike Western ASCII art, which is generally designed to be viewed with a <a href="/wiki/Monospaced_font" title="Monospaced font">monospaced font</a>, Shift_JIS art is designed around the proportional-width (albeit CJK characters are always the same width) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_gothic_typeface" title="Japanese gothic typeface">MS PGothic</a> font supplied with <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a>, which is the default font for web sites in Japanese versions of Windows. This dependency has led to the development of the free <a href="/wiki/Mona_Font" title="Mona Font">Mona Font</a>, in which each character is the same width as its counterpart in MS PGothic. This is useful on operating systems lacking the PGothic font, such as <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Japanese_media">In Japanese media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shift_JIS_art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In Japanese media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Japanese movie and television show, <i><a href="/wiki/Densha_Otoko" title="Densha Otoko">Densha Otoko</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">電車男</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, frequently included Shift_JIS art, both during screen transitions and within the story itself. One of the recurring characters in the TV series was a Shift_JIS artist who would often draw full-screen Shift_JIS works of art as a way of expressing his support and encouraging the lead character.</p>
