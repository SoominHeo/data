<p><i><b>Mononoke</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">モノノ怪</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">Japanese animated</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> produced by <a href="/wiki/Toei_Animation" title="Toei Animation">Toei Animation</a>. A spin-off of 2006's horror anthology series <i><a href="/wiki/Ayakashi:_Samurai_Horror_Tales" title="Ayakashi: Samurai Horror Tales">Ayakashi: Samurai Horror Tales</a></i>, <i>Mononoke</i> follows the character of the medicine seller as he continues to face a myriad of supernatural perils. Mononoke takes place during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edo_Period" title="Edo Period">Edo Period</a> Japan with the four class system, Samurai being the highest class and merchants (such as the medicine seller himself) being in the lowest class. Only Samurai were permitted to carry swords, which is why it comes as a shock to many of the characters that the medicine seller would be carrying a sword.</p>
<p>Produced by Toei Animation, the anime series was directed by <a href="/wiki/Kenji_Nakamura" title="Kenji Nakamura">Kenji Nakamura</a>, written by <a href="/wiki/Chiaki_J._Konaka" title="Chiaki J. Konaka">Chiaki J. Konaka</a>, Ikuko Takahashi, <a href="/wiki/Michiko_Yokote" title="Michiko Yokote">Michiko Yokote</a> and Manabu Ishikawa. Takashi Hashimoto directed the animation and was the character designer, Takashi Kurahashi was the art director, its music was composed by <a href="/wiki/Yasuharu_Takanashi" title="Yasuharu Takanashi">Yasuharu Takanashi</a>, and it was broadcast in <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Television" title="Fuji Television">Fuji Television</a>'s block <a href="/wiki/Noitamina" title="Noitamina">Noitamina</a> between July 2007 and September 2007, lasting 12 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Siren_Visual" title="Siren Visual">Siren Visual</a> licensed it for <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasian</a> region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation of the original Bakeneko arc was published in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Young_Gangan" title="Young Gangan">Young Gangan</a></i> between August 17, 2007 and August 1, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The individual chapters were collected and released in two <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> (collected volumes) by <a href="/wiki/Square_Enix" title="Square Enix">Square Enix</a> on January 25, 2008, and September 25, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> A second manga series started to be published on September 25, 2013 by <a href="/wiki/Tokuma_Shoten" title="Tokuma Shoten">Tokuma Shoten</a> on its magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Comic_Zenon" title="Monthly Comic Zenon">Monthly Comic Zenon</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The last chapter of it was serialized in <i>Monthly Comic Zenon</i> on November 25, 2014.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The series was released into two volumes on July 19, 2014, and December 20, 2014 respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_.26_Characters">Plot &amp; Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot &amp; Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Mononoke</i> follows a wandering, nameless character known only as the <b>"Medicine Seller"</b> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Takahiro_Sakurai" title="Takahiro Sakurai">Takahiro Sakurai</a>). The series is made up of individual chapters in which the medicine seller encounters, combats and subsequently destroys <i>mononoke</i>. The <i>"<a href="/wiki/Y%C5%8Dkai" title="Yōkai">mononoke</a>"</i> are a type of <i>ayakashi</i>, unnatural spirits that linger in the human world.</p>
<p>The Medicine Seller always proceeds in the same manner, using his knowledge of the supernatural to fend off the <i>mononoke</i> until he can learn the spirit's shape (<i>Katachi</i>), truth (<i>Makoto</i>) and reasoning (<i>Kotowari</i>). Only then can he unsheathe his sword and exorcise the demon. The English subtitles translate these three necessities as Form, Truth, and Reason.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episodes_1.E2.80.932:_Zashiki-warashi_.E5.BA.A7.E6.95.B7.E7.AB.A5.E5.AD.90">Episodes 1–2: Zashiki-warashi 座敷童子</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Episodes 1–2: Zashiki-warashi 座敷童子">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>While spending the night in a traditional inn, the Medicine Seller stumbles upon a strange phenomenon. A pregnant woman named Shino who is desperately seeking shelter at the inn is led to the last vacant room. The room, though, is haunted by a group of Zashiki Warashi. When the Zashiki Warashi kill an assassin aiming for Shino's life, protecting Shino and her unborn child, the Medicine Seller inquires into the origin of the mononoke.</p>
<p>The innkeeper reveals that the inn used to be a <a href="/wiki/Yuukaku" title="Yuukaku">brothel</a>, which she owned and ran. The innkeeper forced her prostitutes to abort their children so that they could continue working; eventually, she reserved Shino's room as an offering to the unborn children. The Medicine Seller realizes that the mononoke are attracted to Shino because of their strong desire to be born. The Zashiki Warashi want Shino to give birth to them, and she agrees, much to the Medicine Seller's dismay. She pulls the talisman warding off the mononoke from her stomach. As it turns out the Zashiki Warashi that she had met upon her arrival was in fact her own child. But since it is impossible for more than one spirit to share the same body, he is forced out. As the two say farewell the spirit of her baby is replaced.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episodes_3.E2.80.935:_Umib.C5.8Dzu_.E6.B5.B7.E5.9D.8A.E4.B8.BB">Episodes 3–5: Umibōzu 海坊主</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Episodes 3–5: Umibōzu 海坊主">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_2">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Traveling on a merchant's luxurious ship, the Medicine Seller and the other passengers drift into the <a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Sea" title="Devil's Sea">Dragon's Triangle</a>, a mysterious sea full of ayakashi. Among the passengers are Kayo, a servant girl from the Sakai house of Bakeneko fame, Genkei, a Buddhist monk, and Genyousai, a minstrel and spiritualist. Through the appearance of Umizatou, an ayakashi who demands that the passengers reveal their worst fears, the group discovers that Genkei was the one who set the ship off course. Genkei explains that it was the hatred of his late sister Oyou that attracted the ayakashi and created the Dragon's Triangle—Oyou took Genkei's place in an <a href="/wiki/Utsuro-bune" title="Utsuro-bune">utsurobune</a>, a ship made from a hollowed log, and set sail as a sacrifice to appease the ayakashi. The Medicine Seller discovers that Genkei is responsible for attracting the ayakashi; unable to accept that he had been joyful rather than guilty when his sister took his place, Genkei had allowed his darker emotions to separate from him in the form of a mononoke. The Medicine Seller exterminates the mononoke at Genkei's request and restores calm to the Dragon's Triangle.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episodes_6.E2.80.937:_Noppera-b.C5.8D_.E3.81.AE.E3.81.A3.E3.81.BA.E3.82.89.E3.81.BC.E3.81.86">Episodes 6–7: Noppera-bō のっぺらぼう</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Episodes 6–7: Noppera-bō のっぺらぼう">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_3">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A despairing woman named Ochou, wishing for freedom but unable to escape her oppression, confesses to killing her husband's entire family. The Medicine Seller doubts this story and visits Ochou in her prison cell to ask her for the truth, but encounters a mononoke in a <a href="/wiki/Noh" title="Noh">Noh</a> mask who fights the Medicine Seller and allows Ochou to escape. The man in the mask convinces Ochou that he has given her freedom by helping her kill her family, but the Medicine Seller pursues the two and reveals to Ochou that she had killed not her husband's family, but herself. Ochou married into a good family as her mother wished, but in her desire to please her mother, withstood abuse from her new family to the point of forsaking any happiness she could have gained from her life. When Ochou realizes this, the man in the Noh mask vanishes, and Ochou finds herself in her kitchen. It is implied that the man in the mask was an illusion conjured by the Medicine Seller to help Ochou escape—at the end of the episode, Ochou ignores her husband's orders and leaves her family, gaining the freedom she had long desired.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episodes_8.E2.80.939:_Nue_.E9.B5.BA">Episodes 8–9: Nue 鵺</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Episodes 8–9: Nue 鵺">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_4">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Three men seeking to marry Lady Ruri, the sole heir to the Fuenokouji school of incense (<a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dd%C5%8D" title="Kōdō">Kōdō</a>), arrive at her mansion to participate in a competition of incense only to find that the fourth suitor is missing and that the Medicine Seller has taken his place. During the competition, Lady Ruri is murdered. When the Medicine Seller inquires as to why the three suitors are so desperate to inherit the school even after Lady Ruri's death, the suitors reveal that the competition is not actually over the school of incense, but the Toudaiji, a piece of wood rumored to grant its owner great power.</p>
<p>Although Medicine Seller presides over a second incense contest, none of the three suitors win the Toudaiji, as all are killed. It is revealed that the suitors had already been killed by the Toudaiji, and that the Medicine Seller put on this act to make them realize their deaths. The Medicine Seller then asks the Toudaiji, the true mononoke, to reveal itself. The Toudaiji draws its sense of self-esteem from the fact that people value it so highly, yet in truth, it is nothing but a rotting piece of wood. The Toudaiji kills those who seek it, including Lady Ruri's suitors, perpetuating the bloodshed for its sake. The Medicine Seller destroys the Toudaiji, appeasing the souls of its victims, including Lady Ruri's suitors.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episodes_10.E2.80.9312:_Bakeneko_.E5.8C.96.E7.8C.AB">Episodes 10–12: Bakeneko 化猫</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Episodes 10–12: Bakeneko 化猫">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_5">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Set in a time decidedly later than the previous arcs — implied to be in the 1920s — the Medicine Seller boards a train with several other passengers. Unfortunately, the train hits a ghostly girl on the tracks, and six passengers and the Medicine Seller are locked in the first car. The Medicine Seller questions the passengers to reveal a dark connection between them, shedding light on the murder of a young newspaper reporter. At the end of the episode the woman's spirit has its revenge, the passengers are saved, and the Medicine Seller challenges the audience to reveal to him their Truth and Reason, vowing to continue hunting mononoke as long as they roam the world.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mononoke_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The directing and art have been called "boldly confrontational."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-title_52.26more.3D1.26c.3D1.26tb.3D1.26pb.3D1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-title_52.26more.3D1.26c.3D1.26tb.3D1.26pb.3D1-10">[10]</a></sup> It blends a murder mystery structure with "twist of supernatural and a shake of historical, peppered with plenty of stylistic experimentation." It frequently achieves "the ideal - great directing combined with great animation." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-title_52.26more.3D1.26c.3D1.26tb.3D1.26pb.3D1_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-title_52.26more.3D1.26c.3D1.26tb.3D1.26pb.3D1-10">[10]</a></sup> The Mainichi newspaper said it could not be dismissed as a mere experiment, and that the story's themes were every bit as advanced as the digital animation techniques employed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
