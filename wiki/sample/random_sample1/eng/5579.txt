<p><b>Monica Raymund</b> (born July 26, 1986) is an American actress known for her roles as Maria "Ria" Torres in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_Television_Network" title="Fox Television Network">Fox</a> police drama <i><a href="/wiki/Lie_to_Me" title="Lie to Me">Lie to Me</a></i> (2009–2011), Dana Lodge in the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> legal drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Wife" title="The Good Wife">The Good Wife</a></i> (2011–2012) and Gabriela Dawson in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Fire_(TV_series)" title="Chicago Fire (TV series)">Chicago Fire</a></i> (2012–present).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monica_Raymund&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Raymund was born in St. Petersburg, Florida to Steve Raymund, the board chairman and retired CEO of Tech Data Corp., a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clearwater,_FL" title="Clearwater, FL">Clearwater</a>-based distributor of computer components and software,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-work_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-work-2">[2]</a></sup> and Sonia, "a community volunteer [and the] co-founder of the Soulful Arts Dance Academy in St. Petersburg,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Catch_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Catch-3">[3]</a></sup> who hails from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo,_Dominican_Republic" title="Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic">Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-work_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-work-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lamag_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-lamag-4">[4]</a></sup> Her father is a "Jewish California native" (his family was from Germany and <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lamag_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-lamag-4">[4]</a></sup> Raymund has a brother, Will, who was a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Berklee_College_of_Music" title="Berklee College of Music">Berklee College of Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Catch_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Catch-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lamag_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-lamag-4">[4]</a></sup> She was raised <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a>, and celebrated a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bat_mitzvah" title="Bat mitzvah">bat mitzvah</a>. In high school, she participated in the "Broadway Theater Project in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tampa" title="Tampa">Tampa</a> and at the North Carolina School of the Arts in Winston-Salem."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Catch_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Catch-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Raymund graduated from <a href="/wiki/Shorecrest_Preparatory_School" title="Shorecrest Preparatory School">Shorecrest Preparatory School</a> in St. Petersburg, Florida in 2004, and from the <a href="/wiki/Juilliard_School" title="Juilliard School">Juilliard School</a> in New York City in 2008.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monica_Raymund&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reportedly, Raymund performed in <i>Cymbeline</i> directed by Richard Feldman, <i>The Diviners</i> (directed by Jonathan Bernstein), and <i>Animal Farm</i> (directed by Trizana Beverley) all at the <a href="/wiki/Juilliard_School" title="Juilliard School">Juilliard School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-littlemary_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-littlemary-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Raymund also appeared in her native <a href="/wiki/St._Petersburg,_Florida" title="St. Petersburg, Florida">St. Petersburg, Florida</a> in the LiveArts Peninsula Foundation's original production of <i>Manhattan Casino</i> (directed by <a href="/wiki/Bob_Devin_Jones" title="Bob Devin Jones">Bob Devin Jones</a>) where she originated the leading role of Althea Dunbar and <i>Webb's City: The Musical</i> (music &amp; lyrics by Lee Ahlin, written and directed by William Leavengood)."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-littlemary_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-littlemary-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Raymund "is a three-year alumna of Ann Reinking's <a href="/wiki/Broadway_Theater_Project" title="Broadway Theater Project">Broadway Theater Project</a>, where she has been a featured performer."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-littlemary_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-littlemary-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Raymund has also "worked with playwright <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Rivera_(playwright)" title="José Rivera (playwright)">José Rivera</a> on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boleros_for_the_Disenchanted" title="Boleros for the Disenchanted">Boleros for the Disenchanted</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Huntington_Theatre_Company" title="Huntington Theatre Company">Huntington Theatre Company</a> in Boston" in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Catch_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Catch-3">[3]</a></sup> In April 2008, she appeared the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> legal drama <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>, in season 9 episode 17 titled "<a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit_(season_9)#ep200" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit (season 9)">Authority</a>" in the opening scene as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Strip_search_prank_call_scam" title="Strip search prank call scam">strip search prank call</a> victim. From 2009 to 2011, she starred in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_Television_Network" title="Fox Television Network">Fox</a> police drama <i><a href="/wiki/Lie_to_Me" title="Lie to Me">Lie to Me</a></i>, where she played Maria "Ria" Torres, a human lie detector who is a "natural", meaning she has no formal training in detecting lies or the emotions of others. She was using crutches because of a knee injury when she auditioned for this role.</p>
<p>From 2011 until 2012, Raymund had a recurring role in the <a href="/wiki/The_Good_Wife_(season_3)" title="The Good Wife (season 3)">third season</a> of the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> legal drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Good_Wife" title="The Good Wife">The Good Wife</a></i>, where she played Dana Lodge, an assistant states attorney.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Raymund began starring in the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Fire_(TV_series)" title="Chicago Fire (TV series)">Chicago Fire</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Chicago_Fire_characters#Gabriela_Dawson" title="List of Chicago Fire characters">Gabriela Dawson</a>, the paramedic in charge.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monica_Raymund&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On June 11, 2011, Raymund married writer Neil Patrick Stewart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Raymund and Stewart separated in early 2013 and divorced in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-SW-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Raymund is an ardent supporter of <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> rights. In February 2014, she <a href="/wiki/Coming_out" title="Coming out">came out</a> publicly as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bisexual" title="Bisexual">bisexual</a> for the first time via <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AEBi_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-AEBi-10">[10]</a></sup> Before that, she had been out privately for over ten years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AEBi_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-AEBi-10">[10]</a></sup> On September 16, 2015, Raymund announced via Twitter that she had begun dating Tari Segal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SW_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-SW-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
