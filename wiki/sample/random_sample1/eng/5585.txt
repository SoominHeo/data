<p><b>Maude Apatow</b> (born December 15, 1997)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American actress. She is the eldest daughter of director <a href="/wiki/Judd_Apatow" title="Judd Apatow">Judd Apatow</a> and actress <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Mann" title="Leslie Mann">Leslie Mann</a> and is known for her roles as the daughter to her real-life mother's characters in <i><a href="/wiki/Knocked_Up" title="Knocked Up">Knocked Up</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/Funny_People" title="Funny People">Funny People</a></i> (2009), and <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_40" title="This Is 40">This Is 40</a></i> (2012).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maude_Apatow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Apatow first appeared in the 2007 comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Knocked_Up" title="Knocked Up">Knocked Up</a></i>, written, produced and directed by her father <a href="/wiki/Judd_Apatow" title="Judd Apatow">Judd Apatow</a>. In the film, she plays Sadie to parents Pete (played by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Rudd" title="Paul Rudd">Paul Rudd</a>) and Debbie (played by her real life mother, <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Mann" title="Leslie Mann">Leslie Mann</a>). Her sister in the film, Charlotte, is played by her real-life sister, <a href="/wiki/Iris_Apatow" title="Iris Apatow">Iris Apatow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-THR1-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2009, she played Mable, the daughter to her real-life mother's character again in <i><a href="/wiki/Funny_People" title="Funny People">Funny People</a></i>, another film her father wrote, produced and directed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She reprised her role of Sadie in the spin-off to <i>Knocked Up</i>, the 2012 film <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_40" title="This Is 40">This Is 40</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THR1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-THR1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>When she is not acting, she is noted to have a large <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> fan base, which helped her become a contributor to <a href="/wiki/Zooey_Deschanel" title="Zooey Deschanel">Zooey Deschanel</a>'s website <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hello_Giggles" title="Hello Giggles">Hello Giggles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, her Twitter feed was voted as one of the "Best Twitter Feeds of 2013" by <a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)"><i>Time</i> (magazine)</a>, calling her tweets "funny and earnest".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is currently enrolled at <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_University" title="Northwestern University">Northwestern University</a> studying Radio, Television, Film.</p>
