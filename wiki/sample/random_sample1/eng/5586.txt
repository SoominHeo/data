<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Litany of the Saints</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Litany of the Saints</b> (<a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>: <i><b>Litani√¶ Sanctorum</b></i>) is a formal <a href="/wiki/Prayer" title="Prayer">prayer</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Rite_Orthodox" title="Western Rite Orthodox">Western Rite Orthodox</a> communities. It is a prayer to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triune_God" title="Triune God">Triune God</a>, which also includes invocations for the intercession of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blessed_Virgin_Mary" title="Blessed Virgin Mary">Blessed Virgin Mary</a>, the Angels and all the <a href="/wiki/Martyr" title="Martyr">martyrs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint" title="Saint">saints</a> upon whom <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> was founded, and those recognised as saints through the subsequent history of the church. Following the invocation of the saints, the Litany concludes with a series of supplications to <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">God</a> to hear the prayers of the worshippers. It is most prominently sung during the <a href="/wiki/Easter_Vigil" title="Easter Vigil">Easter Vigil</a>, <a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Day" title="All Saints' Day">All Saints' Day</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/Liturgy" title="Liturgy">liturgy</a> for conferring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Orders" title="Holy Orders">Holy Orders</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Catholic_practice">Catholic practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Litany_of_the_Saints&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Catholic practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The definitive version of the Roman Catholic <i>Litany of the Saints</i> is a Latin text published in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graduale_Romanum" title="Graduale Romanum">Roman Gradual</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR-1">[1]</a></sup> The current edition was published in 1979, and contains a statement of approval from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congregation_for_Divine_Worship" title="Congregation for Divine Worship">Congregation for Divine Worship</a> issued 24 June 1972.</p>
<p>The litany is published in five sections. The first contains a short series of invocations of God, beginning with a threefold <a href="/wiki/Kyrie" title="Kyrie">Kyrie</a>, followed by invocations of <a href="/wiki/God_the_Father" title="God the Father">God the Father</a> of Heaven, the <a href="/wiki/God_the_Son" title="God the Son">Son</a> who redeemed the world, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Spirit_(Christianity)" title="Holy Spirit (Christianity)">Holy Spirit</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Trinity" title="Holy Trinity">Holy Trinity</a>.</p>
<p>The second section lists the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saints" title="Saints">saints</a> who are to be included, given in the following order. Within each category, men are listed in chronological order, followed by women, also in chronological order. Distinctive names are given in brackets so the cantor knows which saint is intended, but a directive notes that the bracketed names may be omitted when the Latin is sung. Additional saints, such as the patron of a place or the founder of a religious order, may be inserted in the appropriate place. The official list of recognized saints can be found in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Martyrology" title="Roman Martyrology">Roman Martyrology</a>.</p>

</ul>
<p>Note that certain names are grouped together by the litany itself (e.g. Michael, Gabriel and Raphael; Francis and Dominic); in the list above, a semi-colon always indicates the next line of the litany. Note also that some priests and religious who are also Doctors of the Church (Catherine of Siena, Teresa of Avila, Bernard of Clairvaux and Thomas Aquinas) are grouped with the "Priests and Religious", rather than with the "Bishops and Doctors". Strict chronological order is not followed in the case of the Jesuit, Francis Xavier (died 1552), who is placed after the founder of the Jesuits, Ignatius Loyola, who died in 1556.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fitz_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fitz-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Part three of the litany is a series of petitions to Christ, the first 15 having the response, "free us O Lord", with a further 10 petitions ending "Have Mercy on us."</p>
<p>The fourth section includes a list of petitions ending <i>te rogamus audi nos</i> (we beseech you to hear us) from which the appropriate prayers can be chosen for a particular occasion, but always ending with petitions for the whole church, for the ministers of the Church, for the lay faithful and for all humanity. The rubrics printed before the litany indicate that other petitions ‚Äúsuitable to the occasion‚Äù and in the form proper to the Litany may be added ‚Äúat the proper place‚Äù.</p>
<p>The final part of the litany consists of a brief invocation calling on Christ to hear the prayers, and a closing <a href="/wiki/Collect" title="Collect">collect</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="At_the_Easter_Vigil">At the Easter Vigil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Litany_of_the_Saints&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: At the Easter Vigil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Litany of Saints is used prominently at the <a href="/wiki/Easter_Vigil" title="Easter Vigil">Easter Vigil</a>, the Mass celebrated on the night before <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Easter_Day" title="Easter Day">Easter Day</a>. At this Mass, adults who have chosen to become Catholic receive the Sacraments of Initiation in the form of <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">Baptism</a>, or simple Reception, with <a href="/wiki/Confirmation" title="Confirmation">Confirmation</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Communion" title="Holy Communion">Holy Communion</a>. Following the readings from Scripture and just before the actual rite of baptism or confirmation, the Litany of Saints is sung. Even if there is no-one to be baptized, the Litany may be sung for the blessing of <a href="/wiki/Holy_water" title="Holy water">holy water</a> in the font. But if the font is not blessed (<i>i.e.</i> holy water is blessed in a simple container on the sanctuary), the Litany is not used.</p>
<p>The Litany given for the Easter Vigil in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Missal" title="Roman Missal">Roman Missal</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RM_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-RM-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> contains a shortened list of saints:</p>
<ul>
<li>Holy Mary, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mother_of_God" title="Mother of God">Mother of God</a>; Saint <a href="/wiki/Michael_(archangel)" title="Michael (archangel)">Michael</a>; all holy angels; <a href="/wiki/John_the_Baptist" title="John the Baptist">John the Baptist</a>; <a href="/wiki/Saint_Joseph" title="Saint Joseph">Joseph</a>; <a href="/wiki/Saint_Peter" title="Saint Peter">Peter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_the_Apostle" title="Paul the Apostle">Paul</a>; <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="At_Infant_Baptisms">At Infant Baptisms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Litany_of_the_Saints&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: At Infant Baptisms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A severely abbreviated form of the litany is given in the official text for the Rite of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paedobaptism" title="Paedobaptism">Baptism of Children</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RBC_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-RBC-5">[5]</a></sup> This consists only of the invocations of Mary Mother of God, St John the Baptist, St Joseph, St Peter and St Paul, and All Holy Men and Women, with the addition of saints relevant to the circumstances of the baptism. In the rite the litany is immediately preceded by suggested or <i>ad hoc</i> prayers for the child(ren) being baptized and family members present, and immediately followed by a prayer of minor exorcism. A similar rite together with a prayer of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minor_exorcism_in_the_Catholic_Church" title="Minor exorcism in the Catholic Church">minor exorcism</a> is used in the Catholic Church.</p>
<p>An extended form of the litany is also permitted for baptisms, beginning with a Kyrie, and followed by the same selection of saints used for the Easter Vigil (as listed above). The saints are followed by brief invocations of Christ and then petitions which include "Give new life to these chosen ones by the grace of baptism".</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_ceremonies">In other ceremonies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Litany_of_the_Saints&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In other ceremonies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Litany of Saints is also prescribed<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fitz_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Fitz-2">[2]</a></sup> for ordination (different saints are added corresponding to the different grades of ordained ministry), religious profession, the blessing of an abbot, and the dedication of churches and altars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin language</a> version of the Litany, the names of one or more saints are chanted by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cantor_(church)" title="Cantor (church)">cantor</a> or <a href="/wiki/Choir" title="Choir">choir</a>, and the congregants reply with either, <i>Ora pro nobis</i> (if one saint is addressed) or <i>Orate pro nobis</i> (using the plural imperative form of the verb, if more than one saint is addressed). Both responses translate to "Pray for us." However, it is permissible to personalize the Litany of Saints for a funeral rite or other Mass for the dead. When this was done during the <a href="/wiki/Funeral_of_Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Funeral of Pope John Paul II">Funeral of Pope John Paul II</a>, the response was <i>Ora[te] pro eo</i>, or "Pray for him."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fitz_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Fitz-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>A Vatican recommendation<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> issued in 1988 proposes that the Litany can be appropriately used for the beginning of the Mass of the First Sunday of Lent, to offer a distinguishing mark for the beginning of Lent.</p>
<p>The iBreviary website offers a text in English<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> of the full Litany of Saints expanded with many additional saints, drawn in part from the bespoke litanies for particular liturgical occasions. It includes a note that in ceremonies involving the Pope, the canonized Popes are moved from their usual place to form part of an expanded list of Popes prior to other bishops and doctors.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origen_Controversy">Origen Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Litany_of_the_Saints&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Origen Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A commercially published setting of the Litany of Saints by John Becker<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Becker_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Becker-11">[11]</a></sup> includes the name of <a href="/wiki/Origen" title="Origen">Origen</a> among its additional saints. Although recognized<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> by Pope Benedict XVI as a significant theologian, Origen is not listed in the Roman Martyrology<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anathematized" title="Anathematized">anathematized</a> in the year 553 for certain opinions he was alleged to have held.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Origen's inclusion in a published litany, albeit without the official sanction of Catholic authorities, has resulted in vigorous comment in the blogosphere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditionalist_Catholic_practice">Traditionalist Catholic practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Litany_of_the_Saints&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Traditionalist Catholic practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the late 1960s, Roman Catholic liturgical texts were changed according to the directives of <i><a href="/wiki/Sacrosanctum_Concilium" title="Sacrosanctum Concilium">Sacrosanctum Concilium</a></i>, a key document of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Vatican_Council" title="Second Vatican Council">Second Vatican Council</a>. The texts in use immediately prior to the Council, those of 1962, may still be used today by priests of the <a href="/wiki/Latin_Church" title="Latin Church">Latin Church</a>, freely if celebrating without the people, under the conditions indicated in article 5 of the 2007 motu proprio <i><a href="/wiki/Summorum_Pontificum" title="Summorum Pontificum">Summorum Pontificum</a></i> if with the people.</p>
<p>The form of the litany in use prior to the Council is given in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Ritual" title="Roman Ritual">Roman Ritual</a>, published in a Latin-English edition in 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RR2_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-RR2-16">[16]</a></sup> Note that the <i>Catholic Encyclopedia</i> article available online<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> entirely reflects pre-Vatican II usage.</p>
<p>This Litany of the Saints begins with a threefold <a href="/wiki/Kyrie" title="Kyrie">Kyrie</a>, followed (as in the current version) by invocations of <a href="/wiki/God_the_Father" title="God the Father">God the Father</a> of Heaven, the <a href="/wiki/God_the_Son" title="God the Son">Son</a> who redeemed the world, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Spirit_(Christianity)" title="Holy Spirit (Christianity)">Holy Spirit</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Trinity" title="Holy Trinity">Holy Trinity</a>. The names of the saints follow:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virgin_Mary" title="Virgin Mary">Virgin Mary</a> is invoked three times, as Holy Mary, as Holy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mother_of_God" title="Mother of God">Mother of God</a> and as Holy Virgin of Virgins; then Saints <a href="/wiki/Michael_(archangel)" title="Michael (archangel)">Michael</a>; <a href="/wiki/Gabriel" title="Gabriel">Gabriel</a>; <a href="/wiki/Raphael_(archangel)" title="Raphael (archangel)">Raphael</a>; all holy angels and archangels; all the holy order of blessed spirits.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patriarchs" title="Patriarchs">Patriarchs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prophets_in_Judaism" title="Prophets in Judaism">Prophets</a>: beginning with Saint <a href="/wiki/John_the_Baptist" title="John the Baptist">John the Baptist</a> [then other names, if they are to be inserted] and always ending with <a href="/wiki/Saint_Joseph" title="Saint Joseph">Saint Joseph</a>; all holy patriarchs and prophets.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Apostles" title="Apostles">Apostles</a> and disciples: <a href="/wiki/Saint_Peter" title="Saint Peter">Peter</a>; <a href="/wiki/Paul_the_Apostle" title="Paul the Apostle">Paul</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Andrew" title="Saint Andrew">Andrew</a>; <a href="/wiki/James,_son_of_Zebedee" title="James, son of Zebedee">James</a>; <a href="/wiki/John_the_Apostle" title="John the Apostle">John</a>; <a href="/wiki/Thomas_the_Apostle" title="Thomas the Apostle">Thomas</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_James_the_Less" title="Saint James the Less">James</a>; <a href="/wiki/Philip_the_Apostle" title="Philip the Apostle">Philip</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Bartholomew" title="Saint Bartholomew">Bartholomew</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Matthew" title="Saint Matthew">Matthew</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Simon_the_Apostle" title="Simon the Apostle">Simon</a>; <a href="/wiki/Jude_the_Apostle" title="Jude the Apostle">Thaddeus</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matthias_the_Apostle" title="Matthias the Apostle">Matthias</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Barnabas" title="Saint Barnabas">Barnabas</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Luke" title="Saint Luke">Luke</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Mark" title="Saint Mark">Mark</a>; all holy apostles and evangelists; all holy disciples of the Lord; all the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_Innocents" title="Holy Innocents">Holy Innocents</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martyrs" title="Martyrs">Martyrs</a>: <a href="/wiki/Saint_Stephen" title="Saint Stephen">Stephen</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Laurence" title="Saint Laurence">Laurence</a>; <a href="/wiki/Vincent_of_Saragossa" title="Vincent of Saragossa">Vincent</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Fabian" title="Saint Fabian">Fabian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Sebastian" title="Saint Sebastian">Sebastian</a>; <a href="/wiki/John_and_Paul" title="John and Paul">John and Paul</a>; <a href="/wiki/Saints_Cosmas_and_Damian" title="Saints Cosmas and Damian">Cosmas and Damian</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saints_Gervasius_and_Protasius" title="Saints Gervasius and Protasius">Gervasius and Protasius</a>; All holy martyrs.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishops" title="Bishops">Bishops</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctors_of_the_Church" title="Doctors of the Church">Doctors of the Church</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pope_Silvester_I" title="Pope Silvester I">Sylvester</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gregory_the_Great" title="Gregory the Great">Gregory</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Ambrose" title="Saint Ambrose">Ambrose</a>; <a href="/wiki/Augustine_of_Hippo" title="Augustine of Hippo">Augustine</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Jerome" title="Saint Jerome">Jerome</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_de_Tours" title="Martin de Tours">Martin</a>; <a href="/wiki/Saint_Nicholas" title="Saint Nicholas">Nicholas</a>; all holy Bishops and Confessors; all holy <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_the_Church" title="Doctor of the Church">Doctors</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Priests" title="Priests">Priests</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Religious_(Catholicism)" title="Religious (Catholicism)">male Religious</a> [without further distinction between priests, deacons, and lay religious]: <a href="/wiki/Anthony_the_Great" title="Anthony the Great">Anthony</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Benedict" title="Saint Benedict">Benedict</a>; <a href="/wiki/Bernard_of_Clairvaux" title="Bernard of Clairvaux">Bernard</a>; <a href="/wiki/Saint_Dominic" title="Saint Dominic">Dominic</a>; <a href="/wiki/Francis_of_Assisi" title="Francis of Assisi">Francis</a>; All ye holy Priests and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Levites" title="Levites">Levites</a>; All ye holy <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monks" title="Monks">Monks</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hermits" title="Hermits">Hermits</a>.</li>
<li>Female saints, ordered as the men were above: <a href="/wiki/Mary_Magdalene" title="Mary Magdalene">Mary Magdalene</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Agatha" title="Saint Agatha">Agatha</a>; <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucy" title="Saint Lucy">Lucy</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Agnes" title="Saint Agnes">Agnes</a>; <a href="/wiki/Saint_Cecilia" title="Saint Cecilia">Cecilia</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Catherine_of_Alexandria" title="Saint Catherine of Alexandria">Catherine (of Alexandria)</a>; <a href="/wiki/Anastasia_of_Sirmium" title="Anastasia of Sirmium">Anastasia</a>; all holy Virgins and Widows; all holy Saints of God.</li>
</ul>
<p>The litany then twice pleads with God to be merciful, and this is followed by 21 invocations for which the response is <i>libera</i> ("O Lord, deliver us"), then 17 petitions with the response <i>Te rogamus audi nos</i> (we beseech thee, hear us). The final part of the litany consists of seven invocations of Christ, the first three under the title "<a href="/wiki/Lamb_of_God" title="Lamb of God">Lamb of God</a>".</p>
