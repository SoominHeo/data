<p><b>Magdalen College</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/ɔː/ 'au' in 'fraud'">ɔː</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɪ/ or /ə/ 'e' in 'roses'">ᵻ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span> <span title="English pronunciation respelling"><a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i><b><span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">MAWD</span></span></b>-lin</i></a></span>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Names_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Names-2">[2]</a></sup> is one of the <a href="/wiki/Colleges_of_the_University_of_Oxford" title="Colleges of the University of Oxford">constituent colleges</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a>. As of 2014<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the college had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Financial_endowment" title="Financial endowment">financial endowment</a> of £180.8 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Magdalen stands next to the <a href="/wiki/River_Cherwell" title="River Cherwell">River Cherwell</a> and has within its grounds a <a href="/wiki/Deer" title="Deer">deer</a> park and <a href="/wiki/Addison%27s_Walk" title="Addison's Walk">Addison's Walk</a>. The large, square <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magdalen_Tower,_Oxford" title="Magdalen Tower, Oxford">Magdalen Tower</a> is an Oxford landmark, and it is a tradition, dating to the days of <a href="/wiki/Henry_VII_of_England" title="Henry VII of England">Henry VII</a>, that the college choir sings from the top of it at 6 a.m. on <a href="/wiki/May_Morning" title="May Morning">May Morning</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-world_and_its_people_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-world_and_its_people-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Magdalen College was founded in 1458 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_of_Waynflete" title="William of Waynflete">William of Waynflete</a>, Bishop of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winchester,_England" title="Winchester, England">Winchester</a> and Lord Chancellor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The founder's statutes included provision for a choral foundation of men and boys (a tradition that has continued to the present day) and made reference to the pronunciation of the name of the college in English. The college received another substantial endowment from the estate of Sir <a href="/wiki/John_Fastolf" title="John Fastolf">John Fastolf</a> of Caister Castle in Norfolk (1380–1459). Another unrelated college named Magdalen Hall adjacent to Magdalen College eventually became part of <a href="/wiki/Hertford_College,_Oxford" title="Hertford College, Oxford">Hertford College</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Magdalen's prominence since the mid-20th century owes much to such famous fellows as <a href="/wiki/C._S._Lewis" title="C. S. Lewis">C. S. Lewis</a> and <a href="/wiki/A._J._P._Taylor" title="A. J. P. Taylor">A. J. P. Taylor</a>, and its academic success to the work of such dons as <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Dewar_Weldon" title="Thomas Dewar Weldon">Thomas Dewar Weldon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Women were first admitted to the college in 1979.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2015, Magdalen topped Oxford's <a href="/wiki/Norrington_Table" title="Norrington Table">Norrington Table</a> of college undergraduate examination results.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grounds">Grounds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Grounds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The college has large grounds, close to the city centre. They stretch north and east from the college, and are most of the area bounded by <a href="/wiki/Longwall_Street" title="Longwall Street">Longwall Street</a>, the <a href="/wiki/High_Street,_Oxford" title="High Street, Oxford">High Street</a> (where the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Porter%27s_lodge" title="Porter's lodge">porter's lodge</a> is located), and <a href="/wiki/St_Clement%27s,_Oxford" title="St Clement's, Oxford">St Clement's</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Grove_.28Deer_Park.29">The Grove (Deer Park)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Grove (Deer Park)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Magdalen_Deer.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Magdalen_Deer.jpg/220px-Magdalen_Deer.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Magdalen_Deer.jpg/330px-Magdalen_Deer.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Magdalen_Deer.jpg/440px-Magdalen_Deer.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Magdalen_Deer.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The deer in the Grove</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This large meadow occupies most of the north west of the college's grounds, from the New Buildings and the Grove Quad up to Holywell Ford. During the winter and spring, it is the home of a herd of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fallow_Deer" title="Fallow Deer">Fallow Deer</a>. It is possible to view the meadow (and also the deer) from the path between New Buildings and Grove Quad, and also from the archway in New Buildings.</p>
<p>In the 16th century, long before the introduction of the deer, the grove consisted of gardens, orchards, and <a href="/wiki/Bowling_green" title="Bowling green">bowling greens</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">Civil War</a>, it was used to house a regiment of soldiers. At one point in the 19th century it was home to three traction engines belonging to the works department of the college. By the 20th century it had become well-wooded with many large trees, but most of them were lost to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_Elm_Disease" title="Dutch Elm Disease">Dutch Elm Disease</a> in the 1970s.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Meadow_.28bounded_by_Addison.27s_Walk.29">The Meadow (bounded by Addison's Walk)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Meadow (bounded by Addison's Walk)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This triangular meadow lies to the east of the college, bounded on all sides by the <a href="/wiki/River_Cherwell" title="River Cherwell">River Cherwell</a>. In the spring, it is filled with the flower <i>Fritillaria meleagris</i> (commonly known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snakeshead_Fritillary" title="Snakeshead Fritillary">Snakeshead Fritillary</a>), which gives it an attractive green-purple colour. These flowers grow in very few places, and have been recorded growing in the meadow since around 1785. Once the flowering has finished, the deer are moved in for the summer and autumn. In wet winters, some or all of the meadow may flood, as the meadow is lower lying than the surrounding path. All around the edge of the meadow is a tree-lined path, Addison's Walk. It is a beautiful and tranquil walk, favoured by students, dons, and visitors alike. It also links the college with Holywell Ford, and the Fellows' Garden.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Fellows.27_Garden">The Fellows' Garden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Fellows' Garden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Magdalen_Fellows%27_Garden.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Magdalen_Fellows%27_Garden.jpg/220px-Magdalen_Fellows%27_Garden.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Magdalen_Fellows%27_Garden.jpg/330px-Magdalen_Fellows%27_Garden.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Magdalen_Fellows%27_Garden.jpg/440px-Magdalen_Fellows%27_Garden.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Magdalen_Fellows%27_Garden.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Fellows' Garden</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Located to the north east of the Meadow, directly behind the new building of the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_Centre_for_Islamic_Studies" title="Oxford Centre for Islamic Studies">Oxford Centre for Islamic Studies</a>. This long and (fairly) narrow garden follows the Cherwell to the edge of the <a href="/wiki/University_Parks" title="University Parks">University Parks</a>. In spring, the ground is covered with flowers. In summer, there are some flowers, many different shrubs, and the varied trees provide dappled cover from the sun. It is linked to Addison's Walk by a bridge.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Buildings">Buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Magdalen_Tower" title="Magdalen Tower">Magdalen Tower</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Magdalen_Tower" title="Magdalen Tower">Great Tower</a> was built between 1492 and 1509 by <a href="/wiki/William_Orchard_(architect)" title="William Orchard (architect)">William Orchard</a>, and is an imposing landmark on the eastern approaches to the city centre. The hall and chapel were built at similar times, though both have undergone some changes in the intervening years.</p>
<p>The Cloister or Great Quad was built in 1474-80 (also by Orchard) and has been altered several times since then. In 1822, the north side was in bad shape, and was knocked down while most of the fellows were away from college (only a small group of fellows were in favour of demolishing it). It was rebuilt shortly afterwards. In the early 1900s, renovations were performed, and it was returned to a more mediaeval character. Student rooms were installed in the (very large) roof space in the 1980s.</p>
<p>The New Building was built across a large lawn to the north of the Great Quad beginning in 1733. Its spacious setting is due to the builders' intentions to create an entirely new quad, but only one side was completed. <a href="/wiki/Edward_Gibbon" title="Edward Gibbon">Edward Gibbon</a> and <a href="/wiki/C._S._Lewis" title="C. S. Lewis">C. S. Lewis</a> had their rooms in this building, and as there are very few student rooms (many being occupied by tutors), they are highly sought after.</p>
<p>The college has four other quads. The irregularly shaped St John's Quad is the first on entering the college, and includes the Outdoor Pulpit and old Grammar Hall. It connects to the Great Quad via the Perpendicular Gothic Founder's Tower, which is richly decorated with carvings and pinnacles and has carved bosses in its vault.</p>
<p>The Chaplain's Quad runs along the side of the Chapel and Hall, to the foot of the <a href="/wiki/Magdalen_Tower" title="Magdalen Tower">Great Tower</a>. St Swithun's Quad and Longwall Quad (which contains the Library) date from the late 19th and early 20th centuries, and make up the southwest corner of the college. The Grove Buildings are the newest, built in the 1990s in a traditional style.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chapel">Chapel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Chapel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Magdalen_College_Chapel_Interior,_Oxford,_UK_-_Diliff.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4813" data-file-width="4320" height="245" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Magdalen_College_Chapel_Interior%2C_Oxford%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg/220px-Magdalen_College_Chapel_Interior%2C_Oxford%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Magdalen_College_Chapel_Interior%2C_Oxford%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg/330px-Magdalen_College_Chapel_Interior%2C_Oxford%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Magdalen_College_Chapel_Interior%2C_Oxford%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg/440px-Magdalen_College_Chapel_Interior%2C_Oxford%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The Chapel of Magdalen College is a <a href="/wiki/Place_of_worship" title="Place of worship">place of worship</a> for members of the college and others in the University of Oxford community and beyond. As a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_Anglican" title="High Anglican">High Anglican</a> chapel, its tradition is influenced by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic_Revival" title="Catholic Revival">Catholic Revival</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a>.</p>
<p>Said and sung services are held daily during term.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Services_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Services-9">[9]</a></sup> The choir sings Choral <a href="/wiki/Evening_Prayer_(Anglican)" title="Evening Prayer (Anglican)">Evensong</a> or Evening Prayer every day at 6pm except on Mondays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Services_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Services-9">[9]</a></sup> On Sundays, a Sung Eucharist is offered in the morning at 11am and <a href="/wiki/Compline" title="Compline">Compline</a> (Night Prayer) and <a href="/wiki/Benediction_of_the_Blessed_Sacrament" title="Benediction of the Blessed Sacrament">Benediction</a> is sung congregationally to <a href="/wiki/Plainsong" title="Plainsong">plainsong</a> at 9pm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Services_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Services-9">[9]</a></sup> Mass is also sung on major holy days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Services_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-Services-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Choir">Choir</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Choir">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Magdalen is one of the four choral foundations in Oxford, meaning that the formation of the choir was part of the statutes of the college, the other three choral foundations being <a href="/wiki/New_College,_Oxford" title="New College, Oxford">New College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christ_Church,_Oxford" title="Christ Church, Oxford">Christ Church</a>, and, since 2007, <a href="/wiki/Merton_College,_Oxford" title="Merton College, Oxford">Merton College</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>The choir consists of 12 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clerk_(choral)" title="Clerk (choral)">academical clerks</a>, or choral scholars, who are students at the college, and 16 boys aged seven to fourteen, all of whom have scholarships at <a href="/wiki/Magdalen_College_School,_Oxford" title="Magdalen College School, Oxford">Magdalen College School</a>. The school was founded for this purpose but has long since become an independent public school.</p>
<p>The choristers' day begins at 7:30, with an early morning practice before school. There is further practice immediately after school, followed by Choral Evensong six nights a week, in term; the Tuesday service is sung by the boys only, and the Friday service only by the Academical Clerks. On Sundays there is a practice at 9:30 am followed by Eucharist, then a further afternoon practice followed by Evensong which ends at 7:00 pm. As of September 2010, the choristers no longer sing on most Saturday evenings following the formation of the Consort of Voices, a mixed voice choir which sings on most Saturdays in full term.</p>
<p>The choir has numerous college duties as well as a recording and touring schedule. Traditionally the choir sings at college Gaudies and at other special events throughout the year, as well as performing on social occasions such as Carols by Candlelight before Christmas and the famous <a href="/wiki/May_Morning" title="May Morning">May Morning</a>. On this occasion the choir sings madrigals at 6:00 am from the top of the college bell-tower to the assembled mass of students and townsfolk celebrating in the streets below.</p>
<p>The choir records regularly. In 2005, it was nominated for a prestigious <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for its <a href="/wiki/Compact_disc" title="Compact disc">CD</a>, <i>With a Merrie Noyse</i>, of music by <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Gibbons" title="Orlando Gibbons">Orlando Gibbons</a>. Other recent works include the BBC's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blue_Planet" title="The Blue Planet">The Blue Planet</a></i> and Paul McCartney's classical piece <i><a href="/wiki/Ecce_Cor_Meum" title="Ecce Cor Meum">Ecce Cor Meum</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Organists">Organists</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Organists">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Organ_scholar" title="Organ scholar">Organ scholar</a></div>
<p>The choir has had many well-known organists, such as <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Purcell" title="Daniel Purcell">Daniel Purcell</a>, Sir <a href="/wiki/John_Stainer" title="John Stainer">John Stainer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Rose_(musician)" title="Bernard Rose (musician)">Bernard Rose</a>. Among the other notable former directors and masters of the choir, who hold the title of <i>Informator Choristarum</i>, are <a href="/wiki/John_Sheppard_(composer)" title="John Sheppard (composer)">John Sheppard</a>, Sir <a href="/wiki/William_McKie_(musician)" title="William McKie (musician)">William McKie</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haldane_Campbell_Stewart" title="Haldane Campbell Stewart">Haldane Campbell Stewart</a>. Past organ scholars include <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Moore" title="Dudley Moore">Dudley Moore</a> and Matthew Martin. Past academical clerks include <a href="/wiki/John_Mark_Ainsley" title="John Mark Ainsley">John Mark Ainsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Christophers" title="Harry Christophers">Harry Christophers</a> (founder and director of <a href="/wiki/The_Sixteen" title="The Sixteen">The Sixteen</a>), <a href="/wiki/Robin_Blaze" title="Robin Blaze">Robin Blaze</a> and conductor/composer <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Rose_(musician)" title="Gregory Rose (musician)">Gregory Rose</a>.</p>
<p>The former Informator Choristarum was the composer <a href="/wiki/Grayston_Ives" title="Grayston Ives">Bill Ives</a>, possibly better known as a former King's Singer. A disc of his music, <i>Listen Sweet Dove</i>, is amongst the choir's releases. The current Informator Choristarum is Daniel Hyde, who studied at Kings College Cambridge where he was the organ scholar. In May 2016 it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Mark_Williams_(organist)" title="Mark Williams (organist)">Mark Williams</a> would take up the position in 2017, following Daniel Hyde's appointment as Organist and Director of Music of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_Church_(Manhattan)" title="Saint Thomas Church (Manhattan)">Saint Thomas Church, New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Centre_for_the_History_of_Childhood">Centre for the History of Childhood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Centre for the History of Childhood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Centre for the History of Childhood, situated at Magdalen College, is the first institution within the United Kingdom that focuses on studying every interdisciplinary aspect of the history of children from the Greeks and Romans to the present day. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Academia">Academia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Academia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Norrington_Table" title="Norrington Table">Norrington Table</a>'s history Magdalen has been top three times, in 2010, 2012 and 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> When over half its finalists achieved firsts in 2010, it claimed the record for the highest ever Norrington Score.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Societies_and_social_life">Societies and social life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Societies and social life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There is a wide variety of clubs and societies at Magdalen College. The Atkin Society provides support and a variety of social events for all Magdalen law students,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and similarly the subject-specific Sherrington Society is a university-wide community for medicine students, based at Magdalen College, which hosts multiple speakers and aims to encourage debate on medical issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The Florio Society centres on the discussion of members' poetry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and the Magdalen College Trust was set up with the aim of providing charitable help for the disadvantaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Beyond these more long-established societies, Magdalen College further plays host to a broad variety of student interests and activities. The arts are particularly well-catered for with the <a href="/wiki/Magdalen_Players" title="Magdalen Players">Magdalen Players</a> putting on regular dramatic performances<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and the Magdalen College Music Society (MCMS) being one of the most active branches of the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Music_Society" title="Oxford University Music Society">Oxford University Music Society</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> providing an outlet for musicians of all abilities with a range of events in both formal and informal settings - within the society are several singing groups, an orchestra and multiple ensembles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The Magdalen Film Society takes advantage of the College's cinema facilities with the 200 seat Grove Auditorium to show several films a week<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Also to be found at Magdalen is a Pool Society,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> the Magdalen College Wine Tasting Society, and the Magdalen College Quiz Society. The latter has been especially successful, winning the <a href="/wiki/University_Challenge" title="University Challenge">University Challenge</a> on four occasions (1997, 1998, 2004, 2011). At the time of the fourth win, no other institution had won it more than twice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>A college entertainment or "Entz" team is also elected on a yearly basis to organise a variety of inclusive social events for the undergraduate body,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and a Commemoration Ball, also organised primarily by undergraduate students, is held on average every three years.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Magdalen College Boat Club (MCBC) has experienced great success in recent years. Magdalen held the Oxford <a href="/wiki/Eights_Week" title="Eights Week">Head of the River</a> between 2004 and 2007, winning the Summer Eights competition in each of these years. <a href="/wiki/Eights_Week" title="Eights Week">Summer Eights</a> is the most prestigious university regatta held in Oxford. The Men's 1st Torpid became Head of the River in the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Torpids" title="Torpids">Torpids</a>. Having dropped two places in 2009, they regained one place in 2013, which places them second behind <a href="/wiki/Pembroke_College,_Oxford" title="Pembroke College, Oxford">Pembroke</a>. The Women's 1st Torpid became Head of the River in 2010, retaining the title in 2011, 2012 and 2013.</p>
<p>As well as the MCBC, Magdalen College is represented by teams in football, hockey, rugby, netball, cricket, lacrosse and squash, amongst others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The College prides itself on its wide array of facilities which are frequently renovated and updated,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and alumni often provide assistance for the purchase of kit and other necessary expenses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_stamp">College stamp</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magdalen_College,_Oxford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: College stamp">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A college stamp was issued in the 1960s and the 1970s to prepay a local delivery of mail by the college porters. It was short-lived and only a few stamps exist. One on cover is known and is detailed in the Great Britain Philatelic Society Journal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
