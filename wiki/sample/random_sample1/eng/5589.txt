<p>The <b>Moravica District</b> (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>: <span lang="sr">Моравички округ / <i>Moravički okrug</i></span>) is one of eight administrative districts of <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0umadija_and_Western_Serbia" title="Šumadija and Western Serbia">Šumadija and Western Serbia</a>. It is located in the central and south-western parts of <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>. The name Moravica, derivatives from the river of <a href="/wiki/Golijska_Moravica" title="Golijska Moravica">Moravica</a>, which forms the <a href="/wiki/West_Morava" title="West Morava">West Morava</a>, both passing through the district. According to the 2011 census results, it has a population of 212,603 inhabitants. The administrative center of the Moravica district is the city of <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Ca%C4%8Dak" title="Čačak">Čačak</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Society_and_culture">Society and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moravica_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Society and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moravica_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the monuments heritage of Čačak a special place take the religious building: monasteries and churches. The ten monasteries of the Ovčarsko-Kablarska Gorge are referred to as the Serbian Athos. Certain of them were built in the time of the Serb gentry of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nemanji%C4%87" title="Nemanjić">Nemanjić</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lazarevi%C4%87" title="Lazarević">Lazarević</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brankovi%C4%87" title="Branković">Branković</a> dynasties. Over centuries these ten monasteries safeguarded the ancient manuscripts, out of which some were also written there.</p>

<p>There are two faculties located in the Moravica District that are within the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kragujevac" title="University of Kragujevac">University of Kragujevac</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/University_of_Kragujevac_Faculty_of_Agronomy" title="University of Kragujevac Faculty of Agronomy">Faculty of Agronomy</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/University_of_Kragujevac_Faculty_of_Technical_Sciences" title="University of Kragujevac Faculty of Technical Sciences">Faculty of Technical Sciences</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moravica_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Čačak, largest city in the District, is one of the major economic centers of Serbia. Its economy is dominated by industrial production. Above all, by metal-processing (the "Sloboda" and "Cer" factories), paper production (Paper Factory), chemical industry (the "1 May"). It has also well developed agriculture ("Stocar").</p>
<p>The employment rate in the Moravica District is among highest in the country, with 48.1% as of 2014. The unemployment rate of 13.9% is among lowest in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
