<p><b>Morrow County</b> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_counties_in_Oregon" title="List of counties in Oregon">county</a> in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>. As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, the population was 11,173.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-QF_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-QF-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> is <a href="/wiki/Heppner,_Oregon" title="Heppner, Oregon">Heppner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR6_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR6-2">[2]</a></sup> The county is named for one of its first white settlers, Jackson L. Morrow, who was a member of the state legislature when the county was created. Half of the <a href="/wiki/Umatilla_Chemical_Depot" title="Umatilla Chemical Depot">Umatilla Chemical Depot</a>, which includes the Umatilla Chemical Agent Disposal Facility, is within the county.</p>
<p>Morrow County is part of the <a href="/wiki/Pendleton,_Oregon" title="Pendleton, Oregon">Pendleton</a>–<a href="/wiki/Hermiston,_Oregon" title="Hermiston, Oregon">Hermiston</a>, OR, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pendleton-Hermiston_micropolitan_area" title="Pendleton-Hermiston micropolitan area">Micropolitan Statistical Area</a>. It is located on the south side of the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_River" title="Columbia River">Columbia River</a> and is included in the eight-county definition of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Oregon" title="Eastern Oregon">Eastern Oregon</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrow_County,_Oregon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Morrow County was created in 1885 from the western portion of <a href="/wiki/Umatilla_County,_Oregon" title="Umatilla County, Oregon">Umatilla County</a> and a small portion of eastern <a href="/wiki/Wasco_County,_Oregon" title="Wasco County, Oregon">Wasco County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Heppner,_Oregon" title="Heppner, Oregon">Heppner</a> was designated the temporary county seat at the time the county was created and narrowly defeated <a href="/wiki/Lexington,_Oregon" title="Lexington, Oregon">Lexington</a> in the election held in 1887 to determine the permanent county seat.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrow_County,_Oregon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Census_Bureau" title="U.S. Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a>, the county has a total area of 2,049 square miles (5,310 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 2,032 square miles (5,260 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 17 square miles (44 km<sup>2</sup>) (0.8%) is covered by water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_census">2000 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrow_County,_Oregon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2000 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-10">[10]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 10,995 people, 3,776 households, and 2,718 families residing in the county. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 5 people per square mile (2/km²). There were 4,296 housing units at an average density of 2 per square mile (1/km²). The racial makeup of the county was 76.27% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a>, 0.14% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 1.42% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 0.45% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 0.08% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 19.54% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 2.14% from two or more races. 24.43% of the population were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> of any race. 13.6% were of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_people" title="German people">German</a>, 12.8% <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a>, 8.3% <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> and 8.0% <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> ancestry. 77.3% spoke <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and 22.3% <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> as their first language.</p>
<p>There were 3,776 households out of which 38.91% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 62.60% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 8.87% had a female householder with no husband present, and 22.70% were non-families. 18.13% of all households were made up of individuals and 7.40% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.90 and the average family size was 3.28.</p>
<p>In the county, the population was spread out with 30.80% under the age of 18, 8.90% from 18 to 24, 27.30% from 25 to 44, 22.40% from 45 to 64, and 10.60% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 33 years. For every 100 females there were 106.50 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 106.10 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the county was $37,525, and the median income for a family was $40,731. Males had a median income of $32,328 versus $22,887 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the county was $15,843. 14.80% of the population and 11.30% of families are below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>. Out of the total population, 21.60% of those under the age of 18 and 10.1% of those 65 and older were living below the poverty line.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrow_County,_Oregon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a>, there were 11,173 people, 3,916 households, and 2,953 families residing in the county.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census-dp1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-census-dp1-11">[11]</a></sup> The population density was 5.5 inhabitants per square mile (2.1/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 4,442 housing units at an average density of 2.2 per square mile (0.85/km<sup>2</sup>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census-density_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-census-density-12">[12]</a></sup> The racial makeup of the county was 77.7% white, 1.2% American Indian, 0.9% Asian, 0.5% black or African American, 0.1% Pacific islander, 16.9% from other races, and 2.6% from two or more races. Those of Hispanic or Latino origin made up 31.3% of the population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census-dp1_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-census-dp1-11">[11]</a></sup> In terms of ancestry, 19.5% were <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a>, 13.1% were <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a>, 10.7% were <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a>, and 5.0% were <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census-dp2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-census-dp2-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Of the 3,916 households, 37.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 59.7% were married couples living together, 9.6% had a female householder with no husband present, 24.6% were non-families, and 19.1% of all households were made up of individuals. The average household size was 2.85 and the average family size was 3.25. The median age was 36.5 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census-dp1_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-census-dp1-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The median income for a household in the county was $43,902 and the median income for a family was $49,868. Males had a median income of $38,045 versus $30,173 for females. The per capita income for the county was $20,201. About 12.4% of families and 15.3% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 21.7% of those under age 18 and 3.9% of those age 65 or over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census-dp3_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-census-dp3-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrow_County,_Oregon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like all counties in eastern Oregon, the majority of registered voters who are part of a <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in Morrow County are members of the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2008" title="United States presidential election, 2008">2008 presidential election</a> 61.94% of Morrow County voters voted for Republican <a href="/wiki/John_McCain" title="John McCain">John McCain</a>, while 34.62% voted for <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> and 3.44% of voters either voted for a <a href="/wiki/Third_party_(politics)" title="Third party (politics)">Third Party</a> candidate or <a href="/wiki/Write-in_candidate" title="Write-in candidate">wrote in a candidate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> These numbers show a slight shift towards the Democratic candidate when compared to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2004" title="United States presidential election, 2004">2004 presidential election</a>, in which 64.8% of Morrow Country voters voted for <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a>, while 33.8% voted for <a href="/wiki/John_Kerry" title="John Kerry">John Kerry</a>, and 1.4% of voters either voted for a Third Party candidate or wrote in a candidate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrow_County,_Oregon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The principal industries in the county today include agriculture, food processing, lumber, livestock, and recreation. The Columbia River also provides Morrow County with a number of related jobs. A coal-fired generating plant in <a href="/wiki/Boardman,_Oregon" title="Boardman, Oregon">Boardman</a> also employs a significant number of people.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ione_Elevator_(Morrow_County,_Oregon_scenic_images)_(morDA0001a).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="488" data-file-width="324" height="331" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Ione_Elevator_%28Morrow_County%2C_Oregon_scenic_images%29_%28morDA0001a%29.jpg/220px-Ione_Elevator_%28Morrow_County%2C_Oregon_scenic_images%29_%28morDA0001a%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b1/Ione_Elevator_%28Morrow_County%2C_Oregon_scenic_images%29_%28morDA0001a%29.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ione_Elevator_(Morrow_County,_Oregon_scenic_images)_(morDA0001a).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A grain elevator just outside <a href="/wiki/Ione,_Oregon" title="Ione, Oregon">Ione</a></div>
</div>

<p>Early cattlemen found an abundance of <a href="/wiki/Rye" title="Rye">rye</a> along the creek bottoms of the region and drove their herds into the area to forage on these natural pastures. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranching" title="Ranching">Ranching</a> was the primary economic force in the county for many years. Increased settlement, the enclosure of the free grazing lands and diminished pastures due to overgrazing, resulted in the decline of ranching during the 19th century, and farming became predominant. The completion of rail lines into the county in 1883 increased access to markets and encouraged wheat production in the area. The advent of technology for <a href="/wiki/Irrigation#Center_pivot" title="Irrigation">center pivot irrigation</a> has been a further stimulus to the local economy.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Morrow" title="Port of Morrow">Port of Morrow</a>, situated on the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_River" title="Columbia River">Columbia River</a> near the city of Boardman, was established in 1957.</p>
<p>The coal-fired electricity generation plant, the <a href="/wiki/Boardman_Coal_Plant" title="Boardman Coal Plant">Boardman Turbine Coal Plant</a> (601 megawatts), is 14 miles (23 km) southwest of the town of Boardman and is owned by <a href="/wiki/Portland_General_Electric" title="Portland General Electric">Portland General Electric</a> (PGE; 66%-owned and operated), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Idaho_Power_Company" title="Idaho Power Company">Idaho Power</a> (10%), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pacific_Northwest_Generating&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pacific Northwest Generating (page does not exist)">Pacific Northwest Generating</a> (10%), and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=General_Electric_Credit_Corp.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="General Electric Credit Corp. (page does not exist)">General Electric Credit Corp.</a> (16%). There are also two <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>-fired plants located at the Port of Morrow; Coyote Springs I (255-276 MW), owned and operated by Portland General Electric; and Coyote Springs II (241-280 MW; PGE operator) owned by Avista Corp.</p>
