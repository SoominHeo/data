<p>The <b>Moronidae</b> are a family of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perciform" title="Perciform">perciform</a> <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fishes</a>, commonly called the <b>temperate basses</b>, consisting of at least six freshwater, brackish water, and marine species. The members of this family are most commonly found near the coastal regions of eastern <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> (including the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>), northern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nelson_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nelson-2">[2]</a></sup> The family includes the genera <i><a href="/wiki/Morone" title="Morone">Morone</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Dicentrarchus" title="Dicentrarchus">Dicentrarchus</a></i>.</p>
<p>Asian seabasses, <i><a href="/wiki/Lateolabrax" title="Lateolabrax">Lateolabrax</a></i> spp., are sometimes<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nelson_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nelson-2">[2]</a></sup> placed in this family instead of their own family, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lateolabracidae" title="Lateolabracidae">Lateolabracidae</a>; this would increase the species number of this family to eight.</p>
<p>They are highly prized as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sport_fishing" title="Sport fishing">sport fish</a>.</p>
