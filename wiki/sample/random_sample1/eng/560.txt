<p>Year <b>213</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">CCXIII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Friday" title="Common year starting on Friday">common year starting on Friday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Aurelius and Calvinus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 966 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 213 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=213&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=213&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=213&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Caracalla" title="Caracalla">Marcus Aurelius Antoninus Augustus (Caracalla)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Balbinus" title="Balbinus">Decimus Caelius Calvinus Balbinus</a> become <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Consul" title="Roman Consul">Roman Consuls</a>.</li>
<li>Emperor Caracalla leaves <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Rome</a> and expels some German marauders from <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a>. He defends the northern <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine frontier</a> against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alamanni" title="Alamanni">Alamanni</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Chatti" title="Chatti">Chatti</a>. Caracalla wins a victory over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_peoples" title="German peoples">German tribes</a> on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Main_(river)" title="Main (river)">River Main</a>, and gives himself the title "Germanicus". It is probably while campaigning in <a href="/wiki/Germania" title="Germania">Germania</a> that he takes a liking to the <i>caracalla</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celtic</a> or German <a href="/wiki/Tunic" title="Tunic">tunic</a> from which he acquires the name by which he is known.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=213&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cao_Cao" title="Cao Cao">Cao Cao</a>, the prime minister of the <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han dynasty</a>, is titled <i>Wei Gong</i> (Duke of Wei) and given a fief of ten cities under his domain. This later becomes the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Wei" title="Kingdom of Wei">Kingdom of Wei</a>.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
