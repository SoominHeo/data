<p><b>Naoko Mori</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">森 尚子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mori Naoko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born 29 November 1971)</span> is a Japanese-British actress who lives and works mainly in the UK. Mori is best known for her roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Torchwood" title="Torchwood">Torchwood</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Absolutely_Fabulous" title="Absolutely Fabulous">Absolutely Fabulous</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naoko_Mori&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mori was born in <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a>, Japan. When Mori was four years old, she moved to <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, USA due to her father's work. She returned to Japan when she was ten and moved to London two years later. When her parents were posted back to Japan, Mori was given the choice of either moving back to Japan with her parents or remaining in London on her own.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-2">[2]</a></sup> She chose to stay in London, partly because she wanted to finish her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GCSE" title="GCSE">GCSEs</a> and gain some qualifications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-2">[2]</a></sup> She attended the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Russell_School" title="Royal Russell School">Royal Russell School</a>. Mori's father opened a <a href="/wiki/Bank_account" title="Bank account">bank account</a> for her, handed her a cheque book and told her to find a flat or a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bed_sit" title="Bed sit">bed sit</a> for herself to live in. Mori said that being on her own at such a young age helped her to be a very independent person, although it was still a scary world to be faced so young.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naoko_Mori&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Naoko_Mori.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="963" data-file-width="829" height="186" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Naoko_Mori.jpg/160px-Naoko_Mori.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Naoko_Mori.jpg/240px-Naoko_Mori.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Naoko_Mori.jpg/320px-Naoko_Mori.jpg 2x" width="160"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Naoko_Mori.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Mori at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">Comic Con</a> 2008.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>While studying for her A-levels, she auditioned and joined London's West End production of <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Saigon" title="Miss Saigon">Miss Saigon</a></i>. She later went on to play Kim and became the first ever Japanese national to play a lead role in the West End.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-g-casting_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-g-casting-3">[3]</a></sup> From 1993–1994, Mori had a regular role on the hospital drama <i><a href="/wiki/Casualty_(TV_series)" title="Casualty (TV series)">Casualty</a></i> as the hospital receptionist Mie Nishikawa. Film roles followed, including appearances in the 1997 musical comedy movie "Spice World" and <i><a href="/wiki/Topsy-Turvy" title="Topsy-Turvy">Topsy-Turvy</a></i> (1999). Mori also appeared in the television programmes <i><a href="/wiki/Thief_Takers" title="Thief Takers">Thief Takers</a></i> (1997), <i><a href="/wiki/Judge_John_Deed" title="Judge John Deed">Judge John Deed</a></i> (2001), <i><a href="/wiki/Spooks_(TV_series)" title="Spooks (TV series)">Spooks</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Mile_High" title="Mile High">Mile High</a></i> (2003)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mile_high1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-mile_high1-4">[4]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Powers_(UK_TV_series)" title="Powers (UK TV series)">Powers</a> (2004)</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1995, she had a small role as a Japanese computer hacker in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Hackers_(film)" title="Hackers (film)">Hackers</a></i>. Mori had a major role in the 2005 BBC docu-drama <a href="/wiki/Hiroshima:_BBC_History_of_World_War_II" title="Hiroshima: BBC History of World War II">Hiroshima</a>, which contained dramatic re-enactments of the <a href="/wiki/Atomic_bombings_of_Hiroshima_and_Nagasaki" title="Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki">1945 atomic bombing</a>. Mori also provided the <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting" title="Voice acting">voice acting</a> for the villainess Mai Hem for the game <i><a href="/wiki/Perfect_Dark_Zero" title="Perfect Dark Zero">Perfect Dark Zero</a></i>.</p>
<p>Also in 2005, Mori had a small role as Dr Sato in the <i><a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Aliens_of_London" title="Aliens of London">Aliens of London</a>". Producer <a href="/wiki/Russell_T_Davies" title="Russell T Davies">Russell T Davies</a> took note of her performance and decided to bring the character back as a regular in the first two series of the <i>Doctor Who</i> spin-off <i><a href="/wiki/Torchwood" title="Torchwood">Torchwood</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torchwood1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-torchwood1-6">[6]</a></sup> Mori's <i>Torchwood</i> character, <a href="/wiki/Toshiko_Sato" title="Toshiko Sato">Toshiko Sato</a>, was killed in "<a href="/wiki/Exit_Wounds_(Torchwood)" title="Exit Wounds (Torchwood)">Exit Wounds</a>", the final episode of <i>Torchwood'</i>s second series, but has not ruled out the possibility of returning to the show at a later date.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She returned to the West End on 20 November 2006, when she took over the role of Christmas Eve from <a href="/wiki/Ann_Harada" title="Ann Harada">Ann Harada</a> in the London production of <i><a href="/wiki/Avenue_Q" title="Avenue Q">Avenue Q</a></i>, a role which she played until 14 April 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Mori portrayed <a href="/wiki/Yoko_Ono" title="Yoko Ono">Yoko Ono</a> in the BBC Four production <i><a href="/wiki/Lennon_Naked" title="Lennon Naked">Lennon Naked</a></i>, which was broadcast in the UK on 23 Wednesday June 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The film reunited her with <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Eccleston" title="Christopher Eccleston">Christopher Eccleston</a>, who played <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a>, opposite whom she played Dr Sato in her <i>Doctor Who</i> appearance. Before joining <a href="/wiki/Torchwood" title="Torchwood">Torchwood</a>, Mori acted as Kim in <a href="/wiki/Miss_Saigon" title="Miss Saigon">Miss Saigon</a> alongside future co-star <a href="/wiki/John_Barrowman" title="John Barrowman">John Barrowman</a> as Chris.</p>
<p>Mori appeared in the movie <a href="/wiki/Everest_(2015_film)" title="Everest (2015 film)"><i>Everest</i></a> (2015), as <a href="/wiki/Yasuko_Namba" title="Yasuko Namba">Yasuko Namba</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> opposite <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jason_Clarke_(actor)" title="Jason Clarke (actor)">Jason Clarke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Josh_Brolin" title="Josh Brolin">Josh Brolin</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Hawkes_(actor)" title="John Hawkes (actor)">John Hawkes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jake_Gyllenhaal" title="Jake Gyllenhaal">Jake Gyllenhaal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Keira_Knightley" title="Keira Knightley">Keira Knightley</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naoko_Mori&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mori is a <a href="/wiki/Buddhism_in_Japan" title="Buddhism in Japan">Japanese Buddhist</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Mori has an elder brother whom she grew up with alongside her parents in New Jersey. At the young age of 16 or 17 she moved into a flat on her own in London. This was when her career expanded. She wanted to become a singer before she was an actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daemonstv.com_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-daemonstv.com-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
