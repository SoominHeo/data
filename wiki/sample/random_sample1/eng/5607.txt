<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Morinaga &amp; Company, Ltd.</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">森永製菓株式会社</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Morinaga Seika Kabushiki-gaisha</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (<a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">TYO</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://www2.tse.or.jp/tseHpFront/StockSearch.do?callJorEFlg=1&amp;method=&amp;topSearchStr=2201" rel="nofollow">2201</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Confectionery" title="Confectionery">confectionery</a> company in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, Japan, in operation since August 15, 1899. Their products include candy and other confectioneries. Morinaga has <a href="/wiki/Ayumi_Hamasaki" title="Ayumi Hamasaki">Ayumi Hamasaki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mao_Asada" title="Mao Asada">Mao Asada</a> appear in their commercials, and in the past has used stars such as the <a href="/wiki/The_Carpenters" title="The Carpenters">Carpenters</a> to advertise their products.</p>
<p>In 1960, the company advertised that women should give <a href="/wiki/Chocolate" title="Chocolate">chocolates</a> to men on <a href="/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day" title="Valentine's Day">Valentine's Day</a>. This action strongly influenced the present culture of Valentine's Day in Japan. Moreover, in 2009, the company made chocolates for men to give women, which are called <i>Gyaku-choco</i>. (<i>Gyaku</i> means <i>reverse</i> in Japanese.)</p>
