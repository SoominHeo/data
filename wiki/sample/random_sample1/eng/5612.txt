<p><b>Takeshi Morishima</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">森嶋 猛</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Morishima Takeshi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (October 15, 1978) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestler</a>.</p>
<p>Morishima is best known for his work with <a href="/wiki/Pro_Wrestling_Noah" title="Pro Wrestling Noah">Pro Wrestling Noah</a> (Noah) in Japan and has also performed for <a href="/wiki/Ring_of_Honor" title="Ring of Honor">Ring of Honor</a> (ROH) in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in the past. He is a former <a href="/wiki/GHC_Heavyweight_Championship#Title_history" title="GHC Heavyweight Championship">three-time</a> <a href="/wiki/GHC_Heavyweight_Championship" title="GHC Heavyweight Championship">GHC Heavyweight Champion</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_ROH_World_Champions" title="List of ROH World Champions">one-time</a> <a href="/wiki/ROH_World_Championship" title="ROH World Championship">ROH World Champion</a>. Morishima currently holds a position on Noah's <a href="/wiki/Board_of_directors" title="Board of directors">board of directors</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAHdirector_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NOAHdirector-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_wrestling_career">Professional wrestling career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Takeshi_Morishima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional wrestling career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Morishima debuted in <a href="/wiki/All_Japan_Pro_Wrestling" title="All Japan Pro Wrestling">All Japan Pro Wrestling</a> in 1998, but left in 2000 to form <a href="/wiki/Pro_Wrestling_Noah" title="Pro Wrestling Noah">Pro Wrestling Noah</a>, where he became a mainstay. He frequently teamed with <a href="/wiki/Takeshi_Rikio" title="Takeshi Rikio">Takeshi Rikio</a> as Wild II,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WildII_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-WildII-4">[4]</a></sup> and the duo have previously held the <a href="/wiki/GHC_Tag_Team_Championship" title="GHC Tag Team Championship">GHC Tag Team Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GHCLineage_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-GHCLineage-5">[5]</a></sup> Morishima has also wrestled in the United States and United Kingdom. He competed in <a href="/wiki/Harley_Race" title="Harley Race">Harley Race</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_League_Wrestling" title="World League Wrestling">World League Wrestling</a> promotion in 2003, defending the WLW Heavyweight Championship, which he had won from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ron_Harris_(wrestler)" title="Ron Harris (wrestler)">Ron Harris</a> while Harris toured Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WLW_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-WLW-6">[6]</a></sup> Morishima returned to the United States in February 2007, wrestling four matches with Ring of Honor. He lost to <a href="/wiki/Samoa_Joe" title="Samoa Joe">Samoa Joe</a> on February 16, 2007 before defeating <a href="/wiki/Homicide_(wrestler)" title="Homicide (wrestler)">Homicide</a> in a match for the <a href="/wiki/ROH_World_Championship" title="ROH World Championship">ROH World Championship</a> on the subsequent evening, thus becoming the first non-American to hold the title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RoHLineage_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-RoHLineage-8">[8]</a></sup> On February 23, 2007, Morishima defeated <a href="/wiki/B.J._Whitmer" title="B.J. Whitmer">B.J. Whitmer</a> in his first title defense, and on the next night he and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_McGuinness" title="Nigel McGuinness">Nigel McGuinness</a> defeated Samoa Joe and Homicide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup> On March 4, 2007, he successfully defended the ROH Championship in Japan, defeating <a href="/wiki/Kenta_Kobayashi" title="Kenta Kobayashi">Kenta</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Budokan" title="Nippon Budokan">Nippon Budokan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup> Morishima could not return to the United States until almost a month and a half later when he and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hero" title="Chris Hero">Chris Hero</a> took on Nigel McGuinness and <a href="/wiki/Doug_Williams_(wrestler)" title="Doug Williams (wrestler)">Doug Williams</a>, and the next night when he successfully defended the belt against McGuinness for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 21, Morishima was honored at the 42nd annual Cauliflower Alley Club banquet, being presented with the "Future Legend" award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Back in ROH, Morishima went on a string of successful title defenses, defeating the likes of <a href="/wiki/Austin_Aries" title="Austin Aries">Austin Aries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shingo_Takagi" title="Shingo Takagi">Shingo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Briscoe" title="Jay Briscoe">Jay Briscoe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Roderick_Strong" title="Roderick Strong">Roderick Strong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup> Morishima also competed twice on ROH's first <a href="/wiki/Pay-per-view" title="Pay-per-view">pay-per-view</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ROH_Respect_Is_Earned" title="ROH Respect Is Earned">Respect Is Earned</a></i>, defeating <a href="/wiki/B.J._Whitmer" title="B.J. Whitmer">BJ Whitmer</a> to retain the ROH Championship, and later teaming with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Bryan" title="Daniel Bryan">Bryan Danielson</a> to defeat <a href="/wiki/Nigel_McGuinness" title="Nigel McGuinness">Nigel McGuinness</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kenta_Kobayashi" title="Kenta Kobayashi">Kenta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup> At the second pay-per-view, <i><a href="/wiki/ROH_Driven" title="ROH Driven">Driven</a></i>, Morishima again competed twice, retaining the title against <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Rave" title="Jimmy Rave">Jimmy Rave</a> and later <a href="/wiki/Adam_Pearce" title="Adam Pearce">Adam Pearce</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup> On July 16, 2007, Morishima wrestled in the main event of Ring of Honor's debut show in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and defeated <a href="/wiki/Nigel_McGuinness" title="Nigel McGuinness">Nigel McGuinness</a> to retain the ROH Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Morishima lost the title to McGuinness at <i><a href="/wiki/ROH_Undeniable" title="ROH Undeniable">Undeniable</a></i> on October 6.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-7"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 2, 2008, Morishima defeated <a href="/wiki/Mitsuharu_Misawa" title="Mitsuharu Misawa">Mitsuharu Misawa</a> at the Budokan Hall Event of Pro Wrestling Noah's Second Navigation Tour to become the <a href="/wiki/GHC_Heavyweight_Championship#Title_history" title="GHC Heavyweight Championship">12th</a> <a href="/wiki/GHC_Heavyweight_Championship" title="GHC Heavyweight Championship">GHC Heavyweight Champion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-8"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup> Morishima has stated that while he plans on returning to ROH in the future, he has no intentions in regaining the ROH Championship, as he will be fully focused on Noah's heavyweight scene. On June 14, 2008, Morishima made his very first defense of the GHC Heavyweight Title against <a href="/wiki/Takashi_Sugiura" title="Takashi Sugiura">Takashi Sugiura</a>. Morishima prevailed after hitting a <a href="/wiki/Suplex#High_angle_belly_to_back_suplex" title="Suplex">backdrop driver</a>. Morishima's second defense happened on July 18, 2008 against former his tag team partner <a href="/wiki/Takeshi_Rikio" title="Takeshi Rikio">Takeshi Rikio</a>. After about 13 and a half minutes, Morishima defeated Rikio with a backdrop suplex. On September 6, 2008 at the Tokyo Nippon Budokan, Morishima lost to <a href="/wiki/Kensuke_Sasaki" title="Kensuke Sasaki">Kensuke Sasaki</a>, who gained a pin at the 22-minute mark with the Northern Lights Bomb. In August 2008 Morishima had a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Dark_match" title="List of professional wrestling terms">dark match</a> tryout against <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Haas" title="Charlie Haas">Charlie Haas</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">WWE</a> before a live <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw</a></i> in Chicago, defeating Haas in 3 minutes with a missile dropkick.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-9"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup> He then beat <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Noble" title="Jamie Noble">Jamie Noble</a> the following night in another dark match, this time for <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-10"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup> On December 7, 2008, Morishima faced <a href="/wiki/Jun_Akiyama" title="Jun Akiyama">Jun Akiyama</a> for the number one contender's spot for the GHC Heavyweight Championship. He lost to Akiyama, then traveled back to the United States to face Bryan Danielson one last time for ROH's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Final_Battle_2008" title="Final Battle 2008">Final Battle 2008</a>. He lost the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fight_Without_Honor" title="Fight Without Honor">Fight Without Honor</a>" to Danielson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OWOW_7-11"><a href="#cite_note-OWOW-7">[7]</a></sup> On September 21, 2009 in Nagoya, Japan, Morishima and Sasaki defeated <a href="/wiki/Akitoshi_Saito" title="Akitoshi Saito">Akitoshi Saito</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bison_Smith" title="Bison Smith">Bison Smith</a> for the GHC Tag Titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PuroCentral_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-PuroCentral-12">[12]</a></sup> However, on December 6, at the last show of Winter Navigation '09, Morishima and Sasaki lost the titles to Takeshi Rikio and <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Yone" title="Muhammad Yone">Muhammad Yone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PuroCentral_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-PuroCentral-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Through Noah's working relationship with <a href="/wiki/Asistencia_Asesor%C3%ADa_y_Administraci%C3%B3n" title="Asistencia Asesoría y Administración">Asistencia Asesoría y Administración</a> (AAA), Morishima has made several trips to Mexico. On March 19, 2010, Morishima teamed up with <a href="/wiki/Taiji_Ishimori" title="Taiji Ishimori">Taiji Ishimori</a> to defeat <i>La Hermandad 187</i> ("The Brotherhood of 187"; <a href="/wiki/Psicosis" title="Psicosis">Nicho el Millonario</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joe_Lider" title="Joe Lider">Joe Lider</a>) to win the <a href="/wiki/AAA_World_Tag_Team_Championship" title="AAA World Tag Team Championship">AAA World Tag Team Championship</a>, representing the AAA heel faction <i><a href="/wiki/La_Legi%C3%B3n_Extranjera" title="La Legión Extranjera">La Legión Extranjera</a></i> ("The Foreign Legion").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAATag5_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-AAATag5-13">[13]</a></sup> On May 23, 2010, Morishima and Ishimori lost the AAA World Tag Team Championship to the team of <a href="/wiki/Atsushi_Aoki" title="Atsushi Aoki">Atsushi Aoki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Go_Shiozaki" title="Go Shiozaki">Go Shiozaki</a> during Pro Wrestling Noah's NOAH Navigation With Breeze - Day 1 show in <a href="/wiki/Niigata,_Niigata" title="Niigata, Niigata">Niigata, Niigata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TagNr06_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-TagNr06-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Morishima was recently on this TV Drama in Japan, "Hesitation I Come" during his time off due to a foot injury. However, on November 23, during (Noah Winter Navigation 2010 - Day 4), Morishima returned to Noah teaming with Kensuke Sasaki and defeating Shuhei Taniguchi and Takashi Sugiura. On January 22, 2012, Morishima defeated Go Shiozaki to win the GHC Heavyweight Championship for the second time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Since this victory, Morishima has defended against Naomichi Marufuji, Maybach Taniguchi, Go Shiozaki, Akitoshi Saito, Kenta, Jun Akiyama, and Kohei Sato. After a year-long reign, Morishima lost the GHC Heavyweight Championship to Kenta on January 27, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On January 5, 2014, Morishima defeated Kenta to win the GHC Heavyweight Championship for the third time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Post-match, Morishima quit <a href="/wiki/Brave_(professional_wrestling)" title="Brave (professional wrestling)">Brave</a> to form a new group with <a href="/wiki/Kenoh" title="Kenoh">Kenoh</a> and Maybach Taniguchi, later named <a href="/wiki/Choukibou-gun" title="Choukibou-gun">Choukibou-gun</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Choukibougun_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Choukibougun-18">[18]</a></sup> turning heel in the process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On January 25, Morishima and Taniguchi defeated <a href="/wiki/The_Mighty_Don%27t_Kneel" title="The Mighty Don't Kneel">TMDK</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mikey_Nicholls" title="Mikey Nicholls">Mikey Nicholls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shane_Haste" title="Shane Haste">Shane Haste</a>), with help from Kenoh and new stablemate <a href="/wiki/Hajime_Ohara" title="Hajime Ohara">Hajime Ohara</a>, to win the GHC Tag Team Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Choukibougun_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Choukibougun-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On February 8, Morishima lost the GHC Heavyweight Championship to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Japan_Pro_Wrestling" title="New Japan Pro Wrestling">New Japan Pro Wrestling</a> representative <a href="/wiki/Yuji_Nagata" title="Yuji Nagata">Yuji Nagata</a> in his first title defense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On May 31, Morishima and Taniguchi lost the GHC Tag Team Championship to <a href="/wiki/Masato_Tanaka" title="Masato Tanaka">Masato Tanaka</a> and Takashi Sugiura.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2015, Morishima was forced to pull out of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Tag_League#2015" title="Global Tag League">2015 Global Tag League</a> due to an injury. On April 21, Noah announced that a blood test had revealed that Morishima had an abnormally high <a href="/wiki/Glycated_hemoglobin" title="Glycated hemoglobin">glycated hemoglobin</a>, suggesting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diabetes" title="Diabetes">diabetes</a>. Following his doctor's recommendation, Morishima decided to immediately retire from professional wrestling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Retirement_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Retirement-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> On December 28, 2015, Noah canceled Morishima's retirement ceremony, announcing that his contract with the promotion would expire at the end of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
