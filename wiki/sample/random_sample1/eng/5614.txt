<p><b>Miho Morikawa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">森川美穂</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Morikawa Miho</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born May 5, 1968, <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>)</span> is a Japanese singer and model.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miho_Morikawa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Miho Morikawa got her start at the age of seventeen after winning a singing contest, which led to her first album, called <i>Sentimental Times,</i> in 1986. Soon after her debut, she started writing lyrics and eventually composing some of the songs as well. On her EP <i>Holiday,</i> Miho played the blues harp in some songs. Her 1992 album, <i>Freestyle</i>, debuted at No.10 on the Japanese Album Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She has done songs for several <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series; including “Blue Water” and “Yes I Will” from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fushigi_no_Umi_no_Nadia" title="Fushigi no Umi no Nadia">Fushigi no Umi no Nadia</a></i>, “Positive” from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranma_1/2" title="Ranma 1/2">Ranma 1/2</a>,</i> and “By Yourself” from a <i><a href="/wiki/Dirty_Pair" title="Dirty Pair">Dirty Pair</a></i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Original_animated_video" title="Original animated video">OAV</a> and "Yahoo!" the second opening of <i><a href="/wiki/Kenichi:_The_Mightiest_Disciple" title="Kenichi: The Mightiest Disciple">Kenichi: The Mightiest Disciple</a></i> with Akira Asakura under the group name <i>Diva x Diva</i>. Her most recent album was <i>Glad</i> (2010).</p>
