<p><b>Moritake Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">森岳駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Moritake-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">JR East</a> railway station located in <a href="/wiki/Mitane,_Akita" title="Mitane, Akita">Mitane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yamamoto_District,_Akita" title="Yamamoto District, Akita">Yamamoto District</a> <a href="/wiki/Akita_Prefecture" title="Akita Prefecture">Akita Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moritake_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moritake Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cu_Main_Line" title="Ōu Main Line">Ōu Main Line</a>, and is located 345.1 km from the terminus of the line at <a href="/wiki/Fukushima_Station_(Fukushima)" title="Fukushima Station (Fukushima)">Fukushima Station</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moritake_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moritake Station consists of two opposed <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platforms</a> connected by an overpass. The station is unattended. Moritake Station is a simple consignment station, administered by <a href="/wiki/Higashi-Noshiro_Station" title="Higashi-Noshiro Station">Higashi-Noshiro Station</a>, and operated by <a href="/wiki/Mitane,_Akita" title="Mitane, Akita">Mitane</a> city authority, with point-of-sales terminal installed. Ordinary tickets, express tickets, and reserved-seat tickets for all JR lines are on sale (no connecting tickets).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moritake_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moritake Station was opened on August 1, 1902 as a station on the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Government_Railways" title="Japanese Government Railways">Japanese Government Railways</a> (JGR), serving the village of Moritake, Akita. The JGR became the JNR (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_National_Railway" title="Japan National Railway">Japan National Railway</a>) after World War II. The station was absorbed into the JR East network upon the <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of the JNR on April 1, 1987.</p>
