<p><b>MOMO Srl</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Design_company" title="Design company">design company</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, Italy that makes accessories and parts for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobiles" title="Automobiles">automobiles</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_history">Company history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momo_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Company history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MOMO was founded by <a href="/wiki/Gentleman" title="Gentleman">gentleman</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_car_driver" title="Race car driver">racer</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gianpiero_Moretti" title="Gianpiero Moretti">Gianpiero Moretti</a> in 1964.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autocarparts.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-autocarparts.com-1">[1]</a></sup> MOMO are the initials for <i>Moretti-Monza</i>. <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a> is a town in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Milan" title="Province of Milan">Province of Milan</a>.</p>
<p>In 1969 MOMO opened production plant in <a href="/wiki/Tregnago" title="Tregnago">Tregnago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Verona" title="Province of Verona">Province of Verona</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autocarparts.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-autocarparts.com-1">[1]</a></sup> Due to increasing <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> federal government safety regulations and the wide adoption of <a href="/wiki/Airbag" title="Airbag">airbags</a> in passenger vehicles internationally, MOMO was sold in 1996 to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Breed_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Breed Technologies (page does not exist)">Breed Technologies</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Steering_wheel" title="Steering wheel">steering wheel</a> airbag manufacturer. Breed Technologies was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Private_equity_firm" title="Private equity firm">private equity firm</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carlyle_Management_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carlyle Management Group (page does not exist)">Carlyle Management Group</a> and merged with other <a href="/wiki/Original_equipment_manufacturer" title="Original equipment manufacturer">OEM</a> automotive businesses they owned to form <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Key_Safety_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Key Safety Systems (page does not exist)">Key Safety Systems</a> in 2003. Key Safety Systems sold the MOMO brand name to an Italian private equity group in 2006, but most of the manufacturing plants and technologies previously owned by MOMO were retained by Key Safety Systems.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momo_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MOMO's product range includes consumer accessories such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steering_wheels" title="Steering wheels">steering wheels</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gear_shift_knobs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gear shift knobs (page does not exist)">gear shift knobs</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alloy_wheels" title="Alloy wheels">alloy wheels</a>, through to race equipment such as racing suits and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Helmets" title="Helmets">helmets</a>. MOMO airbagged steering wheels have become standard accessories on some regional <a href="/wiki/Subaru" title="Subaru">Subaru</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Motors" title="Mitsubishi Motors">Mitsubishi</a> performance cars. MOMO steering wheels were standard features in the <a href="/wiki/UMM_Alter_II" title="UMM Alter II">UMM Alter II</a> from 1987 to 1991. MOMO has also designed a steering wheel for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_peripheral" title="Computer peripheral">computer peripheral</a> company <a href="/wiki/Logitech" title="Logitech">Logitech</a> (called the Logitech MOMO), to be used as a video game controller. Thomas Superwheel controllers (a brand of high-quality, premium priced controllers) also feature the MOMO name. Also watches, eyewear, fragrances, clothing, accessories, etc. are made by brand MOMO DESIGN.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Motorsport_involvement">Motorsport involvement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Momo_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Motorsport involvement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GianpieroMoretti.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="463" data-file-width="800" height="127" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/GianpieroMoretti.jpg/220px-GianpieroMoretti.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/GianpieroMoretti.jpg/330px-GianpieroMoretti.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/GianpieroMoretti.jpg/440px-GianpieroMoretti.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:GianpieroMoretti.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Momo founder Gianpiero Moretti at the wheel of an Alba AR3 at <a href="/wiki/Mazda_Raceway_Laguna_Seca" title="Mazda Raceway Laguna Seca">Laguna Seca</a> in 1984.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The company sponsored many prototype cars in the <a href="/wiki/International_Motor_Sports_Association" title="International Motor Sports Association">IMSA</a> <a href="/wiki/IMSA_GT_Championship" title="IMSA GT Championship">WSC series</a> including the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_333_SP" title="Ferrari 333 SP">Ferrari 333 SP</a> sports prototype car, after <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> had retired from that racing category 20 years earlier.</p>
<p>MOMO was the official steering wheel of the <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" title="Champ Car">Champ Car</a> series from 2004 to 2007, before Champ Car merged to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indy_Racing_League" title="Indy Racing League">Indy Racing League</a>. MOMO are also suppliers of the official steering wheel, racing seats, clothing and racing accessories of the <a href="/wiki/Speedcar_Series" title="Speedcar Series">Speedcar Series</a>.</p>
<p>MOMO also sponsors <a href="/wiki/Lira_Motorsports" title="Lira Motorsports">Lira Motorsports</a> in the <a href="/wiki/ARCA_Racing_Series" title="ARCA Racing Series">ARCA Racing Series</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Camping_World_Truck_Series" title="Camping World Truck Series">Camping World Truck Series</a>.</p>
