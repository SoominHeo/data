<p><i><b>Mobage</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">モバゲー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mobagē</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "Mobile Game")</span> is a portal and social network for games, owned by <a href="/wiki/DeNA" title="DeNA">DeNA</a>. The service has 30 million users, who largely play on <a href="/wiki/Feature_phone" title="Feature phone">feature phones</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DeNA_and_ngmoco_launch_Mobage_global_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-DeNA_and_ngmoco_launch_Mobage_global-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>On February 14, 2011, DeNA announced renaming Mobage Town service name to Mobage, which also merged the <a href="/wiki/Plus%2B" title="Plus+">Plus+</a> network operated by <a href="/wiki/Ngmoco" title="Ngmoco">ngmoco</a> under the new branding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 19, 2014, ngmoco, LLC announced the renaming of Mobage to DeNA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> However, the Japanese site still operates under the mobage name.</p>
