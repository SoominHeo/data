<p>The <b>Mobile Revelers</b> were a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Development_League" title="National Basketball Development League">National Basketball Development League</a> (NBDL) team based in <a href="/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">Mobile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>. Playing their home games at the <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Civic_Center" title="Mobile Civic Center">Mobile Civic Center</a>, the Revelers was a charter franchise in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%932002_NBDL_season" title="2001–2002 NBDL season">2001-02 season</a> and folded after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%932003_NBDL_season" title="2002–2003 NBDL season">2002-03 season</a>. The team was named after the people who took part in <a href="/wiki/Mardi_Gras" title="Mardi Gras">Mardi Gras</a> parades as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mardi_Gras_in_Mobile" title="Mardi Gras in Mobile">Mardi Gras tradition started in Mobile</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA) announced the Revelers as one of the NBDL charter franchises in July 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2003 the Revelers won the League championship, defeating the <a href="/wiki/Fayetteville_Patriots" title="Fayetteville Patriots">Fayetteville Patriots</a>, two games to one. However the league contracted the franchise in June 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
