<p>The <b>Mobile Mysticks</b> were a professional <a href="/wiki/Minor_league" title="Minor league">minor league</a> <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">Mobile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a> that played in the <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a>. They played their home games at the <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Civic_Center" title="Mobile Civic Center">Mobile Civic Center</a>. The team was founded in 1995 as an expansion franchise, joining the ECHL alongside the <a href="/wiki/Louisville_RiverFrogs" title="Louisville RiverFrogs">Louisville RiverFrogs</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louisiana_IceGators" title="Louisiana IceGators">Louisiana IceGators</a>. The Mysticks owed their name to the prevalence of <a href="/wiki/Mystic_society" title="Mystic society">mystic societies</a>—social organizations responsible for throwing parades and balls during the <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnival</a> season—that existed in Mobile since the early 18th century.</p>
<p>The Mysticks qualified for the <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Cup" title="Kelly Cup">Kelly Cup</a> playoffs for five out of their seven seasons, but never advanced past the second round. In 2002, the franchise suspended operations and moved to <a href="/wiki/Duluth,_Georgia" title="Duluth, Georgia">Duluth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_ECHL_season" title="2003–04 ECHL season">2003–04 season</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Franchise_history">Franchise history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mobile_Mysticks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Franchise history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Mobile Mysticks began play in the <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a> as an expansion franchise for the <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_ECHL_season" title="1995–96 ECHL season">1995–96 season</a> under head coach and former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NHL" title="NHL">NHL</a> player <a href="/wiki/Ed_Johnstone" title="Ed Johnstone">Eddie Johnstone</a>. During this time, they served as a minor league affiliate for the NHL's <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Flyers" title="Philadelphia Flyers">Philadelphia Flyers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">AHL</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hershey_Bears" title="Hershey Bears">Hershey Bears</a>.</p>
<p>Following a rough start (the Mysticks finished seventh in their division and did not qualify for the playoffs their inaugural year; the following year they finished fourth in their division and were eliminated from the playoffs in a first-round sweep by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louisiana_IceGators" title="Louisiana IceGators">Louisiana IceGators</a>) the Mysticks decided against renewing Johnstone's contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Johnstone_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Johnstone-1">[1]</a></sup> His replacement, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Matt_Shaw_(ice_hockey)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Matt Shaw (ice hockey) (page does not exist)">Matt Shaw</a>, led Mobile to a fifth-place divisional finish and another first-round loss, again in a three-game sweep by the IceGators. Shaw did not return to the Mysticks for the following season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shaw_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shaw-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jeff_Pyle_(ice_hockey)" title="Jeff Pyle (ice hockey)">Jeff Pyle</a> joined the Mysticks as head coach for the <a href="/wiki/1998%E2%80%9399_ECHL_season" title="1998–99 ECHL season">1998–99 season</a>. With Pyle at the helm, the team saw postseason action for the next three seasons—losing in the 1999 preliminary round to the <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_Bulls_(ECHL)" title="Birmingham Bulls (ECHL)">Birmingham Bulls</a>; falling in the first round of the 2000 playoffs to the <a href="/wiki/Greenville_Grrrowl" title="Greenville Grrrowl">Greenville Grrrowl</a>; and in their most successful playoff bid, falling in double overtime in the second round to the <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina_Stingrays" title="South Carolina Stingrays">South Carolina Stingrays</a> (the eventual Kelly Cup champions) in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pyle_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pyle-3">[3]</a></sup> The Mysticks failed to qualify for the playoffs in 2002, and the franchise suspended operations, eventually moving to <a href="/wiki/Duluth,_Georgia" title="Duluth, Georgia">Duluth, Georgia</a> in 2003 to become the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Gladiators" title="Atlanta Gladiators">Gwinnett Gladiators</a> (branded as the Atlanta Gladiators since the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_ECHL_season" title="2015–16 ECHL season">2015–16 ECHL season</a>).</p>
