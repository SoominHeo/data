<p><i><b>21 Jump Street</b></i> is an American <a href="/wiki/Police_procedural" title="Police procedural">police procedural</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> that aired on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> network and in first run syndication from April 12, 1987, to April 27, 1991, with a total of 103 <a href="/wiki/Episode" title="Episode">episodes</a>. The series focuses on a squad of youthful-looking undercover police officers investigating crimes in high schools, colleges, and other teenage venues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was originally going to be titled <i>Jump Street Chapel</i>, after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deconsecrated" title="Deconsecrated">deconsecrated</a> church building in which the unit has its headquarters, but was changed at Fox's request so as not to mislead viewers into thinking it was a religious program.</p>
<p>Created by <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Hasburgh" title="Patrick Hasburgh">Patrick Hasburgh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_J._Cannell" title="Stephen J. Cannell">Stephen J. Cannell</a>, the series was produced by Patrick Hasburgh Productions and Stephen J. Cannell Productions in association with <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox_Television" title="20th Century Fox Television">20th Century Fox Television</a>. Executive Producers included Hasburgh, Cannell, Steve Beers and <a href="/wiki/Bill_Nuss" title="Bill Nuss">Bill Nuss</a>. The show was an early hit for the fledgling Fox network, and was created to attract a younger audience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The final season aired in first-run <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_syndication" title="Television syndication">syndication</a> mainly on local Fox affiliates. It was later rerun on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FX_(TV_network)" title="FX (TV network)">FX</a> cable network from 1996 to 1998.</p>
<p>The series provided a spark to <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Depp" title="Johnny Depp">Johnny Depp</a>'s nascent acting career, garnering him national recognition as a teen idol. Depp found this status irritating,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-depp-bio_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-depp-bio-3">[3]</a></sup> but he continued on the series under his contract and was paid $45,000 per episode. Eventually he was released from his contract after the fourth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-depp-bio_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-depp-bio-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Spin-off_(media)" title="Spin-off (media)">spin-off</a> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Booker_(TV_series)" title="Booker (TV series)">Booker</a></i>, was produced for the character of Dennis Booker (<a href="/wiki/Richard_Grieco" title="Richard Grieco">Richard Grieco</a>); it ran one season, from September 1989 to June 1990. A <a href="/wiki/21_Jump_Street_(film)" title="21 Jump Street (film)">film adaptation</a> directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phil_Lord_and_Chris_Miller" title="Phil Lord and Chris Miller">Phil Lord and Chris Miller</a> was released on March 16, 2012. The film is set in the same chronology as the series, with Johnny Depp, <a href="/wiki/Holly_Robinson_Peete" title="Holly Robinson Peete">Holly Robinson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_DeLuise" title="Peter DeLuise">Peter DeLuise</a> reprising their characters in <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo appearances</a>. Richard Grieco and <a href="/wiki/Dustin_Nguyen" title="Dustin Nguyen">Dustin Nguyen</a> also have cameos in the 2014 film sequel <i><a href="/wiki/22_Jump_Street" title="22 Jump Street">22 Jump Street</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Premise">Premise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=21_Jump_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Premise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_21_Jump_Street_episodes" title="List of 21 Jump Street episodes">List of 21 Jump Street episodes</a></div>
<p>The series focuses on a group of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Police_officers" title="Police officers">police officers</a> headquartered at the eponymous address. These officers are all young and have especially youthful appearances, which allows them to pass for teenagers. Their assignments generally consist of undercover work in high schools or, less commonly, colleges, where they generally investigate drug trafficking and abuse. The show's plots cover issues such as alcoholism, hate crimes, drug abuse, <a href="/wiki/Homophobia" title="Homophobia">homophobia</a>, AIDS, <a href="/wiki/Child_abuse" title="Child abuse">child abuse</a> and sexual promiscuity. Similarly, each problem is often solved by the end of the hour-long episode, giving an implicit <a href="/wiki/Moral" title="Moral">moral</a> about the impact of a particular activity. When the show originally aired, some episodes were followed immediately by <a href="/wiki/Public_service_announcement" title="Public service announcement">public service announcements</a> featuring cast members.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=21_Jump_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The show starred prominent actors and actresses at the time, including:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Johnny_Depp" title="Johnny Depp">Johnny Depp</a> as Patrolman/Officer Tom Hanson (1987–1990)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Holly_Robinson_Peete" title="Holly Robinson Peete">Holly Robinson</a> as Sergeant Judith "Judy" Hoffs (1987–1991)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peter_DeLuise" title="Peter DeLuise">Peter DeLuise</a> as Officer Douglas "Doug" Penhall (1987–1990)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dustin_Nguyen" title="Dustin Nguyen">Dustin Nguyen</a> as Sergeant Harry Truman Ioki/Vinh Van Tran (H.T. Ioki) (1987–1990)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Frederic_Forrest" title="Frederic Forrest">Frederic Forrest</a> as Captain Richard Jenko (season 1, first 6 episodes)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Steven_Williams" title="Steven Williams">Steven Williams</a> as Captain Adam Fuller (1987–1991)</li>
<li>Sal Jenco as Sal "Blowfish" Banducci (1987–1990)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Richard_Grieco" title="Richard Grieco">Richard Grieco</a> as Detective Dennis Booker (1988–1989)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yvette_Nipar" title="Yvette Nipar">Yvette Nipar</a> as Jackie Garrett (1988-1989)</li>
<li>David Barry Gray as Officer Dean Garrett (1990)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alexandra_Powers" title="Alexandra Powers">Alexandra Powers</a> as Officer Kati Rocky (1990)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Michael_DeLuise" title="Michael DeLuise">Michael DeLuise</a> as Officer Joseph "Joey" Penhall (1990–1991)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Michael_Bendetti" title="Michael Bendetti">Michael Bendetti</a> as Officer Anthony "Mac" McCann (1990–1991)</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jeff_Yagher" title="Jeff Yagher">Jeff Yagher</a> was originally cast as Officer Tom Hanson in the pilot. He was replaced after the original pilot episode was filmed, and his scenes were reshot with Johnny Depp. Midway through the first season, Frederic Forrest was replaced by Steven Williams. On the show, Forrest's character Richard Jenko is killed by a drunk driver. <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Depp" title="Johnny Depp">Johnny Depp</a> left the series at the end of the fourth season, but was credited in the first two episodes of the fifth season, despite not having appeared.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=21_Jump_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=21_Jump_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Set in a fictional city and state (Metropolis, Evergreen State) in the United States, the series was primarily filmed in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>. It was one of the first major television series to use Vancouver as a filming location and helped to establish the city as a center for film production.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=21_Jump_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>21 Jump Street</i>, along with <i><a href="/wiki/Married..._with_Children" title="Married... with Children">Married... with Children</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tracey_Ullman_Show" title="The Tracey Ullman Show">The Tracey Ullman Show</a></i>, debuted in the spring of 1987. All three shows were hits with audiences and helped to establish the then-newly launched Fox network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>21 Jump Street</i> was the first hit series for the Fox network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In August 1987, <i>21 Jump Street</i> became the first Fox series to win its timeslot against a <a href="/wiki/Big_Three_television_networks" title="Big Three television networks">Big three</a> network series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the United Kingdom, it was shown on the similarly nascent, and interrelated, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky_One" title="Sky One">Sky One</a>, which had yet to reach a sizeable audience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>Season 4 was the last season to air on the Fox network. In commentary on the Season 5 DVD set, <a href="/wiki/Peter_DeLuise" title="Peter DeLuise">Peter DeLuise</a> said that Fox had decided to cancel the show after Season 4 because the ratings had fallen below a set limit. Following this season Johnny Depp and Dustin Nguyen left the show. The departure of their characters was never explained in the narrative of the TV series, but in <a href="/wiki/21_Jump_Street_(film)" title="21 Jump Street (film)">the movie</a> it is explained that Hanson was transferred to the DEA and was later joined by Officer Penhall. The <i>Booker</i> spin-off crossover episode, "Wheels and Deals Part One", is included with <i>21 Jump Street'</i>s syndication package, and is also included on the fourth season DVD set.</p>
<p>Officer Dean Garrett (David Barry Gray) makes his first appearance in "Everyday is Christmas". As it became harder for the original cast members to plausibly pass as high school students, his character and Officer Kati Rocky (<a href="/wiki/Alexandra_Powers" title="Alexandra Powers">Alexandra Powers</a>) were intended to be "youthful" replacements, so the show could maintain its original premise of younger looking cops posing as high school students.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="DVD_releases">DVD releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=21_Jump_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: DVD releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Anchor_Bay_Entertainment" title="Anchor Bay Entertainment">Anchor Bay Entertainment</a> released all five seasons of <i>21 Jump Street</i> on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Region_1" title="Region 1">Region 1</a> between 2004 and 2006. The releases contain extensive music substitutions and inaccurate cover art (for example, Richard Grieco appears on the cover of the season 4 set, despite appearing in only two episodes that season, while Johnny Depp appears on the season 5 cover, despite having left the series after the previous season). As of 2010, these releases have been discontinued and are now out of print.</p>
<p>On October 14, 2009, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Mill_Creek_Entertainment" title="Mill Creek Entertainment">Mill Creek Entertainment</a> had acquired the rights to several <a href="/wiki/Stephen_J._Cannell" title="Stephen J. Cannell">Stephen J. Cannell</a> series, including <i>21 Jump Street</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and subsequently re-released the first four seasons. In addition, Mill Creek also released <i>21 Jump Street - The Complete Series</i>, an 18-disc collection featuring all 103 episodes of the series on DVD on July 27, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Region_2" title="Region 2">Region 2</a>, Anchor Bay Entertainment has released all 5 seasons on DVD in the UK. They also released a complete series set on March 5, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Region_4" title="Region 4">Region 4</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madman_Entertainment" title="Madman Entertainment">Madman Entertainment</a> has released all five seasons on DVD in Australia. They also released a complete series collection on May 1, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_adaptation">Film adaptation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=21_Jump_Street&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Film adaptation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/21_Jump_Street_(film)" title="21 Jump Street (film)">21 Jump Street (film)</a></div>
<p>On March 16, 2012, a feature film sequel to the television series from <a href="/wiki/Sony_Pictures" title="Sony Pictures">Sony Pictures</a> was released starring <a href="/wiki/Jonah_Hill" title="Jonah Hill">Jonah Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Channing_Tatum" title="Channing Tatum">Channing Tatum</a> (who are also executive producers) and directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phil_Lord_and_Chris_Miller" title="Phil Lord and Chris Miller">Phil Lord and Chris Miller</a> with the screenplay written by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bacall" title="Michael Bacall">Michael Bacall</a> from a story by both Hill and Bacall. Johnny Depp, Peter DeLuise, and Holly Robinson briefly reprise their roles as Tom Hanson, Doug Penhall, and Judy Hoffs, respectively.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The film proved to be a critically hailed box office success.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Described in concept by Hill as an "R-rated, insane, <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_Boys_(1995_film)" title="Bad Boys (1995 film)">Bad Boys</a></i>-meets-<a href="/wiki/John_Hughes_(filmmaker)" title="John Hughes (filmmaker)">John Hughes</a>-type movie,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> the film departs from the dramatic style of the series and instead features a comedic tone. The film and series are also set in the same continuity. A sequel titled <i><a href="/wiki/22_Jump_Street" title="22 Jump Street">22 Jump Street</a></i> was released in 2014.</p>
