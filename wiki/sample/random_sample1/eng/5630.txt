<p>Most of Echo of Moscow's content consists of news and talk shows focusing on social and political issues, where the station tries to represent different points of view. The current<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Relative_time_references" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="Clarify the applicable timeframe so that it is unambiguous when read at a later date.">timeframe?</span></a></i>]</sup> senior editor is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexey_Venediktov" title="Alexey Venediktov">Alexey Venediktov</a>. Radio hosts of the station include <a href="/wiki/Victor_Shenderovich" title="Victor Shenderovich">Victor Shenderovich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yulia_Latynina" title="Yulia Latynina">Yulia Latynina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Parkhomenko" title="Sergey Parkhomenko">Sergey Parkhomenko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yevgenia_Albats" title="Yevgenia Albats">Yevgenia Albats</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vladimir_Kara-Murza" title="Vladimir Kara-Murza">Vladimir Kara-Murza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Ryzhkov" title="Vladimir Ryzhkov">Vladimir Ryzhkov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Gaidar" title="Maria Gaidar">Maria Gaidar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yevgeny_Yasin" title="Yevgeny Yasin">Yevgeny Yasin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sophie_Shevardnadze" title="Sophie Shevardnadze">Sophie Shevardnadze</a>. As of April 2014<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Echo_of_Moscow&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Yulia_Latynina" title="Yulia Latynina">Yulia Latynina</a> is the most popular presenter at the radio station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In addition to broadcasting, Echo of Moscow runs a website that publishes analytical and factual materials in a variety of fields including international and domestic political affairs, social developments and cultural trends. The articles are written by members of the so-called Echo of Moscow Club, an honorary group of political analysts, academic researchers, columnists and public figures. Among the site's authors are <a href="/wiki/Boris_Akunin" title="Boris Akunin">Boris Akunin</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dmitrii_Bykov" title="Dmitrii Bykov">Dmitrii Bykov</a>, Matvey Ganapolsky, <a href="/wiki/Eduard_Limonov" title="Eduard Limonov">Eduard Limonov</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexey_Navalny" title="Alexey Navalny">Alexey Navalny</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valeriya_Novodvorskaya" title="Valeriya Novodvorskaya">Valeriya Novodvorskaya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victor_Shenderovich" title="Victor Shenderovich">Victor Shenderovich</a>, and a number of others, who have sustained national and international acclaim in their areas of expertise. The Echo of Moscow site is an authoritative source of information, and its publications are regularly cited, relied on and reproduced by major Russian internet publications and other media sources.</p>
<p>As of 2005<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Echo_of_Moscow&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> Echo of Moscow was majority owned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gazprom_Media" title="Gazprom Media">Gazprom Media</a> which holds 66% of its shares, the remaining 34% are held by journalist staff.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Approximately 900,000 people in Moscow and 1,8 millions in other Russian regions listen to Echo of Moscow daily. According to TNS Global (Moscow, summer 2011) the majority of the listeners are middle class and upper middle class Russians 40 years and older with a higher education residing in the city of Moscow. They make up one third of total listeners of the radio station.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Category:Articles_contradicting_other_articles" title="Category:Articles contradicting other articles"><span title="A third is not a majority.">contradictory</span></a></i>]</sup> The radio programs can be also be streamed online and available in audio and video pod-cast formats on the Echo of Moscow website.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critics_and_controversy">Critics and controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Echo_of_Moscow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critics and controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 2009 numerous chief editor replies point out that Gazprom and/or other stock-holders did not interfere with informational policy and were not allowed to.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 November 2014 the station received an official <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roskomnadzor" title="Roskomnadzor">Roskomnadzor</a> warning that a program the station had aired about <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> contained "information justifying <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_crimes" title="War crimes">war crimes</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rferl_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-rferl-4">[4]</a></sup> A radio station can be closed down if it receives two Roskomnadzor warnings in one year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rferl_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-rferl-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
