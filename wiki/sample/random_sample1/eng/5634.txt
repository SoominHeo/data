<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Moscow Philharmonic Orchestra</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Moscow Philharmonic Orchestra</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Orchestra" title="Orchestra">orchestra</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. It was founded in 1951 by <a href="/wiki/Samuil_Samosud" title="Samuil Samosud">Samuil Samosud</a>, as the Moscow Youth Orchestra for young and inexperienced musicians, acquiring its current name in 1953. It is most associated with longtime conductor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiril_Kondrashin" title="Kiril Kondrashin">Kiril Kondrashin</a> under whom it premiered <a href="/wiki/Dmitri_Shostakovich" title="Dmitri Shostakovich">Shostakovich</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._4_(Shostakovich)" title="Symphony No. 4 (Shostakovich)">Fourth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._13_(Shostakovich)" title="Symphony No. 13 (Shostakovich)">Thirteenth</a> symphonies as well as other works. The Orchestra undertook a major tour of Japan with Kondrashin in April 1967 and CDs of the Japanese radio recordings have been made available on the Altus label.</p>
<p>The orchestra has also flourished under <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Simonov" title="Yuri Simonov">Yuri Simonov</a>, the orchestra's principal conductor since 1998. In recent years it has performed in Britain, France, Germany, Slovenia, Croatia, Poland, Lithuania, and Spain, as well as Hong Kong, Japan and South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
