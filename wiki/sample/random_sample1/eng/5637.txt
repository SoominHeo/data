<p><b>Moira Kelly</b> (born March 6, 1968) is an American actress. She is best known for the role of <a href="/wiki/Donna_Hayward" title="Donna Hayward">Donna Hayward</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks:_Fire_Walk_With_Me" title="Twin Peaks: Fire Walk With Me">Twin Peaks: Fire Walk With Me</a></i>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Lara_Flynn_Boyle" title="Lara Flynn Boyle">Lara Flynn Boyle</a> in this prequel to the 1990 TV Series <i><a href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks" title="Twin Peaks">Twin Peaks</a></i>. She is also known for portraying Kate Moseley in the 1992 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cutting_Edge" title="The Cutting Edge">The Cutting Edge</a></i>, as well as single mother <a href="/wiki/List_of_One_Tree_Hill_characters#Karen_Roe" title="List of One Tree Hill characters">Karen Roe</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Teen_drama" title="Teen drama">teen drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/One_Tree_Hill_(TV_series)" title="One Tree Hill (TV series)">One Tree Hill</a></i>, Dorothy Day in <i><a href="/wiki/Entertaining_Angels:_The_Dorothy_Day_Story" title="Entertaining Angels: The Dorothy Day Story">Entertaining Angels: The Dorothy Day Story</a></i>, White House media consultant <a href="/wiki/Mandy_Hampton" title="Mandy Hampton">Mandy Hampton</a> in the first season of <i><a href="/wiki/The_West_Wing" title="The West Wing">The West Wing</a></i>, and the voice of Simba's love interest Nala in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King" title="The Lion King">The Lion King</a></i> and its sequel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King_II:_Simba%27s_Pride" title="The Lion King II: Simba's Pride">The Lion King II: Simba's Pride</a></i>. She also played <a href="/wiki/Hetty_Kelly" title="Hetty Kelly">Hetty Kelly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oona_O%27Neill" title="Oona O'Neill">Oona O'Neill</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Chaplin_(film)" title="Chaplin (film)">Chaplin</a></i> (1992).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moira_Kelly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kelly was born in <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, a daughter of Irish immigrants. Her father, Peter, was trained as a concert violinist, and her mother, Anne, is a nurse. Kelly is the third of six children and was raised in <a href="/wiki/Ronkonkoma,_New_York" title="Ronkonkoma, New York">Ronkonkoma</a>. She was brought up as a Catholic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Kelly attended <a href="/wiki/Connetquot_High_School" title="Connetquot High School">Connetquot Senior High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bohemia,_New_York" title="Bohemia, New York">Bohemia, Long Island</a>, graduating in the class of 1986. Later, she attended <a href="/wiki/Marymount_Manhattan_College" title="Marymount Manhattan College">Marymount Manhattan College</a>.</p>
<p>In her youth, Kelly was cast in a small role in her high school's 1984 production of <i><a href="/wiki/Annie_(musical)" title="Annie (musical)">Annie</a></i>. Due to illness, the girl playing Miss Hannigan was replaced, causing a series of cast changes leading to her choice of career. A faithful Catholic, Kelly had to decide between acting and her childhood ambition of becoming a <a href="/wiki/Nun" title="Nun">nun</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allmovie_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allmovie-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moira_Kelly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kelly made her professional acting debut in the made-for-TV movie <i><a href="/wiki/Love,_Lies_and_Murder" title="Love, Lies and Murder">Love, Lies and Murder</a></i>, playing as a teenage murderer. She went on to have small roles in the films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boy_Who_Cried_Bitch" title="The Boy Who Cried Bitch">The Boy Who Cried Bitch</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hi-Life&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hi-Life (page does not exist)">Hi-Life</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Billy_Bathgate" title="Billy Bathgate">Billy Bathgate</a></i> before being cast as <a href="/wiki/Donna_Hayward" title="Donna Hayward">Donna Hayward</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks:_Fire_Walk_with_Me" title="Twin Peaks: Fire Walk with Me">Twin Peaks: Fire Walk with Me</a></i>. For that film, she went home and got permission from her priest because of an explicit sex scene.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allmovie_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Allmovie-2">[2]</a></sup> In the same year, she starred opposite <a href="/wiki/D._B._Sweeney" title="D. B. Sweeney">D. B. Sweeney</a> in the romantic comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cutting_Edge" title="The Cutting Edge">The Cutting Edge</a></i> and played two roles opposite <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Downey,_Jr." title="Robert Downey, Jr.">Robert Downey, Jr.</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Chaplin_(film)" title="Chaplin (film)">Chaplin</a></i>. According to a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Guide_(magazine)" title="TV Guide (magazine)">TV Guide</a></i> interview, before taking on her role in <i>Daybreak</i>, Kelly once again asked her priest for advice: "Being a Catholic, I wondered if it would be against my religion to play a girl who has premarital sex." The priest told her "it was okay, as long as my artistic intentions were true and I wasn't doing it for the notoriety or the money."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>She has since appeared in the movies <i><a href="/wiki/With_Honors_(film)" title="With Honors (film)">With Honors</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Odessa_(film)" title="Little Odessa (film)">Little Odessa</a></i>, <i>The Tie That Binds</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Dangerous_Beauty" title="Dangerous Beauty">Dangerous Beauty</a></i> amongst others, and provided the adult voice of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_The_Lion_King_characters" title="List of The Lion King characters">Nala</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King" title="The Lion King">The Lion King</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King_II:_Simba%27s_Pride" title="The Lion King II: Simba's Pride">The Lion King II: Simba's Pride</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King_1%C2%BD" title="The Lion King 1½">The Lion King 1½</a></i>. In her independent film career, Kelly had the starring role of activist <a href="/wiki/Dorothy_Day" title="Dorothy Day">Dorothy Day</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Entertaining_Angels:_The_Dorothy_Day_Story" title="Entertaining Angels: The Dorothy Day Story">Entertaining Angels: The Dorothy Day Story</a></i> and starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Close" title="Glenn Close">Glenn Close</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Safety_of_Objects" title="The Safety of Objects">The Safety of Objects</a></i>. She played <a href="/wiki/Helen_Keller" title="Helen Keller">Helen Keller</a> in the made-for-TV movie <i>Monday After the Miracle</i>, which broadcast on November 15, 1998, on <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>.</p>
<p>Kelly starred in the CBS drama <i><a href="/wiki/To_Have_%26_to_Hold_(TV_series)" title="To Have &amp; to Hold (TV series)">To Have &amp; to Hold</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Jason_Beghe" title="Jason Beghe">Jason Beghe</a> before playing <a href="/wiki/Mandy_Hampton" title="Mandy Hampton">Mandy Hampton</a> in the first season of <i><a href="/wiki/The_West_Wing" title="The West Wing">The West Wing</a></i>. In 2003, Kelly began playing single mother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karen_Roe" title="Karen Roe">Karen Roe</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Teen_drama" title="Teen drama">teen drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/One_Tree_Hill_(TV_series)" title="One Tree Hill (TV series)">One Tree Hill</a></i>. She also directed two episodes of the series: "Resolve" (2007) and "I Slept with Someone in Fall Out Boy and All I Got Was This Stupid Song Written About Me" (2006).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In the fifth season, she ceased to be a regular cast member, but made guest appearances in the 100th episode and the sixth season finale. She has made guest appearances in television shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/Heroes_(TV_series)" title="Heroes (TV series)">Heroes</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Numbers_(TV_series)" title="Numbers (TV series)">Numb3rs</a></i>. Kelly has also appeared in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Remember_the_Daze" title="Remember the Daze">Remember the Daze</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Smile_as_Big_as_the_Moon" title="A Smile as Big as the Moon">A Smile as Big as the Moon</a></i> and <i>Taken Back: Finding Haley</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moira_Kelly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2000, Moira Kelly married Steve Hewitt, a Texas businessman. They have two children: son Eamon (born 2003) and daughter Ella (born 2001).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
