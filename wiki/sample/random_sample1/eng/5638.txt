<p><b>Moisés Arias</b> (born April 18, 1994) is an American actor who is best known for his roles as Rico in the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana" title="Hannah Montana">Hannah Montana</a></i> and Biaggio in the 2013 Sundance film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Kings_of_Summer" title="The Kings of Summer">The Kings of Summer</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mois%C3%A9s_Arias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moisés Arias was born on April 18, 1994 in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, the son of immigrants from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paisa_region" title="Paisa region">Paisa region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was raised bilingual and is fluent in both English and Spanish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-passport_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-passport-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His brother is <i><a href="/wiki/Kickin%27_It" title="Kickin' It">Kickin' It</a></i> actor Mateo Arias.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Before starting his acting career, Moises graduated from <a href="/wiki/Barbizon_Modeling_and_Acting_School" title="Barbizon Modeling and Acting School">Barbizon Modeling and Acting School</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta, Georgia</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mois%C3%A9s_Arias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moisés has made guest appearances on shows such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Suite_Life_of_Zack_%26_Cody" title="The Suite Life of Zack &amp; Cody">The Suite Life of Zack &amp; Cody</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Everybody_Hates_Chris" title="Everybody Hates Chris">Everybody Hates Chris</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Wizards_of_Waverly_Place" title="Wizards of Waverly Place">Wizards of Waverly Place</a></i>. He had a recurring role as Rico on <i><a href="/wiki/Hannah_Montana" title="Hannah Montana">Hannah Montana</a></i>, and became a series regular in the show's second season. In 2009, he played Andre in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Disney_Channel_Original_Movies" title="List of Disney Channel Original Movies">Disney Channel Original Movie</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Dadnapped" title="Dadnapped">Dadnapped</a></i>. As a regular on the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a>, he participated on the first and second <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel_Games" title="Disney Channel Games">Disney Channel Games</a> on the red team. He also participated in the third Disney Channel games on the Yellow Team (Comets). His main role while at Disney was as Rico Suave in 'Hannah Montana'.</p>
<p>He appeared in music videos for <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Jam" title="Pearl Jam">Pearl Jam</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Jonas_Brothers" title="Jonas Brothers">Jonas Brothers</a> video for their song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SOS_(Jonas_Brothers_song)" title="SOS (Jonas Brothers song)">SOS</a>", and Parmalee, and the 2006 film <i><a href="/wiki/Nacho_Libre" title="Nacho Libre">Nacho Libre</a></i> in a minor role. He participated in the <a href="/wiki/Guadalajara_International_Film_Festival" title="Guadalajara International Film Festival">Guadalajara Film Festival</a> (2009) during the presentation of the movie <i>The Perfect Game</i> with <a href="/wiki/Eva_Longoria" title="Eva Longoria">Eva Longoria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Arias appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Beethoven%27s_Big_Break" title="Beethoven's Big Break">Beethoven's Big Break</a></i> in 2008. He appeared on the <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN family of networks</a> as a roving 'sideline' reporter conducting interviews with fans and players at the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Little_League_World_Series" title="Little League World Series">Little League World Series</a>. He appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Wizards_of_Waverly_Place" title="Wizards of Waverly Place">Wizards of Waverly Place</a></i> and played Max (<a href="/wiki/Jake_T._Austin" title="Jake T. Austin">Jake T. Austin</a>)'s conscience.</p>
<p>Arias and his brother directed a series of YouTube films under the user name Moiswashere. In 2009 they worked on a film called <i>Motocross Madness</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In mid-2012, Arias filmed <i><a href="/wiki/The_Kings_of_Summer" title="The Kings of Summer">The Kings of Summer</a></i>, which had its premiere on January 19, 2013, at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Sundance_Film_Festival" title="2013 Sundance Film Festival">2013 Sundance Film Festival</a> to positive reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
