<p><b>Mojikō Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">門司港駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mojikō-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima_Main_Line" title="Kagoshima Main Line">Kagoshima Main Line</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moji-ku,_Kitakyushu" title="Moji-ku, Kitakyushu">Moji-ku, Kitakyushu</a>, Japan, operated by the <a href="/wiki/Kyushu_Railway_Company" title="Kyushu Railway Company">Kyushu Railway Company</a> (JR Kyushu).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mojik%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mojikō Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima_Main_Line" title="Kagoshima Main Line">Kagoshima Main Line</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mojik%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neo-Renaissance" title="Neo-Renaissance">Neo-Renaissance</a> style building was built in 1914.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan620_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-railfan620-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The station has a "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midori_no_Madoguchi" title="Midori no Madoguchi">Midori no Madoguchi</a>" staffed ticket counter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jrkyushu_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-jrkyushu-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mojik%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station opened on 1 April 1891.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jnr1985_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-jnr1985-3">[3]</a></sup> With the privatization of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on 1 April 1987, the station came under the control of JR Kyushu.</p>
<p>From 29 September 2012, the 1914-vintage station building was temporarily closed for major renovations. It is scheduled to reopen in March 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railfan620_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-railfan620-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surrounding_area">Surrounding area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mojik%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Surrounding area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mojikō Station is the gateway to various tourist attractions and old buildings which have been preserved in the nearby streets under the name Mojikō Retro.</p>
