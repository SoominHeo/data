<p><b>Mochudi</b> is one of the larger villages in <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> with a population of 44,815 people in 2011. It is situated in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bakgatla&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bakgatla (page does not exist)">Bakgatla</a> tribal region, in <a href="/wiki/Kgatleng_District" title="Kgatleng District">Kgatleng District</a>, about 37 km (23 mi) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Gaborone" title="Gaborone">Gaborone</a>. The village lies several kilometres from the main Gaborone–<a href="/wiki/Francistown" title="Francistown">Francistown</a> road, and can be accessed through a short turn at <a href="/wiki/Pilane_(Botswana)" title="Pilane (Botswana)">Pilane</a>. Mochudi was settled by the <a href="/wiki/Tswana_people" title="Tswana people">Tswana people</a> in 1871.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mochudi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The tourist destinations of note inside Mochudi are the <a href="/wiki/Phuthadikobo_Museum" title="Phuthadikobo Museum">Phuthadikobo Museum</a> at the top of the hill, which contains old photographs and historical texts relating to Mochudi and the history of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bakgatla&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bakgatla (page does not exist)">Bakgatla</a> people. It is housed in a building that was originally the first school of Mochudi. Nearby is the hulk of the first tractor owned by a Motswana and huge footprints that legend says belongs to "Matsieng", a giant and ancestor of the Tswana, who led his people and the animals from the centre of the earth to inhabit the world.<a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.botswana-tourism.gov.bw/attractions/matisieng.html" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></p>
<p>Mochudi is known for its traditional painted houses and still has a traditional <a href="/wiki/Kgotla" title="Kgotla">kgotla</a> (tribal meeting place).</p>
<p>More information about the Bakgatla people can be found in David Suggs' ethnography entitled "A Bagful of Locusts and the Baboon Woman: Constructions of Gender, Change, and Continuity in Botswana."</p>
<p>Mochudi was the home of Mma Ramotswe, the heroine of the <i><a href="/wiki/The_No._1_Ladies%27_Detective_Agency" title="The No. 1 Ladies' Detective Agency">No. 1 Ladies' Detective Agency</a></i> series of books. Michelle Obama visited the village during U.S. President Obama's African tour in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mochudi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mochudi has several public Junior Secondary Schools and one public high school, Molefi Senior Secondary School. Several junior schools are Linchwe II JSS, Kgamanyane JSS, Radikolo JSS, Bakgatle JSS, Sedibelo JSS, and Ithuteng JSS. There is also Podulogong Rehabilitation Centre that offers hands on training courses to blind or vision impaired individuals.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mochudi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mochudi is home to <a href="/wiki/Botswana_Premier_League" title="Botswana Premier League">Botswana Premier League</a> soccer club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mochudi_Centre_Chiefs" title="Mochudi Centre Chiefs">Mochudi Centre Chiefs</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mochudi_Rugby_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mochudi Rugby Club (page does not exist)">Mochudi Rugby Club</a> is a local rugby team based in the village.</p>
