<p><b>Shigeyoshi Mochizuki</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">望月 重良</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mochizuki Shigeyoshi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born July 9, 1973)</span> is a former <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player. He played for <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan national team</a>. He was capped 14 times for the <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japanese national team</a> and scored the winning goal in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_Cup_2000" title="Asian Cup 2000">Asian Cup 2000</a> Final, Japan vs Saudi Arabia.</p>
<p>Mochizuki played for <a href="/wiki/Vissel_Kobe" title="Vissel Kobe">Vissel Kobe</a> and <a href="/wiki/JEF_United_Chiba" title="JEF United Chiba">JEF United Ichihara</a> in the <a href="/wiki/J1_League" title="J1 League">J1 League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is currently the chairman of the football club <a href="/wiki/SC_Sagamihara" title="SC Sagamihara">SC Sagamihara</a>.</p>
