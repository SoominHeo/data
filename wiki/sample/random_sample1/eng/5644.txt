<p><b>Moca</b> is the capital of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Espaillat_(province)" title="Espaillat (province)">Espaillat</a> province, in the <a href="/wiki/Cibao" title="Cibao">Cibao</a> region of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, and it is the tenth-largest city of that country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Moca is located 11 miles/18 kilometers away from the country’s second-largest city, <a href="/wiki/Santiago_de_los_Caballeros" title="Santiago de los Caballeros">Santiago</a>. Known as "La Villa Heroica" (Village of Heroes) due to the amount of men and women from Moca who have played a major role in the Dominican Republic's history in bringing down two dictators, <a href="/wiki/Ulises_Heureaux" title="Ulises Heureaux">Ulises Heureaux</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Trujillo" title="Rafael Trujillo">Rafael Trujillo</a>, and bringing democracy back to the country.</p>

<p>Moca is home to the Corazon Sagrado de Jesus ("Sacred Heart of Jesus") Cathedral. All its pane glass windows were originally brought from <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> depicting the <a href="/wiki/Apostles" title="Apostles">apostles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a>' path to the <a href="/wiki/Crucifixion" title="Crucifixion">crucifixion</a>. Agriculture forms the primary livelihood of the inhabitants. Plantain and yucca are main crops. Most crops are harvested by hand.</p>
<p>Moca is also recognized for its strong political up bringing. Former dictator Rafael Trujillo owned a house in Moca. In fact, the house is located a few blocks from the church Sagrado Corazon De Jesus (picture above).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moca,_Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to Emiliano Tejera, Moca means a tree that overlooks the river banks. Others claim that the word comes from the region of Mocán, a section in the province of La Vega near the river. Others claim that the word Moca is based on the coffee crop name: Mocca. According to Franklin Torres Moca is derived from an Aboriginal term which was adopted by the European settlers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moca,_Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moca has a total area of 339.21 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Square_kilometers" title="Square kilometers">km²</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-listado_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-listado-1">[1]</a></sup> The municipality sits on a volcanic coral reef with <a href="/wiki/Hill" title="Hill">hills</a> to the west and low <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a> to the north; these mountains are part of the <i>Cordillera Septentrional</i>. The highest mountain in the municipality, and in the province, is <i>El Mogote</i> at (970 m). Moca is at an <a href="/wiki/Elevation" title="Elevation">elevation</a> of 183 m with an <a href="/wiki/Average" title="Average">average</a> <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperature</a> in the city of 25.3°C and an average <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainfall" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a> of 1,174.4 mm.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moca,_Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moca has a very important banking sector, the city is home to branches of all commercial banks of the country. A strong business in shops, furniture, supermarkets, warehouses, factories of footwear, food, construction, and hardware. The city has the largest coffee manufacturing industry, Industries Banilejas.<b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></b></p>
<p>The city is also known for its agricultural production, with the plantain, banana and cassava as major crops. Other fruits: bananas, pigeon pea, taro, yams, squash, beans, coffee, oranges, lemons, grapefruit, papaya (papaya), etc. A variety of vegetables such as lettuce, tomatoes, eggplants, peppers, cabbage are cultivated, among others. It also has a great development in poultry products, with a production of 70% in this sector.</p>
