<p>Year <b>220</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">CCXX</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Leap year starting on Saturday">leap year starting on Saturday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Antonius and Eutychianus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 973 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 220 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=220&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=220&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=220&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Goths" title="Goths">Goths</a> invade <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Minor" title="Asia Minor">Asia Minor</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a>.</li>
<li>An <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> delegation visits the Roman emperor <a href="/wiki/Elagabalus" title="Elagabalus">Elagabalus</a>.</li>
<li>Great frost in England is said to have lasted for five months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Imperator" title="Imperator">Imperator</a> <a href="/wiki/Elagabalus" title="Elagabalus">Marcus Aurelius Antoninus Augustus (Elagabalus)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Publius_Valerius_Comazon" title="Publius Valerius Comazon">Publius Valerius Comazon</a> become <a href="/wiki/Roman_consul" title="Roman consul">Roman consuls</a>.</li>
<li>Elagabalus divorces <a href="/wiki/Julia_Cornelia_Paula" title="Julia Cornelia Paula">Julia Paula</a> and marries <a href="/wiki/Aquilia_Severa" title="Aquilia Severa">Aquilia Severa</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Vestal_Virgin" title="Vestal Virgin">Vestal Virgin</a>. The wedding causes an enormous controversy – traditionally, the punishment for breaking <a href="/wiki/Celibacy" title="Celibacy">celibacy</a> is death by being <a href="/wiki/Premature_burial" title="Premature burial">buried alive</a>.</li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Ardashir_I" title="Ardashir I">Ardashir I</a>, founder of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sassanid_dynasty" title="Sassanid dynasty">Sassanid dynasty</a>, gains support from some <a href="/wiki/Parthia" title="Parthia">Parthian</a> sub-kings and revolts against the rule of <a href="/wiki/Vologases_VI" title="Vologases VI">Vologases VI</a>. Ardashir, a grandson of <a href="/wiki/Sasan" title="Sasan">Sasan</a>, had ruled <a href="/wiki/Fars_Province" title="Fars Province">Persis</a> since <a href="/wiki/208" title="208">208</a> and six years ago gained control of the region surrounding <a href="/wiki/Persepolis" title="Persepolis">Persepolis</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=220&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms" title="Three Kingdoms">Three Kingdoms</a>: The northern part of China is under the control of <a href="/wiki/Cao_Cao" title="Cao Cao">Cao Cao</a>, Imperial <a href="/wiki/Chancellor" title="Chancellor">Chancellor</a> and ruler of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Wei" title="Kingdom of Wei">Kingdom of Wei</a>. Following his death on <a href="/wiki/March_15" title="March 15">March 15</a>, his son <a href="/wiki/Cao_Pi" title="Cao Pi">Cao Pi</a> receives the abdication of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xian_of_Han" title="Emperor Xian of Han">Emperor Xian of Han</a> on <a href="/wiki/December_10" title="December 10">December 10</a>, and proclaims himself emperor of <a href="/wiki/Cao_Wei" title="Cao Wei">Cao Wei</a>. This ends the <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han dynasty</a>, the former emperor being created <a href="/wiki/Duke" title="Duke">Duke</a> of <a href="/wiki/Shanyang_County" title="Shanyang County">Shanyang</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=220&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=220&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Cao_Wei" title="Cao Wei">Wei dynasty</a> will give official recognition to <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">Taoism</a> as its religious sect, and the sect's celestial masters will reciprocate by giving spiritual approbation to the Wei as successors to the Han. By the end of the century most powerful families in northern China will subscribe to Daoist principles.</li>
</ul>
