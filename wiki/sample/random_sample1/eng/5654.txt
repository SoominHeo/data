<p><b>Prince Motoyoshi</b> (元良親王, <i>Motoyoshi shinnō</i>, 890-July 26, 943) was a poet and nobleman of the <a href="/wiki/Heian_period" title="Heian period">Heian period</a>. One of his poems is included in the <i><a href="/wiki/Ogura_Hyakunin_Isshu" title="Ogura Hyakunin Isshu">Ogura Hyakunin Isshu</a></i> as number 20 in the anthology. Twenty of his poems were included in the <i><a href="/wiki/Gosen_Wakash%C5%AB" title="Gosen Wakashū">Gosen Wakashū</a></i>; a personal anthology entitled <i>Motoyoshi Shinnō-shū</i> (元良親王集) is also extant.</p>
<p>He was the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Y%C5%8Dzei" title="Emperor Yōzei">Emperor Yōzei</a>. His wives included Shūshi, a daughter of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Daigo" title="Emperor Daigo">Emperor Daigo</a>, and Kaishi, a daughter of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Uda" title="Emperor Uda">Emperor Uda</a>.</p>
