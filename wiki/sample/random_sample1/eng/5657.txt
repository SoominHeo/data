<p><b>Mohamed Kallon</b> (born 6 October 1979) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sierra_Leonean" title="Sierra Leonean">Sierra Leonean</a> international <a href="/wiki/Manager_(association_football)" title="Manager (association football)">manager</a> and former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a>. He is widely considered<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> the most famous footballer from Sierra Leone. He last played for Sierra Leone club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kallon_F.C." title="Kallon F.C.">Kallon F.C.</a>. He is the younger brother of former Sierra Leonean international footballers <a href="/wiki/Kemokai_Kallon" title="Kemokai Kallon">Kemokai Kallon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Musa_Kallon" title="Musa Kallon">Musa Kallon</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kallon was born on 6 October 1979 in <a href="/wiki/Kenema" title="Kenema">Kenema</a>, Sierra Leone. Kallon was raised in <a href="/wiki/Freetown" title="Freetown">Freetown</a>, Sierra Leone's capital. Kallon is the younger brother to former Sierra Leone international footballers <a href="/wiki/Kemokai_Kallon" title="Kemokai Kallon">Kemokai Kallon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Musa_Kallon" title="Musa Kallon">Musa Kallon</a>. Mohamed Kallon attended primary school in Kenema and completed his secondary education at St. Edwards Secondary School in <a href="/wiki/Freetown" title="Freetown">Freetown</a>.</p>
<p>During his early international career for Sierra Leone, Kallon was given the <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">nickname</a> <b>Small Kallon</b> by Sierra Leonean football <a href="/wiki/Fan_(person)" title="Fan (person)">fans</a> because he was the youngest of the three Kallon brothers in the Sierra Leone national team.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After he completed his form three level of education (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grade_9" title="Grade 9">grade 9</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S" title="U.S">U.S</a>) from St. Edwards Secondary School in <a href="/wiki/Freetown" title="Freetown">Freetown</a> in 1994, Kallon signed with local club, <a href="/wiki/Old_Edwardians_F.C." title="Old Edwardians F.C.">Old Edwardians</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone_National_Premier_League" title="Sierra Leone National Premier League">Sierra Leone National Premier League</a> at the age of 15. During the 1994‚Äì95 season, he struck fifteen goals in 24 league games for Old Edwardians and became the youngest player to ever play and score in <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone_National_Premier_League" title="Sierra Leone National Premier League">Sierra Leone National Premier League</a>.</p>
<p>After the 1994‚Äì95 season, Kallon left Old Edwardians and signed for <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanese</a> club <a href="/wiki/Tadamon_Sour" title="Tadamon Sour">Tadamon Sour</a>. He spent one season with <a href="/wiki/Sp%C3%A5nga_IS" title="Sp√•nga IS">Sp√•nga IS</a> in Stockholm, Sweden. He was then signed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">Internazionale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He then loaned to <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Super_League" title="Swiss Super League">Swiss</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Lugano" title="AC Lugano">AC Lugano</a>, Serie A club <a href="/wiki/Bologna_F.C._1909" title="Bologna F.C. 1909">Bologna F.C. 1909</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cagliari_Calcio" title="Cagliari Calcio">Cagliari</a>, and Serie B clubs <a href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C." title="Genoa C.F.C.">Genoa</a> He also farmed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reggina_Calcio" title="Reggina Calcio">Reggina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vicenza_Calcio" title="Vicenza Calcio">Vicenza</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Co-ownership_(football)" title="Co-ownership (football)">co-ownership</a> deal<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> for undisclosed fee and 9,000 billion <a href="/wiki/Italian_lira" title="Italian lira">lire</a> respectively (‚Ç¨4,648,112).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He played with <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_Zanetti" title="Cristiano Zanetti">Cristiano Zanetti</a> at Cagliari, whom he later worked with again at Internazionale.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Internazionale">Internazionale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Internazionale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>2001‚Äì02</dt>
</dl>
<p>After the abolition of the non-EU quota for each team halfway through the <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Serie_A" title="2000‚Äì01 Serie A">2000‚Äì01 season</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Kallon returned to Italian giants <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">Internazionale</a> before the start of the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_Serie_A" title="2001‚Äì02 Serie A">2001‚Äì02 season</a>.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> for a reported 8,500 billion lire transfer fee (‚Ç¨4,389,884).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> With <a href="/wiki/Christian_Vieri" title="Christian Vieri">Christian Vieri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronaldo_(Brazilian_footballer)" title="Ronaldo (Brazilian footballer)">Ronaldo</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Recoba" title="√Ålvaro Recoba">√Ålvaro Recoba</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adriano_Leite_Ribeiro" title="Adriano Leite Ribeiro">Adriano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hakan_%C5%9E%C3%BCk%C3%BCr" title="Hakan ≈û√ºk√ºr">Hakan ≈û√ºk√ºr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Ventola" title="Nicola Ventola">Nicola Ventola</a> also in the side, Kallon was originally a 3rd or 4th choice striker. But injuries to Ronaldo and Recoba meant that Kallon played 29 Serie A matches, scoring 9 goals and becoming the team's 2nd highest scorer of the 2001‚Äì02 Serie A season, behind Vieri, as the club narrowly missed out on the league title, finishing in third place, also reaching the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_UEFA_Cup" title="2001‚Äì02 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a>.</p>
<dl>
<dt>2002‚Äì03</dt>
</dl>
<p>He only played 9 times (scoring 5 goals) in Serie A in <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Serie_A" title="2002‚Äì03 Serie A">2002‚Äì03</a> season due to injuries in August<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and February, as the team managed a second-place finish in the league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Kallon returned to the side in May after <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Batistuta" title="Gabriel Batistuta">Gabriel Batistuta</a> was injured in April. He also played both legs of the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2002‚Äì03 UEFA Champions League">2002‚Äì03 UEFA Champions League</a> semi-final against <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">A.C. Milan</a> as Batistuta was unable to register. He played in both games as a second-half substitute, for <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Recoba" title="√Ålvaro Recoba">√Ålvaro Recoba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_Crespo" title="Hern√°n Crespo">Hern√°n Crespo</a> respectively.</p>
<dl>
<dt>2003‚Äì04</dt>
</dl>
<p>Kallon tested positive for the banned substance <a href="/wiki/Nandrolone" title="Nandrolone">nandrolone</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese Calcio</a> on 27 September 2003<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and was banned from football for eight months. Kallon then struggled to get into the starting eleven during the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Serie_A" title="2003‚Äì04 Serie A">2003‚Äì04 season</a>, primarily due to the rise of young <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nigerian" title="Nigerian">Nigerian</a> striker <a href="/wiki/Obafemi_Martins" title="Obafemi Martins">Obafemi Martins</a> and the return of Brazilian ace <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adriano_Leite_Ribeiro" title="Adriano Leite Ribeiro">Adriano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AS_Monaco">AS Monaco</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: AS Monaco">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt>2004‚Äì05</dt>
</dl>
<p>He signed a 4-year contract with Mon√©gasque club <a href="/wiki/AS_Monaco_FC" title="AS Monaco FC">AS Monaco</a> before the start of the 2004‚Äì05 season, as the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2003‚Äì04 UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> runners-up had lost <a href="/wiki/Dado_Pr%C5%A1o" title="Dado Pr≈°o">Dado Pr≈°o</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Morientes" title="Fernando Morientes">Fernando Morientes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He was impressive during his first season at Monaco, but quickly fell out with French manager <a href="/wiki/Didier_Deschamps" title="Didier Deschamps">Didier Deschamps</a>, and was relegated to the bench in March 2005.</p>
<dl>
<dt>2005-06 (Al-Ittihad)</dt>
</dl>
<p>He went on loan to Saudi club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Ittihad_(Jeddah)" title="Al-Ittihad (Jeddah)">Al-Ittihad</a> during the start of the 2005‚Äì06 season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and helped the team win the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League_2005" title="AFC Champions League 2005">AFC Champions League 2005</a> where he was top scorer in the competition before returning to AS Monaco. He also played at the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIFA_Club_World_Championship" title="2005 FIFA Club World Championship">2005 FIFA Club World Championship</a> with Al-Ittihad.</p>
<dl>
<dt>2006‚Äì08</dt>
</dl>
<p>He played 14 <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> games in his last season for Monaco. He played his last match in Ligue 1 on 11 August 2007 against <a href="/wiki/FC_Lorient" title="FC Lorient">FC Lorient</a>, the 2nd match of the season, before he was released by Monaco. He underwent a trial with <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham</a> in September 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> after previously being linked with <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> in July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> However, Kallon failed to qualify for a work permit in England, as <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone_national_football_team" title="Sierra Leone national football team">Sierra Leone</a> ranked 79th in the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Rankings" title="FIFA World Rankings">FIFA World Rankings</a> in September 1997, but the requirement was above 70th for non-EU internationals. In November 2007, he signed a pre-contract with <a href="/wiki/Al-Hilal_FC" title="Al-Hilal FC">Al Hilal</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> but this later collapsed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AEK_FC">AEK FC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: AEK FC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kallon signed a 6-month contract with <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> giants <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AEK_F.C." title="AEK F.C.">AEK F.C.</a> on 29 January 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He played for the capital club in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup_2007%E2%80%9308" title="UEFA Cup 2007‚Äì08">UEFA Cup 2007‚Äì08</a> round of 32 against <a href="/wiki/Getafe_CF" title="Getafe CF">Getafe CF</a>, but AEK lost 1‚Äì4 on aggregate, with no goals from Kallon. He also played in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_League_Greece_2007%E2%80%9308" title="Super League Greece 2007‚Äì08">Super League Greece playoffs</a> to determine qualification to European competition. He scored once in his 3 appearances, and AEK finished second to qualify for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup_2008%E2%80%9309" title="UEFA Cup 2008‚Äì09">the following year's UEFA Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Al-Shabab">Al-Shabab</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Al-Shabab">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Shabab_(UAE)" title="Al-Shabab (UAE)">Al-Shabab</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> in 2008. He was released after picking up a serious injury in an international match.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kallon_FC">Kallon FC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Kallon FC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2009, he signed for his own club Kallon FC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shaanxi_Zhongjian_Chanba">Shaanxi Zhongjian Chanba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Shaanxi Zhongjian Chanba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 March 2010, he joined the Chinese championship, signing a one-year deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shaanxi_Zhongjian_Chanba_F.C." title="Shaanxi Zhongjian Chanba F.C.">Shaanxi Zhongjian Chanba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Kallon made his <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Super_League" title="Chinese Super League">CSL</a> debut against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dalian_Shide" title="Dalian Shide">Dalian Shide</a> on 28 March and scored a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty kick</a> in 50th minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He decided to leave after his contract was finished in December to be closer to his family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>He returned to Kallon F.C. ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2012_CAF_Confederation_Cup" title="2012 CAF Confederation Cup">2012 CAF Confederation Cup</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and scored the winning goal that got them to the second round.</p>
<p>On 26 June 2014, was named the head coach of Sierra Leone u-17 side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 22 March 2016 he announced his retirement as a player, having last played in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kallon became the youngest player to ever play for the Leone Stars when he made his senior international debut for Sierra Leone at the age of fifteen in April 1995 against <a href="/wiki/Congo_national_football_team" title="Congo national football team">Congo</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1996_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="1996 African Cup of Nations">1996 African Cup of Nations</a> qualifier in Freetown, in which he scored the winner. At the age of 16, Kallon was the youngest player at the 1996 African Nations cup in South Africa. He scored one of his country's two goals as Sierra Leone defeated <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a> 2‚Äì1 in their opening group match at the 1996 African Nations Cup, played at the <a href="/wiki/Free_State_Stadium" title="Free State Stadium">Free State Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bloemfontein" title="Bloemfontein">Bloemfontein</a>, South Africa.</p>
<p>Since then, he is the key member of <i>Leone Stars</i> and active at <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CAF)" title="1998 FIFA World Cup qualification (CAF)">1998</a>, <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CAF)" title="2002 FIFA World Cup qualification (CAF)">2002</a>, <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CAF)" title="2006 FIFA World Cup qualification (CAF)">2006</a> and <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(CAF)" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification (CAF)">2010</a> World Cup qualification. His most recent cap is on 9 February 2011 against <a href="/wiki/Nigeria_national_football_team" title="Nigeria national football team">Nigeria</a> in a friendly. Kallon was the captain of the Sierra Leone national team but quit his captaincy after the team failed to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="2010 African Cup of Nations">2010 African Cup of Nations</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_owner">Club owner</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Club owner">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kallon is the founder and owner of the Sierra Leonean football club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kallon_F.C." title="Kallon F.C.">Kallon F.C.</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-club_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-club-29">[29]</a></sup> currently playing in the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone_National_Premier_League" title="Sierra Leone National Premier League">Sierra Leone National Premier League</a>. Kallon is one of the top clubs in the Sierra Leone National Premier League and play their home games at the National Stadium in Freetown. Previously known as Sierra Fisheries, Kallon acquired the club in 2002 for $30,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-club_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-club-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kallon FC won the Sierra Leonean FA Cup, and the Sierra Leone League title in 2006, and qualifying for the African Champions League.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_2">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mohamed Kallon is a <a href="/wiki/Fundamentalism" title="Fundamentalism">devout</a> <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> and a member of the <a href="/wiki/Mende_people" title="Mende people">Mende</a> ethnic group. Kallon is married to his childhood girlfriend M'mah Mansaray. The couple celebrated their wedding at the <a href="/wiki/Freetown_Central_Mosque" title="Freetown Central Mosque">Freetown Central Mosque</a> in <a href="/wiki/Freetown" title="Freetown">Freetown</a> on 15 June 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Kallon is the younger brother of former Sierra Leonean international footballers <a href="/wiki/Kemokai_Kallon" title="Kemokai Kallon">Kemokai Kallon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Musa_Kallon" title="Musa Kallon">Musa Kallon</a><a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.sierraexpressmedia.com/archives/43652" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mohamed_Kallon_Children.27s_Foundation">Mohamed Kallon Children's Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mohamed_Kallon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Mohamed Kallon Children's Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Apart from the Kalleone Group of Company, comprising a musical recording studio, radio station, newspaper, sportshops, old Skool night club, pharmacy and FC Kallon, Mohamed Kallon is also about to launch his charity foundation, the MKCF, Mohamed Kallon Children's Foundation, which will cater for the needs of hundreds of Sierra Leone's street children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Recently Mohamed Kallon told SierraEye Magazine that as a boy himself who grew up in the streets of Freetown he is moved by the state of Sierra Leone Street Children and want to do all he can to help them. The foundation has made headways recently meeting with the president and working together with the United Nations and other NGOs to provide help for several Sierra Leonean children and also aiding the HIV/AIDS sensitisation programme in Sierra Leone. x The setting up of MKCF by Kallon gained massive media coverage and even the BBC reported on it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
