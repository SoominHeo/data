<p><b>Aida Mohamed</b> (born 12 March 1976 in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungarian</a> foil <a href="/wiki/Fencing" title="Fencing">fencer</a>, silver medallist at the <a href="/wiki/1993_World_Fencing_Championships" title="1993 World Fencing Championships">1993 World Championships</a> and team gold medallist at the <a href="/wiki/2007_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2007 European Fencing Championships">2007 European Championships</a>. She has competed at six Olympic Games from 1996 to 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sports-reference_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sports-reference-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aida_Mohamed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mohamed was born in Budapest from a Hungarian mother and a Syrian father. She was too shy in primary school to join the local fencing team, but her PE teacher persuaded her to give it a go. She then trained at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTK_Hung%C3%A1ria_FC" title="MTK Hungária FC">MTK</a> sports club in Budapest with fencing master Antal Solti, who remained her personal coach as of 2013<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Aida_Mohamed&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>. She won in 1991 a bronze medal at the Cadet European Championships and the gold medal at the Junior World Championships. She retained her Junior title in 1992 and won it again in 1996.</p>
<p>At senior level she won her first major medal in 1992 with a silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/1992_European_Fencing_Championships" title="1992 European Fencing Championships">1992 European Championships</a>, followed with another silver at the <a href="/wiki/1993_World_Fencing_Championships" title="1993 World Fencing Championships">1993 World Championships</a>. She won the bronze medal at the foil <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2006 World Fencing Championships">2006 World Fencing Championships</a> after she lost 15–3 to <a href="/wiki/Valentina_Vezzali" title="Valentina Vezzali">Valentina Vezzali</a> in the semi-finals.</p>
<p>Mohamed transferred to <a href="/wiki/T%C3%B6rekv%C3%A9s_SE" title="Törekvés SE">Törekvés SE</a> in 2009 after 23 years at MTK, but continued training with Antal Solti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UK_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-UK-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aida_Mohamed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She married in 2005 former Canadian Olympic pentathlete and épée fencer, <a href="/wiki/Laurie_Shong" title="Laurie Shong">Laurie Shong</a>, whom she met at the 1999 Seoul World Cup. They have two daughters, Olívia, born in 2009, and Leila, born in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UK_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-UK-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
