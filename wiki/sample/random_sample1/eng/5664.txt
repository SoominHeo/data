<p><b>HR 5553</b> is a binary star system located thirty-eight light-years away, in the constellation <a href="/wiki/Bo%C3%B6tes" title="Boötes">Boötes</a>. The combined spectrum of the pair matches that of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K-type_main_sequence_star" title="K-type main sequence star">K-type main sequence star</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">stellar classification</a> of K0 V. The two stars orbit each other with a <a href="/wiki/Orbital_period" title="Orbital period">period</a> of 125 days and a large <a href="/wiki/Orbital_eccentricity" title="Orbital eccentricity">eccentricity</a> of 0.51.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnras447_1_L75_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-mnras447_1_L75-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Infrared_excess" title="Infrared excess">infrared excess</a> has been detected around this star, most likely indicating the presence of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Circumstellar_disk" title="Circumstellar disk">circumstellar disk</a> at a radius of 34.2 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astronomical_Unit" title="Astronomical Unit">AU</a>. The temperature of this dust is 40 K.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aa555_A11_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-aa555_A11-6">[6]</a></sup> The estimated mass of the dust is 0.0002 times the mass of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aa570_A114_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-aa570_A114-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
