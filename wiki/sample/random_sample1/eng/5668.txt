<p><b>Muk Yan Jong</b> (Cantonese: <i>muk yan jong</i>/Mandarin: <i>mu ren zhuang</i>) is a wooden dummy used in <a href="/wiki/Chinese_martial_arts" title="Chinese martial arts">Chinese martial arts</a> training. It is associated with the martial art of <a href="/wiki/Wing_Chun" title="Wing Chun">Wing Chun</a> and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kung_fu" title="Kung fu">kung fu</a> styles of <a href="/wiki/Northern_and_southern_China" title="Northern and southern China">Southern China</a>. Traditionally made from wood, the dummies are now also made from synthetic materials such as steel and plastic.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Wing_Chun">In Wing Chun</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mu_ren_zhuang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In Wing Chun">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mu_ren_zhuang_(Chinese_martial_arts_training_dummy).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="609" height="277" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Mu_ren_zhuang_%28Chinese_martial_arts_training_dummy%29.jpg/220px-Mu_ren_zhuang_%28Chinese_martial_arts_training_dummy%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Mu_ren_zhuang_%28Chinese_martial_arts_training_dummy%29.jpg/330px-Mu_ren_zhuang_%28Chinese_martial_arts_training_dummy%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Mu_ren_zhuang_%28Chinese_martial_arts_training_dummy%29.jpg/440px-Mu_ren_zhuang_%28Chinese_martial_arts_training_dummy%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mu_ren_zhuang_(Chinese_martial_arts_training_dummy).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Wing Chun Dummy at a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wing_Tsun" title="Wing Tsun">Wing Tsun</a> school</div>
</div>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Wing_Chun" title="Wing Chun">Wing Chun</a> wooden dummy is the most popular form of wooden dummy. A popular legend says it came about when <a href="/wiki/108_(number)#Martial_arts" title="108 (number)">108</a> separate wooden dummies from the Shaolin Temple were combined into one by the nun Ng Mui to make training more efficient and effective. The Wing Chun wooden dummy uses an arm and leg configuration designed to cultivate fighting skill and chi simultaneously.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Hong Kong wooden dummy is a wall mounted version of the Wing Chun Wooden Dummy that hangs using two wooden slats through the body of the wooden dummy. Older versions of the Wing Chun wooden dummy were originally placed in the ground. The modern design was created by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yip_Man" title="Yip Man">Yip Man</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> to fit the needs of living in an apartment. The Wing Chun version of the <i>muk yan jong</i> has three arms and one leg, which represents an opponent's body in various positions and the lines of force the body can give out. The wooden slats on which the <i>muk yan jong</i> is mounted has a springiness that is similar to a human opponent's involuntary reaction and allows the user to practice absorbing energy into his/her stance. Due to this springiness, this type of dummy is considered an "alive" dummy. Therefore, the older version of the dummy without mounting is commonly called a "dead" dummy.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Jeet_Kune_Do">In Jeet Kune Do</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mu_ren_zhuang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In Jeet Kune Do">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bruce_Lee" title="Bruce Lee">Bruce Lee</a>, a student of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yip_Man" title="Yip Man">Yip Man</a>, had also made and modified a <a href="/wiki/Wing_Chun" title="Wing Chun">Wing Chun</a> wooden dummy for his martial arts philosophy of <a href="/wiki/Jeet_Kune_Do" title="Jeet Kune Do">Jeet Kune Do</a> which had a modified neck and a metal leg. Many of the principles of Wing Chun are central to Jeet Kune Do.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Choy_Lee_Fut">In Choy Lee Fut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mu_ren_zhuang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In Choy Lee Fut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ching_Jong">Ching Jong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mu_ren_zhuang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ching Jong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Ching Jong</b> (balanced dummy) has a different orientation to the arms more suited to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Choy_Lee_Fut" title="Choy Lee Fut">Choy Lee Fut</a> style's arm motions. In this version, the single top arm, protruding straight out from the front, moves up and down, anchored traditionally with a rope and heavy weight (the origin of the namesake) and anchored with a spring to the rear in modern times. Furthermore, the two middle arms now protrude outward in a "V", and also an additional lower arm that can be substitute with a traditional Wing Chun dummy's leg. Also, sandbags are mounted on the front and sides, which are struck for hand and finger conditioning, similar to <a href="/wiki/Makiwara" title="Makiwara">makiwara</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sui_Sau_Jong">Sui Sau Jong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mu_ren_zhuang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sui Sau Jong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <b>sui sau jong</b> (breaking hand dummy) features a swinging arm around the shoulder level, hanging sandbags, as well as a rotor-like arm at the top, located at head height. The arms are coordinated so that striking the swinging arm causes the rotation of the helicopter arm, and vice versa. The main aim of this dummy is to train quick reactions for counterattacks.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ma_Jong">Ma Jong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mu_ren_zhuang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ma Jong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Unlike other dummies, the Ma Jong (horse dummy) is a moveable dummy.</p>
<p>The overall shape of the dummy is a horse, with the main body of a heavy log carrying two frames, a tall one at front and the short one in the rear. Each frames is mounted with a horizontal crossbar that is perpendicular to the log, which has spring mechanism that is connected to the wheels. When pushed back, the horse dummy charges forward, forcing the students to control the dummy through a good stances. Furthermore, as the two horizontal arms are interlocking spinning arms, this also trains coordination and speed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Others">Others</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mu_ren_zhuang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Others">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Other dummys used in Choy Lee Fut includes the <i>qiang bao zhuang</i> (墻包樁) (a wall mounted sand bag), <i>sha bao zhuang</i> (沙包樁) (a sand bag apparatus, with one main heavy sand bag connected to two other sand bags on its side through a pulley system), and other mechanical dummies.</p>
