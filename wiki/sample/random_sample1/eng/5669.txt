<p><b>Mokpo Airport</b> was an airport in <a href="/wiki/Mokpo" title="Mokpo">Mokpo</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> (<a href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association_airport_code" title="International Air Transport Association airport code">IATA</a>: <b><s>MPK</s></b>, <a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization_airport_code" title="International Civil Aviation Organization airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>RKJM</b>). In 2006, 16,909 passengers utilized the airport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This airport was closed when the nearby <a href="/wiki/Muan_International_Airport" title="Muan International Airport">Muan International Airport</a> opened in November 2007.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mokpo Airport opened as a stopover between <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>-<a href="/wiki/Jeju_City" title="Jeju City">Jeju</a> in 1969 but closed in 1972. Twenty years later, Mokpo Airport opened again and several airplanes took off and landed. There were six daily flights to Seoul, two to Jeju, and one to Busan.</p>
<p>Problems associated with this airport include a narrow runway as well as frequent misty weather. Additionally, there were fewer customers after the opening of the [Seohaean Expressway] also known as the west coast highway]. These were among the reasons that the airport was closed in 2007.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>On 26 July 1993, an <a href="/wiki/Asiana_Airlines" title="Asiana Airlines">Asiana Airlines</a> <a href="/wiki/Asiana_Airlines_Flight_733" title="Asiana Airlines Flight 733">Flight 733</a> <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737_Classic" title="Boeing 737 Classic">Boeing 737-500</a> (HL7229) struck high ground in poor weather about four kilometres from the runway in <a href="/wiki/Mokpo" title="Mokpo">Mokpo</a> while it was making its third attempt at landing. 2 of the 6 crew members and 66 of the 110 passengers were killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
