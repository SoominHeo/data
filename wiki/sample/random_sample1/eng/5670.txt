<p><b>Mokpo</b> (<i>Mokpo-si</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_South_Korea" title="Administrative divisions of South Korea">city</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Jeolla_Province" title="South Jeolla Province">South Jeolla Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, located at the southwestern tip of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Peninsula" title="Korean Peninsula">Korean Peninsula</a>, close to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yudal_mountain" title="Yudal mountain">Yudal mountain</a>. Mokpo has frequent train service connecting in <a href="/wiki/Daejeon" title="Daejeon">Daejon</a> to <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, and is the terminus for a number of ferry routes serving islands in the adjacent <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Sea" title="Yellow Sea">Yellow Sea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dadohaehaesang_National_Park" title="Dadohaehaesang National Park">Dadohae National Maritime Park</a>.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Korea_under_Japanese_rule" title="Korea under Japanese rule">Japanese Occupation</a> (1910–1945), Mokpo served as a crucial port for both commercial ventures and public transportation, due to its location along the sea routes between the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_archipelago" title="Japanese archipelago">Japanese archipelago</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_mainland" title="Chinese mainland">Chinese mainland</a>. The large number of islands surrounding Mokpo have also served as a protective barrier, making the city less vulnerable to high tides and <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunamis</a>. In the occupation era, large residential areas were built to accommodate the <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> colonists, which are now the city's historic districts. The end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and Korea's independence in 1945 were responsible for the city slowly losing its position as a host to major government organizations and wartime industries. This led to a reduction in the size of Mokpo, which is currently a mid-sized city of the <a href="/wiki/Honam" title="Honam">Honam</a> region.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term Mokpo (木浦 in <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>) literally translates as "the harbour with a large portion of forests". This terminology initially appeared in the <i><a href="/wiki/Goryeosa" title="Goryeosa">Goryeosa</a></i> (<i>History of Goryeo</i>). Therefore, some historians argue that the city's name referred to trees within the region of the port of Mokpo. Another theory suggests that the "mok" in the city's name refers to "the throat," a metaphor for the importance of the city's location as an entrance to the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Sea" title="Yellow Sea">Yellow Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mahan_and_Three_Kingdoms_eras">Mahan and Three Kingdoms eras</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mahan and Three Kingdoms eras">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mokpo belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Mahan_confederacy" title="Mahan confederacy">Mahan confederacy</a> during the early era of the three Han confederacies during the <a href="/wiki/Samhan" title="Samhan">Samhan</a> period, and was called Mul'ahye-gun while under the control of the <a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a> kingdom. After <a href="/wiki/Silla" title="Silla">Silla</a> defeated Baekje, Mokpo's region was absorbed, and its name was changed to Myeonju. In 946, its name was once again changed to Mullyang-gun, and it became part of the Muan prefecture, in the Haeyang province. At the time, the prefecture of Muan referred to a much larger area, which incorporated not only Mokpo but also the area currently called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muan" title="Muan">Muan</a> and several islands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sinan,_Jeollanam-do" title="Sinan, Jeollanam-do">Sinan county</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> According to a <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a> documentary, coastal and offshore areas of Mokpo witnessed superior productivity of crops, and important shipping routes to <a href="/wiki/Naju" title="Naju">Naju</a> were established along the Yeongsan River.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Goryeo_and_Joseon_dynasties">Goryeo and Joseon dynasties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Goryeo and Joseon dynasties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mokpo1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2370" data-file-width="3040" height="172" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Mokpo1.jpg/220px-Mokpo1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Mokpo1.jpg/330px-Mokpo1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Mokpo1.jpg/440px-Mokpo1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

To commemorate his role in the region's history, a statue of Admiral <a href="/wiki/Yi_Sun-sin" title="Yi Sun-sin">Yi Sun-sin</a> now stands guard over Mokpo.</div>

<p>What is today known as Mokpo had its origins in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goryeo_dynasty" title="Goryeo dynasty">Goryeo period</a>. That dynasty's founder, <a href="/wiki/Taejo_of_Goryeo" title="Taejo of Goryeo">Wang Geon</a>, was serving as a general for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Later_Goguryeo" title="Later Goguryeo">later Goguryeo</a> (also known as <a href="/wiki/Taebong" title="Taebong">Taebong</a>), ruled by <a href="/wiki/Gung_Ye" title="Gung Ye">Gung Ye</a>. Taebong covered the central area of the Korean peninsula including current-day <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaeseong" title="Gaeseong">Gaeseong</a>, which was then called Song'ak.</p>
<p>As a naval power, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wang_Geon" title="Wang Geon">Wang Geon</a> was ordered to attack the kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Later_Baekje" title="Later Baekje">Later Baekje</a>, which, like later Goguryeo, was a kingdom which emerged after the disintegration of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unified_Silla" title="Unified Silla">Unified Silla</a>. The operation met fairly successful results. During this time, Wang Geon met his second queen, Janghwa, with whom he had a son, who eventually became <a href="/wiki/Hyejong_of_Goryeo" title="Hyejong of Goryeo">Hyejong of Goryeo</a>.</p>
<p>In 1439, 21 years after King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sejong" title="Sejong">Sejong</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a> came to power, the Mokpo garrison (jin) was established, which was designed to control twelve offshore islands. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_invasions_of_Korea_(1592%E2%80%931598)" title="Japanese invasions of Korea (1592–1598)">Japanese invasions of Korea (1592–1598)</a>, Admiral <a href="/wiki/Yi_Sun-sin" title="Yi Sun-sin">Yi Sun-sin</a> established a garrison of naval forces at Mokpo and the island of Goha, in order to secure a base for provisions and ship repairs, such as the <a href="/wiki/Turtle_ship" title="Turtle ship">turtle ships</a>, also known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geobukseon" title="Geobukseon">geobukseon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Panokseon" title="Panokseon">panokseon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_port">Open port</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Open port">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 1, 1897, Mokpo became an open port, fifteen years after the opening of the port of <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a>. For Japan, Mokpo held a geographical advantage being located midway between <a href="/wiki/Nagasaki" title="Nagasaki">Nagasaki</a>, Japan, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinese_mainland" title="Chinese mainland">Chinese mainland</a>. Japan also valued Mokpo for its proximity to the abundant crops of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeolla" title="Jeolla">Jeolla</a> provinces. After its opening, housing for Japanese settlers was rapidly developed in order to establish self-government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The Japanese settled predominantly in what is today the Yudal-dong neighborhood, close to the main port. The Japanese consulate remained there until the establishment of the Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan-Korea_Treaty_of_1905" title="Japan-Korea Treaty of 1905">protectorate over Korea</a>.</p>
<p>The Japanese settlers gradually bought farmland in the vicinity of Mokpo, which had previously been illegal. These agricultural fields were desirable because they were inexpensive and not heavily taxed. The resulting harvests from these fields made rice cheaper in Japan, although the price of rice in Jeolla province doubled as a result.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After 1905, the influx of Japanese settlers increased and they expanded into Geumhwa-dong, where many cherry trees were planted along the street. Japanese called this area "Sakuramachi", which means "the downtown of cherry blossom trees."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> With Korea's full annexation by Japan in 1910, the city was given a new name, Mokpo-bu, and saw the construction of the colonial institution, the <a href="/wiki/Oriental_Development_Company" title="Oriental Development Company">Oriental Development Company</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Under_Japanese_rule">Under Japanese rule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Under Japanese rule">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dd>See also: <a href="/wiki/Korea_under_Japanese_rule" title="Korea under Japanese rule">Korea under Japanese rule</a></dd>
</dl>
<p>Mokpo's status as an open port made it a viable option to use the nearby county of <a href="/wiki/Wando_County" title="Wando County">Wando</a> as a link to the region's many offshore islands. In 1914, the completion of the <a href="/wiki/Honam_Line" title="Honam Line">Honam railway line</a> connected the city to <a href="/wiki/Daejeon" title="Daejeon">Daejeon</a>. On April 1, 1914, the county of Mokpo-bu was divided into several administrative sections. Only the county's urban downtown area, Bunae-myeon, became what is today Mokpo City. Following Order Number 11 of the General Japanese Government of Korea, Muan county incorporated Jido, Palguem, and Docho, which were the other areas formerly comprising Mokpo-bu. During the Japanese colonial period, Mokpo's harbor was used to forcibly export the produce of the region.</p>
<p>In 1918, the colonists established ginning facilities for manufacturing large amounts of goods in both Mokpo and Iri (current <a href="/wiki/Iksan" title="Iksan">Iksan</a> in North Jeolla province).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1932, the city incorporated parts of Muan, becoming the 6th largest city in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_peninsula" title="Korean peninsula">Korean peninsula</a> with a population of 60,000. Indigenous products were cloth, rice, salt, and coastal foods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The harbor played an important role, supplying goods to Japan, including large quantities of cotton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_independence">After independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: After independence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1949, Mokpo Bu attained the title of Mokpo-si (<i>si</i> meaning "city"), after the SOC government altered the administrative region. In 1973 and 1987, large areas were added to Mokpo city, including one of the most famous tourist spots, Samhakdo (which means "three cranes laid above the sea"). Samhakdo was reclaimed by connecting a series of islands, a process of landfill operations which lasted from 1968 to 1973. The island is divided into three major sections, and over six years the city constructed five bridges to link those three sections. In 2000, the city initiated a plan to restore a naturalized island for tourism, which brought tremendous criticism from environmental groups. On March 1, 2007, Samhakdo was opened to the public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Other projects in Mokpo included a coastal filling process near the North Harbor and the building of a new port in Chungmu-dong.</p>
<p>In 1980, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwangju_Democratization_Movement" title="Gwangju Democratization Movement">Gwangju Democratization Movement</a> swept the area. In Mokpo, student movements were initiated by several people marching along the boulevard from <a href="/wiki/Mokpo_Station" title="Mokpo Station">Mokpo Station</a> to the second plaza, carrying cards displaying the word "Freedom." Mokpo Station served as a hub for many groups supporting the people of Gwangju.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 1, 1997, Mokpo celebrated its <a href="/wiki/Centennial" title="Centennial">centennial</a> as a port city, and announced the second opening of the port. Mokpo is the hometown of former President <a href="/wiki/Kim_Dae-jung" title="Kim Dae-jung">Kim Dae-jung</a>, who received the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a> in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mayor">Mayor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mayor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mokpo began its local government in 1994. In July 1995 it inaugurated its first mayor, Gwon I Dam, who had been the president of the Munhwa broadcasting corporation. The city's lack of supply in the past caused Gwon to focus on improving the city's water supplies. In 2000, the mayor officially provided aid for <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a> in the form of rice shipments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> However, Gwon came into conflict with the leaders of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_2005)" title="Democratic Party (South Korea, 2005)">Democratic Party</a> over fair election practices, causing him to leave the party. He filed a lawsuit against the Democratic Party, which the courts subsequently rejected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jeon Tae Hong, the next mayor, made some important contributions to Mokpo's government,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> but he died suddenly in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Following his death, Jeong Jong Deuk was elected mayor, promising protection of the tourism industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Once in office, Deuk responded to concerns about the depressed condition of the old downtown area by establishing the Office of Original City Replanning and adopted the slogan "City of Light."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Deuk was re-elected in 2006 and, during his second term, the city received several civil and tourism awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He faced criticism from the local press regarding Mokpo's ability to draw people into the city and changing the attitudes regarding the city's imbalanced economy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another dispute arose in 2009, when non-governmental organizations based in Mokpo sued the government to disclose information about budgetary spending, which had been controlled by a city committee chosen by the mayor. The courts sided with the organizations, saying that the budget plan should be reported and monitored.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Disputes_upon_merge">Disputes upon merge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Disputes upon merge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, civil groups promoted merging the three adjacent communities: Sinan, Muan and Mokpo. On September 25, 2009, the city council of Mokpo officially submitted a plan to merge into one central government,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> but the plan was voted down due to objections from the community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, Mokpo population had increased to 90,000 households, with an average of 2.7 people per household. The population density was the 10th highest in South Korea, and it was the second fastest-growing municipality (after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gyeonggi-do" title="Gyeonggi-do">Gyeonggi province</a>). In 2010, Mokpo's <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Korea" title="Bank of Korea">Bank of Korea</a> reported that the population of western South Jeolla province was declining due to several economic, social and educational issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Education_in_South_Korea" title="Education in South Korea">Education in South Korea</a></div>
<p>One of the oldest elementary schools, Bukgyo, was established in 1897 for the aristocracy during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a>, following Order No. 145 of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gojong_of_the_Korean_Empire" title="Gojong of the Korean Empire">Gojong of the Korean Empire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> However, after its annexation by Japan, education in Mokpo was directed towards Japanese students.</p>
<p>Mokpo Commercial high school opened in 1920 as the first secondary-level school. Its name was later changed to Jeonnam Jeil high school. Former president Kim Dae Jung graduated from Jeil.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Universities">Universities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Universities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mokpo_National_University" title="Mokpo National University">Mokpo National University</a> (MNU) became a nationally supported school in 1976, after 30 years of existence. The school has become regionally famous for its shipbuilding research and other culturally related fields of study.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> A 2007 study of national universities ranked MNU highly, equal with <a href="/wiki/Seoul_National_University" title="Seoul National University">Seoul National University</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeonnam_National_University" title="Jeonnam National University">Jeonnam National University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mokpo_National_Maritime_University" title="Mokpo National Maritime University">Mokpo National Maritime University</a> is a government-funded school specializing in maritime-related studies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mokpo_Catholic_University" title="Mokpo Catholic University">Mokpo Catholic University</a> was established in 1967 as Sungshin Nursing College, and later expanded its vocational training to include other fields.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The harbor began operations earlier than other port cities on the Korean peninsula, allowing the city to experience growth, whereas growth in the country since its independence has concentrated on the <a href="/wiki/Yeongnam" title="Yeongnam">Yeongnam</a> area, where trade with Japan and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> are viable due to the access of coastal routes. The addition of the <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_China" title="Economy of China">Chinese economy</a> into the local trade prospects increases the city's potential as a trade center via the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Sea" title="Yellow Sea">Yellow Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commerce">Commerce</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Commerce">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Until late 1980s, commerce had been concentrated around the original downtown near <a href="/wiki/Mokpo_Station" title="Mokpo Station">Mokpo Station</a>. In the late 1990s, large residential areas were built in Yeonsan dong and Hadang, resulting in a significant outflow of the population of the city. This resulted in the city council adopting a bylaw to support the old downtown area,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> however, the outward flow has only worsened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Economic power has been notably imbalanced by the opening of <a href="/wiki/Lotte_Mart" title="Lotte Mart">Lotte Mart</a> in 2001 and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/E-Mart" title="E-Mart">E-Mart</a> in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_plus" title="Home plus">Home plus</a> is the solitary large-scale market in Mokpo, located in old downtown near the Munhwa broadcasting corporation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>The one exception to the declining economy of the city are the fish markets of Dongmyeong and North Harbor which have been a magnet for tourists and local people looking for <a href="/wiki/Octopus" title="Octopus">octopus</a> or other indigenous products. The economic bureau of the city has spent ₩300 million on promoting the market since early 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Companies">Companies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Companies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Companies with operations in Mokpo include the <a href="/wiki/Bohae" title="Bohae">Bohae</a> brewing company and Haengnam <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chinaware" title="Chinaware">chinaware</a>. Bohae brewing company produces a brand of <a href="/wiki/Soju" title="Soju">soju</a> from the Honam area, of which the best selling product is maple soju (<span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">잎새주</span>), the first company to utilizing maple in the brewing of soju. Bohae also entered into a joint development with <a href="/wiki/Asahi_Breweries" title="Asahi Breweries">Asahi Breweries</a> in Japan to expand its soju production operations. In 2008, Bohae became the first Korean brewing company to open up wine sales in the United States, with the exportation of its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rugby_Ball" title="Rugby Ball">Rugby Ball</a> wine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>Another major company is Haengnam chinaware, established in May 1942. Its headquarters is in Mokpo, with two other branches, one in Seoul for design and public relations, and another in <a href="/wiki/Yeoju" title="Yeoju">Yeoju</a> for production. In 1953, the company was the first in Korea to develop coffee cup sets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> In 1963, they began trade in Hong Kong, with exports to China growing to more than 2 million dollars in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> In 2011, its president signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the Ministry of Environment of South Korea to develop a less pollutant-emitting process. The project also includes a publicity campaign to raise public awareness on environmental issues and their effect on rare species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Farming_and_fishing">Farming and fishing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Farming and fishing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mokpo is made up of sparsely populated farmland, with 1,326 households under a half hectare. The area produces high quantities of rice, barley, and beans, with greens and fruits such as tomatoes, cucumbers, and oriental melons increasing in popularity since 2001.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Ships from the harbor often travel to <a href="/wiki/Heuksando" title="Heuksando">Heuksando</a> to fish for skate and hairtail. Mokpo's yield of marine products reached 26,862 tons in 2006, consisting chiefly of fish, with of shellfish, mollusks, and seaweeds also contributing to the total. Mokpo is known for its harvest of small octopus, although this has declined in recent years. The number of fishermen had dropped to fewer than 3,000, but has been rising since 2007.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industries">Industries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Industries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The regional economy relies heavily on the Daebul industrial complex and <a href="/wiki/Hyundai_Samho_Heavy_Industries" title="Hyundai Samho Heavy Industries">Hyundai Samho Heavy Industries</a> located near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yeongam" title="Yeongam">Yeongam</a>. The imports of merchandise support the local economy of Mokpo as they pass through its harbor. The Halla group was the initial owners of Heavy Industries, until they were taken over by Hyundai, which changed the company name to Hyundai Samho Heavy Industries, with Samho meaning three lakes. By 2007, corporate profits amounted to more than 460 million, an increase of about 25% over those from 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> South Jeolla province succeeded in attracting a series of investors for renewable energy companies like solar panels. Innovation Silicon Ltd, invested 10bn <a href="/wiki/Korean_won" title="Korean won">won</a> in developing a silicon producing complex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mokpo sits at the southern end of the Muan peninsula, although the city lies within Sinan county, which mostly consists of 1004 islands. Its neighbor, Muan county, extends northeastward, separated from Mokpo by the Yeongsan river. Yudal mountain (228m) sits at the center of old downtown, and is a source of pride within the city. Its nickname is Gaegol (in <a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">hangul</a>:개골), meaning weird-shaped rocks and peaks. The side of the mountain caused roads around the downtown area to have a hilly aspect. Nojeokbong is a historic site where General Yi Soon Shin made use of its shape during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_invasions_of_Korea_(1592%E2%80%931598)" title="Japanese invasions of Korea (1592–1598)">Japanese invasion</a>. The city has small mountains nearby: Mt. Ibam (121m) to the east, and Yangeul (156m) and Daebak (156m) to the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.EB.AA.A9.ED.8F.AC.EC.A7.80.EB.A6.AC_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-.EB.AA.A9.ED.8F.AC.EC.A7.80.EB.A6.AC-54">[54]</a></sup> The mouth of the Yeongsan River flows along the southern side of the city, containing 13 islands. The existence of a natural <a href="/wiki/Seawall" title="Seawall">seawall</a> created a natural safe harbor.</p>
<p>Most of the area of Mokpo consists of <a href="/wiki/Land_reclamation" title="Land reclamation">land reclamation</a>, including Samhakdo, North harbor (in <a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 북항), and the new city of Hadang. According to the research by Mokpo National University, <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributaries</a> of the Yeongsan river crossed the central area of old downtown. This can be seen in the historic names of the Mokone Dong area: Namgyo-dong (southern side of bridge) and Bukgyo-dong (Northern side of bridge). Both names contained "gyo" (in Hangul:교, in <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>:橋), meaning the bridge, indicating a bridge used to link the areas. Tideland reclamation projects removed large amounts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mud_flat" title="Mud flat">mud flats</a>, including Daebandong, Baekryundong and the coastal shore around Gatbawi. Additionally, the municipal boundaries include 7 <a href="/wiki/Desert_island" title="Desert island">desert islands</a>.</p>
<p>The wall at the end of the Yeongsan river prevented damage from flooding during harsh typhoons. Meanwhile, the water quality of the river has worsened to the point where the ministry in charge of environmental protection began studying the impacts made by man-made structures. In 2010, the report identified the environmental issue in that a widened seawall should be constructed, and dirty water pumped out of the riverbeds and into the harbor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>

<p>Mokpo's weather station was built along with five other sites in Korea, and began observing weather conditions on March 25, 1904.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Mokpo's climate features a short spring and autumn, and it is cold and dry in the winter and warm and humid with seasonal winds in the summer. There is also significant rain in the summer, averaging 1,163 millimetres (45.8 in). Mokpo is usually windy and foggy in the spring and autumn due to its location near the ocean. Mokpo's long period (annual average of 223 days) without frost creates suitable conditions for rice farming. However, its seacoast location and the influence of the continental climate on the city often cause a great disparity between the rise and fall of daily and yearly temperatures. Mokpo, along with the rest of Sinan County, has the highest level of sunlight in Korea, enabling more efficient use of solar energy plants and related energy sources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> The frequency of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yellow_dust" title="Yellow dust">yellow dust</a> is quite similar to that of other cities like <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Incheon</a>, occurring about 10–11 days per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>The climate of Mokpo is a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Cfa</i>). Unexpected blizzards can sweep the region with strong gusts during winter, and the city experiences more than 30 days of snow, and 60 days below 0 °C (32 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Mokpo sometimes falls within the range of passing typhoons, and may suffer peripheral damage, including heavy precipitation during the summer and early autumn. In 2004, <a href="/wiki/2004_Pacific_typhoon_season#Typhoon_Mindulle_.28Igme.29" title="2004 Pacific typhoon season">Typhoon Mindulle</a> gave Mokpo a record rainfall of 64 millimetres (2.5 in) per hour.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Railroad">Railroad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Railroad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Korail_Honam_Line_Mokpo_Station.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="800" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Korail_Honam_Line_Mokpo_Station.jpg/220px-Korail_Honam_Line_Mokpo_Station.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Korail_Honam_Line_Mokpo_Station.jpg/330px-Korail_Honam_Line_Mokpo_Station.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Korail_Honam_Line_Mokpo_Station.jpg/440px-Korail_Honam_Line_Mokpo_Station.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Mokpo Station opened May 15, 1913, it is the final destination of Honam line and accommodates over 1.2 million annually, as well as cargo shipments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>The station is located near Yudal mountain, in the southwestern part of the city. The railroad ends inside the station, although a freight-only line continued on to Samhakdo, carrying coal for the industrial factories. Because of potential danger, the city removed the rail extension in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 1, 2004, <a href="/wiki/Korea_Train_Express" title="Korea Train Express">Korea Train Express</a> advertised a three-hour travel time from Seoul to Mokpo, however, the actual time was longer due to the number of stops along the Honam Line. Three-hour travel is only possible when limiting the train to four stops: <a href="/wiki/Seodaejeon_Station" title="Seodaejeon Station">Seodaejeon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iksan_Station" title="Iksan Station">Iksan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwangju%C2%B7Songjeong_Station" title="Gwangju·Songjeong Station">Gwangjusongjeong</a>, and finally Mokpo. However, that service has not been available since 2008. Currently, there are 10 trains from Yongsan station to Mokpo, with a travel time of 195 minutes. The railroad has plans to improve the high-speed Honam line, reducing travel time to under 100 minutes. There are also plans to develop a high-speed rail route between Honam and Osong, a distance of 230.9 km<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> In July 2010, there are two and six scheduled trains from Mokpo to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saemaul-ho" title="Saemaul-ho">Saemaul-ho</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mugunghwa-ho" title="Mugunghwa-ho">Mugunghwa-ho</a>, respectively. For <a href="/wiki/Gwangju_Station" title="Gwangju Station">Gwangju Station</a>, Mugunghwa Ho is working on having train service twice in a day.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inland_road">Inland road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Inland road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dd><b>See also</b> :<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roads_and_expressways_in_South_Korea" title="Roads and expressways in South Korea">Roads and expressways in South Korea</a></dd>
</dl>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Seohaean_Expressway" title="Seohaean Expressway">Seohaean Expressway</a> connects Mokpo to Seoul via a 4-hour car ride. The Muan-Gwangju expressway was opened in 2008 to ease access to the Muan international airport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> In 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Namhae_Expressway" title="Namhae Expressway">Mokpo to Gwangyang Highway</a> opened, connecting the eastern and western ends of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Jeolla" title="South Jeolla">South Jeolla</a>, as well as continuing on to <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Korea_National_Route_1" title="South Korea National Route 1">National Route 1</a> was planned to connect Mokpo to Sineuju in <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>, however, the route was cut off at <a href="/wiki/Imjingak" title="Imjingak">Imjingak</a> near the truce village of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panmunjeom" title="Panmunjeom">Panmunjeom</a>. It was not until 2000 when Route 1 was extended to North Korea's second largest city, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaesung" title="Gaesung">Gaesung</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two companies provide most of the bus service in the local area. Since 2005, the companies and the city council agreed to expand the number of buses fueled by natural gas (NG), following a 3-year negotiation. The city agreed to assist the companies with a 21,000 US dollar per bus subsidy for each bus equipped with the NG equipment. As of 2006, 32 buses had been retrofitted with the natural gas equipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> The growing suburbs around the city created a demand for additional lines, but there is conflict between the needs of the city and the profit margin of those new lines.</p>
<p>Riders may transfer between buses, free of charge, beginning on March 1, 2007. The lines are classified as inner circle, outer line and artery, which follows the commuting system of <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Buses" title="Seoul Buses">Seoul Buses</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> Buses with a low-floor level were first deployed in South Jeolla province in 2007, although they still remain in limited service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2008, a semi-public bus system has been explored, with the intent of expanding the role of the city government in determining bus routes. The core goal is to share responsibility for operations as well as control over spending and income. However, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nongovernmental_organization" title="Nongovernmental organization">NGOs</a> have withheld support for the idea, resulting in the policy having not been implemented.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Airport">Airport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Airport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Muan_International_Airport" title="Muan International Airport">Muan International Airport</a> is the airport serving Mokpo. Previously, <a href="/wiki/Mokpo_Airport" title="Mokpo Airport">Mokpo Airport</a> operated as the region's airport. The crash of <a href="/wiki/Asiana_Airlines" title="Asiana Airlines">Asiana Airlines</a> <a href="/wiki/Asiana_Airlines_Flight_733" title="Asiana Airlines Flight 733">Flight 733</a> in 1993 increased concerns about the airport, resulting in extending the runway 50 meters. However, the site still had fundamental drawbacks due to common foggy weather and the lack of related facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Korean_Air" title="Korean Air">Korean Air</a> abolished the Gimpo-Mokpo route and <a href="/wiki/Asiana_Airlines" title="Asiana Airlines">Asiana Airlines</a> started to slash services in 2004 with the removal of the Mokpo-Jeju route. The air companies announced deficits due to the high-speed railroad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup></p>
<p>The alternative to Mokpo Airport was Muan International Airport, whose construction was delayed for many years, leading to disappointment among the local people. Eventually, however, the airport was constructed, and Mokpo Airport closed its doors in November 8, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sea">Sea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Sea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Mokpo has three harbors: one each for ferry service, trading and fishing. The main harbor is used to ferry customers and cargoes around nearby islands, while North harbor conducts ferry service to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nonghyup" title="Nonghyup">Nonghyup</a>. Additionally, the newly built harbor is used for container shipping to China.</p>
<p>Over 80 sea-going lines have been available, notably for Heuksando, <a href="/wiki/Hongdo" title="Hongdo">Hongdo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeju-do" title="Jeju-do">Jeju</a>, as well as ferries linking Sinan county. In 2002, the first cruise-line to <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> was opened for operation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> however, bankruptcy concerns forced the discontinuance of the cruise line after only five months. In July 2004, another attempt to start a cruise route to Shanghai, with a newly designed, smaller boat, failed due to Chinese objections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Mokpo coastal terminal was originally built during the 1980s, with the government beginning an update in 2003. The 30bn won, 4-story terminal opened in February 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> Mokpo coastal terminal is currently configured to divide coastal from international travel.</p>
<p>The newly built harbor in Mokpo has seen a growing number of cargoes since it opened in 2004. The harbor set a record for civil investment in a seaport in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> Currently, sea routes have been established for China, Japan, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> with municipal support.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> In January 2011, Samsung electronics decided to choose Mokpo as their chief exporting harbor for merchandise from its factory in Gwangju.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A flower festival is held at Yudal mountain every April and May. The festival of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Largehead_Hairtail" title="Largehead Hairtail">Largehead Hairtail</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>:목포사랑은빛갈치축제) takes place around Yeongsan Lake every October. The festival is extremely popular among fishing fans and family tourists. A fishing contest is held, wherein great numbers of people take part, which is followed by a fireworks display.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Nanyeong Singing Memorial celebrates the famous singer, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Nan-young" title="Lee Nan-young">Lee Nan-young</a>, whose song, "The Tear of Mokpo" gained wide popularity. The memorial was started in 1968 by the Honam Maeil newspaper but was stopped, but was restarted by MBC Mokpo and is held every autumn.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="City_of_Light">City of Light</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: City of Light">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city has a plan for promoting tourism in the old downtown, and South Jeolla province agreed to offer support for the city's plan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> This city's population decline has resulted in the closure of many shops, as well as a drastic reduction in the city's school system. For example, student enrollment at Sanjeong Elementary School has dropped to below 300 from a high of more than 3,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> The project, entitled, "A City of Light", called for establishing a light show along the street which runs next to the railway station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> However, environmentalists have lodged objections, arguing that the plan will have an irrevocable impact, harming the ecological balance of the mountain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the new downtown area, there is now a fountain, which times its waters and light show to the beat of the song. This was to be expanded, but has confronted opposition from several civil groups since 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Islands_offshore">Islands offshore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Islands offshore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Outside of the harbor, thousands of islands dot the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Sea" title="Yellow Sea">Yellow Sea</a>, several of which are actually part of Mokpo. The government has promoted a program of constructing infrastructure on each island. With the slogan of "Lovely island, Uai-dal", <a href="/wiki/Hanok" title="Hanok">Hanok</a> residences on these islands, each with a saltwater pool, have served as accommodations for tourists during the summer.</p>
<p>One of the most famous islands, Goha-do, was used as a base for naval forces in the late 16th century when Japan invaded Joseon. The Japanese navy created artificial caves on this island to conceal armaments from the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allies</a> during the Second World War. Two air-raid shelters have been found. The Mokpo Bridge was publicly opened in June, 2012.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Museums">Museums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Museums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many museums in Mokpo are located near Gatbawi street, due to the city concentrating its investment to exhibition space in this area. The National Maritime museum is one of the oldest museums dedicated to the Korean naval and shipping traditions. The museum changed its name in 2009 to the Research Institute for Maritime History, due to its broadened function.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup> The Institute conducts surveys and research related to ancient ships, trade routes and relics, and is the only research institute for maritime relics in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[92]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Mokpo Natural History Museum has a large collection of fossils from all over the world. It originally began as a single building, but has expanded into a second building, the Cultural Heritage Hall, which specializes in local relics dating as far back as prehistoric time. The museum has an extensive collection of samples and data regarding everything from <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaur</a> fossils to insects and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cetacean" title="Cetacean">cetaceans</a> found in the Yellow Sea. One of the most significant displays is a nest of dinosaur eggs, found on Aphae island, which was restored and opened to the public in 2010. The eggs reach lengths greater than 40 cm, and have been dated to approximately 80 million years ago, during the <a href="/wiki/Mesozoic" title="Mesozoic">Mesozoic</a> period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[93]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Korea_Forest_Service" title="Korea Forest Service">Korea Forest Service</a> chose the museum to record its samples of insects in a national database for biological resources in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[94]</a></sup></p>
<p>Namnong Memorial Hall commemorates the famed <a href="/wiki/Korean_calligraphy" title="Korean calligraphy">calligrapher</a>, Heo Gun. The Hall is situated within a <a href="/wiki/Korean_garden" title="Korean garden">Korean traditional garden</a> and features exhibitions on different series of his works.</p>
<p>The <a class="external text" href="http://doja.mokpo.go.kr/eng/" rel="nofollow">Mokpo Ceramic Museum</a> opened in 2008, was funded by the government to establish ceramics and china exhibits from notable manufacturers. Meanwhile, the <a class="external text" href="http://www.mpmunhak.or.kr" rel="nofollow">Literature hall of Mokpo</a> (in Hangul:목포문학관) is not far from the ceramic museum, and hosts literature classes for the general public, featuring specific writers or novels, in order to promote literature among the local population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[95]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Cuisine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mokpo is famous for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hongeo" title="Hongeo">hongeo</a> (in Hangul : 홍어), a strong smelling fermented cuisine featuring the <a href="/wiki/Skate_(fish)" title="Skate (fish)">skate</a>, a kind of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ray_fish" title="Ray fish">ray fish</a>. Other delicacies of Mokpo are: kimchi and <a href="/wiki/Pyeonyuk" title="Pyeonyuk">pyeonyuk</a> (in Hangul: 홍어삼합); <a href="/wiki/Yeonpotang" title="Yeonpotang">yeonpotang</a>, or octopus with slender legs; <a href="/wiki/Hoe_(dish)" title="Hoe (dish)">hoe</a>; and seasoned <a href="/wiki/Portunus_trituberculatus" title="Portunus trituberculatus">portunus trituberculatus</a>. All of which are seafood dishes. The city started a public market for vendors selling their own delicacies, located downtown. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[96]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities_for_sports_and_cultural_entertainment">Facilities for sports and cultural entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: Facilities for sports and cultural entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yudal Stadium is located next to the 100 Year Memorial Avenue. The stadium consists of ballpark and an eight lane track. Behind the stadium, local groups are able to use public tennis courts. The ground floor of the stadium houses committees of diverse sports.</p>
<p>The city has attracted many athletes to train in the area during the winter, due to sunny and warm weather, and numerous accommodations. The city has been very successful in reaching agreements with 127 teams, totaling almost 6,000 athletes, who trained during the winter in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup> Since 2005, the focus has been on track and field sports in order to boost the number of visiting athletes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[98]</a></sup></p>
<p>Recreational parks are located sparsely around Yudal mountain, while the city is in the process of constructing an additional park in the Samhakdo area.</p>
<p>The Mokpo Cultural Complex is located along the shore near <a href="/wiki/Gatbawi_(Mokpo)" title="Gatbawi (Mokpo)">Gatbawi</a>, consisting of two buildings, one for performance and the other for exhibition. However, the complex is limited by its seating capacity: a maximum of 700 people for the two-story building.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99">[99]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Mokpo Civil Culture &amp; Sports Center was built in 2003 to meet the needs of the increasing population in the downtown area. However, the operating fees have proven a burden to the province. It consists of three halls, with a capacity of more than 1,200 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are two libraries in Mokpo. The Civil Library of Mokpo opened in 1974 at the old consulate of Japan. In 1989, it moved closer to Yudal stadium, and consists of 4 stories, with 1,636 seats. The library is home to over 360,000 books, as well as having internet-access research rooms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-102"><a href="#cite_note-102">[102]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-103"><a href="#cite_note-103">[103]</a></sup> The Public Library of Mokpo is located near North Harbor. It began as part of the Mokpo Educational Institute, but has since gained independence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-104"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Branch offices of KBS and <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a> exist in Mokpo. KBS Mokpo was established in 1942, at a location close to the Yeon dong area, prior to its move next to the current city hall in Yongdang. MBC Mokpo was initially planned as a radio station when it opened in 1967, using a frequency of 1386 kHz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-105"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Far_East_Broadcasting_Company" title="Far East Broadcasting Company">FEBC</a> Mokpo opened an office in 1999, for Christian missions around the <a href="/wiki/Honam" title="Honam">Honam</a> area. For <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cable_Television" title="Cable Television">cable broadcasting</a>, Honam Hanguk cable has its office in Hadang.</p>
<p>South of Mokpo at 34.679364 N 126.445148 E, there is a VLF-transmitter with two very tall masts ( height unknown).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_care">Health care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=38" title="Edit section: Health care">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2008, the number of doctors per capita was 11.41 per thousand, increasing from 10.45 of the prior year. The council of doctors in Mokpo city has stated that the ongoing economic improvement, as well as the growing populations, will cause the need for the construction of middle-sized or larger hospitals and clinics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106"><a href="#cite_note-106">[106]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mokpo National hospital was established in 1962, specifically for the treatment of <a href="/wiki/Tuberculosis" title="Tuberculosis">tuberculosis</a>. Originally a city hospital, the state took over operations in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-107"><a href="#cite_note-107">[107]</a></sup></p>
<p>The ministry of Health chose Mokpo and Incheon as a sites for operating medical helicopters. Hankook hospital was officially designated the location for these emergency services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-108"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mokpo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=44" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Two Jeolla provinces which Mokpo pertains to have been the critical base of the Democratic Party of South Korea. Before former president Kim took the presidency, he caught the strongest power in the reason as a root of his political supporting base. In this context, Mokpo and near area has been called the garden of Democrats.</li>
<li>Nonghyup ferries refer to the regular ferry operated by Nonghyup which is kind of banking and cooperating organ for farmers. The ferry connects each branch in several islands in Sinan and Mokpo.</li>
</ul>
