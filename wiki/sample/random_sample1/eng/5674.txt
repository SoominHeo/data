<p>The <b>Province of Monza and Brianza</b>, (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <i>Provincia di Monza e della Brianza</i>), is an <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Italy" title="Provinces of Italy">administrative province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a> <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_Italy" title="Regions of Italy">region</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Province_of_Monza_and_Brianza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was officially created by splitting the north-eastern part from the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Milan" title="Province of Milan">province of Milan</a> on 12 May 12 2004, and became executive after the provincial elections of 6 and 7 June 2009. It has an area of 405 square kilometres (156 sq mi), that is one of the smallest provincial territories of Italy and a population of about 0.9 million, with a population density of more than 2,000 people per square kilometre, given by its heavily urbanized territory that is part of the urban area of Milan. The capital and most important commune is <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a> (pop. 121,441 as of 30/11/09), only 15 kilometres (9 mi) from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milano" title="Milano">Milano</a>. Other important centres are (2001 population figures): <a href="/wiki/Seregno" title="Seregno">Seregno</a> (39,206), <a href="/wiki/Desio" title="Desio">Desio</a> (35,069), <a href="/wiki/Limbiate" title="Limbiate">Limbiate</a> (31,551), <a href="/wiki/Lissone" title="Lissone">Lissone</a> (34,450), <a href="/wiki/Vimercate" title="Vimercate">Vimercate</a> (25,536), <a href="/wiki/Cesano_Maderno" title="Cesano Maderno">Cesano Maderno</a> (33,094) and <a href="/wiki/Brugherio" title="Brugherio">Brugherio</a> (31,470). It borders the provinces of <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Lecco" title="Province of Lecco">Lecco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Como" title="Province of Como">Como</a> to the north, the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Varese" title="Province of Varese">province of Varese</a> to the west, the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Bergamo" title="Province of Bergamo">province of Bergamo</a> to the east and the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Milan" title="Province of Milan">province of Milan</a> to the south-east.</p>
