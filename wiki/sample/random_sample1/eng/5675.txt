<p><i><b>Monte Carlo</b></i> is a 2011 Hungarian-American <a href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy_film" title="Romantic comedy film">romantic comedy film</a> based on <i><a href="/wiki/Jules_Bass#Writings" title="Jules Bass">Headhunters</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bass" title="Jules Bass">Jules Bass</a>. It was directed by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Bezucha" title="Thomas Bezucha">Thomas Bezucha</a>. <a href="/wiki/Denise_Di_Novi" title="Denise Di Novi">Denise Di Novi</a>, Alison Greenspan, <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Kidman" title="Nicole Kidman">Nicole Kidman</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Arnon_Milchan" title="Arnon Milchan">Arnon Milchan</a> produced the film for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_2000_Pictures" title="Fox 2000 Pictures">Fox 2000 Pictures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regency_Enterprises" title="Regency Enterprises">Regency Enterprises</a>. It began production in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harghita" title="Harghita">Harghita, Romania</a> on May 5, 2010. <i>Monte Carlo</i> stars <a href="/wiki/Selena_Gomez" title="Selena Gomez">Selena Gomez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leighton_Meester" title="Leighton Meester">Leighton Meester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Katie_Cassidy" title="Katie Cassidy">Katie Cassidy</a> as three friends posing as wealthy socialites in <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo, Monaco</a>. The film was released on July 1, 2011. It features the song "<a href="/wiki/Who_Says_(Selena_Gomez_%26_the_Scene_song)" title="Who Says (Selena Gomez &amp; the Scene song)">Who Says</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Selena_Gomez_%26_the_Scene" title="Selena Gomez &amp; the Scene">Selena Gomez &amp; the Scene</a> and numerous songs by British singer <a href="/wiki/Mika_(singer)" title="Mika (singer)">Mika</a>. <i>Monte Carlo</i> received mixed reviews from critics and it earned $39,667,665 on a $20 million budget. Fox Home Entertainment released <i>Monte Carlo</i> on DVD and Blu-ray Disc on October 18, 2011.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monte_Carlo_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grace Bennett (<a href="/wiki/Selena_Gomez" title="Selena Gomez">Selena Gomez</a>) is a Texas high-school student who works as a waitress with her best friend Emma Perkins (<a href="/wiki/Katie_Cassidy" title="Katie Cassidy">Katie Cassidy</a>) to earn money for a trip to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> after graduation. Grace's stepfather pays for her stepsister Meg Kelly (<a href="/wiki/Leighton_Meester" title="Leighton Meester">Leighton Meester</a>) to come with them on the trip. Emma goes to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> despite her boyfriend Owen's proposal of marriage. After being left behind by their tour guide, the three girls seek refuge from the <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> rain in a posh hotel. There, the hotel staff and paparazzi mistake Grace for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Celebutante" title="Celebutante">celebutante</a> British heiress Cordelia Winthrop-Scott, Grace's double, who leaves rather than stay to attend an auction for a Romanian charity for which she is to donate an expensive <a href="/wiki/Bulgari" title="Bulgari">Bulgari</a> necklace. The Americans spend the night in Cordelia's suite, and the next day fly to <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a> with Cordelia's luggage.</p>
<p>At <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a> the girls meet Theo Marchand, the son of the philanthropist hosting Cordelia. Theo dislikes Cordelia's spoiled nature but escorts them to a ball, where Grace successfully fools Cordelia's aunt Alicia and Emma dances with a prince. Meg reunites with Riley, an Australian backpacker she briefly met in Paris. They find they have things in common, and spend time together before he leaves for <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. When Grace has to take part in a game of polo, Alicia discovers the impersonation because of Grace's different riding style. Alicia believes her niece has hired a lookalike to take her place while she parties, but in order not to endanger the charity auction she agrees to keep silent. Theo is attracted to "Cordelia's" frank personality, while Emma's prince invites her to a party on a yacht. Emma dresses for the party in Cordelia's necklace but meets Meg on the way, and Meg takes it for safekeeping, but later forgets it in Riley's backpack. Emma is disillusioned at the party by the prince's arrogance toward the waitresses.</p>
<p>Owen arrives in <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a> in search of Emma. So does Cordelia, and she sees the newspaper account of Grace's appearance at the ball. She finds that the necklace is missing and calls the police. The girls have gone in search of Riley but he shows up at the hotel with the necklace; they find Cordelia in the room. When Cordelia threatens to call and withdraw the necklace from the auction, the girls panic and tackle her to the couch. When people come to the door, they muffle her screams and Grace covers for them. They then tie her to a chair and gag her by stuffing an apple in her mouth so Grace can take her place at the auction. Cordelia escapes, and reveals Grace's fraud at the auction. She demands that Grace be arrested, but after Grace's sincere public confession Alicia bids the unexpectedly large amount of €6 million for the necklace to save her. The film ends with Meg joining Riley on his travel around the world; Owen and Emma moving into their own home in Texas; and Theo Marchand and Grace reuniting at the Romanian school where they are volunteering.</p>

<p><i>Monte Carlo</i> is loosely based on the novel <i><a href="/wiki/Jules_Bass#Writings" title="Jules Bass">Headhunters</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bass" title="Jules Bass">Jules Bass</a>. The novel tells the story of four young Texas women who pretend to be wealthy heiresses while searching for rich potential husbands in Monte Carlo. There, they meet four <a href="/wiki/Gigolo" title="Gigolo">gigolos</a> posing as wealthy playboys. Fox bought the film rights to the novel in 1999, three years prior to the novel's publication.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VarietyKidmanCasted_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-VarietyKidmanCasted-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2005, Hollywood trade magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> announced that siblings Jez and John Henry Butterworth would be writing the script. It also reported that actress <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Kidman" title="Nicole Kidman">Nicole Kidman</a> had signed on to play the lead as well as produce the film with <a href="/wiki/Rick_Schwartz" title="Rick Schwartz">Rick Schwartz</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VarietyKidmanCasted_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-VarietyKidmanCasted-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Butterworths were later fired and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tom_Bezucha" title="Tom Bezucha">Tom Bezucha</a> was hired to direct and co-write <i>Monte Carlo</i>. Bezucha and <a href="/wiki/Maria_Maggenti" title="Maria Maggenti">Maria Maggenti</a> turned in a draft of the screenplay by July 2007; it starred Kidman as "one of three Midwestern schoolteachers who decide to ditch a disappointing no-frills holiday in Paris and pose as wealthy women vacationing in Monaco".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Location_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Location-4">[4]</a></sup> However, in 2010, executives had the film rewritten again after deciding that the film should be made more youthful. The updated script was co-written by Bezucha and April Blair, and changed the three school teachers to two college students and a recent high-school graduate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VarietyProduction_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-VarietyProduction-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>Monte Carlo</i> was shot in Budapest, Hungary; Dunakeszi, Hungary; <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris, France</a>; <a href="/wiki/Harghita_County" title="Harghita County">Harghita, Romania</a>; and Monte Carlo, Monaco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Location_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Location-4">[4]</a></sup> It began filming in Harghita on May 5, 2010, and wrapped on July 7, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SelBlog1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-SelBlog1-6">[6]</a></sup> It is the first film to use the film studio, Raleigh Studios Budapest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2010, it was announced that Selena Gomez had been cast as one of the film's leads following the script's rewrite.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GomezCast_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GomezCast-8">[8]</a></sup> For the role, Gomez spent several weeks learning to play <a href="/wiki/Polo" title="Polo">polo</a>, and practicing how to fake an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_accent#England" title="English accent">English accent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SelBlog1_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-SelBlog1-6">[6]</a></sup> Leighton Meester also negotiated a deal to one of the leads that month, and Katie Cassidy was cast as Emma in April.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CassidyCast_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-CassidyCast-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a> actor Pierre Boulanger made his English-speaking feature debut in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pierre_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pierre-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monte_Carlo_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monte_Carlo_(2011_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Monte Carlo</i> received mixed reviews from critics. <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gives the film a score of 52% based on 91 reviews. The website's consensus states "Although it has its charming moments, <i>Monte Carlo</i> is mostly silly, predictable stuff that never pushes beyond the boundaries of formula."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> the film received a score of 53/100 based on reviews from 23 critics indicating "mixed or average reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ben Sachs of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Reader" title="Chicago Reader">Chicago Reader</a></i> claimed that "the movie hits a surprising range of emotional grace notes, including several moments of genuine regret, and concludes with an understated moral lesson about the value of self-respect over social status."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Sandie Chen of <a href="/wiki/Common_Sense_Media" title="Common Sense Media">Common Sense Media</a> said the film was "silly, but sweet". <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> It was released in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> on August 24, 2011.</p>
