<p><b>Montefredane</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Avellino" title="Province of Avellino">province of Avellino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">Campania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montefredane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area where Montefredane stands today was inhabited in ancient times by the <a href="/wiki/Samnites" title="Samnites">Samnites</a>. Later, Roman populations settled near the present days village of <a href="/wiki/Arcella" title="Arcella">Arcella</a>.<br/>
The main buildings of Montefredane date back to the sixth century AD, when the inhabitants of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abellinum" title="Abellinum">Abellinum</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Atripalda" title="Atripalda">Atripalda</a>) fled here after the destruction of their village.<br/>
In the Middle Ages, Montefredane was mentioned in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Montefredane_Catalogus_Baronum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Montefredane Catalogus Baronum (page does not exist)">Montefredane Catalogus Baronum</a> (1150–68) as part of the county of <a href="/wiki/Avellino" title="Avellino">Avellino</a>. In the following centuries, through ups and downs related to the names of several noble families, such as De Tufo, Capece, Brancaccio and <a href="/wiki/Orsini" title="Orsini">Orsini</a>, Montefredane gained increasing importance, until the plague of 1656 greatly reduced its development. Between 1650 and 1806 the land was owned by the noble family of <a href="/wiki/Caracciolo" title="Caracciolo">Caracciolo</a>, who brought it to its former splendor with the construction of the castle.<br/>
In 1980 the town was badly damaged by the earthquake in <a href="/wiki/Irpinia" title="Irpinia">Irpinia</a>.</p>
