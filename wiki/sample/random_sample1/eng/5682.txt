<p><b>Monteleone d'Orvieto</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> (municipality) in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Terni" title="Province of Terni">Province of Terni</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> region <a href="/wiki/Umbria" title="Umbria">Umbria</a>, located about 35 km southwest of <a href="/wiki/Perugia" title="Perugia">Perugia</a> and about 60 km northwest of <a href="/wiki/Terni" title="Terni">Terni</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monteleone_d%27Orvieto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to 16th century historian Cipriano Manente, Monteleone was founded by the <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> of <a href="/wiki/Orvieto" title="Orvieto">Orvieto</a> in 1052, as a castle guarding its northern boundaries. In 1373 Emperor <a href="/wiki/Charles_IV,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles IV, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles IV</a> assigned it to the viscounts of Turrena, and later it was contended by several local barons such as the Corbara family and a nephew of Pope <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sixtus_IV" title="Sixtus IV">Sixtus IV</a>. In 1481 it was returned to Orvieto.</p>
<p>In 1643, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_of_Castro" title="War of Castro">War of Castro</a> fought between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barberini" title="Barberini">Barberini</a> <a href="/wiki/Pope_Urban_VIII" title="Pope Urban VIII">Pope Urban VIII</a> and the house of <a href="/wiki/House_of_Farnese" title="House of Farnese">Farnese</a>, Monteleone was besieged and destroyed by the troops of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Florence" title="Republic of Florence">Florence</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_sights">Main sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monteleone_d%27Orvieto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Remains of the medieval walls, including the northern gate.</li>
<li>Church of <i>SS. Apostoli Pietro e Paolo</i>. It houses a <i>Madonna with Child between Sts. Paul and Peter</i>, once attributed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perugino" title="Perugino">Perugino</a> but most kiley from his collaborator Giacomo di Ser Guglielmo of Città della Pieve.</li>
<li>Church of <i>SS. Crocifisso</i> (17th century)</li>
<li>Medieval <i>Torrione</i> ("Big Tower"), offering a wide panorama of Umbria, Tuscany and Lazio.</li>
<li><i>Castel Brandetto</i> (11th century, destroyed in 1350). Now it is a private-owned residence showing little remains of the medieval edifice.</li>
</ul>
