<p><b>Maldon</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> in <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. Its council is based in the town of <a href="/wiki/Maldon,_Essex" title="Maldon, Essex">Maldon</a>, and the next largest centre of population is <a href="/wiki/Burnham-on-Crouch" title="Burnham-on-Crouch">Burnham-on-Crouch</a>. The district covers the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dengie_peninsula" title="Dengie peninsula">Dengie peninsula</a> as well as an area to the north of the <a href="/wiki/Blackwater_Estuary" title="Blackwater Estuary">Blackwater Estuary</a>, a total area of 358.78 km².<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The district was formed on 1 April 1974 under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>. It covered the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> of Maldon and <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Burnham-on-Crouch" title="Burnham-on-Crouch">Burnham-on-Crouch</a> along with Maldon Rural District.</p>
<p>The District has a population of 59,418 in 2001.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The majority of people live in the small rural villages, many of which have their origins in connections with the coast or agricultural economy. Many people know the district from its association with sailing.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maldon_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A subdivision of the county of <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">Non-metropolitan district</a> is served by Maldon District Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The area is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliamentary_constituency" title="Parliamentary constituency">parliamentary constituency</a> of <a href="/wiki/Maldon_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Maldon (UK Parliament constituency)">Maldon</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maldon_district_council">Maldon district council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maldon_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Maldon district council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The District Council is <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> dominated. There are 31 district councillors of which there are, 21 Conservative, four independent, four Maldon &amp; District Independent Democratic Alliance (MDIDA) and two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_Labour_Party" title="UK Labour Party">Labour</a> councillors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Elections for all seats are held every four years; the last were held in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elections_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-elections-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>There are 29 town and parish councils.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Former leader of Maldon district council John Smith was killed along with two members of his family while he commanded a light aircraft flight from Oban to Andrewsfield, Essex on 9 April 2007. An Air Accident Investigation report<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> concluded that Smith was 5 times over the blood alcohol prescribed limit, this along with unreliable instrument readings and lack of IMC or instrument rating lead to disorientation and the crash. Smiths health was also questioned at a coroners inquest and it was reported<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> he was not medically cleared to fly.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maldon_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The first evidence of a settlement to the north of Maldon at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Elms_Farm&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Elms Farm (page does not exist)">Elms Farm</a> is the Middle Bronze Age 3500 years ago. From 500 BC onwards the red hills of the <a href="/wiki/River_Crouch" title="River Crouch">Crouch</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Blackwater,_Essex" title="River Blackwater, Essex">Blackwater</a> show us that there was a continuous and extensive activity in salt making which still prospers today.</p>
<p>Later, during the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a>, about 100 BC, there was a port, set among the marshlands at the junction of the Blackwater and <a href="/wiki/River_Chelmer" title="River Chelmer">Chelmer</a> rivers. This settlement may have been of regional religious significance, and there is evidence that it traded in luxury goods with Europe.</p>
<p>Elms Farm continued as a port and market place but was clearly superseded in importance by Colchester in the 1st century AD, when <a href="/wiki/Colchester" title="Colchester">Colchester</a> became the first capital of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> administration.</p>
<p>The Romans founded <a href="/wiki/Othona" title="Othona">Othona</a> in the 3rd century. It was a fortress at the mouth of the Blackwater built to protect the estuary from Saxon pirates. It was here in 654 AD that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Cedd" title="St Cedd">St Cedd</a> founded the church of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Peter-on-the-Wall" title="St Peter-on-the-Wall">St Peter-on-the-Wall</a>, the oldest church in England to retain much of its original fabric.</p>
<p>In 664 AD <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Cedd" title="St Cedd">St Cedd</a> attended the <a href="/wiki/Synod_of_Whitby" title="Synod of Whitby">Synod of Whitby</a> which merged the Anglo-Celtic Church with the Church of Rome. Recent changes in the coastline have revealed the remains of extensive Saxon 5-7th century fish traps. The Church stands today overlooking the North Sea from whence came further invaders in the 10th century - the Danes.</p>
<p>In 912 AD and 914 AD <a href="/wiki/Edward_the_Elder" title="Edward the Elder">Edward the Elder</a> camped at Maldon to organise defences in the desperate fight against the Danes. In 917 AD the Saxons were defeated at Colchester and besieged at Maldon, but eventually the Danes were defeated.</p>
<p>In 991 AD there was a major battle between the pillaging Danes led by Olaf Trygvassen who had already attacked Ipswich, and Earl Bryhtnoth's men who were defending Maldon on the instruction of the Saxon King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethelred_the_Unready" title="Ethelred the Unready">Ethelred the Unready</a>. Earl Bryhtnoth was in his sixties. The battled was recorded in a poem which is regarded as one of the finest examples of early English literature.</p>
<p>The famous <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Maldon" title="Battle of Maldon">Battle of Maldon</a> took place beside the River Blackwater on 10 August 991, during the reign of Ethelred the Unready. The Anglo-Saxons, led by Byrhtnoth and his thegns, fought against a Viking invasion, a battle which ended in defeat for the Anglo-Saxons.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology.2C_landscape_and_ecology">Geology, landscape and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maldon_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geology, landscape and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The district is in the east of Essex (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Maldon_District&amp;params=51_41_N_0_45_E_region:GB_type:adm2nd"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">51°41′N</span> <span class="longitude">0°45′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">51.683°N 0.750°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">51.683; 0.750</span></span></span></a></span>). Most of the district is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dengie_peninsula" title="Dengie peninsula">Dengie peninsula</a> but a significant area is also the area above the <a href="/wiki/Blackwater_Estuary" title="Blackwater Estuary">Blackwater Estuary</a>, bounded by the <a href="/wiki/River_Blackwater,_Essex" title="River Blackwater, Essex">River Blackwater</a> to the west until near <a href="/wiki/Kelvedon" title="Kelvedon">Kelvedon</a>, the boundary then continues south of <a href="/wiki/Tiptree" title="Tiptree">Tiptree</a> to the Salcott inlet on the Blackwater Estuary. There are very few settlements on the boundary of the district with the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>, in contrast with other coastal districts of Essex, due to the Dengie Marshes.</p>
<p>The district has a rural character ranging from the tidal salt marshes to farmland and rolling wooded ridges. A network of country lanes provides access to the countryside from the towns and villages. A remote area of tidal mud-flats and saltmarshes at the eastern end of the Dengie peninsula form the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dengie_SPA" title="Dengie SPA">Dengie Special Protection Area</a>. The River Blackwater and <a href="/wiki/River_Crouch" title="River Crouch">River Crouch</a> are of international importance for nature conservation particularly for their extensive population of wildfowl and waders.</p>
