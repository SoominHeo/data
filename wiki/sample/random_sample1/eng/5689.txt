<p>The <b>Parliament of the Republic of Moldova</b> (<i>Parlamentul Republicii Moldova</i>) is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unicameral" title="Unicameral">unicameral</a> assembly of the <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Republic of Moldova</a>. Its 101 MPs are elected by <a href="/wiki/Election" title="Election">popular vote</a> every 4 years. The parliament then elects a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova" title="President of the Republic of Moldova">president</a>, who functions as the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a>. The president appoints a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Moldova" title="List of Prime Ministers of Moldova">prime minister</a> as <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> who in turn assembles a <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_(government)" title="Cabinet (government)">cabinet</a>, both subject to parliamentary approval.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Apparatus">Apparatus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parliament_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Apparatus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Parliament staff ensures an organizational, informational and technological assistance to activity of the Parliament, the Standing Bureau, standing committees, parliamentary factions and of deputies. The structure and the personal record of the parliament staff are approved by the Parliament.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legislative_procedure">Legislative procedure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parliament_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legislative procedure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Moldova_(1994)" title="Constitution of Moldova (1994)">Constitution of Moldova (1994)</a>, the Parliament is the supreme representative organ and the single legislative authority of the state. The right of legislative initiative belongs to the Members of Parliament, to the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Moldovan_Parliament" title="President of the Moldovan Parliament">Speaker</a> (excepting proposals to revise the Constitution) and to the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Moldova" title="Cabinet of Moldova">Government</a>. In exercise of this right MPs and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova" title="President of the Republic of Moldova">President</a> of the state present to Parliament draft papers and legislative proposals, while the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova" title="Government of the Republic of Moldova">Government</a> presents draft papers.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parliament_Building">Parliament Building</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parliament_of_the_Republic_of_Moldova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Parliament Building">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Parliament Building was formerly the meeting place of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Committee" title="Central Committee">Central Committee</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Moldova" title="Communist Party of Moldova">Moldovan branch</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Communist Party of the Soviet Union">Communist Party of the Soviet Union</a>. It is located on <a href="/wiki/Stephen_III_of_Moldavia" title="Stephen III of Moldavia">Stephen the Great</a> Boulevard formerly known as <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin" title="Vladimir Lenin">Lenin</a> Boulevard. The architects were Alexander Cerdanțev and Grigore Bosenco. The building was damaged during <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_Moldova_civil_unrest" title="2009 Moldova civil unrest">civil unrest in 2009</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and repairs were carried out in 2012 and 2013. Parliament moved back into the restored building in February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
