<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Flag of the Maldives</h1>

<p>The <b>flag of the Republic of <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a></b> is red with a large <a href="/wiki/Green" title="Green">green</a> rectangle in the center bearing a vertical <a href="/wiki/White" title="White">white</a> <a href="/wiki/Crescent" title="Crescent">crescent</a>; the closed side of the crescent is on the hoist side of the flag. It was adopted on July 25, 1965.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Red" title="Red">red</a> rectangle represents the boldness of the nation's heroes, and their willingness to sacrifice their every drop of <a href="/wiki/Blood" title="Blood">blood</a> in defense of their country. The green rectangle in the center symbolizes peace and prosperity. The white <a href="/wiki/Hilal_(crescent_moon)" title="Hilal (crescent moon)">crescent moon</a> symbolizes a state of unified <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islamic</a> faith.</p>
<p>The colors are:</p>
<ul>
<li>red: PMS 186C, RGB (210, 16, 52)</li>
<li>green: PMS 348C, RGB (0, 126, 58)</li>
<li>white</li>
</ul>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_Maldives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The earliest flag of the Maldives consisted of a plain red field. Later, a <a href="/wiki/Black" title="Black">black</a> and <a href="/wiki/White" title="White">white</a> striped <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hoist_(flag)" title="Hoist (flag)">hoist</a> called the <i>Dhandimathi</i> (<a href="/wiki/Maldivian_language" title="Maldivian language">Maldivian</a>: <span lang="dv" xml:lang="dv">ދަނޑިމަތި</span>)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (May 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> was added to the flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>This version of the flag was used until early in the 20th century, when <a href="/wiki/Abdul_Majeed_Didi" title="Abdul Majeed Didi">Abdul Majeed Didi</a> added a crescent to the national flag. At the same time, a distinct state flag was made, which had the crescent on a green rectangle. These changes were made some time between 1926 and 1932, during Abdul Majeed's term as Prime Minister.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1953, the Maldives became a republic, resulting in another flag change. The national flag was dropped and the crescent on the state flag was reversed, so that it faced the hoist. The Sultanate was restored in 1954, but the flag was not changed back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Instead, <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Fareed_Didi" title="Muhammad Fareed Didi">Muhammad Fareed Didi</a> created a new flag specifically for the Sultan, with a five-pointed star next to the crescent. A version of this flag is still used today as the Presidential Standard.</p>
<p>When the Maldives gained independence in 1965, the black and white hoist was removed, giving the flag its modern form.</p>
