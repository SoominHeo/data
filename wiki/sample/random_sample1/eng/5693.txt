<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldivian</a> National Emblem</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> consists of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coconut_palm" title="Coconut palm">coconut palm</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Crescent" title="Crescent">crescent</a>, and two criss-crossing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_of_Maldives" title="Flag of Maldives">National Flags</a> with the traditional Title of the State.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Interpretation">Interpretation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emblem_of_Maldives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Interpretation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The depicted coconut palm represents the livelihood of the Nation according to <a href="/wiki/Maldivian_folklore" title="Maldivian folklore">Maldivian folklore</a> and tradition. The inhabitants believe it to be the most beneficial <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">tree</a> to them as they utilize every part of the tree on various applications ranging from <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a> to boat-building. The Crescent (a universal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic" title="Islamic">Islamic</a> symbol) and its accompanying <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> embodies the Islamic <a href="/wiki/Faith" title="Faith">faith</a> of the State and its authority respectively.</p>
<p>The words of the scroll <b>Ad-Dawlat Al-Mahaldheebiyya</b> are written in the <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> <a href="/wiki/Naskh_(script)" title="Naskh (script)">naskh</a> style of script. They were used by <a href="/wiki/Sultan" title="Sultan">Sultan</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AI-Ghazee_Mohamed_Thakurufaanu_Al-Azam" title="AI-Ghazee Mohamed Thakurufaanu Al-Azam">AI-Ghazee Mohamed Thakurufaanu Al-Azam</a> one of the most illustrious heroes of the nation. The title Ad-Dawlat Al-Mahaldheebiyya (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">الدولة المحلديبية</span>‎‎) means the "State of the Mahal Dibiyat", which is the name <a href="/wiki/Ibn_Battuta" title="Ibn Battuta">Ibn Battuta</a> and other Mediaeval Arab travellers used to refer to the Maldives.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_Usage">Modern Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emblem_of_Maldives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Modern Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Coat of Arms is a symbolic representation of the Maldivian <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">Government</a> and is used frequently in official documents (on the header right underneath the <a href="/wiki/Basmala" title="Basmala">Bismillah</a>) and other Governmental Representations.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_version">Former version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emblem_of_Maldives&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Former version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The crescent and star in the middle of the Maldive Coat of Arms used to be pale blue and white (silver) at the time of its first design in the 1940s during Muhammad Amin Doshimēna Kalēgefānu's regency. The color of the crescent and star was changed to gold in the 1990s.</p>
