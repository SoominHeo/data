<p><b>Molly Sandén</b> (born 3 July 1992) is a Swedish <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> singer and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voice_actress" title="Voice actress">voice actress</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> As a teenager she represented Sweden in the <a href="/wiki/Junior_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2006" title="Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2006">Junior Eurovision Song Contest</a> in 2006, and participated in <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen" title="Melodifestivalen">Melodifestivalen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen_2009" title="Melodifestivalen 2009">2009</a>, and as a young adult in <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen_2012" title="Melodifestivalen 2012">2012</a> and <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen_2016" title="Melodifestivalen 2016">2016</a>. Her sisters <a href="/wiki/Frida_Sand%C3%A9n" title="Frida Sandén">Frida</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mimmi_Sand%C3%A9n" title="Mimmi Sandén">Mimmi</a> are also singers.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molly_Sand%C3%A9n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sandén was born in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> on 3 July 1992. She attended the <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Fredrik%27s_Music_School" title="Adolf Fredrik's Music School">Adolf Fredrik's Music School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-svt.se_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-svt.se-2">[2]</a></sup> She then attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gymnasium_school" title="Gymnasium school">gymnasium school</a> Rytmus Music High School, from which she graduated in spring 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-expressen_130211_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-expressen_130211-4">[4]</a></sup> Sandén practised and wrote music at Helges song studio in <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A4vle" title="Gävle">Gävle</a>, where she also became a member of the band <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Helges_All_Stars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Helges All Stars (page does not exist)">Helges All Stars</a> with <a href="/wiki/Alice_Svensson" title="Alice Svensson">Alice Svensson</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amy_Diamond" title="Amy Diamond">Amy Diamond</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zara_Larsson" title="Zara Larsson">Zara Larsson</a>, and her sisters <a href="/wiki/Frida_Sand%C3%A9n" title="Frida Sandén">Frida</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mimmi_Sand%C3%A9n" title="Mimmi Sandén">Mimmi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloggplatsen.se_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-bloggplatsen.se-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molly_Sand%C3%A9n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9307:_Junior_Eurovision_Song_Contest">2006–07: <i>Junior Eurovision Song Contest</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molly_Sand%C3%A9n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2006–07: Junior Eurovision Song Contest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sandén represented <a href="/wiki/Sweden_in_the_Junior_Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Sweden in the Junior Eurovision Song Contest">Sweden</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Junior_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2006" title="Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2006">Junior Eurovision Song Contest</a> in 2006, singing "Det finaste någon kan få".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She came third, which remains Sweden's best result.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aftonbladet.se_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-aftonbladet.se-7">[7]</a></sup> She appeared in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SVT" title="SVT">SVT</a>'s New Years' celebrations on 31 December 2006, which was broadcast live from <a href="/wiki/Skansen" title="Skansen">Skansen</a>. She also won the talent competition, Stellar Shots.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloggplatsen.se_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-bloggplatsen.se-5">[5]</a></sup> She performed a <a href="/wiki/Duet" title="Duet">duet</a> with <a href="/wiki/Magnus_Carlsson" title="Magnus Carlsson">Magnus Carlsson</a> in the song "Julens tid är här" ("Christmas time is here"), on his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christmas_Music" title="Christmas Music">Christmas album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Sp%C3%A5r_i_sn%C3%B6n" title="Spår i snön">Spår i snön</a></i> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloggplatsen.se_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-bloggplatsen.se-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sandén also met <a href="/wiki/Markoolio" title="Markoolio">Markoolio</a> and sang with him during this period, on his new album called <i>Snow at Christmas rock</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloggplatsen.se_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-bloggplatsen.se-5">[5]</a></sup> In summer 2007, Sandén participated in <a href="/wiki/Diggiloo" title="Diggiloo">Diggiloo</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Lasse_Holm" title="Lasse Holm">Lasse Holm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linda_Bengtzing" title="Linda Bengtzing">Linda Bengtzing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lotta_Engberg" title="Lotta Engberg">Lotta Engberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tony_Roche" title="Tony Roche">Tony Roche</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Perrelli" title="Charlotte Perrelli">Charlotte Perrelli</a>, Thomas Pettersson, <a href="/wiki/Jan_Johansen_(singer)" title="Jan Johansen (singer)">Jan Johansen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niklas_Andersson" title="Niklas Andersson">Niklas Andersson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lasse_Berghagen" title="Lasse Berghagen">Lasse Berghagen</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benjamin_Wahlgren" title="Benjamin Wahlgren">Benjamin Wahlgren</a> and Magnus Johansson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>She sang a duet with <a href="/wiki/Ola_Svensson" title="Ola Svensson">Ola Svensson</a> on <a href="/wiki/You_Are_the_Music_in_Me#Molly_Sand.C3.A9n_and_Ola_Svensson_version" title="You Are the Music in Me">a Swedish version</a> of "<a href="/wiki/You_Are_the_Music_in_Me" title="You Are the Music in Me">You Are the Music in Me</a>" from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/High_School_Musical_2" title="High School Musical 2">High School Musical 2</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> They also sang in the <a href="/wiki/Lilla_Melodifestivalen" title="Lilla Melodifestivalen">Lilla Melodifestivalen</a> final ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Junior_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2007" title="Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2007">Junior Eurovision Song Contest</a> in 2007. The <a href="/wiki/Junior_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2006" title="Junior Eurovision Song Contest 2006">previous year</a>, Sandén was chosen to present the Swedish votes during the contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloggplatsen.se_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-bloggplatsen.se-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>Sandén appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/S%C3%A5_ska_det_l%C3%A5ta" title="Så ska det låta">Så ska det låta</a></i> on 11 April 2008 with <a href="/wiki/Magnus_B%C3%A4cklund_(singer)" title="Magnus Bäcklund (singer)">Magnus Bäcklund</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloggplatsen.se_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-bloggplatsen.se-5">[5]</a></sup> In summer 2008, she participated again in Diggiloo with Lasse Holm, Linda Bengtzing, Lotta Engberg, Thomas Pettersson, <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A5ns_Zelmerl%C3%B6w" title="Måns Zelmerlöw">Måns Zelmerlöw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nanne_Gr%C3%B6nvall" title="Nanne Grönvall">Nanne Grönvall</a>, Stefan and Kim and Magnus Johansson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloggplatsen.se_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-bloggplatsen.se-5">[5]</a></sup> A compilation CD was released and included her performances of "<a href="/wiki/Hallelujah_(Leonard_Cohen_song)" title="Hallelujah (Leonard Cohen song)">Hallelujah</a>" and "Gabriellas song".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Her <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dea_Norberg" title="Dea Norberg">Dea Norberg</a>'s song "Den underbara dagen du kommer hem" was used in advertisements for the fast food chain <a href="/wiki/Sibylla_(fast_food)" title="Sibylla (fast food)">Sibylla</a> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloggplatsen.se_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-bloggplatsen.se-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also in 2008, Sandén participated with <a href="/wiki/Janne_Schaffer" title="Janne Schaffer">Janne Schaffer</a>, Jan Johansen, Johan Boding and <a href="/wiki/Sara_L%C3%B6fgren" title="Sara Löfgren">Sara Löfgren</a> in <i>Sun, Wind &amp; Water</i>, a tribute to <a href="/wiki/Ted_G%C3%A4rdestad" title="Ted Gärdestad">Ted Gärdestad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloggplatsen.se_5-8"><a href="#cite_note-bloggplatsen.se-5">[5]</a></sup> The same year, she and Brandur released a Swedish version of the song "<a href="/wiki/Right_Here,_Right_Now_(High_School_Musical_song)" title="Right Here, Right Now (High School Musical song)">Right Here, Right Now</a>" from <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_School_Musical_3" title="High School Musical 3">High School Musical 3</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloggplatsen.se_5-9"><a href="#cite_note-bloggplatsen.se-5">[5]</a></sup> She participated in the celebrity dancing competition <i><a href="/wiki/Let%27s_Dance_(Swedish_TV_series)" title="Let's Dance (Swedish TV series)">Let's Dance</a></i> in 2010, which was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/TV4_(Sweden)" title="TV4 (Sweden)">TV4</a>; she was placed fourth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen" title="Melodifestivalen">2009 Melodifestivalen</a>, Sandén qualified for the grand final with her song "Så vill stjärnorna", but finished last with only two points, which were awarded by the international jury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-skd.se_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-skd.se-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.93present:_Melodifestivalen">2012–present: <i>Melodifestivalen</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molly_Sand%C3%A9n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–present: Melodifestivalen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sandén entered the Melodifestivalen again in <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen_2012" title="Melodifestivalen 2012">2012</a>, again qualifying for the final with her song "<a href="/wiki/Why_Am_I_Crying" title="Why Am I Crying">Why Am I Crying</a>", for which she came in fifth place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-skd.se_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-skd.se-13">[13]</a></sup> In August 2013, Sandén announced the premiere of her online clothing brand, 'My by Molly'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In November 2015 it was announced that Sandén was to participate in the Melodifestivalen for a third time in <a href="/wiki/Melodifestivalen_2016" title="Melodifestivalen 2016">2016</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-svt.se_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-svt.se-2">[2]</a></sup> She performed in the fourth heat in <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A4vle" title="Gävle">Gävle</a> on 27 February with the song "Youniverse", which she co-wrote with her boyfriend, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Saucedo" title="Danny Saucedo">Danny Saucedo</a>, and John Alexis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-svt.se_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-svt.se-2">[2]</a></sup> She qualified from the semifinal to the final on 12 March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In the final she placed sixth with the juries and televoting, and also placed sixth overall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molly_Sand%C3%A9n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sandén was in a long-term relationship with Swedish singer <a href="/wiki/Eric_Saade" title="Eric Saade">Eric Saade</a> until early 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-expressen.se_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-expressen.se-18">[18]</a></sup> She has been in a relationship with Swedish singer <a href="/wiki/Danny_Saucedo" title="Danny Saucedo">Danny Saucedo</a> since February 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-expressen.se_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-expressen.se-18">[18]</a></sup> and they got engaged in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On 28 September 2012, she announced that she had been diagnosed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Type_1_diabetes" title="Type 1 diabetes">type 1 diabetes</a> after collapsing while on a trip to <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
