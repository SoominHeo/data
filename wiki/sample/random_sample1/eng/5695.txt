<p><b>Molina Aterno</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">town</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_L%27Aquila" title="Province of L'Aquila">province of L'Aquila</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Abruzzo" title="Abruzzo">Abruzzo</a> region of central <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. The name derives from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a> <i>molina</i> ("mill"), the second part having been added in 1889 due to the presence of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aterno" title="Aterno">Aterno</a> river.</p>
<p>Main attractions include the church of <i>Santa Maria del Colle</i> (12th century), the church of <i>San Nicola</i> (with a 1632 bell tower) and the Baronal Palace.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Molina_Aterno&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Molina Aterno has a station on the <a href="/wiki/Terni%E2%80%93Sulmona_railway" title="Terni–Sulmona railway">Terni–Sulmona railway</a>, with trains to <a href="/wiki/L%27Aquila" title="L'Aquila">L'Aquila</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sulmona" title="Sulmona">Sulmona</a>.</p>
