<p><i><b>Papilio polytes</b></i>, the <b>common Mormon</b>, is a common species of <a href="/wiki/Swallowtail_butterfly" title="Swallowtail butterfly">swallowtail</a> <a href="/wiki/Butterfly" title="Butterfly">butterfly</a> widely distributed across <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.</p>
<p>This butterfly is known for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mimic" title="Mimic">mimicry</a> displayed by the numerous forms of its females which mimic <a href="/wiki/Inedible" title="Inedible">inedible</a> red-bodied swallowtails, such as the <a href="/wiki/Pachliopta_aristolochiae" title="Pachliopta aristolochiae">common rose</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crimson_rose" title="Crimson rose">crimson rose</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Names">Names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The common name is an allusion to the <a href="/wiki/Polygamy" title="Polygamy">polygamy</a> formerly practiced by members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mormon" title="Mormon">Mormon</a> sect according to <a href="/wiki/Harish_Gaonkar" title="Harish Gaonkar">Harish Gaonkar</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/Natural_History_Museum,_London" title="Natural History Museum, London">Natural History Museum</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>... the origins of giving common English names to organisms, particularly butterflies for tropical species started in India around the mid 19th century ... The naming of Mormons evolved slowly. I think the first to get such a name was the Common Mormon (<i>Papilio polytes</i>), because it had three different females, a fact that could only have been observed in the field, and this they did in India. The name obviously reflected the ... Mormon sect in America, which as we know, practiced polygamy.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The scientific name is constructed from the Latin word for butterfly, <i>papilio</i>, and the Greek word for many, <i>poly</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">Myanmar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, southern and western <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> provinces), <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ryukyu_Islands" title="Ryukyu Islands">Ryukyu Islands</a>), <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andamans" title="Andamans">Andamans</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nicobars" title="Nicobars">Nicobars</a>, Eastern and <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia" title="Peninsular Malaysia">Peninsular Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brunei" title="Brunei">Brunei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> (except <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moluccas" title="Moluccas">Moluccas</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irian_Jaya" title="Irian Jaya">Irian Jaya</a>), <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Marianas" title="Northern Marianas">Northern Marianas</a> (<a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Savela_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Savela-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Very common. Not threatened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Jet black butterfly with row of white spots along the middle part of hindwing. 90–100 mm.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Male">Male</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Male">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The male has one morph only. It is a dark-coloured swallow-tailed butterfly. The upper <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forewing" title="Forewing">forewing</a> has a series of white spots decreasing in size towards the apex. The upper <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindwing" title="Hindwing">hindwing</a> has a complete discal band of elongated white spots. It may or may not have marginal red crescents. The males are generally smaller in size than the females but not always. Both male and all forms of the female of <i>P. polytes</i> can vary considerably in size depending on climatic region.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Female">Female</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Female">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The female of the common Mormon is <a href="/wiki/Polymorphism_(biology)" title="Polymorphism (biology)">polymorphic</a>. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" title="Indian Subcontinent">Indian Subcontinent</a>, it has three forms or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Morph_(zoology)" title="Morph (zoology)">morphs</a>. These are as follows:<br/>
<b>Form <i>cyrus</i></b><br/>
This form is similar to the male, differing in that it always has strongly marked red crescents. It is the least common of the three forms. It is normally abundant where the <a href="/wiki/Pachliopta_aristolochiae" title="Pachliopta aristolochiae">common rose</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crimson_rose" title="Crimson rose">crimson rose</a> do not occur, such as in <a href="/wiki/Himachal_Pradesh" title="Himachal Pradesh">Himachal Pradesh</a> around <a href="/wiki/Shimla" title="Shimla">Shimla</a>; although a few specimens of form <i>romulus</i> have also been caught alongside.<br/>
<b>Form <i>stichius</i></b><br/>
This female form of the common Mormon <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mimic" title="Mimic">mimics</a> the common rose very closely. This is the commonest form wherever the common rose flies.<br/>
<b>Form <i>romulus</i></b><br/>
This female form <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mimic" title="Mimic">mimics</a> the crimson rose and is common over its range. It is not such a close mimic as the previous form being duller than its model. It is easy to differentiate the mimics from models by the colour of their body—the models are red-bodied and the mimics are black-bodied.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gynandromorphs">Gynandromorphs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Gynandromorphs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This species has considerable genetic variability and is known to produce <a href="/wiki/Gynandromorphism" title="Gynandromorphism">gynandromorphs</a>, genetic aberrations which are part male and part female.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mimicry">Mimicry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mimicry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TS002_Pachliopta_hector.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/TS002_Pachliopta_hector.jpg/220px-TS002_Pachliopta_hector.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/TS002_Pachliopta_hector.jpg/330px-TS002_Pachliopta_hector.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/TS002_Pachliopta_hector.jpg/440px-TS002_Pachliopta_hector.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TS002_Pachliopta_hector.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crimson_rose" title="Crimson rose">Crimson rose</a>, compare with form <i><b>romulus</b></i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In India, this butterfly is considered as the classic example of <a href="/wiki/Batesian_mimicry" title="Batesian mimicry">Batesian mimicry</a> in which edible species resemble unpalatable butterflies in order to escape being eaten by predators.</p>
<p>The populations of the mimicking morphs of the common Mormon are much smaller than that of their models - the common or crimson rose. This is in order to allow first time predators a much greater chance of preying upon the unpalatable model in the first instance and thus learning of their inedibility.</p>
<p>Larger populations of mimics could result in the edible common Mormon mimics being sampled the first time by predators. If this should happen, the predator may not realise that butterflies of that colour and pattern are protected by the poisons they ingest; thus dramatically reducing the effectiveness of this scheme of protection.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Sulawesi" title="Sulawesi">Sulawesi</a>, although the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atrophaneura_aristolochiae" title="Atrophaneura aristolochiae">common rose</a> is abundant, the common Mormon female morph which is found there, mimics a completely different butterfly, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atrophaneura_polyphontes" title="Atrophaneura polyphontes">Atrophaneura polyphontes</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The common Mormon prefers lightly wooded country, but is present everywhere and high up into the hills. It is a regular visitor to gardens, being especially abundant in <a href="/wiki/Orchard" title="Orchard">orchards</a> of its food plants—<a href="/wiki/Orange_(fruit)" title="Orange (fruit)">oranges</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lime_(fruit)" title="Lime (fruit)">limes</a>. It is most common in the <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a> and post-monsoon months.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Common_Mormon_-_Papilio_polytes.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1328" data-file-width="2000" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Common_Mormon_-_Papilio_polytes.jpg/220px-Common_Mormon_-_Papilio_polytes.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Common_Mormon_-_Papilio_polytes.jpg/330px-Common_Mormon_-_Papilio_polytes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Common_Mormon_-_Papilio_polytes.jpg/440px-Common_Mormon_-_Papilio_polytes.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Common_Mormon_-_Papilio_polytes.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Female form <i><b>romulus</b></i> seen laying an egg on <i>Murraya paniculata</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The common Mormon is fond of visiting <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">flowers</a> and its long <a href="/wiki/Proboscis" title="Proboscis">proboscis</a> permits it to feed from flowers having long <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corolla_(flower)" title="Corolla (flower)">corollar</a> tubes. It is particularly fond of <i><a href="/wiki/Lantana" title="Lantana">Lantana</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jatropha" title="Jatropha">Jatropha</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ixora" title="Ixora">Ixora</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Mussaenda" title="Mussaenda">Mussaenda</a></i> in city gardens. In the forests, the common Mormon remains low keeping within ten feet off the floor and its prefer to visit <i><a href="/wiki/Asystasia" title="Asystasia">Asystasia</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Peristrophe" title="Peristrophe">Peristrophe</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jasminum" title="Jasminum">Jasminum</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Nectar" title="Nectar">nectar</a>.</p>
<p>The male common Mormon is a very common visitor to gardens where he will be seen hovering over flowers when the sun is shining. It is a restless insect, zig-zagging fast and straight close to the ground, settling down only when it halts to feed.</p>
<p>The mimic female Mormons, <i>stichius</i> and <i>romulus</i> are very convincing mimics due to their habits, especially the flight patterns, being very similar to those of the rose models. However, lacking the protection of inedibility, they tend to be more easily disturbed than the roses and fly off erratically .</p>
<p>Only the males take part in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mud_puddling" title="Mud puddling">mud puddling</a>, usually in cool shaded spots rather than in open areas. They have been known to collect on <a href="/wiki/Soil_salinity" title="Soil salinity">saline</a> soils to extract <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minerals" title="Minerals">minerals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Both sexes bask in the sun on shrubs close to the ground. They hold their wings flat against the substratum. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forewing" title="Forewing">forewing</a> is lowered to cover part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindwing" title="Hindwing">hindwing</a> and is a typical stance of the common Mormon.</p>
<p>Common Mormons spend the night settled on vegetation with their wings held open, usually quite close to the ground.</p>

<p>The females perch on an exposed branch with wings open or closed. They are courted by the males who approach from behind and slowly and elegantly settle into position.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eggs">Eggs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Eggs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The eggs are laid singly on top of the leaves. They are round and yellow to light-orange in colour.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Caterpillar">Caterpillar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Caterpillar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first few instars of the caterpillar closely resemble those of the <a href="/wiki/Papilio_demoleus" title="Papilio demoleus">lime butterfly</a>.</p>
<p>The later <a href="/wiki/Instar" title="Instar">instars</a> become dark green in colour. There is a transverse black band with an eye-spot on each side on the 4th and 5th segments. This band, being darker and brighter than that of the <a href="/wiki/Papilio_demoleus" title="Papilio demoleus">lime butterfly</a> caterpillar, is the key distinction between the two.</p>
<p>The common Mormon <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar" title="Caterpillar">caterpillar</a> also has a black and white oblique band on the 8th and 9th segments, making it resemble that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princeps_polymnestor" title="Princeps polymnestor">blue Mormon</a>. The deep red osmeterium and yellowish-brown head help distinguish it from the blue Mormon caterpillar which has a greenish head.</p>
<p>Common Mormon caterpillars are heavily parasitised by <a href="/wiki/Chalcid_wasp" title="Chalcid wasp">chalcid wasps</a>, with over a hundred tiny wasps eventually emerging from each Mormon pupa.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pupa">Pupa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Pupa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pupa" title="Pupa">pupa</a> is located on underside of leaves and twigs. The pupa is light green and unmarked. It has two projections to the front on its head and also one on its thorax. It closely resembles the <a href="/wiki/Papilio_demoleus" title="Papilio demoleus">lime butterfly</a> caterpillar but can be distinguished by:</p>
<ul>
<li>the projections on the head which have a deeper indentation between them.</li>
<li>the abdomen which protrudes to a small point on each side.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_plants">Food plants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papilio_polytes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Food plants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li><i><a href="/wiki/Aegle_marmelos" title="Aegle marmelos">Aegle marmelos</a></i> or bael</li>

<li><i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Glycosmis_arborea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Glycosmis arborea (page does not exist)">Glycosmis arborea</a></i></li>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Murraya_koenigii" title="Murraya koenigii">Murraya koenigii</a></i> – curry leaf</li>
