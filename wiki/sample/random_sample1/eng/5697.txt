<p>The <b>Malta women's national football team</b> represents the <a href="/wiki/Malta_Football_Association" title="Malta Football Association">Malta Football Association</a> in international <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_association_football" title="Women's association football">women's football</a> matches sanctioned by <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> As of December 2012 Malta ranks 104th in <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>'s Women's World Ranking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The team first appeared in official competitions in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_UEFA_Women%27s_Championship_qualification" title="2005 UEFA Women's Championship qualification">2005 European Championship's qualifying</a>, debuting on August 10, 2003 in <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a> with a 3–0 loss to <a href="/wiki/Romania_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Romania women's national football team">Romania</a>. Malta lost all eight games, with a 1–35 goal average. The team's first goal was scored by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sarah_Caruana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sarah Caruana (page does not exist)">Sarah Caruana</a> on November 20, 2003 against <a href="/wiki/Croatia_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Croatia women's national football team">Croatia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>After 13 losses Malta achieved its first tie on June 7, 2006 against <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina women's national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> in the last match of the <a href="/wiki/2007_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2007 FIFA Women's World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2007 World Cup's qualifying</a>. The team played for the first time against elite national teams in the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2011 FIFA Women's World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2011 World Cup's qualifying</a>, losing all games including a record 0–13 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Spain_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Spain women's national football team">Spain</a>. On March 3, 2011 Malta won an official match for the first time in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Women%27s_Euro_2013_qualifying" title="UEFA Women's Euro 2013 qualifying">2013 European Championship qualifying</a>'s preliminary round, beating <a href="/wiki/Georgia_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Georgia women's national football team">Georgia</a> 1–0 with a goal by D'Agostino in injury time.</p>
<p>On April 6, 2013 Malta attained its first crushing win by beating <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Luxembourg women's national football team">Luxembourg</a> 6–0 in the <a href="/wiki/2015_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2015 FIFA Women's World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2015 World Cup qualifying</a>'s preliminary round. The team also defeated <a href="/wiki/Latvia_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Latvia women's national football team">Latvia</a> and tied with <a href="/wiki/Albania_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Albania women's national football team">Albania</a> to top the group and make it past a preliminary round for the first time in its fourth attempt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The team has been coached since its foundation by Pierre Brincat. However, it has been taken over by Mark Gatt, ex-U19 coach, on 22 January 2015.</p>
