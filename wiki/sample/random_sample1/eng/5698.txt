<p>The <b>Parliament of Malta</b> (<a href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese</a>: <span lang="mt"><i>Parlament ta' Malta</i></span>) is the constitutional <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">legislative body</a> in <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, located in Valletta. The <a href="/wiki/Parliament" title="Parliament">parliament</a> is <a href="/wiki/Unicameralism" title="Unicameralism">unicameral</a>, with a democratically elected House of Representatives and the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Malta" title="President of Malta">President of Malta</a>. By <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Malta" title="Constitution of Malta">Constitutional</a> law, all <a href="/wiki/Minister_(government)" title="Minister (government)">government ministers</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Malta" title="Prime Minister of Malta">Prime Minister</a>, must be members of the House of Representatives.</p>
<p>Between 1921 and 1933 the Parliament was <a href="/wiki/Bicameralism" title="Bicameralism">bicameral</a>, consisting of a <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Malta" title="Senate of Malta">Senate</a> as well as a Legislative Assembly.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="House_of_Representatives_of_Malta">House of Representatives of Malta</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parliament_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: House of Representatives of Malta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The House of Representatives (<a href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese</a>: <span lang="mt"><i>Kamra tad-Deputati</i></span>) is the unicameral legislature of Malta and a component of the Parliament of Malta. The House is presided over by the <a href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_House_of_Representatives_of_Malta" title="Speaker of the House of Representatives of Malta">Speaker of the House</a>. The <a href="/wiki/President_of_Malta" title="President of Malta">President of Malta</a> is appointed for a five-year term by a resolution of the House.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parliament_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The House is composed of an odd number of members elected for one legislative term of five years. Ordinarily, five members are returned from each of thirteen <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Malta" title="Districts of Malta">electoral districts</a> using the <a href="/wiki/Single_transferable_vote" title="Single transferable vote">single transferable vote</a> electoral system but additional members are elected in cases of dis-proportionality (where a party with an absolute majority of votes fails to win an absolute majority of seats and where only candidates from two parties are elected).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Meeting_place">Meeting place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parliament_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Meeting place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Malteseparliamentofficesandgarden.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="1936" height="295" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Malteseparliamentofficesandgarden.jpg/220px-Malteseparliamentofficesandgarden.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Malteseparliamentofficesandgarden.jpg/330px-Malteseparliamentofficesandgarden.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Malteseparliamentofficesandgarden.jpg/440px-Malteseparliamentofficesandgarden.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

View of the interior of the Parliament House</div>

<p>Between 1921 and 2015, the House of Representatives was housed in the <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster%27s_Palace_(Valletta)" title="Grandmaster's Palace (Valletta)">Grandmaster's Palace</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The House of Representatives is currently housed in the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_House_(Malta)" title="Parliament House (Malta)">Parliament House</a>, near the entrance of <a href="/wiki/Valletta" title="Valletta">Valletta</a>. The present building was inaugurated on 4 May 2015.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Committees">Committees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parliament_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Committees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Standing Orders of the House provide for the creation of eight Parliamentary Standing Committees to make parliamentary work more efficient and enhance Parliament's scrutiny functions.</p>
<p>The Standing Committees are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Standing Committee on House Business</li>
<li>Standing Committee on Privileges</li>
<li>Standing Committee on Public Accounts</li>
<li>Standing Committee on Foreign and European Affairs</li>
<li>Standing Committee on Social Affairs</li>
<li>Standing Committee on Consideration of Bills</li>
<li>Standing Committee on Family Affairs</li>
<li>Standing Committee on Economic and Financial Affairs</li>
</ul>
<p>Other Standing Committees constituted by other statutes include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Standing Committee on Environment and Development Planning</li>
<li>National Audit Office Accounts Committee</li>
</ul>
<p>There are also select committees and non-official committees.</p>
