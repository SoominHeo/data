<p>The <b>flag of Malta</b> (<a href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese</a>: <i>Bandiera ta' Malta</i>) is a basic bi-colour, with white in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hoist_(flag)" title="Hoist (flag)">hoist</a> and red in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fly_(flag)" title="Fly (flag)">fly</a>. A representation of the <a href="/wiki/George_Cross" title="George Cross">George Cross</a>, <a href="/wiki/Award_of_the_George_Cross_to_Malta" title="Award of the George Cross to Malta">awarded to Malta</a> by <a href="/wiki/George_VI" title="George VI">George VI</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> in 1942, is carried, edged with red, in the <a href="/wiki/Canton_(flag)" title="Canton (flag)">canton</a> of the white stripe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Colours">Colours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Colours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The red hue in the Maltese flag is officially documented as <a href="/wiki/Pantone" title="Pantone">Pantone©186c</a>, R207 G20 B43 (# CF142B) or <a href="/wiki/Spot_color" title="Spot color">Spot Colour</a> - 50% rubine red • 50% warm red.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tradition states that the colours of the flag were given to Malta by <a href="/wiki/Roger_I_of_Sicily" title="Roger I of Sicily">Roger I of Sicily</a> in 1090. Roger's fleet landed in Malta on the completion of the <a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_southern_Italy" title="Norman conquest of southern Italy">Norman conquest of Sicily</a>. It is said that local Christians offered to fight by Roger's side against the Arab defenders. In order to recognise the locals fighting on his side from the defenders, Roger reportedly tore off part of his chequered red-and-white flag. This story has, however, been debunked as a 19th-century myth, possibly even earlier due to the <a href="/wiki/Mdina" title="Mdina">Mdina</a>, Malta's old capital, associating its colours with Roger's in the late Middle Ages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The flag of the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">Knights of Malta</a>, a white cross on a red field, was a more likely source of the Maltese colours, inspiring the red and white shield used during the <a href="/wiki/Crown_Colony_of_Malta" title="Crown Colony of Malta">British colonial period</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_George_Cross">The George Cross</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The George Cross">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The George Cross originally appeared on the flag placed on a blue <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_vexillology" title="Glossary of vexillology">canton</a> (see <a href="/wiki/List_of_flags_of_Malta" title="List of flags of Malta">List of flags of Malta</a>). The flag was changed on 21 September 1964 with Malta's independence when the blue canton was replaced by a red <a href="/wiki/Fimbriation" title="Fimbriation">fimbriation</a> the intention being that the Cross appear less prominent. It also made the flag conform to the western rule of <a href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">heraldry</a>, which states that no colour may touch a colour, or metal touch a metal (silver/white and yellow/gold being the two metals). The flag has remained unchanged since.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_ensign">Civil ensign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Malta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Civil ensign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Civil_ensign" title="Civil ensign">civil ensign</a> shows a red field, bordered white and charged with a blank <a href="/wiki/Maltese_cross" title="Maltese cross">Maltese cross</a>.</p>
