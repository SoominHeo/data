<p><b><a href="/wiki/Montgomery_Gentry" title="Montgomery Gentry">Montgomery Gentry</a></b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> duo composed of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eddie_Montgomery" title="Eddie Montgomery">Eddie Montgomery</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Troy_Gentry" title="Troy Gentry">Troy Gentry</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Discography" title="Discography">discography</a> comprises eight <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio albums</a>, four <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation albums</a>, one <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a>, and twenty-six <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a>. The duo made its debut in 1999 with the single "<a href="/wiki/Hillbilly_Shoes" title="Hillbilly Shoes">Hillbilly Shoes</a>," which went to number 13 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> charts, but did not reach Number 1 until mid-2004, with "<a href="/wiki/If_You_Ever_Stop_Loving_Me" title="If You Ever Stop Loving Me">If You Ever Stop Loving Me</a>." The duo has sent four more singles to Number One for a total of five: "<a href="/wiki/Something_to_Be_Proud_Of" title="Something to Be Proud Of">Something to Be Proud Of</a>" (2005), "<a href="/wiki/Lucky_Man_(Montgomery_Gentry_song)" title="Lucky Man (Montgomery Gentry song)">Lucky Man</a>" (2007), "<a href="/wiki/Back_When_I_Knew_It_All" title="Back When I Knew It All">Back When I Knew It All</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Roll_with_Me" title="Roll with Me">Roll with Me</a>" (both 2008). Besides these, Montgomery Gentry has reached Top Ten with 10 additional hit singles. All 15 of these songs have also crossed over to the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, where the duo's highest peak is "If You Ever Stop Loving Me" at number 30.</p>
<p>Montgomery Gentry's first, third and fourth albums — 1999's <i><a href="/wiki/Tattoos_%26_Scars" title="Tattoos &amp; Scars">Tattoos &amp; Scars</a>,</i> 2002's <i><a href="/wiki/My_Town_(album)" title="My Town (album)">My Town</a></i> and 2004's <i><a href="/wiki/You_Do_Your_Thing" title="You Do Your Thing">You Do Your Thing</a></i> — are all <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">certified platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) for shipments of one million copies. 2001's <i><a href="/wiki/Carrying_On" title="Carrying On">Carrying On</a></i>, 2005's <i><a href="/wiki/Something_to_Be_Proud_Of:_The_Best_of_1999%E2%80%932005" title="Something to Be Proud Of: The Best of 1999–2005">Something to Be Proud Of: The Best of 1999–2005</a></i> and 2006's <i><a href="/wiki/Some_People_Change" title="Some People Change">Some People Change</a></i> are all certified gold.</p>
