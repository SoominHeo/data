<p>The <b>Montpellier tramway</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Tramway de Montpellier</i></span>) is a four-line <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">tramway</a> system in the city of <a href="/wiki/Montpellier" title="Montpellier">Montpellier</a> in <a href="/wiki/Languedoc-Roussillon" title="Languedoc-Roussillon">Languedoc-Roussillon</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. The tramway is owned by the <a href="/wiki/Montpellier_M%C3%A9diterran%C3%A9e_M%C3%A9tropole" title="Montpellier Méditerranée Métropole">Montpellier Méditerranée Métropole</a>, and is operated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transports_de_l%27agglom%C3%A9ration_de_Montpellier" title="Transports de l'agglomération de Montpellier">Transports de l'agglomération de Montpellier</a> (TAM) authority.</p>
<p>The first line opened in July 2000 and the second in December 2006. Lines 3 and 4 opened in April 2012 along with an extension of line 1 and a diversion of line 2. The western section of line 4 opened in July 2016, thus completing the loop it makes around the city.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Line_1">Line 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montpellier_tramway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Line 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stadtbahn_Montpellier.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3040" data-file-width="4048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Stadtbahn_Montpellier.jpg/220px-Stadtbahn_Montpellier.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Stadtbahn_Montpellier.jpg/330px-Stadtbahn_Montpellier.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Stadtbahn_Montpellier.jpg/440px-Stadtbahn_Montpellier.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stadtbahn_Montpellier.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Citadis" title="Citadis">Citadis 401</a> tram on line 1 at Odysseum</div>

<p>Line 1 is 15.7 km long and runs entirely within the city limits of Montpellier, going on a looping route from the northwest, through the city centre, and going to the east end of the city, terminating at Odysseum, a shopping and entertainment district. There are 27 stations on the line, with 7 major interchanges. Currently, ridership on the line averages 130,000 passengers per day, exceeding initial ridership projections of 75,000 passengers per day. The line was completed on a budget of €348.8 million. The line is coloured blue.</p>
<p>Line 1 opened on July 1, 2000. The Millénaire station opened on October 11, 2003, and the Malbosc station opened on November 18, 2003. A one-station extension opened on September 21, 2009 from the former Odysseum station, which was renamed Place de France, to a new Odysseum station which serves an additional shopping centre. On April 7, 2012, the west end of the line was extended from <a href="/wiki/Stade_de_la_Mosson" title="Stade de la Mosson">Stade de la Mosson</a> to Mosson to connect with the newly opened Line 3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RG0412_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-RG0412-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the line, stations, station platforms, and urban improvements were done by architect <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Antoine_Garcia-Diaz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Antoine Garcia-Diaz (page does not exist)">Antoine Garcia-Diaz</a>. The livery of trams on Line 1 was decorated in blue with white <a href="/wiki/Swallow" title="Swallow">swallows</a> by stylists <a href="/wiki/%C3%89lisabeth_Garouste" title="Élisabeth Garouste">Élisabeth Garouste</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mattia_Bonetti" title="Mattia Bonetti">Mattia Bonetti</a>. Each tram is named after a community member of the Montpellier agglomeration community.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rolling_stock">Rolling stock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montpellier_tramway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rolling stock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>All rolling stock on line 1 is stored at the Les Hirondelles depot in the western district of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=La_Paillade&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="La Paillade (page does not exist)">La Paillade</a>, which is located between the Saint-Paul station and the Hauts de Massane station. The rolling stock comprises 30 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Citadis" title="Citadis">Citadis 401</a> trams and 3 Citadis 402 trams manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Alstom" title="Alstom">Alstom</a>.</p>
<p>Originally, Line 1 was operated with 28 3-section Citadis 301 trams with a length of 29.8 m. As ridership became successful on the line and trams had been filling up, between July 2002 and May 2003, the trams were extended from a length of 29.8 m and 3 sections to 40.9 m and 5 sections, and two additional Citadis 401 trams were delivered in January 2002, bringing the number of trams on Line 1 up to 30. The trams are numbered from 2001 to 2030.</p>
<p>In early 2007, three Citadis 302 trams, as used on Line 2, were delivered, and became operational for service on May 2, 2007. The three trams are numbered from 2031 to 2033. All trams on Line 1 have a width of 2.65 m.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Line_2">Line 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montpellier_tramway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Line 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tram_Montpellier_11_2013_Line_2_624.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1800" data-file-width="2400" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Tram_Montpellier_11_2013_Line_2_624.JPG/220px-Tram_Montpellier_11_2013_Line_2_624.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Tram_Montpellier_11_2013_Line_2_624.JPG/330px-Tram_Montpellier_11_2013_Line_2_624.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Tram_Montpellier_11_2013_Line_2_624.JPG/440px-Tram_Montpellier_11_2013_Line_2_624.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tram_Montpellier_11_2013_Line_2_624.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Orange with flowers livery on Line 2.</div>
</div>

<p><b>Line 2</b> opened for service on December 16, 2006. It is the longest tram line in France, with a length of 17.5 km and with 28 stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The line is coloured orange. The Victoire 2 station opened April 16, 2007, being the 33rd station on the line. The line travels between <a href="/wiki/Jacou" title="Jacou">Jacou</a> in the northeast and <a href="/wiki/Saint-Jean-de-V%C3%A9das" title="Saint-Jean-de-Védas">Saint-Jean-de-Védas</a> in the southwest, via <a href="/wiki/Castelnau-le-Lez" title="Castelnau-le-Lez">Castelnau-le-Lez</a> (on the boundary with <a href="/wiki/Le_Cr%C3%A8s" title="Le Crès">Le Crès</a>) and the centre of Montpellier. Line 2 was built at a cost of €450 million. In April 2007, with the opening of Lines 3 and Line 4, Line 2 was diverted to run from Corum to the SNCF station at Saint-Roch through Comédie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RG0412_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-RG0412-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Part of Line 2, between Sabines and Saint-Jean-le-Sec, uses 2.5 km of an abandoned rail line. Line 2 is double tracked between Notre-Dame-de-Sablassou and Sabines; the remaining 3.5 km of the line is single tracked with passing loops.</p>
<p>As with Line 1, stations, station platforms, and urban improvements on Line 2 were done by architect <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Antoine_Garcia-Diaz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Antoine Garcia-Diaz (page does not exist)">Antoine Garcia-Diaz</a>. The livery of trams on Line 2 is decorated in orange with flowers by <a href="/wiki/Mattia_Bonetti" title="Mattia Bonetti">Mattia Bonetti</a>. Each tram is named for a historic person in the Montpellier region.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rolling_stock_2">Rolling stock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montpellier_tramway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Rolling stock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Trams on Line 2 are stored at the La Jeune Parque depot, located near the Sabines station.</p>
<p>The rolling stock on Line 2 comprises 24 Citadis 302 trams manufactured by Alstom, with a length of 32.5 m, a width of 2.65 m, and five sections. The trams were delivered between March 2006 and February 2007 and are numbered from 2041 to 2064.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Line_3">Line 3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montpellier_tramway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Line 3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tramway_Montpellier_Ligne_3.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Tramway_Montpellier_Ligne_3.JPG/220px-Tramway_Montpellier_Ligne_3.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Tramway_Montpellier_Ligne_3.JPG/330px-Tramway_Montpellier_Ligne_3.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Tramway_Montpellier_Ligne_3.JPG/440px-Tramway_Montpellier_Ligne_3.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tramway_Montpellier_Ligne_3.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Trams with underwater livery operating on Line 3 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gare_de_Montpellier_Saint-Roch" title="Gare de Montpellier Saint-Roch">Gare Saint-Roch</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Line 3 was inaugurated on April 7, 2012 with a length of 23.0 km and 29 stations. The line runs from Juvignac in the west to Pérols-Etang de l'Or in the southeast. There is also a three-station branch from Boirargues to Lattes-Centre. The line is coloured green. The rolling stock consists on 19 43m <a href="/wiki/Alstom_Citadis" title="Alstom Citadis">Alstom Citadis</a> trams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RG0412_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-RG0412-2">[2]</a></sup> The route is as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>The line starts in <a href="/wiki/Juvignac" title="Juvignac">Juvignac</a>, in a new district, Caunelles, in the north of the village.</li>
<li>In Montpellier, Line 3 runs parallel to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mosson_(river)" title="Mosson (river)">Mosson</a>.
<ul>
<li>The line goes through neighbourhoods in the west end of the city. A part of this segment loops to the north to serve the Alco neighbourhood.</li>
<li>Next, there is a connection to the existing Line 1 and Line 2 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gare_de_Montpellier_Saint-Roch" title="Gare de Montpellier Saint-Roch">Gare Saint-Roch</a>.</li>
<li>After passing Gare Saint-Roch, the line forms a semicircle to serve southern neighbourhoods of the city. Part of this route shares track with Line 2 between Gare Saint-Roch and Nouveau Saint-Roch.</li>
<li>There is an interchange with Line 1 at Port Marianne, then the line travels southeast on Avenue de la Mer and Route de Carnon, and splits with a branch to Lattes and a branch to Pérols.
<ul>
<li>In the community of <a href="/wiki/Lattes,_H%C3%A9rault" title="Lattes, Hérault">Lattes</a>, the branch runs from a station in the community of Boirargues, going on its own right-of-way to the centre of Lattes.</li>
<li>In the community of <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A9rols" title="Pérols">Pérols</a>, the branch runs in its own right-of-way parallel to Route de Carnon to a terminus in the southeast of Pérols. The terminus is 800 metres from the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Line 3 cost €530 million. Liveries of the trams on Line 3 were designed with an underwater theme by designer <a href="/wiki/Christian_Lacroix" title="Christian Lacroix">Christian Lacroix</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>An extension of the line to the Mediterranean coast at Carnon-Plage, or even as far as La Grande Motte, has been planned for years, but currently neither the route nor the funding has been secured for this plan.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Line_4">Line 4</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montpellier_tramway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Line 4">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Montpellier_-_Tram_4_-_Details_(7716584124).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3072" data-file-width="4096" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Montpellier_-_Tram_4_-_Details_%287716584124%29.jpg/220px-Montpellier_-_Tram_4_-_Details_%287716584124%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Montpellier_-_Tram_4_-_Details_%287716584124%29.jpg/330px-Montpellier_-_Tram_4_-_Details_%287716584124%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Montpellier_-_Tram_4_-_Details_%287716584124%29.jpg/440px-Montpellier_-_Tram_4_-_Details_%287716584124%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Montpellier_-_Tram_4_-_Details_(7716584124).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>

<p>In June 2009, plans for a fourth line were published. Line 4 was put into service on April 7, 2012. The line is an 9.2 km circular route and consists of 18 stations. It partially runs on parts of lines 1,2 and 3. It is expected to serve 14000 daily riders. 12 40m Alstom Citadis tram will ply the route.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RG0412_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-RG0412-2">[2]</a></sup> The livery for Line 4 reflects the sunshine of the <a href="/wiki/Languedoc-Roussillon" title="Languedoc-Roussillon">Languedoc-Roussillon</a> region. The livery was inspired from old engravings, with a blaze of gold set off by pearls, stones and jewels forms the backdrop for a series of architectural motifs borrowed from the Château du Peyrou and the Three Graces fountain, two nearby monuments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Line 4 makes an effective loop around the city since its last segment, at the west of the medieval city center (<i>L'Écusson</i>), is operational on July 1, 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Line_5">Line 5</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Montpellier_tramway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Line 5">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Proposals for a fifth line were made in 2005, and the current project was officially presented in January 2009.</p>
<p>The route was determined in late 2012 after a public consultation: the 20.5 km long project was divided up into two phases, the first one consisting of a 15.5 km long light rail between Lavérune, West of Montpellier, and Clapiers, North of town. The second phase, consisting of a northward extension of the first phase towards Prades-le-Lez in the outer suburbs, is expected for 2020, but remains unfunded as of 2013. The initial cost for the new infrastructure was 350 million euros, with the majority of the funding coming from the Montpellier metropolitan authorities, complemented by subsidies from the State, the Region and the Département of Hérault. The public inquiry for the first phase took place in April–May 2013.</p>
<p>Construction was expected to begin in late 2013-early 2014, but a moratorium was established between 2014 and 2016 due to funding issues and protests along part of the route within the Parc Montcalm. But in April 2016, the mayor of Montpellier Philippe Saurel announced that the project is envisaged for 2025 with a shorter and cheaper route.</p>
