<p><b>Mölnlycke</b> is a small town nearby <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greater_Gothenburg" title="Greater Gothenburg">Greater Gothenburg</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Urban_areas_in_Sweden" title="Urban areas in Sweden">locality</a> and the seat of <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A4rryda_Municipality" title="Härryda Municipality">Härryda Municipality</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4stra_G%C3%B6taland_County" title="Västra Götaland County">Västra Götaland County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>. It had 15,608 inhabitants in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scb_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-scb-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Urban_areas_in_Sweden" title="Urban areas in Sweden">urban area</a> stretches over two municipalities, <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6lndal_Municipality" title="Mölndal Municipality">Mölndal Municipality</a> is the second.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%B6lnlycke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first people to settle in the area that would later become Mölnlycke came there during the <a href="/wiki/Stone_Age" title="Stone Age">Stone Age</a>. At this time there were five lakes, which today are formed into one single lake. The lake shore is one of the places where many ancient remains have been found. It is speculated that when people learned to farm, this population moved out of the region to an area better for farming. The next known human settlement was during the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, and this is also when the first <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B6dra_R%C3%A5da_Old_Church" title="Södra Råda Old Church">Råda</a> Church was built. During the Middle Ages, the border with <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> went through what is today Mölnlycke and therefore the area was much affected by war between Denmark and <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-M.C3.B6lnlycke_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-M.C3.B6lnlycke-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first time the name Mölnlycke was mentioned that we know of was 1576. The name was originally made up of the two <a href="/wiki/Old_Swedish" title="Old Swedish">Old Swedish</a> words “mölna” and “lycka”, literally meaning mill field. It probably came from a mill built at the location of today's Mölnlycke Fabriker during the Middle Ages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-M.C3.B6lnlycke_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-M.C3.B6lnlycke-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1849, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gustaf_Ferdinand_Hennig&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gustaf Ferdinand Hennig (page does not exist)">Gustaf Ferdinand Hennig</a> founded <i>Mölnlycke Väfveri AB</i>, a textile manufacturer, which has had a big impact on Mölnlycke. In addition to attracting many workers to the city, it also led to the building of the railway between <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bor%C3%A5s" title="Borås">Borås</a>, which today still has a stop in Mölnlycke.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-M.C3.B6lnlycke_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-M.C3.B6lnlycke-2">[2]</a></sup> Today the company is called <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6lnlycke_Health_Care" title="Mölnlycke Health Care">Mölnlycke Health Care</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%B6lnlycke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mölnlycke is located at a height of 91 meters above sea level<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and is about 10 km from <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a>, the second largest city in Sweden. The short distance to Gothenburg is probably one of the factors of the quick expansion of the city that started during the 20th century, when many people started commuting between Mölnlycke and Gothenburg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%B6lnlycke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mölnlycke Kulturhus, located in the center of Mölnlycke, contains Mölnlycke library, a cinema and a café.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
