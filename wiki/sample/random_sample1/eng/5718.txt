<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Sokka Pagoda of the Pohyonsa Buddhist temple</h1>

<p>The <b>Sokka Pagoda</b> of the <a href="/wiki/Pohyonsa" title="Pohyonsa">Pohyonsa</a> Buddhist temple is 13-storeyed octagonal pagoda located at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mt._Myohyang" title="Mt. Myohyang">Mt. Myohyang</a>, Hyangam-ri, Hyangsan County, North Phyongan Province, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>. It is listed as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Treasures_of_North_Korea" title="National Treasures of North Korea">National Treasure of North Korea</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Details">Details</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sokka_Pagoda_of_the_Pohyonsa_Buddhist_temple&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Details">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Built in 1042 AD,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> the granite structure (also called Sokga Pagoda) was built during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Koryo_Dynasty" title="Koryo Dynasty">Koryo Dynasty</a>. It is 10.03 m high with a 6.58 m body. The body of the pagoda tapers off gradually from the bottom upwards. The eaves of the octagonal roof stone and the eaves of each storey are slightly turned-up at the tips giving them a buoyed look; a total 104 bells are hung on each tip of the eaves. A <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">bronze</a> ornament is on top of the pagoda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naenara_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-naenara-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The various bells and the gilt bronze ornament were seriously damaged by US bombings during the Korean War. They have since been restored to their original state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naenara_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-naenara-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The pagoda is the male counterpart to the feminine <a href="/wiki/Tabo_Pagoda_of_Pohyonsa_Buddhist_temple" title="Tabo Pagoda of Pohyonsa Buddhist temple">Tabo Pagoda</a> of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pulguska_Temple&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pulguska Temple (page does not exist)">Pulguska Temple</a>; it inspired the design of the French Embassy in Seoul by Korean architect <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Chung-up&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kim Chung-up (page does not exist)">Kim Chung-up</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
