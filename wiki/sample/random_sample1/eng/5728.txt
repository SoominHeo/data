<p><b>Murad Bey Mohammed</b> (<abbr title="circa">c.</abbr> 1750 – 22 April 1801) was an <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptian</a> <a href="/wiki/Mamluk" title="Mamluk">Mamluk</a> chieftain (<a href="/wiki/Bey" title="Bey">Bey</a>), <a href="/wiki/Cavalry" title="Cavalry">cavalry</a> commander and joint ruler of Egypt with <a href="/wiki/Ibrahim_Bey_(Mamluk)" title="Ibrahim Bey (Mamluk)">Ibrahim Bey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldschmidt_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Goldschmidt-1">[1]</a></sup> He is often remembered as being a cruel and extortionate ruler, but an energetic courageous fighter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldschmidt_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Goldschmidt-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murad_Bey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Murad was of <a href="/wiki/Georgians" title="Georgians">Georgian</a> origin from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiflis" title="Tiflis">Tiflis</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EFRNW_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-EFRNW-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Circassians" title="Circassians">Circassian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1768 he was sold to the (circassian) Mamluk <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muhammad_Bey_Abu_al-Dhahab" title="Muhammad Bey Abu al-Dhahab">Muhammad Bey Abu al-Dhahab</a> in Egypt.</p>
<p>After the death of his master <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muhammad_Bey_Abu_al-Dhahab" title="Muhammad Bey Abu al-Dhahab">Muhammad Bey Abu al-Dhahab</a>, Murad Bey was in command of the Mamluk army, whereas <a href="/wiki/Ibrahim_Bey" title="Ibrahim Bey">Ibrahim Bey</a> was in charge of administrative duties of Egypt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pawly_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pawly-6">[6]</a></sup> They survived through the persistent Ottoman attempts at overthrowing the Mamluk regime and civil strifes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EFRNW_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-EFRNW-2">[2]</a></sup> They served as <a href="/wiki/Kaymakam" title="Kaymakam">kaymakams</a> (acting governors) in Egypt on occasion, although they effectively held <i>de facto</i> power for decades, even over the appointed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottoman_governor_of_Egypt" title="Ottoman governor of Egypt">Ottoman governor of Egypt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F93_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F93-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F138_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F138-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F156_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F156-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1786, the Ottoman sultan <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abd%C3%BClhamid_I" title="Abdülhamid I">Abdülhamid I</a> sent <a href="/wiki/Kapudan_Pasha" title="Kapudan Pasha">Kapudan Pasha</a> (grand admiral of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Navy" title="Ottoman Navy">Ottoman Navy</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Cezayirli_Gazi_Hasan_Pasha" title="Cezayirli Gazi Hasan Pasha">Cezayirli Gazi Hasan Pasha</a> to drive out Ibrahim and Murad Bey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F181_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F181-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sicilli_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-sicilli-11">[11]</a></sup> Hasan Pasha was fervent and thorough in his efforts and succeeded in the short term, reestablishing direct <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> control over Egypt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F181_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F181-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ismail_Bey" title="Ismail Bey">Ismail Bey</a> was appointed as new Mamluk leader and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shaykh_al-Balad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shaykh al-Balad (page does not exist)">Shaykh al-Balad</a> (civil governor and de facto ruler). However, in 1791, only five years after their expulsion by Hasan Pasha, the duumvirate returned to Cairo from hiding in southern Egypt and took back <i>de facto</i> control.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F317.E2.80.93322.2C_373_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-JabartiPhilipp1994-2.2F317.E2.80.93322.2C_373-12">[12]</a></sup> At this time, Murad Bey served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amir_al-Hajj" title="Amir al-Hajj">Amir al-Hajj</a> (Commander of the Hajj).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>He commanded the Mamluk cavalry and <a href="/wiki/Janissaries" title="Janissaries">Janissaries</a>' infantry in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Shubra_Khit" title="Battle of Shubra Khit">Battle of Shubra Khit</a> on 13 July 1798, but he was defeated by the French <i>Armée</i> and withdrew from the fight. Eight days later, on 21 July, he commanded the Mamluk cavalry during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Pyramids" title="Battle of the Pyramids">Battle of the Pyramids</a>, alongside Ibrahim Bey, and was defeated at the hands of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon</a>'s armies. While Ibrahim Bey fled towards Sinai, Murad had fled to <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a> first and then to <a href="/wiki/Upper_Egypt" title="Upper Egypt">Upper Egypt</a> and began mounting a brief <a href="/wiki/Guerrilla_warfare" title="Guerrilla warfare">guerrilla</a> campaign that staved off <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_Charles_Antoine_Desaix_de_Veygoux" title="Louis Charles Antoine Desaix de Veygoux">Desaix</a> for a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pawly_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pawly-6">[6]</a></sup> It was while pursuing Murad Bey into Upper Egypt that the French discovered the monuments at <a href="/wiki/Dendera" title="Dendera">Dendera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thebes,_Egypt" title="Thebes, Egypt">Thebes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edfu" title="Edfu">Edfu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philae" title="Philae">Philae</a>.</p>
<p>Murad had reportedly offered money to the French forces to leave Egypt and offered to ally himself with the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> in exchange for allowing the British to occupy <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Damietta" title="Damietta">Damietta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rosetta" title="Rosetta">Rosetta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldschmidt_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Goldschmidt-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1800, Murad made peace with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jean_Baptiste_Kl%C3%A9ber" title="Jean Baptiste Kléber">Jean Baptiste Kléber</a>, and agreed to <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a> Cairo, but died of <a href="/wiki/Bubonic_plague" title="Bubonic plague">bubonic plague</a> on his journey there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EFRNW_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-EFRNW-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
