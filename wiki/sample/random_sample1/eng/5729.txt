<p>The <b>Muravyov-Amursky Peninsula</b> (Russian: полуостров Муравьёва-Амурского) is a <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Peter_the_Great_Gulf" title="Peter the Great Gulf">Peter the Great Gulf</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Muravyov-Amursky_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It subdivides the gulf into <a href="/wiki/Amur_Bay" title="Amur Bay">Amur Bay</a> on its west, and <a href="/wiki/Ussuri_Bay" title="Ussuri Bay">Ussuri Bay</a> on its east. It is approximately 30 km long and 12 km wide.</p>
<p>Named after Count <a href="/wiki/Nikolay_Muravyov-Amursky" title="Nikolay Muravyov-Amursky">Nikolay Muravyov-Amursky</a>, the peninsula is the site of the city of <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Bosphorus" title="Eastern Bosphorus">Eastern Bosphorus</a> separates the city and peninsula from the <a href="/wiki/Russky_Island" title="Russky Island">Russky Island</a>. The eastern shore of the peninsula contains the sandy beaches of the <a href="/wiki/Lazurnaya_Bay" title="Lazurnaya Bay">Lazurnaya Bay</a>.</p>
