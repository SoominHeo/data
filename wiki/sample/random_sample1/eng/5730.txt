<p><b>Murakami</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">村上市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Murakami-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in far northern <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Prefecture" title="Niigata Prefecture">Niigata Prefecture</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_region" title="Hokuriku region">Hokuriku region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 1 June 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Murakami,_Niigata&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the city had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 61,747 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 52.6 persons per km². Its total area was 1,174.26 square kilometres (453.38 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murakami,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Murakai was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Echigo_Province" title="Echigo Province">Echigo Province</a>. Murakami developed as a port and a <a href="/wiki/Castle_town" title="Castle town">castle town</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Murakami_Domain" title="Murakami Domain">Murakami Domain</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_shogunate" title="Tokugawa shogunate">Tokugawa shogunate</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. Even today, the downtown area shows the influence of its past existence as a residence of <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a> and merchants. Following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meiji_restoration" title="Meiji restoration">Meiji restoration</a>, the area became part of <a href="/wiki/Iwafune_District,_Niigata" title="Iwafune District, Niigata">Iwafune District, Niigata</a>, and the town of Murakami was established with the establishment of the municipalities system on April 1, 1889. Murakami was raised to city status on March 31, 1954. OnApril 1, 2008, the towns of <a href="/wiki/Arakawa,_Niigata" title="Arakawa, Niigata">Arakawa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sanpoku,_Niigata" title="Sanpoku, Niigata">Sanpoku</a> and the villages of <a href="/wiki/Asahi,_Niigata" title="Asahi, Niigata">Asahi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kamihayashi,_Niigata" title="Kamihayashi, Niigata">Kamihayashi</a> (all from <a href="/wiki/Iwafune_District,_Niigata" title="Iwafune District, Niigata">Iwafune District</a>) were merged into Murakami.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murakami,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Murakami has 21 public elementary schools and seven public middle schools operated by the city. There is one public middle school and three public high schools operated by the Niigata Prefectural Board of Education. These is also one special education school.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_attractions">Local attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murakami,_Niigata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Local attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Famous products of Murakami are <a href="/wiki/Green_tea" title="Green tea">tea</a> (Murakami is the northernmost tea-growing spot in Japan), <a href="/wiki/Chum_salmon" title="Chum salmon">salmon</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wagy%C5%AB" title="Wagyū">Murakami beef</a>. The city is also a tourist spot because of famous Senami <a href="/wiki/Onsen" title="Onsen">Onsen</a> and as a jumping off point to <a href="/wiki/Awashimaura,_Niigata" title="Awashimaura, Niigata">Awashima Island</a>.</p>
