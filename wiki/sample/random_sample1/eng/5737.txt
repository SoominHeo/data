<p><b>Murghab</b> (<a href="/wiki/Tajik_language" title="Tajik language">Tajik</a> <b>Мурғоб</b>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a> <b>Мургаб</b>, from the Persian word مرغاب (margh-ab) meaning "prairie river") is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Murghob_District" title="Murghob District">Murghob District</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pamir_Mountains" title="Pamir Mountains">Pamir Mountains</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gorno-Badakhshan_Autonomous_Region" title="Gorno-Badakhshan Autonomous Region">Gorno-Badakhshan Autonomous Region</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>. With a population of 4,000, Murghab is about the only significant town the eastern half of Gorno-Badakhshan. It is the highest town in <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a> (and of the former <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>) at 3,650 m above sea level. It located at the junction of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Murghab_River_(Tajikistan)" title="Murghab River (Tajikistan)">Murghab River</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pamir_Highway" title="Pamir Highway">Pamir Highway</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/M41_highway" title="M41 highway">Pamir Highway</a> goes north to <a href="/wiki/Sary-Tash" title="Sary-Tash">Sary-Tash</a> and on to Osh in Kyrgyzstan and southwest to the region's capital <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khorog" title="Khorog">Khorog</a> (about 300 kilometers distant). A newer road goes east over the <a href="/wiki/Kulma_Pass" title="Kulma Pass">Kulma Pass</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Karakoram_Highway" title="Karakoram Highway">Karakoram Highway</a> in China connecting to <a href="/wiki/Tashkurgan_Town" title="Tashkurgan Town">Tashkurgan</a> to the south and <a href="/wiki/Kashgar" title="Kashgar">Kashgar</a> in the north. When the new road opened in May 2004, only Chinese and Tajik nationals were allowed to cross the Tajik–Chinese border at that point;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> the status of the border crossing in 2010 remains the same and according to locals in near future no plans are to open Kulma for other nationals.</p>
<p>Murghab's location at the hub of these roads makes it a regional center for trade, reputedly including a significant <a href="/wiki/Illegal_drug_trade" title="Illegal drug trade">trade in illicit drugs</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pamirsky_Post">Pamirsky Post</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murghab,_Tajikistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pamirsky Post">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The place was founded by the Russians as <b>Pamirsky Post</b> in 1893, as their most advanced military <a href="/wiki/Outpost_(military)" title="Outpost (military)">outpost</a> into <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>.</p>
<p>The modern town was constructed during the period of <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> rule of Tajikistan as a rest stop along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pamir_Highway" title="Pamir Highway">Pamir Highway</a>. The population is about 25% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pamiri_people" title="Pamiri people">Pamiri</a> and 75% <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyz_people" title="Kyrgyz people">Kyrgyz</a>. Murghab is expected to become a major center for trade between Xinjiang and central Asia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murghab,_Tajikistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Murghab,_Tajikistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_Climate_Classification" title="Köppen Climate Classification">Köppen Climate Classification</a> sub-type for Murghab is "ET" or a Tundra Climate. The average annual temperature is −3.9 °C (25.0 °F). The warmest month is July with an average temperature of 8.7 °C (47.7 °F) and the coolest month is January with an average temperature of −18.7 °C (−1.7 °F). The average annual precipitation is 347.7 mm (13.69 in) and has an average of 87.1 days with precipitation. The wettest month is May with an average of 45.1 mm (1.78 in) of precipitation and the driest month is September with an average of 10.9 mm (0.43 in) of precipitation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
