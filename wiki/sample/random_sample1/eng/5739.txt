<p><b>Moomin World</b> (<a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a>: <span lang="fi" xml:lang="fi"><i>Muumimaailma</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>: <span lang="sv" xml:lang="sv"><i>Muminvärlden</i></span>) is the <a href="/wiki/Moomin" title="Moomin">Moomin</a> Theme Park especially for children, based on the <a href="/wiki/Moomin" title="Moomin">Moomin</a> books by <a href="/wiki/Tove_Jansson" title="Tove Jansson">Tove Jansson</a>. Moomin World is on the island of Kailo beside the old town of <a href="/wiki/Naantali" title="Naantali">Naantali</a>, near the city of <a href="/wiki/Turku" title="Turku">Turku</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Finland" title="Southwest Finland">Southwest Finland</a> region.</p>
<p>The blueberry-coloured <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moomin_House" title="Moomin House">Moomin House</a> is the main attraction. Tourists are allowed to freely visit all five storeys. Hemulen's yellow house is located next to the Moomin House. It is also possible to see Moominmamma's Kitchen, Fire Station, Snufkin's Camp, Moominpappa's boat, etc. in Moomin World. Visitors may meet Moomin characters there or the Witch in her cottage.</p>
<p>Moomin World isn't a traditional amusement park, as it does not have rides. There are many activities and fantasy paths for children there including Toffle's Path with Witch's Labyrinth, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Hattifatteners" title="The Hattifatteners">The Hattifatteners</a>' Cave and <a href="/wiki/The_Groke" title="The Groke">The Groke</a>'s House. There are also performances in the covered outdoor Moomin Theatre Emma.</p>
<p>Moomin World is open daily from mid-June to mid-August. The nearby <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4ski" title="Väski">Väski</a> Adventure Island<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is also a place to visit with attractions and activities for primary school children and adults as well.</p>
<p><b>Metsä</b> in Japan is a Moomin theme park outside of Finland. Metsä, or 'forest' in the Finnish language, was initially planned to start operation in 2015 for the 100th birth year of Tove Jansson,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and on June 30, 2015, it is announced to open as early as 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Situated in <a href="/wiki/Hann%C5%8D,_Saitama" title="Hannō, Saitama">Hanno</a> city, northwest of Tokyo, the site will include a Moomin zone for attractions, and a free public zone or a park area. Construction site is purchased on hills along the Miyazawa reservoir.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moomin_World&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moomin World is the world's fourth best theme park for children according to <i>The <a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">Independent on Sunday</a></i> (October 2005). Moomin World was elected as the domestic travel destination of 2005 (Matkamessut, Finnish Travel Fair 2006). Moomin World got the Golden Pony Award 2007 by <i>The Games &amp; Parks Industry</i> magazine. The jury said: "Moomin World is welcoming, well themed and full of educational content."</p>
