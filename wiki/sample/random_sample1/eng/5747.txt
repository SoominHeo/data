<p>The <b>Moose River</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> river in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hudson_Plains" title="Hudson Plains">Hudson Plains</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecozone" title="Ecozone">ecozone</a> of northern <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a> which flows 100 km northeast from the junction of the <a href="/wiki/Mattagami_River" title="Mattagami River">Mattagami</a> and <a href="/wiki/Missinaibi_River" title="Missinaibi River">Missinaibi Rivers</a> into <a href="/wiki/James_Bay" title="James Bay">James Bay</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">drainage basin</a> is 108,500 km<sup>2</sup> and it has a mean discharge rate of 1370 m<sup>3</sup>/s. Its full length is 547 kilometres (340 mi) if counted from the head of the Mattagami River.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AtCa_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-AtCa-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>This river formed part of the water route to <a href="/wiki/Lake_Superior" title="Lake Superior">Lake Superior</a> in the days of the <a href="/wiki/North_American_fur_trade" title="North American fur trade">fur trade</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moose_Factory,_Ontario" title="Moose Factory, Ontario">Moose Factory</a>, located on <a href="/wiki/Moose_Factory_Island" title="Moose Factory Island">Moose Factory Island</a> near the river's mouth, was a fur trading post of the <a href="/wiki/Hudson%27s_Bay_Company" title="Hudson's Bay Company">Hudson's Bay Company</a> and Ontario's first <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> settlement. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moosonee,_Ontario" title="Moosonee, Ontario">Moosonee, Ontario</a>, on the north bank of the river, is the northern terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Polar_Bear_Express" title="Polar Bear Express">Polar Bear Express</a> railway route which begins at <a href="/wiki/Cochrane,_Ontario" title="Cochrane, Ontario">Cochrane, Ontario</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Moose_River_Bird_Sanctuary">Moose River Bird Sanctuary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moose_River_(Ontario)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Moose River Bird Sanctuary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moose River Bird Sanctuary lies at the mouth of the Moose River and comprises Ship Sands Island and a piece of land on the eastern flats of the river mouth. The 14.60 km<sup>2</sup> sanctuary is protected under the <a href="/wiki/Migratory_Birds_Convention_Act" title="Migratory Birds Convention Act">Migratory Birds Convention Act</a> and is part of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_James_Bay" title="Southern James Bay">Southern James Bay</a> wetland complex, which was designated a wetland of international importance (<a href="/wiki/Ramsar_Convention" title="Ramsar Convention">Ramsar Convention</a>) in May 1987.</p>
<p>This area plays a significant role in the annual cycle of <a href="/wiki/Waterfowl" title="Waterfowl">waterfowl</a>. The funnel-shaped outline of Hudson and James bays causes birds migrating from the Arctic to concentrate at the southern end of James Bay each autumn, particularly in the late autumn, where the extensive coastal wetlands provide critical staging and moulting areas for migrating <a href="/wiki/Snow_goose" title="Snow goose">lesser snow geese</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dabbling_duck" title="Dabbling duck">dabbling ducks</a> and shorebirds such as <a href="/wiki/Red_knot" title="Red knot">red knot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Short-billed_dowitcher" title="Short-billed dowitcher">short-billed dowitcher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dunlin" title="Dunlin">dunlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greater_yellowlegs" title="Greater yellowlegs">greater yellowlegs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lesser_yellowlegs" title="Lesser yellowlegs">lesser yellowlegs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ruddy_turnstone" title="Ruddy turnstone">ruddy turnstone</a>, and <a href="/wiki/American_golden_plover" title="American golden plover">American golden plover</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Settlement_of_Moose_River">Settlement of Moose River</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moose_River_(Ontario)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Settlement of Moose River">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At mile 142 of the Polar Bear Express railway route there is a small settlement called Moose River. This settlement is just before a large railway bridge that crosses Moose River and is a flag stop on the Polar Bear Express rail route. There is an <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Northland_Railway" title="Ontario Northland Railway">Ontario Northland Railway</a> bunkhouse that used to be a school. The Moose River bridge was built in the 1930s and the settlement was a much larger community at that time. Currently the Moose River stop at mile 142 is used for the residents of the settlement and as a pick-up and drop-off site for boaters.</p>
