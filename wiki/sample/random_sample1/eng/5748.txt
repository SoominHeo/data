<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Muspelheim</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>In <a href="/wiki/Norse_mythology" title="Norse mythology">Norse mythology</a>, <b>Muspelheim</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Norse_language" title="Old Norse language">Old Norse</a>: <span lang="non" xml:lang="non"><i>Múspellsheimr</i></span>), also called <b>Muspell</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Norse_language" title="Old Norse language">Old Norse</a>: <span lang="non" xml:lang="non"><i><b>Múspell</b></i></span>), is a realm of <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">fire</a>. This realm is one of the <a href="/wiki/Norse_cosmology" title="Norse cosmology">Nine Worlds</a>, ruled by <a href="/wiki/Surtr" title="Surtr">Surtr</a> with his consort <a href="/wiki/Sinmara" title="Sinmara">Sinmara</a> in some accounts. The denizens of Muspelheim were usually referred to as the <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eldj%C3%B6tnar" title="Eldjötnar">Eldjötnar</a></b> (or <b>Eldthursar</b>, <i>Eldþursar</i> — "fire giants") in Norse tradition, though they were also identified by other epithets in Eddic poetry, such as the <b>Múspellssynir</b> (or <b>Múspellsmegir</b> — "sons of Muspell"; altn. <i>Múspellmegir</i>, <i>Múspellsynir</i>) and the <b>Rjúfendr</b> (from <i>rjúfa</i> — "to break, tear asunder", <i>Destroyers of Doomsday</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Both of these terms sometimes described an entirely separate mythological species that dwelled alongside or in place of the eldjötnar within this fiery realm. Muspelheim is fire; and the land to the North, <a href="/wiki/Niflheim" title="Niflheim">Niflheim</a>, is ice. The two mixed and created water from the melting ice in <a href="/wiki/Ginnungagap" title="Ginnungagap">Ginnungagap</a>. The sun and the stars originate from Muspelheim.</p>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Ragnar%C3%B6k" title="Ragnarök">Ragnarök</a> prophecies in <a href="/wiki/Snorri_Sturluson" title="Snorri Sturluson">Snorri Sturluson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Gylfaginning" title="Gylfaginning">Gylfaginning</a>, the first part of his <a href="/wiki/Prose_Edda" title="Prose Edda">Prose Edda</a>, the sons of Muspell will break the <a href="/wiki/Bifr%C3%B6st" title="Bifröst">Bifröst</a> bridge, signaling the end of times:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>In the midst of this clash and din the heavens are rent in twain, and the sons of Muspell come riding through the opening. Surtr rides first, and before him and after him flames burning fire. He has a very good sword, which shines brighter than the sun. As they ride over Bifrost it breaks to pieces, as has before been stated. The sons of Muspel direct their course to the plain which is called Vigrid.... The sons of Muspel have there effulgent bands alone by themselves.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The etymology of "Muspelheim" is uncertain, but may come from <i>Mund-spilli</i>, "world-destroyers", "wreck of the world".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Muspelheim&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The realm of Muspelheim appears as 'Muspel' in <a href="/wiki/Gene_Wolfe" title="Gene Wolfe">Gene Wolfe</a>'s book series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wizard_Knight" title="The Wizard Knight">The Wizard Knight</a></i>. It is inhabited by the dragon 'Setr', as a close parallel to Surtr.</li>
<li>"Muspel" is the name of a German colony in Africa in the alternate-reality novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Afrika_Reich" title="The Afrika Reich">The Afrika Reich</a></i> by Guy Saville.</li>
<li>Muspelheim is the name of a reoccurring boss ship in the <i><a href="/wiki/Naval_Ops:_Warship_Gunner" title="Naval Ops: Warship Gunner">Naval Ops: Warship Gunner</a></i> series.</li>
<li>A glimpse of Muspelheim is seen in the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Studios" title="Marvel Studios">Marvel Studios</a> film <a href="/wiki/Thor:_The_Dark_World" title="Thor: The Dark World">Thor: The Dark World</a>.</li>
<li>Múspelheim appears in the science fiction novel <i>Resonance: Ragnarok: Volume Three</i> (Kindle Location 3488) by <a href="/wiki/John_Meaney" title="John Meaney">John Meaney</a></li>
<li>Muspelheim is mentioned in the anime <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sword_Art_Online_II" title="Sword Art Online II">Sword Art Online II</a> during a quest taking place in Jötunheim. Yui - an AI in the show - expresses her concern that even if they complete the quest and defeat Þrymr, the flame giants of Muspelheim will take over Alfheim by burning down Yggdrasil.</li>
<li>In the Video Game <i><a href="/wiki/Valkyrie_Profile" title="Valkyrie Profile">Valkyrie Profile</a></i>, Muspelheim is mentioned within the incantations that compose the great Magic "Ifreet Caress": "I invoke the rites of fiery Muspelheim, now give up thy soul to inferno's embrace! Ifreet Caress!"</li>
<li>In the videogame <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=La_Mulana_2&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="La Mulana 2 (page does not exist)">La Mulana 2</a></i>, Múspelheim (spelled "Muspellheim") is an area within the ruins Eg'Lana and is known as the "Blazing Frost Canopy", a land that's both frozen and on fire. This technically makes it both Múspelheim and Niflheim in one.</li>
<li>In the Japanese light novel <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mahouka_Koukou_no_Rettousei" title="Mahouka Koukou no Rettousei">Mahouka Koukou no Rettousei</a></i>, Muspelheim is the name of a magic spell that creates a field where immense heat, capable of creating plasma, is generated.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/Amon_Amarth" title="Amon Amarth">Amon Amarth</a> song "Destroyer of the Universe" from the album <a href="/wiki/Surtur_Rising" title="Surtur Rising">Surtur Rising</a>, Muspelheim is mentioned in the first verse.</li>
<li>In the Unleashed song "The Bolt Thrower" the title character in the song was "Forged in the fires of Muspelheim."</li>
<li>In the video game <a href="/wiki/Bayonetta_2" title="Bayonetta 2">Bayonetta 2</a> Muspelheim serves as the location of challenge rooms within portals found throughout levels, however the realm is not represented like it is described in Norse Mythology but instead like the typical depictions of Judeo-Christian "Heaven" and "Hell".</li>
<li>In Heroine's Quest, Muspell (actually eldjötnar) appear as enemies.</li>
<li>Múspellsheimr also gives its name to "Muspel," a planet in <a href="/wiki/David_Lindsay_(novelist)" title="David Lindsay (novelist)">David Lindsay</a>'s <a href="/wiki/A_Voyage_to_Arcturus" title="A Voyage to Arcturus">A Voyage to Arcturus</a>.</li>
<li>A zone in the game <a href="/wiki/Dark_Age_of_Camelot" title="Dark Age of Camelot">Dark Age of Camelot</a>. "Home of the Fire Giants and numerous other fire based critters" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>There is a dungeon raid in the MMO <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Heroes_(video_game)" title="Marvel Heroes (video game)">Marvel Heroes</a> which takes place in Muspelheim.</li>
</ul>
