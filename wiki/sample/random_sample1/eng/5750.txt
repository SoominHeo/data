<p>A <b>Muslim</b> is someone who follows or practises <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monotheistic" title="Monotheistic">monotheistic</a> <a href="/wiki/Abrahamic_religions" title="Abrahamic religions">Abrahamic religion</a>. Muslims consider the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a> (Koran), their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holy_book" title="Holy book">holy book</a>, to be the verbatim word of <a href="/wiki/God_in_Islam" title="God in Islam">God</a> as revealed to the <a href="/wiki/Prophets_and_messengers_in_Islam" title="Prophets and messengers in Islam">Islamic prophet and messenger</a> <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_in_Islam" title="Muhammad in Islam">Muhammad</a>. They also follow the teachings and practices of Muhammad (<i><a href="/wiki/Sunnah" title="Sunnah">sunnah</a></i> ) as recorded in traditional accounts (<i><a href="/wiki/Hadith" title="Hadith">hadith</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> "Muslim" is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic word</a> meaning "one who submits (to God)".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The beliefs of Muslims include: that God (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar"><span style="font-size:large;">الله</span></span>‎‎ <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All%C4%81h" title="Allāh"><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="ar ALA transliteration">Allāh</span></a></i>) is eternal, transcendent and absolutely one (<i><a href="/wiki/Tawhid" title="Tawhid">tawhid</a></i> or monotheism); that God is incomparable, self-sustaining and neither begets nor was begotten; that Islam is the complete and universal version of a primordial faith that has been revealed before through many prophets including <a href="/wiki/Abraham_in_Islam" title="Abraham in Islam">Abraham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moses_in_Islam" title="Moses in Islam">Moses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ishmael_in_Islam" title="Ishmael in Islam">Ishmael</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jesus_in_Islam" title="Jesus in Islam">Jesus</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-People-of-the-Book_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-People-of-the-Book-3">[3]</a></sup> that these previous messages and revelations have been partially changed or corrupted over time (<i><a href="/wiki/Tahrif" title="Tahrif">tahrif</a></i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Distorted_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Distorted-4">[4]</a></sup> and that the Qur'an is the final unaltered revelation from God (The Final Testament).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The religious practices of Muslims are enumerated in the <a href="/wiki/Five_Pillars_of_Islam" title="Five Pillars of Islam">Five Pillars of Islam</a>: the declaration of faith (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shahadah" title="Shahadah">shahadah</a></i>), daily prayers (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salat" title="Salat">salat</a></i>), fasting during the month of <a href="/wiki/Ramadan" title="Ramadan">Ramadan</a> (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sawm" title="Sawm">sawm</a></i>), almsgiving (<i><a href="/wiki/Zakat" title="Zakat">zakat</a></i>), and the pilgrimage to <a href="/wiki/Mecca" title="Mecca">Mecca</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Hajj" title="Hajj">hajj</a></i>) at least once in a lifetime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WSU_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-WSU-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIA_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIA-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Who_counts_as_a_Muslim">Who counts as a Muslim</span></h2>
<p>There are customs stating that anyone above the age of fifteen who possesses the faculties of rationality, logic or sanity, but misses numerous successive Friday prayers (<i><a href="/wiki/Jumu%27ah" title="Jumu'ah">jumu'ah</a></i>) without a valid excuse, no longer qualifies as a Muslim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Most Muslims will accept anyone who has publicly pronounced Shahadah as a Muslim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The <i>shahadah</i> states:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>There is no god but the God (Allah) and Muhammad is the last messenger of the God.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The testimony authorized by God in the Quran can found in Surah 3:18 states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TrueIslam_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-TrueIslam-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>"There is no god except God", which in Arabic (La Elaha Ella Allah), is the exact testimony which God Himself utters, also the angels and those who possess knowledge utter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TrueIslam_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-TrueIslam-12">[12]</a></sup></p>

Until at least the mid-1960s, many English-language writers used the term <i><a href="/wiki/Mohammedan" title="Mohammedan">Mohammedans</a></i> or <i>Mahometans</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Although such terms were not necessarily intended to be <a href="/wiki/Pejorative" title="Pejorative">pejorative</a>, Muslims argue that the terms are offensive because they allegedly imply that Muslims worship Muhammad rather than God.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Other obsolete terms include <i>Muslimite</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and <i>Muslimist</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Used_to_describe_earlier_prophets_in_the_Qur.27an">Used to describe earlier prophets in the Qur'an</span></h3>
<p>The Qur'an describes many prophets and messengers within Judiaism and Christianity, and their respective followers, as Muslim: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adam_(Bible)" title="Adam (Bible)">Adam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Noah" title="Noah">Noah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abraham_in_Islam" title="Abraham in Islam">Abraham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacob" title="Jacob">Jacob</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_view_of_Moses" title="Islamic view of Moses">Moses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ishmael_in_Islam" title="Ishmael in Islam">Ishmael</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_view_of_Jesus" title="Islamic view of Jesus">Jesus</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Apostles" title="Apostles">apostles</a> are all considered to be Muslims in the Qur'an. The Qur'an states that these men were Muslims because they submitted to God, preached His message and upheld His values, which included praying, charity, fasting and pilgrimage. Thus, in Surah 3:52 of the Qur'an, Jesus' disciples tell him, "We believe in God; and you be our witness that we are Muslims (<i>wa-shahad be anna muslimūn</i>)." In Muslim belief, before the Qur'an, God had given the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tawrat" title="Tawrat">Tawrat</a> (<a href="/wiki/Torah" title="Torah">Torah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Old_Testament" title="Old Testament">Old Testament</a>) to Moses, the <a href="/wiki/Zabur" title="Zabur">Zabur</a> (<a href="/wiki/Psalms" title="Psalms">Psalms</a>) to <a href="/wiki/David" title="David">David</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Injil" title="Injil">Injil</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gospel" title="Gospel">Gospel</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Testament" title="New Testament">New Testament</a>) to Jesus, who are all considered important <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prophets_of_Islam" title="Prophets of Islam">Muslim prophets</a>.</p>

<p>The most populous Muslim-majority country is Indonesia, home to 12.7% of the world's Muslims,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mgmpPRC_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-mgmpPRC-23">[23]</a></sup> followed by Pakistan (11.0%), Bangladesh (9.2%), and Egypt (4.9%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Islam_by_country_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Islam_by_country-24">[24]</a></sup>. About 20% of the world's Muslims lives in the Middle East and North Africa,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mgmpPRC_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-mgmpPRC-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p><br/>
Sizable minorities are also found in <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_India" title="Islam in India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_China" title="Islam in China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_Russia" title="Islam in Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_Ethiopia" title="Islam in Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_the_Americas" title="Islam in the Americas">the Americas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_Australia" title="Islam in Australia">Australia</a> and parts of <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_Europe" title="Islam in Europe">Europe</a>. The country with the highest proportion of self-described Muslims as a proportion of its total population is Morocco.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Converts and immigrant communities are found in almost every part of the world.</p>
<p>Over 75–90% of Muslims are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunni" title="Sunni">Sunni</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sunni_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sunni-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sunni_Islam_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sunni_Islam-28">[28]</a></sup> The second and third largest sects, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shia" title="Shia">Shia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ahmadiyya" title="Ahmadiyya">Ahmadiyya</a>, make up 10–20%,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shia_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shia-29">[29]</a></sup> and 1%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ahmadi_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-ahmadi-30">[30]</a></sup> respectively.</p>
<p>With about 1.6 billion followers, almost a quarter of <a href="/wiki/World_population" title="World population">earth's population</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mgmpPRC_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-mgmpPRC-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIA_Factbook_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIA_Factbook-32">[32]</a></sup> Islam is the <a href="/wiki/Major_religious_groups" title="Major religious groups">second-largest</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Growth_of_religion" title="Growth of religion">fastest-growing religion in the world</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> due primarily to the young age and high <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fertility_rate" title="Fertility rate">fertility rate</a> of Muslims,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Future_of_World_Religions_p.149_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Future_of_World_Religions_p.149-34">[34]</a></sup> with Muslim having a rate of 3.1 compared to the world average of 2.5.</p>
