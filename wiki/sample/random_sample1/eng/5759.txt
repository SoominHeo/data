<p><b>Al-Muharraq Sports Club</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar">نادي المحرق الرياضي</span>‎‎) is a <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahraini</a> football club based in <a href="/wiki/Muharraq" title="Muharraq">Muharraq</a>. It is one of the oldest sports club in the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula Region</a>. Al-Muharraq Sports Club has won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahraini_Football_Premier_League" title="Bahraini Football Premier League">Bahraini Football Premier League</a> 33 times. Al-Muharraq Sports Club also takes part in other sports than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> like <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">Basketball</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">Volleyball</a>. Historically, Al-Muharraq Sports Club football team resembles most of <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_national_football_team" title="Bahrain national football team">Bahrain national football team</a>.</p>

<p>Al-Muharraq Sports Club have produced some of the current stars of the national team like the captain of the Bahraini National Team <a href="/wiki/Mohamed_Salmeen" title="Mohamed Salmeen">Mohamed Salmeen</a>, Rashid Al Dossary, veteran goalkeeper Ali Hassan, Ali Amer and Ebrahim Al Mishkhas.</p>
<p>Al-Muharraq Sports Club's youth academy has produced players like Abdullah Al Dekheel, Mahmood Abdulrahman, Fahad Showaiter, Hussam Humood Sultan, and Abdullah Al-Kaabi.</p>
<p>Al-Muharraq Sports Club has brought in foreign professionals on certain occasions such as <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> forward <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leandson_Dias_da_Silva" title="Leandson Dias da Silva">Leandson Dias da Silva</a> also known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leandson_Dias_da_Silva" title="Leandson Dias da Silva">Rico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnian</a> Adnan Sarajlic, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> defender <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Juliano_de_Paola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Juliano de Paola (page does not exist)">Juliano de Paola</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jamal_Ebraro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jamal Ebraro (page does not exist)">Jamal Ebraro</a> from <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leandson_Dias_da_Silva" title="Leandson Dias da Silva">Rico</a> won the world's top scorer award in 2008 with 19 goals scored.</p>
<p>2008 was a perfect season for Al-Muharraq Sports Club as they completed a <a href="/wiki/List_of_association_football_teams_to_have_won_four_or_more_trophies_in_one_season" title="List of association football teams to have won four or more trophies in one season">quadruple</a> (Bahraini League, King's Cup, Crown Prince Cup and the <a href="/wiki/AFC_Cup" title="AFC Cup">AFC Cup</a>). Al-Muharraq Sports Club became the first Bahraini club to win a continental championship.</p>
<p>On June 10, 2012 Muharraq won the <a href="/wiki/GCC_Champions_League" title="GCC Champions League">GCC Champions League</a> for the first time.</p>
