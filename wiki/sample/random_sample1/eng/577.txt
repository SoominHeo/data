<p><b>2NE1</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko">투애니원</span>, <small>IPA: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Korean" title="Help:IPA for Korean">[tʰu.ɛ.ni.wʌn]</a></span>) was a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Girl_group" title="Girl group">girl group</a> formed by <a href="/wiki/YG_Entertainment" title="YG Entertainment">YG Entertainment</a> in 2009. The group's final lineup was composed of <a href="/wiki/CL_(singer)" title="CL (singer)">CL</a>, <a href="/wiki/Park_Bom" title="Park Bom">Bom</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sandara_Park" title="Sandara Park">Dara</a>. The fourth and the youngest member, <a href="/wiki/Minzy" title="Minzy">Minzy</a>, officially left the group on April 5, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-minzyleave_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-minzyleave-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>First appearing in "<a href="/wiki/Lollipop_(Big_Bang_and_2NE1_song)" title="Lollipop (Big Bang and 2NE1 song)">Lollipop</a>", a commercial campaign with <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang_(South_Korean_band)" title="Big Bang (South Korean band)">Big Bang</a> for <a href="/wiki/LG_Electronics" title="LG Electronics">LG Electronics</a>, 2NE1's debut single "<a href="/wiki/Fire_(2NE1_song)" title="Fire (2NE1 song)">Fire</a>" was released on May 6, 2009. Since then, the group has released two eponymous <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended plays</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2009_EP)" title="2NE1 (2009 EP)">2NE1</a></i> (2009) and <i><a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2011_EP)" title="2NE1 (2011 EP)">2NE1</a></i> (2011), and two studio albums, <i><a href="/wiki/To_Anyone" title="To Anyone">To Anyone</a></i> (2010) and <i><a href="/wiki/Crush_(2NE1_album)" title="Crush (2NE1 album)">Crush</a></i> (2014). Their first EP spawned the number-one single "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Care_(2NE1_song)" title="I Don't Care (2NE1 song)">I Don't Care</a>", which won them the "Song of the Year" award at the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Mnet_Asian_Music_Awards" title="Mnet Asian Music Awards">Mnet Asian Music Awards</a>. Their follow-up singles, such as "<a href="/wiki/Go_Away_(2NE1_song)" title="Go Away (2NE1 song)">Go Away</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Lonely_(2NE1_song)" title="Lonely (2NE1 song)">Lonely</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I_Am_the_Best" title="I Am the Best">I Am the Best</a>", were similarly successful, with "I Am the Best" winning 2NE1 their second "Song of the Year" award in 2011.</p>
<p>2NE1 officially debuted in Japan in September 2011 with a Japanese version of their second EP, titled <i><a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2011_EP)" title="2NE1 (2011 EP)">Nolza</a></i>. Since then, the group has released one studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Collection_(2NE1_album)" title="Collection (2NE1 album)">Collection</a></i>, and two physical singles in Japan. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a> named 2NE1's performance at the <a href="/wiki/Prudential_Center" title="Prudential Center">Prudential Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> as one of the "Best Concerts of 2012".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In October 2012, Zack Greenburg of <a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a> magazine noted that 2NE1 has sold 27 million digital downloads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes20121016_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-forbes20121016-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group officially disbanded on November 25, 2016, the same day it was revealed Park Bom left YG Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disbandment_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-disbandment-1">[1]</a></sup> Their final single "Good Bye" was released on January 21, 2017, without former member Minzy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finalsingle_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-finalsingle-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-debut">Pre-debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2NE1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>2NE1 was first mentioned by South Korean press in late 2004. Both <a href="/wiki/Spica" title="Spica">Spica</a>'s Bohyung and <a href="/wiki/Fiestar" title="Fiestar">Fiestar</a>'s Linzy were trainees alongside the 2NE1 members. However, they were eliminated before debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/YG_Entertainment" title="YG Entertainment">YG Entertainment</a> formally announced in early 2009 that the group would consist of four members and debut sometime in May of that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-may_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-may-7">[7]</a></sup> The company stated that the group had trained for four years, and that their debut album would contain songs produced by <a href="/wiki/1TYM" title="1TYM">1TYM</a>'s leader <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Park" title="Teddy Park">Teddy Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-may_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-may-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trio_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-trio-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spark_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-spark-9">[9]</a></sup> The group's name was initially announced as "21," however, due to the discovery of a singer with the same name, the group was quickly renamed "2NE1".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The name, pronounced in Korean to approximate English as "To Anyone" or (<a href="/wiki/American_English" title="American English">US</a>) "Twenty One", combines the phrases "21st century" and "New Evolution".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9310:_2NE1_1st_Mini_Album_and_To_Anyone">2009–10: <i>2NE1 1st Mini Album</i> and <i>To Anyone</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2NE1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2009–10: 2NE1 1st Mini Album and To Anyone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang_(South_Korean_band)" title="Big Bang (South Korean band)">Big Bang</a>, 2NE1's first CF song, "<a href="/wiki/Lollipop_(Big_Bang_and_2NE1_song)" title="Lollipop (Big Bang and 2NE1 song)">Lollipop</a>", was digitally released on March 27, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-may_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-may-7">[7]</a></sup> The song was created for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LG" title="LG">LG</a> to promote their Cyon phone. The music video was released on March 28.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cyon_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-cyon-12">[12]</a></sup> "Lollipop" was not a promoted single because it was an advertisement (there were problems with network chart eligibility<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-network_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-network-13">[13]</a></sup>).</p>
<p>2NE1's debut song, "<a href="/wiki/Fire_(2NE1_song)" title="Fire (2NE1 song)">Fire</a>", was released on May 6. The song was written and produced by <a href="/wiki/1TYM" title="1TYM">1TYM</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Park" title="Teddy Park">Teddy Park</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and contained elements of <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip-hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Its music video had two versions — a "space" version and a "street" version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Within the first 24 hours of release, the videos each received over one million views.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daebak_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-daebak-17">[17]</a></sup> The group held their first performance of "Fire" on May 17 on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Inkigayo" title="Inkigayo">Inkigayo</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-first-18">[18]</a></sup> marking the start of the group's activities. 2NE1 also launched their first reality show, <i>2NE1TV</i> to assist the group in their debut promotions. Both the song "Fire" and the group became popular online search terms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The group was awarded three <a href="/wiki/Cyworld" title="Cyworld">Cyworld</a> Digital Music Awards; "Lollipop" and "Fire" both won "Song of the Month" award and the group won "Rookie of the Month" for May 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3timewinners_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-3timewinners-20">[20]</a></sup> 2NE1 received their first Mutizen award after their fourth stage performance in SBS's <i>Inkigayo</i> on June 14. They received their second Mutizen award on the same show the week after. In the same month, 2NE1 signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Fila_(company)" title="Fila (company)">Fila</a> to become the models for their new commercial campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Commercial_deals_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Commercial_deals-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Care_(2NE1_song)" title="I Don't Care (2NE1 song)">I Don't Care</a>" was released as the group's second single on July 1, along with the group's first extended play, <i><a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2009_EP)" title="2NE1 (2009 EP)">2NE1</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-release-22">[22]</a></sup> Promotions for their new release officially commenced in early July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Yang Hyun Suk stated that the song would demonstrate a softer, more feminine image of the group in contrast to "Fire".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-release-22">[22]</a></sup> The single became the most downloaded song of that month. 2NE1 ended promotions for the song by performing a reggae version of "I Don't Care" on <i>Inkigayo</i> in September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unplugged_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-unplugged-24">[24]</a></sup> Due to the remix's popularity, it was released to digital outlets on September 3 as their next digital single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unplugged_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-unplugged-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August, 2NE1 took a temporary hiatus from group promotions, with each member releasing their own solo singles. Along with three other groups representing Korea, 2NE1 performed at the 6th <a href="/wiki/Asia_Song_Festival" title="Asia Song Festival">Asia Song Festival</a> in September, where they received the 2009's "Asian Newcomer's Award".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-songfest_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-songfest-25">[25]</a></sup> 2NE1 received many year-end awards, including the "Song of the Year" award at <a href="/wiki/Mnet_Asian_Music_Awards" title="Mnet Asian Music Awards">Mnet Asian Music Awards</a>. Their debut EP also reached sold a total of 100,000 copies by the end of the year.</p>
<p>On February 9, 2010, without previous announcement, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Follow_Me_(2NE1_song)" title="Follow Me (2NE1 song)">Follow Me</a>" was released via digital outlets and used as the promotional song for <a href="/wiki/Samsung" title="Samsung">Samsung</a>'s Corby Folder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Over the summer of 2010, 2NE1 traveled to Los Angeles and London in order to record English songs for an American debut album with <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" title="The Black Eyed Peas">The Black Eyed Peas</a>'s member and music producer <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a>. The group recorded a total of 10 songs in their initial sessions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-americandebut_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-americandebut-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>The group's first full-length album, <i><a href="/wiki/To_Anyone" title="To Anyone">To Anyone</a></i>, was released on September 9, and debuted at number 7 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> World Album Chart. Prior to the release of the album, it had already received 120,000 preorders. The album contains 12 tracks, including three title tracks, "<a href="/wiki/Clap_Your_Hands_(2NE1_song)" title="Clap Your Hands (2NE1 song)">Clap Your Hands</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Go_Away_(2NE1_song)" title="Go Away (2NE1 song)">Go Away</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Nobody_(2NE1_song)" title="Can't Nobody (2NE1 song)">Can't Nobody</a>", which were promoted simultaneously. 2NE1 won first place on <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M!_Countdown" title="M! Countdown">M! Countdown</a></i> for "Clap Your Hands" on the day of their comeback, followed by a K-Chart win the next day on <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i> for "Go Away", and a Mutizen a week after for their <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Inkigayo" title="Inkigayo">Inkigayo</a></i> performance of "Can't Nobody". Overall, 2NE1 won first place eleven times on various music shows throughout the promotional period for <i>To Anyone</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11_Times_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-11_Times-28">[28]</a></sup> To promote the album, the group made their first entertainment show appearance as a group on KBS variety-talk show <i>Win Win</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Win_Win_2010_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-Win_Win_2010-29">[29]</a></sup> while also airing the second season of <i>2NE1TV</i>. On October 21, Yang Hyun Suk announced that 2NE1 would be releasing a follow-up single on October 31, later revealed to be "<a href="/wiki/It_Hurts_(Slow)" title="It Hurts (Slow)">It Hurts (Slow)</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Halloween_song_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Halloween_song-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-It_Hurts_single_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-It_Hurts_single-31">[31]</a></sup> It was first performed on SBS's <i>Inkigayo</i>. The music video for the single was released on November 1 and utilized a <a href="/wiki/Halloween" title="Halloween">Halloween</a> theme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-It_Hurts_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-It_Hurts-32">[32]</a></sup> On November 26, 2NE1 released the single "Don't Stop the Music" as a "special gift for Thai fans" and as the promotional accompaniment to the group's <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Company" title="Yamaha Motor Company">Yamaha Fiore</a> endorsement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Don.27t_Stop_the_Music_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Don.27t_Stop_the_Music-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of 2010, 2NE1 took home the "Artist of the Year" award and the "Album of the Year" award at the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Mnet_Asian_Music_Awards" title="Mnet Asian Music Awards">Mnet Asian Music Awards</a>, thus making them the only artist to win all 3 grand awards at the said award ceremony.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.9312:_2NE1_2nd_Mini_Album.2C_Japanese_releases_and_New_Evolution">2011–12: <i>2NE1 2nd Mini Album</i>, Japanese releases and <i>New Evolution</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2NE1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2011–12: 2NE1 2nd Mini Album, Japanese releases and New Evolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On January 19, 2011, "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Nobody_(2NE1_song)" title="Can't Nobody (2NE1 song)">Can't Nobody</a> (English Version)" was released on the Japanese digital retailer Recochoku as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ringtones" title="Ringtones">ringtone</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ringback_tones" title="Ringback tones">ringback tone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-recochoku1_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-recochoku1-34">[34]</a></sup> On February 2, the "Can't Nobody (English Version)" music video was made available for download through <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> Japan. On February 22, Avex announced that 2NE1 would release their Japanese debut single "<a href="/wiki/Go_Away_(2NE1_song)" title="Go Away (2NE1 song)">Go Away</a>", previously used as a Korean promotional single, as a ringtone on March 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-go_away_single_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-go_away_single-35">[35]</a></sup> It was later announced to be the theme song of Japanese television program <i><a href="/wiki/Mezamashi_TV" title="Mezamashi TV">Mezamashi TV</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mezamashi_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-mezamashi-36">[36]</a></sup> On March 16, the group released their first Japanese extended play, <i>2NE1</i>, with no promotions held because of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dhoku_Earthquake_and_tsunami" title="Tōhoku Earthquake and tsunami">Tōhoku Earthquake and tsunami</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-situation_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-situation-37">[37]</a></sup> It debuted at #24 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oricon_charts" title="Oricon charts">Oricon charts</a>, selling 3,860 copies in its first week of release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2ne1_week_1_oricon_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-2ne1_week_1_oricon-38">[38]</a></sup> 2NE1 initially planned to make their official Japanese debut live on <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Station" title="Music Station">Music Station</a></i>, but instead postponed their debut to the fall of 2011 due to the earthquake.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-music_station_cancelled_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-music_station_cancelled-39">[39]</a></sup> 2NE1 participated in the Naver "Pray for Japan" campaign alongside many other Korean celebrities, helping to raise funds for earthquake victims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pray_for_japan_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-pray_for_japan-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 18, 2NE1 announced their plans for a Korean comeback, pushing the release of their second self-titled EP, <i><a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2011_EP)" title="2NE1 (2011 EP)">2NE1</a></i>, forward from the fall to "May or June".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-situation_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-situation-37">[37]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Park_Bom" title="Park Bom">Park Bom</a>'s second solo single, "Don't Cry", was unveiled on April 21. After Park Bom concluded her solo promotions by singing an acoustic version of "Don't Cry" on <i>YG on Air</i>, the group officially confirmed that a new single titled "<a href="/wiki/Lonely_(2NE1_song)" title="Lonely (2NE1 song)">Lonely</a>" would be released on May 12. A behind-the-scenes video of "Lonely" was shown on the second episode of <i>YG on Air</i>, with the full music video being released on May 11 via 2NE1's official YouTube account. The song enjoyed similar success to "Don't Cry", ranking number one on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaon_Singles_Chart" title="Gaon Singles Chart">Gaon Singles Chart</a>. The music video also garnered over 4 million views over the course of four days. On June 24, 2NE1 released a single produced by <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Park" title="Teddy Park">Teddy Park</a> at midnight, titled "<a href="/wiki/I_Am_the_Best" title="I Am the Best">I Am the Best</a>". Initially debuting at the number four on the Gaon Chart, the song rose three positions and peaked at number one after a week; and eventually went on to become one the 4th most downloaded single of the year in South Korea. "I Am The Best" was awarded the "Song of the Year" award at the <a href="/wiki/Mnet_Asian_Music_Awards" title="Mnet Asian Music Awards">Mnet Asian Music Awards</a>. On July 21, 2NE1 released an animated music video directed by <a href="/wiki/Mari_Kim" title="Mari Kim">Mari Kim</a> for their next single, "<a href="/wiki/Hate_You_(2NE1_song)" title="Hate You (2NE1 song)">Hate You</a>", which proved to be another success for the group, peaking at number three on the Gaon Chart. On July 28, along with the release of the full mini-album, the final promotional single "Ugly" and its music video were released. The album itself was also a major success, topping the Gaon Chart and selling over 85,000 copies by year's end and over 100,000 copies by September 2012. Promotions for 2NE1's second Korean mini-album officially concluded in late August. Following the end of promotions, 2NE1 held their first sold-out solo concert at the Olympic Hall in the Chamsil Olympic Park on August 26, 27 and 28 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seoul,_South_Korea" title="Seoul, South Korea">Seoul, South Korea</a>.</p>

<p>After concluding Korean promotions for their second extended play, 2NE1 made their official debut in Japan in September 2011. They kicked off their official debut with the release of a Japanese version of their second Korean extended play <i>2NE1</i> (2011), titled <i><a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2011_EP)" title="2NE1 (2011 EP)">Nolza</a></i>. The album was promoted by the release of various singles throughout the summer of 2011 – including Japanese versions of "I Am the Best", "Hate You", and "Lonely". <i>Nolza</i> debuted at #4 on the Oricon charts. They began their Japanese debut tour <i>2NE1 First Japan Tour – NOLZA</i> on September 19 and 20 at the Yokohama Arena, attracting a combined total of 10,000 fans. Altogether, 2NE1 held six concerts for their first tour at three locations, including Yokohama Arena (9/19 – 9/20), Kobe World Commemoration Hall (9/24 – 9/25), and Chiba’s Makuhari Messe Hall. The concerts altogether drew an impressive figure of 70,000 fans and marked a strong debut in Japan. 2NE1 released their first official Japanese single, a Japanese version of "<a href="/wiki/Go_Away_(2NE1_song)" title="Go Away (2NE1 song)">Go Away</a>", on November 18 in three versions, following their September Japanese mini-album promotions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> The music video for the Japanese version of "Go Away" showcased the JS Collage Wings x 2NE1 sneakers designed by American designer <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Scott" title="Jeremy Scott">Jeremy Scott</a>, who has been a contributing factor in their style and global attention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October, MTV Iggy held a global competition, in which 10 bands from all around the world competed in a voting competition for the title of "Best New Band".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> With their song "I Am the Best",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> 2NE1 was crowned winners of the title on November 10, 2011, making this their first US award. 2NE1 performed at <a href="/wiki/Times_Square" title="Times Square">Times Square</a> in December to accept the honor. At the end of 2011, 2NE1 was also nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Record_Award" title="Japan Record Award">Japan Record Award</a> for "Best New Artist", but lost to the Japanese girl group <a href="/wiki/Fairies_(band)" title="Fairies (band)">Fairies</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tokyograph20111120_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-tokyograph20111120-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tokyograph20111230_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-tokyograph20111230-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 28, 2012, 2NE1 released their first full-length Japanese album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Collection_(2NE1_album)" title="Collection (2NE1 album)">Collection</a></i> along with their second Japanese single "Scream". The album includes Japanese remakes of their Korean songs such as "Love Is Ouch", "<a href="/wiki/Fire_(2NE1_song)" title="Fire (2NE1 song)">Fire</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Care_(2NE1_song)" title="I Don't Care (2NE1 song)">I Don't Care</a>", and a cover of <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>'s 1984 hit song "<a href="/wiki/Like_a_Virgin_(song)" title="Like a Virgin (song)">Like A Virgin</a>". Multiple editions of the album were released to include a DVD containing the groups Japanese and Korean music videos, according to <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a>'s music website. The album peaked at #4 on the Oricon Daily Chart and went on to sell over 40,000 copies in Japan.</p>
<p>The group and their fellow label-mates <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang_(South_Korean_band)" title="Big Bang (South Korean band)">Big Bang</a> were invited to perform for the first time at 2012's Springroove festival in Japan alongside some of the top American and Japanese hip hop artists. The previous year, 2NE1 and GD&amp;TOP were invited, but due to the Japan tsunami and earthquake tragedy, the festival was cancelled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> 2NE1 also collaborated with Japanese hip-hop group <a href="/wiki/M-Flo" title="M-Flo">M-Flo</a> for the song "She's So (Outta Control)", which was featured on the artist's sixth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Square_One_(album)" title="Square One (album)">Square One</a></i>. It was released as the leading track from the album, two weeks before the album's release on February 29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> "She's So (Outta Control)" reached number 43 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Hot_100" title="Japan Hot 100">Japan Hot 100</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> 2NE1 subsequently concluded their Japanese promotions by performing at the <a href="/wiki/2012_MTV_Video_Music_Awards_Japan" title="2012 MTV Video Music Awards Japan">2012 MTV Video Music Awards Japan</a>, which took place on June 23 at the <a href="/wiki/Makuhari_Messe" title="Makuhari Messe">Makuhari Messe</a>. They also won the "Best New Artist Video" award for their song "I Am the Best".</p>
<p>On June 6, the group released a promotional single for the Intel Make Thumb Noise Project, titled "Be Mine".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying_reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source. Allkpop is not a reliable source (December 2014)">unreliable source</span></a></i>]</sup> The group served as the public speakers for the 59th <a href="/wiki/Cannes_Lions_International_Festival_of_Creativity" title="Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity">Cannes Lions International Advertising Festival</a> in France in the same month, along with the nation’s top advertising agency <a href="/wiki/Cheil_Worldwide" title="Cheil Worldwide">Cheil Worldwide</a>, after having been picked as the favorite K-pop group by French Hallyu fans. The seminar they gave was titled, “Korean Wave Through Digital Wave.”</p>
<p>The group made their official Korean comeback on July 5 with "<a href="/wiki/I_Love_You_(2NE1_song)" title="I Love You (2NE1 song)">I Love You</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-news.naver.com_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-news.naver.com-51">[51]</a></sup> The song received positive reviews from critics. Christopher Weingarten of <a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a> stated that the song was a "perfect summation of pop's present and future", and compared the production similar to that of David Guetta, especially the use of synths and "ghostly" background vocals, while also comparing the rap-style vocals to that of American singer <a href="/wiki/Kesha" title="Kesha">Kesha</a>.</p>
<p>2NE1 also announced their first world tour, the <i>New Evolution World Tour</i> would be held, and would start off in Seoul, South Korea on July 28. Jeremy Scott designed the costumes for the concert, with <a href="/wiki/Divinity_Roxx" title="Divinity Roxx">Divinity Roxx</a> assisting in music for the concert. In August, 2NE1 embarked on the US leg of their New Evolution tour. On August 24, 2NE1 held an independent concert at the Nokia Theatre in Los Angeles. 6,680 of the 6,714 tickets put on sale were sold, bringing in a gross sales of $653,716, and ranking at #29 on Billboard’s Current Box Score. This made 2NE1 the first girl group to rank on Billboard‘s Current Box Score.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Identifying_reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Identifying reliable sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source. Allkpop is not a reliable source (December 2014)">unreliable source</span></a></i>]</sup> The group also appeared alongside <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a> and other artists including <a href="/wiki/Big_Sean" title="Big Sean">Big Sean</a> and athlete <a href="/wiki/Derrick_Rose" title="Derrick Rose">Derrick Rose</a> to promote their own line of <a href="/wiki/Adidas" title="Adidas">Adidas</a> (a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Scott" title="Jeremy Scott">Jeremy Scott</a>), marking their first entrance into American pop culture. The full video features them dancing in the heart of Tokyo having an impromptu concert for surprise fans.</p>
<p>In December, 2NE1 and Big Bang made MTV Style’s list of "Best Band Style of 2012". The two YG Entertainment groups are the only Asian artists to make the list of 10, which includes <a href="/wiki/One_Direction" title="One Direction">One Direction</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Wanted" title="The Wanted">The Wanted</a>, <a href="/wiki/Backstreet_Boys" title="Backstreet Boys">Backstreet Boys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spice_Girls" title="Spice Girls">Spice Girls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child" title="Destiny's Child">Destiny's Child</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fun_(band)" title="Fun (band)">Fun</a> and <a href="/wiki/No_Doubt" title="No Doubt">No Doubt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.9314:_Single_releases.2C_CRUSH_and_All_or_Nothing_World_Tour">2013–14: Single releases, <i>CRUSH</i> and <i>All or Nothing</i> World Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2NE1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013–14: Single releases, CRUSH and All or Nothing World Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In December 2012, YG Entertainment CEO <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yang_Hyun_Suk" title="Yang Hyun Suk">Yang Hyun Suk</a> announced album production and promotion plans for 2NE1 during the first half of 2013. Initially scheduled to be released in July 2012, the album was delayed until October 2012, and delayed again for a scheduled release date of early 2013 due to potential stresses on the group as a result of its <i>New Evolution Global Tour</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> On February 18, 2013, CL revealed in a <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> interview that the group's comeback was set for April, with a lead single and concept yet to be chosen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-benjamin20130218_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-benjamin20130218-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 14, 2NE1 released a full version of an English single "Take the World On", which was a collaboration with American singer <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a>. The song had previously been used in an Intel <a href="/wiki/Ultrabook" title="Ultrabook">Ultrabook</a> commercial<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> On March 21, in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Elle_(magazine)" title="Elle (magazine)">Elle</a></i>, CL confirmed that the group has not yet planned an album for the United States, but wanted to produce more songs in English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> On April 16, the group collaborated with will.i.am on a second song called "Gettin' Dumb"; this time including his bandmate <a href="/wiki/Apl.de.ap" title="Apl.de.ap">apl.de.ap</a>. The song was featured on will.i.am's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Willpower_(will.i.am_album)" title="Willpower (will.i.am album)">#willpower</a></i>, which was released on April 23.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early May, the website "2NE1Loves.com" was launched along with mysterious videos of the members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> The project was later revealed to be <i>2NE1 Loves Shinsegae</i>, an endorsement for a department store and brand Chrome Hearts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following an announcement on June 17 where Yang Hyun Suk planned to release four new tracks from the group preceding their album release, 2NE1 released the single "<a href="/wiki/Falling_in_Love_(2NE1_song)" title="Falling in Love (2NE1 song)">Falling in Love</a>" on July 8. The song was well-received on many of South Korea's music portal sites, becoming the group's ninth number one single on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaon_Singles_Chart" title="Gaon Singles Chart">Gaon Singles Chart</a>. The next single, "<a href="/wiki/Do_You_Love_Me_(2NE1_song)" title="Do You Love Me (2NE1 song)">Do You Love Me</a>" was announced on July 28, and released online on August 7. The group's third single, "Missing You", was released on November 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> The song proved to be highly successful, topping the Gaon Singles Chart in its first week and holding the #2 spot in its second week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> It serves as 2NE1's 10th number-one single to date as a group.</p>
<p>Prior to their comeback, 2NE1 appeared on an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bachelor_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Bachelor (U.S. TV series)">The Bachelor</a></i> in January 2014 and appeared on the finale for <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Next_Top_Model_(cycle_21)" title="America's Next Top Model (cycle 21)">America's Next Top Model</a></i> where the latter show was filmed in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> In February 2014, YG-Life announced the group's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Crush_(2NE1_album)" title="Crush (2NE1 album)">Crush</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> with its online release date adjusted to February 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> The album's lead singles "<a href="/wiki/Come_Back_Home_(2NE1_song)" title="Come Back Home (2NE1 song)">Come Back Home</a>" and "Happy" were released on March 2. "Come Back Home" peaked at number one on the Gaon Singles Chart and maintained its spot for two weeks, thus making it the number one charting song for the whole month in March. Their third single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Gotta_Be_You_(2NE1_song)" title="Gotta Be You (2NE1 song)">Gotta Be You</a>" was subsequently released on May 20 along with a music video. <i>Crush</i> sold 5,000 copies in 4 days in America, making it chart No.61 on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> and sets a new U.S record for "Highest Charting and Best Selling Kpop Album", beating other big groups like <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Generation" title="Girls' Generation">Girls' Generation</a> and Big Bang.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> On June 26, they released the Japanese version of <i>Crush</i> which charted at #2 on the Oricon Daily Album Chart. The group had their fourth concert tour during the year, <i>AON: All or Nothing</i>. It included stops in China, Singapore, Taiwan, Thailand, Philippines, Japan, Malaysia and Indonesia. From March to October, they performed a total of twenty concerts across 16 cities. The tour received a total of 180,000 attendees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup></p>
<p>2NE1's previous single, "I Am The Best", was released on December 11 in the U.S. under <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a>. On December 18, their song "Gotta Be You" was crowned "Song of the Year" by MTV Iggy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> The group continued to achieve success at the end of 2014 in both South Korea and America. <i><a href="/wiki/Crush_(2NE1_album)" title="Crush (2NE1 album)">Crush</a></i> was the only record by an Asian artist to make it to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fuse_TV" title="Fuse TV">Fuse TV</a>'s "40 Best Albums of 2014", and named the "#1 Best Kpop Album of 2014" according to <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>, ranking #9 for Billboard's World Artist and #11 for World Album. Tracks in <i><a href="/wiki/Crush_(2NE1_album)" title="Crush (2NE1 album)">Crush</a></i> have been downloaded over 5.2 million times in South Korea, making 2NE1 the most downloaded girl group of the year. 2NE1 also won "Best Female Group" and "Top 10 Artist" awards at <a href="/wiki/SBS_Gayo_Daejeon" title="SBS Gayo Daejeon">SBS Gayo Daejeon</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015:_Group_hiatus_and_individual_activities">2015: Group hiatus and individual activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2NE1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2015: Group hiatus and individual activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>2015 saw the members explore ventures outside of the group. Dara, from the beginning of 2015, re-established her acting career throughout the the web dramas <i>Dr.Ian</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/We_Broke_Up" title="We Broke Up">We Broke Up</a></i>, as well as KBS's <i>Missing You</i>. Being the dancer of the group, Minzy opened a dance school by the name of 'Millennium Dance Academy' during the promotional quiet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> CL further embarked on her solo career aspirations in America and released the single "<a href="/wiki/Hello_Bitches" title="Hello Bitches">HELLO BI+CHES</a>" from her upcoming EP <i>Lifted</i>.</p>
<p>The group's only activity in 2015 was a surprise performance at the 2015 Mnet Asian Music Awards in Hong Kong. After CL's performance of her solo singles "The Baddest Female" and the newly released "HELLO BI+CHES", all members reunited to perform their hit songs "Fire" and "I Am the Best".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> The performance is now the most-viewed MAMA performance by a girl-group. It was applauded and as a result, was listed as one of the best performances of 2015 by <a href="/wiki/Fuse_(magazine)" title="Fuse (magazine)">Fuse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite not having made a comeback, 2NE1 nonetheless was the 16th most reblogged K-pop group on <a href="/wiki/Tumblr" title="Tumblr">Tumblr</a> for 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> Additionally, both 2NE1 and CL were given a nod by <a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a> in their 'Year in Music' Twitter Campaign for each achieving over one million listeners, and being played a 'staggering' 165 years worth when combined. They also won <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>'s '2016 Korea First Brand Grand Prize' award for "Top 10 Most Anticipated K-Stars of 2016".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>

<p>On April 5, 2016, YG Entertainment confirmed that Minzy was officially leaving the group. 2NE1's contract expired on May 5, and Minzy was the only member who did not re-sign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-minzyleave_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-minzyleave-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p>
<p>On November 25, 2016, YG Entertainment announced the disbandment of the group. YG also revealed that former members CL &amp; Sandara signed a solo contract, hinting at the departure of Park Bom from the company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 5, 2017, it was announced that 2NE1 would be releasing a formal final single, titled "Goodbye", as the members wished to have a chance to proper say farewell to their fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-finalsingle_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-finalsingle-5">[5]</a></sup> It was released on January 21, with lyrics being credited to CL, who had originally written the song as letter for Minzy. It was also revealed that the song had originally been intended for CL's upcoming U.S. solo album debut.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <p>In 2NE1's debut EP <i><a href="/wiki/2NE1_(2009_EP)" title="2NE1 (2009 EP)">2NE1</a></i> (2009), the group was noted to have a distinct, mixed style. While "<a href="/wiki/Lollipop_(Big_Bang_and_2NE1_song)" title="Lollipop (Big Bang and 2NE1 song)">Lollipop</a>" was categorized as for <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a>, debut single "<a href="/wiki/Fire_(2NE1_song)" title="Fire (2NE1 song)">Fire</a>" is predominately influenced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hip_hop_(music)" title="Hip hop (music)">hip hop</a>. "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Care_(2NE1_song)" title="I Don't Care (2NE1 song)">I Don't Care</a>," however, was intended as a song with a <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> feel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2NE1_EP_genre_81-0"><a href="#cite_note-2NE1_EP_genre-81">[81]</a></sup></p>
<p>Their comeback album, <i><a href="/wiki/To_Anyone" title="To Anyone">To Anyone</a></i>, was marked by a strong shift into electronic music. "<a href="/wiki/Go_Away_(2NE1_song)" title="Go Away (2NE1 song)">Go Away</a>", the most successful promotional single from the album, is classified as <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Go_Away_genre_82-0"><a href="#cite_note-Go_Away_genre-82">[82]</a></sup> However, songs like "<a href="/wiki/It_Hurts_(Slow)" title="It Hurts (Slow)">It Hurts (Slow)</a>" are <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>, another style that also characterized their first EP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-It_Hurts_genre_83-0"><a href="#cite_note-It_Hurts_genre-83">[83]</a></sup> The three title tracks, "<a href="/wiki/Clap_Your_Hands_(2NE1_song)" title="Clap Your Hands (2NE1 song)">Clap Your Hands</a>," "Go Away," and "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Nobody_(2NE1_song)" title="Can't Nobody (2NE1 song)">Can't Nobody</a>" were noted for their lasting impressions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2NE1_25_Days_After_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-2NE1_25_Days_After-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Image">Image</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2NE1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Image">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>2NE1 is known for a "unique and edgier style".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KBS_Image_85-0"><a href="#cite_note-KBS_Image-85">[85]</a></sup> During their debut single, the group was noted for being "hip-hop" and the female counterpart to <a href="/wiki/Big_Bang_(South_Korean_band)" title="Big Bang (South Korean band)">Big Bang</a> but changed this perception with "I Don't Care" into strong, feminine women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-release-22">[22]</a></sup> During the "It Hurts (Slow)" music video, the members were noted for their Gothic, black attire<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-It_Hurts_style_86-0"><a href="#cite_note-It_Hurts_style-86">[86]</a></sup> and strange hair and wigs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-It_Hurts_style_2_87-0"><a href="#cite_note-It_Hurts_style_2-87">[87]</a></sup> Zack Greenburg from the American business magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> described 2NE1 as a pop group characterized by bold, bright outfits and big, catchy hooks punctuated by rap verses and an "in-your-face swagger". He also considers the group to be "bigger than <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psy_(rapper)" title="Psy (rapper)">Psy</a>", singer of the worldwide <a href="/wiki/Hit_single" title="Hit single">hit single</a> <a href="/wiki/Gangnam_Style" title="Gangnam Style">Gangnam Style</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes20121016_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-forbes20121016-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">Influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2NE1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>2NE1 recorded R&amp;B songs with styles that encompasses urban and <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> They have stated that <a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child" title="Destiny's Child">Destiny's Child</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Spice_Girls" title="Spice Girls">Spice Girls</a> and <a href="/wiki/TLC_(group)" title="TLC (group)">TLC</a> are girl group influences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Park_Bom" title="Park Bom">Park Bom</a> cited American singers, <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> as her main influences because of their strong vocals and powerful performances. She has listed hip hop and R&amp;B as her favorite music genres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Park_Bom_interview_90-0"><a href="#cite_note-Park_Bom_interview-90">[90]</a></sup> Youngest member Minzy cited famous entertainer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CL_and_Minzy_influences_91-0"><a href="#cite_note-CL_and_Minzy_influences-91">[91]</a></sup> while leader <a href="/wiki/CL_(singer)" title="CL (singer)">CL</a> said producer <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Park" title="Teddy Park">Teddy Park</a> and American entertainers <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lauryn_Hill" title="Lauryn Hill">Lauryn Hill</a> are her role models.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CL_interview_92-0"><a href="#cite_note-CL_interview-92">[92]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sandara_Park" title="Sandara Park">Dara</a> states that singer-actress <a href="/wiki/Uhm_Jung-hwa" title="Uhm Jung-hwa">Uhm Jung-hwa</a> is her inspiration, and idolizes <a href="/wiki/Regine_Velasquez" title="Regine Velasquez">Regine Velasquez</a>, a well-known Filipino singer and also known as being Asia's songbird. She also enjoys <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>'s style and music, citing her as a role model, whose songs she often sang to while growing up, during American interviews stateside for their New Evolution World Tour stop in California.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CL_and_Minzy_influences_91-1"><a href="#cite_note-CL_and_Minzy_influences-91">[91]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_recognitions">Awards and recognitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2NE1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Awards and recognitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_received_by_2NE1" title="List of awards and nominations received by 2NE1">List of awards and nominations received by 2NE1</a></div>
<p>When the group debuted, their song "<a href="/wiki/Fire_(2NE1_song)" title="Fire (2NE1 song)">Fire</a>" and the subsequent live performances were dubbed unconventional and impressive. They won many awards on the weekly music shows, such as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Music_Trend" title="The Music Trend">The Music Trend</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_show)" title="Music Bank (TV show)">Music Bank</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Commercial_deals_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Commercial_deals-21">[21]</a></sup> "Fire" also brought them the Song of the Month and Rookie of the Month awards for May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2009_Cyworld_SOTM_93-0"><a href="#cite_note-2009_Cyworld_SOTM-93">[93]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Care_(2NE1_song)" title="I Don't Care (2NE1 song)">I Don't Care</a>" brought them similar success and recognition and led 2NE1 to win the Song of the Year award at the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Mnet_Asian_Music_Awards" title="Mnet Asian Music Awards">Mnet Asian Music Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2009_MAMA_94-0"><a href="#cite_note-2009_MAMA-94">[94]</a></sup> The same year, the group earned Cyworld Digital Music Awards for Top Selling Artist, Best Rookie Group, Bonsang Award, and Best Song for "I Don't Care".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2009_Cyworld_DMA_95-0"><a href="#cite_note-2009_Cyworld_DMA-95">[95]</a></sup> The group was also nominated at the 2009 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Disk_Awards" title="Golden Disk Awards">Golden Disk Awards</a> for the Popularity Award, Rookie Award, and the Digital Bonsang.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> At the Melon Music Awards, the group was one and the only rookie of the Top 10 artists in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and the Best New Artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Melon_2009_96-0"><a href="#cite_note-Melon_2009-96">[96]</a></sup> They won This Year’s Stage, This Year's Album, This Year's Song on 2009 GQ Awards. They received 4 out of 10 nominations on Rhythmer Awards 2009 including Rookie of the Year, R&amp;B Artist of the Year, R&amp;B Single of the Year for "I Don't Care" and R&amp;B Album of the Year for their 1st Mini Album. Rhythmer.net is famous for hip-hop and R&amp;B promotion for both mainstream and underground Korean music.</p>
<p>2NE1 won their first Artist of the Year and Album of the Year award for <i><a href="/wiki/To_Anyone" title="To Anyone">To Anyone</a></i> at the 2010 Mnet Asian Music Awards, in addition to awards for Best Female Group and Best music video for "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Nobody_(2NE1_song)" title="Can't Nobody (2NE1 song)">Can't Nobody</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010_MAMA_97-0"><a href="#cite_note-2010_MAMA-97">[97]</a></sup> They are the first artist who has won all 3 grand awards at Mnet Asian Music Awards.</p>
<p>On November 29, 2011, 2NE1 won the Best Vocal Performance by a Group for their song "Lonely" and was awarded their 2nd Song of the Year for "I Am the Best" at the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Mnet_Asian_Music_Awards" title="Mnet Asian Music Awards">Mnet Asian Music Awards</a>. After 2011, 2NE1 has won the most grand awards, for a female artist, in MAMA history with 4 wins (2 Song of the Years, 1 Album of the Year and 1 Artist of the Year).</p>
