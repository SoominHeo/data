<p><b>Munxar</b> (<a href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese</a>: <span lang="mt" xml:lang="mt"><i>Il-Munxar</i></span>) is a village which lies on the southern side of <a href="/wiki/Gozo" title="Gozo">Gozo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, close to the village of <a href="/wiki/Sannat" title="Sannat">Sannat</a>. It has its own local council. As of March 2014, the Munxar population was 1,454.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The name Munxar is from the <a href="/wiki/Maltese_language" title="Maltese language">Maltese language</a>; the English translation is "bucksaw" –the teeth of the saw are represented in the village flag.</p>
<p>Close by to this village, there is <a href="/wiki/Xlendi" title="Xlendi">Xlendi</a> Bay, a popular tourist resort engulfed in a fjord like inlet amongst high cliffs. Xlendi bay is a small fishing village, but is also an ideal bathing and diving resort especially in summer. Otherwise, foreigners and locals enjoy the late spring and early autumn sunsets often captured by artists or keen photographers.</p>
<p>The parish church, a small baroque building built from the typical Maltese stone, is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Paul_the_Apostle" title="Paul the Apostle">St. Paul</a>. It was built between 1914 and 1925 and was consecrated on 18 October 1925. Bishop <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Pace" title="Giuseppe Pace">Giuseppe Pace</a> established it as a parish church on 12 December 1957. As a result, Munxar was the last village in Gozo to become an autonomous parish. The feast officially falls on 10 February which is also a national public holiday; however, the outside festivities are celebrated on the third Sunday of each May.</p>
<p>One of the people of note to have been born and lived in this village is Professor <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Aquilina" title="Joseph Aquilina">Joseph Aquilina</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munxar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first records that were found recording Munxar as an area dates back to the sixteenth century when the area was named Monxiar, which means a bucksaw, taking its name from the two valleys running parallel from the village. From a sixteenth-century census, the village had a population of around 200 inhabitants. The first surnames recorded in the census were Attard, Barberi, Hagius, among a few others which suggests that these were the first Munxarin. In that time, Monxiar and Xlendi were part of the village of Sannat. At the time, the population of Xlendi was only 8 persons – the tower guardian and his family who lived in Xlendi tower.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Munxar_playing_field.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1360" data-file-width="1997" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Munxar_playing_field.jpg/220px-Munxar_playing_field.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Munxar_playing_field.jpg/330px-Munxar_playing_field.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Munxar_playing_field.jpg/440px-Munxar_playing_field.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Munxar_playing_field.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Munxar playing field</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Remains of a megalithic temple were found in limits of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ta%27_Mar%C5%BCiena&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ta' Marżiena (page does not exist)">Ta' Marżiena</a> and also <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> remains were found in Xlendi valley. Geographically, Munxar has always formed part of the neighbouring village of Sannat. People of Munxar attended their spiritual needs at Saint Maragret parish at Sannat; however, historical facts and documented evidence points the existence of three other chapels, now completely demolished.</p>
<p>Late in the nineteenth century, the present parish priest of Sannat noticed the need for a small chapel for the spiritual need of the Munxarin, so a chapel was constructed in the site where the parish church now stands. In the following years, the chapel became too small since the village grew, and the rector of the chapel presented a petition to build a church to the bishop. The building of a new church was approved by the curia and on 2 February 1914 construction of a new church began on a site donated by two brothers and a sister known as 'Ta Katalla'. The church was finished on 19 February 1921. The church was dedicated to Saint Paul and later a statue of Saint Paul was made by Wistin Camilleri in 1933. Munxar church was made a parish on its own rights on 12 December 1957.</p>
