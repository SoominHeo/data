<p><b>Moon Hee</b> (born July 16, 1947) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> actress active since 1965. She was born in <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>, South Korea in 1947. While attending <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seorabeol_Art_College" title="Seorabeol Art College">Seorabeol Art College</a> with a film and theater major, Moon applied for recruiting new actors by <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a> TV. When she was attending for a camera test, Moon was picked up by an assistant director of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee_Man-hee_(film_director)" title="Lee Man-hee (film director)">Lee Man-hee</a> . Moon was cast to star in Lee's film <i>Heukmaek</i>. Moon was commonly referred to as one of the "Troika" along with her rival actresses, <a href="/wiki/Yoon_Jeong-hee" title="Yoon Jeong-hee">Yoon Jeong-hee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nam_Jeong-im" title="Nam Jeong-im">Nam Jeong-im</a> of the 1960s and early 1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KMDb_Korean_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-KMDb_Korean-1">[1]</a></sup> Moon Hee retired from the acting career when she married <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jang_Gang-jae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jang Gang-jae (page does not exist)">Jang Gang-jae</a>, the vice president of <a href="/wiki/Hankook_Ilbo" title="Hankook Ilbo">Hankook Ilbo</a> in November 1971 who later became the chair of the newspaper company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
