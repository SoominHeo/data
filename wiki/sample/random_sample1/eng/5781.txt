<p><b>Wave dash</b> (〜, Unicode U+301C) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fullwidth_character" title="Fullwidth character">fullwidth character</a> represented in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language_and_computers#Character_encodings" title="Japanese language and computers">Japanese character encoding</a>, usually used to represent a range.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Vertical wave dash ( <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wave_DashV.svg"><img alt="Wave DashV.svg" data-file-height="427" data-file-width="427" height="16" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Wave_DashV.svg/16px-Wave_DashV.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Wave_DashV.svg/24px-Wave_DashV.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Wave_DashV.svg/32px-Wave_DashV.svg.png 2x" width="16"/></a> )</dt>
</dl>
<p>Wave dash is also written in <a href="/wiki/Horizontal_and_vertical_writing_in_East_Asian_scripts" title="Horizontal and vertical writing in East Asian scripts">vertical text layout</a>. Vertical wave dash is the vertical form by rotation and flip in Unicode and JIS C 6226.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lunde1999_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lunde1999-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
