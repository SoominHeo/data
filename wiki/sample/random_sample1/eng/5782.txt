<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Pisces–Cetus Supercluster Complex</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>Pisces–Cetus Supercluster Complex</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Galaxy_filament" title="Galaxy filament">galaxy filament</a>. It includes the <a href="/wiki/Virgo_Supercluster" title="Virgo Supercluster">Virgo Supercluster</a> which in turn contains the <a href="/wiki/Local_Group" title="Local Group">Local Group</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Galaxy_cluster" title="Galaxy cluster">galaxy cluster</a> that includes the <a href="/wiki/Milky_Way" title="Milky Way">Milky Way</a>. (However, a 2014 study indicates that the Virgo Supercluster is only a lobe of a greater supercluster, <a href="/wiki/Laniakea_Supercluster" title="Laniakea Supercluster">Laniakea</a>.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tully86_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tully86-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery">Discovery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pisces%E2%80%93Cetus_Supercluster_Complex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Discovery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">Astronomer</a> <a href="/wiki/R._Brent_Tully" title="R. Brent Tully">R. Brent Tully</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hawaii" title="University of Hawaii">University of Hawaii</a>’s Institute of Astronomy identified the Complex in 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extent">Extent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pisces%E2%80%93Cetus_Supercluster_Complex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Extent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Pisces–Cetus Supercluster Complex is estimated to be about 1.0 billion <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> (Gly) long and 150 million light years (Mly) wide. It is one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_cosmic_structures" title="List of largest cosmic structures">largest structures known</a> in the observable universe, but is exceeded by the <a href="/wiki/Sloan_Great_Wall" title="Sloan Great Wall">Sloan Great Wall</a> (1.3 Gly), <a href="/wiki/Clowes%E2%80%93Campusano_LQG" title="Clowes–Campusano LQG">Clowes–Campusano LQG</a> (2.0 Gly), <a href="/wiki/U1.11" title="U1.11">U1.11</a> LQG (2.5 Gly), <a href="/wiki/Huge-LQG" title="Huge-LQG">Huge-LQG</a> (4.0 Gly), and <a href="/wiki/Hercules%E2%80%93Corona_Borealis_Great_Wall" title="Hercules–Corona Borealis Great Wall">Hercules–Corona Borealis Great Wall</a> (10 Gly), respectively.</p>
<p>The complex comprises about 60 clusters and is estimated to have a total mass of 10<sup>18</sup> <a href="/wiki/Solar_mass" title="Solar mass"><i>M</i><sub>☉</sub></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tully87_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tully87-4">[4]</a></sup> According to the discoverer, the complex is composed of 5 parts:</p>
<ol>
<li>The Pisces-Cetus Supercluster</li>
<li>The Perseus-Pegasus chain, including the <a href="/wiki/Perseus-Pisces_Supercluster" title="Perseus-Pisces Supercluster">Perseus-Pisces Supercluster</a></li>
<li>The Pegasus-Pisces chain</li>
<li>The Sculptor region, including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sculptor_Supercluster" title="Sculptor Supercluster">Sculptor Supercluster</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hercules_Supercluster" title="Hercules Supercluster">Hercules Supercluster</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Laniakea_Supercluster" title="Laniakea Supercluster">Virgo–Hydra-Centaurus Supercluster</a>, which contains our <a href="/wiki/Virgo_Supercluster" title="Virgo Supercluster">Virgo Supercluster</a> (Local Supercluster) as well as the <a href="/wiki/Hydra-Centaurus_Supercluster" title="Hydra-Centaurus Supercluster">Hydra-Centaurus Supercluster</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tully87_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-tully87-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ol>
<p>With its mass of 10<sup>15</sup> <a href="/wiki/Solar_mass" title="Solar mass"><i>M</i><sub>☉</sub></a>, our <a href="/wiki/Virgo_Supercluster" title="Virgo Supercluster">Virgo Supercluster</a> accounts only for 0.1 percent of the total mass of the complex.</p>
<p>The complex was named after the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pisces-Cetus_Superclusters&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pisces-Cetus Superclusters (page does not exist)">Pisces-Cetus Superclusters</a>, which are its richest superclusters.</p>
