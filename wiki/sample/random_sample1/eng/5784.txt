<p>The <b>brown dipper</b> (<i>Cinclus pallasii</i>), alternatively known by the common names <b>Pallas's dipper</b>, <b>Asian dipper</b> or the <b>Asiatic dipper</b>, is an aquatic <a href="/wiki/Songbird" title="Songbird">songbird</a> found in the mountains of southern and central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. At 22 cm (8.7 in) and 87 grams (3.1 oz), it is the largest of the <a href="/wiki/Dipper" title="Dipper">dippers</a>. This species, which is not often seen, is found at medium to low elevations where mountain streams flow.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet_and_feeding_biology">Diet and feeding biology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brown_dipper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Diet and feeding biology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The brown dipper can either feed by diving into streams to eat larger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benthic" title="Benthic">benthic</a> organisms, or wade in shallower parts of streams and pick smaller organisms of the bottom. The adults will dive for food from December through April, which is when there are more large benthic organisms. Since this period is also the breeding season of the brown dipper, more food is required, so diving for large food is necessary. However, the adults will forage by wading and picking at the stream bottom for the rest of the year. Brown dipper chicks and fledglings will also forage by diving.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> One small population wintering at a hot spring in <a href="/wiki/Suntar-Khayata_Range" title="Suntar-Khayata Range">Suntar-Khayata Mountains</a> of <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> feeds underwater when air temperatures drop below -55 C <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sh.2F_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-sh.2F-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
