<p><i><b>Mula sa Puso</b></i> (lit. <i>From the Heart</i>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippine_drama" title="Philippine drama">Filipino</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime" title="Primetime">primetime</a> <a href="/wiki/Melodrama" title="Melodrama">melodrama</a> <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">series</a> originally aired by <a href="/wiki/ABS-CBN" title="ABS-CBN">ABS-CBN</a> from March 10, 1997 to April 9, 1999 (the series ended for 2 years in its long-run). <a href="/wiki/Claudine_Barretto" title="Claudine Barretto">Claudine Barretto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rico_Yan" title="Rico Yan">Rico Yan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Diether_Ocampo" title="Diether Ocampo">Diether Ocampo</a> played the roles of the main protagonists in the series. It was re-aired in 2008 through <a href="/wiki/Studio_23" title="Studio 23">Studio 23</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kapamilya_Channel" title="Kapamilya Channel">Kapamilya Channel</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> which are both ABS-CBN subsidiaries. A <a href="/wiki/Mula_sa_Puso_(2011_TV_series)" title="Mula sa Puso (2011 TV series)">2011 remake</a>, starring <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Young" title="Lauren Young">Lauren Young</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JM_De_Guzman" title="JM De Guzman">JM De Guzman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Gil" title="Enrique Gil">Enrique Gil</a>, aired on ABS-CBN from March 28, 2011 to August 12, 2011.</p>
<p>The show also gave critical acclaim to director <a href="/wiki/Wenn_V._Deramas" title="Wenn V. Deramas">Wenn V. Deramas</a> as his first prime time soap project and as a director and gave character actress <a href="/wiki/Princess_Punzalan" title="Princess Punzalan">Princess Punzalan</a> critical acclaim for her character and antagonist as the phenomenal Selina Perriera-Matias which was also reincarnated again for the television remake by award-winning soap actress <a href="/wiki/Eula_Valdez" title="Eula Valdez">Eula Valdez</a>. It was also known for being the most competitive soap, tied with <i><a href="/wiki/Esperanza_(TV_series)" title="Esperanza (TV series)">Esperanza</a></i>, which also ran from 1997 to 1999 and also spanned a film and a soundtrack entitled "Mula Sa Puso ni Esperanza". The TV series had various crossovers with prime time dramas such as <i>Esperanza</i> and the short-lived <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Sa_Sandaling_Kailangan_Mo_Ako" title="Sa Sandaling Kailangan Mo Ako">Sa Sandaling Kailangan Mo Ako</a></i>.</p>
<p>The show was aired from Mondays to Fridays at 6:30 pm after <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Patrol" title="TV Patrol">TV Patrol</a></i> and also become competition in a rival network <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">GMA Network</a>'s soap <i><a href="/wiki/Ikaw_Na_Sana" title="Ikaw Na Sana">Ikaw Na Sana</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mula_sa_Puso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The story depicts on the life of Via and as she gracefully turns 18, she will discover she lives a life full of deceit. She will also discover the truth about the identities of her loved ones and the unstoppable troubles when it comes to love and life itself as she falls in love with her savior Gabriel while she also falls into a love triangle with her persistent longtime childhood friend Michael.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Story">Story</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mula_sa_Puso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Story">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Via (<a href="/wiki/Claudine_Barretto" title="Claudine Barretto">Claudine Barretto</a>), the only daughter of Don Fernando (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juan_Rodrigo_(actor)" title="Juan Rodrigo (actor)">Juan Rodrigo</a>), was raised as his darling princess. On her eighteenth birthday, she found out that her father has promised her hand in marriage to her childhood friend, Michael (<a href="/wiki/Diether_Ocampo" title="Diether Ocampo">Diether Ocampo</a>), and before the birthday party was over, she got kidnapped. She was rescued by a good Samaritan named Gabriel (<a href="/wiki/Rico_Yan" title="Rico Yan">Rico Yan</a>), whom she fell in love with. Michael, at the latter part of the story, became romantically involved with Via's best friend, Trina (<a href="/wiki/Rica_Peralejo" title="Rica Peralejo">Rica Peralejo</a>). As the story unfolds, Via ended up having to decide between the two men in her life, while learning more about her mother Magda (<a href="/wiki/Jaclyn_Jose" title="Jaclyn Jose">Jaclyn Jose</a>) and fighting off her evil aunt Selina (<a href="/wiki/Princess_Punzalan" title="Princess Punzalan">Princess Punzalan</a>).</p>
<p>In the story, Selina was one of the most influential characters, due to her desire to acquire the power and wealth of Don Fernando (Juan Rodrigo), her brother. She possesses intelligence in illegal tactics that made her stronger and she used people in order to manipulate them when a bombing in the departure of Via and her family to start a new life began. Via lived a new identity but came back to her family, and they all faced Selina one last time in dignity and Via restored peace in her family.</p>

She perpetrated the acid attack which left a scar on Magda's face. She married Ysmael and had a daughter with him named Nicole. She spreads her terror on Via and her family. In the end, she was killed by a speeding truck after a duel with Via in an abandoned construction site. As she was dying a bloody death, the flashbacks of the horrible killings she committed were seen including killing the people in the bus and the death of her husband Ysmael. With her ghastly death, her terror was over. In the movie version, she was killed in a car explosion after Via crashed her car into a store beside the road. Her character was given the moniker <i>terminator</i> because she repeatedly cheated death throughout the TV series.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mula_sa_Puso&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The series title was based on the theme song which was sung by Jude Michael composed by Vehnee Saturno as the original acoustic version on television from OctoArts Records in 1997. <a href="/wiki/Roselle_Nava" title="Roselle Nava">Roselle Nava</a> sang the movie version in 1999. The song was re-released in 2010 as part of <i>60 Taon ng Musika at Soap Opera</i>. In 2011, for the remake used a version by <a href="/wiki/Jovit_Baldivino" title="Jovit Baldivino">Jovit Baldivino</a>. <a href="/wiki/Zsa_Zsa_Padilla" title="Zsa Zsa Padilla">Zsa Zsa Padilla</a>'s version from 1987 is used as well often in the series.</p>
