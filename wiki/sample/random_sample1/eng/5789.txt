<p>The <b>wolf herrings</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> (<b>Chirocentridae</b>) of two marine species of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ray-finned_fish" title="Ray-finned fish">ray-finned fish</a> related to the <a href="/wiki/Herring" title="Herring">herrings</a>.</p>
<p>Both species have elongated bodies and jaws with long sharp teeth that facilitate their ravenous appetites, mostly for other fish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EoF_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-EoF-2">[2]</a></sup> Both species reach a length of 1 m. They have silvery sides and bluish backs.</p>
<p>They are commercially fished, and marketed fresh or frozen.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Species">Species</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolf_herring&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Species">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chirocentrus_dorab" title="Chirocentrus dorab">Chirocentrus dorab</a></i> <small>(<a href="/wiki/Peter_Forssk%C3%A5l" title="Peter Forsskål">Forsskål</a>, 1775)</small> - Dorab wolf-herring, found in warm coastal waters from the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> to <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a></li>
<li><i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chirocentrus_nudus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chirocentrus nudus (page does not exist)">Chirocentrus nudus</a></i> <small><a href="/wiki/William_John_Swainson" title="William John Swainson">Swainson</a>, 1839</small> - whitefin wolf-herring, found in a similar range (This species is difficult to distinguish from <i>C. dorab</i>; the former has a black mark on its <a href="/wiki/Dorsal_fin" title="Dorsal fin">dorsal fin</a>. This species is also known to eat <a href="/wiki/Crab" title="Crab">crabs</a> in addition to its usual diet of smaller fish.)</li>
</ul>
