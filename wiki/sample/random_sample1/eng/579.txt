<p><b>SO-152</b> (СО-152) is a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> 152.4 mm <a href="/wiki/Self-propelled_artillery" title="Self-propelled artillery">self-propelled artillery</a> developed in 1968. It was a response to the American 155 mm <a href="/wiki/M109_howitzer" title="M109 howitzer">M109</a>. The development started in 1967 according to the Resolution of the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Ministers_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union">Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union</a> from July 4, 1967. In 1968 the SO-152 was completed and in 1971 entered service. Its <a href="/wiki/GRAU" title="GRAU">GRAU</a> designation is <b>2S3</b> (2С3). The fighting vehicle also received the additional designation Akatsiya (Акация), which is Russian for <a href="/wiki/Acacia" title="Acacia">acacia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2S3_Akatsiya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Designated <i>M1973</i> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Army" title="U.S. Army">U.S. Army</a>, the <i>Akatsiya</i> is armed with a 152.4 mm <a href="/wiki/Howitzer" title="Howitzer">howitzer</a> based on the Soviet 152.4 mm <a href="/wiki/152_mm_towed_gun-howitzer_M1955_(D-20)" title="152 mm towed gun-howitzer M1955 (D-20)">D-20</a> howitzer and is sometimes confused with the <a href="/wiki/M109_howitzer" title="M109 howitzer">M109</a> <a href="/wiki/Self-propelled_artillery" title="Self-propelled artillery">self-propelled artillery</a>. The artillery system was developed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Design_bureau" title="Design bureau">design bureau</a> No. 9 of <a href="/wiki/Yekaterinburg" title="Yekaterinburg">Sverdlovsk</a>. The factory designation of the howitzer is D-22 and the <a href="/wiki/GRAU" title="GRAU">GRAU</a> designation, 2A33. The chassis was developed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Uraltransmash&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Uraltransmash (page does not exist)">Uraltransmash</a>.</p>
<p>The driver's and engine-transmission compartments are located in the front part of a hull, the fighting compartment with rotatory turret in middle and rear parts of the hull. The armor is welded <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolled_steel" title="Rolled steel">rolled steel</a>. The SPG is equipped with an R-123 radio set, an R-124 intercom, an automatic <a href="/wiki/CBRN_defense" title="CBRN defense">CBRN defense</a> system with filtration unit and fire-fighting equipment. The V-59 12-cylinder four-stroke water-cooled diesel engine connects to a mechanical two-gear transmission. The gear box is combined into one block with a <a href="/wiki/Epicyclic_gearing" title="Epicyclic gearing">planetary steering gear</a>. The 2S3 has self-entrenching equipment which allows it to prepare a trench within 20–40 min.</p>
<p>The crew consists of 4–6 men: a driver, a gunner, a loader, a commander, and two ammunition bearers, which are positioned to the rear of the vehicle feeding rounds through two hatches in the hull rear when in masked firing position.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Armament">Armament</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2S3_Akatsiya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Armament">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:152mm_howitzer_D-22_(2A33)_for_2S3_Akatsiya.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="533" data-file-width="800" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/152mm_howitzer_D-22_%282A33%29_for_2S3_Akatsiya.jpg/220px-152mm_howitzer_D-22_%282A33%29_for_2S3_Akatsiya.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/152mm_howitzer_D-22_%282A33%29_for_2S3_Akatsiya.jpg/330px-152mm_howitzer_D-22_%282A33%29_for_2S3_Akatsiya.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/152mm_howitzer_D-22_%282A33%29_for_2S3_Akatsiya.jpg/440px-152mm_howitzer_D-22_%282A33%29_for_2S3_Akatsiya.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:152mm_howitzer_D-22_(2A33)_for_2S3_Akatsiya.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
152.4 mm D-22 howitzer, <a href="/wiki/Motovilikha_Plants" title="Motovilikha Plants">Motovilikha Plants</a> museum, <a href="/wiki/Perm" title="Perm">Perm</a>, Russia</div>

<p>The 152.4 mm L/27 howitzer D-22 (2A33) can be elevated from −4° to +60° with a turret traverse of a full 360°. Rate of fire is 2.6 – 3.5 rounds/min depending on firing conditions. The howitzer is equipped with a double-baffle muzzle brake, a semi-automatic vertical wedge breechblock and an ejector. The 2S3 has a periscopic sight for laying the howitzer, and telescopic sight for engaging targets with direct fire. The howitzer has separate type of loading with ammunition (35 rounds, later increased to up to 46) arranged in two mechanized stowages (in the turret and in the rear of the hull). The <i>Akatsiya</i> can fire OF-540 and OF-25 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HE-Frag" title="HE-Frag">HE-Frag</a> 43.56 kg projectiles (also all types of rounds developed for 152.4 mm towed howitzer-guns ML-20 and D-20, and for towed howitzer D-1) at a maximum range of 18.5 km depending of used charge or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rocket_Assisted_Projectile" title="Rocket Assisted Projectile">rocket assisted projectiles</a> to a maximum of 24 km. Other projectiles available to the <i>Akatsiya</i> include BP-540 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HEAT-FS" title="HEAT-FS">HEAT-FS</a> (sight distance is 3 km, 250 mm armor penetration), Br-540B and Br-540 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armor-piercing_shot_and_shell" title="Armor-piercing shot and shell">AP-T</a> (115–120 mm armor penetration at a 1000 m), OF-38 <i>Krasnopol</i> laser-guided rocket-assisted projectiles, S1 illuminating, ZH3 smoke, nuclear projectiles (with explosive capacity of 2 kt). Secondary armament consists of a remotely controlled 7.62 mm <a href="/wiki/PK_machine_gun" title="PK machine gun">PKT</a> tank machine gun on commander's cupola for anti-aircraft defence and self-defence at close range.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maneuverability">Maneuverability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2S3_Akatsiya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Maneuverability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Akatsiya</i> chassis is based on that of the <a href="/wiki/GM_chassis#Other_types_of_chassis" title="GM chassis">Object 123 tracked chassis</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jedsite_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-jedsite-4">[4]</a></sup> of the <a href="/wiki/2K11_Krug" title="2K11 Krug">2K11 <i>Krug</i></a> surface-to-air missile system; it includes six (rather than seven as in the 2K11 <i>Krug</i>) twin rubber-tired road wheels, four rubber-tired return rollers (two single and two twin), front drive sprocket with detachable sprocket rings (lantern-wheel gear) and idler wheel per side. The track is 14.375 m long, 482 mm wide and has 115 links. The <i>Akatsiya</i> can cross 0.7 m high vertical obstacles, 3.0 m wide trenches, 1.05 m fording depth and climb 30° gradients.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Flegion.wplus.net.2Fguide.2Farmy.2Far.2F2s3.shtml_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Flegion.wplus.net.2Fguide.2Farmy.2Far.2F2s3.shtml-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Farmoured.vif2.ru.2F2s3.htm_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Farmoured.vif2.ru.2F2s3.htm-5">[5]</a></sup> It can be transported by transport plane <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/An-22" title="An-22">An-22</a> (carries two self-propelled howitzers of this type).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_production">Series production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2S3_Akatsiya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Series production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two <b>Object 303</b> prototypes were built in the end of 1968. Factory tests finished in October 1969, and discovered strong gas contamination of the fighting compartment during intensive gunnery, especially during the use of small charges. As a result, army inspectors did not accept four SO-152s built in summer 1969 for ground tests. The problem of gas contamination was solved, allowing the SO-152 to enter service of the Soviet Army in 1971. The first three serial 2S3s were built by Uraltransmash in the end of 1970, and in 1971 nine were produced. The works received an order for 70 2S3 in 1973. The mass production finished in 1993.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variants">Variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2S3_Akatsiya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><b>2S3 (SO-152)</b> – Basic variant, developed in 1968. Produced in 1970–1975. Two modifications used D-11 and D-11M howitzers.</li>
<li><b>2S3M (SO-152M)</b> – Equipped with a mechanized drum-type stowage for 12 rounds, the amount of hatches in rear armored plates of the hull and the turret was reduced, the configuration of those hatches was changed, antenna of R-123 radio set was transferred on a turret top. Ammunition was increased from 40 to 46 rounds (usually consists of 42 OF-540 and OF-25 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HE-Frag" title="HE-Frag">HE-Frag</a> projectiles, and of 4 BP-540 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HEAT-FS" title="HEAT-FS">HEAT-FS</a> projectiles). Much more powerful OF-29 HE-Frag projectiles and OF-38 <i>Krasnopol</i> laser-guided rocket-assisted projectiles were developed for SO-152M. The modernized howitzer has a designator 2A33. Produced in 1975–1987.</li>
<li><b>2S3M1 (SO-152M1)</b> – Equipped with a command data acquisition and display equipment, and with a new SP-538 sight. OF-38 <i>Krasnopol</i> laser-guided rocket-assisted projectiles were added to standard ammunition. Produced in 1987–1993. All 2S3s and 2S3Ms were modernized to 2S3M1 level.</li>
<li><b>2S3M2 (SO-152M2)</b> – Modernized variant equipped with an automatic fire control system, a satellite navigational system, and smoke grenade launchers. Developed in 2000.</li>
<li><b>2S3M2-155</b> – An export-oriented variant of 2S3M2 equipped with a new 155 mm M-385 howitzer. Developed in 2000.</li>
<li><b>2S3M3</b> – An experimental variant of 2S3M2 equipped with a further improved fire control system and a modernized howitzer 2A33M that can fire ordnance of a more powerful 2A65 howitzer that equips the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2S19" title="2S19">2S19</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_and_combat_history">Service and combat history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2S3_Akatsiya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Service and combat history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_USSR">Former USSR</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2S3_Akatsiya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Former USSR">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 2S3 was intended for inventory of artillery regiments of Soviet tank and motor rifle divisions. At first only one gun battery of each artillery regiment was equipped with the <i>Akatsiya</i> (18 self-propelled howitzers).</p>
<p>By the end of 1980s however self-propelled artillery regiments of Soviet first echelon tank and motor rifle divisions (e.g. those belonging to the <a href="/wiki/Group_of_Soviet_Forces_in_Germany" title="Group of Soviet Forces in Germany">Group of Soviet Forces in Germany</a>) each had 36 or 54 self-propelled howitzers of this type (2 or 3 gun battalions). So for example the following artillery regiments all had 54 <i>Akatsiya</i> on their strength:</p>
<ul>
<li>96th self-propelled artillery regiment (stationed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Borna,_Germany" title="Borna, Germany">Borna</a>) of <a href="/wiki/9th_Tank_Division_(Soviet_Union)" title="9th Tank Division (Soviet Union)">9th Tank Division</a></li>
<li>724th Guards Warsaw self-propelled artillery regiment (stationed in <a href="/wiki/Neustrelitz" title="Neustrelitz">Neustrelitz</a>) of <a href="/wiki/16th_Guards_Tank_Division" title="16th Guards Tank Division">16th Guards Tank Division</a></li>
<li>400th Red Banner Transylvania self-propelled artillery regiment (stationed in <a href="/wiki/Bernau_bei_Berlin" title="Bernau bei Berlin">Bernau bei Berlin</a>) of <a href="/wiki/90th_Guards_Tank_Division" title="90th Guards Tank Division">90th Guards Tank Division</a></li>
<li>1054th Red Banner self-propelled artillery regiment (stationed in <a href="/wiki/Rathenow" title="Rathenow">Rathenow</a>) of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/21st_Motor_Rifle_Division" title="21st Motor Rifle Division">21st Motor Rifle Division</a> (actual strength: 53)</li>
<li>54th Guards Red Banner Poznan self-propelled regiment (stationed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halle,_Saxony-Anhalt" title="Halle, Saxony-Anhalt">Halle</a>) of <a href="/wiki/27th_Guards_Motor_Rifle_Division" title="27th Guards Motor Rifle Division">27th Guards Motor Rifle Division</a></li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Combat_history">Combat history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2S3_Akatsiya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Combat history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 2S3 became a well-known <a href="/wiki/Self-propelled_artillery" title="Self-propelled artillery">self-propelled artillery</a> after combat operations in Afghanistan where it proved to be an effective and reliable artillery system. 2S3s were used quite successfully during two Chechen wars and military conflicts on the territory of former USSR.</p>
