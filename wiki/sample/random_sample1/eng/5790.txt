<p><b>Eta<sup>2</sup> Hydri</b> (η<sup>2</sup> Hyi, η<sup>2</sup> Hydri), often catalogued as <b>HD 11977</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Yellow_giant" title="Yellow giant">giant yellow</a> <a href="/wiki/Star" title="Star">star</a> approximately 219<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-van_Leeuwen2007_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-van_Leeuwen2007-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> away in the <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hydrus" title="Hydrus">Hydrus</a>. Based on its mass, it was probably a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">class A</a> star (similar to <a href="/wiki/Vega" title="Vega">Vega</a> or <a href="/wiki/Fomalhaut" title="Fomalhaut">Fomalhaut</a>) when it was on the <a href="/wiki/Main_sequence" title="Main sequence">main sequence</a>, though it is now in the <a href="/wiki/Giant_star" title="Giant star">giant</a> stage of its evolution. It is thought to be around 1.3 billion years old and has expanded to 10 times the Sun's diameter, though is only around 1.8 times as massive as the Sun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa458_2_609_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-aaa458_2_609-2">[2]</a></sup> As of 2005, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extrasolar_planet" title="Extrasolar planet">extrasolar planet</a> was confirmed to be orbiting the star.</p>
