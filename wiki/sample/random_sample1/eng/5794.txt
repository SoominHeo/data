<p><b>Pi Aquarii</b> (π Aqr, π Aquarii) is the <a href="/wiki/Bayer_designation" title="Bayer designation">Bayer designation</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Binary_star" title="Binary star">binary star</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Celestial_equator" title="Celestial equator">equatorial</a> <a href="/wiki/Constellation" title="Constellation">constellation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aquarius_(constellation)" title="Aquarius (constellation)">Aquarius</a>. It shares the <a href="/wiki/List_of_proper_names_of_stars" title="List of proper names of stars">traditional</a> name <b>Seat</b> with <a href="/wiki/Delta_Aquarii" title="Delta Aquarii">Delta Aquarii</a>, where it has long been misspelled <i>Skat.</i> It may be from the Arabic word <i>ši'at</i> "a wish". This system has an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apparent_visual_magnitude" title="Apparent visual magnitude">apparent visual magnitude</a> of a mean <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">apparent magnitude</a> of +4.57.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aass34_1_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-aass34_1-2">[2]</a></sup> Based upon <a href="/wiki/Parallax" title="Parallax">parallax</a> measurements, it is located at a distance of roughly 780 <a href="/wiki/Light-year" title="Light-year">light-years</a> (240 <a href="/wiki/Parsec" title="Parsec">parsecs</a>) from Earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aaa474_2_653_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-aaa474_2_653-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>This is a binary star system with an <a href="/wiki/Orbital_period" title="Orbital period">orbital period</a> of 84.1 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STARS_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-STARS-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibvs6023_1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ibvs6023_1-11">[11]</a></sup> The primary component is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-type_main_sequence_star" title="B-type main sequence star">B-type main sequence star</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">stellar classification</a> of B1 Ve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apjs17_371_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-apjs17_371-3">[3]</a></sup> This is a large star with nearly eleven<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnras410_1_190_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-mnras410_1_190-5">[5]</a></sup> times the mass and over six times the radius of the Sun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnras189_601_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-mnras189_601-6">[6]</a></sup> Pi Aquarii shines about 7,300<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-an331_4_349_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-an331_4_349-7">[7]</a></sup> times as brightly as the Sun. This energy is being radiated from its <a href="/wiki/Stellar_atmosphere" title="Stellar atmosphere">outer atmosphere</a> at an <a href="/wiki/Effective_temperature" title="Effective temperature">effective temperature</a> of 27,094,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnras189_601_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-mnras189_601-6">[6]</a></sup> giving it the blue-hued glow of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-type_star" title="B-type star">B-type star</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csiro_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-csiro-12">[12]</a></sup> It is spinning rapidly with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Projected_rotational_velocity" title="Projected rotational velocity">projected rotational velocity</a> of 215 km/s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-apj573_1_359_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-apj573_1_359-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pi Aquarii is notable for having undergone a transition from a <a href="/wiki/Be_star" title="Be star">Be star</a> (showing hydrogen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emission_line" title="Emission line">emission lines</a>) into an ordinary <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-type_star" title="B-type star">B-type star</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibvs6023_1_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ibvs6023_1-11">[11]</a></sup> It is classified as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gamma_Cassiopeiae_variable" title="Gamma Cassiopeiae variable">Gamma Cassiopeiae type</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCVS_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-GCVS-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Variable_star" title="Variable star">variable star</a> and its brightness varies from magnitude +4.45 to +4.71; a range of 0.28. The dominant variability period, <span class="nowrap">83.8 ± 0.8 days</span>, is nearly the same as the orbital period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ibvs6023_1_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-ibvs6023_1-11">[11]</a></sup> Pi Aquarii has a reasonable chance of becoming a supernova some day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STARS_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-STARS-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_culture">In culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pi_Aquarii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">墳墓</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Fén Mù</span>), meaning <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rooftop_(Chinese_constellation)" title="Rooftop (Chinese constellation)">Tomb</a></i>, refers to an asterism consisting of π Aquarii, <a href="/wiki/Gamma_Aquarii" title="Gamma Aquarii">γ Aquarii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zeta_Aquarii" title="Zeta Aquarii">ζ Aquarii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eta_Aquarii" title="Eta Aquarii">η Aquarii</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Consequently, π Aquarii itself is known as <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">墳墓四</span> (<span lang="zh-Latn" xml:lang="zh-Latn">Fén Mù sì</span>, English: the Fourth Star of Tomb.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the catalogue of stars in the <i>Calendarium</i> of <a href="/wiki/Al_Achsasi_al_Mouakket" title="Al Achsasi al Mouakket">Al Achsasi al Mouakket</a>, this star was designated <b>Wasat al Achbiya</b> (<b>وسط الأخبية</b> - <i>wasath al ahbiyah</i>), which was translated into <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> as <b>Media Tabernaculorum</b>, meaning <i>the middle of luck of the homes (tents)</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnras55_429_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-mnras55_429-16">[16]</a></sup> This star, along with <a href="/wiki/Gamma_Aquarii" title="Gamma Aquarii">γ Aqr</a> (Sadachbia), <a href="/wiki/Zeta_Aquarii" title="Zeta Aquarii">ζ Aqr</a> (Sadaltager / Achr al Achbiya) and <a href="/wiki/Eta_Aquarii" title="Eta Aquarii">η Aqr</a> (Hydria), were <b>al Aḣbiyah</b> (<b>الأخبية</b>), the Tent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pa52_3_12_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-pa52_3_12-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allen1963_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-allen1963-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aqr_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-aqr-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
