<p><i><b>Paspalum distichum</b></i> is a species of <a href="/wiki/Poaceae" title="Poaceae">grass</a>. Common names include <b>knotgrass</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <b>water finger-grass</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BSBI07_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-BSBI07-2">[2]</a></sup> <b>couch paspalum</b>, <b>eternity grass</b>, <b>gingergrass</b>, and <b>Thompson grass</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Its native range is obscure because it has long been present on most continents, and in most areas it is certainly an <a href="/wiki/Introduced_species" title="Introduced species">introduced species</a>. Its native range probably includes parts of the tropical Americas.</p>
<p>This is a perennial grass forming clumps and spreading via <a href="/wiki/Rhizome" title="Rhizome">rhizomes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stolon" title="Stolon">stolons</a>. It grows decumbent or erect to a maximum height near 60 centimeters. The <a href="/wiki/Inflorescence" title="Inflorescence">inflorescence</a> is usually divided into two branches lined with spikelets.</p>
<p><i>Paspalum distichum</i> is a food source for several avian species, including the <a href="/wiki/Long-tailed_widowbird" title="Long-tailed widowbird">long-tailed widowbird</a>.</p>
