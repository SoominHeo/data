<p><i><b>2gether</b></i> is the second studio album by South Korean <a href="/wiki/Pop_rock" title="Pop rock">pop rock</a> <a href="/wiki/Korean_pop_idol" title="Korean pop idol">idol</a> band <a href="/wiki/CNBLUE" title="CNBLUE">CNBLUE</a>. It was released on September 14, 2015, under <a href="/wiki/FNC_Entertainment" title="FNC Entertainment">FNC Entertainment</a>. After ending domestic promotions for <i><a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stop_(EP)" title="Can't Stop (EP)">Can't Stop</a></i> (2014), the band concentrated on individual activities; most notably, frontman <a href="/wiki/Jung_Yong-hwa" title="Jung Yong-hwa">Jung Yong-hwa</a> released his debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/One_Fine_Day_(Jung_Yong-hwa_album)" title="One Fine Day (Jung Yong-hwa album)">One Fine Day</a></i> (2015) in January. The remaining band members sought out opportunities in television, particularly in acting, <a href="/wiki/Variety_show" title="Variety show">variety show</a> appearances, and <a href="/wiki/Master_of_ceremonies" title="Master of ceremonies">MCing</a>. In August 2015, it was announced that CNBLUE would release its second studio album after an absence lasting one year and seven months. With <i>2gether</i>, the band decided to change the direction of its music; it stripped itself of the <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_music" title="Acoustic music">acoustic tracks</a> it was known for and explored <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">electronic dance music</a> in order to create a particular color and form a distinctive sound.</p>
<p>After a series of photo and video teasers, the album and lead single "<a href="/wiki/Cinderella_(CNBLUE_song)" title="Cinderella (CNBLUE song)">Cinderella</a>" were released simultaneously. The band held a "Be My Cinderella" album showcase on the day of release and began promoting the album through music chart programs across various television networks by performing "Cinderella" and other album tracks. The single ultimately led the band to win a total of five music show trophies: two consecutive wins on both <a href="/wiki/SBS_MTV" title="SBS MTV">SBS MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Show_(South_Korean_TV_series)" title="The Show (South Korean TV series)">The Show</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">Korean Broadcasting System</a>'s (KBS) <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i>, and one on <a href="/wiki/MBC_Music" title="MBC Music">MBC Music</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Show_Champion" title="Show Champion">Show Champion</a></i>. <i>2gether</i> was met with generally favorable reviews, particularly for the band's pursuit of electronic elements. The album went on to top the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Album_Chart" title="Gaon Album Chart">Gaon Album Chart</a> in its second week of its release and sold 83,000 copies in its home country. The band embarked on the 2015 CNBLUE Live "Come Together" concert tour, which made stops in South Korea, China, and other countries in Asia.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2gether_(CNBLUE_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After ending domestic promotions for its fifth mini-album <i><a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stop_(EP)" title="Can't Stop (EP)">Can't Stop</a></i> (2014), CNBLUE pursued individual activities. Frontman <a href="/wiki/Jung_Yong-hwa" title="Jung Yong-hwa">Jung Yong-hwa</a> released his debut solo studio album <i><a href="/wiki/One_Fine_Day_(Jung_Yong-hwa_album)" title="One Fine Day (Jung Yong-hwa album)">One Fine Day</a></i> (2015) in January and embarked on an Asian tour. Guitarist <a href="/wiki/Lee_Jong-hyun" title="Lee Jong-hyun">Lee Jong-hyun</a> played the role of a lead character in <a href="/wiki/KBS2" title="KBS2">KBS2</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Orange_Marmalade_(TV_series)" title="Orange Marmalade (TV series)">Orange Marmalade</a></i> and participated on <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC's</a> reality variety program <i><a href="/wiki/We_Got_Married" title="We Got Married">We Got Married</a></i>. Drummer <a href="/wiki/Kang_Min-hyuk" title="Kang Min-hyuk">Kang Min-hyuk</a> took part in MBC's <i><a href="/wiki/I_Live_Alone_(TV_series)" title="I Live Alone (TV series)">I Live Alone</a></i> and KBS2's sports variety show <i><a href="/wiki/Our_Neighborhood_Arts_and_Physical_Education" title="Our Neighborhood Arts and Physical Education">Our Neighborhood Arts and Physical Education</a></i>. Bassist <a href="/wiki/Lee_Jung-shin" title="Lee Jung-shin">Lee Jung-shin</a> was an <a href="/wiki/Master_of_ceremonies" title="Master of ceremonies">MC</a> for <a href="/wiki/SBS_Plus" title="SBS Plus">SBS Plus</a>'s <i>Fashion King: Secret Box</i> and <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s music chart show <i><a href="/wiki/M_Countdown" title="M Countdown">M Countdown</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>During a press conference in June for the 2015 Jung Yong-hwa Live "One Fine Day" concert in <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a>, he disclosed that, upon the completion of his solo promotions, CNBLUE was set to release an album in the second half of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On August 24, 2015, it was announced that the band would release its second studio album in September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-release-3">[3]</a></sup> Four days later, it was revealed that the album was titled <i>2gether</i> and had a set release date of September 14, one year and seven months following <i>Can't Stop</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-covers_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-covers-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_development">Writing and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2gether_(CNBLUE_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Writing and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CNBLUE began developing songs for <i>2gether</i> in midst of writing songs for <i><a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Stop_(EP)" title="Can't Stop (EP)">Can't Stop</a></i>. While staying at a hotel in Japan during the band's Arena Tour 2013: One More Time, bassist Lee sought out to write a song. Jung assigned him and Kang to do it together; after listening to the melody of "Control", the two conceived the idea of writing a love song, which Jung rejected. After discussing it between themselves, Kang and Lee agreed to write a song with the notion that "no one can control me". Initially written in <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>, the two faced difficulties in translating the song into English. With the assistance of Jung, the translations and lyrics were completed; Kang and Lee were left uncredited. The song was originally intended to be included on <i>Can't Stop</i>, but was ultimately excluded. Jung cited the limited space on the release, as well as the "different atmosphere" the song had in comparison to the other songs for its omission. He decided to add it to the band's Japanese single "<a href="/wiki/Go_Your_Way_(song)" title="Go Your Way (song)">Go Your Way</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The lyrics were rewritten in Korean by the bassist for its inclusion on <i>2gether</i>, marking the first song to be credited under his name since the debut of the band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FNC_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FNC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Radio_(CNBLUE_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Radio (CNBLUE song) (page does not exist)">Radio</a>" was previously recorded in <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> for CNBLUE's album <i><a href="/wiki/Wave_(CNBLUE_album)" title="Wave (CNBLUE album)">Wave</a></i>, which was re-recorded in Korean for inclusion on <i>2gether</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SSTV_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-SSTV-8">[8]</a></sup> "Footsteps", originally titled "Heart Song" and included in its Japanese single "<a href="/wiki/Truth_(CNBLUE_song)" title="Truth (CNBLUE song)">Truth</a>", was also rewritten in Korean.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FNC_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-FNC-6">[6]</a></sup> In midst of filming <a href="/wiki/KBS2" title="KBS2">KBS2</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Orange_Marmalade_(TV_series)" title="Orange Marmalade (TV series)">Orange Marmalade</a></i>, guitarist Lee took the opportunity to write "Hero".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hero_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hero-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Often compared to American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pop-rock" title="Pop-rock">pop-rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Maroon_5" title="Maroon 5">Maroon 5</a>, CNBLUE has found the comparisons "honorable", but has expressed its desire to distinguish its musical color and establish a unique sound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10">[10]</a></sup> In CNBLUE's previous albums, Jung made a conscious effort to record music in order to receive recognition for it as an <a href="/wiki/Korean_pop_idol" title="Korean pop idol">idol band</a>. After deciding to disregard those notions, he opted to create "more comfortable" music that suited the band; as a result, the <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_music" title="Acoustic music">acoustic music</a> which was present in the band's previous albums was entirely excluded from <i>2gether</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In developing the record, CNBLUE set out to "really [come] together with our music, working with each other on the compositions". CNBLUE took the initiative to change the sound of its music for a "more mainstream feel" by integrating elements of <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">electronic dance music</a> into the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The title of the album was conceived by Jung while he was eating <a href="/wiki/Patbingsu" title="Patbingsu">patbingsu</a> with bassist Lee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-patbingsu_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-patbingsu-13">[13]</a></sup> It signifies CNBLUE's second studio album, as well as enjoyment of the band's music together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-covers_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-covers-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-patbingsu_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-patbingsu-13">[13]</a></sup> The album was recorded in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> at the <a href="/wiki/FNC_Entertainment" title="FNC Entertainment">FNC Entertainment Studio</a>. Mixing took place there, as well as at <a href="/wiki/Cube_Entertainment" title="Cube Entertainment">Cube Studio</a> and MasterPiece SoundLab; it was mastered at the SUONO Mastering Studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2gether_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-2gether-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_structure">Music structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2gether_(CNBLUE_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Musically, <i>2gether</i> explores the <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">Top 40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electro_(music)" title="Electro (music)">electro</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10">[10]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Cinderella_(CNBLUE_song)" title="Cinderella (CNBLUE song)">Cinderella</a>", the <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> of the album, is a pop-rock song that incorporates <a href="/wiki/Disco" title="Disco">disco</a> and elements of <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-release-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trailer_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-trailer-15">[15]</a></sup> The song begins with "warped vocals, zipping synths, and booming drum machines" before building up into the "explosive chorus", leading into its catchy <a href="/wiki/Hook_(music)" title="Hook (music)">hook</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10">[10]</a></sup> The song was written and composed Jung and serves as a take on the European folktale <a href="/wiki/Cinderella" title="Cinderella">of the same name</a> with "trendy sounds" and "modern sensibilities".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-release-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trailer_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-trailer-15">[15]</a></sup> "Hide and Seek" is a track that affixes a blaring <a href="/wiki/Saxophone" title="Saxophone">saxophone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10">[10]</a></sup> "Roller Coaster" is a pop-rock song with influences of <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ska" title="Ska">ska</a>, melded with a <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">synthesizer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vr_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-vr-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCD_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-SCD-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomato_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tomato-18">[18]</a></sup> In "Domino", Jung mixed CNBLUE's traditional <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> sound with <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">popular music</a> to appeal to the masses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10a_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-10a-19">[19]</a></sup> Consisting of "crunchy" synths coupled with a <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_guitar" title="Rhythm guitar">rhythm guitar</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IZM_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-IZM-20">[20]</a></sup> the 1980s <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> song features Wheein of girl group <a href="/wiki/Mamamoo" title="Mamamoo">Mamamoo</a>, the band's first ever feature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCD_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-SCD-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10a_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-10a-19">[19]</a></sup> Written and composed be guitarist Lee, "Hero" is a song reminiscent of 1990s alternative rock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10">[10]</a></sup> He created it in tribute to his father, his first attempt to write a song for him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hero_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hero-9">[9]</a></sup> "Catch Me" is a rock song which draws inspirations from 1980s <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance music</a> and is blended with a synthesizer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IZM_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-IZM-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVr_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVr-21">[21]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> song,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVr_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-TVr-21">[21]</a></sup> "Drunken Night" describes the emancipation from the burden of expectations with a night out of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alcoholic_beverage" title="Alcoholic beverage">alcohol consumption</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FNC_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-FNC-6">[6]</a></sup> "Hold My Hand" is a medium-tempo <a href="/wiki/Jazz_fusion#Jazz-rock" title="Jazz fusion">jazz pop</a> song infused with a "romantic" clarinet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SSTV_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-SSTV-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCD_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-SCD-17">[17]</a></sup> An <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronic_rock" title="Electronic rock">electronic rock</a> song, "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Radio_(CNBLUE_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Radio (CNBLUE song) (page does not exist)">Radio</a>" features a heavy guitar and a synthesizer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> "Footsteps" is a <a href="/wiki/Britpop" title="Britpop">Britpop</a>-inspired song that deals with the idea of finding footprints along one's path that others have traveled, signifying "you are not alone".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artwork_concept_and_packaging">Artwork concept and packaging</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2gether_(CNBLUE_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Artwork concept and packaging">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>2gether</i> is available in three editions. The concept of version A revolves around the theme of morning and showcases CNBLUE's "sexy change".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pt1_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-pt1-24">[24]</a></sup> It includes a 68-page photobook, one out of four random photo card between two sets, and a poster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-verA_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-verA-25">[25]</a></sup> The concept of version B presents the members in a forest, in which they exhibit "dreamlike atmospheres".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pt2_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-pt2-26">[26]</a></sup> It includes a 64-page photobook, one out of four random photo card between two sets, and a poster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-verB_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-verB-27">[27]</a></sup> The cover art for these two versions display a pattern of geometric diamonds. The album's title is split into '2' and 'gether', encased within the diamond in the center of the cover.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-covers_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-covers-4">[4]</a></sup> The artwork for the special version of <i>2gether</i> was designed by Belgian artist Sammy Slabbinck, his first time working with South Korean musicians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> It includes a 16-page photobook and a poster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-special_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-special-29">[29]</a></sup></p>

<p>On September 2, a set of teaser photos for version A of <i>2gether</i> was published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pt1_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-pt1-24">[24]</a></sup> A second set of teaser photos for version B followed the next day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pt2_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-pt2-26">[26]</a></sup> On the following day, the opening trailer for "<a href="/wiki/Cinderella_(CNBLUE_song)" title="Cinderella (CNBLUE song)">Cinderella</a>" was uploaded on CNBLUE's <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel. The four members are shown awakening, each with a single high heel in their possession.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trailer_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-trailer-15">[15]</a></sup> Between September 7–10, individual opening trailers were released daily, each preceded by the "CNBLUE Cinderella V Week" show on <a href="/wiki/Naver" title="Naver">Naver</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mobile_app" title="Mobile app">mobile app</a> V directly before their release; bassist Lee, drummer Kang, and guitarist Lee held a 10-minute broadcast, while Jung Yong-hwa held a 15-minute broadcast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> This was followed by the release of the music video teaser for "Cinderella" on the subsequent day, as well as a highlight medley for the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> One hour prior to the release of the album, CNBLUE broadcast from Japan on the V app to discuss the album and sing a portion of the lead single, as well as snippets of other selected songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>

Music Core">Show! Music Core</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> "Domino" on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">Seoul Broadcasting System</a>'s (SBS) <i><a href="/wiki/Inkigayo" title="Inkigayo">Inkigayo</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> "Footsteps" on <a href="/wiki/SBS_MTV" title="SBS MTV">SBS MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Show_(South_Korean_TV_series)" title="The Show (South Korean TV series)">The Show</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> and "Domino" on <a href="/wiki/MBC_Music" title="MBC Music">MBC Music</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Show_Champion" title="Show Champion">Show Champion</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> On the September 22 broadcast of <i>The Show</i>, CNBLUE earned its first music show win with "Cinderella",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> followed by its second win on <i>Show Champion</i> the day after.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> The song earned the band its third win on <i>Music Bank</i> that same week; the episode did not air due to the <a href="/wiki/Chuseok" title="Chuseok">Chuseok</a> holiday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> CNBLUE earned two consecutive wins on <i>The Show</i> and <i>Music Bank</i> a week later, bringing the total of music show wins up to five.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2gether_(CNBLUE_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>2gether</i> was met with generally favorably reviews from music critics. Writing for <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine, columnist Jeff Benjamin felt that the similarities between CNBLUE and <a href="/wiki/Maroon_5" title="Maroon 5">Maroon 5</a> were "more striking than ever", noting that it "may position them to be more internationally appealing than ever". In spite of the change in direction of the band's music on the album, Benjamin observed that the "sentimental songwriting" and "vivacious energy" persisted within the band.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_10-6"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-10">[10]</a></sup> In a <i>10asia</i> article where three staff individually picked their favorite song from recent releases, Park Soo-jeong named "Domino" as the highlight of the album. Commending the <a href="/wiki/Yin_and_yang" title="Yin and yang">yin and yang</a> harmonization between Jung's "dreamy vocals" and Wheein's "feminine and lucid voice", she took recognition of the "immensely vast range of Jung Yong-hwa's musical spectrum".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10a_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-10a-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rating the album three and a half stars out of five, Jung Min-jae of online magazine <i><a href="/wiki/IZM" title="IZM">IZM</a></i> sensed that CNBLUE "adheres to its wide-ranging pop rock" in <i>2gether</i>. Lauding Jung's versatile vocal styles throughout the album and the band's blending of music, he felt that the band's "signature smooth melodies and waving grooves are more attractive than ever". He complimented the "stable, tidied color of its sound", acknowledging "how much effort was put in to its solid structure." He named "Domino" and "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Radio_(CNBLUE_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Radio (CNBLUE song) (page does not exist)">Radio</a>" as the highlights of the album that showcased "a new side of the band", while also noting tracks like "Roller Coaster" and "Hero" draw from the band's previous work to strike a balance within the album. He expressed that the "dramatic" progression of CNBLUE's music was "unfathomable" from its career beginnings. In spite of lyrics lacking depth, in addition to <a href="/wiki/Musical_composition" title="Musical composition">compositions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arrangement" title="Arrangement">arrangements</a> which fell short of diversity, he stated that the "neat composition of the tracks makes you feel good", which produced a "well-made pop rock album".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IZM_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-IZM-20">[20]</a></sup> Rating the album three stars out of five, Jung Hae-wook of <i>News Tomato</i> complimented the band on the mixture of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronic_rock" title="Electronic rock">electronic rock</a> and being able to form a distinct sound between the tracks, as well as their effort to experiment in order to establish its musical identity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tomato_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tomato-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Music critic Kim Young-dae of Idology noted that the album exhibited "diverse genres and approaches, fitting well into the current scene as a result". He observed that the band avoided a <a href="/wiki/Clich%C3%A9" title="Cliché">cliché</a> chorus and opted for "trendy brass grooves and a fluent melody" in "Hide and Seek", and dubbed the experimentation in the composition and arrangement of "Domino" as "refreshing". Also writing for Idology as 'Mimyo', musician Moon Yong-min noticed that the album "sets its foot between [the] uneasy cleavages" of <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/K-pop" title="K-pop">K-pop</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance music</a> "from the texture of the approach". He sensed that the mixture of sounds came across as "nonchalant" as opposed to engaging in them for the purpose of "popular appeal or an 'edgy', hybrid element". He recalled past attempts of rock band popular music, but noted that CNBLUE worked well in avoiding the "discomfort" of trying to sell records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> In a mixed review, Cao Jiefeng of Malaysian newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Sin_Chew_Daily" title="Sin Chew Daily">Sin Chew Daily</a></i> claimed that the band lacks the ability to create a "hit <a href="/wiki/Hook_(music)" title="Hook (music)">hook</a>" and that it should strive to develop more "outstanding" melodies. He pointed out that the element of rock music almost disappeared in some tracks, saying that "Drunken Night" was essentially a <a href="/wiki/Techno" title="Techno">techno</a> <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a> song that sounded like a <a href="/wiki/2PM" title="2PM">2PM</a> or <a href="/wiki/B1A4" title="B1A4">B1A4</a> "side-cut". He spotlighted "Domino" as one of the top tracks, stating that the "cool rockers" become "the romantic <a href="/wiki/Bourgeoisie" title="Bourgeoisie">bourgeoisie</a>" in the song. He also applauded "Radio"; he compared it to a British band <a href="/wiki/Coldplay" title="Coldplay">Coldplay</a>'s and Swedish producer <a href="/wiki/Avicii" title="Avicii">Avicii</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/A_Sky_Full_of_Stars" title="A Sky Full of Stars">A Sky Full of Stars</a>", revering its "grand chorus" and "wild guitar solo". He concluded that <i>2gether</i> is a "revolutionary production" that paved the way to a "promising start" in CNBLUE distinguishing itself from Maroon 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCD_17-3"><a href="#cite_note-SCD-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2gether_(CNBLUE_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the chart dated September 13 – 20, 2015, <i>2gether</i> debuted at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Album_Chart" title="Gaon Album Chart">Gaon Album Chart</a>, outranked by <a href="/wiki/Super_Junior" title="Super Junior">Super Junior</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Devil_(Super_Junior_album)" title="Devil (Super Junior album)">Magic</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> The following week, the album rose to the top of the chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gaon_72-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gaon-72">[72]</a></sup> It fell to number three on its third week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> The record ranked at number 30 on Gaon's year-end chart for selling 82,748 copies in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>2gether</i> also debuted at number three on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine's World Albums Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-World_75-0"><a href="#cite_note-World-75">[75]</a></sup> In Japan, the album debuted at number 19 on the weekly <a href="/wiki/Oricon_Albums_Chart" title="Oricon Albums Chart">Oricon Albums Chart</a>, selling 3,716 copies in its first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> It sold an additional 3,069 on the following week, peaking at number 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-O2_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-O2-77">[77]</a></sup> By the end of its chart run, the album sold 12,672 copies in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup></p>
