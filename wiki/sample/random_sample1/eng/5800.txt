<p><b>Mumtaz Madhvani</b> (born 31 July 1947<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DOB_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-DOB-1">[1]</a></sup>) is an <a href="/wiki/Indian_people" title="Indian people">Indian</a> <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a> actress and former model.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mumtaz_(actress)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:194px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mumtaz.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="439" data-file-width="192" height="439" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/58/Mumtaz.jpg" width="192"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mumtaz.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Mumtaz in 2010</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Mumtaz appeared as a child actress in <i>Sone Ki Chidiya</i> (1958). As a teenager she acted as an extra in <i>Vallah Kya Baat Hai</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Stree_(1961_film)" title="Stree (1961 film)">Stree</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Sehra_(film)" title="Sehra (film)">Sehra</a></i> in the early 1960s. As an adult, her first role in A-grade films was that of the role of sister of the hero in <a href="/wiki/O._P._Ralhan" title="O. P. Ralhan">O. P. Ralhan</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Gehra_Daag" title="Gehra Daag">Gehra Daag</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo-2">[2]</a></sup> She got small roles in successful films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Mujhe_Jeene_Do" title="Mujhe Jeene Do">Mujhe Jeene Do</a></i>. Later, she got the role of the main lead heroine in 16 action films, including <i>Faulad</i>, <i>Veer Bhimsen</i>, <i>Tarzan Comes to Delhi</i>, <i>Sikandar-E-Azam</i>, <i>Rustom-E-Hind</i>, <i>Raaka</i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Daku_Mangal_Singh" title="Daku Mangal Singh">Daku Mangal Singh</a></i>, with freestyle wrestler <a href="/wiki/Dara_Singh" title="Dara Singh">Dara Singh</a>, and was labelled as a stunt-film heroine.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (April 2013)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> In the films that the pair Dara Singh-Mumtaz did together, Dara's remuneration was INR 450,000 per film, and Mumtaz's salary was INR 250,000 per film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>It took <a href="/wiki/Raj_Khosla" title="Raj Khosla">Raj Khosla</a>'s blockbuster <i><a href="/wiki/Do_Raaste" title="Do Raaste">Do Raaste</a></i> (1969), starring <a href="/wiki/Rajesh_Khanna" title="Rajesh Khanna">Rajesh Khanna</a>, to finally make Mumtaz a full-fledged star. Although Mumtaz had a minor role, director Khosla filmed four songs with her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oomph_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oomph-4">[4]</a></sup> The film made her popular, and she acknowledged that even though she had a small role, it was still one of her favourite films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1969, her films <i>Do Raaste</i> and <i>Bandhan</i>, with Rajesh Khanna, became top grossers of the year, earning around 65 million and 28 million respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She played <a href="/wiki/Rajendra_Kumar" title="Rajendra Kumar">Rajendra Kumar</a>'s leading lady in <i>Tangewala</i>. <a href="/wiki/Shashi_Kapoor" title="Shashi Kapoor">Shashi Kapoor</a>, who had earlier refused to work with her in <i>Saccha Jootha</i> because she was a "stunt-film heroine", now wanted her to be his heroine in <i><a href="/wiki/Chor_Machaye_Shor" title="Chor Machaye Shor">Chor Machaye Shor</a></i>. She acted opposite Dharmendra as the lead heroine in films like <i>Loafer</i> and <i>Jheel ke us paar</i> (1973).</p>
<p>She won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Best_Actress_Award" title="Filmfare Best Actress Award">Filmfare Best Actress Award</a> for one of her favourite films <i><a href="/wiki/Khilona_(1970_film)" title="Khilona (1970 film)">Khilona</a></i> in 1970, and she was "very happy that the audience accepted her in an emotional role".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo-2">[2]</a></sup> Mumtaz acted with <a href="/wiki/Dharmendra" title="Dharmendra">Dharmendra</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feroz_Khan_(actor,_born_1939)" title="Feroz Khan (actor, born 1939)">Feroz Khan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sanjeev_Kumar" title="Sanjeev Kumar">Sanjeev Kumar</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biswajeet" title="Biswajeet">Biswajeet</a>, and she acted with <a href="/wiki/Rajesh_Khanna" title="Rajesh Khanna">Rajesh Khanna</a> in 10 films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mumtaz_(actress)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mumtaz married businessman <a href="/wiki/Mayur_Madhvani" title="Mayur Madhvani">Mayur Madhvani</a> in 1974. They have two daughters one of whom, Natasha, married actor <a href="/wiki/Feroz_Khan_(actor)" title="Feroz Khan (actor)">Feroz Khan</a>'s son <a href="/wiki/Fardeen_Khan" title="Fardeen Khan">Fardeen Khan</a> in 2006.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mumtaz_(actress)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mumtaz won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Best_Actress_Award" title="Filmfare Best Actress Award">Filmfare Best Actress Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Khilona_(1970_film)" title="Khilona (1970 film)">Khilona</a> in 1970. Actually no one was interested to accept the role of "CHAAND" for Khilona, just because she was a prostitute in the storyline. But with that role Mumtaz bagged her one and only "Filmfare Best Actress Award".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>During her career, she was awarded with one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award" title="Filmfare Award">Filmfare Award</a> for Best Actress, out of three nominations, and one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BFJA_Award" title="BFJA Award">BFJA Award</a> for Best Supporting Actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bfjaaward.com_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-bfjaaward.com-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p><b>Winner</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BFJA_Awards" title="BFJA Awards">BFJA Award</a> for Best Supporting Actress – <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brahmachari_(Hindi_film)" title="Brahmachari (Hindi film)">Brahmachari</a></i> (1968)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bfjaaward.com_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-bfjaaward.com-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Best_Actress_Award" title="Filmfare Best Actress Award">Filmfare Best Actress Award</a> – <i><a href="/wiki/Khilona_(1970_film)" title="Khilona (1970 film)">Khilona</a></i> (1970)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Symbol_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Symbol-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Lifetime_Achievement_Award" title="Filmfare Lifetime Achievement Award">Filmfare Lifetime Achievement Award</a> (1996)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Symbol_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Symbol-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/International_Indian_Film_Academy_Awards" title="International Indian Film Academy Awards">IIFA</a> Outstanding Contribution by an Indian in Cinema, Honorary Award (2008)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Nominated</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Best_Supporting_Actress_Award" title="Filmfare Best Supporting Actress Award">Filmfare Best Supporting Actress Award</a> – <i><a href="/wiki/Ram_Aur_Shyam" title="Ram Aur Shyam">Ram Aur Shyam</a></i> (1967)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Best_Supporting_Actress_Award" title="Filmfare Best Supporting Actress Award">Filmfare Best Supporting Actress Award</a> – <i><a href="/wiki/Aadmi_Aur_Insaan" title="Aadmi Aur Insaan">Aadmi Aur Insaan</a></i> (1969)</li>
</ul>
