<p>The <b>Museumplein</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Dutch" title="Help:IPA for Dutch">[myˌzeːjɵmˈplɛin]</a></span>; English: Museum Square) is a <a href="/wiki/Public_space" title="Public space">public space</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Museumkwartier_(Amsterdam)" title="Museumkwartier (Amsterdam)">Museumkwartier</a> neighbourhood of the <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam-Zuid" title="Amsterdam-Zuid">Amsterdam-Zuid</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stadsdeel" title="Stadsdeel">borough</a> in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. Located at the Museumplein are three major museums – the <a href="/wiki/Rijksmuseum" title="Rijksmuseum">Rijksmuseum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Van_Gogh_Museum" title="Van Gogh Museum">Van Gogh Museum</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stedelijk_Museum_Amsterdam" title="Stedelijk Museum Amsterdam">Stedelijk Museum</a> – and the concert hall <a href="/wiki/Concertgebouw" title="Concertgebouw">Concertgebouw</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mdist_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mdist-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The area was originally a wax candle factory and marshy meadows. Construction began following the completion of the Rijksmuseum in 1885, with a street plan based on the design of <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Cuypers" title="Pierre Cuypers">Pierre Cuypers</a>, the museum’s celebrated architect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mdist_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-mdist-1">[1]</a></sup> The area was the location of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internationale_Koloniale_en_Uitvoerhandel_Tentoonstelling" title="Internationale Koloniale en Uitvoerhandel Tentoonstelling">International Colonial and Export Exhibition</a> in 1883.</p>
<p>The Museumplein was reconstructed after a design by the <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a>/<a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> <a href="/wiki/Landscape_architecture" title="Landscape architecture">landscape architect</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sven-Ingvar_Andersson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sven-Ingvar Andersson (page does not exist)">Sven-Ingvar Andersson</a> in 1999. It now includes underground parking spaces and an underground supermarket. In the winter, the pond can be transformed into an artificial <a href="/wiki/Ice_skating" title="Ice skating">ice skating</a> area.</p>
<p>The space is also used for (mass) events such as <a href="/wiki/Festival" title="Festival">festivals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Party" title="Party">celebrations</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demonstration_(people)" title="Demonstration (people)">demonstrations</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armin_Van_Buuren" title="Armin Van Buuren">Armin Van Buuren</a> honoured <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">The Dutch Team</a> at Museumplein in 2010 by playing <a href="/wiki/Swedish_House_Mafia" title="Swedish House Mafia">Swedish House Mafia</a>.</p>
