<p>The <b>Munich U-Bahn</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de"><i>U-Bahn München</i></span>) system is an <a href="/wiki/Railway_electrification_system" title="Railway electrification system">electric rail</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit network</a> in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. "U-Bahn" is the German contraction for <i>Untergrundbahn</i> or "<a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">underground railway</a>". The Munich U-Bahn began operation in 1971.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-figures_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-figures-3">[2]</a></sup> It is operated by the municipally owned <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchner_Verkehrsgesellschaft" title="Münchner Verkehrsgesellschaft">Münchner Verkehrsgesellschaft</a> (the Munich Transport Company) or MVG. The network is integrated into the <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchner_Verkehrs-_und_Tarifverbund" title="Münchner Verkehrs- und Tarifverbund">Münchner Verkehrs- und Tarifverbund</a> (the Munich Transport and Tariff Association) or MVV and interconnected with the <a href="/wiki/Munich_S-Bahn" title="Munich S-Bahn">Munich S-Bahn</a> system. The Munich U-Bahn system currently comprises eight lines, serving 96 stations (100 stations if four interchange stations with separate levels for different lines are counted twice), and encompassing 103.1 kilometres (64.1 mi) of routes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-figures_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-figures-3">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>The network has 103.1 kilometres (64.1 mi) of active route,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-figures_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-figures-3">[2]</a></sup> and 100 stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-figures_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-figures-3">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stations_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-stations-1">[note 1]</a></sup> In 2014, 390 million passengers rode the U-Bahn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-figures_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-figures-3">[2]</a></sup> The trains operate at speeds up to 80 kilometres per hour (50 mph), which is the top speed among German U-Bahns. There is no continuous operation during the night (break from 1 to 4 am, 2 to 4 am on weekends) except on special occasions such as <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year's Eve">New Year's Eve</a>.</p>
<p>Currently, only the U6 line, crosses the <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipal</a> border to the town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garching" title="Garching">Garching</a>.</p>
<p>Except for the lines U5 and U6, all lines operate completely below ground. U5 only comes above ground at the south terminus <i>Neuperlach-Süd</i>, U6 on the northern section from <i>Studentenstadt</i> (except Garching and Garching-Forschungszentrum stations and tunnels).</p>
<p>There are three "line families", which consist of two lines (not counting peak hour lines) that share a common track in the city centre. The schedules of these lines are coordinated in a way, which yields regular train intervals on the common section.</p>
<p>Most stations have two tracks with an island platform between them. Of the single-line stations, only the stations <i>Olympia-Einkaufszentrum</i> (U1), <i>Richard-Strauss-Straße</i> (U4), <i>Neuperlach Süd</i> (U5), <i>Garching-Hochbrück</i> and <i>Nordfriedhof</i> (both U6) have side platforms.</p>
<p>At the junction stations <i>Scheidplatz</i> and <i>Innsbrucker Ring</i>, the four tracks lie in parallel on the same level with two island platforms allowing <a href="/wiki/Cross-platform_interchange" title="Cross-platform interchange">cross-platform interchange</a>. The stations <i>Hauptbahnhof</i> (lower level), where U1 and U2 branch into two different lines and <i>Münchner Freiheit</i> (U3/U6) also have four tracks, while <i>Implerstraße</i> (U3/U6), <i>Max-Weber-Platz</i> (U4/U5) and <i>Kolumbusplatz</i> (U1/U2) have three: one with a side platform for outbound trains and two with a shared island platform for inbound trains.</p>
<p><i>Olympiazentrum</i>, <i>Fröttmaning</i> and <i>Kieferngarten</i> also have four tracks each, due to the proximity of the Olympic Stadium, the <i><a href="/wiki/Allianz_Arena" title="Allianz Arena">Allianz Arena</a></i> football stadium, and the technical base, respectively.</p>
<p>At <i>Hauptbahnhof</i>, there is a second U-Bahn station for lines U4/5 at a higher level, giving a total of six U-Bahn tracks. <i>Sendlinger Tor</i>, <i>Odeonsplatz</i> and <i>Olympia-Einkaufszentrum</i> also each have two stations at different levels being totally distinct stations on their own, connected with each other by escalators and elevators.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Frequency_and_scheduling">Frequency and scheduling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Frequency and scheduling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most lines operate with trains running at intervals of every 5 minutes during peak hours, but due to lines overlapping, a suitable train for a journey can be as frequent as every 2 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[3]</a></sup> Outside of peak times lines operate trains at frequencies of every 10 minutes; however, around the start of operations and after midnight the line frequency decreases to every 20 minutes or more on most lines. Again with line overlap this means that a suitable train will arrive (often much) more frequently.</p>

<p>In 1980 the U1 commenced operation together with the U8 (now U2). At the beginning it was only operating on a section of U2's track. When the branch to <i><a href="/wiki/Rotkreuzplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Rotkreuzplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Rotkreuzplatz</a></i> was opened, it became a separate line. The line's colour is green.</p>
<p>Today the U1 has a length of 12.2 kilometres (7.6 mi) and 15 stations. It starts at <i><a href="/wiki/Olympia-Einkaufszentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Olympia-Einkaufszentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Olympia-Einkaufszentrum</a></i> in the district of Moosach. The U3 was extended to the same station (but on a different level) in 2007. On the way south it follows Hanauer Straße to <i><a href="/wiki/Georg-Brauchle-Ring_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Georg-Brauchle-Ring (Munich U-Bahn)">Georg-Brauchle-Ring</a></i>, which has been designed by <a href="/wiki/Franz_Ackermann" title="Franz Ackermann">Franz Ackermann</a>, reaching <i><a href="/wiki/Westfriedhof_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Westfriedhof (Munich U-Bahn)">Westfriedhof</a></i>. It continues via <i><a href="/wiki/Gern_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Gern (Munich U-Bahn)">Gern</a></i> to <i><a href="/wiki/Rotkreuzplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Rotkreuzplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Rotkreuzplatz</a></i>, which was its terminus from 1983 to 1998. Below Nympenburger Straße it goes on to <i><a href="/wiki/Maillingerstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Maillingerstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Maillingerstraße</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Stiglmaierplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Stiglmaierplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Stiglmaierplatz</a></i> and finally merges into the U2 track at <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Hauptbahnhof" title="München Hauptbahnhof">München Hauptbahnhof</a></i>.</p>

<p>On the busy city section, U1 and U2 run with a 5-minute offset, yielding 5 minute intervals even beyond peak hours. At Central Station, it also crosses the <a href="/wiki/Munich_S-Bahn" title="Munich S-Bahn">S-Bahn</a> and U4/U5. At the next station, <i><a href="/wiki/Sendlinger_Tor_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Sendlinger Tor (Munich U-Bahn)">Sendlinger Tor</a></i>, it passes below U3/U6. There the U1/U2 platforms for each direction lie in tunnels which are apart from each other and are connected by a pedestrian tunnel.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Fraunhoferstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Fraunhoferstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Fraunhoferstraße</a></i>, the next station, is also reached in separate tunnels, which had to be excavated using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tunneling_shield" title="Tunneling shield">tunneling shields</a> due to the proximity of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Isar" title="River Isar">River Isar</a>. However, the two tubes are connected by the platform, which demanded large pillars that are characteristic for this station. The next station, <i><a href="/wiki/Kolumbusplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Kolumbusplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Kolumbusplatz</a></i>, is a junction which has three tracks. Here the U1 branches off the U2 again.</p>
<p>The southbound branch line was opened in 1997 and traverses the colourful station <i><a href="/wiki/Candidplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Candidplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Candidplatz</a></i>, eventually reaching <i><a href="/wiki/Wettersteinplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Wettersteinplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Wettersteinplatz</a></i>. The following station, <i><a href="/wiki/St.-Quirin-Platz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="St.-Quirin-Platz (Munich U-Bahn)">St.-Quirin-Platz</a></i> has an extraordinary architecture, as it is covered by a large, shell-like structure made from glass and steel, which is drawn nearly down to track level on one side. The U1 terminates at <i><a href="/wiki/Mangfallplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Mangfallplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Mangfallplatz</a></i> below Naupliastraße.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U2">U2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: U2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The route of the U2 line has undergone more changes than any of the other Munich underground lines. It also changed its name as it was first called U8. It is the only line that runs or ran on all three "line families" (U1/U2, U3/U6 and U4/U5). Today it has a length of 24.4 kilometres (15.2 mi) and 27 stations. The line's colour is red.</p>

<p>The U2 starts in the north at <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feldmoching_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Feldmoching (Munich U-Bahn)">Feldmoching</a></i>, where it connects to the S1 to <a href="/wiki/Freising" title="Freising">Freising</a>/<a href="/wiki/Munich_Airport" title="Munich Airport">Airport</a>. The station there is decorated with rural and urban motives of Feldmoching's history. Below <i><a href="/wiki/Hasenbergl_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Hasenbergl (Munich U-Bahn)">Hasenbergl</a></i>, a district which had been known for its social problems, it goes to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D%C3%BClferstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Dülferstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Dülferstraße</a></i>, which provides access to the eastern Hasenbergl and a newly built area on Panzerwiese. <i>Dülferstraße</i> was the terminus from 1993 till 1996.</p>
<p>Via the stations <i><a href="/wiki/Harthof_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Harthof (Munich U-Bahn)">Harthof</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Am_Hart_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Am Hart (Munich U-Bahn)">Am Hart</a></i>, the U2 reaches <i><a href="/wiki/Frankfurter_Ring_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Frankfurter Ring (Munich U-Bahn)">Frankfurter Ring</a></i>. In the tunnel between <i>Am Hart</i> and <i>Frankfurter Ring</i>, there is a white and blue wave pattern, which is the only installation of art in a Munich U-Bahn tunnel outside of stations.</p>
<p>After <i><a href="/wiki/Milbertshofen_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Milbertshofen (Munich U-Bahn)">Milbertshofen</a></i> station the U2 touches the U3 line at <i><a href="/wiki/Scheidplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Scheidplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Scheidplatz</a></i>, where cross-platform interchange is possible. Before the opening of the section to <i>Dülferstraße</i> in 1993, U2 went from <i>Scheidplatz</i> to <i><a href="/wiki/Olympiazentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Olympiazentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Olympiazentrum</a></i>, sharing the track with the U3. Through the district of Maxvorstadt the U2 continues to downtown Munich, reaching the stations <i><a href="/wiki/Hohenzollernplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Hohenzollernplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Hohenzollernplatz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Josephsplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Josephsplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Josephsplatz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Theresienstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Theresienstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Theresienstraße</a></i> und <i><a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Königsplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Königsplatz</a></i>. At <i>Königsplatz</i> one can find artworks from the nearby <a href="/wiki/Glyptothek" title="Glyptothek">Glyptothek</a> on the platform.</p>
<p>At <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Hauptbahnhof" title="München Hauptbahnhof">München Hauptbahnhof</a></i> (Munich Central Station), the U2 meets the U1, with which it shares tracks until <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kolumbusplatz" title="Kolumbusplatz">Kolumbusplatz</a></i> (see above).</p>
<p>After <i>Kolumbusplatz</i> the U2 continues eastward and reaches the stations <i><a href="/wiki/Silberhornstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Silberhornstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Silberhornstraße</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Untersbergstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Untersbergstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Untersbergstraße</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Giesing" title="Giesing">Giesing</a> (Bahnhof)</i>, with an interchange possibility to S7 and S3. The next stations are <i><a href="/wiki/Karl-Preis-Platz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Karl-Preis-Platz (Munich U-Bahn)">Karl-Preis-Platz</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Innsbrucker_Ring_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Innsbrucker Ring (Munich U-Bahn)">Innsbrucker Ring</a></i>, where cross-platform interchange to the U5 is possible. Until 1999, when the branch to the <i>Messestadt</i> stations was opened, the U2 ran from here to Neuperlach.</p>
<p>Via the stations <i><a href="/wiki/Josephsburg_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Josephsburg (Munich U-Bahn)">Josephsburg</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Kreillerstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Kreillerstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Kreillerstraße</a></i> the U2 reaches <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trudering_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Trudering (Munich U-Bahn)">Trudering</a></i>, which features two platforms in separate tunnels, connected by two transversal tunnels. In 1994, during the construction of this section, an accident happened: the ceiling of the new tunnel collapsed due to the intrusion of water and a bus fell into the crater. Two passengers and one construction worker died and the construction was delayed.</p>
<p>Via <i><a href="/wiki/Moosfeld_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Moosfeld (Munich U-Bahn)">Moosfeld</a></i>, the U2 reaches <i><a href="/wiki/Messestadt-West_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Messestadt-West (Munich U-Bahn)">Messestadt-West</a></i> and its terminus <i><a href="/wiki/Messestadt-Ost_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Messestadt-Ost (Munich U-Bahn)">Messestadt-Ost</a></i>. These stations are located between the fairgrounds (Messestadt) in the north and a development area and the <a href="/wiki/Bundesgartenschau" title="Bundesgartenschau">Bundesgartenschau</a> 2005 in the south.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U3">U3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: U3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The U3 is the original Olympic line; the first section was opened for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Summer_Games_1972" title="Olympic Summer Games 1972">Olympic Summer Games 1972</a>. Today the line has a total of length of 21.2 kilometres (13.2 mi) and 25 stations. The line's colour is orange.</p>
<p>Today the U3 starts in the north at <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Moosach_railway_station" title="München Moosach railway station">Moosach</a></i>, Munich's 100th U-Bahn station, where you can change to the S1 to <a href="/wiki/Freising" title="Freising">Freising</a>/<a href="/wiki/Munich_Airport" title="Munich Airport">Airport</a>. From here the line runs east to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moosacher_St.Martin_Platz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Moosacher St.Martin Platz (Munich U-Bahn)">Moosacher St.-Martins-Platz</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Olympia-Einkaufszentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Olympia-Einkaufszentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Olympia-Einkaufszentrum</a></i>, where a change to the U1 is possible. After passing through <i><a href="/wiki/Oberwiesenfeld_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Oberwiesenfeld (Munich U-Bahn)">Oberwiesenfeld station</a></i>, the U1 reaches its original northern terminus at <i><a href="/wiki/Olympiazentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Olympiazentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Olympiazentrum</a></i>. From 1972 until 2010, this station was the end of the original Olympic line.</p>
<p>When Munich was awarded the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Summer_Games_1972" title="Olympic Summer Games 1972">Olympic Summer Games 1972</a> in 1965, the U-Bahn network concept (which was adopted only one year earlier) had to be revised to speed up the construction of a connection to the Olympic venues at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympiapark,_Munich" title="Olympiapark, Munich">Olympic grounds</a>. The Olympic connector (now U3) was redesigned as a branch of the U6 line, because the original plan of a direct connection to the Olympic ground from Munich Central Station was not feasible in the shortness of time. This original U3 sections consists of four stations (from north to south): <i><a href="/wiki/Olympiazentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Olympiazentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Olympiazentrum</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Petuelring_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Petuelring (Munich U-Bahn)">Petuelring</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Scheidplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Scheidplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Scheidplatz</a></i>, where cross-platform interchange to the U2 (the line originally supposed to serve the Olympic venues) is possible since 1980, and <i><a href="/wiki/Bonner_Platz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Bonner Platz (Munich U-Bahn)">Bonner Platz</a></i>. After <i>Bonner Platz</i> the U3 reaches <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchner_Freiheit_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Münchner Freiheit (Munich U-Bahn)">Münchner Freiheit</a></i>, where it joins the U6 to run together through the inner city section to <a href="/wiki/Implerstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Implerstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Implerstraße</a> (for this section see U6 below).</p>
<p>After leaving the three-track junction station <i><a href="/wiki/Implerstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Implerstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Implerstraße</a></i>, the U3 reaches <i><a href="/wiki/Bruderm%C3%BChlstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Brudermühlstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Brudermühlstraße</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Thalkirchen_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Thalkirchen (Munich U-Bahn)">Thalkirchen (Zoo)</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Obersendling_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Obersendling (Munich U-Bahn)">Obersendling</a></i>, which is built 30 metres (98 ft) higher than the Thalkirchen station, because it is located on the "Hochufer" (western tread) of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Isar" title="River Isar">River Isar</a>. Here, interchange to the S-Bahn at <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Siemenswerke_railway_station" title="München Siemenswerke railway station">Siemenswerke</a></i> station is possible. The U3 continues west via <i><a href="/wiki/Aidenbachstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Aidenbachstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Aidenbachstraße</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Machtlfinger_Stra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Machtlfinger Straße (Munich U-Bahn)">Machtlfinger Straße</a></i>, before reaching <i><a href="/wiki/Forstenrieder_Allee_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Forstenrieder Allee (Munich U-Bahn)">Forstenrieder Allee</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Basler_Stra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Basler Straße (Munich U-Bahn)">Basler Straße</a></i>, and eventually the terminus <i><a href="/wiki/F%C3%BCrstenried_West_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Fürstenried West (Munich U-Bahn)">Fürstenried West</a></i>. This southern-eastern section was opened on October 28, 1989, as can be seen from huge date numbers on the western entrance of <i>Obersendling</i> station.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U4">U4</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: U4">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With only 9.2 kilometres (5.7 mi) and 13 stations, the U4 is Munich's shortest U-Bahn line. This line has originally been planned as U9 and is the only line that operates regularly with 2-train sets rather than the full 3-train set. The exceptions are Fridays in the late afternoon and during the <a href="/wiki/Oktoberfest" title="Oktoberfest">Oktoberfest</a>. The line's colour is mint green.</p>
<p>The U4 begins in the west in the <i>Laim</i> neighbourhood at <i><a href="/wiki/Westendstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Westendstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Westendstraße station</a></i>, which it shares with the U5 line. Both U4 and U5 are the only lines of a joint line "family", which only branch out on one end of the common line, as an originally planned western extension of the U4 was first put on hold and was subsequently cancelled altogether.</p>
<p>From <i>Westendstraße</i> the U4 runs east to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen-Heimeranplatz_station" title="München-Heimeranplatz station">Heimeranplatz</a></i>, which connects to S7 and S20 S-Bahn lines. The next two station, <i><a href="/wiki/Schwanthalerh%C3%B6he_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Schwanthalerhöhe (Munich U-Bahn)">Schwanthalerhöhe</a></i> (originally called <i>Messegelände</i> (German for fairgrounds) until the fair relocated to Riem in 1998) and <i><a href="/wiki/Theresienwiese_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Theresienwiese (Munich U-Bahn)">Theresienwiese</a></i>, are gateways to the <a href="/wiki/Oktoberfest" title="Oktoberfest">Oktoberfest</a>, and are therefore highly loaded during this event. Between both aforementioned stations, there is a track that links to <i><a href="/wiki/Implerstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Implerstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Implerstraße</a></i> to provide a connection to the depot in <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%B6ttmaning_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Fröttmaning (Munich U-Bahn)">Fröttmaning</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Munich_subway_station_Theresienwiese.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="852" data-file-width="1136" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Munich_subway_station_Theresienwiese.jpg/220px-Munich_subway_station_Theresienwiese.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Munich_subway_station_Theresienwiese.jpg/330px-Munich_subway_station_Theresienwiese.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Munich_subway_station_Theresienwiese.jpg/440px-Munich_subway_station_Theresienwiese.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p><a href="/wiki/Theresienwiese_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Theresienwiese (Munich U-Bahn)">Theresienwiese</a> is one of only two U-Bahn station in Munich (besides <i><a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%B6ttmaning_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Fröttmaning (Munich U-Bahn)">Fröttmaning station</a></i> which serves <a href="/wiki/Allianz_Arena" title="Allianz Arena">Allianz Arena</a>) to have the command centre booth that is opened during the <a href="/wiki/Oktoberfest" title="Oktoberfest">Oktoberfest</a> for supervising the masses of passengers. The south exit of the station leads to the northern entrance of Oktoberfest. U4 trains arriving from the east often terminate at <i>Theresienwiese</i> rather than continue to <i>Westendstraße</i> even during peak hours due to low traffic volume east of Hauptbahnhof.</p>
<p>After <i>Theresienwiese</i> the U4 reaches <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Hauptbahnhof" title="München Hauptbahnhof">München Hauptbahnhof (Munich Central Station)</a>; passengers can transfer to U1/U2 lines as well as to all S-Bahn lines (except S20) here. The next station is <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Karlsplatz_railway_station" title="München Karlsplatz railway station">Karlsplatz (Stachus)</a></i> with shorter and easier connections to S-Bahn (S1 to S8). Karlsplatz is the deepest station in Munich's U-Bahn network (36 metres (118 ft) below the surface). From this point on, the U4 runs north of the S-Bahn cross-city tunnel.</p>
<p>After passing <i><a href="/wiki/Odeonsplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Odeonsplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Odeonsplatz</a></i>, where an interchange to U3/U6 trains is possible, and <i><a href="/wiki/Lehel_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Lehel (Munich U-Bahn)">Lehel</a></i>, the U4 crosses the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Isar" title="River Isar">River Isar</a> in a tunnel, and reaches <i><a href="/wiki/Max-Weber-Platz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Max-Weber-Platz (Munich U-Bahn)">Max-Weber-Platz</a></i>, the last station that is shares with the U5. Here, the U4 branches off to the north, while the U5 runs south.</p>
<p>Before terminating at <i><a href="/wiki/Arabellapark_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Arabellapark (Munich U-Bahn)">Arabellapark</a></i>, the U4 passes the stations <i><a href="/wiki/Prinzregentenplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Prinzregentenplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Prinzregentenplatz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/B%C3%B6hmerwaldplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Böhmerwaldplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Böhmerwaldplatz</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Richard-Strauss-Stra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Richard-Strauss-Straße (Munich U-Bahn)">Richard-Strauss-Straße</a></i>, the latter being the only station of the line to be equipped with side platforms instead of an island platform.</p>
<p>The original plan called for an extension to <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%BCnchen_Johanneskirchen_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="München Johanneskirchen railway station (page does not exist)">Johanneskirchen station</a></i> (where easy transfer to the S8 S-Bahn line would be possible) via <i>Fideliopark</i>, but was never built, due to low current ridership in the area north of <i>Max-Weber-Platz</i>. The extension of the tram line in the area in 2011 made the plan even more unlikely to materialise. A possible extension in the west to <i>Blumenau</i> is even more improbable.</p>
<p>In the evenings from around 20.40 to the close of operations, the U4 only operates between <i><a href="/wiki/Odeonsplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Odeonsplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Odeonsplatz</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Arabellapark_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Arabellapark (Munich U-Bahn)">Arabellapark</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U5">U5</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: U5">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The U5 currently begins at <i><a href="/wiki/Laimer_Platz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Laimer Platz (Munich U-Bahn)">Laimer Platz</a></i>; an extension to <i>Pasing</i> is planned. The total length currently is 15.4 kilometres (9.6 mi). The line's signature colour is brown.</p>
<p>Via <i><a href="/wiki/Friedenheimer_Stra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Friedenheimer Straße (Munich U-Bahn)">Friedenheimer Straße</a></i>, the U5 reaches <i><a href="/wiki/Westendstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Westendstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Westendstraße</a></i>. From there, the U5 shares the tracks with the U4 to <a href="/wiki/Max-Weber-Platz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Max-Weber-Platz (Munich U-Bahn)"><i>Max-Weber-Platz</i></a> (see above).</p>
<p>At <i><a href="/wiki/Max-Weber-Platz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Max-Weber-Platz (Munich U-Bahn)">Max-Weber-Platz</a></i>, the U5 branches off to the south to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ostbahnhof_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Ostbahnhof (Munich U-Bahn)">East Station (<i>Ostbahnhof</i>)</a>, where changing to all S-Bahn lines is possible. The next station, <i><a href="/wiki/Innsbrucker_Ring_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Innsbrucker Ring (Munich U-Bahn)">Innsbrucker Ring</a></i>, allows cross-platform interchange to the U2.</p>
<p>The U5 continues south to <i><a href="/wiki/Michaelibad_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Michaelibad (Munich U-Bahn)">Michaelibad</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Quiddestra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Quiddestraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Quiddestraße</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Neuperlach_Zentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Neuperlach Zentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Neuperlach Zentrum</a></i>, which is the centre of the satellite town of <a href="/wiki/Neuperlach" title="Neuperlach">Neuperlach</a>, built during the 1960s and 1970s. Going on to <i><a href="/wiki/Therese-Giehse-Allee_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Therese-Giehse-Allee (Munich U-Bahn)">Therese-Giehse-Allee</a></i>, the U5 comes above ground and reaches its terminus <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neuperlach_S%C3%BCd_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Neuperlach Süd (Munich U-Bahn)">Neuperlach Süd</a></i>, where it allows cross-platform interchange with S-Bahn line <a href="/wiki/S7_(Munich)" title="S7 (Munich)">S7</a>. South-east of <i>Neuperlach-Süd</i> is a large parking yard (<i>Betriebsanlage Süd</i>) used to park trains which can't be parked at the technical base in Fröttmaning or within the network.</p>

<p>The U6 is the oldest U-Bahn line of the network and also features the oldest tunnel built: the section below the Lindwurmstraße (between <i>Sendlinger Tor</i> and including the station <i>Goetheplatz</i>) was already built 1938-1941 as part of a planned S-Bahn network. For this reason <i>Goetheplatz</i> has a platform longer than the standard 120 metres (390 ft). Today the line has a length of 27.4 kilometres (17.0 mi). Its signature colour is blue.</p>
<p>Since 2006 the northern terminus of the U6 is <i><a href="/wiki/Garching-Forschungszentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Garching-Forschungszentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Garching-Forschungszentrum</a></i>; via <i><a href="/wiki/Garching_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Garching (Munich U-Bahn)">Garching</a></i> it reaches <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garching-Hochbr%C3%BCck_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Garching-Hochbrück (Munich U-Bahn)">Garching-Hochbrück</a></i>. These three stations are outside the city limits of Munich in the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Garching" title="Garching">Garching</a></p>
<p>The distance of 4.1 kilometres (2.5 mi) to the next station at <i><a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%B6ttmaning_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Fröttmaning (Munich U-Bahn)">Fröttmaning</a></i> is the longest distance between two stations in Munich's U-Bahn network. <i>Fröttmaning</i> has been expanded to two island platforms and four tracks to cater for the <a href="/wiki/Allianz_Arena" title="Allianz Arena">Allianz Arena</a> football stadium, built for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 Football World Cup</a>. The technical base of the U-Bahn is located at Fröttmaning, too. After passing <i><a href="/wiki/Kieferngarten_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Kieferngarten (Munich U-Bahn)">Kieferngarten station</a></i>, which has two island platforms as well, it crosses over a rail bridge to <i><a href="/wiki/Freimann_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Freimann (Munich U-Bahn)">Freimann</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Studentenstadt_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Studentenstadt (Munich U-Bahn)">Studentenstadt</a></i>. Between these two stations is a connection to mainline railway tracks, which is used to bring new trains into the network. The bridge was originally used by the <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">tram</a> and was the only tram track to be converted to be part of the U-Bahn network. The U6 then continues underground for the rest of its way south.</p>
<p>Via <i><a href="/wiki/Alte_Heide_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Alte Heide (Munich U-Bahn)">Alte Heide</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Nordfriedhof_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Nordfriedhof (Munich U-Bahn)">Nordfriedhof</a></i> (station with side platforms), and <i><a href="/wiki/Dietlindenstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Dietlindenstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Dietlindenstraße</a></i>, the U6 reaches <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchner_Freiheit_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Münchner Freiheit (Munich U-Bahn)">Münchner Freiheit</a></i>, where it joins the U3 on the shared inner city tunnel.</p>
<p>Passing <i><a href="/wiki/Giselastra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Giselastraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Giselastraße</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A4t_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Universität (Munich U-Bahn)">Universität</a></i> (University), it arrives at <i><a href="/wiki/Odeonsplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Odeonsplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Odeonsplatz</a></i>, where it connects to the U4/U5 lines. Continuing to <i>Marienplatz</i>, it crosses the S-Bahn lines. During peak hours this station is can get overcrowded, which is why additional pedestrian tunnels were built between 2003 and 2006.</p>
<p>At <i><a href="/wiki/Sendlinger_Tor_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Sendlinger Tor (Munich U-Bahn)">Sendlinger Tor</a></i> the U3/U6 crosses the U1/U2 line, and interchange is possible. The line now uses the tunnel built in 1941 mentioned above as far as <i><a href="/wiki/Goetheplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Goetheplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Goetheplatz</a></i>. The next station, <i><a href="/wiki/Poccistra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Poccistraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Poccistraße</a></i> was added belatedly, constructed between the two existing tunnels which stayed operational. At <i><a href="/wiki/Implerstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Implerstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Implerstraße</a></i> the U3 and U6 separate again. To the north of the station, facing north, there is a branch to the U4/U5 at <i><a href="/wiki/Schwanthalerh%C3%B6he_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Schwanthalerhöhe (Munich U-Bahn)">Schwanthalerhöhe</a></i>, which is not used for passenger transport.</p>
<p>At <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harras_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Harras (Munich U-Bahn)">Harras</a></i> the U6 connects to the S-Bahn lines S7 and S27, and to regional trains to the south. The section via <i><a href="/wiki/Partnachplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Partnachplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Partnachplatz</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Westpark_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Westpark (Munich U-Bahn)">Westpark</a></i> to <i><a href="/wiki/Holzapfelkreuth_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Holzapfelkreuth (Munich U-Bahn)">Holzapfelkreuth</a></i> was constructed for the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Internationale_Gartenbauausstellung&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Internationale Gartenbauausstellung (page does not exist)">Internationalen Gartenbauausstellung (IGA)</a> in 1983 was therefore dubbled "flower line", which is reflected in the design of these stations. Passing <i><a href="/wiki/Haderner_Stern_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Haderner Stern (Munich U-Bahn)">Haderner Stern</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Gro%C3%9Fhadern_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Großhadern (Munich U-Bahn)">Großhadern</a></i>, the U6 reaches its current southern terminus at <i><a href="/wiki/Klinikum_Gro%C3%9Fhadern_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Klinikum Großhadern (Munich U-Bahn)">Klinikum Großhadern</a></i>, where the entrance to the station is covered by a glass pyramid.</p>
<p>An extension to <i>Martinsried</i>, which is only approx. 1 kilometre (0.62 mi) west of the current terminus, is planned and agreed upon, and is supposed to open in 2019.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U7">U7</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: U7">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This new booster line was added in December 2011 along with the new tram extension to St. Emmeram. The U7 runs between <i><a href="/wiki/Westfriedhof_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Westfriedhof (Munich U-Bahn)">Westfriedhof</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Neuperlach_Zentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Neuperlach Zentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Neuperlach Zentrum</a></i> via <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Hauptbahnhof" title="München Hauptbahnhof">München Hauptbahnhof</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Innsbrucker_Ring_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Innsbrucker Ring (Munich U-Bahn)">Innsbrucker Ring</a></i>: it shares the tracks with the U1 from <i><a href="/wiki/Westfriedhof_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Westfriedhof (Munich U-Bahn)">Westfriedhof</a></i> to <i><a href="/wiki/Kolumbusplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Kolumbusplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Kolumbusplatz</a></i>, the U2 from <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Hauptbahnhof" title="München Hauptbahnhof">München Hauptbahnhof</a></i> to <i><a href="/wiki/Innsbrucker_Ring_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Innsbrucker Ring (Munich U-Bahn)">Innsbrucker Ring</a></i>, and the U5 from <i><a href="/wiki/Innsbrucker_Ring_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Innsbrucker Ring (Munich U-Bahn)">Innsbrucker Ring</a></i> to its southern terminus <i><a href="/wiki/Neuperlach_Zentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Neuperlach Zentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Neuperlach Zentrum</a></i>.</p>
<p>The stations along U7 line are <i><a href="/wiki/Westfriedhof_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Westfriedhof (Munich U-Bahn)">Westfriedhof</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gern_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Gern (Munich U-Bahn)">Gern</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Rotkreuzplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Rotkreuzplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Rotkreuzplatz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Maillingerstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Maillingerstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Maillingerstraße</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Stiglmaierplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Stiglmaierplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Stiglmaierplatz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Hauptbahnhof" title="München Hauptbahnhof">München Hauptbahnhof</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sendlinger_Tor_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Sendlinger Tor (Munich U-Bahn)">Sendlinger Tor</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fraunhoferstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Fraunhoferstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Fraunhoferstraße</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kolumbusplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Kolumbusplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Kolumbusplatz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Silberhornstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Silberhornstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Silberhornstraße</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Untersbergstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Untersbergstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Untersbergstraße</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen-Giesing_station" title="München-Giesing station">Giesing Bahnhof</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Karl-Preis-Platz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Karl-Preis-Platz (Munich U-Bahn)">Karl-Preis-Platz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Innsbrucker_Ring_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Innsbrucker Ring (Munich U-Bahn)">Innsbrucker Ring</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Michaelibad_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Michaelibad (Munich U-Bahn)">Michaelibad</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Quiddestra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Quiddestraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Quiddestraße</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Neuperlach_Zentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Neuperlach Zentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Neuperlach Zentrum</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U8">U8</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: U8">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This booster line started operations in December 2013. The U8 begins in the north at <i><a href="/wiki/Olympiazentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Olympiazentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Olympiazentrum</a></i> and shares the tracks with the U3 as far as <i><a href="/wiki/Scheidplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Scheidplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Scheidplatz</a></i>, where it continues along the U2 tracks to <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Hauptbahnhof" title="München Hauptbahnhof">München Hauptbahnhof</a></i> and terminates at <i><a href="/wiki/Sendlinger_Tor_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Sendlinger Tor (Munich U-Bahn)">Sendlinger Tor</a></i>. It is only running on Saturdays to ease crowding on the U2 and U3 lines, and to provide people an easier access to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympiapark,_Munich" title="Olympiapark, Munich">Olympic Park</a> from Munich Central Station.</p>
<p>The stations along U8 line are <i><a href="/wiki/Olympiazentrum_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Olympiazentrum (Munich U-Bahn)">Olympiazentrum</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Petuelring_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Petuelring (Munich U-Bahn)">Petuelring</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Scheidplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Scheidplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Scheidplatz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hohenzollernplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Hohenzollernplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Hohenzollernplatz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Josephsplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Josephsplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Josephsplatz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Theresienstra%C3%9Fe_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Theresienstraße (Munich U-Bahn)">Theresienstraße</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsplatz_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Königsplatz (Munich U-Bahn)">Königsplatz</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchen_Hauptbahnhof" title="München Hauptbahnhof">München Hauptbahnhof</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Sendlinger_Tor_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Sendlinger Tor (Munich U-Bahn)">Sendlinger Tor</a></i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rolling_stock">Rolling stock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Rolling stock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Munich U-Bahn uses three different generations of <a href="/wiki/Electric_multiple_unit" title="Electric multiple unit">electric multiple unit</a> trains. The stock of over 550 carriages is shared between all lines.</p>

<p><b>Class A</b> trains were built between 1967 (prototypes) and 1983. The units consist of two carriages, which always remain coupled in normal operation. The double-carriage units have a length of 37.15 metres (121.9 ft), a height of 3.55 metres (11.6 ft), and a width of 2.90 metres (9.5 ft). Each unit has six doors per side and a capacity of 98 seats and standing room for 192 passengers. A total of 193 double-carriage units were delivered, of which 179 are still in use in Munich. Up to three A double-carriage units can be coupled together to form a 3/3 train (Langzug).</p>
<p><b>Class B</b> trains were built between 1981 and 1994 to provide more stock to service the growing network in the 1980s. As with the class A trains, six prototypes were ordered. However, it took six years until the series production started and the prototypes had to be modified to match the series-production units. B units have the same size as A units but differ in the design (especially of the front window) and use <a href="/wiki/Three-phase_electric_power" title="Three-phase electric power">three-phase current</a> instead of <a href="/wiki/Direct_current" title="Direct current">direct current</a> motors. The other difference is the door opening mechanism. On B units, the passengers only need to pull just one handle to open both doors rather than both handles as on A units. Of a total of 63 units, 57 are still in service (including one prototype), and six have been scrapped. As with A trains, up to three B double-carriage units can form a 3/3 train (Langzug). However, it is not possible to form a mixed train of A and B units, which are not compatible.</p>

<p><b>Class C</b> units were designed in the late 1990s to replace the A units, the operation of which is hardly economically reasonable after 30 years. C units consist of six carriages allowing passage through the whole train. Only the first and last unit of each train have driver's cabs. C units can only operate as six-unit trains which have the same length as 3/3 trains. Ten trains were ordered without prototype units being previously tested, and these were finally put into service in 2002 after long delays due to technical difficulties. Eight more units were delivered in the run-up to the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>A further 21 six-car Class C vehicles, designated as <b>C2</b>, were ordered from Siemens Mobility in November 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup> The C2 vehicles can ferry 940 passengers (as opposed to 912 by C1), have brighter lighting, monitors for passenger information, upholstered seats and coloured LEDs on the doors which signal opening and closing of the doors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup> As of May 2015, none of the C2 carriages had entered either limited or regular service. C2 had not obtained the operating certification due to technical defects, which have largely been addressed. In addition, numerous changes in federal and state law had caused some confusions and problems with preparing the necessary documentations for the certification process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[6]</a></sup> In June 2016, one Class C2 vehicle was permitted to operate on the northern part of U6 line between Kiefrngarten and Garching Forschungzentrum as a trial run for the Technical Supervisory Authority (Technischen Aufsichtsbehörde i.e. TAB) of the Oberbayern government to make their assessment. On 15.11.2016, a further 4 vehicles have been added to the Class C2 fleet and they are now operating on the entire U6 line from Garching Forschungzentrum to Klinikum Grosshadern. Upon approval from TAB, 16 more Class C2 vehicles, which are currently at Siemens, can join the Munich subway fleet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Operation">Operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most lines are serviced by trains of three class A or B units or one class C unit (3/3 trains, "Langzug") during the day. In the early morning and the late evening, some lines are only serviced with two unit trains ("Vollzug") or single unit trains ("Kurzzug")</p>
<p>During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Summer_Games_1972" title="Olympic Summer Games 1972">Olympic Summer Games 1972</a> and the visit of <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a> in 1980, rolling stock from <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_U-Bahn" title="Nuremberg U-Bahn">Nuremberg</a>, which was then compatible with Munich's stock, was used in Munich.</p>
<p>All stock has a maximum speed of 80 kilometres per hour (50 mph) and can be operated by <a href="/wiki/Linienzugbeeinflussung" title="Linienzugbeeinflussung">Linienzugbeeinflussung (LZB)</a>, which allows short train intervals at distances as small as 80 metres (260 ft).</p>
<p>As the control <a href="/wiki/Railway_coupling" title="Railway coupling">couplings</a> are asymmetric, only units facing in the same direction can be (electrically) coupled. As there is no way to turn units in the network, they are tied to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railroad_directions" title="Railroad directions">direction</a>, which is why every train unit as well as every station has a "north" and a "south" end.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents">Accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fortunately, there have been no serious accidents during service on the Munich U-Bahn. To date only three double-carriage units had to be scrapped due to accidents, all of which happened in the depot.</p>
<p>Two double-carriage units were destroyed on 5 September 1983, when a fan cooling the brake system was damaged. The train was taken out of service at <i>Hauptbahnhof</i> and parked on an underground siding track between there and <i>Königsplatz</i>, where it caught fire. Even though the fire brigade were quickly on the scene, the units were damaged beyond repair. Despite the tunnel ceiling being damaged in the incident, operation was quickly resumed.</p>
<p>Another double-carriage unit (one of the former class A prototypes) was damaged on 28 December 1995 during marshalling. It is intended to use the undamaged half of this unit as a display item in the transport department of the <a href="/wiki/Deutsches_Museum" title="Deutsches Museum">Deutsches Museum</a>.</p>
<p>The only fatal accident in connection to the U-Bahn happened on 20 September 1994, when the ceiling of a tunnel under construction collapsed near today's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trudering_station" title="Trudering station">Trudering station</a>, dragging a public bus into the ensuing hole in Truderinger Straße. Two passengers were killed, as well as the construction worker, who tried to warn the bus driver.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Plans for an underground for Munich are quite old. In the 1930s the Nazis forbade the acquisition of new rolling stock for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Munich_tramway" title="Munich tramway">Munich tramways</a> in order to show how "insufficient" the tram system was. At that time trams were the primary means of public transportation in Munich. The Nazis made ambitious plans to change Munich into their "Reichshauptstadt der Bewegung" (Capital of the movement; the Nazi party had come to existence in Munich). This included the construction of an underground system. In the late 1930s construction started in Lindwurmstraße and Sonnenstraße, where Munich's main Lutheran-Protestant church, Matthäuskirche, was torn down because it was supposedly a "traffic obstacle" (so was the Munich's main <a href="/wiki/Synagogue" title="Synagogue">synagogue</a> not far away as well as the tower of the <a href="/wiki/Old_Town_Hall,_Munich" title="Old Town Hall, Munich">Old Town Hall</a>). Construction was abandoned in 1941 as <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> intensified. After the war, the priority was to reconstruct the badly damaged tram system. However, even during the 1950s the Munich City Council discussed plans to run a few of the tram lines underground because the capacity for surface traffic was overstretched. In 1964 plans were, however, changed and it was decided to build a "real" underground network.</p>
<p>Work started on 1 February 1965 at Nordfriedhof (North Cemetery) in Ungererstraße. Today a steel girder at the first building site is a monument to Munich's first underground railway. When the <a href="/wiki/1972_Summer_Olympics" title="1972 Summer Olympics">1972 Summer Olympics</a> were awarded to Munich in 1966, construction had to be sped up to get the "Olympic" line finished on time. On 19 October 1971 the first line commenced operations between Kieferngarten and Goetheplatz with a total length of 12 kilometres (7.5 mi). On 8 May 1972 the line between Münchner Freiheit and Olympiazentrum ("Olympic line") to the venues of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Summer_Games_1972" title="Olympic Summer Games 1972">Olympic Summer Games 1972</a> was opened, just 10 days after the <a href="/wiki/Munich_S-Bahn" title="Munich S-Bahn">Munich S-Bahn</a> commenced operations. To satisfy demand during the Games, some DT1 trains were borrowed from <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_U-Bahn" title="Nuremberg U-Bahn">Nuremberg</a>. On 22 November 1975 the extension from Goetheplatz to Harras was opened. The network has been expanded continuously since 1980.</p>

<dl>
<dt><b>U6 (North):</b> Upgrade of Fröttmaning station</dt>
<dd>The new <a href="/wiki/Allianz_Arena" title="Allianz Arena">Allianz Arena</a> (football stadium) required a larger capacity of the nearby U-Bahn station. A new second platform was built and the whole station was moved north by roughly 100 metres (330 ft). For easy access to the platform, a second pedestrian bridge was built at the north end of the platforms.</dd>
</dl>

New pedestrian tunnel at Marienplatz station</div>

<dl>
<dt><b>U3/U6:</b> Upgrade of Marienplatz station</dt>
<dd>The increase in traffic and the new <a href="/wiki/Allianz_Arena" title="Allianz Arena">Allianz Arena</a> also required a larger capacity of this already overcrowded pivotal transfer station. New pedestrian tunnels were built, which provide more room for passengers transferring from and to the <a href="/wiki/Munich_S-Bahn" title="Munich S-Bahn">S-Bahn</a>. They lie parallel to the existing platforms and are connected to them by 11 portals. At the south end, they meet the transverse tunnel, where the escalators to the S-Bahn platforms are located.</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_plans">Further plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Further plans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U1_.28South.29:_The_Extension_to_Harlaching_Hospital_via_Laurinplatz">U1 (South): The Extension to Harlaching Hospital via Laurinplatz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: U1 (South): The Extension to Harlaching Hospital via Laurinplatz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although the plans for this extension were quite advanced, low passenger forecasts have led to its abandonment in favour of a tram or light rail from <i>Schwanseestraße</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U1_.28North.29:_Extension_to_Hansenbergl_Nord_and_Fasanerie">U1 (North): Extension to Hansenbergl Nord and Fasanerie</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: U1 (North): Extension to Hansenbergl Nord and Fasanerie">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With this extension, the U1 would end at an S-Bahn station and the <i>Olympia-Einkaufszentrum</i> would become the <i>Northern Cross</i> envisioned by the U-Bahn Office. This will also connect to the S-Bahn and the U-Bahn line U2 at Feldmoching, and It will directly terminate at Hasenbergl Nord, which will be 3 km north of the current <a href="/wiki/Hasenbergl_(Munich_U-Bahn)" title="Hasenbergl (Munich U-Bahn)">Hasenbergl</a></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U3_.28West.29:_Extension_to_Untermenzing">U3 (West): Extension to Untermenzing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: U3 (West): Extension to Untermenzing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This extension is planned after the U3 has been extended to <i>Moosach</i>, and it will go via Waldhornstraße.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U4_.28East.29:_Extension_to_Englschaking_Bahnhof">U4 (East): Extension to Englschaking Bahnhof</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: U4 (East): Extension to Englschaking Bahnhof">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This extension is considered in the third medium-term planning (Mittelfristprogramm) along with the plan of moving a portion of S8 line from above ground to underground from Unterföhring Bf. (S) to Leuchtenbergring Bf. (S). Whether the extension would be approved and when will it be built is not clear. The stations do include Cosimapark, Fideliopark and Englschalking Bf.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U5_.28West.29:_Extension_to_Pasing">U5 (West): Extension to Pasing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: U5 (West): Extension to Pasing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 3.7-kilometre extension has been approved on 14 July 2015 with construction to commence in 2019. The date of completion is still not set. The cost is about 547 million euros. The route will have two stations, Willibaldplatz and Am Knie, before terminating at Pasing train station. The Willibaldplatz station is to have three platforms with third track set aside for the possible future extension into the western and southwestern area. The extension will terminate west of Pasing train station to make room for the emergency evacuation and storage: this can, too, be readily extended further west to the city boundary if the demand calls for it.</p>
<p>On 17 July 2015, there are citizen initiative and petition drive<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup> supported by <a href="/wiki/Christian_Social_Union_in_Bavaria" title="Christian Social Union in Bavaria">Christian Social Union</a> (CSU) and <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Germany" title="Social Democratic Party of Germany">Social Democratic Party</a> (SPD) to extend U5 route to <a href="/wiki/Freiham" title="Freiham">Freiham</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> The reason for involvement of political parties and citizen initiative is the rejection by <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnchner_Verkehrsgesellschaft" title="Münchner Verkehrsgesellschaft">MVG</a> of this further extension due to cost factor and due to existence of two <a href="/wiki/Munich_S-Bahn" title="Munich S-Bahn">S-Bahn</a> stations in Freiham.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U6_.28South.29:_Extension_to_Martinsried">U6 (South): Extension to Martinsried</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: U6 (South): Extension to Martinsried">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This extension will give access to the biotech centre at <a href="/wiki/Martinsried" title="Martinsried">Martinsried</a>. As the tracks will cross the municipality boundary of Munich, planning and financing is the responsibility of the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Planegg" title="Planegg">Planegg</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Free State of Bavaria</a>. The further extension of the U6 to Martinsried was approved by the district council in July 2009. The 67 million euro and approximately 1,300-metre (4,270 ft) long line was to have started operation in 2014/2015. However, complications meant that the Bavarian state cabinet did not approve the project until December 2014. Construction is now to begin at the end of 2016 and conclude by 2020.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U6_.28North.29:_Extension_to_Munich_Airport">U6 (North): Extension to Munich Airport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: U6 (North): Extension to Munich Airport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This would have been be the only line with direct service to the Airport. Outside the Airport it should have connect with the S8 at Hallbergmoos, and in the airport it could have transferred with the station terminal at both terminals. The plans were abandoned, however. Originally, it could have went to Eching and Neufahrn.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U9_.28Bypass_Line.29">U9 (Bypass Line)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: U9 (Bypass Line)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The route is: Martinsried – Implerstraße – Central Station – Giselastraße – Garching-Forschungszentrum</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Karte_der_geplanten_U9-Spange_der_U-Bahn_M%C3%BCnchen.png"><img alt="Possible route of the new U9 line" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1580" data-file-width="2260" height="154" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Karte_der_geplanten_U9-Spange_der_U-Bahn_M%C3%BCnchen.png/220px-Karte_der_geplanten_U9-Spange_der_U-Bahn_M%C3%BCnchen.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Karte_der_geplanten_U9-Spange_der_U-Bahn_M%C3%BCnchen.png/330px-Karte_der_geplanten_U9-Spange_der_U-Bahn_M%C3%BCnchen.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Karte_der_geplanten_U9-Spange_der_U-Bahn_M%C3%BCnchen.png/440px-Karte_der_geplanten_U9-Spange_der_U-Bahn_M%C3%BCnchen.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Karte_der_geplanten_U9-Spange_der_U-Bahn_M%C3%BCnchen.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Possible route of the new U9 line; newly constructed sections highlighted in yellow</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On 11 February 2014, SWM/MVG announced a detailed report for the construction of new bypass line,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> a fourth intracity underground line, to be called the U9. The new line will relieve the overburdened U1/U2/U7/U8 and U3/U6 lines, especially the transfer stations at <i>Sendlinger Tor</i> (U1/U2/U7/U8 and U3/U6), <i>Central Station</i> (U1/U2/U7/U8 and U4/U5), and <i>Odeonsplatz</i> (U3/U6 and U4/U5), and will shorten the travel time between the <i>Central Station</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Allianz_Arena" title="Allianz Arena">Allianz Arena</a> by removing the need to transfer at <i>Marienplatz</i> or <i>Odeonsplatz</i>. MVG is predicting tremendous growth of passenger traffic in the northern section of Munich in the next twenty years and wanted to plan accordingly. The U9 line is projected to carry ten million passengers per year and to cost between 250 and 360 million euros, with an estimated completion date of 2025 if funding were approved. Three proposed booster lines — U10, U11, and U12 — would take advantage of new U9 bypass line; however, these booster lines are still under discussion.</p>
<p>The detailed report would have the line begin at <i>Martinsried</i> (the southwest extension to the current U6 line) and continue to <i>Implerstraße</i>, which would be expanded from three-track to four-track platforms. The existing service tunnel between <i>Implerstrasse</i> and <i>Theresienwiese</i> would be extended to two tracks, and at <i>Theresienwiese</i> the U9 would share platforms with the U4/U5 lines;. The station would be rebuilt from two-track to four-track platforms with wider platforms to reduce overcrowding and speed up passenger movement. From <i>Theresienwiese</i>, a new tunnel would spur toward its own platform underneath <i>Central Station</i>, continue to a new station <i>Pinakotheken</i>, then on to <i>Giselastraße</i> where it would rejoin the U3/U6 line in a station expanded from two-track to four-track platforms. The tunnel between there and <i>Münchner Freiheit</i> also would be built out from two tracks to four; and from <i>Münchner Freiheit</i> station, the U9 would continue to the current end of the U6, <i>Garching-Forschungszentrum</i>, while the U6 would terminate sooner at <i>Fröttmaning</i>.</p>
<p>Although Pinakotheken museum district has U2 station, <i>Königsplatz</i>, it is inconveniently located in the southwest of the neighbourhood. The U9 line would feature a <i>Pinakotheken</i> station, more to the centre of the district, which would serve museum visitors and the main campus of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Technische_Universit%C3%A4t_M%C3%BCnchen" title="Technische Universität München">Technische Universität München</a> better, as well as allow for connections to tram lines 27 and 28.</p>
<p>A variant to the four-track platform expansion at <i>Theresienwiese</i> is to build an additional station at <i>Bavariapark</i>; in both cases, the new line would improve capacity in the area during <a href="/wiki/Oktoberfest" title="Oktoberfest">Oktoberfest</a>. In a possible second phase, a spur tunnel could connect the U9 from north of <i>Central Station</i> with the U2/U8 at <i>Theresienstraße</i> station for the future booster U10 and U12 lines.</p>
<p>At <i>Central Station</i>, the new dedicated U9 platform would be placed underneath the current S-Bahn platforms but above U4/U5 platform and new second S-Bahn platforms, and underneath the heavy rail tracks. This could allow for a mezzanine level above the U9 platform to connect not only to other U- and S-Bahn lines, but to the western ends of the heavy rail platforms including those of the heavy rail annexes which flank the main platforms of the central station, meaning improved connections in particular of the annexes. There are serious engineering and design challenges in building a fifth line under the <i>Central Station</i> if quick transfers with the existing two S-Bahn lines and two U-Bahn lines are to be achieved. Possible cost increases from technical challenges at <i>Münchner Freiheit</i>, where the U9 was to have rejoined the U3/U6 line in an earlier proposal for the line, have been mitigated by having the line reconnect one station south at <i>Giselastraße</i> instead.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="More_Booster_Lines">More Booster Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Munich_U-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: More Booster Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl>
<dt><b>U10:</b> Harthof - Münchner Freiheit - Odeonsplatz - Sendlinger Tor - Harras</dt>
<dd>A third booster line, U10, would share U2 line between <i>Harthof</i> and <i>Scheidplatz</i> then switch to U3 line between <i>Scheidplatz</i> and <i>Implerstraße</i> before turning to U6 line at <i>Harras</i>. The northern and southern terminuses have not been determined yet.</dd>
<dt><b>U11:</b> Olympia-Einkaufszentrum - Westfriedhof - Central Station - Sendlinger Tor - Innsbrucker Ring</dt>
<dd>A fourth booster line, U11, would share U1 from <i>Olympia-Einkaufszentrum</i> to <i>Kolumbusplatz</i> before switching to U2 line for the continued journey to <i>Innsbrucker Ring</i> station. Which terminus station, <i>Messestadt Ost</i> in the east or <i>Neuperlach</i> in the south, has not been determined.</dd>
<dt><b>U12:</b> Harthof - Theresienstraße - Central Station - Theresienwiese - Implerstraße - Harras</dt>
<dd>A fifth booster line, U12, would share with U2 between <i>Harthof</i> and <i>Theresienstraße</i> then switch to U9 south of <i>Theresienstraße</i> where it shares with U9 toward <i>Harras</i>.</dd>
</dl>
