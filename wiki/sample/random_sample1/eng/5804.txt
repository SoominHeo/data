<p><b>Mtsensk</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Мценск</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oryol_Oblast" title="Oryol Oblast">Oryol Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the <a href="/wiki/Zusha_River" title="Zusha River">Zusha River</a> (<a href="/wiki/Oka_River" title="Oka River">Oka</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributary</a>) 49 kilometers (30 mi) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Oryol" title="Oryol">Oryol</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">43,222 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">47,807 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-7">[7]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">48,400 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-8">[8]</a></sup> 28,000 (1970).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mtsensk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was first mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Nikon_Chronicle" title="Nikon Chronicle">Nikon Chronicle</a> in 1146 as a part of the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Chernigov" title="Principality of Chernigov">Principality of Chernigov</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The name comes from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mtsena_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mtsena River (page does not exist)">Mtsena River</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributary</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Zusha_River" title="Zusha River">Zusha</a>, beside which the fortress stood. In 1238, Mtsensk was destroyed by <a href="/wiki/Batu_Khan" title="Batu Khan">Batu Khan</a>. Since 1320, it was under the rule of Lithuania, eventually becoming a part of the Russian state in 1505. Since the beginning of the 19th century, Mtsensk was rapidly developing as an industrial town.</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">Operation Barbarossa</a>, German armored forces captured the town in the fall of 1941. In particular, troops of the <a href="/wiki/3rd_Panzer_Division_(Wehrmacht)" title="3rd Panzer Division (Wehrmacht)">3rd Panzer Division</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4th_Panzer_Division" title="4th Panzer Division">4th Panzer Division</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Infantry_Regiment_Gro%C3%9Fdeutschland" title="Infantry Regiment Großdeutschland">Infantry Regiment Großdeutschland</a> saw combat in the immediate vicinity. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kursk" title="Battle of Kursk">Battle of Kursk</a> in 1943, Mtsensk served as the primary war zone.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mtsensk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Mtsensk serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mtsensky_District" title="Mtsensky District">Mtsensky District</a>, even though it is not a part of it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref126_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Ref126-1">[1]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is incorporated separately as the <b><a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">town of oblast significance</a> of Mtsensk</b>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_Oryol_Oblast" title="Administrative divisions of Oryol Oblast">districts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref126_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Ref126-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the town of oblast significance of Mtsensk is incorporated as <b>Mtsensk Urban Okrug</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref837_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ref837-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
