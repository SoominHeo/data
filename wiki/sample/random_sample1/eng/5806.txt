<p><b>Mtwara</b> (<a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i>Montewara</i>) is the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Mtwara_Region" title="Mtwara Region">Mtwara Region</a> in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>. In the 1940s, it was planned and constructed as the export facility for the disastrous <a href="/wiki/Tanganyika_groundnut_scheme" title="Tanganyika groundnut scheme">Tanganyika groundnut scheme</a>, but was somewhat neglected when the scheme was abandoned in 1951. The city is spread out over a large area planned to accommodate up to 200,000 people. The present population is around 80,000. As part of the development associated with the failed <a href="/wiki/Tanganyika_groundnut_scheme" title="Tanganyika groundnut scheme">Tanganyika groundnut scheme</a>, Mtwara features a <a href="/wiki/Mtwara_Port" title="Mtwara Port">deep-water port</a> that can accommodate ocean-going vessels, and a range of large municipal buildings, including a post office. Recent improvements in the port made it possible for big container ships to berth there.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mtwara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mtwara has reasonable transport links considering its remoteness in southern Tanzania. It is linked by paved roads with Dar es Salaam and Lindi to the north and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masasi" title="Masasi">Masasi</a> inland and by partially paved roads to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newala" title="Newala">Newala</a> in-land to the west. Beyond Masasi the road is newly paved for some 60 km towards Tunduru and the <a href="/wiki/Unity_Bridge" title="Unity Bridge">Unity Bridge</a> which provides a crossing point to <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>. The <a href="/wiki/A19_road_(Tanzania)" title="A19 road (Tanzania)">A19</a> links it with <a href="/wiki/Mbamba_Bay" title="Mbamba Bay">Mbamba Bay</a> on <a href="/wiki/Lake_Malawi" title="Lake Malawi">Lake Malawi</a> in the west. It features an airport with a paved runway that can accommodate medium size passenger jets. Precision Air runs a daily morning flight between Mtwara and <a href="/wiki/Dar_es_Salaam" title="Dar es Salaam">Dar es Salaam</a> and Air Tanzania offers a flight in the afternoon. The flight time is around an hour. Facilities for landing do not allow for planes to use the airport when it is hidden in mist. Mtwara serves as the access point for a small but growing tourism industry based in nearby <a href="/wiki/Mikindani" title="Mikindani">Mikindani</a>. New roads of a good standard were being built in 2013 within the city. Most of the roads have never been sealed. There is a choice of bus companies which provide daily services between Mtwara and Dar es Salaam. Also regular buses connect with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Masasi" title="Masasi">Masasi</a>, the biggest town inland from Mtwara.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Port_of_Mtwara">Port of Mtwara</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mtwara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Port of Mtwara">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mtwara_Port" title="Mtwara Port">Mtwara Port</a></div>
<p>The Port is the third largest ocean port in the country and was built as part of the failed <a href="/wiki/Tanganyika_groundnut_scheme" title="Tanganyika groundnut scheme">Tanganyika groundnut scheme</a>. The port was neglected for many years, however with the recent economic boom in the region, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_Tanzania" title="Government of Tanzania">government</a> has spent funds for the upgrade of the port. Recently the port has seen added activity due to the construction of the <a href="/wiki/Dangote_Cement" title="Dangote Cement">Dangote</a> cement factory and increased gas exploration activities. The port in the future is planned to facilitate exports such as Cashew nuts, Iron, Coal and Gas. The port also has an Export Processing zone to facilitate the manufacturing industry in the area.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mtwara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Iron_ore_and_coal">Iron ore and coal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mtwara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Iron ore and coal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mtwara might be the port for the export of iron ore<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and coal. There are plans to build a railway linking Mtwara with <a href="/wiki/Lindi" title="Lindi">Lindi</a> and mines at <a href="/wiki/Mchuchuma" title="Mchuchuma">Mchuchuma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liganga" title="Liganga">Liganga</a> via <a href="/wiki/Songea" title="Songea">Songea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-railwaygazette_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-railwaygazette-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2003, it was proposed to bring coal from the <a href="/wiki/Ludewa" title="Ludewa">Ludewa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Njombe" title="Njombe">Njombe</a> regions by rail to the port of Mtwara in Southern Tanzania as part of the <a href="/wiki/Mtwara_Development_Corridor" title="Mtwara Development Corridor">Mtwara Development Corridor</a> project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mtwara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mtwara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Due to close proximity to the equator and the warm <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, the city experiences tropical climatic conditions similar to all <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzanian</a> coastal cities. The city experiences hot and humid weather throughout much of the year and has a tropical wet and dry climate (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a>: Aw). Annual rainfall is approximately 1,024 mm (40 in), and in a normal year there are two rainy seasons: "the long rains" in April and May and "the short rains" in November and December.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
