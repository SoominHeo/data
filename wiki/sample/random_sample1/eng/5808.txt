<p><b>Luís Miguel Brito Garcia Monteiro</b>, <small><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Order_of_Infante_D._Henrique" title="Order of Infante D. Henrique">OIH</a></small> (born 4 January 1980), known as <b>Miguel</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[miˈɣɛɫ]</a></span>), is a retired Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played mainly as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">right back</a>.</p>
<p>He played the vast majority of his professional career with <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> (five seasons) and <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia</a> (seven), appearing in the vicinity of 200 official games with both clubs and winning a total of four major titles.</p>
<p>Miguel gained 59 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> for <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> during the 2000s, and represented the country in two <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cups</a> and as many <a href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Championship" title="UEFA European Championship">European Championships</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_Monteiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_.2F_Benfica">Early years / Benfica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_Monteiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years / Benfica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, Miguel started his career with local <a href="/wiki/C.F._Estrela_da_Amadora" title="C.F. Estrela da Amadora">C.F. Estrela da Amadora</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">winger</a>. On 30 April 1999, he made his first-team debut by playing 17 minutes in a 1–2 away loss against <a href="/wiki/Boavista_F.C." title="Boavista F.C.">Boavista FC</a>, then added 28 <a href="/wiki/Primeira_Liga" title="Primeira Liga">Primeira Liga</a> appearances in <a href="/wiki/1999%E2%80%932000_Primeira_Liga" title="1999–2000 Primeira Liga">his only full season</a> as the capital club retained its top division status.</p>
<p>Miguel moved to <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a> in the 2000 summer, making the transition to <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Wide_midfielder" title="Midfielder">right midfielder</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Full-back" title="Defender (association football)">right back</a> (by the hand of former Benfica player <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Chalana" title="Fernando Chalana">Fernando Chalana</a>, in his only game as a transition coach) – the position that ultimately brought him international recognition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FULLBACK_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FULLBACK-2">[2]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Primeira_Liga" title="2004–05 Primeira Liga">2004–05 campaign</a>, he contributed with 22 games and two goals as Benfica won the national championship after an 11-year wait.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Valencia">Valencia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_Monteiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Valencia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Miguel joined <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a> in August 2005, for a <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>7.5 million fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Fully established as first-choice, he signed a new five-year deal in September 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and helped the <i>Che</i> <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Copa_del_Rey" title="2007–08 Copa del Rey">win</a> the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> the following year, playing in <a href="/wiki/2008_Copa_del_Rey_Final" title="2008 Copa del Rey Final">the final</a> 3–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Getafe_CF" title="Getafe CF">Getafe CF</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_La_Liga" title="2009–10 La Liga">2009–10</a>, Miguel was challenged by new signing <a href="/wiki/Bruno_(footballer)" title="Bruno (footballer)">Bruno</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> but still appeared in 25 matches as Valencia finished third and <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2010–11 UEFA Champions League">returned</a> to the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>.</p>
<p>In May 2012, Miguel's contract expired and he was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2014, he started practising with the SJPF (syndicate of professional football players) alongside other unemployed players to regain fitness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_Monteiro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portuguese international</a> since making his debut against <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> on 12 February 2003,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FULLBACK_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FULLBACK-2">[2]</a></sup> Miguel first played for his country in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2004" title="UEFA Euro 2004">UEFA Euro 2004</a> which ended in a runner-up run at the hands of <a href="/wiki/Greece_national_football_team" title="Greece national football team">Greece</a>, being subsequently selected for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> squad. In <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">Euro 2008</a> he backed up <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Bosingwa" title="José Bosingwa">José Bosingwa</a>, and only appeared in the 0–2 group stage loss to hosts <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Picked for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> – in spite of an irregular season at Valencia – Miguel was one of three right backs used during Portugal's four matches in the competition, appearing in the group stage 7–0 win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korea_DPR_national_football_team" title="Korea DPR national football team">North Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 9 September 2010, the 30-year-old announced his retirement from international duty.</p>
