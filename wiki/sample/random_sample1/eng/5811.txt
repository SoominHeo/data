<p><b>Miguel Ángel Moyà Rumbo</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[miˈɣel ˈaŋxel moˈʝa]</a></span>; born 2 April 1984) is a Spanish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_%C3%81ngel_Moy%C3%A0&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mallorca">Mallorca</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_%C3%81ngel_Moy%C3%A0&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mallorca">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Binissalem" title="Binissalem">Binissalem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Majorca" title="Majorca">Majorca</a>, Moyà made his professional debut with hometown <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a> on 29 August 2004 in a 0–1 home loss against <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>, appearing in 32 <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> games for a team that <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_La_Liga" title="2004–05 La Liga">barely avoided relegation (17th)</a>. After backing up another youth system product, veteran <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Prats_Cervera" title="Antonio Prats Cervera">Toni Prats</a>, for two years, he became the undisputed starter.</p>
<p>From 2007 to 2009, however, Moyà struggled mightily with injuries, only appearing in thirteen matches for the <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a> side in <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_La_Liga" title="2008–09 La Liga">the latter season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Valencia">Valencia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_%C3%81ngel_Moy%C3%A0&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Valencia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late June 2009, Moyà agreed on a move to fellow top division club <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a> for about <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On 5 August, in a 0–2 preseason defeat at <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>, he featured more than 30 minutes as <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Central_midfielder" title="Midfielder">central midfielder</a> after all the replacements had been made by <a href="/wiki/Unai_Emery" title="Unai Emery">Unai Emery</a> and an injury occurred; the score was then at 0–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Moyà made his official debut against <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla FC</a> in <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_La_Liga" title="2009–10 La Liga">the league</a>'s opener, a 2–0 home win,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> but quickly lost his job to veteran <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_S%C3%A1nchez" title="César Sánchez">César Sánchez</a> after some costly mistakes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Getafe">Getafe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_%C3%81ngel_Moy%C3%A0&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Getafe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 June 2011, Moyà joined <a href="/wiki/Getafe_CF" title="Getafe CF">Getafe CF</a> on loan as part of the deal involving <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Parejo" title="Daniel Parejo">Daniel Parejo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> At the end of <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_La_Liga" title="2011–12 La Liga">the campaign</a>, in which he only missed two league games as the <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> outskirts team again retained their division status, he signed a permanent four-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Moyà suffered a knee injury in March 2014 during a game against <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">RCD Espanyol</a>, being ruled out for the remainder of <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_La_Liga" title="2013–14 La Liga">the season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atl.C3.A9tico_Madrid">Atlético Madrid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_%C3%81ngel_Moy%C3%A0&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Atlético Madrid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 4 June 2014, Moyà signed a three-year contract with league champions <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> for a reported fee of €3 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> During his presentation on the following day, he said: "What better moment than this one to arrive, the demands will be strong but when a football player is born, he wishes to be in a club such as this one. Atlético de Madrid is one of Europe's greatest teams and has clearly demonstrated it".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Moyà made his competitive debut in the first leg of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Supercopa_de_Espa%C3%B1a" title="2014 Supercopa de España">2014 Supercopa de España</a> on 19 August, a 1–1 draw at Real Madrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He remained a starter<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> until the final stretch of <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_La_Liga" title="2014–15 La Liga">the season</a> but, after suffering an injury to his left thigh in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> game against <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer 04 Leverkusen</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> he lost his position to <a href="/wiki/Jan_Oblak" title="Jan Oblak">Jan Oblak</a>, going on to be restricted to <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a> matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_%C3%81ngel_Moy%C3%A0&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_under-21_football_team" title="Spain national under-21 football team">Spanish under-21 international</a>, Moyà participated in the <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship_qualification" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification">qualification stage</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2007 UEFA European Championship</a>, in a final aggregate defeat to <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_under-21_football_team" title="Italy national under-21 football team">Italy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
