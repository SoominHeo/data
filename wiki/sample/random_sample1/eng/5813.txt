<p><b>Miguel Jontel Pimentel</b> (born October 23, 1985), better known as <b>Miguel</b>, is an American singer, songwriter and record producer. Raised in Los Angeles, he began creating music at age thirteen. After signing to <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> in 2007, Miguel released his debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/All_I_Want_Is_You_(album)" title="All I Want Is You (album)">All I Want Is You</a></i>, in November 2010. Although it was underpromoted and performed poorly upon its release, the album became a <a href="/wiki/Sleeper_hit" title="Sleeper hit">sleeper hit</a> and helped Miguel garner commercial standing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rytlewski_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rytlewski-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Graham_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Graham-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>After Jive's dissolution in 2011, he moved to <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> and released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Kaleidoscope_Dream" title="Kaleidoscope Dream">Kaleidoscope Dream</a></i> in 2012 to critical acclaim. On June 29, 2015, Miguel released his third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Wildheart_(album)" title="Wildheart (album)">Wildheart</a></i>, which also received universal critical acclaim. Miguel incorporates <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a>, <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic</a> styles into his music, and has been compared to vocalists <a href="/wiki/Babyface_(musician)" title="Babyface (musician)">Babyface</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Miguel was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro,_Los_Angeles" title="San Pedro, Los Angeles">San Pedro</a>, Los Angeles, California.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wood_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-11">[11]</a></sup> He is one of two sons born to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_American" title="Mexican American">Mexican American</a> father and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">African American</a> mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MN_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-MN-12">[12]</a></sup> Miguel's parents divorced when he was eight years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TBB_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-13">[13]</a></sup> At a young age, Miguel was introduced to the older <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues" title="Rhythm and blues">R&amp;B</a> his mother listened to,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MN_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-MN-12">[12]</a></sup> and his father's musical tastes, including <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Classic_rock" title="Classic rock">classic rock</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wood_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>At age five, Miguel wanted to become a dancer. However, at 13 he began pursuing a career in music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MN_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-MN-12">[12]</a></sup> At 14, he was writing songs and developing ideas with a <a href="/wiki/Multitrack_recording" title="Multitrack recording">four-track recorder</a> he took from his uncle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wood_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-11">[11]</a></sup> In ninth grade, Miguel was introduced by a friend to a member of production company Drop Squad, and he subsequently spent the rest of high school learning his way around a recording studio before seeking a record deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wood_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-11">[11]</a></sup> Miguel is also close high school friends with rapper <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu</a>, who also lived in San Pedro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000.E2.80.9306:_Career_beginnings">2000–06: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000–06: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Miguel signed a production deal with Drop Squad in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fader_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fader-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2004, he auditioned to be part of the group <i>Fatty Koo</i>, but their label, <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> chose someone else to complete the sextet. Later in 2004, Miguel was signed by <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent record label</a> Black Ice and started working on his studio album,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fader_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fader-15">[15]</a></sup> <i>Young &amp; Free</i>. It was scheduled to be released on November 30, 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> but was ultimately shelved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jeffries_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jeffries-6">[6]</a></sup> Black Ice released the single "Getcha Hands Up" with a video debut on <i><a href="/wiki/106_%26_Park" title="106 &amp; Park">106 &amp; Park</a></i>, but Miguel decided to change his sound and image and walked away from Black Ice Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-XXL_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-17">[17]</a></sup> He later said in an interview for <i><a href="/wiki/LA_Weekly" title="LA Weekly">LA Weekly</a></i>, "We shot a video, and if you ever see it, you will laugh your ass off. I have a fitted hat on and a white T-shirt and baggy jeans. I was 19 years old and it was the first time anyone had ever given me money."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wood_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007.E2.80.9311:_Signing_and_belated_debut">2007–11: Signing and belated debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2007–11: Signing and belated debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/All_I_Want_Is_You_(album)" title="All I Want Is You (album)">All I Want Is You (album)</a></div>
<p>For nearly a year, Miguel's manager at the time submitted several songs to music mogul Mark Pitts; after receiving a song titled "Sure Thing"—which Miguel described as a "highly personal ... record that no one was ever supposed to hear"—Pitts scheduled a meeting with Miguel in October 2007. The following month, Miguel signed a <a href="/wiki/Recording_contract" title="Recording contract">recording contract</a> with <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TBB_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-TBB-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-XXL_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-17">[17]</a></sup> Miguel recalled that the signing of his contract took place in a "very small room in a very small office in a small corner of the building at Jive".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Paper_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Paper-18">[18]</a></sup> After being signed, he recorded his debut album <i>All I Want Is You</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipshutz_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lipshutz-19">[19]</a></sup> but was sued by Black Ice for breach of contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stadium_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-stadium-20">[20]</a></sup> Consequently, his album was not released for two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipshutz_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lipshutz-19">[19]</a></sup> In 2007, Miguel also contributed vocals to three tracks on the debut collaborative project of <a href="/wiki/Blu_(rapper)" title="Blu (rapper)">Blu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Exile_(producer)" title="Exile (producer)">Exile</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Below_the_Heavens" title="Below the Heavens">Below the Heavens</a>,</i> appearing under the moniker "Miguel Jontel".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:26%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 90%; background-color: #FFFFF0;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div style="margin:0px 2em;">
<p>That album was a huge learning experience. I left the marketing of my album and me as an artist up to the discretion of the label. They marketed me like the typical R&amp;B artist, which I can't really blame them for, because that's what they know. But that's not what my lifestyle was.</p>
</div>
<div style="position: absolute; left: 0px; top: 0px; text-align: left; font-family:'Times New Roman',serif; font-weight:bold; font-size: large; color: gray;">“</div>
<div style="position: absolute; right: 0px; bottom: 0px; text-align: left; font-family:'Times New Roman',serif; font-weight:bold; font-size: large; color: gray;">”</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: center;">— Miguel, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipshutz_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-Lipshutz-19">[19]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>For the following three years, he contributed to Usher's <i><a href="/wiki/Here_I_Stand_(Usher_album)" title="Here I Stand (Usher album)">Here I Stand</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Raymond_v._Raymond" title="Raymond v. Raymond">Raymond v. Raymond</a></i>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Asher_Roth" title="Asher Roth">Asher Roth</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Asleep_in_the_Bread_Aisle" title="Asleep in the Bread Aisle">Asleep in the Bread Aisle</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Musiq_Soulchild" title="Musiq Soulchild">Musiq Soulchild</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IfULeave" title="IfULeave">IfULeave</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RO_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-RO-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AHH_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-AHH-23">[23]</a></sup> Miguel also released <i>Mischief: The Mixtape</i>; he explained that the title referred to his attempt to "[tell] people that there is an alternative to getting sort of the same thing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AHH_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-AHH-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>After his protracted contract dispute with Black Ice was settled,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wood_11-5"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-11">[11]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/All_I_Want_Is_You_(album)" title="All I Want Is You (album)">All I Want Is You</a></i> was released in November 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rytlewski_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rytlewski-8">[8]</a></sup> It initially performed poorly, debuting at number 109 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> on 11,000 copies sold,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipshutz_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-Lipshutz-19">[19]</a></sup> and was underpromoted by Jive,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rytlewski_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rytlewski-8">[8]</a></sup> amid the label's dissolution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McCarthy_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-McCarthy-24">[24]</a></sup> The album's title track attained radio airplay, while Miguel toured as a supporting act for <a href="/wiki/Trey_Songz" title="Trey Songz">Trey Songz</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Usher_(entertainer)" title="Usher (entertainer)">Usher</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipshutz_19-4"><a href="#cite_note-Lipshutz-19">[19]</a></sup> Its following two singles, "<a href="/wiki/Sure_Thing_(song)" title="Sure Thing (song)">Sure Thing</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Quickie_(song)" title="Quickie (song)">Quickie</a>", performed well on the charts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jeffries_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Jeffries-6">[6]</a></sup> After falling off the chart for three weeks,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ramirez_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ramirez-25">[25]</a></sup> <i>All I Want Is You</i> became a <a href="/wiki/Sleeper_hit" title="Sleeper hit">sleeper hit</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Graham_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Graham-9">[9]</a></sup> as it re-entered the <i>Billboard</i> 200 and climbed the chart for 22 weeks, before peaking at number 37 on May 14, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ramirez_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ramirez-25">[25]</a></sup> It ultimately spent 45 weeks on the chart,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and sold 404,000 copies in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipshutz_19-5"><a href="#cite_note-Lipshutz-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012.E2.80.9313:_Label_change.2C_second_album.2C_and_controversy">2012–13: Label change, second album, and controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2012–13: Label change, second album, and controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Kaleidoscope_Dream" title="Kaleidoscope Dream">Kaleidoscope Dream</a></div>

<p>After Jive was shut down and absorbed by <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>, Miguel was moved to RCA, where Pitts was named president of urban music, and received a new marketing team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipshutz_19-6"><a href="#cite_note-Lipshutz-19">[19]</a></sup> His second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Kaleidoscope_Dream" title="Kaleidoscope Dream">Kaleidoscope Dream</a></i>, was released on October 2, 2012, to critical acclaim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The album debuted at number three on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, selling 71,000 copies in its first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> As of February 2013, <i>Kaleidoscope Dream</i> has sold 322,000 copies, according to <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In February 2013, he released a politically driven video for the song "Candles in the Sun." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The album's <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> "Adorn", which had been released on August 7,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> became a sleeper hit on urban radio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kennedy_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kennedy-32">[32]</a></sup> It was Miguel's second number-one single on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a>, and his highest charting single on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> at number 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> By September, the single had sold 190,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipshutz_19-7"><a href="#cite_note-Lipshutz-19">[19]</a></sup> According to NPR's <a href="/wiki/Audie_Cornish" title="Audie Cornish">Audie Cornish</a>, Miguel "broke through to a national audience in 2012" with both "Adorn" and <i>Kaleidoscope Dream</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The second single "Do You..." was released on September 18,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> and reached number 32 on the Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> On March 3, 2013, "How Many Drinks?" was released as its third single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jonze_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jonze-37">[37]</a></sup> By February 20, <i>Kaleidoscope Dream</i> had charted for 20 weeks on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 and sold 321,400 copies, according to <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2013, the new single "<a href="/wiki/Beautiful_(Mariah_Carey_song)" title="Beautiful (Mariah Carey song)">#Beautiful</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> became Miguel’s third million-seller in the USA, following “Sure Thing” and “Adorn”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> During an energetic performance of "<a href="/wiki/Adorn_(song)" title="Adorn (song)">Adorn</a>" at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Billboard_Music_Awards" title="Billboard Music Awards">Billboard Music Awards</a> on May 19, Miguel failed in an attempt to leap across the stage, injuring two audience members in the process. Barely missing the stage, Miguel landed on two women, hooking his right leg over one and knocking the other into the stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The incident, known as the "Miguel Leg Drop," became an immediate internet sensation and has been satirized in numerous <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">internet memes</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gif" title="Gif">gifs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> It has also been the subject of controversy, with the show's producers claiming that they had denied the singer permission to attempt the leap, while Miguel's representatives have countered that no objections were raised during earlier rehearsals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Khyati Shah, one of the two women involved, has since claimed she suffered a traumatic head injury that was not sufficiently attended to by show organizers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.93present:_Wildheart">2014–present: <i>Wildheart</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014–present: Wildheart">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Miguel released his third studio album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Wildheart_(album)" title="Wildheart (album)">Wildheart</a></i> on June 30, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Album_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Album-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> In December 2014, Miguel released a 3-track EP entitled <i>NWA.HollywoodDreams.Coffee</i> in promotion for the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> The album was nominated for a Grammy in the category Best Urban Contemporary Album, also "<a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">Coffee</a>" was nominated for Best R&amp;B Song. In 2016, Miguel released the single "Waves" that's been remixed by RAC and Tame Impala.</p>
<p>Miguel played a Cuban-American gangster in <a href="/wiki/Ben_Affleck" title="Ben Affleck">Ben Affleck</a>'s period crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Live_by_Night_(film)" title="Live by Night (film)">Live by Night</a></i>, which was released on December 25, 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> He will cover <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_in_Love" title="Crazy in Love">Crazy in Love</a>" for <i><a href="/wiki/Fifty_Shades_Darker_(film)" title="Fifty Shades Darker (film)">Fifty Shades Darker</a></i> (2017).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of September 2016, he is an opening act for <a href="/wiki/Sia_Furler" title="Sia Furler">Sia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nostalgic_for_the_Present_Tour" title="Nostalgic for the Present Tour">Nostalgic for the Present Tour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> The tour runs until November 6th.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artistry">Artistry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Artistry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:25%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 90%; background-color: #FFFFF0;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div style="margin:0px 2em;">
<p>Right now R&amp;B is filled with so many people singing songs that sound like other songs. But Miguel is letting his art rule his whole flow, and that's the best place for an artist to be. To me he kind of feels like <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>, where he's doing this eclectic blend but still coming back to focused songs.</p>
</div>
<div style="position: absolute; left: 0px; top: 0px; text-align: left; font-family:'Times New Roman',serif; font-weight:bold; font-size: large; color: gray;">“</div>
<div style="position: absolute; right: 0px; bottom: 0px; text-align: left; font-family:'Times New Roman',serif; font-weight:bold; font-size: large; color: gray;">”</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: center;">— <a href="/wiki/Salaam_Remi" title="Salaam Remi">Salaam Remi</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/LA_Weekly" title="LA Weekly">LA Weekly</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wood_11-6"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-11">[11]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>Miguel identifies himself as part of the new wave of R&amp;B artists that include <a href="/wiki/Frank_Ocean" title="Frank Ocean">Frank Ocean</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Weeknd" title="The Weeknd">The Weeknd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elle_Varner" title="Elle Varner">Elle Varner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipshutz_19-8"><a href="#cite_note-Lipshutz-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/About.com" title="About.com">About.com</a> editor Mark Edward Nero characterizes his music as "eclectic, artsy R&amp;B-pop".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nero_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Nero-3">[3]</a></sup> Miguel cites musicians <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimi_Hendrix" title="Jimi Hendrix">Jimi Hendrix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phil_Collins" title="Phil Collins">Phil Collins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donny_Hathaway" title="Donny Hathaway">Donny Hathaway</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Notorious_B.I.G." title="The Notorious B.I.G.">The Notorious B.I.G.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kanye_West" title="Kanye West">Kanye West</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saul_Williams" title="Saul Williams">Saul Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Usher_(singer)" title="Usher (singer)">Usher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a> as influences <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTV_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTV-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LAT_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-LAT-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> in addition to expressing his admiration for <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">John Lennon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diane_Warren" title="Diane Warren">Diane Warren</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AHH_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-AHH-23">[23]</a></sup> Miguel revealed his desire to have worked with <a href="/wiki/James_Brown" title="James Brown">James Brown</a>, whom he considers the "last innovator for me when it comes to soul".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Paper_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Paper-18">[18]</a></sup> In addition to singing, Miguel also plays the <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Paper_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-Paper-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Miguel incorporates R&amp;B, <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic</a> styles into his music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-XXL_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-XXL-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RO_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-RO-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LAT_54-1"><a href="#cite_note-LAT-54">[54]</a></sup> Miguel described his sound as "nostalgic in a sense that it's familiar ... it's shocking, edgy, energizing".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RO_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-RO-22">[22]</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Paper_(magazine)" title="Paper (magazine)">Paper</a></i> magazine, he regarded his music as "fly, <a href="/wiki/Funkadelic" title="Funkadelic">funkadelic</a>, intergalactic-hip-hop-meets-sexy-orgasmic crazy, dope shit". Miguel is often compared to <a href="/wiki/Babyface_(musician)" title="Babyface (musician)">Babyface</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Paper_18-3"><a href="#cite_note-Paper-18">[18]</a></sup> His vocals are influenced by classic rock bands such as <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Police" title="The Police">The Police</a> and <a href="/wiki/Def_Leppard" title="Def Leppard">Def Leppard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> Brian McManus of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_Voice" title="The Village Voice">The Village Voice</a></i> writes that "<a href="/wiki/Marvin_Gaye" title="Marvin Gaye">[Marvin] Gaye</a>, Prince, and (his comparison) <a href="/wiki/Van_Morrison" title="Van Morrison">Van Morrison</a> all linger in his voice."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miguel_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Miguel has said that <a href="/wiki/Transcendental_Meditation_technique" title="Transcendental Meditation technique">Transcendental Meditation</a> is the key to balancing his lifestyle: "It’s good to take a breath from everything and just center yourself. That is the best way of describing it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hot93.7_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-hot93.7-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>Miguel has been in a relationship with model Nazanin Mandi since 2005. In January 2016, it was announced that they are engaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
