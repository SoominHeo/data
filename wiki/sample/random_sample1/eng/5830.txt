<p>The <b>United States Army Infantry School</b> is located at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Benning" title="Fort Benning">Fort Benning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, is a school dedicated to training infantrymen for service in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Army_Infantry_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is made up of the following components:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/192nd_Infantry_Brigade_(United_States)" title="192nd Infantry Brigade (United States)">192nd Infantry Brigade</a> (Unit was inactivated on 19 June 2013. Subordinate elements transferred to the <a href="/wiki/194th_Armored_Brigade_(United_States)" title="194th Armored Brigade (United States)">194th Armored Brigade</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Armor_School" title="United States Army Armor School">Armor School</a> )<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-192nd_discontinued_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-192nd_discontinued-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/197th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_States)" title="197th Infantry Brigade (United States)">197th Infantry Brigade</a> (Reflagged from <a href="/wiki/29th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="29th Infantry Regiment (United States)">29th Infantry Regiment</a>)
<ul>
<li>1st Battalion (Mechanized/Stryker), 29th Infantry Regiment
<ul>
<li>US Army Infantry Experimental Forces Company</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M2/M3_Bradley_Fighting_Vehicle" title="M2/M3 Bradley Fighting Vehicle">Bradley</a> Master Gunner Course</li>
<li>Mechanized Leader Course</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stryker" title="Stryker">Stryker</a> Leader &amp; Transition Courses</li>
<li>Stryker/Bradley Proponent Office</li>
<li>Mechanized New Equipment Training Company</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>2nd Battalion, 29th Infantry Regiment
<ul>
<li>Small Arms Ranges, Experimentation and Support</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Sniper_School" title="United States Army Sniper School">Sniper School</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Combatives_School" title="United States Army Combatives School">Combatives School</a></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>CONUS Replacement Center (CRC)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/198th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_States)" title="198th Infantry Brigade (United States)">198th Infantry Brigade</a> (Reflagged from Infantry Training Brigade (ITB))
<ul>
<li>1st Battalion, <a href="/wiki/19th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="19th Infantry Regiment (United States)">19th Infantry</a></li>
<li>2nd Battalion, 19th Infantry</li>
<li>1st Battalion, <a href="/wiki/50th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="50th Infantry Regiment (United States)">50th Infantry</a></li>
<li>2nd Battalion, <a href="/wiki/58th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="58th Infantry Regiment (United States)">58th Infantry</a></li>
<li>2nd Battalion, <a href="/wiki/54th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="54th Infantry Regiment (United States)">54th Infantry</a></li>
<li>2nd Battalion, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=330th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="330th Infantry Regiment (United States) (page does not exist)">330th Infantry</a> (USAR)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-198th_welcomes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-198th_welcomes-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-198th_BDE_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-198th_BDE-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>For new recruits specializing in <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">infantry</a>, the ITB conducts fourteen weeks<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OSUT_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-OSUT-4">[4]</a></sup> of One Station Unit Training (OSUT) consisting of both <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Basic_Training" title="United States Army Basic Training">Basic Combat Training</a> (BCT) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Advanced_Individual_Training" title="Advanced Individual Training">Advanced Individual Training</a> (AIT). The mission of the Infantry Training Brigade is to transform civilians into disciplined infantrymen that possess the Army Values, fundamental soldier skills, physical fitness, character, confidence, commitment, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Soldier%27s_Creed" title="U.S. Soldier's Creed">Warrior Ethos</a> to become adaptive and flexible infantrymen ready to accomplish the mission of the infantry.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/199th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_States)" title="199th Infantry Brigade (United States)">199th Infantry Brigade</a> (Reflagged from <a href="/wiki/11th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="11th Infantry Regiment (United States)">11th Infantry Regiment</a>)
<ul>
<li>HHC, 199th Bde
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Maneuver_Captains_Career_Course" title="Maneuver Captains Career Course">Maneuver Captains Career Course</a></li>
<li>International Student Training Detachment</li>
<li>Officer Candidate School</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>1st Battalion, 11th Infantry (Basic Officer Leader Course II (BOLC II)) (inactivated 23 March 2010)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOLC_II_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-BOLC_II-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>2nd Battalion, 11th Infantry (Infantry <a href="/wiki/Basic_Officer_Leaders_Course" title="Basic Officer Leaders Course">Basic Officer Leader Course</a> (IBOLC))</li>
<li>3rd Battalion, 11th Infantry (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Officer_Candidate_School_(U.S._Army)" title="Officer Candidate School (U.S. Army)">Officer Candidate School</a>) (OCS)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2nd_Squadron,_16th_Cavalry_Regiment" title="2nd Squadron, 16th Cavalry Regiment">2nd Squadron, 16th Cavalry Regiment</a> (Armor Basic Officer Leaders Course (ABOLC))</li>
<li>3rd Battalion, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/81st_Armor_Regiment_(United_States)" title="81st Armor Regiment (United States)">81st Armor Regiment</a> (MCoE Provost)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-199th_BDE_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-199th_BDE-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Henry Caro Non-Commissioned Officers Academy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCOA_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-NCOA-7">[7]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li>Maneuver Senior Leaders Course (M-SLC), formerly Advanced Noncommissioned Officer Course (ANCOC)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-M-SLC_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-M-SLC-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>Advanced Leaders Course (ALC), formerly Basic Noncommissioned Officer Course (BNCOC)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ALC_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ALC-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>Warrior Leader Course (WLC), formerly Primary Leadership Development Course (PLDC)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WLC_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-WLC-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ranger_School" title="Ranger School">Airborne and Ranger Training Brigade</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARTB_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ARTB-11">[11]</a></sup>
<ul>
<li>4th Ranger Training Battalion (Camps Rogers and Darby)</li>
<li>5th Ranger Training Battalion (Camp Frank D. Merrill)</li>
<li>6th Ranger Training Battalion (<a href="/wiki/Camp_Rudder" title="Camp Rudder">Camp Rudder</a>, Auxiliary Field 6, <a href="/wiki/Eglin_Air_Force_Base" title="Eglin Air Force Base">Eglin Air Force Base</a>, Florida)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Reconnaissance_and_Surveillance_Leaders_Course" title="United States Army Reconnaissance and Surveillance Leaders Course">Reconnaissance and Surveillance Leaders Course (RSLC)</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Airborne_School" title="United States Army Airborne School">1st Battalion</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/507th_Infantry_Regiment_(United_States)" title="507th Infantry Regiment (United States)">507th Parachute Infantry Regiment</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Airborne_School" title="United States Army Airborne School">Basic Airborne School</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Jumpmaster_School" title="United States Army Jumpmaster School">Jumpmaster School</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Pathfinder_School" title="United States Army Pathfinder School">Pathfinder School</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Army_Command_Exhibition_Parachute_Team_-_Silver_Wings" title="U.S. Army Command Exhibition Parachute Team - Silver Wings">Silver Wings</a> (MCoE Command Exhibition Parachute Team)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Combined Arms and Tactics Directorate (CATD)</li>
<li>Directorate of Operations and Training/G-3
<ul>
<li>Training Support Center</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Office of Infantry Proponency (OIP) "Warrior Ethos" program that was launched in 2003 by the United States Army.</li>
</ul>
<p>Infantry officers who have completed commissioning and the Basic Officer Leadership Course then attend the Infantry Officer Basic Leadership Course in 2nd battalion. This is a course of instruction, as the name implies, in basic infantry skills, including marksmanship, machine gunnery, tactics, and planning.</p>
<p>The brigade also conducts specialized training for soldiers in Basic Airborne, Pathfinder, and Jumpmaster Courses.</p>
