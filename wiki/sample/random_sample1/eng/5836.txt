<p>The <b>United States Pacific Fleet</b> (<b>USPACFLT</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> theater-level component command of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> that provides naval forces to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Pacific_Command" title="United States Pacific Command">United States Pacific Command</a>. Fleet headquarters is at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pearl_Harbor_Naval_Station" title="Pearl Harbor Naval Station">Pearl Harbor Naval Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>, with large secondary facilities at <a href="/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_North_Island" title="Naval Air Station North Island">North Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Bay" title="San Diego Bay">San Diego Bay</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Contiguous_United_States" title="Contiguous United States">Mainland</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Pacific_Fleet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A Pacific Fleet was created in 1907 when the <a href="/wiki/Asiatic_Squadron" title="Asiatic Squadron">Asiatic Squadron</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Squadron" title="Pacific Squadron">Pacific Squadron</a> were combined. In 1910, the ships of the First Squadron were organized back into a separate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asiatic_Fleet" title="Asiatic Fleet">Asiatic Fleet</a>. The General Order 94 of 6 December 1922 organized the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Fleet" title="United States Fleet">United States Fleet</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Battle_Fleet" title="Battle Fleet">Battle Fleet</a> as the Pacific presence. Until May 1940, the Battle Fleet was stationed on the west coast of the United States (primarily at San Diego). During the summer of that year, as part of the U.S. response to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_expansionism" title="Japanese expansionism">Japanese expansionism</a>, it was instructed to take an "advanced" position at <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Harbor" title="Pearl Harbor">Pearl Harbor</a>, Hawaii. Long term basing at Pearl Harbor was so strongly opposed by the commander, Admiral <a href="/wiki/James_O._Richardson" title="James O. Richardson">James O. Richardson</a>, that he personally protested in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington</a>. Political considerations were thought sufficiently important that he was relieved by Admiral <a href="/wiki/Husband_E._Kimmel" title="Husband E. Kimmel">Husband E. Kimmel</a>, who was in command at the time of <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">the attack on Pearl Harbor</a>.</p>
<p>The Pacific Fleet was formally recreated on 1 February 1941. On that day General Order 143 split the United States Fleet into separate Atlantic, Pacific, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asiatic_Fleet" title="Asiatic Fleet">Asiatic Fleets</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition_of_the_Pacific_Fleet_in_December_1941">Composition of the Pacific Fleet in December 1941</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Pacific_Fleet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition of the Pacific Fleet in December 1941">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 7 December, the Fleet consisted of the Battle Force, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scouting_Force" title="Scouting Force">Scouting Force</a>, Base Force, Amphibious Force (<a href="/wiki/ComPhibPac" title="ComPhibPac">ComPhibPac</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Cruiser Force (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=COMCRUPAC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="COMCRUPAC (page does not exist)">COMCRUPAC</a>), Destroyer Force (<a href="/wiki/COMDESPAC" title="COMDESPAC">COMDESPAC</a>), and the Submarine Force (<a href="/wiki/COMSUBPAC" title="COMSUBPAC">COMSUBPAC</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Also in Hawaii was the <a href="/wiki/United_States_naval_districts" title="United States naval districts">Fourteenth Naval District</a>, commanded by Rear Admiral <a href="/wiki/Claude_C._Bloch" title="Claude C. Bloch">Claude C. Bloch</a>.</p>

<p>These nine battleships were intended to counterbalance the ten battleships of the Imperial Japanese Navy. At the time of the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">attack on Pearl Harbor</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Pennsylvania_(BB-38)" title="USS Pennsylvania (BB-38)"><i>Pennsylvania</i></a> was in dry dock and <a href="/wiki/USS_Colorado_(BB-45)" title="USS Colorado (BB-45)"><i>Colorado</i></a> was being refitted at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bremerton_Navy_Yard" title="Bremerton Navy Yard">Bremerton Navy Yard</a>, Washington State. <a href="/wiki/USS_Arizona_(BB-39)" title="USS Arizona (BB-39)"><i>Arizona</i></a> was mated with <a href="/wiki/USS_Nevada_(BB-36)" title="USS Nevada (BB-36)"><i>Nevada</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/USS_Oklahoma_(BB-37)" title="USS Oklahoma (BB-37)"><i>Oklahoma</i></a> at that time.</p>
<p>Other components of the Battle Force included <a href="/wiki/Commander,_Naval_Air_Forces#History" title="Commander, Naval Air Forces">Aircraft, Battle Force</a>, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carrier_Strike_Group_One" title="Carrier Strike Group One">Carrier Division One</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carrier_Strike_Group_Two" title="Carrier Strike Group Two">Carrier Division Two</a>, plus Cruiser Divisions 4, 5, and 6, as well as Destroyers, Battle Force.:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<p>When the attack took place, all three carriers were absent - <i>Saratoga</i> was in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a> collecting her air group following a major refit, <i>Enterprise</i> was en route back to Hawaii following a mission to deliver aircraft to <a href="/wiki/Wake_Island" title="Wake Island">Wake Island</a>, while <i>Lexington</i> had just departed on a similar mission to <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Midway" title="Midway">Midway</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Scouting_Fleet" title="Scouting Fleet">Scouting Force</a> included Cruiser Division Three, Cruiser Division Nine and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ComSubPac" title="ComSubPac">Submarines, Scouting Force</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Amphibious Force was formally known as Commander, Amphibious Forces, Pacific Fleet (<a href="/wiki/ComPhibPac" title="ComPhibPac">ComPhibPac</a>). On 7 December 1941 the Amphibious Force comprised the Army's <a href="/wiki/3rd_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="3rd Infantry Division (United States)">3rd Infantry Division</a> at Fort Lewis, under Army operational control, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2nd_Marine_Division" title="2nd Marine Division">2nd Marine Division</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Marine_Aircraft_Wing" title="2nd Marine Aircraft Wing">2nd Marine Aircraft Wing</a>, the 2nd Defense Battalion (see <a href="/wiki/Marine_defense_battalions" title="Marine defense battalions">Marine defense battalions</a>), and a depot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> One of PhibPac's subordinate commands during World War II was Transports, Amphibious Force, Pacific Fleet, or TransPhibPac. The commander of TransPhibPac was known as ComTransPhibPac.</p>
<p>In December 1941, the fleet consisted of nine <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleships</a>, three <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carriers</a>, 12 <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">heavy cruisers</a>, eight <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">light cruisers</a>, 50 <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyers</a>, 33 <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarines</a>, and 100 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patrol_bomber" title="Patrol bomber">patrol bombers</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This was approximately the fleet's strength at the time of the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japanese</a> attack on Pearl Harbor. That day, the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Combined_Fleet" title="Combined Fleet">Combined Fleet</a> carried out the attack on Pearl Harbor, drawing the United States into <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> in the Pacific. The Pacific Fleet's <a href="/wiki/Line_of_battle" title="Line of battle">Battle Line</a> took the brunt of the attack, with two battleships destroyed, two salvageable but requiring lengthy reconstruction, and four more lightly to moderately damaged, forcing the U.S. Navy to rely primarily on aircraft carriers and submarines for many months afterward.</p>
<p>Subsequently Pacific Fleet engagements during World War II included the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Guam_(1941)" title="Battle of Guam (1941)">Battle of Guam</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marshalls-Gilberts_raids" title="Marshalls-Gilberts raids">Marshalls-Gilberts raids</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Doolittle_Raid" title="Doolittle Raid">Doolittle Raid</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands_campaign" title="Solomon Islands campaign">Solomon Islands campaign</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Coral_Sea" title="Battle of the Coral Sea">Battle of the Coral Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Midway" title="Battle of Midway">Battle of Midway</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Eastern_Solomons" title="Battle of the Eastern Solomons">Battle of the Eastern Solomons</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Santa_Cruz_Islands" title="Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands">Battle of the Santa Cruz Islands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Philippine_Sea" title="Battle of the Philippine Sea">Battle of the Philippine Sea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_and_Marshall_Islands_campaign" title="Gilbert and Marshall Islands campaign">Gilbert and Marshall Islands campaign</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Leyte_Gulf" title="Battle of Leyte Gulf">Battle of Leyte Gulf</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Okinawa" title="Battle of Okinawa">Battle of Okinawa</a>. More minor battles included the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dutch_Harbor" title="Battle of Dutch Harbor">Battle of Dutch Harbor</a>. The Submarine Force began a sustained campaign of <a href="/wiki/Commerce_raiding" title="Commerce raiding">commerce raiding</a> against Japan's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merchant_marine" title="Merchant marine">merchant marine</a>, beginning the very first day of the war, which ultimately claimed 1,314 ships totalling about 5.3 million tons (by the imperfect postwar reckoning of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joint_Army-Navy_Assessment_Committee" title="Joint Army-Navy Assessment Committee">Joint Army-Navy Assessment Committee</a>, JANAC).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/West_Loch_disaster" title="West Loch disaster">West Loch disaster</a> occurred at Pearl Harbor on 21 May 1944.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_1945">Post 1945</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Pacific_Fleet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post 1945">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Pacific Fleet took part in <a href="/wiki/Operation_Magic_Carpet" title="Operation Magic Carpet">Operation Magic Carpet</a>, the return of U.S. servicemen, after the end of the Second World War. The organization of the Pacific Fleet in January 1947 is shown in Hal M. Friedman's <i>Arguing over the American Lake: Bureaucracy and Rivalry in the U.S. Pacific, 1945-1947.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 1950 the Pacific Fleet has been involved in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>, the two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taiwan_Straits_Crises" title="Taiwan Straits Crises">Taiwan Straits Crises</a>, and a number of other operations including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayaguez_Incident" title="Mayaguez Incident">Mayaguez Incident</a> of 1975, as well as post-Vietnam related operations such as <a href="/wiki/Operation_New_Arrivals" title="Operation New Arrivals">Operation New Arrivals</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIMPAC" title="RIMPAC">RIMPAC</a> exercise series began in 1971.</p>
<p>On 7 March 1984, the Secretaries of Transportation and Navy signed a Memorandum of Agreement which created the Maritime Defense Zones (MDZ).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The Pacific MDZ is an echelon three Navy command under the Commander U.S. Pacific Fleet. The Pacific MDZ has responsibility for coastal defense up to 200 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nautical_miles" title="Nautical miles">nautical miles</a> around the U.S. West Coast, Alaska, Aleutian Islands, and Hawaii during times of hostility. On 1 October 1990, Commander <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=U.S._Naval_Forces_Alaska&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="U.S. Naval Forces Alaska (page does not exist)">U.S. Naval Forces Alaska</a> (COMUSNAVAK) was established as the Naval Component Commander to Commander, <a href="/wiki/Alaskan_Command" title="Alaskan Command">Alaskan Command</a> (COMALCOM). Since its inception, COMUSNAVAK has grown to become responsible for coordinating all Navy activity in the Alaska and Aleutian area, for detailed planning and coordination for the Naval portion of the Joint and Combined <a href="/wiki/Exercise_Northern_Edge" title="Exercise Northern Edge">Exercise Northern Edge</a>, and coordinates high-visibility U.S. Navy ship visits throughout Alaska in support of public relations and recruiting initiatives.</p>
<p>The very large PACEX '89 in the North Pacific involved the USN, Canadian Navy, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Maritime_Self-Defence_Force" title="Japanese Maritime Self-Defence Force">Japanese Maritime Self-Defence Force</a>, and ROK Navy. At the end of Exercise PACEX '89 a 54 ship formation was assembled for photos. It included the flagship, <a href="/wiki/USS_Blue_Ridge_(LCC-19)" title="USS Blue Ridge (LCC-19)">USS <i>Blue Ridge</i> (LCC-19)</a>, the <a href="/wiki/USS_Enterprise_(CVN-65)" title="USS Enterprise (CVN-65)">USS <i>Enterprise</i> (CVN-65)</a> Battle Group, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USS_Carl_Vinson_(CVN-70)" title="USS Carl Vinson (CVN-70)">USS <i>Carl Vinson</i> (CVN-70)</a> Battle Group, two battleship surface action groups formed around the <a href="/wiki/USS_New_Jersey_(BB-62)" title="USS New Jersey (BB-62)">USS <i>New Jersey</i> (BB-62)</a> and <a href="/wiki/USS_Missouri_(BB-63)" title="USS Missouri (BB-63)">USS <i>Missouri</i> (BB-63)</a>, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Maritime_Self-Defence_Force" title="Japanese Maritime Self-Defence Force">Japanese Maritime Self-Defence Force</a> task force.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Other operations undertaken since include participation in the Alaskan Oil Spill Joint Task Force, including participation of Commander, Amphibious Group Three, as deputy CJTF. This was the defence response to the <a href="/wiki/Exxon_Valdez_oil_spill" title="Exxon Valdez oil spill">Exxon Valdez oil spill</a> of March 1989. Also, the Pacific Fleet was involved in Joint Task Force Philippines during the December 1989 coup attempt there, which involved two carrier battle groups, USS <i>Midway</i> and USS <i>Enterprise</i>-with their associated air wings operating in the Philippine Sea, chopped to JTF Philippines. During the operations, the carriers maintained deck alerts and 24-hour coverage of Manila with E-2C aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Around September 10, 1990,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/USS_Princeton_(CG-59)" title="USS Princeton (CG-59)">USS <i>Princeton</i> (CG-59)</a> and the <a href="/wiki/USS_Reuben_James_(FFG-57)" title="USS Reuben James (FFG-57)">USS <i>Reuben James</i> (FFG-57)</a> visited <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a>. This marked the first <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> visit to the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>'s Pacific port of Vladivostok since before World War II. Before the visit was completed, the crew received word that their Pacific cruise was canceled. They returned to Long Beach and joined the <a href="/wiki/USS_Ranger_(CV-61)" title="USS Ranger (CV-61)">USS <i>Ranger</i></a> Battle Group preparing to deploy to the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>.</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/Operation_Fiery_Vigil" title="Operation Fiery Vigil">Operation Fiery Vigil</a> in June 1991, the following vessels participated in the sealift phase of the evacuation: the <i>Lincoln</i> battle group (COMCARGRU 3 embarked): <a href="/wiki/USS_Abraham_Lincoln_(CVN-72)" title="USS Abraham Lincoln (CVN-72)">USS <i>Abraham Lincoln</i> (CVN-72)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Long_Beach_(CGN-9)" title="USS Long Beach (CGN-9)">USS <i>Long Beach</i> (CGN-9)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Lake_Champlain_(CG-57)" title="USS Lake Champlain (CG-57)">USS <i>Lake Champlain</i> (CG-57)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Merrill_(DD-976)" title="USS Merrill (DD-976)">USS <i>Merrill</i> (DD-976)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Gary_(FFG-51)" title="USS Gary (FFG-51)">USS <i>Gary</i> (FFG-51)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Ingraham_(FFG-61)" title="USS Ingraham (FFG-61)">USS <i>Ingraham</i> (FFG-61)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Roanoke_(AOR-7)" title="USS Roanoke (AOR-7)">USS <i>Roanoke</i> (AOR-7)</a>, Amphibious Ready Group Alpha (COMPHIBRON 3 embarked): <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USS_Peleliu_(LHA-5)" title="USS Peleliu (LHA-5)">USS <i>Peleliu</i> (LHA-5)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Cleveland_(LPD-7)" title="USS Cleveland (LPD-7)">USS <i>Cleveland</i> (LPD-7)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Comstock_(LSD-45)" title="USS Comstock (LSD-45)">USS <i>Comstock</i> (LSD-45)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Bristol_County_(LST-1198)" title="USS Bristol County (LST-1198)">USS <i>Bristol County</i> (LST-1198)</a>, and a large number of other vessels: <a href="/wiki/USS_Midway_(CV-41)" title="USS Midway (CV-41)">USS <i>Midway</i> (CV-41)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Curts_(FFG-38)" title="USS Curts (FFG-38)">USS <i>Curts</i> (FFG-38)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Rodney_M._Davis_(FFG-60)" title="USS Rodney M. Davis (FFG-60)">USS <i>Rodney M. Davis</i> (FFG-60)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Thach_(FFG-43)" title="USS Thach (FFG-43)">USS <i>Thach</i> (FFG-43)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Arkansas_(CGN-41)" title="USS Arkansas (CGN-41)">USS <i>Arkansas</i> (CGN-41)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_McClusky_(FFG-41)" title="USS McClusky (FFG-41)">USS <i>McClusky</i> (FFG-41)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_St._Louis_(LKA-116)" title="USS St. Louis (LKA-116)">USS <i>St. Louis</i> (LKA-116)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_San_Bernardino_(LST-1189)" title="USS San Bernardino (LST-1189)">USS <i>San Bernardino</i> (LST-1189)</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=MV_1st_Lt_Lummus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="MV 1st Lt Lummus (page does not exist)">MV <i>1st Lt Lummus</i></a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=MV_American_Condor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="MV American Condor (page does not exist)">MV <i>American Condor</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Niagara_Falls_(AFS-3)" title="USS Niagara Falls (AFS-3)">USS <i>Niagara Falls</i> (AFS-3)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USNS_Ponchatoula_(T-AO-148)" title="USNS Ponchatoula (T-AO-148)">USNS <i>Ponchatoula</i> (T-AO-148)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Passumpsic_(AO-107)" title="USS Passumpsic (AO-107)">USNS <i>Passumpsic</i> (T-AO-107)</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=USNS_Hassayampa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="USNS Hassayampa (page does not exist)">USNS <i>Hassayampa</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Haleakala_(AE-25)" title="USS Haleakala (AE-25)">USS <i>Haleakala</i> (AE-25)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USNS_Spica_(T-AFS-9)" title="USNS Spica (T-AFS-9)">USNS <i>Spica</i> (T-AFS-9)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Cape_Cod_(AD-43)" title="USS Cape Cod (AD-43)">USS <i>Cape Cod</i> (AD-43)</a>. (CNA, 1994, 113) Further operations included JTF Marianas (August–September 1992) and JTF Hawaii (September–October 1992).</p>
<p>Other contingency operation after 1991 included <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Operation_Sea_Angel" title="Operation Sea Angel">Operation Sea Angel</a> (Bangladesh relief) (led by Commander <a href="/wiki/III_Marine_Expeditionary_Force" title="III Marine Expeditionary Force">III Marine Expeditionary Force</a>), <a href="/wiki/Operation_Eastern_Exit" title="Operation Eastern Exit">Operation Eastern Exit</a>, and involvement in the <a href="/wiki/Somali_Civil_War" title="Somali Civil War">Somali Civil War</a> - 'Restore Hope'. During 'Restore Hope,' Navy command arrangements underwent a number of changes during the operation. At the start, the principal naval forces were the <i>Ranger</i> battle group (with Commander, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carrier_Strike_Group_One" title="Carrier Strike Group One">Carrier Group One</a> embarked on <a href="/wiki/USS_Ranger_(CV-61)" title="USS Ranger (CV-61)">USS <i>Ranger</i> (CV-61)</a> as Commander, Naval Forces), the <i>Kitty Hawk</i> battle group, an amphibious task unit including <a href="/wiki/USS_Tripoli_(LPH-10)" title="USS Tripoli (LPH-10)">USS <i>Tripoli</i> (LPH-10)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Juneau_(LPD-10)" title="USS Juneau (LPD-10)">USS <i>Juneau</i> (LPD-10)</a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Rushmore_(LSD-47)" title="USS Rushmore (LSD-47)">USS <i>Rushmore</i> (LSD-47)</a>, and MV <i>Lummus</i>, and three ships from MPSRON TWO (MV <i>Anderson</i>, MV <i>Bonnyman</i>, and MV <i>Phillips</i>). Other events led to the departure of the carriers and, as a result, Commander, Naval Forces responsibilities devolved first to Commander, Carrier Group Three, on <i>Kitty Hawk</i>, and thence to Commander, Amphibious Group Three. Finally Commander, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Amphibious_Squadron_3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Amphibious Squadron 3 (page does not exist)">Amphibious Squadron 3</a> became COMNAVFOR on 15 January with the departure of COMPHIGRU THREE after the completion of the MPF offload. (CNA, 1994, 168)</p>
<p>In 1995 Pacific Fleet surface ships were reshuffled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Effective Oct. 1, 1995 the U.S. Pacific Fleet's surface ships were to be reorganized into six core battle groups and eight destroyer squadrons. Permanent core battle groups were to include a battle group commander, aircraft carrier, carrier air wing and at least two cruisers.</p>

<p>In 1996 two carrier battle groups were sent to the Taiwan area during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Taiwan_Straits_Crisis" title="Third Taiwan Straits Crisis">Third Taiwan Straits Crisis</a>. Later ships of the Pacific Fleet, notably the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ticonderoga_class_cruiser" title="Ticonderoga class cruiser"><i>Ticonderoga</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruiser</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USS_Mobile_Bay_(CG-53)" title="USS Mobile Bay (CG-53)">USS <i>Mobile Bay</i> (CG-53)</a> provided support to the entry of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/INTERFET" title="INTERFET">INTERFET</a> in <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">East Timor</a> in 1999.</p>
<p>Between 25–27 March 2006, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carrier_Strike_Group_Nine" title="Carrier Strike Group Nine">Carrier Strike Group Nine</a> participated in a series of anti-submarine warfare exercises (ASW) in Hawaiian waters while en route to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Seventh_Fleet" title="U.S. Seventh Fleet">U.S. Seventh Fleet</a>'s area of responsibility. In addition to the strike group, the exercise also included the nuclear-powered attack submarines <a href="/wiki/USS_Seawolf_(SSN-21)" title="USS Seawolf (SSN-21)"><i>Seawolf</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Cheyenne_(SSN-773)" title="USS Cheyenne (SSN-773)"><i>Cheyenne</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Greeneville_(SSN-772)" title="USS Greeneville (SSN-772)"><i>Greeneville</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/USS_Tucson_(SSN-770)" title="USS Tucson (SSN-770)"><i>Tucson</i></a>, and <a href="/wiki/USS_Pasadena_(SSN-752)" title="USS Pasadena (SSN-752)"><i>Pasadena</i></a>, as well as land-based <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_P-3_Orion" title="Lockheed P-3 Orion">P-3 Orion aircraft</a> from Commander <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patrol_and_Reconnaissance_Wing_2" title="Patrol and Reconnaissance Wing 2">Patrol and Reconnaissance Wing 2</a> and associated patrol squadrons <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VP-4" title="VP-4">VP-4</a>, <a href="/wiki/VP-9" title="VP-9">VP-9</a>, and <a href="/wiki/VP-47" title="VP-47">VP-47</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CVN72DANFS_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-CVN72DANFS-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NNS060317-06_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-NNS060317-06-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
