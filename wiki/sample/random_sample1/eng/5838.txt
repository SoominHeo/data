<p><b>America's Party</b>, founded as <b>America's Independent Party</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Conservatism_in_the_United_States" title="Conservatism in the United States">conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> political party formed in August 2008 in an offshoot of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_Party_(United_States)" title="Constitution Party (United States)">Constitution Party</a> by supporters of <a href="/wiki/Alan_Keyes" title="Alan Keyes">Alan Keyes</a>, with the goal of an alternative to the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> and <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> party system. It selected party leader <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hoefling" title="Tom Hoefling">Tom Hoefling</a> in the 2012 presidential election, and Hoefling is seeking the party nomination again in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016_presidential_election_in_the_united_states" title="2016 presidential election in the united states">2016 Presidential election</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America%27s_Party_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Founding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alan_Keyes_2008_candidacy_before_the_AIP">Alan Keyes 2008 candidacy before the AIP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America%27s_Party_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Alan Keyes 2008 candidacy before the AIP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alan_Keyes" title="Alan Keyes">Alan Keyes</a> declared his candidacy for President seeking the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)_presidential_primaries,_2008" title="Republican Party (United States) presidential primaries, 2008">nomination</a> on September 14, 2007. Just three days later, Keyes placed third in the <a href="/wiki/Family_Research_Council" title="Family Research Council">Family Research Council</a>'s Values Voter <a href="/wiki/Straw_poll" title="Straw poll">straw poll</a>, behind <a href="/wiki/Mike_Huckabee" title="Mike Huckabee">Mike Huckabee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ron_Paul" title="Ron Paul">Ron Paul</a>, garnering 5% of support.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Keyes campaign drew limited support in any of the caucuses or primaries that took place. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iowa_Caucus" title="Iowa Caucus">Iowa caucuses</a>, Keyes claimed some of the state's ballots did not even list him as a candidate, and his campaign <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEO" title="CEO">CEO</a>, Stephen Stone, stated that the reason Keyes did not show up on most ballots was primarily because Keyes had decided to enter the election cycle so late. He also blamed the <a href="/wiki/News_media" title="News media">media</a> for not recognizing Keyes as a viable candidate, excluding him from debates and providing practically no campaign coverage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Iowa_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Iowa-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Keyes was awarded four Republican Party convention delegates, more than he received in 1996 but less than in 2000. In early 2008, Keyes explored the possibility of allying himself with the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_Party_(United_States)" title="Constitution Party (United States)">Constitution Party</a>, marking this change away from his lifelong involvement in the GOP with a speech on April 15 in <a href="/wiki/Hazleton,_Pennsylvania" title="Hazleton, Pennsylvania">Hazleton, Pennsylvania</a>, a town which has been a battleground in the political fight over illegal immigration.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> However, due primarily to his fundamental disagreement with CP founder <a href="/wiki/Howard_Phillips_(politician)" title="Howard Phillips (politician)">Howard Phillips</a> over foreign policy, the party instead selected <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Baldwin" title="Chuck Baldwin">Chuck Baldwin</a> at the party's convention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Following the defeat, Keyes told a group of his supporters that he was "prayerfully considering" continuing his candidacy as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_(politician)" title="Independent (politician)">independent</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and refused to endorse Baldwin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America%27s_Party_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the failure to come together with the Constitution Party, Keyes' supporters formed America's Independent Party, which held its convention in <a href="/wiki/Fenton,_Michigan" title="Fenton, Michigan">Fenton, Michigan</a> on August 21, 2008, making Keyes their Presidential nominee. A press release from the party said: "<a href="/wiki/John_McCain" title="John McCain">John McCain</a> has abandoned the principles of <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a> – particularly the Reagan pro-life platform plank," and the party also opposed John McCain on other points, such as his opposition to a federal amendment outlawing marriage for same-sex couples, sponsoring McCain/Feingold legislation, which they said was "a direct attack on their First Amendment rights to political free speech and grassroots citizen activism;" they also opposed his support for immigration reform, deriding it as "amnesty" and his support for legislation intended to combat <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The AIP gained ballot access in three states and became a recognized write-in candidate in several dozen others. A struggle within the <a href="/wiki/American_Independent_Party" title="American Independent Party">American Independent Party</a> of California (the California affiliate of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_Party_(United_States)" title="Constitution Party (United States)">Constitution Party</a>), between a pro-Keyes faction and Constitution Party supporters of the 2008 candidacy of Chuck Baldwin, led to the placement of Keyes and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wiley_S._Drake" title="Wiley S. Drake">Wiley S. Drake</a> on the ballot as the AIP-CA nominees, where they received 40,673 votes. Two lawsuits by the Baldwin forces to regain control of the California AIP, one before the election and one after, failed. Keyes also received 2,550 votes on the AIP ticket in Florida,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> 3,051 votes in Colorado, and write-in votes in a few other states, for a nationwide total of 47,941 recorded votes, or 0.04% of the national popular vote, with approximately 85% of votes for the ticket cast in California.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Renaming_and_financing">Renaming and financing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America%27s_Party_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Renaming and financing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the party's official website, it has renamed itself "America's Party".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The party's website announces that, "America's Party does not accept financial contributions.... Instead, we're asking you to directly support fully-vetted, proven, principled candidates, so that 100% of your precious resources will go directly to the most important work."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Instead, the party maintains a "Directory of Approved Candidates, State, County &amp; Local Front Porch Caucuses" on its website (paid for by party Chairman Tom Hoefling). There was a convention on February 18, 2012 to nominate candidates for <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> and <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">Vice President</a> resulting in the nominations of Tom Hoefling as presidential candidate and J.D. Ellis as vice-presidential candidate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> During this convention the America's Party also ratified its 2012 platform.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Outreach">Outreach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America%27s_Party_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Outreach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>America's Party has several outreach groups. Having stated that the mainstream news media is biased against conservatives, the party maintains its own "America's Party News" site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Party members may submit news reports and opinion columns via the "We Are the Media" outreach group. Another outreach group is "Can-Do USA", which appeals to business owners, inventors, entrepreneurs, etc. " Veterans United to Save America" (V-USA)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> is the outreach group to military veterans. That group is chaired by screen actor Tom Kovach,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> who is a former Security Police staff sergeant in the U.S. Air Force.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Issues">Issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America%27s_Party_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tax_reform">Tax reform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America%27s_Party_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tax reform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The party seeks to reform the tax structure by advocating the repeal of the <a href="/wiki/Sixteenth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Sixteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution">16th Amendment</a>, and despite the fact that many members support the <a href="/wiki/FairTax" title="FairTax">FairTax</a>, the platform remains open to other replacements for the federal <a href="/wiki/Income_tax" title="Income tax">income tax</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_issues">Other issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America%27s_Party_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Other issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The party supports the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Marriage_Amendment" title="Federal Marriage Amendment">Federal Marriage Amendment</a> being added to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Constitution" title="U.S. Constitution">U.S. Constitution</a>. It is also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pro-life" title="Pro-life">pro-life</a> on <a href="/wiki/Abortion" title="Abortion">abortion</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_presidential_election">2012 presidential election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America%27s_Party_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2012 presidential election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 18, 2012, Party chairman <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hoefling" title="Tom Hoefling">Tom Hoefling</a> was chosen as the America's Party <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2012" title="United States presidential election, 2012">2012</a> presidential nominee in an online nominating convention. J.D. Ellis was selected as the vice presidential nominee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The ticket received 40,624 votes in the election, or 0.03% of the national popular vote, with about 95% of the votes coming from California.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_presidential_election">2016 presidential election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=America%27s_Party_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2016 presidential election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In late January, founding chairman <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hoefling" title="Tom Hoefling">Tom Hoefling</a> announced that he would again run for president. Three weeks later, on President's Day, V-USA chairman Tom Kovach announced that he was "testing the waters" for a possible presidential campaign. The party's nominating convention was planned for July.</p>
