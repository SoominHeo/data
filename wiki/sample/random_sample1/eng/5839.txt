<p>The <b>"Virgin Islands March"</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Patriotic_song" title="Patriotic song">patriotic song</a> which is considered to be the <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">United States Virgin Islands</a>.</p>
<p>The song was composed by Sam Williams and U.S. Virgin Island native <a href="/wiki/Alton_Adams" title="Alton Adams">Alton Adams</a> in the 1920s. It served as the unofficial anthem of the U.S. Virgin Islands until 1963 when it was officially recognized by Legislative Act.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The song itself consists of a very cheerful melody.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research"><span title="The material near this tag possibly contains original research. (September 2012)">original research?</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Since the U.S. Virgin Islands is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> insular territory, the national anthem is still <a href="/wiki/The_Star-Spangled_Banner" title="The Star-Spangled Banner">The Star-Spangled Banner</a>.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> reporter Alex Marshall compared this anthem favourably to other national anthems, suggesting that it was reminiscent of the music of the <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Mary_Poppins_(film)" title="Mary Poppins (film)">Mary Poppins</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Words_for_the_.22Virgin_Islands_March.22">Words for the "Virgin Islands March"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virgin_Islands_March&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title='Edit section: Words for the "Virgin Islands March"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dd>All hail the Virgin Islands.</dd>
<dd>Em'ralds of the sea,</dd>
<dd>Where beaches bright with coral sand</dd>
<dd>And trade winds bless our native land.</dd>
<dd>All hail the Virgin Islands,</dd>
<dd>Bathe in waters blue,</dd>
<dd>We give our loyalty,</dd>
<dd>Full to thee,</dd>
<dd>And pledge allegiance forever true.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>To thee our Virgin Islands,</dd>
<dd>Loving voices raise</dd>
<dd>A song in praise of brotherhood,</dd>
<dd>Where right makes might to fight for good.</dd>
<dd>To thee our Virgin Islands,</dd>
<dd>Haven of the free,</dd>
<dd>We sing our love to thee,</dd>
<dd>Joyously,</dd>
<dd>Our own fair islands of liberty.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>March on oh Virgin Islands,</dd>
<dd>In the joyful throng,</dd>
<dd>Uphold the right and right the wrong</dd>
<dd>Where only peace and love belong.</dd>
<dd>March on oh Virgin Islands,</dd>
<dd>Democratic land.</dd>
<dd>Together hand in hand,</dd>
<dd>Take your stand,</dd>
<dd>Forever soldiers in freedom's band.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>God bless our Virgin Islands,</dd>
<dd>Humbly now we pray,</dd>
<dd>Where all mankind can join today</dd>
<dd>In friendly warmth of work and play.</dd>
<dd>God bless our Virgin Islands,</dd>
<dd>Beautiful and tall.</dd>
<dd>Beneath a sunny sky,</dd>
<dd>Hilltops high</dd>
<dd>Hold out a welcome for one and all.</dd>
</dl>
