<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Minami-Saitama District, Saitama</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Minami-Saitama</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">南埼玉郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Minami-Saitama-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2012<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Minami-Saitama_District,_Saitama&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the district has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 33,405 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 2,090 persons per km². The total area is 15.95 km².</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Towns_and_villages">Towns and villages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minami-Saitama_District,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Towns and villages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Miyashiro,_Saitama" title="Miyashiro, Saitama">Miyashiro</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="District_timeline">District timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minami-Saitama_District,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: District timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>November 3, 1954
<dl>
<dd>The town of Iwatsuki gained city status.</dd>
<dd>The town of Koshigaya was created by merging with 2 towns and 8 villages.</dd>
</dl>
</li>
<li>January 1, 1955 - The town of Washinomiya merged with the village of Sakurada from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitakatsushika_District,_Saitama" title="Kitakatsushika District, Saitama">Kitakatsushika District</a> to form the town of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Washinomiya,_Saitama&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Washinomiya, Saitama (page does not exist)">Washinomiya</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitakatsushika_District,_Saitama" title="Kitakatsushika District, Saitama">Kitakatsushika District</a>.</li>
<li>July 20, 1955 - The villages of Momoki and Suka merged to form the town of <a href="/wiki/Miyashiro,_Saitama" title="Miyashiro, Saitama">Miyashiro</a>.</li>
<li>August 1, 1955 - The village of Kawayanagi merged into the town of Soka in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitaadachi_District,_Saitama" title="Kitaadachi District, Saitama">Kitaadachi District</a> (now the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soka,_Saitama" title="Soka, Saitama">Soka</a>).</li>
<li>November 3, 1955 - Parts of the town of Soka in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitaadachi_District,_Saitama" title="Kitaadachi District, Saitama">Kitaadachi District</a> merged into the town of Koshigaya.</li>
<li>January 1, 1956 - Parts of the city of <a href="/wiki/Iwatsuki,_Saitama" title="Iwatsuki, Saitama">Iwatsuki</a> merged into the town of Hasuda.</li>
<li>September 28, 1956
<dl>
<dd>Parts of the village of Hachijo merged with the villages of Yawata and Shiodomari to form the village of Yashio.</dd>
<dd>The remaining parts of Hachijo merged into the town of Soka in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitaadachi_District,_Saitama" title="Kitaadachi District, Saitama">Kitaadachi District</a>.</dd>
</dl>
</li>
<li>November 3, 1958 - The town of Koshigaya gained city status.</li>
<li>October 1, 1964 - The village of Yashio gained town status.</li>
<li>October 1, 1971 - The town of Kuki gained city status.</li>
<li>January 15, 1972 - The town of Yashio gained city status.</li>
<li>October 1, 1972 - The town of Hasuda gained city status.</li>
<li>March 23, 2010 - The town of <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Dbu,_Saitama" title="Shōbu, Saitama">Shōbu</a>, along with the towns of <a href="/wiki/Kurihashi,_Saitama" title="Kurihashi, Saitama">Kurihashi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Washimiya,_Saitama" title="Washimiya, Saitama">Washimiya</a>, both from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitakatsushika_District,_Saitama" title="Kitakatsushika District, Saitama">Kitakatsushika District</a>, merged with the city of <a href="/wiki/Kuki,_Saitama" title="Kuki, Saitama">Kuki</a> to form the new city of <a href="/wiki/Kuki,_Saitama" title="Kuki, Saitama">Kuki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>October 1, 2012 - The town of <a href="/wiki/Shiraoka,_Saitama" title="Shiraoka, Saitama">Shiraoka</a> gained city status. This leaves Minami Saitama District with one municipality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
