<p><b>Minami-Sendai Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">南仙台駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Minami-Sendai-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station on the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dhoku_Main_Line" title="Tōhoku Main Line">Tōhoku Main Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Taihaku-ku,_Sendai" title="Taihaku-ku, Sendai">Taihaku-ku</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sendai,_Miyagi" title="Sendai, Miyagi">Sendai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miyagi_Prefecture" title="Miyagi Prefecture">Miyagi Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minami-Sendai_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Minami-Sendai Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dhoku_Main_Line" title="Tōhoku Main Line">Tōhoku Main Line</a>, and is located 344.1 rail kilometers from the official starting point of the line at <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Station" title="Tokyo Station">Tokyo Station</a>. It is also served by the Joban Line, whose trains run past the official terminus at <a href="/wiki/Iwanuma_Station" title="Iwanuma Station">Iwanuma Station</a> on to <a href="/wiki/Sendai_Station_(Miyagi)" title="Sendai Station (Miyagi)">Sendai Station</a> and by the <a href="/wiki/Sendai_Airport_Line" title="Sendai Airport Line">Sendai Airport Line</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minami-Sendai_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Minami-Sendai Station has a single <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platform</a> and a single <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> connected to the station building by an overhead passageway. The station has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_Window" title="Green Window">Green Window</a> ticket counter.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minami-Sendai_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Natori Station opened on September 10, 1924 as <b>Rikuzen-Nakata Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">陸前中田駅</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. It adopted its present name on May 25, 1963. The station was absorbed into the JR East network upon the <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) on April 1, 1987.</p>
