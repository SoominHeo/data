<p><b>Minami-Senju Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">南千住駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Minami-Senju-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Arakawa,_Tokyo" title="Arakawa, Tokyo">Arakawa, Tokyo</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/East_Japan_Railway_Company" title="East Japan Railway Company">East Japan Railway Company</a> (JR East), <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro" title="Tokyo Metro">Tokyo Metro</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Intercity_Railway_Company" title="Metropolitan Intercity Railway Company">Metropolitan Intercity Railway Company</a>. The stations for each of these lines are located in separate buildings, necessitating crossing a road to reach each station's ticket exchange.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minami-Senju_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Jōban Line station opened on 25 December 1896.</p>
<p>The Hibiya Line station opened on 28 March 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2002_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-terada2002-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Tsukuba Express station opened on 24 August 2005.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surrounding_area">Surrounding area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minami-Senju_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Surrounding area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the period between 1650 and 1873, the area was the location of the Kotsukappara execution grounds. Between 100,000 and 200,000 people died here during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokugawa_era" title="Tokugawa era">Tokugawa era</a>. Near the south exit of the Tokyo Metro station, a small temple and burial ground commemorates this. Part of the burial grounds currently lie beneath the Hibiya Line tracks.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>There are many cheap hotels in the area.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Other locations of note include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/San%27ya" title="San'ya">San'ya</a> district</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Japan_National_Route_4" title="Japan National Route 4">National Route 4</a></li>
<li>Sumidagawa Freight Terminal (<a href="/wiki/Japan_Freight_Railway_Company" title="Japan Freight Railway Company">JR Freight</a>)</li>
</ul>
