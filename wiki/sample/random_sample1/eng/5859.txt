<p><b>Minamiaso</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">南阿蘇村</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Minamiaso-mura</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, literally "South Aso")</span> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_villages_in_Japan" title="List of villages in Japan">village</a> in <a href="/wiki/Aso_District,_Kumamoto" title="Aso District, Kumamoto">Aso District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kumamoto_Prefecture" title="Kumamoto Prefecture">Kumamoto Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>It was formed on February 13, 2005 from the merger of the villages of <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dy%C5%8D,_Kumamoto" title="Chōyō, Kumamoto">Chōyō</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hakusui,_Kumamoto" title="Hakusui, Kumamoto">Hakusui</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kugino,_Kumamoto" title="Kugino, Kumamoto">Kugino</a>. Neighbouring towns are <a href="/wiki/Takamori,_Kumamoto" title="Takamori, Kumamoto">Takamori</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ozu,_Kumamoto" title="Ozu, Kumamoto">Ozu</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nishihara,_Kumamoto" title="Nishihara, Kumamoto">Nishihara</a>.</p>
<p>As of December 31, 2009, the village has an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 12,015 (5,776 male, 6,239 female) and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 91.2 persons per km². The total area is 137.30 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiaso,_Kumamoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Minamiaso is situated within Aso <a href="/wiki/Caldera" title="Caldera">Caldera</a>, one of the largest volcanic calderas in the world. For more information, refer to the entry on <a href="/wiki/Mount_Aso" title="Mount Aso">Mt. Aso</a>.</p>
<p>Minamiaso was once three separate villages: <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dy%C5%8D,_Kumamoto" title="Chōyō, Kumamoto">Chōyō</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hakusui,_Kumamoto" title="Hakusui, Kumamoto">Hakusui</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kugino,_Kumamoto" title="Kugino, Kumamoto">Kugino</a>. In 2005, it was decided that these villages would merge along with neighboring area Tateno to become Minamiaso village, which would stretch between Ozu and Takamori.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger">Merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiaso,_Kumamoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Merger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Due to declining birth rate, aging population and the opportunity to share administrative resources, discussions between the three villages (<a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dy%C5%8D,_Kumamoto" title="Chōyō, Kumamoto">Chōyō</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hakusui,_Kumamoto" title="Hakusui, Kumamoto">Hakusui</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kugino,_Kumamoto" title="Kugino, Kumamoto">Kugino</a>) began on April 1, 2003. The merger was complete on February 13, 2005.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiaso,_Kumamoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Tokai Agricultural University</li>
<li>Kumamoto Seiryou High School</li>
</ul>
<p>There are no public high schools in Minamiaso.</p>

<ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Junior_High_School_Merger">Junior High School Merger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiaso,_Kumamoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Junior High School Merger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the beginning of 2016, due to declining student numbers, the three junior high schools merged into a single school, named simply "Minamiaso Junior High School". Chōyō Junior High School was chosen as the new location to host the students and staff.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiaso,_Kumamoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiaso,_Kumamoto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Trains going to <a href="/wiki/Higo-%C5%8Czu_Station" title="Higo-Ōzu Station">Ozu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miyaji_Station" title="Miyaji Station">Miyaji</a> (Aso City) or non-express trains bound for Oita will stop at <a href="/wiki/Tateno_Station_(Kumamoto)" title="Tateno Station (Kumamoto)">Tateno Station</a>. Those trains bound for Ozu will end their journey there and connecting train will be waiting at the adjacent platform. A change will also need to be made at Tateno to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minami_Aso_Railway" title="Minami Aso Railway">Minamiaso Tetsudou line</a>, which is not a JR line so JR Rail Passes will not be valid. From Tateno to Chōyō (the first station in Minamiaso-mura) costs 230 yen. Following the <a href="/wiki/2016_Kumamoto_earthquakes" title="2016 Kumamoto earthquakes">2016 Kumamoto earthquake</a>, the train line is not in operation and is currently receiving repairs (as of 27th January 2017).</p>
