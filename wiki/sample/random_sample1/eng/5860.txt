<p><b>Minamiuonuma</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">南魚沼市</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Minamiuonuma-shi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Japan" title="Cities of Japan">city</a> located in north-central <a href="/wiki/Niigata_Prefecture" title="Niigata Prefecture">Niigata Prefecture</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Hokuriku_region" title="Hokuriku region">Hokuriku region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of 1 June 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Minamiuonuma&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the city had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 42,993 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 223 persons per km². Its total area was 192.74 square kilometres (74.42 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiuonuma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Minamiuonuma_Paddy.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1712" data-file-width="2288" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Minamiuonuma_Paddy.jpg/220px-Minamiuonuma_Paddy.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Minamiuonuma_Paddy.jpg/330px-Minamiuonuma_Paddy.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Minamiuonuma_Paddy.jpg/440px-Minamiuonuma_Paddy.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

Paddy fields in Minamiuonuma</div>

<p>Minamiuonuma is situated in a valley in a mountainous region of Niigata Prefecture known as “<a href="/wiki/Snow_country_(Japan)" title="Snow country (Japan)">Snow Country</a>” because of the heavy snowfall in winter. The city is bounded by <a href="/wiki/Uonuma,_Niigata" title="Uonuma, Niigata">Uonuma</a> and the Echigo-Sanzan mountains in the north, and <a href="/wiki/Yuzawa,_Niigata" title="Yuzawa, Niigata">Yuzawa</a>, a popular ski resort town, in the south. The <a href="/wiki/Uono_River" title="Uono River">Uono River</a> flows through most of the city. The city and its surrounding areas are dotted with many <a href="/wiki/Onsen" title="Onsen">onsen</a> (Japanese hot springs) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ski_resorts" title="Ski resorts">ski resorts</a>, making it a popular destination in winter. There are also a large number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paddy_fields" title="Paddy fields">paddy fields</a>, and popular seasonal fruit like watermelon, as this is a major <a href="/wiki/Koshihikari" title="Koshihikari">Koshihikari</a> rice-growing region in Japan. Parts of the city are within the borders of the <a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Dshin%27etsu-k%C5%8Dgen_National_Park" title="Jōshin'etsu-kōgen National Park">Jōshin'etsu-kōgen National Park</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiuonuma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Minamiuonuma was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Echigo_Province" title="Echigo Province">Echigo Province</a>. The modern city of Minamiuonuma was established on November 1, 2004, from the merger of the towns <a href="/wiki/Muika,_Niigata" title="Muika, Niigata">Muikamachi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yamato,_Niigata" title="Yamato, Niigata">Yamato</a> (both from <a href="/wiki/Minami-Uonuma_District,_Niigata" title="Minami-Uonuma District, Niigata">Minamiuonuma District</a>). On October 1, 2005, the town of <a href="/wiki/Shiozawa,_Niigata" title="Shiozawa, Niigata">Shiozawa</a> (from <a href="/wiki/Minami-Uonuma_District,_Niigata" title="Minami-Uonuma District, Niigata">Minamiuonuma District</a>) was also merged into Minamiuonuma.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiuonuma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Minamiuonuma is based primarily on agriculture and seasonal tourism.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiuonuma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Minamiuonuma has 19 public elementary schools and six public middle schools. There are four public high schools, and also one special education school. The <a href="/wiki/International_University_of_Japan" title="International University of Japan">International University of Japan</a> is also based at Minamiuonuma.</p>
