<p><b>Minamiuwa</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">南宇和郡</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Minamiuwa-gun</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Japan" title="Districts of Japan">district</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Ehime_Prefecture" title="Ehime Prefecture">Ehime Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</p>
<p>As of 2004, the estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> is 28,100 and the total area is 239.54 km².</p>
<p>The district includes one town.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ainan,_Ehime" title="Ainan, Ehime">Ainan</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minamiuwa_District,_Ehime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>1878 — The Minamiuwa District broke off from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uwa_District,_Ehime" title="Uwa District, Ehime">Uwa District</a> through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meiji_era" title="Meiji era">Meiji era</a> land reforms. (7 villages)</li>
<li>February 11, 1923 — The village of Mishō gained town status. (1 town, 6 villages)</li>
<li>February 11, 1923 — The village of Jōhen gained town status. (2 towns, 5 villages)</li>
<li>November 3, 1948 — The village of Uchiumi broke up into Uchiumi and Minamiuchiumi, with some parts merging into Jōhen. (2 towns, 6 villages)</li>
<li>April 1, 1952 — The village of Higashisotoumi gained town status. (3 towns, 5 villages)</li>
<li>September 1, 1952 — The village of Midorisōzu merged into the town of Jōhen. (3 towns, 4 villages)</li>
<li>October 1, 1952 — The village of Nishisotoumi was renamed and gained town status to become the town of Nishiumi. (4 towns, 3 villages)</li>
<li>September 21, 1956 — The town of Higashisotoumi merged into the town of Jōhen. (3 towns, 3 villages)</li>
<li>September 30, 1956 — The village of Minamiuchiumi merged into the town of Mishō. (3 towns, 2 villages)</li>
<li>January 1, 1962 — The village of Ipponmatsu gained town status. (4 towns, 1 village)</li>
<li>October 1, 2004 — The towns of <a href="/wiki/Ipponmatsu,_Ehime" title="Ipponmatsu, Ehime">Ipponmatsu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mish%C5%8D,_Ehime" title="Mishō, Ehime">Mishō</a>, <a href="/wiki/J%C5%8Dhen,_Ehime" title="Jōhen, Ehime">Jōhen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nishiumi,_Ehime" title="Nishiumi, Ehime">Nishiumi</a>, and the village of <a href="/wiki/Uchiumi,_Ehime" title="Uchiumi, Ehime">Uchiumi</a> merged to form the new town of <a href="/wiki/Ainan,_Ehime" title="Ainan, Ehime">Ainan</a>. (1 town)</li>
</ul>
