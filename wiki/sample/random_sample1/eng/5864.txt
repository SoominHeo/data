<p><b>Minnesota United FC</b> is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">soccer</a> team based in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minneapolis-Saint_Paul" title="Minneapolis-Saint Paul">Minneapolis-Saint Paul</a> area in <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>. Founded in 2010, the team played in the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Soccer_League" title="North American Soccer League">North American Soccer League</a> (NASL), a professional league sanctioned by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Soccer_Federation" title="United States Soccer Federation">United States Soccer Federation</a>. The team won the 2011 NASL championship.</p>
<p>On March 25, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Soccer" title="Major League Soccer">Major League Soccer</a> announced it had selected Minnesota United as an expansion team, to begin play in 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The team played its home games at the <a href="/wiki/National_Sports_Center" title="National Sports Center">National Sports Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Blaine,_Minnesota" title="Blaine, Minnesota">Blaine, Minnesota</a>, 18 miles north of <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a>. The team's colors were sky blue, gray, and black with a blue accent.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/National_Sports_Center" title="National Sports Center">National Sports Center</a> began considering fielding a Division 2 men's professional soccer team in December 2009. The then current team, the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Thunder" title="Minnesota Thunder">Minnesota Thunder</a>, were tenants of the National Sports Center and were struggling through financial difficulties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In January 2010, the National Sports Center announced they would field a new team to replace the Minnesota Thunder. The Thunder at the time were not officially folded but were facing financial ruin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Following a team-naming contest, the National Sports Center (NSC) announced the official team name would be <b>NSC Minnesota</b>, with the team nickname being <b>Stars</b> on February 5, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Five days later, former <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">USMNT</a> player <a href="/wiki/Manny_Lagos" title="Manny Lagos">Manny Lagos</a> was named as the clubs first head coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lagos_Appointed_HC_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lagos_Appointed_HC-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The team played its first official game on April 11, 2010, a 2–0 loss to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vancouver_Whitecaps_(1986-2010)" title="Vancouver Whitecaps (1986-2010)">Vancouver Whitecaps</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The first goal in franchise history was scored by <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Wasson" title="Daniel Wasson">Daniel Wasson</a> in their next game, a 1–0 victory over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carolina_RailHawks" title="Carolina RailHawks">Carolina RailHawks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Stars enjoyed some impressive victories in their debut season, including a 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_Baltimore" title="Crystal Palace Baltimore">Crystal Palace Baltimore</a>, a 3–0 win over <a href="/wiki/AC_St._Louis" title="AC St. Louis">AC St. Louis</a>, and a 3–1 win over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Tampa_Bay" title="FC Tampa Bay">FC Tampa Bay</a>, eventually finishing fourth in their conference. Unfortunately for the Stars, they went out of the playoffs at the quarter final stage, 4–0 on aggregate to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carolina_RailHawks" title="Carolina RailHawks">Carolina RailHawks</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> after defender <a href="/wiki/Andres_Arango" title="Andres Arango">Andres Arango</a> was issued a red card after just 38 minutes. <a href="/wiki/Simone_Bracalello" title="Simone Bracalello">Simone Bracalello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Cvilikas" title="Brian Cvilikas">Brian Cvilikas</a> were the Stars' top scorers in 2010, with 5 goals each. The team averaged an attendance of 1,374 which was 10th out of the 12 league teams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3years_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-3years-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Stars announced that for the <a href="/wiki/2011_NSC_Minnesota_Stars_season" title="2011 NSC Minnesota Stars season">2011 season</a> the team would no longer be owned by the National Sports Center but by the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Soccer_League" title="North American Soccer League">North American Soccer League</a>. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Soccer_Federation" title="United States Soccer Federation">United States Soccer Federation</a> created ownership standards where the owner must have a net worth of at least <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">US$</a>20 million, and the National Sports Center did not meet that criterion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The NASL committed to owning the team for three years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3years_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-3years-11">[11]</a></sup> The team set a budget of $2 million based on the goal of averaging 1,000 fans per game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> By June 5 the team was in second place<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> but a four-game losing streak as part of a 0–6–1 stretch sent the team in a slump before finishing with the sixth and final playoff spot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The Stars knocked off the Tampa Bay Rowdies 1–0 in the quarterfinals before defeating the first-seed Carolina RailHawks on penalty kicks after the two-legged series ended 4–4. A 3–1 home victory was enough to win the NASL championship after the second leg ended in a 0–0 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The team averaged around 1,700 fans during the season but drew 2,500 for the playoff semifinals<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and 4,511 for the final home leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 9, 2012, the club announced a new logo as well as a new name. The NSC portion of the name, which had led to confusion, was dropped, with the new name being the <b>Minnesota Stars FC</b>. The new logo was revealed with the word 'NSC' removed and the state motto, <i>L’Étoile du Nord</i>, added.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMSNewLogo_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-IMSNewLogo-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSCNewLogo_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-NSCNewLogo-22">[22]</a></sup> The team continued a search for a new owner in the offseason <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012Stars_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-2012Stars-25">[25]</a></sup> and opened the <a href="/wiki/2012_Minnesota_Stars_FC_season" title="2012 Minnesota Stars FC season">2012 season</a> with a 0–0 draw against the Carolina RailHawks at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metrodome" title="Metrodome">Metrodome</a> in front of a crowd of 8,693.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>Minnesota faced an October 27, 2012, deadline to find new ownership to ensure a place in the NASL for the 2013 season. At the time the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Vikings" title="Minnesota Vikings">Minnesota Vikings</a> expressed interest in professional soccer in Minnesota but were not contemplating purchasing the Stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The league planned to vote on whether or not to fund the team for the 2013 season after the conclusion of the 2012 season. The USSF requirement that owners of a Division II team to be worth at least $20 million and a potential MLS bid by the Minnesota Vikings were seen as possible deterrents to finding a potential new owner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> On November 9, 2012, the league officially announced that the team was purchased by <a href="/wiki/William_W._McGuire" title="William W. McGuire">Bill McGuire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcguire_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-mcguire-29">[29]</a></sup> On March 5, 2013, that was followed up by the team rebranding with the name <b>Minnesota United FC</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-united_announcement_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-united_announcement-30">[30]</a></sup> The $1.2 million budget that the Stars were operating on was expanded and the staff size increased to 20 employees for the start of the 2013 season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and has since more than doubled.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_season">2014 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2014 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Minnesota United FC won the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Soccer_League" title="North American Soccer League">NASL</a> Spring Season Championship. The team finished the season with 6 wins, 2 draws, and 1 loss coming to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Cosmos_(2010)" title="New York Cosmos (2010)">New York Cosmos</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 19, 2014, Minnesota United FC won 2–0 over <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swansea_City_AFC" title="Swansea City AFC">Swansea City</a> in a friendly match played during the break between the NASL Spring and Fall seasons that drew 9,064 fans to the National Sports Center. Goals were scored by defender <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Justin_Davis_(soccer)" title="Justin Davis (soccer)">Justin Davis</a> and midfielder <a href="/wiki/Greg_Jordan" title="Greg Jordan">Greg Jordan</a>. However, Swansea did not field several of their top players, including <a href="/wiki/Michel_Vorm" title="Michel Vorm">Michel Vorm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wilfried_Bony" title="Wilfried Bony">Wilfried Bony</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ki_Sung-Yeung" title="Ki Sung-Yeung">Ki Sung-Yeung</a>, all of whom played in the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> in Brazil.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_season">2015 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2015 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After bringing in several promising new players during the off-season, including Cameroonian national team goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Ndjock" title="Sammy Ndjock">Sammy Ndjock</a>, Minnesota United FC was a heavy favorite entering the year. However, the team struggled during the Spring Season due to chemistry problems both on and off the field and finishing in 4th place. The summer break saw the departure of break-out star player and member of the US Men's National Team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miguel_Ibarra_(soccer,_born_1990)" title="Miguel Ibarra (soccer, born 1990)">Miguel Ibarra</a> who bid farewell by playing one half for both Minnesota United and his new team <a href="/wiki/Club_Le%C3%B3n" title="Club León">Club León</a> in an international friendly in front of 9,388 fans which ended in a 1–1 tie. After several additional roster moves, the Loons came out much stronger in the Fall Season, fighting for the Fall Season Championship and top playoff seed until the last week of the regular season but ultimately finishing in 3rd place.</p>
<p>Minnesota faced the Fall Season champions <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottawa_Fury" title="Ottawa Fury">Ottawa Fury</a> in the semifinal where they took an early lead after a goal by <a href="/wiki/Christian_Ramirez_(soccer,_born_1991)" title="Christian Ramirez (soccer, born 1991)">Christian Ramirez</a>. but ultimately lost 2–1 against the Canadian side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Minnesota finished the 2015 combined season with a 14–11–5 record, tallying 54 goals along the way, the highest of any team in the league.</p>
<p>On December 3, 2015, it was announced that head coach Manny Lagos would move to being the team's full-time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sporting_director" title="Sporting director">sporting director</a> while assistant coach Carl Craig would take over the role as head coach for the 2016 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lagos_GM.2C_Craig_HC_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lagos_GM.2C_Craig_HC-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Move_to_Major_League_Soccer">Move to Major League Soccer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Move to Major League Soccer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minnesota_United_FC_(MLS)" title="Minnesota United FC (MLS)">Minnesota United FC (MLS)</a></div>
<p>On March 25, 2015, the league announced that it had awarded the 22nd MLS team in Minneapolis to a Bill McGuire-led ownership group that includes the owners of the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Timberwolves" title="Minnesota Timberwolves">Timberwolves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Wild" title="Minnesota Wild">Wild</a>. The team had hoped to build a <a href="/wiki/Soccer-specific_stadium" title="Soccer-specific stadium">soccer-specific stadium</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Downtown_Minneapolis" title="Downtown Minneapolis">Downtown Minneapolis</a> near the Minneapolis Farmers Market but that plan was met with opposition from the mayor. The league selected United over a competing bid by the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Vikings" title="Minnesota Vikings">Minnesota Vikings</a> ownership, who sought to use the under-construction <a href="/wiki/U.S._Bank_Stadium" title="U.S. Bank Stadium">U.S. Bank Stadium</a> as its home field.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the announcement, team president Nick Rogers said he expected much of the team, including the name, logo, coach and some players, to remain intact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 23, 2015, the team and the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Paul,_Minnesota" title="Saint Paul, Minnesota">City of Saint Paul</a> announced that a soccer-specific stadium would be built in the Midway area of Saint Paul near the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_State_Highway_51" title="Minnesota State Highway 51">Snelling Avenue</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_Avenue_(Minneapolis-Saint_Paul)" title="University Avenue (Minneapolis-Saint Paul)">University Avenue</a>. The stadium would seat about 21,500 and open in 2018. The team will make the switch to MLS in 2017 playing their home games at the University of Minnesota's TCF Bank Stadium.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Colors_and_badge">Colors and badge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Colors and badge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NSCMinnesotaStars.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="359" data-file-width="277" height="130" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/64/NSCMinnesotaStars.png/100px-NSCMinnesotaStars.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/64/NSCMinnesotaStars.png/150px-NSCMinnesotaStars.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/64/NSCMinnesotaStars.png/200px-NSCMinnesotaStars.png 2x" width="100"/></a>

<p>As the Stars, the team's official colors were blue and yellow, following the tradition established the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Thunder" title="Minnesota Thunder">Minnesota Thunder</a> of Minnesota soccer teams wearing predominantly blue as their home color. Before 2012, the club logo was a shield in two-tone blue and gold, with a stylized star overlaid by the NSC Minnesota wordmark. A new logo was unveiled in January 2012. The logo was designed over a six-month period by a member of the club's supporters group. The word 'NSC' removed and the state motto, <i>L’Étoile du Nord</i>, added along with a soccer ball behind the prominent star.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMSNewLogo_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-IMSNewLogo-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSCNewLogo_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-NSCNewLogo-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>As Minnesota United, the new logo features a stylized <a href="/wiki/Loon" title="Loon">loon</a>, which is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_U.S._state_birds" title="List of U.S. state birds">state bird</a> of <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, with eleven spread feathers. The rebranded team's colors were changed to sky blue, grey, and black, with a red accent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-united_announcement_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-united_announcement-30">[30]</a></sup> Zeus Jones created the designs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ThunderWizards.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2304" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/ThunderWizards.JPG/260px-ThunderWizards.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/ThunderWizards.JPG/390px-ThunderWizards.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/ThunderWizards.JPG/520px-ThunderWizards.JPG 2x" width="260"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ThunderWizards.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
National Sports Center</div>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_Sports_Center" title="National Sports Center">National Sports Center</a>; <a href="/wiki/Blaine,_Minnesota" title="Blaine, Minnesota">Blaine</a>. (Capacity: 8,500). (2010–2012, 2013 Spring Season Finale, Fall season, 2014 season besides August 2 Game)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hubert_H._Humphrey_Metrodome" title="Hubert H. Humphrey Metrodome">Hubert H. Humphrey Metrodome</a>; <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a>. (Capacity: 60,000). (Season openers, 2013 Spring season besides July 4 Game)</li>

<p>United plays its home games in the 8,500-seat stadium at the <a href="/wiki/National_Sports_Center" title="National Sports Center">National Sports Center</a>, a 600-acre (240 ha) multi-sport complex located in <a href="/wiki/Blaine,_Minnesota" title="Blaine, Minnesota">Blaine, Minnesota</a>, which in addition to the soccer stadium, features over 50 full-sized soccer fields, a golf course, a <a href="/wiki/Velodrome" title="Velodrome">velodrome</a>, a meeting and convention facility, and an eight-sheet ice rink, the <a href="/wiki/Schwan_Super_Rink" title="Schwan Super Rink">Schwan Super Rink</a>.</p>
<p>The team played its home games for the 2013 Spring Season at the <a href="/wiki/Hubert_H._Humphrey_Metrodome" title="Hubert H. Humphrey Metrodome">Hubert H. Humphrey Metrodome</a> in Downtown <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a> (now demolished). In the past, it has held its season openers there. In 2014 one game was played on a grass field at <a href="/wiki/TCF_Bank_Stadium" title="TCF Bank Stadium">TCF Bank Stadium</a> as part of a doubleheader with a <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City F.C.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_F.C." title="Olympiacos F.C.">Olympiacos F.C.</a> friendly, which was part of the International Challenge Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, significant temporary seating was added to the National Sports Center to accommodate large increases in attendance. Many matches saw new records set, with typical attendance exceeding 9,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_stadium_in_Midway">New stadium in Midway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: New stadium in Midway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_United_FC_Stadium" title="Minnesota United FC Stadium">Minnesota United FC Stadium</a></div>
<p>A new soccer-specific stadium is planned to be built in the Midway area of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Paul,_Minnesota" title="Saint Paul, Minnesota">Saint Paul</a>. The $200 million stadium is privately funded<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greder1_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-Greder1-44">[44]</a></sup> and will seat 21,500.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Melo1_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-Melo1-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>The team had sought sites to build the stadium in Saint Paul, Minneapolis, and <a href="/wiki/Bloomington,_Minnesota" title="Bloomington, Minnesota">Bloomington</a>. A preliminary plan in March 2015 proposed the stadium be built in downtown Minneapolis, near the Minneapolis Farmers Market. This proposal did not get enough political support from the city or the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greder1_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-Greder1-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>The plans for the Midway site include a ring-shaped stadium, with 2.6 acres (1.1 ha) of outdoor gathering spaces. The site is accessible by transit, with both the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/METRO_Green_Line" title="METRO Green Line">METRO Green Line</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Metro_A_Line_(Minnesota)" title="Metro A Line (Minnesota)">A Line</a> serving near by. A city environmental assessment predicted in June 2016 that nearly a third of the attendees will arrive on the Green Line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Melo2_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-Melo2-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>The stadium will sit on the site of a former bus maintenance facility that was used by <a href="/wiki/Metro_Transit_(Minnesota)" title="Metro Transit (Minnesota)">Metro Transit</a>. A masterplan has been drawn up for the redevelopment of the broad area, including the stadium site and adjacent properties owned by RK Midway. This may include building new hotel and office space and the redevelopment of the existing shopping center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Melo1_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-Melo1-45">[45]</a></sup> The masterplan calls for the redevelopment to be more pedestrian friendly, to accommodate large numbers of fans walking to and from the transit stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Melo2_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-Melo2-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>A glass-like polymer mesh oval exterior with an overhang partially covering the field is expected to soften the noise towards the neighborhood. The stadium will be ringed by three grassy plazas and a fourth green space will be placed along University Avenue, near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snelling_Avenue_Station" title="Snelling Avenue Station">Snelling Avenue Station</a>. These green spaces will be progressively introduced in phases as property owner RK Midway waits for existing leases on its current tenants to end. However, the timeline of these phases has not been released to the city or the public, as of July 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Melo2_46-2"><a href="#cite_note-Melo2-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>The stadium was planned to break ground in June 2016, but has been delayed while the team awaits a tax-exemption from the state, similar to ones granted to other recent stadium projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Melo1_45-2"><a href="#cite_note-Melo1-45">[45]</a></sup> Construction is expected to be completed and the stadium opened in time for the 2018 season.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_culture">Club culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Club culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rivalries">Rivalries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Rivalries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the course of the <a href="/wiki/2011_North_American_Soccer_League_season" title="2011 North American Soccer League season">2011 NASL season</a> a rivalry was formed between the then-Stars and <a href="/wiki/FC_Edmonton" title="FC Edmonton">FC Edmonton</a> known as the <a class="external text" href="https://twitter.com/TheFlyoverCup" rel="nofollow">Flyover Cup</a>. The name was chosen for Edmonton and Minnesota lying in flight paths for transcontinental flights, but often passed over by tourists. The symbol of the cup is a <a href="/wiki/Loon" title="Loon">loon</a>, being an unofficial national bird of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and also the state bird of Minnesota.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporters_groups">Supporters groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Supporters groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The club has several recognized supporters groups: Dark Clouds, Wolf's Head, Mankato Dark Clouds, and True North Elite.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> The club also has several loosely organized unofficial supporters groups.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="primary sources and self-promotion is not appropriate here (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mascot">Mascot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Mascot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Unveiled in July 2014, the club's mascot is a loon, PK, who wears the team jersey and soccer shoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Fans also call the mascot Foot Foot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting">Broadcasting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Broadcasting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013, as with all the teams in the NASL, United hosted webcasts of all its matches on NASL Live. Chris Lidholm handled the play by play along with Eric Nordquist as the sideline reporter. At the end of the 2013 season, two home games were broadcast live, one on Fox Sports North, the other on Channel 45.</p>
<p>For the 2014 season, all home games were broadcast on <a href="/wiki/KSTC-TV" title="KSTC-TV">KSTC-TV</a> Channel 45.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the 2015 season all home and away matches were broadcast on KSTC-45 as well as streamed online through <a href="/wiki/ESPN3" title="ESPN3">ESPN3</a>. U.S. Soccer Hall of Famer <a href="/wiki/Alan_Willey" title="Alan Willey">Alan Willey</a> provided commentary and analysis during the broadcast of the matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, all matches were broadcast on <a href="/wiki/WFTC" title="WFTC">WFTC</a> Channel 29. Lidholm, Willey, and Nordquist continued as the broadcast team.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reserve_squad">Reserve squad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minnesota_United_FC_(2010%E2%80%9316)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Reserve squad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2013, the club became the second NASL team to announce that it would field a reserve squad in the <a href="/wiki/National_Premier_Soccer_League" title="National Premier Soccer League">National Premier Soccer League</a> (<a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Silverbacks" title="Atlanta Silverbacks">Atlanta Silverbacks</a> were the first). <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_United_FC_Reserves" title="Minnesota United FC Reserves">Minnesota United FC Reserves</a> began play in the <a href="/wiki/National_Premier_Soccer_League" title="National Premier Soccer League">NPSL</a> in 2014 at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bielenberg_Sports_Complex&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bielenberg Sports Complex (page does not exist)">Bielenberg Sports Complex</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> and were coached by Donny Mark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>In their inaugural season, the Minnesota United FC Reserves lost to <a href="/wiki/Lansing_United" title="Lansing United">Lansing United</a> in the first round of playoffs after a 6–2–2 regular season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Minnesota United FC Reserves moved to the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League_of_America" title="Premier League of America">Premier League of America</a> to begin the 2016 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
