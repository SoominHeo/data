<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Minigame</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>A <b>minigame</b> (also spelled <b>mini-game</b> or <b>mini game</b> and sometimes called a <b>subgame</b> or <b>microgame</b>) is a short <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> often contained within another video game, and sometimes in application software or on a display of any form of hardware. A minigame is always smaller or more simplistic than the game in which it is contained. Minigames are sometimes also offered separately for free to promote the main game. For instance, the <i><a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_Stadium" title="Pokémon Stadium">Pokémon Stadium</a></i> minigames involve merely pressing a few buttons at specific intervals, with little complexity. Some minigames can also be <a href="/wiki/Bonus_stage" title="Bonus stage">bonus stages</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secret_level" title="Secret level">secret levels</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minigame&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Minigames occur variously as <a href="/wiki/Gameplay" title="Gameplay">gameplay</a> features, or as time fillers while levels are loading, or as <a href="/wiki/Easter_egg_(media)" title="Easter egg (media)">Easter eggs</a> even in non video games e.g. a DOOM-like game or a flight simulator in different versions of <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Excel" title="Microsoft Excel">Microsoft Excel</a>. In the latter case, they are often called "<i>secret games</i>". In the former case, the successful completion of such minigames may or may not be required to finish the encompassing game. They are often included as extra content to use once the main storyline is completed. Minigames occur also on other forms of hardware e.g. on a <a href="/wiki/Dot-matrix_display" title="Dot-matrix display">dot-matrix display</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Pinball" title="Pinball">pinball</a> machine or even as time filler on a traffic light e.g. StreetPong.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minigame_compilations">Minigame compilations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minigame&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Minigame compilations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some games, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wario_(series)#WarioWare" title="Wario (series)"><i>WarioWare</i> series</a> (which are called microgames in the game), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UPL" title="UPL">Universal Research Laboratories</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Sports_game#Beginnings_of_sports_games" title="Sports game">Video Action</a></i>, some <a href="/wiki/Cinemaware" title="Cinemaware">Cinemaware</a> titles like <i><a href="/wiki/Defender_of_the_Crown" title="Defender of the Crown">Defender of the Crown</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/David_Whittaker_(video_game_composer)" title="David Whittaker (video game composer)">David Whittaker's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Lazy_Jones" title="Lazy Jones">Lazy Jones</a></i> or the smartphone satire <i><a href="/wiki/Phone_Story" title="Phone Story">Phone Story</a></i> are made up of many minigames strung together into one video game. Some similar games, such as <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Mario_Party" title="Mario Party">Mario Party</a></i> series, are considered <a href="/wiki/Party_game" title="Party game">party games</a>, specifically developed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multiplayer" title="Multiplayer">multiplayer</a>. In party games, minigames usually involve performing an activity faster or collecting more of a specified item than other players to win.</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_examples">Notable examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Minigame&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Zelda" title="The Legend of Zelda">The Legend of Zelda</a></i> games have many minigames in each game, often having prizes such as Pieces of Heart (increasing Link's health), Rupees (the games' currency), and upgrades (<a href="/wiki/Quiver" title="Quiver">quiver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wallet" title="Wallet">wallet</a>, etc.)</p>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy" title="Final Fantasy">Final Fantasy</a></i> series is notable for featuring minigames in every entry of the series, ever since the first <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy_(video_game)" title="Final Fantasy (video game)">Final Fantasy</a></i> (1987), in which a <a href="/wiki/Sliding_puzzle" title="Sliding puzzle">sliding puzzle</a> in the form of an Easter egg can be unlocked while boarding the ship. In <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy_II" title="Final Fantasy II">Final Fantasy II</a></i> (1988), a <a href="/wiki/Rummy" title="Rummy">matching game</a> can be unlocked while boarding the ice sled and meeting a certain requirement. Later in the series, <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy_VII" title="Final Fantasy VII">Final Fantasy VII</a></i> (1997) was the first video game to include within it at least thirty minigames, which remains the largest number of minigames for a <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_video_game" title="Role-playing video game">role-playing game</a>. The <a href="/wiki/PC_game" title="PC game">PC game</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Chronomaster" title="Chronomaster">Chronomaster</a></i> featured similar puzzle minigames which were crucial to the plot.</p>
<p>Some minigames become so popular that they are eventually published as individual titles by themselves. Notable examples are <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geometry_Wars" title="Geometry Wars">Geometry Wars</a></i>, which was originally a minigame in <i><a href="/wiki/Project_Gotham_Racing_2" title="Project Gotham Racing 2">Project Gotham Racing 2</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Arcomage" title="Arcomage">Arcomage</a>, a relatively complex minigame, reminiscent of <i><a href="/wiki/Magic:_The_Gathering" title="Magic: The Gathering">Magic: The Gathering</a></i>, first introduced in <i><a href="/wiki/Might_and_Magic_VII:_For_Blood_and_Honor" title="Might and Magic VII: For Blood and Honor">Might and Magic VII: For Blood and Honor</a></i>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/PocketStation" title="PocketStation">PocketStation</a> (for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_PlayStation" title="Sony PlayStation">Sony PlayStation</a>) and <a href="/wiki/VMU" title="VMU">VMU</a> (for <a href="/wiki/Dreamcast" title="Dreamcast">Dreamcast</a>) accessories allowed the user to download minigames from the main console onto the pocket device, and often then sync progress in the minigame back on to the console. Two examples of this include the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gameplay_of_Final_Fantasy#Minigames" title="Gameplay of Final Fantasy">Chocobo World</a></i> minigame inside <i><a href="/wiki/Final_Fantasy_VIII" title="Final Fantasy VIII">Final Fantasy VIII</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (which is also playable on PC), and 'Chao Adventure', a minigame in <i><a href="/wiki/Sonic_Adventure" title="Sonic Adventure">Sonic Adventure</a></i>.</p>
<p>The tabletop game <i><a href="/wiki/Mansions_of_Madness" title="Mansions of Madness">Mansions of Madness</a></i> also features minigames in the form of simple puzzles.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Capcom" title="Capcom">Capcom</a>'s</i> <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Fighter" title="Street Fighter">Street Fighter</a></i> series features two minigames as bonus stages in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Street_Fighter_II" title="Street Fighter II">Street Fighter II</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Fighter_III" title="Street Fighter III">Street Fighter III</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Fighter_IV" title="Street Fighter IV">Street Fighter IV</a></i>, after winning matches.</p>
