<p><b>Midland Square</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ミッドランド スクエア</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, officially called <b>Toyota-Mainichi Building</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">豊田・毎日ビルディング</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is a <a href="/wiki/Skyscraper" title="Skyscraper">skyscraper</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Station" title="Nagoya Station">Meieki</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Nakamura-ku,_Nagoya" title="Nakamura-ku, Nagoya">Nakamura-ku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It opened in early 2007. It is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_in_Nagoya" title="List of tallest buildings in Nagoya">tallest building in Nagoya</a> and the fifth tallest building in Japan as of 2015. At 247m (810 feet), it is slightly taller than the nearby <a href="/wiki/JR_Central_Towers" title="JR Central Towers">JR Central Towers</a>.</p>
<p>Midland Square houses offices of many companies including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toyota_Motor_Corporation" title="Toyota Motor Corporation">Toyota Motor Corporation</a>, Towa Real Estate and <a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Shimbun" title="Mainichi Shimbun">Mainichi Shimbun</a>. It features a shopping center with 60 name-brand stores, two automobile showrooms and a cinema. It also holds the record for the highest open-air observation deck in Japan. Also of note are the unusual double-floored elevators, which take only 40 seconds to rise to the top.</p>
<p>The name of the building derives from the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%ABbu_region" title="Chūbu region">Chūbu region</a> (which means "central region") of which Nagoya is the capital.</p>
