<p><b>Miran Pavlin</b> (born 8 October 1971 in <a href="/wiki/Kranj" title="Kranj">Kranj</a>) is a retired <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slovenian_people" title="Slovenian people">Slovenian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miran_Pavlin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He played for clubs such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NK_Olimpija_Ljubljana_(defunct)" title="NK Olimpija Ljubljana (defunct)">Olimpija</a>, <a href="/wiki/SC_Freiburg" title="SC Freiburg">SC Freiburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olympiakos_Nicosia" title="Olympiakos Nicosia">Olympiakos Nicosia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APOEL_F.C." title="APOEL F.C.">APOEL</a>, <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a> and <a href="/wiki/NK_Olimpija_Ljubljana_(2005)" title="NK Olimpija Ljubljana (2005)">Olimpija Ljubljana</a>.</p>
<p>After the original NK Olimpija went bankrupt in 2005, he joined the newly formed NK Bežigrad and successfully took them back to the top tier in record time. After a dispute in 2009, he left the club and joined <a href="/wiki/FC_Koper" title="FC Koper">FC Koper</a> on a combined player-<a href="/wiki/Director_of_football" title="Director of football">director of football</a> contract. In his first season with Koper, he won the Slovenian PrvaLiga. His second season there started poorly – after a resounding 5–1 defeat away to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NK_Dinamo_Zagreb" title="NK Dinamo Zagreb">NK Dinamo Zagreb</a> in <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> qualifiers, he announced his immediate retirement, only to return to the Koper team a week later for a single league match against <a href="/wiki/NK_Rudar_Velenje" title="NK Rudar Velenje">NK Rudar Velenje</a>. Soon, after another dispute with club leadership regarding player signings and departures, he left the club altogether.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miran_Pavlin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pavlin made 63 appearances for the senior <a href="/wiki/Slovenia_national_football_team" title="Slovenia national football team">Slovenia national football team</a> between 1994 and 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was a participant at the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2000" title="UEFA Euro 2000">UEFA Euro 2000</a>. During the second leg of Slovenia's Euro 2000 play-off against <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_football_team" title="Ukraine national football team">Ukraine</a>, it was Pavlin's goal which secured a 1–1 draw and an aggregate victory to send Slovenia to their first major tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
