<p>Year <b>311</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">CCCXI</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Valerius and Maximinus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1064 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 311 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=311&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=311&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=311&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_5" title="May 5">May 5</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Galerius" title="Galerius">Galerius</a> declares on his deathbed religious freedom and issues his <a href="/wiki/Edict_of_Toleration_by_Galerius" title="Edict of Toleration by Galerius">Edict of Toleration</a>, ending <a href="/wiki/Persecution_of_Christians" title="Persecution of Christians">persecution of Christians</a> in the Eastern part of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>. He dies, age 51, from a gruesome disease, possibly <a href="/wiki/Colorectal_cancer" title="Colorectal cancer">bowel cancer</a> or <a href="/wiki/Fournier_gangrene" title="Fournier gangrene">Fournier gangrene</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maximinus_II_(Daia)" title="Maximinus II (Daia)">Maximinus II</a> divides the Eastern Empire between co-emperor <a href="/wiki/Licinius" title="Licinius">Licinius</a>, and mobilises his army for a campaign in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Minor" title="Asia Minor">Asia Minor</a>. He recommences the persecution of <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christians</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Maxentius" title="Maxentius">Maxentius</a>, Roman <a href="/wiki/Usurper" title="Usurper">usurper</a>, reconquers the African provinces from <a href="/wiki/Domitius_Alexander" title="Domitius Alexander">Domitius Alexander</a>.</li>
<li>Maxentius builds the <a href="/wiki/Circus_of_Maxentius" title="Circus of Maxentius">Circus of Maxentius</a> near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Via_Appia" title="Via Appia">Via Appia</a>. The circus is 513 meters long and 91 meters wide, and offers an accommodation to some 10,000 people.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_3" title="December 3">December 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/Diocletian" title="Diocletian">Diocletianus</a> dies at his <a href="/wiki/Diocletian%27s_Palace" title="Diocletian's Palace">palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Split,_Croatia" title="Split, Croatia">Split</a> (<a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>). Possibly he commits <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=311&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Emperor_Huai_of_Jin" title="Emperor Huai of Jin">Jin Huaidi</a>, emperor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jin_Dynasty_(265-420)" title="Jin Dynasty (265-420)">Jin Dynasty</a>, is captured at <a href="/wiki/Luoyang" title="Luoyang">Luoyang</a>.
