<p><i><b>Abeliophyllum</b></i>, also <b>miseonnamu</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> <b>Korean abeliophyllum</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> <b>white forsythia</b>, or <b>Korean abelialeaf</b>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monotype_(biology)" title="Monotype (biology)">monotypic</a> genus of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plants</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Olive" title="Olive">olive</a> family, <a href="/wiki/Oleaceae" title="Oleaceae">Oleaceae</a>. It consists of one species, <i><b>Abeliophyllum distichum</b></i> Nakai, <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>, where it is <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered</a> in the wild, occurring at only seven sites. It is related to <i><a href="/wiki/Forsythia" title="Forsythia">Forsythia</a></i>, but differs in having white, not yellow, flowers.</p>
<p>It is a <a href="/wiki/Deciduous" title="Deciduous">deciduous</a> <a href="/wiki/Shrub" title="Shrub">shrub</a> growing to 1â2 m tall. The <a href="/wiki/Leaf" title="Leaf">leaves</a> are opposite, simple, 6â10Â cm long and 3-4.5Â cm wide, pubescent both above and below. The <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">flowers</a> are produced in early spring before the new leaves appear; they are white and fragrant, about 1Â cm long, with a four-lobed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corolla_(flower)" title="Corolla (flower)">corolla</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a> is a round, winged <a href="/wiki/Samara_(fruit)" title="Samara (fruit)">samara</a> 2â3Â cm diameter.</p>
<p>It is cultivated as an <a href="/wiki/Ornamental_plant" title="Ornamental plant">ornamental plant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation_status">Conservation status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abeliophyllum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Conservation status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1998, a review of the plant's conservation status found that "it is close to extinction and qualifies for the IUCN Category of 'Critically Endangered', indicating a high risk of extinction in the near future.";<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kim_1998_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kim_1998-2">[2]</a></sup> but as of 2009 it has not yet been formally assessed for the <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">IUCN Red List</a>.</p>
<p>In 2015, IUCN classed the species as endangered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
