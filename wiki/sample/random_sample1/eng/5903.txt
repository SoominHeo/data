<p><b>Michelle Suzanne Ruff</b> (born September 22) is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voice_actor" title="Voice actor">voice actress</a>. She is known for her work in <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> and <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video games</a>. In her early career in voice work, she used her mother's name, Georgette Rose, as a pseudonym.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digipedia_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-digipedia-2">[2]</a></sup> Some of her notable roles include: <a href="/wiki/Chi_(Chobits)" title="Chi (Chobits)">Chi</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Chobits" title="Chobits">Chobits</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Rukia_Kuchiki" title="Rukia Kuchiki">Rukia Kuchiki</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Bleach_(anime)" title="Bleach (anime)">Bleach</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Ai_Yori_Aoshi_characters#Aoi_Sakuraba_.28Honjo.29" title="List of Ai Yori Aoshi characters">Aoi Sakuraba</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Ai_Yori_Aoshi" title="Ai Yori Aoshi">Ai Yori Aoshi</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Rave_Master_characters#Elie" title="List of Rave Master characters">Elie</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Rave_Master" title="Rave Master">Rave Master</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yoko_Littner" title="Yoko Littner">Yoko Littner</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Gurren_Lagann" title="Gurren Lagann">Gurren Lagann</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Yuki_Nagato" title="Yuki Nagato">Yuki Nagato</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Melancholy_of_Haruhi_Suzumiya" title="The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya">The Melancholy of Haruhi Suzumiya</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Naka-kon.com_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Naka-kon.com-5">[5]</a></sup> In the more recent <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil" title="Resident Evil">Resident Evil</a></i> games, she voices <a href="/wiki/Jill_Valentine" title="Jill Valentine">Jill Valentine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-revil_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-revil-6">[6]</a></sup> She also voices Cream the Rabbit in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sonic_the_Hedgehog_(series)" title="Sonic the Hedgehog (series)">Sonic the Hedgehog</a></i> video games.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelle_Ruff&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ruff grew up in Michigan and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Michigan_State_University" title="Michigan State University">Michigan State University</a>. While she was there, she worked at a talent agency and attended a radio audition, which marked her first venture into the voiceover business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-omnitude_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-omnitude-7">[7]</a></sup> In Chicago, she studied with <a href="/wiki/The_Second_City" title="The Second City">Second City</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Players_Workshop" title="The Players Workshop">Players Workshop</a> and <a href="/wiki/IO_Theater" title="IO Theater">Improv Olympic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digipedia_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-digipedia-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-resumevo_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-resumevo-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>After moving to Los Angeles, she worked with some directors on looping and voice work for films and TV shows. In an <i>Anime Dream</i> interview, Ruff credits <a href="/wiki/Richard_Epcar" title="Richard Epcar">Richard Epcar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Kramer_(actor)" title="Steve Kramer (actor)">Steve Kramer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Sorich" title="Michael Sorich">Michael Sorich</a> for training her to dub anime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animedream_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-animedream-9">[9]</a></sup> In the <i>Digimon</i> series, she was referred by director <a href="/wiki/Mary_Elizabeth_McGlynn" title="Mary Elizabeth McGlynn">Mary Elizabeth McGlynn</a> to audition for Lopmon and Antylamon in the third season, and in the fourth season she landed the lead part of Zoe. Ruff said that it was her first show that made it to TV, and a show that let her "work my acting muscle".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digipedia_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-digipedia-2">[2]</a></sup> She played tomboy-ish characters <a href="/wiki/Carried_by_the_Wind:_Tsukikage_Ran#Meow" title="Carried by the Wind: Tsukikage Ran">Miyao</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Carried_by_the_Wind:_Tsukikage_Ran" title="Carried by the Wind: Tsukikage Ran">Carried by the Wind: Tsukikage Ran</a></i> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiki_Rosita" title="Kiki Rosita">Kiki Rosita</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Mobile_Suit_Gundam:_The_08th_MS_Team" title="Mobile Suit Gundam: The 08th MS Team">Mobile Suit Gundam: The 08th MS Team</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animedream_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-animedream-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, Ruff received two nominations for the <a href="/wiki/American_Anime_Awards" title="American Anime Awards">American Anime Awards</a>, one for Best Actress for her work in <i>Bleach</i> and <i>Lupin the 3rd</i>, and one for Best Actress in a Comedy for <i>Lupin the 3rd</i>, but lost to <a href="/wiki/Mary_Elizabeth_McGlynn" title="Mary Elizabeth McGlynn">Mary Elizabeth McGlynn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Debi_Derryberry" title="Debi Derryberry">Debi Derryberry</a>, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Anime_News_Network_2007-02-07_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Anime_News_Network_2007-02-07-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2009, the Society for the Promotion of Japanese Animation (SPJA; whom organized the <a href="/wiki/Anime_Expo" title="Anime Expo">Anime Expo</a>) gave her an SPJA Industry Award for Best Voice Actress (English) for her work as Rukia Kukichi in the <i>Bleach</i> movie <i>Memories of Nobody</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
