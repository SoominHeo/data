<p><b>Michelle Stafford</b> (born September 14, 1965) is an American actress, screenwriter and producer. She is perhaps best known for her role as <a href="/wiki/Phyllis_Summers" title="Phyllis Summers">Phyllis Summers</a> on the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> daytime soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i>, for which she has won two <a href="/wiki/Daytime_Emmy_Award" title="Daytime Emmy Award">Daytime Emmy Awards</a>. In 2013, Stafford created and starred in her own comedy <a href="/wiki/Web_series" title="Web series">web series</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Stafford_Project" title="The Stafford Project">The Stafford Project</a></i>. She currently stars as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nina_Clay" title="Nina Clay">Nina Clay</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> daytime soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/General_Hospital" title="General Hospital">General Hospital</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelle_Stafford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1990 Stafford was cast in <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Tribes_(TV_series)" title="Tribes (TV series)">Tribes</a></i>. The show was canceled after 3-month airing on Fox.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She later appeared in two plays directed by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Durning" title="Charles Durning">Charles Durning</a> in Los Angeles stage, and co-starred in several small films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 1994, Stafford began what was supposed to be a short term role of <a href="/wiki/Phyllis_Summers" title="Phyllis Summers">Phyllis Summers</a> on the American <a href="/wiki/CBS_Daytime" title="CBS Daytime">CBS Daytime</a> <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i>; however, her work impressed the producers and she was placed on contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Michelle_Stafford_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Michelle_Stafford-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MS_Bio_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-MS_Bio-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1997, Stafford left the series to pursue other career opportunities, and she was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Sandra_Nelson" title="Sandra Nelson">Sandra Nelson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1997 she was cast in <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Spelling" title="Aaron Spelling">Aaron Spelling</a> primetime soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Pacific_Palisades_(TV_series)" title="Pacific Palisades (TV series)">Pacific Palisades</a></i> as lead character, but show was canceled after 13 episodes. She later guest-starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Two_Guys_and_a_Girl" title="Two Guys and a Girl">Two Guys and a Girl</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Diagnosis:_Murder" title="Diagnosis: Murder">Diagnosis: Murder</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/JAG_(TV_series)" title="JAG (TV series)">JAG</a></i>. In film, Stafford appeared in 1999 thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Double_Jeopardy_(1999_film)" title="Double Jeopardy (1999 film)">Double Jeopardy</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Judd" title="Ashley Judd">Ashley Judd</a>.</p>
<p>In July 2000, Stafford was brought back to the soap opera by former head writer <a href="/wiki/Kay_Alden" title="Kay Alden">Kay Alden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Soapcentral.com_2000-07-14_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Soapcentral.com_2000-07-14-7">[7]</a></sup> From December 2006 to February 2007, Stafford played dual roles on <i>Y&amp;R</i>, portraying <a href="/wiki/Sheila_Carter" title="Sheila Carter">Sheila Carter</a>, formerly played by <a href="/wiki/Kimberlin_Brown" title="Kimberlin Brown">Kimberlin Brown</a> (Sheila had <a href="/wiki/Plastic_surgery" title="Plastic surgery">plastic surgery</a> to look like Phyllis).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2272007sow_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-2272007sow-8">[8]</a></sup> In May 2013, it was announced that Stafford would be exiting the soap opera after sixteen years in the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Stafford later confirmed that her final scenes would air sometime that August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Stafford exited the series on August 2, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stafford_Done_SON_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stafford_Done_SON-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004, Stafford was a <a href="/wiki/Hockey" title="Hockey">hockey</a> correspondent for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_Damn_Sports_Show_Period" title="The Best Damn Sports Show Period">The Best Damn Sports Show Period</a></i>. Stafford starred in Lifetime movie <i>Like Mother, Like Daughter</i> in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2007, Stafford appeared on <a href="/wiki/Tyra_Banks" title="Tyra Banks">Tyra Banks</a>' talk show to help Tyra through "soap opera school."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2007sow_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-2007sow-13">[13]</a></sup> In 2012 she appeared as Lauren on the web series <i>The Grove</i>, created by <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Chappell" title="Crystal Chappell">Crystal Chappell</a>. She also guest-starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Frasier" title="Frasier">Frasier</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Ringer_(TV_series)" title="Ringer (TV series)">Ringer</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> She subsequently launched her web series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Stafford_Project" title="The Stafford Project">The Stafford Project</a></i>, based on her personal life and struggles, with the first episode premiering on her YouTube Channel on August 5, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The series received positive reviews from critics. Michael Logan of <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i> appreciated the web series, calling it "terrific and audacious" and saying that "there's never a dull moment in the life of Michelle Stafford".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TG_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-TG-16">[16]</a></sup> Jamey Giddens of the entertainment website <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zap2it" title="Zap2it">Zap2it</a> said that Stafford is "as viable and fiery as ever", calling <i>The Stafford Project</i> "insanely-funny".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Stafford returned to daytime television with the role of Nina Clay, the wife of Dr. Silas Clay (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Easton" title="Michael Easton">Michael Easton</a>) on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> soap opera, <i><a href="/wiki/General_Hospital" title="General Hospital">General Hospital</a></i>. Stafford’s long-rumored addition to the cast was made official when her character Nina, the presumed-dead wife of Silas, suddenly appeared at the very end of the episode on May 1, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In 2015, she co-starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Tom_Sizemore" title="Tom Sizemore">Tom Sizemore</a> in the independent film <i>Durant's Never Closes</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Stafford launched a skincare product line called Skin Nation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TorontoSunSkin_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-TorontoSunSkin-22">[22]</a></sup> The products are said to be organic, natural, gluten free, vegan and cruelty free.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TorontoSunSkin_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-TorontoSunSkin-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelle_Stafford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stafford was born in 1965,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> in Chicago, Illinois. Her parents moved her and her older sister, Janine, to Montrose, California, where she was raised. After graduating from high school, Stafford chased after her dream of modeling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Stafford welcomed her first child, daughter Natalia Scout Lee, via her embryo and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gestational_surrogacy" title="Gestational surrogacy">gestational surrogate</a> on Monday, December 21, 2009 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In October 2015, Stafford welcomed her second child, a son Jameson Jones Lee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
