<p><b>Michelle Keegan</b> (born 3 June 1987<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Who_is_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Who_is-1">[1]</a></sup>) is an English actress. Keegan is known for portraying <a href="/wiki/Tina_McIntyre" title="Tina McIntyre">Tina McIntyre</a> in the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a></i> between 2008 and 2014. In 2015, Keegan played the role of Tracy in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Lies" title="Ordinary Lies">Ordinary Lies</a></i>. In 2016 she played Corporal Georgie Lane in <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> hit drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Our_Girl" title="Our Girl">Our Girl</a></i> taking over the lead role from <i><a href="/wiki/EastEnders" title="EastEnders">EastEnders</a></i> actress <a href="/wiki/Lacey_Turner" title="Lacey Turner">Lacey Turner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She was awarded <a href="/wiki/FHM%27s_100_Sexiest_Women_(UK)" title="FHM's 100 Sexiest Women (UK)">FHM "Sexiest Woman in The World"</a> in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelle_Keegan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Keegan was born in <a href="/wiki/Stockport" title="Stockport">Stockport, Greater Manchester</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Who_is_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Who_is-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Her father is <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a>, while her <a href="/wiki/Spaniards" title="Spaniards">Spanish</a> maternal grandmother hailed from <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/St_Patrick%27s_Roman_Catholic_High_School" title="St Patrick's Roman Catholic High School">St Patrick's RC High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Salford,_Greater_Manchester" title="Salford, Greater Manchester">Salford</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-modest_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-modest-6">[6]</a></sup> and later the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_School_of_Acting" title="Manchester School of Acting">Manchester School of Acting</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sun1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sun1-7">[7]</a></sup> She worked at <a href="/wiki/Selfridges" title="Selfridges">Selfridges</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Trafford_Centre" title="Trafford Centre">Trafford Centre</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and as a check-in agent at <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport" title="Manchester Airport">Manchester Airport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVNZ_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVNZ-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelle_Keegan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In late 2007, in only her second audition, Keegan was offered the chance to play the part of <a href="/wiki/Tina_McIntyre" title="Tina McIntyre">Tina McIntyre</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a></i>, and accepted the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVNZ_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-TVNZ-9">[9]</a></sup> She left the show in May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In 2009, she went on <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Mulhern" title="Stephen Mulhern">Stephen Mulhern</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Anonymous_(TV_series)" title="Anonymous (TV series)">Anonymous</a></i>, a prank show on ITV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 14 June 2009, Keegan made her radio debut co-hosting the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> programme <i>The Official Chart</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVNZ_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-TVNZ-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Keegan was on the cover of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FHM_magazine" title="FHM magazine">FHM</a></i> magazine in January 2011, and placed number<span class="nowrap"> </span>30 and number<span class="nowrap"> </span>26 in their <a href="/wiki/FHM%27s_100_Sexiest_Women_(UK)" title="FHM's 100 Sexiest Women (UK)">100 Sexiest Women</a> poll for 2010 and 2011, respectively. She would then appear in <i>FHM</i> again in March 2013 and was placed at number four in the 2013 poll, and number one in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FHM_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FHM-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Keegan joined <i><a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a></i> in 2008, beating around 900 other people who were also trying out for the part. She played <a href="/wiki/Tina_McIntyre" title="Tina McIntyre">Tina McIntyre</a> until the character's death in late May 2014. Keegan's last episode on the soap was aired on 27 May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Keegan later flew out to <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to film a special film for <i>Coronation Street</i>, and starred in the 2009 <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Dwarf" title="Red Dwarf">Red Dwarf</a></i> Easter special. In June 2014, Keegan went on a European tour to perform as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tinkerbell" title="Tinkerbell">Tinkerbell</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Peter_Pan" title="Peter Pan">Peter Pan</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2015, she played the role of Tracy in the BBC television drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Ordinary_Lies" title="Ordinary Lies">Ordinary Lies</a></i>. On 20 June 2015, it was announced that Keegan would play the starring role in BBC One's drama <i><a href="/wiki/Our_Girl" title="Our Girl">Our Girl</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The same year, Keegan landed a guest role on the ITV2 comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Plebs_(TV_series)" title="Plebs (TV series)">Plebs</a></i> which aired in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In June 2016, she began filming the new <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_channel)" title="ITV (TV channel)">ITV</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Tina_and_Bobby" title="Tina and Bobby">Tina and Bobby</a></i>, based on Tina Dean's and <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Moore" title="Bobby Moore">Bobby Moore</a>'s relationship in which she played Tina;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> it was a three part series which aired throughout January 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelle_Keegan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Keegan was engaged to singer <a href="/wiki/Max_George_(singer)" title="Max George (singer)">Max George</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Wanted" title="The Wanted">The Wanted</a> after meeting him at one of the band's concerts in December 2010. They broke up in mid-2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> She began dating <a href="/wiki/Mark_Wright_(TV_personality)" title="Mark Wright (TV personality)">Mark Wright</a> in December 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 9 September 2013, it was confirmed the two were engaged<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and they married on 24 May 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wedding_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-wedding-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
