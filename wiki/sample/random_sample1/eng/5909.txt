<p><b>Michel Georges Jean Ghislain Preud'Homme</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> commonly known as <b>Michel Preud'homme</b>, is a former Belgian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a>, and coach of <a href="/wiki/Club_Brugge_KV" title="Club Brugge KV">Club Brugge</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Pro_League" title="Belgian Pro League">Belgian Pro League</a>.</p>
<p>He is considered one of the world's best and most consistent goalkeepers during his career;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> he was the first winner of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yashin_Award" title="Yashin Award">Yashin Award</a> as the best goalkeeper at the <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1994 FIFA World Cup">1994 World Cup</a>.</p>
<p>On club level, Preud'homme played for <a href="/wiki/Standard_Li%C3%A8ge" title="Standard Liège">Standard Liège</a>, <a href="/wiki/KV_Mechelen" title="KV Mechelen">Mechelen</a> and <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a>. With Mechelen, he won the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Cup" title="Belgian Cup">Belgian Cup</a> in 1987, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup_Winners%27_Cup_1987-88" title="UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1987-88">Cup Winner's Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Super_Cup" title="UEFA Super Cup">European Super Cup</a> in 1988 and the Belgian league title in 1989. He also won the <a href="/wiki/Ta%C3%A7a_de_Portugal" title="Taça de Portugal">Portuguese Cup</a> with Benfica in 1996. He retired as a player in 1999, aged 40. For <a href="/wiki/Belgium_national_football_team" title="Belgium national football team">Belgium</a>, Preud'homme was capped 58 times, from 1979 to 1995. Other than the 1994 World Cup, he also played in the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990</a> edition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Standard_Li.C3.A8ge">Standard Liège</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Standard Liège">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Preud'homme is a product of Standard Liège's youth system which he joined 10 years old in 1969. He was first called up to the first team in 1977. He made his senior debut on August 1977, after an early injury of the main goalkeeper Christian Piot and his successor John Paul Crucifix.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mechelen">Mechelen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mechelen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Preud'homme went to <a href="/wiki/KV_Mechelen" title="KV Mechelen">KV Mechelen</a> in 1986.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Benfica">Benfica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Benfica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Preud'homme moved to <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">Benfica</a> in 1994, becoming the first foreign goalkeeper in Benfica history. He made his debut on 21 August, against <a href="/wiki/S.C._Beira-Mar" title="S.C. Beira-Mar">Beira-Mar</a>. On 18 May 1996, Michel won his first trophy in Portuguese football as Benfica defeated Lisbon rivals <a href="/wiki/Sporting_Clube_de_Portugal" title="Sporting Clube de Portugal">Sporting</a> 3–1 in the Portuguese Cup final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-benfica_profile_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-benfica_profile-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>For his outstanding performances and almost impossible saves, he was nicknamed by Benfica fans "Saint Michel". On 10 August 1999, Preud'homme played his last match, an off season friendly against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bayern_Munchen" title="Bayern Munchen">Bayern Munchen</a>. After his retirement, at the age of 40, Preud'homme became Benfica's Director of International Relations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uefa_profile_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-uefa_profile-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Preud'homme made his senior debut on 2 May 1979 in a 0–0 draw with Austria in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1980" title="UEFA Euro 1980">UEFA Euro 1980</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1980_qualifying_Group_2" title="UEFA Euro 1980 qualifying Group 2">qualifying match</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Preud'homme served as third keeper behind <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marie_Pfaff" title="Jean-Marie Pfaff">Jean-Marie Pfaff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Theo_Custers" title="Theo Custers">Theo Custers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1980" title="UEFA Euro 1980">UEFA Euro 1980</a> as Belgium lost in the final against Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uefa_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-uefa-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>He represented the nation in two consecutive FIFA World Cups: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_1990" title="FIFA World Cup 1990">1990</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_1994" title="FIFA World Cup 1994">1994</a> – his last competition, where he was awarded with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yashin_Award" title="Yashin Award">Yashin Award</a> for best goalkeeper,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yashin_Award_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yashin_Award-9">[9]</a></sup> and was included in the <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_awards#All-Star_Team" title="FIFA World Cup awards">FIFA World Cup All-Star Team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94team_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-94team-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>He made his final appearance for Belgium on 17 December 1994 in 1–4 loss against Spain, in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1996" title="UEFA Euro 1996">UEFA Euro 1996</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1996_qualifying_Group_2" title="UEFA Euro 1996 qualifying Group 2">qualifying match</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A world-class, elegant, efficient, and consistent goalkeeper, Preud'homme was mainly known for his excellent shot-stopping abilities, quick reflexes, agility, strength, tenacity, handling, and his ability to come off his line to collect crosses, as well as his tendency to produce spectacular and decisive saves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Preud.27Homme_Had_the_Feeling_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Preud.27Homme_Had_the_Feeling-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_career">Managerial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Managerial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After his professional football career, Michel Preud'homme stayed at Benfica to become technical director. In September 2000, he suggested the club to attract <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mourinho" title="José Mourinho">José Mourinho</a> to become the head coach. At that time, Mourinho was just an interpreter at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Barcelona" title="F.C. Barcelona">F.C. Barcelona</a>. Preud'homme met Mourinho when he once visited Barcelona.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Standard_Li.C3.A8ge_2">Standard Liège</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Standard Liège">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Preud'homme has been the head coach of <a href="/wiki/Standard_Li%C3%A8ge" title="Standard Liège">Standard Liège</a> twice, the first time from December 2000 to May 2002 and the second time from August 2006 to the end of the 2008 season. After leaving his technical director duties at Benfica, Preud'Homme replaced <a href="/wiki/Tomislav_Ivi%C4%87" title="Tomislav Ivić">Tomislav Ivić</a> on 20 December 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On May 2002, he left his coach position and became Standard's sporting director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 30 August 2006, after Dutchman Johan Boskamp was sacked due to poor results, Preud'homme left his sporting director duties and became Standard's manager for the second time in his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> After Preud'homme's return, Standard Liège won in 2008 its first Belgian Championship in 25 years,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and it therefore came as a surprise when he was appointed manager of <a href="/wiki/K.A.A._Gent" title="K.A.A. Gent">Gent</a> for the 2008–09 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gent">Gent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Gent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Preud'homme moved to <a href="/wiki/K.A.A._Gent" title="K.A.A. Gent">K.A.A. Gent</a> on 27 May 2008. With Gent he finished second in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Pro_League" title="Belgian Pro League">Jupiler Pro League</a>, this was their highest place ever (together with the 1954–1955 season). He also won the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Cup" title="Belgian Cup">Belgian Cup</a>, it was the third time in the history of the club that they won that trophy.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Twente">Twente</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Twente">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 May 2010, it was confirmed that Preud'homme would replace <a href="/wiki/Steve_McClaren" title="Steve McClaren">Steve McClaren</a> as the head coach of <a href="/wiki/FC_Twente" title="FC Twente">FC Twente</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> despite the interest of <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AC_Milan" title="AC Milan">AC Milan</a>. On 31 July 2010, Preud'homme won his first trophy in Dutch football as Twente defeated <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> 1–0 in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johan_Cruijff-schaal_XV" title="Johan Cruijff-schaal XV">Johan Cruijff Schaal</a> match. In the last competition match of the season, Twente lost to Ajax and that meant the Dutch title went to Amsterdam and Twente became runners-up. On 8 May 2011, Twente defeated Ajax 3–2 after extra time in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_KNVB_Cup" title="2010–11 KNVB Cup">KNVB Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_KNVB_Cup#Final" title="2010–11 KNVB Cup">final</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/De_Kuip" title="De Kuip">De Kuip</a> in Rotterdam.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Al-Shabab_Riyadh">Al-Shabab Riyadh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Al-Shabab Riyadh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 13 June 2011 FC Twente confirmed on its website Preud'homme's departure to <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Shabab_Riyadh" title="Al-Shabab Riyadh">Al-Shabab Riyadh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> By the end of the transfer window, Preud'homme brought Brazilian central midfielder <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Menegazzo" title="Fernando Menegazzo">Fernando Menegazzo</a> and Uzbekistani midfielder <a href="/wiki/Server_Djeparov" title="Server Djeparov">Server Djeparov</a>.</p>
<p>On 10 September 2011, Al-Shabab won 3–1 at Al-Faisaly in Preud'homme's first <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Professional_League" title="Saudi Professional League">Saudi Professional League</a> game as manager. On 10 March 2012, he succeeding RSC Anderlecht coach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ariel_Jacobs" title="Ariel Jacobs">Ariel Jacobs</a> on winning the Guy Thys Award, the prize for the coach who most contributed to the image of his profession and football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guy_Thys_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guy_Thys-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 April 2012, after a 1–1 draw against Al-Ahli, Al-Shabab clinched the Saudi Professional League title after six years since they had won it for the last time. Shabab finishing the league undefeated, with 19 victories and 7 draws.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> At the end of the season, he was won the Saudi Arabia Manager of the Year award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arabia_award_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-arabia_award-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 May, Preud'homme was rewarded with a new contract extension, running until 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On 18 September 2013, he and Al-Shabab agreed to part ways, allowing him to sign for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Club_Brugge_K.V." title="Club Brugge K.V.">Club Brugge</a> one day later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_Brugge">Club Brugge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Preud%27homme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Club Brugge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Juan_Carlos_Garrido" title="Juan Carlos Garrido">Juan Carlos Garrido</a> was fired, Preud'homme was appointed head coach of <a href="/wiki/Club_Brugge_KV" title="Club Brugge KV">Club Brugge</a> on 19 September 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Six days later, he made his debut in a 1–0 win against KSV Oudenaarde. At the end of the season, Preud'homme signed a new contract to remain as Club Brugge manager till 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 11 February 2015, Preud'homme reached his third Belgian Cup Final, after eliminating rivals <a href="/wiki/Cercle_Brugge_K.S.V." title="Cercle Brugge K.S.V.">Cercle Brugge</a> 8–3 on aggregate. On 22 March 2015, he won that final against rivals <a href="/wiki/Anderlecht" title="Anderlecht">Anderlecht</a>. It was the first trophy for Club Brugge in eight years. At the end of the season, Preud'homme was voted <a href="/wiki/Belgian_professional_football_awards#Professional_Manager_of_the_Year" title="Belgian professional football awards">Belgian Professional Manager of the Year</a> for the second time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bestcoach_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-bestcoach-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Preud'homme started the new season losing the <a href="/wiki/2015_Belgian_Super_Cup" title="2015 Belgian Super Cup">2015 Belgian Super Cup</a> to <a href="/wiki/K.A.A._Gent" title="K.A.A. Gent">K.A.A. Gent</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Belgian_Cup" title="2015–16 Belgian Cup">Belgian Cup</a>, Brugge won 0–2 in the quarter-final against <a href="/wiki/K.V.C._Westerlo" title="K.V.C. Westerlo">Westerlo</a>. On 3 February 2016, they eliminated Gent on the <a href="/wiki/Away_goals_rule" title="Away goals rule">away goals rule</a> to reach the <a href="/wiki/2016_Belgian_Cup_Final" title="2016 Belgian Cup Final">Cup Final</a> for the second year in a row.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Brugge lost the match 2–1 to Standard Liège.</p>
<p>On 15 May 2016, after finishing first in the regular season, Preud'homme won the Jupiler Pro League with Club Brugge in a 4-0 win over Anderlecht. It took the club 11 years since their last victory in the Belgian competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> On 23 May, Preud'homme was voted Manager of The Year for the third time, the second consecutive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-manager_2016_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-manager_2016-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
