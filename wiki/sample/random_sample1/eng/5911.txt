<p><b>Michel-Jean Sedaine</b> (2 June 1719 – 17 May 1797) was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> dramatist and librettist, especially noted for his librettos for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Op%C3%A9ras_comiques" title="Opéras comiques">opéras comiques</a></i>, in which he took an important and influential role in the advancement of the genre from the period of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charles-Simon_Favart" title="Charles-Simon Favart">Charles-Simon Favart</a> to the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">Revolution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel-Jean_Sedaine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sedaine was born in Paris. His father, an architect, died when Sedaine was quite young and left no fortune to inherit, the young Sedaine therefore began life as a mason's labourer. He was at last taken as pupil by an <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> whose kindness he eventually repaid, by the help he was able to give to his benefactor's grandson, the painter <a href="/wiki/Jacques-Louis_David" title="Jacques-Louis David">David</a>. Meanwhile he had done his best to repair his deficiencies of education, and in 1750 he published a <i>Recueil de pièces fugitives</i>, which included fables, songs and pastorals.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writer_of_op.C3.A9ras_comiques">Writer of opéras comiques</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel-Jean_Sedaine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Writer of opéras comiques">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Le_diable_%C3%A0_quatre_-_Michel_Sedaine_-_NGO3-863.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1821" data-file-width="1720" height="233" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Le_diable_%C3%A0_quatre_-_Michel_Sedaine_-_NGO3-863.jpg/220px-Le_diable_%C3%A0_quatre_-_Michel_Sedaine_-_NGO3-863.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Le_diable_%C3%A0_quatre_-_Michel_Sedaine_-_NGO3-863.jpg/330px-Le_diable_%C3%A0_quatre_-_Michel_Sedaine_-_NGO3-863.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Le_diable_%C3%A0_quatre_-_Michel_Sedaine_-_NGO3-863.jpg/440px-Le_diable_%C3%A0_quatre_-_Michel_Sedaine_-_NGO3-863.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Le_diable_%C3%A0_quatre_-_Michel_Sedaine_-_NGO3-863.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A scene from <i>Le diable à quatre</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Sedaine's especial talent was, however, for light opera. He wrote <i>Le diable à quatre</i>, set mainly to <a href="/wiki/Vaudeville_(song)" title="Vaudeville (song)">vaudevilles</a> with additional music by <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois-Andr%C3%A9_Danican_Philidor" title="François-André Danican Philidor">Philidor</a>, Laruette and Baurans. First performed at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foire_Saint-Laurent" title="Foire Saint-Laurent">Foire Saint-Laurent</a> on 19 August 1756, it was produced numerous times with music by different composers and became one of the most performed comic operas in the latter half of the 18th century.</p>
<p>Other such works followed, including <i><a href="/wiki/Blaise_le_savetier" title="Blaise le savetier">Blaise le savetier</a></i> (1759) with music of Philidor; <i>On ne s'avise jamais de tout</i> (1761) and others with <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Alexandre_Monsigny" title="Pierre-Alexandre Monsigny">Pierre-Alexandre Monsigny</a>; <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aucassin_et_Nicolette_(opera)" title="Aucassin et Nicolette (opera)">Aucassin et Nicolette</a></i> (1780), <i><a href="/wiki/Richard_Coeur-de-lion_(opera)" title="Richard Coeur-de-lion (opera)">Richard Coeur-de-lion</a></i> (1784), and <i>Amphitryon</i> (1788) with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Ernest_Modeste_Gr%C3%A9try" title="André Ernest Modeste Grétry">André Grétry</a>.</p>
<p>Among his more important works in the genre were two set by Monsigny: <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Le_roi_et_le_fermier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Le roi et le fermier (page does not exist)">Le roi et le fermier</a></i> (1762), notable for its portrayal of royal recognition of common justice and its 3-act length, and <i><a href="/wiki/Le_d%C3%A9serteur" title="Le déserteur">Le déserteur</a></i> (1769), which included a through-composed finale with chorus.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_as_a_dramatist">Work as a dramatist</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel-Jean_Sedaine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Work as a dramatist">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sedaine's work in opéra comique attracted the attention of Diderot, and two plays of his were accepted and performed at the Théâtre Français. The first and longest, the <i>Philosophe sans le savoir</i>, was acted in 1765; the second, a lively one-act piece, <i>La gageure imprévue</i> in 1768. These two at once took their place as stock pieces and are still ranked among the best French plays, each of its class. Except these two pieces little or nothing of his has kept the stage.</p>

<p>He wrote two historical dramas, <i>Raymond V, comte de Toulouse ou L'épreuve inutile</i>, and <i>Maillard, ou Paris sauvé</i>.</p>
<p>Sedaine became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Acad%C3%A9mie_fran%C3%A7aise" title="Académie française">Academy</a> (1786),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-2">[2]</a></sup> and secretary for architecture of the fine arts division. He died in Paris.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel-Jean_Sedaine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sedaine may be regarded as the literary ancestor of <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_Scribe" title="Eugène Scribe">Scribe</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexandre_Dumas,_p%C3%A8re" title="Alexandre Dumas, père">Dumas</a>. He had the practical knowledge of the theatre, which enabled him to carry out the ideas of <a href="/wiki/Denis_Diderot" title="Denis Diderot">Diderot</a> and give him claims to be regarded as the real founder of the domestic drama in France.</p>
<p>His <i>Œuvres</i> (1826) contain a notice of his life by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Fran%C3%A7ois_Ducis" title="Jean-François Ducis">Ducis</a>.</p>
