<p><b>Michelin</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="/ʃ/ 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="/əl/ 'le' in 'bottle'">əl</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span> or <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="/tʃ/ 'ch' in 'china'">tʃ</span><span title="/əl/ 'le' in 'bottle'">əl</span><span title="/ɪ/ short 'i' in 'bid'">ɪ</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[miʃˈlɛ̃]</a></span>; full name: <span lang="fr"><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_en_commandite_par_actions" title="Société en commandite par actions">SCA</a> Compagnie Générale des Établissements Michelin</i></span>) is a French <a href="/wiki/Tire" title="Tire">tire</a> manufacturer based in <a href="/wiki/Clermont-Ferrand" title="Clermont-Ferrand">Clermont-Ferrand</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auvergne_(region)" title="Auvergne (region)">Auvergne</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Regions_of_France" title="Regions of France">région</a></i> of France. It is one of the three largest tire manufacturers in the world along with <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In addition to the Michelin brand, it also owns the <a href="/wiki/BFGoodrich" title="BFGoodrich">BFGoodrich</a>, Kleber, <a href="/wiki/Tigar_Tyres" title="Tigar Tyres">Tigar</a>, Riken, Kormoran and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Rubber_Company" title="United States Rubber Company">Uniroyal</a> (in North America) tire brands. Michelin is also notable for its Red and Green <a href="/wiki/Michelin_Guide" title="Michelin Guide">travel guides</a>, its roadmaps, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michelin_stars" title="Michelin stars">Michelin stars</a> that the Red Guide awards to restaurants for their cooking, and for its company mascot <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bibendum" title="Bibendum">Bibendum</a></i>, colloquially known as the Michelin Man.</p>
<p>Michelin's numerous inventions include the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Removable_tire&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Removable tire (page does not exist)">removable tire</a>, the pneurail (a tire for trains made to run on rails) and the <a href="/wiki/Radial_tire" title="Radial tire">radial tire</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tires_and_wheels">Tires and wheels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Tires and wheels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Michelin manufactures tires for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_shuttles" title="Space shuttles">space shuttles</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> aircraft, automobiles, heavy equipment, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motorcycle_tire" title="Motorcycle tire">motorcycles</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bicycle_tire" title="Bicycle tire">bicycles</a>. In 2012, the Group produced 166 million tires at 69 facilities located in 18 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1889 two brothers, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%89douard_Michelin_(born_1859)" title="Édouard Michelin (born 1859)">Édouard Michelin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Michelin" title="André Michelin">André Michelin</a>, ran a rubber factory in <a href="/wiki/Clermont-Ferrand" title="Clermont-Ferrand">Clermont-Ferrand</a>, France. One day, a cyclist whose pneumatic tire needed repair turned up at the factory. The tire was glued to the rim, and it took over three hours to remove and repair the tire, which then needed to be left overnight to dry. The next day, Édouard Michelin took the repaired bicycle into the factory yard to test. After only a few hundred metres, the tire failed. Despite the setback, Édouard was enthusiastic about the pneumatic tire, and he and his brother worked on creating their own version, one that did not need to be glued to the rim. Michelin was incorporated on 28 May 1889. In 1891 Michelin took out its first patent for a <b>removable pneumatic tire</b> which was used by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Terront" title="Charles Terront">Charles Terront</a> to win the world's first long distance cycle race, the 1891 <a href="/wiki/Paris%E2%80%93Brest%E2%80%93Paris" title="Paris–Brest–Paris">Paris–Brest–Paris</a>.</p>
<p>In the 1920s and 1930s, Michelin operated large rubber plantations in Vietnam. Conditions at these plantations led to the famous labour movement <a href="/wiki/Phu_Rieng_Do" title="Phu Rieng Do">Phu Rieng Do</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 1934, Michelin introduced a tire which, if punctured, would run on a special foam lining; a design now known as a <b><a href="/wiki/Run-flat_tire" title="Run-flat tire">run-flat tire</a></b> (self-supporting type).</p>
<p>Michelin developed and patented a key innovation in tire history, the 1946 <b><a href="/wiki/Radial_tire" title="Radial tire">radial tire</a>,</b> and successfully exploited this technological innovation to become one of the worlds leading tire manufacturers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jags.org_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-jags.org-8">[8]</a></sup> The radial was initially marketed as the "X" tire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It was developed with the front-wheel-drive <a href="/wiki/Citro%C3%ABn_Traction_Avant" title="Citroën Traction Avant">Citroën Traction Avant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Citro%C3%ABn_2CV" title="Citroën 2CV">Citroën 2CV</a> in mind. Michelin had bought the then-bankrupt <a href="/wiki/Citro%C3%ABn" title="Citroën">Citroën</a> in the 1930s. Because of its superiority in handling and fuel economy, use of this tire quickly spread throughout Europe and Asia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jags.org_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-jags.org-8">[8]</a></sup> In the U.S., the outdated bias-ply <a href="/wiki/Tire" title="Tire">tire</a> persisted, with market share of 87% in 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jags.org_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-jags.org-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1968, Michelin opened its first North American sales office, and was able to grow that market for its products rapidly; by 1989 the company had 10% market share for <a href="/wiki/Original_equipment_manufacturer#Automotive_parts" title="Original equipment manufacturer">OEM</a> tires purchased by American automobile makers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also in 1968, <a href="/wiki/Consumer_Reports" title="Consumer Reports">Consumer Reports</a>, an influential American magazine, acknowledged the superiority of the radial construction, setting off a rapid decline in Michelin's competitor technology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In the U.S., the radial tire now has a market share of 100%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jags.org_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-jags.org-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition to the private label and replacement tire market, Michelin scored an early <a href="/wiki/Original_equipment_manufacturer#Automotive_parts" title="Original equipment manufacturer">OEM</a> tire win in North America, when it received the contract for the 1970 <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Continental_Mark_III" title="Lincoln Continental Mark III">Lincoln Continental Mark III</a>, the first American car with radial tires fitted as standard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1989, Michelin acquired the recently merged tire and rubber manufacturing divisions of the American firms <a href="/wiki/Goodrich_Corporation" title="Goodrich Corporation">B.F. Goodrich Company</a> (founded in 1870) and Uniroyal, Inc. (founded in 1892 as the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Rubber_Company" title="United States Rubber Company">United States Rubber Company</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-10">[10]</a></sup> Uniroyal Australia had already been bought by <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> in 1980. This purchase included the <a href="/wiki/Norwood,_North_Carolina" title="Norwood, North Carolina">Norwood, North Carolina</a> manufacturing plant which supplied tires to the U.S. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Program" title="Space Shuttle Program">Space Shuttle Program</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Michelin also controls 90% of Taurus Tire in Hungary, as well as Kormoran,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> a Polish brand.</p>
<p>As of 1 September 2008, Michelin is again the world's largest tire manufacturer after spending two years as number two behind Bridgestone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Michelin produces tires in France, Spain, Germany, the USA, the UK, Canada, Brazil, Thailand, Japan, Italy and several other countries. On 15 January 2010, Michelin<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> announced the closing of its Ota, Japan plant, which employs 380 workers and makes the Michelin X-Ice tire. Production of the X-Ice will be moved to Europe, North America, and elsewhere in Asia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="MotoGP">MotoGP</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: MotoGP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Michelin participated in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MotoGP" title="MotoGP">MotoGP</a> from 1972 to 2008. They introduced radial construction to MotoGP in 1984, and multi-compound tires in 1994. They achieved 360 victories in 36 years, and from 1993 to 2006, the world championship had gone to a rider on Michelins.</p>
<p>In 2007, <a href="/wiki/Casey_Stoner" title="Casey Stoner">Casey Stoner</a> on <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> tires won the world championship in dominating fashion, and <a href="/wiki/Valentino_Rossi" title="Valentino Rossi">Valentino Rossi</a> and other top riders complained that Michelins were inferior. Rossi wanted Bridgestones for the 2008 season, but Bridgestone was reluctant to provide them; <a href="/wiki/Dorna_Sports" title="Dorna Sports">Dorna</a> threatened to impose a control tire on the series, after which Bridgestone relented.</p>
<p>In 2008, Michelin's tires continued to be perceived as being inferior to Bridgestone's<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, and Michelin committed errors of judgment in allocating adequate tires for some of the race weekends. <a href="/wiki/Dani_Pedrosa" title="Dani Pedrosa">Dani Pedrosa</a>'s team switched to Bridgestones in the midst of the season, a highly unusual move that caused friction between <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_Corporation" title="Honda Racing Corporation">Honda Racing Corporation</a> and their sponsor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Repsol_YPF" title="Repsol YPF">Repsol YPF</a>. Other riders also expressed concerns and it seemed that Michelin might not have any factory riders for the 2009 season, leading to rumours that Michelin would withdraw from the series altogether. Dorna and the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_Motocyclisme" title="Fédération Internationale de Motocyclisme">FIM</a> announced that a control tire would be imposed on MotoGP for the 2009 season and Michelin did not enter a bid, effectively ending its participation in the series at the end of 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Michelin have returned to MotoGP in 2016 as official tire supplier after Bridgestone's withdrawal from the series at the end of the 2015.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formula_One">Formula One</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Formula One">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Formula One tyres</a></div>
<p>Michelin first competed in the <a href="/wiki/1977_Formula_One_season" title="1977 Formula One season">1977 Formula One season</a>, when Renault started development of their <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbocharged</a> F1 car. Michelin introduced <a href="/wiki/Radial_tire" title="Radial tire">radial tire</a> technology to Formula One and won the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers' Champions">Formula One Drivers' Championship</a> with <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, before withdrawing at the end of 1984.</p>
<p>The company returned to Formula One in <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_season" title="2001 Formula One season">2001</a>, supplying the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Williams_F1" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> (renamed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renault_F1" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> in 2002), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prost_(racing_team)" title="Prost (racing team)">Prost</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> teams. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> joined F1 in 2002 with Michelin tires, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> also signed up with the company. Michelin tires were initially uncompetitive but by the 2005 season were totally dominant. This was partly because the new regulations stated that tires must last the whole race distance (and qualifying), and partly because only one top team (Ferrari) was running Bridgestones, and so had to do much of the development work. Michelin in contrast had much more testing and race data provided by the larger number of teams running their tires.</p>
<p>Following the debacle of the <a href="/wiki/2005_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2005 United States Grand Prix">2005 United States Grand Prix</a> where, because of safety concerns, Michelin would not allow the teams it supplies to race, Michelin's share price fell by 2.5% (though it recovered later the same day). On 28 June, Michelin announced that it would offer compensation to all race fans who had bought tickets for the Grand Prix. The company committed to refunding the price of all tickets for the race. Additionally, it announced that it would provide 20,000 complimentary tickets for the 2006 race to spectators who had attended the 2005 event.</p>
<p>Michelin has had a difficult relationship with the sport's governing body (the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile">FIA</a>) since around 2003, and this escalated to apparent disdain between the two parties during the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" title="2005 Formula One season">2005 season</a>. The most high-profile disagreement was at the United States Grand Prix and the acrimony afterwards. Michelin criticised the FIA's intention to move to a single source (i.e. one brand) tire from 2008, and threatened to withdraw from the sport. In a public rebuke FIA President <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> wrote <i>"There are simple arguments for a single tire, and if [Michelin boss Édouard Michelin] is not aware of this, he shows an almost comical lack of knowledge of modern Formula One"</i>. Another bone of contention has been the reintroduction of tire changes during pit-stops from 2006. Michelin criticised the move claiming <i>"this event illustrates F1's problems of incoherent decision-making and lack of transparency".</i></p>
<p>In December 2005, and as a result of the difficult relationship with the sport's governing body, Michelin announced that it would not extend its involvement in Formula One beyond the 2006 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> was then the sole supplier of tires to Formula One until the end of the 2010 season, with Pirelli providing tires for 2011.</p>
<p>The last race won on Michelin tires in Formula One was the <a href="/wiki/2006_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Japanese Grand Prix">2006 Japanese Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> benefitting after the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> engine of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> failed during the race. This gave Michelin a second consecutive <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors' Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> win, with the 2005 and <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" title="2006 Formula One season">2006</a>, after Bridgestone's seven-year winning streak, and brought to a total of four the number of titles for Michelin since this championship's inception back in the <a href="/wiki/1958_Formula_One_season" title="1958 Formula One season">1958 Formula One season</a>; Michelin's other titles were in the <a href="/wiki/1979_Formula_One_season" title="1979 Formula One season">1979</a>, and <a href="/wiki/1984_Formula_One_season" title="1984 Formula One season">1984 seasons</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Endurance_racing">Endurance racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Endurance racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Michelin is involved in endurance races such as the <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Le_Mans_Series" title="American Le Mans Series">American Le Mans Series</a>. In 2009 Michelin supplied tires for 41 of the 55 cars entered in Le Mans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In 2016 they provide the Audi, Porsche and Toyota LMP1 teams, as well as the AF Corse, BMW, Corvette, Ford Ganassi, Porsche and Risi teams in GTE-Pro / GTLM.</p>
<p>Michelin has also supplied tires in the <a href="/wiki/European_Le_Mans_Series" title="European Le Mans Series">European Le Mans Series</a>. The company is exclusive supplier of LMP3 since 2015.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rallying">Rallying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Rallying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/World_Rally_Championship" title="World Rally Championship">World Rally Championship</a>, Michelin has been supplier of the Audi, Citroën, Ford, Lancia, Mitsubishi, Peugeot, Toyota and Volkswagen factory teams. Michelin Group brand BFGoodrich represented the brand in 2006 and 2007. The company was absent from 2008 to 2010, when Pirelli was signed as exclusive supplier.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Active_Wheel">Active Wheel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Active Wheel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Active_Wheel" title="Active Wheel">Active Wheel</a></div>
<p>Active Wheel from Michelin includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/In-wheel_motor" title="In-wheel motor">in-wheel electric motors</a> and a motorised suspension to free up space in the front or rear of the vehicle. This model also eliminates the need for other notorious space hogs like transmissions and exhaust systems. The wheels already have a vehicle ready to receive them, the <a href="/wiki/Heuliez" title="Heuliez">Heuliez</a> Will from Opel, and are also expected to come standard on the <a href="/wiki/Venturi_Automobiles" title="Venturi Automobiles">Venturi</a> Volage sometime in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tire_retailer">Tire retailer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Tire retailer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tire retail in Europe with Euromaster and in the US with its wholly owned subsidiary TCI Tire Centers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tour_guides">Tour guides</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Tour guides">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Michelin_Guide" title="Michelin Guide">Michelin Guide</a></div>
<p>Michelin has long published two guidebook series, the Red Guides to hotels and restaurants, and the Green Guides for tourism. It now publishes several additional guides, as well as digital map and guide products. The <a href="/wiki/City_map" title="City map">city maps</a> in both the Red and the Green guides are of high quality, and are linked to the smaller-scale road maps.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maps">Maps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Maps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Michelin publishes a variety of road <a href="/wiki/Map" title="Map">maps</a>, mostly of France but also of other European countries, Africa, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> and the United States. They have recently embarked in e-commerce selling Michelin maps and guides directly to the public through, for example, their UK website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The Michelin roadmaps were reproduced in England for the Allied invasion during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. The Germans also reproduced in 1940 the 1938 edition Michelin maps for the invasion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_mapping">Online mapping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michelin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Online mapping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/ViaMichelin" title="ViaMichelin">ViaMichelin</a> is a wholly owned subsidiary of Michelin Group, and was started in 2001, to represent Michelin's digital mapping services. As of August 2008, ViaMichelin generates 400 million maps and routes per month on its main website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>ViaMichelin provides mapping and travel solutions for internet, mobile and satellite navigation products with street level coverage of Europe, USA, Australia, and parts of Asia and South America.</p>
