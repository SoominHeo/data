<p><b>Miss Universe 2000</b>, the 49th <a href="/wiki/Miss_Universe" title="Miss Universe">Miss Universe</a> pageant, was held on 12 May 2000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-local_date_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-local_date-1">[1]</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/Eleftheria_Indoor_Hall" title="Eleftheria Indoor Hall">Eleftheria Indoor Hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nicosia" title="Nicosia">Nicosia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lara_Dutta" title="Lara Dutta">Lara Dutta</a> of <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> was crowned by <a href="/wiki/Mpule_Kwelagobe" title="Mpule Kwelagobe">Mpule Kwelagobe</a> of <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> at the end of the event. Seventy-nine delegates competed in this year.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miss_Universe_2000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><span class="flagicon"><img alt="" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="900" data-file-width="1350" height="15" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/41/Flag_of_India.svg/23px-Flag_of_India.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/41/Flag_of_India.svg/35px-Flag_of_India.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/41/Flag_of_India.svg/45px-Flag_of_India.svg.png 2x" width="23"/> </span><a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>: <a href="/wiki/Lara_Dutta" title="Lara Dutta">Lara Dutta</a> competed at Miss intercontinental 1997 where she official won the crown and later participated in Miss Universe 2000 and she won the crown for india for 2nd time</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_notes">Other notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miss_Universe_2000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><b>Venezuela</b>, initially chose <a href="/wiki/Martina_Thorogood" title="Martina Thorogood">Martina Thorogood</a> to represent their country at both Miss Universe and <a href="/wiki/Miss_World_1999" title="Miss World 1999">Miss World 1999</a>. Miss Universe officials objected to this as Thorogood placed first runner-up at Miss World and there was a chance that she could become Miss World should the winner resign or lose her crown. A second, smaller pageant was held among delegates who had competed in previous Miss Venezuela competitions, and <a href="/wiki/Claudia_Moreno" title="Claudia Moreno">Claudia Moreno</a> was chosen to compete at Miss Universe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Venezuela_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Venezuela-2">[2]</a></sup> Moreno went on to place first runner-up. Both Venezuelan candidates placed second in their respective competitions to the candidates from <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a></li>
<li><b>Russia</b> initially chose Miss Russia 1997, Yelena Rogozhina for Miss Universe 2000, but her victory at the 1999 Miss Europe contest in Lebanon made her unable to compete at Miss Universe. The winner of Miss Russia 1999, Anna Kruglova, also was ineligible for Miss Universe due her being underage at the time. The organizers of the Miss Russia pageant decided to pick the Miss Russia 1999 first runner-up, Svetlana Goreva, for Miss Universe 2000.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Host_city">Host city</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miss_Universe_2000&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Host city">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nicosia" title="Nicosia">Nicosia</a> was announced as host city of the pageant on 1 July 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The country invested $3.5 million in the event, in the hope that the publicity would increase tourism, the island's main industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Conservative Cypriot church leaders protested the decision to hold the pageant on the island, claiming that millennium celebrations of the birth of Christ were more important and that the event was scandalous and would promote female nudity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
