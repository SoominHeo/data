<p><i><b>Mistresses</b></i> is an American <a href="/wiki/Mystery_fiction" title="Mystery fiction">mystery</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_(genre)" title="Drama (genre)">drama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> television series based on the 2008–10 <a href="/wiki/Mistresses_(UK_TV_series)" title="Mistresses (UK TV series)">U.K. series of the same name</a>, about the lives of four female friends and their involvement in an array of illicit and complex relationships. This adaptation was developed by K.J. Steinberg, and stars <a href="/wiki/Alyssa_Milano" title="Alyssa Milano">Alyssa Milano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rochelle_Aytes" title="Rochelle Aytes">Rochelle Aytes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yunjin_Kim" title="Yunjin Kim">Yunjin Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jes_Macallan" title="Jes Macallan">Jes Macallan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Esposito" title="Jennifer Esposito">Jennifer Esposito</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tabrett_Bethell" title="Tabrett Bethell">Tabrett Bethell</a> in the lead roles. The series was originally set to premiere on May 27, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> However, ABC announced that <i>Mistresses</i> would premiere on June 3, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-premiere_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 9, 2016, ABC canceled <i>Mistresses</i> after four seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_production">Development and production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistresses_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development and production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2012, ABC announced that it had <a href="/wiki/Green-light" title="Green-light">green-lighted</a> <i>Mistresses</i> with a direct-to-series order and a planned summer 2013 airdate. Thirteen episodes were ordered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-order_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-order-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-remake_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-remake-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HR2-7-12_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-HR2-7-12-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> K.J. Steinberg wrote the <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">pilot episode</a>, which had previously been under consideration for a pilot order for ABC’s fall 2012 schedule.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-order_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-order-8">[8]</a></sup> This is the second attempt by a network to import the British series to the U.S. In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> announced it was developing the series,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> but it never made it to air. The Lifetime version was also to have starred Aytes, alongside Milano's <i><a href="/wiki/Charmed" title="Charmed">Charmed</a></i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Holly_Marie_Combs" title="Holly Marie Combs">Holly Marie Combs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deadlineleads_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-deadlineleads-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 25, 2013, <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> renewed <i>Mistresses</i> for a <a href="/wiki/Mistresses_(U.S._season_2)" title="Mistresses (U.S. season 2)">second season</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> which aired from June 2 to September 1, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On September 30, 2014, ABC renewed the series for a third season with as production of the program being moved from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> to <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>. The season premiered on June 18, 2015, with a special two-hour premiere in a new Thursday timeslot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On September 25, 2015, ABC renewed <i>Mistresses</i> for a fourth season of 13 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mistresses_Season_4_Renewal_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mistresses_Season_4_Renewal-18">[18]</a></sup> In December 2015, <i>Mistresses</i> was selected for the California film and TV tax credit, relocating filming of the series back to Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistresses_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Casting announcements began in early March, with <a href="/wiki/Rochelle_Aytes" title="Rochelle Aytes">Rochelle Aytes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jes_Macallan" title="Jes Macallan">Jes Macallan</a> being the first actors cast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deadlineleads_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-deadlineleads-13">[13]</a></sup> Aytes portrays April, a widow and mother of one, while Macallan plays Josslyn, a real estate broker and the youngest of the women. <a href="/wiki/Yunjin_Kim" title="Yunjin Kim">Yunjin Kim</a> came on board the project in mid-March in the role of Karen, a psychiatrist who is upset over the death of a patient with whom she was involved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kim_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kim-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alyssa_Milano" title="Alyssa Milano">Alyssa Milano</a> completed the lead cast when she signed on to portray Savannah, a married lawyer and older sister to Josslyn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HR3-22-12_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-HR3-22-12-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 30, 2014, Milano announced that she would not be returning for the third season of the series as production would be later moved from Los Angeles to Vancouver in order to cut production costs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Milano_leaving_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Milano_leaving-22">[22]</a></sup> On February 10, 2015, it was confirmed that actress <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Esposito" title="Jennifer Esposito">Jennifer Esposito</a> would join the cast as the new co-lead Calista Raines, described as the creative director of a luxury fashion brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jennifer_Esposito_Joins_Cast_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jennifer_Esposito_Joins_Cast-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Esposito2_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Esposito2-23">[23]</a></sup> On September 25, 2015, the same day when ABC announced <i>Mistresses'</i>s fourth season renewal, Esposito also left the show after one season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mistresses_Season_4_Renewal_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mistresses_Season_4_Renewal-18">[18]</a></sup> On February 19, 2016, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Tabrett_Bethell" title="Tabrett Bethell">Tabrett Bethell</a>, who previously guest starred as Kate Davis in season two for one episode, would return in the remaining lead role during the fourth season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Milano_Still_Out.2C_Bethel_In_.28TVLine.29_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Milano_Still_Out.2C_Bethel_In_.28TVLine.29-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistresses_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistresses_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Mistresses</i> holds a score of 42 out of 100, on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, based on 16 negative reviews by critics from early in the first season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Brian Lowry of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> was quite optimistic about the series, stating: "A frothy prime time soap with a definite naughty streak, the program looks well suited to scratch an itch for daytime serial fans ABC abandoned." He went on to state: "Although the characters and situations largely mirror its inspiration, this U.S. version wisely seeks a somewhat cheekier and less somber tone."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Writing for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i>, Dorothy Rabinowitz likened the show to <i><a href="/wiki/Sex_and_the_City" title="Sex and the City">Sex and the City</a></i>, stating: "<i>Mistresses</i> carries very little hint of any complex lineage, give or take a few scenes that could, with ferocious effort, evoke memories of Carrie Bradshaw and company." Rabinowitz also stated that "the really delectable fun, the best of the sparkle, is to be found in the darkest plots, the looming catastrophes, which promise to come in an endless flow."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In less positive reviews, <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Daily_News" title="New York Daily News">New York Daily News</a>'</i> David Hinckley gave the premiere two out of five stars, stating that "ABC loves racy romantic dramas, but <i>Mistresses</i> is not its best work in the genre." He continued by saying that "the problem with <i>Mistresses</i> may lie with the expectations it has set."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Mary McNamara of the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> stated that executive producer K.J. Steinberg is "grasping the letter yet not the spirit of the thing, appears more interested in replicating plot from the British version rather than character or anything like nuance."</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratings">Ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistresses_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The premiere episode scored a 1.2 in the 18–49 age demographic and 4.40 million viewers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The first season ranked as the No. 2 scripted summer drama among adults 18-49, behind <i><a href="/wiki/Under_the_Dome_(TV_series)" title="Under the Dome (TV series)">Under the Dome</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> and averaged around 5 million viewers and a 2.0 18-49 rating, with DVR ratings factored in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcast">Broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistresses_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Mistresses</i> premiered on <a href="/wiki/CTV_Television_Network" title="CTV Television Network">CTV</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> on June 3, 2013, the same day it premiered in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The series premiered in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> on July 25, 2013, on <a href="/wiki/TLC_(UK_and_Ireland)" title="TLC (UK and Ireland)">TLC</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <i>Mistresses</i> premiered on September 30, 2013, on the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Network" title="Seven Network">Seven Network</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
