<p>The <b>Mitsubishi Fuso Aero King</b> (<a href="/wiki/Kana" title="Kana">kana</a>:三菱ふそう・エアロキング) is a series of <a href="/wiki/Multi-axle_bus" title="Multi-axle bus">heavy-duty</a> <a href="/wiki/Double-decker_bus" title="Double-decker bus">double-decker</a> <a href="/wiki/Coach_(bus)" title="Coach (bus)">coaches</a> built by <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Fuso_Truck_and_Bus_Corporation" title="Mitsubishi Fuso Truck and Bus Corporation">Mitsubishi Fuso Truck and Bus Corporation</a>.</p>
<p>The Aero King first went on sale in 1984, after being introduced at the 1983 <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Motor_Show" title="Tokyo Motor Show">Tokyo Motor Show</a>. It can be either built as an <a href="/wiki/Bus_manufacturing" title="Bus manufacturing">integral bus</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Bus" title="Bus">bus</a> <a href="/wiki/Chassis" title="Chassis">chassis</a>.</p>
<p>The engines that were used in the Fuso Aero Bus were the 8DC9 (turbocharged V-8, 380 PS or 280 kW, used between 1984–1995 for MU515TA/MU525TA), the 8M21 (NA V-8, 420 PS or 310 kW/430 PS or 320 kW, used between 1995–2005 for MU612) and the 6M70 (turbocharged L-6, 420ps, used between 2008-2010 for MU66JS)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
