<p><b>Tsutomu Miyazaki</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">宮﨑 勤</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Miyazaki Tsutomu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, August 21, 1962 – June 17, 2008)</span>, also known as <b>The <a href="/wiki/Otaku" title="Otaku">Otaku</a> Murderer</b> or <b>The Little Girl Murderer</b>, was a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Serial_killer" title="Serial killer">serial killer</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cannibal" title="Cannibal">cannibal</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Necrophile" title="Necrophile">necrophile</a> who abducted and murdered four young girls in <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Prefecture" title="Tokyo Prefecture">Tokyo Prefectures</a> from August 1988 to June 1989. His crimes included <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vampirism" title="Vampirism">vampirism</a> and preservation of body parts as trophies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp.google.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-afp.google.com-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsutomu_Miyazaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the trial, facts surrounding his parentage being the result of his father's incestual relationship with one of his sisters came out in testimony. Subsequently, Tsutomu's premature birth left him with <a href="/wiki/Deformity" title="Deformity">deformed</a> hands, which were permanently gnarled and fused directly to the <a href="/wiki/Wrist" title="Wrist">wrists</a>, which meant he needed to move his entire <a href="/wiki/Forearm" title="Forearm">forearm</a> in order to rotate the hand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charlest_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-charlest-4">[4]</a></sup> Due to his deformity, he was ostracized when he attended Itsukaichi Elementary School, and consequently kept to himself. He attended <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Meidai_Nakano_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Meidai Nakano High School (page does not exist)">Meidai Nakano High School</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%98%8E%E6%B2%BB%E5%A4%A7%E5%AD%A6%E4%BB%98%E5%B1%9E%E4%B8%AD%E9%87%8E%E4%B8%AD%E5%AD%A6%E6%A0%A1%E3%83%BB%E9%AB%98%E7%AD%89%E5%AD%A6%E6%A0%A1" title="ja:明治大学付属中野中学校・高等学校">ja</a>)</span> in <a href="/wiki/Nakano,_Tokyo" title="Nakano, Tokyo">Nakano, Tokyo</a>, and was a star student until his grades dropped dramatically. He was ranked 40 out of 56 in his class, and did not receive the customary admission to <a href="/wiki/Meiji_University" title="Meiji University">Meiji University</a>. Instead of studying <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and becoming a teacher as he originally intended, he attended a local junior college and studied to become a photo technician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charlest_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-charlest-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the mid-1980s, Miyazaki moved back into his parents' house near his father's print shop, sharing a room with his elder sister. Although Miyazaki's family was highly influential in <a href="/wiki/Itsukaichi,_Tokyo" title="Itsukaichi, Tokyo">Itsukaichi</a>, where his father owned a newspaper, Miyazaki expressed no desire to take over his father's job. After his arrest, Miyazaki would say that what he really craved was "being listened to about his problems" but believed that his parents, more worried about the material than the sentimental—"would have not heard [him]; [he] would've been ignored". In the same confession, he said that by this period in his life he had begun to consider <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a>.</p>
<p>Miyazaki was rejected by his two younger sisters, and felt he only received support from his grandfather. In May 1988, his grandfather died. This served to deepen his <a href="/wiki/Depression_(mood)" title="Depression (mood)">depression</a> and isolated him even further. In an attempt to "retain something from him", Miyazaki ate part of his grandfather's ashes. A few weeks later, one of his sisters caught him watching her while she was taking a shower. When she told him to leave, Miyazaki attacked her. When his mother learned of the incident and demanded that he spend more time working, and less time with his videos, he attacked her as well.</p>
<p>According to a high school classmate, Miyazaki suffered from an <a href="/wiki/Inferiority_complex" title="Inferiority complex">inferiority complex</a> due to the size of his penis and would not interact with older women. He would attend college tennis matches for the sole purpose of photographing the players, and would subsequently use them to <a href="/wiki/Masturbation" title="Masturbation">masturbate</a>. In 1984, at the age of 21, Miyazaki began to watch <a href="/wiki/Child_pornography" title="Child pornography">child pornography</a>, which was legal in Japan at that time.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Murders">Murders</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsutomu_Miyazaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Murders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Between August 1988 and June 1989, Miyazaki <a href="/wiki/Mutilation" title="Mutilation">mutilated</a> and killed four girls between ages four and seven, and <a href="/wiki/Child_sexual_abuse" title="Child sexual abuse">sexually molested</a> <a href="/wiki/Necrophilia" title="Necrophilia">their corpses</a>. He drank the blood of one victim and ate a part of her hand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> These crimes—which prior to Miyazaki's apprehension were named the "Little Girl Murders" and later the Tokyo/Saitama Serial Kidnapping Murders of Little Girls <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">東京・埼玉連続幼女誘拐殺人事件</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tōkyō Saitama renzoku yōjo yūkai satsujin jiken</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>—shocked <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Prefecture" title="Saitama Prefecture">Saitama Prefecture</a>, which had few crimes against children.</p>
<p>During the day, Miyazaki was, by all accounts, a mild-mannered individual. Outside of work, he randomly selected children to kill. He wrote to the families of his victims, sending them letters recalling the details of his murders. Police found that the families of the victims had something else in common: all received silent <a href="/wiki/Nuisance_call" title="Nuisance call">nuisance phone calls</a>. If they did not pick up the phone, it would sometimes ring for 20 minutes.</p>
<p>On August 22, 1988, one day after Miyazaki's 26th birthday, Mari Konno, a four-year-old girl, vanished while playing at a friend's house. After failed attempts to find her, Mari's father contacted the police. Miyazaki had led Mari into his black <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nissan_Langley" title="Nissan Langley">Nissan Langley</a> and abducted her. He drove westward of <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> and parked the car under a bridge in a wooded area. There he sat alongside the girl for a half-hour before murdering her. He then <a href="/wiki/Necrophilia" title="Necrophilia">engaged in sexual acts with the corpse</a> and left her corpse in the hills near his home. He took her clothes with him and departed. He allowed Mari Konno's corpse to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Decompose" title="Decompose">decompose</a> for a while before later returning to remove the hands and feet, which he kept in his closet. These were recovered upon his arrest. He charred her remaining bones in his furnace, ground them into powder, and sent them to her family in a box, along with several of her teeth, photos of her clothes, and a postcard which read: "Mari. Cremated. Bones. Investigate. Prove."</p>
<p>On October 3, 1988, Miyazaki was driving along a rural road when he spotted seven-year-old Masami Yoshizawa. He offered her a ride, and she accepted. He then drove to the same place he had killed Mari Konno, and killed Masami. He engaged in sexual acts with the corpse, and took the girl's clothes with him when he departed.</p>
<p>On December 12, 1988, four-year-old Erika Nanba was returning home from a friend's house when Miyazaki kidnapped her, forcing her into his car. He drove to a parking lot in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naguri,_Saitama" title="Naguri, Saitama">Naguri, Saitama</a>, forced her to remove her clothes in the back seat, and began to take pictures of her. After killing her, he tied her hands and feet behind her back, covered her with a bed sheet, and placed the body in his car's trunk. He disposed of the girl's clothes in a wooded area and left the body in the adjoining parking lot. Miyazaki sent a <a href="/wiki/Postcard" title="Postcard">postcard</a> to her family, assembled using words cut out of magazines: "Erika. Cold. Cough. Throat. Rest. Death."</p>
<p>On June 6, 1989, Miyazaki convinced five-year-old Ayako Nomoto to allow him to take pictures of her. He then led her into his car and murdered her. He covered the corpse with a bed sheet and placed her in the trunk of his car, taking the body to his apartment. He spent the next two days engaging in sexual acts with the corpse, taking pictures of it in various positions, and filming it.</p>
<p>When the body began to decompose, Miyazaki dismembered it, abandoning the torso in a cemetery and the head in the nearby hills. He kept the hands, from which he drank blood and ate part of them. Fearing that the police would find the corpse, he returned to the cemetery and the hills two weeks later and carried the remains back to his apartment, where he hid them in his closet.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arrest">Arrest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsutomu_Miyazaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Arrest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On July 23, 1989, Miyazaki attempted to insert a <a href="/wiki/Zoom_lens" title="Zoom lens">zoom lens</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Vagina" title="Vagina">vagina</a> of a schoolgirl in a park near her home and was apprehended by her father. After fleeing naked on foot, Miyazaki eventually returned to the park to retrieve his Toyota car, whereupon he was arrested by police who had responded to a call by the father. A search of Miyazaki's two-room bungalow produced 5,763 videotapes, some containing <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slasher_film" title="Slasher film">slasher</a> films (later used as reasoning for his crimes). Interspersed among them was video footage and pictures of his victims. He was also reported to be a fan of <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror films</a> of which he had a collection. Miyazaki, who retained a perpetually calm and collected demeanor during his trial, appeared indifferent to his capture.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/News_media" title="News media">media</a> called him "The <a href="/wiki/Otaku" title="Otaku">Otaku</a> Murderer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> His killings caused a <a href="/wiki/Moral_panic" title="Moral panic">moral panic</a> against <a href="/wiki/Otaku" title="Otaku">otaku</a>, accusing anime and horror films of making him a murderer. These reports were disputed. In <a href="/wiki/Eiji_%C5%8Ctsuka" title="Eiji Ōtsuka">Eiji Ōtsuka</a>'s book on the crime, he argued that Miyazaki's collection of pornography was probably added or amended by a photographer in order to highlight his perversity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Another critic, Fumiya Ichihashi, suspected the released information was playing up to public stereotypes and fears about otaku, as the police knew they would help cement a conviction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Miyazaki's father, facing mounting local criticism for raping his own daughter, resulting in the birth of such an "abomination", as quoted in his diary, refused to pay for his son's legal defense and committed <a href="/wiki/Suicide_in_Japan" title="Suicide in Japan">suicide</a> in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp.google.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-afp.google.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trial_and_execution">Trial and execution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tsutomu_Miyazaki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Trial and execution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The trial began on March 30, 1990. Often talking nonsensically, Miyazaki blamed his actions on "Rat Man", an <a href="/wiki/Alter_ego" title="Alter ego">alter ego</a> who Miyazaki claimed forced him to kill; he spent time during the trial drawing "Rat Man" in cartoon form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_District_Court" title="Tokyo District Court">Tokyo District Court</a> judged him still aware of the magnitude and consequences of his crimes and therefore accountable. He was sentenced to death on April 14, 1997. His death sentence was upheld by both the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_High_Court" title="Tokyo High Court">Tokyo High Court</a>, on June 28, 2001, and the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Japan" title="Supreme Court of Japan">Supreme Court of Justice</a> on January 17, 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>He described his serial murders as an "act of benevolence".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Child killer <a href="/wiki/Kaoru_Kobayashi_(murderer)" title="Kaoru Kobayashi (murderer)">Kaoru Kobayashi</a> described himself as "the next Tsutomu Miyazaki or <a href="/wiki/Mamoru_Takuma" title="Mamoru Takuma">Mamoru Takuma</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Miyazaki stated, "I won't allow him to call himself 'the second Tsutomu Miyazaki' when he hasn't even undergone a psychiatric examination."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Minister of Justice <a href="/wiki/Kunio_Hatoyama" title="Kunio Hatoyama">Kunio Hatoyama</a> signed his death warrant and Miyazaki was hanged on June 17, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The unusual swiftness of his execution as well as its timing soon after the <a href="/wiki/Akihabara_massacre" title="Akihabara massacre">Akihabara massacre</a> prompted questions regarding the two incidents;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Justice_(Japan)" title="Ministry of Justice (Japan)">Ministry of Justice</a> had no comment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ry%C5%ABz%C5%8D_Saki" title="Ryūzō Saki">Ryūzō Saki</a> said, "His trial was long" and that he was "not willing to criticize Hatoyama".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
