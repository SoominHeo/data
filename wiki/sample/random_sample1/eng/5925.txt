<p><b>Miyake-jima</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">三宅島</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, "Miyake Island")</span> is an inhabited volcanic island in the <a href="/wiki/Izu_Islands" title="Izu Islands">Izu archipelago</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Sea" title="Philippine Sea">Philippine Sea</a> approximately 180 kilometres (110 mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Honshu" title="Honshu">Honshu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> As with the other islands in the Izu Island group, Miyake-jima forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Fuji-Hakone-Izu_National_Park" title="Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park">Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miyake-jima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Miyake-jima-map.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="636" data-file-width="824" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Miyake-jima-map.jpg/220px-Miyake-jima-map.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Miyake-jima-map.jpg/330px-Miyake-jima-map.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Miyake-jima-map.jpg/440px-Miyake-jima-map.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Miyake-jima-map.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Map of Miyake-jima,<br/>
with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C5%8Cnoharajima" title="Ōnoharajima">Ōnoharajima</a> west south-west of it</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The island is a <a href="/wiki/Stratovolcano" title="Stratovolcano">stratovolcano</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granitic</a> composite cone, dating from the late <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a> period, between 10,000 and 15,000 years ago. The island has a roughly circular coastline, 38.3 kilometers in length, with an average diameter of eight kilometers. The highest elevation Mount Oyama <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">雄山</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Oyama-san</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is an active volcano with a height of 775.1 meters. The mountain has been recorded to have erupted numerous times throughout history, and is mentioned as far back as <a href="/wiki/Nara_period" title="Nara period">Nara period</a> written records. Over the past 500 years, it has erupted 13 times, including five times since the start of the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_period" title="Meiji period">Meiji period</a>. A lava flow in 1940 killed 11 people, and other eruptions occurred in 1962 and 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On July 14, 2000, Mount Oyama began another series of eruptions, and by September, the island was completely evacuated. After a four-year period of volcanic emissions, residents were allowed to return permanently on February 1, 2005. After the eruption, there has been a constant flow of <a href="/wiki/Sulfur_dioxide" title="Sulfur dioxide">sulfur dioxide</a> gas coming from Mount Oyama. The residents of the island are required to carry <a href="/wiki/Gas_mask" title="Gas mask">gas masks</a> with them at all times, but they do not need to be worn constantly. Alarms go off if there is a dramatic increase in the levels of toxic gases in the air.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Volcanic gas emissions from this volcano are measured by a <a href="/wiki/Multi-Component_Gas_Analyzer_System" title="Multi-Component Gas Analyzer System">Multi-Component Gas Analyzer System</a>, which detects pre-eruptive degassing of rising magmas, improving <a href="/wiki/Prediction_of_volcanic_activity" title="Prediction of volcanic activity">prediction of volcanic activity</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miyake-jima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Miyake-jima has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Cfa</i>) with very warm summers and mild winters. Precipitation is abundant throughout the year, but is somewhat lower in winter.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miyake-jima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island is administered by the Miyake subprefecture of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metropolis" title="Tokyo Metropolis">Tokyo Metropolitan</a> government. As of June 1, 2016, the population of the island was 2451. <a href="/wiki/Miyake,_Tokyo" title="Miyake, Tokyo">Miyake Village</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">三宅村</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Miyake-mura</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> serves as the local government of the island, and also encloses nearby tiny uninhabited <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C5%8Cnoharajima" title="Ōnoharajima">Ōnoharajima</a> west south-west of Miyake-jima.</p>
<p>Miyake village consists of the five traditional hamlets of Izu, Kamitsuki, Igaya, Ako and Tsubota, with Tsubota on the southeast coast as the administrative center.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miyake-jima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Miyake-jima is home to the rare endemic <a href="/wiki/Izu_thrush" title="Izu thrush">Izu thrush</a> (<i>akakokko</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The island is home to an unusually rich flora and fauna with several rare species of birds and animals, although its natural habitats are constantly under threat from human and volcanic activity. Underwater, the island is valued by divers for its coral reefs and marine fauna (including the dolphin population outside nearby <a href="/wiki/Mikura-jima" title="Mikura-jima">Mikura-jima</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Access">Access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miyake-jima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island is accessible by over-night ferry, the <i>Sarubia Maru</i> or the <i>Camellia</i>, which is operated by Tōkai Kisen. The ferry departs from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Takeshiba_Pier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Takeshiba Pier (page does not exist)">Takeshiba Pier</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Hamamatsuch%C5%8D_Station" title="Hamamatsuchō Station">Hamamatsuchō Station</a>, Tokyo at 22:30 and arrives at Miyakejima at 5:00.</p>
<p>There are flights connecting <a href="/wiki/Miyakejima_Airport" title="Miyakejima Airport">Miyakejima Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haneda_Airport" title="Haneda Airport">Haneda Airport</a> with an approximate flying time of 50 minutes. Flights are also operated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chofu_Airport" title="Chofu Airport">Chofu Airport</a>. The area is prone to high volume of sulfuric gas and flights had been suspended for almost eight years after the July 14, 2000 volcano eruption. Flights resumed during April 2008, after sulfuric gases in the air have dropped to levels below 0.2ppm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> There is also a helicopter that arrives via <a href="/wiki/Izu_%C5%8Cshima" title="Izu Ōshima">Izu Ōshima</a>. There are also two helicopter flights operated by Tokyo Island Shuttle which originate in <a href="/wiki/Aogashima" title="Aogashima">Aogashima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Izu_%C5%8Cshima" title="Izu Ōshima">Izu Ōshima</a> and fly to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toshima_Island" title="Toshima Island">Toshima</a>, Miyake-jima, <a href="/wiki/Mikura-jima" title="Mikura-jima">Mikura-jima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hachij%C5%8D-jima" title="Hachijō-jima">Hachijō-jima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aogashima" title="Aogashima">Aogashima</a>.</p>
