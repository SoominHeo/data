<p>From its southernmost point at Minnesund in <a href="/wiki/Eidsvoll" title="Eidsvoll">Eidsvoll</a> to its northernmost point in <a href="/wiki/Lillehammer" title="Lillehammer">Lillehammer</a> it is 117 km long. At its widest, near <a href="/wiki/Hamar" title="Hamar">Hamar</a>, it is 15 km wide. It is 365 km² in area and its volume is estimated at 56 km³; normally its surface is 123 metres above sea level, and its greatest depth is 468 metres. Its total coastline is estimated at 273 km, of which 30% is built up. Dams built on the distribuary of Vorma in 1858, 1911, 1947, and 1965 raised the level by approximately 3.6 metres in total. In the last 200 years, 20 floods have been registered that added 7 metres to the level of Mjøsa. Several of these floods inundated the city of Hamar.</p>
<p>The cities of <a href="/wiki/Hamar" title="Hamar">Hamar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gj%C3%B8vik" title="Gjøvik">Gjøvik</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lillehammer" title="Lillehammer">Lillehammer</a> were founded along the shores of the lake. Before the construction of railways past the lake, it was an important transport route. Today, aside from minor leisure boating and the steamship <i><a href="/wiki/Skibladner" title="Skibladner">Skibladner</a></i>, there is no water traffic on the lake. Most of its shores are dominated by rolling agricultural areas, among them some of the most fertile grainlands in Norway. The main train line, the <a href="/wiki/Dovre_Line" title="Dovre Line">Dovre Line</a> between <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trondheim" title="Trondheim">Trondheim</a>, goes along its eastern shore, making stops in Hamar and Lillehammer. The largest, and only, island is <a href="/wiki/Helg%C3%B8ya,_Hedmark" title="Helgøya, Hedmark">Helgøya</a>. Except for Helgøya, Mjøsa only contains small islets. The most interesting of these are <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Steinsholmen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Steinsholmen (page does not exist)">Steinsholmen</a>, with the ruins of a Middle Age citadel.</p>
<p>From the south <a href="/wiki/European_route_E6" title="European route E6">European route E6</a> runs along the eastern shore of the lake until the <a href="/wiki/Mj%C3%B8sa_Bridge" title="Mjøsa Bridge">Mjøsa Bridge</a> connects <a href="/wiki/Moelv" title="Moelv">Moelv</a> on the east with <a href="/wiki/Biri,_Norway" title="Biri, Norway">Biri</a> on the west.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fish">Fish</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mj%C3%B8sa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fish">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lake Mjøsa has 20 species of fish. Among the most common are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pike_(fish)" title="Pike (fish)">pike</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_perch" title="European perch">European perch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Common_roach" title="Common roach">common roach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grayling_(species)" title="Grayling (species)">greyling</a>, and the hundertrout, a <a href="/wiki/Brown_trout" title="Brown trout">brown trout</a> which can reach a weight more than 20 kg. Another common species is the European smelt, which is the most important baitfish for the predators. Historically, the most economically significant species is the <i>lågsild</i> (European cisco).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_name">The name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mj%C3%B8sa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Norse_language" title="Old Norse language">Norse</a> <i>Mjǫrs</i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proto-Norse" title="Proto-Norse">Proto-Norse</a> <i>*Merso</i>) must be very old. The meaning is, maybe, 'the bright/shiny one'. (If <i>*mer-</i> is related to the English word <i>mere</i> 'clear, bright', and <i>-so</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Suffix" title="Suffix">suffix</a>.)</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mj%C3%B8sa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1975, the <a href="/wiki/14th_World_Scout_Jamboree" title="14th World Scout Jamboree">14th World Scout Jamboree</a> was held on its shores. More than 17.000 <a href="/wiki/Scouting" title="Scouting">Scouts</a> from 91 countries took part.</p>
<p>In 1995, a Swedish team lifted a Halifax bomber that was shot down in World War II.</p>
