<p><b>Miyun District</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">密云区</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">密雲區</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Mìyún Qū</span></i>) is situated in northeast <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. It has an area of 2,227 square kilometres (860 sq mi) and a population of 460,800 (2010 Census). The district is divided into 2 subdistricts of the city of Miyun, 17 towns, and 1 ethnic rural township.</p>
<p>The Miyun urban area, centered on Gulou Subdistrict, has an estimated population of 124,500.<a class="external autonumber" href="http://harrysworldatlas.blogspot.com/2007/02/china-cn-beijing-metropolitan-province.html" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></p>
<p>The district's facilities include the Nanshan Ski Resort, one of the largest in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Miyun_Reservoir&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Miyun Reservoir (page does not exist)">Miyun Reservoir</a>, a major source of water for the city of Beijing, is also located in the county.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="No_Chinese.2C_China">No Chinese, China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miyun_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: No Chinese, China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>No Chinese, China</b> or <b>English Town, Miyun County, China</b> was a proposed <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English speaking</a> town in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miyun_County" title="Miyun County">Miyun County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> with the intention of attracting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tourists" title="Tourists">tourists</a> until December 2011. The site was expected to be at least 60 hectares of land situated northeast of Beijing according to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wang_Haichen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wang Haichen (page does not exist)">Wang Haichen</a>, county chief of Miyun County. "According to the plan, people can't talk in <a href="/wiki/Varieties_of_Chinese" title="Varieties of Chinese">Chinese</a> inside the walled-city".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The original plan would have required a "tourist <a href="/wiki/Passport" title="Passport">passport</a>" to enter the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNTV_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CNTV-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of December 2011, the <i><a href="/wiki/Global_Times" title="Global Times">Global Times</a></i> of China has reported that "... Miyun County announced on Saturday [December 17, 2011] that it quashed plans to build a 780,000-square meter English speaking only town." Furthermore, "...'[t]he project was meaningless, the investing company was probably trying to create land speculation by making the announcement,' said Chu Zhaohui".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Global_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Global-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Issues_and_controversy">Issues and controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miyun_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Issues and controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>Like <a href="/wiki/Disneyland" title="Disneyland">Disneyland</a>, the "English-language town" could be an interesting place, "but I don't think the town would help people improve their English. It is not an environment for learning foreign languages," Hou said.</p>
<p>Learning a language requires a series of social conditions, the most important of which is the exchange of feelings and ideas, Hou said, noting that the dialogue between buyers and sellers is far from adequate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNTV_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-CNTV-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The issues with such a plan are as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>An "English-only policy" would exclude much of the population who could not speak English</li>
<li>Resources in the city would be drained and not benefit the local community as <a href="/wiki/Varieties_of_Chinese" title="Varieties of Chinese">Chinese</a> would not be allowed to be spoken inside the city. Ironically, English learners in the city would not benefit because they cannot learn English without using Chinese</li>
<li>The plan would not be profitable as this would not be an effective way to learn English.</li>
<li>It demonstrates the "worship of foreigners" and discrimination against Chinese</li>
</ul>
<p>According to the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beijing_News" title="Beijing News">Beijing News</a></i>, the plan received "overwhelming criticism."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Investment">Investment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miyun_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Investment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the article, "... the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Linda_International_Investment_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Linda International Investment Group (page does not exist)">Linda International Investment Group</a>... had signed a 10 billion yuan investment during the 15th <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Beijing-Hong_Kong_Economic_Cooperation_Symposium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Beijing-Hong Kong Economic Cooperation Symposium (page does not exist)">Beijing-Hong Kong Economic Cooperation Symposium</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Global_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Global-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
