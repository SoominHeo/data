<p><i><b>The Mummy</b></i> is an upcoming American <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a>-<a href="/wiki/Adventure" title="Adventure">adventure</a> <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Alex_Kurtzman" title="Alex Kurtzman">Alex Kurtzman</a> and written by <a href="/wiki/Jon_Spaihts" title="Jon Spaihts">Jon Spaihts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_McQuarrie" title="Christopher McQuarrie">Christopher McQuarrie</a>. It is a reboot of <a href="/wiki/The_Mummy_(franchise)" title="The Mummy (franchise)"><i>The Mummy</i> franchise</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andres_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Andres-1">[1]</a></sup> and the first installment in the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Monsters_(2017_film_series)" title="Universal Monsters (2017 film series)">Universal Monsters</a> <a href="/wiki/Shared_universe" title="Shared universe">shared universe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The film stars <a href="/wiki/Tom_Cruise" title="Tom Cruise">Tom Cruise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sofia_Boutella" title="Sofia Boutella">Sofia Boutella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Annabelle_Wallis" title="Annabelle Wallis">Annabelle Wallis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jake_Johnson" title="Jake Johnson">Jake Johnson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Courtney_B._Vance" title="Courtney B. Vance">Courtney B. Vance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russell_Crowe" title="Russell Crowe">Russell Crowe</a>. The film is scheduled to be released on June 9, 2017.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Premise">Premise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mummy_(2017_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Premise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thousands of years ago, an ancient princess (played by <a href="/wiki/Sofia_Boutella" title="Sofia Boutella">Sofia Boutella</a>), whose destiny was unjustly taken from her, was mummified and entombed in an ancient tomb buried deep beneath the desert. She was awakened in the contemporary world, bringing with her a malevolent grudge that has grown over millennia and terrors that will defy all of humanity.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mummy_(2017_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On April 4, 2012, <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Studios</a> announced that they were developing the reboot of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mummy_(franchise)" title="The Mummy (franchise)">The Mummy</a></i> franchise, with <a href="/wiki/Jon_Spaihts" title="Jon Spaihts">Jon Spaihts</a> to write the film and <a href="/wiki/Sean_Daniel" title="Sean Daniel">Sean Daniel</a>, who produced the previous three films, returning as producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On May 1, 2012, Universal signed on with <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Orci" title="Roberto Orci">Roberto Orci</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Kurtzman" title="Alex Kurtzman">Alex Kurtzman</a> a two-year deal to produce the reboot through their <a href="/wiki/K/O_Paper_Products" title="K/O Paper Products">K/O Paper Products</a> banner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On September 24, 2012, it was announced that Universal had set <a href="/wiki/Len_Wiseman" title="Len Wiseman">Len Wiseman</a> to direct the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In December 2012, it was being said that <i>The Mummy</i> reboot will be different from the previous trilogy; it will be a completely new take on the mythology, and it will be set in modern day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On February 14, 2013, Universal set <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games_(film)" title="The Hunger Games (film)">The Hunger Games</a></i> writer <a href="/wiki/Billy_Ray_(screenwriter)" title="Billy Ray (screenwriter)">Billy Ray</a> to write a competitive draft for the reboot against Spaihts' written script.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On July 31, 2013, Wiseman left the project due to scheduling conflicts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On September 13, 2013, it was reported that <i><a href="/wiki/Mama_(2013_film)" title="Mama (2013 film)">Mama</a></i> director <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Muschietti" title="Andrés Muschietti">Andrés Muschietti</a> was in talks to direct the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andres_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Andres-1">[1]</a></sup> In October 2013, Orci spoke to <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a>, hinting that both <i>The Mummy</i> and <i>Van Helsing</i> reboots will have a <a href="/wiki/Shared_universe" title="Shared universe">shared universe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On May 6, 2014, Muschetti left the film due to creative differences, while Spaihts wrote the latest draft of the script, which reimagined <i>The Mummy</i> franchise in modern-day with new characters not seen in previous iterations and a protagonist imbued with a human personality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 16, 2014, Universal announced that they had tapped Kurtzman and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Morgan_(writer)" title="Chris Morgan (writer)">Chris Morgan</a> to develop all their classic film monsters, which include <a href="/wiki/Frankenstein%27s_monster" title="Frankenstein's monster">Frankenstein's monster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Count_Dracula" title="Count Dracula">Count Dracula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Larry_Talbot" title="Larry Talbot">The Wolf Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Creature_from_the_Black_Lagoon" title="Creature from the Black Lagoon">Creature from the Black Lagoon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Griffin_(The_Invisible_Man)" title="Griffin (The Invisible Man)">The Invisible Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bride_of_Frankenstein" title="Bride of Frankenstein">Bride of Frankenstein</a>, and The Mummy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-monster_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-monster-23">[23]</a></sup> The first film they will develop together will be <i>The Mummy</i>, for which they had already begun meetings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-monster_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-monster-23">[23]</a></sup> On July 30, Kurtzman was set to direct the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In mid-October 2015, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> revealed that the new <i>Mummy</i> film might be based on a female character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> On November 24, 2015, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Cruise" title="Tom Cruise">Tom Cruise</a> was reportedly in talks to play the lead role in the film as Tyler Colt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CruiseTalks_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-CruiseTalks-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JohnsonCast_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-JohnsonCast-9">[9]</a></sup> On December 8, 2015, <i>THR</i> reported that <a href="/wiki/Sofia_Boutella" title="Sofia id="cite_ref-Jan2016V_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Jan2016V-5">[5]</a></sup> On March 8, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Annabelle_Wallis" title="Annabelle Wallis">Annabelle Wallis</a> was cast in the film to play Jenny Halsey,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WallisCast_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-WallisCast-8">[8]</a></sup> and following her <a href="/wiki/Jake_Johnson" title="Jake Johnson">Jake Johnson</a> class="reference" id="cite_ref-KenzariCast_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-KenzariCast-14">[14]</a></sup> On April 15, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Courtney_B._Vance" title="Courtney B. Vance">Courtney B. Vance</a> was cast as Colonel Gideon Forster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CourtneyBVance_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-CourtneyBVance-10">[10]</a></sup> On May 4, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Deadline.com" title="Deadline.com">Deadline.com</a>, <i>THR</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> reported that <a href="/wiki/Russell_Crowe" title="Russell Crowe">Russell Crowe</a> was in talks for the role of <a href="/wiki/Dr._Jekyll_and_Mr._Hyde_(character)" title="Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde (character)">Dr. Henry Jekyll</a> after <a href="/wiki/Javier_Bardem" title="Javier Bardem">Javier Bardem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Redmayne" title="Eddie Redmayne">Eddie Redmayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Gordon-Levitt" title="Joseph Gordon-Levitt">Joseph Gordon-Levitt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hardy" title="Tom Hardy">Tom Hardy</a> were considered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crowe_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-crowe-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-twitter_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-twitter-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mummy_(2017_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> on the film began on April 3, 2016, in <a href="/wiki/Oxford" title="Oxford">Oxford</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> It also filmed in <a href="/wiki/Surrey" title="Surrey">Surrey</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Filming on the movie wrapped on July 17, 2016, in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Production then moved to <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a> for two weeks with principal photography on the film being completed on August 13, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Mummy_(2017_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Universal Pictures had previously scheduled the film to be released on March 24, 2017, but has now reportedly postponed the release to June 9, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jan2016V_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Jan2016V-5">[5]</a></sup> Before that, the film was also scheduled for several dates including April 22, 2016, and then June 24, 2016, when Universal announced April 22 for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Huntsman:_Winter%27s_War" title="The Huntsman: Winter's War">The Huntsman: Winter's War</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-release-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>On December 20, 2016, IMAX released a trailer with the wrong audio track attached, which unintentionally prompted the creation of memes and video montages featuring the mistakenly included audio track, which was missing most of the sound effects and instead featured Tom Cruise's grunts and screams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> IMAX reacted by taking down the trailer and issuing DMCA takedown notices in an attempt to stop it from spreading.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup></p>
