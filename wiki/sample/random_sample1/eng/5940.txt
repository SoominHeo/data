<p><b>Sailor Mercury</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">セーラーマーキュリー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sērā Mākyurī</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a fictional character in the <i><a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon" title="Sailor Moon">Sailor Moon</a></i> manga series created by <a href="/wiki/Naoko_Takeuchi" title="Naoko Takeuchi">Naoko Takeuchi</a>. The alternate identity of <b>Ami Mizuno</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">水野 亜美</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mizuno Ami</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, renamed "Amy Mizuno" in some English adaptations)</span>, a teenage Japanese schoolgirl, she is a member of the Sailor Soldiers, female supernatural fighters who protects the <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">Solar System</a> from evil.</p>
<p>Sailor Mercury is the first Sailor Soldier to be discovered by Sailor Moon and serves as the "brains" of the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-materials_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-materials-1">[1]</a></sup> She is highly intelligent and can use a supercomputer to collect useful information in battles. She possesses powers associated with water and ice.</p>
<p>Aside from the main body of the <i>Sailor Moon</i> series, Ami features in her own short story in the manga, <i>Ami's First Love</i>. Originally published in volume fourteen of the manga, this was the only of three "Exam Battle" stories to be made into a "Special" for the anime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exam_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-exam-2">[2]</a></sup> A number of <a href="/wiki/Image_song" title="Image song">image songs</a> mentioning Ami's character have been released as well, including the contents of three different <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3-inch_CD_single" title="3-inch CD single">3-inch CD singles</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Profile">Profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Mercury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ami's most emphasized character trait is that she is extremely intelligent—in the anime and manga she is rumored by other characters to have an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IQ" title="IQ">IQ</a> of three hundred, while in the <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_musicals" title="Sailor Moon musicals">stage musicals</a> this is stated as a fact.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She is adept at English in both the musicals and the anime, and in the live action it was clearly, although briefly, demonstrated by the actress portraying her (actress Chisaki Hama was speaking to an English speaking character as her character was visiting the United States as a medical intern).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Her peers view her with a mixture of awe and distaste, misinterpreting her inherent shyness as <a href="/wiki/Snob" title="Snob">snobbery</a>, and so she tends to have a difficult time making new friends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act2_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-act2-5">[5]</a></sup> Ami is depicted as kind, sweet, gentle, and loyal, as well as slightly insecure. She also dislikes the fights of Sailor Moon and <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Mars" title="Sailor Mars">Sailor Mars</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-doi097_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-doi097-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Anne_Allison" title="Anne Allison">Anne Allison</a> describes her as "a smart girl who needs to relax", calling her "conscientious" and "studious", "everything Usagi is not".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Challenge_to_Hollywood_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Challenge_to_Hollywood-7">[7]</a></sup> Early on in the story, she relies heavily on the approval of her mother, teachers, and friends, but as the series progresses she becomes stronger and more confident in herself. She is generally the most sensible of the main characters, and is often the only one embarrassed when the group has a dull-witted moment. As the story begins, she attends Azabu Jūban Junior High along with <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(character)" title="Sailor Moon (character)">Usagi Tsukino</a> and, later, <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Jupiter" title="Sailor Jupiter">Makoto Kino</a>.</p>

<p>Throughout the series, much of Ami's <a href="/wiki/Leisure" title="Leisure">free time</a> is spent studying. She loves to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reading_(activity)" title="Reading (activity)">read</a>, and dreams aloud of one day being a <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">doctor</a> like her mother and becomes one in both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parallel_Sailor_Moon" title="Parallel Sailor Moon">Parallel Sailor Moon</a> and the live action series. In the musicals, Ami's dream of being a doctor and leaving Japan to study abroad is a recurring theme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The first part of the song <i>Dream Yume wa Ookiku</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ドリーム　夢は大きく</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>lit. Dream - Dreams are Huge</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> shows Ami's conflict between studying abroad and growing up or being with everyone else and staying a young girl. She faces a similar dilemma in the anime, but very directly; given the opportunity to study in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, she gets as far as the airport before deciding to stay in Japan and fight evil alongside her friends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Japan_Pop_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Japan_Pop-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ami has a great appreciation for art as well as science, and, contrary to the usual depiction of a bookworm, enjoys <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pop_culture" title="Pop culture">pop culture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romance_novel" title="Romance novel">romance novels</a> (though she is usually embarrassed to admit it). In both the anime and the manga, Ami's diligence in her studies becomes a <a href="/wiki/Running_gag" title="Running gag">running gag</a>; she often comically scolds Usagi and the others for not doing their homework, and she can become obsessive about being the best student. Her character has been interpreted as a political commentary on the <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Japan" title="Education in Japan">education system of Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Warriors_of_Legend_sample_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Warriors_of_Legend_sample-11">[11]</a></sup> She sometimes displays attraction to boys her age, and other times aversion to the idea. <a href="/wiki/Love_letter" title="Love letter">Love letters</a> are listed as the one thing she has most trouble with,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vol_10_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-vol_10-12">[12]</a></sup> and when she later receives one, it gives her a rash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exam_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-exam-2">[2]</a></sup> In the anime, a classmate named Ryo Urawa learns her identity and expresses attraction to her, but this is never resolved, as he disappears after just two appearances in the first series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Besides reading, Ami is shown playing <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_swimming" title="Human swimming">swimming</a> in order to relax.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vol_10_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-vol_10-12">[12]</a></sup> As the team scholar, <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computers</a> are listed her strong point; she even belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Club_(organization)" title="Club (organization)">club</a> at school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> She loves all her classes, especially <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>. Her favorite foods are given as <a href="/wiki/Sandwich" title="Sandwich">sandwiches</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anmitsu" title="Anmitsu">anmitsu</a>, with her least favorite being <a href="/wiki/Japanese_amberjack" title="Japanese amberjack">yellowtail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vol_10_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-vol_10-12">[12]</a></sup> Other loves include <a href="/wiki/Cat" title="Cat">cats</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toyboxes_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-toyboxes-15">[15]</a></sup> the colors <a href="/wiki/Aquamarine_(color)" title="Aquamarine (color)">aquamarine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blue" title="Blue">blue</a>, the flower <a href="/wiki/Nymphaeaceae" title="Nymphaeaceae">Water Lily</a>, and the gemstone <a href="/wiki/Sapphire" title="Sapphire">sapphire</a>.</p>
<p>Ami is one of the few girls in the series whose family situation is explicitly mentioned in the anime. Her parents are <a href="/wiki/Divorce" title="Divorce">divorced</a>, and she lives with her mother, a busy doctor who is not home very often, named Saeko in the live-action series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> They look very similar, and Ami admires her mother and longs to live up to her example.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Warriors_of_Legend_sample_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Warriors_of_Legend_sample-11">[11]</a></sup> Besides her <a href="/wiki/Workaholic" title="Workaholic">workaholic</a> tendencies, Dr. Mizuno is portrayed as a good person who openly resents not having more time to spend with her daughter. Ami's father is never named, but is stated in the manga and anime to be a painter. The manga says that he never visits them, having decided one day not to come home from the forest where he was relaxing and painting, but he sends her postcards on her birthday. Thinking about this, Ami sometimes resents her parents' selfishness in separating,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Act35_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Act35-17">[17]</a></sup> partly because divorce in Japan is <a href="/wiki/Taboo" title="Taboo">taboo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> However, in the anime Ami seems to appreciate her father and seems to share some of his artistic traits, at one point even composing matching lyrics for a tune that had none.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-doi151_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-doi151-19">[19]</a></sup> In the manga, Ami's mother is revealed to be fairly rich, as they live in a <a href="/wiki/Condominium" title="Condominium">condominium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Act11_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Act11-20">[20]</a></sup> Ami is shown testing the strength of a sword that the Sailor Soldiers received on the Moon by using it to chip a <a href="/wiki/Diamond" title="Diamond">diamond</a> ring. (Classically, a diamond is the <a href="/wiki/Hardness" title="Hardness">hardest</a> mineral.) When the girls panic, she calms them by saying that her mother has many more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Act11_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-Act11-20">[20]</a></sup> In the live-action series, Ami is especially shy and usually wears <a href="/wiki/Glasses" title="Glasses">glasses</a> while in public, even though she does not need them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> At her <a href="/wiki/Middle_school" title="Middle school">middle school</a>, she has no friends before meeting Usagi and always eats lunch alone on the roof so she can study.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Usagi seems to be the only one to realize that Ami is merely shy, not truly standoffish, and in befriending her gradually helps Ami to learn that she is more than just a bookworm. By Act 34, when Ami's mother attempts to transfer her to another school because she thinks Ami's friends are bad influences, Ami rebels, avoiding the admission interview and spending the night at the Sailor Guardians' hideout with <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Mars" title="Sailor Mars">Rei Hino</a>. Later she tells her mother that what she's doing in her life right now is more important than studying, and her mother understands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ami was a kind and gentle person who disliked quarrels and abhorred harming innocent people. This trait was even cited in the title of an anime episode, "Believe in Love! Ami, a Kind Hearted Soldier," where she persuaded Sailor Uranus and Sailor Neptune not to kill Hotaru. Like her comrades, her loyalty to Sailor Moon was unwavering, and she would sacrifice her life for her princess if necessary.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aspects_and_forms">Aspects and forms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Mercury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aspects and forms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a character with different incarnations, special abilities, powers, transformations and ageless extended longevity; an extremely long lifetime virtually spanned between the Silver Millennium era and the 30th century, Ami gains multiple <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/aspect" title="wiktionary:aspect">aspects</a> and aliases as the series progresses.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sailor_Mercury">Sailor Mercury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Mercury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sailor Mercury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ami's Sailor Soldier identity is Sailor Mercury. She wears a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_fuku" title="Sailor fuku">uniform</a> colored in shades of blue. In the manga and live action series, her outfit is initially sleeveless and she adorns three blue studs in each of her ears. Her <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Crystal" title="Sailor Moon Crystal">Sailor Moon Crystal</a> design is largely faithful to her manga design, with the exception of having one blue stud on her right ear and three on the left. In contrast, the original anime initially depicted her with sleeves and a single blue stud in each ear. Sailor Mercury is given specific titles throughout the various series, including Soldier of Water and Intelligence,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-waterint_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-waterint-24">[24]</a></sup> Soldier of Wisdom,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Soldier of Justice and Wisdom,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exam_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-exam-2">[2]</a></sup> and Soldier of Love and Exams. Her personality is no different from when she is a civilian, although certain special abilities are unavailable to her in that form.</p>
<p>In Japanese, the name for the planet Mercury is <i>Suisei</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">水星</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, the first kanji meaning 'water' and the second indicating a celestial object. Although the Roman planet-name is used, Sailor Mercury's abilities are water-based due to this aspect of Japanese mythology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Initially most of her powers are strategic rather than offensive,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act2_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-act2-5">[5]</a></sup> and she possesses various pieces of computerized equipment to help her study the enemy.</p>
<p>As she grows much stronger and more powerful, Sailor Mercury gains additional powers, and at key points her uniform changes to reflect this. The first change takes place in Act 35 of the manga, when she obtains the Mercury Crystal and her outfit becomes similar to that of Super Sailor Moon. She is not given a new title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Act35_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Act35-17">[17]</a></sup> A similar event is divided between episodes 143 and 151 of the anime, and she is given the name Super Sailor Mercury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-doi151_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-doi151-19">[19]</a></sup> A third, manga-only form appears in Act 42, unnamed but analogous to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eternal_Sailor_Moon" title="Eternal Sailor Moon">Eternal Sailor Moon</a> (<i>sans</i> wings).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Dark_Mercury">Dark Mercury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Mercury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dark Mercury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pretty_Guardian_Sailor_Moon" title="Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon">Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon</a></i>, the live-action series, Ami is briefly taken over by the power of the <a href="/wiki/Dark_Kingdom" title="Dark Kingdom">Dark Kingdom</a> and becomes <b>Dark Mercury</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">悪のマーキュリー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Aku no Mākyurī</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. This form first appears in Act 21, as a servant of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shitennou_(Sailor_Moon)#Kunzite" title="Shitennou (Sailor Moon)">Kunzite</a>. Her sailor suit has black <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tulle_netting" title="Tulle netting">tulle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lace" title="Lace">lace</a> on the back bow and sleeves, and tribal designs appear on her <a href="/wiki/Tiara" title="Tiara">tiara</a> and boots. She also gains a chain with a black charm on it around her waist. Her transformation phrase is <i>Dark Power! Make-up!</i> and is said in a much darker tone. She wields a sword fashioned from an icicle, which she creates herself the first time her transformation is displayed. In promotional photos prior to her premiere, she was shown with a different sword, which seemed to have strings on it like a harp or violin; the latter seems more likely, as she is also seen holding a bow. This bow was redecorated and given to Zoisite for use as a sword.</p>
<p>Dark Mercury is created when Kunzite manages to kidnap Ami in a moment of vulnerability while the other Sailor Guardians are busy. He exposes her directly to the power of <a href="/wiki/Dark_Kingdom#Queen_Metaria" title="Dark Kingdom">Queen Metaria</a>, causing drastic personality changes as well as the alterations to her uniform. She is self-confident to the point of <a href="/wiki/Egotism" title="Egotism">egotism</a>, and continues to attend school in civilian form, mainly to antagonize Usagi by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brainwashing" title="Brainwashing">brainwashing</a> all of her former friends. Dressing predominantly in black, evil-Ami tends to move about slowly and dramatically, and when confronting the Sailor Guardians gives a sense of <a href="/wiki/Sadomasochism" title="Sadomasochism">sadistic</a> glee.</p>
<p>Dark Mercury has no intention of being a follower to anyone, and is always trying to pursue her own agenda, which is to kill her friends and become as strong as possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> She shows blatant disrespect to Kunzite and the other <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shitennou_(Sailor_Moon)" title="Shitennou (Sailor Moon)">Shitennou</a></i>, even to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Beryl" title="Queen Beryl">Queen Beryl</a>, perhaps because, unlike even the <i>Shitennou</i>, she had been directly exposed to Metaria's power during her conversion, as opposed to having Queen Beryl or another <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/intermediary" title="wikt:intermediary">intermediary</a> filtering it.</p>
<p>Despite these alterations, certain aspects of the real Ami still remain. She still wants to do well in school, and wants friends, <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hence" title="wikt:hence">hence</a> the brainwashing of her classmates. She seems to retain a sense of <a href="/wiki/Sympathy" title="Sympathy">sympathy</a>, which is evident when she repairs <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shitennou_(Sailor_Moon)#Nephrite" title="Shitennou (Sailor Moon)">Nephrite's</a> cape for him, stating that she does not like to see him alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Periodically, Sailor Moon attempts to heal her friend with the power of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silver_Crystal" title="Silver Crystal">Silver Crystal</a>. Mercury is always snatched away before this can be completed, but it has some effect, ultimately resulting in her recovery. In Act 28, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catalyst" title="Catalyst">catalyst</a> for her finally returning to normal is when, having defeated Sailor Moon in battle, the sight of her injured friend causes her to realize she cares about Usagi, and to remember who she really is. After being healed, she has no memories of what happened while she was Dark Mercury. This haunts her, as she becomes terrified of what she may have done to her friends while not in control.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Princess_Mercury">Princess Mercury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Mercury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Princess Mercury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a>, during the age of Silver Millennium, Sailor Mercury was also the Princess of her <a href="/wiki/Core_worlds" title="Core worlds">home planet</a>. She was among those given the duty of protecting <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(character)#Princess_Serenity" title="Sailor Moon (character)">Princess Serenity</a> of Silver Millennium. As Princess Mercury, she dwelt in Mariner Castle and wore a light blue gown—she appears in this form in the original manga and in supplementary art.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Naoko_Takeuchi" title="Naoko Takeuchi">Naoko Takeuchi</a> once drew her in the arms of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shitennou_(Sailor_Moon)#Zoisite" title="Shitennou (Sailor Moon)">Zoisite</a>, but no further romantic link between them was established in the manga or the first anime adaptation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> However, in <i><a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Crystal" title="Sailor Moon Crystal">Sailor Moon Crystal</a></i>, it is clearly stated that Sailor Mercury and Zoisite were in love during the Silver Millennium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> This is also established in the first <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_musicals" title="Sailor Moon musicals">stage musical</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and in the later <i>Eien Densetsu</i>, where Ami and a disguised Zoisite share a duet, "A Fabricated Forevermore" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">偽りのForevermore</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Itsuwari no forevermore</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.</p>

<p>In the manga, Ami can <a href="/wiki/Dowsing" title="Dowsing">dowse</a> without any aids.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Otherwise, she is not shown using any special powers in her civilian form, and may not be able to.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> She must first transform into a Sailor Guardian by raising a special device (pen, bracelet, wand, or crystal) into the air and shouting a special phrase, originally "Mercury Power, Make-up!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> As she becomes more powerful and obtains new transformation devices, this phrase changes to evoke Mercury Star, Planet, or Crystal Power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sailor Mercury has the power to create and manipulate water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> For the entire <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(arc)" title="Sailor Moon (arc)">first story arc</a>, she uses her water-manipulating capabilities only to create solid and dense clouds of <a href="/wiki/Mist" title="Mist">mist</a> and fog, chilling and blinding the enemy while her allies prepare more direct attacks. In the manga she usually does this without speaking, while in the anime it is given the name <i>Shabon Spray</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In the updated renewal manga, this power is renamed <i>Mercury Aqua Mist</i> to match the live-action series, in which she has an offensive beam attack by that name, capable of destroying weaker enemies. She uses a total of five attacks in this series, most of which are variations on the first.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sailor Mercury's first major offensive attack is <i>Shine Aqua Illusion</i>, introduced in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_R" title="Sailor Moon R">second story arc</a>, which can be used as a projectile, to freeze the enemy in solid ice, or to create a defensive barrier out of solid ice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Aside from variations on her other powers (mostly improving their strength with the addition of "Freezing" or "Snow"), her next named attack is <i>Mercury Aqua Mirage</i>, used during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Super" title="Sailor Moon Super">third arc</a> of the manga and again in the special side-story "Ami's First Love" (manga and anime). Her final and greatest strength and power comes in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_Supers" title="Sailor Moon Supers">fourth story arc</a>, when she takes on her second Sailor Soldier form (Super Sailor Mercury in the anime). At this stage she acquires a special weapon, the Mercury Harp,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Act35_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-Act35-17">[17]</a></sup> and with it <i>Mercury Aqua Rhapsody</i>, which is her primary attack for the duration of the story. In the anime Mercury gains the harp deep within her subconscious. The manga took a different approach with the Mercury Harp making it more of an item rather than a harp formed due to the attack. Furthermore, Sailor Mercury gained the harp through her power guardian. In the manga the Mercury Harp, as well as the other Sailor Soldiers' weapons have their own "wills" and can even speak and give input.</p>
<p>In addition to her own powers, Sailor Mercury has more non-magical items than any other Sailor Soldier. Early on in the series she makes frequent use of an extremely powerful "Micro-miniature <a href="/wiki/Supercomputer" title="Supercomputer">Super Computer</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> that enables her to make special calculations, scan her surroundings, track the movements of allies as well as foes, and determine her enemies' weak points. The computer works in sync with her Mercury Goggles,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> which analyze the area around her and display information in front of her eyes and on the Computer. The visor may be a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hologram" title="Hologram">hologram</a> of some sort; it materializes across her face when she touches her earring. The manga sometimes shows her wearing a small <a href="/wiki/Microphone" title="Microphone">microphone</a> connected to her earring, which she uses to communicate with Luna at the Sailor Soldiers' hideout during the manga's first arc. All of these devices gradually fall out of use as the series progresses.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In the live-action series, like most of the other Sailor Guardians, she receives a <a href="/wiki/Tambourine" title="Tambourine">tambourine</a>-like weapon called the Sailor Star Tambo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> She is one of two Sailor Guardians to use it for a named attack (<i>Mercury Aqua Storm</i>), and later can transform it into a sword. She also creates swords out of water, both as Sailor Mercury and as Dark Mercury.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In the manga, the Mercury Crystal and Mercury Harp are among her most significant magical possessions. The former is her Sailor Crystal and the source of all of her power, which becomes especially important in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sailor_Stars" title="Sailor Stars">fifth story arc</a>. The harp's form is actually that of a <a href="/wiki/Lyre" title="Lyre">lyre</a>, a stringed instrument smaller than a <a href="/wiki/Harp" title="Harp">harp</a>, which according to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_myth" title="Roman myth">Roman myth</a> was invented by the god <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(mythology)" title="Mercury (mythology)">Mercury</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Mercury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Ami was not included in the original proposal for a hypothetical <i><a href="/wiki/Codename:_Sailor_V" title="Codename: Sailor V">Codename: Sailor V</a></i> anime, which instead featured Minako's very similar-looking best friend from that series, Hikaru Sorano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-infinity_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-infinity-46">[46]</a></sup> She was present, however, by the time the concept was expanded to center on <i>Sailor Moon</i>.</p>
<p>Creator <a href="/wiki/Naoko_Takeuchi" title="Naoko Takeuchi">Naoko Takeuchi</a> designed Ami as the "team brain,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-materials_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-materials-1">[1]</a></sup> giving her genius-level intelligence to create the impression that she was not quite human—in fact, the character was originally intended to be a <a href="/wiki/Cyborg" title="Cyborg">cyborg</a> with an accelerator. One possible storyline involved her losing an arm or being injured in some other way and dying from it, but Takeuchi's editor objected, so Ami became a fully human character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sailor Mercury's original costume design, like the others', was fully unique. It featured full-length sleeves, pink ribbons, shoulder guards, green accents, buttons on the stomach, and high-tech goggles. Later, Takeuchi was surprised by these sketches and stated that she did not remember drawing them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> She also describes Ami as looking like <a href="/wiki/Noriko_Sakai" title="Noriko Sakai">Noriko Sakai</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-materials_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-materials-1">[1]</a></sup> a J-pop idol of the early 1990s, and in Ami's original debut, <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(character)" title="Sailor Moon (character)">Usagi</a> thinks to herself that Ami resembles Miss Rain, a character from another Takeuchi series. This reference was removed in the 2003 renewal manga.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kanji" title="Kanji">kanji</a> in Ami's last name translate as "water" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">水</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>mizu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and "field" or "civilian" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">野</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>no</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>; and her first name translates as "Asia" or "second" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">亜</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>a</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and "beauty" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">美</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>mi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. It is structured as a <a href="/wiki/Pun" title="Pun">pun</a>, as the syllable "no" indicates a <a href="/wiki/Possessive" title="Possessive">possessive</a>, so that her name can also be understood as "Beauty of Water." It is frequently mistranslated as "Friend of Water" because of the French word <i>ami</i>, which is included in some Japanese dictionaries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the English manga and other books, "Amy" retains her family name of Mizuno. One early episode of the anime shows her answering the phone in her house by saying "Anderson residence," where in the original she had answered with "This is Mizuno,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> but this surname never appears again, and she is reverted to Mizuno by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cloverway" title="Cloverway">Cloverway</a> in the series' third season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Actresses">Actresses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Mercury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Actresses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the original anime production of <i>Sailor Moon</i>, Ami was voiced by veteran <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seiy%C5%AB" title="Seiyū">voice actress</a> <a href="/wiki/Aya_Hisakawa" title="Aya Hisakawa">Aya Hisakawa</a>. After the show's conclusion, Hisakawa wrote in an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artbook" title="Artbook">artbook</a> that she was "raised by" the character of Ami, and was "really, greatly happy" to have met her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-infinity_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-infinity-46">[46]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <i>Sailor Moon Crystal</i> anime, Ami is voiced by <a href="/wiki/Hisako_Kanemoto" title="Hisako Kanemoto">Hisako Kanemoto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the DIC/Cloverway English adaptation, Ami's name is changed to "Amy". Her voice was provided first by <a href="/wiki/Karen_Bernstein" title="Karen Bernstein">Karen Bernstein</a>, for the original and R series and the movies, and later by <a href="/wiki/Liza_Balkan" title="Liza Balkan">Liza Balkan</a> for the S and SuperS series. <a href="/wiki/Shandi_Sinnamon" title="Shandi Sinnamon">Shandi Sinnamon</a> also provided the English vocals for a song attributed to Amy's character in the second story arc. In the Viz Media English Adaptation, her voice is supplied by <a href="/wiki/Kate_Higgins" title="Kate Higgins">Kate Higgins</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ami has been portrayed by 9 actresses in the <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_musicals" title="Sailor Moon musicals">stage musicals</a>: Ayako Morino, Yukiko Miyagawa, Hisano Akamine, Mariya Izawa, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chieko_Kawabe" title="Chieko Kawabe">Chieko Kawabe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manami_Wakayama" title="Manami Wakayama">Manami Wakayama</a>, Miyabi Matsura, Momoyo Koyama<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> and Yume Takeuchi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Pretty_Guardian_Sailor_Moon_(live-action_series)" title="Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon (live-action series)">Pretty Guardian Sailor Moon</a>, she is played by <a href="/wiki/Rika_Izumi" title="Rika Izumi">Rika Izumi</a>. Child actress <a href="/wiki/Tamaki_Matsumoto" title="Tamaki Matsumoto">Kanki Matsumoto</a> portrays Ami in flashback sequences and childhood photographs.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_and_influence">Reception and influence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sailor_Mercury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception and influence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The official <i>Sailor Moon</i> character popularity polls listed Ami Mizuno and Sailor Mercury as separate entities. In 1992, readers ranked them at seventh and fourth respectively, out of thirty eight choices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> One year later, now with fifty choices, Ami was the eighth most popular while Mercury was ninth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> In 1994, with fifty one choices, Ami was the fifteenth most popular character, and Mercury was sixteenth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> In early 1996, with fifty one choices, Ami was again the fifteenth most popular character, and Mercury was the nineteenth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> Ami was the most popular female character in <a href="/wiki/Animage" title="Animage">Animage</a>'s May 1993 poll, and an episode featuring her, "Love for Ami?! A Boy Who Can Predict the Future", was the eleventh favorite episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> The following year she came second behind <a href="/wiki/Belldandy" title="Belldandy">Belldandy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> and in 1995 she came fifth. In 1995, an episode featuring Ami, "The Labyrinth of Water! Ami the Targeted", was the ninth favorite episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> In 1996, after the debut of <a href="/wiki/Neon_Genesis_Evangelion" title="Neon Genesis Evangelion">Neon Genesis Evangelion</a>, she came sixteenth,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> and in 1997 she came twentieth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>A five-book series was published, one book on each of the Sailor Soldiers and <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon_(character)" title="Sailor Moon (character)">Sailor Moon</a>. Ami's was released in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> This book was later translated into English by <a href="/wiki/Tokyopop" title="Tokyopop">Mixx</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> The episode where Sailor Mercury gained her powers was novelised by Mixx.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was popular with the male audience of <i>Pretty Soldier Sailor Moon</i> due to her computer use and skills.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup></p>
