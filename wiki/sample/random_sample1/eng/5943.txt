<p>The <b><i>Mitscher</i>-class destroyer</b> was an experimental <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a> class of four ships that were built for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> shortly after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Considerably larger than all previous destroyers, they would have been the first post-war destroyer class had they not been reclassified during construction as <a href="/wiki/Destroyer_leader" title="Destroyer leader">destroyer leaders</a> (DL). Commissioned in 1953-1954, two of the class served until 1969, and were scrapped in the 1970s. The other two were converted into <a href="/wiki/Guided_missile_destroyer" title="Guided missile destroyer">guided missile destroyers</a> (DDG), served until 1978, and were sold for scrap by 1980.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitscher-class_destroyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All four <i>Mitscher</i>-class ships were ordered 3 August 1948 and were named for admirals of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>. Each ship displaced 3,331 tons light, 3,642 tons standard and 4,855 tons under full load with a length of 494 feet (151 m), a 50-foot (15 m) <a href="/wiki/Beam_(nautical)" title="Beam (nautical)">beam</a> and a 26-foot (8 m) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Draft_(ship)" title="Draft (ship)">draft</a>.</p>
<p>Beyond that, each ship had a different loadout of propulsion and other systems so as to determine the best course of action for future destroyer design.</p>
<p>In the early 1960s, the <i>Mitscher</i> class underwent modernization through the Class Improvement Program (CIP), which included the replacement of the boilers on the first two ships of the class.</p>
