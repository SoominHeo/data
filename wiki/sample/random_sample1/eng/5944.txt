<p>King <b>Micheon of Goguryeo</b> (died 331, r. 300–331)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shin_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shin-1">[1]</a></sup> was the 15th ruler of <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a>, the northernmost of the <a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms_of_Korea" title="Three Kingdoms of Korea">Three Kingdoms of Korea</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_Rise_to_the_throne">Background and Rise to the throne</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micheon_of_Goguryeo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and Rise to the throne">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was the grandson of the 13th king <a href="/wiki/Seocheon_of_Goguryeo" title="Seocheon of Goguryeo">Seocheon</a>, and the son of the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gochuga&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gochuga (page does not exist)">gochuga</a></i> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Go_Dol-go&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Go Dol-go (page does not exist)">Go Dol-go</a>, who was killed by his brother, the 14th king <a href="/wiki/Bongsang_of_Goguryeo" title="Bongsang of Goguryeo">Bongsang</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KBS_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-KBS-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Korean historical records say that Micheon fled and hid as a servant in a miserable life such as throwing stones into a pond throughout the night to keep his master from being awakened. It is said a year later he left that house to become salt peddler but failed to gain huge asset.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Micheon1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Micheon1-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Meanwhile, King Bongsang became increasingly unpopular, and court officials, led by Prime Minister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chang_Jo-Ri" title="Chang Jo-Ri">Chang Jo-Ri</a>, carried out a coup that overthrew <a href="/wiki/Bongsang_of_Goguryeo" title="Bongsang of Goguryeo">King Bongsang</a>, and placed King Micheon on the throne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KBS_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-KBS-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reign">Reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micheon_of_Goguryeo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Micheon continuously developed the Goguryeo army into a very powerful force. During the disintegration of <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jin_Dynasty_(265-420)" title="Jin Dynasty (265-420)">Jin Dynasty</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHF_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-NHF-5">[5]</a></sup> he expanded Goguryeo's borders into the <a href="/wiki/Liaodong_Peninsula" title="Liaodong Peninsula">Liaodong Peninsula</a> and the other Chinese commanderies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Micheon_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Micheon-6">[6]</a></sup> Since the commanderies were nuisances to be eliminated for Goguryeo’s stability, the first military campaign in 302 headed against the <a href="/wiki/Xuantu_Commandery" title="Xuantu Commandery">Xuantu Commandery</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> with conquering <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daedong_River" title="Daedong River">Daedong River</a> basins of current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pyeongyang" title="Pyeongyang">Pyeongyang</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Consolidating cut-off between commanderies and Chinese mainland, Goguryeo also annexed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lelang_commandery" title="Lelang commandery">Lelang commandery</a> in 313<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shin_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shin-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daifang_commandery" title="Daifang commandery">Daifang commandery</a> in 314 after attacked Seoanpyeong (西安平; near modern <a href="/wiki/Dandong" title="Dandong">Dandong</a>) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liaodong" title="Liaodong">Liaodong</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The series of subjugation around northern Korean peninsular and Manchuria held its significance given that 400-year presence of Chinese forces was completely cleared out of Korean peninsular.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Micheon_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Micheon-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In his reign, Goguryeo was faced with growing <a href="/wiki/Xianbei" title="Xianbei">Xianbei</a> influence in the west, particularly <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Murong_Bu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Murong Bu (page does not exist)">Murong Bu</a> (慕容部) incursions into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liaodong" title="Liaodong">Liaodong</a>. Micheon allied with other Xianbei tribes against the Murong Bu, but their attack was unsuccessful. In 319, the Goguryeo general <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yeo_Noja&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yeo Noja (page does not exist)">Yeo Noja</a> (여노자, 如奴子) was taken captive by the Murong Bu. Throughout this period, Goguryeo and the Murongbu attacked each other's positions in Liaodong, but neither failed to secure regional hegemony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHF_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-NHF-5">[5]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Micheon_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Micheon-6">[6]</a></sup> Since both sides were at stake, Micheon sent its ambassador to Zhou posterior in 330 with a view to making a diversion against Murong Bu from east side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_and_aftermath">Death and aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micheon_of_Goguryeo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death and aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Micheon died and was buried in 331 at Micheon-won, literally the "garden with beautiful stream".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Micheon_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Micheon-6">[6]</a></sup> Twelve years later, in the reign of <a href="/wiki/Gogugwon_of_Goguryeo" title="Gogugwon of Goguryeo">King Gogugwon</a>, his remains were dug up by the <a href="/wiki/Former_Yan" title="Former Yan">Former Yan</a> invaders, and held for ransom.</p>
