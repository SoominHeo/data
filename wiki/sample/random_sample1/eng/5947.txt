<p>The <b>Battle of Midtskogen</b> was the battle fought on the night between 9 and 10 April 1940 during the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a> between a <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">German</a> raiding party and an improvised Norwegian force. The site of the battle was Midtskogen farm, situated approximately 5 km (3.1 mi) west of the town <a href="/wiki/Elverum" title="Elverum">Elverum</a> at the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/%C3%98sterdalen" title="Østerdalen">Østerdalen valley</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>. The invading German troops were out on a raid to capture the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Norwegian_monarchs" title="List of Norwegian monarchs">Norwegian King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haakon_VII_of_Norway" title="Haakon VII of Norway">Haakon VII</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nygaardsvold%27s_Cabinet" title="Nygaardsvold's Cabinet">his cabinet</a> and thereby forcing Norway into submission. After a short battle, the German force withdrew, having lost its commander in the fighting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Opposing_forces">Opposing forces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Midtskogen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Opposing forces">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Norwegian defenders mustered a slightly numerically superior force, the core of which was a <a href="/wiki/Company_(military_unit)" title="Company (military unit)">rifle company</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hans_Majestet_Kongens_Garde" title="Hans Majestet Kongens Garde">Royal Guards</a> and a number of hastily mustered volunteers, consisting mostly of members of local rifle clubs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The German party consisted of approximately 100 <i><a href="/wiki/Fallschirmj%C3%A4ger" title="Fallschirmjäger">Fallschirmjäger</a></i> in a convoy of commandeered Norwegian civilian vehicles. Though somewhat numerically inferior the Germans were vastly superior in terms of training and firepower, possessing numerous modern submachine guns, light machine guns and hand grenades.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle">Battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Midtskogen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The opposing forces clashed at around 01:30 on 10 April with the German vehicles crashing into a Norwegian roadblock. The ensuing fire-fight continued until 03:00, ending with both forces pulling back. The Norwegians were reinforced and regrouped into new positions; the Germans, realizing their raid had failed, retreated to <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Midtskogen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The casualties on both sides were light. The Germans suffered two men killed in action and an unknown number of wounded. One of the German fatalities was their <a href="/wiki/Military_attach%C3%A9" title="Military attaché">military attaché</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hauptmann" title="Hauptmann">Hauptmann</a></i> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eberhard_Spiller&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eberhard Spiller (page does not exist)">Eberhard Spiller</a>. Norwegian losses were three men wounded in action. The action may have been small, but as it prevented capture of the King and cabinet it probably saved Norway for the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">allied</a> cause<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and proved a major boost to Norwegian morale, being very low due to the early German successes in the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Campaign" title="Norwegian Campaign">Norwegian Campaign</a>.</p>
