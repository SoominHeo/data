<p><b>Mitchell Austin Moreland</b> (born <span style="display:none">(<span class="bday">1985-09-06</span>)</span>September 6, 1985) is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/First_baseman" title="First baseman">first baseman</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB). He previously played for the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a> .</p>
<p>Moreland attended <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_State_University" title="Mississippi State University">Mississippi State University</a>, and was drafted by the Rangers as a <a href="/wiki/First_baseman" title="First baseman">first baseman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Outfielder" title="Outfielder">outfielder</a> in the 17th round of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2007 Major League Baseball draft">2007 MLB draft</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amateur_career">Amateur career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitch_Moreland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Amateur career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Amory,_Mississippi" title="Amory, Mississippi">Amory, Mississippi</a>, Moreland attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amory_High_School" title="Amory High School">Amory High School</a>, where he competed in <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">football</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer" title="Soccer">soccer</a>. At Amory, he was a 2004 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louisville_Slugger" title="Louisville Slugger">Louisville Slugger</a> <a href="/wiki/All-America" title="All-America">All-American</a> after posting a 7-1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Win-loss_record_(pitching)" title="Win-loss record (pitching)">win-loss record</a> with a 0.53 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">earned run average</a> (ERA) as a senior, while also <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">batting</a> .456 and <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">striking out</a> just three times during the season. After high school, Moreland attended <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_State_University" title="Mississippi State University">Mississippi State University</a> to play <a href="/wiki/College_baseball" title="College baseball">college baseball</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_State_Bulldogs_baseball" title="Mississippi State Bulldogs baseball">Mississippi State Bulldogs</a>. There, he hit .343 with ten <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home runs</a> his junior season and went 2-0 on the mound with a 3.46 ERA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitch_Moreland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor_leagues">Minor leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitch_Moreland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Minor leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a> selected Moreland in the 17th round of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2007 Major League Baseball draft">2007 Major League Baseball draft</a>. After he signed, Moreland began his professional career with the Short-Season <a href="/wiki/Spokane_Indians" title="Spokane Indians">Spokane Indians</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_League" title="Northwest League">Northwest League</a>. He batted .259 with 28 <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hits</a>, seven <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">doubles</a>, one <a href="/wiki/Triple_(baseball)" title="Triple (baseball)">triple</a>, two home runs and 15 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Runs_batted_in" title="Runs batted in">runs batted in</a> (RBIs).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The following season, Moreland joined the Class-A <a href="/wiki/Clinton_LumberKings" title="Clinton LumberKings">Clinton LumberKings</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Midwest_League" title="Midwest League">Midwest League</a> and hit .324 with 37 doubles, four triples, 18 home runs, 99 RBIs and two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stolen_bases" title="Stolen bases">stolen bases</a>. He was named to the Mid-Season Midwest League All-Star Team on June 17 and the Post-Season Midwest League All-Star Team on August 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Moreland played in two different levels of the Rangers' organization in 2009. He started the season with the Class-A Advanced <a href="/wiki/Bakersfield_Blaze" title="Bakersfield Blaze">Bakersfield Blaze</a> and hit .341 with 19 doubles, eight home runs and 26 RBIs. He was promoted to the Double-A <a href="/wiki/Frisco_RoughRiders" title="Frisco RoughRiders">Frisco RoughRiders</a> where he batted .326 with 19 doubles, three triples, eight home runs and 59 RBIs. Moreland was assigned to the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Fall_League" title="Arizona Fall League">Arizona Fall League</a> at the end of the season and played for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surprise_Rafters" title="Surprise Rafters">Surprise Rafters</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was named the "<a href="/wiki/Tom_Grieve" title="Tom Grieve">Tom Grieve</a> Minor League Player of the Year" by the Texas Rangers after the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Rangers invited Moreland to <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a> in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Moreland played with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_RedHawks" title="Oklahoma City RedHawks">Oklahoma City RedHawks</a> (AAA) in the 2010 season until July 29. As of July 29, he had a season batting average of .289 with the RedHawks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Texas_Rangers">Texas Rangers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mitch_Moreland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Texas Rangers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 29, 2010, Moreland was called up by the Rangers to take the place of Ian Kinsler, who was put on the disabled list. He achieved his first Major League hit in his first at-bat, hitting a single against <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Vin_Mazzaro" title="Vin Mazzaro">Vin Mazzaro</a>. He finished the game 2–4, with two singles, as the Rangers won 7–4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On August 4, Moreland hit his first run of his major league career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Along with the RBI, he went 2–5 with a run scored that night in the Rangers' 11–6 win over the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a>. The next day, Moreland went hitless in two at-bats for the first time, although he did walk twice, but had his first multi-RBI game days later against the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">Yankees</a> and his first home run on August 13, a two-run shot off of <a href="/wiki/Josh_Beckett" title="Josh Beckett">Josh Beckett</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Red Sox</a>.</p>
<p>On September 26, Moreland had his first multi-home run game of his major league career, hitting his sixth and seventh home runs of the year, in a 16–9 win over the <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On October 30, 2010, Moreland hit a second inning, three-run home run off of <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Jonathan Sánchez">Jonathan Sánchez</a> to put the Rangers ahead 3–0 in Game 3 of the <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Series" title="2010 World Series">2010 World Series</a>. He was the first Rangers player in 2010 World Series to hit a home run. The Rangers eventually lost the series against the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a> in 5 games.</p>
<p>On June 21, 2011, Moreland hit his first career walk-off home run off of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enerio_Del_Rosario" title="Enerio Del Rosario">Enerio Del Rosario</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> For the 2011 season, he batted .259 with 16 home runs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> That year, the Rangers entered the World Series for the second season in a row. Mitch hit one home run during the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Series" title="2011 World Series">2011 World Series</a> against the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a>, which the team lost in 7 games.</p>
<p>On June 9, 2012, Moreland became only the second <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> batter in history (<a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston's</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Ortiz" title="David Ortiz">David Ortiz</a> being the first<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>) to hit a home run ball into <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Park" title="AT&amp;T Park">AT&amp;T Park's</a> <a href="/wiki/McCovey_Cove" title="McCovey Cove">McCovey Cove</a>. Moreland's 428-foot, 8th-inning blast off <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Vogelsong" title="Ryan Vogelsong">Ryan Vogelsong</a> was also the first pinch-hit home run hit into <a href="/wiki/McCovey_Cove" title="McCovey Cove">McCovey Cove</a> by an opposing player of either league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2015 season was a career year for Moreland. He was second on the team behind <a href="/wiki/Prince_Fielder" title="Prince Fielder">Prince Fielder</a> with a .278 batting average and 85 RBIs. He was tied with Fielder for the team lead with 23 home runs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Moreland batted .233, hit 22 home runs and batted in 60 RBIs. He also won his first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold_Glove_Award" title="Gold Glove Award">Gold Glove Award</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>On December 8, 2016, Moreland signed a one-year contract with the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
