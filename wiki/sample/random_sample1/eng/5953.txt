<p><b>Mika</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/iː/ long 'e' in 'seed'">iː</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born <b>Michael Holbrook Penniman, Jr.</b>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mirror_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-mirror-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbcradio1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbcradio1-6">[6]</a></sup> 18 August 1983), stylised as <b>MIKA</b>, is a British singer and songwriter.</p>
<p>After recording his first extended play, <i><a href="/wiki/Dodgy_Holiday" title="Dodgy Holiday">Dodgy Holiday</a></i>, Mika was named the number-one predicted breakthrough act of 2007 in an annual <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> poll of music critics, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sound_of_2007" title="Sound of 2007">Sound of 2007</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6231243_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_6231243-7">[7]</a></sup> Mika released his first full-length studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Life_in_Cartoon_Motion" title="Life in Cartoon Motion">Life in Cartoon Motion</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/Island_Records" title="Island Records">Island Records</a> in 2007, which sold more than 5.6 million copies worldwide and helped Mika win a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brit_Award" title="Brit Award">Brit Award</a>—winning Best British Breakthrough act, and receive a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2006, Mika started up his company, Dodgy Holiday Tours Limited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dhtl_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-dhtl-9">[9]</a></sup> Two years later Mika released his second extended play, <i><a href="/wiki/Songs_for_Sorrow" title="Songs for Sorrow">Songs for Sorrow</a></i>, of which limited edition copies are now sold out worldwide. In 2009 Mika released his second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boy_Who_Knew_Too_Much_(album)" title="The Boy Who Knew Too Much (album)">The Boy Who Knew Too Much</a></i>. Finishing his worldwide tour, Mika recorded his third album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Origin_of_Love" title="The Origin of Love">The Origin of Love</a></i>, stating it would be "more simplistic pop, less layered than the last one".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy.co.uk_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy.co.uk-10">[10]</a></sup> The album was released internationally on 16 September 2012 and in the UK on 8 October 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-album_release_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-album_release-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>His latest album, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Place_in_Heaven" title="No Place in Heaven">No Place in Heaven</a></i>, was released 15 June 2015.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Mika was born in <a href="/wiki/Beirut" title="Beirut">Beirut</a>, the third of five children to American parents, Joannie and Michael Holbrook Penniman. His father was a banker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> His mother was born in New York, to Lebanese parents. His father was born in <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>, while his own father was a diplomat there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taratata_200702_video_medley_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-taratata_200702_video_medley-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Mika was a year old, his family was forced to leave war-torn <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> and moved to Paris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taratata_200702_video_medley_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-taratata_200702_video_medley-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The first piano piece he learned to play was "Les Champs-Élysées", by <a href="/wiki/Joe_Dassin" title="Joe Dassin">Joe Dassin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taratata_200702_video_medley_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-taratata_200702_video_medley-14">[14]</a></sup> At the age of seven, he wrote his first song, a piano instrumental called "Angry", which he describes as "awful".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mirror_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-mirror-5">[5]</a></sup> The family moved to London when he was nine years old. There, he attended the <a href="/wiki/Lyc%C3%A9e_Fran%C3%A7ais_Charles_de_Gaulle" title="Lycée Français Charles de Gaulle">Lycée Français Charles de Gaulle</a>, where he experienced severe <a href="/wiki/Bullying" title="Bullying">bullying</a>. He also had problems with <a href="/wiki/Dyslexia" title="Dyslexia">dyslexia</a>. In response to these experiences Mika was <a href="/wiki/Homeschooling" title="Homeschooling">home-schooled</a> by his mother at the age of 12, for six to eight months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mirror_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-mirror-5">[5]</a></sup> He then attended St Philip's School in <a href="/wiki/Kensington" title="Kensington">Kensington</a>, where he was the head of the Schola Cantorum (the St. Philip's Choir). Later he attended <a href="/wiki/Westminster_School" title="Westminster School">Westminster School</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Music" title="Royal College of Music">Royal College of Music</a>, which he left to record his first album at <a href="/wiki/Casablanca_Records" title="Casablanca Records">Casablanca Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-times-16">[16]</a></sup> As a child Mika was trained by <a href="/wiki/Alla_Ablaberdyeva" title="Alla Ablaberdyeva">Alla Ardakov</a> (Ablaberdyeva), a Russian opera professional.</p>
<p>His given name was Mica but, frustrated at how often it was mispronounced, he changed the 'c' to a 'k'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>

<p>Mika has one younger brother and one younger sister along with two older sisters. His sister Yasmine, who works as an artist under the <i><a href="/wiki/Pen_name" title="Pen name">nom de plume</a></i> DaWack, painted the cartoon art for his two albums <i><a href="/wiki/Life_in_Cartoon_Motion" title="Life in Cartoon Motion">Life in Cartoon Motion</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boy_Who_Knew_Too_Much_(album)" title="The Boy Who Knew Too Much (album)">The Boy Who Knew Too Much</a></i>, and is currently a fashion designer.</p>
<p>Mika speaks French,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taratata_200702_video_medley_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-taratata_200702_video_medley-14">[14]</a></sup> Spanish and Italian<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mika_italian_language_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-mika_italian_language-18">[18]</a></sup> fluently; in an interview on 28 September 2009 with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chris_Moyles_Show" title="The Chris Moyles Show">The Chris Moyles Show</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> he commented that he had taken <a href="/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese" title="Mandarin Chinese">Mandarin Chinese</a> lessons for nine years but does not speak it very well; he also mentioned that his three sisters did all speak it fluently. He also speaks a little Arabic, in a <a href="/wiki/Lebanese_Arabic" title="Lebanese Arabic">Lebanese</a> dialect, his mother's native tongue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>His debut radio appearance was on <a href="/wiki/Dermot_O%27Leary" title="Dermot O'Leary">Dermot O'Leary</a>'s BBC Radio 2 show in September 2006. He also appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Later..._with_Jools_Holland" title="Later... with Jools Holland">Later... with Jools Holland</a></i>, and on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Friday_Night_Project" title="The Friday Night Project">The Friday Night Project</a></i> on 19 January 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Mika is rumoured to have a <a href="/wiki/Vocal_range" title="Vocal range">vocal range</a> of five octaves,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-times-16">[16]</a></sup> but claims that it is actually closer to three and a half octaves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>

<p>Mika denied allegations that he steers clear of sexual taboos to appeal to the US market, pointing to the song "<a href="/wiki/Life_in_Cartoon_Motion" title="Life in Cartoon Motion">Billy Brown</a>", which is about a married man who has an affair with another man. He is quoted as saying, "If I was worried about sexual taboos I certainly wouldn't have made the record I made. It has nothing to do with that. It has more to do with self-respect."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In an interview in the US gay magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Out_(magazine)" title="Out (magazine)">Out</a></i> he stated that "there is a way of discussing sexuality without using labels."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a September 2009 interview in <i>Gay &amp; Night</i> Mika commented on his sexuality: "I've never ever labelled myself. But having said that; I've never limited my life, I've never limited who I sleep with... Call me whatever you want. Call me bisexual, if you need a term for me..."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Later he stated in an interview with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evening_Standard" title="Evening Standard"><i>This Is London</i></a> "I consider myself label-less because I could fall in love with anybody – literally – any type, any body. I'm not picky."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In an August 2012 interview with the magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Instinct_(magazine)" title="Instinct (magazine)">Instinct</a></i>, the singer described himself as gay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2010, Mika was honoured in France with the <a href="/wiki/Ordre_des_Arts_et_des_Lettres" title="Ordre des Arts et des Lettres">Order of Arts and Letters</a> (Chevalier (Knight)) for services to music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_career">Musical career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career:_2004.E2.80.9306">Early career: 2004–06</span></h3>
<p>Mika's first single was a limited 7"/download release called "<a href="/wiki/Relax,_Take_It_Easy" title="Relax, Take It Easy">Relax, Take It Easy</a>" (2006). It was play-listed by UK's <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> and was made Record of the Week by DJ <a href="/wiki/Scott_Mills" title="Scott Mills">Scott Mills</a>. The <i><a href="/wiki/Dodgy_Holiday" title="Dodgy Holiday">Dodgy Holiday</a></i> EP also became available for download. The song "<a href="/wiki/Life_in_Cartoon_Motion" title="Life in Cartoon Motion">Billy Brown</a>" was available for free download for a week from the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>. A song titled 'Over-rated', which was recorded in 2004, was "unofficially" released online.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_in_Cartoon_Motion:_2006.E2.80.9308"><i>Life in Cartoon Motion</i>: 2006–08</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Life_in_Cartoon_Motion" title="Life in Cartoon Motion">Life in Cartoon Motion</a></div>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: left; clear: left; margin: 0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0; width:28%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"The comparisons to <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a> are fine. They started long before I made the record – I've even referred to it in Grace Kelly. A Freddie comparison is a compliment. He's a genius and one of the best there's ever been."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">—Mika following being named the BBC's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sound_of_2007" title="Sound of 2007">Sound of 2007</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_6231243_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_6231243-7">[7]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>In January 2007, Mika was on the top of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_News_website" title="BBC News website">BBC News website</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sound_of..." title="Sound of...">Sound of 2007</a> poll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> His single "<a href="/wiki/Grace_Kelly_(song)" title="Grace Kelly (song)">Grace Kelly</a>" was released by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Music" title="Universal Music">Universal Music</a> for digital download on 8 January 2007. It reached number one on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> on 21 January 2007.</p>
<p>Mika toured the United States in June 2007, with support from <a href="/wiki/Sara_Bareilles" title="Sara Bareilles">Sara Bareilles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natalia_Lesz" title="Natalia Lesz">Natalia Lesz</a>. Mika's début album <i><a href="/wiki/Life_in_Cartoon_Motion" title="Life in Cartoon Motion">Life in Cartoon Motion</a></i> was released on 5 February 2007, and has brought comparison with artists such as <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Scissor_Sisters" title="Scissor Sisters">Scissor Sisters</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Williams" title="Robbie Williams">Robbie Williams</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-belfast_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-belfast-34">[34]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-belfast_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-belfast-34">[34]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Grace_Kelly_(song)" title="Grace Kelly (song)">Grace Kelly</a>," in fact, references Mercury in the lyrics: "I try to be like Grace Kelly/But all her looks were too sad/So I try a little Freddie/I've gone identity mad."</p>
<p>Mika was the musical act for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">The Tonight Show with Jay Leno</a></i> on 26 March 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> and 14 February 2008 and for <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i> on 27 March 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> He also performed live on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/So_You_Think_You_Can_Dance_(United_States)" title="So You Think You Can Dance (United States)">So You Think You Can Dance</a></i> on 26 July 2007.</p>

<p>On 10 October 2007, Mika began his Dodgy Holiday Tour, playing at venues across Europe. On 17 November 2007, Mika started the UK leg of his tour with support from <a href="/wiki/Palladium_(band)" title="Palladium (band)">Palladium</a>. The North American leg of his tour began in January 2008 with support from <a href="/wiki/The_Midway_State" title="The Midway State">The Midway State</a> and <a href="/wiki/Creature_(band)" title="Creature (band)">Creature</a> and continued through February with a stop in Los Angeles for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/50th_Grammy_Awards" title="50th Grammy Awards">50th Grammy Awards</a>.</p>
<p>His debut album <i>Life in Cartoon Motion</i> has a coming of age theme and deals with his transition from childhood to the present, though he has stated that not all of the songs are autobiographical. His songs often deal with difficult topics. For instance, in "<a href="/wiki/Big_Girl_(You_Are_Beautiful)" title="Big Girl (You Are Beautiful)">Big Girl (You Are Beautiful)</a>" the theme of larger women suffering from discrimination is explored. Mika has said that the fact that his mother was a big woman, and that he had seen the prejudices against her, helped him to write the song.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In another example of dealing with more difficult subject matter, in the song "Billy Brown", Mika writes about a married man who has a homosexual affair.</p>
<p>On 20 February 2008, Mika opened the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_BRIT_Awards" title="2008 BRIT Awards">2008 BRIT Awards</a> with a live performance of "<a href="/wiki/Love_Today" title="Love Today">Love Today</a>," "<a href="/wiki/Grace_Kelly_(song)" title="Grace Kelly (song)">Grace Kelly</a>," and a duet, <a href="/wiki/Standing_in_the_Way_of_Control_(song)" title="Standing in the Way of Control (song)">"Standing in the Way of Control"</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Beth_Ditto" title="Beth Ditto">Beth Ditto</a>. He was later awarded the BRIT Award for Best British Breakthrough Artist.</p>
<p>Mika has reportedly written songs for other musicians, saying "I write songs for other people under different names most of the time. I have a little family of three names. One of them has been discovered – it's Alice."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Boy_Who_Knew_Too_Much:_2009.E2.80.9310"><i>The Boy Who Knew Too Much</i>: 2009–10</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Boy_Who_Knew_Too_Much_(album)" title="The Boy Who Knew Too Much (album)">The Boy Who Knew Too Much (album)</a></div>
<p>Prior to the release of his second studio album, Mika released a limited-edition <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> titled <i><a href="/wiki/Songs_for_Sorrow" title="Songs for Sorrow">Songs for Sorrow</a></i> on 8 June 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SongsForSorrow_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-SongsForSorrow-39">[39]</a></sup> The EP includes 4 tracks and a 68-page book featuring lyrics and exclusive illustrated interpretations of each song by <a href="/wiki/Songs_for_Sorrow#List_of_artists" title="Songs for Sorrow">some of Mika's favourite artists</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SongsForSorrow_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-SongsForSorrow-39">[39]</a></sup> The song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_Eyes_(Mika_song)" title="Blue Eyes (Mika song)">Blue Eyes</a>" was used to promote the EP, and was A-listed on the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2" title="BBC Radio 2">BBC Radio 2</a> playlist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mika's second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boy_Who_Knew_Too_Much_(album)" title="The Boy Who Knew Too Much (album)">The Boy Who Knew Too Much</a></i> was released on 21 September 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WeAreGolden_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-WeAreGolden-41">[41]</a></sup> Mika recorded the majority of the album in Los Angeles with producer and musician <a href="/wiki/Greg_Wells" title="Greg Wells">Greg Wells</a>, who also produced his debut album <i>Life in Cartoon Motion</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The album has been described as Mika dealing with his adolescent teenage years and "in a sense is kind of part two" of his first album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>The album was originally titled <i>We Are Golden</i> after the first single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/We_Are_Golden" title="We Are Golden">We Are Golden</a>". On 20 July 2009 in an on-air interview with DJ <a href="/wiki/Jo_Whiley" title="Jo Whiley">Jo Whiley</a> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a>, Mika revealed he was considering renaming the album, because he wanted "something a little more ridiculous."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> On 6 August 2009 it was confirmed that the album's title would change to <i>The Boy Who Knew Too Much</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first single from the album, "We Are Golden", made its radio debut in the United Kingdom on 20 July 2009, on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2" title="BBC Radio 2">BBC Radio 2</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> and was released for download on 6 September 2009, with the physical release following on 7 September 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WeAreGolden_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-WeAreGolden-41">[41]</a></sup> The single debuted at number 4 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> on 13 September 2009. Mika's promotional tour of the single included live performances at the iTunes Festival 2009 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Roundhouse" title="The Roundhouse">The Roundhouse</a> in Camden, London and on <a href="/wiki/Friday_Night_with_Jonathan_Ross" title="Friday Night with Jonathan Ross">Friday Night with Jonathan Ross</a> in September 2009. It was reported that Mika spent £25,000 on drinks after inviting fans to join him at his local pub via Twitter on 7 September 2009 to celebrate the release of his single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Blame_It_on_the_Girls" title="Blame It on the Girls">Blame It on the Girls</a>" was released as the second single in the United States and Japan. This was alongside "<a href="/wiki/Rain_(Mika_song)" title="Rain (Mika song)">Rain</a>" being the second single in the United Kingdom which was released on 23 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> It was confirmed that "Blame It on the Girls" will be the third single in the United Kingdom and will be released 15 February 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> His American promotional tour consisted of live performances on <a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a> in New York City on 25 September 2009 and the <a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">Late Show with David Letterman</a> on 14 October 2009.</p>
<p>In November 2009 Mika performed 'Rain' and 'We Are Golden' live at the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Opera_House" title="Sydney Opera House">Sydney Opera House</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Idol" title="Australian Idol">Australian Idol</a> Grand Final. He also performed on <a href="/wiki/Sunrise_(TV_program)" title="Sunrise (TV program)">Sunrise</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Network" title="Seven Network">Seven Network</a> in Australia. On 30 November 2009, Mika performed "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Let_It_Snow" title="Let It Snow">Let It Snow</a>" in a duet with Japanese pop star <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hikaru_Utada" title="Hikaru Utada">Hikaru Utada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> On 21 March 2010 Mika performed "<a href="/wiki/Gave_It_All_Away" title="Gave It All Away">Gave It All Away</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Boyzone" title="Boyzone">Boyzone</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITV1" title="ITV1">ITV1</a> for <i>Boyzone: A Tribute To <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Gately" title="Stephen Gately">Stephen Gately</a></i>.</p>
<p>In May 2010 Mika released his new single "<a href="/wiki/Kick_Ass_(We_Are_Young)" title="Kick Ass (We Are Young)">Kick Ass (We Are Young)</a>", the title track to the 2010 film "<a href="/wiki/Kick-Ass_(film)" title="Kick-Ass (film)">Kick-Ass</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Origin_of_Love.2C_X_Factor_Italy_and_Songbook_Vol.1:_2011.E2.80.9315"><i>The Origin of Love</i>, <i>X Factor Italy</i> and <i>Songbook Vol.1</i>: 2011–15</span></h3>

<p>Before the release of his newest album, Mika stated in numerous interviews that the new album will be more simplistic and less layered than the previous one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-digitalspy.co.uk_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy.co.uk-10">[10]</a></sup> Mika announced the title of the album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Origin_of_Love" title="The Origin of Love">The Origin of Love</a></i> in a French interview on 17 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-albumtitle_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-albumtitle-53">[53]</a></sup> Mika has stated he would include a number of French tracks on the album, and that the musical style will include elements of <a href="/wiki/Daft_Punk" title="Daft Punk">Daft Punk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fleetwood_Mac" title="Fleetwood Mac">Fleetwood Mac</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> He described the album as less childlike and more serious. The first single released from the album was "<a href="/wiki/Elle_me_dit" title="Elle me dit">Elle me dit</a>", Mika's first French track. It was released online on 1 July 2011. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Littlemore" title="Nick Littlemore">Nick Littlemore</a> of <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_the_Sun_(band)" title="Empire of the Sun (band)">Empire of The Sun</a>, Paul Steel, frYars, <a href="/wiki/William_Orbit" title="William Orbit">William Orbit</a>, <a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doriand" title="fr:Doriand">Doriand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Priscilla_Renea" title="Priscilla Renea">Priscilla Renea</a>, Billboard, <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ellie_Goulding" title="Ellie Goulding">Ellie Goulding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benny_Benassi" title="Benny Benassi">Benny Benassi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Solveig" title="Martin Solveig">Martin Solveig</a>, <a href="/wiki/Klas_%C3%85hlund" title="Klas Åhlund">Klas Åhlund</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Hector" title="Wayne Hector">Wayne Hector</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Greg_Wells" title="Greg Wells">Greg Wells</a> were involved in the production of the album. MIKA contributed his vocals to the production of <a href="/wiki/The_All-American_Rejects" title="The All-American Rejects">The All-American Rejects</a>' 2012 album <a href="/wiki/Kids_in_the_Street" title="Kids in the Street">Kids in the Street</a>. On 8 June 2012, the video for "Make You Happy" was released on <a href="/wiki/Vimeo" title="Vimeo">Vimeo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> The song served as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buzz_single" title="Buzz single">buzz single</a> for the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> On 14 June 2012, "<a href="/wiki/Celebrate_(Mika_song)" title="Celebrate (Mika song)">Celebrate</a>", which features <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>, was announced as the album's lead single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> The album was released internationally on 17 September 2012 and in the UK on 8 October 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-album_release_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-album_release-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 March 2013, Mika announced a new tour and that after taking some time off, he had begun writing his fourth album as well as songs for other artists. Mika also hinted that new material might be released towards the end of 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> On 23 April 2013, Mika was revealed to be the new judge on the seventh season of <i><a href="/wiki/X_Factor_(Italian_TV_series)" title="X Factor (Italian TV series)">The X Factor Italy</a></i>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Arisa" title="Arisa">Arisa</a>. He was the first international mentor on the Italian edition of the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> Mika returned to the panel for the eighth and ninth series, of the show before leaving the panel to focus on other projects. <a href="/wiki/Arisa" title="Arisa">Arisa</a> will return as his replacement, for the tenth series. On 18 November 2013, Mika released a compilation album in Italy titled <i><a href="/wiki/Songbook_Vol._1_(Mika_album)" title="Songbook Vol. 1 (Mika album)">Songbook Vol. 1</a></i> including music from his three previous albums, plus new re-worked tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> On 17 January 2014, Mika took part in the Italian television show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Le_Invasioni_Barbariche" title="Le Invasioni Barbariche">Le Invasioni Barbariche</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/La7" title="La7">La7</a>, and performed a duet with the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Literature" title="Nobel Prize in Literature">Nobel Prize in Literature</a> recipient <a href="/wiki/Dario_Fo" title="Dario Fo">Dario Fo</a> of the song "Ho visto un Re", written by the same Fo and played by <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Jannacci" title="Enzo Jannacci">Enzo Jannacci</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> On 17 February 2015, Mika announced that he will be releasing his first live album, recorded during his concerts with the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Montreal Symphony Orchestra">Montreal Symphony Orchestra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<p>He joined <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice:_la_plus_belle_voix" title="The Voice: la plus belle voix">The Voice: la plus belle voix</a></i>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Louis_Bertignac" title="Louis Bertignac">Louis Bertignac</a>, in the 3rd season. The series saw Mika return for the fourth and fifth season, before leaving the show, as well as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(Italy)" title="The X Factor (Italy)">The X Factor (Italy)</a></i>, to focus on other projects. His replacement for the sixth season willl be <a href="/wiki/Lara_Fabian" title="Lara Fabian">Lara Fabian</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="No_Place_in_Heaven:_2015.E2.80.93present"><i>No Place in Heaven</i>: 2015–present</span></h3>
<p>Mika's fourth and latest album, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Place_in_Heaven" title="No Place in Heaven">No Place in Heaven</a></i>, was released 15 June 2015.</p>
