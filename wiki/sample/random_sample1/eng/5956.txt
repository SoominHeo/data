<p><b>Michel</b> is originally a French name. It can be both a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hebrew" title="Hebrew">Hebrew</a> origin, derived from <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="he" xml:lang="he"><b>מִיכָאֵל / מיכאל</b></span>‎‎ <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Hebrew" title="Help:IPA for Hebrew">[ˌmixäˈʔel]</a></span>, meaning <i>Who Is Like God?</i> The name is particularly common in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> (from where the standard English pronunciation is derived), <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a>. In these instances <i>Michel</i> is equivalent to the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Personal_name" title="Personal name">personal name</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Michael" title="Michael">Michael</a></i>. <i>Mitxel</i> which is also an equivalent of <i>Michael</i>. When of Czech, Slovak or Polish origin it is a variant of the personal name <i><a href="/wiki/Michal" title="Michal">Michal</a></i>. When of <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> origin, the surname <i>Michel</i> is a shortened form of various <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> derivatives of <i>Michael</i>. Examples of such are <i>Michelakis</i>, <i>Michelakakis</i>, or <i>Michelakos</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
