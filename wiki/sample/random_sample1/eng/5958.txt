<p><b>Mikawa-Anjō Station</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">三河安城駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mikawa-Anjō-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a railway station in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anj%C5%8D,_Aichi" title="Anjō, Aichi">Anjō, Aichi</a>, Japan, operated by <a href="/wiki/Central_Japan_Railway_Company" title="Central Japan Railway Company">Central Japan Railway Company</a> (JR Central).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mikawa-Anj%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mikawa-Anjō Station is served by the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkaid%C5%8D_Shinkansen" title="Tōkaidō Shinkansen">Tōkaidō Shinkansen</a> and <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkaid%C5%8D_Main_Line" title="Tōkaidō Main Line">Tōkaidō Main Line</a>, and is situated 336.3 rail km from Tokyo.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mikawa-Anj%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mikawa-Anjō Station has four opposed <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platforms</a> serving a total of four tracks.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mikawa-Anj%C5%8D_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dkaid%C5%8D_Shinkansen" title="Tōkaidō Shinkansen">Tōkaidō Shinkansen</a> opened, the distance between <a href="/wiki/Toyohashi_Station" title="Toyohashi Station">Toyohashi Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nagoya_Station" title="Nagoya Station">Nagoya Station</a> was the second longest on the route of the line. In 1969, the mayors of <a href="/wiki/Okazaki,_Aichi" title="Okazaki, Aichi">Okazaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota,_Aichi" title="Toyota, Aichi">Toyota</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anj%C5%8D,_Aichi" title="Anjō, Aichi">Anjō</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gamag%C5%8Dri,_Aichi" title="Gamagōri, Aichi">Gamagōri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nishio,_Aichi" title="Nishio, Aichi">Nishio</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hekinan,_Aichi" title="Hekinan, Aichi">Hekinan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isshiki,_Aichi" title="Isshiki, Aichi">Isshiki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kira,_Aichi" title="Kira, Aichi">Kira</a>, <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dta,_Aichi" title="Kōta, Aichi">Kōta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hazu,_Aichi" title="Hazu, Aichi">Hazu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nukata,_Aichi" title="Nukata, Aichi">Nukata</a> and <a href="/wiki/Otowa,_Aichi" title="Otowa, Aichi">Otowa</a> submitted the petition of establishing Kokuteikōen-Mikawa Station <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">国定公園三河駅</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kokuteikōen-Mikawa-eki</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> in Kota.</p>
<p>These cities and towns formed the "Alliance for Mikawa Station". Kōta town subsequently recommended Ashinoya near <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dda_Station_(Aichi)" title="Kōda Station (Aichi)">Kōda Station</a>. Okazaki city recommended Shōna-chō, Okazaki, Anjō city recommended Furui-chō near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hekikai-Furui_Station" title="Hekikai-Furui Station">Hekikai-Furui Station</a>, and Nihongi-chō where the Tōkaidō Shinkansen and Tōkaidō Main Line intersect as possible candidate locations. The alliance entrusted the decision to the Aichi prefectural government. On January 5, 1984, the prefectural government decided to build the station in Nihongi, Anjō. Construction work started on July 29, 1985, and the station was opened on March 13, 1988.</p>
