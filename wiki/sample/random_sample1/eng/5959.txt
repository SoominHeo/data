<p><b>Mike Hanke</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈmaɪ̯k ˈhaŋkə]</a></span>; born 5 November 1983 in <a href="/wiki/Hamm" title="Hamm">Hamm</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> former <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kicker_2014-12-17_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-kicker_2014-12-17-2">[2]</a></sup> who played the position of <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>. He has been described as a player who "gives it his all in attack and is always dangerous in the opponent's penalty area."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He earned 17 caps for the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">German national side</a> between 2005 and 2007, and was in the teams which finished third at the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2005 FIFA Confederations Cup">2005 Confederations Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 World Cup</a> on home soil.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Hanke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hanke made his debut in the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_Bundesliga" title="2001–02 Bundesliga">2001–02 season</a> of the German <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schalke_04" title="Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a>. He was transferred to <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">VfL Wolfsburg</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Bundesliga" title="2005–06 Bundesliga">2005–06 season</a> upon request by then Wolfsburg manager <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Strunz" title="Thomas Strunz">Thomas Strunz</a>.</p>
<p>In May 2007, Hanke moved to <a href="/wiki/Hannover_96" title="Hannover 96">Hannover 96</a>, and netted eleven league goals, while the side achieved a midtable final position.</p>
<p>After three and a half seasons with Hannover 96, he transferred to <a href="/wiki/Borussia_M%C3%B6nchengladbach" title="Borussia Mönchengladbach">Borussia Mönchengladbach</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 31 May 2013, Hanke joined <a href="/wiki/SC_Freiburg" title="SC Freiburg">SC Freiburg</a> on a free transfer. He said "It's a great package here in Freiburg. I had spoken to <a href="/wiki/Christian_Streich" title="Christian Streich">Christian Streich</a> beforehand and got to know the place, and I think I'll fit in well here, the opportunity to play in Europe again certainly attracted me, but I also see it as my duty to help the younger players achieve their potential.'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 8 July 2014, Hanke transferred to <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Super_League" title="Chinese Super League">Chinese Super League</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guizhou_Renhe" title="Guizhou Renhe">Guizhou Renhe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After half a year, he returned to Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kicker_2014-12-17_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-kicker_2014-12-17-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_Hanke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hanke was first capped for the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany national football team</a> on 8 June 2005, in a 2–2 friendly draw against <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_football_team" title="Russia national football team">Russia</a> in <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6nchengladbach" title="Mönchengladbach">Mönchengladbach</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He replaced <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Asamoah" title="Gerald Asamoah">Gerald Asamoah</a> after 62 minutes. In his second game, on 18 June against <a href="/wiki/Tunisia_national_football_team" title="Tunisia national football team">Tunisia</a> in the group stage of the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIFA_Confederations_Cup" title="2005 FIFA Confederations Cup">2005 FIFA Confederations Cup</a>, he came on for <a href="/wiki/Lukas_Podolski" title="Lukas Podolski">Lukas Podolski</a> in the 87th minute. One minute later, he scored his only international goal, the last one in a 3–0 victory. He made his first start in the third-place play-off against <a href="/wiki/Mexico_national_football_team" title="Mexico national football team">Mexico</a> on 29 June, but was sent off in the 53rd minute.</p>
<p>He was a member of the German squad for the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>, but missed the first two group stage matches due to the red card received in the Confederations Cup. He appeared as a substitute in Germany's 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> in the third-place play-off, replacing <a href="/wiki/Lukas_Podolski" title="Lukas Podolski">Lukas Podolski</a> after 71 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hanke appeared in three of Germany's qualifiers for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">Euro 2008</a>, earning the last of 12 caps on 17 November 2007 in a 4–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_national_football_team" title="Cyprus national football team">Cyprus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
