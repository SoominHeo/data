<p><b>Michele Pazienza</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[miˈkɛle patˈtsjɛntsa]</a></span>; born 5 August 1982) is an Italian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defensive_midfielder" title="Defensive midfielder">defensive midfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/Manfredonia_Calcio" title="Manfredonia Calcio">Manfredonia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michele_Pazienza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foggia">Foggia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michele_Pazienza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Foggia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pazienza was born in <a href="/wiki/San_Severo" title="San Severo">San Severo</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Foggia" title="Province of Foggia">province of Foggia</a>, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Foggia_Calcio" title="Foggia Calcio">Foggia Calcio</a>'s youth team. He made his debut with the first team in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C2" title="Serie C2">Serie C2</a> in 2000. Pazienza played with Foggia until 2003, scoring 6 goals in 88 appearances, and helping the team reach the promotion play-offs in 2002, and promotion to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C1" title="Serie C1">Serie C1</a> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carriera_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-carriera-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Udinese">Udinese</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michele_Pazienza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Udinese">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Serie_A" title="2003–04 Serie A">The following season</a> he then transferred to <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a>, also making his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> debut with the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carriera_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-carriera-1">[1]</a></sup> He made 52 appearances in his two seasons with the club.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fiorentina">Fiorentina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michele_Pazienza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fiorentina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He left on loan to <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Serie_A" title="2005–06 Serie A">2005–06</a> and <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Serie_A" title="2006–07 Serie A">2006–07</a> seasons<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> for a loan fee of €350,000 annually<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and was then purchased outright by the club in June 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-skysports_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-skysports-5">[5]</a></sup> for an additional fee of €3.15 million (a misc. fee that cost <i>La Viola</i> an additional €160,000 was later shifted to other company);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fiorentina2007bilancio_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fiorentina2007bilancio-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fiorentina2008bilancio_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fiorentina2008bilancio-7">[7]</a></sup> he made 52 league appearances for <i>la Viola</i> in total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carriera_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-carriera-1">[1]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Serie_A" title="2007–08 Serie A">2008</a> winter transfer window, he was purchased by <a href="/wiki/S.S.C._Napoli" title="S.S.C. Napoli">Napoli</a> in a 3½ year contract<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> for a transfer fee of €4.25 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fiorentina2007bilancio_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fiorentina2007bilancio-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fiorentina2008bilancio_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fiorentina2008bilancio-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In total, he made 106 appearances for the club, scoring 4 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carriera_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-carriera-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Juventus">Juventus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michele_Pazienza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Juventus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 June 2011 Pazienza signed a 3-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a> on a free transfer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-skysports_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-skysports-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> After only making 8 appearances under <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Conte" title="Antonio Conte">Antonio Conte</a>, he moved on loan from Juventus back to Udinese on 31 January 2012, where he played the remainder of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Serie_A" title="2011–12 Serie A">season</a>, making 15 appearances and scoring a goal, for a total of 23 Serie A appearances that season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bologna">Bologna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michele_Pazienza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bologna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 August 2012 he was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Bologna_F.C._1909" title="Bologna F.C. 1909">Bologna</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> for €300,000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> in a 3-year contract,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bologna2013bilancio_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bologna2013bilancio-13">[13]</a></sup> worth €1,081,615 in the first season and €1,261,569 in the second and the third season respectively in gross.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The transfer also cost Bologna an additional €366,000 as other fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bologna2013bilancio_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bologna2013bilancio-13">[13]</a></sup> Pazienza was ranked joint-4th as the highest earner of Bologna player in 2012–13 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Pazienza made 37 league appearances for Bologna in the first 2 seasons. However, he did not play any game in his last year of contract in <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Serie_B" title="2014–15 Serie B">2014–15 season</a>, which the club was relegated to Serie B.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vicenza_.26_Reggiana">Vicenza &amp; Reggiana</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michele_Pazienza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Vicenza &amp; Reggiana">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 14 July 2015 Pazienza was signed by Serie B club <a href="/wiki/Vicenza_Calcio" title="Vicenza Calcio">Vicenza Calcio</a> on a free transfer;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> he was awarded the number 4 shirt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 4 February 2016, he was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro" title="Lega Pro">Lega Pro</a> side <a href="/wiki/A.C._Reggiana_1919" title="A.C. Reggiana 1919">Reggiana</a> in a 5-month contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In summer 2016, along with other free agents, Pazienza obtained the license to be a youth team coach (UEFA B License).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manfredonia">Manfredonia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michele_Pazienza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Manfredonia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August he started to train with <a href="/wiki/Serie_D" title="Serie D">Serie D</a> club <a href="/wiki/Manfredonia_Calcio" title="Manfredonia Calcio">Manfredonia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> He signed a contract on 24 September.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_of_play">Style of play</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michele_Pazienza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Style of play">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although Pazienza primarily excels as a ball winner, and at breaking down opposition attacks, he is also capable of aiding his team offensively, due to his stamina, work-rate, dynamism, finesse, and ability to make attacking runs into the area; he is also able to aid his team creatively and is an effective assist provider.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
