<p><b>Shin-ichiro Miki</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">三木 眞一郎</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Miki Shin'ichirō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, born March 18, 1968 in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Voice_acting_in_Japan" title="Voice acting in Japan">Japanese voice actor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Doi1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Doi1-1">[1]</a></sup> He is a member of <a href="/wiki/81_Produce" title="81 Produce">81 Produce</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Doi1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Doi1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Miki is most known for the roles of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Pok%C3%A9mon_characters#Team_Rocket" title="List of Pokémon characters">Kojirō</a> ("James") (<i><a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_(anime)" title="Pokémon (anime)">Pokémon</a></i>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Takumi_Fujiwara" title="Takumi Fujiwara">Takumi Fujiwara</a> ("Tak") (<i><a href="/wiki/Initial_D" title="Initial D">Initial D</a></i>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kisuke_Urahara" title="Kisuke Urahara">Kisuke Urahara</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Bleach_(manga)" title="Bleach (manga)">Bleach</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/List_of_Virtua_Fighter_characters#Akira_Yuki" title="List of Virtua Fighter characters">Akira Yuki</a> (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virtua_Fighter_(series)" title="Virtua Fighter (series)">Virtua Fighter</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mobile_Suit_Gundam_00_characters#Lockon_Stratos" title="List of Mobile Suit Gundam 00 characters">Lockon Stratos</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Mobile_Suit_Gundam_00" title="Mobile Suit Gundam 00">Mobile Suit Gundam 00</a></i>), Teppei Iwaki (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Area_no_Kishi" title="Area no Kishi">Area no Kishi</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/List_of_Code:Breaker_characters#Hitomi" title="List of Code:Breaker characters">Hitomi</a> (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Code:_Breaker" title="Code: Breaker">Code: Breaker</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/List_of_Kuroko%27s_Basketball_characters#Others" title="List of Kuroko's Basketball characters">Kagetora Aida</a> (<i><a <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shin-ichiro_Miki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Miki is also a singer with the four-man band Weiß, along with <a href="/wiki/Takehito_Koyasu" title="Takehito Koyasu">Takehito Koyasu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tomokazu_Seki" title="Tomokazu Seki">Tomokazu Seki</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hiro_Y%C5%ABki" title="Hiro Yūki">Hiro Yūki</a>, the four main <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voice_actors" title="Voice actors">voice actors</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Wei%C3%9F_Kreuz" title="Weiß Kreuz">Weiß Kreuz</a></i>. Miki is also very active in <a href="/wiki/Yaoi" title="Yaoi">BL</a> dramas. He won Best Actors in supporting roles in the <a href="/wiki/4th_Seiyu_Awards" title="4th Seiyu Awards">4th Seiyu Awards</a>. He often gets cast as handsome, young men in anime roles, the most notable being <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Youji_Kudou&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Youji Kudou (page does not exist)">Youji Kudou</a> - Balinese of <i><a href="/wiki/Wei%C3%9F_Kreuz" title="Weiß Kreuz">Weiß Kreuz</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Pok%C3%A9mon_characters#Team_Rocket" title="List of Pokémon characters">Kojirō</a> ("James") in the <i><a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_(anime)" title="Pokémon (anime)">Pokémon</a></i> anime.</p>
<p>As well as voicing <a href="/wiki/List_of_Pok%C3%A9mon_characters#Team_Rocket" title="List of Pokémon characters">Kojirō</a> ("James") in the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese language</a> version of <i><a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon_(anime)" title="Pokémon (anime)">Pokémon</a></i>, Miki, like most of the Japanese voice actors, including <a href="/wiki/Mika_Kanai" title="Mika Kanai">Mika Kanai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Satomi_K%C5%8Drogi" title="Satomi Kōrogi">Satomi Kōrogi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Unsh%C5%8D_Ishizuka" title="Unshō Ishizuka">Unshō Ishizuka</a>, also appears in the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English language</a> versions, where he plays <a href="/wiki/Misty_(Pok%C3%A9mon)" title="Misty (Pokémon)">Misty</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Pok%C3%A9mon_(102%E2%80%93151)#Staryu" title="List of Pokémon (102–151)">Staryu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brock_(Pok%C3%A9mon)" title="Brock (Pokémon)">Brock</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Pok%C3%A9mon_(1%E2%80%9351)#Zubat" title="List of Pokémon (1–51)">Zubat</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Pok%C3%A9mon_(1%E2%80%9351)#Golbat" title="List of Pokémon (1–51)">Golbat</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Pok%C3%A9mon_(152%E2%80%93201)#Golbat" title="List of Pokémon (152–201)">Crobat</a>, and also <a href="/wiki/Ash_Ketchum" title="Ash Ketchum">Ash</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Charizard" title="Charizard">Charizard</a>.</p>
