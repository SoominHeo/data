<p><b>Hiroshi Mikitani</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">三木谷浩史</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mikitani Hiroshi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> (born March 11, 1965) is a Japanese businessman and the co-founder and CEO of <a href="/wiki/Rakuten" title="Rakuten">Rakuten</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hiroshi_Mikitani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mikitani was born in <a href="/wiki/Kobe" title="Kobe">Kobe</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyogo_prefecture" title="Hyogo prefecture">Hyogo prefecture</a>, Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Mikitani attended <a href="/wiki/Hitotsubashi_University" title="Hitotsubashi University">Hitotsubashi University</a>, graduating in 1988. While working for the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Bank_of_Japan" title="Industrial Bank of Japan">Industrial Bank of Japan</a>, Mikitani was transferred to the US in 1993. He studied at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Business_School" title="Harvard Business School">Harvard Business School</a>, earning the 9-Week certificate in Advanced Leadership in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>His father was an economist, Ryoichi Mikitani (Kobe University Professor Emeritus, Yale University Professor). His mother, Setsuko spent her elementary school age in New York. After her graduation from Kobe University, she worked for a major trading company. His sister, Ikuko is a physician (MD in Osaka University). His brother, Kenichi is a University of Tokyo professor in Biology.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Career">Early Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hiroshi_Mikitani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mikitani worked at the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Bank_of_Japan" title="Industrial Bank of Japan">Industrial Bank of Japan</a> (now part of <a href="/wiki/Mizuho_Corporate_Bank" title="Mizuho Corporate Bank">Mizuho Corporate Bank</a>) from 1988 to 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1997, he left to start his own consulting group called Crimson Group.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rakuten">Rakuten</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hiroshi_Mikitani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rakuten">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 7, 1997 Mikitani founded <a href="/wiki/Rakuten" title="Rakuten">Rakuten</a> with a few young co-founders and just 250,000 of their own money.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He was president from its founding, and in 2001 he also became chairman. In addition, he is also head of the E-Commerce and Banking Business Units and Head of the Development Unit. Among his other titles are also Chairman of Rakuten Travel, Inc., Chairman of Crimson Football Club, Inc., Director (President of Board of Directors) of PRICEMINISTER S.A.S., Director (Chairman) of Kobo Inc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Focus_on_English_in_Business">Focus on English in Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hiroshi_Mikitani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Focus on English in Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From March 2010, Mikitani has implemented a plan that he calls "Englishization", gradually making English the language of Rakuten, despite the fact the company is based in Japan with mainly Japanese staff. While the plan was dismissed as "stupid" by <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a> president Takanobu Ito in 2010, Mikitani believes that: "English is not an advantage anymore -- it is a requirement."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2011, Mikitani's Englishization initiative was featured in a <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Business_Review" title="Harvard Business Review">Harvard Business Review</a> case study.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Quitting_Keidanren">Quitting Keidanren</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hiroshi_Mikitani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Quitting Keidanren">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mikitani had joined <a href="/wiki/Japan_Business_Federation" title="Japan Business Federation">Keidanren</a>, the powerful Japanese business federation, in 2004. In June 2011, in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Fukushima_Daiichi_nuclear_disaster" title="Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster">Fukushima nuclear disaster</a>, he quit the federation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> announcing it via <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> before sending in his formal letter of resignation, saying it was no longer the same organization he had joined,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and he disagreed with its support for continued reliance on the nuclear industry for electricity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He subsequently pondered setting up a rival body.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 1, 2012 the Japanese Association of New Economy (JANE) was launched in Tokyo. It was a renaming of the "Japan e-business association", which had been established in February 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> to open it to non-online businesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Mikitani currently serves as the Representative Director of JANE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hiroshi_Mikitani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mikitani is married with two children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2015, Mikitani was valued at $8.7 billion by Forbes' World's Billionaires list, ranking at number 151.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recognition">Recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hiroshi_Mikitani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012, Mikitani was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Business_School" title="Harvard Business School">Harvard Business School</a> Alumni Achievement Award, one of the school's highest honors. He was also named to Prime Minister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinzo_Abe" title="Shinzo Abe">Shinzo Abe</a>'s Industrial Competitiveness Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Mikitani is also a recipient of the <a href="/wiki/Legion_of_Honour" title="Legion of Honour">Legion of Honour</a>, an award bestowed by the French government.</p>
