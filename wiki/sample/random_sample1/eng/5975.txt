<p><b>Michael II Asen</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg" xml:lang="bg">Михаил II Асен</span>)<sup class="reference" id="ref_a_1"><a href="#cnote_a">[a]</a></sup> ruled as emperor (<a href="/wiki/Tsar" title="Tsar">tsar</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Second_Bulgarian_Empire" title="Second Bulgarian Empire">Bulgaria</a> from 1246 to 1256. He was the son of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ivan_Asen_II" title="Ivan Asen II">Ivan Asen II</a> and his third wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irene_Komnene_of_Epirus" title="Irene Komnene of Epirus">Irene Komnene of Epirus</a> (nun Xene), daughter of <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Komnenos_Doukas" title="Theodore Komnenos Doukas">Theodore I Ducas</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Despotate_of_Epirus" title="Despotate of Epirus">Despotate of Epirus</a>. He was born between 1238 and 1241 and died in 1256.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_II_Asen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Michael II Asen was still a child when he ascended the throne in succession to his brother <a href="/wiki/Kaliman_I_of_Bulgaria" title="Kaliman I of Bulgaria">Kaliman Asen I</a>. It is assumed that his mother Eirene took over the government as <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regent</a>, but there is little evidence to prove this hypothesis. At the news of a second underage monarch on the Bulgarian throne, the neighboring powers, the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Nicaea" title="Empire of Nicaea">Empire of Nicaea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Despotate_of_Epirus" title="Despotate of Epirus">Despotate of Epirus</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Kingdom of Hungary</a>, invaded Bulgaria and annexed significant territories. The losses included <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a> to Nicaea, much of <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(region)" title="Macedonia (region)">Macedonia</a> to Epirus, and the <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a> area and the <a href="/wiki/Ban_(title)" title="Ban (title)">banate</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cara%C5%9F-Severin" title="Caraş-Severin">Severin</a> to Hungary. In spite of the losses, in 1247 Bulgaria was forced to aid Nicaea against the <a href="/wiki/Latin_Empire" title="Latin Empire">Latin Empire</a>.</p>
<p>In 1253 the government of Michael II Asen concluded a commercial and military treaty with the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ragusa" title="Republic of Ragusa">Republic of Ragusa</a> (a.k.a. <a href="/wiki/Dubrovnik" title="Dubrovnik">Dubrovnik</a>) directed against <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Uro%C5%A1_I" title="Stefan Uroš I">Stefan Uroš I</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Serbia_(medieval)" title="Kingdom of Serbia (medieval)">Kingdom of Serbia</a>. The Bulgarian attempt to conquer Serbia failed completely in spite of an inroad deep into Serbian territory.</p>

<p>The death of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_III_Ducas_Vatatzes" title="John III Ducas Vatatzes">John III Doukas Vatatzes</a> in 1254 inspired an attempt by the Bulgarians to recover the lands lost to Nicaea. By this time Michael II Asen was coming of age and he participated in the campaign, which met with initial success, overrunning Thrace and obtaining the surrender of various fortresses in the <a href="/wiki/Rhodope_Mountains" title="Rhodope Mountains">Rhodope Mountains</a> area by the sympathetic local population. However, the swift advance of the new emperor of Nicaea <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theodore_II_Lascaris" title="Theodore II Lascaris">Theodore II Doukas Laskaris</a> caught the Bulgarians by surprise, and Michael II Asen suffered accidental wounds during his precipitous flight through a forest. In the following year, 1255, Michael II Asen attempted to strike back with an army of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuman" title="Cuman">Cuman</a> <i>foederati</i> and again obtained some initial success. In 1256 the belligerents concluded a peace essentially reflecting conditions before the war.</p>
<p>The meagre sources leave the impression that Michael II Asen spent most of his reign under the influence of one or another powerful figure at court. This role was first fulfilled by his mother Eirene. By the treaty with Dubrovnik in 1253, the sources single out the <i><a href="/wiki/Sebastokrator" title="Sebastokrator">sebastokratōr</a></i> Peter, who had married Michael II Asen's sister Anna (or Theodora). The last years of Michael II Asen's reign seem to reflect the influence of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rostislav_of_Slavonia" title="Rostislav of Slavonia">Rostislav Mikhailovich</a>, a Russian prince of the house of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Chernihiv" title="House of Chernihiv">Chernigov</a>, who had settled in Hungary, married <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anna_of_Hungary_(born_1226)" title="Anna of Hungary (born 1226)">Anna of Hungary</a>, a daughter of King <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9la_IV_of_Hungary" title="Béla IV of Hungary">Béla IV of Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maria_Laskarina" title="Maria Laskarina">Maria Laskarina</a> of <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Nicaea" title="Empire of Nicaea">Nicaea</a>, and had become ban of the Belgrade area. Probably about this time Michael Asen I married a daughter (it is not certain which one) of Rostislav, and in 1256 Rostislav served as an intermediary at the signing of the peace treaty with Nicaea.</p>
<p>Perhaps infuriated with the concessions of Michael Asen I and his father-in-law in the peace treaty, a group of nobles rallied around the emperor's cousin <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaliman_II_of_Bulgaria" title="Kaliman II of Bulgaria">Kaliman Asen</a>. During a hunting trip in the vicinity of the capital <a href="/wiki/Veliko_Tarnovo" title="Veliko Tarnovo">Tărnovo</a>, Kaliman Asen murdered Michael Asen I and usurped the throne.</p>
