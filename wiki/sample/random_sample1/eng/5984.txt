<p><i><b>Najas marina</b></i> is a species of <a href="/wiki/Aquatic_plant" title="Aquatic plant">aquatic plant</a> known by the common names <b>spiny water nymph</b>, <b>spiny naiad</b> and <b>holly-leaved naiad</b>. It is an extremely widespread species, reported across Europe, Asia, Africa, Australia, the Americas and many oceanic islands. It can be found in many types of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freshwater" title="Freshwater">freshwater</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brackish_water" title="Brackish water">brackish</a> aquatic habitat, including bodies of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alkaline" title="Alkaline">alkaline</a> water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trulyscrumptious_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-trulyscrumptious-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollygolightly_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-hollygolightly-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p><i>Najas marina</i> is an annual producing a slender, branching stem up to 40 or 45 centimeters in maximum length. The evenly spaced leaves are up to 4 centimeters long, 1 to 3 millimeters wide, and edged in tiny sawlike teeth. The leaf has prickles along its midvein. Minute flowers occur in the leaf axils. The plant is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plant_sexuality" title="Plant sexuality">dioecious</a>, with male and female flower types occurring on separate individuals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trulyscrumptious_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-trulyscrumptious-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hollygolightly_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-hollygolightly-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>A long list of varietal and subspecific names have been proposed over the years. At present, only 9 are widely accepted:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lillytruscott_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-lillytruscott-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><i>Najas marina</i> subsp. <i>arsenariensis</i> <small>(Maire) L.Triest</small> - <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a></li>
<li><i>Najas marina</i> var. <i>brachycarpa</i> <small>Trautv.</small> - <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a></li>
<li><i>Najas marina</i> subsp. <i>commersonii</i> <small>L.Triest</small> - <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a></li>
<li><i>Najas marina</i> var. <i>grossidentata</i> <small>Rendle</small> - <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">Manchuria</a></li>
<li><i>Najas marina</i> var. <i>intermedia</i> <small>(Wolfg. ex Gorski) Rendle</small> - <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a></li>
<li><i>Najas marina</i> var. <i>marina</i></li>
<li><i>Najas marina</i> subsp. <i>marina</i></li>
<li><i>Najas marina</i> subsp. <i>sumatrana</i> <small>(W.J.de Wilde) L.Triest</small> - <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a></li>
<li><i>Najas marina</i> var. <i>zollingeri</i> <small>Rendle</small> - <a href="/wiki/Bali" title="Bali">Bali</a></li>
</ul>
