<p>Year <b>365</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">CCCLXV</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Common year starting on Saturday">common year starting on Saturday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Augustus and Valens</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1118 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 365 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=365&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=365&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>January – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alamanni" title="Alamanni">Alamanni</a> cross the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a> and invade <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a>. Emperor <a href="/wiki/Valentinian_I" title="Valentinian I">Valentinian I</a> moves to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> to command the army and defend the Gallic cities.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_21" title="July 21">July 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/365_Crete_earthquake" title="365 Crete earthquake">An earthquake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> devastates <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a> and affects <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Palestine_(region)" title="Palestine (region)">Palestine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_28" title="September 28">September 28</a> – <a href="/wiki/Procopius_(usurper)" title="Procopius (usurper)">Procopius</a> revolts and bribes two <a href="/wiki/Roman_legion" title="Roman legion">legions</a> passing by <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>. He proclaims himself <a href="/wiki/Roman_emperor" title="Roman emperor">Emperor</a>, and takes control of <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bithynia" title="Bithynia">Bithynia</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=365&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_30" title="March 30">March 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Sixteen_Kingdoms" title="Sixteen Kingdoms">Sixteen Kingdoms</a>: <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Fei_of_Jin" title="Emperor Fei of Jin">Jin Feidi</a>, age 23, succeeds his brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Ai_of_Jin_China" title="Emperor Ai of Jin China">Jin Aidi</a> as emperor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jin_Dynasty_(265-420)" title="Jin Dynasty (265-420)">Eastern Jin Dynasty</a>. He has no actual power; governmental matters are largely in the hands of his granduncle <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Jianwen_of_Jin" title="Emperor Jianwen of Jin">Sima Yu</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=365&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=365&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Basil_of_Caesarea" title="Basil of Caesarea">Basil of Caesarea</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/Presbyter" title="Presbyter">presbyter</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caesarea_Mazaca" title="Caesarea Mazaca">Caesarea</a>.</li>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Valens" title="Valens">Valens</a> orders the expulsion of the Alexandrian bishop <a href="/wiki/Athanasius_of_Alexandria" title="Athanasius of Alexandria">Athanasius</a> from his <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_see" title="Episcopal see">see</a>, but instead of going into exile Athanasius, now about 67, moves to the outskirts of Alexandria.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Antipope_Felix_II" title="Antipope Felix II">Antipope Felix II</a> dies after a 9-year reign, ending the double occupancy of the <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">papacy</a>.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/Tao_Yuanming" title="Tao Yuanming">Tao Qian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_poetry" title="Chinese poetry">Chinese poet</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jin_Dynasty_(265-420)" title="Jin Dynasty (265-420)">Eastern Jin Dynasty</a> (d. <a href="/wiki/427" title="427">427</a>)</li>
