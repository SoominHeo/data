<p>The <b>Democratic Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">민주당</span>, <i>Minju Dang</i>, DP) was a political party in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Democratic_Party_(South_Korea,_1955)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Democratic Party was established on 18 September 1955. It had its background in a 1952 move by the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Nationalist_Party_(South_Korea)" title="Democratic Nationalist Party (South Korea)">Democratic Nationalist Party</a> to create a pro-reform movement that included both party members and outside figures. Although not initially successful in overcoming the party's poor public image, controversy over a constitutional amendment vote towards the end of 1954 led to the formation of a 60-member bloc in Parliament that became the Democratic Party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HF_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-HF-1">[1]</a></sup> Several prominent conservatives joined the party, resulting in the party refusing to admit left-wing politicians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HF_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-HF-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party did not nominate a presidential candidate in the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_presidential_election,_1956" title="South Korean presidential election, 1956">1956 elections</a>, but put forward <a href="/wiki/Chang_Myon" title="Chang Myon">Chang Myon</a> as its vice-presidential candidate. Chang was elected with 46.4% of the vote. Public attitude turning against the rule of <a href="/wiki/Syngman_Rhee" title="Syngman Rhee">Syngman Rhee</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(South_Korea)" title="Liberal Party (South Korea)">Liberal Party</a> led to increased tensions, and Chang survived an assassination attempt in 1957.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HF_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-HF-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_legislative_election,_1958" title="South Korean legislative election, 1958">1958 parliamentary elections</a> the DP received 34% of the vote, winning 79 seats and finishing second to the ruling <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(South_Korea)" title="Liberal Party (South Korea)">Liberal Party</a>. The party nominated <a href="/wiki/Chough_Pyung-ok" title="Chough Pyung-ok">Chough Pyung-ok</a> as its candidate for the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_presidential_election,_March_1960" title="South Korean presidential election, March 1960">March 1960 presidential elections</a>. However, he died shortly before the elections, leaving Rhee as the sole candidate. Chang was defeated by Liberal candidate <a href="/wiki/Lee_Ki-poong" title="Lee Ki-poong">Lee Ki-poong</a> in the vice-presidential elections. However, student protests over the results led to Rhee going into exile before the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_legislative_election,_1960" title="South Korean legislative election, 1960">June parliamentary elections</a>, in which the DP won a landslide victory, taking 175 of the 233 seats in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_(South_Korea)" title="House of Commons (South Korea)">House of Commons</a> and 31 of the 58 seats in the <a href="/wiki/Senate_(South_Korea)" title="Senate (South Korea)">Senate</a>.</p>
<p>Despite gaining power, the party descended into infighting between the "new" and "old" factions; a compromise resulted in the "new" faction's Chang being elected Prime Minister and <a href="/wiki/Yun_Posun" title="Yun Posun">Yun Posun</a> of the "old" faction being <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_presidential_election,_August_1960" title="South Korean presidential election, August 1960">elected President</a> in an indirect election in August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HF_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-HF-1">[1]</a></sup> However, the internal tensions eventually resulted in the "old" faction breaking away to form a separate party.</p>
<p>However, after less than a year in power, the DP government was overthrown in the <a href="/wiki/May_16_coup" title="May 16 coup">May 16 coup</a> and the party ceased activities. However, when democracy was reintroduced in 1963, the party was reconstituted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HF_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-HF-1">[1]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_legislative_election,_1963" title="South Korean legislative election, 1963">1963 parliamentary elections</a> it won 13 seats, and the following year merged with the Citizens Party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HF_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-HF-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
