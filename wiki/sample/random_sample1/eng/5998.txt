<p>The <b>Milan Metro Line One</b> (<i>Linea Uno</i> in <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>) is the first underground <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit</a> line built in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Milan_Metro" title="Milan Metro">Milan Metro</a> and it is operated by <a href="/wiki/Azienda_Trasporti_Milanesi" title="Azienda Trasporti Milanesi">ATM</a>. Works on the line began in 1957, and the first part was opened on 1 November 1964,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-atmstoria_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-atmstoria-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cartamobilita2011_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-cartamobilita2011-5">[5]</a></sup> running from Sesto Marelli to Lotto station. The line is also called <b>Red Line</b> (<i>Linea Rossa</i> in Italian), as it is visually identified by red signs. Due to its premiership, the line gave its red color to the Milan Metro logo.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milan_Metro_Line_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The line runs underground from the northern suburb of <a href="/wiki/Sesto_San_Giovanni" title="Sesto San Giovanni">Sesto San Giovanni</a> to the city centre, then to western district with two different branches, one to northwest to <a href="/wiki/Rho,_Lombardy" title="Rho, Lombardy">Rho</a>, the other to the west to Bisceglie. It is 27 km (17 mi) long and serves 38 underground stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CR1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CR1-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Key points served by the line are <i><a href="/wiki/Piazza_del_Duomo,_Milan" title="Piazza del Duomo, Milan">Duomo</a></i>, considered the center of Milan; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castello_Sforzesco" title="Castello Sforzesco">Castello Sforzesco</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Cairoli_(Milan_Metro)" title="Cairoli (Milan Metro)">Cairoli</a> station); <i><a href="/wiki/Milano_Cadorna_railway_station" title="Milano Cadorna railway station">Cadorna</a></i>, one of the busiest station in Milan and in Italy; <i><a href="/wiki/Corso_Buenos_Aires" title="Corso Buenos Aires">Corso Buenos Aires</a></i> (with stations <a href="/wiki/Porta_Venezia_(Milan_Metro)" title="Porta Venezia (Milan Metro)">Porta Venezia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lima_(Milan_Metro)" title="Lima (Milan Metro)">Lima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Loreto_(Milan_Metro)" title="Loreto (Milan Metro)">Loreto</a>), an important shopping street; and <a href="/wiki/Fiera_Milano" title="Fiera Milano">Rho Fiera</a>, one of the largest fairground in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-about-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milan_Metro_Line_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On 6 April 1952 the city administration asked for a project of a metro system and on 6 October 1955 a new company, Metropolitana Milanese, was created to manage the construction of the new infrastructure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmstoria_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-mmstoria-8">[8]</a></sup> The project was funded with <a href="/wiki/Italian_lira" title="Italian lira">₤</a> 500 million from the municipality and the rest from a loan. The construction site of the first line was opened in <i>viale Monte Rosa</i> on 4 May 1957.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmstoria_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-mmstoria-8">[8]</a></sup> Stations on the new line were designed by Albini-Helg architecture studio. <a href="/wiki/Bob_Noorda" title="Bob Noorda">Bob Noorda</a> designed the famous signaling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmstoria_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-mmstoria-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>At first, stations were designed without the mezzanine floor. However, these were added to the final design to allow street crossing and the use of gates to collect tickets.</p>
<p>The line from <i>Lotto</i> to <i>Sesto Marelli</i> (21 stations) opened on 1 November 1964, after 7 years of construction works.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rolling_stock">Rolling stock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milan_Metro_Line_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rolling stock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are 4 types of trains running on the line: the original first series trains, revamped original trains, <a href="/wiki/AnsaldoBreda" title="AnsaldoBreda">AnsaldoBreda</a> <i><a href="/wiki/AnsaldoBreda_Meneghino" title="AnsaldoBreda Meneghino">Meneghino</a></i> trains and the new <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=AnsaldoBreda_Leonardo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="AnsaldoBreda Leonardo (page does not exist)">Leonardo</a> train introduced in 2015. The track gauge is the <span class="nowrap">1,435 mm</span> (<span class="nowrap">4 ft <span class="frac">8<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> in</span>) <span class="nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Standard_gauge" title="Standard gauge">standard gauge</a></span>. The entire line is electrified by means of a <a href="/wiki/Third_rail" title="Third rail">third</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fourth_rail" title="Fourth rail">fourth rail</a> at 750 V.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metrobits_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-metrobits-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Among the 63 trains running on the line, 20 entered service between 1964 (opening of the line) and 1970. Those trains are planned to be replaced by new Meneghino trains in the next few years. There are 17 Meneghino trains already operational as of March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oldtrains_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-oldtrains-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extension">Extension</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milan_Metro_Line_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Extension">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An extension on the north from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sesto_1%C2%B0_Maggio_(Milan_Metro)" title="Sesto 1° Maggio (Milan Metro)">Sesto Primo Maggio</a> to <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a> is currently under construction. It is expected to be completed by 2017. The new section will be 1.9 km (1.2 mi) long with 2 stations (<i>Sesto Restellone</i> and <i>Monza Bettola</i>), entirely underground. The total cost will be €206 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
