<p><b>Milena Govich</b> (born October 29, 1976) is an American actress, singer, dancer, and musician, best known for portraying <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NYPD" title="NYPD">NYPD</a> Detective <a href="/wiki/Nina_Cassady" title="Nina Cassady">Nina Cassady</a> on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milena_Govich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Govich was born in <a href="/wiki/Norman,_Oklahoma" title="Norman, Oklahoma">Norman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_County,_Oklahoma" title="Cleveland County, Oklahoma">Cleveland County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>. Both of her parents, Dr. Bruce Michael Govich, who died in 1998, and Dr. Marilyn Green Govich,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> are professors of music: Bruce worked at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oklahoma" title="University of Oklahoma">University of Oklahoma</a> and Marilyn currently teaches voice at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Central_Oklahoma" title="University of Central Oklahoma">University of Central Oklahoma</a>. Her father was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serbian_American" title="Serbian American">Serbian</a> and her mother is of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_American" title="Scottish American">Scottish</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_American" title="English American">English</a> ancestry. Her aunt, Milica Govich, is also an actress who has appeared on Broadway, television, and film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Milena graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southview_High_School_(Lorain,_Ohio)" title="Southview High School (Lorain, Ohio)">Southview High School</a> in 1994 as <a href="/wiki/Valedictorian" title="Valedictorian">valedictorian</a> of her class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She followed her academic parents by graduating from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Central_Oklahoma" title="University of Central Oklahoma">University of Central Oklahoma</a>, once again graduating <a href="/wiki/Valedictorian" title="Valedictorian">valedictorian</a> with a double-degree in performance and <a href="/wiki/Pre-medical" title="Pre-medical">pre-medical</a> studies, and a minor in <a href="/wiki/Dance" title="Dance">dance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">violin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newcoreproductions1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-newcoreproductions1-5">[5]</a></sup> After graduation, Govich moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> to pursue an acting career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milena_Govich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Govich has appeared on <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> in the musical revival of <i><a href="/wiki/Cabaret_(musical)" title="Cabaret (musical)">Cabaret</a></i>, joining the 1998–2004 production in December 2000. She began in the role of Lulu, featuring her singing, dancing, and playing violin, and was the understudy and eventual replacement for the lead role, Sally Bowles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Govich also performed on Broadway in the 2002 musical revival of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boys_from_Syracuse" title="The Boys from Syracuse">The Boys from Syracuse</a></i> and in the musical <a href="/wiki/Good_Vibrations_(musical)" title="Good Vibrations (musical)"><i>Good Vibrations</i></a>. She starred as Millie in the first regional production of <i>Thoroughly Modern Millie</i>. In July 2012, Govich starred in the title role of <i><a href="/wiki/Sweet_Charity" title="Sweet Charity">Sweet Charity</a></i> at The Lyric Theatre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On television, Govich first gained national attention in the United States as Gabby in the <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">pilot episode</a> of CBS's <i>Love Monkey</i>, then as the prostitute/con-artist Candy on <i><a href="/wiki/Rescue_Me_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Rescue Me (U.S. TV series)">Rescue Me</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She also starred in the Dick Wolf series <a href="/wiki/Conviction_(2006_TV_series)" title="Conviction (2006 TV series)"><i>Conviction</i></a> and then starred on the Dick Wolf series <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>. She has been featured or recurred on numerous other television shows: as <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/District_Attorney" title="District Attorney">District Attorney</a> Lyndsey Swann in the <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox_Television" title="20th Century Fox Television">20th Century Fox Television</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/K-Ville_(TV_series)" title="K-Ville (TV series)">K-Ville</a></i>; as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assistant_District_Attorney" title="Assistant District Attorney">Assistant District Attorney</a> Tracy Hunt on the new CBS TV series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Defenders_(2010_TV_series)" title="The Defenders (2010 TV series)">The Defenders</a></i>; and as Regina Turner on <i><a href="/wiki/Make_It_or_Break_It" title="Make It or Break It">Make It or Break It</a></i>.</p>
<p>Currently, Govich is on the MTV drama, <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Carter" title="Finding Carter">Finding Carter</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Govich has also appeared in several movies, including 2004's <i>Bad Behavior</i>, 2006's <i>In Love</i>, 2009's <i>Sordid Things</i>, 2011's <i>A Novel Romance</i>, starring opposite <a href="/wiki/Steve_Guttenberg" title="Steve Guttenberg">Steve Guttenberg</a>, and the upcoming feature <i>Lucky N#mbr</i>, starring opposite <a href="/wiki/Method_Man" title="Method Man">Method Man</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_.26_Order"><i>Law &amp; Order</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milena_Govich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Law &amp; Order">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Govich played the part of Jessica Rossi on the short-lived 2006 <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Legal_drama" title="Legal drama">legal drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Conviction_(2006_TV_series)" title="Conviction (2006 TV series)">Conviction</a></i>, created by <a href="/wiki/Dick_Wolf" title="Dick Wolf">Dick Wolf</a>. The day after that show was cancelled, Dick Wolf invited her to join the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i> for the 17th season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> which premiered on September 22, 2006. Govich played <a href="/wiki/Nina_Cassady" title="Nina Cassady">Det. Nina Cassady</a>, who joined the cast as the junior partner when <a href="/wiki/Jesse_L._Martin" title="Jesse L. Martin">Jesse L. Martin</a>'s character, <a href="/wiki/Ed_Green" title="Ed Green">Det. Ed Green</a>, was elevated to senior partner upon the retirement of <a href="/wiki/Joe_Fontana_(Law_%26_Order)" title="Joe Fontana (Law &amp; Order)">Det. Joe Fontana</a>, who was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Farina" title="Dennis Farina">Dennis Farina</a>. Govich guest-starred on the <i>Law &amp; Order</i> episode "Flaw" (<a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order_(season_16)#Episodes" title="Law &amp; Order (season 16)">Season 16</a>, Episode 2) in 2005, joining a list of <i>Law &amp; Order</i> cast regulars who did the same before having a starring role in the series. <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a> reported on June 1, 2007 that Govich would be replaced by <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Sisto" title="Jeremy Sisto">Jeremy Sisto</a>, marking the end of the program's only female detective.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andreeva_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Andreeva-13">[13]</a></sup> Govich said she was liked by police officers who thought Nina Cassady was a good character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andreeva_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Andreeva-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milena_Govich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Govich is married to David Cornue, a writer, Composer and film producer.They have no children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 10, 2008, Govich sang the <a href="/wiki/The_Star-Spangled_Banner" title="The Star-Spangled Banner">U.S. national anthem</a> at the <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Devils" title="New Jersey Devils">New Jersey Devils</a> home opener at the <a href="/wiki/Prudential_Center" title="Prudential Center">Prudential Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a>. She has also appeared twice on tour as the guest performer with China's biggest pop star, <a href="/wiki/Fei_Xiang" title="Fei Xiang">Fei Xiang</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newcoreproductions1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-newcoreproductions1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
