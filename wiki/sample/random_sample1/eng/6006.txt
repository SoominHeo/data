<p><b>Milko Kelemen</b> (born March 30, 1924) is a <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatian</a> composer.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Milko_Kelemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Milko Kelemen was born in <a href="/wiki/Slatina,_Croatia" title="Slatina, Croatia">Slatina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> (then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Serbs,_Croats_and_Slovenes" title="Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes">Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HDU_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-HDU-1">[1]</a></sup> He studied under <a href="/wiki/Stjepan_%C5%A0ulek" title="Stjepan Šulek">Stjepan Šulek</a> in <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>, under <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Messiaen" title="Olivier Messiaen">Olivier Messiaen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Fortner" title="Wolfgang Fortner">Wolfgang Fortner</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freiburg" title="Freiburg">Freiburg</a> amongst others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HDU_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-HDU-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kelemen founded the <a href="/wiki/Music_Biennale_Zagreb" title="Music Biennale Zagreb">Music Biennale Zagreb</a>, an international <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contemporary_music" title="Contemporary music">contemporary music</a> festival and has served as its <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">president</a> from 1961 to 1979.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vijenac_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vijenac-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He also worked at the Electronic <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a> Studio in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> and was invited to <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> as <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">Composer</a> in Residence.</p>
<p>Kelemen is a recipient of many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Awards" title="Awards">awards</a>, most notably the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Cross_of_Merit" title="Federal Cross of Merit">Federal Cross of Merit</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HDU_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-HDU-1">[1]</a></sup> the prize of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISCM" title="ISCM">ISCM</a>, the Great <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Yugoslav</a> State Prize, and the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> order <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chevalier_des_Arts_et_des_Lettres" title="Chevalier des Arts et des Lettres">Chevalier des Arts et des Lettres</a>.</p>
<p>He lives in <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. His works are published by <a href="/wiki/Hans_Sikorski" title="Hans Sikorski">Hans Sikorski</a> Music Publishers.</p>
