<p><b>Miltiadis Gouskos</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Μιλτιάδης Γκούσκος</span>, 1877 – 9 July 1903) was a <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletics_(sport)" title="Athletics (sport)">athlete</a>. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Zakynthos" title="Zakynthos">Zakynthos</a> and died in the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British Indian Empire</a> from food poisoning. He competed at the <a href="/wiki/1896_Summer_Olympics" title="1896 Summer Olympics">1896 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> in the shot put, placing second to <a href="/wiki/Robert_Garrett" title="Robert Garrett">Robert Garrett</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. Gouskos's best throw was 11.03 metres, short of Garrett's 11.22 metres.</p>
