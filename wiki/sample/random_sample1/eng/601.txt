<p>Year <b>378</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">CCCLXXVIII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Valens and Augustus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1131 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 378 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=378&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=378&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=378&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Spring – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Valens" title="Valens">Valens</a> returns to <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> and mobilises an army (40,000 men). He appoints <a href="/wiki/Sebastianus" title="Sebastianus">Sebastian</a>, newly arrived from Italy, as <i><a href="/wiki/Magister_militum" title="Magister militum">magister militum</a></i> to reorganize the Roman armies in <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a>.</li>
<li>February – The <a href="/wiki/Lentienses" title="Lentienses">Lentienses</a> (part of the <a href="/wiki/Alemanni" title="Alemanni">Alemanni</a>) cross the frozen <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a> and raid the countryside. They are driven back by Roman <i><a href="/wiki/Auxilia_palatina" title="Auxilia palatina">auxilia palatina</a></i> (Celtae and <a href="/wiki/Petulantes" title="Petulantes">Petulantes</a>), who defend the western frontier.</li>
<li>May – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Argentovaria" title="Battle of Argentovaria">Battle of Argentovaria</a>: Emperor <a href="/wiki/Gratian" title="Gratian">Gratian</a> is forced to recall the army he has sent East. The Lentienses are defeated by <a href="/wiki/Mallobaudes" title="Mallobaudes">Mallobaudes</a> near <a href="/wiki/Colmar" title="Colmar">Colmar</a> (France). Gratian gains the title <i>Alemannicus Maximus</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gothic_War_(376%E2%80%93382)" title="Gothic War (376–382)">Gothic War</a>: Valens sends Sebastian with a body of picked troops (2,000 men) to Thrace and renews the <a href="/wiki/Guerrilla_warfare" title="Guerrilla warfare">guerrilla war</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Goths" title="Goths">Goths</a>. He chases down small groups of Gothic raiders around <a href="/wiki/Edirne" title="Edirne">Adrianople</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fritigern" title="Fritigern">Fritigern</a> concentrates his army at <a href="/wiki/Kabile" title="Kabile">Cabyle</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>). The Goths are mainly centred in the river valleys south of the <a href="/wiki/Balkan_Mountains" title="Balkan Mountains">Balkan Mountains</a>, around the towns of <a href="/wiki/Stara_Zagora" title="Stara Zagora">Beroea</a>, Cabyle and <a href="/wiki/Burgas" title="Burgas">Dibaltum</a>.</li>
<li>July – <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Frigeridus</a>, Roman general, fortifies the Succi (<a href="/wiki/Ihtiman" title="Ihtiman">Ihtiman</a>) Pass to prevent the "barbarians" from breaking out to the north-west (<a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Pannonia" title="Diocese of Pannonia">Pannonia</a>).</li>
<li>Gratian sets out from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lauriacum" title="Lauriacum">Lauriacum</a> (Austria) with a body of light armed troops. His force is small enough to travel by boat down the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>. He halts for four days at <a href="/wiki/Sirmium" title="Sirmium">Sirmium</a> (<a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>) suffering from <a href="/wiki/Fever" title="Fever">fever</a>.</li>
<li>August – Gratian continues down the Danube to the "Camp of Mars" (frontier <a href="/wiki/Fortification" title="Fortification">fortress</a> near modern <a href="/wiki/Ni%C5%A1" title="Niš">Niš</a>), where he loses several men in an <a href="/wiki/Ambush" title="Ambush">ambush</a> by a band of <a href="/wiki/Alans" title="Alans">Alans</a>.</li>
<li>Fritigern strikes south from Cabyle, following the <a href="/wiki/Tundzha" title="Tundzha">Tundzha River</a> towards Adrianople, and tries to get behind the <a href="/wiki/Military_supply_chain_management" title="Military supply chain management">supply lines</a> to Constantinople.</li>
<li>Roman <a href="/wiki/Reconnaissance" title="Reconnaissance">reconnaissance</a> detects the Goths. Valens, already west of Adrianople, returns back and establishes a fortified camp outside the city.</li>
<li>The Goths, with their <a href="/wiki/Wagon" title="Wagon">wagons</a> and families vulnerable to attack, withdraw back to the north. Roman scouts fail to detect the <a href="/wiki/Greuthungi" title="Greuthungi">Greuthungi</a> cavalry <a href="/wiki/Foraging" title="Foraging">foraging</a> further up the Tundzha valley.</li>
<li>Fritigern sends a Christian <a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">priest</a> to the Roman camp with an offer of terms and a letter for Valens. The peace overtures are rejected.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_9" title="August 9">August 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Adrianople" title="Battle of Adrianople">Battle of Adrianople</a>: A large <a href="/wiki/Late_Roman_army" title="Late Roman army">Roman army</a> is defeated by the <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a>. Valens is killed along with two-thirds of his army.</li>
<li>The Goths attack Adrianople; they attempt to scale the <a href="/wiki/Defensive_wall" title="Defensive wall">city walls</a> with ladders but are repelled by the defenders, who drop lumps of <a href="/wiki/Masonry" title="Masonry">masonry</a>.</li>
<li>The Goths, supported by the <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a>, move on to Constantinople. Their progress is checked by the <a href="/wiki/Saracen" title="Saracen">Saracens</a>, recruited from <a href="/wiki/Arabia_Petraea" title="Arabia Petraea">Arab</a> tribes who control the eastern fringes of the empire.</li>
<li>October – The Greuthungi, faced with food shortages, split off and move west into Pannonia. Followed by their families, they raid villages and farmland.</li>
<li>Gratian recalls his military commander <a href="/wiki/Theodosius_I" title="Theodosius I">Flavius Theodosius</a>, age 31, son of the executed general <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theodosius_the_Elder" title="Theodosius the Elder">Theodosius the Elder</a>, and appoints him co-emperor of the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Eastern Roman Empire</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=378&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Mesoamerica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Siyaj_K%27ak%27" title="Siyaj K'ak'">Siyaj K'ak'</a> begins to replace <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Mayan</a> kings with relatives of <a href="/wiki/Spearthrower_Owl" title="Spearthrower Owl">Spearthrower Owl</a>, emperor of <a href="/wiki/Teotihuacan" title="Teotihuacan">Teotihuacan</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Siyaj_K%27ak%27" title="Siyaj K'ak'">Siyaj K'ak'</a> conquers <a href="/wiki/El_Per%C3%BA_(Maya_site)" title="El Perú (Maya site)">Waka</a> on January 8.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Siyaj_K%27ak%27" title="Siyaj K'ak'">Siyaj K'ak'</a> conquers <a href="/wiki/Tikal" title="Tikal">Tikal</a> on January 16.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Siyaj_K%27ak%27" title="Siyaj K'ak'">Siyaj K'ak'</a> conquers <a href="/wiki/Uaxactun" title="Uaxactun">Uaxactun</a>.</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/Gregory_of_Nazianzus" title="Gregory of Nazianzus">Gregory of Nazianzus</a> is ordained <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishop</a> of Constantinople.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pope_Damasus_I" title="Pope Damasus I">Pope Damasus I</a> is accused of <a href="/wiki/Adultery" title="Adultery">adultery</a> but is exonerated by Gratian.</li>
</ul>
