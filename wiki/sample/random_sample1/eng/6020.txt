<p><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Vanadzor&amp;params=40_48_46_N_44_29_18_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">40°48′46″N</span> <span class="longitude">44°29′18″E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">40.81278°N 44.48833°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">40.81278; 44.48833</span></span></span></a></span></span></span> <b>Vanadzor</b> (<a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>: <span lang="hy" xml:lang="hy">Վանաձոր</span> <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Armenian" title="Help:IPA for Armenian">[vɑnɑˈd͡zɔɾ]</a></span>), is the third-largest city in <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> and the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Lori_Province" title="Lori Province">Lori Province</a> in the northern part of the country. It is located around 128 kilometres (80 miles) north of the capital <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a>. As of the 2011 census, the city had a population of 86,199, down from 148,876 reported at the 1979 official census. The city was known as <b>Gharakilisa</b> (Karakilisa) during the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian rule</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Sovietization</a> of Armenia, the city was renamed <b>Kirovakan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>: <span lang="hy" xml:lang="hy">Կիրովական</span>) in 1935, after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolshevik" title="Bolshevik">Bolshevik</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Kirov" title="Sergey Kirov">Sergey Kirov</a>. After the independence of Armenia, Kirovakan was renamed <b>Vanadzor</b> in 1992.</p>
<p>The city is in a picturesque setting, with an attractive planned town centre. Mostly crumbling Soviet-era chemical factories dominate much of the valley below the city.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Khachqars-vanadzor.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="566" data-file-width="876" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Khachqars-vanadzor.jpg/220px-Khachqars-vanadzor.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Khachqars-vanadzor.jpg/330px-Khachqars-vanadzor.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Khachqars-vanadzor.jpg/440px-Khachqars-vanadzor.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Khachqars-vanadzor.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Old <a href="/wiki/Khachkar" title="Khachkar">khachkars</a> in Vanadzor</div>

</div>
<p>The area of present-day Vanadzor has been settled since the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a>, based on the tombs and other historic remains found on the nearby hills of <i>Tagavoranist</i> and <i>Mashtots</i>. During the ancient Armenian kingdoms, the area was considered as part of the Tashir canton of <a href="/wiki/Gugark" title="Gugark">Gugark</a>; the 13th province of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_(antiquity)" title="Kingdom of Armenia (antiquity)">Kingdom of Armenia</a> (Armenia Mayor), until the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artaxiad_Dynasty" title="Artaxiad Dynasty">Artaxiad Dynasty</a>'s rule over Armenia in the 1st century AD. Later, the region was ruled by the other Armenian dynasties of the <a href="/wiki/Arsacid_dynasty_of_Armenia" title="Arsacid dynasty of Armenia">Arsacids</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bagratuni_Dynasty" title="Bagratuni Dynasty">Bagratunis</a>. By the end of the 10th century, the area became part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Lori" title="Kingdom of Lori">Kingdom of Lori</a> (Kingdom of Tashir-Dzoraget) until the beginning of the 12th century. With the invasion of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seljuk_dynasty" title="Seljuk dynasty">Seljuk Turks</a>, the region became under the rule of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Seljuk_Empire" title="Great Seljuk Empire">Great Seljuk Empire</a>. The settlement was called <i>Gharakilisa</i> (meaning the <i>black church</i> in Turkic) by the Seljuks possibly as early as the 13th century, from the black-stoned Armenian church of the Holy Mother of God, on the nearby hill.</p>
<p>In 1801, the entire region of Lori became part of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> along with the <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgian</a> state, a fact that made Lori one of the strategically important territories for the Russian defensive forces on the border against Persia. In 1826, the settlement of Gharakilisa was entirely destroyed by Hasan Khan during the Russo-Persian war. In 1828, the Russians founded a new town to become a major centre for the deployment of their troops during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_(1828%E2%80%9329)" title="Russo-Turkish War (1828–29)">Russo-Turkish War of 1828–29</a>. According to <a href="/wiki/Khachatur_Abovian" title="Khachatur Abovian">Khachatur Abovian</a>, the population of Gharakilisa was not more than 600 by the end of the 1820s, mainly migrants from <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a>. In 1849, it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Erivan_Governorate" title="Erivan Governorate">Erivan Governorate</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>. Under the new administrative status, Gharakilisa was flooded with many hundreds of migrating Armenian families from <a href="/wiki/Kars" title="Kars">Kars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ardahan" title="Ardahan">Ardahan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Armenia" title="Western Armenia">Western Armenian</a> cities of <a href="/wiki/Erzurum" title="Erzurum">Karin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Do%C4%9Fubeyaz%C4%B1t" title="Doğubeyazıt">Daroynk</a>).</p>
<p>The town enjoyed considerable uplift through the opening of the railroad towards <a href="/wiki/Tbilisi" title="Tbilisi">Tbilisi</a> in 1899. The vicinity of the town had become site of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Karakilisa" title="Battle of Karakilisa">Battle of Karakilisa</a> in May 1918, when General <a href="/wiki/Tovmas_Nazarbekian" title="Tovmas Nazarbekian">Tovmas Nazarbekian</a>'s outnumbered troops, led by <a href="/wiki/Garegin_Nzhdeh" title="Garegin Nzhdeh">Garegin Nzhdeh</a> successfully defended the region from the invading <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkish_Army" title="Turkish Army">Turkish Army</a>, pushing them back just few days after the crucial Armenian victory in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Sardarapat" title="Battle of Sardarapat">Battle of Sardarapat</a>, thus allowing the <a href="/wiki/First_Republic_of_Armenia" title="First Republic of Armenia">Republic of Armenia</a> to come into existence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On the North side of the <a href="/wiki/Spitak" title="Spitak">Spitak</a>-Vanadzor highway, about 2 km (1 mi) West of the city, there is a little shrine in the ruins of a church, site of a planned monument to that battle.</p>

<p>However, after 2 years of brief independence, Armenia fell under the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> rule. The first city development plan of Gharakilisa initiated by architects <a href="/wiki/Karo_Halabyan" title="Karo Halabyan">Karo Halabyan</a>, Mikayel Mazmanyan and Gevork Kochar was adopted in 1929–1930. Under the new plan, the town was enlarged towards the eastern and western parts. In 1935, the Soviets renamed the city <i>Kirovakan</i> after the Bolshevik leader <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Kirov" title="Sergey Kirov">Sergey Kirov</a>. In 1939, the new remodeling plan of the city created by architects N. Zargaryan and A. Minasyan was introduced to create an industrial district and a summer-resort area. The centre of the city was redeveloped during the 1950s. The central town square named after Sergey Kirov (now <a href="/wiki/Hayk_Square" title="Hayk Square">Hayk Square</a>) was opened along with the surrounding government and administrative buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the independence of Armenia, Kirovakan was renamed <i>Vanadzor</i> after the <a href="/wiki/Vanadzor_River" title="Vanadzor River">Vanadzor River</a> that flows through the city.</p>
<p>Vanadzor, along with <a href="/wiki/Gyumri" title="Gyumri">Gyumri</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spitak" title="Spitak">Spitak</a> (25 km (16 mi) west), suffered a considerable amount of damage from the <a href="/wiki/1988_Armenian_earthquake" title="1988 Armenian earthquake">1988 earthquake</a> when 564 residents died in the city. Unlike the two other cities, the majority of buildings in Vanadzor were unscathed from the earthquake.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Forests_near_Vanadzor.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Forests_near_Vanadzor.jpg/220px-Forests_near_Vanadzor.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Forests_near_Vanadzor.jpg/330px-Forests_near_Vanadzor.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Forests_near_Vanadzor.jpg/440px-Forests_near_Vanadzor.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Forests_near_Vanadzor.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Forests around Vanadzor</div>
</div>

<p>Vanadzor, the capital of Lori Province, is located 128 km (80 mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> and 64 km (40 mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Gyumri" title="Gyumri">Gyumri</a>.</p>
<p>At an average height of 1,350 metres (4,430 feet) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_sea_level" title="Above sea level">above sea level</a>, Vanadzor is located in the valley of <a href="/wiki/Pambak_River" title="Pambak River">Pambak River</a>, on the point where the rivers of <a href="/wiki/Tandzut_River" title="Tandzut River">Tandzut</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vanadzor_River" title="Vanadzor River">Vanadzor</a> join the Pambak river. The city, is surrounded with the over 2,500-metre-high (8,200-foot) mountains of Bazum and Pambak. The southern and eastern regions are densely forested while to the north and west are only covered with bushes and plants.</p>
<p>The climate of Vanadzor is characterized with cool summers and relatively mild winters. The average temperature in winter is −4.2 °C (24.4 °F), while in summer it reaches up to 20 °C (68 °F). Vanadzor's climate is classified as <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">humid continental</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Dfb</i>). The amount of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> is around 650 mm (25.6 inches).</p>
<p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor_Botanical_Garden&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vanadzor Botanical Garden (page does not exist)">Vanadzor Botanical Garden</a> located to the southwest of the city is one of the 5 botanical parks within the Republic of Armenia.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The population of Vanadzor has grown gradually since 1849, after becoming part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yerevan_Governorate" title="Yerevan Governorate">Yerevan Governorate</a>.</p>
<p>The population of Vanadzor increased significantly after the Second World War, when Stalin allowed open immigration to the Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic with the promise of a better life in the historical homeland. More than 150,000 Armenians from different parts of Europe and the Middle East, most being survivors of the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide" title="Armenian Genocide">Armenian Genocide</a>, immigrated and settled in Vanadzor (Kirovakan). Due to the harsh conditions imposed by the Soviets' intelligence and Ministry of the Interior, many of the immigrants were sent to labour camps in Siberia and elsewhere. The reason for such disrespectful treatment towards the Armenian immigrants was because almost all were more educated than the local community, spoke more than 3 languages, and some were open believers of Armenian major religious sects (Apostolic, Evangelical and Catholic) which was considered a threat to national security by the Stalinist Government.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Despite the difficulties more Western Armenians immigrated to Kirovakan in following years.</p>
<p>Most of the immigrants left Vanadzor and went back to Cyprus, Lebanon, Greece, France and the USA. Today there are approximately 2000 Western Armenian settlers living in Vanadzor, most of whom still use the Western Armenian dialect at home.</p>
<p>The majority of the city's population are ethnic Armenians with minor Russian, Ukrainian and Greek communities.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Grigor_Narekatsi_Church.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3456" data-file-width="4704" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Grigor_Narekatsi_Church.png/220px-Grigor_Narekatsi_Church.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Grigor_Narekatsi_Church.png/330px-Grigor_Narekatsi_Church.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Grigor_Narekatsi_Church.png/440px-Grigor_Narekatsi_Church.png 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The majority of the population belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Apostolic_Church" title="Armenian Apostolic Church">Armenian Apostolic Church</a>. Vanadzor is the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Gougark" title="Diocese of Gougark">Diocese of Gougark</a> of the Armenian Apostolic Church, serving the population of Lori province.</p>
<p>As of 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, The city is home to 4 churches and 2 chapels:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Church_of_the_Holy_Mother_of_God,_Vanadzor" title="Church of the Holy Mother of God, Vanadzor">Church of the Holy Mother of God</a>: opened in 1831, also known as Gharakilisa (meaning the Black church), is one of the oldest preserved churches of the city. It was completely renovated in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Kamakatar Chapel, located at the northeast of the city: although it originally dates back to the medieval period, however the current building was reconstructed in 1841.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Church_of_the_Nativity_of_Blessed_Virgin_Mary&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Church of the Nativity of Blessed Virgin Mary (page does not exist)">Church of the Nativity of Blessed Virgin Mary</a>, opened in 1895, is serving the city's small Russian Orthodox community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>Surp Sarkis Church of Vanadzor, opened in 2000, located near the prelacy building.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Gregory_of_Narek_Cathedral&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Saint Gregory of Narek Cathedral (page does not exist)">Saint Gregory of Narek Cathedral</a>, opened in 2005, named after Saint <a href="/wiki/Gregory_of_Narek" title="Gregory of Narek">Gregory of Narek</a> to commemorate the 1000th anniversary of his <b>Book of Lamentations</b>. It is currently the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Gougark" title="Diocese of Gougark">Diocese of Gougark</a> of the Armenian Apostolic Church</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vanadzor-buildings1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Vanadzor-buildings1.jpg/220px-Vanadzor-buildings1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Vanadzor-buildings1.jpg/330px-Vanadzor-buildings1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Vanadzor-buildings1.jpg/440px-Vanadzor-buildings1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vanadzor-buildings1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Vanadzor House of Culture named after <a href="/wiki/Charles_Aznavour" title="Charles Aznavour">Charles Aznavour</a></div>

<p>Vanadzor has several archaeological sites including the nearby Mashtots hill which is home to many remains from the 4th millennium BC. The Lori-Pambak archaeological museum opened in 1938 is dedicated to the history and ethnography of Gugark region. It currently has a collection of around 34,000 pieces.</p>
<p>Vanadzor is the cultural centre of Lori Province and one of the prominent centres within Armenia. The House of Culture named after Charles Aznavour is one of the largest ones in Armenia. The Vanadzor fine art museum is operating since 1974, while the house-museum of writer <a href="/wiki/Stepan_Zoryan" title="Stepan Zoryan">Stepan Zoryan</a> is operating in 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city is home to many theatres including the <i>Bohem Chamber Theatre of Vanadzor</i>, <i>Vanadzor Musical Theatre</i>, <i>Vanadzor Drama Theatre named after Hovhannes Abelyan</i>, and <i>Vanadzor Puppet Theatre</i>. Other famous art ensembles of Vanadzor include the <i>Vanadzor Chamber Music Chorus</i>, <i>Vanadzor Chamber Music Band</i>, and <i>Horevel Dance Ensemble</i>.</p>
<p>Vanadzor has also produced many famous <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">hard rock</a> bands including <i>Lousnelius</i>, <i>Lav Eli</i> and <i>Vordan Karmir</i>.</p>
<p>As of 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Vanadzor has 4 public libraries as well as 4 children's libraries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>The city is connected with other major cities of Armenia with a railway and a motorway. The M-6 Motorway that connects the capital Yerevan with the Georgian capital <a href="/wiki/Tbilisi" title="Tbilisi">Tbilisi</a> passes through Vanadzor. The city is connected with Tavish Province to the east through the M-8 Motorway.</p>
<p>Vanadzor has a railway station that also connects the city with northern and central Armenia.</p>
<p>The nearest airport to the town is the <a href="/wiki/Stepanavan_Airport" title="Stepanavan Airport">Stepanavan Airport</a> (44 km (27 mi) north) which is under renovation and is envisaged to be ready by 2016.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vanadzor-usine.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="971" data-file-width="1600" height="134" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Vanadzor-usine.jpg/220px-Vanadzor-usine.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Vanadzor-usine.jpg/330px-Vanadzor-usine.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Vanadzor-usine.jpg/440px-Vanadzor-usine.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vanadzor-usine.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Chemical industry in Vanadzor</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The city of Vanadzor is the main industrial centre of the province. The industry of the city is quite diversified with many firms specialized in garment manufacturing, chemical products, building materials, and dairy products. The largest garment manufacturers of the city are the "Dav-Gar" garment factory founded in 1962, the Darbbag's Sewing Factory founded in 1977 (privatized in 1996), the "Sarton" Sewing Factory founded in 1990, "Ruz-Dan" garment factory founded in 2000. The largest chemical plants of Vanadzor include the "Vanadzor ChimProm" founded in 1929, the "Gipk" enterprise for polymeric materials founded in 1967, and the "Gary Group" for polymeric materials founded in 1998. The "Beton" enterprise founded in 1988, the "Bionik" plant founded in 2004, and the "Karaberd" plant are specialized in building materials. The "DIET LLC", the "K&amp;K" factory, and the "Katnagorts" plant are specialized in dairy products. Othe major industrial plants of Vanadzor include the "Ardvin" electromechanical plant founded in 1956, the "Gems De Luxe" precious stone processing enterprise founded in 1996, and the "Jerutsogh" factory for heating equipments.</p>
<p>Many small plants for the production of building materials, mainly <a href="/wiki/Tufa" title="Tufa">tufa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basalt" title="Basalt">basalt</a> and clay are also operating in Vanadzor.</p>
<p>The city is home to the Vanadzor Thermal Power Plant opened in 1961.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vanadzor is a resort centre in northern Armenia due to its mild climate, clean air and mineral springs. Many hotels and spa resorts are built in the city and the nearby countryside. The city is connected with other major cities of Armenia with a railway and a motorway. The Armenia Vanadzor Spa and Hotel Complex is among the most significant resorts since the Soviet times.</p>

<p>Vanadzor is home to many educational institutions serving the population of the city and the residents of Lori and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tavush" title="Tavush">Tavush</a> provinces.</p>
<p>The following educational institutions are currently operating in the city:</p>
<ul>

<li>Faculty of History and Geography,</li>
<li>Faculty of Pedagogy,</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanadzor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The most popular sport in the city is <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a>. <a href="/wiki/FC_Lori" title="FC Lori">FC Lori</a> founded in 1936, is one of the oldest football clubs in Armenia. After the independence of Armenia, the team represented the city between 1991 and 2006 in domestic competitions, until it was dissolved due to financial difficulties.</p>
<p>The largest sport venue in the city is the <a href="/wiki/Lori_Stadium" title="Lori Stadium">Lori Stadium</a> with a capacity of 5,000 spectators. It is mainly used for football matches. A renovation plan was scheduled in order to redevelop the stadium to meet the requirements of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other popular sports in the city include: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_handball" title="Team handball">handball</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Footsal" title="Footsal">footsal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">volleyball</a>. The city is home to many sport schools for swimming, athletics, weightlifting, chess, gymnastics, table tennis and badminton.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Football_Federation_of_Armenia" title="Football Federation of Armenia">FFA</a> <a href="/wiki/Vanadzor_Football_Academy" title="Vanadzor Football Academy">Vanadzor Football Academy</a> opened its doors on 29 October 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> It is home to 3 natural grass and 1 artificial turf regular-sized pitches.</p>
<p>There is also a ski resort near Vanadzor.</p>
