<p><b>Bagna càuda</b> (<small>Italian: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[ˈbaɲɲa ˈkauda]</a></span>; <small>locally: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">[ˈbaɲa ˈkɑʊda]</a></span>; from the <a href="/wiki/Piedmontese_language" title="Piedmontese language">Piedmontese</a> "hot dip",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> alternatively written <i>bagna caôda</i> or <i>bagnacauda</i>, etymologically related to <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> root <i>bagn-</i>, meaning "wet", and <i>caldo</i>, meaning "hot") is a warm dip typical of <a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">Piedmont</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, but with numerous local variations. The dish, which is served and consumed in a manner similar to <a href="/wiki/Fondue" title="Fondue">fondue</a>, is made with <a href="/wiki/Garlic" title="Garlic">garlic</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anchovy_(food)" title="Anchovy (food)">anchovies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olive_oil" title="Olive oil">olive oil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Butter" title="Butter">butter</a>, and in some parts of the region <a href="/wiki/Cream" title="Cream">cream</a>. In the past <a href="/wiki/Walnut_oil" title="Walnut oil">walnut</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hazelnut_oil" title="Hazelnut oil">hazelnut</a> oil would have been used.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Sometimes, <a href="/wiki/Truffle" title="Truffle">truffles</a> are used in versions around <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alba,_Italy" title="Alba, Italy">Alba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bc1960_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-bc1960-3">[3]</a></sup> The dish is eaten by dipping raw, boiled or roasted vegetables, especially <a href="/wiki/Cardoon" title="Cardoon">cardoon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carrot" title="Carrot">carrot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bell_pepper" title="Bell pepper">peppers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fennel" title="Fennel">fennel</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bc1960_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-bc1960-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Celery" title="Celery">celery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cauliflower" title="Cauliflower">cauliflower</a>, <a href="/wiki/Artichoke" title="Artichoke">artichokes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Onion" title="Onion">onions</a>. It is traditionally eaten during the autumn and winter months, particularly at Christmas and New Year's, and must be served hot, as the name suggests. Originally, in Piedmont, the Bagna càuda was placed in a big pan (<i>peila</i>) in the center of the table for communal sharing. Now, it is usually served in individual pots (the <i>fojòt</i>, a type of fondue pot traditionally made of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terra_cotta" title="Terra cotta">terra cotta</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Bagna cauda is also a popular winter dish in central <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, an area of predominantly Northern Italian descent.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bagna_c%C3%A0uda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The US television series <i><a href="/wiki/Babylon_5" title="Babylon 5">Babylon 5</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/A_Distant_Star" title="A Distant Star">A Distant Star</a>" featured <a href="/wiki/Michael_Garibaldi" title="Michael Garibaldi">Michael Garibaldi</a>'s effort to smuggle the ingredients for the dish onto the space station in defiance of doctor's orders. A later episode, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Late_Delivery_From_Avalon" title="A Late Delivery From Avalon">A Late Delivery From Avalon</a>", showed him once again having trouble acquiring the ingredients, this time due to a trade embargo and an obstructive <a href="/wiki/Mail_carrier" title="Mail carrier">mail carrier</a>.</p>
