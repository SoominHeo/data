<p>On 7 May 2011, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_uprising_phase_of_the_Syrian_civil_war" title="Civil uprising phase of the Syrian civil war">Civil uprising phase of the Syrian civil war</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_of_Syria" title="Military of Syria">Syrian military</a> launched an operation in the Syrian city of <a href="/wiki/Baniyas" title="Baniyas">Baniyas</a>. The government said it was targeting terrorist groups, while the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_opposition" title="Syrian opposition">Syrian opposition</a> called it a crackdown against pro-democracy protesters. The operation lasted until 14 May 2011.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siege_of_Baniyas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 15 March 2011, a protest movement against the Syrian government began to escalate, as simultaneous demonstrations took place in major cities across Syria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 18 March, the most serious unrest to take place in Syria for decades erupted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aljaz18311_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-aljaz18311-7">[7]</a></sup> After online calls for a "Friday of Dignity" (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">جمعة الكرامة</span>‎‎), after Friday prayers, thousands of protesters demanding an end to alleged government corruption took to the streets of cities across Syria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The protesters were met with violent opposition by state security forces. The protesters chanted "God, Syria, Freedom" and anti-corruption slogans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc18311_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc18311-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 April, unknown gunmen shot at a military bus traveling through Baniyas killing nine soldiers.</p>
<p>On 10 April, protests were held in Baniyas in which violent clashes erupted between the security forces and protesters. Between three and six were reportedly shot dead, while one police officer was reportedly killed by unknown gunmen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 April, snipers killed a Syrian Army soldier in Baniyas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_operation">The operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Siege_of_Baniyas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On 7 May, following the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Daraa" title="Siege of Daraa">successful operation against protestors in Daraa</a>, Syrian Army tanks rolled into Baniyas from three directions. They advanced into Sunni districts, which had been under the control of demonstrators since loyalist forces, known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shabeeha" title="Shabeeha">Shabeeha</a>, fired at opposition members on 10 April. Heavy gunfire was reported as the operation started.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The next day, some 30 tanks were seen patrolling the city, with some of them positioned in the city center. <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Navy" title="Syrian Navy">Syrian Navy</a> boats were also reportedly holding positions near the city's coastline. Special forces units allegedly entered the northern part of the city, from where heavy gunfire was heard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 14 May, the military started to pull out of the city, after their operation ended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
