<p>The <b>Vanilla Alliance</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Alliance Vanille</i></span>) is an <a href="/wiki/Airline_alliance" title="Airline alliance">airline alliance</a> formed in September 2015 to improve air connectivity within the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> region by strengthening cooperation between the airlines of <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean_Commission" title="Indian Ocean Commission">Indian Ocean Commission</a> members. Ultimately, the alliance wishes to increase tourism, trade, and business ties in the region. The founding members are <a href="/wiki/Air_Austral" title="Air Austral">Air Austral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_Madagascar" title="Air Madagascar">Air Madagascar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_Mauritius" title="Air Mauritius">Air Mauritius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_Seychelles" title="Air Seychelles">Air Seychelles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Int%27Air_%C3%8Eles" title="Int'Air Îles">Int'Air Îles</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanilla_Alliance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In August 2010, <a href="/wiki/Seychelles" title="Seychelles">Seychelles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Comoros" title="Comoros">Comoros</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayotte" title="Mayotte">Mayotte</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a> united under the brand "<a href="/wiki/Vanilla_Islands" title="Vanilla Islands">Vanilla Islands</a>" to promote tourism to the entire region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ink_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ink-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012 the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean_Commission" title="Indian Ocean Commission">Indian Ocean Commission</a> (IOC) hosted a conference on the state of air travel within the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ink_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ink-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lemauricien_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-lemauricien-5">[5]</a></sup> It was determined that the current business model of the airlines was unsustainable and that ticket prices for regional air travel were too high, compared to intra-<a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and intra-<a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> fares.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airwaysnews_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-airwaysnews-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2–3 May 2013, the IOC held another conference in Mauritius on the problems with regional air travel. In January 2014, the Commission published a plea entitled "Wings of the Indian Ocean", in which it called for a joint strategy on regional air transport and noted the economic benefits it would have.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lemauricien_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-lemauricien-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The IOC held another conference in July 2014 on tourism and air travel, after which a committee of airline officials and another of civil aviation authorities were created to form the alliance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ink_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-ink-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lemauricien_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-lemauricien-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 20 May 2015, the Council of Ministers of the IOC met in <a href="/wiki/Antananarivo" title="Antananarivo">Antananarivo</a> to commit to signing an agreement for the creation of the alliance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The airlines were expected to sign it on 18 June, however this date was postponed due to a <a href="/wiki/General_strike" title="General strike">strike</a> at <a href="/wiki/Air_Madagascar" title="Air Madagascar">Air Madagascar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airwaysnews_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-airwaysnews-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 21 September 2015, founding airlines <a href="/wiki/Air_Austral" title="Air Austral">Air Austral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_Madagascar" title="Air Madagascar">Air Madagascar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_Mauritius" title="Air Mauritius">Air Mauritius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_Seychelles" title="Air Seychelles">Air Seychelles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Int%27Air_%C3%8Eles" title="Int'Air Îles">Int'Air Îles</a> signed the pact in Antananarivo. The signing was attended by Secretary General Jean-Claude de l'Estrac of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean_Commission" title="Indian Ocean Commission">Indian Ocean Commission</a> and Malagasy president <a href="/wiki/Hery_Rajaonarimampianina" title="Hery Rajaonarimampianina">Hery Rajaonarimampianina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chaviation_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-chaviation-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mggovt_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mggovt-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ink_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-ink-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Goals">Goals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanilla_Alliance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Goals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The initial goals of the Vanilla Alliance include increased air service between the islands, more attractive fares, and optimisation of <a href="/wiki/Codeshare_agreement" title="Codeshare agreement">codesharing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airwaysnews_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-airwaysnews-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> L'Estrac also suggested the creation of a new <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost regional airline</a> to attract more tourists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lemauricien_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-lemauricien-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
