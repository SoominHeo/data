<p><i><b>Umi Monogatari ~Anata ga Ite Kureta Koto~</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">うみものがたり 〜あなたがいてくれたコト〜</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. "Sea Story: That You Were There For Me")</span> is a 12 episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> series, directed by <a href="/wiki/Junichi_Sato" title="Junichi Sato">Junichi Sato</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Zexcs" title="Zexcs">Zexcs</a>. It began airing on <a href="/wiki/Chubu-Nippon_Broadcasting" title="Chubu-Nippon Broadcasting">Chubu-Nippon Broadcasting</a> on June 24, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> An unaired 13th episode was featured in the final DVD volume, released on March 26, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The anime is based on a <a href="/wiki/Pachinko" title="Pachinko">pachinko</a> game manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Sanyo" title="Sanyo">Sanyo Bussan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Umi_Monogatari&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The story centers on Marin and Urin, two “pure” sisters who live alongside the fish in the sea, but yearn to be in the world beyond the water and above ground. One day, a beautiful ring falls in the middle of the sea, and Marin and Urin retrieve it. The two decide to leave their waterbound world for the first time to deliver the ring. After an arduous journey, they come across an isolated island where a high school girl named Kanon lives. Accidentally, Urin unleashes a power sealed long time ago in the island, and the only ones who can defeat it are Marin and Kanon, as priestesses of the sea and the sky.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Umi_Monogatari&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl>
<dt>Marin <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">マリン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Marin</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Kana_Asumi" title="Kana Asumi">Kana Asumi</a></span></dd>
</dl>
<p>A sea dweller who lives with Urin, Sam, and Warin. While people of the sea are born from shells and thus lack parents or direct family, she considers Urin her sister, whom she showers with affection. When Kanon's ring is thrown into the sea, she is the one who finds it and decides to search for its owner. Later, she becomes the priestess of sea and starts fighting Sedna along with Kanon.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Kanon Miyamori <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">宮守夏音</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Miyamori Kanon</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Minako_Kotobuki" title="Minako Kotobuki">Minako Kotobuki</a></span></dd>
</dl>
<p>A high school senior who lives at the island. She is the owner of the ring that Marin found (it was a gift from her boyfriend), which she angrily discarded when she decided to break up. After Urin unleashes the power of Sedna, she becomes the priestess of sky.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Urin <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ウリン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Urin</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Yui_Horie" title="Yui Horie">Yui Horie</a></span></dd>
</dl>
<p>A sea dweller whom Marin sees as her little sister, and who sees Marin as her big sister. She is the one who accidentally unleashes Sedna's power. In the middle of the series, she becomes Sedna's vessel.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Warin <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ワリン</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Warin</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Yukari_Fukui" title="Yukari Fukui">Yukari Fukui</a></span></dd>
</dl>
<p>A sea dweller, friend of Marin and Urin, and frequent companion of Sam.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Sam <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">サム</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Samu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Junji_Majima" title="Junji Majima">Junji Majima</a></span></dd>
</dl>
<p>Another sea dweller who is a friend of Marin and Urin, and frequent companion of Warin.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Matsumoto <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">松本</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Matsumoto</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Rokur%C5%8D_Naya" title="Rokurō Naya">Rokurō Naya</a></span></dd>
</dl>
<p>An old turtle whose form was a statue in the island's forest until it came to life when Sedna was unsealed. Speaking human language, he advises Marin and Kanon regarding their roles as priestesses, comes to live in Kanon's house, and can generate the fireball needed for one of Kanon's spells.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Kojima <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">小島</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kojima</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Miyuki_Sawashiro" title="Miyuki Sawashiro">Miyuki Sawashiro</a></span></dd>
</dl>
<p>Kanon's ex-boyfriend. He and Kanon have some feelings toward each other, and Kojima once gave Kanon a ring, but at the start of the series, Kanon ended their relationship over due to tensions regarding how it was evolving.</p>
<dl>
<dt>Oshima <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">大島</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Oshima</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Aki_Toyosaki" title="Aki Toyosaki">Aki Toyosaki</a></span></dd>
</dl>
<p>A schoolmate of Kanon and Kojima, she is romantically interested in Kojima (and tries to be conspicuous about this), though he does not share her feelings. She considers Kanon to be her rival for Kojima's affections.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Umi_Monogatari&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two manga adaptations of <i>Umi Monogatari</i> were serialized in two different magazines by different publishers. The first one, with the title <i>Umi Monogatari ~Anata ga Ite Kureta Koto~</i>, follows the same story as the anime. The second, titled <i>Umi Monogatari: Minna Aishiteru!</i> (Sea Story: Everyone, I love you!), has a <a href="/wiki/Yonkoma" title="Yonkoma">yonkoma</a> format and follows Marin and Urin's experiences on the island.</p>
<p>The first manga, illustrated by Akira Katsuragi, was serialized in Mag Garden's <i><a href="/wiki/Monthly_Comic_Blade" title="Monthly Comic Blade">Monthly Comic Blade</a></i> magazine. The second manga, illustrated by Tonmi Narihara, was serialized in Futabasha's <i><a href="/wiki/Comic_High!" title="Comic High!">Comic High!</a></i> magazine.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Umi_Monogatari&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The anime uses two <a href="/wiki/Theme_music" title="Theme music">theme songs</a>. The opening theme is "violet" by <a href="/wiki/Marble_(band)" title="Marble (band)">Marble</a> and the ending theme is "Tōmei na Inori" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">透明な祈り</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by <a href="/wiki/Masumi_It%C5%8D" title="Masumi Itō">Masumi Itō</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lantis_(company)" title="Lantis (company)">Lantis</a> has released three soundtracks for <i>Umi Monogatari ~Anata ga Ite Kureta Koto~</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The first, "violet", was released on July 23, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The second, "Treasure!? ~Kimi to Deaeta koto~" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">Treasure！～君と出逢えたコト～</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, was released on August 5, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The third, "Tōmei na Inori" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">透明な祈り</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, was released on August 26, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
