<p><b>Badalona</b> (<small>Catalan pronunciation:¬†</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Catalan" title="Help:IPA for Catalan">[b…ô√∞…ôÀà…´on…ô]</a></span>) is a city in eastern <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>. It is located in the <a href="/wiki/Comarques_of_Catalonia" title="Comarques of Catalonia">comarca</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Barcelon%C3%A8s" title="Barcelon√®s">Barcelon√®s</a>, joined to <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> and part of its <a href="/wiki/Barcelona_metropolitan_area" title="Barcelona metropolitan area">metropolitan area</a>. It is situated on the left bank of the small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bes%C3%B2s_River" title="Bes√≤s River">Bes√≤s River</a> and on the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, backed by the Serra de la Marina mountain range. Badalona is the third most-populated municipality in Catalonia after Barcelona and <a href="/wiki/L%27Hospitalet_de_Llobregat" title="L'Hospitalet de Llobregat">L'Hospitalet de Llobregat</a>. It became a city in 1897. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Badalona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Badalona was founded by the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Romans</a> in the 3rd century BC, with the name of <i><b>Baetulo</b></i>, although human settlements in the area existed from 3500-2500 BC. The Iberians had fortified villages on the Melasas and Bosc√† hills since the 7th century BC. The Roman town's plan was based on their common scheme of the <i><a href="/wiki/Cardo" title="Cardo">cardo</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Decumanus_Maximus" title="Decumanus Maximus">decumanus</a></i>, occupying some 11¬†ha, with a line of walls measuring 413x261¬†m and having large defensive towers. In the 1st century BC it had some 15,000 inhabitants.</p>
<p>The current Badalona was formed in the 10th century, as a new urban nucleus built over and around the old Roman city. It comprised a group of houses built around the square and the church. At the same time, a rural nucleus grew up outside the town walls. This rural and urban dichotomy would remain until the mid-18th century.</p>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sant_Jeroni_de_la_Murtra_Monastery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sant Jeroni de la Murtra Monastery (page does not exist)">Sant Jeroni de la Murtra Monastery</a>, built in the 14th century, is where the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs" title="Catholic Monarchs">Catholic Monarchs</a> would spend their summers. This is also where they received <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> after his first voyage to the Americas.</p>

<p>Badalona was one of the most important towns during the Spanish industrialization process, from the 19th century onwards.</p>
<p>Today, Badalona is in the middle of a process of major urban change which will provide one of the challenges for the 21st century.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Badalona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The harbor is chiefly important for its fishing and boat-building trades, while in town there are gas, chemical and mineral-oil works, as well as the manufacture of woolen and cotton goods, glass, biscuits, sugar and brandy. The surrounding fertile plains produce an abundance of grain, wine and fruit.</p>
<p>The city is home to the historic distillery which produces <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=An%C3%ADs_del_Mono&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="An√≠s del Mono (page does not exist)">An√≠s del Mono</a>, a spirit made of herbs and anise, the most famous <a href="/wiki/Anisette" title="Anisette">anisette</a> in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Badalona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city's most important sport complex is the <a href="/wiki/Palau_Municipal_d%27Esports_de_Badalona" title="Palau Municipal d'Esports de Badalona">Palau Municipal d'Esports de Badalona</a> (Municipal Sports Palace), which won the Mies Van der Rohe award in 1992. The Palace was the setting for basketball competition during the Olympic Games in 1992. Nowadays, it is home of the basketball team from Badalona, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joventut_de_Badalona" title="Joventut de Badalona">Joventut de Badalona</a>, also known as la Penya. This place will also be the center of the Badalona Capital Europea del B√†squet, which is intended to be a theme park celebrating basketball - with a basketball museum, shopping center, cinemas, basketball courts, a harbour, indoor karting and more activities.</p>
