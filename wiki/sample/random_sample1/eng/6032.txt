<p>The monarchy in <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Baden" title="Grand Duchy of Baden">Baden</a> came to an end in 1918 along with the rest of the monarchies that made up the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a>. The last sovereign was Grand Duke <a href="/wiki/Frederick_II,_Grand_Duke_of_Baden" title="Frederick II, Grand Duke of Baden">Frederick II</a> who abdicated at <a href="/wiki/Karlsruhe" title="Karlsruhe">Karlsruhe</a>, 14–22 November 1918.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The current head of the Grand Ducal House is <a href="/wiki/Maximilian,_Margrave_of_Baden" title="Maximilian, Margrave of Baden">Maximilian, Margrave of Baden and Duke of Zähringen</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_of_succession_to_the_throne_of_Baden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Near_extinction">Near extinction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_of_succession_to_the_throne_of_Baden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Near extinction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the early 18th century the grand ducal house was on the verge of extinction. By 1817 the only male members of the family were the reigning <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Grand_Duke_of_Baden" title="Charles, Grand Duke of Baden">Grand Duke Charles</a> and his unmarried uncle <a href="/wiki/Louis_I,_Grand_Duke_of_Baden" title="Louis I, Grand Duke of Baden">Prince Louis</a>. In the event that the male line died out the throne would pass to King <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I_Joseph_of_Bavaria" title="Maximilian I Joseph of Bavaria">Maximilian I Joseph of Bavaria</a>. The only alternative to this was for the grand duke to recognise his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Morganatic" title="Morganatic">morganatic</a> uncles <a href="/wiki/Leopold,_Grand_Duke_of_Baden" title="Leopold, Grand Duke of Baden">Leopold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_William_of_Baden" title="Prince William of Baden">William</a> and Maximilian, the Counts von Hochberg as dynastic members of the grand ducal family. On 4 October 1817 Grand Duke Charles took this step issuing a new house law recognising his uncles as Princes of Baden. The Hochberg's right of succession was recognised by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Powers" title="Great Powers">Great Powers</a> on 10 July 1819.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ADG_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ADG-2">[2]</a></sup> The former Count Leopold von Hochberg succeeded as Grand Duke in 1830 and his descendants went on to rule Baden until 1918. By the early 19th century the succession was once again insecure with the future of the dynasty resting on <a href="/wiki/Berthold,_Margrave_of_Baden" title="Berthold, Margrave of Baden">Prince Berthold of Baden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kasper_Hauser">Kasper Hauser</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_of_succession_to_the_throne_of_Baden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Kasper Hauser">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kasper_Hauser" title="Kasper Hauser">Kasper Hauser</a></div>
<p>According to contemporary rumours – probably current as early as 1829 – Kaspar Hauser was the son of Grand Duke Charles who was born on 29 September 1812, and who, according to known history, had died on 16 October 1812. It was alleged that this prince had been switched with a dying baby, and had subsequently surfaced 16 years later as Kaspar Hauser in Nuremberg. These theories linking him with the princely House of Baden have long since been rejected by professional historians.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Succession_law">Succession law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_of_succession_to_the_throne_of_Baden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Succession law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The succession law is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semi-Salic" title="Semi-Salic">Semi-Salic</a>, with the succession hereditary among the male members. However, in the event of the extinction of the male line the succession could be transmitted to princesses and their descendants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ADG_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ADG-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Line_of_succession_in_1918">Line of succession in 1918</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_of_succession_to_the_throne_of_Baden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Line of succession in 1918">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
