<p><b>Varaždīn</b> (<small>Croatian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[ʋâraʒdiːn]</a></span> or <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[ʋarǎʒdin]</a></span>; also known by other <a href="/wiki/Name" title="Name">alternative names</a>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Croatia" title="Northern Croatia">Northern Croatia</a>, 81 km (50 mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>. The total population is 46,946, with 38,839 on 34.22 km<sup>2</sup> (13.21 sq mi) of the city settlement itself (2011).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census2011_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-census2011-1">[1]</a></sup> The centre of <a href="/wiki/Vara%C5%BEdin_County" title="Varaždin County">Varaždin County</a> is located near the <a href="/wiki/Drava" title="Drava">Drava</a> River, at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;params=46.312_N_16.361_E_"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">46°18′43″N</span> <span class="longitude">16°21′40″E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">46.312°N 16.361°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">46.312; 16.361</span></span></span></a></span>. It is mainly known for its <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">baroque</a> buildings, music,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> textile, food and IT industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icpr3-varazdin_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-icpr3-varazdin-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a> the town is known as <i>Varasd</i>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a> as <i>Varasdinum</i>, and in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> as <i>Warasdin</i>. The name <i>Varaždin</i> traces its origin in the word <i>varoš</i>, a Hungarian loanword.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>The first written reference to Varaždin, whose historical name is Garestin, was on 20 August 1181, when King <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9la_III_of_Hungary" title="Béla III of Hungary">Béla III</a> mentioned the nearby thermal springs (<a href="/wiki/Vara%C5%BEdinske_Toplice" title="Varaždinske Toplice">Varaždinske Toplice</a>) in a legal document.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-var-hist1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-var-hist1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Varaždin was declared a free royal <a href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">borough</a> in 1209 by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Hungarian</a> King <a href="/wiki/Andrew_II_of_Hungary" title="Andrew II of Hungary">Andrew II</a>. The town became the economic and military centre of northern Croatia. Due to <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_wars_in_Europe" title="Ottoman wars in Europe">Ottoman raids</a>, the town was structured defensively around the old <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fortress" title="Fortress">fortress</a>, and acquired the shape of a typical medieval Wasserburg. In the early 13th century, the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitaller" title="Knights Hospitaller">Knights Hospitaller</a> (<a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>: <span lang="hr" xml:lang="hr"><i>Ivanovci</i></span>) came to Varaždin, where they built the church and a monastery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-var-hist1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-var-hist1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of the 14th century, Varaždin fortress passed to the hands of the <a href="/wiki/Counts_of_Celje" title="Counts of Celje">Counts of Celje</a>. Over the following centuries Varaždin had several owners, the most influential being Beatrice <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankopan" title="Frankopan">Frankopan</a>, Margrave <a href="/wiki/George,_Margrave_of_Brandenburg-Ansbach" title="George, Margrave of Brandenburg-Ansbach">Georg of Brandenburg</a>, who built the town hall; the last was Baron Ivan Ungnad, who reinforced the existing fortification. At the end of the 16th century <a href="/wiki/Count" title="Count">Count</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas_Erd%C3%B6dy" title="Thomas Erdödy">Thomas Erdödy</a> became its owner, assuming the hereditary position of Varaždin prefects (župan), and the fortress remained in the ownership of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Erd%C3%B6dy" title="Erdödy">Erdödy</a> family until 1925.</p>

<p>In 1756, the <a href="/wiki/Ban_(title)" title="Ban (title)">Ban</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Francis_Nad%C3%A1sdy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Francis Nadásdy (page does not exist)">Francis Nadásdy</a> chose Varaždin as his official residence, and Varaždin became the capital of all of Croatia. It hosted the Croatian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sabor" title="Sabor">Sabor</a> and the Royal Croatian Council founded by Empress <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maria_Theresa_of_Austria" title="Maria Theresa of Austria">Maria Theresa</a>.</p>
<p>The periods of the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" title="Protestant Reformation">Reformation</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counter-reformation" title="Counter-reformation">counter-reformation</a> had a great influence on Varaždin. With the arrival of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesuits" title="Jesuits">Jesuits</a>, the school (<a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">gymnasium</a>) and the Jesuit house were founded, and churches and other buildings were built in the <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">Baroque</a> style. In the 18th century Varaždin was the seat of many Croatian noblemen, and in 1756 it became the <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatian</a> administrative centre. The fire of 1776 destroyed most of the town, resulting in the administrative institutions moving back to <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-var-hist1_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-var-hist1-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Varaždin was the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Vara%C5%BEdin_County_(former)" title="Varaždin County (former)">Varaždin County</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Croatia-Slavonia" title="Kingdom of Croatia-Slavonia">Kingdom of Croatia-Slavonia</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austro-Hungarian monarchy</a>, ruled by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Kingdom of Hungary</a> after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compromise_of_1867" title="Compromise of 1867">compromise of 1867</a>. The Hungarian stamp, issued in 1881 shows both names.</p>
<p>By the 19th century Varaždin had been completely rebuilt and expanded, with flourishing crafts and trade, and later the manufacture of silk and bricks. The theatre, music school, and fire department were founded.</p>
<p>In the 20th century Varaždin developed into the industrial centre of Northwestern Croatia. The textile manufacturer <i>Tivar</i> was founded in 1918. In the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_War_of_Independence" title="Croatian War of Independence">Croatian War of Independence</a>, 1991, Varaždin suffered directly for only for a few days, because the huge <a href="/wiki/Yugoslav_People%27s_Army" title="Yugoslav People's Army">Yugoslav People's Army</a> base <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Vara%C5%BEdin_Barracks" title="Siege of Varaždin Barracks">quickly surrendered</a>, resulting in a minimal number of casualties, and providing weapons (worth $600m) for the Croatian army.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Monuments_and_Sights">Monuments and Sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Monuments and Sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Varaždin represents the best preserved and richest urban complex in continental Croatia.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>The Old Town (fortress) is an example of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval" title="Medieval">medieval</a> defensive buildings. Construction began in the 14th century, and in the following century the rounded towers, typical of <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic architecture</a> in Croatia, were added. Today it houses the <a class="external text" href="http://www.mdc.hr/varazdin/eng/home.html" rel="nofollow">Town Museum</a>. The fortress is depicted on the <a href="/wiki/Obverse_and_reverse" title="Obverse and reverse">reverse</a> of the Croatian 5 <a href="/wiki/Croatian_kuna" title="Croatian kuna">kuna</a> banknote, issued in 1993 and 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="external text" href="http://www.mdc.hr/varazdin/eng/galerija.html" rel="nofollow">The Old and Contemporary Masters Gallery</a> is located in the Sermage Palace, built in the <a href="/wiki/Rococo" title="Rococo">rococo</a> style in 1750.</p>
<p>In 1523, Margrave <a href="/wiki/George,_Margrave_of_Brandenburg-Ansbach" title="George, Margrave of Brandenburg-Ansbach">Georg of Brandenburg</a> built the town hall in late baroque style, with the Varaždin coat of arms at the foot of the tower, and it has continued in its function until the present day. There is a guard-changing ceremony every Saturday.</p>
<p>Varaždin's Cathedral, a former Jesuit church, was built in 1647, and is distinguished by its baroque entrance, eighteenth-century altar, and paintings.</p>
<p>There are many baroque and rococo palaces and houses in the town. Worth particular mention is Varaždin's Croatian National Theatre, built in 1873 and designed by the famous <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Viennese</a> architects <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herman_Helmer" title="Herman Helmer">Herman Helmer</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Fellner" title="Ferdinand Fellner">Ferdinand Fellner</a>.</p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Baroque_music" title="Baroque music">baroque music</a> festival has been held annually in Varaždin since 1971, and attracts some of the finest musicians and their fans from Croatia and the world. Recommended to visitors is also the historical street festival <i>Špancir fest</i> every September.</p>
<p>The city features its old city guard, named <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Purgari&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Purgari (page does not exist)">Purgari</a>, in various city ceremonies as well as the weekly ceremony of the 'change of the guards' in front of the city hall. Additionally, Varaždin police officers patrol on bicycles in the warmer months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-purgari_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-purgari-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Old_Town_.28Stari_Grad.29">The Old Town (Stari Grad)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Old Town (Stari Grad)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Old Town keep is one of the biggest monuments in the city of Varaždin and one of its biggest tourist attractions. It is located in the north-western section of the city core. Today the keep houses the Varaždin City Museum.</p>
<p>The keep is first mentioned in the 12th century and it is believed to be the center of Varaždin county life. The keep underwent numerous ownership changes and reconstructions over the centuries.</p>
<p>The Old Town was featured on the now defunct 5 <a href="/wiki/Croatian_kuna" title="Croatian kuna">Kuna</a> bill. Interestingly, on the bill the picture of the keep is a mirror image of the actual appearance of the keep.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vara.C5.BEdin_cemetery">Varaždin cemetery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Varaždin cemetery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The cemetery date back to 1773 and it was long time an ordinary place until 1905, when Herman Haller had an idea to make it more beautiful and park-like with large trees and alleys for citizens to stroll through. The reconstruction of the cemetery was done between 1905 and 1947 when it got the form for which it later became one of the most beautiful cemeteries in Europe. It is an outstanding example of landscape architecture and protected cultural and natural park.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Museums">Museums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Museums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>THE OLD TOWN (STARI GRAD) Museum houses the Cultural and Historic Collection. The Old Town Museum has been a part of the Varaždin City Museum organization since 1925. Today is features: over 400 pieces from the glass, ceramics and clocks collection, 10 rooms furnished in chronological period style (renaissance, baroque, rococo, Empire, Biedermeier, historicism and art deco), a chapel and sacristy, and 2 rooms dedicated to two prominent men from Varaždin, <a href="/wiki/Vatroslav_Jagi%C4%87" title="Vatroslav Jagić">Vatroslav Jagić</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Kukuljevi%C4%87_Sakcinski" title="Ivan Kukuljević Sakcinski">Ivan Kukuljević Sakcinski</a>.</li>
<li>THE HERZER PALACE houses the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entomological" title="Entomological">Entomological</a> Collection focusing on The World of Insects. There are over 4500 exhibits which clearly show the biology of bugs through several topics:In the forest, Near the forest and on the meadow, In the water and near the water, At night and underground.</li>
<li>THE SERMAGE PALACE houses the Gallery of Old and Contemporary Masters Art Gallery. It is home to over 5300 works of art separated in 10 collections with works ranging from the 15th to the 20th century.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Road_access">Road access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Road access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Varaždin is connected with <a href="/wiki/A4_(Croatia)" title="A4 (Croatia)">A4</a> <a href="/wiki/Highway" title="Highway">highway</a> which runs between Zagreb (south) and <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cakovec" title="Čakovec">Čakovec</a> / Hungarian border (just north). Varaždin and Zagreb are also connected with <a href="/wiki/D3_road_(Croatia)" title="D3 road (Croatia)">state road D3</a> which serves as an alternative to tolled highway. In <a href="/wiki/Novi_Marof" title="Novi Marof">Novi Marof</a>, just south from Varaždin, state road <a href="/wiki/D24_road_(Croatia)" title="D24 road (Croatia)">D24</a> (which represents connection of Varaždin with majority of <a href="/wiki/Krapina-Zagorje_County" title="Krapina-Zagorje County">Krapina-Zagorje County</a> located south-west from the town) connects with D3 road from the west. D3 also run further north from Varaždin, representing state road connection with <a href="/wiki/Me%C4%91imurje_County" title="Međimurje County">Međimurje</a> -northernmost part of the country (largest destination served being Čakovec).</p>
<p>Major state road which connects Varaždin with north-east and east part of the country (serving regions <a href="/wiki/Podravina" title="Podravina">Podravina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slavonia" title="Slavonia">Slavonia</a>, one of the largest destinations served being <a href="/wiki/Ludbreg" title="Ludbreg">Ludbreg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Koprivnica" title="Koprivnica">Koprivnica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virovitica" title="Virovitica">Virovitica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Osijek" title="Osijek">Osijek</a>) is state road <a href="/wiki/D2_road_(Croatia)" title="D2 road (Croatia)">D2</a>. D2 also connects Varaždin with Croatian-Slovenian <a href="/wiki/Border" title="Border">border</a> at Dubrava Križovljanska, located just north-east. Varaždin is also connected with Krapina-Zagorje County via <a href="/wiki/D35_road_(Croatia)" title="D35 road (Croatia)">D35 road</a>, which run south-west from Varaždin, but more north than D24. Regarding larger destinations, this road is a major connection of Varaždin with <a href="/wiki/Ivanec" title="Ivanec">Ivanec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lepoglava" title="Lepoglava">Lepoglava</a> and <a href="/wiki/Krapina" title="Krapina">Krapina</a>.</p>
<p>D2 and D3 roads are also part of <a href="/wiki/Bypass_(road)" title="Bypass (road)">bypass</a> which encircles the town.</p>
<p>The town is also hub for <a href="/wiki/Bus" title="Bus">bus</a> transportation enterprise "AP Varaždin" which offers regional, distant (multi-regional) and occasional passenger bus transportation service between Varaždin and large number of towns across the country, but also offers international lines.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Railway_access">Railway access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Railway access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Varaždin's railway station is one of the largest and most important <a href="/wiki/Train_station" title="Train station">train stations</a> in northern Croatia. It represents the intersection of three Croatian <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">railway</a> corridors which are used for both passenger and freight traffic. All of the rail corridors which start or end in Varaždin are <a href="/wiki/Single-track_railway" title="Single-track railway">single-tracked</a> and non-electrified. It is the terminus for one local line (<a href="/wiki/L201_railway_(Croatia)" title="L201 railway (Croatia)">L201</a> connecting <a href="/wiki/Novi_Golubovec" title="Novi Golubovec">Golubovec</a>), one regional line (<a href="/wiki/R202_railway_(Croatia)" title="R202 railway (Croatia)">R202</a> connecting <a href="/wiki/Dalj" title="Dalj">Dalj</a> via <a href="/wiki/Koprivnica" title="Koprivnica">Koprivnica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virovitica" title="Virovitica">Virovitica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Osijek" title="Osijek">Osijek</a>) and also lies on <a href="/wiki/R201_railway_(Croatia)" title="R201 railway (Croatia)">R201 line</a> through which trains directly connect the town with Zagreb and most of <a href="/wiki/Krapina-Zagorje_County" title="Krapina-Zagorje County">Krapina-Zagorje County</a> (on south) and with <a href="/wiki/Me%C4%91imurje_County" title="Međimurje County">Međimurje County</a> (just north).</p>
<p>Although the largest number of direct passenger trains from Zagreb to Varaždin run via the <a href="/wiki/R201_railway_(Croatia)" title="R201 railway (Croatia)">R201</a> corridor (through the north-western part of the country), an alternative way to travel by train between those two cities is via the R202 line between Varaždin and Koprivnica, and the <a href="/wiki/M201_railway_(Croatia)" title="M201 railway (Croatia)">M201</a> line between Koprivnica and Zagreb (through the north-eastern part of the country). Although this route requires transfer in Koprivnica in the majority of cases, journeys by this route may take shorter travel time.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Varazdin-0808.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Varazdin-0808.JPG/220px-Varazdin-0808.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Varazdin-0808.JPG/330px-Varazdin-0808.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Varazdin-0808.JPG/440px-Varazdin-0808.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Varazdin-0808.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Streets of Varaždin.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Varaždin is one of the few Croatian cities whose industry did not directly suffer from the war in 1991. Besides textile giant <i>Varteks</i>, it also has nationally important food (<i>Vindija</i>), metal, and construction industries. The Information Technology and financial and banking sector as are well developed. Further economic development has been encouraged with the creation of a free investment zone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icpr3-varazdin_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-icpr3-varazdin-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The city of Varaždin is easily accessible by major roads from all sides. The road infrastructure is good and the main highway connection is <a href="/wiki/A4_(Croatia)" title="A4 (Croatia)">A4</a>, connecting the Hungary border-crossing point in <a href="/wiki/Gori%C4%8Dan" title="Goričan">Goričan</a> with <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>, as well as the coast of the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a> via <a href="/wiki/A1_(Croatia)" title="A1 (Croatia)">A1</a>/<a href="/wiki/A6_(Croatia)" title="A6 (Croatia)">A6</a>. <a href="/wiki/A2_(Croatia)" title="A2 (Croatia)">A2</a> is also accessible through the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zagorje" title="Zagorje">Zagorje</a> region. In addition to the highways, there is also an east-west state route connecting the city to the Slovenian border, and <a href="/wiki/Koprivnica" title="Koprivnica">Koprivnica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osijek" title="Osijek">Osijek</a> and the east part of <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>. The city is connected to the suburbs and villages surrounding it with a public transportation system of buses. The city also has a train and bus station, which are both located about a 10-minute walk from the center. The trains are mainly used for cargo, due to the lack of investment in the country's train infrastructure. Varaždin's bus service is of high quality and use, it offers regular service to many local, domestic and international routes, as well as many additional seasonal routes to the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a>. There is also taxi service available on-call which is situated by the bus station. In the outskirts of the city there is also a small recreational airport, used mainly for sightseeing and farming purposes.</p>

<p>Today Varaždin is a popular destination for the summer holidays. The city has numerous areas of interests ranging from cultural areas (reflected by many museums, galleries and theaters in the area), shopping centers in the downtown core, various sports and recreation facilities, also a rich history in cuisine. The close of the tourist season is marked by two annual festivals. The annual ŠpancirFest begins at the end of August and ends in September (lasts for 10 days). At this time. the city welcomes artists, street performers, musicians and vendors for what is called "the street walking festival".</p>
<p>The city also hosts the <a href="/wiki/Vara%C5%BEdin_Baroque_Evenings" title="Varaždin Baroque Evenings">Varaždin Baroque Evenings</a> festival, first held in 1971. The festival honours <a href="/wiki/Baroque_music" title="Baroque music">baroque music</a> and culture, both of which hold a special place in Varaždin's identity.</p>
<p>Varaždin is also the host of the <a href="/wiki/Radar_Festival" title="Radar Festival">Radar Festival</a>, which hosts concerts at the end of summer. It has already hosted various popular artists as <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Santana" title="Carlos Santana">Carlos Santana</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Animals" title="The Animals">The Animals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manic_Street_Preachers" title="Manic Street Preachers">Manic Street Preachers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Burke" title="Solomon Burke">Solomon Burke</a> and many others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vara%C5%BEdin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Varaždin has a rich and developed system of education, especially for a city of its size. It has seven elementary schools, 10 high schools (2 public gymnasiums, 2 private gymnasiums, trade schools, and other specialized high schools for various paths), 4 higher schools (equivalent to college) and 2 faculties (Faculty of Organization and Information Technology and Geotechnical faculty) that are part of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Zagreb" title="University of Zagreb">University of Zagreb</a>.</p>
