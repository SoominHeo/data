<p><b>Everard des Barres</b> (also <b>Eberhard von Barres</b> or <b>Eberhard De BÃ¤ren</b>) (died 1174) was the third Grand Master of the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Templar" title="Knights Templar">Knights Templar</a> from 1147 to 1151.</p>
<p>As Preceptor of the Templars in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> from 1143, he was one of the highest dignitaries of the Order when <a href="/wiki/Robert_de_Craon" title="Robert de Craon">Robert de Craon</a> died in 1147. He was chosen to succeed Robert, and as soon as he was elected, he accompanied <a href="/wiki/Louis_VII_of_France" title="Louis VII of France">Louis VII of France</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Second_Crusade" title="Second Crusade">Second Crusade</a>, and was among those sent ahead to <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> before Louis' arrival there. He later saved Louis during a battle with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seljuk_Turks" title="Seljuk Turks">Seljuk Turks</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pisidia" title="Pisidia">Pisidia</a>.</p>
<p>According to the chronicler <a href="/wiki/Odo_of_Deuil" title="Odo of Deuil">Odo of Deuil</a>, Everard was extremely pious and valiant. He seems to have had a strong influence on Louis. After the failure of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crusade" title="Crusade">crusade</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Damascus_(1148)" title="Siege of Damascus (1148)">Siege of Damascus</a> in 1148, Louis returned to France, followed by Everard, who was in charge of the king's treasury. Everard's Templars stayed behind and helped defend <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> against a Turkish raid in 1149.</p>
<p>Back in France, Everard abdicated officially in 1151 and became a monk at <a href="/wiki/Clairvaux_Abbey" title="Clairvaux Abbey">Clairvaux</a>, despite the protests of the Templars. He was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Bernard_de_Tremelay" title="Bernard de Tremelay">Bernard de Tremelay</a> (who actually led the Order since Everard's departure in 1149) and died in 1174.</p>
