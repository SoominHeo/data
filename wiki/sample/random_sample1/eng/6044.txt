<p><b>Bareilly</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="noexcerpt"><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/70/Bareilly.ogg" title="Listen"><img alt="Listen" data-file-height="11" data-file-width="11" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Speakerlink-new.svg/11px-Speakerlink-new.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Speakerlink-new.svg/17px-Speakerlink-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Speakerlink-new.svg/22px-Speakerlink-new.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a><sup><span class="IPA" style="color:#00e;font:bold 80% sans-serif;padding:0 .1em"><a href="/wiki/File:Bareilly.ogg" title="File:Bareilly.ogg">i</a></span></sup></span><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/ə/ 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/Bareilly_district" title="Bareilly district">Bareilly district</a> in the northern <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a>. Located on the <a href="/wiki/Ramganga" title="Ramganga">Ramganga</a>, it is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Bareilly_division" title="Bareilly division">Bareilly division</a> and the geographical region of <a href="/wiki/Rohilkhand" title="Rohilkhand">Rohilkhand</a>. The city is 252 kilometres (157 mi) north of the state capital, <a href="/wiki/Lucknow" title="Lucknow">Lucknow</a>, and 250 kilometres (155 mi) east of the national capital, New Delhi. Bareilly is the fourth city in Uttar Pradesh with <a href="/wiki/Compressed_natural_gas" title="Compressed natural gas">compressed natural gas</a> (CNG) <a href="/wiki/Filling_station" title="Filling station">filling stations</a> (after Lucknow, <a href="/wiki/Kanpur" title="Kanpur">Kanpur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Agra" title="Agra">Agra</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is the seventh-largest <a href="/wiki/Metropolis" title="Metropolis">metropolis</a> in Uttar Pradesh and the 50th-largest city in India.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Bareilly also figured amongst the PM <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">Narendra Modi</a>'s ambitious 100 Smart City list in India.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The city is also known by the name Nath Nagri <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>(known for the four <a href="/wiki/Shiva" title="Shiva">Shiva</a> temples located in four corners of the region - Dhopeshwar Nath, Madni Nath, Alakha Nath and Trivati Nath), Ala Hazrat,Shah Sharafat Miyan and Khankahe Niyazia (derived the famous Muslim Mausoleum), Zari nagari and historically as Sanjashya (where the Buddha descended from <a href="/wiki/Tushita" title="Tushita">Tushita</a> to earth).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com-2">[2]</a></sup> The city is a centre for furniture manufacturing and trade in cotton, cereal and sugar. Its status grew with its inclusion in the "<a href="/wiki/National_Capital_Region_(India)#Counter_magnets" title="National Capital Region (India)">counter magnets</a>" list of the <a href="/wiki/National_Capital_Region_(India)" title="National Capital Region (India)">National Capital Region (NCR)</a>, a list also including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hisar,_India" title="Hisar, India">Hissar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patiala" title="Patiala">Patiala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kota,_Rajasthan" title="Kota, Rajasthan">Kota</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gwalior" title="Gwalior">Gwalior</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The city is also known as Bans-Bareilly. Although Bareilly is a production centre for <a href="/wiki/Cane" title="Cane">cane</a> (<i>bans</i>) furniture, "Bans Bareilly" is not derived from the <i>bans</i> market; it was named for two princes: Bansaldev and Baraldev, sons of Jagat Singh Katehriya, who founded the city in 1537.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Bareilly's 2011 population was 898,167.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-census-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Bareilly" title="History of Bareilly">History of Bareilly</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ahichchhatra_Fort_Temple_Bareilly.jpg"><img alt="Multi-coloured political map" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="363" data-file-width="1024" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Ahichchhatra_Fort_Temple_Bareilly.jpg/400px-Ahichchhatra_Fort_Temple_Bareilly.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Ahichchhatra_Fort_Temple_Bareilly.jpg/600px-Ahichchhatra_Fort_Temple_Bareilly.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Ahichchhatra_Fort_Temple_Bareilly.jpg/800px-Ahichchhatra_Fort_Temple_Bareilly.jpg 2x" width="400"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ahichchhatra_Fort_Temple_Bareilly.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Ahichchhatra" title="Ahichchhatra">Ahichchhatra</a> (or Ahi-Kshetra) was the ancient capital of Northern Panchala. The remains of this city have been discovered in Bareilly</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>According to the epic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mah%C4%81bh%C4%81rata" title="Mahābhārata">Mahābhārata</a>, Bareilly region (<a href="/wiki/Panchala" title="Panchala">Panchala</a>) is said to be the birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Draupadi" title="Draupadi">Draupadi</a>, who was also referred to as 'Panchali' (one from the kingdom of Panchāla) by Kṛṣṇā (<a href="/wiki/Krishna" title="Krishna">Lord Krishna</a>). When <a href="/wiki/Yudhishthira" title="Yudhishthira">Yudhishthira</a> becomes the king of Hastinapura at the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mah%C4%81bh%C4%81rata" title="Mahābhārata">Mahābhārata</a>, Draupadi becomes his queen. The folklore says that <a href="/wiki/Gautama_Buddha" title="Gautama Buddha">Gautama Buddha</a> had once visited the ancient fortress city of <a href="/wiki/Ahichchhatra" title="Ahichchhatra">Ahichchhatra</a> in Bareilly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-members.rediff.com_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-members.rediff.com-6">[6]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jain" title="Jain">Jain</a> <a href="/wiki/Tirthankara" title="Tirthankara">Tirthankara</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parshva" title="Parshva">Parshva</a> is said to have attained Kaivalya at <a href="/wiki/Ahichchhatra" title="Ahichchhatra">Ahichchhatra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bareilly.nic.in_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-bareilly.nic.in-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 12th century, the kingdom was under the rule by different clans of <a href="/wiki/Kshatriya" title="Kshatriya">Kshatriya</a> Rajputs. With the Islamic Turkic Invasion the region became a part of the <a href="/wiki/Delhi_Sultanate" title="Delhi Sultanate">Delhi Sultanate</a> before getting absorbed in the emerging <a href="/wiki/Mughal_Empire" title="Mughal Empire">Mughal Empire</a>. The foundation of the modern City of Bareilly foundation was laid by <a href="/wiki/Mukrand_Rai" title="Mukrand Rai">Mukrand Rai</a> in 1657.</p>
<p>Later the region became the capital of <a href="/wiki/Rohilkhand" title="Rohilkhand">Rohilkhand</a> region before getting handed over to Nawab Vazir of Awadh and then to <a href="/wiki/East_India_Company" title="East India Company">East India Company</a> (transferred to the British India) and later becoming an integral part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_India" title="Republic of India">India</a>. The region has, also, acted as a mint for a major part of its history.</p>
<p>From archaeological point of view the district of Bareilly is very rich. The extensive remains of <a href="/wiki/Ahichchhatra" title="Ahichchhatra">Ahichchhatra</a>, the Capital town of Northern Panchala have been discovered near <a href="/wiki/Ramnagar,_Bareilly" title="Ramnagar, Bareilly">Ramnagar village</a> of Aonla Tehsil in the district. It was during the first excavations at <a href="/wiki/Ahichchhatra" title="Ahichchhatra">Ahichchhatra</a> (1940–44) that the painted grey ware, associated with the advent of the Aryans in the Ganges–Yamuna Valley, was recognised for the first time in the earliest levels of the site. Nearly five thousand coins belonging to periods earlier than that of Guptas have been yielded from <a href="/wiki/Ahichchhatra" title="Ahichchhatra">Ahichchhatra</a>. It has also been one of the richest sites in India from the point of view of the total yield of terracotta. Some of the masterpieces of Indian terracotta art are from <a href="/wiki/Ahichchhatra" title="Ahichchhatra">Ahichchhatra</a>. In fact the classification made of the terracotta human figurines from <a href="/wiki/Ahichchhatra" title="Ahichchhatra">Ahichchhatra</a> on grounds of style and to some extent stratigraphy became a model for determining the stratigraphy of subsequent excavations at other sites in the Ganges Valley. On the basis of the existing material, the archaeology of the region helps us to get an idea of the cultural sequence from the beginning of the 2nd millennium BC up to the 11th century AD. Some ancient mounds in the district have also been discovered by the Deptt. of Ancient History and culture, Rohilkhand University, at Tihar-Khera (Fatehganj West), Pachaumi, Rahtuia, Kadarganj and Sainthal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bareilly.nic.in_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-bareilly.nic.in-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding <span id="Foundation"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bareilly was founded in 1537 by Basdeo, a Katehriya Rajput. The city was first mentioned by the historian Budayuni, who wrote that Husain Quli Khan was appointed the governor of "Bareilly and Sambhal" in 1568. The divisions and revenue of the district "being fixed by Todar Mal" were recorded by Abul Fazl in 1596. The foundation of the modern city of Bareilly was laid by <a href="/wiki/Mukrand_Rai" title="Mukrand Rai">Mukrand Rai</a> in 1657. In 1658, Bareilly became the headquarters of the province of Budaun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-library.upenn.edu_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-library.upenn.edu-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Mughal policy of encouraging Afghan settlements to control the Katehriyas succeeded if the central government was strong. After <a href="/wiki/Aurangzeb" title="Aurangzeb">Aurangzeb</a>'s death, the Afghans (who had become local potentates) began to seize and occupy neighbouring villages.</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:UP_region_map.gif"><img alt="Multi-coloured political map" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="829" data-file-width="1175" height="141" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/UP_region_map.gif/200px-UP_region_map.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/UP_region_map.gif/300px-UP_region_map.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/UP_region_map.gif/400px-UP_region_map.gif 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:UP_region_map.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Regions of Uttar Pradesh, including Rohilkhand (capital Bareilly)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>After the fall of the Mughal Empire, many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pathan" title="Pathan">Pathans</a> migrated from <a href="/wiki/Rohilkhand" title="Rohilkhand">Rohilkhand</a>. Bareilly (like other cities in Uttar Pradesh) experienced economic stagnation and poverty, leading to the migration of Rohilla Muslim Pathans to <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> as <a href="/wiki/Indenture" title="Indenture">indentured</a> labour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_East_India_Company">British East India Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: British East India Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Under Barech at the 1761 <a href="/wiki/Third_Battle_of_Panipat" title="Third Battle of Panipat">Third Battle of Panipat</a>, Rohilkhand blocked the expansion of the <a href="/wiki/Maratha" title="Maratha">Maratha</a> Empire into northern India. In 1772 it was invaded by the Marathas, repulsing the invasion with the aid of the <a href="/wiki/Nawab_of_Awadh" title="Nawab of Awadh">Nawab of Awadh</a>. After the war, Nawab <a href="/wiki/Shuja-ud-Daula" title="Shuja-ud-Daula">Shuja-ud-Daula</a> demanded payment for the nawabs' help from Barech. When his demand was refused, the nawab joined the British (under <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_India" title="Governor-General of India">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Warren_Hastings" title="Warren Hastings">Warren Hastings</a> and his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander-in-Chief" title="Commander-in-Chief">Commander-in-Chief</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Champion_(East_India_Company_officer)" title="Alexander Champion (East India Company officer)">Alexander Champion</a>) to invade Rohilkhand. The combined forces of Daula and the Company defeated Barech (who was killed in battle at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miranpur_Katra" title="Miranpur Katra">Miranpur Katra</a>, ending Rohilla rule) in 1774.</p>
<p>Rohilkhand was handed over to Daula, and from 1774 to 1800 the province was ruled by the Nawab of Awadh. By 1801, subsidies due under the treaties to support a British force had fallen into arrears. To pay the debt, Nawab Saadat Ali Khan surrendered Rohilkhand to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honourable_East_India_Company" title="Honourable East India Company">East India Company</a> in a treaty signed on 10 November 1801. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the reign of <a href="/wiki/Shah_Alam_II" title="Shah Alam II">Shah Alam II</a>, Bareilly was the headquarters of Rohilla Sardar Hafiz Rehmat Khan and many coins were minted. The city was later in the possession of Awadh Nawab Asaf-ud-Daulah, and his coins had Bareilly, Bareilly Aasfabad and the Bareilly kite and fish as identification marks. Coins were then minted by the East India Company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_period">Modern period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Modern period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Indian_Rebellion_of_1857.jpg"><img alt="Physical-political map of northern India" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1647" data-file-width="1959" height="185" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857.jpg/220px-Indian_Rebellion_of_1857.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857.jpg/330px-Indian_Rebellion_of_1857.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857.jpg/440px-Indian_Rebellion_of_1857.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Indian_Rebellion_of_1857.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A 1912 map of northern India, showing the principal centres of the 1857–1859 rebellion: <a href="/wiki/Meerut" title="Meerut">Meerut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a>, Bareilly, (<a href="/wiki/Kanpur" title="Kanpur">Kanpur</a>), Lucknow, <a href="/wiki/Jhansi" title="Jhansi">Jhansi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gwalior" title="Gwalior">Gwalior</a></div>

<p>After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rohilla_War" title="Rohilla War">Rohilla War</a>, the change in the power structure increased discontent throughout the district. Increased taxation from 1812<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> to 1814 increased resentment of the British: "Business stood still, shops were shut and multitudes assembled near the courthouse to petition for the abolition of the tax." The Magistrate Dembleton, already unpopular, ordered the assessment to be made by a <a href="/wiki/Kotwal" title="Kotwal">Kotwal</a>. A skirmish between rebels and the sepoys (under Captain Cunningham) cost 300–400 lives. In 1818, Glyn<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (May 2014)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup> was posted as Acting Judge and Magistrate of Bareilly and the Joint Magistrate of Bulundshahr.</p>
<p>Glyn asked Ghulam Yahya to write an account of "craftsmen, the names of tools of manufacture and production and their dress and manners". The most popular trades in and around Bareilly during the 1820s were manufacturing glass, jewellery, glass and <a href="/wiki/Lac" title="Lac">lac</a> bangles and gold and silver thread, crimping, bean drying, wire drawing, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manjaa" title="Manjaa">charpoy</a> weaving, keeping a grocer's shop and selling kebabs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-library.upenn.edu_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-library.upenn.edu-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Rebellion_of_1857">Rebellion of 1857 <span id="Indian_Rebellion_of_1857"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rebellion of 1857">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Bareilly was a centre of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857" title="Indian Rebellion of 1857">Indian Rebellion of 1857</a>. The rebellion began as a <a href="/wiki/Mutiny" title="Mutiny">mutiny</a> of native soldiers (<a href="/wiki/Sepoy" title="Sepoy">sepoys</a>), employed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_East_India_Company" title="British East India Company">British East India Company</a>'s army, against race- and religion-based injustices and inequities on 10 May 1857 in <a href="/wiki/Meerut" title="Meerut">Meerut</a>. It expanded into other mutinies and civilian rebellions, primarily in the major north-central Indian river valleys; local episodes extended northwest to <a href="/wiki/Peshawar" title="Peshawar">Peshawar</a> (on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North-West_Frontier_(military_history)" title="North-West Frontier (military history)">northwest frontier</a> with Afghanistan) and southeast (beyond Delhi). There were riots in many parts of Uttar Pradesh, and Muslims in Bareilly, <a href="/wiki/Bijnor" title="Bijnor">Bijnor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moradabad" title="Moradabad">Moradabad</a> called for the revival of a Muslim kingdom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:United_Provinces_1903.gif"><img alt="Old, multi-coloured physical-political map" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/United_Provinces_1903.gif/220px-United_Provinces_1903.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/United_Provinces_1903.gif/330px-United_Provinces_1903.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/United_Provinces_1903.gif/440px-United_Provinces_1903.gif 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:United_Provinces_1903.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Bareilly was made part of the <a href="/wiki/United_Provinces_of_Agra_and_Oudh" title="United Provinces of Agra and Oudh">United Provinces of Agra and Oudh</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Rohillas actively opposed the British, but were disarmed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1911encyclopedia.org_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-1911encyclopedia.org-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Khan_Bahadur_Khan_Rohilla" title="Khan Bahadur Khan Rohilla">Khan Bahadur Khan Rohilla</a>, grandson of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hafiz_Rahmat_Khan" title="Hafiz Rahmat Khan">Hafiz Rahmat Khan</a>, formed his own government in Bareilly in 1857 and a widespread popular revolt in <a href="/wiki/Awadh" title="Awadh">Awadh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bundelkhand" title="Bundelkhand">Bundelkhand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rohilkhand" title="Rohilkhand">Rohilkhand</a> took place. In 1857, Khan Bhadur Khan issued silver coins from Bareilly as an independent ruler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> When the rebellion failed, Bareilly was subjugated. Khan Bahadur Khan was sentenced to death, and hanged in the police station on 24 February 1860.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Independence">Independence <span id="Independence_of_India"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Independence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bareilly_Central_Jail" title="Bareilly Central Jail">Bareilly Central Jail</a> housed a number of <a href="/wiki/Political_prisoner" title="Political prisoner">political prisoners</a> during the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British Raj</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Yashpal" title="Yashpal">Yashpal</a> (who married while imprisoned on 7 August 1936 was the first such ceremony in an Indian jail). The rules were changed, preventing future prison marriages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bareilly is in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_India" title="Northern India">northern India</a>, at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Bareilly&amp;params=28_10_N_78_23_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">28°10′N</span> <span class="longitude">78°23′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">28.167°N 78.383°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">28.167; 78.383</span></span></span></a></span>. On its east are <a href="/wiki/Pilibhit" title="Pilibhit">Pilibhit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shahjahanpur" title="Shahjahanpur">Shahjahanpur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rampur,_Uttar_Pradesh" title="Rampur, Uttar Pradesh">Rampur</a> on the west, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Udham_Singh_Nagar" title="Udham Singh Nagar">Udham Singh Nagar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Uttarakhand" title="Uttarakhand">Uttarakhand</a>) to the north and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Badaun" title="Badaun">Badaun</a> to the south. The city is level and well-watered, sloping towards the south. Its soil is fertile, with groves of trees. A rain forest in the north, known as the <i>tarai</i>, contains tigers, bears, deer and wild pigs. The river Sarda (or Gogra) forms the eastern boundary and is the principal waterway. The Ramganga receives most of the drainage from the Kumaon mountains, and the Deoha also receives many small streams. The <a href="/wiki/Gomti_River" title="Gomti River">Gomati</a> (or Gumti) is also nearby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1911encyclopedia.org_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-1911encyclopedia.org-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bareilly is on the <a href="/wiki/Ganges" title="Ganges">Ganges</a> plain, with fertile alluvial soil; however, the lower plain is flood-prone. The city is on the Ramganga, with seven other rivers passing through the district. The lower <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Himalaya" title="Himalaya">Himalayas</a> are 40 kilometres (25 mi) north of the river.</p>

<p>The literacy rate in 2011. The predominant languages are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindi_language" title="Hindi language">Hindi</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urdu_language" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>. Other lesser spoken languages are <a href="/wiki/Punjabi_language" title="Punjabi language">Punjabi</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kumauni_language" title="Kumauni language">Kumaoni</a>. Nearly quater of Muslim population of Bareilly migrated to <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> after independence in 1947 while large swathes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punjabi_people" title="Punjabi people">Punjabi</a> <a href="/wiki/Khatri" title="Khatri">Khatris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arora" title="Arora">Aroras</a> from Pakistan settled in the city.</p>
<p>There were 669,681 children under age six (352,479 boys and 317,202 girls) in 2011, a decline of 6.9 percent from the 2001 census. They comprised 15 percent of the population, down from 19.88 percent in 2001. The district is home to 2.24 percent of the total Uttar Pradesh population, unchanged from 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindus" title="Hindus">Hindus</a> were 64.81 percent of the population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icssr.org_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-icssr.org-19">[19]</a></sup> Other groups are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baniya" title="Baniya">Baniya</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punjabi_people" title="Punjabi people">Punjabi</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kayasth" title="Kayasth">Kayasth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurmi" title="Kurmi">Kurmi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jatav" title="Jatav">Jatav</a> and Deval Patwa <a href="/wiki/Rajput" title="Rajput">Thakurs</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> constitute 33.89 percent of the population (90 percent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunni" title="Sunni">Sunni</a> <a href="/wiki/Hanafi" title="Hanafi">Hanafi</a> and 10 percent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shia" title="Shia">Shia</a> <a href="/wiki/Twelver" title="Twelver">Ithnā‘ashariyyah</a>). <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sikhs" title="Sikhs">Sikhs</a> comprise 0.8 percent of population, Christians 0.2 percent, <a href="/wiki/Jainism" title="Jainism">Jains</a> 0.02 percent and others 0.28 percent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icssr.org_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-icssr.org-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entertainment_and_performing_arts">Entertainment and performing arts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Entertainment and performing arts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bareilly has arts and cultural organisations, art galleries and theatres. The annual Craft Fair at YugVeena Library showcases artists. Influenced by the migrants from nearby regions, Bareilly has a varied culture: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brij" title="Brij">Brij</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Mathura" title="Mathura">Mathura</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Awadhi" title="Awadhi">Awadhi</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Lucknow" title="Lucknow">Lucknow</a>), Pahaari (of the Kumaun region of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uttrakhand" title="Uttrakhand">Uttrakhand</a>) and Harayanvi (of northwest Uttar Pradesh). The city hosts a number of fashion shows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The Bollywood film <i>Aligarh</i> casting Manoj Bajpai and <i>Woh 5 Din</i>, a suspense thriller, was shot in Bareilly<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and in 2005's <i><a href="/wiki/Main,_Meri_Patni_Aur_Woh" title="Main, Meri Patni Aur Woh">Main, Meri Patni Aur Woh</a></i>, Rajpal Yadav's wife Veena comes from the city. Bollywood actress and former <a href="/wiki/Miss_World" title="Miss World">Miss World</a> <a href="/wiki/Priyanka_Chopra" title="Priyanka Chopra">Priyanka Chopra</a> is from Bareilly. Parents of Mahanayak Amitabh Bacchan met for the first time in Bareilly College,Bareilly.Besides this notable Bollywood actor <a href="/wiki/Rati_Agnihotri" title="Rati Agnihotri">Rati Agnihotri</a> was born in the city later shifted to Mumbai. <a href="/wiki/Kavita_Seth" title="Kavita Seth">Kavita Seth</a>, famous Sufi and Bollywodd singer was born and brought up in the city.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Cuisine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bareilly has a number of restaurants which follow the tradition of naming a cuisine after its creator. The city is known for its Seekh Kabaabs, which are sold throughout the old city. Minced meat (primarily lamb), mixed with spices and aromatic herbs, is grilled on skewers over <a href="/wiki/Tamarind" title="Tamarind">tamarind</a> charcoal.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 16th Lok Sabha Election for the Bareilly MP was won by <a href="/wiki/Santosh_Gangwar" title="Santosh Gangwar">Santosh Gangwar</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bharatiya_Janta_Party" title="Bharatiya Janta Party">Bharatiya Janta Party</a>. He defeated <a href="/wiki/Praveen_Singh_Aron" title="Praveen Singh Aron">Praveen Singh Aron</a> by huge margin to retain his stronghold. Bareilly has been a traditional battleground between the INC and the <a href="/wiki/Hindu_nationalism" title="Hindu nationalism">saffron parties</a>. Regional parties such as the <a href="/wiki/Samajwadi_Party" title="Samajwadi Party">Samajwadi Party</a>, led by Veerpal Singh Yadav, and the <a href="/wiki/Bahujan_Samaj_Party" title="Bahujan Samaj Party">Bahujan Samaj Party</a> have a limited influence.</p>
<p>Bareilly was a stronghold of the <a href="/wiki/Bharatiya_Janata_Party" title="Bharatiya Janata Party">Bharatiya Janata Party</a> (BJP) for 20 years (1989–2009), when both the Member of Parliament (MP) and the Member of the Legislative Assembly (MLA) was from the BJP. The city saw the emergence of <a href="/wiki/Hindu_nationalism" title="Hindu nationalism">Hindu nationalism</a> during the last two decades, accompanied by the growth of <a href="/wiki/Rashtriya_Swayamsevak_Sangh" title="Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh">Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vishva_Hindu_Parishad" title="Vishva Hindu Parishad">Vishva Hindu Parishad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bajrang_Dal" title="Bajrang Dal">Bajrang Dal</a> in the region.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Santosh_Gangwar" title="Santosh Gangwar">Santosh Gangwar</a> was a Member of Parliament for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bareilly_(Lok_Sabha_Constituency)" title="Bareilly (Lok Sabha Constituency)">Bareilly</a> for 20 years (1989–2009). He was a former Minister of State for Petroleum and Natural Gas, with an additional charge of Parliament Affairs in the 13th <a href="/wiki/Lok_Sabha" title="Lok Sabha">Lok Sabha</a>. Before this, Gangwar was Minister of State of Science and Technology with an additional charge of Parliamentary Affairs from October to November 1999 and chief whip of the BJP in the 14th <a href="/wiki/Lok_Sabha" title="Lok Sabha">Lok Sabha</a>. He was narrowly defeated in the 15th Lok Sabha elections in 2009.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since India began liberalising its economy, Bareilly has experienced rapid growth. Commerce has diversified with mall culture, although the area's rural economy remains agrarian, handicraft (zari-zardosi embroidery work on cloth material), bamboo and cane furniture. The city is equidistant from New Delhi and Lucknow, the capital of Uttar Pradesh.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Agriculture">Agriculture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Agriculture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bareilly has very productive land (Tarai) for growing Sugarcane, Rice, pulses &amp; wheat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hindustan_Unilever" title="Hindustan Unilever">Hindustan Unilever</a> has begun growing rice in Bareilly and the Punjab, but the company desires legal reforms and facility construction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Electricity">Electricity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Electricity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, Uttar Pradesh Power Corporation Limited (UPPCL) awarded pilot contracts to supply power to nine cities to companies who will collect revenue for the state government. Bareilly, Agra, Kanpur, Moradabad and Gorakhpur will be part of the first phase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Indian_Fuel">Indian Fuel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Indian Fuel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Indian government initiated a 10-percent-ethanol-blending programme on a pilot basis in Bareilly and <a href="/wiki/Belgaum" title="Belgaum">Belgaum</a> in Karnataka. The city also has CNG and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liquid_petroleum_gas" title="Liquid petroleum gas">liquid petroleum gas</a> (LPG) outlets. Bareilly district was the first to implement India's bio-fuel standard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>*I</p>

<p>The street system in Bareilly is traditional, with most roads oriented towards different cities. The city centre is the intersection of Nainital Road and Bada Bazaar–Shahamat Ganj Road at a street known as Kutubkhana. It is a congested street, and the entry of cars or heavy vehicles is prohibited during the day. The Ayyub Khan Choraha–Chaupla and Chowki Chauraha–Chaupla Roads run from Lucknow Road to Delhi Road <a href="/wiki/National_Highway_24_(India)" title="National Highway 24 (India)">National Highway 24</a>. <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Uttar_Pradesh" title="Government of Uttar Pradesh">Government of Uttar Pradesh</a> has proposed 200 km six lane expressway from <a href="/wiki/Faridpur,_Uttar_Pradesh" title="Faridpur, Uttar Pradesh">Faridpur</a> near Bareilly to Pariyar in Unnao district (near <a href="/wiki/Bithoor" title="Bithoor">Bithoor</a>) to connect Bareilly with Kanpur and reduce distance between both cities from 10 hours to 3 hours. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh_State_Highway_37" title="Uttar Pradesh State Highway 37">Nainital Road</a> (including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Highway_74_(India)" title="National Highway 74 (India)">Pilibhit By-pass Road</a>) and Badaun Road begain at Kutubkhana. Heavy traffic is allowed on these roads only from Koharapeer Sabji-Mandi and Chaupla Crossroads.</p>
<p>Arterial streets include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Stadium Road (connecting Pilibhit Road (D.D. Puram) to the ShyamGanj crossroad)</li>

<li>Shahamat Ganj–Bareilly Cantt–Chowki Chauraha–Chaupla Road</li>
<li>I.V.R.I. Road (connecting Nainital Road to Pilibhit Road)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Civil_Lines,_Bareilly" title="Civil Lines, Bareilly">Civil Lines</a> Road</li>
<li>Highway connecting Delhi to Lucknow four lane via Bareilly is a 29 km long highway which bypasses the city crowd of Bareilly, ensuring the smooth running of local traffic.</li>

<p>The inter-city satellite bus stand is just outside the city, at the intersection of National Highway 24 and the Pilibhit By-pass Road. The old bus stand in <a href="/wiki/Civil_Lines,_Bareilly" title="Civil Lines, Bareilly">Civil Lines</a> still well-used by state-owned buses to Delhi.</p>

<p>As part of the expansion of National Highway 24 (connecting Lucknow to New Delhi via Shahjahanpur, Bareilly, Rampur, <a href="/wiki/Moradabad" title="Moradabad">Moradabad</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ghaziabad,_India" title="Ghaziabad, India">Ghaziabad</a>) to four lanes, two contracts were awarded on a Design, Build, Finance, Operate and Transfer (DBFOT) basis for the <a href="/wiki/National_Highways_Authority_of_India" title="National Highways Authority of India">National Highways Authority of India</a> (NHAI). Bareilly Ring Road (Bypass) provides an excellent platform for setting up of industries, MNCs, residential townships, shopping malls, school and colleges, hospitals, airport, railways, etc.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail <span id="Railway"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bareilly_Railway_Station" title="Bareilly Railway Station">Bareilly Railway Station</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Bareilly has been connected to the rest of India by rail since the 19th century, and a 1909 map shows that Bareilly was a railway junction during the early 20th century. Six rail lines intersect in the city. In 1890 the Bengal and North Western Railway leased the Tirhoot State Railway to increase the latter's revenue, and the Lucknow-Sitapur-Seramow Provincial State Railway merged with the Bareilly-Pilibheet Provincial State Railway to form the Lucknow-Bareilly Railway on 1 January 1891. The Lucknow-Bareilly Railway was owned by the Government of India, and operated by the Rohilkund and Kumaon Railway.</p>
<p>The Oudh and Tirhut Railway was formed on 1 January 1943 by the merger of the Bengal and North Western Railway, the Tirhut Railway (BNW operated), the Mashrak-Thawe Extension Railway (BNW operated), the Rohilkund and Kumaon Railway and the Lucknow-Bareilly Railway (R&amp;K operated). The Oudh and Tirhut Railway was later renamed the Oudh Tirhut Railway; it merged with the Assam Railway and the Kanpur-Achnera section of the <a href="/wiki/Bombay,_Baroda_and_Central_India_Railway" title="Bombay, Baroda and Central India Railway">Bombay, Baroda and Central India Railway</a> to form <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Eastern_Railway_(India)" title="North Eastern Railway (India)">North Eastern Railway</a> (headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Gorakhpur" title="Gorakhpur">Gorakhpur</a>, with a divisional headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Izzatnagar" title="Izzatnagar">Izzatnagar</a>), one of the 16 zones of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Railways" title="Indian Railways">Indian Railways</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-r-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">

<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bareilly_Junction" title="Bareilly Junction">Bareilly Junction</a> (broad and meter gauge)</li>
<li>C.B. Ganj Station (broad gauge)</li>
<li>Bareilly Cant (broad gauge)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bareilly_City_railway_station" title="Bareilly City railway station">Bareilly City Station</a> (broad and meter gauge)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Izzatnagar" title="Izzatnagar">Izzatnagar</a> Station (meter and broad gauge)</li>
<li>Bhojipura Station (meter and broad gauge)</li>
</ul>
<p>Bareilly is on the <a href="/wiki/Moradabad" title="Moradabad">Moradabad</a>-Lucknow route. Trains from the north (including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jammu_Tawi" title="Jammu Tawi">Jammu Tawi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amritsar" title="Amritsar">Amritsar</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a> running east and northeast (to <a href="/wiki/Gorakhpur" title="Gorakhpur">Gorakhpur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barauni" title="Barauni">Barauni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Howrah" title="Howrah">Howrah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guwahati" title="Guwahati">Guwahati</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dibrugarh" title="Dibrugarh">Dibrugarh</a>) pass through Bareilly, and the city is also on the route from <a href="/wiki/Uttarakhand" title="Uttarakhand">Uttarakhand</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kasganj" title="Kasganj">Kasganj</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mathura" title="Mathura">Mathura</a>. Many trains to railway stations in <a href="/wiki/Uttarakhand" title="Uttarakhand">Uttarakhand</a> pass through Bareilly.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air">Air</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Air">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Indian_Air_Force" title="Indian Air Force">Indian Air Force</a> has a <a href="/wiki/Bareilly_Airport" title="Bareilly Airport">base</a> at Izzatnagar, on the outskirts of Bareilly. Although it is not open to civilian aviation (except for high government officials), there are plans for a civilian terminal adjacent to the runway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Defence_installations">Defence installations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bareilly&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Defence installations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to the air-force base, Bareilly is the regimental centre and a major settlement of the <a href="/wiki/Jat_Regiment" title="Jat Regiment">Jat Regiment</a> (one of the longest-serving and most-decorated <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">infantry</a> regiments of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Army" title="Indian Army">Indian Army</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-india-defence.com_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-india-defence.com-35">[35]</a></sup> The regiment won 19 battle honours from 1839 to 1947,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> and five <a href="/wiki/Battle_honour" title="Battle honour">battle honours</a>, eight <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mahavir_Chakra" title="Mahavir Chakra">Mahavir Chakra</a>, eight <a href="/wiki/Kirti_Chakra" title="Kirti Chakra">Kirti Chakra</a>, 32 <a href="/wiki/Shaurya_Chakra" title="Shaurya Chakra">Shaurya Chakras</a>, 39 <a href="/wiki/Vir_Chakra" title="Vir Chakra">Vir Chakras</a> and 170 <a href="/wiki/Sena_Medal" title="Sena Medal">Sena Medals</a> since independence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-india-defence.com_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-india-defence.com-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
