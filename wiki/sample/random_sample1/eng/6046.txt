<p><b>Barueri</b> (<small>Portuguese pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[bɐˌɾu.eˈɾi]</a></span> or <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Portuguese" title="Help:IPA for Portuguese">[baɾweˈɾi]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Brazil" title="Municipalities of Brazil">municipality</a> in the State of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(state)" title="São Paulo (state)">São Paulo</a> located in the northwestern part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Region_of_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Metropolitan Region of São Paulo">Metropolitan Region of São Paulo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The population is 262,275 (2015 est.) in an area of 65.70 km².<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Its boundaries are <a href="/wiki/Santana_de_Parna%C3%ADba" title="Santana de Parnaíba">Santana de Parnaíba</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Osasco" title="Osasco">Osasco</a> in the east, <a href="/wiki/Carapicu%C3%ADba" title="Carapicuíba">Carapicuíba</a> in the Southeast, <a href="/wiki/Jandira" title="Jandira">Jandira</a> to the south and southwest and <a href="/wiki/Itapevi" title="Itapevi">Itapevi</a> the west. The city is served by the trains of line 8 of the <a href="/wiki/Companhia_Paulista_de_Trens_Metropolitanos" title="Companhia Paulista de Trens Metropolitanos">Companhia Paulista de Trens Metropolitanos</a> (literally <i>São Paulo Metropolitan Railway Company</i>), (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CPTM" title="CPTM">CPTM</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barueri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Foto_antiga_Barueri.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="588" data-file-width="993" height="130" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Foto_antiga_Barueri.png/220px-Foto_antiga_Barueri.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Foto_antiga_Barueri.png/330px-Foto_antiga_Barueri.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Foto_antiga_Barueri.png/440px-Foto_antiga_Barueri.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Foto_antiga_Barueri.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Centro area of Barueri in 1920</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>Barueri</i> means "Red flower that amazes" in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tupi%E2%80%93Guarani_language" title="Tupi–Guarani language">Tupi–Guarani language</a>. According to historians, the history of Barueri dates back to November 11, 1560 with the establishment of <i>Nossa Senhora Da Escada Chapel</i> by jesuit missionary <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_de_Anchieta" title="José de Anchieta">José de Anchieta</a> and his further settlement. The hamlet grew to the point of the construction, in 1870 of the <i>Sorocabana Railway</i> that initiated its duties in 1875 turning Barueri into an important point of connection between <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santana_de_Parna%C3%ADba" title="Santana de Parnaíba">Santana de Parnaíba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pirapora_do_Bom_Jesus" title="Pirapora do Bom Jesus">Pirapora do Bom Jesus</a>. Still belonging to the city of <a href="/wiki/Santana_de_Parna%C3%ADba" title="Santana de Parnaíba">Santana de Parnaíba</a>, Barueri was established as an independent city on December 24, 1948 due to its growth. In 1964, the city's territory shrank to two thirds of its original size when <a href="/wiki/Carapicu%C3%ADba" title="Carapicuíba">Carapicuíba</a> emancipated from it.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barueri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Companies like <a href="/wiki/Azul_Brazilian_Airlines" title="Azul Brazilian Airlines">Azul Brazilian Airlines</a>, <a href="/wiki/AES_Eletropaulo" title="AES Eletropaulo">AES Eletropaulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cielo_S.A." title="Cielo S.A.">Cielo</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Redecard" title="Redecard">Redecard</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DASA_S.A." title="DASA S.A.">DASA</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walmart_Brasil" title="Walmart Brasil">Walmart Brasil</a> and others have their headquarters in Barueri.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barueri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The most notable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football (soccer)</a> club in the city is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gr%C3%AAmio_Recreativo_Barueri" title="Grêmio Recreativo Barueri">Grêmio Barueri</a>, which plays at <a href="/wiki/Arena_Barueri" title="Arena Barueri">Arena Barueri</a>, and as of 2009, it is competing in the <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Brazilian top league</a>. But in February 2010 the team was moved to <a href="/wiki/Presidente_Prudente,_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Presidente Prudente, São Paulo">Presidente Prudente</a>, a city that is located in western <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(state)" title="São Paulo (state)">São Paulo (state)</a>. After moving to <a href="/wiki/Presidente_Prudente,_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Presidente Prudente, São Paulo">Presidente Prudente</a> the team's new home stadium was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_Eduardo_Jos%C3%A9_Farah" title="Estádio Eduardo José Farah">Estádio Eduardo José Farah</a>, which has a maximum capacity of 44,414 people.</p>
<p>At the same time <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Campinas_Futebol_Clube" title="Campinas Futebol Clube">Campinas Futebol Clube</a>, relocated to Barueri and became <a href="/wiki/Sport_Club_Barueri" title="Sport Club Barueri">Sport Club Barueri</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2006, some <a href="/wiki/2006_FIBA_World_Championship_for_Women" title="2006 FIBA World Championship for Women">2006 FIBA World Championship for Women</a> <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> matches were hosted at the city's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barueri_Arena" title="Barueri Arena">Barueri Arena</a>, which is an indoor sporting arena.</p>
