<p><b>Barbora Bobuľová</b> (born 29 April 1974) is a <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovak</a> actress. Since 1995, she has lived and worked mainly in Italy.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_work">Life and work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbora_Bobu%C4%BEov%C3%A1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Martin,_Slovakia" title="Martin, Slovakia">Martin</a>, Bobuľová trained at the National Drama Academy in <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a> before moving to Italy in 1995. She made her feature film debut in the Italian film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Prince_of_Homburg_(film)" title="The Prince of Homburg (film)">The Prince von Homburg</a></i>, that was selected to represent Italy at the <a href="/wiki/1997_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="1997 Cannes Film Festival">1997 Cannes Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-festival-cannes.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-festival-cannes.com-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>For her work in the 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/Cuore_Sacro" title="Cuore Sacro">Cuore Sacro</a></i> (<i>Sacred Heart</i>), she won Best Actress Awards at the <a href="/wiki/David_di_Donatello" title="David di Donatello">David di Donatello</a> awards, the <a href="/wiki/Ciak_d%27oro" title="Ciak d'oro">Ciak d'oro</a> awards and Audience Award for Best Actress at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flaiano_Film_Festival" title="Flaiano Film Festival">Flaiano Film Festival</a> in Pescara. In 2006 she received the Nastro Europeo at the <a href="/wiki/Nastro_d%27Argento" title="Nastro d'Argento">Nastro d'Argento</a> (Silver Ribbon) awards from the Italian National Syndicate of Film Journalists.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Bobuľová's additional film credits include <i>Il siero della vanità</i>, <i>Check and Mate</i>, <i>Mirka</i>, <i>Poor Liza</i>, <i>That's It</i> and <i>Green Ashes</i>. American television audiences know Bobuľová from her starring role in the Hallmark Hall of Fame movie <i><a href="/wiki/In_Love_and_War_(2001_film)" title="In Love and War (2001 film)">In Love and War</a></i>, where she plays <a href="/wiki/Eric_Newby" title="Eric Newby">Eric Newby</a>'s girlfriend Wanda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Her additional TV credits include the Italian miniseries <i>La Cittadella</i>, <i>La guerra è finita</i> and <i>Crociati</i>. In 2008, Bobuľová co-starred with <a href="/wiki/Shirley_MacLaine" title="Shirley MacLaine">Shirley MacLaine</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Coco_Chanel_(film)" title="Coco Chanel (film)">Coco Chanel</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In addition to her native <a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a>, Bobuľová is fluent in <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a>, Italian, and English.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
