<p>The <b>PGE Narodowy</b> (official name since 2015) or <b>National Stadium</b> (<a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <span lang="pl" xml:lang="pl"><i>Stadion Narodowy</i></span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Polish" title="Help:IPA for Polish">[ˈstadjɔn narɔˈdɔvɨ]</a></span>) is a retractable roof <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">stadium</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>. It is used mostly for football matches and it is the home stadium of <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland national football team</a>.</p>
<p>The stadium has a <a href="/wiki/Seating_capacity" title="Seating capacity">seating capacity</a> of 58,145 which makes it the largest association football arena in Poland. Its construction started in 2008 and finished in November 2011. It is located on the site of the former <a href="/wiki/10th-Anniversary_Stadium" title="10th-Anniversary Stadium">10th-Anniversary Stadium</a>, on Aleja Zieleniecka in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Praga_Po%C5%82udnie" title="Praga Południe">Praga Południe</a> district, near the city center. The stadium has a retractable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PVC" title="PVC">PVC</a> roof which unfolds from a nest on a spire suspended above the centre of the pitch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The retractable roof is inspired by the cable-supported unfolding system of <a href="/wiki/Commerzbank-Arena" title="Commerzbank-Arena">Commerzbank-Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, and is similar to the newly renovated roof of <a href="/wiki/BC_Place" title="BC Place">BC Place</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. The stadium is also very similar to the <a href="/wiki/Arena_Na%C8%9Bional%C4%83" title="Arena Națională">Arena Națională</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a> in terms of age, capacity and the roof.</p>
<p>The National Stadium hosted the opening match (a group match), the 2 group matches, a quarterfinal, and the semifinal of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>, co-hosted by Poland and Ukraine.</p>
<p>The stadium is equipped with a heated pitch, training pitch, façade lighting, and underground parking. It is a multipurpose venue able to host sporting events, concerts, cultural events, and conferences. The official stadium opening took place on 19 January 2012, and the first football match was played on 29 February 2012. The match between the <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Polish national football team</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portuguese team</a> ended with a 0–0 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The stadium hosted the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2014–15 UEFA Europa League">2014–15 UEFA Europa League</a> final.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium_specifics">Stadium specifics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Stadium specifics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_and_architecture">Construction and architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Construction and architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Narodowy-otwarcie19.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1034" data-file-width="1551" height="187" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Narodowy-otwarcie19.JPG/280px-Narodowy-otwarcie19.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Narodowy-otwarcie19.JPG/420px-Narodowy-otwarcie19.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Narodowy-otwarcie19.JPG/560px-Narodowy-otwarcie19.JPG 2x" width="280"/></a>

<p>The general contractor of the National Stadium was a German-Austrian-Polish consortium led by Alpine Bau and made up of Alpine Bau Deutschland, Alpine Construction Poland, <a href="/wiki/PBG_SA" title="PBG SA">PBG SA</a> and Hydrobudowa Poland SA. The completion date was set for 24 months from the signing of the contract and the construction process involved approximately 1,200 employees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The stadium has a capacity of 58,145 seats for spectators during football matches and up to 72,900 during concerts and other events (including 106 sites for disabled people). The total volume of the stadium (without the roof) is more than 1,000,000 m² and the total area is 204,000 m². The retractable roof structure is 240 × 270 m and the central spire stands at a height of 124 metres above the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Vistula" title="River Vistula">River Vistula</a> and 100 m above the pitch. The total length of the lower promenade is 924 meters. The stadium has the largest conference center in Warsaw with a capacity of 1600 people including 25,000 m² of commercial office space. Underground parking for 1765 cars is located beneath the pitch. The stadium contains restaurants, a fitness club, a pub, and 69 luxury skyboxes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Warsaw_National_Stadium_before_Germany_-_Italy_(4).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="4608" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Warsaw_National_Stadium_before_Germany_-_Italy_%284%29.jpg/300px-Warsaw_National_Stadium_before_Germany_-_Italy_%284%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Warsaw_National_Stadium_before_Germany_-_Italy_%284%29.jpg/450px-Warsaw_National_Stadium_before_Germany_-_Italy_%284%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Warsaw_National_Stadium_before_Germany_-_Italy_%284%29.jpg/600px-Warsaw_National_Stadium_before_Germany_-_Italy_%284%29.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>

The crown of National Stadium with red and white <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fa%C3%A7ade" title="Façade">façade</a> resembling <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Poland" title="Flag of Poland">Polish national colors</a></div>
</div>

<p>The National Stadium is a multi-sports facility that allows for the organization of sporting events, concerts and cultural events. In addition, it will also serve as an office, market place, hotel, gastronomic point and have other uses. As a result, it is expected that about 2000 to 3000 people will visit the stadium every single day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Facade">Facade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Facade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The stadium's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fa%C3%A7ade" title="Façade">façade</a> refers to the Polish national colors, resembling a waving <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Poland" title="Flag of Poland">Flag of Poland</a> and it consists of silver and red colors. The same palette was used to color the stadium's seating. The facade which consists of painted mesh that was imported from <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, covers the inner aluminum and glass elevation. The stadium is an open structure, which means the lack of a closed facade, so the temperature inside is similar to the environmental temperature, despite the closed roof construction. Such a construction allows for natural ventilation of rooms placed under the stands and access to natural light.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_12.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="921" data-file-width="1024" height="216" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_12.jpg/240px-POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_12.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_12.jpg/360px-POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_12.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_12.jpg/480px-POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_12.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>

<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tip-up_seat" title="Tip-up seat">Tip-up</a></i> standard stadium seats, type: FCB in red and silver colors</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Elevations are stretched on a powerful construction of the pipes that were manufactured in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. This structure is completely independent from the concrete stand construction and it is fundamental to the retractable stadium’s roof. Thanks to this, designers could freely design the space under the stands.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_pitch">The pitch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The pitch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The stadium is equipped with a heated pitch. The pitch is installed with a lawn of Dutch grass, cultivated in <a href="/wiki/Heythuysen" title="Heythuysen">Heythuysen</a>, the Netherlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> During the organization of events such as concerts, the pitch will be covered with special panel, which must be removed within 5 days of its installation. A second option: to install a grass field on a special floating platform, was discarded due to it being too expensive.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_grandstands">The grandstands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The grandstands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The National Stadium was designed by the German-Polish consortium gmp Architects von Gerkan, Marg and Partners, J.S.K Architekci Sp. z o.o. and sbp—schlaich bergermann und partner (the design by Volkwin Marg and Hubert Nienhoff with Markus Pfisterer, Zbigniew Pszczulny, Mariusz Rutz, Marcin Chruslinski).</p>
<p>The structure is composed of two-level stands—top and bottom—with a capacity for 58,145 spectators. All seating in the National Stadium was provided by Polish company Forum Seating (part of the <a href="/wiki/Nowy_Styl_Group" title="Nowy Styl Group">Nowy Styl Group</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Krosno" title="Krosno">Krosno</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> There are 900 seats for media and press, more than 4,600 so-called "premium seats", designed for special guests, 106 seats for disabled people and more than 800 seats in the VIP lodges.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_09.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" height="195" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_09.jpg/260px-POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_09.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_09.jpg/390px-POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_09.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_09.jpg/520px-POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_09.jpg 2x" width="260"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:POL_Stadion_Narodowy_Warszawa_09.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The unique retractable roof <a href="/wiki/Membrane" title="Membrane">membrane</a> during the process of opening</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Under the stands, there are changing rooms, conference halls and living areas with a total area of 130 000 m². The building has eight stories with varied heights. The highest point at the stands, is located 41 meters above the former 10th-Anniversary Stadium pitch, while the highest point of the steel roof structure is 70 meters above that level. The roof can cover not only the stands, but also the pitch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retractable_roof">Retractable roof</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Retractable roof">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Partially transparent, the retractable roof was made of fibreglass covered with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teflon" title="Teflon">teflon</a>. This kind of material is resistant to weather factors (rain, the heat of the sun, and can hold up to 18 cm of wet snow) and the crease tendency. The production technology comes from German company Hightex GmbH, and the textile was produced in Bangkok by the Asia Membrane Co. Ltd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The process of opening or closing the roof takes about 20 minutes and it can only be performed at temperatures above 5 °C and not during rain (this was the reason for a one-day postponement of the football match against England on 16 October 2012). A drive system is used for stretching the <a href="/wiki/Membrane" title="Membrane">membrane</a> during the process of opening and for folding the material during the process of closing the roof. The total weight of the steel-cables supporting the roof structure is 1,200 tons. Under the roof there are four <a href="/wiki/LED_display" title="LED display">LED display</a> screens, each with an area of 200 m².</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_history">Construction history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Construction history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stadion_Narodowy_-_Wisla.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Night view of the National Stadium and the <a href="/wiki/%C5%9Awi%C4%99tokrzyski_Bridge" title="Świętokrzyski Bridge">Świętokrzyski Bridge</a> from the left bank of the <a href="/wiki/Vistula" title="Vistula">Vistula</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On 1 February 2008, the consortium of JSK Architects Ltd., GMP—von Gerkan, Marg und Partner Architekten and SBP—Schleich Bergermann und Partner presented a conceptual design (visualization and scale model) of a new stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-urbanity.pl_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-urbanity.pl-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first pre-construction work began on 15 May 2008 when 126 concrete piles were driven into the soil of the basin of the old stadium's grandstand. On 18 June 2008, the National Sports Centre Ltd submitted documents required to obtain a <a href="/wiki/Construction_permit" title="Construction permit">construction permit</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Voivode#Poland" title="Voivode">governor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Masovian_Voivodeship" title="Masovian Voivodeship">Masovia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-urbanity.pl_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-urbanity.pl-18">[18]</a></sup> This was approved on 22 July 2008, and on 26 September 2008, an agreement with Pol-Aqua SA to implement the first stage of construction work was signed. A few days later, on 7 October 2008, the construction of the stadium began.</p>
<p>On the construction site, close to the National Sports Centre, an outdoor webcam was installed. Broadcasting started on 31 October 2008 and people could track the progress of construction. Since the start of the second stage of construction on 29 June 2009, the entire process was also viewable from a second camera installed on a tower at Washington Roundabout. Images from the cameras are still available on the official websites of the stadium.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_process">Main process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Main process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The first stage of construction included the demolition of concrete structures of the 10th-Anniversary Stadium, preparation of the ground, driving about 7000 concrete piles into the soil, construction of 6700 gravel and concrete columns, and the building of approximately 900 <a href="/wiki/Pile_driver" title="Pile driver">construction piles</a> that now form the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foundation_(architecture)" title="Foundation (architecture)">foundation</a> of the stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 March 2009 the pile driving process was completed, and exactly one month later, opening of the offers from companies wishing to implement the second stage of the stadium construction took place. The best offer was introduced by German-Austrian-Polish consortium of companies - Alpine Bau Deutschland AG, Alpine Bau GmbH and Alpine Construction Poland Ltd., Hydrobudowa Poland SA and PBG SA and it was worth €1 252 755 008.64 zł.</p>
<p>At the end of September, the first construction elements were visible from outside the stadium. The <a href="/wiki/Cornerstone" title="Cornerstone">cornerstone</a> (foundation stone) and a <a href="/wiki/Time_capsule" title="Time capsule">time capsule</a> were set during the ceremony held on 7 October 2009. The time capsule contained flags of <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Poland" title="Flag of Poland">Poland</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Europe" title="Flag of Europe">European Union</a> and the city of <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Warsaw" title="Flag of Warsaw">Warsaw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newspaper" title="Newspaper">newspapers</a> of the day, <a href="/wiki/Coin" title="Coin">coins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Banknote" title="Banknote">banknotes</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/Artifact_(archaeology)" title="Artifact (archaeology)">artifacts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of January the first element of the roof structure arrived at the construction site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> This element was 1 of 72 that became part of the massive steel roof structure. Each of them weighs about 48 tons and is 12.5 meters tall. The completion of installation of all <a href="/wiki/Prefabrication" title="Prefabrication">prefabricated elements</a> took place by 13 August 2010, which represented the entire structure of the stadium stands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Ten days later all concrete works were finished.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Completion_.26_opening">Completion &amp; opening</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Completion &amp; opening">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The National Stadium was originally planned to be completed on 30 June 2011. The stadium was scheduled to be opened to the public on July 22, 2011, while its official opening was scheduled to take place on August 27.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Due to ongoing construction the event was moved to January 2012 and only an inaugural illumination of the facade of the stadium took place in August. A match against the <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany national football team</a> had been scheduled on 6 September 2011 but this was relocated to <a href="/wiki/Gda%C5%84sk" title="Gdańsk">Gdańsk</a>, because the National Stadium wasn't ready yet.</p>
<p>Construction work was officially completed on 29 November 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> One day later, Rafał Kapler - The NCS President submitted to the <a href="/wiki/Site_manager" title="Site manager">site manager</a> an application needed to get a <a href="/wiki/Certificate_of_occupancy" title="Certificate of occupancy">certificate of occupancy</a>. The official opening ceremony of the stadium took place on 29 January 2012. The event was celebrated by concerts by Polish celebrities: <a href="/wiki/Voo_Voo" title="Voo Voo">Voo Voo</a> and Haydamaky, <a href="/wiki/Zakopower" title="Zakopower">Zakopower</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coma_(band)" title="Coma (band)">Coma</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T._Love" title="T. Love">T. Love</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Pank" title="Lady Pank">Lady Pank</a> and ended with an evening fireworks show. On 10 February 2012, installation of heating and irrigation systems and the pitch installation was completed.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Railways_and_metro">Railways and metro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Railways and metro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The stadium is located near the railway station <a href="/wiki/Warszawa_Stadion_railway_station" title="Warszawa Stadion railway station">Warszawa Stadion</a>. The station has two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Side_platforms" title="Side platforms">side platforms</a> flanking the suburban tracks of the <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Cross-City_Line" title="Warsaw Cross-City Line">Warsaw Cross-City Line</a> used by the regional trains run by <a href="/wiki/Koleje_Mazowieckie" title="Koleje Mazowieckie">Koleje Mazowieckie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Szybka_Kolej_Miejska_(Warsaw)" title="Szybka Kolej Miejska (Warsaw)">Szybka Kolej Miejska</a>. The stadium can be reached by the S1 and S2 lines. The trip from central Warsaw takes about 5 minutes, and during the rush hours trains run every 4 minutes. Within an hour about 26000 people could reach stadium only by trains. In early 2012, the station has undergone thorough modernization in preparation for the new stadium and for the UEFA Euro 2012.</p>
<p>The stadium is accessible from the <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_Metro" title="Warsaw Metro">Warsaw Metro</a>. The closest station is <a href="/wiki/Stadion_Narodowy_metro_station" title="Stadion Narodowy metro station">Stadion Narodowy metro station</a> (C14) opened in March, 2015.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Buses_and_trams">Buses and trams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Buses and trams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Around the stadium there are several tram and bus stops. The most convenient way to reach the stadium from the city centre is to use the transport hub located on Rondo Waszyngtona.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Euro_2012_matches">Euro 2012 matches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Euro 2012 matches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_football">American football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: American football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 15, 2012, two weeks after the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2012" title="UEFA Euro 2012">UEFA Euro 2012</a>, the National Stadium hosted the VII SuperFinal PLFA (more commonly known as the <a href="/wiki/Polish_Bowl" title="Polish Bowl">Polish Bowl</a>), the championship game of the <a href="/wiki/Polish_American_Football_League" title="Polish American Football League">Polish American Football League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_Picnic">Science Picnic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Science Picnic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The stadium is the venue for the <a href="/wiki/Science_Picnic" title="Science Picnic">Science Picnic</a>, an annual science education fair, since 2013. During the 2013 Science Picnic, the stadium was visited by 142,573 people, which was at the time record attendance at any type of event held at the stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_FIVB_Volleyball_Men.27s_World_Championship">2014 FIVB Volleyball Men's World Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Stadium,_Warsaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: 2014 FIVB Volleyball Men's World Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 30, 2014, the National Stadium hosted the opening ceremony and match (Poland vs. Serbia) of the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIVB_Volleyball_Men%27s_World_Championship" title="2014 FIVB Volleyball Men's World Championship">2014 FIVB Volleyball Men's World Championship</a>. Poland beat Serbia in front of 61,500 spectators – a new record for an FIVB volleyball match.</p>
