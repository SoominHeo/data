<p><b>Barquisimeto</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Spanish" title="Help:IPA for Spanish">[barkisiˈmeto]</a></span>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. It is the capital of the state of <a href="/wiki/Lara_(state)" title="Lara (state)">Lara</a> and head of <a href="/wiki/Iribarren_Municipality" title="Iribarren Municipality">Iribarren Municipality</a>. It is an important urban, industrial, commercial and transportation center of the country, recognized as the fourth-largest city by population and area in Venezuela after <a href="/wiki/Caracas" title="Caracas">Caracas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maracaibo" title="Maracaibo">Maracaibo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencia,_Venezuela" title="Valencia, Venezuela">Valencia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barquisimeto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barquisimeto was founded in 1552 by Juan de Villegas, as a headquarters and to have better control of the territory believed to be rich in gold. Its original name was <b>"Nueva Segovia de Barquisimeto"</b>.</p>
<p>This city had four settlements due to ignorance of the physical environment of the region. The first one was in 1552 nearby Buría River, but moved in 1556 due to frequent floods suffered by inhabitants. The second one was in the valley of the Turbio River where the city stayed until <a href="/wiki/Lope_de_Aguirre" title="Lope de Aguirre">Lope de Aguirre</a> burned it down in 1561. Its rebuilding was made 102 km (63 mi), but in 1562 they asked for permission to move to another site due to strong winds blowing in the place. Finally, Barquisimeto was located on the north plateau of the Turbio River in 1563.</p>
<p>During the country's independence, Barquisimeto joined the liberation movement and its deputy José Ángel Álamo signed the Independence Act on July 5, 1811.</p>
<p>In 1929, the city went through a modernization program carried out by General Juan Vicente Gomez. He fixed the streets and avenues and buildings were built, like the <a href="/wiki/Jacinto_Lara" title="Jacinto Lara">Jacinto Lara</a> Headquarters, the Government Palace and the Ayacucho Park.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Names_and_etymology">Names and etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barquisimeto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Names and etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the German adventurer <a href="/wiki/Nikolaus_Federmann" title="Nikolaus Federmann">Nikolaus Federmann</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caquet%C3%ADo" title="Caquetío">Caquetío</a> aborigines used to call it Variquicimeto, which translates as "ash-colored river", the name with which the natives distinguished the water stream near the city. This river was named "Turbio River" by the Spanish conquerors, a name that is still in use today. Another possible name origin is due to a red dye called <i>bariquí</i>.</p>
<p>When Juan de Villegas founded it, he named the city "Nueva Segovia de Barquisimeto", but years later it became just "Barquisimeto", a word popularized by Oviedo y Baños in his book <i>History and Conquest of the Venezuelan Population</i>.</p>
<p>It is known as the <b>Dusk City</b> due to its beautiful sunsets.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barquisimeto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city's modern <a href="/wiki/Barquisimeto_Cathedral" title="Barquisimeto Cathedral">Barquisimeto Cathedral</a> is the cathedral episcopal see of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Barquisimeto" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Barquisimeto">Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Barquisimeto</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Divina_Pastora">Divina Pastora</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barquisimeto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Divina Pastora">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fachada_de_la_Catedral_Metropolitana_de_Barquisimeto.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1920" data-file-width="2560" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Fachada_de_la_Catedral_Metropolitana_de_Barquisimeto.jpg/220px-Fachada_de_la_Catedral_Metropolitana_de_Barquisimeto.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Fachada_de_la_Catedral_Metropolitana_de_Barquisimeto.jpg/330px-Fachada_de_la_Catedral_Metropolitana_de_Barquisimeto.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Fachada_de_la_Catedral_Metropolitana_de_Barquisimeto.jpg/440px-Fachada_de_la_Catedral_Metropolitana_de_Barquisimeto.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Divina_Pastora_(Barquisimeto)" title="Divina Pastora (Barquisimeto)">Divina Pastora</a> (Divine Shepherdess) is a statue of the Virgin Mary holding the infant Jesus, with a lamb at her side. It is considered to be one of the most important religious icons of Venezuela. Divina Pastora is the patron saint of the city of Barquisimeto and of the Venezuelan National Militia. The original image dates from 1735. Divina Pastora is celebrated in a procession on January 14 of each year, when a massive Marian procession occurs, considered to be one of the largest in the world, attracting thousands of pilgrims.</p>
<p>The statue is removed from its shrine and is carried on the main streets of Barquisimeto in a procession which starts at the Iglesia de la Divina Pastora in Santa Rosa until it reaches the Barquisimeto Cathedral. This procession is unlike other mass Marian celebrations in the world, where the image does not leave its temple. This procession occurs due to the devotion the people of Barquisimeto have towards it as gratitude towards saving the city from a cholera outbreak that occurred in the city in the 19th century. In 2013, 3,000,000 faithful honored the Divina Pastora.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barquisimeto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barquisimeto is located on the terrace of the same name, on the banks of the Turbio River, 622 metres (2,040 feet) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" title="Above mean sea level">above sea level</a> and a population that exceeds 800,000 inhabitants. It has a location on the central western of Venezuela (363 km (226 mi) from Caracas, the country's capital city), being a point of convergence of many of the major land routes and rail, characterized also by its street order and appropriate signage due to the location of the city with a relief almost completely flat, which facilitated the distribution of the urban grid and with the streets numbered in ascending numerical order, factors that help the foreign citizen and easily locate addresses. Its climate is pleasant in the months of December to March, with an average temperature of 26 °C (79 °F) throughout the year.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barquisimeto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the city, the cool <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">climate</a> is dominant. Located in that region, Barquisimeto records 650 mm (25.6 in) of rain per year and its average temperature ranges 19 °C (66 °F).</p>
<p>Although, in higher regions near the Andes, temperatures are lower and the rain is more constant. For instance, in Sanare city, the annual index of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitations</a> is 835 mm (32.9 in).</p>

<p>Barquisimeto is a city with a historic vocation by academic knowledge, and boasts a considerable sample of universities and institutes of higher education in Venezuela, has a high and growing student population from all over the country.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barquisimeto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mode of transportation most commonly used are the “rapiditos”, old automobiles with an average capacity of 5 passengers, with a route similar to the buses and used mainly to shuttle people between downtown and the suburbs, like the shared cabs in other countries.</p>
<p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Transbarca&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Transbarca (page does not exist)">Transbarca</a> system is a new "<a href="/wiki/Bus_rapid_transit" title="Bus rapid transit">bus rapid transit</a>" (BRT) system under construction, which was originally planned to use <a href="/wiki/Trolleybus" title="Trolleybus">trolleybuses</a>. When construction began, in 2006, the system was projected to serve an average of 170,000 people per day when completed, using 80 trolleybuses operating along 30 km (19 mi) of exclusive lanes, across the metropolitan area. Service was planned to include an express route of 22 km (14 mi) along with one local route of 8 km (5 mi). With 52 stations, the BRT system is to include a centralized system of communication and security, and structures for easy access for elderly and disabled people. It is to be complemented with feeder routes, covering the areas of Greater Barquisimeto the BRT line will not serve, which will connect with the main line at terminal stations.</p>
<p>Although 80 <a href="/wiki/Articulated_bus" title="Articulated bus">articulated</a> trolleybuses were purchased from <a href="/wiki/Neoplan" title="Neoplan">Neoplan</a> and delivered around 2008–09,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tm306_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tm306-4">[4]</a></sup> the plans to operate the Transbarca system with those vehicles were dropped in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tm311-p138_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tm311-p138-5">[5]</a></sup> In addition to reasons of cost, an inadequate supply of electricity with which to power the system was cited in the announcement of the decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-el-impulso2013_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-el-impulso2013-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Buses are the main means of mass transportation. The system runs a variety of bus types, operated by several companies on normal streets and avenues:</li>
<li><b>bus</b>; large buses.</li>
<li><b>buseta</b>; medium-sized buses.</li>
<li><b>microbus</b> or <b>colectivo</b>; vans or minivans.</li>
<li><b>rapiditos</b>; old cars with capacity for 5 or more people.</li>
</ul>
<p>The airport is called <a href="/wiki/Jacinto_Lara_International_Airport" title="Jacinto Lara International Airport">Jacinto Lara International Airport</a>.</p>
