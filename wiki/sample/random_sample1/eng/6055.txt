<p><b>Bartosz Kapustka</b> (born 23 December 1996) is a Polish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midfielder_(association_football)" title="Midfielder (association football)">Winger</a> for <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bartosz_Kapustka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kapustka was signed by <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club Leicester City for a reported £7.5m in August 2016, signing a five-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bartosz_Kapustka:_Leicester_City_sign_Poland_winger_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bartosz_Kapustka:_Leicester_City_sign_Poland_winger-1">[1]</a></sup> He cited manager <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Ranieri" title="Claudio Ranieri">Claudio Ranieri</a> as his motivation for joining the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bartosz_Kapustka_explains_why_he_wants_move_to_Leicester_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bartosz_Kapustka_explains_why_he_wants_move_to_Leicester-2">[2]</a></sup> However, Kapustka did not receive any first team football for the first half of the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Premier_League" title="2016–17 Premier League">2016-17</a> season and was only named in two match day squads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leicester_boss_Claudio_Ranieri_unsure_when_.C2.A37.5m_signing_Bartosz_Kapustka_will_be_ready_for_Premier_League_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leicester_boss_Claudio_Ranieri_unsure_when_.C2.A37.5m_signing_Bartosz_Kapustka_will_be_ready_for_Premier_League-3">[3]</a></sup> Ranieri admitted he was unsure when Kapustka "will be ready to play in the Premier League".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leicester_City_recruitment_under_fire_as_Claudio_Ranieri_admits_.C2.A37.5m_signing_is_not_ready_for_Premier_League_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leicester_City_recruitment_under_fire_as_Claudio_Ranieri_admits_.C2.A37.5m_signing_is_not_ready_for_Premier_League-4">[4]</a></sup> Having already played for the <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy" title="Leicester City F.C. Reserves and Academy">Leicester Under-23s</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan</a> move was not possible due to FIFA rules prohibiting a player from appearing for three different clubs in one season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leicester_misfit_Bartosz_Kapustka_travels_with_squad_for_FA_Cup_game_against_Everton_as_club_consider_loan_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leicester_misfit_Bartosz_Kapustka_travels_with_squad_for_FA_Cup_game_against_Everton_as_club_consider_loan-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kapustka finally made his senior debut for Leicester in a 2-1 <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> third-round win over <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a> at <a href="/wiki/Goodison_Park" title="Goodison Park">Goodison Park</a> on 7 January 2017, coming on as a 84th minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Marc_Albrighton" title="Marc Albrighton">Marc Albrighton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Everton_1-2_Leicester_City_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Everton_1-2_Leicester_City-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bartosz_Kapustka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 7 September 2015, Kapustka made his debut appearance for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poland_National_Football_Team" title="Poland National Football Team">Poland national team</a> in a match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gibraltar_National_Football_Team" title="Gibraltar National Football Team">Gibraltar</a>, substituting <a href="/wiki/Jakub_B%C5%82aszczykowski" title="Jakub Błaszczykowski">Jakub Błaszczykowski</a>. He scored his first goal for the national team on his debut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In the build up to the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">Euro 2016</a>, Kapustka was involved in a brawl outside a night club, in which he sustained an injury. Following the incident, he made a phone call personally to Polish national team manager <a href="/wiki/Adam_Nawa%C5%82ka" title="Adam Nawałka">Adam Nawałka</a>, to ask for his forgiveness and ask that he not be excluded from the squad for the upcoming tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Euros_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Euros-9">[9]</a></sup> Kapustka was eventually selected for Poland's Euro 2016 campaign, and produced a standout performance in the team's opening match victory over <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national football team">Northern Ireland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Euros_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Euros-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
