<p><b>Gbarpolu</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Liberia" title="Counties of Liberia">county</a> in the northern portion of <a href="/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a>. One of 15 counties that comprise the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_division" title="Administrative division">first-level of administrative division</a> in the nation, it has six <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Liberia" title="Districts of Liberia">districts</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bopulu" title="Bopulu">Bopulu</a> serves as the capital with the area of the county measuring 9,689 square kilometres (3,741 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census2008_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-census2008-1">[1]</a></sup> As of the 2008 Census, it had a population of 83,758, making it the eleventh-most populous county in Liberia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census2008_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-census2008-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Created in 2001 when it was split from <a href="/wiki/Lofa_County" title="Lofa County">Lofa County</a>, Gbarpolu is the youngest county in Liberia. As of 2013<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Gbarpolu_County&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the County Superintendent was Allen Gbowee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The county is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cape_Mount_County" title="Grand Cape Mount County">Grand Cape Mount County</a> to the west, <a href="/wiki/Bomi_County" title="Bomi County">Bomi County</a> to the southwest, <a href="/wiki/Bong_County" title="Bong County">Bong County</a> to the south, and Lofa County to the east and north. The northwest part of Gbarpolu borders the nation of <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a>. The Gola Forest staddles this border and is home to the <a href="/wiki/Gbarpolu_County#Gola_Forest_community" title="Gbarpolu County">Gola Forest community</a>.</p>
<p>The majority of Gbarpolu County consists of forest. Mining was the primary economic activity prior to the <a href="/wiki/First_Liberian_Civil_War" title="First Liberian Civil War">Liberian Civil War</a>, in addition to subsistence farming. However, the <a href="/wiki/Second_Liberian_Civil_War" title="Second Liberian Civil War">war</a> devastated all sectors of the county.</p>
<p>Gbarpolu County has several gold and diamond mines. Most of the mines are governed by traditions, stating that investors who intend to partner with Gbarpolu County will follow traditional terms by showing desire to the community ruler of their interest in going into partnership with the community. Such traditions normally involve several items being bought by the investor or partners..</p>
<p>Gbarpolu County also produces timber and coal.</p>

<p>The Gola Forest straddles the border between Liberia and <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone" title="Sierra Leone">Sierra Leone</a> and is home to diverse species of importance to the country. In Sierra Leone, the forest is incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/Gola_National_Forest" title="Gola National Forest">Gola National Forest</a>, and because of the unity of the forest with Liberia, considerations of conservation must consider a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Collaborative_management&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Collaborative management (page does not exist)">collaborative management</a> with Liberian inhabitants as well.</p>
<p>Within the Liberian portion of the forest reside four clans: the Sorkpo Clan in <a href="/wiki/Porkpa_District" title="Porkpa District">Porkpa District</a>, the Tonglay and Zuie clans within Kongba district and the Jawijah Chiefdom. There are 24 villages within the Liberian portion of the forest, most of which are accessible by road, but several accessible only by foot path. Residents of these remote villages use foot paths to bring their commodities and services to the other towns with motor roads.</p>
<p>Leadership within the community is purely by traditional authorities, but centers of such authority are very few in relation to the number of temporary mining camps. The landlord-stranger system is the main means to regulate activities of migrants to the forest (mainly miners), but the system is currently weak due to town being distance and limited roads, transportation and communications. Some chiefs are not correctly installed or properly elected and therefore lack authority amongst disgruntled illegal miners.</p>
<p>Most of the original Gola inhabitants became refugees during the <a href="/wiki/First_Liberian_Civil_War" title="First Liberian Civil War">First Liberian Civil War</a> and were slow to return and reassert control of their villages. The war had lasted for over fourteen years, a time too long for people who have had several relocations to return to their former communities. With the coming of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Cluster&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Western Cluster (page does not exist)">Western Cluster</a> in the community, many of the people are expected to return to their original communities, as many could begin finding employment. Others will have time to begin other agricultural activities that might contribute to loss of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biological_diversity" title="Biological diversity">biological diversity</a>.</p>
