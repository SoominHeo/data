<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Bonney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bonney was born in <a href="/wiki/Montclair,_New_Jersey" title="Montclair, New Jersey">Montclair</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>. As a child she studied piano and cello. When Bonney was 13 her family moved to Maine, where she became part of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Portland_Youth_Orchestra&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Portland Youth Orchestra (page does not exist)">Portland Youth Orchestra</a> as a cellist. She spent two years at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_New_Hampshire" title="University of New Hampshire">University of New Hampshire</a> (UNH) studying German and music including voice with Patricia Stedry, and spent her junior year at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Salzburg" title="University of Salzburg">University of Salzburg</a>, where she switched from cello to voice. While there, she studied at the Salzburg <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mozarteum" title="Mozarteum">Mozarteum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Years later she received an honorary doctorate from UNH.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Bonney&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1979, Bonney joined the <a href="/wiki/Staatstheater_Darmstadt" title="Staatstheater Darmstadt">Staatstheater Darmstadt</a>, where she made her debut as Anna in <i>Die lustigen Weiber von Windsor</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/The_Merry_Wives_of_Windsor" title="The Merry Wives of Windsor">The Merry Wives of Windsor</a></i>). In the subsequent five years she made appearances throughout Germany and Europe, notably at the Royal Opera House <a href="/wiki/Covent_Garden" title="Covent Garden">Covent Garden</a> in London and <a href="/wiki/La_Scala" title="La Scala">La Scala</a> in Milan. She made her <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Opera" title="Metropolitan Opera">Metropolitan Opera</a> debut in 1987 in <a href="/wiki/Richard_Strauss" title="Richard Strauss">Richard Strauss'</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Ariadne_auf_Naxos" title="Ariadne auf Naxos">Ariadne auf Naxos</a></i> in the role of Nyade and her <a href="/wiki/Vienna_State_Opera" title="Vienna State Opera">Vienna Staatsoper</a> debut the same year as Sophie in <i><a href="/wiki/Der_Rosenkavalier" title="Der Rosenkavalier">Der Rosenkavalier</a></i>. Since then, she has appeared at the major opera houses of the world and at the <a href="/wiki/Salzburg_Festival" title="Salzburg Festival">Salzburg Festival</a>, where she was <i>Servilia</i> in Mozart's <i><a href="/wiki/La_clemenza_di_Tito" title="La clemenza di Tito">La clemenza di Tito</a></i>.</p>
<p>Along with her repertoire in opera as a lyric soprano, she is a distinguished recitalist, and has appeared on more than 90 recordings, including 15 solo recitals. For two years, starting in 1999, Bonney did not perform in opera, to focus on lieder recitals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, she noted that solo recitals lacked the camaraderie of performing in an opera production with many other people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2002, she contributed "The Willow Song" to the <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/When_Love_Speaks" title="When Love Speaks">When Love Speaks</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/EMI_Classics" title="EMI Classics">EMI Classics</a>), which features famous actors and musicians interpreting <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">Shakespeare's</a> <a href="/wiki/Sonnet" title="Sonnet">sonnets</a> and play excerpts. Other albums include her 2006 work on <i>Welcome to the Voice</i>, composed by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Nieve" title="Steve Nieve">Steve Nieve</a>.</p>
<p>On August 1, 2006, <a href="/wiki/IMG_Artists" title="IMG Artists">IMG Artists</a> announced that all forthcoming appearances by Bonney were cancelled and that they would no longer be representing her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At the time, the IMG Artists website only stated the reason as "due to personal circumstances", but Bonney has stated in a July 2007 article that those circumstances included her "difficult" divorce from Maurice Whitaker. Previously, she was married to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hakan_Hagegard" title="Hakan Hagegard">Hakan Hagegard</a> for seven years, and this marriage ended in divorce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bonney is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Academy_of_Music" title="Royal Swedish Academy of Music">Royal Swedish Academy of Music</a> and is visiting professor at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Music" title="Royal Academy of Music">Royal Academy of Music</a>, London. She is also on the faculty of the Mozarteum, Salzburg, as University Professor of Singing. In addition to her academic activities and singing career, she opened a business based in Salzburg in 2011, on her 55th birthday, LUNA Dress Design.</p>
