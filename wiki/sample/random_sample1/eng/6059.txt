<p><b>Barbara D. Broccoli</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">OBE</a> Hon (born June 18, 1960) is an American film producer known for her work on the <a href="/wiki/James_Bond_in_film" title="James Bond in film"><i>James Bond</i> film series</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Broccoli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Broccoli is the daughter of the <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> producer <a href="/wiki/Albert_R._Broccoli" title="Albert R. Broccoli">Albert R. "Cubby" Broccoli</a> and actress Dana Wilson Broccoli (née Natol). In 1995, Cubby Broccoli handed over control of <a href="/wiki/Eon_Productions" title="Eon Productions">Eon Productions</a>, the production company responsible for the James Bond series of films, to Barbara and her half-brother <a href="/wiki/Michael_G._Wilson" title="Michael G. Wilson">Michael G. Wilson</a>; they continue to run the company as of 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Broccoli married actor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederick_M._Zollo" title="Frederick M. Zollo">Frederick M. Zollo</a> , but has since divorced him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was appointed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Officer_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Officer of the Order of the British Empire">Officer of the Order of the British Empire</a> (OBE) by the Queen of the United Kingdom in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Year_Honours_2008" title="New Year Honours 2008">2008 New Year Honours</a>. In 2014 she was selected as a member of the jury for the <a href="/wiki/64th_Berlin_International_Film_Festival" title="64th Berlin International Film Festival">64th Berlin International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Berlin_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Berlin-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Broccoli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="James_Bond_franchise">James Bond franchise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Broccoli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: James Bond franchise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Broccoli started in the Bond franchise at the age of 17, working in the publicity department of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Spy_Who_Loved_Me_(film)" title="The Spy Who Loved Me (film)">The Spy Who Loved Me</a></i>. Six years later, she became an <a href="/wiki/Assistant_director" title="Assistant director">assistant director</a> on 1983's <i><a href="/wiki/Octopussy" title="Octopussy">Octopussy</a></i>. Soon after, she progressed to become an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Associate_producer" title="Associate producer">associate producer</a> of the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Living_Daylights" title="The Living Daylights">The Living Daylights</a></i> in 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> However, her most notable role has been as a producer of the Bond films starring <a href="/wiki/Pierce_Brosnan" title="Pierce Brosnan">Pierce Brosnan</a> and later <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Craig" title="Daniel Craig">Daniel Craig</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chitty_Chitty_Bang_Bang"><i>Chitty Chitty Bang Bang</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Broccoli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chitty Chitty Bang Bang">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following her father's death in 1996, Broccoli worked with London theatre producer Michael Rose, to create the stage musical version of <i><a href="/wiki/Chitty_Chitty_Bang_Bang_(musical)" title="Chitty Chitty Bang Bang (musical)">Chitty Chitty Bang Bang</a></i> based on the 1968 <a href="/wiki/Musical_film" title="Musical film">musical film</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Dick_Van_Dyke" title="Dick Van Dyke">Dick Van Dyke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sally_Ann_Howes" title="Sally Ann Howes">Sally Ann Howes</a>. Broccoli rehired the original songwriters from the film to write the new material for the stage version. The <a href="/wiki/Sherman_Brothers" title="Sherman Brothers">Sherman Brothers</a> wrote five new songs for the show which debuted on April 16, 2002. The show ran at the <a href="/wiki/London_Palladium" title="London Palladium">London Palladium</a> and was the most financially successful show to have ever played there, breaking numerous other impressive records.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <i>Chitty Chitty Bang Bang</i> later transferred to Broadway, but was considered a failure, receiving poor reviews and playing just 319 performances, closing with the loss of a large proportion of the initial $15m investment. The musical has toured extensively in the UK and in Asia, with a revised version of the show touring the United States in 2008.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chariots_of_Fire_.282012_stage_play.29"><i>Chariots of Fire</i> (2012 stage play)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Broccoli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chariots of Fire (2012 stage play)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Broccoli co-produced <i><a href="/wiki/Chariots_of_Fire_(play)" title="Chariots of Fire (play)">Chariots of Fire</a></i>, the stage adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Chariots_of_Fire" title="Chariots of Fire">the film of the same title</a>. Broccoli's involvement with <i>Chariots of Fire</i> extends back to 1980, when she introduced her friend <a href="/wiki/Dodi_Fayed" title="Dodi Fayed">Dodi Fayed</a> to the screenplay. He later co-financed the film and became its executive producer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She co-produced the play along with <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Hudson" title="Hugh Hudson">Hugh Hudson</a>, who directed the 1981 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Oscar</a>-winning film.</p>
