<p><b>March 1</b> is the 60th day of the year (61st in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 305 days remaining until the end of the year. This date is slightly more likely to fall on a Tuesday, Thursday or Sunday (58 in 400 years each) than on Friday or Saturday (57), and slightly less likely to occur on a Monday or Wednesday (56).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=March_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/752_BC" title="752 BC">752 BC</a> – <a href="/wiki/Romulus" title="Romulus">Romulus</a>, legendary first king of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Rome</a>, celebrates the first <a href="/wiki/Roman_triumph" title="Roman triumph">Roman triumph</a> after his victory over the <a href="/wiki/Caenina_(town)" title="Caenina (town)">Caeninenses</a>, following <a href="/wiki/The_Rape_of_the_Sabine_Women" title="The Rape of the Sabine Women">The Rape of the Sabine Women</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/509_BC" title="509 BC">509 BC</a> – <a href="/wiki/Publius_Valerius_Publicola" title="Publius Valerius Publicola">Publius Valerius Publicola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_consul" title="Roman consul">Roman consul</a>, celebrates the first triumph of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman Republic</a> after his victory over the deposed king <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Tarquinius_Superbus" title="Lucius Tarquinius Superbus">Lucius Tarquinius Superbus</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Silva_Arsia" title="Battle of Silva Arsia">Battle of Silva Arsia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/86_BC" title="86 BC">86 BC</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucius_Cornelius_Sulla" title="Lucius Cornelius Sulla">Lucius Cornelius Sulla</a>, at the head of a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman Republic</a> army, enters <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, removing the <a href="/wiki/Tyrant" title="Tyrant">tyrant</a> <a href="/wiki/Aristion" title="Aristion">Aristion</a> who was supported by troops of <a href="/wiki/Mithridates_VI_of_Pontus" title="Mithridates VI of Pontus">Mithridates VI of Pontus</a> ending the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Athens_and_Piraeus_(87%E2%80%9386_BC)" title="Siege of Athens and Piraeus (87–86 BC)">Siege of Athens and Piraeus</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/293" title="293">293</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Diocletian" title="Diocletian">Diocletian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maximian" title="Maximian">Maximian</a> appoint <a href="/wiki/Constantius_Chlorus" title="Constantius Chlorus">Constantius Chlorus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Galerius" title="Galerius">Galerius</a> as <a href="/wiki/Caesar_(title)" title="Caesar (title)">Caesars</a>. This is considered the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Tetrarchy" title="Tetrarchy">Tetrarchy</a>, known as the <i>Quattuor Principes Mundi</i> ("Four Rulers of the World").</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/317" title="317">317</a> – <a href="/wiki/Crispus" title="Crispus">Crispus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Constantine_II_(emperor)" title="Constantine II (emperor)">Constantine II</a>, sons of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Emperor" title="Roman Emperor">Roman Emperor</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constantine_I" title="Constantine I">Constantine I</a>, and Licinius Iunior, son of Emperor <a href="/wiki/Licinius" title="Licinius">Licinius</a>, are made <i><a href="/wiki/Caesar_(title)" title="Caesar (title)">Caesares</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/350" title="350">350</a> – <a href="/wiki/Vetranio" title="Vetranio">Vetranio</a> is asked by <a href="/wiki/Constantina" title="Constantina">Constantina</a>, sister of <a href="/wiki/Constantius_II" title="Constantius II">Constantius II</a>, to proclaim himself Caesar.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/834" title="834">834</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Louis_the_Pious" title="Louis the Pious">Louis the Pious</a> is restored as sole ruler of the <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Frankish Empire</a>. After his re-accession to the throne, his eldest son <a href="/wiki/Lothair_I" title="Lothair I">Lothair I</a> flees to <a href="/wiki/Burgundy" title="Burgundy">Burgundy</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1457" title="1457">1457</a> – The <i><a href="/wiki/Unitas_Fratrum" title="Unitas Fratrum">Unitas Fratrum</a></i> is established in the village of Kunvald, on the Bohemian-Moravian borderland. It is to date the second oldest Protestant denomination.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1476" title="1476">1476</a> – Forces of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs" title="Catholic Monarchs">Catholic Monarchs</a> engage the combined Portuguese-Castilian armies of <a href="/wiki/Afonso_V_of_Portugal" title="Afonso V of Portugal">Afonso V</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_II_of_Portugal" title="John II of Portugal">Prince John</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Toro" title="Battle of Toro">Battle of Toro</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1562" title="1562">1562</a> – Twenty-three <a href="/wiki/Huguenot" title="Huguenot">Huguenots</a> are massacred by <a href="/wiki/Catholicism" title="Catholicism">Catholics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wassy" title="Wassy">Wassy</a>, France, marking the start of the <a href="/wiki/French_Wars_of_Religion" title="French Wars of Religion">French Wars of Religion</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1565" title="1565">1565</a> – The city of <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a> is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1628" title="1628">1628</a> – Writs issued in February by <a href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_England" title="Charles I of England">Charles I of England</a> mandate that every county in England (not just seaport towns) pay <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ship_tax" title="Ship tax">ship tax</a> by this date.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1633" title="1633">1633</a> – <a href="/wiki/Samuel_de_Champlain" title="Samuel de Champlain">Samuel de Champlain</a> reclaims his role as commander of <a href="/wiki/New_France" title="New France">New France</a> on behalf of <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_Richelieu" title="Cardinal Richelieu">Cardinal Richelieu</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1642" title="1642">1642</a> – Georgeana, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> (now known as <a href="/wiki/York,_Maine" title="York, Maine">York, Maine</a>), becomes the first incorporated city in the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1692" title="1692">1692</a> – <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Good" title="Sarah Good">Sarah Good</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Osborne" title="Sarah Osborne">Sarah Osborne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tituba" title="Tituba">Tituba</a> are brought before local magistrates in <a href="/wiki/Danvers,_Massachusetts" title="Danvers, Massachusetts">Salem Village, Massachusetts</a>, beginning what would become known as the <a href="/wiki/Salem_witch_trials" title="Salem witch trials">Salem witch trials</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1700" title="1700">1700</a> – Sweden introduces its own <a href="/wiki/Swedish_calendar" title="Swedish calendar">Swedish calendar</a>, in an attempt to gradually merge into the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>, reverts to the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a> on this date in <a href="/wiki/1712" title="1712">1712</a>, and introduces the Gregorian calendar on this date in <a href="/wiki/1753" title="1753">1753</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1713" title="1713">1713</a> – The siege and destruction of <a href="/wiki/Fort_Neoheroka" title="Fort Neoheroka">Fort Neoheroka</a> begins during the <a href="/wiki/Tuscarora_War" title="Tuscarora War">Tuscarora War</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, effectively opening up the colony's interior to European colonization.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1781" title="1781">1781</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Continental_Congress" title="Continental Congress">Continental Congress</a> adopts the <a href="/wiki/Articles_of_Confederation" title="Articles of Confederation">Articles of Confederation</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1790" title="1790">1790</a> – The first <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States census</a> is authorized.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1793" title="1793">1793</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_Revolutionary_War" title="French Revolutionary War">French Revolutionary War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aldenhoven_(1793)" title="Battle of Aldenhoven (1793)">Battle of Aldenhoven</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Flanders_Campaign" title="Flanders Campaign">Flanders Campaign</a>.</li>
