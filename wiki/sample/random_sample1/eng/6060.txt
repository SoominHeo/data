<p><b>Barbara Crampton</b> (born December 27, 1958) is an American actress. She made her screen debut on the daytime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Days_of_Our_Lives" title="Days of Our Lives">Days of Our Lives</a></i> (1983), and her film debut in 1984's <i><a href="/wiki/Body_Double" title="Body Double">Body Double</a></i>. She became known as a "<a href="/wiki/Scream_queen" title="Scream queen">scream queen</a>" after a series of roles in <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror</a> films, beginning with the horror comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Re-Animator" title="Re-Animator">Re-Animator</a></i> (1985).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Other films Crampton starred in include <i><a href="/wiki/Chopping_Mall" title="Chopping Mall">Chopping Mall</a></i> (1986), <i><a href="/wiki/From_Beyond_(film)" title="From Beyond (film)">From Beyond</a></i> (1986), <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_Freak" title="Castle Freak">Castle Freak</a></i> (1995), <i><a href="/wiki/You%27re_Next" title="You're Next">You're Next</a></i> (2011), and <i><a href="/wiki/We_Are_Still_Here" title="We Are Still Here">We Are Still Here</a></i> (2015). She is also known for playing <a href="/wiki/Leanna_Love" title="Leanna Love">Leanna Love</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i> from 1987 to 1993.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Crampton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Crampton was born in <a href="/wiki/Levittown,_New_York" title="Levittown, New York">Levittown</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long_Island,_New_York" title="Long Island, New York">Long Island, New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She was raised Catholic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Crampton grew up in <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>, and spent summers while growing up traveling the country with the <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">carnival</a>, as her father was a <a href="/wiki/Carny" title="Carny">carny</a>. She started acting in school plays when she was in seventh grade and went on to study acting in high school. She received a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Bachelor of Arts</a> degree in Theater Arts from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Castleton_State_College" title="Castleton State College">Castleton State College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>. After graduation, Barbara made a brief stop in New York, where she appeared as <a href="/wiki/Cordelia" title="Cordelia">Cordelia</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/King_Lear" title="King Lear">King Lear</a></i> for the American Theater of Actors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torontostar_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-torontostar-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was a Theater Arts Major at Castleton State College from 1976 to 1981.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Crampton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From New York, Crampton moved to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> where she went on to star in the cult horror features <i><a href="/wiki/Re-Animator" title="Re-Animator">Re-Animator</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/From_Beyond_(film)" title="From Beyond (film)">From Beyond</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_Freak" title="Castle Freak">Castle Freak</a></i>. She also has feature roles in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Puppet_Master_(film)" title="Puppet Master (film)">Puppet Master</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Fraternity_Vacation" title="Fraternity Vacation">Fraternity Vacation</a></i>.</p>
<p>She made her television debut on the daytime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Days_of_Our_Lives" title="Days of Our Lives">Days of Our Lives</a></i>, where she played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trista_Evans_Bradford" title="Trista Evans Bradford">Trista Evans Bradford</a>. She went on to play <a href="/wiki/Leanna_Love" title="Leanna Love">Leanna Love</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i> from 1987 to 1993, which earned her a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soap_Opera_Digest_Award" title="Soap Opera Digest Award">Soap Opera Digest Award</a>. She returned to the role from 1998 to 2002 and again in 2006–2007. She also played <a href="/wiki/Mindy_Lewis" title="Mindy Lewis">Mindy Lewis</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Guiding_Light" title="Guiding Light">Guiding Light</a></i> from 1993 to 1995 and left when her contract expired and when she got engaged to L.A.-based actor and director Kristoffer Tabori in April 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sloan_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-sloan-7">[7]</a></sup> By September of the same year, their engagement was called off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sloan2_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-sloan2-8">[8]</a></sup> She soon joined the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bold_and_the_Beautiful" title="The Bold and the Beautiful">The Bold and the Beautiful</a></i> playing <a href="/wiki/Forrester_family" title="Forrester family">Maggie Forrester</a> from 1995 to 1998.</p>
<p>Crampton was a special guest at Creation Entertainment's Weekend of Horror 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> She had a supporting role in the 2013 horror slasher film <i><a href="/wiki/You%27re_Next" title="You're Next">You're Next</a></i> and played the leading role Anne Sacchetti in <i><a href="/wiki/We_Are_Still_Here" title="We Are Still Here">We Are Still Here</a></i> (2015),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> co-starring <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Marie_(actress)" title="Lisa Marie (actress)">Lisa Marie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Larry_Fessenden" title="Larry Fessenden">Larry Fessenden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Both films received positive reviews from critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Barbara next appears in <a href="/wiki/Abner_Pastoll" title="Abner Pastoll">Abner Pastoll</a>'s "taut Euro thriller" <i><a href="/wiki/Road_Games_(film)" title="Road Games (film)">Road Games</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> in which she speaks both French and English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2015 she starred along with <a href="/wiki/Robert_Englund" title="Robert Englund">Robert Englund</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Trejo" title="Danny Trejo">Danny Trejo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kane_Hodder" title="Kane Hodder">Kane Hodder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Moseley" title="Bill Moseley">Bill Moseley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Berryman" title="Michael Berryman">Michael Berryman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doug_Bradley" title="Doug Bradley">Doug Bradley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gunnar_Hansen" title="Gunnar Hansen">Gunnar Hansen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Foree" title="Ken Foree">Ken Foree</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dee_Wallace" title="Dee Wallace">Dee Wallace</a>, in the Harrison Smith horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Death_House" title="Death House">Death House</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>

<p>On October 1, 1988, she married director of photography David Boyd; they divorced in 1990. In December 1986, Crampton appeared in a nude pictorial in <i><a href="/wiki/Playboy" title="Playboy">Playboy</a></i> magazine titled "Simply Beastly. Behind every successful monster, there's a woman." Crampton lives outside of San Francisco with her husband, Robert Bleckman, and their two children.</p>
