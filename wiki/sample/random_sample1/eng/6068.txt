<p><b>Bas Verwijlen</b> (born October 1, 1983 in <a href="/wiki/Oss" title="Oss">Oss</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Fencing" title="Fencing">fencer</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He started fencing when he was five years old at fencing club Zaal Verwijlen in Oss, owned by his father <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Roel_Verwijlen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Roel Verwijlen (page does not exist)">Roel Verwijlen</a>, who is also the Dutch national coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> Until he was twelve years old, he fenced in two different weapons, but he has since chosen to focus on the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89p%C3%A9e" title="Épée">épée</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> He became part of the Dutch national team and took part in the <a href="/wiki/Universiade" title="Universiade">Universiade</a> (17th in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, 2001), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Youth_Olympics" title="Youth Olympics">Youth Olympics</a>, Dutch national championships, but also European and World Championships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> As a junior he won eleven national titles, he became third at the World Championships under-16 in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and represented the Netherlands at every European and World Championship since 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> He won two World Cups for Juniors, a performance never achieved by another fencer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was time for his senior career and also managed to achieve World class performances there. At the 2005 World Championships in <a href="/wiki/Leipzig" title="Leipzig">Leipzig</a> he won the bronze medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> At the European Championships 2005 in <a href="/wiki/Zalaegerszeg" title="Zalaegerszeg">Zalaegerszeg</a> he and his Dutch team mates came sixth in the nations tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> He reached the final and became second at the 2006 European Championships in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Izmir" title="Izmir">Izmir</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> After the Grand Prix meeting in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> in 2008 he was secure of qualification for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> in Beijing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> In the preparations for this tournament he won the World Cup meeting in <a href="/wiki/Cali" title="Cali">Cali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> in June and he also won the Dutch national title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-bio-1">[1]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> he reached the last eight, losing to the eventual gold medalist, <a href="/wiki/Matteo_Tagliariol" title="Matteo Tagliariol">Matteo Tagliariol</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2011_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2011 European Fencing Championships">2011 European Championships</a>, he finished in 2nd place, a result he repeated at the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2011 World Fencing Championships">2011 World Championships</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>, finishing in 13th place, despite a knee injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
