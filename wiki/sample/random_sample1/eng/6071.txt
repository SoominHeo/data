<p><b>Waasland-Beveren</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgian</a> association football club located in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Beveren" title="Beveren">Beveren</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Flanders" title="East Flanders">East Flanders</a>. They currently play in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Pro_League" title="Belgian Pro League">Belgian Pro League</a>. The club was known as <b>K.V. Red Star Waasland</b> from 2002 until 2010, when they moved to the nearby stadium of defunct club <a href="/wiki/K._Sint-Niklase_S.K.E." title="K. Sint-Niklase S.K.E.">Sint-Niklase</a> and merged with <a href="/wiki/K.S.K._Beveren" title="K.S.K. Beveren">K.S.K. Beveren</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Waasland" title="Waasland">Waasland</a> refers to the region where the towns of Haasdonk (a neighbourhood in <a href="/wiki/Beveren" title="Beveren">Beveren</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Sint-Niklaas" title="Sint-Niklaas">Sint-Niklaas</a> are located. In the summer of 2010, the club merged with neighbour <a href="/wiki/K.S.K._Beveren" title="K.S.K. Beveren">KSK Beveren</a> to form Waasland-Beveren. KSK Beveren, a former Belgian champion and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Pro_League" title="Belgian Pro League">first division</a> regular, had just finished at the last place in the second division and was struggling with financial problems. The club kept the history and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matricule" title="Matricule">matricule number</a> of KV Red Star Waasland but moved from Sint-Niklaas to Beveren to play in a bigger stadium.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waasland-Beveren&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded in 1936 as <b>Red Star</b> and registered to an amateur football association. In 1944, they joined the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Belgian_Football_Association" title="Royal Belgian Football Association">Royal Belgian Football Association</a> and started playing in the lowest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Provincial_leagues" title="Belgian Provincial leagues">provincial league</a> of West Flanders. Red Star Haasdonk first reached national football in <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_in_Belgian_football" title="2000â01 in Belgian football">2000â01</a>, and they finished first of the series in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Promotion" title="Belgian Promotion">Promotion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> They also reached the <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Belgian_Cup" title="2000â01 Belgian Cup">2000â01 Belgian Cup</a> round of 32, losing to first division club <a href="/wiki/K.A.A._Gent" title="K.A.A. Gent">Gent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> After their first season in the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Third_Division" title="Belgian Third Division">third division</a>, they moved to the bigger stadium of defunct club Sint-Niklase SKE, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Puyenbekestadion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Puyenbekestadion (page does not exist)">Puyenbekestadion</a>, changing their name to KV Red Star Waasland.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_in_Belgian_football" title="2003â04 in Belgian football">2003â04</a>, they finished first in their series of the third division and promoted for the first time to the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Second_Division" title="Belgian Second Division">second division</a>. In their first season at the second-highest level of football, Red Star Waasland finished 5th and qualified for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Second_Division_Final_Round" title="Belgian Second Division Final Round">final round</a>. The next season, they finished 4th, their best result as of 2010 but did not qualify for the final round. They reached again the round of 32 of the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Belgian_Cup" title="2005â06 Belgian Cup">2005â06 Belgian Cup</a>, losing to neighbours KSK Beveren. In the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Belgian_Cup" title="2007â08 Belgian Cup">2007â08 Belgian Cup</a>, Red Star Waasland reached the round of 16, their best cup result, by eliminating <a href="/wiki/K.S.C._Lokeren_Oost-Vlaanderen" title="K.S.C. Lokeren Oost-Vlaanderen">Lokeren</a>. They lost to <a href="/wiki/R.S.C._Anderlecht" title="R.S.C. Anderlecht">Anderlecht</a> in the round of 16. The following season, they finished 4th again in the second division. In the summer of 2010 Red Star Waasland changed its name to Waasland-Beveren and moved to the bigger stadium of Beveren, the <a href="/wiki/Freethiel_Stadion" title="Freethiel Stadion">Freethiel Stadion</a>.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_in_Belgian_football" title="2011â12 in Belgian football">2011â12</a>, they finished second in the Belgian second tier league and promoted to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_Pro_League" title="Belgian Pro League">Belgian Pro League</a> for the 2012â13 season.</p>
