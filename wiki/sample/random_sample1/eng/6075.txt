<p><b>Bao Xishun</b> (also known as <b>Xi Shun</b>; born 1951) is a <a href="/wiki/Herder" title="Herder">herdsman</a> from <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, recognized by <i><a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a></i> as one of the world's tallest living men. On September 17, 2009, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey's</a> <a href="/wiki/Sultan_K%C3%B6sen" title="Sultan Kösen">Sultan Kösen</a> overtook Bao Xishun as the tallest living man in the world, when he was measured by Guinness World Records, standing 2.46 metres (8 ft 1 in) tall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bao_Xishun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bao Xishun worked at the <a href="/wiki/Chifeng" title="Chifeng">Chifeng</a> City local hospital for several months. Bao suffers from <a href="/wiki/Rheumatism" title="Rheumatism">rheumatism</a>. Bao Xishun claims to have been of normal height until he was sixteen years old. Bao served in the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> for three years. He later returned to Inner Mongolia to live with his mother. He soon became known as the world's tallest living man. In 2005 he became the worlds tallest man. In December 2006, Bao Xishun was asked by veterinarians to assist them in removing shards of plastic from the <a href="/wiki/Stomach" title="Stomach">stomachs</a> of two <a href="/wiki/Dolphin" title="Dolphin">dolphins</a>. The dolphins had accidentally swallowed the shards, which had settled in their stomachs and caused a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loss_of_appetite" title="Loss of appetite">loss of appetite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Depression_(mood)" title="Depression (mood)">depression</a>. Veterinarians had failed to remove them, so Bao Xishun used his 1.06-metre long arms to reach into the dolphins' stomachs and remove the plastic manually.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bao Xishun married saleswoman Xia Shujuan on March 24, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NZ_Herald_10431350_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-NZ_Herald_10431350-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The Mongolian ceremony took place on July 12, 2007. Bao's son was born at a hospital in <a href="/wiki/Zunhua" title="Zunhua">Zunhua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebei" title="Hebei">Hebei</a> province October 2, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Bao appeared on the eleventh episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Race_16" title="The Amazing Race 16">The Amazing Race 16</a></i> as the pitstop greeter. The episode aired on May 2, 2010. His second child was born on January 5 2017.</p>
