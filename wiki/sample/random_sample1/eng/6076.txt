<p><b>Baoying County</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">宝应县</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">寶應縣</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Bǎoyīng Xiàn</span></i>) is under the administration of <a href="/wiki/Yangzhou" title="Yangzhou">Yangzhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jiangsu" title="Jiangsu">Jiangsu</a> province, China. It has a population of 919,900 (2004) and a land area of 1,483 square kilometres (573 sq mi). The northernmost <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County-level_division" title="County-level division">county-level division</a> of Yangzhou City, it borders the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prefecture-level_cities" title="Prefecture-level cities">prefecture-level cities</a> of <a href="/wiki/Yancheng" title="Yancheng">Yancheng</a> to the east and <a href="/wiki/Huai%27an" title="Huai'an">Huai'an</a> to the north and west.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_sub-districts">Administrative sub-districts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baoying_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Administrative sub-districts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baoying administers a total of fourteen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Town_(China)" title="Town (China)">towns</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baoying_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the Southern Dynasties period, Baoying was Anyi County of Yangping Commandery. In Tang, it was renamed Baoying to reflect 13 jade treasures acquired there by a nun named Zhenru. Its first local gazetteer was compiled in 1530, an outline in four chapters, followed by a 10-chapter gazetteer of 1567-70. The county suffered from flooding and from piracy in Ming times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
