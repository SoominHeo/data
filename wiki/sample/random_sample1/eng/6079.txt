<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Model_category_structure_of_the_Bousfield_localization">Model category structure of the Bousfield localization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bousfield_localization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Model category structure of the Bousfield localization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Given a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_(mathematics)" title="Class (mathematics)">class</a> <i>C</i> of morphisms in a model category <i>M</i> the left Bousfield localization is a new model structure on the same category as before. Its equivalences, cofibrations and fibrations, respectively, are</p>
<ul>
<li>the <i>C</i>-local equivalences</li>
<li>the original cofibrations of <i>M</i></li>
</ul>
<p>and (necessarily, since cofibrations and weak equivalences determine the fibrations)</p>
<ul>
<li>the maps having the right <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lifting_property" title="Lifting property">lifting property</a> with respect to the cofibrations in <i>M</i> which are also <i>C</i>-local equivalences.</li>

<p>If <i>M</i> is a simplicial model category (such as, say, simplicial sets or topological spaces), then "map" above can be taken to be the derived simplicial mapping space of <i>M</i>.</p>
<p>This description does not make any claim about the existence of this model structure, for which see below.</p>
<p>Dually, there is a notion of <i>right Bousfield localization</i>, whose definition is obtained by replacing cofibrations by fibrations (and reversing directions of all arrows).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Existence">Existence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bousfield_localization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Existence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The left Bousfield localization model structure, as described above, is known to exist in various situations, provided that <i>C</i> is a set:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>M</i> is left proper (i.e., the pushout of a weak equivalence along a cofibration is again a weak equivalence) and combinatorial</li>
<li><i>M</i> is left proper and cellular.</li>
</ul>
<p>Combinatoriality and cellularity of a model category guarantee, in particular, a strong control over the cofibrations of <i>M</i>.</p>
<p>Similarly, the right Bousfield localization exists if <i>M</i> is right proper and cellular or combinatorial and C is a set.</p>

<p>The Bousfield localization is the appropriate analogous notion for model categories, keeping in mind that isomorphisms in ordinary category theory are replaced by weak equivalences. <li>There is a <a href="/wiki/Quillen_adjunction" title="Quillen adjunction">left Quillen</a> functor <span><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bousfield_localization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Localization_and_completion_of_a_spectrum">Localization and completion of a spectrum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bousfield_localization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Localization and completion of a spectrum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Localization and completion of a spectrum at a prime number <i>p</i> are both examples of Bousfield localization, resulting in a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Local_spectrum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Local spectrum (page does not exist)">local spectrum</a>. For example, localizing the <a href="/wiki/Sphere_spectrum" title="Sphere spectrum">sphere spectrum</a> <i>S</i> at <i>p</i>, one obtains a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Local_sphere" title="Local sphere">local sphere</a> <span><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;"><math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">
<semantics>
<mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">
<mstyle displaystyle="true" scriptlevel="0">
<msub>
<mi>S</mi>

<mi>p</mi>
<mo stretchy="false">)</mo>
</mrow>
</msub>
</mstyle>
</mrow>
<annotation encoding="application/x-tex">{\displaystyle S_{(p)}}</annotation>
</semantics>
</math></span><img alt="S_{{(p)}}" aria-hidden="true" class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline" src="https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/6a86fc8449a2a863850bfd214b81133aa17308ad" style="vertical-align: -1.171ex; width:3.796ex; height:3.009ex;"/></span>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stable_model_structure_on_spectra">Stable model structure on spectra</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bousfield_localization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Stable model structure on spectra">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stable_homotopy_category" title="Stable homotopy category">stable homotopy category</a> is the homotopy category (in the sense of model categories) of spectra, endowed with the stable model structure. The stable model structure is obtained as a left Bousfield localization of the level (or projective) model structure on spectra, whose weak equivalences (fibrations) are those maps which are weak equivalences (fibrations, respectively) in all levels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Morita_model_structure_on_dg_categories">Morita model structure on dg categories</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bousfield_localization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Morita model structure on dg categories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Morita model structure on the category of small dg categories is Bousfield localization of the standard model structure (the one for which the weak equivalences are the quasi-equivalences).</p>
