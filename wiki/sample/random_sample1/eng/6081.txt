<p><b>Baltazar Maria de Morais Júnior</b> (born 17 July 1959), known simply as <b>Baltazar</b>, is a Brazilian retired <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.</p>
<p>During an 18-year professional career he played, other than in his country, in Spain, Portugal, France and Japan, winning several individual scoring honours. He appeared with the <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazilian national team</a> that won the <a href="/wiki/1989_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1989 Copa América">1989 Copa América</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baltazar_Maria_de_Morais_J%C3%BAnior&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Goi%C3%A2nia" title="Goiânia">Goiânia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goi%C3%A1s" title="Goiás">Goiás</a>, Baltazar started playing with hometown club <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Clube_Goianiense" title="Atlético Clube Goianiense">Atlético Clube Goianiense</a>. In 1979 he signed for <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%AAmio_Foot-Ball_Porto_Alegrense" title="Grêmio Foot-Ball Porto Alegrense">Grêmio Foot-Ball Porto Alegrense</a>, going on to score in double digits during his entire four-season spell a recording a best of 14 in 1980 while being an instrumental attacking unit in the team's back-to-back <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Ga%C3%BAcho" title="Campeonato Gaúcho">Rio Grande do Sul State League</a> conquests; in the <a href="/wiki/1981_Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="1981 Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">1981</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Série A</a> final against <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_FC" title="São Paulo FC">São Paulo FC</a>, after missing a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_kick_(association_football)" title="Penalty kick (association football)">penalty kick</a> in the first leg (2–1 home win), he scored the only goal in the second game for a first-ever national championship conquest.</p>
<p>In the following four years, Baltazar played for <a href="/wiki/Sociedade_Esportiva_Palmeiras" title="Sociedade Esportiva Palmeiras">Sociedade Esportiva Palmeiras</a> (two spells), <a href="/wiki/Clube_de_Regatas_do_Flamengo" title="Clube de Regatas do Flamengo">Clube de Regatas do Flamengo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Botafogo_de_Futebol_e_Regatas" title="Botafogo de Futebol e Regatas">Botafogo de Futebol e Regatas</a>, netting 13 times for the second side in another <a href="/wiki/1983_Campeonato_Brasileiro_S%C3%A9rie_A" title="1983 Campeonato Brasileiro Série A">Brazilian championship conquest</a>. Aged 26 he had his first abroad experience, <a href="/wiki/1985%E2%80%9386_La_Liga" title="1985–86 La Liga">being relegated</a> from the Spanish <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> with <a href="/wiki/Celta_de_Vigo" title="Celta de Vigo">Celta de Vigo</a>.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/1986%E2%80%9387_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1986–87 Segunda División">1986–87</a>, Baltazar propelled the <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicians</a> back into the top level by scoring a career-best 34 goals, also a best-ever in the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">second division</a>. In a game in December, he accidentally collided with <a href="/wiki/CD_M%C3%A1laga" title="CD Málaga">CD Málaga</a> goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Antonio_Gallardo" title="José Antonio Gallardo">José Antonio Gallardo</a> who died days later from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cerebral_haemorrhage" title="Cerebral haemorrhage">cerebral haemorrhage</a>; he mourned the death which some had blamed him for.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Marca_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Marca-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Baltazar only found the net on six occasions in <a href="/wiki/1987%E2%80%9388_La_Liga" title="1987–88 La Liga">the following season</a>, but the club retained its league status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He subsequently stayed in the country and joined <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Madrid" title="Atlético Madrid">Atlético Madrid</a>, scoring 35 goals in 36 contests in <a href="/wiki/1988%E2%80%9389_La_Liga" title="1988–89 La Liga">his first season</a> – his second <a href="/wiki/Pichichi_Trophy" title="Pichichi Trophy">Pichichi</a> in three years – and adding 18 in <a href="/wiki/1989%E2%80%9390_La_Liga" title="1989–90 La Liga">the following</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> however, after the emergence of younger <a href="/wiki/Manolo_(footballer)" title="Manolo (footballer)">Manolo</a>, the 31-year-old was deemed surplus to requirements by manager <a href="/wiki/Tomislav_Ivi%C4%87" title="Tomislav Ivić">Tomislav Ivić</a> and, in November 1990, signed for <a href="/wiki/FC_Porto" title="FC Porto">FC Porto</a> in Portugal, being used almost exclusively as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> during <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1990%E2%80%9391_Primeira_Liga" title="1990–91 Primeira Liga">his only season</a>.</p>
<p>Until his retirement at the age of 37 in 1996, Baltazar played for <a href="/wiki/Stade_Rennais_F.C." title="Stade Rennais F.C.">Stade Rennais FC</a> (France), <a href="/wiki/Goi%C3%A1s_Esporte_Clube" title="Goiás Esporte Clube">Goiás Esporte Clube</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kyoto_Sanga_FC" title="Kyoto Sanga FC">Kyoto Purple Sanga</a> (Japan).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baltazar_Maria_de_Morais_J%C3%BAnior&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baltazar played for <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1989_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica" title="1989 Copa América">1989 Copa América</a> which was held on home soil, appearing in three group stage matches for the eventual winners (including the 0–0 against <a href="/wiki/Colombia_national_football_team" title="Colombia national football team">Colombia</a> as a starter).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> However, during nearly one full decade, he only gained a total of six <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a>, scoring two goals.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-retirement">Post-retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baltazar_Maria_de_Morais_J%C3%BAnior&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Highly <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religious</a>, Baltazar was nicknamed <i>O Artilheiro de Deus</i> (God's striker). He became a <a href="/wiki/Minister_(Christianity)" title="Minister (Christianity)">minister</a> after retiring from football, settling in his hometown and fathering two children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
