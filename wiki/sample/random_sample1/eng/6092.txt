<p><b>Padma Choling</b> (<a href="/wiki/Tibetan_alphabet" title="Tibetan alphabet">Tibetan</a>: <span lang="bo" style="font-family: 'Qomolangma-Dunhuang', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sarchen', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sarchung', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Suring', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sutung', 'Qomolangma-Title', 'Qomolangma-Subtitle', 'Qomolangma-Woodblock', 'DDC Uchen', Kailash, Jomolhari, 'TCRC Youtso Unicode', 'Tibetan Machine Uni', 'Noto Sans Tibetan', 'Microsoft Himalaya'; font-size:1.25em; vertical-align:top; word-wrap: break-word;" xml:lang="bo">པདྨ་འཕྲིན་ལས་</span>, <a href="/wiki/Wylie_transliteration" title="Wylie transliteration">Wylie</a>: <i>padma 'phrin-las</i>, <a href="/wiki/Standard_Tibetan" title="Standard Tibetan">Lhasa dialect</a> <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">IPA</a>: <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[pɛ́mɑ̀ ʈʰĩ́lɪ᷈ː]</span>; alternatively Pema Thinley, Pelma Chiley, Baima Chilin;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mil_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mil-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">白玛赤林</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Báimă Chìlín</span></i>; born October 1952) is a politician. He was the eighth and Chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region" title="Tibet Autonomous Region">Tibet Autonomous Region</a> (TAR), <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, but in January 2013, was replaced by his deputy <a href="/wiki/Losang_Jamcan" title="Losang Jamcan">Losang Jamcan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chinadaily_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-chinadaily-3">[3]</a></sup> Later he served as the Tibet Autonomous Region People's Congress. As Chairman of TAR, Choling was the "most senior <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_people" title="Tibetan people">ethnic Tibetan</a> in the regional government",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-man_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-man-4">[4]</a></sup> though he was subordinate to the TAR <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communist_Party_Chief" title="Communist Party Chief">Communist Party Chief</a> <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Qingli" title="Zhang Qingli">Zhang Qingli</a>, and later his successor <a href="/wiki/Chen_Quanguo" title="Chen Quanguo">Chen Quanguo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mil_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mil-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Padma_Choling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Padma Choling was born in 1952 in a farming family in <a href="/wiki/D%C3%AAngq%C3%AAn_County" title="Dêngqên County">Dêngqên County</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamdo_Prefecture" title="Chamdo Prefecture">Chamdo Prefecture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tib_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tib-5">[5]</a></sup> He joined the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People's Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qinghai_Province" title="Qinghai Province">Qinghai Province</a> at 17, and served in the army for seventeen years. In the army he played basketball and mastered the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese language</a>. He joined the <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> in October 1970. He was an official in the Tibetan regional government since December 1969,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> working in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xigaz%C3%AA_Prefecture" title="Xigazê Prefecture">Xigaze</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Lhasa" title="Lhasa">Lhasa</a>, rising to the vice-chairmanship of the TAR in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tib_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-tib-5">[5]</a></sup> After his anodyne handling of an earthquake in <a href="/wiki/Damxung_County" title="Damxung County">Damxung County</a> outside Lhasa, he was elected the chairman of the Tibet Autonomous Region in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lschange_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-lschange-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chairmanship">Chairmanship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Padma_Choling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chairmanship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Personally, he has been described as "stern, but... amiable" in contrast to his "soft-spoken" predecessor <a href="/wiki/Qiangba_Puncog" title="Qiangba Puncog">Qiangba Puncog</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lschange_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-lschange-7">[7]</a></sup> In addition to stability and ethnic harmony, he has set a goal of 12 percent <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">GDP</a> growth and a <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">¥</a>4,000 per capita GDP for farmers and herders in the Tibet Autonomous Region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> To bring this about, he has signed an agreement with the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Commerce_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Ministry of Commerce of the People's Republic of China">Ministry of Commerce</a> of the Central Government to allow it to promote trade with <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Padma is of the opinion that there is no "issue of Tibet" that the Dalai Lama says, and questions the Lama's ability to judge the situation in Tibet since he has not been in Tibet since 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lash_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-lash-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>On the issue of religion, Padma has said that he and the <a href="/wiki/Karmapa" title="Karmapa">Karmapa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ogyen_Trinley_Dorje" title="Ogyen Trinley Dorje">Ogyen Trinley Dorje</a> are "mates from the same hometown".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-man_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-man-4">[4]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2010_National_People%27s_Congress" title="2010 National People's Congress">2010 National People's Congress</a>, he announced that the choosing of the 15th Dalai Lama would abide by the "requirements of <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Buddhism" title="Tibetan Buddhism">Tibetan Buddhist</a> tradition", including approval by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="Government of the People's Republic of China">government</a>, instead of being the choice of the 14th Dalai Lama. Already, he has dismissed the Dalai Lama's choice for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/11th_Panchen_Lama" title="11th Panchen Lama">11th Panchen Lama</a> as "invalid".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He has included monks and nuns over the age of 60 into the Chinese state <a href="/wiki/Social_security" title="Social security">social security</a>, and works with legislation delivering roads, electricity, and water to <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monasteries</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lash_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-lash-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Moreover, Pema Thinley, said at a Press Conference that <a href="/wiki/Gedhun_Choekyi_Nyima" title="Gedhun Choekyi Nyima">Gedun Choki Nyima</a> - the child recognized by the Dalai Lama as the reincarnation of the 10th <a href="/wiki/Panchen_Lama" title="Panchen Lama">Panchen Lama</a> - is an ordinary person, and that he and his parents wanted to stay away from public attention for their own safety.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Padma is a member of the <a href="/wiki/18th_Central_Committee_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" title="18th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China">18th Central Committee of the Communist Party of China</a>.</p>
