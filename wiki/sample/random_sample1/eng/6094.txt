<p><b>Bayburt</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Anatolia_Region_(statistical)" title="Northeast Anatolia Region (statistical)">northeast Turkey</a> lying on the <a href="/wiki/%C3%87oruh_River" title="Çoruh River">Çoruh River</a> and is the provincial capital of <a href="/wiki/Bayburt_Province" title="Bayburt Province">Bayburt Province</a>.</p>
<p>Bayburt was once an important center on the ancient <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a>. It was visited by <a href="/wiki/Marco_Polo" title="Marco Polo">Marco Polo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evliya_Celebi" title="Evliya Celebi">Evliya Çelebi</a>. Remains of its medieval castle still exist. There are several historical mosques, <a href="/wiki/Turkish_bath" title="Turkish bath">Turkish baths</a>, and tombs in the city. There are also ancient historical sites such as the Çatalçeşme Underground Complex and natural wonders like the Sirakayalar Waterfall in the other parts of the province.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name of the town was formerly written in English as <b>Baiburt</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271878_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271878-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911-4">[4]</a></sup> It was known as <b>Paipert</b> under the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantines</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The name derives from the medieval <a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a> <i>Baydbert</i> (<span lang="hy">Բայտբերդ</span>), which derives from its ancient name <i>Ambatavan</i> (<span lang="hy">Ամբատավան</span>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bayburt strattles the <a href="/wiki/%C3%87oruh_River" title="Çoruh River">Çoruh</a> amid an open and fertile <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateau</a> on the route between <a href="/wiki/Trabzon" title="Trabzon">Trabzon</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Erzerum" title="Erzerum">Erzurum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911-4">[4]</a></sup> It was originally founded by the <a href="/wiki/Hayasa-Azzi" title="Hayasa-Azzi">Azzi</a> people.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It was subsequently settled or conquered by the <a href="/wiki/Cimmerians" title="Cimmerians">Cimmerians</a> in the <span class="nowrap">8th century <span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">BC</span></span>,</span> the <a href="/wiki/Medes" title="Medes">Medes</a> in the <span class="nowrap">7th century <span class="smallcaps"><span style="font-variant: small-caps; text-transform: lowercase;">BC</span></span>,</span> then the <a href="/wiki/Persian_people" title="Persian people">Persians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Pontus" title="Kingdom of Pontus">Pontus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Rome</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantines</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_(Bagratid)" title="Kingdom of Armenia (Bagratid)">Bagratid Armenian Kingdom</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seljuk_Turks" title="Seljuk Turks">Seljuk Turks</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aq_Qoyunlu" title="Aq Qoyunlu">Aq Qoyunlu</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Safavid_Persia" title="Safavid Persia">Safavid Persia</a>, and then the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ottoman_Turks" title="Ottoman Turks">Ottoman Turks</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The town was the site of an <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_(antiquity)" title="Kingdom of Armenia (antiquity)">Armenian</a> fortress in the 1st century and may have been the <b>Baiberdon</b> fortified by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Byzantine_emperors" title="List of Byzantine emperors">emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Justinian_I" title="Justinian I">Justinian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271878_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271878-3">[3]</a></sup> It was a stronghold of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa" title="Republic of Genoa">Genovese</a> in the late Middle Ages<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271878_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271878-3">[3]</a></sup> and prospered in the late 13th and early 14th century because of the commerce between <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Trebizond" title="Empire of Trebizond">Trebizond</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persia" title="Persia">Persia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eastern_Turkey_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eastern_Turkey-5">[5]</a></sup> It contained a mint under the <a href="/wiki/Seljuk_Empire" title="Seljuk Empire">Seljuks</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkhanid_Empire&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ilkhanid Empire (page does not exist)">Ilkhanids</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eastern_Turkey_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Eastern_Turkey-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Under Ottoman rule, the town was the center of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt_Sanjak&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bayburt Sanjak (page does not exist)">Bayburt</a> <a href="/wiki/Sanjak" title="Sanjak">Sanjak</a> in <a href="/wiki/Erzurum_Eyalet" title="Erzurum Eyalet">Erzurum Eyalet</a>. When <a href="/wiki/Erzurum" title="Erzurum">Erzurum</a> was devastated in the early 16th century, Bayburt served for a time as the <i>de facto</i> capital of the province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eastern_Turkey_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Eastern_Turkey-5">[5]</a></sup> The area was raided by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Safavid_Persia" title="Safavid Persia">Safavids</a> in 1553. Bayburt was captured by a <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian</a> army under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Paskevich" title="General Paskevich">General Paskevich</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911-4">[4]</a></sup> and its fortifications thoroughly demolished in 1829.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271878_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271878-3">[3]</a></sup> It was the furthest westward reach of the Russians during that campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911-4">[4]</a></sup> The British traveller <a href="/wiki/William_John_Hamilton" title="William John Hamilton">William John Hamilton</a> commented on the ruins in the 1840s,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Researches_in_Asia_Minor_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Researches_in_Asia_Minor-6">[6]</a></sup> though the population grew to 6000 by the 1870s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271878_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271878-3">[3]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Bazaar" title="Bazaar">bazaar</a>, however, remained poor and the town long lacked industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911-4">[4]</a></sup> Prior to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">First World War</a>, the population of 10,000 was mostly <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turkish</a> with some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armenian_people" title="Armenian people">Armenians</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bayburt has a <a href="/wiki/Continental_climate" title="Continental climate">continental climate</a> with freezing cold winters, the majority of the time snowing heavily. Summers are warm with cool nights. The climate is best described as <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">humid continental</a> by the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen Climate System</a> <i>Dfb</i>, on the border of <i>Dsb</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Most of the precipitation occurs in late spring and early summer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights">Sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nature">Nature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bayburt has many delightful parks and open spaces like ''Aslan dağı Ormanı '' (Lion Mount Forest ) "Gençlik Parkı" (Youth Park), "Şehit Nusret Bahçesi" (Martyr Nusret Gardens) and "Yenişehir Parkı" (New City Park). The city has 535.780m² of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nursery_(horticulture)" title="Nursery (horticulture)">nursery</a> areas, where young plants are raised for the forests of Bayburt. Also there are two caves that visitors can see the interesting natural shapes of the stones. These caves are, "<a class="external text" href="http://www.cimagilmagarasi.com/" rel="nofollow">Çimağıl Mağarası</a>" and "Helva Köyü Buz Mağarası".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Castle">Castle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Castle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:BayburtKalesi.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2304" data-file-width="3072" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/BayburtKalesi.jpg/200px-BayburtKalesi.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/BayburtKalesi.jpg/300px-BayburtKalesi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/BayburtKalesi.jpg/400px-BayburtKalesi.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>

</div>
<p><a class="external text" href="http://www.bayburtkalesi.com/" rel="nofollow">Bayburt Castle</a> stands on the steep rocks north of Bayburt. It was held by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bagratuni_Dynasty" title="Bagratuni Dynasty">Bagratuni Dynasty</a> in the 9th, 10th, and 11th centuries. It was completely rebuilt by the Saltuk ruler Mugis-al-Din Tugrul Sah between 1200 and 1230, as attested by an inscription in the walls of the castle. The massive size of its walls and the quality of its masonry place it amongst the finest of all the castles in Anatolia but for its destruction by the Russians<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eastern_Turkey_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Eastern_Turkey-5">[5]</a></sup> during the early nineteenth century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Rebuilding was done during the Ottoman period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eastern_Turkey_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-Eastern_Turkey-5">[5]</a></sup> The castle was inhabited till the destruction<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eastern_Turkey_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-Eastern_Turkey-5">[5]</a></sup> of 1829.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTE.27.27EB.27.271911-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ayd.C4.B1ntepe_Underground_City"><a class="external text" href="http://www.aydintepeyeraltisehri.com/index.html" rel="nofollow">Aydıntepe Underground City</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aydıntepe Underground City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Located in the away from 45km district of Bayburt, the city consists of galleries, vaulted rooms and wider spaces opened by the main rock, without using any building material within 2-2.5 meters from the surface in the tuff. Vaulted galleries about one meter wide and 2 to 2.5 meters tall are expanding on both sides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Museum">Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baks.C4.B1_Museum"><a class="external text" href="http://en.baksi.org" rel="nofollow">Baksı Museum</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Baksı Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Baksı Museum stands near the Black Sea, 45 km from Bayburt on a hilltop overlooking the Çoruh Valley. Rising in what used to be called Baksı and is now the village of Bayraktar, this unusual museum offers contemporary art and traditional handicrafts side by side under one roof.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city has one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_club" title="Football club">football club</a> currently competing in the Turkish Amateur Football Championship, struggling for promotion to the Turkish Third League, called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburtspor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bayburtspor (page does not exist)">Bayburtspor</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%9Ealcilarspor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Şalcilarspor (page does not exist)">Şalcilarspor</a> played in the Second League between 1986-1988.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bayburt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="external text" href="http://www.bayburt.edu.tr/en" rel="nofollow">Bayburt University</a></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bayburt_University" title="Bayburt University">Bayburt University</a> is located at the center of the city, university has several faculties such as Engineering, Arts and Sciences and Administrative Sciences. There are also several vocational schools.</p>
