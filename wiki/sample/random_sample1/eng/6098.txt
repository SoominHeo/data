<p><b>Maria Anna Victoria of Bavaria</b>, <i>Dauphine of France</i> (Maria Anna Christina Victoria; 28 November 1660 – 20 April 1690) was <a href="/wiki/Dauphine_of_France" title="Dauphine of France">Dauphine of France</a> as spouse of <a href="/wiki/Louis,_Grand_Dauphin" title="Louis, Grand Dauphin">Louis, Grand Dauphin</a>, son and heir of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_XIV" title="Louis XIV">Louis XIV</a>. She was known as the <i>Dauphine Marie Anne Victoire</i> or <i>la Grande Dauphine</i>. The dauphine was a 'pathetic' figure at the court of France, isolated and unappreciated due to the perception that she was dull, unattractive and sickly.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duchess_Maria_Anna_Victoria_of_Bavaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maria Anna was the eldest daughter of <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Maria,_Elector_of_Bavaria" title="Ferdinand Maria, Elector of Bavaria">Ferdinand Maria, Elector of Bavaria</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/Princess_Henriette_Adelaide_of_Savoy" title="Princess Henriette Adelaide of Savoy">Princess Henriette Adelaide of Savoy</a>. Her maternal grandparents were <a href="/wiki/Victor_Amadeus_I,_Duke_of_Savoy" title="Victor Amadeus I, Duke of Savoy">Victor Amadeus I, Duke of Savoy</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christine_Marie_of_France" title="Christine Marie of France">Christine Marie of France</a>, the second daughter of <a href="/wiki/Henry_IV_of_France" title="Henry IV of France">Henry IV of France</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marie_de%27_Medici" title="Marie de' Medici">Marie de' Medici</a>, thus her husband the dauphin was her second cousin.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duchess_Maria_Anna_Victoria_of_Bavaria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in Munich, capital of the Electorate of <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, Maria Anna was betrothed to the dauphin of France in 1668, at the age of eight, and was carefully educated to fulfill that role. Besides her native language of German, she was taught to speak French, Italian and Latin. She was said to have looked forward to the fate of becoming dauphine of France. Maria Anna was very close to her mother, who died in 1676. Her siblings included <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Violante_of_Bavaria" title="Violante of Bavaria">Violante of Bavaria</a>, future wife of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinando_(III)_de%27_Medici" title="Ferdinando (III) de' Medici">Ferdinando de' Medici</a> as well as the future Elector of Bavaria, <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_II_Emanuel,_Elector_of_Bavaria" title="Maximilian II Emanuel, Elector of Bavaria">Maximilian II Emanuel</a>.</p>
<p>Prior to her marriage to the dauphin, there was a proxy ceremony in Munich on 28 January 1680; the couple would meet for the first time on 7 March 1680 in <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2lons-en-Champagne" title="Châlons-en-Champagne">Châlons-sur-Marne</a>. She was the first dauphine of France since <a href="/wiki/Mary,_Queen_of_Scots" title="Mary, Queen of Scots">Mary, Queen of Scots</a> married <a href="/wiki/Francis_II_of_France" title="Francis II of France">Francis II of France</a> in 1558.</p>
<p>Upon her marriage, Maria Anna took on the rank of her husband as a <i><a href="/wiki/Fils_de_France" title="Fils de France">Fille de France</a></i> (Daughter of France); this meant that she was entitled to the style "<a href="/wiki/Royal_Highness" title="Royal Highness">Royal Highness</a>" and the form of address <i>Madame la Dauphine</i>.</p>
<p>When she first arrived in France, Maria Anna made a good impression with her good French. When she entered <a href="/wiki/Strasbourg" title="Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a>, she was addressed in German, but interrupted the greeting by saying, "Gentlemen, I speak French!" The impression of her appearance, however, was not as good, and she was called "terribly ugly". Others said, that although she may not have been beautiful, she did have personal charm.</p>
<p>As soon as she married the dauphin, Maria Anna was the second most important woman at court after her mother-in-law, Queen <a href="/wiki/Maria_Theresa_of_Spain" title="Maria Theresa of Spain">Maria Theresa of Spain</a>. When the queen died in July 1683, Maria Anna ranked as the most prominent female at court and was given the apartments of the late queen. The king expected her to perform the functions of the first lady at court, but her ill health made it very difficult for her to carry out her duties. The king was completely unsympathetic to her situation and accused her falsely of <a href="/wiki/Hypochondriasis" title="Hypochondriasis">hypochondria</a>.</p>
<p>Her husband took mistresses, and she lived an isolated life in her apartments, where she spoke with her friends in German, a language her husband could not understand. She was very close to a fellow German at court, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Charlotte_of_the_Palatinate" title="Elizabeth Charlotte of the Palatinate">Elizabeth Charlotte of the Palatinate</a>, the wife of the king's younger brother <a href="/wiki/Philippe_I,_Duke_of_Orl%C3%A9ans" title="Philippe I, Duke of Orléans">Philippe</a>. She was said to be depressed having to live at a court where beauty was so much prized, not being beautiful herself. She died in 1690. An autopsy revealed a multitude of internal disorders that completely vindicated her complaints of chronic and severe illness.</p>
<p>Maria Anna was buried at the <a href="/wiki/Basilica_of_St_Denis" title="Basilica of St Denis">Royal Basilica of Saint Denis</a>. She is an ancestor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_Henri,_Count_of_Paris,_Duke_of_France" title="Prince Henri, Count of Paris, Duke of France">Prince Henri</a> the <a href="/wiki/Count_of_Paris" title="Count of Paris">Count of Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orl%C3%A9anist" title="Orléanist">Orléanist</a> pretender to the French throne. Also <a href="/wiki/Juan_Carlos_I_of_Spain" title="Juan Carlos I of Spain">Juan Carlos I of Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albert_II_of_Belgium" title="Albert II of Belgium">Albert II, King of the Belgians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henri,_Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Henri, Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Henri, Grand Duke of Luxembourg</a> and of <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Emanuele,_Prince_of_Naples" title="Vittorio Emanuele, Prince of Naples">Vittorio Emanuele, Prince of Naples</a>, a pretender to the Italian throne.</p>
