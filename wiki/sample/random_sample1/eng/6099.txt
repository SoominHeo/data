<p><b>Emma of Altdorf</b>, also known as <b>Hemma</b> (<abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;"> 808</span> – 31 January 876), a member of the <a href="/wiki/Elder_House_of_Welf" title="Elder House of Welf">Elder House of Welf</a>, was the wife of King <a href="/wiki/Louis_the_German" title="Louis the German">Louis the German</a>, and thereby <a href="/wiki/List_of_German_queens" title="List of German queens">Queen consort of East Francia</a> from 843 until her death.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hemma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Her father was <a href="/wiki/Welf_(father_of_Judith)" title="Welf (father of Judith)">Welf I</a> (d. 825), Count of <a href="/wiki/Weingarten,_W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Weingarten, Württemberg">Altorf</a> in <a href="/wiki/Alamannia" title="Alamannia">Alamannia</a>; her mother was <a href="/wiki/Hedwig,_Duchess_of_Bavaria" title="Hedwig, Duchess of Bavaria">Hedwig</a> (Heilwig; c. 775 – after 833), a daughter of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Saxony" title="Duchy of Saxony">Saxon</a> count <a href="/wiki/Isambart" title="Isambart">Isambart</a>. Emma's elder sister was <a href="/wiki/Judith_of_Bavaria_(died_843)" title="Judith of Bavaria (died 843)">Judith</a> (sometimes called <i>of Bavaria</i>), who in February 819 married the <a href="/wiki/Carolingian_dynasty" title="Carolingian dynasty">Carolingian</a> emperor <a href="/wiki/Louis_the_Pious" title="Louis the Pious">Louis the Pious</a>, and thereby became Queen consort of the Franks and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Holy_Roman_Empresses" title="List of Holy Roman Empresses">Holy Roman Empress</a>. The marriage marked a crucial step forward in the rise of the Welf dynasty.</p>
<p>In 827, probably at the instigation of Judith, Emma married <a href="/wiki/Louis_the_German" title="Louis the German">Louis the German</a>, the youngest son of Emperor Louis the Pious from his first marriage with <a href="/wiki/Ermengarde_of_Hesbaye" title="Ermengarde of Hesbaye">Ermengarde of Hesbaye</a>, and stepson of Emma's sister Judith. The wedding cereminy possibly took place in <a href="/wiki/Regensburg" title="Regensburg">Regensburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Bavaria" title="Duchy of Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, where since 817 Louis the German was to rule as a <a href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Bavaria" title="List of rulers of Bavaria">King of Bavaria</a> subordinate to his father; Emma was thus <a href="/wiki/List_of_Bavarian_consorts" title="List of Bavarian consorts">Queen consort of Bavaria</a>. She was given in 833, by her husband Louis, <a href="/wiki/Oberm%C3%BCnster,_Regensburg" title="Obermünster, Regensburg">Obermünster Abbey</a> in Regensburg.</p>
<p>Emperor Louis died in 840. After severe innerdynastic struggles, the <a href="/wiki/Carolingian_Empire" title="Carolingian Empire">Carolingian Empire</a> eventually was divided according to the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Verdun" title="Treaty of Verdun">Treaty of Verdun</a> in 843. The Kingdom of Bavaria was merged in Louis the German's Kingdom of <a href="/wiki/East_Francia" title="East Francia">East Francia</a> (the predecessor of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Germany" title="Kingdom of Germany">Kingdom of Germany</a>), and his wife Emma became first East Frankish queen.</p>
<p>Emma is rarely mentioned in contemporary sources; she does not seem to have had much influence on her husband's rule. The <i><a href="/wiki/Annales_Bertiniani" title="Annales Bertiniani">Annales Bertiniani</a></i> written by Archbishop <a href="/wiki/Hincmar" title="Hincmar">Hincmar of Reims</a> however reproach Emma for a pride which displeased the people of Italy. She is also said to have inordinately favoured her son <a href="/wiki/Carloman_of_Bavaria" title="Carloman of Bavaria">Carloman</a>, designated heir of his father in Bavaria, which led to a revolt by his brothers.</p>
<p>Emma suffered a stroke in 874 and subsequently became paralyzed and speechless; King Louis visited her the last time in 875. She died on 31 January 876, a few months before her husband, and was buried in <a href="/wiki/St._Emmeram%27s_Abbey" title="St. Emmeram's Abbey">St. Emmeram's Abbey</a>, Regensburg. Her tomb, erected around 1300, is considered a masterpiece of medieval sculpture.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hemma&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By Louis, she had seven children:</p>
<ul>
<li>Hildegard (828–856)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carloman,_King_of_Bavaria" title="Carloman, King of Bavaria">Carloman</a> (829–880)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Irmgard_of_Chiemsee" title="Irmgard of Chiemsee">Ermengard</a> ( c. 830/833-866)</li>
<li>Gisela of East Francia, married to Berctolf, Graf of <a href="/wiki/Swabia" title="Swabia">Swabia</a>. Grandmother of Cunigunde of Swabia, wife of <a href="/wiki/Conrad_I,_Duke_of_Swabia" title="Conrad I, Duke of Swabia">Conrad I</a>.</li>
<li>Emma of East Francia</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Louis_the_Younger" title="Louis the Younger">Louis the Younger</a> (830–882)</li>
<li>Bertha (died 877)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Charles_the_Fat" title="Charles the Fat">Charles the Fat</a> (839–888)</li>
</ul>
<p>Her sons became Kings; three of her daughters became nuns.</p>
