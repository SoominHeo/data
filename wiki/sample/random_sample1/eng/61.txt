<p><br/>
Year <b>1288</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MCCLXXXVIII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Leap year starting on Thursday">leap year starting on Thursday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1288&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_area">By area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1288&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1288&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>April – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_B%E1%BA%A1ch_%C4%90%E1%BA%B1ng_(1288)" title="Battle of Bạch Đằng (1288)">Battle of Bạch Đằng</a>: <a href="/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i_Vi%E1%BB%87t" title="Đại Việt">Đại Việt</a> (<a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnamese</a>) general <a href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BA%A7n_H%C6%B0ng_%C4%90%E1%BA%A1o" title="Trần Hưng Đạo">Trần Hưng Đạo</a> sinks the fleet of an invading <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongol</a> army of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuan_Dynasty" title="Yuan Dynasty">Yuan Dynasty</a> by placing steel-tipped bamboo stakes in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bach_Dang_River" title="Bach Dang River">Bach Dang River</a> near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ha_Long_Bay" title="Ha Long Bay">Ha Long Bay</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Japanese_era_name" title="Japanese era name">Japanese era</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Koan_(first)" title="Koan (first)">Koan</a> ends, and the <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8D%C5%8D_(Kamakura_period)" title="Shōō (Kamakura period)">Shōō</a> era begins.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>: Prince Subaru conquers the provinces of Gunma and Tochigi.</li>
<li>Li Ting leads troops and portable cannon of <a href="/wiki/Kublai_Khan" title="Kublai Khan">Kublai Khan</a> against the rebel prince <a href="/wiki/Nayan_(Mongol_Prince)" title="Nayan (Mongol Prince)">Nayan</a> at Banlachengzi in <a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">Manchuria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1288&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_20" title="January 20">January 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_Emlyn_Castle" title="Newcastle Emlyn Castle">Newcastle Emlyn Castle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> is recaptured by English forces, bringing <a href="/wiki/Rhys_ap_Maredudd" title="Rhys ap Maredudd">Rhys ap Maredudd</a>'s revolt to an end.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_5" title="June 5">June 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Worringen" title="Battle of Worringen">Battle of Worringen</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_I_of_Brabant" title="John I of Brabant">John I of Brabant</a> defeats the duchy of <a href="/wiki/Guelders" title="Guelders">Guelders</a> in one of the largest battles in Europe of the Middle Ages, thus winning possession of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Limburg" title="Duchy of Limburg">duchy of Limburg</a>. The battle also liberates the city of <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> from rule by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishopric_of_Cologne" title="Archbishopric of Cologne">Archbishopric of Cologne</a>; it had previously been one of the major ecclesiastical principalities of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_8" title="August 8">August 8</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Nicholas_IV" title="Pope Nicholas IV">Pope Nicholas IV</a> proclaims a crusade against King <a href="/wiki/Ladislaus_IV_of_Hungary" title="Ladislaus IV of Hungary">Ladislaus IV of Hungary</a>, who had lost credibility by favoring his semi-pagan <a href="/wiki/Cumans" title="Cumans">Cuman</a> subjects and in general refusing to conform to the social standards of <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">western Europe</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Scotland" title="Parliament of Scotland">Scots Parliament</a> creates a law allowing women to propose <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marriage</a> to men during <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>; men who refuse such proposals are required to pay a fine to the spurned bride-to-be.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Markets">Markets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1288&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Markets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_16" title="June 16">June 16</a> – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishop_of_V%C3%A4ster%C3%A5s" title="Bishop of Västerås">bishop of Västerås</a> buys 1/8 of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stora_Kopparberg" title="Stora Kopparberg">Stora Kopparberg</a> copper mine in <a href="/wiki/Falun" title="Falun">Falun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.</li>
<li>The Flemish city of <a href="/wiki/Ghent" title="Ghent">Ghent</a> seeks rights to start redeeming its already issued annuities. It is a clear indication of financial difficulty and maybe an early sign of the crisis of the 13th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>The oldest known <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">bronze</a> <a href="/wiki/Handgun" title="Handgun">handgun</a> in the world is dated to this year, a <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">Chinese</a> gun found in <a href="/wiki/Acheng_District" title="Acheng District">Acheng District</a> that was once used to suppress the rebellion of the <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mongol" title="Mongol">Mongol</a> Prince Nayan in 1287–1288.</li>
