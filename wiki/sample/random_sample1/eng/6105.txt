<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bail%C3%A9n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bailén is probably the ancient Baecula, where the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Romans</a>, under <a href="/wiki/Scipio_Africanus" title="Scipio Africanus">Scipio the elder</a>, signally defeated the <a href="/wiki/Punics" title="Punics">Carthaginians</a> in 209 and 206 B.C. In its neighbourhood, also, in 1212, was fought the great <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Las_Navas_de_Tolosa" title="Battle of Las Navas de Tolosa">Battle of Las Navas de Tolosa</a>, in which, according to the ancient chroniclers, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castilians</a> under <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_VIII_of_Castile" title="Alfonso VIII of Castile">Alphonso VIII</a>, slew 200,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Almohads" title="Almohads">Almohads</a>, and themselves only lost 25 men. Although this estimate is absurd, the victory of the Christians was complete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>There is a <a href="/wiki/Abbey" title="Abbey">convent</a> that dates from 729.</p>
<p>In 1808, during the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">Peninsular War</a>, it was the site of a series of clashes (the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bail%C3%A9n" title="Battle of Bailén">Battle of Bailén</a>) at which <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Javier_Casta%C3%B1os,_1st_Duke_of_Bail%C3%A9n" title="Francisco Javier Castaños, 1st Duke of Bailén">General Castaños</a> defeated General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pierre-Antoine,_comte_Dupont_de_l%27%C3%89tang" title="Pierre-Antoine, comte Dupont de l'Étang">Pierre Dupont</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The capitulation, signed at <a href="/wiki/And%C3%BAjar" title="Andújar">Andújar</a> by Dupont on the 23rd of July 1808, involved the surrender of 17,000 men to the Spaniards, and was the first severe blow suffered by the French in the Peninsular War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
