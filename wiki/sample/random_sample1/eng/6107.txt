<p><b>Václav Neumann</b> (29 September 1920 – 2 September 1995) was a <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech</a> <a href="/wiki/Conducting" title="Conducting">conductor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">violinist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Viola" title="Viola">viola</a> player.</p>
<p>Neumann was born in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>, where he studied at the <a href="/wiki/Prague_Conservatory" title="Prague Conservatory">Prague Conservatory</a> with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Josef_Micka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Josef Micka (page does not exist)">Josef Micka</a> (<a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">violin</a>), and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pavel_D%C4%9Bde%C4%8Dek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pavel Dědeček (page does not exist)">Pavel Dědeček</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Metod_Dole%C5%BEil&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Metod Doležil (page does not exist)">Metod Doležil</a> (<a href="/wiki/Conducting" title="Conducting">conducting</a>). He co-founded the <a href="/wiki/Smetana_Quartet" title="Smetana Quartet">Smetana Quartet</a>, playing 1st violin and then viola, before conducting in <a href="/wiki/Karlovy_Vary" title="Karlovy Vary">Karlovy Vary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brno" title="Brno">Brno</a>. In 1956, he began to conduct at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Komische_Oper" title="Komische Oper">Komische Oper</a> in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, leaving in 1964 to become conductor of the <a href="/wiki/Leipzig_Gewandhaus_Orchestra" title="Leipzig Gewandhaus Orchestra">Leipzig Gewandhaus Orchestra</a>. He stayed there until 1968, when he became principal conductor of the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Philharmonic" title="Czech Philharmonic">Czech Philharmonic</a>, a post he held until 1990.</p>
<p>Neumann taught conducting at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prague_Academy_for_Music" title="Prague Academy for Music">Prague Academy for Music</a>, where his students included <a href="/wiki/Oliver_von_Dohn%C3%A1nyi" title="Oliver von Dohnányi">Oliver von Dohnányi</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%ADt%C4%9Bzslav_Podrazil&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vítězslav Podrazil (page does not exist)">Vítězslav Podrazil</a>.</p>
<p>Neumann was particularly noted as a champion of Czech music, and made the first studio recording of <a href="/wiki/Leo%C5%A1_Jan%C3%A1%C4%8Dek" title="Leoš Janáček">Leoš Janáček</a>'s opera <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Excursions_of_Mr._Brou%C4%8Dek" title="The Excursions of Mr. Brouček">The Excursions of Mr. Brouček</a></i> in 1962.</p>
<p>Neumann can be seen conducting the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Philharmonic" title="Czech Philharmonic">Czech Philharmonic</a> in a recording of the Dvořák <a href="/wiki/Cello_Concerto_(Dvo%C5%99%C3%A1k)" title="Cello Concerto (Dvořák)">Cello Concerto</a> with <a href="/wiki/Julian_Lloyd_Webber" title="Julian Lloyd Webber">Julian Lloyd Webber</a>. This is featured in the 1988 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dvorak_-_In_Love%3F" title="Dvorak - In Love?">Dvorak - In Love?</a></i> by director <a href="/wiki/Tony_Palmer" title="Tony Palmer">Tony Palmer</a>.</p>
