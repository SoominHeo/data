<p><b>Bakari Koné</b> (born 17 September 1981, in <a href="/wiki/Abidjan" title="Abidjan">Abidjan</a>) is an Ivorian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Paris_FC" title="Paris FC">Paris FC</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Winger" title="Forward (association football)">winger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bakari_Kon%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bakari_Kon%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Like many other Ivorian stars, he began his career at the famed youth academy at <a href="/wiki/ASEC_Mimosas" title="ASEC Mimosas">ASEC Mimosas</a>, run by Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marc_Guillou" title="Jean-Marc Guillou">Jean-Marc Guillou</a> in his home country. Guillou recalls: <i>"One day, when I was passing through his district, some kids stopped me. They kept saying, ‘We’ve got <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9" title="Pelé">Pelé</a> living here, you’ve got to take a look at him’. Then Kone came along. He was slightly built but I never doubted for one moment that he was a real find. I took him into the academy after a trial of just ten minutes, but I did tell him that the Pele nickname would have to go. That's when he became Baky, and I think it's better that way."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Koné stayed at the academy for five years before joining the first team squad and turning professional in 1999. During his time with the ASEC first team Bakari won the Ivorian League three times, and also the African Supercup before moving on.</p>
<p>In 2002, he joined <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatari</a> club <a href="/wiki/Al-Gharafa_Sports_Club" title="Al-Gharafa Sports Club">Al-Ittihad</a>, staying in the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Gulf</a> for just six months, before moving to France where he joined the then <a href="/wiki/Ligue_2" title="Ligue 2">Ligue 2</a> outfit <a href="/wiki/FC_Lorient" title="FC Lorient">FC Lorient</a> following a successful trial. It was here that Koné rose to fame. In his first season at the club he scored a respectable 10 goals, but then bagged an impressive 24 in 35 appearances in the 2004–05 season, earning him the <i>Player of the Year</i> and <i>Top Scorer</i> titles in the <a href="/wiki/Ligue_2" title="Ligue 2">Ligue 2</a>. At the end of the season, Lorient narrowly missed out on promotion to Ligue 1, and with speculation mounting linking the Ivorian to top flight sides such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OSC_Lille" title="OSC Lille">OSC Lille</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RC_Strasbourg" title="RC Strasbourg">RC Strasbourg</a> and <a href="/wiki/AJ_Auxerre" title="AJ Auxerre">AJ Auxerre</a>, Koné received a standing ovation in his final appearance for his club. In the summer, Bakari finally made his move, relocating to the <a href="/wiki/French_Riviera" title="French Riviera">French Riviera</a> at top flight side OGC Nice, where he signed a four-year contract.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="OGC_Nice">OGC Nice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bakari_Kon%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: OGC Nice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bakari made his Nice debut against <a href="/wiki/Troyes_AC" title="Troyes AC">Troyes</a> on 30 July 2005. In his first season at the club, he made 32 appearances, scoring seven times, and eight goals in 33 appearances in his second. In his second season at the club, English side <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>'s manager <a href="/wiki/Ars%C3%A8ne_Wenger" title="Arsène Wenger">Arsène Wenger</a> labelled him a 'phenomenon'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and one of the hottest prospects in French football, leading to speculation that the forward would be playing in the English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premiership</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kon.C3.A9_dreams_of_English_move_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kon.C3.A9_dreams_of_English_move-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bakari made an impressive start to the 2007–08 season for Nice. After signing a contract extension to 2009 with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Azur" title="Côte d'Azur">Côte d'Azur</a> side, he went on to score four times in his first eight appearances for the club. He won the Ligue 1 player of the month award for both September and October 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kon.C3.A9_dreams_of_English_move_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kon.C3.A9_dreams_of_English_move-4">[4]</a></sup> and finished the 2007–08 Ligue 1 season top scorer for his club, after netting 14 goals in 30 appearances for the Côte d'Azur outfit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2008, Koné began talks with French giants <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Olympique de Marseille</a> with a view to move to the club. The proposed transfer was held up however, with a disagreement over the fee, a situation which caused Koné to miss pre-season training at OGC Nice apparently as a protest. The striker spoke out to state that he had been carrying an injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The saga went further, with Nice president Maurice Cohen stating that their south-coast rivals had till 15 July to sign the striker, otherwise the club would speak to other interested parties. One reportedly interested club was capital club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain" title="Paris Saint-Germain">Paris Saint-Germain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The striker was quoted as saying: <i>"I know that this is going to get sorted out but I can't tell you when. If we talk about (the) 15 July (deadline)...I'm not worried about that."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Marseille president <a href="/wiki/Pape_Diouf" title="Pape Diouf">Pape Diouf</a> echoed Koné's comments, stating: <i>"Ultimatums not only fail to impress me, but they also never provide a solution. Now, concerning Baki, it doesn't just depend on us but on the wishes of the two clubs and, in particular, the two presidents. Personally I think that good sense can do a lot in this case. I remain calm."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 17 July, Thursday, Koné spoke to the media once again regarding his drawn-out transfer saga. He stated: <i>"It's heading down a good road, I hope that it is all going to be done quickly. The important thing is that the two clubs reach an understanding."</i></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Olympique_de_Marseille">Olympique de Marseille</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bakari_Kon%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Olympique de Marseille">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 18 July, it was announced on the official Marseille website that the transfer of the diminutive striker had been completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The striker commented: <i>"Marseille is a big club. It's a club that has proven its qualities in the past and continues to do it today. I wanted to take part in that."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2009 Koné was named Ivorian Player of the Year, ahead of <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> midfielder <a href="/wiki/Yaya_Tour%C3%A9" title="Yaya Touré">Yaya Touré</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lekhwiya">Lekhwiya</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bakari_Kon%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lekhwiya">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 26 May 2010, Koné signed for Qatar League side Lekhwiya along with his Ivorian teammate <a href="/wiki/Aruna_Dindane" title="Aruna Dindane">Aruna Dindane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2010-11_Qatar_Stars_League" title="2010-11 Qatar Stars League">2010-11 Qatar Stars League</a> season, Koné led Lekhwiya to their first ever league championship. He was also named best player of the season by <a href="/wiki/Qatar_Football_Association" title="Qatar Football Association">QFA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qatar_SC">Qatar SC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bakari_Kon%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Qatar SC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 18 May 2012, Koné signed a one-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Qatar_SC" title="Qatar SC">Qatar SC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ajman_club">Ajman club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bakari_Kon%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Ajman club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kone signed for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ajman_club&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ajman club (page does not exist)">Ajman club</a> of UAE at the start of the 2013-14 season signing a two-year contract worth 2 million dollars net per season.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bakari_Kon%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koné made his international debut for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Ivoire_national_football_team" title="Côte d'Ivoire national football team">Ivorian national team</a> against <a href="/wiki/Sudan_national_football_team" title="Sudan national football team">Sudan</a> on 5 September 2004.</p>
<p>He was part of the Ivorian team for the 2006 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_Cup_of_Nations" title="African Cup of Nations">African Cup of Nations</a> in Egypt. He scored a vital equaliser against <a href="/wiki/Cameroon_national_football_team" title="Cameroon national football team">Cameroon</a> in the quarter-final. His team won the penalty shootout 12–11. However, in the final, he was one of two Ivorian players to have his penalty saved as the Ivorians lost to hosts <a href="/wiki/Egypt_national_football_team" title="Egypt national football team">Egypt</a>.</p>
<p>The forward was selected as part of the Ivorian side for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_2006" title="FIFA World Cup 2006">2006 World Cup</a> in Germany. He was given the number 14 shirt, and played his first match for Côte d'Ivoire against the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> on 16 June 2006. He scored his first World Cup goal in that match, where Côte d' Ivoire lost 2–1.</p>
<p>Koné was also selected as part of the Ivorian squad for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_African_Cup_of_Nations" title="2008 African Cup of Nations">2008 African Cup of Nations</a> in Ghana. He made four substitute appearances for the Elephants, and scored once against West African rivals <a href="/wiki/Guinea_national_football_team" title="Guinea national football team">Guinea</a> in the quarter final stage, where the Ivorians ran out 5–1 winners.</p>
<p>He was also selected in the preliminary Côte d'Ivoire squad for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>, but was eventually dropped along with <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Yapi_Yapo" title="Gilles Yapi Yapo">Gilles Yapi Yapo</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emerse_Fae" title="Emerse Fae">Emerse Fae</a>, who played with him in the 2006 World Cup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bakari_Kon%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koné has nine brothers and five sisters. He is the older brother of fellow Ivorian international <a href="/wiki/Arouna_Kon%C3%A9" title="Arouna Koné">Arouna Koné</a>, who is a striker for English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Everton_FC" title="Everton FC">Everton FC</a>. He also has another brother who plays football, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Karamoko_Kon%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Karamoko Koné (page does not exist)">Karamoko Koné</a>, who is part of the reserve team setup at Dutch Eredivisie club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roda_JC" title="Roda JC">Roda JC</a>.</p>
