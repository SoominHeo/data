<p><b>Bad Münstereifel</b> is a historical <a href="/wiki/Spa_town" title="Spa town">spa town</a> in the district of <a href="/wiki/Euskirchen_(district)" title="Euskirchen (district)">Euskirchen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, with about 17,000 inhabitants, situated in the far south of the German state of <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>. The little town is one of only few historical towns in the very south of North Rhine-Westphalia, and because of this is often overcrowded by tourists throughout Spring and Summer.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bad_M%C3%BCnstereifel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bad Münstereifel is the seat of SIGNO innovation society Eifel Association, Association of the greatest inventors in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Signo is a project of the Federal Ministry of Economics and <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">Technology</a>. Joined the Association is a youth and children's department, may acquire in the children and young people in a club's workshop, the basic knowledge of <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bad Münstereifel has four elementary <a href="/wiki/School" title="School">schools</a> in the central city as well as the districts Arloff, Mutscheid, and Houverath. A <a href="/wiki/Hauptschule" title="Hauptschule">Hauptschule</a> and <a href="/wiki/Realschule" title="Realschule">Realschule</a> share facilities, while the city has two <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(school)" title="Gymnasium (school)">Gymnasium</a>: the public St. Michael-Gymnasium and the diocesan-run St.-Angela-Gymnasium. Furthermore, the <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_school" title="Apostolic school">Apostolic school</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Legionaries_of_Christ" title="Legionaries of Christ">Legionaries of Christ</a> has its seat in the city.</p>
<p>Since 1976, the city is also home to its own urban community college, with a specialty in law-related professions. There are also several private training facilities in the city, including the Kurt-Schumacher-Academy of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friedrich-Ebert-Stiftung" title="Friedrich-Ebert-Stiftung">Friedrich-Ebert-Stiftung</a> and the House of Labor Security of the Trade Association for Electrical and Precision Engineering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> There is also a house of the Youth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> as an educational facility for the National Association (North Division).</p>
<p>Bad Münstereifel has a public library with a stock of 16,000 media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_council">Local council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bad_M%C3%BCnstereifel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Local council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<ul>
<li>CDU: 12 seats</li>
<li>SPD: 8 seats</li>
<li>FDP: 4 seats</li>
<li>UWV: 4 seats</li>

</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people_and_gatherings">Notable people and gatherings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bad_M%C3%BCnstereifel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable people and gatherings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2013<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bad_M%C3%BCnstereifel&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> the folk-singer <a href="/wiki/Heino" title="Heino">Heino</a> (born 1938) has lived in the town. From 1996–2012 he operated a cafe there.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Joseph_Haass" title="Friedrich Joseph Haass">Friedrich Joseph Haass</a>, (1780–1853), the "holy doctor of <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>", was born in Bad Münstereifel.</p>
<p>The unofficial <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a> fan convention, NCON, was held in the town's St.-Angela-Gymnasium in the early years of the 21st century.</p>
<p>The Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_(Portugal)" title="Socialist Party (Portugal)">Socialist Party</a>, one of the two main Portuguese parties, was founded here in 1973 with the guidance of the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Germany" title="Social Democratic Party of Germany">Social Democratic Party of Germany</a>, who wanted to create a political force which will be able to keep <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> a member of the <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
