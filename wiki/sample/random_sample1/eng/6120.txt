<p><b>Al Bāţinah</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">الباطنة</span>‎‎) was one of the regions <i>(<a href="/wiki/Mintaqah" title="Mintaqah">mintaqah</a>)</i> of <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>. On 28 October 2011, Al Batinah Region was split into <a href="/wiki/Al_Batinah_North_Governorate" title="Al Batinah North Governorate">Al Batinah North Governorate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Al_Batinah_South_Governorate" title="Al Batinah South Governorate">Al Batinah South Governorate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Al Batinah Region, otherwise known as Al Batinah, occupied an important location on the coast of <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman" title="Gulf of Oman">Gulf of Oman</a>. It lay between Khatmat Malahah in the north and Ras al-Hamra in the south and confined between the <a href="/wiki/Al_Hajar_Mountains" title="Al Hajar Mountains">Al Hajar Mountains</a> in the west and the Gulf of Oman in the east. Most of Oman's population were in that region, because of the green plains between the <a href="/wiki/Al_Hajar_Mountains" title="Al Hajar Mountains">Al Hajar Mountains</a> and the sea.</p>
<p>Al Batinah Region contained the largest number of <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Oman" title="Provinces of Oman">provinces</a> (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wilayat" title="Wilayat">wilayat</a></i>), numbering twelve: <a href="/wiki/Sohar" title="Sohar">Sohar</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ar_Rustaq" title="Ar Rustaq">Ar Rustaq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shinas" title="Shinas">Shinas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liwa_Province" title="Liwa Province">Liwa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saham" title="Saham">Saham</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Khaburah&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Al Khaburah (page does not exist)">Al Khaburah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suwayq" title="Suwayq">Suwayq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nakhal" title="Nakhal">Nakhal</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wadi_Al_Maawil&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wadi Al Maawil (page does not exist)">Wadi Al Maawil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Al_Awabi" title="Al Awabi">Al Awabi</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Al_Musanaah&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Al Musanaah (page does not exist)">Al Musanaah</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barka_(Oman)" title="Barka (Oman)">Barka</a>. <a href="/wiki/Suwayq" title="Suwayq">Suwayq</a> is considered as the biggest walyah in the Batinah Region</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sohar" title="Sohar">Sohar</a> was the regional capital. It is a populous city with a <a href="/wiki/Corniche" title="Corniche">corniche</a>, fish <a href="/wiki/Souq" title="Souq">souq</a> and numerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mosques" title="Mosques">mosques</a>. There's a town nearby called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Majan" title="Majan">Majan</a>.</p>
