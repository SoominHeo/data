<p>The <b>Vatican City national football team</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <span lang="it" xml:lang="it"><i>Selezione di calcio della Città del Vaticano</i></span>) is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> team that represents <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City</a> under the control of the Federazione Vaticanese Giuoco Calcio, headquartered in the Vatican's Cortile di San Damaso.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7_squadre_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-7_squadre-1">[1]</a></sup> The Vatican City football association was founded in 1972. Its current president is Domenico Ruggerio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_Friendly_Game_for_a_Beatific_State_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-A_Friendly_Game_for_a_Beatific_State-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vaticano_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vaticano-3">[3]</a></sup> Gianfranco Guadagnoli, an Italian, is the current head coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_Friendly_Game_for_a_Beatific_State_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-A_Friendly_Game_for_a_Beatific_State-2">[2]</a></sup> The team has been managed by <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Trapattoni" title="Giovanni Trapattoni">Giovanni Trapattoni</a> in the past.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His first match as manager was played on 23 October 2010 when Vatican City faced a team composed of Italian financial police.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vatikan-Auswahl_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vatikan-Auswahl-5">[5]</a></sup> The team played its first match in 1985, a 3–0 victory against a representative of <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austrian</a> journalists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SQUADRE_PAPALI_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-SQUADRE_PAPALI-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Prince_Albert_of_Monaco_with_Vatican_football_players.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="381" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Prince_Albert_of_Monaco_with_Vatican_football_players.jpg/175px-Prince_Albert_of_Monaco_with_Vatican_football_players.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Prince_Albert_of_Monaco_with_Vatican_football_players.jpg/263px-Prince_Albert_of_Monaco_with_Vatican_football_players.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Prince_Albert_of_Monaco_with_Vatican_football_players.jpg/350px-Prince_Albert_of_Monaco_with_Vatican_football_players.jpg 2x" width="175"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Prince_Albert_of_Monaco_with_Vatican_football_players.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Albert_II,_Prince_of_Monaco" title="Albert II, Prince of Monaco">Albert II, Prince of Monaco</a> greeting team in June 2013</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the year 2000, <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a> established a Vatican sports department with the aim of "reinvigorating the tradition (of sport) within the Christian community".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vatican_Cup_lifts_spirits_in_Rome_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vatican_Cup_lifts_spirits_in_Rome-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2006, Vatican <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State" title="Secretary of State">Secretary of State</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">Cardinal</a> <a href="/wiki/Tarcisio_Bertone" title="Tarcisio Bertone">Tarcisio Bertone</a> suggested that the Vatican could field a team of men from Catholic <a href="/wiki/Seminary" title="Seminary">seminaries</a>. About the prospect, the cardinal stated, "If we just take the Brazilian students from our <a href="/wiki/Pontifical_university" title="Pontifical university">Pontifical universities</a> we could have a magnificent squad." The cardinal also noted that in the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 FIFA World Cup</a>, there were 42 players in the final round who attended <a href="/wiki/Salesians_of_Don_Bosco" title="Salesians of Don Bosco">Salesian</a> training centres worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pray_as_you_play_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pray_as_you_play-8">[8]</a></sup> For example, <a href="/wiki/Marcelino_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Marcelino Martínez">Marcelino</a>, Spanish hero of the <a href="/wiki/1964_European_Nations%27_Cup" title="1964 European Nations' Cup">1964 European Nations' Cup</a> was a former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seminarian" title="Seminarian">seminarian</a>. It was Bertone's proposal that the Vatican's players, even if accepted by <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a>, would be drawn from the population within the Catholic Church worldwide, not just citizens of Vatican City. He was unclear at the time whether the Vatican would grant these players Vatican citizenship to make this possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-el_Vaticano_quiere_es_Selecci.C3.B3n_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-el_Vaticano_quiere_es_Selecci.C3.B3n-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the smallest population of any nation, approximately 900, it is difficult to form a squad. The Vatican City squad consists entirely of employees of the Vatican: police officers, postal workers, government officials and members of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swiss_Guard" title="Swiss Guard">Swiss Guard</a>, the Vatican’s de facto army, charged with protecting the pope. Since most Vatican citizens are members of the Swiss Guard, they cannot be amassed in large numbers for a long time. Therefore, the national team has played only a few rare international matches, often drawing a fair amount of interested press.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_Friendly_Game_for_a_Beatific_State_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-A_Friendly_Game_for_a_Beatific_State-2">[2]</a></sup> When Vatican City played its first match in 2002, only one player, Marcello Rosatti, had a Vatican passport. In 2006, Vatican City was invited to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Viva_World_Cup" title="Viva World Cup">Viva World Cup</a> by the <a href="/wiki/N.F.-Board" title="N.F.-Board">N.F.-Board</a> and were expected to participate<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_World_Cup_For_Everyone_Else_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_World_Cup_For_Everyone_Else-10">[10]</a></sup> but were unable to do so because they could not assemble a 15-man roster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-La_Selecci.C3.B3n_de_f.C3.BAtbol_del_Vaticano_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-La_Selecci.C3.B3n_de_f.C3.BAtbol_del_Vaticano-11">[11]</a></sup> In total, Vatican City have played only four full international matches against other nations, one draw and three defeats to <a href="/wiki/Monaco_national_football_team" title="Monaco national football team">Monaco</a> in 2002, 2011, 2013, and 2014 respectively.</p>
<p>In addition to its full international matches, the team has played a friendly match, its first, against the <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_national_football_team" title="San Marino national football team">San Marino</a> reserve team in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vatican_Football_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vatican_Football-12">[12]</a></sup> The final score of that match is believed to be a 0–0 draw but Steve Menary’s book ‘Outcasts: The Lands that FIFA Forgot’ states that Vatican insiders told him that the match ended 1–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Praying_for_a_win_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Praying_for_a_win-13">[13]</a></sup> In 2010, the Vatican organized a team to play a friendly game against <a href="/wiki/Palestine_national_football_team" title="Palestine national football team">Palestine</a>. However, the team was made up of Catholic priests and was not considered the Vatican City national team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Willey_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Willey-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2006, the Vatican City played SV Vollmond, a team from <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Stadio_Petriana" title="Stadio Petriana">Stadio Petriana</a> with Vatican City prevailing 5–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pray_as_you_play_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pray_as_you_play-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fussball_im_Vatikan_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fussball_im_Vatikan-15">[15]</a></sup> The team has also competed against a representative team from the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Limburg" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Limburg">Diocese of Limburg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Trap_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Trap-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Vatican.27s_stance_on_football">The Vatican's stance on football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Vatican's stance on football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cortile.del.belvedere.1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="290" height="276" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/26/Cortile.del.belvedere.1.jpg/200px-Cortile.del.belvedere.1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/26/Cortile.del.belvedere.1.jpg 1.5x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cortile.del.belvedere.1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A carousel in the <a href="/wiki/Cortile_del_Belvedere" title="Cortile del Belvedere">Cortile del Belvedere</a> in 1565, 44 years after the first football match was held in the Vatican</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Vatican footballing history began on 7 January 1521 when the first football match was played in the Vatican in the <a href="/wiki/Cortile_del_Belvedere" title="Cortile del Belvedere">Cortile del Belvedere</a>, in the presence of <a href="/wiki/Pope_Leo_X" title="Pope Leo X">Pope Leo X</a>. The first Vatican league was created in 1973 and was first won by employees of <i><a href="/wiki/L%27Osservatore_Romano" title="L'Osservatore Romano">L'Osservatore Romano</a></i>, the newspaper of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lo_scudetto_vaticano.3F_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lo_scudetto_vaticano.3F-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Vatican has typically expressed strong support for football. <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a> was reportedly a goalkeeper in his youth in <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, and an ardent supporter of <a href="/wiki/KS_Cracovia_(football)" title="KS Cracovia (football)">Cracovia Kraków</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edition.cnn.com_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-edition.cnn.com-18">[18]</a></sup> The former <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> pope <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XVI" title="Pope Benedict XVI">Pope Benedict XVI</a> is an ardent supporter of <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a> since his youth growing up in <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Benedict is quoted as saying, "The sport of football can be a vehicle of education for the values of honesty, solidarity and fraternity, especially for the younger generation."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edition.cnn.com_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-edition.cnn.com-18">[18]</a></sup> In October 2007, the Pope was presented with a #16 shirt (in reference to the sixteenth use of his papal name) by <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Ancona" title="A.C. Ancona">Ancona</a> after Benedict supported their initiative to become a "beacon of morality" by adopting an "innovative, ethical model of practising football".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-edition.cnn.com_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-edition.cnn.com-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2010, Benedict and the Vatican reaffirmed their belief that football should be a beacon of morality by lashing out at <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> after matches for the upcoming season were scheduled at 12:30pm on Sundays to appease pay-per-view companies wishing to spread out Serie A matches over the weekend. The Vatican previously questioned the league's decision to play matches on Sundays at all, but "I consider this a truly harmful development," <a href="/wiki/Monsignor" title="Monsignor">Monsignor</a> Carlo Mazza told <a href="/wiki/Tuttosport" title="Tuttosport">Tuttosport</a>. "Putting people in front of the television screen at 12.30 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Time" title="Central European Time">CET</a>, when they are having lunch with their families, to me seems like a 'pitch invasion' on life."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Additionally, on 18 December 2006, <a href="/wiki/Tarcisio_Bertone" title="Tarcisio Bertone">Tarcisio Bertone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_Secretary_of_State" title="Cardinal Secretary of State">Cardinal Secretary of State</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>, stated, but only in jest, that he did not preclude the possibility that the Vatican, in the future, could put together a football team of great value, that could play on the same level as, <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Internazionale</a> and <a href="/wiki/A.C._Milan" title="A.C. Milan">Milan</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genoa_C.F.C" title="Genoa C.F.C">Genoa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The current Argentinian pope, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a> is an ardent fan of his hometown club <a href="/wiki/San_Lorenzo_de_Almagro" title="San Lorenzo de Almagro">San Lorenzo</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and exhibited disappointment when <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> lost the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="2014 FIFA World Cup Final">2014 World Cup final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="FIFA_membership">FIFA membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: FIFA membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Vatican is one of only nine fully recognized <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign states</a> whose national team is not a <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> member. The others are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Micronesia_national_football_team" title="Micronesia national football team">Federated States of Micronesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kiribati_national_football_team" title="Kiribati national football team">Kiribati</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Islands" title="Marshall Islands">Marshall Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monaco_national_football_team" title="Monaco national football team">Monaco</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nauru_national_soccer_team" title="Nauru national soccer team">Nauru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palau_national_football_team" title="Palau national football team">Palau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuvalu_national_football_team" title="Tuvalu national football team">Tuvalu</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_national_football_team" title="United Kingdom national football team">United Kingdom</a> (though the UK's four <a href="/wiki/Countries_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Countries of the United Kingdom">countries</a> each have individual FIFA teams). In May 2014, Domenico Ruggerio, president of the FA, stated that "I prefer to be amateur...To join FIFA, at that level, will be like a business" after stating "The important message of friendship and love is demonstrated by the sport — the real sport, not the business that is in football these days...It is not just important to win a match; it is how you carry yourself." Therefore, that, he added, meant that "the <a href="/wiki/Ethos" title="Ethos">ethos</a> of the Vatican’s soccer team was, at odds with FIFA membership."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_Friendly_Game_for_a_Beatific_State_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-A_Friendly_Game_for_a_Beatific_State-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kit">Kit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Kit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The team's current kit is provided by <a href="/wiki/Diadora" title="Diadora">Diadora</a>. The shorts are all white while the top is solid yellow with a narrow blue and white line around the right upper quadrant of the body.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-First_half_goals_secure_Monaco_win_against_Vatican_City_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-First_half_goals_secure_Monaco_win_against_Vatican_City-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Matches">Matches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vatican_City_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Matches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
