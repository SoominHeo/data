<p><b>Bahar Pars</b> (born 1979<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> actress and director. Pars won the Medea-award för Actress of the year 2015, and she also received the STOCKmotion award for Best Film of the Year for the short film <i>Rinkebysvenska</i> which she had directed. She was nominated for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guldbaggen" title="Guldbaggen">Guldbaggen</a> award in the Best Supporting Actress category in 2016 for her role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Man_Called_Ove" title="A Man Called Ove">A Man Called Ove</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahar_Pars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahar_Pars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bahar was born in <a href="/wiki/Shiraz" title="Shiraz">Shiraz</a>, Iran, and came to <a href="/wiki/Trelleborg" title="Trelleborg">Trelleborg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> in 1989 after her family fled the war between Iran and <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Between 2003 and 2007 she studied to become an actress at Teaterhögskolan in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> (nowadays <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stockholms_dramatiska_h%C3%B6gskola" title="Stockholms dramatiska högskola">Stockholms dramatiska högskola</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahar_Pars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pars has had several roles in plays and also several films. She made her film debut in the 2006 film <i>När mörkret fallet</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2008 she performed the role of Nina in <i>Farnaz Arbabis Måsen</i> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Backa_teater" title="Backa teater">Backateatern</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At Uppsala stadsteater Pars played the title roles in the plays <i><a href="/wiki/Anna_Karenina" title="Anna Karenina">Anna Karenina</a></i> in 2010, the stage version of <i>Låt den rätta komma</i> in 2011, and <i><a href="/wiki/Hedda_Gabler" title="Hedda Gabler">Hedda Gabler</a></i> in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

Bahar Pars receives her Medea Award in Stockholm, 2015</div>

<p>In 2014 Pars acted in the <a href="/wiki/Jonas_Hassen_Khemiri" title="Jonas Hassen Khemiri">Jonas Hassen Khemiris</a> play <i>Jag ringer mina bröder</i> as the character Valerie, at Kulturhuset Stadsteatern.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In November the same year she performed the monologue play <i>På alla fyra</i> at Kilenscenen at Kulturhuset Stadsteatern.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The newspaper <i>Svenska Dagbladet</i> reviewed Pars' performance favourably and described it as "she had total control of her ways of expressions". Outside of acting, Pars has also directed and been one of the creators <i>Nyårsklockan</i>, in which fifteen female culture-personalities appear.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015 she was awarded the Medea Award with the citation "In her own personal way Bahar Pars widens the ways to play classical female roles and bring them into our present time".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She had the leading role as Bertha in the play <i>Marodörer</i> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dramaten" title="Dramaten">Dramaten</a> in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ahead of the <a href="/wiki/51st_Guldbagge_Awards" title="51st Guldbagge Awards">2016 Guldbaggen awards</a> she was nominated in the category <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Best Supporting Actress">Best Supporting Actress</a> for her role as Parvaneh in the 2015 film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Man_Called_Ove" title="A Man Called Ove">A Man Called Ove</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
