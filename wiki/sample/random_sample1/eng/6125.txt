<p><b>Vahdat</b> (also <b>Vakhdat</b>, meaning <i>unity</i> in Persian) is a city in western Tajikistan, just 10 km east of Dushanbe. In the last century the city has been renamed several times. The city was known as Yangi-Bazar until 1936, when it was renamed Ordzhonikidzeabad in honor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgian_people" title="Georgian people">Georgian</a> <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communist</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grigoriy_Ordzhonikidze" title="Grigoriy Ordzhonikidze">Grigoriy Ordzhonikidze</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1992 after Tajikistan became independent the city was renamed Kofarnihon (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> for "a place where unbelievers hide", کافرنهان) and in 2003 it was renamed Vahdat.</p>
