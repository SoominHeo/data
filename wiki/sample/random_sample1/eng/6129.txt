<p><b>Park Gyu-su</b> (박규수, 朴珪壽, 1807–1877) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scholar-bureaucrat" title="Scholar-bureaucrat">scholar-bureaucrat</a>, a teacher, a politician and a diplomat of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a> <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>. He was known as a pioneer of the enlightenment group. Park Gyu-su was a grandson of <a href="/wiki/Park_Ji-won" title="Park Ji-won">Park Ji-won</a>, the great <a href="/wiki/Silhak" title="Silhak">Silhak</a> scholar of the Joseon. nickname was <b>Hwanjae</b> (환재, 瓛齋), <b>Hwanjae</b> (환재, 桓齋) en <b>Heonjae</b> (헌재, 獻齋), <b>Hwanjaegeosa</b> (환재거사 瓛齋居士).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Gyu-su&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When Park was 20, he became acquainted with <a href="/wiki/Crown_Prince_Hyomyeong" title="Crown Prince Hyomyeong">Crown Prince Hyomyeong</a> (1809-1830). They became close friends and discussed politics. When the crown prince died a sudden death, Park was so shocked that he shut himself away for 20 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1848, He successed <a href="/wiki/Gwageo" title="Gwageo">Gwageo</a> exam on forth class. In the middle of the 1870s, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pak_Yeong-hyo" title="Pak Yeong-hyo">Pak Yeong-hyo</a> visited Park Gyu-su and be taught about the Enlightenment Thought.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Okgyun" title="Kim Okgyun">Kim Okgyun</a> was affected Park too.</p>
<p>During the period of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Gojong_of_the_Korean_Empire" title="Emperor Gojong of the Korean Empire">King Gojong</a>'s reign, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Sinjeong" title="Queen Sinjeong">Queen Sinjeong</a>, who was a wife of Prince Hyomyeong, promote Park. When the <a href="/wiki/General_Sherman_incident" title="General Sherman incident">General Sherman incident</a> occurred, Park was the governor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pyongan" title="Pyongan">Pyongan</a>. Park ordered the General Sherman's crew to depart from Joseon. The ship's crew ignored Park's order and captured <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yi_Hyeon-ik&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yi Hyeon-ik (page does not exist)">Yi Hyeon-ik</a>, an adjutant of Park. Park rescued Yi and destroyed the General Sherman.</p>
<p>In 1872, Park Gyu-su was Joseon chief envoy to the court of the <a href="/wiki/Tongzhi_Emperor" title="Tongzhi Emperor">Tongzhi Emperor</a>, offering congratulations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
