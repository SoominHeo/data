<p><b>Park Sang-in</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">박상인</span>, born 15 November 1952) is a former <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> and football manager who manages <a href="/wiki/Korea_National_League" title="Korea National League">Korea National League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Busan_Transportation_Corporation_FC" title="Busan Transportation Corporation FC">Busan Transportation Corporation</a>. His sons <a href="/wiki/Park_Hyuk-soon" title="Park Hyuk-soon">Park Hyuk-soon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Park_Seung-min" title="Park Seung-min">Park Seung-min</a> are also footballers.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Sang-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Player_career">Player career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Sang-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Player career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Park began his career at Changnyeong Middle School at the age of 13. He went through Dongnae High School, graduating in 1972. In the same year he joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commercial_Bank_of_Korea" title="Commercial Bank of Korea">Commercial Bank of Korea</a>. One year later, he joined the army in order to <a href="/wiki/Conscription_in_South_Korea" title="Conscription in South Korea">fulfil his military service</a>.</p>
<p>In 1981, he had trials for Dutch club, <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a>, receiving a favorable response from the club. However, the contract foundered due to an objection of the player's labor union.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In July 1981, he signed a contract with <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> club <a href="/wiki/MSV_Duisburg" title="MSV Duisburg">MSV Duisburg</a> for 1 year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He played only two league games though, due to a thigh injury.</p>
<p>He returned to South Korea and joined <a href="/wiki/Hallelujah_FC" title="Hallelujah FC">Hallelujah FC</a>. He lifted the first championship of the <a href="/wiki/K_League" title="K League">K League</a> with <a href="/wiki/Hallelujah_FC" title="Hallelujah FC">Hallelujah FC</a> in 1983. He then went on to play for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyundai_Horang-i" title="Hyundai Horang-i">Hyundai Horang-i</a> from 1986 to 1987.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manager_career">Manager career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Sang-in&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Manager career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After retiring from club football, he was appointed as his <a href="/wiki/Alma_mater" title="Alma mater">alma mater</a>'s football team manager from 1988 to 2001. In the middle of the term, in 1992–1993, he managed the <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_under-20_football_team" title="South Korea national under-20 football team">South Korea U20 team</a>, going to the <a href="/wiki/1993_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="1993 FIFA World Youth Championship">1993 FIFA World Youth Championship</a>. The South Korean youth team finished unbeaten, with three draws. From 2006, he has managed newly formed <a href="/wiki/Busan_Transportation_Corporation_FC" title="Busan Transportation Corporation FC">Busan Transportation Corporation</a>. In 2009, he went to the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2009_East_Asian_Games" title="Football at the 2009 East Asian Games">2009 East Asian Games</a> as the South Korea team manager.</p>
