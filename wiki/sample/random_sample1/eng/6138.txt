<p><b>Park Seo-joon</b> (born Park Yong-gyu) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a> actor. He is best known for his roles in the television dramas <i><a href="/wiki/She_Was_Pretty" title="She Was Pretty">She Was Pretty</a></i> (2015) and <i><a href="/wiki/Hwarang:_The_Poet_Warrior_Youth" title="Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth">Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth</a></i> (2016-2017).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Seo-joon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Park was born in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> as the oldest of three brothers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After graduating from An-Nam high school, he attended <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Institute_of_the_Arts" title="Seoul Institute of the Arts">Seoul Institute of the Arts</a> and enlisted for mandatory military service in 2008.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Seo-joon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Park made his entertainment debut in 2011 by appearing in the <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bang_Yong-guk" title="Bang Yong-guk">Bang Yong-guk</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> "I Remember."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He has then appeared in <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">television dramas</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Dream_High_2" title="Dream High 2">Dream High 2</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/Pots_of_Gold" title="Pots of Gold">Pots of Gold</a></i> (2013),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Witch%27s_Romance" title="Witch's Romance">Witch's Romance</a></i> (2014). From October 2013 to April 2015, he was the host of <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>His breakout role came in 2015 as the male lead of the television series <i><a href="/wiki/She_Was_Pretty" title="She Was Pretty">She Was Pretty</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In the same year, Park starred in thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Evil" title="The Chronicles of Evil">The Chronicles of Evil</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2016, Park was cast in youth historical drama <i><a href="/wiki/Hwarang:_The_Poet_Warrior_Youth" title="Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth">Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He was also cast in <i>Youth Cop</i>, which marked his first big-screen leading role.</p>
