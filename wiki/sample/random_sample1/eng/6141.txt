<p><b>Park Sung-hwan</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 박성환; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: 朴成煥; born September 4, 1984 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeju-do" title="Jeju-do">Jeju-do</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>) is a male <a href="/wiki/Badminton" title="Badminton">badminton</a> player from <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. Park is the top-ranked men's singles player in South Korea and has succeeded at an international level, winning individual medals at both the <a href="/wiki/2010_BWF_World_Championships" title="2010 BWF World Championships">2010 World Championships</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2010_Asian_Games" title="Badminton at the 2010 Asian Games">2010 Asian Games</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Sung-hwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004-2007">2004-2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Sung-hwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2004-2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, Park won the <a href="/wiki/Indonesia_International" title="Indonesia International">Indonesia International</a> and then competed at the <a href="/wiki/2007_BWF_World_Championships" title="2007 BWF World Championships">2007 BWF World Championships</a> in the men's singles division; for the latter, he was defeated in the third round by <a href="/wiki/Chen_Yu_(badminton)" title="Chen Yu (badminton)">Chen Yu</a>, of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">China</a>, with a final score of: 15-21, 21-16, 21-15.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008">2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Sung-hwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2008, Park competed in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympic_Games" title="2008 Summer Olympic Games">2008 Summer Olympic Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, China, but was unable to move beyond the first round and second rounds. He was defeated by <a href="/wiki/Taufik_Hidayat" title="Taufik Hidayat">Taufik Hidayat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hyun-il" title="Lee Hyun-il">Lee Hyun-il</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sho_Sasaki" title="Sho Sasaki">Sho Sasaki</a> (Japan), <a href="/wiki/Lee_Chong_Wei" title="Lee Chong Wei">Lee Chong Wei</a> (Malaysia) and the world number one player at the time, <a href="/wiki/Lin_Dan" title="Lin Dan">Lin Dan</a> (<a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>). During this year, Park won the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_Asia_Championships" title="Badminton Asia Championships">Badminton Asia Championships</a> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Korea_International_Challenge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Korea International Challenge (page does not exist)">Korea International Challenge</a> titles, beating China's <a href="/wiki/Chen_Jin_(badminton)" title="Chen Jin (badminton)">Chen Jin</a> (21-18, 21-18) and <a href="/wiki/South_Korean" title="South Korean">South Korean</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lee_Cheol_Ho&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lee Cheol Ho (page does not exist)">Lee Cheol Ho</a> (21-14, 21-13) respectively.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009">2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Sung-hwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Park entered the Proton <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Open_(badminton)" title="Malaysia Open (badminton)">Malaysian Open</a>, held in January, and played the world number one-ranked player at the time, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Chong_Wei" title="Lee Chong Wei">Lee Chong Wei</a>, in the final—Park lost with a score of 14-21, 13-21. The following week, Park joined the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Open_(badminton)" title="Korea Open (badminton)">Korea Open</a> Super Series and was again stopped by the number-one seed, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Chong_Wei" title="Lee Chong Wei">Lee Chong Wei</a> of <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, with a score of 21-18, 7-21, 16-21 in the quarter-final match. Park skipped the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_England_Open" title="All England Open">All England Open</a> Super Series and the Wilson <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swiss_Open" title="Swiss Open">Swiss Open</a> Super Series, both held in March.</p>
<p>In May, he entered the Li Ning <a href="/wiki/Sudirman_Cup" title="Sudirman Cup">Sudirman Cup</a>, held in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The Sudirman Cup was a team competition and the South Korean team reached the final in which they played against the Chinese team. Park Sung-hwan was defeated in the final, with a score of 14-21, 18-21, in a match against the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic</a> champion, <a href="/wiki/Lin_Dan" title="Lin Dan">Lin Dan</a>, and lost one point for his team. The South Korean team was eventually awarded the <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">silver</a> medal, having lost 0-3 to <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.</p>
<p>Park then participated in the Aviva <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Open_(badminton)" title="Singapore Open (badminton)">Singapore Open</a> Super Series in April, where he reached the semi-finals. <a href="/wiki/Boonsak_Ponsana" title="Boonsak Ponsana">Boonsak Ponsana</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, eventually won the tournament, beating Park with a score of 19-21, 21-18, 13-21. A week later, Park joined the Djarum <a href="/wiki/Indonesia_Open_(badminton)" title="Indonesia Open (badminton)">Indonesia Open</a> Super Series and lost to the world number one, Lee Chong Wei, 9-21, 13-21, in the quarter-final match. Two months later, Park entered the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Taipei" title="Chinese Taipei">Chinese Taipei</a> Grand Prix Gold Open, which is held in the last week of August, and was beaten by <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Hafiz_Hashim" title="Muhammad Hafiz Hashim">Muhammad Hafiz Hashim</a> from <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, 15-21, 18-21, again in the quarter-final match. Two weeks later, Park was to be defeated again in the quarter-final matches of the Li Ning China Masters Super Series in <a href="/wiki/Changzhou" title="Changzhou">Changzhou</a>, China (to 4th seed badminton ace <a href="/wiki/Lin_Dan" title="Lin Dan">Lin Dan</a> of China, 15-21, 21-13, 21-23), and the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Open_(badminton)" title="Japan Open (badminton)">Japan Open</a> Super Series (to Indonesian, <a href="/wiki/Taufik_Hidayat" title="Taufik Hidayat">Taufik Hidayat</a>, 21-12, 26-28, 15-21).</p>
<p>In November, Park participated in the Yonex Sunrise <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Open_(badminton)" title="Hong Kong Open (badminton)">Hong Kong Open</a> and lost 15-21, 16-21, against Hong Kong player, <a href="/wiki/Chan_Yan_Kit" title="Chan Yan Kit">Chan Yan Kit</a>, in the second round. Park's next event was the <a href="/wiki/China_Open_(badminton)" title="China Open (badminton)">China Open</a> Super Series, held in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, China, where he reached the semi-final round and lost to the 2009 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Badminton_Championships" title="World Badminton Championships">World Badminton Championships</a> champion, <a href="/wiki/Lin_Dan" title="Lin Dan">Lin Dan</a>; the final score was 10-21, 11-21.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Park_Sung-hwan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Park entered the <a href="/wiki/BWF_Super_Series" title="BWF Super Series">Super Series</a> tournament of the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Open_(badminton)" title="Korea Open (badminton)">Korea Open</a>, which is held in January, and was eliminated in the quarter-final stage by Denmark's <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gade" title="Peter Gade">Peter Gade</a>, 21-17, 16-21, 20-22. Park then lost 16-21, 17-21, to Du Pengyu from China in the first round of the <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Open_(badminton)" title="Malaysia Open (badminton)">Malaysia Open</a>. In March, at the <a href="/wiki/All_England_Open_Badminton_Championships" title="All England Open Badminton Championships">All England Open</a>, Park lost in the second round, 5-21, 10-21, to 6th seed, <a href="/wiki/Bao_Chunlai" title="Bao Chunlai">Bao Chunlai</a> of China, after defeating Japan's Sho Sasaki, 21-13 21-11, in the opening round. The All England Open was followed by the Wilson Swiss Open, held at the <a href="/wiki/Basel" title="Basel">Basel</a> Stadium in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, and after defeating Lithuania's Kestutis Navickas, 21-13, 21-13, in the opening round, Park lost to Dane, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Gade" title="Peter Gade">Peter Gade</a>, in straight games: 15-21, 16-21.</p>
<p>In May, Park returned to Malaysia for the 2010 Proton Thomas &amp; Uber Cup Finals, held in the capital city, <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a>. Park again was beaten by China's Lin Dan, 18-21, 23-25, in the second round and had conceded one point for his team. In the quarter-final match, Park played against Dan once again and lost 16-21, 15-21. Meanwhile, his team members also lost to the other Chinese players. The final score was 0-3 in favour of the Chinese team.</p>
<p>In August, Park's entered the <a href="/wiki/2010_BWF_World_Championships" title="2010 BWF World Championships">2010 BWF World Championships</a>, where he made it through to the semi-final, registering a win against Lin Dan, 21-13 21-13, in the quarter-final. However, Indonesia's Taufik Hidayat later defeated Park with a score of 10-21, 20-22, in the last four; Park emerged with the <a href="/wiki/Bronze" title="Bronze">bronze</a> medal. After the world championships, Park played in the China Masters Super Series in Changzhou, China, in September. On this occasion, he lost to Lin Dan, 11-21, 17-21, in the opening round. Park then entered the Japan Open Super Series and was again matched up against Lin Dan following a victory in the second round. In the final eight, Park was knocked out by Lin Dan, 20-22, 16-21.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Asian_Games" title="Asian Games">Asian Games</a> team challenge was held in November in Guangzhou, China. Park represented Korea in the finals, meeting Lin Dan on yet another occasion, and, again, lost with a score of 21-19, 16-21, 18-21. The Korean team eventually won the silver medal, losing 1-3 to the Chinese team. In the individual match section of the Asian Games, Park faced Lin Dan in the semi-finals and was defeated 14-21, 10-21, winning a bronze medal for Korea. The next month, Park joined the <a href="/wiki/China_Open_(badminton)" title="China Open (badminton)">China Open</a> Super Series in Shanghai and defeated China's <a href="/wiki/Wang_Zhengming" title="Wang Zhengming">Wang Zhengming</a> in the opening round with a score of 21-5, 11-21, 21-17. In the second round, Park lost to 5th seed, <a href="/wiki/Chen_Long" title="Chen Long">Chen Long</a> of China, 12-21, 16-21, signaling the South Korean's exit from the tournament.</p>
