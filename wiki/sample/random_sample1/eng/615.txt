<p>Year <b>421</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">CDXXI</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Common year starting on Saturday">common year starting on Saturday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Agricola and Eustathius</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1174 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 421 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=421&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=421&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=421&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_8" title="February 8">February 8</a> – <a href="/wiki/Constantius_III" title="Constantius III">Constantius III</a> is appointed co-emperor (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Augustus_(title)" title="Augustus (title)">Augustus</a></i>) with his ineffectual brother-in-law, <a href="/wiki/Honorius_(emperor)" title="Honorius (emperor)">Honorius</a>, and becomes the real ruler of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Roman_Empire" title="Western Roman Empire">Western Roman Empire</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_25" title="March 25">March 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a> is founded at twelve o'clock noon (according to legend) with the dedication of the first church, <a href="/wiki/San_Giacomo_di_Rialto" title="San Giacomo di Rialto">San Giacomo</a>, at the islet of <a href="/wiki/Rialto" title="Rialto">Rialto</a> (<a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_7" title="June 7">June 7</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Theodosius_II" title="Theodosius II">Theodosius II</a> marries <a href="/wiki/Aelia_Eudocia" title="Aelia Eudocia">Aelia Eudocia</a>, a woman of Greek origin. The <a href="/wiki/Wedding" title="Wedding">wedding</a> is celebrated at <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> with <a href="/wiki/Chariot_racing" title="Chariot racing">chariot racing</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hippodrome_of_Constantinople" title="Hippodrome of Constantinople">Hippodrome</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_2" title="September 2">September 2</a> – Constantius III dies suddenly of an illness; his wife <a href="/wiki/Galla_Placidia" title="Galla Placidia">Galla Placidia</a> becomes for the second time a <a href="/wiki/Widow" title="Widow">widow</a>. She departs with her children <a href="/wiki/Justa_Grata_Honoria" title="Justa Grata Honoria">Grata Honoria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valentinian_III" title="Valentinian III">Valentinian</a> to the court of Constantinople.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=421&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rugila" title="Rugila">Rugila</a>, chieftain of the <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a>, attacks the <a href="/wiki/Roman_diocese" title="Roman diocese">dioceses</a> of <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Dacia" title="Diocese of Dacia">Dacia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Thrace" title="Diocese of Thrace">Thrace</a> (<a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a>). Theodosius II allows <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pannonia_(Roman_province)" title="Pannonia (Roman province)">Pannonian</a> <a href="/wiki/Ostrogoths" title="Ostrogoths">Ostrogoths</a> to settle in Thrace, to defend the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> frontier.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Franks</a> conquer new territories in their kingdom and sack the old <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> capital <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Augusta_Treverorum" title="Augusta Treverorum">Augusta Treverorum</a></i> (modern <a href="/wiki/Trier" title="Trier">Trier</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Persia">Persia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=421&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Persia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman%E2%80%93Sassanid_War_(421%E2%80%93422)" title="Roman–Sassanid War (421–422)">Roman–Sassanid War</a>: Theodosius II starts a war against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sassanids" title="Sassanids">Sassanids</a>, sending an expeditionary force under command of <a href="/wiki/Ardabur_(consul_427)" title="Ardabur (consul 427)">Ardaburius</a>, and invades <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>.</li>
<li>Autumn – Ardaburius devastates <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aghdznik" title="Aghdznik">Arzanene</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_(antiquity)" title="Kingdom of Armenia (antiquity)">Armenia</a>) and forces the Persians to retreat to <a href="/wiki/Nusaybin" title="Nusaybin">Nisibis</a> (<a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>). King <a href="/wiki/Bahram_V" title="Bahram V">Bahram V</a> allies himself with the <a href="/wiki/Lakhmids" title="Lakhmids">Lakhmids Arabs</a> of <a href="/wiki/Al-Hirah" title="Al-Hirah">Hirah</a>.</li>
</ul>
