<p><b>Pak Je-ga</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">박제가</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">朴齊家</span>; 1750–1815) was a scholar of school of <a href="/wiki/Silhak" title="Silhak">Practical learning (Silhak)</a> in the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was a student of <a href="/wiki/Park_Ji-won" title="Park Ji-won">Park Ji-won</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pak_Je-ga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pak Je Ga was a famous Silhak scholar in the late Joseon Dynasty. He was a student of another famous Silhak scholar, Park Ji Won. Pak Je Ga was born in 1750 and died in 1815. Pak Je Ga went to Qing (also referred to as "Ching"), which was the name of China at that time, after becoming a student of Park Ji Won. He learned about China’s modern culture, advanced techniques, and its economic system while there. After returning from Qing, Park Ji Won worked in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jiphyeonjeon" title="Jiphyeonjeon">Jiphyeonjeon</a>, a place where important scholars and scientists worked, during the time of King <a href="/wiki/Jeongjo_of_Joseon" title="Jeongjo of Joseon">Jeongjo</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pak_Je-ga&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Because the Joseon government supported ‘Yuhak', a neo-Confucianist conservative ideology, Pak Je Ga had an important role and his desire was to make <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon</a> rich in science and commerce.</p>
<p>He authored several books; the compilation is titled, <i>Jeongyungyp.</i> He wrote about making the commerce system stronger in <i>Umyoungnomchogo</i> and <i>Gunyeonjib</i>. At that time, it was quite shocking to believe in <a href="/wiki/Silhak" title="Silhak">Silhak</a>, whose ideas opposed Yuhak.</p>
<p>Pak Je Ga developed the way of agriculture, and he also enhanced some of the farming machinery to cultivate plants.</p>
