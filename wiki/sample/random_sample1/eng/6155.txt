<p><b>Grace Park</b> (born 6 March 1979) is a retired <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Golf" title="Golf">golfer</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LPGA_Tour" title="LPGA Tour">LPGA Tour</a>. She was a member of the LPGA Tour from 2000 until her retirement in 2012 and won six LPGA Tour events, including one <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_major_golf_championships" title="Women's major golf championships">major championship</a>, during her career.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amateur_career">Amateur career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grace_Park_(golfer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Amateur career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Park was born <b>Park Ji-eun</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: 박지은) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seoul,_South_Korea" title="Seoul, South Korea">Seoul, South Korea</a>. She moved to <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> at the age of 12, and then to <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>. She received the 1996 <a href="/wiki/Dial_Award" title="Dial Award">Dial Award</a> as top female high-school scholar-athlete in the United States. She attended <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_University" title="Arizona State University">Arizona State University</a> and graduated from <a href="/wiki/Ewha_Womans_University" title="Ewha Womans University">Ewha Womans University</a> in 2003.</p>
<p>Park had an outstanding amateur career in the United States being Rolex Junior Player of the Year in 1994 and 1996,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ajga_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ajga-1">[1]</a></sup> winning several amateur championships in 1998 including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Women%27s_Amateur" title="U.S. Women's Amateur">U.S. Women's Amateur</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Western_Amateur" title="Women's Western Amateur">Women's Western Amateur</a>. She tied for eighth as an amateur in the 1999 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Women%27s_Open" title="U.S. Women's Open">U.S. Women's Open</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grace_Park_(golfer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Park turned professional in 1999 and decided to play on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Futures_Tour" title="Futures Tour">Futures Tour</a> instead of taking exemptions to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LPGA_Tour" title="LPGA Tour">LPGA Tour</a> tournaments. She won five of the ten tournaments she entered and became one of the first three golfers to gain automatic LPGA Tour exempt status by finishing top of the money list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Futures_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Futures-2">[2]</a></sup> She was named Rookie of the Year and Player of the Year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FutPOY_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FutPOY-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>She won at least one LPGA tournament in each season from 2000 to 2004, including her only major, the 2004 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kraft_Nabisco_Championship" title="Kraft Nabisco Championship">Kraft Nabisco Championship</a>. The years 2005 and 2006 were difficult for Park as she suffered from back and neck injuries, and success continued to elude her the following two seasons as well. In April 2009 it was reported that Park had undergone a successful hip surgery, and that she would be off the LPGA tour for several months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2012, Park announced her retirement from golf.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston_Globe" title="Boston Globe">Boston Globe</a></i> has described Park as "the striking beauty, the tall and proud walk, the dazzling smile" and she has attracted sponsorship from <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rolex" title="Rolex">Rolex</a>.</p>
