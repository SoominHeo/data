<p><b>Pakch'ŏn County</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_North_Korea" title="Administrative divisions of North Korea"><i>kun</i></a>, or county, in southern <a href="/wiki/North_Pyongan_Province" title="North Pyongan Province">North P'yŏngan</a> province, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>. It is bordered to the north by <a href="/wiki/Taechon_County" title="Taechon County">T'aech'ŏn</a>, to the east and southeast by <a href="/wiki/Nyongbyon_County" title="Nyongbyon County">Nyŏngbyŏn</a>, and to the west by <a href="/wiki/Unjon_County" title="Unjon County">Unjŏn</a> counties. To the south, it looks across the <a href="/wiki/Chongchon_River" title="Chongchon River">Ch'ŏngch'ŏn River</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anju_(city)" title="Anju (city)">Anju</a> city and <a href="/wiki/Mundok_County" title="Mundok County">Mundŏk</a> county in <a href="/wiki/South_Pyongan_Province" title="South Pyongan Province">South P'yŏngan</a> province. In 1952, 4 <i>myŏn</i> of Pakch'ŏn were split off to join Unjŏn county; since then, the county's administrative divisions have been revised in 1954, 1956, 1958, 1978, 1980, and 1982.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakchon_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pakch'ŏn's terrain is dominated by rolling hills and plains, with few points exceeding 300 m above sea level. The highest point is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chongryongsan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chongryongsan (page does not exist)">Ch'ŏngryongsan</a> (청룡산, 322 m). The Pakch'ŏn Plain spreads over 100 km² along the <a href="/wiki/Taeryong_River" title="Taeryong River">Taeryong</a> and Ch'ŏngch'ŏn Rivers. Only 30% of the county's terrain is forested, with pine the dominant tree, while 50% is cultivated.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_Divisions">Administrative Divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakchon_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative Divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakchon_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The year-round average temperature is 8.8 °C, with temperatures averaging -9.6 °C in January and 23.9 °C in August. The annual rainfall is 1274 mm.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakchon_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pakch'ŏn is a center of rice production; other crops include maize, soybeans, barley, and wheat. Livestock are also raised, and the county leads North Py'ŏngan in the area devoted to orchards. Mineral resources include gold, silver, and mica. In 1991, it was revealed that nuclear facilities are located in Pakch'ŏn.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakchon_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pyongui_Line" title="Pyongui Line">P'yŏngŭi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pakchon_Line" title="Pakchon Line">Pakch'ŏn</a> lines of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_State_Railway" title="Korean State Railway">Korean State Railway</a> pass through the county.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_Interest">Places of Interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pakchon_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Places of Interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The temple of <a href="/wiki/Simwonsa_(Pakchon)" title="Simwonsa (Pakchon)">Simwonsa</a> in Sangyang-ri is designated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Treasures_of_North_Korea" title="National Treasures of North Korea">North Korean national treasure</a> 21.</p>
<p>Pakchon is home to Pakchon Technical School (박천전문학교).</p>
