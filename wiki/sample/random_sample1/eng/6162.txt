<p><b>Bhaktapur</b> (<a href="/wiki/Nepali_language" title="Nepali language">Nepali</a>: <span lang="ne" xml:lang="ne">भक्तपुर</span> <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration">Bhaktapur</span> <span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Bhaktapur_clip.wav" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/10/Bhaktapur_clip.wav" title="Bhaktapur clip.wav">Listen</a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Bhaktapur_clip.wav" title="File:Bhaktapur clip.wav"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span> ), literally translates to <b>Place of devotees</b>. Also known as <b>Bhadgaon</b> or <b>Khwopa</b> (<a href="/wiki/Newar_language" title="Newar language">Newar</a>: <span lang="new" xml:lang="new">ख्वप</span> <span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration">Khwopa</span>), it is an ancient <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newar" title="Newar">Newar</a> city in the east corner of the <a href="/wiki/Kathmandu_Valley" title="Kathmandu Valley">Kathmandu Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, about 8 miles (13 km) from the capital city, <a href="/wiki/Kathmandu" title="Kathmandu">Kathmandu</a>. It is located in <a href="/wiki/Bhaktapur_District" title="Bhaktapur District">Bhaktapur District</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bagmati_Zone" title="Bagmati Zone">Bagmati Zone</a>.</p>
<p>Bhaktapur was the largest of the three Newar kingdoms of the Kathmandu Valley, and was the capital of Nepal during the great 'Malla Kingdom' until the second half of the 15th century. Today it is the third largest city in the <a href="/wiki/Kathmandu_Valley" title="Kathmandu Valley">Kathmandu Valley</a>, with a population of more than 80,000, of which the vast majority are still Newars. Historically more isolated than the other two kingdoms, Kathmandu and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patan,_Nepal" title="Patan, Nepal">Patan</a>, Bhaktapur has a distinctly different form of <a href="/wiki/Newar_language" title="Newar language">Nepal Bhasa</a> language.</p>
<p>Bhaktapur has the best preserved palace courtyards and old city center in Nepal, and is listed as a <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> by UNESCO for its rich culture, temples, and wood, metal and stone artworks. This is supported by the restoration and preservation efforts of German-funded Bhaktapur Development Project (BDP).</p>
<p>The city is famous for special type of <a href="/wiki/Yogurt" title="Yogurt">curd</a> called "Ju Ju(king) dhau(curd). It is experienced by the curd makers that the taste of curd prepared in this location cannot be found elsewhere all over Nepal</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhaktapur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>KHWOPA is the ancient name of Bhaktapur. The term "Bhaktapur" (<a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a>/Nepali: भक्तपुर) refers to "The City Of Devotees". This Bhaktapur City is also known as "Khwopa" (Nepal Bhasa: ख्वप) or "Bhadgaon" (Nepali:भादगाँउ) or "Ancient Newari Town" throughout the Kathmandu Valley. "Kh0apa" actually refers to the masks which are believed to have been worn by gods and goddesses. Bhaktapur is popular for different forms of mask dances based on lives of different deities and therefore, it was named "Khwapa" which later came to become just "Khwopa," which is also near to meaning masks.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhaktapur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is the home of traditional art and architecture, historical monuments and craft works, magnificent windows, pottery and weaving industries, excellent temples, beautiful ponds, rich local customs, culture, religion, festivals, musical mystic and so on. Bhaktapur is still an untouched as well as preserved ancient city that is itself a world to explore for tourists.</p>
<p>From time immemorial it lay on the trade route between <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibet</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. This position on the main caravan route made the town rich and prosperous.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Layaku_.28Durbar_Square.29">Layaku (Durbar Square)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhaktapur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Layaku (Durbar Square)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Bhaktapur_Durbar_Square" title="Bhaktapur Durbar Square">Bhaktapur Durbar Square</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bhaktapur_Durbar_Square" title="Bhaktapur Durbar Square">Bhaktapur Durbar Square</a> is a conglomeration of pagoda and shikhara-style <a href="/wiki/Temple" title="Temple">temples</a>, mostly dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> gods and goddesses grouped around a 55-window palace of brick and wood. The square is one of the most charming architectural showpieces of the valley as it highlights the ancient arts of Nepal. The golden effigies of the kings perched on the top of stone monoliths, the guardian deities looking out from their sanctuaries, the wood carvings in every place — struts, lintels, uprights, tympanums, gateways and windows — all seem to form a well-orchestrated symphony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Destination_Nepal_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Destination_Nepal-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The royal palace was originally situated at Dattaraya square and was only later moved to the Durbar square location. The square in Bhaktapur was severely damaged by an earthquake in 1934 and hence appears more spacious than the ones at <a href="/wiki/Kathmandu" title="Kathmandu">Kathmandu</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patan,_Nepal" title="Patan, Nepal">Patan</a>.</p>

<p><i>Nyatapola Temple'</i> is a 5-story <a href="/wiki/Pagoda" title="Pagoda">pagoda</a> located in Bhaktapur, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>. The temple was erected by Nepali King Bhupatindra Malla during a 5-month period from late 1701 into 1702. It is the temple of Siddha Laxmi, the Hindu goddess of prosperity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Destination_Nepal_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Destination_Nepal-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<p>This is another pagoda temple of lord <a href="/wiki/Bhairava" title="Bhairava">Bhairab</a>, the dreadful aspect of Lord <a href="/wiki/Shiva" title="Shiva">Shiva</a>. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Changu_Narayan">Changu Narayan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhaktapur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Changu Narayan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p><a href="/wiki/Changu_Narayan" title="Changu Narayan">Changu Narayan</a> is an ancient Hindu temple located near the village of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Changunarayan,_Nepal" title="Changunarayan, Nepal">Changunarayan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kathmandu_Valley" title="Kathmandu Valley">Kathmandu Valley</a> on top of a hill at the eastern end of the valley. It is 6 kilometres (3.7 mi) to the north of Bhakathapur and 22 kilometres (14 mi) from Kathmandu. The temple is one of the oldest Hindu temples of the valley, and is believed to have been constructed first in the 4th century. Changu Narayan is the name of <a href="/wiki/Vishnu" title="Vishnu">Vishnu</a>, and the temple is dedicated to him. A stone slab discovered in the vicinity of the temple dates to the 5th century, and is the oldest such stone inscription discovered in Nepal. It was rebuilt after the old temple was devastated. Many stone sculptures here date to the Licchavi period. Changu Narayan Temple is listed by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> as a World Heritage Site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Changu_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Changu-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The temple is a double-roofed structure where the idol of Lord <a href="/wiki/Vishnu" title="Vishnu">Vishnu</a> in his incarnation as <a href="/wiki/Narayana" title="Narayana">Narayana</a> is deified. The exquisitely built temple has intricate roof struts showing multi-armed <a href="/wiki/Tantra" title="Tantra">Tantric</a> deities. A kneeling image of <a href="/wiki/Garuda" title="Garuda">Garuda</a> (dated to the 5th century), the <a href="/wiki/Vahana" title="Vahana">vahana</a> or vehicle of Vishnu with a snake around its neck, faces the temple. The gilded door depicts stone lions guarding the temple. Gilded windows also flank the door. A conch and a disc, symbols of <a href="/wiki/Vishnu" title="Vishnu">Vishnu</a>, are carved on the two pillars at the entrance. Non-Hindus are not allowed inside the temple.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Changu_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Changu-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ta_Pukhu_.28Siddha_Pokhari.29">Ta Pukhu (Siddha Pokhari)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhaktapur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ta Pukhu (Siddha Pokhari)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

Siddha Pokhari</div>

<p>Ta Pukhu (Siddha Pokhari) is a big rectangular water pond near the main city gate. It was built during the reign of King Yakshya Malla in the early 15th century and is associated with a number of myths. From this spot a wide range of snowy peaks are visible on clear days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Destination_Nepal_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-Destination_Nepal-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kailashnath_Mahadev_Statue">Kailashnath Mahadev Statue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhaktapur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Kailashnath Mahadev Statue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kailashnath_Mahadev_Statue" title="Kailashnath Mahadev Statue">Kailashnath Mahadev Statue</a></div>
<p>Kailashnath Mahadev is the World's Tallest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_Shiva" title="Lord Shiva">Lord Shiva</a> statue. The height of this statue is 143 feet high and is situated 20 km from Kathmandu, epal.The statues construction work was started in 2004 and was completed in 2012.The statue's inauguration took place on the 21st of June'12. This statue stands on the 32nd position in the list of all statues by height in the whole world. It has been made of copper, cement, zinc, and steel. To make this gigantic structure possible there were many professional workers and statue makers from India.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhaktapur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bhaktapur_street_01.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2056" data-file-width="3088" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Bhaktapur_street_01.JPG/220px-Bhaktapur_street_01.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Bhaktapur_street_01.JPG/330px-Bhaktapur_street_01.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Bhaktapur_street_01.JPG/440px-Bhaktapur_street_01.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Bhaktapur is also known as city of festivals and celebrations. The city celebrates festivals each month starting from new year festival to the Holi puni at the end of the year.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bisket_Jatra" title="Bisket Jatra">Bisket Jatra</a> (New year festival)</li>
</ul>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Lyesingdya.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3264" data-file-width="1836" height="391" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Lyesingdya.jpg/220px-Lyesingdya.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Lyesingdya.jpg/330px-Lyesingdya.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Lyesingdya.jpg/440px-Lyesingdya.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Lyesingdya.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lingo erected in Bisket Jatra,2015</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This ancient annual festival of bhaktapur takes place at the New Year of the Bikram Sambat calendar. A few days before the New Year, usually at Chaitra 27 or Chaitra 28, if 31 days in month..,the goddess 'bhadrakali' and the god 'bhairab' are enshrined in their raths, or immense chariots,&amp; pulled through the narrow streets of Bhaktapur by crowds of young men.</p>
<p>The chariots rest at certain time-honored places in the city and people come out to throw offerings of flower, rice, coins and red sindur powder. On the last day of the old year a towering wooden pole known as ' lyesing dha: or lingo 'is erected at the edge of town. Long banners hang from the pole, symbolizing snakes..Nag and nagini.On New Year's Day, the erected pole is brought down symbolizing victory over evil !..People enjoy New year day along with victory over evils .On baisakh 5, the chariot is again pulled to narrow street of bhaktapur and brought to its origin place, Taumadhi square marking end of Bisket Jatra.</p>
<ul>
<li>Kumar Khasti (Sithi Nakha)</li>
</ul>
<p>Before modern piping system, wells and kuwas were the basis for water supply in bhaktapur. In this auspicious day, people clean wells and worship Naag favoring good fall in monsoon .They celebrate by eating delicious delicacy like bara and chatamari.* *</p>
<ul>
<li>Gathamaga charya</li>
</ul>
<p>Gathamaga is made up of straw that symbols a devil.Local make gathamaga in their locality and burn fire to remove devils of their locality.It is also said that mosquito loses its one leg in this day.</p>
<ul>
<li>Sa: Paaru (Gai Jatra)</li>
</ul>
<p>The celebration of Gai Jatra Bhaktapur is interesting among Kathmandu and Lalitpur. In this day, Tall bamboo poles wrapped in cloth and topped horn of straw and an umbrella is carried around the town in memory of dead.The photo of dead is hung in this pole called Ta macha.</p>
<p>Also A colorful procession known as Ghing tang gishi is main attraction of this festival.People enjoy and dance in beats of music and play with stick in accordance to the beat. People decorate themselves in mask, paints and even in female outfitsoutfits.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gunla" title="Gunla">Gunla</a></li>
</ul>
<p>Gunla is the name of 10th month of Nepal Era calendar.It is sacred month dedicated to Lord Buddha celebrated in Kathmandu, Patan and Bhaktapur</p>
<p>But In Bhaktapur, Last day of Gunla Panchadan is celebrated differently.The Five Buddhas are brought to Taumadhi square accompanied with Gunla Baja, a special musical instrument played only in Gunla and Buddhas are rounded around bhaktapur town</p>
<ul>
<li>Pulu Kisi (Indra Jatra)</li>
</ul>
<p>The son of Lord of heaven, is believed to have been kidnapped by a devil named Maisasur who then tied Indra's son in a pole and killed .Indra was shocked to hear his son's death and sent an elephant, pulukisi to find and kill Maisasur.But legend says Pulukisi couldn't find maisur till date.This jatra is a play to that legendary story where pulu kisi is rounded round the town to search for Maisasur.</p>
<ul>
<li>Sithi Nakha</li>
</ul>
<p>In Bhaktapur a palanquin with an image of the Hindu goddess Bhagawati is carried in colorful procession through Nyatapol square this day.</p>
<ul>
<li>Dhanya purnima (Yomari purnima)</li>
</ul>
<p>In this day Farmers of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kathmandu_valley" title="Kathmandu valley">Kathmandu valley</a> worship to Annapurna, the Goddess of grains.l for good rice harvest and enjoy feast after all the hard work of the season.<a href="/wiki/Yomari" title="Yomari">Yomari</a> is the chief item on the menu in this day.So is called Yomari puni.</p>
<ul>
<li>Maghe sankranti (Ghya-chaku sankranti) (Makar Sankranti)</li>
</ul>
<p>This festival marks the winter solstice and Newars partake of a feast .The days special. Menu include butter (ghyu), molasses (chaku) and yam.</p>
<p>In Bhaktapur, On this auspicious day samyak Dan is performed by Buddhist .The shakyas and Bajracharya gather in a samyak ground near Napukhu pond accompanied with panchabuddhas and samyak buddhas. They are offered chaku balls and rice.Bhaktapur is the only one in valley that conducts samyak Dan once in a year.</p>
<ul>
<li>Shree panchami</li>
</ul>
<p>People regard this day as propitious day for starting new enterprise. Devotee throng Devi shrine to seek blessing.In Bhaktapur, People visit Sashwo:dega in this auspicious day where the idol of Buddhist deity Mahamanjushree is kept.Hindus regard it as saraswoti and worship whereas Buddhist worship it as Manjushree.</p>

<p>Portions of the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Buddha" title="Little Buddha">Little Buddha</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Keanu_Reeves" title="Keanu Reeves">Keanu Reeves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bridget_Fonda" title="Bridget Fonda">Bridget Fonda</a> were filmed in the <a href="/wiki/Bhaktapur_Durbar_Square" title="Bhaktapur Durbar Square">Bhaktapur Durbar Square</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_earthquake">2015 earthquake</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bhaktapur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2015 earthquake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Magnitude 7.8 <a href="/wiki/April_2015_Nepal_earthquake" title="April 2015 Nepal earthquake">2015 Nepal earthquake</a> that struck on 25 April 2015 (12 Baisakh 2072 <a href="/wiki/Vikram_Samvat" title="Vikram Samvat">B.S.</a>, Saturday, at local time 11:56<span class="nowrap"> </span>am) damaged 116 heritages in the city. Of them, 67 were completely damaged while 49 suffered from partial damages.The quake has badly damaged the Durbar square, a significant heritage site included in the UNESCO world heritage list. The main premises of Taleju Temple here also witnessed damages in the disaster.</p>
<p>The Nepal–Bihar earthquake in 1934 demolished several buildings that were never rebuilt. Chyasilin Mandap has been rebuilt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
