<p><b>Park Hong-keun</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">박홍근</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">朴洪根</span>; born 8 October 1969) is a South Korean politician in the liberal <a href="/wiki/Minjoo_Party_of_Korea" title="Minjoo Party of Korea">Minjoo Party of Korea</a>, presently a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(South_Korea)" title="National Assembly (South Korea)">National Assembly</a> for <a href="/wiki/Jungnang_District" title="Jungnang District">Jungnang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, since 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Originally elected to the Assembly in the Jungnang B constituency by a margin of 854 votes—0.9 percent—over his <a href="/wiki/Saenuri_Party" title="Saenuri Party">Saenuri Party</a> competitor, Kang Dong-ho,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_legislative_election,_2016" title="South Korean legislative election, 2016">2016 election</a> he faced off another challenge from Kang, defeating him by a margin of 7.6 percent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Supporting <a href="/wiki/Chung_Dong-young" title="Chung Dong-young">Chung Dong-young</a>'s bid in the <a href="/wiki/South_Korean_presidential_election,_2007" title="South Korean presidential election, 2007">2007 presidential election</a>, Park chaired the <a href="/wiki/United_New_Democratic_Party" title="United New Democratic Party">United New Democratic Party</a>'s Youth Campaign that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He entered the Assembly in 2012. In September 2012, he submitted a legislative amendment allowing the government to rescind the licenses of businesses refusing to abide by local ordinances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Park attacked the <a href="/wiki/Park_Geun-hye" title="Park Geun-hye">Park Geun-hye</a> government for abetting, as he saw it, Japan's resumption of a right to collective self-defense under <a href="/wiki/Shinz%C5%8D_Abe" title="Shinzō Abe">Shinzō Abe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Subsequently, in the context of the controversy over the <a href="/wiki/Air_Defense_Identification_Zone_(East_China_Sea)" title="Air Defense Identification Zone (East China Sea)">Chinese Air Defense Identification Zone</a> in the <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China Sea</a>, in December that year he noted the importance of <a href="/wiki/Socotra_Rock" title="Socotra Rock">Socotra Rock</a>, an underwater reef disputed by South Korea and China. He stated that only two thirds of the middle- and high-school textbooks he had analyzed mentioned the reef, and called for textbooks to be revised to include information on it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Park received his undergraduate degree in Korean language at literature from <a href="/wiki/Kyung_Hee_University" title="Kyung Hee University">Kyung Hee University</a>, and went on to take a master's degree in public administration there. He was subsequently active in a number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civic_group" title="Civic group">civic groups</a>, serving as co-president of the Korea Youth Corps and director of Volunteering Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
