<p><b>Vannes</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[van]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Breton_language" title="Breton language">Breton</a>: <span lang="br" xml:lang="br"><i>Gwened</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France">commune</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Morbihan" title="Morbihan">Morbihan</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brittany_(administrative_region)" title="Brittany (administrative region)">Brittany</a> in north-western France. It was founded over 2000 years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vannes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vannes is located on the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Morbihan" title="Gulf of Morbihan">Gulf of Morbihan</a> at the mouth of two rivers, the Marle and the Vincin. It is around 100 kilometres (62 miles) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Nantes" title="Nantes">Nantes</a> and 450 km (280 mi) south west of Paris. Vannes is a market town and often linked to the sea.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vannes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Veneti_(Gaul)" title="Veneti (Gaul)">Veneti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bro_Gwened" title="Bro Gwened">Bro Gwened</a>.</div>
<p>The name <i>Vannes</i> comes from the <a href="/wiki/Veneti_(Gaul)" title="Veneti (Gaul)">Veneti</a>, a seafaring <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celtic</a> people who lived in the south-western part of <a href="/wiki/Armorica" title="Armorica">Armorica</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a> before the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> invasions. The region seems to have been involved in a cross channel trade for thousands of years, probably using hide boats and perhaps <a href="/wiki/Ferriby_Boats" title="Ferriby Boats">Ferriby Boats</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cunliffe_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cunliffe-2">[2]</a></sup> Wheat that apparently was grown in the Middle East was part of this trade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Balter_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Balter-3">[3]</a></sup> At about 150 BC the evidence of trade (such as Gallo-Belgic coins) with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thames_estuary" title="Thames estuary">Thames estuary</a> area of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> dramatically increased.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Veneti were defeated by <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a>'s fleet in 56 BC in front of <a href="/wiki/Locmariaquer" title="Locmariaquer">Locmariaquer</a>; many of the Veneti were then either slaughtered or sold into slavery. The Romans settled a town called <b>Darioritum</b> in a location previously belonging to the Veneti. From the 5th to the 7th century, the remaining <a href="/wiki/Gauls" title="Gauls">Gauls</a> were displaced or assimilated by waves of immigrant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britons_(Celtic_people)" title="Britons (Celtic people)">Britons</a> fleeing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saxon_invasions_of_Britain" title="Saxon invasions of Britain">Saxon invasions of Britain</a>. Under the <a href="/wiki/Breton_language" title="Breton language">Breton</a> name <b>Gwened</b> (also derived from the Veneti), the town was the center of an independent principality or kingdom variously called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bro-Wened" title="Bro-Wened">Bro-Wened</a> ("Vannes") or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bro-Ereg" title="Bro-Ereg">Bro-Ereg</a> ("land of <a href="/wiki/Waroch_I" title="Waroch I">Gwereg</a>"), the latter for a prominent member of its dynasty, which claimed descent from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caradog_Freichfras" title="Caradog Freichfras">Caradog Strongarm</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Vannes" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Vannes">diocese of Vannes</a> was erected in the 5th century. The Council of Vannes was held there in 461. The realm annexed <a href="/wiki/Cornouaille" title="Cornouaille">Cornouaille</a> for a time in the early 6th century but was permanently joined with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Domnonia" title="Domnonia">Domnonia</a> under its king and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Judica%C3%ABl" title="Saint Judicaël">saint Judicaël</a> around 635.</p>
<p>In 1342, Vannes was besieged four times between forces from both sides of the Breton War of Succession. The city's defending commander, <a href="/wiki/Olivier_de_Clisson" title="Olivier de Clisson">Olivier de Clisson</a> IV was captured by the English, but finally released. The French eventually executed him on suspicion that the ransom was unusually low and therefore he may have been a traitor.</p>
<p>In 1759 Vannes was used as the staging point for a <a href="/wiki/Planned_French_invasion_of_Britain_(1759)" title="Planned French invasion of Britain (1759)">planned French invasion of Britain</a>. A large army was assembled there, but it was never able to sail following the French naval defeat at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Quiberon_Bay" title="Battle of Quiberon Bay">Battle of Quiberon Bay</a> in November 1759.</p>
<p>In 1795, during the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>, French forces based in Vannes successfully repelled a planned British-Royalist <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_France_(1795)" title="Invasion of France (1795)">invasion</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vannes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Train</b><br/>
The <a href="/wiki/Gare_de_Vannes" title="Gare de Vannes">Gare de Vannes</a> railway station offers connections to <a href="/wiki/Quimper" title="Quimper">Quimper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rennes" title="Rennes">Rennes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nantes" title="Nantes">Nantes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and several regional destinations.<br/>
With the fast train <a href="/wiki/TGV" title="TGV">TGV</a>, the journey takes:<br/>
– 30 minutes to Lorient,<br/>
– 1 hour to Nantes or Rennes,<br/>
– 3.5 to 4 hours to Paris.<br/>
The <a href="/wiki/Transport_express_r%C3%A9gional" title="Transport express régional">Transport express régional</a> or TRE is a slower train to join railway stations in the close neighborhood, as <a href="/wiki/Auray" title="Auray">Auray</a> or <a href="/wiki/Questembert" title="Questembert">Questembert</a>.<br/>
There is no direct railway from Vannes to <a href="/wiki/Saint-Brieuc" title="Saint-Brieuc">Saint-Brieuc</a> (118 km away in the north of Brittany), so the train from Vannes to Saint Brieuc goes via Rennes, which doubles the travel time and cost: it takes 2 to 3 hours to go from Vannes to Saint Brieuc by train.</p>
<p><b>Car</b><br/>
Two highways, in the north of Vannes, provide fast connections by car:<br/>
– N165: drive west to Lorient (58 km) and Quimper (122 km), south east to Nantes (111 km)<br/>
– N166: drive north east to Rennes (113 km)<br/>
+ a network of small roads connects Vannes to smaller cities. There is no highway from Vannes to Saint-Brieuc, so the way to northern Brittany consists of small roads. The lack of highway or railway between Vannes and Saint-Brieuc (118 km north) cuts the communications between northern and southern Brittany, and limits Brittany economic performance.</p>
<p><b>Airplanes</b><br/>
Vannes has a small airfield in the village of <a href="/wiki/Monterblanc" title="Monterblanc">Monterblanc</a>, called Vannes-Meucon airport, or "Vannes – Golfe du Morbihan airport". It used to be a military airport, but it is now dedicated to general aviation aircraft. It belongs to Vannes Agglomeration community, the group of cities gathered around Vannes, and the main users of this airfield are Vannes flying club, the local <a href="/wiki/Ultralight_aviation" title="Ultralight aviation">ultralight aviation</a> club, and Vannes school of skydiving.</p>
<p><b>Bus</b><br/>
There are 2 bus networks in Vannes: – Kicéo, proposes short travels starting from Vannes Place de la Republique on behalf of Vannes Agglomeration community,<br/>
– CAT, propose longer travel starting from the railway station on behalf of Morbihan.<br/>
So there are 2 central bus stations in Vannes: one on Place de la Libération, the other at the railway station.</p>
<p><b>Bike</b><br/>
Vannes city has a public bicycle rental program, called Velocea based on the same idea as the Paris <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A9lib%27" title="Vélib'">Vélib'</a>. Hundreds of bicycles are available in 20 automated rental stations each with 10 to fifteen bikes/spaces. Each Velocea service station is equipped with an automatic rental terminal and stands for bicycles. The bicycles are robust and heavy 18 kilograms (40 lb), and the user can take a bike in any station, and let it in any station, for a cost as: free the first 4 hours, 1 euro the next 30 minutes, 2 euros per hour.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_fiction">In fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vannes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: In fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In the last of the Three Musketeers novels of <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Dumas" title="Alexandre Dumas">Alexandre Dumas</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Vicomte_of_Bragelonne:_Ten_Years_Later" title="The Vicomte of Bragelonne: Ten Years Later">The Vicomte of Bragelonne: Ten Years Later</a></i>, published in 1847, the musketeer <a href="/wiki/Aramis" title="Aramis">Aramis</a> appears as <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Vannes" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Vannes">bishop of Vannes</a> before becoming General of the Society of Jesus.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Roch" title="Sébastien Roch">Sébastien Roch</a></i>, a novel by <a href="/wiki/Octave_Mirbeau" title="Octave Mirbeau">Octave Mirbeau</a> published in 1890, Sebastien is sent to a school in Vannes, Saint-François-Xavier, where he is a victim of sexual abuse.</li>

He doesn't remain in Vannes, since after winning in another battle, the Black Prince dubs him a knight and Nigel returns to England to wed the Lady Mary.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jean-Fran%C3%A7ois_Parot" title="Jean-François Parot">Jean-François Parot</a> has written a series of crime fictions printed up to 2010 taking place in the 18th century, whose main character is Nicolas Le Floch, a Police Commissioner who was also educated in the school of Saint François-Xavier in Vannes, but he didn't share Sebastien Roch's misfortune. The Nicolas Le Floch novels have been adapted as a television series.</li>
<li>In <i>The Secret Of The Missing Boat</i>, a children's book by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Berna" title="Paul Berna">Paul Berna</a> published in 1966 as <i>La Voile Rouge</i>.</li>
<li>In "Charlemagne and Florent," a short story by Ranylt Richildis published in 2014 by <i>Myths Inscribed</i>.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vannes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The local <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> team is <a href="/wiki/Vannes_OC" title="Vannes OC">Vannes OC</a>, members of the Championnat de France de <a href="/wiki/Ligue_2" title="Ligue 2">Ligue 2</a> for the 2009-10 season.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Club_Vannes" title="Rugby Club Vannes">Rugby Club Vannes</a> is the rugby union team and competed in Pro D2 for the 2015-16 season.</p>
<p>Both teams play at the <a href="/wiki/Stade_de_la_Rabine" title="Stade de la Rabine">Stade de la Rabine</a> built in 2001.</p>
