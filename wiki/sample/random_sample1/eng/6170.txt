<p><i><b>Van Helsing</b></i> is a 2004 American <a href="/wiki/Dark_fantasy" title="Dark fantasy">dark fantasy</a> <a href="/wiki/Action_film" title="Action film">action</a>-<a href="/wiki/Adventure_film" title="Adventure film">adventure</a> <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Sommers" title="Stephen Sommers">Stephen Sommers</a>. It stars <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Jackman" title="Hugh Jackman">Hugh Jackman</a> as <a href="/wiki/Vigilante" title="Vigilante">vigilante</a> monster hunter <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Van_Helsing" title="Abraham Van Helsing">Van Helsing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kate_Beckinsale" title="Kate Beckinsale">Kate Beckinsale</a> as Anna Valerious. The film is an homage and tribute to the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Monsters" title="Universal Monsters">Universal Horror Monster</a> films from the 1930s and 1940s (also produced by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Studios" title="Universal Studios">Universal Studios</a> which were in turn based on novels by <a href="/wiki/Bram_Stoker" title="Bram Stoker">Bram Stoker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mary_Shelley" title="Mary Shelley">Mary Shelley</a>), of which Sommers is a fan.</p>
<p>The eponymous character was inspired by the Dutch vampire hunter <i>Abraham Van Helsing</i> from Irish author Bram Stoker's novel <i><a href="/wiki/Dracula" title="Dracula">Dracula</a></i>. Distributed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Pictures" title="Universal Pictures">Universal Pictures</a>, the film includes a number of monsters such as <a href="/wiki/Count_Dracula" title="Count Dracula">Count Dracula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frankenstein%27s_monster" title="Frankenstein's monster">Frankenstein's monster</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Werewolves" title="Werewolves">werewolves</a> in a way similar to the multi-monster movies that Universal produced in the 1940s, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Frankenstein_Meets_the_Wolf_Man" title="Frankenstein Meets the Wolf Man">Frankenstein Meets the Wolf Man</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/House_of_Dracula" title="House of Dracula">House of Dracula</a></i>.</p>
<p>Despite mostly negative reviews, the film grossed over $300 million worldwide.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Van_Helsing_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1887 <a href="/wiki/Transylvania" title="Transylvania">Transylvania</a>, Doctor <a href="/wiki/Victor_Frankenstein" title="Victor Frankenstein">Victor Frankenstein</a> creates a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankenstein%27s_Monster" title="Frankenstein's Monster">monster</a> with the anticipated aid of <a href="/wiki/Count_Dracula" title="Count Dracula">Count Dracula</a>, who reveals that he plans to use the creature for his own evil plans and kills the doctor as his castle is raided by a mob of villagers. His assistant <a href="/wiki/Igor_(character)" title="Igor (character)">Igor</a> escapes, but the villagers chase the monster to an old windmill and set it ablaze. The villagers are scared off by Dracula and his brides, who witness the monster and the doctor's research apparently destroyed by the fire.</p>
<p>One year later, after killing the elusive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mr._Hyde" title="Mr. Hyde">Mr. Hyde</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Notre_Dame_de_Paris" title="Notre Dame de Paris">Notre-Dame Cathedral</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, monster hunter Van Helsing returns to the <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>. He learns his mission to destroy Dracula and his <a href="/wiki/Amnesia" title="Amnesia">amnesia</a> are penance for past, unremembered sins that cause nightmares. He is tasked with helping the last of an ancient Romanian bloodline, Velkan Valerious and his sister Anna, who must kill Dracula before their ancestors may enter Heaven. Carl, a <a href="/wiki/Friar" title="Friar">friar</a>, provides Van Helsing with aid and weapons. Meanwhile, in Transylvania, Velkan is apparently killed during an attempt to trap and kill a <a href="/wiki/Werewolf" title="Werewolf">werewolf</a>, and Anna is attacked by Dracula's brides shortly after Van Helsing's arrival. He saves her life and kills Marishka, one of the brides, but as the last of her line Anna becomes more determined to kill Dracula. In order to protect her, Van Helsing <a href="/wiki/Sleeping_gas" title="Sleeping gas">knocks her out</a> to prevent her from leaving. Later that night, she awakens and encounters Velkan, who reveals Dracula has a dark secret, but to her horror, he becomes a werewolf and attacks. After she and Van Helsing track him to Frankenstein's castle, they discover that the vampires are attempting to give life to their born-dead children, using Velkan as the source of the power. Van Helsing, dubbed "<a href="/wiki/Gabriel" title="Gabriel">Gabriel</a>" by Dracula, realizes they had ties in the past. Van Helsing refuses Dracula's offer to return his memory and escapes. The experiment fails however, and the vampire children die, but Velkan turns on Anna as he becomes completely consumed by his curse. The pair then escape together.</p>
<p>Under the remains of the old windmill, Van Helsing and Anna encounter the monster. Because he is not evil, Van Helsing cannot allow it to be killed, even though he claims to be the key to the failed experiment. The werewolf witnesses this and escapes to inform Dracula. Meanwhile, Carl discovers a painting that comes to life, depicting a werewolf and a vampire battling. They travel by carriage to <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, successfully luring the remaining brides into a trap and killing Verona. Velkan ambushes the real carriage and bites Van Helsing before being shot and killed. Anna is captured and held as a bargaining chip to exchange her for the monster. They hide the monster in a crypt, but he is taken by the count's undead underlings while Van Helsing and Carl rescue Anna from the count's trap. Returning to the Valerious' castle, Carl discovers an inscription, part of which Van Helsing received in Rome and creates a doorway to the castle. After failing to free the monster from his imprisonment, he lets them know of a cure to lycanthropy that Dracula possesses. Carl determines that the bite of a werewolf is the only way to kill Dracula. He and Anna take Igor to find the cure while Van Helsing goes to free the monster. Igor escapes while the final bride, Aleera, assaults Anna as Carl tries to deliver the cure to Van Helsing. After escaping, the monster saves Anna and urges her to help Carl and Van Helsing. She kills Aleera and arrives at the castle. Meanwhile, the werewolf Van Helsing and Dracula battle. Dracula tries to reason with the werewolf, but Van Helsing bites Dracula, who dissolves into a skeleton. Anna bursts in, causing her to be attacked and accidentally killed by Van Helsing, but not before she delivers the cure. Van Helsing returns to normal, stricken with grief over what he has done.</p>
<p>At a pyre funeral, Van Helsing witnesses the spirits of Anna and her family ascending into the clouds while Frankenstein's monster rows away on a raft out to sea. Van Helsing and Carl ride off into the sunset.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Van_Helsing_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film opened at #1 in the weekend of May 7–9, 2004. The film grossed US$300,257,475 worldwide of which US$120,177,084 was from the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Van_Helsing_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Van Helsing</i> received mostly negative reviews by critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a>, reports that 23% of 213 surveyed critics gave the film a positive review; the average rating was 4.2/10. The site's consensus reads: "A hollow creature feature that suffers from CGI overload."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> rated it 35/100 based on 38 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun_Times" title="Chicago Sun Times">Chicago Sun Times</a></i> gave the film 3 stars out of 4 stating that "At the outset, we may fear Sommers is simply going for f/x overkill, but by the end, he has somehow succeeded in assembling all his monsters and plot threads into a high-voltage climax. <i>Van Helsing</i> is silly, spectacular and fun."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Van_Helsing_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Van_Helsing_(soundtrack)" title="Van Helsing (soundtrack)">film's original soundtrack</a> was composed by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Silvestri" title="Alan Silvestri">Alan Silvestri</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Merchandise">Merchandise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Van_Helsing_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Merchandise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Board_games">Board games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Van_Helsing_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Board games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Van Helsing appears in a board game<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> designed by Fréderic Moyersoen the creator of numerous successful board games such as <a href="/wiki/Saboteur_(card_game)" title="Saboteur (card game)">Saboteur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The board game was released by <a href="/wiki/Mayfair_Games" title="Mayfair Games">Mayfair Games</a> in 2010.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Van_Helsing_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Vivendi_Games" title="Vivendi Games">Vivendi Universal Games</a> published a <a href="/wiki/Van_Helsing_(video_game)" title="Van Helsing (video game)"><i>Van Helsing</i> video game</a> for <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xbox_(console)" title="Xbox (console)">Xbox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">PC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Game_Boy_Advance" title="Game Boy Advance">Game Boy Advance</a>. The game follows a similar plot to the movie, has gameplay similar to <i><a href="/wiki/Devil_May_Cry" title="Devil May Cry">Devil May Cry</a></i> and the PS2 and Xbox versions feature the voice talent of many of the actors including <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Jackman" title="Hugh Jackman">Hugh Jackman</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Slot_games">Slot games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Van_Helsing_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Slot games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Van Helsing also features in a slot game produced by <a href="/wiki/International_Game_Technology" title="International Game Technology">International Game Technology</a>. The game is available in real world casinos and online, though users in Argentina, Australia, Canada, China, France, Germany, Israel, Italy, Netherlands, Nigeria, Norway, Russia, South Africa, Thailand, Turkey and the US are excluded from playing the online games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spin-offs">Spin-offs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Van_Helsing_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Spin-offs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sommers expanded the story of Van Helsing in two direct spin-offs:</p>
<ul>
<li>The animated prequel titled <i><a href="/wiki/Van_Helsing:_The_London_Assignment" title="Van Helsing: The London Assignment">Van Helsing: The London Assignment</a></i> takes place before the main events of the film, focusing on Van Helsing's mission to try to stop <a href="/wiki/Jack_the_Ripper" title="Jack the Ripper">Jack the Ripper</a> from terrorizing London. But the killer turns out to be Mr. Hyde.</li>
<li>There is also a one-shot comic book, published by <a href="/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics" title="Dark Horse Comics">Dark Horse Comics</a>, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Van_Helsing:_From_Beneath_the_Rue_Morgue" title="Van Helsing: From Beneath the Rue Morgue">Van Helsing: From Beneath the Rue Morgue</a></i>, that follows Van Helsing on a self-contained adventure that occurs during the events of the film, just after the death of Jekyll/Hyde in Paris but before Van Helsing returned to Rome. In the adventure, Van Helsing deals with <a href="/wiki/The_Island_of_Doctor_Moreau" title="The Island of Doctor Moreau">Doctor Moreau</a> and his hybrid mutants.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reboot_and_shared_universe">Reboot and shared universe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Van_Helsing_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reboot and shared universe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 2012, Universal Pictures announced that they are <a href="/wiki/Reboot_(fiction)" title="Reboot (fiction)">rebooting</a> the film with <a href="/wiki/Alex_Kurtzman" title="Alex Kurtzman">Alex Kurtzman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Orci" title="Roberto Orci">Roberto Orci</a> as a two-year deal to produce a modern reimagining and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Cruise" title="Tom Cruise">Tom Cruise</a> to star as the title character and also produce the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Sanders" title="Rupert Sanders">Rupert Sanders</a> is in talks to direct the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Orci spoken to <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> that he has hinted that both <i>The Mummy</i> and <i>Van Helsing</i> reboots will have a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Monsters_Cinematic_Universe" title="Universal Monsters Cinematic Universe">shared universe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On November 11, 2015, Variety reports that <a href="/wiki/Jon_Spaihts" title="Jon Spaihts">Jon Spaihts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Heisserer" title="Eric Heisserer">Eric Heisserer</a> will write the reboot, but Cruise left the film. However, in 2016, Cruise signed back on to star in Kurtzman's, "The Mummy", it is set to be released in theaters on June 9, 2017. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
