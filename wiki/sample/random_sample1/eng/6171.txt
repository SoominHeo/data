<p><b>Van Gelder's bat</b> (<i>Bauerus dubiaquercus</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Vesper_bat" title="Vesper bat">vesper bat</a> in the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vespertilionidae" title="Vespertilionidae">Vespertilionidae</a>. It is found in <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">Belize</a>, <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. The species is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monotypic" title="Monotypic">monotypic</a> within its <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simmons_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Simmons-2">[2]</a></sup> It is part of the tribe <a href="/wiki/Antrozoini" title="Antrozoini">Antrozoini</a> within the subfamily <a href="/wiki/Vespertilioninae" title="Vespertilioninae">Vespertilioninae</a> and is related to the <a href="/wiki/Pallid_bat" title="Pallid bat">pallid bat</a> (<i>Antrozous pallidus</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The bat is found in forest habitat from sea level to elevations as high as 2300 m, although not usually above 1300 m, and is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insectivorous" title="Insectivorous">insectivorous</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crepuscular" title="Crepuscular">crepuscular</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">[1]</a></sup> It apparently has a fragmented distribution, and is threatened by <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<p>The bat was discovered by Richard Van Gelder, then curator of mammalogy at the <a href="/wiki/American_Museum_of_Natural_History" title="American Museum of Natural History">American Museum of Natural History</a>. The bat was collected on the AMNH Puritan Expedition to Baja California in 1957<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> on the <a href="/wiki/Islas_Mar%C3%ADas" title="Islas Marías">Tres Maria Islands</a> (south of Baja) by Richard Zweifel (expedition herpetologist) and Oakes Plimpton (expedition assistant). Van Gelder dubbed the bat "dubiaquercus" in honor of the collectors: <i>dubia</i> means "doubt" in Latin, as <i>zweifel</i> does in German; <i>quercus</i> is Latin for "oak".</p>
