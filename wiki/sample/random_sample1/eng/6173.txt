<p><b>Bandai</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">磐梯町</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Bandai-machi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Towns_of_Japan" title="Towns of Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Yama_District,_Fukushima" title="Yama District, Fukushima">Yama District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fukushima_Prefecture" title="Fukushima Prefecture">Fukushima Prefecture</a>, in northern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honsh%C5%AB" title="Honshū">Honshū</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As of September 2014, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 3,599 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> of 60.3 persons per km². The total area was 59.69 km².</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bandai,_Fukushima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bandai is located at the far northern <a href="/wiki/Aizu" title="Aizu">Aizu</a> region of Fukushima Prefecture, bordered by <a href="/wiki/Yamagata_Prefecture" title="Yamagata Prefecture">Yamagata Prefecture</a> to the north. The climate is like many other parts of northern Japan, with cold winters and snowfall averaging 2 meters.</p>
<ul>
<li>Mountains : <a href="/wiki/Mount_Bandai" title="Mount Bandai">Mount Bandai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mount_Nekomadake" title="Mount Nekomadake">Mount Nekomadake</a></li>
<li>Rivers : Nippashi-gawa, Oya-gawa</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bandai,_Fukushima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area of present-day Bandai was part of ancient <a href="/wiki/Mutsu_Province" title="Mutsu Province">Mutsu Province</a>. It was the center of a <a href="/wiki/Sacred_mountains" title="Sacred mountains">mountain cult</a> based on Mount Bandai and numerous Buddhist temples were founded from the <a href="/wiki/Heian_period" title="Heian period">Heian period</a>. These temples grew to have a strong military and economic power towards the <a href="/wiki/Kamakura_period" title="Kamakura period">Kamakura period</a>, and were only suppressed in the <a href="/wiki/Sengoku_period" title="Sengoku period">Sengoku period</a>. The area formed part of the holdings of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aizu_Domain" title="Aizu Domain">Aizu Domain</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a>, it was organized as part of <a href="/wiki/Yama_District,_Fukushima" title="Yama District, Fukushima">Yama District</a>.</p>
<p>Modern Bandai village was created on March 31, 1889 with the establishment of the municipalities system, and was raised to town status in 1960.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bandai,_Fukushima&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From the early modern period, hydroelectric power generation and chemical production by <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Soda_Co.,_Ltd." title="Nippon Soda Co., Ltd.">Nippon Soda Co., Ltd.</a> had been mainstays of the local economy. However, the local economy has now shifted to precision machinery production and seasonal tourism, especially golf courses and ski resorts.</p>
