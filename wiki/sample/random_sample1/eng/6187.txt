<p><b>BanYa</b> (반야), sometimes spelled <b>BANYA</b> or <b>Banya</b>, is South Korean <a href="/wiki/Andamiro" title="Andamiro">Andamiro</a>'s musical group responsible for creating original songs for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Pump_it_Up_video_games" title="List of Pump it Up video games">Pump It Up</a>. The style of its music varies greatly, from <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> to <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crossover_(music)#Classical_crossover" title="Crossover (music)">classical crossovers</a>.</p>
<p>Classical remixes are among Banya's most popular productions. Several sonatas, symphonies and other pieces feature in different versions. Mixing violins, guitars and heavy beats, these songs draw particular attention from players and passers-by. BanYa also composes original music including <a href="/wiki/Trance_music" title="Trance music">trance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Techno" title="Techno">techno</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hardcore_techno" title="Hardcore techno">hardcore</a> and ambient breaks.</p>
<p>Beginning in NX, former BanYa member Yahpp became a solo artist and in turn his new music became credited to him. Starting in Fiesta, msgoon, another former member, did the same. All other former members, starting on NX, became credited as "BanYa Production". For the consistency of the article, all songs by the original BanYa collective are listed here.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Song_catalogue">Song catalogue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BanYa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Song catalogue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The group's first two releases under the name of Banya were Ignition Starts and Hypnosis, although Bee, Solitary and The Final Audition had been already recorded by Yahpp as an independent artist. Up to 2004 they released 3 albums, however some nonstop remixes of several BanYa songs have also been made for Pump It Up.</p>
<p>On Exceed 2, <i>Radezky Can Can</i> was moved from the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K-Pop" title="K-Pop">K-Pop</a> Channel" to the "BanYa Channel", even though it was made by F2 System, who worked with Andamiro to make Extra. <i>Holiday</i>, the other F2 song, has only showed up on Extra, The Prex and The Prex 2.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_1st_Step_to_the_Banya">The 1st Step to the Banya</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BanYa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: The 1st Step to the Banya">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This album comprised all their songs from "Pump It Up: the 1st Dance Floor" to "The OBG SE", excluding Creamy Skinny and Koul, from the 2nd Dance Floor. It also included an electric guitar version of Ignition Starts instead of the original hardcore version, a version of Hate sung by Pp (the same girl who sang Pumping Up) and a completely new song, a ballad entitled Warm Shadow in a Stranger's Eyes (rough translation, as it's very hard to translate this title from Korean). All the songs that existed in a version longer than the one appearing in the arcade were included in their original versions, except The Final Audition.</p>
