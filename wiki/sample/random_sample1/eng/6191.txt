<p>The <b>New Anticapitalist Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Nouveau Parti anticapitaliste</i></span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[nuvo paʁti ɑ̃tikapitaˈlist]</a></span>, NPA) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Far-left" title="Far-left">far-left</a> <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> founded in February 2009. Its name was originally intended to be temporary; a vote on the name being held at the founding congress on 6–8 February 2009, where NPA won over "Revolutionary Anticapitalist Party" (<i>Parti anticapitaliste révolutionnaire</i>) with 53% of the vote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The party launched with 9,200 members and was intended to unify the fractured movements of the French radical Left, and attract new activists drawing on the combined strength of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Far-left" title="Far-left">far-left</a> parties in <a href="/wiki/French_presidential_election,_2002" title="French presidential election, 2002">presidential elections in 2002</a>, where they achieved 10.44% of the vote. (Being 7% in <a href="/wiki/French_presidential_election,_2007" title="French presidential election, 2007">2007</a> and 13% in <a href="/wiki/French_presidential_election,_2012" title="French presidential election, 2012">2012</a>).</p>
<p>The party is closely associated with postal worker <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Besancenot" title="Olivier Besancenot">Olivier Besancenot</a>, the main spokesman of the former strongest far left party, the <a href="/wiki/Revolutionary_Communist_League_(France)" title="Revolutionary Communist League (France)">Revolutionary Communist League</a> (LCR). In March 2011, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Myriam_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Myriam Martin (page does not exist)">Myriam Martin</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Christine_Poupin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Christine Poupin (page does not exist)">Christine Poupin</a> were elected the main spokespersons of the NPA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spokespersons_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-spokespersons-1">[1]</a></sup> In May 2012, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Myriam_Martin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Myriam Martin (page does not exist)">Myriam Martin</a> supported the candidate of the <a href="/wiki/Left_Front_(France)" title="Left Front (France)">Left Front</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Luc_M%C3%A9lenchon" title="Jean-Luc Mélenchon">Jean-Luc Mélenchon</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_presidential_election" title="French presidential election">2012 presidential election</a> against the candidate of the NPA, a worker and union activist at Ford's car plant in Bordeaux <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Poutou" title="Philippe Poutou">Philippe Poutou</a>, who came eighth in the first round with 411,160 votes, 1.15% of the total votes. She left the NPA in July 2012.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding_conference">Founding conference</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Anticapitalist_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Founding conference">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the founding conference (6 to 8 February 2009), 630 delegates voted on a series of documents, which had gone through a long process of amendment and re-amendment in local and regional assemblies.</p>
<ul>
<li>The first document "Founding principles" detailed the party's analysis of the impasse of capitalism and the need for both mass mobilization, and, in the long term, the overthrowing of existing institutions.</li>
<li>The second document was the provisional rulebook, which will remain in effect until the next conference.</li>
<li>The third document was the Perspectives document, which attempted to set out priorities for the year ahead, and key demands to be pushed for in the immediate future.</li>
<li>Finally, a document on the European elections expressed the attitude of the party towards the June European elections.</li>
</ul>
<p>However, a number of contentious issues within the party - especially those relating to secularism, religion and islamophobia - were left open for further debate, leading to a number of difficulties and a tendency to devote considerable time and energy on internal debates, rather than activity.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Anticapitalist_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Besancenot has stated that it has no single leader and is instead run collectively, being represented by elected spokespersons.</p>
<p>The basic structure of the party is the local committee, which organises local activities. A National Political Council decides on general policy. Delegates to the Council are elected at the congress on a proportional basis, ensuring the representation of different tendencies or 'platforms'.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ideals">Ideals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Anticapitalist_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ideals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The party's stated aim is to "build a new socialist, democratic perspective for the twenty-first century".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Olivier Besancenot has said that the party will be "the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left_(politics)" title="Left (politics)">left</a> that fights <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anticapitalism" title="Anticapitalism">anticapitalist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Internationalism_(politics)" title="Internationalism (politics)">internationalist</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antiracist" title="Antiracist">antiracist</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecologist" title="Ecologist">ecologist</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feminist" title="Feminist">feminist</a> struggles, opposing all forms of <a href="/wiki/Discrimination" title="Discrimination">discrimination</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The LCR's distinctive identification with <a href="/wiki/Trotskyism" title="Trotskyism">Trotskyism</a> will not be continued by the NPA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unlike in previous LCR documents, although feminism is very present, patriarchy theory is not mentioned. Such issues as the withdrawal of French troops from <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, a rise in the <a href="/wiki/Minimum_wage" title="Minimum wage">minimum wage</a> and public services, however, are accepted by all members of the NPA.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alliances_and_splits">Alliances and splits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Anticapitalist_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Alliances and splits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Debate continues within the NPA about what sort of alliances with other groups are acceptable. The majority of militants emphasize the dangers of allying with forces which are likely to end up in joint local or national governments with the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_(France)" title="Socialist Party (France)">Socialist Party</a> (PS). A minority believes there is work to be done in wide alliances with antiliberal parties of the left, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Party_of_the_Left_(France)" title="Party of the Left (France)">Party of the Left</a> (PG).</p>
<p>In January 2009, the NPA signed a joint declaration with several other parties of the left, calling for the building of the January 29 national strike. A minority (16%) claimed that such unity in the <a href="/wiki/Strike_action" title="Strike action">strike</a> movements means sufficient basis can be found for joint slates at the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2009_(France)" title="European Parliament election, 2009 (France)">European elections</a>, while the majority made a sharp distinction between alliances for social movements and electoral alliances. The party received 4.98% of the vote in the European election.</p>
<p>Discussions were held in the course of 2009 with other parties to the left of the PS, concerning the possibility of joint slates for the <a href="/wiki/French_regional_elections,_2010" title="French regional elections, 2010">regional elections in 2010</a>. Finally, the NPA presented independent lists in a majority of regions, but joined the <a href="/wiki/Left_Front_(France)" title="Left Front (France)">Left Front</a> in three regions, and the <a href="/wiki/Left_Party_(France)" title="Left Party (France)">Left Party</a> in two others.</p>
<p>Since its foundation, the NPA has suffered a number of breakaways, and by the beginning of 2015 total membership had declined to 2,100, compared with 9,200 at the time of its Founding Conference in February 2009. In addition to the loss of individual members, three organised groups have left the party in order to join the Left Front: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gauche_Unitaire" title="Gauche Unitaire">Gauche Unitaire</a> in 2009, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Convergences_et_Alternative&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Convergences et Alternative (page does not exist)">Convergences et Alternative</a> in 2011 and the largest of the three, <a href="/wiki/Anticapitalist_Left_(France)" title="Anticapitalist Left (France)">Anticapitalist left</a> (Gauche Anticapitaliste) in July 2012.</p>
<p>In addition, failure to reach the required level of support in the presidential and parliamentary elections has deprived the party of state funding, leading to a financial crisis. As a result of these setbacks, the NPA is engaged in an internal debate with the aim of refounding the party and reforming its internal structures. However, the NPA continues to be active in various social movements. It produces a weekly newspaper, <i>Tout est à nous !</i> (originating as a slogan to be chanted on demonstrations, the name roughly translates as 'Everything belongs to us !') and a monthly magazine of the same name.</p>
<p>In December 2013, the Revolutionary Marxists faction in the <a href="/wiki/Fourth_International_(post-reunification)" title="Fourth International (post-reunification)">Fourth International</a> declared the Anticapitalism and Revolution current in the NPA and criticized the reformist orientation of the party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_Anticapitalist_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party became the target of criticism after <a href="/wiki/Ilham_Moussa%C3%AFd" title="Ilham Moussaïd">Ilham Moussaïd</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> woman wearing a <a href="/wiki/Hijab" title="Hijab">hijab</a>, became a candidate for <a href="/wiki/French_regional_elections,_2010" title="French regional elections, 2010">2010 regional elections</a>. Supporters of the party were noted to chant slogans against <a href="/wiki/Islamophobia" title="Islamophobia">Islamophobia</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and the party's move was considered by some as "radical pragmatism".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
