<p>In <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a>, a <b>hundred twenty-eighth note</b> or <b>semihemidemisemiquaver</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Musical_note" title="Musical note">note</a> played for <span class="nowrap">‚Ää</span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>‚ÅÑ<sub>128</sub></span> of the duration of a <a href="/wiki/Whole_note" title="Whole note">whole note</a>. It lasts half as long as a <a href="/wiki/Sixty-fourth_note" title="Sixty-fourth note">sixty-fourth note</a>. It has a total of five flags or <a href="/wiki/Beam_(music)" title="Beam (music)">beams</a>.</p>
<p>Notes this short are very rare in printed music, but not unknown. They are principally used for brief, rapid sections in slow <a href="/wiki/Movement_(music)" title="Movement (music)">movements</a>. For example, they occur in the first movement of <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_van_Beethoven" title="Ludwig van Beethoven">Beethoven</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Piano_Sonata_No._8_(Beethoven)" title="Piano Sonata No. 8 (Beethoven)"><i>Path√©tique</i> Piano Sonata (Op. 13)</a>, to notate rapid <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Musical_scale" title="Musical scale">scales</a>. Another example is in Mozart's Variations on <i>Je suis lindor</i>, where many of them are used in the slow twelfth variation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>These five-beamed notes also appear occasionally where a passage is to be performed rapidly, but where the actual <a href="/wiki/Tempo" title="Tempo">tempo</a> is at the discretion of the performer rather than being a strict division of the <a href="/wiki/Beat_(music)" title="Beat (music)">beat</a>. In such cases, the aggregate time of the notes may not add up exactly to a full measure, and the phrase may be marked with an odd time division to indicate this. Sometimes such notation is made using smaller notes, sized like grace notes. One rare instance where such five-beamed notes occur as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acciacatura" title="Acciacatura">acciacaturas</a> occurs in the final measures of No. 2 of <a href="/wiki/Charles-Valentin_Alkan" title="Charles-Valentin Alkan">Charles-Valentin Alkan</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Trois_grandes_%C3%A9tudes" title="Trois grandes √©tudes">Trois grandes √©tudes</a></i>, Op. 76.</p>
<p><b>Hundred twenty-eighth <a href="/wiki/Rest_(music)" title="Rest (music)">rests</a></b> are also rare, but again not unknown. One is used in <a href="/wiki/Piano_Sonata_No._13_(Beethoven)" title="Piano Sonata No. 13 (Beethoven)">Beethoven's Piano Sonata No. 13 "Quasi una fantasia"</a> (b. 24 in the slow movement).</p>
