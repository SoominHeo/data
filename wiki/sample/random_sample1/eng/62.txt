<p><b>...Baby One More Time Tour</b> was the debut <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">concert tour</a> by American recording artist <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>. It supported her debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_(album)" title="...Baby One More Time (album)">...Baby One More Time</a></i> (1999) and visited United States and Canada. The tour was announced in March 1999, with dates released a month later. <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Hilfiger" title="Tommy Hilfiger">Tommy Hilfiger</a> was chosen as the tour sponsor. The show was divided into various segments, with each segment being followed by an interlude to the next segment, and it ended with an encore. The setlist consisted of songs from her debut album and several <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">covers</a>. The tour received positive feedback from critics; many highlighted Spears' persona and edgy look.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_development">Background and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=...Baby_One_More_Time_Tour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On March 5, 1999, it was reported that Spears was planning her first headlining tour to support her first studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_(album)" title="...Baby One More Time (album)">...Baby One More Time</a></i> (1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Shortly after, she announced that the tour would start in July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tuesday_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tuesday-2">[2]</a></sup> On May 12, 1999, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Hilfiger" title="Tommy Hilfiger">Tommy Hilfiger</a> was announced as the main tour sponsor. During the time of the announcement, Spears was being featured in the "AllStars" campaign launched by the company. Hilfiger spoke about the sponsorship saying,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"My passion for music has always inspired my designs. This year we have really put music in the forefront of everything we do. Britney represents the spirit of Tommy Jeans and of youth today. I cannot think of a better way to continue this exciting year by sponsoring one of today's hottest, young recording artists".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tommy_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-tommy-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The secondary sponsor was supposed to be <a href="/wiki/Nestl%C3%A9" title="Nestlé">Nestlé</a>, but they pulled out soon after provocative photographs of Spears shot by <a href="/wiki/David_LaChapelle" title="David LaChapelle">David LaChapelle</a> were published in <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nestle_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nestle-4">[4]</a></sup> Tour dates were released through <a href="/wiki/Pollstar" title="Pollstar">Pollstar</a> on April 9, 1999, with the tour kicking off on June 28, 1999, in <a href="/wiki/Pompano_Beach,_Florida" title="Pompano Beach, Florida">Pompano Beach, Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-schedule_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-schedule-5">[5]</a></sup> Several dates were added and rescheduled, and the complete schedule was released two months later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fullschedule_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-fullschedule-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Spears talked to <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a> about her involvement during the development of the tour, stating that she had designed the entire tour herself, including costumes and concept.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sayso_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-sayso-7">[7]</a></sup> Spears worked with fashion designer <a href="/wiki/Gia_Ventola" title="Gia Ventola">Gia Ventola</a> to create the costumes for her and the dancers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gia_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-gia-8">[8]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Proscenium" title="Proscenium">proscenium</a> stage was simple and had only one main prop, a staircase in the middle. The band was in both sides of the staircase and consisted of five musicians. There were also six dancers, that took the stage during interludes. The setlist consisted of eight songs from her debut album and several covers by well-known artists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concert_synopsis">Concert synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=...Baby_One_More_Time_Tour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Concert synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The show began with a dance introduction by Spears' dancers among smoke effects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-10">[10]</a></sup> She appeared shortly after at the top of the staircase wearing a hot pink vinyl <a href="/wiki/Tube_top" title="Tube top">tube top</a> and white vinyl pants with pink knee patches, to perform "<a href="/wiki/(You_Drive_Me)_Crazy" title="(You Drive Me) Crazy">(You Drive Me) Crazy</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-10">[10]</a></sup> In "Soda Pop" she danced and interacted with the audience, before leaving the stage while her dancers continued. She appeared sitting on the staircase to sing "<a href="/wiki/Born_to_Make_You_Happy" title="Born to Make You Happy">Born to Make You Happy</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/From_the_Bottom_of_My_Broken_Heart" title="From the Bottom of My Broken Heart">From the Bottom of My Broken Heart</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-10">[10]</a></sup> The show continued with a dance interlude set to <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Vogue_(Madonna_song)" title="Vogue (Madonna song)">Vogue</a>" in which she named Madonna and <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">Janet Jackson</a> as her biggest inspirations. She then took the stage to perform a <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover</a> of Madonna's "<a href="/wiki/Material_Girl" title="Material Girl">Material Girl</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-10">[10]</a></sup> After this, she performed two covers of Janet Jackson songs, "<a href="/wiki/Black_Cat_(song)" title="Black Cat (song)">Black Cat</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nasty_(song)" title="Nasty (song)">Nasty</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-10">[10]</a></sup> She ended the section with a performance of <a href="/wiki/Sonny_%26_Cher" title="Sonny &amp; Cher">Sonny &amp; Cher</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/The_Beat_Goes_On_(Sonny_%26_Cher_song)" title="The Beat Goes On (Sonny &amp; Cher song)">The Beat Goes On</a>", accompanied by psychedelic lights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-10">[10]</a></sup> After a dance interlude, she performed the album track "I Will Be There" and a cover of "<a href="/wiki/Open_Arms_(Journey_song)" title="Open Arms (Journey song)">Open Arms</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Journey_(band)" title="Journey (band)">Journey</a>, ending with a smile at the top of the staircase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_10-6"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-10">[10]</a></sup> After "<a href="/wiki/Sometimes_(Britney_Spears_song)" title="Sometimes (Britney Spears song)">Sometimes</a>", she waved and left the stage. The encore consisted of a performance of "<a href="/wiki/...Baby_One_More_Time_(song)" title="...Baby One More Time (song)">...Baby One More Time</a>", in which Spears wore a black bra under pink <a href="/wiki/Halter" title="Halter">halter</a>, a pink sequined plaid mini-skirt, and black <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thigh-high_stockings" title="Thigh-high stockings">thigh-high stockings</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_10-7"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=...Baby_One_More_Time_Tour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The tour garnered generally positive reviews from critics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-9">[9]</a></sup> Jeffrey Haney of the <i><a href="/wiki/Deseret_News" title="Deseret News">Deseret News</a></i> described the show as "funky and flashy".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deseretn_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-deseretn-11">[11]</a></sup> A reporter from <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> called Spears' performance "assured and energetic".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-9">[9]</a></sup> Jim Farber of the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Daily_News" title="New York Daily News">New York Daily News</a></i> pointed out that Spears seemed to have two <a href="/wiki/Persona" title="Persona">personas</a> during the show, one when singing songs from her album and a more edgy look when singing the covers. He also added that "Spears' nods to edginess no doubt reflects her desire to grow into a more mature career".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dailyn_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-dailyn-12">[12]</a></sup> Jane Ganahl of the <i><a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Chronicle" title="San Francisco Chronicle">San Francisco Chronicle</a></i> said that "she may be somewhat entertaining, but she's also just another prefab act – longer on packaging than actual talent".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfc_10-8"><a href="#cite_note-sfc-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasts_and_recordings">Broadcasts and recordings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=...Baby_One_More_Time_Tour&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Broadcasts and recordings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In August 1999, the concert in Orlando, Florida was filmed for the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel_in_Concert" title="Disney Channel in Concert">Disney Channel in Concert</a> series which Spears was also joined by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Kids_On_The_Block" title="New Kids On The Block">New Kids On The Block</a> member <a href="/wiki/Joey_McIntyre" title="Joey McIntyre">Joey McIntyre</a> (who was embarking on his solo career). The concert entitled <i>Britney Spears &amp; Joey McIntyre in Concert</i> aired on October 16, 1999. In the special, Spears performed 7 songs in front of the <a href="/wiki/Rock_%27n%27_Roller_Coaster_Starring_Aerosmith" title="Rock 'n' Roller Coaster Starring Aerosmith">Rock 'n' Roller Coaster Starring Aerosmith</a> attraction at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney_MGM_Studios" title="Disney MGM Studios">Disney MGM Studios</a>. It also featured Spears visiting her former routes working at the park during her year's of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mickey_Mouse_Club" title="Mickey Mouse Club">Mickey Mouse Club</a> and shopping at <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Disney" title="Downtown Disney">Downtown Disney</a> with her dancers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
