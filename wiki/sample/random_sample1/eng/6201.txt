<p>In the classification of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_languages" title="African languages">African languages</a>, <b>Bantoid</b> is a putative branch of the <a href="/wiki/Benue%E2%80%93Congo_languages" title="Benue–Congo languages">Benue–Congo</a> branch of the <a href="/wiki/Niger%E2%80%93Congo_languages" title="Niger–Congo languages">Niger–Congo family</a>. The term 'Bantoid' was first used by Krause in 1895 for languages that showed resemblances in vocabulary to <a href="/wiki/Bantu_languages" title="Bantu languages">Bantu</a>. <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Greenberg" title="Joseph Greenberg">Greenberg</a> in his 1963 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Languages_of_Africa" title="The Languages of Africa">The Languages of Africa</a></i> defined Bantoid as the group to which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narrow_Bantu" title="Narrow Bantu">(Narrow) Bantu</a> belongs together with its closest relatives; this is the sense in which the term is still used today. The Bantoid languages probably do not actually form a coherent group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>A proposal that divided Bantoid into North and <a href="/wiki/Southern_Bantoid_languages" title="Southern Bantoid languages">South Bantoid</a> was introduced in Williamson (1989, based on work presented in Blench [1987]). In this proposal, the Mambiloid and <a href="/wiki/Dakoid_languages" title="Dakoid languages">Dakoid languages</a> (and later <a href="/wiki/Tikar_language" title="Tikar language">Tikar</a>) are grouped together as North Bantoid, while everything else Bantoid is subsumed under South Bantoid; the <a href="/wiki/Ethnologue" title="Ethnologue">Ethnologue</a> uses this classification. The legitimacy of the North Bantoid group is questionable, and the Dakoid languages are often now placed outside Bantoid, but the work did establish Southern Bantoid as a valid genetic unit. Southern Bantoid includes the well known and numerous <a href="/wiki/Bantu_languages" title="Bantu languages">Bantu</a> subfamily.</p>
