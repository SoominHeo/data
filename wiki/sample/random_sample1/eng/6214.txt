<p><b>Valérie Trierweiler</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[valeʁi tʁiɛʁvɛlɛːʁ]</a></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Married_and_maiden_names" title="Married and maiden names">née</a> <b>Massonneau</b>; born 16 February 1965) is a <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> and author.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Val.C3.A9rie_Trierweiler.2C_la_femme_discr.C3.A8te_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Val.C3.A9rie_Trierweiler.2C_la_femme_discr.C3.A8te-1">[1]</a></sup> She has hosted political talk shows and has contributed to <i><a href="/wiki/Paris_Match" title="Paris Match">Paris Match</a></i>. She is best known for having been the partner of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President_of_the_French_Republic" title="President of the French Republic">President of the French Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Hollande" title="François Hollande">François Hollande</a>, until January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Val%C3%A9rie_Trierweiler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Val%C3%A9rie_Trierweiler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Valérie Massonneau was born in <a href="/wiki/Angers" title="Angers">Angers</a>, the fifth child of six.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Le_Monde_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Le_Monde-3">[3]</a></sup> Her father, Jean-Noël Massonneau, had lost a leg on a landmine during the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>, when he was 13,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guichoux_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guichoux-4">[4]</a></sup> and died at the age of 53, when his daughter was 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Le_Monde_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Le_Monde-3">[3]</a></sup> Her mother worked at the front desk of the Angers <a href="/wiki/Ice_rink" title="Ice rink">ice rink</a> following the death of her father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Le_Monde_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Le_Monde-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>She studied History<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Le_Monde_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Le_Monde-3">[3]</a></sup> and Political Science and obtained a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dipl%C3%B4me_d%27%C3%A9tudes_sup%C3%A9rieures_sp%C3%A9cialis%C3%A9es#Master_of_Advanced_Studies_.28MAS.29.2C_Dipl.C3.B4me_d.27.C3.89tudes_Approfondies_.28DEA.29_in_France_and_francophone_countries" title="Diplôme d'études supérieures spécialisées">DESS</a> in political science from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Paris_1_Pantheon-Sorbonne" title="University of Paris 1 Pantheon-Sorbonne">University of Paris 1 Pantheon-Sorbonne</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Val%C3%A9rie_Trierweiler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:292px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1555.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2595" data-file-width="3893" height="193" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1555.jpg/290px-PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1555.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1555.jpg/435px-PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1555.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1555.jpg/580px-PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1555.jpg 2x" width="290"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>In 2005, she began to host political <a href="/wiki/Talk_show" title="Talk show">talk shows</a>, especially interviews, on the <i><a href="/wiki/Direct_8" title="Direct 8">Direct 8</a></i> television channel. She fronted the weekly political talk show <i>Le Grand 8</i> until 2007 and with Mikaël Guedj has <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Co-host&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Co-host (page does not exist)">co-hosted</a> the weekly show <i>Politiquement parlant</i> ("politically speaking") since September of that year.</p>
<p>In 2012, she announced that she would keep her contract as a <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> with the <i><a href="/wiki/Paris_Match" title="Paris Match">Paris Match</a></i> magazine despite her boyfriend being elected as President of France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 12 June 2012, she caused widespread controversy by tweeting in support of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Olivier_Falorni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Olivier Falorni (page does not exist)">Olivier Falorni</a>, who was standing for election as a dissident socialist candidate at <a href="/wiki/La_Rochelle" title="La Rochelle">La Rochelle</a>, against <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9gol%C3%A8ne_Royal" title="Ségolène Royal">Ségolène Royal</a>, François Hollande's former partner. Hollande had already made public his own support for Royal's campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Val%C3%A9rie_Trierweiler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Her first marriage, to <a href="/wiki/Childhood" title="Childhood">childhood</a> friend <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Franck_Thurieau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Franck Thurieau (page does not exist)">Franck Thurieau</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> ended in divorce with no children. Her second marriage was to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Denis_Trierweiler&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Denis Trierweiler (page does not exist)">Denis Trierweiler</a>, a sub-editor at <i><a href="/wiki/Paris_Match" title="Paris Match">Paris Match</a></i> magazine,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and a writer and academic. This marriage produced three children and divorce proceedings took three years (2007-2010).</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1712.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2538" data-file-width="3806" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1712.jpg/260px-PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1712.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1712.jpg/390px-PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1712.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1712.jpg/520px-PR-2012-05-15_IMG_1712.jpg 2x" width="260"/></a>

<p>She met <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Hollande" title="François Hollande">François Hollande</a> during the <a href="/wiki/French_legislative_election,_1988" title="French legislative election, 1988">parliamentary elections of 1988</a> while he was living with <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9gol%C3%A8ne_Royal" title="Ségolène Royal">Ségolène Royal</a>. They began their relationship in 2007, while she was still married, and made it public in October 2010 after her divorce was publicized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guichoux_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guichoux-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2014, a story in the celebrity magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Closer_(magazine)" title="Closer (magazine)">Closer</a></i> featured seven pages of alleged revelations and photos about an affair between Hollande and the French actress <a href="/wiki/Julie_Gayet" title="Julie Gayet">Julie Gayet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Trierweiler was subsequently admitted to hospital on 10 January "for rest and some tests".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> On 17 January, Hollande made his first private visit to see her in hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On 25 January, it was announced her relationship with Hollande had ended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2014, a book written by Trierweiler, <i>Merci pour ce moment</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Thank_You_for_This_Moment" title="Thank You for This Moment">Thank You for This Moment</a></i>), was published. It details her relationship with Hollande and their breakup.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Ségolène Royal supported Hollande, describing Trierweiler's book as "total nonsense."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HollandePoor_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-HollandePoor-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
