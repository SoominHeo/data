<p><b>Valeria Solarino</b> (born 4 November 1978) is an Italian actress, born in <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>.</p>
<p>She was born at <a href="/wiki/Barcelona,_Venezuela" title="Barcelona, Venezuela">El Morro de Barcelona</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicilian</a> father and <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turinese</a> mother. After studying philosophy at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Turin" title="University of Turin">University of Turin</a>, she debuted as actress in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Teatro_Stabile&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Teatro Stabile (page does not exist)">Teatro Stabile</a>.</p>
<p>In 2003 she was chosen by director <a href="/wiki/Mimmo_Calopresti" title="Mimmo Calopresti">Mimmo Calopresti</a> for a small role in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_felicit%C3%A0_non_costa_niente" title="La felicitÃ  non costa niente">La felicitÃ  non costa niente</a></i> (<i>Happiness Costs Nothing</i>) (2003), after which her career took off: in the same year she played Maja in the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fame_chimica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fame chimica (page does not exist)">Fame chimica</a></i> (<i>Chemical Hunger</i>) by Paolo Vari and Antonio Bocola and Bea in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Che_ne_sar%C3%A0_di_noi" title="Che ne sarÃ  di noi">Che ne sarÃ  di noi</a></i> (<i>What Will Happen to Us</i>) by <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Veronesi" title="Giovanni Veronesi">Giovanni Veronesi</a>, who is her current partner.</p>
<p>In 2005 Solarino starred in <i>La febbre</i> by <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_D%27Alatri" title="Alessandro D'Alatri">Alessandro D'Alatri</a>, in 2006 in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viaggio_segreto" title="Viaggio segreto">Viaggio segreto</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Roberto_And%C3%B2" title="Roberto AndÃ²">Roberto AndÃ²</a> and, in 2007, <a href="/wiki/Valzer_(film)" title="Valzer (film)">Valzer</a> by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Salvatore_Maira&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Salvatore Maira (page does not exist)">Salvatore Maira</a>. In 2009 she played the role of a lesbian lover in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viola_di_mare" title="Viola di mare">Viola di mare</a></i> (<i>Sea Purple</i>), a historical film by Donatella Maiorca.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> which was shown at the <a href="/wiki/Rome_Film_Festival" title="Rome Film Festival">Rome Film Festival</a>.</p>
