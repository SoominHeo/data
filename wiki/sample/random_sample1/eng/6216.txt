<p><b>Valerian Vladimirovich Kuybyshev</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Валериа́н Влади́мирович Ку́йбышев</span>; 6 June [<a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">O.S.</a> 25 May] 1888 – 25 January 1935) was a Russian revolutionary, <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> officer, and prominent Soviet <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valerian_Kuybyshev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valerian_Kuybyshev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kuybyshev was born in <a href="/wiki/Omsk" title="Omsk">Omsk</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> on 6 June [<a href="/wiki/Old_Style_and_New_Style_dates" title="Old Style and New Style dates">O.S.</a> 25 May] 1888. He studied at the Omsk Military Cadet School. He joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolshevik" title="Bolshevik">Bolshevik</a> faction of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Social_Democratic_Labour_Party" title="Russian Social Democratic Labour Party">Russian Social Democratic Labour Party</a> in 1904. The following year, he entered a military medical academy, but was expelled in 1906 for controversial political activities.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Revolutionary_career">Revolutionary career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valerian_Kuybyshev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Revolutionary career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between 1906 and 1914, Kuybyshev performed subversive activities for the Bolsheviks throughout the Empire, for which he was exiled to <a href="/wiki/Narym" title="Narym">Narym</a> in <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> where—together with <a href="/wiki/Yakov_Sverdlov" title="Yakov Sverdlov">Yakov Sverdlov</a>—he set up a local Bolshevik organization. In May 1912 he fled and returned to <a href="/wiki/Omsk" title="Omsk">Omsk</a>, where he was arrested the next month, and imprisoned for a year. He was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Tambov" title="Tambov">Tambov</a> to live independently under police surveillance, but soon fled again, whereafter he spent 1913–14 encouraging civil unrest in the cities of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Petersburg" title="St. Petersburg">St. Petersburg</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kharkov" title="Kharkov">Kharkov</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vologda" title="Vologda">Vologda</a>; relocated to <a href="/wiki/Samara,_Russia" title="Samara, Russia">Samara</a> in 1917; and became president of the local soviet—a position he held at the time of the <a href="/wiki/October_Revolution" title="October Revolution">October Revolution</a> and for the next year. During the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Civil_War" title="Russian Civil War">Russian Civil War</a> he chaired the revolutionary committee of Samara province and became a <a href="/wiki/Political_commissar" title="Political commissar">political commissar</a> in the First and Fourth Red Armies.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valerian_Kuybyshev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1920 Kuybyshev was elected a member of Presidium of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_International_of_Trade_Unions" title="Red International of Trade Unions">Red International of Trade Unions</a>, which charged him with the implementation of the <a href="/wiki/GOELRO_plan" title="GOELRO plan">GOELRO plan</a>. From 6 July 1923 to 5 August 1926 he was the first economical inspector of the USSR. From 1926 to 1930 he chaired the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supreme_Council_of_the_National_Economy" title="Supreme Council of the National Economy">Supreme Council of the National Economy</a>, from 1930 to 1934 he directed <a href="/wiki/Gosplan" title="Gosplan">Gosplan</a>, and he served as a full member of the <a href="/wiki/Politburo_of_the_Communist_Party_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Politburo of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union">Politburo</a> from 1934 until his death. As a principal economic advisor to <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a>, he was one of the most influential members in the Communist Party. He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Red_Banner" title="Order of the Red Banner">Order of the Red Banner</a>. Kuybyshev was one of the initiators of the first edition of the <i><a href="/wiki/Great_Soviet_Encyclopedia" title="Great Soviet Encyclopedia">Great Soviet Encyclopedia</a></i> and was a member of its chief editorial board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kuybyshev died in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> on 25 January 1935 of heart failure and alcoholism. In 1938, at the height of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Purge" title="Great Purge">Great Purge</a>, his former wife and brother were executed under obscure charges.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>As Bolshevik tradition had established, he was buried outside the <a href="/wiki/Kremlin" title="Kremlin">Kremlin</a> walls.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valerian_Kuybyshev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kuybyshev married twice, but never had any children. He was a practiced musician and poet. One of his wives was the niece of <a href="/wiki/Yevgenia_Bosch" title="Yevgenia Bosch">Yevgenia Bosch</a>, Galina Aleksandrovna Troyanovskaya.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commemoration">Commemoration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valerian_Kuybyshev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Commemoration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city of <a href="/wiki/Samara,_Russia" title="Samara, Russia">Samara</a> (the administrative city of the <a href="/wiki/Samara_Oblast" title="Samara Oblast">Samara Oblast</a>, Russia), the town of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolgar_(town)" title="Bolgar (town)">Bolgar</a> (in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Tatarstan" title="Republic of Tatarstan">Republic of Tatarstan</a>, Russia), and the town of <a href="/wiki/Haghartsin,_Armenia" title="Haghartsin, Armenia">Haghartsin, Armenia</a> were all renamed Kuybyshev during the period between 1935 and 1991. The towns of <a href="/wiki/Kuybyshev,_Novosibirsk_Oblast" title="Kuybyshev, Novosibirsk Oblast">Kuybyshev</a> in <a href="/wiki/Novosibirsk_Oblast" title="Novosibirsk Oblast">Novosibirsk Oblast</a>, Russia, and <a href="/wiki/Kuybyshev,_Armenia" title="Kuybyshev, Armenia">Kuybyshev, Armenia</a>, still have his name.</p>
