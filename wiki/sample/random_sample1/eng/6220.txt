<p><b>Valentina Cortese</b> (born 1 January 1923) is an Italian <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actress</a>. She was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress</a> for her performance in <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Truffaut" title="François Truffaut">François Truffaut</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Day_for_Night_(film)" title="Day for Night (film)">Day for Night</a></i> (1973).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valentina_Cortese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milan,_Italy" title="Milan, Italy">Milan</a> to a family from <a href="/wiki/Stresa" title="Stresa">Stresa</a> (<a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">Piedmont</a>), Cortese married <a href="/wiki/Richard_Basehart" title="Richard Basehart">Richard Basehart</a>, her co-star in <i><a href="/wiki/The_House_on_Telegraph_Hill" title="The House on Telegraph Hill">The House on Telegraph Hill</a></i>, in 1951, and had one son with him, the actor <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Basehart" title="Jackie Basehart">Jackie Basehart</a>; they divorced in 1960. She never remarried.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Jackie Basehart died in Milan in 2015, predeceasing Cortese.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valentina_Cortese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Valentina_Cortese.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2260" data-file-width="3390" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Valentina_Cortese.jpg/220px-Valentina_Cortese.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Valentina_Cortese.jpg/330px-Valentina_Cortese.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Valentina_Cortese.jpg/440px-Valentina_Cortese.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Valentina_Cortese.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Cortese in 2012</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>She made her screen debut in Italians films in 1940, leading to her first internationally acclaimed roles in Riccardo Freda's 1948 Italian film <i><a href="/wiki/Les_Mis%C3%A9rables_(1948_film)" title="Les Misérables (1948 film)">Les Misérables</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Marcello_Mastroianni" title="Marcello Mastroianni">Marcello Mastroianni</a>, in which she played both Fantine and Cosette, and the 1949 British film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Glass_Mountain_(film)" title="The Glass Mountain (film)">The Glass Mountain</a></i> (1949),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdbprofile_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-imdbprofile-3">[3]</a></sup> which led to a number of roles in American movies of the period, but continued to make movies in Europe with such directors as <a href="/wiki/Michelangelo_Antonioni" title="Michelangelo Antonioni">Michelangelo Antonioni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Federico_Fellini" title="Federico Fellini">Federico Fellini</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Truffaut" title="François Truffaut">François Truffaut</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdbprofile_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-imdbprofile-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>She signed a contract with <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">20th Century Fox</a> in 1948. She starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Malaya_(film)" title="Malaya (film)"><i>Malaya</i></a></i> (1949), a Second World War movie about smuggling and guerrilla warfare against the Japanese with <a href="/wiki/Spencer_Tracy" title="Spencer Tracy">Spencer Tracy</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Stewart" title="James Stewart">James Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jules_Dassin" title="Jules Dassin">Jules Dassin</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Thieves%27_Highway" title="Thieves' Highway">Thieves' Highway</a></i> (1949) with <a href="/wiki/Richard_Conte" title="Richard Conte">Richard Conte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lee_J._Cobb" title="Lee J. Cobb">Lee J. Cobb</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_House_on_Telegraph_Hill" title="The House on Telegraph Hill">The House on Telegraph Hill</a></i> (1951) directed by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Wise" title="Robert Wise">Robert Wise</a>, and co-starring <a href="/wiki/Richard_Basehart" title="Richard Basehart">Richard Basehart</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_Lundigan" title="William Lundigan">William Lundigan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Joseph_L._Mankiewicz" title="Joseph L. Mankiewicz">Joseph L. Mankiewicz</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Barefoot_Contessa" title="The Barefoot Contessa">The Barefoot Contessa</a></i> (1954), with Humphrey Bogart, Ava Gardner, Edmond O'Brien. In Europe she starred in <a href="/wiki/Michelangelo_Antonioni" title="Michelangelo Antonioni">Michelangelo Antonioni</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Amiche" title="Le Amiche">Le Amiche</a></i> (1955), <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9rard_Brach" title="Gérard Brach">Gérard Brach</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boat_on_the_Grass" title="The Boat on the Grass">The Boat on the Grass</a></i> (1971), <a href="/wiki/Terry_Gilliam" title="Terry Gilliam">Terry Gilliam</a>'s British film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Adventures_of_Baron_Munchausen" title="The Adventures of Baron Munchausen">The Adventures of Baron Munchausen</a></i> (1988), and in <a href="/wiki/Franco_Zeffirelli" title="Franco Zeffirelli">Franco Zeffirelli</a> projects such as the film <i><a href="/wiki/Brother_Sun,_Sister_Moon" title="Brother Sun, Sister Moon">Brother Sun, Sister Moon</a></i> (1972), the <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Jesus_of_Nazareth_(miniseries)" title="Jesus of Nazareth (miniseries)">Jesus of Nazareth</a></i> (1977) and the film <i><a href="/wiki/Sparrow_(1993_film)" title="Sparrow (1993 film)">Sparrow</a></i>n (1993). Her final American film role was in <i><a href="/wiki/When_Time_Ran_Out" title="When Time Ran Out">When Time Ran Out</a></i> (1980).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
