<p>The <b>Balkans Cup</b> was an international <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> competition for clubs from <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. It was introduced in 1961 and was very popular in the 1960s (the 1967 final attracted 42,000 spectators),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Balkans_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Balkans-1">[1]</a></sup> being the second most important international club competition for clubs from the region (after the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">European Champions' Cup</a> in which the champions could play; the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="UEFA Cup Winners' Cup">UEFA Cup Winners' Cup</a> originally attracted few teams from the region as many did not organise domestic cups regularly and only Yugoslavia had significant representation in the Fairs Cup).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Balkans_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Balkans-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>It later declined after Balkan clubs obtained more representation in the two minor UEFA competitions, in contrast to the (much older but also defunct) <a href="/wiki/Balkan_Cup" title="Balkan Cup">Balkan Cup</a> (not Balkans) for national teams.</p>

<li>Competition's formats:</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd><b>type-A:</b> One group of 5 clubs.</dd>
<dd><b>type-B:</b> Two groups of 4, top clubs qualifying for the final.</dd>
<dd><b>type-C:</b> Two groups of 3, top clubs qualifying for the final.</dd>
<dd><b>type-D:</b> One group of 3 clubs.</dd>
<dd><b>type-E:</b> Knock-out (Quarterfinals → Semifinals → Final).</dd>
<dd><b>type-F:</b> Two groups of 3 and one group of 4, top clubs plus the best runner-up qualifying for the Knock-out Semifinals.</dd>
<dd><b>type-G:</b> Knock-out (Preliminary round qualifying 2 clubs out of 4 → Semifinals → Final).</dd>
<dd><b>type-H:</b> Knock-out (Preliminary round qualifying 1 club out of 2 → Semifinals → Final).</dd>
<dd><b>type-I:</b> Knock-out (Semifinals → Final).</dd>
</dl>

<li>Next to Beroe's 4, their compatriots <a href="/wiki/PFC_Slavia_Sofia" title="PFC Slavia Sofia">Slavia Sofia</a> reached <b>3 Finals</b> (2 wins), while Romanian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FC_Arge%C5%9F_Pite%C5%9Fti" title="FC Argeş Piteşti">Argeş Piteşti</a> had 2 (no win), plus one runner-up spot in group (each time they entered the competition, ended up in the losing side).</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_club_2">By club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balkans_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: By club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_country_2">By country</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balkans_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: By country">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgarian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkish</a> sides were present at each one of the 28 Balkans Cup editions, while <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Yugoslavian</a> were absent 9 times in total, entering just twice during its last decade of existence (ironically, reaching both finals). Generally, <a href="/wiki/Slavs" title="Slavs">South Slavs</a> (both <a href="/wiki/Football_Association_of_Yugoslavia" title="Football Association of Yugoslavia">Football Association</a> and clubs) were never keen supporters of the competition, as they had neither been enthusiastic about the national teams' <a href="/wiki/Balkan_Cup" title="Balkan Cup">Balkan Cup</a>, too.</p>

<p>Despite the fact that in each edition there was at least one <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkish</a> side initially entering, in no less than 5 occasions it withdrew before playing a single match and in 5 more quit during group stage, after unsuccessful results. <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greeks</a> followed with 1 withdrawal and 4 quits, while clubs from all countries had sporadically terminated their participation in some early stage of the competition, except for <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albanian</a>. Multiple winner and several other records holder <a href="/wiki/PFC_Beroe_Stara_Zagora" title="PFC Beroe Stara Zagora">Beroe Stara Zagora</a>, became the only Bulgarian side ever to withdraw or quit a Balkans Cup match and the single one to do so in a final, by not showing up for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Balkans_Cup_1970&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Balkans Cup 1970 (page does not exist)">1970 edition's</a> return leg against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KF_Partizani_Tirana" title="KF Partizani Tirana">Partizani Tirana</a> of Albania, following an 1-1 draw at home.</p>
