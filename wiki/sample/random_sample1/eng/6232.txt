<p><b>Walther Funk</b> (18 August 1890 – 31 May 1960) was an <a href="/wiki/Economist" title="Economist">economist</a> and prominent <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Party" title="Nazi Party">Nazi</a> official who served as <a href="/wiki/German_Reich" title="German Reich">Reich</a> Minister for Economic Affairs from 1938 to 1945 and was tried and convicted as a major <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_criminal" title="War criminal">war criminal</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_trials" title="Nuremberg trials">International Military Tribunal at Nuremberg</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walther_Funk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Funk was born into a merchant family in 1890 in Danzkehmen (present-day Sosnowka in the Russian <a href="/wiki/Kaliningrad_Oblast" title="Kaliningrad Oblast">Kaliningrad Oblast</a>) near <a href="/wiki/Yasnaya_Polyana,_Kaliningrad_Oblast" title="Yasnaya Polyana, Kaliningrad Oblast">Trakehnen</a> in <a href="/wiki/East_Prussia" title="East Prussia">East Prussia</a>. He was the only one of the Nuremberg defendants who was born in the <a href="/wiki/Former_eastern_territories_of_Germany" title="Former eastern territories of Germany">former eastern territories of Germany</a>. He was the son of Wiesenbaumeister Walther Funk the elder and his wife Sophie (née Urbschat). He studied law, economics, and philosophy at the <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_University_of_Berlin" title="Humboldt University of Berlin">Humboldt University of Berlin</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Leipzig" title="University of Leipzig">University of Leipzig</a>. In <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, he joined the infantry, but was discharged as unfit for service in 1916. In 1920, Funk married Luise Schmidt-Sieben. Following the end of the first war, he worked as a <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a>, and in 1924 he became the editor of the centre-right financial newspaper the <i>Berliner Börsenzeitung</i>.</p>

<p>Funk, who was a nationalist and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-Marxist" title="Anti-Marxist">anti-Marxist</a>, resigned from the newspaper in the summer of 1931 and joined the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Party" title="Nazi Party">Nazi Party</a>, becoming close to <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Strasser" title="Gregor Strasser">Gregor Strasser</a>, who arranged his first meeting with <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>. Partially because of his interest in economic policy, he was elected a <a href="/wiki/Reichstag_(Weimar_Republic)" title="Reichstag (Weimar Republic)">Reichstag</a> deputy in July 1932, and within the party, he was made chairman of the Committee on Economic Policy in December 1932, a post that he did not hold for long. After the Nazi Party came to power, he stepped down from his Reichstag position and was made Chief Press Officer of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Reich" title="Third Reich">Third Reich</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Third_Reich_career">Third Reich career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walther_Funk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Third Reich career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 1933, Funk was appointed as a State Secretary (<i>Staatssekretär</i>) at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Public_Enlightenment_and_Propaganda" title="Ministry of Public Enlightenment and Propaganda">Ministry of Public Enlightenment and Propaganda</a> (<i>Reichsministerium für Volksaufklärung und Propaganda</i>). In 1938, he assumed the title of Chief <a href="/wiki/Plenipotentiary" title="Plenipotentiary">Plenipotentiary</a> for Economics (<i>Wirtschaftsbeauftragter</i>). He also became Reich Minister of Economics (<i>Reichswirtschaftsminister</i>) in February 1938, replacing <a href="/wiki/Hjalmar_Schacht" title="Hjalmar Schacht">Hjalmar Schacht</a>, who had been dropped in November 1937. Schacht had been dismissed in a power struggle with <a href="/wiki/Reichsmarschall" title="Reichsmarschall">Reichsmarschall</a> <a href="/wiki/Hermann_G%C3%B6ring" title="Hermann Göring">Hermann Göring</a>, who was quick to tie the ministry more closely to his <a href="/wiki/Four_Year_Plan" title="Four Year Plan">Four Year Plan</a> Office.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Confiscation_of_Jewish_property">Confiscation of Jewish property</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walther_Funk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Confiscation of Jewish property">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At Nuremberg, Funk was accused by Allied prosecutors of having been closely involved in the State confiscation and disposal of the property of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Jew" title="German Jew">German Jews</a> from this time. He boasted that by 1938, the German state had confiscated Jewish property worth two million marks, using decrees from Hitler and other top Nazis to force German Jews to leave their property and assets to the State if they emigrated. They were forced by Göring to pay for the damage caused by the Nazis to their own property on <i><a href="/wiki/Kristallnacht" title="Kristallnacht">Kristallnacht</a></i>, and increasingly deprived of their personal wealth and assets as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> approached.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reichsbank">Reichsbank</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walther_Funk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reichsbank">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the year April 1938-March 1939 Funk was a Director of the Swiss-based multi-national Bank of International Settlements,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and in January 1939, Hitler appointed Funk as President of the <a href="/wiki/Reichsbank" title="Reichsbank">Reichsbank</a>, replacing Schacht. He was appointed to the Central Planning Board in September 1943.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nuremberg">Nuremberg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walther_Funk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Nuremberg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite poor health, Funk was tried with other Nazi leaders at the <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_trials" title="Nuremberg trials">Nuremberg trials</a>. Accused of conspiracy to commit crimes against peace; planning, initiating and waging wars of aggression; <a href="/wiki/War_crime" title="War crime">war crimes</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crime_against_humanity" title="Crime against humanity">crimes against humanity</a>, he argued that, despite his titles, he had very little power in the regime. At the Nuremberg trials American Chief Prosecutor Jackson labeled Funk as "The Banker of Gold Teeth", referring to the practice of extracting <a href="/wiki/Gold_teeth" title="Gold teeth">gold teeth</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nazi_concentration_camp" title="Nazi concentration camp">Nazi concentration camp</a> victims, and forwarding the teeth to the <a href="/wiki/Reichsbank" title="Reichsbank">Reichsbank</a> for melting down to yield <a href="/wiki/Bullion" title="Bullion">bullion</a>. Many other gold items were stolen from victims, such as <a href="/wiki/Jewellery" title="Jewellery">jewellery</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eyeglasses" title="Eyeglasses">eyeglasses</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finger_ring" title="Finger ring">finger rings</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hermann_G%C3%B6ring" title="Hermann Göring">Göring</a> described Funk as "an insignificant subordinate," but documentary evidence and his wartime biography <i>Walther Funk, A Life for Economy</i> were used against him during the trial, leading to his conviction on counts 2, 3 and 4 of the indictment and his sentence of life imprisonment.</p>
<p>Funk was held at <a href="/wiki/Spandau_Prison" title="Spandau Prison">Spandau Prison</a> along with other <a href="/wiki/List_of_Nazi_Party_leaders_and_officials" title="List of Nazi Party leaders and officials">senior Nazis</a>. He was released on 16 May 1957 because of ill health. He made a last-minute call on <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Hess" title="Rudolf Hess">Rudolf Hess</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Speer" title="Albert Speer">Albert Speer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baldur_von_Schirach" title="Baldur von Schirach">Baldur von Schirach</a> before leaving the prison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He died three years later in <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diabetes" title="Diabetes">diabetes</a>.</p>
