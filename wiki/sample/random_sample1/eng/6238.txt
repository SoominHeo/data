<p><b>Dae Insu</b>, also known as <b>King Seon</b> (r. 818-830) was the 10th king of the <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Korean</a> kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Balhae" title="Balhae">Balhae</a>. He restored national strength, and is remembered today as the last of the great <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rulers_of_Korea#Balhae" title="Rulers of Korea">Balhae rulers</a> before its fall.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seon_of_Balhae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dae Insu was a 4th-generation descendant of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dae_Joyeong" title="Dae Joyeong">Dae Joyeong</a>'s younger brother, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dae_Ya-bal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dae Ya-bal (page does not exist)">Dae Ya-bal</a>. In spite being from the collateral branch, he succeeded to the throne during the years of 817 and 818. He reestablished royal authority, and strengthened the military tremendously.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reign">Reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seon_of_Balhae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Balhae-Territory_in_830.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="926" data-file-width="1115" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Balhae-Territory_in_830.JPG/200px-Balhae-Territory_in_830.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Balhae-Territory_in_830.JPG/300px-Balhae-Territory_in_830.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Balhae-Territory_in_830.JPG/400px-Balhae-Territory_in_830.JPG 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Balhae-Territory_in_830.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The territory of Balhae, In 830s during the reign of king Seon of Balhae.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>King Seon concentrated heavily on the empire's territorial expansion, and led campaigns that resulted in the absorption of many northern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malgal" title="Malgal">Malgal</a> tribes including <a href="/wiki/Heishui_Mohe" title="Heishui Mohe">Heishui Mohe</a>. Southwest <a href="/wiki/Little_Goguryeo" title="Little Goguryeo">Little Goguryeo</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liaodong" title="Liaodong">Liaodong</a> was absorbed into Balhae, and also he ordered southward expansion towards <a href="/wiki/Silla" title="Silla">Silla</a>.</p>
<p>During his 12-year reign, he dispatched embassies five times to <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, which was aimed at establishing diplomatic relations as well as increasing trade between the two kingdoms. Balhae emissaries were treated favorably even though Japan wanted Balhae to restrict the size of the embassies due to the costs associated with hosting them. The trade routes established across the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Japan" title="Sea of Japan">Sea of Japan</a> (East Sea) led to Balhae becoming one of Japan's most important trading partners.</p>
<p>He died in 830 and his grandson <a href="/wiki/Dae_Ijin" title="Dae Ijin">Dae Ijin</a> succeeded to the throne.</p>
