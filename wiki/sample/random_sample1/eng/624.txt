<p>Year <b>475</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">CDLXXV</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Wednesday" title="Common year starting on Wednesday">common year starting on Wednesday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Zeno without colleague</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1228 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 475 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=475&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=475&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=475&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_9" title="January 9">January 9</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Zeno_(emperor)" title="Zeno (emperor)">Zeno</a> abdicates under pressure, as his wife's uncle <a href="/wiki/Basiliscus" title="Basiliscus">Basiliscus</a> stages a <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup d'état</a> at <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>, with support from Zeno's trusted adviser and fellow Isaurian <a href="/wiki/Illus" title="Illus">Illus</a>. Basiliscus usurps the <a href="/wiki/Throne" title="Throne">throne</a> and is proclaimed new emperor (<i><a href="/wiki/Augustus_(honorific)" title="Augustus (honorific)">Augustus</a></i>) of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Roman_Empire" title="Eastern Roman Empire">Eastern Roman Empire</a>. He begins a 20-month reign; Zeno and his supporters flee to <a href="/wiki/Isauria" title="Isauria">Isauria</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_9" title="April 9">April 9</a> – Basiliscus issues a circular letter (<i>Enkyklikon</i>) to the <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishops</a> of his empire, promoting the <a href="/wiki/Miaphysitism" title="Miaphysitism">Miaphysite</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christological" title="Christological">christological</a> position. The religious views will make him highly unpopular.</li>
<li>Summer – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Julius_Nepos" title="Julius Nepos">Julius Nepos</a> grants the <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoth</a> king <a href="/wiki/Euric" title="Euric">Euric</a> legal tenure of his conquests, which include <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">Provence</a> (region of <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a>), in exchange for full independence.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_28" title="August 28">August 28</a> – <a href="/wiki/Orestes_(father_of_Romulus_Augustulus)" title="Orestes (father of Romulus Augustulus)">Flavius Orestes</a> takes control of the government at <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a> and forces Julius Nepos to flee to <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">Dalmatia</a>. Unable to rule himself, he appoints his son <a href="/wiki/Romulus_Augustulus" title="Romulus Augustulus">Romulus Augustus</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_31" title="October 31">October 31</a> – Romulus Augustus ("Little Augustus") is installed as emperor by his father Orestes, who becomes <i><a href="/wiki/Magister_militum" title="Magister militum">magister militum</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regent</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Roman_Empire" title="Western Roman Empire">Western Roman Empire</a>. Augustus's reign will last for 9 months and he will be the last ruler of the Western Empire.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=475&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bodhidharma" title="Bodhidharma">Bodhidharma</a>, Buddhist monk, travels to China and begins teachings of the <a href="/wiki/La%E1%B9%85k%C4%81vat%C4%81ra_S%C5%ABtra" title="Laṅkāvatāra Sūtra">Laṅkāvatāra Sūtra</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gongju" title="Gongju">Gongju</a> becomes the capital of <a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a>, and is threatened by <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a> who conquers the <a href="/wiki/Han_River_(Korea)" title="Han River (Korea)">Han River</a> valley (Korea).</li>
</ul>
