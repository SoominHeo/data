<p><b>Bright Future</b> (<a href="/wiki/Icelandic_language" title="Icelandic language">Icelandic</a>: <span lang="is"><i>Björt framtíð</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberal</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lansford2015_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lansford2015-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Iceland" title="List of political parties in Iceland">political party in Iceland</a>.</p>
<p>The party is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Party" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party">Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe</a> (ALDE) party and has links to the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Group" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Group">ALDE Group</a> in the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grape_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-grape-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bright_Future_(Iceland)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party was founded on 4 February 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Founding_policy_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Founding_policy-1">[1]</a></sup> Before the 2013 general election, it included two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Members_of_Parliament" title="Members of Parliament">Members of Parliament</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gu%C3%B0mundur_Steingr%C3%ADmsson" title="Guðmundur Steingrímsson">Guðmundur Steingrímsson</a> (who defected from the <a href="/wiki/Progressive_Party_(Iceland)" title="Progressive Party (Iceland)">Progressive Party</a>) and <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B3bert_Marshall" title="Róbert Marshall">Róbert Marshall</a> (who defected from the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Alliance" title="Social Democratic Alliance">Social Democratic Alliance</a>). Guðmundur had been elected as a candidate of the Progressive Party, but left the party to sit as an independent. In 2012, Guðmundur formed Bright Future with the <a href="/wiki/Best_Party" title="Best Party">Best Party</a>, with which it shares initials in Icelandic, "BF".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The party was formed to contest the <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_parliamentary_election,_2013" title="Icelandic parliamentary election, 2013">April 2013 parliamentary election</a>. The party won six seats, making it the fifth largest in parliament, but has since dropped significantly in opinion polls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ideology">Ideology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bright_Future_(Iceland)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ideology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party supports Iceland joining the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> and adopting the <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">euro</a> currency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lansford2015_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lansford2015-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grape_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-grape-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
