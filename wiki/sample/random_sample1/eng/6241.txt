<p><i><b>Bambino!</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">バンビーノ!</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Banbiino!</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. "Baby")</span> is a Japanese cooking <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> written and illustrated by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tetsuji_Sekiya&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tetsuji Sekiya (page does not exist)">Tetsuji Sekiya</a>. The manga has been serialized in <a href="/wiki/Shogakukan" title="Shogakukan">Shogakukan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Seinen_manga" title="Seinen manga">seinen magazine</a> (aimed at older men) <i><a href="/wiki/Big_Comic_Spirits" title="Big Comic Spirits">Big Comic Spirits</a></i>. As of February 2009, <a href="/wiki/Shogakukan" title="Shogakukan">Shogakukan</a> has published 14 <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> of the manga. It received the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Shogakukan_Manga_Award" title="Shogakukan Manga Award">Shogakukan Manga Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seinen" title="Seinen">seinen</a>/general manga along with Takeshi Natsuhara's and Kuromaru's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurosagi" title="Kurosagi">Kurosagi</a></i>.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nippon_Television" title="Nippon Television">NTV</a> broadcast the live-action <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dorama" title="Dorama">TV drama</a> from April 18, 2007 to June 27, 2007. It was broadcast in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a> by TV Japan, an affiliate of <a href="/wiki/NHK" title="NHK">NHK</a>, from January to March 2008.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack_CD">Soundtrack CD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bambino!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Soundtrack CD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On March 30, 2007, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wint&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wint (page does not exist)">wint</a> released a soundtrack CD for the <i>Bambino!</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_drama" title="TV drama">TV drama</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bambino!&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i>Bambino!</i> manga received the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Shogakukan_Manga_Award" title="Shogakukan Manga Award">Shogakukan Manga Award</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seinen" title="Seinen">seinen</a>/general manga along with Takeshi Natsuhara's and Kuromaru's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurosagi" title="Kurosagi">Kurosagi</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sho_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-sho-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the <b>53rd Television Drama Academy Awards</b>, the <i>Bambino!</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_drama" title="Television drama">television drama</a> was awarded four prizes. They were: Best Actor (<a href="/wiki/Jun_Matsumoto" title="Jun Matsumoto">Jun Matsumoto</a>), Supporting Actor (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kitamura_Kazuki" title="Kitamura Kazuki">Kitamura Kazuki</a>), Best Director and Special Award.</p>
