<p><b>Vandœuvre-lès-Nancy</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[vɑ̃dœvʁ lɛ nɑ̃si]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France">commune</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Meurthe-et-Moselle" title="Meurthe-et-Moselle">Meurthe-et-Moselle</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a> in north-eastern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. Its inhabitants are called <i>Vandopériens</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vand%C5%93uvre-l%C3%A8s-Nancy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With 30,569 inhabitants (2012), Vandœuvre is the second-largest commune in the Meurthe-et-Moselle department, after the capital <a href="/wiki/Nancy,_France" title="Nancy, France">Nancy</a>, of which it is a <a href="/wiki/Suburb" title="Suburb">suburb</a>. These two cities belong to the same municipal area: Grand Nancy.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Municipal_Market">Municipal Market</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vand%C5%93uvre-l%C3%A8s-Nancy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Municipal Market">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Colorful, vibrant, dynamic, cosmopolitan, municipal market Vandoeuvre is the image of his city. Reputed This is one of the most important markets of North -East of France. It hosts Sunday morning 120 to 150 merchants and 6-8000 visitors.</p>
