<p><i><b>Wandering Son</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">放浪息子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Hōrō Musuko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Takako_Shimura" title="Takako Shimura">Takako Shimura</a>. It was originally serialized in <i><a href="/wiki/Comic_Beam" title="Comic Beam">Comic Beam</a></i> from the December 2002 to August 2013 issue, and published in 15 <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes by <a href="/wiki/Enterbrain" title="Enterbrain">Enterbrain</a> from July 2003 to August 2013. The series is licensed in English by <a href="/wiki/Fantagraphics_Books" title="Fantagraphics Books">Fantagraphics Books</a>, which released the first volume in North America in July 2011. A 12-episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> adaptation produced by <a href="/wiki/Anime_International_Company" title="Anime International Company">AIC Classic</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Ei_Aoki" title="Ei Aoki">Ei Aoki</a> aired in Japan between January and March 2011. Eleven episodes aired on television, with episodes 10 and 11 edited into a single episode, and were released individually on their respective BD/DVD volumes.</p>
<p>The story depicts a young student named Shuichi Nitori, described by the author as a boy who wants to be a girl, and Shuichi's friend Yoshino Takatsuki, described as a girl who wants to be a boy. The series deals with issues such as <a href="/wiki/Transgender" title="Transgender">transgenderism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gender_identity" title="Gender identity">gender identity</a>, and the beginning of <a href="/wiki/Puberty" title="Puberty">puberty</a>. Shimura was originally going to write the story about a girl in high school who wants to be a boy, but she realized that a boy who wants to become a girl before entering into puberty would have a lot of worries related to growing up, and changed the story to fit this model. <i>Wandering Son</i> was selected as a recommended work by the awards jury of the tenth <a href="/wiki/Japan_Media_Arts_Festival" title="Japan Media Arts Festival">Japan Media Arts Festival</a> in 2006. The series has been lauded for its use of gender reversal as the core of the story, though the emotional realism of the young characters has been called into question.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wandering_Son&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the start of <i>Wandering Son</i>, Shuichi Nitori is a young, <a href="/wiki/Femininity" title="Femininity">feminine</a> child assigned male at birth (<a href="/wiki/Sex_assignment" title="Sex assignment">AMAB</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Fifth_grade" title="Fifth grade">fifth grade</a> who transfers into a new school. Shuichi quickly becomes friends with another student, tall, boyish Yoshino Takatsuki. Yoshino soon learns of Shuichi's desire to be a girl. In a show of friendship, Yoshino confesses a similar desire to be a boy. Shuichi also becomes friends with Saori Chiba and Kanako Sasa, two other girls in the class. Saori instantly takes a liking to Shuichi and continuously encourages Shuichi to wear feminine clothes. After Shuichi, Yoshino, and their friends enter sixth grade, Shuichi meets Makoto Ariga, another AMAB student their age from another class who also secretly wants to be a girl. Shuichi and Yoshino become friends with an adult <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transsexualism" title="Transsexualism">transsexual</a> woman named Yuki who is living with a man named Shiina. Shuichi's older sister Maho becomes a model and eventually becomes friends with Maiko, a teen model whom she idolizes, and two other teen models: Tamaki Satō and Anna Suehiro. Maho gets a boyfriend, Riku Seya, and Shuichi confesses a crush on Yoshino, but Yoshino cannot reciprocate Shuichi's feelings. After Saori learns of this, she confesses she likes Shuichi, but Shuichi too cannot return her feelings. This results in a falling-out between Shuichi's friends as they prepare to enter junior high school.</p>
<p>In junior high school, they meet a tall, eccentric girl who befriends everyone named Chizuru Sarashina and her prickly friend Momoko Shirai, who does not get along well with the others—especially Saori. Eventually, Saori and Yoshino rejoin Shuichi's group of friends, though Saori says she still hates Yoshino and Momoko. Shuichi and Anna start dating, much to the surprise of their friends and Shuichi's sister. Yoshino and Saori manage to halfway repair their friendship, though Saori is still standoffish to others. Shuichi's friends are split up into several classes upon entering their second year in junior high school. Shuichi becomes friends with Shinpei Doi, who previously teased Shuichi about wanting to be a girl. Yoshino attends school in a boy's uniform for a short time, and Shuichi tries to go to school dressed as a girl one day, but is laughed at, and becomes discouraged. Shuichi's friends worry as Shuichi begins skipping school. Although Shuichi eventually starts attending school regularly again, Anna breaks off their relationship. By the time Shuichi, Yoshino, and their friends enter their third year in junior high school, Shuichi's <a href="/wiki/Puberty#Voice_change" title="Puberty">voice is changing</a>. The group of friends start thinking about their future high school plans, and Shuichi and Anna start dating again.</p>
<p>Shuichi begins attending the same all-boy high school as Makoto and Doi, while Yoshino and Saori begin attending a high school where uniforms are not required. Saori starts dating Fumiya Ninomiya. Yoshino starts working at Anna's modeling agency and Shuichi begins working at a cafe, but later quits. Shuichi starts writing a semi-autobiographical novel. Yoshino later tells Shuichi that Yoshino does not think about wanting to be a boy anymore, and also confesses a romantic attraction to Shuichi. Shuichi still identifies as a girl, and even after informing Anna of this, she stays with Shuichi. After graduating from high school, Shuichi moves out and goes to the same college as Doi. Shuichi continues to write the novel, which is given the title <i>The Boy Who's a Girl</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ぼくは、おんなのこ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Boku wa, Onna no Ko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>.</p>

<dt>Shuichi Nitori <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">二鳥 修一</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nitori Shūichi</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Kōsuke Hatakeyama</span></dd>
<dd>Shuichi, one of two protagonists, is a young, <a href="/wiki/Effeminacy" title="Effeminacy">effeminate</a> child who at the beginning of <i>Wandering Son</i> is in the fifth grade of <a href="/wiki/Elementary_school" title="Elementary school">elementary school</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch1-1">[1]</a></sup> Otherwise known by the nicknames Shu <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">シュウ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shū</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> and Nitorin <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">にとりん</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, Shuichi was assigned male at birth, but <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transsexualism" title="Transsexualism">wants to be a girl</a> and often <a href="/wiki/Cross-dressing" title="Cross-dressing">cross-dresses</a> to assume the role of the female gender. Shuichi is described as cute by many of the other characters and is able to appear as a girl when cross-dressing, because of a <a href="/wiki/Femininity" title="Femininity">feminine</a>-looking face and physical build. Shuichi enjoys wearing cute clothes; although initially not a cross-dresser, Shuichi's friends Yoshino Takatsuki and Saori Chiba encourage Shuichi to dress and act femininely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch2-2">[2]</a></sup> As Shuichi grows up, <a href="/wiki/Puberty" title="Puberty">puberty</a> becomes a topic of increasing concern, such as the growth of body hair,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch44_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch44-3">[3]</a></sup> a deepening voice,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch39_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch39-4">[4]</a></sup> and the onset of <a href="/wiki/Pimple" title="Pimple">pimples</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch57_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch57-5">[5]</a></sup> Shuichi exhibits signs of <a href="/wiki/Gender_identity" title="Gender identity">gender</a> <a href="/wiki/Dysphoria" title="Dysphoria">dysphoria</a> and displays an outward attraction to two girls in the series—Yoshino<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch31_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch31-6">[6]</a></sup> and Anna Suehiro. Shuichi and Anna date for a time in junior high,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch53_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch53-7">[7]</a></sup> until she breaks off their relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch74_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch74-8">[8]</a></sup> However, the two soon resume their relationship with each other.</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Shuichi is interested in doing indoor activities, and does not partake in activities favored by boys of the same age, such as playing sports. Shuichi enjoys and is skillful in <a href="/wiki/Baking" title="Baking">baking</a> <a href="/wiki/Confectionery" title="Confectionery">sweet foods</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch1-1">[1]</a></sup> though is never shown <a href="/wiki/Cooking" title="Cooking">cooking</a> other, more conventional foods. After starting an exchange diary with Yoshino, Shuichi becomes interested in writing stories,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch28_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch28-9">[9]</a></sup> and even joins the drama club as a writer with Saori in junior high school after they co-write a rendition of <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a></i> performed by members of their class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch52_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch52-10">[10]</a></sup> Shuichi has an honest personality, and easily gets along with others. As a child, Shuichi has an obedient, dutiful attitude. However, upon entering puberty, Shuichi sometimes experiences outbursts of intense emotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch27_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch27-11">[11]</a></sup> Shuichi can be overly sensitive at times and is often shown crying in front of others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch28_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch28-9">[9]</a></sup> Due to these personality quirks, it is easier for Shuichi to associate with girls of the same age. Accordingly, Shuichi has few male friends.</dd>
</dl>
<p><span id="Yoshino_Takatsuki"></span></p>
<dl>
<dt>Yoshino Takatsuki <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">高槻 よしの</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Takatsuki Yoshino</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Asami_Seto" title="Asami Seto">Asami Seto</a></span></dd>
<dd>Yoshino, the other protagonist, is a tall, <a href="/wiki/Masculinity" title="Masculinity">masculine</a> child who at the beginning of <i>Wandering Son</i> is a fifth grader in Shuichi's class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch1_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-ch1-1">[1]</a></sup> Otherwise known as Takatsuki-kun <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">高槻くん</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by their classmates, with an <a href="/wiki/Japanese_honorifics" title="Japanese honorifics">honorific</a> used mainly with boys, Yoshino was assigned female at birth, but <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transsexualism" title="Transsexualism">wants to be a boy</a>. Yoshino usually refrains from dressing in traditionally feminine clothes, such as skirts or dresses. Despite this, Yoshino's mother insists on buying such clothes for Yoshino.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch1_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-ch1-1">[1]</a></sup> Shortly after becoming friends with Shuichi, Yoshino gets a haircut in a boyish style, taking on the appearance of a boy their age, especially when Yoshino is dressed in a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_school_uniform#Gakuran" title="Japanese school uniform">male school uniform</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch4_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch4-12">[12]</a></sup> Like Shuichi, Yoshino becomes increasingly concerned and resentful about undergoing puberty, such as at the beginning of <a href="/wiki/Menstruation" title="Menstruation">menstruation</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch7_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch7-13">[13]</a></sup> or the growth of breasts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch40_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch40-14">[14]</a></sup> Yoshino goes to buy a chest-flattening garment to escape the necessity of wearing a bra.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch56_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch56-15">[15]</a></sup> Also like Shuichi, Yoshino shows signs indicating gender dysphoria,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch57_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch57-5">[5]</a></sup> though later stops thinking that way due to personal exposure to modeling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch116_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch116-16">[16]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>Yoshino prefers to dress and act like a boy, but usually abstains from anything which would draw attention, such as going to school in a male uniform after entering junior high. However, Yoshino does on occasion dress in a male school uniform and go to neighboring cities while dressed as a boy; during one such time, Yoshino enjoys being hit on by an older woman.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch4_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch4-12">[12]</a></sup> Yoshino wants to look "cool" and wears clothes which are not girlish. When teased by others, Yoshino is prone to getting emotional and is known to get violent on occasion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch8_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch8-17">[17]</a></sup> Yoshino takes up an interest in basketball after entering junior high school and joins the female basketball team with Chizuru Sarashina.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch36_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch36-18">[18]</a></sup> In junior high, Yoshino decides not to get a haircut after a comment by Saori,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch58_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch58-19">[19]</a></sup> but later cuts it back to a short, boyish style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch75_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch75-20">[20]</a></sup> Yoshino's family consists of a father, mother, older brother, and older sister.</dd>
</dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Classmates">Classmates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wandering_Son&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Classmates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><span id="Saori_Chiba"></span></p>
<dl>
<dt>Saori Chiba <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">千葉 さおり</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Chiba Saori</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuuka_Nanri" title="Yuuka Nanri">Yuuka Nanri</a></span></dd>
<dd>Saori, nicknamed Saorin <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">さおりん</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, is a girl who is a fifth grader in Shuichi's class at the beginning of <i>Wandering Son</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch1_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-ch1-1">[1]</a></sup> She takes an interest in encouraging Shuichi to cross-dress, even going so far as to buy Shuichi an expensive dress for Shuichi's birthday, which Shuichi later returns, much to her displeasure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch6_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch6-21">[21]</a></sup> Saori finds it difficult to associate with others; she has few friends, though becomes close to Shuichi. She tends to convey what is on her mind and disregards how others may take what she says. As Saori grows up, she gradually becomes more standoffish and stoic; she even has bad relations with her teachers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch10_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch10-22">[22]</a></sup> Despite her self-centered attitude, she remains popular among her male classmates due to her physical attractiveness. She is a sensitive girl whose temper sometimes gets the better of her when reprimanding bullies that tease Shuichi, because of Shuichi's femininity and cross-dressing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch17_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch17-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch47_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch47-24">[24]</a></sup> Saori is often emotionally affected by circumstances involving Shuichi, because of her feelings for Shuichi. Saori converts to <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> partly because of this influence to seek forgiveness for her previous actions,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch7_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch7-13">[13]</a></sup> though she only attends mass when feeling guilty about her behavior.</dd>
</dl>
<p><span id="Kanako_Sasa"></span></p>
<dl>
<dt>Kanako Sasa <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">佐々 かなこ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sasa Kanako</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Yoshino_Nanj%C5%8D" title="Yoshino Nanjō">Yoshino Nanjō</a></span></dd>
<dd>Kanako Sasa, usually referred to by her surname, is a girl who is a fifth grader in Shuichi's class at the beginning of <i>Wandering Son</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch1_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-ch1-1">[1]</a></sup> She is sometimes called Kanabun <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">カナブン</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhomborrhina_japonica" title="Rhomborrhina japonica">drone beetle</a>)</span> by her younger brother and Chizuru. She makes her first appearance in chapter two of the manga, but is not named until volume two. She is a short, energetic girl who thinks of herself as everyone's friend and becomes distressed when her friends fight among themselves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch21_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch21-25">[25]</a></sup> Sasa acts as a mediator between her friends in such times and tries not to leave anyone alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch36_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch36-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch33_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch33-26">[26]</a></sup> She has been friends with Yoshino since pre-school,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch13_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch13-27">[27]</a></sup> and later becomes close with Chizuru. Sasa is an innocent, childish girl who does not have many worries aside from her friends' quarrels. She tends to be a handful for her mother, who picks out her clothes for her and even helps her get ready for school in the morning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch25_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch25-28">[28]</a></sup> She mentions that if she thinks too hard about something, her brain becomes itchy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch3_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch3-29">[29]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p><span id="Makoto_Ariga"></span></p>
<dl>
<dt>Makoto Ariga <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">有賀 誠</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ariga Makoto</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%ABichi_Iguchi" title="Yūichi Iguchi">Yūichi Iguchi</a></span></dd>
<dd>Makoto first appears when the cast is in sixth grade, though is in a different class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch21_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch21-25">[25]</a></sup> Known as Mako for short, though mostly only to Shuichi, Makoto was also assigned male at birth, but wishes to be a girl and shares Shuichi's interest in cross-dressing;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch21_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-ch21-25">[25]</a></sup> it is this that spurs Makoto to become Shuichi's closest friend. Due to facial <a href="/wiki/Freckle" title="Freckle">freckles</a> and round glasses, Makoto is not able to appear as cute as Shuichi when wearing feminine clothes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch50_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch50-30">[30]</a></sup> Makoto's primary reason for wanting to be a girl may be due to an attraction to men, something Shuichi cannot relate to; Makoto is a romantic who wants to be in a relationship with a cool, adult man.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch36_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-ch36-18">[18]</a></sup> Makoto is unusually mature and is able to think calmly and objectively while providing advice to friends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch25_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch25-28">[28]</a></sup> Makoto also gets along well with boys and girls of the same age, because of good listening skills; Makoto often becomes an onlooker to what is going on in other characters' lives. Makoto is an only child whose parents run a bakery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch25_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-ch25-28">[28]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p><span id="Chizuru_Sarashina"></span></p>
<dl>
<dt>Chizuru Sarashina <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">更科 千鶴</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Sarashina Chizuru</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Saeko_Chiba" title="Saeko Chiba">Saeko Chiba</a></span></dd>
<dd>Chizuru, nicknamed Chii-chan, is a tall girl who first appears as a classmate of Shuichi when they both enter junior high school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch34_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch34-31">[31]</a></sup> She is Momoko's childhood friend, and has a stylish demeanor, highlighted by her stature and long hair, which captivates both Shuichi and Yoshino when they first meet her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch34_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch34-31">[31]</a></sup> Chizuru is described as a free spirit, someone who enjoys doing unconventional, often outrageous things which surprise those around her; she frequently acts without thinking, and as a result projects a childish personality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch39_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch39-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch41_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch41-32">[32]</a></sup> Her impulsive behavior sometimes gets her in trouble with other characters, but she quickly becomes ashamed when she realizes the consequences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch52_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch52-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch43_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch43-33">[33]</a></sup> She tries to be everyone's friend, though Saori strongly dislikes her impulsiveness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch38_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch38-34">[34]</a></sup> Chizuru joins the girls' basketball team in junior high. Her family runs a <a href="/wiki/Soba" title="Soba">soba</a> restaurant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch34_31-2"><a href="#cite_note-ch34-31">[31]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p><span id="Momoko_Shirai"></span></p>
<dl>
<dt>Momoko Shirai <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">白井 桃子</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shirai Momoko</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Aki_Toyosaki" title="Aki Toyosaki">Aki Toyosaki</a></span></dd>
<dd>Momoko is a childhood friend and classmate in junior high school of Chizuru;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch34_31-3"><a href="#cite_note-ch34-31">[31]</a></sup> she is nicknamed Momo. She constantly hangs around Chizuru and is visibly annoyed when Chizuru socializes with others, or even if someone simply sits too close to her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch50_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch50-30">[30]</a></sup> Momoko inevitably starts fighting Saori, who does not particularly respect Chizuru, when they talk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch46_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch46-35">[35]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>

<p><span id="Maho_Nitori"></span></p>
<dl>
<dt>Maho Nitori <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">二鳥 真穂</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nitori Maho</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span></dt>

<dd>Maho is Shuichi's sister. She is one year older than Shuichi is, and at the beginning of <i>Wandering Son</i>, she is in the sixth grade of elementary school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch1_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-ch1-1">[1]</a></sup> Maho shows a strong interest in clothes, and spends much of her money on new apparel. She is a big fan of a teen fashion model named Maiko, and to meet her, Maho auditions for the same modeling agency as Maiko.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch18_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch18-36">[36]</a></sup> After she is hired and becomes recognized as a model, Maho enjoys being asked her autograph by complete strangers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch37_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch37-37">[37]</a></sup> While her modeling career initially starts out slowly, Maho soon gains confidence in her abilities, and becomes friends with Maiko and two other models, Anna Suehiro and Tamaki Satō.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch27_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch27-11">[11]</a></sup> In contrast to her younger sibling, Maho is very out-spoken and tends to be rough with Shuichi, even hitting or slapping Shuichi on occasion,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch21_25-3"><a href="#cite_note-ch21-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch48_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch48-38">[38]</a></sup> and she often forces things on Shuichi with little consideration of Shuichi's opinion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch21_25-4"><a href="#cite_note-ch21-25">[25]</a></sup> She does not like Shuichi's cross-dressing and gets angry and disturbed when she finds Shuichi dressed as a girl.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch36_18-3"><a href="#cite_note-ch36-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch48_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch48-38">[38]</a></sup> In junior high school, Maho becomes attracted to her classmate Riku Seya, and eventually starts dating him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch33_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch33-26">[26]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wandering_Son&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In an interview in August 2003, Takako Shimura stated that the theme of <i>Wandering Son</i> is similar to the second half of her previous manga series <i>Shikii no Jūnin</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimura-interview1_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shimura-interview1-55">[55]</a></sup> Shimura took the junior high school teacher Kentarō Kaneda from <i>Shikii no Jūnin</i> and inserted him into <i>Wandering Son</i>, where he teaches at Shuichi's junior high school, because she really liked his character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aw2_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-aw2-56">[56]</a></sup> Shimura originally planned to use a female high school student who wants to become a boy as the main character. However, she realized that a boy who wants to become a girl before entering into <a href="/wiki/Puberty" title="Puberty">puberty</a> would have many worries related to growing up, and changed the story accordingly. Shimura used her realization that the boy would go through significant changes as he grew up to deepen the development of the story and characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimura-interview1_55-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shimura-interview1-55">[55]</a></sup> The Japanese title, <i>Hōrō Musuko</i>, is a <a href="/wiki/Pun" title="Pun">pun</a> on <i>hōtō musuko</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">放蕩息子</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, meaning "<a href="/wiki/Parable_of_the_Prodigal_Son" title="Parable of the Prodigal Son">prodigal son</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimura-interview2.1_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shimura-interview2.1-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shimura mainly found her characters' names by looking through name dictionaries, although she also took the names of acquaintances and slightly changed them, and even used <a href="/wiki/Train_station" title="Train station">train station</a> names for side characters appearing only once.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimura-interview2.1_57-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shimura-interview2.1-57">[57]</a></sup> Out of all the characters, Shimura is most pleased with Kanako Sasa. For the designs of clothes for the female characters, Shimura consulted various <a href="/wiki/List_of_fashion_magazines" title="List of fashion magazines">fashion magazines</a> for girls in their early teens, especially <i><a href="/wiki/Nicola_(magazine)" title="Nicola (magazine)">Nicola</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shimura-interview2.1_57-2"><a href="#cite_note-Shimura-interview2.1-57">[57]</a></sup> Shimura commented <a href="/wiki/Self-deprecation" title="Self-deprecation">self-deprecatingly</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Afterword" title="Afterword">afterword</a> of volume one that, like her other series, her characters do not look very different from each other, her <a href="/wiki/Panel_(comics)" title="Panel (comics)">panels</a> are too white, and there is much <a href="/wiki/Pathos" title="Pathos">pathos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aw1_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-aw1-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wandering_Son&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wandering_Son&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Wandering_Son_chapters" title="List of Wandering Son chapters">List of Wandering Son chapters</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> <i>Wandering Son</i> is written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Takako_Shimura" title="Takako Shimura">Takako Shimura</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vol1_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-vol1-59">[59]</a></sup> It was serialized in the monthly <i><a href="/wiki/Seinen_manga" title="Seinen manga">seinen</a></i> (aimed at younger adult men) <a href="/wiki/List_of_manga_magazines" title="List of manga magazines">manga magazine</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Comic_Beam" title="Comic Beam">Comic Beam</a></i> from the December 2002 to August 2013 issue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-manga-end_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-manga-end-61">[61]</a></sup> The individual chapters were collected and published in 15 <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> volumes by <a href="/wiki/Enterbrain" title="Enterbrain">Enterbrain</a> from July 25, 2003 to August 28, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vol1_59-1"><a href="#cite_note-vol1-59">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vol15_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-vol15-62">[62]</a></sup> <i>Wandering Son</i> was one of several manga titles included with the launch in December 2009 of the manga distribution service of the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Store" title="PlayStation Store">PlayStation Store</a> for the Japanese <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a> <a href="/wiki/Handheld_game_console" title="Handheld game console">handheld game console</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<p>The series is licensed in English by <a href="/wiki/Fantagraphics_Books" title="Fantagraphics Books">Fantagraphics Books</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> which began releasing the series in North America in <a href="/wiki/Hardcover" title="Hardcover">hardcover</a> format starting with the first volume on July 5, 2011. <a href="/wiki/Gary_Groth" title="Gary Groth">Gary Groth</a> of Fantagraphics Books said in an interview he licensed <i>Wandering Son</i> because "it's not a typical choice for a manga title published in the U.S. and it's not typical subject matter for comics in general,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Groth_66-0"><a href="#cite_note-Groth-66">[66]</a></sup> saying that the subject is "perfectly legitimate ... for literature—or comics."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Groth_66-1"><a href="#cite_note-Groth-66">[66]</a></sup> The series is also licensed by <a href="/wiki/Ever_Glory_Publishing" title="Ever Glory Publishing">Ever Glory Publishing</a> in Taiwan<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> and by <a href="/wiki/Haksan_Culture_Company" title="Haksan Culture Company">Haksan Culture Company</a> in Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wandering_Son&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Wandering_Son_episodes" title="List of Wandering Son episodes">List of Wandering Son episodes</a></div>
<p>A 12-episode <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> TV series adaptation produced by <a href="/wiki/Anime_International_Company" title="Anime International Company">AIC Classic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aniplex" title="Aniplex">Aniplex</a> aired in Japan between January 13 and March 31, 2011 on <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Television" title="Fuji Television">Fuji TV</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Noitamina" title="Noitamina">Noitamina</a> <a href="/wiki/Block_programming" title="Block programming">programming block</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-anime-news_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-anime-news-69">[69]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-anime-news-2_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-anime-news-2-70">[70]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a> simulcasted the anime on their streaming website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> Aniplex released the anime on six <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a> and DVD compilation volumes in Japan between April 27 and September 21, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bd-dvd_72-0"><a href="#cite_note-bd-dvd-72">[72]</a></sup> Of the 11 episodes to be aired on TV, episodes 10 and 11 were edited into a single episode, and were released individually on their respective BD/DVD volumes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10.2B11_73-0"><a href="#cite_note-10.2B11-73">[73]</a></sup> Following the arrest of Ai Takabe, the voice actress who played Maiko, for drug possession in October 2015, <a href="/wiki/Bandai_Channel" title="Bandai Channel">Bandai Channel</a> removed the series from its streaming catalog.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> The anime adapts the story from the point where the characters enter junior high school.</p>
<p>The anime is directed by Ei Aoki and the screenplay was written by <a href="/wiki/Mari_Okada" title="Mari Okada">Mari Okada</a>. Chief animator Ryūichi Makino based the character design used in the anime on Takako Shimura's original concept and the main animator is Michio Satō.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-staff_75-0"><a href="#cite_note-staff-75">[75]</a></sup> The music was produced by Satoru Kosaki and Keiichi Okabe, both from Monaca,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> and the sound director is Jin Aketagawa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-staff_75-1"><a href="#cite_note-staff-75">[75]</a></sup> The anime's opening theme song is "Itsudatte." <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">いつだって。</span><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> by Daisuke and the single was released on March 2, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> The ending theme is "For You" by <a href="/wiki/Rie_fu" title="Rie fu">Rie fu</a> and the single was released on February 16, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> The original soundtrack was released on August 24, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wandering_Son&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was reported in June 2013 that approximately 1.05 million copies of the manga are in print in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-manga-end_61-1"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-manga-end-61">[61]</a></sup> <i>Wandering Son</i> was selected as a recommended work by the awards jury of the tenth <a href="/wiki/Japan_Media_Arts_Festival" title="Japan Media Arts Festival">Japan Media Arts Festival</a> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10thJMAF_80-0"><a href="#cite_note-10thJMAF-80">[80]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Young_Adult_Library_Services_Association" title="Young Adult Library Services Association">Young Adult Library Services Association</a> nominated <i>Wandering Son</i> for its 2012 Great Graphic Novels for Teens list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> The anime was awarded the honorable mention prize for technical achievement in broadcast animation at the 65th Motion Picture and Television Engineering Society of Japan Awards in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a review of the first volume by Rebecca Silverman of <a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">Anime News Network</a> (ANN), she praised the slow pace of the storytelling, which "gives it a more realistic feel."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-vol1-review_83-0"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-vol1-review-83">[83]</a></sup> Silverman praises Takako Shimura for making Shuichi into a "human protagonist", but notes that "most of the children act much older than they are."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-vol1-review_83-1"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-vol1-review-83">[83]</a></sup> The second volume was featured in ANN's Right Turn Only <a href="/wiki/Column_(periodical)" title="Column (periodical)">column</a> in March 2007 as the Import of the Month, where Carlo Santos lauded the series for using gender reversal as the "actual heart of the story" in contrast to "every other series" involving cross-dressing, which use "gender reversal as a goofy plot device."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-RTO_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-RTO-84">[84]</a></sup> The art was praised as "simple [with] few lines, but incredibly expressive" which Santos claimed is a "style that's the most difficult and beautiful of all." Santos criticized the "emotional realism" of the work for having the young characters' "unrealistically mature attitude" towards "issues above their grade level."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-RTO_84-1"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-RTO-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Matt_Thorn" title="Matt Thorn">Matt Thorn</a>, the English translator of the manga, wrote that fans of <i><a href="/wiki/Anne_of_Green_Gables" title="Anne of Green Gables">Anne of Green Gables</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rose_of_Versailles" title="The Rose of Versailles">The Rose of Versailles</a></i> would also enjoy <i>Wandering Son</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup> and Silverman compared <i>Wandering Son</i> to <i><a href="/wiki/Mizuiro_Jidai" title="Mizuiro Jidai">Mizuiro Jidai</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN-vol1-review_83-2"><a href="#cite_note-ANN-vol1-review-83">[83]</a></sup> Thorn described the art as "clean and lovely" and went on to cite <i>Wandering Son</i> as "sweet, thought-provoking, funny, and moving, and I think it will resonate with readers regardless of their gender identity or sexual orientation."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> The first manga volume as translated by Fantagraphics Books had an early debut at the May 2011 Toronto Comic Arts Festival and sold out within the first two hours of the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup></p>
