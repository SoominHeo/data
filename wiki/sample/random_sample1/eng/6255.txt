<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Trigger strategy</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>In <a href="/wiki/Game_theory" title="Game theory">game theory</a>, a <b>trigger strategy</b> is any of a class of strategies employed in a repeated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Game_Theory" title="Game Theory">non-cooperative game</a>. A player using a trigger strategy initially cooperates but punishes the opponent if a certain level of defection (i.e., the trigger) is observed. The level of punishment and the sensitivity of the trigger vary with different trigger strategies.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trigger_strategies">Trigger strategies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trigger_strategy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Trigger strategies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tit_for_tat" title="Tit for tat">Tit for tat</a> (the punishment continues as long as the other player defects)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tit_for_tat#Tit_for_two_tats" title="Tit for tat">Tit for two tats</a> (a more forgiving variant of tit for tat)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Grim_trigger" title="Grim trigger">Grim trigger</a> (the punishment continues indefinitely after the other player defects just once)</li>
</ul>
