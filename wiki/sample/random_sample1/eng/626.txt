<p>Year <b>477</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">CDLXXVII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Common year starting on Saturday">common year starting on Saturday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year after the Consulship of Basiliscus and Armatus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1230 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 477 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=477&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=477&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=477&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_25" title="January 25">January 25</a> – King <a href="/wiki/Genseric" title="Genseric">Genseric</a> dies a natural death at <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthage</a>, and is succeeded by his eldest son <a href="/wiki/Huneric" title="Huneric">Huneric</a>. He maintains control with his <a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandal</a> fleet over the islands in the western <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, and rescinds his father's policy of persecuting the Roman <a href="/wiki/Catholicism" title="Catholicism">Catholics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Africa_(Roman_province)" title="Africa (Roman province)">Africa</a>.</li>
<li>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon_Chronicle" title="Anglo-Saxon Chronicle">Anglo-Saxon Chronicle</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aelle_of_Sussex" title="Aelle of Sussex">Aelle</a>, first king of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sussex" title="Kingdom of Sussex">South Saxons</a>, lands on the <a href="/wiki/Sussex" title="Sussex">Sussex</a> coast (<a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>), with his three sons, near <a href="/wiki/Cymenshore" title="Cymenshore">Cymenshore</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britons_(historical)" title="Britons (historical)">Britons</a> engage him upon landing, but his superior force <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">besieges</a> them at <a href="/wiki/Pevensey" title="Pevensey">Pevensey</a> and drives them into the <a href="/wiki/Weald" title="Weald">Weald</a>. Over the next nine years, <a href="/wiki/Saxon_Shore" title="Saxon Shore">Saxon</a> coastal holdings are gradually expanded.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=477&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Byzantine Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Armatus" title="Armatus">Armatus</a>, Byzantine military commander (<i><a href="/wiki/Magister_militum" title="Magister militum">magister militum</a></i>), is killed by order of emperor <a href="/wiki/Zeno_(emperor)" title="Zeno (emperor)">Zeno</a>. He is murdered by his own friend <a href="/wiki/Onoulphus" title="Onoulphus">Onoulphus</a> after supporting the rebellion of his uncle <a href="/wiki/Basiliscus" title="Basiliscus">Basiliscus</a> (see <a href="/wiki/475" title="475">475</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=477&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Emperor_Shun_of_Liu_Song" title="Emperor Shun of Liu Song">Shun Di</a>, age 10, becomes emperor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liu_Song_Dynasty" title="Liu Song Dynasty">Liu Song Dynasty</a>, after his brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Houfei_of_Liu_Song" title="Emperor Houfei of Liu Song">Houfei Di</a> is <a href="/wiki/Assassination" title="Assassination">assassinated</a> by general <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Gao_of_Southern_Qi" title="Emperor Gao of Southern Qi">Xiao Daocheng</a>. He installs him as <a href="/wiki/Puppet_ruler" title="Puppet ruler">puppet ruler</a> and sets himself up as <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regent</a>. Xiao receives near-imperial powers and establishes <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Buddhism" title="Chinese Buddhism">Buddhism</a> as the <a href="/wiki/State_religion" title="State religion">state religion</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Shaolin_Monastery" title="Shaolin Monastery">Shaolin Monastery</a> is founded (according to the <i>Continued Biographies of Eminent Monks</i> (<a href="/wiki/645" title="645">645</a>) by <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A0oxu%C4%81n" title="Dàoxuān">Dàoxuān</a>; the monastery is built on the north side of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shaoshi_Mountain" title="Shaoshi Mountain">Shaoshi Mountain</a>, the western peak of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Song" title="Mount Song">Mount Song</a>, one of the four <a href="/wiki/Sacred_Mountains_of_China" title="Sacred Mountains of China">Sacred Mountains of China</a>, by emperor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_Xiaowen_of_Northern_Wei_China" title="Emperor Xiaowen of Northern Wei China">Xiao Wen Di</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Wei_Dynasty" title="Northern Wei Dynasty">Northern Wei Dynasty</a> in 477. <a href="/wiki/Yang_Xuanzhi" title="Yang Xuanzhi">Yang Xuanzhi</a>, in the <i>Record of the Buddhist Monasteries of Luoyang</i> (<a href="/wiki/547" title="547">547</a>), and Li Xian, in the <i>Ming Yitongzh</i>i (<a href="/wiki/1461" title="1461">1461</a>), concur with Daoxuan's location and attribution. For alternate founding date, see <a href="/wiki/495" title="495">495</a> or <a href="/wiki/497" title="497">497</a>).</li>
<li>Xiao Wen Di sets up the "Three Leaders" system, under which native <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(place)" title="Hamlet (place)">hamlet</a>, village and district officers are responsible for <a href="/wiki/Tax" title="Tax">taxation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conscription" title="Conscription">conscription</a>.</li>
<li>This year is the earliest date for the oldest known painted depiction of a <a href="/wiki/Horse_collar" title="Horse collar">horse collar</a>, on a cave <a href="/wiki/Mural" title="Mural">mural</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dunhuang" title="Dunhuang">Dunhuang</a>, during the Northern Wei Dynasty.</li>
</ul>
