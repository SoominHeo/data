<p><b>School holidays</b> (also referred to as <b>vacations</b>, <b>breaks</b>, and <b>recess</b>) are the periods during which schools are closed. The dates and periods of school holidays vary considerably throughout the world, and there is usually some variation even within the same jurisdiction. Governments often legislate on the total number of school days for state schools. The holidays given below apply to primary and secondary education. Teaching sessions (terms or semesters) in tertiary education are usually shorter.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Christmas_holiday">Christmas holiday</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Christmas holiday">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In predominantly <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> countries, the "Christmas holidays" (or "winter break" or "winter recess" in some countries) include both the Christmas and New Year holidays. They normally last for about 2–3 weeks. However the Christmas holiday in Europe is shorter, sometimes two weeks or slightly less. In the southern hemisphere, Christmas is in the summer holiday period and the break is much longer (six to eight weeks).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Easter">Easter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Easter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In countries with a Christian religious tradition, Easter holiday (in the United States it is sometimes known as Spring Break or Spring Vacation) is a school holiday that takes place in the northern spring, with the date varying by country and level of schooling.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Summer_holiday">Summer holiday</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Summer holiday">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Summer_vacation" title="Summer vacation">Summer vacation</a></div>
<p>In all countries, the longest break in the school year is during summer, lasting between 5 and 14 weeks. In America, Ireland, Italy, Lithuania and Russia, summer holidays are normally three months, compared to six to eight weeks in Britain, The Netherlands and Germany.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Holidays_in_the_world">Holidays in the world</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Holidays in the world">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nigeria">Nigeria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Nigeria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Nigeria, the longest holiday is from Mid July to second week of September for Secondary schools and a shorter duration within same period for tertiary institutions. Holidays are basically determined by schools and it starts from June to Early days in September.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In South Africa, the main holiday usually lasts from early December to early or mid-January (4 or 6 weeks). There is an autumn break of up to 2-week in late March or early April, a longer winter break in late June and early July, and a spring break in late September or early October. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.kwathabeng.co.za/travel/south-africa/destinations.html?cal=school" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Americas">Americas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Americas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Brazil">Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Brazil, summer holidays start in early December and end in late January or early February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Winter holidays are generally the entire month of July. Some schools in the tropical north follow a different school year.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Carnival" title="Brazilian Carnival">Brazilian Carnival</a> is 40 days before Easter Sunday and those dates are not school holidays.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Brazilian schools must have at least 200 school days. Some national and Catholic holidays are usually celebrated in 3 or 4 days, like Easter, Corpus Christi, Carnival and Tiradentes Day.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Canada, the summer holiday includes the months of July and August, with students returning to school in late August or early September. The winter break lasts for two weeks (sometimes a day or two longer), beginning on Saturday, that encompass Christmas Day and New Year's Day. The spring break is one to two weeks long depending on the city or province. Good Friday and Easter may or may not fall within spring break. In Ontario, Canada's largest province, spring break is a four-day-long weekend between Good Friday and Easter Monday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Chile">Chile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Chile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Academic_term" title="Academic term">school year</a> is divided into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Semester" title="Semester">semesters</a>. The first semester runs from the end of February or early March to July. Following a two-week winter break school resumes and lasts until late November or early December, followed by <a href="/wiki/Summer_vacation" title="Summer vacation">summer vacations</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Mexico, school usually ends in the second week of July and resumes the third week of August depending on what scheduling system a school uses, and whether it is high school or college. Christmas holiday is 2 weeks. Also there is the Easter recess which lasts 2 weeks.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/School_holidays_in_the_United_States" title="School holidays in the United States">School holidays in the United States</a></div>
<p>In the United States, there are typically 180 school days in a year<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> (although school years at colleges and universities are often shorter). Private schools tend to have classes for 170 days each year, but sometimes have them more or fewer days.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Event-based">Event-based</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Event-based">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<ul>
<li><i><b><a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving" title="Thanksgiving">Thanksgiving</a> Holiday</b> or <b>Break</b></i> - Occurs at the end of November. This holiday usually consists of the week of Thanksgiving - the day before Thanksgiving, Thanksgiving Day, and the day after.. Some areas have the Monday and Tuesday of the Thanksgiving week as regular days and take Wednesday as an early dismissal day; with Thanksgiving and the day after off. Other schools take the whole week off and have an early release on the Friday before break.</li>
<li><i><b><a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a></b> or <b>Holiday Break</b></i> - Varies in length per school; usually starts at the third Saturday in December and ends a day or two after New Year's Day (sometimes the first Monday after the New Year's Day), unless New Year's Day falls on a Sunday in which case the first Monday (January 2) is the official holiday and schools may not begin until January 3; the most recent occurrence of this was in the 2011-12 holiday break when January 1, 2012 fell on a Sunday. In the Southern hemisphere Christmas and the New Year are at the beginning of the long summer holiday.</li>
<li><i><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_Luther_King,_Jr._Day" title="Martin Luther King, Jr. Day">Martin Luther King Day</a> Break</b></i> - Martin Luther King Day is always on the third Monday of January each year. Some students have a full or half-day Friday and have the weekend, Martin Luther King day, and sometimes Tuesday off.</li>
<li><i><b><a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a> Break</b></i> - Usually lasts for one or two weeks in March or April (often starting on <a href="/wiki/Lazarus_Saturday" title="Lazarus Saturday">Lazarus Saturday</a>). Also referred to as <i>Spring Break</i>.</li>
<li><i><b><a href="/wiki/Columbus_Day" title="Columbus Day">Columbus Day</a> Break</b></i> - Columbus Day is always the second Monday in October. Many schools take a week off, though some areas only take Columbus Day itself off. In some states, however, Columbus Day is not observed as a school holiday at all.</li>
</ul>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Season-based">Season-based</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Season-based">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<ul>
<li><i><b>Autumn</b> or <b>Fall Break</b></i> - Usually lasts for one week in October (in the week of Columbus Day). See <i>Columbus Day</i> above.</li>
<li><i><b>Winter Break</b></i> - One week in February (usually at or around <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President%27s_Day" title="President's Day">President's Day</a>) or March (depending on the region).</li>
<li><i><b><a href="/wiki/Spring_break" title="Spring break">Spring Vacation</a></b> or <b>Break</b></i> - See <i>Easter Break</i> above.</li>
<li><i><b><a href="/wiki/Summer_vacation" title="Summer vacation">Summer Vacation</a></b> or <b>Break</b></i> - Lasts for about 12 weeks, starting anywhere from late May to late June, and ending anywhere from late August to the day after Labor Day in early September. This often depends on the region - for example, most schools in the <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">Northeastern United States</a> end in June and start just after Labor Day, while the majority of schools in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">Southern U.S.</a> have schools end in May and start again in August.</li>
<li><i><b>Teacher's Day off</b></i> - Exists in some school districts, being two to three days at any time in school calendar</li>
<li><i><b><a href="/wiki/Inservice_program" title="Inservice program">Inservice day(s)</a></b></i> - Scheduled breaks for teachers to participate in in-season training or discussions, or to attend the state or national conventions held by a <a href="/wiki/List_of_education_trade_unions" title="List of education trade unions">teacher's union</a>.</li>
<li><i>All federal and state holidays</i> - That includes religious holidays, such as <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday" title="Good Friday">Good Friday</a>; and sometimes Jewish and Islamic holidays, depending on school demographic.</li>
<li><i>Snow days</i> - (in regions where it snows in winter) - Usually is one or two days because of a snowstorm, but can last anywhere up to a full week.</li>
</ul>
<p><i>Note:</i> Often in Charter Schools breaks are shorter due to the extended number of days students spend in school (200 days vs. 180 days for standard public schools).</p>
<p><b>College breaks</b></p>
<p>Colleges and universities vary widely. Some closely follow the K-12 break schedule, others have the same but longer breaks to accommodate students who live farther away and wish to return home for holidays. Most colleges and universities have the following breaks/holidays:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Thanksgiving Holiday</b> - End of November (The week of Thanksgiving - 3 days before Thanksgiving, Thanksgiving Day, and the day after - the Friday before the break is considered a half-day).</li>
<li><b>Christmas/Holiday Break</b> - mid-December to late January</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Spring_break" title="Spring break">Spring Break</a></b> - a week in March or April (usually starting on Good Friday)</li>
<li><b>Summer Break</b> - Early/mid May to between late August and the day after Labor Day in September</li>
<li><b>Snow Days</b> (In regions where it snows in winter) - usually a few days up to a full week, and no days to make up.</li>
</ul>
<p>Most colleges and university years are divided into two semesters. The first starting from day after Labor Day in early September until mid-December, and the second lasting from the end of January until early May. Winter and summer classes could be taken in between the breaks.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Hong_Kong">Hong Kong</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Hong Kong">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Hong Kong summer holidays last from mid-July to the end of August. Christmas, Lunar New Year and Easter holidays last usually for one and a half weeks.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="South_India">South India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: South India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>In South India, summer vacations start in the last of April to beginning of June.</p>
<p>The Diwali break begins in either October or November, depending on the month <a href="/wiki/Diwali" title="Diwali">Diwali</a> occurs in that year. It lasts for about 1 week. Christmas break begins a few days before <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> and lasts till the weekend after New Year (also known as winter vacations).</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="North_India">North India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: North India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>In north India, the typical summer vacation runs from mid May to the end of June, school starting from July 1 usually.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dusherra" title="Dusherra">Dusherra</a> break is for usually for 10 days, and dusherra lies in the middle.</p>
<p>Christmas holidays are not usually given, however, some schools combine the Christmas holidays with the winter break. Schools in <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a> give a holiday on <a href="/wiki/Charan_Singh" title="Charan Singh">Charan Singh</a>'s birth anniversary, and that's when the winter break starts for some. For some other schools the winter break starts on 28 December, and usually end on the second weekend of the new year.</p>
<p>Schools get an off for two weeks after their academic year ends(contrary to the summer vacations between two academic years in the south.) The new academic year starts on first April.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Most_of_India">Most of India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Most of India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>There are many public holidays in India, depending on the region. Muslim holidays like <a href="/wiki/Eid_al-Fitr" title="Eid al-Fitr">Eid</a> are enjoyed throughout the country by students of all religions.</p>
<p>Certain Holidays and mandated across the Nation.</p>
<p>January 26 : Republic Day</p>
<p>August 15 : Independence Day</p>
<p>October 2 : Gandhi Jayanti (Birthday of Mahatma Gandhi )</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Indonesia">Indonesia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Indonesia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Indonesia, there are three main school holidays:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>New school year holiday</b> - known as <i>libur kenaikan kelas</i> (up-grading holiday), usually takes place for 2–3 weeks between late June and early July. Dates are vary, depending on each province/region. The holiday starts from Monday in the first week to Saturday in the last week.</li>
<li><b>Mid-term holiday</b>, aims to separate the two semesters. It takes place for two weeks between late December and early January, coinciding with Christmas and New Year holidays. The starting and ending day are just like the new school year holiday.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Eid_al-Fitr" title="Eid al-Fitr">Eid al-Fitr</a> holiday</b> (or <a href="/wiki/Lebaran" title="Lebaran">lebaran</a> holiday), lasts for around two weeks. Date varies according to the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_calendar" title="Islamic calendar">Islamic calendar</a>.</li>
<li><b>Fasting Holiday / Ramadhan Holiday</b> (or puasa holiday), about 1 month. Date varies according to Islamic Calendar. This break mostly combined with the Eid al-Fitr holiday. Sometimes it can be 2 month holiday. School usually start at the first Monday in the new month. Its even can be up to 3-4 month holiday if new school year holiday (<i>libur kenaikan kelas</i>) happen before or at the month of Ramadhan, But, if Christmas happen after Eid al-Fitr, the school will start in the new year (some province, Maluku, etc.). The school will start at Monday. On the first meeting, student and teacher will say sorry to each other and shake hand each other, known as "<i>Halal Bihalal</i>" it tooks about 3 day, and after <i>Halal Bihalal</i>, parents will receive "<i>rapot</i>" or a reports book show student achievement for 1-year study.</li>
</ul>
<p>Beside the main holidays, there are many small holidays which takes place for 1–2 days, such as the <a href="/wiki/Proclamation_of_Indonesian_Independence" title="Proclamation of Indonesian Independence">Independence Day</a>, religious breaks, and Teacher's Day.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Japan, school holidays take place from late March to early April, from the late July until early September, and over the Christmas and New Year period from December 24 to January 7. Additionally there is a full holiday week in May called "Golden Week."</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Korea">Korea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Korea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Summer vacations and winter vacations are usually similar in length, lasting about 1 month each. There is also spring vacation which lasts about 2 weeks. In addition, there are a couple of 3-5 day long holidays per year, including "Chuseok" which is in fall and "Seolnal" which is in late winter.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Malaysia">Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>There are two main school holidays. The mid-year holidays last for two weeks, from late May until mid-June, in between the two school terms. The year-end holidays last for six weeks, from mid-November until early January, in between two school years. Each school term has a mid-term break; one week in March for the first semester and one week in September for the second semester.</p>
<p>Schools are closed on national and state <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Malaysia" title="Public holidays in Malaysia">public holidays</a>. Schools are allowed to have a few special holidays without replacement for events such as school anniversary and <a href="/wiki/Sports_day" title="Sports day">sports day</a>. For festivities such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hari_Raya_Puasa" title="Hari Raya Puasa">Hari Raya Puasa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_New_Year" title="Chinese New Year">Chinese New Year</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deepavali" title="Deepavali">Deepavali</a>, schools usually apply for additional holidays to allow longer breaks for students to visit relatives in their hometowns. However, every day missed exceeding the special holiday allowance would be replaced by having classes on weekends.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Philippines">Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, summer break starts from the late March to early June, semestral break starts from late October to early November, and Christmas break starts from mid December to early January. Event-based holidays are declared by the President (for national holidays) and the provincial governor or city mayor for local holidays through the Department of Education (DepEd) for Basic Education and Commission on Higher Education (CHED) for Colleges.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Singapore">Singapore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Singapore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Singapore, there are four school terms. Terms 1 and 2 are referred to as <i>Semester 1</i>, as terms 3 and 4 are referred to as <i>Semester 2</i>. Each term consists of ten school weeks. Term 1 starts the day immediately after New Year's Day. If the first school day is a Thursday or a Friday, it is not counted as a school week. After term 1, there is a break of a week, called the March Holidays. Thereafter, term 2 commences and is followed by a break of four weeks, the June Holidays. It is followed by term 3, after which there will be another break of one week, the September Holidays. Then, term 4 would start, and after it there is the December holidays, which would be either five or six weeks long, depending on whether the first week of the year was counted as a school week. Students are also given days off on public holidays, as well as <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Day" title="Children's Day">Children's Day</a>, Youth's Day, Teacher's Day and the day after <a href="/wiki/National_Day_(Singapore)" title="National Day (Singapore)">National Day</a>.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Turkey">Turkey</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Turkey">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Winter holidays last from late January to early February (2 weeks). Summer holidays last from early June to mid September (14 weeks).</p>
<ul>
<li><b>January 1</b> - New Year</li>
<li><b>April 23</b> - National Sovereignty and Children's Day</li>
<li><b>May 1</b> - Labour and Solidarity Day</li>
<li><b>May 19</b> - Commemoration of Atatürk, Youth and Sports Day</li>
<li><b>August 30</b> - Victory Day</li>
<li><b>October 29</b> - Republic Day</li>
</ul>
<p>There are <a href="/wiki/Eid_al-Fitr" title="Eid al-Fitr">Ramadan Feast</a> (3 days) and <b><a href="/wiki/Eid_al-Adha" title="Eid al-Adha">Sacrifice Feast</a></b> (4 days) holidays, but dates of these holidays changing every year because Ramadan &amp; Sacrifice Feasts are calculated according to Muslim calendar. If these feasts are at the middle of the week, then the break becomes a full week break with the Arefe (the day before the feast) day.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Austria">Austria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Austria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Austria, the summer holidays are usually between early July and early September. There is, with the exception of <a href="/wiki/Vorarlberg" title="Vorarlberg">Vorarlberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a>, no Autumn break but there a Christmas break (from December 24 until January 6) and an Easter break (lasts for 10 days). The mid-term break in February lasts for a week and the Whitsun break lasts for 4 days including the weekend. There are also free days during religious holidays (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feast_of_the_Assumption" title="Feast of the Assumption">Assumption</a>, <a href="/wiki/Feast_of_the_Ascension" title="Feast of the Ascension">Ascension</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_(feast)" title="Corpus Christi (feast)">Corpus Christi</a> etc.).</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Belgium">Belgium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Belgium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>For primary schools, the summer holiday in Belgium starts on July 1 (one or two days earlier if the 1st is a weekend day) and ends on September 1 (2 or 3rd if the 1st is a weekend day). Secondary schools and universities often close about a week earlier, as soon as school results have been processed and published. A week of autumn break is usually scheduled during the week of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=All-saints&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="All-saints (page does not exist)">all-saints</a>, November 1. The winter- or Christmas holiday lasts two weeks and encompasses both Christmas and New Year's Day. The dates for both the one-week spring break and the two week Easter holiday vary. In catholic regions where carnival is celebrated, spring break usually takes place during the carnival week. To get al balance in school days between spring break and summer holiday, the two weeks of Easter holiday can take place with Easter both at the beginning, the middle or the end of the holiday period. In some cases, when Easter is at the complete end of the holiday, Easter Monday serves as an additional day off.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Croatia">Croatia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Croatia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Croatia, there are three breaks throughout the school year at public schools.</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Summer break</b> runs from mid-June to early September.</li>
<li><b>Winter (Christmas) break</b> starts several days before December 25 and ends after January 6.</li>
<li><b>Spring (Easter) break</b> lasts about 10 days.</li>
</ul>
<p>Other free days include <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Croatia" title="Public holidays in Croatia">public holidays</a> and local patron saint days.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Czech_Republic">Czech Republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Czech Republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, the summer holidays begin at the end of the school year on June 30 and end at the start of the school year in the first week of September. Then there are autumn holidays: two days plus October 28 (the date of the proclamation of <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>). The winter (Christmas) holidays last usually from December 23 to January 2. There is also a one-day half term holiday on January 31. The spring holidays are a week long and may occur in February or March, depending on the region, sometimes they continue with Easter holidays (Maundy Thursday, Good Friday and Easter Monday). There are also free days such as May 1, May 8 and September 28. The school director is authorised to add up to three more free days during the school year. The school holidays are determined by the Ministry of Schools for each school year and are valid for every basic and high school.</p>

<p>In <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> the summer holiday lasts 6 weeks. Most schools also have one week of winter holiday and one week of autumn holiday, usually in February and October respectively. Finally, the schools are closed from just before Christmas until just after New Year.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Estonia">Estonia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Estonia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, summer holidays last for three months from June to August. Christmas holidays last for two weeks, autumn and spring holidays are each one week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2016 Estonian Ministry of Education decided to add a new holiday, starting from 2017, to balance the learning weeks. There are going to be 5 holidays: Autumn holiday (21.- 29. October), Winter Holiday (23. December- 7. January), a new holiday (24. February- 4. March), Spring holiday (21. April- 1. May) and Summer holiday (12. June- 31. August). Summer breaks starts 1 week later.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=38" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>French school holidays are scheduled each year by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minister_of_National_Education_(France)" title="Minister of National Education (France)">Ministry of Education</a>. This can lead to overcrowding in tourist resorts such as the Mediterranean coast and the ski resorts.</p>
<p>To alleviate this problem, the holiday schedules are staggered by dividing the country into three zones. Despite these measures, the synchronized school holiday schedules still cause some crowding effects, as families head to popular holiday locations all at the same time. This can result in price increases and availability problems in some locations during the school holiday periods. Holidays are divided into three separate zones except for the first two holidays: All Saints and the Christmas / New Year.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=39" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Germany, summer holidays are mostly between July and late August, with regional differences. Some regions begin as soon as early to mid June, some end as late as the middle of September. Depending on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bundesland_(Germany)" title="Bundesland (Germany)">state</a>, further holidays can be around Pentecost or in winter between the first and the second half of the school year.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Ireland">Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=41" title="Edit section: Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

Summer holidays start around the last week of May or the first week of June for secondary schools, or the end of June for primary education, and end around the last week in August or the first week in September. The Junior Certificate and Leaving Certificate exams start on the first Wednesday in June, and last for two and a half weeks, intruding on students' summer holidays.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=42" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Italy, most school holidays are determined by the Ministry of Education, University and Research, and are valid for all public schools of every order and grade. <p>In Slovakia, summer holidays begin at the end of school year on June 30 and end at the start of the school year in the first week of September. The autumn holidays are at the end of October. <p>In Slovenia, Summer holidays begin on June 24 (June 25 is National day, which is also a public holiday) and end on September 1. Other holidays are usually one week long, they expand if national holidays fall on the work days. The Autumn holidays ("Krompirjeve počitnice" - "Potato holidays") begin around October 31, which is Reformation day and November 1 which is called "All Souls Day". In the middle of February there are Winter holidays, which are divided into two parts. In each part there are other parts of Slovenian students who have holidays. The second part of the Winter holiday usually ends at the end of February. There are also free days such as February 8 (Slovenian cultural festival - Prešeren Day) and Easter Monday, other national holidays like August 15 (Feast of the Assumption) fall on school holidays and are more important to adults. Students from last year of Primary and Secondary schools usually start Summer holidays one week earlier due to the final exams. This holidays are valid for Primary and Secondary schools only and are determined by the Ministry of Schools. <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sweden">Sweden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=53" title="Edit section: Sweden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Sweden, the school year starts around mid or late August (September 1 at the universities). The week before All Saint's Day (taken as a Saturday around November 2) primary and secondary school students have an autumn holiday. Christmas holiday starts around December 15–16, depending on the day of the week on which Christmas Eve occurs. It usually ends in the second week of January (January 6 is also a public holiday). In the 8th–10th weeks after New Year, primary and secondary school students have one week of winter vacation; the exact week varies by location. Easter public holidays last four days from Friday to Monday. Easter school holidays are either the week preceding Easter or the week immediately following, depending on location. Primary and secondary school summer holidays last about ten weeks, starting in the second week of June.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=54" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>School holidays in the United Kingdom follow a standard pattern, with a school year of 190 days of teaching, beginning with the Autumn Term,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> but the exact timing varies between countries and counties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Local authorities set term dates which apply to all Community, Voluntary Controlled, Community Special Schools and Maintained Nursery Schools. Academies, Free schools, Voluntary Aided schools and Foundation schools are able to choose their own dates but many follow the same dates as the Local Authority.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Although these schools can set their own term dates, they are still required to open for the same length of time. Independent private schools tend to set longer holidays than state schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="England_and_Wales">England and Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=55" title="Edit section: England and Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Summer holidays in State schools are usually six or seven weeks long, starting in late July and ending in early September. Schools have Christmas and Easter holidays, each usually lasting about two weeks. The school year is split up into three sections: Autumn term (between Summer and Christmas); Spring term (between Christmas and Easter); and Summer term (between Easter and the Summer holiday). Roughly half-way through each term, pupils will get one week off school, known as half-term.</p>
<p>Independent schools often have longer holidays including up to 10 weeks for Summer, but often have longer school days and sometimes lessons on Saturday mornings.</p>
<p>See <a href="/wiki/English_school_holidays" title="English school holidays">School Holidays in England</a> for the impact of school holidays on tourism and life for parents and others.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Scotland">Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=56" title="Edit section: Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>In Scotland the academic year usually begins in the third week of August. In October, there are one or two weeks off, which is the half-way point between Summer and Christmas holidays. The Christmas holiday usually begins a few days before December 25 and ends a few days after January 5. There is up to a week off half-way through the Spring term and a two-week break for Easter. After Easter is <a href="/wiki/Summer_term" title="Summer term">Summer term</a> and the 3rd half term break is the half-way point to the summer holidays. The summer holiday usually begins at the end of June for most pupils in primary 1–7 and secondary years 1–3. Years 4, 5, and 6 have a period of exam leave at the end of May or early June, but may return for the last few weeks before the summer holiday starts.</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Ireland">Northern Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=57" title="Edit section: Northern Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Schools in Northern Ireland have similar school holidays to those in the Republic of Ireland. Schools in Northern Ireland have shorter mid-term breaks than those in the rest of the UK and, to substitute, a longer summer holiday, with the entirety of July and August off.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia_and_New_Zealand">Australia and New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=58" title="Edit section: Australia and New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, academic years for primary and secondary institutions are divided into two semesters, with each semester being further divided into two terms (thus totalling four terms per year). Although historically the year was divided into three terms with an extended Easter break interrupting the first term, the year has been divided into four terms since the late 1980s (with the exception of the Australian state of <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a> which did not change until 2013).</p>
<p>Following southern hemisphere seasons, the main summer holiday between academic years encompasses most of December, all of January and sometimes a few days at the beginning of February, and always encompasses <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">Christmas</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Year" title="New Year">New Year</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Australia_Day" title="Australia Day">Australia Day</a> on 26 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In year 12, however, the term ends in November; for those who go on to university, the term starts in March. New Zealand celebrates <a href="/wiki/Waitangi_Day" title="Waitangi Day">Waitangi Day</a> on 6 February; the summer holidays in New Zealand may or may not extend as far as that day, depending on the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> There is typically a break of two weeks mid-semester (i.e. after Term 1 and after Term 3) and a break of three weeks in the middle of the year, although this can vary between jurisdictions. In the year 2000, due to the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Summer Olympics</a> in Sydney, the state of New South Wales extended the break after Term 3 to three weeks, compensating by reducing the break in the middle of the year to two weeks.</p>
<p>Historically, the Term 1 holidays have been scheduled around <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>, reflecting the three-term system's notion of an extended Easter break within Term 1; although since the mid-1990s this has gradually changed, and now only <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a> tie the school holidays closely to Easter; the remainder of Australia and all of New Zealand now have a fixed length to Term 1 which leads to the Easter period falling within Term 1 in some years with an early Easter, such as 2016.</p>
<p>Typically, the Term 1 holidays will run for two weeks within April; the mid-year holidays encompass the last week of June and the first two weeks of July; and the Term 3 holidays encompass the last week of September and the first week of October. This varies between jurisdictions, and of course exact dates depend on what day of the week these respective months begin, as (with the exception of the beginning and end of the academic year), terms tend to begin and end with full weeks.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Common_practices_and_effects">Common practices and effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=School_holiday&amp;action=edit&amp;section=59" title="Edit section: Common practices and effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Summer holidays for most children are a time to relax, enjoy themselves, and catch up on studies, or to spend time with family or relatives. Many families travel together on school holidays.</p>
<p>School holidays have many effects on a country, ranging from the price of travel and holiday accommodation, which increase as demand increases while children are off school, to a change in crime rate: Example <a class="external text" href="http://www.ukcrimestats.com/National_Picture/" rel="nofollow">UK crime rate monthly figures</a>. Traffic congestion and heavy passenger loads on <a href="/wiki/Public_transport" title="Public transport">public transport</a> on <a href="/wiki/School_run" title="School run">school run</a> routes may be worse on school days during term time. The number of families taking holidays away from urban areas can reduce traffic and use of public transport in cities while loading long-distance highways and means of transport.</p>
<p>In some countries, including Germany and France, where many families travel to their holiday destinations, the start and end dates of school holidays in different regions are <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/staggered" title="wikt:staggered">staggered</a> to reduce the heavy traffic and pressure on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Means_of_transport" title="Means of transport">means of transport</a> that would otherwise occur on these dates.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
