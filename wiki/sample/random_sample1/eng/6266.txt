<p>A <b>volleyball</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Ball" title="Ball">ball</a> used to play <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indoor_volleyball" title="Indoor volleyball">indoor volleyball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beach_volleyball" title="Beach volleyball">beach volleyball</a>, or other less common <a href="/wiki/Volleyball_variations" title="Volleyball variations">variations of the sport</a>. Volleyballs are round and traditionally consist of eighteen nearly rectangular panels of synthetic or genuine leather, arranged in six identical sections of three panels each, wrapped around a bladder. However, in 2008, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIVB" title="FIVB">FIVB</a> adopted as its official indoor ball a new <a href="/wiki/Mikasa_Sports" title="Mikasa Sports">Mikasa</a> with dimples and only eight panels for a softer touch and truer flight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Valve" title="Valve">valve</a> permits the internal air <a href="/wiki/Pressure" title="Pressure">pressure</a> to be adjusted.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Volleyball_characteristics">Volleyball characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volleyball_(ball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Volleyball characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Indoor volleyballs</i> are designed for the indoor version of the sport, and <i>beach volleyballs</i> for the beach game.</p>
<p>Indoor volleyballs may be solid white or a combination of two or three different easily distinguishable colors. They are made in two versions: the youth version is slightly smaller and weighs much less than an adult volleyball and than the standard version to accommodate youth's use.</p>
<p>Beach volleyballs are slightly larger than standard indoor balls, have a rougher external texture, and a lower internal pressure. They can be brightly colored or solid white. The very first volleyballs were made from leather paneling over a rubber carcass.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_brands">Major brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volleyball_(ball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Major brands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are several brands of competitive volleyballs in use, including, but not limited to:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tachikara" title="Tachikara">Tachikara</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Molten_Corporation" title="Molten Corporation">Molten</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wilson_Sporting_Goods" title="Wilson Sporting Goods">Wilson</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mikasa_Sports" title="Mikasa Sports">Mikasa</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mizuno_Corp." title="Mizuno Corp.">Mizuno</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nike_Inc" title="Nike Inc">Nike</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spalding_(sports_equipment)" title="Spalding (sports equipment)">Spalding</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Beta_sport&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Beta sport (page does not exist)">Beta</a></li>
</ul>
<p><br/>
Most of these brands also make cheaper variations for recreational (non-competitive) use.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adopted_use">Adopted use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volleyball_(ball)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Adopted use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Mikasa makes the official balls of the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_Volleyball" title="Fédération Internationale de Volleyball">Fédération Internationale de Volleyball</a> and the CEV - <a href="/wiki/European_Volleyball_Confederation" title="European Volleyball Confederation">European Volleyball Confederation</a> (beach and indoor).</li>
<li>Molten makes the official ball of <a href="/wiki/USA_Volleyball" title="USA Volleyball">USA Volleyball</a> (indoor).</li>
<li>Wilson makes the official ball of the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Volleyball_Professionals" title="Association of Volleyball Professionals">Association of Volleyball Professionals</a> (beach).</li>
</ul>
