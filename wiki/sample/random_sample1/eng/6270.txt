<p><b>Bad Meets Evil</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> duo composed of <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rappers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22" title="Royce da 5'9&quot;">Royce da 5'9"</a> (Bad) and <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> (Evil). Bad Meets Evil was formed in 1999, thanks to the duo's mutual friend, <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a>. Their discography consists of one <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) and four <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a>. In 1999, the duo released a double non-album single, "<a href="/wiki/Nuttin%27_to_Do" title="Nuttin' to Do">Nuttin' to Do</a>" and "Scary Movies"; the former peaked at 36 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Songs" title="Hot Rap Songs">Hot Rap Songs</a> chart, while the latter peaked at 63 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, and was featured on the soundtrack of the 2000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horror_comedy" title="Horror comedy">horror comedy</a> <a href="/wiki/Parody_film" title="Parody film">parody film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Scary_Movie" title="Scary Movie">Scary Movie</a></i>.</p>
<p>The duo broke up after a <a href="/wiki/Royce_da_5%279%22#Feud_with_D12" title="Royce da 5'9&quot;">feud</a> between Royce and the members of Eminem's group <a href="/wiki/D12" title="D12">D12</a>. The feud ended when Proof, a D12 member and Eminem's best friend—as well as a friend of Royce's—was killed in April 2006. After Royce's super-group <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(group)" title="Slaughterhouse (group)">Slaughterhouse</a> signed to the Eminem-founded record label <a href="/wiki/Shady_Records" title="Shady Records">Shady Records</a>, a reunion of Bad Meets Evil followed with the extended play <i><a href="/wiki/Hell:_The_Sequel" title="Hell: The Sequel">Hell: The Sequel</a></i> (2011), which reached number one on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and was <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified gold</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) and the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">Australian Recording Industry Association</a> (ARIA). The EP's lead single "<a href="/wiki/Fast_Lane_(Bad_Meets_Evil_song)" title="Fast Lane (Bad Meets Evil song)">Fast Lane</a>" peaked at number 32 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, while the second single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lighters_(Bad_Meets_Evil_song)" title="Lighters (Bad Meets Evil song)">Lighters</a>", featuring <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Mars" title="Bruno Mars">Bruno Mars</a>, peaked at number four on the same chart. The duo returned in 2014 for the 15 year anniversary album of Shady Records, SHADYXV, for "Vegas", and again in 2015 to record two tracks for the boxing film <a href="/wiki/Southpaw_(film)" title="Southpaw (film)">Southpaw</a>, titled "All I Think About" and "Raw".</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foundation_and_break_up">Foundation and break up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bad_Meets_Evil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Foundation and break up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Eminem met Royce da 5'9" in 1997 when Royce was opening for entertainer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Usher_(entertainer)" title="Usher (entertainer)">Usher</a> at the Palladium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Eminem and Royce da 5'9", became quick friends before Eminem's rise to fame, and collaborated on the track which led to the duo's foundation, "Bad Meets Evil", for Eminem's 1999 major label debut <i><a href="/wiki/The_Slim_Shady_LP" title="The Slim Shady LP">The Slim Shady LP</a></i>. The duo's first work, a 1999 double-single, which was originally recorded in 1998, consisting of "<a href="/wiki/Nuttin%27_to_Do" title="Nuttin' to Do">Nuttin' to Do" and "Scary Movies</a>", achieved respectable chart success, peaking at 36 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Rap_Songs" title="Hot Rap Songs">Hot Rap Songs</a> chart, while the latter peaked at 63 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>. A year later, the song "Scary Movies" was featured on the soundtrack of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horror_comedy" title="Horror comedy">horror comedy</a> <a href="/wiki/Parody_film" title="Parody film">parody film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Scary_Movie" title="Scary Movie">Scary Movie</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic-scary_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-scary-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>"<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renegade_(Jay-Z_song)" title="Renegade (Jay-Z song)">Renegade</a>" was originally a song featuring Eminem recorded for Royce's first studio debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_City_(Royce_da_5%279%22_album)" title="Rock City (Royce da 5'9&quot; album)">Rock City</a></i> (2002), but Royce's verses were later replaced with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>'s for his 2001 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blueprint" title="The Blueprint">The Blueprint</a></i>. Jay-Z contacted Eminem for a collaboration and beat while the song was being made. Limited in time for production, Eminem sent Jay-Z the beat for "Renegade" with approval from Royce. However, Eminem was still featured on Royce's <i>Rock City</i> album, on the title track.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a> heard one of Royce's mix tapes through Eminem, deciding to sign him to <a href="/wiki/Aftermath_Entertainment" title="Aftermath Entertainment">Aftermath Entertainment</a>. Eminem secured him a ghostwriting position on Dre's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/2001_(Dr._Dre_album)" title="2001 (Dr. Dre album)">2001</a></i>. After his manager Kino stated: "I've seen Em sit Dre down like a pupil and coach him on rhymes" on a phone interview, Dr. Dre requested that Royce cut ties with his manager. Royce refused to fire his manager, thus his relationship with Dre ended.</p>
<p>After Royce turned down Eminem's offer to join his <a href="/wiki/Anger_Management_Tour" title="Anger Management Tour">Anger Management Tour</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Hype_man" title="Hype man">hype man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a>, member of Eminem's band <a href="/wiki/D12" title="D12">D12</a> and his best friend, also a good friend of Royce's, took the place. Later, Royce wanted to continue working with Eminem, who was busy working with D12, which lead Royce to believe that D12 was "souring" his relationship with Eminem. A feud with a series of <a href="/wiki/Diss_track" title="Diss track">diss tracks</a> followed, resulting in the duo's break up.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reunion">Reunion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bad_Meets_Evil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reunion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late 2003, D12's <a href="/wiki/Proof_(rapper)" title="Proof (rapper)">Proof</a> confronted Royce outside a club in Detroit. Violence erupted between the rappers' entourage, leading to the police being called, and Proof and Royce's arrest. The two were detained overnight in adjacent cells where they talked out their differences and ended the feud. Royce and Eminem reconciled after Proof's death in April 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beef_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-beef-3">[3]</a></sup> Eminem said: "I think after we lost Proof, we realized how stupid this beef shit is."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beef_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-beef-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oral_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-oral-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Royce's rap group <a href="/wiki/Slaughterhouse_(group)" title="Slaughterhouse (group)">Slaughterhouse</a> signed to Eminem's founded label Shady Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slaughter_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-slaughter-5">[5]</a></sup> This led to a reunion of Bad Meets Evil with the debut <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hell:_The_Sequel" title="Hell: The Sequel">Hell: The Sequel</a></i>, released on June 14, 2011 after 11 years of inactivity in the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> A chart success, it peaked at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and was <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified gold</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) and the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">Australian Recording Industry Association</a> (ARIA).</p>
<p>"Fast Lane" was released on May 3, 2011 as the lead single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interscope_Records_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Interscope_Records-7">[7]</a></sup> It was recorded by Mike Strange at Effigy Studios (<a href="/wiki/Ferndale,_Michigan" title="Ferndale, Michigan">Ferndale, Michigan</a>). Recorded a few months before its release, the song was written by Eminem, Royce da 5'9" and Sly "Pyper" Jordan, who also sings the <a href="/wiki/Refrain" title="Refrain">chorus</a> to the song with additional vocals from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denaun_Porter" title="Denaun Porter">Denaun Porter</a>. Eminem requested that Sly perform the chorus, after hearing <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>'s hit single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kush_(Dr._Dre_song)" title="Kush (Dr. Dre song)">Kush</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-struck_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-struck-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Supa_Dups" title="Supa Dups">Supa Dups</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jason_Gilbert" title="Jason Gilbert">Jason "JG" Gilbert</a> produced the song; Eminem and Mike Strange <a href="/wiki/Audio_mixing_(recorded_music)" title="Audio mixing (recorded music)">mixed</a> the song. JG and Supa Dups also sample their own vocals. According to Supa Dups, he was asked to make a beat with JG, without knowing it was for Bad Meets Evil. He said that "[They] didn't even have Eminem in mind [when they made the beat]." According to this interview with <i>Mixtape Daily</i>, Supa Dups had little knowledge about the project, but simply submitted the beat to Eminem- Months after recording the song, on April 28, 2011, when it leaked onto the Internet, Supa Dups was impressed by the finished version, lyrically, and was proud to have participated in the project. The song peaked at number 34 on the Hot 100 chart.</p>
<p>The second single, "Lighters", was originally intended to be featured on Royce's fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Success_Is_Certain" title="Success Is Certain">Success Is Certain</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exclaim_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-exclaim-9">[9]</a></sup> but the single itself had ended up on <i>Hell: The Sequel</i>. It was produced solely by Rochester, New York producer Battle Roy. After Royce had presented the track to Eminem, he was inspired to write and record the first verse, prompting Royce to write his the day afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exclaim_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-exclaim-9">[9]</a></sup> Bad Meets Evil then flew to Los Angeles, where <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Mars" title="Bruno Mars">Bruno Mars</a> heard the song. Eminem and Mars then made minor changes to the musical arrangement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exclaim_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-exclaim-9">[9]</a></sup> The song was recorded at Effigy Studios by Strange, Isolation Studios by Asar and Levcon Studios (<a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>) by Ari Levine of <a href="/wiki/The_Smeezingtons" title="The Smeezingtons">The Smeezingtons</a>, a music production and songwriting group consisting of Philip Lawrence and Mars. Eminem, The Smeezingtons and Battle Roy produced the song. Battle Roy and Joe Strange also engineered the song. <a href="/wiki/Luis_Resto_(musician)" title="Luis Resto (musician)">Luis Resto</a> provided additional keyboards for the song. On May 25, 2011, when the track listing of <i>Hell: The Sequel</i> was announced, "Lighters" was revealed to the public to feature Mars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> "Lighters" hit <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_hit_radio" title="Contemporary hit radio">contemporary hit radio</a> on July 5, 2011 as the second single from the EP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FM_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FM-11">[11]</a></sup> "Lighters" performed better on the charts than "Fast Lane", peaking at number four on the Hot 100 chart.</p>
<p>Bad Meets Evil released a new song, entitled "Vegas", for the compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/Shady_XV" title="Shady XV">Shady XV</a></i>, which was released on November 24, 2014 through Shady Records.</p>
<p>In 2015, two new Bad Meets Evil songs were released. The two songs, "All I Think About" and "Raw" appear on the <i><a href="/wiki/Southpaw_(soundtrack)" title="Southpaw (soundtrack)">Southpaw Soundtrack</a></i>, produced by Shady Records.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Hell:_The_Sequel" title="Hell: The Sequel">Hell: The Sequel</a></i> was certified Gold by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> For shipments of 500,000 copies in the US.</p>
