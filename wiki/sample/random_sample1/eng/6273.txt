<p><b>Barret Spencer Oliver</b> (born August 24, 1973) is an American photographer and former child actor. He is known for his role as Bastian Balthazar Bux in the <a href="/wiki/The_NeverEnding_Story_(film)" title="The NeverEnding Story (film)">film adaptation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Ende" title="Michael Ende">Michael Ende</a>'s novel <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_NeverEnding_Story" title="The NeverEnding Story">The NeverEnding Story</a></i>, followed by roles in <i><a href="/wiki/D.A.R.Y.L." title="D.A.R.Y.L.">D.A.R.Y.L.</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cocoon_(film)" title="Cocoon (film)">Cocoon</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cocoon:_The_Return" title="Cocoon: The Return">Cocoon: The Return</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barret_Oliver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oliver had minor roles in television and film, until starring as Bastian in the 1984 movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_NeverEnding_Story_(film)" title="The NeverEnding Story (film)">The NeverEnding Story</a></i>. Subsequently, he was cast as the lead in <a href="/wiki/Tim_Burton" title="Tim Burton">Tim Burton</a>'s short film <i><a href="/wiki/Frankenweenie_(1984_film)" title="Frankenweenie (1984 film)">Frankenweenie</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and as the <a href="/wiki/Android_(robot)" title="Android (robot)">android</a> 'Daryl' in the 1985 film <i><a href="/wiki/D.A.R.Y.L." title="D.A.R.Y.L.">D.A.R.Y.L.</a></i>, a part for which he won a <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award" title="Saturn Award">Saturn Award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>His last role in a feature film was Willie Saravian in <a href="/wiki/Paul_Bartel" title="Paul Bartel">Paul Bartel</a>'s 1989 ensemble comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Scenes_from_the_Class_Struggle_in_Beverly_Hills" title="Scenes from the Class Struggle in Beverly Hills">Scenes from the Class Struggle in Beverly Hills</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Later Oliver became a printer and photographer, specializing in nineteenth century processes such as <a href="/wiki/Collodion_process" title="Collodion process">collodion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woodburytype" title="Woodburytype">Woodburytype</a>. His work has been displayed in museum and gallery exhibitions and used in films. In 2007, his book <i>A History of the Woodburytype</i> was published by Carl Mautz Publishing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
