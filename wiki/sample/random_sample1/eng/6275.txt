<p>The <b>Museum of Maritime Science</b> (船の科学館, <i>Fune-no-kagakukan</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Marine_science" title="Marine science">marine science</a> museum located in Higashiyashio, <a href="/wiki/Shinagawa" title="Shinagawa">Shinagawa, Tokyo</a> on Odaiba, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. Exhibits include Japanese boats, items related to the navy, shipping industry, fishing, sailing, maritime recreation, ship design and building, and the environment of the seas and oceans around Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The museum building itself is modelled after the British ocean liner <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_2" title="Queen Elizabeth 2">Queen Elizabeth 2</a></i>.</p>
<p>Outside the museum building are a number of exhibits including a cannon from the Imperial Japanese Navy battleship <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Mutsu" title="Japanese battleship Mutsu">Mutsu</a>, a large screw propeller, Ayumi I-Go Ocean Floor House, Tankai Submarine and PC-18 submersible, a wooden fishing boat from <a href="/wiki/Kuj%C5%ABkuri" title="Kujūkuri">Kujūkuri</a>, Osesaki lighthouse and <a href="/wiki/Anorisaki_Lighthouse" title="Anorisaki Lighthouse">Anorisaki Lighthouse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since May 1979 the icebreaker <a href="/wiki/S%C5%8Dya_(icebreaker)" title="Sōya (icebreaker)">Sōya</a> has been moored alongside the museum open to the public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
