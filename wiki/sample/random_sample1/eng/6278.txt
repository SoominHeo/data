<p><i><b>Battery</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">バッテリー</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Batterī</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Light_novel" title="Light novel">light novel</a> series by <a href="/wiki/Atsuko_Asano_(writer)" title="Atsuko Asano (writer)">Atsuko Asano</a> that was published by <a href="/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten" title="Kadokawa Shoten">Kadokawa Shoten</a>. The series is about Harada Takumi and Gō Nagakura, two boys who start a baseball team. For the work the author received the Noma Prize for Juvenile Literature in 1997 and the <a href="/wiki/Shogakukan" title="Shogakukan">Shogakukan</a> Children's Publication Culture Award in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JBBY_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-JBBY-2">[2]</a></sup> It has sold over 10 million copies in Japan,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and has been adapted into a <a href="/wiki/The_Battery_(2007_film)" title="The Battery (2007 film)">film</a> in 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and an anime television series in 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battery_(novel_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battery_(novel_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An anime television series adaptation began airing on July 14, 2016 on the <a href="/wiki/Noitamina" title="Noitamina">noitaminA</a> block of Fuji TV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AirDate_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-AirDate-6">[6]</a></sup> The series is directed and written by Tomomi Mochizuki, with animation by the studio Zero-G. Hideoki Kusama is serving as character designer and chief animation director and Akira Senju is in charge of the music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Characters_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-Characters-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
