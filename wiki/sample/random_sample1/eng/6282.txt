<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">List of <i>Battlestar Galactica</i> (2004 TV series) episodes</h1>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">For the original 1978–80 television series and Galactica 1980, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_Battlestar_Galactica_(1978_TV_series)_and_Galactica_1980_episodes" title="List of Battlestar Galactica (1978 TV series) and Galactica 1980 episodes">List of Battlestar Galactica (1978 TV series) and Galactica 1980 episodes</a>.</div>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(2004_TV_series)" title="Battlestar Galactica (2004 TV series)">Battlestar Galactica</a></i> is an American <a href="/wiki/Military_science_fiction" title="Military science fiction">military science fiction</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a>, and part of the <a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica" title="Battlestar Galactica"><i>Battlestar Galactica</i> franchise</a>. The show was developed by <a href="/wiki/Ronald_D._Moore" title="Ronald D. Moore">Ronald D. Moore</a> as a re-imagining of the 1978 <a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(1978_TV_series)" title="Battlestar Galactica (1978 TV series)"><i>Battlestar Galactica</i></a> television series created by <a href="/wiki/Glen_A._Larson" title="Glen A. Larson">Glen A. Larson</a>. The series first aired as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(TV_miniseries)" title="Battlestar Galactica (TV miniseries)">three-hour miniseries</a> (comprising four broadcast hours) in December 2003 on the <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Sci-Fi Channel</a>. The television series debuted in the United Kingdom on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky1" title="Sky1">Sky1</a> on October 18, 2004, and premiered in the United States on the Sci-Fi Channel on January 14, 2005.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Story_arc" title="Story arc">story arc</a> of <i>Battlestar Galactica</i> is set in a distant star system, where a civilization of humans live on a series of planets known as the <a href="/wiki/Twelve_Colonies" title="Twelve Colonies">Twelve Colonies</a>. In the past, the Colonies had been at war with a cybernetic race of their own creation, known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cylon_(re-imagining)" title="Cylon (re-imagining)">Cylons</a>. With the unwitting help of a human named <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Baltar" title="Gaius Baltar">Gaius Baltar</a>, the Cylons, now in human form, launch a sudden <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sneak_attack" title="Sneak attack">sneak attack</a> on the Colonies, laying waste to the planets and devastating their populations. Out of a population numbering in the billions, only approximately 50,000 humans survive, most of whom were aboard civilian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spaceships" title="Spaceships">spaceships</a> that avoided destruction. Of all the Colonial Fleet, the eponymous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(ship)" title="Battlestar Galactica (ship)">Battlestar <i>Galactica</i></a> appears to be the only military capital ship that survived the attack. Under the leadership of Colonial Fleet officer <a href="/wiki/William_Adama" title="William Adama">Commander William "Bill" Adama</a> and President <a href="/wiki/Laura_Roslin" title="Laura Roslin">Laura Roslin</a>, the <i>Galactica</i> and its crew take up the task of leading the small fugitive fleet of survivors into space in search of a fabled refuge known as <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_availability">Online availability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Battlestar_Galactica_(2004_TV_series)_episodes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Online availability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2006, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a> began offering the miniseries, season 1 and season 2 episodes for purchase on the U.S. version of its <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In December 2007, the <i>Battlestar Galactica</i> episodes were removed from iTunes along with other NBC Universal content.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The episodes returned to iTunes when NBC Universal announced their return to iTunes in September 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In February 2009, the series became available in <a href="/wiki/High-definition_video" title="High-definition video">high-definition</a> format at the UK iTunes Store.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>The series has also been available via <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Video" title="Amazon Video">Amazon Video</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">Hulu</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Network" title="PlayStation Network">PlayStation Network</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xbox_Video" title="Xbox Video">Xbox Video</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
