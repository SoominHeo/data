<p><b>Baek Sung-dong</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">백성동</span>; born 13 August 1991) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Striker_(football)#Striker" title="Striker (football)">striker</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> side <a href="/wiki/V-Varen_Nagasaki" title="V-Varen Nagasaki">V-Varen Nagasaki</a> on loan from <a href="/wiki/Sagan_Tosu" title="Sagan Tosu">Sagan Tosu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jleague_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jleague-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baek_Sung-dong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007, Baek participated in youth program of the English professional football club <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> on the recommendation of the Korea Football Association. He was capped four times for the national team representing them in the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIFA_U-20_World_Cup" title="2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup">2011 FIFA U-20 World Cup</a>. Following his good performances in the competitions, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_under-23_football_team" title="South Korea national under-23 football team">South Korean under-23 team</a> coach <a href="/wiki/Hong_Myung-bo" title="Hong Myung-bo">Hong Myung-bo</a> included him in the national squad.</p>
<p>On 29 December 2011, Baek signed his first professional contract with <a href="/wiki/J1_League" title="J1 League">J1 League</a> club <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BAbilo_Iwata" title="Júbilo Iwata">Júbilo Iwata</a>, agreeing a three-year deal with the club.</p>
