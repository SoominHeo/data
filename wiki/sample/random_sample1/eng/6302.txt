<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Alpha Cygni variable</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>Alpha Cygni variables</b> are <a href="/wiki/Variable_star" title="Variable star">variable stars</a> which exhibit non-radial pulsations, meaning that some portions of the stellar surface are contracting at the same time others parts expand. They are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supergiant" title="Supergiant">supergiant</a> stars of <a href="/wiki/Stellar_classification" title="Stellar classification">spectral types</a> B or A. Variations in brightness on the order of 0.1 <a href="/wiki/Apparent_magnitude" title="Apparent magnitude">magnitudes</a> are associated with the pulsations, which often seem <a href="/wiki/Irregular_variable" title="Irregular variable">irregular</a>, due to <a href="/wiki/Beat_(acoustics)" title="Beat (acoustics)">beating</a> of multiple pulsation periods. The pulsations typically have periods of several days to several weeks.</p>
<p>The prototype of these stars, <a href="/wiki/Deneb" title="Deneb">Deneb</a> (α Cygni), exhibits fluctuations in brightness between magnitudes +1.21 and +1.29. Small amplitude rapid variations have been known in many early supergiant stars, but they were not formally grouped into a class until the 4th edition of the General Catalogue of Variable Stars was published in 1985. It used the acronym ACYG for Alpha Cygni variable stars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Many <a href="/wiki/Luminous_blue_variable" title="Luminous blue variable">luminous blue variables</a> (LBVs) show Alpha Cygni-type variability during their quiescent (hot) phases, but the LBV classification is generally used in these cases.</p>
<p>A large number (32) were discovered by Christoffel Waelkens and colleagues analysing Hipparcos data in a 1998 study.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-waelkens_1998_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-waelkens_1998-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pulsations">Pulsations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alpha_Cygni_variable&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pulsations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The pulsations of Alpha Cygni Variable stars are not fully understood. They are not confined to a narrow range of temperatures and luminosities in the way that most pulsating stars are. Instead, most luminous A and B supergiants, and possibly also O and F stars, show some type of unpredictable small-scale pulsations. <a href="/wiki/Adiabatic_process" title="Adiabatic process">Nonadiabatic</a> strange mode <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radial_pulsations" title="Radial pulsations">radial pulsations</a> are predicted but only for the most luminous supergiants. Pulsations have also been modelled for less luminous supergiants by assuming they are low mass post-red supergiant stars, but most Alpha Cygni variables do not appear to have passed through the red supergiant stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saio_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-saio-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SaioGeorgy2013_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-SaioGeorgy2013-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The pulsations are likely induced by <a href="/wiki/Kappa_mechanism" title="Kappa mechanism">kappa mechanism</a>, caused by iron opacity variations, with strange modes producing the observed short periods for both radial and non-radial pulsations. Non-adiabatic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G-mode_pulsation" title="G-mode pulsation">g-modes</a> may produce longer period variations, but these have not been observed in Alpha Cygni variables.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saio2011_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saio2011-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
