<p>The <b>Vancouver Canadians</b> are a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minor_league_baseball" title="Minor league baseball">minor league</a> <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>. The Canadians are the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_League" title="Northwest League">Northwest League</a> affiliate of the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a>. The C's have previously affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a> between 2000–2010. They are the only <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> team in the "affiliated" minor leagues—i.e., part of the farm system of a <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> team. This came about after the <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Lynx" title="Ottawa Lynx">Ottawa Lynx</a> moved to <a href="/wiki/Allentown,_Pennsylvania" title="Allentown, Pennsylvania">Allentown, Pennsylvania</a>. The NWL Canadians supplanted a Triple-A franchise of the same name that played for 22 seasons (1978–99) in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a>. The current incarnation of the Canadians was established in <a href="/wiki/2000_in_baseball" title="2000 in baseball">2000</a> after the AAA Canadians moved to <a href="/wiki/West_Sacramento,_California" title="West Sacramento, California">West Sacramento, California</a> and became the <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_River_Cats" title="Sacramento River Cats">Sacramento River Cats</a> (which is now an affiliate of the San Francisco Giants). </p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vancouver_Canadians&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Northwest_League" title="Northwest League">Northwest League</a> franchise (dating back to 1979 in <a href="/wiki/Medford,_Oregon" title="Medford, Oregon">Medford, Oregon</a>), the Canadians have had players such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Canseco" title="José Canseco">José Canseco</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pat_Britt&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pat Britt (page does not exist)">Pat Britt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rod_Beck" title="Rod Beck">Rod Beck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rickey_Henderson" title="Rickey Henderson">Rickey Henderson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Brosius" title="Scott Brosius">Scott Brosius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Giambi" title="Jason Giambi">Jason Giambi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Hudson" title="Tim Hudson">Tim Hudson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Swisher" title="Nick Swisher">Nick Swisher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Brown" title="Jeremy Brown">Jeremy Brown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Troy_Glaus" title="Troy Glaus">Troy Glaus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Sosa" title="Sammy Sosa">Sammy Sosa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Windsor" title="Jason Windsor">Jason Windsor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Blanton" title="Joe Blanton">Joe Blanton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rich_Harden" title="Rich Harden">Rich Harden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travis_Buck" title="Travis Buck">Travis Buck</a>, local Sean Triplett, Danny Putnam, <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Braden" title="Dallas Braden">Dallas Braden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Tejada" title="Miguel Tejada">Miguel Tejada</a>. The team formed after <a href="/wiki/Medford,_Oregon" title="Medford, Oregon">Medford, Oregon</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Southern_Oregon_Timberjacks" title="Southern Oregon Timberjacks">Southern Oregon Timberjacks</a> relocated to Vancouver.</p>
<p>Vancouver was a heavy player in the early history of the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_League" title="Northwest League">Northwest League</a>, a charter member in every version of the league that would eventually form the NWL, notably as the sole team that survived the collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Western_International_League" title="Western International League">Western International League</a> in 1922 when it reformed in 1937, winning four pennants in the WIL (1942, 1947, 1949, 1954) as the Vancouver Capilanos (1939–1954). However, even though they were the final champions of the WIL, Vancouver was not part of its reformation into the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_League" title="Northwest League">Northwest League</a>, due to the NWL's shedding of all of its Canadian teams in order to focus on the American <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a>. Vancouver was without professional baseball in 1955, but in 1956 the highest calibre of minor league play, in the form of the Open-Classification Pacific Coast League, came to British Columbia when <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Oaks_(PCL)" title="Oakland Oaks (PCL)">Oakland Oaks</a> transferred there as the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Mounties" title="Vancouver Mounties">Vancouver Mounties</a>. The Mounties played in the PCL from 1956 through 1962, and from 1965 through 1969.</p>
<p>A Triple-A franchise named the Vancouver Canadians played for 22 seasons (1978–99) in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a> at <a href="/wiki/Scotiabank_Field_at_Nat_Bailey_Stadium" title="Scotiabank Field at Nat Bailey Stadium">Nat Bailey Stadium</a>. Following the 1999 season, in which they won the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_World_Series" title="Triple-A World Series">Triple-A World Series</a>, the team was purchased by a group led by Art Savage and moved to <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a> for the 2000 season and became the <a href="/wiki/Sacramento_River_Cats" title="Sacramento River Cats">Sacramento River Cats</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_team_and_management">Current team and management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vancouver_Canadians&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current team and management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Canadians announced January 10, 2007 that Rick Magnante would return as <a href="/wiki/Manager_(baseball)" title="Manager (baseball)">Manager</a> for 2007, as well as pitching coach <a href="/wiki/Craig_Lefferts" title="Craig Lefferts">Craig Lefferts</a>, who entered his 4th season as the pitching coach. Former Vancouver Canadian slugger <a href="/wiki/Chris_Pritchett" title="Chris Pritchett">Chris Pritchett</a> was named the team's latest hitting coach in May 2007.</p>
<p>In 2007, local Vancouver businessmen, Jake Kerr and Jeff Mooney purchased the Vancouver Canadians and secured a 25-year lease with the City of Vancouver Parks Board. Extensive work began that offseason in a full-scale stadium renovation which improved washrooms, concessions, concourses, and Children's Play Area. Point-of-purchase concessions increased substantially.</p>
<p>The Canadians announced that former <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals" title="Washington Nationals">Washington Nationals</a> executive Andy Dunn would become the President and General Manager of the Canadians, on January 7, 2008.</p>
<p>In the spring of 2010, the Vancouver Canadians and Scotiabank announced a long-term partnership that would see Nat Bailey Stadium renamed to Scotiabank Field at Nat Bailey Stadium.</p>
<p>In the fall of 2010, the Canadians became the Short Season A affiliate of the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a>. This helped see the team's attendance rise to 162,162 for the season, a team record. In September 2011, the Vancouver Canadians won their first Northwest League Championship title, defeating the <a href="/wiki/Tri-City_Dust_Devils" title="Tri-City Dust Devils">Tri-City Dust Devils</a> 9-2 to win the final series 2-1. The following year, the Vancouver Canadians became back-to-back champions for the first time in franchise history, defeating the <a href="/wiki/Boise_Hawks" title="Boise Hawks">Boise Hawks</a> 12-9 to win the final series 2-1.</p>
<p>On January 25, 2013, the Blue Jays announced that the player development contract with the Canadians had been extended to include the 2016 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2013, outfielder <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Pillar" title="Kevin Pillar">Kevin Pillar</a> became the first alumnus of the team to play in the major leagues for Toronto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 9, 2013 the Canadians became just the third Northwest League team to win three straight championships, defeating the Boise Hawks 5-0 at Scotiabank Field at Nat Bailey Stadium to win the final series 2-1 in front of a sold out crowd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2013 season also saw the Vancouver Canadians draw over 195,000 fans to Scotiabank Field, a fifth consecutive team record which included 23 sold out games.</p>
<p>On November 4, 2013, the Vancouver Canadians were named the 2013 recipient of the John H. Johnson President’s Trophy. The prestigious award is given to minor-league baseball’s top organization. It was the first time that a Canadian-based franchise won minor league baseball's most prized award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The Blue Jays extended their player-development contract with the Canadians through the 2018 season in early 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<li>2012: Defeated <a href="/wiki/Everett_AquaSox" title="Everett AquaSox">Everett</a> 2-0 in semifinals; defeated <a href="/wiki/Boise_Hawks" title="Boise Hawks">Boise</a> 2-1 to win championship.</li>
<li>2013: Defeated <a href="/wiki/Everett_AquaSox" title="Everett AquaSox">Everett</a> 2-0 in semifinals; defeated <a href="/wiki/Boise_Hawks" title="Boise Hawks">Boise</a> 2-1 to win championship.</li>
<li>2014: Defeated <a href="/wiki/Spokane_Indians" title="Spokane Indians">Spokane</a> 2-0 in semifinals; lost to <a href="/wiki/Hillsboro_Hops" title="Hillsboro Hops">Hillsboro</a> 2-0 in finals.</li>
