<p><b>Vyatka Governorate</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru"><i>Вятская губерния</i></span>) was a governorate of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a>, with its capital in city Vyatka (now known as Kirov), from 1796 to 1929. In the governorate’s area were situated most parts of modern <a href="/wiki/Kirov_Oblast" title="Kirov Oblast">Kirov Oblast</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Udmurt_Republic" title="Udmurt Republic">Udmurt Republic</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vyatka_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vyatka Governorate was bordered with <a href="/wiki/Vologda_Governorate" title="Vologda Governorate">Vologda Governorate</a> (to the north), <a href="/wiki/Perm_Governorate" title="Perm Governorate">Perm Governorate</a> (to the east), <a href="/wiki/Nizhny_Novgorod_Governorate" title="Nizhny Novgorod Governorate">Nizhny Novgorod</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazan_Governorate" title="Kazan Governorate">Kazan</a> governorates (to the south), and <a href="/wiki/Kostroma_Governorate" title="Kostroma Governorate">Kostroma Governorate</a> (to the west). Its area was approximately 169,629 km<sup>2</sup> (65,494 sq mi).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vyatka_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 1897 census, the population of the Vyatka Governorate was 3,030,831. Russian people composed 77.4% of the population; <a href="/wiki/Udmurt_people" title="Udmurt people">Udmurt people</a> – 12.5%; <a href="/wiki/Mari_people" title="Mari people">Mari people</a> – 4.8%, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tatar_people" title="Tatar people">Tatar people</a> – 4.1%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> According to 1958 data, the population was 2,123,904; according 1910 data it was 3,747,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
