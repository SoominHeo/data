<p><b>Vanessa Joy Amorosi</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BackOnTrack_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BackOnTrack-1">[1]</a></sup> (born 8 August 1981)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ralphcarr_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ralphcarr-2">[2]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">singer-songwriter</a> and recording artist. Her combined album and single sales have reached over 2 million worldwide. In August 2012, Amorosi announced she was no longer signed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Music" title="Universal Music">Universal Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vanessa_Amorosi_leaves_Universal_to_rebuild_career_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vanessa_Amorosi_leaves_Universal_to_rebuild_career-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanessa_Amorosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Amorosi was born in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a>, in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> family of <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italian</a> origin, the daughter of singers Frank and Joy Amorosi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BackOnTrack_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-BackOnTrack-1">[1]</a></sup> When Amorosi was four, along with her younger sisters Mellissa and Natasha, she would go to tap, jazz and classical ballet classes, which were being run by her uncle. She attended <a href="/wiki/Emerald,_Victoria" title="Emerald, Victoria">Emerald</a> Primary School and Emerald Secondary College.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> At the age of 12, Amorosi started to perform in shopping centers and local council concerts under the supervision of her family. She also performed on Young Talent Time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Milestones_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Milestones-6">[6]</a></sup> Amorosi was discovered performing at Matrioshka,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> a Russian restaurant in <a href="/wiki/Carnegie,_Victoria" title="Carnegie, Victoria">Carnegie</a>, by Jack Strom and signed a recording contract in 1997. After being signed to MarJac Productions (co-owned by managers <a href="/wiki/Mark_Holden" title="Mark Holden">Mark Holden</a> and Jack Strom) and recording a promo CD titled "<a href="/wiki/Get_Here" title="Get Here">Get Here</a>", she secured a deal with Transistor Music Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanessa_Amorosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1999.E2.80.932003:_The_Power">1999–2003: <i>The Power</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanessa_Amorosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1999–2003: The Power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 1999, Amorosi released her debut single "<a href="/wiki/Have_a_Look" title="Have a Look">Have a Look</a>" which became a Top 20 hit and went gold in Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HonestWoman_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-HonestWoman-9">[9]</a></sup> This was followed by her second single "<a href="/wiki/Absolutely_Everybody" title="Absolutely Everybody">Absolutely Everybody</a>", an anthemic <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> song, which reached the Top 10. It was certified double platinum and became the longest charting Australian single by an Australian female artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HonestWoman_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-HonestWoman-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It remained in the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">ARIA</a> Top 50 Singles Chart for over six months after its release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> "Absolutely Everybody" was released internationally and reached the Top 10 in many European countries including the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a> and <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> where it received a gold certification. Amorosi performed at many large events such as the <a href="/wiki/National_Rugby_League" title="National Rugby League">NRL Grand Final</a>, the opening of the Motorcycle Grand Prix at Phillip Island and the new millennium on "New Year's Eve" at Darling Harbour.</p>
<p>Following the success of the first two singles Amorosi released two more singles in 2000 "<a href="/wiki/Shine_(Vanessa_Amorosi_song)" title="Shine (Vanessa Amorosi song)">Shine</a>" and "The Power" became major Australian hits. Both were accredited gold by the ARIA Australian music industry. The rights to create a remixed version of "Shine" were purchased by the airline <a href="/wiki/Ansett_Australia" title="Ansett Australia">Ansett Australia</a>, who used this version for a commercial.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Amorosi was one of many celebrities to appear in the commercial.</p>
<p>From 29 April to 25 May, Amorosi supported <a href="/wiki/Savage_Garden" title="Savage Garden">Savage Garden</a> for 16 shows on the Australian leg of their <a href="/wiki/Affirmation_(Savage_Garden_album)#Affirmation_World_Tour" title="Affirmation (Savage Garden album)"><i>Affirmation</i> World Tour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Milestones_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Milestones-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>Her album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Power_(album)" title="The Power (album)">The Power</a></i> reached number one on the Australian album charts. Amorosi received six ARIA nominations in the year 2000 awards<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and <i>The Power</i> was nominated<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> for "Best Female Artist" and "Best Pop Release" in the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Music_Awards_of_2001" title="ARIA Music Awards of 2001">ARIA Music Awards of 2001</a>. Her largest audience came in September 2000 with performances at the opening and closing ceremonies of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">2000 Sydney Olympics</a> and at the <a href="/wiki/Paralympic_Games" title="Paralympic Games">Paralympic Games</a>. At the opening ceremony of the aforementioned Olympics she sang the Olympic song titled "Heroes Live Forever". Amorosi won the <a href="/wiki/APRA_Awards_(Australia)" title="APRA Awards (Australia)">APRA Award</a> in 2001 for "Most Played Song" on Australian Radio with "<a href="/wiki/Shine_(Vanessa_Amorosi_song)" title="Shine (Vanessa Amorosi song)">Shine</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2001APRA_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-2001APRA-15">[15]</a></sup> and received international acclaim in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> where she won "Best International Newcomer" at the 2001 <a href="/wiki/VIVA_(TV_station)" title="VIVA (TV station)">VIVA</a> Comet Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Later that year she also made a special appearance at the <a href="/wiki/Goodwill_Games" title="Goodwill Games">Goodwill Games</a> Opening and Closing ceremonies in Brisbane. In 2001, she performed at the <a href="/wiki/AFL_Grand_Final" title="AFL Grand Final">AFL Grand Final</a>.</p>
<p>In August 2002, Amorosi traveled to <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> where she was one of the stars of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Games" title="Commonwealth Games">Commonwealth Games</a> Hand-Over Ceremony, performing her single "Shine" and a new song written especially for the event, "I'll Always Be a Melbourne Girl".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biography_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-biography-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2003, Amorosi performed her <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">blues</a> set for the first time at the Melbourne International Music and Blues Festival<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and traveled to Germany to perform her latest European single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/True_To_Yourself" title="True To Yourself">True To Yourself</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Pops" title="Top of the Pops">Top of the Pops</a></i> and the hugely successful long-running show <i><a href="/wiki/Wetten,_dass..%3F" title="Wetten, dass..?">Wetten, dass..?</a></i> which screens throughout Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2003.E2.80.932009:_Somewhere_in_the_Real_World">2003–2009: <i>Somewhere in the Real World</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanessa_Amorosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2003–2009: Somewhere in the Real World">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 January 2006, it was revealed that Amorosi signed with manager Ralph Carr, having completed her seven-year management with MarJac Productions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> She is quoted on the website as saying, "I have been with my previous management for seven years and thought both parties had gone as far as we could together... Moving over to Ralph Carr Management seemed like a natural progression, given the acts he has successfully managed. I am excited and enthused." On 22 November, it was announced that Amorosi had signed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Music" title="Universal Music">Universal Music</a> Australia.</p>
<p>Amorosi's involvement with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kids_Help_Line" title="Kids Help Line">Kids Help Line</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Variety_Club" title="Variety Club">Variety Club</a> led to a number of honours,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> including a 2003 nomination for Australian of the Year. She still has the farm where endangered animals can find refuge, although she does not get to visit much; her friends run it for her. She supported <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">Kiss</a> on their 2008 tour of Australia.</p>
<p>In 2008, Amorosi released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Somewhere_in_the_Real_World" title="Somewhere in the Real World">Somewhere in the Real World</a></i>, through Universal Music Australia, on 24 May 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heraldsunhit_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-heraldsunhit-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The album tracks "Perfect" and "Start It" have both been used in promotions for the Seven Network. "Start It" is Channel 7's AFL song for 2008 and was used again in 2009. Released digitally and in stores on 24 May 2008, <i>Somewhere in the Real World</i> reached gold sales in Australia.</p>
<p>The single "<a href="/wiki/Kiss_Your_Mama!" title="Kiss Your Mama!">Kiss Your Mama!</a>" was serviced to radio on 3 August and released 8 September 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> On a special edition of <i>Australian Idol</i> she sang her hit song "Kiss Your Mama!".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> This marked Amorosi's returned to the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">ARIA</a> charts the first single in many years to be released, the song peaked at No. 15.</p>
<p>Amorosi released her second single "<a href="/wiki/Perfect_(Vanessa_Amorosi_song)" title="Perfect (Vanessa Amorosi song)">Perfect</a>" digitally on 5 April and physically on 26 April 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The single made it to number 1 on the digital iTunes charts on 7 June 2008, and a then equal highest charting singles of her career at No. 4. "Perfect" reached Platinum sales in Australia and was the most played Australian song on Australian radio for 2008. "Perfect" was nominated for the 2009 APRA awards for 'Most Played Australian Work'.</p>
<p>Following the success of her first two singles a third single was released from the album, the song "<a href="/wiki/The_Simple_Things_(Something_Emotional)" title="The Simple Things (Something Emotional)">The Simple Things (Something Emotional)</a>" was released on 20 September 2008. The Song received a video and achieved a top 40 peak. In December 2008 a video for the song "My House" was released onto her website, but never released as a single.</p>
<p>On 20 April 2009, a Vanessa fan website revealed details of a collaboration with U.S. band <a href="/wiki/Hoobastank" title="Hoobastank">Hoobastank</a> on a single titled "<a href="/wiki/The_Letter_(Hoobastank_song)" title="The Letter (Hoobastank song)">The Letter</a>". The website, <a class="external text" href="http://www.vanessaamorosi.net/" rel="nofollow">VanessaAmorosi.net</a>, also provided the duet to be streamed in full.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Amorosi also appears in the video which was shot in Los Angeles at Lacy Street Studio with director <a href="/wiki/P._R._Brown" title="P. R. Brown">Paul Brown</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_White" title="Jack White">Jack White</a>). The video and single was serviced to Australian media on 4 May. The single was released in Australia on 19 June 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.932010:_Hazardous_and_international_releases">2009–2010: <i>Hazardous</i> and international releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanessa_Amorosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2009–2010: Hazardous and international releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vanessa Amorosi's album <i><a href="/wiki/Hazardous_(album)" title="Hazardous (album)">Hazardous</a></i>, became her third ARIA Top 10 album debuting at No. 7, It was also the week's highest album debut by an Australian artist and has been accredited Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">Australian Recording Industry Association</a>. The album has produced the singles "<a href="/wiki/This_Is_Who_I_Am_(song)" title="This Is Who I Am (song)">This Is Who I Am</a>" (Amorosi's first #1), top 30 hit "<a href="/wiki/Hazardous_(song)" title="Hazardous (song)">Hazardous</a>" and third single "<a href="/wiki/Mr._Mysterious" title="Mr. Mysterious">Mr. Mysterious</a>" featuring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seany_B_(artist)" title="Seany B (artist)">Seany B</a>. Amorosi released the album to the German market in early June 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 28 August 2009, Amorosi's new song "<a href="/wiki/This_Is_Who_I_Am_(song)" title="This Is Who I Am (song)">This Is Who I Am</a>" was serviced to Australian radio. On 31 August 2009, Universal Music Australia released a press release for Amorosi's new single with details of new single and album.</p>

<p>"This Is Who I Am" debuted at number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">ARIA Charts</a> for the week beginning 18 October 2009. This is Amorosi's first <a href="/wiki/List_of_number-one_singles_of_2009_(Australia)" title="List of number-one singles of 2009 (Australia)">number 1 single</a>. The single held the top spot for a second week in a row - becoming the first Australian artist to debut at No. 1 and hold No. 1 single since <a href="/wiki/Silverchair" title="Silverchair">Silverchair</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Straight_Lines_(song)" title="Straight Lines (song)">Straight Lines</a>" in 2007. "This Is Who I Am" has achieved 2× platinum sales in Australia making it her most successful single to date in her homeland.</p>
<p>With the success of her album and first single "This Is Who I Am", Amorosi released her second single and title track from the album <i>Hazardous</i>. Amorosi flew to Los Angeles to film the video for "<a href="/wiki/Hazardous_(song)" title="Hazardous (song)">Hazardous</a>" with director Paul Brown (<a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>, Alicia Keys), which was serviced Thursday, 17 December 2009. The single peaked at No. 29 on the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">ARIA</a> Charts a solid follow-up single to her first number 1.</p>

<p>It was announced that Amorosi would collaborate with Mary J. Blige, so Amorosi flew to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> on 1 February to film the music video for her duet with <a href="/wiki/Mary_J._Blige" title="Mary J. Blige">Mary J. Blige</a> for the upcoming single "Each Tear". The new version of the track will feature on an international re-release of Blige's latest record <i><a href="/wiki/Stronger_with_Each_Tear" title="Stronger with Each Tear">Stronger with Each Tear</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 4 March 2010, Amorosi announced "<a href="/wiki/Mr._Mysterious" title="Mr. Mysterious">Mr. Mysterious</a>" as the third single from the <i>Hazardous</i> album via newsletter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> During March 2010 Vanessa Amorosi supported <a href="/wiki/Rob_Thomas_(musician)" title="Rob Thomas (musician)">Rob Thomas</a> on his Australian leg of the <a href="/wiki/Cradlesong_Tour" title="Cradlesong Tour">Cradlesong Tour</a>, as well as performing headline shows for her fans separately.</p>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Holiday_(Vanessa_Amorosi_song)" title="Holiday (Vanessa Amorosi song)">Holiday</a>" was released as the fourth single off <i>Hazardous</i> and charted in the top 50 on the ARIA charts.</p>
<p>In September 2010, Vanessa revealed on her official Twitter account she was back in the studio again with MachoPsycho in London. In October 2010, Amorosi wrote to her mailing list announcing she was writing and recording in Los Angeles for her new studio album,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> which is described to "push boundaries" and "may even shock people".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011.E2.80.93present:_New_Material">2011–present: New Material</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanessa_Amorosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2011–present: New Material">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Amorosi's first single "<a href="/wiki/Gossip_(Vanessa_Amorosi_song)" title="Gossip (Vanessa Amorosi song)">Gossip</a>" from her forthcoming album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/V_(Vanessa_Amorosi_album)" title="V (Vanessa Amorosi album)">V</a></i> was released on 1 July 2011 and became the No. 1 added song on Australian radio in the first week,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> but did not chart in the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">ARIA</a> Top 100 Singles. The second single "<a href="/wiki/Amazing_(Vanessa_Amorosi_song)" title="Amazing (Vanessa Amorosi song)">Amazing</a>" was released 9 September 2011 and peaked at No. 83 on the ARIA Singles Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The song was featured on Channel 7's <a class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jlq4a3dEoAA" rel="nofollow">AFL Grand Final ad</a> which also resulted in Vanessa Amorosi performing at the<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vanessa_AFL_Grand_Final_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vanessa_AFL_Grand_Final-37">[37]</a></sup> AFL Grand Final in 2011. 2011 also saw Vanessa work on such campaigns as Proactiv, Headspace and Kids Helpline. On 3 November 2011, Amorosi announced her album due for release on 11 November would be delayed until further notice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In August 2012, Amorosi announced she was no longer with Universal Music, and would be looking to rebuild her career as an album artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vanessa_Amorosi_leaves_Universal_to_rebuild_career_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Vanessa_Amorosi_leaves_Universal_to_rebuild_career-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a June 2014 interview with <a href="/wiki/APN_News_%26_Media" title="APN News &amp; Media">APN</a>, she said she is currently working on a <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">gospel</a> album and her fifth studio album, which she revealed will be "completely different" to her previous releases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MorningBulletin_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-MorningBulletin-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/David_A._Stewart" title="David A. Stewart">Dave Stewart</a> announced on his website <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-his_website_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-his_website-40">[40]</a></sup> that he has begun writing amazing new material with Vanessa that would be released later in the year or early next year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vanessa is currently recording a new album at <a href="/wiki/Royal_Studios" title="Royal Studios">Royal Studios</a> in Memphis, with <a href="/wiki/David_A._Stewart" title="David A. Stewart">Dave Stewart</a> serving as the producer. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> The album has a tentative release date of early 2017, the title of which is currently undisclosed.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanessa_Amorosi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the release of <i>Hazardous</i> in 2009, Amorosi relocated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>, where she currently lives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MorningBulletin_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-MorningBulletin-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
