<p>The <i><b>Vermonter</b></i> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Passenger_train" title="Passenger train">passenger train</a> operated by <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a> between <a href="/wiki/St._Albans_station_(Vermont)" title="St. Albans station (Vermont)">St. Albans (Vermont)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Station_(New_York_City)" title="Pennsylvania Station (New York City)">New York City</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Union_Station_(Washington,_D.C.)" title="Union Station (Washington, D.C.)">Washington, D.C.</a> It replaced the overnight <i><a href="/wiki/Montrealer_(train)" title="Montrealer (train)">Montrealer</a></i>, which terminated in <a href="/wiki/Central_Station_(Montreal)" title="Central Station (Montreal)">Montreal</a> until 1995.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Montrealer">Montrealer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vermonter_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Montrealer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Montrealer_(train)" title="Montrealer (train)">Montrealer (train)</a></div>
<p>The <i>Vermonter</i> was preceded by an overnight train between Montreal and Washington that was known as the <i>Montrealer</i>. In the days before Amtrak the <i>Montrealer</i> was a service of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston_and_Maine_Railroad" title="Boston and Maine Railroad">Boston and Maine Railroad</a>. Another train, the <i>Ambassador</i> ran the same route during the <a href="/wiki/Daytime" title="Daytime">daytime</a>, but terminated in New York City. Both services used the Boston and Maine's <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_River_Line" title="Connecticut River Line">Connecticut River Line</a> south of <a href="/wiki/Vernon,_Vermont" title="Vernon, Vermont">Vernon, Vermont</a>, rather than the current route over the <a href="/wiki/New_England_Central_Railroad" title="New England Central Railroad">New England Central</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vermonter">Vermonter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vermonter_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Vermonter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vermonter_at_Brattleboro,_August_1997.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="320" data-file-width="500" height="141" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Vermonter_at_Brattleboro%2C_August_1997.jpg/220px-Vermonter_at_Brattleboro%2C_August_1997.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Vermonter_at_Brattleboro%2C_August_1997.jpg/330px-Vermonter_at_Brattleboro%2C_August_1997.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Vermonter_at_Brattleboro%2C_August_1997.jpg/440px-Vermonter_at_Brattleboro%2C_August_1997.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vermonter_at_Brattleboro,_August_1997.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <i>Vermonter</i> at Brattleboro in August 1997</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <i>Vermonter</i> replaced the <i>Montrealer</i> on April 1, 1995, bringing daytime Amtrak service to Vermont.</p>
<p>Business Class was added to replace the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sleeping_cars" title="Sleeping cars">sleeping cars</a> that were taken out of service upon the change to the <i>Vermonter</i>. The route was changed to allow travelers from Vermont to once again stop in <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Massachusetts" title="Springfield, Massachusetts">Springfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hartford,_Connecticut" title="Hartford, Connecticut">Hartford</a>. This was made possible by the use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Control_car_(rail)" title="Control car (rail)">cab cars</a> or locomotives on both ends so that the train could travel east from Springfield to <a href="/wiki/Palmer,_Massachusetts" title="Palmer, Massachusetts">Palmer, Massachusetts</a>, and reverse direction to continue north on the <a href="/wiki/Central_Vermont_Railway" title="Central Vermont Railway">Central Vermont</a>. This detour added an hour of running time, but at the time was judged more practical than seeking to use the direct route over the former Boston and Maine Railroad owned by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guilford_Rail_System" title="Guilford Rail System">Guilford Rail System</a>. The train travels from Washington to <a href="/wiki/New_Haven,_Connecticut" title="New Haven, Connecticut">New Haven</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Corridor" title="Northeast Corridor">Northeast Corridor</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Electric_locomotive" title="Electric locomotive">electric locomotives</a> are substituted for the <a href="/wiki/Diesel_locomotive" title="Diesel locomotive">diesel locomotives</a> used north of that location.</p>
<p>Vermont declined to pay for continuing the <i>Vermonter</i> to Montreal due to high labor and terminal costs in Montreal. For a time Amtrak offered passengers a connecting <a href="/wiki/Amtrak_Thruway_Motorcoach" title="Amtrak Thruway Motorcoach">Thruway bus</a> service, operated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vermont_Transit" title="Vermont Transit">Vermont Transit</a>, which met the train at St. Albans for connections to and from Montreal. Ridership plunged when the train schedule was moved two hours earlier, requiring a southbound departure before 5:00 a.m. The schedule was returned to its previous position, but the service was dropped by Vermont Transit (which had been running it without a subsidy as part of its regular schedule) on October 30, 2005.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vermonter_9646_Wallingford_CT_2012-09-01.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2000" data-file-width="3008" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Vermonter_9646_Wallingford_CT_2012-09-01.JPG/220px-Vermonter_9646_Wallingford_CT_2012-09-01.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Vermonter_9646_Wallingford_CT_2012-09-01.JPG/330px-Vermonter_9646_Wallingford_CT_2012-09-01.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Vermonter_9646_Wallingford_CT_2012-09-01.JPG/440px-Vermonter_9646_Wallingford_CT_2012-09-01.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vermonter_9646_Wallingford_CT_2012-09-01.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i>Vermonter</i> at <a href="/wiki/Wallingford,_Connecticut" title="Wallingford, Connecticut">Wallingford, Connecticut</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On October 30, 2006, the <i>Vermonter</i> began stopping at the towns of <a href="/wiki/Wallingford,_Connecticut" title="Wallingford, Connecticut">Wallingford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Locks,_Connecticut" title="Windsor Locks, Connecticut">Windsor Locks</a> (near <a href="/wiki/Bradley_International_Airport" title="Bradley International Airport">Bradley International Airport</a>) in Connecticut for the first time in its existence.</p>
<p>In the late 2000s Amtrak and the state of <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a> considered the purchase of <a href="/wiki/Diesel_multiple_unit" title="Diesel multiple unit">diesel multiple unit</a> (DMU) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trainsets" title="Trainsets">trainsets</a> for use on the <a href="/wiki/New_Haven,_Connecticut" title="New Haven, Connecticut">New Haven</a>–<a href="/wiki/St._Albans_(city),_Vermont" title="St. Albans (city), Vermont">St. Albans</a> stretch of the line,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> with Amtrak offering a $2 million grant to help make the switch and market the new service. The new cars would purportedly have saved $4.25 million over three years, being four times more fuel efficient than a locomotive-hauled train.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2008 the Vermont state legislature approved the purchase of five cars from <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Railcar" title="Colorado Railcar">Colorado Railcar</a> at the cost of $18.2 million, but the company closed while the decision was awaiting approval of Governor <a href="/wiki/Jim_Douglas" title="Jim Douglas">Jim Douglas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> With no other DMU designs available that were capable of operating in mixed traffic with other trains the plan was dropped.</p>
<p>On November 9, 2010, the State of Vermont, Amtrak, and New England Central began a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_dollar" title="US dollar">$</a>70 million project to increase train speeds along the route in Vermont to 59 miles per hour (95 km/h) between St. Albans and <a href="/wiki/White_River_Junction,_Vermont" title="White River Junction, Vermont">White River Junction, Vermont</a>, and to 79 miles per hour (127 km/h) between White River Junction and <a href="/wiki/Vernon,_Vermont" title="Vernon, Vermont">Vernon, Vermont</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 5, 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Railroad_Administration" title="Federal Railroad Administration">Federal Railroad Administration</a> announced the completion of track work within the states of Vermont and <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a> for the above-mentioned stimulus plan. 190 miles (310 km) of track within the states of Vermont and New Hampshire were refurbished. The track work included installation of <a href="/wiki/Track_(rail_transport)#Continuous_welded_rail" title="Track (rail transport)">continuous welded rail</a>, road-crossing improvements, ballast replacement, tie replacement, bridge repair and renovation, and embankment improvements. The top speed of the line within Vermont was increased to 79 miles per hour (127 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The Massachusetts portion of the track work was ongoing as of 2015.</p>
<p>On October 5, 2015, the southbound <i>Vermonter</i> derailed in <a href="/wiki/Northfield,_Vermont" title="Northfield, Vermont">Northfield, Vermont</a> after striking a rock slide. Five cars and the engine derailed; the engine and an empty car slid down an embankment. Five passengers and two crew members were injured, one seriously.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_route_change">2014 route change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vermonter_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2014 route change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Northbound_Amtrak.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="854" data-file-width="1321" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Northbound_Amtrak.jpg/220px-Northbound_Amtrak.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Northbound_Amtrak.jpg/330px-Northbound_Amtrak.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Northbound_Amtrak.jpg/440px-Northbound_Amtrak.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Northbound_Amtrak.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A <i>Vermonter</i> backing up at Palmer in 2007. Visible are two GE P42DCs and six Amfleet cars.</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_River_Line" title="Connecticut River Line">Connecticut River Line</a></div>
<p>Until 1987, the <i>Montrealer</i> traveled on the Connecticut River Line between Springfield and Brattleboro with a stop in <a href="/wiki/Northampton,_Massachusetts" title="Northampton, Massachusetts">Northampton</a>. Due to the deteriorating condition of the tracks in that section Amtrak ceased service of the train.</p>
<p>When the <i>Vermonter</i> service restored train service between Springfield, Brattleboro and points north in 1995, the <i>Vermonter</i> traveled a somewhat indirect route east to <a href="/wiki/Palmer,_Massachusetts" title="Palmer, Massachusetts">Palmer, Massachusetts</a>, and then up the east side of the <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_River" title="Connecticut River">Connecticut River</a> via <a href="/wiki/Amherst,_Massachusetts" title="Amherst, Massachusetts">Amherst, Massachusetts</a>. It used <a href="/wiki/CSX_Transportation" title="CSX Transportation">CSX Transportation</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Boston_Subdivision" title="Boston Subdivision">Boston Subdivision</a> between Springfield and Palmer. At Palmer, it made a backup move on to the <a href="/wiki/New_England_Central_Railroad" title="New England Central Railroad">New England Central Railroad</a> (NECR), as no direct track connection existed. Massachusetts rehabilitated the more direct Connecticut River Line route with $10 million in state and $73 million in federal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Recovery_and_Reinvestment_Act" title="American Recovery and Reinvestment Act">American Recovery and Reinvestment Act</a> funds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hopes_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-hopes-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>During rehabilitation of the Connecticut River Line, <i>Vermonter</i> service was restored to it on December 29, 2014. With the re-route, the train ceased stopping at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amherst_(Amtrak_station)" title="Amherst (Amtrak station)">Amherst</a> but a stop was restored to <a href="/wiki/Union_Station_(Northampton,_Massachusetts)" title="Union Station (Northampton, Massachusetts)">Northampton</a> and, for the first time for Amtrak, a stop at <a href="/wiki/John_W._Olver_Transit_Center" title="John W. Olver Transit Center">Greenfield</a> was added. The re-route and consequent elimination of the backup move is expected to eliminate about 25 minutes of travel time between Springfield and <a href="/wiki/Brattleboro,_Vermont" title="Brattleboro, Vermont">Brattleboro</a> when the line rehabilitation is complete sometime in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DHG2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-DHG2-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VA_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-VA-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BR_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-BR-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2015, the number of <i>Vermonter</i> riders using the two new stations (in Northampton and Greenfield) was up 84 percent compared to the equivalent station in Amherst the previous year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>A stop in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holyoke_Railroad_Station" title="Holyoke Railroad Station">Holyoke</a> was added in August 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Planned_extension_of_the_Vermonter_to_Montreal">Planned extension of the <i>Vermonter</i> to Montreal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vermonter_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Planned extension of the Vermonter to Montreal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Efforts have been underway for many years to extend the <i>Vermonter</i> to Montreal. In 2012 the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Railroad_Administration" title="Federal Railroad Administration">Federal Railroad Administration</a> awarded $7.9 million to allow for the upgrade of the existing freight rail line between St. Albans and the Canada–US border.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Work on this project was completed in late 2014.</p>
<p>On March 16, 2015, the United States and Canada signed the "Agreement on Land, Rail, Marine, and Air Transport Preclearance Between the Government of the United States of America and the Government of Canada". The agreement would allow for the establishment of a preclearance customs and immigration facility within <a href="/wiki/Central_Station_(Montreal)" title="Central Station (Montreal)">Central Station</a> in Montreal that could be used by the <i>Vermonter</i> and Amtrak's <i><a href="/wiki/Adirondack_(train)" title="Adirondack (train)">Adirondack</a></i> train.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Before the <i>Vermonter</i> can be extended to Montreal the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> must pass enabling legislation for the preclearance agreement and the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Canada" title="Parliament of Canada">Parliament of Canada</a> must <a href="/wiki/Ratification" title="Ratification">ratify</a> the agreement. Construction of a preclearance facility in Central Station is expected to take about three years; one year for planning and permitting and two years for construction. Construction of the preclearance facility is not expected to start until after the preclearance agreement has been approved by both governments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Possible_increased_service">Possible increased service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vermonter_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Possible increased service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Department_of_Transportation" title="Massachusetts Department of Transportation">Massachusetts Department of Transportation</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Vermont_Agency_of_Transportation" title="Vermont Agency of Transportation">Vermont Agency of Transportation</a> are conducting a major study to "examine the opportunities and impacts of more frequent and higher speed intercity passenger rail service on two major rail corridors known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inland_Route" title="Inland Route">Inland Route</a> [the New Haven to Springfield route] and the Boston to Montreal Route."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NNIRI_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-NNIRI-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Boston to Montreal corridor being studied runs west from Boston to <a href="/wiki/Springfield_Union_Station_(Massachusetts)" title="Springfield Union Station (Massachusetts)">Springfield Union Station</a>. From Springfield the rail corridor follows the route of the <i>Vermonter</i> northerly to St. Albans. From St. Albans the corridor continues to the Canada–US border and onward to Montreal Central Station in Quebec. The study is the precursor for possible increased passenger rail service on the route of the <i>Vermonter</i> north of Springfield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NNIRI_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-NNIRI-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vermonter_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The <i>Vermonter</i> uses Amtrak's <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Corridor" title="Northeast Corridor">Northeast Corridor</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Haven%E2%80%93Springfield_Line" title="New Haven–Springfield Line">New Haven–Springfield Line</a> between Washington, D.C., and <a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Massachusetts" title="Springfield, Massachusetts">Springfield, Massachusetts</a>. It then uses the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Department_of_Transportation" title="Massachusetts Department of Transportation">Massachusetts Department of Transportation</a> (MassDOT)-owned <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_River_Line" title="Connecticut River Line">Connecticut River Line</a> between Springfield and <a href="/wiki/Northfield,_Massachusetts" title="Northfield, Massachusetts">Northfield, Massachusetts</a>. North of Northfield it uses the New England Central Railroad.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vermonter_(train)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A typical <i>Vermonter</i> currently consists of four <a href="/wiki/Amfleet" title="Amfleet">Amfleet</a> passenger cars and an Amfleet cafe car. Between New Haven and Washington the train is pulled by an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amtrak_Cities_Sprinter" title="Amtrak Cities Sprinter">Siemens ACS-64</a> electric locomotive (Amtrak used to pull the train with a <a href="/wiki/EMD_AEM-7" title="EMD AEM-7">EMD AEM-7</a> until Amtrak retired them in 2016). North of New Haven diesel <a href="/wiki/GE_Genesis" title="GE Genesis">GE P42DCs</a> handles the train. Prior to the 2014 reroute the <i>Vermonter</i> operated with an Ex-Budd Metroliner cab-car, three Amfleet coaches, a Business Class/Cafe, and usually a P42 on the rear south of Springfield. (North of Springfield this consist was reversed.) During winter periods the Cab-car would be swapped for an Amfleet coach, and a P42 would be on either end regardless of their position north of New Haven.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vat_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-vat-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
