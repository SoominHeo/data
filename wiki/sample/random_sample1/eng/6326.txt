<p>The <b>Bubba Gump Shrimp Company</b> Restaurant and Market is an American seafood restaurant chain inspired by the 1994 film <i><a href="/wiki/Forrest_Gump" title="Forrest Gump">Forrest Gump</a></i>. As of July 2015, forty Bubba Gump Shrimp Co. restaurants operate worldwide. Twenty-nine of these locations are in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, four are in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, three are in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, and one each in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. The company is based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a>, and has been a division of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landry%27s_Restaurants" title="Landry's Restaurants">Landry's Restaurants</a> since 2010.</p>
<p>The first Bubba Gump restaurant opened in 1996 in <a href="/wiki/Monterey,_California" title="Monterey, California">Monterey, California</a> by Viacom Consumer Products. That firm's parent company, <a href="/wiki/Viacom" title="Viacom">Viacom</a>, is owner of <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a>, the distributor of <i>Forrest Gump</i>, and also owns the <b>Rusty Pelican</b> restaurant chain. The Bubba Gump restaurant is named after the film's characters Benjamin Buford <b>"Bubba"</b> Blue and Forrest <b>Gump</b>. In the film, Bubba suggested getting in the shrimping business and, ultimately, Forrest pursued the idea after Bubba's death in the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bubba_Gump_Shrimp_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1995, Paramount Pictures approached Rusty Pelican Restaurants Inc. with a desire to create a restaurant based on a theme from Paramount's 1994 film <i>Forrest Gump</i>. The Bubba Gump Shrimp Co. was created as a result. Within a year the Bubba Gump Shrimp Co. created a concept idea for the restaurant chain which was then licensed by Paramount Licensing, Inc. In 1996, the first Bubba Gump Shrimp Co. restaurant was opened in <a href="/wiki/Monterey,_California" title="Monterey, California">Monterey, California</a>; its success led to its franchising on an international scale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2010, Landry's, Inc. acquired Bubba Gump Shrimp Co. for an undisclosed amount.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The Bubba Gump Shrimp Co. purchase also included a lone Rusty Pelican in <a href="/wiki/Newport_Beach,_California" title="Newport Beach, California">Newport Beach, California</a>.</p>

<p>Like the name suggests, Bubba Gump Shrimp Co.'s menu consists mostly of <a href="/wiki/Shrimp#As_food" title="Shrimp">shrimp</a> dishes and other <a href="/wiki/Seafood" title="Seafood">seafood</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Cuisine_of_the_Southern_United_States" title="Cuisine of the Southern United States">Southern</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cajun_cuisine" title="Cajun cuisine">Cajun cuisine</a>. The restaurant offers dishes named after characters in the movie, like Jenny’s Catch and the restaurant’s bestseller Forrest’s Seafood Feast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Restaurants display movie memorabilia throughout the restaurant. Guests can play <i>Forrest Gump</i> movie trivia and can signal their waiter with a “Stop, Forrest, Stop” sign. Bubba Gump Shrimp Co. also has a children’s menu.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Employee_social_media_policy">Employee social media policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bubba_Gump_Shrimp_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Employee social media policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013, a former Bubba Gump employee claimed that the social media policy in the company's employee handbook had a restrictive effect on employees’ rights by prohibiting them from discussing their jobs online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2015, a <a href="/wiki/National_Labor_Relations_Board" title="National Labor Relations Board">National Labor Relations Board</a> administrative law judge ruled that Bubba Gump did not violate employees’ rights as they did not explicitly prohibit employees from discussing job-related subjects, but only expected them to do so in a civil manner. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> There have been many cases in the last few years in which the National Labor Relations Board found companies social media policies to be overbroad and ruled in favor of the employee(s).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_employees">Notable employees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bubba_Gump_Shrimp_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable employees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Actor <a href="/wiki/Chris_Pratt" title="Chris Pratt">Chris Pratt</a> was discovered in 2000 while working as a waiter at the Bubba Gump Shrimp Co. in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maui,_Hawaii" title="Maui, Hawaii">Maui, Hawaii</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He waited on the table of actor/director <a href="/wiki/Rae_Dawn_Chong" title="Rae Dawn Chong">Rae Dawn Chong</a>, who had starred in one of Pratt's favorite films, <i><a href="/wiki/Commando_(1985_film)" title="Commando (1985 film)">Commando</a></i>. Chong offered Pratt a role in a short movie she was directing at the time.</p>
