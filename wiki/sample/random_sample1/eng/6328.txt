<p>Bustech was founded in 1998 by the proprietors of <a href="/wiki/Surfside_Buslines" title="Surfside Buslines">Surfside Buslines</a>, Joe and Tony Calabro, in the <a href="/wiki/Gold_Coast,_Queensland" title="Gold Coast, Queensland">Gold Coast</a> suburb of Ernest, very close to Surfside's depot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It initially bodied buses on <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_buses" title="Mercedes-Benz buses">Mercedes-Benz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volvo_Buses" title="Volvo Buses">Volvo</a> chassis.</p>
<p>Most of the early buses were built for the Calabro operations Surfside, <a href="/wiki/Busabout_Sydney" title="Busabout Sydney">Busabout</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hawkesbury_Valley_Buses" title="Hawkesbury Valley Buses">Hawkesbury Valley</a>. Following the collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Clifford_Corporation" title="Clifford Corporation">Clifford Corporation</a>, Bustech was successful in winning orders from a variety of operators, primarily in Queensland and New South Wales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In October 2004 Bustech moved to a new factory in Calabro Way, <a href="/wiki/Burleigh_Heads,_Queensland" title="Burleigh Heads, Queensland">West Burleigh</a>.</p>
<p>In 2008 Bustech became part of the <a href="/wiki/Transit_Australia_Group" title="Transit Australia Group">Transit Australia Group</a> as part of a restructuring of the Calabro operations.</p>
<p>In 2008 Bustech launched its first integral bus (both chassis and body), the 10.5 metre MDi. This was followed by the 12.5 metre XDi and in July 2011 by the 12.5 metre CDi double decker, the first double deck citybus built for Australian operation since 1973.</p>
<p>In November 2009 Bustech completed its 1,000th bus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2015, in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Swinburne_University_of_Technology" title="Swinburne University of Technology">Swinburne University of Technology</a>, the Automotive Cooperative Research Centre (AutoCRC) and the Malaysian Automotive Institute, Bustech unveiled its first electric bus to be designed, engineered and manufactured in Australia. Bustech has already signed a AUD$170 million contract to manufacture electric buses for the Malaysian government in late 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Models">Models</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bustech&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Models">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Currently, Bustech produces six bus models:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>VST/VSTH/VSTC</b>: 12.5m low-floor citybus body – built on a variety of chassis, including <a href="/wiki/MAN_Truck_%26_Bus" title="MAN Truck &amp; Bus">MAN</a> 18.310 HOCL-NL, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_OC500LE" title="Mercedes-Benz OC500LE">Mercedes-Benz O500LE</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scania_K_UB" title="Scania K UB">Scania K280UB</a>, <a href="/wiki/Volvo_B12BLE" title="Volvo B12BLE">Volvo B12BLE</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volvo_B7RLE" title="Volvo B7RLE">B7RLE</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>VSTM</b>: 11m low-floor citybus body – built on a Scania K280UB Chassis, The VSTM is a smaller VST body which is similar to but smaller than the MDi Body. State Transit Authority Sydney were the first to take deliveries of the VSTM.</li>
<li><b>SBV/SBM</b>: School/Charter bus body – built on a variety of chassis, including <a href="/wiki/MAN_Truck_%26_Bus" title="MAN Truck &amp; Bus">MAN</a> 18.280, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a> OH1830L, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scania_K94IB" title="Scania K94IB">Scania K94IB</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volvo_B7R" title="Volvo B7R">Volvo B7R</a>. Previously known as the <b>Graduate</b>. SBM stands for School Bus MAN/Mercedes-Benz whilst SBV stands for School Bus Volvo<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>MDi</b>: 10.5m, 43-seater low-floor citybus integral – available with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cummins_ISC" title="Cummins ISC">Cummins ISC</a> 8.3-litre engine and <a href="/wiki/ZF_Friedrichshafen" title="ZF Friedrichshafen">ZF</a> axles RL85A / A132<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>XDi</b>: 12.5m, 55-seater (51 when specified with centre-door) low-floor citybus integral - Available with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cummins_ISC" title="Cummins ISC">Cummins ISC</a> 8.3-litre or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cummins_ISL" title="Cummins ISL">Cummins ISL</a> 9-litre engines and ZF axles RL85A / A132<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>XDi-E</b>: 12.5m, 55-seater (51 when specified with centre-door) low-floor city bus integral. The XDi-E is a Bustech XDi but with an all-electric engine. There was only 1 built as of 2015.</li>
<li><b>CDi</b>: 12.5m, 96-seater low-floor double-deck citybus integral - Available with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cummins_ISL" title="Cummins ISL">Cummins ISL</a> 9-litre engines. <a href="/wiki/Surfside_Buslines" title="Surfside Buslines">Surfside Buslines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Busways_(New_South_Wales)" title="Busways (New South Wales)">Busways</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forest_Coach_Lines" title="Forest Coach Lines">Forest Coach Lines</a> received the first deliveries for high capacity transport during peak periods.</li>
<li><b>ADi</b>: 12.5m, 53-seater low-floor tour bus integral - Available with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cummins_ISL" title="Cummins ISL">Cummins ISL</a> 9-litre engine, <a href="/wiki/Surfside_Buslines" title="Surfside Buslines">Surfside Buslines</a> received the first deliveries as the Gold Coast Shuttle.</li>
<li><b>SDi</b>: 12.5m, 61-seater <a href="/wiki/School_bus" title="School bus">school bus</a> integral – Available with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cummins_ISL" title="Cummins ISL">Cummins ISL</a> 9-litre engine, <a href="/wiki/Surfside_Buslines" title="Surfside Buslines">Surfside Buslines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rover_Coaches" title="Rover Coaches">Rover Coaches</a> received the first deliveries for school transport deliveries.</li>
<li><b>ZDi</b>: 12.5m, The Bustech ZDi is an all-electric Bustech-bodied bus which is the first of its kind, and only 1 ZDi built so far in 2015.</li>
</ul>
<p>Both the MDi and XDi were also available with <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar_Inc." title="Caterpillar Inc.">Caterpillar</a> C7 engines. However, as they have ceased production of highway engines, the vehicles can now only be specified with <a href="/wiki/Cummins" title="Cummins">Cummins</a> engines.</p>
<p>The VST was also available with Volvo B12BLE engine however it ceased production. The Volvo B7RLE engine is used instead on VST's from 2011.</p>
