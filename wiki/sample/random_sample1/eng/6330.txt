<p><b>Virginia</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_County,_Minnesota" title="St. Louis County, Minnesota">Saint Louis County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mesabi_Iron_Range" title="Mesabi Iron Range">Mesabi Iron Range</a>. The population was 8,712 at the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010_Census_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-2010_Census-6">[6]</a></sup> According to a water tower in the middle of town, the city's nickname is the "Queen City", or "Queen City of the North".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virginia,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Virginia was laid out in 1892, and named after <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, the native state of a large share of the lumbermen in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> A post office has been in operation at Virginia since 1893.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virginia,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 19.18 square miles (49.68 km<sup>2</sup>); 18.85 square miles (48.82 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 0.33 square miles (0.85 km<sup>2</sup>) is water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_files_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer_files-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virginia,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Virginia is located on the <a href="/wiki/Mesabi_Range" title="Mesabi Range">Mesabi Range</a>, one of sub-regions within <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Iron_Range" title="Iron Range">Iron Range</a>. Virginia is considered the commerce center of the <a href="/wiki/Mesabi_Range" title="Mesabi Range">Mesabi Range</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virginia,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FactFinder_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FactFinder-3">[3]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 8,712 people, 4,242 households, and 2,019 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 462.2 inhabitants per square mile (178.5/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 4,738 housing units at an average density of 251.4 per square mile (97.1/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 94.7% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.6% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 1.0% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.3% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">other races</a>, and 2.9% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 1.5% of the population.</p>
<p>There were 4,242 households of which 21.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 31.7% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 12.0% had a female householder with no husband present, 4.0% had a male householder with no wife present, and 52.4% were non-families. 46.3% of all households were made up of individuals and 20% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 1.95 and the average family size was 2.74.</p>
<p>The median age in the city was 44.9 years. 18.9% of residents were under the age of 18; 9.3% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 21.8% were from 25 to 44; 27.9% were from 45 to 64; and 22% were 65 years of age or older. The gender makeup of the city was 47.9% male and 52.1% female.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_census">2000 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virginia,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2000 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of 2000, there were 9,157 people, 4,333 households, and 2,270 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 486.1 people per square mile (187.7/km²). There were 4,692 housing units at an average density of 249.1 per square mile (96.2/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 95.17% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.46% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 2.24% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.55% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.01% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.17% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.40% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 0.80% of the population. 21.4% were of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finnish_people" title="Finnish people">Finnish</a>, 13.3% <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a>, 9.9% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_people" title="Norwegian people">Norwegian</a>, 8.8% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_people" title="Italian people">Italian</a> and 7.8% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_people" title="Swedish people">Swedish</a> ancestry.</p>
<p>There were 4,333 households out of which 22.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 38.4% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 10.7% had a female householder with no husband present, and 47.6% were non-families. 42.6% of all households were made up of individuals and 19.6% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.00 and the average family size was 2.73.</p>
<p>In the city the population was spread out with 19.0% under the age of 18, 9.4% from 18 to 24, 25.0% from 25 to 44, 23.3% from 45 to 64, and 23.2% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 43 years. For every 100 females there were 88.4 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 85.9 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the city was $28,873, and the median income for a family was $43,419. Males had a median income of $38,834 versus $22,473 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $17,776. About 10.6% of families and 15.9% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 16.6% of those under age 18 and 10.0% of those age 65 or over.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virginia,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The public high school is <a href="/wiki/Virginia_High_School_(Minnesota)" title="Virginia High School (Minnesota)">Virginia High School</a>, and the public elementary schools are Parkview Learning Center (early childhood programs through second grade) and Roosevelt Elementary (grades three through six). The higher education institution for the city is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mesabi_Range_Community_and_Technical_College" title="Mesabi Range Community and Technical College">Mesabi Range Community and Technical College</a>. The Virginia Public Library is also featured as an educational place for people of all ages. Marquette Catholic School is a private elementary school operated by the Duluth Diocese. Northland Learning Center, a cooperative alternative school for students with disciplinary problems or other special circumstances, operates in the former James Madison Elementary School, which is still owned by ISD No. 706.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Olcott_Park">Olcott Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virginia,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Olcott Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Olcott_Park" title="Olcott Park">Olcott Park</a></i> is a 40-acre (160,000 m<sup>2</sup>) piece of land in Virginia reserved for the city's enjoyment. It has a fountain in the northern part built during the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>. Also, there is a bandstand in the center, used mainly for city band performances. To the south, it borders Parkview Learning Center (see education), to the east 9th Avenue West, to the north 9th Street North, and to the west Greenwood Cemetery. The Olcott Park is also home to the <a href="/wiki/Olcott_Park_Greenhouse" title="Olcott Park Greenhouse">Olcott Park Greenhouse</a>. Olcott Park is named after <a href="/wiki/William_J._Olcott" title="William J. Olcott">William J. Olcott</a> who headed The Oliver Mining Company, which was the largest mining company on the Iron Range for decades.</p>
