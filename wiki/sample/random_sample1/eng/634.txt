<p><i><b>4 Walls</b></i> is the fourth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a> by South Korea-based girl group <a href="/wiki/F(x)_(band)" title="F(x) (band)">f(x)</a>, released by <a href="/wiki/S.M._Entertainment" title="S.M. Entertainment">S.M. Entertainment</a> on October 27, 2015. <a href="/wiki/Lee_Soo-man" title="Lee Soo-man">Lee Soo-man</a>, former president of S.M. Entertainment, served as the <a href="/wiki/Executive_producer" title="Executive producer">executive producer</a> of the album. It marked the group's first release as a four-member group after former member <a href="/wiki/Sulli" title="Sulli">Sulli</a> left the group in August 2015.</p>
<p>To promote the album, f(x) appeared and performed on several <a href="/wiki/Music_programs_of_South_Korea" title="Music programs of South Korea">music programs</a> including <i><a href="/wiki/M_Countdown" title="M Countdown">M Countdown</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Show!_Music_Core" title="Show! Music Core">Show! Music Core</a></i>. The group further embarked on its first concert tour <a href="/wiki/Dimension_4_%E2%80%93_Docking_Station" title="Dimension 4 – Docking Station">Dimension 4 – Docking Station</a> (2016), which visited South Korea and Japan in January and February 2016. The title track "<a href="/wiki/4_Walls_(song)" title="4 Walls (song)">4 Walls</a>" was released as a <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> in conjunction with the release of the album. The record's musical styles are diverse, incorporating the group's signature <a href="/wiki/Electropop" title="Electropop">electropop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> sound with elements of various <a href="/wiki/Music_genre" title="Music genre">genres</a> including <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">EDM</a> and <a href="/wiki/UK_garage" title="UK garage">UK garage</a>.</p>
<p>The album received favorable reviews from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_critics" title="Music critics">music critics</a>, who noted that the other members had the opportunity to showcase their vocal abilities more following the departure of Sulli. The release peaked atop South Korea's <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Album_Chart" title="Gaon Album Chart">Gaon Album Chart</a> and has sold over 76,000 physical copies in the country as of January 2016; in addition, <i>4 Walls</i> topped the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> World Albums Chart and charted at number thirty-nine on the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Oricon_Albums_Chart" title="Oricon Albums Chart">Oricon Albums Chart</a>. The single "4 Walls", meanwhile, peaked at numbers two on the <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Digital_Chart" title="Gaon Digital Chart">Gaon Digital Chart</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Digital_Songs" title="World Digital Songs"><i>Billboard</i> World Digital Songs</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=4_Walls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>South Korea-based girl group <a href="/wiki/F(x)_(band)" title="F(x) (band)">f(x)</a> had been known as a five-member girl group, with its original lineup consisting of members <a href="/wiki/Krystal_Jung" title="Krystal Jung">Krystal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amber_Liu_(singer)" title="Amber Liu (singer)">Amber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Song" title="Victoria Song">Victoria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sulli" title="Sulli">Sulli</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luna_(singer)" title="Luna (singer)">Luna</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The group's last release as a five-member group was <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Light_(album)" title="Red Light (album)">Red Light</a></i>, released in July 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> During the promotion of <i>Red Light</i>, member Sulli did not participate in live performances due to her "suffering from continual negative comments and false rumors," according to the group's parent company <a href="/wiki/S.M._Entertainment" title="S.M. Entertainment">S.M. Entertainment</a>'s official statement on July 25, 2014. f(x)'s promotional activities thereafter involved the four other members, while Sulli took a career hiatus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On August 7, 2015, it was announced that Sulli had officially withdrawn from the group to focus on her acting career, and f(x) would continue to promote as a four-member group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_Review_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BB_Review-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_promotion">Release and promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=4_Walls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Release and promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>f(x) was announced to be working on its then-forthcoming studio album on September 11, 2015, when the group traveled to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeju_Island" title="Jeju Island">Jeju Island</a> to film a <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The album, titled <i>4 Walls</i>, was released <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digitally</a> worldwide on October 27, 2015 by S.M. Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4WiTunes_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-4WiTunes-7">[7]</a></sup> In South Korea, the album was distributed digitally and <a href="/wiki/Compact_disc" title="Compact disc">physically</a> by S.M. Entertainment and <a href="/wiki/KT_Music" title="KT Music">KT Music</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hanteo_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-hanteo-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-melon_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-melon-9">[9]</a></sup> The music video for the title track "4 Walls" was released simultaneously.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The song impacted <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">Korean Broadcasting System</a>'s "K-Pop Connection" radio on October 29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the release of the album, f(x) appeared and performed on several <a href="/wiki/Music_programs_of_South_Korea" title="Music programs of South Korea">music programs</a>. Its first televised appearance was on July 29, 2014 on <a href="/wiki/Mnet_(TV_channel)" title="Mnet (TV channel)">Mnet</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/M_Countdown" title="M Countdown">M Countdown</a></i>, where the group performed "4 Walls" and "Diamond".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The group subsequently appeared on <a href="/wiki/Korean_Broadcasting_System" title="Korean Broadcasting System">KBS</a>' <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Bank_(TV_series)" title="Music Bank (TV series)">Music Bank</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Munhwa_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Munhwa Broadcasting Corporation">MBC</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Show!_Music_Core" title="Show! Music Core">Show! Music Core</a></i> on the two following days, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pontianak_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-pontianak-13">[13]</a></sup> To further promote the album, f(x) embarked on the concert tour <a href="/wiki/Dimension_4_%E2%80%93_Docking_Station" title="Dimension 4 – Docking Station">Dimension 4 – Docking Station</a>, which was its first concert tour since their debut in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The tour kicked off in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, South Korea at <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Park,_Seoul" title="Olympic Park, Seoul">Olympic Park</a> from January 29 to 31, 2016, and further included six shows in four cities of Japan: <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka" title="Fukuoka">Fukuoka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a>. The tour lasted from February 20 to 28, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>

<p>The album, in the words of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a>, keeps up with f(x)'s signature <a href="/wiki/Electropop" title="Electropop">electropop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">synthpop</a> styles that the group cultivated through its previous albums <i><a href="/wiki/Pink_Tape" title="Pink Tape">Pink Tape</a></i> (2013) and <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Light_(album)" title="Red Light (album)">Red Light</a></i> (2014).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pitchfork_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork-19">[19]</a></sup> Jeff Benjamin of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> noted the emphasis on <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> genres on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_Review_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-BB_Review-4">[4]</a></sup> The opening track "<a href="/wiki/4_Walls_(song)" title="4 Walls (song)">4 Walls</a>" was described as a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_house" title="Tropical house">tropical house</a> song with "slinky" <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">synthesizers</a> and "dreamy" <a href="/wiki/Hook_(music)" title="Hook (music)">hooks</a> by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Star_(Malaysia)" title="The Star (Malaysia)">The Star</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-star_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-star-18">[18]</a></sup> It was characterized as an "update" of <a href="/wiki/UK_garage" title="UK garage">UK garage</a> by <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spin_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-spin-17">[17]</a></sup> while <i>Billboard</i> detailed it as having a <a href="/wiki/Deep_house" title="Deep house">deep house</a> sound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_Review_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-BB_Review-4">[4]</a></sup> The next song, "Glitter", is an <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> and synthpop number.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spin_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-spin-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hankyung_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-hankyung-20">[20]</a></sup> "Deja Vu" mixes <a href="/wiki/Industrial_music" title="Industrial music">industrial</a> <a href="/wiki/Beat_(music)" title="Beat (music)">beats</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glitch_pop" title="Glitch pop">glitch pop</a> sounds, while "X" draws influences from the 1980s <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a>, 1990s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disco_music" title="Disco music">disco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues" title="Rhythm and blues">R&amp;B</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spin_17-3"><a href="#cite_note-spin-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_Review_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-BB_Review-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>"Rude Love" is described as a <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a> house song with dance fusion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spin_17-4"><a href="#cite_note-spin-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_Review_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-BB_Review-4">[4]</a></sup> "Diamond" is an electropop song, while "Traveler" features <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retro" title="Retro">retro</a>-styled melodies and smooth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rap" title="Rap">rap</a> verses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-star_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-star-18">[18]</a></sup> The track "Papi", instrumented by synthesizers, infuses <a href="/wiki/Latin_music_(genre)" title="Latin music (genre)">Latin music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">EDM</a> styles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_Review_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-BB_Review-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hankyung_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-hankyung-20">[20]</a></sup> "Cash Me Out" is an EDM song that incorporates elements of American electropop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_Review_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-BB_Review-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hankyung_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-hankyung-20">[20]</a></sup> The album closer "When I'm Alone" is a synthpop track which was initially written by Canadian singer <a href="/wiki/Carly_Rae_Jepsen" title="Carly Rae Jepsen">Carly Rae Jepsen</a> during recording sessions for her third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Emotion_(Carly_Rae_Jepsen_album)" title="Emotion (Carly Rae Jepsen album)">Emotion</a></i> (2015), but its inclusion was withdrawn and its rights were later purchased by S.M. Entertainment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spin_17-5"><a href="#cite_note-spin-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hankyung_20-3"><a href="#cite_note-hankyung-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<p><i>4 Walls</i> was subject to favorable reviews from music critics. Jeff Benjamin from <i>Billboard</i> shared that despite the fact that the album was "arguably worse" than f(x)'s previous albums <i><a href="/wiki/Pink_Tape" title="Pink Tape">Pink Tape</a></i> (2013) and <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Light_(album)" title="Red Light (album)">Red Light</a></i> (2014), it allowed the other members to showcase their vocal abilities more following the departure of Sulli. He praised it as a "rarity in K-pop to do once" by "finding the right sonicscapes" and appreciated the "house and trendy dance" production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_Review_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-BB_Review-4">[4]</a></sup> Benjamin subsequently named <i>4 Walls</i> the second best K-pop album of 2015, writing: "Known for crafting some of the best full-length K-pop albums, f(x) did not disappoint with their long-awaited 4 Walls comeback."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jakob Dorof on behalf of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a> labeled the record a "superficial rebirth" following f(x)'s lineup change and commented that the other members had the chance to express their talents more, particularly with Amber's <a href="/wiki/Alto" title="Alto">alto</a> "harmonies" on "Rude Love".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pitchfork_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-pitchfork-19">[19]</a></sup> He, upon reviewing the album for <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_(magazine)" title="Spin (magazine)">Spin</a></i>, disliked the songs "Deja Vu", "Papi", "Cash Me Out" and "When I'm Alone". He nonetheless labeled them "minor misgivings" and complimented <i>4 Walls</i> on featuring "sophisticated and innovative" musical styles that could "signal a surprise second life for the world's greatest living pop group."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spin_17-7"><a href="#cite_note-spin-17">[17]</a></sup> Echoing Dorof's viewpoint, Chester Chin of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Star_(Malaysia)" title="The Star (Malaysia)">The Star</a></i> was not impressed by the inclusion of the "derivative" tracks "Glitter" and "Cash Me Out", yet expressed his admiration for the "solid and edgy" album's "more cohesive" sound as well as "more polished and sharper" beats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-star_18-4"><a href="#cite_note-star-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=4_Walls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>4 Walls</i> was a commercial success in South Korea, and also charted in Japan and the United States. It debuted atop South Korea's <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Album_Chart" title="Gaon Album Chart">Gaon Album Chart</a> on the chart issue dated October 25–31, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gaon_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-gaon-23">[23]</a></sup> It was the fourth best-selling physical album of October 2015 in South Korea, with sales figures standing at 65,933 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-monthly_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-monthly-24">[24]</a></sup> The album was additionally the eighteenth highest-selling album of November with 7,820 units sold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Overall, it ranked at number thirty-three on the Gaon's 2015 year-end album chart, with accumulated sales of 75,625 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yearend_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-yearend-26">[26]</a></sup> <i>4 Walls</i> debuted at number thirty-nine on Japan's <a href="/wiki/Oricon_Albums_Chart" title="Oricon Albums Chart">Oricon Albums Chart</a> on November 16, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricon_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricon-27">[27]</a></sup> It peaked at number one on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts"><i>Billboard</i> World Albums Chart</a> on November 14, 2015, becoming f(x)'s second number one on the chart following 2013's <i><a href="/wiki/Pink_Tape" title="Pink Tape">Pink Tape</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bill_f.28x.29_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-bill_f.28x.29-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> <i>4 Walls</i> was the group's best-charting album on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Top_Heatseekers" title="Top Heatseekers">Heatseekers Albums Chart</a>, where it charted at number seven.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bill_f.28x.29_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-bill_f.28x.29-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The single "4 Walls" debuted at number two on South Korea's <a href="/wiki/Gaon_Digital_Chart" title="Gaon Digital Chart">Gaon Digital Chart</a> for the week of October 25, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> It was the twenty-sixth best-selling single of October 2015 (199,976 digital units sold),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> and the twelfth best-selling single of November 2015 (247,232 digital units sold) in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The track debuted at number two on the U.S. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Digital_Songs" title="World Digital Songs"><i>Billboard</i> World Digital Songs</a>, becoming their highest-charting song on the chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
