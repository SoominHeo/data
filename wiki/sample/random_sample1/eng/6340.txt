<p><i><b>The Bucket List</b></i> is a 2007 <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy-drama" title="Comedy-drama">comedy-drama</a> <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Rob_Reiner" title="Rob Reiner">Rob Reiner</a>, produced by Reiner, Alan Greisman, <a href="/wiki/Craig_Zadan" title="Craig Zadan">Craig Zadan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neil_Meron" title="Neil Meron">Neil Meron</a>, written by <a href="/wiki/Justin_Zackham" title="Justin Zackham">Justin Zackham</a>, and starring <a href="/wiki/Jack_Nicholson" title="Jack Nicholson">Jack Nicholson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Freeman" title="Morgan Freeman">Morgan Freeman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The main plot follows two <a href="/wiki/Terminal_illness" title="Terminal illness">terminally ill</a> men (portrayed by Nicholson and Freeman) on their <a href="/wiki/Road_trip" title="Road trip">road trip</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Wish_list" title="Wish list">wish list</a> of things to do before they "<a href="/wiki/Kick_the_bucket" title="Kick the bucket">kick the bucket</a>", from which the movie's title gets its name.</p>
<p>The film premiered on December 15, 2007 in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a>. It opened in <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">limited release</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> on December 25, 2007 and was distributed by <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a> The film opened in wide release in the United States and Canada on January 11, 2008 and was released in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> on February 8, 2008, and in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> on February 21, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It received mixed reviews from film critics, but was a box office success, opening at the top of the box office and grossing a total of $175.4 million worldwide.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bucket_List&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two 70-year-old men, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue-collar" title="Blue-collar">blue-collar</a> mechanic Carter Chambers (Freeman) and billionaire hospital magnate Edward Cole (Nicholson) meet for the first time in the hospital after both have been diagnosed with terminal <a href="/wiki/Lung_cancer" title="Lung cancer">lung cancer</a>. Although Edward is reluctant to share a room with Carter, complaining that he "looks half-dead already", they become friends as they undergo their respective treatments.</p>
<p>Carter is a gifted amateur historian and family man who had wanted to become a history professor in his youth, had been "black, broke, [and with a] baby on the way" and, thus, never rose above his status as a mechanic at the McCreath body shop. Carter loves sharing his knowledge, and his favorite show is <i><a href="/wiki/Jeopardy!" title="Jeopardy!">Jeopardy!</a></i>. Edward is a four-time divorced health-care tycoon and cultured loner who enjoys nothing more than tormenting his personal valet/servant, Thomas (Hayes), who later reveals his name is actually Matthew. Edward prefers to call him Thomas because he finds the name Matthew "too biblical". Edward enjoys drinking <a href="/wiki/Kopi_Luwak" title="Kopi Luwak">Kopi Luwak</a>, one of the most expensive coffees in the world.</p>
<p>During their time in the ward, both Carter and Edward seem to find common ground as they have intellectual personalities. Carter begins writing a "bucket list", or things to do before he "<a href="/wiki/Kick_the_bucket" title="Kick the bucket">kicks the bucket</a>". After hearing he has less than a year, Carter discards the list. Edward finds it the next morning and urges Carter to do everything on the list (and adds more things to do), and offers to finance the trip for both of them. Carter agrees, and despite the protests of his wife, Virginia (Todd), Edward and Carter begin their around-the-world vacation. They go skydiving together, drive a <a href="/wiki/Shelby_Mustang" title="Shelby Mustang">Shelby Mustang</a>, fly over the <a href="/wiki/North_Pole" title="North Pole">North Pole</a>, eat dinner at <a href="/wiki/Chevre_d%27or" title="Chevre d'or">Chevre d'or</a> in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, visit and praise the beauty and history of <a href="/wiki/Taj_Mahal" title="Taj Mahal">Taj Mahal</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, ride motorcycles on the <a href="/wiki/Great_Wall_of_China" title="Great Wall of China">Great Wall of China</a>, attend a lion <a href="/wiki/Safari" title="Safari">safari</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>, and visit the base of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mt._Everest" title="Mt. Everest">Mt. Everest</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a> (which was unfortunately shrouded in cloud).</p>
<p>Atop the <a href="/wiki/Great_Pyramid_of_Giza" title="Great Pyramid of Giza">Great Pyramid</a>, looking out over the pyramids of <a href="/wiki/Pyramid_of_Khafre" title="Pyramid of Khafre">Khafre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pyramid_of_Menkaure" title="Pyramid of Menkaure">Menkaure</a>, they confide about faith and family, revealing that Carter has long been feeling less in love with his wife and that Edward is deeply hurt by his estrangement from his only daughter, who disowned him after he drove away her <a href="/wiki/Domestic_violence" title="Domestic violence">abusive</a> husband. In <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, Edward hires a prostitute named Angelica (King) for Carter, who has never been with any woman but his wife. Carter declines and, realizing that he loves his wife, asks to return home.</p>
<p>On the drive back, Carter reciprocates by trying to reunite Edward with his daughter. Believing it as a breach of trust, Edward angrily storms off. Carter returns home to his wife, children, and grandchildren where they have a nice family dinner telling stories and sharing jokes while a frustrated Edward stays home eating frozen dinners alone. The family reunion is short-lived. While preparing for a romantic interlude, Carter suffers a seizure and is rushed to the hospital, with the cancer having spread to his brain.</p>
<p>Edward, who is now in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Remission_(medicine)" title="Remission (medicine)">remission</a>, visits him and they share a few moments, wherein Carter reveals with great amusement how Edward's Kopi Luwak coffee is grown in <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a>, and then fed to and defecated by a <a href="/wiki/Asian_palm_civet" title="Asian palm civet">jungle cat</a> before being harvested because of the special aroma of the gastric juices. Carter crosses off "laugh till I cry" from his bucket list and insists Edward finish the list without him. Carter goes into surgery, but the procedure is unsuccessful and he dies on the operating table.</p>
<p>As news of Carter's death is given to his wife and family, Edward finally attempts to reconcile with his daughter. She accepts him back into her life and introduces him to the granddaughter he never knew he had. After greeting the little girl with a kiss on the cheek, Edward crosses "kiss the most beautiful girl in the world" off the list. Edward delivers a eulogy at the funeral, explaining that he and Carter had been complete strangers, but the last three months of Carter's life were the best three months of his. He crosses off "help a complete stranger for the good" from the list.</p>
<p>The epilogue reveals that Edward lived until age 81, and his ashes were then taken to the summit of an unnamed peak in the <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">Himalayas</a> by his assistant Matthew. As Matthew places a <a href="/wiki/Chock_full_o%27Nuts" title="Chock full o'Nuts">Chock full o'Nuts</a> coffee can alongside another can, he crosses off the last item on the bucket list ("witness something truly majestic") and places it between the cans. Carter's narration reveals the two cans contain their ashes and that Edward would have loved this, because he was "buried on the mountain, and that was against the law".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bucket_List&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bucket_List&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Bucket List</i> received mixed reviews from critics. On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the film has a rating of 41%, based on 167 reviews, with an average rating of 5.1/10. The site's critical consensus reads, "Not even the earnest performances of the two leads can rescue <i>The Bucket List</i> from its schmaltzy script".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> gave the film a score of 42 out of 100, based on 34 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a>, who had <a href="/wiki/Thyroid_cancer" title="Thyroid cancer">thyroid cancer</a>, criticized the film's portrayal of cancer sufferers, writing in his one-star review that <i>The Bucket List</i> "...thinks dying of cancer is a laff riot followed by a dime-store <a href="/wiki/Epiphany_(feeling)" title="Epiphany (feeling)">epiphany</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bucket_List&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The film opened in wide release in the United States and Canada on January 11, 2008 and grossed $19,392,416 from 2,911 theaters, averaging $6,662 per theater and ranking #1 at the box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The film closed on June 5, 2008, never having a weekend-to-weekend decline of more than 40%, and ended up with a final gross of $93,466,502 in the United States and Canada and another $81,906,000 overseas, for a total gross of $175,372,502 worldwide, easily recouping the film's considerable $45 million budget and turning a sizable profit for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Pictures" title="Warner Bros. Pictures">Warner Bros.</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boxofficemojo_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-boxofficemojo-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bucket_List&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Named one of the Top Ten Films of the Year by the <a href="/wiki/National_Board_of_Review" title="National Board of Review">National Board of Review</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Bucket_List&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Film_score" title="Film score">score</a> album from Varèse Sarabande was released on January 15, 2008, featuring composer <a href="/wiki/Marc_Shaiman" title="Marc Shaiman">Marc Shaiman</a>'s original score for the film as well as a selection of newly recorded themes from Shaiman's previous scoring projects, including <i><a href="/wiki/City_Slickers" title="City Slickers">City Slickers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Simon_Birch" title="Simon Birch">Simon Birch</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Addams_Family_(film)" title="The Addams Family (film)">The Addams Family</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mother_(1996_film)" title="Mother (1996 film)">Mother</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/North_(1994_film)" title="North (1994 film)">North</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sleepless_in_Seattle" title="Sleepless in Seattle">Sleepless in Seattle</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Park:_Bigger_Longer_%26_Uncut" title="South Park: Bigger Longer &amp; Uncut">South Park: Bigger Longer &amp; Uncut</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Saturday_Night" title="Mr. Saturday Night">Mr. Saturday Night</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Stuart_Saves_His_Family" title="Stuart Saves His Family">Stuart Saves His Family</a></i>. It also features a rearranged version of the <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> theme "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goldfinger_(song)" title="Goldfinger (song)">Goldfinger</a>" (titled "Printmaster"), with Shaiman's own voice and lyrics in which he spoofs the industry's habit of tracking music in scenes where they don't belong.</p>
