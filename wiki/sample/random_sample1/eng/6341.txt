<p><b>Buckingham Browne &amp; Nichols School</b>, often referred to as <b>BB&amp;N</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Private_school" title="Private school">private</a> <a href="/wiki/Day_school" title="Day school">day school</a> on three campuses in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_Massachusetts" title="Cambridge, Massachusetts">Cambridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, educating students from pre-kindergarten (called "Beginners") to 12th grade. BB&amp;N was established by the 1974 merger of two independent schools, <b>Buckingham School</b>, founded in 1889, and <b>Browne &amp; Nichols School</b> (B&amp;N), founded in 1883.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upper_School" title="Upper School">Upper School</a>, grades 9–12, is located at 80 Gerry's Landing Road on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Charles_River" title="Charles River">Charles River</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_School" title="Middle School">Middle School</a>, grades 7–8, is on Sparks Street, and the Lower School, grades pre-K–6, is on Buckingham Street. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston_Magazine" title="Boston Magazine">Boston Magazine</a> ranked BB&amp;N #5 in a list of the top private high schools in greater Boston for 2009 and, in late 2007, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" title="Wall Street Journal">Wall Street Journal</a> listed BB&amp;N as one of the 50 best schools in the world for its success in preparing students to enter top American universities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> BB&amp;N received another kind of attention in 2006 when <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> magazine found it to be the ninth most expensive private school in the country, the only day school in the top ten.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The school has produced three of the 27 <a href="/wiki/Presidential_Scholars_Program" title="Presidential Scholars Program">Presidential Scholars</a> from Massachusetts since the inception of the program in 1964 and is a member of the <a href="/wiki/G20_Schools" title="G20 Schools">G20 Schools</a> group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bivouac">Bivouac</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckingham_Browne_%26_Nichols&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Bivouac">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The "Bivouac", an eleven-day camping trip at the school's property in <a href="/wiki/Harrisville,_New_Hampshire" title="Harrisville, New Hampshire">Harrisville, New Hampshire</a>, at the beginning of each freshmen year is a 56-year tradition. On the first day of school, the freshman class is bussed up to the Bivouac location (the former Camp Marienfeld) near <a href="/wiki/Mount_Monadnock" title="Mount Monadnock">Mount Monadnock</a> for the eleven-day trip. Students cook over open fires, bathe in a lake, build latrines, construct A-frames, and participate in nature hikes and ropes courses, all while getting to know their classmates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The original Bivouac was at a site in Maine made available to B&amp;N through a faculty member, Gibby Graves.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckingham_Browne_%26_Nichols&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>BB&amp;N is home to a school newspaper (The Vanguard), a political opinion magazine (The Point of View), a school art and literature magazine (The Spectator), and a satirical newspaper (The Mouthguard).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Vanguard"><i>The Vanguard</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckingham_Browne_%26_Nichols&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Vanguard">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vanguardstitch.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="391" data-file-width="255" height="337" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bf/Vanguardstitch.jpg/220px-Vanguardstitch.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/bf/Vanguardstitch.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vanguardstitch.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
BB&amp;N's newspaper, the Vanguard.</div>

<p>The school's official student-run newspaper, <i>The Vanguard</i> regularly wins gold medals from the Columbia Scholastic Press Association, a for-profit section of Columbia University's Graduate School of Journalism that offers "critiques" of print publications for a yearly fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Founded in 1973 by John Decker, the school-funded paper publishes eight issues a year. Repeating monthly features include Notable Quotables, Horns &amp; Halos, Readers Respond, 5 Things, and Face-Off. The paper features on- and off-campus news, features, arts, sports, cartoons, editorials, photos, and columns.</p>
<p>The editor at large of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Republic" title="The New Republic">The New Republic</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Beinart" title="Peter Beinart">Peter Beinart</a>, is a former <i>Vanguard</i> writer, <a href="/wiki/Mindy_Kaling" title="Mindy Kaling">Mindy Kaling</a> was the comedic (and often cutting) back page columnist for two years, and former <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Crimson" title="Harvard Crimson">Harvard Crimson</a> President and current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wall_St._Journal" title="Wall St. Journal">Wall St. Journal</a> contributor Lauren Schuker served as Editorials Editor. <i>The Vanguard</i> has featured interviews with <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Howard_Dean" title="Howard Dean">Howard Dean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_Poggioli" title="Sylvia Poggioli">Sylvia Poggioli</a>, an alumna of the school. Stephen Burgard, director of the Northeastern Univ. School of Journalism, was among the staffers for the paper's B&amp;N predecessor, <i>The B&amp;N News</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Point_of_View">The Point of View</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckingham_Browne_%26_Nichols&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Point of View">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The official political opinion magazine of BB&amp;N is <i>The Point of View</i>, or POV, and is BB&amp;N's only full color publication. Publishing a magazine each trimester, the POV is entirely student run. The POV's major sections are "The News," in which student writers contribute their opinions on large news stories of recent times, "Food for Thought," in which writers contribute pieces that are thought provoking, "Controversy," in which students voice their opinions on highly controversial issues, and "Opinion," in which the POV's columnists and the occasional writer contribute articles on a range of topics. With the exception of a few contributors, the POV's writers are for the most part liberal, which is reflective of the political leanings of the school. The POV's final page is home to a column, "Rebuttal," in which one of the POV's conservative writers voices his or her opinion on a topic.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Spectator">The Spectator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckingham_Browne_%26_Nichols&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Spectator">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The official arts and literature magazine of BB&amp;N is <i>The Spectator</i>, which has often included many pieces recognized by the Scholastic Art and Writing Awards. <i>The Spectator</i> prints twice a year and is entirely student run. Unlike BB&amp;N's other publications, <i>The Spectator</i> accepts submissions rather than having writers or artists on the magazine's staff. Submissions are reviewed by the staff of <i>The Spectator</i> without looking at the name of the student who submitted the work. In past issues, <i>The Spectator</i> has accepted musical submissions by adding a page of QR codes that lead viewers to a YouTube page where the student performance is shown.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Basketball">Basketball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckingham_Browne_%26_Nichols&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Basketball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rowing">Rowing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckingham_Browne_%26_Nichols&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Rowing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The name of the school's athletic teams, "the Knights," has its origins in a 1920s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boston_Globe" title="The Boston Globe">Boston Globe</a></i> article which referred to the rowing team in particular, undefeated against the likes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harvard" title="Harvard">Harvard</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MIT" title="MIT">MIT</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kent_School" title="Kent School">Kent School</a>, as "the Black Knights of the Charles," itself a reference to the <a href="/wiki/Army_Black_Knights" title="Army Black Knights">Army Black Knights</a>. In addition to taking the team name, Browne &amp; Nichols also took black and white as its colors after the article. The Buckingham School's colors, blue and gold, were made the combined school's colors after the merger. The school was the first American schoolboy crew to win the <a href="/wiki/Henley_Royal_Regatta" title="Henley Royal Regatta">Henley Royal Regatta</a> in Henley-on-Thames, England, winning the Thames Challenge Cup in 1929.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Washington Post commented:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"The Thames Challenge Cup, prize of England's famous rowing tournament, was captured today by eight young <a href="/wiki/Thumbnail" title="Thumbnail">thumbnail</a> oarsmen from the Browne and Nichols School...The American boys, after each victory, gave a fine display of school spirit and overflowing "pep" which added to their already great popularity on the river...Their success was the more impressive when it is considered that the average age of the oarsmen is younger than the average of their defeated rivals. The boys will be received by the American Ambassador at London Monday and then will begin an educational tour of England."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baseball">Baseball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckingham_Browne_%26_Nichols&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Baseball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The varsity baseball team won ISL titles in 2009 and 2010, going undefeated in the latter season.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fencing">Fencing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckingham_Browne_%26_Nichols&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Fencing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The BB&amp;N Fencing Team fields Épée, Sabre, and Foil squads for both men and women. Over the years, the team has had numerous individual state champions, a national Division III champion and, in 2013 and 2014, won the Massachusetts High School State Championship.</p>
