<p><b>Burton Leon</b> "<b>Burt</b>" <b>Reynolds</b> (born February 11, 1936) is an American actor, director and producer. He has starred in many films, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Deliverance" title="Deliverance">Deliverance</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Longest_Yard_(1974_film)" title="The Longest Yard (1974 film)">The Longest Yard</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Smokey_and_the_Bandit" title="Smokey and the Bandit">Smokey and the Bandit</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Boogie_Nights" title="Boogie Nights">Boogie Nights</a></i>, in which he was nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor">Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Burt_Reynolds_Gunsmoke_1962.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="853" data-file-width="678" height="214" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Burt_Reynolds_Gunsmoke_1962.JPG/170px-Burt_Reynolds_Gunsmoke_1962.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Burt_Reynolds_Gunsmoke_1962.JPG/255px-Burt_Reynolds_Gunsmoke_1962.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Burt_Reynolds_Gunsmoke_1962.JPG/340px-Burt_Reynolds_Gunsmoke_1962.JPG 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Burt_Reynolds_Gunsmoke_1962.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Reynolds in September 1962</div>
</div>

<p>Reynolds is the son of Fern H. (née Miller; 1902–1992) and Burton Milo Reynolds (1906–2002). He has <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_American" title="English American">English</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_American" title="Scottish American">Scottish</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scotch-Irish_American" title="Scotch-Irish American">Scots-Irish</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_American" title="Dutch American">Dutch</a> ancestry, and is also said to have Cherokee roots.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bravo_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bravo-2">[2]</a></sup> In his 2014 book, <i>But Enough About Me</i>, Reynolds said his mother had <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_American" title="Italian American">Italian</a> ancestry. During his career, Reynolds often claimed to have been born in Waycross, Georgia, but confirmed in 2015 that he was actually born in <a href="/wiki/Lansing,_Michigan" title="Lansing, Michigan">Lansing, Michigan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was born on February 11, 1936,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orsg_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-orsg-4">[4]</a></sup> and in his <a href="/wiki/Autobiography" title="Autobiography">autobiography</a> stated that Lansing is where his family lived when his father was <a href="/wiki/Conscription" title="Conscription">drafted</a> into the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-birthplace_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-birthplace-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Reynolds, his mother and sister joined his father at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Leonard_Wood,_Missouri#Military_base" title="Fort Leonard Wood, Missouri">Fort Leonard Wood</a>, Missouri, and lived there for two years. When Reynolds' father was sent to Europe, the family moved to <a href="/wiki/Lake_City,_Michigan" title="Lake City, Michigan">Lake City, Michigan</a>, where his mother had been raised.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1946, the family moved to <a href="/wiki/Riviera_Beach,_Florida" title="Riviera Beach, Florida">Riviera Beach, Florida</a>. His father became Chief of Police of <a href="/wiki/Riviera_Beach,_Florida" title="Riviera Beach, Florida">Riviera Beach</a>, which is adjacent to the north of <a href="/wiki/West_Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="West Palm Beach, Florida">West Palm Beach, Florida</a>. During <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/10th_grade" title="10th grade">10th grade</a> at <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_Lakes_Community_High_School" title="Palm Beach Lakes Community High School">Palm Beach High School</a>, Reynolds was named First Team All State and All <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_United_States" title="Southeastern United States">Southern</a> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fullback_(American_football)" title="Fullback (American football)">fullback</a>, and received multiple scholarship offers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>After graduating from Palm Beach High in West Palm Beach, he attended <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_University" title="Florida State University">Florida State University</a> on a <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">football</a> scholarship and played <a href="/wiki/Running_back" title="Running back">halfback</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> While at Florida State, Reynolds became roommates with now notable college football broadcaster and analyst <a href="/wiki/Lee_Corso" title="Lee Corso">Lee Corso</a>, and also became a brother of the <a href="/wiki/Phi_Delta_Theta" title="Phi Delta Theta">Phi Delta Theta</a> fraternity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Reynolds hoped to be named to All-American teams and to have a career in professional football, but he was injured in the first game of his sophomore season, and a car accident later that year worsened the injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>With his university football career over, Reynolds considered becoming a police officer, but his father suggested that he finish university and become a <a href="/wiki/Probation_officer" title="Probation officer">parole officer</a>. To keep up with his studies, he began taking classes at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_Community_College" title="Palm Beach Community College">Palm Beach Junior College</a> (PBJC) in neighboring <a href="/wiki/Lake_Worth,_Florida" title="Lake Worth, Florida">Lake Worth</a>. In his first term at PBJC, Reynolds was in an English class taught by <a href="/wiki/Watson_B._Duncan_III" title="Watson B. Duncan III">Watson B. Duncan III</a>. Duncan pushed Reynolds into trying out for a play he was producing, <i><a href="/wiki/Outward_Bound_(play)" title="Outward Bound (play)">Outward Bound</a></i>. He cast Reynolds in the lead role based on having heard Reynolds read Shakespeare in class, leading to Reynolds winning the 1956 Florida State Drama Award for his performance. In his autobiography, Reynolds refers to Duncan as his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mentor" title="Mentor">mentor</a> and the most influential person in his life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage">Stage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The Florida State Drama Award included a scholarship to the Hyde Park Playhouse, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_stock_theater" title="Summer stock theater">summer stock theater</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Hyde_Park,_New_York" title="Hyde Park, New York">Hyde Park, New York</a>. Reynolds saw the opportunity as an agreeable alternative to more physically-demanding summer jobs, but did not yet see acting as a possible career. While working there, Reynolds met <a href="/wiki/Joanne_Woodward" title="Joanne Woodward">Joanne Woodward</a>, who helped him find an agent and was cast in <i><a href="/wiki/Tea_and_Sympathy_(play)" title="Tea and Sympathy (play)">Tea and Sympathy</a></i> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neighborhood_Playhouse" title="Neighborhood Playhouse">Neighborhood Playhouse</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. After his Broadway debut <i>Look, We've Come Through</i>, he received favorable reviews for his performance and went on tour with the cast, driving the bus and appearing on stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> After the tour, Reynolds returned to New York and enrolled in acting classes. His classmates included <a href="/wiki/Frank_Gifford" title="Frank Gifford">Frank Gifford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carol_Lawrence" title="Carol Lawrence">Carol Lawrence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Buttons" title="Red Buttons">Red Buttons</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jan_Murray" title="Jan Murray">Jan Murray</a>. After a botched improvisation in acting class, Reynolds briefly considered returning to Florida, but he soon got a part in a revival of <i><a href="/wiki/Mister_Roberts_(play)" title="Mister Roberts (play)">Mister Roberts</a></i>, in which <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Heston" title="Charlton Heston">Charlton Heston</a> played the starring role. After the play closed, the director, <a href="/wiki/John_Forsythe" title="John Forsythe">John Forsythe</a>, arranged a film audition with <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Logan" title="Joshua Logan">Joshua Logan</a> for Reynolds. The film was <i><a href="/wiki/Sayonara" title="Sayonara">Sayonara</a>.</i> Reynolds was told that he could not be in the film because he looked too much like <a href="/wiki/Marlon_Brando" title="Marlon Brando">Marlon Brando</a>. Logan advised Reynolds to go to <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a>, but Reynolds did not feel confident enough to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He worked in a variety of different jobs, such as waiting tables, washing dishes, driving a delivery truck and as a <a href="/wiki/Bouncer_(doorman)" title="Bouncer (doorman)">bouncer</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Roseland_Ballroom" title="Roseland Ballroom">Roseland Ballroom</a>. While working as a <a href="/wiki/Stevedore" title="Stevedore">dockworker</a>, Reynolds was offered $150 to jump through a glass window on a live television show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_and_film">Television and film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Television and film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Loni_Anderson_and_Burt_Reynolds.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="666" data-file-width="842" height="174" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Loni_Anderson_and_Burt_Reynolds.jpg/220px-Loni_Anderson_and_Burt_Reynolds.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Loni_Anderson_and_Burt_Reynolds.jpg/330px-Loni_Anderson_and_Burt_Reynolds.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Loni_Anderson_and_Burt_Reynolds.jpg/440px-Loni_Anderson_and_Burt_Reynolds.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Reynolds guest-starred in the <i><a href="/wiki/Pony_Express_(TV_series)" title="Pony Express (TV series)">Pony Express</a></i> episode, "The Good Samaritan", which aired in 1960 on the centennial of the <a href="/wiki/Pony_Express" title="Pony Express">famed mail route</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He used television fame to secure leading roles for low-budget films and played the titular role in <i><a href="/wiki/Navajo_Joe" title="Navajo Joe">Navajo Joe</a></i>. He later disparaged the series, telling <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Carson" title="Johnny Carson">Johnny Carson</a> that Dan August had "two forms of expression: "mean and meaner". Reynolds appeared on ABC's <i><a href="/wiki/The_American_Sportsman" title="The American Sportsman">The American Sportsman</a></i> hosted by outdoors <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Grits_Gresham" title="Grits Gresham">Grits Gresham</a>, who took celebrities on <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shooting" title="Shooting">shooting</a> trips around the world. <a href="/wiki/Saul_David" title="Saul David">Saul David</a> considered Reynolds to star in <i><a href="/wiki/Our_Man_Flint" title="Our Man Flint">Our Man Flint</a></i>, but <a href="/wiki/Lew_Wasserman" title="Lew Wasserman">Lew Wasserman</a> rejected him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Albert_R._Broccoli" title="Albert R. Broccoli">Albert R. Broccoli</a> asked Reynolds to play <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a>, but he turned the role down, saying "An American can't play James Bond. It just can't be done."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The role went to <a href="/wiki/George_Lazenby" title="George Lazenby">George Lazenby</a>. Reynolds then filmed <i><a href="/wiki/Shark!_(film)" title="Shark! (film)">Shark!</a></i> with director <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sam_Fuller" title="Sam Fuller">Sam Fuller</a> and Fuller disowned the rough cuts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Reynolds made his breakout performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Deliverance" title="Deliverance">Deliverance</a></i> and gained notoriety when he appeared in the April 1972 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_(magazine)" title="Cosmopolitan (magazine)">Cosmopolitan</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Reynolds claims the centerfold in Cosmopolitan hurt the chances for the film and cast to receive Academy Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In 1977, Reynolds and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Nolte" title="Nick Nolte">Nick Nolte</a> declined the role of <a href="/wiki/Han_Solo" title="Han Solo">Han Solo</a> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i> franchise, which then went to <a href="/wiki/Harrison_Ford" title="Harrison Ford">Harrison Ford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> After starring in <i><a href="/wiki/Boogie_Nights" title="Boogie Nights">Boogie Nights</a></i>, he refused to appear in <a href="/wiki/Paul_Thomas_Anderson" title="Paul Thomas Anderson">Paul Thomas Anderson</a>'s third film, <i><a href="/wiki/Magnolia_(film)" title="Magnolia (film)">Magnolia</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_roles">Other roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1973, Reynolds released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Ask_Me_What_I_Am" title="Ask Me What I Am">Ask Me What I Am</a></i> and sang along with <a href="/wiki/Dolly_Parton" title="Dolly Parton">Dolly Parton</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_Little_Whorehouse_in_Texas_(film)" title="The Best Little Whorehouse in Texas (film)">The Best Little Whorehouse in Texas</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On March 15, 1978, Reynolds earned a star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a> and built a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dinner_theatre" title="Dinner theatre">dinner theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jupiter,_Florida" title="Jupiter, Florida">Jupiter, Florida</a> followed by other franchise locations of the Reynolds Celebrity Dinner Theater including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beacham_Theater" title="Beacham Theater">Beacham Theater</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orlando" title="Orlando">Orlando</a>. His celebrity was such that he drew not only big-name stars to appear in productions, but also to sell-out audiences. He sold the venue in the early 1990s, but a museum highlighting his career still operates nearby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> From 1977 to 1981, Reynolds topped the Quigley Publications poll of movie exhibitors, who voted him the top box-office attraction in the country. Only Bing Crosby won the poll more consecutive years. Despite much success, Reynolds' finances expired, and he filed for bankruptcy, due in part to an extravagant lifestyle, a divorce from <a href="/wiki/Loni_Anderson" title="Loni Anderson">Loni Anderson</a> and failed investments in some Florida restaurant chains in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-EW-26">[26]</a></sup> The filing was under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chapter_11" title="Chapter 11">Chapter 11</a>, from which Reynolds emerged two years later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-EW-26">[26]</a></sup> Reynolds co-authored the children's book <i>Barkley Unleashed A Pirate</i>, a "whimsical tale [that] illustrates the importance of perseverance, the wonders of friendship and the power of imagination".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In 2002, he voiced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avery_Carrington" title="Avery Carrington">Avery Carrington</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Theft_Auto:_Vice_City" title="Grand Theft Auto: Vice City">Grand Theft Auto: Vice City</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Reynolds' close friends include <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Carson" title="Johnny Carson">Johnny Carson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dom_DeLuise" title="Dom DeLuise">Dom DeLuise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Reed" title="Jerry Reed">Jerry Reed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Nelson_Reilly" title="Charles Nelson Reilly">Charles Nelson Reilly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tammy_Wynette" title="Tammy Wynette">Tammy Wynette</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lucie_Arnaz" title="Lucie Arnaz">Lucie Arnaz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adrienne_Barbeau" title="Adrienne Barbeau">Adrienne Barbeau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tawny_Little" title="Tawny Little">Tawny Little</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dinah_Shore" title="Dinah Shore">Dinah Shore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Evert" title="Chris Evert">Chris Evert</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Reynolds was married to <a href="/wiki/Judy_Carne" title="Judy Carne">Judy Carne</a> from 1963 to 1965, and to <a href="/wiki/Loni_Anderson" title="Loni Anderson">Loni Anderson</a> from 1988 to 1993. He and Anderson adopted a son, Quinton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He had a relationship with <a href="/wiki/Sally_Field" title="Sally Field">Sally Field</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlanta_nightclub">Atlanta nightclub</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Atlanta nightclub">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the late 1970s, Reynolds opened Burt's Place, a nightclub restaurant in the Omni International Hotel in the <a href="/wiki/Hotel_District" title="Hotel District">Hotel District</a> of <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Atlanta" title="Downtown Atlanta">Downtown Atlanta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_team_owner">Sports team owner</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sports team owner">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1982, Reynolds became a co-owner of the <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Bandits" title="Tampa Bay Bandits">Tampa Bay Bandits</a>, a professional American football team in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Football_League" title="United States Football League">USFL</a>, whose nickname was inspired by the <i>Smokey and the Bandit</i> trilogy and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skoal_tobacco#Bandits_and_Pouches" title="Skoal tobacco">Skoal Bandit</a>, a primary sponsor for the team as a result of also sponsoring Reynolds' race team. Reynolds also co-owned a <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winston_Cup" title="Winston Cup">Winston Cup</a> team, <a href="/wiki/Mach_1_Racing" title="Mach 1 Racing">Mach 1 Racing</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Hal_Needham" title="Hal Needham">Hal Needham</a>, which ran the #33 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skoal_tobacco#Bandits_and_Pouches" title="Skoal tobacco">Skoal Bandit</a> car with driver <a href="/wiki/Harry_Gant" title="Harry Gant">Harry Gant</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health">Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While filming <i><a href="/wiki/City_Heat" title="City Heat">City Heat</a></i>, Reynolds was struck in the face with a metal chair, which broke his jaw and caused <a href="/wiki/Temporomandibular_joint_dysfunction" title="Temporomandibular joint dysfunction">temporomandibular joint dysfunction</a>. He lost thirty pounds from not eating. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Painkillers" title="Painkillers">painkillers</a> he was prescribed led to addiction, which took several years to break. Reynolds underwent back surgery in May 2009 and a quintuple <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heart_bypass" title="Heart bypass">heart bypass</a> in February 2010.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_problems">Financial problems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burt_Reynolds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Financial problems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 16, 2011, Merrill Lynch Credit Corporation filed foreclosure papers, claiming Reynolds owed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_dollar" title="US dollar">$</a>1.2 million on his home in <a href="/wiki/Hobe_Sound,_Florida" title="Hobe Sound, Florida">Hobe Sound, Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Reynolds owned the Burt Reynolds Ranch, where scenes for <i>Smokey and the Bandit</i> were filmed and which once had a petting zoo, until its sale during bankruptcy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In April 2014, the 153-acre rural property was rezoned for residential use and the <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_County,_Florida" title="Palm Beach County, Florida">Palm Beach County</a> school system could sell it to residential developer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K._Hovnanian_Homes" title="K. Hovnanian Homes">K. Hovnanian Homes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
