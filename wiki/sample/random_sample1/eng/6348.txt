<p>The original <b>Buffalo Bisons</b> <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> club played in the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> between <a href="/wiki/1879_in_baseball" title="1879 in baseball">1879</a> and <a href="/wiki/1885_in_baseball" title="1885 in baseball">1885</a>. The Bisons played their games at <a href="/wiki/Riverside_Park_(stadium)" title="Riverside Park (stadium)">Riverside Park</a> (1879–83) and <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Park_(Buffalo)" title="Olympic Park (Buffalo)">Olympic Park</a> (1884-85) in <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>. The NL Bisons are included in the history of the minor-league team <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Bisons" title="Buffalo Bisons">of the same name</a> that still plays today; it is thus the only extant NL team from the 19th century that both still exists and no longer plays in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Highlights_and_memorable_moments">Highlights and memorable moments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buffalo_Bisons_(NL)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Highlights and memorable moments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1878_in_baseball" title="1878 in baseball">1877</a>: A precursor to the Bisons played an independent schedule, finishing with a 79-28-3 record. The team subsequently joined the National League. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.minorleaguebaseball.com/app/milb/history/top100.jsp?idx=24" rel="nofollow">[1]</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1880_in_baseball" title="1880 in baseball">1880</a>: Future Hall of Fame pitcher <a href="/wiki/Charles_Radbourn" title="Charles Radbourn">Charlie Radbourn</a> debuted as a <a href="/wiki/Second_baseman" title="Second baseman">second baseman</a> on May 5</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1880_in_baseball" title="1880 in baseball">1880</a>: <a href="/wiki/Pud_Galvin" title="Pud Galvin">Pud Galvin</a> pitched a <a href="/wiki/No-hitter" title="No-hitter">no-hitter</a> against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Worcester_Ruby_Legs" title="Worcester Ruby Legs">Worcester Ruby Legs</a> on Aug. 20</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1881_in_baseball" title="1881 in baseball">1881</a>: 2B <a href="/wiki/Davy_Force" title="Davy Force">Davy Force</a> recorded 12 <a href="/wiki/Putout" title="Putout">putouts</a>, seven <a href="/wiki/Assist_(baseball)" title="Assist (baseball)">assists</a>, two unassisted <a href="/wiki/Double_play" title="Double play">double plays</a>, participated in a <a href="/wiki/Triple_play" title="Triple play">triple play</a>, and made just one <a href="/wiki/Error_(baseball)" title="Error (baseball)">error</a> in 20 <a href="/wiki/Total_chances" title="Total chances">chances</a> in a 12-inning game against Worcester, on September 15.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1882_in_baseball" title="1882 in baseball">1882</a>: <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>-born <a href="/wiki/Curry_Foley" title="Curry Foley">Curry Foley</a> became the first major league player ever to <a href="/wiki/Hitting_for_the_cycle" title="Hitting for the cycle">hit for the cycle</a> (including a <a href="/wiki/Grand_slam_(baseball)" title="Grand slam (baseball)">grand slam</a>), on May 25, and <a href="/wiki/Dan_Brouthers" title="Dan Brouthers">Dan Brouthers</a> led the National League with a .368 <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">batting average</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1883_in_baseball" title="1883 in baseball">1883</a>: Brouthers won his second consecutive NL batting title with a .374 average and Galvin posted 46 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Win_(baseball)" title="Win (baseball)">wins</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1884_in_baseball" title="1884 in baseball">1884</a>: Brouthers hit <a href="/wiki/Triple_(baseball)" title="Triple (baseball)">triples</a> in four consecutive games, set a season team-record with 14 <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home runs</a>, and Galvin won 46 games for the second year in a row. Galvin threw another no-hitter, on August 4. The Bisons 18-0 score remains the greatest margin of victory in a no-hitter in Major League history. Two years after Foley, Jim O'Rourke became the fourth player in MLB history to hit for the cycle, on June 16.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1885_in_baseball" title="1885 in baseball">1885</a>: Brouthers hit .359, ending second in the NL batting race behind <a href="/wiki/Roger_Connor" title="Roger Connor">Roger Connor</a> (.371)</li>
</ul>
