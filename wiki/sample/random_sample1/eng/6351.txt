<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_with_third_party_firmware">Use with third party firmware</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buffalo_AirStation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Use with third party firmware">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many of Buffalo's residential gateways use <a href="/wiki/Broadcom" title="Broadcom">Broadcom</a> microprocessor <a href="/wiki/Chipset" title="Chipset">chipsets</a>, allowing a variety of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_wireless_router_firmware_projects" title="List of wireless router firmware projects">third party open source firmwares</a> to be installed. Some of their most recent routers with Atheros-based chipsets are shipped by Buffalo with a branded version of <a href="/wiki/DD-WRT" title="DD-WRT">DD-WRT</a> already installed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="CSIRO_controversy">CSIRO controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buffalo_AirStation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: CSIRO controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In late November 2007, Buffalo announced they would temporarily stop supplying wireless LAN products in the USA due to the ongoing lawsuit filed by the 802.11a, 802.11g and 802.11n patent holder <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CSIRO" title="CSIRO">CSIRO</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
