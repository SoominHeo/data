<p><b>Panarthropoda</b> is an animal <a href="/wiki/Taxon" title="Taxon">taxon</a> combining the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phylum_(biology)" title="Phylum (biology)">phyla</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthropoda" title="Arthropoda">Arthropoda</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tardigrada" title="Tardigrada">Tardigrada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Onychophora" title="Onychophora">Onychophora</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid18192181_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid18192181-1">[1]</a></sup> Not all studies support it,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nature_phylo_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nature_phylo-2">[2]</a></sup> but most do, including neuroanatomical,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitogenome" title="Mitogenome">mitogenomic</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and palaeontological<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> studies. Originally, they were considered to be closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Annelid" title="Annelid">annelids</a>, grouped together as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Articulata_Hypothesis" title="Articulata Hypothesis">Articulata</a>, but newer studies place them among the <a href="/wiki/Ecdysozoa" title="Ecdysozoa">Ecdysozoa</a>.</p>
<p>Common characteristics of the Panarthropoda include the presence of legs and <a href="/wiki/Claw" title="Claw">claws</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ventral_nervous_system" title="Ventral nervous system">ventral nervous system</a>, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Segment_(biology)" title="Segment (biology)">segmented body</a>.</p>
