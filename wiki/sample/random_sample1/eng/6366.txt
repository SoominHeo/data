<p>A <b>justice ministry</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Ministry_(government_department)" title="Ministry (government department)">ministry</a> or other <a href="/wiki/Government_agency" title="Government agency">government agency</a> charged with <a href="/wiki/Justice" title="Justice">justice</a>. The ministry is often headed by a <b>minister for justice</b> or <b>secretary of justice</b> or <b>secretary for justice</b>; sometimes the head of a <b>department of justice</b> is entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Attorney_general" title="Attorney general">attorney general</a></i>.</p>
<p>Specific duties may relate to organizing the <a href="/wiki/Criminal_justice" title="Criminal justice">justice system</a>, overseeing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_prosecutor" title="Public prosecutor">public prosecutor</a> and maintaining the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Justice_system" title="Justice system">legal system</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_order" title="Public order">public order</a>. Some ministries have additional responsibilities in related policy areas overseeing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elections" title="Elections">elections</a>, directing the <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">police</a>, law reform. The duties of the ministry of justice may in some countries be split from separate responsibilities of an <a href="/wiki/Attorney_general" title="Attorney general">attorney general</a> (often responsible for the justice system) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interior_minister" title="Interior minister">interior minister</a> (often responsible for public order). Sometimes the prison system is separated into another government department called Corrective Services.</p>
