<p><b>Vegalta Sendai</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja" xml:lang="ja">ベガルタ仙台</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Begaruta Sendai</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> club, currently playing in <a href="/wiki/J1_League" title="J1 League">J1 League</a>. The team is located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sendai,_Miyagi" title="Sendai, Miyagi">Sendai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miyagi_Prefecture" title="Miyagi Prefecture">Miyagi Prefecture</a>. Their home stadium is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sendai_Stadium" title="Sendai Stadium">Yurtec Stadium Sendai</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Izumi-ku,_Sendai" title="Izumi-ku, Sendai">Izumi-ku, Sendai</a>, although a few home games have also been played at nearby <a href="/wiki/Miyagi_Stadium" title="Miyagi Stadium">Miyagi Stadium</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vegalta_Sendai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Founded in 1988 as <i><a href="/wiki/Tohoku_Electric_Power" title="Tohoku Electric Power">Tohoku Electric Power</a> Co., Inc. Soccer Club</i>, Vegalta joined the J-League in 1999 after playing a few years in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Football_League_(former)" title="Japan Football League (former)">JFL</a>, with the nickname <i>Brummell Sendai</i>, to which they had been promoted in 1995 from the Tohoku Regional League. When joining the J-League, the name Vegalta was chosen as a homage to the famous <a href="/wiki/Tanabata" title="Tanabata">Tanabata</a> festival in Sendai. The names of the two celestial stars of the Tanabata legend, <i><a href="/wiki/Vega" title="Vega">Vega</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Altair" title="Altair">Altair</a></i> were <a href="/wiki/Portmanteau" title="Portmanteau">combined</a> to form <i>Vegalta</i>.</p>
<p>They were first promoted to the top flight in 2002, but went back down the following season. They were promoted again for the 2010 season.</p>
<p>In 2011, despite the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">earthquake and tsunami</a>, they achieved their highest position up to that time, 4th place in the top division. In 2012, despite leading the table for most of the season, <span class="sortkey" style="display:none;">Sanfrecce Hiroshima !</span><span class="sorttext"><a href="/wiki/Sanfrecce_Hiroshima" title="Sanfrecce Hiroshima">Sanfrecce Hiroshima</a></span>'s challenge proved too strong, and losing the penultimate week game to relegation battler <span class="sortkey" style="display:none;">Albirex Niigata !</span><span class="sorttext"><a href="/wiki/Albirex_Niigata" title="Albirex Niigata">Albirex Niigata</a></span> cost them the title, rendering them second-place winners, their highest position in history.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporters_and_rivalries">Supporters and rivalries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vegalta_Sendai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Supporters and rivalries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like most football clubs, Sendai's fans are to be heard singing and dancing throughout the match; but, most of the songs used by fans of other clubs are eschewed in favor of a more eclectic set. The club's theme, sung before every game, is <i><a href="/wiki/Take_Me_Home,_Country_Roads" title="Take Me Home, Country Roads">Take Me Home, Country Roads</a></i>, while during the game altered lyrics are sung to the tunes of <i>The Lambrusco Kid</i> by the <a href="/wiki/Toy_Dolls" title="Toy Dolls">Toy Dolls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blitzkrieg_Bop" title="Blitzkrieg Bop">Blitzkrieg Bop</a>, and other songs by <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">KISS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Twisted_Sister" title="Twisted Sister">Twisted Sister</a>.</p>
<p>Vegalta's traditional rivals are <a href="/wiki/Montedio_Yamagata" title="Montedio Yamagata">Montedio Yamagata</a> from <a href="/wiki/Yamagata_Prefecture" title="Yamagata Prefecture">Yamagata Prefecture</a>. The two have been rivals since meeting in the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Regional_Leagues#Tohoku" title="Japanese Regional Leagues">Tohoku Football League</a> in 1991.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Finances">Finances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vegalta_Sendai&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Finances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite leading J2 in attendance for the past three years, the club has struggled financially since being relegated from J1. In 2005, the team ran a deficit of 163 million yen, the only year since joining the J. League that they failed to turn a profit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Up until the closing date of the 2006 season (January 31, 2007), the team was projecting a 150 million yen deficit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> However, the transfer fee for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Humberlito_Borges_Teixeira" title="Humberlito Borges Teixeira">Borges</a> was enough to drive them into the black for the season. The total profit in 2006 was 27 million yen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
