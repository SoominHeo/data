<p>The <b>Russian Theatre</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Русский театр Эстонии</span>, <a href="/wiki/Estonian_language" title="Estonian language">Estonian</a>: <span lang="et" xml:lang="et"><i>Vene teater</i></span>) is a Russian-language theatre in <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a>, Estonia. It is housed in a building that was originally built as a <a href="/wiki/Movie_theater" title="Movie theater">cinema</a> in 1926 and is in <a href="/wiki/Art_Deco" title="Art Deco">Art Deco</a> style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-draamaa_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-draamaa-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teateree_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-teateree-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-visitee_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-visitee-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>A small Russian theatre community has existed in Tallinn since the late 19th century; it grew following the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Revolution" title="Russian Revolution">Russian Revolution</a> when Russian actors settled in Estonia as refugees. A permanent Russian-language theatre was not set up in the city until 1948, however. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estonian_SSR" title="Estonian SSR">Soviet occupation of Estonia</a>, it was one of several Russian-language theatres of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and also toured around the whole country. Since Estonia re-gained its independence, the theatre has focused more on a local audience.</p>
<p>The Russian theatre has put up both Russian-language classical plays by e.g. <a href="/wiki/Anton_Chekhov" title="Anton Chekhov">Anton Chekhov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Griboyedov" title="Alexander Griboyedov">Alexander Griboyedov</a> as well as translations of plays from other languages, including Estonian plays in cooperation with Estonian-language directors.</p>
