<p><b>Vänersborg</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Urban_areas_in_Sweden" title="Urban areas in Sweden">locality</a> and the seat of <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4nersborg_Municipality" title="Vänersborg Municipality">Vänersborg Municipality</a>, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4stra_G%C3%B6taland_County" title="Västra Götaland County">Västra Götaland County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> with 21,699 inhabitants (out of a municipal total of 37,369) Until 1997 it was the capital of <a href="/wiki/%C3%84lvsborg_County" title="Älvsborg County">Älvsborg County</a>, which was dissolved in 1998. Since 1999 Vänersborg has been the seat of the regional parliament of Västra Götaland County. The city is located on the southern shores of lake <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4nern" title="Vänern">Vänern</a>, close to where the river <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6ta_%C3%A4lv" title="Göta älv">Göta älv</a> leaves the lake.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A marketplace was established at <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%A4tte" title="Brätte">Brätte</a> at the southern end of Vassbotten (southernmost part of Vanern), south of modern Vänersborg, by the end of the medieval period. The settlement developed with paved streets lined with houses and farms and it was granted <a href="/wiki/Town_privileges" title="Town privileges">town privileges</a> in 1580. The site, however, became unsuitable for trans-shipment of goods due to silting of its harbour and it was difficult to protect. Consequently, in 1644, the town was moved 3½ kilometres north to Huvudnäset, and the new town of Vänersborg was established, despite the reluctance of its inhabitants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vmb_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-vmb-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The name "Vänersborg" means "fortress at Vänern". It comes from the fortlet which was built in 1644 for the new town's protection. The <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> is also from 1644, depicting a golden ship (a <i><a href="/wiki/Bojort" title="Bojort">bojort</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vn_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-vn-3">[3]</a></sup>) with two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_flag" title="Swedish flag">Swedish flags</a>.</p>
<p>Around 1800, Vänersborg had a population of 1,500 inhabitants. Vänersborg was the point at which freight was transshipped from lake ships to below the <a href="/wiki/Trollh%C3%A4ttan_Falls" title="Trollhättan Falls">Trollhättan Falls</a> (and vice versa) where ships could continue unhindered to <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a> and beyond. In 1778, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=King_Karl_Canal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="King Karl Canal (page does not exist)">King Karl Canal</a> was opened between Vänersborg and Trollhättan, shortening the overland <a href="/wiki/Portage" title="Portage">portage</a> and in 1800, the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trollh%C3%A4tte_Canal" title="Trollhätte Canal">Trollhätte Canal</a> and its locks allowed shipping to move between Vanern and the sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vn_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-vn-3">[3]</a></sup> The waterway between Vänern and Gothenburg enabled the town's importance to grow further.</p>

<p>In 1834, most of the town's wooden houses burnt down. It was rebuilt in a grid pattern, unique to this day.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Torpaskolan" title="Torpaskolan">Torpaskolan</a> (secondary school) was opened in 1965 and replaced in 2011 by the <a href="/wiki/Torpaskolan" title="Torpaskolan">Nya Torpaskolan</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Arena_V%C3%A4nersborg" title="Arena Vänersborg">Arena Vänersborg</a> was opened in 2009.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vänersborg has a cold <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic climate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It also has a relatively wet climate for being in Sweden, with average precipitation of 709.1 millimetres (27.92 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Differences between summers and winters are smaller than in many other inland areas of Sweden, being moderated by its proximity to <a href="/wiki/Kattegat" title="Kattegat">Kattegat</a> and the lake <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4nern" title="Vänern">Vänern</a>. In spite of this, the climate is quite continental considering its classification as marine.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Handicraft, trade, shipping and its former status as the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> of Älvsborgs County has benefited Vänersborg's development. Ever since the 1600s, the city's inhabitants consisted of a dynamic mix of administrators, businessmen, artisans and skilled craftsmen. Today the municipality is characterized by more high-tech engineering companies, <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT</a> and electronics, education and skilled service. <a href="/wiki/Public_administration" title="Public administration">Public administration</a> still plays an important role.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vänersborg has several schools, including <a href="/wiki/Torpaskolan" title="Torpaskolan">Torpaskolan</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=T%C3%A4rnanskolan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tärnanskolan (page does not exist)">Tärnanskolan</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Birger_Sj%C3%B6berg_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Birger Sjöberg High School (page does not exist)">Birger Sjöberg High School</a>, as well as the independent school <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fridaskolan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fridaskolan (page does not exist)">Fridaskolan</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bandy" title="Bandy">bandy</a> club <a href="/wiki/IFK_V%C3%A4nersborg" title="IFK Vänersborg">IFK Vänersborg</a> plays in the highest division <a href="/wiki/Elitserien_(bandy)" title="Elitserien (bandy)">Elitserien</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bl%C3%A5suts_BK" title="Blåsuts BK">Blåsuts BK</a> is usually playing in the second-tier <a href="/wiki/Allsvenskan_(bandy)" title="Allsvenskan (bandy)">Allsvenskan</a>. For the 2016-17 season IFK Vänersborg has acquired three Russian world champions, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pavel_Bulatov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pavel Bulatov (page does not exist)">Pavel Bulatov</a>,<a class="external autonumber" href="https://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&amp;sl=sv&amp;tl=en&amp;u=http%3A%2F%2Fifkvanersborg.se%2Fvarldsspelare-klar-for-ifk%2F&amp;sandbox=1" rel="nofollow">[1]</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nikita_Ivanov_(bandy)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nikita Ivanov (bandy) (page does not exist)">Nikita Ivanov</a>,<a class="external autonumber" href="https://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&amp;sl=sv&amp;tl=en&amp;u=http%3A%2F%2Fifkvanersborg.se%2Fytterligare-en-varldsmastare-till-ifk%2F" rel="nofollow">[2]</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sergey_Lomanov,_Jr." title="Sergey Lomanov, Jr.">Sergey Lomanov, Jr.</a>.<a class="external autonumber" href="https://translate.google.com/translate?hl=en&amp;sl=sv&amp;tl=en&amp;u=http%3A%2F%2Fifkvanersborg.se%2Fsergej-lomanov-jr-klar-for-ifk-vanersborg%2F" rel="nofollow">[3]</a></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Arena_V%C3%A4nersborg" title="Arena Vänersborg">Arena Vänersborg</a> is one of the most modern indoor arenas for bandy. <a href="/wiki/Arena_V%C3%A4nersborg" title="Arena Vänersborg">Arena Vänersborg</a> caused a great commotion, due to it exceeding its budget, landing the final cost of the arena at a 300 000 000 SEK, and counting.</p>
<p>Vänersborg hosted the <a href="/wiki/2013_Bandy_World_Championship" title="2013 Bandy World Championship">2013 Bandy World Championship</a> for men's teams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> For the second time Group A was a separate event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Districts">Districts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Districts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vänersborg is made out of several small and large districts: <a href="/wiki/Onsj%C3%B6,_V%C3%A4nersborg" title="Onsjö, Vänersborg">Onsjö</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mariedal,_V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mariedal, Vänersborg (page does not exist)">Mariedal</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bl%C3%A5sut,_V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Blåsut, Vänersborg (page does not exist)">Blåsut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Varg%C3%B6n" title="Vargön">Vargön</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lilleskog&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lilleskog (page does not exist)">Lilleskog</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Korseberg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Korseberg (page does not exist)">Korseberg</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Torpa,_V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Torpa, Vänersborg (page does not exist)">Torpa</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nordstan,_V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nordstan, Vänersborg (page does not exist)">Nordstan</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Restad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Restad (page does not exist)">Restad</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%96xnered" title="Öxnered">Öxnered</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%A4nersborg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 22 November 2010, Vänersborg's <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">Mayor</a>, Lars-Göran Ljunggren (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_Democrat" title="Social Democrat">Social Democrat</a>), resigned following two scandals of <a href="/wiki/Arena_V%C3%A4nersborg" title="Arena Vänersborg">Arena Vänersborg</a>, whose costs greatly exceeded the original budget, and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Toppfrys&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Toppfrys (page does not exist)">Toppfrys</a> deal, which was investigated by the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Commission&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Economic Commission (page does not exist)">Economic Commission</a>. Ljunggren had spent 30 years on Vänersborg City Council, and three and a half years as mayor. He was succeeded in 2011 by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conservative" title="Conservative">Conservative</a> politician Gunnar Lidell. Mayor since the election 2014 is Marie Dahlin, Social Democrat.</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Poet" title="Poet">poet</a> <a href="/wiki/Birger_Sj%C3%B6berg" title="Birger Sjöberg">Birger Sjöberg</a> (1885–1929) was born in Vänersborg. He wrote appreciatively about Vänersborg, most notably in <i>Fridas bok</i> (1922), wherein the comparison "Vänersborg – little Paris" is found. The park in the northern part of Vänersborg, Skräckleparken, offers a picturesque view over lake Vänern, and therein also stands this statue of mentioned Ragnar.</p>
<p>The prominent Swedish explorer and trader, <a href="/wiki/Axel_Wilhelm_Eriksson" title="Axel Wilhelm Eriksson">Axel Eriksson</a> was also born in Vänersborg. The Municipal Museum displays a collection of birds from south-western Africa that were collected by Eriksson.</p>
<p>Swedish singer <a href="/wiki/Agnes_Carlsson" title="Agnes Carlsson">Agnes Carlsson</a> was born in Vänersborg.</p>
