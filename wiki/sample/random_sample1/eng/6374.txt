<p><b>Benevento Calcio</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Italy" title="Football in Italy">Italian association football club</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Benevento" title="Benevento">Benevento</a>, Campania. The club was founded in 1929 and refounded in 2005. Currently it plays in <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benevento_Calcio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded as <b>Associazione Calcio Benevento</b> in 1929,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> their original home was the <i>Meomartini</i> which was built by Ciccio Minocchia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After working their way up lower divisions in their earliest years, Benevento reached <a href="/wiki/Prima_Divisione" title="Prima Divisione">Prima Divisione</a>, the third highest professional league at that time, in the 1934–35 season. Here it finished above clubs such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reggina_Calcio" title="Reggina Calcio">Reggina</a> in its first season within the league. Although it did not win promotion to <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a>, the team did qualify to remain in the third tier of Italian football for the 1935–36 season, re-organized to a smaller 64-team league renamed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serie_C" title="Serie C">Serie C</a>.</p>
<p>The company <b>F.C. Sporting Benevento S.r.l.</b> folded in 2005. At the same time <b>Benevento Calcio S.p.A.</b> was found.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Serie_C2" title="2007–08 Serie C2">2007–08 Serie C2</a> regular season the team finished first in Girone C, winning direct promotion to the now called <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Prima_Divisione" title="Lega Pro Prima Divisione">Lega Pro Prima Divisione</a> for the 2008–09 season. In the 2008–09 season, Benevento's first season in Lega Pro Prima Divisione, they finished in 2nd place. This meant Benevento would be in a 2 leg play-off. They won their first 2 legged play-off, but lost to <a href="/wiki/F.C._Crotone" title="F.C. Crotone">Crotone</a> 1–0 (2–1) on aggregate in the final.</p>
<p>Gaetano Auteri was appointed as the head manager for the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Lega_Pro" title="2015–16 Lega Pro">2015–16 season</a>. In this season, Benevento won its league and reached the <a href="/wiki/Serie_B" title="Serie B">Serie B</a> for the first time in its history. The mathematical certainty arrived on April 30, 2016, after defeating <a href="/wiki/U.S._Lecce" title="U.S. Lecce">Lecce</a> 3–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
