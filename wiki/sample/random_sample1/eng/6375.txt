<p><b>Benesse Corporation</b> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ベネッセコーポレーション</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Benesse Kōporēshon</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> company which focuses on correspondence education and publishing. Based in <a href="/wiki/Okayama" title="Okayama">Okayama</a>-City, it is the parent company of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berlitz_Language_Schools" title="Berlitz Language Schools">Berlitz Language Schools</a>, which in turn is the parent company of <a href="/wiki/ELS_Language_Centers" title="ELS Language Centers">ELS Language Centers</a>. Benesse is listed on the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">Tokyo Stock Exchange</a> (listing code 9783).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_the_company_name">Origin of the company name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benesse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin of the company name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company name is derived from the Latin words "bene" (well) and "esse" (being).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_history">Company history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benesse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Company history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company was founded in 1955 as Fukutake Publishing Co., Ltd. <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">株式会社福武書店</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kabushiki-gaisha Fukutake Shoten</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>. In 1994, it completed the construction of the Fukutake Shoten Tokyo Building (now Benesse Corporation Tokyo Building) in <a href="/wiki/Tama,_Tokyo" title="Tama, Tokyo">Tama</a>-City, Tokyo. In April 1995, the company was renamed Benesse Corporation.</p>
<p>A major breakthrough in the company's history was the acquisition of a majority stake in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berlitz_Language_Schools" title="Berlitz Language Schools">Berlitz Language Schools</a>, which had gone public in 1989. In 2001, Benesse completed the take-over by acquiring 100% ownership of Berlitz and making it a private company once again.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Benesse_Foundation">Benesse Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benesse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Benesse Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Benesse House is a 10-room hotel located inside a contemporary art museum on the island of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naoshima" title="Naoshima">Naoshima</a> in the Seto Inland Sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimesJapaIsland_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimesJapaIsland-1">[1]</a></sup> Built in 1992, it is the only hotel on the island and was designed in partnership with architect <a href="/wiki/Tadao_Ando" title="Tadao Ando">Tadao Ando</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimesJapaIsland_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimesJapaIsland-1">[1]</a></sup> It was built by the Benesse Foundation which is funded by the Benesse Corporation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimesJapaIsland_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimesJapaIsland-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
