<p>The <b>Venice People Mover</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <span lang="it" xml:lang="it"><i>People Mover di Venezia</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Venetian_language" title="Venetian language">Venetian</a>: <span lang="vec" xml:lang="vec"><i>People Mover de Venesia</i></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/People_mover" title="People mover">people mover</a>-type <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_transit" title="Public transit">public transit</a> system in <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a>, Italy opened in 2010. It connects <a href="/wiki/Piazzale_Roma" title="Piazzale Roma">Piazzale Roma</a> with the city's <a href="/wiki/Tronchetto" title="Tronchetto">Tronchetto island</a> car parking facility, via a stop above the <a href="/wiki/Marittima" title="Marittima">Marittima</a> cruise terminal.</p>
<p>The transport system started operating on April 19, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The two vehicles of the system are pulled by a <a href="/wiki/Wire_rope" title="Wire rope">cable</a> similar to a <a href="/wiki/Funicular" title="Funicular">funicular</a>. The whole 853-metre-long (0.5Â mi) journey takes three minutes.</p>
