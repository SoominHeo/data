<p><b>Benny Peter Agbayani, Jr.</b> AKA The Hawaiian Hammer (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/æ/ short 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/aɪ/ long 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɑː/ 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'n' in 'no'">n</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>; born December 28, 1971) is a retired professional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baseball_player" title="Baseball player">baseball player</a>. Born in Hawaii to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filipino_people" title="Filipino people">Filipino</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samoan_people" title="Samoan people">Samoan</a> parents,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> he attended <a href="/wiki/Saint_Louis_School" title="Saint Louis School">Saint Louis School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Pacific_University" title="Hawaii Pacific University">Hawaii Pacific University</a>. He also attended the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Institute_of_Technology" title="Oregon Institute of Technology">Oregon Institute of Technology</a>. He played in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) for the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a> (NPB) for the <a href="/wiki/Chiba_Lotte_Marines" title="Chiba Lotte Marines">Chiba Lotte Marines</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor_leagues">Minor leagues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Agbayani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Minor leagues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was originally drafted by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/California_Angels" title="California Angels">California Angels</a> but did not sign. Agbayani was later <a href="/wiki/1993_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="1993 Major League Baseball draft">drafted</a> in the 30th round by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> on June 3, 1993. He became a local star at the Triple-A minor league team, the <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Tides" title="Norfolk Tides">Norfolk Tides</a>. He has since been part of the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Cincinnati Reds</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a> organizations. He last played for the <a href="/wiki/Chiba_Lotte_Marines" title="Chiba Lotte Marines">Chiba Lotte Marines</a> in the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Pacific_League" title="Pacific League">Pacific League</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_York_Mets">New York Mets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Agbayani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: New York Mets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agbayani made his major league debut with the New York Mets on June 17, 1998, as part of an 8th-inning double-switch in which he defensively replaced <a href="/wiki/Center_fielder" title="Center fielder">center fielder</a> <a href="/wiki/Butch_Huskey" title="Butch Huskey">Butch Huskey</a> during a 5-4 loss to the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a>. He made his first start and picked up his first big league <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hit</a> on June 19. Agbayani led off the bottom of the first inning by singling to second base off <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Florida_Marlins" title="Florida Marlins">Florida Marlins</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Brian_Meadows" title="Brian Meadows">Brian Meadows</a>.</p>
<p>His 11 home runs prior to the All Star break in 1999 tie him with <a href="/wiki/Ike_Davis" title="Ike Davis">Ike Davis</a> (2010) for the second-most by a Mets rookie, behind <a href="/wiki/Ron_Swoboda" title="Ron Swoboda">Ron Swoboda</a> (15, in 1965).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 12, 2000 while a member of the Mets, Agbayani was involved in a particularly memorable play. In the fourth inning, with the Mets leading 1-0, the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">Giants</a> loaded the bases after a double, an error, and a hit batsman. With one out, Giants catcher <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Estalella_(catcher)" title="Bobby Estalella (catcher)">Bobby Estalella</a> hit a fly pop to Agbayani in left field. Agbayani, thinking that the catch made three outs, gave the ball to a child in the stands and began to trot toward the dugout. It took him a moment to realize his mistake, but once he did, he sprinted back to the stands, pulled the ball from the hands of the young fan he had given it to, and fired a throw toward home plate. Unfortunately for Agbayani, once the ball left the field, the play was dead, and all three runners were awarded two bases—causing Jeff Kent and Ellis Burks to score, and the Giants to take the lead, 2-1. The Mets went on to win the game, 3-2, and Agbayani gave another ball to the fan who had given the previous one back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Agbayani is also fondly remembered by Mets fans for two clutch <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home runs</a> hit during the 2000 season, earning him the nickname "Hawaiian Punch" (after the popular <a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_Punch" title="Hawaiian Punch">fruit drink</a>). On March 30, his 11th inning <a href="/wiki/Grand_slam_(baseball)" title="Grand slam (baseball)">grand slam</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> gave the Mets their first win of the season, and a split in the two-game series the Mets and Cubs played in Tokyo, Japan. (It remains the only regular-season MLB grand slam ever hit in Japan.) Later that year, on October 7, he hit a game-winning home run in the 13th inning of Game 3 of the <a href="/wiki/National_League_Division_Series" title="National League Division Series">National League Division Series</a> against <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Fultz" title="Aaron Fultz">Aaron Fultz</a> of the Giants. Agbayani also drove in the winning run in the only game the Mets won in the 2000 World Series.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Colorado_Rockies">Colorado Rockies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Agbayani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Colorado Rockies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 21, 2002, Agbayani was part of a 10-player, three-team trade between the Mets, Rockies and <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a>, that sent him from New York to Colorado. He struggled in 48 games with the Rockies, hitting .205 with four home runs and 19 RBI before he was placed on waivers in late August.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boston_Red_Sox">Boston Red Sox</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Agbayani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Boston Red Sox">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a> selected Agbayani off waivers from the Rockies on August 26, 2002, as the club made a drive for the playoffs. He played relatively well down the stretch, hitting .297 and driving in eight runs in 37 at-bats over 13 games with Boston. In his final MLB game, September 29, Agbayani went 1-for-4 with a walk and a strikeout as the Red Sox defeated the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Devil_Rays" title="Tampa Bay Devil Rays">Tampa Bay Devil Rays</a> at <a href="/wiki/Fenway_Park" title="Fenway Park">Fenway Park</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nippon_Professional_Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Agbayani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nippon Professional Baseball">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On October 26, 2005, Agbayani and the <a href="/wiki/Chiba_Lotte_Marines" title="Chiba Lotte Marines">Chiba Lotte Marines</a> swept the <a href="/wiki/Hanshin_Tigers" title="Hanshin Tigers">Hanshin Tigers</a> in 4 games during the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Series" title="Japan Series">Japan Series</a>. This was the Marines' first title in 31 years. On November 13, 2005, they won the inaugural <a href="/wiki/2005_Asia_Series" title="2005 Asia Series">Asia Series</a> after defeating the champions of <a href="/wiki/Korea_Baseball_Organization" title="Korea Baseball Organization">South Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/China_Baseball_League" title="China Baseball League">China</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CPBL" title="CPBL">Taiwan</a>. They defeated the <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Lions" title="Samsung Lions">Samsung Lions</a> in the championship and Agbayani was named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MVP" title="MVP">MVP</a> of the series. Agbayani's manager with Chiba Lotte was his manager with the Mets, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Valentine" title="Bobby Valentine">Bobby Valentine</a>. He retired following the 2009 season, his sixth in Japan. After baseball he was hired as an educational assistant in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oahu,_Hawaii" title="Oahu, Hawaii">Oahu, Hawaii</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_and_achievements">Career and achievements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benny_Agbayani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Career and achievements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Agbayani is one of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Filipino_American_sportspeople#Baseball" title="List of Filipino American sportspeople">ten known players</a> in Major League Baseball history with a <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Filipino</a> background, either by birth, citizenship, or heritage.</li>
<li>Appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Howard_Stern" title="Howard Stern">Howard Stern</a> radio show around the time the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a> faced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Yankees" title="The Yankees">the Yankees</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2000_World_Series" title="2000 World Series">2000 World Series</a>. He predicted the Mets would win the series in 5 games, which they did not. The Yankees, however, did win in five games.</li>
<li>He is a 1999 inductee of Hawaii's athletic Hall of Honor. Despite having only limited and occasional success in the Major Leagues, Agbayani was a popular figure with fans, particular in New York, where his successes were often met with "Benny, Benny!" chants as well as "Benny and the Mets", a parody of <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benny_and_the_Jets" title="Benny and the Jets">Benny and the Jets</a>.</li>
</ul>
