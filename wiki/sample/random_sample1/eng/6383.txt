<p><b>Beni Suef</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egyptian_Arabic_language" title="Egyptian Arabic language">Egyptian Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="arz" xml:lang="arz">محافظة بنى سويف</span>‎‎ <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Egyptian Spoken Arabic transliteration">Muḥāfẓet Beni Suef</span></i>) is one of the <a href="/wiki/Governorates_of_Egypt" title="Governorates of Egypt">governorates</a> of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>. It is situated in the center of the country.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beni_Suef_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This governorate's capital is the city of <a href="/wiki/Beni_Suef" title="Beni Suef">Beni Suef</a>, located about 120 km south of <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a> on the west bank of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nile_River" title="Nile River">Nile River</a>. The area is well known in Egypt for its cement factories. The nearby <a href="/wiki/Meidum" title="Meidum">Meidum</a> pyramid is the only prominent tourist attraction in the area.</p>
<p>Due to different ways of transcribing the spelling it can also be known as Beni Suef, or Beni Swaif.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beni_Suef_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to population estimates from 2015 the majority of residents in the governorate live in rural areas, with an urbanization rate of only 23.2%. Out of an estimated 2,856,812 people residing in the governorate, 2,193,871 people live in rural areas as opposed to only 662,941 in urban areas. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pop_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-pop-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_zones">Industrial zones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beni_Suef_Governorate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Industrial zones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the Egyptian Governing Authority for Investment and Free Zones (GAFI), in affiliation with the Ministry of Investment (MOI), the following industrial zones are located in this governorate:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndustrialZones_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-IndustrialZones-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Kom Abu Radi</li>
<li>Baiad Al Arab</li>
<li>The industrial zone 1/31</li>
<li>The industrial zone 2/31</li>
<li>The industrial zone 3/31</li>
<li>The industrial zone 4/31</li>
<li>Heavy industrial zone Gabal Ghareb</li>
<li>(New urban community industrial zone) New Beni Suef</li>
</ul>
