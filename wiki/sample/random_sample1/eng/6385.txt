<p><b>Veniamin Igorevich Tayanovich</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Вениамин Игоревич Таянович</span>; born April 6, 1967 in <a href="/wiki/Ufa" title="Ufa">Ufa</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> <a href="/wiki/Freestyle_swimming" title="Freestyle swimming">freestyle</a> <a href="/wiki/Swimming_(sport)" title="Swimming (sport)">swimmer</a>.</p>
<p>Tayanovich is best known for winning the gold medal in the Men's 4 × 200 m Freestyle event at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> at <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Pyshnenko" title="Vladimir Pyshnenko">Vladimir Pyshnenko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Lepikov" title="Dmitry Lepikov">Dmitry Lepikov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aleksey_Kudryavtsev" title="Aleksey Kudryavtsev">Aleksey Kudryavtsev</a> (heats), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yury_Valeryevich_Mukhin" title="Yury Valeryevich Mukhin">Yury Mukhin</a> (heats) and <a href="/wiki/Yevgeny_Sadovyi" title="Yevgeny Sadovyi">Yevgeny Sadovyi</a>. At the same Olympic Games he also claimed the silver medal with the 4 × 100 m freestyle team, swimming in the qualifying heats.</p>
