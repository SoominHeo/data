<p><b>Vedad Ibišević</b> (born 6 August 1984) is a Bosnian professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> for German club <a href="/wiki/Hertha_BSC" title="Hertha BSC">Hertha BSC</a> as captain and for the <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina national team</a>.</p>
<p>He has also played in Switzerland, the United States and France. In 2008, he was awarded the <i><a href="/wiki/Idol_Nacije" title="Idol Nacije">Idol Nacije</a></i> award for Bosnian Footballer of the Year.</p>
<p>A full international since 2007, Ibišević has earned over 70 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> for Bosnia and Herzegovina. He was selected for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, where he scored Bosnia's first goal in a major tournament.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vedad_Ibi%C5%A1evi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ibišević was born in <a href="/wiki/Vlasenica" title="Vlasenica">Vlasenica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Republic_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Socialist Republic of Bosnia and Herzegovina">SR Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>, then a republic within <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">SFR Yugoslavia</a>. He and his family left Bosnia and Herzegovina in 2000 to move to <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, where Ibišević was signed by <a href="/wiki/FC_Baden" title="FC Baden">FC Baden</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canton_Aargau" title="Canton Aargau">Canton Aargau</a>. His family, however, left Switzerland after only ten months, moving to <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, in the United States. In St. Louis, Ibišević flourished as one of the region's most promising football players, and after his senior season in 2002, was named by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soccer_America_Magazine" title="Soccer America Magazine">Soccer America</a></i> as one of the nation's top 25 recruits. He played his high school career at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roosevelt_High_School_(St._Louis,_Missouri)" title="Roosevelt High School (St. Louis, Missouri)">Roosevelt High School</a> in St. Louis.</p>
<p>Ibišević signed to play <a href="/wiki/College_soccer" title="College soccer">college soccer</a> in his adopted hometown at one of the nation's most respected football establishments, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Louis_University" title="Saint Louis University">Saint Louis University</a>. He quickly established himself in his <a href="/wiki/Freshman" title="Freshman">freshman</a> year, registering 18 goals and four assists in 22 games for the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Louis_Billikens_men%27s_soccer" title="Saint Louis Billikens men's soccer">Billikens</a>, while leading a strong SLU team deep into the NCAA Tournament. For his achievements, Ibišević was named the NCAA Freshman of the Year, as well as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Division_I_First-Team_All-American_(soccer)" title="Division I First-Team All-American (soccer)">first team All-American</a>. During his college years, he also played in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USL_Premier_Development_League" title="USL Premier Development League">USL Premier Development League</a> with both the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Strikers" title="St. Louis Strikers">St. Louis Strikers</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago_Fire_Premier" title="Chicago Fire Premier">Chicago Fire Premier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>During training with the team, Ibišević was spotted by <a href="/wiki/Paris_Saint-Germain_F.C." title="Paris Saint-Germain F.C.">Paris Saint-Germain</a>'s Bosnian manager <a href="/wiki/Vahid_Halilhod%C5%BEi%C4%87" title="Vahid Halilhodžić">Vahid Halilhodžić</a>, who quickly signed him to play for the renowned French team for the coming season. Ibišević, however, initially saw little action, and was loaned to French second division club <a href="/wiki/Dijon_FCO" title="Dijon FCO">Dijon</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1899_Hoffenheim">1899 Hoffenheim</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vedad_Ibi%C5%A1evi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1899 Hoffenheim">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>In May 2006, Ibišević signed a three-year deal with <a href="/wiki/Alemannia_Aachen" title="Alemannia Aachen">Alemannia Aachen</a>, and on 12 July 2007, he moved to another German club, <a href="/wiki/TSG_1899_Hoffenheim" title="TSG 1899 Hoffenheim">1899 Hoffeneheim</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ibišević kicked off the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Bundesliga" title="2008–09 Bundesliga">2008–09 Bundesliga</a> season in good fashion, scoring two goals in Hoffenheim's first ever appearance in the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> against <a href="/wiki/FC_Energie_Cottbus" title="FC Energie Cottbus">Energie Cottbus</a> with the game ending 3–0 in Hoffenheim's favour. He scored another goal on his second game against <a href="/wiki/Borussia_M%C3%B6nchengladbach" title="Borussia Mönchengladbach">Borussia Mönchengladbach</a>. In his third Bundesliga match for the club, he scored his fourth goal of the season against <a href="/wiki/Bayer_04_Leverkusen" title="Bayer 04 Leverkusen">Bayer Leverkusen</a>, although his side lost 5–2. He scored another two goals in Hoffenheim's 4–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Borussia_Dortmund" title="Borussia Dortmund">Borussia Dortmund</a>. During the first half of Hoffenheim's debut season in the top flight, Ibišević recorded 18 goals and seven assists in 17 games, making him the league's top scorer before he was injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was voted <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Bundesliga#Player_of_the_Month" title="2008–09 Bundesliga">Bundesliga Player of the Month</a> for October 2008.</p>
<p>On 14 January 2009, during the Bundesliga's winter break, Ibišević was injured in a training match against <a href="/wiki/Hamburger_SV" title="Hamburger SV">Hamburger SV</a> in Spain. The final examination confirmed an <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament_injury" title="Anterior cruciate ligament injury">anterior right cruciate ligament rupture</a>, which took him out of action for the rest of the 2008–09 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He returned at the beginning of the 2009–10 season. He was scoreless until Hoffenheim's seventh Bundesliga match, when he scored a hat-trick against <a href="/wiki/Hertha_BSC" title="Hertha BSC">Hertha BSC</a> in a remarkable comeback. This became the second-fastest hat-trick in Bundesliga history and the fastest goal scored this season, scored 47 seconds into the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After the season, he signed a contract extension that would have kept him at Hoffenheim until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_TSG_1899_Hoffenheim_season" title="2012–13 TSG 1899 Hoffenheim season">2012–13 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="VfB_Stuttgart">VfB Stuttgart</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vedad_Ibi%C5%A1evi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: VfB Stuttgart">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 January 2012, Ibišević moved to <a href="/wiki/VfB_Stuttgart" title="VfB Stuttgart">VfB Stuttgart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for VfB on 11 February 2012 with the opening goal in a 5–0 home victory against Hertha BSC. He also provided an assist in that match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> He scored two more goals in a 2–1 derby victory over former club Hoffenheim on 16 March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> His good form continued as he netted a brace in his side's emphatic 4–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/1._FSV_Mainz_05" title="1. FSV Mainz 05">Mainz 05</a>, bringing Stuttgart ever closer to securing European football for next season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ibišević's first goal of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Bundesliga" title="2012–13 Bundesliga">2012–13 Bundesliga</a> season came on 29 September 2012, scoring within the opening minute as Stuttgart defeated <a href="/wiki/1._FC_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="1. FC Nürnberg">1. FC Nürnberg</a> 2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 8 December, Ibišević scored his first hat-trick for Stuttgart, netting all three goals as the home side moved into fifth spot in the league with a 3–1 defeat of <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 September 2013, Ibišević netted a hat-trick against former club Hoffenheim, powering his side to a 6–2 <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Bundesliga" title="2013–14 Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> victory at the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_Arena_(Stuttgart)" title="Mercedes-Benz Arena (Stuttgart)">Mercedes-Benz Arena</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He was given a five-match ban on 9 February for appearing to strike <a href="/wiki/FC_Augsburg" title="FC Augsburg">FC Augsburg</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jan-Ingwer_Callsen-Bracker" title="Jan-Ingwer Callsen-Bracker">Jan-Ingwer Callsen-Bracker</a> in a home defeat to Augsburg. The <a href="/wiki/German_Football_Association" title="German Football Association">German Football Association</a>'s decision was reached as Ibišević was labelled as a "repeat offender", having been sent off for a similar offence at the start of the previous season. The suspension would cause him to miss a game against his former team, Hoffenheim, and bottom team <a href="/wiki/Eintracht_Braunschweig" title="Eintracht Braunschweig">Eintracht Braunschweig</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Ibišević apologised for his actions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 August 2014, Ibišević extended his contract with VfB Stuttgart until June 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hertha_BSC">Hertha BSC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vedad_Ibi%C5%A1evi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hertha BSC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ibišević moved to Hertha BSC on 30 August 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On 22 September 2015, he scored twice in a 2–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/1._FC_K%C3%B6ln" title="1. FC Köln">1. FC Köln</a>, ending a 25-game goal drought.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On 3 October 2015, Ibišević scored two goals in three minutes in a 3–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Hamburger_SV" title="Hamburger SV">Hamburger SV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> On 17 October, Ibišević received a straight red card in the 18th minute against Schalke 04 for a challenge on <a href="/wiki/Max_Meyer_(footballer)" title="Max Meyer (footballer)">Max Meyer</a> in an eventual 2–1 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Ibišević scored his third brace of the season against <a href="/wiki/SV_Darmstadt_98" title="SV Darmstadt 98">Darmstadt 98</a> on 12 December to take his tally up to six for the season, with all his goals coming in pairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 August 2016, Ibišević was named Hertha's club captain by head coach <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A1l_D%C3%A1rdai" title="Pál Dárdai">Pál Dárdai</a>, replacing the captain of three years, <a href="/wiki/Fabian_Lustenberger" title="Fabian Lustenberger">Fabian Lustenberger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vedad_Ibi%C5%A1evi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On 24 March 2007, Ibišević debuted – and started – for <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_national_football_team" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina national football team">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> in a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a> <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2008 qualifying">qualifying match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> On 13 October 2007, he was called up for the Euro 2008 qualifier against <a href="/wiki/Greece_national_football_team" title="Greece national football team">Greece</a> in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, where he scored his first international goal, Bosnia's second in a 3–2 loss.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 September 2012, Ibišević scored his first hat-trick for the national team away to <a href="/wiki/Liechtenstein_national_football_team" title="Liechtenstein national football team">Liechtenstein national football team</a> during <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification">qualifiers</a> for <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>. He has also set up <a href="/wiki/Edin_D%C5%BEeko" title="Edin Džeko">Edin Džeko</a> for a goal during the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> He opened the scoring for Bosnia in a 3–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_national_football_team" title="Lithuania national football team">Lithuania</a> in a World Cup qualifier on 16 October 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> After scoring the only goal of the match against the same opposition almost a year later, Ibišević ensured Bosnia would make its first ever appearance at a <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> On 15 June 2014, he scored the first ever Bosnian goal at the World Cup finals in a 2–1 loss against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maracan%C3%A3_(stadium)" title="Maracanã (stadium)">Maracanã</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Bosnia's penultimate <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying">Euro 2016 qualifier</a>, in the absence of Edin Džeko, Ibišević started against <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales</a> and scored a crucial goal in the 90th minute to keep Bosnia's qualification hopes alive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vedad_Ibi%C5%A1evi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ibišević is from a <a href="/wiki/Bosniaks" title="Bosniaks">Bosniak</a> family. At an early age, his family moved to Switzerland, and ten months later to St. Louis, Missouri, where he grew up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> While he has spoken some about his experiences during the <a href="/wiki/Bosnian_War" title="Bosnian War">Bosnian War</a> that forced his family to leave Bosnia, he is not known to have ever revealed all of the details. According to a profile on Ibišević by American writer <a href="/wiki/Wright_Thompson" title="Wright Thompson">Wright Thompson</a> in 2014,</p>
<blockquote>
<p>No one in Germany knows the whole story about his escape from the war. During his three years in St. Louis, he never told a single person at school, not a friend, teacher or coach. The most common answers he gives to any question about the war is "It's OK" or "We were lucky."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thompson_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Thompson-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Even his wife Zerina, who lost her father in the war, believes her husband has not told her the whole story; in the Thompson piece, she said, "I still have a feeling that I know maybe 20 percent. I swear."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thompson_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-Thompson-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the time of the Thompson story, Ibišević and his wife lived near <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a> with their young son Ismail. Thompson noted that when Ibišević looked for a house when he transferred to Stuttgart, he "found himself drawn" to one that reminded him of the family home in Bosnia that they were forced to abandon during the war. He also bought a home for his father Šaban in Tuzla that had previously been owned by his father's employer, which according to Thompson was "a symbol of what the war had taken". The home is more a spiritual balm for Šaban, since he continues to live in St. Louis, where he manages apartment buildings that his son owns. Ibišević also rebuilt his father's boyhood home in their former village of Gerovi, and has built several war memorials in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Thompson_33-2"><a href="#cite_note-Thompson-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ibišević is a <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Islam in Bosnia and Herzegovina">Sunni Muslim</a>. During the 2014 World Cup, he, along with Bosnia teammates <a href="/wiki/Muhamed_Be%C5%A1i%C4%87" title="Muhamed Bešić">Muhamed Bešić</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edin_Vi%C5%A1%C4%87a" title="Edin Višća">Edin Višća</a>, visited a <a href="/wiki/Mosque" title="Mosque">mosque</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cuiab%C3%A1" title="Cuiabá">Cuiabá</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
