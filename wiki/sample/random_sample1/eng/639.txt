<p>Year <b>500</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">D</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Leap year starting on Saturday">leap year starting on Saturday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <i>Year of the Consulship of Patricius and Hypatius</i> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1253 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 500 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years. The year 500 is considered the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Byzantine Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Anastasius_I_Dicorus" title="Anastasius I Dicorus">Anastasius I</a> concludes treaties with a number of <a href="/wiki/Nomad" title="Nomad">nomad tribes</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a>. In return for <a href="/wiki/Tribute" title="Tribute">tribute</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> and military defense of these eastern territories, such tribes are permitted to settle and farm <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agricultural</a> lands in Arabia (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Britannia">Britannia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Britannia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Possible date for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mons_Badonicus" title="Battle of Mons Badonicus">Battle of Mons Badonicus</a>: <a href="/wiki/Romano-British_culture" title="Romano-British culture">Romano-British</a> and <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celts</a> defeat an <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxons" title="Anglo-Saxons">Anglo-Saxon</a> army, that may have been led by the <a href="/wiki/Bretwalda" title="Bretwalda">bretwalda</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aelle_of_Sussex" title="Aelle of Sussex">Aelle of Sussex</a> or possibly <a href="/wiki/Cerdic_of_Wessex" title="Cerdic of Wessex">Cerdic of Wessex</a> (approximate date; suggested dates range from 490 to 517). This battle may have influenced the legend of <a href="/wiki/King_Arthur" title="King Arthur">King Arthur</a>.</li>
<li>Possible date at which <a href="/wiki/Fergus_M%C3%B3r" title="Fergus Mór">Fergus Mór</a> begins his reign – the historicity of Mór is doubtful.</li>
<li>Approximate beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Heptarchy" title="Heptarchy">Heptarchy</a> period in the <a href="/wiki/History_of_England" title="History of England">history of England</a>.</li>
<li>Approximate year of the founding of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Essex" title="Kingdom of Essex">Kingdom of Essex</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Battle of <a href="/wiki/Dijon" title="Dijon">Dijon</a>: A coalition of <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Franks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Burgundians" title="Burgundians">Burgundians</a> crush the forces under <a href="/wiki/Gundobad" title="Gundobad">Gundobad</a>. King <a href="/wiki/Clovis_I" title="Clovis I">Clovis I</a> pursues him to <a href="/wiki/Avignon" title="Avignon">Avignon</a>, where he surrenders and promises to pay a yearly tribute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Frankish Kingdom</a> is formed (approximate date).</li>
<li>The monument of <a href="/wiki/Ale%27s_Stones" title="Ale's Stones">Ale's Stones</a> is built in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li>Roman <a href="/wiki/Catacombs" title="Catacombs">catacomb</a> burials end (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thrasamund" title="Thrasamund">Thrasamund</a>, king of the <a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a>, marries <a href="/wiki/Amalafrida" title="Amalafrida">Amalafrida</a> (widowed sister of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theodoric_the_Great" title="Theodoric the Great">Theodoric the Great</a>). She brings with her a large <a href="/wiki/Dowry" title="Dowry">dowry</a> and an elite <a href="/wiki/Goths" title="Goths">Gothic</a> force of 5,000 soldiers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Traders from southern <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a> settle in northern <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xuanwu_of_Northern_Wei" title="Emperor Xuanwu of Northern Wei">Xuan Wu Di</a> becomes sovereign of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Wei_Dynasty" title="Northern Wei Dynasty">Northern Wei Dynasty</a> (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Mesoamerica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/Uxmal" title="Uxmal">Uxmal</a> is founded (approximate date).</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arian_Baptistry" title="Arian Baptistry">Arian Baptistry</a> is erected by Theodoric the Great, at the same time as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basilica_of_Sant%27_Apollinare_Nuovo" title="Basilica of Sant' Apollinare Nuovo">Basilica of Sant' Apollinare Nuovo</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a>).</li>
<li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Codex_Argenteus" title="Codex Argenteus">Codex Argenteus</a></i>, Gothic manuscript of bishop <a href="/wiki/Ulfilas" title="Ulfilas">Ulfilas</a>'s translation of the <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a>, is written (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
