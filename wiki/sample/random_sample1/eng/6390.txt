<p><b>Berane</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cyrillic" title="Cyrillic">Cyrillic</a>: Беране, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[bɛ̂ranɛ]</a></span>) is one of the largest towns of northeastern <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a> and a former administrative centre of the <a href="/wiki/Ivangrad_District" title="Ivangrad District">Ivangrad District</a>. It is a seat of one of many multi-ethnic and multi-religious municipalities in Montenegro. The town is located on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lim_river" title="Lim river">Lim river</a>, being part of <a href="/wiki/Sand%C5%BEak" title="Sandžak">Sandžak</a> region. Its name is derived from the Turkish word meaning "One house". The town has a population of over 11 thousand, whereas <a href="/wiki/Berane_Municipality" title="Berane Municipality">its municipality area</a> reaches nearly 30 thousand people, making it one of the largest centres of Polimlje area.</p>
<p>During the medieval period the land of Berane was known as Budimlja. It was of great holistic, political and economic importance in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ra%C5%A1ka_(state)" title="Raška (state)">Serbian state of Raska</a>. Until 1455, when Turks took the city, it was part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval_Serbia" title="Medieval Serbia">Medieval Serbian state</a>. Since the <a href="/wiki/First_Serbian_Uprising" title="First Serbian Uprising">first Serbian Uprising</a> until its final liberation, fights against Turks were constant. Famous battles occurred from 1825 to 1862 when the most important Rudes battle was fought on 7 April in which the Montenegrin rebels won. Berane was finally liberated in 1912. The town shared the destiny with its country being damaged in both World Wars. From 1949 until 1992 it was named as <b>Ivangrad</b> (Иванград) in honour to people's hero <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Milutinovi%C4%87" title="Ivan Milutinović">Ivan Milutinović</a>. As Ivangrad, the town was meeting with prosperity and population growth being of one the Yugoslav industrial centres. Although the Civil War in Yugoslavia did not reach Montenegro, the city suffered from it as the industry collapsed and the people started to leave it. Nowadays, Berane is one of the poorest settlements in Montenegro so most of the population lives in difficult conditions. Despite that, the town remains an important educational, medical, religious and sporting centre, having produced many successful individuals in those categories.</p>
<p>Berane is administrative centre of municipality of the same name. There are 13 elementary schools, four high schools and four institutions of higher education. Having a solid sport infrastructure, the most successful sport collectives in the town are handball club and table tennis club. Berane is connected with rest of the country by two-lane motorways. It also has an airport which hasn’t been used for a while. In 2012, Berane was to celebrate 150 years since its official foundation.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>I the Middle Ages, Berane was known as <i>Budimlja</i>. It was of great holistic, political, and economic importance in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval_Serbia" title="Medieval Serbia">Medieval Serbia</a>. <a href="/wiki/Saint_Sava" title="Saint Sava">Saint Sava</a>, the first Serbian <a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">archbishop</a>, founded one of the first Serbian eparchies here in 1219. The Monastery <a href="/wiki/%C4%90ur%C4%91evi_stupovi,_Montenegro" title="Đurđevi stupovi, Montenegro">Đurđevi stupovi</a> was built by the end of the 12th century by Prvoslav, the son of <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Nemanja" title="Stefan Nemanja">Nemanja's</a> brother Tihomir. Many monasteries and churches tell the story on the rich holistic life of Serbs in this area. There were seven bishops and nine metropolitans.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (May 2013)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> Literary and painting schools existed in this Monastery. Apart from Đurđevi stupovi, the second most important monastery in this area was Šudikovo, which was destroyed and burned by the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Turks</a> in 1738. Archbishop's throne was vacant for more than 350 years. However, the Budimlja episcopate, known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eparchy_of_Budimlja-Nik%C5%A1i%C4%87" title="Eparchy of Budimlja-Nikšić">Budimlja-Nikšić eparchy</a> has been restored in 2002 upon the appointment of bishop Joanikije. The area of Berane municipality and its wider neighbourhood, was part of the Medieval Serbian state ntil 1455, when Turks took the city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bihor,_Montenegro" title="Bihor, Montenegro">Bihor</a> and Budimlja. This valley was populated with Serbians until the mid 17th century, when it went under terrible suffering and many of them left. The Turks distributed the land to the Islamised population.</p>

<p>During the <a href="/wiki/First_Serbian_Uprising" title="First Serbian Uprising">First Serbian Uprising</a> (1804) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kara%C4%91or%C4%91e_Petrovi%C4%87" title="Karađorđe Petrović">Karađorđe's</a> march to <a href="/wiki/Novi_Pazar" title="Novi Pazar">Novi Pazar</a>, the people of this area revolted and met the Serbian dukes <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Anto_Bogi%C4%87evi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Anto Bogićević (page does not exist)">Anto Bogićević</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Had%C5%BEi-Prodan_Gligorijevi%C4%87" title="Hadži-Prodan Gligorijević">Hadži-Prodan Gligorijević</a>. Then, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mojsije_Ze%C4%8Devi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mojsije Zečević (page does not exist)">Mojsije Zečević</a>, prior of Đurđevi stupovi, had the leading role among the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Orthodox</a> people of Budimlja. He was one of the closest allies of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petar_I_Petrovi%C4%87-Njego%C5%A1" title="Petar I Petrović-Njegoš">Petar I Petrović-Njegoš</a> and <a href="/wiki/Petar_II_Petrovi%C4%87-Njego%C5%A1" title="Petar II Petrović-Njegoš">Petar II Petrović-Njegoš</a>, the archbishops and political leaders of Montenegro. Since the First Serbian Uprising until its final liberation, fights against Turks were constant, especially during the second part of the 19th century. Famous battles occurred from 1825, until 1862, but the most important one was Rudes battle on 7 April 1862, in which the Serbian and Montenegrin rebels won. Berane was finally liberated from Turks in 1912 and it was incorporated in Montenegro.</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, there was a civil war in the region between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partisans_(Yugoslavia)" title="Partisans (Yugoslavia)">Partisans</a> (communists) and <a href="/wiki/Chetniks" title="Chetniks">Chetniks</a> (royalists and nationalists). During the communists' reprisals in 1944 and 1945 thousands of people were killed. From July 1949 to March 1992, Berane was known as <b>Ivangrad</b> as a tribute to <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Milutinovi%C4%87" title="Ivan Milutinović">Ivan Milutinović</a>. In 1992, its original name was restored.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture.2C_education_and_science">Culture, education and science</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Culture, education and science">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main impact for the development of culture of this area historically, and up to recent times, came from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serb_Orthodox_Church" title="Serb Orthodox Church">Serb Orthodox Church</a>, or it monasteries in the area: Šudikova, Đurdjevi stupovi and other, as the cornerstones of spirituality of the Serb population. That part is later to a certain extent taken over by schools, and, more and more institutions dealing with culture.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Berane_gimnazija.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="395" data-file-width="596" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Berane_gimnazija.jpg/250px-Berane_gimnazija.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Berane_gimnazija.jpg/375px-Berane_gimnazija.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Berane_gimnazija.jpg/500px-Berane_gimnazija.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Berane_gimnazija.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Panto_Mali%C5%A1i%C4%87_Gymnasium,_Berane" title="Panto Mališić Gymnasium, Berane">Panto Mališić Gymnasium</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>A special role in that process was that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beranska_gimnazija" title="Beranska gimnazija">Berane Gymnasium</a>, founded in 1913. Its classrooms were a home to many prominent artists and scientists in various disciplines such as: Mihailo Lalić, Dušan Kostić, Radovan Zogović, Jovan Zonjić, Mišo Popović, Aleksandar Rafajlović, Luka Radojević and the others. The institutions in Berane that are still working in culture, there are: Cultural Centre (with the library), and Polimski Museum, with a very rich collection of valuable artefacts; and the House of the Duke <a href="/wiki/Gavro_Vukovi%C4%87" title="Gavro Vuković">Gavro Vuković</a>, the first jurist with a university degree, a long term Minister of Foreign Affairs in the Principality of Montenegro, and the writer of famous “Memoirs” has been reconstructed, planned to be used for cultural events. Berane also has a school for primary musical education, and there are also few amateur clubs and societies in various disciplines of artistic creation.</p>
<p>There are 13 elementary schools, four secondary schools and one school for elementary musical education in municipality of Berane. Berane has four institutions of higher education: Faculty of management in transport and communications, Faculty of Teacher-training, Higher Medical School and Applied Computer engineering (study program of Faculty of Electrical Engineering, Podgorica). Berane, as is usually the case with small communities, does not have a specialised scientific institution, but in spite that fact, many prominent scientists from various scientific disciplines come from here. A remarkable contribution to science was given by, among others, chemist Vukić Mićović, bacteriologist Milutin Đurišić, geographer Milisav Lutovac, and historian Miomir Dašić.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy.2C_infrastructure_and_sports">Economy, infrastructure and sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy, infrastructure and sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Berane_fabrika.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="291" data-file-width="500" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Berane_fabrika.jpg/250px-Berane_fabrika.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Berane_fabrika.jpg/375px-Berane_fabrika.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e9/Berane_fabrika.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Berane_fabrika.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Overview of the former industrial zone.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The municipality of Berane is one of the poorest in Montenegro. The level of industrial production is very low, since a big number of companies have stopped working in the past 15 years due to the known crisis in former Yugoslavia. In the earlier period (60’s and 70’s) many village, agricultural households have moved to the town in the process of “industrialisation”, which decreased agricultural production, and industry later collapsed so most of the population lives in difficult conditions. Many small private companies have been started recently, mostly in the areas of commerce and catering industry. There is a very small number of companies that are creating new value. Workers in those new companies nave no insurance or health service, so those companies do not have a bigger positive impact on the community. Total number of employed in the municipality is approximately 3,000.</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Beranski_stadion.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="402" data-file-width="604" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Beranski_stadion.jpg/250px-Beranski_stadion.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Beranski_stadion.jpg/375px-Beranski_stadion.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Beranski_stadion.jpg/500px-Beranski_stadion.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Beranski_stadion.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
City Stadium.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Berane is connected with rest of Montenegro by two-lane motorways. <a href="/wiki/Bijelo_Polje" title="Bijelo Polje">Bijelo Polje</a> is 35 km away, where there lies the major junction (railway and the road) towards <a href="/wiki/Podgorica" title="Podgorica">Podgorica</a> and the coast, and to <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>. Serbia can also be reached eastwards via <a href="/wiki/Ro%C5%BEaje" title="Rožaje">Rožaje</a> (for <a href="/wiki/Novi_Pazar" title="Novi Pazar">Novi Pazar</a>). Berane is on the corridor of the future <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beograd_-_Bar_motorway" title="Beograd - Bar motorway">Beograd - Bar motorway</a>. Berane has an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berane_Airport" title="Berane Airport">airport</a>, which hasn't been used for years, although there have been plans for its revitalisation and usage as regional airport. <a href="/wiki/Podgorica_Airport" title="Podgorica Airport">Podgorica Airport</a> is some 150 km (93 mi) away, and has regular flights to and from major <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> destinations.</p>
<p>Berane has solid sport infrastructure. The most successful sport collectives are: <a href="/wiki/RK_Berane" title="RK Berane">handball club Berane</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=STK_Budim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="STK Budim (page does not exist)">table tennis club Budim</a>. There are also <a href="/wiki/FK_Berane" title="FK Berane">football club Berane</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=KK_Lim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="KK Lim (page does not exist)">basketball club Lim</a>, boxing clubs <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=BK_Ba%C4%87o_King&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="BK Baćo King (page does not exist)">Baćo King</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=BK_Radni%C4%8Dki_Berane&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="BK Radnički Berane (page does not exist)">Radnički Berane</a>, athletic club, tennis club etc. The Sports Centre consists of many sport objects. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gradski_Stadion_Berane" title="Gradski Stadion Berane">City Stadium</a> with capacity of 11,000 people is the second largest <a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">stadium</a> in Montenegro. Just next to the stadium, a new modern city hall is under construction.</p>
