<p><b>Beromünster</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Switzerland" title="Municipalities of Switzerland">municipality</a> in the district of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sursee_(Amt)" title="Sursee (Amt)">Sursee</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Cantons_of_Switzerland" title="Cantons of Switzerland">canton</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucerne_(canton)" title="Lucerne (canton)">Lucerne</a> in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. On 1 January 2004 the former municipality of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Schwarzenbach,_Lucerne&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Schwarzenbach, Lucerne (page does not exist)">Schwarzenbach</a> merged into the municipality of Beromünster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mutation_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mutation-3">[3]</a></sup> On 1 January 2009 the former municipality of <a href="/wiki/Gunzwil" title="Gunzwil">Gunzwil</a> merged into the municipality of Beromünster followed by <a href="/wiki/Neudorf,_Lucerne" title="Neudorf, Lucerne">Neudorf</a> on 1 January 2013.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beromünster is first mentioned in 1223 as <i>villa Beronensis</i>. In 1333 it was mentioned as <i>Münster in Ergöwe</i>. Until 1934 it was known simply as Münster (LU).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HDS_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-HDS-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:2004_beromuenster_flecken.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/2004_beromuenster_flecken.jpg/220px-2004_beromuenster_flecken.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/2004_beromuenster_flecken.jpg/330px-2004_beromuenster_flecken.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/2004_beromuenster_flecken.jpg/440px-2004_beromuenster_flecken.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:2004_beromuenster_flecken.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Flecken in Beromünster</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Beromünster has an area of 42.17 km<sup>2</sup> (16.28 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arealstatistik_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Arealstatistik-1">[1]</a></sup> Of this area, 64.7% is used for agricultural purposes, while 21.1% is forested. Of the rest of the land, 14.1% is settled (buildings or roads) and the remainder (0.2%) is non-productive (rivers, glaciers or mountains).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFSO_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-SFSO-5">[5]</a></sup> In the 1997 land survey<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, 16.32% of the total land area was forested. Of the agricultural land, 72.03% is used for farming or pastures, while 3.54% is used for orchards or vine crops. Of the settled areas, 4.29% is covered with buildings, 0.24% is industrial, 0.68% is classed as special developments, 0.27% is parks or greenbelts and 2.49% is transportation infrastructure. Of the unproductive areas, 0.1% is unproductive flowing water (rivers) and 0.03% is other unproductive land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LUSTAT_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-LUSTAT-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The municipality is located in the upper <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wynental" title="Wynental">Wynental</a>. It consists of two separate settlements, <i>Flecken</i> in a <a href="/wiki/Depression_(geology)" title="Depression (geology)">depression</a> and the church owned village of Beromünster on the western side of a hill. <i>Flecken</i> consists of three rows of houses, built following a fire in 1764, along the main and parallel side road. Since 1 January 2009 it has also included the municipality of Gunzwil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The merged municipality in 2009 had an area of 29.35 km<sup>2</sup> (11.33 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-updated_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-updated-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beromünster has a population (as of 31 December 2015) of 6,445.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> As of 2007<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, 13.4% of the population was made up of foreign nationals. The combined municipality has a population of 4,588 of which 10.1% are foreign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-updated_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-updated-8">[8]</a></sup> Over the last 10 years the population has grown at a rate of 2.1%. Most of the population (as of 2000<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>) speaks German (90.1%), with Albanian being second most common ( 4.6%) and Serbo-Croatian being third ( 2.1%).</p>
<p>In the 2007 election the most popular party was the <a href="/wiki/Christian_Democratic_People%27s_Party_of_Switzerland" title="Christian Democratic People's Party of Switzerland">CVP</a> which received 45.9% of the vote. The next three most popular parties were the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_People%27s_Party" title="Swiss People's Party">SVP</a> (23%), the <a href="/wiki/Free_Democratic_Party_of_Switzerland" title="Free Democratic Party of Switzerland">FDP</a> (19.1%) and the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_Switzerland" title="Green Party of Switzerland">Green Party</a> (5.5%).</p>
<p>The age distribution in Beromünster is; 1,190 people or 26.8% of the population is 0–19 years old. 1,067 people or 24.1% are 20–39 years old, and 1,514 people or 34.1% are 40–64 years old. The senior population distribution is 502 people or 11.3% are 65–79 years old, 138 or 3.1% are 80–89 years old and 25 people or 0.6% of the population are 90+ years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LUSTAT_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-LUSTAT-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The entire Swiss population is generally well educated. In Beromünster about 71.1% of the population (between age 25-64) have completed either non-mandatory <a href="/wiki/Education_in_Switzerland#Secondary" title="Education in Switzerland">upper secondary education</a> or additional higher education (either university or a <i><a href="/wiki/Fachhochschule" title="Fachhochschule">Fachhochschule</a></i>).</p>
<p>As of 2000<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> there are 1,542 households, of which 400 households (or about 25.9%) contain only a single individual. 253 or about 16.4% are large households, with at least five members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LUSTAT_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-LUSTAT-6">[6]</a></sup> As of 2000<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> there were 917 inhabited buildings in the municipality, of which 669 were built only as housing, and 248 were <a href="/wiki/Mixed-use_development" title="Mixed-use development">mixed use</a> buildings. There were 496 single family homes, 98 double family homes, and 75 multi-family homes in the municipality. Most homes were either two (398) or three (192) story structures. There were only 40 single story buildings and 39 four or more story buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LUSTAT_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-LUSTAT-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Beromünster has an unemployment rate of 1.18%. As of 2005<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, there were 91 people employed in the <a href="/wiki/Primary_sector_of_the_economy" title="Primary sector of the economy">primary economic sector</a> and about 32 businesses involved in this sector. 308 people are employed in the <a href="/wiki/Secondary_sector_of_the_economy" title="Secondary sector of the economy">secondary sector</a> and there are 38 businesses in this sector. 709 people are employed in the <a href="/wiki/Tertiary_sector_of_the_economy" title="Tertiary sector of the economy">tertiary sector</a>, with 96 businesses in this sector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFSO_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-SFSO-5">[5]</a></sup> As of 2000<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> 50.5% of the population of the municipality were employed in some capacity. At the same time, females made up 40.5% of the workforce.</p>
<p>In the 2000 census<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> the religious membership of Beromünster was; 3,650 (82.1%) were Roman Catholic, and 330 (7.4%) were Protestant, with an additional 73 (1.64%) that were of some other Christian faith. There are 2 individuals (0.04% of the population) who are Jewish. There are 165 individuals (3.71% of the population) who are Muslim. Of the rest; there were 1 (0.02%) individuals who belong to another religion, 113 (2.54%) who do not belong to any organized religion, 114 (2.56%) who did not answer the question.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LUSTAT_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-LUSTAT-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sights">Sights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Beromünster is the site of the <a href="/wiki/Blosenbergturm" title="Blosenbergturm">Blosenbergturm</a>, a 215–217 m (705–712 ft) tall radio tower built in 1937.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The castle at Beromünster was the site of one of the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spread_of_the_printing_press#Switzerland" title="Spread of the printing press">printing presses</a> in Switzerland, built in 1470.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Weather">Weather</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berom%C3%BCnster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Weather">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beromünster has an average of 138.1 days of rain per year and on average receives 1,223 mm (48.1 in) of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a>. The wettest month is June during which time Beromünster receives an average of 150 mm (5.9 in) of precipitation. During this month there is precipitation for an average of 13.2 days. The month with the most days of precipitation is May, with an average of 13.7, but with only 126 mm (5.0 in) of precipitation. The driest month of the year is October with an average of 74 mm (2.9 in) of precipitation over 13.2 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MeteoSchweiz_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-MeteoSchweiz-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
