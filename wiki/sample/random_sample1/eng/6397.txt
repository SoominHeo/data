<p><b>Bernardo Sepúlveda Amor</b> (born <span style="display:none">(<span class="bday">1941-12-14</span>)</span>14 December 1941) is a <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> diplomat and jurist. He was <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_Foreign_Affairs_(Mexico)" title="Secretary of Foreign Affairs (Mexico)">Secretary of Foreign Affairs</a> during the 1980s under President <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_la_Madrid" title="Miguel de la Madrid">Miguel de la Madrid</a> and is a former judge of the <a href="/wiki/International_Court_of_Justice" title="International Court of Justice">International Court of Justice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icj_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-icj-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bernardo_Sep%C3%BAlveda_Amor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>, where he studied law at the <a href="/wiki/National_Autonomous_University_of_Mexico" title="National Autonomous University of Mexico">National Autonomous University</a> (UNAM, 1964). He then pursued post-graduate studies, specialising in international law at <a href="/wiki/Queens%27_College,_Cambridge" title="Queens' College, Cambridge">Queens' College, Cambridge</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> (1966).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mx-bios_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-mx-bios-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is a professor of international law and international organisations at <a href="/wiki/El_Colegio_de_M%C3%A9xico" title="El Colegio de México">El Colegio de México</a> and also teaches at the Matías Romero Institute. His other academic activities have seen him work at the <a href="/wiki/Centro_de_Investigaci%C3%B3n_y_Docencia_Econ%C3%B3micas" title="Centro de Investigación y Docencia Económicas">Centro de Investigación y Docencia Económicas</a> (CIDE) and the UNAM.</p>
<p>From 16 March to 30 November 1982, he served as <a href="/wiki/Ambassador_of_Mexico_to_the_United_States" title="Ambassador of Mexico to the United States">Ambassador to the United States of America</a> and from 1989 to 1993, as <a href="/wiki/Embassy_of_Mexico,_London#Ambassador" title="Embassy of Mexico, London">Ambassador to the United Kingdom</a>. Between those two diplomatic postings from December 1982 to 1988, he served as <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_Foreign_Affairs_(Mexico)" title="Secretary of Foreign Affairs (Mexico)">Secretary of Foreign Affairs</a> under <a href="/wiki/President_of_Mexico" title="President of Mexico">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_la_Madrid" title="Miguel de la Madrid">Miguel de la Madrid</a>. During his time in the Cabinet, he was instrumental in establishing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contadora_Group" title="Contadora Group">Contadora Group</a>, which worked to bring peace to <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>, and in the creation of the <i>Grupo de Ocho</i>, since expanded to become the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Group" title="Rio Group">Rio Group</a>.</p>
<p>In 1984 he was awarded the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_of_Asturias_Prize" title="Prince of Asturias Prize">Prince of Asturias Prize</a> for his international co-operation efforts. The following year, <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> awarded him its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar_Prize" title="Simón Bolívar Prize">Simón Bolívar Prize</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icj_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-icj-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1996 he was elected to serve on the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> <a href="/wiki/International_Law_Commission" title="International Law Commission">International Law Commission</a>; he was re-elected to the same position in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icj_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-icj-4">[4]</a></sup> On 7 November 2005 he was elected to a nine-year period as one of the judges of the <a href="/wiki/International_Court_of_Justice" title="International Court of Justice">International Court of Justice</a>.</p>
<p>In 2012, Sepúlveda-Amor was elected by the judges of the ICJ to serve as its vice-president, for a three-year term beginning 6 February 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ICJrelease6Feb2012_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ICJrelease6Feb2012-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
