<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Field Army Bernolák</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p><b>The Field Army Bernolák</b> (<a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a>: <span lang="sk" xml:lang="sk"><i>Slovenská Poľná Armádna skupina "Bernolák"</i></span>) was an infantry unit of the <a href="/wiki/Axis_powers" title="Axis powers">Axis</a> <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Republic_(1939%E2%80%931945)" title="Slovak Republic (1939–1945)">Slovak Republic</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. It was named after <a href="/wiki/Anton_Bernol%C3%A1k" title="Anton Bernolák">Anton Bernolák</a>, the first codifier of the literary <a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak language</a>.</p>
<p>The division took part in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slovak_invasion_of_Poland" title="Slovak invasion of Poland">Invasion of Poland</a> from September 1939. In October it returned to Slovakia and was upgraded to a fully motorized division.</p>
<p>A component, the <b>Fast Troops Group <i>Kalinčiak</i></b>, took part in <a href="/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">Operation Barbarossa</a> in 1941, advancing under <a href="/wiki/Army_Group_South" title="Army Group South">Army Group South</a>.</p>
<p>In August 1943 the Fast Division was restructured and renamed the 1st Infantry Division of that year. However, the 1st Infantry Division saw very little action and was used for rear area duties until it was disbanded in July 1944 to form construction units.</p>
