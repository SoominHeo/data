<p><b>Gianni Versace S.p.A.</b> (<small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[ˈdʒanni verˈsaːtʃe]</a></span>), usually referred to as <b>Versace</b>, is an Italian <a href="/wiki/Luxury_goods" title="Luxury goods">luxury</a> fashion company and trade name founded by <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Versace" title="Gianni Versace">Gianni Versace</a> in 1978. The main collection of the brand is Versace, which produces upmarket Italian-made <a href="/wiki/Ready-to-wear" title="Ready-to-wear">ready-to-wear</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leather" title="Leather">leather</a> accessories, while other diffusion lines are Versace Collection (mainly in the US), Versus Versace and Versace Jeans. The Versace logo is the head of <a href="/wiki/Medusa" title="Medusa">Medusa</a>, a Greek mythological figure. The logo came from the floor of ruins in Rome that the Versace siblings played in as children. Gianni Versace chose Medusa as the logo because she made people fall in love with her and they had no way back. He hoped his company would have the same effect on people. The Versace brand is known for having flashy prints and bright colors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-:3-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_operations">History and operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Versace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1972, Gianni Versace designed his first collections for Callaghan, Genny, and Complice. In 1978, the company launched under the name "Gianni Versace Donna."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> The first Versace boutique was opened in Milan's <a href="/wiki/Via_della_Spiga" title="Via della Spiga">Via della Spiga</a> in 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Versace was one of the few independent designers who was in control of everything about the brand, from designing to retailing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:2-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1982, the company expanded into the accessory, jewelry, home furnishing, and china industries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1993, Donatella Versace created the "Young Versace" line as well as "Versus."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1994, the brand gained widespread international coverage due to the "<a href="/wiki/Black_Versace_dress_of_Elizabeth_Hurley" title="Black Versace dress of Elizabeth Hurley">Black Versace dress of Elizabeth Hurley</a>", referred to at the time as "that dress".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-:3-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gundle2008_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gundle2008-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Versace was often described as the "Rock n' Roll designer" because they designed for many famous clients, including <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>, and Shenki Versace. Versace designed the stage costumes and album cover costumes for <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a> in 1992. Versace has also designed clothing for the <a href="/wiki/Princess_of_Wales" title="Princess of Wales">Princess of Wales</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princess_Caroline_of_Monaco" title="Princess Caroline of Monaco">Princess Caroline of Monaco</a>. The Versace company is known for using the same models in their ads as they do on the runway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the murder of <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Versace" title="Gianni Versace">Gianni Versace</a> in 1997, his sister <a href="/wiki/Donatella_Versace" title="Donatella Versace">Donatella Versace</a>, formerly vice-president, took over as <a href="/wiki/Creative_director" title="Creative director">creative director</a> and his older brother <a href="/wiki/Santo_Versace" title="Santo Versace">Santo Versace</a> became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEO" title="CEO">CEO</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Donatella's daughter <a href="/wiki/Allegra_Versace" title="Allegra Versace">Allegra Versace</a> was left a 50 percent stake in the company, which she assumed control of on her eighteenth birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2000, the "<a href="/wiki/Green_Versace_dress_of_Jennifer_Lopez" title="Green Versace dress of Jennifer Lopez">green Versace dress</a>" worn by <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a> at the <a href="/wiki/42nd_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="42nd Annual Grammy Awards">42nd Annual Grammy Awards</a> gained a lot of media attention, being voted as the "fifth most iconic dress of all time" in 2008, with Hurley's 1994 dress being voted first in a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daily_Telegraph" title="Daily Telegraph">Daily Telegraph</a></i> poll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph1_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph1-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company's profits were in decline in the early 2000s;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Massimo_Cacciatori" title="Fabio Massimo Cacciatori">Fabio Massimo Cacciatori</a> was hired as interim CEO to reorganise and restructure the Versace Group in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-designtaxi_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-designtaxi-16">[16]</a></sup> Cacciatori resigned in December 2003 due to "disputes with the Versace family".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-designtaxi_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-designtaxi-16">[16]</a></sup> From 2004 Giancarlo di Risio, from IT Holding, was CEO of the group until his resignation in 2009 due to disagreements with Donatella.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Since July 2009, Gian Giacomo Ferraris, previously of <a href="/wiki/Jil_Sander_(brand)" title="Jil Sander (brand)">Jil Sander</a>, has operated as CEO for the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2014 <a href="/wiki/The_Blackstone_Group" title="The Blackstone Group">The Blackstone Group</a> purchased a 20 percent stake in Versace for <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>210 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wwdBlackstone_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-wwdBlackstone-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of August 2013<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Versace&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, more than 80 boutiques operate worldwide; the first boutique outside of Italy was opened in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glasgow,_Scotland" title="Glasgow, Scotland">Glasgow, Scotland</a> in 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collaborations">Collaborations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Versace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Collaborations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2006, Gianni Versace S.p.A. entered into a partnership with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobili_Lamborghini_S.p.A." title="Automobili Lamborghini S.p.A.">Automobili Lamborghini S.p.A.</a> to produce the Lamborghini Murciélago LP640 VERSACE. The car had a Versace white satin interior with the Versace logo embroidered into the seats. The car was available in black and white. There were only ten units produced. The car was sold with a complementary luggage set, luxury driving shoes, and driving gloves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Versace company teamed up with <a href="/wiki/AgustaWestland" title="AgustaWestland">AgustaWestland</a> in 2008 to create the AgustaWestland AW109 Grand Versace VIP luxury helicopter. The helicopter includes Versace leather interior and a design on the outside.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Versace and <a href="/wiki/H%26M" title="H&amp;M">H&amp;M</a> released a new line of clothing that would be sold in <a href="/wiki/H%26M" title="H&amp;M">H&amp;M</a> stores. The store sold both men's and women's clothing and some home items such as pillows and blankets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Versace collaborated with dancer <a href="/wiki/Lil_Buck" title="Lil Buck">Lil Buck</a> to release a line of sneakers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnerships">Partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Versace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Partnerships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Versace partnered with the Mind Group firm in China in 2015. The two companies designed luxury residence towers called the "Versace Residencies." The goal of the creators were to combine Versace's luxury home elements with elements of traditional Chinese culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> This same year, Versace partnered with the ABIL Group in India to develop another residential project. These luxury homes are located in South Mumbai.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, <a href="/wiki/Donatella_Versace" title="Donatella Versace">Donatella Versace</a> was featured in <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Tisci" title="Riccardo Tisci">Riccardo Tisci</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Givenchy" title="Givenchy">Givenchy</a> campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Palazzo_Versace">Palazzo Versace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Versace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Palazzo Versace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Soheil Abedian, of the <a href="/wiki/Sunland_Group" title="Sunland Group">Sunland Group</a>, approached Versace in 1997 and successfully proposed a luxury hotel built upon the Versace brand; the first <a href="/wiki/Palazzo_Versace_Australia" title="Palazzo Versace Australia">Palazzo Versace</a> opened on Australia's <a href="/wiki/Gold_Coast,_Queensland" title="Gold Coast, Queensland">Gold Coast</a> on 15 September 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The hotel was sold to a Chinese consortium in December 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The second Palazzo, the <a href="/wiki/Palazzo_Versace_Dubai" title="Palazzo Versace Dubai">Palazzo Versace Dubai</a>, was completed in December 2015 and is located in the Persian Gulf.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> A third Palazzo is under construction. The Palazzo Versace Macau was announced in 2013 and will be built in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>'s largest casino operator <a href="/wiki/Sociedade_de_Turismo_e_Divers%C3%B5es_de_Macau" title="Sociedade de Turismo e Diversões de Macau">SJM Holdings</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The Palazzo Versace hotels are the first fashion-branded hotels in the world and place an emphasis on beauty and luxury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
