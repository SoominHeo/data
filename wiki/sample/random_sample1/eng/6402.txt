<p><i><b>Berserk</b></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ベルセルク</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Beruseruku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Dark_fantasy" title="Dark fantasy">dark fantasy</a> <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Kentaro_Miura" title="Kentaro Miura">Kentaro Miura</a>. Set in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval" title="Medieval">medieval</a> <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>-inspired <a href="/wiki/Dark_fantasy" title="Dark fantasy">dark</a> <a href="/wiki/Fantasy_world" title="Fantasy world">fantasy world</a>, the story centers on the characters of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guts_(Berserk)" title="Guts (Berserk)">Guts</a>, a lone <a href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">mercenary</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Griffith_(Berserk)" title="Griffith (Berserk)">Griffith</a>, the leader of a mercenary band called the "Band of the Hawk." Themes of isolation, camaraderie, and the question of whether <a href="/wiki/Human_nature" title="Human nature">humanity</a> is fundamentally <a href="/wiki/Problem_of_evil" title="Problem of evil">good or evil</a> pervade the story, as it explores both the best and worst of human nature. Both the manga and anime are noted for their use of <a href="/wiki/Graphic_violence" title="Graphic violence">graphic violence</a> and sexual content.</p>
<p>Miura premiered a prototype of <i>Berserk</i> in 1988. The actual series began the following year in the now-defunct magazine <i>Monthly Animal House</i>, which was replaced in 1992 by the semimonthly magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Animal_(magazine)" title="Young Animal (magazine)">Young Animal</a></i>, where <i>Berserk</i> continues to be serialized intermittently. The series was adapted into a twenty-five episode <a href="/wiki/Berserk_(1997_TV_series)" title="Berserk (1997 TV series)">anime series</a> covering the series' <i>Golden Age</i> arc by <a href="/wiki/OLM,_Inc." title="OLM, Inc.">Oriental Light and Magic</a> from October 7, 1997 to March 31, 1998. A trilogy of films, <i><a href="/wiki/Berserk:_The_Golden_Age_Arc" title="Berserk: The Golden Age Arc">Berserk: The Golden Age Arc</a></i>, were released beginning in 2012. A second <a href="/wiki/Berserk_(2016_TV_series)" title="Berserk (2016 TV series)">anime adaptation</a> began broadcasting on July 1, 2016.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Berserk_characters" title="List of Berserk characters">List of Berserk characters</a></div>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guts_(Berserk)" title="Guts (Berserk)">Guts</a> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ガッツ</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Gattsu</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> is a wandering mercenary whose entire world from birth is warfare and bloodshed. His adoptive mother dies from the plague when Guts is young and he is enlisted within his adoptive father Gambino's mercenary group. Gambino eventually offers Guts to another mercenary and he is subsequently sexually assaulted. In an altercation, Guts kills Gambino by accident and is forced to flee. Years later, Guts continues to prove his worth on the battlefield defeating the famous fighter Bazuso in a duel one on one. This catches the attention of the leader of "Band of the Hawk" <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">鷹の団</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Taka no Dan</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span>, Griffith, who swiftly defeats Guts, forcing him to join The Band of the Hawk. Guts soon becomes Griffith's right-hand man while making a name as the group's renowned raid leader. Shortly afterwards during an encounter with a monstrous man known as Nosferatu Zodd who defeats both Guts and Griffith, Zodd notices the Crimson Behelit in Griffith's possession; a trinket in the shape of a face, and leaves promptly, warning Guts that his continued friendship with Griffith will result in his death.</p>
<p>The Hawks play a pivotal role in ending the 100-year war that has ravaged the country of Midland and are initially welcomed as heroes, but Griffith's aspirations to rule his own kingdom are shattered when Guts leaves the Hawks to find his own dream. Unable to cope with the loss, Griffith seduces the King's daughter Charlotte but is caught and ends up in the dungeon for a year. By the time Guts learns of this and helps the outlawed remnants of the Hawks rescue him, Griffith has been horribly mutilated and rendered a mute shadow of his former self.</p>
<p>The weight of knowing his body is too damaged for him to fulfill his dream proves too much for Griffith and he accidentally activates the Crimson Behelit on his person. This brings the Hawks to another plane where they encounter archdemons known as the God Hand in a ceremony known as "the Eclipse". Urged onward by the eldritch beings, Griffith sacrifices his soldiers to the God Hand's "apostles" — humans like Zodd who sacrificed their loved ones and humanity for power — so that he can become the God Hand's fifth and final member, taking the name Femto.</p>
<p>Branded, Guts witnesses the brutal slaughter of his comrades, though he and his lover Casca are saved from certain death by the mysterious Skull Knight. Guts loses an arm and an eye while Casca is driven insane after being raped by the reborn Griffith. Leaving Casca in the care of Rickert, a survivor of the Band of the Hawk who was not spirited into the other plane, and the blacksmith Godo, Guts begins hunting down the apostles in search of revenge against Griffith, although his Brand of Sacrifice makes him a target for restless spirits that hunt him nightly.</p>
<p>Years after beginning his journey, Guts is joined by the elf Puck whose company he eventually accepts. Guts is later reunited with Casca as she is about to be burned as a witch in the city of St. Albion. There, encountering Farnese of the Holy See Church's Holy Iron Chain Knights, Guts endures a nightmarish ordeal that ends in Griffith's physical form restored. As Guts takes Casca to the Elven realm of Elfhelm for sanctuary with the help of his new companions, Griffith creates a second Band of the Hawk with Zodd and other Apostles among its ranks to battle the invading Kushan army. The war between Griffith and the Kushan emperor, a rogue apostle, climaxes with the emperor's destruction and the overlapping of the mortal realm and the supernatural. No longer challenged by demon or man, Griffith establishes his rule over Midland with the endorsement of the Pope and Princess Charlotte, creating the city of Falconia to provide the Midlanders with refuge from the ever-increasing attacks of mythical beasts, unaware their new ruler is actually one of them.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Berserk_chapters" title="List of Berserk chapters">List of Berserk chapters</a></div>
<p>Miura first premiered <i>Berserk</i> in 1988 with a 48-page prototype, which won a prize at the Comi Manga School he was enrolled in at the time. On November 26, 1990, the first volume of the manga was published by <a href="/wiki/Hakusensha" title="Hakusensha">Hakusensha</a> in its <a href="/wiki/Hakusensha#Imprints" title="Hakusensha">Jets Comics</a> collection. Three more volumes appeared until <i>Berserk</i> was serialized by <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Animal_(magazine)" title="Young Animal (magazine)">Young Animal</a></i> (Hakusensha) in 1992 and new chapters are still being released in the semiweekly magazine. Bound volumes are still published biannually in Japan by Hakusensha (Jets Comics collection); as of June 2016, 38 <i><a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a></i> of the manga have been published in Japan. The manga was published on an irregular schedule from late 2006 until July 2015. From then to December 2015 it was published monthly before returning to an irregular schedule. It returned to a monthly schedule starting in June 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>In North America and the United Kingdom, the manga is translated and co-published between <a href="/wiki/Dark_Horse_Comics" title="Dark Horse Comics">Dark Horse Comics</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_Manga_Publishing" title="Digital Manga Publishing">Digital Manga Publishing</a>, which has released 37 volumes so far, the first on October 22, 2003. The series has also been published in France by Samourai Editions, Dynamic Visions and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gl%C3%A9nat_(publisher)" title="Glénat (publisher)">Glénat</a>, in Spain by MangaLine Ediciones, in the Netherlands by Glénat, in South Korea by Dai Won, in Italy, Germany, Brazil, and Mexico by <a href="/wiki/Panini_Comics" title="Panini Comics">Panini Comics</a>, and in Poland by J.P.Fantastica.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_series_.281997.29">First series (1997)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: First series (1997)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Berserk_(1997_TV_series)" title="Berserk (1997 TV series)">Berserk (1997 TV series)</a></div>
<p>Produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oriental_Light_and_Magic" title="Oriental Light and Magic">Oriental Light and Magic</a> and directed by Naohito Takahashi, the 25-episode TV anime series adaptation <i>Berserk: Kenpū Denki</i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">剣風伝奇ベルセルク</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kenpū Denki Beruseruku</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, lit., "Berserk: Sword-Wind Tales")</span> first aired from October 7, 1997 to March 31, 1998. The series begins with the Black Swordsman arc, continuing through the Golden Age arc. Though the manga storyline remains largely intact, several changes were made, with the modification or elimination of characters (one notable example being Puck), some of the series' most violent and brutal scenes, and material that would have extended the storyline beyond the planned run of the anime series. Themes of friendship and ambition are more developed and emphasized than those of causality and the supernatural, each of which were made with the approval of Miura.</p>
<p>In Asia, Vap Video has released thirteen VHS and twelve VCD including two episodes each (a single one in the last VHS and three in the last VCD) from 1998 to 1999 in Japan. The seven discs "DVD-BOX", using Audio-CD cases, was released in Japan in 2001, with the seven volumes being re-released later in individual DVD regular cases in 2003.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_series_.282012-2013.29">Film series (2012-2013)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Film series (2012-2013)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Berserk:_The_Golden_Age_Arc" title="Berserk: The Golden Age Arc">Berserk: The Golden Age Arc</a></div>
<p>In September 2010, a wraparound jacket band on volume 35 of the <i>Berserk</i> manga announced a new anime project,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> which was later revealed to be a series of films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The first two films, <i><a href="/wiki/Berserk:_The_Golden_Age_Arc#The_Egg_of_the_King" title="Berserk: The Golden Age Arc">Berserk: Golden Age Arc I - The Egg of the King</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Berserk:_The_Golden_Age_Arc#The_Battle_for_Doldrey" title="Berserk: The Golden Age Arc">Berserk: Golden Age Arc II - The Battle for Doldrey</a></i>, were released in Japan in February and June 2012. <i><a href="/wiki/Berserk:_The_Golden_Age_Arc#The_Advent" title="Berserk: The Golden Age Arc">Berserk: Golden Age Arc III - The Advent</a></i> was released on February 1, 2013 in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first two movies are substantially a retelling of the 1997 TV series. The third movie features new footage beyond the TV series storyline based on the manga. The movies are primarily animated using <a href="/wiki/Computer_animation" title="Computer animation">CGI</a> although rendered with a flat two-dimensional effect which matches the TV series visual style.</p>
<p>The voice cast features <a href="/wiki/Hiroaki_Iwanaga" title="Hiroaki Iwanaga">Hiroaki Iwanaga</a> as Guts (replacing <a href="/wiki/Nobutoshi_Canna" title="Nobutoshi Canna">Nobutoshi Canna</a> from the original anime), <a href="/wiki/Takahiro_Sakurai" title="Takahiro Sakurai">Takahiro Sakurai</a> as Griffith (replacing <a href="/wiki/Toshiyuki_Morikawa" title="Toshiyuki Morikawa">Toshiyuki Morikawa</a>), and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Toa_Yukinari&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Toa Yukinari (page does not exist)">Toa Yukinari</a> as Casca (replacing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuko_Miyamura" title="Yuko Miyamura">Yuko Miyamura</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The English voice cast features <a href="/wiki/Marc_Diraison" title="Marc Diraison">Marc Diraison</a> as Guts, Kevin T. Collins as Griffith, and <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Keranen" title="Carrie Keranen">Carrie Keranen</a> as Casca, all reprising their roles from the original anime.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_series_.282016.29">Second series (2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Second series (2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Berserk_(2016_TV_series)" title="Berserk (2016 TV series)">Berserk (2016 TV series)</a></div>
<p>The Japanese magazine <a href="/wiki/Young_Animal_(magazine)" title="Young Animal (magazine)">Young Animal</a> announced on December 22nd, 2015 that a second televised anime adaptation of the Berserk manga would be produced and broadcast on Japanese television network <a href="/wiki/WOWOW" title="WOWOW">WOWOW</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mainichi_Broadcasting_System" title="Mainichi Broadcasting System">MBS</a> starting in July 2016. The first promotional video for the new series was revealed at NBC’s winter <a href="/wiki/Comiket" title="Comiket">Comiket</a>, which featured Guts in his Black Swordsmen attire fighting off dozens of demonic skeletons with his characteristic sword Dragon Slayer. The new series will apparently be primarily animated using <a href="/wiki/Computer_animation" title="Computer animation">CGI</a> rather than the previous series' traditional animated style. The new series commences with the "Conviction" story arc starting around chapter 115 of the manga, and introduces Puck, Isidro, Farnese, and Serpico as characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The second season, covering the "Hawk of the Millennium Empire" arc, is scheduled to begin in Q2 2017.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Susumu_Hirasawa" title="Susumu Hirasawa">Susumu Hirasawa</a> participated in the soundtracks for all adaptations of the manga, using an eclectic selection of instruments. He received the offer to compose music for the series due to Miura being a fan of his works and listening to them while he works on Berserk, that also led to Miura almost never requesting a specific composition to Hirasawa. Hirasawa has said that Berserk does not feel like a self-restrained story, and that composing for the series is similar to the sensation of unlocking one of his inner restraints, leading to compositions on a "Berserk" style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Hirasawa has made five songs with lyrics for the series, the last two of them were written in a language Hirasawa created by mixing elements of <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Besides Hirasawa, the video games and film trilogy feature work by composers Masaya Imoto, Hiromi Murakami, Shinya Chikamori, Hiroshi Watanabe, Yasushi Hasegawa, Tomoyo Nishimoto and <a href="/wiki/Shir%C5%8D_Sagisu" title="Shirō Sagisu">Shirō Sagisu</a>; the anime and the film trilogy feature theme songs by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Penpals_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Penpals (band) (page does not exist)">PENPALS</a>, Silver Fins, <a href="/wiki/Ai_(singer)" title="Ai (singer)">AI</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chemistry_(band)" title="Chemistry (band)">CHEMISTRY</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two video games based on <i>Berserk</i> have been developed by <a href="/wiki/Yuke%27s" title="Yuke's">Yuke's</a>. The first game, <i><a href="/wiki/Sword_of_the_Berserk:_Guts%27_Rage" title="Sword of the Berserk: Guts' Rage">Sword of the Berserk: Guts' Rage</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ベルセルク 千年帝国の鷹篇 喪失花の章</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Beruseruku Sennen Teikoku No Taka Hen Wasurebana no Shō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, Berserk Millennium Falcon Arc: Chapter of the Lost Flowers)</span>, was released for the <a href="/wiki/Dreamcast" title="Dreamcast">Dreamcast</a> in Japan by <a href="/wiki/ASCII_Corporation" title="ASCII Corporation">ASCII Corporation</a> in late 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It was localized in western regions early the following year by <a href="/wiki/Eidos_Interactive" title="Eidos Interactive">Eidos Interactive</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The second game, <i><a href="/wiki/Berserk:_Millennium_Falcon_Hen_Seima_Senki_no_Sh%C5%8D" title="Berserk: Millennium Falcon Hen Seima Senki no Shō">Berserk: Millennium Falcon Hen Seima Senki no Shō</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ベルセルク 千年帝国の鷹篇 聖魔戦記の章</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Beruseruku Sennen Teikoku No Taka Hen Seima Senki no Shō</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>, Berserk Millennium Falcon Arc: Chapter of the Record of the Holy Demon War)</span>, was published by <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Corporation" title="Sammy Corporation">Sammy Corporation</a> exclusively in Japan on the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> A <i>Berserk</i>-themed spinoff of <a href="/wiki/Koei_Tecmo#Omega_Force" title="Koei Tecmo">Omega Force</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Dynasty_Warriors" title="Dynasty Warriors">Dynasty Warriors</a> series, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Berserk_and_the_Band_of_the_Hawk" title="Berserk and the Band of the Hawk">Berserk and the Band of the Hawk</a></i> <span style="font-weight: normal">(<span class="t_nihongo_kanji" lang="ja">ベルセルク無双</span><span class="t_nihongo_comma" style="display:none">,</span> <i>Berserk Musou</i><sup class="t_nihongo_help noprint"><a href="/wiki/Help:Installing_Japanese_character_sets" title="Help:Installing Japanese character sets"><span class="t_nihongo_icon" style="color: #00e; font: bold 80% sans-serif; text-decoration: none; padding: 0 .1em;">?</span></a></sup>)</span> has been announced for a 2016 release for <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">PlayStation 4</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Vita" title="PlayStation Vita">PlayStation Vita</a> and PC via <a href="/wiki/Steam" title="Steam">Steam</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_merchandise">Other merchandise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other merchandise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In addition to video games and game guidebooks, <i>Berserk</i> has spurred on a range of different <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merchandise" title="Merchandise">merchandise</a>, from lighters to keyrings to <a href="/wiki/Kubrick_(toy)" title="Kubrick (toy)">Kubrick</a>. Statues and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Action_figures" title="Action figures">action figures</a> are produced by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Art_of_War_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Art of War (company) (page does not exist)">Art of War</a>. A Berserk trading card game was released by <a href="/wiki/Konami" title="Konami">Konami</a> in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> A figure of Guts was released by Max Factory in its Figma line in January 2012.</p>
<p>A new anime project was announced in December 2015, and a preview was shown at NBC Universal's Comiket Winter booth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berserk_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of July 2015, the manga has sold 27 million volumes in Japan and 8 million overseas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> As of January 2016, reported 40 million volumes have been sold worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The 1988 Prototype chapter won a prize at the Comi Manga School Miura attended and the main series won the Tezuka Osamu Award for Excellence at the sixth installment of <a href="/wiki/Tezuka_Osamu_Cultural_Prize" title="Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prize">Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prize</a> in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
