<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Parc de Bercy</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Parc_Bercy.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Parc_Bercy.JPG/250px-Parc_Bercy.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Parc_Bercy.JPG/375px-Parc_Bercy.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Parc_Bercy.JPG/500px-Parc_Bercy.JPG 2x" width="250"/></a>

<p><b>Parc de Bercy</b> is a public park located along the right bank of the <a href="/wiki/Seine" title="Seine">Seine</a> in the <a href="/wiki/12th_arrondissement_of_Paris" title="12th arrondissement of Paris">12th arrondissement of Paris</a>. It was created in 1994-1997 as one of the major architectural projects of French President <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Mitterrand" title="François Mitterrand">François Mitterrand</a> on the site of a former wine depot. With a combined area of 13.9 <a href="/wiki/Hectare" title="Hectare">hectares</a>, composed of three different gardens on different themes connected by foot bridges, it is the tenth largest park in the city. It is accessible by <a href="/wiki/Bercy_(Paris_M%C3%A9tro)" title="Bercy (Paris Métro)">Bercy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cour_Saint-%C3%89milion_(Paris_M%C3%A9tro)" title="Cour Saint-Émilion (Paris Métro)">Cour Saint-Émilion</a> Métro stations, and by a foot bridge to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Library_of_France" title="National Library of France">National Library of France</a> on the other side of the river.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_three_gardens">The three gardens</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parc_de_Bercy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The three gardens">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Paris_12th.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="856" data-file-width="1200" height="157" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Paris_12th.png/220px-Paris_12th.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Paris_12th.png/330px-Paris_12th.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Paris_12th.png/440px-Paris_12th.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Paris_12th.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The 12th arrondissement, with the park by the river Seine</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The park is made up of three gardens designed by architects <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bernard_Huet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bernard Huet (page does not exist)">Bernard Huet</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Madeleine_Ferrand&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Madeleine Ferrand (page does not exist)">Madeleine Ferrand</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jean-Pierre_Feugas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jean-Pierre Feugas (page does not exist)">Jean-Pierre Feugas</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bernard_Leroy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bernard Leroy (page does not exist)">Bernard Leroy</a>, and by landscapers <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ian_Le_Caisne&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ian Le Caisne (page does not exist)">Ian Le Caisne</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Philippe_Raguin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Philippe Raguin (page does not exist)">Philippe Raguin</a> between 1993 and 1997:</p>
<ul>
<li>The "Romantic Garden", which includes fishponds and dunes;</li>
<li>The "Flowerbeds", dedicated to plant life;</li>
<li>"The Meadows", an area of open lawns shaded by tall trees.</li>
</ul>
<p>In the north-east of the park stands the <a href="/wiki/Cin%C3%A9math%C3%A8que_Fran%C3%A7aise" title="Cinémathèque Française">Cinémathèque Française</a> (the former American Center) designed by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Gehry" title="Frank Gehry">Frank Gehry</a>, and on the raised terraces are the 21 sculptures of <a href="/wiki/Rachid_Khimoune" title="Rachid Khimoune">Rachid Khimoune</a>'s "Children of the World" installation, created in 2001 to honour children's rights.</p>
<p>The park is linked directly to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Library_of_France" title="National Library of France">National Library of France</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Passerelle_Simone-de-Beauvoir" title="Passerelle Simone-de-Beauvoir">Simone de Beauvoir footbridge</a> over the Seine.</p>
<p>The area used to be an important location for Paris wine warehouses, and some remains of the industry can still be seen in the park.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9e_des_Arts_Forains" title="Musée des Arts Forains">Musée des Arts Forains</a> is also located here.</p>
