<p><b>Andriy Mykhailovych Danylko</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Андрі́й Миха́йлович Дани́лко</span>; born 2 October 1973), better known for his <a href="/wiki/Drag_queen" title="Drag queen">drag</a> stage persona <b>Verka Serduchka</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Вєрка Сердючка</span>), is a <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukrainian</a> comedian and <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance</a> singer. Danylko represented <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2007" title="Ukraine in the Eurovision Song Contest 2007">Ukraine in the Eurovision Song Contest 2007</a> as Verka Serduchka and finished in second place. Serduchka has sold over 600,000 records during her career in Ukraine.</p>
<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Character_actor" title="Character actor">character actor</a>, Danylko has also appeared in over a dozen <a href="/wiki/Television_film" title="Television film">television films</a> and is also known for appearing as Serduchka in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Spy_(2015_film)" title="Spy (2015 film)">Spy</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Melissa_McCarthy" title="Melissa McCarthy">Melissa McCarthy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rose_Byrne" title="Rose Byrne">Rose Byrne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Hart" title="Miranda Hart">Miranda Hart</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Andriy Danylko was born to a <a href="/wiki/Surzhyk" title="Surzhyk">Surzhyk</a>-speaking working-class family in <a href="/wiki/Poltava" title="Poltava">Poltava</a>. In 1980, at seven, he lost his father to lung cancer. An avid amateur performer since childhood, he graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Kiev_Municipal_Academy_of_Variety_and_Circus_Arts" title="Kiev Municipal Academy of Variety and Circus Arts">Kiev State College of Circus and Variety Arts</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Danylko_Theater"><i>Danylko Theater</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Danylko Theater">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1990, Andriy Danylko began to create the character of Verka Serduchka, a flamboyant middle-aged woman from a rural family, working as a railroad <a href="/wiki/Sleeping_car" title="Sleeping car">sleeping car</a> attendant. Danylko presented Serduchka publicly for the first time in a <a href="/wiki/Poltava" title="Poltava">Poltava</a> comedy competition on January 4, 1991. He invented the character's name by combining the randomly picked first name Verka and the last name of a former school classmate, Anya Serduk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-why_serduk_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-why_serduk-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Danylko later gave his Serduchka character a "mother," played by actress Inna Bilokon, who had been Danylko's close friend since school.</p>
<p>After Verka Serduchka, Danylko created other characters, such as a <a href="/wiki/Militsiya" title="Militsiya">police officer</a>, a soldier and a female ballet dancer. He established the troupe "Danylko Theater" to tour the cities of Russia and Ukraine. In 2002, Danylko, along with his group, toured in the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">CIS</a> and Baltic countries with the program "I am a Revolution" for the whole year. The following year he was awarded the title of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honored_Artist_of_Ukraine" title="Honored Artist of Ukraine">Honored Artist of Ukraine</a> soon after the Danylko Theater concert tour "I Was Born for Love".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_and_TV-movie_appearances">Television and TV-movie appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Television and TV-movie appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the late 1990s and early 2000s, Danylko hosted a <a href="/wiki/Talk_show" title="Talk show">talk show</a> called "SV-show" ("SV" is an abbreviation for <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Спальний Вагон, "Spalnyy Vagon"</span>, which means <i><a href="/wiki/Sleeping_car" title="Sleeping car">sleeping car</a></i>) on various Ukrainian television channels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, Danylko made his first appearance in one of several musicals produced for television, mostly in female comic portrayals based on the Serduchka character (see Filmography). Several of these productions were shown in the <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Eve" title="New Year's Eve">New Year's Eve</a> broadcasts of <a href="/wiki/Russia-1" title="Russia-1">Russia-1</a> television.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pianist_and_composer">Pianist and composer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pianist and composer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Apart from pop and dance performances as Serduchka, Danylko has also performed <a href="/wiki/Ambient_music" title="Ambient music">ambient</a> musical compositions under his real name, including the 2005 album После тебя (<i>Posle tebya</i>: <i>After you</i>).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eurovision_Song_Contest_2007">Eurovision Song Contest 2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Eurovision Song Contest 2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</div>
<p>Verka Serduchka was chosen to <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2007" title="Ukraine in the Eurovision Song Contest 2007">represent Ukraine</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2007" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2007">Eurovision Song Contest 2007</a> in <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, with the song "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_Lasha_Tumbai" title="Dancing Lasha Tumbai">Dancing Lasha Tumbai</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-podrobnosti_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-podrobnosti-4">[4]</a></sup> One of Ukraine's nationwide FM radio stations organized a protest action in February 2007 to express their disapproval of the selection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Some Ukrainians and even members of the <a href="/wiki/Verkhovna_Rada" title="Verkhovna Rada">Ukrainian Parliament</a> also expressed their disapproval, viewing the character of Serduchka as "grotesque and vulgar".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Serduchka's song "Dancing Lasha Tumbai" was sung in four languages: <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>. The invented words "lasha tumbai" caused some controversy after the performance, as many people noted the phrase's similarity to "Russian goodbye". In early publicity appearances, Serduchka explained that "lasha tumbai" was a <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_language" title="Mongolian language">Mongolian</a> expression for "whipped cream." His statement was denied by several Mongols who gave the correct phrase on a <a href="/wiki/Talk_show" title="Talk show">talk show</a> broadcast by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Channel_One_(Russia)" title="Channel One (Russia)">Channel One (Russia)</a> just before the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2007" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2007">Eurovision Song Contest 2007</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The Mongolian embassy in Moscow also said that "lasha tumbai" was total <a href="/wiki/Gibberish" title="Gibberish">gibberish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The "Dancing Lasha Tumbai" single reached #6 in the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> charts and went on to be #28 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Official_UK_Singles_Chart" title="The Official UK Singles Chart">The Official UK Singles Chart</a> on 20 May 2007. This was the first time a non-UK non-winning Eurovision entry had made UK charts since 1974.</p>
<p>Danylko later made a brief phone-in appearance on the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Graham_Norton_Show" title="The Graham Norton Show">The Graham Norton Show</a>,</i> in which his comments made no sense whatsoever. Another call was made, but with a translator in the audience. In this instance, Danylko appeared to tell guest <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Lloyd_Webber" title="Andrew Lloyd Webber">Andrew Lloyd Webber</a> he was "rubbish". However, in an interview with Andrew Williams in the 60 Seconds column of metro.co.uk (30 July 2007), Danylko clarified his meaning:</p>
<p>AW: Why did you call Andrew Lloyd Webber ‘rubbish’ on The Graham Norton Show? AD: Someone interpreted Serduchka wrongly. I meant I watched "Cats: the Musical" on TV. It was boring. You should watch a musical on the stage. Don't watch "Cats" on TV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the same interview, he stated that "lasha tumbai" was a somewhat made-up phrase that sounds like the Mongolian phrase for "milkshake" and that many Russians nevertheless managed to interpret it as saying "Russia goodbye!".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 2011, a writer from British newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> described "Dancing Lasha Tumbai" as the "best song never to win Eurovision" before going on to describe Serduchka as "an oven-ready <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Biggins" title="Christopher Biggins">Christopher Biggins</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_life">Political life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Political life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Danylko announced in 2007 that he would front a new political party, "For Ours!", to run in that year's <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_parliamentary_election,_2007" title="Ukrainian parliamentary election, 2007">Ukrainian parliamentary election</a>, but he later abandoned the idea. A public opinion poll in July 2007 placed 'For Ours! Verka Serduchka' at 2% overall support.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eurovision_2011">Eurovision 2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Eurovision 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Serduchka had planned to participate in the <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_in_the_Eurovision_Song_Contest_2011" title="Ukraine in the Eurovision Song Contest 2011">Ukrainian national selection</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2011" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2011">Eurovision Song Contest 2011</a>. He automatically qualified for the final,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but withdrew his nomination in October 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance_in_Spy">Appearance in <i>Spy</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Appearance in Spy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Spy_(2015_film)" title="Spy (2015 film)">Spy (2015 film)</a></div>
<p>Danylko appeared as the character Verka Serduchka in a cameo role in the 2015 American action comedy movie <i><a href="/wiki/Spy_(2015_film)" title="Spy (2015 film)">Spy</a></i>, which premiered May 22, 2015 in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and June 4, 2015 in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>. In the scene, set in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, Serduchka's open-air performance of "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_Lasha_Tumbai" title="Dancing Lasha Tumbai">Dancing Lasha Tumbai</a>" is interrupted by CIA agents Susan Cooper (<a href="/wiki/Melissa_McCarthy" title="Melissa McCarthy">Melissa McCarthy</a>) and Rick Ford (<a href="/wiki/Jason_Statham" title="Jason Statham">Jason Statham</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eurovision_2016">Eurovision 2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Eurovision 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 2016 telecast of the <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_2016" title="Eurovision Song Contest 2016">Eurovision Song Contest</a>, Serduchka announced the results from the Ukrainian jury. Bilokon, portraying Serduchka's mother, accompanied Danylko.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2003, Danylko was honored with the title <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Artist_of_Ukraine" title="People's Artist of Ukraine">People's Artist of Ukraine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verka_Serduchka&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013, Danylko bought a <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Motors" title="Rolls-Royce Motors">Rolls-Royce</a> that once belonged to <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a>, with plans to donate it to a museum devoted to the group <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>Danylko has said in interviews that he does not engage in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transvestite" title="Transvestite">transvestitism</a> outside of his public performances, with a distinction between his stage persona and his personal life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He is reluctant to discuss his private affairs beyond this, though he has stated in interviews that he had a difficult relationship with a woman with whom he lived with for 8 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In January 2015, photos of Danylko intimately kissing Inna Bilokon were posted online by a friend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
