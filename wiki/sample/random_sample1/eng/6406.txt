<p><b>Berke Khan</b> (died 1266) (also <b>Birkai</b>; <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_language" title="Mongolian language">Mongolian</a>: <span lang="mn" xml:lang="mn">Бэрх хаан</span>, <a href="/wiki/Tatar_language" title="Tatar language">Tatar</a>: <span lang="tt" xml:lang="tt">Бәркә хан</span>) was the <a href="/wiki/Ruler" title="Ruler">ruler</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Horde" title="Golden Horde">Golden Horde</a> (division of the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongol Empire</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> who effectively consolidated the power of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_Horde" title="Blue Horde">Blue Horde</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_Horde" title="White Horde">White Horde</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> from 1257 to 1266. He succeeded his brother <a href="/wiki/Batu_Khan" title="Batu Khan">Batu Khan</a> of the Blue Horde (West) and was responsible for the first official establishment of Islam in a khanate of the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_Empire" title="Mongol Empire">Mongol Empire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He allied with the Egyptian <a href="/wiki/Mamluk" title="Mamluk">Mamluks</a> against another Mongol khanate based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persia" title="Persia">Persia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ilkhanate" title="Ilkhanate">Ilkhanate</a>. Berke supported <a href="/wiki/Ariq_B%C3%B6ke" title="Ariq Böke">Ariq Böke</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Toluid_Civil_War" title="Toluid Civil War">Toluid Civil War</a>, but did not intervene militarily in the war.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Berke is a name used by both <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turkic peoples</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mongols" title="Mongols">Mongols</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_language" title="Mongolian language">Mongolian language</a> "Berkh" literally means "strong". "Berk" also has many meanings like "solid", "powerful", and "in power" in the <a href="/wiki/Old_Turkic_language" title="Old Turkic language">Old Turkic language</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Berke was one of the sons of <a href="/wiki/Jochi" title="Jochi">Jochi</a>, the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Genghis_Khan" title="Genghis Khan">Genghis Khan</a>. Berke was present, with several of his brothers, at the inauguration of his uncle <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%96gedei" title="Ögedei">Ögedei</a> as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Khan" title="Great Khan">Great Khan</a> in 1229.</p>
<p>In 1236, Berke joined his brothers <a href="/wiki/Orda_Khan" title="Orda Khan">Orda</a>, Sinkur, and Siban and an assortment of cousins under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Batu_Khan" title="Batu Khan">Batu Khan</a>. The vast army, comprising some 150,000 soldiers, marched from <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> and into the territory of the Muslim <a href="/wiki/Volga_Bulgaria" title="Volga Bulgaria">Volga Bulgars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kipchaks" title="Kipchaks">Kipchaks</a>, whom they subdued. Batu and <a href="/wiki/Subutai" title="Subutai">Subutai</a> sent Berke to the country north of the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> to conquer the Kipchaks there. Next they devastated the principalities of <a href="/wiki/Ryazan" title="Ryazan">Ryazan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suzdal" title="Suzdal">Suzdal</a> in 1237, and marched further into Russia. During the winter of 1238-39, Berke defeated the Kipchaks and imprisoned the chief of the <a href="/wiki/Merkit" title="Merkit">Merkits</a>. He afterwards subdued the steppe watered by the <a href="/wiki/Kuma_River_(Russia)" title="Kuma River (Russia)">Kuma</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Terek_River" title="Terek River">Terek</a>.</p>
<p>Berke further served under his brother during the invasion of Europe, fighting at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Mohi" title="Battle of the Mohi">Battle of the Mohi</a>, where the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Hungarian</a> army was decimated. When <a href="/wiki/%C3%96gedei_Khan" title="Ögedei Khan">Ögedei Khan</a> died, and all the princes of the blood were summoned to return to <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a> to select a Great Khan, Berke and his brothers joined Batu in his bid for power. When that failed, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kipchak_Khanate" title="Kipchak Khanate">Kipchak Khanate</a> settled on the territories, which are now parts of Russia and Kazakhstan, and looked east to defend themselves against their cousins.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conversion_to_Islam">Conversion to Islam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Conversion to Islam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Berke Khan adopted Islam in the city of Bukhara. When he was at <a href="/wiki/Saray-J%C3%BCk" title="Saray-Jük">Saray-Jük</a>, Berke met a <a href="/wiki/Caravan_(travellers)" title="Caravan (travellers)">caravan</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bukhara" title="Bukhara">Bukhara</a> and questioned them about their faith. Berke was convinced to convert by the caravan travellers and became a Muslim. Berke then persuaded his brother Tukh-timur to convert to <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> as well.</p>
<p>In 1248 Batu sent Berke, along with his brother Tukh-timur, to Mongolia in order to install <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6ngke_Khan" title="Möngke Khan">Möngke Khan</a> on the throne of Great Khan. When he arrived, he invited the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chagatai_khans" title="Chagatai khans">Chagatai</a> and Ogedeyd families several times. That is why, Berke conducted the <a href="/wiki/Kurultai" title="Kurultai">kurultai</a> in 1251 and had Möngke enthroned. Berke organized everything under strict conditions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Assuming_the_Golden_Horde">Assuming the Golden Horde</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Assuming the Golden Horde">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Golden_Horde_1389.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="684" data-file-width="962" height="284" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Golden_Horde_1389.svg/400px-Golden_Horde_1389.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Golden_Horde_1389.svg/600px-Golden_Horde_1389.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Golden_Horde_1389.svg/800px-Golden_Horde_1389.svg.png 2x" width="400"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Golden_Horde_1389.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The domains of the Golden Horde in 1389. The gold star shows the location of New <a href="/wiki/Sarai_(city)" title="Sarai (city)">Sarai</a>, capital of the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Horde" title="Golden Horde">Golden Horde</a>.</div>

<p>When Batu died in 1255, he was briefly succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Sartaq_Khan" title="Sartaq Khan">Sartak</a>, before Berke assumed leadership in 1257. He was an able ruler and succeeded in maintaining and stabilizing the Golden Horde, western khanate of Mongol Empire. During his government, the Mongols finally defeated the rebellion of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danylo_of_Halych" title="Danylo of Halych">Danylo of Halych</a> and made a <a href="/wiki/Second_Mongol_invasion_of_Poland" title="Second Mongol invasion of Poland">second attack against Poland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>, led by general <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burundai" title="Burundai">Burundai</a> (Lublin, Zawichost, Sandomierz, Kraków and Bytom were plundered) in 1259. Also in 1265 there was a raid against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> and Byzantine <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a>. Michael of <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> also sent much valuable fabric to the Golden Horde as a tribute thereafter.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Berke-Hulagu_War">Berke-Hulagu War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Berke-Hulagu War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Berke%E2%80%93Hulagu_war" title="Berke–Hulagu war">Berke–Hulagu war</a></div>
<p>Berke soon became a devout Muslim. His conversion resulted in the Blue Horde becoming primarily Muslim, although there were still animists and Buddhists among them. Berke had a deadly determination to deal with <a href="/wiki/Hulagu_Khan" title="Hulagu Khan">Hulagu Khan</a>, who had murdered the Caliph <a href="/wiki/Al-Musta%27sim" title="Al-Musta'sim">Al-Musta'sim</a>, and whose territorial ambitions in Syria and Egypt threatened Berke's fellow Muslims.</p>
<p>In the meantime, the Ilkhanates led by <a href="/wiki/Kitbuqa" title="Kitbuqa">Kitbuqa</a> had fallen out with the crusaders holding the coast of Palestine, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mamluks" title="Mamluks">Mamluks</a> were secured a pact of neutrality with them, pass through their territory, and destroy the Ilkhanate army at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ain_Jalut" title="Battle of Ain Jalut">Battle of Ain Jalut</a>. Kitbuqa was killed. <a href="/wiki/Palestine_(region)" title="Palestine (region)">Palestine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> were permanently lost, the border remaining the <a href="/wiki/Tigris" title="Tigris">Tigris</a> for the duration of Hulagu's dynasty. Berke's vow of vengeance against Hulagu had to wait until the latter's return to his lands after the death of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6ngke_Khan" title="Möngke Khan">Möngke Khan</a>.</p>
<p>Hulagu returned to his lands by 1262, but instead of being able to avenge his defeats, was drawn into civil war with Berke and the Blue Horde. Berke Khan had promised such a defeat in his rage after Hulagu's sack of Baghdad.</p>
<p>Muslim historian <a href="/wiki/Rashid-al-Din_Hamadani" title="Rashid-al-Din Hamadani">Rashid-al-Din Hamadani</a> quoted Berke Khan as telling his Mongols and Muslim subjects, in protest at the attack on Baghdad:</p>
<dl>
<dd><b>"He (Hulagu) has sacked all the cities of the <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a>, and has brought about the death of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caliph" title="Caliph">Caliph</a>. With the help of <a href="/wiki/Allah" title="Allah">God</a> I will call him to account for so much innocent blood."</b> (see <i>The Mongol Warlords</i>, quoting Rashid al-Din's record of Berke Khan's pronouncement; this quote is also found in <i>The Mamluk-Ilkhanid War</i>)</dd>
</dl>
<p>Before his succession, he also complained to Batu "We helped Möngke to enthrone. But he forgot who the enemy is or friend is. Now, he is starving the lands of our friend Caliph. It is abject". It is notable that Berke Khan kept his promise, allying himself with the Mamluks, (Berke sought an alliance with the <a href="/wiki/Mamluk" title="Mamluk">Mamluk</a> sultan <a href="/wiki/Baibars" title="Baibars">Baibars</a> against Hulagu) and when Hulagu returned to his lands in 1262, after the succession was finally settled with Kublai as the last Great Khan, and massed his armies to avenge Ain Jalut and attack the Mamluks, Berke Khan initiated a series of raids in force which drew Hulagu north to meet him. This was the first open conflict between Mongols, and signalled the end of the unified empire.</p>
<p>But the reasons for the conflict between Berke and Hulagu was not only religion. It was territory. Möngke Khan gave <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, which had been given to Jochi by <a href="/wiki/Genghis_Khan" title="Genghis Khan">Genghis Khan</a>, to his brother Hulagu. Although Berke did not like the situation, he was patient till Möngke's death.</p>
<p>Berke at first desisted from fighting Hulagu out of Mongol brotherhood, he said <i>Mongols are killed by Mongol swords. If we were united, then we would have conquered all of the world.</i> but the economic situation of the Golden Horde due to the actions of the Ilkhanate led him to declare jihad because of the Ilkhanids domination of the wealth of North Iran, and the Ilkhanate's demands for the Golden Horde to not sell slaves to the Mamluks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Elverskog2011_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Elverskog2011-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1262 the conflict turned into open war. Hulagu Khan suffered a severe defeat in an attempted invasion north of the Caucasus in 1263. Hulagu's forces were crushed at the <a href="/wiki/Terek_River" title="Terek River">Terek</a> river by Berke's nephew Nogai, forcing Hulagu into retreat; he died in 1265. Also Chagatai Khan <a href="/wiki/Alghu" title="Alghu">Alghu</a> invaded Khwarizm and annexed Golden Horde lands. Jochid army unsuccessfully tried to halt his advance.</p>
<p>Berke also supported Great Khan claimant <a href="/wiki/Ariq_B%C3%B6ke" title="Ariq Böke">Ariq Böke</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Toluid_Civil_War" title="Toluid Civil War">Toluid Civil War</a>, and he minted coins in the name of Ariq Böke. However Kublai defeated Ariq Böke by 1264. Kublai called both Hulagu and Berke to discuss Ariq Böke. However, both of them noted that they could not come to the Kurultai at the moment, and the new Kurultai was never held.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As Berke sought to cross the Kura river to attack Hulagu's son, <a href="/wiki/Abaqa_Khan" title="Abaqa Khan">Abaqa Khan</a>, he fell ill and died sometime between 1266 and 1267.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He was succeeded by his grandnephew, <a href="/wiki/Mengu-Timur" title="Mengu-Timur">Mengu-Timur</a>. The policy of alliance with the Mamluks, and containment of the Ilkhanate, was continued by Mengu-Timur. Many historians<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> are in agreement that the intervention by Berke against Hulagu saved the remainder of the "Holy Land", including Mecca and Jerusalem, from the same fate as Baghdad.</p>
