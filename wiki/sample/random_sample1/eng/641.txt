<p>Year <b>517</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DXVII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Common year starting on Sunday">common year starting on Sunday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Agapitus and Paulus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1270 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 517 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=517&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=517&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Sigismund_of_Burgundy" title="Sigismund of Burgundy">Sigismund of Burgundy</a> is opposed by his son Sigeric and has him <a href="/wiki/Strangling" title="Strangling">strangled</a>. Overcome with remorse, he retreats to the <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monastery</a> that he founded, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Maurice%27s_Abbey" title="St. Maurice's Abbey">St. Maurice's Abbey</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=517&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Wu_of_Liang" title="Emperor Wu of Liang">Wu Di</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liang_Dynasty" title="Liang Dynasty">Liang Dynasty</a> becomes a <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhist</a>, and introduces the new <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religion</a> to central <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. He demands that <a href="/wiki/Sacrifice" title="Sacrifice">sacrifices</a> to imperial ancestors be changed to using dried meat, instead of the traditional animals (<a href="/wiki/Goat" title="Goat">goats</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pig" title="Pig">pigs</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cow" title="Cow">cows</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=517&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=517&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Council_of_Epaone" title="Council of Epaone">Council of Epaone</a>: <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">Bishops</a> of southern <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a> convene near Epao (present <a href="/wiki/Anneyron" title="Anneyron">Anneyron</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Burgundy" title="Kingdom of Burgundy">Burgundy</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Synod" title="Synod">synod</a> enacts the first legislation against wooden <a href="/wiki/Altar" title="Altar">altars</a>, forbidding the building of any but stone altars with <a href="/wiki/Chrism" title="Chrism">chrism</a> (a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Holy_anointing_oil" title="Holy anointing oil">oil</a> and spice).</li>
</ul>
