<p><b>Bernhard "Bernd" Kannenberg</b> (born 20 August 1942) is a retired <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West German</a> race walker who won the 50 km event at the <a href="/wiki/1972_Summer_Olympics" title="1972 Summer Olympics">1972 Summer Olympics</a>. He also competed in the 20 km at the 1972 and 1976 Games, but failed to finish. In 1972 Kannenberg became the first German athlete to walk 50 km within 4 hours; the same year he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Silbernes_Lorbeerblatt" title="Silbernes Lorbeerblatt">Silbernes Lorbeerblatt</a>. During his career he set five world records, and won six outdoor and four indoor West German titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kannenberg was a professional soldier and trained at the sports school of the German <a href="/wiki/Bundeswehr" title="Bundeswehr">Bundeswehr</a> at <a href="/wiki/Warendorf" title="Warendorf">Warendorf</a>. After retiring from competitions he coached the German race walking team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-r1-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
