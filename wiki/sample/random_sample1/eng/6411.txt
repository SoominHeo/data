<p><b>Prince Bernhard's titi</b> (<i>Callicebus bernhardi</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Titi" title="Titi">titi</a>, a type of <a href="/wiki/New_World_monkey" title="New World monkey">New World monkey</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endemic_(ecology)" title="Endemic (ecology)">endemic</a> to <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. It was formally described in 2002 by <a href="/wiki/Marc_van_Roosmalen" title="Marc van Roosmalen">Marc van Roosmalen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russell_Mittermeier" title="Russell Mittermeier">Russell Mittermeier</a>, and named after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bernhard_of_Lippe-Biesterfeld" title="Bernhard of Lippe-Biesterfeld">Prince Bernard</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Locals were shown a photograph of the monkey and asked if they knew of its whereabouts, it was referred to as a "Zog-Zog".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> This titi has dark orange sideburns and chest, a reddish-brown back, and a black tail with a white tip.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EllisNTB_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-EllisNTB-6">[6]</a></sup> It is about 94 centimeters (37Â in) in length, although 56 centimeters (22Â in) is taken up by its tail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EllisNTB_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-EllisNTB-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
