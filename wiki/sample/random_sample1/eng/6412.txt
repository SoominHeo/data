<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Internationale Funkausstellung Berlin</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>IFA</b> or <b>Internationale Funkausstellung Berlin</b> (International radio exhibition Berlin, a.k.a. 'Berlin Radio Show') is one of the oldest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_exhibition" title="Industrial exhibition">industrial exhibitions</a> in Germany. Between 1924 and 1939 it was an annual event, but as from 1950 it was organized on a two yearly basis until 2005. Since then it has become an annual event again, held in September. Today it is one of world's leading trade shows for consumer electronics and home appliances.</p>
<p>It offers the opportunity to exhibitors to present their latest products and developments to the general public. As a result of daily reporting in almost all the German media, the radio exhibition achieves a large spreading of the information – and advertising messages and has also become international. In the course of its history, a large number of world innovations were first seen at the exhibition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gramo1189206_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gramo1189206-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Internationale_Funkausstellung_Berlin&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> IFA is “Europe’s biggest tech show”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> 245,000 visitors and 1,645 exhibitors attended IFA 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Internationale_Funkausstellung_Berlin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10300,_Berlin,_Einstein_auf_der_Funkausstellung.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="551" data-file-width="800" height="152" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10300%2C_Berlin%2C_Einstein_auf_der_Funkausstellung.jpg/220px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10300%2C_Berlin%2C_Einstein_auf_der_Funkausstellung.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10300%2C_Berlin%2C_Einstein_auf_der_Funkausstellung.jpg/330px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10300%2C_Berlin%2C_Einstein_auf_der_Funkausstellung.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10300%2C_Berlin%2C_Einstein_auf_der_Funkausstellung.jpg/440px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10300%2C_Berlin%2C_Einstein_auf_der_Funkausstellung.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_102-10300,_Berlin,_Einstein_auf_der_Funkausstellung.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Einstein opens the IFA in 1930</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>German physicist and inventor <a href="/wiki/Manfred_von_Ardenne" title="Manfred von Ardenne">Manfred von Ardenne</a> gave a public demonstration of a television system using a <a href="/wiki/Cathode_ray_tube" title="Cathode ray tube">cathode ray tube</a> for both transmission (using flying-spot image scans, not a camera) and reception, at the 1931 show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1933 The <a href="/wiki/Volksempf%C3%A4nger" title="Volksempfänger">Volksempfänger</a> (VE 301 W),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nazi" title="Nazi">Nazi</a>-sponsored radio receiver design, was introduced. Ordered by Dr. <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Goebbels" title="Joseph Goebbels">Joseph Goebbels</a>, designed by <a href="/wiki/Otto_Griessing" title="Otto Griessing">Otto Griessing</a>, sold by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gustav_Seibt&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gustav Seibt (page does not exist)">Gustav Seibt</a>, it was presented at the tenth Berliner Funkausstellung on 18 August 1933,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> its price fixed at 76 <a href="/wiki/Reichsmark" title="Reichsmark">Reichsmark</a> (RM). 100,000 units were sold during the exhibition.</p>
<p>In 1938 the DKE 38 (<i>Deutscher Kleinempfänger 38</i>, i.e. German miniature receiver 1938) followed, the price fixed at 35 RM.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/AEG" title="AEG">AEG</a>, founded in 1883 by <a href="/wiki/Emil_Rathenau" title="Emil Rathenau">Emil Rathenau</a>, showed the first practical audio <a href="/wiki/Tape_recorder" title="Tape recorder">tape recorder</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Magnetophon" title="Magnetophon">Magnetophon</a> K1, at the August 1935 show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mix9106_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-mix9106-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1939 the exhibition was called <i>Grosse Deutsche Funk- und Fernseh-Ausstellung</i> (Great German Radio and Television Exhibition). The <i><a href="/wiki/Einheitsempf%C3%A4nger" title="Einheitsempfänger">Einheits-Fernseh-Empfänger E1</a></i>, a TV set designed to be affordable for everybody, was introduced. The physical display size was 7.68" × 8.86". Plans for large-scale manufacture were thwarted by the outbreak of World War II. Color TV was also introduced (a prototype), based on an invention by Werner Flechsig (cf. <a href="/wiki/Shadow_mask" title="Shadow mask">shadow mask</a>).</p>
<p>Multinational Dutch electronics corporation <a href="/wiki/Philips" title="Philips">Philips</a> introduced the <a href="/wiki/Compact_Cassette" title="Compact Cassette">compact audio cassette</a> medium for <a href="/wiki/Sound_recording_and_reproduction" title="Sound recording and reproduction">audio storage</a> and the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cassette_recorder" title="Cassette recorder">cassette recorder</a> (the Philips EL3300), developed by ir. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lou_Ottens&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lou Ottens (page does not exist)">Lou Ottens</a> and his team at the Philips factory in <a href="/wiki/Hasselt" title="Hasselt">Hasselt</a>, at the 1963 show, on Friday 30 August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
