<p>The <b>Beuth University of Applied Sciences Berlin</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> <i>Beuth Hochschule für Technik Berlin</i>) also: <i>Beuth Hochschule</i> or <i>BHT Berlin</i> ( formerly known as <i>Technische Fachhochschule Berlin</i>) is the second largest <a href="/wiki/Fachhochschule" title="Fachhochschule">University of Applied Sciences</a> in Berlin, Germany. There are around 12,000 students studying at BHT in more than 70 majors and 795 employees, under which there are 291 professors and 43 guest lecturers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BHT_Facts_and_Figures_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-BHT_Facts_and_Figures-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beuth_University_of_Applied_Sciences_Berlin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The BHT traces its roots back to the establishment of the Royal Academy for Gardening (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> <i>Königliche Gärtner-Lehranstalt</i>) in 1823.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1878 it was expanded into the Academy of Architectural Engineering followed and then into the <i>Erste Handwerkerschule Berlin</i> in 1878. Two years later the university of applied sciences for carpenters and bricklayers was established.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1909 the <i>Beuth Academy for Engineering Berlin</i> was established. Followed by several smaller institutes and academies across the metropolitan area such as the <i>Baugewerkschule Neukölln</i> in 1919 and the <i>School of Engineering</i> the institution was upgraded amongst the years.</p>
<p>The present university of applied sciences called <i>Technische Fachhochschule Berlin</i> was established in 1971 when several faculties such as <a href="/wiki/Food_technology" title="Food technology">food technology</a> were also integrated. Those faculties have since then been accommodated at the new <a href="/wiki/Campus" title="Campus">campus</a> at the <i>Luxemburger Straße</i> in Berlin. On January 17, 2008 the academic general assembly decided to change the name of the institution into <i>Beuth Hochschule für Technik Berlin</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The new name refers to <a href="/wiki/Christian_Peter_Wilhelm_Beuth" title="Christian Peter Wilhelm Beuth">Christian Peter Wilhelm Beuth</a> ,a former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prussian" title="Prussian">Prussian</a> ministry representative who is being considered to be the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spiritual_father" title="Spiritual father">spiritual father</a> of engineering training in Germany because he was in charge of establishing the <i>Technisches Institut</i> in Berlin in 1821.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <i>Beuth Hochschule</i> is divided into several <a href="/wiki/Academic_department" title="Academic department">departments</a> instead of <a href="/wiki/Faculty_(division)" title="Faculty (division)">faculties</a> like general public universities. Currently there are eight departments with up to nine study courses.</p>
<p>The <i>BHT</i> is the first educational <a href="/wiki/Institution" title="Institution">institution</a> in Germany that offers its students to pursue a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor" title="Bachelor">Bachelor</a> or <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">Master</a> degree. It is also the only public institution in Germany that offers people to study theater engineering and event technology and management.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Educational_partnerships">Educational partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beuth_University_of_Applied_Sciences_Berlin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Educational partnerships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i>BHT</i> maintains a co-operative programme featuring national companies such as the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bahn" title="Deutsche Bahn">Deutsche Bahn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burger_King" title="Burger King">Burger King</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_promotion_agency" title="Berlin promotion agency">Berlin promotion agency</a> and <a href="/wiki/Audi" title="Audi">Audi</a>. The programme enables students to gain practical work experience via a co-operative education programme on-the-job.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics">Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beuth_University_of_Applied_Sciences_Berlin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Athletics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>BHT</i> maintains a very famous institute for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collegiate_sports" title="Collegiate sports">collegiate sports</a>. It is also famous for being the Olympic Support Centre of Berlin. The faculties include a training centre for public <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Security_guards" title="Security guards">security guards</a>.</p>
