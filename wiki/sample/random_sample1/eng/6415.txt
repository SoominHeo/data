<p>The <b>Treaty of Berlin</b> was the final act of the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Berlin" title="Congress of Berlin">Congress of Berlin</a> (13 June – 13 July 1878), by which the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Third_Republic" title="French Third Republic">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> under Sultan <a href="/wiki/Abdul_Hamid_II" title="Abdul Hamid II">Abdul Hamid II</a> revised the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_San_Stefano" title="Treaty of San Stefano">Treaty of San Stefano</a> signed on 3 March the same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The most important task of the Congress was to decide the fate of the <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Bulgaria" title="Principality of Bulgaria">Principality of Bulgaria</a> established in the Treaty of San Stefano,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Krasner_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Krasner-2">[2]</a></sup> even though Bulgaria itself was excluded from participation in the talks at Russian insistence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Krasner_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Krasner-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> At the time, being non-existent on the world map, Bulgaria was not a subject of international law, neither were the Bulgarians themselves. This exclusion was already an established fact in the <a href="/wiki/Constantinople_Conference" title="Constantinople Conference">Constantinople Conference</a> of the Great Powers, held one year before without any Bulgarian participation. The most notable result of the conference is the <i>de jure</i> recognition of <i>de facto</i> independent states of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Principality_of_Romania" title="Principality of Romania">Romania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Serbia" title="Principality of Serbia">Serbia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Montenegro" title="Principality of Montenegro">Montenegro</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Berlin_(1878)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The treaty formally recognized the independence of the <i>de facto</i> sovereign principalities of <a href="/wiki/United_Principalities" title="United Principalities">Romania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Serbia" title="Principality of Serbia">Serbia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Montenegro" title="Principality of Montenegro">Montenegro</a>, together with the autonomy of Bulgaria - though the latter <i>de facto</i> functioned independently and was divided into three parts: the Principality of Bulgaria, the autonomous province of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Rumelia" title="Eastern Rumelia">Eastern Rumelia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Macedonia_(region)" title="Macedonia (region)">Macedonia</a>, which was given back to the Ottomans,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> thus undoing Russian plans for an independent—and Russophile—"<a href="/wiki/Greater_Bulgaria" title="Greater Bulgaria">Greater Bulgaria</a>". The Treaty of San Stefano had created a Bulgarian state, which was just what Great Britain and Austria-Hungary feared most.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Treaty of Berlin confirmed most of the Russian gains from the Ottoman Empire specified in the Treaty of San Stefano, although the valley of <a href="/wiki/Ele%C5%9Fkirt" title="Eleşkirt">Alashkerd</a> and the town of <a href="/wiki/Do%C4%9Fubeyaz%C4%B1t" title="Doğubeyazıt">Bayazid</a> were returned to the Ottomans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite the pleas of the Romanian delegates, Romania was forced to cede <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Bessarabia" title="Southern Bessarabia">southern Bessarabia</a> to the Russian Empire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hitchins_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hitchins-8">[8]</a></sup> As a compensation, Romania received <a href="/wiki/Dobruja" title="Dobruja">Dobruja</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Danube_Delta" title="Danube Delta">Danube Delta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hitchins_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hitchins-8">[8]</a></sup> The treaty also limited the Russian occupation of Bulgaria to 9 months, which limited the time during which Russian troops and supplies could be moved through Romanian territory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hitchins_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hitchins-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>The three newly independent states subsequently proclaimed themselves kingdoms: <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Romania" title="Kingdom of Romania">Romania</a> in 1881, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Serbia" title="Kingdom of Serbia">Serbia</a> in 1882 and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Montenegro" title="Kingdom of Montenegro">Montenegro</a> in 1910, while <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bulgaria" title="Kingdom of Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> proclaimed full independence in 1908 after uniting with <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Rumelia" title="Eastern Rumelia">Eastern Rumelia</a> in 1885. Austria-Hungary annexed Bosnia in 1908, sparking a <a href="/wiki/Bosnian_crisis" title="Bosnian crisis">major European crisis</a>.</p>
<p>The Treaty of Berlin accorded special legal status to some religious groups; it also served as a model for the <a href="/wiki/Minority_Treaties" title="Minority Treaties">Minorities System</a> that was subsequently established within the framework of the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It stipulated that Romania recognize non-Christians (Jews and Muslims) as full citizens. It also vaguely called for a border rectification between <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Greece" title="Kingdom of Greece">Greece</a> and the Ottoman Empire, which occurred after protracted negotiations in 1881 with the transfer of <a href="/wiki/Thessaly" title="Thessaly">Thessaly</a> to Greece.</p>
<p>In the "Salisbury Circular" of 1 April 1878, British Foreign Secretary, the Marquess of Salisbury, made clear his and his government's objections to the Treaty of San Stefano and the favorable position in which it left Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Historian <a href="/wiki/A._J._P._Taylor" title="A. J. P. Taylor">A. J. P. Taylor</a> wrote: "If the treaty of San Stefano had been maintained, both the Ottoman Empire and Austria-Hungary might have survived to the present day. The British, except for <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Disraeli" title="Benjamin Disraeli">Beaconsfield</a> in his wilder moments, had expected less and were therefore less disappointed. Salisbury wrote at the end of 1878: "We shall set up a rickety sort of Turkish rule again south of the Balkans. But it is a mere respite. There is no vitality left in them."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Kosovo_Vilayet" title="Kosovo Vilayet">Kosovo Vilayet</a> remained part of the Ottoman Empire. Austria-Hungary was allowed to station military garrisons in the Ottoman <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_Vilayet" title="Bosnia Vilayet">Vilayet of Bosnia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sanjak_of_Novi_Pazar" title="Sanjak of Novi Pazar">Sanjak of Novi Pazar</a>. The Vilayet of Bosnia was placed under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina_in_Austria-Hungary" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina in Austria-Hungary">Austro-Hungarian occupation</a>, though formally remaining a part of the Ottoman Empire until being <a href="/wiki/Bosnian_crisis" title="Bosnian crisis">annexed by Austria-Hungary in 1908</a>. The Austro-Hungarian garrisons in the Sanjak of Novi Pazar were withdrawn in 1908, following the annexation of the Vilayet of Bosnia and the resulting <a href="/wiki/Bosnian_crisis" title="Bosnian crisis">Bosnian crisis</a>, in order to reach a compromise with the Ottoman Empire (the Ottoman government was struggling with internal strife due to the <a href="/wiki/Young_Turk_Revolution" title="Young Turk Revolution">Young Turk Revolution</a> in 1908, which also paved the way for the <a href="/wiki/Bosnian_crisis" title="Bosnian crisis">loss of Bosnia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Declaration_of_Independence" title="Bulgarian Declaration of Independence">loss of Bulgaria</a> in the same year.)</p>
