<p><b>Berri-UQAM</b> is the central <a href="/wiki/Metro_station" title="Metro station">station</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Metro" title="Montreal Metro">Montreal Metro</a> <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit system</a> operated by the <a href="/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_de_transport_de_Montr%C3%A9al" title="Société de transport de Montréal">Société de transport de Montréal</a> (STM).</p>
<p>Berri-UQAM is located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quartier_Latin_(Montreal)" title="Quartier Latin (Montreal)">Quartier Latin</a>, in the borough of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ville-Marie_(Montreal)" title="Ville-Marie (Montreal)">Ville-Marie</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The station opened on October 14, 1966, as part of the original network of the Metro.</p>
<p>Berri-UQAM is the 2nd deepest station in the network, and the only one to have 3 lines stacked on each other. Berri-UQAM is also the busiest station in the network, transfers not included.</p>
<p>If transfers were included, the 13 million passengers number would rise to about 35-40 million a year. The <a href="/wiki/Green_Line_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Green Line (Montreal Metro)">Green</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orange_Line_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Orange Line (Montreal Metro)">Orange</a> Lines pass through Berri, and the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Line_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Yellow Line (Montreal Metro)">Yellow Line</a> terminates there.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berri-UQAM_(Montreal_Metro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Berri_UQAM_Metro_station3.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Berri_UQAM_Metro_station3.jpg/220px-Berri_UQAM_Metro_station3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Berri_UQAM_Metro_station3.jpg/330px-Berri_UQAM_Metro_station3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Berri_UQAM_Metro_station3.jpg/440px-Berri_UQAM_Metro_station3.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>Designed by Longpré and Marchand, the station serves three lines: the <a href="/wiki/Green_Line_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Green Line (Montreal Metro)">Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orange_Line_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Orange Line (Montreal Metro)">Orange</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Line_(Montreal_Metro)" title="Yellow Line (Montreal Metro)">Yellow</a> lines.</p>
<p>The main part of the station is a cruciform <a href="/wiki/Tunnel#Cut-and-cover" title="Tunnel">cut and cover</a> volume built underneath the intersection of rue Berri and <a href="/wiki/De_Maisonneuve_Boulevard" title="De Maisonneuve Boulevard">boul. de Maisonneuve</a>; the volume is so large that the station's design had to include massive pillars to support the street.</p>
<p>This central volume contains three levels. The upper level contains the rectangular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mezzanine_(architecture)" title="Mezzanine (architecture)">mezzanine</a> at its centre, with <a href="/wiki/Turnstile" title="Turnstile">fare gates</a> on all four sides; the arms extend out to the station's entrances, with two more entrances at the crossing, and are also lined with shops and services.</p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stairway" title="Stairway">Staircases</a> lead from the mezzanine to the landings on either side of the Orange Line. These landings provide views of the great volumes over the Green Line platforms below.</p>
<p>From the Green Line level, <a href="/wiki/Escalator" title="Escalator">escalators</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hallway" title="Hallway">hallways</a> connect the rest of the station to the Yellow Line terminus, built in a <a href="/wiki/Tunnel" title="Tunnel">tunnel</a> a block away under <a href="/wiki/Saint_Denis_Street" title="Saint Denis Street">rue Saint-Denis</a>. Due to limited space in the tail tracks, trains reverse both in the tail tracks and in the foretracks leaving the station.</p>
<p>All three lines have <a href="/wiki/Side_platform" title="Side platform">side platforms</a>. This station was the first to be equipped with the MétroVision information screens which displays news, commercials, and the time until the next train arrives.</p>
<p>In 2009, the station was made partially <a href="/wiki/Wheelchair" title="Wheelchair">wheelchair</a>-<a href="/wiki/Accessibility" title="Accessibility">accessible</a> through the addition of <a href="/wiki/Elevator" title="Elevator">elevators</a>. Two elevators now connect the Orange Line platforms to the mezzanine. The other two lines are not yet accessible. The mezzanine can be accessed from the street via elevators in the <a href="/wiki/Grande_Biblioth%C3%A8que_du_Qu%C3%A9bec" title="Grande Bibliothèque du Québec">Grande Bibliothèque du Québec</a> and <a href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_du_Qu%C3%A9bec_%C3%A0_Montr%C3%A9al" title="Université du Québec à Montréal">UQAM</a>'s Pavillon Judith-Jasmin; however, these buildings are not open throughout the Metro's operating hours. However, there is an elevator in the Saint-Denis exit that opened in June 2010.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture_and_art">Architecture and art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berri-UQAM_(Montreal_Metro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architecture and art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Berri-UQAM_Metro_station2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="3872" height="134" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Berri-UQAM_Metro_station2.jpg/200px-Berri-UQAM_Metro_station2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Berri-UQAM_Metro_station2.jpg/300px-Berri-UQAM_Metro_station2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Berri-UQAM_Metro_station2.jpg/400px-Berri-UQAM_Metro_station2.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Berri-UQAM_Metro_station2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The <i>Hommage aux fondateurs de la ville de Montréal</i> mural</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The station has a total of four independent exits: three integrated into buildings (the Berri, Saint-Denis, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Place_Dupuis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Place Dupuis (page does not exist)">Place Dupuis</a> exits) and one free-standing kiosk (the Sainte-Catherine exit). The station also contains several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Underground_city,_Montreal" title="Underground city, Montreal">underground city</a> connections, listed below.</p>
<p>The work of five artists is exhibited in this station. The largest work is a stained-glass mural by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pierre_Gaboriau&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pierre Gaboriau (page does not exist)">Pierre Gaboriau</a> and <a href="/wiki/Osterrath" title="Osterrath">Pierre Osterrath</a> entitled <i>Hommage aux fondateurs de la ville de Montréal</i> (homage to the founders of the city of Montreal). A gift of the Union régionale de Montréal des <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caisses_populaires_Desjardins" title="Caisses populaires Desjardins">caisses populaires Desjardins</a> and installed in 1969, it depicts <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_le_Royer_de_la_Dauversi%C3%A8re" title="Jérôme le Royer de la Dauversière">Jérôme le Royer de la Dauversière</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeanne_Mance" title="Jeanne Mance">Jeanne Mance</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paul_Chomedey_de_Maisonneuve" title="Paul Chomedey de Maisonneuve">Paul Chomedey, sieur de Maisonneuve</a>. It is located over the eastern portal of the Green Line tunnel.</p>
<p>Three paintings by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_LaPalme&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Robert LaPalme (page does not exist)">Robert LaPalme</a> are located over the main staircase leading to the Yellow Line terminus. Originally located at the entrance to <a href="/wiki/Expo_67" title="Expo 67">Expo 67</a>, they represent three themes of the Expo: science, recreation, and culture.</p>
<p>A plaque by LaPalme and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Georges_Lauda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Georges Lauda (page does not exist)">Georges Lauda</a>, commemorating the inauguration of the Metro, is located at the centre of the mezzanine. It is enclosed in a black circular bench, a popular meeting site, referred to as <i>la rondelle</i> (the hockey puck) or <i>la pilule</i> (the pill) or "le banc des fous" (the crazy bench).</p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:112px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Emilie_Gamelin.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1775" data-file-width="1204" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Emilie_Gamelin.JPG/110px-Emilie_Gamelin.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Emilie_Gamelin.JPG/165px-Emilie_Gamelin.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Emilie_Gamelin.JPG/220px-Emilie_Gamelin.JPG 2x" width="110"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Emilie_Gamelin.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i><a href="/wiki/%C3%89milie_Gamelin" title="Émilie Gamelin">Mère Émilie Gamelin</a></i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>There is also a piece of work located in the newer Sainte-Catherine entrance pavilion, by architect Gaétan Pelletier. The work is a statue of Mother <a href="/wiki/%C3%89milie_Gamelin" title="Émilie Gamelin">Émilie Gamelin</a> by <a href="/wiki/Raoul_Hunter" title="Raoul Hunter">Raoul Hunter</a>, commemorating <a href="/wiki/Place_%C3%89milie-Gamelin" title="Place Émilie-Gamelin">Place Émilie-Gamelin</a> (also called Berri Square) in which the entrance is located. The statue is owned by the City of Montreal.</p>
<p>The most recent art piece put in place inside the station is the Wall of Peace on the concourse level of the Yellow Line. It consists of coloured metal plates bearing the word "peace" in multiple languages.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berri-UQAM_(Montreal_Metro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Berri-UQAM was the site of the Metro's inauguration on October 14, 1966.</p>
<p>On September 2, 2001, a canister of <a href="/wiki/Tear_gas" title="Tear gas">tear gas</a> was set off inside the station, forcing the evacuation of the 300 passengers inside.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_the_name">Origin of the name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berri-UQAM_(Montreal_Metro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Origin of the name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Berri-UQAM is named for both Rue Berri, so called since 1663 and named after the <a href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_du_Qu%C3%A9bec_%C3%A0_Montr%C3%A9al" title="Université du Québec à Montréal">Université du Québec à Montréal</a>. (The University has taken to using UQAM as its abbreviation which it displays as UQÀM (with a <a href="/wiki/Grave_accent" title="Grave accent">grave accent</a> over the a) as its logo; the Metro station retains the UQAM form.)</p>
<p>Until 1988, the station was named <b>Berri-de-Montigny</b>; rue de Montigny is the former name of boulevard de Maisonneuve in this area.</p>
<p>Small stubs of de Montigny street still survive, in downtown between <a href="/wiki/Saint_Laurent_Boulevard" title="Saint Laurent Boulevard">St-Laurent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Urbain_Street" title="Saint Urbain Street">St-Urbain</a> and in the suburb <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montr%C3%A9al-Est" title="Montréal-Est">Montréal-Est</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Renovations">Renovations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berri-UQAM_(Montreal_Metro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Renovations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From August 2010 until mid 2017 the station is currently undergoing a 90 million dollar renovation plan that will change the appearance of the station, secure the tunnels and roofs. Other work being done includes mechanical, structural and architectural improvements.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The first phase of the work was done from August 2010 until the summer of 2011, included the removal of the gray tiles on all 3 platforms and station walls. Temporary walls were put up until the next phase.</p>
<p>The second phase began in January 2012 and will continue until June 2012. The work in this phase includes reinforcing walls, renovating mechanical, structural and architectural components and the removing of four red columns on the mezzanine level staircases. Once this work is completed, the temporary walls and finishes will be removed and replaced by new permanent architectural facings.</p>
