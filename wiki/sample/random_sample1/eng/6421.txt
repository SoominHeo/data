<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Bebelplatz</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Bebelplatz</b> (formerly colloquially <b>Opernplatz</b>) is a public square in the central <a href="/wiki/Mitte_(locality)" title="Mitte (locality)">Mitte</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.</p>
<p>The square is located on the south side of the <a href="/wiki/Unter_den_Linden" title="Unter den Linden">Unter den Linden</a> boulevard, a major east-west thoroughfare in the city centre. It is bounded to the east by the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_State_Opera" title="Berlin State Opera">State Opera</a> building (hence its prewar name), to the west by buildings of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Humboldt_University" title="Humboldt University">Humboldt University</a>, and to the southeast by <a href="/wiki/St._Hedwig%27s_Cathedral" title="St. Hedwig's Cathedral">St. Hedwig's Cathedral</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> church built in <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia" title="Kingdom of Prussia">Prussia</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" title="Protestant Reformation">Reformation</a>. The square is named after <a href="/wiki/August_Bebel" title="August Bebel">August Bebel</a>, a founder of the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Germany" title="Social Democratic Party of Germany">Social Democratic Party of Germany</a> in the 19th century.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bebelplatz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The square, then called <i>Platz am Opernhaus</i> (i.e. square at the opera house), was laid out between 1741 and 1743 under the rule of King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederick_II_of_Prussia" title="Frederick II of Prussia">Frederick II of Prussia</a>. On 12 August 1910 it was named <i>Kaiser-Franz-Josef-Platz</i>, in honour of Emperor <a href="/wiki/Franz_Joseph_I_of_Austria" title="Franz Joseph I of Austria">Francis Joseph I of Austria</a> on the occasion of his 87th birthday. The buildings surrounding the square were largely destroyed in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> by <a href="/wiki/Bombing_of_Berlin_in_World_War_II" title="Bombing of Berlin in World War II">air raids</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Berlin" title="Battle of Berlin">Battle of Berlin</a>. The ensemble was restored in the 1950s and the square renamed on 31 August 1947 as Bebelplatz.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nazi_book_burning">Nazi book burning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bebelplatz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nazi book burning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Nazi_book_burnings" title="Nazi book burnings">Nazi book burnings</a></div>
<p>The Bebelplatz is known as the site of one of the infamous <a href="/wiki/Nazi_book_burnings" title="Nazi book burnings">Nazi book burning</a> ceremonies held in the evening of 10 May 1933 in many German university cities. The book burnings were initiated and hosted by the nationalist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deutsche_Studentenschaft" title="Deutsche Studentenschaft">German Student Association</a>, thus stealing a march on the <a href="/wiki/National_Socialist_German_Students%27_League" title="National Socialist German Students' League">National Socialist German Students' League</a>. The assembly of the books had started on the sixth, when students dragged the contents of the <a href="/wiki/Institut_f%C3%BCr_Sexualwissenschaft" title="Institut für Sexualwissenschaft">Institut für Sexualwissenschaft</a> library into the square. At the Student Association's invitation Propaganda Minister <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Goebbels" title="Joseph Goebbels">Joseph Goebbels</a> held an inflammatory speech prior to the burning. Besides other spectators, it was attended by members of the Nazi Students' League, the <a href="/wiki/Sturmabteilung" title="Sturmabteilung">SA</a> ("brownshirts"), <a href="/wiki/Schutzstaffel" title="Schutzstaffel">SS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hitler_Youth" title="Hitler Youth">Hitler Youth</a> groups. They burned around 20,000 books, including works by <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Mann" title="Heinrich Mann">Heinrich Mann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erich_Maria_Remarque" title="Erich Maria Remarque">Erich Maria Remarque</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Heine" title="Heinrich Heine">Heinrich Heine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karl_Marx" title="Karl Marx">Karl Marx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Albert Einstein</a> and many other authors. <a href="/wiki/Erich_K%C3%A4stner" title="Erich Kästner">Erich Kästner</a>, whose books were also among those burned, was present at the scene and described it with bitter irony in his diary.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Today a memorial by <a href="/wiki/Micha_Ullman" title="Micha Ullman">Micha Ullman</a> consisting of a glass plate set into the cobbles, giving a view of empty bookcases (large enough to hold the total of the 20,000 burnt books), commemorates the book burning. Furthermore, a line of <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Heine" title="Heinrich Heine">Heinrich Heine</a> from his play, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Almansor" title="Almansor">Almansor</a> (1821), is engraved on a plaque inset in the square: "Das war ein Vorspiel nur, dort wo man Bücher verbrennt, verbrennt man am Ende auch Menschen." (in English: "That was only a prelude; where they burn books, they will in the end also burn people").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Students at Humboldt University hold a book sale in the square every year to mark the anniversary.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bebelplatz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012 several protests were caused by the announced plan of an underground carpark serving the attendees of the opera to be erected under the square and around the subsurface memorial.</p>
<p>In 2010 an exhibition of "<a href="/wiki/United_Buddy_Bears" title="United Buddy Bears">United Buddy Bears</a>" was held in the square, for the third time in Berlin. The exhibition consisted of more than 180 bear sculptures, each two metres high and designed by a different artist. Due to its difficult past the use of Bebelplatz remains disputed, recently sparked off by a wintery skating rink and a party tent of the Berlin <a href="/wiki/Fashion_week" title="Fashion week">fashion week</a>.</p>
