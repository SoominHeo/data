<p><b>Vestas Wind Systems A/S</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> manufacturer, seller, installer, and servicer of <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">wind turbines</a>. It was founded in 1945, and as of 2013, it is the largest wind turbine company in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The company operates manufacturing plants in Denmark, Germany, India, Italy, Romania, the United Kingdom, Spain, Sweden, Norway, Australia, China, and the United States,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vest_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-vest-4">[4]</a></sup> and employs more than 21,000 people globally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ansatte_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ansatte-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vestas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vestas traces its roots to 1898 where Hand Smith Hansen bought a black smith shop which operated as a family business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After the second world war Vestas was founded in 1945 by his son Peder Hansen as "Vestjysk Stålteknik <a href="/wiki/Aktieselskab" title="Aktieselskab">A/S</a>" (West-Jutlandish steel technology). The company initially manufactured household appliances, moving its focus to agricultural equipment in 1950, <a href="/wiki/Intercooler" title="Intercooler">intercoolers</a> in 1956, and hydraulic <a href="/wiki/Crane_(machine)" title="Crane (machine)">cranes</a> in 1968. It entered the wind turbine industry in 1979,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hist-1971-86_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-hist-1971-86-6">[6]</a></sup> and produced wind turbines exclusively from 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rewPlay_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-rewPlay-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company's North American headquarters was relocated in 2002 from <a href="/wiki/Palm_Springs,_California" title="Palm Springs, California">Palm Springs, California</a> to <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oregonianSep2009_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-oregonianSep2009-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oregonianAug2010_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-oregonianAug2010-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2003, the company merged with the Danish wind turbine manufacturer <a href="/wiki/NEG_Micon" title="NEG Micon">NEG Micon</a> to create the largest wind turbine manufacturer in the world, under the banner of Vestas Wind Systems. After a sales slump and an operational loss in 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vest_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-vest-4">[4]</a></sup> Vestas recovered in 2006 with a 28% market share<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vest_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-vest-4">[4]</a></sup> and increased production although market share slid to between 12.5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuBTM_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-reuBTM-10">[10]</a></sup> and 14%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuMAKE_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-reuMAKE-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vestas began a <a href="/wiki/Whistleblower" title="Whistleblower">whistleblower</a> program in 2007, among the first in Denmark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whistleblower_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-whistleblower-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 December 2008 Vestas announced plans to expand its North American headquarters in Portland through construction of a 600,000-square-foot (56,000 m<sup>2</sup>) new building, but this plan was mothballed in 2009 due to the economic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Late-2000s_recession" title="Late-2000s recession">recession</a>, and in August 2010 the company announced a revised plan, scaled back in size, to expand its Portland headquarters by renovating an existing-but-vacant 172,000 sq ft (16,000 m<sup>2</sup>) building.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-portland-expand_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-portland-expand-13">[13]</a></sup> At that time, Vestas employed about 400 in Portland and committed to add at least 100 more employees there within five years; the new building will have space for up to 600 workers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-portland-expand_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-portland-expand-13">[13]</a></sup> The company moved its Portland offices to the new headquarters building, a renovated <a href="/wiki/Meier_%26_Frank_Delivery_Depot" title="Meier &amp; Frank Delivery Depot">historic building</a>, in May 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pbj-may2012_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-pbj-may2012-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2009, the company announced the production of two new turbine types, the 3-megawatt V112 and 1.8-megawatt V100. The new models were to be available in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2009, Vestas announced its manufacturing operations on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight" title="Isle of Wight">Isle of Wight</a> in England would close due to a lack of UK demand, affecting 525 jobs there and 100 in Southampton. Approximately 25 workers at the wind turbine factory on the island occupied the administration offices in protest on 20 July 2009, demanding nationalisation to save their jobs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2009 Vestas hired more than 5,000 extra workers for its new factories in <a href="/wiki/Wind_power_in_China" title="Wind power in China">China</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Wind_power_in_the_United_States" title="Wind power in the United States">United States</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wind_power_in_Spain" title="Wind power in Spain">Spain</a>. The company said it was "expanding heavily in China and the US because these markets were growing the fastest, in contrast to the sluggish pace of wind farm development in the UK".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guard-expands_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-guard-expands-17">[17]</a></sup> As part of this gradual shift in production away from Europe and towards China and the US, in October 2010, the company announced it was closing 5 factories in Denmark and Sweden, with the loss of 3,000 jobs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuCut_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-reuCut-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2010, Vestas shut down the 70-person staff advisory department 'Vestas Excellence', responsible for securing competitiveness, handling suppliers, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quality_Assurance" title="Quality Assurance">Quality Assurance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">globalization</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ingEx_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-ingEx-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-borsEx_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-borsEx-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2012, the company suggested firing 1,600 out of its 3,000 U.S. workers if the U.S. did not renew the 2.2 cents-per-kilowatt-hour <a href="/wiki/United_States_Wind_Energy_Policy#Renewable_Electricity_Production_Tax_Credit" title="United States Wind Energy Policy">Production Tax Credit</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> which was extended in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> On 13 August 2012, an estimated 90 workers were laid off from the Pueblo facility. Six long colored lines, leading to an exit, had been placed on the floor. Those laid off were given one of six different colored papers, and then instructed to follow the colored line that matched the colored paper they had been given.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In 2013, the tower factory in Pueblo began ramping up to full utilization as orders rebounded from the 2012 slump.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vestas80Pueblo_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vestas80Pueblo-24">[24]</a></sup> Other facilities in Colorado include a further 750 persons employed at a blade manufacturing facility in <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Colorado" title="Windsor, Colorado">Windsor, Colorado</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (January 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> Vestas has nacelle and blade manufacturing facilities in <a href="/wiki/Brighton,_Colorado" title="Brighton, Colorado">Brighton, Colorado</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and also operates a tower facility in <a href="/wiki/Pueblo,_Colorado" title="Pueblo, Colorado">Pueblo, Colorado</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-windenergyweekly2010-05-14_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-windenergyweekly2010-05-14-26">[26]</a></sup> Vestas said it decided to build its North American production facilities in Colorado because of the state’s central location, extensive transportation infrastructure and rail system, existing manufacturing base, and skilled workforce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-windenergyweekly2010-05-14_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-windenergyweekly2010-05-14-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2013, Marika Fredriksson became the company's new Executive Vice President and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Financial_Officer" title="Chief Financial Officer">Chief Financial Officer</a> after her predecessor Dag Andresen resigned for personal reasons. Her strategy is to lead Vestas back to higher earnings after the important losses faced by the company: from €166 million losses in 2011 and increasing to €963 million in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<p>In September 2013, Vestas made a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> for offshore wind turbines with <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Heavy_Industries" title="Mitsubishi Heavy Industries">Mitsubishi Heavy Industries</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2013 Vestas sold its four casting and two machining factories to VTC Partners GmbH.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2014, Vestas announced it would be adding hundreds of jobs to its Colorado Windsor and Brighton facilities and following a rough 2012 it called 2013 one of Vestas’s "best years ever".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Vestas also added employees in Pueblo and expected the tower facility to eventually top 500.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vestas80Pueblo_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-Vestas80Pueblo-24">[24]</a></sup> Vestas stated that it expected to have 2800 employees in Colorado by the end of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> As of 2016<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vestas&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, Vestas has a US nacelle production capacity of 2.6 GW.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2015, Vestas announced it would be upping jobs by 400 at its blade manufacturing facility in Windsor and stated "We had a very successful 2014".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In 2015 almost half of all Vestas turbines were going to the American market<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> (nearly 3 GW for US out of 7.5 GW worldwide).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> Vestas intends to build a blade factory in India in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014 and 2015, 26 dishonest employees were detected with the company's whistleblower program (the first in Denmark), and disciplined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whistleblower_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-whistleblower-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2016, Vestas got its largest order of 1,000 MW (278 x 3.6 MW) for the <a href="/wiki/Fosen" title="Fosen">Fosen</a> project near <a href="/wiki/Trondheim" title="Trondheim">Trondheim</a> in Norway. It costs DKK 11 billion, and should deliver 3.4 TWh per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1Q 2016, the average wind turbine price was 0.83 million Euro per MW, compared to 0.91 a year before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2016, Vestas was voted number 7 on the Clean200 list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_and_development">Research and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vestas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Research and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vestas spent €92 million ($128 million), or 1.4% of revenue, on <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> in 2009. It has filed 787 wind turbine patents (227 in 2010) according to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Intellectual_Property_Office" title="United Kingdom Intellectual Property Office">United Kingdom Intellectual Property Office</a> (UK-IPO), while <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> has 666 and <a href="/wiki/Siemens_Wind_Power" title="Siemens Wind Power">Siemens Wind Power</a> has 242.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloIP_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-bloIP-44">[44]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October 2009, Vestas and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/QinetiQ" title="QinetiQ">QinetiQ</a> claimed a successful test of a <a href="/wiki/Stealth_technology" title="Stealth technology">stealth</a> wind turbine blade mitigating <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">radar</a> reflection problems for aviation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-refVQ_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-refVQ-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reutVQ_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-reutVQ-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trVQ_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-trVQ-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saVQ_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-saVQ-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>In December 2010 Vestas were developing the V164 7 MW offshore turbine,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rewPlay_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-rewPlay-7">[7]</a></sup> with a 164 m rotor diameter. Prototypes of it will be manufactured at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lind%C3%B8" title="Lindø">Lindø</a> (the former <a href="/wiki/Maersk" title="Maersk">Maersk</a> shipyard) due to size, crane and port access requirements. <a href="/wiki/Mass_production" title="Mass production">Series production</a> of nacelles for the 32 turbines (256 MW) extension of the 90 MW <a href="/wiki/Burbo_Bank_Offshore_Wind_Farm" title="Burbo Bank Offshore Wind Farm">Burbo Bank Offshore Wind Farm</a> will occur at Lindø, while blades are made at Vestas' <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Wight#Industry_and_agriculture" title="Isle of Wight">Isle of Wight facilities</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-burbo_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-burbo-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7lindoe_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-7lindoe-52">[52]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/DONG_Energy" title="DONG Energy">DONG Energy</a> will test a prototype in the sea off <a href="/wiki/Frederikshavn" title="Frederikshavn">Frederikshavn</a> in 2013, at a cost of DKK 240 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-econ15y_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-econ15y-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fushi_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-fushi-54">[54]</a></sup> A V164 was installed for testing in <a href="/wiki/%C3%98sterild_Wind_Turbine_Test_Field" title="Østerild Wind Turbine Test Field">Østerild Wind Turbine Test Field</a> in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2011, the Vestas <a href="/wiki/Supercomputer" title="Supercomputer">supercomputer</a> Firestorm was number 53 on the <a href="/wiki/TOP500" title="TOP500">TOP500</a>-list of the world's most powerful computers<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> calculating worldwide weather in a 3x3 km grid, and it delivers daily <a href="/wiki/Weather_forecasting" title="Weather forecasting">weather reports</a> to the newspaper <a href="/wiki/Ekstra_Bladet" title="Ekstra Bladet">Ekstra Bladet</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> and similar purposes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> In 2012, Vestas donated the older 1344-<a href="/wiki/Multi-core_processor" title="Multi-core processor">core</a> supercomputer from 2008 to <a href="/wiki/Aalborg_University" title="Aalborg University">Aalborg University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup></p>

<p>In October 2011, Vestas participated in the deployment of a <a href="/wiki/Floating_wind_turbine" title="Floating wind turbine">floating wind turbine</a> offshore of Portugal. Vestas supplied a v80 2.0 MW offshore turbine to Windplus, S.A. (a joint-venture company including <a href="/wiki/Energias_de_Portugal" title="Energias de Portugal">Energias de Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Repsol" title="Repsol">Repsol</a>, Principle Power, A. Silva Matos, Inovcapital and Portugal Ventures).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Windplus_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-Windplus-60">[60]</a></sup> The system, known as the WindFloat, consists of a semi-submersible type floating foundation, a conventional catenary mooring, and the wind turbine. The successful deployment represents the first offshore multi-megawatt wind turbine to be installed without the use of any heavy-lift or specialized offshore construction equipment.</p>
<p>In 2012, Vestas scaled back and closed some of its R&amp;D offices in Houston, Marlborough, Louisville, China, Singapore and Denmark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2013, Vestas started operating its 20 MW test bench for nacelles in Aarhus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 5 September 2013, Dr. Chris Spruce, Vestas Senior Product Engineer, served as member of the Scientific Advisory Board (SAB) for the kite-energy-systems project ERC HIGHWIND, a project at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KU_Leuven" title="KU Leuven">KU Leuven</a> dedicated to the research and development of tethered airfoils dedicated to generating energy by airborne wind energy (AWE).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2016, Vestas installed a 900kW <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quadrotor" title="Quadrotor">quadrotor</a> test wind turbine at <a href="/wiki/Ris%C3%B8_DTU_National_Laboratory_for_Sustainable_Energy" title="Risø DTU National Laboratory for Sustainable Energy">Risø</a>, made from 4 recycled 225kW V29 turbines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> Three months of testing have so far confirmed theoretical models. Vestas has no immediate plans of commercializing the prototype.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup></p>

<p>As of 2012 Vestas has installed over 48,000 wind turbines for a capacity of 55 GW in over 70 countries on five continents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> As of 31 2012 the company has built production facilities in more than 12 countries, among them China, Spain and the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pdf_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-pdf-69">[69]</a></sup> In China, Vestas employs 2,600 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rpChin_70-0"><a href="#cite_note-rpChin-70">[70]</a></sup></p>
