<p><i><b>Best of World Championship Wrestling</b></i> (a.k.a. <i><b>Best of Championship Wrestling</b></i>) was an hour-long Sunday evening edition of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TBS_(TV_network)" title="TBS (TV network)">TBS'</a> Saturday night wrestling shows, <i><a href="/wiki/Georgia_Championship_Wrestling" title="Georgia Championship Wrestling">Georgia Championship Wrestling</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/WCW_Saturday_Night" title="WCW Saturday Night">NWA World Championship Wrestling</a></i>. The rights to <i>Best of World Championship Wrestling</i> now belong to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">World Wrestling Entertainment</a> (WWE).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_of_World_Championship_Wrestling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Best of World Championship Wrestling</i> began in the 1970s to complement <i>GCW</i>, which by 1984 had been renamed <i>World Championship Wrestling</i>. It became inactive when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vincent_Kennedy_McMahon" title="Vincent Kennedy McMahon">Vince McMahon</a> bought GCW on July 14, 1984 (a.k.a. <i><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Saturday_(1984)" title="Black Saturday (1984)">Black Saturday</a></b></i>). When McMahon sold the Saturday Night time slot to <a href="/wiki/Jim_Crockett_Promotions" title="Jim Crockett Promotions">Jim Crockett Promotions</a>, the Sunday edition of <i>World Championship Wrestling</i> was resurrected, first as <i>Best of Championship Wrestling</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and later as <i>Best of World Championship Wrestling</i>. However, airings of Sunday editions became infrequent due to TBS' coverage of the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Format">Format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_of_World_Championship_Wrestling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>BoWCW</i>'s format was essentially a magazine format, mainly featuring re-caps of matches which had previously aired on other related programming. In addition, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Apter" title="Bill Apter">Bill Apter</a>, then of <a href="/wiki/Pro_Wrestling_Illustrated" title="Pro Wrestling Illustrated">Pro Wrestling Illustrated</a>, had a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sit-down_interview&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sit-down interview (page does not exist)">sit-down interview</a> segment called the <i>PWI Scouting Report</i>. During the show's GCW years, the show was hosted solo by <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Miller_(broadcaster)" title="Freddie Miller (broadcaster)">Freddie Miller</a>. During the show's JCP years, it was hosted solo by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Schiavone" title="Tony Schiavone">Tony Schiavone</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_angles">Notable angles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_of_World_Championship_Wrestling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable angles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One of the most-remembered wrestling <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Angle" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">angles</a> to air on this show was the announcement of then-NWA president <a href="/wiki/Bob_Geigel" title="Bob Geigel">Bob Geigel</a> stripping <a href="/wiki/Magnum_T.A." title="Magnum T.A.">Magnum T.A.</a> of the <a href="/wiki/WWE_United_States_Championship" title="WWE United States Championship">NWA U.S. Heavyweight Title</a> for "conduct unbecoming a champion" due to Magnum clotheslining Geigel after Geigel reprimanded him for an attack on <a href="/wiki/Nikita_Koloff" title="Nikita Koloff">Nikita Koloff</a>. This set up the famous "best-of-seven" wrestling series to fill the title vacancy. Koloff defeated Magnum 4-3 to win the vacant title.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cancellation">Cancellation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_of_World_Championship_Wrestling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cancellation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In early <a href="/wiki/1988_in_television" title="1988 in television">1988</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ted_Turner" title="Ted Turner">Ted Turner</a> expressed interest in a new Sunday evening wrestling show which featured original matches. Turner got his wish when <i><a href="/wiki/WCW_Main_Event" title="WCW Main Event">NWA Main Event</a></i> debuted to strong <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_ratings" title="Nielsen ratings">ratings</a>. In November 1988, Jim Crockett Promotions was sold to Turner, who renamed the organization <a href="/wiki/World_Championship_Wrestling" title="World Championship Wrestling">World Championship Wrestling</a>.</p>
