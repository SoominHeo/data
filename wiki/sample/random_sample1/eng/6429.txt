<p>The <i><b>Westdeutsche Allgemeine Zeitung</b></i> is a leading commercial newspaper from <a href="/wiki/Essen" title="Essen">Essen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WAZ-Mediengruppe" title="WAZ-Mediengruppe">WAZ-Mediengruppe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_profile">History and profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Westdeutsche_Allgemeine_Zeitung&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Westdeutsche Allgemeine Zeitung</i> was founded by <a href="/wiki/Erich_Brost" title="Erich Brost">Erich Brost</a> and first published 3 April 1948.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The paper has its headquarters in Essen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gh_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-gh-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the third quarter of 1992 <i>Westdeutsche Allgemeine Zeitung</i> had a circulation of 626,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gh_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-gh-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
