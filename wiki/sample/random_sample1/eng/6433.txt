<p>The <b>Baseball Grounds of Jacksonville</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Baseball_park" title="Baseball park">baseball park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">Jacksonville, Florida</a>. It is the home stadium of the <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville_Jumbo_Shrimp" title="Jacksonville Jumbo Shrimp">Jacksonville Jumbo Shrimp</a> baseball team, who play in the Class <a href="/wiki/Double-A_(baseball)" title="Double-A (baseball)">Double-A</a> <a href="/wiki/Southern_League_(baseball)" title="Southern League (baseball)">Southern League</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville_Armada_FC" title="Jacksonville Armada FC">Jacksonville Armada FC</a> of the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Soccer_League" title="North American Soccer League">North American Soccer League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The facility is known as <b>Community First Park</b> for Jacksonville Armada games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The facility opened in 2003.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_Grounds_of_Jacksonville&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Baseball Grounds were proposed as part of the city planning program known as the <a href="/wiki/Better_Jacksonville_Plan" title="Better Jacksonville Plan">Better Jacksonville Plan</a>. It was designed to replace the aging <a href="/wiki/Wolfson_Park" title="Wolfson Park">Wolfson Park</a>, the Jumbo Shrimp's previous home. The facility cost $34 million and broke ground in 2002, with construction being completed the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbp_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbp-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The ballpark was the first completed project of the <a href="/wiki/Better_Jacksonville_Plan" title="Better Jacksonville Plan">Better Jacksonville Plan</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Coast_Conference" title="Atlantic Coast Conference">Atlantic Coast Conference</a> baseball championship was held at the venue from 2005–2008. The <a href="/wiki/Georgia_Tech_Yellow_Jackets" title="Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets">Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets</a> won the first ACC Tournament at the Baseball Grounds of Jacksonville, followed by the <a href="/wiki/Clemson_Tigers" title="Clemson Tigers">Clemson Tigers</a> in 2006, the <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Tar_Heels" title="North Carolina Tar Heels">North Carolina Tar Heels</a> in 2007, and the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Hurricanes" title="Miami Hurricanes">Miami Hurricanes</a> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbp_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-bbp-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Baseball Grounds hosts an annual game between the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Gators_baseball" title="Florida Gators baseball">Florida Gators</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_Seminoles_baseball" title="Florida State Seminoles baseball">Florida State Seminoles</a>. The most recent game was on March 25, 2014 when Florida defeated Florida State 4–1 in front of 10,125 fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 17, 2013 the Baseball Grounds hosted the <a href="/wiki/Southern_League_(baseball)" title="Southern League (baseball)">Southern League</a> All-Star Game. The South Division defeated the North Division 6–0 in front of a crowd of 9,373.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>It has nearly 6,000 stadium-style chairs and can accommodate more than 11,000 fans, the highest capacity in all of Double-A baseball, with an old-fashioned design, brick facade and a grass seating berm and bleacher seating. It also features 12 luxury skyboxes, four skydecks, a large scoreboard and videoboard, a playground, and the "knuckle," a unique 9-foot-high (2.7 m) mound for seating at the left field corner. Other ballpark features include a souvenir shop, first aid facility, various seating levels and perspectives, an ample number of restrooms and concession areas, first-class in-seat concession services behind home plate, wide concourse and seating aisles and a state-of-the-art video scoreboard in left center field.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbp_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-bbp-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The park has an in-house video broadcast of games provided by The Schelldorf Television Network. The ballpark is located in downtown Jacksonville, situated between <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville_Veterans_Memorial_Arena" title="Jacksonville Veterans Memorial Arena">Jacksonville Veterans Memorial Arena</a> and <a href="/wiki/EverBank_Field" title="EverBank Field">EverBank Field</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbp_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-bbp-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attendance_records">Attendance records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baseball_Grounds_of_Jacksonville&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Attendance records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The largest crowd for a baseball game at the ballpark was 17,860, which occurred on May 25, 2005 during an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_ACC_Baseball_Tournament" title="2005 ACC Baseball Tournament">ACC Baseball Tournament</a> game between <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_Seminoles_baseball" title="Florida State Seminoles baseball">Florida State</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Tar_Heels_baseball" title="North Carolina Tar Heels baseball">North Carolina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
