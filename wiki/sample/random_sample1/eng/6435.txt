<p><b>Bay City</b> is a <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> in <a href="/wiki/Matagorda_County,_Texas" title="Matagorda County, Texas">Matagorda County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The population was 17,614 at the 2010 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CensusQF_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-CensusQF-1">[1]</a></sup> It is the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> of <a href="/wiki/Matagorda_County,_Texas" title="Matagorda County, Texas">Matagorda County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR6_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR6-6">[6]</a></sup> The current mayor is Mark Bricker.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bay_City,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 8.5 square miles (22.0 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 8.5 square miles (22.0 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 0.12% is covered by water.</p>
<p>Bay City was formerly named "Bay Prairie", as the natural ecosystems that surround the town are prairies crisscrossed by creeks that lead into the bay.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bay_City,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The climate in this area is characterized by hot, humid summers and generally mild to cool winters. According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_Climate_Classification" title="Köppen Climate Classification">Köppen Climate Classification</a> system, Bay City has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a>, abbreviated "Cfa" on climate maps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bay_City,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-2">[2]</a></sup> of 2000, 18,667 people, 6,912 households, and 4,769 families resided in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 2,196.0 people per square mile (847.9/km²). There were 8,113 housing units at an average density of 954.4 per square mile (368.5/km²). The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">racial makeup</a> of the city was 61.62% White, 17.26% African American, 0.74% Native American, 0.88% Asian, 0.07% Pacific Islander, 16.84% from other races, and 2.59% from two or more races. Hispanics or Latinos of any race were 34.74% of the population.</p>
<p>Of the 6,912 households, 37.9% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 49.0% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 16.1% had a female householder with no husband present, and 31.0% were not families. About 27.1% of all households were made up of individuals and 10.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.66 and the average family size was 3.25.</p>
<p>In the city, the population was distributed as 30.9% under the age of 18, 9.8% from 18 to 24, 28.2% from 25 to 44, 20.0% from 45 to 64, and 11.1% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 32 years. For every 100 females, there were 95.0 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 91.1 males.</p>
<p>The median income for a household in the city was $30,446, and for a family was $39,281. Males had a median income of $38,202 versus $23,058 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $15,284. About 18.3% of families and 21.4% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 27.7% of those under age 18 and 14.3% of those age 65 or over. Minorities make up the largest ethnic portion of Bay City, Texas.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bay_City,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bay City formerly housed the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Stores" title="Stanley Stores">Stanley Stores</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2newstores_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-2newstores-8">[8]</a></sup> The chain made several donations to the Bay City Museum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Attractions">Attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bay_City,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bay City is home to the Matagorda County Birding Nature Center, a 35-acre (140,000 m<sup>2</sup>) expanse of gardens and wildlife along the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_River_(Texas)" title="Colorado River (Texas)">Colorado River</a> of Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other attractions include the Matagorda County Museum,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <i>Market Days</i> every 3rd Saturday,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and a variety of small shops and boutiques downtown. The Bay City Art League, also located here, has recently undergone major renovations and is currently working to revitalize the art scene in Matagorda County. In addition, the Bay City Community Theatre group (CAST) regularly produces shows at various local venues.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bay_City,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bay City is served by the <a href="/wiki/Bay_City_Independent_School_District" title="Bay City Independent School District">Bay City Independent School District</a>, consisting of elementary, intermediate, junior high, and high schools (including <a href="/wiki/Bay_City_High_School" title="Bay City High School">Bay City High School</a>). The district previously operated seven schools until grade levels were condensed. It is now operating five schools and is led by superintendent Keith Brown.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Wharton_County_Junior_College" title="Wharton County Junior College">Wharton County Junior College</a> has a campus in Bay City.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_care">Health care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bay_City,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Health care">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
