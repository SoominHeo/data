<p><b>Line 15</b> of the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Subway" title="Beijing Subway">Beijing Subway</a> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">北京地铁15号线</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant" xml:lang="zh-Hant">北京地鐵15號線</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">běijīng dìtiě shíwǔhào xiàn</span></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit</a> rail line in northern <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> with one portion in operation and the remainder under construction. In all, the line is set to have a total length of 45.7 kilometres (28.4 mi) across northern Beijing from Xiyuan in <a href="/wiki/Haidian_District" title="Haidian District">Haidian District</a> in the west, to Hedong in <a href="/wiki/Shunyi_District" title="Shunyi District">Shunyi District</a> in the east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-2">[2]</a></sup> The line uses Sparcs CBTC system supplied by Nippon Signal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is being built in two phases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">[4]</a></sup> Phase I covers the eastern half of Line 15 from Qinghuadongluxikou in Haidian District to Fengbo in Shunyi District.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated7_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated7-5">[5]</a></sup> It will cost <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">¥</a>17.6 billion. Section 3 of Phase 1 opened in 2014. Phase II is expected to be completed by 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated8_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated8-6">[6]</a></sup> The line features 6-car trains similar to other lines in the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Subway" title="Beijing Subway">Beijing Subway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated9_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated9-7">[7]</a></sup> Line 15 tunnels will be up to 38 metres (125 ft) underground, making them the deepest in Beijing's subway system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated9_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated9-7">[7]</a></sup> Trains run every five minutes during the <a href="/wiki/Rush_hour" title="Rush hour">rush hour</a>, with a journey from Fengbo to Qinghuadongluxikou Station taking 60 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15I3_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-15I3-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_15,_Beijing_Subway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Phase I is planned to be 38.3 km long (23.8 mi) with 18 stations,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated7_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated7-5">[5]</a></sup> covers the eastern half of Line 15 and connects the suburbs of Shunyi District with the existing subway network at Wangjing West (<a href="/wiki/Line_13,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 13, Beijing Subway">Line 13</a>), Datun Lu East (<a href="/wiki/Line_5,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 5, Beijing Subway">Line 5</a>) and at the Olympic Green (<a href="/wiki/Line_8,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 8, Beijing Subway">Line 8</a>). Currently all three sections of <span class="nowrap">Phase I</span> are operational. It has 18 stations and is over 41 km (25 mi) in length. The operational sections run from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qinghuadongluxikou" title="Qinghuadongluxikou">Qinghuadongluxikou</a> in Haidian District to Fengbo in Shunyi District. Line 15 travels underground from Qinghuadongluxikou to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maquanying" title="Maquanying">Maquanying</a>, and then goes above ground on an elevated track to Houshayu before returning underground and going underneath the <a href="/wiki/Chaobai_River" title="Chaobai River">Chaobai River</a> to Fengbo. The elevated section is high enough to allow elevated roads to pass underneath. A major public venue on the Line 15 route is the New China International Exposition Center (New CIEC) 新国展 at the China International Exhibition Center station 国展站. New CIEC holds the annual Beijing International Automotive Exhibition, which attracted 800,000 visitors in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_15,_Beijing_Subway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During planning of Line 15, the eastern terminus was moved from Fuqian Jie <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-11">[11]</a></sup> to the east bank of the <a href="/wiki/Chaobai_River" title="Chaobai River">Chaobai River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-2">[2]</a></sup> Construction began on Phase I of Line 15 in Shunyi District on April 11, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-12">[12]</a></sup> At that time, Phase I of Line 15 was only 31.15 km (19.36 mi) long with 12 stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-2">[2]</a></sup> According to the construction schedule in 2009, the eastern-most Shunyi section of Phase I (18.5 km or 11.5 mi in length with seven stations) would be opened first on December 30, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated9b_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated9b-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated6_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated6-14">[14]</a></sup> The remainder of Phase I would follow in May 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-2">[2]</a></sup> However, on October 28, 2009, when track-laying began on Phase I of Line 15, Line 15's construction schedule was revised with Phase I expanded from 31 km (19 mi) and 12 stations to 38 km (24 mi) and 18 stations. <span class="nowrap">Phase II</span> plans also underwent a revision; instead of two sections, Phase I would be built and opened in three sections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated7_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated7-5">[5]</a></sup> The first section of <span class="nowrap">Phase I</span>, from Wangjing West to Houshayu, opened on December 30, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated7_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated7-5">[5]</a></sup> The second, from Houshayu to Fengbo, followed at the end of 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated7_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated7-5">[5]</a></sup> The third section, from Qinghuadongluxikou to Guanzhuang, opened on December 28, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15I3_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-15I3-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route_Under_Construction">Route Under Construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_15,_Beijing_Subway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Route Under Construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The route of Line 15 has been revised several times, and the unfinished portions may still change.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Phase_II">Phase II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_15,_Beijing_Subway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Phase II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of April 2009, Phase II plans showed the western half of Line 15 running from Xiyuan in <a href="/wiki/Haidian_District" title="Haidian District">Haidian District</a>, through the campus of <a href="/wiki/Tsinghua_University" title="Tsinghua University">Tsinghua University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zhongguancun" title="Zhongguancun">Zhongguancun</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Green" title="Olympic Green">Olympic Green</a>, and joining <span class="nowrap">Phase I</span> at Wangjing West.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated4_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated4-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2013, the design of Xiyuan station still leaves enough space to build another platform in between <a href="/wiki/Line_4,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 4, Beijing Subway">Line 4</a> and <a href="/wiki/Line_16,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 16, Beijing Subway">Line 16</a> platform for potential Line 15 westward extension.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> But by October 28, 2009, those of <span class="nowrap">Phase II</span> were undergoing major revision, with one proposal extending Line 15 westward as originally planned with additional stations<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sina_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-sina-19">[19]</a></sup> and another proposal sending Line 15 southward as parallel to Line 8 to provide relief for congested <a href="/wiki/Line_4,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 4, Beijing Subway">Line 4</a> and <a href="/wiki/Line_5,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 5, Beijing Subway">5</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
