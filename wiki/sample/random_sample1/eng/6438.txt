<p>The <b>Batong Line</b> of the <a href="/wiki/Beijing_Subway" title="Beijing Subway">Beijing Subway</a> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">八通线</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">bātōng xiàn</span></i>) is a surface level <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit</a> rail line in eastern <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>. It extends <a href="/wiki/Line_1,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 1, Beijing Subway">Line 1</a> further east from <a href="/wiki/Sihui,_Beijing" title="Sihui, Beijing">Sihui</a> to the suburbs in <a href="/wiki/Tongzhou_District,_Beijing" title="Tongzhou District, Beijing">Tongzhou District</a>. The Batong Line derives its name by combining the first character of <b>Ba</b>wangfen (八王坟), another place name for Sihui, and <b>Tong</b>zhou (通州) the eastern suburban district of Beijing which the line serves. The Batong Line is sometimes abbreviated as <b>8T</b> but it should not be confused with <b><a href="/wiki/Line_8,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 8, Beijing Subway">Line 8</a></b> of the Beijing Subway.</p>
<p>The Batong Line is entirely above ground and runs 18.9 km from the <a href="/wiki/Sihui_Station" title="Sihui Station">Sihui Station</a> in the west to <a href="/wiki/Tuqiao_Station" title="Tuqiao Station">Tuqiao</a> in the east. Transfers are available to Line 1 at the Sihui and <a href="/wiki/Sihui_East_Station" title="Sihui East Station">Sihui East</a> stations. The colour of Batong Line that is shown on maps, like that of Line 1, is red.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fare">Fare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batong_Line,_Beijing_Subway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Starting fare of <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">RMB(¥)</a> 3.00 that increases according to the distance fare scheme introduced in December 2014.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hours_of_Operation">Hours of Operation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batong_Line,_Beijing_Subway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hours of Operation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first east-bound train departs <a href="/wiki/Sihui_Station" title="Sihui Station">Sihui</a> at 6:00am. The first west-bound train departs Tuqiao at 5:20am. The last east-bound train leaves Sihui 10:45pm. The last west-bound train leaves Tuqiao at 10:05pm. For the official timetable, see <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.bjsubway.com/chinese_ver/cczn/TimeTableDetails.asp?TimeID=14" rel="nofollow">[2]</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batong_Line,_Beijing_Subway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Batong Line follows the <a href="/wiki/Jingtong_Expressway" title="Jingtong Expressway">Beijing-Tongzhou Expressway</a> eastward from Sihui to Guanzhuang. Between <a href="/wiki/Gaobeidian_Station" title="Gaobeidian Station">Gaobeidian</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Guanzhuang,_Beijing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Guanzhuang, Beijing (page does not exist)">Guanzhuang</a>, the Batong Line and its stations are built into the highway's median. At <a href="/wiki/Baliqiao" title="Baliqiao">Baliqiao</a>, the Batong Line turns to the southeast and follows the Jingtang Road to Tuqiao, which is located just inside the Tuqiao Bridge exit on the eastern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/6th_Ring_Road" title="6th Ring Road">6th Ring Road</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batong_Line,_Beijing_Subway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Construction of the Batong Line began on December 28, 2001 and the line opened on December 27, 2003.</p>
<p>In 2012, traffic on Batong Line was relieved by the opening of the parallel <a href="/wiki/Line_6,_Beijing_Subway" title="Line 6, Beijing Subway">Line 6</a>, with a 12.14% decrease in daily ridership and a 10-20% reduction in flow during rush hour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ridership_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ridership-4">[4]</a></sup> However, during peak hours sections the line still operate above 100% capacity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ridership_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ridership-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future">Future</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batong_Line,_Beijing_Subway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Future">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There will be platform doors on the Batong Line. There might be a south-eastern extension to Huanqiuyingcheng via Taiyuyuan, connecting the Yizhuang Line (future). The Batong Line might be combined with Line 1. There might be a Hugezhuang Line that starts from Batong Line's Tongzhoubeiyuan to Hugezhuang.</p>
