<p><b>Beijing Capital Airlines</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans" xml:lang="zh-Hans">首都航空</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Shǒudū Hángkōng</span></i>), operates under the marketing brand <b>Capital Airlines,</b> a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Hainan_Airlines" title="Hainan Airlines">Hainan Airlines</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beijing_Capital_Airlines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:DeerJetChina.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="90" data-file-width="258" height="77" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/67/DeerJetChina.jpg/220px-DeerJetChina.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/67/DeerJetChina.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:DeerJetChina.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Deer Jet logo</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The company was established in 1995 as <b>Deer Jet Airlines</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh" xml:lang="zh">金鹿航空</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Jīnlù Hángkōng</span></i>). In 1998, it began offering international services under the <b>Deer Air</b> branding. In October 2007, it received its first <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A320_family" title="Airbus A320 family">Airbus A319</a> and began returning the formerly operated <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737s</a>. Deer Jet began providing charter services in December 2008 using a fleet of A319s and <a href="/wiki/Business_jet" title="Business jet">corporate jets</a>. The airline was authorized by the <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aviation_Administration_of_China" title="Civil Aviation Administration of China">Civil Aviation Administration of China</a> to operate scheduled air services in 2009.</p>
<p>On 2 April 2010, Beijing Capital Airlines CO., LTD. launched its first service, based in Beijing Capital International Airport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Deer Jet Airlines was divided into two companies on May 4, 2010. While the charter operation has kept the Deer Jet branding, scheduled operations using Airbus aircraft were renamed <b>Capital Airlines</b>. Capital Airlines operates mainly in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macao" title="Macao">Macao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>), focusing on international air passenger service and cargo transport operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Destinations">Destinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beijing_Capital_Airlines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Destinations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Capital Airlines is committed to the development of international tourism routes. It plans to operate flights between <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hangzhou" title="Hangzhou">Hangzhou</a> as the core bases, and it is constantly opening international air routes to <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and so on. To date, Capital Airlines has opened the following international routes: Beijing - <a href="/wiki/Cheongju" title="Cheongju">Cheongju</a>, Xi'an - <a href="/wiki/Zhanjiang" title="Zhanjiang">Zhanjiang</a> - <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, Xi'an - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phuket" title="Phuket">Phuket</a>, Hangzhou - <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>, Hangzhou - <a href="/wiki/Jeju_City" title="Jeju City">Jeju</a>, Hangzhou - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">Okinawa</a>, Hangzhou - <a href="/wiki/Shizuoka,_Shizuoka" title="Shizuoka, Shizuoka">Shizuoka</a>, Beijing - <a href="/wiki/Mal%C3%A9" title="Malé">Malé</a>, Guangzhou - <a href="/wiki/Nha_Trang" title="Nha Trang">Nha Trang</a>, Hangzhou - Phuket, Hangzhou - <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sanya" title="Sanya">Sanya</a> - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Da_Nang" title="Da Nang">Da Nang</a>, Sanya - Nha Trang, <a href="/wiki/Jinan" title="Jinan">Jinan</a> - Shizuoka, Jinan - Osaka.</p>
<p>Since 2015, long haul flights between China and other cities such as <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>, Madrid and <a href="/wiki/Maldives" title="Maldives">Maldives</a> have been operated by Beijing Capital Airlines with Airbus A330 aircraft. Capital Airlines plans to connect China to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> in 2016. In addition, the airline plans to commence long-haul flights to <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a> in the first half of 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The opening of these international routes aims to solve the lack of international direct flight services. These also significantly stimulate domestic and international market and push forward the development of tourism resources, as well as promote local economic and social development. In the future, Capital Airlines will be leveraging the Chinese outbound tourism market, developing more tourism products, further improving the domestic and international route network. Capital Airlines wishes to make an important contribution to domestic and international cultural exchange and development.</p>
<p>In November 2016, Beijing Capital Airlines announced the launch of a Vancouver-<a href="/wiki/Qingdao" title="Qingdao">Qingdao</a>-Hangzhou service on December 30, 2016. The new international route between Canada and China will be served by an Airbus A330 wide-body, twin aisle aircraft and will operate every Wednesday, Friday and Sunday. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vancouver_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vancouver-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
