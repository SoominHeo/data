<p>Year <b>534</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DXXXIV</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Common year starting on Sunday">common year starting on Sunday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the Consulship of Iustinianus and Paulinus</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1287 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 534 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=534&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=534&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=534&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Byzantine Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_1" title="January 1">January 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/List_of_Roman_consuls" title="List of Roman consuls">Decimus Theodorius Paulinus</a> is appointed <a href="/wiki/Roman_consul" title="Roman consul">consul</a> (the last to hold this office in the West).</li>
<li>March – King <a href="/wiki/Gelimer" title="Gelimer">Gelimer</a> surrenders to <a href="/wiki/Belisarius" title="Belisarius">Belisarius</a>, after spending a winter in the mountains of <a href="/wiki/Numidia" title="Numidia">Numidia</a>. He and large numbers of captured <a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a> are transported to <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Vandal_Kingdom" title="Vandal Kingdom">Vandal Kingdom</a> ends, and the <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">African</a> provinces return to the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>.</li>
<li>April – Belisarius leaves a small force in Africa under the Byzantine general <a href="/wiki/Solomon_(Byzantine_general)" title="Solomon (Byzantine general)">Solomon</a>, to continue the subjugation of the province. He is appointed governor (<i><a href="/wiki/Exarch" title="Exarch">Exarch</a></i>) and pacifies with success the <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moorish</a> tribes. <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a> becomes a Byzantine province (until <a href="/wiki/870" title="870">870</a>).</li>
<li>Summer – Belisarius arrives in Constantinople and is permitted by Emperor <a href="/wiki/Justinian_I" title="Justinian I">Justinian I</a> to celebrate a <a href="/wiki/Roman_triumph" title="Roman triumph">triumph</a>, the first non-imperial triumph for over 500 years. In the procession are paraded the spoils of the <a href="/wiki/Temple_in_Jerusalem" title="Temple in Jerusalem">Temple of Jerusalem</a> and the Vandal treasure.</li>
<li>Justinian I commemorates the victory against the Vandals by stamping medals in his honor with the inscription <i>"Gloria Romanorum"</i> (approximate date).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_16" title="November 16">November 16</a> – A second and final revision of the <i><a href="/wiki/Corpus_Juris_Civilis" title="Corpus Juris Civilis">Codex Justinianus</a></i> is published.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=534&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Toledo,_Spain" title="Toledo, Spain">Toledo</a> becomes the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Visigothic_Kingdom" title="Visigothic Kingdom">Visigothic Kingdom</a> that controls the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a>. King <a href="/wiki/Theudis" title="Theudis">Theudis</a> expands <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoth</a> rule in the southern regions (<a href="/wiki/Hispania_Baetica" title="Hispania Baetica">Hispania Baetica</a>).</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Frankish</a> kings <a href="/wiki/Childebert_I" title="Childebert I">Childebert I</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chlothar_I" title="Chlothar I">Chlothar I</a> overthrow <a href="/wiki/Godomar" title="Godomar">Godomar</a>, king of the <a href="/wiki/Burgundians" title="Burgundians">Burgundians</a>, and end the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Burgundy" title="Kingdom of Burgundy">Kingdom of Burgundy</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cynric_of_Wessex" title="Cynric of Wessex">Cynric</a> becomes king of <a href="/wiki/Wessex" title="Wessex">Wessex</a> (according to the <i><a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon_Chronicle" title="Anglo-Saxon Chronicle">Anglo-Saxon Chronicle</a></i>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_2" title="October 2">October 2</a> – King <a href="/wiki/Athalaric" title="Athalaric">Athalaric</a> dies of <a href="/wiki/Tuberculosis" title="Tuberculosis">tuberculosis</a>, age 18, having dissipated his youth in drink and debauchery. His mother, <a href="/wiki/Amalasuntha" title="Amalasuntha">Amalasuntha</a>, proposes to her cousin <a href="/wiki/Theodahad" title="Theodahad">Theodahad</a>, the kingdom's largest <a href="/wiki/Land_tenure" title="Land tenure">landowner</a> and her father's last male heir, that he share the <a href="/wiki/Throne" title="Throne">throne</a> with her but that he will be king of the <a href="/wiki/Ostrogoths" title="Ostrogoths">Ostrogoths</a> in name only. Theodahad has secret conversations with the Byzantine <a href="/wiki/Ambassador" title="Ambassador">ambassador</a>, and promises to turn over <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a> in exchange for a large sum of money, the rank of <a href="/wiki/Senate" title="Senate">senator</a>, and permission to live at Constantinople.</li>
</ul>
