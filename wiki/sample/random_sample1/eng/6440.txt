<p>The <b>Northern Arizona Suns</b> are an <a href="/wiki/NBA_Development_League" title="NBA Development League">NBA Development League</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Prescott_Valley,_Arizona" title="Prescott Valley, Arizona">Prescott Valley, Arizona</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Farm_team" title="Farm team">minor league affiliate</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a>. The franchise began as the <b>Long Beach Jam</b> in 2003 under the revived <a href="/wiki/American_Basketball_Association_(2000%E2%80%93present)" title="American Basketball Association (2000–present)">American Basketball Association</a> and moved to Bakersfield in the D-League in 2006 as the <b>Bakersfield Jam</b>; after 10 years in <a href="/wiki/Bakersfield,_California" title="Bakersfield, California">Bakersfield, California</a>, the franchise was moved to Prescott Valley in 2016 by the Phoenix Suns and were subsequently renamed the Northern Arizona Suns.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Arizona_Suns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Long_Beach_Jam_.282003.E2.80.932005.29">Long Beach Jam (2003–2005)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Arizona_Suns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Long Beach Jam (2003–2005)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the year-long hiatus that the American Basketball Association had in the 2002–03 season, one of the teams the league approved of would be the <b>Long Beach Jam</b>. In the team's inaugural season, the Jam managed to procure NBA Hall of Famer <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Rodman" title="Dennis Rodman">Dennis Rodman</a> and Japanese point guard <a href="/wiki/Yuta_Tabuse" title="Yuta Tabuse">Yuta Tabuse</a> on their squad. They also got former NBA Finals winning head coach <a href="/wiki/Paul_Westhead" title="Paul Westhead">Paul Westhead</a> during the start of the season, but he managed to procure himself an assistant coach gig with the <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Magic" title="Orlando Magic">Orlando Magic</a> during the season. Nevertheless, with the presence of Rodman and Tabuse, the Jam would hold a 24–7 record, which was not only considered the best record of the league that season, but also gave the Jam a bye all the way into the Finals, where they competed against the winner of the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Knights" title="Kansas City Knights">Kansas City Knights</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ju%C3%A1rez_Gallos_de_Pelea" title="Juárez Gallos de Pelea">Juárez Gallos</a>. In the championship round, the Jam barely escaped against the Knights to win the ABA Championship with a final score of 126–123.</p>
<p>In their second and final season under the Long Beach name, the Jam had former player and future head coach of the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Mercury" title="Phoenix Mercury">Phoenix Mercury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corey_Gaines" title="Corey Gaines">Corey Gaines</a>, coach them during the second half of their season. In that season, they managed to produce an 18–10 record in a greatly expanded ABA, which produced them with the second-best record in the Red Division. The Jam competed in the playoffs and made it to the quarterfinals, where they lost 130–115 to the Utah Snowbears (who produced a 25–1 that season), who would surprisingly end up forfeiting their last match to the Bellevue Blackhawks (potentially due to sunk costs with the team) since the Snowbears folded soon afterwards, while Bellevue lost the championship match to the <a href="/wiki/Arkansas_RimRockers" title="Arkansas RimRockers">Arkansas RimRockers</a>. After the end of that season, the Jam would withdraw themselves from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005-06_ABA_season" title="2005-06 ABA season">2005-06 ABA season</a> to move to the <a href="/wiki/NBA_Development_League" title="NBA Development League">NBA Development League</a> next season, with the intent to also move to Bakersfield soon afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bakersfield_Jam_.282006.E2.80.932016.29">Bakersfield Jam (2006–2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Arizona_Suns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bakersfield Jam (2006–2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After their first season in Bakersfield ended in 2007, there was a contest where the fans could decide on a new name for the team. The choices were to rename the team <i>Desperados</i>, <i>Roughnecks</i>, <i>Oilers</i>, or keep the name Jam. On Thursday, May 17, the team announced that the team will remain the Bakersfield Jam, as that name won the poll in a landslide vote. After the contest, the Jam unveiled a new scheme for the logo. They replaced the blue to red.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 29, 2009, it was reported that the Jam had ceased operations, citing lack of sufficient fan attendance. However, on June 18, the Jam announced that they had not shut down and plan to play in the 2009–10 season, with further details to come the following day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 30, 2014, it was reported that the Jam would enter a hybrid operation with the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a>. Not only would that lead to the Suns having exclusive collaborations with the team, but it would also allow the Jam to operate under their own management in the process. On May 9, it was confirmed that the Suns and the Jam would agree to working under a hybrid affiliation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Four days later, the Jam completed their transition by allowing Suns scout Bubba Barrage to be the team's newest general manager and letting <a href="/wiki/Nate_Bjorkgren" title="Nate Bjorkgren">Nate Bjorkgren</a> coach the Jam in place of <a href="/wiki/Will_Voigt" title="Will Voigt">Will Voigt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Since the <a href="/wiki/Dignity_Health_Event_Center" title="Dignity Health Event Center">Dignity Health Event Center</a> seated only 500, the team did not sell individual general admission tickets and were sold to season ticket holders only.</p>
<p>On May 29, 2015, it was announced that Nate Bjorkgren would leave the Jam to take part in the Suns' organization as a leading player development and assistant coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Three months later, former NBA coach <a href="/wiki/Chris_Jent" title="Chris Jent">Chris Jent</a> would be the newest head coach for the Jam.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Move_to_Prescott_Valley">Move to Prescott Valley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Arizona_Suns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Move to Prescott Valley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 12, 2016, the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Suns" title="Phoenix Suns">Phoenix Suns</a> announced that the organization had officially purchased the Jam and were relocating the franchise to the town of <a href="/wiki/Prescott_Valley,_Arizona" title="Prescott Valley, Arizona">Prescott Valley, Arizona</a> for the 2016–17 season, where the team would be known as the Northern Arizona Suns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In response, the previous owners of the Jam franchise, Stan Ellis and David Higdon, announced that they have been working with the D-League in securing a new franchise and affiliation before the 2016–17 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On May 2, it was announced that former <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Sundogs" title="Arizona Sundogs">Arizona Sundogs</a> and current <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Rattlers" title="Arizona Rattlers">Arizona Rattlers</a> president, Chris Presson, would become the new team president for the Suns. The new team logo and jerseys for Northern Arizona would be officially unveiled on May 11, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The new front office personnel and coaching staff would officially be unveiled on August 16, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
