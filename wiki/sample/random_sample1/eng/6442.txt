<p><b>Baily James Cargill</b> (born 5 July 1995) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> for <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">AFC Bournemouth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Cargill is a left footed centre back who can also play at full back.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baily_Cargill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bournemouth">Bournemouth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baily_Cargill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bournemouth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Winchester" title="Winchester">Winchester</a>, England, Cargill grew up as a <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> fan and even had a season ticket for the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baily_Cargill_Southampton_Supporter_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Baily_Cargill_Southampton_Supporter-4">[4]</a></sup> After spells at the academies of <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> (in which he stayed on for two years<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baily_Cargill_Southampton_Supporter_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Baily_Cargill_Southampton_Supporter-4">[4]</a></sup>), Cargill joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AFC_Bournemouth" title="AFC Bournemouth">AFC Bournemouth</a>'s youth ranks, which Cargill stated he joined the club's U13 academy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and turned professional in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After a loan spell with A.F.C. Totton and Welling, Cargill signed his first professional contract on a one-year contract in April 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>After making an impressive display in the pre-season friendly and his loan spell at Welling at the first half of the 2013–14 season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Cargill signed a two-year and a half contract, keeping him until 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In effort of making a first team breakthrough ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_A.F.C._Bournemouth_season" title="2014–15 A.F.C. Bournemouth season">2014–15</a> season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Cargill made his first senior appearance for Bournemouth on 12 August 2014 as a centre-back in a 2–0 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Cup" title="Football League Cup">League Cup</a> win at <a href="/wiki/Exeter_City_F.C." title="Exeter City F.C.">Exeter City</a>, and the following week he was rewarded with a place on the bench in Bournemouth's 2–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest_F.C." title="Nottingham Forest F.C.">Nottingham Forest</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_Championship" title="Football Championship">Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The defender retained his place for the next round of the League Cup on 26 August as Bournemouth beat <a href="/wiki/Northampton_Town_F.C." title="Northampton Town F.C.">Northampton Town</a> 3–0, and also played in the third round 3–0 victory at <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> on 23 September and the Cherries' 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> in the fourth round on 28 October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> His impressive display earned him a contract extension, keeping him until 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Then, Cargill started in the match against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Football_League_Cup#Fifth_round" title="2014–15 Football League Cup">fifth round of the Football League Cup</a>, which Bournemouth lost 3–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> During the match, Cargill unintentionally made headlines after having a wig, which "fell off his head, and onto the pitch".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> One of his teammates, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Arter" title="Harry Arter">Harry Arter</a> came to his defense after he believed that <a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a> wrote an article about Cargill and accused the newspaper "bullying".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_spells">Loan spells</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baily_Cargill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Loan spells">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2012 he was loaned to <a href="/wiki/Southern_Football_League" title="Southern Football League">Southern League</a> club <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Totton" title="A.F.C. Totton">AFC Totton</a> on a one-month deal as part of a work experience arrangement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The loan was later extended until the end of the season. Cargill made his Totton debut in a 2–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Forest_Green_Rovers_F.C." title="Forest Green Rovers F.C.">Forest Green Rovers</a> in the FA Trophy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> At Totton, Cargill cemented himself as a first-team regular, scoring 3 goals in 26 appearances and being voted the Fans' Player of the Season. Cargill later credited Totton with helping to make him the player he is today.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 4 October 2013, Cargill joined <a href="/wiki/Welling_United_F.C." title="Welling United F.C.">Welling United</a> on a one-month loan until 2 November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Cargill made his Welling United debut the next day, in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Barnet_F.C." title="Barnet F.C.">Barnet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Cargill loan spell with Welling United was soon extended until 29 December 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Once again, Cargill was extended further until 22 January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> After his loan spell with Welling United came to an end with thirteen appearance, Cargill said his time at Welling United helped him with confidence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the end of the 2013–14 season <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hargreaves" title="Chris Hargreaves">Chris Hargreaves</a>, having recently moved from a coaching role at Bournemouth's academy to become manager of <a href="/wiki/Torquay_United_F.C." title="Torquay United F.C.">Torquay United</a>, signed Cargill on loan to play at left-back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Cargill made his Torquay United debut, in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Rovers_F.C." title="Bristol Rovers F.C.">Bristol Rovers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Cargill then provided assist for <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Yeoman" title="Ashley Yeoman">Ashley Yeoman</a>, in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Exeter_City_F.C." title="Exeter City F.C.">Exeter City</a> on 21 April 2014, in hope of survival relegation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> However, the pair were unable to prevent the Gulls' relegation to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_Conference" title="Football Conference">Conference</a>, as Cargill made five appearance for the club.</p>
<p>On 1 February 2016, Cargill joined <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a> on loan until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_in_English_football" title="2015–16 in English football">season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baily_Cargill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 6 November 2014, Cargill was called into the England youth set up for the first time, being named in the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-20_football_team" title="England national under-20 football team">England U20</a> squad for matches against Portugal and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> He started the Canada game and capped a dream debut by scoring a goal, which he described as the "best feeling".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
