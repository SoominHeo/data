<p><b>Beja</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar">باجة</span>‎‎ <i><span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Beja.wav" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/19/Beja.wav" title="Beja.wav">Bāja</a></span></span></i>, French: <span lang="fr"><i>Béja</i></span>) is a city in <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>, Africa. It is the capital of the <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9ja_Governorate" title="Béja Governorate">Béja Governorate</a>. It is located 105 kilometers (65 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Tunis" title="Tunis">Tunis</a>, between the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medjerdah_River" title="Medjerdah River">Medjerdah River</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>, against the foothills of the <a href="/wiki/Khroumire" title="Khroumire">Khroumire</a>, the town of Beja is situated on the sides of Djebel Acheb, facing the greening meadows, its white terraces and red roofs dominated by the imposing ruins of the old <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_empire" title="Roman empire">Roman</a> fortress.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B%C3%A9ja&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Vaga_(Tunisia)" title="Vaga (Tunisia)">Vaga (Tunisia)</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B%C3%A9ja&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Classical_era_period">Classical era period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B%C3%A9ja&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Classical era period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city endured brutal assaults by the <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthaginians</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Numidians" title="Numidians">Numidians</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Romans</a>, and, later on, by the <a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a>. The Numidian king <a href="/wiki/Jugurtha" title="Jugurtha">Jugurtha</a> made the town his governing headquarters. Originally the town was named Waga, which became Vacca and then Vaga under the Romans and eventually Baja under the <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a> and Béja under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_occupation_of_Tunisia" title="French occupation of Tunisia">French</a>.</p>
<p>The Romans destroyed the old Carthaginian citadel and replaced it with a new one; they built fortifications that are still standing today. Under the Roman domination, Béja became prosperous and was the center of a diocese. According to <a href="/wiki/Sallust" title="Sallust">Sallust</a>, who relates the details of the <a href="/wiki/Jugurthine_War" title="Jugurthine War">Jugurthine War</a> between Jugurtha and <a href="/wiki/Quintus_Caecilius_Metellus_Numidicus" title="Quintus Caecilius Metellus Numidicus">Quintus Caecilius Metellus Numidicus</a> to possess Béja, Béja was the wealthiest warehouse of the kingdom and the center of intense commerce.</p>
<p>The city was taken and destroyed by the Vandals. The citadel and ramparts were demolished. The abandoned town remained in that state for a century until the arrival of the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantines</a>. They renovated the fortress and took real pleasure in beautifying Béja.</p>
<p>After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Umayyad_conquest_of_North_Africa" title="Umayyad conquest of North Africa">Umayyad conquest of North Africa</a>, the city became part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Umayyad" title="Umayyad">Umayyad</a> Caliphate.</p>
<p>In 1880 France occupied Tunisia. On April 24, 1881 Béja in its turn was occupied by the column led by Logerot who had arrived from <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Kef_Governorate" title="Kef Governorate">Kef</a>.</p>
<p>Béja remains a picturesque city with wide horizons, a healthy climate and rich and fertile soil. Another distinctive feature is its family life, its traditional friendliness and hospitality towards foreigners.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B%C3%A9ja&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 16, 1942 a German military delegation came to Béja to give Mayor Jean Hugon a 24 hours ultimatum to surrender the city. As a reaction to the ultimatum the latter informed civil governor Mr Clement who in turn sent the message to Algiers. The next day, November 17, the first parachuted British battalion landed on the hills north of the city. Thursday November 19 German planes bombed the town for the first time, as a warning. This broke the long period of peace Béja had known for many centuries. The next day, Friday November 20, Béja was furiously bombed by German airplanes for many hours, because of its key position leading to the roads of Tabarka, Mateur, Bizerte and Algeria. The town became the stage for ferocious battles between the Germans and the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allies</a> who fiercely defended it, at the expense of severe military and civil losses. The final German assault <a href="/wiki/Operation_Ochsenkopf" title="Operation Ochsenkopf">Operation <i>Ochsenkopf</i></a> – was launched from Mateur and was halted 15 km from Béja, on the night of February 28, 1943 by British troops.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_features">Geographical features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B%C3%A9ja&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geographical features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Located in <a href="/wiki/North_West_Tunisia" title="North West Tunisia">north-western Tunisia</a> on the <i>White Hill</i> and crossed by the <a href="/wiki/Medjerda_River" title="Medjerda River">Medjerda River</a>, the features made the city famous for its fertile soil, Béja drew all the masters of the Mediterranean. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phoenicians" title="Phoenicians">Phoenicians</a> set up important trading posts. Their presence is felt through numerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punic" title="Punic">Punic</a> <a href="/wiki/Necropolis" title="Necropolis">necropolis</a> which have been unearthed in 1887. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carthaginians" title="Carthaginians">Carthaginians</a>, recognizing the importance of maintaining their authority in this area, built a garrison and fortified the town. Béja was extremely desirable, not only because of its fertile soil but because of its geographic location. It was at the doorway of the mountains and it was the crossroad for <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthage</a> and Tunis going toward <a href="/wiki/Cirta" title="Cirta">Cirta</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hippone" title="Hippone">Hippone</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B%C3%A9ja&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Like the rest of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tunisians" title="Tunisians">Tunisians</a>, most of the Béjeans are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> with a small minority of irreligious. In the past there was a small community of <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> and a bigger one of Christians (most of them were European colons), but after the independence of <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> from France, all them quit the city to Europe, North America and <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>. The city shelters many religion buildings like mosques, churches and synagogues. The oldest mosque of the town is <i>Great Mosque of Béja</i> which was built in 944 by the <a href="/wiki/Fatimid_Caliphate" title="Fatimid Caliphate">Fatimidis</a> on an old Christian <a href="/wiki/Basilica" title="Basilica">basilica</a>, near it there is an other mosque, <i>The Bey's Mosque</i>, which was built in 1675 by <a href="/wiki/Murad_II_Bey" title="Murad II Bey">Murad II Bey</a> for <a href="/wiki/Hanafi" title="Hanafi">Hanafi Muslims</a> of the city and in 1685 <a href="/wiki/Mohamed_Bey_El_Mouradi" title="Mohamed Bey El Mouradi">Mohamed Bey El Mouradi</a> added a <a href="/wiki/Madrasa" title="Madrasa">Madrasa</a> to the mosque.</p>

<p>After the settlement of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_protectorate_in_Tunisia" title="French protectorate in Tunisia">French protectorate in Tunisia</a>, many Europeans come to the city to exploit the rich agricultural land, so to satisfy their religious demands the colonial authorities decided a church which was completed in 1883. After the increasing in number of colons in Béja, the church become too small for them, so the authorities decided to demolish it and build instead of it a bigger one, <i>The Notre-Dame-du-Rosaire Church</i>. After the independence of Tunisia and the migration of Christians from the country, the church become a cultural centre in Béja.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_Places">Historical Places</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=B%C3%A9ja&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Historical Places">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Kasbah_of_B%C3%A9ja" title="Kasbah of Béja">Kasbah of Béja</a></b> : The Kasbah of Béja, is a castle built during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Africa" title="Roman Africa">Roman</a> area over the ruins of another <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthaginian</a> castle, the Kasbah was destroyed during the Vandal invasion of Tunisia, it was lately rebuilt by the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> and improved and fortified through the years of the Islamic rule, the Kasbah played a major role in protecting the city from various invasion, it's located on the top of the city.<br/>
<b>Béja Bardo Palace</b> : Is an ancient royal residence built by the heir of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Husainid_Dynasty" title="Husainid Dynasty">Husainid</a> thrown <a href="/wiki/Abu_l-Hasan_Ali_I" title="Abu l-Hasan Ali I">Ali Pecha II</a> in 1734 then it was renovated by <a href="/wiki/Ali_II_ibn_Hussein" title="Ali II ibn Hussein">Ali Pecha II</a>. The Bardo Palace of Béja is now in ruins.<br/>
<b>The Great Mosque of Béja</b> : The Great Mosque of Béja is one of the oldest mosques of the country, built in 944 by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatimide" title="Fatimide">Fatimide</a> Caliph <a href="/wiki/Al-Mansur_Billah" title="Al-Mansur Billah">Al-Mansur Billah</a> in 944 on the ruins of an old Roman basilica.<br/>
<b>Mosque Al-Jazzar</b> is a very old mosque built by Ahmed Al-Jazzar, a Muslim ascetic, during the <a href="/wiki/Aghlabids" title="Aghlabids">Aghlabib era</a> in the 10th century.<br/>
<b>The Hanafi Mosque</b> : The Hanafi Mosque is a mosque built in Béja in 1675 by <a href="/wiki/Murad_II_Bey" title="Murad II Bey">Murad II Bey</a>, it's called the Hanafit mosque because it was dedicated to the <a href="/wiki/Hanafi" title="Hanafi">Hanafi</a> minority of the city.<br/>
<b>Sidi Boutefeha Mausoleum</b> : Sidi Boutefeha Mausoleum is a mausoleum built in the 17th century in memory to the young Sufi <a href="/wiki/Wali" title="Wali">Wali</a> Sidi Sulaymeb Al-Tamimi who was known as Boutefeha (The Father of The Appel).<br/>
<b>Sidi Baba Ali Smadhi Mausoleum</b> : Sidi Baba Ali Smadhi Mausoleum is mausoleum built 1666 by the Sufi <a href="/wiki/Marabout" title="Marabout">Marabout</a> Ali Smadhi. The Mausoleum played a major role during the Husainid-Pechist civil war, it's also an important cultural and political center of Béja.<br/></p>

<p><b>The Qadiriyya Mausoleum</b> : The Qadiriyya Mausoleum is a mausoleum belonging to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qadiri" title="Qadiri">Qadiri</a> Sufi order who was very influential in the city, the mausoleum was built in 1816 by the Sufi poet Miled Jaweni Cherif. The Qadiriyya was also known as the Nakhla Mosque.<br/>
<b>The Khadharin Mausoleum</b> : The Khadharin Mausoleum is another Qadiri Sufi order mausoleum built in 1780 by Ahmed Blagui.<br/>
<b>Sidi Salah Zlaoui Mausoleum</b> : Sidi Salah Zlaoui Mausoleum is mausoleum built in the 18th century by Salah Ibn Mohamed Zlaoui who was a very famous Sufist in Béja, the mausoleum was transformed by Jilani Zlaoui, one of Sidi Salah great-grandsons, into a mosque.<br/>
<b>Hammam Bousandel</b> : Hammam Bousandel, is a public bath built in the 10th century, the bath is still operative till today.<br/>
<b>Sabil Saheb Ettabaâ</b> : Sabil Saheb Ettabaâ is a fountain built by <a href="/wiki/Head_of_Government_of_Tunisia" title="Head of Government of Tunisia">Grand Vizier</a> Youssef Saheb Ettabaâ in 1800.</p>
