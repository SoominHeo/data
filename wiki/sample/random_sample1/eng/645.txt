<p>Year <b>536</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DXXXVI</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Tuesday" title="Leap year starting on Tuesday">leap year starting on Tuesday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year after the Consulship of Belisarius</b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 1289 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination 536 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=536&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=536&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=536&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Byzantine Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Spring – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Justinian_I" title="Justinian I">Justinian I</a> appoints his cousin <a href="/wiki/Germanus_(cousin_of_Justinian_I)" title="Germanus (cousin of Justinian I)">Germanus</a> as <i><a href="/wiki/Magister_militum" title="Magister militum">magister militum</a></i> to deal with the crisis in <a href="/wiki/Africa_(Roman_province)" title="Africa (Roman province)">Africa</a>. He sends a mobile force of <i><a href="/wiki/Comitatenses" title="Comitatenses">comitatenses</a></i> (mostly cavalry) and an elite guard. <a href="/wiki/Solomon_(Byzantine_general)" title="Solomon (Byzantine general)">Solomon</a> returns to <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>Summer – <a href="/wiki/Gothic_War_(535%E2%80%93554)" title="Gothic War (535–554)">Gothic War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Belisarius" title="Belisarius">Belisarius</a> crosses the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Messina" title="Strait of Messina">Strait of Messina</a> and invades <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. He conquers the city of <a href="/wiki/Reggio_Calabria" title="Reggio Calabria">Rhegium</a> and advances to <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>.</li>
<li>October – <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Naples_(536)" title="Siege of Naples (536)">Siege of Naples</a>: Belisarius captures Naples after a month's <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a>, by sending troops into the city through an abandoned <a href="/wiki/Roman_aqueduct" title="Roman aqueduct">Roman aqueduct</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_9" title="December 9">December 9</a> – Belisarius enters <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Porta_Asinaria" title="Porta Asinaria">Asinarian Gate</a>; the Gothic garrison (4,000 men) flee the capital. He sends an urgent request for reinforcements to Justinian I, meanwhile preparing Rome for a siege by bringing in great quantities of <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">food</a> and other supplies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Winter – Belisarius sets up his headquarters on the <a href="/wiki/Pincian_Hill" title="Pincian Hill">Pincian Hill</a> and repairs the neglected <a href="/wiki/Aurelian_Walls" title="Aurelian Walls">city walls</a> of Rome. He stations a 5,000-man garrison, of whom half are his personal bodyguard (<i><a href="/wiki/Bucellarius" title="Bucellarius">bucellarii</a></i>). To hold parts of the city, he recruits 20,000 young Romans to man the walls.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=536&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>March – King <a href="/wiki/Theodahad" title="Theodahad">Theodahad</a> cedes <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">Provence</a> and upper <a href="/wiki/Alamannia" title="Alamannia">Alamannia</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Franks</a> gaining their support in the war. He sends a large <a href="/wiki/Goths" title="Goths">Gothic</a> army into <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">Dalmatia</a>. They defeat the Byzantines, <a href="/wiki/Mundus_(general)" title="Mundus (general)">Mundus</a> is killed during the fightings at <a href="/wiki/Salona" title="Salona">Salona</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_army" title="Byzantine army">Byzantine army</a> withdraws.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Summer – Constantinianus, <i>magister militum per Illyricum</i>, retakes Dalmatia. The Goths abandon Salona and withdraw to the north. The <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantines</a> rebuild its walls and reclaim the province.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vitiges" title="Vitiges">Vitiges</a> deposes his rival Theodahad at <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a> and marries <a href="/wiki/Matasuntha" title="Matasuntha">Matasuntha</a> (daughter of queen <a href="/wiki/Amalasuntha" title="Amalasuntha">Amalasuntha</a>). He becomes king of the <a href="/wiki/Ostrogoths" title="Ostrogoths">Ostrogoths</a> and assembles an army to fight against Belisarius.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=536&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>March – Belisarius sails to <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthage</a> with 1,000 men, to suppress a <a href="/wiki/Mutiny" title="Mutiny">mutiny</a> against Solomon. Meanwhile the capital is besieged by 9,000 rebels, including many <a href="/wiki/Vandals" title="Vandals">Vandals</a>, under <a href="/wiki/Stotzas" title="Stotzas">Stotzas</a>. Belisarius defeats the mutineers and hurries back to <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=536&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Emperor_Senka" title="Emperor Senka">Senka</a> succeeds his brother <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ankan" title="Emperor Ankan">Ankan</a> as the 28th <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">emperor of Japan</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=536&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=536&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_22" title="April 22">April 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Agapetus_I" title="Pope Agapetus I">Pope Agapetus I</a> dies in Constantinople after a reign of just ten months. He is succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Silverius" title="Pope Silverius">Silverius</a> as the 58th <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pope</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patriarch_Anthimus_I_of_Constantinople" title="Patriarch Anthimus I of Constantinople">Anthimus I</a> is deposed as <a href="/wiki/Ecumenical_Patriarch_of_Constantinople" title="Ecumenical Patriarch of Constantinople">patriarch of Constantinople</a>, in favour of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patriarch_Menas_of_Constantinople" title="Patriarch Menas of Constantinople">Menas</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Little_Hagia_Sophia" title="Little Hagia Sophia">Little Hagia Sophia</a> in Constantinople (begun in 527) is completed.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorology">Meteorology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=536&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Meteorology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Extreme_weather_events_of_535%E2%80%93536" title="Extreme weather events of 535–536">Extreme weather events</a>, thought to have been caused by an extensive veil of dust in the atmosphere, begin in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">Northern Hemisphere</a>. They continue until the following year, causing unseasonal <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">weather</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plant_pathology" title="Plant pathology">crop failure</a> worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-failedbread536_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-failedbread536-6">[n 1]</a></sup> It is possible this was caused by the eruption of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ilopango" title="Lake Ilopango">Ilopango</a>.</li>
</ul>
