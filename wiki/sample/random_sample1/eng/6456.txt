<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">List of diplomatic missions of Vietnam</h1>

<p>This is a <b>list of diplomatic missions of Vietnam</b>, excluding <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honorary_consul" title="Honorary consul">honorary consulates</a>. The first overseas presence of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Vietnam" title="Democratic Republic of Vietnam">Democratic Republic of Vietnam</a> (the antecedent to the Socialist Republic of Vietnam) was a representative office in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, approximately during the period of the <a href="/wiki/Fontainebleau" title="Fontainebleau">Fontainebleau</a> Conference in 1946–1947. There was later a representative office operating in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a> from 1948, although it was closed in 1951 when the Thai government recognised the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Vietnam" title="Republic of Vietnam">Republic of Vietnam</a>. The Democratic Republic of Vietnam's first embassy was opened in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> in 1950, followed by <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> in 1952, and consulates in <a href="/wiki/Nanning" title="Nanning">Nanning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kunming" title="Kunming">Kunming</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guangzhou" title="Guangzhou">Guangzhou</a> opening shortly afterwards. In 1964 the DRV had opened 19 diplomatic missions abroad; six years later this number increased to 30.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Vietnam" title="Republic of Vietnam">Republic of Vietnam</a> also had its own separate diplomatic network until the <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_Saigon" title="Fall of Saigon">fall of Saigon</a> in 1975.</p>
