<p>Vietnam is a unitary state in South East Asian. Vietnam is officially subdivided into 3 administrative tiers:</p>
<ul>
<li>First tier or province-level (<i>cấp tỉnh</i>): <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Vietnam" title="Provinces of Vietnam">provinces</a> (<i>tỉnh</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Vietnam" title="Municipalities of Vietnam">municipalities</a> (<i>thành phố trực thuộc trung ương</i>)</li>
<li>Second tier or district-level (<i>cấp huyện</i>): <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Districts_of_Vietnam" title="Districts of Vietnam">rural districts</a> (<i>huyện</i>), <a href="/wiki/List_of_urban_districts_of_Vietnam" title="List of urban districts of Vietnam">urban districts</a> (<i>quận</i>), <a href="/wiki/Provincial_city_(Vietnam)" title="Provincial city (Vietnam)">provincial cities</a> (<i>thành phố trực thuộc tỉnh</i>) and <a href="/wiki/District-level_town_(Vietnam)" title="District-level town (Vietnam)">towns</a> (<i>thị xã</i>)</li>
<li>Third tier or commune-level (<i>cấp xã</i>): <a href="/wiki/Rural_commune_(Vietnam)" title="Rural commune (Vietnam)">communes</a> (<i>xã</i>), <a href="/wiki/Ward_(Vietnam)" title="Ward (Vietnam)">wards</a> (<i>phường</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Commune-level_town_(Vietnam)" title="Commune-level town (Vietnam)">townships</a> (<i>thị trấn</i>)</li>
</ul>
<p>The fourth tier or village-level also exists as hamlet (<i>xóm</i>, <i>ấp</i>) and village (<i>làng</i>, <i>thôn</i>, <i>bản</i>). However this is not an official tier.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Province-level">Province-level</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Subdivisions_of_Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Province-level">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are:</p>
<ul>
<li>5 <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Vietnam" title="Municipalities of Vietnam">municipalities</a> (also known as centrally-controlled cities): <a href="/wiki/Hanoi" title="Hanoi">Ha Noi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City" title="Ho Chi Minh City">Ho Chi Minh City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haiphong" title="Haiphong">Hai Phong</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Da_Nang" title="Da Nang">Da Nang</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Can_Tho" title="Can Tho">Can Tho</a></li>
<li>58 <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Vietnam" title="Provinces of Vietnam">provinces</a></li>
</ul>
<p>The local authority at province-level includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>People's Council (<i>hội đồng nhân dân</i>) - legislative branch: headed by a Chairman</li>
<li>People's Committee (<i>ủy ban nhân dân</i>) - executive branch: headed by a Chairman</li>
<li>People's Court (<i>tòa án nhân dân</i>) - judiciary branch: headed by a Chief Judge</li>
<li>Committee of the Communist Party (<i>ban chấp hành đảng bộ</i>): headed by a Secretary</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="District-level">District-level</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Subdivisions_of_Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: District-level">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>see also <a href="/wiki/List_of_district-level_subdivisions_in_Vietnam" title="List of district-level subdivisions in Vietnam">List of district-level subdivisions in Vietnam</a></i></p>
<p>The provinces are subdivided into provincial cities, towns (for urban area) and rural districts. Normally the provincial city or town is the capital of that province.</p>
<p>The municipalities are subdivided into rural districts and urban districts. There is no official capital of the municipality but usually the seat of local authority is located at a central urban district.</p>
<p>Based on latest sources of General Satistics Office of Vietnam (GSO), there are 713 district-level subdivisions with 546 districts, 49 urban districts, 51 towns, 67 provincial cities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The rural districts can be upgraded to towns which can be upgraded to provincial cities.</p>
<p>The local authority at district-level includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>People's Council (<i>hội đồng nhân dân</i>) - legislative branch: headed by a Chairman. However, since 2008, Vietnam started to abolish the People's Council at some districts and urban districts.</li>
<li>People's Committee (<i>ủy ban nhân dân</i>) - executive branch: headed by a Chairman</li>
<li>People's Court (<i>tòa án nhân dân</i>) - judiciary branch: headed by a Chief Judge</li>
<li>Committee of the Communist Party (<i>ban chấp hành đảng bộ</i>): headed by a Secretary</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commune-level">Commune-level</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Subdivisions_of_Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commune-level">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The provincial cities and towns are subdivided into wards (for urban area) and communes (for rural area). There is no designated capital for provincial cities or towns, but usually the seat of local authority is located in a central ward.</p>
<p>The urban districts are subdivided into wards. The rural districts are subdivided into townships (for urban area) and communes (for rural area). There is no designated capital for urban and rural districts, but usually the seat of local authority is located in a central ward or central township</p>
<p>There are 11162 Commune-level units with 8978 communes, 1581 wards, 603 townships, GSO said in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Townships are known as thị trấn in Vietnamese, but less common type of townships are farm townships (thị trấn nông trường). <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The communes can be upgraded to townships (in rural districts) or wards (in provincial cities or towns).</p>
<p>The local authority at commune-level includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>People's Council (<i>hội đồng nhân dân</i>) - legislative branch: headed by a Chairman. However, since 2008, Vietnam started to abolish the People's Council at some wards.</li>
<li>People's Committee (<i>ủy ban nhân dân</i>) - executive branch: headed by a Chairman</li>
<li>Committee of the Communist Party (<i>ban chấp hành đảng bộ</i>): headed by a Secretary</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_subdivisions">Other subdivisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Subdivisions_of_Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other subdivisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Apart from administrative divisions, Vietnam is also divided into <a href="/wiki/List_of_regions_of_Vietnam" title="List of regions of Vietnam">geographical regions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_of_Vietnam" title="Electoral divisions of Vietnam">electoral divisions</a> and military regions.</p>

<p>Vietnam is geographically divided into:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Northern_Vietnam" title="Northern Vietnam">Northern Vietnam</a> (<i>Bắc Bộ</i>): <a href="/wiki/Northwest_(Vietnam)" title="Northwest (Vietnam)">Northwest</a> (<i>Tây Bắc Bộ</i>), <a href="/wiki/Northeast_(Vietnam)" title="Northeast (Vietnam)">Northeast</a> (<i>Đông Bắc Bộ</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Red_River_Delta" title="Red River Delta">Red River Delta</a> (<i>Đồng Bằng Sông Hồng</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Central_Vietnam" title="Central Vietnam">Central Vietnam</a> (<i>Trung Bộ</i>): <a href="/wiki/North_Central_Coast" title="North Central Coast">North Central Coast</a> (<i>Bắc Trung Bộ</i>), <a href="/wiki/South_Central_Coast" title="South Central Coast">South Central Coast</a> (<i>Nam Trung Bộ</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Central_Highlands_(Vietnam)" title="Central Highlands (Vietnam)">Central Highland</a> (<i>Tây Nguyên</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Southern_Vietnam" title="Southern Vietnam">Southern Vietnam</a> (<i>Nam Bộ</i>): <a href="/wiki/Southeast_(Vietnam)" title="Southeast (Vietnam)">Southeast</a> (<i>Đông Nam Bộ</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Mekong_Delta" title="Mekong Delta">Mekong Delta</a> (<i>Đồng Bằng Sông Cửu Long</i>)</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Electoral_divisions">Electoral divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Subdivisions_of_Vietnam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Electoral divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_of_Vietnam" title="Electoral divisions of Vietnam">Electoral divisions of Vietnam</a></div>
<p>For electoral purpose, each province or municipality is divided into electoral units (<i>đơn vị bầu cử</i>) which are further divided into voting zones (<i>khu vự bỏ phiếu</i>). The number of electoral divisions varies from election to election and depends on the population of that province or municipality.</p>
<p>Since the parliamentary election in 2011, there are 183 electoral units and 89,960 voting zones.</p>

<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vietnam_People%27s_Army" title="Vietnam People's Army">Vietnam People's Army</a> is organised into 8 military regions:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_Command_of_Capital_Hanoi" title="High Command of Capital Hanoi">High Command of Capital Hanoi</a> (<i>Bộ Tư lệnh Thủ đô Hà Nội</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Hanoi" title="Hanoi">Ha Noi</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1st_Military_Region_(Vietnam_People%27s_Army)" title="1st Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)">1st Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)</a> (<i>Quân khu 1</i>) in Northeast</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/2nd_Military_Region_(Vietnam_People%27s_Army)" title="2nd Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)">2nd Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)</a> (<i>Quân khu 2</i>) in Northwest</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/3rd_Military_Region_(Vietnam_People%27s_Army)" title="3rd Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)">3rd Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)</a> (<i>Quân khu 3</i>) in Red River Delta</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/4th_Military_Region_(Vietnam_People%27s_Army)" title="4th Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)">4th Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)</a> (<i>Quân khu 4</i>) in North Central Coast</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/5th_Military_Region_(Vietnam_People%27s_Army)" title="5th Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)">5th Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)</a> (<i>Quân khu 5</i>) in South Central Coast and Central Highland</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/7th_Military_Region_(Vietnam_People%27s_Army)" title="7th Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)">7th Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)</a> (<i>Quân khu 7</i>) in Southeast</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/9th_Military_Region_(Vietnam_People%27s_Army)" title="9th Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)">9th Military Region (Vietnam People's Army)</a> (<i>Quân khu 9</i>) in Mekong Delta</li>
</ul>
