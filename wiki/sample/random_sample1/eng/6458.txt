<p><b>Elizabeth</b> "<b>Betty</b>" <b>Ross</b> (later <b>Betty Talbot</b> and then <b>Betty Banner</b>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">fictional character</a> created by <a href="/wiki/Stan_Lee" title="Stan Lee">Stan Lee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Kirby" title="Jack Kirby">Jack Kirby</a>, that appears in comic books published by <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel Comics</a>. She made her first appearance in <i>Incredible Hulk</i> #1 (1962) as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romantic_interest" title="Romantic interest">romantic interest</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Hulk_(comics)" title="Hulk (comics)">Hulk</a> (Dr. Bruce Banner) and is the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Thunderbolt_Ross" title="Thunderbolt Ross">General Thunderbolt Ross</a>. Over the years, the character has undergone multiple transformations, including the <a href="/wiki/Antihero" title="Antihero">antiheroine</a> <b>Red She-Hulk</b> (or <b>She-Rulk</b>).</p>
<p>She was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Connelly" title="Jennifer Connelly">Jennifer Connelly</a> in the 2003 film <i><a href="/wiki/Hulk_(film)" title="Hulk (film)">Hulk</a></i> and in the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Cinematic_Universe" title="Marvel Cinematic Universe">Marvel Cinematic Universe</a> by <a href="/wiki/Liv_Tyler" title="Liv Tyler">Liv Tyler</a> in the 2008 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Hulk_(film)" title="The Incredible Hulk (film)">The Incredible Hulk</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publication_history">Publication history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Publication history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Betty Ross debuted in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Hulk_(comic_book)" title="The Incredible Hulk (comic book)">The Incredible Hulk</a></i> #1 (May 1962) by writer <a href="/wiki/Stan_Lee" title="Stan Lee">Stan Lee</a> and artist <a href="/wiki/Jack_Kirby" title="Jack Kirby">Jack Kirby</a>. She was an on-and-off again <a href="/wiki/Supporting_character" title="Supporting character">supporting character</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hulk_(comics)" title="Hulk (comics)">Hulk</a>'s various series for decades, serving as his longest running <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_interest" title="Love interest">love interest</a>. In 1989, Betty Ross Banner received an entry in the <i><a href="/wiki/Official_Handbook_of_the_Marvel_Universe" title="Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe">Official Handbook of the Marvel Universe Update '89</a></i> #1.</p>
<p>Stan Lee originally portrayed Betty Ross as a strong willed and independent-minded, yet conventionally polite woman. Mid-1980s <i>Incredible Hulk</i> writer/artist <a href="/wiki/John_Byrne_(comics)" title="John Byrne (comics)">John Byrne</a> portrayed her as more wilful and confrontational, characterizations which would remain in place during <a href="/wiki/Peter_David" title="Peter David">Peter David</a>'s long run as the series' writer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shayer_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shayer-2">[2]</a></sup> Betty has a <a href="/wiki/Miscarriage" title="Miscarriage">miscarriage</a> in <i>The Incredible Hulk</i> vol.2, #360. Though this occurred during David's run on the series, the issue was instead written by editor <a href="/wiki/Bob_Harras" title="Bob Harras">Bob Harras</a>. David recalled, "The reason I refused to do it was because Betty was really losing her child to editorial fiat. It was decided by <a href="/wiki/The_powers_that_be_(phrase)" title="The powers that be (phrase)">the powers-that-be</a> that Betty and Bruce were not to become parents because that would make the characters seem ‘too old’ to the younger readers. My run on the book almost ended with that issue; I nearly walked over it. But there were so many stories I still wanted to tell that ultimately I stayed with it, even though I fumed about it for quite a while."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <i>Hulk</i> vol. 2 #15 (September 2009), she appears for the first time as Red She-Hulk, who was created by writer <a href="/wiki/Jeph_Loeb" title="Jeph Loeb">Jeph Loeb</a> and artist <a href="/wiki/Ed_McGuinness" title="Ed McGuinness">Ed McGuinness</a>. Loeb said, "We've been very careful with the creation of this character. We wanted to make sure she didn't come off as silly -my memory of the introduction to [the original] <a href="/wiki/She-Hulk" title="She-Hulk">She-Hulk</a>- before anyone had read a page. But the character was a completely different take on the Hulk, a Hulk we'd never seen before. Jen [Walters] is a wonderful character. Our intention is [that] Red She-Hulk will make an equally important impression on the Marvel Universe  [<i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a></i>]."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Red She-Hulk is also present in the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chaos_War_(comics)" title="Chaos War (comics)">Chaos War</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and "<a href="/wiki/Fear_Itself_(comics)" title="Fear Itself (comics)">Fear Itself</a>" storylines<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CoreMiniseries.237_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CoreMiniseries.237-6">[6]</a></sup> in 2010 and 2011 respectively. Red She-Hulk becomes a member of the superhero team the <a href="/wiki/Defenders_(comics)" title="Defenders (comics)">Defenders</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Defenders_(comic_book)" title="The Defenders (comic book)">The Defenders</a></i> vol. 4 #1 (December 2011) by writer <a href="/wiki/Matt_Fraction" title="Matt Fraction">Matt Fraction</a> and artist <a href="/wiki/Terry_Dodson" title="Terry Dodson">Terry Dodson</a>. Fraction said, "The way I write her is somewhere between <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Jones" title="Indiana Jones">Indiana Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Knoxville" title="Johnny Knoxville">Johnny Knoxville</a>; after a lifetime of being fought over and treated like a human football, she's [now] seven foot tall and 62-52-62 or whatever and bulletproof. She goes around leaving these Betty-shaped holes in the wall."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The series was discontinued in November 2012, after 12 issues.</p>
<p>In October 2012, as part of <a href="/wiki/Marvel_NOW!" title="Marvel NOW!">Marvel NOW!</a>, <i>Hulk</i> was retitled <i>Red She-Hulk'</i> starting with issue #58 by writer <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Parker_(comics)" title="Jeff Parker (comics)">Jeff Parker</a> and artist <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Pagulayan" title="Carlo Pagulayan">Carlo Pagulayan</a>. About the series Parker said, "She's become convinced of a threat to humanity which is essentially, all people like her. She's conflicted over losing her own human side, and she's acting out on a large scale based on that. But the thing is she may well be right."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>The only daughter of General <a href="/wiki/Thunderbolt_Ross" title="Thunderbolt Ross">Thaddeus E. "Thunderbolt" Ross</a>, Betty spent her formative years firmly under her father's strict supervision. After her mother died during Betty's teenage years, she was sent away to <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school" title="Boarding school">boarding school</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> After graduating, the introverted young woman returns to her father's side while he is in charge of a top-secret project to create a new type of weapon involving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gamma_radiation" title="Gamma radiation">gamma radiation</a>, known as the Gamma Bomb. The head scientist on the project is Dr. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Bruce_Banner" title="Robert Bruce Banner">Robert Bruce Banner</a>. Betty is immediately captivated by Banner's intellect and soft-spoken manner. However, less than an hour after their first meeting, Banner is caught in a test detonation of the Gamma Bomb and becomes the <a href="/wiki/Hulk_(comics)" title="Hulk (comics)">Incredible Hulk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Banner's efforts to keep his condition a secret from Betty only serve to alienate her from him. She is then romantically pursued by <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">Major</a> <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Talbot" title="Glenn Talbot">Glenn Talbot</a>, the new aide attached to her father's <a href="/wiki/Hulkbusters" title="Hulkbusters">Hulkbuster</a> task force.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (August 2012)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>After his dual identity becomes public knowledge Banner becomes a hunted fugitive. But with the help of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reed_Richards" title="Reed Richards">Reed Richards</a>, Banner is able to gain control over his transformations. Banner is pardoned and later proposes to Betty. But during the wedding ceremony, the Hulk's archenemy <a href="/wiki/Leader_(comics)" title="Leader (comics)">the Leader</a> causes Banner to transform back into the savage Hulk, and Banner, once again, becomes a fugitive. General Ross is seriously injured when the Hulk runs amok while battling the Rhino, and Glenn Talbot promises Betty that the Hulk would pay for it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a result of the failed wedding and Banner's relapse into the Hulk, Betty suffers a nervous breakdown and is hospitalized. In an effort by the Sandman to rid himself of his glass like form, the Sandman orders the Dr. Marquand to provide him with a patient with the same blood type as him which turns out to Betty Ross. As the result of the blood transfusion, the Sandman reverts to his previous form, yet Betty receives the glass properties, which Sandman surmises would eventually kill her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Incredible_Hulk_1971_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Incredible_Hulk_1971-11">[11]</a></sup> Betty's father recruits Dr. Leonard Samson to reverse the effect by siphoning the Hulk's Gamma and psionic energies to simultaneously cure Bruce and Betty. The result would have permanently cured both of them, if not for Bruce purposefully re-exposing himself to the siphoned Gamma energy, as a means to combat Samson who had also done so, and was subsequently flirting with Betty.This ended in Hulk defeating Samson, yet later on Samson would pursue the Hulk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Incredible_Hulk_1971_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Incredible_Hulk_1971-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Finding him in the aftermath of a battle as the Hulk, Betty hears Banner mumble, "<a href="/wiki/Jarella" title="Jarella">Jarella</a>... my love..."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> After Banner seemingly disappears from Earth forever, Betty accepts a marriage proposal from Glenn Talbot. While Betty and Talbot are on their honeymoon, her father is captured and sent to a Soviet prison. Talbot takes part in a successful rescue mission, but is captured in the process, held prisoner by the <a href="/wiki/Gremlin_(comics)" title="Gremlin (comics)">Gremlin</a> at Bitterfrost (a top secret Soviet installation in Siberia), and believed dead.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>The villain <a href="/wiki/MODOK" title="MODOK">MODOK</a> kidnaps her and subjects her to gamma radiation, at a higher level than Banner had been subjected to, transforming her into the insane and lethal Harpy creature. MODOK tells the Harpy where to find the Hulk and she flies off in search of him. She ambushes him, and after a lengthy fight knocks him out with a ray blast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Before she could take the Hulk back to MODOK, they are abducted by the <a href="/wiki/Bi-Beast" title="Bi-Beast">Bi-Beast</a> to his city in the sky. Banner agrees to repair the machines that cause the city to float in exchange for permission to use the advanced equipment to cure Betty. MODOK comes to the island and instigates a fight just as Banner starts the equipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Banner nonetheless manages to escape the collapsing city with a now-cured Betty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return">Return</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Talbot is eventually rescued by General Ross, Clay Quartermain and the Hulk.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (August 2012)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup> During his time in captivity by the Gremlin, Talbot was made into a mindless husk. In order to unblock Talbot's mind, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_Leonard_Samson" title="Doctor Leonard Samson">Doctor Leonard Samson</a> has the Hulk (who was Banner under control by a special helmet) unblock what was keeping him in a mindless state. The process is a success. However, The Talbots' marriage later becomes strained.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (August 2012)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>When General Ross suffers a nervous breakdown, Talbot returns to the military as a Colonel and it is revealed that he had fired a ray gun that sent the Hulk to the Sub-Atomic universe after the Hulk stormed into Gamma Base looking for Jarella. This incident proves to be the last straw in Talbot's already deteriorating relationship with Betty, and their marriage later ends in divorce. Blaming the failure of his marriage on Banner, whom he also tried to have court-martialed, Talbot steals the War Wagon prototype and dies in Japan while trying to destroy the Hulk. Betty admits to Rick Jones afterwards that she had never stopped loving Banner all the while she was married to Talbot.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (August 2012)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>When Betty learns that her father had conspired with MODOK to kill the Hulk, she accuses him of treason. Realizing Betty was right, Ross nearly commits suicide and then disappears.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (August 2012)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Banner again achieves a state in which he can control his transformations and maintain his normal personality and intelligence while in the form of the Hulk. But Betty is upset because she wants Banner to be rid of the Hulk, not to control him, and leaves him once again.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (August 2012)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup> When the Hulk disappears from Earth for an extended period, Betty begins dating a man named Ramon. Upon learning that the Hulk had been sighted on Earth once again, Betty leaves Ramon and returns to Gamma Base, where the Hulk is subjected to a process that splits Banner and the Hulk into separate entities. Believing himself finally cured, Banner proposes to Betty, and she accepts. Betty's father appears at the wedding, armed with a gun and demanding that the marriage not take place before shooting Rick Jones, who tries to stop him. Betty confronts her father, accusing him of domineering her throughout her life, as well as calling him out on his hostility towards Banner over the years, and cows him into surrendering the gun. Finally, Banner and Betty are pronounced husband and wife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>However, Banner begins dying as a result of being physically separated from the Hulk. The two are secretly merged once more. Betty soon discovers this. General Ross later dies before his daughter's eyes, sacrificing his life to destroy an unnamed mutant that nearly killed both Betty and Banner seeking a strong host to whom to be parasitically linked.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (August 2012)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Betty becomes distraught on learning that Banner had sometimes consciously triggered his transformation in the past and is willing to become the Hulk in order to deal with very menacing threats. Betty leaves her husband and returns to Ramon, but then changes her mind and abandons Ramon as well. She is then captured by the Leader, who sets her free after learning that she is pregnant with Banner's child, but after being tormented with terrible nightmares by the demons <a href="/wiki/Nightmare_(Marvel_Comics)" title="Nightmare (Marvel Comics)">Nightmare</a> and <a href="/wiki/D%27Spayre" title="D'Spayre">D'Spayre</a>, Betty loses her unborn baby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is eventually reunited with Banner, but soon afterward the Hulk seemingly perishes in a tremendous explosion at Gammatown. Believing Banner and the Hulk dead, Betty leaves for New York City, where she eventually begins training to become a nun. Betty spends some months in a convent to recover from the ordeal, but eventually reunites with Banner. They spend years living together as fugitives until the Hulk's enemy <a href="/wiki/Abomination_(comics)" title="Abomination (comics)">Abomination</a> uses his own blood to poison Betty, which would appear to be the work of the Hulk himself (due to the high levels of gamma radiation present in both of their bodies). Betty is placed in <a href="/wiki/Cryogenics" title="Cryogenics">cryogenic</a> suspension by her father.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (August 2012)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In a later retconned story arc, Betty is seemingly revived by the <a href="/wiki/Leader_(comics)" title="Leader (comics)">Leader</a>, undergoes surgery which considerably alters her appearance, is granted superhuman strength; and for a time aids her fugitive husband as his shadowy contact, Mr. Blue.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (August 2012)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Her resurrection is later revealed as a reality-distorting hallucination created by Nightmare, who supposedly raped her in her sleep to conceive his daughter, Daydream.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Red_She-Hulk">Red She-Hulk</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Red She-Hulk">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>During the <i><a href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Hulks" title="Fall of the Hulks">Fall of the Hulks</a></i> storyline, Betty Ross is revealed to have been resurrected by the Leader and <a href="/wiki/MODOK" title="MODOK">MODOK</a> at the urgings of their new ally, her father Thunderbolt Ross, who had previously kept her body in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cryonic" title="Cryonic">cryonic</a> stasis. She also underwent the same process that had turned her father into the Red Hulk, which granted her superhuman physical power. The now villainous <a href="/wiki/Doc_Samson" title="Doc Samson">Doc Samson</a> also helps Leader brainwash Betty into an extremely confused and aggressive state.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (January 2012)">volume &amp; issue needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Ross' allies, aware of his intentions to betray them, send Betty, as the "Red She-Hulk", to help assassinate her father, who is hunting the <a href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">mercenary</a> <a href="/wiki/Domino_(comics)" title="Domino (comics)">Domino</a>, after she witnesses him transform from his human form. Their encounter ends with Red She-Hulk kicking Red Hulk off the <a href="/wiki/Empire_State_Building" title="Empire State Building">Empire State Building</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>After Ross fakes his own death, Betty also appears as herself at his "funeral", accompanied by a <a href="/wiki/Life_Model_Decoy" title="Life Model Decoy">Life Model Decoy</a> of Glenn Talbot to constantly monitor and control her, and expresses distrust of Bruce due to his recent marriage to <a href="/wiki/Caiera" title="Caiera">Caiera</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sakaar" title="Sakaar">Sakaar</a> and his subsequent <a href="/wiki/World_War_Hulk" title="World War Hulk">attack on Manhattan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <i><a href="/wiki/World_War_Hulks" title="World War Hulks">World War Hulks</a></i> storyline, after Skaar stabs her with his sword, the new She-Hulk reverts to her human form, exposing her true identity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Betty explains how she was brought back to life, and asks that Bruce allow her to die. But when Samson arrives, Betty's anger at his betrayal transforms her back into the Red She-Hulk, thus healing her injuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Now once again in control of her own mind, Betty (as Red She-Hulk) helps Bruce/Hulk to reconcile with his son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Skaar_(character)" title="Skaar (character)">Skaar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> When Bruce gains the upper hand in the ensuing final battle against Ross, Betty becomes worried for her father, which, combined with her heightened aggression when transformed, leads to conflict with the original She-Hulk, who prevails. After Ross is defeated and imprisoned, Betty convinces Bruce to grant her father an opportunity for rehabilitation and redemption.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the aftermath of the Leader's attempted takeover, Betty tells Bruce that they are no longer married, since she was declared legally dead and everyone else knows that Bruce had married Caiera.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> But in the last series, the Hulk family defeats <a href="/wiki/Fin_Fang_Foom" title="Fin Fang Foom">Fin Fang Foom</a>. Afterwards, Betty and Bruce resume their romantic relationship but it gets shaky as Bruce becomes obsessed with regaining the power of the Hulk.</p>
<p>During the <i><a href="/wiki/Fear_Itself_(comics)" title="Fear Itself (comics)">Fear Itself</a></i> storyline, Red She-Hulk travelled to <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Spider-Woman_(Jessica_Drew)" title="Spider-Woman (Jessica Drew)">Spider-Woman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ms._Marvel" title="Ms. Marvel">Ms. Marvel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Noh-Varr" title="Noh-Varr">Protector</a>, to fight Hulk, who was transformed into Nul: Breaker of Worlds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> She later receives an enchanted Asgardian sword from Iron Man and joins the heroes in the final battle against the <a href="/wiki/Serpent_(comics)" title="Serpent (comics)">Serpent</a> and his forces. After the battle, the Stark-Asgardian weapons were returned to Asgard to be melted but Red She-Hulk kept her sword.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following the <i><a href="/wiki/Original_Sin_(comics)" title="Original Sin (comics)">Original Sin</a></i> storyline, the Hulk persona emerges as the result of an attempt to assassinate Bruce and efforts to save his life using the <a href="/wiki/Extremis#The_Extremis_virus" title="Extremis">Extremis virus</a>. This new Hulk, calling himself "Doc Green", decides that gamma-powered superhumans are a threat to humanity that must be eliminated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Betty is being despaired of Red She-Hulk and cured of her insanity and her mutation by Doc Green. Although whether this will be permanent is debatable, as Banner in any form has never been able to cure himself or any gamma mutate to date on a permanent basis. But she realized that she had made the same mistake Bruce had made, she had created a monster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the <i><a href="/wiki/Civil_War_II" title="Civil War II">Civil War II</a></i> storyline, Betty Ross was among those who learned about Bruce Banner's death and ended up mourning it at his funeral.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powers_and_abilities">Powers and abilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Powers and abilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harpy_2">Harpy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Harpy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the Harpy, Betty had superhuman strength, stamina, speed and durability with which were enough to fight the Hulk. She also had big bird-like wings from her back that she used to fly at high speeds through the air and perform aerial attacks. In addition, she could project blasts of nuclear energy and had razor sharp talons which were strong enough to cut through metal or carry heavy objects.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Red_She-Hulk_2">Red She-Hulk</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Red She-Hulk">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the Red She-Hulk, Betty has enormous superhuman strength, speed, stamina, and durability, and a <a href="/wiki/Healing_factor" title="Healing factor">healing factor</a> that allows her to easily survive what would normally be fatal injuries to humans, such as stab wounds to the leg and abdomen by <a href="/wiki/Wolverine_(character)" title="Wolverine (character)">Wolverine</a>'s claws.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hulkv2.2316_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hulkv2.2316-33">[33]</a></sup> Similar to her ex-husband, Betty's strength level is so vast that it warps the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laws_of_physics" title="Laws of physics">laws of physics</a> even further than standard for other characters in the same fictional continuity, for example allowing her to punch her way through dimensional barriers between different universes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hulk607_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hulk607-34">[34]</a></sup> However, she was defeated easily by the original <a href="/wiki/She-Hulk" title="She-Hulk">She-Hulk</a>, mainly due to her lack of experience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hulk24_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hulk24-35">[35]</a></sup> She shares the Red Hulk's ability to absorb energy, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gamma_radiation" title="Gamma radiation">gamma radiation</a> from other Hulks, thereby reverting those beings to human form, and at least temporarily boosting herself. According to Banner, there were plans to remove this ability through the same process that removed the Red Hulk's based on the fact that this ability would eventually kill her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Also like her father, Red She-Hulk has yellow blood, produces yellow energy from her eyes when angry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hulkv2.2316_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hulkv2.2316-33">[33]</a></sup> and can discharge energy by touch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Red She-Hulk can be returned to human form if she is suddenly startled or frightened, though she can revert to her Hulk form at will.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> As Red She-Hulk, she still maintains control of her humanity, though if extremely angered she can become "pure Hulk", further increasing her strength but losing control of her mind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
<p>Red She-Hulk carries a <a href="/wiki/Zweih%C3%A4nder" title="Zweihänder">great sword</a> that she affectionately calls her "big ass sword". The sword, forged by <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man" title="Iron Man">Tony Stark</a>, is made of Stark Industries repulsor technology and enchanted uru metal from <a href="/wiki/Asgard_(comics)" title="Asgard (comics)">Asgard</a> and was first given to Red She-Hulk during the "Fear Itself" storyline along with similar weapons given to other heroes in order to defeat Cul "The Serpent", <a href="/wiki/Odin_(comics)" title="Odin (comics)">Odin's</a> long-forgotten brother. While the other weapons are eventually returned and melted at the end of the storyline, Red She-Hulk manages to hold on to hers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The sword is later taken by a massive global machine called "The Terranometer" during the "Hell Hath No Fury" story arc and is held there until Red She-Hulk is able to stop the U.S. government from inadvertently creating a dystopian future in which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gamma_radiation" title="Gamma radiation">gamma</a> enhanced super-soldiers take over the Earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_versions">Other versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".22Heroes_Reborn.22">"Heroes Reborn"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title='Edit section: "Heroes Reborn"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the "<a href="/wiki/Heroes_Reborn_(comics)" title="Heroes Reborn (comics)">Heroes Reborn</a>" Universe created during 1996–1997 storyline by <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Richards" title="Franklin Richards">Franklin Richards</a> in the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/Onslaught_(comics)" title="Onslaught (comics)">Onslaught</a> crisis, Betty, known as <b>Liz Ross</b>, served as the head of security for <a href="/wiki/Stark_Industries" title="Stark Industries">Stark International</a>. As a result, she took <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man" title="Iron Man">Iron Man</a>'s apparent appointment as Tony Stark's personal bodyguard as a personal insult, and was also present when Bruce Banner was exposed to the gamma radiation that would turn him into the Hulk (The same accident that resulted in Stark donning the Iron Man armor in the first place). Shortly before the heroes returned to their world, it was revealed that Liz was dying of cancer as a result of the gamma exposure, Banner being particularly affected by news of the disease to the extent that he turned back from the Hulk to embrace her as Banner after learning about her condition.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id=".22House_of_M.22">"House of M"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title='Edit section: "House of M"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the alternate universe seen in the 2005 "<a href="/wiki/House_of_M" title="House of M">House of M</a>" storyline, Betty Ross is married to Major Glenn Talbot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ultimate_Marvel">Ultimate Marvel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Betty_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Ultimate Marvel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Marvel" title="Ultimate Marvel">Ultimate Marvel</a> Universe, Betty Ross is still the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Thunderbolt_Ross" title="Thunderbolt Ross">General "Thunderbolt" Ross</a>. She was the college roommate of <a href="/wiki/Wasp_(comics)#Ultimate_Wasp" title="Wasp (comics)">Janet van Dyne</a>. She earned a degree in communications at <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">Berkeley</a> and dated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultimate_Hulk" title="Ultimate Hulk">Bruce Banner</a> until his failed attempts at cracking the super soldier problem turned him into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultimate_Hulk" title="Ultimate Hulk">Hulk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup></p>
<p>When the <a href="/wiki/Ultimates" title="Ultimates">Ultimates</a> were assembled, she became their Director of <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">Communications</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Relations" title="Public Relations">Public Relations</a> Officer. After being spurned by Betty, Bruce responded by injecting himself with a version of the Hulk formula that incorporated the recently discovered Captain America's blood, and went on a rampage as the Hulk, during which he killed more than 800 civilians. During his trial, Betty declared her love for Bruce, who was eventually convicted, and sentenced to death, but Bruce escaped the nuclear bomb intended to execute him by turning into the Hulk and escaping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Bruce returned during the <i>Ultimates 2</i> miniseries after Fury, the President of the United States, Washington D.C. and New York had been captured by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Liberators_(Marvel_Comics)" title="The Liberators (Marvel Comics)">Liberators</a>. Bruce, now exhibiting greater control over his transformations, helped repel the Liberators, and Betty was seen tending to an exhausted Bruce after the battle with the Liberators and <a href="/wiki/Loki_(comics)" title="Loki (comics)">Loki</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_DC" title="Washington DC">Washington DC</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <i>Ultimate Wolverine vs. Hulk</i> miniseries, <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Nick_Fury" title="Ultimate Nick Fury">Nick Fury</a> sends <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Wolverine" title="Ultimate Wolverine">Wolverine</a> to find and kill the Hulk, but their battle was interrupted by <b><a href="/wiki/She-Hulk" title="She-Hulk">She-Hulk</a></b> after Betty injected herself with the Hulk serum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Fury reveals that S.H.I.E.L.D. is working to keep her transformations under control,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> and imprisoned in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cube_(comics)" title="Cube (comics)">Cube</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
