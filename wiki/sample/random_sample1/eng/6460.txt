<div class="hatnote" role="note">Not to be confused with its predecessor the former <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Borough_of_Bexley" title="Municipal Borough of Bexley">Municipal Borough of Bexley</a> in north west Kent.</div>
<p>The <b>London Borough of Bexley</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="noexcerpt"><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/aa/En-uk-LBBexley.ogg" title="Listen"><img alt="Listen" data-file-height="11" data-file-width="11" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Speakerlink-new.svg/11px-Speakerlink-new.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Speakerlink-new.svg/17px-Speakerlink-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Speakerlink-new.svg/22px-Speakerlink-new.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a><sup><span class="IPA" style="color:#00e;font:bold 80% sans-serif;padding:0 .1em"><a href="/wiki/File:En-uk-LBBexley.ogg" title="File:En-uk-LBBexley.ogg">i</a></span></sup></span><span class="IPA nopopups"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_English" title="Help:IPA for English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/ primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/ɛ/ short 'e' in 'bed'">ɛ</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/i/ 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span></span> is a London borough in south-east London, England. It has common borders with the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Bromley" title="London Borough of Bromley">London Borough of Bromley</a> to the south, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Borough_of_Greenwich" title="Royal Borough of Greenwich">Royal Borough of Greenwich</a> to the west, across the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> to the north it borders the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Havering" title="London Borough of Havering">London Borough of Havering</a>, the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Barking_and_Dagenham" title="London Borough of Barking and Dagenham">London Borough of Barking and Dagenham</a> and there is a small boundary with the unitary authority of <a href="/wiki/Thurrock" title="Thurrock">Thurrock</a> in <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a> to the north-east. There is a border with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dartford_(borough)" title="Dartford (borough)">Dartford borough</a> to the east and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sevenoaks_(district)" title="Sevenoaks (district)">Sevenoaks district</a> to the south-east, both being in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceremonial_county" title="Ceremonial county">ceremonial county</a> of Kent. The London Borough of Bexley is within the <a href="/wiki/Thames_Gateway" title="Thames Gateway">Thames Gateway</a>, an area designated as a national priority for <a href="/wiki/Urban_renewal" title="Urban renewal">urban regeneration</a>. The local authority is <a href="/wiki/Bexley_London_Borough_Council" title="Bexley London Borough Council">Bexley London Borough Council</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to the 19th century the area now forming the Borough was practically unoccupied: very few of the present settlements were mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a>, although the village of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Bexley" title="Old Bexley">Bexley</a> has a charter dated 814CE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_brief_history_of_Bexley_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-A_brief_history_of_Bexley-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Erith" title="Erith">Erith</a> was a port on the River Thames until the 17th century; the opening of the sewage works at nearby <a href="/wiki/Crossness" title="Crossness">Crossness</a> in the late 19th century turned it into an industrial town.</p>
<p>Today's settlement pattern is the result of the gradual extension of the London influence. Until the 19th century it was an area with a few isolated buildings such as the <a href="/wiki/Georgian_architecture" title="Georgian architecture">Georgian</a> <a href="/wiki/Danson_House" title="Danson House">Danson House</a>. With the coming of the railways building began apace, although the area is still composed of many disconnected settlements, interspersed with area of open ground and parks.</p>
<p>The London Borough of Bexley was formed in 1965, under the <a href="/wiki/London_Government_Act_1963" title="London Government Act 1963">London Government Act 1963</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Borough_of_Bexley" title="Municipal Borough of Bexley">Municipal Boroughs of Bexley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Borough_of_Erith" title="Municipal Borough of Erith">Erith</a>; <a href="/wiki/Crayford_Urban_District" title="Crayford Urban District">Crayford Urban District</a>: and part of <a href="/wiki/Chislehurst_and_Sidcup_Urban_District" title="Chislehurst and Sidcup Urban District">Chislehurst and Sidcup Urban District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Council now styles itself the "London Borough of Bexley" in common with its formal name, although it previously styled itself "Bexley London Borough" until 1999 (possibly to maintain the link with its predecessor, Bexley Municipal Borough), and "Bexley Council" from 1999 to October 2007, when it reverted to its full formal name in conjunction with the adoption of a new Council logo bearing this name alongside the 1965 Coat of Arms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The town of <a href="/wiki/Bexley,_Ohio" title="Bexley, Ohio">Bexley, Ohio</a>, a suburb of the city of <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus</a>, was named at the suggestion of an early resident, Mr. Kilbourne, whose family's roots were in Bexley, England<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. In addition, a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, Australia bears the name of <a href="/wiki/Bexley,_New_South_Wales" title="Bexley, New South Wales">Bexley</a> and a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Christchurch" title="Christchurch">Christchurch</a>, New Zealand is also called <a href="/wiki/Bexley,_New_Zealand" title="Bexley, New Zealand">Bexley</a>. These other places outside England that are also called Bexley derive their names from the village of Bexley in Kent and not from the London Borough of Bexley that did not exist at the time they were named. The London Borough of Bexley itself also derived its name from the village of Bexley. The village currently lies within the London Borough.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_the_London_Borough_of_Bexley" title="Coat of arms of the London Borough of Bexley">Coat of arms of the London Borough of Bexley</a></div>
<p>The coat of arms of the borough includes symbols for the main rivers in the area, the industry and the border to <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The administrative centre of the Borough is in <a href="/wiki/Bexleyheath" title="Bexleyheath">Bexleyheath</a>.</p>
<p>There are 21 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wards_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards of the United Kingdom">wards</a> represented on Bexley Council; each ward elects three councillors, 63 in all.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The wards are shown on the accompanying map.</p>
<p>All seats were up for re-election during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_local_elections_2006" title="London local elections 2006">election</a> on 4 May 2006. Until that date, Labour held control of the council by a margin of one (32 Labour to 31 Conservative councillors). In the election, the Conservatives took 23 seats from Labour, giving them the majority.</p>
<p>Insofar as central government is concerned, the borough is divided among the constituencies of <a href="/wiki/Old_Bexley_and_Sidcup_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Old Bexley and Sidcup (UK Parliament constituency)">Old Bexley and Sidcup</a>; <a href="/wiki/Erith_and_Thamesmead_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Erith and Thamesmead (UK Parliament constituency)">Erith and Thamesmead</a> (shared with the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Borough_of_Greenwich" title="Royal Borough of Greenwich">Royal Borough of Greenwich</a>); and <a href="/wiki/Bexleyheath_and_Crayford_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Bexleyheath and Crayford (UK Parliament constituency)">Bexleyheath and Crayford</a>.</p>
<p>After the 2005 General Election, Erith and Thamesmead is represented by a Labour Party MP while the remaining two seats were held by the Conservative Party; although on 29 January 2008 the Conservative Party whip was withdrawn from Old Bexley and Sidcup's MP <a href="/wiki/Derek_Conway" title="Derek Conway">Derek Conway</a> following alleged misuse of funds, leaving him as an Independent MP.</p>
<p>2010 election the result was 52 for conservatives and 11 for Labour, meaning the former held the council</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_districts_in_Bexley" title="List of districts in Bexley">List of districts in Bexley</a></div>
<p>Bexley, lying as it does on the outer fringe of London, has many relatively large areas of open space. The ridge of higher ground in South London crosses the Borough from its high point of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shooters_Hill" title="Shooters Hill">Shooters Hill</a>, on the boundary with the Royal Borough of Greenwich, to end above the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> at <a href="/wiki/Belvedere,_London" title="Belvedere, London">Belvedere</a>, where the land drops down to the old port of <a href="/wiki/Erith" title="Erith">Erith</a>. This high land, whose geology is the sand and pebbles of the <i>Blackheath beds</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and which results in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heathland" title="Heathland">heathland</a>, provided the line on the old <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> road (<a href="/wiki/Watling_Street" title="Watling Street">Watling Street</a>) ran between <a href="/wiki/Crayford" title="Crayford">Crayford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Welling" title="Welling">Welling</a>. The land falls away to the north of the high ground, across the Erith Marshes to the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a>, which here makes a loop to the north at <a href="/wiki/Crossness" title="Crossness">Crossness</a>. There is further ridge of less higher ground from the west terminating at <a href="/wiki/Sidcup" title="Sidcup">Sidcup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A_brief_history_of_Bexley_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-A_brief_history_of_Bexley-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Apart from the River Thames the other rivers within the Borough are the <a href="/wiki/River_Darent" title="River Darent">River Darent</a>, which, with its tributary the <a href="/wiki/River_Cray" title="River Cray">River Cray</a> and the smaller <i>Stanham River</i>, all form part of its north-eastern boundary; and the <a href="/wiki/River_Shuttle" title="River Shuttle">River Shuttle</a>, a tributary of the Cray.</p>
<p>The major centres of settlement can be considered in two parts: the older established erstwhile villages; and the infill areas of suburban houses and centres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Among the former are <a href="/wiki/Erith" title="Erith">Erith</a>, in the 17th century a port on the Thames, and an industrial town in the later 19th century; <a href="/wiki/Bexleyheath" title="Bexleyheath">Bexleyheath</a>, created at the same time on the London to <a href="/wiki/Dover" title="Dover">Dover</a> road. By the earlier 20th century, both were created <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">Urban District Councils</a> (UDC), as was <a href="/wiki/Foots_Cray" title="Foots Cray">Foots Cray</a> (an ancient village site). <a href="/wiki/Thamesmead" title="Thamesmead">Thamesmead</a>, the "new town" built on what was the Erith Marshes, extends into the Borough: both Thamesmead North and South are located here. <a href="/wiki/Crayford" title="Crayford">Crayford</a> was mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a>, and its parish later included the hamlets of <a href="/wiki/North_End,_Bexley" title="North End, Bexley">North End</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slade_Green" title="Slade Green">Slade Green</a>.</p>
<p>Smaller settlements include <a href="/wiki/Welling" title="Welling">Welling</a>, like Bexleyheath a staging post on the Dover Road, which was at one time of less importance than the nearby <a href="/wiki/East_Wickham" title="East Wickham">East Wickham</a> (also an ancient village), was absorbed in Bexley UDC. <a href="/wiki/Barnes_Cray" title="Barnes Cray">Barnes Cray</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Cray" title="North Cray">North Cray</a> were two hamlets in the Cray Valley; and <a href="/wiki/Belvedere,_London" title="Belvedere, London">Belvedere</a> was the location of a medieval monastery.</p>
<p>The map of Bexley<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> shows that a large proportion of its area comprises <a href="/wiki/Suburb" title="Suburb">suburbia</a>. Some named places, like <a href="/wiki/Albany_Park,_Bexley" title="Albany Park, Bexley">Albany Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barnehurst" title="Barnehurst">Barnehurst</a>, are names given to developments engendered by the building of the railways. Some came into being when large estates and farmland were broken up for the sole purpose of suburban building: these include <a href="/wiki/Blackfen" title="Blackfen">Blackfen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lamorbey" title="Lamorbey">Lamorbey</a> and part of <a href="/wiki/Falconwood" title="Falconwood">Falconwood</a>. Others simply reflect the nature of area: <a href="/wiki/Lessness_Heath" title="Lessness Heath">Lessness Heath</a>; <a href="/wiki/Longlands" title="Longlands">Longlands</a> (part of Sidcup); <a href="/wiki/Northumberland_Heath" title="Northumberland Heath">Northumberland Heath</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Heath,_London" title="West Heath, London">West Heath</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_Spaces">Open Spaces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Open Spaces">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bexley_parks_and_open_spaces" title="Bexley parks and open spaces">Bexley parks and open spaces</a></div>
<p>Despite the suburbia mentioned above, there are still open spaces among the borough's streets and avenues. The Borough owns and maintains over one hundred parks and open spaces, large and small; and there is still a part of the <a href="/wiki/Erith_Marshes" title="Erith Marshes">Erith Marshes</a> bordering the River Thames. The Crayford Marshes lie to the east of that river, as do <i>Foots Cray Meadows</i> further south.</p>
<p>The largest of the open spaces are <a href="/wiki/Lesnes_Abbey" title="Lesnes Abbey">Lesnes Abbey Woods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danson_Park" title="Danson Park">Danson Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hall_Place" title="Hall Place">Hall Place</a> Gardens. There are also many golf courses and sports fields, particularly to the west of Crayford.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hall_Place">Hall Place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hall Place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:327px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HallPlace.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="683" data-file-width="1024" height="217" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/HallPlace.jpg/325px-HallPlace.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/HallPlace.jpg/488px-HallPlace.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/HallPlace.jpg/650px-HallPlace.jpg 2x" width="325"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HallPlace.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Hall Place, showing 16th and 17th Century wings.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hall_Place" title="Hall Place">Hall Place</a> is a former stately home, today a Grade I listed building and Scheduled Ancient Monument, beside the River Cray on the outskirts of Crayford, south-east of Bexleyheath and north-east of Old Bexley. It is situated just off the A223, Bourne Road, south of Watling Street (A207) and north of the <a href="/wiki/Black_Prince,_Bexley" title="Black Prince, Bexley">Black Prince</a> interchange of the A2 Rochester Way with the A220.</p>
<p>The house dates back to around 1540 when wealthy merchant <a href="/wiki/John_Champneys" title="John Champneys">Sir John Champneys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lord_Mayor_of_London" title="Lord Mayor of London">Lord Mayor of London</a> in 1534, built himself a country house. In 1649, the house was sold to another wealthy <a href="/wiki/City_of_London" title="City of London">City</a> merchant, Sir <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Robert_Austen,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir Robert Austen, 1st Baronet">Robert Austen</a> (1587–1666), who added a second wing built of red bricks, doubling the size of the house.</p>
<p>Currently, the building houses a museum of local artefacts, and a history and tourist information centre. In the gardens there is a topiary lawn, herb garden, tropical garden and long herbaceous cottage garden-styled borders. The former walled gardens include a tropical house housing plants and a large vegetable garden. Model gardens have been created to show visitors how to make use of space in small urban gardens. Hall Place also has three galleries inside the house, presenting art exhibitions and museum displays.</p>

<p>In 1801, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parishes</a> that form the modern borough had a total population of 4,165. This rose slowly throughout the 19th century, as the district became built up; reaching 10,963 in the middle of the century. When the railways arrived the rate of <a href="/wiki/Population_growth" title="Population growth">population growth</a> increased. The population peaked in the 1970s, when industry began to relocate from London.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001 Census</a>, the borough has a population of 218,307 – of whom 105,148 were male, and 113,159 female. Of the population, 44.3% were in full-time employment and 11.6% in part-time employment – compared to a London average of 42.6% and 8.6%, respectively. Residents were predominantly owner-occupiers, with 31.7% owning their house outright, and a further 46.5% owning with a mortgage. Only 2.2% were in <a href="/wiki/Public_housing" title="Public housing">local authority housing</a>, with a further 11.5% renting from a <a href="/wiki/Housing_association" title="Housing association">housing association</a>, or other registered social landlord.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>

<p>The greater part of the population are nominal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglicans" title="Anglicans">Anglicans</a>, but a number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> churches and non-conformist congregations exist. In the 2001 Census, 22.7% of the area's population described themselves as <a href="/wiki/Irreligion" title="Irreligion">non-religious</a> or chose not to state their faith.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Baptists" title="Baptists">Baptists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Brethren" title="Plymouth Brethren">Plymouth Brethren</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methodist" title="Methodist">Methodist</a> are among the other congregations. In 2008 the former Crayford Methodist Church was purchased by the North West Kent Muslim Association to become a mosque serving Bexley and Dartford boroughs. There are no Jewish congregations.</p>
<p>In 2011, 58.1% of Bexley's <a href="/wiki/Population_proportion" title="Population proportion">population proportion</a> was between 20 and 64 years old, the lowest in London. 16% of the population is over 65, the third highest behind <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Bromley" title="London Borough of Bromley">Bromley</a> and <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Havering" title="London Borough of Havering">Havering</a>. The average household size is 2.49, up from 2.43 in 2001. The number of households increased by 3.52% to 92,600 between 2001 and 2011, one of the lowest increases in the capital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, 62.5% of the borough's population identified themselves as Christian, with 24.1% having no religion. Both figures are above London's average.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads_and_paths">Roads and paths</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Roads and paths">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The principal roads through the Borough include the <a href="/wiki/A2_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A2 road (Great Britain)">A2 trunk road</a>; the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A20_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A20 road (Great Britain)">A20</a> (Sidcup By-Pass) which generally marks its southern boundary; the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A207_road" title="A207 road">A207</a>, which is the route of the erstwhile <a href="/wiki/Watling_Street" title="Watling Street">Watling Street</a>; the <a href="/wiki/A206_road" title="A206 road">A206</a> which takes traffic from <a href="/wiki/Woolwich" title="Woolwich">Woolwich</a> and Dartford; and the latter's newer counterpart, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A2016_road" title="A2016 road">A2016</a> through <a href="/wiki/Thamesmead" title="Thamesmead">Thamesmead</a>.</p>
<p>There are also some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Long-distance_footpath" title="Long-distance footpath">Long-distance footpaths</a> in the Borough: among them the <a href="/wiki/Thames_Path" title="Thames Path">Thames Path</a> and the <a href="/wiki/London_Outer_Orbital_Path" title="London Outer Orbital Path">London Outer Orbital Path</a>. Bexley Borough has joined with three other adjoining boroughs to for the <a href="/wiki/South_East_London_Green_Chain" title="South East London Green Chain">South East London Green Chain</a> linking green spaces.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are three suburban railway lines crossing the Borough, all destined to come together at <a href="/wiki/Dartford_railway_station" title="Dartford railway station">Dartford</a>. The most northerly is the <a href="/wiki/North_Kent_Line" title="North Kent Line">North Kent Line</a>, then the <a href="/wiki/Bexleyheath_Line" title="Bexleyheath Line">Bexleyheath Line</a> which runs through the centre of the borough and then finally the <a href="/wiki/Dartford_Loop_Line" title="Dartford Loop Line">Dartford Loop Line</a> which runs furthest south. In common with many other boroughs south of the Thames, Bexley has no <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">London Underground</a> lines or any other London-based transport methods like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Tramlink" title="London Tramlink">London Tramlink</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Docklands_Light_Railway" title="Docklands Light Railway">Docklands Light Railway</a> or <a href="/wiki/London_Overground" title="London Overground">London Overground</a>. However, from December 2018 <a href="/wiki/Crossrail" title="Crossrail">Crossrail</a> services will commence from <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Wood_railway_station" title="Abbey Wood railway station">Abbey Wood</a> station.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Travel_to_work">Travel to work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Travel to work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_services">Public services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Borough_of_Bexley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Public services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The London Borough of Bexley has three fire stations controlled by the <a href="/wiki/London_Fire_Brigade" title="London Fire Brigade">London Fire Brigade</a> within its boundary; those at Erith, Sidcup and Bexley. Bexley fire station's station ground is the largest of the three; covering 23.7 km<sup>sq</sup>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> One pumping appliance, a fire rescue unit and an incident response unit reside there. Although it does not have the largest station ground, Erith was the busiest station in 2006/2007, responding to 1,314 incidents - two pumping appliances are based there. The third station, Sidcup, attended the least incidents in the same time period - 785. One pumping appliance is situated there. The three fire stations attended 3,095 incidents in the 2006/2007 period. It is the only London borough with a 'below average' crime rate.</p>
