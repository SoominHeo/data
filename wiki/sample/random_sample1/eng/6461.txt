<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Vector-valued differential form</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>In <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, a <b>vector-valued differential form</b> on a <a href="/wiki/Manifold" title="Manifold">manifold</a> <i>M</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Differential_form" title="Differential form">differential form</a> on <i>M</i> with values in a <a href="/wiki/Vector_space" title="Vector space">vector space</a> <i>V</i>. More generally, it is a differential form with values in some <a href="/wiki/Vector_bundle" title="Vector bundle">vector bundle</a> <i>E</i> over <i>M</i>. Ordinary differential forms can be viewed as <b>R</b>-valued differential forms.</p>
<p>An important case of vector-valued differential forms are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lie_algebra-valued_forms" title="Lie algebra-valued forms">Lie algebra-valued forms</a>. (A <a href="/wiki/Connection_form" title="Connection form">connection form</a> is an example of such a form.)</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pullback">Pullback</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vector-valued_differential_form&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pullback">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>One can define the <a href="/wiki/Pullback_(differential_geometry)" title="Pullback (differential geometry)">pullback</a> of vector-valued forms by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smooth_map" title="Smooth map">smooth maps</a> just as for ordinary forms. The pullback of an <i>E</i>-valued form on <i>N</i> by a smooth map φ : <i>M</i> → <i>N</i> is an (φ*<i>E</i>)-valued form on <i>M</i>, where φ*<i>E</i> is the <a href="/wiki/Pullback_bundle" title="Pullback bundle">pullback bundle</a> of <i>E</i> by φ.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Basic_or_tensorial_forms_on_principal_bundles">Basic or tensorial forms on principal bundles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vector-valued_differential_form&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Basic or tensorial forms on principal bundles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Let <i>E</i> → <i>M</i> be a smooth vector bundle of rank <i>k</i> over <i>M</i> and let <i>π</i> : F(<i>E</i>) → <i>M</i> be the (<a href="/wiki/Associated_bundle" title="Associated bundle">associated</a>) <a href="/wiki/Frame_bundle" title="Frame bundle">frame bundle</a> of <i>E</i>, which is a <a href="/wiki/Principal_bundle" title="Principal bundle">principal</a> GL<sub><i>k</i></sub>(<b>R</b>) bundle over <i>M</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Pullback_bundle" title="Pullback bundle">pullback</a> of <i>E</i> by <i>π</i> is canonically isomorphic to F(<i>E</i>) ×<sub>ρ</sub> <b>R</b><sup><i>k</i></sup> via the inverse of [<i>u</i>, <i>v</i>] →<i>u</i>(<i>v</i>), where ρ is the standard representation. Therefore, the pullback by <i>π</i> of an <i>E</i>-valued form on <i>M</i> determines an <b>R</b><sup><i>k</i></sup>-valued form on F(<i>E</i>). It is not hard to check that this pulled back form is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Equivariant" title="Equivariant">right-equivariant</a> with respect to the natural <a href="/wiki/Group_action" title="Group action">action</a> of GL<sub><i>k</i></sub>(<b>R</b>) on F(<i>E</i>) × <b>R</b><sup><i>k</i></sup> and vanishes on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vertical_bundle" title="Vertical bundle">vertical vectors</a> (tangent vectors to F(<i>E</i>) which lie in the kernel of d<i>π</i>). Such vector-valued forms on F(<i>E</i>) are important enough to warrant special terminology: they are called <i>basic</i> or <i>tensorial forms</i> on F(<i>E</i>).</p>
<p>Let <i>π</i> : <i>P</i> → <i>M</i> be a (smooth) <a href="/wiki/Principal_bundle" title="Principal bundle">principal <i>G</i>-bundle</a> and let <i>V</i> be a fixed vector space together with a <a href="/wiki/Group_representation" title="Group representation">representation</a> <i>ρ</i> : <i>G</i> → GL(<i>V</i>).
