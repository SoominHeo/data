<p><b>Benjamin Walker Scodelario-Davis</b> (born June 21, 1982),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BW-2009-10-20_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BW-2009-10-20-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BroadwaySpace.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-BroadwaySpace.com-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Benjamin Walker</b>, is an American actor and stand-up comedian. He may be best known for his title role in the 2012 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln:_Vampire_Hunter_(film)" title="Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter (film)">Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter</a></i>, as well as his appearances in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Kinsey_(film)" title="Kinsey (film)">Kinsey</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Flags_of_Our_Fathers_(film)" title="Flags of Our Fathers (film)">Flags of Our Fathers</a></i>, and his critically acclaimed role as <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Jackson" title="Andrew Jackson">Andrew Jackson</a> in the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Bloody_Andrew_Jackson" title="Bloody Bloody Andrew Jackson">Bloody Bloody Andrew Jackson</a></i>. In 2016, he starred as <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Bateman" title="Patrick Bateman">Patrick Bateman</a> in the Broadway <a href="/wiki/American_Psycho_(musical)" title="American Psycho (musical)">musical adaptation</a> of the film <i><a href="/wiki/American_Psycho_(film)" title="American Psycho (film)">American Psycho</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Walker_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Walker was born in <a href="/wiki/Cartersville,_Georgia" title="Cartersville, Georgia">Cartersville, Georgia</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> the son of Jeannine (née Walker), a music teacher, and Greg Davis, who owned a movie rental store and works in financial services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He took his mother's maiden name as his stage name because there was a Benjamin Davis already registered with the <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild" title="Screen Actors Guild">Screen Actors Guild</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tms12_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-tms12-7">[7]</a></sup> He attended Cartersville High School in <a href="/wiki/Cartersville,_Georgia" title="Cartersville, Georgia">Cartersville</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Interlochen_Center_for_the_Arts" title="Interlochen Center for the Arts">Interlochen Arts Academy</a> near <a href="/wiki/Traverse_City,_Michigan" title="Traverse City, Michigan">Traverse City</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Juilliard_School" title="Juilliard School">Juilliard School</a> in New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He graduated from the Juilliard Actor Training Program in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Walker_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage">Stage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Walker_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2007, Walker portrayed Bertram Cates in the Broadway revival of <i><a href="/wiki/Inherit_the_Wind_(play)" title="Inherit the Wind (play)">Inherit the Wind</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The production opened at the Lyceum Theatre on April 12 and enjoyed a 10-week run to a full house. The show was nominated for four Tony awards, including Best Revival. In April 2008, Walker appeared as the Chevalier Danceny in the <a href="/wiki/Roundabout_Theatre_Company" title="Roundabout Theatre Company">Roundabout Theatre Company</a>'s production of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Les_Liaisons_Dangereuses" title="Les Liaisons Dangereuses">Les Liaisons Dangereuses</a></i>. He played the inexperienced lover who becomes the sex pawn of the pernicious La Marquise de Merteuil. Described as the "definitive battle of the sexes" by <i>Broadway World</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> the production enjoyed a full house and was nominated for six Tony awards.</p>
<p>In December 2007, Walker starred as <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Jackson" title="Andrew Jackson">Andrew Jackson</a> in the musical <i><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Bloody_Andrew_Jackson" title="Bloody Bloody Andrew Jackson">Bloody Bloody Andrew Jackson</a></i> during its world premiere at the <a href="/wiki/Center_Theatre_Group" title="Center Theatre Group">Center Theatre Group</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kirk_Douglas_Theatre" title="Kirk Douglas Theatre">Kirk Douglas Theatre</a> in Los Angeles. In 2010, he reprised his role in the musical, first off-Broadway at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Theater" title="Public Theater">Public Theater</a> and then at the <a href="/wiki/Bernard_B._Jacobs_Theatre" title="Bernard B. Jacobs Theatre">Bernard B. Jacobs Theatre</a> on Broadway. The musical enjoyed a successful, but short, run. It was also a critical hit, but was only nominated for awards for set design. Walker starred in numerous incarnations of the show to great critical acclaim, including <i>Bloody Bloody Andrew Jackson: The Concert Version</i> (a four-week workshop run at Public Theater in March 2009), and the thrice-extended return at the Public Theater in March 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The production closed on January 2, 2011.</p>
<p>From 2012 to 2013, Walker portrayed Brick in a revival of <i><a href="/wiki/Cat_on_a_Hot_Tin_Roof" title="Cat on a Hot Tin Roof">Cat on a Hot Tin Roof</a></i>. His other stage credits include <i>The Arrangements</i> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_Theatre_Company" title="Atlantic Theatre Company">Atlantic Theatre Company</a> (2005), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincoln_Center_Theater" title="Lincoln Center Theater">Lincoln Center Theater</a> workshop of <i><a href="/wiki/Spring_Awakening_(musical)" title="Spring Awakening (musical)">Spring Awakening</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Lady_Windermere%27s_Fan" title="Lady Windermere's Fan">Lady Windermere's Fan</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a></i> (2006), "<a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/The_Choice" title="The Choice">The Choice</a>" (2016), <a href="/wiki/American_Psycho_(musical)" title="American Psycho (musical)"><i>American Psycho</i></a> (2016).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film">Film</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Walker_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Walker starred in the short film <i>All Saint's Day</i> (2007), which was the Savannah College of Art and Design winner for the narrative short category. In 2009, he appeared in the independent films <i>Coach</i> and <i>The War Boys</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2010, Walker was cast as <a href="/wiki/Beast_(comics)" title="Beast (comics)">Hank McCoy/Beast</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_First_Class" title="X-Men: First Class">X-Men: First Class</a></i>, but dropped out of the role to star in the Broadway musical <i><a href="/wiki/Bloody_Bloody_Andrew_Jackson" title="Bloody Bloody Andrew Jackson">Bloody Bloody Andrew Jackson</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> It was announced in January 2011 that Walker would star as <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln:_Vampire_Hunter_(film)" title="Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter (film)">film adaptation</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln,_Vampire_Hunter_(novel)" title="Abraham Lincoln, Vampire Hunter (novel)">Abraham Lincoln, Vampire Hunter</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The film was released in June 2012. Walker told <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone_magazine" title="Rolling Stone magazine"><i>Rolling Stone</i> magazine</a> that he read a number of biographies on Lincoln to prepare for the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> He was cast as Archangel Michael in the film adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Paradise_Lost" title="Paradise Lost">Paradise Lost</a></i>, but production was cancelled in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> He starred as Kevin Connolly in the 2012 film <i><a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Ali%27s_Greatest_Fight" title="Muhammad Ali's Greatest Fight">Muhammad Ali's Greatest Fight</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stand-up_comedy_career">Stand-up comedy career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Walker_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stand-up comedy career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Walker has performed stand-up comedy at <a href="/wiki/Carolines_on_Broadway" title="Carolines on Broadway">Caroline's</a>, Comedy Village, and <a href="/wiki/The_Comedy_Store" title="The Comedy Store">The Comedy Store</a>. His comedy show, <i>Find the Funny</i>, features comics and taped short narratives and skits, and is performed regularly in New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Walker_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Walker became engaged to actress <a href="/wiki/Mamie_Gummer" title="Mamie Gummer">Mamie Gummer</a> in 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BW-2009-10-20_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-BW-2009-10-20-1">[1]</a></sup> and they were married in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> They lived in an apartment in <a href="/wiki/Park_Slope" title="Park Slope">Park Slope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>. In March 2013, it was announced that they had amicably separated and plan to divorce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Walker began dating his co-star, <a href="/wiki/Kaya_Scodelario" title="Kaya Scodelario">Kaya Scodelario</a>, during the filming of The Moon and the Sun, formerly known as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_King%27s_Daughter_(2016_film)" title="The King's Daughter (2016 film)">The King's Daughter</a></i> in April 2014. They became engaged on December 28, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He and Scodelario wed in late 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The couple announced they were expecting their first child, a boy, on June 7, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
