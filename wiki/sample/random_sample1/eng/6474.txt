<p><i><b>Agrostis</b></i> (<b>bent</b> or <b>bentgrass</b>) is a large and very nearly <a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_distribution" title="Cosmopolitan distribution">cosmopolitan</a> genus of plants in the <a href="/wiki/Poaceae" title="Poaceae">grass family</a>, found in nearly all the countries in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It has been bred as a GMO creeping bent grass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-watrud_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-watrud-14">[14]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agrostis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some species of bents are commonly used for <a href="/wiki/Lawn" title="Lawn">lawn</a> grass. This is a desirable grass for <a href="/wiki/Golf" title="Golf">golf</a> course tees, fairways and greens.</p>
<p>Bentgrass is used in turf applications for its numerous advantages: it can be mowed to a very short length without damage, it can handle a great amount of foot traffic, it has a shallow root system that is thick and dense allowing it to be seeded and grow rather easily, and it has a pleasing, deep green appearance. The name "bent" refers to the shallow roots, which bend just below the surface of the soil to propagate laterally.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Creeping_bent">Creeping bent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agrostis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Creeping bent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>(<i><a href="/wiki/Agrostis_stolonifera" title="Agrostis stolonifera">Agrostis stolonifera</a></i>) is the most commonly used species of <i>Agrostis</i>. Historically, it was often called <b><a href="/wiki/Orcheston" title="Orcheston">Orcheston</a> long grass</b>, after a village on <a href="/wiki/Salisbury_Plain" title="Salisbury Plain">Salisbury Plain</a>, England. It is cultivated almost exclusively on golf courses, especially on putting greens. Creeping bent aggressively produces horizontal stems, called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stolons" title="Stolons">stolons</a>, that run along the soil's surface. These allow creeping bent to form dense stands under conducive conditions and outcompete bunch-type grass and broadleaf weeds. As such, if infested in a home lawn, it can become a troublesome weed problem. The leaves of the bentgrass are long and slender.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Creeping bentgrass has been genetically engineered to be <a href="/wiki/Glyphosate" title="Glyphosate">glyphosate</a> tolerant, as "one of the first wind-pollinated, perennial, and highly outcrossing transgenic crops". In 2003 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scotts_Company" title="Scotts Company">Scotts Company</a> planted it as part of a large (about 160 ha) field trial in central Oregon near <a href="/wiki/Madras,_Oregon" title="Madras, Oregon">Madras, Oregon</a>. In 2004, its pollen was found to have reached wild growing bentgrass populations up to 14 kilometres away. Cross-pollinating <i><a href="/wiki/Agrostis_gigantea" title="Agrostis gigantea">Agrostis gigantea</a></i> was even found at a distance of 21 kilometres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-watrud_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-watrud-14">[14]</a></sup> The grower could not remove all genetically engineered plants and in 2007, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Agriculture" title="U.S. Department of Agriculture">U.S. Department of Agriculture</a> fined the grower $500 thousand for non-compliance with regulations in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Common_bent">Common bent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agrostis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Common bent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>(<i><a href="/wiki/Agrostis_capillaris" title="Agrostis capillaris">Agrostis capillaris</a></i> or Colonial bent) was brought to America from Europe. This was the type of grass that was used on the lawns of most estates. It is the tallest of the bents with very fine texture and like most bent grasses grows very densely. Although this species has been used on golf courses and sporting fields it is better suited for lawns. Colonial bent is fairly easy to grow from seeds and fertilization of the lawn is not as intense. This grass also takes longer to establish than creeping bent. However it does not require the intense maintenance.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Velvet_bent">Velvet bent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Agrostis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Velvet bent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>(<i><a href="/wiki/Agrostis_canina" title="Agrostis canina">Agrostis canina</a></i>) gets it name for the velvet appearance that this grass produces. It has the finest texture of all the bent grasses. This grass was used in Europe for estate lawns and golf courses because it could be cut so short. Velvet bent grass requires similar upkeep and maintenance to creeping bent. Velvet bent has recently had a resurgence in the UK due to the high demands on greens from inclement weather and speed expectations. This species also has a lighter color than the two previous species.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
