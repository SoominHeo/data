<p>The Constitution does not mention the Federal Parliament as such; it stipulates that the federal legislative power is exercised by the King and the Chamber of Representatives (and exceptionally the Senate), and defines when the United Chambers convene.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chamber_of_Representatives">Chamber of Representatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgian_Federal_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Chamber of Representatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Representatives_(Belgium)" title="Chamber of Representatives (Belgium)">Chamber of Representatives (Belgium)</a></div>
<p>The Chamber of Representatives holds its plenary meetings in the Palace of the Nation, Brussels. Eligibility requirements for the Chamber are a minimum age of 21, <a href="/wiki/Citizenship" title="Citizenship">citizenship</a>, and residency in Belgium.</p>
<p>The number of seats in the <b>Chamber</b> is constitutionally set at 150 elected from 11 electoral districts. The districts are divided along linguistic lines: 5 <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flemish</a> (79 seats), 5 <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Walloon</a> (49 seats), and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bilingual" title="Bilingual">bilingual</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde" title="Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde">Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde</a> (22 seats). The districts are the provinces, except for the districts of Leuven (part of <a href="/wiki/Flemish_Brabant" title="Flemish Brabant">Flemish Brabant</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde" title="Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde">Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde</a>. Each district is given a number of seats proportional to its population (not number of voters) ranging from 4 for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luxembourg_(province_of_Belgium)" title="Luxembourg (province of Belgium)">Luxembourg</a> to 24 for <a href="/wiki/Antwerp_(province)" title="Antwerp (province)">Antwerp</a>. All districts have an electoral threshold of 5%, except for <a href="/wiki/Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde" title="Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde">Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leuven" title="Leuven">Leuven</a>; all districts are monolingual, except for <a href="/wiki/Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde" title="Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde">Brussels-Halle-Vilvoorde</a> which encompasses both the 19 bilingual municipalities from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brussels-Capital_region" title="Brussels-Capital region">Brussels-Capital region</a> and some 35 Dutch-speaking municipalities in <a href="/wiki/Flemish_Brabant" title="Flemish Brabant">Flemish Brabant</a>, incl. 7 with <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_with_language_facilities" title="Municipalities with language facilities">language facilities</a> for <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>-speakers.</p>
<p>The current composition was elected at the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_federal_election,_2014" title="Belgian federal election, 2014">federal elections of 2014</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Senate">Senate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgian_Federal_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Senate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Senate_(Belgium)" title="Senate (Belgium)">Senate (Belgium)</a></div>
<p>Since 2014, the <b>Senate</b> consists of 60 members. There are two categories of senators: co-opted senators and senators of community and regional parliaments.</p>
<p>50 senators are elected by and from the community and regional parliaments: 29 by the <a href="/wiki/Flemish_Parliament" title="Flemish Parliament">Flemish Parliament</a>, 10 by the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_French_Community" title="Parliament of the French Community">Parliament of the French Community</a>, 8 by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walloon_Parliament" title="Walloon Parliament">Walloon Parliament</a>, 2 by the French-language group of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_Brussels-Capital_Region" title="Parliament of the Brussels-Capital Region">Parliament of the Brussels-Capital Region</a>, and 1 by the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_German-speaking_Community" title="Parliament of the German-speaking Community">Parliament of the German-speaking Community</a>.</p>
<p>The 10 other senators are co-opted: elected by the 50 other senators. Eligibility requirements for the Senate are identical to those for the Chamber.</p>
<p>Before 2014, the Senate consisted of 71 senators, only 21 of which were elected by the community parliaments. 25 were directly elected by the Flemish constituency and 15 by the French-speaking constituency. The last direct election of these 40 members occurred in the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_federal_election,_2010" title="Belgian federal election, 2010">2010 federal elections</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Belgian_federal_election,_2014" title="Belgian federal election, 2014">2014 elections</a> are the first one with the reformed Senate.</p>
<p>The President of the Senate is <a href="/wiki/Christine_Defraigne" title="Christine Defraigne">Christine Defraigne</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mouvement_R%C3%A9formateur" title="Mouvement Réformateur">MR</a>) as of 2014.</p>
<p>The Senate holds its plenary meetings in the Palace of the Nation, Brussels.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legislative_procedure">Legislative procedure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgian_Federal_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legislative procedure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since the elections of 21 May 1995, there has been a breakdown of powers between the Chamber of Representatives and the Senate, which resulted in the latter having fewer competences than the Chamber of Representatives. Prior to that, the Chamber of Representatives and the Senate did the same parliamentary work on an equal footing, but now there are three different legislative procedures that can be followed: the one-chamber procedure, the optional two-chamber procedure, and the mandatory two-chamber procedure.</p>
<p>In certain matters both Chambers have equal power. These include constitutional revisions, laws requiring a qualified majority, laws on the basic structure of the Belgian State, laws approving agreements of cooperation between the <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Belgium" title="Politics of Belgium">Federal State</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communities_and_regions_of_Belgium" title="Communities and regions of Belgium">Communities</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Communities_and_regions_of_Belgium" title="Communities and regions of Belgium">Regions</a>, laws on the approval of international treaties, and laws on the organisation of the judiciary, the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_State_(Belgium)" title="Council of State (Belgium)">Council of State</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constitutional_Court_of_Belgium" title="Constitutional Court of Belgium">Constitutional Court</a>. In this case, the mandatory bicameral procedure applies, which means that both Chambers must pass exactly the same version of the bill.</p>
<p>For most other legislation, the Chamber of Representatives takes precedence over the Senate and the optional bicameral procedure applies. This means that the Senate may still intervene as a chamber of consideration and reflection. It has the opportunity to, within specific time limits, examine the bills adopted by the Chamber of Representatives and, if there is a reason to do so, make amendments. The Chamber may subsequently adopt or reject the amendments proposed by the Senate or make new proposals. The Senate can also submit a bill it has adopted to the Chamber, which can approve, reject or amend it. Whatever the case, the Chamber has the final word.</p>
<p>The one-chamber procedure applies in cases where the Chamber of Representatives has the sole power to legislate. It means that the Senate cannot intervene and that the Senate's approval is not required for the bill to pass. The matters for which the Chamber of Representatives is exclusively responsible include <a href="/wiki/Naturalization" title="Naturalization">naturalisations</a>, ministerial liability, State budget and accounts and military quotas.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Chambers">United Chambers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgian_Federal_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United Chambers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The United Chambers (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <span lang="nl"><i>Verenigde Kamers</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>Chambres réunies</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de"><i>Vereinigten Kammern</i></span>) is the name given to the body created when both chambers of the Federal Parliament meet in <a href="/wiki/Joint_session" title="Joint session">joint session</a>. The United Chambers are convened only on certain occasions enumerated in the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Belgium" title="Constitution of Belgium">Belgian Constitution</a>: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_monarchy" title="Belgian monarchy">King</a> must take the constitutional oath before the United Chambers, in accordance with article 91 of the Constitution, and the United Chambers must provide for the <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regency</a> in the event that the successor to the Crown is a minor or the King is unable to reign, in accordance with articles 92 and 93 of the Constitution. The last session of the United Chambers took place on 21 July 2013, when King <a href="/wiki/Philippe_of_Belgium" title="Philippe of Belgium">Philippe of Belgium</a> took the constitutional oath.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Palace_of_the_Nation">Palace of the Nation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belgian_Federal_Parliament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Palace of the Nation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Palace_of_Nations,_Brussels,_Belgium-LCCN2001697908.tif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2560" data-file-width="3481" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/The_Palace_of_Nations%2C_Brussels%2C_Belgium-LCCN2001697908.tif/lossy-page1-220px-The_Palace_of_Nations%2C_Brussels%2C_Belgium-LCCN2001697908.tif.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/The_Palace_of_Nations%2C_Brussels%2C_Belgium-LCCN2001697908.tif/lossy-page1-330px-The_Palace_of_Nations%2C_Brussels%2C_Belgium-LCCN2001697908.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/The_Palace_of_Nations%2C_Brussels%2C_Belgium-LCCN2001697908.tif/lossy-page1-440px-The_Palace_of_Nations%2C_Brussels%2C_Belgium-LCCN2001697908.tif.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_Palace_of_Nations,_Brussels,_Belgium-LCCN2001697908.tif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A view of the Palace of the Nation in the 1890s</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Palace of the Nation (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr"><i>Palais de la Nation</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <span lang="nl"><i>Paleis der Natie</i></span>) was built to a <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical</a> design by French architect <a href="/wiki/Gilles-Barnab%C3%A9_Guimard" title="Gilles-Barnabé Guimard">Gilles-Barnabé Guimard</a> from 1779 to 1783 and includes sculptures by <a href="/wiki/Gilles-Lambert_Godecharle" title="Gilles-Lambert Godecharle">Gilles-Lambert Godecharle</a>. Under Austrian rule it housed the Sovereign <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Brabant" title="Council of Brabant">Council of Brabant</a> before being used as a courthouse during the French period. During the Dutch period it was one of two homes of the Parliament of the United Kingdom of the Netherlands, the other being in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">the Hague</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Government_of_Belgium" title="Provisional Government of Belgium">provisional Government of Belgium</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_National_Congress" title="Belgian National Congress">Belgian National Congress</a> moved into the building in 1830 and the first session of the House of Representatives and Senate was held there a year later.</p>
<p>It stands near the <a href="/wiki/Coudenberg" title="Coudenberg">site of the former palace</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dukes_of_Brabant" title="Dukes of Brabant">Dukes of Brabant</a>, which was destroyed by fire in 1731, and has itself been badly damaged by fire, in 1820 and 1883.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
