<p>The <b>Belfries of Belgium and France</b> are a group of 56 historical buildings designated by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> as <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>, in recognition of an architectural manifestation of emerging civic independence from feudal and religious influences in <a href="/wiki/County_of_Flanders" title="County of Flanders">historic Flanders</a> and neighboring regions of the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Burgundy" title="Duchy of Burgundy">Duchy of Burgundy</a>.</p>
<p>UNESCO inscribed 32 towers onto its list of <b>Belfries of <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Wallonia</a></b> in 1999. In 2005, the belfry of <a href="/wiki/Gembloux" title="Gembloux">Gembloux</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Walloon Region</a> of Belgium and 23 belfries from the <a href="/wiki/Nord-Pas-de-Calais" title="Nord-Pas-de-Calais">Nord-Pas-de-Calais</a> and <a href="/wiki/Picardy" title="Picardy">Picardy</a> regions in the northern tip of France were appended to the renamed list. One notable omission is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brussels_City_Hall" title="Brussels City Hall">Brussels City Hall</a> belfry, as it is already part of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Place" title="Grand Place">Grand Place</a> World Heritage Site.</p>
<p>However, despite this list being concerned with civic tower structures, additional six church towers were also made part of it under the pretext that they had served as watchtowers or alarm bell towers. These are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cathedral_of_Our_Lady,_Antwerp" title="Cathedral of Our Lady, Antwerp">Cathedral of Our Lady</a> in Antwerp, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Rumbolds_Cathedral" title="St. Rumbolds Cathedral">St. Rumbolds Cathedral</a> in Mechelen, the <a href="/wiki/St._Peter%27s_Church,_Leuven" title="St. Peter's Church, Leuven">St. Peter's Church, Leuven</a>, the St. Germanus Church in <a href="/wiki/Tienen" title="Tienen">Tienen</a>, the Basilica of Our Lady in <a href="/wiki/Tongeren" title="Tongeren">Tongeren</a> and the <a href="/wiki/St._Leonard%27s_Church,_Zoutleeuw" title="St. Leonard's Church, Zoutleeuw">St. Leonard's Church</a> in <a href="/wiki/Zoutleeuw" title="Zoutleeuw">Zoutleeuw</a>.</p>
<p>Most of the structures in this list are towers projecting from broader buildings. However, a few are notably standalone, of which, a handful are rebuilt towers formerly connected to adjacent buildings.</p>

<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">
<ol class="references">
<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The belfry is known as <i>Halletoren</i>, because of an adjacent Cloth Hall that no longer exists; the tower is now free-standing.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-2"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-2">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The belfry is known as <i>Hallentoren</i> or Tower of the Halls, plural: of the two adjacent wings or halls, only one remains, hence Cloth Hall, singular.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-3"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-3">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The city centre's <i>Landhuis</i> (literally: 'country-house') was once the seat of the <i>kasselrij</i> or <i>burggraafschap</i> (viscounty) <a href="/wiki/Veurne-Ambacht" title="Veurne-Ambacht">Veurne-Ambacht</a>, serving the countryside; here as opposed to the adjacent <i>Stadhuis</i> (literally: 'city-house' though always meaning the City Hall) serving the city. The <i>Landhuis</i> later became the Court of Justice and recently a place for cultural purposes, e.g. exhibitions, dance acts, concerts, etc.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-4"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-4">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">The name <i>Mammelokker</i> (assumedly: 'Allurer of breasts') for the guard house at the part of the Cloth Hall that once served as a prison, refers to the <a href="/wiki/Belfry_of_Ghent#Cloth_hall_and_Mammelokker" title="Belfry of Ghent">story of a prisoner</a>.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-5"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-5">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Quote from <a href="#External_links">external link</a> <i>Detailed argumentation for list ID 943/943bis, UNESCO Website</i>: "The Hôtel de Ville in Antwerpen (1564) is an excellent example of the transposition of Renaissance principles in the central risalith with superposed diminishing registers flanked by obelisks and scrollwork and finished with a pediment, reiterating the theme of the central belfry." – <i>Hôtel de Ville</i> is French for 'City Hall', <i>Antwerpen</i> is the native name of 'Antwerp' in Dutch.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-6"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-6">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">UNESCO states, inappropriately in French: ID 943-016 <i>Tour de Saint-Rombaut</i> ; in native Dutch language this is <i>Sint-Romboutstoren</i> which is the main tower of the cathedral, once also used as a watchtower against fires.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-7"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-7">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">UNESCO states, inappropriately in French: ID 943-015 <i>Ancienne Halle avec Beffroi</i> ; in native Dutch language this is <i>Oude [or: Voormalige] Halle met Belfort</i>. This 14th-century Cloth Hall with never to its designed height built Belfry – both hardly ever used for the intended purposes – with more recent adjacent buildings, constitute the present-day City Hall.</span></li>
<li id="cite_note-Dunkirk-8"><span class="mw-cite-backlink">^ <a href="#cite_ref-Dunkirk_8-0"><sup><i><b>a</b></i></sup></a> <a href="#cite_ref-Dunkirk_8-1"><sup><i><b>b</b></i></sup></a></span> <span class="reference-text">UNESCO states: ID 943-040 <i>Beffroi de l’Hôtel de Ville</i>, ID 943-039 <i>Beffroi de l'église Saint-Eloi</i> – further reading from other source: <span class="languageicon" style="font-size:0.95em; font-weight:bold; color:#555;">(French)</span> <a class="external text" href="http://www.nouzautes.net/Dunkerque/monuments.htm" rel="nofollow">Monuments in Dunkirk</a></span></li>
</ol>
