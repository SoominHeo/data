<p><b>Princess Joséphine Charlotte of Belgium</b> (Joséphine Charlotte Ingeborg Elisabeth Maria Josepha/Marie-José Margarethe/Marguerite Astrid; 11 October 1927 - 10 January 2005), also <b>Grand Duchess Joséphine Charlotte of Luxembourg</b> was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Duchess" title="Grand Duchess">Grand Duchess</a> consort of <a href="/wiki/Jean,_Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Jean, Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Jean, Grand Duke of Luxembourg</a>. She was the first cousin of <a href="/wiki/Harald_V_of_Norway" title="Harald V of Norway">King Harald V of Norway</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princess_Astrid_of_Norway" title="Princess Astrid of Norway">Princess Astrid of Norway</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princess_Ragnhild_of_Norway" title="Princess Ragnhild of Norway">Princess Ragnhild of Norway</a>. She was the first child of <a href="/wiki/Leopold_III_of_Belgium" title="Leopold III of Belgium">Leopold III of Belgium</a>, and sister of the late King <a href="/wiki/Baudouin_of_Belgium" title="Baudouin of Belgium">Baudouin of Belgium</a> and King <a href="/wiki/Albert_II_of_Belgium" title="Albert II of Belgium">Albert II of Belgium</a>, the former king of Belgium, and aunt of the current king.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Childhood">Childhood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Jos%C3%A9phine_Charlotte_of_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Childhood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She was born at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Palace_of_Brussels" title="Royal Palace of Brussels">Royal Palace of Brussels</a> as the oldest child and only daughter of King <a href="/wiki/Leopold_III_of_Belgium" title="Leopold III of Belgium">Leopold III of Belgium</a> and his first wife Princess <a href="/wiki/Astrid_of_Sweden" title="Astrid of Sweden">Astrid of Sweden</a>. While expecting her daughter, Astrid had been reading a biography of the Empress Josephine, the consort of Napoleon Bonaparte. She must have found Josephine very appealing, as she decided to name the little Princess, born October 11, 1927, after her. Josephine was also the name of one of the child's great-aunts, the dearest sister of King Albert I. Astrid was a devoted mother to her "little Jo." The young Princess spent her childhood at the Stuyvenberg Palace just outside Brussels with her parents, Prince Léopold and Princess Astrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lu_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-lu-1">[1]</a></sup> Her mother, who was born a Princess of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> and a direct descendant of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">Napoleon Bonaparte</a>'s first wife <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9phine_de_Beauharnais" title="Joséphine de Beauharnais">Joséphine</a>, was killed in an automobile accident in 1935 at age 29. Her father was a direct descendant of <a href="/wiki/St%C3%A9phanie_de_Beauharnais" title="Stéphanie de Beauharnais">Stéphanie de Beauharnais</a>, cousin of Joséphine's first husband and adopted daughter of Joséphine's second husband. She was the sister of Belgian monarchs Baudouin and Albert II.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Jos%C3%A9phine_Charlotte_of_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Princess Joséphine Charlotte first attended school at the Royal Palace, where a small class had been organized for her. At the end of 1940, she entered a boarding school and then continued her education with her own private teachers. On 7 June 1944, the day after the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied Forces</a> landed in <a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, she and her father were sent to <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and kept there under house-arrest. The Royal Family, which included her brothers <a href="/wiki/Baudouin_of_Belgium" title="Baudouin of Belgium">Baudouin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albert_II_of_Belgium" title="Albert II of Belgium">Albert</a> and their stepmother, <a href="/wiki/Lilian,_Princess_of_R%C3%A9thy" title="Lilian, Princess of Réthy">Mary Lilian Baels</a>, Princess of Réthy, was freed on 7 May 1945 and settled in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pr%C3%A9gny&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Prégny (page does not exist)">Prégny</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lu_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-lu-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Princess Joséphine Charlotte continued her studies at the "<i>Ecole Supérieure de Jeunes Filles</i>" in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. Afterwards, she took <a href="/wiki/Jean_Piaget" title="Jean Piaget">Jean Piaget</a>'s lectures on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Child_psychology" title="Child psychology">child psychology</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Geneva" title="University of Geneva">University of Geneva</a>. When she returned to <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> the princess took up her official duties. At the same time, she also devoted herself to social problems and developed her interest in the arts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lu_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-lu-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage">Marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Princess_Jos%C3%A9phine_Charlotte_of_Belgium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Joséphine Charlotte was joined in marriage on 9 April 1953 in <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a> to <a href="/wiki/Jean,_Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Jean, Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Prince Jean</a>, who at the time was The Hereditary Grand Duke and heir-apparent to the throne of Luxembourg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">[2]</a></sup> Although politically motivated, it was a happy match. During their 52-year marriage, the royal couple had five children:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lu_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-lu-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Archduchess_Marie_Astrid_of_Austria" title="Archduchess Marie Astrid of Austria">Princess Marie Astrid of Luxembourg</a> (b. 17 February 1954)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Henri,_Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg" title="Henri, Grand Duke of Luxembourg">Henri, Grand Duke of Luxembourg</a> (b. 16 April 1955)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prince_Jean_of_Luxembourg" title="Prince Jean of Luxembourg">Prince Jean of Luxembourg</a> (b. 15 May 1957)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaretha_of_Liechtenstein" title="Princess Margaretha of Liechtenstein">Princess Margaretha of Luxembourg</a> (b. 15 May 1957)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prince_Guillaume_of_Luxembourg" title="Prince Guillaume of Luxembourg">Prince Guillaume of Luxembourg</a> (b. 1 May 1963)</li>
</ul>
<p>As a princess in the royal house of Belgium, Joséphine Charlotte brought a wealth of elegance, taste and refinement to her new homeland. She carried out many social, cultural and humanitarian duties. She focused on several initiatives that she would ardently support, particularly matters pertaining to children and families. She was also president of the Luxembourg Youth Section of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her favorite hobbies included <a href="/wiki/Gardening" title="Gardening">gardening</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horticulture" title="Horticulture">horticulture</a>. She also enjoyed <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skiing" title="Skiing">skiing</a> and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watersports" title="Watersports">watersports</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lu_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-lu-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Grand Duchess, who suffered from <a href="/wiki/Lung_cancer" title="Lung cancer">lung cancer</a> for a long time, died at her home, <a href="/wiki/Fischbach_Castle" title="Fischbach Castle">Fischbach Castle</a>, at the age of 77, in the presence of her family.</p>
