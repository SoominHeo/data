<p>The <b>Belarusian People's Republic</b> (<a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>: <span lang="be" xml:lang="be">Белару́ская Наро́дная Рэспу́бліка</span>, <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Belarusian" title="Help:IPA for Belarusian">[bʲeɫaˈruskaja naˈrodnaja rɛsˈpublʲika]</a></span>, <a href="/wiki/Transliteration" title="Transliteration">transliterated</a> as <i>Bielaruskaja Narodnaja Respublika</i>, <b>BNR</b>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charter97-bnr_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-charter97-bnr-1">[1]</a></sup> historically referred to as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_Ruthenia" title="White Ruthenia">White Ruthenian</a> Democratic Republic (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Weißruthenische Volksrepublik</i></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>) was a failed attempt to create a <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarusian</a> state on the territory controlled by the German Imperial Army during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. The BNR existed for ten months in 1918. The only diplomatic mission was formed by its founders in Berlin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The BNR was declared on March 25, 1918 in <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a>, but was replaced by a Communist government on January 5, 1919. It became known also as the "Belarusian Democratic Republic". It ceased to exist when Minsk was <a href="/wiki/Soviet_westward_offensive_of_1918%E2%80%9319" title="Soviet westward offensive of 1918–19">captured by the forces of Bolshevik Russia</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byelorussian_SSR" title="Byelorussian SSR">Byelorussian SSR</a> was founded in Minsk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-charter97-bnr_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-charter97-bnr-1">[1]</a></sup> Two years later, the founders of the BNR formed a <a href="/wiki/Government_in_exile" title="Government in exile">government in exile</a> abroad. As of 2016, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rada_BNR" title="Rada BNR">Rada (Council) of the Belarusian People's Republic</a> is the oldest still <a href="/wiki/Government_in_exile#Current_governments_in_exile" title="Government in exile">existing government-in-exile</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_People%27s_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Belarusian People's Republic was declared on the territory of modern-day Belarus three weeks after the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Brest-Litovsk" title="Treaty of Brest-Litovsk">Treaty of Brest-Litovsk</a> was signed on 3 March 1918 between the new Bolshevik government of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_Russia" title="Soviet Russia">Soviet Russia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Central_Powers" title="Central Powers">Central Powers</a> in the border city of Brest-Litovsk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCT_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-SCT-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the 1917 <a href="/wiki/February_Revolution" title="February Revolution">February Revolution</a> in Russia, active discussions started in Belarus about either gaining autonomy within the new democratic Russia or declaring independence. Representatives of most Belarusian regions and of different (mostly left-wing) political powers, including the <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_Socialist_Assembly" title="Belarusian Socialist Assembly">Belarusian Socialist Assembly</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_Christian_Democracy" title="Belarusian Christian Democracy">Christian democratic movement</a> and the <a href="/wiki/General_Jewish_Labour_Bund_in_Lithuania,_Poland_and_Russia" title="General Jewish Labour Bund in Lithuania, Poland and Russia">General Jewish Labour Bund</a>, formed a Belarusian National Council in late 1917. The Council started working on establishing Belarusian governmental institutions. Both the Bolsheviks and Germans refused to recognize it and interfered in its activity. However, the Germans saw an independent Belarus as part of the implementation of their plan for <a href="/wiki/Buffer_state" title="Buffer state">buffer states</a> within <a href="/wiki/Mitteleuropa" title="Mitteleuropa">Mitteleuropa</a>. The Bolsheviks had negotiations with the Belarusian Democratic Republic regarding an eventual recognition, but later decided instead to establish a Soviet puppet government of Belarus - the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_Socialist_Republic_of_Belarus" title="Soviet Socialist Republic of Belarus">Soviet Socialist Republic of Belarus</a>.</p>
<p>Parallel with negotiations that started between the Germans and <a href="/wiki/Bolsheviks" title="Bolsheviks">Bolsheviks</a>, the Belarusian Council started actively demanding recognition of an autonomous status for <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>, with continuing internal discussions on whether it should become an autonomy within Russia or declare national independence.</p>

<p>In its First Constituent Charter, passed on February 21, 1918, the Belarusian Council declared itself the only legitimate power in the territory of Belarus. On March 9, following the signing of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Brest-Litovsk" title="Treaty of Brest-Litovsk">Treaty of Brest-Litovsk</a> between the Germans and Bolsheviks, the Belarusian Council issued a Second Charter where it declared the establishment of the Belarusian People's Republic. The Belarusian Council became the provisional government of Belarus and was renamed the Council of the Belarusian People's Republic.</p>
<p>On March 25, 1918, the Council issued a third charter declaring the independence of Belarus. Following that, local meetings were held within Belarusian cities that issued resolutions supporting the creation of an independent republic.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Territory">Territory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_People%27s_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Territory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In its Third Constituent Charter, the following territories were claimed for BNR: <a href="/wiki/Mogilev_Governorate" title="Mogilev Governorate">Mogilev Governorate</a> (province), as well as Belarusian parts of <a href="/wiki/Minsk_Governorate" title="Minsk Governorate">Minsk Governorate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grodno_Governorate" title="Grodno Governorate">Grodno Governorate</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Bia%C5%82ystok" title="Białystok">Belastok</a>), <a href="/wiki/Vilna_Governorate" title="Vilna Governorate">Vilna Governorate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vitebsk_Governorate" title="Vitebsk Governorate">Vitebsk Governorate</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Smolensk_Governorate" title="Smolensk Governorate">Smolensk Governorate</a>, and parts of bordering governorates populated by Belarusians, rejecting the then split of the Belarusian lands between Germany and Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The areas were claimed because of a Belarusian majority or large minority (as in Grodno and Vilna Governorate), although there were also numbers of <a href="/wiki/Lithuanians" title="Lithuanians">Lithuanians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poles" title="Poles">Poles</a> and people speaking mixed varieties of <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>, as well as many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jews</a>, mostly in towns and cities (in some towns they made up a majority). Some of the Jews spoke Russian as their native tongue; others spoke <a href="/wiki/Yiddish" title="Yiddish">Yiddish</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military">Military</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_People%27s_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Military">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ba%C5%82achowicz.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="282" data-file-width="192" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Ba%C5%82achowicz.JPG/150px-Ba%C5%82achowicz.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b6/Ba%C5%82achowicz.JPG 1.5x" width="150"/></a>

<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ba%C5%82achowicz.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
President and General <a href="/wiki/Stanis%C5%82aw_Bu%C5%82ak-Ba%C5%82achowicz" title="Stanisław Bułak-Bałachowicz">Stanisław Bułak-Bałachowicz</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>There were attempts to create regular <a href="/wiki/Armed_forces" title="Armed forces">armed forces</a> of the newly established Belarusian republic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bib_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-bib-6">[6]</a></sup> Belarusian military units started to form within the disorganized <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Army" title="Imperial Russian Army">Russian army</a> already in 1917.</p>
<p>According to the historian <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Latyszonek" title="Oleg Latyszonek">Oleg Latyszonek</a>, about 11 thousand people, mostly volunteers, served in the army of the Belarusian Republic<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25pytanniau_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-25pytanniau-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>General <a href="/wiki/Stanis%C5%82aw_Bu%C5%82ak-Ba%C5%82achowicz" title="Stanisław Bułak-Bałachowicz">Stanisław Bułak-Bałachowicz</a> supported the Government of BNR and openly positioned his army as a Belarusian national army, also acting as the first President of the Belarusian Provisional Government shortly after the downfall of the BNR before again handing power to the people.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> For his resistance against Bolshevik forces that killed local Belarusian peasantry, members of <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_minority_in_Poland" title="Belarusian minority in Poland">Belarusian minority in Poland</a> regard him as their national hero.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>The major military action of the Belarusian People's Republic army was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slutsk_defence_action" title="Slutsk defence action">Slutsk defence action</a> in late 1920. The Council of the BNR, based at that time in <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>, sent officers to help organize armed <a href="/wiki/Anti-bolshevism" title="Anti-bolshevism">anti-Bolshevik</a> resistance in the town of <a href="/wiki/Slutsk" title="Slutsk">Slutsk</a>. The Belarusian army managed to resist a month against the greater strength of the Red Army.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_actions">Other actions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_People%27s_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other actions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During its short existence, the government of Belarus established close ties with the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_People%27s_Republic" title="Ukrainian People's Republic">Ukrainian People's Republic</a>, organized food supplies to Belarus from Ukraine and thereby prevented hunger in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Diplomatic representations of Belarus had been created in Germany, Estonia, Ukraine and other countries to lobby for Belarusian interests or to support Belarusian soldiers and refugees who landed in different parts of the former Russian Empire.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Beginning in 1918, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Anton_%C5%81uckievi%C4%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Anton Łuckievič (page does not exist)">Anton Łuckievič</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> of Belarus, met with <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin" title="Vladimir Lenin">Vladimir Lenin</a> hoping to gain recognition for the independence of Belarus by Soviet Russia. The Belarusian delegation even proposed the creation of a federation with the RSFSR and the adoption of the Soviet Constitution in Belarus in exchange for Russia recognizing the independent status of Belarus, but Lenin did not agree to these proposals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Diplomatic_mission_of_BNR.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1018" data-file-width="1360" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Diplomatic_mission_of_BNR.jpg/220px-Diplomatic_mission_of_BNR.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Diplomatic_mission_of_BNR.jpg/330px-Diplomatic_mission_of_BNR.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Diplomatic_mission_of_BNR.jpg/440px-Diplomatic_mission_of_BNR.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Diplomatic_mission_of_BNR.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Military and Diplomatic Mission of the Belarusian Democratic Republic in Riga.</div>
</div>

<p>The government also managed to create between 150 and 350 schools and preparations for the creation of a University in Minsk were initiated.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1919, a Delegation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belarusian_Democratic_Republic" title="Belarusian Democratic Republic">Belarusian Democratic Republic</a> under Prime Minister <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Anton_%C5%81uckievi%C4%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Anton Łuckievič (page does not exist)">Anton Łuckievič</a> participated in the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Peace_Conference,_1919" title="Paris Peace Conference, 1919">Paris Peace Conference</a>, attempting to gain international recognition of the independence of Belarus. On the way to the conference, the delegation was received by <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovak</a> president <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tom%C3%A1%C5%A1_Masaryk" title="Tomáš Masaryk">Tomáš Masaryk</a> in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>. During the conference, Łuckievič had meetings with the exiled Foreign Minister of admiral <a href="/wiki/Kolchak" title="Kolchak">Kolchak</a>'s Russian government <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Sazonov" title="Sergey Sazonov">Sergey Sazonov</a> and the Prime Minister of Poland <a href="/wiki/Ignacy_Jan_Paderewski" title="Ignacy Jan Paderewski">Ignacy Jan Paderewski</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Being surrounded by more powerful neighbors and having no allies, the BNR quickly lost its independence and did not become a real state with a <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> or defined territory. However, many modern Belarusian historians suggest that creation of the Belarusian People's Republic was the reason for Bolsheviks creating the puppet <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belarusian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Belarusian Soviet Socialist Republic">Belarusian Soviet Socialist Republic</a> and allowing some elements of national cultural life in the 1920s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exile">Exile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_People%27s_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Exile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Rada_of_the_Belarusian_Democratic_Republic#In_exile" title="Rada of the Belarusian Democratic Republic">Rada of the Belarusian Democratic Republic § In exile</a></div>
<p>In December 1918, the German army retreated from the territory of Belarus and the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> moved in to establish the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Socialist_Soviet_Republic_of_Belarus" title="Socialist Soviet Republic of Belarus">Socialist Soviet Republic of Belarus</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Rada" title="Rada">Rada</a> (Council) of the BNR moved to <a href="/wiki/Grodno" title="Grodno">Grodno</a>, the center of a semi-autonomous Belarusian region within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Lithuania" title="Republic of Lithuania">Republic of Lithuania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bib_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-bib-6">[6]</a></sup> During the subsequent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish-Soviet_War" title="Polish-Soviet War">1919 Polish invasion</a>, the Rada went into exile and facilitated an anti-Communist struggle within the country during the 1920s.</p>
<p>In 1925, the exiled government of the Belarusian Democratic Republic discussed relinquishing its authority in favor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belarusian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Belarusian Soviet Socialist Republic">Belarusian Soviet Socialist Republic</a> controlling the <a href="/wiki/East_Belarus" title="East Belarus">eastern part of Belarus</a>. Despite many members of the democratic government advocating this idea, the proposal was not approved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the Belarusian government-in-exile, based in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>, refused to cooperate with <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> or with the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belarusian_Central_Rada" title="Belarusian Central Rada">Belarusian Central Rada</a>", the pro-German puppet government, and issued statements in support of the Western allies.</p>
<p>The advance of the Red Army in 1945 forced the Rada of the BNR to relocate to the Western part of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, occupied by <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> troops. In February 1948, the Rada passed a special manifesto, by which it declared its return to activity. In April 1948 the Rada, together with representatives of the Belarusian post-war refugees, held a conference in <a href="/wiki/Osterhofen" title="Osterhofen">Osterhofen</a>, Bavaria.</p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">dissolution of the Soviet Union</a> in the 1990s, similar governments-in-exile of the neighboring countries (Lithuania, Poland and others) handed back their mandates to the corresponding independent governments.</p>
<p>Upon declaration of independence of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belarusian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Belarusian Soviet Socialist Republic">Belarusian Soviet Socialist Republic</a> in 1990, it was stated then that the Rada was ready to hand its status to a democratically elected parliament of Belarus. The parliament of Belarus of that time had been elected under Soviet rule. However, these plans were dropped after president <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lukashenko" title="Alexander Lukashenko">Alexander Lukashenko</a>, elected in 1994, established an authoritarian regime accompanied by a return to Soviet policies in regards to Belarusian language and culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rada_BNR" title="Rada BNR">Rada BNR</a> still exists as a <a href="/wiki/Government_in_exile" title="Government in exile">government in exile</a> and attempts to lobby for interests of the Belarusian diaspora in countries where it has its representatives.</p>
<p>Since the late 1980s, March 25, the Independence Day of the Belarusian Democratic Republic, is widely celebrated by the Belarusian national democratic opposition as <i><a href="/wiki/Freedom_Day_(Belarus)" title="Freedom Day (Belarus)">Freedom Day</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>: <span lang="be" xml:lang="be">Дзень волі</span>). It is usually accompanied by mass opposition rallies in <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a> and by celebration events of the Belarusian diaspora organizations supporting the Belarusian government in exile.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Archives">Archives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_People%27s_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Archives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1998, Belarusian linguist and translator Siarhiej Shupa published a two-volume collection of BNR archives (<i>Архівы Беларускай Народнай Рэспублікі. Менск-Вільня-Прага-Нью-Ёрк</i>). The total size of the two volumes is more than 1700 pages. Essentially these are the processed and re-organized documents from the Lithuanian archival fund #582 in <a href="/wiki/Vilnius" title="Vilnius">Vilnius</a> and they constitute roughly 60% of all the BNR official documents from 1918. Another 20% of BNR official documentation is located in the Minsk archives, and the fate of the remaining 20% is unknown.</p>
