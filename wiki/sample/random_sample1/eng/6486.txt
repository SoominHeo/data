<p>At the top level of administration, <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a> is divided into six <i>regions</i> and the city of <a href="/wiki/Minsk" title="Minsk">Minsk</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> which has a special status being the capital of Belarus. Minsk is also the capital of <a href="/wiki/Minsk_Region" title="Minsk Region">Minsk Region</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the second level, the regions are divided into <i><a href="/wiki/Raion" title="Raion">raions</a></i> ("districts").</p>
<p>The layout and extent of the regions were set in 1960 when Belarus (then <a href="/wiki/Byelorussian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic">Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic</a>) was a part of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kge_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-kge-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regions_of_Belarus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the start of the 20th century, the boundaries of the Belarusian lands within the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> were still being defined. Basically in 1900 it contained the entire <a href="/wiki/Minsk_Governorate" title="Minsk Governorate">Minsk Governorate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mogilev_Governorate" title="Mogilev Governorate">Mogilev Governorate</a>, the majority of <a href="/wiki/Grodno_Governorate" title="Grodno Governorate">Grodno Governorate</a>, parts of <a href="/wiki/Vitebsk_Governorate" title="Vitebsk Governorate">Vitebsk Governorate</a> and the parts of <a href="/wiki/Vilna_Governorate" title="Vilna Governorate">Vilna Governorate</a> (the latter is now in <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kge_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-kge-3">[3]</a></sup> World War I, the independence of Poland, as well as the 1920-1921 <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Soviet_War" title="Polish–Soviet War">Polish–Soviet War</a> affected the boundaries. In 1921, Belarus had what is now all of Minsk Governorate except for the western fringe, the western part of Gomel Region, a western slice of Mahilyow, and a small part of Vitebsk Region. In 1926 the eastern part of Gomel region was added. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kge_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-kge-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byelorussian_SSR" title="Byelorussian SSR">Soviet Belarus</a>, new administrative units, called <i>voblast</i> (cognate of <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a> word <i><a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Prothesis_(linguistics)" title="Prothesis (linguistics)">prothetic</a> v-) were introduced in 1938. And again, during World War II, the boundaries fluctuated. In 1939 they were reset with Belarus gaining territory to the west, Baranavichy, Belastok (Bialystok), Brest, Pinsk, and Vileyka oblasts. In 1944 Belastok was eliminated and the new oblasts of Babruysk, Grodno, and Polotsk were created. At that same time, Vileika Voblast was renamed Molodechno Voblast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kge_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-kge-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>At different times between 1938 and 1960, the following <i>voblasts</i> existed:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Babruysk_Voblast" title="Babruysk Voblast">Babruysk Voblast</a>, created 1944, eliminated 1954</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Baranavichy_Voblast" title="Baranavichy Voblast">Baranavichy Voblast</a>, created 1939, eliminated 1954</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belastok_Voblast" title="Belastok Voblast">Belastok Voblast</a>, created 1939, eliminated 1944 (now Bialystok in Poland)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brest_Voblast" title="Brest Voblast">Brest Voblast</a>, created 1939</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gomel_Voblast" title="Gomel Voblast">Gomel Voblast</a>, created 1938</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grodno_Voblast" title="Grodno Voblast">Grodno Voblast</a>, created 1944</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maladzyechna_Voblast" title="Maladzyechna Voblast">Maladzyechna Voblast</a> renamed from Vileyka Voblast 1944, eliminated 1960</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mogilev_Voblast" title="Mogilev Voblast">Mogilev Voblast</a>, created 1938</li>
