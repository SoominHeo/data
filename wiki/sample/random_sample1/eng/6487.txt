<p><b>Belaya Kholunitsa</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Бе́лая Холуни́ца</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Belokholunitsky_District" title="Belokholunitsky District">Belokholunitsky District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kirov_Oblast" title="Kirov Oblast">Kirov Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the <a href="/wiki/Belaya_Kholunitsa_River" title="Belaya Kholunitsa River">Belaya Kholunitsa River</a> (<a href="/wiki/Vyatka_River" title="Vyatka River">Vyatka</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributary</a>), 82 kilometers (51 mi) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Kirov,_Kirov_Oblast" title="Kirov, Kirov Oblast">Kirov</a>, the administrative center of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">11,232 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-3">[3]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">11,975 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-7">[7]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">13,367 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belaya_Kholunitsa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was founded in 1764 due to the construction of an <a href="/wiki/Ironworks" title="Ironworks">ironworks</a> as the settlement of <b>Kholunitsky</b> (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Холуницкий</span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gr_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-gr-5">[5]</a></sup> It was later renamed <b>Belokholunitsky</b> (<span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Белохолуницкий</span>) when the river it stood on was renamed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It was granted town status in 1965.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belaya_Kholunitsa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Belaya Kholunitsa serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Belokholunitsky_District" title="Belokholunitsky District">Belokholunitsky District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KirovO_admlist_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-KirovO_admlist-1">[1]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is, together with nine <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">rural localities</a>, incorporated within Belokholunitsky District as the <b><a href="/wiki/Town_of_district_significance" title="Town of district significance">Town</a> of Belaya Kholunitsa</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KirovO_admlist_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-KirovO_admlist-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the Town of Belaya Kholunitsa is incorporated within Belokholunitsky Municipal District as <b>Belokholunitskoye Urban Settlement</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KirovO_mun_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-KirovO_mun-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belaya_Kholunitsa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Industrial companies in the town include a load-and-carry equipment plant, two <a href="/wiki/Logging" title="Logging">logging</a> and two <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a> companies, a wood-processing and furniture-making plant, a <a href="/wiki/Bread" title="Bread">bread</a>-making plant, among others.</p>
<p>There are deposits of <a href="/wiki/Iron_ore" title="Iron ore">iron ore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">clay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gravel" title="Gravel">gravel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lime_(material)" title="Lime (material)">lime</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rubble" title="Rubble">rubble</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Peat" title="Peat">peat</a> in the town.</p>
