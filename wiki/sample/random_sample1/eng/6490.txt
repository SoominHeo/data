<p><b>Bellamy Young</b> (born <b>Amy Maria Young</b>; February 19, 1970)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American actress, singer and producer, best known for her role as <a href="/wiki/First_Lady_of_the_United_States" title="First Lady of the United States">First Lady of the United States</a> Melody "Mellie" Grant in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Scandal_(TV_series)" title="Scandal (TV series)">Scandal</a></i> (2012–present). In 2014, for her portrayal of Mellie, Young won the <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Television_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Drama_Series" title="Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Drama Series">Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Drama Series</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bellamy_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Young was born as Amy Maria Young<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Asheville,_North_Carolina" title="Asheville, North Carolina">Asheville, North Carolina</a>, and raised by adoptive parents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube-3">[3]</a></sup> Her mother was her teacher from sixth grade through eighth grade. She had to change her name to join the <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild" title="Screen Actors Guild">Screen Actors Guild</a> since there was another Amy Young registered<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-4">[4]</a></sup> and chose the name Bellamy as a tribute to her late father's best friend, Bill, who had helped to raise her after her father died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>She graduated from <a href="/wiki/Asheville_High_School" title="Asheville High School">Asheville High School</a> in 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She attended <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> studying English and Theatre and graduated in 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Young spent a summer during college at the <a href="/wiki/British_American_Drama_Academy" title="British American Drama Academy">British American Drama Academy</a> at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">Oxford University</a> in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buddytv_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-buddytv-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bellamy_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1995.E2.80.932011">1995–2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bellamy_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1995–2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Young began her acting career in theatre. In the 1990s, she performed in a number of <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> shows including the original production of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_(musical)" title="The Life (musical)">The Life</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canby_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Canby-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brantley1997_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brantley1997-10">[10]</a></sup> On Off-Broadway, Young appeared on <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Sondheim" title="Stephen Sondheim">Stephen Sondheim</a>'s musical <i><a href="/wiki/Merrily_We_Roll_Along_(musical)" title="Merrily We Roll Along (musical)">Merrily We Roll Along</a></i> (1994), and <a href="/wiki/Randy_Newman" title="Randy Newman">Randy Newman</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Faust" title="Faust">Faust</a></i> (1995–1996).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvg_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brantley1996_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brantley1996-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1995, Young made her television debut in a recurring role on the NBC daytime soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Another_World_(TV_series)" title="Another World (TV series)">Another World</a></i> as Dr. Courtney Evans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buddytv_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-buddytv-8">[8]</a></sup> She guest-starred on <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i> in 1997 and 1998, in two different roles. In 1999 she made her film debut with a small role in the crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Black_and_White_(1999_drama_film)" title="Black and White (1999 drama film)">Black and White</a></i> and later co-starred in several independent films. In 2000's she began appearing in guest-starring roles on a number of television dramas and comedies, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Drew_Carey_Show" title="The Drew Carey Show">The Drew Carey Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">The X Files</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Frasier" title="Frasier">Frasier</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_West_Wing" title="The West Wing">The West Wing</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS_(TV_series)" title="NCIS (TV series)">NCIS</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Medium_(TV_series)" title="Medium (TV series)">Medium</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Grey%27s_Anatomy" title="Grey's Anatomy">Grey's Anatomy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Private_Practice_(TV_series)" title="Private Practice (TV series)">Private Practice</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Two_and_a_Half_Men" title="Two and a Half Men">Two and a Half Men</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Supernatural (U.S. TV series)">Supernatural</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Drop_Dead_Diva" title="Drop Dead Diva">Drop Dead Diva</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Castle_(TV_series)" title="Castle (TV series)">Castle</a></i>. From 2000 to 2011, Young made over 30 guest appearances on television shows.</p>
<p>Young was a regular cast member in the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Peacemakers_(TV_series)" title="Peacemakers (TV series)">Peacemakers</a></i> in 2003. The show was cancelled after one season of nine episodes. She had recurring roles in the <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a> legal drama series <i><a href="/wiki/For_the_People_(2002_TV_series)" title="For the People (2002 TV series)">For the People</a></i> as Deputy Dist. Atty. Agnes Hunt in 2002, on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> period drama <i><a href="/wiki/American_Dreams" title="American Dreams">American Dreams</a></i> as Diane Shaw in 2003, on NBC's <i><a href="/wiki/Scrubs_(TV_series)" title="Scrubs (TV series)">Scrubs</a></i> as Dr. Grace Miller in 2004, as Assistant State Attorney Monica West on CBS's <i><a href="/wiki/CSI:_Miami" title="CSI: Miami">CSI: Miami</a></i> (2005–06), and on ABC primetime soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Dirty_Sexy_Money" title="Dirty Sexy Money">Dirty Sexy Money</a></i> (2008–2009) as Ellen Darling, the eldest daughter-in-law of the Darling family. She also had a recurring role in <i><a href="/wiki/Criminal_Minds" title="Criminal Minds">Criminal Minds</a></i> as Beth Clemmons from 2011 to 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<p>Young has appeared in supporting roles in a couple of films, including <i><a href="/wiki/We_Were_Soldiers" title="We Were Soldiers">We Were Soldiers</a></i> (2002) and <i><a href="/wiki/Mission:_Impossible_III" title="Mission: Impossible III">Mission: Impossible III</a></i> (2006), along with independent movies like <i><a href="/wiki/Larceny_(film)" title="Larceny (film)">Larceny</a></i> (2004), <i>Eve of Understanding</i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Trust_Me_(2007_film)" title="Trust Me (2007 film)">Trust Me</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_Not_a_Test_(2008_film)" title="This Is Not a Test (2008 film)">This Is Not a Test</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/In_My_Sleep" title="In My Sleep">In My Sleep</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Freebie_(film)" title="The Freebie (film)">The Freebie</a></i> (2010), and <i><a href="/wiki/Joint_Body" title="Joint Body">Joint Body</a></i> (2014).</p>

<p>In 2011 <a href="/wiki/Shonda_Rhimes" title="Shonda Rhimes">Shonda Rhimes</a> cast Young in the recurring role of First Lady, then 2016 Republican Presidential nominee, Melody "Mellie" Grant on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> political thriller television series <i><a href="/wiki/Scandal_(TV_series)" title="Scandal (TV series)">Scandal</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Kerry_Washington" title="Kerry Washington">Kerry Washington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Goldwyn" title="Tony Goldwyn">Tony Goldwyn</a>. Before <i>Scandal</i>, Young appeared in Shonda Rhimes' <i>Grey's Anatomy</i> and <i>Private Practice</i>. She appeared in every episode of the first season of <i>Scandal</i> and was upgraded to a series regular as of season two.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Young later said that originally her part was a minor role for the three episode recurring arc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>Young has received critical acclaim for her performance as Melody Grant throughout her time on the show .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Beast" title="The Daily Beast">The Daily Beast</a></i> named her the "Breakout Star" of the show in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Many critics praised Young's performance in her character's centered episode "<a href="/wiki/Everything%27s_Coming_Up_Mellie" title="Everything's Coming Up Mellie">Everything's Coming Up Mellie</a>" of the third season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Cicely K. Dyson's review for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> states: "Bellamy Young has been an amazing scene stealer for two-and-a-half seasons, and this time she finally got her chance to shine."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Robert Rorke, writing for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_New_York_Post" title="The New York Post">The New York Post</a></i>, states that, "In Mellie, the show has its most fleshed-out character and in Young, its most compelling performer."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> She won a <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Television_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Drama_Series" title="Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Drama Series">Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Drama Series</a> for her performance in season three.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> She also participated in an online <a href="/wiki/Unmute" title="Unmute">Unmute</a> conference call with thousands of her fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 15, 2015, Young released her first album, <i>Far Away So Close</i>, on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The 10-song album <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">covers</a> songs from <a href="/wiki/Pink_(singer)" title="Pink (singer)">Pink</a> to <a href="/wiki/Fleetwood_Mac" title="Fleetwood Mac">Fleetwood Mac</a>.</p>
<p>In 2016, it was announced Young had joined the cast of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disney" title="Disney">Disney</a>'s live action adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Wrinkle_in_Time_(2018_film)" title="A Wrinkle in Time (2018 film)">A Wrinkle in Time</a></i> along with <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" title="Oprah Winfrey">Oprah Winfrey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reese_Witherspoon" title="Reese Witherspoon">Reese Witherspoon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mindy_Kaling" title="Mindy Kaling">Mindy Kaling</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bellamy_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Young is close friends with ex-boyfriend <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Leonard" title="Joshua Leonard">Joshua Leonard</a> whom she dated from 2000-2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> Young began dating British actor <a href="/wiki/Ed_Weeks" title="Ed Weeks">Ed Weeks</a> in January 2016 and the two went public with their relationship the following month at the <a href="/wiki/Elton_John_AIDS_Foundation_Academy_Award_Party" title="Elton John AIDS Foundation Academy Award Party">Elton John AIDS Foundation Academy Award Party</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Young resides in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and has 1 dog and 3 cats.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_philanthropy">Politics and philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bellamy_Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Politics and philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Young is a <a href="/wiki/Feminism" title="Feminism">feminist</a> and has been <a href="/wiki/Veganism" title="Veganism">vegan</a> since 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Via <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a>, Young has stated she is in favour of <a href="/wiki/Gun_control" title="Gun control">gun control</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>

<p>In 2015, Young won <i>Celebrity <a href="/wiki/Jeopardy!" title="Jeopardy!">Jeopardy!</a></i> and as a result won $50,000 for Operation Blankets of Love - a charity which donates blankets to animals shelters. At the 2015 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GMHC" title="GMHC">GMHC</a> <a href="/wiki/AIDS_Walk_New_York" title="AIDS Walk New York">AIDS Walk New York</a>, Young gave a speech at the opening ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> A long time supporter of shelter adoption, Young starred in a 2015 televised ad campaign titled 'The Shelter Pet Project', with <a href="/wiki/The_Humane_Society_of_the_United_States" title="The Humane Society of the United States">The Humane Society of the United States</a>. As a teenager, Young began to suffer from <a href="/wiki/Migraine" title="Migraine">migraines</a> and still suffers from them today. She has been a partner with <a href="/wiki/GlaxoSmithKline" title="GlaxoSmithKline">GlaxoSmithKline</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treximet" title="Treximet">Treximet</a>, since 2015, to spread awareness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Young is a <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrat</a> and headlined at a fundraiser event for <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a> in April 2016 - for which ticket prices were up to $5000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> In August, September, October and November 2016, Young hit the campaign trail for Clinton and visited <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> After performing at the 2015 National Christmas Tree Lighting, it was announced that in 2016 Young would be co-ambassador, along with <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Clinton" title="Chelsea Clinton">Chelsea Clinton</a>, of the Trust for the <a href="/wiki/National_Mall" title="National Mall">National Mall</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Young is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pro_Choice" title="Pro Choice">Pro Choice</a> and in 2016 was one of seven actresses to campaign for "draw the line", which aims to draw the line against attacks on safe and legal abortion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In the same year, Young participated in <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">PETA</a>'s "Being Vegan Keeps Me Young" campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> In May 2016, Young announced via <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> she would once again be working with <a href="/wiki/The_Humane_Society_of_the_United_States" title="The Humane Society of the United States">The Humane Society of the United States</a> on a televised <a href="/wiki/Public_service_announcement" title="Public service announcement">Public service announcement</a> titled "Honestly".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Young is the honorary chair for the <a href="/wiki/Domestic_violence" title="Domestic violence">Domestic violence</a> <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit_organization" title="Nonprofit organization">Nonprofit</a> 'Helpmate' and has helped fundraise for victims of domestic violence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> As part of the <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_Barack_Obama" title="Presidency of Barack Obama">Obama administration legacy</a>, in 2016, Young appeared in 'Women Film' by The United State of Women along with several other public figures such as <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Obama" title="Michelle Obama">Michelle Obama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meryl_Streep" title="Meryl Streep">Meryl Streep</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey" title="Oprah Winfrey">Oprah Winfrey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> In August 2016 it was announced that Young will be an honorary committee member for the 2017 Vettys Presidential <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_inaugural_balls" title="United States presidential inaugural balls">Inaugural Ball</a> along with several of her <a href="/wiki/Scandal_(TV_series)" title="Scandal (TV series)">Scandal</a> cast members and the likes of <a href="/wiki/Kate_Walsh_(actress)" title="Kate Walsh (actress)">Kate Walsh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Douglas" title="Michael Douglas">Michael Douglas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>Young partnered with <a href="/wiki/Merck_%26_Co." title="Merck &amp; Co.">Merck &amp; Co.</a>, in November 2016, to help educate people on the importance of <a href="/wiki/Biomarker_discovery" title="Biomarker discovery">Biomarker Testing</a> in non-small cell Lung Cancer - a cause dear to Young as her adopted father was diagnosed with lung cancer in August 1984 and died 10 months later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> As a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yale_alumni" title="Yale alumni">Yale Graduate</a>, Young was given the opportunity to endow a scholarship to a student attending Yale in 2016.</p>
