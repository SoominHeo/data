<p><b>Belene</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg" xml:lang="bg">Белене</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pleven_Province" title="Pleven Province">Pleven Province</a>, Northern <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>. It is the administrative centre of the homonymous <a href="/wiki/Belene_Municipality" title="Belene Municipality">Belene Municipality</a>. The town is situated on the right bank of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danube_river" title="Danube river">Danube river</a>, close to the town of <a href="/wiki/Svishtov" title="Svishtov">Svishtov</a>. As of December 2009, it has a population of 8,905 inhabitants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-statistika_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-statistika-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Belene was proclaimed a town in 1964,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSI-Belene1964_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NSI-Belene1964-2">[2]</a></sup> which is celebrated every year in the first week of September with a market and cultural events.</p>
<p>The place is best known for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belene_prison" title="Belene prison">Belene prison</a> (formerly a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concentration_camp" title="Concentration camp">concentration camp</a>) on <a href="/wiki/Belene_Island" title="Belene Island">Belene Island</a> where thousands of innocent Bulgarians found their death during the communist dictatorship, and as the proposed site of <a href="/wiki/Belene_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Belene Nuclear Power Plant">Bulgaria's second nuclear power plant</a>, which has been under construction 7 kilometres (4 miles) east of the town until it was interrupted in 1990 due to the severe economic crisis that followed the fall of communism in the country. There are plans to start construction again as a replacement for reactors 3 and 4 at <a href="/wiki/Kozloduy_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Kozloduy Nuclear Power Plant">Kozloduy Nuclear Power Plant</a>, which Bulgaria shut down as a condition for EU membership. The total capacity of the two reactors will be 2,000 MW.</p>
<p>The main confessions in the town are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodoxy" title="Eastern Orthodoxy">Eastern Orthodoxy</a>, with two Roman Catholic and one Orthodox church being present. The Catholic Church of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blessed_Virgin_Mary" title="Blessed Virgin Mary">Blessed Virgin Mary</a> was built in 1860, the Eastern Orthodox Church of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_George" title="St George">St George</a> in 1874 and the Catholic Church of Saint <a href="/wiki/Anthony_of_Padua" title="Anthony of Padua">Anthony of Padua</a> was constructed in 1893.</p>
<p>Belene was the focal point for the feature documentary film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mosquito_Problem_and_Other_Stories" title="The Mosquito Problem and Other Stories">The Mosquito Problem and Other Stories</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Andrey_Paounov" title="Andrey Paounov">Andrey Paounov</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The film features many of the town's colourful characters, highlighting their daily plight of a nuclear plant of empty promises and a year-round <a href="/wiki/Mosquito" title="Mosquito">mosquito</a> epidemic.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Belene_Cove" title="Belene Cove">Belene Cove</a> in <a href="/wiki/Livingston_Island" title="Livingston Island">Livingston Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/South_Shetland_Islands" title="South Shetland Islands">South Shetland Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> is named after Belene.</p>
