<p>The <b>State Anthem of the Byelorussian SSR</b> (<a href="/wiki/Belarusian_language" title="Belarusian language">Belarusian</a>: <span lang="be">Дзяржаўны гімн Беларускай Савецкай Сацыялiстычнай Рэспублiкi</span>, <i>Dziaržawny himn Biełaruskaj Savieckaj Sacyjalistyčnaj Respubliki</i>, <a href="/wiki/Belarusian_Arabic_alphabet" title="Belarusian Arabic alphabet">ࢮارژاونِ هِمن بيَلارُصقاي صاويَࢯقاي صاࢯِيالِصطِچناي رَصپُبلِقِ</a>) was the <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">state anthem</a> of <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a> when it was a republic of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byelorussian_SSR" title="Byelorussian SSR">Byelorussian SSR</a>. The anthem was used from 1952 until Belarusian independence in 1991. It took over 11 years to create the music and lyrics for the anthem,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> even producing a version that mentions then-Soviet leader <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Stalin" title="Joseph Stalin">Joseph Stalin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The music was composed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nestar_Sakalowski" title="Nestar Sakalowski">Nestar Sakalowski</a> (Нестар Сакалоўскі), and the lyrics were written by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mihas%27_Klimovich" title="Mihas' Klimovich">Mihas' Klimovich</a> (Міхась Клімковіч). The melody is used in the <a href="/wiki/My_Belarusy" title="My Belarusy">current national anthem of Belarus</a>, with different lyrics.</p>
