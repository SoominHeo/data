<p>This <b>flag</b> was adopted by the <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byelorussian_SSR" title="Byelorussian SSR">Byelorussian SSR</a></b> on December 25, 1951. Prior to this, the flag was red with the <a href="/wiki/Cyrillic_script" title="Cyrillic script">Cyrillic</a> characters <i>БССР</i> (<i>BSSR</i>) in gold in the top-left corner, surrounded by a gold border. Between 1937 and the adoption of the above flag in the 1940s, the flag was the same, but with a gold <a href="/wiki/Hammer_and_sickle" title="Hammer and sickle">hammer and sickle</a> above the Cyrillic characters and no border. Between 1919 and 1937, the flag was red, with the <a href="/wiki/Cyrillic_script" title="Cyrillic script">Cyrillic</a> characters <i>ССРБ</i> (<i>SSRB</i>) in the top left-hand corner. In early 1919, a plain red flag was used. The final BySSR flag was used until the collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> in 1991. A flag based on this design is used as the current national <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Belarus" title="Flag of Belarus">flag</a> of <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_on_the_1951_flag">Design on the 1951 flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_Byelorussian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design on the 1951 flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the end of the 1940s, the political need had arisen to have somewhat visually different designs of the flags of the USSR republics, especially for those that were UN members. For the BSSR flag, the image of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Belarusian_folk_design&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Belarusian folk design (page does not exist)">Belarusian folk design</a> had been finally chosen as a distinctive feature of the flag. Then the picture of the embroidery on the «ruchnik» (handtowel) had been found in the pre-World War II archives of the Belpramsavyet. The embroidery had been made in 1917 by peasant Ms. Matrona Markyevich of the village Kastsilishcha of the Sennin region, and had been named «The Rising Sun». Artist M. I. Gusyew had prepared the project of the BSSR flag, basing it on the embroidery, with several symbolic elements added.</p>
<div class="center">
<div class="floatnone"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Belarus_flag_pattern_(1951-1991).svg"><img alt="Belarus flag pattern (1951-1991).svg" data-file-height="66" data-file-width="300" height="66" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Belarus_flag_pattern_%281951-1991%29.svg/300px-Belarus_flag_pattern_%281951-1991%29.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Belarus_flag_pattern_%281951-1991%29.svg/450px-Belarus_flag_pattern_%281951-1991%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Belarus_flag_pattern_%281951-1991%29.svg/600px-Belarus_flag_pattern_%281951-1991%29.svg.png 2x" width="300"/></a></div>
</div>
<p>Symbolically, the design on the BSSR flag is decoded as follows (considering design strip positioned <i>horizontally</i>):</p>
<ul>
<li>the central rhombus figure symbolises the rising Sun;</li>
<li>the horn-like figures to the left and right of it symbolise wealth and welfare;</li>
<li>the figure inside of the rhombus is the "key to happiness";</li>
<li>the rectangular figure with the smaller rectangles adjoining from the left and right is the "votive sign", expressing the desire for fulfillment of hopes;</li>
<li>the pattern figure symbolises bread (it was absent from the original embroidery, added after some discussion).</li>
</ul>
<p>Note: All proper names and places' names in this article are rendered in BGN/PCGN.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Previous_flags_of_the_Soviet_era">Previous flags of the Soviet era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_Byelorussian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Previous flags of the Soviet era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before 1951, several different flags had been in use since the Revolution. The earliest flag was plain red, and was used in 1919 during the existence of the <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian%E2%80%93Byelorussian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic" title="Lithuanian–Byelorussian Soviet Socialist Republic">Lithuanian–Byelorussian SSR</a>. After the formation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byelorussian_SSR" title="Byelorussian SSR">Byelorussian SSR</a>, the lettering <i>ССРБ</i> (SSRB) was added in gold to the top hoist. This design was changed in 1937, when a hammer and sickle and red star were placed above the letters; at the same time, the typeface was changed, and the text of the lettering was altered to <i>БССР</i> (BSSR). During the 1940s, the hammer and sickle and red star were removed from the flag, and a gold border was added to the letters. This flag remained in use until the adoption of the 1951 flag. In September 1991, the white-red-white flag was reintroduced as the new flag of the newly independent Byelorussia (Belarus).</p>
