<p><i><b>WWE Velocity</b></i> was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">television program</a> produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Wrestling_Entertainment" title="World Wrestling Entertainment">World Wrestling Entertainment</a> (WWE). It replaced two syndicated WWE shows, <i><a href="/wiki/WWF_Jakked/Metal" title="WWF Jakked/Metal">Jakked/Metal</a></i>. Once a weekly Saturday night show on <a href="/wiki/Spike_(TV_network)" title="Spike (TV network)">Spike TV</a> and on Sky Sports 2 in the UK on Sunday mornings, Velocity became a webcast from 2005 to 2006. The newest episode would be uploaded to WWE.com on Saturdays and be available for the next week. Older webcast episodes were also archived. It was the counterpart show to <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">WWE SmackDown</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">WWE Raw</a></i> and was recorded before the television taping of <i>SmackDown</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWE_Velocity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Former_WWE_Smackdown_Arena.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Former_WWE_Smackdown_Arena.jpg/200px-Former_WWE_Smackdown_Arena.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Former_WWE_Smackdown_Arena.jpg/300px-Former_WWE_Smackdown_Arena.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Former_WWE_Smackdown_Arena.jpg/400px-Former_WWE_Smackdown_Arena.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Former_WWE_Smackdown_Arena.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
WWE Velocity set used from May 25, 2002 to June 11, 2006, which is exactly the same set used for <i>SmackDown</i>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>WWE Velocity</i> was primarily used to summarize major occurrences on the latest episode of <i>SmackDown!</i>, which aired Thursday and later Friday nights on <a href="/wiki/UPN" title="UPN">UPN</a>. Due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WWE_Brand_Extension" title="WWE Brand Extension">WWE Brand Extension</a>, <i>Velocity</i> aired matches and content from the <a href="/wiki/WWE_SmackDown" title="WWE SmackDown">SmackDown brand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclopedia_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclopedia-1">[1]</a></sup> The format was set to mirror that of <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Heat" title="WWE Heat">WWE Heat</a></i> and its relation to the <a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">Raw brand</a><i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Encyclopedia_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Encyclopedia-1">[1]</a></sup></i></p>
<p>Following <i><a href="/wiki/WWE_Raw" title="WWE Raw">WWE Raw</a>'</i>s move from Spike TV back to the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a> in 2005, <i>Velocity</i> and its Raw brand counterpart, <i>Heat</i>, were discontinued from television broadcast in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and became <a href="/wiki/Webcast" title="Webcast">webcasts</a> streamed on WWE.com. Internationally, <i>Velocity</i> and <i>Heat</i> continued to be broadcast on their respective television networks due to WWE's international programming commitments.</p>
<p>The last episode of <i>Velocity</i> aired internationally on June 10, 2006. It was also the last episode to be streamed on WWE.com.</p>
