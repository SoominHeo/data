<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="130">130</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle selfreference noprint" role="note">This section is <a href="/wiki/Transclusion" title="Transclusion">transcluded</a> from <a href="/wiki/130" title="130">130</a>. <span class="plainlinks" style="font-style: normal"><small>(<a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=130&amp;action=edit">edit</a> | <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=130&amp;action=history">history</a>)</small></span></div>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=130&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="130">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=130&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="130">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">law</a> is passed in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Rome</a> banning the execution of <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a> without a trial.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temple_of_Olympian_Zeus_(Athens)" title="Temple of Olympian Zeus (Athens)">Temple of Olympian Zeus</a> is completed at <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>.</li>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Hadrian" title="Hadrian">Hadrian</a> visits the cities <a href="/wiki/Petra" title="Petra">Petra</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gerasa" title="Gerasa">Gerasa</a> (Jerash).</li>
<li>A Triumphal Arch for Hadrian is built in Gerasa.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Canopus" title="Canopus">Canopus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hadrian%27s_Villa" title="Hadrian's Villa">Hadrian's Villa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tivoli,_Italy" title="Tivoli, Italy">Tivoli, Italy</a>, is started to be built.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Hadrian%27s_Gate" title="Hadrian's Gate">Hadrian's Gate</a> in <a href="/wiki/Antalya" title="Antalya">Antalya</a> (<a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>) is constructed, Hadrian visits the city.</li>
</ul>

<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire_2">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=131&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="131">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Hadrian" title="Hadrian">Hadrian</a> builds the city <a href="/wiki/Aelia_Capitolina" title="Aelia Capitolina">Aelia Capitolina</a> on the location of <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Praetor%27s_Edict" title="Praetor's Edict">Praetor's Edict</a> is definitively codified by <a href="/wiki/Salvius_Julianus" title="Salvius Julianus">Salvius Julianus</a> on Hadrian's orders. This change means that senatorial decrees become a mere confirmation of the imperial speech (<i>oratio principis</i>) which initiated them.</li>
<li>Reorganization of the Imperial Council: Central administration is reinforced, and administrative positions are entrusted to Knights according to a very strict hierarchy. Under the reorganization, the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Senate" title="Roman Senate">Roman Senate</a> is excluded from controlling the business of state.</li>
<li>Hadrian restores the monarchist policy of <a href="/wiki/Claudius" title="Claudius">Claudius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Domitian" title="Domitian">Domitian</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Equestrian_order" title="Equestrian order">equestrian order</a> is given full legal status and attains the second order of the state.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> is divided into legal districts managed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consuls" title="Consuls">consuls</a>, a direct blow to the power and prestige of the Senate.</li>
</ul>

They will be defeated by the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Senate" title="Roman Senate">senator</a> Tiberius Haterius Nepos Atinas, governor of <a href="/wiki/Pannonia" title="Pannonia">Pannonia</a>, in <a href="/wiki/138" title="138">138</a>.</li>

<li>The Roman province of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iudaea" title="Iudaea">Iudaea</a> (plus Galilee) becomes <a href="/wiki/Syria_Palaestina" title="Syria Palaestina">Syria Palaestina</a>, the first use of the name <a href="/wiki/Palestine_(region)" title="Palestine (region)">Palestine</a> as a designation for <a href="/wiki/Judea" title="Judea">Judea</a>.</li>
<li>Hadrian dictates his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Memoirs" title="Memoirs">memoirs</a> at his <a href="/wiki/Hadrian%27s_Villa" title="Hadrian's Villa">villa</a> near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tivoli,_Italy" title="Tivoli, Italy">Tivoli</a> (Tibur) outside <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Rome</a>.</li>
<li>Hadrian uncovers a new conspiracy among certain senators. He adopts <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Aelius" title="Lucius Aelius">Lucius Aelius</a> as his heir.</li>
</ul>

<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire_8">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=137&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="137">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place_9">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=138&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="138">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire_9">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=138&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="138">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_25" title="February 25">February 25</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Hadrian" title="Hadrian">Hadrian</a> makes <a href="/wiki/Antoninus_Pius" title="Antoninus Pius">Antoninus Pius</a> his successor, on condition that he adopts <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Aurelius" title="Marcus Aurelius">Marcus Aurelius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Verus" title="Lucius Verus">Lucius Verus</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_10" title="July 10">July 10</a> – Hadrian dies after a <a href="/wiki/Heart_failure" title="Heart failure">heart failure</a> at <a href="/wiki/Baiae" title="Baiae">Baiae</a>, he is buried at <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Rome</a> in the Gardens of Domitia beside his wife, <a href="/wiki/Vibia_Sabina" title="Vibia Sabina">Vibia Sabina</a>.</li>
<li>Antoninus Pius succeeds Hadrian as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Emperor" title="Roman Emperor">Roman Emperor</a> and asks the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Senate" title="Roman Senate">Senate</a> to confer divine honors for Hadrian.</li>
<li>Construction begins on the Theater of Philadelphia (<a href="/wiki/Amman" title="Amman">Amman</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hadrian%27s_Villa" title="Hadrian's Villa">Hadrian's Villa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tivoli,_Italy" title="Tivoli, Italy">Tivoli, Italy</a>, is finished.</li>
</ul>

<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Commerce">Commerce</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=138&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-4" title="138">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver">silver</a> content of the Roman <a href="/wiki/Denarius" title="Denarius">denarius</a> falls to 75 percent under emperor Antoninus Pius, down from 87 percent under the reign of Hadrian.</li>
</ul>

<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire_10">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=139&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="139">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Castel_Sant%27Angelo" title="Castel Sant'Angelo">Tomb of Hadrian</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Rome</a> is completed; emperor <a href="/wiki/Antoninus_Pius" title="Antoninus Pius">Antoninus Pius</a> cremates the body of <a href="/wiki/Hadrian" title="Hadrian">Hadrian</a> and places his <a href="/wiki/Cremation" title="Cremation">ashes</a> together with that of his wife <a href="/wiki/Vibia_Sabina" title="Vibia Sabina">Vibia Sabina</a> and his adopted son, <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Aelius" title="Lucius Aelius">Lucius Aelius</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Mausoleum" title="Mausoleum">mausoleum</a>.</li>

<li>Antoninus Augustus Pius and <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Bruttius_Praesens_(consul_AD_139)" title="Gaius Bruttius Praesens (consul AD 139)">Gaius Bruttius Praesens</a> become <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Consul" title="Roman Consul">Roman Consuls</a>.</li>
