<p>Year <b>563</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DLXIII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 563 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=563&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=563&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=563&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Byzantine Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Justinian_I" title="Justinian I">Justinian I</a> pardons <a href="/wiki/Belisarius" title="Belisarius">Belisarius</a>; he orders his release from <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">prison</a>, and restores his properties and <a href="/wiki/Honour" title="Honour">honours</a>. He permits the general to live in obscurity, and gives him a veterans' <a href="/wiki/Pension" title="Pension">pension</a>.</li>
<li>The new <a href="/wiki/Hagia_Sophia" title="Hagia Sophia">Hagia Sophia</a> (costs 20,000 pounds of <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>), with its numerous <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapels</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shrine" title="Shrine">shrines</a>, octagonal <a href="/wiki/Dome" title="Dome">dome</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mosaic" title="Mosaic">mosaics</a>, becomes the centre and most visible monument of <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodoxy</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=563&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Tauredunum_event" title="Tauredunum event">Tauredunum event</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A mountain landslide into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhone" title="Rhone">Rhone</a> river destroys a fort and two villages, and creates a <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Geneva" title="Lake Geneva">Lake Geneva</a>. The wave which reaches <a href="/wiki/Lausanne" title="Lausanne">Lausanne</a> is thirteen metres high, and eight metres high by the time it hits <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>. Describing the event, <a href="/wiki/Marius_Aventicensis" title="Marius Aventicensis">Marius Aventicensis</a> writes that the tsunami "devastated very old villages with their men and cattle, it even destroyed many sacred places", and swept away "the bridge in Geneva, windmills and men".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=563&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=563&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Columba" title="Columba">Columba</a>, Irish <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionary</a> <a href="/wiki/Monk" title="Monk">monk</a>, travels to <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> with twelve companions. On the island of <a href="/wiki/Iona" title="Iona">Iona</a>, he founds a monastery (<a href="/wiki/Iona_Abbey" title="Iona Abbey">Iona Abbey</a>) on the west coast in the <a href="/wiki/Inner_Hebrides" title="Inner Hebrides">Inner Hebrides</a>.</li>
