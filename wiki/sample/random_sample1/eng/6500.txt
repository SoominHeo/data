<p><b>Velika Polana</b> (<small>pronounced¬†</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Slovene" title="Help:IPA for Slovene">[Àà ãeÀêlika ÀàpoÀêlana]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <span lang="hu" xml:lang="hu"><i>Nagypalina</i></span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vengust_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vengust-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Prekmurje_Slovene" title="Prekmurje Slovene">Prekmurje Slovene</a>: <i>Velka Polana,</i><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> or <i>V√∂lka Polana,</i><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Teufelslochn</i></span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>) is a town and a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Slovenia" title="Municipalities of Slovenia">municipality</a> in <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>.</p>
<p>It has been officially designated a "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_Stork" title="White Stork">stork</a> village" (i.e., a settlement with 10 or more storks‚Äô nests). It was also the home of the Slovene writer <a href="/wiki/Mi%C5%A1ko_Kranjec" title="Mi≈°ko Kranjec">Mi≈°ko Kranjec</a>. The house where he was born (which is also the tourist information office) can be visited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The village is surrounded by fenland and fields of sunflowers, pumpkins, and wheat. The best way to explore the area is by cycling or walking the numerous trails.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a> in the settlement is dedicated to the <a href="/wiki/Sacred_Heart" title="Sacred Heart">Sacred Heart of Jesus</a> and belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Murska_Sobota" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Murska Sobota">Roman Catholic Diocese of Murska Sobota</a>. It was built in 1924.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
