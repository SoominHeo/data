<p>The <b>Strait of Belle Isle</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>détroit de Belle Isle (Beautiful Island)</i></span>) is a waterway in eastern <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> that separates the <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Peninsula" title="Labrador Peninsula">Labrador Peninsula</a> from the island of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>, in the province of <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Newfoundland and Labrador</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Belle_Isle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The strait is the northern outlet for the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Saint_Lawrence" title="Gulf of Saint Lawrence">Gulf of Saint Lawrence</a>, the other two being the <a href="/wiki/Cabot_Strait" title="Cabot Strait">Cabot Strait</a> and <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Canso" title="Strait of Canso">Strait of Canso</a>. As such, it is also considered part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_Seaway" title="St. Lawrence Seaway">Great Lakes-St. Lawrence Seaway</a> system. The strait is approximately 125 kilometres long and ranges from a maximum width of 60 km to just 15 km at it narrowest, the average width being 18 km.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins_of_name">Origins of name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Belle_Isle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins of name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sobi.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="982" data-file-width="1600" height="135" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Sobi.jpg/220px-Sobi.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Sobi.jpg/330px-Sobi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Sobi.jpg/440px-Sobi.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sobi.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Looking across the Strait of Belle Isle in moonlight from Green Island Brook March 24, 2005. From left to right Point Amour light, L'Anse-au-Loup and Capstan Island.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The name is derived from <a href="/wiki/Belle_Isle_(Newfoundland_and_Labrador)" title="Belle Isle (Newfoundland and Labrador)">Belle Isle</a> (Beautiful Island), located at the extreme eastern end of the strait and roughly equidistant between <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Table_Head,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Table Head, Newfoundland and Labrador (page does not exist)">Table Head</a>, <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cape_Bauld" title="Cape Bauld">Cape Bauld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Navigation_and_transport">Navigation and transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Strait_of_Belle_Isle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Navigation and transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Navigation in the strait can be extremely hazardous with strong tidal currents interacting with the <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Current" title="Labrador Current">Labrador Current</a>, depths reaching several hundred metres in places, <a href="/wiki/Sea_ice" title="Sea ice">sea ice</a> for 8–10 months of the year, and variable weather conditions including gales and fog. While sea ice prevents year-round shipping, the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Coast_Guard" title="Canadian Coast Guard">Canadian Coast Guard</a> maintains a <a href="/wiki/Vessel_traffic_service" title="Vessel traffic service">vessel traffic service</a> (VTS) to ensure collisions do not occur. The VTS is voluntary currently.</p>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferry</a> service operates at the western part of strait between <a href="/wiki/St._Barbe,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. Barbe, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. Barbe</a>, Newfoundland, and <a href="/wiki/Blanc-Sablon,_Quebec" title="Blanc-Sablon, Quebec">Blanc Sablon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> New road construction for the <a href="/wiki/Trans-Labrador_Highway" title="Trans-Labrador Highway">Trans-Labrador Highway</a> resulted in the removal of ferry services to <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_outport" title="Newfoundland outport">outports</a> in the northeastern part of the strait in 2002.</p>
<p>The idea of building a <a href="/wiki/Fixed_link" title="Fixed link">fixed link</a> across the strait between Labrador and Newfoundland, known as the <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland-Labrador_fixed_link" title="Newfoundland-Labrador fixed link">Newfoundland-Labrador fixed link</a>, has been raised<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (November 2014)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup> numerous times in recent decades<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (November 2014)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> following an unsuccessful attempt to build a tunnel carrying electrical wires in the mid-1970s. If such a link were built, it would likely be a 17-km-long submerged <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">rail</a> tunnel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-expensivehole_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-expensivehole-2">[2]</a></sup> The proposal is meant to reduce the province's reliance upon the <a href="/wiki/Marine_Atlantic" title="Marine Atlantic">Marine Atlantic</a> ferry service to <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, but the project's high costs and lack of suitable road network between Labrador and Quebec have been cited as major obstacles. The October 2003 provincial election resulted in the newly elected <a href="/wiki/Progressive_Conservative_Party_of_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Progressive Conservative Party of Newfoundland and Labrador">Progressive Conservative</a> government announcing joint federal-provincial funding for a study of the concept.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-expensivehole_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-expensivehole-2">[2]</a></sup> A 35 km submarine cable across the strait is under construction as part of the <a href="/wiki/Lower_Churchill_Project" title="Lower Churchill Project">Lower Churchill Project</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
