<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ring (diacritic)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/w/index.php?title=Modifier_letter_ring_above&amp;redirect=no" title="Modifier letter ring above">Modifier letter ring above</a>)</span></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">The ring diacritic (˚) redirects here. It is not to be confused with the <a href="/wiki/Superior_letter" title="Superior letter">superscript</a> lower-case o (<sup>o</sup>), the <a href="/wiki/Ordinal_indicator" title="Ordinal indicator">ordinal indicator</a> (º), or the <a href="/wiki/Degree_symbol" title="Degree symbol">degree symbol</a> (°).</div>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">For other uses, see <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Ring_(disambiguation)" title="Ring (disambiguation)">Ring (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p>A <b>ring</b> <a href="/wiki/Diacritic" title="Diacritic">diacritic</a> may appear above or below letters. It may be combined with some <a href="/wiki/Letter_(alphabet)" title="Letter (alphabet)">letters</a> of the extended <a href="/wiki/Latin_alphabet" title="Latin alphabet">Latin alphabets</a> in various contexts.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ring_above">Ring above</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ring_(diacritic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ring above">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Unicode, the above encoding is: <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+030A</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌̊</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">COMBINING RING ABOVE</span>.</p>
<p>Though the <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walloon_language" title="Walloon language">Walloon</a> character <a href="/wiki/%C3%85" title="Å">Å</a> (å) is derived from an <a href="/wiki/A" title="A">A</a> with a ring, it is considered a distinct <a href="/wiki/Letter_(alphabet)" title="Letter (alphabet)">letter</a> in those languages. The letter Å is the symbol of the unit <a href="/wiki/%C3%85ngstr%C3%B6m" title="Ångström">ångström</a>, named after the Swedish physicist <a href="/wiki/Anders_Jonas_%C3%85ngstr%C3%B6m" title="Anders Jonas Ångström">Anders Jonas Ångström</a>.</p>
<p>The character Ů (ů; a Latin <a href="/wiki/U" title="U">U</a> with ring above, or <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/krou%C5%BEek" title="wikt:kroužek">kroužek</a> in Czech) is a <a href="/wiki/Grapheme" title="Grapheme">grapheme</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech language</a> preserved for historic reasons, which identifies a <a href="/wiki/Vowel_shift" title="Vowel shift">vowel shift</a>. For example, the word for "horse" used to be written <i>kóň</i>, which evolved, along with pronunciation, into <i>kuoň</i>. Ultimately, the vowel <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[o]</span> disappeared completely, and the <i>uo</i> evolved into <i>ů</i>, modern form <i>kůň</i>. The letter <i>ů</i> now has the same pronunciation as the letter <i><a href="/wiki/%C3%9A" title="Ú">ú</a></i> (long <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[uː]</span>), but changes to a short <i>o</i> when a word is morphed (e.g. nom. <i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/k%C5%AF%C5%88" title="wikt:kůň">kůň</a></i> → gen. <i>koně</i>, nom. <i>dům</i> → gen. <i>domu</i>), thus showing the historical evolution of the language. <i>Ů</i> cannot occur in initial position, however, <i>ú</i> occurs almost exclusively in initial position or at the beginning of a word <a href="/wiki/Root_(linguistics)" title="Root (linguistics)">root</a> in a compound. These characters are used also in <a href="/wiki/Silesian_language" title="Silesian language">Steuer's Silesian alphabet</a>. The <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[uo]</span> pronunciation has prevailed in some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moravian_language" title="Moravian language">Moravian</a> dialects, as well as in the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak language</a>, which uses the letter <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%94" title="Ô">ô</a> instead of ů.</p>
<p>The ring is used in some dialects of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emiliano-Romagnolo_language" title="Emiliano-Romagnolo language">Emilian-Romagnol</a> to distinguish the sound /ʌ/ (å) from <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/a/</span> (a).</p>
<p>ů has been used in Old <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian</a> in Lithuania Minor from the 16th till the beginning of the 20th century and for a shorter time in 16th-century Lithuania Major for diphthong <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[uo]</span>.</p>
<p>The ring has been used in the Lithuanian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cyrillic" title="Cyrillic">Cyrillic</a> alphabet promoted by Russian authorities in the last quarter of the 19th century with the letter У̊ / у̊ used to represent the <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/wɔ/</span> diphthong (now written <i>uo</i> in Lithuanian orthography).</p>
<p>Ring upon e (e̊) is used by certain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dialectologist" title="Dialectologist">dialectologists</a> of the Walloon language (especially <a class="extiw" href="https://wa.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Jacques_Gaziaux" title="wa:Jean-Jacques Gaziaux">Jean-Jacques Gaziaux</a>) to note the <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/ə/</span> vowel typically replacing <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/i/</span> and <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/y/</span> in the <a href="/wiki/Walloon_Brabant" title="Walloon Brabant">Brabant province</a> central Walloon dialects. The difficulty of type-writing it has led some writers to prefer <i>ë</i> for the same sound.</p>
<p>Many more characters can be created in <a href="/wiki/Unicode" title="Unicode">Unicode</a> using the <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+030A</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌̊</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">COMBINING RING ABOVE</span>, including the above-mentioned у̊ (Cyrillic у with ring above) or ń̊ (n with acute and ring above). The standalone (spacing) symbol is <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+02DA</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">˚</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">RING ABOVE</span></p>
<p>Although similar in appearance, it is not to be confused with the Japanese <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Handakuten" title="Handakuten">handakuten</a></i> (<span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+309C</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">゜</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">KATAKANA-HIRAGANA SEMI-VOICED SOUND MARK</span>), a diacritic used with the <i>kana</i> for syllables starting with <i>h</i> to indicate that they should instead be pronounced with <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[p]</span>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ring_below">Ring below</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ring_(diacritic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ring below">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Unicode" title="Unicode">Unicode</a> encodes the ring below at <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+0325</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌̥</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">COMBINING RING BELOW</span></p>
<p>The diacritic is used in <a href="/wiki/International_Phonetic_Alphabet" title="International Phonetic Alphabet">IPA</a> to indicate <a href="/wiki/Voice_(phonetics)" title="Voice (phonetics)">voicelessness</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Indo-European_studies" title="Indo-European studies">Indo-European studies</a> or in <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a> transliteration (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IAST" title="IAST">IAST</a>) to indicate <a href="/wiki/Syllabic_consonant" title="Syllabic consonant">syllabicity</a> of r, l, m, n etc. (e.g. <span class="Unicode" lang="ine" style="white-space: nowrap" title="Proto-Indo-European language" xml:lang="ine">r̥</span> corresponding to IPA <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">[ɹ̩]</span>).</p>
<p>Examples:</p>
<ul>
<li><span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+1E00</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">Ḁ</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">LATIN CAPITAL LETTER A WITH RING BELOW</span></li>
<li><span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+1E01</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">ḁ</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">LATIN SMALL LETTER A WITH RING BELOW</span></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Emilian-Romagnol">Emilian-Romagnol</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ring_(diacritic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Emilian-Romagnol">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Romagnol_dialect" title="Romagnol dialect">Romagnol</a>, <i>e̥</i> is used to represent /ə/ in diphthongs, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Santarcangelo_di_Romagna" title="Santarcangelo di Romagna">Santarcangelo</a> dialect <i>ame̥ig, ne̥ud</i> [aˈməig, ˈnəud] "friend, naked". In <a href="/wiki/Emilian_dialect" title="Emilian dialect">Emilian</a>, <i>e̥</i> can be used to represent unstressed /ə/ in very accurate transcriptions.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Half_rings">Half rings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ring_(diacritic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Half rings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Half rings also exist as diacritic marks, these are characters <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+0351</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌͑</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">COMBINING LEFT HALF RING ABOVE</span> and <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+0357</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌͗</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">COMBINING RIGHT HALF RING ABOVE</span>. These characters may be used in the <a href="/wiki/International_Phonetic_Alphabet" title="International Phonetic Alphabet">International Phonetic Alphabet</a>, denoting <a href="/wiki/Roundedness" title="Roundedness">roundedness</a>. They are here given with the lowercase a: a͑ and a͗.</p>
<p><span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+1E9A</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">ẚ</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">LATIN SMALL LETTER A WITH RIGHT HALF RING</span> is similar in appearance but differs from a͗ because its <a href="/wiki/Unicode_equivalence" title="Unicode equivalence">compatibility decomposition</a> uses <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+02BE</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">ʾ</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">MODIFIER LETTER RIGHT HALF RING</span> instead of <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+0357</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌͗</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">COMBINING RIGHT HALF RING ABOVE</span>.</p>
<p>Other, similar signs are in use in <a href="/wiki/Armenian_alphabet" title="Armenian alphabet">Armenian</a>: the <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+0559</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌ՙ</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">ARMENIAN MODIFIER LETTER LEFT HALF RING</span> and the <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+055A</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌՚</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">ARMENIAN APOSTROPHE</span>.</p>
<p>Unicode also supports <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+031C</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌̜</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">COMBINING LEFT HALF RING BELOW</span> and <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+0339</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌̹</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">COMBINING RIGHT HALF RING BELOW</span>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_uses">Other uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ring_(diacritic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ring is used in the transliteration of the <a href="/wiki/Abkhaz_language" title="Abkhaz language">Abkhaz language</a> to represent the letter ҩ. It may also be used in place of the abbreviation symbol <i>॰</i> when transliterating the <a href="/wiki/Devanagari" title="Devanagari">Devanagari</a> alphabet.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Similar_marks">Similar marks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ring_(diacritic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Similar marks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ring as a diacritic mark should not be confused with the <a href="/wiki/Dot_(diacritic)" title="Dot (diacritic)">dot</a> or <span class="nowrap"><span style="font-family: monospace, monospace;">U+0366</span></span> <span style="font-size:125%">◌ͦ</span> <span class="smallcaps" style="font-variant: small-caps; font-size: smaller;">COMBINING LATIN SMALL LETTER O</span> diacritic marks, or with the degree sign °.</p>
<p>The half ring as a diacritic mark should not be confused with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Comma_(diacritic)" title="Comma (diacritic)">comma</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ogonek" title="Ogonek">ogonek</a> diacritic marks.</p>
