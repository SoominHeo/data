<p>Year <b>585</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DLXXXV</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 585 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=585&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=585&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=585&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Childebert_II" title="Childebert II">Childebert II</a>, age 15, takes up his sole rule of <a href="/wiki/Austrasia" title="Austrasia">Austrasia</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Frankish</a> army under King <a href="/wiki/Guntram" title="Guntram">Guntram</a> marches to <a href="/wiki/Comminges" title="Comminges">Comminges</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pyrenees" title="Pyrenees">Pyrenees</a>), and besieges the <a href="/wiki/Citadel" title="Citadel">citadel</a> of <a href="/wiki/Saint-Bertrand-de-Comminges" title="Saint-Bertrand-de-Comminges">Saint-Bertrand</a>.</li>
<li>July – <a href="/wiki/Gundoald" title="Gundoald">Gundoald</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merovingian_Dynasty" title="Merovingian Dynasty">Merovingian</a> usurper king, and his followers are defeated during the <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a> of Saint-Bertrand. He is executed and Guntram stages a triumphal entry in <a href="/wiki/Orl%C3%A9ans" title="Orléans">Orléans</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a> under King <a href="/wiki/Liuvigild" title="Liuvigild">Liuvigild</a> devastate the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suebic_Kingdom_of_Galicia" title="Suebic Kingdom of Galicia">Suevic Kingdom</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gallaecia" title="Gallaecia">Gallaecia</a> (northwest <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>). After the conquest, Liuvigild reintroduces the <a href="/wiki/Arianism" title="Arianism">Arian Church</a> among the <a href="/wiki/Suebi" title="Suebi">Sueves</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>Winter – <a href="/wiki/Famine" title="Famine">Famine</a> strikes <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a> (according to <a href="/wiki/Gregory_of_Tours" title="Gregory of Tours">Gregory of Tours</a>). <a href="/wiki/Merchant" title="Merchant">Traders</a> plunder the people by selling scarcely a peck of <a href="/wiki/Cereal" title="Cereal">grain</a> or half measure of <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">wine</a> for the third of a <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> piece.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Persia">Persia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=585&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Persia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sassanid_Empire" title="Sassanid Empire">Persian</a> commander, <a href="/wiki/Kardarigan_(6th_century)" title="Kardarigan (6th century)">Kardarigan</a> ("black hawk"), begins an unsuccessful <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a> of <a href="/wiki/K%C4%B1z%C4%B1ltepe" title="Kızıltepe">Monokarton</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=585&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hussa_of_Bernicia" title="Hussa of Bernicia">Hussa</a> succeeds his brother <a href="/wiki/Frithuwald_of_Bernicia" title="Frithuwald of Bernicia">Frithuwald</a> as king of <a href="/wiki/Bernicia" title="Bernicia">Bernicia</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Creoda_of_Mercia" title="Creoda of Mercia">Creoda</a> becomes king of <a href="/wiki/Mercia" title="Mercia">Mercia</a> (according to the <i><a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon_Chronicle" title="Anglo-Saxon Chronicle">Anglo-Saxon Chronicle</a></i>).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=585&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_15" title="September 15">September 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Bidatsu" title="Emperor Bidatsu">Emperor Bidatsu</a>, age 47, dies of <a href="/wiki/Smallpox" title="Smallpox">smallpox</a> after a 13-year reign, and is succeeded by his brother <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Y%C5%8Dmei" title="Emperor Yōmei">Yōmei</a> as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Emperors_of_Japan" title="List of Emperors of Japan">31st emperor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</li>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Jing_of_Western_Liang" title="Emperor Jing of Western Liang">Xiao Jing Di</a> succeeds his father <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ming_of_Western_Liang" title="Emperor Ming of Western Liang">Xiao Ming Di</a> as ruler of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liang_Dynasty" title="Liang Dynasty">Liang Dynasty</a> (<a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=585&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=585&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Wideok_of_Baekje" title="Wideok of Baekje">Wideok</a> of <a href="/wiki/Baekje" title="Baekje">Baekje</a> (<a href="/wiki/Three_Kingdoms_of_Korea" title="Three Kingdoms of Korea">Korea</a>) sends an official escort (bearing <a href="/wiki/Tribute" title="Tribute">tribute</a>), along with a master of <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhist meditation</a>, a reciter of Buddhist <a href="/wiki/Spell_(paranormal)" title="Spell (paranormal)">magic spells</a>, a temple architect, and a <a href="/wiki/Sculpture" title="Sculpture">sculptor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Buddhist_art" title="Buddhist art">Buddhist images</a>, to the Chinese court of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sui_Dynasty" title="Sui Dynasty">Sui Dynasty</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenian</a> bishop Kardutsat goes with 7 <a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">priests</a>, on a missionary trip to the <a href="/wiki/Steppe" title="Steppe">steppes</a> north of the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a>. He succeeds in <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptizing</a> many <a href="/wiki/Huns" title="Huns">Huns</a> and in translating books into their language.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Columbanus" title="Columbanus">Columbanus</a>, Irish <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionary</a>, gathers 12 companions for his journey to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a>, probably to the <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> coast. After a short time, he crosses the <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a> and lands in <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a> (<a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Zhiyi" title="Zhiyi">Zhiyi</a>, Chinese <a href="/wiki/Bhikkhu" title="Bhikkhu">monk</a>, returns to the city of <a href="/wiki/Nanjing" title="Nanjing">Jinling</a>, where he completes his commentarial works on the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Sutra" title="Lotus Sutra">Lotus Sutra</a>.</li>
</ul>
