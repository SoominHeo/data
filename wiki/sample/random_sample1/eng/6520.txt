<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Dark-sky movement</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>dark-sky movement</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Social_movement" title="Social movement">campaign</a> to reduce <a href="/wiki/Light_pollution" title="Light pollution">light pollution</a>. The advantages of reducing light pollution include an increased number of stars visible at night, reducing the effects of electric lighting on the environment, and cutting down on energy usage. <a href="/wiki/Earth_Hour" title="Earth Hour">Earth Hour</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_Dark-Sky_Week" title="National Dark-Sky Week">National Dark-Sky Week</a> are two examples of such efforts.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Social_movement" title="Social movement">movement</a> started with professional and amateur <a href="/wiki/Astronomer" title="Astronomer">astronomers</a> alarmed that nocturnal <a href="/wiki/Skyglow" title="Skyglow">skyglow</a> from urban areas was blotting out the sight of stars. For example, the world-famous <a href="/wiki/Palomar_Observatory" title="Palomar Observatory">Palomar Observatory</a> in California is threatened by sky-glow from the nearby city of <a href="/wiki/Escondido,_California" title="Escondido, California">Escondido</a> and local businesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> For similar reasons, astronomers in Arizona helped push the governor there to veto a bill in 2012 which would have lifted a ban on illuminated billboards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nocturnal" title="Nocturnal">Nocturnal</a> animals can be harmed by light pollution because they are biologically evolved to be dependent on an environment with a certain amount of hours of uninterrupted daytime and nighttime. The <a href="/wiki/Over-illumination" title="Over-illumination">over-illumination</a> of the night sky is affecting these organisms (especially birds). This biological study of darkness is called <a href="/wiki/Scotobiology" title="Scotobiology">scotobiology</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Light pollution has also been found to affect human circadian rhythms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The dark-sky movement encourages the use of <a href="/wiki/Light_pollution#Improving_lighting_fixtures" title="Light pollution">full-cutoff fixtures</a> that cast little or no light upward in public areas and generally to encourage communities to adopt lighting regulations. A 2011 project is to establish "dark sky oasis" in suburban areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Skyglow">Skyglow</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dark-sky_movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Skyglow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Skyglow" title="Skyglow">Skyglow</a> is the illumination of the <a href="/wiki/Night_sky" title="Night sky">night sky</a> or parts of it, resembling an orange "<a href="/wiki/Smog" title="Smog">smog</a>". It occurs from both natural and human-made sources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cat_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-cat-6">[6]</a></sup> Artificial skyglow is caused by the <a href="/wiki/Over-illumination" title="Over-illumination">over-illumination</a> of the sky from large city centres, shopping centres, or stadiums. It consists of light that is either emitted directly upward or reflected from the ground that is then scattered by dust and gas molecules in the atmosphere, producing a luminous background or light dome. These artificial skyglows cause the sky to be 5–10 times brighter in urban areas than a naturally dark sky that is unaffected by artificial light. Natural skyglow can come from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_lighting" title="Natural lighting">natural light</a> sources, such as the <a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">Sun</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Moonlight" title="Moonlight">Moon</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Starlight" title="Starlight">stars</a>, or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aurora_(astronomy)" title="Aurora (astronomy)">auroras</a>.</p>
<p>Some communities are becoming aware of this problem and are putting forth efforts to minimize the hazy, orange skyglow. A community in particular is the city of <a href="/wiki/Merritt,_British_Columbia" title="Merritt, British Columbia">Merritt, British Columbia</a>. An article published July 8, 2010 states that they are making minor changes to lighting in and around Merritt, such as the installment of down-cast lighting to commercial buildings, as part of their light pollution abatement program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-merrittnews.net_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-merrittnews.net-7">[7]</a></sup> The benefits of this technological change include "saving energy through better focused lights, preserving the environment by reducing excess light that may affect flora and fauna, reducing crime and increasing safety by more adequately illuminating areas, and reducing health risks."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-merrittnews.net_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-merrittnews.net-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scotobiology">Scotobiology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dark-sky_movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Scotobiology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Scotobiology" title="Scotobiology">Scotobiology</a> is the study of the role darkness plays in living organisms and shows that interrupting darkness by light pollution creates drastic effects for most organisms; changing their food gathering and feeding habits, their mating and reproduction behavior, migration behaviour (birds and insects) and social behavior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sampaa.org_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-sampaa.org-8">[8]</a></sup> Approximately 30% of vertebrates and 60% of invertebrates are nocturnal, meaning that they depend on darkness. Their everyday behaviors are biologically evolved to adapt in uninterrupted darkness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Human health is also adversely affected by the effects of light pollution. Light during night time hours has been linked to human cancers and psychological disorders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sampaa.org_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-sampaa.org-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dark-sky_preserves">Dark-sky preserves</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dark-sky_movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dark-sky preserves">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dark-sky_preserve" title="Dark-sky preserve">Dark-sky preserves</a> are the main contributors to the dark-sky movement. They are protected areas found mostly in national parks that have a zero light pollution policy set in by the government and controlled by the National Dark Sky Association.</p>
<p>As of February 6, 2012, there were 35 formally recognized dark-sky preserves in the world with Canada in the lead containing 15 preserves. These preserves are located in Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Quebec, Ontario, Saskatchewan, Alberta, and British Columbia. Other countries that have dark-sky preserves are the Czech Republic, Hungary, Poland, Slovakia, Spain, United Kingdom, and the United States. A current list of designated parks is maintained by the Dark Skies Advisory Group <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> of the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN).</p>
<p>The parks are put in place by the Dark Sky Places program with the intention to remind us that the night sky serves just as much importance to our culture and history as our day-time sky.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_Dark-Sky_Association">International Dark-Sky Association</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dark-sky_movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International Dark-Sky Association">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The International Dark-Sky Association (IDA) began in 1988. A non-profit, it manages the Fixture Seal of Approval program, which offers a third-party rating system judging the "sky-friendliness" of lighting fixtures. In 2009, the IDA opened an office for public policy and government affairs in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C" title="Washington, D.C">Washington, D.C</a> to inform lawmakers and lobbyists about the energy efficiency of outdoor lighting and to promote the adoption of energy-saving measures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The IDA has implemented several <i>simple guidelines</i> to responsible outdoor lighting along with some <i>practical considerations</i>.</p>
<ul>
<li>In regard to safety, one needs only the right amount of light, in the right place, at the right time. More light often means wasted light and energy.</li>
<li>Use the lowest wattage of lamp that is feasible. The maximum wattage for most commercial applications should be 250 watts of high intensity discharge lighting, but less is usually sufficient.</li>
<li>Incorporate curfews (i.e., turn lights off automatically after a certain hour when businesses close or traffic is minimal).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
