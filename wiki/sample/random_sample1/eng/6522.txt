<p>The <b>Byeongsan Seowon</b> is a <i><a href="/wiki/Seowon" title="Seowon">seowon</a></i> located in the neighborhood of Byeongsan-dong, Yecheon-myeon in the city of <a href="/wiki/Andong" title="Andong">Andong</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Gyeongsang_Province" title="North Gyeongsang Province">North Gyeongsang Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. Seowon is a type of local <a href="/wiki/Academy" title="Academy">academy</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_Dynasty" title="Joseon Dynasty">Joseon Dynasty</a> (1392–1897). It was first established as Jondeosa (尊德祠) by local <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Confucian" title="Confucian">Confucian</a> scholars especially <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jeong_Gyeong-se&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jeong Gyeong-se (page does not exist)">Jeong Gyeong-se</a> (鄭經世) in 1613, the fifth year of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gwanghaegun_of_Joseon" title="Gwanghaegun of Joseon">King Gwanghaegung</a>'s reign, to commemorate the scholarly achievement and virtue of the notable Confucian scholar and politician <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yu_Seong-ryong" title="Yu Seong-ryong">Yu Seong-ryong</a>. The predecessor of the seowon was Pungak Seodang (淵岳書堂) which was an educational institution located in <a href="/wiki/Pungsan-eup" title="Pungsan-eup">Pungsan</a> to teach the Pungsan Yu clan during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goryeo_period" title="Goryeo period">Goryeo period</a>. Yu Seong-ryong moved the <a href="/wiki/Seodang" title="Seodang">seodang</a> to the current place in 1572.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
