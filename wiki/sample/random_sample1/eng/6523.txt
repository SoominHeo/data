<p><b>On-street running</b> or <b>street running</b> is the routing of a <a href="/wiki/Track_(rail_transport)" title="Track (rail transport)">railroad track</a> or <a href="/wiki/Tramway_track" title="Tramway track">tramway track</a> running directly along public streets, without any separation. The rails are embedded in the roadway pavement, and the train shares the street directly with pedestrians and automobile traffic. Trains generally travel at reduced speed for safety reasons.</p>
<p>If there are stations on the section, they can appear similar in style to a <a href="/wiki/Tram_stop" title="Tram stop">tram stop</a>, but often lack platforms, pedestrian islands, or other amenities. Passengers may be required to wait on a distant sidewalk, and then to board or disembark directly among mixed traffic in mid-pavement, rather than at curbside.</p>
<p>Although bridges and tunnels are not streets, rails can still be embedded in the surface of bridges and tunnels like the <a href="/wiki/Inuyama_Bridge" title="Inuyama Bridge">Inuyama Bridge</a> (Japan) or the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anton_Anderson_Memorial_Tunnel" title="Anton Anderson Memorial Tunnel">Anton Anderson Memorial Tunnel</a> (US). This is also considered to be "street running".</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Street_running&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

Aside from those and <a href="/wiki/Industrial_spur" title="Industrial spur">industrial spurs</a> (<span lang="de" xml:lang="de">Anschlussbahnen</span>), there are also some street-running railways:</p>

<div class="thumbcaption">

"<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mollibahn" title="Mollibahn">Mollibahn</a>" running through <a href="/wiki/Bad_Doberan" title="Bad Doberan">Bad Doberan</a>, Germany</div>
</div>
</div>
In the north east of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, the steam "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mollibahn" title="Mollibahn">Mollibahn</a>" narrow gauge railway travels on-street through the town of <a href="/wiki/Bad_Doberan" title="Bad Doberan">Bad Doberan</a> at the start of its journey.</li>
<li>Near Mannheim, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oberrheinische_Eisenbahn" title="Oberrheinische Eisenbahn">Oberrheinische Eisenbahn</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rhein-Haardt-Bahn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rhein-Haardt-Bahn (page does not exist)">Rhein-Haardt-Bahn</a> are street running through several villages.</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Street_running&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>One of the most famous locations is when the steam-powered <a href="/wiki/Darjeeling_Himalayan_Railway" title="Darjeeling Himalayan Railway">Darjeeling Himalayan Railway</a> "Toy train" squeezes between narrow shop fronts down past a bazaar in India.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Indonesia">Indonesia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Street_running&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Indonesia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Indonesia used to have an extensive "steam tramways" (more accurately defined as local railways) network, which had many street running sections in various towns and cities in Java and Sumatra.</p>
<p>Two sections remain in use in 2010: part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wonogiri" title="Wonogiri">Wonogiri</a> branch runs along the Slamet Riyadi street in <a href="/wiki/Surakarta" title="Surakarta">Surakarta</a>, and a short branch to an oil depot in <a href="/wiki/Madiun" title="Madiun">Madiun</a>. The earlier line sees both passenger and freight service (including a steam-hauled tourist train), while the other line is exclusively for freight.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>In Aguas Calientes, the town at the foot of <a href="/wiki/Machu_Picchu" title="Machu Picchu">Machu Picchu</a>, the railway shares the streets with pedestrians, as well as in other towns further up the line. This railway serves as the only viable way of reaching Machu Picchu from <a href="/wiki/Cusco" title="Cusco">Cusco</a> without walking.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Serbia">Serbia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Street_running&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Serbia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1999, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=%C5%BDe%C5%BEelj_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Žeželj Bridge (page does not exist)">Žeželj Bridge</a>, a railway and road bridge in <a href="/wiki/Novi_Sad" title="Novi Sad">Novi Sad</a> (with separated traffic) was destroyed during <a href="/wiki/NATO_bombing_of_Yugoslavia" title="NATO bombing of Yugoslavia">NATO bombing of Yugoslavia</a>. As a temporary replacement, a street running <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Road-Railway_Bridge" title="Road-Railway Bridge">Road-Railway Bridge</a> was constructed in 2000. It still stands to this day, and is actively used.</p>

<p>Swiss law does not distinguish between trams and railways, making the distinction between street running by trams and that by railways subjective.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berninabahn" title="Berninabahn">Berninabahn</a> has three sections of street running, in <a href="/wiki/Le_Prese" title="Le Prese">Le Prese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miralago" title="Miralago">Miralago</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Tirano" title="Tirano">Tirano</a> (in Italy), where the approach to the station involves street running and crossing a public square.</p>
