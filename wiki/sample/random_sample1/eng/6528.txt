<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Vonones II</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Vonones II of Parthia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa" xml:lang="fa">ونن دوم</span>‎‎, <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el" xml:lang="el">Ονωνης</span>, flourished second half of 1st century BC – 51 AD<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Parthian_Empire" title="Parthian Empire">Parthian</a> prince who served as a King of <a href="/wiki/Atropatene" title="Atropatene">Media Atropatene</a> and briefly as King of the <a href="/wiki/Parthian_Empire" title="Parthian Empire">Parthian Empire</a>.</p>
<p>Vonones II was the second born son of an unnamed Arsacid Parthian Princess<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> who was a relation to the King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vonones_I_of_Parthia" title="Vonones I of Parthia">Vonones I of Parthia</a> and her husband, <a href="/wiki/Darius_II_of_Media_Atropatene" title="Darius II of Media Atropatene">the Median Atropatenian Prince, Darius</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> His known grandparents which were his paternal ones were the Monarchs <a href="/wiki/Artavasdes_I_of_Media_Atropatene" title="Artavasdes I of Media Atropatene">Artavasdes I of Media Atropatene</a> and his wife, <a href="/wiki/Athenais_of_Media_Atropatene" title="Athenais of Media Atropatene">Athenais</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Vonones II had an elder brother the Parthian King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artabanus_III_(of_Parthia)" title="Artabanus III (of Parthia)">Artabanus III</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Vonones II was the namesake of his maternal relative Vonones I, as he was born and raised in the Parthian Empire.</p>
<p>Prior to his Parthian Kingship from about 11 until 51, Vonones II served as a King of Media Atropatene.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Little is known on his reign as King of Media Atropatene.</p>
<p>After the death of his nephew Gotarzes II,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Vonones II was raised to the Parthian Kingship in 51. The Kingship of Vonones II was brief. After a few months of him being appointed as King of Parthia, Vonones II died and was succeeded by his son, <a href="/wiki/Vologases_I_of_Parthia" title="Vologases I of Parthia">Vologases I</a>. Tacitus described Vonones II as “he knew neither success nor failure which have deserved to be remembered to him. It was a short and inglorious reign”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>From a Greek concubine,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Vonones II had 5 sons who held the thrones of Parthia and Armenia who were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacorus_II_of_Parthia" title="Pacorus II of Parthia">Pacorus II</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vologases_I_of_Parthia" title="Vologases I of Parthia">Vologases I</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Osroes_I_of_Parthia" title="Osroes I of Parthia">Osroes I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tiridates_I_of_Armenia" title="Tiridates I of Armenia">Tiridates I</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mithridates_IV_of_Parthia" title="Mithridates IV of Parthia">Mithridates IV</a>. His sons were born and raised during his Kingship of Media Atropatene.</p>
