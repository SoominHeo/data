<p><b>Bonnie</b>, <b>Bonny</b> or <b>Bonne</b> is a given name commonly used for female. It comes from the <a href="/wiki/Scots_language" title="Scots language">Scots</a> word "bonnie" meaning "pretty, attractive", which was itself derived from Middle French <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/bonne#French" title="wikt:bonne">bonne</a> "good" as a way to describe a fair, good and beautiful girl. That is in turn derived from the Latin word "bonus" meaning good. The name can also be used as a pet form of <a href="/wiki/Bonita_(name)" title="Bonita (name)">Bonita</a>. Bonnie is often used as a nickname or a form of endearment towards a loved one.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural">Cultural</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bonnie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cultural">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bonnie is the main character in the Scottish folk song "<a href="/wiki/My_Bonnie_Lies_over_the_Ocean" title="My Bonnie Lies over the Ocean">My Bonnie Lies over the Ocean</a>". It was recorded by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Sheridan" title="Tony Sheridan">Tony Sheridan</a>, backed by <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">the Beatles</a> a year after their formation in 1961, peaking at #5 in a German chart. Legend suggests the record <i><a href="/wiki/My_Bonnie" title="My Bonnie">My Bonnie</a></i> played a role in their early recognition and later success.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bonnie_(orangutan)" title="Bonnie (orangutan)">Bonnie</a> the <a href="/wiki/Orangutan" title="Orangutan">orangutan</a> was noted for her musical abilities, namely whistling.</p>
