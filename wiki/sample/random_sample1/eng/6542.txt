<p><b>Voronya River</b> (Воронья in <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Kola_Peninsula" title="Kola Peninsula">Kola Peninsula</a> in <a href="/wiki/Murmansk_Oblast" title="Murmansk Oblast">Murmansk Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. It is 155 km in length. The area of its <a href="/wiki/Drainage_basin" title="Drainage basin">drainage basin</a> is 9,940 km². The Voronya River flows out of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Lovozero" title="Lake Lovozero">Lake Lovozero</a> and into the <a href="/wiki/Barents_Sea" title="Barents Sea">Barents Sea</a>.</p>
<p>There are two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroelectric" title="Hydroelectric">hydroelectric</a> power stations on the river, with a total capacity of 351 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt">MW</a> and an annual production of 1069 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GWh" title="GWh">GWh</a>.</p>
