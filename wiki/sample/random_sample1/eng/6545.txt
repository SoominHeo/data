<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borja,_Zaragoza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The municipality borders with <a href="/wiki/Ablitas" title="Ablitas">Ablitas</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>) <a href="/wiki/Ag%C3%B3n" title="AgÃ³n">AgÃ³n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ainz%C3%B3n" title="AinzÃ³n">AinzÃ³n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberite_de_San_Juan" title="Alberite de San Juan">Alberite de San Juan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albeta" title="Albeta">Albeta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ambel" title="Ambel">Ambel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulbuente" title="Bulbuente">Bulbuente</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Buste" title="El Buste">El Buste</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fr%C3%A9scano" title="FrÃ©scano">FrÃ©scano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magall%C3%B3n" title="MagallÃ³n">MagallÃ³n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malej%C3%A1n" title="MalejÃ¡n">MalejÃ¡n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mall%C3%A9n" title="MallÃ©n">MallÃ©n</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tabuenca" title="Tabuenca">Tabuenca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tarazona" title="Tarazona">Tarazona</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vera_de_Moncayo" title="Vera de Moncayo">Vera de Moncayo</a>. It is the administrative seat of the comarca of <a href="/wiki/Campo_de_Borja" title="Campo de Borja">Campo de Borja</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borja,_Zaragoza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town's origins date back to the 5th century BC, when a <a href="/wiki/Celtiberians" title="Celtiberians">Celtiberian</a> settlement, known as <b>Bursau</b> or <b>Bursao</b>, existed near the current ruins of the castle. After the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> conquest (1st century BC) also the slopes of the hill were populated, though the town started to expand significantly only after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslim_conquest_of_Spain" title="Muslim conquest of Spain">Muslim conquest</a> in the 8th century AD.</p>
<p>In the 12th century it was conquered by the Christians from the north, and in the 15th/16th centuries it was converted into a military fortress against the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castillan</a> invasions, but at the same time received much of its historical architectural heritage, with numerous churches and palaces. It received the title of "city" by King <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_V_of_Aragon" title="Alfonso V of Aragon">Alfonso V of Aragon</a> in 1438. During the reign of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs" title="Catholic Monarchs">Catholic Monarchs</a>, the Jews, forming an important part of the Borjan community, were expelled.</p>
<p>Borja lived through a period of recession and plagues in the 17th and 18th centuries. It recovered economically starting from the 19th century, when a railway connecting the city to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cortes_de_Navarra" title="Cortes de Navarra">Cortes de Navarra</a> was inaugurated. In the 20th century agriculture, traditionally the mainstay of Borja's economy, started to lose its importance, and, without a consistent industrial base, the city lost economical and political importance in the area: much of the population therefore emigrated to other more developed areas. The industrial sector is intended to be boosted by businesses being attracted to the ongoing development "PolÃ­gono Industrial Barbalanca", the Barbalanca Industrial Estate.</p>
