<p><b>Linden</b> is one of three English names for the tree genus <i><a href="/wiki/Tilia" title="Tilia">Tilia</a></i> (also known as <i>lime</i> and <i>basswood</i>). Several of the common names for species within the <i>Tilia</i> genus have Linden in their names. They include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_linden" title="American linden">American linden</a>, a common name for <i>Tilia americana</i></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Large-leaved_linden" title="Large-leaved linden">Large-leaved linden</a>, a common name for <i>Tilia platyphyllos</i></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Little-leaf_linden" title="Little-leaf linden">Little-leaf linden</a>, a common name for <i>Tilia cordata</i></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silver_linden" title="Silver linden">Silver linden</a>, a common name for <i>Tilia tomentosa</i></li>
</ul>
<p>Other trees include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Viburnum" title="Viburnum">Viburnum</a> linden, a common name for <i>Viburnum dilatatum</i></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Linden_Oak" title="Linden Oak">Linden Oak</a>, a white oak in Bethesda, Maryland, United States</li>
</ul>
