<p><b>Boman Irani</b> (born 2 December 1959) is an <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> film and theatre actor, voice artist and photographer. He is widely known for his comedic and villain roles in <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a> films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Don_(2006_Hindi_film)" title="Don (2006 Hindi film)">Don</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Munna_Bhai_MBBS" title="Munna Bhai MBBS">Munna Bhai MBBS</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/3_Idiots" title="3 Idiots">3 Idiots</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_New_Year_(2014_film)" title="Happy New Year (2014 film)">Happy New Year</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/PK_(film)" title="PK (film)">PK</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Dilwale_(2015_film)" title="Dilwale (2015 film)">Dilwale</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boman_Irani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Irani was born on 2 December 1959, in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>. He finished his schooling in <a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s_School,_Mumbai" title="St. Mary's School, Mumbai">St. Mary's School, Mumbai</a>. He is a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Mithibai_College" title="Mithibai College">Mithibai College</a> in Mumbai. After his polytechnic diploma course, he joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taj_Mahal_Palace_%26_Tower" title="Taj Mahal Palace &amp; Tower">Taj Mahal Palace &amp; Tower</a> and worked there as a waiter and room service staff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hindustantimes1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hindustantimes1-2">[2]</a></sup> Thereafter, he joined his mother to run their ancestral bakery shop between Novelty and Apsara Cinema in South Mumbai.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Irani turned to photography in 1987 and continued until he became an actor.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boman_Irani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Irani began his acting career in theatre and moved to films in 2000. Irani gained attention for his role in the 2003 comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Munna_Bhai_M.B.B.S." title="Munna Bhai M.B.B.S.">Munna Bhai M.B.B.S.</a></i>. He later appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Lage_Raho_Munna_Bhai" title="Lage Raho Munna Bhai">Lage Raho Munna Bhai</a></i> for which he received several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Indian_Film_Academy" title="International Indian Film Academy">IIFA</a> award nominations and in <i><a href="/wiki/3_Idiots" title="3 Idiots">3 Idiots</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Aamir_Khan" title="Aamir Khan">Aamir Khan</a> which earned him a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award" title="Filmfare Award">Filmfare Award</a> and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Star_Screen_Award" title="Star Screen Award">Star Screen Award</a> for Best Actor in a Villainous Role.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theatre_career">Theatre career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boman_Irani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Theatre career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Acting had been a passion to Irani during school and college days. He trained under acting coach Hansraj Siddhia from 1981 to 1983 and adopted professional acting methods. Irani preferred British theatre, and he soon developed different accents.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Irani's mentor was <a href="/wiki/Alyque_Padamsee" title="Alyque Padamsee">Alyque Padamsee</a> — the veteran theatre actor best known for his role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohammad_Ali_Jinnah" title="Mohammad Ali Jinnah">Mohammad Ali Jinnah</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Gandhi_(film)" title="Gandhi (film)">Gandhi</a></i>. He was introduced to Padamsee by <a href="/wiki/Shiamak_Davar" title="Shiamak Davar">Shiamak Davar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hindustantimes1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hindustantimes1-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Irani's earliest theatrical appearance was in <i>Roshni</i> where he played a cameo at the Regional Theatre in <a href="/wiki/Versova,_Mumbai" title="Versova, Mumbai">Versova</a>. He followed this up with serials like <i>Family Ties</i> and <i>Mahatma vs Gandhi</i>, playing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gandhi" title="Gandhi">Gandhi</a> after the role was turned down by <a href="/wiki/Darshan_Jariwala" title="Darshan Jariwala">Darshan Jariwala</a>. His most illustrious play till date has been <i>I am not Bajirao</i> which ran for 10 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hindustantimes1_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hindustantimes1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_career">Film career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boman_Irani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Irani started out in a number of advertisements like <a href="/wiki/Fanta" title="Fanta">Fanta</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEAT_Limited" title="CEAT Limited">Ceat</a> and Krackjack biscuits (as Mr. Jack of the Krack and Jack duo).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>His small but significant role in <i><a href="/wiki/Darna_Mana_Hai" title="Darna Mana Hai">Darna Mana Hai</a></i> earned him acclaim. The film didn't fare too well, but his was one of the most remembered segments of the film along with <a href="/wiki/Saif_Ali_Khan" title="Saif Ali Khan">Saif Ali Khan</a>. With <i><a href="/wiki/Munna_Bhai_M.B.B.S." title="Munna Bhai M.B.B.S.">Munna Bhai M.B.B.S.</a></i>, Irani became popular. His role as J. Asthana earned him a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award" title="Filmfare Award">Filmfare Award</a> nomination for <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Award_for_Best_Performance_in_a_Comic_Role" title="Filmfare Award for Best Performance in a Comic Role">Best Comedian</a>. His comic as well as minor antagonist type role in <i><a href="/wiki/3_Idiots" title="3 Idiots">3 Idiots</a></i> is also still remembered. Irani has appeared in many comedic (<i><a href="/wiki/Main_Hoon_Na" title="Main Hoon Na">Main Hoon Na</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Lage_Raho_Munna_Bhai" title="Lage Raho Munna Bhai">Lage Raho Munna Bhai</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dostana_(2008_film)" title="Dostana (2008 film)">Dostana</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Khosla_Ka_Ghosla" title="Khosla Ka Ghosla">Khosla Ka Ghosla</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Waqt:_The_Race_Against_Time" title="Waqt: The Race Against Time">Waqt: The Race Against Time</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Entry" title="No Entry">No Entry</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Housefull_(2010_film)" title="Housefull (2010 film)">Housefull</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ek_Main_Aur_Ekk_Tu" title="Ek Main Aur Ekk Tu">Ek Main Aur Ekk Tu</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Housefull_2" title="Housefull 2">Housefull 2</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cocktail_(2012_film)" title="Cocktail (2012 film)">Cocktail</a></i>), supporting (<i><a href="/wiki/Veer-Zaara" title="Veer-Zaara">Veer-Zaara</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Maine_Gandhi_Ko_Nahin_Mara" title="Maine Gandhi Ko Nahin Mara">Maine Gandhi Ko Nahin Mara</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Lakshya_(film)" title="Lakshya (film)">Lakshya</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Honeymoon_Travels_Pvt._Ltd." title="Honeymoon Travels Pvt. Ltd.">Honeymoon Travels Pvt. Ltd.</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sorry_Bhai!" title="Sorry Bhai!">Sorry Bhai!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Page_3_(film)" title="Page 3 (film)">Page 3</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/My_Wife%27s_Murder" title="My Wife's Murder">My Wife's Murder</a></i>), and negative roles (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don:_The_Chase_Begins_Again" title="Don: The Chase Begins Again">Don: The Chase Begins Again</a></i> <i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don_2:_The_King_Is_Back" title="Don 2: The King Is Back">Don 2: The King Is Back</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eklavya" title="Eklavya">Eklavya</a></i>). He acted in his first Telugu film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attharintiki_Daaredhi" title="Attharintiki Daaredhi">Attharintiki Daaredhi</a></i> as the grandfather of <a href="/wiki/Pawan_Kalyan" title="Pawan Kalyan">Pawan Kalyan</a>.</p>
<p>Irani hosted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IIFA_Awards" title="IIFA Awards">IIFA Awards</a> with actor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ritesh_Deshmukh" title="Ritesh Deshmukh">Ritesh Deshmukh</a> in <a href="/wiki/9th_IIFA_Awards" title="9th IIFA Awards">2008</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, <a href="/wiki/10th_IIFA_Awards" title="10th IIFA Awards">2009</a> <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>, <a href="/wiki/11th_IIFA_Awards" title="11th IIFA Awards">2010</a> <a href="/wiki/Colombo" title="Colombo">Colombo</a> and <a href="/wiki/12th_IIFA_Awards" title="12th IIFA Awards">2011</a> in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He has been on <i><a href="/wiki/Kaun_Banega_Crorepati" title="Kaun Banega Crorepati">Kaun Banega Crorepati</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Sanjay_Dutt" title="Sanjay Dutt">Sanjay Dutt</a>. He also hosted the quiz show <i><a href="/wiki/Bollywood_Ka_Boss" title="Bollywood Ka Boss">Bollywood Ka Boss</a></i>. Irani featured in OneTouch Glucose Meters TV commercials in 2015 with <a href="/wiki/Simone_Singh" title="Simone Singh">Simone Singh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boman_Irani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Boman Irani is an <a href="/wiki/Irani_(India)" title="Irani (India)">Irani</a> and follows the religion of <a href="/wiki/Zoroastrianism" title="Zoroastrianism">Zoroastrianism</a>. He speaks English, <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gujarati_language" title="Gujarati language">Gujarati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bengali_language" title="Bengali language">Bengali</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marathi_language" title="Marathi language">Marathi</a> fluently. Irani is a member of the Parsee Television Actors Community and is its secretary. He is married to Zenobia, with whom he has two sons, Danesh and <a href="/wiki/Kayoze_Irani" title="Kayoze Irani">Kayoze Irani</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
