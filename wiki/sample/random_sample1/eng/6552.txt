<p><b>Bobai County</b> is a county of <a href="/wiki/Guangxi" title="Guangxi">Guangxi</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. It is under the administration of <a href="/wiki/Yulin,_Guangxi" title="Yulin, Guangxi">Yulin</a> city.</p>
<p>Bobai was the home town of the linguist <a href="/wiki/Wang_Li_(linguist)" title="Wang Li (linguist)">Wang Li</a>, who described the <a href="/wiki/Bobai_dialect" title="Bobai dialect">Bobai dialect</a> with its unusually large number of <a href="/wiki/Tone_(linguistics)" title="Tone (linguistics)">tones</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobai_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The county is served by the Yulin–<a href="/wiki/Tieshangang_District" title="Tieshangang District">Tieshangang Railway</a>. The railway opened for freight service in May 2015. Passenger service to the new Bobai Station will start on April 1, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contestation_and_protests">Contestation and protests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobai_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Contestation and protests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 2007, Bobai was the site of large protests held by local people against China's one-child policy, <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and triggered the protest in <a href="/wiki/Yangmei,_Rong_County" title="Yangmei, Rong County">Yangmei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rong_County,_Guangxi" title="Rong County, Guangxi">Rongxian</a>.</p>
<p>The small office of a grassroots NGO for sex workers' rights was ransacked and its leader <a href="/wiki/Ye_Haiyan" title="Ye Haiyan">Ye Haiyan</a> was also attacked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
