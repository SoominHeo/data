<p><b>Bobbie Diane Eakes</b> (born July 25, 1961) is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actress" title="Actress">actress</a> and singer. She is known for her role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macy_Alexander_Sharpe" title="Macy Alexander Sharpe">Macy Alexander</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bold_and_the_Beautiful" title="The Bold and the Beautiful">The Bold and the Beautiful</a></i> (1989–2000, 2001, 2002–2003) and for her role as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Krystal_Carey_Chandler" title="Krystal Carey Chandler">Krystal Carey</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/All_My_Children" title="All My Children">All My Children</a></i> (2003–2011).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobbie_Eakes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobbie_Eakes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She is the youngest of five daughters in an Air Force family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Eakes was <a href="/wiki/Miss_Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Miss Georgia (U.S. state)">Miss Georgia</a> 1982, placing among the top 10 at <a href="/wiki/Miss_America_1983" title="Miss America 1983">Miss America 1983</a>. She studied at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Georgia" title="University of Georgia">University of Georgia</a>. In 1992 she married novelist and actor David Steen on July 4. She has no children. Her sisters are Susan, Sharon, Shelly, and Sandra.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobbie_Eakes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Eakes received bit roles on nighttime television series, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Cheers" title="Cheers">Cheers</a></i>, before becoming successful in the soap opera genre. In the mid-1980s, she was the lead singer of the dance-pop group <a href="/wiki/Big_Trouble_(band)" title="Big Trouble (band)">Big Trouble</a>. The group released one <a href="/wiki/Big_Trouble_(Big_Trouble_album)" title="Big Trouble (Big Trouble album)">self-titled</a> CD in 1987, which was produced by <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Moroder" title="Giorgio Moroder">Giorgio Moroder</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Their single "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_World_(Big_Trouble_song)" title="Crazy World (Big Trouble song)">Crazy World</a>" charted on the U.S. singles chart, reaching #71.</p>
<p>She first achieved fame in the role of <a href="/wiki/Macy_Alexander" title="Macy Alexander">Macy Alexander</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bold_and_the_Beautiful" title="The Bold and the Beautiful">The Bold and the Beautiful</a></i>, which she played from 1989 to 2000 (on contract), 2001 (guest appearance) and 2002 to 2003 (on contract). A very popular "good girl" kind of character, Macy battled with her inner demons (including an addiction to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alcoholic_beverage" title="Alcoholic beverage">alcohol</a>) before finally finding happiness with <a href="/wiki/Deacon_Sharpe" title="Deacon Sharpe">Deacon Sharpe</a> (played by <a href="/wiki/Sean_Kanan" title="Sean Kanan">Sean Kanan</a>. On the show, Macy was an accomplished singer in her own right (in tune with Bobbie Eakes' hobby).</p>
<p>Between stints as Macy, Eakes hosted shows on the country music television channel <a href="/wiki/Great_American_Country" title="Great American Country">Great American Country</a>. She was also a member of an all-girl band named BIG TROUBLE, where she was the lead singer. She was also featured on country music singer <a href="/wiki/Collin_Raye" title="Collin Raye">Collin Raye</a>'s 2000 single "Tired of Loving This Way", which peaked at #50 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> Hot Country Singles &amp; Tracks (now <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a>) charts.</p>
<p>On July 7, 2000, her character "died" in a fiery explosion when her car crashed into a gasoline truck when the writers of the show felt the character of Macy no longer contributed to the show's storyline. She was brought back to the show during the <i>B&amp;B</i> location shoot at <a href="/wiki/Portofino" title="Portofino">Portofino</a>, Italy in December 2002, and was then killed off after a chandelier fell on her during a concert in 2003. The decision was made to kill Macy off a second time after Eakes had decided to take the role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Krystal_Carey" title="Krystal Carey">Krystal Carey</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/All_My_Children" title="All My Children">All My Children</a></i>, as the complete opposite of Macy, playing the sexy and sassy character. The role of Krystal is loosely based on Eakes' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._South" title="U.S. South">Southern</a> upbringing.</p>
<p>Eakes also starred in productions of <i><a href="/wiki/Cinderella" title="Cinderella">Cinderella</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Letters_(play)" title="Love Letters (play)">Love Letters</a></i> opposite her former costar <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Trachta" title="Jeff Trachta">Jeff Trachta</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. The two are still friends offscreen. The CD they recorded together, "Bold and Beautiful Duets", went double-platinum in Europe.</p>
<p>With All My Children airing its final episode on ABC in September 2011, in June 2011 it was highly rumored that Bobbie Eakes would be returning to <i>The Bold and the Beautiful</i>. And also stars on as Katherine "Kitty" The Grove: The Series created by <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Chappell" title="Crystal Chappell">Crystal Chappell</a></p>
