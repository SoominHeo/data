<p><b>Bobigny</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[bo.bi.ɲi]</a></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France"><i>commune</i></a>, or town, in the northeastern suburbs of Paris, France. It is located 9.1 km (5.7 mi) from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kilometre_Zero#France" title="Kilometre Zero">center of Paris</a>. Bobigny is the <i><a href="/wiki/Prefectures_in_France" title="Prefectures in France">préfecture</a></i> (capital) of the <a href="/wiki/Seine-Saint-Denis" title="Seine-Saint-Denis">Seine-Saint-Denis</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">département</a></i>, as well as the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Arrondissement_of_Bobigny" title="Arrondissement of Bobigny">Arrondissement of Bobigny</a>. It is only the 12th-largest <i>commune</i> in Seine-Saint-Denis (1999 census).</p>
<p>Inhabitants are called <i>Balbyniens</i>.</p>
<p>Its name is derived from Roman-period <i>Balbiniacum</i>, "the place of Balbo or Balbinus or Balbinius"; or "of the dumb or silent man/men" (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaulish" title="Gaulish">Gaulish</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_Gaelic" title="Irish Gaelic">Irish Gaelic</a> <i>balbh</i> = "dumb, silent").</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobigny&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bobigny is served by two stations on <a href="/wiki/Paris_M%C3%A9tro_Line_5" title="Paris Métro Line 5">Paris Métro Line 5</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bobigny_-_Pantin_-_Raymond_Queneau_(Paris_M%C3%A9tro)" title="Bobigny - Pantin - Raymond Queneau (Paris Métro)">Bobigny – Pantin – Raymond Queneau</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bobigny_-_Pablo_Picasso_(Paris_M%C3%A9tro)" title="Bobigny - Pablo Picasso (Paris Métro)">Bobigny – Pablo Picasso</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobigny&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Valeo" title="Valeo">Valeo</a> has management branches (Valeo Transmissions group and Valeo Friction Materials group) here. It was also the manufacturing base by Meccano of French <i>Dinky Toys</i> from 1933 to 1970 when the factory was closed and later demolished. Production of Dinky Toys was transferred to the Meccano factory in Calais until 1972 when the last new model, a Renault 4 la poste, was produced.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobigny&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During World War II, approximately 20,000 Jews were shipped from a railway station in Bobigny. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobigny&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The commune has 14 public preschools (<i>écoles maternelles</i>), 15 public elementary schools, four public junior high schools, three public senior high schools/sixth-form colleges, and one private school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>Junior high schools: Collège Auguste Delaune, Collège Jean-Pierre Timbaud, Collège Pierre Sémard, and Collège République et SEGPA<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Secschools_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Secschools-2">[2]</a></sup></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heraldry">Heraldry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobigny&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Heraldry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="wikitable">
<tr>
<td style="text-align:center;padding:5px 8px;width:120px;">
<div class="center">
<div class="floatnone"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Blason_Bobigny_93.svg" title="Arms of Bobigny"><img alt="Arms of Bobigny" data-file-height="660" data-file-width="600" height="132" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Blason_Bobigny_93.svg/120px-Blason_Bobigny_93.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Blason_Bobigny_93.svg/180px-Blason_Bobigny_93.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Blason_Bobigny_93.svg/240px-Blason_Bobigny_93.svg.png 2x" width="120"/></a></div>
</div>
</td>
<td style="vertical-align:top;">The arms of Bobigny are blazoned :<br/>
<i>Or, a saltire gules, overall on an inescutcheon azure, a basket filled with fruit and flowers and topped with 7 ears of wheat argent.</i>
<p>The village of Bobigny was under Saint Andrew, hence the cross of Saint Andrew (<a href="/wiki/Saltire" title="Saltire">saltire</a>). The small shield in the middle evokes the agricultural nature of the commune, before the spread of built-up Paris surrounded it.</p>
