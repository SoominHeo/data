<p><i><b>Protect the Boss</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">보스를 지켜라</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Boseureul Jikyeora</span></i>) is a 2011 <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> workplace romantic comedy <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">television series</a> starring <a href="/wiki/Choi_Kang-hee_(actress)" title="Choi Kang-hee (actress)">Choi Kang-hee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ji_Sung" title="Ji Sung">Ji Sung</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Jaejoong" title="Kim Jaejoong">Kim Jaejoong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wang_Ji-hye" title="Wang Ji-hye">Wang Ji-hye</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Park_Yeong-gyu" title="Park Yeong-gyu">Park Yeong-gyu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It aired on <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Broadcasting_System" title="Seoul Broadcasting System">SBS</a> from August 3 to September 29, 2011 on Wednesdays and Thursdays at 21:55.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Among its early <a href="/wiki/Working_title" title="Working title">working titles</a> were <i>The Boss Has Changed</i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">보스가 달라졌어요</span>) and <i>The Last Secretary</i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">마지막 여비서</span>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Protect_the_Boss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cha Ji-heon (<a href="/wiki/Ji_Sung" title="Ji Sung">Ji Sung</a>) is an incredibly immature young man who is useless at his job as a director at DN Group, where the chairman is his father (<a href="/wiki/Park_Yeong-gyu" title="Park Yeong-gyu">Park Yeong-gyu</a>). Ji-heon has a longstanding rivalry in work and love with cousin Cha Mu-won (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kim_Jaejoong" title="Kim Jaejoong">Kim Jaejoong</a>), as Mu-won is a mature, hardworking, and seemingly perfect executive, and the two both have a romantic history with Seo Na-yoon (<a href="/wiki/Wang_Ji-hye" title="Wang Ji-hye">Wang Ji-hye</a>). Spunky and tough Noh Eun-seol (<a href="/wiki/Choi_Kang-hee_(actress)" title="Choi Kang-hee (actress)">Choi Kang-hee</a>) is struggling to find full-time work because of her juvenile delinquent record and poor academic background. After giving the hiring directors a piece of her mind during a job interview at DN Group, she is surprised to find herself hired by Mu-won (who was captivated by her interview) to be the secretary to Ji-heon. Determined not to be fired from her first professional job, Eun-seol works diligently at putting up with Ji-heon's childishness and keeping him in check. As their working relationship progresses, they earn each other's trust and friendship, and Eun-seol helps Ji-heon deal with his phobias and prove himself capable of becoming DN Group's successor. Things get more complicated when Ji-heon and Mu-won both fall for Eun-seol, who doesn't want to get mixed up with either of them... or does she?</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_broadcast">International broadcast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Protect_the_Boss&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International broadcast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The series was popular in Japan and aired on multiple channels, including: paid satellite channels KNTV,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> DATV, KBS Japan, BS Japan TV,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and major broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">TBS</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li>The series airs every weeknights at 7:15pm in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> via <a href="/wiki/Jeepney_TV" title="Jeepney TV">Jeepney TV</a> under its "Asian Express" block.</li>
<li>The series previously played in <a href="/wiki/8TV_(Malaysia)" title="8TV (Malaysia)">8TV (Malaysia)</a> in Malaysia.</li>
<li>It aired in Vietnam on <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City_Television" title="Ho Chi Minh City Television">HTV3</a> from January 15, 2014.</li>
<li>It aired in Thailand on PPTV beginning November 18, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
