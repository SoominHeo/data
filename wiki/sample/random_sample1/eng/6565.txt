<p><b>Boscoreale</b> <small>Italian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Italian" title="Help:IPA for Italian">[boskoreˈaːle]</a></span> is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> and town in the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_City_of_Naples" title="Metropolitan City of Naples">Metropolitan City of Naples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">Campania</a>, with a population of 27,457 in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-istat_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-istat-1">[1]</a></sup> Located in the <a href="/wiki/Vesuvius_National_Park" title="Vesuvius National Park">Parco Nazionale del Vesuvio</a>, under the slopes of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Vesuvius" title="Mount Vesuvius">Mount Vesuvius</a>, it is known for the fruit and vineyards of <a href="/wiki/Lacryma_Christi" title="Lacryma Christi">Lacryma Christi del Vesuvio</a>. There is also a fine Vesuvian lava stone production.</p>

<div class="hatnote" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Villa_Boscoreale" title="Villa Boscoreale">Villa Boscoreale</a></div>
<p>The neighborhood Monte Bursaccio, which was overcome by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vesuvius" title="Vesuvius">eruption of Vesuvius</a> in 79 CE that obliterated and preserved its better-known neighbors, <a href="/wiki/Pompeii" title="Pompeii">Pompeii</a> and <a href="/wiki/Herculaneum" title="Herculaneum">Herculaneum</a>, is famous for the frescoes of its <a href="/wiki/Roman_villa" title="Roman villa">aristocratic villas</a>, excavated before World War I. A hoard of Roman silver and coins that had been hurriedly stashed in a cistern for protection at the time of the eruption was also recovered in Boscoreale in 1895, and divided among several museums, including the <a href="/wiki/Louvre" title="Louvre">Louvre</a> and the <a href="/wiki/British_Museum" title="British Museum">British Museum</a>.</p>
<p>Boscoreale, about a kilometer north of <a href="/wiki/Pompeii" title="Pompeii">Pompeii</a> of which it was an expansive, more rural outlying suburb, was notable in antiquity for having numerous aristocratic country villas and was preserved as a hunting park - hence its name, meaning "Royal Grove" - by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Naples" title="Kingdom of Naples">kings of Naples</a>.</p>
<p>The villa of P. Fannius Synistor (see <a href="/wiki/Villa_Boscoreale" title="Villa Boscoreale">Villa Boscoreale</a>) was built and decorated shortly after mid-first century BC. The quality of its frescoes seems to have preserved them from changes in fashion, before the villa was entombed in the eruption.</p>
<p>The Antiquarium of Boscoreale was founded in 1991 by the Soprintendenza archeologica di Pompei thanks to the finds from Pompeii, Herculaneum, Oplontis, Stabiae, Terzigno, and Boscoreale and to a didactic apparatus.</p>
<p>The neighboring <a href="/wiki/Boscotrecase" title="Boscotrecase">Boscotrecase</a> yielded some elite works of art to excavators at the same time, in particular at the Villa of Agrippa Postumus, also known as the Imperial Villa or the Villa of Augusta.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boscoreale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The municipality is part of <a href="/wiki/Naples_metropolitan_area" title="Naples metropolitan area">Naples metropolitan area</a>, and borders with <a href="/wiki/Boscotrecase" title="Boscotrecase">Boscotrecase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poggiomarino" title="Poggiomarino">Poggiomarino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pompei" title="Pompei">Pompei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scafati" title="Scafati">Scafati</a> (<a href="/wiki/Province_of_Salerno" title="Province of Salerno">SA</a>), <a href="/wiki/Terzigno" title="Terzigno">Terzigno</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Torre_Annunziata" title="Torre Annunziata">Torre Annunziata</a>. It counts the hamlets (<i><a href="/wiki/Frazione" title="Frazione">frazioni</a></i>) of Cangiani, Marchesa, Marra, Passanti, and Pellegrini.</p>

<ul>
<li><cite class="citation audio-visual">Vitale, Veronica (2010). <i>Nel mio Bosco Reale</i>. Frankfurt am Main, Germany: FBP Music Publishing.</cite><span class="Z3988" title="ctx_ver=Z39.88-2004&amp;rfr_id=info%3Asid%2Fen.wikipedia.org%3ABoscoreale&amp;rft.aufirst=Veronica&amp;rft.aulast=Vitale&amp;rft.btitle=Nel+mio+Bosco+Reale&amp;rft.date=2010&amp;rft.genre=unknown&amp;rft.place=Frankfurt+am+Main%2C+Germany&amp;rft.pub=FBP+Music+Publishing&amp;rft_val_fmt=info%3Aofi%2Ffmt%3Akev%3Amtx%3Abook"><span style="display:none;"> </span></span></li>

<p>"Nel mio bosco Reale" - debut album of Veronica Vitale a.k.a. Ivee, international pop artist and multi-talented researcher in the field of systems theory. Her first album released worldwide in 2011 is entitled "Nel mio bosco Reale", outright inspired to her ancient hometown Boscoreale. Because of her outstanding works done as scientist and artist abroad her own country Veronica Vitale has got the Excellency Honor from Boscoreale Town on 27 September 2014. With "Nel mio bosco Reale" album,achieves the Amazon.de Top Ten and the Foreign Critics Award for contributing to revolutionize the inner structure and the very essence of the typical Italian pop music system in Germany, by promoting and empowering the value of the Italian linguistic diversity in music,enhancing it just like English,contributing to an international Italian music style abroad her own country, writing off the typical stereotypes about the Italian people, choosing rather to display all over the world the true Italian culture, art, and history.</p>
