<p>The <b>Boston Reds</b> were a 19th-century <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baseball_team" title="Baseball team">baseball team</a> located in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a> that played in the <a href="/wiki/Players%27_League" title="Players' League">Players' League</a> in 1890 and in the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_(19th_century)" title="American Association (19th century)">American Association</a> in 1891. They played in the <a href="/wiki/Congress_Street_Grounds" title="Congress Street Grounds">Congress Street Grounds</a> in the 1890s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The team took its name from the successful <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> club of the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Professional_Base_Ball_Players" title="National Association of Professional Base Ball Players">National Association</a> and <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> formerly known as the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">(Boston) Red Stockings</a>, who had changed their name to the <a href="/wiki/Boston_baked_beans" title="Boston baked beans">Beaneaters</a> in 1883. The club lasted only two seasons, but in those two seasons they were league champions.</p>
<p>In 1890 the Reds won the Players' League <a href="/wiki/Pennant_(sports)" title="Pennant (sports)">pennant</a> when they finished first ahead of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Giants_(PL)" title="New York Giants (PL)">New York Giants</a>, and then won the American Association pennant when they finished first ahead of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis_Browns_(NL)" title="St. Louis Browns (NL)">St. Louis Browns</a> (now the Cardinals). The Boston Reds are one of two major league teams to win back-to-back pennants spanning two different leagues. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Dodgers" title="Brooklyn Dodgers">Brooklyn Dodgers</a> did it also, winning the AA pennant in 1889 and the NL pennant in 1890.</p>
<p>At the conclusion of the 1891 season, the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> pressed for the consolidation of the American Association with the National League. Part of the posturing included the <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> directing its champion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston_Beaneaters" title="Boston Beaneaters">Boston Beaneaters</a> not to play the Reds in a <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a>. The leagues settled, adding four <a href="/wiki/American_Association_(19th_century)" title="American Association (19th century)">AA</a> clubs to a combined circuit. As part of the settlement, the owners of the four clubs not joining the combined circuit, including the Reds, were paid $135,000 and their players dispersed to the surviving clubs.</p>
<p>Their abandoned ballpark was revived for use by the National League club in 1894, during the weeks that <a href="/wiki/South_End_Grounds" title="South End Grounds">South End Grounds</a> was being rebuilt following a fire. The Congress Street Grounds, with its close left field foul line, quickly gained some more history, as <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Lowe" title="Bobby Lowe">Bobby Lowe</a> hit four home runs in one game there, the first player to accomplish that feat.</p>
