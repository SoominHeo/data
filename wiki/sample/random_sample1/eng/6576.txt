<p><b>Fort Boyard</b> (<small>pronounced: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[fɔʁ bɔjaʀ]</a></span> in english and <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_French" title="Help:IPA for French">[fɔʁ bwajaʀ]</a></span> <span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Fr-Fort_boyard_homme_17.oga" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/86/Fr-Fort_boyard_homme_17.oga" title="Fr-Fort boyard homme 17.oga">pronunciation</a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Fr-Fort_boyard_homme_17.oga" title="File:Fr-Fort boyard homme 17.oga"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span> in french) is a fort located between the <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele-d%27Aix" title="Île-d'Aix">Île-d'Aix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ol%C3%A9ron" title="Oléron">Île d'Oléron</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pertuis_d%27Antioche" title="Pertuis d'Antioche">Pertuis d'Antioche</a> straits, on the west coast of France and is the filming location for the <a href="/wiki/Fort_Boyard_(TV_series)" title="Fort Boyard (TV series)">TV gameshow of the same name</a>. Though a fort on Boyard bank was suggested as early as the 17th century, it was not until the 1800s under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_Bonaparte" title="Napoleon Bonaparte">Napoleon Bonaparte</a> that work began. Building started in 1801 and was completed in 1857. In 1967, the final scene of the French film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Les_aventuriers_(1967_film)" title="Les aventuriers (1967 film)">Les aventuriers</a></i> was filmed at the remains of the fort.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Layout">Layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Boyard_(fortification)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fort_boyard_vue_a%C3%A9rienne.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="683" height="258" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Fort_boyard_vue_a%C3%A9rienne.jpg/220px-Fort_boyard_vue_a%C3%A9rienne.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Fort_boyard_vue_a%C3%A9rienne.jpg/330px-Fort_boyard_vue_a%C3%A9rienne.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Fort_boyard_vue_a%C3%A9rienne.jpg/440px-Fort_boyard_vue_a%C3%A9rienne.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fort_boyard_vue_a%C3%A9rienne.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Fort Boyard from the air</div>

<p>Fort Boyard is oval-shaped, 68 metres (223 ft) long and 31 m (102 ft) wide. The walls were built 20 m (66 ft) high. At the centre is a yard, and the ground floor provided stores and quarters for the men and officers. The floor above contained <a href="/wiki/Casemate" title="Casemate">casemates</a> for the emplacements of guns and further quarters. Above that were facilities for <a href="/wiki/Barbette" title="Barbette">barbette</a> guns and <a href="/wiki/Mortar_(weapon)" title="Mortar (weapon)">mortars</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lepage206_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lepage206-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Boyard_(fortification)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The construction of the fort was first considered during a build-up of the French armed forces undertaken by <a href="/wiki/Louis_XIV_of_France" title="Louis XIV of France">Louis XIV</a> between 1661 and 1667.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Fort Boyard was to form a line of fortification with <a href="/wiki/Fort_Enet" title="Fort Enet">Fort Enet</a> and Fort de la Rade on <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele-d%27Aix" title="Île-d'Aix">Île-d'Aix</a> to protect the arsenal of <a href="/wiki/Rochefort,_Charente-Maritime" title="Rochefort, Charente-Maritime">Rochefort</a> from <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> incursions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Due to the limited range of artillery in the 17th century, the <a href="/wiki/Field_of_fire_(weaponry)" title="Field of fire (weaponry)">fields of fire</a> between the fortifications on the islands of Aix and Oléron did not overlap. A fort on Boyard bank, roughly midway between the two, would have filled that gap. In 1692 the French engineer Descombs began planning the programme of building the fort; however, once it became clear how expensive it would be the scheme was abandoned. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vauban" title="Vauban">Vauban</a>, Louis XIV's leading <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_engineer" title="Military engineer">military engineer</a>, famously advised against it, saying "Your Majesty, it would be easier to seize the moon with your teeth than to attempt such an undertaking in such a place".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lepage206_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Lepage206-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a British raid on Île-d'Aix in 1757, plans for a fort on Boyard bank were once again considered. Though plans were drawn up, the logistical problems again ensured it was abandoned. Efforts were renewed under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon Bonaparte</a> in 1800, and the following year engineers Ferregeau and <a href="/wiki/Armand_Samuel_de_Marescot" title="Armand Samuel de Marescot">Armand Samuel de Marescot</a>, and Vice-Admiral <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_%C3%89tienne_de_Rosily-Mesros" title="François Étienne de Rosily-Mesros">François Étienne de Rosily-Mesros</a> designed a fort to be built on the bank. To facilitate the work, a port was established on île d'Oléron. The village of <a href="/wiki/Boyardville" title="Boyardville">Boyardville</a> was built for the workers. The first stage of construction was to establish a plateau, some 100 by 50 m (330 by 160 ft), to act as foundations. To this end, stones were piled up on the bank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The project was suspended in 1809. Construction resumed in 1837, under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis-Philippe_of_France" title="Louis-Philippe of France">Louis-Philippe</a>, following renewed tensions with the United Kingdom. The fortifications were completed in 1857, with sufficient room for a garrison of 250 men. However, by the time of its completion, the range of cannons had significantly increased, making the fort unnecessary for national defence.</p>
<p>After 1871, Fort Boyard was briefly used as a military prison, before being abandoned at the beginning of the 20th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As time went by, the fort slowly crumbled and deteriorated into the sea as it was left unmaintained. In 1950 it was made a listed building, and in 1961 was sold to Charente Maritime Regional Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>It has been the filming location of both the French and international versions of the <a href="/wiki/Fort_Boyard_(TV_series)" title="Fort Boyard (TV series)">TV gameshow of the same name</a> since 1990, and was also the location for filming <i><a href="/wiki/The_Last_Adventure_(1967_film)" title="The Last Adventure (1967 film)">The Last Adventure</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Alain_Delon" title="Alain Delon">Alain Delon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lino_Ventura" title="Lino Ventura">Lino Ventura</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joanna_Shimkus" title="Joanna Shimkus">Joanna Shimkus</a>.</p>
