<a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin" xml:lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Bózhōu</span></i>), is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mongol" title="Mongol">Mongol</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_prefecture" title="Autonomous prefecture">autonomous prefecture</a> in <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang" title="Xinjiang">Xinjiang</a> Uyghur Autonomous Region of the <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bortala_Mongol_Autonomous_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bortala is located in the southwestern part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dzungarian_Basin" title="Dzungarian Basin">Dzungarian Basin</a>. It occupies a V-shaped basin between the <a href="/wiki/Dzungarian_Alatau" title="Dzungarian Alatau">Dzungarian Alatau</a> in the northwest and the <a href="/wiki/Borohoro_Mountains" title="Borohoro Mountains">Borohoro Mountains</a> in the southwest.</p>
<p>The prefecture borders <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a> to the north and west, and has an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/China-Kazakhstan_border" title="China-Kazakhstan border">international border</a> of 385 km (239 mi). To the east it borders <a href="/wiki/Wusu" title="Wusu">Wusu</a> City and <a href="/wiki/Toli_County" title="Toli County">Toli County</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tacheng_Prefecture" title="Tacheng Prefecture">Tacheng Prefecture</a>; to the south it borders <a href="/wiki/Nilka_County" title="Nilka County">Nilka County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yining_County" title="Yining County">Yining County</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Huocheng_County" title="Huocheng County">Huocheng County</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ili_Kazakh_Autonomous_Prefecture" title="Ili Kazakh Autonomous Prefecture">Ili Kazakh Autonomous Prefecture</a>.</p>
<p>The prefecture has two large lakes, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ebi-Nur" title="Ebi-Nur">Ebi-Nur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sayram_Lake" title="Sayram Lake">Sayram Lake</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bortala_Mongol_Autonomous_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bortala is divided into two <a href="/wiki/County-level_city" title="County-level city">county-level cities</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bole,_Xinjiang" title="Bole, Xinjiang">Bole</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alashankou" title="Alashankou">Alashankou</a>; and two counties: <a href="/wiki/Jinghe_County" title="Jinghe County">Jinghe County</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wenquan_County" title="Wenquan County">Wenquan County</a>. In addition, it is home to the Fifth Agricultural Division of the <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang_Production_and_Construction_Corps" title="Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps">Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps</a> and its 11 regiment-level farms / ranches.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bortala_Mongol_Autonomous_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang dynasty</a> created the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shuanghe_Protectorate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shuanghe Protectorate (page does not exist)">Shuanghe Protectorate</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> (<a class="extiw" href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%8F%8C%E6%B2%B3%E9%83%BD%E7%9D%A3%E5%BA%9C" title="zh:双河都督府">zh</a>)</span> in this area. During the <a href="/wiki/Yuan_dynasty" title="Yuan dynasty">Yuan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ming_dynasty" title="Ming dynasty">Ming dynasties</a> the area was the territory of the <a href="/wiki/Oirats" title="Oirats">Oirats</a>. , <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chahar_(Mongols)" title="Chahar (Mongols)">Chahar</a> Mongols were moved here during the <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a> from <a href="/wiki/Zhangjiakou" title="Zhangjiakou">Kalgan</a>, while <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Torgut_Mongols" title="Torgut Mongols">Torghuud</a> <a href="/wiki/Oirats" title="Oirats">Oirats</a> moved eastwards from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volga" title="Volga">Volga</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> established the autonomous prefecture on July<span class="nowrap"> </span>13, 1954.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bortala_Mongol_Autonomous_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are 35 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nationalities_of_China" title="Nationalities of China">nationalities</a> in Bortala. 67% of the 424,300 inhabitants are <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Han Chinese</a>, while the remainder are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mongol" title="Mongol">Mongol</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uyghur_people" title="Uyghur people">Uyghur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhs" title="Kazakhs">Kazakh</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hui_Chinese" title="Hui Chinese">Hui</a>, or of other nationalities.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bortala_Mongol_Autonomous_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004 the prefecture had a total <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">gross domestic product</a> of 3.69 billion <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">Renminbi</a> (including the XPCC 5th division), an increase of 11.9% over the previous year. Annual total imports and exports totalled <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a> 554 million, an increase of 96.8% over the previous year. Average annual salary was 11000 <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">Renminbi</a>, an increase of 7.6%; average annual pure income per capita for agricultural workers was 3904 <a href="/wiki/Renminbi" title="Renminbi">Renminbi</a>, an increase of 10.8%.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bortala_Mongol_Autonomous_Prefecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alashankou" title="Alashankou">Alashankou</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Port_of_entry" title="Port of entry">port of entry</a> with both <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railroad" title="Railroad">railroads</a> and <a href="/wiki/Road" title="Road">roads</a> linking China with <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>; it is also one of China's national first-class port of entry (国家一类口岸). The volume of imports / exports passing through Alashankou accounts for 90% of the total for all of Xinjiang, and has been second to only <a href="/wiki/Manzhouli" title="Manzhouli">Manzhouli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a> among land ports-of-entry in China for 8 days.</p>
