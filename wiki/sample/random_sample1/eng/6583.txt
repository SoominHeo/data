<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Premise">Premise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boy_A&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Premise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The book is the story of a child criminal released into society as an adult, taking its title from the court practice of concealing the identity of child defendants and child killers.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Success">Success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boy_A&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Published by <a href="/wiki/Serpent%27s_Tail" title="Serpent's Tail">Serpent's Tail</a> in English speaking countries, the novel has been translated into French under the title <i>Jeux d'Enfants</i> published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallimard" title="Gallimard">Gallimard</a> and also into Chinese, Russian, Dutch and Spanish. It won the 2004 <a href="/wiki/John_Llewellyn_Rhys_Prize" title="John Llewellyn Rhys Prize">John Llewellyn Rhys Prize</a> and the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Waverton_Good_Read_Award" title="Waverton Good Read Award">Waverton Good Read Award</a>. On <a href="/wiki/World_Book_Day#World_Book_Day_in_the_UK_and_Ireland" title="World Book Day">World Book Day (UK)</a> 2008, <i>Boy A</i> was voted the most discussion-worthy novel by a living writer in the <i>Spread The Word</i> poll.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_adaptation">Television adaptation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boy_A&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Television adaptation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Boy_A_(film)" title="Boy A (film)">Boy A (film)</a></div>
<p>The book was adapted into a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA_award" title="BAFTA award">BAFTA award</a>-winning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_movie" title="TV movie">TV movie</a> that was released theatrically in some areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The film was made by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cuba_Pictures" title="Cuba Pictures">Cuba Pictures</a> and written by <a href="/wiki/Mark_O%27Rowe" title="Mark O'Rowe">Mark O'Rowe</a>, and premiered at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It was directed by <a href="/wiki/John_Crowley_(director)" title="John Crowley (director)">John Crowley</a> and starred <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Garfield" title="Andrew Garfield">Andrew Garfield</a> (who won the 2008 Best Actor BAFTA TV Award for his performance), <a href="/wiki/Peter_Mullan" title="Peter Mullan">Peter Mullan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Katie_Lyons" title="Katie Lyons">Katie Lyons</a>. The film was released cinematically in the USA by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weinstein_Company" title="Weinstein Company">Weinstein Company</a> on 23 July 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Similarities_with_the_James_Bulger_case">Similarities with the James Bulger case</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boy_A&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Similarities with the James Bulger case">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film shares several similarities with the notorious murder of <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_James_Bulger" title="Murder of James Bulger">James Bulger</a> at the hands of two ten-year-old boys in 1993. Jonathan Trigell has said that the character of Jack/Eric is actually based on a childhood friend of his who had struggled with life outside prison after serving a lengthy sentence (although not, as the writer states, for murder). Trigell has however admitted that the media frenzy surrounding the Bulger murderer's release at the time the novel was written did serve as an influence of sorts on the book.</p>
