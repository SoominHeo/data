<p><i><b>Voyage of Time</b></i> is a 2016 American <a href="/wiki/IMAX" title="IMAX">IMAX</a> <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary film</a> written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Terrence_Malick" title="Terrence Malick">Terrence Malick</a>. The film is said to be an examination of the birth and death of the known universe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Malick has been working on the film for over forty years and it has been described by Malick himself as "one of my greatest dreams".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film will be released in two versions: a forty-minute IMAX version with narration by <a href="/wiki/Brad_Pitt" title="Brad Pitt">Brad Pitt</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/35_mm_film" title="35 mm film">35-millimetre</a> feature-length edition that will be narrated by <a href="/wiki/Cate_Blanchett" title="Cate Blanchett">Cate Blanchett</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The feature-length edition was selected to compete for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Lion" title="Golden Lion">Golden Lion</a> at the <a href="/wiki/73rd_Venice_International_Film_Festival" title="73rd Venice International Film Festival">73rd Venice International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-venice_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-venice-5">[5]</a></sup> The IMAX version of the film was released on October 7, 2016, by <a href="/wiki/IMAX_Corporation" title="IMAX Corporation">IMAX Corporation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Broad_Green_Pictures" title="Broad Green Pictures">Broad Green Pictures</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The IMAX version opened to strongly positive reviews from critics.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voyage_of_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Voyage of Time</i> is a celebration of the universe, displaying the whole of time, from its start to its final collapse. This film examines all that occurred to prepare the world that stands before us now: science and spirit, birth and death, the grand cosmos and the minute life systems of our planet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voyage_of_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voyage_of_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The origins of <i>Voyage of Time</i> stem back to the late 1970s when Malick began developing a project for Paramount, titled <i>Q</i>, that explored the origins of life on earth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-10">[10]</a></sup> Production began with <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a> backing the film with $1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-:2-10">[10]</a></sup> Malick ended up walking away from the project, though elements and footage from <i>Q</i> were later utilized for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tree_of_Life_(film)" title="The Tree of Life (film)">The Tree of Life</a></i> (2011), and <i>Voyage of Time.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-:2-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Brad_Pitt" title="Brad Pitt">Brad Pitt</a> was confirmed to narrate the film in 2009, while <a href="/wiki/Cate_Blanchett" title="Cate Blanchett">Cate Blanchett</a> joined in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Emma_Thompson" title="Emma Thompson">Emma Thompson</a> also joined in 2011 and recorded narration, but she was ultimately cut from the finished production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voyage_of_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Filming for <i>Voyage of Time</i> has been undertaken all over the world. At the <a href="/wiki/Cannes_Film_Festival" title="Cannes Film Festival">Cannes Film Festival</a> market screening of <i>Voyage of Time</i> in 2014, Malick and his producers showed footage shot in locations such as <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_United_States" title="Southwestern United States">Southwestern U.S</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monterey,_California" title="Monterey, California">Monterey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palau" title="Palau">Palau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a> and elsewhere.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-production">Post-production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voyage_of_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dan Glass (<i><a href="/wiki/The_Matrix_(franchise)" title="The Matrix (franchise)">The Matrix</a></i> trilogy) is said to be the special effects supervisor on the film, with <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Trumbull" title="Douglas Trumbull">Douglas Trumbull</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/2001:_A_Space_Odyssey_(film)" title="2001: A Space Odyssey (film)">2001: A Space Odyssey</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Close_Encounters_of_the_Third_Kind" title="Close Encounters of the Third Kind">Close Encounters of the Third Kind</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Blade_Runner" title="Blade Runner">Blade Runner</a></i>) as his consultant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawsuit">Lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voyage_of_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Lawsuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2013, the film was the subject of a lawsuit, after backers of the film, Seven Seas, sued <a href="/wiki/Sycamore_Pictures" title="Sycamore Pictures">Sycamore Pictures</a>, claiming that "Malick did little or no work on what is supposed to be a trio of projects", and that Malick was only allowed to work on other projects if it didn't interfere with the documentary, despite beginning work on <a href="/wiki/The_Tree_of_Life_(film)" title="The Tree of Life (film)"><i>The Tree of Life</i></a>, <i><a href="/wiki/To_the_Wonder" title="To the Wonder">To the Wonder</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Knight_of_Cups_(film)" title="Knight of Cups (film)">Knight of Cups</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weightless_(film)" title="Weightless (film)">Weightless</a></i> during the production stage of <i>Voyage of Time</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-:3-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Seven Seas further claimed that “Sycamore [Pictures] has blown through nearly $6 million over the last six years on this project with nothing to show for it... Our contract specified that funds were to be used exclusively for <i>Voyage of Time</i> but we believe some were redirected to other films Malick was producing during that period, in flagrant disregard for our agreement.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-:3-16">[16]</a></sup> In September 2013, Malick's lawyer commented on the matter stating: "The claims of Seven Seas are without merit. The film was on budget, on schedule, and all funds were used appropriately." They have now followed up with their own filing, which alleges that Seven Seas "concocted the story told in its Complaint" as it "either ran out of, or never had, the funds necessary to meet its financing obligations".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In February 2014, a deal was made to pay the film back in nine months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voyage_of_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2015, <a href="/wiki/Broad_Green_Pictures" title="Broad Green Pictures">Broad Green Pictures</a> acquired distribution rights to the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The feature-length-version of the film had its world premiere at the <a href="/wiki/Venice_Film_Festival" title="Venice Film Festival">Venice Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-venice_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-venice-5">[5]</a></sup> It will also screen at the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The film was released on IMAX on October 7, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voyage_of_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The IMAX version of the film currently holds a 77/100 on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> and a 92% on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>. In <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/A._O._Scott" title="A. O. Scott">A. O. Scott</a> called it a "fusion of digital imagery and photographic imagination that seems at once utterly natural and completely impossible."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The feature-length version holds a 68/100 on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, based on 10 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> also reports a 65% approval rating, based on 17 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
