<p><i><b>Rubus coreanus</b></i>, known as <b>bokbunja</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> <b>Korean black raspberry</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">[3]</a></sup> or <b>Korean bramble</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BSBI07_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BSBI07-4">[4]</a></sup> is a species of <a href="/wiki/Raspberry" title="Raspberry">raspberry</a> native to <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It produces edible <a href="/wiki/Berry" title="Berry">berries</a> (not <a href="/wiki/Berry_(botany)" title="Berry (botany)">true berries</a> in the botanical sense) that are fermented into <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bokbunja_ju" title="Bokbunja ju">bokbunja ju</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Korean_alcoholic_beverages#Fruit_wines" title="Korean alcoholic beverages">Korean fruit wine</a> (although the majority of fruit commercially grown for producing this drink are actually <i><a href="/wiki/Rubus_occidentalis" title="Rubus occidentalis">Rubus occidentalis</a></i>, native to North America).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The wine made of <i>Rubus coreanus</i> is called <i>bokbunjaju</i>(<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko" xml:lang="ko">복분자주</span>).</p>
