<p><i><b>Sympathy for Mr. Vengeance</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang">복수는 나의 것</span>; <a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">RR</a>: <i><span class="Unicode" style="white-space:normal; text-decoration: none" title="Korean transliteration">Boksuneun Naui Geot</span></i>; lit. "Vengeance Is Mine") is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_South_Korean_films_of_2002" title="List of South Korean films of 2002">2002</a> <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Korea" title="Cinema of Korea">South Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Thriller_(genre)" title="Thriller (genre)">thriller</a> <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Park_Chan-wook" title="Park Chan-wook">Park Chan-wook</a> which follows the character Ryu trying to earn enough money for his sister's kidney transplant and the path of vengeance that follows. It is the first part of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Vengeance_Trilogy" title="The Vengeance Trilogy">The Vengeance Trilogy</a></i> and is followed by <i><a href="/wiki/Oldboy_(2003_film)" title="Oldboy (2003 film)">Oldboy</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/List_of_South_Korean_films_of_2003" title="List of South Korean films of 2003">2003</a>) and <i><a href="/wiki/Lady_Vengeance" title="Lady Vengeance">Lady Vengeance</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/List_of_South_Korean_films_of_2005" title="List of South Korean films of 2005">2005</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sympathy_for_Mr._Vengeance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ryu is a <a href="/wiki/Deaf-mute" title="Deaf-mute">deaf-mute</a> man working in a factory. His ailing sister is in desperate need of a <a href="/wiki/Kidney" title="Kidney">kidney</a> transplant, but Ryu's is not a match. After he is laid off from his job, Ryu takes his severance money and contacts a <a href="/wiki/Black_market" title="Black market">black market</a> organ dealer to exchange one of his kidneys for one that his sister can use. The dealers disappear after taking Ryu's kidney and money. Three weeks later, Ryu learns from his doctor that a donor has been found, but he is unable to afford the operation.</p>
<p>To raise money for the operation, Yeong-mi, Ryu's radical anarchist girlfriend, suggests kidnapping the daughter of an executive that fired Ryu. They kidnap Yu-sun, the young daughter of Dong-jin. The girl stays with Ryu's sister, who believes Ryu is merely babysitting her. Ryu and Yeong-mi successfully collect the <a href="/wiki/Ransom" title="Ransom">ransom</a> from Dong-jin. However, Ryu's sister discovers the plan and commits suicide to stop being a burden to him.</p>
<p>Ryu takes Yu-sun and his sister's body to a suburban riverbed they used to frequent as children, and begins to bury her. Distracted by the burial and unable to hear, Ryu is unaware when Yu-sun slips into the river, and she drowns.</p>
<p>As Dong-jin mourns his daughter, he hires an investigator to find her kidnappers. Dong-jin finds Yeong-mi and tortures her. She apologizes for Yu-sun's death, but warns Dong-jin that her friends (a terrorist group) will kill him if she dies. Unfazed by the threats, Dong-jin electrocutes her. Meanwhile, Ryu locates and murders the organ dealers. He returns to Yeong-mi's apartment and sees the police removing her corpse.</p>
<p>Ryu and Dong-jin wait at each other's residence in an attempt to kill the other. Ryu, the first to return home, is knocked unconscious by Dong-jin's booby trap. Dong-jin takes Ryu to the riverbed where his daughter died, slashes his <a href="/wiki/Achilles_tendon" title="Achilles tendon">Achilles tendons</a> and waits for Ryu to bleed to death.</p>
<p>After dismembering Ryu's corpse, Dong-jin begins digging a hole, when a group of men arrive. After surrounding and stabbing Dong-jin, one man pins a note to his chest with a knife. The note reveals them to be Yeong-mi's friends. The group leave Dong-jin dying next to the bloody tools and bags containing Ryu's body parts.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sympathy_for_Mr._Vengeance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Sympathy for Mr. Vengeance</i> opened in South Korea on March 29, 2002 and had a worldwide box office gross of <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$1,954,937</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-1">[1]</a></sup> <i>Sympathy for Mr. Vengeance</i> received a low-profile <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> theatrical release from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tartan_Films" title="Tartan Films">Tartan Films</a> beginning August 19, 2005, over three years after it debuted in South Korea. In its opening weekend, it collected <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$9,827</span> (<span style="white-space: nowrap">US$3,276</span> per screen) from three <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> theaters. It played on six screens at its most widespread, and its total North American box office take was <span style="white-space: nowrap">US$45,243</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mojo_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The film was named the best movie of 2002 by <a href="/wiki/Harry_Knowles" title="Harry Knowles">Harry Knowles</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Ain%27t_It_Cool_News" title="Ain't It Cool News">Ain't It Cool News</a></i>, who praised the acting and the story.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Bobo Deng from <i>HDFest</i> complimented the director on his visuals and the gruesome effects, stating that although the film has extreme violence, it is used to help develop the story.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Elaine Perrone from <i>eFilmCritic</i> commented on the cinematography of Kim Byung-il, saying that the film is "far more visually striking" than its successor <i><a href="/wiki/Oldboy_(2003_film)" title="Oldboy (2003 film)">Oldboy</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remake">Remake</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sympathy_for_Mr._Vengeance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Remake">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
