<p>The <b>Battle of Yan Province</b> was a battle fought between the warlords <a href="/wiki/Cao_Cao" title="Cao Cao">Cao Cao</a> and <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BC_Bu" title="Lü Bu">Lü Bu</a> for control of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yanzhou_(ancient_China)" title="Yanzhou (ancient China)">Yan Province</a> (covering present-day southwestern <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a> and eastern <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/End_of_the_Han_dynasty" title="End of the Han dynasty">late Eastern Han dynasty</a>. The battle lasted for at least one hundred days with an indecisive conclusion.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Yan_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 192, remnants of the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Turban_Rebellion" title="Yellow Turban Rebellion">Yellow Turban rebels</a> from Qing Province invaded Yan Province and occupied <a href="/wiki/Dongping_County" title="Dongping County">Dongping</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rencheng_District" title="Rencheng District">Rencheng</a>. <a href="/wiki/Liu_Dai" title="Liu Dai">Liu Dai</a>, Inspector of Yan Province, wanted to attack the rebels but <a href="/wiki/Bao_Xin" title="Bao Xin">Bao Xin</a>, Chancellor of Jibei, advised him against it. Liu Dai ignored Bao Xin's warning, engaged the rebels in battle, and was eventually defeated and killed.</p>
<p>At that time, <a href="/wiki/Cao_Cao" title="Cao Cao">Cao Cao</a>'s advisor <a href="/wiki/Chen_Gong" title="Chen Gong">Chen Gong</a> urged him to take control of Yan Province and secure it as a base for conquering other territories. Chen Gong volunteered to persuade Liu Dai's followers to join Cao Cao. Bao Xin, who was friendly towards Cao Cao, travelled to Dong <a href="/wiki/Commandery_(China)" title="Commandery (China)">Commandery</a> (southwest of present-day <a href="/wiki/Puyang" title="Puyang">Puyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>) to invite Cao to be the new Inspector of Yan Province.</p>
<p>Cao Cao then attacked the Yellow Turban rebels at Shouzhang but failed to defeat them. After that, Cao Cao reformed his army, enforced military rules more strictly, and used incentives to encourage his men. He recognized that the rebels did not have a stable flow of supplies, as they relied on plundering to sustain themselves. Hence, Cao Cao launched surprise attacks on the rebels, prevented them from pillaging, and achieving ultimate victory and forcing the rebels to retreat north. Cao Cao pursued the rebels and defeated them again at Jibei (south of present-day <a href="/wiki/Changqing_District" title="Changqing District">Changqing District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shandong" title="Shandong">Shandong</a>). The rebels, numbering more than 300,000, including 100,000 civilians, surrendered to Cao Cao. Cao reorganized the surrendered troops to form the Qingzhou Corps (青州兵), while implementing the <i><a href="/wiki/Tuntian" title="Tuntian">tuntian</a></i> system for the people to provide for themselves and the military.</p>
<p>In 193, Cao Cao's father <a href="/wiki/Cao_Song" title="Cao Song">Cao Song</a> was killed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zhang_Kai_(Han_dynasty)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zhang Kai (Han dynasty) (page does not exist)">Zhang Kai</a> (張闓), a subordinate of <a href="/wiki/Tao_Qian_(Han_dynasty)" title="Tao Qian (Han dynasty)">Tao Qian</a>, Governor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xuzhou_(ancient_China)" title="Xuzhou (ancient China)">Xu Province</a>. In retaliation, Cao Cao launched an attack on Tao Qian, sweeping through Tao's lands and slaughtering thousands of Xu Province's population, including civilians. Tao Qian retreated to Tancheng (郯城), where Cao Cao was unable to conquer the city and retreated after his army was running low on supplies.</p>
<p>The following year, Cao Cao attacked Tao Qian again, conquering many counties in Langya and Donghai commanderies of Xu Province. Just then, Cao Cao's subordinates <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Miao" title="Zhang Miao">Zhang Miao</a> and Chen Gong rebelled against him and aided <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BC_Bu" title="Lü Bu">Lü Bu</a> in taking over his home base of Yan Province. Cao Cao then decided to abandon his campaign on Tao Qian and turn back to retake Yan Province.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_battle">The battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Yan_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cao Cao and Lü Bu's forces were locked in a stalemate for at least a hundred days, with Cao on the disadvantageous end. Eventually, Lü Bu abandoned his position because of a famine outbreak. Cao Cao then laid siege to Lü Bu at Juye, Puyang. Cao Cao's advisors <a href="/wiki/Xun_Yu" title="Xun Yu">Xun Yu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cheng_Yu" title="Cheng Yu">Cheng Yu</a> defended the cities of Juancheng, Fan and Dong'e, but this left only two counties with solid defenses, so Cao Cao led his army back. Lü Bu arrived and was unable to take Juancheng by siege so he went west and garrisoned Puyang. Lü Bu re-stationed his forces to the east at Shanyang.</p>
<p>In battle, Lü Bu used his cavalry in the first clash of arms, charging towards Cao Cao's Qingzhou Corps. The Qingzhou Corps fled in terror and Cao's formations were in complete disarray. Cao Cao, seeing the confusion, quickly galloped ahead when a fire broke out, but he fell from his horse and burnt the palm of his left hand. Cao's army halted before they reached camp, because most of Cao's generals had not seen their lord and feared for his safety. Cao Cao then strained himself to rouse his men, ordering that siege weapons be prepared immediately so that they can besiege Lü Bu again.</p>
<p>Zhang Miao followed Lü Bu and left his brother <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Chao" title="Zhang Chao">Zhang Chao</a> (張超), Administrator of Guangling, to take care of their family at Yongqiu. Cao Cao laid siege on Yongqiu for several months and eventually captured the city, killing Zhang Chao and his family. Zhang Miao pleaded for assistance from <a href="/wiki/Yuan_Shu" title="Yuan Shu">Yuan Shu</a> but was rejected and killed by his soldiers. At the same time, a locust plague broke out, causing many to starve while some resorted to <a href="/wiki/Cannibalism" title="Cannibalism">cannibalism</a>. Lü Bu had also used up all his provisions, horse feed, and grain supplies so both sides were forced to withdraw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Cao Cao later surrounded Lü Bu at Puyang, the influential Tian clan, who were initially on Lü's side, switched allegiance to Cao and allowed his forces to enter the city. Cao Cao set fire to the eastern gate as a sign that he had no intention of reversing course, thereupon he came under attack and was defeated. Some of Lü Bu's horsemen captured Cao Cao but were unaware of his identity. Cao Cao saw a man riding on a yellow horse and lied that the man was him, so Lü Bu's horsemen released him and chased the rider. Cao Cao then dashed through the burning eastern gate and escaped from Puyang.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KMA_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-KMA-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within two years, Cao Cao was able to recapture all the cities in Yan Province and defeated Lü Bu at Juye. Lü Bu then fled eastwards to join <a href="/wiki/Liu_Bei" title="Liu Bei">Liu Bei</a>, who had taken over Xu Province from Tao Qian.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Yan_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Battle of Yan Province led to the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Xiapi" title="Battle of Xiapi">Battle of Xiapi</a> in 198, when the allied forces of Cao Cao and Liu Bei launched an attack on Lü Bu in Xu Province. Lü Bu was executed on Cao Cao's order after his defeat.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Yan_Province&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The battle was featured as a stage in <a href="/wiki/Koei" title="Koei">Koei</a>'s video game <i><a href="/wiki/Dynasty_Warriors_5" title="Dynasty Warriors 5">Dynasty Warriors 5</a>: Xtreme Legends</i> called the "Battle of Yan Province". In <i><a href="/wiki/Dynasty_Warriors_7" title="Dynasty Warriors 7">Dynasty Warriors 7</a></i>, the battle is part of the "Battle of Xu Province", which has Lü Bu attacking Cao Cao's main camp. It should not be confused with the "Battle of Yan Province", which deals with remnants of the Yellow Turban rebels.</p>
