<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Taxonomy of the Gastropoda (Bouchet &amp; Rocroi, 2005)</h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><p>The <b>taxonomy of the Gastropoda</b> as it was revised in 2005 by <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Bouchet" title="Philippe Bouchet">Philippe Bouchet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Rocroi" title="Jean-Pierre Rocroi">Jean-Pierre Rocroi</a> is a system for the scientific <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biological_classification" title="Biological classification">classification</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gastropoda" title="Gastropoda">gastropod</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mollusk" title="Mollusk">mollusks</a>. (Gastropods are a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taxonomic" title="Taxonomic">taxonomic</a> <a href="/wiki/Class_(biology)" title="Class (biology)">class</a> of animals which consists of <a href="/wiki/Snail" title="Snail">snails</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slug" title="Slug">slugs</a> of every kind, from the land, from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freshwater" title="Freshwater">freshwater</a>, and from <a href="/wiki/Seawater" title="Seawater">saltwater</a>.) The paper setting out this taxonomy was published in the journal <i><a href="/wiki/Malacologia" title="Malacologia">Malacologia</a></i>. The system encompasses both living and extinct groups, as well as some fossils whose classification as gastropods is uncertain.</p>
<p>The Bouchet &amp; Rocroi system was the first complete gastropod taxonomy that primarily employed the concept of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clades" title="Clades">clades</a>, and was derived from research on <a href="/wiki/Molecular_phylogenetics" title="Molecular phylogenetics">molecular phylogenetics</a>; in this context a clade is a "natural grouping" of organisms based upon a statistical <a href="/wiki/Cluster_analysis" title="Cluster analysis">cluster analysis</a>. In contrast, most of the previous overall taxonomic schemes for gastropods relied on <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(biology)" title="Morphology (biology)">morphological features</a> to classify these animals, and used <a href="/wiki/Taxon" title="Taxon">taxon</a> ranks such as <a href="/wiki/Order_(biology)" title="Order (biology)">order</a>, superorder and suborder, which are typical of classifications that are still inspired by <a href="/wiki/Linnaean_taxonomy" title="Linnaean taxonomy">Linnaean taxonomy</a>.</p>
<p>In the Bouchet &amp; Rocroi taxonomy, clades are used between the rank of <a href="/wiki/Class_(biology)" title="Class (biology)">class</a> and the rank of <a href="/wiki/Taxonomic_rank" title="Taxonomic rank">superfamily</a>. The clades are unranked. Bouchet and Rocroi use six main clades: <a href="/wiki/Patellogastropoda" title="Patellogastropoda">Patellogastropoda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vetigastropoda" title="Vetigastropoda">Vetigastropoda</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cocculiniformia" title="Cocculiniformia">Cocculiniformia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neritimorpha" title="Neritimorpha">Neritimorpha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caenogastropoda" title="Caenogastropoda">Caenogastropoda</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Heterobranchia" title="Heterobranchia">Heterobranchia</a>. The first three of these major clades have no nesting clades within them: the taxonomy goes immediately to the superfamily level. Within the Caenogastropoda there is one extra clade. In contrast, within the Heterobranchia, for some of the <a href="/wiki/Nudibranch" title="Nudibranch">nudibranch</a> groups there are six separate clades above the level of superfamily, and in the case of most of the <a href="/wiki/Land_snail" title="Land snail">land snails</a>, there are four clades above the level of superfamily.</p>
<p>In some parts of the taxonomy, instead of "clade", Bouchet and Rocroi labelled groupings of taxa as a "group" or an "informal group". A clade must by definition contain only one lineage, and it was considered to be the case that these "informal groups" may either contain more than one lineage, or only contain part of a lineage. Further research will eventually resolve these questions. Since the publication of this taxonomic system in 2005, various proposals for changes have been published by other authors, for more information see <a href="/wiki/Changes_in_the_taxonomy_of_gastropods_since_2005" title="Changes in the taxonomy of gastropods since 2005">changes in the taxonomy of gastropods since 2005</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Context">Context</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taxonomy_of_the_Gastropoda_(Bouchet_%26_Rocroi,_2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Context">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Systems of classification such as this one are primarily of value to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malacologist" title="Malacologist">malacologists</a> (people who study mollusks) and other <a href="/wiki/Biologist" title="Biologist">biologists</a>. Biological classification schemes are not merely a convenience, they are an attempt to show the actual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phylogeny" title="Phylogeny">phylogeny</a> (the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evolutionary" title="Evolutionary">evolutionary</a> relatedness) within a group of <a href="/wiki/Organism" title="Organism">organisms</a>. Thus a taxonomy such as this one can be seen as an attempt to elucidate part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tree_of_life_(science)" title="Tree of life (science)">tree of life</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Phylogenetic_tree" title="Phylogenetic tree">phylogenetic tree</a>.</p>
<p>The Bouchet &amp; Rocroi 2005 system of gastropod <a href="/wiki/Taxonomy_(biology)" title="Taxonomy (biology)">taxonomy</a> was laid out in a book-length paper entitled "Classification and Nomenclator of Gastropod Families", which was published in the journal <i><a href="/wiki/Malacologia" title="Malacologia">Malacologia</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet-1">[1]</a></sup> and which was written in collaboration with J. Frýda, B. Hausdorf, <a href="/wiki/Winston_Ponder" title="Winston Ponder">W. Ponder</a>, Á. Valdés and A. Warén. This system supersedes the system of <a href="/wiki/Taxonomy_of_the_Gastropoda_(Ponder_%26_Lindberg,_1997)" title="Taxonomy of the Gastropoda (Ponder &amp; Lindberg, 1997)">Ponder and Lindberg from 1997</a>. <a href="/wiki/Changes_in_the_taxonomy_of_gastropods_since_2005" title="Changes in the taxonomy of gastropods since 2005">Subsequent revisions by other authors</a> have been made since the publication of this paper.</p>
<p>The taxonomy set out by Bouchet &amp; Rocroi is an attempt to get one step closer to representing this part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evolution_of_Mollusca" title="Evolution of Mollusca">evolutionary history of the phylum Mollusca</a>. Bouchet &amp; Rocroi's classification system is a hybrid of the pre-existing, more traditional <a href="/wiki/Linnaean_taxonomy" title="Linnaean taxonomy">Linnaean taxonomy</a> along with some more recent far-reaching revisions which are based on molecular work and use <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clades" title="Clades">clades</a> as <a href="/wiki/Taxon" title="Taxon">taxa</a>, (see <a href="/wiki/Cladistics" title="Cladistics">cladistics</a>). In the past, the taxonomy of gastropods was largely based on the <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(biology)" title="Morphology (biology)">morphological</a> characters of the taxa, such as the <a href="/wiki/Gastropod_shell" title="Gastropod shell">shell</a> characteristics (including the <a href="/wiki/Protoconch" title="Protoconch">protoconch</a>) in shelled species, and the internal <a href="/wiki/Anatomy" title="Anatomy">anatomy</a>, including the structure of the <a href="/wiki/Radula" title="Radula">radula</a> and details of the <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_system_of_gastropods" title="Reproductive system of gastropods">reproductive system</a>. Recent advances are based more on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Molecular" title="Molecular">molecular</a> characteristics of the <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a> and <a href="/wiki/RNA" title="RNA">RNA</a>. This shift in emphasis has meant that the newer taxa and their hierarchy are subject to debate, a debate that is not likely to be resolved soon.</p>
<p>This proposed classification has tried to integrate the results of recent molecular work by using unranked <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">clades</a> for taxa below the traditional rank of class (class Gastropoda) but above the rank of <a href="/wiki/Taxonomic_rank" title="Taxonomic rank">superfamily</a> (replacing the ranks subclass, superorder, order, and suborder), while still using the traditional <a href="/wiki/Linnaean_taxonomy" title="Linnaean taxonomy">Linnaean</a> ranks for superfamilies and all taxa below the rank of superfamily (i.e., family, subfamily, tribe, genus, subgenus, and species. The clades have been given names which are similar to, or in some cases the same as, traditional Linnaean names for taxa above the level of superfamily. Whenever <a href="/wiki/Monophyly" title="Monophyly">monophyly</a> (a single ancestry) has not yet been tested and confirmed, or where a traditional taxon of gastropods has already been discovered to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paraphyletic" title="Paraphyletic">paraphyletic</a> (that it excludes some of its descendants) the term "group" or "informal group" has been used. Both Linnaean taxa and clades are invalid if it turns out they are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polyphyletic" title="Polyphyletic">polyphyletic</a>, in other words if they consist of more than one lineage.</p>
<p>In this taxonomy 611 valid families are recognized. Of these, 202 families are exclusively <a href="/wiki/Fossil" title="Fossil">fossil</a>, and this is indicated here with a dagger †. Superfamily names are standardized so that they all end in the suffix "-oidea", also commonly used for superorders and subclasses, replacing the "-acea" ending found especially in the older literature. Classification of families into subfamilies however is often not well resolved, and should be regarded as the best possible <a href="/wiki/Hypothesis" title="Hypothesis">hypothesis</a>.</p>
<p>The publication Bouchet &amp; Rocroi (2005)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bouchet_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bouchet-1">[1]</a></sup> also includes a <a href="/wiki/Nomenclator_(nomenclature)" title="Nomenclator (nomenclature)">nomenclator</a> of about 2400 suprageneric taxa (taxa above the level of <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a>) of gastropods, from the subtribe to the superfamily. A full bibliographic reference is provided for each taxon, giving the name of the authority, the original publication, the date of that publication, the type genus for the taxon, its nomenclatural status, and its validity under the rules of the <a href="/wiki/International_Code_of_Zoological_Nomenclature" title="International Code of Zoological Nomenclature">ICZN</a>.</p>
<p>Since the publication of this taxonomic system in 2005, various changes have been published by other authors, for more information see <a href="/wiki/Changes_in_the_taxonomy_of_gastropods_since_2005" title="Changes in the taxonomy of gastropods since 2005">changes in the taxonomy of gastropods since 2005</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_clades.2C_groups_and_informal_groups">Main clades, groups and informal groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taxonomy_of_the_Gastropoda_(Bouchet_%26_Rocroi,_2005)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Main clades, groups and informal groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Here the information is displayed in the form of a <a href="/wiki/Cladogram" title="Cladogram">cladogram</a> (an evolutionary tree of descent.) It is worth bearing in mind however that this taxonomy is provisional: many of the taxa are still only known as "groups" or "informal groups", and these are very likely to be changed as more information becomes available.</p>
