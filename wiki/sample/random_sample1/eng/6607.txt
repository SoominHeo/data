<p><b>Lady's slipper orchids</b> (also known as <b>lady slipper orchids</b> or <b>slipper orchids</b>) are <a href="/wiki/Orchidaceae" title="Orchidaceae">orchids</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Subfamily" title="Subfamily">subfamily</a> <b>Cypripedioideae</b>, which comprises the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Cypripedium" title="Cypripedium">Cypripedium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexipedium" title="Mexipedium">Mexipedium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paphiopedilum" title="Paphiopedilum">Paphiopedilum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phragmipedium" title="Phragmipedium">Phragmipedium</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Selenipedium" title="Selenipedium">Selenipedium</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> They are characterised by the slipper-shaped <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pouch_(orchids)" title="Pouch (orchids)">pouches</a> (modified <a href="/wiki/Labellum_(botany)" title="Labellum (botany)">labellums</a>) of the <a href="/wiki/Flower" title="Flower">flowers</a> – the pouch traps insects so they are forced to climb up past the <a href="/wiki/Staminode" title="Staminode">staminode</a>, behind which they collect or deposit <a href="/wiki/Pollinium" title="Pollinium">pollinia</a>, thus fertilizing the flower.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cypripedioideae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taxonomy_of_the_orchid_family" title="Taxonomy of the orchid family">Taxonomy of the orchid family</a></div>
<p>Unlike most other orchids, slipper orchids have two fertile <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anther" title="Anther">anthers</a> — they are "diandrous". For that reason, experts have debated whether this <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">clade</a> should be classified within the orchid family (<a href="/wiki/Orchidaceae" title="Orchidaceae">Orchidaceae</a>), or whether they should compose a separate family altogether called Cypripediaceae.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Around the year 2000, <a href="/wiki/Molecular_phylogenetics" title="Molecular phylogenetics">molecular phylogenetics</a> and DNA sampling have come to play an increasingly important role in classification.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> This has led to the conclusion that recognition of a distinct Cypripediaceae family would be inappropriate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cypripediumcalceolus-levels.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="699" data-file-width="475" height="250" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9d/Cypripediumcalceolus-levels.jpg/170px-Cypripediumcalceolus-levels.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9d/Cypripediumcalceolus-levels.jpg/255px-Cypripediumcalceolus-levels.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9d/Cypripediumcalceolus-levels.jpg/340px-Cypripediumcalceolus-levels.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cypripediumcalceolus-levels.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Lady's slipper orchid (<i><a href="/wiki/Cypripedium_calceolus" title="Cypripedium calceolus">Cypripedium calceolus</a></i>)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The subfamily Cypripedioideae is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monophyletic" title="Monophyletic">monophyletic</a> and consists of five genera:</p>
<dl>
<dd>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Cypripedium" title="Cypripedium">Cypripedium</a></i>, found across much of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, as well as in parts of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> (one species) and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. The state flower of <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> is the showy lady's slipper (<i><a href="/wiki/Cypripedium_reginae" title="Cypripedium reginae">Cypripedium reginae</a></i>); the pink lady's slipper (<i><a href="/wiki/Cypripedium_acaule" title="Cypripedium acaule">Cypripedium acaule</a></i>) is the official flower of the <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> province of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a>.</li>
</ul>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Paphiopedilum" title="Paphiopedilum">Paphiopedilum</a></i>, found in the tropical forests of <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">southeast Asia</a> reaching as far north as southern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. <i>Paphiopedilum</i> is quite easy to cultivate and therefore is popular among orchid enthusiasts. In fact, over-collection of this genus has been so extensive that many species are now sub-viable in their natural habitats.</li>
</ul>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Phragmipedium" title="Phragmipedium">Phragmipedium</a></i>, found across northern South and Central America, is also easy to cultivate as it requires lower <a href="/wiki/Temperature" title="Temperature">temperatures</a> than <i>Paphiopedilum</i>, eliminating the need for a <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse" title="Greenhouse">greenhouse</a> in many areas.</li>
</ul>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Mexipedium" title="Mexipedium">Mexipedium</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Monotypic_taxon" title="Monotypic taxon">monotypic</a> genus, consisting of a single species that was found in a single locality in Oaxaca, Mexico.</li>
</ul>
</dd>
</dl>
<dl>
<dd>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Selenipedium" title="Selenipedium">Selenipedium</a></i>, found in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.</li>
</ul>
</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbolism">Symbolism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cypripedioideae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Symbolism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The province of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Island" title="Prince Edward Island">Prince Edward Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> adopted the lady's slipper as its <a href="/wiki/Floral_emblem" title="Floral emblem">floral emblem</a> in 1947.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
