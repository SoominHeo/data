<p>Year <b>605</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCV</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Friday" title="Common year starting on Friday">common year starting on Friday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 605 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=605&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=605&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=605&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Byzantine Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Phocas" title="Phocas">Phocas</a> recognizes <a href="/wiki/Agilulf" title="Agilulf">Agilulf</a> as king of the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombards</a>, and signs a <a href="/wiki/Peace_treaty" title="Peace treaty">peace treaty</a>. He pays a <a href="/wiki/Tribute" title="Tribute">tribute</a> and cedes <a href="/wiki/Orvieto" title="Orvieto">Orvieto</a> (<a href="/wiki/Central_Italy" title="Central Italy">Central Italy</a>), among other towns. The <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_army" title="Byzantine army">Byzantine army</a> is withdrawn from the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkan Peninsula</a>.</li>
<li>Phocas has <a href="/wiki/Constantina_(empress)" title="Constantina (empress)">Constantina</a>, empress consort of <a href="/wiki/Maurice_(emperor)" title="Maurice (emperor)">Maurice</a>, and her three daughters arrested. He accuses her of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conspiracy_(crime)" title="Conspiracy (crime)">conspiracy</a>, and has them executed at <a href="/wiki/Chalcedon" title="Chalcedon">Chalcedon</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bithynia" title="Bithynia">Bithynia</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=605&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%86thelfrith_of_Northumbria" title="Æthelfrith of Northumbria">Æthelfrith</a> annexes the neighboring kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Deira" title="Deira">Deira</a> (<a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>). The region between the <a href="/wiki/River_Forth" title="River Forth">Forth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Humber" title="Humber">Humber</a> rivers will hereafter be known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northumbria" title="Northumbria">Northumbria</a>, the most powerful of the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxons" title="Anglo-Saxons">Anglo-Saxon</a> kingdoms.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Persia">Persia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=605&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Persia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>As a result of a quarrel between the <a href="/wiki/Lakhmids" title="Lakhmids">Lakhmids</a> (Southern <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>) and King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khosrau_II" title="Khosrau II">Khosrau II</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sassanid_Empire" title="Sassanid Empire">Persian</a> frontier with <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a> is no longer guarded (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=605&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Yang_of_Sui" title="Emperor Yang of Sui">Yángdi</a> orders the capital to be transferred from <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a> to <a href="/wiki/Luoyang" title="Luoyang">Luoyang</a>. He begins the construction of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Canal_of_China" title="Grand Canal of China">Grand Canal</a>, that will link existing <a href="/wiki/Waterway" title="Waterway">waterways</a> to the new Chinese capital; it will be built by a million <a href="/wiki/Laborer" title="Laborer">laborers</a>.</li>
<li>Yángdi introduces an <a href="/wiki/Imperial_examination" title="Imperial examination">imperial examination</a>, designed to select the best administrative officials (after they receive the <b>jinshi</b>) for the state; this begins a long <a href="/wiki/Bureaucracy" title="Bureaucracy">bureaucratic</a> tradition of <a href="/wiki/Scholar-official" title="Scholar-official">scholar-officialdom</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Anji_Bridge" title="Anji Bridge">Zhaozhou Bridge</a> is completed under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sui_Dynasty" title="Sui Dynasty">Sui Dynasty</a>, the earliest known fully stone open-spandrel segmental <a href="/wiki/Arch_bridge" title="Arch bridge">arch bridge</a> in the world (although the earlier Roman <a href="/wiki/Trajan%27s_Bridge" title="Trajan's Bridge">Trajan's Bridge</a> featured segmental arches).</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amshuvarma" title="Amshuvarma">Amshuvarma</a> becomes king of the <a href="/wiki/Licchavi_(kingdom)" title="Licchavi (kingdom)">Licchavi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>. He is credited for opening <a href="/wiki/Trade_route" title="Trade route">trade routes</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibet</a>. His ruling period is known as the "Golden Period".</li>
</ul>
