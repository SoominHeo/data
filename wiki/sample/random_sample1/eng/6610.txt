<p><b>Sir Vaughan Frederick Randal Jones</b> <span style="font-size:85%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knight_Companion_of_The_New_Zealand_Order_of_Merit" title="Knight Companion of The New Zealand Order of Merit">KNZM</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellow_of_The_Royal_Society" title="Fellow of The Royal Society">FRS</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellow_of_The_Royal_Society_of_New_Zealand" title="Fellow of The Royal Society of New Zealand">FRSNZ</a></span> <span style="font-size:85%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Australian_Academy_of_Science" title="Fellow of the Australian Academy of Science">FAA</a></span> (born 31 December 1952) is a New Zealand and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> mathematician, known for his work on <a href="/wiki/Von_Neumann_algebra" title="Von Neumann algebra">von Neumann algebras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Knot_polynomial" title="Knot polynomial">knot polynomials</a>. He was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Fields_Medal" title="Fields Medal">Fields Medal</a> in 1990, and famously wore a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Blacks" title="All Blacks">New Zealand rugby</a> jersey when he gave his acceptance speech in Kyoto.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vaughan_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vaughan Jones was born in <a href="/wiki/Gisborne,_New_Zealand" title="Gisborne, New Zealand">Gisborne, New Zealand</a> and brought up in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_New_Zealand" title="Cambridge, New Zealand">Cambridge, New Zealand</a>, completing secondary school at <a href="/wiki/Auckland_Grammar_School" title="Auckland Grammar School">Auckland Grammar School</a>. His undergraduate studies were at the University of Auckland, from where he obtained a BSc in 1972 and an MSc in 1973. For his graduate studies, he went to <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, where he completed his PhD at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Geneva" title="University of Geneva">University of Geneva</a> in 1979. His thesis, titled <i>Actions of finite groups on the hyperfinite II<sub>1</sub> factor</i>, was written under the supervision of <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Haefliger" title="AndrÃ© Haefliger">AndrÃ© Haefliger</a>. In 1980, he moved to the United States, where he taught at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Los_Angeles" title="University of California, Los Angeles">University of California, Los Angeles</a> (1980â1981) and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania" title="University of Pennsylvania">University of Pennsylvania</a> (1981â1985), before being appointed as Professor of Mathematics at the University of California, Berkeley.</p>
<p>His work on <a href="/wiki/Knot_polynomial" title="Knot polynomial">knot polynomials</a>, with the discovery of what is now called the <a href="/wiki/Jones_polynomial" title="Jones polynomial">Jones polynomial</a>, was from an unexpected direction with origins in the theory of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Von_Neumann_algebras" title="Von Neumann algebras">von Neumann algebras</a>, an area of <a href="/wiki/Analysis" title="Analysis">analysis</a> already much developed by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Connes" title="Alain Connes">Alain Connes</a>. It led to the solution of a number of classical problems of <a href="/wiki/Knot_theory" title="Knot theory">knot theory</a>, and to increased interest in <a href="/wiki/Low-dimensional_topology" title="Low-dimensional topology">low-dimensional topology</a>.</p>
<p>Jones has since 2011 been at <a href="/wiki/Vanderbilt_University" title="Vanderbilt University">Vanderbilt University</a> as Stevenson Distinguished Professor of mathematics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He remains Professor Emeritus at <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a> where he has been on the faculty since 1985<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and is a Distinguished Alumni Professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Auckland" title="University of Auckland">University of Auckland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was made an honorary vice-president for life of the <a href="/wiki/International_Guild_of_Knot_Tyers" title="International Guild of Knot Tyers">International Guild of Knot Tyers</a> in 1992.</p>
