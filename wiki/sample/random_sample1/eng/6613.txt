<p><i><b>Salanoia</b></i> is a genus of <a href="/wiki/Eupleridae" title="Eupleridae">euplerid</a> <a href="/wiki/Carnivora" title="Carnivora">carnivoran</a> with two currently described species found in <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-durbin_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-durbin-1">[1]</a></sup> They are mongoose-like, which is reflected in the older versions of their English names, for example <a href="/wiki/Brown-tailed_mongoose" title="Brown-tailed mongoose">brown-tailed mongoose</a> which is now called brown-tailed vontsira.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-isbn1-84162-245-1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-isbn1-84162-245-1-2">[2]</a></sup> The name <i>Salanoia</i> is derived from one of the vernacular names for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salanoia_concolor" title="Salanoia concolor">Salanoia concolor</a></i>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salano" title="Salano">Salano</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-isbn1-84162-245-1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-isbn1-84162-245-1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, the two described members of the genus were referred to by the common name <i>vontsira</i> in announcements of the discovery of <i><a href="/wiki/Salanoia_durrelli" title="Salanoia durrelli">Salanoia durrelli</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <i>Vontsira</i> is a Malagasy vernacular name that seems to apply to a few local species of local mongoose-like carnivores in the related genera <i>Salanoia</i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galidia" title="Galidia">Galidia</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Galidictis" title="Galidictis">Galidictis</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-isbn1-84162-245-1_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-isbn1-84162-245-1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
