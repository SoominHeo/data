<p>In <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economics</a>, the <b>monetary base</b> (also <b>base money</b>, <b>money base</b>, <b>high-powered money</b>, <b>reserve money</b>, <b>outside money</b>, <b>central bank money</b> or, in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>, <b>narrow money</b>) in a country is defined as the portion of a <a href="/wiki/Commercial_bank" title="Commercial bank">commercial banks</a>' <a href="/wiki/Bank_reserves" title="Bank reserves">reserves</a> that consist of the commercial bank's accounts with its <a href="/wiki/Central_bank" title="Central bank">central bank</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> plus the total <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> circulating in the public, plus the currency, also known as <i>vault cash</i>, that is physically held in the <a href="/wiki/Bank_vault" title="Bank vault">bank's vault</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <b>monetary base</b> should not be confused with the <a href="/wiki/Money_supply" title="Money supply">money supply</a> which consists of the total <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> circulating in the public plus the non-bank deposits with <a href="/wiki/Commercial_bank" title="Commercial bank">commercial banks</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Management">Management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monetary_base&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:U.S._Monetary_base.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2480" data-file-width="3508" height="156" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/U.S._Monetary_base.png/220px-U.S._Monetary_base.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/U.S._Monetary_base.png/330px-U.S._Monetary_base.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/U.S._Monetary_base.png/440px-U.S._Monetary_base.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:U.S._Monetary_base.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
U.S. Monetary base</div>
</div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Open_market_operation" title="Open market operation">Open market operations</a> are <a href="/wiki/Monetary_policy" title="Monetary policy">monetary policy</a> tools which directly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Expansionary_monetary_policy" title="Expansionary monetary policy">expand</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contractionary_monetary_policy" title="Contractionary monetary policy">contract</a> the monetary base.</p>
<p>The monetary base and monetary policy are typically controlled by the same national institution, usually either the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Finance_ministry" title="Finance ministry">finance ministry</a> or the central bank. These institutions change the monetary base through open market transactions: the buying and selling of government bonds. Typically, they can also influence banking activities by manipulating interest rates and setting reserve requirements (how much money banks must keep on hand instead of loaning out to borrowers).</p>
<p>The monetary base (MB) is called <b>high-powered</b> because its increase will typically result in a much larger increase in the supply of <a href="/wiki/Demand_deposit" title="Demand deposit">demand deposits</a> through banks' loan-making; a ratio called the <a href="/wiki/Money_multiplier" title="Money multiplier">money multiplier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mankiw_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mankiw-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monetary_base&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 8 June 2016, the monetary base in the United States was USD 3,869,723,000,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
