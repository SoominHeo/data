<p>The <b>Volcanic Repeating Arms Company</b> was a U.S. company formed in 1855 by partners <a href="/wiki/Horace_Smith_(inventor)" title="Horace Smith (inventor)">Horace Smith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_B._Wesson" title="Daniel B. Wesson">Daniel B. Wesson</a> to develop <a href="/wiki/Walter_Hunt_(inventor)" title="Walter Hunt (inventor)">Walter Hunt</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Rocket_Ball" title="Rocket Ball">Rocket Ball</a> ammunition and <a href="/wiki/Lever_action" title="Lever action">lever action</a> mechanism. Volcanic made an improved version of the Rocket Ball ammunition, and a <a href="/wiki/Carbine" title="Carbine">carbine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pistol" title="Pistol">pistol</a> version of the lever action gun to fire it. While the Volcanic Repeating Arms Company was short lived, its descendants, <a href="/wiki/Smith_%26_Wesson" title="Smith &amp; Wesson">Smith &amp; Wesson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Winchester_Repeating_Arms_Company" title="Winchester Repeating Arms Company">Winchester Repeating Arms Company</a>, became major firearms manufacturers.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volcanic_Repeating_Arms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original 1848 "Volition Repeating Rifle" design by Hunt was revolutionary, introducing an early iteration of the lever action repeating mechanism and the tubular <a href="/wiki/Magazine_(firearms)" title="Magazine (firearms)">magazine</a> still common today. However, Hunt's design was far from perfect, and only a couple of prototypes were developed; the only one known is currently in the Firearms Museum in <a href="/wiki/Cody,_Wyoming" title="Cody, Wyoming">Cody, Wyoming</a>. Lewis Jennings patented an improved version of Hunt's design in 1849, and versions of the Jenning's patent design were built by Robbins &amp; Lawrence Co. (under the direction of shop foreman <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Tyler_Henry" title="Benjamin Tyler Henry">Benjamin Tyler Henry</a>) and sold by C. P. Dixon. Horace Smith was also hired by Courtlandt Palmer to improve the Jennings Rifle, patenting the Smith-Jennings in 1851. It is estimated that fewer than 2000 of these two models were made until 1852, when financial troubles ceased production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taylor_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-taylor-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sawyer_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-sawyer-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1854, partners <a href="/wiki/Horace_Smith_(inventor)" title="Horace Smith (inventor)">Horace Smith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_B._Wesson" title="Daniel B. Wesson">Daniel B. Wesson</a> joined with Courtlandt Palmer, the businessman who had purchased the Jennings and Smith-Jennings patent rights,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Henshaw1993_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Henshaw1993-3">[3]</a></sup> and further improved on the operating mechanism, developing the Smith &amp; Wesson Lever pistol, and a new Volcanic cartridge. Production was in the shop of Horace Smith in <a href="/wiki/Norwich,_Connecticut" title="Norwich, Connecticut">Norwich, Connecticut</a>. The new cartridge improved upon the Hunt Rocket Ball with the addition of a primer. Originally using the name "Smith &amp; Wesson Company", the name was changed to "Volcanic Repeating Arms Company" in 1855, with the addition of new investors, one of which was <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Winchester" title="Oliver Winchester">Oliver Winchester</a>. The Volcanic Repeating Arms Company obtained all rights for the Volcanic designs (both rifle and pistol versions were in production by this time) as well as the ammunition, from the Smith &amp; Wesson Company. Wesson remained as plant manager for 8 months before rejoining Smith to found the "Smith &amp; Wesson Revolver Company" upon obtaining the licensing of the <a href="/wiki/Rollin_White" title="Rollin White">Rollin White</a> rear loading cylinder patent. Winchester forced the insolvency of the Volcanic Arms Company in late 1856, took over ownership and moved the plant to <a href="/wiki/New_Haven,_Connecticut" title="New Haven, Connecticut">New Haven, Connecticut</a>, where it was reorganized as the New Haven Arms Company in April 1857. B. Tyler Henry was hired as plant superintendent when Robbins &amp; Lawrence suffered financial difficulties and Henry left their employ. While continuing to make the Volcanic rifle and pistol, Henry began to experiment with the new <a href="/wiki/Rimfire_ammunition" title="Rimfire ammunition">rimfire</a> ammunition, and modified the Volcanic lever action design to use it. The result was the <a href="/wiki/Henry_rifle" title="Henry rifle">Henry rifle</a>. By 1866, the company once again reorganized, this time as the Winchester Repeating Arms company, and the name of Winchester became synonymous with lever action rifles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sawyer_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-sawyer-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volcanic_Repeating_Arms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a 2012 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Pawn_Stars" title="Pawn Stars">Pawn Stars</a></i>, titled "Bear-ly There", a .41 caliber Volcanic Repeating Arms pistol was purchased by the Gold &amp; Silver Pawn Shop for $6,500.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Webster2016_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Webster2016-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>A Volcanic repeating rifle is featured in the western movie <a href="/wiki/For_a_Few_Dollars_More" title="For a Few Dollars More">For a Few Dollars More</a> and is used by the <a href="/wiki/Man_with_No_Name" title="Man with No Name">Man with No Name</a> and El Indio for a brief period of time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Webster2016_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Webster2016-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Volcanic pistol is available as a weapon in <a href="/wiki/Activision" title="Activision">Activision</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Gun_(video_game)" title="Gun (video game)">Gun</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Rockstar_Games" title="Rockstar Games">Rockstar Games'</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Dead_Redemption" title="Red Dead Redemption">Red Dead Redemption</a></i>. Under the name "Volcanic Pistol", a Volcanic pistol appears in <a href="/wiki/Techland" title="Techland">Techland's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Juarez" title="Call of Juarez">Call of Juarez</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Juarez:_Bound_in_Blood" title="Call of Juarez: Bound in Blood">Call of Juarez: Bound in Blood</a></i>. It is the most powerful handgun available in both games. The Western-themed card game <i><a href="/wiki/Bang!_(card_game)" title="Bang! (card game)">Bang!</a></i> also has a Volcanic; it has the shortest range of any gun, but compensates with the unique special ability to fire more than once per turn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Utter2015_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Utter2015-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Rider, the titular character of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_M._Erdelac&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Edward M. Erdelac (page does not exist)">Edward M. Erdelac</a>'s Judeocentric Lovecraftian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weird_west" title="Weird west">weird west</a> series Merkabah Rider, carries a Volcanic pistol inlaid with gold and silver and bearing various Solomonic talismans and wards, including a jeweled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tree_of_Sephiroth" title="Tree of Sephiroth">Tree of Sephiroth</a> on the handle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Erdelac2009_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Erdelac2009-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>An 1855 Volcanic pistol was featured during the <i><a href="/wiki/Sons_of_Guns" title="Sons of Guns">Sons of Guns</a></i> season 3 episode "World's Largest Machine Gun" in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>A Volcanic pistol appears as an unlockable item for the <a href="/wiki/Shinsengumi" title="Shinsengumi">Shinsengumi</a> agent in <i><a href="/wiki/Total_War:_Shogun_2" title="Total War: Shogun 2">Total War: Shogun 2</a></i> and is available as a starter weapon in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fistful_of_Frags" title="Fistful of Frags">Fistful of Frags</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
