<p>The <b>Volta River</b> is a river, primarily flowing in Volta Region and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Dagbon" title="Kingdom of Dagbon">Dagbon</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Region_of_Ghana" title="Northern Region of Ghana">Northern Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>, that drains into the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">Gulf of Guinea</a> in Ada. It has three main tributaries—the <a href="/wiki/Black_Volta" title="Black Volta">Black Volta</a>, <a href="/wiki/White_Volta" title="White Volta">White Volta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red_Volta" title="Red Volta">Red Volta</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reservoir">Reservoir</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volta_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reservoir">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lake_Volta" title="Lake Volta">Lake Volta</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Reservoir" title="Reservoir">reservoir</a> impounded by the <a href="/wiki/Akosombo_Dam" title="Akosombo Dam">Akosombo Dam</a> on the lower Volta River in southern Ghana. It is one of the largest reservoirs in the world. It extends from the <a href="/wiki/Akosombo_Dam" title="Akosombo Dam">Akosombo Dam</a> in southeastern Ghana to the town of <a href="/wiki/Yapei" title="Yapei">Yapei</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Dagbon" title="Kingdom of Dagbon">Dagbon</a>, some 400 kilometres (250 mi) to the north.</p>
<p>The reservoir's dam generates electricity for the <a href="/wiki/Volta_River_Authority" title="Volta River Authority">Volta River Authority</a>, and the reservoir provides inland water transport routes. It may be a resource for irrigation and fish farming.</p>
<p>The depth of the river is 45 feet (14 m) below Lake Volta. The Volta River is crossed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adome_Bridge" title="Adome Bridge">Adome Bridge</a> below the Akosombo Dam and the reservoir.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volta_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Volta River was named by Portuguese gold traders in Ghana. It was their farthest extent of exploration before returning (<i>volta</i> is Portuguese for "twist" or "turn").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Volta_-_etymology_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Volta_-_etymology-4">[4]</a></sup> <i>"River of return"</i> (perhaps because it was where ships turned around and headed for home) or “river of bend,” in reference to its course.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Volta_-_etymology_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Volta_-_etymology-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bakewell_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bakewell-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
