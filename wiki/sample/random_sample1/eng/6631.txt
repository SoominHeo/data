<p><b>Wolfsburg</b> (<small>German pronunciation:</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈvɔlfsbʊʁk]</a></span><small class="nowrap metadata"> (<a href="/wiki/File:De-Wolfsburg.ogg" title="File:De-Wolfsburg.ogg"><img alt="" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" height="13" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/13px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/20px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Speaker_Icon.svg/26px-Speaker_Icon.svg.png 2x" width="13"/></a> <a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8e/De-Wolfsburg.ogg" title="De-Wolfsburg.ogg">listen</a>)</small>) is the fifth largest city in the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a>. Located on the River <a href="/wiki/Aller" title="Aller">Aller</a> northeast of <a href="/wiki/Braunschweig" title="Braunschweig">Braunschweig</a> (<i>Brunswick</i>), it lies about 75 km (47 mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Hanover" title="Hanover">Hanover</a> and 230 km (143 mi) west of <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>.</p>

<p>In 2013, Wolfsburg ranked as the richest city in Germany with a GDP per capita of $128,000, due to its thriving auto industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Wolfsburg is famous as the location of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volkswagen_AG" title="Volkswagen AG">Volkswagen AG</a>'s headquarters and the world's biggest car plant. The <a href="/wiki/Autostadt" title="Autostadt">Autostadt</a> is a visitor attraction next to the Volkswagen factory that features the company's model range: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audi_AG" title="Audi AG">Audi</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bentley_Motors" title="Bentley Motors">Bentley</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bugatti_Automobiles_S.A.S." title="Bugatti Automobiles S.A.S.">Bugatti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ducati" title="Ducati">Ducati</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobili_Lamborghini_S.p.A." title="Automobili Lamborghini S.p.A.">Lamborghini</a>, <a href="/wiki/MAN_SE" title="MAN SE">MAN</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neoplan" title="Neoplan">Neoplan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Porsche_AG" title="Porsche AG">Porsche</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scania_AB" title="Scania AB">Scania</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SEAT,_S.A." title="SEAT, S.A.">SEAT</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0koda_Auto" title="Škoda Auto">Škoda Auto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Commercial_Vehicles" title="Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles">Volkswagen Commercial Vehicles</a>. Wolfsburg is one of the few German cities built during the first half of the 20th century. From its foundation on 1 July 1938 as a home for workers producing the "KdF-Wagen" (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VW_Beetle" title="VW Beetle">VW Beetle</a>) until 25 May 1945, the city was called "Stadt des KdF-Wagens bei Fallersleben". In 1972, the population first exceeded 100,000.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wolfsburg is located at the Southern edge of the ancient river valley of the <a href="/wiki/Aller" title="Aller">Aller</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mittellandkanal" title="Mittellandkanal">Mittellandkanal</a> (<i>Midland Canal</i>). It is bordered by the districts of <a href="/wiki/Gifhorn_(district)" title="Gifhorn (district)">Gifhorn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Helmstedt_(district)" title="Helmstedt (district)">Helmstedt</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The total annual precipitation is about 532 mm (21 in) which is quite low as it belongs to the lowest tenth of the measured data in Germany. Only 7% of all observation stations of the <a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Wetterdienst" title="Deutscher Wetterdienst">Deutscher Wetterdienst</a> (<i>German Weather Service</i>) record lower data. The driest month is October, most precipitation is measured in June where observation stations measure 1.9 times more precipitation than in October. Precipitation hardly diversifies and is constantly distributed all over the year. Only 17% of observation stations measure lower annual deviation.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The castle "<a href="/wiki/Wolfsburg_Castle" title="Wolfsburg Castle">Wolfsburg</a>" was first mentioned in 1302 in a document as the domicile of the noble lineage of Bartensleben. Originally a keep next to the Aller, it was protected by a moat some centuries later. In 1372, the first documentary reference to the Burg Neuhaus (<i>castle of Neuhaus</i>) near Wolfsburg appeared. After the extinction of the Bartensleben line in 1742, the property and its Schloss Wolfsburg (<i>Wolfsburg Castle</i>) passed on to the Earls of the Schulenburg. The communal manor was an important employer for the nearby settlements <i>Rothenfelde</i> and <i>Heßlingen</i>.</p>
<p>Some of today's urban districts, including <i>Heßlingen</i>, belonged to the Duke of <a href="/wiki/Magdeburg" title="Magdeburg">Magdeburg</a> during the 18th century. In 1932, these districts were detached from the Prussian province <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a> and integrated in the administrative district of Lüneburg belonging to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hannover" title="Hannover">Hannover</a>.</p>
<p>Other urban districts, like <i>Vorsfelde</i> and the villages transferred to Wolfsburg from the county of <a href="/wiki/Helmstedt" title="Helmstedt">Helmstedt</a>, belonged to the later Duke of <a href="/wiki/Braunschweig" title="Braunschweig">Braunschweig</a> (<i>Brunswick</i>) for centuries. <i>Fallersleben</i> and other villages belonged to the Electorate of Braunschweig - Lüneburg or the Kingdom of Hanover.</p>
<p>Wolfsburg was founded on 1 July 1938 as the <b>Stadt des KdF-Wagens bei Fallersleben</b> (English: City of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kraft_durch_Freude" title="Kraft durch Freude">KdF</a> Car at <a href="/wiki/Fallersleben" title="Fallersleben">Fallersleben</a>), a planned town centred around the village of Fallersleben, built to house workers of the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen" title="Volkswagen">Volkswagen</a> (<i>people's car</i>) factories erected to assemble the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Beetle" title="Volkswagen Beetle">Volkswagen Beetle</a>.</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> military cars, aeroplanes, and other military equipment were built there, mainly by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forced_labour_in_Germany_during_World_War_II" title="Forced labour in Germany during World War II">forced workers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prisoner_of_war" title="Prisoner of war">POWs</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. In 1942, the Nazis established the <a href="/wiki/Arbeitsdorf" title="Arbeitsdorf">Arbeitsdorf</a> concentration camp in the city for a few months.</p>
<p>At the urging of the British occupying power, the city was renamed as <b>Wolfsburg</b> on 25 May 1945, after the eponymous castle located there. In 1951, Wolfsburg was separated from the District of Gifhorn, and became an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_German_urban_districts" title="List of German urban districts">urban district</a>.</p>
<p>In 1955 the one-millionth VW Beetle was manufactured in Wolfsburg. Postwar Beetle production ended in Wolfsburg in 1974, though German Beetle production continued in <a href="/wiki/Emden" title="Emden">Emden</a> until 1978. The factories in Wolfsburg remain a key part of Volkswagen's production capacity.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Wirtschaftswunder" title="Wirtschaftswunder">German economic miracle</a> Wolfsburg experienced a large influx of immigrant workers, especially from Italy.</p>
<p>In 1958 the city hall was built. In 1960 the Volkswagenwerk GmbH (limited partnership with a limited liability) was changed into an AG (public limited company).</p>
<p>In the course of a land reform in Lower Saxony in 1972, 20 localities were added to the city through the "Wolfsburg-Act". Wolfsburg gained the status of major city with nearly 131,000 inhabitants. The city's area grew from 35 to nearly 204 square kilometers. In 1973, the city's population peaked at 131,971.</p>
<p>In 1982 the A39, a side road of the A2 (Oberhausen - Hannover - Werder), was built as a direct freeway to Wolfsburg.</p>
<p>In 1988, the city became a university town with the establishment of for the University of Applied Science Braunschweig/Wolfenbüttel. Today its name is Ostfalia University of Applied Science.</p>
<p>As a launch promotion for the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Golf_Mk5" title="Volkswagen Golf Mk5">5th generation</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Golf" title="Volkswagen Golf">Volkswagen Golf</a> the city of Wolfsburg welcomed visitors on the internet, on the official stationery, and on every city limit sign with the name "<b>Golfsburg</b>" from 25 August to 10 October 2003. This campaign gained the nationwide attention of press, radio, and TV broadcasting.</p>
<p>In the summer of 2009, Wolfsburg gained nationwide attention when their football team, <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">VfL Wolfsburg</a>, won the German football league. A party was held in the city centre with about 100,000 people, and was a first in the history of the city.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Population_development">Population development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Population development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From about 1,000 inhabitants in 1938 the population of the city increased to 25,000 in 1950 and doubled to 50,000 until 1958. On 1 July 1972 the population of Wolfsburg first went beyond the mark of 100,000 because of the suburbanization of several villages which made Wolfsburg a major city. In 1973 the population reached its highest level: 131,971. At the end of December 2010 121,451 people were registered with their principal residence in Wolfsburg. Until the end of 2012 this number climbed up to 123,144.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city of Wolfsburg is organized into 40 districts. One or more districts make up one of the total of 16 localities which are represented by their own councils. Every council has a local official as its mayor.</p>
<p>First the councils were only established in the 11 localities suburbanized in 1972. They partly took over the functions of the former town councils of each of the districts. In 1991 and 2001 some of the localities were split into smaller areas so that today there are 16 localities, each with its own council which are directly voted by the citizens.</p>
<p>The only exception from this organization is the <a href="/wiki/Allerpark" title="Allerpark">Allerpark</a> (<i>Aller Park</i>), a local recreation area surrounding the <a href="/wiki/Allersee" title="Allersee">Allersee</a> lake, and the area of the Volkswagen factory which are both located in the central city area.</p>
<p>The administrative area of Wolfsburg includes six nature reserves. Five of them are located in the ancient Aller river valley.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Head of the young "Stadt des KdF-Wagens" became the government assessor Karl Bock on enactment #145 of the chief president of the government of <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCneburg" title="Lüneburg">Lüneburg</a> effective from 1 July 1938. His followers were also deployed by the government.</p>
<p>In 1946, the military government of the British zone of occupation established a communal constitution following the British example. After this, citizens voted for a council which elected a volunteer mayor/ lord mayor as the city's leader and representative. After 1946, the council elected a full-time director to lead the city council. In 2001, the city council's dual leadership was abolished. It is led by a full-time lord mayor who is also the city's representative, Klaus Mohrs. Since 2001, citizens directly elect the lord mayor. The council still has its own chairperson elected by the council's constitutive conference after every local election.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="City_council">City council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: City council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The city council is made up of the fractions of the different parties (47 seats) and the lord mayor with one seat. The lord mayor is head of administration, thus the superior of all employees of the city council. The lord mayor is supported by four departmental heads that are voted by the council on his proposal. Together, they make up the board of directors of the city administration where the most important decisions concerning administration are deliberated weekly.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Emblems">Emblems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Emblems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Wolfsburg's emblem shows a silver two-tower castle with a closed gate on red ground over a green base with silver waved timbers. A golden wolf with a blue tongue paces over the castle's battlement. The city's flag is green and white.</p>
<p>Lower Saxony's Department of the Interior awarded the city of Wolfsburg's emblem in 1952 after it had been constituted in the association articles in 1947. In 1961, it was improved heraldically and newly awarded by the governmental executive committee of Lüneburg. The symbols of the wolf and the castle reflect the city's name (canting arm) and do not have a historical, directly conveyed reference. The flag was adopted in 1955.</p>
<p>Volkswagen used a modified version of the Wolfsburg coat of arms as its steering wheel emblem, (and occasionally as a hood ornament, on classic Beetles) until the early 1980s, when it was replaced by the VW roundel.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_authorities">Regional authorities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Regional authorities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_castles">Historical castles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Historical castles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The <b>Schloss Wolfsburg</b> (<i>castle of Wolfsburg</i>), a Weser renaissance castle of the 13th century was first documented as the domicile of the noble lineage of Bartensleben in 1302. As the city is named after this castle, it is Wolfsburg's landmark.</li>
<li>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Burg_Neuhaus_(Wolfsburg)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Burg Neuhaus (Wolfsburg) (page does not exist)">Burg Neuhaus</a> (<i>castle of Neuhaus</i>) is a medieval moat from the 14th century which has been owned by the city government since 1981.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schloss_Fallersleben" title="Schloss Fallersleben">Schloss Fallersleben</a> (<i>castle of Fallersleben</i>) was completed in 1551. Since 1991 it has housed the Hoffmann-von-Fallersleben-Museum.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Museums">Museums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Museums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The <b>Kunstmuseum Wolfsburg</b> (<i>Art museum Wolfsburg</i>) is internationally renowned and has shown contemporary and modern international art since 1994</li>
<li>The <b>Städtische Galerie</b> (<i>Municipal Gallery</i>), located in the <b>Schloss Wolfsburg</b> (<i>castle of Wolfsburg</i>) shows multifarious pieces of contemporary art</li>
<li>The <b>AutoMuseum Volkswagen</b> was opened in an old textile factory in Heßlingen in 1985</li>
<li>The <b>Stadtmuseum Wolfsburg</b> (<i>Town Museum</i>) is a modern museum with an exhibition about the history of the castle, the region and the city. It is located inside the castle of Wolfsburg.</li>
<li>The <b>Hoffmann-von-Fallersleben-Museum</b> in the castle of <a href="/wiki/Fallersleben" title="Fallersleben">Fallersleben</a> shows the history of German poetry and democracy, especially focused on the life of Hoffmann von Fallersleben between 1798 and 1874.</li>
<li>The <b>Heinrich-Büssing-Haus</b> in <b>Nordsteimke</b> was opened on the initiative of the MAN-group in the house of Büssing's birth in 1988. It shows the life of Büssing and the development from craft to industry.</li>
<li>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Burg_Neuhaus_(Wolfsburg)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Burg Neuhaus (Wolfsburg) (page does not exist)">Burg Neuhaus</a> (<i>castle of Neuhaus</i>) is a moat showing an exhibition of models of the castle and the water mill, late medieval weapons and documents concerning the life of people of the time before 1800.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Autostadt" title="Autostadt">Autostadt</a> is besides the Disneyland in Paris the most frequented theme park in Europe. The topic is (auto) mobility.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ph%C3%A6no" title="Phæno">Phæno</a> is a science center with 250 experiment stations on an exhibition space of nearly 6,000 square meters. The architecturally uncommon construction was designed by <a href="/wiki/Zaha_Hadid" title="Zaha Hadid">Zaha Hadid</a>.</li>
<li>The <b>Romantikpark Landleben</b> (<i>theme park Romantik Park Landleben</i>) in Kästorf shows a historical Lower Saxon village combined with parks and restaurants.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wolfsburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The most famous professional sports club in the town is <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">VfL Wolfsburg</a>, established in 1945. The men's football team won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fu%C3%9Fball-Bundesliga" title="Fußball-Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> in 2009, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DFB_Pokal" title="DFB Pokal">DFB Pokal</a> in 2015 and the <a href="/wiki/DFL-Supercup" title="DFL-Supercup">DFL-Supercup</a> in 2015. The <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg_(women)" title="VfL Wolfsburg (women)">women's football team</a> has been even more successful, winning the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga_(women)" title="Bundesliga (women)">Bundesliga</a> in 2013 and 2014 as well as the <a href="/wiki/DFB-Pokal_(women)" title="DFB-Pokal (women)">DFB-Pokal</a> in 2013 and 2015. The women's team has also succeeded in winning the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Women%27s_Champions_League" title="UEFA Women's Champions League">UEFA Women's Champions League</a> in two consecutive years, 2013 and 2014.</p>
<p>Wolfsburg is also the home of the ice hockey team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EHC_Wolfsburg_Grizzly_Adams" title="EHC Wolfsburg Grizzly Adams">EHC Wolfsburg Grizzly Adams</a>, which since 2007 has made it to a leading position in the German ice-hockey league.</p>
<p>Also based in town is the tennis tournament <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Challenger" title="Volkswagen Challenger">Volkswagen Challenger</a>, which has been held annually in Wolfsburg since 1993.</p>
