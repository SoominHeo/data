<p><b>Bolkhov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Бо́лхов</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bolkhovsky_District" title="Bolkhovsky District">Bolkhovsky District</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oryol_Oblast" title="Oryol Oblast">Oryol Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, located on the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nugr_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nugr River (page does not exist)">Nugr River</a> (<a href="/wiki/Oka_River" title="Oka River">Oka</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributary</a>), 56 kilometers (35 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Oryol" title="Oryol">Oryol</a>, the administrative center of the <a href="/wiki/Oblast" title="Oblast">oblast</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">11,421 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">12,148 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-8">[8]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">13,071 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-9">[9]</a></sup> 12,800 (1969); 20,703 (1897).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolkhov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bolkhov was first documented in a chronicle from 1196.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Establish_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Establish-6">[6]</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/Mongol_invasion_of_Rus%27" title="Mongol invasion of Rus'">Mongol invasion of Rus'</a>, it became the seat of a local princely dynasty, whose descendants may be traced until the 19th century. In the 16th century, it became one of the fortified posts for defending <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Tatars" title="Tatars">Tatars</a> on the south. It was there that the army of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vasily_IV_of_Russia" title="Vasily IV of Russia">Vasily IV</a> was defeated by <a href="/wiki/False_Dmitry_II" title="False Dmitry II">False Dmitry II</a> in 1608.</p>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, Bolkhov was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">German Army</a> from October 9, 1941 to July 28, 1943.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolkhov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Bolkhov serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bolkhovsky_District" title="Bolkhovsky District">Bolkhovsky District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OryolO_adm_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-OryolO_adm-1">[1]</a></sup> As an administrative division, it is incorporated within Bolkhovsky District as the <b><a href="/wiki/Town_of_district_significance" title="Town of district significance">town of district significance</a> of Bolkhov</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OryolO_adm_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-OryolO_adm-1">[1]</a></sup> As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the town of district significance of Bolkhov is incorporated within Bolkhovsky Municipal District as <b>Bolkhov Urban Settlement</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OryolOBolkhovskyD_mun_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-OryolOBolkhovskyD_mun-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolkhov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bolkhov preserves four churches dating from the turn of the 18th century, including the five-domed Trinity Monastery Cathedral (1688-1706) and the Trinity church with an elongated belfry. By far the largest church in the city is the Savior's Transfiguration Cathedral, built in 1841-1851 to a design by one of <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Thon" title="Konstantin Thon">Konstantin Thon</a>'s disciples.</p>

<li><span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Орловский областной Совет народных депутатов. Закон №522-ОЗ от 6 июля 2005 г. «Об административно-территориальном устройстве Орловской области», в ред. Закона №1187-ОЗ от 1 апреля 2011 г «О внесении изменений в законодательные акты Орловской области». Вступил в силу с момента официального опубликования. Опубликован: "Орловская правда", №116, 13 июля 2005 г.</span> (Oryol Oblast Council of People's Deputies. Law #522-OZ of July 6, 2005 <i>On the Administrative-Territorial Structure of Oryol Oblast</i>, as amended by the Law #1187-OZ of April 1, 2011 <i>On Amending the Legislative Acts of Oryol Oblast</i>. Effective as of the moment of the official publication.).</li>
<li><span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Орловский областной Совет народных депутатов. Закон №464-ОЗ от 28 декабря 2004 г. «О статусе, границах и административных центрах муниципальных образований на территории Болховского района Орловской области», в ред. Закона №1109-ОЗ от 3 сентября 2010 г. «О внесении изменений в некоторые законодательные акты Орловской области о статусе, границах и административных центрах муниципальных образований». Вступил в силу со дня официального опубликования. Опубликован: "Орловская правда", №233, 30 декабря 2004 г.</span> (Oryol Oblast Council of People's Deputies. Law #464-OZ of December 28, 2004 <i>On the Status, Borders, and Administrative Centers of the Municipal Formations on the Territory of Bolkhovsky District of Oryol Oblast</i>, as amended by the Law #1109-OZ of September 3, 2010 <i>On Amending Various Legislative Acts of Oryol Oblast on the Status, Borders, and Administrative Centers of the Municipal Formations</i>. Effective as of the day of the official publication.).</li>
</ul>
