<p><i><b>One Fine Spring Day</b></i> is a South Korean feature film released by <a href="/wiki/Sidus_Pictures" title="Sidus Pictures">Sidus</a> and Applause Films <a href="/wiki/2001_in_film" title="2001 in film">in 2001</a>. Directed by <a href="/wiki/Hur_Jin-ho" title="Hur Jin-ho">Hur Jin-ho</a> (of <i><a href="/wiki/Christmas_in_August" title="Christmas in August">Christmas in August</a></i>), the film is a portrait of a love affair - from its blossoming in spring, to its decline as the years pass.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=One_Fine_Spring_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sound engineer Sang-woo meets local DJ Eun-soo on a recording trip in the quest for nature's voice. They succeed in capturing various sensual sounds as well as each other's tenderness. Their love flourishes as spring comes along, but Sang-woo's ever intensifying passion often reminds Eun-soo of her tragic past. She knows only too well how passion can vanish like a sound, and how love always surrenders to its end.</p>
