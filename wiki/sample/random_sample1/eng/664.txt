<p>Year <b>619</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCXIX</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 619 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=619&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=619&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=619&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Byzantine Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Byzantine%E2%80%93Sasanian_War_of_602%E2%80%93628#Egypt" title="Byzantine–Sasanian War of 602–628">Byzantine–Sasanian War</a>: The besieged city of <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a> is captured by the Persians. <a href="/wiki/Nicetas_(cousin_of_Heraclius)" title="Nicetas (cousin of Heraclius)">Nicetas</a>, cousin of Emperor <a href="/wiki/Heraclius" title="Heraclius">Heraclius</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chalcedonian_Christianity" title="Chalcedonian Christianity">Chalcedonian</a> patriarch, <a href="/wiki/John_the_Merciful" title="John the Merciful">John V</a>, flee to <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frye_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Frye-1">[1]</a></sup> King <a href="/wiki/Khosrow_II" title="Khosrow II">Khosrow II</a> extends his rule southwards along the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>Heraclius prepares to leave <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> and moves the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> capital to <a href="/wiki/Carthage" title="Carthage">Carthage</a>, but is convinced to stay by <a href="/wiki/Sergius_I_of_Constantinople" title="Sergius I of Constantinople">Sergius I</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecumenical_Patriarch_of_Constantinople" title="Ecumenical Patriarch of Constantinople">patriarch of Constantinople</a>. He begins to rebuild the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_army" title="Byzantine army">Byzantine army</a> with the aid of funds from church treasures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greatrex-Lieu_p._II.2C_198_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Greatrex-Lieu_p._II.2C_198-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Pannonian_Avars" title="Pannonian Avars">Avars</a> attack the outskirts of Constantinople. Numerous <a href="/wiki/Slavs" title="Slavs">Slavic</a> tribes rebel against Avar overlordship; they carve out their own sovereign territory in <a href="/wiki/Moravia" title="Moravia">Moravia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lower_Austria" title="Lower Austria">Lower Austria</a> (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=619&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Second_Perso-Turkic_War" title="Second Perso-Turkic War">Second Perso-Turkic War</a> is fought and ends with a decisive <a href="/wiki/Sasanian_Empire" title="Sasanian Empire">Persian</a> victory.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Meccan_boycott_of_the_Hashemites" title="Meccan boycott of the Hashemites">Meccan boycott of the Hashemites and Muhammad</a> ends.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_2" title="November 2">November 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Tang_campaigns_against_the_Western_Turks" title="Tang campaigns against the Western Turks">Tang campaigns against the Western Turks</a>: A <a href="/wiki/Khagan" title="Khagan">khagan</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Turkic_Khaganate" title="Western Turkic Khaganate">Western Turkic Khaganate</a> is assassinated in a Chinese palace by <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6kt%C3%BCrks#Eastern_Turkic_Khaganate" title="Göktürks">Eastern Turkic</a> rivals, with the approval of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Gaozu_of_Tang" title="Emperor Gaozu of Tang">Emperor Gaozu of Tang</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=619&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Arts_and_sciences">Arts and sciences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=619&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Arts and sciences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The calculation of the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_calendar" title="Chinese calendar">Chinese calendar</a> begins to use true motions of the sun and moon, modeled using two offset opposing <a href="/wiki/Parabola" title="Parabola">parabolas</a>.</li>
<li>The Chinese begin using large <a href="/wiki/Orchestra" title="Orchestra">orchestras</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=619&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_23" title="December 23">December 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pope_Boniface_V" title="Pope Boniface V">Pope Boniface V</a> succeeds <a href="/wiki/Pope_Adeodatus_I" title="Pope Adeodatus I">Adeodatus I</a> as the 69th pope of Rome.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a>'s wife, <a href="/wiki/Khadija_bint_Khuwaylid" title="Khadija bint Khuwaylid">Khadija</a>, dies after 24 years of marriage in the <a href="/wiki/Year_of_Sorrow" title="Year of Sorrow">Year of Sorrow</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kubrat" title="Kubrat">Kubrat</a>, ruler of the <a href="/wiki/Bulgars" title="Bulgars">Bulgars</a>, is <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptised</a> in Constantinople.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mellitus" title="Mellitus">Mellitus</a> becomes <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishop of Canterbury">Archbishop of Canterbury</a>.</li>
</ul>
