<p><b>Pongdong Station</b> is a railway station located in <a href="/wiki/Pongsan_County" title="Pongsan County">Pongsan County</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Hwanghae_Province" title="North Hwanghae Province">North Hwanghae</a> province, <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hayato_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hayato-1">[1]</a></sup> It is on located on the <a href="/wiki/Pyongbu_Line" title="Pyongbu Line">P'yŏngbu Line</a>, which was formed from part of the <a href="/wiki/Gyeongui_Line" title="Gyeongui Line">Kyŏngŭi Line</a> to accommodate the shift of the capital from <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> to <a href="/wiki/Pyongyang" title="Pyongyang">P'yŏngyang</a>; though this line physically connects P'yŏngyang to <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Pusan</a> via <a href="/wiki/Dorasan_Station" title="Dorasan Station">Dorasan</a>, in operational reality it ends at <a href="/wiki/Kaesong_Station" title="Kaesong Station">Kaesŏng</a> due to the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Demilitarized_Zone" title="Korean Demilitarized Zone">Korean Demilitarized Zone</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hayato_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hayato-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pongdong_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the Kyŏngŭi Line was originally opened on 3 April 1906, the station itself was opened by the <a href="/wiki/Chosen_Government_Railway" title="Chosen Government Railway">Chosen Government Railway</a> for passenger and freight service on 1 July 1923. The station was closed after the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>. The station, as well as the disused section from Kaesŏng across the DMZ to Dorasan, was rebuilt in 2003, and a special train inaugurating the reopened line ran on 17 May 2007. Regular freight service began between Dorasan and the <a href="/wiki/Kaesong_Industrial_Region" title="Kaesong Industrial Region">Kaesŏng Industrial Region</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and although passenger service for South Korean workers exists between Dorasan and the Kaesŏng Industrial Area, apart from the inaugural special train, there has been no passenger service to Sonha station. The freight and passenger services have been interrupted several times as a result of political events between North and South that have caused the closure of the industrial district; it was most recently reopened on 16 September 2013 after a five-month shutdown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
