<p><i><b>Bugak</b></i> is a variety of <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Twigim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Twigim (page does not exist)">twigim</a></i> (튀김), or fried <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Korean_traditional_dish" title="Korean traditional dish">Korean traditional dishes</a>, made by <a href="/wiki/Deep_frying" title="Deep frying">deep frying</a> dried <a href="/wiki/Vegetable" title="Vegetable">vegetables</a> or <a href="/wiki/Seaweed" title="Seaweed">seaweed</a> that have been coated with <i>chapssalpul</i> (찹쌀풀, <a href="/wiki/Glutinous_rice" title="Glutinous rice">glutinous rice</a> paste) for preservation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Food_in_Korea_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Food_in_Korea-1">[1]</a></sup> The culinary technique is rare along with <i>twigak</i> (튀각, deep fried vegetables without coating) in Korean cuisine, all of which are regarded as specialty of <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Buddhist_cuisine" title="Buddhist cuisine">Buddhist cuisine</a>.</p>
<p>The ingredients for making <i>bugak</i> are <a href="/wiki/Kombu" title="Kombu">kombu</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gim_(food)" title="Gim (food)">gim</a></i> (Korean <a href="/wiki/Laver_(seaweed)" title="Laver (seaweed)">laver</a>), <a href="/wiki/Perilla" title="Perilla">perilla</a> leaves, <a href="/wiki/Chili_pepper" title="Chili pepper">chili pepper</a> leaves, <i>ueong</i> (우엉, <a href="/wiki/Arctium_lappa" title="Arctium lappa">greater burdock</a> leaves), <a href="/wiki/Shoot" title="Shoot">shoots</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Ailanthus_altissima" title="Ailanthus altissima">Ailanthus altissima</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Camellia" title="Camellia">camellia</a> leaves, <a href="/wiki/Chrysanthemum" title="Chrysanthemum">chrysanthemum</a> leaves, sliced <a href="/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">potato</a> and so forth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncyKorea_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-EncyKorea-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vegetable oils such as <a href="/wiki/Perilla_oil" title="Perilla oil">perilla oil</a> or <a href="/wiki/Soybean_oil" title="Soybean oil">soybean oil</a> are used for frying.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EncyKorea_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-EncyKorea-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Varieties">Varieties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bugak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Varieties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Kkaetip bugak (깻잎부각), made with perilla leaves<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Deulkkaesongi bugak (들깨송이부각), made with cluster of perilla</li>
<li>Dasima bugak (다시마부각), made with <a href="/wiki/Kombu" title="Kombu">kombu</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Gim bugak (김부각), made with <a href="/wiki/Gim_(food)" title="Gim (food)">gim</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Gamja bugak (감자부각), made with sliced potato<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Gochu jaban (고추자반), made with chili pepper leaves</li>
<li>Gochu bugak (고추부각), made with chili pepper<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>Ueong jaban (우엉자반), made with <a href="/wiki/Arctium_lappa" title="Arctium lappa">greater burdock</a> leaves</li>
<li>Gajuk bugak (가죽부각)</li>
<li>Ssuk bugak (쑥부각), made with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ssuk" title="Ssuk">ssuk</a></i> (쑥, <a href="/wiki/Artemisia_princeps" title="Artemisia princeps">Artemisia princeps</a>)</li>
<li>Gamip bugak (감잎부각), made with <a href="/wiki/Persimmon" title="Persimmon">persimmon</a> leaves</li>
<li>Dureup bugak (두릅부각), fried shoots of <a href="/wiki/Aralia_elata" title="Aralia elata">Aralia elata</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
