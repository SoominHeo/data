<p><b>AD 61</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">LXI</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Common year starting on Thursday">common year starting on Thursday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. At the time, it was known as the <b>Year of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consulship" title="Consulship">Consulship</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turpilianus" title="Turpilianus">Turpilianus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Caesennius_Paetus" title="Lucius Caesennius Paetus">Caesennius</a></b> (or, less frequently, <b>year 814 <i><a href="/wiki/Ab_urbe_condita" title="Ab urbe condita">Ab urbe condita</a></i></b>). The denomination AD 61 for this year has been used since the early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medieval_period" title="Medieval period">medieval period</a>, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AD_61&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AD_61&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Roman_Empire">Roman Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AD_61&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Roman Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Publius_Petronius_Turpilianus" title="Publius Petronius Turpilianus">Publius Petronius Turpilianus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Caesennius_Paetus" title="Lucius Caesennius Paetus">Lucius Caesennius Paetus</a> become <a href="/wiki/Roman_consul" title="Roman consul">Roman consuls</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Galba" title="Galba">Galba</a> becomes governor of <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Tarraconensis" title="Hispania Tarraconensis">Hispania Tarraconensis</a>.</li>
<li><i>The following events in</i> <a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">Roman Britain</a> <i>(Britannia) take place in</i> <b>60</b> <i>or</i> <b>61</b>:
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gaius_Suetonius_Paulinus" title="Gaius Suetonius Paulinus">Gaius Suetonius Paulinus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_governor" title="Roman governor">Roman governor</a> of Britain, captures the island of Mona (<a href="/wiki/Anglesey" title="Anglesey">Anglesey</a>), the last stronghold of the <a href="/wiki/Druid" title="Druid">druids</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBH-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prasutagus" title="Prasutagus">Prasutagus</a>, king of the <a href="/wiki/Iceni" title="Iceni">Iceni</a> (in modern <a href="/wiki/East_Anglia" title="East Anglia">East Anglia</a>), dies leaving a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Will_(law)" title="Will (law)">will</a> which passes his kingdom to his two daughters and emperor <a href="/wiki/Nero" title="Nero">Nero</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Roman_army" title="Roman army">Roman army</a> however annexes the kingdom as if conquered, depriving the nobles of their hereditary lands and plundering the land. The king's widow, <a href="/wiki/Boudica" title="Boudica">Boudica</a>, is <a href="/wiki/Flagellation" title="Flagellation">flogged</a> and forced to watch their daughters publicly <a href="/wiki/Rape" title="Rape">raped</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Roman financiers, including <a href="/wiki/Seneca_the_Younger" title="Seneca the Younger">Seneca the Younger</a>, call in their loans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Boudica" title="Boudica">Boudica</a> leads a rebellion of the Iceni against Roman rule<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassell.27s_Chronology_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassell.27s_Chronology-5">[5]</a></sup> in alliance with the <a href="/wiki/Trinovantes" title="Trinovantes">Trinovantes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cornovii_(Midlands)" title="Cornovii (Midlands)">Cornovii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Durotriges" title="Durotriges">Durotriges</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britons_(Celtic_people)" title="Britons (Celtic people)">Celtic Britons</a>. The Iceni and Trinovantes first destroy the Roman capital <a href="/wiki/Camulodunum" title="Camulodunum">Camulodunum</a> (<a href="/wiki/Colchester" title="Colchester">Colchester</a>), wipe out the infantry of the <a href="/wiki/Legio_IX_Hispana" title="Legio IX Hispana">Legio IX Hispana</a> (commanded by <a href="/wiki/Quintus_Petillius_Cerialis" title="Quintus Petillius Cerialis">Quintus Petillius Cerialis</a>) and go on to burn <a href="/wiki/Londinium" title="Londinium">Londinium</a> (London) (probably destroying <a href="/wiki/London_Bridge" title="London Bridge">London Bridge</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Verulamium" title="Verulamium">Verulamium</a> (<a href="/wiki/St_Albans" title="St Albans">St Albans</a>), in all cases massacring the inhabitants in thousands.</li>
<li>Paulinus defeats the rebels at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Watling_Street" title="Battle of Watling Street">Battle of Watling Street</a> using a <a href="/wiki/Flying_wedge" title="Flying wedge">flying wedge</a> formation, and imposes wide-ranging punishments on native Britons, but is removed from office after an enquiry instituted by <a href="/wiki/Gaius_Julius_Alpinus_Classicianus" title="Gaius Julius Alpinus Classicianus">Gaius Julius Alpinus Classicianus</a> (appointed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Procurator_(Roman)" title="Procurator (Roman)">procurator</a> 61)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBH-1">[1]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">Romanization of Britain</a> continues. <a href="/wiki/Boudica" title="Boudica">Boudica</a> either poisons herself<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> or falls sick and dies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
