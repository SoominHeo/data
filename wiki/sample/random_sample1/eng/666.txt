<p>Year <b>623</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCXXIII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Common year starting on Saturday">common year starting on Saturday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 623 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>

<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byzantine%E2%80%93Sassanid_War_of_602%E2%80%93628" title="Byzantine–Sassanid War of 602–628">Byzantine–Sassanid War</a>: Emperor <a href="/wiki/Heraclius" title="Heraclius">Heraclius</a> invades <a href="/wiki/Medieval_Armenia" title="Medieval Armenia">Armenia</a>, leaving his son <a href="/wiki/Constantine_III_(Byzantine_emperor)" title="Constantine III (Byzantine emperor)">Constantine</a> (age 11) and co-regent <a href="/wiki/Bonus_(patrician)" title="Bonus (patrician)">Bonus</a> to defend <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sassanid_Empire" title="Sassanid Empire">Persians</a> still at <a href="/wiki/Chalcedon" title="Chalcedon">Chalcedon</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>). <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=623&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clothar_II" title="Clothar II">Clothar II</a> gives <a href="/wiki/Austrasia" title="Austrasia">Austrasia</a> to his son <a href="/wiki/Dagobert_I" title="Dagobert I">Dagobert I</a>, age 20, effectively granting the kingdom semi-<a href="/wiki/Autonomy" title="Autonomy">autonomy</a> in repayment for the support of its <a href="/wiki/Nobility" title="Nobility">nobles</a>, most notably <a href="/wiki/Pepin_of_Landen" title="Pepin of Landen">Pepin of Landen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_the_Palace" title="Mayor of the Palace">Mayor of the Palace</a>), and in recognition of calls from the Austrasians for a king of their own. <a href="/wiki/Arnulf_of_Metz" title="Arnulf of Metz">Arnulf</a>, bishop of <a href="/wiki/Metz" title="Metz">Metz</a>, becomes advisor to Dagobert.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Samo" title="Samo">Samo</a>, reputedly a <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Frankish</a> merchant, is elected king of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slavic_peoples" title="Slavic peoples">Slavs</a> in <a href="/wiki/Moravia" title="Moravia">Moravia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lower_Austria" title="Lower Austria">Lower Austria</a>. A string of victories over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avars_(Carpathians)" title="Avars (Carpathians)">Avars</a> proves his <i>utilitas</i> (usefulness) to his subjects, and he secures the <a href="/wiki/Throne" title="Throne">throne</a> to establish his own kingdom, which stretch from the upper <a href="/wiki/Elbe" title="Elbe">Elbe</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tuyuhun_invasion_of_Gansu" title="Tuyuhun invasion of Gansu">Tuyuhun invasion of Gansu</a>: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang</a> forces under <a href="/wiki/Chai_Shao" title="Chai Shao">Chai Shao</a> defeat the <a href="/wiki/Tuyuhun" title="Tuyuhun">Tuyuhun</a>, and prevent further incursions in <a href="/wiki/Gansu" title="Gansu">Gansu</a> (<a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>).</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=623&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jewish</a> community in <a href="/wiki/Medina" title="Medina">Medina</a> (<a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>) rejects the idea of <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a> being a leader of <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Judaism</a>. He and his followers stop <a href="/wiki/Bowing" title="Bowing">bowing</a> toward <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> and start bowing toward <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ka%27ba" title="Ka'ba">Ka'ba</a>. Muhammad abandons <a href="/wiki/Saturday" title="Saturday">Saturday</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Sabbath" title="Sabbath">Sabbath</a>.</li>
</ul>
