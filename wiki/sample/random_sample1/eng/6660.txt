<p>The <b>Burgundian Wars</b> (1474–1477) were a conflict between the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Burgundy" title="Duchy of Burgundy">Dukes of Burgundy</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Old_Swiss_Confederacy" title="Old Swiss Confederacy">Old Swiss Confederacy</a> and its allies. Open war broke out in 1474, and in the following years the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Burgundy" title="Duke of Burgundy">Duke of Burgundy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_the_Bold" title="Charles the Bold">Charles the Bold</a>, was defeated three times on the battlefield and killed in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nancy" title="Battle of Nancy">Battle of Nancy</a> in 1477. The Duchy of Burgundy and several other Burgundian lands then became part of France, while the <a href="/wiki/Burgundian_Netherlands" title="Burgundian Netherlands">Burgundian Netherlands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Franche-Comt%C3%A9" title="Franche-Comté">Franche-Comté</a> were inherited by Charles's daughter <a href="/wiki/Mary_of_Burgundy" title="Mary of Burgundy">Mary of Burgundy</a>, and eventually passed to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg" title="House of Habsburg">House of Habsburg</a> upon her death because of her marriage to <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor">Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_situation">General situation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burgundian_Wars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: General situation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Karte_Haus_Burgund_4_EN.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1690" data-file-width="1200" height="282" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Karte_Haus_Burgund_4_EN.png/200px-Karte_Haus_Burgund_4_EN.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Karte_Haus_Burgund_4_EN.png/300px-Karte_Haus_Burgund_4_EN.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Karte_Haus_Burgund_4_EN.png/400px-Karte_Haus_Burgund_4_EN.png 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Karte_Haus_Burgund_4_EN.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Territories of the house of Valois-Burgundy during the reign of Charles the Bold</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/House_of_Valois#List_of_Valois_Dukes_and_Duchesses_of_Burgundy" title="House of Valois">dukes of Burgundy</a> had succeeded, over a period of about 100 years, in establishing their rule as a strong force between the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> and France. Their possessions included, besides their original territories of the <a href="/wiki/Franche-Comt%C3%A9" title="Franche-Comté">Franche-Comté</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Burgundy" title="Duke of Burgundy">Duchy of Burgundy</a>, the economically strong regions of <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brabant_(province)" title="Brabant (province)">Brabant</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>.</p>
<p>The dukes of Burgundy generally pursued an aggressive expansionist politics, especially in <a href="/wiki/Alsace" title="Alsace">Alsace</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lorraine_(province)" title="Lorraine (province)">Lorraine</a>, seeking to geographically unite their northern and southern possessions. Having already been in conflict with the French king (Burgundy had sided with the English in the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Years%27_War" title="Hundred Years' War">Hundred Years' War</a>), Charles' advances along the Rhine brought him in conflict with the Habsburgs and especially emperor <a href="/wiki/Frederick_III,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Frederick III, Holy Roman Emperor">Frederick III</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conflict">Conflict</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burgundian_Wars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conflict">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Charles_the_Bold_1460.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1356" data-file-width="900" height="301" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Charles_the_Bold_1460.jpg/200px-Charles_the_Bold_1460.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Charles_the_Bold_1460.jpg/300px-Charles_the_Bold_1460.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Charles_the_Bold_1460.jpg/400px-Charles_the_Bold_1460.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Charles_the_Bold_1460.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Charles_the_Bold" title="Charles the Bold">Charles the Bold</a>, a contemporary portrait by <a href="/wiki/Rogier_van_der_Weyden" title="Rogier van der Weyden">Rogier van der Weyden</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Initially in 1469, Duke <a href="/wiki/Sigismund,_Archduke_of_Austria" title="Sigismund, Archduke of Austria">Sigismund of Habsburg</a> of Austria assigned his possessions in the <a href="/wiki/Alsace" title="Alsace">Alsace</a> as a fiefdom to the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Burgundy" title="Duke of Burgundy">Duke of Burgundy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_the_Bold" title="Charles the Bold">Charles the Bold</a>, to have them protected better against the expansion of the <i><a href="/wiki/Eidgenossenschaft" title="Eidgenossenschaft">Eidgenossen</a></i> (or <a href="/wiki/Old_Swiss_Confederacy" title="Old Swiss Confederacy">Old Swiss Confederacy</a>). Charles' involvement west of the Rhine gave him no reason to attack the confederates as Sigismund had wanted, but his embargo politics against the cities of <a href="/wiki/Basel" title="Basel">Basel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Strasbourg" title="Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mulhouse" title="Mulhouse">Mulhouse</a>, directed by his <a href="/wiki/Vogt" title="Vogt">reeve</a> <a href="/wiki/Peter_von_Hagenbach" title="Peter von Hagenbach">Peter von Hagenbach</a>, prompted these to turn to <a href="/wiki/Bern" title="Bern">Bern</a> for help. Charles' expansionist strategy suffered a first setback in his politics when his attack on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archbishopric_of_Cologne" title="Archbishopric of Cologne">Archbishopric of Cologne</a> failed after the unsuccessful <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Neuss" title="Siege of Neuss">Siege of Neuss</a> (1473–1474).</p>
<p>In a second phase, Sigismund sought to achieve a peace agreement with the Swiss confederates, which eventually was concluded in <a href="/wiki/Konstanz" title="Konstanz">Konstanz</a> in 1474 (later called the <i>Ewige Richtung</i>). He wanted to buy back his Alsace possessions from Charles, which the latter refused. Shortly afterwards, von Hagenbach was captured and executed by decapitation in Alsace, and the Swiss, united with the Alsace cities and Sigismund of Habsburg in an "anti-Burgundian league", conquered part of the Burgundian Jura (<a href="/wiki/Franche-Comt%C3%A9" title="Franche-Comté">Franche-Comté</a>) when they won the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_H%C3%A9ricourt" title="Battle of Héricourt">Battle of Héricourt</a> in November 1474. The next year, Bernese forces conquered and ravaged <a href="/wiki/Vaud" title="Vaud">Vaud</a>, which belonged to the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Savoy" title="Duchy of Savoy">Duchy of Savoy</a>, who was allied with Charles the Bold. In the <a href="/wiki/Valais" title="Valais">Valais</a>, the independent republics of the <i>Sieben Zenden</i>, with the help of Bernese and other confederate forces, drove the Savoyards out of the lower Valais after a victory in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_on_the_Planta" title="Battle on the Planta">Battle on the Planta</a> in November 1475. In 1476 Charles retaliated and marched to <a href="/wiki/Grandson,_Switzerland" title="Grandson, Switzerland">Grandson</a>, which belonged to Pierre de Romont of Savoy, but which had recently been taken by the Swiss, where he had the garrison hanged or drowned in the lake despite their capitulation. When the Swiss confederate forces arrived a few days later, he was defeated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Grandson" title="Battle of Grandson">Battle of Grandson</a>, and he was forced to flee the battlefield, leaving behind his artillery and many provisions and valuables. Having rallied his army, he was dealt a devastating blow by the confederates in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Morat" title="Battle of Morat">Battle of Morat</a>. Charles the Bold raised a new army, but fell in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Nancy" title="Battle of Nancy">Battle of Nancy</a> in 1477, where the Swiss fought alongside an army of <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_II,_Duke_of_Lorraine" title="René II, Duke of Lorraine">René II, Duke of Lorraine</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects">Effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burgundian_Wars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Map_France_1477-en.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="970" data-file-width="900" height="237" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Map_France_1477-en.svg/220px-Map_France_1477-en.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Map_France_1477-en.svg/330px-Map_France_1477-en.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Map_France_1477-en.svg/440px-Map_France_1477-en.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Map_France_1477-en.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Burgundian territories (orange/yellow) and limits of France (red) after the Burgundian War.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>With the death of Charles the Bold, the dynasty of the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Burgundy" title="Duke of Burgundy">dukes of Burgundy</a> died out. The <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flemish</a> territories of the Dukes of Burgundy subsequently became a possession of the Habsburgs, when <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor">Archduke Maximilian of Austria</a>, who would later become <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor</a>, married Charles' only daughter <a href="/wiki/Mary_of_Burgundy" title="Mary of Burgundy">Mary of Burgundy</a>. The duchy proper reverted to the crown of France under king <a href="/wiki/Louis_XI_of_France" title="Louis XI of France">Louis XI</a>. The Franche-Comté initially also became French, but was ceded to Maximilian's son <a href="/wiki/Philip_I_of_Castile" title="Philip I of Castile">Philip</a> in 1493 by the French king <a href="/wiki/Charles_VIII_of_France" title="Charles VIII of France">Charles VIII</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Senlis" title="Treaty of Senlis">treaty of Senlis</a>, in an attempt to bribe the Emperor to remain neutral during Charles's planned invasion of Italy.</p>
<p>The victories of the <i>Eidgenossen</i> (Swiss Confederation) over one of the most powerful military forces in Europe at the time gained them a reputation of near invincibility<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, and the Burgundian Wars marked the beginning of the rise of <a href="/wiki/Swiss_mercenaries" title="Swiss mercenaries">Swiss mercenaries</a> on the battlefields of Europe<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Inside the Confederacy itself, however, the outcome of the war did lead to internal conflict when the city cantons insisted on having the lion's share of the proceeds since they had supplied the most troops. The country cantons resented this and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dreizehn_Orte" title="Dreizehn Orte">Dreizehn Orte</a> disputes almost led to war. They were settled by the <a href="/wiki/Stanser_Verkommnis" title="Stanser Verkommnis">Stanser Verkommnis</a> of 1481.</p>
