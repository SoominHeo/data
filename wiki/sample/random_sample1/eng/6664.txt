<p><b>Bursaray</b> (sometimes stylized as <i>BursaRay</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit</a> system in the city of <a href="/wiki/Bursa" title="Bursa">Bursa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marmara_Region" title="Marmara Region">Marmara Region</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, built in 2000 by <a href="/wiki/T%C3%9CVASA%C5%9E" title="TÃVASAÅ">TÃVASAÅ</a>, and operated by BurulaÅ. The name <i>Bursaray</i> is a portmanteau of <i>Bursa</i>, and <i>Ray</i>, the Turkish word for "rail". The Bursaray metro opened for passenger service on 24 April 2002. The metro system presently consists of two lines, which share a main line in the east, and branch into two lines at the western end of the system.</p>
<p>It is planned to expand the Bursaray system to a 50-kilometer (31Â mi) network in the future.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bursaray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Bursaray system is made up of two lines that operate on 38.9 kilometers (24.2Â mi) of route, serving 38 stations (7 of which are underground stations).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-route_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-route-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-specs_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-specs-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The track gauge is similar to all Turkish metro systems (such as in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Metro" title="Istanbul Metro">Istanbul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ankara_Metro" title="Ankara Metro">Ankara</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C4%B0zmir_Metro" title="Ä°zmir Metro">Izmir</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adana_Metro" title="Adana Metro">Adana</a>); the entire network consists of standard gauge rail of <span class="nowrap">1,435Â mm</span> (<span class="nowrap">4Â ftÂ <span class="frac">8<span class="visualhide">Â </span><sup>1</sup>â<sub>2</sub></span>Â in</span>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-specs_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-specs-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Station platforms are 120 meters (390Â ft) long, and 92 centimeters (3.02Â ft) high; most stations use <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platforms</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-specs_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-specs-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rolling_stock">Rolling stock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bursaray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rolling stock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A total of 48 cars of the B80 type (27.7-meter (91Â ft) long) were delivered by <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RayHaber_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-RayHaber-2">[2]</a></sup> (in collaboration with the local company <a href="/wiki/T%C3%9CVASA%C5%9E" title="TÃVASAÅ">TÃVASAÅ</a>). Siemens TS supplied the signalling automation systems and the power supply system.</p>
<p>Later the fleet was enlarged with 30 <a href="/wiki/Bombardier_Transportation" title="Bombardier Transportation">Bombardier</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RayHaber_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-RayHaber-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Flexity_Swift" title="Flexity Swift">Flexity Swift</a> light rail cars (similar to those on the <a href="/wiki/U5_(Frankfurt_U-Bahn)" title="U5 (Frankfurt U-Bahn)">U5 line</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_U-Bahn" title="Frankfurt U-Bahn">Frankfurt U-Bahn</a>) from Bombardier. After that, Burulas purchased 44 used 'Sneltram' <a href="/wiki/D%C3%9CWAG" title="DÃWAG">DÃWAG</a> SG2 (Duewag T-cars) from Rotterdam;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RayHaber_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-RayHaber-2">[2]</a></sup> 25 of which were placed in service while the other 19 were used to provide spare parts as needed.</p>
<p>All the system's cars are air-conditioned. On Line 1 only units of three-car trains are used; four-car trains are used on Line 2.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
