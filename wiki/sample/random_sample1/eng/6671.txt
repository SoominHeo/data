<p><b>Burim Kukeli</b> (born 16 January 1984) is an <a href="/wiki/Albanians" title="Albanians">Albanian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Midfielder_(association_football)" title="Midfielder (association football)">midfielder</a> for Swiss club <a href="/wiki/FC_Z%C3%BCrich" title="FC Zürich">FC Zürich</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania national team</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kukeli was born in <a href="/wiki/Gjakova" title="Gjakova">Gjakova</a>, <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Autonomous_Province_of_Kosovo" title="Socialist Autonomous Province of Kosovo">SAP Kosovo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Republic_of_Serbia" title="Socialist Republic of Serbia">SR Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">SFR Yugoslavia</a>, but moved to <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> at the age of four following his father road along with other part of his family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ekskluzive:_Burim_Kukeli_aSport.al_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ekskluzive:_Burim_Kukeli_aSport.al-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He started his youth career at age of 9 with the <a href="/wiki/FC_Solothurn" title="FC Solothurn">Solothurn</a> youth academies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ekskluzive:_Burim_Kukeli_aSport.al_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ekskluzive:_Burim_Kukeli_aSport.al-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burim_Kukeli_sign_for_Z.C3.BCrich_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Burim_Kukeli_sign_for_Z.C3.BCrich-3">[3]</a></sup> Later he was in the squad of <a href="/wiki/FC_Wangen_bei_Olten" title="FC Wangen bei Olten">Wangen bei Olten</a> and <a href="/wiki/SC_Zofingen" title="SC Zofingen">SC Zofingen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burim_Kukeli_sign_for_Z.C3.BCrich_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Burim_Kukeli_sign_for_Z.C3.BCrich-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Zofingen">Zofingen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Zofingen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With <a href="/wiki/SC_Zofingen" title="SC Zofingen">Zofingen</a> he played 21 matches and scored 4 goals during the 2004–05 season of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1._Liga_Promotion" title="1. Liga Promotion">Swiss 3rd league</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burim_Kukeli_-_Albania_National_Football_Team_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Burim_Kukeli_-_Albania_National_Football_Team-4">[4]</a></sup> He also managed to play a full 90-minutes match, valid for the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Swiss_Cup" title="2004–05 Swiss Cup">Swiss Cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/FC_Schaffhausen" title="FC Schaffhausen">Schaffhausen</a>, finished in the 1–5 loss.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sch.C3.B6tz">Schötz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Schötz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He moved to <a href="/wiki/FC_Sch%C3%B6tz" title="FC Schötz">FC Schötz</a> during the 2005 Summer Transfers Window. In the 2005–06 season of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1._Liga_Promotion" title="1. Liga Promotion">1. Liga Promotion</a> (Swiss 3rd league) Kukeli played in total 11 matches all as a starter and in only 1 substituted off, also he scored 4 goals and where Schötz relegated to the <a href="/wiki/1._Liga_Classic" title="1. Liga Classic">1. Liga Classic</a> (Swiss 4th league) for the next season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burim_Kukeli_-_Albania_National_Football_Team_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Burim_Kukeli_-_Albania_National_Football_Team-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kukeli missed out the entire 2006–07 season of 1. Liga Classic due to a long injury. Schötz were promoted again in the 1. Liga Promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burim_Kukeli_-_Albania_National_Football_Team_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Burim_Kukeli_-_Albania_National_Football_Team-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the 2007–08 season first half Kukeli made it 14 Appearances and scored 2 goals. He played 1 match in the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Swiss_Cup" title="2007–08 Swiss Cup">Swiss cup</a> against <a href="/wiki/FC_Mendrisio-Stabio" title="FC Mendrisio-Stabio">Mendrisio</a>, which finished in the 3–1 loss, where Kukeli himself played a full 90-minutes match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burim_Kukeli_-_Albania_National_Football_Team_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Burim_Kukeli_-_Albania_National_Football_Team-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Luzern">Luzern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Luzern">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2008 Kukeli was bought from <a href="/wiki/FC_Luzern" title="FC Luzern">Luzern</a> during the Winter Transfers Window for the second half of the 2007–08 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burim_Kukeli_sign_for_Z.C3.BCrich_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Burim_Kukeli_sign_for_Z.C3.BCrich-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kukeli made his first professional debut at age of 23 with Luzern on <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Super_League" title="Swiss Super League">Swiss Super League</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Swiss_Super_League" title="2007–08 Swiss Super League">2007–08 season</a>. He finished his first season with Luzern with 10 matches played.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burim_Kukeli_-_Albania_National_Football_Team_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Burim_Kukeli_-_Albania_National_Football_Team-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>He suffered an injury which kept him out of play from the first half of the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Swiss_Super_League" title="2008–09 Swiss Super League">2008–09 season</a>. He played first match of the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Swiss_Super_League" title="2008–09 Swiss Super League">2008–09 season</a> only in the second half when he came in as a substitute in the 85th minute for <a href="/wiki/Davide_Chiumiento" title="Davide Chiumiento">Davide Chiumiento</a> in the 2–3 loss against <a href="/wiki/BSC_Young_Boys" title="BSC Young Boys">Young Boys</a> on 15 February 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The second and last match of that season came on 30 May 2009 when he played as a starter in the 2–1 victory against <a href="/wiki/Neuch%C3%A2tel_Xamax" title="Neuchâtel Xamax">Neuchâtel Xamax</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Z.C3.BCrich">Zürich</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Zürich">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 April 2012 it was announced that Kukeli had agreed to join his former <a href="/wiki/FC_Luzern" title="FC Luzern">FCL</a> coach <a href="/wiki/Rolf_Fringer" title="Rolf Fringer">Rolf Fringer</a> at <a href="/wiki/FC_Z%C3%BCrich" title="FC Zürich">FC Zürich</a> in the summer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Kukeli signed for Zürich a three-year contract until June 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burim_Kukeli_sign_for_Z.C3.BCrich_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Burim_Kukeli_sign_for_Z.C3.BCrich-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>He made his first debut for Zürich on 15 July 2012 against exactly his latest club Luzern, in the opening match of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Swiss_Super_League" title="2012–13 Swiss Super League">2012–13 season</a>, which finished in the 0–0 away draw and Kukeli played the full 90-minute match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He played 12 matches for the entire 2012–13 season's first half and all as a starter where substituted off only in 1 match.</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Long-time_injury">Long-time injury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Long-time injury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 3 February 2013, Zürich were playing a test match against <a href="/wiki/AC_Bellinzona" title="AC Bellinzona">AC Bellinzona</a> and in to the 72nd minute of the game, it came to a battle between Kukeli and <a href="/wiki/Simon_Grether" title="Simon Grether">Simon Grether</a>, were Kukeli injured with a tibia and fibula fracture in the leg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It was named as a "horrorr" injury were his leg clearly seen with the naked eye that nearly broke away from other part and went completely in the reverse side. Player slammed into the ground, and colleagues, technical staffs of both teams, as well as over 400 fans in the stadium were shocked and the referee stopped the game immediately. After the intervention of doctors were urgently called ambulances. After about 5 minutes on the field, Kukeli stretcher was taken initially and then was transported to the hospital. After this serious incident, none of the players was not able to play, so the referee rightly canceled it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On 19 February 2013 medical doctors announced that Kukeli will remain 9 months out of the pitch until his recovery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2014, with upcoming of a fellow Albanian player in Zürich, <a href="/wiki/Armando_Sadiku" title="Armando Sadiku">Armando Sadiku</a>, Kukeli returned to the team training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> His first match after recovery from the long injury came on 14 January 2014, in a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/MSV_Duisburg" title="MSV Duisburg">MSV Duisburg</a> but there were shown that he was still unrecovered from the injury, as he got replaced in the first half of the match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In the next month February, Kukeli declared that he was returned at this time to the full-training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2014, media reported that a wrong medication in his leg caused him an infection and he was operated again for the second time and on 28 April when he was ready to come on the pitch, doctors suggested him to undergo another operation and he came to be operated again for the 3rd time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Due to the injury Kukeli did not played any match and even called up for the team fixtures, missing the 2012–13 season second half and also the entire 2013–14 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_from_the_long_injury">Return from the long injury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Return from the long injury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 11 June 2014 the Zürich coach <a href="/wiki/Urs_Meier_(footballer)" title="Urs Meier (footballer)">Urs Meier</a> declared that Kukeli trained again fully with the team among his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Nef" title="Alain Nef">Alain Nef</a>, also returned from the injury, also they were back on track as the two veterans recount their long injury lay-offs for us almost as inflows.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On 14 June 2014 Kukeli played in the first pre-season test match of the Zürich against <a href="/wiki/FC_Vaduz" title="FC Vaduz">Vaduz</a> coming on as a substitute for the second half in place of <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Teixeira" title="Jorge Teixeira">Ederson</a>, where the test match finished in the 1–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> On 24 June 2014 Kukeli played as a starter in another test match against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Austria_Lustenau" title="Austria Lustenau">Austria Lustenau</a> finished in the 0–2 victory and where Kukeli played only in the first half as he got substituted off for Yapi in the second half.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014.E2.80.9315_season">2014–15 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2014–15 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Kukeli played his first match after the long-time injury on 20 July 2014 in the opening match of the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Swiss_Super_League" title="2014–15 Swiss Super League">2014–15 season</a> against <a href="/wiki/Grasshopper_Club_Z%C3%BCrich" title="Grasshopper Club Zürich">Grasshopper</a> finished in the 1–0 victory for Zürich, where Kukeli himself gave the assist for the victory goal scored by <a href="/wiki/Davide_Chiumiento" title="Davide Chiumiento">Davide Chiumiento</a>, also he registered a full 90-minutes match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> He played another 90-minutes full match on 23 July 2014 in the next fixture match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Thun" title="FC Thun">Thun</a>, finished in the 2–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>After a short time from the recovery, Kukeli injured once again during the match between <a href="/wiki/FC_Basel" title="FC Basel">Basel</a> and Zürich (4–1) where according to magnetic resonance was assigned diagnosis that he damaged legs' muscle and will recover in 3 weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> He returned soon from the injury and played as a starter in the 0–2 victory on 31 August 2014 against <a href="/wiki/FC_St._Gallen" title="FC St. Gallen">St. Gallen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Kukeli scored his first goal for Zürich on 5 October 2014 in the 11th game week of the 2014–15 season against <a href="/wiki/FC_Vaduz" title="FC Vaduz">Vaduz</a>, where he scored the opening goal in the 5th minute, a goal which successed by a double-goal from <a href="/wiki/Marco_Sch%C3%B6nb%C3%A4chler" title="Marco Schönbächler">Marco Schönbächler</a> in the 15th and 24th minutes to fix the result in the 3–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kukeli received an invitation from <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania</a>'s coach, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gianni_de_Biasi" title="Gianni de Biasi">Gianni de Biasi</a> in May 2012 for the friendlies against <a href="/wiki/Qatar_national_football_team" title="Qatar national football team">Qatar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iran_national_football_team" title="Iran national football team">Iran</a>, but he declined because he was too tired at that time and needed rest after the end of the season, but however he said that he would respond positively in the future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SportKosova1_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-SportKosova1-25">[25]</a></sup> On 24 August 2012, it was reported that the Albanian government had started the procedures to give him an Albanian citizenship by the end of August 2012, so that he could play for Albania in the qualifiers of the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(UEFA)" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification (UEFA)">2014 FIFA World Cup qualification</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KohaNet_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-KohaNet-26">[26]</a></sup> He received the Albanian citizenship on 4 September 2012 among with fellow Albanian descent <a href="/wiki/Alban_Meha" title="Alban Meha">Alban Meha</a> to be eligible to play for Albania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Debut_.26_FIFA_World_Cup_2014_qualifiers">Debut &amp; FIFA World Cup 2014 qualifiers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Debut &amp; FIFA World Cup 2014 qualifiers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kukeli made it his debut by playing 90 minutes in a 3–1 victory against Cyprus on 7 September 2012, match valid for the opening of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_-_UEFA_Group_E" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification - UEFA Group E">2014 FIFA World Cup qualifiers - Group E</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-panoramasport9.2F7.2F12_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-panoramasport9.2F7.2F12-28">[28]</a></sup> He played another 3 matches in a row, even valids for the 2014 FIFA World Cup qualifiers - Group E in September and October months and also all as a starter,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> where the only match which he substituted off was in the 2–0 loss against <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a> 4 days after his debut, on 15 October where his place was left for <a href="/wiki/Jahmir_Hyka" title="Jahmir Hyka">Jahmir Hyka</a> in the 72nd minute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Then due to his long-time injury at Zürich, he missed out other matches of the 2014 FIFA World Cup qualifiers - Group E, where was and 6 matches left from 10 in total.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifiers">UEFA Euro 2016 qualifiers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: UEFA Euro 2016 qualifiers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After 2 years he returned to the national team after the injures, as he called up to play against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a> on 7 September 2014, match valid for the start of another competition where Albania participates, the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying_Group_I" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying Group I">UEFA Euro 2016 qualifiers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> He played for Albania after the long injury on 7 September 2014 in the opening match of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016_qualifying_Group_I" title="UEFA Euro 2016 qualifying Group I">UEFA Euro 2016 qualifiers</a> against <a href="/wiki/Portugal_national_football_team" title="Portugal national football team">Portugal</a>, where he played as a starter and substituted off for <a href="/wiki/Ergys_Ka%C3%A7e" title="Ergys Kaçe">Ergys Kaçe</a> in the 66th minute as the match finished in the away 0–1 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Euro_2016">Euro 2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Burim_Kukeli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Euro 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 May 2016, Kukeli was named in Albania's preliminary 27-man squad for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2016" title="UEFA Euro 2016">UEFA Euro 2016</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and in Albania's final 23-man UEFA Euro 2016 squad on 31 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kukeli played as a starter in 2 first matches against <a href="/wiki/Switzerland_national_football_team" title="Switzerland national football team">Switzerland</a> in a 0–1 loss where he played the full 90-minutes match and hosts <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a> where they lost 2–0. He received a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_card#Yellow_card" title="Penalty card">yellow card</a> in each match and wasn't unable then to play in the next match against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a> where Albania took a historical 1–0 win with a goal scored by <a href="/wiki/Armando_Sadiku" title="Armando Sadiku">Armando Sadiku</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Albania finished the group in the third position with three points and with a goal difference –2, and was ranked last in the third-placed teams, which eventually eliminated them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
