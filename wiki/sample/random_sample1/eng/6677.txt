<p><b>Bouthaina Shaaban</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">بثينة شعبان</span>‎‎) (born 1953) is a Syrian politician and is currently the political and media adviser to the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Syria" title="President of Syria">President of Syria</a>. Shaaban served as the first <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Expatriates_(Syria)" title="Ministry of Expatriates (Syria)">Minister of Expatriates</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syrian_Arab_Republic" title="Syrian Arab Republic">Syrian Arab Republic</a>, between 2003 and 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-B_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-B-1">[1]</a></sup> and has been described as the Syrian government's face to the outside world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wright-p.250_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-wright-p.250-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_education">Life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bouthaina_Shaaban&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Homs" title="Homs">Homs</a> and a member of the <a href="/wiki/Arab_Socialist_Ba%27ath_Party_%E2%80%93_Syria_Region" title="Arab Socialist Ba'ath Party – Syria Region">Syrian Regional Branch</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ba%27ath_Party_(Syrian-dominated_faction)" title="Ba'ath Party (Syrian-dominated faction)">Arab Socialist Ba'ath Party</a> since the age of 16, she was educated in <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Britain</a> and obtained her Ph.D. in <a href="/wiki/English_literature" title="English literature">English literature</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Warwick" title="University of Warwick">University of Warwick</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bar_S.444_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bar_S.444-3">[3]</a></sup> She is married with two daughters and a son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-B_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-B-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bouthaina_Shaaban&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shaaban worked first as an interpreter for the Syrian presidents <a href="/wiki/Hafez_al-Assad" title="Hafez al-Assad">Hafez al-Assad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bashar_al-Assad" title="Bashar al-Assad">Bashar al-Assad</a>, his son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wright-p.250_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-wright-p.250-2">[2]</a></sup> Under Hafez she became an "adviser to the Foreign Ministry,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wright-p.250_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-wright-p.250-2">[2]</a></sup> and in 2003 she was named Minister of Expatriates, "a new post created to try to lure wealthy Syrian expatriates abroad — or at least their resources — back home."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wright-p.250_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-wright-p.250-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2008, she was appointed political and media adviser to president <a href="/wiki/Bashar_al-Assad" title="Bashar al-Assad">Bashar al-Assad</a>. Between 1985 and 2003 she was also the professor of <a href="/wiki/Romantic_poetry" title="Romantic poetry">Romantic poetry</a> at the English department of <a href="/wiki/Damascus_University" title="Damascus University">Damascus University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-B_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-B-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shaaban was particularly visible in English-speaking media after the Valentine's Day 2005 assassination of former Lebanese Prime Minister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rafiq_Hariri" title="Rafiq Hariri">Rafiq Hariri</a>, when she participated in several television interviews and wrote several op-ed pieces attacking the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> probe into Syrian involvement in the murder and insisted that <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> were responsible for Hariri's assassination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wright-p.252_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-wright-p.252-4">[4]</a></sup> In August 2011, the US sanctioned Shaaban together with other five other Syrian officials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>She is the author of "Both Right and Left Handed: Arab Women Talk About Their Lives" (1988). A book composed mostly of interviews with Syrian, Lebanese, Palestinian, and Algerian women, Shaaban invites them to talk openly about their lives and the roles of women in their societies, how they feel they've changed through different times of war and crisis, and what they think the future holds for Arab women. She represents the feminist perspective of Syrian politics.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Connections_to_Syrian_leadership">Connections to Syrian leadership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bouthaina_Shaaban&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Connections to Syrian leadership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shaaban's rise within the Syrian government is due to her close friendship with <a href="/wiki/Bushra_al-Assad" title="Bushra al-Assad">Bushra Al-Assad</a>. Sometime in the late 1980s, Shaaban also introduced Bushra to her future husband <a href="/wiki/Assef_Shawkat" title="Assef Shawkat">Assef Shawkat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honors">Honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bouthaina_Shaaban&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2005 Shaaban was presented with "the Most Distinguished Woman in a Governmental Position" award by the <a href="/wiki/Arab_League" title="Arab League">Arab League</a>.</p>
