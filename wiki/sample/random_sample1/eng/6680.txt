<p><b>Busan International High School</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: <span lang="ko-Hang" xml:lang="ko-Hang">부산국제고등학교</span>; <a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a>: <span lang="ko-Hani" xml:lang="ko-Hani">釜山國際高等學校</span>) is a prominent high school located in <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> specializing in the <a href="/wiki/Humanities" title="Humanities">humanities</a> &amp; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_Science" title="Social Science">social science</a>. Busan International High School is a co-educational public high school, opened in 1997 for the purpose of preparing students as experts in international relations. Schools for this purpose are called "International High Schools," and Busan International High School is one of them along with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cheongshim_International_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cheongshim International High School (page does not exist)">Cheongshim International High School</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Seoul_International_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Seoul International High School (page does not exist)">Seoul International High School</a>. These schools are classified as "Special Purpose High Schools" (<i>teuksu mokjeok godeung hakgyo</i> 특수목적고등학교).</p>
<p>Unlike <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Foreign_Language_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Foreign Language High School (page does not exist)">Foreign Language High Schools</a>, students at Busan International High School are not divided into classes based on what language they major in. Instead, they are distributed randomly into eight classes in each year, and select their second foreign language among <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, and <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>.</p>
<p>Middle school grades, and interviews along with future scholastic plans are taken into consideration in order to select 160 students per year. The 160 students are then divided into eight classes, which consist of roughly twenty students each. Almost all of the students lives in the <a href="/wiki/Dormitory" title="Dormitory">dormitory</a> and they go home only on friday. The reputation of the school in Busan is relatively high, and many students choose the school for the reason that they can study with excellent students. Busan International high school has ranked 9th place in the Korean SAT scores nationwide.(based on 2015 records)</p>
<p>Every freshmen in the school has to choose a club to join, in which it will last for the 3 years of their high school years.</p>
<p>The school is located in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Baegyang_Mountain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Baegyang Mountain (page does not exist)">Baegyang Mountain</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Danggam-dong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Danggam-dong (page does not exist)">Danggam-dong</a>.</p>
