<p><b>Bussero</b> is a suburban station on <a href="/wiki/Milan_Metro_Line_2" title="Milan Metro Line 2">Line 2</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Milan_Metro" title="Milan Metro">Milan Metro</a> in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Bussero" title="Bussero">the same name</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bussero_(Milan_Metro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station was opened in 1968, as a stop on <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>-<a href="/wiki/Gorgonzola,_Milan" title="Gorgonzola, Milan">Gorgonzola</a> fast tramway line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was one of four stations not included in the original project (together with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cascina_Burrona" title="Cascina Burrona">Cascina Burrona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Villa_Fiorita_(Milan_Metro)" title="Villa Fiorita (Milan Metro)">Villa Fiorita</a> and <a href="/wiki/Villa_Pompea_(Milan_Metro)" title="Villa Pompea (Milan Metro)">Villa Pompea</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Since 4 December 1972 the section from <a href="/wiki/Cascina_Gobba_(Milan_Metro)" title="Cascina Gobba (Milan Metro)">Cascina Gobba</a> to <a href="/wiki/Gorgonzola_(Milan_Metro)" title="Gorgonzola (Milan Metro)">Gorgonzola</a>, where this station is located, was connected to <a href="/wiki/Milan_Metro_Line_2" title="Milan Metro Line 2">Milan Metro Line 2</a> and operates as part of it ever since.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_structure">Station structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bussero_(Milan_Metro)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station has two concrete platforms, covered with translucent panels, and two tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Passenger building is located at the northern end of the platforms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The station is built entirely of prefabricated elements and its structure is similar to that of the stations of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cascina_Burrona" title="Cascina Burrona">Cascina Burrona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Villa_Fiorita_(Milan_Metro)" title="Villa Fiorita (Milan Metro)">Villa Fiorita</a> and <a href="/wiki/Villa_Pompea_(Milan_Metro)" title="Villa Pompea (Milan Metro)">Villa Pompea</a>, constructed at the same time.</p>
