<p>The <b>adrenal medulla</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <span lang="la" xml:lang="la"><i>medulla glandulae suprarenalis</i></span>) is part of the <a href="/wiki/Adrenal_gland" title="Adrenal gland">adrenal gland</a>. It is located at the center of the gland, being surrounded by the <a href="/wiki/Adrenal_cortex" title="Adrenal cortex">adrenal cortex</a>. It is the innermost part of the adrenal gland, consisting of cells that secrete <a href="/wiki/Epinephrine" title="Epinephrine">epinephrine</a> (adrenaline), <a href="/wiki/Norepinephrine" title="Norepinephrine">norepinephrine</a> (noradrenaline), and a small amount of <a href="/wiki/Dopamine" title="Dopamine">dopamine</a> in response to stimulation by <a href="/wiki/Sympathetic_nervous_system" title="Sympathetic nervous system">sympathetic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preganglionic_neurons" title="Preganglionic neurons">preganglionic neurons</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Basic">Basic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adrenal_medulla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Basic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The adrenal medulla consists of irregularly shaped cells grouped around blood vessels. These cells are intimately connected with the sympathetic division of the autonomic nervous system (ANS). In fact, these adrenal medullary cells are modified postganglionic neurons, and preganglionic autonomic nerve fibers lead to them directly from the central nervous system. The adrenal medulla therefore affects available energy, heart rate, and metabolism.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Function">Function</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adrenal_medulla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Function">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rather than releasing a neurotransmitter, the cells of the adrenal medulla secrete <a href="/wiki/Hormone" title="Hormone">hormones</a>.</p>
<p>Composed mainly of hormone-producing <a href="/wiki/Chromaffin_cell" title="Chromaffin cell">chromaffin cells</a>, the adrenal medulla is the principal site of the conversion of the <a href="/wiki/Amino_acid" title="Amino acid">amino acid</a> <a href="/wiki/Tyrosine" title="Tyrosine">tyrosine</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Catecholamine" title="Catecholamine">catecholamines</a> epinephrine, norepinephrine, and <a href="/wiki/Dopamine" title="Dopamine">dopamine</a>.</p>
<p>Because the ANS, specifically the sympathetic division, exerts direct control over the chromaffin cells the hormone release can occur rather quickly. In response to <a href="/wiki/Stressor" title="Stressor">stressors</a> such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exercise" title="Exercise">exercise</a> or imminent danger, medullary cells release the catecholamines adrenaline and noradrenaline into the blood. Adrenaline composes about 85% of the released catecholamines, and noradrenaline the other 15%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Notable effects of adrenaline and noradrenaline include increased <a href="/wiki/Heart_rate" title="Heart rate">heart rate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blood_pressure" title="Blood pressure">blood pressure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vasoconstriction" title="Vasoconstriction">blood vessel constriction</a> in the skin and gastrointestinal tract, smooth muscle (<a href="/wiki/Bronchiole" title="Bronchiole">bronchiole</a> and capillary) dilation, and increased <a href="/wiki/Metabolism" title="Metabolism">metabolism</a>, all of which are characteristic of the <a href="/wiki/Fight-or-flight_response" title="Fight-or-flight response">fight-or-flight response</a>. Release of catecholamines is stimulated by nerve impulses, and receptors for catecholamines are widely distributed throughout the body.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adrenal_medulla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Medullary cells are derived from the <a href="/wiki/Embryo" title="Embryo">embryonic</a> <a href="/wiki/Neural_crest" title="Neural crest">neural crest</a> and, as such, are simply modified <a href="/wiki/Neuron" title="Neuron">neurons</a>.</p>
<p>In particular, they are modified <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postganglionic_cell" title="Postganglionic cell">postganglionic cells</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Autonomic_nervous_system" title="Autonomic nervous system">autonomic nervous system</a> that have lost their <a href="/wiki/Axon" title="Axon">axons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dendrite" title="Dendrite">dendrites</a>, receiving innervation from corresponding <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preganglionic_fiber" title="Preganglionic fiber">preganglionic fibers</a>. The cells form clusters around large blood vessels.</p>
<p>As a cluster of neuron cell bodies, the adrenal medulla is considered a <i><a href="/wiki/Autonomic_ganglion" title="Autonomic ganglion">ganglion</a></i> of the sympathetic nervous system.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pathology">Pathology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adrenal_medulla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Pathology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Neoplasms include:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fung_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-fung-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pheochromocytoma" title="Pheochromocytoma">Pheochromocytoma</a> (most common), a <a href="/wiki/Catecholamine" title="Catecholamine">catecholamine</a>-secreting tumor of the adrenal medulla</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Neuroblastoma" title="Neuroblastoma">Neuroblastoma</a>, a neuroendocrine tumor of any neural crest tissue of the sympathetic nervous system</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ganglioneuroma" title="Ganglioneuroma">Ganglioneuroma</a>, a tumor in the nerve cells of the peripheral nervous system</li>
</ul>
<p>The adrenal medulla may be poorly formed or absent in cases of <a href="/wiki/Absent_adrenal_gland" title="Absent adrenal gland">absent adrenal gland</a>. The deficiency in circulating catecholamines is mildly symptomatic due to compensation by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_nervous_system" title="Autonomous nervous system">autonomous nervous system</a>, except in episodes of <a href="/wiki/Hypoglycemia" title="Hypoglycemia">hypoglycemia</a> where <a href="/wiki/Glycogenolysis" title="Glycogenolysis">glycogenolysis</a> cannot be stimulated by circulating epinephrine .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fung_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-fung-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Dopamine_beta_hydroxylase_deficiency" title="Dopamine beta hydroxylase deficiency">dopamine beta hydroxylase deficiency</a>, the entire body cannot efficiently produce epinephrine and norepinephrine from dopamine, this results in severe <a href="/wiki/Dysautonomia" title="Dysautonomia">dysautonomia</a> but most crucially due to autonomous nervous system failure which requires epinephrine and norepinephrine as <a href="/wiki/Neurotransmitter" title="Neurotransmitter">neurotransmitters</a>, dopamine being used in this pathology as an inadequate substitute.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fung_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-fung-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
