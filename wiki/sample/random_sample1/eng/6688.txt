<p><b>Buea</b> (Bakweri | Gbea) is the capital of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southwest_Region,_Cameroon" title="Southwest Region, Cameroon">Southwest Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>. The town is located on the eastern slopes of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Cameroon" title="Mount Cameroon">Mount Cameroon</a> and has a population of 90,088 (at the 2005 Census).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldgazetteer_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-worldgazetteer-1">[1]</a></sup> About 150,000 people live in Buea (including the towns of Bokwaongo, Muea; Bomaka; Tole; Mile 16 (Bolifamba); Mile 17; Mile 15; Mile 14 (Dibanda); Bova; Bonjongo; Likombe; Buasa; and surrounding villages).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Schloss_von_Puttkamer.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="888" data-file-width="1000" height="195" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Schloss_von_Puttkamer.jpg/220px-Schloss_von_Puttkamer.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Schloss_von_Puttkamer.jpg/330px-Schloss_von_Puttkamer.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Schloss_von_Puttkamer.jpg/440px-Schloss_von_Puttkamer.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Schloss_von_Puttkamer.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Residence of <a href="/wiki/Jesko_von_Puttkamer" title="Jesko von Puttkamer">von Puttkamer</a> in Buea</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Buea, originally spelled "Gbea," was founded by Njia Tama Lifanje a hunter who came from the Bomboko Area. Coming from the Bomboko side of the Mountain, he exclaimed in amazement of the new found land "Ebe'eya" meaning literally a "place of happenings". A prominent King of the Bakweri whose headquarters is Buea was chie Kuva Likenye, whose clashes with German troops during the <a href="/wiki/Kwe_people" title="Kwe people">Bakweri</a> resistance remain popular folklore; currently ruled by the Endeleys. <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">Tea</a> growing is an important local industry, especially in Tole. Buea was the <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colonial</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_(political)" title="Capital (political)">capital</a> of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Kamerun" title="Kamerun">Kamerun</a> from 1901 to 1919, the capital of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Cameroons" title="Southern Cameroons">Southern Cameroons</a> from 1949 until 1961 and the capital of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=West_Cameroon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="West Cameroon (page does not exist)">West Cameroon</a> until 1972, when <a href="/wiki/Ahmadou_Ahidjo" title="Ahmadou Ahidjo">Ahmadou Ahidjo</a> abolished the Federation of <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>. The German colonial administration in Buea was temporarily suspended during the eruption of Mount Cameroon from 28 April – June 1909. Originally, Buea's population consisted mainly of the Bakweri people. However, due to its position as a university town and the regional capital, there are significant numbers of other ethnic groups.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_institutions">Notable institutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notable institutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Buea hosts the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Buea" title="University of Buea">University of Buea</a>, Cameroon's first <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">anglophone</a> university. It is the site of several other higher institutes of learning including St Francis schools of Nursing and Midwifery presently known as Biaka University Institute of Buea (BUIB) and one of Cameroon's three Catholic universities.</p>
<p>A handful of colonial era buildings are surviving, notably the palatial former residence of the German governor, <a href="/wiki/Jesko_von_Puttkamer" title="Jesko von Puttkamer">Jesko von Puttkamer</a>. Other German colonial buildings are still standing, but some of them suffer from lack of maintenance and old age.</p>
<p>The Nigerian Consulate in anglophone Cameroon and the main operational hub of the <a href="/wiki/Naigahelp" title="Naigahelp">Naigahelp</a> medical aid organisation are in Buea.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buea&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tourists_climbing_Mount_Cameroon.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1232" data-file-width="1632" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Tourists_climbing_Mount_Cameroon.jpg/220px-Tourists_climbing_Mount_Cameroon.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Tourists_climbing_Mount_Cameroon.jpg/330px-Tourists_climbing_Mount_Cameroon.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Tourists_climbing_Mount_Cameroon.jpg/440px-Tourists_climbing_Mount_Cameroon.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tourists_climbing_Mount_Cameroon.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Tourists climbing <a href="/wiki/Mount_Cameroon" title="Mount Cameroon">Mount Cameroon</a> in Buea</div>

<p>Because of its location at the foot of Mount Cameroon, the climate in Buea tends to be <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Humid" title="Humid">humid</a>, with the neighbourhoods at higher elevations enjoying cooler temperatures while the lower neighbourhoods experience a hotter climate. Extended periods of rainfall, characterized by incessant <a href="/wiki/Drizzle" title="Drizzle">drizzle</a>, which can last for weeks, are common during the rainy season as are damp <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">fogs</a>, rolling off the mountain into the town below.</p>
