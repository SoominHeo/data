<p><b>Booza</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: Ø§ÙØ¨ÙØ¸Ø© Ø§ÙØ¹Ø±Ø¨ÙØ©), or <b>Arabic mastic <a href="/wiki/Ice_cream" title="Ice cream">ice cream</a></b>, is an elastic, sticky, high level melt resistant ice cream, which should delay melting in the hotter climates of the <a href="/wiki/Arab_world" title="Arab world">Arab world</a>, where it is most commonly found.</p>
<p>The ice cream is usually and traditionally made with an ingredient called <a href="/wiki/Salep" title="Salep">Salep</a>, which provides it with the ability to resist melting at the speed that other regular ice creams are affected by. Salep is also a primary ingredient in the Turkish version of this style of ice cream called <i><a href="/wiki/Dondurma" title="Dondurma">Dondurma</a></i>, and the Persian version called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bastani" title="Bastani">Bastani</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Iraq">Iraq</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Booza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Iraq">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, it is customary to eat the ice cream on square wooden sticks.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Syria.2C_Lebanon_and_Israel">Syria, Lebanon and Israel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Booza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Syria, Lebanon and Israel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Booza is prevalent mainly in <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>, and Arab settlements in the <a href="/wiki/Galilee" title="Galilee">Galilee</a> region of Northern <a href="/wiki/Palestine_(region)" title="Palestine (region)">Palestine</a>, because of their proximity to the regional growth of a tree called <i><a href="/wiki/Pistacia_lentiscus" title="Pistacia lentiscus">Pistacia lentiscus</a></i>. There are gum Arabic ice cream vendors who tend to show tourists how they grind the focus ingredient, <a href="/wiki/Mastic_(plant_resin)" title="Mastic (plant resin)">mastic</a>, and pound it while singing and playing music.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_Damascus">Ancient Damascus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Booza&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ancient Damascus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the Old City of <a href="/wiki/Damascus" title="Damascus">Damascus</a>, there is a shop called "sanctuary" that is famous throughout the Arab world for its ice cream and gum Arabic. It is a popular attraction for tourists.</p>
