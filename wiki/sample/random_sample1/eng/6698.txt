<p><b>Boo Ji-Young</b> (born September 16, 1971) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> director and scriptwriter. After graduating from the Korean Academy of Film Arts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> she began her career in independent filmmaking in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>. She created her first film <i><a href="/wiki/Sisters_on_the_Road" title="Sisters on the Road">Sisters on the Road</a></i> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup> She is best known for her film <i><a href="/wiki/Cart_(film)" title="Cart (film)">Cart</a></i> (2014), which was screened at many international film festivals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_Life_and_Education">Early Life and Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boo_Ji-Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early Life and Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Boo graduated from Korean Academy of Film Arts. After that, she was the script supervisor on <a href="/wiki/E_J-yong" title="E J-yong">Lee Jae-yong</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Untold_Scandal" title="Untold Scandal">Untold Scandal</a></i> in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Boo_Ji-Young&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Boo made her first feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Sisters_on_the_Road" title="Sisters on the Road">Sisters on the Road</a></i> in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup> The film examined the thought and state of womanhood in modern South Korean society.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup> She was invited to the <a href="/wiki/Busan_International_Film_Festival" title="Busan International Film Festival">Busan International Film Festival</a>, International Women's Film Festival, and the Tokyo International Women's Film Festival to screen the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> Later, she created an experimental omnibus documentary called <i>Myselves: The Actress No Makeup Project (2012).</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup> She has also collaborated on several omnibus film projects such as the human rights project <i>If You Were Me 5</i> (2010) and the Jeonju International Film Festival's '<i>Short! Short! Short!</i> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Boo is best known for her film <i><a href="/wiki/Cart_(film)" title="Cart (film)">Cart</a></i> from 2014, based on a real events in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-:1-2">[2]</a></sup> It was screened at the Toronto International Film Festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> Boo was interested in the issues related to women and labourers in South Korea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She believes that the public should see women directors as equal to men directors, rather than categorizing women directors as "women's cinema" when they make films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
