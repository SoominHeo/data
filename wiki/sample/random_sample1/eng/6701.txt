<p><b>Fanfins</b> or <b>hairy anglerfish</b> are a family, <b>Caulophrynidae</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Anglerfish" title="Anglerfish">anglerfishes</a>. They are found in deep, lightless waters of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic">Atlantic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Oceans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>They are distinguished from other anglerfishes by the lack of the expanded <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esca_(fish_anatomy)" title="Esca (fish anatomy)">escal</a> bulb — the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bioluminescent" title="Bioluminescent">bioluminescent</a> lure at the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Illicium_(fish_anatomy)" title="Illicium (fish anatomy)">illicium</a> — and by their very long <a href="/wiki/Dorsal_fin" title="Dorsal fin">dorsal</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anal_fin" title="Anal fin">anal fin</a> rays.</p>
<p>As in other anglerfishes, males are one-tenth the size of females and, after <a href="/wiki/Larva" title="Larva">larval</a> and adolescent free-living stages, spend the rest of their lives parasitically attached to a female.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The fanfin has a small, spherical body with long protuberances.</p>
