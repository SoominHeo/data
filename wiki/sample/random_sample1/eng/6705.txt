<p>The <b>Treaty of Bucharest</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Peace_treaty" title="Peace treaty">peace treaty</a> between <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Romania" title="Kingdom of Romania">Romania</a> on one side and the <a href="/wiki/Central_Powers" title="Central Powers">Central Powers</a> on the other, following the stalemate reached after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romanian_Campaign_(World_War_I)" title="Romanian Campaign (World War I)">campaign of 1916–17</a> and Romania's isolation after Russia's unilateral exit from <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Brest-Litovsk" title="Treaty of Brest-Litovsk">Treaty of Brest-Litovsk</a>). It was signed at <a href="/wiki/Buftea" title="Buftea">Buftea</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a>, on 7 May 1918.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terms">Terms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Bucharest_(1918)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Terms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Romania had to return <a href="/wiki/Southern_Dobruja" title="Southern Dobruja">Southern Dobruja</a> (the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cadrilater" title="Cadrilater">Cadrilater</a>) and to cede the southern part of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Dobruja" title="Northern Dobruja">Northern Dobruja</a> (see the maps) to <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>, while the rest of the province remained under the joint control of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Powers" title="Central Powers">Central Powers</a>.</li>
<li>Romania had to give <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a> control of the passes of the <a href="/wiki/Carpathian_Mountains" title="Carpathian Mountains">Carpathian Mountains</a> (see the maps).</li>
<li>Romania had to lease its <a href="/wiki/Oil_well" title="Oil well">oil wells</a> to Germany for 90 years.</li>
<li>The Central Powers recognized the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Bessarabia_with_Romania" title="Union of Bessarabia with Romania">Union of Bessarabia with Romania</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>German civil servants with the power to veto decisions by Romanian cabinet ministers and to fire Romanian civil servants were appointed to oversee every Romanian ministry, in effect stripping Romania of its independence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treaty_of_Bucharest_(1918)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alexandru_Marghiloman" title="Alexandru Marghiloman">Alexandru Marghiloman</a> signed the treaty at <a href="/wiki/Buftea" title="Buftea">Buftea</a> (near Bucharest) on 7 May 1918 and it was ratified by the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_(Romania)" title="Chamber of Deputies (Romania)">Chamber of Deputies</a> on 28 June and by the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Romania" title="Senate of Romania">Senate</a> on 4 July 1918. However, King <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_I_of_Romania" title="Ferdinand I of Romania">Ferdinand I of Romania</a> refused to sign it.</p>
<p>The treaty put Romania in a unique situation compared to the other German occupied countries. It completely respected Romanian de jure independence, as it did not impose any form of vassalage or protectorate over Romania, and even though the country had to cede land, it still emerged bigger than before entering the war, thanks to the Union with Bessarabia.</p>
<p>Although Bulgaria received a part of Northern Dobruja, it continued to lobby Germany and Austria-Hungary for the annexation of the whole province, including the condominium established by the Treaty of Bucharest. After negotiations, a protocol regarding the transfer of the jointly administered zone in Northern Dobruja to Bulgaria was signed in Berlin, on 25 September 1918, by Germany, Austria-Hungary, the Ottoman Empire and Bulgaria. In compensation, Bulgaria agreed to cede the left bank of the <a href="/wiki/Maritsa" title="Maritsa">Maritsa</a> river to Turkey. However, this agreement was short-lived because after 4 days, on 29 September Bulgaria had to capitulate in the face of the advancing Allied forces (<i>see also the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armistice_with_Bulgaria_(World_War_I)" title="Armistice with Bulgaria (World War I)">Armistice with Bulgaria</a></i>).</p>
<p>The treaty was denounced in October 1918 by the Alexandru Marghiloman government and subsequently nullified by the terms of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armistice_with_Germany_(Compi%C3%A8gne)" title="Armistice with Germany (Compiègne)">Armistice</a> of 11 November 1918.</p>
<p>In 1919, Germany was forced in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Versailles" title="Treaty of Versailles">Treaty of Versailles</a> to renounce all the benefits provided by the Treaty of Bucharest in 1918.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The territorial transfers to Austria-Hungary and Bulgaria were annulled by the treaties of <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Saint-Germain-en-Laye_(1919)" title="Treaty of Saint-Germain-en-Laye (1919)">Saint-Germain</a> (1919), <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Neuilly-sur-Seine" title="Treaty of Neuilly-sur-Seine">Neuilly</a> (1919) and <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Trianon" title="Treaty of Trianon">Trianon</a> (1920), respectively.</p>
