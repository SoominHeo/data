<p><a href="/wiki/Bhutan" title="Bhutan">Bhutan</a> was founded and unified as a country by <a href="/wiki/Ngawang_Namgyal" title="Ngawang Namgyal">Ngawang Namgyal</a>, 1st <a href="/wiki/Zhabdrung_Rinpoche" title="Zhabdrung Rinpoche">Zhabdrung Rinpoche</a> in the mid–17th century. After his death in 1651, Bhutan nominally followed his recommended "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dual_system_of_government" title="Dual system of government">dual system of government</a>". Under the dual system, government control was split between a civil administrative leader, the <a href="/wiki/Druk_Desi" title="Druk Desi">Druk Desi</a> (<span lang="dz"><span style="font-family: 'Qomolangma-Dunhuang', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sarchen', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sarchung', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Suring', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sutung', 'Qomolangma-Title', 'Qomolangma-Subtitle', 'Qomolangma-Woodblock', 'DDC Uchen', Kailash, Jomolhari, 'TCRC Youtso Unicode', 'Tibetan Machine Uni', 'Noto Sans Tibetan', 'Microsoft Himalaya'; font-size:1.25em; vertical-align:top; word-wrap: break-word;">འབྲུག་སྡེ་སྲིད་</span></span>, aka <i>Deb Raja</i>);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[nb 1]</a></sup> and a religious leader, the <a href="/wiki/Je_Khenpo" title="Je Khenpo">Je Khenpo</a> (<span lang="dz"><span style="font-family: 'Qomolangma-Dunhuang', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sarchen', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sarchung', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Suring', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sutung', 'Qomolangma-Title', 'Qomolangma-Subtitle', 'Qomolangma-Woodblock', 'DDC Uchen', Kailash, Jomolhari, 'TCRC Youtso Unicode', 'Tibetan Machine Uni', 'Noto Sans Tibetan', 'Microsoft Himalaya'; font-size:1.25em; vertical-align:top; word-wrap: break-word;">རྗེ་མཁན་པོ་</span></span>).</p>
<p>Both the Druk Desi and Je Khenpo were under the nominal authority of the Zhabdrung Rinpoche, a reincarnation of Ngawang Namgyal. In practice however, the Zhabdrung was often a child under the control of the Druk Desi, and regional <a href="/wiki/Penlop" title="Penlop">penlops</a> often administered their districts in defiance of the power of the Druk Desis until the rise of the unified <a href="/wiki/House_of_Wangchuck" title="House of Wangchuck">House of Wangchuck</a> in 1907.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USLCCS1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-USLCCS1-2">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since the rise of the unified House of Wangchuk in 1907, the <a href="/wiki/Druk_Gyalpo" title="Druk Gyalpo">Druk Gyalpo</a> (<span lang="dz"><span style="font-family: 'Qomolangma-Dunhuang', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sarchen', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sarchung', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Suring', 'Qomolangma-Uchen Sutung', 'Qomolangma-Title', 'Qomolangma-Subtitle', 'Qomolangma-Woodblock', 'DDC Uchen', Kailash, Jomolhari, 'TCRC Youtso Unicode', 'Tibetan Machine Uni', 'Noto Sans Tibetan', 'Microsoft Himalaya'; font-size:1.25em; vertical-align:top; word-wrap: break-word;">འབྲུག་རྒྱལ་པོ་</span></span>; lit. "<a href="/wiki/Druk" title="Druk">Dragon</a> King") have been the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bhutan" title="Kingdom of Bhutan">Kingdom of Bhutan</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kings_of_Bhutan_.281907.E2.80.93present.29">Kings of Bhutan (1907–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_rulers_of_Bhutan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Kings of Bhutan (1907–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Druk_Gyalpo" title="Druk Gyalpo">Druk Gyalpo</a> and <a href="/wiki/House_of_Wangchuck" title="House of Wangchuck">House of Wangchuck</a></div>
<p>The Bhutanese monarchy was established in 1907, unifying the country under the control of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Wangchuk" title="House of Wangchuk">Wangchuk family</a>, hereditary <i><a href="/wiki/Penlop" title="Penlop">penlops</a></i> (governors) of <a href="/wiki/Trongsa" title="Trongsa">Trongsa</a> district. The king of Bhutan, formally known as the <a href="/wiki/Druk_Gyalpo" title="Druk Gyalpo">Druk Gyalpo</a> ("Dragon King"), also occupies the office of Druk Desi under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dual_system_of_government" title="Dual system of government">dual system of government</a>. Since the enactment of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Bhutan" title="Constitution of Bhutan">Constitution of 2008</a>, the Druk Gyalpo has remained <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a>, while the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Bhutan" title="Prime Minister of Bhutan">Prime Minister of Bhutan</a> acts as executive and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliamentary_democracy" title="Parliamentary democracy">parliamentary democracy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CoBE_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-CoBE-6">[4]</a></sup></p>
