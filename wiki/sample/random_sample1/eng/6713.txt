<p>The <b>Battle of Bukhara</b> took place in 557<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sassanid_Empire" title="Sassanid Empire">Sassanid Empire</a> allied with the <a href="/wiki/Western_Turkic_Khaganate" title="Western Turkic Khaganate">Western Turkic Khaganate</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Hephthalite_Empire" title="Hephthalite Empire">Hephthalite Empire</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Context">Context</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Bukhara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Context">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 484, <a href="/wiki/Peroz_I" title="Peroz I">Peroz I</a>, the grandfather of <a href="/wiki/Khosrow_I" title="Khosrow I">Khosrow I</a>, was killed in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Herat_(484)" title="Battle of Herat (484)">Battle of Herat (484)</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Hephthalite_Empire" title="Hephthalite Empire">Hephthalites</a> that allowed them to annex much of <a href="/wiki/Greater_Khorasan" title="Greater Khorasan">Khorasan</a> from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sassanids" title="Sassanids">Sassanids</a>.</p>
<p>After a stable peace agreement with the Byzantines in the west, Khosrow I was able to focus his attention on the Eastern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hephthalites" title="Hephthalites">Hephthalites</a> and avenge the death of his grandfather. Even with the growth of Persian military power under Khosrow's reforms, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sasanians" title="Sasanians">Sasanians</a> were still uneasy at the prospect of attacking the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hephthalite" title="Hephthalite">Hephthalites</a> on their own and sought allies. Their answer came in the form of <a href="/wiki/Turkic_peoples" title="Turkic peoples">Turkic</a> incursions into <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDrayage200930_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDrayage200930-4">[4]</a></sup> The movement of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Turkic_people" title="Turkic people">Turkic people</a> into Central Asia quickly made them natural enemies and competitors to the Hephthalites.</p>
<p>The Hephthalites possessed military power, but they lacked the organization to fight on multiple fronts. The Persians and the Turkic tribes made an alliance and launched a two pronged attack on the Hephthalites, taking advantage of their disorganization and disunity. As a result, the Turkic tribes took the territory north of the <a href="/wiki/Amu_Darya" title="Amu Darya">Oxus river</a>, while the Persians annexed the land south of the river.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFrye1983_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFrye1983-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Bukhara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Even though the Hephthalites lost control of <a href="/wiki/Transoxiana" title="Transoxiana">Transoxiana</a>, Hephthalite kingdoms remained in <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>.</p>
<p>Friendly relations between the Turks and the Persians quickly deteriorated after the conquest of the Hephthalite peoples. Both the Turks and the Persians wanted to dominate the <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a> and the trade between the west and the far east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFrye1983_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFrye1983-5">[5]</a></sup> In 568, a Turkish ambassador was sent to <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> to propose an alliance and a two-pronged attack on the Sassanian Empire. Fortunately for the Persians, nothing came from this proposal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDingasWinter2007115_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDingasWinter2007115-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
