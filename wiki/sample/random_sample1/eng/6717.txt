<p><b>Lanao del Norte</b> (<a href="/wiki/Cebuano_language" title="Cebuano language">Cebuano</a>: <span lang="ceb"><i>Amihanang Lanaw</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Maranao_language" title="Maranao language">Maranao</a>: <i>Ranao Amiyanan</i>; <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">Filipino</a>: <span lang="fil"><i>Hilagang Lanaw</i></span>), officially the <b>Province of Lanao del Norte</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_the_Philippines" title="Provinces of the Philippines">province</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mindanao" title="Northern Mindanao">Northern Mindanao</a> <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_the_Philippines" title="Regions of the Philippines">region</a>. Its capital is <a href="/wiki/Tubod,_Lanao_del_Norte" title="Tubod, Lanao del Norte">Tubod</a>.</p>
<p>The province borders <a href="/wiki/Lanao_del_Sur" title="Lanao del Sur">Lanao del Sur</a> to the southeast, <a href="/wiki/Zamboanga_del_Sur" title="Zamboanga del Sur">Zamboanga del Sur</a> to the west, <a href="/wiki/Illana_Bay" title="Illana Bay">Illana Bay</a> to the southwest, <a href="/wiki/Iligan_Bay" title="Iligan Bay">Iligan Bay</a> to the north, Iligan City to the northeast, and is separated from <a href="/wiki/Misamis_Occidental" title="Misamis Occidental">Misamis Occidental</a> by <a href="/wiki/Panguil_Bay" title="Panguil Bay">Panguil Bay</a> to the northwest. According to the 2015 census, the province has a total population of 676,395 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSO15-10_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NSO15-10-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Situated within Lanao del Norte is the highly urbanized city of <a href="/wiki/Iligan" title="Iligan">Iligan</a>, which is governed independently from the province.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanao_del_Norte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Lanao_(province)" title="Lanao (province)">Lanao (province)</a></div>
<p>The province of <a href="/wiki/Lanao_(province)" title="Lanao (province)">Lanao</a> existed from 1914 until 1959. <i>Republic Act No. 2228</i> divided Lanao into two (2) provinces creating Lanao del Norte and <a href="/wiki/Lanao_del_Sur" title="Lanao del Sur">Lanao del Sur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ChanRobles-RA2228_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ChanRobles-RA2228-3">[3]</a></sup> The new province was inaugurated on July 4, 1959 making <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iligan_City" title="Iligan City">Iligan City</a> as the capital.</p>

<p>In 1977, President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferdinand_E._Marcos" title="Ferdinand E. Marcos">Ferdinand E. Marcos</a> signed <i>Resolution No. 805, series of 1977</i> of the Sangguniang Panlalawigan (<i>Parliamentary Bill No. 586</i>) sponsored by Assemblyman Abdullah D. Dimaporo, into <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presidential_Decree" title="Presidential Decree">Presidential Decree</a> 181</i> transferring the province's capital from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iligan_City" title="Iligan City">Iligan City</a> to the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Tubod,_Lanao_del_Norte" title="Tubod, Lanao del Norte">Tubod</a>.</p>
<p>In October 1984, inaugural ceremonies were held to celebrate the occasion of the transfer of the Provincial Capitol from Poblacion, Tubod to the Don Mariano Marcos Government Center (now Governor Arsenio A. Quibranza Provincial Government Center) at Pigcarangan, in Tubod.</p>
<p>Despite the outbreak of the conflict of the <a href="/wiki/Moro_Islamic_Liberation_Front" title="Moro Islamic Liberation Front">MILF</a> led by Abdullah "Commander Bravo" Goldiano Macapaar bin Sabbar and the <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_the_Philippines" title="Armed Forces of the Philippines">Philippine Army</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kauswagan" title="Kauswagan">Kauswagan</a> on March 2000, through the provincial government's effort, peace and order was restored in the province.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanao_del_Norte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lanao del Norte is a rugged province that ranges from the coastal shorelines in the north to the high plateaus and mountains in the south. It has also diverse flora and fauna.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag needs further explanation. (June 2016)">further explanation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Lanao del Norte covers a total area of 3,346.57 square kilometres (1,292.12 sq mi)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nscb_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nscb-4">[4]</a></sup> occupying the southwestern section of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mindanao" title="Northern Mindanao">Northern Mindanao</a> region in <a href="/wiki/Mindanao" title="Mindanao">Mindanao</a>. When Iligan City is included for geographical purposes, the province's land area is 4,159.94 square kilometres (1,606.16 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nscb_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-nscb-4">[4]</a></sup> The province is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Lanao_del_Sur" title="Lanao del Sur">Lanao del Sur</a> to the southeast, <a href="/wiki/Zamboanga_del_Sur" title="Zamboanga del Sur">Zamboanga del Sur</a> to the west, <a href="/wiki/Illana_Bay" title="Illana Bay">Illana Bay</a> to the southwest, <a href="/wiki/Iligan_Bay" title="Iligan Bay">Iligan Bay</a> to the north, Iligan City to the northeast, and the <a href="/wiki/Panguil_Bay" title="Panguil Bay">Panguil Bay</a> to the northwest.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanao_del_Norte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lanao del Norte comprises 22 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippine_municipality" title="Philippine municipality">municipalities</a>. The city of <a href="/wiki/Iligan" title="Iligan">Iligan</a> administers itself independently as a <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_the_Philippines#Independent_cities" title="Cities of the Philippines">highly urbanized city</a>.</p>

<p>The population of Lanao del Norte in the 2015 census was 676,395 people,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSO15-10_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-NSO15-10-2">[2]</a></sup> with a density of 200 inhabitants per square kilometre or 520 inhabitants per square mile. When Iligan City is included for geographical purposes, the province's population is 1,019,013 people, with a density of 245 inhabitants per square kilometre (630/sq mi).</p>
<p>The people in Lanao del Norte are a mixture of Maranaos and Cebuanos, with some Chabacanos, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tausug_people" title="Tausug people">Tausugs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yakan_people" title="Yakan people">Yakan</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sama-Bajau_people" title="Sama-Bajau people">Sama-Bajau</a>. Historically, immigrants who came from the Christian provinces of Mindanao, Visayas and Luzon tended to settle in the Northern part of Lanao while the Maranaos inhabited the south. There are also a minority of Higaunons settling in the hinterlands of Iligan City.</p>
<p>The Maranao is an ethnic group of Malay descent. They settled in the area long before the advent of the Spaniards in the Philippines. They possess their own culture and civilization which makes them quite unique. Their language, customs, traditions, religion, social system, costumes, music, and other features are factors that make Lanao peculiar and distinct from other Philippine provinces.</p>
<p>The main languages are <a href="/wiki/Maranao_language" title="Maranao language">Maranao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cebuano_language" title="Cebuano language">Cebuano</a>. Other language spoken is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chabacano_language" title="Chabacano language">Chabacano</a>, spoken by both Christians and Muslims.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanao_del_Norte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A vast majority of people of Lanao del Norte adhere to <a href="/wiki/Christianity_in_the_Philippines" title="Christianity in the Philippines">Christianity</a> (predominantly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_the_Philippines" title="Roman Catholicism in the Philippines">Roman Catholic</a>) most of them are Cebuanos and Chabacanos. In 2006, 56.9%<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> are Roman Catholic according to <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Iligan" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Iligan">Iligan (Diocese)</a> [Catholic-Hierarchy], a suffragan of <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Ozamiz" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Ozamiz">Archdiocese of Ozamiz</a>. Practitioners of <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> comprises almost 42%.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanao_del_Norte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy in Lanao del Norte is predominantly based on agriculture and fishing. Factories are mostly based in Iligan City. Lanao del Norte is the home of Agus Power plants 4-7 that stretch from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baloi,_Lanao_del_Norte" title="Baloi, Lanao del Norte">Baloi</a> to Iligan City. It serves at about 70% of power supply in Mindanao.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Tourism is also a growing economy in the province.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_and_government">Politics and government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lanao_del_Norte&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Politics and government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Salvador T. Lluch was the first Governor of Lanao del Norte. Second was Mohammad Ali Dimaporo, from January 1960 to September 1965 when Governor Dimaporo ran and won the congressional seat of the province. By operation of the law of succession, Vice Governor Arsenio A. Quibranza became the third Provincial Chief Executive of the province. By the mandate of the inhabitants, Governor Quibranza was elected Governor in 1967 and almost unanimously re-elected in 1971 and again in 1980.</p>
<p>By virtue of the power and impact of the <a href="/wiki/People_Power_Revolution" title="People Power Revolution">People Power Revolution</a> at <a href="/wiki/EDSA_(road)" title="EDSA (road)">EDSA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a> on February 25, 1986, local government all over the Philippines changed the political atmosphere overnight. Lanao del Norte became one among the many provinces affected by the sudden changes brought by the "Snap Election" on February 1986. Local heads of offices and employees particularly those holding political positions were destabilized but with the installation of President <a href="/wiki/Corazon_Aquino" title="Corazon Aquino">Corazon Aquino</a> as president, OIC Atty. Francisco L. Abalos became the appointed governor of the province on March 3, 1986. On February 2, 1988, Atty. Abalos was elected as governor of the province.</p>
<p>In the Synchronized National Election of May 11, 1992, Congressman Abdullah D. Dimaporo, a legislator and economist, was elected Provincial Governor. The Provincial government embarked on a comprehensive planning and set the groundwork for the development of the province.</p>
<p>In the 1998 national and local elections, Imelda Quibranza-Dimaporo, wife of Governor Abdullah. D. Dimaporo was elected as Provincial Governor until 2007.</p>
<p>The eldest son of former Governor and now 2nd district representative Abdullah D. Dimaporo and incumbent Gov. Imelda Q. Dimaporo, Mohammad Khalid Q. Dimaporo enter the politics in the province since 2007 - 2016.</p>
