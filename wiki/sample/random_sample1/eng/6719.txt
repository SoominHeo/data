<p>The <b>Army of Northern Virginia</b> was the primary military force of the <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederate States of America</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Theater_of_the_American_Civil_War" title="Eastern Theater of the American Civil War">Eastern Theater</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, as well as the primary command structure of the Department of Northern Virginia. It was most often arrayed against the <a href="/wiki/Union_Army" title="Union Army">Union</a> <a href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Potomac" title="Army of the Potomac">Army of the Potomac</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_of_Northern_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Battle_flag_of_the_Confederate_States_of_America.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="600" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Battle_flag_of_the_Confederate_States_of_America.svg/150px-Battle_flag_of_the_Confederate_States_of_America.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Battle_flag_of_the_Confederate_States_of_America.svg/225px-Battle_flag_of_the_Confederate_States_of_America.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Battle_flag_of_the_Confederate_States_of_America.svg/300px-Battle_flag_of_the_Confederate_States_of_America.svg.png 2x" width="150"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Battle_flag_of_the_Confederate_States_of_America.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Army of Northern Virginia Battle Flag, designed by <a href="/wiki/William_Porcher_Miles" title="William Porcher Miles">William Porcher Miles</a></div>

<p>The name <i>Army of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Virginia" title="Northern Virginia">Northern Virginia</a></i> referred to its primary area of operation, as did most <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_Army" title="Confederate States Army">Confederate States Army</a> names at the time. The Army originated as the <a href="/wiki/Confederate_Army_of_the_Potomac" title="Confederate Army of the Potomac">(Confederate) Army of the Potomac</a>, which was organized on June 20, 1861, from all operational forces in northern Virginia. On July 20 and July 21, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Shenandoah_(Confederate)" title="Army of the Shenandoah (Confederate)">Army of the Shenandoah</a> and forces from the District of Harpers Ferry were added. Units from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Northwest_(Confederate)" title="Army of the Northwest (Confederate)">Army of the Northwest</a> were merged into the Army of the Potomac between March 14 and May 17, 1862. The Army of the Potomac was renamed <i>Army of Northern Virginia</i> on March 14. The <a href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Peninsula" title="Army of the Peninsula">Army of the Peninsula</a> was merged into it on April 12, 1862.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-E889_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-E889-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Robert_E._Lee" title="Robert E. Lee">Robert E. Lee</a>'s biographer, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Douglas_S._Freeman" title="Douglas S. Freeman">Douglas S. Freeman</a>, asserts that the army received its final name from Lee when he issued orders assuming command on June 1, 1862.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, Freeman does admit that Lee corresponded with <a href="/wiki/Joseph_E._Johnston" title="Joseph E. Johnston">Joseph E. Johnston</a>, his predecessor in army command, prior to that date and referred to Johnston's command as the Army of Northern Virginia. Part of the confusion results from the fact that Johnston commanded the Department of Northern Virginia (as of October 22, 1861) and the name Army of Northern Virginia can be seen as an informal consequence of its parent department's name. <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Davis" title="Jefferson Davis">Jefferson Davis</a> and Johnston did not adopt the name, but it is clear that the organization of units as of March 14 was the same organization that Lee received on June 1, and thus it is generally referred to today as the Army of Northern Virginia, even if that is correct only in retrospect.</p>
<p>In addition to Virginians, it included <a href="/wiki/Regiment" title="Regiment">regiments</a> from all over the Confederacy, some from as far away as <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">Arkansas</a>. One of the most well known was the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Brigade" title="Texas Brigade">Texas Brigade</a>, made up of the 1st, 4th, and 5th Texas, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3rd_Arkansas" title="3rd Arkansas">3rd Arkansas</a>, which distinguished themselves in numerous battles, such as during their fight for the <a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Den" title="Devil's Den">Devil's Den</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gettysburg" title="Battle of Gettysburg">Battle of Gettysburg</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Command_under_Brigadier_General_P._G._T._Beauregard">Command under Brigadier General P. G. T. Beauregard</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_of_Northern_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Command under Brigadier General P. G. T. Beauregard">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pgt_beauregard.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1434" data-file-width="1053" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Pgt_beauregard.jpg/100px-Pgt_beauregard.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Pgt_beauregard.jpg/150px-Pgt_beauregard.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Pgt_beauregard.jpg/200px-Pgt_beauregard.jpg 2x" width="100"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pgt_beauregard.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Gen. P. G. T. Beauregard</div>

</div>
<p>The first commander of the Army of Northern Virginia was General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P.G.T._Beauregard" title="P.G.T. Beauregard">P.G.T. Beauregard</a> (under its previous name, the Confederate Army of the Potomac) from June 20 to July 20, 1861. His forces consisted of six brigades, with various militia and artillery from the former Department of Alexandria. During his command, Gen. Beauregard is noted for creating the battle flag of the army, which came to be the primary battle flag for all corps and forces under the Army of Northern Virginia. The flag was designed due to confusion during battle between the <a href="/wiki/Flags_of_the_Confederate_States_of_America" title="Flags of the Confederate States of America">Confederate "Stars and Bars" flag</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_United_States" title="Flag of the United States">flag of the United States</a>. Beauregard continued commanding these troops as the new First Corps under Gen. J. E. Johnston as it was joined by the Army of the Shenandoah on July 20, 1861, when command was relinquished to General J. E. Johnston. The following day this army fought its first major engagement in the First Battle of Manassas.</p>

Johnston</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_of_Northern_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Command under General J. <p>With the merging of the Army of the Shenandoah, Gen. <a href="/wiki/Joseph_E._Johnston" title="Joseph E. Johnston">Joseph E. Johnston</a> took command from July 20, 1861, until May 31, 1862.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Corps_organization_under_Johnston_1861">Corps organization under Johnston 1861</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_of_Northern_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Corps organization under Johnston 1861">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>First Corps – commanded by General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P.G.T._Beauregard" title="P.G.T. Beauregard">P.G.T. Beauregard</a></li>
<li>Second Corps – commanded by Maj. Gen. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G.W._Smith" title="G.W. Smith">G.W. Smith</a></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wing_organization_under_Johnston_1862">Wing organization under Johnston 1862</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_of_Northern_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Wing organization under Johnston 1862">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Left Wing – commanded by Maj. Gen. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D.H._Hill" title="D.H. Hill">D.H. Hill</a></li>
<li>Center Wing – commanded by Maj. Gen. <a href="/wiki/James_Longstreet" title="James Longstreet">James Longstreet</a></li>
<li>Right Wing – commanded by Maj. Gen. <a href="/wiki/John_B._Magruder" title="John B. Magruder">John B. Magruder</a></li>
<li>Reserve – commanded by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_General_(CSA)" title="Major General (CSA)">Maj. Gen.</a> G.W. Smith</li>
</ul>
<p>Under the command of Johnston, the Army immediately entered into the First Battle of Manassas. On October 22, 1861, the Department of Northern Virginia was officially created, officially ending the Army of the Potomac. The Department comprised three districts: Aquia District, Potomac District, and the <a href="/wiki/Valley_District" title="Valley District">Valley District</a>. In April 1862 the Department was expanded to include the Departments of Norfolk and the Peninsula (of Virginia). Gen. Johnston was eventually forced into maneuvering the Army southward to the defenses of Richmond during the opening of the Peninsula Campaign, where it conducted delay and defend tactics until Johnston was severely wounded at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Seven_Pines" title="Battle of Seven Pines">Battle of Seven Pines</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Temporary_command_under_Major_General_G._W._Smith">Temporary command under Major General G. W. Smith</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_of_Northern_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Temporary command under Major General G. W. Smith">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Maj. Gen. <a href="/wiki/Gustavus_Woodson_Smith" title="Gustavus Woodson Smith">Gustavus Woodson Smith</a> commanded the ANV on May 31, 1862, following the wounding of Gen. J. E. Johnston during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Seven_Pines" title="Battle of Seven Pines">Battle of Seven Pines</a>. With Smith seemingly having a nervous breakdown, President Jefferson Davis drafted orders to place Gen. Robert E. Lee in command the following day.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Command_under_General_R._E._Lee">Command under General R. E. Lee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_of_Northern_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Command under General R. E. Lee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Robert_E._Lee_in_camp.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="927" data-file-width="666" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f5/Robert_E._Lee_in_camp.jpg/100px-Robert_E._Lee_in_camp.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f5/Robert_E._Lee_in_camp.jpg/150px-Robert_E._Lee_in_camp.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f5/Robert_E._Lee_in_camp.jpg/200px-Robert_E._Lee_in_camp.jpg 2x" width="100"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Robert_E._Lee_in_camp.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
General Robert E. Lee, commander of the Army of Northern Virginia</div>
</div>

<p>On June 1, 1862, its most famous and final leader, General <a href="/wiki/Robert_E._Lee" title="Robert E. Lee">Robert E. Lee</a>, took command after Johnston was wounded, and Smith suffered what may have been a nervous breakdown, at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Seven_Pines" title="Battle of Seven Pines">Battle of Seven Pines</a>. In the first year of his command, Lee had two principal subordinate commanders. The right wing of the army was under the command of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lieutenant_General_(CSA)" title="Lieutenant General (CSA)">Lt. Gen.</a> <a href="/wiki/James_Longstreet" title="James Longstreet">James Longstreet</a> and the left wing under Lt. Gen. <a href="/wiki/Stonewall_Jackson" title="Stonewall Jackson">Thomas J. "Stonewall" Jackson</a>. These wings were redesignated as the First Corps (Longstreet) and Second Corps (Jackson) on November 6, 1862. Following Jackson's death after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Chancellorsville" title="Battle of Chancellorsville">Battle of Chancellorsville</a>, Lee reorganized the army into three <a href="/wiki/Corps" title="Corps">corps</a> on May 30, 1863, under Longstreet, Lt. Gen. <a href="/wiki/Richard_S._Ewell" title="Richard S. Ewell">Richard S. Ewell</a>, and Lt. Gen. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.P._Hill" title="A.P. Hill">A.P. Hill</a>. A Fourth Corps, under Lt. Gen. <a href="/wiki/Richard_H._Anderson" title="Richard H. Anderson">Richard H. Anderson</a>, was organized on October 19, 1864; on April 8, 1865, it was merged into the Second Corps. The commanders of the first three corps changed frequently in 1864 and 1865. The cavalry, organized into a division on August 17, 1862, and into a corps on September 9, 1863, was commanded by Maj. Gen. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.E.B._Stuart" title="J.E.B. Stuart">J.E.B. Stuart</a> until May 11, 1864 (the day he was mortally wounded). The cavalry corps was then temporarily split into divisions, but was merged again on August 11, 1864 under command of Lt. Gen. <a href="/wiki/Wade_Hampton_III" title="Wade Hampton III">Wade Hampton III</a>. The Reserve Artillery was commanded by Brig. Gen. <a href="/wiki/William_N._Pendleton" title="William N. Pendleton">William N. Pendleton</a> for most of the war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-E889_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-E889-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After taking over command in mid-1862, Lee began preparing to lead the Army of Northern Virginia for the first time. However, his aggressiveness to attack the Union led to the loss of many troops especially at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Antietam" title="Battle of Antietam">Battle of Antietam</a>, which ended up being a turning point in the war for the Union. After the costly victories during the Seven Days Battles and at Second Manassas in August 1862, Lee had now lost a total of 30,000 of his approximately 92,000 troops within three months of becoming the Confederate's top general. Lee then planned to take his troops north into Maryland to destroy a critical railroad bridge across the Susquehanna River at Harrisburg in a letter written to President Davis. Lee even questioned his own plan, as he wrote, "I am aware that the movement is attended with much risk, yet I do not consider success impossible..."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In addition, historians question Lee's aggressiveness to move his army to Maryland. "There can be no sort of doubt that Lee underestimated the exhaustion of his army after Second Manassas. That is, in reality, the major criticism of the Maryland operation: he carried worn-out men across the Potomac."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His men were also underarmed and underfed, so the journey to Maryland added to the overall exhaustion. Once Lee arrived in Maryland and was preparing for Antietam, he made another controversial decision. Against the advice from General Longstreet and Jackson, Lee split his troops into four parts to attack the Union from different fronts. Clearly outnumbered and opposed to Lee's plan, Longstreet stated, "General, I wish we could stand still and let the damned Yankees come to us!"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> As the fighting played out on September 17, 1862, known as the bloodiest single-day battle in American history, the battles at Dunker Church and Burnside's Bridge proved to be too much for Lee and his Confederate army. Luckily for Lee, the arrival of A.P. Hill's troops and the mixture of McClellan's and Burnside's sluggishness, saved Lee's Army of Northern Virginia and allowed them to barely hold off the Union in Maryland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>On April 9, 1865, the Army of Northern Virginia surrendered to the <a href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Potomac" title="Army of the Potomac">Army of the Potomac</a> at <a href="/wiki/Appomattox_Court_House_National_Historical_Park" title="Appomattox Court House National Historical Park">Appomattox Court House</a>, effectively ending the Civil War, with General Lee signing the papers of surrender to General <a href="/wiki/Ulysses_S._Grant" title="Ulysses S. Grant">Ulysses S. Grant</a>. The day after his surrender, Lee issued his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lee%E2%80%99s_Farewell_Address" title="Lee’s Farewell Address">Farewell Address</a> to the Army of Northern Virginia.</p>

<p>At the outset of the <a href="/wiki/Peninsula_Campaign" title="Peninsula Campaign">Peninsula Campaign</a> the Army of Northern Virginia had more than 55,633 soldiers. The cannon was assigned to the brigades, as well as the Reserve's artillery. Nominally, Jackson's <a href="/wiki/Corps" title="Corps">Corps</a> in the Shenandoah Valley, was subordinate to the Army. Since Jackson led his own campaign at the time of the Peninsula Campaign and was not under Lee's direct command this overview does not include his three divisions.</p>
<p>The Army's organization soon proved inept in the course of the Peninsula Campaign. The corps-like structure was rearranged before the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seven_Days_Battle" title="Seven Days Battle">Seven Days Battle</a> to converge with the requirements of actual command. In the course of this battle the Army featured two Corps; Jackson's and Magruder's, with four and three divisions respectively, and three actual divisions with five to six brigades. Also the Defense District of North Carolina answered directly to the Army as well as the Reserve Artillery with six battalions and the cavalry with six regiments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The army's complete strength was about 90,000 soldiers. The exact strength cannot be determined, because only a few notes for actual provisionings survived. The estimated strength results, if not explicitly noted, from in-battle dispatches.</p>

He introduced a corps-like structure of command, and as an intermediate army management he named the left and right wing. <p>The Army's Reserve Artillery consisted of one regiment and two battalions. They stayed in the area of Richmond in the course of the whole <a href="/wiki/Northern_Virginia_Campaign" title="Northern Virginia Campaign">Northern Virginia Campaign</a> and only returned on September 3, 1862 to the Army. Major General Hill's Division also remained in the eastern parts of Richmond with the order to bind <a href="/wiki/George_B._McClellan" title="George B. McClellan">McClellan's</a> attention as long as possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> As it became predictable that the Army of the Potomac would soon be transferred to support Pope, Lee ordered the Division north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Hill never entered battle in the campaign. A total of about 54,000 soldiers saw action throughout the campaign.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization_at_the_beginning_of_the_Maryland_Campaign">Organization at the beginning of the Maryland Campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Army_of_Northern_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Organization at the beginning of the Maryland Campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>While organization of the corps was found to be generally reliable, the corps' subdivision into four or five divisions hampered overall ease of command. General Lee had already considered before the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Antietam" title="Battle of Antietam">Battle of Antietam</a> to slim down the overall structure, but intended there be no changes in leadership. The Confederate Congress authorized the establishment of the Corps, and President Davis affirmed the assignment of the commanders and promoted Major Generals Longstreet and Jackson to Lieutenant Generals. General Lee announced this in Special Order 234 on November 6, 1862.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> About 60,000 soldiers served at the Maryland Campaign.</p>

<p>Lee ordered the artillery battalions of the Reserve Artillery to serve directly with the Corps for the duration of the <a href="/wiki/Gettysburg_Campaign" title="Gettysburg Campaign">Gettysburg Campaign</a>. The Army of Northern Virginia now comprised a total of 75,054 soldiers at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gettysburg" title="Battle of Gettysburg">Battle of Gettysburg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The army fielded more than 241 cannons following the Battle of Gettysburg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The artillery battalions were merged into the Artillery Reserve again following the end of the campaign.</p>
<p>On September 9, General Lee had to dispatch the First Corps to <a href="/wiki/Braxton_Bragg" title="Braxton Bragg">Braxton Bragg</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Army_of_Tennessee" title="Army of Tennessee">Army of Tennessee</a>. Following this the army was resubordinated again. Changes were not significant; only the cavalry saw important reorganization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Army's strength was then 55,221 soldiers. The changes in command until December 31, 1863 were only minor. Cooke's Brigade was assigned to serve with Heth's Division, Hampton's Division grew by a cavalry brigade and the Third Corps gained an additional artillery battalion. Imboden's Command remained at Shenandoah Valley and was taken over by Major General Early as the Defense District of Shenandoah Valley. The strength of the army was 54,715 men on December 31.</p>
<p>The organization of the Army of Northern Virginia did not change until the end of the war. The Army featured several corps, the corps featured several divisions, and the artillery was divided between the corps. The strength of the Army grew in the first six months from about 46,380 to 62,230 soldiers. The army was assigned in July to the Defense District of North Carolina and Richmond. In the course of the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Petersburg" title="Siege of Petersburg">Richmond-Petersburg Campaign</a> the number of soldiers temporarily grew to 82,633 while parts of the Army were under the command by Lieutenant General Early in Shenandoah Valley.</p>

<p>Following Lieutenant General A.P. Hill's death on April 2, 1865 the Third Corps was dissolved and assigned to the First Corps. On April 9, 1865, General Lee surrendered. One day later he thanked his men and his officers for their bravery and sturdiness and announced the dismissal of all troops on their word of honor in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Order_No._9" title="General Order No. 9">General Order No. 9</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The listings of the Army of Northern Virginia say that 28,231 soldiers were dismissed on their word of honor on April 10, 1865.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>

<p>Although often outnumbered, the Army of Northern Virginia won victories in the American Civil War at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Fredericksburg" title="Battle of Fredericksburg">Fredericksburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Bull_Run" title="Second Battle of Bull Run">Second Manassas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Chancellorsville" title="Battle of Chancellorsville">Chancellorsville</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cold_Harbor" title="Battle of Cold Harbor">Cold Harbor</a>. Additionally, it successfully drove the Army of the Potomac away from the "gates" of Richmond in the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Days_Battles" title="Seven Days Battles">Seven Days Battles</a>, effectively ending McClellan's <a href="/wiki/Peninsula_Campaign" title="Peninsula Campaign">Peninsula Campaign</a> in 1862. The army held out against long odds in the 1864 <a href="/wiki/Overland_Campaign" title="Overland Campaign">Overland Campaign</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Petersburg" title="Siege of Petersburg">Siege of Petersburg</a>.</p>
