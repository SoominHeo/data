<p>The <b>northern bettong</b> (<i>Bettongia tropica</i>) is a small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Potoroid" title="Potoroid">potoroid</a> <a href="/wiki/Marsupial" title="Marsupial">marsupial</a> which is restricted to some areas of mixed open <i><a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus" title="Eucalyptus">Eucalyptus</a></i> woodlands and <i><a href="/wiki/Allocasuarina" title="Allocasuarina">Allocasuarina</a></i> forests bordering <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforests</a> in far northeastern <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.</p>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Bettong" title="Bettong">bettong</a> is a solitary animal and is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nocturnal_animal" title="Nocturnal animal">nocturnal</a>. It spends the day in a well concealed nest constructed beneath either a tree within a clump of grass or from other litter collected at ground level. Nesting material is carried using its prehensile tail. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ectomycorrhizal" title="Ectomycorrhizal">Ectomycorrhizal</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fungal" title="Fungal">fungal</a> <a href="/wiki/Sporocarp_(fungi)" title="Sporocarp (fungi)">sporocarps</a> are the staple diet of the northern bettong. These are dug from beneath the soil at the bases of trees, and work to date suggests substantial nightly movements are often required to detect this patchily distributed food source. Other foods in the diet include grass roots and tubers, lilies, herbs, and sedges. The northern bettong appears to breed all year round, and like other relatives of the <a href="/wiki/Kangaroo" title="Kangaroo">kangaroos</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joey_(marsupial)" title="Joey (marsupial)">joey</a> is carried in a pouch until it is old enough to follow the mother as a 'young-at-foot'.</p>
<p>The northern bettong is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endangered_animal" title="Endangered animal">endangered animal</a>, and only populates three regions of Queensland, Australia, all within 80 miles of each other.</p>
