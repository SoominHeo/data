<p>The <b>43rd parallel north</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_latitude" title="Circle of latitude">circle of latitude</a> that is 43 <a href="/wiki/Degree_(angle)" title="Degree (angle)">degrees</a> <a href="/wiki/True_north" title="True north">north</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth's</a> <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equatorial plane</a>. It crosses <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>.</p>
<p>On 21 June the <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">sun</a> is at 70.83 degrees in the sky.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>At this latitude the sun is visible for 15 hours, 22 minutes during the <a href="/wiki/Summer_solstice" title="Summer solstice">summer solstice</a> and 9 hours, 0 minutes during the <a href="/wiki/Winter_solstice" title="Winter solstice">winter solstice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
