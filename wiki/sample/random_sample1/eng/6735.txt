<p>The <b>North Caucasus</b> (or <b>Ciscaucasia</b>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Се́верный Кавка́з</span>; <small>IPA: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Russian" title="Help:IPA for Russian">[ˈsʲevʲɪrnɨj kɐfˈkas]</a></span>) is the northern part of the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> region between the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> and within <a href="/wiki/European_Russia" title="European Russia">European Russia</a>.</p>
<p>Politically, the Northern Caucasus (territory north of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Caucasus" title="Greater Caucasus">Greater Caucasus</a> Range) includes the Russian Republics of the North Caucasus. As part of the Russian Federation, the Northern Caucasus region is included in the <a href="/wiki/North_Caucasian_Federal_District" title="North Caucasian Federal District">North Caucasian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southern_Federal_District" title="Southern Federal District">Southern Federal Districts</a> and consists of <a href="/wiki/Krasnodar_Krai" title="Krasnodar Krai">Krasnodar Krai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stavropol_Krai" title="Stavropol Krai">Stavropol Krai</a>, and the constituent republics, approximately from west to east: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Adygea" title="Republic of Adygea">Republic of Adygea</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karachay%E2%80%93Cherkessia" title="Karachay–Cherkessia">Karachay–Cherkessia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kabardino-Balkaria" title="Kabardino-Balkaria">Kabardino-Balkaria</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Ossetia%E2%80%93Alania" title="North Ossetia–Alania">North Ossetia–Alania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ingushetia" title="Ingushetia">Ingushetia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chechnya" title="Chechnya">Chechnya</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Dagestan" title="Republic of Dagestan">Republic of Dagestan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Since October 2007, separatists and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caucasian_Front_(Chechen_War)" title="Caucasian Front (Chechen War)">Caucasus Front</a> soldiers led by <a href="/wiki/Dokka_Umarov" title="Dokka Umarov">Dokka Umarov</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Akhmed_Yevloyev" title="Akhmed Yevloyev">Akhmed Yevloyev</a> have claimed all of the North Caucasian regions from Karachay–Cherkessia to the Caspian Sea as part of their territory invaded by Russia during the 18th century that expelled most of the Caucasians towards Turkey, Iran, Bulgaria, Syria, and Jordan. This proclaimed <a href="/wiki/Caucasus_Emirate" title="Caucasus Emirate">Caucasus Emirate</a> has been met with opposition within the Caucasus region from the <a href="/wiki/Chechen_Republic_of_Ichkeria" title="Chechen Republic of Ichkeria">Chechen Republic of Ichkeria</a>.</p>
<p>Geographically, the term North Caucasus comprises the northern slope and western extremity of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caucasus_Major" title="Caucasus Major">Caucasus Major</a> mountain range, as well as a part of its southern slope to the West (until the <a href="/wiki/Psou_River" title="Psou River">Psou River</a>). The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pontic-Caspian_steppe" title="Pontic-Caspian steppe">Forecaucasus steppe</a> area is often also encompassed under the notion of "North Caucasus", thus the northern boundary of the geographical region is generally considered to be the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kuma-Manych_Depression" title="Kuma-Manych Depression">Kuma-Manych Depression</a>. It is bounded by the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Azov" title="Sea of Azov">Sea of Azov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kerch_Strait" title="Kerch Strait">Kerch Strait</a> on the west, and the Caspian Sea on the east. According to the <i>Concise Atlas of the World, Second Edition</i> (2008), most of the Ciscaucasian region lies on the European side of the "commonly accepted division" that separates Europe from Asia.</p>
<p>In 2011, the Russian government has put forward plans to create a "Caucasian version of <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a>", at a cost of 32 billion rubles ($1.1 billion) as part of on-going efforts to generate opportunities in the troubled Russian region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
