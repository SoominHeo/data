<p><b>Woensdrecht</b> (<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:236_Woensdrecht.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/27/236_Woensdrecht.ogg" title="236 Woensdrecht.ogg">pronunciation</a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:236_Woensdrecht.ogg" title="File:236 Woensdrecht.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> (named after the <a href="/wiki/Woensdrecht_(village)" title="Woensdrecht (village)">village</a>) in the southern <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.</p>
<p>Woensdrecht is the home of the <a href="/wiki/Woensdrecht_Air_Base" title="Woensdrecht Air Base">Woensdrecht Air Base</a>, which is located to the north-east of the village of Woensdrecht and to the north-west of <a href="/wiki/Huijbergen" title="Huijbergen">Huijbergen</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Woensdrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_World_War">Second World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Woensdrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Second World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 October 1944, at the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Scheldt" title="Battle of the Scheldt">Battle of the Scheldt</a> (2 October-8 November 1944), the Canadian 2nd Division began its advance north from Antwerp. Stiff fighting at Woensdrecht ensued on 6 October, the objective of the first phase. The Germans, reinforced by Battle Group Chill, saw the priority in holding there, controlling direct access to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Beveland" title="South Beveland">South Beveland</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Walcheren_Island" title="Walcheren Island">Walcheren Island</a>. There were heavy casualties as the Canadians attacked over open, flooded land. Driving rain, booby traps and land mines made advance very difficult. On 13 October, what would come to be known as "Black Friday", the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_5th_Infantry_Brigade" title="Canadian 5th Infantry Brigade">Canadian 5th Infantry Brigade</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Watch_(Royal_Highland_Regiment)_of_Canada" title="The Black Watch (Royal Highland Regiment) of Canada">Black Watch</a> was virtually wiped out in an unsuccessful attack. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calgary_Highlanders" title="Calgary Highlanders">Calgary Highlanders</a> were to follow up with a more successful action, and their Carrier Platoon succeeded in taking the rail station at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Korteven&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Korteven (page does not exist)">Korteven</a>. Heavy fighting at Hoogerheide also ensued, but by 16 October, Woensdrecht was secured, cutting the land link to South Beveland and Walcheren. The Canadians was successful in completing their objective.</p>
<p>After that the Buffaloes, ground forces dedicating to causing as much impact to the enemy troops during the barrage, secured the Breskens pocket on the north side of the <a href="/wiki/Scheldt" title="Scheldt">Scheldt</a> river.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cold_War_era">Cold War era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Woensdrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cold War era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1983, it was decided that the United States would station 48 <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">nuclear armed</a> <a href="/wiki/Cruise_missile" title="Cruise missile">cruise missiles</a> at the air base in the district, unless the USSR reduced their number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SS-20" title="SS-20">SS-20</a> missiles to 378. Since the number was still higher in 1985, stationing was to proceed. Among the Dutch population there was <a href="/wiki/Hollanditis" title="Hollanditis">protest</a> against this. The plan was cancelled in 1987 due to the <a href="/wiki/Intermediate-Range_Nuclear_Forces_Treaty" title="Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces Treaty">Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces Treaty</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Woensdrecht&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The climate in this area has mild differences between highs and lows, and there is adequate rainfall year round. According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_Climate_Classification" title="Köppen Climate Classification">Köppen Climate Classification</a> system, Woensdrecht has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_west_coast_climate" title="Marine west coast climate">marine west coast climate</a>, abbreviated "Cfb" on climate maps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
