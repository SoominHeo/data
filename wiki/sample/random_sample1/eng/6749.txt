<p><b>Bullet for My Valentine</b>, often abbreviated as <b>BFMV</b> or <b>B4MV</b>, are a Welsh <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metal_music" title="Metal music">heavy metal</a> band from <a href="/wiki/Bridgend" title="Bridgend">Bridgend</a>, formed in 1998. The band is composed of <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Tuck" title="Matthew Tuck">Matthew Tuck</a> (lead vocals, rhythm guitar), <a href="/wiki/Michael_Paget" title="Michael Paget">Michael Paget</a> (lead guitar, backing vocals), <a href="/wiki/Michael_Thomas_(musician)" title="Michael Thomas (musician)">Michael Thomas</a> (drums) and Jamie Mathias (bass guitar). Former members include Nick Crandle and <a href="/wiki/Jason_James_(musician)" title="Jason James (musician)">Jason James</a>; both were on bass. They were formed under the name <b>Jeff Killed John</b> and started their music career by covering songs by <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nirvana_(band)" title="Nirvana (band)">Nirvana</a>. Jeff Killed John recorded six songs which were not released; two of these tracks were reworked later in their career as Bullet for My Valentine. Financial difficulties dictated the name change, which was followed by a change in music direction. In 2002, the band secured a five-album deal with <a href="/wiki/Sony_BMG" title="Sony BMG">Sony BMG</a>. The band has stated that their music is influenced by classic metal acts such as <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slayer" title="Slayer">Slayer</a>. The band is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardiff_music_scene" title="Cardiff music scene">Cardiff music scene</a>.</p>
<p>Bullet for My Valentine's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Poison" title="The Poison">The Poison</a></i>, was released on 3 October 2005 in the United Kingdom and on 14 February 2006 in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> to coincide with <a href="/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day" title="Valentine's Day">Valentine's Day</a>, in a nod to the band's name. The album entered the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at number 128. It was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a>. The band made appearances at the <a href="/wiki/Download_Festival" title="Download Festival">Download Festival</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Kerrang!" title="Kerrang!">Kerrang!</a></i> XXV, and undertook a U.S. tour with <a href="/wiki/Rob_Zombie" title="Rob Zombie">Rob Zombie</a>. Bullet for My Valentine's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Scream_Aim_Fire" title="Scream Aim Fire">Scream Aim Fire</a></i>, was released on 29 January 2008 and debuted at number four on the <i>Billboard</i> 200. The band's third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Fever_(Bullet_for_My_Valentine_album)" title="Fever (Bullet for My Valentine album)">Fever</a></i>, was released on 26 April 2010 and debuted at number three on the <i>Billboard</i> 200. On February 8, 2013, the band released their fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Temper_Temper_(Bullet_for_My_Valentine_album)" title="Temper Temper (Bullet for My Valentine album)">Temper Temper</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> which peaked at number 13 on the <i>Billboard 200</i>. The band has sold over one million albums in the United States and over 5,000,000 albums worldwide<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and are the most-successful act in the <i><a href="/wiki/Kerrang!_Awards" title="Kerrang! Awards">Kerrang! Awards</a></i> category of "Best British Band" with three wins.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jeff_Killed_John_and_record_deal_.281998.E2.80.932005.29">Jeff Killed John and record deal (1998–2005)</span></h3>
<p>Jeff Killed John was the forerunner band to Bullet for My Valentine and was formed in 1998 by Matthew Tuck, Michael "Padge" Paget, Nick Crandle, and Michael "Moose" Thomas while studying music at <a href="/wiki/Bridgend_College" title="Bridgend College">Bridgend College</a>. They started playing <a href="/wiki/Nirvana_(band)" title="Nirvana (band)">Nirvana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cover_songs" title="Cover songs">cover songs</a>. In 1999, the band released their first ever EP, <a href="/wiki/Better_Off_Alone_(EP)" title="Better Off Alone (EP)">Better Off Alone</a>. They released another two-track <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> in 2002, <i>You/Play with Me</i>, which was produced by Greg Haver. The EP was financed through the Pynci scheme for new Welsh musicians and the release garnered radio airplay on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a>'s broadcast at <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Wales" title="Newport, Wales">Newport</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T.J.%27s" title="T.J.'s">T.J.'s</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_biography_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_biography-3">[3]</a></sup> Jeff Killed John's music followed the <a href="/wiki/Nu_metal" title="Nu metal">nu metal</a> trend set by bands such as <a href="/wiki/Korn" title="Korn">Korn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Limp_Bizkit" title="Limp Bizkit">Limp Bizkit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_biography_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_biography-3">[3]</a></sup> Shortly after, the band released another two-track <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> titled <i>Eye Spy</i>. In 2003, their second EP was released titled <i>Don't Walk Away</i>. This was a promotional CD and was shipped to different record companies, as well as being given out locally in Bridgend. Bassist Crandle left the band on the eve of entering the recording sessions for the band's self-titled EP and was replaced by Jason James.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_biography_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_biography-3">[3]</a></sup> The band then changed their name to Bullet for My Valentine and reworked their musical strategy; they decided to play heavy metal songs with "harmony guitars and big angelic choruses," according to frontman Tuck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-R13_interview_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-R13_interview-4">[4]</a></sup> In late 2003, they released their final EP before record labels actually began to notice their potential. This happened due to the sudden change in their strategy and sound, which the band claims came "directly out of their heads."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Their self-titled EP consisted of five songs; most of the songs became precursors of tracks from The Poison.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Roadrunner_Records" title="Roadrunner Records">Roadrunner Records</a> showed interest in Bullet for My Valentine and offered the band a deal. The offer was ultimately turned down, and the band signed a five-album record deal with <a href="/wiki/Sony_BMG" title="Sony BMG">Sony BMG</a> and a UK licensing deal with <a href="/wiki/Visible_Noise" title="Visible Noise">Visible Noise</a>. According to Tuck, they chose Sony because, "We thought that a lot more doors would be open to us."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-R13_interview_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-R13_interview-4">[4]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Bullet_for_My_Valentine_(EP)" title="Bullet for My Valentine (EP)">self-titled</a> EP was released on 15 November 2004 in the UK. Produced by <a href="/wiki/Colin_Richardson" title="Colin Richardson">Colin Richardson</a>, it featured five tracks and marked the band's first official release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_biography_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_biography-3">[3]</a></sup> A second EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Hand_of_Blood" title="Hand of Blood">Hand of Blood</a>,</i> was released on 22 August 2005 through <a href="/wiki/Trustkill_Records" title="Trustkill Records">Trustkill Records</a> and was only available in the U.S.; the release contained one extra track than the self-titled EP, "<a href="/wiki/4_Words_(To_Choke_Upon)" title="4 Words (To Choke Upon)">4 Words (To Choke Upon)</a>." Daniel Lukes of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Decibel_Magazine" title="Decibel Magazine">Decibel Magazine</a></i> reviewed the EP by stating, "The worst part is that the music itself isn’t all that bad, for the genre." He went on to comment that the band should be "embarrassed" about the release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Screamocore_takes_a_dump_where_it_eats_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Screamocore_takes_a_dump_where_it_eats-6">[6]</a></sup> Zeromag's Josh Joyce complimented the band on "how technical they can get without confusing the kids."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_For_My_Valentine.C2.A0.E2.80.94_Hand_of_Blood_EP_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_For_My_Valentine.C2.A0.E2.80.94_Hand_of_Blood_EP-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Poison_.282005.E2.80.9307.29"><i>The Poison</i> (2005–07)</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:BulletForMyValentine2006.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="490" data-file-width="761" height="161" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/BulletForMyValentine2006.PNG/250px-BulletForMyValentine2006.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/BulletForMyValentine2006.PNG/375px-BulletForMyValentine2006.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/BulletForMyValentine2006.PNG/500px-BulletForMyValentine2006.PNG 2x" width="250"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

Michael Paget (left) and Matthew Tuck (right) at 2006's <a href="/wiki/Roskilde_Festival" title="Roskilde Festival">Roskilde Festival</a> held in Denmark.</div>

<p>Bullet for My Valentine's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Poison" title="The Poison">The Poison</a></i>, was released on 3 October 2005 in the UK and on Valentine's Day, 2006 in the US. It entered the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> at number 128,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chart_Listing_For_The_Week_Of_4_Mar_2006:_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chart_Listing_For_The_Week_Of_4_Mar_2006:-8">[8]</a></sup> and attained number 11 on the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_-_Albums_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_-_Albums-9">[9]</a></sup> On 30 January 2009, the album was certified gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> after 500,000 copies were sold in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Four singles were released from <i>The Poison</i>: "<a href="/wiki/4_Words_(To_Choke_Upon)" title="4 Words (To Choke Upon)">4 Words (To Choke Upon)</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Suffocating_Under_Words_of_Sorrow_(What_Can_I_Do)" title="Suffocating Under Words of Sorrow (What Can I Do)">Suffocating Under Words of Sorrow (What Can I Do)</a>", "<a href="/wiki/All_These_Things_I_Hate_(Revolve_Around_Me)" title="All These Things I Hate (Revolve Around Me)">All These Things I Hate (Revolve Around Me)</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Tears_Don%27t_Fall" title="Tears Don't Fall">Tears Don't Fall</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_-_Singles_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_-_Singles-11">[11]</a></sup> Bullet for My Valentine promoted the album by touring across the world. In 2005, with increased popularity, they played on the larger <a href="/wiki/Download_Festival" title="Download Festival">Download Festival</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Download_festival#Download_2005" title="Download festival">Snickers stage</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Download_Festival_2005_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Download_Festival_2005-12">[12]</a></sup> in 2006, the band were moved to near the top of the main stage bill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Download_Festival_2006_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Download_Festival_2006-13">[13]</a></sup> Other tours included opening for Metallica and <a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a> in the summer of 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_-_Matt_Tuck.2C_Vocals.2FGuitars_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_-_Matt_Tuck.2C_Vocals.2FGuitars-14">[14]</a></sup> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vans_Warped_Tour" title="Vans Warped Tour">Vans Warped Tour</a> and Earthday Birthday.</p>
<p>The band's headline performance at Kerrang XXV, a one-off gig at <a href="/wiki/Brixton_Academy" title="Brixton Academy">Brixton Academy</a> in London on 28 January 2006, was filmed for their first DVD, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Poison:_Live_at_Brixton" title="The Poison: Live at Brixton">The Poison: Live at Brixton</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> During June 2007, Tuck suffered from <a href="/wiki/Laryngitis" title="Laryngitis">laryngitis</a>, which let to an emergency <a href="/wiki/Tonsillectomy" title="Tonsillectomy">tonsillectomy</a> in July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Bullet for My Valentine were forced to cancel several shows, including supporting <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> for three dates on their Sick of the Studio '07 tour on 29 June in <a href="/wiki/Bilbao" title="Bilbao">Bilbao</a>, 5 July in Vienna, and 8 July at the recently completed <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a> in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Machine_Head_Added_To_METALLICA.27s_Wembley_Bill_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Machine_Head_Added_To_METALLICA.27s_Wembley_Bill-18">[18]</a></sup> Unable to speak, Tuck wrote that as soon as doctors cleared him he would be in the studio working on the band's next album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_update_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_update-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scream_Aim_Fire_.282007.E2.80.9308.29"><i>Scream Aim Fire</i> (2007–08)</span></h3>
<p>The band's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Scream_Aim_Fire" title="Scream Aim Fire">Scream Aim Fire</a></i>, was recorded at Sonic Ranch Studios and was produced by <a href="/wiki/Colin_Richardson" title="Colin Richardson">Colin Richardson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_In_The_Thick_Of_New_Album_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_for_My_Valentine_In_The_Thick_Of_New_Album-20">[20]</a></sup> Tuck commented at the time, "It's a lot more up <a href="/wiki/Tempo" title="Tempo">tempo</a>, a lot more aggressive."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BULLET_FOR_MY_VALENTINE_Frontman_Says_New_Album_Is_.27A_Lot_More_Uptempo.2C_A_Lot_More_Aggressive.27_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-BULLET_FOR_MY_VALENTINE_Frontman_Says_New_Album_Is_.27A_Lot_More_Uptempo.2C_A_Lot_More_Aggressive.27-21">[21]</a></sup> The album was released in the U.S. on 29 January 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> It sold 53,000 copies in its first week and peaked at number four on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Keys_Notches_Fourth_Week_At_No..C2.A01_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Keys_Notches_Fourth_Week_At_No..C2.A01-23">[23]</a></sup> Three songs were released as singles: "Scream Aim Fire," "Hearts Burst into Fire" and "Waking the Demon."</p>
<p>In support of the new album, the band toured North America and Australia in the spring of 2008 for the <a href="/wiki/Taste_of_Chaos" title="Taste of Chaos">Taste of Chaos</a> tour alongside <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atreyu_(band)" title="Atreyu (band)">Atreyu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blessthefall" title="Blessthefall">Blessthefall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Avenged_Sevenfold" title="Avenged Sevenfold">Avenged Sevenfold</a>. Bullet for My Valentine cut short the Canada portion of the tour and flew back home to support the daughter of James who was in hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BULLET_FOR_MY_VALENTINE_Cancels_Seattle.2C_Canada_Dates_-_14_Apr._2008_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-BULLET_FOR_MY_VALENTINE_Cancels_Seattle.2C_Canada_Dates_-_14_Apr._2008-24">[24]</a></sup> The band performed in North America again in the summer of 2008 as part of the <a href="/wiki/No_Fear" title="No Fear">No Fear</a> Tour with <a href="/wiki/Bleeding_Through" title="Bleeding Through">Bleeding Through</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cancer_Bats" title="Cancer Bats">Cancer Bats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Tide" title="Black Tide">Black Tide</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In late 2008, the band toured Europe, supported by <a href="/wiki/Lacuna_Coil" title="Lacuna Coil">Lacuna Coil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bleeding_Through" title="Bleeding Through">Bleeding Through</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Black_Tide" title="Black Tide">Black Tide</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In December 2008, <i>Scream Aim Fire</i> was re-released with four bonus tracks that were recorded during the album's studio sessions, but with re-recorded vocal-lines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_For_My_Valentine.E2.80.99s_Matt_Tuck_on_new_album_-_16_Mar._2009_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_For_My_Valentine.E2.80.99s_Matt_Tuck_on_new_album_-_16_Mar._2009-27">[27]</a></sup></p>

<p>Matt Tuck spoke to Daniel Marez of <i><a href="/wiki/Metal_Hammer" title="Metal Hammer">Metal Hammer</a></i> in March 2009 about the band's progress on writing and recording their third album. Tuck stated that there were four or five songs that were finished and that "usually we write and record 14 tracks that I then have to write vocal lines for, but this time I’m trying to write the vocals as I write the music."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_For_My_Valentine.E2.80.99s_Matt_Tuck_on_new_album_-_16_Mar._2009_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_For_My_Valentine.E2.80.99s_Matt_Tuck_on_new_album_-_16_Mar._2009-27">[27]</a></sup> Bullet for My Valentine confirmed in August 2009 that they were aiming for an early 2010 release for the new album, while Tuck stated that the upcoming record is "a lot more mature-sounding, it's a lot more classic. It'll stand the test of time [more] than both the previous things we've done."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blabbermouth_swigged_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-blabbermouth_swigged-28">[28]</a></sup> In a January 2010 interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Sound" title="Rock Sound">Rock Sound</a></i>, Tuck stated explained details for the album: it would be titled <i><a href="/wiki/Fever_(Bullet_for_My_Valentine_album)" title="Fever (Bullet for My Valentine album)">Fever</a></i>, and the 11-track record would have a sound closer to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Poison" title="The Poison">The Poison</a></i> rather than <i><a href="/wiki/Scream_Aim_Fire" title="Scream Aim Fire">Scream Aim Fire</a></i> and contain no <a href="/wiki/Ballad" title="Ballad">ballads</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rocksound.tv_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-rocksound.tv-29">[29]</a></sup></p>

Engage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slayer" title="Slayer">Slayer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Manson_(band)" title="Marilyn Manson (band)">Marilyn Manson</a> on the main stage;<sup class="reference" href="/wiki/Sonisphere" title="Sonisphere">Sonisphere</a> tour at Knebworth, headlining the second stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The band headlined the second stage at the Download Festival 2010 on the Friday night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> The band was also confirmed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nova_Rock" title="Nova Rock">Nova Rock</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metaltown" am Ring</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rock_on_the_Range" title="Rock on the Range">Rock on the Range</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Bamboozle" title="The Bamboozle">The Bamboozle</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bilbao_Live_Festival" title="Bilbao Live Festival">Bilbao Live Festival</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Fortarock<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graspop" <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Temper_Temper_.282012.E2.80.9313.29"><i>Temper Temper</i> (2012–13)</span></h3>
<p>On 28 January 2011, Michael Paget stated that the group had already drawn proportions for the next studio album and will sound a lot like <i>Fever</i>. He followed-along with stating that the band plans to have the songs for it written within 2011 and will begin recording the album by the end of the year. A couple tracks left from the <i>Fever</i> sessions might be re-done, re-arranged and re-recorded for the new record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> The band played at <a href="/wiki/Uproar_Festival" title="Uproar Festival">Uproar Festival 2011</a>, after which they began writing material for a fourth studio album.</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ursynalia_2013,_Bullet_For_My_Valentine_C05.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1333" data-file-width="2000" height="160" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Ursynalia_2013%2C_Bullet_For_My_Valentine_C05.jpg/240px-Ursynalia_2013%2C_Bullet_For_My_Valentine_C05.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Ursynalia_2013%2C_Bullet_For_My_Valentine_C05.jpg/360px-Ursynalia_2013%2C_Bullet_For_My_Valentine_C05.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Ursynalia_2013%2C_Bullet_For_My_Valentine_C05.jpg/480px-Ursynalia_2013%2C_Bullet_For_My_Valentine_C05.jpg 2x" width="240"/></a>

<p>On 7 October, <a href="/wiki/RCA_Music_Group" title="RCA Music Group">RCA Music Group</a> announced it was disbanding <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> and <a href="/wiki/J_Records" title="J Records">J Records</a>. With the shutdown, the band (and all other artists previously signed to these three labels) will release their future material (including their next studio album) on the <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a> brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> Also in October, frontman Matt Tuck announced that he will be working on a new side project which he has described as "metal as fuck", influenced by bands such as <a href="/wiki/Pantera" title="Pantera">Pantera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slipknot_(band)" title="Slipknot (band)">Slipknot</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> On 1 May 2012, it was revealed that the project will be called <a href="/wiki/AxeWound" title="AxeWound">AxeWound</a>, and will feature Liam Cormier, Mike Kingswood, Joe Copcutt and Jason Bowld. In May 2012, it was announced that Bullet for My Valentine would be playing at the South African music festival <a href="/wiki/Oppikoppi" title="Oppikoppi">Oppikoppi</a>, as well as a once-off gig in Cape Town alongside <a href="/wiki/Seether" title="Seether">Seether</a> and <a href="/wiki/Enter_Shikari" title="Enter Shikari">Enter Shikari</a>. They were slated to play in the country for the Coke Zero Fest of 2009, but pulled out at the last minute to record Fever.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> It was confirmed on 6 August that the final recording session of the band's fourth studio record was complete, and that the album would be out sometime in late 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> Later that month on the 17th, the second line-up for the Australian music festival <a href="/wiki/Soundwave_(Australian_music_festival)" title="Soundwave (Australian music festival)">Soundwave</a> was announced, containing Bullet for My Valentine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup></p>
<p>Bullet for My Valentine released the track "Temper Temper" on 25 November in the UK and 30 October worldwide across all digital providers. The song was debuted live by the band on Monday 22 October at BBC Radio 1's Rock Week where they performed at Maida Vale studios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> It was announced the day prior to the performance that the band's fourth studio album would also be named <i><a href="/wiki/Temper_Temper_(Bullet_for_My_Valentine_album)" title="Temper Temper (Bullet for My Valentine album)">Temper Temper</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> The release date was confirmed to be the 12 February 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBCAlbum4_59-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBCAlbum4-59">[59]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band began touring in support of <i>Temper Temper</i> on February 10, 2013, and finished the tour on November 3, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> The tenth show of the tour, held at Birmingham's O2 Academy, was filmed by music video website Moshcam.com.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Venom_and_new_record_deal_.282013.E2.80.93present.29"><i>Venom</i> and new record deal (2013–present)</span></h3>
<p>In an interview, Matt Tuck said that Bullet for My Valentine were going to start work on a new album before the Rule Britannia Tour which took place between 1 December 2013 till the 6 December 2013 in which the band played arenas in Britain supported by <a href="/wiki/Young_Guns_(band)" title="Young Guns (band)">Young Guns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asking_Alexandria" title="Asking Alexandria">Asking Alexandria</a>. In the interview Matt also said that they were strongly considering working with <a href="/wiki/Terry_Date" title="Terry Date">Terry Date</a>, who has worked with bands such as <a href="/wiki/Pantera" title="Pantera">Pantera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Deftones" title="Deftones">Deftones</a>. He also said the band would bring back the <a href="/wiki/Thrash_metal" title="Thrash metal">thrash metal</a> elements from <i>Scream Aim Fire</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 13 November 2013, Bullet for My Valentine revealed though their Facebook page they were working on a new song. A short snippet of the song, titled "<a href="/wiki/Raising_Hell_(Bullet_for_My_Valentine_song)" title="Raising Hell (Bullet for My Valentine song)">Raising Hell</a>," was released on Matt Tuck's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vine_(software)" title="Vine (software)">Vine</a> profile on 15 November 2013. The song was first played on 18 November 2013 via <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1's Rock Show</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> It was made available for streaming on 20 November 2013, and a music video for the song was released a week later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 6 January 2014, Matt announced on Twitter that the band were entering the studio to record a fifth album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> In an interview with <i>Kerrang!</i> on 21 January 2015, Matt revealed that Bullet for My Valentine will be hitting the studio next month with producer Colin Richardson who produced both <i><a href="/wiki/The_Poison" title="The Poison">The Poison</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Scream_Aim_Fire" title="Scream Aim Fire">Scream Aim Fire</a>,</i> rather than Terry Date, who was the suggested producer initially. Tuck also said that their fifth album will be their heaviest album yet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 February 2015, Bullet for My Valentine announced the departure of bassist Jason James, adding that they would announce a replacement when they felt "the time was right." They went on to say that until the announcement, they'd be busy and focused on recording their new record.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup></p>
<p>It was announced on the band's Facebook page on 11 May 2015 that a new song, "No Way Out," would debut on BBC Radio 1 on 17 May 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> The band also revealed their fifth studio album to be titled <i><a href="/wiki/Venom_(Bullet_for_My_Valentine_album)" title="Venom (Bullet for My Valentine album)">Venom</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> and their new bassist was going to be Jamie Mathias, formerly of metal band Revoker.</p>
<p><i>Venom</i> was confirmed for release on 18 May 2015, set for 14 August 2015, the same day an expansive UK tour was announced. It was also revealed that Bullet For My Valentine will also headline London's Camden Rocks festival which takes place on 30 May 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 February 2016, Bullet for My Valentine announced that drummer Michael Thomas will sit out for a few months. Jason Bowld from AxeWound will replace him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 1 April 2016, the band announced that they have signed a new worldwide deal with <a href="/wiki/Spinefarm_Records" title="Spinefarm Records">Spinefarm Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 7 November 2016, the band released a new single titled "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don%27t_Need_You_(Bullet_for_My_Valentine_song)" title="Don't Need You (Bullet for My Valentine song)">Don't Need You</a>". A music video was released with the video as well.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influences">Musical style and influences</span></h2>
<p>Bullet for My Valentine has been described as <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">hard rock</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rocksound.tv_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-rocksound.tv-29">[29]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Thrash_metal" title="Thrash metal">thrash metal</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UG-thrash-new-album_75-0"><a href="#cite_note-UG-thrash-new-album-75">[75]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Metalcore" title="Metalcore">metalcore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> The band describe two of their albums, <i>The Poison</i> and <i>Fever</i>, as having a "super dark" tone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> Bullet for My Valentine member Matt Tuck said, "…we're a hard rock band with metal influences, and I’ve said that from day one."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rocksound.tv_29-2"><a href="#cite_note-rocksound.tv-29">[29]</a></sup> Kirk Miller of <i><a href="/wiki/Decibel_(magazine)" title="Decibel (magazine)">Decibel</a></i> praised the band for the effort in synchronised song structures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_For_My_Valentine_-_The_Poison_83-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_For_My_Valentine_-_The_Poison-83">[83]</a></sup> When asked of their views on their looks, members of the band have stated that they would not change their sound or image for a commercial approach; Tuck also said, "Without sounding harsh, we're more interested in what our music sounds like than what our fucking hair looks like."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_For_My_Valentine_Interview_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_For_My_Valentine_Interview-84">[84]</a></sup></p>
<p>The band has cited being influenced by bands such as <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Annihilator_(band)" title="Annihilator (band)">Annihilator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pantera" title="Pantera">Pantera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Machine_Head_(band)" title="Machine Head (band)">Machine Head</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sepultura" title="Sepultura">Sepultura</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artistdirect.com_85-0"><a href="#cite_note-artistdirect.com-85">[85]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/AC/DC" title="AC/DC">AC/DC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Testament_(band)" title="Testament (band)">Testament</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stuck_Mojo" title="Stuck Mojo">Stuck Mojo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korn" title="Korn">Korn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slayer" title="Slayer">Slayer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Judas_Priest" title="Judas Priest">Judas Priest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Megadeth" title="Megadeth">Megadeth</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alice_In_Chains" title="Alice In Chains">Alice In Chains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nirvana_(band)" title="Nirvana (band)">Nirvana</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bullet_For_My_Valentine_InterviewUG_87-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bullet_For_My_Valentine_InterviewUG-87">[87]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Deep_Purple" title="Deep Purple">Deep Purple</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Sabbath" title="Black Sabbath">Black Sabbath</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artistdirect.com_85-1"><a href="#cite_note-artistdirect.com-85">[85]</a></sup> According to Michael Thomas, these are the bands that inspire Bullet for My Valentine's "catchy vocals, aggressive riffs and melodies,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rage_89-0"><a href="#cite_note-rage-89">[89]</a></sup> Matt Tuck has stated in an interview, his songwriting is highly influenced by <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bob_Seger" title="Bob Seger">Bob Seger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a>. He goes on to say that "these bands just are proper old school songwriters."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup></p>
