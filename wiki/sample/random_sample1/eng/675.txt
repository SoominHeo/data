<p><b>June 27</b> is the 178th day of the year (179th in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 187 days remaining until the end of the year. This date is slightly more likely to fall on a Monday, Wednesday or Saturday (58 in 400 years each) than on Thursday or Friday (57), and slightly less likely to occur on a Tuesday or Sunday (56).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=June_27&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1358" title="1358">1358</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ragusa" title="Republic of Ragusa">Republic of Ragusa</a> (<i>Republic of Dubrovnik</i>) is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1497" title="1497">1497</a> – <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornish</a> rebels <a href="/wiki/Michael_An_Gof" title="Michael An Gof">Michael An Gof</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Flamank" title="Thomas Flamank">Thomas Flamank</a> are executed at <a href="/wiki/Tyburn" title="Tyburn">Tyburn</a>, London, England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1556" title="1556">1556</a> – The thirteen <a href="/wiki/Stratford_Martyrs" title="Stratford Martyrs">Stratford Martyrs</a> are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burned_at_the_stake" title="Burned at the stake">burned at the stake</a> near London for their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Protestant" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> beliefs.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1743" title="1743">1743</a> – In the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dettingen" title="Battle of Dettingen">Battle of Dettingen</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_II_of_Great_Britain" title="George II of Great Britain">George II</a> becomes the last reigning British monarch to participate in a battle.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1759" title="1759">1759</a> – General <a href="/wiki/James_Wolfe" title="James Wolfe">James Wolfe</a> begins the siege of <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1760" title="1760">1760</a> – <a href="/wiki/Cherokee" title="Cherokee">Cherokee</a> warriors defeat <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British</a> forces at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Echoee" title="Battle of Echoee">Battle of Echoee</a> near present-day <a href="/wiki/Otto,_North_Carolina" title="Otto, North Carolina">Otto, North Carolina</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Cherokee_War" title="Anglo-Cherokee War">Anglo-Cherokee War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1806" title="1806">1806</a> – British forces take <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a> during the first <a href="/wiki/British_invasions_of_the_River_Plate" title="British invasions of the River Plate">British invasions of the River Plate</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1844" title="1844">1844</a> – <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Smith" title="Joseph Smith">Joseph Smith</a>, founder of the <a href="/wiki/Latter_Day_Saint_movement" title="Latter Day Saint movement">Latter Day Saint movement</a>, and his brother <a href="/wiki/Hyrum_Smith" title="Hyrum Smith">Hyrum Smith</a>, are <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Joseph_Smith" title="Death of Joseph Smith">killed by a mob</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Carthage,_Illinois" title="Carthage, Illinois">Carthage, Illinois</a> jail.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1895" title="1895">1895</a> – The inaugural run of the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_and_Ohio_Railroad" title="Baltimore and Ohio Railroad">Baltimore and Ohio Railroad</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Royal_Blue_(train)" title="Royal Blue (train)"><i>Royal Blue</i></a> from Washington, D.C., to New York City, the first U.S. passenger train to use <a href="/wiki/Railway_electrification_system" title="Railway electrification system">electric locomotives</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1898" title="1898">1898</a> – The first solo <a href="/wiki/Circumnavigation" title="Circumnavigation">circumnavigation</a> of the globe is completed by <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Slocum" title="Joshua Slocum">Joshua Slocum</a> from Briar Island, <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1905" title="1905">1905</a> – During the <a href="/wiki/Russo-Japanese_War" title="Russo-Japanese War">Russo-Japanese War</a>, sailors start a mutiny aboard the <a href="/wiki/Russian_battleship_Potemkin" title="Russian battleship Potemkin">Russian battleship Potemkin</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1927" title="1927">1927</a> – <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Japan" title="Prime Minister of Japan">Prime Minister of Japan</a> <a href="/wiki/Tanaka_Giichi" title="Tanaka Giichi">Tanaka Giichi</a> leads a conference to discuss <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japan</a>'s plans for China; later, a document detailing these plans, the "<a href="/wiki/Tanaka_Memorial" title="Tanaka Memorial">Tanaka Memorial</a>" is leaked, although it is now considered a forgery.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1941" title="1941">1941</a> – <a href="/wiki/Romania_in_World_War_II" title="Romania in World War II">Romanian authorities</a> launch <a href="/wiki/Ia%C8%99i_pogrom" title="Iași pogrom">one of the most violent pogroms</a> in Jewish history in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ia%C8%99i" title="Iași">Iași</a>, resulting in the murder of at least 13,266 Jews.</li>
<li>1941 – German troops capture the city of <a href="/wiki/Bia%C5%82ystok" title="Białystok">Białystok</a> during <a href="/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">Operation Barbarossa</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1946" title="1946">1946</a> – In the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Citizenship_Act_1946" title="Canadian Citizenship Act 1946">Canadian Citizenship Act</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Canada" title="Parliament of Canada">Parliament of Canada</a> establishes the definition of Canadian citizenship.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1950" title="1950">1950</a> – The United States decides to send troops to fight in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>.</li>
<li>1950 – <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_83" title="United Nations Security Council Resolution 83">United Nations Security Council Resolution 83</a> is adopted.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1952" title="1952">1952</a> – Guatemala passes <a href="/wiki/Decree_900" title="Decree 900">Decree 900</a>, ordering the redistribution of uncultivated land.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1954" title="1954">1954</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Obninsk_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Obninsk Nuclear Power Plant">Obninsk Nuclear Power Plant</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>'s first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_power_station" title="Nuclear power station">nuclear power station</a>, opens in <a href="/wiki/Obninsk" title="Obninsk">Obninsk</a>, near Moscow.</li>
<li>1954 – The <a href="/wiki/1954_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1954 FIFA World Cup">1954 FIFA World Cup</a> quarterfinal match between <a href="/wiki/Hungary_national_football_team" title="Hungary national football team">Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a>, highly anticipated to be exciting, instead <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Berne_(1954_FIFA_World_Cup)" title="Battle of Berne (1954 FIFA World Cup)">turns violent</a>, with three players ejected and further fighting continuing after the game.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1957" title="1957">1957</a> – <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Audrey" title="Hurricane Audrey">Hurricane Audrey</a> makes landfall near the <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>–<a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> border, killing over 400 people, mainly in and around <a href="/wiki/Cameron,_Louisiana" title="Cameron, Louisiana">Cameron, Louisiana</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1971" title="1971">1971</a> – After only three years in business, rock promoter <a href="/wiki/Bill_Graham_(promoter)" title="Bill Graham (promoter)">Bill Graham</a> closes <a href="/wiki/Fillmore_East" title="Fillmore East">Fillmore East</a> in New York, the "Church of Rock and Roll".</li>
