<p><b>Unsinkable Sam</b> (also known as <b>Oskar</b> or <b>Oscar</b>) was the nickname of an alleged German <a href="/wiki/Ship%27s_cat" title="Ship's cat">ship's cat</a> who reportedly saw service in both the <a href="/wiki/Kriegsmarine" title="Kriegsmarine">Kriegsmarine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, serving on board three vessels and surviving the sinking of all three.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100_cats_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-100_cats-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service">Service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unsinkable_Sam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bismarck"><i>Bismarck</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unsinkable_Sam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bismarck">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_193-04-1-26,_Schlachtschiff_Bismarck.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="502" data-file-width="799" height="138" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Bundesarchiv_Bild_193-04-1-26%2C_Schlachtschiff_Bismarck.jpg/220px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_193-04-1-26%2C_Schlachtschiff_Bismarck.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Bundesarchiv_Bild_193-04-1-26%2C_Schlachtschiff_Bismarck.jpg/330px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_193-04-1-26%2C_Schlachtschiff_Bismarck.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Bundesarchiv_Bild_193-04-1-26%2C_Schlachtschiff_Bismarck.jpg/440px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_193-04-1-26%2C_Schlachtschiff_Bismarck.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_193-04-1-26,_Schlachtschiff_Bismarck.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i>Bismarck</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The black and white patched cat had been owned by an unknown crewman of the German battleship <a href="/wiki/German_battleship_Bismarck" title="German battleship Bismarck"><i>Bismarck</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100_cats_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-100_cats-1">[1]</a></sup> He was on board the ship on 18 May 1941 when it set sail on <a href="/wiki/Operation_Rhein%C3%BCbung" title="Operation Rheinübung">Operation <i>Rheinübung</i></a>, <i>Bismarck'</i>s first and only mission. <i>Bismarck</i> was <a href="/wiki/Last_battle_of_the_battleship_Bismarck" title="Last battle of the battleship Bismarck">sunk after a fierce sea-battle</a> on 27 May, from which only 118 from its crew of over 2,200 survived. Hours later, Oskar was found floating on a board and picked from the water, the only survivor (alongside 114 others) to be rescued by the homeward-bound British destroyer <a href="/wiki/HMS_Cossack_(F03)" title="HMS Cossack (F03)">HMS <i>Cossack</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Piekalkiewicz_p142_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Piekalkiewicz_p142-2">[2]</a></sup> Unaware of what his name had been on <i>Bismarck</i>, the crew of <i>Cossack</i> named their new mascot "Oscar".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Piekalkiewicz_p142_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Piekalkiewicz_p142-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="HMS_Cossack">HMS <i>Cossack</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unsinkable_Sam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: HMS Cossack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HMS_Cossack.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="560" data-file-width="785" height="157" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/HMS_Cossack.jpg/220px-HMS_Cossack.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/HMS_Cossack.jpg/330px-HMS_Cossack.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/HMS_Cossack.jpg/440px-HMS_Cossack.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HMS_Cossack.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
HMS <i>Cossack</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The cat served on board <i>Cossack</i> for the next few months as the ship carried out convoy escort duties in the Mediterranean and north Atlantic. On 24 October 1941, <i>Cossack</i> was escorting a convoy from <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a> to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> when it was severely damaged by a <a href="/wiki/Torpedo" title="Torpedo">torpedo</a> fired by the <a href="/wiki/German_submarine_U-563" title="German submarine U-563">German submarine <i>U-563</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Piekalkiewicz_p170_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Piekalkiewicz_p170-3">[3]</a></sup> Crew were transferred to the destroyer <a href="/wiki/HMS_Legion_(G74)" title="HMS Legion (G74)">HMS <i>Legion</i></a>, and an attempt was made to tow the badly listing <i>Cossack</i> back to Gibraltar, but worsening weather conditions meant the task became impossible and had to be abandoned. On 27 October, a day after the tow was slipped, <i>Cossack</i> sank to the west of Gibraltar. The initial explosion had blown off one third of the forward section of the ship, killing 159 of the crew, but Oscar survived this too and was brought to the shore establishment in Gibraltar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Piekalkiewicz_p170_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Piekalkiewicz_p170-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="HMS_Ark_Royal">HMS <i>Ark Royal</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unsinkable_Sam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: HMS Ark Royal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HMS_Ark_Royal_sinking.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="574" data-file-width="773" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/HMS_Ark_Royal_sinking.jpg/220px-HMS_Ark_Royal_sinking.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/HMS_Ark_Royal_sinking.jpg/330px-HMS_Ark_Royal_sinking.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/HMS_Ark_Royal_sinking.jpg/440px-HMS_Ark_Royal_sinking.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HMS_Ark_Royal_sinking.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
HMS <i>Ark Royal</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Now nicknamed "Unsinkable Sam", he was soon transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carrier</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Ark_Royal_(91)" title="HMS Ark Royal (91)">HMS <i>Ark Royal</i></a>, which coincidentally had been instrumental in the destruction of <i>Bismarck</i>. However, Sam was to find no more luck there, and when returning from <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a> on 14 November 1941, this ship too was torpedoed, this time by <a href="/wiki/German_submarine_U-81_(1941)" title="German submarine U-81 (1941)"><i>U-81</i></a>. Attempts were also made to tow <i>Ark Royal</i> to Gibraltar, but the unstoppable inflow of water made the task futile. The carrier rolled over and sank 30 miles from Gibraltar. The slow rate at which the ship sank meant that all but one of the crew could be saved. The survivors, including Sam, who had been found clinging to a floating plank by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motor_launch" title="Motor launch">motor launch</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iwm_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-iwm-4">[4]</a></sup> and described as "angry but quite unharmed"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jameson_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jameson-5">[5]</a></sup> were transferred to <a href="/wiki/HMS_Lightning_(G55)" title="HMS Lightning (G55)">HMS <i>Lightning</i></a> and the same <a href="/wiki/HMS_Legion_(G74)" title="HMS Legion (G74)">HMS <i>Legion</i></a> which had rescued the crew of <i>Cossack</i>. <i>Legion</i> would itself be sunk in 1942, and <i>Lightning</i> in 1943.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">Retirement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unsinkable_Sam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Retirement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The loss of <i>Ark Royal</i> proved the end of Sam's shipborne career and he was transferred first to the offices of the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Gibraltar" title="Governor of Gibraltar">Governor</a> in Gibraltar, and then sent back to the United Kingdom, where he saw out the remainder of the war living in a seaman's home in <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a> called the "Home for Sailors".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Sam died in 1955.</p>
<p>A pastel portrait of Sam (titled "Oscar, the Bismarck's Cat") by the artist Georgina Shaw-Baker is in the possession of the <a href="/wiki/National_Maritime_Museum" title="National Maritime Museum">National Maritime Museum</a>, Greenwich.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iwm_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-iwm-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Existence">Existence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Unsinkable_Sam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Existence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some authorities question whether Oskar/Sam's biography might be a "<a href="/wiki/Nautical_fiction" title="Nautical fiction">sea story</a>", because for example, there are pictures of two different cats identified as Oskar/Sam. The sinking of the <i>Bismarck</i>, and rescue of a limited number of survivors, took place in desperate conditions; British ships were ordered not to stop as there was believed to be a U-boat in the area and many survivors were left to drown. There is no mention of this incident in <a href="/wiki/Ludovic_Kennedy" title="Ludovic Kennedy">Ludovic Kennedy</a>'s detailed account of the sinking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
