<p>The <b>Nesomyidae</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Family" title="Family">family</a> of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> <a href="/wiki/Rodent" title="Rodent">rodents</a> in the large and complex superfamily <a href="/wiki/Muroidea" title="Muroidea">Muroidea</a>. It includes several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subfamilies" title="Subfamilies">subfamilies</a>, all of which are native to either continental Africa or to <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>. Included in this family are <a href="/wiki/Nesomyinae" title="Nesomyinae">Malagasy rats and mice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dendromurinae" title="Dendromurinae">climbing mice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Petromyscus" title="Petromyscus">African rock mice</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Delanymys" title="Delanymys">swamp mice</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cricetomyinae" title="Cricetomyinae">pouched rats</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mystromys_albicaudatus" title="Mystromys albicaudatus">white-tailed rat</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nesomyidae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nesomyids are small- to medium-sized rodents, with the largest being the size of a <a href="/wiki/Rat" title="Rat">rat</a>. Physically, they may resemble <a href="/wiki/Mouse" title="Mouse">mice</a>, rats, <a href="/wiki/Vole" title="Vole">voles</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Hamster" title="Hamster">hamsters</a>, depending on the species and subfamily. Their diets vary from fairly strict <a href="/wiki/Herbivore" title="Herbivore">herbivory</a> to nearly pure <a href="/wiki/Insectivore" title="Insectivore">insectivory</a>. Their habits are similarly variable, with some species climbing trees, and others burrowing in the ground. They give birth to up to four young after a <a href="/wiki/Gestation" title="Gestation">gestation</a> period around six weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EoM_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-EoM-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nesomyidae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many of these animals were once thought to be related to other groups of <a href="/wiki/Muroidea" title="Muroidea">muroid</a> <a href="/wiki/Rodent" title="Rodent">rodents</a>, but this African-based <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">clade</a> has been proposed and confirmed on the basis of <a href="/wiki/Gene" title="Gene">genetic</a> studies. Such alternate arrangement include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cricetomyinae" title="Cricetomyinae">pouched rats</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Muridae" title="Muridae">Muridae</a>, and the white-tailed rat in the family <a href="/wiki/Cricetidae" title="Cricetidae">Cricetidae</a>. Likewise, all members of the Nesomyidae are often placed in the family Muridae along with all other members of the Muroidea.</p>
<p>Nesomyids are classified in six <a href="/wiki/Subfamily" title="Subfamily">subfamilies</a>, 21 <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genera</a>, and 68 <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>.</p>
