<p>The <b>red-faced spider monkey</b> (<i>Ateles paniscus</i>) also known as the <b>Guiana spider monkey</b> or <b>red-faced black spider monkey</b>, is a species of <a href="/wiki/Spider_monkey" title="Spider monkey">spider monkey</a> found in the rain forests in northern <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.</p>
<p>The species faces issues with <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a> and habitat loss, so is listed as <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species">Vulnerable</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IUCN_Redlist" title="IUCN Redlist">IUCN Redlist</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-faced_spider_monkey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Macaco-Aranha_(Red-Faced_Spider_Monkey).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2882" data-file-width="4323" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Macaco-Aranha_%28Red-Faced_Spider_Monkey%29.jpg/300px-Macaco-Aranha_%28Red-Faced_Spider_Monkey%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Macaco-Aranha_%28Red-Faced_Spider_Monkey%29.jpg/450px-Macaco-Aranha_%28Red-Faced_Spider_Monkey%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Macaco-Aranha_%28Red-Faced_Spider_Monkey%29.jpg/600px-Macaco-Aranha_%28Red-Faced_Spider_Monkey%29.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Macaco-Aranha_(Red-Faced_Spider_Monkey).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<i>Ateles paniscus</i></div>
</div>

<p>The red-faced spider monkey has long, black hair and a red or pink face that is bare except for a few short, white hairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CawthonLang2007_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-CawthonLang2007-4">[4]</a></sup> Infants are born with dark faces, which lighten as they age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CawthonLang2007_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-CawthonLang2007-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism" title="Sexual dimorphism">Sexual dimorphism</a> in the species is small; the head-body length of the male is 55.7 centimetres (21.9 in) on average, while the female is around 55.2 cm (21.7 in) in length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CawthonLang2007_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-CawthonLang2007-4">[4]</a></sup> The male weighs around 9.1 kg (20 lb), while the female weighs around 8.4 kg (19 lb).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup> The tail is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prehensile" title="Prehensile">prehensile</a> (capable of grasping) and its fingers and limbs are long, agile and strong.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behavior">Behavior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-faced_spider_monkey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behavior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The red-faced spider monkey exhibits a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fission-fusion_society" title="Fission-fusion society">fission-fusion society</a>, associating with large groups of up to 30 individuals during the night, but choosing to spend the days travelling, foraging, and resting in much smaller groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CawthonLang2007_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-CawthonLang2007-4">[4]</a></sup> At dusk, they recongregate using a greeting call to communicate. At night, they often sleep in large groups called bands. Bands typically consist of several females, with their respective young, along with a few males for protection. The only range size estimate was 255 hectares (630 acres), of which 220 ha (540 acres) had suitable habitat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>The red-faced spider monkey feeds on a variety of foods and would be considered an omnivore. It will eat termites and grubs, but also feeds on supple leaves, flowers, berries and fruit.</p>
<p>It has a gestation period of 226–232 days, is <a href="/wiki/Weaning" title="Weaning">weaned</a> after four or five years when it reaches sexual maturity, and has a life span of up to 33 years in captivity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CawthonLang2007_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-CawthonLang2007-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat_and_distribution">Habitat and distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-faced_spider_monkey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat and distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The red-faced spider monkey is a habitat specialist, found in undisturbed primary rainforests,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CawthonLang2007_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-CawthonLang2007-4">[4]</a></sup> in northern Brazil, Suriname, Guyana, and French Guiana.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup> Because of its ability to climb and jump, the red-faced spider monkey tends to live in the upper layers of the rainforest trees and forages in the high canopy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CawthonLang2007_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-CawthonLang2007-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-faced_spider_monkey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The red-faced spider monkey occurs in many protected area across its range,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup> and is protected in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon_Rainforest" title="Amazon Rainforest">Amazon</a> under the Amazon Animal Protection Act of 1973. It is listed by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">IUCN Red List</a> as <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species">Vulnerable</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
