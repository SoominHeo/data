<p><b>Elisabeth of Württemberg</b> (Elisabeth Wilhelmine Luise; 21 April 1767 – 18 February 1790) was by birth a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duchess_of_W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Duchess of Württemberg">Duchess of Württemberg</a> and by marriage an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archduchess_of_Austria" title="Archduchess of Austria">Archduchess of Austria</a>.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duchess_Elisabeth_of_W%C3%BCrttemberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She was the eighth of the twelve children of <a href="/wiki/Frederick_II_Eugene,_Duke_of_W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Frederick II Eugene, Duke of Württemberg">Frederick II Eugene, Duke of Württemberg</a>, by his wife, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Margravine_Sophia_Dorothea_of_Brandenburg-Schwedt" title="Margravine Sophia Dorothea of Brandenburg-Schwedt">Sophia Dorothea of Brandenburg-Schwedt</a>. She was baptised with the names <i>Elisabeth Wilhelmine Luise</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Duchess_Elisabeth_of_W%C3%BCrttemberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the age of 15, she was called by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Joseph II, Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor Joseph II</a> to Vienna and educated in the <i>Salesianerinnenkloster</i> where she also converted to Catholicism. The purpose of this was to make her the future wife of Joseph II's nephew <a href="/wiki/Francis_II,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Francis II, Holy Roman Emperor">Francis</a>, the future emperor.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a> on 6 January 1788, Elisabeth and Francis were married. At this time, Emperor Joseph was in ill health; the young archduchess was close to the emperor and brightened his last years with her youthful charm. At the end of 1789, Elisabeth became pregnant; however, her condition was very delicate. On the night of 18 February, she prematurely gave birth to <a href="/wiki/Archduchess_Ludovika_Elisabeth_of_Austria" title="Archduchess Ludovika Elisabeth of Austria">Archduchess Ludovika Elisabeth of Austria</a>, who lived for only 16 months. Elisabeth didn't survive the childbirth, which lasted more than 24 hours, and died, despite an emergency operation to save her life.</p>
<p>She is buried in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Crypt" title="Imperial Crypt">Imperial Crypt</a>, in Vienna.</p>
