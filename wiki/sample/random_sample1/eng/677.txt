<p>Year <b>700</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCC</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Leap year starting on Thursday">leap year starting on Thursday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 700 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Byzantine Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pannonian_Avars" title="Pannonian Avars">Avar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slavs" title="Slavs">Slavic tribes</a> conquer <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> territories in the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a>, occupying lands as far south as the <a href="/wiki/Peloponnese" title="Peloponnese">Peloponnese</a> peninsula in southern <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Cunipert" title="Cunipert">Cunipert</a> dies after a 12-year reign, and is succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Liutpert" title="Liutpert">Liutpert</a>. He rules the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lombard_Kingdom" title="Lombard Kingdom">Lombard Kingdom</a> together with <a href="/wiki/Ansprand" title="Ansprand">Ansprand</a>, duke of <a href="/wiki/Asti" title="Asti">Asti</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regent</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Raginpert" title="Raginpert">Raginpert</a>, duke of <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>, deposes King Liutpert after an eight months' reign. He <a href="/wiki/Usurper" title="Usurper">usurps</a> the Lombard <a href="/wiki/Throne" title="Throne">throne</a> and puts his son <a href="/wiki/Aripert_II" title="Aripert II">Aripert</a> in line for the succession.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Byz700_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Byz700-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pepin_of_Herstal" title="Pepin of Herstal">Pepin of Herstal</a>, mayor of the palace, extends the <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Frankish Kingdom</a> and annexes <a href="/wiki/Thuringia" title="Thuringia">Thuringia</a>. He turns the war towards the <a href="/wiki/Alemanni" title="Alemanni">Alemanni</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li class="mw-empty-elt"></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Geraint_of_Dumnonia" title="Geraint of Dumnonia">Geraint of Dumnonia</a> receives a letter from <a href="/wiki/Aldhelm" title="Aldhelm">Aldhelm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Sherborne" title="Bishop of Sherborne">bishop of Sherborne</a>, who insists that the <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Christianity" title="Celtic Christianity">Celtic Church</a> comply with the <a href="/wiki/Doctrine" title="Doctrine">doctrines</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>.</li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Ine_of_Wessex" title="Ine of Wessex">Ine of Wessex</a> begins to dispense with <a href="/wiki/Wessex" title="Wessex">Wessex</a> sub-kings and replace them with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ealdormen" title="Ealdormen">ealdormen</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/E%C3%B3ganachta" title="Eóganachta">Eóganachta</a>, an Irish dynasty centred around <a href="/wiki/Rock_of_Cashel" title="Rock of Cashel">Cashel</a>, begins to dominate southern <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/History_of_Southampton#Anglo_Saxon_period_.28400.E2.80.931066.29" title="History of Southampton">Hamwic</a> emerges as a major Wessex <a href="/wiki/Trade" title="Trade">trading</a> town (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Arabian_Empire">Arabian Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Arabian Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Abd_al-Rahman_ibn_Muhammad_ibn_al-Ash%27ath" title="Abd al-Rahman ibn Muhammad ibn al-Ash'ath">Mohammad ibn al-Ash'ath</a> revolts against Caliph <a href="/wiki/Abd_al-Malik_ibn_Marwan" title="Abd al-Malik ibn Marwan">Abd al-Malik ibn Marwan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sistan" title="Sistan">Sistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Balochistan" title="Balochistan">Balochistan</a> regions (<a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>).</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad</a> prince <a href="/wiki/Abdallah_ibn_Abd_al-Malik" title="Abdallah ibn Abd al-Malik">Abdallah ibn Abd al-Malik</a> captures the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> stronghold of <a href="/wiki/Erzurum" title="Erzurum">Theodosiopolis</a> in <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Byz700_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Byz700-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Musa_bin_Nusair" title="Musa bin Nusair">Musa ibn Nusayr</a> defeats the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berber_people" title="Berber people">Berber</a> forces in <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>, ending resistance against the <a href="/wiki/Arab_Muslims" title="Arab Muslims">Arabs</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li>The African <a href="/wiki/History_of_slavery#Islamic_powers" title="History of slavery">slave trade</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> is so extensive, that the town of <a href="/wiki/Mahdia" title="Mahdia">Zawila</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>) is established.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mesoamerica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maya_Civilization" title="Maya Civilization">Maya Civilization</a>: <a href="/wiki/Tikal_Temple_I" title="Tikal Temple I">Tikal Temple I</a>, called the "Temple of the Giant Jaguar" (tomb of <a href="/wiki/Jasaw_Chan_K%27awiil_I" title="Jasaw Chan K'awiil I">Jasaw Chan K'awiil I</a>), <a href="/wiki/Tikal" title="Tikal">Tikal</a>, (<a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>), is built .</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Mount_Edziza_volcanic_complex" title="Mount Edziza volcanic complex">Mount Edziza volcanic complex</a> erupts in northern <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Diquis_culture" title="Diquis culture">Diquis culture</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>) starts in <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="South_America">South America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: South America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wari%27_people" title="Wari' people">Wari people</a> invade and occupy the <a href="/wiki/Cusco" title="Cusco">Cuzco Valley</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>) in the southern highlands (approximate date).</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Moche_culture" title="Moche culture">Moche culture</a> in the northern part of modern day <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> collapses, largely due to environmental problems and/or political and social unrest (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Art">Art</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Art">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Amit%C4%81bha" title="Amitābha">Amida Buddha</a>, a fresco in the <a href="/wiki/Main_Hall_(Japanese_Buddhism)" title="Main Hall (Japanese Buddhism)">kon-dō</a> (<a href="/wiki/H%C5%8Dry%C5%AB-ji" title="Hōryū-ji">Hōryū-ji Temple</a>), is made (<a href="/wiki/Nara_period" title="Nara period">Nara period</a>) (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=700&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adomn%C3%A1n" title="Adomnán">Adomnán</a>, Irish <a href="/wiki/Abbot" title="Abbot">abbot</a>, convinces 51 kings to adopt the <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1in_Adomn%C3%A1in" title="Cáin Adomnáin">Cáin Adomnáin</a>, which defines the relationship between women and <a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">priests</a>.</li>
<li>Queen <a href="/wiki/Cuthburh" title="Cuthburh">Cuthburh</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northumbria" title="Northumbria">Northumbria</a> enters <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religious</a> life. The <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon_architecture" title="Anglo-Saxon architecture">Anglo-Saxon</a> religious community at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lady_St._Mary_Church,_Wareham" title="Lady St. Mary Church, Wareham">St. Mary's Nunnery</a> is re-founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Willibrord" title="Willibrord">Willibrord</a>, Anglo-Saxon <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionary</a>, founds a mission post at <a href="/wiki/Emmerich_am_Rhein" title="Emmerich am Rhein">Emmerich am Rhein</a> (<a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>), in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishopric_of_Utrecht" title="Bishopric of Utrecht">Utrecht</a> Diocese.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Beverley_Grammar_School" title="Beverley Grammar School">Beverley Grammar School</a> (<a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Yorkshire</a>) is founded by bishop <a href="/wiki/John_of_Beverley" title="John of Beverley">John of Beverley</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Lindisfarne_Gospels" title="Lindisfarne Gospels">Lindisfarne Gospels</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Illuminated_manuscript" title="Illuminated manuscript">illuminated manuscript</a> (<a href="/wiki/Gospel_Book" title="Gospel Book">Gospel Book</a>), is produced in Northumbria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>The famous <a href="/wiki/Miracle_of_Lanciano" title="Miracle of Lanciano">Catholic Eucharistic Miracle</a> occurs in <a href="/wiki/Lanciano" title="Lanciano">Lanciano</a> (<a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>).</li>
</ul>
