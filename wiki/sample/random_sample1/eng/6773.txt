<p><b>BrahMos-II</b> or <b>BrahMos-2</b> or <b>BrahMos Mark II</b> (note: not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/BrahMos" title="BrahMos">BrahMos</a> block-2) is a <a href="/wiki/Cruise_missile#Hypersonic" title="Cruise missile">hypersonic cruise missile</a> currently under joint development by <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NPO_Mashinostroeyenia" title="NPO Mashinostroeyenia">NPO Mashinostroeyenia</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Defence_Research_and_Development_Organisation" title="Defence Research and Development Organisation">Defence Research and Development Organisation</a>, which have together formed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BrahMos_Aerospace_Private_Limited" title="BrahMos Aerospace Private Limited">BrahMos Aerospace Private Limited</a>. It is the second of the <a href="/wiki/BrahMos" title="BrahMos">BrahMos</a> series of cruise missiles. The BrahMos-II is expected to have a range of 290 kilometres (180 mi; 160 nmi) and a speed of Mach 7. During the cruise stage of flight the missile will be propelled by a <a href="/wiki/Scramjet" title="Scramjet">scramjet</a> <a href="/wiki/Airbreathing_jet_engine" title="Airbreathing jet engine">airbreathing jet engine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiatimes1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-indiatimes1-2">[2]</a></sup> Other details, including production cost and physical dimensions of the missile, are yet to be published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brahmos2_official_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-brahmos2_official-5">[5]</a></sup> It is expected to be ready for testing by 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The planned operational range of the BrahMos-II has been restricted to 290 kilometers as Russia is a signatory to the <a href="/wiki/Missile_Technology_Control_Regime" title="Missile Technology Control Regime">Missile Technology Control Regime</a> (MTCR), which prohibits it from helping other countries develop missiles with ranges above 300 kilometres (190 mi; 160 nmi). However, now that India is also a MTCR signatory, it is trying to extend the range of BrahMos.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brahmos2_official_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-brahmos2_official-5">[5]</a></sup> Its top speed will be double that of the current BrahMos-I, and it has been described as the fastest cruise missile in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-brahmos2_eco_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-brahmos2_eco-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brahmos2_ria_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brahmos2_ria-8">[8]</a></sup> Russia is developing a special and secret fuel formula to enable the BrahMos-II to exceed Mach 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Design of multiple variants of the missile was completed by October 2011, with testing starting in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Fourth-generation multi-purpose Russian Naval destroyers (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Project_21956" title="Project 21956">Project 21956</a>) are also likely to be equipped with the BrahMos II.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>BrahMos Aerospace named the missile <i>BrahMos-II (K)</i> in honour of the former <a href="/wiki/President_of_India" title="President of India">President of India</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APJ_Abdul_Kalam" title="APJ Abdul Kalam">APJ Abdul Kalam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-htsingh_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-htsingh-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The BrahMos-II is suspected to be the export variant of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3M22_Tsirkon" title="3M22 Tsirkon">3M22 Tsirkon</a>, a hypersonic <a href="/wiki/Anti-ship_missile" title="Anti-ship missile">anti-ship missile</a> being developed by Russia for its <a href="/wiki/Kirov-class_battlecruiser" title="Kirov-class battlecruiser"><i>Kirov</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Battlecruiser" title="Battlecruiser">battlecruisers</a>, just as the original BrahMos was in relation to the <a href="/wiki/P-800_Oniks" title="P-800 Oniks">P-800 Oniks</a> it was developed from.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
