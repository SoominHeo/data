<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><script>function mfTempOpenSection(id){var block=document.getElementById("mf-section-"+id);block.className+=" open-block";block.previousSibling.className+=" open-block";}</script><div class="hatnote" role="note">This article is about an award for young footballers. <p>The <b>Bravo Award</b> is an annual award which is handed out by the Italian magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Guerin_Sportivo" title="Guerin Sportivo">Guerin Sportivo</a></i> to the most outstanding young <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>. The first winner of the award was Englishman <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Case" title="Jimmy Case">Jimmy Case</a>. Until 1992, only under-23 players participating in one of the three European club cups (<a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a>, <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a>, <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="UEFA Cup Winners' Cup">UEFA Cup Winners' Cup</a>) were eligible. Since 1992, any under-21 player from any European League is eligible.</p>
<p>The winners are chosen for the autumn-spring season, not the calendar year.</p>
