<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Br%C3%ADan_F._O%27Byrne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>O'Byrne was born <b>Brían Francis O'Byrne</b> in <a href="/wiki/Mullagh,_County_Cavan" title="Mullagh, County Cavan">Mullagh</a>, a village in the south-east of <a href="/wiki/County_Cavan" title="County Cavan">County Cavan</a>, on 16 May 1967. He attended the Samuel Beckett Centre at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College</a> (T.C.D.) in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>. He moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 1990, and was cast in the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Repertory_Theatre" title="Irish Repertory Theatre">Irish Repertory Theatre</a> production of <i><a href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Here_I_Come!" title="Philadelphia, Here I Come!">Philadelphia, Here I Come!</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Br%C3%ADan_F._O%27Byrne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>O'Byrne first attracted notice for his performances in the <a href="/wiki/Martin_McDonagh" title="Martin McDonagh">Martin McDonagh</a> plays <i><a href="/wiki/The_Beauty_Queen_of_Leenane" title="The Beauty Queen of Leenane">The Beauty Queen of Leenane</a></i> (1996) as Pato Dooley (for which he received a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> nomination for Best Featured Actor in a Play) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lonesome_West" title="The Lonesome West">The Lonesome West</a></i> (1997). He is known for taking on serious and dramatic roles, such as a <a href="/wiki/Serial_killer" title="Serial killer">serial killer</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Frozen_(play)" title="Frozen (play)">Frozen</a></i> (1998) (for which he won a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a>) and a priest accused of <a href="/wiki/Child_sexual_abuse" title="Child sexual abuse">child molestation</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Doubt:_A_Parable" title="Doubt: A Parable">Doubt: A Parable</a></i> (2004), for which he received a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> nomination. O'Byrne also appeared as a priest in the 2004 film <i><a href="/wiki/Million_Dollar_Baby" title="Million Dollar Baby">Million Dollar Baby</a></i>. In May 2007, O'Byrne was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> for his performance as <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Herzen" title="Alexander Herzen">Alexander Herzen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tom_Stoppard" title="Tom Stoppard">Tom Stoppard</a>'s 2002 trilogy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Coast_of_Utopia" title="The Coast of Utopia">The Coast of Utopia</a></i>. He appeared in the play by <a href="/wiki/John_Patrick_Shanley" title="John Patrick Shanley">John Patrick Shanley</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Outside_Mullingar" title="Outside Mullingar">Outside Mullingar</a></i> on Broadway in 2014, and received a nomination for the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Outer_Critics_Circle_Award" title="Outer Critics Circle Award">Outer Critics Circle Award</a>, Outstanding Actor In A Play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, O'Byrne was nominated for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actor_in_a_Miniseries_or_a_Movie" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Miniseries or a Movie">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Miniseries or Movie</a> for his performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Mildred_Pierce_(miniseries)" title="Mildred Pierce (miniseries)">Mildred Pierce</a></i> as Bert Pierce. O'Byrne was featured in the 2009–2010 <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/FlashForward" title="FlashForward">FlashForward</a></i>, in which he played <a href="/wiki/FlashForward#Main_characters" title="FlashForward">Aaron Stark</a>. The show was not renewed. <i>FlashForward</i> began airing in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> on January 4, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, O'Byrne was cast in the ABC drama pilot <i><a href="/wiki/Gilded_Lilys" title="Gilded Lilys">Gilded Lilys</a></i> created and produced by <a href="/wiki/Shonda_Rhimes" title="Shonda Rhimes">Shonda Rhimes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Br%C3%ADan_F._O%27Byrne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>O'Byrne is married to American actress <a href="/wiki/Heather_Goldenhersh" title="Heather Goldenhersh">Heather Goldenhersh</a>, with whom he co-starred in the Broadway play <i>Doubt: A Parable</i>. The couple have two daughters together, the first born in 2008, the second in 2010.</p>
