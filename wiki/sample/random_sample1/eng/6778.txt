<p><b>Brian Ernest Gordon</b> (born August 16, 1978) is an American professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> who is currently a free agent. He has played for the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/SK_Wyverns" title="SK Wyverns">SK Wyverns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Lions" title="Samsung Lions">Samsung Lions</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Baseball_Organization" title="Korea Baseball Organization">Korea Baseball Organization</a> in South Korea.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His parents are Ernie Gordon, who grew up a Yankees fan in <a href="/wiki/Hyde_Park,_New_York" title="Hyde Park, New York">Hyde Park, New York</a>, and Wendy Abrahamsen. They have since divorced. His father was an Army engineer stationed at West Point when he was born,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> but the family moved to Round Rock, Texas, before he was 2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His mother works as a secretary for the <a href="/wiki/Round_Rock_Express" title="Round Rock Express">Round Rock Express</a>.</p>
<p>At <a href="/wiki/Round_Rock_High_School" title="Round Rock High School">Round Rock High School</a>, his career record was 43-3, and his team, the Dragons, won the Texas class 5A state championship in 1997.</p>
<p>Gordon and his wife, Amanda, have a daughter, Finley and a son, Riggs.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Gordon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gordon was drafted by the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a> in the 7th round of the <a href="/wiki/1997_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="1997 Major League Baseball draft">1997 Major League Baseball draft</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Outfielder" title="Outfielder">outfielder</a>. With the Diamondbacks organization, he made it as high as Triple-A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tucson_Sidewinders" title="Tucson Sidewinders">Tucson</a> in <a href="/wiki/2003_in_baseball" title="2003 in baseball">2003</a> and became a <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agent</a> after the season. Gordon signed with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anaheim_Angels" title="Anaheim Angels">Anaheim Angels</a> where he played in the next two years with their Triple-A affiliate, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_Stingers" title="Salt Lake Stingers">Salt Lake Stingers</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/2005_in_baseball" title="2005 in baseball">2005</a> season, Gordon signed with the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> organization. Playing the entire year in Triple-A, he <a href="/wiki/Batting_average" title="Batting average">batted</a> only .241, and after the season decided to convert to pitcher<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> with the help of Hall-of-Famer <a href="/wiki/Nolan_Ryan" title="Nolan Ryan">Nolan Ryan</a>, who was a special assistant to the Astros.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gordon began <a href="/wiki/2007_in_baseball" title="2007 in baseball">2007</a> with the Double-A <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi_Hooks" title="Corpus Christi Hooks">Corpus Christi Hooks</a>. In 30 <a href="/wiki/Relief_pitcher" title="Relief pitcher">relief appearances</a>, he had an <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">ERA</a> of 2.88 and received a promotion to Triple-A <a href="/wiki/Round_Rock_Express" title="Round Rock Express">Round Rock</a>. In <a href="/wiki/2008_in_baseball" title="2008 in baseball">2008</a>, Gordon started the season with Double-A Corpus Christi, but after just one appearance, he was released. He signed with the Texas Rangers and did not allow a <a href="/wiki/Run_(baseball)" title="Run (baseball)">run</a> in 22 innings pitched for Double-A <a href="/wiki/Frisco_RoughRiders" title="Frisco RoughRiders">Frisco</a>. He was promoted to Triple-A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oklahoma_RedHawks" title="Oklahoma RedHawks">Oklahoma</a> where he <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">started</a> and pitched in relief. Gordon was a September call up for the Rangers in <a href="/wiki/2008_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2008 Major League Baseball season">2008</a> and made his major league debut on September 17.</p>
<p>Gordon attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venezuelan_Winter_League" title="Venezuelan Winter League">Venezuelan Winter League</a> for the 2008–2009 season with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiburones_de_la_Guaira" title="Tiburones de la Guaira">Tiburones de la Guaira</a>, posting a 4-2 record with a 1.79 ERA in 45.1 innings. In his last home game before returning to the US, Gordon faced hard rivals <a href="/wiki/Leones_del_Caracas" title="Leones del Caracas">Leones del Caracas</a> in front of a crowd of 20,000, posting 8 innings with 1 allowed run and 9 strikeouts. After the game ended, all la Guaira fans shouted Brian's last name, with Gordon walking out of the dugout and kissing the team's shirt in one of La Guaira's most emotive moments of the season.</p>
<p>Gordon was granted free agency after the 2009 season and he signed with the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a> for the 2010 season. He remained with the Phillies for the start of the 2011 season.</p>
<p>On June 14, 2011, Gordon opted out of his minor league contract with the Phillies and signed with the New York Yankees, who promoted him to the major leagues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In his first MLB start, Gordon pitched <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">5<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings for the Yankees, allowing two earned runs in a Yankees 3-2 victory in 12 innings at Yankee Stadium.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He made history by wearing a non-leather glove, the first major leaguer to do so. When <a href="/wiki/Bartolo_Col%C3%B3n" title="Bartolo Colón">Bartolo Colón</a> was activated from the disabled list on July 2, he was optioned to the Triple-A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scranton/Wilkes-Barre_Yankees" title="Scranton/Wilkes-Barre Yankees">Scranton/Wilkes-Barre Yankees</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>He was purchased by <a href="/wiki/SK_Wyverns" title="SK Wyverns">SK Wyverns</a> in Korea on July 8, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> On January 13, 2012, Gordon opted to stay in Korea, signing with the <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Lions" title="Samsung Lions">Samsung Lions</a>.</p>
<p>Gordon was signed by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> to a minor-league contract on December 3, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On June 15, 2014, the Yankees released Gordon.</p>
