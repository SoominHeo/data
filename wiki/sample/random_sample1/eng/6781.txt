<p><b>Bryan Neathway Brown</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Australia" title="Order of Australia">AM</a> (born 23 June 1947) is an <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> actor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> He has performed in over eighty film and television projects since the late 1970s, both in his native Australia and abroad. Notable films include <i><a href="/wiki/Breaker_Morant_(film)" title="Breaker Morant (film)">Breaker Morant</a></i> (1980), <i><a href="/wiki/Give_My_Regards_to_Broad_Street_(film)" title="Give My Regards to Broad Street (film)">Give My Regards to Broad Street</a></i> (1984), <i><a href="/wiki/F/X" title="F/X">F/X</a></i> (1986), <i><a href="/wiki/Cocktail_(1988_film)" title="Cocktail (1988 film)">Cocktail</a></i> (1988), <i><a href="/wiki/Gorillas_in_the_Mist" title="Gorillas in the Mist">Gorillas in the Mist</a></i> (1988), <i><a href="/wiki/F/X2" title="F/X2">F/X2</a></i> (1991), <i><a href="/wiki/Along_Came_Polly" title="Along Came Polly">Along Came Polly</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/Australia_(2008_film)" title="Australia (2008 film)">Australia</a></i> (2008), <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_Me_Three_Times" title="Kill Me Three Times">Kill Me Three Times</a></i> (2014) and <i><a href="/wiki/Gods_of_Egypt_(film)" title="Gods of Egypt (film)">Gods of Egypt</a></i> (2016). He was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> for his performance in the television <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Thorn_Birds_(miniseries)" title="The Thorn Birds (miniseries)">The Thorn Birds</a></i> (1983).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Brown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brown was born in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, the son of John (Jack) Brown and Molly Brown, a pianist in the early days of the Langshaw School of Ballet, who also worked as a house cleaner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He grew up with his younger sister, Kristine, in the south-western Sydney suburb of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panania,_New_South_Wales" title="Panania, New South Wales">Panania</a>, and began working at <a href="/wiki/AMP_Limited" title="AMP Limited">AMP</a> as an actuarial student. He started to act in amateur theatre performances,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> where he discovered a passion for acting.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Brown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brown went to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">Britain</a> in 1964, eventually winning minor roles at <a href="/wiki/The_Old_Vic" title="The Old Vic">the Old Vic</a>. He returned to Australia and became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Genesian_Theatre" title="Genesian Theatre">Genesian Theatre</a>, Sydney. He appeared in Colleen Clifford's production of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Man_for_All_Seasons" title="A Man for All Seasons">A Man for All Seasons</a></i>, before joining the Queensland Theatre Company. He made his cinema debut in 1977 with a small role in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Love_Letters_from_Teralba_Road" title="The Love Letters from Teralba Road">The Love Letters from Teralba Road</a></i> and appeared in several more Australian films over the next two years such as <i><a href="/wiki/Stir_(film)" title="Stir (film)">Stir</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Money_Movers" title="Money Movers">Money Movers</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1980, Brown became known to international audiences for his performance in <i><a href="/wiki/Breaker_Morant_(film)" title="Breaker Morant (film)">Breaker Morant</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> While he continued appearing in Australian productions, he also appeared in another Australian TV mini-series, "<a href="/wiki/A_Town_Like_Alice_(miniseries)" title="A Town Like Alice (miniseries)">A Town Like Alice</a>", that won popularity in the United States.</p>
<p>He is best known to American television audiences for his role as Luke O'Neil in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Thorn_Birds_(miniseries)" title="The Thorn Birds (miniseries)">The Thorn Birds</a></i> (1983), starring <a href="/wiki/Richard_Chamberlain" title="Richard Chamberlain">Richard Chamberlain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Ward" title="Rachel Ward">Rachel Ward</a> (whom he later married). Brown was nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor_%E2%80%93_Series,_Miniseries_or_Television_Film" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor – Series, Miniseries or Television Film">Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor – Series, Miniseries or Television Film</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actor_%E2%80%93_Miniseries_or_a_Movie" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor – Miniseries or a Movie">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor – Miniseries or a Movie</a> for his work.</p>
<p>He starred in several international productions including <i><a href="/wiki/Tai-Pan_(film)" title="Tai-Pan (film)">Tai-Pan</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Joan_Chen" title="Joan Chen">Joan Chen</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/F/X" title="F/X">F/X – Murder by Illusion</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-2">[2]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Gorillas_in_the_Mist" title="Gorillas in the Mist">Gorillas in the Mist</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Sigourney_Weaver" title="Sigourney Weaver">Sigourney Weaver</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cocktail_(1988_film)" title="Cocktail (1988 film)">Cocktail</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Cruise" title="Tom Cruise">Tom Cruise</a>.</p>
<p>In the 1990s and more recently, Brown appeared in American and Australian TV productions and films, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Two_Hands_(1999_film)" title="Two Hands (1999 film)">Two Hands</a></i> (1999), as well as in British TV commercials.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_work">Production work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Brown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brown's production company made the series <i><a href="/wiki/Twisted_Tales_(TV_series)" title="Twisted Tales (TV series)">Twisted Tales</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Two_Twisted" title="Two Twisted">Two Twisted</a></i> (similar to <i><a href="/wiki/Alfred_Hitchcock_Presents" title="Alfred Hitchcock Presents">Alfred Hitchcock Presents</a></i>). The second series had an additional twist: both stories in each episode were connected in some way, and the audience was invited to try to spot the connection.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours_and_awards">Honours and awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Brown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Honours and awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brown was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Logie_Hall_of_Fame" title="Logie Hall of Fame">Logie Hall of Fame</a> in 1989. He received the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Film_Institute_Award_for_Best_Actor_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="Australian Film Institute Award for Best Actor in a Supporting Role">Australian Film Institute Award for Best Actor in a Supporting Role</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Breaker_Morant_(film)" title="Breaker Morant (film)">Breaker Morant</a></i> (1980) and for <i><a href="/wiki/Two_Hands_(1999_film)" title="Two Hands (1999 film)">Two Hands</a></i> (1999).</p>
<p>In June 2005, Brown was made a Member of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Australia" title="Order of Australia">Order of Australia</a> "for service to the community through a range of charitable organisations committed to providing assistance and support to families and young people and to the Australian film and television industry."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Brown&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When Bryan Brown was first introduced to <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Ward" title="Rachel Ward">Rachel Ward</a> on the set of the TV miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/The_Thorn_Birds_(miniseries)" title="The Thorn Birds (miniseries)">The Thorn Birds</a></i> in 1983, he read her palm and predicted she would have three children. They married a few months after filming wrapped. They have three children, one of whom, <a href="/wiki/Matilda_Brown" title="Matilda Brown">Matilda Brown</a>, is also an actress.</p>
<p>He is a strong supporter of <a href="/wiki/Republicanism_in_Australia" title="Republicanism in Australia">Australian republicanism</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
