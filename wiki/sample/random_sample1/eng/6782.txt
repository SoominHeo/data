<p><b>Bryan Jay Singer</b> (born September 17, 1965)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmref_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-filmref-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American film director, film producer, writer, and actor. He is the founder of <a href="/wiki/Bad_Hat_Harry_Productions" title="Bad Hat Harry Productions">Bad Hat Harry Productions</a> and he has produced or co-produced almost all of the films he has directed. He wrote and directed his first film in 1988 after graduating from university. His next film, <i><a href="/wiki/Public_Access" title="Public Access">Public Access</a></i> (1993), was a co-winner of the Grand Jury Prize at the 1993 <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>. In the mid-1990s, Singer received critical acclaim for directing the neo-noir crime thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Usual_Suspects" title="The Usual Suspects">The Usual Suspects</a></i> (1995), which starred <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Baldwin" title="Stephen Baldwin">Stephen Baldwin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benicio_del_Toro" title="Benicio del Toro">Benicio del Toro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Spacey" title="Kevin Spacey">Kevin Spacey</a>. He followed this with another thriller, <i><a href="/wiki/Apt_Pupil_(film)" title="Apt Pupil (film)">Apt Pupil</a></i> (1998), about a boy's fascination with a Nazi war criminal.</p>
<p>In the 2000s, he became known for big budget superhero films such as <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men_(film)" title="X-Men (film)">X-Men</a></i> (2000), for which Singer won the 2000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saturn_Award_for_Best_Direction" title="Saturn Award for Best Direction">Saturn Award for Best Direction</a>, its sequel <i><a href="/wiki/X2_(film)" title="X2 (film)">X2</a></i> (2003), and <i><a href="/wiki/Superman_Returns" title="Superman Returns">Superman Returns</a></i> (2006). He then directed the WWII historical thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Valkyrie_(film)" title="Valkyrie (film)">Valkyrie</a></i> (2008), co-wrote/co-produced <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_First_Class" title="X-Men: First Class">X-Men: First Class</a></i> (2011), and directed the fantasy adventure film <i><a href="/wiki/Jack_the_Giant_Slayer" title="Jack the Giant Slayer">Jack the Giant Slayer</a></i> (2013), as well as two more X-Men films, <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_Days_of_Future_Past" title="X-Men: Days of Future Past">X-Men: Days of Future Past</a></i> (2014) and <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_Apocalypse" title="X-Men: Apocalypse">X-Men: Apocalypse</a></i> (2016). He has also directed and produced television shows, including two episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i> in 2004.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Singer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Singer was born in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and was adopted by Grace Sinden, an environmental activist, and Norbert Dave Singer, a corporate executive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-filmref_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-filmref-1">[1]</a></sup> He grew up in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jewish</a> household in <a href="/wiki/West_Windsor_Township,_New_Jersey" title="West Windsor Township, New Jersey">West Windsor Township, New Jersey</a>. He attended <a href="/wiki/West_Windsor-Plainsboro_High_School_South" title="West Windsor-Plainsboro High School South">West Windsor-Plainsboro High School South</a> (formerly just West Windsor-Plainsboro High School), graduating in 1984.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> For college, Singer studied filmmaking for two years at New York's <a href="/wiki/School_of_Visual_Arts" title="School of Visual Arts">School of Visual Arts</a> and later transferred to the <a href="/wiki/USC_School_of_Cinematic_Arts" title="USC School of Cinematic Arts">USC School of Cinematic Arts</a> in Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Actors <a href="/wiki/Lori_Singer" title="Lori Singer">Lori</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Singer" title="Marc Singer">Marc Singer</a> are his cousins.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Singer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s.E2.80.931990s">1980s–1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Singer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1980s–1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After graduating from university, Singer directed a short film in 1988 called <i><a href="/wiki/Lion%27s_Den_(1988_film)" title="Lion's Den (1988 film)">Lion's Den</a></i> involving a number of friends, including actor <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Hawke" title="Ethan Hawke">Ethan Hawke</a> whom he knew from his childhood in New Jersey<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and editor <a href="/wiki/John_Ottman" title="John Ottman">John Ottman</a> who he had met while working on a friend's short film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> After a screening of <i>Lion's Den</i>, Singer was approached by someone who knew of a Japanese company that funded low-budget films. Singer wrote the concept for <i><a href="/wiki/Public_Access" title="Public Access">Public Access</a></i> (1993) with high school friend <a href="/wiki/Christopher_McQuarrie" title="Christopher McQuarrie">Christopher McQuarrie</a>, and fellow <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">USC</a> student Michael Feit Dougan wrote the first draft in ten days about a supposedly idyllic small town. Ottman again served as editor but this time also composed the score for the film. At the 1993 <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>, the film was named as co-winner of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Jury_Prize" title="Grand Jury Prize">Grand Jury Prize</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Singer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In early 2001, Singer was planning to direct <i><a href="/wiki/Confessions_of_a_Dangerous_Mind" title="Confessions of a Dangerous Mind">Confessions of a Dangerous Mind</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Depp" title="Johnny Depp">Johnny Depp</a> in the lead role, from <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Kaufman" title="Charlie Kaufman">Charlie Kaufman</a>'s script based on the <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Barris" title="Chuck Barris">Chuck Barris</a> book of the same name. Financial troubles delayed production and Singer moved on. The film was later directed by <a href="/wiki/George_Clooney" title="George Clooney">George Clooney</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miramax_Films" title="Miramax Films">Miramax Films</a> with <a href="/wiki/Sam_Rockwell" title="Sam Rockwell">Sam Rockwell</a> in the lead role. Singer has said that he was "very impressed" by Clooney's debut as a director, and the film itself. In late 2001, Singer was planning to help DeSanto produce a new <i><a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica" title="Battlestar Galactica">Battlestar Galactica</a></i> television series for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studios_USA" title="Studios USA">Studios USA</a> (now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBC_Universal" title="NBC Universal">NBC Universal</a> Television Studio) and the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">FOX</a> network. Singer was scheduled to direct the mini-series which would have served as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Backdoor_pilot" title="Backdoor pilot">backdoor pilot</a> for a potential series. Speaking to <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> in February 2001, Singer said he was "confident that the <i>Galactica</i> brand is a sleeping giant. It was a show I watched during its initial run, from the pilot to the final episode. The essence and the brand name is quite potent in a climate where there's a great deficit of sci-fi programming."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Despite his enthusiasm, production delays caused by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/September_11,_2001_attacks" title="September 11, 2001 attacks">September 11, 2001 attacks</a> meant Singer had to drop out due to his commitment to direct <i><a href="/wiki/X2_(film)" title="X2 (film)">X-Men 2</a></i>. FOX then lost interest in <i>Galactica</i> and Studios USA took the project to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sci_Fi_Channel_(United_States)" title="Sci Fi Channel (United States)">Sci Fi Channel</a> and a different production team. This resulted in the new <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(TV_miniseries)" title="Battlestar Galactica (TV miniseries)">Battlestar Galactica</a></i> 2003 mini-series and 2004 television series, which ran until 2008. By August 2012 the script was being rewritten, with Singer explaining that "It will exist, I think, quite well between the Glen Larson and Ron Moore universes".</p>
<p>In mid-2004, Singer was in negotiations to direct <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_The_Last_Stand" title="X-Men: The Last Stand">X-Men: The Last Stand</a></i> for Fox. Fox and Singer could not meet an agreement and, after an extended détente, Singer was offered the chance to direct the new <a href="/wiki/Superman" title="Superman">Superman</a> film, which was ready to go. On July 19, 2004, <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> reported that Singer had signed on to direct <i><a href="/wiki/Superman_Returns" title="Superman Returns">Superman Returns</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a> In retaliation, Fox terminated their production deal with Bad Hat Harry Productions, Singer's production company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <i>Superman Returns</i> was filmed in Australia in 2005, and was released on June 28, 2006. Singer claims that though he had not read the comics, he had always admired and identified with the character, citing the fact that he and Superman are both orphans. He instead based <i>Returns</i> on his love of the 1978 film made by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Donner" title="Richard Donner">Richard Donner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<p>In August 2009, Universal Pictures announced that Singer would direct and produce a big screen reimagining of the <i><a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica" title="Battlestar Galactica">Battlestar Galactica</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(1978_TV_series)" title="Battlestar Galactica (1978 TV series)">television series</a> of the late-1970s, which would not draw any material from the Syfy Channel <a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(2004_TV_series)" title="Battlestar Galactica (2004 TV series)">reimagined series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On September 10, 2009, it was announced NBC has partnered with Bryan Singer and <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Fuller" title="Bryan Fuller">Bryan Fuller</a> to adapt <a href="/wiki/Augusten_Burroughs" title="Augusten Burroughs">Augusten Burroughs</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Sellevision" title="Sellevision">Sellevision</a></i> into a series. The one-hour dramatic comedy, to be written by Fuller and directed by Singer, will focus on the inner workings of a fictional home shopping network, according to <i>The Hollywood Reporter</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> At the premiere of <a href="/wiki/James_Cameron" title="James Cameron">James Cameron</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar_(2009_film)" title="Avatar (2009 film)">Avatar</a></i> on December 16, 2009, Singer confirmed that he would be directing <i><a href="/wiki/Jack_the_Giant_Slayer" title="Jack the Giant Slayer">Jack the Giant Slayer</a></i> (2013) for <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a>, and that he had signed on to do <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_First_Class" title="X-Men: First Class">X-Men: First Class</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> but conflicts between the two projects led to Singer being only a producer and co-screenwriter on <i>First Class</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deadline_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-deadline-15">[15]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Vaughn" title="Matthew Vaughn">Matthew Vaughn</a> taking over directorial duties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Singer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2012, it was announced that Singer would direct the next movie in the series, <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_Days_of_Future_Past" title="X-Men: Days of Future Past">X-Men: Days of Future Past</a></i>; Vaughn stayed on as a producer and screenwriter, and the film was released in May 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Singer produced the commercials for the ice cream <a href="/wiki/Magnum_(ice_cream)" title="Magnum (ice cream)">Magnum Gold</a>, which featured <a href="/wiki/Benicio_del_Toro" title="Benicio del Toro">Benicio del Toro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2012, Singer was the executive producer alongside <a href="/wiki/Jane_Lynch" title="Jane Lynch">Jane Lynch</a> of the short film, <i>Ronny and I</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> directed by Guy Shalem that screened at Outfest<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and Cannes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <i>Ronny and I</i> was considered a "revolution" in filmmaking due to the fact that the film was shot entirely on a smart phone. Singer directed another X-Men film, <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_Apocalypse" title="X-Men: Apocalypse">X-Men: Apocalypse</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> that he also produced and co-wrote with <a href="/wiki/Simon_Kinberg" title="Simon Kinberg">Simon Kinberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dan_Harris_(screenwriter)" title="Dan Harris (screenwriter)">Dan Harris</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dougherty" title="Michael Dougherty">Michael Dougherty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <i>Days of Future Past</i> stars <a href="/wiki/James_McAvoy" title="James McAvoy">James McAvoy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Fassbender" title="Michael Fassbender">Michael Fassbender</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Hoult" title="Nicholas Hoult">Nicholas Hoult</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lawrence" title="Jennifer Lawrence">Jennifer Lawrence</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Evan_Peters" title="Evan Peters">Evan Peters</a> re-teamed with Singer for <i>Apocalypse</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In November 2016, it was announced that Singer would direct in the upcoming <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a> biopic, <i><a href="/wiki/Bohemian_Rhapsody_(film)" title="Bohemian Rhapsody (film)">Bohemian Rhapsody</a></i>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Singer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Singer is openly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bisexual" title="Bisexual">bisexual</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and has said that his life experiences of growing up as a <a href="/wiki/Minority_group" title="Minority group">minority</a> influenced his movies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In October 2014, it was confirmed he was expecting a child with actress <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Clunie" title="Michelle Clunie">Michelle Clunie</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The couple's first son, Dashiell Julius William Clunie-Singer, was born on January 5, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sexual_abuse_allegations">Sexual abuse allegations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Singer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sexual abuse allegations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1997, a 14-year-old extra accused Singer of asking him and other minors to film a shower scene in the nude for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Apt_Pupil_(film)" title="Apt Pupil (film)">Apt Pupil</a></i>. A lawsuit was filed but dismissed for insufficient evidence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2014, Singer was accused in a civil lawsuit of <a href="/wiki/Sexual_assault" title="Sexual assault">sexual assault</a> of a minor. According to the suit filed by attorney <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Herman" title="Jeff Herman">Jeff Herman</a>, Singer is alleged to have <a href="/wiki/Date_rape" title="Date rape">drugged and raped</a> actor and model Michael Egan in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> and <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> in the late 1990s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Singer's attorney called the allegations "completely fabricated" and said Singer planned to <a href="/wiki/Counterclaim" title="Counterclaim">countersue</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> Singer denied the allegations in a statement calling them "outrageous, vicious, and completely false."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> On May 22, 2014, Singer's attorney presented evidence to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_District_Judge" title="Federal District Judge">Federal District Judge</a> <a href="/wiki/Susan_Oki_Mollway" title="Susan Oki Mollway">Susan Oki Mollway</a> stating that neither Singer nor Egan were in Hawaii at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In early August 2014, Egan sought to withdraw his lawsuit via a Request for Court Order of Dismissal, and asked that it be granted "without prejudice or an award of costs or fees, in the interest of justice."</p>
<p>In May 2014, another lawsuit was filed by attorney Jeff Herman on behalf of an anonymous British man. Both Singer and producer <a href="/wiki/Gary_Goddard" title="Gary Goddard">Gary Goddard</a> (who was also named separately in the first case) were accused of sexually assaulting "John Doe No. 117." According to the lawsuit, Goddard and Singer met the man for sex when he was a minor and engaged in acts of "gender violence" against him while in London for the premiere of <i><a href="/wiki/Superman_Returns" title="Superman Returns">Superman Returns</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The charge against Singer in this case was dismissed, at the accuser's request, in July 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> The case against Goddard remained active.</p>
<p>Singer is cited in the 2014 documentary film on child sexual abuse in Hollywood, <i><a href="/wiki/An_Open_Secret" title="An Open Secret">An Open Secret</a></i>, though the film makes no specific allegations against him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup></p>
