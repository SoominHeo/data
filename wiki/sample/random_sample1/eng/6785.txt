<p><b>Brian Tyler</b> (born May 8, 1972) is an American composer, conductor, arranger, producer, musician, and songwriter, who has composed scores for film, television and video games. <i><a href="/wiki/Iron_Man_3" title="Iron Man 3">Iron Man 3</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Avengers:_Age_of_Ultron" title="Avengers: Age of Ultron">Avengers: Age of Ultron</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Danny_Elfman" title="Danny Elfman">Danny Elfman</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Furious_7" title="Furious 7">Furious 7</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Now_You_See_Me_(film)" title="Now You See Me (film)">Now You See Me</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Truth_(2015_film)" title="Truth (2015 film)">Truth</a></i>, the current fanfare of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Pictures" title="Universal Pictures">Universal Pictures</a> logo, the updated <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Studios" title="Marvel Studios">Marvel Studios</a> logo, which debuted with <i><a href="/wiki/Thor:_The_Dark_World" title="Thor: The Dark World">Thor: The Dark World</a></i> (2013), as well as the <i>NFL Theme</i> for <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MarvelFanfare_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MarvelFanfare-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>For his work as a film composer, he has won multiple awards, including 23 <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_Music,_Inc." title="Broadcast Music, Inc.">BMI Film Music Award for Best Film Music</a> and <a href="/wiki/American_Society_of_Composers,_Authors_and_Publishers" title="American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers">ASCAP Awards</a> and the <a href="/wiki/International_Film_Music_Critics_Association" title="International Film Music Critics Association">IFMCA Awards</a> 2014 Composer of the Year.</p>
<p>His composition for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Call_(2002_film)" title="Last Call (2002 film)">Last Call</a></i> earned him the first of three Emmy nominations and went on to win 23 BMI and ASCAP Film Awards, a gold record, and was inducted into the music branch of the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Motion_Picture_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences">Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ampas_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ampas-2">[2]</a></sup> As of May 2015, his films have grossed $9.5 billion worldwide which puts him in to the top 10 highest-grossing film composers of all time in the category of worldwide box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Tyler has scored 3 of the top 10 films of all time in global box office.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Tyler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tyler was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_California" title="Orange County, California">Orange County, California</a>. His grandfather was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a>-winning art director <a href="/wiki/Walter_H._Tyler" title="Walter H. Tyler">Walter H. Tyler</a>. One of his first major influences was his pianist grandmother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He holds a bachelor's degree from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Los_Angeles" title="University of California, Los Angeles">University of California, Los Angeles</a> and a Master's from <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-bio-5">[5]</a></sup> Growing up, he taught himself to play at least 30 different types of musical instruments, including drums, piano, guitar, bass, cello, world percussion, synthesizer, charango and bouzouki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Tyler is an atheist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tyler began scoring features shortly after graduating from Harvard. <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kraft_(composer)" title="Robert Kraft (composer)">Robert Kraft</a>, who was impressed by Tyler's music, encouraged him to pursue a career in film scoring. He began his career by composing the film score for the <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent film</a> <i>Bartender</i> (1997), directed by Gabe Torres. The following year, he and <a href="/wiki/Red_Elvises" title="Red Elvises">Red Elvises</a> composed the film score for <i><a href="/wiki/Six-String_Samurai" title="Six-String Samurai">Six-String Samurai</a></i> (1998).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tyler's breakthrough came in 2001, after composing for <i><a href="/wiki/Frailty_(film)" title="Frailty (film)">Frailty</a></i> (2001). His work on <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Call_(2002_film)" title="Last Call (2002 film)">Last Call</a></i> (2002) earned him an induction into the music branch of the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Motion_Picture_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences">Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ampas_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ampas-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/William_Friedkin" title="William Friedkin">William Friedkin</a>, after being impressed by his work on <i>Frailty</i>, called Tyler in to begin composing for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunted_(2003_film)" title="The Hunted (2003 film)">The Hunted</a></i> (2003), which went on to earn Tyler a <a href="/wiki/World_Soundtrack_Awards_2002" title="World Soundtrack Awards 2002">World Soundtrack Award in 2002</a> for Best New Film Composer of the Year. Starting in 2003, he began working on big-budget films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Timeline_(film)" title="Timeline (film)">Timeline</a></i> (2003), <i><a href="/wiki/Godsend_(2004_film)" title="Godsend (2004 film)">Godsend</a></i> (2004), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Greatest_Game_Ever_Played" title="The Greatest Game Ever Played">The Greatest Game Ever Played</a></i> (2005), and <i><a href="/wiki/Constantine_(film)" title="Constantine (film)">Constantine</a></i> (2005).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> His score for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious:_Tokyo_Drift" title="The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift">The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift</a></i> (2006) hit #1 on the iTunes soundtrack sales charts, while his soundtrack for "Children of Dune" reached #4 on the Amazon.com album charts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. His cues for <i>Children of Dune</i> were used in multiple other theatrical film trailers, including <i><a href="/wiki/Master_and_Commander:_The_Far_Side_of_the_World" title="Master and Commander: The Far Side of the World">Master and Commander</a></i> (2003), <i><a href="/wiki/Sahara_(2005_film)" title="Sahara (2005 film)">Sahara</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Cinderella_Man" title="Cinderella Man">Cinderella Man</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe" title="The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe">The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe</a></i> (2005), <i><a href="/wiki/Indiana_Jones_and_the_Kingdom_of_the_Crystal_Skull" title="Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull">Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull</a></i> (2008), and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_(film)" title="Star Trek (film)">Star Trek</a></i> (2009).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The track "Summon the Worms" was used as a leader for the French show <i>Peking Express</i>, and in the first leaked promotional reel for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Golden_Compass_(film)" title="The Golden Compass (film)">The Golden Compass</a></i> (2007).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, he was hired to compose for <i><a href="/wiki/Partition_(2007_film)" title="Partition (2007 film)">Partition</a></i> (2007), where he had to integrate Indian and Middle Eastern music with orchestral writing. He conducted the orchestral portion of the score with the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Studio_Symphony" title="Hollywood Studio Symphony">Hollywood Studio Symphony</a> in Los Angeles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 5, 2011, Tyler announced that he was currently in talks for scoring the 2011 remake of <i><a href="/wiki/Highlander_(film)#Remake" title="Highlander (film)">Highlander</a></i>, as well as pilot episodes for the animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Transformers:_Prime" title="Transformers: Prime">Transformers: Prime</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He went on to score four episodes of the series.</p>
<p>In addition to working as a composer, Tyler also wrote and produced the song "<a href="/wiki/Shell_Shocked_(song)" title="Shell Shocked (song)">Shell Shocked</a>" with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kill_the_Noise" title="Kill the Noise">Kill the Noise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiz_Khalifa" title="Wiz Khalifa">Wiz Khalifa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ty_Dolla_Sign" title="Ty Dolla Sign">Ty Dolla Sign</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juicy_J" title="Juicy J">Juicy J</a> and Moxie that was released in the summer of 2014 under his artist name <i>Madsonik</i>. The track went on to peak at #2 on the iTunes hip-hop singles charts and has sold over 500,000 copies.</p>
<p>On October 12, 2016, he was hired to write and compose the musical score for the 2017 action-adventure film <i><a href="/wiki/XXX:_Return_of_Xander_Cage" title="XXX: Return of Xander Cage">XXX: Return of Xander Cage</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He has also contributed to the soundtrack as Madsonik in a collaboration with Tom Morello and Kill The Noise entitled "Divebomb".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
