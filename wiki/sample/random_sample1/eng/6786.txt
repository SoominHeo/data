<p><b>Brian Edmund Posehn</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (born July 6, 1966) is an American actor, voice actor, musician, writer, and <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">comedian</a>, known for his roles as Jim Kuback on <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">The WB's</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Mission_Hill" title="Mission Hill">Mission Hill</a></i> and Brian Spukowski on <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central" title="Comedy Central">Comedy Central</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sarah_Silverman_Program" title="The Sarah Silverman Program">The Sarah Silverman Program</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Posehn was born<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BPosehnBorn-AVClub_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-BPosehnBorn-AVClub-2">[2]</a></sup> and raised<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BPosehnRaised-MetroTimes_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-BPosehnRaised-MetroTimes-3">[3]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a>. He is of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_people" title="German people">German</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ada_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ada-4">[4]</a></sup> Posehn graduated from <a href="/wiki/Sonoma_Valley_High_School" title="Sonoma Valley High School">Sonoma Valley High School</a> in 1984.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mit_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-mit-5">[5]</a></sup> He attended college at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sacramento_State_University" title="Sacramento State University">Sacramento State University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_work">Television work</span></h3>
<p>Posehn began with guest appearances and mainly small roles in TV shows. He was on 28 episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Show_with_Bob_and_David" title="Mr. Show with Bob and David">Mr. Show with Bob and David</a></i> (1995–1998), a <a href="/wiki/Sketch_comedy" title="Sketch comedy">sketch comedy</a> series on <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a>. In a 1996 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Friends" title="Friends">Friends</a></i>, he delivered the manuscript in which Joey Tribbiani's soap opera character "Dr. Drake Ramoray" is killed off. He appeared as two different characters in <i><a href="/wiki/NewsRadio" title="NewsRadio">NewsRadio</a></i>: a fan with questions for Jimmy James at a book reading (1997), and a member of Dave's <a href="/wiki/A_cappella" title="A cappella">a cappella</a> group "Chock Full o' Notes" (1998). In the <i><a href="/wiki/Seinfeld" title="Seinfeld">Seinfeld</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Burning_(Seinfeld)" title="The Burning (Seinfeld)">The Burning</a>" (1998), he played a patient, when <a href="/wiki/Cosmo_Kramer" title="Cosmo Kramer">Kramer</a> "was given" <a href="/wiki/Gonorrhea" title="Gonorrhea">gonorrhea</a>. His character was instructed to "act out" to a group of medical students how a surgeon left a sponge in him post surgery. Posehn also wrote the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_Ghost:_Coast_to_Coast" title="Space Ghost: Coast to Coast">Space Ghost: Coast to Coast</a></i> episode "Cahill" (1998) with <a href="/wiki/Ben_Karlin" title="Ben Karlin">Ben Karlin</a>. He appeared on 29 episodes of the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Just_Shoot_Me!" title="Just Shoot Me!">Just Shoot Me!</a></i> (1999–2003). He played the voice of Jim in <i><a href="/wiki/Mission_Hill" title="Mission Hill">Mission Hill</a></i> on the <a href="/wiki/The_WB" title="The WB">WB</a> (1999–2002), and Del Swanson in <i><a href="/wiki/3_South" title="3 South">3 South</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> (2002–2003). On an <a href="/wiki/Adult_Swim" title="Adult Swim">Adult Swim</a> production, <i><a href="/wiki/Aqua_Teen_Hunger_Force" title="Aqua Teen Hunger Force">Aqua Teen Hunger Force</a></i>, he voiced the Wisdom Cube in the 2003 episode "The Cubing".</p>
<p>Posehn performed the voice of Gibbons, a tiny man, on several episodes of the <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Tom_Goes_to_the_Mayor" title="Tom Goes to the Mayor">Tom Goes to the Mayor</a></i> (2005–2006). He also appeared in the 2005 pilot for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Showbiz_Show_with_David_Spade" title="The Showbiz Show with David Spade">The Showbiz Show with David Spade</a></i>, in a segment called "The Nerd Perspective", in which he gave a scathing criticism of <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> and its declining quality. He also played a mortician in several episodes of Comedy Central's <i><a href="/wiki/Reno_911!" title="Reno 911!">Reno 911!</a></i>. He was featured on the 2005 documentary series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Comedians_of_Comedy" title="The Comedians of Comedy">The Comedians of Comedy</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central" title="Comedy Central">Comedy Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a>. He was in a 2007 episode of the improv series <i><a href="/wiki/Thank_God_You%27re_Here_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Thank God You're Here (U.S. TV series)">Thank God You're Here</a></i> on NBC and was a celebrity judge on the revived 1970s game show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Gong_Show_with_Dave_Attell" title="The Gong Show with Dave Attell">The Gong Show with Dave Attell</a></i> (2008), on Comedy Central. He co-stars on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sarah_Silverman_Program" title="The Sarah Silverman Program">The Sarah Silverman Program</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Steve_Agee" title="Steve Agee">Steve Agee</a> as a gay couple who is friends with Silverman,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and also wrote the season three finale "<a href="/wiki/List_of_The_Sarah_Silverman_Program_episodes#Season_3:_2010" title="List of The Sarah Silverman Program episodes">Wowschwitz</a>". He played himself in the episode "Spagett" of <i><a href="/wiki/Tim_and_Eric_Awesome_Show,_Great_Job!" title="Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job!">Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job!</a></i>, appeared at the <i><a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central_Roast" title="Comedy Central Roast">Comedy Central Roast</a></i> of <a href="/wiki/Bob_Saget" title="Bob Saget">Bob Saget</a>, played the role of a physically disabled man (Scooter Man) in the second season's premiere episode "Slip of the Tongue" of <i><a href="/wiki/Californication_(TV_series)" title="Californication (TV series)">Californication</a></i> (2008), on <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a>, and played <a href="/wiki/Dethklok" title="Dethklok">Dethklok</a>'s second manager in the <i><a href="/wiki/Metalocalypse" title="Metalocalypse">Metalocalypse</a></i> episode "Dethsources", he also wrote the episode "Fatherklok". In 2007 he joined the first season of the MTV sketch comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Human_Giant" title="Human Giant">Human Giant</a></i>, as a writer and performer, and voices Glen Furlblam, the biggest fan of Dr. Two-Brains on the <a href="/wiki/PBS_Kids" title="PBS Kids">PBS Kids</a> animated series <i><a href="/wiki/WordGirl" title="WordGirl">WordGirl</a></i>. In 2012 he co-wrote the <a href="/wiki/Metalocalypse_(season_4)" title="Metalocalypse (season 4)">fourth season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Metalocalypse" title="Metalocalypse">Metalocalypse</a></i>.</p>

<p>Movie appearances from Posehn include the 2003 comedy film sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Dumb_and_Dumberer:_When_Harry_Met_Lloyd" title="Dumb and Dumberer: When Harry Met Lloyd">Dumb and Dumberer: When Harry Met Lloyd</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Grind_(2003_film)" title="Grind (2003 film)">Grind</a></i>, the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Rob_Zombie" title="Rob Zombie">Rob Zombie</a> horror film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Devil%27s_Rejects" title="The Devil's Rejects">The Devil's Rejects</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sleeping_Dogs_Lie_(2005_film)" title="Sleeping Dogs Lie (2005 film)">Sleeping Dogs Lie</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four:_Rise_of_the_Silver_Surfer" title="Fantastic Four: Rise of the Silver Surfer">Fantastic Four: Rise of the Silver Surfer</a></i>, and the 2007 animated feature <i><a href="/wiki/Surf%27s_Up_(film)" title="Surf's Up (film)">Surf's Up</a></i>, where he played Glen Maverick. Posehn appeared as himself in the 2007 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Super_High_Me" title="Super High Me">Super High Me</a></i> starring 'marijuana comedian' <a href="/wiki/Doug_Benson" title="Doug Benson">Doug Benson</a>, the 2008 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Nerdcore_Rising_(film)" title="Nerdcore Rising (film)">Nerdcore Rising</a></i> about <a href="/wiki/MC_Frontalot" title="MC Frontalot">MC Frontalot</a> and in a supporting role in <i><a href="/wiki/Sarah_Silverman:_Jesus_Is_Magic" title="Sarah Silverman: Jesus Is Magic">Sarah Silverman: Jesus Is Magic</a></i>.</p>
<p>Posehn voices the character of Murray, a robot, in Rob Zombie's animated <i><a href="/wiki/The_Haunted_World_of_El_Superbeasto" title="The Haunted World of El Superbeasto">The Haunted World of El Superbeasto</a></i>. He also voiced the character Hayashi in the English dub of <i><a href="/wiki/Pom_Poko" title="Pom Poko">Pom Poko</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stand-up_comedy">Stand-up comedy</span></h3>
<p>In 2002, Posehn appeared on Comedy Central Presents, followed by the release of 2005's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Comedians_of_Comedy" title="The Comedians of Comedy">The Comedians of Comedy</a></i>, a documentary/live special chronicling a 2004 small-club comedy tour he participated in. The film was followed up by a television series on Comedy Central of the same name. Posehn's debut comedy album <i><a href="/wiki/Live_In:_Nerd_Rage" title="Live In: Nerd Rage">Live In: Nerd Rage</a></i> was released in 2006. He participated in the Comedy Lineup of the 2008 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bonnaroo_Music_and_Arts_Festival" title="Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival">Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival</a>, which included <a href="/wiki/Louis_C.K." title="Louis C.K.">Louis C.K.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Janeane_Garofalo" title="Janeane Garofalo">Janeane Garofalo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zach_Galifianakis" title="Zach Galifianakis">Zach Galifianakis</a>. Posehn performed as part of the Rock N' Roll Comedy set with <a href="/wiki/Jim_Norton_(comedian)" title="Jim Norton (comedian)">Jim Norton</a> and Michelle Buteau. During his 2008 routine on <i><a href="/wiki/Comedy_Central_Presents" title="Comedy Central Presents">Comedy Central Presents</a></i> he referred to his Wikipedia article, which he supposedly vandalized.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2010, Posehn released his second album <i><a href="/wiki/Fart_and_Wiener_Jokes" title="Fart and Wiener Jokes">Fart and Wiener Jokes</a></i>. In 2011, Posehn agreed to perform at the <a href="/wiki/Gathering_of_the_Juggalos" title="Gathering of the Juggalos">Gathering of the Juggalos</a>. Some of his fans criticized this decision as being "not metal".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JoeRogan_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-JoeRogan-9">[9]</a></sup> Posehn countered that "getting a paycheck is metal", and expressed respect towards the <a href="/wiki/Juggalo" title="Juggalo">Juggalo</a> fan culture, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Independent_music" title="Independent music">independent music</a> success of <a href="/wiki/Insane_Clown_Posse" title="Insane Clown Posse">Insane Clown Posse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Psychopathic_Records" title="Psychopathic Records">Psychopathic Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JoeRogan_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-JoeRogan-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2013, Posen released his third comedy album and first DVD, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fartist" title="The Fartist">The Fartist</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span></h3>
<p>In 2006, <a href="/wiki/Relapse_Records" title="Relapse Records">Relapse Records</a> released his first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Live_In:_Nerd_Rage" title="Live In: Nerd Rage">Live In: Nerd Rage</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It includes "Metal by Numbers", a song mocking bands that term themselves "<a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">metal</a>" but are clearly not.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The instrumental tracks feature musicians such as guitarist <a href="/wiki/Scott_Ian" title="Scott Ian">Scott Ian</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Anthrax_(American_band)" title="Anthrax (American band)">Anthrax</a>), bassist <a href="/wiki/Joey_Vera" title="Joey Vera">Joey Vera</a> (then of Anthrax, but also of <a href="/wiki/Armored_Saint" title="Armored Saint">Armored Saint</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fates_Warning" title="Fates Warning">Fates Warning</a>), drummer <a href="/wiki/John_Tempesta" title="John Tempesta">John Tempesta</a> (of <a href="/wiki/The_Cult" title="The Cult">The Cult</a> and <a href="/wiki/White_Zombie_(band)" title="White Zombie (band)">White Zombie</a>), and lead guitarist Jonathan Donais (of <a href="/wiki/Shadows_Fall" title="Shadows Fall">Shadows Fall</a>). Posehn also appeared in the Anthrax <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for "What Doesn't Die". Posehn appeared on a Season 4 episode of the music talk show <i><a href="/wiki/That_Metal_Show" title="That Metal Show">That Metal Show</a></i> and Lamb of God's <i>Walk With Me In Hell</i> DVD, and performed "More Metal Then You", a song that was included on his non-musical stand-up comedy album <i>Fart &amp; Weiner Jokes</i>, with "Brian Posehn's All-Star Band" on the <i>2010 Revolver Golden Gods Awards</i>. Posehn appeared in <a href="/wiki/The_Damned_Things" title="The Damned Things">The Damned Things</a> music video for "We've Got A Situation Here". He provided backing vocals for <a href="/wiki/Evile" title="Evile">Evile</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Five_Serpent%27s_Teeth" title="Five Serpent's Teeth">Five Serpent's Teeth</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span></h3>
<p>Posehn has provided voice work for video games <i><a href="/wiki/Br%C3%BCtal_Legend" title="Brütal Legend">Brütal Legend</a></i> as The Hunter<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Warped" title="Star Warped">Star Warped</a></i> as co-narrator Brian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Posehn has also voiced Grunts and various <a href="/wiki/Marines" title="Marines">Marines</a> in the 2004 video game <i><a href="/wiki/Halo_2" title="Halo 2">Halo 2</a></i>.</p>
<p>Posehn appeared in the commercial "Ink Fairy" for <a href="/wiki/Staples_Inc." title="Staples Inc.">Staples</a> office supply store in its ad campaign featuring the "Easy Button."</p>
<p>In 2006, Posehn co-wrote the comic book <i>The Last Christmas</i> with writer Gerry Duggan, published by <a href="/wiki/Image_Comics" title="Image Comics">Image Comics</a> (<a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/1582406766">ISBN 1582406766</a>).</p>
<p>In April 2009 Posehn hosted the first American "Golden Gods Awards" for metal music hosted by <a href="/wiki/Revolver_(magazine)" title="Revolver (magazine)">Revolver Magazine</a>.</p>
<p>As part of the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_NOW!" title="Marvel NOW!">Marvel NOW!</a> initiative Posehn and Gerry Duggan co-wrote the <a href="/wiki/Deadpool" title="Deadpool">Deadpool</a> ongoing comic series which launched in November 2012, lasting 45 issues. His run ended in April 2015. The first six issues were illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Moore_(artist)" title="Tony Moore (artist)">Tony Moore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2012, Posehn has hosted a <a href="/wiki/Podcast" title="Podcast">podcast</a> called <i>Nerd Poker</i>, where a group of comedians play <a href="/wiki/Dungeons_%26_Dragons" title="Dungeons &amp; Dragons">Dungeons &amp; Dragons</a>. Co-hosts include comic book writer Gerry Duggan and comedian <a href="/wiki/Blaine_Capatch" title="Blaine Capatch">Blaine Capatch</a>, among others.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Posehn married Melanie Truhett in 2004 and has a son with her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interview_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-interview-16">[16]</a></sup> While once known for his use of <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">cannabis</a>, Posehn quit smoking marijuana in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pot_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-pot-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
