<p><b>Bryan Fuller</b> (born July 27, 1969) is an American screenwriter and television producer. Fuller has worked exclusively as a writer/producer in television, creating a number of critically acclaimed television series, including <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Like_Me" title="Dead Like Me">Dead Like Me</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Wonderfalls" title="Wonderfalls">Wonderfalls</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pushing_Daisies" title="Pushing Daisies">Pushing Daisies</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Hannibal_(TV_series)" title="Hannibal (TV series)">Hannibal</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Fuller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fuller was born in <a href="/wiki/Lewiston,_Idaho" title="Lewiston, Idaho">Lewiston, Idaho</a> and raised in <a href="/wiki/Clarkston,_Washington" title="Clarkston, Washington">Clarkston, Washington</a>. After graduating from <a href="/wiki/Clarkston_High_School_(Washington)" title="Clarkston High School (Washington)">Clarkston High School</a>, Fuller attended <a href="/wiki/Lewis%E2%80%93Clark_State_College" title="Lewis–Clark State College">Lewis–Clark State College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lewiston,_Idaho" title="Lewiston, Idaho">Lewiston, Idaho</a>. He later transferred to the <a href="/wiki/USC_School_of_Cinematic_Arts" title="USC School of Cinematic Arts">USC School of Cinematic Arts</a>, but dropped out and began working as an office temp for the next five years. He was raised <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Fuller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a contributing writer, Fuller's work has been featured on several shows, including <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Voyager" title="Star Trek: Voyager">Star Trek: Voyager</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Deep_Space_Nine" title="Star Trek: Deep Space Nine">Star Trek: Deep Space Nine</a></i>, earning twenty-two episode writing credits for the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a></i> franchise. Fuller is himself a <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_fandom" title="Science fiction fandom">fan of science fiction</a>, and in an interview said that his favorite <i>Star Trek</i> series were the <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Original_Series" title="Star Trek: The Original Series">1960s original</a>, followed by <i>Deep Space Nine</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Next_Generation" title="Star Trek: The Next Generation">The Next Generation</a></i> and <i>Voyager</i>. Fuller has called <i>DS9</i> his favorite spin-off, stating: "There were lots of new and innovative things going on during <i>Deep Space Nine</i> and that's why it's my favorite of the new series'. It was much more character-based".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-comicbookresources_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-comicbookresources-2">[2]</a></sup> Fuller worked on the <i>DS9</i> episodes "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Darkness_and_the_Light_(DS9_episode)" title="The Darkness and the Light (DS9 episode)">The Darkness and the Light</a>" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empok_Nor_(DS9_episode)" title="Empok Nor (DS9 episode)">Empok Nor</a>".</p>
<p>Fuller wrote the <a href="/wiki/Teleplay" title="Teleplay">teleplay</a> for the 2002 adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Carrie_(2002_film)" title="Carrie (2002 film)">Carrie</a></i>, based on the <a href="/wiki/Carrie_(novel)" title="Carrie (novel)">novel of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a>. Next, he created <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Like_Me" title="Dead Like Me">Dead Like Me</a></i> which ran on <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> for two seasons from 2003 to 2004, although Fuller left early into the first season. He then co-created <i><a href="/wiki/Wonderfalls" title="Wonderfalls">Wonderfalls</a></i> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Todd_Holland_(producer)" title="Todd Holland (producer)">Todd Holland</a>, which ran on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> in 2004, with only four episodes being aired and the rest premiering on DVD. Near the end of 2004, Fuller got a pilot commitment from <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> for <i>The Assistants</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> although the show never got past the script stage. In 2005, Fuller wrote the pilot to the animated comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Amazing_Screw-On_Head" title="The Amazing Screw-On Head">The Amazing Screw-On Head</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Sci Fi Channel</a>, which aired in 2006 but was not picked up for a series. He next worked on NBC series <i><a href="/wiki/Heroes_(TV_series)" title="Heroes (TV series)">Heroes</a></i>, where he joined as a consulting producer after the pilot and became a co-executive producer for the first season. He also wrote a couple episodes for <i>Heroes</i>, including "<a href="/wiki/Company_Man_(Heroes)" title="Company Man (Heroes)">Company Man</a>" which <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i> named one of the 100 greatest episodes in television history.</p>

<p>Next he created <i><a href="/wiki/Pushing_Daisies" title="Pushing Daisies">Pushing Daisies</a></i>, about a pie-maker (<a href="/wiki/Lee_Pace" title="Lee Pace">Lee Pace</a>) who can bring dead things back to life temporarily, debuted on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> on October 3, 2007. On July 17, 2008 the show was nominated for twelve <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Awards</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Television_Arts_%26_Sciences" title="Academy of Television Arts &amp; Sciences">Academy of Television Arts &amp; Sciences</a>, including one for Fuller for Outstanding Writing of a Comedy Series. It ultimately won seven Emmy Awards: for Best Supporting Actress (Kristin Chenoweth), Best Art Direction, Best Costume, Best Music, Best Make-Up, Best Editing, and Best Direction of a Comedy Series (Barry Sonnenfeld). The second season of <i>Pushing Daisies</i> began October 1, 2008 on ABC. In mid-November, ABC announced that it would not order new episodes for season two after the 13th.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The series's final episode aired on June 13, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>With the cancellation of <i>Pushing Daisies</i>, Fuller signed a seven-figure, two-year deal with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Media_Studios" title="Universal Media Studios">Universal Media Studios</a>. He rejoined the writing staff of <i>Heroes</i> for the 20th episode of the third season, and again became a consulting producer, playing a "key role" on the writing staff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After working on some of the <a href="/wiki/Story_arc" title="Story arc">story arcs</a> for the next season of <i>Heroes</i>, Fuller announced he was moving on to other projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> His next projects, both a result of the Universal deal and developed for NBC, were <i>Sellevision</i>, developed with <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Singer" title="Bryan Singer">Bryan Singer</a> and based on the <a href="/wiki/Sellevision" title="Sellevision">book of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Augusten_Burroughs" title="Augusten Burroughs">Augusten Burroughs</a>, and <i>No Kill</i>, Fuller's first sitcom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Neither project got past the scripting stage. Fuller's next project, again for NBC, was <i><a href="/wiki/Mockingbird_Lane" title="Mockingbird Lane">Mockingbird Lane</a></i>, a revival of the classic sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/The_Munsters" title="The Munsters">The Munsters</a></i>. A pilot was produced, but the show did not receive a series order, and the pilot aired as a Halloween special. Fuller next worked on <i>Mind Fields</i> with Lisa Joy for <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> which was not picked up.</p>
<p>Fuller owns Living Dead Guy Productions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Fuller's next project was the <a href="/wiki/Hannibal_Lecter" title="Hannibal Lecter">Hannibal Lecter</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Hannibal_(TV_series)" title="Hannibal (TV series)">Hannibal</a></i>, which premiered on NBC in 2013, and renewed for a second season in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Fuller had developed <i>High Moon</i> for <a href="/wiki/Syfy" title="Syfy">Syfy</a>, based on the book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lotus_Caves" title="The Lotus Caves">The Lotus Caves</a></i>, which filmed a pilot in late 2013, but was not given a series order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fullerverse">Fullerverse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bryan_Fuller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fullerverse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nicknamed the "Fullerverse" by Fuller's fans, all of Fuller's series are implied to take place in the same universe.</p>
<p>Marianne Marie Beetle (played by actress <a href="/wiki/Beth_Grant" title="Beth Grant">Beth Grant</a>) first appears in the <i><a href="/wiki/Wonderfalls" title="Wonderfalls">Wonderfalls</a></i> episode "Muffin Buffalo", and has subsequently appeared in the <i><a href="/wiki/Pushing_Daisies" title="Pushing Daisies">Pushing Daisies</a></i> episode "Comfort Food"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and in the pilot of <i><a href="/wiki/Mockingbird_Lane" title="Mockingbird Lane">Mockingbird Lane</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Similarly, Gretchen Speck-Horowitz (played by <a href="/wiki/Chelan_Simmons" title="Chelan Simmons">Chelan Simmons</a>) first appears in the <i>Wonderfalls</i> episode "Pink Flamingos" and subsequently appeared in the <i><a href="/wiki/Hannibal_(TV_series)" title="Hannibal (TV series)">Hannibal</a></i> episode "Amuse-Bouche", reverting to her maiden name after her divorce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>Meanwhile, in the <i>Pushing Daisies</i> episode "Bzzzzzzzzz!", Ned mentions that he works for Happy Time Temp Agency when trying to go undercover. Happy Time Temp Agency is the place where Georgia "George" Lass works in <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Like_Me" title="Dead Like Me">Dead Like Me</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Also, the fictional brand "Lil' Ivey's" first appeared in the <i>Wonderfalls</i> episode "Cocktail Bunny" on a box of cocktail cherries and later appeared in the <i>Pushing Daisies</i> episode "Kerplunk", this time on a bag of macaroni.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a more subtle nod, Fuller has also reinterpreted a few of his characters in later series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Georgia "George" Lass (played by <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Muth" title="Ellen Muth">Ellen Muth</a>), having first appeared in <i>Dead Like Me</i> was reinterpreted as Georgia Madchen (also played by Muth) in the <i>Hannibal</i> episodes "Buffet Froid" and "Relevés". This similarity is furthered by their last names: "lass" is an English synonym for girl while "Mädchen" is a German word meaning the same. In another nod to Georgia Lass being a reaper (therefore both dead and alive) in <i>Dead Like Me</i>, in <i>Hannibal</i> Georgia Madchen is a killer who suffers from numerous medical conditions including <a href="/wiki/Cotard_delusion" title="Cotard delusion">Cotard's syndrome</a>, a delusion disorder that has her convinced she is actually dead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> In a similar manner, Reggie Lass from <i>Dead Like Me</i> (played by <a href="/wiki/Britt_McKillip" title="Britt McKillip">Britt McKillip</a>) was reinterpreted as Miriam Lass (played by <a href="/wiki/Anna_Chlumsky" title="Anna Chlumsky">Anna Chlumsky</a>), who first appears in the <i>Hannibal</i> episode "Entrée".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Similarly, in the <i>Hannibal</i> episode "Takiawase", an acupuncturist (played by <a href="/wiki/Amanda_Plummer" title="Amanda Plummer">Amanda Plummer</a>) who is under investigation for lobotomizing patients is named Katherine Pimms, which is also the cover name used by Charlotte "Chuck" Charles (played by <a href="/wiki/Anna_Friel" title="Anna Friel">Anna Friel</a>) in multiple episodes of <i>Pushing Daisies</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>A visual nod to <i>Wonderfalls</i> was given in the <i>Heroes</i> first season episode "Seven Minutes to Midnight" when the smoosh-faced wax lion central to <i>Wonderfalls</i> was seen on a bedside table.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
