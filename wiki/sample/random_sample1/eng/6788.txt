<p><b>Brian Hansen</b> (born September 3, 1990, in <a href="/wiki/Evanston,_Illinois" title="Evanston, Illinois">Evanston, Illinois</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating" title="Speed skating">speed skater</a> and silver medalist in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winter_Olympics" title="Winter Olympics">Winter Olympics</a>.</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2010_Winter_Olympics" title="2010 Winter Olympics">2010 Winter Olympics</a>, Hansen won a silver medal in the team pursuit along with <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Kuck" title="Jonathan Kuck">Jonathan Kuck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chad_Hedrick" title="Chad Hedrick">Chad Hedrick</a>. Hansen has also won a two medals in the <a href="/wiki/World_Single_Distance_Championships" title="World Single Distance Championships">World Single Distance Championships</a> and two medals (one silver, one bronze) at the <a href="/wiki/World_Junior_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="World Junior Speed Skating Championships">World Junior Championships</a>.</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Games">Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Hansen_(speed_skater)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the 1500 m, Hansen placed 18th with a time of 1:48.45.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>The American pursuit team consisted of Hansen, <a href="/wiki/Chad_Hedrick" title="Chad Hedrick">Chad Hedrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Kuck" title="Jonathan Kuck">Jonathan Kuck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Marsicano" title="Trevor Marsicano">Trevor Marsicano</a>. Kuck, Hedrick and Marsicano eliminated Japan in the quarterfinal, which advanced them to face the heavily favored Netherlands in the semifinal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Kuck, Hedrick and Hansen then beat the Dutch team by .4 seconds, with a final time of 3:42.71.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The American team were defeated by the Canadians in the gold medal final. The same trio that skated the semifinal trailed Canada in the gold medal final by as much as 0.73 seconds early in the race, trimming that margin to 0.21 at the finish with a time of 3:41.58. The Canadians hit the line at 3:41.37 to win the gold medal, leaving the American team with silver. The bronze went to the Netherlands, which set an Olympic record in the B final with a time of 3:39.95.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Hansen_(speed_skater)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brian was born in <a href="/wiki/Evanston,_Illinois" title="Evanston, Illinois">Evanston, Illinois</a>, and was raised in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glenview,_Cook_County,_Illinois" title="Glenview, Cook County, Illinois">Glenview, Illinois</a>. Hansen is coached by 4-time Olympian <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Swider-Peltz" title="Nancy Swider-Peltz">Nancy Swider-Peltz</a>. Hansen is the youngest member of the Long Track Speedskating Team and the only junior. Brian attended Attea Middle School and <a href="/wiki/Glenbrook_South_High_School" title="Glenbrook South High School">Glenbrook South High School</a>, both in Glenview. He currently attends University of Colorado Boulder in Boulder, Colorado.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
