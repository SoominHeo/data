<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=710&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="710">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>The Byzantine outpost of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chersonesus_(Crimea)" title="Chersonesus (Crimea)">Cherson</a> (<a href="/wiki/Crimea" title="Crimea">Crimea</a>) rebels (with <a href="/wiki/Khazars" title="Khazars">Khazar</a> assistance) against Emperor <a href="/wiki/Justinian_II" title="Justinian II">Justinian II</a>. He sends a fleet under the <i><a href="/wiki/Patrician_(ancient_Rome)#Late_Roman_and_Byzantine_periods" title="Patrician (ancient Rome)">patrikios</a></i> Stephen, which retakes the city and restores Byzantine control. The fleet, however, is struck by a <a href="/wiki/Storm" title="Storm">storm</a> on its way back and loses many ships, while the Chersonites, again with the aid of the Khazars, rebel anew.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>The Byzantine general <a href="/wiki/Leo_III_the_Isaurian" title="Leo III the Isaurian">Leo</a> (future emperor Leo III) recovers the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Abkhazia" title="Kingdom of Abkhazia">Abkhazia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a>) for the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>, from the Arabs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=710&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="710">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Roderic" title="Roderic">Roderick</a> becomes king of the <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a>, but the Visigothic <a href="/wiki/Nobility" title="Nobility">nobles</a> in <a href="/wiki/Septimania" title="Septimania">Septimania</a> rebel, and proclaim the previous ruler's son <a href="/wiki/Achila_II" title="Achila II">Akhila</a> king. The <a href="/wiki/Visigothic_Kingdom" title="Visigothic Kingdom">Visigothic Kingdom</a> is divided into two sub-kingdoms, suffering the first <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> raid expedition against the southern <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>An <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arab_people" title="Arab people">Arab</a> army is invited into <a href="/wiki/Ceuta" title="Ceuta">Ceuta</a> by its governor, <a href="/wiki/Julian,_Count_of_Ceuta" title="Julian, Count of Ceuta">Julian</a>, who is an opponent of Roderick. He encourages them to invade the Iberian Peninsula. <a href="/wiki/Tariq_ibn_Ziyad" title="Tariq ibn Ziyad">Tariq ibn Ziyad</a> is appointed governor of <a href="/wiki/Tangier" title="Tangier">Tangier</a> (<a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>), and establishes a <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moorish</a> garrison of 1,700 men.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lupus_I_of_Aquitaine" title="Lupus I of Aquitaine">Lupus I</a>, duke of <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Gascony" title="Duchy of Gascony">Gascony</a>, is assassinated in his attempt to seize <a href="/wiki/Limoges" title="Limoges">Limoges</a> (<a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>). Eudes becomes ruler over both Gascony and <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Aquitaine" title="Duchy of Aquitaine">Aquitaine</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Madara_Rider" title="Madara Rider">Madara Rider</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Early_Middle_Ages" title="Early Middle Ages">early medieval</a> <a href="/wiki/Rock_relief" title="Rock relief">rock relief</a>, is carved on the <a href="/wiki/Madara_(village)" title="Madara (village)">Madara Plateau</a> east of <a href="/wiki/Shumen" title="Shumen">Shumen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=710&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-4" title="710">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>Kings <a href="/wiki/Ine_of_Wessex" title="Ine of Wessex">Ine of Wessex</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nothhelm_of_Sussex" title="Nothhelm of Sussex">Nothhelm of Sussex</a> fight against King <a href="/wiki/Geraint_of_Dumnonia" title="Geraint of Dumnonia">Geraint of Dumnonia</a>, who dies in battle. Ine's advance brings him control of what is now <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>; he establishes a fortress at <a href="/wiki/Taunton" title="Taunton">Taunton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Beorhtfrith fights against the <a href="/wiki/Picts" title="Picts">Picts</a> between Haefe and Caere<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> (assumed to be between the rivers <a href="/wiki/River_Avon,_Falkirk" title="River Avon, Falkirk">Avon</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Carron_(Forth)" title="River Carron (Forth)">Carron</a>, which flow into the <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Forth" title="Firth of Forth">Firth of Forth</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=710&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-5" title="710">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Salih_I_ibn_Mansur" title="Salih I ibn Mansur">Salih I ibn Mansur</a> founds the Muslim <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Nekor" title="Kingdom of Nekor">Kingdom of Nekor</a> (Morocco). He converts the local <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berber_people" title="Berber people">Berber</a> tribes to <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=710&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-6" title="710">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_5" title="April 5">April 5</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Zhongzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Zhongzong of Tang">Zhong Zong</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a> has his chief ministers of court, sons-in-law, and high-ranking military officers engage (during the <a href="/wiki/Cold_Food_Festival" title="Cold Food Festival">Cold Food Festival</a>) in the festive game of <a href="/wiki/Tug_of_war" title="Tug of war">tug of war</a>, within a palace of <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_3" title="July 3">July 3</a> – Zhong Zong is assassinated, allegedly <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poisoned</a> by Empress <a href="/wiki/Empress_Wei_(Tang_dynasty)" title="Empress Wei (Tang dynasty)">Wei</a>, who fails to install her daughter <a href="/wiki/Princess_Anle" title="Princess Anle">Li Guo'er</a> as heir to the <a href="/wiki/Throne" title="Throne">throne</a>. <a href="/wiki/Princess_Taiping" title="Princess Taiping">Princess Taiping</a> and her nephew <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xuanzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xuanzong of Tang">Li Longji</a> launch a coup, and restore <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ruizong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Ruizong of Tang">Rui Zong</a> as emperor.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Asuka_period" title="Asuka period">Asuka period</a>, the second and last part of the <a href="/wiki/Yamato_period" title="Yamato period">Yamato period</a>, ends, and the <a href="/wiki/Nara_period" title="Nara period">Nara period</a> begins; <a href="/wiki/Heij%C5%8D-ky%C5%8D" title="Heijō-kyō">Heijō-kyō</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nara,_Nara" title="Nara, Nara">Nara</a>) becomes the capital of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place_2">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=711&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="711">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire_2">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=711&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="711">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Philippikos_Bardanes" title="Philippikos Bardanes">Philippicus</a> incites the inhabitants of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chersonesus_(Crimea)" title="Chersonesus (Crimea)">Cherson</a> to revolt, with the help of the <a href="/wiki/Khazars" title="Khazars">Khazars</a>. Emperor <a href="/wiki/Justinian_II" title="Justinian II">Justinian II</a> sallies forth from <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> to oppose the rebels in the <a href="/wiki/Crimea" title="Crimea">Crimea</a>. Philippicus defeats the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> forces in northern <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a>, and seizes the capital. He is proclaimed emperor and Justinian is executed, ending the house of <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire_under_the_Heraclian_dynasty" title="Byzantine Empire under the Heraclian dynasty">Heraclius</a>, that has ruled since <a href="/wiki/610" title="610">610</a>.</li>
<li>December – Empress <a href="/wiki/Theodora_of_Khazaria" title="Theodora of Khazaria">Theodora</a>, hearing the news of Justinian's death, escapes with her 6-year-old son <a href="/wiki/Tiberius_(son_of_Justinian_II)" title="Tiberius (son of Justinian II)">Tiberius</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Sanctuary" title="Sanctuary">sanctuary</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_St._Mary_of_Blachernae_(Istanbul)" title="Church of St. Mary of Blachernae (Istanbul)">St. Mary's Church</a> (<a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>). She is pursued by Philippicus' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henchmen" title="Henchmen">henchmen</a>, who drag the child from the <a href="/wiki/Altar" title="Altar">altar</a> and murder him outside the church.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe_2">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=711&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="711">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ansprand" title="Ansprand">Ansprand</a>, duke of <a href="/wiki/Asti" title="Asti">Asti</a>, returns from <a href="/wiki/Exile" title="Exile">exile</a> to <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> with a large <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Bavaria" title="Duchy of Bavaria">Bavarian</a> army. Many <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austrians</a> (with troops of <a href="/wiki/Veneto" title="Veneto">Venetia</a>) join him in support. King <a href="/wiki/Aripert_II" title="Aripert II">Aripert II</a>, who has <a href="/wiki/Usurper" title="Usurper">usurped</a> the <a href="/wiki/Throne" title="Throne">throne</a> (see <a href="/wiki/701" title="701">701</a>), is defeated and tries to escape from <a href="/wiki/Pavia" title="Pavia">Pavia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a> with his <a href="/wiki/Treasury" title="Treasury">treasury</a>, but he drowns in the <a href="/wiki/Ticino_(river)" title="Ticino (river)">Ticino River</a>. He is the last Bavarian to wear the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Crown_of_Lombardy" title="Iron Crown of Lombardy">Iron Crown</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li>Peaceful relations between <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Franks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frisia" title="Frisia">Frisians</a> are consolidated by the marriage of <a href="/wiki/Pepin_of_Herstal" title="Pepin of Herstal">Pepin of Herstal</a>'s son <a href="/wiki/Grimoald_the_Younger" title="Grimoald the Younger">Grimoald</a> to <a href="/wiki/Thiadsvind" title="Thiadsvind">Theudesinda</a>, daughter of King <a href="/wiki/Redbad,_King_of_the_Frisians" title="Redbad, King of the Frisians">Radbod</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_23" title="April 23">April 23</a> – King <a href="/wiki/Childebert_III" title="Childebert III">Childebert III</a> dies after a 16-year reign, and is succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Dagobert_III" title="Dagobert III">Dagobert III</a> as ruler of <a href="/wiki/Austrasia" title="Austrasia">Austrasia</a>. Pepin of Herstal becomes his <a href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">regent</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Arabian_Empire">Arabian Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=711&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-5" title="711">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_27" title="April 27">April 27</a> – <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_conquest_of_Hispania" title="Umayyad conquest of Hispania">Umayyad conquest of Hispania</a>: Muslim troops (7,000 men) led by <a href="/wiki/Tariq_ibn_Ziyad" title="Tariq ibn Ziyad">Tariq ibn Ziyad</a> land at <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>, and begin their <a href="/wiki/Invasion" title="Invasion">invasion</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a> (<a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Al-Andalus</a>). Tariq begins his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_conquests" title="Islamic conquests">Islamic conquest</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Visigothic_Kingdom" title="Visigothic Kingdom">Visigothic Kingdom</a>, which during the decade he occupies and brings under <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad</a> sovereignty.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_19" title="July 19">July 19</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Guadalete" title="Battle of Guadalete">Battle of Guadalete</a>: The Muslim <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berber_people" title="Berber people">Berbers</a> defeat the <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigothic</a> army (33,000 men) under King <a href="/wiki/Roderic" title="Roderic">Roderick</a>, who dies in battle. The Visigoth capital of <a href="/wiki/Toledo,_Spain" title="Toledo, Spain">Toledo</a> open its <a href="/wiki/City_gate" title="City gate">city gates</a>; Tariq ibn Ziyad sends <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moorish</a> detachments to capture the cities of <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba,_Andalusia" title="Córdoba, Andalusia">Córdoba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> (<a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a>).</li>
</ul>

Qasim sends expeditions to <a href="/wiki/Saurashtra_(region)" title="Saurashtra (region)">Surashtra</a>, where he makes peaceful treaty settlements with the <a href="/wiki/Rashtrakuta_dynasty" title="Rashtrakuta dynasty">Rashtrakuta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Muhammad ibn Qasim captures the fortress city of <a href="/wiki/Multan" title="Multan">Multan</a> after a long <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a>, and raids with his forces the <a href="/wiki/Punjab,_Pakistan" title="Punjab, Pakistan">Punjab</a> region, with only light <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> casualties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Bulgars" title="Bulgars">Bulgars</a> under <a href="/wiki/Tervel_of_Bulgaria" title="Tervel of Bulgaria">Tervel</a>, ruler (<i><a href="/wiki/Khagan" title="Khagan">khagan</a></i>) of the <a href="/wiki/First_Bulgarian_Empire" title="First Bulgarian Empire">Bulgarian Empire</a>, raid <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a> and reach the <a href="/wiki/Defensive_wall" title="Defensive wall">city walls</a> of <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>. Skirmishes continue until <a href="/wiki/716" title="716">716</a>; Emperor <a href="/wiki/Philippikos_Bardanes" title="Philippikos Bardanes">Philippicus</a> transfers a <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> army from the <a href="/wiki/Opsikion" title="Opsikion">Opsikion Theme</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Minor" title="Asia Minor">Asia Minor</a>, to police the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkan Peninsula</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe_3">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=712&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="712">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>February – King <a href="/wiki/Ansprand" title="Ansprand">Ansprand</a> dies, and is succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Liutprand,_King_of_the_Lombards" title="Liutprand, King of the Lombards">Liutprand</a> as ruler of the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombards</a>. During his reign, Liutprand becomes the greatest of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_kings_of_the_Lombards" title="List of kings of the Lombards">Lombard Kings</a>. Coins and documents from his court at <a href="/wiki/Pavia" title="Pavia">Pavia</a> confirm the impression of a strong and effective <a href="/wiki/Monarch" title="Monarch">monarch</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Arabian_Empire_2">Arabian Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=712&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-4" title="712">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Umayyad_conquest_of_Hispania" title="Umayyad conquest of Hispania">Umayyad conquest of Hispania</a>: From <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Musa_bin_Nusayr" title="Musa bin Nusayr">Musa ibn Nusayr</a> lands in <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Al-Andalus</a>), with an army of 18,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berber_people" title="Berber people">Berbers</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arab_people" title="Arab people">Arabs</a>. He joins the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_conquests" title="Islamic conquests">Islamic conquest</a> and captures the city of <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> (<a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a>), where he meets stiff resistance after 3-months of <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a>.</li>
<li>Arab forces under <a href="/wiki/Qutayba_ibn_Muslim" title="Qutayba ibn Muslim">Qutayba ibn Muslim</a> conquer <a href="/wiki/Khwarezm" title="Khwarezm">Khwarezm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samarkand" title="Samarkand">Samarkand</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia_3">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=712&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-5" title="712">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_8" title="September 8">September 8</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ruizong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Ruizong of Tang">Rui Zong</a> abdicates after a brief reign, in favor of his 27-year-old son <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xuanzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xuanzong of Tang">Xuan Zong</a>, who ascends the imperial <a href="/wiki/Throne" title="Throne">throne</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a> (<a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>).</li>
<li>Xuan Zong reestablishes its control over the <a href="/wiki/Amu_Darya" title="Amu Darya">Oxus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syr_Darya" title="Syr Darya">Jaxartes</a> valleys. During his reign he defeats the invading Arab armies, in a series of campaigns in <a href="/wiki/Fergana" title="Fergana">Fergana</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Go_of_Balhae" title="Go of Balhae">Dae Jo-yeong</a> of <a href="/wiki/Balhae" title="Balhae">Balhae</a> (<a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>) resumes <a href="/wiki/Tribute" title="Tribute">tributary</a> payments to the Tang Dynasty. The <a href="/wiki/Tai_peoples" title="Tai peoples">Tai peoples</a> are forced to accept Chinese <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">sovereignty</a> (approximate date).</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_3" title="June 3">June 3</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Philippikos_Bardanes" title="Philippikos Bardanes">Philippicus</a> is <a href="/wiki/Political_mutilation_in_Byzantine_culture" title="Political mutilation in Byzantine culture">blinded</a>, deposed, and sent into <a href="/wiki/Exile" title="Exile">exile</a> by conspirators of the <a href="/wiki/Opsikion" title="Opsikion">Opsikion</a> army in <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a>, after a reign of 1 year and 6 months. He is succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Anastasios_II" title="Anastasios II">Anastasios II</a>, a bureaucrat and imperial secretary, who restores internal order and begins the reorganization of the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_army" title="Byzantine army">Byzantine army</a>. He executes the <a href="/wiki/Officer" title="Officer">officers</a> who have been directly involved in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_conspiracies_(political)" title="List of conspiracies (political)">conspiracy</a> against Philippicus.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Arab%E2%80%93Byzantine_wars#Attacks_against_Byzantine_holdings_in_Africa.2C_Sicily_and_the_East" title="Arab–Byzantine wars">Arab–Byzantine wars</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arab_people" title="Arab people">Arabs</a> under <a href="/wiki/Al-Abbas_ibn_al-Walid" title="Al-Abbas ibn al-Walid">al-Abbas ibn al-Walid</a>, son of caliph <a href="/wiki/Al-Walid_I" title="Al-Walid I">al-Walid I</a>, sack <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antioch_in_Pisidia" title="Antioch in Pisidia">Antioch in Pisidia</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>), which never recovers.</li>
</ul>

<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Arabian_Empire_3">Arabian Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=713&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-4" title="713">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Umayyad_conquest_of_Hispania" title="Umayyad conquest of Hispania">Umayyad conquest of Hispania</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Visigothic_Kingdom" title="Visigothic Kingdom">Visigothic Kingdom</a> is finally defeated at the battle of <a href="/wiki/Tejeda_y_Segoyuela" title="Tejeda y Segoyuela">Segoyuela</a> (<a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">Castile and León</a>). Prince <a href="/wiki/Theudimer" title="Theudimer">Theudimer</a> signs the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Orihuela" title="Treaty of Orihuela">Treaty of Orihuela</a> with <a href="/wiki/Abd_al-Aziz_ibn_Musa" title="Abd al-Aziz ibn Musa">Abd al-Aziz</a>, governor of <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Al-Andalus</a>, and is permitted to retain his authority in the area subsequently known as <a href="/wiki/Region_of_Murcia" title="Region of Murcia">Tudmir</a>. He keeps the citadel of <a href="/wiki/Orihuela" title="Orihuela">Orihuela</a> and several other settlements, including <a href="/wiki/Alicante" title="Alicante">Alicante</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lorca,_Spain" title="Lorca, Spain">Lorca</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>Arab forces under <a href="/wiki/Musa_bin_Nusayr" title="Musa bin Nusayr">Musa ibn Nusayr</a> conquer the fortress city of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9rida,_Spain" title="Mérida, Spain">Mérida</a>, located on the borders of <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a>. It becomes part of the Umayyad <a href="/wiki/Caliphate_of_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Caliphate of Córdoba">Caliphate of Córdoba</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=713&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-5" title="713">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xuanzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xuanzong of Tang">Xuan Zong</a> liquidates the highly lucrative "Inexhaustible Treasury", which is run by a prominent Buddhist <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monastery</a> in Chang'an. This monastery collects vast amounts of money, <a href="/wiki/Silk" title="Silk">silk</a>, and treasures through multitudes of rich people's repentances, left on the premises anonymously. Although the monastery is generous in donations, Xuan Zong issues a <a href="/wiki/Decree" title="Decree">decree</a> abolishing their treasury, on the grounds that their banking practices were fraudulent, collects their riches, and distributes the wealth to various other <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhist</a> monasteries, <a href="/wiki/Taoism" title="Taoism">Daoist</a> abbeys, and to repair statues, halls, and bridges in the city.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an#Citywide_events" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a>, for the annual <a href="/wiki/Lantern_Festival" title="Lantern Festival">Lantern Festival</a> of this year, recently abdicated emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Ruizong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Ruizong of Tang">Rui Zong</a> erects an enormous <a href="/wiki/Lantern" title="Lantern">lantern</a> wheel at a <a href="/wiki/City_gate" title="City gate">city gate</a>, with a recorded height of 200 ft. The frame is draped in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brocade_(fabric)" title="Brocade (fabric)">brocades</a> and silk <a href="/wiki/Gauze" title="Gauze">gauze</a>, adorned with <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jade" title="Jade">jade</a> jewelry, and when its total of some 50,000 <a href="/wiki/Oil" title="Oil">oil</a> cups is lit, the radiance of it can be seen for miles.</li>
<li>Xuan Zong allots the money of 20 million copper <a href="/wiki/Coin" title="Coin">coins</a>, and assigns about 1,000 craftsmen to construct a hall at a Buddhist monastery with tons of <a href="/wiki/Portrait_painting" title="Portrait painting">painted portraits</a> of himself, and of deities, ghosts, etc.</li>
<li>Xuan Zong wins a power struggle with his sister, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Taiping" title="Princess Taiping">Princess Taiping</a>. He executes a large number of her allies and forcing her to commit <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe_4">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=714&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="714">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Septimania" title="Septimania">Septimania</a>, local Visigothic <a href="/wiki/Nobility" title="Nobility">nobles</a> of the anti-<a href="/wiki/Roderic" title="Roderic">Roderick</a> party are offered peace terms similar to those of Prince <a href="/wiki/Theudimer" title="Theudimer">Theudimer</a> (see <a href="/wiki/713" title="713">713</a>), and accept <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> overlordship. Other <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigoths</a> revolt and proclaim <a href="/wiki/Ardo" title="Ardo">Ardo</a> as king. Visigothic <a href="/wiki/Refugee" title="Refugee">refugees</a> gather in the <a href="/wiki/Picos_de_Europa" title="Picos de Europa">Picos de Europa</a> in the mountains of <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">Asturias</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_16" title="December 16">December 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/Pepin_of_Herstal" title="Pepin of Herstal">Pepin II (of Herstal)</a>, mayor of the <a href="/wiki/Merovingian_dynasty" title="Merovingian dynasty">Merovingian</a> palace, dies at <a href="/wiki/Jupille-sur-Meuse" title="Jupille-sur-Meuse">Jupille</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>). His infant grandson <a href="/wiki/Theudoald" title="Theudoald">Theudoald</a> (age 7) becomes the nominal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_the_palace" title="Mayor of the palace">mayor of the palace</a>, while his repudiated wife <a href="/wiki/Plectrude" title="Plectrude">Plectrude</a> holds actual power and imprisons Pepin's illegitimate son <a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">Civil War</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Pippinids" title="Pippinids">Pepinid</a> clan: A revolt erupts between the <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Neustrian Franks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frisians" title="Frisians">Frisians</a>. King <a href="/wiki/Redbad,_King_of_the_Frisians" title="Redbad, King of the Frisians">Radbod</a> forces bishop <a href="/wiki/Willibrord" title="Willibrord">Willibrord</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Saint_Benedict" title="Order of Saint Benedict">Benedictine</a> monks to flee, and advances as far as <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> (<a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>). <a href="/wiki/Frisian_Kingdom" title="Frisian Kingdom">Frisia</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>) becomes again independent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li>Duke <a href="/wiki/Odo_the_Great" title="Odo the Great">Eudes</a> proclaims himself the independent prince of <a href="/wiki/Aquitaine" title="Aquitaine">Aquitaine</a> (located north-east of the <a href="/wiki/Garonne" title="Garonne">Garonne River</a>), thereby asserting legal as well as practical independence from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankish_Kingdom" title="Frankish Kingdom">Frankish Kingdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Grimoald_the_Younger" title="Grimoald the Younger">Grimoald the Younger</a>, mayor of the palace of <a href="/wiki/Neustria" title="Neustria">Neustria</a>, is assassinated while on <a href="/wiki/Pilgrimage" title="Pilgrimage">pilgrimage</a> to visit the <a href="/wiki/Tomb" title="Tomb">tomb</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lambert_of_Maastricht" title="Lambert of Maastricht">Saint Lambert</a> at <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge" title="Liège">Liège</a>, on orders of his father-in-law King <a href="/wiki/Redbad,_King_of_the_Frisians" title="Redbad, King of the Frisians">Radbod</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Arabian_Empire_4">Arabian Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=714&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="714">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Umayyad_conquest_of_Hispania" title="Umayyad conquest of Hispania">Umayyad conquest of Hispania</a>: Continuing campaigns of Muslim domination of the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a>. Arab forces raid the valley of the <a href="/wiki/Ebro" title="Ebro">Ebro River</a>, and capture the fortress city of <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>. <a href="/wiki/Musa_bin_Nusayr" title="Musa bin Nusayr">Musa ibn Nusayr</a> is made protector (<i><a href="/wiki/Wali" title="Wali">wali</a></i>) of <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Al-Andalus</a>, with his capital at <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tariq_ibn_Ziyad" title="Tariq ibn Ziyad">Tariq ibn Ziyad</a> conquers the cities of <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Narbonne" title="Narbonne">Narbonne</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Visigoths" title="Visigoths">Visigothic</a> nobles accept <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad</a> overlordship, in return for autonomy in <a href="/wiki/Septimania" title="Septimania">Septimania</a> (Southern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>). Muslims also raid <a href="/wiki/Avignon" title="Avignon">Avignon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lyon" title="Lyon">Lyon</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li>Musa ibn Nusayr and Tariq ibn Ziyad are summoned back to <a href="/wiki/Damascus" title="Damascus">Damascus</a> by caliph <a href="/wiki/Al-Walid_I" title="Al-Walid I">Al-Walid I</a>. They are ordered to deliver all the <a href="/wiki/Looting" title="Looting">spoils of war</a>. Musa complains and is stripped of his rank. <a href="/wiki/Abd_al-Aziz_ibn_Musa" title="Abd al-Aziz ibn Musa">Abd al-Aziz</a>, son of Musa, becomes governor of Al-Andalus (modern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Al-Hajjaj_ibn_Yusuf" title="Al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf">Al-Hajjaj ibn Yusuf</a>, governor of <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>, dies at <a href="/wiki/Wasit,_Iraq" title="Wasit, Iraq">Wasit</a> (<a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>) after a 20-year administration. He is credited for improving <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agricultural</a> production and introducing the <a href="/wiki/Diacritic" title="Diacritic">diacritic points</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Arabic_script" title="Arabic script">Arabic script</a>. Al-Hajjaj convinces Al-Walid I to adopt an Arab <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="China_2">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=714&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-4" title="714">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xuanzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xuanzong of Tang">Xuan Zong</a> forbids all commercial <a href="/wiki/Vendor" title="Vendor">vendors</a> and shops in the Chinese capital city of <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a> to copy and sell <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Buddhism" title="Chinese Buddhism">Buddhist</a> <a href="/wiki/Sutra" title="Sutra">sutras</a>, so that the emperor can give the <a href="/wiki/Clergy" title="Clergy">clergy</a> of the Buddhist <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monasteries</a> the sole right to distribute written sutras to the <a href="/wiki/Laity" title="Laity">laity</a>.</li>
<li>Summer – Xuan Zong makes his general <a href="/wiki/Xue_Ne" title="Xue Ne">Xue Ne</a> chancellor <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i>, and commissions him with an Chinese army (60,000 men) to attack the <a href="/wiki/Khitan_people" title="Khitan people">Khitans</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>). Xue falls into a Khitan trap and the Tang forces are crushed, at an 80-90% casualty rate.</li>
<li>Fall – Xue Ne repels a <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Empire" title="Tibetan Empire">Tibetan</a> invasion of the Lan Prefecture (modern <a href="/wiki/Lanzhou" title="Lanzhou">Lanzhou</a>). Xuan Zong creates <a href="/wiki/Li_Ying_(prince)" title="Li Ying (prince)">Li Ying</a>, his second son, <a href="/wiki/Crown_prince" title="Crown prince">crown prince</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tang_Dynasty" title="Tang Dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a>.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cologne" title="Battle of Cologne">Battle of Cologne</a>: <a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a>, mayor of the palace of <a href="/wiki/Austrasia" title="Austrasia">Austrasia</a>, is defeated by the <a href="/wiki/Neustria" title="Neustria">Neustrians</a> under King <a href="/wiki/Chilperic_II" title="Chilperic II">Chilperic II</a> and his mayor <a href="/wiki/Ragenfrid" title="Ragenfrid">Ragenfrid</a> near <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> (now part of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>), who have invaded Austrasia to impose their will on the competing <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Frankish</a> factions of <a href="/wiki/Theudoald" title="Theudoald">Theudoald</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plectrude" title="Plectrude">Plectrude</a>, grandson (and designated heir) and widow of <a href="/wiki/Pepin_of_Herstal" title="Pepin of Herstal">Pepin of Herstal</a>. Simultaneously, <a href="/wiki/Redbad,_King_of_the_Frisians" title="Redbad, King of the Frisians">Radbod</a>, king (or <a href="/wiki/Duke" title="Duke">duke</a>) of the <a href="/wiki/Frisians" title="Frisians">Frisians</a>, attacks Austrasia and allies with the Neustrians. <li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ambl%C3%A8ve" title="Battle of Amblève">Battle of Amblève</a>: Charles Martel defeats his Neustrian and Frisian rivals near <a href="/wiki/Amel" title="Amel">Amel</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>). His forces attack the army of Chilperic II and his allies, as they return triumphantly from Cologne. According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Annales_Mettenses_priores" title="Annales Mettenses priores">Annals of Metz</a></i>, Charles uses a <a href="/wiki/Feigned_retreat" title="Feigned retreat">feigned retreat</a> to destroy his foes while they are resting, and recovers much of the <a href="/wiki/Ransom" title="Ransom">ransom</a> paid by Plectrude to Chilperic. He will remain undefeated until his death 25 years later.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place_8">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=717&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="717">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire_7">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=717&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="717">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_25" title="March 25">March 25</a> – Emperor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theodosios_III" title="Theodosios III">Theodosios III</a> is deposed after a reign of 1 year and 10 months. He is succeeded by the 32-year-old <a href="/wiki/Leo_III_the_Isaurian" title="Leo III the Isaurian">Leo III the Isaurian</a>, a general (<i><a href="/wiki/Strategos#Byzantine_use" title="Strategos">strategos</a></i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Anatolic_Theme" title="Anatolic Theme">Anatolic Theme</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>). Theodosios and his son enter the <a href="/wiki/Clergy" title="Clergy">clergy</a>, and he probably becomes <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishop</a> of <a href="/wiki/Metropolis_of_Ephesus" title="Metropolis of Ephesus">Ephesus</a>. Leo brings an end to the <a href="/wiki/Twenty_Years%27_Anarchy" title="Twenty Years' Anarchy">Twenty Years' Anarchy</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>, which marks the beginning of the so-called <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire_under_the_Isaurian_dynasty" title="Byzantine Empire under the Isaurian dynasty">Isaurian Dynasty</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Arab%E2%80%93Byzantine_wars#Attacks_against_Byzantine_holdings_in_Africa.2C_Sicily_and_the_East" title="Arab–Byzantine wars">Arab–Byzantine War</a>: Muslim general <a href="/wiki/Maslama_ibn_Abd_al-Malik" title="Maslama ibn Abd al-Malik">Maslama ibn Abd al-Malik</a> leads his army of 80,000 men from <a href="/wiki/Pergamon" title="Pergamon">Pergamum</a> to <a href="/wiki/Abydos_(Hellespont)" title="Abydos (Hellespont)">Abydos</a>, where he crosses the <a href="/wiki/Dardanelles" title="Dardanelles">Hellespont</a>. To prevent interference by the <a href="/wiki/Bulgars" title="Bulgars">Bulgars</a>, or by any Byzantine forces in <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a>, he sends part of his army to a covering position near <a href="/wiki/Edirne" title="Edirne">Adrianople</a>; with his main body, Maslama builds <a href="/wiki/Investment_(military)" title="Investment (military)">siege lines</a> to blockade <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>, which is protected by the massive <a href="/wiki/Walls_of_Constantinople#Theodosian_Walls" title="Walls of Constantinople">Theodosian Walls</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_15" title="August 15">August 15</a> – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Constantinople_(717%E2%80%9318)" title="Siege of Constantinople (717–18)">Siege of Constantinople</a>: Maslama begins a combined land and sea effort to capture Constantinople. The capital controls the <a href="/wiki/Bosphorus" title="Bosphorus">Bosporus</a>, access between the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>, and is defended by a <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a> of roughly 25,000 men. Leo III orders the <a href="/wiki/Granary" title="Granary">granaries</a> be restocked and <a href="/wiki/Siege_engine" title="Siege engine">siege engines</a> installed. The Arab besiegers are suffering immense losses due to <a href="/wiki/Disease" title="Disease">disease</a>, and from attrition of <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege warfare</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_1" title="September 1">September 1</a> – A Muslim <a href="/wiki/Naval_fleet" title="Naval fleet">armada</a>, consisting of 1,800 ships commanded by Admiral Suleiman, sails into the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Marmara" title="Sea of Marmara">Sea of Marmara</a> and drops anchor below the sea walls of Constantinople, to supply their forces ashore. Leo III orders the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_navy" title="Byzantine navy">Byzantine fleet</a> to sally forth from their protected <a href="/wiki/Harbor" title="Harbor">harbors</a> with <a href="/wiki/Greek_fire" title="Greek fire">Greek fire</a>, setting alight the thickly-packed Muslim ships. Many vessels burst into flames, while others collide with each other before sinking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></li>
<li>Fall – <a href="/wiki/Basil_Onomagoulos" title="Basil Onomagoulos">Basil Onomagoulos</a>, Byzantine official, declares himself rival emperor in <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a> after the news arrives that Constantinople has fallen to an Arab <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a>. Leo III dispatches a <i><a href="/wiki/Chartoularios" title="Chartoularios">chartoularios</a></i> named Paul, with imperial instructions for the Byzantine army on the island. Basil is arrested and executed; his head is sent to Leo, while the other rebels are <a href="/wiki/Political_mutilation_in_Byzantine_culture" title="Political mutilation in Byzantine culture">multilated</a> and <a href="/wiki/Exile" title="Exile">exiled</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe_7">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=717&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="717">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_21" title="March 21">March 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Vincy" title="Battle of Vincy">Battle of Vincy</a>: <a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a> invades <a href="/wiki/Neustria" title="Neustria">Neustria</a> and defeats the forces of King <a href="/wiki/Chilperic_II" title="Chilperic II">Chilperic II</a> at <a href="/wiki/Les_Rues-des-Vignes" title="Les Rues-des-Vignes">Vincy</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Cambrai" title="Cambrai">Cambrai</a>. He pursues him and his mayor of the palace <a href="/wiki/Ragenfrid" title="Ragenfrid">Ragenfrid</a> to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, before turning back to deal with his stepmother <a href="/wiki/Plectrude" title="Plectrude">Plectrude</a> at <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>, to turn over half the wealth of his late father <a href="/wiki/Pepin_of_Herstal" title="Pepin of Herstal">Pepin of Herstal</a>. Charles allows both Plectrude and the young <a href="/wiki/Theudoald" title="Theudoald">Theudoald</a> to live, and obliges her to accept his sovereignty.</li>
<li>Charles Martel consolidates his power, proclaims <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clotaire_IV" title="Clotaire IV">Clotaire IV</a> king of <a href="/wiki/Austrasia" title="Austrasia">Austrasia</a> in opposition to Chilperic, and deposes <a href="/wiki/Rigobert" title="Rigobert">Rigobert</a>, bishop of <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Reims" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Reims">Reims</a>, replacing him with <a href="/wiki/Milo_(bishop_of_Trier)" title="Milo (bishop of Trier)">Milo</a>. He marches against <a href="/wiki/Redbad,_King_of_the_Frisians" title="Redbad, King of the Frisians">Radbod</a>, king (or <a href="/wiki/Duke" title="Duke">duke</a>) of the <a href="/wiki/Frisia" title="Frisia">Frisians</a>, and pushes him back into his territory (later part of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>). Charles sends the <a href="/wiki/Saxons" title="Saxons">Saxons</a> back over the <a href="/wiki/Weser" title="Weser">Weser River</a>, and secures the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a> border—in the name of Clotaire.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Paolo_Lucio_Anafesto" title="Paolo Lucio Anafesto">Paolo Lucio Anafesto</a> dies after a 20-year reign, and is succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Marcello_Tegalliano" title="Marcello Tegalliano">Marcello Tegalliano</a> as the second <a href="/wiki/Doge" title="Doge">doge</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Republic of Venice</a>.</li>
</ul>

<li>Caliph <a href="/wiki/Sulayman_ibn_Abd_al-Malik" title="Sulayman ibn Abd al-Malik">Sulayman ibn Abd al-Malik</a> dies after a 2-year reign, and is succeeded by his cousin <a href="/wiki/Umar_II" title="Umar II">Umar II</a>. During his rule he grants <a href="/wiki/Tax" title="Tax">tax</a> exemption, and tries to reorganize the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad</a> finances.</li>
<li>A Muslim expedition under <a href="/wiki/Al-Hurr_ibn_Abd_al-Rahman_al-Thaqafi" title="Al-Hurr ibn Abd al-Rahman al-Thaqafi">Al-Hurr ibn Abd al-Rahman al-Thaqafi</a> cross the <a href="/wiki/Pyrenees" title="Pyrenees">Pyrenees</a> into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aquitania" title="Aquitania">Aquitanian</a> territory, leading a small raiding party into <a href="/wiki/Septimania" title="Septimania">Septimania</a> (Southern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>).</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place_9">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=718&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="718">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire_8">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=718&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="718">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>Spring – A Muslim supply fleet of 760 ships under Sufyan arrives from <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ifriqiya" title="Ifriqiya">North Africa</a>, concealing itself along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Minor" title="Asia Minor">Asiatic</a> shore. The <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantines</a> learn of the fleet's location from defecting <a href="/wiki/Copts" title="Copts">Christian Egyptians</a> sailors. Emperor <a href="/wiki/Leo_III_the_Isaurian" title="Leo III the Isaurian">Leo III</a> sends the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_navy" title="Byzantine navy">Byzantine navy</a> again; his <a href="/wiki/Greek_fire" title="Greek fire">Greek fire</a> ships destroy the enemy vessels in the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Marmara" title="Sea of Marmara">Sea of Marmara</a> and seize their supplies on shore, denying the sieging army vital provisions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> On land the Byzantine troops ambush an advancing Arab army, and destroy it in the hills around <a href="/wiki/Sapanca" title="Sapanca">Sophon</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Nicomedia" title="Nicomedia">Nicomedia</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The Arab besiegers are still suffering from <a href="/wiki/Hunger" title="Hunger">hunger</a> and pestilence.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_15" title="August 15">August 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Constantinople_(717%E2%80%93718)" title="Siege of Constantinople (717–718)">Siege of Constantinople</a>: A <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Empire" title="Bulgarian Empire">Bulgar</a> relief force attacks the <a href="/wiki/Investment_(military)" title="Investment (military)">siege lines</a> at <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>, on the west side of the <a href="/wiki/Bosphorus" title="Bosphorus">Bosporus</a>. Contemporary <a href="/wiki/Chronicle" title="Chronicle">chroniclers</a> report that at least 22,000–32,000 Arabs are killed during the Bulgarian attacks. Caliph <a href="/wiki/Umar_II" title="Umar II">Umar II</a> is forced to lift the <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a> after 13 months; the Muslim army attempts to withdraw back through <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a>, while the rest escapes by sea in the remaining vessels. The Arab fleet suffers further casualties to <a href="/wiki/Storm" title="Storm">storms</a>, and an <a href="/wiki/Types_of_volcanic_eruptions" title="Types of volcanic eruptions">eruption</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">volcano</a> of <a href="/wiki/Santorini" title="Santorini">Thera</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> According to Arab sources 150,000 Muslims perish during the campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe_8">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=718&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="718">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Soissons_(718)" title="Battle of Soissons (718)">Battle of Soissons</a>: King <a href="/wiki/Chilperic_II" title="Chilperic II">Chilperic II</a> of <a href="/wiki/Neustria" title="Neustria">Neustria</a> and his mayor of the palace <a href="/wiki/Ragenfrid" title="Ragenfrid">Ragenfrid</a>, allied with <a href="/wiki/Odo_the_Great" title="Odo the Great">Eudes</a>, independent <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Aquitaine" title="Duke of Aquitaine">duke of Aquitaine</a>, march on <a href="/wiki/Soissons" title="Soissons">Soissons</a> in <a href="/wiki/Picardy" title="Picardy">Picardy</a> (northern France). Unfortunately, an army of <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Frankish</a> veterans under <a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a> defeat the Neustrian allies, who sue for peace. Chilperic flees to the land south of the River <a href="/wiki/Loire" title="Loire">Loire</a> and Ragenfrid escapes to <a href="/wiki/Angers" title="Angers">Angers</a>. Charles diplomatically chooses not to execute the enemy leaders, and becomes undisputed <i><a href="/wiki/Duke_of_the_Franks" title="Duke of the Franks">dux Francorum</a></i>, ending the Frankish <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil war</a>.</li>
<li>Summer – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Covadonga" title="Battle of Covadonga">Battle of Covadonga</a>: <a href="/wiki/Pelagius_of_Asturias" title="Pelagius of Asturias">Pelagius (Don Pelayo)</a> is proclaimed king (<i><a href="/wiki/Caudillo" title="Caudillo">caudillo</a></i>), and defeats the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad</a> forces under <a href="/wiki/Munuza" title="Munuza">Munuza</a>, provincial governor of <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">Asturias</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Picos_de_Europa" title="Picos de Europa">Picos de Europa</a> (near <a href="/wiki/Covadonga" title="Covadonga">Covadonga</a>). This marks the beginning of the <i><a href="/wiki/Reconquista" title="Reconquista">Reconquista</a></i>, the <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christian</a> reconquest of the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a>. He founds the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Asturias" title="Kingdom of Asturias">Kingdom of Asturias</a>, and establishes a military base at <a href="/wiki/Cangas_de_On%C3%ADs" title="Cangas de Onís">Cangas de Onís</a> (northwest of Spain).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Liutprand,_King_of_the_Lombards" title="Liutprand, King of the Lombards">Liutprand</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombards</a> builds a close <a href="/wiki/Military_alliance" title="Military alliance">alliance</a> with Charles Martel, and attacks the <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavarian</a> castles on the <a href="/wiki/Adige" title="Adige">River Adige</a>, maintaining strategic control of the Alpine <a href="/wiki/Mountain_pass" title="Mountain pass">passes</a> in the Italian <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a> (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain_6">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=718&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-4" title="718">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Coenred_of_Northumbria" title="Coenred of Northumbria">Coenred of Northumbria</a> dies after a 2-year reign. The throne is seized by <a href="/wiki/Osric_of_Northumbria" title="Osric of Northumbria">Osric</a>, probably a younger brother, or half-brother, of the late king <a href="/wiki/Osred_I_of_Northumbria" title="Osred I of Northumbria">Osred I</a>.</li>
<li>Former queen <a href="/wiki/Cuthburh" title="Cuthburh">Cuthburh</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northumbria" title="Northumbria">Northumbria</a>, abbess of <a href="/wiki/Wimborne_Minster_(church)" title="Wimborne Minster (church)">Wimborne</a>, dies at her <a href="/wiki/Abbey" title="Abbey">abbey</a> and is buried there (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic_9">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=718&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-5" title="718">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion_8">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=718&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-6" title="718">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Wessex" title="Wessex">Wessex</a>-born <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionary</a> <a href="/wiki/Saint_Boniface" title="Saint Boniface">Boniface</a> sets out for <a href="/wiki/Frisia" title="Frisia">Frisia</a> a second time. He travels to Rome, where <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_II" title="Pope Gregory II">Pope Gregory II</a> sends him on a mission to convert the <a href="/wiki/Saxons" title="Saxons">Saxons</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a> (modern-day Germany).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hugh_of_Champagne" title="Hugh of Champagne">Hugh of Champagne</a>, cousin of Charles Martel, enters the <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monastery</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jumi%C3%A8ges_Abbey" title="Jumièges Abbey">Jumièges</a> (<a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a>), and embraces as <a href="/wiki/Abbot" title="Abbot">abbot</a> the religious life.</li>
</ul>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place_10">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=719&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-1" title="719">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire_9">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=719&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-2" title="719">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>Ex-Emperor <a href="/wiki/Anastasios_II" title="Anastasios II">Anastasios II</a> starts a revolt against <a href="/wiki/Leo_III_the_Isaurian" title="Leo III the Isaurian">Leo III</a> with considerable support, including <a href="/wiki/Auxiliaries" title="Auxiliaries">auxiliaries</a> provided by <a href="/wiki/Tervel_of_Bulgaria" title="Tervel of Bulgaria">Tervel</a>, emperor (<i><a href="/wiki/Khagan" title="Khagan">khagan</a></i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_Empire" title="Bulgarian Empire">Bulgarian Empire</a>. His attack on <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> fails; Anastasios is captured and is put to death (by <a href="/wiki/Decapitation" title="Decapitation">beheading</a>), on orders of Leo.</li>
</ul>
<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe_9">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=719&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-3" title="719">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_invasion_of_Gaul#Ummayad_conquest_of_Septimania" title="Islamic invasion of Gaul">Ummayad conquest of Gaul</a> (first major <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> attack upon <a href="/wiki/Visigothic_Kingdom" title="Visigothic Kingdom">Visigothic</a> <a href="/wiki/Septimania" title="Septimania">Septimania</a>, in southern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>): Governor <a href="/wiki/Al-Samh_ibn_Malik_al-Khawlani" title="Al-Samh ibn Malik al-Khawlani">Al-Samh</a> takes or re-takes <a href="/wiki/Narbonne" title="Narbonne">Narbonne</a> (Arbouna for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arab_people" title="Arab people">Arabs</a>), before raiding the <a href="/wiki/Toulouse" title="Toulouse">Toulouse</a> area. Many town defenders and inhabitants are killed in the aftermath by the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad</a> forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frisian-Frankish_wars#Rhine_delta_control" title="Frisian-Frankish wars">Frisian–Frankish War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a> defeats <a href="/wiki/Redbad,_King_of_the_Frisians" title="Redbad, King of the Frisians">Redbad, King of the Frisians</a>. He easily invades <a href="/wiki/Frisian_Kingdom" title="Frisian Kingdom">Frisia</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>) and subjugates the territory. Charles also crosses the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a> and annexes "farther" <a href="/wiki/Frisia" title="Frisia">Frisia</a>, to the banks of the River <a href="/wiki/Vlie" title="Vlie">Vlie</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Halbertsma1982_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Halbertsma1982-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">(p795)</sup></li>
<li>Duke <a href="/wiki/Grimoald_of_Bavaria" title="Grimoald of Bavaria">Grimoald</a> becomes sole ruler of <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Bavaria" title="Duchy of Bavaria">Bavaria</a>, after the deaths of his brothers <a href="/wiki/Theodbert_of_Bavaria" title="Theodbert of Bavaria">Theodbert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theobald_of_Bavaria" title="Theobald of Bavaria">Theobald</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tassilo_II_of_Bavaria" title="Tassilo II of Bavaria">Tassilo II</a>. He reunites the duchy after a <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil war</a>, and makes his capital <a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a> (approximate date).</li>
<li>May – <a href="/wiki/Chilperic_II" title="Chilperic II">Chilperic II</a> is raised on the <a href="/wiki/Shield#Middle_Ages" title="Shield">shield</a> after the death of <a href="/wiki/Chlothar_IV" title="Chlothar IV">Chlothar IV</a>, and recognized by Charles Martel as king (<i><a href="/wiki/Roi_fain%C3%A9ant" title="Roi fainéant">roi fainéant</a></i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Franks</a>. Charles, however, gains a <a href="/wiki/Monopoly" title="Monopoly">monopoly</a> on power and royal offices.</li>
</ul>

<h6><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion_9">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=719&amp;action=edit&amp;section=T-5" title="719">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h6>
<ul>
<li>The church of <a href="/wiki/Nubia" title="Nubia">Nubia</a> transfers its <a href="/wiki/Allegiance" title="Allegiance">allegiance</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox Church</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Coptic_Orthodox_Church_of_Alexandria" title="Coptic Orthodox Church of Alexandria">Coptic Church</a> (approximate date).</li>
