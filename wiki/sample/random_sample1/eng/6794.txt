<p><b>Brač</b> (<small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[brâːtʃ]</a></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chakavian_dialect" title="Chakavian dialect">local Chakavian</a>: <i>Broč</i>, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA for Serbo-Croatian">[broːtʃ]</a></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <span lang="la" xml:lang="la"><i>Bretia</i></span>, <span lang="la" xml:lang="la"><i>Brattia</i></span>; <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <span lang="it" xml:lang="it"><i>Brazza</i></span>) is an island in the <a href="/wiki/Adriatic_Sea" title="Adriatic Sea">Adriatic Sea</a> within <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>, with an area of 396 square kilometres (153 sq mi),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Duplancic-Cala-Ujevic_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Duplancic-Cala-Ujevic-1">[1]</a></sup> making it the largest island in <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">Dalmatia</a>, and the third largest in the Adriatic. It is separated from the mainland by the <a href="/wiki/Bra%C4%8D_Channel" title="Brač Channel">Brač Channel</a>, which is 5 to 13 km (3 to 8 mi) wide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The island's tallest peak, <a href="/wiki/Vidova_gora" title="Vidova gora">Vidova gora</a>, or Mount St. Vid, stands at 780 m,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yearbook_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yearbook-3">[3]</a></sup> making it the highest island point in the Adriatic. The island has a population of 13,956,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yearbook_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Yearbook-3">[3]</a></sup> living in numerous settlements, ranging from the main town <a href="/wiki/Supetar" title="Supetar">Supetar</a>, with more than 3,300 inhabitants, to <a href="/wiki/Murvica,_Split-Dalmatia_County" title="Murvica, Split-Dalmatia County">Murvica</a>, where less than two dozen people live. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bol_Airport" title="Bol Airport">Bol Airport</a> on Brač is the largest airport of all islands surrounding <a href="/wiki/Split,_Croatia" title="Split, Croatia">Split</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bra%C4%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Archaeological findings date the existence of human communities on the island back to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palaeolithic" title="Palaeolithic">palaeolithic</a> (in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kopa%C4%8Dina_cave&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kopačina cave (page does not exist)">Kopačina cave</a> between Supetar and Donji Humac). Nevertheless, there are no traces of human habitation from the <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">neolithic</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illyria" title="Illyria">Illyrian</a> tribes populated the inner parts of the island. Numerous villages existed at that time (but none of them survived).</p>
<p>In the 4th century BC Greek colonisation spread over many Adriatic islands and along the shore, but none of them on Brač. Nevertheless, Greeks visited the island and also traded with the Illyric tribes; Greek <a href="/wiki/Artifact_(archaeology)" title="Artifact (archaeology)">artifacts</a> were found in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bay_of_Vi%C4%8Dja&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bay of Vičja (page does not exist)">bay of Vičja</a> near <a href="/wiki/Lo%C5%BEi%C5%A1%C4%87a" title="Ložišća">Ložišća</a> on the estate of the Rakela-Bugre brothers. Many of the objects belonging to this still unexamined site are now on display in the Archeological Museum of Split. Brač lay on the crossroads of several trade routes from <a href="/wiki/Salona" title="Salona">Salona</a> (today <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solin,_Croatia" title="Solin, Croatia">Solin</a>) to <a href="/wiki/Vis_(island)" title="Vis (island)">Issa</a> (today Vis) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Po_River" title="Po River">Po River</a>.</p>

<p>In the year AD 9, the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Romans</a> finally conquered <a href="/wiki/Dalmatia" title="Dalmatia">Dalmatia</a> after long fights against the native tribes. <a href="/wiki/Salona" title="Salona">Salona</a> became the capital of the new province and, probably because of its proximity to Salona, no bigger villages or towns were founded on the island. Signs of Roman habitation can be found all over the islands, but they usually remain single <a href="/wiki/Roman_villa" title="Roman villa">Roman villas</a>, cisterns, and especially early quarries between <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0krip" title="Škrip">Škrip</a> and <a href="/wiki/Splitska" title="Splitska">Splitska</a>. Splitska also became the most important harbour to carry stone to Salona and the whole of Dalmatia. <a href="/wiki/Diocletian%27s_Palace" title="Diocletian's Palace">Diocletian's Palace</a>, which later became Split, was largely built with <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> that was quarried on Brač.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Also <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">wine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olive" title="Olive">olives</a>, began in the same era.</p>
<p>After the destruction of Salona by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avars_(Carpathians)" title="Avars (Carpathians)">Avar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Croats" title="Croats">Croatian</a> tribes, Brač became a refuge for many denizens of the shore. Tradition has it that Škrip was founded by <a href="/wiki/Refugee" title="Refugee">refugee</a> Salonans, but the town is actually much older than that. In 872, the island was sacked by Saracen raiders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>From AD 1268 to 1357 the island recognised the supremacy of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Republic of Venice</a>, and after that they bowed to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary_in_the_Middle_Ages" title="Kingdom of Hungary in the Middle Ages">Kingdom of Hungary</a>. In the summer of 1390, together with the whole region, they accepted the rule of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bosnia" title="Kingdom of Bosnia">Bosnian</a> <a href="/wiki/Tvrtko_I_of_Bosnia" title="Tvrtko I of Bosnia">King Tvrtko</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kotromani%C4%87" title="Kotromanić">Kotromanić</a>, who died the next year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Soon after his death, Hungary claimed the island again. In this whole period, they kept their basic autonomy and old structures - the island was never rich or strategically interesting enough to justify serious intervention. Local nobility administered and ruled Brač and the seat of the council was <a href="/wiki/Nere%C5%BEi%C5%A1%C4%87a" title="Nerežišća">Nerežišća</a> in the island's center. The leader was selected from the noble families. Only in 1420 did the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Venetian_Republic" title="Venetian Republic">Venetian Republic</a> reclaim the island, finally sending a representative to assume rule over it.</p>
<p>Venice ruled for more than four centuries, until 1797, when the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Monarchy" title="Habsburg Monarchy">Habsburg Monarchy</a> annexed most of its territory in a deal with Napoleonic France. The official language was Latin. During this time, the Bosnian realm fell to the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> and many refugees settled on the islands, especially on Brač. Many towns were founded in that time and the population began moving from the interior of the island to its coast: to Bol, Milna, Postira, Povlja, Pučišća, Splitska, Sumartin, Supetar i Sutivan.</p>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a>, Brač was conquered by the <a href="/wiki/First_French_Empire" title="First French Empire">French Empire</a> for a short time in 1806. In 1807, Prince-Bishop <a href="/wiki/Petar_I_Petrovi%C4%87_Njego%C5%A1" title="Petar I Petrović Njegoš">Petar I Njegoš</a> of <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a> managed to seize Brač with the help of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian</a> navy, however already at the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a> in 1815 the island was returned to the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Empire" title="Austrian Empire">Austrian Empire</a>. Brač was incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austrian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crownland" title="Crownland">crownland</a> of Dalmatia from and became a part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transleithania" title="Transleithania">Transleithania</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy" title="Monarchy">Monarchy</a> of <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a> from 1867. After the fall of Austria-Hungary 1918, Brač became part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Serbs,_Croats_and_Slovenes" title="Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes">Kingdom of Serbs, Croats and Slovenes</a>, or Yugoslavia since 1929. In 1939 an autonomous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Croatian_Banate" title="Croatian Banate">Croatian Banate</a> was created that included the island.</p>

<p>The population of the island drastically decreased in the beginning of the 20th century due to heavy emigration, mostly to <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, and to <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. The emigration continued during the whole century, only later generations preferring to move to European countries, especially <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Among others, the <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> writer <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Sk%C3%A1rmeta" title="Antonio Skármeta">Antonio Skármeta</a> is descended from such immigrants.</p>
<p>In 1941 <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> forces occupied the island. In the mountainous regions of the island, native rebels fought a quite effective <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guerrilla_war" title="Guerrilla war">guerrilla war</a>, but the occupiers answered harshly with arrests and executions. After the Italian capitulation in 1943, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> troops occupied the island on January 12 and 13 of 1944, but in July they were defeated and the island was freed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> As part of Croatia it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia</a>, until Croatia gained its independence in 1991, receiving recognition in 1992. The <a href="/wiki/Croatian_War_of_Independence" title="Croatian War of Independence">Croatian War of Independence</a> was barely fought on the island (there was a brief bombing of Milna), but the aftermath of the war, especially the loss in tourism, was disastrous for the island. Only now is the island regenerating from the decade-long drainage of its most important revenue.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bra%C4%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The economy of Brač is based mostly on <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> (especially <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">wine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olive" title="Olive">olives</a>) are very important too, as is its precious white stone which was used in building <a href="/wiki/Diocletian%27s_Palace" title="Diocletian's Palace">Diocletian's Palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Split,_Croatia" title="Split, Croatia">Split</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_National_Vimy_Memorial" title="Canadian National Vimy Memorial">Canadian National Vimy Memorial</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Very widespread is also the claim that it was used in the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a> in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Historically, Brač was famous for goats; even <a href="/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder" title="Pliny the Elder">Pliny</a> comments that from the island of Brattia (the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> name for the island) comes excellent cheese, wine and olive oil.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration_and_settlements">Administration and settlements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bra%C4%8D&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Administration and settlements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The island is administratively divided into one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Croatia" title="List of cities in Croatia">city</a> and seven <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Croatia" title="Municipalities of Croatia">municipalities</a>, including the twenty-two settlements. Population numbers are given per 2011 census. The island is part of the <a href="/wiki/Split-Dalmatia_County" title="Split-Dalmatia County">Split-Dalmatia County</a>, but is not represented there as a whole, only through its city and municipalities.</p>
