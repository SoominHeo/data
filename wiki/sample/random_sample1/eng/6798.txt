<p><b>Branko Oblak</b> (born 27 May 1947 in <a href="/wiki/Ljubljana" title="Ljubljana">Ljubljana</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_Slovenia" title="People's Republic of Slovenia">PR Slovenia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_People%27s_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Federal People's Republic of Yugoslavia">FPR Yugoslavia</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> coach and former international player. He usually played as an <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">attacking midfielder</a> or <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">deep-lying playmaker</a>, notable for his excellent dribbling, stamina, great vision and precision passing. He was also capable of helping out defensively.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Branko_Oblak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brane, as he is often known, started playing football at the youth team of <a href="/wiki/NK_Svoboda" title="NK Svoboda">Svoboda</a>. In 1965 he went to Ljubljana's more famous club, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NK_Olimpija_Ljubljana_(1911)" title="NK Olimpija Ljubljana (1911)">Olimpija</a>. He made his debut on 20 May 1966 against <a href="/wiki/FK_Partizan" title="FK Partizan">Partizan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a>, where he scored both Olimpija's goals for a 1–2 win. He stayed with Olimpija until 1973. During that time he played 181 matches and scored 33 goals.</p>
<p>In 1973, he moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hajduk_Split" title="Hajduk Split">Hajduk Split</a> and stayed there for two seasons. In both seasons Hajduk won the national champion and cup winner titles. In 1975 he signed a two-year contract with <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a> of the German <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> for what was a world record fee for that season. In the 1976–77 season, Oblak's second year at the club, Schalke 04 finished the league in second place, behind <a href="/wiki/Borussia_M%C3%B6nchengladbach" title="Borussia Mönchengladbach">Borussia Mönchengladbach</a>. In the summer of 1977, Oblak went to <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>. He stayed there for three seasons and in his final season with the club (1979–80), Bayern became the German champion. After that, Oblak played in various <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austrian</a> amateur clubs until 1987, when he retired as a football player.</p>
<p>Oblak's debut for the <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia_national_football_team" title="Yugoslavia national football team">Yugoslav national team</a> came in a friendly match against <a href="/wiki/Romania_national_football_team" title="Romania national football team">Romania</a> in 1971. The same year he had the honour of playing at <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9" title="Pelé">Pelé</a>'s farewell match at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_do_Maracan%C3%A3" title="Estádio do Maracanã">Maracanã</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/1974_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1974 FIFA World Cup">1974 World Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, he and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Danilo_Popivoda" title="Danilo Popivoda">Danilo Popivoda</a> became the first <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenians</a> to play in a World Cup. Even more impressive is that Oblak was selected into the best squad of the World Cup. In 1976 he played at the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1976" title="UEFA Euro 1976">European Championship</a>, which was hosted by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SFR_Yugoslavia" title="SFR Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</a>. His excellent pass for the first goal in the semi final match against <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_football_team" title="Germany national football team">Germany</a> is still remembered among football experts. After the tournament, he retired from international football. In altogether 46 matches he scored six goals.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Branko_Oblak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After retirement as a football player, Oblak started coaching at various Slovenian clubs, including three times at Olimpija Ljubljana,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/NK_Naklo_(defunct)" title="NK Naklo (defunct)">NK Naklo</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Koper" title="FC Koper">FC Koper</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He proved to be a good youth coach, so in February 2004 he was named the new Slovenia under-21 national coach. In May 2004, Slovenian national team head coach <a href="/wiki/Bojan_Pra%C5%A1nikar" title="Bojan Prašnikar">Bojan Prašnikar</a> was fired due to bad results and on 24 May 2004 Oblak was chosen to replace him. The team started brightly under his guidance, with the highlight being a 1–0 over <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> (eventual World Champions) in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_2006" title="FIFA World Cup 2006">World Cup</a> qualifying match. However, the team soon ran into a series of bad results, and the pressure mounted on Oblak, until finally in late 2006, he was fired from the job. He was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Matja%C5%BE_Kek" title="Matjaž Kek">Matjaž Kek</a>. In 2009 he again took the managers job at his home club <a href="/wiki/NK_Olimpija_Ljubljana_(2005)" title="NK Olimpija Ljubljana (2005)">NK Olimpija Ljubljana</a>. He was relieved of his duties after only four games.</p>
<p>In November 2003, <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> member football associations organized surveys to find the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Jubilee_Awards" title="UEFA Jubilee Awards">best national players</a> in 50-year period of existence of UEFA. In Slovenia Branko Oblak was awarded this title, "beating" rivals such as <a href="/wiki/Sre%C4%8Dko_Katanec" title="Srečko Katanec">Srečko Katanec</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zlatko_Zahovi%C4%8D" title="Zlatko Zahovič">Zlatko Zahovič</a>.</p>
