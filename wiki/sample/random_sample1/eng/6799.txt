<p><b>Richard Francis Xavier Manning</b>, known as <b>Brennan Manning</b> (April 27, 1934 – April 12, 2013)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was an American author, <a href="/wiki/Laicization_(Catholic_Church)" title="Laicization (Catholic Church)">laicized</a> priest, and public speaker.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meet_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Meet-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sermon_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sermon-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He is best known for his bestselling book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ragamuffin_Gospel" title="The Ragamuffin Gospel">The Ragamuffin Gospel</a></i>.</p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brennan_Manning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Depression-era</a> <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, Manning finished high school, enlisted in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Marine_Corps" title="US Marine Corps">US Marine Corps</a>, and fought in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>. After returning to the United States, he enrolled at <a href="/wiki/Saint_Francis_University" title="Saint Francis University">Saint Francis University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Loretto,_Pennsylvania" title="Loretto, Pennsylvania">Loretto, Pennsylvania</a>. Upon his graduation from the seminary in 1963, Manning was ordained a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franciscan" title="Franciscan">Franciscan</a> priest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meet_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Meet-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the late 1960s, Manning joined the <a href="/wiki/Little_Brothers_of_Jesus" title="Little Brothers of Jesus">Little Brothers of Jesus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Foucauld" title="Charles de Foucauld">Charles de Foucauld</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Religious_institute" title="Religious institute">religious institute</a> committed to an uncloistered, contemplative life among the poor. Manning transported water via donkey, worked as a mason's assistant and a dishwasher in France, was imprisoned (by choice) in Switzerland, and spent six months in a remote cave somewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a> desert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ragamuffin_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ragamuffin-5">[5]</a></sup> In the 1970s, Manning returned to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and began writing after confronting his <a href="/wiki/Alcoholism" title="Alcoholism">alcoholism</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Rich_Mullins" title="Rich Mullins">Rich Mullins</a> named his band, <a href="/wiki/A_Ragamuffin_Band" title="A Ragamuffin Band">A Ragamuffin Band</a>, after one of Manning's books.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Healing_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Healing-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Warren_Barfield" title="Warren Barfield">Warren Barfield</a>'s music is also often inspired by Manning, as is the work of singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Perryman_Jones" title="Matthew Perryman Jones">Matthew Perryman Jones</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>The following quote appeared in the prelude to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dc_Talk" title="Dc Talk">dc Talk</a>'s song "What if I Stumble?" It also appeared on an intro track for the Christian metalcore band <a href="/wiki/War_of_Ages" title="War of Ages">War of Ages</a> on its album <i>Fire From the Tomb</i>. Manning added: "That is what an unbelieving world simply finds unbelievable."</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>The greatest single cause of <a href="/wiki/Atheism" title="Atheism">atheism</a> in the world today is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christians" title="Christians">Christians</a> who acknowledge <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a> with their lips and walk out the door and deny Him by their lifestyle. That is what an unbelieving world simply finds unbelievable.</p>
</blockquote>
