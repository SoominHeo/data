<p>Year <b>724</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCCXXIV</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Saturday" title="Leap year starting on Saturday">leap year starting on Saturday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 724 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=724&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=724&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ragenfrid" title="Ragenfrid">Ragenfrid</a>, ex-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_the_palace" title="Mayor of the palace">mayor of the palace</a> of <a href="/wiki/Neustria" title="Neustria">Neustria</a>, revolts against <a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a>. He is easily defeated, and Ragenfrid gives up his sons as <a href="/wiki/Hostage" title="Hostage">hostages</a>, in turn for keeping his lands in <a href="/wiki/Anjou" title="Anjou">Anjou</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Arabian_Empire">Arabian Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=724&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Arabian Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_26" title="January 26">January 26</a> – Caliph <a href="/wiki/Yazid_II" title="Yazid II">Yazid II</a> dies of <a href="/wiki/Tuberculosis" title="Tuberculosis">tuberculosis</a> after a 4-year reign. He is succeeded by his brother <a href="/wiki/Hisham_ibn_Abd_al-Malik" title="Hisham ibn Abd al-Malik">Hisham ibn Abd al-Malik</a>, who appoints <a href="/wiki/Khalid_al-Qasri" title="Khalid al-Qasri">Khalid al-Qasri</a> as governor of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Umayyad_governors_of_Iraq" title="List of Umayyad governors of Iraq">Iraq</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Turgesh" title="Turgesh">Turgesh</a> <a href="/wiki/Khanate" title="Khanate">Khaganate</a> scores a major victory over the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Arabs</a>, in the "<a href="/wiki/Day_of_Thirst" title="Day of Thirst">Day of Thirst</a>" near <a href="/wiki/Khujand" title="Khujand">Khujand</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>).</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Arab_Muslims" title="Arab Muslims">Muslim</a> fleet raids the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a>-ruled <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a>, as well as Byzantine <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a> and Lombard <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=724&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_3" title="March 3">March 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/Empress_Gensh%C5%8D" title="Empress Genshō">Empress Genshō</a> abdicates the <a href="/wiki/Throne" title="Throne">throne</a>, in favor of her 23-year-old nephew <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Sh%C5%8Dmu" title="Emperor Shōmu">Shōmu</a>. He is the son of the late emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Monmu" title="Emperor Monmu">Monmu</a>, and becomes the 45th <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan" title="Emperor of Japan">monarch of Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=724&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mesoamerica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/K%27ak%27_Tiliw_Chan_Yopaat" title="K'ak' Tiliw Chan Yopaat">K'ak' Tiliw Chan Yopaat</a> becomes king (<i><a href="/wiki/Ajaw" title="Ajaw">ajaw</a></i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Maya</a> city-state of <a href="/wiki/Quirigu%C3%A1" title="Quiriguá">Quiriguá</a> (<a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>), until his death in <a href="/wiki/785" title="785">785</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=724&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=724&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Shōmu orders that houses of the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Nobility" title="Nobility">nobility</a> be roofed with green <a href="/wiki/Tile" title="Tile">tiles</a>, as in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, and have white walls with red roof poles (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=724&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Saint_Pirmin" title="Saint Pirmin">Pirmin</a>, Visigothic <a href="/wiki/Monk" title="Monk">monk</a>, is appointed <a href="/wiki/Abbot" title="Abbot">abbot</a> of Mittelzell Abbey at <a href="/wiki/Reichenau_Island" title="Reichenau Island">Reichenau Island</a>, which he has founded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hugh_of_Champagne" title="Hugh of Champagne">Hugh of Champagne</a>, grandson of <a href="/wiki/Pepin_of_Herstal" title="Pepin of Herstal">Pepin of Herstal</a>, is appointed <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishop</a> of <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Bayeux" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Bayeux">Bayeux</a>.</li>
</ul>
