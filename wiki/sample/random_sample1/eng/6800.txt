<p><b>Robert Creel "Brad" Davis</b> (November 6, 1949 – September 8, 1991) was an American actor, known for starring in the 1978 film <i><a href="/wiki/Midnight_Express_(film)" title="Midnight Express (film)">Midnight Express</a></i> and 1982 film <i><a href="/wiki/Querelle" title="Querelle">Querelle</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Davis_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born <b>Robert Creel Davis</b> in <a href="/wiki/Tallahassee,_Florida" title="Tallahassee, Florida">Tallahassee, Florida</a> to Eugene Davis (a dentist whose career declined due to <a href="/wiki/Alcoholism" title="Alcoholism">alcoholism</a>) and his wife, Anne (née Creel) Davis. His brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gene_Davis_(actor)" title="Gene Davis (actor)">Gene</a> is also an actor. According to an article in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> published in 1987, Davis suffered <a href="/wiki/Physical_abuse" title="Physical abuse">physical abuse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Child_sexual_abuse" title="Child sexual abuse">sexual abuse</a> at the hands of both parents. As an adult, he was an <a href="/wiki/Alcoholism" title="Alcoholism">alcoholic</a> and an intravenous drug user before becoming <a href="/wiki/Sobriety" title="Sobriety">sober</a> in 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">[1]</a></sup> Davis was known as "Bobby" during his youth, but took Brad as his stage name in 1973.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">[1]</a></sup> Davis attended and graduated from Titusville High School, Titusville, Florida. Also attending school with Brad and Gene was Denny Terrio (known as Dennis Mahan at that time) of "Dance Fever" and "Saturday Night Fever" fame who also lived just four houses away around the corner from the Davis home.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Davis_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At 16, after winning a music-talent contest, Davis worked at Theater Atlanta. He later moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and attended the <a href="/wiki/American_Academy_of_Dramatic_Arts" title="American Academy of Dramatic Arts">American Academy of Dramatic Arts</a>, as well as studied acting at the American Place Theater. After a role on the <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/How_to_Survive_a_Marriage" title="How to Survive a Marriage">How to Survive a Marriage</a></i>, he performed in <a href="/wiki/Off-Broadway" title="Off-Broadway">Off-Broadway</a> plays.</p>
<p>In 1976, he was cast in the television mini-series <i><a href="/wiki/Roots_(1977_miniseries)" title="Roots (1977 miniseries)">Roots</a></i>, then as <a href="/wiki/Sally_Field" title="Sally Field">Sally Field</a>'s love interest in the television film <i><a href="/wiki/Sybil_(1976_film)" title="Sybil (1976 film)">Sybil</a></i>. In 1981 he played American track star <a href="/wiki/Jackson_Scholz" title="Jackson Scholz">Jackson Scholz</a> in the Academy Award winning film <i><a href="/wiki/Chariots_of_Fire" title="Chariots of Fire">Chariots of Fire</a></i>. He played the lead role in <a href="/wiki/Larry_Kramer" title="Larry Kramer">Larry Kramer</a>'s play about <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AIDS" title="AIDS">AIDS</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Normal_Heart" title="The Normal Heart">The Normal Heart</a></i> (1985). His most successful film role was as the main character, <a href="/wiki/Billy_Hayes_(writer)" title="Billy Hayes (writer)">Billy Hayes</a>, in <i><a href="/wiki/Midnight_Express_(film)" title="Midnight Express (film)">Midnight Express</a></i> (1978), for which he won the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_New_Star_of_the_Year_%E2%80%93_Actor" title="Golden Globe Award for New Star of the Year – Actor">Golden Globe Award for New Star of the Year – Actor</a> and the Golden Globe Award for Best Motion Picture Acting Debut – Actor. He was also nominated for a similar award at that year's <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_of_Film_and_Television_Arts" title="British Academy of Film and Television Arts">BAFTA Awards</a>, in addition to receiving Best Actor nominations at both ceremonies.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Davis_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Davis married <a href="/wiki/Susan_Bluestein" title="Susan Bluestein">Susan Bluestein</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a>-winning casting director. They had one child, Alex, a <a href="/wiki/Trans_man" title="Trans man">transgender man</a> born as Alexandra.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Davis acknowledged having had sex with men and being bisexual in an interview with <a href="/wiki/Boze_Hadleigh" title="Boze Hadleigh">Boze Hadleigh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Davis_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/HIV" title="HIV">HIV</a> in 1985, Davis kept his condition a secret until shortly before his death at age 41, on September 8, 1991, in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. It was revealed in a book proposal that Davis had written before his death that he had to keep his medical condition a secret in order to be able to continue to work and support his family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
