<p><b>Brad Anderson</b> (born 1964) is an American <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a>, producer and writer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Anderson_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Anderson-1">[1]</a></sup> A director of thriller and horror films and television projects, he is best known for having directed <i><a href="/wiki/The_Machinist" title="The Machinist">The Machinist</a></i> (2004), starring <a href="/wiki/Christian_Bale" title="Christian Bale">Christian Bale</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Call_(2013_film)" title="The Call (2013 film)">The Call</a></i> (2013), starring <a href="/wiki/Halle_Berry" title="Halle Berry">Halle Berry</a>. He also produced and directed several installments of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Network" title="Fox Broadcasting Network">FOX</a> <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Fringe_(TV_series)" title="Fringe (TV series)">Fringe</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Anderson_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Anderson_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Anderson was born in <a href="/wiki/Madison,_Connecticut" title="Madison, Connecticut">Madison, Connecticut</a>, the son of Pamela Taylor Anderson, a community services administrator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is the nephew of <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a>-winning actress <a href="/wiki/Holland_Taylor" title="Holland Taylor">Holland Taylor</a>. Before he began his film career, he attended <a href="/wiki/Bowdoin_College" title="Bowdoin College">Bowdoin College</a>, where he <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academic_major" title="Academic major">majored</a> in <a href="/wiki/Anthropology" title="Anthropology">anthropology</a> and Russian. He then went to London to finish his film education before returning to Boston.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_career">Film career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Anderson_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Anderson started out directing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy" title="Romantic comedy">romantic comedy</a> films <i><a href="/wiki/Next_Stop_Wonderland" title="Next Stop Wonderland">Next Stop Wonderland</a></i> (1998) and <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Accidents_(film)" title="Happy Accidents (film)">Happy Accidents</a></i> (2000). The films were <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a> audience favorites.</p>
<p>His next film was the 2001 <a href="/wiki/Psychological_horror" title="Psychological horror">psychological horror</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Session_9" title="Session 9">Session 9</a></i>. Unsuccessful at the box office, the film has since gained a <a href="/wiki/Cult_following" title="Cult following">cult following</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2002, Anderson was a member of the dramatic jury at the Sundance Film Festival.</p>
<p>This was followed by his most notable work to date, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Machinist" title="The Machinist">The Machinist</a></i> (2004), starring <a href="/wiki/Christian_Bale" title="Christian Bale">Christian Bale</a>. The film has helped earn Anderson a cult following.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>His next two films were <i><a href="/wiki/Transsiberian_(film)" title="Transsiberian (film)">Transsiberian</a></i> (2008), a thriller starring <a href="/wiki/Woody_Harrelson" title="Woody Harrelson">Woody Harrelson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emily_Mortimer" title="Emily Mortimer">Emily Mortimer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ben_Kingsley" title="Ben Kingsley">Ben Kingsley</a> and the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Vanishing_on_7th_Street" title="Vanishing on 7th Street">Vanishing on 7th Street</a></i> (2010), starring <a href="/wiki/Hayden_Christensen" title="Hayden Christensen">Hayden Christensen</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Leguizamo" title="John Leguizamo">John Leguizamo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thandie_Newton" title="Thandie Newton">Thandie Newton</a>. Notably, both <i>Transsiberian</i> and <i>The Machinist</i> were funded by Anglo-German production companies.</p>
<p>At one point, he was also one of the candidates to direct the sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/Paranormal_Activity" title="Paranormal Activity">Paranormal Activity</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013 Anderson directed <i><a href="/wiki/The_Call_(2013_film)" title="The Call (2013 film)">The Call</a></i>, a thriller starring <a href="/wiki/Halle_Berry" title="Halle Berry">Halle Berry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abigail_Breslin" title="Abigail Breslin">Abigail Breslin</a>.</p>
<p>His latest film is <i><a href="/wiki/Stonehearst_Asylum" title="Stonehearst Asylum">Stonehearst Asylum</a></i> (aka Eliza Graves), with <a href="/wiki/Kate_Beckinsale" title="Kate Beckinsale">Kate Beckinsale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Sturgess" title="Jim Sturgess">Jim Sturgess</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Thewlis" title="David Thewlis">David Thewlis</a>, in the leading roles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_work">Television work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Anderson_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Television work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Anderson has directed numerous episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Fringe_(TV_series)" title="Fringe (TV series)">Fringe</a></i>, as well as two episodes each of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Wire_(TV_series)" title="The Wire (TV series)">The Wire</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Killing_(US_TV_series)" title="The Killing (US TV series)">The Killing</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Boardwalk_Empire" title="Boardwalk Empire">Boardwalk Empire</a></i>.</p>
<p>Anderson was one of the contributors to the horror series <i><a href="/wiki/Masters_of_Horror" title="Masters of Horror">Masters of Horror</a></i>, directing the season two episode "<a href="/wiki/Sounds_Like" title="Sounds Like">Sounds Like</a>".</p>
<p>Anderson directed the pilot episode of the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_television" title="Primetime television">prime time</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Forever_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Forever (U.S. TV series)">Forever</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1.01_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-1.01-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_projects">Future projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Anderson_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Future projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Anderson replaced Joseph Ruben as director of Bold Films thriller <i>Jack</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> in May 2010, and cast <a href="/wiki/John_Cusack" title="John Cusack">John Cusack</a> for the lead,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> who has since been replaced by <a href="/wiki/Liev_Schreiber" title="Liev Schreiber">Liev Schreiber</a>. Anderson will direct <i>The Living and the Dead</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> based on the novel of the same name by Robert Tinnell and Todd Livingston.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>After working together on <i>The Machinist</i>, Anderson and Christian Bale plan to collaborate again on an adaptation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.G._Ballard" title="J.G. Ballard">J.G. Ballard</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/Concrete_Island" title="Concrete Island">Concrete Island</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
