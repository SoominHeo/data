<p><b>Bradley Roland Will</b> (June 14, 1970 – October 27, 2006) was an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Activist" title="Activist">activist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Videographer" title="Videographer">videographer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a>. He was affiliated with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indymedia" title="Indymedia">Indymedia</a>. On October 27, 2006 during a <a href="/wiki/2006_Oaxaca_protests" title="2006 Oaxaca protests">labor dispute</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> city of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oaxaca,_Oaxaca" title="Oaxaca, Oaxaca">Oaxaca</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indymedia_bradwill_global_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-indymedia_bradwill_global-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nycimc_bradwill_name_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nycimc_bradwill_name-2">[2]</a></sup> Will was shot twice, possibly by government-aligned paramilitaries, resulting in his death.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Will&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Brad_Will_at_the_Dreamtime_Village_Corroboree.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="723" data-file-width="1024" height="155" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Brad_Will_at_the_Dreamtime_Village_Corroboree.jpg/220px-Brad_Will_at_the_Dreamtime_Village_Corroboree.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Brad_Will_at_the_Dreamtime_Village_Corroboree.jpg/330px-Brad_Will_at_the_Dreamtime_Village_Corroboree.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Brad_Will_at_the_Dreamtime_Village_Corroboree.jpg/440px-Brad_Will_at_the_Dreamtime_Village_Corroboree.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>Will was born in <a href="/wiki/Evanston,_Illinois" title="Evanston, Illinois">Evanston, Illinois</a>, and raised in the affluent community of <a href="/wiki/Kenilworth,_Illinois" title="Kenilworth, Illinois">Kenilworth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rs_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-rs-3">[3]</a></sup> He graduated from <a href="/wiki/New_Trier_High_School" title="New Trier High School">New Trier High School</a> in 1988, then attended <a href="/wiki/Allegheny_College" title="Allegheny College">Allegheny College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Meadville,_Pennsylvania" title="Meadville, Pennsylvania">Meadville, Pennsylvania</a>, where he earned a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">B.A.</a> in <a href="/wiki/English_studies" title="English studies">English</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rs_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-rs-3">[3]</a></sup> Beginning in the summer of 1991, he was a regular attendee at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jack_Kerouac_School_of_Disembodied_Poetics" title="Jack Kerouac School of Disembodied Poetics">Jack Kerouac School of Disembodied Poetics</a>, the summer writing program of <a href="/wiki/Naropa_University" title="Naropa University">Naropa University</a> and was a teaching assistant to <a href="/wiki/Peter_Lamborn_Wilson" title="Peter Lamborn Wilson">Peter Lamborn Wilson</a> (a.k.a. Hakim Bey). In 1995, after spending time at <a href="/wiki/Dreamtime_Village" title="Dreamtime Village">Dreamtime Village</a> in southwest <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> where he <a href="/wiki/Squatting" title="Squatting">squatted</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Lower_East_Side" title="Lower East Side">Lower East Side</a> before moving to <a href="/wiki/Williamsburg,_Brooklyn" title="Williamsburg, Brooklyn">Williamsburg, Brooklyn</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Will&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S.">U.S.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Will&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: U.S.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At Naropa, Will participated in a satirical <a href="/wiki/Performance_art" title="Performance art">performance art</a> piece designed to mock the Colorado <a href="/wiki/Christian_right" title="Christian right">socially conservative Christian</a> community and protest a proposed amendment to the Colorado constitution which sought to limit <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gay_rights" title="Gay rights">gay rights</a>. Will pretended to marry another man in a ceremony conducted by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Lamborn_Wilson" title="Peter Lamborn Wilson">Peter Lamborn Wilson</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Universal_Life_Church" title="Universal Life Church">Universal Life Church</a>-ordained minister. The mock ceremony included a procession of their wedding party in <a href="/wiki/Drag_(clothing)" title="Drag (clothing)">drag</a>, parading in front of a <a href="/wiki/Promise_Keepers" title="Promise Keepers">Promise Keepers</a> event in <a href="/wiki/Boulder,_Colorado" title="Boulder, Colorado">Boulder, Colorado</a>.</p>
<p>While in New York City, Will became involved in the squatters movement of the <a href="/wiki/Lower_East_Side" title="Lower East Side">Lower East Side</a>, and also became involved in what has become known as <a href="/wiki/Freeganism" title="Freeganism">freeganism</a>, an effort to live outside the mainstream economic grid by means that includes collecting and eating food discarded by stores and food manufacturers. He fought the removal of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Community_gardens" title="Community gardens">community gardens</a>, including the <a href="/wiki/Chico_Mendez_Mural_Garden" title="Chico Mendez Mural Garden">Chico Mendez Mural Garden</a> on the Lower East Side, named after Brazilian activist <a href="/wiki/Chico_Mendes" title="Chico Mendes">Chico Mendes</a>. Will was an active participant in protests across the country, usually for various <a href="/wiki/Social_justice" title="Social justice">social justice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a> causes, and was involved with <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmental</a> movements such as <a href="/wiki/Earth_First!" title="Earth First!">Earth First!</a> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fall_Creek_Tree_Village&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fall Creek Tree Village (page does not exist)">Fall Creek Tree Village</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Willamette_National_Forest" title="Willamette National Forest">Willamette National Forest</a> outside <a href="/wiki/Eugene,_Oregon" title="Eugene, Oregon">Eugene, Oregon</a>.</p>
<p>Will gained some notoriety for his efforts to prevent New York City from demolishing a squat on Fifth Street in the Lower East Side. When construction crews arrived at the building to begin taking it down, he stood atop the roof waving his arms. His efforts stalled the demolition, but the city eventually leveled the building, which housed a café, a meeting place and a performance space. Will later talked about the building in a program produced by <a href="/wiki/Paper_Tiger_Television" title="Paper Tiger Television">Paper Tiger Television</a> called "ABC Survives, Fifth Street Buried Alive".</p>
<dl>
<dd>"We were making a home out of a crumbling building. The interior of the building needed help, and we brought that building back to life. It was standing strong. And the only reason it was standing was because people were living in it. If we had let it go the way the city wanted it to go– they tore out the stairwell, they punched holes in the roof. The water– the rain was rotting that building from the inside out. We replaced the joists. We rebuilt the floors. We sheet rocked the walls and made the building alive. What did they do? They killed it. That building is over a hundred years old. It was standing strong."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DN_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-DN-4">[4]</a></sup></dd>
</dl>
<p>Will was a proponent of anti-corporate media and he hosted his own program on <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Steal_This_Radio&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Steal This Radio (page does not exist)">Steal This Radio</a>, a Lower East Side-based <a href="/wiki/Pirate_radio" title="Pirate radio">pirate radio</a> station.</p>
<p>In 1998, he and partner Hazel spoke at the Ecosaloon, a weekly activism forum held on Tuesday evenings at the Wetlands Preserve nightclub. Their presentation connected the struggle to defend the forests of the Pacific Northwest with the fight against the gentrification of Manhattan's Lower East Side. After helping to organize <a href="/wiki/Buy_Nothing_Day" title="Buy Nothing Day">Buy Nothing Day</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Reclaim_the_Streets" title="Reclaim the Streets">Reclaim the Streets</a> protest in <a href="/wiki/Times_Square" title="Times Square">Times Square</a>, Will traveled to <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WTO_Ministerial_Conference_of_1999_protest_activity" title="WTO Ministerial Conference of 1999 protest activity">1999 WTO protests</a>, and was a long-standing participant in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=New_York_City_Direct_Action_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="New York City Direct Action Network (page does not exist)">New York City Direct Action Network</a> (DAN) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=New_York_Independent_Media_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="New York Independent Media Center (page does not exist)">New York Independent Media Center</a> (IMC). In August 2001 he joined participants for a roving exploration of usable food in Tribeca garbage; they were accompanied by a TV crew from <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a>'s <i>Life 360</i>. In his latter years, Will began recording documentary videos, releasing them on the internet through the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indymedia" title="Indymedia">Indymedia</a> network of websites. During the summer of 2006, Will continued videotaping demonstrations, including a June 15 protest at the Mexican Consulate in response to a police incursion into a teacher's plantón (encampment). On June 29 he video-recorded and helped organize a protest against <a href="/wiki/Victoria%27s_Secret" title="Victoria's Secret">Victoria's Secret</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Mall" title="Manhattan Mall">Manhattan Mall</a> in response to the resources used to print the high volume of mail-order catalogs which the company mails out. He also filmed a street theater performance by the group A for Anarchy, organized in response to the release of the film V for Vendetta. At the time of his death, he was working on a documentary about <a href="/wiki/Folk_punk" title="Folk punk">folk punk</a> music.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Will&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, while visiting the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a> Will attended a protest in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a> against the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a> Summit. He later traveled through <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> while participating in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-globalization" title="Anti-globalization">anti-globalization</a>. He reached <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">Chiapas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> in that trip.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Will&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bradley_roland_will.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="332" data-file-width="500" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Bradley_roland_will.jpg/220px-Bradley_roland_will.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Bradley_roland_will.jpg/330px-Bradley_roland_will.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Bradley_roland_will.jpg/440px-Bradley_roland_will.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bradley_roland_will.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Will in Oaxaca, days before his death</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Traveling under a tourist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Visa_(document)" title="Visa (document)">visa</a>, Will arrived in Oaxaca in early October 2006 in order to document and film the teachers' strike.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On October 27, he was videotaping near a barricade erected by pro-strike protesters when he was shot twice. In the last few minutes of the video Will filmed before being shot, voices speaking in Spanish are heard demanding that filming be stopped and cameras be turned off. In the final seconds of the video a voice in Spanish reprimands Will for not having turned off the camera. In the middle of this statement a gunshot is heard, there is a scream, the camera pans down to the ground and the video ends.</p>
<p>Will died while being carried from the area in search of medical help. Two others, Esteban Zurita López and teacher Emilio Alonso Fabián, were also killed. Several others were injured. 26 protesters were ultimately murdered during this mobilization,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and the Mexican Supreme Court eventually found the governor of Oaxaca to be responsible.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (February 2016)">dubious</span></a> <span class="metadata">– <a href="/wiki/Talk:Brad_Will#Dubious" title="Talk:Brad Will">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup></p>
<p>During a news conference on October 29, 2006, Oaxaca <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Mart%C3%ADnez_(mayor)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Manuel Martínez (mayor) (page does not exist)">Manuel Martínez</a> said that four men, all local public officials, were being detained in connection with the shooting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bradwill_cpj_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-bradwill_cpj-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reaction">Reaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Will&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reaction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An <a href="/wiki/Open_letter" title="Open letter">open letter</a> written "to honor the memory" of Will and support "the Oaxacan people's efforts to establish a popular government that recognizes local traditions and values," was signed by numerous academics and activists, including <a href="/wiki/Noam_Chomsky" title="Noam Chomsky">Noam Chomsky</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Graeber" title="David Graeber">David Graeber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Naomi_Klein" title="Naomi Klein">Naomi Klein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Moore" title="Michael Moore">Michael Moore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arundhati_Roy" title="Arundhati Roy">Arundhati Roy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Starhawk" title="Starhawk">Starhawk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Howard_Zinn" title="Howard Zinn">Howard Zinn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_Police_mobilized">Federal Police mobilized</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Will&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Federal Police mobilized">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Later on the day of the shooting, the Oaxaca airport was closed to commercial flights as the Federal Police arrived to reestablish government control of the city. The army was also mobilized to provide intelligence support to the police, but not to engage directly with protesters. APPO representatives declared their intention to resist the government's armed response to the crisis. At the beginning of November, violent clashes continued between protesters and police.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The government claimed that the Federal Police that entered Oaxaca were unarmed.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demonstrations_at_Mexican_consulates">Demonstrations at Mexican consulates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Will&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Demonstrations at Mexican consulates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On October 30, more than 200 protesters convened outside the Mexican <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Consulate" title="Consulate">consulate</a> in New York City to deplore the killing of Will and others and to demand an end to the violence. The protest was organized by the grassroots group named in his honor, The Friends of Brad Will.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-consulate_protest1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-consulate_protest1-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-consulate_protest2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-consulate_protest2-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Committee_to_Protect_Journalists">Committee to Protect Journalists</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Will&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Committee to Protect Journalists">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Committee_to_Protect_Journalists" title="Committee to Protect Journalists">Committee to Protect Journalists</a> sent an open letter on October 30 to the Mexican <a href="/wiki/Attorney_general" title="Attorney general">attorney general</a> calling on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_government" title="Mexican government">Mexican government</a> to launch an investigation into Will's death. The statement said that <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=David_Vega_Vera&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="David Vega Vera (page does not exist)">David Vega Vera</a>, special independent prosecutor, had begun gathering information related to the case.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cpj_bwill_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-cpj_bwill-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commemoration_and_memorial">Commemoration and memorial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brad_Will&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Commemoration and memorial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Brad_Will-Poster_at_the_NYC_Halloween_Parade.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2048" data-file-width="2356" height="191" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Brad_Will-Poster_at_the_NYC_Halloween_Parade.jpg/220px-Brad_Will-Poster_at_the_NYC_Halloween_Parade.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Brad_Will-Poster_at_the_NYC_Halloween_Parade.jpg/330px-Brad_Will-Poster_at_the_NYC_Halloween_Parade.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Brad_Will-Poster_at_the_NYC_Halloween_Parade.jpg/440px-Brad_Will-Poster_at_the_NYC_Halloween_Parade.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Brad_Will-Poster_at_the_NYC_Halloween_Parade.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Poster of Will at the 2006 New York City Halloween Parade</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>On November 11 and 12, 2006, Friends of Brad Will organized a gathering in New York City to commemorate Will's life. The event included a memorial service at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Mark%27s_Church_in-the-Bowery" title="St Mark's Church in-the-Bowery">St Mark's Church in-the-Bowery</a> attended by 250 people, as well as speeches and concerts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raucous_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-raucous-13">[13]</a></sup> Outside the church was an array of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freegan" title="Freegan">freegan</a> food as well as a pile of Will's personal possessions, from which attendees were urged to take.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raucous_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-raucous-13">[13]</a></sup> It was followed by a procession through the <a href="/wiki/East_Village,_Manhattan" title="East Village, Manhattan">East Village</a> described by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Villager_(Manhattan)" title="The Villager (Manhattan)">The Villager</a></i> as "jubilant and rowdy", which culminated in marchers breaking into the former CHARAS/El Bohio - inside which they cavorted, scrawled <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti</a>, twirled fire <a href="/wiki/Bolas" title="Bolas">bolas</a> and cycled - for about 20 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-raucous_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-raucous-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
