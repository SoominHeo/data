<p><b>Bracknell</b> is a town and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bracknell_Forest" title="Bracknell Forest">Borough of Bracknell Forest</a> in Berkshire, England. It lies 11 miles (18 km) to the east of <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Berkshire" title="Reading, Berkshire">Reading</a>, 9 miles (14 km) south of <a href="/wiki/Maidenhead" title="Maidenhead">Maidenhead</a>, 10 miles (16 km) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Berkshire" title="Windsor, Berkshire">Windsor</a>, 16 miles (26 km) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Guildford" title="Guildford">Guildford</a> and 34 miles (55 km) west of central London. The town has a population of 77,256 and is twinned with the German city of <a href="/wiki/Leverkusen" title="Leverkusen">Leverkusen</a>.</p>
<p>The town is surrounded, on the east and south, by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swinley_Woods" title="Swinley Woods">Swinley Woods</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crowthorne" title="Crowthorne">Crowthorne</a> Woods. The urban area has absorbed parts of many local outlying areas including <a href="/wiki/Warfield" title="Warfield">Warfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Winkfield" title="Winkfield">Winkfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Binfield" title="Binfield">Binfield</a>, and is itself, along with Binfield, a component of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_London_Built-up_Area" title="Greater London Built-up Area">Greater London Urban Area</a> as defined by the ONS.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bracknell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Holy_Trinity,_Bracknell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1526966.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="480" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Holy_Trinity%2C_Bracknell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1526966.jpg/220px-Holy_Trinity%2C_Bracknell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1526966.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Holy_Trinity%2C_Bracknell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1526966.jpg/330px-Holy_Trinity%2C_Bracknell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1526966.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Holy_Trinity%2C_Bracknell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1526966.jpg/440px-Holy_Trinity%2C_Bracknell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1526966.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>The name Bracknell is first recorded in a Winkfield Boundary Charter of AD 942 as <i>Braccan heal</i>, and may mean "Nook of land belonging to a man called Bracca", from the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>Braccan</i> (genitive singular of a personal name) + <i>heal, healh</i> (a corner, nook or secret place).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> An early form of the town's name, <i>Brakenhale</i>, still survives as <a href="/wiki/Brakenhale_Academy" title="Brakenhale Academy">the name of one of its schools</a>. The town covers all of the old village of <a href="/wiki/Easthampstead" title="Easthampstead">Easthampstead</a> (though not all of the old parish) and the hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Harmans_Water" title="Harmans Water">Ramslade</a>.</p>
<p>There is a <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> <a href="/wiki/Round_barrow" title="Round barrow">round barrow</a> at Bill Hill. <a href="/wiki/Easthampstead_Park" title="Easthampstead Park">Easthampstead Park</a> was a favoured royal hunting lodge in Windsor Forest and <a href="/wiki/Catherine_of_Aragon" title="Catherine of Aragon">Catherine of Aragon</a> was banished there until her divorce was finalised.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbhep_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rbhep-2">[2]</a></sup> It was later the home of the <a href="/wiki/William_Trumbull" title="William Trumbull">Trumbulls</a> who were patrons of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Pope" title="Alexander Pope">Alexander Pope</a> from Binfield.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbhep_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-rbhep-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>To the north-east of the town is to be found the Quelm Stone, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standing_stone" title="Standing stone">standing stone</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and to the south-west, just over the border in <a href="/wiki/Crowthorne" title="Crowthorne">Crowthorne</a>, is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caesar%27s_Camp,_Bracknell" title="Caesar's Camp, Bracknell">Caesar's Camp</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a> <a href="/wiki/Hillfort" title="Hillfort">hill fort</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbhcc_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-rbhcc-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>One of the oldest buildings in the town is the 'Old Manor' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_house" title="Public house">public house</a>, a 17th-century brick <a href="/wiki/Manor_house" title="Manor house">manor house</a> featuring a number of <a href="/wiki/Priest_hole" title="Priest hole">priest holes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbhb_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-rbhb-5">[5]</a></sup> Next door once stood the 'Hind's Head' coaching inn, where it is said <a href="/wiki/Dick_Turpin" title="Dick Turpin">Dick Turpin</a> used to drink.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbhb_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-rbhb-5">[5]</a></sup> It is believed that there were once underground tunnels between the two, along which the famous <a href="/wiki/Highwayman" title="Highwayman">highwayman</a> could escape from the authorities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbhb_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-rbhb-5">[5]</a></sup> Other surviving old pubs are the Red Lion and the Bull, all timber-framed and dating from before the 18th century.</p>
<p>The oldest place of worship in the town is the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England_parish_church" title="Church of England parish church">parish church</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Michael_and_St_Mary_Magdalene" title="St Michael and St Mary Magdalene">St Michael and St Mary Magdalene</a> in <a href="/wiki/Easthampstead" title="Easthampstead">Easthampstead</a>. There has been a church there since <a href="/wiki/Saxons" title="Saxons">Saxon times</a>, although the present building dates from the mid 19th century, except for the lower portions of the Tudor tower.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbhec_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-rbhec-6">[6]</a></sup> Holy Trinity Church near the town centre was built in 1851.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_town">New town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bracknell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: New town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bracknell was designated a <a href="/wiki/New_towns_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="New towns in the United Kingdom">new town</a> in 1949,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> in the aftermath of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>. The site was originally a village <i>cum</i> small town in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> of <a href="/wiki/Warfield" title="Warfield">Warfield</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Easthampstead_Rural_District" title="Easthampstead Rural District">Easthampstead Rural District</a>. Very little of the original Bracknell is left. The location was preferred to <a href="/wiki/White_Waltham" title="White Waltham">White Waltham</a>, which was also considered, because the Bracknell site avoided encroaching on good quality agricultural land. It also had the additional advantage of being on a railway line.</p>
<p>The new town was planned for 25,000 people; it was intended to occupy over 1,000 hectares (about 6 square miles) of land in and around 'Old Bracknell' in the area now occupied by Priestwood, Easthampstead, Bullbrook and Harmans Water. The existing town centre and industrial areas were to be retained with new industry brought in to provide jobs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> However, the town has since expanded far beyond its intended size into farmland to the south, and major expansion is now, as of 2008, under way (<a href="/wiki/Jennett%27s_Park" title="Jennett's Park">Jennett's Park</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>) to the west of the town at Peacock Farm and <a href="/wiki/Harmans_Water#The_Parks" title="Harmans Water">The Parks</a> on the site of the former RAF Staff College.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the heart of most Bracknell neighbourhoods is a church, a small parade of shops, a primary school, a community centre and a pub. The neighbourhoods varied in population from 3,000 to 9,000. The plans included pedestrianisation, the construction of a ring road, and segregation of industrial areas from residential areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>A slightly confusing feature of some of the estates is that streets only have names, not titles – in <a href="/wiki/Birch_Hill" title="Birch Hill">Birch Hill</a>, Crown Wood, Great Hollands and others there is no 'Road', '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Street_name" title="Street name">Avenue</a>', 'Street', just 'Frobisher', 'Jameston', 'Juniper', 'Jevington'. The residential streets are, however, named in alphabetical order in <a href="/wiki/Great_Hollands" title="Great Hollands">Great Hollands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wildridings" title="Wildridings">Wildridings</a>, with As, through Ds, such as Donnybrook, in <a href="/wiki/Hanworth,_Bracknell" title="Hanworth, Bracknell">Hanworth</a>, Js, such as 'Jameston', 'Juniper' and 'Jevington' in <a href="/wiki/Birch_Hill" title="Birch Hill">Birch Hill</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regeneration">Regeneration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bracknell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Regeneration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Because of Bracknell's age, it was decided that it should undergo renovation. Designs and plans were submitted and rejected first time round. The council went for a second attempt and were accepted, work was due to commence early in 2008 but due to the global credit crises plans have been postponed. The cost is estimated at around £750 million. It is hoped that the regeneration will provide brand new services, a completely redeveloped town centre, 1,000 new homes and new police and bus stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<p>A major refurbishment of the town centre is taking place with the Borough Council working in partnership with the Bracknell Regeneration Limited Partnership a registered company in Jersey, Channel Islands (<a href="/wiki/Legal_%26_General" title="Legal &amp; General">Legal &amp; General</a> and <a href="/wiki/Schroders" title="Schroders">Schroders</a>) to regenerate the town centre with new shops and facilities.</p>
<p>The first stage of the redevelopment began with the opening of a new <a href="/wiki/Waitrose" title="Waitrose">Waitrose</a> store in December 2011. By June 2013 shops in the northern part of the town in Broadway and Crossway had been vacated. Demolition of this area then began in September 2013, and was completed in December 2013. Construction of new shops, restaurants, and a Cineworld cinema began in April 2015, with a completion date of September 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 4 September 2015 it was announced that the new development would be known as 'The Lexicon' <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bracknell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 2011 Census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>94% of Bracknell residents can speak English. The second language being Nepalese, at 0.90%, followed by Polish at 0.70%, Tagalog/Filipino at 0.30% and French and Spanish, both at 0.30%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>61% of residents identify themselves as Christian. The second most common belief is 'none', with 35% of residents choosing this in the census, in third place is Hinduism at 1.61%, followed by Islam at 1.13% and Buddhism at 0.73%.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>

<p>The town is home to companies such as <a href="/wiki/Panasonic" title="Panasonic">Panasonic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fujitsu" title="Fujitsu">Fujitsu</a> (formerly <a href="/wiki/International_Computers_Limited" title="International Computers Limited">ICL</a>), <a href="/wiki/Dell" title="Dell">Dell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brocade_Communications_Systems" title="Brocade Communications Systems">Brocade Communications Systems</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siemens_AG" title="Siemens AG">Siemens</a> (originally <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nixdorf_Computer_AG" title="Nixdorf Computer AG">Nixdorf</a>), <a href="/wiki/Riverbed_Technology" title="Riverbed Technology">Riverbed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honeywell" title="Honeywell">Honeywell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cable_%26_Wireless_Communications" title="Cable &amp; Wireless Communications">Cable and Wireless</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intercall" title="Intercall">Intercall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Broadcom" title="Broadcom">Broadcom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Avnet" title="Avnet">Avnet Technology Solutions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Novell" title="Novell">Novell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vodafone" title="Vodafone">Vodafone</a> and WorkForce Software. The Royal Warrant Holders as chimney sweepers are based in Neuman Crescent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Southern Industrial Area houses the head office of <a href="/wiki/Waitrose" title="Waitrose">Waitrose</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The 70-acre (280,000 m<sup>2</sup>) site which houses the Waitrose head office also houses the central distribution centre. Waitrose has operated from the town since the 1970s.</p>
<p>Manufacturing industry has largely disappeared since the 1980s. Former significant sites included Clifford's Dairy in Downshire Way and <a href="/wiki/British_Aerospace" title="British Aerospace">British Aerospace</a> (originally <a href="/wiki/Sperry_Corporation" title="Sperry Corporation">Sperry Gyroscope</a>) now occupied by Arlington Square, a 22-acre (8ha) business park<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> of which the first stage was completed in 1995. The Thomas Lawrence brickworks on the north side of the town was famous for 'red rubber' bricks to be found in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Westminster_Cathedral" title="Westminster Cathedral">Westminster Cathedral</a>, and in restoration work at <a href="/wiki/10_Downing_Street" title="10 Downing Street">10 Downing Street</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hampton_Court_Palace" title="Hampton Court Palace">Hampton Court Palace</a>.</p>
<p>In the town centre was the 12-storey Winchester House, formerly owned by <a href="/wiki/3M" title="3M">3M</a> who moved to new premises in <a href="/wiki/Farley_Wood" title="Farley Wood">Farley Wood</a> on the town's northern edge in 2004. The building was demolished and is to be replaced with blocks of flats<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The town was also the home of <a href="/wiki/Racal" title="Racal">Racal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ferranti" title="Ferranti">Ferranti Computer Systems Ltd</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Met_Office" title="Met Office">Met Office</a> maintained a large presence in the town until 2003, when it relocated to <a href="/wiki/Exeter" title="Exeter">Exeter</a> in Devon; however, the junction of the A329 and A3095 is still named the "Met Office Roundabout". Many businesses are located on the town's three industrial areas.</p>
<p>Easthampstead Park in the southern suburb of Easthampstead is now a conference centre owned by Bracknell Forest Borough Council.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government">Local government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bracknell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Local government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bracknell was made a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in its own right in 1955. Under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, the entire Easthampstead Rural District became the Bracknell District on 1 April 1974. In 1988, it was granted <a href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">borough</a> status, and it changed its name to Bracknell Forest. When Berkshire <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_Council" title="County Council">County Council</a> was abolished on 1 April 1998 (and the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_and_non-metropolitan_counties_of_England" title="Metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties of England">non-metropolitan county</a> was reclassified as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceremonial_county" title="Ceremonial county">ceremonial county</a>), Bracknell Forest became one of the six <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authorities</a> which together make up Berkshire.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bracknell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town covers areas previously in the parishes of <a href="/wiki/Easthampstead" title="Easthampstead">Easthampstead</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warfield" title="Warfield">Warfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Binfield" title="Binfield">Binfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Winkfield" title="Winkfield">Winkfield</a>. The town's centre lies just north of the <a href="/wiki/Bracknell_railway_station" title="Bracknell railway station">railway station</a> with completely pedestrianised and much undercover shopping around Princess Square, Charles Square and the Broadway. There are 'out-of-town' shops, a multiscreen cinema and ten pin bowling complex at the Peel Centre. Just to the west are the Western and Southern industrial estates, either side of the railway line. There are many residential suburbs (see settlement table below) of varying dates, the oldest being <a href="/wiki/Priestwood" title="Priestwood">Priestwood</a> and, of course, <a href="/wiki/Easthampstead" title="Easthampstead">Easthampstead</a> village.</p>
<p>The former <a href="/wiki/RAF_Staff_College,_Bracknell" title="RAF Staff College, Bracknell">RAF Staff College</a> buildings in <a href="/wiki/Harmans_Water" title="Harmans Water">Harmans Water</a>, now closed, was part of the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Services_Command_and_Staff_College" title="Joint Services Command and Staff College">Joint Services Command and Staff College</a>. This site is now, as of 2008, being redeveloped for housing by Wimpey, with an estimated 730 houses on the college's former site. The south-western corner of the town remains rural around Easthampstead Park and the wooded Yew Tree Corner. However, a new housing development called <a href="/wiki/Jennett%27s_Park" title="Jennett's Park">Jennett's Park</a> is currently being built (from 2007) at Peacock Farm and on part of what was historically the grounds of Easthampstead Park. There are large ponds at <a href="/wiki/Farley_Wood" title="Farley Wood">Farley Wood</a> and the Easthampstead Mill Pond between <a href="/wiki/Great_Hollands" title="Great Hollands">Great Hollands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wildridings" title="Wildridings">Wildridings</a>, and two lakes at <a href="/wiki/South_Hill_Park" title="South Hill Park">South Hill Park</a>. The <i>Bull Brook</i> emerges above ground just within the bounds of the suburb of <a href="/wiki/Bullbrook" title="Bullbrook">Bullbrook</a>.</p>

<p>In the south of the town is <a href="/wiki/South_Hill_Park" title="South Hill Park">South Hill Park</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Mansion" title="Mansion">mansion</a> dating from 1760, although much rebuilt, that now houses a large <a href="/wiki/Arts_centre" title="Arts centre">arts centre</a>. The Wilde Theatre was opened in 1984, named after <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Wilde" title="Oscar Wilde">Oscar Wilde</a> who created the character 'Lady Bracknell' in his play <i><a href="/wiki/The_Importance_of_Being_Earnest" title="The Importance of Being Earnest">The Importance of Being Earnest</a></i>. South Hill Park has been home to a number of major music festivals over the years:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>1975 – 1990s <a href="/wiki/Bracknell_Jazz_Festival" title="Bracknell Jazz Festival">Bracknell Jazz Festival</a></li>
<li>1970s – 1980s Bracknell Folk Festival ("The Handsome Mouldiwarp Festival")</li>
<li>1988 – <a href="/wiki/World_of_Music,_Arts_and_Dance" title="World of Music, Arts and Dance">Womad</a> Festival</li>
<li>1980s – 1990s – Bracknell Music Festival / South Hill Park Festival</li>
<li>2000s – 2013 – Big Day Out festival, a free, annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Music" title="World Music">World Music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_music" title="Acoustic music">acoustic</a>/<a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a> festival</li>
</ul>
<p>Bracknell has been used in the filming of many TV shows and films, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Philosopher%27s_Stone_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (film)">Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Martins_Heron" title="Martins Heron">Martins Heron</a>) and <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Bandits" title="Time Bandits">Time Bandits</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Birch_Hill" title="Birch Hill">Birch Hill</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Bracknell is given the name 'Laxton' in the TV detective series <a href="/wiki/Pie_in_the_Sky_(TV_series)" title="Pie in the Sky (TV series)">Pie in the Sky</a> and Waterside Park was used for the exterior of the police HQ in the same series. Bracknell has also featured in the 1991 <a href="/wiki/Roger_Daltrey" title="Roger Daltrey">Roger Daltrey</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Buddy%27s_Song_(film)" title="Buddy's Song (film)">Buddy's Song</a></i>.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Offence" title="The Offence">The Offence</a></i> (1972), a psychological thriller with <a href="/wiki/Sean_Connery" title="Sean Connery">Sean Connery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ian_Bannen" title="Ian Bannen">Ian Bannen</a>, was filmed in Bracknell. There are scenes in the town centre, on Broadway, Charles Square and Market Street. The flat for Connery's character was filmed at the <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed</a> Point Royal, and the bulk of the outdoor scenes were taken around Wild Ridings, specifically Arncliffe, Crossfell, Mill Pond and Mill Lane.</p>
<p>The wages snatch scene in <i><a href="/wiki/Villain_(1971_film)" title="Villain (1971 film)">Villain</a></i> (1971), a gangster film with <a href="/wiki/Richard_Burton" title="Richard Burton">Richard Burton</a>, was filmed in Ellesfield Avenue on the Southern Industrial Estate outside the former Clark Eaton glass factory,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> with the ICL tower block (under construction at the time) visible in the background; after the robbery the gang make their getaway along Peacock Lane nearby and hijack a car at the junction with the footpath from Tarmans Copse (now Osprey Avenue on the <a href="/wiki/Jennett%27s_Park" title="Jennett's Park">Jennett's Park</a> estate).</p>
<p>Bracknell is featured in the PlayStation 3 title <i>Resistance: Fall of Man</i> set in 1951, as the location at which power conduits travel deep underground <a href="/wiki/South_East_England" title="South East England">South East England</a> to power the Chimeran fortresses. It also featured in the sequel <i>Resistance:2</i> with a similar role.</p>
<p>The BBC show <a href="/wiki/The_Wrong_Mans" title="The Wrong Mans">The Wrong Mans</a> is set almost entirely in Bracknell.</p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Story_of_Tracy_Beaker_(TV_series)" title="The Story of Tracy Beaker (TV series)">Tracy Beaker</a></i> actress <a href="/wiki/Dani_Harmer" title="Dani Harmer">Dani Harmer</a> was brought up in Bracknell.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bracknell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town of Bracknell has two railway stations, <a href="/wiki/Bracknell_railway_station" title="Bracknell railway station">Bracknell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martins_Heron_railway_station" title="Martins Heron railway station">Martins Heron</a>, both of which are on the <a href="/wiki/Waterloo_to_Reading_Line" title="Waterloo to Reading Line">Waterloo to Reading Line</a>, built by the <a href="/wiki/London_and_South_Western_Railway" title="London and South Western Railway">London and South Western Railway</a> and now operated by <a href="/wiki/South_West_Trains" title="South West Trains">South West Trains</a>. Bracknell is a commuter centre with its residents travelling in both directions (westwards to <a href="/wiki/Reading_railway_station" title="Reading railway station">Reading</a> and eastwards to <a href="/wiki/London_Waterloo_station" title="London Waterloo station">London Waterloo</a>).</p>
<p>The town has good road links and is situated at the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A329(M)" title="A329(M)">A329(M)</a>, midway between Junction 3 of the <a href="/wiki/M3_motorway_(Great_Britain)" title="M3 motorway (Great Britain)">M3</a> and Junction 10 of the <a href="/wiki/M4_motorway" title="M4 motorway">M4 motorways</a>. A proposed motorway link between the M3 and the M4 – to be called the <a href="/wiki/M31_motorway" title="M31 motorway">M31</a> – would have passed to the west of the town centre, but only the section that is now the A329(M) and the A3290 was built.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bracknell_bus_services">Bracknell bus services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bracknell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Bracknell bus services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bracknell bus station serves the town of Bracknell. The bus station is on The Ring in the Town Centre across the road from <a href="/wiki/Bracknell_railway_station" title="Bracknell railway station">Bracknell railway station</a>. The bus station consists of three long shelters each with three stands.</p>
<p>Bus services go from Bracknell as far afield as <a href="/wiki/Crowthorne" title="Crowthorne">Crowthorne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Camberley" title="Camberley">Camberley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wokingham" title="Wokingham">Wokingham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reading,_Berkshire" title="Reading, Berkshire">Reading</a>, <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Berkshire" title="Windsor, Berkshire">Windsor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slough" title="Slough">Slough</a>. Local bus services are provided by <a href="/wiki/Courtney_Buses" title="Courtney Buses">Courtney Buses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reading_Buses" title="Reading Buses">Reading Buses</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_South" title="Stagecoach South">Stagecoach</a>. There are coach services provided by <a href="/wiki/Green_Line_Coaches" title="Green Line Coaches">Green Line</a> (towards London Victoria) and <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express</a>. Bus services to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> are operated by <a href="/wiki/Green_Line_Coaches" title="Green Line Coaches">Green Line</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport_and_leisure">Sport and leisure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bracknell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sport and leisure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p><a href="/wiki/Bracknell_Town_F.C." title="Bracknell Town F.C.">Bracknell Town F.C.</a> are members of the <a href="/wiki/Hellenic_Football_League" title="Hellenic Football League">Hellenic Football League</a> Premier Division, and play their home matches at Larges Lane. The <a href="/wiki/Bracknell_Bees" title="Bracknell Bees">Bracknell Bees Ice Hockey Club</a> are former national champions, who currently play in the English Premier League. The Bracknell Blazers are the 2009 <a href="/wiki/British_Baseball_Federation" title="British Baseball Federation">BBF National League</a> champions. The town is also represented by teams playing rugby, <a href="/wiki/Bracknell_RFC" title="Bracknell RFC">Bracknell RFC</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> hockey<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and cricket.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>The town has a large leisure centre, which includes swimming and athletics facilities, whilst there is also the Coral Reef Water Park, the Downshire Golf Complex, the Bracknell Lawn Tennis Club,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> and Esporta, the Royal County of Berkshire Club. The <a href="/wiki/John_Nike" title="John Nike">John Nike</a> Leisuresport Complex houses a dry ski slope and an ice rink, and there are 2,600 acres (11 km<sup>2</sup>) of Crown Estate woodland at the Look Out Discovery Centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> A number of organisations are active in the area. These consist of an Army Cadet Force detachment(7 Platoon Bracknell)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and an Air Training Corps (2211 Squadron) which are both located on the same site, Saint Johns Ambulance Cadets, other youth groups that involve a younger crowd including several troops of Scouts and the Bracknell Forest Lions Club, which was formed in 1968 to help those in need.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bracknell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The area has various schools including St Joseph's Catholic Primary School, <a href="/wiki/Brakenhale_Academy" title="Brakenhale Academy">Brakenhale Academy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Easthampstead_Park_School" title="Easthampstead Park School">Easthampstead Park School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garth_Hill_College" title="Garth Hill College">Garth Hill College</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ranelagh_Church_of_England_School" title="Ranelagh Church of England School">Ranelagh Church of England School</a>. <a href="/wiki/Bracknell_and_Wokingham_College" title="Bracknell and Wokingham College">Bracknell and Wokingham College</a> of further education is also based in the area.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Silwood_Park" title="Silwood Park">Silwood Park</a> campus of <a href="/wiki/Imperial_College_London" title="Imperial College London">Imperial College London</a> is 5 miles (8.0 km) to the east of Bracknell town centre. The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Reading" title="University of Reading">University of Reading</a> is 8 miles (13 km) to the northwest, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Holloway_College" title="Royal Holloway College">Royal Holloway College</a> 8 miles (13 km) to the east.</p>
