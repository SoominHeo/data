<p><b>Brandon J. Laird</b> (born September 11, 1987) is an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Third_baseman" title="Third baseman">third baseman</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido_Nippon-Ham_Fighters" title="Hokkaido Nippon-Ham Fighters">Hokkaido Nippon-Ham Fighters</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a> (NPB). Previously, he played for the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="High_school_and_college_career">High school and college career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brandon_Laird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: High school and college career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Laird attended <a href="/wiki/La_Quinta_High_School_(Westminster,_California)" title="La Quinta High School (Westminster, California)">La Quinta High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Westminster,_California" title="Westminster, California">Westminster, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was drafted by the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a> in the 27th round of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2005 Major League Baseball draft">2005 Major League Baseball draft</a>. He did not sign,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> opting to attend <a href="/wiki/Cypress_College" title="Cypress College">Cypress College</a>, where he played <a href="/wiki/College_baseball" title="College baseball">college baseball</a> for the Cypress Chargers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brandon_Laird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_York_Yankees">New York Yankees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brandon_Laird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: New York Yankees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> selected Laird in the 27th round of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2007 Major League Baseball draft">2007 Major League Baseball draft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He signed with the Yankees.</p>
<p>On August 2, 2010, Laird was promoted from the <a href="/wiki/Trenton_Thunder" title="Trenton Thunder">Trenton Thunder</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_AA" title="Class AA">Class AA</a> <a href="/wiki/Eastern_League_(baseball)" title="Eastern League (baseball)">Eastern League</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scranton/Wilkes-Barre_Yankees" title="Scranton/Wilkes-Barre Yankees">Scranton/Wilkes-Barre Yankees</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_AAA" title="Class AAA">Class AAA</a> <a href="/wiki/International_League" title="International League">International League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Laird was named both the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_League_Most_Valuable_Player_Award" title="Eastern League Most Valuable Player Award">Most Valuable Player</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eastern_League_Rookie_of_the_Year_Award" title="Eastern League Rookie of the Year Award">Rookie of the Year</a> of the Eastern League in 2010, following a breakout season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Following the 2010 season, he was added to the Yankees' 40 man roster in order to protect him from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rule_5_Draft" title="Rule 5 Draft">Rule 5 Draft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>Laird was promoted to the majors for the first time on July 18, 2011, when <a href="/wiki/Ramiro_Pe%C3%B1a" title="Ramiro Peña">Ramiro Peña</a> was placed on the <a href="/wiki/Disabled_list" title="Disabled list">disabled list</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On July 22, at <a href="/wiki/Yankee_Stadium" title="Yankee Stadium">Yankee Stadium</a>, Laird made his major league debut, and got his first major league hit and RBI against <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/Craig_Breslow" title="Craig Breslow">Craig Breslow</a> on a single to center field. He scored his first run earlier in the game, scoring on a single by <a href="/wiki/Nick_Swisher" title="Nick Swisher">Nick Swisher</a> after working a walk off of A's pitcher <a href="/wiki/Joey_Devine" title="Joey Devine">Joey Devine</a>. The Yankees <a href="/wiki/Designated_for_assignment" title="Designated for assignment">designated</a> Laird for assignment on August 27, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Houston_Astros">Houston Astros</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brandon_Laird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Houston Astros">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Laird was claimed by the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> on September 1, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Laird started the 2013 season with the Triple-A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_RedHawks" title="Oklahoma City RedHawks">Oklahoma City RedHawks</a>. The Astros promoted Laird to the big leagues on April 18, 2013, after <a href="/wiki/Brett_Wallace" title="Brett Wallace">Brett Wallace</a> was optioned to Oklahoma City. Laird had been hitting .353 in 12 games to earn his promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He was outrighted off the roster on October 2, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Laird ended his 2013 season batting .169.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Washington_Nationals">Washington Nationals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brandon_Laird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Washington Nationals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Laird signed a minor league deal with the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a> on November 23, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On March 15, 2014, Laird was traded to the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals" title="Washington Nationals">Washington Nationals</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Player_to_be_named_later" title="Player to be named later">player to be named later</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He played for the <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_Chiefs" title="Syracuse Chiefs">Syracuse Chiefs</a> of the International League, and was named the league's player of the week for the week of June 16‐22.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hokkaido_Nippon_Ham_Fighters">Hokkaido Nippon Ham Fighters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brandon_Laird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hokkaido Nippon Ham Fighters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2014, Laird signed with the <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido_Nippon-Ham_Fighters" title="Hokkaido Nippon-Ham Fighters">Hokkaido Nippon-Ham Fighters</a> of Japan's <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Professional_Baseball" title="Nippon Professional Baseball">Nippon Professional Baseball</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Laird struggled to start the 2015 season with Hokkaido, batting under .200 into June, but finished his first season with 34 home runs and a batting average of .231.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> In 2016, Laird led the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_League" title="Pacific League">Pacific League</a> with 39 home runs. He hit the game-winning home run in Game 4 of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Japan_Series" title="2016 Japan Series">2016 Japan Series</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/Grand_slam_(baseball)" title="Grand slam (baseball)">grand slam</a> in Game 6. Laird won the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Series_Most_Valuable_Player_Award" title="Japan Series Most Valuable Player Award">Japan Series Most Valuable Player Award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal">Personal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brandon_Laird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brandon's brother, <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Laird" title="Gerald Laird">Gerald</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Catcher" title="Catcher">catcher</a> who most recently played in the Majors in 2015 for the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a> and currently plays for the <a href="/wiki/Toros_de_Tijuana" title="Toros de Tijuana">Toros de Tijuana</a> of the Mexican League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
