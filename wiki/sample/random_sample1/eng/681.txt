<p>Year <b>736</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCCXXXVI</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Leap year starting on Sunday">leap year starting on Sunday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 736 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=736&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=736&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=736&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a>, Merovingian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_the_palace" title="Mayor of the palace">mayor of the palace</a>, forms local <a href="/wiki/Military_alliance" title="Military alliance">alliances</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Burgundy" title="Kingdom of Burgundy">Burgundians</a>, and imposes <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Frankish</a> domination on <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">Provence</a>. He defeats <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> forces at <a href="/wiki/Sernhac" title="Sernhac">Sernhac</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beaucaire,_Gard" title="Beaucaire, Gard">Beaucaire</a> in <a href="/wiki/Septimania" title="Septimania">Septimania</a> (Southern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_N%C3%AEmes" title="Battle of Nîmes">Battle of Nîmes</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Franks</a> under Charles Martel fail to capture <a href="/wiki/Narbonne" title="Narbonne">Narbonne</a> but devastate most of the other settlements, including <a href="/wiki/N%C3%AEmes" title="Nîmes">Nîmes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agde" title="Agde">Agde</a>, <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A9ziers" title="Béziers">Béziers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Villeneuve-l%C3%A8s-Maguelone" title="Villeneuve-lès-Maguelone">Maguelonne</a>, which he views as potential strongholds of the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyads</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=736&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/%C3%86thelbald_of_Mercia" title="Æthelbald of Mercia">Æthelbald of Mercia</a> is described in the <i><a href="/wiki/Ismere_Diploma" title="Ismere Diploma">Ismere Diploma</a></i> as ruler (<i><a href="/wiki/Bretwalda" title="Bretwalda">bretwalda</a></i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Mercia" title="Mercia">Mercians</a>, and all the provinces in southern <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. He is also named "Rex Britanniae" (king of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fletcher_98_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fletcher_98-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/%C3%93engus_I" title="Óengus I">Óengus I</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Picts" title="Picts">Picts</a> invades the neighbouring kingdom of <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1l_Riata" title="Dál Riata">Dál Riata</a>, which is subjugated. He takes the fortress of <a href="/wiki/Dunadd" title="Dunadd">Dunadd</a>, and establishes his rule in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> for over two decades.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=736&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/R%C5%8Dben" title="Rōben">Rōben</a>, scholar-<a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhist</a> monk, invites Shinshō to give lectures on the <i><a href="/wiki/Avatamsaka_Sutra" title="Avatamsaka Sutra">Avatamsaka Sutra</a></i> at Kinshōsen-ji (later <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Ddai-ji" title="Tōdai-ji">Tōdai-ji</a>); this event is considered to be the roots of the <a href="/wiki/Kegon" title="Kegon">Kegon</a> school of <a href="/wiki/Buddhism_in_Japan" title="Buddhism in Japan">Buddhism</a> founded in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=736&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mesoamerica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>A diplomatic team from <a href="/wiki/Calakmul" title="Calakmul">Calakmul</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Wamaw_K%27awiil" title="Wamaw K'awiil">Wamaw K'awiil</a>, meets with <a href="/wiki/Quirigu%C3%A1" title="Quiriguá">Quiriguá</a> leader <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%E2%80%99ak_Tiliw_Chan_Yopaa" title="K’ak Tiliw Chan Yopaa">K’ak Tiliw Chan Yopaa</a>, in an attempt to negotiate an end to the city's rebellion during the <a href="/wiki/Third_Tikal-Calakmul_War" title="Third Tikal-Calakmul War">Third Tikal-Calakmul War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yik%27in_Chan_K%27awiil" title="Yik'in Chan K'awiil">Yik'in Chan K'awiil</a>, ruler (<i><a href="/wiki/Ajaw" title="Ajaw">ajaw</a></i>) of the leading <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Maya</a> city state of <a href="/wiki/Tikal" title="Tikal">Tikal</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a>), conquers rival <a href="/wiki/Calakmul" title="Calakmul">Calakmul</a>, within the northern <a href="/wiki/Pet%C3%A9n_Basin" title="Petén Basin">Petén</a> region of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_region" title="Yucatán region">Yucatán region</a> (Southern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>)..</li>
</ul>
