<p>The <b>Brampton Beast</b> is a professional <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> team based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brampton,_Ontario" title="Brampton, Ontario">Brampton, Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. An affiliate of the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Canadiens" title="Montreal Canadiens">Montreal Canadiens</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">American Hockey League</a>'s <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s_IceCaps" title="St. John's IceCaps">St. John's IceCaps</a>, the team originally played in the <a href="/wiki/Central_Hockey_League" title="Central Hockey League">Central Hockey League</a> for one year during the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_CHL_season" title="2013–14 CHL season">2013–14 season</a> prior to the league's folding. The Beast play their home games at the <a href="/wiki/Powerade_Centre" title="Powerade Centre">Powerade Centre</a> on Kennedy Road. The team is the only active <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> team in the <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a> since the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_ECHL_season" title="2014–15 ECHL season">2014–15 season</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brampton_Beast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1998 to 2013, the Powerade Centre was home to the <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Hockey_League" title="Ontario Hockey League">Ontario Hockey League</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Brampton_Battalion" title="Brampton Battalion">Brampton Battalion</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Junior_ice_hockey#Major_junior" title="Junior ice hockey">major junior hockey team</a>. The Battalion relocated to <a href="/wiki/North_Bay,_Ontario" title="North Bay, Ontario">North Bay, Ontario</a> in the 2013 off-season after several years of low attendance and struggling financially. On January 23, 2013, the Central Hockey League announced that it had signed a Letter of Intent with the ownership group led by Gregg Rosen, former <a href="/wiki/Kingston_Voyageurs" title="Kingston Voyageurs">Kingston Voyageurs</a> owner, and president of KIMCO Steel in Kingston. Minority owner, <a href="/wiki/Cary_Kaplan" title="Cary Kaplan">Cary Kaplan</a>, would serve as the President &amp; General Manager.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bramptonloi_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bramptonloi-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leaseterms_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-leaseterms-2">[2]</a></sup> The lease was officially signed and the agreement executed on March 8, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officialchl_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-officialchl-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardianofficial_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardianofficial-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 22, the Beast announced that Brampton native and former <a href="/wiki/Fayetteville_FireAntz" title="Fayetteville FireAntz">Fayetteville FireAntz</a> coach, the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Southern_Professional_Hockey_League" title="Southern Professional Hockey League">SPHL</a> coach of the year, <a href="/wiki/Mark_DeSantis_(ice_hockey)" title="Mark DeSantis (ice hockey)">Mark DeSantis</a> would serve as the team's first head coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-desantiscoach_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-desantiscoach-5">[5]</a></sup> DeSantis shortly named former Brampton Battalion player and his former teammate, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brent_Hughes_(ice_hockey,_born_1982)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brent Hughes (ice hockey, born 1982) (page does not exist)">Brent Hughes</a> as Beast assistant coach.</p>
<p>In June 2013, the Beast announced its first player signed was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Smith_(ice_hockey)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brett Smith (ice hockey) (page does not exist)">Brett Smith</a>, the former assistant captain of the <a href="/wiki/Fort_Wayne_Komets" title="Fort Wayne Komets">Fort Wayne Komets</a> and member of the CHL Championship team in 2011. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cal_Wild&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cal Wild (page does not exist)">Cal Wild</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jason_Pitton&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jason Pitton (page does not exist)">Jason Pitton</a> were announced on July 9, 2013. Defenceman <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Couch&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Michael Couch (page does not exist)">Michael Couch</a> and right winger <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tylor_Michel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tylor Michel (page does not exist)">Tylor Michel</a> were signed on August 16, 2013. On August 30, 2013 the Beast added two forwards, former <a href="/wiki/New_York_Islanders" title="New York Islanders">New York Islander</a> <a href="/wiki/Rob_Collins" title="Rob Collins">Rob Collins</a> and defending SPHL League MVP <a href="/wiki/Josh_McQuade" title="Josh McQuade">Josh McQuade</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Collins_McQuade_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Collins_McQuade-6">[6]</a></sup> On September 28, 2013, the Beast Mascot "Boomer" was born.</p>
<p>The 2013–14 schedule was released on July 26, 2013 and featured the Beast playing the first CHL game on Canadian soil against the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Sundogs" title="Arizona Sundogs">Arizona Sundogs</a> on October 18, 2013. The Beast would finish the season with 15 of their final 19 games at home, more than any other team. On August 22, 2013, <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Lightning" title="Tampa Bay Lightning">Tampa Bay Lightning</a> general manager <a href="/wiki/Steve_Yzerman" title="Steve Yzerman">Steve Yzerman</a> announced that the Lightning of the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_Crunch" title="Syracuse Crunch">Syracuse Crunch</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">American Hockey League</a> would affiliate with the Brampton Beast for the 2013–14 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beastlightning_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-beastlightning-7">[7]</a></sup> The Beast retired #7 in honour of the Brampton Beast fans being "the 7th man" during their first home game vs. the Arizona Sundogs.</p>
<p>For the team's second season in 2014, head coach Mark DeSantis was replaced by his assistant Brent Hughes as the team's new head coach and Cal Wild was named the team's second Captain. Key signings included former <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Maple_Leafs" title="Toronto Maple Leafs">Toronto Maple Leaf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phil_Oreskovic" title="Phil Oreskovic">Phil Oreskovic</a>, former ECHL MVP and OHL scoring leader <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Donati" title="Tyler Donati">Tyler Donati</a>, 2013 Beast scoring leader Jason Pitton, perennial point a game player <a href="/wiki/Chad_Painchaud" title="Chad Painchaud">Chad Painchaud</a>, former Battalion players <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stephon_Thorne&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stephon Thorne (page does not exist)">Stephon Thorne</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jason_Dale&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jason Dale (page does not exist)">Jason Dale</a>, former <a href="/wiki/Kalamazoo_Wings" title="Kalamazoo Wings">Kalamazoo Wings</a> Captain <a href="/wiki/Elgin_Reid" title="Elgin Reid">Elgin Reid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida_Everblades" title="Florida Everblades">Florida Everblades</a> goaltender <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Cann" title="Trevor Cann">Trevor Cann</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_MacIsaac&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mike MacIsaac (page does not exist)">Mike MacIsaac</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Grant_Rollheiser&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Grant Rollheiser (page does not exist)">Grant Rollheiser</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brayden_Rose&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brayden Rose (page does not exist)">Brayden Rose</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Darrigo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andrew Darrigo (page does not exist)">Andrew Darrigo</a> returned for their second season with the Beast.</p>
<p>On October 7, 2014, not long before the 2014–15 season was set to begin, it was announced that the Central Hockey League had ceased operations. The Beast, along with the <a href="/wiki/Allen_Americans" title="Allen Americans">Allen Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quad_City_Mallards" title="Quad City Mallards">Quad City Mallards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri_Mavericks" title="Missouri Mavericks">Missouri Mavericks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rapid_City_Rush" title="Rapid City Rush">Rapid City Rush</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tulsa_Oilers" title="Tulsa Oilers">Tulsa Oilers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wichita_Thunder" title="Wichita Thunder">Wichita Thunder</a>, were all approved for expansion membership into the <a href="/wiki/ECHL" title="ECHL">ECHL</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_ECHL_season" title="2014–15 ECHL season">2014–15 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chljoinsechl_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-chljoinsechl-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-echladdschl_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-echladdschl-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Beast finished their inaugural ECHL season on April 12, 2015 against the <a href="/wiki/Wheeling_Nailers" title="Wheeling Nailers">Wheeling Nailers</a>. Captain Cal Wild was awarded the ECHL Community Player of the Year,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Calwildcommunityaward_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Calwildcommunityaward-10">[10]</a></sup> one year after winning the Central Hockey League's Man of the Year award. Jason Pitton second successful season with the Beast made him the all-time franchise scorer, in addition to a number of other statistical categories (all-time goals, assists, points, shots on goal). Chad Painchaud was recognized as February's ECHL Player of the Month after scoring 17 goals - including three hat-tricks and a four-goal game - in addition to three assists for 20 points during the month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paunchaudpotm_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-paunchaudpotm-11">[11]</a></sup> However, the team finished 25th out of 28 teams and would fire head coach Brent Hughes after the end of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 5, 2015, the Beast announced <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Colin_Chaulk&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Colin Chaulk (page does not exist)">Colin Chaulk</a>, former <a href="/wiki/Kalamazoo_Wings" title="Kalamazoo Wings">Kalamazoo Wings</a> assistant coach and former long time captain of the Fort Wayne Komets, had been hired as the new head coach for the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_ECHL_season" title="2015–16 ECHL season">2015–16 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 28, 2015, the Beast announced their affiliation with the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Canadiens" title="Montreal Canadiens">Montreal Canadiens</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">American Hockey League</a>'s <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s_IceCaps" title="St. John's IceCaps">St. John's IceCaps</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beasthabs_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-beasthabs-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the 2015–16, the Beast had a number of major signings. Significant among them former NHler and prolific Battalion defenseman <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mike_Vernace" title="Mike Vernace">Mike Vernace</a>, ECHL Scoring Champion (2014) and CHL MVP (2012) <a href="/wiki/Brandon_Marino" title="Brandon Marino">Brandon Marino</a>, top ECHL goal scorer and championship winner Chris Auger, AHL veteran and ECHL captain Jordan Henry. Back in the fold are captain Cal Wild (until he retired), the league's top rookie scoring defenseman Matthew Maione, and the return of the Beast first season's points per game leader, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Howes" title="Scott Howes">Scott Howes</a>. Montreal Canadiens have provided the Beast several players in 2015 through the season, notably veteran standout AHL goalie <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eddie_Pasquale" title="Eddie Pasquale">Eddie Pasquale</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Travis_Brown_(ice_hockey)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Travis Brown (ice hockey) (page does not exist)">Travis Brown</a>, as well as draft picks <a href="/wiki/Tim_Bozon" title="Tim Bozon">Tim Bozon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dalton_Thrower" title="Dalton Thrower">Dalton Thrower</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Josiah_Didier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Josiah Didier (page does not exist)">Josiah Didier</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Fournier" title="Stefan Fournier">Stefan Fournier</a>.</p>
<p>After being the only player to appear in all three Beast seasons, as well as being named captain in each season and two-time Brampton Beast's Man of the Year, Cal Wild announced his retirement on November 22, 2015. He retired second in both career and points and games played, behind only <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jason_Pitton&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jason Pitton (page does not exist)">Jason Pitton</a> in both categories.</p>
<p>In January 2016, the team asked the Brampton City Council for a financial partnership to operate out of the Powerade Center for the 2016–17 season noting that the City's lack of ownership stake in the building is a significant impediment to running a viable hockey franchise. GM <a href="/wiki/Cary_Kaplan" title="Cary Kaplan">Cary Kaplan</a> stated that if the team and city are unable to come to agreement to help offset the team's financial losses, the team would not play the next season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On March 9, 2016, the city council agreed in an 8-2 vote to a 3-year sponsorship agreement of $1.5 Million to help mitigate the predicted financial losses. This money will be refunded to the City if the team earns a profit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The 2016–17 season had the most returning players in Beast history, led by captain Brandon Marino, Jordan Henry, Like Pither, Luc Blain, Tim Billingsley. Free agent signings included top-ten scorer David Pacan, and former team leaders in scoring David Vallorani and Brandon MacLean.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Team_name">Team name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brampton_Beast&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Team name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 30, the team officially announced a name-the-team contest,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nametheteamlaunch_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-nametheteamlaunch-17">[17]</a></sup> with the final ten names being announced on March 19, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nametheteamfinalten_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-nametheteamfinalten-18">[18]</a></sup> The final two of ten finalists were Brampton Beast and Brampton Bandits, eliminated April 10, 2013. Other options were Arrows (eliminated March 27), Bengals (eliminated March 22), Blizzard (eliminated April 3), North Stars (eliminated April 4), Renegades (eliminated March 28), Smash (eliminated March 21), Vipers (eliminated April 5), and Wolverines (eliminated March 29).</p>
<p>The Brampton Beast name was announced as the winner on April 10. The name was submitted by four fans<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beastguardian_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-beastguardian-19">[19]</a></sup> including Joseph Stephen and his son Alexander. The team logo and colours were released at City Hall in front of 350 fans on Saturday April 27. Team colours are Silver, Black, Red &amp; White.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buzz_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-buzz-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
