<p>The <b>Free Hanseatic City of Bremen</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Freie Hansestadt Bremen</i></span>, <small>pronounced</small> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA_for_German" title="Help:IPA for German">[ˈbʁeːmən]</a></span>) is the smallest of Germany's 16 <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">states</a>. A more informal name, but used in some official contexts, is <i>Land Bremen</i> ('State of Bremen').</p>
<p>The state consists of two enclaves with two cities (<a href="/wiki/Bremen" title="Bremen">Bremen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bremerhaven" title="Bremerhaven">Bremerhaven</a>) in the North of Germany, surrounded by the larger state of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bremen_(state)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The state of Bremen consists of two separated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enclave" title="Enclave">enclaves</a>. These enclaves contain <a href="/wiki/Bremen" title="Bremen">Bremen</a>, officially the 'City' (<i>Stadtgemeinde Bremen</i>) which is the state capital and located in both enclaves, and the city of <a href="/wiki/Bremerhaven" title="Bremerhaven">Bremerhaven</a> (<i>Stadt Bremerhaven</i>). Both are located on the River <a href="/wiki/Weser" title="Weser">Weser</a>; Bremerhaven is further downstream than the main parts of Bremen and serves as a <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harbour" title="Harbour">harbour</a> (the name <i>Bremerhaven</i> means "Bremen's harbour"). Both enclaves are completely surrounded by the neighbouring State of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a> (<i>Niedersachsen</i>). The two cities are the only administrative subdivisions the state has.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bremen_(state)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the unwinding of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a> in 1806 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_Imperial_City" title="Free Imperial City">Free Imperial City</a> of Bremen (as of 1646, after earlier privileges of autonomy of 1186) was not <a href="/wiki/Mediatisation" title="Mediatisation">mediatised</a> but became a sovereign state officially titled <i><a href="/wiki/Hanseatic_League" title="Hanseatic League">Free Hanseatic City</a> of Bremen</i>. Its currency was the <a href="/wiki/Bremen_thaler" title="Bremen thaler">Bremen thaler</a> (until 1873). In 1811 the <a href="/wiki/First_French_Empire" title="First French Empire">First French Empire</a> annexed the <a href="/wiki/City-state" title="City-state">city-state</a>. Upon the first, albeit only preliminary, defeat of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napol%C3%A9on_Bonaparte" title="Napoléon Bonaparte">Napoléon Bonaparte</a>, Bremen resumed its pre-1811 status as city-state in 1813.</p>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vienna_Congress" title="Vienna Congress">Vienna Congress</a> of 1815 confirmed Bremen's—as well as <a href="/wiki/Free_City_of_Frankfurt" title="Free City of Frankfurt">Frankfurt's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg's</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Free_City_of_L%C3%BCbeck" title="Free City of Lübeck">Lübeck's</a>—independence after pressuring by Bremen's emissary, and later <a href="/wiki/Burgomaster" title="Burgomaster">burgomaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johann_Smidt" title="Johann Smidt">Johann Smidt</a>. Bremen became one of 39 sovereign states of the <a href="/wiki/German_Confederation" title="German Confederation">German Confederation</a>. In 1827 the state of Bremen bought the tract of land from the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hanover" title="Kingdom of Hanover">Kingdom of Hanover</a>, where future Bremerhaven would be established. Bremen became part of the <a href="/wiki/North_German_Confederation" title="North German Confederation">North German Confederation</a> in 1867 and became an autonomous component state of the new-founded <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a> in 1871 and stayed with Germany in its following forms of government.</p>
<p>Bremen, which in 1935 had become a regular city at the de facto abolition of statehood of all component German states within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Reich" title="Third Reich">Third Reich</a>, was reestablished as a state in 1947. Being—at that time—actually located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allied_Occupation_Zones_in_Germany" title="Allied Occupation Zones in Germany">British Zone of Occupation</a> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Control_Commission_for_Germany_-_British_Element" title="Control Commission for Germany - British Element">Control Commission for Germany - British Element</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Military_Government,_United_States" title="Office of Military Government, United States">Office of Military Government for Germany, U.S. (OMGUS)</a> agreed in 1947 to constitute the cities of <i>Bremen</i> and then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weserm%C3%BCnde" title="Wesermünde">Wesermünde</a>—in their borders altered in 1939—as a <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">German state</a> named again <i>Free Hanseatic City of Bremen</i>, becoming at that occasion an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave">exclave</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allied_Occupation_Zones_in_Germany" title="Allied Occupation Zones in Germany">American Zone of Occupation</a> within the <i>British zone</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1949 the city-state joined the then West German <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">Federal Republic of Germany</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bremen_(state)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_system">Political system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bremen_(state)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Political system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<tr>
<td class="mbox-image"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg"><img alt="[icon]" data-file-height="31" data-file-width="44" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" width="20"/></a></td>
<td class="mbox-text"><span class="mbox-text-span"><b>This section needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Bremen_(state)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small> <small><i>(November 2009)</i></small></span></td>

<p>The legislature of the state of Bremen is the <a href="/wiki/B%C3%BCrgerschaft_of_Bremen" title="Bürgerschaft of Bremen">Bürgerschaft</a> (citizens' assembly), elected by the citizens in the two cities of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bremen_(city)" title="Bremen (city)">Bremen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bremerhaven" title="Bremerhaven">Bremerhaven</a>.</p>
<p>The executive is constituted by the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Bremen" title="Senate of Bremen">Senate of Bremen</a>, elected by the Bürgerschaft. The Senate is chaired by the President of the senate (<i>Senatspräsident</i>), who is also one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Bremen" title="Mayor of Bremen">mayors of the city of Bremen</a> (<i>Bürgermeister</i>) and is elected directly by the Bürgerschaft. The Senate selects of its members as a second mayor who serves as deputy of the president. In contrast to the Federal Chancellor of Germany or other German states, the President of the Senate has no authority to override senators on policy, which is decided upon by the senate collectively. Since 1945, the Senate has continuously been dominated by the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Germany" title="Social Democratic Party of Germany">Social Democratic Party</a>.</p>
<p>On a municipal level, the two cities in the state are administered separately:</p>
<ul>
<li>The administration of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bremen_(city)" title="Bremen (city)">city of Bremen</a> is headed by the two mayors and controlled by the portion of the <a href="/wiki/B%C3%BCrgerschaft_of_Bremen" title="Bürgerschaft of Bremen">Bürgerschaft</a> elected in the city of Bremen.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bremerhaven" title="Bremerhaven">Bremerhaven</a>, on the other hand, has a municipal assembly distinct from the state legislature and an administration under a distinct head mayor (<i>Oberbürgermeister</i>) and a distinct second mayor.</li>
</ul>

<p>Dr. <a href="/wiki/Henning_Scherf" title="Henning Scherf">Henning Scherf</a> (SPD) remained Mayor and Senate President, in an SPD-CDU <a href="/wiki/Grand_coalition" title="Grand coalition">grand coalition</a>. As promised he resigned after half of the legislative period. The Mayor and Senate President since 8 November 2005 is <a href="/wiki/Jens_B%C3%B6hrnsen" title="Jens Böhrnsen">Jens Böhrnsen</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bremen_(state)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bremen" title="University of Bremen">University of Bremen</a> is the largest university in Bremen. Furthermore, Bremen has a <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Arts_Bremen" title="University of the Arts Bremen">University of the Arts Bremen</a>, a University of Applied Sciences in Bremen and another one in <a href="/wiki/Bremerhaven" title="Bremerhaven">Bremerhaven</a>, and more recently the <a href="/wiki/Jacobs_University_Bremen" title="Jacobs University Bremen">Jacobs University Bremen</a>.</p>
