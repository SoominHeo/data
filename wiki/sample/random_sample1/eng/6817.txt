<p><i><b>Brake</b></i> is an 2012 American <a href="/wiki/Thriller_film" title="Thriller film">thriller film</a> directed by Gabe Torres, written by Timothy Mannion, and starring <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Dorff" title="Stephen Dorff">Stephen Dorff</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brake_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jeremy Reins, a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secret_Service" title="United States Secret Service">U.S. Secret Service</a> special agent assigned to the presidential detail, is drugged, kidnapped and held captive within a glass box in the cramped, dark trunk of a car. At first Jeremy thinks it is a prank from people he owes money for gambling debts, but he quickly learns that the truth is far more sinister. Jeremy begins to endure mental and physical torture as terrorists attempt to extract information. The information needed is the location of the secret bunkers, dubbed "Roulette", that are <a href="/wiki/Presidential_Emergency_Facility" title="Presidential Emergency Facility">used</a> by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">president</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_United_States" title="Vice President of the United States">vice president</a> during a national emergency.</p>
<p>Jeremy's only contact is Henry, another hostage that is also locked in a trunk of a car; both hostages have timers in the trunk with them that counts down. The terrorists have left old radios in the trunk to allow them to communicate with their hostages as well as allowing Jeremy and Henry to talk to one another. Through long conversations, Jeremy learns that the cars are actually bombs, and that they are currently in Maryland traveling towards Washington D.C. The car is almost pulled over by police and a high speed chase ensues but Jeremy is unable to be rescued, although the box is shot, leaving a hole in the glass. Jeremy is tortured every time the counter hits zero, at one point bees are released into his tank, since he is allergic to bee stings. However, the terrorists give him an Epi-pen injection, saving his life: they need him alive. The terrorists also kidnap his estranged wife, Molly, and hold her in another trunk. After enduring much emotional and mental stress, Jeremy still refuses to give up the location. After his countdown reaches zero, Jeremy's glass box begins to flood with liquid. After nearly drowning, Jeremy is pulled out by someone, revealed to be Henry.</p>
<p>It is revealed that the whole situation was an exercise to test whether Jeremy would break or not. Everyone he saw or was in contact with is present, along with their radios, which they used to play their parts. Jeremy collapses from his wounds and is put in an ambulance with Molly. On the way to the hospital, he sees the Washington monument through the window and chuckles. Noticing this, Molly looks out the window and asks if the monument was the location, but Jeremy dismisses it as unimportant and gives her an engagement ring, asking her to marry him. When she replies that they are already married, Jeremy smiles and asks her to marry him again. As they kiss, Molly handcuffs him to the gurney and pulls a wire from her shirt. Revealing themselves to really be with the terrorists, Molly and Henry are told over the radio that they have the location now that they lured him into a false sense of security and Jeremy is to be killed. As Molly holds a gas-filled mask over Jeremy's face, the film cuts to black.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brake_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Filming took place in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, primarily <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Hollywood,_California" title="North Hollywood, California">North Hollywood</a>, using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Red_One_(camera)" title="The Red One (camera)">Red One</a> camera.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brake_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a>, reports that 44% of 25 surveyed critics gave the film a positive review; the average rating was 5/10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> rated it 38/100 based on eleven reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Holden" title="Stephen Holden">Stephen Holden</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> wrote that the film exists for its <i>Twilight Zone</i>-style "gotcha" twist ending.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-3">[3]</a></sup> Glenn Whipp of the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> wrote that the film's twists are preposterous and negate everything that came before them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Robert Koehler of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> wrote that the script "leaves audiences feeling played" but Dorff "fully commits" to the role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Overall, the film's first half was praised but many felt the second ending to be unnecessary.</p>
<p>The film has been compared to a similarly themed film, <i><a href="/wiki/Buried_(film)" title="Buried (film)">Buried</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Reynolds" title="Ryan Reynolds">Ryan Reynolds</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
