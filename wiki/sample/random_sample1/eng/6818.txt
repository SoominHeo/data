<p><i><b>The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 2 (Original Motion Picture Soundtrack)</b></i> is the <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack_album" title="Soundtrack album">soundtrack album</a> to the film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_Breaking_Dawn_%E2%80%93_Part_2" title="The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 2">The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 2</a></i>. It is the fifth soundtrack in the saga's chronology, and was released on November 13, 2012. The soundtrack is once again produced by Alexandra Patsavas, the music director for the previous three films. The track list for the album was revealed on October 4, 2012, which included the announcement of the album's lead single. The album sold 229,000 copies in the US in 2012, making it the third best-selling soundtrack album of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yahoo_Chartwatch_01-03-2013_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo_Chartwatch_01-03-2013-3">[3]</a></sup> It has sold 303,000 copies as of April 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Idol_Chatter_04-21-2013_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Idol_Chatter_04-21-2013-4">[4]</a></sup></p>

<p>The score, like the <a href="/wiki/Twilight_(2008_film)" title="Twilight (2008 film)">original film</a> as well as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_Breaking_Dawn_%E2%80%93_Part_1" title="The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 1">Breaking Dawn – Part 1</a></i>, was composed by <a href="/wiki/Carter_Burwell" title="Carter Burwell">Carter Burwell</a>, following <a href="/wiki/Howard_Shore" title="Howard Shore">Howard Shore</a>, who scored <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_Eclipse" title="The Twilight Saga: Eclipse">Eclipse</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Desplat" title="Alexandre Desplat">Alexandre Desplat</a>, who scored <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_New_Moon" title="The Twilight Saga: New Moon">New Moon</a></i>. The album was released in North America on November 27, 2012 by <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>. It contains elements from the scores of both <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_New_Moon" title="The Twilight Saga: New Moon">New Moon</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Twilight_Saga:_Eclipse" title="The Twilight Saga: Eclipse">Eclipse</a></i>.</p>

<p>Based on four reviews, <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> assigned the <i>Breaking Dawn – Part 2</i> soundtrack an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weighted_mean" title="Weighted mean">average</a> score of 74, indicating "generally favorable reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Heather Phares, reviewing for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a>, said "It's fitting that the film's soundtrack is dominated by ballads that feel like a long goodbye to Bella, Edward, and Jacob", and said "As with the other volumes of the series' music, however, this collection provides a surprisingly good showcase for female, indie-ish talent." Phares concluded, "While <i>Breaking Dawn</i> isn't one of the more dynamic <i>Twilight Saga</i> soundtracks, it is one of the more emotive ones, and just may help fans get some closure as one of the biggest film franchises of the 2000s and 2010s comes to a close."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allmusic_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
