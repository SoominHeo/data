<p>Year <b>737</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCCXXXVII</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Tuesday" title="Common year starting on Tuesday">common year starting on Tuesday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 737 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=737&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=737&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=737&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Avignon" title="Battle of Avignon">Battle of Avignon</a>: <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Frankish</a> forces under <a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a>, Merovingian <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_the_Palace" title="Mayor of the Palace">Mayor of the Palace</a>, retake <a href="/wiki/Avignon" title="Avignon">Avignon</a> from the Muslim forces, and destroy the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad</a> stronghold. Charles sends his brother <a href="/wiki/Childebrand_I" title="Childebrand I">Childebrand I</a>, duke of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Burgundy" title="Kingdom of Burgundy">Burgundy</a>, to besiege the city. After his arrival, Charles leads the Frankish troops by using <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rope_ladder" title="Rope ladder">rope ladders</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battering_ram" title="Battering ram">battering rams</a> to attack the <a href="/wiki/Defensive_wall" title="Defensive wall">fortified walls</a>, which are burned to the ground following its capture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Narbonne_(737)" title="Battle of Narbonne (737)">Battle of Narbonne</a>: Frankish forces under Charles Martel besiege <a href="/wiki/Narbonne" title="Narbonne">Narbonne</a>, occupied by a Umayyad <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison</a>, but is unable to retake the fortress city. A <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombard</a> army under King <a href="/wiki/Liutprand,_King_of_the_Lombards" title="Liutprand, King of the Lombards">Liutprand</a> crosses the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a>, to aid Charles in expelling the Muslims from <a href="/wiki/Septimania" title="Septimania">Septimania</a>. Meanwhile <a href="/wiki/Maurontus" title="Maurontus">Maurontus</a>, duke or count of <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">Provence</a>, raises a revolt from his unconquered city of <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">Marseille</a>, and threatens the rear of the Franks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_River_Berre" title="Battle of the River Berre">Battle of the River Berre</a>: Frankish forces under Charles Martel intercept a large Muslim army sent from <a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">Al-Andalus</a>, (modern Spain) led by <a href="/wiki/Uqba_ibn_al-Hajjaj" title="Uqba ibn al-Hajjaj">Uqba ibn al-Hajjaj</a> to relieve the <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a> of Narbonne. The Franks destroy them at the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89tang_de_Berre" title="Étang de Berre">River Bierre</a> (northwest of Marseille), and drive the survivors into the nearby sea-<a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoons</a>, taking many prisoners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fouracre2000p97_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fouracre2000p97-4">[4]</a></sup> Charles effectively prevents Umayyad expansion in <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a>.</li>
<li>Following the death of <a href="/wiki/Theuderic_IV" title="Theuderic IV">Theuderic IV</a>, king of the Franks, the throne is left vacant for seven years. Charles Martel has his son <a href="/wiki/Childeric_III" title="Childeric III">Childeric III</a> exiled to a <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">monastery</a>, and becomes sole ruler of the <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Frankish Kingdom</a>.</li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Pelagius_of_Asturias" title="Pelagius of Asturias">Pelagius of Asturias</a> dies, and is succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Favila_of_Asturias" title="Favila of Asturias">Favila</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.C3.84rab150_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-.C3.84rab150-5">[5]</a></sup> He founds the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_de_Cangas_de_On%C3%ADs" title="Santa Cruz de Cangas de Onís">Church of Santa Cruz</a>, in his capital <a href="/wiki/Cangas_de_On%C3%ADs" title="Cangas de Onís">Cangas de Onís</a> (northwest of Spain).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Orso_Ipato" title="Orso Ipato">Orso Ipato</a> is murdered at the instigation of <a href="/wiki/Eutychius_(exarch)" title="Eutychius (exarch)">Eutychius</a>, exarch of <a href="/wiki/Exarchate_of_Ravenna" title="Exarchate of Ravenna">Ravenna</a>. He is succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Domenico_Leoni" title="Domenico Leoni">Domenico Leoni</a>, who is elected as the fourth <a href="/wiki/Doge" title="Doge">doge</a> of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Venice</a>.</li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Ongendus" title="Ongendus">Ongendus</a> of the Danes reinforces the <a href="/wiki/Danevirke" title="Danevirke">Danevirke</a> fortifications in <a href="/wiki/Schleswig-Holstein" title="Schleswig-Holstein">Schleswig-Holstein</a>. He orders a <a href="/wiki/Palisade" title="Palisade">palisade</a> rampart built on the frontier of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Saxony</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=737&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Ceolwulf_of_Northumbria" title="Ceolwulf of Northumbria">Ceolwulf of Northumbria</a> abdicates in favour of his cousin, <a href="/wiki/Eadberht_of_Northumbria" title="Eadberht of Northumbria">Eadberht</a>, and becomes a <a href="/wiki/Monk" title="Monk">monk</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lindisfarne_Priory" title="Lindisfarne Priory">Lindisfarne Priory</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=737&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>: Christians invade from the south, with the aim of protecting the <a href="/wiki/Patriarch_of_Alexandria" title="Patriarch of Alexandria">patriarch of Alexandria</a> (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=737&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Arab%E2%80%93Khazar_War" title="Second Arab–Khazar War">Second Arab–Khazar War</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Khazars" title="Khazars">Khazars</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Hazer_Tarkhan" title="Hazer Tarkhan">Hazer Tarkhan</a> defeat the Muslims near the <a href="/wiki/Volga_River" title="Volga River">Volga River</a>, and force them to retreat. By holding the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> against Islamic aggression, the Khazars delay their conquest of Eastern Europe, and thwart the Umayyad desire to attack <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a> from the north.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_30" title="September 30">September 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Baggage" title="Battle of the Baggage">Battle of the Baggage</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Turgesh" title="Turgesh">Turgesh</a> drive back an Umayyad invasion of <a href="/wiki/Khuttal" title="Khuttal">Khuttal</a>, pursue them south of the <a href="/wiki/Amu_Darya" title="Amu Darya">River Oxus</a> (northern <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>), and capture their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baggage_train" title="Baggage train">baggage train</a>. In the winter, the Turgesh and their <a href="/wiki/Transoxiana" title="Transoxiana">Transoxianan</a> allies launch a major counter-invasion, but are halted.</li>
<li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xuanzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xuanzong of Tang">Xuan Zong</a> discards the policy of <a href="/wiki/Conscription" title="Conscription">conscripting</a> men into the Chinese army to be replaced every three years, replacing them with long-service soldiers who are more battle-hardened and efficient (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
