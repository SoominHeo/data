<p><b>Brendan Gleeson</b> (born 29 March 1955) is an Irish actor. He is the recipient of three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IFTA_Awards" title="IFTA Awards">IFTA Awards</a>, two <a href="/wiki/British_Independent_Film_Awards" title="British Independent Film Awards">BIFA Awards</a>, one <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> and has been nominated twice for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award" title="BAFTA Award">BAFTA Award</a> and three times for a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a>.</p>
<p>His best-known performances include supporting roles in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Braveheart" title="Braveheart">Braveheart</a></i> (1995), <i><a href="/wiki/Mission:_Impossible_II" title="Mission: Impossible II">Mission: Impossible II</a></i> (2000), <i><a href="/wiki/Gangs_of_New_York" title="Gangs of New York">Gangs of New York</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/28_Days_Later" title="28 Days Later">28 Days Later</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Troy_(film)" title="Troy (film)">Troy</a></i> (2004), as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alastor_Moody" title="Alastor Moody">Alastor Moody</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)"><i>Harry Potter</i> films</a> (2005-11), <i><a href="/wiki/Albert_Nobbs" title="Albert Nobbs">Albert Nobbs</a></i> (2011), and <i><a href="/wiki/Assassin%27s_Creed_(film)" title="Assassin's Creed (film)">Assassin's Creed</a></i> (2016), and leading roles in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/In_Bruges" title="In Bruges">In Bruges</a></i> (2009), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guard_(2011_film)" title="The Guard (2011 film)">The Guard</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Calvary_(film)" title="Calvary (film)">Calvary</a></i> (2014) and <i><a href="/wiki/Live_by_Night_(film)" title="Live by Night (film)">Live by Night</a></i> (2016). He won an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Lead_Actor_%E2%80%93_Miniseries_or_a_Movie" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor – Miniseries or a Movie">Emmy Award</a> in 2009 for his portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> in the television film <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Storm_(2009_film)" title="Into the Storm (2009 film)">Into the Storm</a></i>.</p>
<p>He is also the father of actors <a href="/wiki/Domhnall_Gleeson" title="Domhnall Gleeson">Domhnall Gleeson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Gleeson_(actor)" title="Brian Gleeson (actor)">Brian Gleeson</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brendan_Gleeson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gleeson was born in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, the son of Pat and Frank Gleeson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Gleeson has described himself as having been an avid reader as a child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dunphy_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dunphy-2">[2]</a></sup> He received his second level education at St. Joseph's CBS in Fairview, Dublin where he was a member of the school drama group. After training as an actor, he worked for several years as a secondary school teacher of Irish and English at the now defunct Catholic Belcamp College in North <a href="/wiki/County_Dublin" title="County Dublin">County Dublin</a>, which closed in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He was working simultaneously as an actor while teaching, doing semi-professional and professional productions in Dublin and surrounding areas. He left the teaching profession to commit full-time to acting in 1991.</p>
<p>In an <a href="/wiki/NPR" title="NPR">NPR</a> interview to promote <a href="/wiki/Calvary_(film)" title="Calvary (film)"><i>Calvary</i></a>, he revealed that he was abused by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Brothers_of_Ireland" title="Christian Brothers of Ireland">Christian Brother</a>, saying, "I remember a particular Christian Brother dropped the hand on me at one point. It wasn’t very traumatic and it wasn’t at all sustained, it was just one of these things where something odd happened."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brendan_Gleeson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a member of the Dublin-based Passion Machine, Gleeson appeared in several of the theatre company's early and highly successful plays such as <i>Wasters</i> (1985), <i>Brownbread</i> (1987) and <i>Home</i> (1988). He has also written three plays for Passion Machine: <i>The Birdtable</i> (1987) and <i>Breaking Up</i> (1988), both of which he directed, and <i>Babies and Bathwater</i> (1994) in which he acted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Among his other Dublin theatre work are Patrick Süskind's one-man play <i>The Double Bass</i> and John B. Keane's <i>The Year of the Hiker</i>.</p>
<p>Gleeson started his film career at the age of 34.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He first came to prominence in Ireland for his role as <a href="/wiki/Michael_Collins_(Irish_leader)" title="Michael Collins (Irish leader)">Michael Collins</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Treaty_(film)" title="The Treaty (film)">The Treaty</a></i>, a television film broadcast on <a href="/wiki/RT%C3%89_One" title="RTÉ One">RTÉ One</a>, and for which he won a <a href="/wiki/Jacob%27s_Award" title="Jacob's Award">Jacob's Award</a> in 1992. He has acted in such films as <i><a href="/wiki/Braveheart" title="Braveheart">Braveheart</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Went_Down" title="I Went Down">I Went Down</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Michael_Collins_(film)" title="Michael Collins (film)">Michael Collins</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gangs_of_New_York" title="Gangs of New York">Gangs of New York</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cold_Mountain_(film)" title="Cold Mountain (film)">Cold Mountain</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/28_Days_Later" title="28 Days Later">28 Days Later</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Troy_(film)" title="Troy (film)">Troy</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Heaven_(film)" title="Kingdom of Heaven (film)">Kingdom of Heaven</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Lake_Placid_(film)" title="Lake Placid (film)">Lake Placid</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/A.I._Artificial_Intelligence" title="A.I. Artificial Intelligence">A.I. Artificial Intelligence</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mission:_Impossible_II" title="Mission: Impossible II">Mission: Impossible II</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Village_(2004_film)" title="The Village (2004 film)">The Village</a></i>. He won critical acclaim for his performance as Irish gangster <a href="/wiki/Martin_Cahill" title="Martin Cahill">Martin Cahill</a> in <a href="/wiki/John_Boorman" title="John Boorman">John Boorman</a>'s 1998 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_General_(1998_film)" title="The General (1998 film)">The General</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2003, Gleeson was the voice of Hugh the Miller in an episode of the <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> animated series <i>Wilde Stories</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>While Gleeson portrayed Irish statesman Michael Collins in <i>The Treaty</i>, he later portrayed Collins' close collaborator <a href="/wiki/Liam_Tobin" title="Liam Tobin">Liam Tobin</a> in the film <i>Michael Collins</i> with <a href="/wiki/Liam_Neeson" title="Liam Neeson">Liam Neeson</a> taking the role of Collins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Gleeson later went on to portray <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Into_the_Storm_(2009_film)" title="Into the Storm (2009 film)">Into the Storm</a></i>. Gleeson won an <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">Emmy Award</a> for his performance. Gleeson played <a href="/wiki/Hogwarts" title="Hogwarts">Hogwarts</a> professor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mad-Eye_Moody" title="Mad-Eye Moody">Mad-Eye Moody</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Goblet_of_Fire_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (film)">fourth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Order_of_the_Phoenix_(film)" title="Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (film)">fifth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_and_the_Deathly_Hallows_%E2%80%93_Part_1" title="Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows – Part 1">seventh</a> <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter_(film_series)" title="Harry Potter (film series)"><i>Harry Potter</i> films</a>. His son <a href="/wiki/Domhnall_Gleeson" title="Domhnall Gleeson">Domhnall</a> played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bill_Weasley" title="Bill Weasley">Bill Weasley</a> in the seventh film.</p>

<p>Gleeson provided the voice of Abbot Cellach in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Secret_of_Kells" title="The Secret of Kells">The Secret of Kells</a></i>, an animated film co-directed by <a href="/wiki/Tomm_Moore" title="Tomm Moore">Tomm Moore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nora_Twomey" title="Nora Twomey">Nora Twomey</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Saloon" title="Cartoon Saloon">Cartoon Saloon</a> which premiered in February 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Jameson_Irish_Whiskey" title="Jameson Irish Whiskey">Jameson</a> Dublin International Film Festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gleeson starred in the short film <i><a href="/wiki/Six_Shooter_(film)" title="Six Shooter (film)">Six Shooter</a></i> in 2006, which won an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Live_Action_Short_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Live Action Short Film">Academy Award for Best Live Action Short Film</a>. This film was written and directed by <a href="/wiki/Martin_McDonagh" title="Martin McDonagh">Martin McDonagh</a> who also wrote and directed <i><a href="/wiki/In_Bruges" title="In Bruges">In Bruges</a></i> in 2008. The film, and Gleeson's performance, enjoyed huge critical acclaim, earning Gleeson several award nominations, including his first Golden Globe nomination. In the movie, Gleeson plays a mentor-like figure for <a href="/wiki/Colin_Farrell" title="Colin Farrell">Colin Farrell</a>'s hitman. In his review of <i>In Bruges</i>, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> described the elder Gleeson as having a "noble shambles of a face and the heft of a boxer gone to seed."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gleeson will be making his directorial debut in a film adaptation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flann_O%27Brien" title="Flann O'Brien">Flann O'Brien</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/At_Swim-Two-Birds" title="At Swim-Two-Birds">At Swim-Two-Birds</a></i>. The Irish production company Parallel Pictures will produce the film with a budget of $11 million. Colin Farrell, <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Byrne" title="Gabriel Byrne">Gabriel Byrne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cillian_Murphy" title="Cillian Murphy">Cillian Murphy</a> have been attached to star in the film, which was originally set for release in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In October 2009, however, Gleeson expressed concern that the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Film_Board" title="Irish Film Board">Irish Film Board</a>'s budget might be reduced given the state of the Irish economy and that <i>At Swim-Two-Birds</i> might fall through.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Gleeson confirmed in July 2011, that he has secured funding for the project. He described the writing of the script as tortuous, saying that it has taken fourteen drafts so far.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2012, he started filming <i><a href="/wiki/The_Grand_Seduction" title="The Grand Seduction">The Grand Seduction</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Kitsch" title="Taylor Kitsch">Taylor Kitsch</a>, a remake of <a href="/wiki/Jean-Fran%C3%A7ois_Pouliot" title="Jean-François Pouliot">Jean-François Pouliot</a>'s French-Canadian <i><a href="/wiki/Seducing_Doctor_Lewis" title="Seducing Doctor Lewis">La Grande Séduction</a></i> (2003) directed by <a href="/wiki/Don_McKellar" title="Don McKellar">Don McKellar</a>; the film was released in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2016, he appeared in the video game adaptation <i><a href="/wiki/Assassin%27s_Creed_(film)" title="Assassin's Creed (film)">Assassin's Creed</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Ben_Affleck" title="Ben Affleck">Ben Affleck</a>'s crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Live_by_Night_(film)" title="Live by Night (film)">Live by Night</a></i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_talent">Musical talent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brendan_Gleeson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Musical talent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gleeson is a <a href="/wiki/Fiddle" title="Fiddle">fiddle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mandolin" title="Mandolin">mandolin</a> player, with an interest in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_folklore" title="Irish folklore">Irish folklore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dunphy_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dunphy-2">[2]</a></sup> He played the fiddle during his role in <i>Cold Mountain</i>, <i>Michael Collins</i> and also <i>The Grand Seduction</i>, and also features on <a href="/wiki/Altan_(band)" title="Altan (band)">Altan</a>'s 2009 live album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mpi_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-mpi-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brendan_Gleeson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He has been married to Mary (née Weldon) since 1982. He has four sons; <a href="/wiki/Domhnall_Gleeson" title="Domhnall Gleeson">Domhnall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Gleeson_(actor)" title="Brian Gleeson (actor)">Brían</a>, Fergus, and Rúairí. Domhnall and Brían are also actors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Gleeson speaks fluent <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a> and is an advocate of the promotion of the Irish language. Gleeson is a fan of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Football League Championship</a> team <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>, as is his son Domhnall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
