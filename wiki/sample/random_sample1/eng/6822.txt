<p><b>Brenton Thwaites</b> (born 10 August 1989) is an Australian actor who first came to prominence with his portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Slide_(TV_series)#Cast_and_characters" title="Slide (TV series)">Luke Gallagher</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Fox8" title="Fox8">Fox8</a> teen drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Slide_(TV_series)" title="Slide (TV series)">SLiDE</a></i> (2011), and later <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stu_Henderson" title="Stu Henderson">Stu Henderson</a> in the soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Home_and_Away" title="Home and Away">Home and Away</a></i> (2011–12). Since moving to the United States, Thwaites has headlined films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Lagoon:_The_Awakening" title="Blue Lagoon: The Awakening">Blue Lagoon: The Awakening</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/Oculus_(film)" title="Oculus (film)">Oculus</a></i> (2013), and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Giver_(film)" title="The Giver (film)">The Giver</a></i> (2014), the last, opposite <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Bridges" title="Jeff Bridges">Jeff Bridges</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meryl_Streep" title="Meryl Streep">Meryl Streep</a>. Thwaites was the lead actor in 2016's <i><a href="/wiki/Gods_of_Egypt_(film)" title="Gods of Egypt (film)">Gods of Egypt</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Butler" title="Gerard Butler">Gerard Butler</a>, and will next star in <i><a href="/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean:_Dead_Men_Tell_No_Tales" title="Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Men Tell No Tales">Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Men Tell No Tales</a></i>, the 2017 installment of the <i><a href="/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean_(film_series)" title="Pirates of the Caribbean (film series)">Pirates of the Caribbean</a></i> franchise, as Henry.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brenton_Thwaites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1989.E2.80.932010:_Early_life">1989–2010: Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brenton_Thwaites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1989–2010: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Thwaites was born on 10 August 1989<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Home_away_from_Home_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Home_away_from_Home-1">[1]</a></sup> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cairns,_Queensland" title="Cairns, Queensland">Cairns, Queensland</a>, to Fiona and Peter Thwaites. He has a sister, Stacey. He underwent secondary education at <a href="/wiki/Cairns_State_High_School" title="Cairns State High School">Cairns State High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Far_North_Queensland" title="Far North Queensland">Far North Queensland</a>, graduating in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Home_away_from_Home_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Home_away_from_Home-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>During his youth, Thwaites was drawn to the idea of becoming a policeman or a firefighter, having been interested in movies involving the latter. However, his interests diverted to the processes of <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">filmmaking</a> and "letting [one's] personality shine through so many different characters." At the age of 16, Thwaites made his first performance in front of a live audience in a rendition of the <a href="/wiki/Shakespearean_tragedy" title="Shakespearean tragedy">Shakespearean tragedy</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_and_Juliet" title="Romeo and Juliet">Romeo and Juliet</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interests_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-interests-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Thwaites studied acting at the <a href="/wiki/Queensland_University_of_Technology" title="Queensland University of Technology">Queensland University of Technology</a> (QUT) for three years, before graduating in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qut2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-qut2-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-QUTalumni_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-QUTalumni-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BrentonSoaps_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-BrentonSoaps-5">[5]</a></sup> He then relocated to <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a> to join the long-running soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Home_and_Away" title="Home and Away">Home and Away</a></i> before moving to the United States in 2011/12 to pursue his career in acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeout_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-timeout-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1stApperance_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-1stApperance-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HeraldSun_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-HeraldSun-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Thwaites cited fellow Australian actors <a href="/wiki/Heath_Ledger" title="Heath Ledger">Heath Ledger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Jackman" title="Hugh Jackman">Hugh Jackman</a> as inspirations of his, admiring how Ledger "was a young Aussie kid [...] who was doing really well overseas"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ledger_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ledger-9">[9]</a></sup> and commending Jackman as "a cool Aussie guy who works very hard and he’s a fantastic actor."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jackman_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-jackman-10">[10]</a></sup> He also found inspiration in comedy actors <a href="/wiki/Robin_Williams" title="Robin Williams">Robin Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Carrey" title="Jim Carrey">Jim Carrey</a> when practicing the <a href="/wiki/North_American_English_regional_phonology" title="North American English regional phonology">American accent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-accent_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-accent-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9312:_Career_beginnings">2010–12: Career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brenton_Thwaites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010–12: Career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to graduating from the Queensland University of Technology, Thwaites made his <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/debut" title="wikt:debut">film debut</a> in <i>Charge Over You</i>, a 2010 <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature film</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-COY1_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-COY1-12">[12]</a></sup> After he graduated, Thwaites went on to appear in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Sea_Patrol" title="Sea Patrol">Sea Patrol</a></i> and in the <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short film</a>, <i>Headsmen</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-QNews_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-QNews-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sea_Patrol_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sea_Patrol-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2010, Thwaites, along with Adele Perovic, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Schumann" title="Ben Schumann">Ben Schumann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emily_Robins" title="Emily Robins">Emily Robins</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gracie_Gilbert" title="Gracie Gilbert">Gracie Gilbert</a>, was cast in a new <a href="/wiki/Fox8" title="Fox8">Fox8</a> Australian <a href="/wiki/Teen_drama" title="Teen drama">teen drama</a> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Slide_(TV_series)" title="Slide (TV series)">SLiDE</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Throng_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Throng-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TvTonight_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-TvTonight-16">[16]</a></sup> The show follows five Brisbane teenagers making their way to adulthood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TvTonight_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-TvTonight-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Austar_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Austar-17">[17]</a></sup> Thwaites believes his character, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luke_Gallagher" title="Luke Gallagher">Luke Gallagher</a>, "stands back a bit. He watches. He's struggling with family issues. He's drawn in as it was a chance to find better friends and an opportunity to be part of a group. He's a cool, accepted kid. Some friends he can love and help him mature."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-QNews_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-QNews-13">[13]</a></sup> Thwaites made his first appearance on <i>SLiDE</i> in the series' <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Slide_episodes" title="List of Slide episodes">premiere episode</a> on 16 August 2011. The series ran one season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TvTonight_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-TvTonight-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Shortly after Thwaites moved to Sydney from Cairns in April 2011, he was given a five–month recurring role of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stu_Henderson" title="Stu Henderson">Stu Henderson</a> in the 24th season of <i>Home and Away</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BrentonSoaps_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-BrentonSoaps-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1stApperance_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-1stApperance-7">[7]</a></sup> Thwaites called the show a great learning experience and said his co-stars were easy to work with.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HeraldSun_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-HeraldSun-8">[8]</a></sup> Thwaites made his first appearance as Stu, a member of the River Boys, on 23 August 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1stApperance_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-1stApperance-7">[7]</a></sup> Tristan Swanwick of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Courier-Mail" title="The Courier-Mail">The Courier-Mail</a></i> said Thwaites was a "bit too pretty to be a bad-ass River Boy" but that some fake tattoos would balance that impression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BrentonSoaps_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-BrentonSoaps-5">[5]</a></sup> Of his character, Thwaites said, "Stu's awesome, he has some fights, he gets the girls, it's awesome fun."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BrentonSoaps_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-BrentonSoaps-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-H.26A_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-H.26A-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following Thwaites' relocation to the United States, he signed on to play the male lead in the 2012 television film <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Lagoon:_The_Awakening" title="Blue Lagoon: The Awakening">Blue Lagoon: The Awakening</a></i> opposite Australian actress <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Evans" title="Indiana Evans">Indiana Evans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The film received generally mixed reviews, with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zap2it" title="Zap2it">Zap2it</a></i> praising both Thwaites and Evans' "(mostly) believable" acting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zap_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Zap-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present:_Breakthrough">2013–present: Breakthrough</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brenton_Thwaites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013–present: Breakthrough">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</div>
<p>In 2013, Thwaites starred in the psychological/supernatural horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Oculus_(film)" title="Oculus (film)">Oculus</a></i>, as the male lead Tim Russell. Production on the film began in October 2012 in <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, and was completed a few weeks later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The film was first released on 5 September 2013, at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2013 Toronto International Film Festival">2013 Toronto International Film Festival</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and received a worldwide theatrical release on 11 April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> His performance as Russell was well received by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indiewire" title="Indiewire">Indiewire</a></i>, which claimed that Thwaites maintained a "credibly frightened demeanor" throughout the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2014, Thwaites briefly returned to Australia for the filming of crime thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Son_of_a_Gun_(film)" title="Son of a Gun (film)">Son of a Gun</a></i>. Primarily shot in <a href="/wiki/Perth" title="Perth">Perth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kalgoorlie" title="Kalgoorlie">Kalgoorlie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Thwaites had to audition for the part of JR "up to 10 times before landing the role." Regarding his character, Thwaites said, "I jumped at the opportunity to audition when this came up and I found the character was a very vulnerable kid amongst strange men that are in some way an inspiration. I just thought that was interesting as a young man myself."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Thwaites received critical acclaim for his portrayal of JR, with <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> contending that he "makes a suitably keen-eyed, clean-scrubbed lead, retaining a kind of porous naivete even as the character gets his blood up"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sydney_Morning_Herald" title="The Sydney Morning Herald">The Sydney Morning Herald</a></i> praising his role as the embattled hero of the plot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The film premiered in Australia on 16 October 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Thwaites appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Maleficent_(film)" title="Maleficent (film)">Maleficent</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Disney%27s_Sleeping_Beauty_characters#Prince_Phillip" title="List of Disney's Sleeping Beauty characters">Prince Phillip</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Angelina_Jolie" title="Angelina Jolie">Angelina Jolie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elle_Fanning" title="Elle Fanning">Elle Fanning</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Additionally he starred in the <a href="/wiki/William_Eubank" title="William Eubank">William Eubank</a>'s science fiction thriller film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Signal_(2014_film)" title="The Signal (2014 film)">The Signal</a></i> as the male lead, Nic Eastman, opposite <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Fishburne" title="Laurence Fishburne">Laurence Fishburne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivia_Cooke" title="Olivia Cooke">Olivia Cooke</a>. The film opened at the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Festival</a> in January 2014, seven months after shooting on location in the <a href="/wiki/Southwestern_United_States" title="Southwestern United States">American Southwestern</a> desert of <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Thwaites received praise by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Plain_Dealer" title="The Plain Dealer">The Plain Dealer</a></i>, noting that his performance in <i>The Signal</i> was "as a compelling presence in the driver's seat as things get freakier and freakier around him."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-signal_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-signal-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>He also starred in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Giver_(film)" title="The Giver (film)">The Giver</a></i> as one of the male leads, Jonas, beside <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Bridges" title="Jeff Bridges">Jeff Bridges</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meryl_Streep" title="Meryl Streep">Meryl Streep</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thegiver_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-thegiver-34">[34]</a></sup> Primarily filmed in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timeout_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-timeout-6">[6]</a></sup> the film was met with generally mixed to negative <a href="/wiki/The_Giver_(film)#Critical_response" title="The Giver (film)">reviews</a>, with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Telegraph</a></i> criticising Thwaites' portrayal for "he [struggled] to give us the layered, conflicted hero this film [needed]".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-giver1_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-giver1-35">[35]</a></sup> However, his portrayal was well received by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huffington_Post" title="Huffington Post">Huffington Post</a></i>, who commended Thwaites' acting<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-giver2_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-giver2-36">[36]</a></sup> and by <i>The Plain Dealer</i>, which noted that the "fast-rising Thwaites...does a nice job with the lead role."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-giver3_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-giver3-37">[37]</a></sup> He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Australians_in_Film" title="Australians in Film">Australians in Film</a> "Breakthrough Award" for his role in <i>The Giver</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-award1_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-award1-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Thwaites starred in <a href="/wiki/Alex_Proyas" title="Alex Proyas">Alex Proyas</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Gods_of_Egypt_(film)" title="Gods of Egypt (film)">Gods of Egypt</a></i>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Butler" title="Gerard Butler">Gerard Butler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Rush" title="Geoffrey Rush">Geoffrey Rush</a>. He played Bek, a human thief.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brenton_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brenton-39">[39]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">Principal photography</a> on the film began on 20 March 2014 at the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Studios_Australia" title="Fox Studios Australia">Fox Studios</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, Australia, and the film was released worldwide on 26 February 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-releasedate_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-releasedate-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>In late 2014, Thwaites was cast in the fifth instalment of the <i><a href="/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean_(film_series)" title="Pirates of the Caribbean (film series)">Pirates of the Caribbean</a></i> franchise, <i><a href="/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean:_Dead_Men_Tell_No_Tales" title="Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Men Tell No Tales">Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Men Tell No Tales</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Thwaites will portray <a href="/wiki/List_of_Pirates_of_the_Caribbean_characters#Henry" title="List of Pirates of the Caribbean characters">Henry</a>, opposite <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Depp" title="Johnny Depp">Johnny Depp</a>, Geoffrey Rush and <a href="/wiki/Javier_Bardem" title="Javier Bardem">Javier Bardem</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pc52_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-pc52-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pc53_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-pc53-46">[46]</a></sup> Shooting of the film commenced in February 2015, on the <a href="/wiki/Gold_Coast,_Queensland" title="Gold Coast, Queensland">Gold Coast</a> of Queensland, Australia<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p5_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-p5-47">[47]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a> announcing that it would be released on 26 May 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blogs.disney.com_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-blogs.disney.com-48">[48]</a></sup></p>
<p>Following <i>Pirates</i>, Thwaites will have a leading role in the 2017 <a href="/wiki/Christian_film_industry" title="Christian film industry">Christian film</a>, <i>An Interview with God</i>, portraying a young journalist, Paul Asher. In this role, he will star opposite <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Award</a>-nominee <a href="/wiki/David_Strathairn" title="David Strathairn">David Strathairn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interviewgod_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-interviewgod-49">[49]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brenton_Thwaites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thwaites has been dating his Gold Coast-based girlfriend Chloe Pacey since 2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baby_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-baby-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baby2_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-baby2-51">[51]</a></sup> after the two had been <a href="/wiki/Roommate" title="Roommate">room mates</a> whilst Thwaites was in Australia filming <i>Pirates</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baby3_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-baby3-52">[52]</a></sup> Their daughter was born in March 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baby4_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-baby4-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-baby5_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-baby5-54">[54]</a></sup></p>
