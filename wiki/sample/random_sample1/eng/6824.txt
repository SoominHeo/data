<p><b>Brett Patrick Dalton</b> (born January 7, 1983 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>) is an American actor. He is best known for playing Grant Ward and <a href="/wiki/Hive_(comics)" title="Hive (comics)">Hive</a> in <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>'s series <i><a href="/wiki/Agents_of_S.H.I.E.L.D." title="Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.">Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Dalton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dalton graduated in 2001 from Westmont High School in <a href="/wiki/Campbell,_California" title="Campbell, California">Campbell, California</a>, where he became interested in acting after auditioning for a production of <i><a href="/wiki/One_Flew_Over_the_Cuckoo%27s_Nest_(play)" title="One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest (play)">One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffPo_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-HuffPo-3">[3]</a></sup> and where he was <a href="/wiki/California_Scholarship_Federation" title="California Scholarship Federation">California Scholarship Federation</a> President and Associated Student Body President. He played the lead in <i>My Favorite Year</i>. After studying at <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a> for his undergraduate degree,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffPo_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-HuffPo-3">[3]</a></sup> Dalton received a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Fine_Arts" title="Master of Fine Arts">Master of Fine Arts</a> from <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffPo_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-HuffPo-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> One of his Yale classmates was Oscar winner <a href="/wiki/Lupita_Nyong%27o" title="Lupita Nyong'o">Lupita Nyong'o</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube.com_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube.com-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Dalton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2012, he was cast in a starring role in the <a href="/wiki/Joss_Whedon" title="Joss Whedon">Joss Whedon</a> TV series, <i>Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D</i> as Agent Grant Ward. The series takes place in the <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Cinematic_Universe" title="Marvel Cinematic Universe">Marvel Cinematic Universe</a>, following character <a href="/wiki/Phil_Coulson" title="Phil Coulson">Agent Phil Coulson</a> and his small team of agents, Dalton's character among them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVLine_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVLine-7">[7]</a></sup> Dalton's other television credits include <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Bloods_(TV_series)" title="Blue Bloods (TV series)">Blue Bloods</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Army_Wives" title="Army Wives">Army Wives</a>,</i> and National Geographic Channel's <i><a href="/wiki/Killing_Lincoln_(film)" title="Killing Lincoln (film)">Killing Lincoln</a></i>, a Tony and Ridley Scott Production. His theatre credits include <i>Passion Play</i>, <i>Romeo and Juliet</i>, and <i>Happy Now?</i> (Yale Repertory); <i>Sweet Bird of Youth</i> and <i>Demon Dreams</i> (Williamstown Theatre Festival); <i>Macbeth</i> (Macbeth) and <i>You Can't Take It With You</i> (Chautauqua Theater Company).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTV_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-CTV-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>On May 16, 2014, Dalton was cast in indie drama film <i>The Tourist</i>, as Eric Lazard, a heartbroken former college football star who gets in over his head with a dangerous Florentine sport and alluring local woman, alongside <a href="/wiki/Stana_Katic" title="Stana Katic">Stana Katic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emily_Atack" title="Emily Atack">Emily Atack</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Dalton voiced the character Mike in <i><a href="/wiki/Until_Dawn" title="Until Dawn">Until Dawn</a></i> (2015), an <a href="/wiki/Action_game" title="Action game">action</a> <a href="/wiki/Survival_horror" title="Survival horror">survival horror</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> developed by Supermassive Games and published by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sony_Computer_Entertainment" title="Sony Computer Entertainment">Sony Computer Entertainment</a> for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">PlayStation 4</a>. In February, Dalton was nominated by the <a href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Video_Game_Trade_Reviewers" title="National Academy of Video Game Trade Reviewers">National Academy of Video Game Trade Reviewers</a> for his performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dalton stars in <a href="/wiki/The_Resurrection_of_Gavin_Stone" title="The Resurrection of Gavin Stone">The Resurrection of Gavin Stone</a>, a film to be released on January 20, 2017 by <a href="/wiki/Walden_Media" title="Walden Media">Walden Media</a> and Vertical Church Films.</p>
