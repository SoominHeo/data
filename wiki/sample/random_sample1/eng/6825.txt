<p><b>Brett Ratner</b> (born March 28, 1969) is an American <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">film producer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_editor" title="Film editor">film editor</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Music_video_director" title="Music video director">music video director</a>. He is known for directing the <a href="/wiki/Rush_Hour_(film_series)" title="Rush Hour (film series)"><i>Rush Hour</i> film series</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Family_Man" title="The Family Man">The Family Man</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Dragon_(film)" title="Red Dragon (film)">Red Dragon</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_The_Last_Stand" title="X-Men: The Last Stand">X-Men: The Last Stand</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Tower_Heist" title="Tower Heist">Tower Heist</a></i>. He was also a producer on the Fox drama series, <i><a href="/wiki/Prison_Break" title="Prison Break">Prison Break</a></i>, as well as the comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Horrible_Bosses" title="Horrible Bosses">Horrible Bosses</a></i> and its 2014 <a href="/wiki/Horrible_Bosses_2" title="Horrible Bosses 2">sequel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Ratner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ratner was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Miami_Beach,_Florida" title="Miami Beach, Florida">Miami Beach, Florida</a>, the son of Marsha Pratts (remarried), a socialite, and Ronald Ratner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He grew up in a "middle-class Jewish family".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hwodg_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-hwodg-5">[5]</a></sup> His father was the son of a wealthy Miami businessman. His mother was born in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, and immigrated to the U.S. in the 1960s with her parents, Fanita and Mario Presman (their families had originally moved to Cuba from Eastern Europe).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nancyjosales.com_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-nancyjosales.com-8">[8]</a></sup> Ratner's mother was sixteen when he was born.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hwodg_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-hwodg-5">[5]</a></sup> Ratner told Aventura Business Monthly in a May, 2011, cover story interview that he "really didn't know" his biological father, and that he considers <a href="/wiki/Alvin_Malnik" title="Alvin Malnik">Alvin Malnik</a>, who opened the famous <a href="/wiki/The_Forge_(restaurant)" title="The Forge (restaurant)">Forge restaurant</a> in Miami Beach, to be his dad, "the one who raised" him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Ratner's biological father was chronically homeless in Miami Beach, a situation which inspired the adult Brett to become the director and board member of the nationwide nonprofit organization Chrysalis, which helps the homeless find work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ratner attended <a href="/wiki/Rabbi_Alexander_S._Gross_Hebrew_Academy" title="Rabbi Alexander S. Gross Hebrew Academy">Rabbi Alexander S. Gross Hebrew Academy</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> elementary school and attended Alexander Muss High School in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nancyjosales.com_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-nancyjosales.com-8">[8]</a></sup> and graduated in 1986 from <a href="/wiki/Miami_Beach_Senior_High_School" title="Miami Beach Senior High School">Miami Beach Senior High School</a>. He is a 1990 graduate of <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2010, he cited the 1980 boxing film <i><a href="/wiki/Raging_Bull" title="Raging Bull">Raging Bull</a></i> as his inspiration to enter the world of film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Ratner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Directing">Directing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Ratner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Directing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ratner had directed several music videos and one film, the 1997 action comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Money_Talks_(1997_film)" title="Money Talks (1997 film)">Money Talks</a></i>, before reaching commercial success with the action-comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Rush_Hour_(1998_film)" title="Rush Hour (1998 film)">Rush Hour</a></i> (1998), starring <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Chan" title="Jackie Chan">Jackie Chan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Tucker" title="Chris Tucker">Chris Tucker</a>, both of whom reunited with Ratner for two sequels, <i><a href="/wiki/Rush_Hour_2" title="Rush Hour 2">Rush Hour 2</a></i> (2001) and <i><a href="/wiki/Rush_Hour_3" title="Rush Hour 3">Rush Hour 3</a></i> (2007). Ratner was under consideration to direct <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men_(film)" title="X-Men (film)">X-Men</a></i> (2000) and <i><a href="/wiki/Superman_Returns" title="Superman Returns">Superman Returns</a></i> (2006), although both were eventually directed by <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Singer" title="Bryan Singer">Bryan Singer</a>. After Singer left the <i>X-Men</i> franchise to direct <i>Superman Returns</i>, Ratner became director of <i><a href="/wiki/X-Men:_The_Last_Stand" title="X-Men: The Last Stand">X-Men: The Last Stand</a></i> (2006).</p>
<p>Ratner directed a Super Bowl ad<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> for <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Las_Vegas" title="Wynn Las Vegas">Wynn Las Vegas</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Steve_Wynn" title="Steve Wynn">Steve Wynn</a> (Wynn stood on top of Wynn Las Vegas for the commercial as he introduced the property).</p>
<p>In May 2008, it was announced that Ratner would be directing the long in-development <i><a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_Cop_(franchise)#Fourth_film" title="Beverly Hills Cop (franchise)">Beverly Hills Cop IV</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> which was confirmed on December 6, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> He also edited the English version of the film <i><a href="/wiki/Kites_(film)" title="Kites (film)">Kites</a></i>, starring Indian superstar <a href="/wiki/Hrithik_Roshan" title="Hrithik Roshan">Hrithik Roshan</a> and Mexican actress <a href="/wiki/B%C3%A1rbara_Mori" title="Bárbara Mori">Bárbara Mori</a> using techniques developed in the critically acclaimed series <i><a href="/wiki/Kung_Faux" title="Kung Faux">Kung Faux</a></i>.</p>
<p>Ratner directed and served as an executive producer on the 2011 <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> comedy-drama television series <i><a href="/wiki/CHAOS_(TV_series)" title="CHAOS (TV series)">CHAOS</a></i>. In the same year, Ratner also directed the ensemble comedy caper <i><a href="/wiki/Tower_Heist" title="Tower Heist">Tower Heist</a></i>.</p>
<p>In May 2011, it was announced that Ratner signed on to direct <i><a href="/wiki/The_39_Clues#Film" title="The 39 Clues">The 39 Clues</a></i>, the live-action adaptation of the young-adult book series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Screen_appearances">Screen appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Ratner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Screen appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ratner was seen on MTV series <i><a href="/wiki/Punk%27d" title="Punk'd">Punk'd</a></i> when <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Jackman" title="Hugh Jackman">Hugh Jackman</a>, who portrays <a href="/wiki/Wolverine_(character)" title="Wolverine (character)">Wolverine</a> in the <i>X-Men</i> films, was the subject of a practical joke that made it appear Ratner's $3.6 million home in Beverly Hills was destroyed by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBQ" title="BBQ">BBQ</a> grill explosion. <a href="/wiki/Ashton_Kutcher" title="Ashton Kutcher">Ashton Kutcher</a> later arrived at his home and <a href="/wiki/Hug" title="Hug">hugged</a> him after Jackman was punk'd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 25, 2007, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting" title="Fox Broadcasting">Fox Broadcasting</a> announced that he, <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Fisher" title="Carrie Fisher">Carrie Fisher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garry_Marshall" title="Garry Marshall">Garry Marshall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jon_Avnet" title="Jon Avnet">Jon Avnet</a> would be the judges for the network's filmmaking-competition, reality TV series, <i><a href="/wiki/On_the_Lot" title="On the Lot">On the Lot</a></i>, which premiered to low ratings in May 2007.</p>
<p>He also appeared as himself in <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i>, in an episode that was shot at his actual Beverly Hills home, according to an interview he did in <i>Aventura Business Monthly</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and in the film <i>Black and White</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Publishing">Publishing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Ratner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Publishing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His publishing company, <a class="external text" href="http://Rat%20Press" rel="nofollow">Rat Press</a>, is re-releasing out of print books about Hollywood. The first three books, released on March 25, 2009, are about <a href="/wiki/Marlon_Brando" title="Marlon Brando">Marlon Brando</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Evans_(producer)" title="Robert Evans (producer)">Robert Evans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Brown" title="Jim Brown">Jim Brown</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Brett Ratner also created his own magazine titled "Ratmag" through celebrity magazine publisher MYMAG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Producing">Producing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Ratner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Producing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ratner produced a remake of <a href="/wiki/Snow_White" title="Snow White">Snow White</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mirror_Mirror_(film)" title="Mirror Mirror (film)">Mirror Mirror</a></i> (2012), based on the screenplay <i>The Brothers Grimm: Snow White</i> by Melisa Wallack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ratner has produced feature films, TV series, and documentaries. He executive produced the 2010 documentary film, <i><a href="/wiki/Catfish_(film)" title="Catfish (film)">Catfish</a></i> and the 2011 TV documentary, <i>American Masters: WoodyAllen – A Documentary</i>. Ratner also produced <i><a href="/wiki/Skyline_(film)" title="Skyline (film)">Skyline</a></i> (2010) and <i><a href="/wiki/Horrible_Bosses" title="Horrible Bosses">Horrible Bosses</a></i> (2011).</p>
<p>In December 2012, Ratner and Australian media mogul <a href="/wiki/James_Packer" title="James Packer">James Packer</a> formed a joint venture, RatPac Entertainment. The firm will produce independent films and co-produce big-budget films with a major studio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> RatPac and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dune_Entertainment" title="Dune Entertainment">Dune Entertainment</a> formed a film investment vehicle, which in September 2013, entered a multi-year, 75-film co-financing partnership with <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>In June 2014, Ratner's RatPac Entertainment and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Class_5_Films&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Class 5 Films (page does not exist)">Class 5 Films</a> acquired the movie rights to the non-fiction article <i>American Hippopotamus</i>, by <a href="/wiki/Jon_Mooallem" title="Jon Mooallem">Jon Mooallem</a>, about the meat shortage in the U.S. in 1910 and the attempts made by Major <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Russell_Burnham" title="Frederick Russell Burnham">Frederick Russell Burnham</a>, Captain <a href="/wiki/Fritz_Joubert_Duquesne" title="Fritz Joubert Duquesne">Fritz Joubert Duquesne</a> and Congressman <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Broussard" title="Robert Broussard">Robert Broussard</a> to import hippopotamuses into the Louisiana bayous and to convince Americans to eat them. The movie will highlight the Burnham - Duquesne rivalry, two famous spies who had previously been under orders to assassinate each other. Ranter, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Norton" title="Edward Norton">Edward Norton</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=William_Migliore&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="William Migliore (page does not exist)">William Migliore</a> will produce this feature film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ratner executive-produced the <a href="/wiki/Rush_Hour_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Rush Hour (U.S. TV series)"><i>Rush Hour</i> TV series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>On January 19, 2017, Ratner received a star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a> for his contributions to the motion picture industry, located at 6801 <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Boulevard" title="Hollywood Boulevard">Hollywood Boulevard</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Academy_Awards">Academy Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brett_Ratner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Academy Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On August 4, 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Motion_Picture_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences">Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences</a> announced Ratner would produce the <a href="/wiki/84th_Academy_Awards" title="84th Academy Awards">84th Academy Awards</a> with <a href="/wiki/Don_Mischer" title="Don Mischer">Don Mischer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> However, Ratner resigned on November 8, 2011<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> after remarking that "rehearsal is for fags".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> Ratner later apologized for his remarks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Murphy" title="Eddie Murphy">Eddie Murphy</a>, who was scheduled to host the ceremony, also resigned in deference to a new production team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Ratner was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Brian_Grazer" title="Brian Grazer">Brian Grazer</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and Murphy was replaced by previous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Oscar</a> host <a href="/wiki/Billy_Crystal" title="Billy Crystal">Billy Crystal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>
