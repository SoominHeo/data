<p><b>Bronson Alcott Pinchot</b> (born May 20, 1959) is an American actor. He is best known for playing <a href="/wiki/Balki_Bartokomous" title="Balki Bartokomous">Balki Bartokomous</a> in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Perfect_Strangers_(TV_series)" title="Perfect Strangers (TV series)">Perfect Strangers</a></i> (1986–93).</p>
<p>He also starred in films, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Risky_Business" title="Risky Business">Risky Business</a></i> (1983), <i><a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_Cop" title="Beverly Hills Cop">Beverly Hills Cop</a></i> (1984), <i><a href="/wiki/True_Romance" title="True Romance">True Romance</a></i> (1993), <i><a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_Cop_III" title="Beverly Hills Cop III">Beverly Hills Cop III</a></i> (1994), <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_My_Party_(film)" title="It's My Party (film)">It's My Party</a></i> (1996), <i><a href="/wiki/Courage_Under_Fire" title="Courage Under Fire">Courage Under Fire</a></i> (1996) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_First_Wives_Club" title="The First Wives Club">The First Wives Club</a></i> (1996), as well as on television series, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Prankster" title="The Prankster">The Prankster</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Lois_%26_Clark:_The_New_Adventures_of_Superman" title="Lois &amp; Clark: The New Adventures of Superman">Lois &amp; Clark: The New Adventures of Superman</a></i>. In 2012, he starred in his own reality series, <i>The Bronson Pinchot Project</i> on the <a href="/wiki/DIY_Network" title="DIY Network">DIY Network</a>.</p>
<p>Pinchot has worked extensively as an audiobook narrator, with over 100 recordings as of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/AudioFile_(magazine)" title="AudioFile (magazine)">AudioFile</a></i> magazine recognized him as Best Voice in Fiction &amp; Classics for his 2010 renderings of <a href="/wiki/Flannery_O%27Connor" title="Flannery O'Connor">Flannery O'Connor</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Everything_That_Rises_Must_Converge" title="Everything That Rises Must Converge">Everything That Rises Must Converge</a></i> (1965), <a href="/wiki/Karl_Marlantes" title="Karl Marlantes">Karl Marlantes</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Matterhorn:_A_Novel_of_the_Vietnam_War" title="Matterhorn: A Novel of the Vietnam War">Matterhorn: A Novel of the Vietnam War</a></i> (2009) and <a href="/wiki/David_Vann_(writer)" title="David Vann (writer)">David Vann</a>'s <i>Caribou Island</i> (2011).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronson_Pinchot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pinchot was born in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His mother, Rosina (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Married_and_maiden_names" title="Married and maiden names">née</a> Asta) was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_American" title="Italian American">Italian American</a> and a typist, and his father, Henry (originally Poncharavsky) was of <a href="/wiki/Russians" title="Russians">Russian</a> descent and a bookbinder, who was born in New York and raised in Paris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> His younger brother, Justin is also an actor. He was raised in <a href="/wiki/Southern_California" title="Southern California">Southern California</a>, where he graduated from <a href="/wiki/South_Pasadena_High_School" title="South Pasadena High School">South Pasadena High School</a> at the top of his class and was appointed valedictorian. He earned a full scholarship to <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>, where he was placed in <a href="/wiki/Morse_College" title="Morse College">Morse College</a> and graduated <a href="/wiki/Latin_honors" title="Latin honors"><i>magna cum laude</i></a>. Pinchot began his Yale career in fine art, but after he was cast in a college play, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Casting_director" title="Casting director">casting director</a> discovered him, which resulted in his film debut, <i><a href="/wiki/Risky_Business" title="Risky Business">Risky Business</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 1999, Pinchot spent a great deal of time in <a href="/wiki/Harford_Township,_Susquehanna_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Harford Township, Susquehanna County, Pennsylvania">Harford, Pennsylvania</a>, restoring the circa 1839 mansion of former Pennsylvania state Senator Edward Jones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Susquehanna_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Susquehanna-8">[8]</a></sup> He purchased six properties in the small, rural town of 1,300<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> "in an effort to revive the town's 19th-century aesthetic".</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronson_Pinchot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Pinchot appeared in several <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature films</a>, such as <i>Risky Business</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_Cop" title="Beverly Hills Cop">Beverly Hills Cop</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_First_Wives_Club" title="The First Wives Club">The First Wives Club</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/True_Romance" title="True Romance">True Romance</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Courage_Under_Fire" title="Courage Under Fire">Courage Under Fire</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_My_Party_(film)" title="It's My Party (film)">It's My Party</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Fisher_Stevens" title="Fisher Stevens">Fisher Stevens</a> asked Pinchot to play Ben Jabituya in the 1986 film <i><a href="/wiki/Short_Circuit_(1986_film)" title="Short Circuit (1986 film)">Short Circuit</a></i>, but Pinchot was fired.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He also played Dennis Kemper in the short-lived <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Sara_(U.S._1985_TV_series)" title="Sara (U.S. 1985 TV series)">Sara</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Balki_Bartokomous" title="Balki Bartokomous">Balki Bartokomous</a> in the long-running <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Situation_comedy" title="Situation comedy">sitcom</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Perfect_Strangers_(TV_series)" title="Perfect Strangers (TV series)">Perfect Strangers</a></i>.</p>
<p>In 2008, Pinchot published his novel <i>The Learners</i> in audio editions, author <a href="/wiki/Chip_Kidd" title="Chip Kidd">Chip Kidd</a>'s followup to <i>The Cheese Monkeys</i>. He also voiced Max, the fully restored Black 1964 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VW_Beetle" title="VW Beetle">VW Beetle</a>, in the 2009 <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen" title="Volkswagen">Volkswagen</a> "Das Auto" campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2009 and 2014, Pinchot narrated over 100 audiobooks, in which he won a number of awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In 2010, Pinchot read the audio version of the novels <i><a href="/wiki/Matterhorn:_A_Novel_of_the_Vietnam_War" title="Matterhorn: A Novel of the Vietnam War">Matterhorn</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Blood_Oath_(Farnsworth_novel)" title="Blood Oath (Farnsworth novel)">Blood Oath</a></i>. For the <a href="/wiki/Blackstone_Audio" title="Blackstone Audio">Blackstone Audio</a> collection <i><a href="/wiki/Patricia_Highsmith" title="Patricia Highsmith">Patricia Highsmith</a>: Selected Novels and Short Stories,</i> he provided a reading of several stories, including Highsmith's memorably creepy novel <i><a href="/wiki/Strangers_on_a_Train_(novel)" title="Strangers on a Train (novel)">Strangers on a Train</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He was chosen by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audible.com" title="Audible.com">Audible.com</a> as their 2010 Narrator of the Year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> On February 12, 2012, Pinchot starred in a home restoration show on <a href="/wiki/DIY_Network" title="DIY Network">DIY Network</a> <i>The Bronson Pinchot Project</i>. The program is based on his hobby of restoring old homes using salvaged materials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
