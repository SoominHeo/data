<p>Year <b>741</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCCXLI</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Common year starting on Sunday">common year starting on Sunday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 741 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=741&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=741&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=741&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Byzantine Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_18" title="June 18">June 18</a> – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Leo_III_the_Isaurian" title="Leo III the Isaurian">Leo III</a> ("the Isaurian") dies of <a href="/wiki/Edema" title="Edema">dropsy</a> at <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>, after a 24-year reign that has saved the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a> and delivered <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a> from the threat of an <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arab</a> conquest. He is succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Constantine_V" title="Constantine V">Constantine V</a>, who invades <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, but has to withdraw to deal with a <a href="/wiki/Civil_war" title="Civil war">civil war</a> in <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Asia Minor</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Artabasdos" title="Artabasdos">Artabasdos</a>, Byzantine general (<i><a href="/wiki/Strategos#Byzantine_use" title="Strategos">strategos</a></i>) of the Armeniac <a href="/wiki/Theme_(Byzantine_district)" title="Theme (Byzantine district)">theme</a>, defeats Constantine V and advances on Constantinople, where he is crowned emperor. He secures the support of the themes of <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Opsikion" title="Opsikion">Opsikion</a>, and abandons Leo's religious policy of <a href="/wiki/Iconoclasm" title="Iconoclasm">iconoclasm</a>. Constantine seeks the support of the <a href="/wiki/Anatolic_Theme" title="Anatolic Theme">Anatolic</a> theme.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=741&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_22" title="October 22">October 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Charles_Martel" title="Charles Martel">Charles Martel</a>, Merovingian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_the_palace" title="Mayor of the palace">mayor of the palace</a>, dies in his palace at <a href="/wiki/Quierzy" title="Quierzy">Quirzy-sur-Oise</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/Picardy" title="Picardy">Picardy</a>). His territories are divided between his adult sons <a href="/wiki/Carloman_(mayor_of_the_palace)" title="Carloman (mayor of the palace)">Carloman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pepin_the_Short" title="Pepin the Short">Pepin the Short</a>, although the <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Frankish Kingdom</a> has had no true king since the death of <a href="/wiki/Theuderic_IV" title="Theuderic IV">Theuderic IV</a> (see <a href="/wiki/737" title="737">737</a>). Lands to the east, including <a href="/wiki/Austrasia" title="Austrasia">Austrasia</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alemannia" title="Alemannia">Alemannia</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavaria</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Vassal" title="Vassal">vassal</a>) go to Carloman, while Pepin receives <a href="/wiki/Neustria" title="Neustria">Neustria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Burgundy" title="Kingdom of Burgundy">Burgundy</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Aquitaine" title="Aquitaine">Aquitaine</a> as a vassal). <a href="/wiki/Grifo" title="Grifo">Grifo</a>, youngest son of Charles, succeeds him as mayor of the palace, and probably receives a strip of land between Neustria en Austrasia.</li>
<li>Pepin the Short marries <a href="/wiki/Bertrada_of_Laon" title="Bertrada of Laon">Bertrada of Laon</a>, daughter of count <a href="/wiki/Charibert_of_Laon" title="Charibert of Laon">Charibert of Laon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESettipani1989_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESettipani1989-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Switzerland">Switzerland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=741&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Switzerland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<ul>
<li>In 741 and 744, documents in the archives of <a href="/wiki/Abbey_of_Saint_Gall" title="Abbey of Saint Gall">St. Gallen Abbey</a> describe the village of <a href="/wiki/Kempraten" title="Kempraten">Kempraten</a> as <i>Centoprato</i>, another document in <a href="/wiki/863" title="863">863</a> as <i>Centiprata</i>, inspired by the Latin name <i><a href="/wiki/Centum_Prata" title="Centum Prata">Centum Prata</a></i>.</li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">nunnery</a> given by the <a href="/wiki/Alemanni" title="Alemanni">Alamannic</a> noblewoman Beata on <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCtzelau" title="Lützelau">Lützelau</a> island is first mentioned. In <a href="/wiki/744" title="744">744</a>, the nunnery is sold to <a href="/wiki/Einsiedeln_Abbey" title="Einsiedeln Abbey">Einsiedeln Abbey</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ufenau" title="Ufenau">Ufenau</a> island in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> is first mentioned in 741 as "Hupinauia", and in 744 as "Ubinauvia" — island of Huppan of Huphan.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=741&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Berber_Revolt#The_Syrian_Expedition" title="Berber Revolt">Great Berber Revolt</a>: Caliph <a href="/wiki/Hisham_ibn_Abd_al-Malik" title="Hisham ibn Abd al-Malik">Hisham ibn Abd al-Malik</a> appoints <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kulthum_ibn_Iyad_al-Qasi" title="Kulthum ibn Iyad al-Qasi">Kulthum ibn Iyad al-Qasi</a> as governor (<i><a href="/wiki/Wali" title="Wali">wali</a></i>) of <a href="/wiki/Ifriqiya" title="Ifriqiya">Ifriqiya</a> (<a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>). A fourth expedition is sent from <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate" title="Umayyad Caliphate">Umayyad Caliphate</a> to crush the rebellion in the <a href="/wiki/Atlas_Mountains" title="Atlas Mountains">Atlas</a> region, but is defeated at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bagdoura" title="Battle of Bagdoura">Battle of Bagdoura</a>, in the plain of the <a href="/wiki/Gharb-Chrarda-B%C3%A9ni_Hssen" title="Gharb-Chrarda-Béni Hssen">Ghrab</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>). The counter-attack of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kharijite" title="Kharijite">Kharijite</a> rebels to the East is successful, but fails to conquer <a href="/wiki/Kairouan" title="Kairouan">Kairouan</a> from the loyalists. A more radical branch of the <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisian</a> Kharijites, (the <a href="/wiki/Sufri" title="Sufri">Sufrists</a>) however, manages to take the city soon after.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_topic">By topic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=741&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: By topic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=741&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_28" title="November 28">November 28</a> – Pope <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_III" title="Pope Gregory III">Gregory III</a> dies at <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, after a 10-year reign. He is succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Zachary" title="Pope Zachary">Zachary</a>, as the 91st <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pope</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Catholic_Encyclopedia_1913_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Catholic_Encyclopedia_1913-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/York_Minster" title="York Minster">York Minster</a> Church is destroyed in a <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">fire</a>. It is later rebuilt as a more impressive structure, containing thirty <a href="/wiki/Altar" title="Altar">altars</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> authorities <a href="/wiki/Decree" title="Decree">decree</a> that <a href="/wiki/Buddhism_in_Japan" title="Buddhism in Japan">Buddhist</a> temples should be established throughout the country (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
