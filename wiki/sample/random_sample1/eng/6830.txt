<p>The <b>Bronx Kill</b> is a narrow <a href="/wiki/Strait" title="Strait">strait</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> delineating the southernmost extent of <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a> and separating it from <a href="/wiki/Randalls_and_Wards_Islands" title="Randalls and Wards Islands">Randalls Island</a>. It connects the <a href="/wiki/Harlem_River" title="Harlem River">Harlem River</a> to the <a href="/wiki/East_River" title="East River">East River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronx_Kill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally, the Bronx Kill was a sizeable waterway, approximately 600 feet (180 m) in width. There were also plans<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (February 2011)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_War" title="United States Department of War">War Department</a> to dredge a 24-foot (7.3 m) deep channel, 480 feet (150 m) in width, to improve navigation and reduce tidal currents. For this reason, in the early 20th century the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Connecting_Railroad" title="New York Connecting Railroad">New York Connecting Railroad</a> built a movable bridge across the Bronx Kill on the approach to the <a href="/wiki/Hell_Gate_Bridge" title="Hell Gate Bridge">Hell Gate Bridge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Similarly, the truss bridge of the <a href="/wiki/Triborough_Bridge" title="Triborough Bridge">Triborough Bridge</a> across the Bronx Kill was designed to be convertible to a lift bridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> However, much of the Bronx Kill was later filled in to expand the parkland on Randalls Island.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Navigation">Navigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronx_Kill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Navigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Bronx Kill offers a venue for kayakers and other human-powered boaters without the worry of larger vessel traffic. Canoe and kayak expeditions through the waterway begin on the Harlem River, near the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Avenue_Bridge_(Manhattan)" title="Third Avenue Bridge (Manhattan)">Third Avenue Bridge</a>. Crossings must be well timed for the tides, to ensure both the correct current direction and sufficient water height: at low water, parts of the Bronx Kill entirely bottom out, revealing muddy stretches, and assorted junk. There is also low <a href="/wiki/Air_draft" title="Air draft">air draft</a> under the Randalls Island Connector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Consequently, no commercial vessels navigate the kill, with local businesses mostly opting instead for road and rail transport, including the <a href="/wiki/Oak_Point_Link" title="Oak Point Link">Oak Point Link</a> along its north bank.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronx_Kill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 21st century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2001, the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Power_Authority" title="New York Power Authority">New York Power Authority</a> offered to construct a pedestrian bridge linking the Bronx with Randalls Island—part of the agency's remuneration to the community for building two new power plants in the <a href="/wiki/South_Bronx" title="South Bronx">South Bronx</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> That plan, however, fell by the wayside when local officials argued that an improved Triborough Bridge path would be sufficient. The state authority instead paid for energy efficiency measures in the borough as a whole, including a <a href="/wiki/Green_roof" title="Green roof">green roof</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Bronx_County_Courthouse" title="Bronx County Courthouse">Bronx County Courthouse</a>.</p>
<p>Following years of negotiation for land with the operators of the Harlem River Rail Yard on the north bank of the Kill,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> the Randalls Island Connector was constructed across the Kill to provide pedestrian and bicycle access between the island and the <a href="/wiki/Port_Morris,_Bronx" title="Port Morris, Bronx">Port Morris</a> neighborhood of the Bronx and to the South Bronx Greenway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The connector opened November 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>A 2006 plan for a <a href="/wiki/Water_park" title="Water park">water park</a>—the first in the nation for a large city—on the northwest corner of Randalls Island was controversial. Announced by the <a href="/wiki/Rudy_Giuliani" title="Rudy Giuliani">Giuliani</a> administration as a $48 million, 15-acre (61,000 m<sup>2</sup>) project, the proposal expanded to encompass 26 acres (110,000 m<sup>2</sup>) at a projected cost of $168 million before being cancelled in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
