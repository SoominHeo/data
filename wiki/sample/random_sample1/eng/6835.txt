<p><b>Bruno Junk</b> (September 27, 1929 in <a href="/wiki/Valga,_Estonia" title="Valga, Estonia">Valga</a> – September 22, 1995 in <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a>) was an <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonian</a> athlete who competed mainly in walking events. He trained in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> and later in <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dynamo_(sports_society)" title="Dynamo (sports society)">Dynamo</a>.</p>
<p>He competed for the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">USSR</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1952_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1952 Summer Olympics">1952 Summer Olympics</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Helsinki" title="Helsinki">Helsinki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> in the 10 kilometre walk where he won the bronze medal. He returned four years later in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1956_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1956 Summer Olympics">1956 Summer Olympics</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> in the 20 kilometre walk, which had now replaced the shorter 10 kilometre walk, where he again won the bronze medal.</p>
