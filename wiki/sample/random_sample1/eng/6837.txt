<p><b>Bruce Howard Kulick</b> (born December 12, 1953) is an American guitarist, musician and since 2000 a member of the band <a href="/wiki/Grand_Funk_Railroad" title="Grand Funk Railroad">Grand Funk Railroad</a>. Previously, Kulick had been a long-time member of the band <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">Kiss</a> (1984-1996). He was also a member of <a href="/wiki/Union_(band)" title="Union (band)">Union</a> with <a href="/wiki/John_Corabi" title="John Corabi">John Corabi</a> from 1997-2002. Bruce is Jewish and also went to Hebrew school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In addition, he is also known for being a former member of the band <a href="/wiki/Blackjack_(American_band)" title="Blackjack (American band)">Blackjack</a> with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bolton" title="Michael Bolton">Michael Bolton</a>, and also played on several Bolton solo albums. (Bolton later co-wrote the Kiss song "<a href="/wiki/Forever_(Kiss_song)" title="Forever (Kiss song)">Forever</a>", from <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_in_the_Shade" title="Hot in the Shade">Hot in the Shade</a></i>).</p>
<p>Kulick has also released several solo albums, in addition to session work with various artists.</p>
<p>Kulick's brother is session guitarist/producer <a href="/wiki/Bob_Kulick" title="Bob Kulick">Bob Kulick</a>, whose performance credits include <a href="/wiki/W.A.S.P." title="W.A.S.P.">W.A.S.P.</a> and Kiss.</p>
<p>"Stay", a Blackjack song co-written by Kulick and Bolton, was sampled by rapper <a href="/wiki/Jay_Z" title="Jay Z">Jay Z</a> for the song "A Dream" off his 2002 album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Blueprint_2:_The_Gift_%26_the_Curse" title="The Blueprint 2: The Gift &amp; the Curse">The Blueprint 2: The Gift &amp; the Curse</a></i> which reached #1 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard</a> charts.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kiss_.281984.E2.80.931996.29">Kiss (1984–1996)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bruce_Kulick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Kiss (1984–1996)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bruce Howard Kulick is best known for being the former lead guitarist for the rock band <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">Kiss</a> from September 1984 to August 1996. Kulick never wore Kiss's facepaint onstage, which they removed in 1983. He's also one of only two members of Kiss (the other being guitarist <a href="/wiki/Mark_St._John" title="Mark St. John">Mark St. John</a>, whom Kulick replaced in 1984) never to wear make-up in the band.</p>
<p>Originally brought in as a touring member after St. John was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Reactive_arthritis" title="Reactive arthritis">Reiter's Syndrome</a>, Kulick played on five Kiss studio albums: <i><a href="/wiki/Asylum_(Kiss_album)" title="Asylum (Kiss album)">Asylum</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Crazy_Nights" title="Crazy Nights">Crazy Nights</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_in_the_Shade" title="Hot in the Shade">Hot in the Shade</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Revenge_(Kiss_album)" title="Revenge (Kiss album)">Revenge</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Carnival_of_Souls:_The_Final_Sessions" title="Carnival of Souls: The Final Sessions">Carnival of Souls: The Final Sessions</a></i>; he also appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Alive_III" title="Alive III">Alive III</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KISS_Unplugged" title="KISS Unplugged">KISS Unplugged</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Music_Legends_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Music_Legends-4">[4]</a></sup> The song "I Walk Alone", from <i><a href="/wiki/Carnival_of_Souls:_The_Final_Sessions" title="Carnival of Souls: The Final Sessions">Carnival of Souls: The Final Sessions</a></i>, is the only Kiss track to feature Kulick as lead vocalist. To this day, Kulick is featured on more than 20 Kiss releases.</p>
<p>When the original members of the band regrouped, starting in 1995 with the <i>MTV Unplugged</i> special that carried over into re-adopting their make-up and costuming for the <a href="/wiki/Alive/Worldwide_Tour" title="Alive/Worldwide Tour">Alive/Worldwide Tour</a> in 1996, Kulick and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Singer" title="Eric Singer">Eric Singer</a> were paid weekly during the tour; while "sidelined" from Kiss, both were allowed to do other projects so long as Simmons and Stanley okayed them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Kulick officially left Kiss in December 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Kulick was with the band longer than any member aside from the original foursome and drummer Eric Singer. (Singer has done three discontinuous stints with the band, each much shorter than Kulick's.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> When <a href="/wiki/Ace_Frehley" title="Ace Frehley">Ace Frehley</a> again left Kiss (in 2002, after the <a href="/wiki/Kiss_Farewell_Tour" title="Kiss Farewell Tour">Kiss Farewell Tour</a>), Kulick was not asked to rejoin, as Simmons and Stanley thought that Tommy Thayer (former <a href="/wiki/Black_%27n_Blue" title="Black 'n Blue">Black 'n Blue</a> guitarist and Kiss tour manager) could capture ( copy ) Frehley's persona better than Kulick; however, Kulick continued to work with Kiss after his departure, contributing uncredited guitar work to the albums <i><a href="/wiki/You_Wanted_the_Best,_You_Got_the_Best!!" title="You Wanted the Best, You Got the Best!!">You Wanted the Best, You Got the Best!!</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Psycho_Circus" title="Psycho Circus">Psycho Circus</a></i></p>
<p>Kulick's brother <a href="/wiki/Bob_Kulick" title="Bob Kulick">Bob Kulick</a> also played with Kiss as a session guitarist, and in addition appeared on <a href="/wiki/Paul_Stanley" title="Paul Stanley">Paul Stanley</a>'s 1978 solo album. Bob Kulick was also a member of Stanley's touring band for his 1989 club tour.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Union">Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bruce_Kulick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Union_(band)" title="Union (band)">Union</a> was formed from recently released musician Bruce Kulick, <a href="/wiki/John_Corabi" title="John Corabi">John Corabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brent_Fitz" title="Brent Fitz">Brent Fitz</a>, and Jamie Hunting in 1996. Corabi and Kulick wrote what would become the first <a href="/wiki/Union_(Union_album)" title="Union (Union album)">Union</a> (1998) album (self titled) and proceeded with a nationwide acoustic tour, to promote the album, featuring just Corabi and Kulick (both on guitar). Soon the full band went on a world tour headlining small venues in support of the Union album. The second album "Live in the Galaxy" was a live recording with 2 acoustic tracks recorded in a mobile studio in 1999. The third and final Union album, <a href="/wiki/The_Blue_Room_(album)" title="The Blue Room (album)">The Blue Room</a> (2000), was a more polished sound than the first studio CD. Union never officially called it quits (playing live shows every so often with guest drummers and bass players), but with Kulick playing along with <a href="/wiki/Grand_Funk_Railroad" title="Grand Funk Railroad">Grand Funk Railroad</a>, Corabi playing with many bands, most notably <a href="/wiki/Ratt" title="Ratt">Ratt</a>, and both members being tied up in solo projects, <a href="/wiki/Union_(band)" title="Union (band)">Union</a> will probably never return to write new studio material..</p>

<p>Kulick is the lead guitarist for <a href="/wiki/Grand_Funk_Railroad" title="Grand Funk Railroad">Grand Funk Railroad</a>, with whom he has been playing since 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solo_work">Solo work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bruce_Kulick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Solo work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kulick has released three solo albums, <i>Audio Dog</i> in 2001, <i>Transformer</i> in 2003, and most recently <i>BK3</i>, which was released on February 2, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bruce_Kulick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Good Rats.</p>
<p>In the late 1970s, following the release of <a href="/wiki/Meat_Loaf" title="Meat Loaf">Meat Loaf</a>'s first album <i><a href="/wiki/Bat_Out_of_Hell" title="Bat Out of Hell">Bat Out of Hell</a></i>, Kulick played rhythm guitar/lead guitar on tour with Meat Loaf's band alongside his brother Bob on lead guitar/rhythm guitar. In the tour introductions, Meat Loaf introduced Bruce and Bob as 'Pretty Boy' and 'Killer' respectively.</p>
<p>Before joining Meat Loaf, Bruce Kulick toured for a couple months as the lead guitarist of the <a href="/wiki/Andrea_True" title="Andrea True">Andrea True</a> Connection.</p>
<p>Kulick has also appeared on all album releases by Eric Singer's solo project ESP (<a href="/wiki/Eric_Singer_Project" title="Eric Singer Project">Eric Singer Project</a>): <i>Lost and Spaced</i> (1998), <i>ESP</i> (1999), and <i>Eric Singer Project: Live in Tokyo</i> (2006). Kulick also appeared on the DVD <i>Eric Singer Project: Live at the Marquee</i> (2006), which was filmed live in Australia.</p>
<p>Kulick appears on the <a href="/wiki/Lordi" title="Lordi">Lordi</a> March 2006 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Arockalypse" title="The Arockalypse">The Arockalypse</a></i>, playing lead guitar on the song "<a href="/wiki/It_Snows_in_Hell" title="It Snows in Hell">It Snows in Hell</a>".</p>
<p>Kulick appears on <a href="/wiki/Paul_Stanley" title="Paul Stanley">Paul Stanley</a>'s 2006 album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live_To_Win" title="Live To Win">Live To Win</a></i>, playing bass.</p>
<p>In 2006, Kulick performed on the compilation <i>Butchering the Beatles</i>, playing lead on the song "Drive My Car".</p>
<p>Kulick cut a guest guitar solo for the track "The Edge of the Razor" (featured on the album <i>Emotional Coma</i>) by Swedish metal group Lion's Share.</p>
<p>Kulick was a guest star along with Paul Stanley at <a href="/wiki/Rock_%27n_Roll_Fantasy_Camp" title="Rock 'n Roll Fantasy Camp">Rock 'n Roll Fantasy Camp</a> in New York City (Aug. 31 – September 3, 2003).</p>
<p>In his 2004 feature interview with Paul Stanley, Kulick discussed his very first recorded project from 1974, something called KKB. It featured performances by his childhood friends Mike Katz and Guy Bois (the other K and B of KKB, respectively) and he likened its sound to that of <a href="/wiki/Cream_(band)" title="Cream (band)">Cream</a>. Kulick only recently found the master tape from those sessions and issued it via limited edition CD, available during his appearances at <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">Kiss</a> expos and via his website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In a follow-up interview with Paul Stanley, Kulick also discussed his lifelong love of <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a> and its historical similarities to <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">Kiss</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Kulick is one of the characters in the book <i><a href="/wiki/Sex_Tips_from_Rock_Stars" title="Sex Tips from Rock Stars">Sex Tips from Rock Stars</a></i> by Paul Miles, published by <a href="/wiki/Omnibus_Press" title="Omnibus Press">Omnibus Press</a> in July 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
