<p>Year <b>756</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCCLVI</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Leap year starting on Thursday">leap year starting on Thursday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 756 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=756&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=756&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Byzantine_Empire">Byzantine Empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=756&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Byzantine Empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Byzantine%E2%80%93Bulgarian_wars#Constantine_V.27s_wars" title="Byzantine–Bulgarian wars">Byzantine–Bulgarian War</a>: Emperor <a href="/wiki/Constantine_V" title="Constantine V">Constantine V</a> builds a series of <a href="/wiki/Fortification" title="Fortification">fortifications</a> along the Byzantine frontier on the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a>, and starts settling Christian <a href="/wiki/Armenians" title="Armenians">Armenians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syrian</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Thracesian_Theme" title="Thracesian Theme">Thracesian Theme</a>. In response, <a href="/wiki/Kormisosh" title="Kormisosh">Kormisosh</a>, ruler (<i><a href="/wiki/Khagan" title="Khagan">khagan</a></i>) of the <a href="/wiki/First_Bulgarian_Empire" title="First Bulgarian Empire">Bulgarian Empire</a>, demands the payment of <a href="/wiki/Tribute" title="Tribute">tribute</a>. Constantine refuses, and the <a href="/wiki/Bulgars" title="Bulgars">Bulgars</a> raid into Thrace, reaching the <a href="/wiki/Anastasian_Wall" title="Anastasian Wall">Anastasian Wall</a> stretching between the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sea_of_Marmara" title="Sea of Marmara">Sea of Marmara</a> (near the <a href="/wiki/Rural%E2%80%93urban_fringe" title="Rural–urban fringe">outskirts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Marcellae_(756)" title="Battle of Marcellae (756)">Battle of Marcellae</a>: Constantine V sends a Byzantine expeditionary force (500 ships and 6,000 <a href="/wiki/Cavalry" title="Cavalry">cavalry</a>) to <a href="/wiki/Thrace" title="Thrace">Thrace</a>, and defeats the Bulgars around the <a href="/wiki/Danube_Delta" title="Danube Delta">Danube Delta</a> and near the fortress city of <a href="/wiki/Markeli" title="Markeli">Markeli</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>). Kormisosh is forced to accept a <a href="/wiki/Peace_treaty" title="Peace treaty">peace treaty</a>, and confirms the existing frontier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Due to the constant political crisis, the Bulgarian Empire is on the verge of destruction. Kormisosh is deposed during a palace <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d'état">coup</a> and succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Vinekh" title="Vinekh">Vinekh</a>, a member of the <a href="/wiki/Vokil" title="Vokil">Vokil</a> clan.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=756&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Aistulf" title="Aistulf">Aistulf</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombards</a> again threatens Rome with a view to making it his capital, but the <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Franks</a> under <a href="/wiki/Pepin_the_Short" title="Pepin the Short">Pepin III</a> ("the Short") arrives with his sons <a href="/wiki/Charlemagne" title="Charlemagne">Charles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carloman_I" title="Carloman I">Carloman</a>, defeats Aistulf, and confers the <a href="/wiki/Donation_of_Pepin" title="Donation of Pepin">Donation of Pepin</a>, which establishes the <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a> (including lands of <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pentapolis" title="Pentapolis">Pentapolis</a>). Pepin has taken territory that legally belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>; he gives them to <a href="/wiki/Pope_Stephen_II" title="Pope Stephen II">Pope Stephen II</a>.</li>
<li>Aistulf dies in a hunting accident, and is succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Desiderius" title="Desiderius">Desiderius</a> as king of the Lombards. Ex-king <a href="/wiki/Ratchis" title="Ratchis">Ratchis</a> attempts unsuccessfully to seize the throne, but is opposed by Stephen II.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Galla_Gaulo" title="Galla Gaulo">Galla Gaulo</a> is deposed, blinded and exiled. He is succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Domenico_Monegario" title="Domenico Monegario">Domenico Monegario</a> as the sixth <a href="/wiki/Doge_of_Venice" title="Doge of Venice">doge</a> of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Venice" title="Republic of Venice">Venice</a>. During his reign the Venetians become maritime traders.</li>
<li>May – Prince <a href="/wiki/Abd_al-Rahman_I" title="Abd al-Rahman I">Abd al-Rahman I</a> and his followers capture the city of <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a> without violence. He defeats <a href="/wiki/Yusuf_ibn_%27Abd_al-Rahman_al-Fihri" title="Yusuf ibn 'Abd al-Rahman al-Fihri">Yusuf ibn 'Abd al-Rahman al-Fihri</a> in a battle for control of the Muslim-ruled parts of the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a> (<a href="/wiki/Al-Andalus" title="Al-Andalus">al-Andalus</a>). Abd al-Rahman establishes the <a href="/wiki/Emirate_of_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Emirate of Córdoba">Emirate of Córdoba</a>. During his reign trade and <a href="/wiki/Islamic_culture" title="Islamic culture">culture</a> flourish, along with the construction of <a href="/wiki/Islamic_architecture" title="Islamic architecture">Islamic architecture</a> (including the <a href="/wiki/Mosque%E2%80%93Cathedral_of_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Mosque–Cathedral of Córdoba">Great Mosque of Córdoba</a>).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=756&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Battle of Newanbirig: Kings <a href="/wiki/%C3%93engus_I" title="Óengus I">Óengus I</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Picts" title="Picts">Picts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eadberht_of_Northumbria" title="Eadberht of Northumbria">Eadberht of Northumbria</a> attack King <a href="/wiki/Dumnagual_III_of_Alt_Clut" title="Dumnagual III of Alt Clut">Dumnagual III</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Strathclyde" title="Kingdom of Strathclyde">Strathclyde</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Dumbarton_Castle" title="Dumbarton Castle">Dumbarton Castle</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>). However, Eadberht's entire force is subsequently wiped out, probably by the <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Britons" title="Celtic Britons">Britons</a>.</li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Cuthred_of_Wessex" title="Cuthred of Wessex">Cuthred of Wessex</a> dies after a 16-year reign. He is succeeded by his distant kinsman <a href="/wiki/Sigeberht_of_Wessex" title="Sigeberht of Wessex">Sigeberht</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Abbasid_Caliphate">Abbasid Caliphate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=756&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Abbasid Caliphate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ibn_al-Muqaffa%27" title="Ibn al-Muqaffa'">Ibn al-Muqaffa'</a>, Muslim writer and thinker, is tortured at <a href="/wiki/Basra" title="Basra">Basra</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>), on orders from Caliph <a href="/wiki/Al-Mansur" title="Al-Mansur">al-Mansur</a>. His limbs are severed and he is thrown, still alive, into a burning oven (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="China">China</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=756&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: China">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>January – <a href="/wiki/An_Lushan_Rebellion" title="An Lushan Rebellion">An Lushan Rebellion</a>: The Chinese eastern capital of <a href="/wiki/Luoyang" title="Luoyang">Luoyang</a> falls to the 200,000 army of the rebel general <a href="/wiki/An_Lushan" title="An Lushan">An Lushan</a>, who defeats loyalist forces under <a href="/wiki/Feng_Changqing" title="Feng Changqing">Feng Changqing</a>. The rebels cross the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_River" title="Yellow River">Yellow River</a>, and march on to capture the cities <a href="/wiki/Chenliu" title="Chenliu">Chenliu</a> and Yingyang (modern <a href="/wiki/Zhengzhou" title="Zhengzhou">Zhengzhou</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henan" title="Henan">Henan</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Yongqiu" title="Battle of Yongqiu">Battle of Yongqiu</a>: A <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang</a> garrison (2,000 men), under <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Xun_(Tang_dynasty)" title="Zhang Xun (Tang dynasty)">Zhang Xun</a>, successfully defends their fortress against the rebel army at <a href="/wiki/Qi_County,_Kaifeng" title="Qi County, Kaifeng">Yongqiu</a>. Zang achieves a victory after a 4-month <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a>, and prevents the rebels from capturing the fertile Tang territory south of the <a href="/wiki/Huai_River" title="Huai River">Huai River</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_5" title="February 5">February 5</a> – An Lushan declares himself emperor at Luoyang, establishing a new empire, called the <a href="/wiki/Yan_(An%E2%80%93Shi)" title="Yan (An–Shi)">Great Yan</a>. He pushes on towards the primary Tang capital at <a href="/wiki/Chang%27an" title="Chang'an">Chang'an</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Xi%27an" title="Xi'an">Xi'an</a>). An decides to seize southern China, to cut off loyalist reinforcements. Meanwhile, numerous soldiers join the rebellion.</li>
<li>May – Emperor <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xuanzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xuanzong of Tang">Xuan Zong</a> hires 4,000 Muslim <a href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">mercenaries</a> to help defend Chang'an against the rebels. Loyalist forces take defensible positions in the mountain passes, but chancellor <a href="/wiki/Yang_Guozhong" title="Yang Guozhong">Yang Guozhong</a> give orders to leave their posts. An Lushan crushes the Tang troops, leaving the capital wide open.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_14" title="July 14">July 14</a> – Xuan Zong flees (along with the <a href="/wiki/Court_(royal)" title="Court (royal)">imperial court</a>) the capital of Chang'an for <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a>, as rebel forces advance through the <a href="/wiki/Tongguan_County" title="Tongguan County">Tongguan</a> Pass toward the city. Meanwhile, An Lushan is ailing, perhaps with <a href="/wiki/Diabetes_mellitus" title="Diabetes mellitus">diabetes</a>. He is nearly blind and suffers from extreme <a href="/wiki/Irritation" title="Irritation">irascibility</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_15" title="July 15">July 15</a> – Xuan Zong is ordered by his <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Guards_(Tang_dynasty)" title="Imperial Guards (Tang dynasty)">Imperial Guards</a> to execute Yang Guozhong, by forcing him to commit suicide or face a mutiny. He permits his consort <a href="/wiki/Yang_Guifei" title="Yang Guifei">Yang Guifei</a> to be strangled by his chief <a href="/wiki/Eunuch" title="Eunuch">eunuch</a>. An Lushan has also other members of the emperor's family killed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_12" title="August 12">August 12</a> – Xuan Zong abdicates the throne after a 44-year reign. He is succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Suzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Suzong of Tang">Su Zong</a>, as emperor of the <a href="/wiki/Tang_dynasty" title="Tang dynasty">Tang Dynasty</a>. He hires 22,000 Muslim mercenaries to reinforce his decimated army at <a href="/wiki/Lingwu" title="Lingwu">Lingzhou</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=756&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_4" title="June 4">June 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Sh%C5%8Dmu" title="Emperor Shōmu">Emperor Shōmu</a> (retired since <a href="/wiki/749" title="749">749</a>) dies at <a href="/wiki/Nara,_Nara" title="Nara, Nara">Nara</a>. His wife <a href="/wiki/Empress_K%C5%8Dmy%C5%8D" title="Empress Kōmyō">Kōmyō</a> dedicates over 600 items to the <a href="/wiki/Daibutsu" title="Daibutsu">Great Buddha</a>, and donates large sums of money in the <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%8Ds%C5%8Din" title="Shōsōin">Shōsō-in treasure</a> (<a href="/wiki/Warehouse" title="Warehouse">storehouse</a>) in <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Ddai-ji" title="Tōdai-ji">Tōdai-ji</a>.</li>
</ul>

<ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/Hisham_I_of_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Hisham I of Córdoba">Hisham I</a>, Muslim <a href="/wiki/Emir" title="Emir">emir</a> of <a href="/wiki/Emirate_of_C%C3%B3rdoba" title="Emirate of Córdoba">Córdoba</a> (d. <a href="/wiki/796" title="796">796</a>)</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Nikephoros_(Caesar)" title="Nikephoros (Caesar)">Nikephoros</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/Constantine_V" title="Constantine V">Constantine V</a> (or <a href="/wiki/758" title="758">758</a>)</li>
</ul>

<li><a href="/wiki/June_4" title="June 4">June 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Sh%C5%8Dmu" title="Emperor Shōmu">Shōmu</a>, emperor of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> (b. <a href="/wiki/701" title="701">701</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_15" title="July 15">July 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/Yang_Guifei" title="Yang Guifei">Yang Guifei</a>, consort of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Xuanzong_of_Tang" title="Emperor Xuanzong of Tang">Xuan Zong</a> (b. <a href="/wiki/719" title="719">719</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Aistulf" title="Aistulf">Aistulf</a>, duke of <a href="/wiki/Friuli" title="Friuli">Friuli</a> and king of the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombards</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cuthred_of_Wessex" title="Cuthred of Wessex">Cuthred</a>, king of <a href="/wiki/Wessex" title="Wessex">Wessex</a> (approximate date)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dantidurga" title="Dantidurga">Dantidurga</a>, founder of the <a href="/wiki/Rashtrakuta_dynasty" title="Rashtrakuta dynasty">Rashtrakuta Empire</a> (b. <a href="/wiki/735" title="735">735</a>)</li>
