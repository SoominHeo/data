<p><b>Kanesha Nichole Brookes</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (born October 5, 1984),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YHIF_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-YHIF-3">[3]</a></sup> better known by her stage name <b>Brooke Valentine</b>, is an American singer, actress, and model. Her single "<a href="/wiki/Girlfight_(song)" title="Girlfight (song)">Girlfight</a>" peaked on U.S. music charts in 2005, paving the way for her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Chain_Letter_(album)" title="Chain Letter (album)">Chain Letter</a></i> released via Subliminal Entertainment imprint on <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Records" title="Virgin Records">Virgin Records</a> and went on to sell more than 290,000 units worldwide. After a first attempt at a comeback, Brooke officially returned to the spotlight in 2012 with two singles "Forever" and the Adult R&amp;B Top 40 hit "Don't Wanna Be In Love".</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brooke_Valentine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1984.E2.80.932004:_Early_life_and_career_beginnings">1984–2004: Early life and career beginnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brooke_Valentine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1984–2004: Early life and career beginnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Valentine was born in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">[4]</a></sup> She started her musical career as a member of the female group <b>Best Kept Secret</b>. To pursue a solo career, she moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a> with producer and Subliminal Entertainment CEO Deja the Great and signed to <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Records" title="Virgin Records">Virgin Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YHIF_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-YHIF-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2004-06:_Chain_Letter">2004-06: <i>Chain Letter</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brooke_Valentine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2004-06: Chain Letter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Brooke Valentine made her debut with the hit single, "<a href="/wiki/Girlfight_(song)" title="Girlfight (song)">Girlfight</a>", which featured <a href="/wiki/Lil_Jon" title="Lil Jon">Lil Jon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Big_Boi" title="Big Boi">Big Boi</a>, which peaked at #23 on the Billboard Hot 100 and #13 on the R&amp;B chart. It succeeded internationally, peaking in the Top 50 in Australia and Ireland, and Top 40 in New Zealand and the United Kingdom. She then released her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Chain_Letter_(album)" title="Chain Letter (album)">Chain Letter</a></i> in 2005<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-4">[4]</a></sup> which debuted at #16 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and #3 on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_R%26B/Hip_Hop_Albums" title="Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums">Top R&amp;B/Hip Hop Albums</a> charts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_albums_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_albums-5">[5]</a></sup> A follow-up single "<a href="/wiki/Long_as_You_Come_Home" title="Long as You Come Home">Long as You Come Home</a>" peaked at #71 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">US R&amp;B/Hip-Hop</a> chart, while a third (the ballad "Covergirl") was released digitally but not sent to radio.</p>
<p>Later that year, collaborated with rappers <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yo-Yo_(rapper)" title="Yo-Yo (rapper)">Yo Yo</a> on the single "Boogie Oogie Oogie" for the <i><a href="/wiki/Roll_Bounce" title="Roll Bounce">Roll Bounce</a></i> soundtrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006.E2.80.9309:_Physical_Education">2006–09: <i>Physical Education</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brooke_Valentine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2006–09: Physical Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, Valentine set work on her second studio album, <i>Physical Education</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She released the set's lead single "<a href="/wiki/D-Girl_(DopeGirl)" title="D-Girl (DopeGirl)">D-Girl</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Pimp_C" title="Pimp C">Pimp C</a> that year, peaking at #92 on the Billboard R&amp;B/Hip Hop Singles chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-D_Girl_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-D_Girl-8">[8]</a></sup> A follow-up single "<a href="/wiki/Pimped_Out" title="Pimped Out">Pimped Out</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Dem_Franchize_Boyz" title="Dem Franchize Boyz">Dem Franchize Boyz</a> was released but failed to catch on.</p>
<p>Eventually, the <i>Physical Education</i> project was put on hold due to <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Records" title="Virgin Records">Virgin Records</a>’ merger with <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a> in 2008. Subliminal Entertainment CEO Deja the Great then acquired the masters and the contract rights from Virgin Records, and material from the project was released independently on the <i>Physical Education Mixtape</i> in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>She later said in an interview that the project was a "paper album" with Virgin, as it was a forced effort due to her being on the label's timeline for a new release and being set up with all the "hot" producers at the time. In addition to not turning in the complete album to the label, Valentine was also dealing with the death of her cousin during the "D-Girl" single release, all of which added to the album not being initially released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.932013:_Forever_.2F_Love_Letters_EP">2010–2013: <i>Forever / Love Letters EP</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brooke_Valentine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–2013: Forever / Love Letters EP">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a 2010 interview, Valentine said that she took a break and has been writing music for other artists, and was now working on new material.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>That year, she also gave birth to her first child, a son named London. In 2012, she did an interview with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Essence_Magazine" title="Essence Magazine">Essence Magazine</a> discussing raising her son's cerebral palsy diagnosis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> She also penned an open letter to him with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ebony_Magazine" title="Ebony Magazine">Ebony Magazine</a>, calling him her "tiny hero."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Adding to her stage name the alias B. Valentine, she released a music video for the song "Insanity" in January 2012, shot entirely on an Apple iPad and directed by Subliminal CEO Deja the Great. She announced a new album titled <i>Forever</i> set for release later in the year, led by a new single "Forever" which premiered on February 14, 2012. "Rub It In" (a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Tyrese" title="Tyrese">Tyrese</a>) was announced as the set's second single<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and released as a digital single, but the album was later pushed back in lieu of a new project.</p>
<p>In 2013, she released a new EP titled <i>Love Letters</i> which garnered a positive response.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> A single from the set, "Don't Wanna Be In Love" was released and peaked at #29 on the Billboard Adult R&amp;B chart, marking her first chart appearance in six years.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014-present:_.23CRAIG_and_new_projects">2014-present: <i>#CRAIG and new projects</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brooke_Valentine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014-present: #CRAIG and new projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, she joined the L.A.-spin off cast of <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s hit reality series <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop">Love &amp; Hip Hop</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> which did not get picked up. Since then, she's been working on her second studio album.</p>
<p>In 2016, she released a series of new digital singles, with "Grow Up" on April 29, "#CRAIG" (produced by Yonni)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> on July 2, and "Games" on October 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
