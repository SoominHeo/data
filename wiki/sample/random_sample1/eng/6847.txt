<p><b>Brussels Airport - Zaventem</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> located beneath <a href="/wiki/Brussels_Airport" title="Brussels Airport">Brussels Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flemish_Brabant" title="Flemish Brabant">Flemish Brabant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. The station opened in 1958 on the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Belgian_railway_line_36C&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Belgian railway line 36C (page does not exist)">Lines 36C</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Belgian_railway_line_25N&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Belgian railway line 25N (page does not exist)">Lines 25N</a>. The train services are operated by <a href="/wiki/National_Railway_Company_of_Belgium" title="National Railway Company of Belgium">National Railway Company of Belgium</a> (NMBS/SNCB).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brussels_National_Airport_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station was opened in 1958 in the old airport building, mainly for the <a href="/wiki/Expo_58" title="Expo 58">universal exposition</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>. With the opening of a new airport terminal in 1994, the railway station was moved in 1998 to a new location inside the new building. Following the opening of the new <a href="/wiki/Diabolo_project" title="Diabolo project">Diabolo project</a>, the station was extended in June 2012 to enhance the comfort and passengers experience. The trains arrive and leave from level -2, with ticket desks on level -1. Passengers can go to the different floors in the airport and railway station by using a set of escalators and elevators, a couple of meters away of the platforms.</p>
<p>There is no dedicated car park but Brussels Airport carparks are available a short walk away, without any discount for rail customers.</p>
<p>The railway station connects the airport directly to the main Brussels stations, including the international <a href="/wiki/Brussels-South_railway_station" title="Brussels-South railway station">Brussels-South</a> and centrally-located <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brussels-Central_railway_station" title="Brussels-Central railway station">Brussels-Central</a> stations. The railway stations of <a href="/wiki/Leuven" title="Leuven">Leuven</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mechelen" title="Mechelen">Mechelen</a> can also be directly reached from the airport. From all these stations, one can easily travel to every city of Belgium; there are direct trains to the cities of <a href="/wiki/Antwerp" title="Antwerp">Antwerp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruges" title="Bruges">Bruges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ghent" title="Ghent">Ghent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halle,_Belgium" title="Halle, Belgium">Halle</a> or <a href="/wiki/Mons" title="Mons">Mons</a>.</p>
<p>Since December 2014, the Benelux train allows passengers to travel directly to <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Airport_Schiphol" title="Amsterdam Airport Schiphol">Amsterdam Airport Schiphol</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/2016_Brussels_bombings" title="2016 Brussels bombings">bomb attacks</a> of 22 March 2016 the station was closed and all trains were either deviated or cancelled until further notice. Shuttle bus services were put in place between <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zaventem_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zaventem railway station (page does not exist)">Zaventem station</a> and the airport. The airport station itself was undamaged, but it is connected to a part of the airport building that was severely damaged during the attacks.</p>
<p>With the <a href="/wiki/Schuman-Josaphat_tunnel" title="Schuman-Josaphat tunnel">Schuman-Josaphat tunnel</a> entering into service on 4 April 2016, a new connection was established to connect Brussels Airport directly to the stations of the EU quarter—<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schuman_station" title="Schuman station">Brussels-Schuman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brussels-Luxembourg_railway_station" title="Brussels-Luxembourg railway station">Brussels-Luxembourg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> This brought the travel time between the Airport and the EU quarter to about 15 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The tunnel was originally scheduled to open on 12 December 2015, but it was delayed due to the <a href="/wiki/Brussels_lockdown" title="Brussels lockdown">Brussels lockdown</a>. When it eventually opened in April 2016, it could not yet be used to its full potential as the airport station remained closed following the Brussels bombings.</p>
<p>Train service to the airport station resumed on 22 April 2016, temporarily using the entrance/exit of the old airport station. On 8 June 2016, service via the regular station resumed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Train_services">Train services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brussels_National_Airport_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Train services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station has direct services to most major Belgian cities, as well as a regular service to the Netherlands. The only major town that hasn't got a direct line to the airport yet is Liège. Passengers traveling to Liège can connect at Leuven station.</p>
<p>The station is served by the following services:</p>
<ul>
<li>High speed service: (<i>Thalys</i>) Brussels Airport - Brussels - Paris (summer seasonal)</li>
<li>Intercity services (IC-35) Amsterdam - The Hague - Rotterdam - Roosendaal - Antwerp - Brussels Airport - Brussels</li>
<li>Intercity services (IC-06) Tournai - Ath - Brussels - Brussels Airport</li>
<li>Intercity services (IC-06A) Mons - Brussels - Brussels Airport</li>
<li>Intercity services (IC-08) Antwerpen - Mechelen - Brussels Airport - Leuven (- Hasselt)</li>
<li>Intercity services (IC-17) Brussels Airport - Brussels-Luxembourg - Namur - Dinant</li>
<li>Intercity services (IC-23) Ostend - Bruges - Kortrijk - Zottegem - Brussels - Brussels Airport - Brussels - Ghent (- Bruges)</li>
<li>Intercity services (IC-23A) Bruges - Ghent - Brussels - Brussels Airport (weekdays)</li>
<li>Intercity services (IC-23A) Ghent - Brussels - Brussels Airport (weekends)</li>
<li>Intercity services (IC-27) Brussels Airport - Brussels-Luxembourg - Nivelles - Charleroi</li>
<li>Intercity services (IC-29) De Panne - Ghent - Aalst - Brussels - Brussels Airport - Leuven (- Landen)</li>
</ul>
