<p><b>Brussels-North</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr"><i>Bruxelles-Nord</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <span lang="nl" xml:lang="nl"><i>Brussel-Noord</i></span>) is one of the three major <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway stations</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>; the other two are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brussels-Central_railway_station" title="Brussels-Central railway station">Brussels Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brussels-South_railway_station" title="Brussels-South railway station">Brussels South</a>. The station's bilingual French-Dutch name is generally translated to "Brussels North".</p>
<p>Every regular domestic and international train (except <a href="/wiki/Thalys" title="Thalys">Thalys</a>) passing here also has a planned stop.</p>
<p>Brussels North also is the end point of the underground <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brussels_trams" title="Brussels trams">tram</a> north-south axis and an important node of <a href="/wiki/Brussels_Intercommunal_Transport_Company" title="Brussels Intercommunal Transport Company">MIVB/STIB</a> and <a href="/wiki/De_Lijn" title="De Lijn">De Lijn</a> bus lines. More than 30 regional bus lines depart from here, as do international <a href="/wiki/Eurolines" title="Eurolines">Eurolines</a> coach services.</p>
<p>The station has 200,000 passengers per week, mainly commuters. It opens up to the Noordruimte/Espace Nord business district and is a starting place for inner-city visits.</p>
<p>Just like the other bigger stations in Brussels, Brussels-North is characterized by late maintenance and old-fashioned design. Meanwhile, there are plans to renovate the building, platforms and pedestrian subways. These long-due works should start at the end of 2008, when the renovation of Brussels-Central will be finished.</p>
<p>The station is located in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Schaerbeek" title="Schaerbeek">Schaerbeek</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, in the middle of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Quarter_(Brussels)" title="Northern Quarter (Brussels)">Northern Quarter</a> <a href="/wiki/Central_business_district" title="Central business district">business district</a>, with several corporation headquarters such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgacom_Towers" title="Belgacom Towers">Belgacom Towers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rogier_Tower" title="Rogier Tower">Rogier Tower</a> and others, government offices and Flemish ministries.</p>
<p>Just next to the station is <a href="/wiki/Rue_d%27Aerschot" title="Rue d'Aerschot">Aarschot Street</a>, an area of prostitution "behind windows".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brussels-North_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ancienne_Gare_du_Nord_Bruxelles.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="670" data-file-width="1060" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Ancienne_Gare_du_Nord_Bruxelles.jpg/220px-Ancienne_Gare_du_Nord_Bruxelles.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Ancienne_Gare_du_Nord_Bruxelles.jpg/330px-Ancienne_Gare_du_Nord_Bruxelles.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Ancienne_Gare_du_Nord_Bruxelles.jpg/440px-Ancienne_Gare_du_Nord_Bruxelles.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>The first station in Brussels was Groendreef / Allée Verte, where in 1835 the first train on a public railway departed on the European continent. This was replaced in March 1846 by the first Brussels-North station, designed by F. Coppens and situated on Rogier Place. It had 27 tracks.</p>
<p>In 1952 a new Brussels-North station was built to link with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North-South_connection" title="North-South connection">North-South Connection</a>. The old station was razed in 1955.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail_lines">Rail lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brussels-North_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rail lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brussels North has 12 platforms. These passenger lines join in the station:</p>
<ul>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North-South_connection" title="North-South connection">Line 0</a>, the Brussels North-South connection</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Belgian_railway_line_25" title="Belgian railway line 25">Line 25</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Belgian_railway_line_27&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Belgian railway line 27 (page does not exist)">27</a> to <a href="/wiki/Antwerpen-Centraal_railway_station" title="Antwerpen-Centraal railway station">Antwerp Central station</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Belgian_railway_line_36" title="Belgian railway line 36">Line 36</a> to <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge-Guillemins_railway_station" title="Liège-Guillemins railway station">Liège-Guillemins</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Belgian_railway_line_50" title="Belgian railway line 50">Line 50</a> to <a href="/wiki/Gent-Sint-Pieters_railway_station" title="Gent-Sint-Pieters railway station">Gent-Sint-Pieters</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Belgian_railway_line_161" title="Belgian railway line 161">Line 161</a> to Namur</li>
</ul>
<p>Few trains originate from Brussels North. Instead, most trains through Brussels depart from Brussel South, some from <a href="/wiki/Schaarbeek_railway_station" title="Schaarbeek railway station">Schaarbeek</a>.</p>
