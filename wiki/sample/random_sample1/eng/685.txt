<p>Year <b>770</b> (<b><a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">DCCLXX</a></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Common year starting on Monday">common year starting on Monday</a> (link will display the full calendar) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>. The denomination 770 for this year has been used since the early medieval period, when the <a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a> <a href="/wiki/Calendar_era" title="Calendar era">calendar era</a> became the prevalent method in Europe for naming years.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=770&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_place">By place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=770&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=770&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Charlemagne" title="Charlemagne">Charlemagne</a> signs a peace treaty with Duke <a href="/wiki/Tassilo_III,_Duke_of_Bavaria" title="Tassilo III, Duke of Bavaria">Tassilo III of Bavaria</a>, and marries the <a href="/wiki/Lombards" title="Lombards">Lombard</a> princess <a href="/wiki/Desiderata_of_the_Lombards" title="Desiderata of the Lombards">Desiderata</a> (daughter of King <a href="/wiki/Desiderius" title="Desiderius">Desiderius</a>). He travels to the Lombard court at <a href="/wiki/Pavia" title="Pavia">Pavia</a> to conclude arrangements. <a href="/wiki/Pope_Stephen_III" title="Pope Stephen III">Pope Stephen III</a> opposes the marriage, and protests about a <a href="/wiki/Francia" title="Francia">Frankish</a>-Lombard <a href="/wiki/Political_alliance" title="Political alliance">alliance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hedeby" title="Hedeby">Hedeby</a>, an important trading settlement, in the <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a>-northern <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> borderland is founded (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=770&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Alhred_of_Northumbria" title="Alhred of Northumbria">Alhred of Northumbria</a> takes an interest in continental <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionary</a> activities, and sends <a href="/wiki/Willehad" title="Willehad">Willehad</a> to <a href="/wiki/Frisia" title="Frisia">Frisia</a> in modern-day <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> (approximate date).</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Abbasid_Caliphate">Abbasid Caliphate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=770&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Abbasid Caliphate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul>
<li>Caliph <a href="/wiki/Al-Mansur" title="Al-Mansur">al-Mansur</a> orders the closing of the <a href="/wiki/Canal_of_the_Pharaohs" title="Canal of the Pharaohs">Canal of the Pharaohs</a> (<a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>). The only remaining land routes to <a href="/wiki/Transshipment" title="Transshipment">transship</a> camel caravans' goods are from <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a> to ports on the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a>, or the northern Byzantine termini of the <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a>.</li>
</ul>
