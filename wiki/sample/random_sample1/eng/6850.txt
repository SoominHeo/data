<p><b>Brno City Theatre</b> (<a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a>: <b>Městské divadlo Brno</b>, further in text <b>MdB</b>) is a repertoire <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brno" title="Brno">Brno</a>, Czech Republic, that focuses on dramatic and music production, mainly musical theatre. The building of the theatre is located on Lidická street in town square Brno-center. It has two acting areas – dramatical scene (capacity 365 places) and musical theatre scene (capacity 680 places).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> As of 2011, the manager and director of the theatre is <a href="/wiki/Stanislav_Mo%C5%A1a" title="Stanislav Moša">Stanislav Moša</a>, art manager of the musical ensemble is <a href="/wiki/Petr_Gazd%C3%ADk" title="Petr Gazdík">Petr Gazdík</a> and <a href="/wiki/Operetta" title="Operetta">operetta</a> manager is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Igor_Ond%C5%99%C3%AD%C4%8Dek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Igor Ondříček (page does not exist)">Igor Ondříček</a>.</p>
<p>The MdB is one of the theatres with very good ensemble and wide audience.</p>
<p>There are many different art pieces in both of its musical and dramatic repertoire, lot of space is dedicated for many new works, which are often written directly for MdB. The theatre also has its own orchestra, so the audience can experience live <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scenic_music" title="Scenic music">scenic music</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brno_City_Theatre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The former <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Svobodn%C3%A9_divadlo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Svobodné divadlo (page does not exist)">Svobodné divadlo</a> (Free Theatre) on Falkensteiner Street was founded closely after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, in summer 1945, by people around director <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Milan_P%C3%A1sek&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Milan Pásek (page does not exist)">Milan Pásek</a>, supported by professor <a href="/wiki/Ji%C5%99%C3%AD_Kroha" title="Jiří Kroha">Jiří Kroha</a>. Since its beginning it hosted mainly young artists, which sought to found second theatre in the city (the first one was <i>Mahenovo divadlo</i> (<a href="/wiki/Mahen_Theatre" title="Mahen Theatre">Mahen Theatre</a>), where they could perform plays by contemporary writers. The theatre has changed its name by the time: Městské a oblastní divadlo (Municipal and Regional Theatre), <a href="/wiki/Kraj" title="Kraj">Krajsk</a>é oblastní divadlo (County and regional theatre) and in 1962 <i>Divadlo bratří Mrštíků</i> (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mr%C5%A1t%C3%ADk_Brothers_Theatre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mrštík Brothers Theatre (page does not exist)">Mrštík Brothers Theatre</a>).</p>
<p>In 1988, by decision of a new regulation, the theatre was merged with some quite genre different theatres – satirical theatre <i>Večerní Brno</i> and puppet theatre <i>Loutkové divadlo Radost</i>.</p>
<p>After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Velvet_revolution" title="Velvet revolution">Velvet revolution</a> the theatre was renamed the <i>Městské divadlo Brno</i> (Municipal Theatre Brno) and in 1990 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jan_Kolegar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jan Kolegar (page does not exist)">Jan Kolegar</a> was elected as its director. Jan Moša, former producer, dramatist and since 1990 the artistic director of drama, became the director in 1992. He was responsible for the reconstruction of insufficient places at former cinema on Lidická street, where the theatre now resides.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the 1990s the declining scene, that had great distress of the audience, has become one of the most successful theatres not only in Brno, but throughout the country.</p>
<p>In 1995 the MdB went through <a href="/wiki/Building_restoration" title="Building restoration">reconstruction</a>, mainly modification of the theatre scene itself. In addition to changes of building the ensemble of theatre was also renewed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In the 1990s the MdB has focused mainly on quality musical theatre production, using graduates of the musical acting classes at the <a href="/wiki/Jan%C3%A1%C4%8Dek_Academy_of_Music_and_Performing_Arts" title="Janáček Academy of Music and Performing Arts">Janáček Academy of Music and Performing Arts</a>.</p>
