<p>The <b>Brittany football select</b> is the professional football team of <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. It is administered by the Breton Football Association (BFA). It is neither affiliated to <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> nor <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a> but is characterised as one of the six <a href="/wiki/Celtic_nations" title="Celtic nations">Celtic nations</a>. Its games are held under the auspices of the <a href="/wiki/French_Football_Federation" title="French Football Federation">French Football Federation</a> and FIFA Regulations<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Amateur football in Brittany is administered by both the <i>Ligue de Bretagne</i> and the <i>Ligue Atlantique</i>, which are regional associations within the French FA.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Squad_selection">Squad selection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brittany_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Squad selection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brittany plays unofficial internationals. BFA has a pool of around 100 players in the first three professional divisions to choose from, some of them with proven international football experience. Brittany's <a href="/wiki/St%C3%A9phane_Guivarc%27h" title="Stéphane Guivarc'h">Stéphane Guivarc'h</a> won the 1998 World Cup with France.</p>
<p>Brittany notably held Cameroon to a 1–1 draw before the 1998 World Cup finals, featuring <a href="/wiki/Paul_Le_Guen" title="Paul Le Guen">Paul Le Guen</a>. Six games had to be called off between 1999 and 2005 because of the then French FA administration, which contradicted its own rules. Fortunately, the head of the French FA administration changed and BFA finally recovered in order to fully resume its activities in 2008. Its latest game was played versus Mali (1–0) on 28 May 2013.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celtic_Cup_Ambitions">Celtic Cup Ambitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brittany_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Celtic Cup Ambitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>BFA offered other Celtic nations to join in a Celtic Nations Championship between 1985 and 1987. On 9 September 1985, BFA Secretary Fañch Gaume, visiting Cardiff on the eve of a World Cup qualifier between Wales and Scotland, sounded both the <a href="/wiki/Football_Association_of_Wales" title="Football Association of Wales">FA of Wales</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Football_Association" title="Scottish Football Association">Scottish FA</a> about participation to a <a href="/wiki/Nations_Cup_(football)" title="Nations Cup (football)">Celtic Nations Cup</a>. Informal conversations were followed up by correspondence and further personal exchanges, whenever the opportunity presented itself before international games.</p>
<p>While Wales showed a genuine interest, the offer finally fell on barren ground with Scotland. Rejection letters from the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Football_Association" title="Scottish Football Association">SFA</a> for non-entry stated the difficulties to find suitable dates but, as the Sports Editor of "The Glasgow Herald" Jim Reynolds presented it: "It is just two years since England and Scotland broke up the British International Championship by calling a halt to regular games featuring Northern Ireland and Wales. So, the chances of a Celtic Championship involving Scotland must be remote." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brittany recently renewed its claims to organise and take part in the new <a href="/wiki/Nations_Cup_(football)" title="Nations Cup (football)">Celtic Nations Cup</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> with the Republic of Ireland, Scotland and Wales by 2015 at the earliest or 2017.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eligible_players_2015-16">Eligible players 2015-16</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brittany_national_football_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Eligible players 2015-16">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>To be included in the Breton squad, according to FIFA national teams rules, it is eligible a player: - born into one of five historical Breton departments. - with parents from Brittany. - with grandparents from Brittany - grown up in Brittany since the age of seven.</p>
