<p><i><b>Bro Gozh ma Zadoù</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Breton_language" title="Breton language">Breton</a>: <span lang="br" xml:lang="br"><i>Old Land of My Fathers</i></span>) is the <a href="/wiki/Anthem" title="Anthem">anthem</a> of <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>, sometimes presented as the "<a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> although it has no official status. It is sung to the same tune as that of the national anthem of <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hen_Wlad_Fy_Nhadau" title="Hen Wlad Fy Nhadau">Hen Wlad Fy Nhadau</a></i>, and has similar words. The <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornish</a> anthem, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bro_Goth_Agan_Tasow" title="Bro Goth Agan Tasow">Bro Goth Agan Tasow</a></i>, is also sung to the same tune.</p>
<p>The Breton lyrics are the creation of <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Jaffrennou" title="François Jaffrennou">François Jaffrennou</a> in 1897, and the music was that composed by <a href="/wiki/James_James" title="James James">James James</a>, of <a href="/wiki/Pontypridd" title="Pontypridd">Pontypridd</a>, Wales, for <i>Hen Wlad Fy Nhadau</i>. The new song was first published in 1898, and circulated as <i>Henvelidigez</i> ("Adaptation"). It was chosen as national anthem (and a song to celebrate friendship between the Welsh and Bretons) in 1903, at a Congress of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_R%C3%A9gionaliste_Bretonne" title="Union Régionaliste Bretonne">Union Régionaliste Bretonne</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Lesneven" title="Lesneven">Lesneven</a>. <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Duhamel" title="Maurice Duhamel">Maurice Duhamel</a> adapted it for the <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a>, and it was first recorded by <a href="/wiki/Path%C3%A9_Records" title="Pathé Records">Pathé</a> in 1910.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lyrics">Lyrics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bro_Gozh_ma_Zado%C3%B9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lyrics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div>
<table class="multicol" role="presentation" style="border-collapse: collapse; padding: 0; border: 0; background:transparent; width:100%;">
<tr>
<td style="width:50%; text-align:left; vertical-align:top;">
<dl>
<dt>Breton<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></dt>
</dl>
<p>Ni, Breizhiz a galon, karomp hon gwir vro!<br/>
Brudet eo an Arvor dre ar bed tro-dro.<br/>
Dispont 'kreiz ar brezel, hon tadoù ken mat,<br/>
A skuilhas eviti o gwad.<br/></p>
<p>O! Breizh, ma bro, me 'gar ma bro.<br/>
Tra ma vo mor 'vel mur 'n he zro,<br/>
Ra vezo digabestr ma bro!<i><br/></i></p>
<p>Breizh, douar ar Sant Kozh, douar ar varzhed,<br/>
N'eus bro all a garan kement 'barzh ar bed.<br/>
Pep menez, pep traoñienn d'am c'halon zo ker,<br/>
Eno 'kousk meur a Vreizhad taer!<br/></p>
<p>Ar Vretoned 'zo tud kalet ha kreñv.<br/>
N'eus pobl ken kalonek a-zindan an neñv.<br/>
Gwerz trist, son dudius a ziwan enno.<br/>
O! pegen kaer ez out, ma bro!<br/></p>
<p>Mard eo bet trec'het Breizh er brezelioù bras,<br/>
He yezh a zo bepred ken bev ha biskoazh,<br/>
He c'halon virvidik a lamm c'hoazh 'n he c'hreiz.<br/>
Dihunet out bremañ, ma Breizh!<br/></p>
</td>
<td style="width:50%; text-align:left; vertical-align:top;">
<dl>
<dt>English</dt>
</dl>
<p>Us, Bretons by heart, love our true country!<br/>
<a href="/wiki/Armorica" title="Armorica">Armorica</a>, famous throughout the world.<br/>
Without any fear in battle, our so good fathers,<br/>
Shed their blood for you.<br/></p>
<p>Brittany, my country that I love,<br/>
As long as the sea, like a wall surrounds you,<br/>
Shall my country be free!</p>
<p>Brittany, land of the Old Saints, land of the bards,<br/>
There is no other country that I love as much.<br/>
Every mountain, every glen is the dearest to my heart,<br/>
Many an heroic Breton are resting there.<br/></p>
<p>The Bretons are a strong and tough people.<br/>
No people under the skies is as brave as them,<br/>
Whether they may sing a sad <a href="/wiki/Gwerz" title="Gwerz">gwerz</a> or a nice song.<br/>
Oh, my so beautiful country!<br/></p>
<p>If in the past Brittany may have been defeated in battle,<br/>
Her language will always remain well alive,<br/>
Her flaming heart is still beating in her chest :<br/>
You are now awakened, my dear Brittany!</p>
</td>
</tr>
