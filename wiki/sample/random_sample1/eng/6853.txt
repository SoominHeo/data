<p><b>Brianne Sidonie Desaulniers</b> (born October 1, 1989),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> known professionally as <b>Brie Larson</b>, is an American actress, director, and singer. Born in <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a>, Larson was home-schooled before she studied acting at the <a href="/wiki/American_Conservatory_Theater" title="American Conservatory Theater">American Conservatory Theater</a>. She began her acting career in television, appearing as a regular on the 2001 sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Raising_Dad" title="Raising Dad">Raising Dad</a></i>, for which she was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Young_Artist_Award" title="Young Artist Award">Young Artist Award</a>.</p>
<p>As a teenager, Larson had brief roles in the 2004 films <i><a href="/wiki/13_Going_on_30" title="13 Going on 30">13 Going on 30</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Sleepover_(film)" title="Sleepover (film)">Sleepover</a></i>. Her performance in the comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Hoot_(film)" title="Hoot (film)">Hoot</a></i> (2006) was praised, and she subsequently played supporting roles in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Greenberg_(film)" title="Greenberg (film)">Greenberg</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Scott_Pilgrim_vs._the_World" title="Scott Pilgrim vs. the World">Scott Pilgrim vs. the World</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/21_Jump_Street_(film)" title="21 Jump Street (film)">21 Jump Street</a></i> (2012), and <i><a href="/wiki/Don_Jon" title="Don Jon">Don Jon</a></i> (2013). From 2009 to 2011, Larson featured as a rebellious teenager in the television series <i><a href="/wiki/United_States_of_Tara" title="United States of Tara">United States of Tara</a></i>.</p>
<p>Larson's breakthrough role came with the independent drama <i><a href="/wiki/Short_Term_12" title="Short Term 12">Short Term 12</a></i> (2013), for which she received critical acclaim. Further success came in 2015 when she starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Room_(2015_film)" title="Room (2015 film)">Room</a></i>, an acclaimed drama based on <a href="/wiki/Emma_Donoghue" title="Emma Donoghue">Emma Donoghue</a>'s novel of the <a href="/wiki/Room_(novel)" title="Room (novel)">same name</a>. She won several awards for her portrayal of a troubled mother kidnap victim in the film, including the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Actress">Academy Award</a>, <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Leading_Role" title="BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role">BAFTA Award</a>, <a href="/wiki/Critics%27_Choice_Movie_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Actress">Critic's Choice Award</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Motion_Picture_%E2%80%93_Drama" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Motion Picture – Drama">Golden Globe Award</a> and <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award_for_Outstanding_Performance_by_a_Female_Actor_in_a_Leading_Role" title="Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Leading Role">Screen Actors Guild Award</a> for Best Actress.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Larson was born in <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a>, the first daughter of Heather (née Edwards) and Sylvain Desaulniers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her parents were chiropractors who ran a practice together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Her parents divorced when she was a child, and she moved to Los Angeles with her mother and her sister. She studied acting at the <a href="/wiki/American_Conservatory_Theater" title="American Conservatory Theater">American Conservatory Theater</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Before that she was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home-school" title="Home-school">home-schooled</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brie_Larson_on_Biogs.com_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brie_Larson_on_Biogs.com-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her paternal grandparents were <a href="/wiki/Franco-Manitoban" title="Franco-Manitoban">French-Canadian from Manitoba</a>. Her paternal grandmother, Gabrielle Desaulniers, died in December 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> One of her paternal 4th great-grandmothers was described as a <a href="/wiki/Michilimackinac" title="Michilimackinac">Mackinac Island</a>-born <a href="/wiki/Red_hair" title="Red hair">redheaded</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%A9tis_people_(Canada)" title="Métis people (Canada)">Métis</a> woman fluent in <a href="/wiki/Canadian_French" title="Canadian French">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canadian_English" title="Canadian English">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cree_language" title="Cree language">Cree</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michif_language" title="Michif language">Michif</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Mixed_language" title="Mixed language">mixed language</a> of Cree and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9tis_French" title="Métis French">Métis French</a>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Growing up, Larson's first language was <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brie stated in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Glamour_(magazine)" title="Glamour (magazine)">Glamour</a></i> that she chose "Larson" as her stage name from her <a href="/wiki/Swedes" title="Swedes">Swedish</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> great-grandmother's maiden name, as her surname was too difficult to pronounce. She stated on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></i> that she took her stage name from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kirsten_Larson" title="Kirsten Larson">Kirsten Larson</a>, an <a href="/wiki/American_Girl" title="American Girl">American Girl</a> doll she received as a child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hines_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hines-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span></h3>
<p>Larson's first job was performing in <a href="/wiki/Sketch_comedy" title="Sketch comedy">sketches</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Jay_Leno" title="The Tonight Show with Jay Leno">The Tonight Show with Jay Leno</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Soon after, she was cast in the television series <i>Schimmel</i>, which never aired because its star, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Schimmel" title="Robert Schimmel">Robert Schimmel</a>, was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a> shortly before filming was to begin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> After it was "on hold" for ten months, <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> canceled the show. Larson later auditioned for a new show for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_WB_Television_Network" title="The WB Television Network">The WB</a> called <i>In Your Dreams</i>, and landed the role of <a href="/wiki/Bob_Saget" title="Bob Saget">Bob Saget</a>'s character's younger daughter, Emily. The show was selected for the 2001–02 fall season, and was renamed <i><a href="/wiki/Raising_Dad" title="Raising Dad">Raising Dad</a></i>. It was then canceled after 27 episodes. Larson was also cast in the <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">pilot</a> for the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Hope_%26_Faith" title="Hope &amp; Faith">Hope &amp; Faith</a></i>, but she and some other cast members were dropped after the unaired pilot.</p>

<p>In 2003, she starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Beverley_Mitchell" title="Beverley Mitchell">Beverley Mitchell</a> on the Disney Channel original movie <i>Right on Track</i>. In July 2004, she co-starred with a large cast of teen actors in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Sleepover_(film)" title="Sleepover (film)">Sleepover</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> She also had a small role in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/13_Going_on_30" title="13 Going on 30">13 Going on 30</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Larson appeared in the 2006 film <i><a href="/wiki/Hoot_(film)" title="Hoot (film)">Hoot</a></i>, opposite actors <a href="/wiki/Logan_Lerman" title="Logan Lerman">Logan Lerman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cody_Linley" title="Cody Linley">Cody Linley</a>. Her voice is also featured in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Farce_of_the_Penguins" title="Farce of the Penguins">Farce of the Penguins</a></i> as a teenage penguin. She played Angie in the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Comedy-drama" title="Comedy-drama">comedy-drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Remember_the_Daze" title="Remember the Daze">Remember the Daze</a></i> and also appeared in the 2008 short film <i>The Babysitter</i>, written and directed by <a href="/wiki/David_H._Steinberg" title="David H. Steinberg">David H. Steinberg</a>. She appeared in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Ghost_Whisperer" title="Ghost Whisperer">Ghost Whisperer</a></i> in January 2008. She also has a role as Kate in the 2008 film, <i><a href="/wiki/Tanner_Hall_(film)" title="Tanner Hall (film)">Tanner Hall</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Francesca_Gregorini" title="Francesca Gregorini">Francesca Gregorini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tatiana_von_F%C3%BCrstenberg" title="Tatiana von Fürstenberg">Tatiana von Fürstenberg</a>. Larson played the character Kate Gregson on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_channel)" title="Showtime (TV channel)">Showtime</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/United_States_of_Tara" title="United States of Tara">United States of Tara</a></i>. The show premiered on January 18, 2009 and was not renewed after its third season. The season finale aired on June 20, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2012, Larson played Molly in the <a href="/wiki/21_Jump_Street_(film)" title="21 Jump Street (film)">film adaptation</a> of the popular 1980s TV show <i><a href="/wiki/21_Jump_Street" title="21 Jump Street">21 Jump Street</a></i>, co-starring with <a href="/wiki/Jonah_Hill" title="Jonah Hill">Jonah Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Channing_Tatum" title="Channing Tatum">Channing Tatum</a>. In 2013, she guest-starred as Rachel in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Community_(TV_series)" title="Community (TV series)">Community</a></i> titled "<a href="/wiki/Herstory_of_Dance" title="Herstory of Dance">Herstory of Dance</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and again in the episode "<a href="/wiki/Analysis_of_Cork-Based_Networking" title="Analysis of Cork-Based Networking">Analysis of Cork-Based Networking</a>" the following year. In 2013, she also starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Short_Term_12" title="Short Term 12">Short Term 12</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> She appeared as a guest on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Late_Late_Show_with_Craig_Ferguson" title="The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson">The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson</a></i> several times between 2010 and 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, Larson played the sister of <a href="/wiki/Amy_Schumer" title="Amy Schumer">Amy Schumer</a>'s character in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Trainwreck_(film)" title="Trainwreck (film)">Trainwreck</a></i>. She also starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Room_(2015_film)" title="Room (2015 film)">Room</a></i>, a film adapted from the <a href="/wiki/Room_(novel)" title="Room (novel)">novel of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Emma_Donoghue" title="Emma Donoghue">Emma Donoghue</a>. The film played at the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">Toronto International Film Festival</a>, and began its theatrical release on October 16, 2015. It has received widespread critical acclaim, particularly for Larson's performance, which earned her several awards, including the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Actress">Academy Award for Best Actress</a> as well as Golden Globe &amp; BAFTA Awards.</p>
<p>In early 2016, Larson was cast in <i><a href="/wiki/Kong:_Skull_Island" title="Kong: Skull Island">Kong: Skull Island</a></i>, along with <a href="/wiki/Samuel_L._Jackson" title="Samuel L. Jackson">Samuel L. Jackson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hiddleston" title="Tom Hiddleston">Tom Hiddleston</a> with filming taking place in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queensland,_Australia" title="Queensland, Australia">Queensland, Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> In the following month, it was confirmed that Larson would reunite with her <i><a href="/wiki/Short_Term_12" title="Short Term 12">Short Term 12</a></i> director, <a href="/wiki/Destin_Daniel_Cretton" title="Destin Daniel Cretton">Destin Daniel Cretton</a>, for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Glass_Castle_(film)" title="The Glass Castle (film)">The Glass Castle</a>.</i> The Lionsgate’s script is an adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Jeannette_Walls" title="Jeannette Walls">Jeannette Walls'</a> memoir. It tells the story of a successful young woman who was raised by dysfunctional and nonconformist parents. Her world gets turned upside down when they move to New York to be near her. <a href="/wiki/Woody_Harrelson" title="Woody Harrelson">Woody Harrelson</a>, is already on board as the woman’s alcoholic father with Naomi Watts joins the project as Larson's eccentric mother. The film begins production in West Virginia on May 2016, which Gil Netter is producing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Later on in June, <i>The Hollywood Reporter</i> reported that Larson was the favorite to star as <a href="/wiki/Carol_Danvers" title="Carol Danvers">Carol Danvers / Captain Marvel</a> in the upcoming Marvel Studios film <i>Captain Marvel</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Marvel later announced her casting at a panel at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con" title="San Diego Comic-Con">San Diego Comic-Con</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Brie_Larson_cropped.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1085" data-file-width="800" height="231" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Brie_Larson_cropped.jpg/170px-Brie_Larson_cropped.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Brie_Larson_cropped.jpg/255px-Brie_Larson_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Brie_Larson_cropped.jpg/340px-Brie_Larson_cropped.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>

Larson in 2010</div>

<p>Larson has launched a career as a musician, singer and songwriter, and after releasing her debut album in a major record label in 2005, and has been performing in live concerts throughout the United States since then. A second album came to production and was completed but never released. Most recently, Larson revealed plans to release an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a>, but that also never reached the public. She did not give up on her career as a musician, but it is not known when she will release new songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-myspace1_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-myspace1-28">[28]</a></sup> Her last public appearance as a singer was in 2010, when she performed "Black Sheep", along with the band <a href="/wiki/Metric_(band)" title="Metric (band)">Metric</a> in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Scott_Pilgrim_vs._the_World" title="Scott Pilgrim vs. the World">Scott Pilgrim vs. the World</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her interest in being a singer started in early 2003, when she began to promote her songs through her personal website and was already showing interest in releasing an album. The first leaked was "Go Goodbye", a song about "two great people who just aren't great together", in her own words<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and along with "Invisible Girl", were the tracks that led Larson to sign a record deal some time after with <a href="/wiki/Casablanca_Records" title="Casablanca Records">Casablanca Records</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ame_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-ame-31">[31]</a></sup> a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Records" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a> imprint. Both songs were produced and written with the help of Michael Binikos and Craig Bartock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sty_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-sty-32">[32]</a></sup> In 2003, she leaked another track, "Not a Freak" and this time there was even a music video for it, which was available for purchase online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In late spring of 2004, she removed all the songs on her website due to Casablanca's contract restrictions and was in the process of recording new songs for her first album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>In October of that same year, her willingness to release an album was coming closer and "She Said", the first single from her yet-to-be-released debut album at that time was available to listen via her official website<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> gaining an official release in <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> in December<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> and was sent to radios across the country in the following month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fmqb_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-fmqb-37">[37]</a></sup> Despite having been the 99th best-selling CD single in the country in 2005, according to <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">SoundScan</a> and peaking at number 31 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> Single Sales, the song failed to reach the Top 40 of the most played songs in the country, receiving low airplay. She also recorded live performances for AOL Sessions on October 11, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<p>Her only released album, <i><a href="/wiki/Finally_Out_of_P.E." title="Finally Out of P.E.">Finally Out of P.E.</a></i>, went on sale in late 2005 and sold 3,500 copies in U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fried_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-fried-39">[39]</a></sup> Before its release, her record label released a DVD featuring "She Said" music video, the making of it and her AOL Sessions performances. The entire album was focused in teenage audiences and that became clear while she was playing in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teen_People" title="Teen People">Teen People</a>'s Rock in Shop Tour, went on tour with <a href="/wiki/Jesse_McCartney" title="Jesse McCartney">Jesse McCartney</a> and received a lot of airplay from children-oriented radios like <a href="/wiki/Radio_Disney" title="Radio Disney">Radio Disney</a> and Radio KOL. The music video for "She Said", directed by Chris Applebaum,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> showed a story about a bored fast food restaurant worker who was aiming to sing and, that way, "finding herself". She also sang "Hope Has Wings", the song from the credits of the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Barbie_and_the_Magic_of_Pegasus" title="Barbie and the Magic of Pegasus">Barbie and the Magic of Pegasus</a></i>. A music video for the song appears as a bonus feature on the movie's DVD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2006, she recorded a song slightly different from her previous works for the soundtrack of the <i><a href="/wiki/Hoot_(film)" title="Hoot (film)">Hoot</a></i> movie, "Coming Around", co-written with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Brautbar" title="Jacques Brautbar">Jacques Brautbar</a> and Rami Perlman,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> vocalist and guitarist of a <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>-based independent band. On March 1, 2006, Larson performed an acoustic set of new songs at L.A.'s famous Viper Room.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> The songs played were "Dear Universe", "Powershift", "Stilts and the Titanic", "Superstition", a cover of <a href="/wiki/Indigo_Girls" title="Indigo Girls">Indigo Girls</a>' "Closer to Fine" and "Falling Into History", a track from her album. From the new songs, the only one to be leaked in a studio recording was "Stilts and the Titanic", produced and co-written by Binikos. The song was made available to listen and download as a demo version in Larson's Official Myspace in the summer 2006. On October 22, 2008 she announced to fans in a blog published in her official <a href="/wiki/Myspace" title="Myspace">Myspace</a> page that she will be recording a 5 track EP with new material and will provide backing vocals for artist <a href="/wiki/Caitlin_Crosby" title="Caitlin Crosby">Caitlin Crosby</a>'s latest album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-myspace1_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-myspace1-28">[28]</a></sup> The EP was to include a re-recorded version of <i>Ugly</i> (a track from her album) and four new songs: "Dear Universe", "Sharpen My Nails", "Arielle", and "If All Goes Well".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-myspace1_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-myspace1-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span></h3>
<p>Larson has written and directed two short films, titled <i>Weighting</i> (2011), and <i>The Arm</i> (2012). She has also appeared in a stage production of <i><a href="/wiki/Our_Town" title="Our Town">Our Town</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Williamstown_Theatre_Festival" title="Williamstown Theatre Festival">Williamstown Theatre Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Larson will be making her directorial debut in the comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Unicorn_Store" title="Unicorn Store">Unicorn Store</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Larson has been in a relationship with musician <a href="/wiki/Alex_Greenwald" title="Alex Greenwald">Alex Greenwald</a> since 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> In May 2016, Larson's rep confirmed that she and Greenwald are engaged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In February 2016, in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Elle_(magazine)" title="Elle (magazine)">Elle</a></i> magazine, Larson revealed that she has not spoken to her father in over ten years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span></h2>
