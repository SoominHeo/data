<p><b>Britt Ekland</b> (born <b>Britt-Marie Eklund</b>; 6 October 1942) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People_of_Sweden" title="People of Sweden">Swedish</a> film, stage, and television actress, and singer. She appeared in numerous films in her heyday throughout the 1960s and 1970s, including critically acclaimed roles in <a href="/wiki/William_Friedkin" title="William Friedkin">William Friedkin</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Night_They_Raided_Minsky%27s" title="The Night They Raided Minsky's">The Night They Raided Minsky's</a></i> (1968), and the British crime film <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Carter" title="Get Carter">Get Carter</a></i> (1971), which established her as a movie <a href="/wiki/Sex_symbol" title="Sex symbol">sex symbol</a>. She also starred in the British <a href="/wiki/Cult_film" title="Cult film">cult</a> horror film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wicker_Man_(1973_film)" title="The Wicker Man (1973 film)">The Wicker Man</a></i> (1973) and appeared as a <a href="/wiki/Bond_girl" title="Bond girl">Bond girl</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Man_with_the_Golden_Gun_(film)" title="The Man with the Golden Gun (film)">The Man with the Golden Gun</a></i> (1974).</p>
<p>Her high-profile social life and her 1964 marriage to actor <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sellers" title="Peter Sellers">Peter Sellers</a> attracted considerable press attention, leading to her being one of the most photographed celebrities in the world during the 1970s.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Britt_Ekland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ekland was born Britt-Marie Eklund in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stockholm,_Sweden" title="Stockholm, Sweden">Stockholm, Sweden</a> to Maj Britt, a secretary, and Sven Axel Eklund, who ran an upscale clothing store in Stockholm and was captain of the Swedish national <a href="/wiki/Curling" title="Curling">curling</a> team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-people-2">[2]</a></sup> Ekland's mother died of <a href="/wiki/Alzheimer%27s_disease" title="Alzheimer's disease">Alzheimer's disease</a> in the 1980s, which had a profound effect on her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ci_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ci-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ekland grew up with three younger brothers, and has said that she was overweight for much of her childhood: "I was very heavy. God, I was brutal-looking. I always tried to be funny to make up for the fact that I was fat and ugly."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-people-2">[2]</a></sup> As a teenager, Ekland left school to travel with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theater_company" title="Theater company">theater company</a>, and was spotted by a talent agent in a coffee shop while in Italy, who sent her to London to audition for films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-people-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_career">Film career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Britt_Ekland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Film career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_roles:_1960.E2.80.931970">Early roles: 1960–1970</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Britt_Ekland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early roles: 1960–1970">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ekland began her career with bit parts and uncredited walk-on roles, including her first onscreen role in <i><a href="/wiki/G.I._Blues" title="G.I. Blues">G.I. Blues</a></i> (1960). This was followed with a small supporting part in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Happy_Thieves" title="The Happy Thieves">The Happy Thieves</a></i> (1960). She had small roles in the Swedish films <i>Kort är sommaren</i> (1962) and <i>Det är hos mig han har varit</i> (1963), before landing her first major supporting part in the <a href="/wiki/George_Marshall" title="George Marshall">George Marshall</a> Western <i><a href="/wiki/Advance_to_the_Rear" title="Advance to the Rear">Advance to the Rear</a></i> (1964).</p>
<p>In 1964, she appeared in the Christmas television film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Carol_for_Another_Christmas" title="A Carol for Another Christmas">A Carol for Another Christmas</a></i>, meeting her future husband, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sellers" title="Peter Sellers">Peter Sellers</a>, her co-star in the film. She followed this with <i><a href="/wiki/After_the_Fox" title="After the Fox">After the Fox</a></i> (1966), also starring Sellers; she made one more film with Sellers, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bobo" title="The Bobo">The Bobo</a></i> (1967). This was followed with a lead role as an <a href="/wiki/Amish" title="Amish">Amish</a> girl-turned New York City <a href="/wiki/Burlesque" title="Burlesque">burlesque</a> dancer in <a href="/wiki/William_Friedkin" title="William Friedkin">William Friedkin</a>'s musical <i><a href="/wiki/The_Night_They_Raided_Minsky%27s" title="The Night They Raided Minsky's">The Night They Raided Minsky's</a></i> (1968), which earned Ekland critical acclaim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Next came <i><a href="/wiki/Stiletto" title="Stiletto">Stiletto</a></i> (1969), a crime drama, based on a novel by Harold Robbins, co-starring <a href="/wiki/Alex_Cord" title="Alex Cord">Alex Cord</a>. She then starred in a string of Italian films, <i><a href="/wiki/Machine_Gun_McCain" title="Machine Gun McCain">Machine Gun McCain</a></i> (1969), <i><a href="/wiki/Nell%27anno_del_Signore" title="Nell'anno del Signore">The Conspirators</a></i> (1969), and as <a href="/wiki/Antigone" title="Antigone">Antigone</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/I_cannibali" title="I cannibali">The Cannibals</a></i> (1970).</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sex_symbol_and_horror_roles:_1971.E2.80.931988">Sex symbol and horror roles: 1971–1988</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Britt_Ekland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sex symbol and horror roles: 1971–1988">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1971, she was cast as a leading lady and <a href="/wiki/Gun_moll" title="Gun moll">gun moll</a> in the iconic crime film <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Carter" title="Get Carter">Get Carter</a></i>, opposite <a href="/wiki/Michael_Caine" title="Michael Caine">Michael Caine</a>, which firmly established her as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blonde_bombshell_(stereotype)" title="Blonde bombshell (stereotype)">blonde bombshell</a>. The 1970s also saw Ekland in several horror films, including <i><a href="/wiki/What_the_Peeper_Saw" title="What the Peeper Saw">What the Peeper Saw</a></i> (1972) as a disturbed bride; the <a href="/wiki/Agatha_Christie" title="Agatha Christie">Agatha Christie</a> adaptation <i><a href="/wiki/Endless_Night_(1972_film)" title="Endless Night (1972 film)">Endless Night</a></i> (1972), playing the friend and companion of an American heiress; and as a hallucinatory figure in the anthology film <i><a href="/wiki/Asylum_(1972_horror_film)" title="Asylum (1972 horror film)">Asylum</a></i> (1972) opposite <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Rampling" title="Charlotte Rampling">Charlotte Rampling</a>. Her most iconic horror role came in the 1973 <a href="/wiki/Cult_film" title="Cult film">cult</a> horror film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wicker_Man_(1973_film)" title="The Wicker Man (1973 film)">The Wicker Man</a></i>, in which she played a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pagan" title="Pagan">Pagan</a> villager and seductress; however, her voice was dubbed in the film to disguise her Swedish-accented English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Other roles included in the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ultimate_Thrill" title="The Ultimate Thrill">The Ultimate Thrill</a></i> (1974) and the British drama <i><a href="/wiki/Baxter!" title="Baxter!">Baxter!</a></i> (1973). On television, she was cast in the TV film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Six_Million_Dollar_Man" title="The Six Million Dollar Man">The Six Million Dollar Man: Wine, Women and War</a></i> (1973) opposite <a href="/wiki/Lee_Majors" title="Lee Majors">Lee Majors</a>. Ekland's next prominent role came when she was cast as the lead <a href="/wiki/Bond_girl" title="Bond girl">Bond girl</a>, Mary Goodnight, in the 1974 <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Man_with_the_Golden_Gun_(film)" title="The Man with the Golden Gun (film)">The Man with the Golden Gun</a></i>, which received mixed reviews but furthered Ekland's status as a <a href="/wiki/Sex_symbol" title="Sex symbol">sex symbol</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ci_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-ci-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1976 she provided the French spoken part at the end of then boyfriend <a href="/wiki/Rod_Stewart" title="Rod Stewart">Rod Stewart</a>'s hit single "<a href="/wiki/Tonight%27s_the_Night_(Gonna_Be_Alright)" title="Tonight's the Night (Gonna Be Alright)">Tonight's the Night (Gonna Be Alright)</a>". Ekland also portrayed biographical characters, such as the one based on real-life actress <a href="/wiki/Anny_Ondra" title="Anny Ondra">Anny Ondra</a> (boxer <a href="/wiki/Max_Schmeling" title="Max Schmeling">Max Schmeling</a>'s wife) in the television movie <i>Ring of Passion</i> (1978). Ekland was also featured in the horror pictures <i><a href="/wiki/The_Monster_Club" title="The Monster Club">The Monster Club</a></i> (1980) and <i><a href="/wiki/Satan%27s_Mistress" title="Satan's Mistress">Satan's Mistress</a></i> (1982).</p>
<p>Ekland had supporting roles in independent films, and appeared in the 1985 comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Fraternity_Vacation" title="Fraternity Vacation">Fraternity Vacation</a></i>, followed by a role in the slasher film <i><a href="/wiki/Moon_in_Scorpio" title="Moon in Scorpio">Moon in Scorpio</a></i> (1988) and as prostitute Mariella Novotny in the feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Scandal_(1989_film)" title="Scandal (1989 film)">Scandal</a></i> (1989) about the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Profumo_Affair" title="Profumo Affair">Profumo Affair</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stage_and_television:_1989.E2.80.93present">Stage and television: 1989–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Britt_Ekland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stage and television: 1989–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She has guest-starred on various TV shows, including an appearance on the popular TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Superboy_(TV_series)" title="Superboy (TV series)">Superboy</a></i>, playing an alien disguised as <a href="/wiki/Lara_(comics)" title="Lara (comics)">Lara</a>, Superboy's biological mother, during the show's second season in 1990. Ekland published a beauty and fitness book in 1984 <i>Sensual Beauty: How to achieve it</i>, followed by a fitness video in 1992. In the 1999 <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/I_Love_the_%2770s_(UK_TV_series)" title="I Love the '70s (UK TV series)">I Love the '70s</a></i> she hosted the 1971 episode in homage to her role as "Anna" in the film), and <i>The Wicker Man</i>.</p>
<p>Ekland's later career has mainly consisted of stage and television, with her last feature film role being in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Children_(1990_film)" title="The Children (1990 film)">The Children</a></i> (1990). She appeared on stage as a cast member in <i><a href="/wiki/Cinderella" title="Cinderella">Cinderella</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Regent_Theatre,_Stoke-on-Trent" title="Regent Theatre, Stoke-on-Trent">Regent Theatre, Stoke-on-Trent</a> in December 1999 and January 2000. She also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Grumpy_Old_Women_Live" title="Grumpy Old Women Live">Grumpy Old Women Live</a></i>, participated in December 2007 in the Swedish reality show <i><a href="/wiki/Stj%C3%A4rnorna_p%C3%A5_slottet" title="Stjärnorna på slottet">Stjärnorna på slottet</a></i> (<i>The stars at the castle</i>) along with <a href="/wiki/Peter_Stormare" title="Peter Stormare">Peter Stormare</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arja_Saijonmaa" title="Arja Saijonmaa">Arja Saijonmaa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jan_Malmsj%C3%B6" title="Jan Malmsjö">Jan Malmsjö</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magnus_H%C3%A4renstam" title="Magnus Härenstam">Magnus Härenstam</a>, and in December 2007 and January 2008 she starred again in <i>Cinderella</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Wyvern_Theatre" title="Wyvern Theatre">Wyvern Theatre</a>, Swindon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She appeared as a guest on the British daytime television show <i><a href="/wiki/Loose_Women" title="Loose Women">Loose Women</a></i>, in January 2008. From December 2008 to January 2009, Britt starred in <i>Cinderella</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Shaw_Theatre" title="Shaw Theatre">Shaw Theatre</a> in London. In a rare instance of her singing, she performed the song <i>My Prince</i>, originally recorded by <a href="/wiki/Lara_Pulver" title="Lara Pulver">Lara Pulver</a> on the album <i>Act One – Songs from the Musicals of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_S._Bermange" title="Alexander S. Bermange">Alexander S. Bermange</a></i>. In 2009–10 she played the Fairy Godmother in <i>Cinderella</i> at Princess Theatre, Torquay. In December 2010 she starred as the 'Fairy Pea Pod' in Jack and the Beanstalk at the Kings Theatre, Southsea. She starred in further Pantomimes at the Theatre Royal, Windsor, in 2011 and 2012.</p>
<p>Ekland was one of the housewives of <i><a href="/wiki/Svenska_Hollywoodfruar" title="Svenska Hollywoodfruar">Svenska Hollywoodfruar</a></i> (en:Swedish Hollywoodwives) on <a href="/wiki/TV3_(Sweden)" title="TV3 (Sweden)">TV3</a> during the 2013 season. In 2010 Ekland took part in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reality_TV_show" title="Reality TV show">reality TV show</a> <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_a_Celebrity...Get_Me_Out_of_Here!" title="I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!">I'm a Celebrity...Get Me Out of Here!</a></i> where she was fourth to be voted off. During the program she developed a close relationship with <a href="/wiki/Stacey_Solomon" title="Stacey Solomon">Stacey Solomon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Havers" title="Nigel Havers">Nigel Havers</a>, while making an enemy of <a href="/wiki/Gillian_McKeith" title="Gillian McKeith">Gillian McKeith</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>Ekland is fluent in English, French, and German, in addition to her native Swedish.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships_and_family">Relationships and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Britt_Ekland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Relationships and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ekland became famous overnight as a result of her 1964 whirlwind romance and marriage to English actor and comedian, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sellers" title="Peter Sellers">Peter Sellers</a>, who proposed after seeing her photograph in the paper and then meeting in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sikov_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sikov-10">[10]</a></sup> She stood by him after he suffered a series of heart attacks shortly after their marriage. Ekland was stepmother to Sellers' children Sarah and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Sellers_(actor)" title="Michael Sellers (actor)">Michael</a> (who died of a heart attack at about the same age as his father). In January 1965 they had a daughter, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Sellers" title="Victoria Sellers">Victoria</a>. The couple made three films together—<i><a href="/wiki/A_Carol_for_Another_Christmas" title="A Carol for Another Christmas">A Carol for Another Christmas</a></i> in 1964, <i><a href="/wiki/After_the_Fox" title="After the Fox">After the Fox</a></i> in 1966 and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bobo" title="The Bobo">The Bobo</a></i> in 1967—before divorcing in 1968.</p>
<p>In June 1973 she had a son, Nic Adler, with record producer <a href="/wiki/Lou_Adler" title="Lou Adler">Lou Adler</a>.</p>
<p>She also had a much publicised romance with rock star <a href="/wiki/Rod_Stewart" title="Rod Stewart">Rod Stewart</a>; they were introduced in 1975 by <a href="/wiki/Joan_Collins" title="Joan Collins">Joan Collins</a> and lived together for more than two years, with Ekland giving up her career to focus on the relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 1979–1981 she dated and became engaged to <a href="/wiki/Girl_(band)" title="Girl (band)">Girl</a> frontman and future <a href="/wiki/L.A._Guns" title="L.A. Guns">L.A. Guns</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Phil_Lewis" title="Phil Lewis">Phil Lewis</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In 1984, at the age of 42, she married <a href="/wiki/Stray_Cats" title="Stray Cats">Stray Cats</a> drummer <a href="/wiki/Slim_Jim_Phantom" title="Slim Jim Phantom">Slim Jim Phantom</a>, who was 19 years younger than her, aged 23. They had a son, Thomas Jefferson (born in 1988).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> They divorced in 1992.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Britt_Ekland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the 1970s Ekland was one of the most photographed and talked-about celebrities in the world and in 1980 her best-selling autobiography, <i><a href="/wiki/True_Britt" title="True Britt">True Britt</a></i>, was published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>While <a href="/wiki/Rod_Stewart" title="Rod Stewart">Rod Stewart</a>'s domestic partner, Ekland inspired his hit song <i><a href="/wiki/Tonight%27s_the_Night_(Gonna_Be_Alright)" title="Tonight's the Night (Gonna Be Alright)">Tonight's the Night (Gonna Be Alright)</a></i>—1977's <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Year-End_Hot_100_singles_of_1977" title="Billboard Year-End Hot 100 singles of 1977">overall #1 song</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-People_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-People-14">[14]</a></sup> The song features a French spoken part from Ekland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-People_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-People-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004 Ekland was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Charlize_Theron" title="Charlize Theron">Charlize Theron</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_and_Death_of_Peter_Sellers" title="The Life and Death of Peter Sellers">The Life and Death of Peter Sellers</a></i>. Theron invited her to be her date at the <a href="/wiki/Cannes_Film_Festival" title="Cannes Film Festival">Cannes Film Festival</a>, where she became highly emotional when she saw the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
