<p><b>Brittany Mariko Ishibashi</b> (born November 2, 1980) is an American television and film actress best known for her roles as Anne Ogami on the USA Network miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Political_Animals_(miniseries)" title="Political Animals (miniseries)">Political Animals</a></i>, and Maggie Zeddmore in the <i><a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Supernatural (U.S. TV series)">Supernatural</a></i> spin-off <i><a href="/wiki/Supernatural_(U.S._TV_series)#Ghostfacers" title="Supernatural (U.S. TV series)">Ghostfacers</a></i>. Ishibashi portrayed <a href="/wiki/Karai_(Teenage_Mutant_Ninja_Turtles)" title="Karai (Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles)">Karai</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Teenage_Mutant_Ninja_Turtles:_Out_of_the_Shadows" title="Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Out of the Shadows">Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Out of the Shadows</a></i>, the sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/Teenage_Mutant_Ninja_Turtles_(2014_film)" title="Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2014 film)">Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brittany_Ishibashi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ishibashi was born and raised in Orange County, California, and comes from a family of entertainers. She is of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> descent. Her father, Gerald Ishibashi, is a concert promoter and musician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Her mother, Lisa Ishibashi, is a singer. Her two sisters, Brianna Ishibashi and Brooke Ishibashi, are actresses and writers. Growing up backstage with Rock and Roll Hall of Famers naturally fueled her interest in the arts, and Ishibashi discovered her passion for musical theatre at a very young age. A formally trained dancer, singer, and actress, Ishibashi is also a black belt in <a href="/wiki/Taekwondo" title="Taekwondo">taekwondo</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brittany_Ishibashi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ishibashi's first big break came when JJ Abrams cast her in <i>Felicity</i> just out of high school. She continued her formal education at <a href="/wiki/UCLA_School_of_Theater,_Film_and_Television" title="UCLA School of Theater, Film and Television">UCLA School of Theater, Film and Television</a> while continuing to build her resume outside of classes. Ishibashi has been consistently working ever since, with recurring roles and guest appearances in over three dozen television shows, including <i>The Office</i>, <i>Grey's Anatomy</i>, and <i>Supernatural</i>. After an exciting debut at Comic-Con, Ishibashi reprised her role as Maggie Zeddmore in the <i>Supernatural</i> spin-off <i>Ghostfacers</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> which has developed a global fan following. She has also worked on nearly two dozen films and multimedia projects, under the direction of such creatives as Joan Scheckel, Robert Redford, and Danny DeVito.</p>
<p>Ishibashi is probably best known for her role as Anne Ogami on USA Network's Golden Globe and Emmy-nominated series, <i><a href="/wiki/Political_Animals_(miniseries)" title="Political Animals (miniseries)">Political Animals</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Ishibashi will next be seen in the original Netflix series, <i><a href="/wiki/Grace_and_Frankie" title="Grace and Frankie">Grace and Frankie</a></i>, and starring in <a href="/wiki/Wong_Fu_Productions" title="Wong Fu Productions">Wong Fu Productions</a> first feature film, <i>Everything Before Us</i>, released June 3, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2015, Ishibashi launched her production shingle, Mana Moments, with a focus on comedic, female driven content.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ishibashi played <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karai_(character)" title="Karai (character)">Karai</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Teenage_Mutant_Ninja_Turtles:_Out_of_the_Shadows" title="Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Out of the Shadows">Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Out of the Shadows</a></i>, the sequel to <i><a href="/wiki/Teenage_Mutant_Ninja_Turtles_(2014_film)" title="Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2014 film)">Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> released in theaters on June 3, 2016.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brittany_Ishibashi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 29, 2011, Ishibashi married Jeff Horowitz at the Mauna Kea Beach Hotel on the Big Island of Hawaii. SNL alum, <a href="/wiki/Nasim_Pedrad" title="Nasim Pedrad">Nasim Pedrad</a>, was the officiant at their wedding. The couple lives in Los Angeles with their son, Kai, born June 3, 2014.</p>
