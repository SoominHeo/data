<p><b>Tree: Live Tour 2014</b> (stylized as <b>Tohoshinki Live Tour 2014 ～TREE～</b>), also known as the <b>Tree Tour</b>, was the seventh Japanese <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concert_tour" title="Concert tour">concert tour</a> (eleventh overall) by South Korean pop duo <a href="/wiki/TVXQ" title="TVXQ">Tohoshinki</a>, and was launched in support of their seventh Japanese studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Tree_(TVXQ_album)" title="Tree (TVXQ album)">Tree</a></i> (2014). It was first announced by the duo in November 2013, during the last show of their fanclub event tour, <i>The Mission II</i>. The theme of the tour was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tree_of_Life" title="Tree of Life">Tree of Life</a>, in which Tohoshinki, two beings born from the tree of life, use their spirit to save humanity from darkness.</p>
<p>The Tree Tour kicked off in <a href="/wiki/Yokohama" title="Yokohama">Yokohama</a> on April 22, 2014 and visited a total of ten cities and eleven venues in Japan. It concluded on June 22, 2014 in <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">Osaka</a>. Over 600,000 people attended the tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attendance_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-attendance-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tree:_Live_Tour_2014&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On November 23, 2013, Tohoshinki held their fanclub event tour <i>The Mission II</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Super_Arena" title="Saitama Super Arena">Saitama Super Arena</a>, where they performed several songs from their last two albums and interacted with fans. At the end of the show, Tohoshinki revealed their plans to embark on a new nationwide tour in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tour_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tour-2">[2]</a></sup> Following the duo's unveil, twenty-six dates for their tour were announced by their record label <a href="/wiki/Avex_Trax" title="Avex Trax">Avex Trax</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tour_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-tour-2">[2]</a></sup> but the name of the tour was not officially revealed until January 2014, when Tohoshinki announced the release of their seventh Japanese album, <i><a href="/wiki/Tree_(TVXQ_album)" title="Tree (TVXQ album)">Tree</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officialtour:_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-officialtour:-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Unlike the duo's previous <a href="/wiki/Time:_Live_Tour_2013" title="Time: Live Tour 2013">Time: Live Tour 2013</a>, which visited all five of Japan's major concert domes including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nissan_Stadium_(Yokohama)" title="Nissan Stadium (Yokohama)">Nissan Stadium</a>, the Tree Tour visited smaller concert halls and arenas. The duo stated that they wanted to perform in smaller venues in order to have better interactions with their audience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-small_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-small-4">[4]</a></sup> The Tree Tour began with three shows in the <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_Arena" title="Yokohama Arena">Yokohama Arena</a> in April 2014.</p>
<p>On February 24, 2014, three additional dates were announced, extending the tour's shows at <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Dome" title="Tokyo Dome">Tokyo Dome</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Osaka_Dome" title="Osaka Dome">Osaka Dome</a> to four days each,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-extension_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-extension-5">[5]</a></sup> making Tohoshinki the first non-Japanese band to hold concerts at the Tokyo Dome for three years in a row.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On March 20, the tour's official website and merchandise were launched.</p>

<p><i><b>Tohoshinki Live Tour 2014 ～TREE～</b></i> is a live <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> <a href="/wiki/Concert_film" title="Concert film">concert film</a> by South Korean pop duo <a href="/wiki/TVXQ" title="TVXQ">Tohoshinki</a>, released on August 27, 2014 in Japan. The DVD was filmed during the duo's seventh Japan-wide concert tour Tree, which ran from April to June 2014. The tour stopped by ten cities and visited eleven different venues in Japan, including the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Dome" title="Tokyo Dome">Tokyo Dome</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Osaka_Dome" title="Osaka Dome">Kyocera Dome Osaka</a>, rounding up to a total of 29 shows.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tree:_Live_Tour_2014&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The DVD documents the live performances during the tour's four-day stop at the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Dome" title="Tokyo Dome">Tokyo Dome</a>, between May 20 and 24, 2014. It was released in three separate editions; a 2-DVD standard edition, a 3-DVD limited edition, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray</a>. The limited edition also includes a third DVD, which features backstage interviews, behind-the-scene footage, a tour documentary, an MC digest segment, and an additional photobook pamphlet.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tree:_Live_Tour_2014&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Live Tour 2014: Tree</i> debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon DVD Chart</a>, selling 104,000 copies on the first week of release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It was Tohoshinki's fifth DVD release to debut at number-one in Japan. The Blu-ray edition also debuted at number one on the Oricon Blu-ray Chart, selling 18,000 copies on the first week.</p>
<p>The following week, both the DVD and the Blu-ray fell to number three. The DVD stayed charted in the top ten for four consecutive weeks. It ultimately charted for 13 weeks and sold 116,000 units in Japan,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oricondvd_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-oricondvd-9">[9]</a></sup> becoming the eleventh best-selling DVD of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yr_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-yr-10">[10]</a></sup> The Blu-ray edition stayed charted for six weeks, selling 20,000 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oriconb_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-oriconb-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
