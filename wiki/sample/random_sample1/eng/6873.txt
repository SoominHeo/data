<p><b>Vladimir "Vladi" Luxuria</b> (born <b>Wladimiro Guadagno</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Foggia" title="Foggia">Foggia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apulia" title="Apulia">Apulia</a>, on June 24, 1965) is an Italian <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actress</a>, <a href="/wiki/Writer" title="Writer">writer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_host" title="Television host">television host</a>. Luxuria was a <a href="/wiki/Communist_Refoundation_Party" title="Communist Refoundation Party">Communist Refoundation Party</a> member of the Italian parliament, belonging to <a href="/wiki/Romano_Prodi" title="Romano Prodi">Romano Prodi</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Union_(political_coalition)" title="The Union (political coalition)">L'Unione</a> coalition. She was the first openly <a href="/wiki/Transgender" title="Transgender">transgender</a> member of Parliament in Europe, and the world's second openly transgender MP after New Zealander <a href="/wiki/Georgina_Beyer" title="Georgina Beyer">Georgina Beyer</a>. She lost her seat in the election of April, 2008.</p>
<p>Although Luxuria lives exclusively as a female, she has not undergone <a href="/wiki/Sex_change" title="Sex change">sex change</a> surgery and she remains legally male. She has stated on occasion that she perceives herself as neither male nor female.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gay_rights_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gay_rights-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Italian_general_election,_2006" title="Italian general election, 2006">2006 general election</a>, Luxuria was elected to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Chamber_of_Deputies" title="Italian Chamber of Deputies">Chamber of Deputies</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Lazio" title="Lazio">Lazio</a> 1 constituency in Rome. She lost her seat in the <a href="/wiki/Italian_general_election,_2008" title="Italian general election, 2008">2008 election</a>. After the retirement of Beyer and Luxuria, there were no transgender MPs reported in the world, until 2011, when <a href="/wiki/Anna_Grodzka" title="Anna Grodzka">Anna Grodzka</a> was elected to <a href="/wiki/Sejm" title="Sejm">the Polish parliament</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vladimir_Luxuria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vladimir_Luxuria_-_Roma_Pride_2008.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="1944" height="240" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Vladimir_Luxuria_-_Roma_Pride_2008.JPG/180px-Vladimir_Luxuria_-_Roma_Pride_2008.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Vladimir_Luxuria_-_Roma_Pride_2008.JPG/270px-Vladimir_Luxuria_-_Roma_Pride_2008.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Vladimir_Luxuria_-_Roma_Pride_2008.JPG/360px-Vladimir_Luxuria_-_Roma_Pride_2008.JPG 2x" width="180"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

<p>Luxuria moved to Rome in 1985 to study foreign languages and literature. She also began to act, notably in <a href="/wiki/Cabaret" title="Cabaret">cabaret</a>, and through this developed her <a href="/wiki/Gender_identity" title="Gender identity">gender ambiguity</a> as a hallmark. Her assumed surname, Luxuria, means lust in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Standard_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Standard-4">[4]</a></sup> She earned her first acting credit in <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cena_alle_nove&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cena alle nove (page does not exist)">Cena alle nove</a></i> by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Paolo_Breccia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Paolo Breccia (page does not exist)">Paolo Breccia</a> in 1991; and began organizing parties and <a href="/wiki/Gay_pride" title="Gay pride">gay pride</a> events, becoming director of the <i>Muccassassina</i>, the self-financing party of the <i>Circle of homosexual culture Mario Mieli</i>. She organized Italy's first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pride_festival" title="Pride festival">pride festival</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Standard_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Standard-4">[4]</a></sup> in Rome on June 2, 1994, which attracted some ten thousand people. From 2001 to 2003 she toured Italian theatres with the musical <i>Emotions</i> co-starring with <a href="/wiki/Sabrina_Salerno" title="Sabrina Salerno">Sabrina Salerno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ambra_Angiolini" title="Ambra Angiolini">Ambra Angiolini</a>.</p>
<p>Her career as performer was not restricted to stage shows, and in 2005 she hosted a television show about nostalgia for 1980s music and culture on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Music" title="All Music">All Music</a>. She also became well known for participating in charity organizations before making the transition to politics.</p>
<p>Luxuria identifies using the English word "transgender" and prefers feminine pronouns, titles, and adjectives. She is legally male. Despite choosing not to undergo sex reassignment surgery, she has had some operations to make herself more identifiably female; such as the permanent removal of <a href="/wiki/Facial_hair" title="Facial hair">facial hair</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Rhinoplasty" title="Rhinoplasty">rhinoplasty</a> and partial breast construction. Upon entering parliament, she made the decision to stop wearing her trademark <a href="/wiki/Drag_queen" title="Drag queen">drag clothing</a> – an extravagant cocktail of <a href="/wiki/Sequin" title="Sequin">sequins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Feather_boa" title="Feather boa">feather boas</a> and bouffant wigs – saying that the legislature was "not a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Discoth%C3%A8que" title="Discothèque">discothèque</a>" and that, "It wouldn't be useful to provoke [people] in such a stupid way."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gay_rights_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gay_rights-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>After her bid for re-election failed in April 2008, Luxuria appeared on <i>L'Isola dei Famosi</i>, which has been described as "Italy's celebrity answer to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Survivor_(TV_series)" title="Survivor (TV series)">Survivor</a></i>". The show, which saw her pip Argentine model and showgirl <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belen_Rodriguez" title="Belen Rodriguez">Belen Rodriguez</a> (former girlfriend of Italy footballer <a href="/wiki/Marco_Borriello" title="Marco Borriello">Marco Borriello</a>) to first place in a public poll, took place in <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>. Luxuria said of her victory that "The Italian public has shown itself to be more forward-looking than our politicians, who thought I would turn up in parliament dressed like (former porn-star politician) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cicciolina" title="Cicciolina">Cicciolina</a>." Pledging to donate half of her €200,000 prize-money to charity, Luxuria chose <a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a>, saying "I know that I won't have children and I want to help disadvantaged children in my own way."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mag_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-mag-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>She was chosen to host the 2012 edition of the Italian TV program <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%27isola_dei_famosi" title="L'isola dei famosi">L'Isola dei Famosi</a></i>, on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RaiDue" title="RaiDue">RaiDue</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Election_in_2006">Election in 2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vladimir_Luxuria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Election in 2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although her Lazio 1 constituency was seen as a safe <a href="/wiki/Communist_Refoundation_Party" title="Communist Refoundation Party">Communist</a> seat, her election was not without difficulties, particularly after it was disclosed that she had been a <a href="/wiki/Sex_worker" title="Sex worker">sex worker</a> for a time shortly after arriving in Rome due to the difficulty of finding a normal job as a transgender individual. <a href="/wiki/Clemente_Mastella" title="Clemente Mastella">Clemente Mastella</a>, the leader of the centrist UDEUR party (a fellow member of the coalition) called her "a ridiculous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cicciolina" title="Cicciolina">Cicciolina</a>". <a href="/wiki/Alessandra_Mussolini" title="Alessandra Mussolini">Alessandra Mussolini</a>, said, referring to Luxuria, that it was "better to be a fascist than a faggot" (<i>meglio fascista che frocio</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> However, Luxuria's name was placed second on the list of Communist candidates for Lazio, after party leader <a href="/wiki/Fausto_Bertinotti" title="Fausto Bertinotti">Fausto Bertinotti</a>, which increased her chances of being elected (Italy uses a system of <a href="/wiki/Proportional_representation" title="Proportional representation">proportional representation</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Standard_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Standard-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the election, she and another candidate were attacked by a group of fifteen people, allegedly including <a href="/wiki/National_Alliance_(Italy)" title="National Alliance (Italy)">Alleanza Nazionale</a> politicians; they pelted her with <a href="/wiki/Fennel" title="Fennel">fennel</a> (in Italian <i>finocchio</i>, a word also meaning "faggot"). The politicians in question were suspended by AN; Luxuria criticized the police for the time it took for them to respond to the incident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Service">Service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vladimir_Luxuria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Her service in the Italian parliament got off to a rocky start, when in October 2006 <a href="/wiki/Forza_Italia" title="Forza Italia">Forza Italia</a> MP <a href="/wiki/Elisabetta_Gardini" title="Elisabetta Gardini">Elisabetta Gardini</a> insisted that she should not be allowed to use women's washrooms in the parliament building and called for the creation of a third washroom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Gardini described finding Luxuria presence there as a "sexual violence"; and later faced condemnations from coalition deputies for displaying prejudice tantamount to racism. Luxuria declared that she had used the toilets for years and that using the male lavatory would engender even greater problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Italian_general_election,_2008" title="Italian general election, 2008">2008 election</a>, the Re-foundation Communist Party joined a coalition of left-wing parties known as the <a href="/wiki/The_Left_%E2%80%93_The_Rainbow" title="The Left – The Rainbow">Rainbow Left</a>. However this group gained only 3.2% of the vote and lost all of its seats in parliament. <a href="/wiki/Silvio_Berlusconi" title="Silvio Berlusconi">Silvio Berlusconi</a>'s centre-right coalition swept to victory. Luxuria was not re-elected. <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Ferrero" title="Paolo Ferrero">Paolo Ferrero</a>, then leader of the Communist Refoundation Party, said that he would be open to the idea of her returning to politics as a nominee for the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2009_(Italy)" title="European Parliament election, 2009 (Italy)">2009 European Parliament election</a> after her win on <i>L'Isola dei Famosi</i>, but Luxuria said that she had no plans to re-enter politics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mag_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-mag-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gay_rights">Gay rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vladimir_Luxuria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gay rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Luxuria has long been a strong advocate for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gay_rights" title="Gay rights">gay rights</a> and a participant in events promoting equality for homosexuals. She helped organize Italy's first gay pride festival in 1994 and continued her activism throughout her tenure as a politician; in May 2007, she took part in the second <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Pride" title="Moscow Pride">Muscovite gay pride</a> parade.</p>
<p>She used her prominence in Italian politics once elected as a platform for advocating gay rights. In the lead-up to her election, Luxuria made gay rights an issue of her campaign and felt herself to be a representative of the <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> community, saying, "We don't want privileges – we want our rights." In addition, Luxuria called for <a href="/wiki/Civil_union" title="Civil union">civil unions</a> to be enabled for gay couples and for Italy to accommodate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Political_asylum" title="Political asylum">political asylum</a> for "all gays who try to get into Italy from countries where homosexuality is punishable by death".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gay_rights_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Gay_rights-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Luxuria also campaigned prior to the elections for gays to have <a href="/wiki/Cohabitation" title="Cohabitation">cohabitation</a> rights, and had helped campaign by winning the support of Italy's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left-wing" title="Left-wing">left</a>. Furthermore, Luxuria outlined her long-term support for full gay marriage rights, comparable with <a href="/wiki/Same-sex_marriage_in_Spain" title="Same-sex marriage in Spain">Spain's implementation of the law</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vatican_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vatican-10">[10]</a></sup> In September 2006, she stated that the <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican</a>'s ongoing influence in politics, specifically in regards to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gay_marriage" title="Gay marriage">gay marriage</a>, contravened clauses of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Constitution" title="Italian Constitution">Italian Constitution</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vatican_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Vatican-10">[10]</a></sup> Luxuria reacted to <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XVI" title="Pope Benedict XVI">Pope Benedict XVI</a>'s end-of-year speech in 2008, when he compared protecting the environment with saving humanity from a "blurring of gender" (homosexual or transsexual behaviour), by saying that such comments were "hurtful".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
