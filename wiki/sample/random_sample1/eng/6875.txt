<p><b>Vladimir Alexandrovich Voevodsky</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Влади́мир Алекса́ндрович Воево́дский</span>, born 4 June 1966) is a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematician</a>. His work in developing a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homotopy_theory" title="Homotopy theory">homotopy theory</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Algebraic_varieties" title="Algebraic varieties">algebraic varieties</a> and formulating <a href="/wiki/Motivic_cohomology" title="Motivic cohomology">motivic cohomology</a> led to the award of a <a href="/wiki/Fields_Medal" title="Fields Medal">Fields Medal</a> in 2002. He is also known for the proof of the <a href="/wiki/Milnor_conjecture" title="Milnor conjecture">Milnor conjecture</a> and motivic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bloch-Kato_conjectures" title="Bloch-Kato conjectures">Bloch-Kato conjectures</a> and for the <a href="/wiki/Univalent_foundations" title="Univalent foundations">univalent foundations</a> of mathematics and <a href="/wiki/Homotopy_type_theory" title="Homotopy type theory">homotopy type theory</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vladimir_Voevodsky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vladimir Voevodsky's father, Aleksander Voevodsky, was head of the Laboratory of High Energy Leptons in the Institute for Nuclear Research at the Russian Academy of Sciences. His mother was a chemist. Voevodsky attended <a href="/wiki/Moscow_State_University" title="Moscow State University">Moscow State University</a> and received his <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D.</a> in mathematics from <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> in 1992, advised by <a href="/wiki/David_Kazhdan" title="David Kazhdan">David Kazhdan</a>. Currently he is a full professor at the <a href="/wiki/Institute_for_Advanced_Study" title="Institute for Advanced Study">Institute for Advanced Study</a> in <a href="/wiki/Princeton,_New_Jersey" title="Princeton, New Jersey">Princeton, New Jersey</a>.</p>
<p>While he was a first year undergraduate, he was given a copy of <a href="/wiki/Esquisse_d%27un_Programme" title="Esquisse d'un Programme">Esquisse d'un Programme</a> (submitted a few months earlier by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Grothendieck" title="Alexander Grothendieck">Alexander Grothendieck</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CNRS" title="CNRS">CNRS</a>) by his advisor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=George_Shabat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="George Shabat (page does not exist)">George Shabat</a>. He learnt the <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French language</a> "with the sole purpose of being able to read this text" and started his research on some of the themes mentioned there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vladimir_Voevodsky&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Voevodsky's work is in the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Algebraic_geometry" title="Algebraic geometry">algebraic geometry</a> with <a href="/wiki/Algebraic_topology" title="Algebraic topology">algebraic topology</a>. Along with <a href="/wiki/Fabien_Morel" title="Fabien Morel">Fabien Morel</a>, Voevodsky introduced a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homotopy_theory" title="Homotopy theory">homotopy theory</a> for <a href="/wiki/Scheme_(mathematics)" title="Scheme (mathematics)">schemes</a>. He also formulated what is now believed to be the correct form of <a href="/wiki/Motivic_cohomology" title="Motivic cohomology">motivic cohomology</a>, and used this new tool to prove <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milnor%27s_conjecture" title="Milnor's conjecture">Milnor's conjecture</a> relating the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milnor" title="Milnor">Milnor</a> <a href="/wiki/K-theory" title="K-theory">K-theory</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Field_(mathematics)" title="Field (mathematics)">field</a> to its <a href="/wiki/%C3%89tale_cohomology" title="Étale cohomology">étale cohomology</a>. For the above, he received the <a href="/wiki/Fields_Medal" title="Fields Medal">Fields Medal</a> at the 24th International Congress of Mathematicians held in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>He is coauthor (with <a href="/wiki/Andrei_Suslin" title="Andrei Suslin">Andrei Suslin</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eric_M._Friedlander" title="Eric M. Friedlander">Eric M. Friedlander</a>) of <i>Cycles, Transfers and Motivic Homology Theories</i>, which develops the theory of motivic cohomology in some detail.</p>
<p>In January 2009, at an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IHES" title="IHES">IHES</a> anniversary conference about <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Grothendieck" title="Alexander Grothendieck">Alexander Grothendieck</a>, Voevodsky announced a proof of the full <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bloch-Kato_conjectures" title="Bloch-Kato conjectures">Bloch-Kato conjectures</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009 he constructed the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Univalent_model&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Univalent model (page does not exist)">univalent model</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin-L%C3%B6f_type_theory" title="Martin-Löf type theory">Martin-Löf type theory</a> in <a href="/wiki/Simplicial_set" title="Simplicial set">simplicial sets</a>. This led to important advances in type theory and in the development of new <a href="/wiki/Univalent_foundations" title="Univalent foundations">Univalent foundations</a> of mathematics that Voevodsky is currently working on.</p>
<p>In April 2016 the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Gothenburg" title="University of Gothenburg">University of Gothenburg</a> decided to award an honorary doctorate to Voevodsky.</p>
