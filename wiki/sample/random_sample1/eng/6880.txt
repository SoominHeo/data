<p><i><b>Black Sails</b></i> is an American <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">dramatic</a> <a href="/wiki/Adventure_fiction" title="Adventure fiction">adventure</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> set on <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">New Providence</a> Island and a prequel to <a href="/wiki/Robert_Louis_Stevenson" title="Robert Louis Stevenson">Robert Louis Stevenson</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/Treasure_Island" title="Treasure Island">Treasure Island</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zap2it.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-zap2it.com-1">[1]</a></sup> The series was created by <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_E._Steinberg" title="Jonathan E. Steinberg">Jonathan E. Steinberg</a> and Robert Levine for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Starz_(TV_channel)" title="Starz (TV channel)">Starz</a>. It debuted online for free on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> and other various streaming platform and video on demand services on January 18, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The debut on cable television followed a week later on January 25, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-premiere_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-3">[3]</a></sup> Steinberg is executive producer, alongside <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bay" title="Michael Bay">Michael Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Fuller" title="Bradley Fuller">Brad Fuller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Form" title="Andrew Form">Andrew Form</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Michael_Angeli" title="Michael Angeli">Michael Angeli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doris_Egan" title="Doris Egan">Doris Egan</a>, and Levine are co-executive producers.</p>
<p>On July 26, 2013, Starz renewed the show for a ten-episode second season, which premiered on January 24, 2015. The early renewal, six months before the first season premiered, was based on the positive fan reaction to the show at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">San Diego Comic-Con</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The series was renewed for a third season on October 12, 2014, and a fourth season on July 31, 2015, both before the respective previous seasons had premiered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 20, 2016, Starz announced that the series' upcoming fourth season would be its last; the season premiered on January 29, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Sails_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Black_Sails_episodes" title="List of Black Sails episodes">List of Black Sails episodes</a></div>
<p><i>Black Sails</i> is set roughly two decades before the events of <i>Treasure Island</i> and during the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Age_of_Piracy" title="Golden Age of Piracy">Golden Age of Piracy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zap2it.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-zap2it.com-1">[1]</a></sup> Feared <a href="/wiki/Captain_Flint" title="Captain Flint">Captain Flint</a> brings on a younger crew member as they fight for the survival of <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">New Providence</a> island. According to the first episode, "In 1715 <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>, the pirates of New Providence Island threaten maritime trade in the region. The laws of every civilized nation declare them <i><a href="/wiki/Hostis_humani_generis" title="Hostis humani generis">hostis humani generis</a></i>, enemies of all mankind. In response, the pirates adhere to a doctrine of their own....war against the world." Real life pirates who are fictionalized in the show include <a href="/wiki/Anne_Bonny" title="Anne Bonny">Anne Bonny</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Hornigold" title="Benjamin Hornigold">Benjamin Hornigold</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jack_Rackham" title="Jack Rackham">Jack Rackham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Vane" title="Charles Vane">Charles Vane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Low" title="Edward Low">Ned Low</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edward_Teach" title="Edward Teach">Blackbeard</a>.</p>
<p>The plot of the first season focuses on the hunt for the Spanish treasure galleon <i><a href="/wiki/Urca_de_Lima" title="Urca de Lima">Urca de Lima</a>.</i> At the beginning of the second season, the treasure from the Urca de Lima has been stranded on the shores of Florida with Spanish soldiers guarding it, but by the conclusion of the second season, the treasure has been taken by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jack_Rackham" title="Jack Rackham">Jack Rackham</a> and his crew and brought to New Providence Island. During the second season, the questions of how and why Flint—a man who was a navy officer and London gentleman—turned to piracy are answered.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Sails_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series is filmed at Cape Town Film Studios in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Town,_South_Africa" title="Cape Town, South Africa">Cape Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, with local production company Film Afrika.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sa_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-sa-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>The opening title sequence was made by Imaginary Forces and directors Michelle Dougherty and Karin Fong with the backing <a href="/wiki/Sea_shanty" title="Sea shanty">sea shanty</a> inspired theme composed by <i><a href="/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_(2004_TV_series)" title="Battlestar Galactica (2004 TV series)">Battlestar Galactica</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Walking_Dead_(TV_series)" title="The Walking Dead (TV series)">The Walking Dead</a></i> composer <a href="/wiki/Bear_McCreary" title="Bear McCreary">Bear McCreary</a>. It accurately features an instrument of the period in the form of the <a href="/wiki/Hurdy-gurdy" title="Hurdy-gurdy">hurdy-gurdy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>For the amount of detail that was added to the vessels, it took over 300 workers to build just one ship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Sails_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first season of <i>Black Sails</i> received mixed reviews from critics. On <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> the season holds a rating of 64%, based on 44 reviews, with an average rating of 6/10. The site's consensus reads, "<i>Black Sails</i> boasts visual appeal, but the show's bland characters aren't strong enough to keep the show from being dragged down into its murky depths of aimless exposition".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> season one has a score of 58 of 100, based on 27 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>Tim Goodman of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i> said, "This ambitious pirate story is helped immensely by going beyond the pay cable freedoms that often bog down lesser shows in boobs, blood and sex. <i>Black Sails</i> steers itself out of that realm after a few episodes and makes a play for bigger, more complicated stories".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Robert Lloyd of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Los_Angeles_Times" title="The Los Angeles Times">The Los Angeles Times</a></i> said, "<i>Black Sails</i> is a pirate treasure. The Starz series vividly depicts the daily life of pirates."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Jeff Jensen of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> said, "Not even a guilty pleasure, <i>Black Sails</i> is arrrrrr-estingly good".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Tom Long of the <i>Detroit News</i> commented "Alliances are made and broken, power shifts go this way and that, blood is spilled, and wenches keep wenching. It's oddly addictive, and the cast—made up mostly of British, Australian, and Canadian actors—is as sharp as you'd expect from pay cable".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Conversely, Brian Lowry of <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> said, "<i>Black Sails</i> never quite takes off, developing into a tired treasure hunt with indifferent casting and stock characters. Counting Michael Bay among its producers, this South Africa–lensed production might tempt adventure-seeking viewers to plunge into its crystal-blue waters, but despite some handsome aspects, the show ultimately proves as hollow as its CGI-rendered ships".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> Todd VanDerWerff of <a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a> said, "<i>Black Sails</i> is a handsome illusion at times, but it rarely finds its way beyond that."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second season of the show currently holds a 100% rating on Rotten Tomatoes, based on 5 reviews, with an average rating of 8.6/10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a> gave the season a score of 8.8/10, saying that it featured "great action... blended with an awesome and illuminating backstory".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Neil_Genzlinger" title="Neil Genzlinger">Neil Genzlinger</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> said, "Starz knows the formula for these costume-heavy action dramas from experience with shows like 'Spartacus' and 'Camelot.' And that formula is executed with particular skill in 'Black Sails,' thanks to some strong performances and an exploration of the consequences of greed that could have come out of modern-day Wall Street".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_Sails_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Black Sails</i> received four nominations for Outstanding Main Title Theme Music, Outstanding Main Title Design, Outstanding Special Visual Effects in a Supporting Role and Outstanding Sound Editing for a Series at the <a href="/wiki/66th_Primetime_Creative_Arts_Emmy_Awards" title="66th Primetime Creative Arts Emmy Awards">66th Primetime Creative Arts Emmy Awards</a>. The series won Outstanding Special Visual Effects in a Supporting Role, and Outstanding Sound Editing for a Series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></p>
